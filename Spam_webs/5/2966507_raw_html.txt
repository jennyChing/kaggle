    <h2>
     05.1.09
    </h2>
    <div class="post-723 post hentry category-quotes" id="post-723">
     <h3 class="storytitle">
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/2009/05/01/everything-i-know-in-math-i-learned-from-my-profs/" rel="bookmark">
       Everything I know in math, I learned from my profs
      </a>
     </h3>
     <div class="meta">
      Filed under:
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/category/quotes/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Quotes">
       Quotes
      </a>
        Travis @
     </div>
     <div class="storycontent">
      <p class="note">
       A collection of quotes from some of my professors back when I was a student.
      </p>
      <p>
       Be sure to show your work for partial credit.  You guys live and die by partial credit In fact,
       <em>
        everybody
       </em>
       lives and dies by partial credit.  Nobody gets anything right anymore. (Albert Stralka)
      </p>
      <p>
       Break ties with parantheses. (Sam Buss)
      </p>
      <p>
       You should always worry about the integers in the dot dot dot portion of a definition. (Sam Buss)
      </p>
      <p>
       Its Monday, so I use Delta z notation.  (Carl FitzGerald)
      </p>
      <p>
       This is proof by repeated assertion.  (Joel Franklin)
      </p>
      <p>
       We will prove this by the method of prolonged staring.  (Joel Franklin)
      </p>
      <p>
       A
       <em>
        method
       </em>
       in mathematics is a trick that is used more than once. (Ron Getoor)
      </p>
      <p>
       One should always generalize. [Man muss immer generalisieren].  (Carl Jacobi)
      </p>
      <p>
       A correct answer, ill gotten, does not beget full credit.  (Tom ONeil)
      </p>
      <p>
       The biggest matrix on the test will be 3 X 3 or 4 X 4.  This is a test, not real life. (Michael Sand)
      </p>
      <p>
       Mathematical maturity is when youre grown up enough to handle a 2 epsilon. (Michael Sharpe)
      </p>
     </div>

     04.30.09

    <div class="post-721 post hentry category-quotes category-urban-legends" id="post-721">
     <h3 class="storytitle">
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/2009/04/30/poetic-wisdom/" rel="bookmark">
       Poetic wisdom
      </a>
     </h3>
     <div class="meta">
      Filed under:
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/category/quotes/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Quotes">
       Quotes
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/category/urban-legends/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Urban legends">
       Urban legends
      </a>
        Travis @
     </div>
     <div class="storycontent">
      <p>
       For people with small horizons, every function is constant. (Oscar Bruno)
      </p>
      <p>
       All you need for differentiation is a strong right arm and a weak mind. (Ron Getoor)
      </p>
      <p>
       Obvious is in the the of the beholder. (Ron Getoor)
      </p>
      <p>
       Sometimes the Devil lurks in sets of measure zero. (Ron Getoor)
      </p>
      <p>
       There are two kinds of results in mathematics: those that are obvious and those that are false. (Ron Getoor)
      </p>
      <p>
       There are no deep theorems  only theorems that we have not understood very well. (Nicholas P. Goodman)
      </p>
      <p>
       The world is everywhere dense with idiots. (L. F. S.)
      </p>
      <p>
       Its only the false things that are nontrivial. (Michael Sharpe)
      </p>
      <p>
       Everything is trivial when you know the proof. (D. V. Widder)
      </p>
      <p class="note">
       My thanks to Jason Lee, who compiled many of these.
      </p>

    <div class="post-718 post hentry category-quotes category-urban-legends" id="post-718">
     <h3 class="storytitle">
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/2009/04/29/what-is-a-mathematician/" rel="bookmark">
       What is a mathematician?
      </a>
     </h3>
     <div class="meta">
      Filed under:
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/category/quotes/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Quotes">
       Quotes
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/category/urban-legends/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Urban legends">
       Urban legends
      </a>
        Travis @
     </div>
     <div class="storycontent">
      <p>
       The good Christian should be aware of mathematicians and all those who make empty prophecies.  The danger already exists that mathematicians have made a covenant with the devil to darken the spirit and confine man in the bonds of Hell.  (St. Augustine)
       <sup>
        1
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       A man whose mind has gone astray should study mathematics.  (Francis Bacon)
      </p>
      <p>
       A person who can, within a year, solve x
       <sup>
        2
       </sup>
        92y
       <sup>
        2
       </sup>
       = 1 is a mathematician. (Brahmagupta)
      </p>
      <p>
       A mathematician is a blind man in a dark room looking for a black cat which isnt there. (Charles R Darwin)
      </p>
      <p>
       A mathematician is a device for turning coffee into theorems. (P. Erdos)
      </p>
      <p>
       Mathematicians, like cows in the dark, all look alike to me. (Abraham Flexner)
      </p>
      <p>
       Mathematicians are like Frenchmen: whatever you say to them, they translate it into their own language, and forthwith it means something entirely different. (Johann Wolfgang von Goethe)
      </p>
      <p>
       Medicine makes people ill, mathematics make them sad and theology makes them sinful. (Martin Luther)
      </p>
      <p>
       He who can properly define and divide is to be considered a god. (Plato)
      </p>
      <p>
       God geometrizes. (Pluto)
      </p>
      <p class="note">
       Tai Melcher suggested including this quote.  Quoth she, Maybe if theyre scared of us, they wont laugh at us anymore. Or at least not as loudly.
      </p>

    <h2>
     04.28.09
    </h2>
    <div class="post-716 post hentry category-quotes category-urban-legends" id="post-716">
     <h3 class="storytitle">
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/2009/04/28/famously-amusing-quotation/" rel="bookmark">
       Famously amusing quotation
      </a>
     </h3>
     <div class="meta">
      Filed under:
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/category/quotes/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Quotes">
       Quotes
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://komplexify.com/epsilon/category/urban-legends/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Urban legends">
       Urban legends
      </a>
        Travis @
     </div>
     <div class="storycontent">
      <p>
       The four branches of arithmetic  ambition, distraction, uglification and derision. (Lewis Caroll,
       <cite>
        Alice in Wonderland
       </cite>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       As far as the laws of mathematics refer to reality, they are not certain, and as far as they are certain, they do not refer to reality. (Albert Einstein)
      </p>
      <p>
       If you cant explain what you are doing to a nine-year-old, then either you still dont understand it very well, or its not all that worthwile in the first place.  (Albert Einstein)
      </p>
      <p>
       Only two things are infinite: the universe and human stupidity, and Im not sure about the former.  (Albert Einstein)
      </p>
      <p>
       The two most common things in the Universe are hydrogen and stupidty.  (Harlan Ellison)
      </p>
      <p>
       Ive heard that the government wants to put a tax on the mathematically ignorant. Funny, I thought thats what the lottery was! (Gallagher)
      </p>
      <p>
       Old mathematicians never die; they just lose some of their functions.  (John C. George)
      </p>
      <p>
       I turn away with fear and horror from this lamentable plague of functions which do not have derivatives.  (Hermite, in a letter to Stieltjes)
      </p>
      <p>
       Mathematics is a game played according to certain simple rules with meaningless marks on paper. (David Hilbert)
      </p>
      <p>
       Physics is much too hard for physicists. (David Hilbert)
      </p>
      <p>
       A Ph.D. dissertation is a paper of the professor written under aggravating circumstances. (Adolf Hurwitz)
      </p>
      <p>
       Nature laughs at the difficulties of integration. (Pierre-Simon de Laplace)
      </p>
      <p>
       Everything good is either illegal, immoral, or equivalent to the Axiom of Choice. (Josh Laison)
      </p>
      <p>
       If I have seen farther than others, it is because I was standing on the shoulder of giants. (Isaac Newton)
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        If I have not seen as far as others, it is because giants were standing on my shoulders. (Hal Abelson)
       </p>
       <p>
        Mathematicians stand on each others shoulders. (Gauss)
       </p>
       <p>
        Computer scientists stand on each others feet. (Richard Hamming)
       </p>
       <p>
        Software engineers dig each others graves. (unknown)
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Mathematics is like checkers in being suitable for the young, not too difficult, amusing, and without peril to the state. (Plato)
      </p>
      <p>
       Mathematics consists of proving the most obvious thing in the least obvious way. (George Polya)
      </p>
      <p>
       As long as algebra is taught in school, there will be prayer in school.  (Cokie Roberts)
      </p>
      <p>
       Algebraic symbols are used when you do not know what you are talking about.  (Philippe Shnoebelen)
      </p>
      <p>
       Basic research is what I am doing when I dont known what I am doing. (Werner von Braun)
      </p>
      <p>
       In mathematics you dont understand things. You just get used to them.  (Johann von Neumann)
      </p>
      <p>
       Five out of four people have trouble with fractions.  (Steven Wright)
      </p>
     </div>
