       <span itemprop="description">
        <p>
         'The Monuments Men' was a critical disaster and George Clooney got very, very worried.
        </p>
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="EchoTopic">
       <span id="intelliTxt">
        <span class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/george-clooney">
           George Clooney
          </a>
          lost sleep and sent a flurry of early morning emails to Sony boss
          <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/amy-pascal">
           Amy Pascal
          </a>
          after critics ripped into his World War II movie
          <em>
           The Monuments Men
          </em>
          . In the latest Sony email leak, it is revealed that Clooney was worried about how Sony head honchos would react given the hugely anticipated movie appear to be taking a critical mauling.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="George Clooney" height="370" src="http://images.contactmusic.com/images/feature-images/the-monuments-men-george-clooney-636-370.jpg" style="vertical-align: top; border: 1px solid black; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="George Clooney" width="636"/>
          <span style="font-size: x-small;">
           George Clooney headed up the cast in
           <em>
            The Monuments Men
            <br/>
            <br/>
           </em>
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 1.538em;">
           On January 29, Clooney emailed Amy Pascal with the subject line "it's getting worse," and requesting "protection from all the reviews."
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          "Let's just make it a hit. I haven't slept in 30 hours. And it's 7am," Clooney added.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           More:
           <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/movie-review/the-monuments-men">
            read our full review of
            <em>
             The Monuments Men
            </em>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Pascal reacted confidently, telling the Hollywood star, "We will protect you by making money . that's the best revenge," according to the Daily Beast.
         </p>
         <p>
          Clooney replied: "I adore you Amy. You are literally the only person running a studio that loves film. I fear I've let you all down. Not my intention. I apologise. I've just lost touch ... Who knew?" said Clooney, "Sorry. I won't do it again."
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="The Monuments Men" height="370" src="http://images.contactmusic.com/images/feature-images/the-monuments-men-bm-gc-636-370.jpg" style="vertical-align: top; border: 1px solid black; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="The Monuments Men" width="636"/>
          <span style="font-size: x-small;">
           Bill Murray [L] co-starred in
           <em>
            The Monuments Men
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Featuring a glittering cast including Clooney himself, Bill Murray, Matt Damon and John Goodman,
          <em>
           The Monuments Men
          </em>
          told the true story of a World War II platoon tasked by FDR to go into Germany and rescue artistic masterpieces from Nazi thieves.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           More:
           <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/article/the-interview-sony-leaked-emails_4509891">
            Sony Leak: Sony terrified over "desperately unfunny"
            <em>
             The Interview
            </em>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          However, it failed to come together and production was marred by delays and re-edits.
         </p>
         <p>
          "For all its tremendous production values, George Clooney's The Monuments Men is a profoundly frustrating and unsatisfying film. Clooney simply can't settle on a tone for the story he is trying to tell," said Geoffrey McNab of the Independent.
         </p>
         <p>
          Nevertheless, the movie opened second on the box-office and made $155 million, more than doubling its production budget of $70 million.
         </p>
