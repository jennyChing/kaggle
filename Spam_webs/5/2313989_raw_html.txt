            New results from the Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer on the International Space Station
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div id="audata">
           September 18th, 2014
          </div>
          <div class="entry">
           <div class="post-thumbnail">
           </div>
           <p>
            The new results on energetic cosmic ray electrons and positrons are announced today. They are based on the first 41 billion events measured with the Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer (AMS) on the International Space Station (ISS).  These results provide a deeper understanding of the nature of high energy cosmic rays and shed more light on the dark matter existence.

          <div class="textwidget">
           <br/>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The
            <strong>
             Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer
            </strong>
            (AMS-02) is a state-of-the-art particle physics detector designed to operate as an external module on the International Space Station. It will use the unique environment of space to study the universe and its origin by searching for antimatter, dark matter while performing precision measurements of cosmic rays composition and flux. The AMS-02 observations will help answer fundamental questions, such as
            <strong>
             "What makes up the universe's invisible mass?"
            </strong>
            or
            <strong>
             "What happened to the primordial antimatter?"
            </strong>
            <br/>
            <br/>
           </p>
