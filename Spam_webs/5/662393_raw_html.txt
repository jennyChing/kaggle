    <h3>
     Featured: All Players Rated FIDE2700 and Above!
    </h3>
    <p>
     The Live Top List has been an unofficial
     <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chess_rating">
      chess rating
     </a>
     list that covered all players with live
ratings of 2700 and above in the
     <a href="http://www.fide.com">
      FIDE
     </a>
     <a href="http://www.fide.com/info/handbook?id=11&amp;view=category">
      rating system
     </a>
     . The official
     <a href="http://ratings.fide.com/top.phtml?list=men">
      FIDE list
     </a>
     is published 6 times each year, while this list has been updated and published as games and tournaments have been played, typically with daily updates during tournaments
featuring top players.
    </p>
    <p>
     In 2011 the work of keeping the site updated proved to require more than time and resources allowed for, and since I never managed to bring myself completely out of the loop, despite excellent help from Dambi, I've decided to stop providing live updates. The site lives on, though, currently as a live rating archive for the period from April 2008 to August 2011 - a total of 778 updates. More rating related content can be expected to be added to this site later, hopefully including both articles, statistics and more.
    </p>
    <p>
     For anyone wanting to stay current on live ratings, I recommend going to
     <a href="http://www.2700chess.com">
      2700chess.com
     </a>
     or visiting Dambi's
     <a href="http://chesspro.ru/guestnew/looknullmessage/?themeid=110&amp;id=2&amp;page=777#bp">
      ChessPro based forum
     </a>
     where ratings typically are updated once per day and often even more frequently. I think it's great that someone with more time on their hands have taken up the challenge of continuing what I started, and I wish all of them good luck!
    </p>

    <h3>
     The Live Top List Archive
    </h3>
    <p>
     The Live Top List has become the Live Top List Archive - for the time being. The site will not go away, and all the 778 lists published between April 2008 and August 2011 will be kept around as historical items. I also intend to improve the look-up mechanisms so that it becomes easier to browse and look at the lists and the players that entered the lists during the period updates were actively produced.
    </p>
    <p>
     I'm happy that so many found my site useful during the 3+ years I published live ratings. I'm also grateful for all the help I got from numerous people to stay up to date, ranging from normal chess fans to managers and elite players themselves. I do intend to continue to provide rating-related data on this site, so it might be worthwhile to check back at some later time. However, I expect to announce new content here through other, wide-read sources, so don't strain your mouse finger unnecessarily. Meanwhile I recommend going to www.2700chess.com for daily rating updates - they're doing a great job!
    </p>
