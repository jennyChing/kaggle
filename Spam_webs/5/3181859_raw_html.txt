              <h1 class="header_font bd_headings_text_shadow zero_color prk_break_word" id="blog_ttl">
               How To Measure The Quality Of A Nursing Home
              </h1>
              <div class="header_font" id="single_blog_meta">
               <div class="single_blog_meta_div" id="samba_single_date">
                <div class="navicon-clock-2 left_floated not_zero_color">
                </div>
                <div class="left_floated">
                 May 21, 2015
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="single_blog_meta_div">
                <div class="navicon-link not_zero_color left_floated">
                </div>

                  How To
                 </a>
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="single_blog_meta_div">

                 by                                                 Gary Wilson
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="single_blog_meta_div right_floated">

              <div class="flexslider boxed_shadow" data-color="#ad7234" id="single_slider">

              <div class="on_colored prk_no_composer prk_break_word" id="single_post_content">
               <div id="triberr_endorsement">
               </div>
               <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="How To Measure The Quality Of A Nursing Home" data-url="http://creativesolvibrations.com/how-to-measure-the-quality-of-a-nursing-home/">
               </div>
               <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
               </div>
               <p>
                There comes a point in life when the elderly may need a nursing home. Although, many families would love to take in their loved ones at their own home, it may not be the best option. Nursing homes are skilled in 24 hour care for the elderly. They have nurses, doctors, and medication that can help the patient much better than a relative. However, you dont want to send your loved one to any nursing home, which is why you must do your
                <a href="http://health.usnews.com/best-nursing-homes/area/ga?int=980d08" target="_blank">
                 research to find a quality nursing home
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Check the Ratio
                </strong>
                <br/>
                Selecting a quality nursing home is like selecting a daycare for your child in a way. The patient may not be able to verbalize and understand what is going on at the nursing home, which is why you must select properly. One important criterion to measure is the proportion of direct caregivers from temp agencies. Temporary employees will not be there long or know the details involved with your loved one. You also want to find a nursing home close to your residence or work, because visiting nursing home patients is correlated to a decrease in depression in residents.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Check With Friends/Family
                </strong>
                <br/>
                Speak with the residents at the nursing home or friends that you know have family there. The people that are directly involved in the facility will know best whether your family member should be there. They will also know what problems can arise and how the staff is able to fix the issue. You can also check with the state social service agency and agency on aging to determine the reports of nursing homes in regards to cleanliness and complaints.
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="http://i1.wp.com/creativesolvibrations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/nursing_home1.jpg">
                 <img alt="nursing_home1" class="alignleft wp-image-9662" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/creativesolvibrations.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/nursing_home1.jpg?resize=1022%2C681"/>
                </a>
                <br/>
                <strong>
                 Check With Staff/Residents
                </strong>
                <br/>
                Check with the staff and residents to see what activities they have during the day. You want to ensure they are stimulating learning and activities for your family member and not having them sit in their wheelchair all day long. Also, think about what is important in regards to your loved ones religious preferences, habits, and special dietary meals. Some nursing homes also have rehabilitation for their patients that may have suffered injuries or strokes. These
                <a href="http://www.agrhodes.org/our-homes/wesley-woods/" target="_blank">
                 rehabilitation homes
                </a>
                may be for short-term stays and not a long-term stay for your family members.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Check the Cost
                </strong>
                <br/>
                Although you dont want to sacrifice your family members quality of life, you must check on the cost of the facility as well to determine if it is in the budget. One thing people may not know is that Medicaid requires the depletion of most of the assets of the resident prior to taking over payments. Some homes will not accept Medicaid, so this must also be checked and researched. If money is an issue, it may be cost-efficient to share a room and save more money. But, if privacy is an issue and the loved one has lived alone for a while, it may be necessary to have a private room. These decisions are important in the care and security of your loved one.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Visit the Homes
                </strong>
                <br/>
                Now that you have performed these activities, it is time to visit the top nursing homes on your list and watch how the staff interacts with the residents in their home. This will be a very important part of the decision-making process. Make sure to visit during mealtime and watch how they help the residents eat. You want to have family members at a facility that will help them eat safely.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Pick Your Home
                </strong>
                <br/>
                Once you have done your research and visited the nursing homes of your choice, you should have the proper ammunition to select the nursing home that fits best with your loved one. Take your loved one up there to visit and see how they react to that environment and if it seems to be a good fit, enroll them there! Make sure to follow-up and see how their transition to the home goes.
               </p>
