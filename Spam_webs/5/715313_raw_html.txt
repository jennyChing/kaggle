                     </strong>

                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Why You Should:
                     </strong>

                      Why You Should:
                     </strong>
                     Writer-director Greg Araki always delivers fresh, honest  and usually shocking - films and this is no different, but then you also get to see Joseph Gordon-Levitt deliver a performance unlike any other in his career  that of abuse victim-turned-male prostitute Neil. Its a role that really set him apart as an incredible talent to watch.

                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Why You Should:
                     </strong>
                     It's a shocking, brutally honest look at drug running, as seen by a young boy firmly entrenched in a world filled with crack-peddling gangsters. Drama unfolds in a gratifyingly rich and complex way as the boy uses the chess lessons taught to him by his alcoholic father (played brilliantly by Samuel L. Jackson) to devise an escape plan.

                     <strong>
                      Why You Haven't Seen It:

                     <strong>
                      Why You Should:
                     </strong>

                     <strong>

                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Why You Should:
                     </strong>

                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Why You Should:
                     </strong>

                    </p>

                     <em>
                      Magnolia
                     </em>
                     .

It also stars Robert Downey Jr. In a film based on a Shakespeare play. How can anyone not want to see that?

                    <p>
                     <strong>

                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Why You Should:
                     </strong>
                     Forgetting that the film marks Thandie Newton's movie debut and also sees appearances by Nicole Kidman and Naomi Watts, the  story boasts complex characters, a low-key, humdrum realism and many memorable scenes.
