         Washi tape ornaments using acrylic shapes
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">

        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="7456481" data-link="http://washitapecrafts.com/2013/12/washi-tape-ornaments-using-acrylic-shapes/" data-summary="" data-title="Washi tape ornaments using acrylic shapes">
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Easy-washi-tape-Christmas-ornaments-using-plastic-shapes.jpg">
          <img alt="Easy washi tape Christmas ornaments using plastic shapes" class="size-full wp-image-1275 aligncenter" height="433" src="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Easy-washi-tape-Christmas-ornaments-using-plastic-shapes.jpg" title="Easy washi tape Christmas ornaments using plastic shapes" width="600"/>
         </a>
         I have a boatload of holiday washi tape that needed a home. I guess thats how I look at washi tape now . . . where does this particular roll belong? What can I make out of it? This time, it was ornaments  Ive never actually done that before. Which seems so counterintuitive, because washi tape definitely belongs on ornaments. This time I used Martha Stewart brand acrylic shapes to make the process really easy. These would make great ornaments, but also great tags for packages. Or hostess gifts! Heres how I made them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Gather These Supplies
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Martha Stewart acrylic shapes
         </li>
         <li>
          Washi tape  your choice of patterns
         </li>
         <li>
          Scissors or a craft knife
         </li>
         <li>
          Bakers twine
         </li>
         <li>
          White acrylic paint (optional)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_2864.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_2864" class="size-full wp-image-1272 aligncenter" height="597" src="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_2864.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         Heres the selection of washi tape that I used. How did I end up with so much holiday washi tape?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_2865.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_2865" class="size-full wp-image-1270 aligncenter" height="637" src="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_2865.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         Start at one edge and start taping. You can also start in the middle if you want things to be a bit more symmetrical. And you can do it horizontally or at an angle. Go wild and crazy with it!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_2866.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_2866" class="size-full wp-image-1273 aligncenter" height="516" src="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_2866.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         Smooth the tape out and then flip the ornaments over. Use scissors or a craft knife to trim the edges. I find a craft knife to work more easily than scissors.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_2867.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_2867" class="size-full wp-image-1271 aligncenter" height="518" src="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_2867.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         I finished my ornaments off by painting on the back with white acrylic paint, because I realized that they were a little transparent and I didnt like that. You dont have to do this part.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Easy-washi-tape-Christmas-ornaments-using-plastic-shapes.jpg">
          <img alt="Easy washi tape Christmas ornaments using plastic shapes" class="size-full wp-image-1275 aligncenter" height="433" src="http://washitapecrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Easy-washi-tape-Christmas-ornaments-using-plastic-shapes.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         Add some bakers twine and bingo  you are good to go. The whole process took just a few minutes. Happy Holidays!
        </p>
