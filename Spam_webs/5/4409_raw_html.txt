      <h1 itemprop="name">
       Angry Birds creator secures 25m euro loan from EU
      </h1>
      <h2>
       The European Investment Bank announced on Thursday that it has agreed a 25 million euro financing deal with Rovio to support the Finnish firms technological innovations in various entertainment sectors and mobile gaming. The move is part of a Europe-wide drive to support innovation by mid-sized companies.
      </h2>
      <ul class="some default ">
       <li class="facebook ">
        <div id="fb-root">
        </div>
        <div class="fb-like" data-action="recommend" data-font="arial" data-href="http://yle.fi/uutiset/7281819" data-layout="standard" data-send="false" data-show-faces="false">
        </div>
       </li>
      </ul>
     </div>
    </header>
    <div class="text">
     <figure class="full wRelatedContent vmedia" itemprop="image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Photograph">
      <a class="openoverlay" href="http://img.yle.fi/uutiset/kotimaa/article6612214.ece/ALTERNATES/w960/vappu%20vappupallo%20ilmapallo%20angry%20birds" title="">
       <img alt="Angry Birds -vappupalloja." data-imagesource="Image: Antti Laakso / Yle" itemprop="image" src="http://img.yle.fi/uutiset/kotimaa/article6612214.ece/ALTERNATES/w580/vappu%20vappupallo%20ilmapallo%20angry%20birds"/>
      </a>
      <figcaption>
       <span class="caption" itemprop="description">
       </span>
       <span class="source" itemprop="copyrightHolder">
        Image: Antti Laakso / Yle
       </span>
      </figcaption>
     </figure>
     <p>
      The creator of the globally popular Angry Birds game is to receive 25 million euros of funding from the European Investment Bank (EIB) to support further technical innovation.
     </p>
     <p>
      The loan is part of a new drive by the European Commission and the EIB to support job creation and growth through innovation by medium-sized companies.
     </p>
     <p>
      A statement from the EIB said the financing will be used specifically for the development of Rovios innovative platform for mobile games and other entertainment services.
     </p>
     <p>
      Jonathan Taylor, EIB Vice-President responsible for the Banks operations in Finland, underlined the importance of medium-sized firms. Mid-cap companies have a key role to play in competitiveness, job creation and growth in Europe. However, they often face difficulties accessing appropriate financial resources, he said.
     </p>
     <p>
      He added that its strong growth record and focus on innovation made Rovio an ideal candidate for EIB funding.
     </p>
     <p>
      Since the worldwide success of Rovios Angry Birds game, the company has expanded from employing 200 people at the beginning of 2012 to over 800 by the end of last year.
     </p>
     <p>
      The companys Angry Birds app quickly became the most downloaded game of all time, and spawned an ever-growing range of spin-off products. An animated feature film is due for release in July 2016.
     </p>
