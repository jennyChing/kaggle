     <div class="story-header-left twoCol">
      <h1 class="title" itemprop="name">
       Home builders to woo entry-level buyers: Real estate pro
      </h1>
      <div class="source" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <a href="/michelle-fox/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        Michelle Fox
       </a>
       |
       <span class="twitter-url">
        <a href="http://twitter.com/MFoxCNBC">
         @MFoxCNBC
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <time class="datestamp" datetime="2014-09-24T17:15:47-0400" itemprop="datePublished">
       Wednesday, 24 Sep 2014 |  5:15  PM ET
      </time>
      <span class="sourcelogo" id="sourcelogo-cnbccom" itemprop="sourceOrganization">
       CNBC.com
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="story-header-right">
      <div>
       <div class="share-count-container hide">
        <div class="share-total">
        </div>
        <div class="share-text">
         SHARES
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="gigyasharebar" id="social_bar_top">
       </div>
      </div>
      <a data-comments-label="Article" data-disqus-identifier="cnbcnewsstory_102030185" href="#">
      </a>
     </div>
    </div>
    <section class="cols2">
     <div class="unit col1">
      <article>
       <div class="story">
        <div class="content" id="article_body">
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="player embed-container cnbcvideo autoplay">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="player" data-src="http://player.cnbc.com/cnbc_global?byGuid=3000313395&amp;autoPlay=true&amp;endCardGuids=3000383239|3000383217|3000383353" frameborder="0" height="298" id="player_0_0" mozallowfullscreen="" msallowfullscreen="" oallowfullscreen="" scrolling="no" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="533">
            <p>
             First time home buyer reappearing?
            </p>
            <p>
             Bob Wetenhall, RBC Capital Markets, discusses the state of housing and what's behind the earnings miss of KB Home. Bradley Hunter, Metrostudy, provides insight.
            </p>
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            As home builders try to gain more business, more will be going after the entry-level buyer, real estate expert Bradley Hunter told CNBC Wednesday.
           </p>
           <p>
            "It's been hard to do up until now because the builders are saying 'the profit margins are so good in that move-up segment of market, I'm going to do move up. I'm not even going to mess with entry level,'" said Hunter, chief economist with Metrostudy, which provides real estate research and analysis.
           </p>
           <p>
            However, in order to increase housing starts, he thinks more builders will have to go to the "periphery" to be able to serve the needs of entry-level buyers.  Hunter told "
            <a href="/street-signs/" target="_blank">
             Street Signs
            </a>
            " those buyer's aren't necessarily purchasing a home for the first time, but are looking in the lower price range.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/09/24/10-urban-property-hotspots-to-watch.html" target="_blank">
             10 urban property hotspots to watch
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="15085" data-inline-quote-symbol="DHI" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/DHI" target="_blank">
             D.R. Horton
            </a>
            already addresses that segment of the market, he added, and
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="23462" data-inline-quote-symbol="LEN" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/LEN" target="_blank">
             Lennar
            </a>
            recently said it will build homes in the $175,000 to $200,000 price point.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="embed-container image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
           <img alt="Workers apply stucco at a housing development under construction in Jupiter, Fla." height="298" src="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2014/09/24/102030273-new-housing.530x298.jpg?v=1411591626" width="530"/>
           <meta content="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2014/09/24/102030273-new-housing.jpg?v=1411591626" itemprop="image"/>
           <meta content="width='530'" itemprop="width"/>
           <meta content="height='298'" itemprop="height"/>
           <meta content="2014-09-24T16:47:06-0400" itemprop="datePublished"/>
           <meta content="2014-09-24T16:45:01-0400" itemprop="dateCreated"/>
           <meta content="2014-09-24T16:47:08-0400" itemprop="dateModified"/>
           <meta content="2014-09-24T16:45:01-0400" itemprop="uploadDate"/>
           <meta content="2099-12-31" itemprop="expires"/>
           <!-- TODO: defaulting to 2099 until we get the expiry date from toolbelt -->
           <meta content="jpg" itemprop="encodingFormat"/>
           <meta content="Mark Elias | Bloomberg | Getty Images" itemprop="author"/>
           <meta content="CNBC" itemprop="publisher"/>
           <meta content="Workers apply stucco at a housing development under construction in Jupiter, Fla." itemprop="caption"/>
           <div class="attribution">
            Mark Elias | Bloomberg | Getty Images
           </div>
           <div class="caption">
            Workers apply stucco at a housing development under construction in Jupiter, Fla.
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Last week, Lennar, the second largest U.S. home builder,
            <asset_inline nid="102008777" target="_blank">
             reported a better-than-expected
            </asset_inline>
            47 percent jump in quarterly profits. Its average selling price rose 15 percent to $332,000 in the quarter ending Aug. 31.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/09/24/new-home-sales-power-higher-in-august-soar-by-18-percent.html" target="_blank">
             US home sales surge 18 percent in August
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="22547" data-inline-quote-symbol="KBH" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/KBH" target="_blank">
             KB Home
            </a>
            , however,
            <asset_inline nid="102028611" target="_blank">
             disappointed Wall Street
            </asset_inline>
            on Wednesday when it reported a profit of $28.4 million in its fiscal third quarter, or 28 cents a share. Analysts had expected earnings of 38 cents per share, according to Zacks Investment Research.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container last">
          <div class="player embed-container cnbcvideo">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="player" data-src="http://player.cnbc.com/cnbc_global?byGuid=3000312407&amp;endCardGuids=3000383239|3000383217|3000383353" frameborder="0" height="298" id="player_2_0" mozallowfullscreen="" msallowfullscreen="" oallowfullscreen="" scrolling="no" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="533">
            <p>
             Housing: Why West is the best
            </p>
            <p>
             What's behind the Western housing boom? Bradley Hunter, Metrostudy chief economist, discusses data from today's housing report and explains why the Western housing market is so hot.
            </p>
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            RBC Capital Markets analyst Robert Wetenhall believes the first-time homebuyer is making a gradual reappearance, and said that will be the theme heading into 2015.
           </p>
           <p>
            "We're not calling for a great recovery. It's going to be gradual. It's going to be choppy," Wetenhall said in an interview with"Street Signs."
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/09/17/home-builder-confidence-hits-highest-level-since-2005.html" target="_blank">
             Builder confidence at highest level since 2005
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            He said the "huge gains" in the average selling prices are going to flatline, and agreed with Hunter that builders will look to the entry-level buyer to increase earnings.
           </p>
           <p>
            "To get that incremental volume, you're going to see the builders lowering prices."
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Reuters and the Associated Press contributed to this report.
            </em>
           </p>
