         The Best Ten Books On China. For Business.
        </a>
       </h1>
       <span class="post-byline">
        By
        <a href="http://harrismoure.com/our-team/dan-harris/" rel="author external" title="Visit Dan Harriss website">
         Dan Harris
        </a>
        on
        <time class="post-date">
         December 22nd, 2009
        </time>
       </span>
       <span class="post-categories">
        Posted in
        <a href="http://www.chinalawblog.com/recommended-reading/" rel="category tag">
         Recommended Reading
        </a>
       </span>
      </header>
      <section class="post-content">
       <p>
        If you like this blog and have not yet joined up as a member of the
        <a href="http://www.linkedin.com/groups?home=&amp;gid=2533354&amp;trk=anet_ug_hm&amp;goback=.anh_2533354">
         China Law Blog Group
        </a>
        on Linkedin, you are really missing out.
        <br/>
        About two weeks ago, I asked the Group
        <a href="http://www.linkedin.com/groupAnswers?viewQuestionAndAnswers=&amp;gid=2533354&amp;discussionID=10822152&amp;goback=.anh_2533354">
         to list out
        </a>
        what they thought to be the best ten books on China business. Within about a week, Forbes Magazine asked me to write an article on the same thing. That article, entitled, 
        <a href="http://www.forbes.com/2009/12/21/china-business-global-economy-opinions-contributors-daniel-p-harris.html">
         Best 10 Books On China: Planning your first business trip to China? Heres a guide,
        </a>
         just came out today.
        <br/>
        So without further ado, heres my list (in the order in which I am suggesting they be read, not in order of quality):
        <br/>
        1.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00139VUS8?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00139VUS8">
         Lost on Planet China: The Strange and True Story of One Mans Attempt to Understand the Worlds Most Mystifying Nation or How He Became Comfortable Eating Live Squid
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00139VUS8" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by J. Maarten Troost.
        <br/>
        2.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001DDI8Z2?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001DDI8Z2">
         Chinese Lessons: Five Classmates and the Story of the New China
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B001DDI8Z2" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by John Pomfret.
        <br/>
        3.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0060855029?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0060855029">
         River Town: Two Years on the Yangtze (P.S.)
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0060855029" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        and
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000GCFD8Q?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B000GCFD8Q">
         Oracle Bones
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000GCFD8Q" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , both by Peter Hessler.
        <br/>
        4.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001B8NWGY?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001B8NWGY">
         Out of Maos Shadow: The Struggle for the Soul of a New China
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B001B8NWGY" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by Phillip P. Pan.
        <br/>
        5.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001NLL13U?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001NLL13U">
         Postcards from Tomorrow Square: Reports from China
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B001NLL13U" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by James Fallows.
        <br/>
        6.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0618919066?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0618919066">
         China Shakes the World: A Titans Rise and Troubled Future  and the Challenge for America
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0618919066" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by James Kynge.
        <br/>
        7.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0015DWMYS?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B0015DWMYS">
         The China Price
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B0015DWMYS" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by Alexandra Harney.
        <br/>
        8.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0060761407?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0060761407">
         Mr. China: A Memoir
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0060761407" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by Tim Clissold.
        <br/>
        9.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000FCKGZC?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B000FCKGZC">
         One Billion Customers: Lessons from the Front Lines of Doing Business in China
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000FCKGZC" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by James McGregor.
        <br/>
        10.
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0470821922?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0470821922">
         China CEO: Voices of Experience from 20 International Business Leaders
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0470821922" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        ; and/or
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1439228302?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=chinalawblogc-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1439228302">
         Where East Eats West: The Street-Smarts Guide to Business in China
        </a>
        <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=chinalawblogc-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1439228302" style="border:none !important;margin:0px !important" width="1"/>
        , by Sam Goodman.
        <br/>
        If you want my explanation of the above, please go
        <a href="http://www.forbes.com/2009/12/21/china-business-global-economy-opinions-contributors-daniel-p-harris.html">
         here
        </a>
        for the Forbes article. And if you disagree (or agree) with me, please let me (and everyone else) know by commenting on this blog post or by adding to the ongoing discussion on
        <a href="http://www.linkedin.com/groups?home=&amp;gid=2533354&amp;trk=anet_ug_hm&amp;goback=.anh_2533354">
         our Linkedin Group
        </a>
        .
       </p>

            <p>
             The above list seems okay to me, though I would suggest at least one book written by a professional historian.
             <br/>
             Maurice Meisners book *Maos China and After* (3rd edition) is the single best survey of P.R.C history available. Though not a business book, per se, it still has much to offer those hoping to better understand Chinas recent past. Meisners book is a true classic that is frequently assigned to undergraduate students of Chinese history in many American universities. Its a useful alternative to other surveys (i.e., Jonathan Spences *The Search for Modern China*) that devote most of their text to a discussion of pre-20th century China. Meisners book provides the kind of historical context that the books in the list above do not.
             <br/>
             As far as the list goes, I also highly recommend the books by Philip Pan, James Kynge, and Peter Hessler.
