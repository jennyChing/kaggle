       <div class="intro">
        <h2>
         The Perfect Storm
        </h2>
        <p>
         Extended meth use causes permanent damage to a user's body, including their teeth. It creates a "perfect storm" for dental disease and tooth decay. Explore the causes below to see how a meth habit can destroy a user's mouth.
        </p>
        <p class="cta">
         Turn switches
         <strong>
          on
         </strong>
         or
         <strong>
          off
         </strong>
         to see details
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="toggle-off">
          Lack of Saliva
         </li>
         <li class="toggle-off">
          Decreased Blood Flow
         </li>
         <li class="toggle-off">
          Tooth Grinding or Clenching
         </li>
         <li class="toggle-off">
          Horrible Hygiene
         </li>
         <li class="toggle-off">
          Poor Diet
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
       <div class="teeth">
        <div class="topjaw">
         <img alt="Decay Illustration" height="413" src="/img/interactive-facts/perfect-storm/decay-0.jpg" width="413"/>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="detail" id="saliva" style="display:none;">
        <p>
         Saliva is needed to neutralize harsh acids and reduce bacteria in the mouth. Meth dries up the salivary glands, letting the acids and bacteria build up and rot teeth and gums.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="detail" id="bloodflow" style="display:none;">
        <p>
         Meth causes blood vessels to shrink, reducing the supply of blood to the teeth and gums. This can lead to infections and gum disease.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="detail" id="grinding" style="display:none;">
        <p>
         Meth users often clench and grind their teeth causing cracked teeth and sores in the mouth.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="detail" id="hygene" style="display:none;">
        <p>
         Consumed by their addiction, Meth users pay little to no attention to anything else, including oral hygiene. They stop brushing their teeth and let bacteria build up in their mouths.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="detail" id="poordiet" style="display:none;">
        <p>
         A user's poor nutrition is a key factor in rotting teeth. Meth also dehydrates users so they tend to crave a lot sugary drinks like soda. All that sugar makes the problem worse.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>

     <div id="termView">
      <h2 class="slidetitle">
       <span class="auralText">
        Terms
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul class="term-list">
       <li class="term1">
        <a href="javascript:void(0)">
         Bruxism
        </a>
       </li>
       <li class="term2">
        <a href="javascript:void(0)">
         Gingivitis
        </a>
       </li>
       <li class="term3">
        <a href="javascript:void(0)">
         Trench Mouth
        </a>
       </li>
       <li class="term4">
        <a href="javascript:void(0)">
         Xerostomia
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <ul id="def-wrap">
       <li class="definition" id="term1">
        <p>
         Clenching or grinding of the teeth. Methamphetamine can cause users to clench and grind their teeth resulting in tooth enamel erosion and cracked teeth.
         <a class="button" href="/answers/what-does-meth-do-to-your-body.html#Cindys-Story">
          Cindy's Story
         </a>
        </p>
       </li>
       <li class="definition" id="term2">
        <p>
         A form of periodontal disease that destroys tissues that support the teeth, including the gums, ligaments, and the tooth sockets. Meth users often suffer from gingivitis due to poor oral hygiene, diet, and the drugs effect on the immune system.
        </p>
       </li>
       <li class="definition" id="term3">
        <p>
         A severe form of gingivitis, with painful swelling and ulcers in the gums. Symptoms include bad breath and crater-like ulcers between the teeth.  Meth users can develop this due to poor oral health, hygiene, and diet.
        </p>
        <!-- Removed img src="/img/hard-facts/terms/terms-Q17-A6-trenchmouth.png" width="167" height="193" class="termimg"-->
       </li>
       <li class="definition" id="term4">
        <p>
         Also known as dry mouth, this happens when Meth restricts blood flow in the gums and lessens the amount of saliva. Without the protection of saliva, cavities and chronic bad breath can develop.
        </p>
       </li>
      </ul>
