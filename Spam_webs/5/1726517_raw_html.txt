        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         $5 Paleo: Liver, Sausage &amp; Pepper Scramble
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-12-12T01:17:42+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 12, 2013
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://grokgrub.com/author/rachelmarion/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Grok Grub
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://grokgrub.com/2013/12/12/5-paleo-liver-sausage-pepper-scramble/#comments">
           2 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a class="YUMMLY-YUM-BUTTON" href="//yummly.com" rel="nofollow">
         </a>
         <script src="https://www.yummly.com/js/widget.js?wordpress">
         </script>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" src="http://grokgrub.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Five-Dollar-Paleo-Liver-Sausage-Peppers-by-GrokGrub.com_.jpg"/>
         <br/>
         Are you an adventurous eater? Do you chow down on liver no sweat, or would you welcome some sausage to help the medicine go down as Mary Poppins said? This is a delicious way to get your liver in thats tasty, quick, and best of all costs only $5 per serving! If I can put a meal on the table for that price per person I count it quite the win, especially in this topsy-turvy world in which real food is pricier than processed frankenfood.
        </p>
        <p>
         I bought my ingredients from Whole Foodswhich is the most expensive grocery store in my hoodto make sure this still clocks in at $5/serving (even at the priciest market). I always buy liver when Im feeling strapped for cash, because at $3.99 or so a pound its a bargain protein choice. I also found a gluten-free, sugar-free sausage to bring the warm savoriness of this dinner to the next level, and rounded it out with thinly-sliced bell peppers and onions.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is simple food. Real food. The kind of Paleo thats sustainable for physiological and financial health. Comment below if youre a fan, and Ill post some more ideas.
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://grokgrub.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         <br/>
        </p>
        <center>
         <script src="http://ib.adnxs.com/ttj?id=4889501&amp;referrer=grokgrub.com" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
        </center>
        <br/>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          $5 Paleo: Liver, Sausage &amp; Pepper Scramble
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://grokgrub.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Five-Dollar-Paleo-Liver-Sausage-Peppers-by-GrokGrub.com-Square1.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://grokgrub.com/easyrecipe-print/2649-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTimes">
          <div class="ERSTime">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Prep time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT10M" itemprop="prepTime">
             10 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTime ERSTimeRight">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Cook time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT20M" itemprop="cookTime">
             20 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTime ERSTimeRight">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Total time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT30M" itemprop="totalTime">
             30 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClearLeft">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          This &lt;strong&gt;$5 Paleo&lt;/strong&gt; dish combines nutrient-rich chicken liver with savory sausage and sweet green pepper with onions.
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Grok Grub
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            2
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 lb chicken liver
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 sausages (gluten-free, sugar-free)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 green bell pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 onion
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Salt (optional)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Pepper (optional)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Heat a large, heavy skillet over medium heat.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Slice the sausage into  inch pieces, then add to the skillet. Allow the sausage's fat to render for a few minutes while you slice the onion.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Thinly slice the onion, add to the sausage.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Wash and thinly slice the pepper and add to the onion and sausage.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Drain the liver for a few moments, then add to the skillet.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cook for approximately 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the liver has browned and the vegetables are soft.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste, then spoon into bowls.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Serve hot.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           If you like it spicy, sprinkle with red pepper flakes. If you have calf liver. . .experiment with that, instead.
          </div>
