        <h2 class="sectiontitle">
         The Style
        </h2>
        <div class="imgright" style="width: 153px;">
         <div class="imgmain">
          <a href="/img/2s4ps6d55rdvzh5x/">
           <img alt="----" height="216" src="http://img.wikinut.com/img/2s4ps6d55rdvzh5x/jpeg/180x300/.jpeg" title="----" width="149"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="imgtitle">
          <a href="/img/2s4ps6d55rdvzh5x/">
           ----
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         Beginning in the
         <a href="http://guides.wikinut.com/RastafarI-Part-1/3l9muvxm/" title="RastafarI - Part 1">
          1930s,
         </a>
         formulised in the 40s and 50s, established in the 60s,
         <a href="http://guides.wikinut.com/Rastafari-Part-II/34or53dj/" title="Rastafari - Part II">
          RastafarI
         </a>
         became
         <i>
          popular
         </i>
         in the 1970s.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Many Jamaicans and others, flirted with Rasta; growing their hair, eating ital using ganja as a sacrament, attending Niyibinghis or Twelve Tribe functions,  reading Scripture and trying to live an
         <i>
          upful
         </i>
          life.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Many restrictions fell away, and although it took until the mid 1980s for a case to be decided successfully that a dread locked child could not be denied entrance to High School,  many schools did not discriminate.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         By the 1980s Rasta was in the professions, there were however, only three lawyers and two doctors who sported dread locks,  and during the 1983 - 1989
         <a href="http://guides.wikinut.com/Jamaica-s-Lumpen/1wbyfs-_/" lumpen="" s="" title="Jamaica">
          One Party Parliament
         </a>
         the first (and still only) Rasta Senator was appointed.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Although there were again attempt to codify, to set up some kind of school, this was again a failure as the newer Rasta were more followers of Bob Marley than of
         <a href="http://guides.wikinut.com/RastafarI-Part-1/3l9muvxm/" title="RastafarI - Part 1">
          RastafarI
         </a>
         itself.
        </p>
        <a id="section-1" name="Bob-Marley">
        </a>
        <h2 class="sectiontitle">
         Bob Marley
        </h2>
        <div class="imgtop" style="width: 301px;">
         <div class="imgmain">
          <a href="/img/1h41jroqg7z1jp77/">
           <img alt="-----" height="297" src="http://img.wikinut.com/img/1h41jroqg7z1jp77/jpeg/700x1000/.jpeg" title="-----" width="297"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="imgtitle">
          <a href="/img/1h41jroqg7z1jp77/">
           -----
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         <br/>
         Bob was a Rasta, he was not an elder, he was not a spokesman, he was a member.  Although he did propound basic Niyibingi doctrine, and was often seen at 12 Tribe functions, there were many elders who disapproved of some of what he did.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         This disapproval was one of the 'death knells for Rasta.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         When Bob died in 1981 people who had been inspired by him desired some kind of official RastafarI to instruct them.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Elders who arrived from Jamaica found a
         <i>
          'Bob Marley Fan Club
         </i>
         , people more interested in Bob than the Doctrine.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         The elders who had usually dismissed Bob were themselves dismissed. The Doctrine they expounded was replaced by a cut and paste from Bobs songs taking the place of Bible study and lifestyle.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         During this period the
         <b>
          Rent-A-Dread
         </b>
         Industry arose.
        </p>
        <a id="section-2" name="Rent-A-Dread">
        </a>
        <h2 class="sectiontitle">
         Rent-A-Dread
        </h2>
        <div class="imgtop" style="width: 594px;">
         <div class="imgmain">
          <a href="/img/3j1dnd07d94jg7l9/">
           <img alt="-----" height="456" src="http://img.wikinut.com/img/3j1dnd07d94jg7l9/jpeg/700x1000/.jpeg" title="-----" width="590"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="imgtitle">
          <a href="/img/3j1dnd07d94jg7l9/">
           -----
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         As many tourists came to Sunsplash, a local
         <a href="http://travel.wikinut.com/Why-The-Music-Is-So-Loud/13hcrvep/" title="Why The Music Is So Loud">
          music
         </a>
         festival, for sun, sea and sex, the desire was to get a bona fide Rasta as a gigolo.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Many beach boys began to carefully grow their hair so as to portray Rasta and get the benefits of an all-expense paid week or two in Montego Bay.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         As the Sunsplash venue would be walled and booths inside would sell food and craft, it was simplicity for five male prostitutes to band together, rent a booth, buy a bunch of cheap souvenirs and festoon the structure with Red/Gold/Green banners and  make themselves available.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         The white women, from America, England, etc. would buy this 
         <i>
          forbidden fruit
         </i>
         .   Money didnt precisely change hands.  The woman would feed and dress and gift her Dread, and he would enjoy the benefits of her hotel room, her room service, all the activities available.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         The woman would not understand that this man was not in love with her, he was there for free sex and whatever else she could supply; some ambitious Dreads saw a VISA so marriage was on the table.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Most Rentas lived with their common law wives and children in a imitation of an upful life;  and only stumbled around  Sunsplash season.   Others became fixtures in the area so were available all year long.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         This style moved to other islands which had Beach Boy Industries, so that it was possible to get an Antigua dread or a St.Lucian Renta.
        </p>
        <a id="section-3" name="Becoming-Rasta">
        </a>
        <h2 class="sectiontitle">
         Becoming Rasta
        </h2>
        <div class="imgright" style="width: 184px;">
         <div class="imgmain">
          <a href="/img/25faerk5wtlmms1t/">
           <img alt="----" height="248" src="http://img.wikinut.com/img/25faerk5wtlmms1t/jpeg/180x300/.jpeg" title="----" width="180"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="imgtitle">
          <a href="/img/25faerk5wtlmms1t/">
           ----
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         In other parts of the world, many people, white as well as black, have adopted dread locks as a hairstyle without any comprehension of where it comes from, (Numbers 6:6) or what it means.  To them its  just a style, and they can grow and cut their hair without concern.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Many Rasta have raised their children without much understanding.  Children with long uncut dread locks going into KFC, buying and eating salt and grapes and meat, who have never opened a Bible.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Many children of Rasta parents have trimmed and dress as immodestly as anyone else, and might, when older, join some
         <a href="http://writing.wikinut.com/Perhaps-Not-the-Right-Church/zuf2qvk7/" title="Perhaps Not the Right Church">
          church
         </a>
         , for to them Rasta has no depth because their parents never instructed them, nor, perhaps, were their parents instructed.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         By the year 2000 there were many people with the hairstyle but few with the lifestyle.  Those who did try to live upful. Some were grounded in the Old Testament, others copied what their parents did or do, or tried to live according to what they think is right.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Many just wear a hair style theyll cut when it bores them, and have no more connection to the Doctrine than one wearing a costume to a Halloween party has to the character they portray.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </p>
