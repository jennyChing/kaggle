           <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
            <a href="http://corazonleon.blogspot.com/2010/08/new-medieval-typography-made-in-honor.html">
             A NEW MEDIEVAL TYPOGRAPHY MADE IN HONOR TO THE MC ANNIVERSAY OF THE KINGDOM OF LEON
            </a>
           </h3>
           <h2 class="date-header">
            18 de agosto de 2010
           </h2>
           <div class="post-header-line-1">
           </div>
           <div class="post-body entry-content">
            <p>
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             The kingdom of Leon was  the most important realm in the High Middle Ages in Spain, and was born in the year 910 AD when the Asturians kings moved their capital city from Oviedo to Leon.
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             As this year is the MC anniversary of the kingdom, I have created a new medieval typography based on the documents of the royal chancery of Fernando II and Alfonso IX of Leon. In fact, I used the document number 1067 from the Leon's Cathedral Archive. It's a privilege granted by Fernando II in 1186 to all the clergy of the capital city of Leon.
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_rcELo74Bj68/TGzmiyrZMJI/AAAAAAAAB0k/mEheL1G-gxg/s400/IMG_5711.1280.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
              <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_rcELo74Bj68/TGzmiyrZMJI/AAAAAAAAB0k/mEheL1G-gxg/s320/IMG_5711.1280.jpg" width="213"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             It was prepared by the Chancellor  Petri Vele, who was also responsible for the royal chancery in the early years of the reign of Alfonso IX. That is, almost certainly he was also responsible for formulating the decree of the famous Cortes of 1188, considered to be the birth of the parliamentary system.
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_rcELo74Bj68/TGv8wRqjz7I/AAAAAAAAB0E/ngTkeg-92zI/s1600/comparaci%C3%B3n+antigua+moderna.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
              <img border="0" height="80" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_rcELo74Bj68/TGv8wRqjz7I/AAAAAAAAB0E/ngTkeg-92zI/s400/comparaci%C3%B3n+antigua+moderna.jpg" width="400"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: center;">
             <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: x-small;">
              <i>
               An original line of the document 1065 (above), and the same line written with the new typography (below)
              </i>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             This type of writting isa kind of lateCaroline minuscule, bordering on early Gothic script. I gave it the name Leonesa Cancilleresca (Leonese Chancery), and I made two differents versions: the Leonesa Cancilleresca, that is very faithful to the original medieval writting, and the Leonesa Cancilleresca Modern, with a more modern form (smaller capitals and letters like l, b...)
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             You can download them free ofcharge here:
             <br/>
             <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/ricardochao02/LeonesaCancilleresca.ttf?attredirects=0">
              Leonesa Cancilleresca
             </a>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/ricardochao02/LeonesaCancillerescaModern.ttf?attredirects=0">
              Leonesa Cancillereca Modern
             </a>
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: justify;">
             <br/>
            </div>

             You can read a longer post about the creation of this typography
             <a href="http://corazonleon.blogspot.com/2010/08/leonesa-cancilleresca-una-nueva.html">
              here
             </a>
             (in Spanish)
