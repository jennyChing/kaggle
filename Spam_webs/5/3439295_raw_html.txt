         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lifeinabreakdown.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Snow-Boots.jpg">
           <img alt="Snow Boots" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11204" height="584" src="http://www.lifeinabreakdown.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Snow-Boots-700x700.jpg" width="584"/>
          </a>
          The weather is increasingly getting cold and wet and as much as I hate to admit it even to myself it looks like summer is well behind us and winter is clawing its way into our lives. The past few years have brought for at least some of the winter quite a large amount of snow so what better way to get ready for what could be to come this year than to make sure your wardrobe contains
          <a href="http://www.brantano.co.uk/snow-boots" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           a pair of snow boots.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Ive already started trying to hunt down my favourite pair and these just happen to be come that have made it to my short list.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Hi Tec New Moon 200 Snowboot 27 (on sale for 16)
          </strong>
          <br/>
          These are not only the most colourful in my run down but also the cheapest coming in at just 16 right now in the sale so I honesty cannot think of a reason to not like these ones as they are after all even a made by a decent brand.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Hi Tec St Moritz 200 Snowboot Animal Print  80
          </strong>
          <br/>
          For me I think this pair could be slightly leading the race, they look not only warm and comfy but like they offer a good grip which is such a necessity to this unsteady walker.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Hi Tec St Moritz Classic Snowboot  95
          </strong>
          <br/>
          I love the colour of this pair  the light brown just seems like it would very graceful on a white crisp winters morning while the snow or frost crunches beneath your feet.
         </p>
         <p>
