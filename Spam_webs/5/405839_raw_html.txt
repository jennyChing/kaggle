  <meta content="
I was recently re-invited to Table Verte, a French restaurant that serves 
only vegetarian dishes.I am definitely a carnivore and, most of the meals I 
had when I was a kid, growing up in France, had a dish with either a meat, 
fish or poultry. My wife Jodi, loves burgers and filet mignon, but have an 
inclination to order vegetarian dishes. It is with her, when we were 
dating, that I went to my first vegetarian restaurant: Zen Palate, at their 
location in Union Square that since closed. Then I went to others, like 
Gobo or Angelica kitchen, the latter being the worst vegetarian meal I ever 
had, the food being bland and mushy. I discovered Table Verte beginning of 
the year, and it made me realize how we easily get stuck with labels. Think 
about it: in Italian restaurant, we may order pasta with a tomato and basil 
sauce. We do not call that vegetarian. A cheese pizza is not called 
vegetarian. A ratatouille is not called vegetarian. etc. etc. I could go 
forever with dishes that we love but never think about associating them 
with being vegetarian. Execute Chef Ken Larsen (left) &amp; Sous Chef Matt 
Roth So, what makes Table Verte different from other vegetarian 
restaurants? Over there, Ken Larsen, the Executive Chef (who lived and 
worked in France) crafted a menu where he did not try to replace the meat 
or fish by other soy based proteins such as seitan or tofu. In the years he 
worked in France, he understood the importance of the garnish on the plate. 
In France, everything has to taste good: the garnish included. So, he 
decided to eliminate the meat and make the garnish the star of the dish. He 
also does not try to recreate a specific dish, but rather be inspired by it 
and make his own creation.
The difference between my last visit and today was, besides new dishes, 
that they replaced the a la carte formula by a series of prix-fixe menus. I 
admit that I prefer when both a la carte and prix-fixe are available, as we 
sometimes just want to have one dish each.
So, here we are at Table Verte (the green table), in the dining room, where 
all tables are...green.

We decided to share some dishes and started with appetizers. We started off 
with roasted Brussels sprouts with balsamic glazed strawberries.
I rediscovered Brussels sprouts few years ago, after eating a couple in a 
restaurant, regretting not to have more on my plate. Yes, when I was a kid, 
I hated it, but it might have been the way my Mom cooked them (Sorry Mom!) 
or maybe my palate evolved...What was interesting with this dish was the 
pairing with strawberries, not a sweet and salty combination because 
strawberries are not that sweet, but rather adding a different texture and 
maybe a delicate sour taste that made this dish successful.
The second dish was beets with a horseradish sherbet:

Chef Larsen thought about this dish after a trip in France back in August 
where he tried some crepes with savory flavored sherbets. Although it was 
very creative, it was my least favorite dish, the sherbet being a bit too 
icy and not having that much taste.
Then, we got my favorite appetizer: truffled mushroom duxelle.
Chef Larsen wanted to add a spread on the menu, similar to a pt that is a 
traditional appetizer in France. The presentation was identical, with 
cornichons and mustard, but, instead of bread, he made a pte sable that 
is a rich crumbly crust.
The way you eat it is by putting a some duxelle on the pte sable, with or 
without mustard.

I have to say that I loved that dish. When it came to the table, we could 
smell the truffle, and the taste of the mushrooms was divine.

Then, for the entres, we picked the eggplant lasagna:

It was made of goat cheese, Swiss cheese, spice roasted eggplant, harissa 
chickpea ragu and sauted spinach. The pasta was cooked al dente. Jodi 
loved it to say the least! I liked it, especially because there was lost of 
cheese and was expecting a little kick from the harissa, but it was not the 
case.
The second entre was Olive Polenta, made with an onion confit 
(fantastic!), roasted peppers, artichokes and cippolini onions with parsley 
caper sauce.
The presentation was sensational and it was as if the delicious olive 
polenta was like a piece of meat, soft, but not too much, perfectly paired 
with the onions that gave some sweetness to the dish and the haricots verts 
some crunch. These two entres are the perfect example of dishes that do 
not need meat or fish and left us satisfied. But of course not enough to 
skip dessert! We ordered the Banana Brle made of French pastry cream, 
sweet cookies, with a caramelized sugar coating:
If you like banana, you cannot miss this dessert that is between the banana 
pudding and the creme brle.
It was creamy and not too sweet; so good that we finished it!
The second dessert was the tarte au chocolat:
I had that dessert last time, but it slightly changed: the crust was made 
with raisins this time and you could definitely taste it.
The chocolate part was rich, dense with a strong chocolate taste and if you 
wonder how they make it, I know that they replace the butter with avocado 
that is a perfect substitute and a vegan dessert.
I had again a nice dinner at Table Verte, a restaurant that more carnivores 
should know!
Enjoy (I did)!

          <a class="u-url" href="/post/ustwanttoeat.com/2013/11/best-french-vegetarian-nyc-table-verte.html" rel="bookmark">
           Revisit: Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="meta-below-title">
          <div class="entry-dateline">
           <time class="dt-published published entry-date" datetime="2013-11-20" pubdate="">
            <a class="entry-dateline-link" href="/post/ustwanttoeat.com/2013/11/best-french-vegetarian-nyc-table-verte.html">
             November 20, 2013
            </a>
           </time>
           <time class="dt-updated updated" datetime="2014-01-15">
           </time>

          <div class="entry-byline">
           <span class="entry-author vcard">
            <a class="p-author author url fn entry-byline-link" href="/post/?author=53cc112ee4b09ce5b3381561" rel="author">
             jean-philippe gerbi

        <div class="entry-content e-content">
         <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" id="item-53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b5b">
          <div class="row sqs-row">
           <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-53ebb666e4b0aba5f6f33d8b">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b5c/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="320" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b5d/1407956524791/1000w/" title="Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="237"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               I was recently re-invited to Table Verte, a French restaurant that serves only vegetarian dishes.I am definitely a carnivore and, most of the meals I had when I was a kid, growing up in France, had a dish with either a meat, fish or poultry. My wife Jodi, loves burgers and filet mignon, but have an inclination to order vegetarian dishes. It is with her, when we were dating, that I went to my first vegetarian restaurant: Zen Palate, at their location in Union Square that since closed. Then I went to others, like Gobo or Angelica kitchen, the latter being the worst vegetarian meal I ever had, the food being bland and mushy. I discovered Table Verte beginning of the year, and it made me realize how we easily get stuck with labels. Think about it: in Italian restaurant, we may order pasta with a tomato and basil sauce. We do not call that vegetarian. A cheese pizza is not called vegetarian. A ratatouille is not called vegetarian. etc. etc. I could go forever with dishes that we love but never think about associating them with being vegetarian.
              </div>
              <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
               <tbody>
                <tr>
                 <td style="text-align: center;">
                  <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b5e/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                   <img alt="image of Execute Chef Ken Larsen (left) &amp; Sous Chef Matt Roth from Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="300" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b5f/1407956524791/1000w/" title="Execute Chef Ken Larsen (left) &amp; Sous Chef Matt Roth from Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="400"/>
                  </a>
                 </td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                 <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                  Execute Chef Ken Larsen (left) &amp; Sous Chef Matt Roth
                 </td>
                </tr>
               </tbody>
              </table>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               So, what makes Table Verte different from other vegetarian restaurants? Over there, Ken Larsen, the Executive Chef (who lived and worked in France) crafted a menu where he did not try to replace the meat or fish by other soy based proteins such as seitan or tofu. In the years he worked in France, he understood the importance of the garnish on the plate. In France, everything has to taste good: the garnish included. So, he decided to eliminate the meat and make the garnish the star of the dish. He also does not try to recreate a specific dish, but rather be inspired by it and make his own creation.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               The difference between my last visit and today was, besides new dishes, that they replaced the a la carte formula by a series of prix-fixe menus. I admit that I prefer when both a la carte and prix-fixe are available, as we sometimes just want to have one dish each.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b60/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="300" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b61/1407956524791/1000w/" title="Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="400"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              So, here we are at Table Verte (the green table), in the dining room, where all tables are...green.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               We decided to share some dishes and started with appetizers. We started off with roasted Brussels sprouts with balsamic glazed strawberries.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b62/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of roasted Brussels sprouts at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b63/1407956524791/1000w/" title="roasted Brussels sprouts at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               I rediscovered Brussels sprouts few years ago, after eating a couple in a restaurant, regretting not to have more on my plate. Yes, when I was a kid, I hated it, but it might have been the way my Mom cooked them (Sorry Mom!) or maybe my palate evolved...What was interesting with this dish was the pairing with strawberries, not a sweet and salty combination because strawberries are not that sweet, but rather adding a different texture and maybe a delicate sour taste that made this dish successful.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               The second dish was beets with a horseradish sherbet:
               <br/>
               <a name="more">
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b64/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of beets with horseradish sherbet at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b65/1407956524791/1000w/" title="beets with horseradish sherbet at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               Chef Larsen thought about this dish after a trip in France back in August where he tried some crepes with savory flavored sherbets. Although it was very creative, it was my least favorite dish, the sherbet being a bit too icy and not having that much taste.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               Then, we got my favorite appetizer: truffled mushroom duxelle.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b66/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of truffled mushroom duxelle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b67/1407956524791/1000w/" title="truffled mushroom duxelle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               Chef Larsen wanted to add a spread on the menu, similar to a pt that is a traditional appetizer in France. The presentation was identical, with cornichons and mustard, but, instead of bread, he made a pte sable that is a rich crumbly crust.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b68/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of pate sablee and truffled mushroom duxelle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b69/1407956524791/1000w/" title="pate sablee and truffled mushroom duxelle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              The way you eat it is by putting a some duxelle on the pte sable, with or without mustard.
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b6a/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of pate sablee and truffled mushroom duxelle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b6b/1407956524791/1000w/" title="pate sablee and truffled mushroom duxelle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              I have to say that I loved that dish. When it came to the table, we could smell the truffle, and the taste of the mushrooms was divine.
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b6c/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of pate sablee and truffled mushroom duxelle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b6d/1407956524791/1000w/" title="pate sablee and truffled mushroom duxelle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              Then, for the entres, we picked the eggplant lasagna:
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b6e/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of eggplant lasagna at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b6f/1407956524791/1000w/" title="eggplant lasagna at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               It was made of goat cheese, Swiss cheese, spice roasted eggplant, harissa chickpea ragu and sauted spinach. The pasta was cooked al dente. Jodi loved it to say the least! I liked it, especially because there was lost of cheese and was expecting a little kick from the harissa, but it was not the case.
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               The second entre was Olive Polenta, made with an onion confit (fantastic!), roasted peppers, artichokes and cippolini onions with parsley caper sauce.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b70/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of olive polenta at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b71/1407956524791/1000w/" title="olive polenta at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               The presentation was sensational and it was as if the delicious olive polenta was like a piece of meat, soft, but not too much, perfectly paired with the onions that gave some sweetness to the dish and the haricots verts some crunch.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               These two entres are the perfect example of dishes that do not need meat or fish and left us satisfied. But of course not enough to skip dessert! We ordered the Banana Brle made of French pastry cream, sweet cookies, with a caramelized sugar coating:
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b72/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of banana creme brle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b73/1407956524791/1000w/" title="banana creme brle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               If you like banana, you cannot miss this dessert that is between the banana pudding and the creme brle.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b74/1407956524791/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of banana creme brle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b75/1407956524792/1000w/" title="banana creme brle at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               It was creamy and not too sweet; so good that we finished it!
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               The second dessert was the tarte au chocolat:
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b76/1407956524792/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of tarte au chocolat at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b77/1407956524792/1000w/" title="tarte au chocolat at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               I had that dessert last time, but it slightly changed: the crust was made with raisins this time and you could definitely taste it.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b78/1407956524792/1000w/" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="image of tarte au chocolat at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" border="0" height="426" src="https://static.squarespace.com/static/53cc1616e4b0f4361f8ace24/53ebb61ee4b0aba5f6f30726/53ebb62ce4b0aba5f6f31b79/1407956524792/1000w/" title="tarte au chocolat at Table Verte, French vegetarian restaurant in NYC, New York" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               The chocolate part was rich, dense with a strong chocolate taste and if you wonder how they make it, I know that they replace the butter with avocado that is a perfect substitute and a vegan dessert.
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               I had again a nice dinner at Table Verte, a restaurant that more carnivores should know!
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div style="text-align: justify;">
               Enjoy (I did)!
              </div>
