           <h2 class="hidden">
            About this video
           </h2>
           <h2 class="standfirst">
            How to teach a machine to learn.
           </h2>
           <p>
            This video from LSE Research explores the work of Martin Anthony, a theoretical mathematician who is interested in the design of machine intelligence.
           </p>
           <p>
            His research is exploring the design of intelligent systems capable of performing cognitive-type tasks (such as pattern recognition) to analyse large data sets. These could be anything from the identification and diagnosis of tumours in medical imaging to handwriting recognition.
           </p>
           <p>
            In order to design intelligent systems however, one must understand how such systems operate:
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             "One of the central problems for machine learning is working out how many examples you need to show the machine before it is able to recognise new tokens of the type."
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            The solution seems to lie not in specific applications, but at a more general level, using mathematical models to devise ways in which intelligent systems can analyse and scale upwards to cope with increasing data-sets.
           </p>
