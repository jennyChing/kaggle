         <h1>
          Love Songs For People Who Arent In Love: A Valentines Day Playlist
         </h1>
         <p>
          As with many things, the key to compiling a good playlist is to know your audience. Now, I dont want to make too many assumptions about the people reading this article, but I think there are three types of people that we can safely assume probably arent going to be too interested in a Valentines Day playlist (or at least, the kind Im throwing down here).
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           if you recently went through a breakup, dont bother lying about it  we all know youve got Taylor Swifts I Knew You Were Trouble
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          For example: if youre madly in love and looking forward to spending the day with your special someone, Im going to hazard a guess that you arent spending much time on the internet reading random blog posts, except most likely for gift ideas,
          <a href="http://blog.befunky.com/valentines-day-cards/" target="_blank" title="How To Create Personalized Valentine's Day Cards">
           <strong>
            Valentines Day Cards
           </strong>
          </a>
          and fancy chocolate. Likewise, if youre one of those people who aggressively celebrates Singles Awareness Day, love songs probably arent going to be your jam, either. And if you recently went through a breakup, dont bother lying about it  we all know youve got Taylor Swifts I Knew You Were Trouble blaring on repeat right now.
         </p>
         <h3>
          So who is this playlist for, you ask?
         </h3>
         <p>
          Its for everyone inhabits the vast twilight expanse between those three aforementioned extremes  the solidly single, if you will. Its for everyone whos benevolently neutral about Valentines Day, but still feels a twinge once in a while when they realize theyre spending another year on their own. If you nodded in agreement while reading those last two sentences, congratulations 
          <strong>
           this playlist is for you.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_7890" style="width: 360px">
          <a href="http://www.befunky.com/explore/gallery/9265099/" title="Single Person Starter Pack">
           <img alt="The Single Person Starter Pack" class="wp-image-7890" height="350" src="http://blog.befunky.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/C3196219-1423034824large1.jpg" width="350"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The Single Person Starter Pack
          </p>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLmSgnIxJ7OhrMXf65b4XAp2M8sPpbY7NI" target="_blank" title="A Valentines Day Playlist">
           <strong>
            <em>
             Listen: Uninterrupted Playlist
            </em>
           </strong>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1. Amy Winehouse  Tears Dry on Their Own
          </strong>
          <br/>
          When I set about making this playlist, I *knew* that Amy Winehouse needed to be on this list. Almost all of her Back To Black album fits with the theme, but Tears Dry on Their Own strikes the perfect balance between rueful and optimistic. And it has some added poignancy for being one of her last real music videos.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ojdbDYahiCQ?feature=oembed" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2. Barcelona  Please Dont Go
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Cool story about Barcelona: prior to their appearance at SXSW in 2011, someone slapped this song over some footage of people watching the fish in an enormous aquarium. The band never got around to recording an actual music video for this, probably because this video just works.Every time I watch it, I get emotional and I dont know why.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/AJtHg9FbLUA?feature=oembed" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3. Rilo Kiley  Silver Lining
           <br/>
          </strong>
          I once read an article years ago (that I havent been able to find since) about how Jenny Lewis blazed a trail for the current crop of confessional, uber-frank female singers. If you ever come across that article, let me know. Until then, listen to this.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="675" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/jVtSSCzASR0?feature=oembed" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4. Vance Joy  From Afar
           <br/>
          </strong>
          My best friend sent me this track a few months ago, and I promptly listened to it about 8000 times. Most people are probably used to Vance sounding a little more upbeat (as he does on Riptide), but he does sad and longing pretty well, too.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/nYUcqmpHJbk?feature=oembed" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5. San Fermin  Sonsick
          </strong>
          <br/>
          I never get tired of listening to this track by SXSW/NPR darlings San Fermin, and its an apt reminder that sometimes we fall in love not because we should, but because we want to. Not that theres anything wrong with that.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/Ld81Y6Em3NM?feature=oembed" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           6. The Kooks  Around Town
          </strong>
          <br/>
          I kinda missed this song when it first came out, but when I saw The Kooks in concert a few months ago, this was the one song that really stuck out in my mind. Fair warning: the music video is pretty dark.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/v6Gxin8VKZc?feature=oembed&amp;start=122" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           7. Broods  Never Gonna Change
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Broods singer Georgia Nott sings with way more intensity than any 20 year-old should be able to muster, and nowhere is that more apparent than on this song. I aint mad about it, though.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/9QrCFSJyabs?feature=oembed" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           8. Future Islands  Seasons (Waiting on You)
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Speaking of intense, it doesnt get more intense than Future Islands. Now admittedly, Ive heard one or two hapless couples refer to this as their song and/or make out during this jam at festivals, but LISTEN TO THE LYRICS, MAN. Do not put this track on a mixtape for your boo unless youre trying to be reaaaally passive-aggressive about how long it takes them to get ready.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/-5Ae-LhMIG0?feature=oembed" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           9. Panama  Always
          </strong>
          <br/>
          If you feel a little convicted when you hear Jarrah McCleary say Im a coward, when it comes to love, dont worry  theres always next year. Until then, it looks like that wine glass needs a refill, amiright?
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/UylPrMcurB8?feature=oembed" width="900">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          These are songs about love, which we can all dig, but theyre not about actually being in love, which most of us can relate to. So sit down with that glass of wine (I know youve already poured yourself one) and enjoy my favorite love songs for people who arent actually in love.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.befunky.com/explore/gallery/9268956/" rel="attachment wp-att-7977" target="_blank" title="Love Songs For People Who Aren't In Love">
           <img alt="Love Songs For Single People3" class="aligncenter wp-image-7977" height="225" src="http://blog.befunky.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Love-Songs-For-Single-People31.jpg" width="250"/>
          </a>
         </p>
