      <h2>
       Keno history
      </h2>
      Keno dates back to ancient China and the legend says that it started as a childrens poem, known as Thousand Character Classic. This was a poem used to teach kids to read, as there were a thousand unique characters.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Around the year 200 BCE, Cheung Leung, the Chinese general started facing difficulties in founding his battles. Since the citizens were no longer interested in paying more taxes and were thus becoming hostile, the general developed the idea of building a huge wall to separate his kingdom from the barbarians that were coming from the north. Again, funds were limited, so the theory says that Cheung Leung invented a game based on 120 characters of the Thousand Character Classic poem, in which players had to pick two characters. The general himself drew characters and came up with winning combinations. The game was an instant success; people loved it and started playing it throughout the kingdom. The winning combinations were announced with the help of carrier pigeons so the game was named the White Pigeon Game.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Bets from the game were exactly what the general needed to build the Great Wall of China and to fund the army. It is therefore believed that Keno played an essential role in saving the Han Dynasty.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Keno remained popular throughout generations and, as with a lot of other Chinese games, it traveled along with Chinese immigrant railway workers in the 1850s.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      It was illegal at first, especially in areas like San Francisco but its popularity increased even more once the Chinese characters of the game were replaced by simple numbers that dropped from 120 to just 80.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      This move boosted the games popularity even more so that in the late 19th century it started being widely played.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      The first
      <a href="/online-bingo/keno">
       Keno game
      </a>
      was opened in the Palace Club, Nevada in 1936. Being a lottery-type game, it was still illegal in Nevada, but since horse races were allowed, the game experienced a slight change, becoming known as horse race keno, under the pretext that each number was a horse that players were betting on.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Its success convinced the government to allow it and it thus became legal, but with a limit of $25,000 payout. This amount was raised to $50,000 and eventually limits were abolished and casinos were free to pay out whatever prizes they wanted.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <h2>
       How to play Keno
      </h2>
      The online version of Keno is played with a box of 80 numbers that also features a stake selector on the side and 80 Keno balls which are numbered.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      In order to play, you must first select the wager and then choose which numbers you wish to play. Usually, all you have to do is click on the numbers of the board in order to select them. Depending on the online casino you choose to play Keno at, you may select up to 20 numbers per game.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Once the numbers have been selected, the game will begin. Hit PLAY and a number of balls are drawn, equal to the number of spots you have chosen. If the numbers are the drawn balls are the same with the ones you have selected on your board, then you will receive payouts accordingly. The more spots you select, the more winning chances.

      <br/>
