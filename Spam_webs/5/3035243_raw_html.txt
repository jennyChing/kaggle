                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Golden Straw Bunting
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5476132202390421683" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bizEcYGUuiQ/UzY9puoYmbI/AAAAAAAAL0c/IuoZqxTrLyY/s1600/straw+bunting.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="">
                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bizEcYGUuiQ/UzY9puoYmbI/AAAAAAAAL0c/IuoZqxTrLyY/s1600/straw+bunting.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Ozu0tFKD1_0/UzY9mHZtkYI/AAAAAAAAL0I/qTKD2N47ZQA/s1600/straw+bunting+5.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="">
                       <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Ozu0tFKD1_0/UzY9mHZtkYI/AAAAAAAAL0I/qTKD2N47ZQA/s1600/straw+bunting+5.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      Buntings aren't dead yet. But, I'm trying something a little less country, shabby chic-ish, with this gold straw bunting. It's light and airy and almost feels like a piece of jewelry for your home. This Christmas, when we were in Boise, ID, the Anthropology store had a fabulous display made with straws painted gold. I've been wanting to make a scaled down version for my mantel ever since.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      This simple bunting is season-less and can coordinate with any home decor or party theme.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-EW1fRwnsp1E/UzY9nBRb2eI/AAAAAAAAL0Q/NuGQqUim3jc/s1600/straw+bunting+6.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="">
                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-EW1fRwnsp1E/UzY9nBRb2eI/AAAAAAAAL0Q/NuGQqUim3jc/s1600/straw+bunting+6.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     1. You'll need lots of paper straws. For each triangle you'll need to full length straws and one half length. You'll also need some heavy thread or twine and a large needle.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     2. Start by threading the needle and thread through the small straw.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     3. Pull a long length of the thread through the straw.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     4. Put the thread the one of the full length straws.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-YMVwe-iqtHY/UzY9pbT23MI/AAAAAAAAL0g/pzW64gBfepQ/s1600/straw+bunting+7.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="">
                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-YMVwe-iqtHY/UzY9pbT23MI/AAAAAAAAL0g/pzW64gBfepQ/s1600/straw+bunting+7.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     5. Thread the needle and thread through the second full length straw.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     6. Now thread the needle back through the short straw. Pull tight so that the straws are tight against each other at the points.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     7. Start again, threading on the short straw and continue with steps 2-6.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     8. Using gold spray paint, paint the bunting in a well ventilated area. You will need to turn the bunting over and rotate the straws a couple times to cover all sides of the straws.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1LFSVmgs6U0/UzY9jlr4-RI/AAAAAAAALz0/__sNgXS90lU/s1600/straw+bunting+3.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>

                       <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-FwF78q-ngP4/UzY9pQjGebI/AAAAAAAAL0k/N7ep8fDBpUc/s1600/straw+bunting+8.jpg"/>
                      </a>
