         <img alt="No Gravatar" height="40/" src="http://www.gravatar.com/avatar.php?gravatar_id=daf7798e765b11cfb63740289a3ac2d4&amp;default=http://use.perl.org/images/pix.gif" style="float: left; margin-right: 10px; border: none;" width="40"/>
         <div>
          <strong>
          </strong>
          <em>
           Thanks to Elena from
           <a href="http://www.kreativreisen.at">
            KreativReisen.at
           </a>
           for this great guest post! Elena also regularly writes for the
           <a href="http://blog.tripwolf.com/de/blog/author/elena/">
            German tripwolf blog
           </a>
           .
           <br/>
          </em>
         </div>
         <div>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.kreativreisen.at">
            <br/>
           </a>
          </em>
         </div>
         <div>
          Make your own cheese, learn how to bake an Austrian cake,sketch your next watercolor painting the fresh Salzburg mountain air, learn how to carve, felt, weave or print, learn a traditional dance Everything is possible when
          <strong>
           creativity comes to play while you travel
          </strong>
          . I have termed this unique type of travel, what else, but
          <strong>
           <em>
            creative travel
           </em>
          </strong>
          . I have found that many others share this interest with me!
         </div>
         <div>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10306" style="width: 510px">
           <img alt="(c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena" class="size-medium wp-image-10306" height="369" src="http://blog.tripwolf.com/en/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Kreativ3-500x369.jpg" title="(c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena" width="500"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            (c) Creative Tourism New Zealand
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           I first developed my passion for this form of traveling in New Zealand where Creative Tourism New Zealand offers exciting activities such as bone carving, harakeke flax weaving, Maori cooking or nature workshops. If you, like me, are driven by questions about art and culture, try to incorporate more creative travel experiences in your next trip. It is all about experiencing
           <strong>
            unique cultural traditions
           </strong>
           by getting out there, getting your hands in it and making unique memories as well as your own souvenir.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div>
          <span id="more-10300">
          </span>
          <strong>
           I do and I understand
          </strong>
         </div>
         <div>
          Creative travel and interactive learning experiences, in my opinion, give a particular edge to mass-marketed cultural tourism experiences all over the world. Everybody can buy a Maori indigenous souvenir in New Zealand,
          <strong>
           but to make one yourself?
          </strong>
          Now, thats something special! Not only do professional tutors
          <strong>
           teach you a new skill
          </strong>
          to impress your friends with (learning how to carve, for instance), but you can really meet the people behind the experience, see eye to eye their artistic endeavors, the local traditions, and the value of the craft in the community. A sustainable, emotional and hands-on
          <strong>
           travel experience does not need to be expensive
          </strong>
          : Depending on location, material or food and overnight arrangements, many experiences are available starting from a budget price range.
         </div>
         <div>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10307" style="width: 510px">
           <img alt="(c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena" class="size-medium wp-image-10307" height="331" src="http://blog.tripwolf.com/en/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/KreativR-500x331.jpg" title="(c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena" width="500"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            (c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Fortunately for people like me, the message has spread and currently reaches thousands of travelers motivated to
          <strong>
           look behind the scenes on their typical trip.
          </strong>
         </div>
         <div>
          The city of
          <a href="http://www.barcelonacreativa.info/" target="_blank">
           Barcelona
          </a>
          has offered creative tourism activities since 2006, including
          <strong>
           photo safaris, mosaic workshops, painting
          </strong>
          or
          <strong>
           music classes
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           cooking workshops
          </strong>
          with
          <strong>
           visits to the local markets
          </strong>
          .
          <a href="http://www.santafecreativetourism.org/" target="_blank">
           Santa Fe
          </a>
          , a city part of UNESCOs Creative Cities Network, offers you a myriad of creative workshops and experiences all year round, such as cooking or
          <strong>
           painting, glass art, theatre
          </strong>
          , dance or music. It focuses on local artists having chosen to live in or around Santa Fe, thereby enhancing the citys cultural life and offering opportunities for visitors to engage with the local cultural fabric.
         </div>
         <div>
          Similar networks and creative travel offers are now found throughout
          <a href="http://www.creativetourismnetwork.org/" target="_blank">
           Europe
          </a>
          or even Asia (Thailand and India), but often the idea goes a lot further than the classic painting holiday in Tuscany or Provence. Nowhere else can you
          <strong>
           learn how to jodel
          </strong>
          but in the Alpine valleys of Salzburg or Tyrol; nowhere else can you
          <strong>
           hunt truffles
          </strong>
          and enjoy
          <strong>
           pasta and truffle cooking lessons
          </strong>
          such as in certain areas of Italy (Umbria or Piemont, where theses activities are still practiced.) Creative travel is therefore all about the particular aspects of a certain destination: uniqueness, coolness and creativity all come to play here.
         </div>
         <div>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10308" style="width: 510px">
           <img alt="(c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena" class="size-medium wp-image-10308" height="370" src="http://blog.tripwolf.com/en/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Kreativ2-500x370.jpg" title="(c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena" width="500"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            (c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <strong>
           Creative tourism in Austria
          </strong>
         </div>
         <div>
          <strong>
          </strong>
          I am particularly excited about the many possibilities to discover my own backyard through creative travel. While Im constantly on the lookout for and
          <a href="http://blog.kreativreisen.at/" target="_blank">
           blogging
          </a>
          about new experiences available throughout Austria, the network
          <a href="http://www.kreativreisen.at/" target="_blank">
           www.kreativreisen.at
          </a>
          already offers over 100 different creative courses and destinations, such as jodling, carving, cooking &amp; baking or painting and jewelry-making classes.
         </div>
         <div>
          One of them is Lucie who originally hails from NYC and now offers
          <strong>
           creative shopping tours
          </strong>
          in Vienna meeting local designers, jewellers and food merchants. Her enthusiasm about the creative tourism movement is really palpable, and we hope it is going to continue showing a way forward where tourists and locals come together in an equally valued exchange of knowledge and skills for travelers and merchants alike in the future.
         </div>
         <div>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10309" style="width: 367px">
           <img alt="(c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena" class="size-full wp-image-10309" height="475" src="http://blog.tripwolf.com/en/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/kreativ4.jpg" title="(c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena" width="357"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            (c) Creative Tourism Austria and Elena
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <strong>
           Leave me a message if you are keen to learn more! I look forward to hearing from you on this exciting new travel topic.
          </strong>
         </div>
         <div>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Cheers, Elena
           </em>
          </strong>
