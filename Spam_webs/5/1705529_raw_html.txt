             <h1 class="post-title">
              Fantasy Life Review: Love The Life You Live
             </h1>
             <span class="entry-auth-link">
              by
              <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/author/adam/">
               Adam Ballard
              </a>
             </span>
             <p>
              Opening my 3DS and firing up Fantasy Life has become a blissful, Pavlovian experience. Tucked behind a charming art style (character design by Yoshitaka Amano and music by Nobuo Uematsu, both of Final Fantasy fame) and whimsical story is a system thats brilliantly simple and wonderfully diverse. At any given moment I can choose to hunt a legendary beast, mine for exotic materials or spend the day fishing. Sure theres a story to complete but with so many fun things to do who has the time?
             </p>
             <div class="ad-inserter ad-inserter-1" style="float: right; margin: 10px;">
              <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
              </script>
              <!-- G9 Middle Ad -->
              <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-6267359740539804" data-ad-slot="6414784374" style="display:inline-block;width:300px;height:250px">
              </ins>
              <script>
               (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
              </script>
             </div>
             <p>
              The main hook of Fantasy Life revolves around the earlier mentioned facets: simplicity and diversity. Players can choose between 12 different Lifes (roles similar to Final Fantasys job system). Each Life features a distinct play style making each of the 12 occupations feel refreshingly varied. The Paladin uses sword and shield to face off against foes in a traditional action/RPG fashion whereas crafting Lifes such as the Cook use a more mini-game focused approach to improve their skills.
             </p>
             <p>
              And with the ability to swap between Lifes, players can enjoy the variety of play styles on a whim. I started my game as a Mercenary (who excels at using 2-handed weapons) slaying monsters across the land. My wife started her play through as a Tailor whose quests were completed via crafting mini games. As different as our approaches were it was ridiculously fun to play co-op together and use our strengths to benefit one another. If she needed help felling a dragon, I was there. If I needed a rare garment for a quest, she had my back.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Fantasy-Life.jpg">
               <img alt="Fantasy-Life" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40715" height="384" src="http://www.geekbinge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Fantasy-Life.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Experimenting with each Life slowly revealed just how masterful developer Level-5 is at game design. On any given screen you can see a plethora of activities. Trees to be cut down by Woodcutters, exotic gems to unearthed by Miners and rare ingredients to be used by Alchemists are everywhere. Its a terrific melding of Final Fantasy-esque action/RPG mechanics and sim/craftinggames like Rune Factory or
              <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/tag/animal-crossing/" title="Animal Crossing">
               Animal Crossing
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              Despite my genuine love for Fantasy Life, Id be remiss not to mention its biggest flaw: the storys dialogue. Dont get me wrong, the characters are enjoyable and the story is charming but sweet Jesus there is a lot of dialogue in the story missions. Its a problem thats immediately noticeable at the start with an overly drawn out prologue and continues up to the 30 hour mark. The writers deliver clever puns and generally amusing dialogue throughout the play through, but I would routinely close my 3DS during story sequences just because I was tired of reading. It may sound like a petty complaint, but for a game thats so addictive the story slogs can mar the experience.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Fantasy-Life-3DS-Review.jpg">
               <img alt="Fantasy-Life-3DS-Review" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40716" height="471" src="http://www.geekbinge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Fantasy-Life-3DS-Review.jpg" width="778"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              That said, even after completing the main story I eagerly return to Fantasy Life on a daily basis. The thrill of learning new techniques, mastering different Lifes and hunting down legendary creatures/treasures has created a gameplay loop that I can enjoy for a 15 minute sitting or an all night marathon. The campaign can be completed simply by focusingon a single Life but theres much to be enjoyed outside of that narrow approach.
             </p>
