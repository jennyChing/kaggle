        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Genesis: The Custom Motorcycle
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <span class="posted-on">
          <a href="http://theoldmotor.com/?p=2377" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date published updated" datetime="2011-01-21T12:41:49+00:00">
            January 21, 2011
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content clear">
        <p>
         <a data-slb-active="1" data-slb-group="2377" data-slb-internal="110456" href="http://theoldmotor.com/?attachment_id=110456" rel="attachment wp-att-110456">
          <img alt="custom" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110456" height="514" src="http://theoldmotor.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/custom.jpg" width="592"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ahh.Im not sure what to make of this two wheeler. The actual photo can be found in the book titled
         <em>
          Victorian and Edwardian Cycling and Motoring From Old Photographs
         </em>
         and is dated as circa 1909. The engine is certainly a De Dion Bouton with a jet carburetor as is the rear brake that is normally mounted to the front on a DDB machine. Center hub steering on a flimsy looking frame that doesnt have any form of springing. The first custom motorcycle? Decide for yourself. You can also see
         <em>
          <a href="http://theoldmotor.com/?cat=21&amp;paged=6">
           100s more motorcycle photos
          </a>
         </em>
         here on
         <em>
          The Old Motor.
         </em>
        </p>
