       <h1 class="page_title">
        Paperless World: 50 Tips and Tools for Reading On Your iPod
       </h1>
       <div class="meta_info">
        Posted on 06.12.2008
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        By Britney Wilkins
       </p>
       <p>
        By now, you probably know that the iPod is for more than just music. It can be a great device for catching up on your reading, too. Whether you're downloading audio books or study guides, the iPod makes a handy and compact reader on the go, and you can make the most of it with the resources we've collected here.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Tips &amp; Guides
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Follow these instructions to get your iPod into ultimate reading shape.
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RSS_(file_format)">
           Make use of RSS
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Instead of manually updating your iPod, you can use RSS to automatically give you updated news whenever you plug it into your computer.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://docs.info.apple.com/article.html?artnum=93951">
           Storing and reading notes with your iPod
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Read up on what Apple has to say about using the iPod to read notes from your computer.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.oreillynet.com/pub/a/mac/2004/12/17/ipod_reader.html">
           Build an eDoc Reader for your iPod
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Learn how to make books, PDF documents, and other electronic texts easy to read on your iPod with this guide.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.tuaw.com/2008/01/20/mac-automation-notes-on-your-ipod/">
           Mac Automation: notes on your iPod
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This quick tutorial shows you how to make creating iPod notes an automated process.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipodlinux.org/forums/viewtopic.php?t=59">
           Reading ebooks on an iPod
          </a>
         </strong>
         : AndyBrain runs down the details of reading eBooks on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.geeksaresexy.net/2007/12/28/turning-your-ipod-into-an-information-depository/">
           Turning your iPod into an information depository
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This guide offers tips for getting audio books and more on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.wikihow.com/Create-an-iPod-Tarot-Deck">
           How to Create an iPod Tarot Deck
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Follow this guide to make an impressive Tarot deck in your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.oreillynet.com/pub/a/mac/2006/12/12/ipod-notes-above-and-beyond.html">
           iPod Notes: Above and Beyond
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Read this guide to find out how to best leverage the power of your iPod's notes function.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://mdernest.wordpress.com/2007/09/05/reading-documents-in-your-ipod/">
           Reading Documents on Your iPod
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Read this user's painstakingly simple guide to reading articles and other text on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2004/04/16/pts-how-to-fridays-read-rss-feeds-on-your-ipod/">
           Read RSS feeds on your iPod
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Check out Engadget's tutorial to find out how you can get automatically updated content to read on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://developer.apple.com/hardwaredrivers/ipod/iPodNotesFeatureGuideCB.pdf">
           iPod Notes Feature Guide
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Get the official word on Notes features from Apple here.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2006/08/25/books/25audi.html?ex=1314158400&amp;en=5441d083f6d4aca4&amp;ei=5088&amp;partner=rssnyt&amp;emc=rss">
           Listen to audiobooks
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Instead of taxing your eyes on a small screen, just listen to the books you'd like to read.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipodobserver.com/story/32683">
           Speed Reading
          </a>
         </strong>
         : If you're listening to audio books, you can speed read by changing settings that will allow you to listen to them faster.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Tools
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Make use of these tools, and getting the reading resources you want onto your iPod will be a snap.
       </p>
       <ol start="14">
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://googleget.markwheeler.net/">
           GoogleGet
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This little program will download news and weather information from Google News and Weather Room that you can read on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.staylazy.net/canto/">
           Canto Pod
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Read the lyrics to your favorite song as you listen to it with this handy little tool.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.google.com/reader">
           Google Reader
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Get this popular RSS feed reader to get automatically updated content that can be read on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.merlinsoftware.com/ebook/index.htm">
           eBook to Images
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This tool will extract the text out of PDFs and turn it into multiple text images that can be read easily on your iPod's screen.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.zapptek.com/ipda/">
           iPDA
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This handy little tool pretty much turns your iPod into a PDA, offering documents, RSS feeds, news, and lots more.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.codent.com/rss/rss2ical.php">
           RSS2iCal
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Use this script to subscribe to news sources through Apple's iCal.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.freeipodsoftware.com/index.php">
           MP3 to iPod Audiobook Converter
          </a>
         </strong>
         : With this converter, you can create audiobooks that are much more manageable than regular mp3s.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipodsoft.com/ipodagent.aspx">
           iPod Agent
          </a>
         </strong>
         : iPod Agent takes the guesswork out of transferring text and other files to your iPod, even breaking down pages into iPod-readable 4k size. It will also gather RSS news feeds for you and put them on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipodsoft.com/site/index.php?n=ChapterMaster.HomePage">
           Chapter Master
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This nifty tool will add chapter stops to your audiobook files, and even offers the option to create images for each chapter.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipodsoft.com/site/index.php?n=MarkAble.HomePage">
           MarkAble
          </a>
         </strong>
         : If you're listening to an audiobook, use this tool to keep your sot for next time.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.theplaceforitall.com/ipodulator/">
           iPodulator
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Using this site, all you have to do is enter a URL of a page or RSS feed, and you'll get a plain text download that can be read on your iPod. It even has a
         <a href="http://www.theplaceforitall.com/ipodulator/bookconvert.php?filename=">
          bookmarklet
         </a>
         to use in your browser that makes things even easier.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipodsoft.com/site/pmwiki.php?n=igadget.Homepage">
           iGadget
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This handy little gadget will not only pull RSS feeds for you to read on your iPod, it will help you transfer Outlook data and even create a text library.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://ebookhood.com/ipod-ebook-creator">
           eBook Hood
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Using this tool, you can convert any eBook into a format that is readable on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipod-notes.com/">
           iPod-notes
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Use iPod-notes, a tool that will create iPod ebooks from text files, or even a web page.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.touchtip.com/iphone-and-ipod-touch/evernote-powerful-note-taking-software-for-desktop-web-and-mobile/">
           Evernote
          </a>
         </strong>
         : If you have an iPhone or iPod Touch, make use of Evernote to get back the notes functionality you lost in the switch.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.bloglines.com/">
           Bloglines
          </a>
         </strong>
         : With Bloglines, you'll get a simple RSS feed reader that gives you lots of control and the ability to keep clip excerpts.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Reading Sources
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Visit these sources to find ebooks, audiobooks, and more reading content that you can load on your iPod.
       </p>
       <ol start="30">
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.raybook.com/">
           Raybook
          </a>
         </strong>
         : On Raybook, you'll find study guides, cookbooks, travel guides, and much more.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipreppress.com/Pages/studyguides.htm">
           SparkNotes
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Download these study guides from SparkNotes to cram on the way to class.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://journals.worldnomads.com/language-guides/">
           Learn the Lingo
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Get language guides on your iPod with everything from Mandarin Chinese to French.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://iphone.texterity.com/magazines/">
           Texterity
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Check out Texterity for the iPhone and iPod Touch, a program that makes it easy to read digital versions of popular magazines.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.learnoutloud.com/Home">
           LearnOutLoud
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Download audiobooks from a wide variety of categories on this site.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://librivox.org/">
           LibriVox
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Visit LibriVox to download free audio of books that are in the public domain.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipreppress.com/Pages/ClassicLit.htm">
           Classic Literature
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Here you'll find loads of classic literature that you can download as ebooks for the iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.alexwilson.com/telltale/">
           Telltale Weekly
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Use Telltale Weekly to get loads of low-cost, high quality audio book downloads.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.bibleplayer.com/">
           Audio Bible
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Catch up on your bible reading using this free and easy download that offers an mp3 bible.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://encyclopodia.sourceforge.net/en/index.html">
           Encyclopodia
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Keep an entire dictionary in your pocket using this tool that puts Wikipedia on your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.freeclassicaudiobooks.com/">
           Free Classic Audio Books
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Listen to human narrated books like Huck Finn and Treasure Island on Free Classic Audio Books.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.mobipocket.com/en/HomePage/default.asp?Language=EN">
           Mobipocket
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Check out bestsellers, enews, elearning and more using Mobipocket.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipreppress.com/Pages/gamerules.htm">
           Game Rules
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Always have handy rules that you can reference from anywhere when playing card games and competitive sports with this download for your iPod.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ereader.com/">
           eReader
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This online bookstore has more than 30,000 titles available.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.wowio.com/">
           Wowzio
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Download everything from graphic novels to biography on Wowzio.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.freesfonline.de/">
           Free Speculative Fiction Online
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Check out speculative fiction ebooks on this site, which offers a rating system and recommended stories.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.portablereading.com/">
           Text on Phone
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Get more than 20,000 books, novels, and articles that you can read and comment on using your iPhone or iPod Touch.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.mslit.com/default.asp">
           MS Reader
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Get classics, reference books, and lots more from Microsoft's reader.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.ipreppress.com/Pages/Reference/MWPocket.htm">
           MWPocket
          </a>

         : Download this pocket dictionary to always have word reference in your pocket.

         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.gutenberg.org/catalog/">
           Project Gutenberg Library
