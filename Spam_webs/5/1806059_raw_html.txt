     Speed Training Section
    </h1>

    <p>
     This section of the website focuses on the key principles of speed training.
    </p>
    <p>
     What is speed?  It is the ability to reach a high velocity of movement in whatever mode of locomotion  running, cycling, skating swimming etc (1).
    </p>
    <p>
     Very often,
     <a href="http://www.sport-fitness-advisor.com/agility-exercises.html" onmouseout="window.status=''; return true" onmouseover="window.status='Agility exercises'; return true">
      agility
     </a>
     is more relevant to successful sports performance than all-out speed.  Agility is the ability to explosively brake, change direction and accelerate again.
    </p>
    <p>
     Another element of fitness closely related to speed training is
     <a href="http://www.sport-fitness-advisor.com/speedendurance.html" onmouseout="window.status=''; return true" onmouseover="window.status='Speed endurance training'; return true">
      speed endurance
     </a>
     .  Many athletes must maintain a high velocity for longer than 6 seconds or produce repeated sprints with minimal rest periods in between.
    </p>
    <p>
     The combination of speed, agility and speed endurance an athlete requires is determined by his or her sport.  But regardless of the event, there are several modes of training that are integral to developing a fast athlete:
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      <font color="CC0000">
       Strength &amp; Power Training
      </font>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Speed is chiefly determined by the capacity to apply a large amount of force in a short period of time.  This is also known as
     <strong>
      power
     </strong>
     .  Many athletic movements take place in 0.1 to 0.2 seconds but maximal force production takes 0.6 to 0.8 seconds.  The athlete who can apply most force in the short period of available time is said to be the most powerful.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="http://www.sport-fitness-advisor.com/strengthtraining.html" onmouseout="window.status=''; return true" onmouseover="window.status='Strength training section'; return true">
      Strength training
     </a>
     increases maximal force production.  Assuming as a result, more force can be produced in the same period of time, strength training alone can increase power.  However, it makes more sense to increase both maximal force production
     <strong>
      and
     </strong>
     the rate of force development.  This can be achieved through
     <a href="http://www.sport-fitness-advisor.com/power-training.html" onmouseout="window.status=''; return true" onmouseover="window.status='Power training'; return true">
      power training
     </a>
     .  Both strength and power training are integral to improvement of speed.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <hr align="left" size="2" width="30%"/>
    <p>
     <strong>
      <font color="CC0000">
       Speed Training
      </font>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Practising moving and accelerating faster helps to condition the neuromuscular system to improve the firing patterns of fast twitch muscle fibers.  Two variations of basic speed training are
     <strong>
      assisted
     </strong>
     and
     <a>
      resisted speed training.  Assisted training (also called overspeed training helps to improve stride frequency (2,3,4).  Resisted speed training helps to improve speed-strength and stride length (2,3,4).
     </a>
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <hr align="left" size="2" width="30%"/>
    <p>
     <strong>
      <font color="CC0000">
       Agility Training
      </font>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Most team sports consist of very few movements that occur only in a straight line.  Nor do those movements occur at a fixed pace or for a fixed length of time.  Agility and quickness training improves an athletes ability to change direction, brake suddenly and perform sport-specific skills with speed and dexterity.
    </p>
    <p>
     Compare speed training to strength training for a moment.  A
     <a href="http://www.sport-fitness-advisor.com/strengthtrainingprograms.html" onmouseout="window.status=''; return true" onmouseover="window.status='Strength training programs'; return true">
      sport-specific strength training program
     </a>
     will first aim to develop basic strength.  This is on the premise that a solid base of strength offers greater physical potential to work with when converting it to sport-specific strength later on.  Basic speed training along with power training maximises the athletes ability to move rapidly.  Agility training helps an athlete to apply their speed to sport-specific scenarios.
    </p>
    <p>
