          Spooky Inspiration: Mexicos Da De Los Muertos

           <a href="http://blog.ospreypacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/Dia-de-los-muertos.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-4446" height="349" src="http://blog.ospreypacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/Dia-de-los-muertos.jpg" title="Dia de los muertos" width="500"/>

           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            photo by Giovanna Borgna

          <p>
           Looking for some fresh Halloween inspiration? Check out Matador Trips editor Hal Amens photos from the 2008Da delos Muertos in Mexico City
           <a href="http://wayworded.blogspot.com/2008/11/death-in-style.html">
            here
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://matadortravel.com/traveler/halamen/blog/d%C3%AD-de-los-muertos-vs-halloween">
            Via Hal Amen on Matador Travel:
           </a>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            No holiday screams Mxico! louder than Da de los Muertos (Day of the Dead), celebrated November 12 to honor departed family and friends.
           </p>
           <p>
            The trappings of this annual event are familiar to anyone with knowledge of Mexican culture: carefully constructed altars overflowing with personalized
            <em>
             ofrendas
            </em>
            for the deceased, bright orange marigolds
            <em>
             la flor de muertos
            </em>
            sugar skulls, an infinite variety of flamboyantly adorned skeletons.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
