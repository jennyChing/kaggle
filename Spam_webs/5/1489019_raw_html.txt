          <p>
           Theres something about the simplicity and beautiful craftsmanship of a bungalow home that draws us in time and time again. Popular back at the turn of the 20th century, American bungalows have recently made a comeback in the minds of homebuyers, retirees, young couples, and people with physical disabilities across America. Why, do you ask? To answer that we should probably delve into what a bungalow is first.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-3380">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           According to About.com and Wikipedia, bungalow homes are a type of house, with varying meanings across the world. Common features to many (but not all) of these definitions include being detached, low-rise (single or one-and-a-half stories), and the use of verandahs.
          </p>
          <p>
           While bungalows can be found throughout the world, were going to focus on American bungalows, specifically the American Craftsman and California variety. What are some key differences between the two?
          </p>
          <p>
           OldHouses.com describes American Craftsman (or just Craftsman) bungalow homes as having:
          </p>
          <ul>
           1.	Low-pitched gabled roof with wide, unenclosed eave overhang
           <br/>
           2.	Roof rafters usually exposed
           <br/>
           3.	Decorative beams or braces under gables
           <br/>
           4.	Porch support bases extending to ground level
           <br/>
           5.	Porch supports usually squared and sometimes slanting inward
          </ul>
          <p>
           Wikipedia describes California bungalows as having sloping roofs and eaves with unenclosed rafters, and typically feature a gable (or an attic vent designed to look like one) over the main portion of the house. Ideally, bungalows are horizontal in massing, and are integrated with the earth by use of local materials and transitional plantings. This helps create the signature look most people associate with the California Bungalow. The entry goes on to mention that exterior features of a California bungalow include wooden shingles, stucco or horizontal siding, stone or brick chimneys, and L-shaped porches on the larger models.
          </p>
          <p>
           Aside from the aesthetic appeal, why do we love bungalow homes so much? For starters, its the perfect size for a small family, like a young couple with one or two small kids, or a couple of retirees. We also love the welcoming porches that bungalow homes have, giving visitors a sense of warmth when they swing by for a cup of coffee, while giving residents a sense of seclusion and privacy from the street.
          </p>
          <p>
           Speaking of privacy, since bungalow homes are usually detached, its easy to keep neighboring eyes from peering in to your home with just the addition of trees, bushes and other flora.
          </p>
          <p>
           If you step foot into a bungalow home youll notice that the floor plan is a bit smaller than your average single-family home. The Bungalow Company aptly explains that this is because [bungalow] designs trade raw square footage for efficient use of space. Lets say you opened the front door of a Craftsman style bungalow. Youd be immediately let into the living room, greeted by a warm fire in the fireplace. You would then walk through the dining area and right into the kitchen.
          </p>
          <p>
           Bungalow floor plans usually eliminate the foyer or extra hallways to get from one room to the next, although weve seen a couple that have a small foyer before you hit the living quarters. Though the floor plan may be a tad smaller, another benefit is that in some bungalows the rooms are well connected and are situated around the main living quarters.
          </p>
          <p>
           Would we live in a bungalow home? We think so, even as our first home. Once our families start to grow we may consider staying or entertain the idea of moving up to a larger single-family home. In our idyllic reverie, bungalow homes would be a lovely place to raise a family.
           <br/>
           <i>
            <br/>
            Do you live in a bungalow home? What do you love about it and did you purchase it with the intent of making a few upgrades? Please share with us in the comments below.
            <br/>
           </i>
          </p>
