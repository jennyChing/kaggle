       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         At one point or another, youve been mistaken for an employee of a store. You accidentally dressed yourself in an outfit that resembles the stores uniform and the locals cant stop asking you questions.
        </p>
        <p>
         This happens all the time in
         <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/target-reviews" target="_blank" title="Target">
          Target
         </a>
         . For the most part, all you need to be wearing is a red shirt for a stranger to ask you where they can find light bulbs, peanut butter and greeting cards. But it isnt just Target. Cases of customer-employee mistaken identity happen in all kinds of stores.
        </p>
        <p>
         At
         <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/best-buy-reviews" target="_blank" title="Best Buy">
          Best Buy
         </a>
         , if youre wearing a blue polo shirt with khakis, you should be ready for a mother to inquire about a new war game for her son. All you have to be wearing in an
         <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/apple-reviews" target="_blank" title="Apple">
          Apple
         </a>
         store is a blue t-shirt and maybe some Vans or Toms shoes for a twenty-something with a messenger bag to ask, Can I buy an iPhone here?
        </p>
        <p>
         Sometimes, your attitude adds to the uniform. If youre shopping at
         <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/hot-topic-inc-reviews" target="_blank" title="Hot Topic">
          Hot Topic
         </a>
         while rocking dyed hair, piercings, and apathy, chances are youll be asked about a shirt with some old internet joke pasted on it. If youre mulling about
         <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/nordstrom-reviews" target="_blank" title="Nordstrom">
          Nordstrom
         </a>
         in cocktail attire and matching sneer, a customer might pick you to ask, Why is this Obey shirt $80?
         <strong>
          <em>
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           For the full visual effect, check out the infographic:
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <div class="mceTemp mceIEcenter">
         <div class="mceTemp mceIEcenter" style="text-align: left">
          <dl>
           <dt>
            <a href="http://www.hallway.com/blogs/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/NotToWear_FINAL-01.png">
             <img alt="How To Avoid Other Customers Thinking You're An Employee" class="size-full wp-image-117" height="4289" src="http://www.hallway.com/blogs/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/NotToWear_FINAL-01.png" width="1000"/>
            </a>
            <strong>
            </strong>
           </dt>
           <dt>
            <strong>
            </strong>
           </dt>
          </dl>
          <p>
           Expect questions about tight-fitting sweaters and skinny jeans if youre shopping at
           <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/urban-outfitters-inc-reviews" target="_blank" title="Urban Outfitters">
            Urban Outfitters
           </a>
           with tattoos. If youre wearing a blue vest in a
           <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/walmart-stores-reviews" target="_blank" title="Walmart">
            Walmart
           </a>
           , maybe you shouldnt be surprised when an old lady asks you where the McDonalds is located.
          </p>
          <p>
           If youre staring off into space at
           <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/macys-reviews" target="_blank" title="Macy's">
            Macys
           </a>
           with an argyle sweater on, theres a good chance someones going to ask you if the expensive jeans are discounted. Someone might want you to help fix their kitchen if youre wearing your own orange workmans apron inside a
           <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/home-depot-reviews" target="_blank" title="Home Depot">
            Home Depot
           </a>
           . And dont be taken aback when someone needs 600 packages of Top Ramen at
           <a href="http://www.hallway.com/companies/costco-wholesale-reviews" target="_blank" title="Costco">
            Costco
           </a>
           and they follow you around because youre wearing a red vest with Shape Ups.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
           </strong>
           Just be sure what you wear to different stores, so you never have to run a price check or attend customer service meetings.
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>

             here
            </a>
            .
           </em>
          </p>
