        How-To Geek
       </h1>

       <div class="thepost" id="post-190811">
        <div style="max-width:650px;width:100%;margin-bottom:0px;margin-top:-10px;min-height:24px;">
         <div style="clear:both;">
         </div>
        </div>
        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/190811/how-and-why-to-use-otr-for-private-instant-messaging/" rel="bookmark" title="How (and Why) to Use OTR For Private Instant Messaging">
          How (and Why) to Use OTR For Private Instant Messaging
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="thecontent">
         <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
         <p>
          <img alt="private-instant-messaging" border="0" height="300" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/private-instant-messaging.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="private-instant-messaging" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          OTR stands for off the record. Its a way to have encrypted private instant message conversations online. It uses end-to-end encryption so your network provider, government, and even the instant-messaging service itself cant see the content of your messages.
         </p>
         <p>
          This isnt too hard to set up, although both people will have to use the correct software and go through a quick setup process before your conversations will be encrypted.
         </p>
         <div id="div-1342-1" style="margin-top:10px;padding-top:10px;border-top:1px solid #CCC;margin-bottom:10px;border-bottom:1px solid #CCC;">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           tmntag.adTag('div-1342-1');
          </script>
         </div>
         <h3>
          How OTR Works
         </h3>
         <div class="relatedside">
          <span class="relatedtext" style="font-family:bebas_neueregular;font-size:16px;z-index:1;position:relative;top:-7px;padding-left:10px;float:right;background-color:#FFF;font-weight:normal;color:#666;">
           RELATED ARTICLE
          </span>
          <div class="relateditemwrap" style="border-top:4px solid #CCC;border-bottom:4px solid #CCC;margin-left:6px;padding:8px;padding-left:0px;">
           <div class="relateditemimg" style="float:left;width:128px;margin-left:2px;">
            <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/33949/htg-explains-what-is-encryption-and-how-does-it-work/" style="font-style:90%;text-decoration:none;font-weight:bold;">
             <img src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/thumbcache/120/80/ba42bac9ea3e5e30e55c56a5e8fdded4/wp-content/uploads/gg/up/xsshot519296eb4f7db.png.pagespeed.ic.z-b0b9OySY.jpg"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <span>
            <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/33949/htg-explains-what-is-encryption-and-how-does-it-work/" style="font-style:90%;text-decoration:none;font-weight:bold;">
             HTG Explains: What is Encryption and How Does It Work?
            </a>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-size:90%;color:#555;">
            Encryption has a long history dating back to when the ancient Greeks and Romans sent secret messages by substituting letters only decipherable with a secret key. Join us for a quick history lesson and learn more about how encryption works.
            <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/33949/htg-explains-what-is-encryption-and-how-does-it-work/" style="font-style:90%;text-decoration:none;">
             [Read Article]
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          Like all software, OTR isnt perfect. Any vulnerability in libpurple  the mssaging library used in both Pidgin and Adium  or a vulnerability in the OTR plug-in itself could allow an attacker to compromise your secure session. If the NSA really wanted to snoop on you, its possible they already have a way to break OTR.
         </p>
         <p>
          But OTR has more uses than just hiding your conversations from the NSA. It provides an additional layer of encryption and authentication over AIM, Google Talk, ICQ, Yahoo! Messenger, MSN Messenger,or any other protocol Pidgin or Adium support. This hides what youre talking about from the instant messaging service youre using, your Internet service provider, your local network operator, and  in theory  intelligence agencies monitoring your Internet usage.
         </p>
         <p>
          OTR also provides authentication, so you have some guarantee youre talking to the actual person. Even if their account were compromised and someone else attempted to talk to you with their screen name, youd see an error because the
          <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/33949/htg-explains-what-is-encryption-and-how-does-it-work/">
           encryption
          </a>
          information wouldnt match.
         </p>
         <p>
          While OTR probably isnt perfect, it can add some additional privacy if you need to talk about sensitive matters online.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Set Up OTR
         </h3>
         <p>
          OTR is a plug-in for the Pidgin instant messenger. To use it, youll need to install
          <a href="https://pidgin.im/">
           Pidgin
          </a>
          and the
          <a href="https://otr.cypherpunks.ca/">
           Pidgin-OTR plug-in
          </a>
          . Both are available for Windows and should be in your Linux distributions software repositories. Mac OS X users will have to use
          <a href="https://adium.im/">
           Adium
          </a>
          instead.
         </p>
         <p>
          After installation, launch Pidgin and set up your accounts if you havent already. Visit the Tools &gt; Plugins menu and activate the Off-the-Record Messaging plug-in.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="enable-off-the-record-messaging-in-pidgin" border="0" height="495" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/enable-off-the-record-messaging-in-pidgin.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="enable-off-the-record-messaging-in-pidgin" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Click the Configure Plugin button to view its options. Select the account you want to chat privately with and click the Generate button to create a private key for that specific account. This key will be used to encrypt your messages.
         </p>
         <p>
          Youll need to generate keys separately for each account if you want to use OTR with multiple accounts.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="generate-otr-encryption-key-in-pidgin" border="0" height="500" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/generate-otr-encryption-key-in-pidgin.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="generate-otr-encryption-key-in-pidgin" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          If the person you want to talk to doesnt have OTR set up yet, theyll need to go through this process on their own computer to set up their software and generate a private key.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Initiate a Private Conversation
         </h3>
         <p>
          Next, open a conversation window with the person you want to talk to. Youll see an OTR button saying Not private if a conversation isnt secured with OTR. Click the button and select Start private conversation to begin.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="start-private-conversation-with-otr-in-pidgin[6]" border="0" height="389" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/start-private-conversation-with-otr-in-pidgin6.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="start-private-conversation-with-otr-in-pidgin[6]" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Youll now see a message saying the session is secured with encryption, but that your buddy hasnt been verified. If this doesnt work, your buddy likely doesnt have OTR set up and configured properly.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pidgin-otr-unverified-private-conversation-started" border="0" height="338" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/pidgin-otr-unverified-private-conversation-started.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="pidgin-otr-unverified-private-conversation-started" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Authenticate Your Buddy
         </h3>
         <p>
          Youll now want to authenticate, or verify, your buddy. To start this process, click the OTR button again and select Authenticate buddy.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pidgin-otr-authenticate-buddy" border="0" height="338" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/pidgin-otr-authenticate-buddy.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="pidgin-otr-authenticate-buddy" width="649"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Select Question and answer, Shared secret, or Manual fingerprint verification. The idea here is that youre verifying the person youve connected to is actually your buddy and not an imposter. For example, you could meet in person ahead of time and choose a secret phrase youll use later or ask a question only they would know.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pidgin-otr-authentication-methods" border="0" height="466" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/pidgin-otr-authentication-methods.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="pidgin-otr-authentication-methods" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Your buddy will see the authentication prompt and will have to respond with the exact message you typed. Its case-sensitive.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pidgin-otr-authentication-prompt-linux[4]" border="0" height="333" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/pidgin-otr-authentication-prompt-linux4.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="pidgin-otr-authentication-prompt-linux[4]" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Once authentication is complete, the status of your conversation will change from Unverified to Private.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pidgin-otr-authentication-successful" border="0" height="401" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/pidgin-otr-authentication-successful.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="pidgin-otr-authentication-successful" width="649"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Known Key Fingerprints
         </h3>
         <p>
          The OTR plug-in will now remember your buddys key fingerprint. The next time you connect to that buddy, it will check that theyre using the same key and automatically verify them. If someone else compromises their account and attempts to connect with a different key fingerprint, youll know about it.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pidgin-otr-known-key-fingerprints" border="0" height="434" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/pidgin-otr-known-key-fingerprints.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="pidgin-otr-known-key-fingerprints" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Make Future Conversations Private
         </h3>
         <p>
          The plug-in should now automatically initiate a secure conversation with your buddy each time you talk to them.
         </p>
         <p>
          Note that the first message sent and received in each conversation is sent unencrypted! The secure conversation is only initiated after the message is sent. For this reason, its a good idea to start conversations with a quick greeting like Hi. Dont start a conversation with something sensitive, like Lets protest at [location] or by revealing a sensitive business secret.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="pidgin-otr-first-message-unencrypted" border="0" height="309" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/pidgin-otr-first-message-unencrypted.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="pidgin-otr-first-message-unencrypted" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          OTR probably isnt necessary for the vast majority of conversations, but it provides some additional privacy when you need to talk about something sensitive. It should work well enough, but we should all probably assume there are security holes somewhere in Pidgin or the OTR plug-in that intelligence agencies could take advantage of, just like there are in all pieces of software.
         </p>
         <p>
          Of course, using OTR will always be more private than talking in clear text! (Unless the NSA begins paying more attention to you when they see youre using encryption software, which is also a possibility.)
         </p>
