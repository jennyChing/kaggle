         Krysten Ritter, who played Jesse Pinkman's ill-fated girlfriend on AMC's now-classic crime show Breaking Bad, is playing the lead. Mike Colter will play Luke Cage, David Tennant will play Kilgrave and Rachael Taylor has been cast as Patricia Walker in the upcoming series.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         "AKA Jessica Jones." is based on Brian Michael Bendis and Michael Baydos' comic series Alias. Jessica Campbell Jones Cage made her first appearance in the series Alias. Jones is a former superhero who suffers from Post Traumatic Stress Disorder. She opens a detective agency called Alias Private Investigations. The embittered former superheroine used the aliases Jewel, Knightress, and Power Woman.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         According to the Jessica Jones character breakdown, Carrie-Anne's character is a "a no-nonsense woman who could prove a powerful ally to Jessica (Krysten Ritter) ...if Jessica doesn't completely alienate her first."
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         "AKA Jessica Jones" is one of four series that will debut on Netflix that centers Marvel heroes. Netflix committed to at least four 13-episode series that will start streaming in 2015. The first Netflix Marvel series will be "Daredevil," followed by "Jessica Jones." Netflix's partnership with Marvel hopes to bring a new aesthetic to the Marvel Cinematic Universe by focusing on street-level heroes. The show promises to be different from other Marvel TV Shows like Agents of S.H.E.I.L.D. and Agent Carter.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         "'A.K.A. Jessica Jones'- Working as a private investigator in New York's Hell's Kitchen, a troubled ex-superhero's past comes back to haunt her in the live-action series, 'Marvel's A.K.A. Jessica Jones,'" the teaser reads.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         Netflix Chief Content Officer Ted Sarandos recently explained the status and release of AKA Jessica Jones, Luke Cage, Iron Fist, and The Defenders, as to IGN at the Television Critics Association Press Tour, saying "You should think of it as about roughly a year. Some will roll in as early as eight months and others as 15 months apart, and basically what it is is we're not trying to meet a fall programming schedule, or trying to hit a grid number, so really we want to give the shows enough time.
