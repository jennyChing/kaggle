                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Pimm's Trifle + 13 Ways with Pimms
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1823889573256874127" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/fruitcakey/8612371449/" title="pimms-trifle-1 by spicyicecream, on Flickr">
                      <img alt="pimms-trifle-1" class="" height="667" id="blogsy-1367139716039.2407" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8116/8612371449_efb09ddce3_o.jpg" width="500"/>

                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Hello hello! I was a little bit busy/slack with scheduling posts before I left for an amazing week in Malaysia, but I really wanted to share this recipe with you.If you've been reading for a while, you'll already know how I feel about Pimm's.It's the perfect summery drink for sharing with the girls, but I also love experimenting with it in desserts too, which inspired this boozy little roundup. If you haven't had Pimm's before, I hope these ideas can persuade you to try it soon!

                     <h3>

                      </span>

                     <h3>
                      <span style="color: #b45f06;">

                     </h3>
                     <div>
                      <span style="color: #b45f06;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/fruitcakey/8612318625/" style="-webkit-composition-fill-color: rgba(175, 192, 227, 0.230469); -webkit-composition-frame-color: rgba(77, 128, 180, 0.230469); -webkit-tap-highlight-color: rgba(26, 26, 26, 0.296875);" title="13 Ways with Pimms by spicyicecream, on Flickr">
                      <img alt="13 Ways with Pimms" class="" height="332" id="blogsy-1367139716016.8508" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8239/8612318625_00bc6a3cc2_o.jpg" width="500"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <em style="-webkit-composition-fill-color: rgba(175, 192, 227, 0.230469); -webkit-composition-frame-color: rgba(77, 128, 180, 0.230469); -webkit-tap-highlight-color: rgba(26, 26, 26, 0.296875);">

                      <a href="http://www.spicyicecream.com.au/2010/11/pimms-cocktail.html" target="_blank">
                       Spicy Icecream
                      </a>

                       Ichigo Shortcake
                      </a>
                     </em>
                     <br/>
                     <em>
                      <br style="-webkit-composition-fill-color: rgba(175, 192, 227, 0.230469); -webkit-composition-frame-color: rgba(77, 128, 180, 0.230469); -webkit-tap-highlight-color: rgba(26, 26, 26, 0.296875);"/>

                      1. If you haven't tried Pimm's before, the first step is to make a
                      <strong>
                       <a href="http://www.spicyicecream.com.au/2010/11/pimms-cocktail.html" target="_blank">
                        Pimm's Cup cocktail
                       </a>
                      </strong>
                      with all the traditional accompaniments - strawberries, lemon, cucumber and mint leaves, mixed with lemonade or ginger ale. It's one of my favourite cocktails, especially on a warm summer day. Take a sip and pretend you're at a garden party.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="-webkit-composition-fill-color: rgba(175, 192, 227, 0.230469); -webkit-composition-frame-color: rgba(77, 128, 180, 0.230469); -webkit-tap-highlight-color: rgba(26, 26, 26, 0.296875);">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="-webkit-composition-fill-color: rgba(175, 192, 227, 0.230469); -webkit-composition-frame-color: rgba(77, 128, 180, 0.230469); -webkit-tap-highlight-color: rgba(26, 26, 26, 0.296875);">
                      2. I also love Ichigo Shortcake's Christmassy adaptation of the traditional Pimm's, to become a
                      <a href="http://www.ichigoshortcake.com/2011/12/pimms-cup-with-a-gingery-twist-merry-christmas.html" style="font-weight: bold;" target="_blank" title="">
                       Gingery Pimm's Cup
                      </a>
                      . Yum! I think it would go down a treat at a summery Aussie Christmas, especially with the addition of seasonal strawberries and blueberries!
                     </span>
                     <span style="-webkit-composition-fill-color: rgba(175, 192, 227, 0.230469); -webkit-composition-frame-color: rgba(77, 128, 180, 0.230469); -webkit-tap-highlight-color: rgba(26, 26, 26, 0.296875);">

                      <a href="http://bronmarshall.com/2012/christmas/pimms_cup_no1_granita.html" target="_blank" title="">
                       Bron Marshall
                      </a>
                      &amp;
                      <a href="http://www.womanandhome.com/recipes/291514/orange-cheesecake-with-strawberry-and-pimms-jelly-and-strawberry-and-mint-salad" target="_blank" title="">
                       Woman and Home
                      </a>
                     </em>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     9. This
                     <strong>
                      <a href="http://bronmarshall.com/2012/christmas/pimms_cup_no1_granita.html" target="_blank" title="">
                       Pimm's Granita
                      </a>
                     </strong>
                     from Bron Marshall looks so beautiful and refreshing! I had my fair share of crushed ice desserts in Malaysia and they will always be a favourite on a stinking hot day! And of course, being me, I love the idea of boozing it up a little with a splash of Pimm's!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     10. This beautiful
                     <strong>
                      <a href="http://www.womanandhome.com/recipes/291514/orange-cheesecake-with-strawberry-and-pimms-jelly-and-strawberry-and-mint-salad" target="_blank" title="">
                       Orange Cheesecake with Strawberry Pimm's Jelly
                      </a>
                     </strong>
                     is courtesy of Woman and Home. I can just imagine how good this would taste, especially with the little strawberry and mint salad that accompanies it! This would make an elegant dinner party dessert, or served individually for a ladies high tea.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/fruitcakey/8613316304/" title="13 Ways with Pimms by spicyicecream, on Flickr">
                      <img alt="13 Ways with Pimms" class="" height="332" id="blogsy-1367139715969.4968" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8381/8613316304_8b72485352_o.jpg" width="500"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <em>
                      Picture Credits:
                      <a href="http://thedesignfiles.net/2012/12/tasty-tuesday-cucumber-apple-mint-lime-pop-with-pimms/" target="_blank" title="">
                       The Design Files
                      </a>
                      &amp;
                      <a href="http://www.spicyicecream.com.au/2011/10/pimms-jelly.html" target="_blank" title="">
                       Spicyicecream
                      </a>
                     </em>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     11. A
                     <strong>
                      <a href="http://thedesignfiles.net/2012/12/tasty-tuesday-cucumber-apple-mint-lime-pop-with-pimms/" target="_blank" title="">
                       Cucumber, Apple, Mint &amp; Lime Popsicle
                      </a>
                     </strong>
                     may not be everyone's cup of tea, but imagine how good it would taste dunked into a fruity glass of Pimms as it slowly melts. The smart cookies from Liana Raine came up with this stunning combination for one of my favourite blogs, The Design Files. A delicious grownup treat, yes please!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     12. One of my earliest forays into cooking with Pimm's was these
                     <strong>
                      <a href="http://www.spicyicecream.com.au/2011/10/pimms-jelly.html" target="_blank" title="">
                       Pimm's Jellies
                      </a>
                      ,
                     </strong>
                     adapted from Jamie Oliver, and which I've also used as a component in the trifles below. This recipe actually became one of my most popular posts, and for good reason too, it was delicious.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/fruitcakey/8613478168/" title="pimms-trifle-2 by spicyicecream, on Flickr">
                      <img alt="pimms-trifle-2" class="" height="667" id="blogsy-1367139715990.3608" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8256/8613478168_b25a68c771_o.jpg" width="500"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     13. And finally, my latest creation - the
                     <strong>
                      Pimm's Trifle!
                     </strong>
                     From the bottom up - Pimm's jelly, sponge cake soaked in a lemon, lime &amp; Pimm's syrup, topped with fresh strawberries and a ginger and vanilla custard. Get all the elements together in the one spoonful and it's delicious!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I would have loved to make a ginger sponge cake from scratch, but I ran out of time and used a leftover store bought sponge, which worked well anyway. Baking sponge cakes makes me nervous, and you only need a small amount for these trifles, so the store bought cake worked well for me. The recipe below might seem like a lot of work, but as with most trifles, most of the elements can be made in advance, and refrigerated until ready to serve.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     After a stinking hot (but totally amazing) week in Malaysia, I was so happy to come home to a warm sunny and not humid day! I think these trifles are the perfect goodbye to what has been a fabulous summer! I've been editing photos all day, so stay tuned for a recap of what we got up to in Kuala Lumpur, Ipoh and Penang coming soon!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <strong>
                      Pimm's Trifle
                     </strong>
                     <br/>
                     Serves 4
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      1. Pimm's Jelly
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="-webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0);">
                       4g powdered gelatine
                      <br/>
                       75ml Pimms
                      <br/>
                       225ml Lemonade or Ginger Beer
                      <br/>
                       2 tablespoons sugar (optional)
                      <br/>
                       2-3 mint sprigs, leaves picked
                      <br/>
                       4-6 strawberries, finely chopped
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="-webkit-text-size-adjust: auto; background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0);">
                      <strong>
                       To make Pimm's Jelly
                      </strong>
                      , sprinkle the gelatine over 1 tablespoon of water in a small bowl to soften. Gently heat the Pimms, lemonade, sugar if using and mint sprigs in a saucepan until sugar is dissolved and the mixture is just starting to steam. Do not allow it to boil. Remove the mint stalks, add softened gelatine and whisk until dissolved.Remove from the heat and leave to cool. Divide the strawberries between 4 serving glasses and then add the Pimms mixture evenly. Chill in the fridge for at least 2 hours.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      2. Sponge Cake
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     Use your favourite sponge cake recipe or a quality store-bought one. Cut small cubes of the cake and place carefully on top of set jelly layer in the serving glasses.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      3. Lemon, Lime &amp; Pimm's Syrup
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                      1/2 cup sugar
                     <br/>
                      1/2 cup water
                     <br/>
                      1 cup Pimm's
                     <br/>
                      Zest of 1 lemon &amp; 1 lime, removed with a vegetable peeler
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      To make the syrup,
                     </b>
                     place sugar and water in a small saucepan. Bring to boil and stir to dissolve sugar.
Add Pimms and stir, and return to boil. Add lemon and lime zest. Boil, stirring occasionally, until mixture is reduced to 3/4 cup in volume.
Refrigerate until ready to use. Drizzle syrup over sponge cake until soaked. You may have a little syrup leftover which is great on ice cream. It will keep for several weeks in a bottle or air-tight container in the fridge.
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <b>
                      4. Fresh strawberries
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      Place a layer of fresh hulled and sliced strawberries on top of the soaked sponge cake.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       5. Ginger Custard
                      </b>
                     </div>
                      420ml pouring (single) cream
                     <br/>
                       vanilla bean, split and seeds scraped
                     <br/>
                      Thumb sized piece of ginger, sliced
                     <br/>
                      5 egg yolks
                     <br/>
                      60g caster sugar
                     <br/>
                      1 tablespoon cornflour
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      To make the custard
                     </b>
                     , place cream and vanilla bean seeds and ginger in a small saucepan over medium heat until it just comes to the boil. Cover and allow to infuse for at least 20 minutes. Place the egg yolks, sugar and cornflour in a bowl and whisk to combine. Slowly pour the warm cream mixture over the eggs and whisk to combine. Return mixture to the saucepan and stir over low heat for 6-8 minutes or until mixture is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Remove the vanilla bean and ginger and allow custard to cool completely. When cool, spoon custard on top of trifles. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       6. To serve
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      Garnish with more sliced strawberries and cucumbers.
                     </div>
