        <p>
         Windows 8 does pretty good job introducing metro apps(now called as modern apps), but as always it also does a good job hiding the way to see the used space and size of metro apps in both windows 8 &amp; windows 8.1. Actually this option is buried deep inside windows 8 PC settings, So read on to find out how much space are your apps taking up on your hard drive
        </p>
        <div style="width: auto; min-height: 70px; margin-top: -17px; text-align:center;">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-8949749535570321" data-ad-slot="3707208174" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
         </ins>

          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <h3>
         Size Of Metro Apps In Windows 8
        </h3>
        <p>
         This is pretty simple, First Of all you have to access PC settings: To do that press WIN + I and select Change PC Settings.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="windows-8-settings" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1579" height="282" src="http://www.stugon.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/windows-8-settings.png" width="346"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now If you are using Windows 8, Select General and scroll down on the right hand side until you see the App Sizes button. Clicking on this will bring up a dialog box with all the app sizes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now if you are using Windows 8.1, pressing WIN + I opens up the windows 8.1 control panel, Here select Search &amp; Apps -&gt; App Sizes. This will automatically determine the sizes of all the apps installed in your PC and will show you the same.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.stugon.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/windows-8-app-size.png" target="_blank">
          <img alt="windows-8-app-size" class="aligncenter wp-image-1580" height="403" src="http://www.stugon.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/windows-8-app-size-1024x575.png" width="717"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats all there is to do, Now as you know the sizes of all the installed apps  You can manage the hard drive space more efficiently.
        </p>
