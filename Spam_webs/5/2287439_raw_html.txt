        <h2>
         Put Your Teamwork To Work
        </h2>
        <table align="right" border="0" cellpadding="10" cellspacing="1" style="width: 300px;">
         <tbody>
          <tr>
           <td>
            <h4>
             "Never doubt that a small group ofthoughtful,committed citizenscan change the world; indeed, it's the only thing that ever has."
            </h4>
            <h4 style="text-align: right;">
             - Margaret Mead
            </h4>
           </td>
          </tr>
         </tbody>
        </table>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         As we wrap up another great season of hockey and step out of our arenas into spring, dont let that last over-the-shoulder wave be the last time you see your teammates until the next puck drops in September. You have been working shoulder to shoulder with this group for months, working towards a common goal. You have a history, camaraderie, trust, spirit, drive and you have something else - a community that you all share. A community that helps make it possible for you to play rec hockey at all. If you have ever thought that it might be good to volunteer more, get more involved or give back in some way to help make a positive impact on your community, imagine what a team of you working together could accomplish? Grab the team email list and suggest some ways you can make use of the summer months and affect change together. (Who knows, getting the team name and players out there might present some sponsorship or new player opportunities, too!)
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Here are some suggestions for how your team can give back to the community between games:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <img alt="Teamwork for hockey players on and off the ice" src="/Userfiles/Images/BlogPosts/teamwork-383939_1280.jpg" style="width: 300px; height: 197px; margin-left: 10px; margin-right: 10px; float: right;"/>
          More hockey
         </strong>
          is there an unused tennis court in the local park? A gym that sits empty while school is out? A dead end street in need of a little life? Look into organizing some regular shinny or pick-up hockey games, talk to the city, the school or the neighbours and see if it is ok to put up some nets and some posters to promote getting active on a particular day each week. Or find out about using the local park or sports field for informal conditioning, training and off-ice drills. Hockey communities are healthy communities, show your neighbours you care about keeping them and their kids active too.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Spring Cleaning
         </strong>
          every spring when the snow melts every community has the same problem, garbage that was hidden and built up over the winter is now everywhere. Join in or organize a community clean up with your team, think about adopting a road or a park in your teams name and keep it beautiful all year long.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Keep active for a cause
         </strong>
          they say staying active is easier when you have a buddy (or buddies!) and there are lots of charity runs and events that allow you to enter as a team. Pick a charity run that resonates with your group and push each other to train hard and fundraise harder! If you can get on the fundraising leaderboard it is some great exposure for your team!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Fill up a food bank
         </strong>
          food banks tend to drop off everyones radars it the summer months but the need doesnt go away. They still need volunteers, they still need donations and they still need awareness as people need to eat year-round. Think about organizing an event, doing collections through your work places and getting together to help sort or organize as a team.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         What does your community need? Who in your community needs the most help? How can you and your team members make a difference? You are a great team on the ice; you can be a great team off the ice too.
        </p>
