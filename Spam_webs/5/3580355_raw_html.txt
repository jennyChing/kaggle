          <h1 class="title" id="page-title">
           Guitar Warm-up
          </h1>
          <div class="tabs">
          </div>
          <div class="region region-content">
           <div class="block block--managed block-even" id="block--managed-4">
            <div class="content">
             <div style="text-align:left">
              <div class="adsense" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px;">
               <script type="text/javascript">
                &lt;!--

           <div class="block block-system block-odd" id="block-system-main">
            <div class="content">
             <article class="node node-page node-odd published with-comments clearfix" id="node-2057">
              <div class="content">
               <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
               <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                <div class="field-items">
                 <div class="field-item even">
                  <p>
                   This chapter contains short solo guitar licks that are easy to memorize in order topractice. The point is to do them with maximum accuracy.
                  </p>
                  <p class="bodyText">
                   The examples come in two shapes: as Sibelius Scorch - .sib web page where you can also listen in MIDI to the exercise (besides seeing or printing it) and in jpeg - .gif format for those who don't want to download the Scorch software.
                  </p>
                  <p class="bodyText">
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym1.gif">
                     Gym 1
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Here you'll find very simple exercises to warm up your fingers and improve their agility.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym2.gif">
                     Gym 2
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Some position changing. Try to do them as fast as possible and, of course, you may create new ones.
                  </p>
                  <p class="bodyText">
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym3.gif">
                     Gym 3
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Neo  classical licks that will mainly improve your little finger technique.
                  </p>
                  <p class="bodyText">
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym4.gif">
                     Gym 4
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Simple arpeggios. Do them with alternate picking.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym5.gif">
                     Gym 5
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Major scales runs. Increase gradually the speed until you are able to play them real fast.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym6.gif">
                     Gym 6
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Chromatic runs are a good warm-up for all four fingers of your left hand.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym7.gif">
                     Gym 7
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Other exercises that will strengthen your little finger. See the difference between linked and staccato playing.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym8.gif">
                     Gym 8
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Arpeggios played with alternate picking and strings - skipping.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym9.gif">
                     Gym 9
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Scale runs on a single string that will make your left hand move along the neck.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym10.gif">
                     Gym 10
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Pull-offs to increase the control of your little finger.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym11.gif">
                     Gym 11
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Guitarists mostly use thirds and sixths of all intervals in their solos.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym12.gif">
                     Gym 12
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Typical in-scale patterns.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym13.gif">
                     Gym 13
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   And a well-known melody with some tricky picking.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym14.gif">
                     Gym 14
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Another well-known joke with some chromatic runs.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym15.gif">
                     Gym 15
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Descending diminished arpeggio.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    <a href="/new/sites/default/files/gym16.gif">
                     Gym 16
                    </a>
                    :
                   </strong>
                   <br/>
                   Training your little finger.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   The circled numbers show the left-hand fingering: 1 - index; 2 - middle; 3 - ring; 4 - small.
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
