       <div class="pb-sig-line hasnt-headshot has-0-headshots hasnt-bio is-not-column">
        <span class="pb-byline" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
         By
         <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/people/jason-samenow">
          <span itemprop="name">
           Jason Samenow
          </span>
         </a>

         August 19, 2013
        </span>
        <span class="pb-tool email">
         <a href="mailto:jason.samenow@washpost.com?subject=Reader feedback for 'Noctilucent clouds and aurora: all in one view (VIDEO)'">
          <span class="fa fa-envelope">
          </span>

        </span>
        <span class="tweet-authors">
         <span class="pb-twitter-follow unprocessed">
          <a class="twitter-follow-button" data-lang="en" data-show-count="false" href="https://twitter.com/@capitalweather">
           Follow @capitalweather

         </span>

       <article itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         You may have seen footage of these high-altitude phenomena separately, but I doubt in the same shot. Here together are aurora AND noctilucent (or night shining) clouds!
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="343" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/E7PQbfnErEw" width="610">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         Both of the phenomena can be seen simultaneously about midway into the video.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Link:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noctilucent_cloud">
          What are noctilucent clouds?
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The spectacular time lapse is brought to you by Maciej Winiarczyk, who recorded the scene in northern Scotland August 4 and 5.
        </p>
        <p>
         Big tip of the hat to
         <a href="http://apod.nasa.gov/apod/ap130819.html">
          NASAs Astronomy Photo of the Day Web site
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Want to see another entrancing view of noctilucent clouds? Heres a compilation of time lapse views from Sweden
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://vimeo.com/72146022">
          Noctilucent clouds 2013 The Film
         </a>
         from
         <a href="http://vimeo.com/heden">
          P-M Hedn
         </a>
         on
         <a href="https://vimeo.com">
          Vimeo
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         (Hat tip:
         <a href="http://www.universetoday.com/104021/beautiful-noctilucent-clouds-2013-the-movie/">
          Universe Today
         </a>
         )
        </p>
