        <p>
         <br/>
         What is it to succeed? What is your fair share? Has someone prevented you from achieving your dreams? Funny how times have changed. Imagine fighting for a country who referred to you as, 
         <em>
          boy
         </em>
         , rather than by name. Consider you have the worn down planes and equipment. If this is not degrading enough, try being told the color of your skin does not permit you from even fighting in the war. The
         <a href="http://www.tuskegeeairmen.org/default.aspx" title="The Airmen of Tuskegee Organization Homepage">
          Airmen from Tuskegee
         </a>
         had to go deep within themselves and
         <a href="http://tollieschmidt.com/purpose-life-school-teaches/" title="The Purpose of Life that School Never Teaches">
          find their purpose
         </a>
         to fight, find their motivation to succeed.
        </p>

        </div>

        <p>
         Tuskegee Airmen refers to all who were involved in the so-called Tuskegee Experiment, the Army Air Corps program to train African Americans to fly and maintain combat aircraft. The Tuskegee Airmen included pilots, navigators, bombardiers, maintenance and support staff, instructors, and all the personnel who kept the planes in the air.
        </p>
        <p>
         Critics of the plan to put Black Americans into the cockpits of combat aircraft during World War II, stating that Blacks would be incapable of learning to fly, or would prove inept and be a liability in battle. With sheer
         <a href="http://tollieschmidt.com/the-illusion-is-the-belief-in-limits-tollies-daily-mojo/" title="The Illusion is the Belief in Limits  Tollies Daily Mojo">
          courage and determination
         </a>
         , the Tuskegee Airmen proved them wrong. The character of these men is what the term American Exceptionalism , is all about. Its not about 
         <em>
          feeling good
         </em>
         , or 
         <em>
          self-esteem
         </em>
         , its about something much deeper, how you
         <a href="http://tollieschmidt.com/evoke-powerful-emotions-choosing-words/" title="Evoke Powerful Emotions! Choosing your Words">
          communicate
         </a>
         internally in order to change your perceptions of the world.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <div class="inner">
          <p>
           We count our victories by the bombers we get to their targets, by the husbands we return to their wives, by the fathers we give back to their children. To the last minute, to the last man we fight. We fight!  Colonel A.J. Bullard
          </p>
         </div>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         If you continue to believe that the reason for your life not being exactly as you had hoped, and there must be some exterior force or person preventing you, then you are allowing 
         <em>
          excuses
         </em>
         , to stop you. Everyday we unconsciously have internal conversations with ourselves, these conversations take the form of a series of questions and answers. This is how we determine the meaning and feelings we experience behind any event or situation in life. We see how a child raised in a family where a parent took part in a extramarital affair and consistently lied about the act, grows up to have a distrust in the opposite sex which causes dysfunction in the relationship. Heres the truth, your brain does not know the difference between the truth and a vivid imagination. So YOUR, truth is merely what you choose to believe.
        </p>

        <p>
         The airmen of Tuskegee had every right to blame their situation on many factors which were stacked against them in WWII. They were segregated, they were subject to racism, they were treated like 
         <em>
          boys
         </em>
         , not men. Yet, these great men took to the skies and waited for their time, their opportunity, and when they were called to action they rose to the challenge, they knew they would succeed and win. They never allowed the excuses, racism, and external
         <a href="http://tollieschmidt.com/perseverance-imagine-being-a-teenager-and-finding-out-you-have-cancer/" title="Perseverance Imagine Being a Teenager and Finding Out You Have Cancer">
          adversity
         </a>
         prevent them from training, flying, and working towards their successful role in the war. Because of this superior mindset, the airmen from Tuskegee and the 99th Fighter Squadron 72 Tuskegee Airmen shot down 112 enemy aircraft, including the best of the German fighters.  Members of the 332d Fighter Group downed at least ten airplanes on four separate days in 1944 and 1945. Learn more about the
         <a href="http://tollieschmidt.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/112-Victories.pdf" title="Download the Tuskegee Airmen 112 WWII Victories">
          Tuskegee Victories HERE
         </a>
         .
        </p>
