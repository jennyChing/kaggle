       <p>
        Fall is officially here and its time to start thinking about an awesome school Halloween party! When my son was in 1
        <sup>
         st
        </sup>
        grade, I was a classroom parent. I got to spend the day helping the kids play tons of fun games. Now, at his school, they call it a Fall Party rather than a Halloween one, but we all knew what it really was.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="School Halloween Party Supplies at #DollarGeneral" src="http://i1109.photobucket.com/albums/h430/OurFamilyWorld/Brands/Dollar%20General/DGHalloweenDisplay_zps2ece54fd.jpg" style="width: 580px; height: 433px;"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Whether youre a classroom parent, member of the PTA, homeschooler or just a mom who wants to throw a fun Halloween party for kids,
        <a href="http://app.socialspark.com/clicks?lid=37036&amp;oid=13132494" rel="nofollow">
         Dollar General
        </a>
        has all your needs covered. As always, I went there with a general (pun intended) idea of what I wanted to do, then browsed their Halloween section to really finalize my plans.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         <strong>
          DIY Halloween Pumpkin Toss Game
         </strong>
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="School Halloween Party Pumpkin Toss Game" src="http://i1109.photobucket.com/albums/h430/OurFamilyWorld/Brands/Dollar%20General/DGHalloweenPumpkinToss2_zps161dfe98.jpg" style="width: 580px; height: 870px;"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Lets start with a fun game, shall we? After all, you cant have a school Halloween party without something to keep the kids busy! This game is incredibly easy to make. The fact that I made it should tell you that!
       </p>
       <p>
        What you need:
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         A large cardboard box (I used a flat box that had an art print shipped in it)
        </li>
        <li>
         Black poster board
        </li>
        <li>
         Package of fake spider web material
        </li>
        <li>
         A stuffed pumpkin
        </li>
        <li>
         Box cutter
        </li>
        <li>
         Black tape (like duct tape)
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        I got all of my supplies, except the cardboard box and box cutter, at Dollar General.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         How to Make it:
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Cut your box to fit the poster board, leaving a bit on the side to fold back and help it stand up.
        </li>
        <li>
         Tape the poster board to the cardboard using the black tape.
        </li>
        <li>
         Use the box cutter to cut holes. Dont worry if theyre not perfect circles, no one is judging you here!
        </li>
        <li>
         Stretch the web across the board to make it look spooky. Also, I went a little heavier on the places that I made mistakes! Shhh, no one has to know. Make sure you stretch the webbing around the holes so theyre not totally blocked.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        To play the game, have the kids stand back and try to toss the little stuffed pumpkin through the holes. Thats pretty much it!
       </p>
       <h3>
        <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         <strong>
          Fun school Halloween party supplies at Dollar General
         </strong>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <img alt="Fun Halloween Party Supplies &amp; Pumpkin Toss Game #DollarGeneral" src="http://i1109.photobucket.com/albums/h430/OurFamilyWorld/Brands/Dollar%20General/DGHalloweenSupplies_zpsec0d906b.jpg" style="width: 580px; height: 813px;"/>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        A party isnt a party without some fun themed supplies, right? I found some great items at Dollar General, including a really cute plastic tablecloth, some Halloween sticker treats to give out as prizes and little Halloween baskets for kids to keep their goodies in. The best part? Nothing that I bought cost more than $1 each.
       </p>
       <p>
        Wondering why there are rolls of toilet paper? No, theyre not for TP-ing the principals office, although.no, that wouldnt be a good idea, now would it? Use the rolls of toilet paper to let the kids wrap each other up like mummies! The catch? The wrapper has to be blindfolded!
       </p>
       <p>
        Head to your local Dollar General and check out their Halloween section. You never know what kind of inspiration will strike!
