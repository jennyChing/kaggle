     <p>
      <b>
       <i>
        cis
       </i>
       -3-Hexen-1-ol
      </b>
      , also known as
      <b>
       (
       <i>
        Z
       </i>
       )-3-hexen-1-ol
      </b>
      and
      <b>
       leaf alcohol
      </b>
      , is a colorless oily liquid with an intense grassy-green
      <a href="/wiki/Odor" title="Odor">
       odor
      </a>
      of freshly cut green
      <a href="/wiki/Grass" title="Grass">
       grass
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">
       leaves
      </a>
      . It is produced in small amounts by most
      <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">
       plants
      </a>
      and it acts as an
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Attractant&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Attractant (page does not exist)">
       attractant
      </a>
      to many
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Predator" title="Predator">
       predatory
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">
       insects
      </a>
      .
      <i>
       cis
      </i>
      -3-Hexen-1-ol is a very important
      <a href="/wiki/Aroma_compound" title="Aroma compound">
       aroma compound
      </a>
      that is used in fruit and vegetable
      <a href="/wiki/Flavor" title="Flavor">
       flavors
      </a>
      and in
      <a href="/wiki/Perfume" title="Perfume">
       perfumes
      </a>
      . The yearly production is about 30
      <a href="/wiki/Tonne" title="Tonne">
       tonnes
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       cis
      </i>
      -3-Hexen-1-ol is an
      <a href="/wiki/Alcohol" title="Alcohol">
       alcohol
      </a>
      and its
      <a href="/wiki/Ester" title="Ester">
       esters
      </a>
      are also important flavor and fragrance raw materials. The related
      <a href="/wiki/Aldehyde" title="Aldehyde">
       aldehyde
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Cis-3-Hexenal" title="Cis-3-Hexenal">
       <i>
        cis
       </i>
       -3-hexenal
      </a>
      (leaf aldehyde) has a similar and even stronger smell but is relatively unstable and
      <a href="/wiki/Isomerization" title="Isomerization">
       isomerizes
      </a>
      into the
      <a href="/wiki/Conjugated_system" title="Conjugated system">
       conjugated
      </a>
      <i>
       trans
      </i>
      -2-hexenal.
     </p>
