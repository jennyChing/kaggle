        <h1>
         How to pronounce Russian consonants
        </h1>
        <h3>
         Part 1: Basic rules
        </h3>
        <p>
         <font size="2">
          In this lesson you will learn 21 consonant letters of the
          Russian alphabet (
          <strong>
           , , , , , , , , , , , , , , , , , , , , 
          </strong>
          ) and how to pronounce them.
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="2">
          <br/>
          With few exceptions, Russian consonants can be pronounced either "soft" or
    "hard" depending on the type of a letter that comes after them. For this reason,
    the 20 consonant letters of the Russian alphabet can designate 37 distinct consonant
    sounds!
         </font>
        </p>
        <h2>
         "Soft" and "hard" consonants
        </h2>
        <p>
         <font size="2">
          The basic rule is that consonants are "soft" if
    they are followed by
          <a href="aa032301b.shtml">
           soft-indicating vowels
          </a>
          or the soft sign.
    Consonants are "hard" when they are followed by
          <a href="aa032301a.shtml">
           hard-indicating
  vowels
          </a>
          or the hard sign. You may want to
          <span lang="en-us">
           review
          </span>
          <span lang="en-us">
           a
          </span>
          lesson on
          <a href="aa032301a.shtml">
           Russian
    vowels
          </a>
          before you proceed any further.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font size="2">
          The concept of "soft" and "hard"
    consonants should be familiar to you if you speak English. The following example
          <span lang="en-us">
           roughly
          </span>
          shows
    that the letters
          <strong>
           m
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           l
          </strong>
          can be both "hard"
    and "soft" in English. The word "soft" here means that your tongue
    should be arched against the roof of your mouth (called the soft palate) so that it
    "softens" the sound.
         </font>
        </p>

        <h2>
         Voiced and voiceless consonants
        </h2>
        <p>
         <font size="2">
          Just like in English, several Russian consonants come in
    pairs where one letter is pronounced voiced and another one is similar but voiceless. For
    example, the English letters
          <strong>
           b
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           p
          </strong>
          make the pair (try
    saing
          <strong>
           bat - pat
          </strong>
          to feel the difference). The word "voiced" here
    means that your vocal chords should vibrate when making a sound. Vibration of vocal chords
    is the main factor in grouping Russian consonants into voiced and voiceless.
         </font>
        </p>

         <font size="2">
          Now you are ready for the important rule
    that Russian voiced consonants are pronounced as their voicless counterparts when they
    appear in the the very end of the word. For instance, the word
          <strong>

         </font>
         <strong>
          <font color="#CE0000">

         </strong>
         <font size="2">

    is actually pronounced as
          <strong>
           
          </strong>
         </font>
         <strong>
          <font color="#CE0000">
           
          </font>
         </strong>
         <font size="2">
          , the word
          <strong>
           
          </strong>
         </font>
         <strong>
          <font color="#CE0000">
           
          </font>
         </strong>
         <font size="2">
          (eye)
    sounds like
          <strong>

          <strong>
           
          </strong>
         </font>
         <strong>
          <font color="#CE0000">
           
          </font>
         </strong>
         <font size="2">
          , etc.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font size="2">
          Another rule is that voiced consonants become voiceless
    when they are followed by other voiceless consonants. For example, the word
          <strong>
           
          </strong>
         </font>
         <strong>
          <font color="#CE0000">
           
          </font>
          <font size="2">
           
          </font>
         </strong>
         <font size="2">
          (signature) sounds like
          <strong>
           
          </strong>
         </font>
         <strong>
          <font color="#CE0000">
           
          </font>
          <font size="2">
           
          </font>
         </strong>
         <font size="2">
          because the voiced letter
          <strong>
           
          </strong>
          is
    followed by the voiceless
          <strong>
           
          </strong>
          .
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font size="2">
          On the other hand, voiceless consonants sound voiced when
    they are followed by voiced consonants. For example, the word

         </strong>
