  <meta content="Michael Kusz is a Yorkshire based sculptor who creates his sculptures using 
recycled materials. His pieces range from quirky constructions to cast 
bronze sculptures, from butterflies to dragons and from small to life-size 
dimensions.

The quirky birds are my favourite, especially the first picture (above) 
which has an air of Professor Yaffle about it. Now I'm showing my age, but 
if you remember Bagpuss then you'll know what I'm talking about!

Michael predominantly works with copper because it has such fabulous 
qualities. It can be bent, bashed, stretched, shrunk, shaped and cast. In 
fact he can do almost anything with it.

Michael also loves the colour properties of copper. It can take up to 90 
years outside for it to turn from a bright shiny copper colour to verdigris 
green so depending on where the material has come from he can create some 
fantastic pieces with really interesting colour textures.

Michael is often commissioned to make larger pieces but he also makes and 
sells pieces that are smaller and readily available, like these &quot;Love 
Bats&quot;...

Here's some more of Michael's commissioned work. The Herons I think would 
look quite realistic next to a large informal pond...

If you're interested to learn more about Michael and to see more pictures 
of his work, these can be found on the Graculus Sculptures website.

             <h1 class="entry-title" data-content-field="title">
              <a href="/blog/blog/metal-garden-sculpture-by-michael-kusz">
               Metal garden sculpture by Michael Kusz
              </a>
             </h1>
             <span class="article-dateline">
              <span class="date">
               <a href="/blog/blog/metal-garden-sculpture-by-michael-kusz">
                <time class="published" datetime="2013-01-07">
                 January 07, 2013
                </time>
               </a>
               <span class="delimiter">
                /
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
             <span class="article-byline">
              <span class="author">
               <a href="/blog/?author=53995bbbe4b05ebd6aba849b" rel="author">
                Lisa Cox
               </a>
              </span>
             </span>
            </header>
            <!--POST BODY-->
            <div class="body entry-content">
             <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" id="item-5413692ee4b094b0b04de2ed">
              <div class="row sqs-row">
               <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
                <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-5413692ee4b094b0b04de2ec">
                 <div class="sqs-block-content">
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17507">
                    <img alt="Graculus bird sculpture by Michael Kusz" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17507" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d3299/1357324625000/Graculus-bird-sculpture-by-Michael-Kusz.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                   Michael Kusz is a Yorkshire based sculptor who creates his sculptures using recycled materials. His pieces range from quirky constructions to cast bronze sculptures, from butterflies to dragons and from small to life-size dimensions.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17505">
                    <img alt="Bolton Rooks sculpture by Michael Kusz" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-17505" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d3293/1357324621000/Bolton-Rooks-sculpture-by-Michael-Kusz.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   The quirky birds are my favourite, especially the first picture (above) which has an air of Professor Yaffle about it. Now I'm showing my age, but if you remember Bagpuss then you'll know what I'm talking about!
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17504">
                    <img alt="Boiler broiler sculpture by Graculus Sculptures" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17504" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d3290/1357324619000/Boiler-broiler-sculpture-by-Graculus-Sculptures.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Michael predominantly works with copper because it has such fabulous qualities. It can be bent, bashed, stretched, shrunk, shaped and cast. In fact he can do almost anything with it.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17506">
                    <img alt="Duck sculpture by Michael Kusz" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17506" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d3296/1357324623000/Duck-sculpture-by-Michael-Kusz.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Michael also loves the colour properties of copper. It can take up to 90 years outside for it to turn from a bright shiny copper colour to verdigris green so depending on where the material has come from he can create some fantastic pieces with really interesting colour textures.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17510">
                    <img alt="King of the Castle sculpture by Michael Kusz" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17510" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d32a2/1357324632000/King-of-the-Castle-sculpture-by-Michael-Kusz.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Michael is often commissioned to make larger pieces but he also makes and sells pieces that are smaller and readily available, like these "Love Bats"...
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17536">
                    <img alt="Love bat by Michael Kusz" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17536" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d32b1/1357478667000/Love-bat-by-Michael-Kusz.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Here's some more of Michael's commissioned work. The Herons I think would look quite realistic next to a large informal pond...
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17509">
                    <img alt="Heron sculpture by Michael Kusz" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17509" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d329f/1357324629000/Heron-sculpture-by-Michael-Kusz.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17508">
                    <img alt="Heron by Graculus sculptures" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17508" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d329c/1357324627000/Heron-by-Graculus-sculptures.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="/" rel="attachment wp-att-17503">
                    <img alt="Bird sculptures by Michael Kusz" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17503" src="http://static.squarespace.com/static/539954d3e4b05f207a4595f6/54136350e4b094b0b04cf3cc/54136390e4b094b0b04d328d/1357324617000/Bird-sculptures-by-Michael-Kusz.jpg?format=original"/>
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   If you're interested to learn more about Michael and to see more pictures of his work, these can be found on the
                   <a href="http://www.graculus.co.uk/" target="_blank" title="Graculus Sculptures">
                    Graculus Sculptures website
                   </a>
                   .
                  </p>
                  <p>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Images: Graculus Sculptures
                  </p>
