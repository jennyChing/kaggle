        <h4>
         Its 2:30am, Lachlan is crying for his late night feeding, but I need to change his diaper first.
        </h4>
        <p>
         My ONLY goal is to get him changed as quickly as humanly possible so that he doesnt wake his older brother, Will.  Ive gotten pretty good at the sub one-minute pit stop/change.  But where I fall down is getting the dirty diaper actually
         <i>
          into
         </i>
         the diaper pail.  Ive been using the Diaper Genie since William was an infant, and, while it does a really good job of locking smelly diapers away, its not always the most user-friendly contraption.  So, more often than not, the midnight diaper change sits on top of the diaper pail until morning.  In all honesty, its not uncommon for that to happen during the day too, since I am usually holding Lachlan when I try to put the diaper in the pail.  There are only so many seconds I can fiddle with something thats low to the ground before my knees start to hurt and back tweaks because Ive got an 18 pound infant wiggling away in my other arm.
        </p>
        <h4>
         About a month ago, the kind folks at Munchkin gave me a brand new one-hand diaper pail to test.
        </h4>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_6839" style="width: 310px">
         <a href="http://www.getbuttonedup.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/DSC02587.JPG">
          <img alt="The new Munchkin Diaper Pail Waiting to Be Tested" class="size-medium wp-image-6839" height="200" src="http://www.getbuttonedup.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/DSC02587-300x200.jpg" title="DSC02587" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The new Arm &amp; HammerTM Diaper Pail by Munchkin Waiting to Be Tested
         </p>
        </div>
        <h4>
         Like the Diaper Genie, this new Arm &amp; HammerTM Diaper Pail by Munchkin seals away smelly diapers so you dont get a blast of nastiness when you open it.  But its different on a few fronts:
        </h4>
        <p>
         <b>
           You can truly open and close it with one hand.
         </b>
         The lid opens right up at the push of a button.  Ive been using it for a week now and not once have I had to leave a dirty diaper on top of the pail until I had two free hands to deal with it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
           The seal is activated simply by closing the lid.
         </b>
         You dont have to twist anything to make it work.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
           There arent any fake scents used to cover up dirty diaper smells.
         </b>
         Instead the pail uses natural baking soda to do the dirty work.  Each time you close the lid, a little bit of baking soda pours onto the top of the bag.  Thats it, and it works like a charm.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
           The bag extraction process is SO easy!
         </b>
         I have to cop to the fact that I used to delegate the swap out of the bag for my old diaper pail to my husband because I found it so frustrating.  You had to hold down a cut button and twist the top around and whenever I did that, it never cut enough, so bits of the bag would get stuck in the top and Id have to rip it by hand.  Plus you had to tie tops and bottoms.  In short, it was a pain.  Worth it because it contained the smells, but a pain nonetheless.  With this new Munchkin pail, all you do is lift the bag out and snap it shut.  Even I can do that!  To put a new bag in, all you have to do is open the top, snap the plastic pieces into place and youre done.  No tying, twisting, or cutting.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_6843" style="width: 210px">
         <a href="http://www.getbuttonedup.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/underneath-the-diaper-pail-hoods.jpg">
          <img alt="The Munchkin pail (left) has a super easy to extract bag and requires no twisting to seal dirty diapers away" class="size-medium wp-image-6843" height="300" src="http://www.getbuttonedup.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/underneath-the-diaper-pail-hoods-200x300.jpg" title="underneath the diaper pail hoods" width="200"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The Arm &amp; HammerTM Diaper Pail by Munchkin (left) has a super easy to extract bag and requires no twisting to seal dirty diapers away
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         In short, I love it.  The only minor issues I have are:
         <br/>
         1) The bag doesnt seem to hold quite as many diapers before you have to change it (but maybe I think that because Im actually changing the bag in this one instead of delegating to my husband!). In all actuality, the Arm &amp; HammerTM Diaper Pail by Munchkin is bigger  so it probably holds more.
         <br/>
         2) The first time it ever gets full, you might think youve broken it because the lid makes a grating/popping sound when it closes and it may not close at all.  But once you realize whats going on, that issue goes away completely.
        </p>
        <p>
         So  thanks Munchkin team for the new diaper pail.  I definitely feel as though my nursery has gotten a real upgrade!  And if youve got kids in diapers at home  I definitely recommend upgrading to this pail yourself.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_6840" style="width: 210px">
         <a href="http://www.getbuttonedup.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/DSC02591.jpg">

         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          And the winner is...Munchkin's new Arm &amp; Hammer pail!
         </p>
