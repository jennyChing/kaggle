       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Sunnyville New York Tour 2015 Day 4
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://sunnyvillestories.com/2015/04/sunnyville-new-york-tour-2015-day-4/" rel="bookmark" title="8:19 am">
         <span class="entry-date">
          April 7, 2015
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://sunnyvillestories.com/author/pwpadmin/" title="View all posts by Max West">
          Max West
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <iframe allowtransparency="true" class="fbLikeContainer" frameborder="0" id="fbLikeIframe" name="fbLikeIframe" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Fsunnyvillestories.com%2F2015%2F04%2Fsunnyville-new-york-tour-2015-day-4%2F&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=true&amp;width=300&amp;height=25&amp;action=like&amp;font=arial&amp;colorscheme=light" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:300px; height:25px; display:inline;">
        </iframe>
        <div style="float: right; margin-left: 10px;">
         <a class="twitter-share-button" data-count="vertical" data-url="http://sunnyvillestories.com/2015/04/sunnyville-new-york-tour-2015-day-4/" href="https://twitter.com/share">
          Tweet
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Yesterday marked my visit to
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ridgewood,_Queens" target="_blank" title="Ridgewood, Queens">
          Ridgewood
         </a>
         , a neighborhood in Queens, New York where I grew up. This area was important to the development of Sunnyville Stories.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_2558" style="width: 650px">
         <a href="http://sunnyvillestories.com/wp-content/uploads/DSCN2282.jpg">
          <img alt="Traffic Avenue, Ridgewood, Queens" class="size-large wp-image-2558" height="480" src="http://sunnyvillestories.com/wp-content/uploads/DSCN2282-1024x768.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Traffic Avenue, Ridgewood, Queens
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         This stretch of road right by my former home is Traffic Avenue. My own former home was situated right by here. Traffic Avenue lived up to its name; factories here sent out trucks throughout the week, occasionally causing damage to our property.
         <span id="more-2557">
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_2559" style="width: 650px">
         <a href="http://sunnyvillestories.com/wp-content/uploads/DSCN2284.jpg">
          <img alt="Grove Street, Ridgewood, Queens" class="size-large wp-image-2559" height="480" src="http://sunnyvillestories.com/wp-content/uploads/DSCN2284-1024x768.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Grove Street, Ridgewood, Queens
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         This street you see here is the street I used to live on in the 1980s. Grove Street was important to Sunnyvilles development. It was during my time here the seeds for Sunnyville were planted. I explained in
         <a href="http://sunnyvillestories.com/2010/09/origins-of-sunnyville-part-1/" title="Origins of Sunnyville, Part 1">
          the origins of Sunnyville
         </a>
         that I was used to having everything I needed in the immediate area and a move to a rural area was a jarring experience. Getting back to what I said before, I was lucky to have exposure to many cartoons of the 1980s as well as cable television while I lived in Ridgewood. The cartoons of the 1980s, especially to
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maple_Town" target="_blank" title="Maple Town">
          Maple Town
         </a>
         (the inspiration for Sunnyville), were integral components to Sunnyvilles creations.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://sunnyvillestories.com/wp-content/uploads/DSCN2292.jpg">
          <img alt="Public School 71, Ridgewood, Queens" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2560" height="480" src="http://sunnyvillestories.com/wp-content/uploads/DSCN2292-1024x768.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This building you see here is Public School #71 of Ridgewood. I went to elementary school here. It was definitely a much more innocent time back in those days  nowadays, New York City public schools always have armed police officers inside with the authority to arrest students at the drop of a hat!
        </p>
        <p>
         Moving on with my discussion, I had a teacher here who taught me something that remains with me to this day. I was always taught to think of unusual ideas. That remains with me and helped in the creation of Sunnyville. I wanted to do something that was more innocent and fun as opposed to the cliche storms of superhero comics or the rebellious, anti-establishment attitude of many small press/underground comics that I saw at the time. Why not do something very different?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://sunnyvillestories.com/wp-content/uploads/DSCN2293.jpg">
          <img alt="Ridgewood Branch of Queens Public Library" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2561" height="480" src="http://sunnyvillestories.com/wp-content/uploads/DSCN2293-1024x768.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This building you see here is the Ridgewood branch of the Queens Public Library. I made many visits here as a child. Here, I read many books on all kinds of different subjects of fiction and nonfiction. On top of that, I used to come here in the summer. One of the fun activities they had here during the summer was showing film adaptations of famous stories such as
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frog_and_Toad" target="_blank" title="Frog and Toad">
          <em>
           Frog and Toad
          </em>
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Why_Mosquitoes_Buzz_in_People%27s_Ears" target="_blank" title="Why Mosquitoes Buzz in People's Ears">
          <em>
           Why Mosquitoes Buzz in Peoples Ears
          </em>
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         It was nice to see Ridgewood again as it was once my home and also important to the development of Sunnyville Stories. At the same time, it felt sad to see how much everything had changed. The population of Germans and Italians who used to live there have largely been replaced by Slavic peoples such as Poles and Romanians. Much of what I remember is gone. On top of that, gentrification is happening slowly but surely in Ridgewood which is something that is common throughout New York City.
        </p>
        <p>
         Things have changed a lot.
        </p>
