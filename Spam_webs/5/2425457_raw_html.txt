           <a href="http://www.amazingposts.com/2008/11/height-of-recycling-turning-urine-into.html">
            Height Of Recycling - Turning Urine Into Drinking Water
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="post-header-line-1">
          </div>
          <div class="post-body">
           <p>
            Two hundred and fifty miles above the Earth puts you a long way from the nearest kitchen tap. And at $15,000 a pint, the cost of shipping fresh water aboard the space shuttle is, well, astronomical.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_pte2XO66Nwg/SSmfAig9KTI/AAAAAAAAC9g/V5fufe6rC8c/s1600-h/urine%2Bto%2Bwater.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
             <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5271919670427724082" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_pte2XO66Nwg/SSmfAig9KTI/AAAAAAAAC9g/V5fufe6rC8c/s400/urine%2Bto%2Bwater.jpg" style="margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 369px;"/>
            </a>
            <br/>
            So astronauts on the International Space Station have to recapture every possible drop. That includes water evaporated from showers, shaving, tooth brushing and hand washing, plus perspiration and water vapor that collects within the astronauts space suits. They even transfer water from the fuel cells that provide electric power to the space shuttle.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Until now, however, NASA has not attempted to tap one major potential source of water: urine. That will soon change with the deployment of the new Water Recovery System.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            It was taken up on the last shuttle launch on November 14th.
            <br/>
            <br/>

          <h3 class="post-title">
           <a href="http://www.amazingposts.com/2008/10/toaster-printer.html">
            Toaster Printer
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="post-header-line-1">
          </div>
          <div class="post-body">
           <p>
            <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_pte2XO66Nwg/SOTbpcKg6PI/AAAAAAAACl8/jiaccwxnTSw/s1600-h/mad-toaster.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
             <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5252564570402253042" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_pte2XO66Nwg/SOTbpcKg6PI/AAAAAAAACl8/jiaccwxnTSw/s400/mad-toaster.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer;"/>
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            This is real folks. though not yet available.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             The Scan Toaster connects to a PC over USB and downloads everything from local weather conditions and the current time to the mornings news headlines.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Inside the toaster is a network of toasting modules, each heated by a hot wire. Each module can move by 30 and, once the user selects what they want on their toast - excluding a topping, of course - the modules align themselves and burn the appropriate content onto the bread.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             The appliance is a finalist in design competition run by manufacturer Electrolux and designer Sung Bae Chang said he got the idea whilst - you guessed it - making some toast.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             No plans to manufacture the toaster on a mass scale have popped up yet.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.reghardware.co.uk/2008/09/11/wacky_toaster/">
             Link to story
            </a>
           </p>
