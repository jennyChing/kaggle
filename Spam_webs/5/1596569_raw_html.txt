      <h2 class="entry-title">
       <a href="http://www.swiss-miss.com/2015/04/why-you-turn-up.html" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Why You Turn Up">
        Why You Turn Up
       </a>
      </h2>
      <div class="entry-content">
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         You turn up because of the people around you.
        </p>
        <p>
         You know, the ones with common interests and sense of ambition.
        </p>
        <p>
         The ones who want to invent new things and do old things better.
        </p>
        <p>
         The ones whose distortion of reality present challenges that stretch you.
        </p>
        <p>
         The ones who give you freedom to do things differently.
        </p>
        <p>
         The ones you trust not to drop the ball (there are no ball droppers here).
        </p>
        <p>
         You even turn up for the ones who grate on you (theyre also brilliant).
        </p>
        <p>
         You turn up because the people around you value what you do.
        </p>
        <p>
         They believe in you and give you what you need.
        </p>
        <p>
         Together you create something more than a job.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont settle for anything less.
        </p>
       </blockquote>

        <a href="https://medium.com/@domgoodrum/why-you-turn-up-f163c1e05e4a">

        <a href="https://medium.com/@domgoodrum/why-you-turn-up-f163c1e05e4a">
         Friends
        </a>
        <em>
         ( formerly Studiomates)
        </em>
        .
       </p>

      <h2 class="entry-title">
       <a href="http://www.swiss-miss.com/2015/02/5-rules-for-a-creative-culture-2.html" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Ben Chestnuts 5 Rules for a Creative Culture">
        Ben Chestnuts 5 Rules for a Creative Culture
       </a>
      </h2>
      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        Theres not many CEOs I admire as much as MailChimp Co-founder Ben Chestnut. I keep reminding myself of his
        <a href="http://www.fastcompany.com/1767793/creative-cultures-mailchimp-grants-employees-permission-be-creative">
         5 rules for a creative culture
        </a>
        :
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         1. Avoid rules. Avoid order. Dont just embrace chaos, but create a little bit of it. Constant change, from the top-down, keeps people nimble and flexible (and shows that you want constant change).
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Give yourself and your team permission to be creative. Permission to try something new, permission to fail, permission to embarrass yourself, permission to have crazy ideas.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Hire weird people. Not just the tattood and pierced-in-strange-places kind, but people from outside your industry who would approach problems in different ways than you and your normal competitors.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Meetings are a necessary evil, but you can avoid the conference room and meet people in the halls, the water cooler, or their desks. Make meetings less about delegation and task management and more about cross-pollination of ideas (especially the weird ideas). This is a lot harder than centralized, top-down meetings. But this is your job  deal with it.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Structure your company to be flexible. Creativity is often spontaneous, so the whole company needs to be able to pivot quickly and execute on them (see #1).
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        If you enjoyed this, Id recommend you watch
        <a href="http://creativemornings.com/talks/ben-chestnut/1">
         Bens CreativeMornings talk
        </a>
        . I guarantee you its 40minutes well spent.
       </p>
      </div>
