      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       Could A Twitter Party Boost Your Business's Followers?
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->
      <div class="mninst">
      </div>
      <div class="comment_links">

      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <div class="zemanta-img">
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 310px">
          <img alt="Image representing Twitter as depicted in Crun..." class="zemanta-img-configured" height="300" src="http://b-i.forbesimg.com/drewhendricks/files/2013/09/2755v33-max-450x4501.png" width="300"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Image via CrunchBase
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         Businesses have only begun to learn the many ways Twitter can be used to build an audience. As hashtags, trending topics, and retweets show the possibilities for engagement on the social media site, businesses are beginning to learn that they can actually schedule events on the site and invite participants.
        </p>
        <p>
         A Twitter party is a great way to bring a large group of people together on the site. Whether youre launching a new product or featuring a guest speaker to boost business, a Twitter party can help your business gain new followers.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Before You Plan Your Twitter Party
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Before you announce your party, do a quick run-through of your Twitter profile. Is your profile picture one that accurately represents your business image? The goal is to build your brand, so ideally your username will incorporate some version of your businesss name. Sony is @Sony, Disney is @Disney, and Whole Foods is @WholeFoods. Even if you have an account for each employee in your organization, your Twitter party should be hosted by an account that explicitly states your business name.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Choose a Hashtag
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Your event should have a short, to-the-point name for your hashtag that invitees can remember. If your event is to announce a new makeover contest, #NewYou might be a good party name. If your party is a chat led by a social media expert, #TweetBetter could be a suitable name for your event.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remember, not only will your attendees search for your event by the hashtag you send, others will view the Tweets, as well. If your hashtag sounds like something they might want to attend, youll have more company at your event, which could translate to more customers.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Use a Social Media Service
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Services like HootSuite can help you tackle the daunting task of following an ongoing conversation. HootSuite allows you to create a tab dedicated solely to your Twitter party. Any item that comes through with your hashtag will be displayed in one place, which will better help you keep up with the conversation.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Offer Prizes
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If a business hosts an open house and invites members of the community, that business will have much more success by offering a door prize. In a similar manner, if you promote that party attendees will have the chance to win free products or services, your attendance will increase.
        </p>
        <p>
         Your prizes dont have to break the bank. You can offer free samples of your own products or the opportunity to win a $50 gift card to a restaurant or attraction. If you offer your own products, youll be able to get the word out to potential customers who may recommend them to their own friends and associates.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Attend Other Twitter Parties
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Several resources provide information on ongoing Twitter chats, including this one from
         <a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheet/ccc?key=0AhisaMy5TGiwcnVhejNHWnZlT3NvWFVPT3Q4NkIzQVE#gid=52">
          TweetReports.com
         </a>
         . Many of these events are recurring, making them more events than parties, but by attending one or two, you can get a feel for how a Twitter gathering works. Youll learn tips that will make you a better host, as well as see how businesses use events to attract customers.
        </p>
