             Do not text and Drive
            </span>
           </h2>
           <div class="component_content">
            <div>
             <p>
              Not many years ago,
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/mercedesdriving/funny-car-accidents/" target="_blank">
                accidents
               </a>
              </strong>
              and their resulting deaths and injuries prompted action to reduce the incidence of drunk driving in this country. The electronic revolution and some of the gadgets spawned by it has brought about a new threat to traffic safety, which has come to be known as
              <a href="http://www.nyautoschool.com/texting-driving/" target="_blank">
               <strong>
                texting and driving
               </strong>
              </a>
              . This article is committed is publicizing this issue to address this topic and assist in reducing the risk.
             </p>
             <p>
              The term texting and driving refers to the sending, receiving, composing or reading of messages on a mobile device. This habit involves those of all ages, but is of particular concern when practiced by younger motorists; they are beset with many other distractions when driving while they do not have a lot of experience behind the wheel.
             </p>
             <p>
              As with
              <a href="http://www.nyautoschool.com/" target="_blank">
               driving
              </a>
              while under the influence of alcohol or drugs, the dangers of texting and driving can be summed up in some somber statistics. In one recent year, distracted driving was a factor in nearly 20 percent of the traffic accidents in the U.S. These crashes claimed approximately 3,000 lives and more than 400,000 injuries. Although the exact relationship between accidents and the use of mobile communications equipment is uncertain, about 10% of the American drivers aged 18 to 20 admitted in
              <span class="GINGER_SOFATWARE_correct">
               survey
              </span>
              conducted by
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.nyu.edu/" target="_blank">
                New York University
               </a>
              </strong>
              in 2012 to texting while driving. In another study from the same institution, nearly half of the young Americans who were questioned claimed they have ridden in a vehicle that was being driven by a person who at some point used a mobile phone.
             </p>
             <p>
              The problem of texting is that it essentially reduces a drivers reaction time, the period from when something occurs and when the driver can respond to the event. Drivers who are texting take their eyes off the road, at least one of their hands off the steering wheels resulting in taking their attention from other important duties associated with driving.
             </p>
             <p>
              Attaining a
              <strong>
               driving license
              </strong>
              is a great responsibility that has many rewards, and
              <strong>
               driving lessons in Queens
              </strong>
              can help someone reach that goal. One of the responsibilities of driving is having a
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.nyautoschool.com/texting-driving/" target="_blank">
                commitment to safety
               </a>
               ,
              </strong>
              and the avoidance of texting and driving can help in reaching that goal.
             </p>

               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post_wrapper">
               <div class="date" title="1379898300000">
                September 22, 2013
               </div>
               <div class="content">
                <p>
                 Kids are at an increased risk of suffering injury when involved in a
                 <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/mercedesdriving/funny-car-accidents/" target="_blank">
                  vehicle accident
                 </a>
                 . Parents and guardians need to understand this and go out of their way to
                 <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/06/25/what-parent-leaves-child-in-broiling-car_n_3497956.html" target="_blank">
                  protect them
                 </a>
                 . This is not as simple as making them use the seat belt or securing them in a car seat.They must do more if they want to ensure that the kids arrive to their destination safely.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  <a href="http://www.squidoo.com/safe-is-the-new-cool" target="_blank">
                   Safe driving
                  </a>
                  :
                 </strong>
                 A motorist with children in the car must drive with utmost caution. When on the road, s/he should drive at the speed limit and avoid going through red lights or doing other dangerous maneuvers. Safedrivingmust be always the case but when there are children in the vehicle, it is necessary. We should always prevent problems from occurring in the first place; safe driving will reduce accidents and prevent bad situations when there are kids in the car.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.nyautoschool.com/children-in-the-car/" target="_blank">
                  <strong>
                   Work with the children:
                  </strong>
                 </a>
                 Kids in the car should be taught some principles which they have to followwhile being in the vehicle. After a motorist takes steps to secure kids in a car, he or she should do more; he/she should talkto them about the importance of being calm and not distracting him/her. As soon as the
                 <a href="http://mercedesdrivingschool.webs.com/" target="_blank">
                  vehicle
                 </a>
                 is moving, the children must remain relatively quiet and avoid any kind of dangerous moves. The driver should offer the childrens books or other items to keep them occupied during the trip.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.nyautoschool.com/children-in-the-car/" target="_blank">
                  <strong>
                   Inspect belts:
                  </strong>
                 </a>
                 A child might not know how to wear his/her seat belt properly and this can cause
                 <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/08/29/us/arizona-fatal-joyride/index.html" target="_blank">
                  accidents
                 </a>
                 during quick maneuvers. When the driver wants tosecurekids in a car, he or she should inspect the seat belt and make sure that they are wearing it correctly. If the
                 <strong>
                  child
                 </strong>
                 is in a car seat, a
                 <strong>
                  parent
                 </strong>
                 should know how to install and use the device; many parents do not use the car seat correctly and as a result, the children are vulnerable when an accident occurs.
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: left;">
                 <a href="http://www.nyautoschool.com/children-in-the-car/" target="_blank">
                  A car ride does not have to be dangerous for a child.
                 </a>
                 When following these basic ideas, a driver can get to his or her destination
                 <strong>
                  safely
                 </strong>
                 and protect the well-being of the children.
                </p>
