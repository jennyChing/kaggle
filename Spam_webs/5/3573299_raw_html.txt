           </a>
          </h1>
          <p class="sp_method_toc" id="method_toc">
           <span>

           </span>

          <p>
           A skirt lining ensures that skirt fabric doesnt ride up on the wearers legs. Most good tailoring includes a lining, but you can install one after the fact if you need to. The best way to make a skirt lining is to measure it from the pattern of the skirt and stitch it in before you hem the skirt; however, you can also approximate a lining with a ready-made garment.

               Purchase several yards of fabric.
              </b>
              Measure the length of the skirt and the width at the widest hem to see how much fabric you will need. Double the width and add one inch (2.5cm).

              <b class="whb">
               Set the lining facing up on your worktable.
              </b>
              Place the inside out skirt on top of it. Draw around the edges of the skirt with a fabric pen. Measure and mark any slits or zippers on the lining fabric as best you can.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Use the skirt pattern as a template in place of this step for a homemade skirt.

              <b class="whb">
               Cut the lining material to size with fabric scissors.
              </b>
              Cut one-half inch (1.3cm) outside of the pattern line to account for seam allowances. Cut the bottom hem one inch (2.5cm) shorter so that the lining doesnt go past the bottom hem.

              5

             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Use a seam ripper to take up the facing material, if your skirt has it.
              </b>
              This is the material that folds inside the skirt, leaving finished edges.
              <ul>
               <li>
                For a better lining, you will want to set the lining inside the facing and sew it down with a basting stitch.
               </li>
              </ul>

           <div class="altblock">

           <span class="mw-headline" id="Sewing_the_Lining">
            Part 3 of 3: Sewing the Lining
           </span>
          </h3>
          <div class="section_text" id="steps_3">
           <ol class="steps_list_2">
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Line-a-Skirt-Step-10.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Line-a-Skirt-Step-10.jpg">
               <img alt="Line a Skirt Step 10.jpg" height="503" id="55a38cafc300a" src="http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/8/88/Line-a-Skirt-Step-10.jpg/670px-Line-a-Skirt-Step-10.jpg" width="670"/>

              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              1
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Sew a zigzag stitch around the exterior of the lining to prevent fraying.
              </b>
              Use thread that matches the lining fabric.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Line-a-Skirt-Step-11.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Line-a-Skirt-Step-11.jpg">

               <img alt="Line a Skirt Step 11.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/a/a5/Line-a-Skirt-Step-11.jpg/670px-Line-a-Skirt-Step-11.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              2
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Pin the lining underneath the facing material.
              </b>
              If you dont have facing material, pin it to the inside of the skirt, below the waistband and above the hem.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Pin the sides of the skirt. Your two pieces of lining should overlap at the edges.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">

               <img alt="Line a Skirt Step 12.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/7/72/Line-a-Skirt-Step-12.jpg/670px-Line-a-Skirt-Step-12.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              3
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Cut around zippers or slits.
              </b>

             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li class="final_li">
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Line-a-Skirt-Step-13.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Line-a-Skirt-Step-13.jpg">
               <img alt="Line a Skirt Step 13.jpg" height="503" id="55a38cafc34f5" src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/9/9f/Line-a-Skirt-Step-13.jpg/670px-Line-a-Skirt-Step-13.jpg" width="670"/>

               </script>

              <noscript>
               <img alt="Line a Skirt Step 13.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/9/9f/Line-a-Skirt-Step-13.jpg/670px-Line-a-Skirt-Step-13.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              4
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Hand stitch around your seams.
              </b>
              Use a whip stitch in a thread that matches your skirt fabric. When you stitch be careful to go through the lining material and the facing, but only gather a few inside threads on the skirts outside fabric.
              <ul>
               <li>
                You dont want the stitches to show through.
               </li>
               <li>
                A whip stitch is used to hem fabric down. The stitch will show on the inside more than the outside. After knotting the thread and anchoring it on the back side, insert the needle through a few threads of the outer fabric, point your needle forward one-eighth inch (0.3cm), and bring the needle up through the facing and lining layer. Pull the thread through and a long stitch will wrap around the edge on the inside. Repeat until the hem is complete.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
