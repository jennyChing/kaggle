         <h1 class="articletitle">
          Publishers: Take back control
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="author">
         <img class="avatar" src="http://45.55.255.229/O6ATJEx_YpvJAKF8xGRw8qtsRjs=/34x34/smart/http://45.55.255.229/user/image/738793/shahnaznewmuseum2015-png"/>
         <span>
          By
          <span class="name">
           Shahnaz  Mahmud
          </span>
          on Nov 06, 2014
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="bodycopy">
         <p>
          Dr. Seuss said many wise things, including: Only you can control your future. For the ad publishing community, control has become a problem, given the complex nature branded content has become.
         </p>
         <p>
          Control is a topic of discussion at
          <a href="http://ad-tech.com/ny/whyattend?gclid=CPyMgrn52cECFQMT7AodrBYAVQ" rel="nofollow">
           ad:tech New York 2014
          </a>
          . Romain Job, general manager US at
          <a href="http://smartadserver.com/rtb-plus/rtb-publishers-adnetworks" rel="nofollow">
           Smart Adserver
          </a>
          will be discussing How to Regain Control and Increase Margin as a Brand Publisher on Day Two (Nov. 6) of the conference.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its a
          <a href="http://blog.smartadserver.com/2014/01/why-publishers-should-regain-control-advertising-operations/" rel="nofollow">
           subject
          </a>
          that Smart Adserver feels strongly about, having written about it for the ad community earlier this year. The company says to existon the advertising market and to best embrace the digital revolution, publishers need to regain control of the technology behind: Whether homebuilt or professional, this advertising platform should be fully integrated, programmatic enabled and data-safe in order to best generate higher revenue and protect publishers margin.
         </p>
         <p>
          Smart Adserver points out that while display advertising is on the rise, publishers have yet to reap the rewards advertising dollar-wise and often miss new opportunities that are cropping up. The company believes the display supply side market is dominated by a few global players.
         </p>
         <p>
          A source of consternation actually lies with technology suppliers, to whom publishers have become reliant upon. In working together, tech providers have become a greater part of the value chain, thus taking some of that control away from the publishers. Not to mention third-party performance partners, who have also muscled in on their territory.
         </p>
         <p>
          An interesting
          <a href="http://adage.com/article/digitalnext/publishers-back-control-consumer-data/293987/" rel="nofollow">
           Ad Age article
          </a>
          notes that digital publishers are compromised by third parties [like] Google, Facebook, advertising vendors, social media widget providers and others) that take, use and monetize the customer data the publishers generate. And it's precisely that behind-the-scenes access to customer data that causes its value to diminish and threatens publisher profitability.
         </p>
         <p>
          To take back some of that control over their advertising operations, Smart Adserver suggests creating entities, like a built-in private ad exchange using third party solutions. Dailymotion, for example - the worlds second largest online video platform - has already created one.
         </p>
         <p>
          Publishers should also try to avoid the middleman, which sees third party providers monetizing a sizable chunk of inventory.
         </p>
         <p>
          The biggest is ensuring to keep control of the very first impression. This will enable all of your demand sources (whether premium, programmatic or performance sources) compete in real time at the impression level and win the highly valued first impression auction, according to Smart Adserver data. This, in turn, will lead to greater revenue generation.
         </p>
         <p>
          Wise, indeed.
         </p>

         </p>
