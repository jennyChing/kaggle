            Eudaimonism is a type of moral philosophy that focuses on an action and asks whether or not that action enhances the well-being of the person who performs the action.
           </span>
           According to Aristotle, the way to eudaimonia is by performing our actions in accordance with the virtues. If one is a truly moral being then their actions will reflect this.
          </p>
          <p class="p1">
           For a person who follows virtue ethics it is their goal to identify, develop, and achieve desirable virtues. Anything less would translate to a less virtuous life and a loss of potential. For the virtuous man, every choice is based on that effort.
          </p>
