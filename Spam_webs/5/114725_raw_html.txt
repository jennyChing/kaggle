       <article class="post-122 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-office-fun category-office-supplies tag-gadgets tag-geek single-template-1 clearfix" id="post-122">
        <div class="post-content">
         <h2 class="title">
          The Ultimate Desktop  Bumptop
         </h2>
         <header class="details clearfix">
          <time class="detail left index-post-date" datetime="2008-09-22">
           Sep 22, 2008
          </time>
          <span class="detail left index-post-author">
           <em>
            by
           </em>
           <a href="http://blog.shoplet.com/author/jessica-h/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jessica Choi">
            Jessica Choi
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="detail left index-post-category">
           <em>
            in
           </em>
           <a href="http://blog.shoplet.com/category/office-fun/" title="View all posts in Office Fun">
            Office Fun
           </a>
          </span>
         </header>
         <!-- END .details -->
         <div class="single-content">
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="Bumptop - the ultimate Laptop" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3269/2878500609_6440ae2d84.jpg?v=0" title="Bumptop - the ultimate Laptop" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Christmas came early this year ! As I without fail checked my Gmail account last Friday September 19th, expecting another bunch of emails that normally suffers through the inevitable fate of mass deletion, something piqued my eye luckily before my fingers were able to hit the delete key. Could it be?! Yes it is! The Bumptop has finally arrived at my door steps, or rather my electronic portal! I was ecstatic that my month of anxious waiting and yearning to be among one of the first Bumptops Beta users has been not for nought. I got the confirmation download code to this softwares beta release. For those of you who are not fortunate enough to be using this next-generation desktop interface, I took some screen shot highlighting some of the main features of this amazing desktop application. Enjoy.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3012/2878500643_37965465de.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The unruly files on my desktop can be at the click of a button, be easily piled into various stacks of similarly typed file document. Oh, and Anand, Steve Jobs watches over me too.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3009/2878500677_fe5e795024.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           My desktop, at a disarray. File icons can be sized up or down to give visual, hierarchical, as well as physical weight to your desktop files. Two of the most brilliant features of this application are the default printer and email icons hanging on the wall. Dragging files to the printer icon will command the printer to print out the desired files, and dragging the file to the email icon will cause your default mail application to automatically attach the desired files to a new email.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3113/2878500705_b465676e92.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Bumptop has the option to enter infinity mode, meaning there is more or less no limit to the amount of virtual desktop space you can use to spread your files!
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3274/2878500883_a61cb4021f.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Bumptop has amazing ways of accessing files when they are stacked indiscriminately together. The files can be spread like poker cards.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3254/2878500751_e9d313a9ac.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The files can be flipped through like photo albums. This clever little file access mode can be simply accessed through the turning of the wheel on your mouse.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3130/2878500799_b895e2585c.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           These files can be neatly displayed in grid structure to provide organized viewing.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2242/2878500825_b989b40264.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Anything, and I mean anything can be tacked on the wall to resemble my own personal miniature art photography gallery.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3247/2879334224_12b8b5f6a5.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Another personalized viewing of my art gallery. Yes, I took all these photos ( and yes, I know these are good ).
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3011/2879334288_84f9e609d2.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Last, but not least, within each folder, lies the capability to be turned into its own miniature Bumptop! Brilliant
          </p>
          <p>
           Oh, and my only suggestion for this wonderful desktop application, can someone ( hello Anand, a.k.a. the coolest Asian dude after Harold and Kumar, and the dittiest asian rapper all around, and the
           <a href="http://bumptop.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://bumptop.com/', 'Bumptop Team ');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Bumptop Team
           </a>
           ) please put a giant vacuum somewhere so I can easily suck away all the junk files I create in the process of creation, or just something thats really really easy to clean house! See below!
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="middle" alt="" height="313" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3253/2879623302_ee9190cecb.jpg?v=0" title="" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Much much love to the Bump Top Team!
          </p>
