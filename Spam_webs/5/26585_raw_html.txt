          <img alt="Slow Cooker Greek Roast ~ Butter With A Side of Bread" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6956" height="427" src="http://butterwithasideofbread.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Slow-Cooker-Greek-Roast.IMG_0163.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I wanted to make a pot roast in the crock pot last Sunday, but I wanted something
         <em>
          a little different
         </em>
         . I got several new seasonings from
         <a href="http://spiceologist.com/" target="_blank">
          Spiceologist
         </a>
         and couldnt wait to try them. I ended up using the
         <a href="http://spiceologist.com/products/greek-freak" target="_blank">
          Greek Freak seasoning
         </a>
         and oh heavens it was incredible! I made a gravy but it went unused as the flavor of the meat was just SO amazing in itself. Youve GOT to try this!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Slow Cooker Greek Pot Roast
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 2-3lb chuck roast {I got my roasts from
          <a href="http://goo.gl/nhMQH1" target="_blank">
           Zaycon Fresh
          </a>
           theyre the best roasts Ive ever had!}
         </li>
         <li>
          2 cups beef broth
         </li>
         <li>
          3/4 cup red cooking wine
         </li>
         <li>
          1 medium onion, diced
         </li>
         <li>
          2 TBSP Greek seasoning
         </li>
         <li>
          salt &amp; pepper
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Place roast in slow cooker on high.
        </p>
        <p>
         Pour beef broth and red cooking wine into slow cooker. Add onion. Pour 1 TBSP of the Greek seasoning into the liquid in the crockpot and stir to incorporate. Sprinkle the other 1 TBSP onto the top of the roast. Sprinkle with salt &amp; pepper.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cook on LOW for about 8 hours. Increase temperature to HIGH and cook an additional 2 hours. OR cook on HIGH for 6-7 hours. If you have time, cook it on LOW for about 13 hours and its heavenly!
        </p>
