         Simple Apple Smoothie
        </h1>

         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         My kids and I enjoy making smoothies for a quick snack or light lunch. This Apple Smoothie is my favorite Fall smoothie! It is simple to make, but tasty and filling, which makes it perfect for a whipping up a quick treat. With fresh picked apples it is the perfect fall beverage.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.upstateramblings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/apple-smoothie.png">
          <img alt="apple smoothie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12906" height="750" src="http://www.upstateramblings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/apple-smoothie.png" width="750"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I like to make this smoothie without peeling the apple, and I have a Vitamix blender, which has no problem blending up the skin. I think itt looks pretty with all the flecks of red in the smoothie, and there are quite a few nutrients in the skin too. But if you have a less powerful blender you might want to peel the apple first. This is perfect for tart apples like Northern Spy, Braeburn or Paula Red.
         <span id="more-12904">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         It has all the taste of apple pie, without all the calories! I think it tastes best with almond milk, because that makes the smoothie rich and creamy. But I have made it with regular milk and it is still tasty.
         <br/>
        </p>
