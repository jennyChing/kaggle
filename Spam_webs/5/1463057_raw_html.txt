                     <em>
                      Atlantic
                     </em>
                     staff, is
                     <strong>
                      Nicolas Yan
                     </strong>
                     , a Year 13 student at Kings College, an independent secondary school in Auckland, New Zealand. His essay  on Martin Luther King, Jr.s I Have a Dream speech  stood out as a successful demonstration of analytical writing. For his accomplishment, Mr. Yan is being recognized at
                     <a href="http://theatlantic.com/atlanticedu" target="_blank" title="Go to The Atlantic magazine website">
                      <em>
                       The Atlantic
                      </em>
                      Education Summit
                     </a>
                     in Washington, D.C., on June 15. In addition, his essay will be published in the September 2015 issue of
                     <em>
                      The Atlantic.
                     </em>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     We have been encouraged by the positive response of students who, through this exercise, learned the importance of analytical writing, an essential skill for college and career success. 40 college professors of history, political science, and composition served as judges and read over 3,000 submitted essays from around the globe. They were impressed by the seriousness students displayed in engaging with seminal American historical documents.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     As part of next years
                     <em>
                      The
                     </em>
                     <em>
                      Atlantic
                     </em>
                     &amp; College Board Writing Prize, students will be invited to submit essays analyzing an
                     <strong>
                     </strong>
                     influential work of art. If you will be in high school next year and are interested in art history, we encourage you to submit an entry again and pass it along to other students who might be interested. Submission details will be available on the College Boards website by mid-August.
                    </p>

              <h2>
               The Importance of Writing
              </h2>
              <div class="embed">
               <div class="tip-callout">
                <div class="callout-content">
                 <h2>
                  <div class="field field-name-field-tip-title field-type-text field-label-hidden">
                   <div class="field-items">
                    <div class="field-item even">
                     Teacher Resources
                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                  <div class="field-items">
                   <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                    <p>
                     Encourage students to engage critically with primary sources in order to produce exemplary analytical writing.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Learn about the close
                     <a href="https://advancesinap.collegeboard.org/sites/default/files/essay/reading/ap-us-history-reading-strategies.html" target="_blank">
                      reading
                     </a>
                     of and
                     <a href="https://advancesinap.collegeboard.org/sites/default/files/essay/writing/ap-us-history-writing-strategies.html" target="_blank">
                      writing
                     </a>
                     from challenging primary sources.
                    </p>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
               <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="Prize: Resources Tip" property="dc:title">
               </span>
              </div>
              <p>
               Writing is one of the most important skills to master. Not only is writing essential for college and career, but learning to write clearly also helps studentsdevelop theirthinking skills. To be successful at analytical writing, studentsmust support your arguments with evidence found in the text and clearly convey information to the reader. It is this kind of writing that allows studentsto build knowledge, deepen understanding, and develop informed opinions.
              </p>
              <p>
               With this in mind,
               <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com" target="_blank">
                <em>
                 The Atlantic
                </em>
               </a>
               and the College Board have collaborated to create this annual contest. The focus of this contest will change each year to align with the introduction of a newly redesigned AP course and exam.
              </p>
