           Subway touts itself as having healthier choices than other fast food chains, but that may not necessarily be true. Also, apixaban in Afib and diffusion tensor imaging in stroke.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Subway as Unhealthy as McDonald's
           </strong>
          </p>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-left desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           Despite their commercials to the contrary, the Subway fast food chain menu may not be much healthier than the McDonald's menu, according to a study in
           <em>
            Journal of Adolescent Health
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Meals that teens and young adults (ages 12 to 21) typically bought at McDonald's contained an average of 1,038 calories, while at Subway, the calorie count in the average purchase was 955, reported Lenard Lesser, MD, of UCLA Fielding School of Public Health, and colleagues.
          </p>
          <p>
           There was no significant difference between the two and participants ate too many calories at both restaurants, researchers said, referring to an Institute of Medicine (IOM) recommendation of no more than 850 calories in school lunches.
          </p>
          <p>
           The average calories per sandwich purchased were 784 at Subway versus 572 at McDonald's. The sodium content at both restaurants was three times higher than the IOM recommended daily dose: 2,149 mg at Subway versus 1,829 mg at McDonald's.
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Apixaban Effect Constant
           </strong>
          </p>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-left tablet-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           The treatment effect of the oral anticoagulant apixaban (Eliquis) to prevent stroke in atrial fibrillation patients was constant across a broad range of patient and country international normalized ratios (INR), a subanalysis of the ARISTOTLE trial found.
          </p>
          <p>
           In the lowest and highest quartiles of time in therapeutic range (TTR) among countries, hazard ratios for clinical outcomes were:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Stroke or systemic embolism  --  0.73 (95% CI 0.53 to 1.00) and 0.88 (95% CI 0.57 to 1.35,
            <em>
             P
            </em>
            interaction = 0.078)
           </li>
           <li>
            Mortality  --  0.91 (95% CI 0.74 to 1.13) and 0.91 (95% CI 0.71 to 1.16,
            <em>
             P
            </em>
            interaction = 0.34)
           </li>
           <li>
            Major bleeding  --  0.50 (95% CI 0.36 to 0.70) and 0.75 (95% CI 0.58 to 0.97,
            <em>
             P
            </em>
            interaction = 0.095)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Similar results were seen at different levels of predicted individual patient TTR:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Lowest quartiles  --  HR 0.70 (95% CI 0.52 to 0.94)
           </li>
           <li>
            Highest quartiles  --  HR 0.87 (95% CI 0.57 to 1.33)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           The interaction with continuous individual patient TTR was
           <em>
            P
           </em>
           =0.060, according to Lars Wallentin, MD, of Uppsala University in Uppsala, Sweden, and colleagues.
          </p>
          <p>
           The study was published online in
           <em>
            Circulation: Journal of the American Heart Association.
           </em>
          </p>

          <p>
           <strong>
            Congenital Heart Defects Spawn New Mutations
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Patients with congenital heart disease are seven times more likely to have unique genetic mutations not inherited from parents than the average population, researchers found.
          </p>
          <p>
           Many of these mutations occur in genes that can modify a group of histones  --  proteins that can regulate the expression of key developmental genes, including those involved in heart development, according to Richard P. Lifton, MD, PhD, of Yale University School of Medicine, and colleagues.
          </p>
          <p>
           Collectively, these
           <em>
            de novo
           </em>
           mutations contribute to around 10% of severe congenital heart disease cases, they wrote in the journal
           <em>
            Nature
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Researchers reached these results by comparing the incidence of
           <em>
            de novo
           </em>
           mutations in 362 severe congenital heart disease cases and 264 controls.
          </p>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right tablet-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
            High-Tech Imaging Predicts Stroke Deficit
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Among various MRI measures, a sophisticated imaging of axonal damage in the corticospinal tract was the only independent predictor of long-term motor outcome, researchers found.
          </p>
          <p>
           In 70 patients with middle cerebral artery stroke, researchers evaluated the clinical severity, infarct volume, location of corticospinal tract damage on diffusion tensor tractography, and ratios of fractional anisotropy between affected and unaffected sides of the corticospinal tract at the pons.
          </p>
          <p>
           Only the ratios of fractional anisotropy correlated with the degree of motor deficit at 2 years, and it was the only independent predictor of long-term motor outcome at 30 days, according to Josep Puig, MD, Girona Biomedical Research Institute in Girona, Spain, and colleagues.
          </p>
          <p>
           However, the usefulness of the ratios of fractional anisotropy as a surrogate must be validated further, researcher wrote in the study published online in
           <em>
            Stroke: Journal of the American Heart Association
           </em>
           .
          </p>
