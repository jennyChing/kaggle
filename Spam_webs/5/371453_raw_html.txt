         <h1>
          Sept. 15: National Pick a Human Target Day
         </h1>
        </hgroup>
        <p class="pubdate">
         <span style="color:#666666;">
          Published: Sep 14, 2014
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="mobilecmeandclinical">
        </p>
        <div class="clinicalcontextinfo">
        </div>
        <div class="byline">
         <div class="bylineleft">
          <div class="bylinepic">

          <div class="bylinetext">
           <div class="bylinename">
            By
            <a href="mailto:i.oransky@medpagetoday.com">
             Ivan Oransky MD
            </a>
            , Global Editorial Director, MedPage Today
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>

       <aside class="sidebar">

       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Sept. 15 is
        <a href="http://nationaldaycalendar.com/calendar-at-a-glance/september/" target="_blank">
         apparently already National Felt Hat Day
        </a>
        , International Dot Day, and National Creme de Menthe Day.
       </p>
       <p>
        Perhaps it should also be National Pick a Human Target Day.
       </p>
       <p>
        Every day,
        <a href="/MeetingCoverage/ACP/45229" target="_blank">
         88 people in the U.S. die
        </a>
        from gun-related injuries,
        <em>
         MedPage Today
        </em>
        reported earlier this year.
       </p>
       <p>
        But statistically speaking, more people may die from such injuries tomorrow. That's what Randall Loder found when he
        <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3865450/" target="_blank">
         looked at data from 1993 to 2008
        </a>
        in a study published in the
        <em>
         Journal of Injury and Violence Research
        </em>
        :
       </p>
       <p>
        "The overall peak was Sept. 15 with several exceptions. Injuries from BB guns had a peak on May 22, a diagnosis of a foreign body on July 11, and patients ages 10 to 14 years on April 9. The peak day was always Saturday/Sunday when significant variation existed."
       </p>
       <p>
        Indeed, while Loder tells me he hasn't analyzed newer data,
        <em>
         The New York Times
        </em>
        '
        <a href="http://nocera.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/09/16/weekend-gun-report-september-13-15-2013/" target="_blank">
         Gun Report for September 15, 2013
        </a>
        lists more than three dozen incidents, including a number of deaths.
       </p>
       <p>
        Tell your patients to stay safe out there tomorrow. And every day, for that matter.
       </p>
