          <section class="article-content">
           <div class="shift-to-hero">
            <div class="content-gallery" data-id="">
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1390433" data-fragment="m!4bf8" data-image="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzEwLzA4LzAwXzM5XzA2XzQyMF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/1e586b3f" data-micro="1" data-width="640" src="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzEwLzA4LzAwXzM5XzA2XzQyMF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTIwMHg5NjAwPg/1e586b3f" title="NYC Skyline"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Ah,
            <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aqlJl1LfDP4/" target="_blank">
             New York
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Like all major cities, the Big Apple has its own share of unique and iconic places: the Statue of Liberty, the Empire State Building, Times Square, the Brooklyn Bridge and many more.
           </p>
           <p>
            But also iconic to New York are the people who live here. Maybe it's the large, diverse population of different cultures packed so tightly together  or maybe it's just something in the water. Whatever the case, there's nothing quite like walking through the streets of Gotham.
           </p>
           <p>
            (Really, where else can you take a casual stroll with a
            <a href="http://gothamist.com/2012/07/13/video_rat_man_still_putting_rats_in.php/" target="_blank">
             family of brightly colored rats
            </a>
            on your shoulders and barely get a second glance?)
           </p>
           <p>
            We've compiled a list of some of the people exclusive enough to only be seen here. Browse through the gallery below to see our collection of the top 10.
           </p>
           <section class="gallery" data-display-mode="gallery" data-id="7643" data-slide-title-pos="" data-slug="10-people-youll-only-see-in-nyc">

                  nearly

                </p>
                <p>
                 Donning a cowboy hat, boots, briefs and one strategically placed guitar, Robert John Burck can almost always be seen in Times Square strumming his six-string and waving to tourists.
                </p>
                <p>
                 He's stuck his nearly-naked foot in politics, too: In 2009, he challenged New York Mayor Michael Bloomberg in the election. The following year, he announced he was challenging President Barrack Obama in the 2012 election as a GOP candidate. He withdrew from both races shortly after entering.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <em>
                  Image courtesy of Flickr,

               <h2 class="title">
                Ranting Elmo
               </h2>
               <div class="caption">
                <p>
                 Just like "Tickle Me Elmo," only anti-Semitic and scary.
                </p>
                <p>
                 He claims his real name is Adam Sandler --
                 <em>
                  Mashable
                 </em>
                 full disclosure:
                 <em>
                  not
                 </em>
                 the
                 <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0001191/" target="_blank">
                  Adam Sandler
                 </a>
                 we know and love -- and has a history of shouting anti-Semitic rants while dressed in an Elmo costume.
                </p>
                <p>
                 He's been arrested several times for disorderly conduct, most recently
                 <a href="http://cityroom.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/09/19/ranting-elmo-returns-and-is-arrested-in-times-square/" target="_blank">
                  in Times Square
                 </a>

              <figure>
               <img alt="8e4acd0b" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzEwLzA4LzAwXzM5XzA2XzYyNF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDg1MD4KZQlqcGc/8e4acd0b.jpg"/>

                <p>
                 Check out
                 <a href="http://vimeo.com/8249248/" target="_blank">

              </figure>
              <div class="meta">
               <h2 class="title">
                The Upper East Side Lingerie Jogger
               </h2>
               <div class="caption">
                <p>
                 Elliot Offen, better known as the "UES Lingerie Jogger," has gained notoriety around Manhattan's Upper East Side for his ladies' underwear-sporting runs.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The outspoken fitness fanatic is also a frequent guest on
                 <em>
                  The Howard Stern Show
                 </em>
                 , where he yells and swears a lot -- seriously,
                 <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZSTE0kkBfvw/" target="_blank">
                  watch this clip
                 </a>
                 .
                </p>

              </figure>

               <h2 class="title">
                East Village Topless Lady
               </h2>
               <div class="caption">
                <p>
                 In 1992, a law was passed that gave women the right to go shirtless anywhere in Manhattan. Philadelphia topless activist Moira Johnston, most often seen in the East Village, wants to raise awareness of it.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Back in May, she was detained by cops for walking shirtless in public, then released shortly afterwards when they realized she wasn't breaking any laws.
                </p>

              <figure>
               <img alt="B31883fc" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzEwLzA4LzAwXzM5XzA2Xzk2M19maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDg1MD4KZQlqcGc/b31883fc.jpg"/>
              </figure>
              <div class="meta">
               <h2 class="title">
                Rat Man
               </h2>
               <div class="caption">
                <p>
                 It's an often-repeated statistic that there are as many rats in New York as there are people; some go as far as to say there are
                 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rats_in_New_York_City/" target="_blank">
                  four times
                 </a>
                 as many rats as New Yorkers.
                </p>
                <p>
                 One New York resident, known largely as "Rat Man," has embraced that ratio. He's been seen, usually in Times Square, with a gang of rats on his shoulders, head and -- sometimes -- in his mouth. He's dyed some of them different colors, too.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Watch the
                 <a href="http://gothamist.com/2012/07/13/video_rat_man_still_putting_rats_in.php/" target="_blank">
                  latest video
                 </a>
                 of him and his rodent roommies.
                </p>

                  Image courtesy of Flickr,
