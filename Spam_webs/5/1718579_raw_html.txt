     <p>
      <b>
       Clearview
      </b>
      , also known as
      <b>
       Clearview Hwy
      </b>
      , is the name of a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VOX-ATypI_classification#Humanist_2" title="VOX-ATypI classification">
       humanist
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Sans-serif" title="Sans-serif">
       sans-serif
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Typeface" title="Typeface">
       typeface
      </a>
      family for
      <a href="/wiki/Traffic_sign" title="Traffic sign">
       guide signs
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">
       roads
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      . It was developed by independent researchers with the help of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Texas_Transportation_Institute" title="Texas Transportation Institute">
       Texas Transportation Institute
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Transportation_Institute" title="Pennsylvania Transportation Institute">
       Pennsylvania Transportation Institute
      </a>
      , under the supervision of the
      <a href="/wiki/Federal_Highway_Administration" title="Federal Highway Administration">
       Federal Highway Administration
      </a>
      (FHWA). It was once expected to replace the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FHWA_Series_fonts" title="FHWA Series fonts">
       FHWA typefaces
      </a>
      in many applications, although newer studies of its effectiveness have called its benefits into question.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yaffa_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-yaffa-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grays_Harbor_Denial_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Grays_Harbor_Denial-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Testing found Clearview was two to eight percent more legible in daytime and nighttime viewing than the then-dominant Series E (Modified) on overhead signs, particularly benefiting older drivers, with a six percent increase in legibility distance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CastroHorberry2004_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-CastroHorberry2004-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A design goal of Clearview was the reduction of irradiation effects of retroreflective sign materials.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CastroHorberry2004_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-CastroHorberry2004-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The same reduction in nighttime overglow or haloing that benefits legibility for humans is expected to result in improved recognition rates for computer road sign detection.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LiWang2007_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-LiWang2007-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clearview_(typeface)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ClearviewBGS-I696W-I275-I96.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="354" data-file-width="690" height="128" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/ClearviewBGS-I696W-I275-I96.JPG/250px-ClearviewBGS-I696W-I275-I96.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/ClearviewBGS-I696W-I275-I96.JPG/375px-ClearviewBGS-I696W-I275-I96.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/ClearviewBGS-I696W-I275-I96.JPG/500px-ClearviewBGS-I696W-I275-I96.JPG 2x" width="250"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ClearviewBGS-I696W-I275-I96.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        A Clearview highway sign in
        <a href="/wiki/Farmington_Hills,_Michigan" title="Farmington Hills, Michigan">
         Farmington Hills, Michigan
        </a>
        , installed in 2005 near the terminus of westbound
        <a href="/wiki/Interstate_696" title="Interstate 696">
         I-696
        </a>
        . Note that numbers within the shields use the traditional
        <a href="/wiki/Highway_Gothic" title="Highway Gothic">
         FHWA typeface
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The standard FHWA typefaces, developed in the 1940s, were designed to work with a system of highway signs in which almost all words are capitalized. The designers of Clearview sought to create a typeface adapted for mixed-case signage, initially expecting it would be based on an existing European sans-serif typeface.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Instead, using a similar weight to the FHWA fonts, a new font was created from scratch. Two key differences are much larger
      <a href="/wiki/Counter_(typography)" title="Counter (typography)">
       counter
      </a>
      spaces, the enclosed spaces in letters like the lower case "e" or "a," and a higher
      <a href="/wiki/X-height" title="X-height">
       x-height
      </a>
      , the relative height of the lower case "x" to the upper case "X." Smaller counter spaces in the FHWA fonts reduced legibility, particularly when the letters glowed from headlight illumination at night. The typeface's general appearance resembles the design of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transport_typeface" title="Transport typeface">
       Transport typeface
      </a>
      family, designed by
      <a href="/wiki/Jock_Kinneir" title="Jock Kinneir">
       Jock Kinneir
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Calvert" title="Margaret Calvert">
       Margaret Calvert
      </a>
      in 195763 for the British highway sign system.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Official_acceptance">
       Official acceptance
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clearview_(typeface)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Official acceptance">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Clearview was granted interim approval by the FHWA for use on positive contrast road signs (light legend on dark background, such as white on black, green, blue, brown, purple or red) on September 2, 2004.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fha2004_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-fha2004-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The FHWA has not granted approval for Clearview to be used on negative-contrast road signs (dark legend on light background, such as black on white, yellow or orange), given its inferior legibility to the existing FHWA typefaces in these applications.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Despite this, it is used in negative-contrast applications by some agencies. The FHWA also refused to add Clearview to the 2009
      <a href="/wiki/Manual_on_Uniform_Traffic_Control_Devices" title="Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices">
       MUTCD
      </a>
      , citing lack of testing on Clearview's numerals, symbols, and narrower typefaces.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In April 2014, FHWA indicated it expects to rescind Interim Approval to use Clearview.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grays_Harbor_Denial_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Grays_Harbor_Denial-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In Canada, since 2006, Clearview has been adopted as the standard typeface for signs in the province of
      <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">
       British Columbia
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is also used for street signs in the city of
      <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">
       Toronto
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In Indonesia, Clearview has been adopted as the standard typeface for roadsigns in Indonesia since 2014.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Variants">
       Variants
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clearview_(typeface)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Variants">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In addition to its appearance on road signage, a customized version of the ClearviewText typeface was adopted by
      <a href="/wiki/AT%26T" title="AT&amp;T">
       AT&amp;T
      </a>
      for corporate use, including advertising, beginning in 2006.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yaffa_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-yaffa-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ClearviewText and ClearviewADA are versions of the typeface intended for use in general graphic design and
      <a href="/wiki/Americans_with_Disabilities_Act_of_1990" title="Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990">
       ADA-compliant
      </a>
      signage.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Clearview.png">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="214" data-file-width="320" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Clearview.png/220px-Clearview.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/06/Clearview.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/06/Clearview.png 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Clearview.png" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        An example of the Clearview typeface.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>

     </div>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Adoption">
       Adoption
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clearview_(typeface)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Adoption">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">
       United States
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clearview_(typeface)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: United States">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Between 20 and 30 states have adopted the use of the typeface as of 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-murphy2013_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-murphy2013-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strizver2013_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Strizver2013-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mutcd_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-mutcd-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is not the official font recommended for use by the FHWA, and states must request interim approval from the Federal Highway Administration to use the font.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-murphy2013_12-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-murphy2013-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">
       Canada
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clearview_(typeface)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Canada">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Transportation Association of Canada's
      <a href="/wiki/Manual_on_Uniform_Traffic_Control_Devices#Other_jurisdictions" title="Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices">
       MUTCD for Canada
      </a>
      allows the use of Clearview, and the
      <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Transportation_of_Ontario" title="Ministry of Transportation of Ontario">
       Ministry of Transportation of Ontario
      </a>
      uses it for positive contrast guide signs.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Toronto has been replacing its black-on-white street signs with newer signs that use Clearview since 2004, with exceptions for certain older neighborhoods.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesia">
       Indonesia
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clearview_(typeface)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Indonesia">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Ministry of Tranportation passed a regulation in 2014 to introduce new
      <a href="/wiki/Road_signs_in_Indonesia" title="Road signs in Indonesia">
       road signs
      </a>
      , including new Clearview typeface.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This new regulation was intended to meet
      <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations" title="Association of Southeast Asian Nations">
       ASEAN Economic Community
      </a>
      standards, starting in 2015.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The older road signs are using
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FHWA_Series_fonts" title="FHWA Series fonts">
       FHWA Series fonts
      </a>
      , had been in use since 1993.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
