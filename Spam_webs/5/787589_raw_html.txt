       <p>
        Everyone can lose up to one hundred hairs per day and this is nothing to fear as it is a natural process. When you brush your hair you will lose several hairs, but hair rids its dead cells to make way for new hairs. However, hair loss can indicate a far serious problem and here are several ways in which it can occur in women.
       </p>

        <span id="more-193">
        </span>
       </p>
       <h2>
        Hair Loss Post Pregnancy
       </h2>
       <p>
        Telogen effluvium can occur after pregnancy, after extreme weight loss or during stressful periods in your life. This condition causes a heavy loss of hair during normal every day procedures, including washing the hair, styling and brushing. Telogen effluvium can also be caused through antidepressant medications, anti inflammatory drugs and beta blockers. The hair has a natural growth process, but during telogen effluvium, the hair transports rapidly into the resting phase before it sheds.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Hair Loss Through Androgenetic Alopecia
       </h2>
       <p>
        This common hair loss is inherited through your familys genes, although you may have a higher risk of developing this condition if both your parents have experienced hair loss. It can occur in individuals as young as twenty.
       </p>
       <h2>
        An Overactive Thyroid Gland Can Cause Hair Loss
       </h2>
       <p>
        The thyroid gland supports the bodys metabolism and heart rate, but hypothyroidism (an underactive thyroid) can result in hair loss. The thyroid gland produces thyroxine, which supports the growth of hair, skin and nails. An overactive thyroid gland can result in weight loss, hyperactivity and
        <a href="http://fitnessgirls.org/why-yoga-should-be-a-staple-of-your-fitness-routine/">
         insomnia
        </a>
        . If the body does not receive the right level of thyroxine it will function differently.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Hair Loss Through Iron Deficiency Anaemia
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://fitnessgirls.org/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/541563051_3f601ceb79.jpg">
         <img alt="541563051_3f601ceb79" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-422" height="333" src="http://fitnessgirls.org/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/541563051_3f601ceb79.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Some women can experience heavy periods, which results in a loss of iron. If this mineral is not replaced through the diet, an iron deficiency may result. Foods which are rich in iron include leafy green vegetables like spinach, fish, beans and red meat. Iron deficiency anaemia can result in fatigue, headaches, difficulty in concentration and hair loss.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Hair Loss Through Lupus
       </h2>

       <p>
        When the immune system attacks healthy tissue, the resulting disease is lupus. It is known to affect approximately 1.5 million people worldwide, especially in childbearing women. Hair loss can occur during the shampooing or brushing of hair or a rash may appear on the scalp.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Hair Loss Through Polycystic Ovaries
       </h2>
       <p>
        Polycystic ovary syndrome occurs in many women, often from a very young age with the symptoms causing hair loss and infertility. This occurs because the body overproduces male hormones, caused by a
        <a href="http://fitnessgirls.org/how-women-can-achieve-a-hormone-balance-naturally/">
         hormonal imbalance
        </a>
        . Polycystic ovaries can create other symptoms including acne, facial hair growth, ovarian cysts and irregular periods.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Hair Loss Through Excessive Styling
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://fitnessgirls.org/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/creative-hair-dresses.jpg">
         <img alt="creative-hair-dresses" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-421" height="330" src="http://fitnessgirls.org/wp-content/uploads//2013/07/creative-hair-dresses-500x330.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Excessive styling, dying or shampooing of the hair can put hair under stress. Heat damage can weaken hair and make it more prone to breakage. The resulting damage of excess blow-drying, hair colour and keratin treatments can cause hair loss. If you have long hair and rely on heat treatments to style your hair, use a specialist conditioner that will protect your hair from heat damage.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Skin Conditions Can Result in Hair Loss
       </h2>
       <p>
        There are certain skin conditions that can cause hair loss. These include psoriasis, dandruff and the fungal infection, ringworm. The inflammation that occurs on the scalp can create more of a risk of hair loss. Dandruff can result in dead skin that rests in the hair or upon the shoulders, while psoriasis causes excess skin cell turnover that leaves thick white scaling on the scalp, which can be prone to bleed when picked. Ringworm results in red patches on the scalp.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Hair Loss Through Alopecia Areata
       </h2>
       <p>
        When the immune system attacks the hair follicles the disorder is known as alopecia areata. It can equally affect both men and women and is generally caused through illness or stress. It can result in patches of baldness in areas of the body, while total hair loss on the scalp is called alopecia tolalis and complete hair loss from the hair and body is called alopecia universalis.
       </p>
