           <div class="expert-content-text">
            <p>
             <em>
              February, 2015
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Scammers and dishonest P2P individuals use false torrents to
              <a href="/od/scamsandidentitytheft/ig/Phishing-Scams-and-Email-Cons/">
               phish
              </a>
              people's identities, bilk them for money, or vandalize their computers through trojan infections
             </strong>
             . Gratefully, helpful P2P users like'
             <em>
              already_dead
             </em>
             ' and other longtime supportersof
             <a href="/od/readerpicks/ss/Alternatives-to-Pirate-Bay-The-Best-Torrent-Sites-of-2015.htm">
              Isohunt.com
             </a>
             have built upmethods of identifying false torrents and avoiding them.
            </p>
            <p>
             Below are 10 strong suggestions on how to spot fake torrent movie and music files. This list is changing, and will update regularly with changes in torrent swarming technology.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Related
             </strong>
             :
             <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/About-Internet-for-Beginners/198370876872397">
              Submit your own suggestions on how to spot fake torrents
             </a>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Related
             </strong>
             : Always use a reliable antivirus product like
             <a href="/od/guidesfavorites/f/avira_antivir.htm">
              Avira
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <figure class="content-img content-img-small right-gutter">
             <div class="content-img-wrapper">
              <img alt="Businessman Using Laptop - Ryuhei Shindo/The Image Bank/Getty Images" class=" addPinit" data-description="Don't Get Fooled - Simple Ways to Spot a Fake Movie Torrent File: Confirm Both the Torrent and the Movie Release Date with a Third Party" data-pinit-img-url="http://f.tqn.com/y/netforbeginners/1/S/s/V/6575-000126.jpg" height="198" itemprop="image" src="http://f.tqn.com/y/netforbeginners/1/L/s/V/6575-000126.jpg" width="300"/>
             </div>
             <figcaption class="content-img-subtitle">
              <span class="credit pull-right">
               Ryuhei Shindo/The Image Bank/Getty Images
              </span>
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              1.
Confirm Both the Torrent and the Movie Release Date with a Third Party
             </h3>
             <p>
              For brand new movie torrents, take a minute to visit these two sites:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.imdb.com">
                www.imdb.com
               </a>
               to check release dates
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.vcdq.com" target="_blank" zt="-o1/XJ">
                www.VCDQ.com
               </a>
               to confirm that the new movie exists as a torrent
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              If the torrent does not exist at VCDQ.com, and/or is released before the actual movie date, then do not trust it.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense" id="adsense1">
           </div>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              2.
You Can Trust .AVI and .MKV Files as a General Rule; Avoid .WMA and .WMV Files
             </h3>
             <p>
              For the most part,true movie files are either .avi (audio video interleave) or .mkv (Matroska files) format.
              <strong>
              </strong>
              Conversely, the great majority of .wma and .wmv files are fake. While there are some authentic examples, most .wma and .wmv files will link to other sites to get paid codecs or malware downloads.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              3.
Check for 'Verified' Status on the Torrent
             </h3>
             <p>
              Sites like
              <a href="/od/readerpicks/ss/Alternatives-to-Pirate-Bay-The-Best-Torrent-Sites-of-2015.htm">
               Kickass
              </a>
              and Torrentbox will actually employ a committee of core users to confirm and 'verify' torrents. While these verified files are small in number, they are very
              <a data-inlink="LtHm0Xz3aD0-MxbRlHu9Zw==" href="/od/peersharing/tp/Torrent-Download-Guide.htm">
               likely true torrents
              </a>
              that can be trusted. Keep your Avira antivirus updated and active, and 'verified' files should be safe to download.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense" id="adsense2">
           </div>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              4.
Always Read the Comments
             </h3>
             <p>
              Some torrent sites like Isohunt.com will capture user comments. Like eBay feedback on other eBay users, these comments can give you a sense of how legit the file is. If you see no comments on a file, be suspicious. If you see any negative comments on the file, then move on and find a better torrent.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              5.
Beware a Disportionate Number of Thousands of Seeds, but No Comments
             </h3>
             <p>
              Abusive uploaders will falsify the number of seeds and peers.
              <a data-inlink="8SNvfQubaqxYUlCIx561oQ==" href="http://macs.about.com/od/glossaryqt/g/software.htm">
               By using software
              </a>
              tools like BTSeedInflator, abusers will make their torrents look like 10,000+ users are sharing it. If you see these massively large seed/peer numbers, but there are no user comments on the file, then avoid the file! Any
              <a data-inlink="FTbm4vJGrNX_5mUdEEgjcg==" href="http://compnetworking.about.com/od/p2ppeertopeer/g/bldef_torrent.htm">
               true torrent
              </a>
              that has more than a few thousand seeds should also have positive user comments.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="1" class="adsense-slot adsense" id="adsense3">
           </div>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              6.
Beware if Password Instructions, Special Instructions, or Exe Files Are Included
             </h3>
             <p>
              If you see a file in the movie/music torrent that says 'password', 'special instructions', 'codec instructions', 'unrar instructions, 'important read me first', 'download instructions here', then the torrent could be a scam fake. The instigator is likely looking redirect you to a shady website to download a dubious movie player as a precondition to opening the movie file. Furthermore, if there is an exe executable file included, then definitely avoid that torrent download! Executable files for movies and music are a big red flag, as there is no need for an
              <a data-inlink="93kfidAaxl2KUAxQNAf9Fg==" href="http://personalweb.about.com/od/linkingideas/ht/exefiles.htm">
               executable file
              </a>
              to be present.
              <a data-inlink="HY2lgfctTRLfKRdiGI56CQ==" href="http://personalweb.about.com/od/linkingideas/a/408exefiles.htm">
               Exe files
              </a>
              and any passwords or special download instructions are likely a sign that you should move on and find a
              <a data-inlink="3KdtQ5Zhl5RkFLBCFMCNEA==" href="/od/downloadingfiles/tp/How-to-Download-Torrents.htm">
               better torrent download
              </a>
              elsewhere.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              7.
Distrust .RAR, .TAR, .ACE Files
             </h3>
             <p>
              Yes, there are legit uploaders who use .rar archives to share files. But for movies and music, the majority of
              <a href="http://pcsupport.about.com/od/fileextensions/f/rar-file.htm">
               rar
              </a>
              and archive files are fake. Abusers use the rar format to conceal trojans and
              <a data-inlink="3yZYplejA0iTwMgDbyBvUQ==" href="http://pcsupport.about.com/od/fileextensions/f/codecfile.htm">
               codec scam files
              </a>
              . Video is already compressed, so there is no need to compress further.
             </p>
             <p>
              If you see an
              <a data-inlink="vRPDBD9mYgjRQdEwlzH95A==" href="http://pcsupport.about.com/od/fileextensions/fl/torrentfile.htm">
               attractive torrent movie file
              </a>
              that is in .rar, .tar, or .ace format, distrust it and examine its listed file contents before you download. If there is no list of the contents, do not trust it. If the filelist is disclosed, but the file list includesan .exe executable file, or text files with the words 'password' or 'download instructions', do not trust it.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              8.
Avoid Using the Following Software
             </h3>
             <p>
              <a data-inlink="nkGmbPgNyVvwrtKnFZkN3g==" href="http://compnetworking.about.com/od/bittorrent/f/besttorrent.htm">
               These torrent software clients
              </a>
              have earned a bad reputation for seeding malware, fraudulent codec downloaders, keyloggers and trojans. Avoid using any of the following:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               BitLord
              </li>
              <li>
               BitThief
              </li>
              <li>
               Get-Torrent
              </li>
              <li>
               TorrentQ
              </li>
              <li>
               Torrent101
              </li>
              <li>
               Bitroll
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>
           </section>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              9.
Beware Trackers that Can't Be Found on Google
             </h3>
             <p>
              Open the published torrent details, and copy-paste the tracker names into Google. If a tracker is legitimate, you will see many Google hits where
              <a data-inlink="7EnYs8Nm3erORUiZu7IaXA==" href="/od/peersharing/gr/torrent_site_review_cinematorrents.htm">
               many torrent sites
              </a>
              point to the copy-pasted tracker. If the tracker is false, you will find many unrelated hits at Google, often with the words 'fake'
              <a data-inlink="sTHBB5xfv_CGlmL_7neHcw==" href="http://voip.about.com/od/voipbasics/g/whatisP2P.htm">
               as P2P users
              </a>
              post warnings on that fake tracker.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              10.
Only Use the Following Media Players
             </h3>
             <p>
              These are the trusted movie and music players of 2015. Anything other than these products is a candidate for being a trojan or malware tool.
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               WinAmp
              </li>
              <li>
               Windows Media Player
              </li>
              <li>
               VLC Media Plyer
              </li>
              <li>
               GMPlayer
              </li>
              <li>
               KMPlayer
              </li>
             </ol>
            </div>
