        <p>
         <a href="http://www.laprogressive.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/moral-monday-355.gif">
          <img alt="moral monday north carolina" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-90169" height="302" src="http://www.laprogressive.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/moral-monday-355.gif" width="350"/>
         </a>
         <span class="dropcap">
          L
         </span>
         ike that little choo-choo in the childrens book, Moral Monday is the little movement that says, I think I can  and it just keeps chugging up the hill.
        </p>
        <p>
         This progressive citizens action coalition in North Carolina became a full-throttle citizens uprising in the Tar Heel state early last year. Fueled by rising public outrage at the rampant right-wing extremism of the Republican-run state government, a few advocates for workers, civil rights, and other peoples issues went inside the state capitol on a Monday in April. Led by Rev. William Barber, head of the state NAACP, they literally put their bodies on the line in protest of the GOPs reckless crusade to turn North Carolina into a privatized utopia for unfettered corporate greed and tea party wackiness.
        </p>
        <p>
         Several of the small group were arrested that day, and Republican leaders berated their protest as Moron Monday. But the politicos arent laughing now. The protestors kept coming and their numbers kept growing, for Moral Monday had struck a chord, and the protest spread across the state. A rally in February drew more than 80,000 people, and public approval ratings for the governor and state assembly have tanked.
        </p>
        <p>
         The legislature is now out of session, but Moral Monday still has weekly meetings and is launching a 50-county organizing and voter education campaign this summer. Its no longer a protest, but a burgeoning multi-issue, grassroots movement for progressive change. And it is literally on the move branching out to other states  Moral Monday Georgia is going full steam this year, South Carolina has a Truthful Tuesday movement gaining steam, and the movement is getting started in Alabama, Florida, New York, and Wisconsin.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="jim hightower" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-77570" height="194" src="http://www.laprogressive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/jim-hightower.gif" width="200"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="dropcap">
          S
         </span>
         ome of these states are mighty steep political hills for progressives to climb, but success begins with someone saying, I think I can. To have a movement, youve gotta start moving!
        </p>
