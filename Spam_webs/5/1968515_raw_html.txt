           <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
            <span>
             <a class="formaticon" href="" title="Post Format: standard">
             </a>
             Dislike Running? You Wont Anymore! (We Promise)
            </span>
           </h1>
           <div class="entry-meta">
            <span class="date">
             <i class="fa fa-clock-o">
             </i>
             <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-09-18T12:50:28+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
              18 September, 2014
             </time>
            </span>
            <span class="author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
             By
             <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://blog.pumpup.co/post/author/user/" itemprop="url" rel="author" title="View all posts by pumpup">
              pumpup
             </a>
            </span>
            <span class="categories-links">
             <i class="fa fa-folder-open">
             </i>
             <a href="http://blog.pumpup.co/post/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag">
              Uncategorized
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
          </header>
          <div class="entry-media">
           <a class="post-thumbnail large" href="http://blog.pumpup.co/post/dislike-running-not-anymore-cinnamonontop-siya-natseva-p/" title="Dislike Running? You Wont Anymore! (We Promise)">
            <img alt="Dislike Running? You Won't Anymore! (We Promise)" class="large-thumbnail" itemprop="image" src="//31.media.tumblr.com/91aa9b40664105523abfdfd3a8c2113e/tumblr_inline_nc2r5dNhIe1sfmto5.png"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
           <p>
            <img src="//31.media.tumblr.com/91aa9b40664105523abfdfd3a8c2113e/tumblr_inline_nc2r5dNhIe1sfmto5.png"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Guest blog by
             <a href="http://cinnamonontop.wordpress.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://cinnamonontop.wordpress.com', 'Siya Natseva');">
              Siya Natseva
             </a>
             . Follow Siya on her
             <a href="http://cinnamonontop.wordpress.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://cinnamonontop.wordpress.com', 'blog');">
              blog
             </a>
             , PumpUp (@cinnamonontop), and Instagram (@cinnamonontop8).
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             An active runner? A beginner? A runner-to-be? A runner-to-never-be?
            </strong>
            Regardless of how you describe yourself, Im sure we agree that running has an unfairly bad reputation. Its often reduced to a treadmill in a confined space. In our minds, running is the definition of boring! One foot in front of the other, to eternity and back. But running shouldnt be mundane. Lets see how
           </p>
           <p>
            <img src="//31.media.tumblr.com/a96cb7f9df354ec0b719c3918cafe092/tumblr_inline_nc2q4xYflR1sfmto5.gif"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span>
             (Photo
            </span>
            <a href="http://omghowgirl.tumblr.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://omghowgirl.tumblr.com', ': omghowgirl)');">
             : omghowgirl)
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <h3>
            1.  Music is the solution to every problem!
           </h3>
           <p>
            Your grandparents may not grasp the idea of blocking your hearing by means of headphones and a blast of sound, but for the 21
            <sup>
             st
            </sup>
            century kid musical accompaniment is a must. Running is
            <em>
             no
            </em>
            exception. When you focus on your favourite song versus the fact youre drenched in sweat and exhausted, youforget that youre drenched in sweat and exhausted!
            <strong>
             Create a playlist to
             <em>
              at least
             </em>
             cover your estimated jog duration.
            </strong>
            You should aim for upbeat songs, not devastating rock ballads. Dont be fooled: Im a rock chick but I cant imagine myself pumping it up (see what I did there?) to Bon Jovis 
            <em>
             Always
            </em>
            .
           </p>
           <h3>
            2. Changing Gears
           </h3>
           <p>
            <span>
             Due to its nature, running
            </span>
            <em>
             can
            </em>
            <span>
             become a tedious form of working out. This is particularly true when you discover a comfortable pace and settle for it. In this sense, upping the speed and / or length of your training is crucial. In addition  the changing gears part  you can modify your pace. Run fast for, say, three minutes and then lower your speed for three minutes.
             <strong>
              No hardcore interval training required; small adjustments make a difference.
             </strong>
             In this case, youll also benefit from a balanced music list. Harmonise your jogging with it.
            </span>
           </p>
           <h3>
            3. The oh-so-important scenery
           </h3>
           <p>
            <span>
             Running is what we ought to complete
            </span>
            <em>
             in the gym
            </em>
            <span>
             before the real deal happens. Perhaps this is your error. Running neednt be done in the gym with air being scarce and people killing themselves on the machines. Visit the local park. Is there a secluded area which can serve as your running ground?
             <strong>
              Running in the open is a life saver.
             </strong>
             Having a jog, whereby you connect with nature, is beneficial in numerous ways. And, hey, theres plenty of oxygen to feed your cells! New locations are a distraction from your physical discomfort and lead to better results!
            </span>
           </p>
           <h3>
            4. Empty your head
           </h3>
           <p>
            <span>
             Running is an effective stress-reliever. You can test this statement by fully concentrating onyou. Ditching technology isnt half bad. We live in an extraordinarily hectic world and have to remind ourselves to breathe, embrace the moment and enjoy it. Free yourself of thoughts and simply run. Pay no attention to anything or anyone. Listen to your breathing and admire what youre capable of!
             <strong>
              Youll be surprised to feel extremely refreshed and worry-free after a run which is about you and you alone.
             </strong>
             Need to resolve an issue? By ridding yourself of mental effort, youre likely to do so without even realising it!
            </span>
           </p>
           <h3>
            5. Buddy Up
           </h3>
           <p>
            <span>
             For those afraid of solitary confinement, teaming up with a friend may be what you need. Unlike other sports  notably swimming  running provides you with the opportunity to socialise! Having a companion will transform running into a fun activity that you participate in together.
             <strong>
              Sometimes the best workouts are those that dont resemble a workout in the slightest.
             </strong>
             Its certainly worth attempting, especially if you lose motivation quickly, for itll awake your competition spirit. Youll be discouraged from whining and quitting. Instead, youll be invested in burning calories, toning up your body and catching up on gossip!
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img src="//31.media.tumblr.com/0c9a9aef09c9ff8c1dab79935a788e6b/tumblr_inline_nc2rw0A3Hk1sfmto5.gif"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             (Photo: Starling Fitness)
            </em>
