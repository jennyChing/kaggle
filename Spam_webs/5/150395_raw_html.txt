         </a>
         , president and founder of GO MOM! Inc., shares some tips for mastering your schedule and reclaiming your life. Heres to an organized 2011!
        </em>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       I love the start of the new year! January holds the magical power to turn our hearts towards a bright future and to pursue
       <a href="http://www.gomominc.com/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=369:go-mom-teaches-you-to-make-a-dream-board&amp;catid=88:weekly-vlog-grow&amp;Itemid=121">
        our dreams
       </a>
       no matter how big or small.
      </p>
      <p>
       Here are 7 ways to organize your life to make sure 2011 is a year full of personal joy and not just busy days.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Know your planner:
       </strong>
       When you dont like your planner, you wont manage your time. Simple as that. The key is to find a
       <a href="http://www.mycarolinatoday.com/2011/01/molly-gold-planners/">
        planner
       </a>
       that speaks to your needs. Make a list of what you like (and dont like) about your current planner. If it doesnt make the grade, change to one that will naturally encourage you to use it to the fullest.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Take the job:
       </strong>
       Time doesnt disappear. Every minute you have is given away to some kind of task. Be the gatekeeper. Always having your calendar on hand to check dates before giving your time away. This is your best defense to make sure your daily reality reflects your familys priorities.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Follow the filter:
       </strong>
       Apply a
       <a href="http://www.gomominc.com/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=441&amp;Itemid=74">
        Schedule. Organize. Grow.
       </a>
       filter to everything you do. Keeping your schedule current and staying organized helps you lead the most productive and efficient life possible. Tackle these together and youll find you suddenly have more time to do the things you love.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Be flexible:
       </strong>
       At the start of the year, fill out your monthly calendar for the next twelve months. Then complete your weekly calendar with greater details including kids schedules. This gives you a snapshot of the chaos, tells you where to make changes, and how to best plug in your own personal interests around responsibilities that cannot be changed.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Teach your family:
       </strong>
       As the gatekeeper, you have the master key to the who, what, when, where, why of your familys days. Share it with them using a
       <a href="http://www.staples.com/2011-Staples-Desk-Pad-Calendar/product_854602?cmArea=page_rr">
        simple wall calendar
       </a>
       or multi-user electronic calendar and then remind them to check it daily so they always know whats next. And dont forget to color code to make it easier for everyone to read and understand at a glance.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Duplicate yourself:
       </strong>
       When you find yourself with a schedule that has more activities than you can handle, get help. Consider joining a carpool, hiring a babysitter, or researching services that will help you with periodic cleaning or meal preparations. Short cuts like these will save your sanity during the crazy times.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        A look into the future:
       </strong>
       In all you do, challenge yourself to estimate how long it will take to complete a task or project. Do this by listing the steps needed to accomplish your goal. For example, while planning grocery shopping, schedule time weekly for menu planning and
       <a href="http://www.couponmom.com/">
        coupon clipping
       </a>
       separately from your time at the store. Then block a specific time on your calendar for each task. By far this is your best defense to stay on track and on time.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Celebrate:
       </strong>
       No matter how busy things get, protect family time with the fierceness of a mother tiger. Get creative and know that it doesnt have to be grand to be treasured. Most of all, know that family is your focus and every minute you dont spend with them is time youll never get back.
      </p>
