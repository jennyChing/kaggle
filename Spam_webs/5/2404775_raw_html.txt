       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         DIY Fire &amp; Ice Lanterns
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-02-17T12:24:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 17, 2012
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://madincrafts.com/author/madincrafts/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            madincrafts
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://madincrafts.com/diy-fire-ice-lanterns/#comments">
           6 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="5447254" data-link="http://madincrafts.com/diy-fire-ice-lanterns/" data-summary="" data-title="DIY Fire &amp; Ice Lanterns">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div align="center">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center;">
         <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6fHuFm3jaP8/ULO2ykwIgpI/AAAAAAAAQ0o/7Dfqu2LalG8/s1600/DIY+Fire+&amp;+Ice+Lanterns.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6fHuFm3jaP8/ULO2ykwIgpI/AAAAAAAAQ0o/7Dfqu2LalG8/s1600/DIY+Fire+&amp;+Ice+Lanterns.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a count-layout="horizontal" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.madincrafts.com%2F2012%2F02%2Fdiy-fire-ice-lanterns.html&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2F1.bp.blogspot.com%2F-6fHuFm3jaP8%2FULO2ykwIgpI%2FAAAAAAAAQ0o%2F7Dfqu2LalG8%2Fs1600%2FDIY%2BFire%2B%26%2BIce%2BLanterns.jpg&amp;description=DIY%20Fire%20%26%20Ice%20Lantern%20(from%20dollar%20store%20materials)%20-%20Mad%20in%20Crafts">
           <img border="0" src="http://assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         <strong>
          The old becomes new: A new variation on the outdoor lantern.
         </strong>
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         We have had an unusually warm winter in Michigan this year, and while the temps have been nice, I have had to hold off on this project idea until we got a cold snap. We finally got some snow and cold earlier this week, so I was able to try out this fun winter project.
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         I had pinned a product called
         <a href="http://acornonline.com/product.aspx?p=15812v">
          Globe Ice Lantern Kits
         </a>
         a few months back recognizing that I could DIY them at home. Originally priced at nearly $30 for a kit that makes 6 lanterns, I think they are very reasonable for a small batch. If you are making several (say, for a special event or to light a walkway), you can make 3 of these DIY versions for only $2 worth of supplies from the dollar store.
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="center">
         <em>
          <br/>
         </em>
         <em>
          To make DIY Fire &amp; Ice Lanterns, you will need:
         </em>
        </div>
        <div align="center">
         <em>
          a package of 3 punch balloons from the dollar store
         </em>
        </div>
        <div align="center">
         <em>
          a package of 3 LED tea lights from the dollar store
         </em>
        </div>
        <div align="center">
         <em>
          bowls
         </em>
        </div>
        <div align="center">
         <em>
          small cylinder (shot glass, pop bottle, etc.)
         </em>
        </div>
        <div align="center">
         <em>
          water
         </em>
        </div>
        <div align="center">
         <em>
          freezer or below-freezing outdoor temps
         </em>
        </div>
        <p>
         <u>
         </u>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-BhQiafUPUm4/Tz5G1tXygLI/AAAAAAAAG_M/D1eYILZjC48/s1600-h/attach-balloon-to-faucet4.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="attach balloon to faucet" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-X9KAug3QMWg/Tz5G18XqMOI/AAAAAAAAG_U/z1TzrcU9O2g/attach-balloon-to-faucet_thumb1.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="attach balloon to faucet" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         You are getting a glimpse of my less-than-sparkling kitchen sink in this post. Be kind in your judgments.
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         First, stretch the opening of one punch balloon over your faucet. The punch balloons are thicker than normal party balloons, so they can take quite a bit of stretching and snapping.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-Ry6SdkjYmPs/Tz5G2rdfzcI/AAAAAAAAG_c/2JDk-p10gpU/s1600-h/fill-balloon-with-water4.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="fill balloon with water" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-sb8cWp1k1d8/Tz5G24tphxI/AAAAAAAAG_k/hxBkkf1Vzwo/fill-balloon-with-water_thumb1.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="fill balloon with water" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         Turn on the faucet and let the water pressure fill the balloon to your desired size. My lanterns were about 6-8 inches in diameter.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-GagONPDki7Y/Tz5G3lKKHjI/AAAAAAAAG_s/35AsraOK3Pc/s1600-h/use-the-punch-elastic-to-tie-off-the%25255B1%25255D.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="use the punch elastic to tie off the balloon" border="0" height="375" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-XYiAsI_4N9A/Tz5G3x46iCI/AAAAAAAAG_0/R0tIUSvUH90/use-the-punch-elastic-to-tie-off-the%25255B2%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="use the punch elastic to tie off the balloon" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         Pinch off the opening of the balloon and wrap the attached rubber band around the neck of the balloon (like a ponytail) to temporarily tie it off. This will take a little finesse. The water in the balloon wont want to stay in there, so it will try to spout back at you. I may possibly know from experience.
        </div>
        <p>
         If you were to freeze the balloon as it is, you could make a lovely ice orb. If you want to be able to insert a light inside the ice lantern, you need to create a notch for it in the candle.
         <br/>
         <u>
         </u>
         <br/>
         <a href="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-Hlmgc-TaZP8/Tz5G4eAjODI/AAAAAAAAG_8/ZLjgQm_61ZE/s1600-h/put-small-cylinder-in-balloon8.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="put small cylinder in balloon" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-gg2w0OoTaOM/Tz5G48Wi4eI/AAAAAAAAHAE/-P5qfhWOC-A/put-small-cylinder-in-balloon_thumb2.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="put small cylinder in balloon" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         Stretch the opening of the balloon neck over whatever cylinder you choose to use. Just make sure the diameter of the cylinder is slightly larger than your candle.
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         Once the cylinder is fully plugging the opening of the balloon, you can remove the rubber band. Little by little, work the neck of the balloon over the cylinder until a few inches of the cylinder are submerged in the water.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-4XLLKl1qnPI/Tz5G5W-04YI/AAAAAAAAHAM/iEgMoB8LTKo/s1600-h/put-the-balloon-outside8.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="put the balloon outside" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-ipFOZEfqqzQ/Tz5G5kw73MI/AAAAAAAAHAU/g5f2vqaWjEg/put-the-balloon-outside_thumb2.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="put the balloon outside" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         Place the balloon in a bowl that is roughly the size and shape of the filled balloon. For two of my lanterns, I used a bowl that was too small, which resulted in more egg-shaped lanterns than perfect spheres. Place the bowl in a freezer or in a snow bank for 12-24 hours.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-ZYcU254CHpo/Tz5G6cA4PsI/AAAAAAAAHAc/kyoj6YImLVo/s1600-h/pour-hot-water-into-the-cylinder3.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="pour hot water into the cylinder" border="0" height="375" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-gARh3cXiB4M/Tz5G6lcKrDI/AAAAAAAAHAk/NrtvIHLSha8/pour-hot-water-into-the-cylinder_thu.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="pour hot water into the cylinder" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         I brought in my first lantern after 12 hours. I poured hot water into the cylinder to loosen it from the ice.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-1BUo1sBjTFo/Tz5G7RmG8XI/AAAAAAAAHAs/ZI1G_unkej8/s1600-h/peel-off-balloon3.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="peel off balloon" border="0" height="375" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-aqJcwNp5iEc/Tz5G7pTaKiI/AAAAAAAAHA0/2OIMUESoX10/peel-off-balloon_thumb.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="peel off balloon" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         One quick slice with a knife and the balloon peels away from the ice. Carefully remove the ice lantern from the bowl. Because this lantern only froze for 12 hours, the shell of ice was thin and the center was not frozen. The water inside the shell will need to be emptied into the sink to avoid a mess. Again, I might possibly know from experience.
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-Rtn6bKEzBuw/Tz5G8W7cgRI/AAAAAAAAHA8/137k8y9-E8E/s1600-h/thin-shell-ice-lantern8.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="thin shell ice lantern" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/-SHdL8Uc3YYc/Tz5G8-CHPDI/AAAAAAAAHBE/UM3xIfNAZPw/thin-shell-ice-lantern_thumb2.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="thin shell ice lantern" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         Place an LED tea light onto your table and set the lantern carefully over it. If you choose to use a real candle instead of a tea light, you will need to create a chimney at the top of the lantern, either by drilling or melting, to allow oxygen into the lantern. Also, the lantern will obviously melt more quickly with real fire inside.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-S3Rs2iVyOLk/Tz5G9LlTzYI/AAAAAAAAHBM/guq7W0Zn_OY/s1600-h/thin-ice-lantern-at-night4.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="thin ice lantern at night" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-AQqFXbBcz9Q/Tz5G9kyxEoI/AAAAAAAAHBU/YcclZbsAV8c/thin-ice-lantern-at-night_thumb1.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="thin ice lantern at night" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         The thin shell of the lantern allows a lot of light to shine through the ice, but it is quite fragile.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-b91iwsEqbgA/Tz5G-SsOB8I/AAAAAAAAHBc/LFJvw-hdFN8/s1600-h/thin-and-thick-ice-lanterns7.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="thin and thick ice lanterns" border="0" height="375" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-V5wu2qndgPs/Tz5G-_qC89I/AAAAAAAAHBk/uYF0Vp3Jby4/thin-and-thick-ice-lanterns_thumb1.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="thin and thick ice lanterns" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         You can see that I damaged the top of the lantern on the left while moving it outside. The lantern on the right was allowed to freeze for 24 hours and is much thicker, more opaque, and stronger.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh5.ggpht.com/-rTCi-inWOrw/Tz5G_ac9SUI/AAAAAAAAHBs/ghHYP7ZBM8Y/s1600-h/blue-ice-lantern4.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="blue ice lantern" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-o-xrMIe9FEI/Tz5G_1PI84I/AAAAAAAAHB0/JuRv6yY7MyM/blue-ice-lantern_thumb1.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="blue ice lantern" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         As an experiment, I added some blue food coloring to the water on the final lantern as I filled it. The result was pretty cool. When making these again, I might try other add-ins, like glitter or flower petals.
        </div>
        <p>
         <u>
         </u>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-X-bsdhr7LP0/Tz5HAWX4UgI/AAAAAAAAHB8/sTZaDjuk2hQ/s1600-h/lit-blue-ice-lantern4.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="lit blue ice lantern" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-0E1rhsvyXLk/Tz5HAx1WFUI/AAAAAAAAHCE/52kKCx7FETM/lit-blue-ice-lantern_thumb1.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="lit blue ice lantern" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         If you use food coloring in a lantern, realize that the dyed water will melt onto whatever surface on which you have place the lantern; and food coloring stains. A placemat or lipped tray would catch the drips.
        </div>
        <p>
         <u>
         </u>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lh5.ggpht.com/-t_uBqfJJ0Vs/Tz5HBSuZh0I/AAAAAAAAHCM/zHg_48IOhMo/s1600-h/lit-clear-ice-lantern4.jpg">
          <u>
          </u>
          <img alt="lit clear ice lantern" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-eklkIHqhAI8/Tz5HBkfr9xI/AAAAAAAAHCU/bZ0UJB8If9Q/lit-clear-ice-lantern_thumb1.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="lit clear ice lantern" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div align="justify">
         I think these lanterns would be lovely lighting the pathway up to your home over the holidays.
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         You could also use them as inexpensive centerpieces at a winter wedding. Place the lantern in a wide, shallow bowl and surround it with evergreen clippings or fresh flowers. The slowly melting ice would provide water for the cut flowers and greenery.
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         <u>
         </u>
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-eJYPjsHiIrQ/Tz5HCBOZnjI/AAAAAAAAHCc/XlYIXXOPJ-8/s1600-h/frost-poem9.jpg">
          <img alt="frost poem" border="0" height="480" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-hHdfOAI1A1g/Tz5HCgk2INI/AAAAAAAAHCk/pGThF5s7Pmg/frost-poem_thumb3.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; border-top-width: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin: 0px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px;" title="frost poem" width="360"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
        </div>
        <div align="justify">
         You didnt think I would make it through an entire post about Fire &amp; Ice without bringing up the Frost poem, did you?
        </div>
