          <div class="entry-content">
           <p>
            Like I always say 
            <em>
             sharing is caring
            </em>
             and what better thing to share than your great findings and conversations on the web?
            <strong>
             Isnt it wonderful to experience a less constipated mailbox not spammed by your friends links and jokes
            </strong>
            ?  While we all have become quite familiar with social networking and bookmarking tools such as Delicious and StumbleUpon the following services just kick it up a notch!
           </p>
           <h2>
            FriendFeed:
           </h2>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             
             <em>
              FriendFeed is a service that makes it easy to share with friends online. It offers a fun and interactive way to discover and discuss information among friends
             </em>
             .
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            <img alt="friendfeed-logo" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2483" height="33" src="http://blog.thoughtpick.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/nano-logo1.png" style="margin: 10px;" width="150"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            -
            <strong>
             This is like your Facebook news feed, except bigger, better and searchable.
            </strong>
            You add a bunch of your disparate accounts on social networking diaspora, blogs, and internet presence and receive all your updates in one big news feed.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Why should I sign up?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <em>
              Friend lists
             </em>
             -
             <strong>
              Group your friends together so that you minimize the clutter
             </strong>
             , further customize this view by hiding or unhiding certain elements such Hide all Twitter updates from certain users. This can be really helpful especially with services that lack or have a non existent filtering, yes Im looking at you Twitter.
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Groups
             </em>
             - Are you interested in following a certain topic or allowing others to do so?
             <strong>
              Creating a group can be a powerful tool to siphon the discussions into a single stream
             </strong>
             . This is one of the most popular features since it allows you to let people recommend stuff you are interested in, its like that really cool friend that keeps on recommending the right stuff to you all the time almost.
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Advanced /Saved searches
             </em>
              Do you want to check out what others have said about Twitter in the past 24 hours on all of your services in 1-click?
             <strong>
              Made by 4 ex-Googlers you would expect exceptional search capabilities and FriendFeed will meet your expectations.
             </strong>
             <a href="http://mashable.com/2009/08/10/friendfeed-tips-saved-searches/" target="_blank" title="HOW TO: Take Advantage of FriendFeed's Unique Features">
              Tweak your searches
             </a>
             by defining which users or groups to search, on a certain service, or how liked or commented on the item is. Its Twitters saved searches without the limitations, like not being able to search for a tweet that is older than 14 days, and with much space for tweaking your searches.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Why you shouldnt bother with it?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span id="more-2473">
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            After
            <a href="http://mashable.com/2009/08/10/facebook-acquires-friendfeed/" target="_blank" title="BREAKING: Facebook Acquires FriendFeed">
             signing
            </a>
            on to be another piece into the Frankenstein that is Facebook, it might not have long to live, but that helped highlight the use of other
            <a href="http://mashable.com/2009/08/11/streamy-friendfeed/" target="_blank" title="FriendFeed Alternative Streamy Hammered By New Traffic">
             similar services
            </a>
            such as
            <a href="http://www.readwriteweb.com/archives/why_streamy_could_be_the_next_friendfeed.php" target="_blank" title="Why Streamy Could be the Next FriendFeed">
             Streamy
            </a>
            . That should not discourage you from using the service and getting familiar with it, especially if you are a Facebook user. After all there is a very high chance that FriendFeed will be the next FaceBook news feed.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Diigo:
           </h2>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             
             <em>
              If you browse or read a lot on the web, we believe you will find Diigo indispensable. Diigo is two services in one  it is a research and collaborative research tool on the one hand, and a knowledge-sharing community and social content site on the other
             </em>
             .
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            <img alt="diigoLOGO_transparent" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2484" height="71" src="http://blog.thoughtpick.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/diigoLOGO_transparent1.png" style="margin-right: 10px; margin-left: 10px;" width="150"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            - You know how StumbleUpon is all play and no work and del.icio.us is just a very dull dull boy? Well here comes Diigo, a really easy way to just highlight and  nitpick from the web. Making it an indispensable research and information collaboration tool.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Why should I sign up?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <em>
              Highlight
             </em>
              Have you ever done research on a topic and you just wanted to highlight a certain passage or paragraph for later use? Or did you just wanted to save that
             <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0RvAkTuL02A" target="_blank" title="Diigo V3: Highlight &amp; Share the Web! Social Bookmarking 2.0">
              little flash video
             </a>
             from a webpage and share it with others? Now you can just do so and just
             <strong>
              focus on whats important
             </strong>
             .
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Sticky Notes
             </em>
             
             <strong>
              We all use sticky notes in one way or another.
             </strong>
             Have you opened your mothers cookbook, where she just adds a little secret ingredient step on sticky notes attached to that blueberry pancake recipe? You can do exactly the same thing now on the web and share it with your family and friends or create just a private commenting section on a blog. You can even export a page with all the highlights and sticky notes to someone who isnt on Diigo through
             <a href="http://www.diigo.com/06uay" target="_blank" title="Annotated Link Example :Thoughpick">
              an annotated link like this
             </a>
             . You can also tweet that link from the toolbar as well.
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Lists and Annotations
             </em>
              Did you ever get boggled down by
             <strong>
              how irrelevant some of the results are when you click on a tag
             </strong>
             in delicious? Lists on Diigo are hand-picked topic specific bookmarks with the advantage of inline excerpts and comments that are always a good place to start your research in.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Why you shouldnt bother with it?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            To get the most out of Diigo, you need to install the toolbar making your browser even more cultured and sometimes websites that have a lot of public highlights and notes can get confusing and spam your screen. Although lets face it we can do away with toolbar or two on our browser, you know one of those messenger toolbars perhaps, and you can also
            <a href="http://blog.diigo.com/2008/03/27/tip-of-the-day-how-to-customize-diigo-toolbar/" target="_blank" title="Diigo Blog  Tip of the day ~ How to customize Diigo toolbar">
             customize or hide your toolbar
            </a>
            as you see fit. As for the clutter of notes, you can just wish them away with a couple of clicks.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Finally
           </h2>
           <p>
            It would be really nice to connect with you on
            <a href="http://www.diigo.com/user/bambamieh" target="_blank" title="Bambamieh Diigo bookmarks">
             Diigo
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://friendfeed.com/bambamieh" target="_blank" title="BamBamieh FriendFeed">
             FriendFeed
            </a>
            and share the web.
            <strong>
             Do you use FriendFeed or Diigo? What do you use them for? What do you think will change with FriendFeed now that they were bought by Facebook
            </strong>
            ?
           </p>
