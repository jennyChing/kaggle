     <p>
      <b>
       <span class="nowrap">
        2012 VP
        <sub>
         113
        </sub>
       </span>
      </b>
      is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planetoid" title="Planetoid">
       planetoid
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Sednoid" title="Sednoid">
       outer reaches of the Solar System
      </a>
      . It is the object with the largest known
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/perihelion" title="wikt:perihelion">
       perihelion
      </a>
      (closest approach to the Sun) in the Solar System, larger than
      <a href="/wiki/90377_Sedna" title="90377 Sedna">
       Sedna
      </a>
      's.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-20140326_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYT-20140326-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its discovery was announced on 26 March 2014.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nature_letter_4-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-nature_letter-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nature_news_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nature_news-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It has an
      <a href="/wiki/Absolute_magnitude#Solar_System_bodies_.28H.29" title="Absolute magnitude">
       absolute magnitude (H)
      </a>
      of 4.0,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-MPC-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      which makes it
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_possible_dwarf_planets" title="List of possible dwarf planets">
       likely to be a dwarf planet
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown-dplist_5-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Brown-dplist-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and it is accepted as a dwarf planet by some.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sheppard_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Sheppard-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is expected to be about half the size of
      <a href="/wiki/90377_Sedna" title="90377 Sedna">
       Sedna
      </a>
      and similar in size to
      <a href="/wiki/38628_Huya" title="38628 Huya">
       Huya
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lakdawalla2014_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Lakdawalla2014-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Its surface is believed to have a pink tinge, resulting from chemical changes produced by the effect of radiation on frozen water, methane, and carbon dioxide.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This optical color is consistent with formation in the gas-giant region and not the classical
      <a href="/wiki/Kuiper_belt" title="Kuiper belt">
       Kuiper belt
      </a>
      , which is dominated by ultra-red colored objects.

       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery">
       Discovery
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_VP113&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Discovery">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <span class="nowrap">
       2012 VP
       <sub>
        113
       </sub>
      </span>
      was first observed on 5 November 2012
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPEC2014-F40_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-MPEC2014-F40-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      with
      <a href="/wiki/National_Optical_Astronomy_Observatory" title="National Optical Astronomy Observatory">
       NOAO's
      </a>
      4-meter
      <a href="/wiki/V%C3%ADctor_M._Blanco_Telescope" title="Vctor M. Blanco Telescope">
       Vctor M. Blanco Telescope
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Tololo_Inter-American_Observatory" title="Cerro Tololo Inter-American Observatory">
       Cerro Tololo Inter-American Observatory
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_announcement_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NASA_announcement-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Institution_for_Science" title="Carnegie Institution for Science">
       Carnegies
      </a>
      6.5-meter
      <a href="/wiki/Magellan_Telescopes" title="Magellan Telescopes">
       Magellan telescope
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Las_Campanas_Observatory" title="Las Campanas Observatory">
       Las Campanas Observatory
      </a>
      in Chile was used to determine its orbit and surface properties.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_announcement_11-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-NASA_announcement-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Before being announced to the public, it was only tracked by Cerro Tololo Inter-American Observatory (
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_observatory_codes#800-899" title="List of observatory codes">
       807
      </a>
      ) and Las Campanas Observatory (
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_observatory_codes#300-399" title="List of observatory codes">
       304
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC_6-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-MPC-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It has an
      <a href="/wiki/Observation_arc" title="Observation arc">
       observation arc
      </a>
      of about 2 years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpldata_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-jpldata-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Two
      <a href="/wiki/Precovery" title="Precovery">
       precovery
      </a>
      measurements from 22 October 2011 have been reported.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPC_6-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-MPC-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A primary issue with observing it, and finding precovery observations of it, is that at an
      <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">
       apparent magnitude
      </a>
      of 23, it is too faint for most telescopes to easily observe.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Nickname">
       Nickname
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_VP113&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nickname">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <span class="nowrap">
       2012 VP
       <sub>
        113
       </sub>
      </span>
      was abbreviated "VP" and nicknamed "Biden" by the discovery team, after
      <a href="/wiki/Joe_Biden" title="Joe Biden">
       Joe Biden
      </a>
      , the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice_president_of_the_United_States" title="Vice president of the United States">
       Vice President (VP) of the United States
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nature_news_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nature_news-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Orbit">
       Orbit
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_VP113&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Orbit">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <span class="nowrap">
       2012 VP
       <sub>
        113
       </sub>
      </span>
      has the largest
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/perihelion" title="wikt:perihelion">
       perihelion
      </a>
      distance of any known object in the
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">
       Solar System
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpl-search_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-jpl-search-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its last
      <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
       perihelion
      </a>
      was around 1979,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        a
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      at a distance of 80
      <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">
       AU
      </a>
      ;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpldata_2-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-jpldata-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      it is currently 83AU from the Sun. Only four other Solar System objects are known to have perihelia larger than 47 AU:
      <a href="/wiki/90377_Sedna" title="90377 Sedna">
       Sedna
      </a>
      (76 AU),
      <span class="nowrap">
       <a href="/wiki/2004_XR190" title="2004 XR190">
        2004 XR
        <span style="position: relative; top: 0.3em;">
         <span style="font-size:80%;">
          190
         </span>
        </span>
       </a>
      </span>
      (51 AU),
      <span class="nowrap">
       <a href="/wiki/2010_GB174" title="2010 GB174">
        2010 GB
        <span style="position: relative; top: 0.3em;">
         <span style="font-size:80%;">
          174
         </span>
        </span>
       </a>
      </span>
      (48 AU), and
      <span class="nowrap">
       <a href="/wiki/2004_VN112" title="2004 VN112">
        2004 VN
        <span style="position: relative; top: 0.3em;">
         <span style="font-size:80%;">
          112
         </span>
        </span>
       </a>
      </span>
      (47 AU).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpl-search_12-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-jpl-search-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The paucity of bodies with perihelia at 5075 AU appears not to be an observational artifact.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nature_letter_4-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-nature_letter-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      It is possibly a member of a hypothesized inner
      <a href="/wiki/Oort_cloud" title="Oort cloud">
       Oort cloud
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lakdawalla2014_3-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Lakdawalla2014-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_announcement_11-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-NASA_announcement-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wall2014_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Wall2014-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It has a perihelion,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argument_of_perihelion" title="Argument of perihelion">
       argument of perihelion
      </a>
      and current position in the sky similar to those of Sedna.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lakdawalla2014_3-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Lakdawalla2014-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In fact, all known Solar System bodies with semi-major axes over 150 AU and perihelia greater than
      <a href="/wiki/Neptune" title="Neptune">
       Neptune
      </a>
      's have arguments of perihelion clustered near 34055.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nature_letter_4-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-nature_letter-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This could indicate a similar formation mechanism for these bodies.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nature_letter_4-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-nature_letter-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <span class="nowrap">
       <a href="/wiki/(148209)_2000_CR105" title="(148209) 2000 CR105">
        (148209) 2000 CR
        <span style="position: relative; top: 0.3em;">
         <span style="font-size:80%;">
          105
         </span>
        </span>
       </a>
      </span>
      was the first such object discovered.
     </p>
     <p>
      It is currently unknown how
      <span class="nowrap">
       2012 VP
       <sub>
        113
       </sub>
      </span>
      acquired a perihelion distance beyond the Kuiper belt. The characteristics of its orbit, like those of Sedna's, have been explained as possibly created by a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_nearest_stars#Future_and_past" title="List of nearest stars">
       passing star
      </a>
      or a trans-Neptunian planet of several
      <a href="/wiki/Earth_mass" title="Earth mass">
       Earth masses
      </a>
      hundreds of astronomical units from the Sun.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-phys.2014.edge_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-phys.2014.edge-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The orbital architecture of the trans-Plutonian region may signal the presence of more than one planet.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcos2014_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Marcos2014-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcos2015_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Marcos2015-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <span class="nowrap">
       2012 VP
       <sub>
        113
       </sub>
      </span>
      could even be captured from another planetary system.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sheppard_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Sheppard-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, it is considered more likely that the perihelion of
      <span class="nowrap">
       2012 VP
       <sub>
        113
       </sub>
      </span>
      was raised by multiple interactions within the crowded confines of the
      <a href="/wiki/Open_cluster" title="Open cluster">
       open star cluster
      </a>
      in which the Sun formed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lakdawalla2014_3-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-Lakdawalla2014-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
