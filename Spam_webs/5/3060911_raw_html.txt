         Authors On My Shelves
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-07-29T00:00:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          July 29, 2014
         </time>
         
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://rivercityreading.com/2014/07/authors-on-my-shelves.html#comments">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/rivercityreading.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Harry1.jpg">
          <img alt="Harry" class="size-full wp-image-1909 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/rivercityreading.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Harry1.jpg?resize=720%2C431"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Todays Top Ten Tuesday topic from
         <a href="http://www.brokeandbookish.com/">
          The Broke and The Bookish
         </a>
         is Ten Authors I Own The Most Books From. Ah, so this is where it gets weird to be someone who doesnt read many book seriesor classics. Somehow you end up with tons of pairs and start tobuildcollections of a few favorite authors, which is where I am right now. But we can still take a peek. First,I do have a series that tops my list: thanks to
         <strong>
          J.K. Rowling
         </strong>
         and
         <strong>
          Harry Potter
         </strong>
         , thats seven books from one author.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/rivercityreading.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Books1.jpg">
          <img alt="Books1" class="size-full wp-image-1895 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/rivercityreading.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Books1.jpg?resize=700%2C350"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         After reading and falling in love with
         <a href="http://rivercityreading.com/2013/03/the-interestings-by-meg-wolitzer.html">
          <strong>
           The Interestings
          </strong>
         </a>
         last year, Ive been building up my Meg Wolitzer backlist through library sales (plus
         <a href="http://rivercityreading.com/2014/06/bea-wrap-part-2-book-expo.html">
          BEA for the upcoming
          <strong>
           Belzhar
          </strong>
         </a>
         ) and its almost complete.
        </p>
        <p>
         Erik Larson iswell loved by both my husband and I, as evidenced by the condition of his books.
         <a href="http://www.indiebound.org/book/9780375725609">
          <strong>
           The Devil in the White City
          </strong>
         </a>
         was
         <a href="http://rivercityreading.com/2013/11/non-fiction-november-week-1-favorite.html">
          my non-fiction gateway
         </a>
         , so it gets high ranking on my bookshelf.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/rivercityreading.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Books2.jpg">
          <img alt="Books2" class="size-full wp-image-1897 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/rivercityreading.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Books2.jpg?resize=700%2C350"/>
         </a>
         Ive been building a haphazard Margaret Atwood collection over the years, mostly through used book stores and library sales. Id really like them to all look pretty, but Im quite in love with
         <a href="http://instagram.com/p/p4BYbtNnJL/?modal=true">
          my old copy of
          <strong>
           The Handmaids Tale
          </strong>
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         My Dave Eggers stack is pretty, but sometimes posts like this make you realize things like, WHERE IS MY COPY OF
         <a href="http://www.indiebound.org/book/9780375725784">
          <strong>
           A HEARTBREAKING WORK OF STAGGERING GENIUS
          </strong>
         </a>
         !?!?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/rivercityreading.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Books4.jpg">
          <img alt="Books4" class="size-large wp-image-1896 aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/rivercityreading.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Books4.jpg?resize=700%2C513"/>
         </a>
         The rest are some of my stacks of three, which seems to be the most common number when it comes to books from a single author, though several of those collections still feel incomplete.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Who are some of the authors you own the most books from?
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
