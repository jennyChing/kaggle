							&lt;h1&gt;Home Improvement Can Wait&lt;/h1&gt;
				

				&lt;div class="meta single"&gt;&lt;span class="date"&gt;October 12, 2010&lt;/span&gt; &lt;a href="http://www.another71.com/cpa-exam/club-75-cpa-exam-review-community/" rel="category tag"&gt;Club 75 Candidate Bloggers&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
			

				&lt;div class="content"&gt;&lt;div class="bottomcontainerBox" style="border:1px solid #808080;background-color:#F0F4F9;"&gt;
			&lt;div style="float:left; width:95px;padding-right:10px; margin:4px 4px 4px 4px;height:30px;"&gt;
			&lt;iframe src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.another71.com%2Fclub-75-cpa-exam-blogger-jw-2%2F&amp;layout=button_count&amp;show_faces=false&amp;width=95&amp;action=like&amp;font=verdana&amp;colorscheme=light&amp;height=21" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowTransparency="true" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:95px; height:21px;"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
			&lt;div style="float:left; width:80px;padding-right:10px; margin:4px 4px 4px 4px;height:30px;"&gt;
			&lt;g:plusone size="medium" href="http://www.another71.com/club-75-cpa-exam-blogger-jw-2/"&gt;&lt;/g:plusone&gt;
			&lt;/div&gt;
			&lt;div style="float:left; width:95px;padding-right:10px; margin:4px 4px 4px 4px;height:30px;"&gt;
			&lt;a href="http://twitter.com/share" class="twitter-share-button" data-url="http://www.another71.com/club-75-cpa-exam-blogger-jw-2/"  data-text="Home Improvement Can Wait" data-count="horizontal"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;
			&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div style="float:left; width:120px;padding-right:10px; margin:4px 4px 4px 4px;height:30px;"&gt;&lt;script type="in/share" data-url="http://www.another71.com/club-75-cpa-exam-blogger-jw-2/" data-counter="right"&gt;&lt;/script&gt;&lt;/div&gt;			
			&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div style="clear:both"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div style="padding-bottom:4px;"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;center&gt;&lt;img src="http://www.another71.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/family.jpg"&gt;&lt;/center&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;em&gt;J.W. is a weekly Club 75 Blogger as he documents his journey through the CPA Exam. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;He has been a member of Another71.com&#8217;s &lt;a href="http://club75.another71.com/"&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Club 75&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/a&gt; since August 2010.&lt;/em&gt; &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Week 2:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Captain Obvious statement of the year: &lt;strong&gt;Studying for the CPA Exam is rough&lt;/strong&gt;. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I will admit it has been tough to get started, and when I do get started it&#8217;s hard to maintain studying 3 to 4 hours a day when there are so many other distractions and things that need attention aside from work: little league, that bathroom that is about 90% finished, the overgrown plants, the evolving mess that is slowly over-taking our house. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;That&#8217;s what I&#8217;m looking forward to the most when this journey is over. That&#8217;s right, painting, pulling weeds and changing the oil in the car. No celebratory dinner or party will feel as good as putting on a pair of old jeans and T-Shirt and getting up on a ladder to edge the ceiling and thinking of nothing else except that I hope I don&#8217;t get any paint on the ceiling. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I want to do these things without thinking that if I get a 73 on FAR, I&#8217;m going to regret not studying right now and then the feeling of guilt creeps over.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I&#8217;ve done better with my studying recently. With the calendar hitting October, it clicked almost like magic that I need to study more, which I have done. I&#8217;ve always been like that, a moderate procrastinator. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Hopefully I&#8217;ll be able to keep it up and I&#8217;ll catch up to my original plan:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Finish the Yaeger CPA Review DVD&#8217;s in 10 days. &lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Re-write notes for a week while doing 30 MCQ&#8217;s a day. &lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Then MCQ my butt of for the 10 days up until the test, while throwing in reviewing flashcards, the Yaegar FAR handout and Jeff&#8217;s FAR Study Guide.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;28 days left..&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Week 3:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I am just past the halfway point in my studying for FAR, I am 4 weeks in, with 3 weeks left to go. Ive begun writing down my study goals and that has been working out very well for me. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;If I want to get through a &lt;a href="http://www.another71.com/goto/yaeger"&gt;Yaeger&lt;/a&gt; DVD by a certain date, I have it written down, and Ive been sticking to it which is good. I am starting to get the sinking feeling that I may have over-estimated how much time Id have studied by now. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I will have finished off the DVDs by the end of week 5, with 2 weeks left after that I plan on doing at least 60 questions per day for the 15 days I will have left. Which will give me about 900 MCQs in the final 2 weeks. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I havent kept up with the exact amount Ive done so far, if I had to estimate, Id say  its around 300 or so and Ill probably do another 150 or so this week. Ill supplement this with re-writing my notes and perhaps re-watching sections of the DVDs where I am not quite comfortable. At this rate, by test time Ill have put in about 100 hours if I continue at my now 3 hour a day pace. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Ive really ramped it up now that its getting to crunch time, Im just hoping my slow start wont land me a dreaded 74.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;-J.W.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
