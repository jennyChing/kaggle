          <p>
           <i>
            Avoiding financial errors as a landlord
           </i>
          </p>
          <p>
           When you are the owner of an apartment like one at Western Heights in Andheri (W), Mumbai  2/3/4 BHK homes by Adani Realty  you will have no trouble getting tenants due to the quality of the apartment as well as the in-demand location. However, getting tenants is only the beginning of ones challenges as a landlord. Landlords must also be careful to avoid making costly financial mistakes.
          </p>
          <p>
           Even before letting the tenants in, one should figure out how to price the rent. Over-pricing may lead to prospective tenants ignoring your property, and under-pricing eats away at your income  the best way to figure out the right rent to charge is to look at what similar properties in the area are charging.
          </p>
          <p>
           Another mistake is not effectively handling late-paying tenants. One way to motivate a late-paying tenant is to simply charge a penalty for every late day from the date on which the rent is due  this should be part of the rental agreement. One should closely monitor a late-paying tenant, and have a set policy regarding the level of rental arrears leading to eviction, which one should execute without any sympathy.
          </p>
          <p>
           You may be legally liable in the event of tenants getting injured due to lack of timely maintenance and repairs  so promptly carry out repairs as and when issues arise. Taking landlord insurance is important as it may insure you against the many risks associated with owning a rental property, like malicious and accidental damage.
          </p>
          <p>
           A mistake that many landlords make is that they assume that they can do all the work themselves, and try to save some money by not appointing a property manager. But hiring a property manager can save one a lot of money in the long run, as they will take care of inspections as well as screen tenants for any history of defaulting on rental payments, malicious damage, eviction etc.
          </p>
          <p>
           A landlord needs to manage his rental property smartly in order to make it a sound income-generating investment.
