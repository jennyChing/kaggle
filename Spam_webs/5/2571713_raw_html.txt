    Acacia
   </b>
   (
   <i>
    Acacia Nilotica
   </i>
   )
   <font size="-1">
    Also called gum arabic.
    <br/>
    Gender: Masculine, Planet: Sun, Element: Air, Deities: Osiris,
Astarte, Diana, Ra
   </font>

  <p>
   Protection, Psychic Powers. Burn with sandalwood to open psychic
centers. Parts used: dried gum, leaves, wood
   <br/>

   <a name="anchor31106">

   <b>
    Aconite
   </b>
   (
   <i>
    Aconitum Napellus
   </i>
   )
   <font size="-1">
    Also called wolfsbane, monkshood, blue rocket
   </font>
   <b>
    <font color="#ff0000" size="-1">
     *POISON* Don't ingest.
    </font>
   </b>
   <font size="-1">
    <br/>
    Gender: Feminine, Planet: Saturn, Sign: Capricorn, Element: Water,
Deities: Hecate, Medea
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   Protection, Invisibility. Use this herb with great caution
to consecrate the athame or ritual knife. Make an infusion with
the leaves or root to banish prior energy from magickal blades
and to infuse it with protection. The root or leaves may be burned
as incense for the same purpose. Gather the fresh flowers to make
a tincture to refresh the power of the knives. Use an infusion
as a magickal wash for ritual tools or sacred space. Brings protection
and magickal watchfulness against negative energies in ritual.
Wash a new cauldron in the infusion or burn aconite in its first
fire. Used to invoke Hecate. Wrap the seed in a lizard skin and
carry to become invisible at will. Used to poison arrow tips in
early times.
   <font size="-1">
   </font>
   Also as protection from and
a cure for werewolves.

qualities, therefore it is excellent in dream pillows, especially
mixed with mugwort. Enhances magickal healing. A wash or oil increases
effectiveness of all forms of ritual healing, psychic healing,
or distance healing. Anoint hands with oil to cleanse auras. Creates
a barrier against negative energies. Use if you feel to be under
psychic attack. A counter-magick herb, it not only breaks hexes,
but sends them back to the hexer.
