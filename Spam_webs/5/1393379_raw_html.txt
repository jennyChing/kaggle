          <h3>
           Saturday Slop: Smoot says RGIII Will Get Better Every Game
          </h3>
          <p>
           The weekend edition of the 
           <em>
            Daily Slop
           </em>
           , as seen daily on
           <em>
            Hogs Haven.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/redskins-vs.-bills-game-review-offense/article/2504562?custom_click=rss#.UCY-kqFlSGY" target="_blank">
             Redskins vs. Bills game review (offense) | WashingtonExaminer.com
            </a>
           </strong>
           <br/>
           Speed does make a difference on handoffs. It might not be much of one, but it exists. Granted, sometimes the landmarks might be a little different in these scenarios, but the overall difference was consistent. When Robert Griffin III handed off in a stretch zone run, it took him approximately 1.10 seconds to get it to the running back each time. When Rex Grossman did it, it took him nearly 1.25 seconds. Thats not a knock on Grossman, but it is an example of the speed. Oh, and it wasnt just Griffin. Kirk Cousins times were about the same as Griffins.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://washingtonexaminer.com/redskins-vs.-bills-game-review-defense/article/2504615?custom_click=rss#.UCY8_aFlSGY" target="_blank">
             Redskins vs. Bills game review (defense) | WashingtonExaminer.com
            </a>
           </strong>
           <br/>
           Linebacker Brian Orakpo lined up in a four-point stance the entire first series (all of three plays). There are some NFL coaches who still think hed be best used as a 4-3 defensive end, so it makes sense to have him play with his hand in the dirt on occasion. Orakpo likes doing this and it works at times. Like on the first play when he badly whipped the left tackle with a terrific get-off and speed around the edge. That forced
           <a href="http://www.sbnation.com/nfl/players/3219/ryan-fitzpatrick">
            Ryan Fitzpatrick
           </a>
           up in the pocket. However, thanks to
           <a href="http://www.sbnation.com/nfl/players/4267/adam-carriker">
            Adam Carriker
           </a>
           collapsing the pocket with power, Fitzpatrick had nowhere to go. He sort of slid to his left, but thats where
           <a href="http://www.sbnation.com/nfl/players/131080/ryan-kerrigan">
            Ryan Kerrigan
           </a>
           was waiting. Kerrigan had looped all the way around, going behind the QB and to the other side. When the right tackle bumped into the left tackle in the backfield, it enabled Kerrigan to get home for the sack. But he got there thanks to the initial work of others.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.realredskins.com/rich-tandlers-real-redsk/2012/08/need-to-know-practice-time-changes.html" target="_blank">
             Need to Know: Practice time changes  Rich Tandlers Real Redskins
            </a>
           </strong>
           <br/>
