         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          How to Make Your Morning Run More Smoothly
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          by
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Ashley Pichea
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          <a href="/disclosure" target="_blank">
           <img height="25px" src="http://blog.ashleypichea.com/wp-content/Disclosure.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- Top of Single Post -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-1681596176384384" data-ad-slot="1439289283" style="display:inline-block;width:728px;height:15px">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre like me,
          <a href="http://blog.ashleypichea.com/hello-mornings/" target="_blank" title="Hello, Mornings?">
           youre not a morning person
          </a>
          , and
          <a href="http://blog.ashleypichea.com/struggling-with-self-control-hellomornings/" target="_blank" title="Struggling with Self-Control {HelloMornings}">
           beginning each day may be a struggle
          </a>
          . Ive learned that my mornings can be greatly affected by making some changes at night.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="How to Make Your Morning Run More Smoothly" src="http://blog.ashleypichea.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tips-for-Better-Mornings.jpg" width="700"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          These tips will help make your morning run more smoothly, taking some of the stress out of having to get out of bed:
         </p>
         <h2>
          Prepare as much as you can the night before.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Pick out the
          <a href="http://blog.ashleypichea.com/tag/what-i-wore-wednesday/" target="_blank">
           clothes youre going to wear
          </a>
          the next day. This goes for your kits, too. Help them find their clothes, put their shoes where they can easily get them, and get them to bed early.
         </p>
         <p>
          While your kids are getting ready for bed, you can take that time to prepare lunch boxes if your children take them to school. If you take the time to fix as much as possible of their lunch the night before, it wont take as long the next morning. You can hand them their lunches, already made, right before they have to leave for school.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           This is also a great time to throw together a
           <a href="http://blog.ashleypichea.com/banana-oatmeal-muffin-recipe/" target="_blank" title="Banana Oatmeal Muffin Recipe">
            grab-and-go breakfast
           </a>
           , saving prep time in the morning.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          If you havent already done so, go through your childs backpack. They may have papers you need to sign or sheets of information you need to know about. This is the perfect time to take care of these things so you wont miss an important date.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Get enough rest.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Be sure to go to bed early enough that you can get at least eight hours of sleep. Does this mean you have to go to bed at the same time as the children? Absolutely not, but you do want to get the rest you need, too.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Im thankful that my kids sleep in a bit  it makes dragging myself to bed at midnight a little less exhausting.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <h2>
          Set your alarm clock but place it across the room.
         </h2>
         <p>
          If your alarm is right next to the bed it will be too easy for you to hit the snooze button. Place your clock on the dresser or a table away from the bed. This will cause you to have to physically get up to turn your alarm off.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Try to get up early enough that you wont be rushed.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Give yourself enough time to take care of yourself before you wake the children. I highly recommend (though struggle to do so myself)
          <a href="http://blog.ashleypichea.com/31-pray-for-spouse/" target="_blank" title="31 Days to Pray for Your Spouse Challenge">
           spending a few minutes in prayer
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://blog.ashleypichea.com/a-womans-guide-to-reading-the-bible-in-a-year-diane-stortz/" target="_blank" title="A Womans Guide to Reading the Bible in a Year {Diane Stortz}">
           reading the Bible
          </a>
          .This will help you start the day with a sense of peace and calm before all the hubbub starts.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Play music while you go about your morning routine.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Put on your favorite music to get you moving. If you have peppy music playing, youll be surprised how much you can get done and how good youll feel doing it.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Not everyone is a morning person by nature. That doesnt mean you cant make some changes to your schedule which will help you make your day run smoother. You can do as much as you can the night before, place your alarm across the room, or play music to get you going. Above all, get a good nights sleep so youre rested and able to function.
         </p>
         <h2>
          How do you prepare the night before to help with a positive morning experience?
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
