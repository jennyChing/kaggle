          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/yacks/8611278215/in/photostream/">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="756" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8108/8611278215_f11d32341c_c.jpg" title="Banana Cream Chia Pudding" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Before I show you this amazing recipe, today, let me talk about last year for a moment. As Im prepping myself to write this, is still feels like I have a pit in my stomach, but this will end in a positive light! Some of you may have read about
           <a href="http://veganyackattack.com/2012/04/19/baked-spicy-sweet-potato-tots-with-chipotle-aioli/" target="_blank" title="Baked Spicy Sweet Potato Tots with Chipotle Aioli">
            my grandmother
           </a>
           last year. I very briefly mentioned that she would be moving in with my family because she wasnt doing well and had lung cancer. After that, I didnt say anything else about her, and it wasnt for no reason.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/yacks/8612386252/in/photostream/">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="756" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8545/8612386252_bc43b11f5b_c.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Soon after she moved in, she mentioned that she had felt bloated and never hungry, and really couldnt eat much. I would give her a tiny smoothie and she would be full. I felt like I couldnt help her nourish her body, so that she could heal, even just a little bit. But, two weeks or so after she came to California, we had to take her to the ER; turns out that she had a perforated ulcer (basically a hole in your stomach) and needed to be operated on. While doing tests on her, they found out that the cancer had already spread to her liver; which was, in a word, terrifying.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/yacks/8612386640/in/photostream/">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="756" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8532/8612386640_66e48edc70_c.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           They decided to go through with the operation, and everything was going well for a couple of days. Then liquid started to build up in her lungs, they couldnt stop it, then everything started falling apart. She ended up on life-support with failed kidneys and so many things wrong with her. And Ill never forget the way she looked, and I would never wish that upon anyone. When it was clear that she would not be coming back from this, our family said our goodbyes and her soul left her body. It was a terrible day, less than a month before her 80th birthday. All of us were deeply affected by her passing, and as I type this, I am realizing that this still weighs very heavily on my heart.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/yacks/8612385812/in/photostream/">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="756" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8404/8612385812_914452b9f7_c.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           My grandmother was a crafty woman, shes the one that taught me how to crochet; but she was also an avid quilter. She had tons of quilting fabric, yards and yards of it in so many colors and patterns. Plus, some awesome kitchen ware, which her, my mom, and I share the same passion for. Support was something that my grandma often offered, with the turn of events in my life, which led me to where I am today (photography/blogging/etc.). She would tell me that I was capable and creative, which helped me immensely through some tough times. But, I dont think she knew just how much she would help me.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/yacks/8611279217/in/photostream/">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="756" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8125/8611279217_20b738c0d6_c.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Soon after we sorted through the fabrics, I got the photo gig with Happy Herbivore. Knowing that I would need to photograph 80 different recipes gave me a bit of anxiety. How would I make these shots so different? Then the light went on, Grandmas fabrics! Without those, it would have been 80% harder for me to do what I did, and I just wish that I could thank her now. Ive chosen this post to tell you all this, because three of the items in the photograph belonged to her. Two of the fabrics and this delightful dish, used for cooling food to be served. Im so glad that I can have her continue to help me in my quest to become a better photographer, and I dedicate this post to her. &lt;3
          </p>

           <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
            Banana Cream Chia Pudding
           </div>
           <div class="ERSClear">
           </div>
           <div class="ERSDetails">
            <div class="ERSAuthor">
             Author:
             <span itemprop="author">
              Jackie of Vegan Yack Attack
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="ERSHead">
             Recipe type:
             <span itemprop="recipeCategory">
              Breakfast, Snack
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="ERSHead">
             Cuisine:
             <span itemprop="recipeCuisine">
              American
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="ERSClear">
            </div>
            <div class="ERSTimes">
             <div class="ERSHead">
              Prep time:
              <time datetime="PT7M" itemprop="prepTime">
               7 mins
              </time>
             </div>
             <div class="ERSHead">
              Cook time:
              <time datetime="PT45M" itemprop="cookTime">
               45 mins
              </time>
             </div>
             <div class="ERSHead">
              Total time:
              <time datetime="PT52M" itemprop="totalTime">
               52 mins
              </time>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="ERSClear">
            </div>
            <div class="ERSHead">
             Serves:
             <span itemprop="recipeYield">
              1
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="ERSClear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
            Banana Cream pie-inspired chia pudding! Much healthier and so delicious.
           </div>
           <div class="ERSIngredients">
            <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
             Ingredients
            </div>
            <ul>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               C. Banana
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               C. Canned Light Coconut Milk
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 Tbsp. Water
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 Tbsp. Maple Syrup or Sweeten with a little Stevia
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               tsp. Vanilla Extract
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              Pinch of Sea Salt
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 Tbsp. Chia Seeds
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              Optional: A sprinkling of unsweetened coconut shreds
             </li>
            </ul>
            <div class="ERSClear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSInstructions">
            <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
             Instructions
            </div>
            <ol>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Place the banana, coconut milk, water, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and sea salt into a blender.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Puree on high until completely smooth, then pour into a small jar or bowl.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Add the chia seeds to the liquid and stir in completely. Leave in the refrigerator for at least 45-60 minutes. Or you can leave it overnight.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Sprinkle with coconut shreds and serve chilled!
             </li>
            </ol>
