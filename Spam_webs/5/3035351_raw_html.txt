        <p>
         The healing power of art is on full display in Tulsa at a new exhibit that opened Friday at Living Arts in the Brady District.
        </p>
        <p>
         Speaking Volumes is all about transforming hate as the artists turned racist propaganda into thought-provoking art.
        </p>
        <p>
         My work as artist and an activist is to create art within the context of human rights education, said Exhibit Curator, Kate Knight.
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope that they have conversations with each other about their experiences, what they've observed and what we can do. I hope that they bring their children and their students to learn about what our shared history is and help us shape a brighter more just future, she said.
        </p>
        <p>
         Knight said she's not positive art can change our direction, but that it's a lifeline for her.
        </p>
