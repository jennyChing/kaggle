       <div class="entry-content col-md-offset-1 col-md-10 col-lg-offset-2 col-lg-8">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="380" src="http://images.proflowers.com/rtimages/SharisBerries/custom/blog/2014/1/pb_1.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0;" width="615"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         We dont need a holiday to celebrate peanut butter, but its a good excuse. This year, in honor of National Peanut Butter Lovers Day, we decided to take the beloved PB sandwich to the next level, from the deliciously inventive (like PB and rose petal jelly), to the daringly unsanctioned (by us) PB and tuna fringe.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Breakfast of Champions
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          PB Eggs Benedict:
         </span>
         Sub creamy peanut butter for Hollandaise sauce and add sesame oil for an Asian twist on the brunch classic.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          PB egg and cheese:
         </span>
         What to eat  peanut butter toast or fried eggs? Why not both? Born from one bloggers have it all (for breakfast) mentality, this sandwich merges peanut butter toast with fried eggs and cheese.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Waffle it:
         </span>
         Make your favorite peanut butter sandwich and toss it on the waffle iron for added crunch.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          With French flair:
         </span>
         Peanut butter and jelly on French toast will make all the other bread in your kitchen seem boring.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Add cornflakes:
         </span>
         Part French toast, part fried chicken, this sandwich is coated in crushed corn flakes and grilled. Youll want to start and end your day with it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Wrap it up:
         </span>
         For breakfast on the go, mix peanut butter with honey, granola, nuts and dried cherries and wrap it all up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Not Your Grannys Jam
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="380" src="http://images.proflowers.com/rtimages/SharisBerries/custom/blog/2014/1/pb_5.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0;" width="615"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Exotic preserves:
         </span>
         Youll never go back to grape once you try Habanero apricot jelly. Theres no limit to the unusual jellies and jams around from rose, hibiscus, prickly pear and whiskey clementine, to lavender and baobab.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Whole fruit:
         </span>
         Moms are always on the lookout for the latest ways to keep kids interested. Ditch the jelly and insert their favorite fruit, whole. Start with orange slices, apples, bananas or grapes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Fruit and cream cheese:
         </span>
         Make a peanut butter sandwich that tastes like peanut butter cheesecake. Simply add strawberry cream cheese or blueberry jam to create your favorite flavor.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          The matrix:
         </span>
         Create nine sandwiches in one. Use six different spreads to make three rows of peanut butter and jelly on one slice of bread and three columns on the other. The result: nine transcendental squares of flavor pairs.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Get Crunchy
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Pickles:
         </span>
         Its not as strange as it sounds, say lovers of this duo. Speared, sliced, wholepick your pickle and take a bite.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Potato chips:
         </span>
         If you like mixing sweet with salty, PB and potato chips will be your new favorite food group.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Chocolate-covered potato chips:
         </span>
         Whats better than PB and potato chips? PB and chocolate-covered potato chips, of course.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Pretzels:
         </span>
         Spread a thick layer of peanut butter between pretzels instead of bread. Dip the mini-sandwiches in melted chocolate, take a batch to your next party and watch them vanish.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          You say Tomato, We Say Tomato Peanut Butter Sandwich
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="380" src="http://images.proflowers.com/rtimages/SharisBerries/custom/blog/2014/1/pb_2.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0;" width="615"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Celery:
         </span>
         Top celery sticks with peanut butter and raisins and eat it on a hot dog bun for the sandwich version of ants on a log.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Tomato:
         </span>
         It seems unusual, but this combo has been around awhile. Some of its biggest fans also add mayo, lettuce, salad dressing or even banana.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Onion:
         </span>
         This odd duo has devotees all over the web. Some have reportedly been eating peanut butter and raw onion since they were kids.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Cabbage and Carrots:
         </span>
         Bring out your inner Thai with a combo that tastes almost like a lettuce wrap, especially when you add a dash of soy sauce.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Celebrity Style
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="380" src="http://images.proflowers.com/rtimages/SharisBerries/custom/blog/2014/1/pb_4.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0;" width="615"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Seinfeld:
         </span>
         The famous comedian claims hes been a peanut butter fanatic since he was a kid. New York Citys Peanut Butter &amp; Co., a peanut butter sandwich shop, named their peanut butter, honey and cinnamon bagel sandwich after him.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Elvis:
         </span>
         The King loved to fry his peanut butter, bacon and banana sandwiches. His mom used to brown them in a frying pan with butter.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Bill Clinton:
         </span>
         In numerous newspaper interviews, the 42nd president chose peanut butter and banana as his favorite sandwich.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          George W. Bush:
         </span>
         Peanut butter and honey was one of the presidents favorite lunches while living in the White House.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Bread 2.0
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Chocolate:
         </span>
         Chocolate makes everything better, like chocolate bread with PB and raspberry jam. Is it a sandwich or dessert? Either way, well eat it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Fruit:
         </span>
         Spread peanut butter between two slices of banana or apple and add a square of dark chocolate. Its a delicious treat that takes seconds to make.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Puzzle it:
         </span>
         Get a little artsy and use a puzzle-shaped cookie-cutter to shape the bread on your favorite PB sandwich. Its a hit with kids and kids at heart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Cake:
         </span>
         Peanut butter and jelly between cake slices? We dont know anyone who would say no to that.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          PB &amp; sushi:
         </span>
         No chopsticks required. Spread peanut butter and jelly on a slice of bread, cut and roll into sushi pieces.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          The PB Fringe
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="380" src="http://images.proflowers.com/rtimages/SharisBerries/custom/blog/2014/1/pb_3.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0;" width="615"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Tuna:
         </span>
         There are more than a few admitted PB and tuna connoisseurs out there combining their PB with tuna, mayo and occasionally cucumber.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Tuna &amp; PB burger:
         </span>
         Even
         <em>
          Bon Appetit
         </em>
         has jumped on the PB burger bandwagon. Some recipes add bacon, which as we all know, makes everything better.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Sardines:
         </span>
         The peanut butter made it better, according to a YouTube video of people who actually tried this combo. They liked the saltiness of the fish mixed with the crunchiness of chunky peanut butter.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Salmon:
         </span>
         Whether youre using salmon cream cheese or a grilled plank of the fish itself, weve also seen internet evidence people have actually tried this comboand enjoyed it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Your Creation: PB &amp; 
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now its your turn. Tell us what scrumptious peanut butter combo you love. We promise well try it, unless it has tuna in it
        </p>
