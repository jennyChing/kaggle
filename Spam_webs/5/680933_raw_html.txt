       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Why Are Christians Leaving the Church? Turns Out Its the Churches Fault
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2011-09-29T15:00:14+00:00">
         September 29, 2011
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://www.patheos.com/blogs/friendlyatheist/author/hemant-mehta/" rel="author" title="Hemant Mehta">
           Hemant Mehta
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://www.patheos.com/blogs/friendlyatheist/2011/09/29/why-are-christians-leaving-the-church-turns-out-its-the-churches-fault/#comments">
          <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="45601 http://www.patheos.com/blogs/friendlyatheist/?p=45601">
           100 Comments
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <strong>
          David Kinnaman
         </strong>
         , the president of the Barna Group (a Christian-focused polling organization), just released his newest book,
         <em>
          <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0801013143/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=wwwfriendlyat-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=217145&amp;creative=399373&amp;creativeASIN=0801013143" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
           You Lost Me: Why Young Christians are Leaving Church and Rethinking Faith
          </a>
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0801013143/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=wwwfriendlyat-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=217145&amp;creative=399373&amp;creativeASIN=0801013143" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="500" src="http://covers.booktopia.com.au/big/978080/101/9780801014086.jpg" width="323"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p>
         So why are Christians 
         <em>
          real
         </em>
         , churchgoing Christians  leaving the faith later in life?
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <em>
           No single reason dominated the break-up between church and young adults. Instead, a variety of reasons emerged.
           <strong>
            Overall, the research uncovered six significant themes why nearly three out of every five young Christians (59%) disconnect either permanently or for an extended period of time from church life after age 15.
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         You can see
         <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.barna.org/teens-next-gen-articles/528-six-reasons-young-christians-leave-church#.ToN57Urpisu.facebook" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
          Kinnamans six reasons here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         In short
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          The Church is too insular  They tell you everything
          <em>
           outside
          </em>
          the church is bad and wrong even though young people know thats not the case.
         </li>
         <p>
         </p>
         <li>
          Church isnt important, relevant, or interesting to the younger generation.
         </li>
         <p>
         </p>
         <li>
          Christians are too anti-science.
         </li>
         <p>
         </p>
         <li>
          Christians are sex-negative, wrongly pushing abstinence-only education and avoiding frank discussions about sex.
         </li>
         <p>
         </p>
         <li>
          Christianity is too exclusive  youre either one of them or youre the enemy.
         </li>
         <p>
         </p>
         <li>
          Christians are hostile to those who doubt any part of the faith.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         I have no reason to doubt Kinnamans findings  and keep in mind these are only the
         <em>
          top
         </em>
         six reasons, not
         <em>
          all
         </em>
         the reasons.  Ill admit to being surprised that I just stopped believing what they preached and I was ashamed of the churchs stance on homosexuality didnt make the cut.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres the takeaway from the findings, though:
         <strong>
          Christians arent leaving the faith because people like us are pulling them away from it.  Theyre leaving the faith because the Church is pushing them away.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Even if atheists never wrote another book or blog post, young people will continue to fall away from Christianity.  We can always help the process move faster, though, by constantly discussing/debating these issues in our communities, challenging the lies they tell, pointing out their hypocrisy and intolerance at every opportunity, and giving brand new atheists a community to join when they leave their Christian groups.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Church is doing the bulk of our work for us.  But theres no reason we cant help them out.
        </p>
        <p>
         (Thanks to
         <strong>
          Nancy
         </strong>
         for the link!)
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </p>
