     <p>
      <b>
       Michael "Mike" John Rinder
      </b>
      (
      <span class="nowrap">
       <span class="IPA nopopups">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">
         /
         <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
          <span title="// primary stress follows">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'r' in 'rye'">
           r
          </span>
          <span title="// short 'i' in 'bid'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'n' in 'no'">
           n
          </span>
          <span title="'d' in 'dye'">
           d
          </span>
          <span title="// 'a' in 'about'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'r' in 'rye'">
           r
          </span>
         </span>
         /
        </a>
       </span>
      </span>
      ; born April 10, 1955) is an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_people" title="Australian people">
       Australian
      </a>
      former senior executive of the
      <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scientology_International" title="Church of Scientology International">
       Church of Scientology International
      </a>
      (CSI) and the
      <a href="/wiki/Sea_Org" title="Sea Org">
       Sea Organization
      </a>
      based in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-john_cook_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-john_cook-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      From 1982 to 2007, Rinder served on the Board of Directors of CSI and also held the post of Executive Director of its
      <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Special_Affairs" title="Office of Special Affairs">
       Office of Special Affairs
      </a>
      , overseeing the corporate, legal and public relations matters of the Church at the international level.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bio-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Rinder left the Church in 2007 after becoming disillusioned with what he perceived to be the increasingly
      <a href="/wiki/Authoritarianism" title="Authoritarianism">
       authoritarian
      </a>
      nature of senior management under
      <a href="/wiki/David_Miscavige" title="David Miscavige">
       David Miscavige
      </a>
      , instead becoming an
      <a href="/wiki/Free_Zone_(Scientology)" title="Free Zone (Scientology)">
       independent Scientologist
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leaving_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leaving-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCIndependent_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBCIndependent-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      <span class="mw-headline" id="Scientology_career">
       Scientology career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [

     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">
       Early years
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Rinder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Rinder is an
      <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
       Australian
      </a>
      national who joined the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_Organization" title="Sea Organization">
       Sea Org
      </a>
      on the Apollo at 18. In a 2006 interview with
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">
        Rolling Stone
       </a>
      </i>
      , Rinder said he had experienced discrimination in Australia during the period when the Australian state of Victoria had banned Scientology: "You couldn't own Scientology books ... If you did, you had to hide them because if the police came and found them, they'd take them away."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reitman_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-reitman-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Office_of_Special_Affairs">
       Office of Special Affairs
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Rinder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Office of Special Affairs">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <table class="metadata plainlinks ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation">
      <tr>
       <td class="mbox-image">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg">
         <img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/>
        </a>
       </td>
       <td class="mbox-text">
        <span class="mbox-text-span">
         This section requires

           (May 2015)

         </small>

     <p>
      As Executive Director of the Office of Special Affairs, he served as the chief spokesperson and representative of the Church to the media, until replaced by
      <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Davis_(Scientology)" title="Tommy Davis (Scientology)">
       Tommy Davis
      </a>
      in 2005 under orders from David Miscavige.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lwright_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-lwright-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Departure">
       Departure
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Rinder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Departure">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In March 2007, Rinder was sent as the international spokesman of the Church to defend Scientology from
      <a href="/wiki/John_Sweeney_(journalist)" title="John Sweeney (journalist)">
       John Sweeney
      </a>
      , who was filming a BBC documentary titled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Scientology_and_Me" title="Scientology and Me">
        Scientology and Me
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leaving_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leaving-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Rinder defended Scientology leader
      <a href="/wiki/David_Miscavige" title="David Miscavige">
       David Miscavige
      </a>
      , but Miscavige was unhappy with the documentary.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leaving_5-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leaving-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As a result, Rinder "was to report to the church's facility in Sussex, England, and dig ditches" and then was allowed to return to the United States.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leaving_5-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leaving-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Rinder decided to leave the church instead.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leaving_5-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leaving-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Rinder went to Virginia and told the church he wanted to talk to his wife and also wanted his possessions. He did not talk to his wife, but was sent a FedEx package with a check for $5,000, but his family photos were not sent.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leaving_5-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leaving-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Rinder and his first wife, Cathy, divorced after 35 years. He and his second wife, Christie King Collbran, were married in 2013 and have a son. He has no contact with his two adult children from his first marriage.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leaving_5-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leaving-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Rinder's official biography has since been removed from the official Church of Scientology website.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-bio-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2009,
      <i>
       St Petersburg Times
      </i>
      asked Rinder for an interview, but he declined.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TruthRunDown_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-TruthRunDown-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Then a month later, two Washington-based Scientology lawyers went to his home unannounced, informed Rinder that they knew about the newspaper's visit and asked what he had revealed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TruthRunDown_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-TruthRunDown-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Subsequently, he spoke to the
      <i>
       Times
      </i>
      about Scientology's management and repeated beatings he gave as well as received.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>

      <a href="/wiki/Clearwater,_Florida" title="Clearwater, Florida">

      </a>

       </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="British Broadcasting Corporation">

      The dispute continued into the office of an unsuspecting and startled doctor. During the incident Rinder's then wife, Cathy, said she was attacked by Mike Rinder after trying to talk to him. She also stated that he shoved their daughter and almost broke his brother's finger. Rinder recalls Cathy physically trying to stop him from getting away from her. The police report of the incident says Cathy's injury came about as "incidental contact". (Pinellas County Sheriff's Office, Report # SO10-111460.) Rinder has admitted to almost breaking his brother's finger."
