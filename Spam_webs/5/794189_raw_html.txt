           <div class="box-share-item for-expanded">
            <a class="addthis_button_expanded">
             More 
            </a>
           </div>

         <div class="featured-image">
          <img alt="" class="attachment-post-thumbnail" data-description="The Inion family, the People's Choice Travelers of the Year for 2014, in Puerto Vallarta, Mexico (Photograph by Jillian Mitchell)" height="394" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2014/10/inion-family-puerto-vallarta-mitchell-2.jpg" title="Picture of the Inion family on the beach - Puerto Vallarta, Mexico" width="590"/>
          <div class="caption" style="width:590px;">
           The Inion family, the People's Choice Travelers of the Year for 2014, in Puerto Vallarta, Mexico (Photograph by Jillian Mitchell)
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="main">
          <div class="content entry-content">
           <p>
            Numbers add upjust ask the 
            <strong>
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/travelers-of-the-year-2014/inion-family/" target="_blank" title="Nat Geo Travel site - Travelers of the Year; Inion family">
              Inion Eleven
             </a>
            </strong>
            . Backed by a bounty of votes, this fearless family of global nomads takes the top spot in our Peoples Choice contest.
           </p>
           <p>
            Over weeks of frenzied voting, ballots piled up for each of
            <em>
             Traveler
            </em>
            magazines latest class of
            <strong>
             <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/travelers-of-the-year/">
              Travelers of the Year
             </a>
            </strong>
            . In the end, the Inion Familyparents Stacey-Jean and Brent, along with their nine kids, five of whom have special needsdemonstrated that theres strength in numbers and nobility in humility.
           </p>
           <p>
            We are grateful for the honor your readers have extended to us. Our children are elated, says Stacey-Jean from
            <a href="http://www.visitmexico.com/en/colima" target="_blank" title="Visit Mexico site - Colima">
             Colima, Mexico
            </a>
            . We are standing on some broad, strong shouldersfriends that have stood with our family through the decades and others who have become dear friends through our travels. Whether you prayed for us, cheered us along, repaired our 91 Winnebago, or supported us during our babys heart repair in Mexico, you formed our 2014 Travel Teamand were thankful.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Inion family has been traveling since 2007, overcoming everyday obstacles in Asia and Latin America. Having grown up inclose-knit
            <a href="http://amish.net/amish-lifestyle/" target="_blank" title="Amish site - Amish lifestyle">
             Amish
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Order_Mennonite" target="_blank" title="Wikipedia site - Old Order Mennonite">
             Old Order Mennonite
            </a>
            communities, Brent and Stacey-Jean have long beenfamiliar with family dynamics. But whatever hardship their own growing family has faced while on the road has only increased their desire to engage with the world. Travel is for everyone, says Stacey-Jean. You will find the world to be safer, friendlier, more delicious, and breathtakingly more awe-inspiring than you ever imagined.
           </p>
           <p>
            Itracked down the amazing (and indefatigable) Inions to ask a few more questions.
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_56856" class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_56856" style="width: 236px">
            <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2014/10/inion-family-puerto-vallarta-mitchell-1.jpg">
             <img alt="The Inions set out from their home in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, on a family road trip in their RV in 2007, and just kept going. (Photograph by Jillian Mitchell)" class=" wp-image-56856" height="339" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2014/10/inion-family-puerto-vallarta-mitchell-1.jpg" width="226"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_56856">
             The Inions set out from their home in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, on a family road trip in their RV in 2007, and just kept going. (Photograph by Jillian Mitchell)
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            <b>
             George W. Stone:
            </b>
            <b>
             Whats the most important thing you can tell readers?
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Brent:
            </b>
            In a word,
            <i>
             today
            </i>
            is the day to travel. Sell, store, or give away the stuff that accumulates and robs people of our most precious commoditytime.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             GWS:
            </b>
            <b>
             Why do you say that?
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Brent:
            </b>
            Travel, as all of life, is not all white sand and palm trees. As a family with children, we hear the clock ticking most loudly. For our children both with
            <i>
             and
            </i>
            without special needs,
            <i>
             today
            </i>
            is the day to invest in their lives. We have just a little time to teach them how wide and wild and intricately designed the world is.
            <i>
             Today
            </i>
            is the day to show them the colors, tastes, and textures of different cultures.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             GWS:
            </b>
            <b>
             How do you encourage timid travelers to start exploring?
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Brent:
            </b>
            Start exploring your own backyard. We began traveling by taking staycations. You will be amazed how much there is to discover in your surrounding neighborhoods. Play tourist in your community. From there, it will be a natural leap. Hop aboard a train, buy a bus ticket, pile into an RV, book a flight, or start pedalingget going.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             GWS:Wed love to hear from the kids. How has travel changed their lives?
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Stacey-Jean:
            </b>
            Our energetic four-year-old, Jeremiah, takes cultural change in stride. He states that he enjoys seeing toys, trains, and boats from different countries.
           </p>
           <p>
            Our six-year-old, Hadassah, is enjoying acquiring more Spanish each day. I can talk to more people, making new friends as we go, she says. She has a special fascination with the undersea world and has been enjoying snorkeling.
           </p>
           <p>
            Our 12-year-old, Hannah, has celebrated her birthday in six spots around the globe. In this last year, she has added traditional weaving and native basketry to her life skills. She shared that travel has given her perspective, [allowing her to understand] that my way is not always the right way. When we asked her if if she would like to travel or or take a break she was emphatic: I want to travel. I enjoy seeing things for myself. An author can only give his side of the story. When I visit places for myself, I leave with a greater understanding.
           </p>
           <p>
            Josiah, who has just turned 15, has had the opportunity to learn much in our seven years of travel. This year he learned how to hand-carve a table from a Mexican carpenter. He has a tremendous appreciation of world history. He carries memories from the
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banaue_Rice_Terraces" target="_blank" title="Wikipedia site - Banaue Rice Terraces">
             Banaue Rice Terraces
            </a>
            in the Philippines and Mayan ruins in Mexico and Belize. Over our travels, Josiah has also developed a love of nature: I love watching creatures native to the area, like tropical birds building nests, he says. [This] has given me a great appreciation for the world around me.
           </p>
           <p>
            For our children with special needs (who cannot voice their responses), we watch their eyes beam in new surroundings as they experience love around the globe. They laugh in the salty sea, bask in the sunshine, and challenge their palates with local spices, sauces, and fruits. They teach us more than we teach them. Our children thrive with travel therapy.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             GWS:
             <i>
             </i>
            </b>
            <b>
             As a parent, how do you measure the impact of travel on your children?
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Stacey-Jean:
            </b>
            Growing up cross-culturally has made the children more sensitive to others. Several of them echoed Josiahs words, adding that they have developed a greater cultural understanding. [For instance, one of them told me that] being polite can be different in each culture. Its important not to impose my perspective of politeness on others. Instead, I learn what is appropriate in a persons culture where we visit.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             GWS:
             <i>
             </i>
            </b>
            <b>
             What does the future hold for the Inion Eleven?
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Stacey-Jean and Brent:
            </b>
            Our baby has one last cardiologist visit in December. Then we look forward to moving on to Guatemala. We cant wait to climb to the top of the large temples of
            <a href="http://www.visitguatemala.com/en/destinations/peten-adventure-in-the-mayan-world/tikal-national-park#.VFJxoN7Cnns" target="_blank" title="Visit Guatemala site - Tikal">
             Tikal
            </a>
            , taking in the panoramic view above the trees and spotting black monkeys. We are looking forward to swimming in the waters of
            <a href="http://www.atitlan.com/index.php" target="_blank" title="Atitlan site - Lake Atitlan">
             Lake Atitlan
            </a>
            . There are a few childrens homes we have been invited to visit. We have read that Guatemala has thousands of street children. We look forward to meeting and making a difference with some of them.
           </p>
           <p>
            We plan to get close up with the amazing migration of scarlet macaws and hike
            <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/274" target="_blank" title="UNESCO site - Machu Picchu">
             Machu Picchu
            </a>
            in Peru. We want to learn from locals, feel the soft fur of alpacas, observe traditional natural dyeing and weaving techniques. We want to find the rare blue cotton that grows nowhere else in the world. Were dreaming of playing in Bolivias salt flats and drinking a
            <em>
             mate
            </em>
            in Argentina. The children are most excited about seeing penguins and blue-footed boobies in the Galpagos. We want to see whales in Chile and sloths in Costa Rica.
           </p>
           <p>
            After that, we may ship our RV over for an overland tour of Africa into Asia and then meander into Europe. Visiting Israel and following the footsteps of Jesus is high on our dream list. There is so much to do and see as we keep trekking, from Pennsylvania to Argentina. We hope readers will follow along with us on our blog,
            <a href="http://traveldeepandwide.com/" target="_blank" title="Travel Deep and Wide site">
             Travel Deep and Wide
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
