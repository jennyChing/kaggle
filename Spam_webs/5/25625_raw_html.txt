      <div class="articleHeading">
       Asus Transformer All-in-One: the worlds largest tablet
      </div>
      <div class="articleDetails">
       by Sam Kieldsen | Jun 5, 2012 |
       <a data-disqus-identifier="303616" href="#disqus_thread">
        Comment Now
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="seperator-dotted">
      </div>
      <span class="introText">
       <b>
        New desktop/tablet hybrid features a detachable 18.4-inch screen and dual Android and Windows 8 operating systems.
       </b>
      </span>
      <br/>
      <div align="center" style="padding: 10px 0px 10px 0px;">
       <img alt="Asus Transformer All-in-One: the world&#8217;s largest tablet" class="mainArticleImage" id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolderBody_ArticleImage" src="http://i.nextmedia.com.au/Utils/ImageResizer.ashx?n=http%3a%2f%2fi.nextmedia.com.au%2fNews%2f20120605071426_asus-transformer-aio.jpg&amp;w=600" style="border-width:0px;" title="Asus Transformer All-in-One: the world&#8217;s largest tablet"/>
      </div>
      <span class="articleBodyText">
       <p>
        Asus has gone and done it: its made an 18.4-inch tablet. While wed ordinarily dismiss such a huge device as a pointless look at me exercise, the Asus Transformer All-in-One (or AiO) has an excuse: its really an iMac-style one-piece desktop, first and foremost  the screen can just be removed if desired.
       </p>
       <p>
        Unveiled today at Computex, the AiO also runs two operating systems, booting either to Windows 8 or Android depending on your preference. According to
        <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2012/06/04/asus-transformer-aio/" target="_blank">
         Engadget
        </a>
        , Windows 8 is the default OS, and you switch to Android by pressing a button  presumably when you want to pick up that giant screen and retreat to the sofa.
       </p>
       <p>
        The AiO wasnt the only tablet Asus revealed. There was also the Taichi, a thin-and-light Windows 8 laptop with a sleek exterior which, when the device is closed, becomes a touchscreen. When open, you can also switch this second screen on so that two people can view the same image while sitting on opposite sides of a desk. It will come in 11.6 and 13.3-inch varieties and yes, its a bit bizarre.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div align="center">
        <img alt="asus taichi" height="375" src="http://i.nextmedia.com.au/News/asus-taichi.jpg" width="500"/>
       </div>
       <p>
        Then theres the Asus Tablet 810, an 11.6-inch slate with Windows 8 and a new Clover Trail Intel Atom processor. Itll be compatible with a special dock that adds a QWERTY keyboard and extends battery life.
       </p>
       <p>
        Then theres the Tablet 600, a 10.1-inch mode with an Nvidia Tegra 3 processor. And finally, the Asus Transformer Book, a Windows 8, Ivy Bridge-based notebook with a removeable touchscreen. Itll come in 11.6, 13 and 14-inch sizes, all of which have full 1920 x 1080 HD resolutions.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div align="center">
        <img alt="asus transformer book" height="375" src="http://i.nextmedia.com.au/News/asus-transformer-book.jpg" width="500"/>
       </div>
       <p>
        Theres no word on pricing or availability for any of these devices yet.
       </p>

       </p>
