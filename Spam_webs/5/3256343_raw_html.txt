     <h1 class="nodetitle">
      Falling in love with fictional characters
     </h1>
    </div>
    <form action="/title/Falling+in+love+with+fictional+characters" enctype="multipart/form-data" id="pagebody" method="post" name="pagebody">
     <input name="node_id" type="hidden" value="748040"/>
     <div class=" item">
      <div class="contentinfo contentheader">
       <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">

          <a name="aphexious">

           <a class="author" href="/user/aphexious">
            aphexious

          </strong>
         </td>
         <td class="wu_dtcreate" style="text-align:right">

          </small>
         </td>

      <div class="content">
       <b>
        The character
       </b>
       :
       <a class="populated" href="/title/dark+humor" title="dark humor">
        dark humored
       </a>
       , quick wit, a
       <a class="populated" href="/title/suicidal+writer+type+who+inevitably+turns+out+to+be+gay" title="suicidal writer type who inevitably turns out to be gay">
        suicidal writer type who inevitably turns out to be gay
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="populated" href="/title/nice+smile" title="nice smile">
        nice smile
       </a>
       .
       <p>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Everything+I+should+have+learned+to+avoid+by+now" title="Everything I should have learned to avoid by now">
         Everything I should have learned to avoid by now
        </a>
        , and I fell tonight, hard.  Only a movie... with a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Bob+Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">
         Bob Dylan
        </a>
        soundtrack, though,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/chaining+emotions+from+out+of+bounds" title="chaining emotions from out of bounds">
         chaining emotions from out of bounds
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        Going home from the theater empty-handed, and no relief in the fact that
        <a class="populated" href="/title/it+would+only+be+another+mistake+if+it+were+actually+happening+to+me" title="it would only be another mistake if it were actually happening to me">
         it would only be another mistake if it were actually happening to me
        </a>
        .  Which is why I'm here, now,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/writing+into+an+empty+void" title="writing into an empty void">
         writing into an empty void
        </a>
        about why
        <a class="populated" href="/title/my+pulse+is+a+little+faster" title="my pulse is a little faster">
         my pulse is a little faster
        </a>
        tonight.
       </p>
       <p>
        I fall in love with real people, too, but only after they're gone, it seems.
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Memory+is+the+finest+thread+for+weaving+your+own+perfect+story" title="Memory is the finest thread for weaving your own perfect story">
         Memory is the finest thread for weaving your own perfect story
        </a>
        .  I do halfway try to stay in touch with the writers, at least, they're easier to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/glorify" title="glorify">
         glorify
        </a>
        and make for
        <a class="populated" href="/title/keepsakes+of+letters" title="keepsakes of letters">
         keepsakes of letters
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/I+have+a+cedar+chest+full+of+them" title="I have a cedar chest full of them">
         I have a cedar chest full of them
        </a>
        , pages and pages of beautiful words, typed,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/inked" title="inked">
         inked
        </a>
        , the pencils fading.  Some still
        <a class="populated" href="/title/folded+into+intricate+little+packages" title="folded into intricate little packages">
         folded into intricate little packages
        </a>
        , and many in my own hand.
       </p>
       <p>
        The best have been
        <a class="populated" href="/title/composed+across+great+canyons+of+time+and+space" title="composed across great canyons of time and space">
         composed across great canyons of time and space
        </a>
        , less diluted by the trivialities of real life.  A thoughtful concentrate, wrapped in the lyrics of a bard.  I may find
        <a class="populated" href="/title/myself" title="myself">
         myself
        </a>
        a writer, in
        <a class="populated" href="/title/erratic+spurts+of+inspiration" title="erratic spurts of inspiration">
         erratic spurts of inspiration
        </a>
        that seem to fall right around the time I find myself in love again, with someone to write for, that is.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Life+explodes+in+fantasy+adventure" title="Life explodes in fantasy adventure">
         Life explodes in fantasy adventure
        </a>
        for a few brief moments until I realize what's happening and leave,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/no+forwarding+address" title="no forwarding address">
         no forwarding address
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        I have simply written a scene around me, leaving my favorite character behind, confused, to realize:
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Every+story+comes+to+an+end" title="Every story comes to an end">
         Every story comes to an end
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        But, I have many memories yet to weave,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/many+lives+yet+to+lead" title="many lives yet to lead">
         many lives yet to lead
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        This story's not over yet.
       </p>

      <div class="content">
       <p>
        I fall in love with
        <a class="populated" href="/title/fiction" title="fiction">
         fiction
        </a>
        al
        <a class="populated" href="/title/characters" title="characters">
         characters
        </a>
        too.  I fall in love with
        <a class="populated" href="/title/conform" title="conform">
         people
        </a>
        in
        <a class="populated" href="/title/books" title="books">
         books
        </a>
        .  Can I love in real life as much as I do within a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/text" title="text">
         text
        </a>
        ?
        <br/>
        "Real life" doesn't happen in the same place as the written
        <a class="populated" href="/title/word" title="word">
         word
        </a>
        does. Book time is distilled time, heavy time.  Everything is significant, everything is
        <a class="populated" href="/title/symbol" title="symbol">
         symbol
        </a>
        , every word is a prayer in this religion of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/language" title="language">
         language
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        Written love encompasses eternity because it is frozen in time.
A moment's thought becomes a
longstanding condition, an unalterable state-of-being.
       </p>
       <p>
        I am more dramatic in writing than I am in
life.
        <br/>
        My emotions can never hope to live up to the
way I write them. (
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Is+this+true%253F+Maybe.+But+it+might+be+the+opposite" title="Is this true? Maybe. But it might be the opposite">
         Is this true? Maybe. But it might be the opposite
        </a>
        .) When you grow up with
        <a class="populated" href="/title/books" title="books">
         books
        </a>
        equally as
important as the real-time world, who can blame you
for craving intensity?  Or for being confused at the
impossibility of flitting between one person's
thoughts and another's, panicking at being constrained
to one's own mind and experiences exclusively.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Letters" title="Letters">
         Letters
        </a>
        . If I write enough I can make myself into a fictional character, I can write the book of me, I can live within the book and make it into an
        <a class="populated" href="/title/architectural+space" title="architectural space">
         architectural space
        </a>
        .  Letters between two people build a space between them and the space is stretched taut between two points.  Can you live in the space if you are, by
        <a class="populated" href="/title/definition" title="definition">
         definition
        </a>
        , one of it's
        <a class="populated" href="/title/pole" title="pole">
         pole
        </a>
        s?  Or is it just spun out of you, like silk from a worm, coalescing into some ephemeral
        <a class="populated" href="/title/cocoon" title="cocoon">
         cocoon
        </a>
        ?
       </p>
       <p>
        I fall in love with love letters.  I will become a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/nun" title="nun">
         nun
        </a>
        , and devote myself to the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/church" title="church">
         church
        </a>
        of love letters, I will marry (
        <a class="populated" href="/title/symbol" title="symbol">
         symbol
        </a>
        ically) the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/God" title="God">
         God
        </a>
        of text, the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/deity" title="deity">
         deity
        </a>
        of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/language" title="language">
         language
        </a>
        , the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/empty+set" title="empty set">
         empty set
        </a>
        that can never be defined, the super-word, the meta-
        <a class="populated" href="/title/cipher" title="cipher">
         cipher
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        I will lose myself in the text and allow myself to be
        <a class="populated" href="/title/decipher" title="decipher">
         decipher
        </a>
        ed, for the text and I are fully devoted to each other, insofar as we depend on each other to read and to be read.  Nothing can be held back because the text displays itself to me in all of its nakedness, everything that it is is there for me to see, and it enters into me to remain forever engraved upon my memory.
       </p>
