        <p>
         These easy homemade Lemon Coconut Granola Bars are a fun snack that everyone will love! They are chewy with a bit of crunch, and the flavors are so bright!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://backforseconds.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Easy-Homemade-Lemon-Coconut-Granola-Bars.jpg">
          <img alt="Easy Homemade Lemon Coconut Granola Bars" class="alignnone wp-image-7561 size-full" height="790" src="http://backforseconds.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Easy-Homemade-Lemon-Coconut-Granola-Bars.jpg" width="525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont know what it is, but I just cant get enough lemon lately! I want to lemon all the things!! So when I was trying to come up with a new snack to make for my kids, lemon granola bars sounded awesome! My kids love lemon as much as I do and we all love homemade granola bars!
         <span id="more-7563">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://backforseconds.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Easy-Lemon-Granola-Bars.jpg">
          <img alt="Easy Lemon Granola Bars" class="alignnone wp-image-7564 size-full" height="790" src="http://backforseconds.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Easy-Lemon-Granola-Bars.jpg" width="525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Usually I make no bake granola bars, but this time I was more in the mood for a crunchy/chewy bar, so I decided to bake these instead. Now, because of the honey and brown sugar in the recipe you are going to want to line your pan with foil and spray it with cooking spray before baking. I hear they can be a little tricky to get out of the pan otherwisenot that I would know from personal experience
        </p>
        <p>
         I like to add lemon zest anytime I use lemon in a recipe. It really adds a lot of brightness and punches up the lemon flavor.
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope you enjoy these bars as much as we did!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

         <div class="gmc-recipe-description" itemprop="description">
          These easy homemade Lemon Coconut Granola Bars are a fun snack that everyone will love! They are chewy with a bit of crunch, and the flavors are so bright!
         </div>
         <div class="gmc-recipe-ingredients">
          <h2 class="gmc-recipe-subtitle">
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul class="gmc-ingredient-list">
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 tablespoons unsalted butter
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup honey
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/3 cup brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/3 cup lemon Juice
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup coconut
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 1/2 cup old fashioned oats
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 1/2 cup crisp rice cereal
           </li>
           <li class="gmc-ingredient-list-item" itemprop="ingredients">
            zest of one lemon
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="gmc-recipe-steps">
          <h2 class="gmc-recipe-subtitle">
           Directions
          </h2>
          <table class="gmc-step-list">
           <tr>
            <td class="gmc-step-list-title" colspan="2">
             Step 1
            </td>
           </tr>
           <tr class="gmc-step-list-item">
            <td class="gmc-step-desc" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Preheat oven to 350
             <br/>
             In a saucepan over medium low heat, melt the butter with the honey and sugar, and continue stirring until all sugar is dissolved. Remove from heat and stir in lemon juice, coconut, oats, and cereal until evenly coated. Press firmly into a 9x13" pan, lined with foil and greased. Bake 18-20 minutes.  Sprinkle with the zest of a lemon. Allow to cool before lifting bars from pan and cutting into 24 pieces. Store in a sealed container.
            </td>
           </tr>
          </table>
