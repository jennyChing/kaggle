          The essential guide to choosing and buying your wedding rings
         </h1>
        </header>

        <section class="entry">
         <p class="intro">
          Your wedding ring is something that youll want to wear for the rest of your life, so its got to be perfect! Whether you want a traditional band or something more unique, follow our top tips and learn how to choose the perfect style for you.
         </p>
         <p class="intro">
          <img alt="wedding-ring-buying-guide-octoberward" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-full wp-image-22490" data-lazy-src="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/wedding-ring-buying-guide-octoberward.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="350" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" title="The essential guide to buying your wedding ring  October Ward" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="wedding-ring-buying-guide-octoberward" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22490" height="350" src="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/wedding-ring-buying-guide-octoberward.jpg" title="The essential guide to buying your wedding ring  October Ward" width="650"/>
         </noscript>
         <h2>
          Precious metals
         </h2>
         <p>
          Choosing the metal for your
          <a href="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/affordable-wedding-rings/" title="Diamond Manufacturers launch a new range of affordable wedding rings">
           wedding ring
          </a>
          is a good place to start. There are numerous metals available, all with different properties and styles that make some more suitable than others.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <a href="http://www.weddingrings-direct.com/" rel="nofollow">
           <img alt="" class="lazy lazy-hidden alignright wp-image-7536" data-lazy-src="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/wedding-ring-buyer-guide-materials.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="357" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" style="border: 0px none" width="350"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-7536" height="357" src="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/wedding-ring-buyer-guide-materials.jpg" style="border: 0px none" width="350"/>
           </noscript>
          </a>
          Yellow gold
          <strong>
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Yellow gold is a good choice for traditional brides and grooms-to-be.
         </p>
         <p>
          Generally, 22ct gold is too soft to wear every day, so consider buying a 18ct, 14ct or 9ct gold wedding ring. These rings are combined with stronger metals and are better suited to the demands of day-to-day life.
         </p>
         <h3>
          White gold
         </h3>
         <p>
          This wedding ring is the perfect compliment to a platinum engagement ring.
         </p>
         <p>
          White gold wedding rings are often plated with rhodium to enhance their appearance. While this plating should be expected to wear off over time, it can be easily re-applied and make your wedding ring look as good as new.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Platinum
         </h3>
         <p>
          These wedding rings are becoming increasingly popular with modern couples.
         </p>
         <p>
          This white-coloured metal is extremely tough and stands up well to everyday wear and tear. Its rarer than gold and much more expensive but a platinum wedding ring will last, unmarked, forever.
         </p>
         <p>
          If a platinum wedding ring blows your budget, consider buying a palladium wedding ring instead. Palladium has become a popular alternative to platinum because of its similar colour yet more budget-friendly price tag.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <div class="sidebox" id="related-articles">
          <h3>
           Like this? You'll love

         <h3>
          Titanium
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
          </strong>
          Titanium is one of the more modern jewellery metals. Its strikingly different in colour to platinum and palladium, scratch-resistant and feels light on the finger.
         </p>
         <p>
          Zirconium, black zirconium and tungsten carbide are other contemporary metals with similar properties and appearance.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="366" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/azlPkkVwYmc?feature=oembed" width="650">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Shaped wedding rings
         </h2>
         <p>
          After youve decided on the metal, you need to consider if this new wedding ring will complement your engagement ring.
         </p>
         <p>
          Both rings will be worn side by side for the rest of your life, so its important that they sit well together. You may have never considered the profile of the ring before, but youll begin to see subtle differences once you compare the styles side by side.
         </p>
         <p>
          If this is case you may decide to choose a shaped wedding ring, often referred to as a curved or fitted wedding ring. These come in a range of designs including a delicate curve, a pronounced Z-shape or a cut-out in the ring.
         </p>
         <p>
          Even if you dont need it to be shaped, consider the ring profile  youll be able to choose from court shape (rounded on the inside and out), flat profiles (completely rectangular), D-shape (flat on the inside, round on the outside) and more.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="wedding-rings-guide-kerriemitchell" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-full wp-image-22486" data-lazy-src="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/wedding-rings-guide-kerriemitchell.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="350" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" title="The essential guide to buying a wedding ring kerriemitchell" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="wedding-rings-guide-kerriemitchell" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22486" height="350" src="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/wedding-rings-guide-kerriemitchell.jpg" title="The essential guide to buying a wedding ring kerriemitchell" width="650"/>
         </noscript>
         <h2>
          Diamond set wedding rings
         </h2>
         <p>
          Once youve chosen the wedding ring, now is the time to consider including a diamond or other precious gemstone. Diamond-set wedding rings
          <em>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </em>
          are becoming more fashionable and are more commonly found in womens wedding rings to provide a sparkly backdrop for the engagement ring.
         </p>
         <p>
          But grooms shouldnt discount diamonds. Diamond wedding rings are finding favour with men, too, as a way of remembering someone close to their heart.
          <a href="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/imprint-ring/" title="The imprint ring  An encouraging reminder or just missing the point?">
           Another fun way to do this is with an imprint of your fingerprint.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Diamonds in wedding rings are usually
          <a href="http://www.weddingideasmag.com/celebrity-engagement-rings/" title="Celebrity Engagement Rings">
           brilliant-cut (round) or princess-cut (square)
          </a>
          , but you can also find designs with pear-shaped, emerald and marquise-cut stones. The more unusual the diamond shape, the more glamorous your wedding ring  and probably, the more expensive!
         </p>
         <p>
          Words 
          <a href="http://www.weddingrings-direct.com/" title="Wedding Rings Direct">
           Wedding Rings Direct
          </a>
         </p>

          Ryan Reynolds spent $2 million on Blake Livelys ring. How much is too much to spend on a wedding ring?
         </h3>

Metal preferences seem to be regional  East and West coast couples seem more willing to experiment with platinum and titanium, while Southern and Midwest folks are pretty much sticking with traditional yellow gold. It would be great to see some more creativity everywhere.
               </p>
