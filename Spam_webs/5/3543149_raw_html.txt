               Prototype
              </td>
             </tr>
            </table>
            <table class="contentpaneopen">
             <tr>
              <td valign="top">
               <div>
                <div style="text-align: center">
                 <a class="fancybox" href="/media/prototypeLarge.jpg">
                  <img alt="Prototype" border="0" src="/media/Side.jpg" title="Prototype"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
               </div>
               <div>
                <strong>
                 Prototypes purpose
                </strong>
               </div>
               <div>
                The sole purpose of the prototype is to demonstrate the 4MC. To achieve this it was not necessary to equip the prototype with mudguards, lights, or bodywork.
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                <strong>
                 Prototypes adjustable features
                </strong>
               </div>
               <div>
                The prototype includes many adjustable features that a production model would not need. For example, rake/trail, steering offset, Ackermann steering differential, toe in/out, front/rear brake force differential, remote suspension damper housings to aid damper adjustment.
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                <strong>
                 Other features on the prototype
                </strong>
               </div>
               <div>
                To be able to see the front suspension working, the fuel tank was positioned between the back wheels. On a production 4MC the exhaust could be positioned between the back wheels with the fuel tank conventionally placed in front of the rider.
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                <strong>
                 Prototypes limitations
                </strong>
               </div>
               <div>
                (i) The 4MCs leading and trailing suspension arms are ideally suited to being bolted directly to the engine/transmission unit. However, the prototypes excellent Yamaha YP400cc engine was not designed to have suspension arms bolted to it. Therefore, the prototype had to have a separate frame which weighs 67kg and raises the prototypes centre of gravity.
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                (ii) The prototypes separate frame required the front suspension to be mounted a lot higher than necessary. This also raises the prototypes centre of gravity.
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                (iii) The space needed for the prototypes adjustable rake/trail has made the prototypes wheelbase a little longer than required by a production 4MC.
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                (iv) The prototypes YP400cc engine/transmission includes a reduction gearbox which would not be needed on a production 4MC. The reduction gearbox has made the prototypes wheelbase a lot longer than a production 4MC would require.
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                <strong>
                 Final remarks about the prototype
                </strong>
               </div>
               <div>
                The prototype works extremely well despite its above limitations and the very small budget from which it was created. It is exciting to imagine just how good the 4MC will be once a major manufacturer has developed it properly.
               </div>
