                <div class="mailmunch-forms-before-post" style="display: none !important;">
                </div>
                <p>
                 A woman from Washougal is in some serious trouble after striking a 5-year-old boy with her pickup truck Friday afternoon.
                </p>
                <p>
                 36-year-old Marsha Yumi Perry was driving with a suspended license when she hit the boy, who was riding his Big Wheel out of his driveway. Instead of stopping to see if the child was okay, Perry tried to drive off.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Unfortunately for her, she was unable to move her truck, so she jumped out and started walking north toward Washougal River Road.
                </p>
                <p>
                 When police arrived a short time later, the woman had successfully escaped the crime scene. But the moment of freedom didnt last long for Perry. Officer Kyle Day and his police dog, Ranger, were able to track her scent through a field.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Ranger stopped within five feet of the hole Perry was hiding in. The officer was still not able to see Perry, but he knew she was close because of Rangers signal.
                </p>
                <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
                </div>
                <p>
                 According to the report, Day yelled out a warning to the woman. He told her that he would unleash the dog if she didnt come out. Thats when Day noticed the ground moving. It was Perry, climbing out of the hole with dirt covering her body.
                </p>
                <p>
                 She was arrested on charges of driving with a suspended license and felony hit-and-run. Police are still investigating the incident, but we do know the little boy is expected to be okay. He was treated at a local hospital for cuts to his face, elbows and knees.
                </p>
