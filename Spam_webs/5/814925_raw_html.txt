           <h4 style="text-align: justify;">

           <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
            According to trend reports a shirt-dress is the key piece for this spring-summer season. At least, as seen at Dior, Balenciaga, Wang and many others fashion shows. The new perfect shirt-dress is notably menswear inspired and looks like a typical shirt for the office, but much longer. Most likely it is white, grey or blue, tiny striped are allowed and it's surely made of cotton.
            <br/>
           </h4>
           <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
            I guess the idea is to create an image of a girl who runs out in a hurry dressed in her boyfriend's shirt (no matter whether it was her conscious forward-thinking choiceorwas the only thing left to put on). Do you remember this scene in "Sex in the city" when Carry came back home dressed in a Mr.Big's white shirt accentuated with a dark belt (something very similar was seen at Donna Karan's SS2014 show)? Well, this what I am driving at. Undoubtedly, a shirt-dress is a very romantic item, in the truest sense of this world.
            <br/>
           </h4>
           <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
            Besides, we have a unique opportunity to leave home in one "shirt" only and it's a crime not to use it. Our "absence of mind" will be pretty excusable -in case of any accusations of breach of peace, we may always refer to Dior, Kors, Wang and Co.
            <br/>
           </h4>
           <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
            My own khaki shirt-dress by

            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">

               Outnet
              </a>

            a few months ago and at that time. This item is a comfortable-yet-cool classic and at the same timeit's easy to wear. Sounds like a dream, is not it?
