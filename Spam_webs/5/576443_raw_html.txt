        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Warm + Roasted Winter Salad Bowl
        </h1>
        <span class="date updated" style="display:none;">
         January 21, 2015
        </span>
        <span class="vcard author" style="display:none;">
         <span class="fn">
          Angela (Oh She Glows)
         </span>
        </span>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         <span>
          <a href="http://ohsheglows.com/2015/01/21/warm-roasted-winter-salad-bowl/#comments" rel="nofollow">

       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <img alt="warmroastedgreenbeanpotatoquinoasalad-4805" border="0" height="891" src="http://cdn.ohsheglows.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/warmroastedgreenbeanpotatoquinoasalad-4805.jpg" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px" title="warmroastedgreenbeanpotatoquinoasalad-4805" width="595"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This salad is a good example of how I love my salads in the winter  warm, hearty, and bursting with nutrition. Like a big hug. I have no desire to eat a cold salad right now (unless its
         <a href="http://ohsheglows.com/2014/11/24/crowd-pleasing-vegan-caesar-salad/" target="_blank">
          this one
         </a>
         , I suppose). Its light, but still has that stick to your ribs quality I crave soooo much right now. Give me all the healthy comfort food!
        </p>
        <p>
         I started by roasting Yukon Gold potatoes (one of my all-time favourite winter comfort foods!) and crunchy green beans with a bit of olive oil and a generous seasoning of sea salt and pepper. While its roasting, whip up a batch of quinoa (speltberries or millet might be nice too) and chop some green onion and kale. Whisk together a simple red wine vinaigrette and well add some freshly roasted garlic (if you are already roasting veggies, you might as well roast a few cloves of garlic too!). When the potatoes and green beans are golden and lightly charred on the bottoms, throw it into a big bowl and fold in the quinoa, dressing, green onions, and kale. Toss on a big handful of pepita seeds for crunch and my favourite salad topper  sliced avocado. Its the perfect soul-soothing, warm bowl of comfort food in the middle of January.
        </p>
        <p>

        </p>

            <div class="summary" itemprop="description">
             <p>
              The ultimate bowl of winter comfort food! Yukon Gold potatoes and green beans are roasted until golden and then tossed with warm rainbow quinoa, shredded kale, green onion, and a quick red wine vinaigrette. I topped it with crunchy pepita seeds and sliced avocado. Feel free to change up the potatoes with what you have on hand. Instead of quinoa, speltberries or millet might work nicely too.
             </p>

          <div class="ingredients clear" style="margin-top: 10px; font-size: 13px; font-size: 1.3rem; line-height: 19px; line-height: 1.9rem;">
           <h4 style="margin: 0 0 10px 0; margin: 0 0 1rem 0;">
            Ingredients:
           </h4>
           <h5>
            For the salad:
           </h5>
           <ul class="ingredients">
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 cup uncooked rainbow or regular quinoa + 1.5 cups water
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              3/4 pound Yukon Gold potatoes, chopped into 1/2-inch chunks (about 2 1/2 cups)
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed and chopped into 1-inch pieces (about 3 cups)
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 extra-large or 3 large garlic cloves (peel left on)
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 cup stemmed and finely chopped kale (I used lacinato, but curly works fine too)
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              3 green onions, thinly sliced
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              3 tablespoons pepita seeds (I added a handful of sesame seeds at the last minute too)
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              Sliced avocado
             </span>
            </li>
           </ul>
           <h5>
            For the dressing:
           </h5>
           <ul class="ingredients">
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 cup red wine vinegar
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 teaspoon pink Himalayan salt or fine grain sea salt
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              Freshly ground pepper, to taste
             </span>
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
          <div class="clear" id="instructions" style="font-size: 13px; margin: 10px 0 10px 10px; margin: 1rem 0 1rem 1rem;">
           <h4>
            Directions:
           </h4>
           <div class="instructions">
            <ol>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Preheat oven to 400F and line one extra-large (or 2 large) baking sheets with parchment paper.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Place the chopped potatoes, green beans, and garlic cloves (leave the peel on) onto the baking sheet. Toss with the oil and season with a generous amount of salt and pepper. Spread into an even layer.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Roast for 15 minutes, remove from the oven and flip, and continue roasting about 10-20 more minutes until the potatoes and beans are tender and golden. Watch closely during the last 10 minutes to avoid burning.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Meanwhile, cook the quinoa by adding the quinoa into a medium pot along with 1.5 cups water. Bring to a low boil, reduce heat to medium-low, cover with lid, and cook for 13-17 minutes until the water is absorbed and the quinoa is fluffy. Remove from heat, fluff with a fork, and leave the lid on to keep warm.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Prepare the dressing by whisking the dressing ingredients together in a small bowl. Set aside.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Chop the kale and green onions.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              When the vegetables are finished roasting, remove garlic cloves and set aside. Spoon the potatoes and beans into a large serving bowl. Stir in the quinoa and the other chopped vegetables.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Trim the end off each garlic clove and push the roasted garlic out. Finely chop or mash the garlic. Whisk the garlic into the dressing until combined. Pour all of the dressing onto the vegetables and toss to coat.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve immediately. Leftovers can be reheated in a saucepan with some oil and the salad will keep in the fridge for a few days.
             </li>
            </ol>
           </div>
          </div>
