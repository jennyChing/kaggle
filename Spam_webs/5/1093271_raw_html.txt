       <p>
        Here you go heres another one!- a
        <strong>
         CHOCOLATE- PEANUT BUTTER CUP CHEESECAKE CAKE
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29273" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        What can I say? Im just totally crazy about cheesecake cakes. Maybe one of these days Ill write a cookbook totally devoted to this type of cake. Wouldnt that be a fun book to have? Goodness, I think Id gain about 40 pounds writing a book like that! Anyway, the combination of chocolate and peanut butter is appealing to so many people these days, so I thought this would be a good combo for my latest cheesecake cake. There are two layers of chocolate cake, and sandwiched in between the cake layers is a layer of peanut butter cheesecake. The whole cake is covered with peanut butter frosting, topped with chocolate ganache, and then sprinkled with peanut butter cups. ITS VERY RICH so a small slicell do ya just fine!
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake Prep 1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29266" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-Prep-1.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Ive told you about my secret before, right? &gt;&gt;
        <span style="color: #800000;">
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000I1UXUI?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B000I1UXUI&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=reciandente-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           <span style="color: #800000;">
            these cake strips
           </span>
          </a>
         </strong>
        </span>
        ?? They are the best baking invention known to mankind. Soak them in water, wrap them around each of your cake layers, and those cake layers will stay nice and flat for your cake. You dont want to have to deal with rounded mounds of cake! I use them for every cake I bake. Theyre cheap ($8.10 for two), and they last forever.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake Prep 2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29267" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-Prep-2.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres how to assemble this cheesecake cake: one cake layer goes on your platter. I like to slip little pieces of wax paper under the edges so the frosting doesnt mess up my platter.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake Prep 3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29268" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-Prep-3.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        A small amount of peanut butter frosting goes on top of the first cake layer.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake Prep 4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29269" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-Prep-4.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Then the cheesecake layer goes on next!
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake Prep 5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29270" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-Prep-5.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        More frosting is spread onto the cheesecake layer (the frosting just kind of helps the layers stick together a little better).
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake Prep 6" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29271" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-Prep-6.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        The 2nd layer of cake tops the cheesecake.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake Prep 7" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29272" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-Prep-7.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Then the whole thing is frosted. Once you have everything covered, you can then slip out the wax paper and throw it away.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake #recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29265" height="900" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-recipe.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        I added a chocolate ganache to the top. Im not sure you really need it since the cake is completely and totally decadent and perfect all on its own. Its totally your call. My chocolate seized up on me a little bit, so its not super shiny and beautiful (but it can be!) Otherwise, the peanut butter cups are sprinkled over the top. Dont skip out on those! The Reeses mini cups can be found in your markets candy aisle.
        <span id="more-29263">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake #recipe - RecipeGirl.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29264" height="900" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-recipe-RecipeGirl.com_.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Ahhhh, the unveiling of the inside of the cake! You know why I love this cake so much? Because there are three kinds of desserts going on here &gt; cake, cheesecake and candy! You wont need dessert for a week after eating a slice of this! I served this at a little dinner party I had at my house recently. A friend of mine that I hadnt seen since high school was in town and we had him and his partner over for dinner. They raved about this cake, which was really, really nice since my friend is a
        <span style="color: #800000;">
         <strong>
          <a href="http://butlerforhire.com/users/awp.php?ln=718368" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

            caterer in San Francisco

        </span>
        !!  Im so glad it was a big hit. Im thinking it would be cool for Fathers Day too if your man or your Dad or your dude is into the chocolate-peanut butter combo. Its a special treat indeed!
       </p>

        <div class="recipemain">
         <span itemprop="name">
          <h2 class="fn">
           Chocolate- Peanut Butter Cup Cheesecake Cake
          </h2>
         </span>
         <img align="right" class="photo nopin" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Chocolate-Peanut-Butter-Cup-Cheesecake-Cake-200x150.jpg"/>
         <h3 id="ingredients">
          Ingredients:
         </h3>
         <div class="ingredients">
          <p>
           <strong>
            PEANUT BUTTER CHEESECAKE LAYER:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           Two 8-ounce packages cream cheese, at room temperature
           <br/>
           1 1/4 cups creamy peanut butter (do not use natural)
           <br/>
           1 cup granulated white sugar
           <br/>
           3/4 cup sour cream
           <br/>
           3 large eggs
           <br/>
           2 teaspoons vanilla extract
           <br/>
           One 8-ounce bag Reeses minis (or chopped Reeses Peanut Butter Cups (optional)
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            CHOCOLATE CAKE:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           2 cups granulated white sugar
           <br/>
           1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
           <br/>
           3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
           <br/>
           1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
           <br/>
           1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
           <br/>
           1 1/2 teaspoons salt
           <br/>
           2 large eggs
           <br/>
           1 cup milk
           <br/>
           1/2 cup vegetable or canola oil
           <br/>
           2 teaspoons vanilla extract
           <br/>
           1 cup boiling water
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            PEANUT BUTTER FROSTING:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           3/4 cup butter, at room temperature
           <br/>
           1 1/2 cups creamy peanut butter (do not use natural)
           <br/>
           1/4 cup milk (or a little more, as needed)
           <br/>
           3 cups powdered sugar
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            ON TOP:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           Use
           <span style="color: #800000;">
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/ina-garten/chocolate-ganache-recipe.html" target="_blank">
              <span style="color: #800000;">
               this recipe
              </span>
             </a>
            </strong>
           </span>
           for chocolate ganache- totally optional (because it's so decadent!
           <br/>
           One 8-ounce bag Reeses minis (or chopped Reeses Peanut Butter Cups)
          </p>
         </div>
         <h3 id="directions">
          Directions:
         </h3>
         <div class="instructions">
          <span itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           <p>
            1.
            <strong>
             Prepare the cheesecake layer:
            </strong>
            Preheat the oven to 325 degrees F.  Spray a 9-inch springform pan with nonstick spray.  It's also nice if you can line it with a round of parchment paper, if you have it.  In a large bowl, use an electric mixer to combine the cream cheese and peanut butter until smooth.  Mix in the sugar, then beat in the sour cream, eggs and vanilla.  Keep mixing until everything is nice and smooth.  If you'd like to have Reeses in the cheesecake, stir them in now.  Scrape the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top.  Bake until it is no longer super wiggly in the middle when you (gently) shake the pan.  A tiny bit of wiggle is fine, but if there's a lot then it's not quite done yet.  It should take between 1 hour and 1 hour and 15 minutes to bake the cheesecake until done (it's completely okay if the cheesecake happens to crack- it will not be noticeable in the cake).  Remove from the oven and let cool on the counter.  Transfer to the freezer and keep frozen until ready to assemble the cake.
           </p>
           <p>
            2.
            <strong>
             Prepare the cake layers:
            </strong>
            Preheat oven to 350F. Grease and flour two 9-inch round baking pans.  Whisk together sugar, flour, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda and salt in large mixer bowl. Add eggs, milk, oil and vanilla; beat on medium speed of electric mixer for 2 minutes. Stir in water (batter will be thin). Pour batter evenly into prepared pans.  Bake 30-35 minutes or until wooden pick inserted in center comes out clean. Cool 10 minutes; remove from pans to wire racks. Cool completely.
           </p>
           <p>
            3.
            <strong>
             Prepare the frosting:
            </strong>
            In a large bowl, use an electric mixer to combine the butter and peanut butter. Beat until smooth and well-combined. Mix in 1/4 cup milk, then add in the sugar a little at a time until it is all mixed in. Sprinkle in a little more milk, if needed, and continue to beat until the frosting is a good spreading consistency. It should be creamy and delicious!
           </p>
           <p>
            4.
            <strong>
             Assemble the cheesecake cake:
            </strong>
            Place one cake layer into the center of a cake plate or platter. Lightly frost the first layer of cake.  Remove the cheesecake from the freezer, take off the sides of the pan, and slide a knife under the the cheesecake to release it from the pan and peel off the parchment paper. Use a sharp knife to trim the cheesecake around the edges, as needed to match the size of your cake layers (see *Tips). Place the cheesecake layer on top of the first cake layer. Lightly frost the cheesecake layer.  Place the second cake layer on top of the cheesecake.
           </p>
           <p>
            5.
            <strong>
             Frost the cake:
            </strong>
            Scoop a good amount of frosting onto the top of the cake.  Use a flat spatula to spread the frosting smoothly across the top and down the sides.  If you're using the chocolate ganache, follow the instructions for that and spread it over the top.  Sprinkle Reeses on top.
           </p>
           <p>
            6.  Refrigerate this cake until ready to serve (see tips below for serving and storage).
           </p>
          </span>
         </div>
         <h3 id="extras">
          Tips:
         </h3>
         <div class="extras">
          <p>
           *Measure your cheesecake layer against the cake layers. If the cheesecake layer turns out to be slightly larger size round than your cake layers, move it to a cutting board and use a knife to gently shave off some of the exterior of the cheesecake.
           <br/>
           *If you are serving this cake at a party, its perfectly okay to leave the cake at room temperature for a couple of hours (the cheesecake thaws very quickly). Otherwise, keep it stored in the refrigerator. It also freezes perfectly. Freeze it as is, then wrap it in plastic wrap and store it in the freezer until it is needed. Leftover slices may also be wrapped and frozen.
          </p>
         </div>
