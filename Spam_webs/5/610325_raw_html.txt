        <div class="entry-title">
         Vegetarian Carrot Top Pesto Pizza
        </div>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vegetarian Carrot Top Pesto Pizza | healthynibblesandbits.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4344" height="1083" src="http://healthynibblesandbits.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carrot-Top-Pesto-Pizza-3.jpg" width="720"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         For no goodreason at all, I declared that this is the year whenI start growing my own herbs. I dont have a clue about what Im doing. I just want my balcony to look less barren, like someone actually lives here. It took me a good19 months to realize this. Raising herbs is a huge responsibility, after all. Meet the latest addition to the House of Lin:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Herbs" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4347" height="479" src="http://healthynibblesandbits.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Herbs.jpg" width="720"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Okay, so I
         <del>
          cheated a little
         </del>
         got a huge helping hand by buying seedlings from the farmers market. But Im proud that I moved those seedlings into big boy pots with my bare hands! Baby steps, right?My ultimate goal is to keep these alive for 2 months. Ill go ahead and keep my fingers crossed.
        </p>
        <p>
         I guess this wasnt the first time Ive gotten my green thumb on. I remember one of my projects in elementary school was to create seedlings. We eachgot a ziploc bag, inserted a wet sheet of paper towel, and added a row of staples on the bag. Then, we placedseeds on top the staples, taped the bag to the window, and watched it grow. Voilaseedlings! Somehow, I my gardening skills got worseover time.
        </p>
        <p>
         Maybe one day, my herb garden will be so big that I can actually make this carrot top pesto without buying the ingredients. But for now, go get yourself a bunch of basil and carrots, and get ready for some fun.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vegetarian Carrot Top Pesto Pizza | healthynibblesandbits.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4342" height="1083" src="http://healthynibblesandbits.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carrot-Top-Pesto-Pizza-2.jpg" width="720"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You know when you buy a bunch of carrots sometimes, they come with the tops still in tact? Dont throw them away! Theyre perfect for pesto making adventures. The carrot tops dont add much flavor to the pesto, but they definitely help mellow out the basil flavor AND helps the pesto stay a vibrant green for longer. Just rip off the leafs, store it in a plastic bag, and it will keep in the refrigerator for up to 3 weeks before they start to turn yellow. Make sure to rinse them thoroughly before using. A lot of dirt gets stuck in there!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Carrot Top" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4346" height="479" src="http://healthynibblesandbits.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carrot-Top.jpg" width="720"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been using this carrot top and basil pesto to make thin crust pizzas, slathered it on avocado pesto toast, and mixed it pasta Its one of the best pesto sauces Ive made so far. For serious. Want to give it a try? Ashley of
         <a href="http://www.spoonfulofflavor.com/2015/04/29/vegetarian-carrot-top-pesto-pizza/" target="_blank">
          Spoonful of Flavor
         </a>
         has been kind enough to let me share this recipe on
         <a href="http://www.spoonfulofflavor.com/2015/04/29/vegetarian-carrot-top-pesto-pizza/" target="_blank">
          her blog
         </a>
         , so be sure to hop on over. Pizza in 30 minutes!You wont be disappointed, I promise.
        </p>
       </div>
