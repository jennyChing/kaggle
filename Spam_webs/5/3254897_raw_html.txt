     <h1>
      <em>
       The Chicago Manual of Style Favorite Things 2013
      </em>
     </h1>
     <div class="text-meta" role="contentinfo">
      <ul class="text-data">
       <li class="date">
        Dec 15, 2013
       </li>
      </ul>
     </div>
     <!-- end text-meta -->
     <p class="intro">
      Join us for two weeks of all the people, places, and things that made our lives a little better in 2013.
     </p>
     <div class="text-section">
      <p>
       <font size="5">
        Day 11
       </font>
       <em>
        <font size="5">
         <i>
          Chicago Manual of
         </i>
        </font>
       </em>
       <em>
        <font size="5">
         <i>
          Style
         </i>
        </font>
       </em>
       <font size="5">
        Favorite
       </font>
       <font size="5">
        The Serial Comma
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img align="left" alt="" src="http://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/dms/cmos/images/FavoriteThingsDay11/Comma.jpg"/>
       Rules may come and go but we stand firm on our commitment to the serial comma. It brings clarity to even the most complex
       <a href="http://www.slate.com/blogs/lexicon_valley/2013/12/10/oxford_comma_sky_news_tweet_suggests_that_obama_and_castro_have_set_a_date.html" target="_blank">
        world events
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Wishing you holidays that are warm, merry, and full of style!
      </p>
      <p style="clear:left;">
      </p>
      <p>
       <font size="5">
        Day 10
       </font>
       <em>
        <font size="5">
         <i>
          Chicago Manual of
         </i>
        </font>
       </em>
       <em>
        <font size="5">
         <i>
          Style
         </i>
        </font>
       </em>
       <font size="5">
        Favorite
       </font>
       <font size="5">
        Language Apps
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img align="left" alt="" height="160" src="http://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/dms/cmos/images/FavoriteThingsDay10/GrammarPop.png" width="160"/>
       When planes are delayed or family gatherings get awkward, a good mobile game can make the time fly. Even better is when we can improve our word skills at the same time. Mignon Fogartys Grammar Girl blog posts and podcasts bring clarity to grammar, and so we love her new
       <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/grammar-pop-hd/id666851934?mt=8&amp;ign-mpt=uo%3D4" target="_blank">
        Grammar Pop
       </a>
       app. And one of our go-to dictionaries is boosting our vocabulary on the fly with their
       <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/quizzitive-merriam-webster/id645814720?ls=1&amp;mt=8" target="_blank">
        Quizzitive
       </a>
       app. Have another favorite? Share it on our Facebook
       <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/The-Chicago-Manual-of-Style/139396659150?ref=hl" target="_blank">
        page
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p style="clear:left;">
      </p>
      <p>
       <font size="5">
        Day 9
       </font>
       <em>
        <font size="5">
         <i>
          Chicago Manual of
         </i>
        </font>
       </em>
       <em>
        <font size="5">
         <i>
          Style
         </i>
        </font>
       </em>
       <font size="5">
        Favorite
       </font>
       <font size="5">
        Movie Versions of Books
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://robintatlowlord.com/2011/07/13/book-hermione-movie-hermione">
        <img align="left" alt="" src="http://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/dms/cmos/images/FavoriteThingsDay9/Hermione.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Okay, hear us out: While we believe that the book version almost always trumps the movie version of a tale, were still glad that moviemakers are finding inspiration in great books. When the adaptation is good, they can add a delightful complement to the versions that played out in our head. And even when they miss the mark,
       <a href="http://www.thestar.com/entertainment/movies/2013/05/09/the_great_gatsby_the_movie_boosts_sales_of_the_great_gatsby_the_book.html" target="_blank">
        time
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="http://www.csmonitor.com/Books/chapter-and-verse/2013/0430/The-Hunger-Games-trilogy-drives-juvenile-fiction-sales-up-13.1-percent-for-2012" target="_blank">
        time
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/life/books/2013/06/27/book-buzz/2464199/" target="_blank">
        time
       </a>
       again movies give a big boost to book sales. If it gets more people reading, then were all for it!
      </p>
      <p>
       <font size="1">

        <a href="http://www.robintatlowlord.com" target="_blank">
         Robin Tatlow-Lord

      </p>

        Day 8

       <em>
        <font size="5">
         <i>
          Chicago Manual of

       <em>

       <font size="5">
        Favorite
       </font>
       <font size="5">
        Librarians
       </font>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img align="left" alt="Librarians Have Heart" src="http://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/dms/cmos/images/FavoriteThingsDay8/SuperLibrarian.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/pinsonlibrary/librarians-the-original-search-engine">
        original search engine
       </a>
       , librarians have shaped our lives from the short shelves of the childrens section to the deep archives of the research library. In a sea of information, they provide a guiding light while deftly handling every questionyes, even those that start with, It was a smallish book with a blue cover . . .
      </p>

       We love authors (especially those who know and, most important, use
       <em>
        The Chicago Manual of Style
       </em>

       <em>
        <a href="http://www.subversivecopyeditor.com/" target="_blank">

        </a>

        book
       </a>
       fits in stockings hung by chimneys with (or without) care and is the perfect gift for the editor on your list. Even jollier? The paperback is available for $10 on our
       <a href="https://cdcshoppingcart.uchicago.edu/Cart/Cart.aspx?ISBN=9780226734255&amp;Q=1PRESS=CHICAGO&amp;PROMO=SCE2013" target="_blank">
        website

       with code SCE2013, through December 31.

      <p>
       We are grateful for dictionaries, both print and online. Theyre usually our first stop when trying to figure out the correct spelling (hint, we usually go with the first instance) and whether
       <em>
        irregardless
       </em>
       has truly been accepted as a word. And we love the new
       <a href="http://public.oed.com/appeals/" target="_blank">
        <em>
         OED
        </em>
        Appeals
       </a>
       , which gives everyone a chance to assist researchers in finding the earliest recorded date (or some other key aspect) of a word.

      </p>
      <p>
       We have all had teachers who inspired us to work at our writing and think critically about what we read. With patience and a careful eye they review stacks of papers and they teach students that good writing matters and careful revising matters more. They are our first editors, and we wouldnt be where we are today without their encouragement. In these difficult times in education, we are so very thankful for teachers. Is there a teacher who made a difference in your writing? Give them a shout-out on our
       <a href="https://www.facebook.com/permalink.php?story_fbid=10151887227689151&amp;id=139396659150" target="_blank">

       You know the difference between grammar and style, you worry about hyphenation, and you have favorite paragraphs committed to memory (CMOS 6:18 anyone?). You are a fan of the
       <em>
        Chicago Manual of Style
       </em>
       . Whether you own a print copy festooned with sticky notes or an online version tagged with notes and bookmarks, you know exactly what you dont know, and where you should look it up. Were glad to have you as readers and look forward to another year of comments, parodies, and
