        <p>

          Beach Tree
         </a>
         restaurant. It was amazing! I asked for the recipe and finally got around to recreating it!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://dietersdownfall.com/chocolate-zuccotto/1-dd-chocolate-zuccotto-35/" rel="attachment wp-att-2300">
          <img alt="Chocolate Bomb Cake. What's on the inside? It's a surprise!" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2300" height="900" src="http://dietersdownfall.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/1-dd-Chocolate-Zuccotto-35-600x900.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         Of course at the restaurant, they cant make a huge dessert like this. It was made in a single serving bomb with chocolate cherry ice cream and a huge chocolate curl on top. Check out all the desserts I got to try on my
         <a href="http://instagram.com/p/qvlnBKAb_F/?modal=true" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Instagram feed
         </a>
         . Im kinda wishing I made a big chocolate decoration for the top, but honestly, all the fun stuff was inside!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://dietersdownfall.com/chocolate-zuccotto/1-dd-chocolate-zuccotto-41/" rel="attachment wp-att-2301">
          <img alt="1 dd Chocolate Zuccotto (41)" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2301" height="900" src="http://dietersdownfall.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/1-dd-Chocolate-Zuccotto-41-600x900.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         Zuccotto is also called a Bomb Cake and I made one a few years ago on
         <a href="http://www.pintsizedbaker.com/2012/03/chocolate-strawberry-bomb-cake.html" target="_blank">
          Pint Sized Baker
         </a>
         . As you can see, Ive come a long way since Ive made that cake, thats for sure.
        </p>
        <p>
         I loved the little cubes of cake and the mousse in the middle was fantastic.
        </p>
        <p>
         The mousse wasnt too sweet, in fact, I wish it were a bit sweeter. However, with the ice cream and the Luxardo cherries, it was wonderful! The bittersweet chocolate used is not sweet (as the name implies) and the little crunchy bits of cookies along with the sweet cherries was so good.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://dietersdownfall.com/chocolate-zuccotto/1-dd-chocolate-zuccotto-60/" rel="attachment wp-att-2302">
          <img alt="This decadent dessert is actually easy to make.          Id like to thank Abby from the
         <a href="http://www.fourseasons.com/hualalai/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

The next time youre in Kona, Hawaii, I highly suggest making a reservation at the Beach Tree or at Ulu at the Four Seasons. I got to trythe Double Chocolate Cherry Zuccotto, thelemon crostata with olive oil ice cream and the famous ice cream bon bons at Beach Tree with my daughter.
        </p>
        <p>
         I went back a week later with my mom for a Mommy/Daughter date where we had a chocolateSouffl, tumpura friedcheesecake, and a chocolate fudge dessert they called C4. Talk about chocolate overload!

         <div class="er-top-box">
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">

          <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
           Chocolate Zuccotto
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="er-recipe-details">
          <div class="er-half-width er-float-left">
           <p>
            Prep time:
            <span>
             <time datetime="PT30M" itemprop="prepTime">
              30 mins
             </time>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Cook time:
            <span>
             <time datetime="PT15M" itemprop="cookTime">
              15 mins
             </time>
            </span>
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="er-half-width er-float-left" itemprop="nutrition" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/NutritionInformation">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="er-photo-details">
          <div class="er-photo-container">
           <img itemprop="image" src="http://dietersdownfall.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/1-dd-Chocolate-Zuccotto-70-600x900.jpg"/>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
           Chocolate Zuccotto (bomb cake) with a light spongy cake exterior and filled with chocolate mousse and cherries served with Buttermilk Ice Cream and Amaretti Cookies.
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Cake
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup Dutch-process cocoa
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon baking powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            5 large eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon vanilla
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Mousse
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            12 oz bittersweet chocolate (64%)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            24 oz Heavy Cream (divided)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             c sugar
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Zuccotto
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            <a href="http://dietersdownfall.com/amaretti-cookies/" target="_blank">
             Amaertti Cookies
            </a>
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00852OTXE/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00852OTXE&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=pinsizbak-20&amp;linkId=CV7HNMZV3QMKJMHP">
             Luxardo, Maraschino Cherries
            </a>
            <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=pinsizbak-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00852OTXE" style="border:none !important; margin:0px !important;" width="1"/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Cake
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat the oven to 350 and move the rack to the lower half of the oven.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Grease an 18x13 inch jelly roll pan. Grease the bottom, then add parchment paper, then grease the paper.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Combine flour, cocoa, baking powder and salt in a bowl.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In your electric mixer, beat the 5 eggs. Gradually add sugar. Whip until very thick and frothy. This may take up to 8 to 10 minutes. Then add in vanilla.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Sift the dry ingredients into the egg mixture. (I used a little strainer as my sifter and it works out  great. You basically don't want lumps to form.)
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Then fold the egg and flour to combine. No stirring or mixing! Fold gently. You want to keep all that air in the mixture as possible.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour into prepared pan and bake for 15 minutes.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            While it's cooking, prepare parchment paper in the counter greased with some shortening.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            When done, immediately flip the cake over on to the parchment paper. (This was the most difficult task) and let it cool for 15 minutes. Cut into 1-inch squares. I found that a pizza cutter worked great for this
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           To For the Mousse
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Chop the chocolate into small pieces and place into a large glass bowl.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bring 12 oz of cream to a simmer and pour over the chocolate. Let it sit for 1 minute to melt the chocolate then whisk until smooth. Leave it at room temperature for at least an hour to cool.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Whip the remaining 12 oz of cream, gradually adding the sugar, until soft peaks form. When the chocolate is cool enough, fold in the whipped cream to make a mousse.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           To assemble
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Line a 1 quart bowl with plastic wrap leaving excess over the edges.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Line to bowl with the cake squares being sure to pack them tightly, no gaps should be seen. Add  of the mousse to the bowl then crumble 8 amaretti cookies and add  cup of Luxardo cherries and juice. Cover with the  of the remaining mousse and add another layer of cookie crumbs and cherries. Cover with remaining mousse and smooth with an off set spatula.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cover and freeze overnight to set.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Invert the bowl over a serving platter and carefully use the plastic wrap to umold the zuccotto. Let it thaw for an hour in the fridge, top with powdered sugar and serve.
           </li>
          </ol>
