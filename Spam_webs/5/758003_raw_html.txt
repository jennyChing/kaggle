           <h1>
            Who founded Mothers Day?
           </h1>
           <div id="information">
            By Adrienne Burke | Yahoo Small Business
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="social-links social-links-top">
           <a class="tumblr-ico" href="https://www.tumblr.com/share/photo?clickthru=https%3A%2F%2Fsmallbusiness.yahoo.com%2Fadvisor%2Fblogs%2Fprofit-minded%2Ffounded-mother-day-entrepreneurial-woman-course-172247751.html&amp;caption=Who%20founded%20Mother%E2%80%99s%20Day%3F&amp;source=https%3A%2F%2Fs.yimg.com%2Fbt%2Fapi%2Fres%2F1.2%2FPpmFl9BTlJ2EDtQkwB2MzA--%2FYXBwaWQ9eW5ld3M7cT04NTt3PTE5MA--%2Fhttp%3A%2F%2Fmedia.zenfs.com%2Fen-US%2Fblogs%2Fprofitminded%2FAnnaMarieJarvis.jpg" target="_blank">
           </a>
           <a class="facebook-ico" href="https://www.facebook.com/dialog/feed?app_id=90376669494&amp;link=https%3A%2F%2Fsmallbusiness.yahoo.com%2Fadvisor%2Fblogs%2Fprofit-minded%2Ffounded-mother-day-entrepreneurial-woman-course-172247751.html&amp;name=Who%20founded%20Mother%E2%80%99s%20Day%3F&amp;description=Who%20founded%20Mother%E2%80%99s%20Day%3F&amp;picture=https%3A%2F%2Fs.yimg.com%2Fbt%2Fapi%2Fres%2F1.2%2FPpmFl9BTlJ2EDtQkwB2MzA--%2FYXBwaWQ9eW5ld3M7cT04NTt3PTE5MA--%2Fhttp%3A%2F%2Fmedia.zenfs.com%2Fen-US%2Fblogs%2Fprofitminded%2FAnnaMarieJarvis.jpg&amp;redirect_uri=https%3A%2F%2Fsmallbusiness.yahoo.com%2Fadvisor%2Fblogs%2Fprofit-minded%2Ffounded-mother-day-entrepreneurial-woman-course-172247751.html&amp;display=popup&amp;show_error=yes" target="_blank">
           </a>
           <a class="twitter-ico" href="https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=Who%20founded%20Mother%E2%80%99s%20Day%3F&amp;url=https%3A%2F%2Fsmallbusiness.yahoo.com%2Fadvisor%2Fblogs%2Fprofit-minded%2Ffounded-mother-day-entrepreneurial-woman-course-172247751.html&amp;via=YSmallBusiness" target="_blank">
           </a>
           <a class="google-ico" href="https://plus.google.com/share?url=https%3A%2F%2Fsmallbusiness.yahoo.com%2Fadvisor%2Fblogs%2Fprofit-minded%2Ffounded-mother-day-entrepreneurial-woman-course-172247751.html" target="_blank">
           </a>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="content">
           <div class="yom-mod yom-art-content " id="mediablogbody" itemprop="articleBody">
            <div class="bd">
             <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
             <p class="first">
              <span class="yom-figure yom-fig-right" style="width:190px;">
               <a href="https://s1.yimg.com/bt/api/res/1.2/slY5uPg3hkAktJiVITSp_Q--/YXBwaWQ9eW5ld3M7cT04NQ--/http://media.zenfs.com/en-US/blogs/profitminded/AnnaMarieJarvis.jpg">
                <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1716" height="227" src="https://s.yimg.com/bt/api/res/1.2/PpmFl9BTlJ2EDtQkwB2MzA--/YXBwaWQ9eW5ld3M7cT04NTt3PTE5MA--/http://media.zenfs.com/en-US/blogs/profitminded/AnnaMarieJarvis.jpg" title="AnnaMarieJarvis" width="190"/>
               </a>
               <span class="legend">
                AnnaMarieJarvis
               </span>
              </span>
              Mothers Day was founded in 1907 by a West Virginia woman as a tribute to her own mother. In her 40s, Anna Marie Jarvis, a college graduate, quit her job and incorporated herself as the Mothers Day International Association.
             </p>
             <p>
              Jarvis was so passionate about her vision that she succeeded within 6 years in persuading the governors of nearly every state in the union to embrace Mothers Day. By 1914, she had won over the U.S. Congress. That year President Woodrow Wilson signed a Congressional resolution declaring the second Sunday in May the nations day to honor mothers (for their role in the family, however, not the public sphere).
             </p>
             <p>
              Rampant commercialization of Mothers Day has kept it alive for a century, but the strong-willed Jarvis, ironically, detested any profiting from the holiday. She believed offspring should honor mothers with handmade gifts and letters, rather than with printed greeting cards and floral arrangements. So, after succeeding in seeing Mothers Day widely adopted, Jarvis spent the rest of her life and funds fighting those who tried to profit from it, it has been
              <a href="http://womenshistory.about.com/od/mothersday/a/anna_jarvis.htm">
               widely reported
              </a>
              . Her battles included filing a lawsuit against New Yorks governor and publicly criticizing Eleanor Roosevelt for work with a Mothers Day committee.
             </p>
             <p>
              Jarvis set about fulfilling her vision with the passion of a typical entrepreneur. With funding from familyincluding from the estate of her brother, the founder of a taxi companyshe trademarked the phrase Mothers Day and pursued a plan to see the holiday adopted worldwide, according to
              <a href="http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=F00E12F73D5E167B93C7AB178AD95F4C8485F9">
               her 1948 New York Times obituary
              </a>
              . Unfortunately, like many entrepreneurs, it seems Jarvis was better at the execution of her vision than at managing the day-to-day business. Though at one point she had the resources to purchase a building solely for housing her prolific correspondences, the
              <em>
               Times
              </em>
              reported upon her death that her finances had become almost a hopeless muddle. Other biographies say she died in poverty.
             </p>
             <p>
              <span class="yom-figure yom-fig-right" style="width:310px;">
               <a href="https://s.yimg.com/bt/api/res/1.2/UBV9qsd3xOliotHO1azzrA--/YXBwaWQ9eW5ld3M7cT04NQ--/http://media.zenfs.com/en-US/blogs/profitminded/149069038.jpg">
                <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-1710" height="207" src="https://s3.yimg.com/bt/api/res/1.2/qPm9JmtvEdRP4YdQGDMytA--/YXBwaWQ9eW5ld3M7cT04NTt3PTMxMA--/http://media.zenfs.com/en-US/blogs/profitminded/149069038.jpg" width="310"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              One final irony of Jarviss life, according to her
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anna_Jarvis">
               Wikipedia biography
              </a>
              : Anna Marie Jarvis never married and had no children. Perhaps she was too busy running her organization.
             </p>
