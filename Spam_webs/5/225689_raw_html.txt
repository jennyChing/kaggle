    Vaporizer (inhalation device)
   </h1>

     <p>
      <br/>
      A
      <b>
       vaporizer
      </b>
      or
      <b>
       vaporiser
      </b>
      is a device used to vaporize the active ingredients of plant material, commonly
      <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">
       cannabis
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">
       tobacco
      </a>
      , or other herbs or blends for the purpose of
      <a href="/wiki/Inhalation" title="Inhalation">
       inhalation
      </a>
      . However, they can be used with pure chemicals when mixed with plant material (e.g. tobacco-free nicotine).
     </p>
     <p>
      Vaporizers contain various forms of extraction chambers including straight bore,
      <a href="/wiki/Venturi_effect" title="Venturi effect">
       venturi
      </a>
      , or sequential venturi, and are made of materials such as metal or glass. The extracted vapor may be collected in an inflatable bag, or inhaled directly through a hose or pipe. With no combustion happening when used properly and cooler temperatures,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      a significantly better efficiency in extracting the ingredients can be obtained.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Hence, the irritating and harmful effects of smoking are heavily reduced,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17429350_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pmid17429350-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      as is
      <a href="/wiki/Passive_smoking" title="Passive smoking">
       secondhand smoke
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="E-cigarette_vaporizers">
       E-cigarette vaporizers
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cig_a_like--_2013-10-21_03-47.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1335" data-file-width="2000" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Cig_a_like--_2013-10-21_03-47.jpg/220px-Cig_a_like--_2013-10-21_03-47.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Cig_a_like--_2013-10-21_03-47.jpg/330px-Cig_a_like--_2013-10-21_03-47.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Cig_a_like--_2013-10-21_03-47.jpg/440px-Cig_a_like--_2013-10-21_03-47.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cig_a_like--_2013-10-21_03-47.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        A first generation electronic cigarette resembling a tobacco cigarette.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      An
      <a href="/wiki/Electronic_cigarette" title="Electronic cigarette">
       electronic cigarette
      </a>
      (e-cig or e-cigarette), personal vaporizer (PV) or electronic nicotine delivery system (ENDS) is a
      <a href="/wiki/Battery_(electricity)" title="Battery (electricity)">
       battery
      </a>
      -powered vaporizer that simulates the feeling of
      <a href="/wiki/Tobacco_smoking" title="Tobacco smoking">
       smoking
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caponnetto2012_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Caponnetto2012-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      They are often cylindrical, with many variations.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grana2014_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Grana2014-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Some e-cigarettes look like traditional cigarettes, but others do not.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pepper2013_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pepper2013-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There are disposable or reusable versions.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EbbertAgunwamba2015_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-EbbertAgunwamba2015-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The user inhales an
      <a href="/wiki/Aerosol" title="Aerosol">
       aerosol
      </a>
      , commonly called
      <a href="/wiki/Vapor" title="Vapor">
       vapor
      </a>
      , rather than
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cigarette_smoke" title="Cigarette smoke">
       cigarette smoke
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cheng2014_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cheng2014-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      E-cigarettes typically have a
      <a href="/wiki/Heating_element" title="Heating element">
       heating element
      </a>
      that atomizes a
      <a href="/wiki/Solution#Liquid_solutions" title="Solution">
       liquid solution
      </a>
      known as e-liquid.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weaver2014_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Weaver2014-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      E-liquids usually contain
      <a href="/wiki/Propylene_glycol" title="Propylene glycol">
       propylene glycol
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Glycerol" title="Glycerol">
       glycerin
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nicotine" title="Nicotine">
       nicotine
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Flavor#Flavorants_or_flavorings" title="Flavor">
       flavorings
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grana2014_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Grana2014-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      E-liquids are also sold without propylene glycol, without nicotine, or without flavors.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kacker2014_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Kacker2014-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brandon2015_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Brandon2015-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cochrane2014_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cochrane2014-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The benefits and
      <a href="/wiki/Safety_of_electronic_cigarettes" title="Safety of electronic cigarettes">
       health risks of electronic cigarettes
      </a>
      are uncertain.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EbbertAgunwamba2015_11-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-EbbertAgunwamba2015-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harrell2014_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Harrell2014-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      One review found limited evidence of a benefit as a
      <a href="/wiki/Smoking_cessation" title="Smoking cessation">
       smoking cessation
      </a>
      aid,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cochrane2014_16-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cochrane2014-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but there is no evidence they are better than regulated medication for quitting smoking.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harrell2014_17-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Harrell2014-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Their usefulness in
      <a href="/wiki/Tobacco_harm_reduction" title="Tobacco harm reduction">
       tobacco harm reduction
      </a>
      is unclear.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drummond2014_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Drummond2014-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      One review found their safety risk is similar to that of
      <a href="/wiki/Smokeless_tobacco" title="Smokeless tobacco">
       smokeless tobacco
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caponnetto2013_19-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Caponnetto2013-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Another found United States
      <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration" title="Food and Drug Administration">
       Food and Drug Administration
      </a>
      (FDA) approved products, such as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicotine_inhaler" title="Nicotine inhaler">
       nicotine inhalers
      </a>
      , are probably safer than e-cigarettes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Drummond2014_18-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Drummond2014-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The e-liquid has a low level of
      <a href="/wiki/Toxicity" title="Toxicity">
       toxicity
      </a>
      , and contamination with various chemicals has been identified in the product.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bertholon2013_20-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bertholon2013-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Marijuana_vaporizers">
       Marijuana vaporizers
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Vaporizers are also used to inhale
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marijuana" title="Marijuana">
       marijuana
      </a>
      . Of the studies about vaporizing marijuana, few have addressed the quality of the vapor extracted and delivered; instead, studies usually focus on the mode of usage of the vaporizers. There are 483 identifiable chemical constituents known to exist in the
      <a href="/wiki/Cannabis" title="Cannabis">
       cannabis
      </a>
      plant,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and at least 85 different
      <a href="/wiki/Cannabinoid" title="Cannabinoid">
       cannabinoids
      </a>
      have been isolated from the plant.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The aromatic terpenoids begin to vaporize at 126.0C (258.8F),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but the more bio-active
      <a href="/wiki/Tetrahydrocannabinol" title="Tetrahydrocannabinol">
       tetrahydrocannabinol (THC)
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Cannabidiol" title="Cannabidiol">
       cannabidiol (CBD)
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Cannabinol" title="Cannabinol">
       cannabinol (CBN)
      </a>
      do not vaporize until near their respective
      <a href="/wiki/Boiling_point" title="Boiling point">
       boiling points
      </a>
      : THC 157C (315F),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russo01_24-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-russo01-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      CBD 160180 C (320356 F),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russo01_24-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-russo01-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and CBN 185C (365F).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russo01_24-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-russo01-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Vaporizing is more efficient than smoking, because approximately 30% of THC in marijuana or hashish cigarettes is destroyed by
      <a href="/wiki/Pyrolysis" title="Pyrolysis">
       pyrolysis
      </a>
      during smoking.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huestis_25-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-huestis-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Vaporizers_for_medical_use">
       Vaporizers for medical use
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:132px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Volcano_Vaporizer.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2560" data-file-width="1072" height="310" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Volcano_Vaporizer.jpg/130px-Volcano_Vaporizer.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Volcano_Vaporizer.jpg/195px-Volcano_Vaporizer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Volcano_Vaporizer.jpg/260px-Volcano_Vaporizer.jpg 2x" width="130"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Volcano_Vaporizer.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        A forced-air vaporizer. The detachable balloon (top) fills with vapors that are then inhaled.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Studies have shown that vaporizing cannabis exposes the user to lower levels of harmful substances than smoking cannabis.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gieringer_2004_26-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Gieringer_2004-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hazekamp_2006_29-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hazekamp_2006-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      These findings are important for it is estimated that 1020 percent of patients with
      <a href="/wiki/Chronic_pain" title="Chronic pain">
       chronic pain
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">
       multiple sclerosis
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Epilepsy" title="Epilepsy">
       epilepsy
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">
       HIV
      </a>
      /
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS">
       AIDS
      </a>
      have admitted to smoking cannabis for therapeutic purposes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      For patients, a study found that smoking cannabis sativa reduced daily pain by 34%, a statistically significant amount.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In a study published in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Journal_of_Psychopharmacology" title="Journal of Psychopharmacology">
        Journal of Psychopharmacology
       </a>
      </i>
      in May 2008, it was stated that vaporizers were a "suitable method for the administration of THC".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Journal_of_Psychopharmacology_32-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Journal_of_Psychopharmacology-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A 2007 study by the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_San_Francisco" title="University of California, San Francisco">
       University of California, San Francisco
      </a>
      , published in the
      <i>
       Journal of the American Academy of Neurology
      </i>
      , founded that "there was virtually no exposure to harmful combustion products using the vaporizing device".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17429350_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-pmid17429350-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A 2006 study performed by researchers at
      <a href="/wiki/Leiden_University" title="Leiden University">
       Leiden University
      </a>
      found that vaporizers were "safe and effective
      <a href="/wiki/Cannabinoid" title="Cannabinoid">
       cannabinoid
      </a>
      delivery system(s)". The study stated that the amount of THC delivered by vaporizers were equivalent to the amount delivered by smoking.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hazekamp_2006_29-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hazekamp_2006-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Because of those studies and other studies, vaporizers are medically sound devices for delivering THC.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gieringer_2001_33-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Gieringer_2001-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Efficiency">
       Efficiency
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The proposed factors affecting output include:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gieringer_2004_26-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Gieringer_2004-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hazekamp_2006_29-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hazekamp_2006-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Temperature
      </li>
      <li>
       Specimen
       <a href="/wiki/Density" title="Density">
        density
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       Weight, content of water and
       <a href="/wiki/Essential_oil" title="Essential oil">
        essential oils
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       Consistency of material in the filling chamber
      </li>
      <li>
       Storage time of the vapor
      </li>
      <li>
       Inhalation method (breathing technique)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Not all those have been scientifically tested. Research using vaporizers found the delivery efficiency highest at around 226C (439F), falling to about half efficiency at 150C (302F) to 180C (356F) degrees depending on material.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hazekamp_2006_29-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hazekamp_2006-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The purest preparations produced the highest efficiencies, about 56% for pure THC versus 29% for plant material (female flower tops) with 12%
      <a href="/wiki/Tetrahydrocannabinolic_acid" title="Tetrahydrocannabinolic acid">
       THCA
      </a>
      content. Besides THC, several other cannabinoids as well as a range of other plant components including terpenoids were detected in the plant material. Using pure THC in the vaporizer, no degradation products (delta-8-THC (D8-THC), cannabinol (CBN), or unknown compounds) were detected by
      <a href="/wiki/High-performance_liquid_chromatography" title="High-performance liquid chromatography">
       HPLC
      </a>
      analysis.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hazekamp_2006_29-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hazekamp_2006-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The longer vapor is stored, the more THC is lost as it condenses on the surface of the vaporizer or the balloon. This loss may be negligible over a few minutes but may exceed 50% after 90 minutes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hazekamp_2006_29-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hazekamp_2006-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Leiden University study found that as much as 3040% of inhaled THC was not absorbed by the lungs but simply exhaled. However, they did not find large individual differences in the amounts exhaled.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hazekamp_2006_29-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hazekamp_2006-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Culinary_application">
       Culinary application
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Vaporizers are sometimes used by chefs as a method of applying controlled heat to herbs and spices to release flavors that are otherwise difficult to titrate or apply, or that might be spoiled by overheating during cooking.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caterer_34-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-caterer-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_35-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_36-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Grant Achatz, chef-proprietor of
      <a href="/wiki/Alinea_(restaurant)" title="Alinea (restaurant)">
       Alinea
      </a>
      in Chicago, "uses the aroma-filled bags as place-mats, punctured when plates are placed in front of the customer".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caterer_34-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-caterer-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
