     <div class="entry-content">
      <p>
       Compiling a list of the 125 films every man must see was no easy task  personal favourites were discarded, profanity was rampant and it became abundantly clear exactly why nobody likes to see a grown man cry.
      </p>
      <p>
       But we did it  125 masterful movies that everyone here at Fataldose could live with. The question is, can you?
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        1.Oldboy (2003)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Scene from Oldboy (2003)." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2480" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/oldboy-2003.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Most infamous for its live octopus-eating scene, in which actor Choi Min-sik actually ate octopi on set (four were used; he said a prayer for each one), Oldboys depiction of the effects of solitude on the human psyche are gripping and inspiring in equal measure.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Your gravest mistake wasnt failing to find the answer. You cant find the right answer if you ask the wrong questions.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        2.Inception (2010)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Inception" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2727" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Inception.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       For many, Inception was the film that underlined Christopher Nolans status as one the hottest directors in the business, and for very good reason. A rare occasion where bleeding-edge special effects are matched by an equally impressive story and solid characters, Inceptions action/sci-fi dreamscapes will leave you open-mouthed and aching for more.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       They say we only use a fraction of our brains true potential. Now thats when were awake. When were asleep, we can do almost anything.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        3. Citizen Kane (1941)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="CitizenKane" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2728" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/CitizenKane.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       For many Citizen Kane remains the greatest movie ever made; for others an overrated bore fest lauded by those desperately seeking to appear cultured. Whatever your take on Orson Welles classic theres no denying its impact, and few would criticise the magnificent performance by Welles himself as Kane, or those of his contemporaries.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Old age. Its the only disease, Mr. Thompson, that you dont look forward to being cured of.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        4.The Terminator (1984)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Terminator" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2730" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Terminator.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The golden age of Arnie began with The Terminator, a classic ahead of its time that tapped into the raging desire for sci-fi robot action ignited by Bladerunner. Perfectly suiting the Austrian Oaks penchant for doing his talking with his muscles, the quintessential monosyllabic cybernetic organism rapidly gained cult status and remains Schwarzeneggers most iconic role to date.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       That son of a bitch took my pants.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        5.Rocky (1976)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Rocky" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2731" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Rocky.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The definitive take on man overcoming the odds,
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocky" target="_blank" title="Rocky">
        Rocky
       </a>
       was more than just a boxing movie. Perfectly encompassing the sorts of clichs about heart, guts, blood sweat and tears that are typically screamed at high-school quarterbacks by desperate coaches, Rocky Balboa lived the dream AND got the girl in a classic blend of triumphing over adversity.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Youre gonna eat lightnin and youre gonna crap thunder!
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        6.The Godfather (1972)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Godfather" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2732" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Godfather.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Much imitated but never bettered, The Godfather recently came second in an Empire magazine best films of all time list to Empire Strikes Back, but despite this trilogys own claims for a superior sequel, Coppolas classic is a true masterpiece, showcasing career-defining performances from its leads that brilliantly deliver both the beauty and the fury of the Italian mafia to the big screen.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       I have to get back to work. Its part of the wedding. No Sicilian can ever refuse a request on his daughters wedding day.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        7.Blade Runner (1982)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Bladerunner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2733" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Bladerunner.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ridley Scotts quintessential sci-fi showed a darker side to an already A-list Harrison Ford, spawning an endless collection of special editions and alternate cuts. Scotts Final Cut is probably the best place to start; the only one in which he had full artistic control, but the sheer scope for reimagining underlines the grand scale of whichever vision of a replicant-infested 2019 Los Angeles you choose to enjoy.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Quite an experience to live in fear, isnt it? Thats what it is to be a slave.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        8.Rudy (1993)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Rudy" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2734" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Rudy.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Warming up nicely for his role as a hobbit in Lord of the Rings, Sean Astin showed that being short neednt stop you from fulfilling your dreams as he took the part of Daniel E. Rudy Ruettiger in this biopic about a boy whose dream it was to play college football, despite being half the size of his team mates.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Who cares how much effort I put in, if it doesnt produce any results.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        9.Once Upon a Time in the West (1968)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Once Upon a Time in the West" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2736" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Once-Upon-a-Time-in-the-West.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       In a classic western that combines all of the most essential elements of a rip-roaring story of heroism and chivalry, Charles Bronson comes to the aid of a heroine whose family has been slaughtered by a deadly assassin. Death comes to those who hear the sweet silvery sound of the Harmonica.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       How can you trust a man who wears both a belt and suspenders? The man cant even trust his own pants.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        10.The Incredibles (2004)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Incredibles" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2737" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Incredibles.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Incredibles was a grand celebration of everything Disney and Pixar had learned about creating entertaining animations that could be enjoyed in equal measure by child and adult. Frequently funny, often exciting and always entertaining, the story of super-powered family forced from the comfort of their quiet suburban life to fight crime is a rip-roaring success on every level.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Weve frozen all of Syndromes assets. If he even sneezes, well be there with a hanky and a pair of handcuffs.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        11.Batman Begins (2005)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="batman" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2738" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/batman.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The saviour of
       <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0372784/" target="_blank" title="Batman">
        Batman
       </a>
       fans the world over, Christopher Nolans reimagining of The Dark Knight allowed us to cast the pun-ridden, nipple-suited, icon-destroying travesties of recent years firmly from memory. Batman was back, and despite the lack of a major villain in the first reboot it laid solid, origin-driven foundations for what was to become arguably the greatest superhero trilogy ever made.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       But a criminal is not complicated. What you really fear is inside yourself. You fear your own power. You fear your anger, the drive to do great or terrible things.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        12.Aliens (1986)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="aliens" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2739" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/aliens.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Conceiving a sequel for Ridleys Scotts tense but slow-burning sci-fi horror was no easy task, but when James Cameron took the helm he simply focused on what he does best  guns, essentially, lots and lots of guns. Though it split the fan-base somewhat, few could argue that it remains one of the most frantic and nail-bitingly tense action films ever made.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Wed better get back, cause itll be dark soon, and they mostly come at night mostly.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        13.The Great Escape (1963)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Great Escape" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2740" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Great-Escape.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Perhaps Steve McQueens greatest hour, this stirring tale of daring do was based on the true story of a group of escape-minded POWs imprisoned in a German camp during World War 2. A beautifully blended mix of comedy and adventure, plus that iconic soundtrack, make this one to put on the list.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       I havent seen Berlin yet, from the ground or from the air, and I plan on doing both before the war is over.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        14.Blazing Saddles (1974)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="blazing" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2741" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/blazing.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Gene Wilder brings his comic best to king-of-the-spoofs Mel Brooks parody of the classic western as an unlikely Sherrif is appointed to guard over a town where everyone seems to be called Johnson. Typically timeless, there are plenty of laughs to be had even with forty years under its belt.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Badges? We dont need no stinking badges.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        15.Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid (1969)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="butch" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2753" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/butch.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       One of, if not
       <em>
        the
       </em>
       classic movie duo, Butch brings the brains and Sundance brings the can-do spirit to a crime biography that sees serial train/bank robbers flee to Bolivia to escape their pursuers. Newman and Redford are ideal foils but it was the writing, cinematography and music that won it four Oscars, having been nominated for three more.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       You just keep thinkin, Butch. Thats what youre good at.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        16.Star Wars: Episode IV  A New Hope (1977)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Star Wars" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2743" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Star-Wars.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       With a total of six Academy Awards under its belt, all of which were based around production, its clearly no exaggeration to say that the original Star Wars was one of the most groundbreaking films ever made. Introducing us to a franchise that would become one of the most iconic in all of cinema, the original is timeless to this day. And yes, thats the one without all of that digital remastering.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Hokey religions and ancient weapons are no match for a good blaster at your side, kid.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        17.Ghostbusters (1984)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Ghostbusters" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2744" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Ghostbusters.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ghosts are not traditionally a market that has been easy to sell to kids, but all of that changed when Ghostbusters beautifully balanced combination of comedy, wackiness and a handful of quite genuine scares hit the screens. Bill Murray and co. made it perfectly clear that you shouldnt be afraid of no ghosts, creating an instant classic that was equally appealing to children and adults alike.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Weve been going about this all wrong. This Mr. Stay Pufts okay! Hes a sailor, hes in New York; we get this guy laid, we wont have any trouble!
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        18.Reservoir Dogs (1992)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Reservoir Dogs" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2752" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Reservoir-Dogs.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Thanks to its infamous ear-chopping scene, which for todays horror-obsessed generation is about as shocking as a trip to the zoo, Quentin Tarantinos name went household with this talky crime-thriller. Though it emerged that QT was (is?), how shall we say, often inspired by other films, this didnt stop Reservoir Dogs from being the defining moment in launching his career.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       I dont wanna kill anybody. But if I gotta get out that door, and youre standing in my way, one way or the other, youre gettin outta my way.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        19.Glory (1989)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Glory" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2751" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Glory.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       An unusually sombre Matthew Broderick leads the charge but Denzel Washingtons performance as Private Trip stands out in this drama set during the US Civil War, which followed the first all-black volunteer company. It bagged him his first Oscar, for best actor in a supporting role.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       We fight for men and women whose poetry is not yet written but which will presently be as enviable and as renowned as any.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        20.The Big Lebowski (1998)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Big Lebowski" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2746" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Big-Lebowski.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Coen Brothers unique brand of comic flair was used to great effect in this comedy/crime caper, which follows The Dude Lebowski, a grimy down-and-out with a penchant for bowling whos mistaken for a millionaire. Though it received a mixed reception on release, its now firmly embroiled in cult status.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Walter, I love you, but sooner or later, youre going to have to face the fact youre a goddamn moron.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        21.American History X (1998)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="American History X" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2748" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/American-History-X.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Edward Nortons turn as a former neo-nazi skinhead was one dramatic change of pace, and his performance as leader of a violent white supremacist gang was convincing enough to earn him an Oscar nomination. Fresh out of prison, Nortons Derek goes to lengths to help prevent his brother Danny from making the mistakes that scarred his own past.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Im the most dangerous man in this prison. You know why? Cause I control the underwear.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        22.Jaws (1975)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Jaws" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2762" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Jaws.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Spielbergs strength was in creating dramatic films that have genuine impact and keep you gripped. Jaws had this in spades, and as the first of what was to become many classic blockbusters was one of few to be genuinely frightening. The safe to go back in the water tagline of the sequel was one that most audiences could genuinely relate to.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Im not going to waste my time arguing with a man whos lining up to be a hot lunch.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        23.Hotel Rwanda (2004)
       </strong>
      </h3>

       I dont give a shit about sleeping, Leon. I want love, or death. Thats it.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        119.The Wolf of Wall Street (2013)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="WolfOfWallStreet" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2830" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/WolfOfWallStreet.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The DiCaprio-Scorsese love-in goes from strength to strength in this black comedy about a New York stockbroker who wilfully engages in security fraud and corruption on Wall Street in the 1990s. Not short of controversy (amongst other things, it holds the record for the most uses of the word fuck in a non-documentary); it was well nominated but fell short of a major prize.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Let me tell you something. Theres no nobility in poverty. Ive been a poor man, and Ive been a rich man. And I choose rich every fucking time.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        120.Risky Business (1983)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="RiskyBusiness" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2831" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/RiskyBusiness.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       One of Tom Cruises earliest outings made full use of his grinny persona in a Ferris Bueller-style romp that inspired millions of teenagers to long for the day their parents would go away, and millions of parents to beef up the security on their liquor cabinets and luxury cars.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Say what the fuck. If you cant say it, you cant do it.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        121.Star Wars: Episode VI  Return of the Jedi (1983)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="ReturnOfTheJedi" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2832" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ReturnOfTheJedi.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Lucas rounds off his uber-trilogy with a happier tone, and a healthy dose of criticism from those who knew not what was to come. Return of the Jedi didnt strike a chord with all of its growing audience of die-hard fans, but remained generally respected for repeating much of the formula that made its predecessors so successful.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       Im rather embarrassed, General Solo, but it appears that you are to be the main course at a banquet in my honor.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        122.Vertigo (1958)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Vertigo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2833" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Vertigo.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Forced into retirement after developing a fear of heights and dizziness, John Scottie Ferguson is hired by an acquaintance to follow his wife, on whom he develops a dangerous obsession. Though it received mixed reviews on first release, Vertigo is now looked back on fondly as one of Hitchcocks finest pieces of work.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       You shouldnt keep souvenirs of a killing. You shouldnt have been that sentimental.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        123.Snatch (2000)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Snatch" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2834" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Snatch.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Guy Ritchies
       <em>
        Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels
       </em>
       re-introduced the hard line of second-rate English backwater crime to the world, and hit even harder with the follow-up, thanks in part to a barely intelligible Brad Pitts One-Punch Mikey. Snatch pulls none of the aforementioned punches and clarifies for the world exactly what it means to be a proper nasty bastard.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       In the quiet words of the Virgin Mary come again?
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        124.Blood Diamond (2006)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Blood Diamond" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2835" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Blood-Diamond.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       DiCaprio shows his range as Archer, a tortured man who wilfully braves an African war zone rife with insurgent forces driven by greed and ambition in a search for the renowned blood diamonds, riches to kill for that were typically used to profit warlords during the Sierra Leone Civil War.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       In America, its bling bling. But out here its bling bang.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        125.Letters from Iwo Jima (2006)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <img alt="Letters Iwo Jima" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2836" height="396" src="http://www.fataldose.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Letters-Iwo-Jima.jpg" width="704"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The essential accompaniment to Clint Eastwoods Flags of our Fathers (he directed both) tells the story of the battle of Iwo Jima from the perspective of the Japanese, and though each was successful and highly regarded, this did better in the box office and is the more revered of the two. Powerful, personal and deeply moving.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Quote:
       </strong>
       We can die here, or we can continue fighting. Which would better serve the emperor?
      </p>
