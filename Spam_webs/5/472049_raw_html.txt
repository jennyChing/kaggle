      Why Hawaii Dolphins Want Humans To Overcome Chronic Pain?
     </h1>

      Published on 02. 18. 2015
     </i>

      <strong>
       Learn about the amazing benefits of dolphin therapy for people with chronic pain. Heres an article about the therapeutic effects of dolphins and how many Americans need experiential therapy with dolphins.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="dolphins assisted therapy" height="526" src="http://www.hawaiianrecovery.com//User_Files/P3210039.JPG" width="700"/>

     <h3 style="color: #f15b29;">
      <strong>
       If you have pain lasting more than 12 weeks, go see the dolphins!
      </strong>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Have you ever tried swimming with dolphins and wondered how they make you feel good just by staring back at you? Or longed to stay in a supportive and tropical environment with intelligent and emotionally-relaxing dolphins while battling with the inner You in pain?
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Hawaii dolphins are highly therapeutic marine mammals that can help you recover from chronic pain with its pain-detection signals (echolocation), sensitivity to humans and gentle behavior.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Dolphins are intelligent, social and playful. They thrive in shallow tropical oceans while there are very rare species living on rivers. Most of them are gray in color; they are sleek and smooth gliding. Dolphins are usually eight feet long, though it can be as short as four feet (Maui whale) or as long as 25 feet (Orca whale).
     </p>
     <h3 style="color: #f15b29;">
      <strong>
       Chronic pain and dolphin therapy
      </strong>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Chronicpain is a complex condition that goes beyond physical pain. It has psychological and emotional components that can be managed by AAT (Animal-Assisted Therapy), a form of treatment that uses animals.
     </p>
     <p>
      Animals, such as wild dolphins in Hawaii can reduce stress and rebuff side-effects of chronic pain such as anxiety, depression, anger, and despair. Several studies prove that animal-assisted therapy also improves the social, intellectual and emotional functioning of a person suffering from chronic pain.
     </p>

     <h3 style="color: #f15b29;">
      <strong>
       Why use the wild dolphins and not any other animal?
      </strong>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <span>
       As a human being, you may want to work with something that you can really connect with. Many people want an animal which values socialization, develops strong bonds and reveals real emotions and acts on personal intelligence. Thats where dolphins come in.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span>
       Like humans, dolphins are mammals too. They are large-brain animals that resemble total dependence on their parents. The nursing stage can range from 11 months to two years. Calves (baby dolphins) also depend on their parents from three years old until they become independent
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Wild dolphins are playful and agile. A mere look at their synchronized movements with fellow dolphins or breathtaking leaps and hops out of the tropical sea, or the way they tail around ships are surely entertaining. They also communicate with each other. So, its no wonder why they have established dolphin communities and change groups.
     </p>
     <p>
      Its also interesting that some wild dolphins live in a social group of at least five dolphins while some prefer bigger groups of a hundred or more. They use their social groups and echolocation (bio sonar or using echoes to locate or identify something) to catch sea animals for food.
     </p>
     <h3 style="color: #f15b29;">
      <strong>
       How intelligent are dolphins?
      </strong>
     </h3>
     <p>
      It depends on your definition of intelligence. But, even though you gauge it by survival, brain size, communication skills or ability to play-dolphins would surely top the rank. If the dolphins brain bigger than humans dont impress you much, take a look at their four brain lobes. Some experts suggest that this may have contributed to the dolphins ability to make difficult judgment.
     </p>
     <h3 style="color: #f15b29;">
      <strong>
       Can dolphins detect pain?
      </strong>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Several patients suffering from chronic pain report thatdolphins tend to swim close to them when they are in severe pain, and after a while their pain is gone. While skeptics hate this idea of dolphins detecting pain, one thing is sure-the dolphin experience has psychological benefits. The gentle movement of dolphins is a relaxing and consoling experience for someone in pain.
     </p>
     <h3 style="color: #f15b29;">
      <strong>
       Benefits of AAT using dolphins
      </strong>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <span>
       Dolphins can be incorporated into different treatment programs like pain management, addiction treatment and other rehabilitation procedures. If you enroll in an AAT program, ask the treatment provider to explain program goals and check if it matches your personal recovery goals.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span>
       The following are some examples of dolphin therapy benefits:
      </span>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <span>
        Swimming around dolphins
       </span>
       <span>
        improves fine motor skills.
       </span>
      </li>
      <li>
       <span>
        Swimming works the large muscles of your body, increases blood flow to the brain through contraction and relaxation of muscles and strengthens your stamina.
       </span>
      </li>
      <li>
       <span>
        It also builds muscle coordination and strengthens the arms and legs together.
       </span>
      </li>
      <li>
       <span>
        People on wheelchair should try swimming with dolphins to improve their wheelchair skills and avoid poor circulation due to prolonged sitting.
       </span>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      <span>
       Swimming also improves blood circulation. Poor circulation is oftentimes responsible for chronic pain. Remember that the circulatory system sends nutrients and oxygen through the blood throughout the body. Poor blood circulation often happens in arms and legs. As you swim, your circulation improves resulting to good brain oxygenation and fast absorption of nutrients.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Mental.
      </strong>
      <span>
       As you kick your arms and legs while swimming with dolphins, your brain function also improves. Your attention span and ability to finish tasks increase and your anxiety levels decrease.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       <span>
        Psychological.
       </span>
      </strong>
      <span>
       Dolphins have a way of making you feel good about yourself. AAT using dolphins reduces loneliness and anxiety. It helps you develop leisure activities like swimming and other water exercises.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span>
       Interactions with the dolphins helps develop social skills, boost self-esteem and relieve physical discomfort. Dolphin therapy helps you interact with others in your group or with the staff. It can also be a very motivational experience because of the relaxing environment and the comforting contacts with dolphins.
      </span>
     </p>
     <h3 style="color: #f15b29;">
      <strong>
       Is wild dolphin therapy safe?
      </strong>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Yes! Dolphin therapy is a personal treatment without side-effects. It deals with your chronic pain on all three levels-physical, mental and behavioral. And, what do you get? Relaxed mind, healthy body and highly improved social skills.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Guided interaction with wild dolphins is an experiential therapy that can encourage you to unravel the deep psychological issues related to your chronic pain. It is also advisable for people with addiction issues because it is a very effective form of psychotherapy that helps a person take responsibility for his (or her) actions.
     </p>
     <p>
      How many Americans need wild dolphin therapy? Aside from 161 million Americans with chronic pain (
