          March 2, 2015
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://elizabethscala.com/author/elizabeth/" itemprop="url" rel="author">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://elizabethscala.com/top-5-quotes-to-inspire-nurses/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Inspirational Quotes for Nurses #nursingfromwithin" class="alignleft wp-image-8161 size-medium" height="300" src="http://elizabethscala.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Stop-worrying-about-your-identity-and-200x300.png" title="Inspirational Quotes for Nurses #nursingfromwithin" width="200"/>
         </a>
         Today is a Monday. Which, for me, typically means writing a new blog post. Well, I had a very busy day two calls about the
         <a href="http://elizabethscala.com/landing/aon2/" target="_blank">
          Art of Nursing 2.0 program
         </a>
         with CNOs; two video interviews taped for the same. Lots of emails, social media sharing and reading of nurse blogs.
        </p>
        <p>
         I went to walk my dog and felt this pang of guilt over not having written my blog post- yet.
        </p>
        <p>
         When I returned from my walk, I looked over the mail as I walked upmy driveway. There was a pleasant surprise: a book gifted to me from another nurse author and keynote speaker. Connie Merritt sent me her book 
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002B5A1GK/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002B5A1GK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=elizscalnursf-20&amp;linkId=CHE7NPQJAGLIX4HX" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002B5A1GK/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002B5A1GK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=elizscalnursf-20&amp;linkId=CHE7NPQJAGLIX4HX');" target="_blank">
          <em>
           Too Busy for Your Own Good: Get More Done in Less Time- with Even More Energy
          </em>
         </a>
          and it was as though I was meant to receive itright in that very moment.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://elizabethscala.com/top-5-quotes-to-inspire-nurses/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Inspirational Quotes for Nurses #nursingfromwithin" class="alignright wp-image-8160 size-medium" height="300" src="http://elizabethscala.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Were-having-a-super-sale-in-this-shop-300x300.png" title="Inspirational Quotes for Nurses #nursingfromwithin" width="300"/>
         </a>
         I opened up the book and read the first chapter immediately, yet it was what was in the first few chapters that caught my attention immediately. Connie starts off the book describing how this busyness of life has become somewhat of a status symbol. How when we interact with others, we attempt to outdo them in our conversations with just how busy we are.
        </p>
        <p>
         And I did that very same thing- to myself- today. Comparing how much I got done versus how much I thought I ought to do. Judging just how much is enough to have a productive day. It was quote apparent to me that the truth really is:
         <em>
          <strong>
           Insanity: doing the same thing over and over again and expecting different results.  Albert Einstein
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://elizabethscala.com/top-5-quotes-to-inspire-nurses/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Inspirational Quotes for Nurses #nursingfromwithin" class="alignleft wp-image-8159 size-medium" height="300" src="http://elizabethscala.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/I-was-once-asked-why-I-dont-300x300.png" title="Inspirational Quotes for Nurses #nursingfromwithin" width="300"/>
         </a>
         So I decided to take a break. Take a break from coming up with blog content to share with you five of my favorite quotes I use for inspiration.Here you go
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are quotes for empowerment. Quotes for nursing inspiration. Quotes for nurse leaders to uplift, enjoy and reconnect their passion for their profession.
        </p>
        <p>
         You see everything comes down to the power of the mind. We can be in control of our greatest asset. We simply have to become aware of our thoughts and how very powerful they can become. Take control of your thoughts- live the life of your dreams. What if it couldbe that simple?
        </p>
