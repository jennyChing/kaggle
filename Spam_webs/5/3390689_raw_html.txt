    Ronald Kingsley Read

     <p>
      <b>
       Ronald Kingsley Read
      </b>
      (February 19, 1887 February 1975) was one of four contestants chosen to share the prize money for the design of the
      <a href="/wiki/Shavian_alphabet" title="Shavian alphabet">
       Shavian alphabet
      </a>
      , a completely new alphabet intended for writing English. He was later appointed sole responsible designer of the alphabet.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1966, after extensive testing of Shavian with English speakers from around the world, Read introduced
      <a href="/wiki/Quikscript" title="Quikscript">
       Quikscript
      </a>
      , a revised form of his Shavian alphabet. Quikscript, also known as the "Read alphabet", has more
      <a href="/wiki/Typographic_ligature" title="Typographic ligature">
       ligatures
      </a>
      than Shavian, which makes it easier to write by hand. Its appearance is more cursive than Shavian.
     </p>
     <p>
      A few days before his death, he completed a new alphabet called
      <i>
       Soundspell
      </i>
      (now
      <i>
       Readspel
      </i>
      ), based, probably for increased chances of popular acceptance, on the
      <a href="/wiki/Latin_script" title="Latin script">
       Latin script
      </a>
      .
     </p>
