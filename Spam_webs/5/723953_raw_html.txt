                          <a id="dd_start">

                          <h6>
                           With the help of Redditors, we picked 10 of the creepiest buildings in Los Angeles, and dug deep in their property reports to tell their real estate story.
                          </h6>
                          <p>
                           Rarely has there been a horror story without a brick and mortar location to serve as its background.
                           <br/>
                           And since theres such a strong connection between real estate and terrifying tales, we thought wed answer some of the questions on everybodys mind:
                          </p>
                          <ul>
                           <li>
                            Who owns these dilapidated buildings?
                           </li>
                           <li>
                            How much do creepy backstories slash off the price of a property?
                           </li>
                           <li>
                            Are there any building permits pulled out for them? Any activity at all?
                           </li>
                          </ul>
                          <p>
                           <span id="more-15286">
                           </span>
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           Well, lets take them one at a time:
                          </p>
                          <h3>
                           1.
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16342277">
                            The Linda Vista Hospital
                           </a>
                          </h3>
                          <p style="text-align: right; font-size: 75%; margin-bottom: 5px;">
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Linda-Vista-Hospital-the-Atlantic.com_.jpg">
                            <img alt="Linda Vista Hospital" class="aligncenter wp-image-15297" height="493" src="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Linda-Vista-Hospital-the-Atlantic.com_.jpg" style="margin-bottom: 5px;" width="743"/>
                           </a>
                           Image via
                           <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/health/archive/2012/08/linda-vista-the-ruins-of-an-abandoned-los-angeles-hospital/260592/" target="_blank">
                            The Atlantic
                           </a>
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Spooky because:
                           </strong>
                           Its been abandoned over two decades ago and reports of paranormal activities (mostly hauntings) have been literally pouring throughout the years  credited to the large number of gang shooting, stabbing, and beating victims that crossed the hospitals doors in the 70s and 80s.
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Real estate spooks:
                           </strong>
                           the numerous permits filed for the building show intensive work over at the former Linda Vista Hospital. The building permits section of the
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16342277" target="_blank">
                            property report
                           </a>
                           show a change of use from historic hospital to affordable senior housing; because nothing tells grandma you love her quite like a place in a popular ghosts hangout.
                          </p>
                          <h3>
                           2.
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16330105" target="_blank">
                            The Cecil Hotel
                           </a>
                          </h3>
                          <p style="text-align: right; font-size: 75%; margin-bottom: 5px;">
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Cecil-Hotel-2-the-Guardian.com_.jpg">
                            <img alt="Cecil Hotel, Los Angeles" class="aligncenter wp-image-15291" height="405" src="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Cecil-Hotel-2-the-Guardian.com_.jpg" style="margin-bottom: 5px;" width="675"/>
                           </a>
                           Image via
                           <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/world/2013/feb/21/body-found-hotel-cistern-water" target="_blank">
                            The Guardian
                           </a>
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Spooky because:
                           </strong>
                           Not too long ago a girl was found dead in some form of water tank at the Cecil. Residents there later realized that they were using and possibly drinking decomposing corpse water. Anyways, kind of a mystery because the girls death was ruled suicide, yet a video suggested that she may have been followed
                           <em>
                            .
                           </em>
                             via
                           <a href="http://www.reddit.com/r/LosAngeles/comments/2j4f98/spookiest_building_in_la/" target="_blank">
                            Reddit
                           </a>
                           .
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Real estate spooks:
                           </strong>
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16330105">
                            The Cecil Hotel
                           </a>
                           sold earlier this year for $15 million  thats $11 million short of what it was bought for back in 2007 (talk about a ROI horror tale!).
                          </p>
                          <h3>
                           3.
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16285153" target="_blank">
                            The Elegant Manor
                           </a>
                          </h3>
                          <p>
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/West-Adamss-Elegant-Manor.jpg">
                            <img alt="West Adams's Elegant Manor" class="aligncenter wp-image-15301" height="483" src="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/West-Adamss-Elegant-Manor.jpg" style="margin-bottom: 5px;" width="659"/>
                           </a>
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Spooky because:
                           </strong>
                           Since 1906, the 3115 W Adams Bldv manor served as home to pretty much everything from weddings to religious gatherings, AA meetings, and social events of all sorts. It wasnt until January 2004 when  according to the
                           <a href="http://articles.latimes.com/2004/nov/19/local/me-manor19/2" target="_blank">
                            LA Times
                           </a>
                            two teen siblings were shot and killed by gang members during a party on the premises, did the property become famous for its hauntings. Currently, this house is being used for the interactive haunted play Delusion. I was just there last Friday, said one of the Redditors, not only is the play fantastically scary, but this house is spooky as hell.
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Real estate spooks
                           </strong>
                           : With a troubled history of liens, the 3115 W Adams Blvd. home was scheduled for auction twice in 2006, and again in 2007. The
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16285153" target="_blank">
                            property report
                           </a>
                           also shows that the Elegant Manor is currently up for sale; with a $999,000 price tag, the house is being marketed primarily to architects, building restoration experts, and collectors.
                          </p>
                          <h3>
                           4.
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16219715">
                            The Weissmuller/ Nicolosi Estate
                           </a>
                          </h3>
                          <p style="text-align: right; font-size: 75%; margin-bottom: 5px;">
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Weissmuller-House.jpg">
                            <img alt="3.	The Weissmuller/ Nicolosi Estate" class="aligncenter wp-image-15300" height="494" src="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Weissmuller-House.jpg" style="margin-bottom: 5px;" width="658"/>
                           </a>
                           Image via
                           <a href="http://mymurdermysteryhomes.blogspot.ro/" target="_blank">
                            MyMurderMysteryHomes.com
                           </a>
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Spooky because:
                           </strong>
                           With a rich past of celebrity tenants  ranging from Tarzan-actor/five-time Olympic-gold-medalist Johnny Weissmuller to William Randolph Hearsts mistress, actress Marion Davies  the estate was completely gutted by a fire sometime in the late 80s. It is said that a previous tenant has set it on fire on Christmas Eve, fleeing and leaving his family inside.
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Real estate spooks:
                           </strong>
                           The building permits section of the
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16219715">
                            property report
                           </a>
                           shows that a Nicolosi still owns the property, and that she pulled out permits for fire damage repair and reconstruction of 3,597 square feet back in 1999. Yet the $1.2 million repairs did not move past the planning stage, and the last year when the property was altered is 1987.
                          </p>
                          <h3>
                           5.
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/mason/Property-Report/?propkey=16322100">
                            The Metropolitan Courthouse
                           </a>
                          </h3>
                          <p style="text-align: right; font-size: 75%; margin-bottom: 5px;">
                           <a href="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Metropolitan-courthouse.jpg">
                            <img alt="Metropolitan courthouse" class="aligncenter wp-image-15302" height="666" src="http://www.propertyshark.com/Real-Estate-Reports/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Metropolitan-courthouse.jpg" style="margin-bottom: 5px;" width="666"/>
                           </a>
                           Image via
                           <a href="https://foursquare.com/v/los-angeles-superior-metropolitan-courthouse/49ff904bf964a52031701fe3/photos" target="_blank">
                            Foursquare
                           </a>
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Spooky because:
                           </strong>
                           it looks like it was taken straight out of George Orwells 1984.
                          </p>
                          <p>
                           <strong>
                            Real estate spooks:
                           </strong>
