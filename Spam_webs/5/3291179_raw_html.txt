        <h1 class="title">
         The Art of Omri Koresh  Giving a Human Face to Mythical Characters
        </h1>
        <div class="meta">
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/tag/design/" rel="tag">
           design
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/tag/fantasy-art/" rel="tag">
           fantasy art
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/tag/illustration/" rel="tag">
           Illustration
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/tag/inspiration/" rel="tag">
           Inspiration
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="date">
          <span class="glyphicon glyphicon-calendar">
          </span>
          21.02.2015
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="body">
         <p>
          It is not an easy job to create believable portraits of fantastic characters from books and mythology. It is very difficult to make them universally accepted by people who each have a different mental image of their favorite characters from, say, the Bible, or childrens book.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, this difficulty did not stop illustrator Omri Koresh from trying, and our opinion is that he succeeded to put a face on famous mythical names.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Bible Characters Show Their Human Side
         </h2>
         <p>
          It is a hard job tackling biblical subjects. It is very easy to offend someone and to be labeled as a heretic (even these days). Omri Koreshs attempt to illustrate famous Bible scenes is very brave on its own. And it is also executed with great talent and feeling.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/omri-koresh-lots-grief.jpg">
           <img alt="omri-koresh-lots-grief" class="alignnone wp-image-2798" height="769" src="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/omri-koresh-lots-grief.jpg" width="618"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/omri-koresh-Jonahs-torment.jpg">
           <img alt="omri-koresh-Jonahs-torment" class="alignnone wp-image-2799" height="935" src="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/omri-koresh-Jonahs-torment.jpg" width="625"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The characters are extremely human and real, their emotions expressed with a lot of skill. The scenes are rendered in black and white and have a solemnity and depth in line with the theme.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Fantastic Scenes Come Alive
         </h2>
         <p>
          On the land of fantasy, Omri Koresh shows a great generosity in vibrant colors and surprising contrasts. The portraits are very rich in detail, almost similar to photo manipulations. Once again the great skill consists in making a perfect blend between the humanity of the characters and the outlandish quality of the landscape.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/002-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg">
           <img alt="002-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2800" height="720" src="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/002-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg" width="540"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/005-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg">
           <img alt="005-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2801" height="690" src="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/005-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg" width="540"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/008-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg">
           <img alt="008-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2802" height="421" src="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/008-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg" width="540"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          From the Gothic inspiration to fairytale characters, the artist has the ability to create a consistent impression of unity in creation  you can recognize his hand, his unique style no matter how different one image is from the other.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Alice in Wonderland
         </h2>
         <p>
          You cannot talk of the blend between fantasy and reality without taking a trip down the rabbit hole and into the quizzical world of Lewis Carrolls Alice in Wonderland. Alice and the Mad Hatter are timeless characters for fantastic scenes and Omri Koresh shows his own vision in a lot of illustrations. He has a Tim Burton-ish approach, without being too dark and gloomy. The characters are alive, their vitality concentrated in their eyes or grins.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/019-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg">
           <img alt="019-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2803" height="675" src="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/019-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg" width="540"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/017-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg">
           <img alt="017-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2804" height="339" src="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/017-fantastic-portraits-omri-koresh.jpg" width="540"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/omri-koresh-Vale-Of-Tears-s.jpg">
           <img alt=" Omri Koresh" class="alignnone wp-image-2805" height="401" src="http://vectorvice.com/Blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/omri-koresh-Vale-Of-Tears-s.jpg" width="712"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Approaching fantastic reality themes is tricky and can land a designer into the deep pit of ridicule. But Omri Koresh trusts his skills and he is right  he is up into our hall of fame for the best illustrators we know of.
         </p>
         <p>
