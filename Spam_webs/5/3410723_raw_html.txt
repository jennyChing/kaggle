        <h2 class="post-title">
         <a href="http://marketingtrw.com/blog/why-you-shouldnt-market-your-art-to-a-large-audience/" rel="bookmark" title="Why You Shouldnt Market Your Art to a Large Audience">
          Why You Shouldnt Market Your Art to a Large Audience
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="post-date">
          <a href="http://marketingtrw.com/blog/why-you-shouldnt-market-your-art-to-a-large-audience/" title="Why You Shouldnt Market Your Art to a Large Audience">
           May 17, 2014
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="date-sep">
          /
         </span>
         <span class="post-author">
          <a href="http://marketingtrw.com/blog/author/kevin/" rel="author" title="Posts by Kevin Chung">
           Kevin Chung
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="date-sep">
          /
         </span>
         <a href="http://marketingtrw.com/blog/why-you-shouldnt-market-your-art-to-a-large-audience/#comments" title="Comment on Why You Shouldnt Market Your Art to a Large Audience">
          21 Comments
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <!-- /post-header -->
       <div class="post-content">
        <h2>
         Looking for a large market to sell your art to? Then youre probably doing it wrong.
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span class="wp-caption-text">
          Main Photo by
          <a href="http://picjumbo.com/writing-on-the-macbook-pro-close-up/" target="_blank">
           Viktor Hanacek
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Have you ever thought to yourself Where can I sell my art to a lot of people? or How do I attract buyers when there are thousands of other artists selling the same thing as me? If you have, youre thinking about your art in the wrong way.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-91">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         The Problem with Art School
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_62">
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/problem-with-art-school.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by vancouverfilmschool" class="wp-image-62 size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/problem-with-art-school.jpg?resize=1000%2C667" title="The Problem with Art School"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="http://www.imcreator.com/free/education/summer-workshop" target="_blank">
           vancouverfilmschool
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The problem with art schools is, they will teach you the principles of great art and methods of creating great art, but they dont teach you where to sell your art or how to sell it. Your first thought may be to get your art in front of as many eyeballs as you possibly can. Ill just post my work on
         <a href="https://www.etsy.com/" target="_blank">
          Etsy
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.cafepress.com/" target="_blank">
          Cafepress
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.artfire.com/" target="_blank">
          Artfire
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.ebay.com/" target="_blank">
          eBay
         </a>
         , etc.and my work will sell itself. If you decide to go this route, you mightsee success, but you are also competing with thousands of other artists trying to do the exact same thing.
        </p>
        <h2>
         How You Should Approach Your Art Business
        </h2>
        <h3>
         Is There a Market For Your Art?
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_67">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/is-there-market-for-your-art.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by Ingrid Truemper" class="size-full wp-image-67" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/is-there-market-for-your-art.jpg?resize=1000%2C667" title="Is There a Market for Your Art?"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/46406260@N00/2538956362">
           Ingrid Truemper
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The first important thing to consider before you begin selling your art is whether or not people like it and are willing to buy it. The harsh reality is, no matter how much we may like our own artwork, unless someone else is willing to buy it, you are not going to sell it. The good news is, if youve found people who like your art and are willing to spend their hard earned money on it, there are others out there who will do the same.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Are You Competing on the Wrong Turf?
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_68">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/are-you-competing-wrong-turf.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by LoggaWiggler" class="size-full wp-image-68" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/are-you-competing-wrong-turf.jpg?resize=1000%2C750" title="Is there a market for your art?"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="http://pixabay.com/en/cathedral-square-human-group-51205/?oq=crowd">
           LoggaWiggler
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         If you are selling on a large marketplace like
         <a href="https://www.etsy.com/" target="_blank">
          Etsy
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://www.storenvy.com/" target="_blank">
          Storenvy
         </a>
         , you will likely see many items that are similar to yours. How do you stand out in this crowded marketplace? If you are relying solely on those sites, you will face some stiff competition and you will feel compelled to lower your prices just to compete. The answer to selling your art may lie outside of those sites.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Change Your Mindset
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_70">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/change-your-mindset.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by p_a_h" class="size-full wp-image-70" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/change-your-mindset.jpg?resize=1000%2C667" title="Change Your Mindset"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="http://www.fotopedia.com/items/flickr-6871526743" target="_blank">
           p_a_h
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The mindset of many artists is If I get this in front of enough people, it will start selling. When this strategy fails they begin to doubt themselves. They think Nobody wants my work, How can I compete with so many artists?, or Im not as good as everyone else. Instead of adapting this defeatist attitude, you need to change your mindset from Im not good enough to Let me find people who think I am MORE than GOOD ENOUGH.
        </p>
        <p>
         The founding executive editor of Wired magazine
         <a href="http://kk.org/" target="_blank">
          Kevin Kelly
         </a>
         developed the theory of
         <a href="http://kk.org/thetechnium/2008/03/1000-true-fans/" target="_blank">
          1,000 True Fans
         </a>
         , which makes a very compelling case that individual artists, producers, inventors, and makers need to acquire only 1,000 True Fans to make a living. These fans are defined as someone who will purchase anything and everything you make. Instead of seeking out a massive number of people to market to, you should be in direct contact with your true fans and they will be more than willing to buy from you.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Marketing Yourself
        </h2>
        <h3>
         Know Your Audience
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_71">
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/know-your-audience.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by D.H. Parks" class="size-full wp-image-71" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/know-your-audience.jpg?resize=1000%2C725" title="Know Your Audience"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="http://www.fotopedia.com/items/flickr-7411103744" target="_blank">
           D.H. Parks
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Before you try to market yourself, you must first know who to market to. The problem with many marketing strategies is they are not focused enough. When you ask most artists who their target customers are, most will respond Everyone of course. The problem with this thought process is, everyone does not want your product. In order to create a strong relationship with true fans, you must learn more about them.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Finding Your Audience
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_72">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/finding-your-audience.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by olarte.ollie " class="size-full wp-image-72" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/finding-your-audience.jpg?resize=1000%2C770" title="Finding Your Audience"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="http://www.fotopedia.com/items/flickr-7411103744" target="_blank">
           olarte.ollie
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The best way to find your audience is to see if there is a commonality among the customers you do have. Finding the commonalities amongst your customers will make it easier to connect with them and will allow you to create a more personal relationship with them.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Is there anything they share in common?
         </li>
         <li>
          Are they mostly men or women?
         </li>
         <li>
          Are they young or old
         </li>
         <li>
          Are the middle class or rich?
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Once youve figure out some commonalities, you should go to the places they hang out on-line. Some good places to check out may be
         <a href="http://www.reddit.com/" target="_blank">
          Reddit
         </a>
         , niche blogs, or forums. Also check out some influencers your target market might follow. The key here is to interact with your fans here. Let them know who you are and that you care. If you are active and helpful, you will have an easier time getting featured in these places!
        </p>
        <h3>
         How Do I Connect with My Audience?
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_74">
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/how-do-i-connect-with-audience.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by Sam Howzit" class="size-full wp-image-74" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/how-do-i-connect-with-audience.jpg?resize=1000%2C667" title="How Do I Connect with My Audience?"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/aloha75/7282703370" target="_blank">
           Sam Howzit
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         This step is key in creating a sustainable living as an artist. Once you have an audience, you have to connect with them on an emotional level. Find out what makes your artwork unique and share it with the world.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Do you drive around and find supplies for your work?
         </li>
         <li>
          Do you use only recycled materials?
         </li>
         <li>
          Do you repurpose old items and create something new with them?
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         All of these things can help you stand out in the sea of sameness that is the art marketplace. Once you figured out what makes your art unique, write about it. People love to hear what you have to think about your work. Share your thoughts, your workflow, your hopes, your fears, or anything else that helps your customers feel a connection with you. Your customers want to know the person behind the artwork. You are selling yourself as well as your artwork.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Sharing Your Work
        </h2>
        <h3>
         Why You Should Start a Blog
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_76">
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/why-you-should-start-blog.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by Beeldredacteur" class="size-full wp-image-76" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/why-you-should-start-blog.jpg?resize=1000%2C750" title="Why You Should Start a Blog"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="http://pixabay.com/en/typing-laptop-computer-296047/?oq=typing" target="_blank">
           Beeldredacteur
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         One of the easiest and most common ways to share your work ideas is through blogging. Blogging allows you to gather all your thoughts in one place and easily share your ideas and images. A good way to learn how to blog is by following your favorite artists. If there is an artist that you love, they are likely doing something right. Figure out how they are making those connections with their fans and incorporate some of their ideas into your blog. If you want a great example of an artist who does this well, take a look at
         <a href="http://austinkleon.com/" target="_blank">
          Austin Kleon
         </a>
         . He tells you how to
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0074QGGK6/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0074QGGK6&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=whdeit-20&amp;linkId=DR4RZJRMB56EJJCW" target="_blank">
          Steal Like An Artist
         </a>
         and also how to
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00GU2RGGI/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00GU2RGGI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=whdeit-20&amp;linkId=ETHMTUZAVT4YCR5R" target="_blank">
          Show Your Work!
         </a>
         He has created a huge base of true fans by making those connections and showing his work.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Newsletters and Why Theyre Important
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_77">
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/newsletters-and-why-theyre-important.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by 92five" class="size-full wp-image-77" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/newsletters-and-why-theyre-important.jpg?resize=1000%2C750" title="Newsletters and Why They're Important"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="http://92five.deviantart.com/art/Simple-Newsletter-Form-213375192" target="_blank">
           92five
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The e-mail newsletter is the best way for your fans to stay up to date with your work. If you are a huge fan of a company or artist, you know that irresistible urge to open every e-mail they send out to you. This is the same response that you want from your true fans. You will keep them up to date with your progress and they will be eagerly waiting the moment you post your next piece of art! The key to the newsletter is you want it to be highly visible on your blog and easy to sign up for.
        </p>
        <h3>
         What is the Best Social Media or Search Engine Optimization Strategy?
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_78">
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/best-social-media-search-engine-strategy.jpg">
          <img alt="Photo by kdonovangaddy" class="size-full wp-image-78" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/marketingtrw.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/best-social-media-search-engine-strategy.jpg?resize=1000%2C900" title="What is the Best Social Media or Search Engine Optimization Strategy?"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Photo by
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/kdonovan_gaddy/4314365065/in/photolist-8onC9R-br5x86-e5wZ3t-8d9dGt-9gXesF-6tXvwF-d41HES-8az8WH-7ew6Zc-e1yRKg-aFyhaH-e1HpQq-e5CAgW-8KkoYZ-99Wjs1-5XNfPs-dUmKE4-7zffqz-btpW68-amC4jN-6mYWTq-6u2DBs-dZxNRq-5XJ1Qc-aFy3bt-99BVQZ-9eVCSc-9MoWtb-epHEE2-9x7H6Z-8bspY4-6u2Dkq-f1qbGh-e4CDj3-7rY7do-6tXvgR-axnKy3-7YNkh5-71ZNv4-9yMPBV-8NyVNa-6qPE85-yv3t2-6qPE89-7oytoK-dU7Cn1-9FjwKu-6qPE8f-czBUG9-8KkxE4" target="_blank">
           kdonovangaddy
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         In the beginning, you may be tempted by the buzz words of Social Media and SEO. This is where people often become overwhelmed and start drowning in the sea of endless things to update and worry about. Youll often hear things like I just have to try harder and I dont have time to do anything else. These are a key indicator that you should focus, instead of being lured by those shiny features. For now, unless you know what you are doing, you need to worry more about creating your art and focusing on a single platform like your blog. Once youve built a sizable, engaged, and connected fan base, you can begin to think about optimizing your social media presence or search engine optimization.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Conclusion
        </h2>
        <p>
         If youve been stuck trying to market your product to large audiences and it hasnt been working, you should consider changing strategies. Why should you compete against thousands of other artists doing the exact same thing? I challenge you to change your strategy and seek those true fans.
        </p>

         Do you know other artists who are trying to market to everyone? Send them this link and help spread the word.
