       <div class="title">
        <h1>
         This DIY Marble-Dipped Vase is Fun and Easy to Make. Try it!
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="byline">
        <span>
         Faith-towers
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="body" id="body">
        <figure class="medium image">
         <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <img alt="Easy DIY Marble-Dipped Vase" class="lazyload" data-cfsrc="//images.curbly.com/h7SrMzS3rx4X__Qa9QODaQC2mYM=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2387/Marbled-vase-title_large_jpg.jpg" data-pinsrc="http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2387/Marbled-vase-title_large_jpg.jpg" data-src="//images.curbly.com/h7SrMzS3rx4X__Qa9QODaQC2mYM=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2387/Marbled-vase-title_large_jpg.jpg" src="//images.curbly.com/xTNHpdHg1G618xCwmp9onA6ji8w=/980x0/smart/filters:strip_icc():blur(2):quality(10)/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2387/Marbled-vase-title_large_jpg.jpg" width="980"/>
          <meta content="//images.curbly.com/h7SrMzS3rx4X__Qa9QODaQC2mYM=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2387/Marbled-vase-title_large_jpg.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
         </span>
         <figcaption>
          <a href="http://www.curbly.com/users/faith-towers/posts">
           Faith Towers
          </a>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I have about a million plain glass vases. And a ton of old nail polish. If you browse Pinterest long enough, you're bound to runinto a million different marbled glass tutorials using nail polish (like this beauty Rachel did for
         <a href="http://www.poppytalk.com/2014/09/diy-marble-dipped-vase.html">
          Poppytalk
         </a>
         )... so I decided to give it a go!Click through to find out how to do this super easy project.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pagebreak">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <figure class="medium image">
         <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <img alt="Easy DIY Marble-Dipped Vase" class="lazyload" data-cfsrc="//images.curbly.com/saLMwIZ8741eAdgfz7qdT0U_918=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2388/DSC_7829e_large_jpg.jpg" data-pinsrc="http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2388/DSC_7829e_large_jpg.jpg" data-src="//images.curbly.com/saLMwIZ8741eAdgfz7qdT0U_918=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2388/DSC_7829e_large_jpg.jpg" src="//images.curbly.com/gFDsHMxfDY9JYkRmr3_EgkdOr2o=/980x0/smart/filters:strip_icc():blur(2):quality(10)/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2388/DSC_7829e_large_jpg.jpg" width="980"/>
          <meta content="//images.curbly.com/saLMwIZ8741eAdgfz7qdT0U_918=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2388/DSC_7829e_large_jpg.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
         </span>
         <figcaption>
          <a href="http://www.curbly.com/users/faith-towers/posts">
           Faith Towers
          </a>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I liked the idea of leaving some of the clear glass visible, so I decided to tape off the top section. Feel free to tape as much of the glass off as you like, but just make sure that the water in the bowl is deep enough to dip the whole untaped portion. Here are the materials that you'll need for this project:
        </p>
        <p>
         - Multiple nail polish colors
         <br/>
         - A clear glass vase
         <br/>
         - A toothpick
         <br/>
         - Painter's tape
         <br/>
         - A bowl of water
         <span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <figure class="medium image">
         <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <img alt="Easy DIY Marble-Dipped Vase" class="lazyload" data-cfsrc="//images.curbly.com/A1t7j_1m4dlwYHeqKl9oK76BepA=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2389/Marbled-collage1_large_jpg.jpg" data-pinsrc="http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2389/Marbled-collage1_large_jpg.jpg" data-src="//images.curbly.com/A1t7j_1m4dlwYHeqKl9oK76BepA=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2389/Marbled-collage1_large_jpg.jpg" src="//images.curbly.com/1ImAY-sS6wDxzgZYhn-enLl2ba4=/980x0/smart/filters:strip_icc():blur(2):quality(10)/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2389/Marbled-collage1_large_jpg.jpg" width="980"/>
          <meta content="//images.curbly.com/A1t7j_1m4dlwYHeqKl9oK76BepA=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2389/Marbled-collage1_large_jpg.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
         </span>
        </figure>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         1.
         <span>
          Begin by sectioning off your vase using painters tape.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         2.
         <span>
          Fill a bowl with room temperature water. Choose two or three different nail polish colors, and pour them slowly onto the surface of the water. Some of the polish will sink to the bottom, and thats okay.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         3.Working quickly now, use a toothpick to swirl the polish into a marbled pattern.
        </p>
        <figure class="medium image">
         <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <img alt="Easy DIY Marble-Dipped Vase" class="lazyload" data-cfsrc="//images.curbly.com/3Azp4qcNqIehJ2l4zRblH4wa9Pw=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2390/Marbled-collage2_large_jpg.jpg" data-pinsrc="http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2390/Marbled-collage2_large_jpg.jpg" data-src="//images.curbly.com/3Azp4qcNqIehJ2l4zRblH4wa9Pw=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2390/Marbled-collage2_large_jpg.jpg" src="//images.curbly.com/eupbvHB3TYSL-NUyuLEmPzwESWo=/980x0/smart/filters:strip_icc():blur(2):quality(10)/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2390/Marbled-collage2_large_jpg.jpg" width="980"/>
          <meta content="//images.curbly.com/3Azp4qcNqIehJ2l4zRblH4wa9Pw=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2390/Marbled-collage2_large_jpg.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
         </span>
        </figure>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         4.Carefully dip your vase into the marbled water, ensuring that you dip far enough so that the polish overlaps onto the painters tape.
        </p>
        <p>
         5.
         <span>
          Remove it very slowly.
         </span>
        </p>
        <figure class="medium image">
         <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <img alt="Easy DIY Marble-Dipped Vase" class="lazyload" data-cfsrc="//images.curbly.com/1sMMkfWgccQQGCcFV5VJcxNSW8w=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2391/Marbled-collage3_large_jpg.jpg" data-pinsrc="http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2391/Marbled-collage3_large_jpg.jpg" data-src="//images.curbly.com/1sMMkfWgccQQGCcFV5VJcxNSW8w=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2391/Marbled-collage3_large_jpg.jpg" src="//images.curbly.com/hkNy6qY0Mm0hkkUgrg3LM3dPTH0=/980x0/smart/filters:strip_icc():blur(2):quality(10)/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2391/Marbled-collage3_large_jpg.jpg" width="980"/>
          <meta content="//images.curbly.com/1sMMkfWgccQQGCcFV5VJcxNSW8w=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2391/Marbled-collage3_large_jpg.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
         </span>
        </figure>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         6.Place it upside down to dry.
         <span>
          The great thing about this project is that the polish can be removed from the vase using nail polish remover if you are unhappy with your initial attempt.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         7.
         <span>
          Once youre satisfied with your vase, let the polish dry thoroughly and then remove the painters tape. And that's it! Now you have a chic vase perfect for a bouquet of roses.
         </span>
        </p>
        <figure class="medium image">
         <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
          <img alt="Easy DIY Marble-Dipped Vase" class="lazyload" data-cfsrc="//images.curbly.com/iTDogr0jgIJmWGHzGQINXeql18U=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2392/DSC_7834e_large_jpg.jpg" data-pinsrc="http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2392/DSC_7834e_large_jpg.jpg" data-src="//images.curbly.com/iTDogr0jgIJmWGHzGQINXeql18U=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2392/DSC_7834e_large_jpg.jpg" src="//images.curbly.com/0A3wNC5PsLl3Syj3EAmrg0tuM3I=/980x0/smart/filters:strip_icc():blur(2):quality(10)/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2392/DSC_7834e_large_jpg.jpg" width="980"/>
          <meta content="//images.curbly.com/iTDogr0jgIJmWGHzGQINXeql18U=/980x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0017/2392/DSC_7834e_large_jpg.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
         </span>
        </figure>
