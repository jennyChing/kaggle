      <p class="byline">
       by
       <span class="author vcard">

      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        They say that on your wedding day, you will look the best youve ever looked in your whole life. It makes sense since many brides spend months prepping for the big day with routine facials, hair upkeep and, of course, a personal trainer. I could have never said this about myself at the time but on my wedding day, I had the sickest body. It cost a bit of money and took several very early mornings in the gym but I did it.
       </p>
       <p>
        While I havent reverted back to the pre-wedding bod entirely, I havent been the best about hotbod maintenance. Despite thinking my
        <a href="http://xcelfitness.net/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://xcelfitness.net/']);">
         personal trainer
        </a>
        is HANDS DOWN the best in DC, Im not in a position to maintain that expense. So its up to me. [insert hopeless sour face]
       </p>
       <p>
        Its starting to hit me that our first anniversary vaycay is right around the corner. Since my poor poor husband spent our ENTIRE honeymoon in a neck brace (due to a tragic dive-gone-wrong), weve vowed to count our blessings and make our 1st anniversary trip our honeymoon, take II. Which means, I need to get back into the program and I have 6 weeks to do it! Ive been taking this class at my gym called Tabata which is awesome because it guides me I feel like without direction, I stand in the gym floundering. But more than direction right now, I need motivation!! Heres a few things helping me to get there.
       </p>
