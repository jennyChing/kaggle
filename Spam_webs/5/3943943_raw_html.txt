          <h1>
           <strong>
            See It Instead
            <em>
             : I, Frankenstein
            </em>
           </strong>
          </h1>
          <p>
           Sometimes a
           <em>
            movie
           </em>
           comes along and makes you aware of an itch you never knew you had. Perhaps a
           <em>
            <a href="http://deluxevideoonline.org/category/movie-reviews/">
             review
            </a>
           </em>
           piqued your interest, or youd rather stay in and pay yourself $10 for a small popcorn and watch a
           <em>
            movie
           </em>
           on the cheap. Perhaps youre valiantly struggling through your queue on
           <em>
            Netflix
           </em>
           or
           <em>
            Amazon Prime
           </em>
           , and need a wise, cultured voice to direct you to where the real
           <em>
            movie
           </em>
           viewing gold is hiding amidst the terrible Zombie Stripper movies
           <em>
           </em>
           . Well, look no further.
           <a href="http://deluxevideoonline.org/category/see-it-instead/">
            <strong>
             See It Instead
            </strong>
           </a>
           is here to take todays
           <em>
            new releases
           </em>
           and guide you to what you should really be watching.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2273" style="width: 211px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Zombie_strippers.jpg">
            <img alt="Zombie Strippers movie starring robert englund and jenna jamesom  See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class="size-medium wp-image-2273 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Zombie_strippers.jpg?resize=201%2C300" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            So I guess theres no retirement plan for Porn Stars, eh Jenna?
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <em>
             I, Frankenstein
            </em>
            (2014)
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Recently re-animated this week at a cinema of dubious taste near you is
           <em>
            I, Frankenstein
           </em>
           . The latest attempt to strike some of the
           <em>
            Sin City
           </em>
           gold,
           <em>
            I, Frankenstein
           </em>
           is adapted from a graphic novel youve probably never read, into a movie you probably shouldnt see. After luring his creator into the frozen north, Victor Frankensteins creation is attacked by demons and then rescued by gargoyles, because fuck it, weve run out of ideas. At least good ones. Despite featuring the talented Aaron Eckhart, this ghoulish creation smells like its past its expiration date.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Serious Pick
            <em>
             :
             <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0791217/" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
              Mary Shelley
             </a>
             s Frankenstein
            </em>
            (1994)
           </strong>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_2274" style="width: 210px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Mary_shelleys_frankenstein_ver2.jpg">
            <img alt="Mary Shelley's Frankenstein (1994)  film, See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class="size-medium wp-image-2274 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Mary_shelleys_frankenstein_ver2.jpg?resize=200%2C300" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Looks complicated, but its a great bed for back troubles.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           During the mid 90s,
           <a href="http://www.rottentomatoes.com/celebrity/kenneth_branagh" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
            Kenneth Branagh
           </a>
           took a break from creating a film version of literally everything Shakespeare ever wrote in order to create the definitive adaptation of Mary Shelleys famous monster parable. Heck, he even included her name in the title, so you know hes sticking close to the source material. Or read it once between takes on Sonnet 67, The Movie.
          </p>
          <p>
           Starring Branagh as Victor Frankenstein, this film tells the tale of a talented (if not ethically challenged) young doctor who charges recklessly forward during the hey-day of scientific advancement, hoping to use the recently harnessed power of electricity and galvanism in order to cure the greatest disease haunting mankind: death. His efforts are eventually successful, but the brash scientist is unready for the consequences of having created new life, especially as he discovers that his new creation is not a superman, but essentially a child in a hideously cobbled body. He repents of his endeavor and abandons the creature (played by Robert De Niro), but the creature does not die. In fact, the creature learns to read and speakand to envy. Denied any social interaction, the monster confronts Victor on the eve of his wedding, demanding that Victor create him a helpmate so that he will not have to live in solitude. Victor balks, and two become relentless enemies.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2275" style="width: 310px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/DeNiroFrankenstein4.jpg">
            <img alt="Robert Deniro mary shelley's Frankentein 1994 film, See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class="size-medium wp-image-2275 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/DeNiroFrankenstein4.jpg?resize=300%2C162" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            OK, looks good, Robert. But next time wear the makeup we gave you. Zing!
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2276" style="width: 225px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/kenneth-b-frankenstein.jpeg">
            <img alt="Mary Shelley's frankenstein 1994 film, See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class=" wp-image-2276 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/kenneth-b-frankenstein.jpeg?resize=215%2C196" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Well, melodramatic and a bit vain to be honest
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <em>
            Mary Shelleys Frankenstein
           </em>
           is gorgeous and atmospheric, and attains a wonderful sense of period that highlights the struggles of science versus human frailty. The acting is top notch, though those who are not fans of Branaghs style may find his portrayal to be a bit melodramatic. A box narrative breaks up the frantic action of the finale, but
           <em>
            Frankenstein
           </em>
           was never meant to be an action film per se. For those hoping for a faithful introduction to the material, Branaghs
           <em>
            Frankenstein
           </em>
           is the best entry point outside of reading the classic book.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Lighthearted Pick:
            <em>
             <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0072431/" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
              Young Frankenstein
             </a>
            </em>
            (1974)
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Ladies and Gentlemen, Madames et Monsieurs, may I present for your intellectual and philosophical pleasure this cultured, sophisticatedman about town. Hit it!
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2277" style="width: 250px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i1.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/genewilder.jpg">
            <img alt="young Frankenstein 1974, Mel Brooks  film, See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class="size-full wp-image-2277 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/genewilder.jpg?resize=240%2C300" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Super Duper!
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Consistently rated one of the funniest comedies of all time, and arguably Mel Brooks finest creation,
           <em>
            Young Frankenstein
           </em>
           skewers the original while remaining largely a faithful retelling of the original. This tale follows the talented (if not sexually challenged) young doctor who attempts to harness the power of his grandfathers legacy to cure people of mispronouncing his name. Frahnkensteen. Its Frahnkensteen.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i1.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/gene.jpg">
            <img alt="young Frankenstein 1974, gene wilder, Mel Brooks  film, See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-2278" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/gene.jpg?resize=150%2C150"/>
           </a>
           Having received the will of the late Victor Frankenstein, young Frederick (Gene Wilder at his manic best) returns to Transylvania, and with the help of
           <a href="http://www.rottentomatoes.com/celebrity/marty_feldman" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
            Marty Feldman
           </a>
           (as the demented Igor) and Teri Garr (as his devoted assistant Inga), they manage to recreate the seminal experiment and unleash a new creature upon the world. Peter Boyle shines as the monster, and great cameos are made by Madeline Kahn, Cloris Leachman, and Gene Hackman.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2279" style="width: 310px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/cast-young-frank.jpg">
            <img alt="young Frankenstein 1974, gene wilder, Mel Brooks  film, See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class=" wp-image-2279 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/cast-young-frank.jpg?resize=300%2C179"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Seda-give!?!
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2280" style="width: 206px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i1.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Adventure_of_sherlock_holmes_smarter_brother_xlg_movie_poster.jpg">
            <img alt="Sherlock Holmes Smarter Brother starring Gene Wilder, Madeline Kahn, Marty Feldman " class="size-medium wp-image-2280 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Adventure_of_sherlock_holmes_smarter_brother_xlg_movie_poster.jpg?resize=196%2C300" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Worth a peek.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           If you havent seen this gem, its about high time you had a riot. If you have seen it, why not watch it again, and then reunite with the cast for
           <em>
            <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0072608/" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
             The Adventure of Sherlock Holmes Smarter Brother
            </a>
            .
           </em>
           ..the twin feature that the studio roped the stars into making. Its not great, but it is another chance to appreciate the amazing chemistry Wilder, Feldman and Kahn created on Frankenstein.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_2281" style="width: 310px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/young-frank-monster.jpg">
            <img alt="young Frankenstein 1974, gene wilder, Mel Brooks  film, See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class="size-medium wp-image-2281 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/young-frank-monster.jpg?resize=300%2C225" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Was it good for you?
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Unconventional Pick:
            <em>
             <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0059205/" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
              Frankenstein Conquers the World
             </a>
            </em>
            (1965)
           </strong>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_2282" style="width: 136px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i1.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/frank-conquer.jpg">
            <img alt="Frankenstein Conquers the World (1965) See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class=" wp-image-2282 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/frank-conquer.jpg?resize=126%2C180" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            With a subtitle like that, you know its going to be good!
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Another epic offering from those mad geniuses at Toho studios, famous for their work on the
           <em>
            Godzilla
           </em>
           series of monster-fests. To nobodys surprise, this movie is at heart a monster-fest. The film starts with some mumbo jumbo about Nazis bringing the heart of the Frankensteins monster to Hiroshima to conduct secret experiments on, before it is bombed straight to hell by the allied forces. Years pass, and wouldnt you know it, that heart has become attached to a feral boy who is now growing like a weed thanks to the radiation and botched Nazi chicanery. Is there anything you cant blame on the Nazis?
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2283" style="width: 218px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/fanta.jpg">
            <img alt="Fanta Can " class=" wp-image-2283 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/fanta.jpg?resize=208%2C243"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Nope, Nazis made this, too.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           The local military police are alarmed by attacks on civilians, and blame the monstrous child. The creature is detained, and it is determined that he is impervious to radiation and growing at an alarming rate. So they get alarmed. And chain him up. Sounds like science to me.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2284" style="width: 310px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/frank-science.jpg">
            <img alt="Frankenstein Conquers the World (1965) See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class="size-medium wp-image-2284 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/frank-science.jpg?resize=300%2C128" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Pictured: science.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Meanwhile (theres always a meanwhile in these films) the attacks have continued, and the scientists discover another monster, Baragon, has been behind the destruction all along. Oops. Baragon is a pretty vanilla Toho monster, but is still tougher than the model tanks and pop guns the military sends his way. So whats a terrified populace to do? Unleash Frank and let him Siberian suplex that mofo back into the stone age, thats what!
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2285" style="width: 310px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/frank-love.jpg">
            <img alt="Frankenstein Conquers the World (1965) See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class=" wp-image-2285 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/frank-love.jpg?resize=300%2C168" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            No! I love YOU more!
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2286" style="width: 275px">
           <a data-rel="prettyPhoto[image-2272]" href="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/frank.jpg">
            <img alt="Frankenstein Conquers the World (1965) See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein" class="size-full wp-image-2286 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/deluxevideoonline.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/frank.jpg?resize=265%2C190" title="See It Instead:  I, Frankenstein"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            I would seriously advise you to say no to this face.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Utterly camp-tastic,
           <em>
            Frankenstein Conquers the World
           </em>
           managed to even garner a sequel, because really, who could say no to this face?
          </p>
