                   September 29, 2014
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Bold Beets #raw #juice #recipe
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7230049046173378585" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     Lacking fiber in your diet? Did you know that beets and apples are packed with it? I used to use beets while making baby food for help with tummy troubles, and used them and apples alot throughout the second half of my pregnancy to keep my body running right!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-W58RU0B-4s4/UxTKkBeSxlI/AAAAAAAAIK4/zGXs1W5WAJI/s1600/Beet+juice.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-W58RU0B-4s4/UxTKkBeSxlI/AAAAAAAAIK4/zGXs1W5WAJI/s1600/Beet+juice.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     2 apples (cored)
                     <br/>
                     1 beet
                     <br/>
                     1 large handful baby carrots
                     <br/>
                     1 handful beet greens
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     First and foremost, you'll want to wash your beet and beet greens really well; they can be really gritty and have that
                     <i>
                      fresh from the garden
                     </i>
                     taste if you don't! Once you've washed your beet, cut off the rind. Core your apples and you're all set.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Push your beet greens through, followed by your beet, carrots and apples. This recipe will yield about 12 oz. of fresh juice.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     If the taste is a little strong for you (it was for me), a squirt of fresh lemon will do the trick to brighten it up!
