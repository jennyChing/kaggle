        Flip Clock Screen Saver - Mac and Windows
       </h1>
       <div class="entryviewheadersub">
        <span class="hiddentext">
         Published
        </span>
        Tuesday, August 23, 2011 11:49 AM
       </div>
       <div align="center">
        <img alt="" src="http://dl.getdropbox.com/u/1986906/webimages/9031%20%E2%80%BA%20Goodies.jpg"/>
        <br/>
       </div>
       <br/>
       I don't know what it is about those vintage flip clocks but I love them. I think they are so cool so when I found this screen saver I had to have it. The screen saver is such an unnecessary feature of todays computers that it's almost comical.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       The first screen savers were meant to prevent phosphor burn-in on the old CRT monitors. If images stayed on your screen for too long you would continue to see ghosting of the image even after it had left the screen. This is why many screen savers featured blank screens or moving images to prevent any portion of the screen from getting an image burned in.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Today they are simply esthetic and sometime used for security, requiring a password to remove the screen saver before returning to the desktop. This flip clock screen saver is pretty cool and works with Mac and Windows. It's free so go and check it out.
