          A Mix of Thai Chili Pepper Sauces

        <p>
         Today, Im sharing a few
         <em>
         </em>
         <strong>
          Thai Chili Pepper Sauce
         </strong>
         recipes made with Thai Birds Eye Chili Peppers for this weeks
         <a href="http://blog.foodnetwork.com/fn-dish/2012/10/stuff-it-with-spinach-fall-fest/">
          Food Network Fall Fest
         </a>
         event where Peppers are being featured
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10253" style="width: 650px">
         <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html/thai-bird-chilies" rel="attachment wp-att-10253">
          <img alt="Thai Bird's Eye Chilies" class="size-full wp-image-10253 pib-hover-img" data-pin-description="Thai Birds Eye Chili Pepper Sauce Recipes ~ http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com" data-pin-url="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html" height="515" src="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Thai-Bird-Chilies.jpg" title="Thai Bird's Eye Chilies" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Thai Birds Eye Chilies
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         I have a way of doing a deep dive into cuisines when Im trying to learn about the food behind different cultures. It becomes almost like an obsession  it probably is an obsession  but its the best way for me to really get a good feel for the flavors and ingredients in the recipes for a particular country.
        </p>
        <p>
         The last few weeks, I have been obsessed with Thai cuisine. I learned how to make Pad Thai about ten years ago, but have only tried to make a few other Thai dishes over the years. Like all new cuisines Ive come across, I found the ingredients to be a bit intimidating at first.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kaffir lime leaves, lemongrass, red curry paste, green curry paste, galangal, palm sugar, fish sauce, tamarind, and Thai birds eye chilies are some of the ingredients Ive become more familiar with over the years. Although not quite as intimidating as Indian cuisine, there was definitely a learning curve when I first started trying Thai recipes, e.g., how to cook dried rice noodles (versus fresh rice noodles that I was accustomed to), how to make tamarind pulp,
         <a href="http://chezpim.com/pantry/palm_sugar">
          how to use the disks of palm sugar
         </a>
         , how to crush lemongrass, what the difference is between all the curry pastes (not at all like any other curry Id come across).
        </p>
        <p>
         Something that has intrigued me recently is Thai chili sauce. Our local Asian market carries bags of Thai bird chili peppers and Ive always wondered what people do with all these chilies. You cant possible use them all up in any short period of time, or can you? Ive made chili vinegar pickles with them before from the Momofuku cookbook as a way of preserving all the extra chilies I didnt use, but had never tried making any chili sauce out of them. It occurred to me that Sriracha Sauce, the popular condiment that is now used as a seasoning in anything from burgers to spaghetti sauce, just might be a common condiment used in Thailand, albeit it might not be in the same form or formula as it has taken on now that it is manufactured commercially.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10257" style="width: 650px">
         <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html/sriracha-sauce" rel="attachment wp-att-10257">
          <img alt="Homemade Sriracha Sauce" class="size-full wp-image-10257 pib-hover-img" data-pin-description="Thai Birds Eye Chili Pepper Sauce Recipes ~ http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com" data-pin-url="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html" height="516" src="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Sriracha-Sauce.jpg" title="Homemade Sriracha Sauce" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Homemade Sriracha Sauce
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         There are recipes floating around for making Sriracha Sauce at home, which I thought of trying, but then, I decided to see what authentic Thai chili sauces were really all about. Since I cant travel to Thailand right now, my only source was to scour Thai cookbooks and the internet. Interestingly, theres not a lot of information on this topic. A few images showed an assortment of chili sauces, dried chilies and sugar in a set of condiment jars at Thai noodle shops. From what I can gather, these spicy condiments are used to jazz up noodle dishes and rice dishes at local food joints.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10258" style="width: 650px">
         <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html/rice-noodles-with-sriracha-sauce" rel="attachment wp-att-10258">
          <img alt="Fresh Rice Noodles with Sriracha Sauce" class="size-full wp-image-10258 pib-hover-img" data-pin-description="Thai Birds Eye Chili Pepper Sauce Recipes ~ http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com" data-pin-url="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html" height="553" src="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Rice-Noodles-with-Sriracha-Sauce.jpg" title="Fresh Rice Noodles with Sriracha Sauce" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Fresh Rice Noodles with Sriracha Sauce
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Four different chili sauces caught my eye  each one used chili slices mixed with a different sauce. One was chilies with vinegar (white or coconut), another was chilies with fish sauce (some add garlic, vinegar and sugar), another was chilies with sweet soy sauce, and yet another resembled Sriracha Sauce (fermented chilies with vinegar, sugar and salt). So, I chopped up my bag of chilies and split them up into four bowls. The first three sauces were ready to be used as soon as they were mixed. The Sriracha Sauce needed to ferment on the counter top for a few days. I did not write down the recipe for the Sriracha Sauce, so its pictured here, but Ill have to post the recipe for it later (I have another batch fermenting on my counter).
        </p>
        <p>
         Caution: Be sure to wear gloves when handling hot chile peppers!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10145" style="width: 650px">
         <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html/thai-chili-pepper-sauce-and-pad-thai-2" rel="attachment wp-att-10145">
          <img alt="Thai Chili Pepper Sauces and Pad Thai" class="size-full wp-image-10145 pib-hover-img" data-pin-description="Thai Birds Eye Chili Pepper Sauce Recipes ~ http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com" data-pin-url="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html" height="991" src="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Thai-Chili-Pepper-Sauce-and-Pad-Thai1.jpg" title="Thai Chili Pepper Sauces and Pad Thai" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Thai Chili Pepper Sauces and Pad Thai
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         To test all these sauces out, I made
         <strong>
          Pad Thai with Chicken, Shrimp and Pressed Tofu
         </strong>
         and let the kids go at it with the different Thai Chili Sauces I had made (except the Srirachi Sauce which was still fermenting). Although they preferred the
         <strong>
          Thai Chili Sauce with Vinegar
         </strong>
         and the
         <strong>
          Thai Chili Sauce with Sweet Soy Sauce
         </strong>
         , I liked drizzling a little bit of each Chili Sauce on top to add a little sour, salty, sweet and spicy flavor. So good. In fact, Ive been drizzling these sauces on fried rice, hard boiled eggs, omelettes, and just about anything I can think of.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10254" style="width: 650px">
         <a href="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html/thai-chili-fish-sauce-w-fried-rice" rel="attachment wp-att-10254">
          <img alt="Thai Chili Fish Sauce with Thai Fried Rice" class="size-full wp-image-10254 pib-hover-img" data-pin-description="Thai Birds Eye Chili Pepper Sauce Recipes ~ http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com" data-pin-url="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html" height="352" src="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Thai-Chili-Fish-Sauce-w-Fried-Rice.jpg" title="Thai Chili Fish Sauce with Thai Fried Rice" width="640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Thai Chili Fish Sauce with Thai Fried Rice
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The ratio Id seen used seemed to be roughly 1 part chilies to 1 part liquid (fish sauce, vinegar, sweet soy sauce). Its really a matter of personal preference I think.
        </p>

          Thai Bird Chili Pepper Sauce Recipes

          <img class="pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/2012/10/thai-chili-pepper-sauces.html" itemprop="image" src="http://jeanetteshealthyliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Thai-Chili-Pepper-Sauces.jpg" width="205"/>

         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          Be sure to wear gloves when handling hot chile peppers. I loved playing around with these simple chili pepper sauces - drizzle on Pad Thai, fried rice, omelettes, just about anything for some heat. These chili peppers are very hot, so just use the sauce (don't eat the chili peppers) if you want the flavor, but not the heat.
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Chili Vinegar Sauce
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon minced Thai bird chili peppers
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup coconut or white vinegar
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Chili Fish Sauce
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon minced Thai bird chili peppers
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup fish sauce
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Chili Sweet Soy Sauce
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon minced Thai bird chili peppers
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup kecap manis
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Directions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place chili peppers in three small bowls. To each bowl, add the vinegar, fish sauce and kecap manis. Keep in the refrigerator.
           </li>
          </ol>
