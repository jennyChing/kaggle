          <p>
           An explosion ripped out an exterior wall at Echo Bay Sports Complex early Friday.
          </p>
          <p>
           No one was injured in a blast that shook homes near the Bay Street site. The complex was vacant, said Const. Monique Baker. Cause of the blast that caused extensive damage is unknown. The incident was reported to OPP at 5 a.m.
          </p>
          <p>
           An Ontario Fire Marshal investigator arrived at the scene early Friday afternoon.
          </p>
          <p>
           OPP are assisting, but not investigating the incident.
          </p>
          <p>
           There's no foul play suspected at this time, said Baker. We'll see where the investigation (by OFM) goes.
          </p>
          <p>
           The explosion may have happened in a Zamboni room, said Lynne Duguay, clerk-administrator of Macdonald, Meredith and Aberdeen Additional Township.
          </p>
          <p>
           The east wall is basically completely gone, she said. Duguay is hopeful the building can be repaired and used again.
          </p>
          <p>
           We've got our fingers crossed that the structure is going to be fine, she told The Sault Star Friday morning.
          </p>
          <p>
           Echo Bay mayor Lynn Watson said he felt the explosion in his home, about one kilometre from the arena. Volunteer firefighters who live on top of Findlay Hill, nearly three kilometres away, also felt it, he added.
          </p>
          <p>
           Watson said it was unbelievably fortunate the blast occurred when the facility was not in use and no one was present.
          </p>
          <p>
           All things seem to be that if it's going to happen, it couldn't have happened in a better situation, or at a better time, he said.
          </p>
          <p>
           The ice resurfacing machine was still on fire a bit when Watson arrived on the scene shortly after 5 a.m. He said the propane tank on the machine was still intact.
          </p>
          <p>
           The explosion woke up Patti Kastikainen and her husband.
          </p>
          <p>
           We heard the boom, said the owner of Lucy Loos on Highway 17B in Echo Bay.
          </p>
          <p>
           Her home is beside the restaurant, about 250 metres from the complex.
          </p>
          <p>
           I initially thought is was something in my house because it was that strong, she said.
          </p>
          <p>
           There did not appear to be any damage in the restaurant or her home, Kastikainen said Friday morning.
          </p>
          <p>
           The seasonal complex includes a canteen, change rooms, washrooms, electrical and Zamboni rooms and a natural ice surface. The venue, which draws users from Echo Bay, Laird and Sault Ste. Marie, was enjoying a banner year thanks to cold temperatures to keep the ice in good condition.
          </p>
          <p>
           An estimated 500 people use the gathering place for the community each week to skate and play hockey.
          </p>
          <p>
           The ice was last used Thursday night for an hour-long shinny game for ages 15 and up that ended at 10 p.m. A caretaker would have closed the complex by 11 p.m., said Duguay.
          </p>
          <p>
           This is probably one of the best years we've had in regard to the number of rentals when the arena opened, she said. The ice is in amazing shape. Mother Nature has been kind that way.
          </p>
          <p>
           The mayor knocked on Duguay's home door at 5 a.m. to alert her to the explosion.
          </p>
          <p>
           The complex typically opens in early January and closes in late March after the school break.
          </p>
          <p>
           Union Gas, Algoma Power and Echo Bay and Townships Fire Department responded to the explosion.
          </p>
