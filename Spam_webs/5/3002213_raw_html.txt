            How to Make the Cutest DIY Emergency Car Kit
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="meta">
           <span class="meta-cats">

          <p>
           <em>

          <p>

          <p>
           You know what is one of my greatest fears? (Besides a zombie apocalypse, that is): getting stranded in my car!!! As crafty and savvy as I am with a paint brush, you can count me
           <em>
            out
           </em>
           in the DIY Auto Ninja category. I know
           <em>
            nothing
           </em>
           about cars.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ohwait. I
           <em>
            do
           </em>
           know how to change a tire (woo hoo!). Aside from that, Im useless.
          </p>
          <p>
           I stick to our vehiclesmaintenance schedule, along with getting routine oil changes. Helpful,but we need more protection and preparation for when things go wrong.
          </p>
          <p>
           This winter has been pretty mild so far, but weve started getting bad weather, and its getting colder. Im always worried about getting stranded in the winter. So I decided to put my craft skillz to use and make the cutest little DIY emergency kit
           <em>
            juuuuust
           </em>
           in case.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/How-to-Make-the-Cutest-DIY-Emergency-Kit-from-Thrift-DIving-Blog.jpg">
             <img alt="How to Make the Cutest DIY Emergency Kit from Thrift DIving Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10009" height="1024" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/How-to-Make-the-Cutest-DIY-Emergency-Kit-from-Thrift-DIving-Blog-685x1024.jpg" width="685"/>
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Last week I stopped by
           <a href="http://www.dropshopandoil.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.dropshopandoil.com', 'Walmart']);" rel="nofollow">
            Walmart
           </a>
           to get an oil change and to pick up some items to put in the emergency car kit. First, let me just say,
           <em>
            I had no idea that some Walmarts do oil changes
           </em>
           . Why should I be surprised?? Ive heard that Walmart sells
           <em>
            caskets
           </em>
           , for goodness sakes! If anything, I should be surprised if Walmart
           <em>
            didnt
           </em>
           sell something! Oil changes are no exception. (Thats my ride right there, parked in front of the Automotive Care Center!)
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="IMG_3241" class="aligncenter wp-image-9994 size-large" height="1024" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/IMG_3241-767x1024.jpg" width="767"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I had the option of getting the Pennzoil High Mileage Vehicle oil change, which was perfect for my car (my trusty Accord is still pushing it at 126,000 miles!). The prices were actually pretty affordable and didntkill my budget! Depending on what oil or additional services you get done will determine the price. Mine came to about $46.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="IMG_3243" class="aligncenter wp-image-9995 size-large" height="1024" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/IMG_3243-767x1024.jpg" width="767"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           They offer the Conventional Motor Oil and Platinum, but since my car is over 75,000 miles, it made sense to go with the better Pennzoil oil change. It helps clean out the sludge that lesser oils leave behind. Also, higher mile vehicles like my Honda need oil that helps to reduce leaks and keep the engine clean and strong for a long time. Lord knows Mama has to keep this Honda functioning because getting another car isnt an option :).
          </p>
          <p>
           (Its also important to mention that Pennzoil offers a free warranty that covers 15 engine parts that may fail due to engine wear or abnormal wear due to friction. You can find out more info about that
           <a href="http://pennzoilwarranty.com/login/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://pennzoilwarranty.com/login/', 'HERE']);" rel="nofollow">
            HERE
           </a>
           ).
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="IMG_3250" class="aligncenter wp-image-9998 size-large" height="1024" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/IMG_3250-767x1024.jpg" width="767"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I was happy that I could shop for items I needed for myDIY emergency car kit while I waited. You know, the whole kill two birds with one stone kind of thing. In fact, I took so long that
           <em>
            they
           </em>
           were done with my car before
           <em>
            I
           </em>
           was done.
           <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I already had the metal box that I picked up from the thrift store for about $5.00 for holding all my emergency items. (You know how I
           <em>
            love
           </em>
           metal boxes,
           <a href="http://www.thriftdiving.com/make-a-diy-love-box-freedom-to-be-spontaneous/" title="Make a DIY Love Box: Freedom to be Spontaneous">
            like this other one
           </a>
           ). I picked up this one many months ago (last year??)and just stashed it in my craft room for the perfect project (now!!). It was in excellent condition, but Ill paint anything if Ive got the materials. And this metal container is not the exception.
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: center;">
           BEFORE
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="DSC_2073" class="aligncenter wp-image-9990 size-large" height="1024" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_2073-682x1024.jpg" width="682"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           And although Im not a girly-girl, I do love me some pink. Why not add polka dots, too? I spray painted it and added some black chalk paintpolka dots.
          </p>
          <p>
           Hm, I dont knowit might be too
           <em>
            pretty
           </em>
           to put in the dirty trunk of mycar!
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           AFTER!
          </h1>
          <p>
           <img alt="DSC_2128" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10002" height="1024" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_2128-682x1024.jpg" width="682"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I hate the smell of spray paint, but it was the perfect color for what I wanted. And I already had it on hand and didnt have to buy more materials.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="DSC_2074" class="aligncenter wp-image-9991 size-large" height="1024" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_2074-682x1024.jpg" width="682"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Just be sure when you use spray paint and other chemicals that you wear a full face mask. Youd be amazed how you smell
           <em>
            nothing
           </em>
           when youre wearing this thing!
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="DSC_2078" class="aligncenter wp-image-9992 size-full" height="533" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_2078.jpg" width="800"/>
          </p>
          <h2>
           What To Includein a DIY Emergency Kit
          </h2>
          <p>
           I sorta of
           <em>
            did
           </em>
           think about the zombie apocalypse when I decided to buy a siphon pump. Dont you remember all those episodes where Rick and his crew ran out of gas?! Just in case we ever have such an emergency, you better believewell be prepared. HA!
          </p>
          <p>
           I also think that including emergency supplies thatdont use batteries is key. So I picked up a crank flashlight, too.
          </p>
          <p>
           Toss in some gloves, small flashlights (for the kids), and some bandages and gauze, and were in business.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_2102.jpg">
            <img alt="DSC_2102" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10000" height="533" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_2102.jpg" width="800"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           I already had some jumper cables so I didnt buy another pair. I put my old pair inside the emergency car kit, too.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="DSC_2134" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10004" height="1024" src="http://www.thriftdiving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_2134-696x1024.jpg" width="696"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           And heres the coolest feature: painting an emergency I.C.E (In Case of Emergency) number on the back! My mother-in-law is the best person to list, just in case hubby and I are together and theres a super huge emergency.
          </p>

           (Of course I wont give you here phone number, so I blurred that part out).
