       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Save a Peppermint Stick for Old Saint Nick
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2006-12-09T03:34:38+00:00">
         December 9, 2006
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://www.culinaryconcoctionsbypeabody.com/author/peabody/" rel="author">
           Peabody
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://www.culinaryconcoctionsbypeabody.com/2006/12/09/save-a-peppermint-stick-for-old-saint-nick/#comments">
          31 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://images.ccbypea.com/candycanecheesecake2.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The tree is up, the stockings are hung and cookies have been a baking. Im official in the holiday mood, it just took a little longer this year is all. I turn once again to the beloved candy cane. In my previous life in Phoenix, when I was a school teacher, I actually grew quite sick of candy canes. Every year kids would give me Christmas cards and every year there would be a candy cane attached to it. When you teach junior high and you teach a little over 100 kidsthat adds up to a lot of candy canes. But now that I have been out of teaching for a few years my love for peppermint candy canes has been growing back againjust like a lizards tail. I still cant put them on the treethis year I think ours are jolly rancher flavored, but I have brought them back into my baking and into my hot chocolate(yum!).
        </p>
        <p>
         I was violently killing candy canes with a mallet for another idea that I had. It went bad and I had to do somehting else. My friend A, or my goat as I like to call her, has a hubby who thinks he should get cheesecake from me for his own personal cheesecake of the month club.and so far it has worked out that way. I head over there tomorrow for a cookie exchange so I thought why not bring him a cheesecake? This is not exactly your typical candy cane cheesecake and you might be scared off a tad by the orange juice, but it isnt really noticable and it brings out the mint flavor nicely.
        </p>
        <p>
         So if you are really wanting something good from Santa this year, maybe you should leave a piece of this out instead of a cookie or a peppermint stick.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://images.ccbypea.com/candycanecheesecake3.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Candy Cane Cheesecake
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ingredients:
         <br/>
         Crust
         <br/>
         2 cups oreo cookie crumbs
         <br/>
         1/2 cup crushed candy canes
         <br/>
         1/4 cup sugar
         <br/>
         1/4 cup butter, melted
         <br/>
         Cheesecake
         <br/>
         1 1/2 lb. cream cheese
         <br/>
         1 1/2 tbsp. peppermint schnapps
         <br/>
         1/2 tbsp. orange juice
         <br/>
         1/2 tbsp. vanilla extract
         <br/>
         1/2 cup heavy cream
         <br/>
         1 cup sugar
         <br/>
         4 eggs
         <br/>
         2 tbsp. sour cream
         <br/>
         1/4 cup half and half
         <br/>
         1 cup crushed candy canes
         <br/>
         Instructions:
         <br/>
         Crust
        </p>
        <p>
         Combine all ingredients and mix well.
        </p>
        <p>
         Butter two 8 inch cake (spring form) pans.
        </p>
        <p>
         Divide crumb mixture between the two pans, making 1/2 inch crust.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cheesecake
        </p>
        <p>
         Beat cream cheese, schnapps, concentrate, vanilla, cream, and sugar together until light and fluffy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Add eggs, one at a time, beating after each addition.
        </p>
        <p>
         Add remaining ingredients and beat well until blended.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fill both prepared pans with cheesecake batter. Place pans in water bath of boiling water and bake at 325 degrees for 1 1/2 hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cool on rack and chill overnight.
        </p>
        <p>
         Source: Chef Eddie Matney
        </p>
