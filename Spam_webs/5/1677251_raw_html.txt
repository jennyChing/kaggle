        <h1 class="title">
         My 30 Best Travel Tips After 4 Years Traveling The World
        </h1>
        <div class="meta clearfix">
         By
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/author/matthewkarsten/" rel="author" title="Posts by Matthew Karsten">
          Matthew Karsten
         </a>
         in
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/popular/" rel="category tag">
          Popular
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/travel-tips/" rel="category tag">
          Travel Tips
         </a>
         |
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/best-travel-tips/#comments">
          279 Comments
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="Favorite Travel Tips" src="https://expertvagabond.com/wp-content/uploads/best-travel-tips-photo.jpg" title="My Best Tips for World Travel"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          My Best Tips for World Travel
         </p>
        </div>
        <h5>
         Travel Tips
        </h5>
        <p class="lede">
         Its now been 4 years since I sold everything and left the United States to travel the world. These are the best travel tips Ive discovered along the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         It all started when I took a one-way flight from Miami to Guatemala City, leaping nervously into the unknown and leaving much of my old life behind while
         <a href="https://expertvagabond.com/about/">
          embarking on an epic travel adventure around the world
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Its been a wild ride, and Ive learned a lot since I first left. To celebrate my 4 year travelversary, Ive decided to share a collection of my best and most useful travel tips to help inspire you to make travel a priority in your life.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Feel free to share your own best travel tips at the end!
         </em>
        </p>
        <h2>
         1. Patience Is Important
        </h2>
        <p>
         Dont sweat the stuff you cant control. Life is much too short to be angry &amp; annoyed all the time. Did you miss your bus? No worries, there will be another one. ATMs out of money? Great! Take an unplanned road trip over to the next town and explore. Sometimes freakouts happen regardless.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Just take a deep breath and remind yourself that it could be worse.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2>
         2. Wake Up Early
        </h2>
        <p>
         Rise at sunrise to have the best attractions all to yourself while avoiding crowds. Its also a magical time for photos due to soft diffused light, and usually easier to interact with locals. Sketchy areas are less dangerous in the morning too. Honest hardworking people wake up early; touts, scammers, and criminals sleep in.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="Favorite Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Oceania/Fiji/i-QSXpBMf/7/900x900/skydive-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Laugh at Yourself"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Laugh at Yourself
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         3. Laugh At Yourself
        </h2>
        <p>
         You will
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/skydiving-fiji/">
          definitely look like a fool
         </a>
         many times when traveling to new places. Rather than get embarrassed, laugh at yourself. Dont be afraid to screw up, and dont take life so seriously.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once a whole bus full of Guatemalans laughed with glee when I forced our driver to stop so I could urgently pee on the side of the road. Returning to the bus and laughing with them gave me new friends for the remainder of the journey.
        </p>
        <h2>
         4. Stash Extra Cash
        </h2>
        <p>
         Cash is king around the world. To cover your ass in an emergency, make sure to stash some in a few different places. I recommend at least a couple hundred dollars worth. If you lose your wallet, your card stops working, or the ATMs run out of money, youll be glad you did.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some of my favorite stash spots include socks, under shoe inserts, a toiletry bag, around the frame of a backpack, even sewn behind a patch on your bag. Oh, and make sure you have a
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/travel-banking-101/">
          good travel banking system
         </a>
         setup too.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Africa/South-Africa/i-DSwqvT7/3/900x900/khula-township-family-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Meet Local People"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Meet Local People
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         5. Meet Local People
        </h2>
        <p>
         Make it a point to avoid other travelers from time to time and start conversations with local people. Basic English is spoken widely all over the world, so its easier to communicate with them than you might think, especially when you combine hand gestures and body language. Learn from those who live in the country youre visiting.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          People enrich your travels more than sights do.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2>
         6. Pack A Scarf
        </h2>
        <p>
         I happen to use a
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/shemagh-keffiyeh/">
          shemagh
         </a>
         , but
         <a href="http://amzn.to/1N10CuZ" target="_blank">
          sarongs
         </a>
         work great too. This simple piece of cotton cloth is one of my most useful travel accessories with many different practical applications. Its great for sun protection, a makeshift towel, carrying stuff around, an eye mask, and much more.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Central-America/Nicaragua/i-WXRVWV6/4/900x900/granada-buildings-blue-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Observe Daily Life"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Observe Daily Life
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         7. Observe Daily Life
        </h2>
        <p>
         If you really want to get a feel for the pulse of a place, I recommend spending a few hours sitting in a park or on a busy street corner by yourself just watching day to day life happen in front of you.
        </p>
        <p>
         Slow down your thoughts and pay close attention to the details around you. The smells, the colors, human interactions, and sounds. Its a kind of meditation  and youll see stuff you never noticed before.
        </p>
        <h2>
         8. Back Everything Up
        </h2>
        <p>
         When my
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/tracking-stolen-laptop/">
          laptop computer was stolen in Panama
         </a>
         , having most of my important documents and photos backed up saved my ass. Keep both digital and physical copies of your passport, visas, drivers license, birth certificate, health insurance card, serial numbers, and important phone numbers ready to go in case of an emergency.
        </p>
        <p>
         Backup your files &amp; photos on an external hard drive as well as online with software like
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/go/backblaze" target="_blank">
          Backblaze
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Central-America/Guatemala/i-8sWdS5n/1/900x900/volcano-santiaguito-exploding-eruption-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Take Lots of Photos"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Take Lots of Photos
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         9. Take Lots Of Photos
        </h2>
        <p>
         You may only see these places &amp; meet these people once in your lifetime. Remember them forever with plenty of photos. Dont worry about looking like a tourist. Are you traveling to look cool? No one cares. Great photos are the ultimate souvenir.
        </p>
        <p>
         They dont cost anything, theyre easy to share with others, and they dont take up space in your luggage. Just remember once you have your shot to get out from behind the lens and
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/iceland-photography/" target="_blank">
          enjoy the view
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h2>
         10. Theres Always A Way
        </h2>
        <p>
         Nothing is impossible. If you are having trouble going somewhere or doing something, dont give up. You just havent found the best solution or met the right person yet. Dont listen to those who say it cant be done.
        </p>
        <p>
         Perseverance pays off. I cant tell you how many times Ive been told what I want isnt possible, only to prove it wrong later when I dont listen to the advice and try anyway.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Central-America/Nicaragua/i-jbftbFf/1/900x900/leon-local-girls-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Smile &amp; Say Hello"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Smile &amp; Say Hello
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         11. Smile &amp; Say Hello
        </h2>
        <p>
         Having trouble interacting with locals? Do people seem unfriendly? Maybe its your body language. One of my best travel tips is to make eye contact and smile as you walk by. If they smile back, say hello in the local language too. This is a fast way to make new friends.
        </p>
        <p>
         You cant expect everyone to just walk around with a big stupid grin on their face. Thats your job. Usually all it takes is for you to initiate contact and theyll open up.
        </p>
        <h2>
         12. Splurge A Bit
        </h2>
        <p>
         Im a huge fan of budget travel, as it lets you travel longer and actually experience more of the fascinating world we live in rather than waste money on stuff you dont need. You can
         <a href="http://www.nomadicmatt.com/travel-blogs/five-destinations-under-30-dollars-per-day/" target="_blank">
          travel many places for $30 a day
         </a>
         with no problems.
        </p>
        <p>
         That said, living on a shoestring gets old after a while. Its nice (and healthy) to go over your budget occasionally. Book a few days at a nice hotel, eat out at a fancy restaurant, or spend a wild night on the town.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Central-America/Nicaragua/i-6FNdDLz/3/900x900/telica-cock-fighting-ring-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Keep an Open Mind"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Keep an Open Mind
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         13. Keep An Open Mind
        </h2>
        <p>
         Dont judge the lifestyles of others if
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/central-american-cockfighting/" target="_blank">
          different from your own
         </a>
         . Listen to opinions you dont agree with. Its arrogant to assume your views are correct and other people are wrong. Practice empathy and put yourself in someone elses shoes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Embrace different possibilities, opportunities, people, suggestions and interests. Ask questions. You dont have to agree, but you may be surprised what youll learn.
        </p>
        <h2>
         14. Try Couchsurfing
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.couchsurfing.org/" target="_blank">
          Couchsurfing.org
         </a>
         is a large online community of travelers who share their spare rooms or couches with strangers for free. If you truly want to experience a country and its people, staying with a local is the way to go.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are millions of couchsurfers around the world willing to host you and provide recommendations. Its fun and safe too. Expensive hotels are not the only option, there are all kinds of
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/cheap-travel-accommodation/">
          cheap travel accommodation options
         </a>
         out there.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Central-America/Guatemala/i-vzRg4BR/1/900x900/san-pedro-charity-work-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Volunteer Occasionally"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Volunteer Occasionally
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         15. Volunteer Occasionally
        </h2>
        <p>
         Make it a point to
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/build-poop-oven/">
          volunteer some of your time
         </a>
         for worthwhile projects when traveling. Not only is it a very rewarding experience, but youll often learn more about the country and its people while also making new friends.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a great site called
         <a href="http://grassrootsvolunteering.org/" target="_blank">
          Grassroots Volunteering
         </a>
         where you can search for highly recommended volunteer opportunities around the world.
        </p>
        <h2>
         16. Pack Ear Plugs
        </h2>
        <p>
         This should actually be #1 on the list. I love my earplugs! Muffle the sounds of crying babies, drunk Australians, barking dogs, honking horns, dormitory sex, natural gas salesmen, and more. A travelers best friend.
         <a href="http://amzn.to/1blKzMy" target="_blank">
          These are my favorite earplugs
         </a>
         for comfort &amp; effectiveness.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/North-America/Mexico/Veracruz/i-J5zR28R/1/900x900/veracruz-marine-soldiers-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Don't Be Afraid"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Dont Be Afraid
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         17. Dont Be Afraid
        </h2>
        <p>
         The world is not nearly as dangerous as the media makes it out to be. Keep an eye out for sketchy situations but dont let that be the focus of your whole trip. Use common sense and youll be ok. Most people are friendly, trustworthy, generous, and willing to help you out.
        </p>
        <p>
         This goes for women too. I realize Im not a woman, but Ive met plenty of
         <a href="http://www.ottsworld.com/blogs/going-it-alone/" target="_blank">
          experienced
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.legalnomads.com/2013/02/female-solo-travel.html" target="_blank">
          female
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.bemytravelmuse.com/solo-female-travel/" target="_blank">
          travelers
         </a>
         who agree.
        </p>
        <h2>
         18. Get Lost On Purpose
        </h2>
        <p>
         If you want to see the parts of town where real people live &amp; work, you need to go visit them. The best way to do this is on foot  without knowing where youre going. Write down the name of your hotel so you can catch a taxi back if needed, then just pick a direction and start walking.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont worry too much about stumbling into dangerous neighborhoods either, as locals will generally warn you before you get that far.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Asia/Thailand/i-wD8cc64/0/900x900/bangkok-khaosan-scorpion-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Eat Local Food"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Eat Local Food
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         19. Eat Local Food
        </h2>
        <p>
         Think you already know what Mexican food tastes like?
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/mexican-food-yucatan/">
          Youre probably wrong
         </a>
         . Taste a bit of everything when you travel, especially if you dont know what it is. Ask local people for recommendations. Eat street food from vendors with big lines out front.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been very sick only twice in my travels. Dont be scared of the food.
        </p>
        <h2>
         20. Say Yes Often
        </h2>
        <p>
         Be impulsive and say yes when someone randomly invites you to meet their family, try a new activity, or explore a place you didnt know existed. Its these unexpected and unplanned situations that add spice to your travels and always turn into the best stories later.
        </p>
        <p>
         Accept the kindness of strangers when you travel  youll have plenty of opportunities to do so.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Central-America/Guatemala/i-DFM4hB3/5/900x900/lake-atitlan-guide-pedro-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Slow Down"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Slow Down
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         21. Slow Down
        </h2>
        <p>
         Please dont try to cram 6 countries into 6 weeks of travel. All the good stuff happens when you really take the time to explore. Youll learn about activities that arent in your guidebook and meet people who are eager to show you around.
        </p>
        <p>
         I can honestly say that NONE of my
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/best/">
          best travel experiences
         </a>
         happened within the first few days of arriving somewhere. Spend more time in fewer places for maximum enjoyment.
        </p>
        <h2>
         22. Keep Good Notes
        </h2>
        <p>
         My memory for details sucks. When I first started traveling the world 4 years ago, I didnt keep a good journal, and now Im regretting it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Information like the names of people I met, conversations I had, feelings about a new experience, or what a particular town smelled like. If you ever want to write about your travels, these details are handy.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Asia/Thailand/i-5bbmKCc/3/900x900/sak-yant-traditional-tattoo-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Break Out of Your Comfort Zone"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Break Out of Your Comfort Zone
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         23. Break Out Of Your Comfort Zone
        </h2>
        <p>
         Challenge yourself to
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/sak-yant-tattoo/">
          try things that normally give you anxiety
         </a>
         . The more you do this, the more that anxiety will fade away. Not a hiker? Go on more hikes. Have trouble talking to strangers? Talk to everyone. Scared of weird food? Eat the weirdest thing you can find.
        </p>
        <p>
         The reason this works so well while traveling is because everything is already so different, whats one more new/uncomfortable experience?
        </p>
        <h2>
         24. Dont Plan Too Much
        </h2>
        <p>
         I cringe when readers ask how many days they should spend in a particular country or city. The truth is I have no idea what youll enjoy or who youll meet. I thought Id rocket through Nicaragua in a week or two, but ended up living there for 4 months.
        </p>
        <p>
         My advice is to pick a starting point, 1 or 2 must-do activities, and an ending point (or not). Then just
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/hitchhiking-america/">
          let the universe determine the rest
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/photos/i-btp5szr/0/900x900/i-btp5szr-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Pack Less Stuff"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Pack Less Stuff
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         25. Pack Less Stuff
        </h2>
        <p>
         You dont need 1/2 the gear you think you do to travel anywhere. Weve all done it. Its a right of passage for travelers to slowly become better at packing less. My first backpack was 70 liters packed full, my current bag is only 38 liters.
        </p>
        <p>
         As a full-time vagabond,
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/travel-gear-guide/">
          everything I own fits on my back
         </a>
         . If youre not sure about packing something, you dont need it. Its also possible to buy most things at your destination country if you discover you need them.
        </p>
        <h2>
         26. Listen To Podcasts
        </h2>
        <p>
         Podcasts are awesome. Its like creating your own personal radio station and filling it with shows and music you always want to listen to. I never thought Id actually look forward to a 10 hour bus ride. But with podcasts, its possible (well, as long as the seats are comfortable).
        </p>
        <p>
         Time will fly by as you listen to incredible storytelling, fun music, or interviews with experts. Here are some of my favorites:
         <a href="http://www.thisamericanlife.org/podcast" target="_blank">
          This American Life
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://themoth.org/about/programs/the-moth-podcast" target="_blank">
          The Moth
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://risk-show.com/" target="_blank">
          RISK!
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.radiolab.org/series/podcasts/" target="_blank">
          Radiolab
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.smartpassiveincome.com/category/podcast/" target="_blank">
          Smart Passive Income
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://electro-swing.com/" target="_blank">
          Electro-Swing
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/North-America/Mexico/Chihuahua/i-svtVPbm/1/900x900/tarahumara-running-matt-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Exercise More"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Treat Your Body Well
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         27. Treat Your Body Well
        </h2>
        <p>
         Travel can throw your body out of whack. When youre moving from place to place its difficult to maintain a workout routine, and many of us slack off. Or we dont sleep enough. Or we
         <a href="http://www.everywhereist.com/new-york-cupcakes-a-descent-into-madness/" target="_blank">
          eat too many cupcakes
         </a>
         . Im guilty of not flossing my teeth.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remember to be nice to your body. Get enough sleep, stay hydrated, eat healthy, use sunscreen, and exercise often (
         <a href="http://www.nerdfitness.com/blog/2009/12/09/beginner-body-weight-workout-burn-fat-build-muscle/" target="_blank">
          check out this bodyweight routine
         </a>
         , no gym required!). And, yes, flossing too I guess.
        </p>
        <h2>
         28. Stay In Touch
        </h2>
        <p>
         Remember to call your family &amp; friends from time to time. Maybe surprise them and go old-school by sending a postcard (its in the mail, Mom!). Travel isnt lonely, far from it. You constantly meet other people. But many of those relationships are fleeting. So maintaining a strong connection with the people who know you best is important.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 910px">
         <img alt="My Best Travel Tips" src="http://travelphotos.expertvagabond.com/Central-America/Panama/i-j8R3GB4/8/900x900/darien-river-piragua-900x900.jpg" title="Travel Tip: Get Off the Beaten Path"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Travel Tip: Get Off the Beaten Path
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         29. Get Off The Beaten Path
        </h2>
        <p>
         I know its clich, but you should still attempt it. Seek out
         <a href="http://expertvagabond.com/hiking-darien-national-park/">
          interesting and unusual places
         </a>
         that dont see much tourism. Many memorable travel experiences have happened to me in areas that are not easy to visit. By all means travel to popular sites, but dont rule out other locations just because theyre not on the tourist trail.
        </p>
        <p>
         Although please realize that just because an area is remote or dangerous doesnt necessarily mean youll have a life-changing experience.
        </p>
        <h2>
         30. Travel More
        </h2>
        <p>
         If theres one thing Ive noticed over the past 4 years, its that many people back home love to tell me how lucky I am while making excuses why they cant travel. Its too expensive. They cant get time off work. Who will feed their pets?
        </p>
        <p>
         When I suggest solutions to these problems, they still dont take action. Why? Because theyre often hiding behind the true reason: theyre scared.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Unfortunately most people who wait to travel the world never do.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         You dont need to sell all your worldly possessions and become a homeless vagabond like me. Just get out there more than you do now. Start with a weekend in a different state. Then maybe try a week in the country next door.
        </p>
        <p>
         The new car, remodeling project, and iPhone can wait. If you truly want to travel more, you can make it happen. Career breaks are possible. You have friends who would love to watch your pets.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its a big, beautiful, exciting, and fascinating world out there. Explore some of it now, rather than later. 
        </p>

                   <p>
                    Mariana: Im a 28 year old female who has been traveling nonstop for nearly 3 years. More than 2 of those years have been solo! I completely agree with this listcouldnt have said it better myself! I have to respond to your assumption that its easier for a man. I actually disagree with this. Ive travelled alone through places like Colombia, Mexico, El Salvador, Honduras, Cambodia, among others, and have never had a major issue. Ive found nothing but kind, helpful people along the way and havent had trouble meeting people to travel with whenever I felt uneasy being alone. Hostels are amazing for easily meeting travel buddies! Just be friendly and open.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    In some ways its easier. Youd be surprised how many people/strangers look after you when youre a solo female. Also, as a woman, Id say we have the advantage of having an excellent natural intuition. We tend to be a bit more cautious and listen to our inner voices better than the average dude. This goes far when galavanting around the world and can keep you out of many compromising situations.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Also, there are loads of blogs (including my own) these days featuring the voices of solo female travelers. If youre looking for confidence, check them out!
