          <h1 class="title">
           <a href="/blog/2014/6/go-starbucks">
            Go Starbucks
           </a>
          </h1>
          <h2 class="date-author">
           <time datetime="2014-07-02">
            July  2, 2014
           </time>
           <a class="author" href="/blog/?author=52912966e4b05a7f92e56216">
            NVR Guys
           </a>
          </h2>
          <!--MAIN CONTENT-->
          <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1403275524859" id="item-53a446f2e4b0bd08dbb133c1">
           <div class="row sqs-row">
            <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
             <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-edf7946832941f41aa6d">
              <div class="sqs-block-content">
               <p>
                We recently wrote about how pleased we are with Seattle's decision to implement a plan that gruffly
                <a href="/blog/2014/6/employment">
                 increases the city's minimum wage to $15.
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                For us, it's all about bulking up the middle class before it disappears completely.
               </p>
               <p>
                A college education  almost prohibitively expensive for those who need it most  is probably the best path to a better life. College graduates can pretty much expect to have double the lifetime earnings of those with only a high school education. Plus, kids of college educated parents are more tun twice as likely to attend college themselves.
               </p>
               <p>
                Those are great statistics.
               </p>
               <p>
                Today, we applaud
                <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/06/16/us/starbucks-to-provide-free-college-education-to-thousands-of-workers.html?_r=0" target="_blank">
                 the decision that Starbucks made to provide a college education to any employee that works 20+ hours a week
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                Starbucks has a reputation among those in our field as being a solidly employee-friendly place to work. This decision bumps them up to an entirely new level.
               </p>
               <p>
                With that, we're gonna walk down to our local Starbucks and feel pretty good about forking over nearly five bucks for an Oprah Chai.
               </p>
               <p>
                <em>
                 One more week (until 7 July) --&gt; Please
                 <a href="/best-advice">
                  pop in your best advice over on this page
                 </a>
                 , and we'll donate $2. Thanks!
                </em>
               </p>
              </div>
