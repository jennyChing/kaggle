        <h1 class="entry-title">
         My Blogger Burn Out
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         It comes and goes like waves on a shore. The tide will rise so far up and then gets discouraged when sessions are only half of what they were the week before. Sound familiar?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burnout.jpg">
          <img alt="burnout" class="aligncenter wp-image-2266" height="600" src="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burnout.jpg" width="398"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Burn out. Ive noticed the weeks that I am the least motivated to put effort into my blog come days after I hit a new high in my analytics. Hitting a high number and then having to work overtime, scrape, and push harder to try to match that number makes me withdraw. But when did it become all about the numbers?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burnout11.jpg">
          <img alt="burnout11" class="aligncenter wp-image-2267" height="600" src="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burnout11.jpg" width="289"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I work in marketing and I blog because from the first grade, when I discovered that my 8th grade math would be taught by a REALLY scary nun, I retreated from numbers and into works of fiction, fashion, and social studies. Then, when I got into my life, like social media analytics, page views, personal finance, and online sales that involve percentages, I came back to numbers  hard. But numbers dont make great, inspired writing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im going to have to change the way I think about my blog as a business. Here are some thoughts:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burnout1-copy.jpg">
          <img alt="burnout1 copy" class="aligncenter wp-image-2262" height="399" src="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burnout1-copy.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burn-out-2-.jpeg">
          <img alt="burn out 2" class="aligncenter wp-image-2263" height="400" src="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burn-out-2-.jpeg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burnout-3-copy.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-2265" height="443" src="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/burnout-3-copy.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether you blog or are self-employed, how do you stick to your roots?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/alexandra.png">
          <img alt="signature" class="alignleft wp-image-85 size-full" height="32" src="http://www.littlewildheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/alexandra.png" width="135"/>
         </a>
        </p>
