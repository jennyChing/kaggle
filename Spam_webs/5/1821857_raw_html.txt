                         I am so, so excited to share these chutney recipes with you for so many reasons. First of all,chutney is easier to make than you think it is. It literally takes five minutes to make any of these chutneys, I am about to share with you. Second, chutney can be made out of literally anything you like. I am sharing my four favorite and most commonly known chutneys, but you can make chutney from any combination of fruits, vegetables and spices you like. Third, chutney can be sweet, savory and spicy. There is a flavor that will please anyone in your home. Most importantly, chutney can be used on everything! Chutney can be paired with
                         <a href="http://www.shefspantry.com/2011/05/chicken-tikka-masala-with-naan.html">
                          naan
                         </a>
                         , used as a sauce for meat dishes and pasta dishes or as a dipping sauce for your favorite snacks. The best part about chutney is that is lasts forever! You can store chutney in airtight containers for several months or freeze it and pull it out when you need a quick flavorful dipping sauce. I encourage you take some time and play around with different combinations offruits, vegetables and spices until you come up with something you like. I'm looking forward to hearing about you chutney adventures!

                       <div closure_uid_ud120e="710" style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
                        1 teaspoon salt
                       </div>
                       <div closure_uid_ud120e="710" style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">

                       </div>
                       <div closure_uid_ud120e="710" style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
                        2 teaspoons cumin seeds
                       </div>
                       <div closure_uid_ud120e="710" style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
                        1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
                       </div>
                       <div closure_uid_ud120e="710" style="border-bottom: medium none; border-left: medium none; border-right: medium none; border-top: medium none;">
                        1/2 lemon, juiced
                       </div>

                        4 tablespoons cold water

                        <br/>
                        In a blender or food processor, add apple butter, salt, hot cayenne pepper, cumin seeds, ground cloves and lemon juice. Pulse to mix and slowly pour in water, 1 tablespoon at a time, until mixture comes together. With a spatula, scrape down the edges of the blender or food processor and mix again until just combined. Pour chutney in an airtight container and store in refrigerator.
