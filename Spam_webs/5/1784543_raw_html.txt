         <h1 class="story-title">
          5 underrated acts up for Hamilton Music Awards
         </h1>
        </div>
        <h3 class="story-deck">
        </h3>
        <p class="small lighttext">
         <span class="spaced">
          ByAdam Carter,
          <a href="/news/cbc-news-online-news-staff-list-1.1294364">
           CBC News
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="delimited">
          Posted: May 21, 2015 9:36 AM ET
         </span>
         <span>
          Last Updated: May 21, 2015 9:50 AM ET
         </span>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="story-primary">
        <div class="story-leadmedia" id="leadmedia">
         <div class="figure">
          <img alt="Burlington rock band Dirty Jeans is up for punk recording of the year at the 2015 Hamilton Music Awards." height="349" src="http://i.cbc.ca/1.3081131.1432155900!/fileImage/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/16x9_620/dirty-jeans.jpg" title="" width="100%"/>
          <p class="figure-caption">
           Burlington rock band Dirty Jeans is up for punk recording of the year at the 2015 Hamilton Music Awards. (Marissa Saunders)
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>

        <div aria-labelledby="storyhead" class="story-body" role="main">
         <div class="story-content">
          <div class="story-middle">
          </div>
          <p>
           It's almost Hamilton Music Awards weekend, and the city is preparing to celebrate the staggering amount of talent Hamilton has to offer.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           There are big names and internationally renowned artists nominated, like Arkells, Diana Panton, Blackie and the Rodeo Kings, Jeremy Fisher and lots more.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           But there are also loads of underrated bands you might not have heard of who are up for awards.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Here are five of the lesser-known acts hoping to take home trophies this weekend that you should definitely check out.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Scott Orr
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <iframe seamless="" src="http://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=3897499228/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; width: 100%; height: 120px;">
           &lt;a href="http://shop.othersongsmusic.com/album/a-long-life"&gt;A Long Life by Scott Orr&lt;/a&gt;
          </iframe>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Iron and Wine fans, rejoice  you have a local version who writes ridiculously beautiful, lo-fi folk.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Scott Orr's newest album
           <em>
            A Long Life
           </em>
           is full of soft, lilting balladry that is nothing short of idyllic. He has a clear and elevated sense of melody and song structure that's on full display here.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Orr is up for folk/traditional album of the year.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Dirty Jeans
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=3691789367/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; width: 100%; height: 120px;">
           &lt;a href="http://dirtydirtyjeans.bandcamp.com/album/dirty-jeans"&gt;Dirty Jeans by Dirty Jeans&lt;/a&gt;
          </iframe>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The best rock and roll bands are all about energy, and Burlington's Dirty Jeans clearly knowthat.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Each track on the all-girl band's self-titled EP has an underlying intensity coupled with sugary sweet vocals. Fans of Veruca Salt will get what this band is all about.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Dirty Jeans is up for punk recording of the year.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Varga
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="348" mozallowfullscreen="" src="https://player.vimeo.com/video/100867051?color=9f7b3f&amp;title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="100%">
          </iframe>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Canadian metal fans might know Varga, but the average person likely doesn't.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The band's tight, progressive metal sound has been well honed throughout the years, and is on full display on their newest release,
           <em>
            Return of the Metal
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Yeah, it's a little cheesy  but the band fully embraces the classic metal aesthetic, complete with Rob Halford-esque falsetto vocals and slightly atonal syncopated riffs. If you're a classic metal fan, these guys are for you.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Varga is up for metal recording of the year.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Katie Bulley
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/track=105671089/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; width: 100%; height: 120px;">
           &lt;a href="http://katiebulley.bandcamp.com/track/chasing"&gt;Chasing by Katie Bulley&lt;/a&gt;
          </iframe>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Singer songwriter Katie Bulley's solo debut
           <em>
            Sun Wolf
           </em>
           was very clearly influenced by her solo trip across the U.S.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           It has that hazy, Americana-influenced roots sound best associated with a long car trip.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Bulley sounds like a songwriter who is really starting to find her own sound and her own voice. She's up for female artist of the year.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Bud Roach
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/8szi8vzkFcg" width="100%">
          </iframe>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Bud Roach doesn't scream classical music virtuoso. First off, his name is Bud Roach. That isn't the kind of stage name that evokes images of softly plucked baroque arias.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           He's every inch an unassuming looking guy, too  but as soon as Roach starts performing, you're instantly transported back in time hundreds of years.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The tenor's pristine voice and immaculate guitar playing make him totally stand out from the rest of the HMA pack, if for nothing else than his total eschewing of popular music norms and full-on embrace of classical music.
          </p>
          <p>
           Roach is nominated for male artist of the year.
          </p>

           <h2>
            Hamilton Music Awards festival
           </h2>
           <p>
            More than 100 acts will perform in 13 downtown venues during the Hamilton Music Awards festival this week.
           </p>
           <p>
            Performers in the main stage concert series include Lee Harvey Osmond, Laura Cole, Harrison Kennedy, Diana Panton, Jesse Cook and Justin Dunlop.
           </p>
           <p>
            Festival performers include Rita Chiarelli, Simply Saucer, Tomi Swick, Silverstein, Hachey the MouthPeace and more.
           </p>
           <p>
            Tickets available at
            <a href="http://ticketbreak.com">
             ticketbreak.com
            </a>
            , or1-866-943-8849, or at Picks and Sticks at 140 Locke Street S. More information is available at
            <a href="http://HamiltonMusicAwards.com">
             HamiltonMusicAwards.com
            </a>
