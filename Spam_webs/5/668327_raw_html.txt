           /*&lt;![CDATA[*/(adsbygoogle=window.adsbygoogle||[]).push({});/*]]&gt;*/
          </script>
         </center>
         <p>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #999999;">
          I felt almost guilty posting this article because we live on the west coast, where the weather is reasonably balmy, the crocuses and tulips are blooming, the spring birds are chirping, and its warm enough I am sitting working on my blogs with the deck doors open to the fresh air.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #999999;">
          <a href="http://www.featheringtheemptynest.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/5410124375_329c304a64.jpg" style="color: #999999;" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Blizzard snow drifts." class="alignright wp-image-12619" height="185" src="http://www.featheringtheemptynest.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/5410124375_329c304a64.jpg" title="blizzard drifts" width="123"/>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #999999;">
          Then, I took a look at Facebook posts from family members on the east coast over the past couple of days and realize we are the lucky ones.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #999999;">
          <a href="http://www.featheringtheemptynest.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/My-Signature.png" style="color: #999999;">
           <img alt="My Signature" class=" wp-image-8885 alignleft" height="53" src="http://www.featheringtheemptynest.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/My-Signature.png" width="81"/>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #999999;">
          ____________________
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         As the temperatures drop and the cold winds blow in the winter, most of us spend more time seeking warm shelter indoors. However, you might have noticed some of that chilly winter weather sneaking through the windows and intruding into your warm sanctuary!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="7 ways to winterize windows." class=" wp-image-12621 alignleft" height="248" src="http://www.featheringtheemptynest.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Winterize-windows..png" width="375"/>
         Of all the areas of your home, your windows are one of the top culprits for leaking heat. Old homes and windows are especially prone to poor heat insulation, but newer homes suffer from this as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not only does a leaky window make your home colder and less comfortable, but it can really impact your energy bills!
        </p>
        <p>
         Since your heating system has to overcompensate for the heat loss, youll be spending more money than you need to. On top of all that, the environmental cost is higher when you have to use more energy to heat your home.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont fret!
        </p>
        <p>
         There are a lot of great ways to winterize your windows. Follow these tips and youll be able to keep the cold weather out and the cosy warmth in.
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #99ccff;">
          Drapes, shades, or curtains.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Just because an idea is simple, doesnt mean its not going to work. Yes, something as easy as hanging up some good thick curtains over your windows can have a positive impact. Mount them as close to the window as possible for the maximum benefit. You can open up the curtain during the day to get direct sunlight, but make sure to draw them closed again when the sun goes down.
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #99ccff;">
          Find leaks.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Some cracks and gaps will be easy to spot, but even the little ones that are hard to see can lead to serious heat loss. Take a long candle or stick of incense and move it along all the seams around your windows. Wherever there is a leak, you will be able to see the air move the flame or smoke.
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #99ccff;">
          Fix structural issues around the windows.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Cracked panes and frames can leak a lot of heat, so repair or replace these right away. If you are going to replace the windows, make sure to choose modern, high-efficiency multi-pane windows.
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #99ccff;">
          Caulk.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Any stationary gaps and seams all around your window can be
         <a href="http://energy.gov/energysaver/projects/savings-project-how-seal-air-leaks-caulk" rel="nofollow">
          sealed with caulking
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #99ccff;">
          Weather stripping.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         For any gaps that move when the windows are opened and closed, use weather stripping instead of caulk.
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #99ccff;">
          Storm windows.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         If you dont already own some, invest in a set of storm windows and install them. There are lots of options to choose from, so you have a good chance of finding some that fit your windows and your needs. For maximum winterization, choose storm windows with glass panes and low-e coatings. You can also have
         <a href="http://www.clerawindows.com/blog/what-are-the-benefits-of-storm-doors/" rel="nofollow">
          storm doors installation along with windows
         </a>
         for maximum result.
        </p>
        <h4>
         <span style="color: #99ccff;">
          Insulation film.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         There are a variety of plastic film kits available at many hardware and DIY stores. While they dont always have an aesthetic appeal, they can keep the heat in and save you a lot of money.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #999999;">

         </span>
