            <!-- text -->
            Welcome to
            <b>
             CANCER
             <i>
              Mondial
             </i>
            </b>
            .
											This website provides access to various databases containing information on the
											occurrence of cancer worldwide held and managed by theSectionofCancerSurveillance (
            <a href="http://www.iarc.fr/en/research-groups/sec1/index.php" target="_blank">
             CSU
            </a>
            )
											of IARC.
            <p>
             <font color="#037cb5">
              <i>
               CSU
              </i>
              databases:
             </font>
            </p>
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr valign="top">
           <td align="left" class="normal">
            <br/>
            <img border="0" height="9" src="/images/arrow.gif" width="6"/>
            <i>
             GLOBOCAN
            </i>
            provides access
												to the most recent
            <i>
             estimates
            </i>
            (for 2012) of the cancer
            <b>
             incidence
            </b>
            ,
            <b>
             mortality
            </b>
            and
            <b>
             prevalence
            </b>
            for 28 cancers
            <b>
             worldwide
            </b>
            .
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr valign="top">
           <td align="left" class="normal">
            <br/>
            <img border="0" height="9" src="/images/arrow.gif" width="6"/>
            <i>
             CI5
            </i>
            (Cancer Incidence
													in Five Continents) provides access to detailed information on the
            <b>
             incidence
            </b>
            of cancer
            <i>
             recorded
            </i>
            by
            <b>
             cancer registries
            </b>
            (regional or national)
													worldwide.
           </td>
          </tr>

          <tr valign="top">
           <td align="left" class="normal">
            <br/>
            <img border="0" height="9" src="/images/arrow.gif" width="6"/>
            <i>
             WHO
            </i>
            presents long time
												series of selected cancer
            <b>
             mortality
            </b>
            <i>
             recorded
            </i>
            in selected
            <b>
             countries
            </b>
            of
												the world, together with advanced prediction and trends analysis facilities.
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr valign="top">
           <td align="left" class="normal">
            <br/>
            <p>
            </p>
            <font color="#037cb5">
             Collaborative projects:
            </font>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img border="0" height="9" src="/images/arrow.gif" width="6"/>
            <i>
             ACCIS
            </i>
            (Automated Childhood Cancer Information System) provides access to
											data on cancer
            <b>
             incidence
            </b>
            and
            <b>
             survival
            </b>
            of children
            <i>
             collected
            </i>
            by
											European
            <b>
             cancer registries
            </b>
            (regional or national).
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img border="0" height="9" src="/images/arrow.gif" width="6"/>
            <i>
             IICC
            </i>
            (The International Incidence of Childhood Cancer) provides access to
											data on cancer
            <b>
             incidence
            </b>
            in children
            <i>
             collected
            </i>
            by
            <b>
             cancer registries
            </b>
            (regional or national) worldwide.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img border="0" height="9" src="/images/arrow.gif" width="6"/>
            <i>
             ECO
            </i>
            (European Cancer Observatory) is a one-stop web-based tool for accessing, analysing and downloading European cancer data.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img border="0" height="9" src="/images/arrow.gif" width="6"/>
            <i>
             NORDCAN
            </i>
            presents up-to-date long time series of cancer
            <b>
             incidence, mortality, prevalence and survival
            </b>
            from 50 cancers recorded by the
            <span class="tooltip" title="Denmark; Finland; Faeroe Islands; Greenland; Iceland; Norway and Sweden">
             <b>
              Nordic countries
             </b>
            </span>
            , together with advanced prediction and trends analysis facilities.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img border="0" height="9" src="/images/arrow.gif" width="6"/>
            <i>
             SurvCan
            </i>
            presents cancer
            <b>
             survival
            </b>
            data from
            <b>
             cancer registries in low and middle income regions
            </b>
            of the world.
            <br/>
