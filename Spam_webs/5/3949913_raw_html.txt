   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Hyperion (tree)
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

     <div class="hatnote">
      This article is about Hyperion, the tallest living tree.  For other uses, see
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyperion_(disambiguation)" title="Hyperion (disambiguation)">
       Hyperion (disambiguation)
      </a>
      .

     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Redwood_National_Park_REDW9343.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2970" data-file-width="2013" height="325" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Redwood_National_Park_REDW9343.jpg/220px-Redwood_National_Park_REDW9343.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Redwood_National_Park_REDW9343.jpg/330px-Redwood_National_Park_REDW9343.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Redwood_National_Park_REDW9343.jpg/440px-Redwood_National_Park_REDW9343.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Redwood_National_Park_REDW9343.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>

        <a href="/wiki/Redwood_National_and_State_Parks" title="Redwood National and State Parks">

     <p>
      <b>
       Hyperion
      </b>
      is the name of a
      <a href="/wiki/Sequoia_sempervirens" title="Sequoia sempervirens">
       coast redwood
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       Sequoia sempervirens
      </i>
      ) in
      <a href="/wiki/Northern_California" title="Northern California">
       Northern California
      </a>
      that was measured at 115.61m (379.3ft), which ranks it as the world's tallest known living tree.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Earle2011_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Earle2011-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
       Overview
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hyperion_(tree)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Hyperion was discovered August 25, 2006, by
      <a href="/wiki/Natural_history" title="Natural history">
       naturalists
      </a>
      Chris Atkins and
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Taylor_(forester)" title="Michael Taylor (forester)">
       Michael Taylor
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The tree was verified as standing 115.55m (379.1ft) tall by
      <a href="/wiki/Stephen_C._Sillett" title="Stephen C. Sillett">
       Stephen Sillett
      </a>
      . The tree was found in a remote area of
      <a href="/wiki/Redwood_National_and_State_Parks" title="Redwood National and State Parks">
       Redwood National and State Parks
      </a>
      purchased in 1978.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schrepfer1983_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Schrepfer1983-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The exact location of the tree has not been revealed to the public for fear that human traffic would upset the
      <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">
       ecosystem
      </a>
      the tree inhabits. The tree is estimated to contain 530m
      <sup>
       3
      </sup>
      (18,600cuft) of wood
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and to be roughly 700800 years old.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martin2006_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Martin2006-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Researchers stated that
      <a href="/wiki/Woodpecker" title="Woodpecker">
       woodpecker
      </a>
      damage at the top may have prevented the tree's growth. According to Michael Taylor, "It's possible it could've topped out at 380 feet [or 116 metres]".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martin2006_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Martin2006-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In February 2012, Hyperion was featured in the
      <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio" title="BBC Radio">
       BBC Radio
      </a>
      4 documentary
      <i>
       James and the Giant Redwoods
      </i>
      by James Aldred.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
