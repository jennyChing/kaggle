        <h1 class="calluna" itemprop="name" style="font-weight: normal;">
         Amazon's Kindle Unlimited lets you read and listen all you want for $9.99 a month
        </h1>
        <p class="titleMetaGrey calluna noMarg" rel="author">
         BY
         <a class="upcased" href="/appnn/author/aldrin-calimlim">
          Aldrin Calimlim
         </a>
         on
         <time class="" datetime="2014-07-18" pubdate="">
          Fri July 18th,  2014
         </time>
         <span itemprop="keywords">
          <span class="tag">
           <a href="/appnn/tag/amazon">
            amazon
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="tag">
           <a href="/appnn/tag/e-reading-apps">
            e-reading apps
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="tag">
           <a href="/appnn/tag/kindle">
            kindle
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="tag">
           <a href="/appnn/tag/kindle-unlimited">
            kindle unlimited
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
       </hgroup>
       <section class="contentBody contentPad stdRounded lteshaddowWide" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p class="leadImageBlock" style="text-align: center;">
         <img class="size-large aligncenter" src="http://wpuploads.appadvice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/kindleunlimited.jpg" title="Amazon's Kindle Unlimited lets you read and listen all you want for $9.99 a month"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         It didnt take long for the rumors of an e-book subscription service from Amazon to be confirmed. Just a couple of days ago, it was
         <a href="http://appadvice.com/appnn/2014/07/amazon-is-testing-out-an-e-book-subscription-service-to-compete-against-oyster-scribd">
          reported
         </a>
         that Amazon had been testing something called Kindle Unlimited to compete against the likes of
         <a href="http://getapp.cc/app/672803850">
          Oyster
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://getapp.cc/app/542557212">
          Scribd
         </a>
         . Now, the company itself has officially announced the service.
        </p>
        <p>
         Indeed called
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/kindleunlimited">
          Kindle Unlimited
         </a>
         , the new service allows subscribers to read freely from a selection of more than 600,000 Kindle edition e-books for just $9.99 a month. Whats more, included in the subscription is the added perk of being able to listen to thousands of audiobooks from
         <a href="http://getapp.cc/app/379693831">
          Audible
         </a>
         , with the ability to switch between reading and listening via Whispersync for Voice. Amazon notes in its announcement:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Finding a great book is easy, and there are never any due datesjust look for the Kindle Unlimited logo on eligible titles and click Read for Free. Customers can choose from best sellers like The Hunger Games, Diary of a Wimpy Kid, and The Lord of the Rings, and with thousands of professionally narrated audiobooks from Audible, like The Handmaids Tale and Water for Elephants, the story can continue in the car or on the go.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/ku-freetrial">
          Kindle Unlimited is available now with a free 30-day trial
         </a>
         , but it appears to be initially U.S.-only. Its accessible on Kindle e-readers and on
         <a href="http://getapp.cc/app/302584613">
          Kindle
         </a>
         e-reading apps for iOS, Mac, and other supported platforms.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kindle Unlimited subscribers also get a free three-month Audible membership that provides access to Audibles entire audiobook catalog (not just the ones included in Kindle Unlimited).
        </p>
        <p>
         As an avid reader (of both print and electronic books), Im excited to see what Kindle Unlimited has in store. Ive actually just activated my free trial of the service. As it turns out, Kindle Unlimited is limited to only 10 titles at a time. Be that as it may, Amazon appears to have a potentially bestselling e-book subscription service right here.
        </p>
        <p>
         Is Apples 
         <a href="http://getapp.cc/app/364709193">
          iBooks
         </a>
         Unlimited next?
        </p>
       </section>
