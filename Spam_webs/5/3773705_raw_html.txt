        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         A Glimpse of my Potipot Island Experience
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-04-22T22:00:15+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 22, 2012
         </time>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         I never liked camping or the idea of roughing it but when sis asked me to go to Potipot Island this weekend, I said yes. We were going to Subic anyway, might as well drop by the place since Ive heard so much about the island. Both good and bad reviews.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="440" src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/-6489JTom4yg/T5QNNrb6BqI/AAAAAAAAM4I/qhWkBk_Uldo/s800/Potipot.jpg" width="660"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I didnt set any expectations, I dont like going somewhere without electricity. But just as I expected, Potipot Island is naturally very beautiful. The water around it is clear and theres fine white sand on its shoreline.
        </p>
        <p>
         The problem is that the owner of the island charges for entrance fee but they dont maintain the cleanliness of the area. It could seriously use a trash pump. Or they should have at least hired someone to clean the restrooms and rake the leaves.
        </p>
        <p>
         With the way things are going, that natural beauty will soon fade away if they dont take care of it.
        </p>
