            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Update Your Beauty Routine
            </h2>
            <p>
             Summers here, which means a whole new slinkier wardrobe. But its not just your clothes that need to be swapped out for summer  your beauty routine also needs a tune-up this season. "As the weather gets warmer, we turn down the heat and are exposing ourselves to environmental stressors more, so our skin's needs change," says Francesca Fusco, MD, an assistant clinical professor of dermatology at Mount Sinai School of Medicine in New York.
            </p>
            <p>
             Find out what products top beauty professionals recommend for looking gorgeous despite seasonal skin assaults.
            </p>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Protect Your Skin
            </h2>
            <p>
             Increased ultraviolet exposure is one of your skin's biggest enemies. "UV light damages the skin, creates spots, and accelerates aging," says Fusco.
            </p>
            <p>
             To stay protected, slather on a sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher every morning and reapply throughout the day. Elizabeth Tanzi, MD, a clinical instructor in dermatology at Johns Hopkins Hospital Center and codirector of the Washington Institute of Dermatologic Laser Surgery in Washington, DC, recommends
             <strong>
              Yes to Cucumbers Natural Sunscreen SPF 30
             </strong>
             for its mineral composition that doesn't leave your skin feeling greasy. "As an added plus, the lotion contains organic cucumbers, green tea, and aloe vera, which offer anti-inflammatory benefits and help soothe the skin," she says.
            </p>
           </span>

             Show Your Lips Some Love
            </h2>
            <p>
             Spring weather and dry indoor heat can do a number on your lips. Buff off dry flakes with a DIY lip exfoliation. "You can use a baby toothbrush and sugar scrub to get rid of the dead skin cells," suggests Fusco.
            </p>
            <p>
             After exfoliating, swipe on a hydrating lip treatment, like
             <strong>
              Primitive Pearl Lip Treatments
             </strong>
             , a favorite of green beauty expert, Paige Padgett. "I like that the formula is vegan and doesn't contain preservatives, additives, or artificial color," she says of the green tea and rose hip-infused concoction.

             Pamper Your Eyes
            </h2>
            <p>
             Between seasonal allergies and extended sun exposure, the thin skin around your eyes can become puffy and irritated in the summer. So look for an eye treatment with antioxidants like grape seed oil and stash it in the fridge between uses. When applied cold, it'll help reduce some of the swelling.
            </p>
            <p>
             Tanzi says that
             <strong>
              Caudalie Premier Cru The Eye Cream
             </strong>
             tops her list as the best overall eye cream. "The anti-aging formula includes a potent mix of antioxidants such as grape seed extract, resveratrol, and a powerful dose of hydration with vinolevure," she says, adding that "the ingredients, cultivated at the company's private vineyard, makes the skin around your eyes look firmer and puffiness and dark circles fade."
            </p>

             Bare Smoother Legs
            </h2>
            <p>

             Swap Serums
            </h2>
            <p>
             Treat your complexion to a vitamin or peptide-based serum that'll protect your skin from the elements this season, says Fusco. "But discontinue use of retinoid serums you may have been using in the winter because they're sun-sensitizing and you'll probably be spending more time outdoors this time of year," she advises.
            </p>

The peptide and tocopheryl acetate (a form of vitamin E) formula can be used every night and in the morning and even under your eyes for an added boost of rejuvenation.
