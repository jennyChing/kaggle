       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         WDW46  This Time Its Personal
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="sep">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.parkeology.com/2014/11/wdw46-this-time-its-personal.html" rel="bookmark" title="8:00 am">
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-11-04T08:00:54+00:00" pubdate="">
           November 4, 2014
          </time>
         </a>
         <span class="by-author">
          <span class="sep">
           by
          </span>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.parkeology.com/author/shane" rel="author" title="View all posts by shane">
            shane
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         If you know about Parkeology at all, chances are its because of something called WDW47.
        </p>
        <p>
         And now, like any crass sequel, we are going to attempt to revisit the original. Just when you thought it was safe to go back in the parks.
        </p>
        <p>
         But first, a brief timelineto show how we got to this moment:
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          April 2013
         </em>
         <br/>
         Parkeology
         <a href="http://www.parkeology.com/2013/04/wdw47.html">
          announces
         </a>
         the WDW47 attempt. One Day. Every ride. It is initially intended as a solo attempt by Shane, for maximum efficiency.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Throughout the spring, Shane and Ted grapple with the enormity of the project, coming up with dozens if not hundreds of strategies and maneuvers, and despairing of ever whittling them down to the most efficient plan possible.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Certain failure is predicted.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          June 9, 2013
         </em>
         <br/>
         With a week to go, Parkeology chucks the solo plans out the window and doubles down. Ted joins the fellowship and books passage to Orlando on United Airlines.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          June 15, 2013
         </em>
         <br/>
         Shane and Ted meet in Epcot on the eve of the big day to go over last minute plans. It is the first time these two friends have ever met in person.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          June 16, 2013 Fathers Day
          <br/>
         </em>
         Shane and Ted arrive at the Magic Kingdom at 8:15 a.m. for the run. It is brutally hot. Impossibly crowded. And so it begins.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Delays on Dumbo nearly throw the whole plan off in the first hour. A FastPass fiasco at Hollywood Studios sends them scurrying for alternate plans. Test Track and Living with the Land both go 101 just the team reaches Epcot. A herd of rhinos sidetracks the Kilimanjaro Safari for 20 minutes. But with steady persistence, Parkeology checks off ride after ride.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         They live-tweet
         <a href="https://twitter.com/intent/user?screen_name=Parkeology" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://twitter.com/intent/user?screen_name=Parkeology', '@Parkeology']);">
          @Parkeology
         </a>
         (hashtag #WDW47) throughout the day. It starts to catch on. They end the day exhausted, dehydrated, with at least one broken bone (not joking). But success is within reach. In the end, a massive end-of-the-world thunderstorm shuts down Tomorrowland Speedway and the AstroOrbiter in the midnight hour, forcing substitutions. But the plan is vindicated and the team shares a victory meal at the Hess gas station at 2:00 a.m.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          July, 2013
         </em>
         <br/>
         The
         <a href="http://www.parkeology.com/2013/07/wdw47-the-video.html">
          video of the attempt
         </a>
         is released. Parkeology begins to gain notoriety for the insanity of the attempt. On July 25, WDW47 is featured in the
         <a href="http://www.orlandosentinel.com/travel/attractions/the-daily-disney/os-dewayne-bevil-disney-world-rides-20130725-column.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.orlandosentinel.com/travel/attractions/the-daily-disney/os-dewayne-bevil-disney-world-rides-20130725-column.html', 'Orlando Sentinel']);">
          Orlando Sentinel
         </a>
         , which poses the all-important question: Why? To which Parkeology brashly declares: Because it was there.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          Fall, 2013
         </em>
         <br/>
         Walt Disney World begins to introduce FastPass+, a new version of their ride reservation system. The rules begin to tighten. Number of FastPasses, park restrictions, tiering systems, magic bands  perhaps you sense a disquieting metamorphosis.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         By winter, the rules have changed all together. The vaunted WDW47 plan is rendered obsolete, never to be used again.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          May, 2014
         </em>
         <br/>
         The Magic Kingdom opens the Seven Dwarfs Mine Train, the last addition to New Fantasyland. The themed coaster debuts to massive crowds and obscenewait times. It also increases the Walt Disney World total ride count to 48.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          Summer, 2014
          <br/>
         </em>
         Parkeology commissions Harrison Buzz Price of the Stanford Research Institute to conduct feasibility studies on a proposed WDW48 run to take place in late summer.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         The results are frankly terrifying. Not only is the new Mine Train ride routinely clocking in at 80 minute waits throughout the summer, but the new FastPass+ restrictions are all but crippling to an attempt. To make matters worse, several rides are down for refurbishment the entire summer.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          August, 2014
          <br/>
         </em>
         Parkeology books hotel reservations for early October  the first available window for an attempt at WDW48. Ted books airline reservations.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Disney promptly schedules a refurbishment of the Walt Disney World Railroad, destroying the October plans.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          September, 2014
          <br/>
         </em>
         Disney announces the permanent closure of Maelstrom, taking the ride count back down to 47.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         About a week later, they announce the closure of the Backlot Tour. The ride count now stands at 46.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         WDW48 would be unmasked as a fraud  never even truly possible except on a bare handful of days, due to refurbishments. And in hindsight, the summer of 2013 was the last good window ever for WDW47  at least until the Frozen ride opens.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          October, 2014
          <br/>
         </em>
         Parkeologys ride analysis division achieves a scientific breakthrough to the previously-unsolvable Soarin-Toy Story-Mine Train triumvirate. Calendars areconsulted. Wheels are set in motion.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          November 4, 2014
          <br/>
         </em>
         Parkeology officially announces WDW46.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.parkeology.com/wdw46-faq/wdw46_2014" rel="attachment wp-att-2663">
          <img alt="WDW46: One Day. Every Ride" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2663" height="666" src="http://www.parkeology.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/wdw46_2014-500x666.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         As we line up the attempt, the first question that must be answered, quite frankly, is the issue of the ride count. Isnt it easier to see 46 rides this year, when last year called for 47?
        </p>
        <p>
         Frankly, a case could be made that it is much harder. The old plan included Maelstrom and Backlot Tour  two rides that we waited a combined 20 minutes during peak hours of peak summer touring time last year.
        </p>
        <p>
         And were replacing them with the Seven Dwarfs Mine Train, which sells out of FastPasses 65 days in advance, has a 45 minute standby wait
         <em>
          before it even opens
         </em>
         , and has a nasty habit ofbreaking down or
         <a href="http://www.people.com/article/seven-dwarfs-train-ride-catches-fire-disney-world" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.people.com/article/seven-dwarfs-train-ride-catches-fire-disney-world', 'catching fire']);">
          catching fire
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Sure, Backlot was a long attraction but the net time investment for the Mine Train far eclipses the two rides it replaced.
        </p>
        <p>
         Secondly, Disney changed the game.
        </p>
        <p>
         WDW47 was crazy enough last year, with an open FastPass system that could be manipulated to the advantage of anyone intimately familiar with the details. But in this, the Year of the Magic Bands, things may have taken a turn of the worse. We will go into more detail later, but suffice it to say, we believe WDW46 is a harder challenge than ever.
        </p>
        <p>
         But we think we can do it.
        </p>
        <p>
         And even though Disney may have changed how the parks can be toured, the rules of the WDW46 challenge remain the same as last year. Every ride in all four parks in a single operating day. No cheating. Weve created a
         <a href="http://www.parkeology.com/wdw46-faq">
          WDW46 FAQ
         </a>
         page if you want to review the insane restrictions weve placed on ourselves.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once again, well be live-tweeting throughout the attempt, so please follow us on Twitter (
         <a href="https://twitter.com/intent/user?screen_name=Parkeology" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://twitter.com/intent/user?screen_name=Parkeology', '@Parkeology']);">
          @Parkeology
         </a>
         ). Well also use the hashtag #WDW46.
        </p>
        <p>
         We did learn a few things from last year (probably the biggest one is to stay hydrated). Expect to see a different plan in place for this years run. A mix of aggressive offense and cautious defense. The details, as Dr. Marsh is fond of saying, are proprietary, but hopefully weve accounted for all the potential calamities.
        </p>
        <p>
         But there is one small, tiny thing out of our control If its not too much trouble, would you please say a prayer about the weather?
        </p>
