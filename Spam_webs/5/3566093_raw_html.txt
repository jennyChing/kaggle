       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         How to Make Your Own Crochet Hooks
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-10-24T11:00:32+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 24, 2012
         </time>
         By:
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
           Tamara Kelly
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.mooglyblog.com/make-your-own-crochet-hooks/#comments">
           13 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pf-content">
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Crochet hooks  for such a simple tool, they inspire endless debate, and often loyalty. Inline vs. non-inline, pencil hold vs knife hold vs whatever it is that person over there is doing  its all good. You can buy them in any craft store, most little yarn stores, and even some major retailers or you can make your own!
          <a href="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/moogly-make-hooks.jpg">
           <img alt="how to make your own crochet hook tutorial carve crochet hooks how to make hooks" class="size-full wp-image-2832 aligncenter" height="286" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/moogly-make-hooks.jpg" title="How to Make You Own Crochet Hooks! at mooglyblog.com" width="252"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-2796">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          In any big box craft store in the US youll find Boyes and Bates hooks, and often a few Takumi bamboo hooks as well. Occasionally youll see Rosewood or specialty hooks, and depending on the store, the choices just keep expanding. You can get hooks that
          <a href="http://amzn.to/TqN9WG" target="_blank" title="Light Up Crochet Hooks on Amazon">
           light up
          </a>
          , hooks that are
          <a href="http://amzn.to/T62lSI" target="_blank" title="Ergonomic Crochet Hooks on Amazon">
           ergonomic
          </a>
          , and hooks with
          <a href="http://amzn.to/XHPYBy" target="_blank" title="Interchangeable Hook Set on Amazon">
           interchangeable heads
          </a>
          . And if you head over to etsy, there are people creating gorgeous handmade hooks. Not to mention the amazing
          <a href="http://jimbosfrontporch.blogspot.com/" title="Jimbo's Front Porch - extraordinary crochet hooks!">
           Jimbo
          </a>
          , who has a knack for making my jaw drop on every visit.
         </p>
         <dl class="wp-caption alignright" id="" style="width: 178px;">
          <dt class="wp-caption-dt">
           <img alt="how to make your own crochet hook, making crochet hooks tutorial" class=" " height="143" src="http://aberrantcrochet.files.wordpress.com/2012/09/wpid-2012-09-25_20-57-13_-2-0ev-2.jpg?w=280&amp;h=238" title="Aberrant Crochet - Crochet Hook Challenge! " width="168"/>
          </dt>
         </dl>
         <p>
          So what about making your own crochet hooks? Ill admit I havent done it yet. But Julia at
          <a href="http://aberrantcrochet.wordpress.com/" title="Aberrant Crochet">
           Aberrant Crochet
          </a>
          has been working on a
          <a href="http://aberrantcrochet.wordpress.com/tag/crochet-hook-challenge/" title="Aberrant Crochet ">
           Crochet Hook Challenge
          </a>
          and its very inspiring! Those are her amazing hands on the right, and heres her video on Crochet Hook Engineering:
          <br/>
          <iframe frameborder="0" height="360" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/r-M5jziMryI" width="480">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Doesnt she make it look fun? Julia and Aberrant Crochet are awesome, so be sure to check out her site. And there are other great tutorials out there as well:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.wikihow.com/Carve-a-Crochet-Hook" target="_blank" title="Wiki-How - How to Carve a Crochet Hook">
            Wiki-How
           </a>
           has a really good How to Carve a Crochet Hook Tutorial, with great photos and some really nifty tips  like using a pencil sharpener to shape your ends!
           <img alt="crochet hooks how to make crochet hooks how to carve crochet hook" class="aligncenter" height="199" src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/8/8d/Carve_crochet_4.jpg/629px-Carve_crochet_4.jpg" title="How to Carve a Crochet Hook - links to lots of tutorials and great info on mooglyblog.com" width="264"/>
          </li>
          <li>
           On
           <a href="http://www.cutoutandkeep.net/projects/make_your_own_crochet_hooks" target="_blank" title="Cut Out and Keep">
            Cut Out and Keep
           </a>
           , theres a photo tutorial on making your own hooks from chopsticks! You wont get the bigger sizes this way, but great info to have in an emergency! The photos are a bit blurry, but you still get the idea.
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="150" src="http://images0.cdn.coplusk.net/projects/24178/steps/35976/small_100_0107_1255778915.jpg" title="Making Crochet Hooks from Chopsticks! Learn more at mooglyblog.com" width="200"/>
          </li>
          <li>
           On
           <a href="http://serendipity.gpvm.com/straight.html" title="Serendipity Crochet">
            Serendipity Crochet
           </a>
           , theres a step by step tutorial on making your own hooks from dowels. Dowels are a great place to start because they are uniform and straight, and because they are so nice and long you can make your own cro-hooks and tunisian hooks too!
           <img alt="serendipity how to make your own crochet cooks making hooks tutorial free" class="aligncenter" height="134" src="http://serendipity.gpvm.com/images/straighthook3.jpg" title="How to Make Your Own Crochet Hooks - a tutorial roundup on mooglyblog.com!" width="388"/>
          </li>
          <li>
           If youve got wood turning or lathe experience, then making crochet hooks can get really fancy! On
           <a href="http://www.turnedtreasures.com/project.html/crochet_hook.html" title="Turned Treasures">
            Turned Treasures
           </a>
           theres an in-depth look at how to make wood turned crochet hooks. Were talking exotic hardwoods, baby mmm.
           <img alt="how to make crochet hooks how to make a crochet hook wooden crochet hook tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="200" src="http://www.turnedtreasures.com/images/crochet_hook/top_design_opt_sm.jpg" title="How to Make Crochet Hooks - handcarved and lathe, roundup of techniques on mooglyblog.com!" width="200"/>
          </li>
          <li>
           But if you arent ready to wield a pocketknife, knife carver, or lathe just yet, you can still get creative with your hooks. Create your own ergonomic set by covering them with polymer clay! The tutorial at
           <a href="http://sewisee.blogspot.com/2012/03/clay-covered-crochet-hooks.html" title="Sew I See - Covering hooks in clay">
            Sew I See!
           </a>
           is the best Ive seen, and her results are so pretty. Make sure to mark the size on the end as shown though!
           <img alt="crochet hook tutorial, covered crochet hooks, clay covered hooks how to make a crochet hook" class="aligncenter" height="240" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7052/6985376795_97bffb195c_b.jpg" title="Custom Crochet Hooks? Find out how at mooglyblog.com!" width="360"/>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          UPDATE! The amazing Jimbo himself visited and left this link, to his own
          <a href="http://jimbosfrontporch.blogspot.com/2006/08/hook-in-progress-gone-to-crick-be-back.html" title="Jimbo's Front Porch - Hook In Progress!">
           AMAZING day by day guide to making a crochet hook
          </a>
          ! Do yourself a favor and check it out!
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive got a big collection of hooks, but suddenly Im wishing for something a little more beautiful. Its important to our enjoyment of crochet to pay attention to the tools we use  and how we use them! For wrist and hand health, its good to have tools that fit our hands and feel good, as well as giving the results and projects we love. Making your own crochet hooks, or working with an artisan who makes them, is a great way to feel even better about the art of crochet!
         </p>
