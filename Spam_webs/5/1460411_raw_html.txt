        <section class="bodycopy">
         <h2 id="about">
          About
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What People Think I Do / What I Really Do
          </strong>
          is a series of visual charts depicting a range of preconceptions associated with a particular field of occupation or expertise. Unlike image macro series that are based on singular stereotypes like
          <a href="/memes/advice-animals">
           Advice Animals
          </a>
          , this series compares varying impressions about ones profession held by others, self-image and the often mundane reality of the job.
         </p>
         <h2 id="origin">
          Origin
         </h2>
         <p>
          The Science Student instance of the series was first spotted on Facebook February 7th, 2012. The chart summarized various preconceived notions and generalizations that are associated with that field of study.
         </p>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <center>
          <a href="/photos/267953">
           <img data-src="http://i1.kym-cdn.com/photos/images/newsfeed/000/267/953/8f9.jpg" src="http://a.kym-cdn.com/assets/blank-b3f96f160b75b1b49b426754ba188fe8.gif" width="425"/>
          </a>
         </center>
         <br/>
         <h4 id="precursor">
          Precursor
         </h4>
         <p>
          The multi-perspective approach in visual jokes has been previously adapted by other survey template memes like
          <a href="http://knowyourmeme.com/memes/how-people-view-me-after-i-say-im-x">
           How People View Me After I Say Im X
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://knowyourmeme.com/memes/what-i-watchedexpectedgot">
           What I Watched vs. What I Expected vs. What I Got
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <center>
          <a href="/photos/27286">
           <img data-src="http://i1.kym-cdn.com/photos/images/newsfeed/000/027/286/whatIwatchedtemplate.jpeg" src="http://a.kym-cdn.com/assets/blank-b3f96f160b75b1b49b426754ba188fe8.gif" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </center>
         <br/>
         <h2 id="spread">
          Spread
         </h2>
         <p>
          The Contemporary Artist instance was uploaded by artist Garnet Hertz via his Facebook page
          <sup class="footnote" id="fnr9">
           <a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn9">
            [9]
           </a>
          </sup>
          on February 9th, 2012. The Property Manager variation was created by Know Your Meme member fastnup on February 10th, 2012 and the trend logged. Similar image what people think directors do was posted by Canvas user ItchyBarracuda
          <sup class="footnote" id="fnr1">
           <a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn1">
            [1]
           </a>
          </sup>
          and the thread spawned several derivative versions including freelance programmer, tech support and videogame beta tester.
         </p>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <center>
          <a href="/photos/250063">
           <img data-src="http://i2.kym-cdn.com/photos/images/newsfeed/000/250/063/a47.jpg" src="http://a.kym-cdn.com/assets/blank-b3f96f160b75b1b49b426754ba188fe8.gif" width="425"/>
          </a>
         </center>
         <br/>
         <p>
          The Director instance was picked up by a number of personal blogs
          <sup class="footnote" id="fnr3">
           <a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn3">
            [3]
           </a>
          </sup>
          <sup class="footnote" id="fnr4">
           <a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn4">
            [4]
           </a>
          </sup>
          <sup class="footnote" id="fnr5">
           <a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn5">
            [5]
           </a>
          </sup>
          and professional websites on the following days, spawning additional derivatives along the way. On February 13th, PC Magazine
          <sup class="footnote" id="fnr6">
           <a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn6">
            [6]
           </a>
          </sup>
          reported on the series as a trending meme on Facebook. The same day, photography website Fstoppers
          <sup class="footnote" id="fnr2">
           <a class="footnote-superscript" href="#fn2">
            [2]
           </a>
          </sup>
          presented the photographers edition in an article titled Photographer
          <span class="caps">
           MEME
          </span>
          : What People Really Think I Do.
         </p>
