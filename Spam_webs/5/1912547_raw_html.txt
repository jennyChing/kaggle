                           This electric unicycle incorporates motorcycle-like steering geometry that uses accelerometers and gyros to accelerate, brake, and help you stay balanced. Anticipated to reach market in early 2013 at $4500, the Ryno offers a 20-mph top speed and an estimated cruising range of 20 miles from its lithium iron phosphate battery. Christopher Hoffman, who heads the Portland, Oreg.based outfit, says that unlike the Segway, his creation is "very gentle and responsive, and feels like a part of your body. It's weird." We'll say.
                          </span>
                         </i>
                        </b>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <i>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </i>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-etTyi44ZXWo/VD5I98ZUAyI/AAAAAAAAJxo/e2tni7mwko4/s1600/alterna-cycles-01-0412-lgn.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </div>

                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <u>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                          BRP Can-Am Spyder RS-S:
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <i>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          Brought to you by the same folks who build Ski-Doo snowmobiles, this so-called backwards trike has become a cult favorite on urban roads. Plenty of die-hard two-wheelers still disparage the three-wheeled set, but the Spyder's RS-S spinoff offers gas-charged Fox Racing front suspension that works in conjunction with ABS, power steering, and traction and stability control hooked into a 998-cc V-twin Rotax engine pumping 106 hp. This Canadian-built oddity is quite possibly the techiest way to hit the road on three wheels and starts at $16,499.
                         </span>
                        </i>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <i>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </i>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-rO_sFNML4zM/VD5JMPB0vII/AAAAAAAAJxw/KJ_kGSrJ6YU/s1600/alterna-cycles-02-0412-lgn.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </div>
                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                         <u>
                          Uno Cycle:
                         </u>
                        </span>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <i>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          Ben Gulak was only 16 when, after visiting smog-choked China, he dreamed up this eco-conscious transportation solution. But the two-wheeled concept never made it to market. The inventive Canadian has since enrolled at MIT and developed decidedly more aggressive recreational products, such as the DTV (Dual Track Vehicle) Shredder, which looks like the love child of a Segway and a tank. Inspired by the success of Bombardier (the company behind the BRP Can-Am Spyder), Gulak says his Boston-based BPG Werks has presold 5000 Shredders. Still, we wouldn't be surprised if his Uno concept somehow reappears down the line. We never forget our first love, do we?
                         </span>
                        </i>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">

                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                         <u>
                          Peraves Montoracer:
                         </u>
                        </span>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          This sleek, Swiss-built bullet has automotive aspirations but a sportbike soul. Using a retractable rear tire that acts as a stabilizer when stationary, the Monotracer can reach lean angles of up to 52 degrees in turns. Claiming complete wind protection and air-conditioned comfort, thanks to a carbon and Kevlar monocoque, the slim-profile ride is powered by a four-cylinder BMW motorcycle powerplant capable of achieving 65 mpg at a constant 55 mph. More interesting to pilot than a car but coming with no helmet requirements or safety gear traditionally associated with two-wheelers, the Monotracer promises a futuristicif priceyway to get around.
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">

                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ab8VztF-VBk/VD5JwJj0oeI/AAAAAAAAJyA/IWkfwcDXhcc/s1600/alterna-cycles-04-0412-lgn.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </div>

                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <u>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                          Boxx:
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          It may have all the visual charm of a pizza box, but this battery-powered square scooter actually incorporates some clever design features, including an all-aluminum, weld-free chassis, and a 10-pound motor embedded in a wheel that doubles as a heat sink. This hip-to-be-square scooter is so unconventionally enticing that we also featured it in our recentscooter roundup.And while it costs an extra $599 on top of the $3995 asking price to get a battery that doubles the maximum range to 80 miles, we still can't wait until this Dubai-funded, Portland, Ore.built ride hits streets and sidewalks in the latter part of this year.
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">

                       <br/>

                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px;">
                       <i>
                        <span style="color: purple; font-size: large;">
                         Piaggio MP3:
                        </span>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          Piaggio's oddly named MP3 has absolutely nothing to digital music, except for the way it blasts itself past slower moving traffic when roads turn twisty. Thanks to a parallelogram suspension and two front wheels, the MP3 exhibits a shocking amount of grip when it's leaned into a turn. And while its oddball looks certainly aren't for everyone, the MP3's underseat storage and tall windshield make it a surprisingly effective commute weapon. You'll pay a premium for an MP3$7199 for the 250, $8699 for the 400, and $8899 for the 500but such is the cost of originality.
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>

                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_vtmHg06xMU/VD5KMaO0ICI/AAAAAAAAJyQ/WP395VN6aNA/s1600/alterna-cycles-06-0412-lgn.jpg" width="320"/>

                       <i>
                        <u>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                          Campagna T-Rex 14RR:
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          A 197-hp Kawasaki four-cylinder propels the flagship model to 60 mph in a voracious 3.9 seconds, and its open-air cockpit makes it one loud, screaming beast. But like its carnivorous dinosaur namesake, this three-wheeler is a tad one-dimensionalexcellent at devouring pavement but not for much else. For a mellower, everyday ride, look no further than its Harley-Davidson V-twin- powered stablemate, the V13R Roadster.
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">

                      </div>

                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <u>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                          Sbarro Pendolauto Concept:
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          It may be old news in an ever-changing world of transportation technology, but this concept that debuted at the 2008 Geneva Motor Show still captures our fancy with its clear wheels, form-fitting body, and leaning four-wheel design. Specs were sparse when this one-off was unveiled, and now that it's faded into obscurity, we just want Franco Sbarro's whimsical creation even more.
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>

                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-q86l9vVW6T0/VD5KxZlNI1I/AAAAAAAAJyg/Wq_4C1ZeD18/s1600/alterna-cycles-08-0412-lgn.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </div>

                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px;">
                       <i>
                        <u>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                          Boss Hoss Gangsta Trike:
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          In a world obsessed with fuel economy, it's strangely reassuring that someone's still building wholly unreasonable fuel-sucking throwbacks. Packing a 4.8-liter or 6.2-liter V-8the latter driving 445 hp and 405 lb-ft of torque to 275-mm rear tiresthis big-boned anachronism offers so much of a good thing, it's downright bad. Add its lazily sculpted "trunk" to the equation and you've got yourself one stubbornly un-P.C. way to cruise down the highway, so long as you're carrying a passenger who doesn't mind being seen on a three-wheeler that makes a Humvee seem sensible.
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>
                      </div>

                       <img border="0" height="206" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-X0q9zog0l18/VD5K9fyIv2I/AAAAAAAAJyo/Yhrw03b4B_Y/s1600/alterna-cycles-09-0412-lgn.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </div>

                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                          Evolve Xenon Lightcycle:
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </i>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          If you're a deep-pocketed Tron geek, you may find it impossible to resist this $55,000 Lightcycle replica built by the same folks responsible for the ultra socially consciousEvolve electric scooters. Clad in conceptual bodywork and OLED light tape, this lithium-ion-battery-powered ride features huge, hubless 32-inch wheels and a 100 mph top speed. We're guessing the target market for this futuristic ride is more concerned about sweet sound effects and styling details than commuter friendliness or ergonomics, though Evolve says it's phasing out this model and working on a "more rideable, practical" version in the coming months. Costume sold separately.
                         </span>
                        </b>
                       </i>
                      </div>
                      <div class="imageContent" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        <b>
                         <span style="color: purple; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                          <br/>

                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-OdThLYxMnZw/VD5LNXgHw8I/AAAAAAAAJyw/5si9jXMkMPI/s1600/alterna-cycles-10-0412-lgn.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </div>
                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="color: magenta; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                        <i>
                         <u>
                          Bonus: PAL-V One:
                         </u>
                        </i>
                       </span>
                      </h3>
                      <h3 class="slideTitle" style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px;">
                       <div class="imageContent" style="text-align: justify;">
                        <i>
                         <span style="color: magenta; font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: small;">
                          How do you one-up this eclectic list of alternacycles? Perhaps there's no better way than by featuring theDutch-built PAL-V One as the pice de rsistance. Short for Personal Air and Land Vehicle, this leaning three-wheeler takes aeronautical form when its rotor unfolds and its tail extends, transforming it from land cruiser to high-flier at the drop of a hat. Currently in prototype form and expected to become commercially available in 2014, this dual-purpose ride looks like a scaled-down version of an Apache helicopter. Now, that's a futuristic ride we can stand behind.
                         </span>
                        </i>

                <h2 class="title">
                 Hi What Causes A Rainbow To Form?
                </h2>
                <div class="widget-content">
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#6633ff;">
                   What you are actually looking at when you see a rainbow is falling rain.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#ffcc00;">
                   The falling rain causes white sunlight to bend into separate wavelengths of visible light.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#330099;">
                   This is what causes the colorful bands that form the rainbow.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#cc33cc;">
                   To see a rainbow it must be raining in one part of the sky (in front of you) and the sun must be shining behind you.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#ff6666;">
                   Each color that we see has a particular wavelength that corresponds to the above color spectrum chart.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#cc33cc;">
                   Short wavelengths are perceived as purple or blue and longer wavelengths are perceived as red or orange.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#993300;">
                   The actual colors in the rainbow are dependent upon which wavelengths the rain bends the light into from your position.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#330099;">
                   The raindrops act like a prism separating white sunlight into its separate wavelengths.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#663333;">
                   When you see a rainbow the sun will always be in back of you and the falling rain will be in front of you.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#993300;">
                   The rainbow continually changes because each falling raindrop bends the light slightly differently.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#33cc00;">
                   In addition, no two people ever see the same rainbow because the wavelengths of light will be slightly different depending on where you are in relation to the falling rain.
                  </span>
                 </i>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <i>
                  <span style="color:#ff0000;">
                   Contrary to popular folklore, there is no pot of gold at the end of the rainbow.
                  </span>
                 </i>
