         <p>
          Common fast food meals can contain 1200-2200 calories per meal. For most athletes, this calorie and fat bomb can make performance plummet. Making smart selections when eating at your favorite fast food restaurant can help you stay lean, fast and strong.
         </p>
         <p>
          Making smart sports substitutions will help you avoid meals that are too high in fat. Choose a chicken burger with bbq sauce instead of mayo, and order a baked potato with chili as a side. Even a small fry (rather than supersized) is a smarter sports choice. Drink water, milk or unsweetened tea with meals. If you need extra calories try having another chicken burger, plain hamburger or add a yogurt parfait along with your meal.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            **FEED YOUR GAME EXPERT TIP**
           </strong>
           :
          </em>
          Watch for foods that are crispy, creamy or generally start with C. Candy, coke, chips, creamy sauces, and crispy foods are generally NOT performance enhancers.
         </p>
