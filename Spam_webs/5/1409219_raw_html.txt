             <h1 class="post-title">
              Star Wars: Battlefront Blow Out  The Trailer, Release Date, and New Info Arrives For The Highly Awaited Game
             </h1>
             <span class="entry-auth-link">
              by
              <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/author/legarreta/">
               Matthew Legarreta
              </a>
             </span>
             <h4>
              <strong>
               Its a Battlefront Bonanza.
              </strong>
             </h4>
             <p>
              First of all, sorry this grand news wrap-up is a day late  trust me, if it was completely up to me, I would have had this gone up like twenty minutes after yesterdays big
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/tag/star-wars-battlefront/" title="Star Wars: Battlefront">
                Star Wars: Battlefront
               </a>
              </strong>
              panel at
              <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/tag/star-wars-celebration/" title="Star Wars Celebration">
               Star Wars Celebration
              </a>
              . But life had other plans, so here I am, more than 24 hours after the fact, reporting on all the big news. Because boy was there a lot of them in this Battlefront Blow-Out.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-inserter ad-inserter-1" style="float: right; margin: 10px;">
              <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
              </script>
              <!-- G9 Middle Ad -->
              <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-6267359740539804" data-ad-slot="6414784374" style="display:inline-block;width:300px;height:250px">
              </ins>
              <script>
               (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
              </script>
             </div>
             <p>
              First things first the big one: the new, in-engine trailer for the game. Now thats a tricky word, in-engine. Its the way developers are trying to wow gamers with their graphic prowess, and convince them that this is actually how the game will play. Rest assured, this is essentially a cinematic trailer: yes its built with in engine graphics but, as far as I can see, it sports no actual gameplay. So yeah, thats a bummer, but Im sure EA is saving all the goods on that for E3. At least the trailers good on its own, right?
             </p>
             <div class="video-container">
              <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
               <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="464" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ZwWLns7-xN8?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="770">
               </iframe>
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
              Now as you probably noticed, that trailer sports a November 17 release date, which is officially when the game will be hitting Playstation 4, Xbox One, and PC. That falls in line with the release date zone we heard before, and is only a few weeks before the big release of
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/tag/star-wars-the-force-awakens/" title="Star Wars: The Force Awakens">
                Star Wars: The Force Awakens
               </a>
              </strong>
              so, woo, synergy.
             </p>
             <p>
              And on that note, DICE revealed that a free DLC pack would be arriving for anybody who purchased the game that will directly tie into The Force Awakens, entitled The Battle of Jakku. Jakku is the planet featured at the start of both trailers, if you were wondering, and the DLC will center on the battle that caused the massive Imperial cruiser to crash in the sand. Anybody who pre-orders the game also gets the DLC a week early, because thats how the video game industry works now. PRE-ORDER EVERYTHING, YALL!
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Star-Wars-Battlefront-_4-17_B-670x377.jpg">
               <img alt="Star-Wars-Battlefront-_4-17_B-670x377" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48246" height="377" src="http://www.geekbinge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Star-Wars-Battlefront-_4-17_B-670x377.jpg" width="670"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Meanwhile, some other various odds and ends:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               So far, theres only four planets that have been confirmed for the game: Endor, Hoth, Tatooine, and Sullust. Thats a pretty paltry list, so lets hope some more will be announced.
              </li>
              <li>
               Two characters have been confirmed in the game (Darth Vader and Boba Fett), but more are sure to appear. It wasnt officially confirmed, but it seems likely that the characters will appear in a killstreak-esque playable fashion just like the last game (which is a plus, if you ask me.)
              </li>
              <li>
               There will be no campaign which is, you know, whatever. I dont mind not having a campaign, but lets just hope the multiplayer is good enough that I wont need one. There will be these things called missions that you can play with a co-op partner, but no details have been released about them at this time.
              </li>
              <li>
               The multiplayer will have a 40 player cap which, hey, sounds good to me! Enough for a fun amount of variety, but not too much to break the game.
              </li>
              <li>
               The game will focus primarily on the original trilogy, using its settings, characters, vehicles, and the like only. Because no one talks about the prequels. THEY DIDNT HAPPEN.
              </li>
              <li>
               You will be able to play the game in both first person and third person, which is great news to me (third person forever.)
              </li>
              <li>
               Finally, no space battles, unfortunately. DICE wants to make sure it nails the other elements, so is putting space on the backburner. You will still be able to fly ships on the planets, however.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p style="text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://www.geekbinge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Star-Wars-Battlefront.png">
               <img alt="Star-Wars-Battlefront" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-44256" height="741" src="http://www.geekbinge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Star-Wars-Battlefront.png" width="587"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              So far, all this information is promising, if not overwhelmingly so. I really need to see some gameplay before I can get
              <em>
               too
              </em>
              excited, and I am kind of worried about how barebones the game seems to be (no space battles, few planets, etc.) Theres still more than half a year until the game releases though, so lets hope DICE has plenty more to reveal in the meantime. For now though, are you happy with what we learned in this Star Wars: Battlefront blow out? Let us know in the comments!
             </p>
             <p>
              -Matthew
             </p>
