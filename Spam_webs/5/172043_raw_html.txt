     <p>
      "
      <b>
       I Should Have Never Gone Ziplining
      </b>
      " is the sixth episode of the
      <a href="/wiki/South_Park_(season_16)" title="South Park (season 16)">
       sixteenth season
      </a>
      of the American animated sitcom
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/South_Park" title="South Park">
        South Park
       </a>
      </i>
      , and the 229th episode overall. It was aired on
      <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">
       Comedy Central
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      on April 18, 2012 and is rated
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV-MA_L" title="TV-MA L">
       TV-MA L
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In the spirit of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reality_show" title="Reality show">
       reality shows
      </a>
      such as
      <a href="/wiki/Animal_Planet" title="Animal Planet">
       Animal Planet
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/I_Shouldn%27t_Be_Alive" title="I Shouldn't Be Alive">
        I Shouldn't Be Alive
       </a>
      </i>
      (and incorporating that show's narrator Eric Meyers), the four boys go to the Colorado Mountains to try
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ziplining" title="Ziplining">
       ziplining
      </a>
      , but their trip keeps taking unexpected turns.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Should_Have_Never_Gone_Ziplining&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As Kenny, Stan, Kyle, and Eric Cartman try to decide what to do on
      <a href="/wiki/Spring_break" title="Spring break">
       spring break
      </a>
      , they suddenly find themselves in a unanimous agreement over a plan to try ziplining for the first time. However, the activity proves tedious, with a lot of safety briefings, small talk and very long waiting and travelling times. The boys decide to leave after the first descent; however, they are horrified after the tour guide tells them that there are 16 more ziplines on the tour.
     </p>
     <p>
      Things get worse when Cartman, loaded with sugar and caffeine from drinking excessive amounts of
      <a href="/wiki/Mountain_Dew" title="Mountain Dew">
       Mountain Dew
      </a>
      , constantly farts; instead of stopping, he goes on to drink "Double Dew" which has twice the caffeine of Mountain Dew. After being further delayed by a free lunch of wet sandwiches, the boys use twigs to form the word "HELP" on the ground and wait. Cartman gets a
      <a href="/wiki/Diarrhea" title="Diarrhea">
       diarrhea
      </a>
      attack and tries to find a restroom; Kyle tries to persuade him use the forest as a restroom, but Cartman claims the diarrhea would attract beavers. Stan walks over to one of the employees to tell them that one of his friends has
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herpes" title="Herpes">
       herpes
      </a>
      as an excuse to leave the group, so, despite their enthusiasm with ziplining, the employees points to a nearby horse ranch, and the boys eagerly head towards it. However, it turns out that the horses are used for a tour group as well and cannot go faster than four miles per hour. While the tour group stops for lunch, Kyle lies on a rock, weak with boredom (annoyed by the fact that one of the tourists keeps saying "to make a long story short" but are actually making it long). Stan notices a
      <a href="/wiki/Marina" title="Marina">
       marina
      </a>
      nearby, and so they decide to get a
      <a href="/wiki/Boat" title="Boat">
       boat
      </a>
      to try and escape. A
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viewer_discretion" title="Viewer discretion">
       viewer discretion
      </a>
      warning appears, flashing to a
      <a href="/wiki/Live_action" title="Live action">
       live action
      </a>
      sequence.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_boat" title="Power boat">
       power boat
      </a>
      turns out to be slow as well (going five miles an hour), and the boys snap. Cartman is blamed for his diarrhea, everyone is trying to recall whose idea it was to go ziplining to begin with, except for Kenny, who does not care as he is apparently dying from a combination of boredom and herpes. Cartman defecates into the water; it attracts beavers, which the boys fight off. They then realize that falling asleep might result in their death, so in order to keep everyone awake, Kyle asks Cartman to share his drink (now "Diet Double Dew" which has half the caffeine of "Double Dew"), but he refuses to let Kenny touch it. Kenny eventually dies and the three boys end up contracting herpes anyway. When Kyle and Cartman start arguing on whose idea it was to go ziplining again, Stan admits that
      <i>
       he
      </i>
      was the one who signed them up for the ziplining tour, in order to receive a free 5th generation
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IPod_nano" title="IPod nano">
       iPod nano
      </a>
      (which cannot be traded). Stan however revealed this earlier in the episode in one of the interview scenes that excessively occur throughout, although he was reluctant to tell his friends. Kyle and Cartman are outraged and they all have an
      <a href="/wiki/Catharsis" title="Catharsis">
       emotional breakdown
      </a>
      . Just as all hope seems lost, the boys are miraculously rescued by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr._Hankey" title="Mr. Hankey">
       Mr. Hankey
      </a>
      who takes them home. At this point, the live action sequence ends and reverts to its original animation format (however, the short interview scenes were still animated during the live action sequence).
     </p>
     <p>
      In the aftermath, Kenny's body is delivered to his family. Stan and Cartman are treated for their herpes (Cartman now claims it was a cold sore), and Kyle is hospitalized due to Cartman's
      <a href="/wiki/Feces" title="Feces">
       feces
      </a>
      getting stuck in his
      <a href="/wiki/Nose" title="Nose">
       nose
      </a>
      following Cartman's incessant farting. Motivated to raise awareness about the boredom risks of ziplining, Stan makes a video, but ends up "jackin' it in San Diego"  la
      <a href="/wiki/Jason_Russell" title="Jason Russell">
       Jason Russell
      </a>
      again as he did in the
      <a href="/wiki/Butterballs_(South_Park)" title="Butterballs (South Park)">
       previous episode
      </a>
      . Cartman goes back to drinking Diet Double Dew.
     </p>
