        <p>
         Did you know that denim was born in Italy? (How fun is that, Irene?
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" data-lazy-src="http://cdn.sergerpepper.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
         ) Denim was originally worn by Italian sailors from Genoa in the 1500s and was known as the blue de Genes. Nimes (at France) tried to replicate the fabric but failed. Their product was called de Nimes. Hence the english words Blue Jeans and Denim!
        </p>
        <p>
         In the late 1800s, during the gold rush in the USA, Levi Strauss brought denim to the United States. The pants quickly became popular because of their durability and are worn ever since throughtout the world.
         <span style="font-size: x-small;">
          (Source: jeanswest.com)
         </span>

        </p>
