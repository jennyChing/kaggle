           <h1 class="title">
            Puff Politely: Japan Tobacco's 'Smoking Manners for Adults' Ads, Part 3
           </h1>
           <!-- google_ad_section_end -->
           <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">
            <meta content="Puff Politely: Japan Tobacco's 'Smoking Manners for Adults' Ads, Part 3" itemprop="headline"/>
            <meta content="Puff Politely: Japan Tobacco's 'Smoking Manners for Adults' Ads, Part 3" itemprop="alternativeHeadline"/>
            <meta content="http://inventorspot.com/files/blog1/manners27.gif" itemprop="image"/>
            <meta content="Welcome to the third installment of Japan Tobacco's 'Smoking Manners for Adults' ads. With a total of 73 ads making the rounds of Japanese ad space, it's unlikely you'll see the same ad twice  but you'll definitely get the message that smoking demands consideration. Here are ads 21 through 30..." itemprop="description"/>
            <meta content="2008-11-05" itemprop="datePublished"/>
            <div class="node ntype-blog-2" id="node-15345">
             <!-- google_ad_section_start(weight=ignore) -->
             <span class="submitted">
              by
              <a href="/user/steve_levenstein" title="View user profile.">
               Steve Levenstein
              </a>
             </span>
             <!-- google_ad_section_end -->
             <div class="content">
              <div class="share-horiz">

               <div class="share42init" data-image="http://inventorspot.com/files/blog1/manners27.gif" data-title="Puff Politely: Japan Tobacco's 'Smoking Manners for Adults' Ads, Part 3" data-url="http://inventorspot.com/articles/puff_politely_japan_tobaccos_smoking_manners_adults_ads_part_3_15345">

              <div align="center" class="article-header-block">
               <div class="adsense adsense_oldcode">

              <div class="KonaBody">
               <span itemprop="articleBody">
                <!--paging_filter-->
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="Japan Tobacco's 'Smoking Manners for Adults' Series of Ads" class="image image-_original" height="300" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/02-smoking-manners-sign.jpg" title="Japan Tobacco's 'Smoking Manners for Adults' Series of Ads" width="400"/>
                 <span class="caption">
                  <strong>
                   Japan Tobacco's 'Smoking Manners for Adults' Series of Ads
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                </span>
                <br/>
                <b>
                 Japan Tobacco
                </b>
                finds itself in a bind. Like other cigarette makers and distributors, JT is saddled with a product that is at the same time popular and unhealthy. Advertising cigarettes, when it is allowed at all, has to be handled with care and discretion. With their
                <b>
                 Smoking Manners for Adults
                </b>
                ads, JT avoids the whole
                <i>
                 to smoke or not to smoke
                </i>
                conundrum, focusing instead on ways smokers can lower their profile and not annoy non-smokers. The results are not only amusing, but give insight into Japanese culture and society.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Ads
                <b>
                 21 through 30
                </b>
                , presented here in a spirit of bemusement and accompanied by commentary by yours truly, show that being a Good Smoker doesn't require a lot of smoke &amp; mirrors. At least, not any mirrors...
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  21) "When I looked back, I realized I was colliding with the person behind me even though we weren't touching."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners21.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                <b>
                 My cigarette smoke; my self...
                </b>
                this ad illustrates the fact that to non-smokers, the odoriferous cloud that trails a walking smoker can be considered an extension of said smoker. In Japan, personal space is respected and treasured. It remains to be seen just how smokers can keep their smoke to themselves when out in public, however. Can somebody invent a
                <i>
                 Super Smoke-eating Vacutron
                </i>
                please? In
                <b>
                 Hello Kitty
                </b>
                motif for the ladies.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  22) "I was passing through a crowd carrying a flame. But that's best left to the Olympic torch runner."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners22.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                This very timely ad somewhat exaggerates the fact that brandishing a burning tube of combustible material, the tip of which glows red hot at a temperature of
                <b>
                 800 degrees
                </b>
                , is not to be taken lightly. To quote Elaine (Seinfeld),
                <i>
                 I don't know how you guys walk around with those things.
                </i>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  23) "I was looking for shells, but all I could find were cigarette butts."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners23.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                Ah yes, a day at the
                <b>
                 beach
                </b>
                ... for too many Japanese, the world's largest ashtray. While our intrepid shell collector may be disappointed by the frequency of tossed butts, it could be worse. They could be beachcombing the Jersey shore where cigarette butts mingle amidst used hypos and the odd mob hit.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  24) "When I bumped into someone, I apologized. When my smoke hit your face, I said nothing."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners24.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                This ad states plainly that your smoke packs a punch, even to the extent of equating an actual physical bump with a, er, gas-ical bump. Since this is Japan, one is expected to bow to the offended party. Public smokers ought to be flipping up &amp; down like a toilet seat on bingo night.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  25) "The cool cowboy flicks his cigarette butt into the street. But he lives in an old movie."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners25.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                Try as they might, JT just can't erase the image of Jimmy Stewart, Gary Cooper etc taking that final puff and disdainfully flicking the butt away before setting out to clean up the town. They do have a point, however...
                <i>
                 who's going to clean up the town after Stewart, Cooper etc cleans up the town?
                </i>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  26) "You tossed your cigarette out the window. You looked like you were fleeing the scene of a crime."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners26.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                A little Film Noir imagery here  with the added bonus of a car that
                <i>
                 runs away
                </i>
                . Well, Film Noir plus The Flintstones, I guess. The crowning glory is the classic
                <b>
                 chalk outline
                </b>
                surrounding the cigarette butt body.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  27) "Before passing gas I look behind me. But I don't bother when I'm smoking."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners27.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                <i>
                 Silent but deadly...
                </i>
                a great slogan for the anti-secondhand smoke lobby! Of course, the reason (most) people scout the scene when flatulence is imminent is that silence can
                <i>
                 never
                </i>
                be assured. With that in mind, I suggest cigarette makers build tiny
                <b>
                 kazoo's
                </b>
                into each smoke.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  28) "The fire disappears beneath his shoe. Unfortunately, the butt still remains."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners28.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                While the manufacturers are installing those tiny kazoo's, have them mix some
                <b>
                 bubblegum
                </b>
                into the filter. That way, when the smoker steps on his discarded butt, it will remain... stuck to the bottom of his shoe. Dibs on both innovations, by the way.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  29) "When someone walks a step away from me, maybe it's a silent complaint about the cigarette in my hand."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <span class="inline inline-center">
                 <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners29.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                </span>
                <br/>
                Smokers seem to think their emanations smell just fine - that goes for their
                <i>
                 gas
                </i>
                as well. I'd like to avoid the former as well as the latter, wouldn't you?
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h3>
                 <b>
                  30) "Don't smoke in a crowd. Coats are expensive."
                 </b>
                </h3>
                <p>
                 <br/>
                 <span class="inline inline-center">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="309" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/manners30.gif" title="" width="435"/>
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 In the USA, you could replace the word
                 <i>
                  smoke
                 </i>
                 with
                 <i>
                  shoot
                 </i>
                 . Either way, i'm not sure the smoker/shooter would care overly much. We could ask why the smoker in this ad goes coatless  and hatless  but we might also wonder if this ad is rotated out during the summer; replaced by one that advises
                 <i>
                  skin is expensive
                 </i>
                 .
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span class="inline inline-right">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="200" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/smoking-is-annoying.png" title="" width="400"/>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 So concludes our third go-round showcasing numbers 21 through 30 of Japan Tobacco's
                 <b>
                  "Smoking Manners for Adults"
                 </b>
                 ads.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Bet you can't wait to see their
                 <i>
                  "Smoking Manners for Children"
                 </i>
                 campaign... this is Japan, after all, where
                 <a href="/articles/ten_bizarre_japanese_soft_drinks_5225" target="_blank">
                  Kids Beer
                 </a>
                 is advertised a-plenty!
                 <i>
                  (via
                  <a href="http://www.conbinibento.com/photos/index.php?gallery=./Smoking%20Manners" target="_blank">
                   Conbinibento
                  </a>
                  )
                 </i>
                </p>
