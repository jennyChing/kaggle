              <b class="whb">
               Build your own vertical trellis.
              </b>
              For a completely customized vertical garden structure, build your own out of wood. Building your own structure allows you to create a garden with the right dimensions for your yard. Here's a step-by-step guide for building a 6-foot high garden structure:
              <ul>
               <li>
                Build a 2 foot (0.6m) square by 11 inch (27.9cm) high box from redwood, cedar, or pressure-treated 2 x 6s. Screw 8-foot pressure-treated 2 x 2s at the center and one corner in back, and 6-foot 2 x 2s at the other corner and 18 inches (45.7cm) from the back on both sides.
               </li>
               <li>
                Pre-drill, glue and screw 1 x 2s every 6 inches (15.2cm), starting at the top of the box. Extend the 1 x 2s at least an inch beyond each 2 x 2 to minimize the danger of splitting.
               </li>
               <li>
                Add platforms by screwing on 3-foot 1 x 2s, then screwing on 1 x 2s laid flat to create a miniature deck.
               </li>
               <li>
                Add one more 6-foot long 2 x 2 in the center at the front, and screw it to the crosspieces.
               </li>
               <li>
                Add a 1 x 4 crosspiece at the top of the two longest 2 x 2s. Attach a bird house to the crosspiece.
               </li>
               <li>
                Fill the box with good soil, then plant climbing plants and train them up the lattice. Add other plants in pots and hanging baskets.
               </li>
              </ul>

           <div class="altblock">
           </div>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Starting_the_Garden">
            Method 3 of 3: Starting the Garden
           </span>
          </h3>
          <div class="section_text" id="steps_3">
           <ol class="steps_list_2">
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-7.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-7.jpg">

              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              1
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Pick a good spot.
              </b>
              <sup class="reference" id="_ref-4">
               <a href="#_note-4">
                [4]
               </a>
              </sup>
              A vertical garden, like any other type of garden, needs to be planted in an area with good soil drainage and the right combination of shade and sunlight. Figure out what conditions the plants you're growing need to thrive, then choose a suitable area in your yard or on your patio.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Most vertical gardens do well with a lot of sunlight.
               </li>
               <li>
                Pick a spot close to a wall if you want your garden to eventually climb the walls of your house.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">

               <img alt="Build a Vertical Garden Step 8.jpg" data-src="http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/d/dd/Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-8.jpg/670px-Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-8.jpg" height="503" id="55a3945d8c905" src="" width="670"/>

             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Make sure the garden structure is stable.
              </b>
              If you're using a trellis, a stake or a pole, be sure to bury it deep in the ground so that it doesn't wobble or fall over. Remember that the garden will get heavier as it grows, and it will need a solid foundation that can stand up to wind and rain.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-9.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-9.jpg">
               <img alt="Build a Vertical Garden Step 9.jpg" data-src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/d/d9/Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-9.jpg/670px-Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-9.jpg" height="503" id="55a3945d8ca82" src="" width="670"/>

             <div class="step_num">
              3
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Plant around the base of the structure.
              </b>
              Taking into account the individual needs of the plants you are using, plant seeds or seedlings in holes spaced around the base of the trellis, pole or other structure you are using. Make sure they aren't too close to each other or the structure so that they'll have room to root and grow.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>

             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">

              4
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               <a href="/Grow-Healthy-Plants" title="Grow Healthy Plants">
                Care for the plants
               </a>
               according to their needs.
              </b>
              Make sure they get adequate water and that you fertilize them, protect them with mulch, and take other measure they need to grow healthy and strong.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li class="final_li">
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-11.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Build-a-Vertical-Garden-Step-11.jpg">

              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              5
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Train the plants.
              </b>
              When the seedlings get a few inches high, it's time to train them to wrap around the structure you erected. Gently wrap the stems of the plants around the base of the structure, taking care not to bend or break them. Use ties to hold the stems in place as they grow.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Don't tie the stems too tightly. This prevents them from growing properly and may actually damage the plant.
               </li>
               <li>
                As the stems begin to grow and wind around the structure, continue using ties to train them to grow in the direction you want them to grow.
               </li>
              </ul>
