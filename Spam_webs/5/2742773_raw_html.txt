         <h1 class="post-title single-title" itemprop="name">
          5 Incredibly Easy Ways to Double Your Productivity
         </h1>
         <meta content="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-g9fNg3Jp17o/VFvSgc0I92I/AAAAAAAAAEc/TdCFMa4Uzdc/s1600/5%2BIncredibly%2BEasy%2BWays%2Bto%2BDouble%2BYour%2BProductivity.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>
         <meta content="4344307944570710983" itemprop="blogId"/>
         <meta content="7835854788560253046" itemprop="postId"/>
         <meta content="https://www.blogger.com/profile/16893393423320536898" itemprop="url"/>
         <a name="7835854788560253046">
         </a>
         <div class="meta">
          <div class="tags">
           <a href="http://www.hubtitle.com/search/label/Outdoors" rel="tag">
            Outdoors
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.hubtitle.com/search/label/Sticky%20News" rel="tag">

          </div>
          <div class="post-apps">
           <a class="zoom-text zoom-in-text" href="#A+">
            A
            <span>
             +
            </span>
           </a>
           <a class="zoom-text zoom-out-text" href="#A-">

         </div>
         <div class="post-single-body post-body" id="post-body-7835854788560253046" itemprop="description articleBody">
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: center;">
          <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <!-- 336*280 -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7968379591452850" data-ad-slot="4165288923" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Dont you wish that you could get more work done in less time? Well, you can.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          It is simple to double your productivity, anyone can do it, but that doesnt mean its easy. You have to change your habits and have a certain degree of self discipline. That is all there is to it.
         </div>
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-g9fNg3Jp17o/VFvSgc0I92I/AAAAAAAAAEc/TdCFMa4Uzdc/s1600/5%2BIncredibly%2BEasy%2BWays%2Bto%2BDouble%2BYour%2BProductivity.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img border="0" height="418" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-g9fNg3Jp17o/VFvSgc0I92I/AAAAAAAAAEc/TdCFMa4Uzdc/s1600/5%2BIncredibly%2BEasy%2BWays%2Bto%2BDouble%2BYour%2BProductivity.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Follow just one of these tips and you will find your productivity skyrocketing.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          1. Make a list
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          In order to stop procrastinating and to double your productivity, it is important to make a list.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Write down all the tasks you have to do and a time frame to do them in. You can do this as soon as you wake up, but it is better to do it the night before or even at the start of the week.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          When writing the list, ask yourself the question, If I could only get one task done, what would it be?
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          This will allow you to get straight down to work, instead of wasting valuable time wondering where you should begin.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Knowing what you have to do, and when you have to do it can at the very least double your productivity.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          2. Unplug
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          The internet, as useful as it is, is the biggest reason for procrastination out there. Checking your email, facebook, twitter, the news and the latest gossip might be entertaining, but it minimises your chances of getting much done.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Dont fall into this trap like so many others do. Simply unplug your internet connection.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          I realise that this might not be possible for everyone as you might need the internet for your work. If so, then exercise some self discipline. You could take a 10 minute break every hour to check your email, facebook etc.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Just doing this can dramatically increase the amount of work you get done, and can double your productivity.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          3. Take a day off
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          This might sound counter-intuitive, but take one day off work every week. Do anything you want that day apart from work.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          This will make sure you dont feel burnt out, and are able to work at your very best the next day.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Youve had a whole day off to do anything you want so you know that the next day you have to buckle down, and get some work done. Taking a day off serves as a great motivation tool and is an excellent way to double your productivity.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          4. Stop trying to make everything perfect
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Im sure youve all been there before. You spend all day trying to make something perfect, trying to find the perfect colour for your website, trying to find the perfect style of font, trying to make the margins of something you just printed perfectly symmetrical etc leaving no time to start anything else.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          The more time you spend trying to make something perfect, the less time you will have to dedicate to other tasks.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Instead of trying to make something perfect, make it good enough. Good enough is good enough! You can then move onto other tasks that need doing.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Remember that you can always come back to a task to make it perfect. Just not until everything else is finished though!
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          5. Single-task
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          In order to really be able to focus, you must work on one task at a time.
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          Work on the one task that is more important than the others, and dont let anything distract you. Give your full attention to it, and dont start another task until the one you began is finished. If the phone rings, let it go to voicemail, if you receive an email, wait until you take a break to check it, if people constantly knock on your door  put up a do not disturb sign etc.
         </div>

		settings = jQuery.extend({
		travelocity: 0.07
		}, settings);		
		return this.each(function(){
				var $strip = jQuery(this);
				$strip.addClass("newsticker")
				var stripWidth = 1;
				$strip.find("li").each(function(i){
				stripWidth += jQuery(this, i).outerWidth(true); // thanks to Michael Haszprunar and Fabien Volpi
				});

				var totalTravel = stripWidth+containerWidth;
				var defTiming = totalTravel/settings.travelocity;	// thanks to Scott Waye		
				function scrollnews(spazio, tempo){
				$strip.animate({left: '-='+ spazio}, tempo, "linear", function(){$strip.css("left", containerWidth); scrollnews(totalTravel, defTiming);});
				}
				scrollnews(totalTravel, defTiming);				
				$strip.hover(function(){
				jQuery(this).stop();
				},
				function(){
				var offset = jQuery(this).offset();
				var residualSpace = offset.left + stripWidth;
				var residualTime = residualSpace/settings.travelocity;
				scrollnews(residualSpace, residualTime);
				});			
		});
