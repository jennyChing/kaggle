     <p>
      <b>
       Stanisaw Kiecal
      </b>
      (September 14, 1886  October 15, 1910), better known in the
      <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">
       boxing
      </a>
      world as
      <b>
       Stanley Ketchel
      </b>
      , was a
      <a href="/wiki/Polish_American" title="Polish American">
       Polish American
      </a>
      professional
      <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">
       boxer
      </a>
      who became one of the greatest World
      <a href="/wiki/Middleweight" title="Middleweight">
       Middleweight
      </a>
      Champions in history. He was nicknamed "The Michigan Assassin." He was murdered at a ranch in Missouri at the age of 24.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Ketchel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stanley_Ketchel_LOC.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3645" data-file-width="3169" height="253" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Stanley_Ketchel_LOC.jpg/220px-Stanley_Ketchel_LOC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Stanley_Ketchel_LOC.jpg/330px-Stanley_Ketchel_LOC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Stanley_Ketchel_LOC.jpg/440px-Stanley_Ketchel_LOC.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stanley_Ketchel_LOC.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Ketchel in fighting pose
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      He was born in 1886 in
      <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">
       Grand Rapids, Michigan
      </a>
      to Tomasz Kiecal and Julia Kiecal (ne Olbinska), whose family immigrated from the village of Sulmierzyce in
      <a href="/wiki/Piotrk%C3%B3w_Trybunalski" title="Piotrkw Trybunalski">
       Piotrkw Trybunalski
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guberniya" title="Guberniya">
       Guberniya
      </a>
      in modern day central
      <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">
       Poland
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He avoided school, instead falling in with a gang of street kids and often getting into fist fights. At twelve years old, he ran away from home, becoming a child hobo. As a teenager he lived in Butte, Montana, where he found employment first as a hotel bellhop and then as a bouncer. This profession obviously led to many scraps that established his reputation as the best fist fighter in town. Soon enough sixteen-year-old Stanley was performing in backroom boxing matches with older locals for twenty dollars a week. He began traveling throughout Montana, offering to take on any man brave enough to face him. Between 1903 and 1906, he lost just twice in thirty-nine contests and, in 1907, moved to California, where he knew most of boxing's big names and big fights waited for him.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_boxing_career">
       Professional boxing career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Ketchel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional boxing career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Only a
      <a href="/wiki/Middleweight" title="Middleweight">
       middleweight
      </a>
      , Ketchel was also known for taking on
      <a href="/wiki/Heavyweight" title="Heavyweight">
       heavyweights
      </a>
      , who sometimes outweighed him by more than 30 pounds (14kg). Ketchel used a very unusual method in his fights. He had a very close and loving relationship with his mother. It is rumored that before each of his fights, he would imagine that his opponent had insulted his mother; thus, he would be fighting with almost insane fury.
     </p>
     <p>
      He started boxing professionally in 1904 in
      <a href="/wiki/Butte,_Montana" title="Butte, Montana">
       Butte, Montana
      </a>
      . In his first fight, Ketchel knocked out
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kid_Tracy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kid Tracy (page does not exist)">
       Kid Tracy
      </a>
      in one round. In his second fight, he was beaten by decision in six rounds by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maurice_Thompson_(boxer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maurice Thompson (boxer) (page does not exist)">
       Maurice Thompson
      </a>
      . He boxed his first 41 bouts in Montana, and had a record of 36 wins, two losses and three draws during that span. He lost once more to Thompson in their rematch and then controversially drew with him in their
      <a href="/wiki/Rubber_match" title="Rubber match">
       rubber match
      </a>
      , in a bout that many people thought Ketchel had won. Afterwards, he would then go onto beat
      <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kingsley" title="Tom Kingsley">
       Tom Kingsley
      </a>
      , among others, before moving his campaign on to
      <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">
       California
      </a>
      in 1907.
     </p>
     <p>
      There, he won three fights that year, and drew one in
      <a href="/wiki/Marysville,_California" title="Marysville, California">
       Marysville
      </a>
      against the man many considered the World Middleweight Champion,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Thomas_(boxer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joe Thomas (boxer) (page does not exist)">
       Joe Thomas
      </a>
      . In his next bout, he and Thomas had a rematch and Ketchel won, by knockout in 32 rounds. Ketchel was then recognized by many as the World Middleweight Champion. He finished the year by beating Thomas again, this time by decision.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Middleweight_Champion">
       Middleweight Champion
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Ketchel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Middleweight Champion">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stanley_Ketchel_vs._Billy_Papke_III.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="489" data-file-width="640" height="229" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Stanley_Ketchel_vs._Billy_Papke_III.jpg/300px-Stanley_Ketchel_vs._Billy_Papke_III.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Stanley_Ketchel_vs._Billy_Papke_III.jpg/450px-Stanley_Ketchel_vs._Billy_Papke_III.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Stanley_Ketchel_vs._Billy_Papke_III.jpg/600px-Stanley_Ketchel_vs._Billy_Papke_III.jpg 2x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stanley_Ketchel_vs._Billy_Papke_III.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Ketchel standing over a downed
        <a href="/wiki/Billy_Papke" title="Billy Papke">
         Billy Papke
        </a>
        during their third fight
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      On February 8, 1908, Ketchel met the man who was generally recognized as the World Welterweight Champion and one of the leading middleweights of the era,
      <a href="/wiki/Mike_%22Twin%22_Sullivan" title='Mike "Twin" Sullivan'>
       Mike "Twin" Sullivan
      </a>
      , knocking him out in the first round and winning general recognition as World Middleweight Champion. Sullivan often fought above the Welterweight limit, making him a light Middleweight. Whether Ketchel became world champion when he defeated Thomas or Mike Sullivan has always been up to debate, but the fact remains that it is Mike Sullivan and not Thomas who is historically remembered as a world champion.
     </p>
     <p>
      He proceeded to retain the title against Mike's twin brother, Jack "Twin" Sullivan, also a former world champion, by a
      <a href="/wiki/Knockout" title="Knockout">
       knockout
      </a>
      in 20 rounds, against future world champion
      <a href="/wiki/Billy_Papke" title="Billy Papke">
       Billy Papke
      </a>
      by decision in 10, against Hugo Kelly by a knockout in three and against Thomas, by a knockout in two.
     </p>
     <p>
      Then, he lost the belt to Papke by a knockout in twelve, but he and Papke had an immediate rematch and Ketchel regained the title when he beat Papke by a knockout in eleven in their third match.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ketchel began 1909 by fighting reigning
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_Heavyweight" title="Light Heavyweight">
       Light Heavyweight
      </a>
      Champion
      <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Jack_O%27Brien" title="Philadelphia Jack O'Brien">
       Philadelphia Jack O'Brien
      </a>
      . Ketchel survived a terrible beating at the hand of the slick, quick O'Brien in the early rounds only to mount a terrific comeback and score four knockdowns in the ninth and tenth rounds. When the final bell rang at the end of the tenth round, O'Brien was lying unconscious on the mat, his head in a resin box in his corner. Under New York rules at the time, though, O'Brien had been saved by the bell and because official decisions were outlawed in New York boxing the fight was declared a "No Decision." A few weeks later, Ketchel had a rematch with O' Brien, knocking out Philadelphia Jack in three rounds.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A fourth fight with Billy Papke followed. Ketchel again won in a tumultuous slugfest to defend his championship and end their series of fights with a record of 3-1 in their four encounters. This (fourth) fight took place in the outdoor Mission Street Arena in Colma, California, during a terrible thunderstorm, yet neither fighter relented in his pursuit of victory until Stanley took the twenty-round decision.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Ketchel fought
      <a href="/wiki/Sam_Langford" title="Sam Langford">
       Sam Langford
      </a>
      on April 27, 1910. It was a hard pressed fight by both men, each displaying terrific hitting power for all six rounds of the short bout. No knock downs were scored and both had plenty of energy in the end. Langford won by decision. A longer rematch bout was rumored, but never happened. Some disputed the decision, although a majority of people felt that Langford had won the bout, which following a decision-appealing vote, it was decided (in a non-controversial manner) that it would stand as a decision win for Langford.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Ketchel_vs._Johnson">
       Ketchel vs. Johnson
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Ketchel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ketchel vs. Johnson">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Ketchel's battle with
      <a href="/wiki/Jack_Johnson_(boxer)" title="Jack Johnson (boxer)">
       Jack Johnson
      </a>
      has been called by many a modern day "David and Goliath."
     </p>
     <p>
      In the 12th round Ketchel floored Johnson with a right hand. Johnson got up and knocked out Ketchel with a right uppercut.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dWs75YlzEHE_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-dWs75YlzEHE-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Ketchel showed no fear against his larger and stronger foe. He was knocked down several times in the fight and was punished yet kept coming back. Johnson said to his trainer seconds between rounds "That man isn't human." In round twelve of that fight, Ketchel reached Johnson with a right to the chin that sent Johnson to the canvas.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dWs75YlzEHE_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-dWs75YlzEHE-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The punch shocked Johnson on two levels. One, it came from a much smaller Ketchel. Two, it was rumored that Ketchel and Johnson, when they agreed to the fight, agreed to take the fight to the full 20 rounds and Ketchel would allow Johnson to win in the 20th. The reason for this was each man was interested in making as much money off the fight as possible. A 20 round fight would guarantee boxing fans would pay to go to local theatres to watch the replay of the fight. When Johnson deviated from the plan of "no blood should be drawn," Ketchel, already bloodied, knocked Johnson down, then, in the 12th, Ketchel faced the unmitigated wrath of Jack Johnson.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dWs75YlzEHE_5-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-dWs75YlzEHE-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Upon regaining his feet, Jack Johnson knocked out Ketchel with a blow full in the mouth. Ketchel did not wake up for many minutes and some of his teeth were knocked out by the blow, some imbedded in Johnson's glove.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Murder">
       Murder
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Ketchel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Murder">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stanley_Ketchel_Gravestone_LOC.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="528" data-file-width="443" height="262" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Stanley_Ketchel_Gravestone_LOC.jpg/220px-Stanley_Ketchel_Gravestone_LOC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Stanley_Ketchel_Gravestone_LOC.jpg/330px-Stanley_Ketchel_Gravestone_LOC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Stanley_Ketchel_Gravestone_LOC.jpg/440px-Stanley_Ketchel_Gravestone_LOC.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stanley_Ketchel_Gravestone_LOC.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Ketchel's Gravestone
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The following year, 1910, Ketchel fought six times (including one exhibition), but his fast living had worn him down.
     </p>
     <p>
      Hoping for a rematch with Jack Johnson, Ketchel moved to the ranch of his friend, R.P. Dickerson, near (on what is now referred to as Dickerson Ranch Road)
      <a href="/wiki/Conway,_Missouri" title="Conway, Missouri">
       Conway, Missouri
      </a>
      , where he had hoped to regain his strength. Dickerson had just hired a cook, Goldie Smith, and a ranch hand, who Smith said was her husband, Walter Kurtz.
     </p>
     <p>
      Walter Kurtz turned out to be Walter Dipley. Walter Dipley and Goldie Smith were not married and, in fact, had just met each other a month before Dickerson had hired them.
     </p>
     <p>
      After being upbraided by the "Michigan Assassin" for beating a horse on the morning of October 14, Dipley decided to get even with Ketchel by robbing him. The following morning, Smith seated Ketchel at the breakfast table with his back to the door and Dipley, armed with a .22 caliber rifle, came up behind him and shouted, "Get your hands up!" Ketchel stood up and as he turned around, Dipley shot him. The bullet traveled from his shoulder into his lung and Ketchel fell to the floor mortally wounded. Dipley then took Ketchel's handgun and smashed Ketchel in the face with it. At the same time, Smith rifled Ketchel's pockets for his money.
     </p>
     <p>
      After promising to meet Goldie Smith later that night, Dipley ran from the ranch.
     </p>
     <p>
      Unaware that, as he lay dying, Ketchel told the former ranch foreman, C.E. Bailey, that Goldie Smith had robbed him, she told police officers that Ketchel had raped her and that that was the reason Dipley shot him. Her story fell apart and she admitted her complicity in the robbery but stated she did not know Dipley was going to kill the former champion.
     </p>
     <p>
      In an effort to save the young fighter's life, R.P. Dickerson chartered a special train to take Stanley Ketchel to a hospital in
      <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Missouri" title="Springfield, Missouri">
       Springfield, Missouri
      </a>
      . But Ketchel died at approximately 7 o'clock that night His last words were: "I'm so tired. Take me home to mother."
     </p>
     <p>
      Dickerson also offered a $5,000 dead or alive reward (preferably dead) for Dipley, who was captured at a neighboring farmhouse the next day.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath_of_Murder">
       Aftermath of Murder
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Ketchel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath of Murder">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Both Walter Dipley and Goldie Smith were found guilty of murder and robbery at a jury trial in January 1911 and both were given a life sentence. Goldie Smith had her murder conviction overturned and she served 17 months for the robbery. Walter Dipley served 23 years before he was paroled. He died in 1956, 22 years after his release from prison.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">
       Legacy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Ketchel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legacy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Ketchel was buried at Holy Cross Cemetery at
      <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">
       Grand Rapids, Michigan
      </a>
      . His funeral was the most attended until the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ford" title="Ford">
       Ford
      </a>
      family surpassed him during the 20th century. There is a plaque in his honor at the corner of Stocking Ave and 3rd St.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ketchel is now enshrined in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Boxing_Hall_Of_Fame" title="International Boxing Hall Of Fame">
       International Boxing Hall Of Fame
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Ring_(magazine)" title="The Ring (magazine)">
        The Ring
       </a>
      </i>
      Magazine in 2004 ranked Ketchel as the eighth greatest
      <a href="/wiki/Middleweight" title="Middleweight">
       middleweight
      </a>
      of all time, behind
      <a href="/wiki/Harry_Greb" title="Harry Greb">
       Harry Greb
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Ray_Robinson" title="Sugar Ray Robinson">
       Sugar Ray Robinson
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carlos_Monzon" title="Carlos Monzon">
       Carlos Monzon
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marvin_Hagler" title="Marvin Hagler">
       Marvin Hagler
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jake_LaMotta" title="Jake LaMotta">
       Jake LaMotta
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Charley_Burley" title="Charley Burley">
       Charley Burley
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Flowers" title="Tiger Flowers">
       Tiger Flowers
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2004,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ring_Magazine" title="Ring Magazine">
       <i>
        Ring
       </i>
       magazine
      </a>
      named Ketchel #6 on their list, 100 Best Punchers of All Time.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Nat_Fleischer" title="Nat Fleischer">
       Nat Fleischer
      </a>
      , the late ring historian and founding editor of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ring_magazine" title="The Ring magazine">
       The Ring magazine
      </a>
      , considered Stanley to be the greatest middleweight in history.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He had a record of 51 wins, four losses, four draws, 1 no contest and four no decisions (Newspaper Decisions: 2-1-1), with 48 wins by knockout. He was the first Middleweight Champion to regain the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_title" title="World title">
       world title
      </a>
      after losing it.
     </p>
     <p>
      Subject of
      <i>
       The Killings of Stanley Ketchel
      </i>
      , a novel by
      <a href="/wiki/James_Carlos_Blake" title="James Carlos Blake">
       James Carlos Blake
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Subject of the short story "The Light of the World" by Ernest Hemingway.
