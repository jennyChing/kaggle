     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Thief of Time
       </b>
      </i>
      is the 26th
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Discworld" title="Discworld">
        Discworld
       </a>
      </i>
      <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">
       novel
      </a>
      written by
      <a href="/wiki/Terry_Pratchett" title="Terry Pratchett">
       Terry Pratchett
      </a>
      , a 2002 Locus Award nominee.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WWE-2002_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-WWE-2002-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was the last Discworld novel with a cover by
      <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kirby" title="Josh Kirby">
       Josh Kirby
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">
       Plot summary
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thief_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auditors_of_Reality" title="Auditors of Reality">
       The Auditors
      </a>
      are upset because the human race (although this appears to include all races on the
      <a href="/wiki/Discworld_(world)" title="Discworld (world)">
       Discworld
      </a>
      , not just the humans) are living their lives in - what the Auditors consider to be - an unpredictable (and therefore not understandable) way. To fix this matter once and for all, they decide to convince a young clock maker,
      <a href="/wiki/Technology_of_the_Discworld#Jeremy_Clockson" title="Technology of the Discworld">
       Jeremy Clockson
      </a>
      , in
      <a href="/wiki/Ankh-Morpork" title="Ankh-Morpork">
       Ankh-Morpork
      </a>
      to build a perfect glass clock. They do not reveal that this will imprison
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphic_personifications_(Discworld)#Time" title="Anthropomorphic personifications (Discworld)">
       Time
      </a>
      (the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphic_personifications_(Discworld)" title="Anthropomorphic personifications (Discworld)">
       anthropomorphic personification
      </a>
      ) and thereby freeze
      <a href="/wiki/Time" title="Time">
       time
      </a>
      (the physical quantity) on the
      <a href="/wiki/Discworld_(world)" title="Discworld (world)">
       Discworld
      </a>
      . By freezing time, the Auditors intend to eliminate the unpredictability that humans cause through their everyday actions (and have enough time, for once, to file all the paperwork).
      <a href="/wiki/Death_(Discworld)" title="Death (Discworld)">
       Death
      </a>
      discovers their plans, but is unable to act directly because he cannot see - and interact with -
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphic_personifications_(Discworld)#Time" title="Anthropomorphic personifications (Discworld)">
       Time
      </a>
      (the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphic_personifications_(Discworld)" title="Anthropomorphic personifications (Discworld)">
       anthropomorphic personification
      </a>
      ). Instead, Death sends his granddaughter
      <a href="/wiki/Susan_Sto_Helit" title="Susan Sto Helit">
       Susan
      </a>
      to stop them, assisted (apparently) by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_of_Rats" title="Death of Rats">
       Death of Rats
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Death_(Discworld)#Quoth" title="Death (Discworld)">
       Quoth
      </a>
      the
      <a href="/wiki/Raven" title="Raven">
       Raven
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Meanwhile, in a distant valley, a young apprentice of the
      <a href="/wiki/History_Monks" title="History Monks">
       History Monks
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/History_Monks#Lobsang_Ludd" title="History Monks">
       Lobsang Ludd
      </a>
      , is apprenticed to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lu-Tze" title="Lu-Tze">
       Lu-Tze
      </a>
      , known throughout the Oi Dong Monastery as 'The Sweeper'. Following a highly impressive patch-up job with time (the physical quantity) by Lobsang, he and Lu-Tze are brought before the Abbot, where they hear that a glass clock is being built. Lu-Tze knows of such a clock's side-effects, since he was sent to prevent a previous clock from being built in
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%9Cberwald_(Discworld)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="berwald (Discworld) (page does not exist)">
       berwald
      </a>
      . However, due to the difficulties inherent in berwald (including the difficulty in determining which specific bolt of
      <a href="/wiki/Lightning" title="Lightning">
       lightning
      </a>
      hitting which
      <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">
       castle
      </a>
      might start the glass clock) he failed to stop the original - but Time only froze for a moment before a metal
      <a href="/wiki/Spring_(device)" title="Spring (device)">
       spring
      </a>
      snapped and caused the clock to shatter. Having figured out that the new glass clock would be likely built in Ankh-Morpork, Lu-Tze and Lobsang head for the city to try to stop Jeremy from building it.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Auditors end up using one of their own as an agent when contacting Jeremy.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auditors_of_Reality#Myria_LeJean_.2F_Unity" title="Auditors of Reality">
       Myria LeJean
      </a>
      takes a human form and becomes quite disturbed by "her" experiences as "she" becomes more human and individual, as opposed to the collective Auditors. As she begins to understand more about humans, she opposes the activation of the clock and finds ways to delay its creation. This annoys the other Auditors who create human bodies of their own and - naming themselves after
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colour" title="Colour">
       colours
      </a>
      - they prevent Myria from smashing the clock with a hammer, and organise a localised
      <a href="/wiki/Storm" title="Storm">
       storm
      </a>
      , a bolt of lightning hits Jeremy Clockson's workshop and the clock is started. Lobsang Ludd is just smashing his way through the window of the workshop at the moment the clock starts, having been delayed when he went back to help Lu-Tze, but he is too late to stop it
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Week_z00.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Week_z00.jpg/220px-Week_z00.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Week_z00.jpg/330px-Week_z00.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Week_z00.jpg/440px-Week_z00.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Week_z00.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Myria uses absurd signs to confuse and slow down the Auditors.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      However, Lobsang is able to continue moving. He meets Susan Sto Helit (whose own attempts to stop the clock being started were less successful) and finds out after his time-storer stops spinning, that, as a creature not limited by time, he was not affected by the starting of the glass clock. Meeting up with Myria LeJean, they work to correct the problem and fight the Auditors. Since Myria is no longer part of the Collective
      <a href="/wiki/Group_mind_(science_fiction)" title="Group mind (science fiction)">
       hive mind
      </a>
      of the Auditors, Susan suggests she change her name to Unity - which she likes.
     </p>
     <p>
      Susan and the newly named Unity discover that Lobsang Ludd and Jeremy Clockson seem to share an astonishing mental connection. When Lobsang speaks, Jeremy (who was knocked unconscious by the lightning that started the clock and whose body was moved away from the Auditors by Myria/Unity) attempts to form the same words, despite his
      <a href="/wiki/Coma" title="Coma">
       comatose
      </a>
      state. Susan is able to shed light on this when she reveals that she met the
      <a href="/wiki/Midwife" title="Midwife">
       midwife
      </a>
      who brought Lobsang and Jeremy into the world - none other than the "best midwife in the world" -
      <a href="/wiki/Nanny_Ogg" title="Nanny Ogg">
       Nanny Ogg
      </a>
      . Nanny has told her that she delivered two boys an instant apart and Susan realises that both Lobsang and Jeremy are the same person.
     </p>
     <p>
      Lobsang and Jeremy become infused, although they are nothing more than blue mist to begin with. Susan continues to call the mist Lobsang, since she is told that "Lobsang had the better memories". Using
      <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">
       chocolate
      </a>
      , Susan, Unity and a re-animated Lu-Tze kill a number of Auditors (the taste sensation of chocolate literally blows them away) and Lobsang is able to destroy the clock, freeing Time and unfreezing time. He then meets with his mother, Time, and his father, Wen the Eternally Surprised (the first Abbot of Oi Dong monastery and founder of the History Monks) before effectively taking over Time's job. He attempts to learn Lu-Tze's fifth surprise (an ongoing theme in the novel as the sweeper refuses to tell him), after which he is defeated in the iron dojo by Lu-Tze, but passes his training anyway and becomes a sweeper. He asks Lu-Tze's advice about Susan and he returns to Ankh-Morpork, where the two have an unspecified "perfect moment."
     </p>
     <p>
      Throughout the story, Death is trying to round-up the other Horsemen of the
      <a href="/wiki/Apocalypse" title="Apocalypse">
       Apocalypse
      </a>
      , War, Famine and Pestilence, none of whom want to join up (compare with
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sourcery" title="Sourcery">
        Sourcery
       </a>
      </i>
      when the Four Horsemen of the "Apocralypse" ride out, but having stopped off at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_house" title="Public house">
       pub
      </a>
      three of them have their Horses stolen). The Fifth Horseman - Ronnie Soak (originally called Kaos - Soak spelled backwards - as in
      <a href="/wiki/Chaos_(cosmogony)" title="Chaos (cosmogony)">
       Chaos
      </a>
      )
      <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Beatle" title="Fifth Beatle">
       who left before they became famous
      </a>
      - agrees to join Death in the fight against the Auditors, and the other three also arrive as the battle prepares to start. Kaos is useful due to his sword of chaos, which goes against the rules and though flaming, is used to keep his milk cool. An Angel with an Iron book appears during the battle. It is not explained who won the fight, but since Death (at least) appears in books set chronologically later it can only be assumed that the Horsemen triumphed.
     </p>
