     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Robot Unicorn Attack
       </b>
      </i>
      is an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Online_video_game" title="Online video game">
       online
      </a>
      "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endless_running" title="Endless running">
       endless running
      </a>
      "
      <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">
       video game
      </a>
      featured on the
      <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">
       Adult Swim
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Flashline_Games" title="Flashline Games">
       Flashline Games
      </a>
      website. The game was produced by
      <i>
       Spiritonin Media Games
      </i>
      and was released in February 4, 2010. The game's soundtrack is the 1994 song "
      <a href="/wiki/Always_(Erasure_song)" title="Always (Erasure song)">
       Always
      </a>
      ," by the British band
      <a href="/wiki/Erasure" title="Erasure">
       Erasure
      </a>
      , in its "2009 mix" version.
     </p>
     <p>
      With one million plays within the first week of its release,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BWP_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BWP-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       Robot Unicorn Attack
      </i>
      is one of the most popular and most played games featured on Adult Swim. As a result of its popularity, Adult Swim has made official
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merchandise" title="Merchandise">
       merchandise
      </a>
      for the game, and has released it on the
      <a href="/wiki/App_Store_(iOS)" title="App Store (iOS)">
       App Store
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Google_Play" title="Google Play">
       Google Play
      </a>
      . Adult Swim released three followups to
      <i>
       Robot Unicorn Attack
      </i>
      , subtitled
      <i>
       Heavy Metal
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Christmas Edition
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       Evolution
      </i>
      respectively.
     </p>
     <p>
      A sequel titled
      <i>
       Robot Unicorn Attack 2
      </i>
      was developed by
      <a href="/wiki/PikPok" title="PikPok">
       PikPok
      </a>
      and released on iOS on April 25, 2013, and Android on July 12, 2013.
     </p>

     <p>
      <i>
       Robot Unicorn Attack
      </i>
      is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sidescrolling" title="Sidescrolling">
       sidescrolling
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Platform_game" title="Platform game">
       platform game
      </a>
      in which the user controls the movement of a
      <a href="/wiki/Robot" title="Robot">
       robotic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Unicorn" title="Unicorn">
       unicorn
      </a>
      in a manner similar to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Canabalt" title="Canabalt">
        Canabalt
       </a>
      </i>
      , a game released in 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eurogamer_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Eurogamer-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The object of the game is to prolong gameplay without falling off the stage, crashing into the edges of platforms, or colliding with crystal stars (without first dashing). Points are earned with play time, by collecting
      <a href="/wiki/Pixie" title="Pixie">
       pixies
      </a>
      , and by destroying crystal stars by dashing through them. As the game progresses, the stage slides faster. Jumps and dashes can be chained together while the unicorn is airborne. The player has three lives (referred to as "wishes"), and the sum of the scores from each life count for the player's final score.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Evolution">
       <i>
        Evolution
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robot_Unicorn_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Evolution">

     <p>
      <i>
       Robot Unicorn Attack Evolution
      </i>
      plays identically to the original, but after three stars are broken in a row (four in a row in the Facebook version), the Robot Unicorn evolves into other robotic creatures. There are also multiple fairies per platform. In the Facebook version, the player must keep breaking stars in order to transform to the next animal; missing a star will cause the animal to revert back to the unicorn.

      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">
       Sequel
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robot_Unicorn_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sequel">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A sequel titled
      <i>
       Robot Unicorn Attack 2
      </i>
      was developed by
      <a href="/wiki/PikPok" title="PikPok">
       PikPok
      </a>
      and released on iOS on April 25, 2013, and Android on July 12, 2013.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      This sequel expands upon the original's template by adding new maneuvers like the "Rainbow Savior" and "Gallow's Gallop." It also introduces enemies, missions, and the option to customise your unicorn's appearance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The game also introduces the ability to fly by equipping wings that can be unlocked in-game. The core gameplay and controls remain the same as in the original browser game, but the game also adds a number of new features.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (January 2015)">
          original research?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      The player can collect teardrops that can be used to customize his or her character. Missions can be completed to unlock new content and abilities. The game also adds enemies, including giant golems. Egyptian- and medieval-themed unicorns were introduced in an update, as well as the new threat of solar geysers.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      The visuals also received a makeover. The unicorn itself is much more detailed and fluid, and the painted backgrounds also have much more depth and detail.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The newest update introduced a Celestial Unicorn, as well as a new Lava course available for purchase with real-world currency.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      This is the first
      <i>
       Robot Unicorn Attack
      </i>
      to include in-game purchases; the player can buy credits to upgrade his or her unicorn and additional sound-packs.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Erasure" title="Erasure">
       Erasure
      </a>
      's seminal song "
      <a href="/wiki/Always_(Erasure_song)" title="Always (Erasure song)">
       Always
      </a>
      "  which was a main part of the original game  is not part of the default package and must be purchased separately for $0.99.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      "I will never be able to separate Always from Robot Unicorn Attack," wrote
      <a href="/wiki/Kieron_Gillen" title="Kieron Gillen">
       Kieron Gillen
      </a>
      in 2010. "I can't even imagine wanting to do such a thing. It'd be like decapitating the Mona Lisa. It merges with the sparkles of sound effects and the explosions of light and makes it complete."
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      The song is not included with the base game due to copyright, and as Adult Swim wanted the game to be free-to-play, it was made into an in-game purchase.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Other songs by such bands as
      <a href="/wiki/Blind_Guardian" title="Blind Guardian">
       Blind Guardian
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Slade" title="Slade">
       Slade
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Limahl" title="Limahl">
       Limahl
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Corey_Hart_(singer)" title="Corey Hart (singer)">
       Corey Hart
      </a>
      are also available for purchase.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">
       Critical reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robot_Unicorn_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Critical reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Ivan Williams of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/1UP.com" title="1UP.com">
        1UP.com
       </a>
      </i>
      stated that, "Whether it's the song constantly on an [sic] loop or the simple desire to get a better score than the millions of other gamers playing, I challenge anyone to play Robot Unicorn Attack and not have that game pop into your head every now and then."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In reviews of the major flash games of 2010,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Eurogamer" title="Eurogamer">
        Eurogamer
       </a>
      </i>
      writer
      <a href="/wiki/Kieron_Gillen" title="Kieron Gillen">
       Kieron Gillen
      </a>
      said, "Like a comet made of gold, glitter and Lady Gaga's eyelashes, Robot Unicorn Attack circled the Earth and filled the firmament with its irresistible radiance for the whole of 2010. It changed lives. It challenged sexualities. It involves pressing two buttons. It is undoubtedly the greatest game of all time which features a Robot Unicorn, unless you're a metalhead who digs its sequel."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eurogamer_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Eurogamer-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Scott Sharkey of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/UGO_Networks" title="UGO Networks">
        UGO Networks
       </a>
      </i>
      said that while "the aesthetic is a good gag for a few minutes", the important point of Robot Unicorn Attack is that "the game itself is addictive enough to last much, much longer. At least, until someone catches you playing and ribs you about it for the next week or so."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Neon Kelly, Previews Editor of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/VideoGamer.com" title="VideoGamer.com">
        VideoGamer.com
       </a>
      </i>
      , concluded after playing that "Somehow the whole thing ends up being extremely addictive - despite the fact that the game's tongue is so firmly wedged in its cheek that it's in danger of giving itself permanent facial damage. If you've not yet done so, I heartily urge you to go try it."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Toby Green of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">
        The Independent
       </a>
      </i>
      wrote in a short review that the game was "Great fun", giving it four out of five stars.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In a review of the iPhone version of the game,
      <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">
       CNN
      </a>
      writer Topher Kohan concluded, "Easy-to-use controls, great soundtrack, the ability to turn the sound off and get useful feedback via vibrate and fun in-game tidbits. This feels like a game you'll put on your phone, then pull out to play again and again."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In a twin review for the
      <a href="/wiki/Australian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Australian Broadcasting Corporation">
       Australian Broadcasting Corporation
      </a>
      by reviewers
      <a href="/wiki/Stephanie_Bendixsen" title="Stephanie Bendixsen">
       Stephanie Bendixsen
      </a>
      ("Hex") and
      <a href="/wiki/Steven_O%27Donnell_(Australian_actor)" title="Steven O'Donnell (Australian actor)">
       Steven O'Donnell
      </a>
      ("Bajo"), Hex finished by saying, ""This game is utterly
      <i>
       riDONKulous
      </i>
      , so I'm giving it the utterly ridonkulous score of 8971. I can't wait to press Z to chase my dreams again", to which reviewer Bajo responded, "I'm not sure how to score it after that."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">

        </span>
        23
        <span>

        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
