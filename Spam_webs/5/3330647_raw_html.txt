          <h1 class="firstHeading" itemprop="name">
           <a href="http://www.wikihow.com/Tell-if-a-Dog-Is-in-Pain" itemprop="url">
            How to Tell if a Dog Is in Pain
           </a>
          </h1>
          <p class="sp_method_toc" id="method_toc">
           <span>
            Two Parts:
           </span>
           <a class="" href="#Looking_for_Bodily_Changes_sub">
            Looking for Bodily Changes
           </a>
           <a class="" href="#Looking_for_Behavioral_changes_sub">
            Looking for Behavioral changes
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Dog's are generally thought to have a higher tolerance for pain than people. This can make it hard to to tell when a dog is in pain. Some dogs will show you they are in pain, while others can be very stoic and make that determination difficult.
           <sup class="reference" id="_ref-1">
            <a href="#_note-1">
             [1]
            </a>
           </sup>
           Many times dogs will hide their pain, if possible, as a natural survival mechanism. Even so, there are many ways of determining if your dog is in pain. The sooner you can make this determination, the sooner you can seek help. This can help to keep minor problems from turning into major ones.
          </p>

             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Beware of biting.
              </b>
              Pain can change a dog's behavior. Even a friendly dog in great pain will be more likely to bite.
              <sup class="reference" id="_ref-9">
               <a href="#_note-9">
                [9]
               </a>
              </sup>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Even a dog that has never bitten before may bite when approached if it is experiencing significant pain.
               </li>
               <li>
                A dog in pain may also bite if you touch or move a painful area.
                <sup class="reference" id="_ref-10">
                 <a href="#_note-10">
                  [10]
                 </a>
                </sup>
                The natural response, when a painful area is touched, is to turn toward that area. The dog may try to bite out of instinct.
               </li>
               <li>
                You may first see warning signs such as growling. A dog on the verge of biting may pin its ears back or show its teeth. This is a natural protection mechanism that a dog may use to try and prevent further pain.
                <sup class="reference" id="_ref-11">
                 <a href="#_note-11">
                  [11]
                 </a>
                </sup>
               </li>
              </ul>

               <a href="#_note-12">
                [12]

              </ul>

             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Tell-if-a-Dog-Is-in-Pain-Step-5.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Tell-if-a-Dog-Is-in-Pain-Step-5.jpg">
               <img alt="Tell if a Dog Is in Pain Step 5.jpg" data-src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/1/14/Tell-if-a-Dog-Is-in-Pain-Step-5.jpg/670px-Tell-if-a-Dog-Is-in-Pain-Step-5.jpg" height="503" id="55a3fe7bef9dd" src="" width="670"/>
               <script>
                var img = document.getElementById('55a3fe7bef9dd');
defer.add(img);
               </script>
              </a>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="Tell if a Dog Is in Pain Step 5.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/1/14/Tell-if-a-Dog-Is-in-Pain-Step-5.jpg/670px-Tell-if-a-Dog-Is-in-Pain-Step-5.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              6
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Look for avoidance behaviors.
              </b>
              Avoidance behaviors, such as hiding or shying away from contact with people or other animals are common when a dog is in pain.
              <sup class="reference" id="_ref-20">
               <a href="#_note-20">
                [20]
               </a>
              </sup>
              These behaviors are efforts to avoid potentially painful situations.
              <ul>
               <li>
                If your dog moves its head away when you try to pet it or makes other moves to avoid touch, this can be a sign it is in pain. Take note of such behaviors if your dog normally likes being touched.
               </li>
               <li>
                These behaviors can go along with a dog being more withdrawn and less interactive than normal.
               </li>
               <li>
                You may also notice an attitude of depression or mental dullness if your dog is experiencing pain.
                <sup class="reference" id="_ref-21">
                 <a href="#_note-21">
                  [21]
                 </a>
                </sup>
               </li>
               <li>
                Note that some dogs may seek more attention, rather than avoiding it, if they are in pain.
                <sup class="reference" id="_ref-22">
                 <a href="#_note-22">
                  [22]
                 </a>
                </sup>
                You should take note of either avoidance or unusually high levels of attention-seeking behaviors.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li class="final_li">
             <div class="step_num">
              7
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Pay attention to your dog's potty habits.
              </b>
              Being aware of your dog's normal bathroom habits can help you recognize many different problems.
              <ul>
               <li>
                When a dog is in pain you may notice that it exhibits a different posture when it urinates or defecates. For example, a male dog that normally lifts his leg to urinate might not.
               </li>
               <li>
                You may also see a change in how often your dog needs to do its business. Or, your dog may have accidents if it is painful for them to get to the area where they usually go.
               </li>
               <li>
                Pain can also change the consistency of a dog's stool, due to the related stress. It can also lead to constipation.
                <sup class="reference" id="_ref-23">
                 <a href="#_note-23">
                  [23]
                 </a>
                </sup>
               </li>
              </ul>

         <div class="section tips sticky ">
          <h2>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Tips">
            Tips
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="section_text" id="tips">
           <ul>
            <li>
             Be careful not to make the pain worse. Sometimes you have to find the pain to know it's there. With a dog, sometimes the only way to do this is to touch or move an area that can cause pain. You may have seen your veterinarian do this when they have examined your dog, but vets are trained to do this without causing further injury. Lacking this training, you might hurt a dog your are trying to diagnose.
            </li>
            <li>
             Your goal should not necessarily be to locate the pain, but to determine if your dog is in pain. Once you have an answer, it's time to consult with your veterinarian for possible solutions.
            </li>
            <li>
             Many of the signs that your dog is in pain are subtle. It's much easier to pick up on these subtle changes if you know what is normal. If you are aware of your dog's normal activities and behaviors, you will more easily recognize any changes.
             <sup class="reference" id="_ref-24">
              <a href="#_note-24">
               [24]
              </a>
             </sup>
            </li>
           </ul>

         <div class="section warnings sticky ">
          <h2>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Warnings">
            Warnings
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="section_text" id="warnings">
           <ul>
            <li>
             Do not attempt to medicate your dog at home without instructions from your veterinarian. Human medications for pain and inflammation can be dangerous for dogs, especially in the wrong dosages.
            </li>
            <li>
             Likewise, if you suspect your dog is in pain but aren't sure, visit your vet.
            </li>
            <li>
             While all of the signs discussed above can suggest a painful situation for your dog, some of them can also indicate other issues. If any of these signs are persistent, worsen over 24 to 48 hours, consult your veterinarian.
            </li>
           </ul>
