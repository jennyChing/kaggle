          <h1 class="entry-title">
           Paper Stars
          </h1>
          <div class="byline-meta">
           Written
           <em>
            by
           </em>
           <span class="author vcard">

         <div class="entry-content">
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://annekata.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/toastgarland21.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25" height="390" src="http://annekata.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/toastgarland21.jpg" title="Paper Stars" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Ready for some more paper stars. A few days ago, I saw this pretty garland in the TOAST catalog (top left) and thought, hey I can probably make those, that looks easy. And guess what it is (top right). As ever, the web provides. All you need is paper, scissors and a pencil. Extreme Cards and Paper Crafting, offers a wonderful
           <a href="http://extremecards.blogspot.com/2009/06/five-pointed-origami-star.html" target="_blank">
            tutorial
           </a>
           including a template.
          </p>
          <p>
           I didnt have the Indian paper used by TOAST, but my box full of vintage music sheets, made a great substitution:
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://annekata.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/musicstars.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26" height="450" src="http://annekata.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/musicstars.jpg" title="Paper Stars" width="600"/>
           </a>
           I made them with all kinds of paper to use for gift wrapping, garlands and decorations:
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://annekata.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/origamistars1.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-27" height="450" src="http://annekata.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/origamistars1.jpg" title="Paper Stars" width="600"/>
           </a>
           And as with all things small and humble, they look best in a cluster. Have fun making lots of stars.
           <br/>
           Glad youre here.
          </p>
