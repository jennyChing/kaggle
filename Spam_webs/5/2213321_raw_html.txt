       <a href="http://www.decoist.com/2013-01-26/3-eco-friendly-materials-for-home-renovations/">
        eco-friendly
       </a>
       , utilizing sustainable materials and energy sources.
      </p>
      <p>
       Its undeniably interesting to see how architects and interior designers come together to create a groundbreaking shelter! How do you furnish an underground home? Should it blend into the landscape or stand out? Are underground homes hidden, or do they boast windows that look out onto the countryside? Browse the images below to answer these questions and more
      </p>
      <h2>
       Switzerlands Villa Vals
      </h2>
      <p>
       We begin in Switzerland with
       <a href="http://www.villavals.ch/">
        Villa Vals
       </a>
       , a holiday retreat built into the alpine slopes of Bals. SeARCH and CMA collaborated on this amazing dwelling, the design of which reveals a sincere respect for nature. The homes facade is made from Valser quartzite recovered from the site and found in the areas nearby thermal baths!
       <em>
        [from
        <a href="http://search.nl/#!content/villa-vals">
         SeARCH Architecture and Urban Planning
        </a>
        ]
       </em>
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55740" style="width: 610px">
       <a href="http://www.decoist.com/2013-02-26/unforgettable-underground-homes/underground-holiday-retreat/">
        <img alt="Underground holiday retreat Unforgettable Underground Homes" class="size-full wp-image-55740" height="400" src="http://cdn.decoist.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Underground-holiday-retreat.jpg" width="600"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Underground holiday retreat
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       The dwelling features a stone courtyard with a natural spring hot tub, the perfect place to enjoy the breathtaking view in comfort and style:
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55742" style="width: 610px">
       <a href="http://www.decoist.com/2013-02-26/unforgettable-underground-homes/stone-courtyard-of-underground-home/">
        <img alt="Stone courtyard of underground home Unforgettable Underground Homes" class="size-full wp-image-55742" height="400" src="http://cdn.decoist.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Stone-courtyard-of-underground-home.jpg" width="600"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Stone courtyard of an underground home
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       The levels of the homes interior are arranged like nesting concrete boxes. On the first floor is the living room, kitchen, and a bedroom that pulls double duty as a library.Dutch designer Thomas Eyck put his touch on the interiors. Furniture, textiles and ceramics were created by Dutch designers such as
       <a href="http://www.jongeriuslab.com/">
        Hella Jongerius
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.studiojob.be/">
        Studio Job
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55747" style="width: 610px">
       <a href="http://www.decoist.com/2013-02-26/unforgettable-underground-homes/living-area-in-swiss-underground-home/">
        <img alt="Living area in Swiss underground home" class="size-full wp-image-55747" height="399" src="http://cdn.decoist.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Living-area-in-Swiss-underground-home.jpg" width="600"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Living area in Swiss underground home
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       An elongated table and artistic pendant lighting grace the dining area of this vacation rental, which makes group dining a chic reality:
      </p>

      <p>
       And just how is Villa Vals so eco-friendly? This retreat is thermally insulated and uses hydroelectric power, thanks to a nearby reservoir. Below we see the villa at night, an elliptical focal point against the scenic hillside
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55743" style="width: 610px">

      <h2>
       Bolton Eco House in England
      </h2>
      <p>
       Next we head to Northwest England to celebrate the wonder that is Bolton Eco House. Referred to as the house of the future, this home is embedded into the hillside of Mount Pennine, complete with a grass roof and an 8,000-square-foot layout. The home was designed in close collaboration between the client, Bolton Council and CABE.
       <em>
        [from
        <a href="http://www.makearchitects.com/#/projects/9067/">
         Make Architects
        </a>
        ]
       </em>
      </p>

      </div>

       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Eco House underground home

Below we see the homes interior, complete with its contrast of dark walls and a white ceiling:
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55755" style="width: 610px">

      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55757" style="width: 610px">
       <a href="http://www.decoist.com/2013-02-26/unforgettable-underground-homes/exterior-of-eco-house-underground-home/">

       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Exterior of Eco House underground home

       Bella Vista Hotel in Italy
      </h2>
      <p>
       For some, vacationing involves lavish hotel suites, strawberries and champagne. For others, a modern eco-friendly lodging arrangement is ideal, especially when the design is this groundbreaking! Created by architect and designer Matteo Thun, the latest KlimaHotel in Bozen, Italy will consist of a series of 11 hillside hostels, as shown below.
       <em>
        [from
        <a href="http://ifitshipitshere.blogspot.ro/2010/04/new-underground-eco-friendly-hotel.html">
         if its hip, its here
        </a>
        ]
       </em>
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55763" style="width: 610px">
       <a href="http://www.decoist.com/2013-02-26/unforgettable-underground-homes/italys-eco-friendly-bella-vista-hotel/">
        <img alt="Italy's eco-friendly Bella Vista Hotel" class="size-full wp-image-55763" height="447" src="http://cdn.decoist.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Italys-eco-friendly-Bella-Vista-Hotel.jpg" width="600"/>

       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Italys eco-friendly Bella Vista Hotel

      </p>

       <a href="http://www.decoist.com/2013-02-26/unforgettable-underground-homes/eco-friendly-underground-hotel/">
        <img alt="Eco-friendly underground hotel" class="size-full wp-image-55765" height="403" src="http://cdn.decoist.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Eco-friendly-underground-hotel.jpg" width="600"/>

        Eco-friendly underground hotel

      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55767" style="width: 610px">
       <a href="http://www.decoist.com/2013-02-26/unforgettable-underground-homes/bella-vista-at-night/">

       Wales Hobbit House
      </h2>
      <p>

       <a href="http://www.simondale.net/hobbit.htm">
        Simon Dale
       </a>
       along with family and friends, this cozy home in Wales housed Dale and family while he was doing ecological woodland management in the surrounding area. While not technically underground, it was dug into the hillside and has a true Hobbit feel, dont you think?
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_55736" style="width: 610px">

       <a href="http://www.simondale.net/hobbit.htm">
        BeingSomewhere.net
       </a>
       .
      </p>

A metal chimney pokes out from the grassy roof, as shown below. This home was commissioned bypolitician Bob Marshall-Andrews and his wife, Gill.

        <a href="http://www.architecturaldigest.com/architecture/2012-09/most-innovative-houses-last-century-slideshow_slideshow_item7_8">
