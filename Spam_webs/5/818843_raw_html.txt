        <div class="article-content clearfix">
         <p>
          After a month in the hothouse, last weeks live event at the Brooklyn club Verboten, finally saw the premieres of the
          <a href="http://www.spin.com/articles/spin-microsoft-connect-sessions-introduction/" target="_blank">
           Connect Sessions powered by Microsoft
          </a>
          collaborations between three pairs of musicians and visual artists. And now you can experience them for yourself. WAMS by the photographer
          <a href="http://ronamstutz.com/" target="_blank">
           Ron Amstutz
          </a>
          with original music by Ian Williams (of
          <a href="http://www.spin.com/tags/battles/" target="_blank">
           Battles
          </a>
          ) is a wonderful piece of cut-up video abstraction full of playful dynamics and colors, married to a glitch-techno sound bed. The video artist
          <a href="http://www.candystations.com/" target="_blank">
           CandyStations
          </a>
          marries
          <a href="http://www.spin.com/gallery/connect-sessions-in-the-studio-with-com-truise-candystations/" target="_blank">
           Com Truise
          </a>
          s midtempo electronic soundscapes to a video evoking the psychedelic qualities of celestial geometry. While
          <a href="http://www.spin.com/tags/how-dress-well/" target="_blank">
           How to Dress Well
          </a>
          and the creative studio
          <a href="http://www.intrusst.com/" target="_blank">
           TRUSST
          </a>
          tweaked the visuals of the soulful pop bands live show to include newly created, organic graphics controlled by Microsoft motion-sensitive Kinect technology. (Check out their contribution
          <a href="http://www.spin.com/articles/connect-sessions-battles-com-truise-how-to-dress-well-event-video/" target="_blank">
           here
          </a>
          .)
         </p>
