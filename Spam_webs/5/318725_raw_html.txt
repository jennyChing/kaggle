     <h1>
      15 of the worlds most legendary recording studios
     </h1>
     <div class="post-inner clearfix">
      <div class="post-meta">
       <div class="post-meta-author">
        by
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/author/matthershberger/">
         Matt Hershberger
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="post-meta-date">
        April 17, 2014
       </div>
       <div class="post-avatar">
        <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-100" height="100" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/authors/matthershberger.jpg" width="100"/>
       </div>
       <div class="post-share-wrapper post-share--vertical" data-post-id="244049" data-sticky="" data-sticky-max-width="991" data-sticky-offset-top="69" data-sticky-parent-contain="" data-sticky-parent-selector=".page-section">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        <span style="text-transform:uppercase;">
         I was visiting London
        </span>
        a few weeks ago and on a slow day decided to do the Beatles walking tour (which was inevitably called the Magical Mystery Tour). The tour of course ended at the legendary Abbey Road Studios in northwest London, and I got to see no fewer than six tourists nearly get killed stepping directly into oncoming traffic while trying to catch the iconic crosswalk photo.
       </p>
       <p>
        Modern music fans usually dont listen to music live, unlike our ancestors, who listened to live music exclusively. As I stood outside Abbey Road Studios and watched a 16-year-old Colombian girl weep at the site where the likes of Golden Slumbers, A Day in the Life, and All You Need Is Love were recorded, I realized that a musical tour of the world  a tour of the songs that moved you to tears, or helped you through a hard time, or amped you up for a big moment  would actually be a tour of the studios, these often nondescript buildings that are typically hidden in plain sight in our cities. Here are some of the worlds greatest studios.
       </p>
       <h5>
        Abbey Road Studios
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Abbey-Road.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Abbey Road" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Abbey-Road-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244052 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Abbey-Road-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The studio itself doesnt stand out particularly from the rest of the buildings around it, and it sits in a fairly quiet posh northwestern London suburb. If it werent for the tourists crowding the crosswalk and the Beatles-related graffiti covering its outer gate, one might pass and never notice it. The most famous image of Abbey Road is of course the crosswalk right outside the studio. Vehicles in London are legally required to wait at so-called zebra crossings as long as you physically stay in motion, so you can take as long as you like taking your picture, as long as you move in slow motion.
       </p>
       <p>
        Aside from most of the Beatles albums, Abbey Road (formerly EMI Studios) is also the recording site of Pink Floyds
        <em>
         The Dark Side of the Moon
        </em>
        (1973), Duran Durans eponymous debut album (1981), parts of Radioheads
        <em>
         The Bends
        </em>
        (1995) and
        <em>
         OK Computer
        </em>
        (1997), and Lady Gagas
        <em>
         Born This Way
        </em>
        (2011).
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://www.twc.edu/twcnow/blogs/student/9351/abbey-road-and-london-graduate-fair">
          via
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbey_Road_Studios">
          via
         </a>
         , &amp;
         <a href="http://www.transsiberianmarchband.com/posts/tsmb-at-abbey-road-studios/">
          via
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h5>
        The Dungeon
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Dungeon-Family.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Dungeon Family" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Dungeon-Family-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244054 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Dungeon-Family-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The Dungeon is probably better known for the hip-hop collective that was born out of it, the Dungeon Family. The Dungeon itself was a studio in producer Rico Wades mothers basement in Atlanta, Georgia, but the collective has included some of the greatest hip-hop acts of the South and, consequently, of all time.
       </p>
       <p>
        At the top left is the only picture Ive been able to find of the Dungeon  pictured in it are the Dungeon Family and production-company founders of Organized Noize, Sleepy Brown, Ray Murray, and Rico Wade (from left to right). Probably the most famous members of the Dungeon Family are Big Boi and Andre 3000 (bottom left). Virtually all of Outkasts albums were recorded with the Dungeon Family. Its also the home of Gnarls Barkley, Cee-Lo Green, Bubba Sparxxx, Janelle Monae, and Future (pictured to the right with a Dungeon Family tattoo on his forearms).
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://www.cocaineblunts.com/blunts/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/onp2.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://allhiphop.com/2014/01/03/dungeon-family-affiliate-rico-wade-confirms-outkast-reunion-at-coachella/">
          via
         </a>
         , &amp;
         <a href="http://www.nickburchell.com/blog/?m=201209">
          via
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h5>
        Muscle Shoals Sound Studio
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Muscle-Shoals-Sound-Studio01.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Muscle Shoals Sound Studio01" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Muscle-Shoals-Sound-Studio01-940x600.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244055 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Muscle-Shoals-Sound-Studio01-940x600.jpg" height="600" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Muscle Shoals may be best known for a song that wasnt recorded at Muscle Shoals: Lynyrd Skynyrds Sweet Home Alabama. One of the lines is Now Muscle Shoals has got the Swampers / And they been known to pick a song or two. Muscle Shoals was formed when a band, the Muscle Shoals Sound Rhythm Section (nicknamed the Swampers) broke away from the great FAME Studios nearby and formed their own. While theyve got a slightly bigger studio these days, its still in the tiny town of Muscle Shoals, way off the beaten path in northwestern Alabama.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Muscle-Shoals-Sound-Studio02.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Muscle Shoals Sound Studio" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Muscle-Shoals-Sound-Studio02-940x626.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244057 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Muscle-Shoals-Sound-Studio02-940x626.jpg" height="626" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Even though the original studio looked like a roadside mechanics garage, the Muscle Shoals Sound Studio would go on to record tracks for the likes of the Rolling Stones (Brown Sugar and Wild Horses from
        <em>
         Sticky Fingers
        </em>
        in 1971), Paul Simons Kodachrome (1973), Bob Segers
        <em>
         Night Moves
        </em>
        (1976), the Black Keys awesome
        <em>
         Brothers
        </em>
        (2009, at the new studio), and, of course, Lynyrd Skynyrds first album (but not released till much later),
        <em>
         Skynyrds First
        </em>
        (1978).
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://davidhoodbass.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/jackson-hwy-lg.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         &amp;
         <a href="http://guitarcenter.lithium.com/legacyfs/online/17268_MuscleShoals.jpg">
          via
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h5>
        Trident Studios
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Trident-Studios01.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Trident Studios" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Trident-Studios01-940x527.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244058 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Trident-Studios01-940x527.jpg" height="527" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Its hard to understate how important London studios were to rock n roll in the 60s and 70s, and high among those studios was Trident. Tucked back in an alley in Londons posh Soho neighborhood, Trident is barely noticeable from the street, and it takes a little bit of searching to even realize its a studio.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Trident-Studios02.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Trident Studios" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Trident-Studios02-940x706.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244060 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Trident-Studios02-940x706.jpg" height="706" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Relative anonymity aside, Trident Studios were responsible for the discovery of Queen and their first four albums,
        <em>
         Queen
        </em>
        (1973),
        <em>
         Queen II
        </em>
        (1974),
        <em>
         Sheer Heart Attack
        </em>
        (1974), and
        <em>
         A Night at the Opera
        </em>
        (1975), as well as James Taylors eponymous debut album (1968), the Rolling Stones
        <em>
         Let it Bleed
        </em>
        (1969), David Bowies
        <em>
         The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust
        </em>
        and
        <em>
         The Spiders from Mars
        </em>
        (1972), and Lou Reeds
        <em>
         Transformer
        </em>
        (1972).
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-v1RWt_kvWwA/UKrB9d6lPDI/AAAAAAAABgM/0kmlV3Xdt5M/s1600/lds_Trident+Studios2.jpg">
          via
         </a>

         <a href="http://soundofthehound.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/trident-studio-a1.jpg">

       </p>
       <h5>
        Sunset Sound Recorders
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sunset-Sound-Recorders01.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Sunset Sound Recorders" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sunset-Sound-Recorders01-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244062 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sunset-Sound-Recorders01-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        On the other side of the world, we have Sunset Sound Recorders, on Sunset Boulevard in Hollywood, California. It was originally built for recording the music to Walt Disney movies, and you can thank them for
        <em>
         Mary Poppins
        </em>
        ,
        <em>
         Bambi
        </em>
        , and
        <em>
         101 Dalmatians
        </em>
        , but they went on to much greater rock heights.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sunset-Sound-Recorders02.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Sunset Sound Recorders" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sunset-Sound-Recorders02-940x674.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244064 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sunset-Sound-Recorders02-940x674.jpg" height="674" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Probably the most famous album recorded here was the Rolling Stones
        <em>
         Exile on Main Street
        </em>
        (1972, and pictured above), generally believed to be their best ever, but it was also the home of the Beach Boys best album,
        <em>
         Pet Sounds
        </em>
        (1966). My personal favorites, however, are Led Zeppelins albums
        <em>
         Led Zeppelin II
        </em>
        (1969) and
        <em>
         Led Zeppelin IV
        </em>
        (1972), both of which were partially recorded and mixed here. Other famous ones include the Doors
        <em>
         The Doors
        </em>
        (1967) and
        <em>
         Strange Days
        </em>
        (1967), Jets
        <em>
         Get Born
        </em>
        (2003), the
        <em>
         O Brother, Where Art Thou?
        </em>
        soundtrack, and, of course, Macy Grays
        <em>
         On How Life Is
        </em>
        (2000).
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://bradfernquist.files.wordpress.com/2008/09/l1010737.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         &amp;
         <a href="http://i248.photobucket.com/albums/gg185/hbwriter/jaggersunset.jpg">
          via
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h5>
        Headley Grange
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Headley-Grange01.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Headley Grange" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Headley-Grange01-940x814.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244066 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Headley-Grange01-940x814.jpg" height="814" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Headley Grange is a former poorhouse in Headley, England, and it gets on this list for a single reason: its stairwell. During a recording session in the room next door, Jimmy Page was trying out the riff to When the Levee Breaks, when the crew started setting up John Bonhams drum kit in the hall. He went out, start playing, and they recorded it from the stairwell. The result is one of rocks best ever sounds. Bad Company, Fleetwood Mac, Genesis, and Peter Frampton recorded here as well.
        <br/>
        <em>
         Image
         <a href="http://www.genesispictures.co.uk/images/frontpage/HeadleyGrange.jpg">
          via
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h5>
        Motown
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Motown.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Motown" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Motown-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244067 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Motown-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Of course Motown is on here. Technically, the studio itself is called Hitsville, U.S.A. (now a museum, pictured at the bottom), but the site was also the home of Motowns headquarters in Detroit, and as such Im calling it Motown. It was without a doubt one of the most important recording studios of all time, and if you say the name Motown now, it evokes an entire genre of music put out by Berry Gordys Motown label.
       </p>
       <p>
        Among the many great albums recorded at Hitsville are Marvin Gayes
        <em>
         Whats Going On
        </em>
        (1971) and
        <em>
         Lets Get it On
        </em>
        (1973), the Jackson 5s debut
        <em>
         Diana Ross Presents the Jackson 5
        </em>
        (1969  Ross and the Supremes are pictured at the top left with Berry Gordy), the Marvelettes
        <em>
         Please Mr. Postman
        </em>
        (1961), and Stevie Wonders debut,
        <em>
         The Jazz Soul of Little Stevie
        </em>
        (1962).
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://www.tedeytan.com/2012/05/19/10715">
          via
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://s658.photobucket.com/user/fkt974/media/MotownStudio-HitsvilleUSA.jpg.html">
          via
         </a>
         , &amp;
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hitsville_U.S.A.">
          via
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h5>
        Electric Lady Studios
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Electric-Lady-Studios01.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Electric Lady Studios" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Electric-Lady-Studios01-940x697.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244068 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Electric-Lady-Studios01-940x697.jpg" height="697" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Electric Lady Studios (as youve probably guessed) was founded by Jimi Hendrix after how much it cost him to record his epic album
        <em>
         Electric Ladyland
        </em>
        . Hendrix was only able to use the studio for four weeks before he died, but the studio, in New Yorks Greenwich Village, is still very much in use.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Electric-Lady-Studios02.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Electric Lady Studios" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Electric-Lady-Studios02-940x436.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244069 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Electric-Lady-Studios02-940x436.jpg" height="436" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        We can thank Electric Lady Studios for Zeppelins
        <em>
         Houses of the Holy
        </em>
        (1973) and
        <em>
         Physical Graffiti
        </em>
        (1975), Patti Smiths
        <em>
         Horses
        </em>
        (1975) The Clashs
        <em>
         Combat Rock
        </em>
        (1982), Billy Idols
        <em>
         Rebel Yell
        </em>
        (1983) Weezers eponymous 1995 album, Santanas
        <em>
         Supernatural
        </em>
        (1999), the White Stripes
        <em>
         De Stijl
        </em>
        (2000), the Roots
        <em>
         Game Theory
        </em>
        (2006), as well as a ton of Kiss albums.
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Electric_Lady_Studios_2013.jpg/800px-Electric_Lady_Studios_2013.jpg">

       <h5>
        Sun Studio
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sun-Studio01.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Sun Studio" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sun-Studio01-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244061 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sun-Studio01-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Weve been focusing a lot on rock, so lets just get this out of our system: Sun Studio, in Memphis, Tennessee, was originally more of a blues outfit. But blues begat rock, and it begat it right in Sun Studios in the form of Elvis Presley, Jerry Lee Lewis, Roy Orbison, and Johnny Cash, all of whom recorded albums here.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sun-Studio02.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Sun Studio" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sun-Studio02-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244071 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Sun-Studio02-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Aside from the founders of rock, Sun Studio also recorded albums for blues greats B.B. King, Howlin Wolf, and Junior Parker. It closed for a while but then reopened in 1987, where, probably most notably, it recorded U2s
        <em>
         Rattle and Hum
        </em>
        (1988).
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/18/Sun_Studio,_Memphis.jpg">
          via

         &amp;
         <a href="http://www.weekendnotes.com/im/008/03/sun-recording-studio1.jpg">
          via

       <h5>
        Studio One
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Studio-One01.jpeg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Studio One" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Studio-One01-940x705.jpeg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244072 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Studio-One01-940x705.jpeg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>

       <p>
        Its called the Motown of Jamaica, but really, it should just be called Studio One. Because Studio One is the home of reggae, and it doesnt need the Motown qualifier.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Studio-One02.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Studio  One" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Studio-One02-940x635.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244073 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Studio-One02-940x635.jpg" height="635" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Founded by Clement Coxsone Dodd (the man with the microphone) back in 1963, Studio One recorded albums for Bob Marley and the Wailers, Lee Scratch Perry, Burning Spear, and Toots and the Maytals. Youre welcome, world.
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://cdn.thehundreds.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Jamaica-Jamaica-10.jpeg">

       <h5>
        Rolling Stones Mobile Studio
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Stones.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Stones" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Stones-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244074 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Stones-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        This one could get on here just for the novelty of having whats basically a truck with a recording studio in it, but its actually been the site of a number of insanely good recordings. It was set up by Mick Jagger when he got sick of all the problems of using regular recording studios. They set up a studio in his home and then, so they could move it around, put a control room into this van.
       </p>
       <p>
        We can thank the mobile studio for songs like Deep Purples Smoke on the Water, and  because its mobile  for the most famous live recording of Bob Marleys No Woman, No Cry  the one appearing on the posthumous
        <em>
         Legend
        </em>
        (1984). It also recorded parts of a number of Stones and Zeppelin albums, as well as Simple Minds 1979 debut album,
        <em>
         Life in a Day
        </em>
        , and live performances by Patti Smith and the Ramones.
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://retrorides.proboards.com/thread/81301/rolling-stones-mobile-studio">
          via

       <h5>
        Capitol Studios
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Capitol-Studios01.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Capitol Studios" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Capitol-Studios01-940x413.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244075 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Capitol-Studios01-940x413.jpg" height="413" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The home of Capitol Records, Capitol Studios gets on this list for the sheer breadth of the artists theyve recorded here. All major record labels are going to have crazy amounts of awesome musical artists recording in their studios, but Capitol Records is best known for its echo chambers, which are part of an underground concrete bunker designed by legendary guitarist and sound engineer Les Paul to get a better reverb sound.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Capitol-Studios02.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Capitol Studios" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Capitol-Studios02-940x626.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244076 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Capitol-Studios02-940x626.jpg" height="626" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The studios are most famous for being the place where Frank Sinatra did a lot of his recordings  his microphone is still here, and the band Bastille recently recorded on it  as well as being a home to Nat King Cole and the Beach Boys. But it wasnt just older music: Oasis, Daft Punk, Aaliyah, Outkast, and fun. have all recorded here.
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://www.capitolstudios.com/wp-content/gallery/misc-new/caps_recordmix_studios_studioa_slide4.jpg">
          via
         </a>
         &amp;
         <a href="http://www.capitolstudios.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/EchoChamber.jpg">
          via
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <h5>
        Lee Scratch Perrys Black Ark
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Black-Ark.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Black Ark" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Black-Ark-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244077 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Black-Ark-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Easily the most fascinating studio on this list is Lee Scratch Perrys Black Ark Studio in Kingston, Jamaica. While not quite as mainstream, and definitely more low-tech than nearby Studio One, the Black Ark was known for Perrys innovative producing techniques, and also for his incredibly strange behavior. He was known for blowing ganja smoke into the tape decks, burying tapes, and spraying the unprotected tapes with blood, urine, and whiskey to bless them. Eventually, after a few rough years of being extorted by gangsters, Perry covered the entire building in magic-marker drawings and then burned it to the ground to get rid of bad spirits. Other than producing many of Perrys own records (and basically inventing the dub genre), Black Ark gave us recordings from Bob Marley, Paul McCartney and Wings, the Clash, and Junior Murvin.
        <br/>
        <em>
         Images
         <a href="http://www.globetripper.com/tag/rocksteady/">
          via
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://info.umkc.edu/specialcollections/archives/669">
          via
         </a>
         , &amp;
         <a href="http://www.soundonsound.com/sos/jul07/articles/dubmixing.htm">
          via
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>

        Hans Zimmers Music Lair
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Hans-Zimmers-Music-Lair01.jpg">
         <noscript>

        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        You may not have heard of Hans Zimmer, but youve definitely listened to him. Zimmer is the German composer known for writing the scores to movies like
        <em>
         Gladiator
        </em>
        ,
        <em>
         The Dark Knight
        </em>
        ,
        <em>
         Inception
        </em>
        , and
        <em>
         The Lion King
        </em>
        . Ive always been a fan of his music  try listening to
        <em>
         The Dark Knight
        </em>
        when youre trying to get some work done, its second only to Daft Punks
        <em>
         Alive
        </em>
         but I never knew he had an awesome pad like this. It looks like what I imagined Hogwarts looking like. Yes, those are skull lamps, and those arent bookshelves in the back  thats a synthesizer.
        <br/>
        <em>
         Image
         <a href="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2743/4313394946_f46e4d0bd7_b.jpg">

       <h5>
        Chase Park Transduction
       </h5>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Fotor03279573.jpg">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="Chase Park Transduction" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Fotor03279573-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-244080 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/04/Fotor03279573-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Athens, Georgia, has become synonymous with awesome music, and one of its most prolific studios is Chase Park Transduction. Its recorded the granddaddy of Athens rock bands, REM, as well as acts like Bright Eyes, Deerhunter, Animal Collective, and Queens of the Stone Age.
