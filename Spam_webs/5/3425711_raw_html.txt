     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Abydonians">
       Abydonians
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_recurring_alien_characters_in_Stargate_SG-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Abydonians">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Abydonians
      </b>
      are the people whom Colonel O'Neill's team encounters on another planet in the
      <a href="/wiki/Stargate_(film)" title="Stargate (film)">
       <i>
        Stargate
       </i>
       film
      </a>
      . They are the slaves of the alien
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra_(Stargate)" title="Ra (Stargate)">
       Ra
      </a>
      and are descendants from
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">
       ancient Egyptians
      </a>
      brought through the Stargate to mine the fictional mineral
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naqahdah" title="Naqahdah">
       naqahdah
      </a>
      . The film gives the location of their homeworldnamed Abydos in
      <i>
       SG-1
      </i>
      's pilot episode "
      <a href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Gods" title="Children of the Gods">
       Children of the Gods
      </a>
      "as the Kaliem galaxy "on the far side of the known universe" (a vague reference to the constellation
      <a href="/wiki/Caelum" title="Caelum">
       Caelum
      </a>
      , perhaps) in the film; and as the closest planets to Earth in the Stargate network in "Children of the Gods". In the film, O'Neill and Daniel Jackson inspire the Abydonians and their leader, Kasuf, to rise up against Ra. The military personnel return to Earth, while Daniel falls in love with Kasuf's daughter Sha're and remains behind. In "Children of the Gods", set a year after the film, the Goa'uld
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apophis_(Stargate)" title="Apophis (Stargate)">
       Apophis
      </a>
      attacks Abydos, abducting Sha're and her brother Skaara to serve as hosts for his queen Amonet and son Klorel. In season 6's "
      <a href="/wiki/Full_Circle_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Full Circle (Stargate SG-1)">
       Full Circle
      </a>
      ", the Goa'uld
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anubis_(Stargate)" title="Anubis (Stargate)">
       Anubis
      </a>
      destroys Abydos, but
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oma_Desala" title="Oma Desala">
       Oma Desala
      </a>
      helps its entire population
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ascension_(Stargate)" title="Ascension (Stargate)">
       Ascend
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <div class="thumb tright">
        <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
         <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Avari,_Erick_1.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1936" data-file-width="2656" height="160" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Avari%2C_Erick_1.jpg/220px-Avari%2C_Erick_1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Avari%2C_Erick_1.jpg/330px-Avari%2C_Erick_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Avari%2C_Erick_1.jpg/440px-Avari%2C_Erick_1.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
         </a>
         <div class="thumbcaption">
          <div class="magnify">
           <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Avari,_Erick_1.jpg" title="Enlarge">
           </a>
          </div>
          <a href="/wiki/Erick_Avari" title="Erick Avari">
           Erick Avari
          </a>
          played Kasuf in the film and the television series.
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <span id="Kasuf">
       </span>
       <b>
        Kasuf
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">
        Hebrew
       </a>
       for 'silver-colored'), played by
       <a href="/wiki/Erick_Avari" title="Erick Avari">
        Erick Avari
       </a>
       (film, seasons 24)  The leader of the Abydonians in the film, and the father of Sha're and Skaara. In season 2's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secrets_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Secrets (Stargate SG-1)">
        Secrets
       </a>
       ", one year after
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apophis_(Stargate)" title="Apophis (Stargate)">
        Apophis
       </a>
       's kidnapping of Kasuf's children in "Children of Gods", Daniel returns to Abydos and learns that Sha're has become pregnant by Apophis. Kasuf helps Daniel to hide the newborn child from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heru-ur_(Stargate)" title="Heru-ur (Stargate)">
        Heru-ur
       </a>
       . Kasuf returns in season 3's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forever_in_a_Day_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Forever in a Day (Stargate SG-1)">
        Forever in a Day
       </a>
       " when his daughter Sha're dies. Kasuf last appears in season 4's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Absolute_Power_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Absolute Power (Stargate SG-1)">
        Absolute Power
       </a>
       ", introducing SG-1 to his rapidly aged grandchild, Shifu.
      </li>
      <li>
       <span id="Sha.27re">
       </span>
       <span id="Shau.27ri">
       </span>
       <span id="Amaunet">
       </span>
       <span id="Amonet">
       </span>
       <b>
        Sha're
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        Sha'uri
       </b>
       in the film), played by
       <a href="/wiki/Mili_Avital" title="Mili Avital">
        Mili Avital
       </a>
       (film) and
       <a href="/wiki/Vaitiare_Bandera" title="Vaitiare Bandera">
        Vaitiare Bandera
       </a>
       (seasons 13)  Kasuf's daughter who becomes Daniel Jackson's wife and the host of the Goa'uld
       <b>
        Amonet
       </b>
       (also spelled
       <b>
        Amaunet
       </b>
       ). In the film, her father Kasuf offers Sha're to Daniel Jackson as a gift, and although he initially refuses to take her as his wife, they eventually fall in love with each other. After one year of marriage in "Children of the Gods",
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apophis_(Stargate)" title="Apophis (Stargate)">
        Apophis
       </a>
       takes Sha're hostage and makes her a host for his symbiote queen Amonet against her will. Daniel meets a now vastly-pregnant Sha're on a visit to Abydos in season 2's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secrets_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Secrets (Stargate SG-1)">
        Secrets
       </a>
       ". She hides her child, a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harcesis" title="Harcesis">
        Harcesis
       </a>
       fathered by Apophis, from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heru-ur_(Stargate)" title="Heru-ur (Stargate)">
        Heru-ur
       </a>
       as Amonet is dormant during the pregnancy. When Sha're gives birth to a boy,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shifu_(Stargate)" title="Shifu (Stargate)">
        Shifu
       </a>
       , Amonet takes control of Sha're but keeps information about the child to herself. Amonet returns for the child one year later in season 3's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forever_in_a_Day_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Forever in a Day (Stargate SG-1)">
        Forever in a Day
       </a>
       ", sending him to the planet Kheb with one of her handmaidens. During a battle at Abydos in "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forever_in_a_Day_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Forever in a Day (Stargate SG-1)">
        Forever in a Day
       </a>
       ", Amonet attacks Daniel with her
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hand_device" title="Hand device">
        hand device
       </a>
       in a tent, and Teal'c kills Sha're with his
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Staff_weapon" title="Staff weapon">
        staff weapon
       </a>
       to prevent Daniel's death.
      </li>
      <li>
       <span id="Skaara">
       </span>
       <span id="Klorel">
       </span>
       <b>
        Skaara
       </b>
       , played by
       <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Cruz" title="Alexis Cruz">
        Alexis Cruz
       </a>
       (film, seasons 13, 6)  The son of Kasuf and brother to Sha're. In the film, Skaara and his friends aid O'Neil and his soldiers to beat
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra_(Stargate)" title="Ra (Stargate)">
        Ra
       </a>
       . In "Children of the Gods", Skaara is taken hostage by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apophis_(Stargate)" title="Apophis (Stargate)">
        Apophis
       </a>
       and is made the host for his symbiote son
       <b>
        Klorel
       </b>
       against his will. SG-1 invades Klorel's ship in the season 1 finale, "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Within_the_Serpent%27s_Grasp_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Within the Serpent's Grasp (Stargate SG-1)">
        Within the Serpent's Grasp
       </a>
       ", but Skaara is only able to emerge shortly. After O'Neill shoots Klorel to prevent him from killing Dr. Jackson,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bra%27tac" title="Bra'tac">
        Bra'tac
       </a>
       revives him in a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarcophagus_(Stargate)" title="Sarcophagus (Stargate)">
        Sarcophagus
       </a>
       . Skaara and Apophis flee before their ships are destroyed in the season 2 opener, "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Serpent%27s_Lair_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="The Serpent's Lair (Stargate SG-1)">
        The Serpent's Lair
       </a>
       ". In season 3's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretense_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Pretense (Stargate SG-1)">
        Pretense
       </a>
       ", Klorel's ship crashes on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tollan_(Stargate)" title="Tollan (Stargate)">
        Tollan
       </a>
       homeworld while fleeing from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heru-ur_(Stargate)" title="Heru-ur (Stargate)">
        Heru-ur
       </a>
       's forces. With help from the Tollan technology, Skaara regains control and participates in a Tollan trial to get the symbiote separated from his body. Skaara wins the trial and eventually returns to Abydos, where he meets SG-1 one last time in the season 6 finale, "
       <a href="/wiki/Full_Circle_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Full Circle (Stargate SG-1)">
        Full Circle
       </a>
       ". Skaara helps SG-1 in the search for the
       <a href="/wiki/Eye_of_Ra" title="Eye of Ra">
        Eye of Ra
       </a>
       before
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anubis_(Stargate)" title="Anubis (Stargate)">
        Anubis
       </a>
       can find it on Abydos. Skaara is mortally wounded during the firefight with Anubis' Jaffa, but ascends with the help of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oma_Desala" title="Oma Desala">
        Oma Desala
       </a>
       .
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Ancients">
       Ancients
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_recurring_alien_characters_in_Stargate_SG-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ancients">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main articles:
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_(Stargate)" title="Ancient (Stargate)">
       Ancient (Stargate)
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_characters_in_Stargate" title="Ancient characters in Stargate">
       Ancient characters in Stargate
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Ancients
      </b>
      are the original builders of the
      <a href="/wiki/Stargate_(device)" title="Stargate (device)">
       Stargate
      </a>
      network, who by the time of
      <i>
       Stargate SG-1
      </i>
      have
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ascension_(Stargate)" title="Ascension (Stargate)">
       ascended
      </a>
      beyond corporeal form into a higher plane of existence. The humans of Earth are the "second evolution" of the Ancients. The Ancients (originally known as the Alterans) colonized the Milky Way galaxy millions of years ago and built a great empire. They also colonized the Pegasus galaxy and seeded human life there before being driven out by the
      <a href="/wiki/Wraith_(Stargate)" title="Wraith (Stargate)">
       Wraith
      </a>
      . The civilization of the Ancients in the Milky Way was decimated thousands of years ago by a plague and those who did not learn to ascend died out.      <p>
      The
      <b>
       Goa'uld
      </b>
      are the dominant race in the Milky Way and the primary adversaries from seasons 1 to 8 of
      <i>
       Stargate SG-1
      </i>
      . They are a parasitic species that resemble finned snakes, which can burrow themselves into a humanoid's neck and wrap around the spinal column. The Goa'uld symbiote then takes control of its host's body and mind, while providing longevity and perfect health. Thousands of years ago, the Goa'uld ruled over Earth, masquerading as gods from ancient mythologies. They transplanted humans throughout the galaxy to serve as slaves and hosts, and they created the Jaffa to serve as incubators for their larvae. The most powerful Goa'uld in the galaxy are collectively known as the System Lords.

     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Jaffa
      </b>
      are an offshoot of humanity, genetically engineered by the
      <a href="/wiki/Goa%27uld" title="Goa'uld">
       Goa'uld
      </a>
      . They have an abdominal pouch which serves to incubate larval Goa'uld. The infant Goa'uld provides strength, longevity, and good health, at the cost of supplanting the Jaffa's natural immune system, making them dependent on the Goa'uld for more symbiotes. The Jaffa have a warrior culture and form the armies of the Goa'uld. In season 8 of
      <i>
       Stargate SG-1
      </i>
      , the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jaffa_Resistance" title="Jaffa Resistance">
       Jaffa Resistance
      </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_Jaffa_Nation" title="Free Jaffa Nation">

      The
      <b>
       Lucian Alliance
      </b>

      <a href="/wiki/Goa%27uld" title="Goa'uld">

       The Ties That Bind

       Ori
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">

     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Ori_(Stargate)" title="Ori (Stargate)">
       Ori (Stargate)
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Ori
      </b>
      are Ascended beings who use their infinite knowledge of the universe to force lesser beings to worship them. In essence, they used to be Ancients, however they split into separate groups due to different views of life. The Ori are religious while the Ancients prefer science. The Ori sway lesser-developed planets into worshipping them by promising Ascension through an invented and empty religion called "Origin". This religion states that they created humanity and as such are to be worshipped by their creations. It also promises its followers that, on death, they will Ascend. However, Origin was designed to channel energy from the human worshippers to the Ori. As such, the Ori never help anyone else Ascend because then they would have to share the power that they sap from their worshippers. Their ultimate goal is to completely destroy the Ascended Ancients, who they know as "the Others". All of their efforts, including their technology, are for the purpose of garnering worshippers. As Ascended beings, the Ori do not interfere directly in the mortal plane. Instead, they use humans called
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prior_(Stargate)" title="Prior (Stargate)">
       Priors
      </a>
      , which they artificially evolve so that they are one step from Ascension, giving the Priors godly powers. Because the Ori have worshippers across the entire home galaxy of the Ancients, and using their knowledge to spread, they are nearly unstoppable.

     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:

      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Replicators
      </b>
      are a potent mechanical life-form using a quiron-based technology composed of building blocks using
      <a href="/wiki/Nanotechnology" title="Nanotechnology">
       nanotechnology
      </a>
      . They strive to increase their numbers and spread across the universe by assimilating advanced technologies. They are hostile to all other life-forms in the universe, but are opposed primarily by the Asgard. In the episode "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unnatural_Selection_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Unnatural Selection (Stargate SG-1)">
       Unnatural Selection
      </a>
      ", the Replicators had developed human-form Replicators, based on the technology they extracted from their Android creator, that appear just like humans and are able to change their form. Standard Replicators are resistant to energy weapons, and can only be destroyed by projectile weapons. Human-form Replicators, on the other hand, are resistant to projectile weapons as well due to the change in their nature from large blocks to smaller units the size of organic cells (cell blocks). In the episode "
      <a href="/wiki/New_Order_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="New Order (Stargate SG-1)">
       New Order (Part 2)
      </a>
      ", an
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_(Stargate)" title="Ancient (Stargate)">
       Ancient
      </a>
      weapon called the Replicator Disruptor was developed by O'Neill while he still had the knowledge of the Ancients in his mind. It works by blocking the cohesion between the blocks that make up the Replicators. The Replicators in the Milky Way galaxy were wiped out by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dakara_Superweapon" title="Dakara Superweapon">
       Dakara Superweapon
      </a>
      in the two-part episode "
      <a href="/wiki/Reckoning_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Reckoning (Stargate SG-1)">
       Reckoning
      </a>
      " at the climax of Season 8. It has been indicated that the Asgard used the same technology to defeat the Replicators in their own home galaxy as well.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Tollan">
       Tollan
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_recurring_alien_characters_in_Stargate_SG-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tollan">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Tollan
      </b>
      are an advanced human civilization who are introduced in season 1's "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enigma_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Enigma (Stargate SG-1)">
       Enigma
      </a>
      " when the SGC helps a group of them relocate from the original Tollan homeworld that had undergone catastrophic volcanic activity. The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nox_(Stargate)" title="Nox (Stargate)">
       Nox
      </a>
      take the Tollan in while they continue to search for a new home, later revealed to be named Tollana. The Tollan have a strict policy against sharing technology with more "primitive" races, instituted after such a transfer caused the civilization of their neighboring planet Serita to destroy itself in a single day.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Enigma_34-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Enigma-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Teal'c notes in season 3's "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretense_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Pretense (Stargate SG-1)">
       Pretense
      </a>
      " that despite the Tollans' technological superiority, they "do not think strategically". The Tollan are wiped out by the forces of the Goa'uld
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tanith_(Stargate)" title="Tanith (Stargate)">
       Tanith
      </a>
      in season 5's "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Between_Two_Fires_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Between Two Fires (Stargate SG-1)">
       Between Two Fires
      </a>
      " after the Goa'uld
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anubis_(Stargate)" title="Anubis (Stargate)">
       Anubis
      </a>
      developed shields impervious to Tollan weaponry.
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <span id="Narim">
       </span>
       <b>
        Narim
       </b>
       , played by
       <a href="/wiki/Garwin_Sanford" title="Garwin Sanford">
        Garwin Sanford
       </a>
       (seasons 1, 3, 5)  An influential Tollan who befriends SG-1 after they save him and a group of fellow Tollans in "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enigma_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Enigma (Stargate SG-1)">
        Enigma
       </a>
       ". He develops an apparent attraction to Carter and reconfirms his feelings for her in "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretense_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Pretense (Stargate SG-1)">
        Pretense
       </a>
       ", although she informs him that she is not looking for a relationship at that time. After the death of the Tollan leader, Omoc, in "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Between_Two_Fires_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Between Two Fires (Stargate SG-1)">
        Between Two Fires
       </a>
       ", Narim and SG-1 discover that his government was collaborating with the Goa'uld. Narim takes action to spare Earth from destruction, but the Goa'uld begin attacking the planet. Narim escorts SG-1 to the Stargate and stays behind to help his people fight. Shortly afterwards, Narim informs Earth of Tollana's devastations via a transmission, which ends abruptly.
      </li>
      <li>
       <span id="Travell">
       </span>
       <b>
        Travell
       </b>
       , played by
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marie_Stillin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marie Stillin (page does not exist)">
        Marie Stillin
       </a>
       (seasons 3, 5)  High Chancellor and a member of the Curia, the Tollan's highest ruling body. She is first seen in "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretense_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Pretense (Stargate SG-1)">
        Pretense
       </a>
       ", where she presides over the hearings about the future of the Goa'uld
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Klorel" title="Klorel">
        Klorel
       </a>
       and his unwilling host
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skaara" title="Skaara">
        Skaara
       </a>
       . In season 3's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shades_of_Grey_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Shades of Grey (Stargate SG-1)">
        Shades of Grey
       </a>
       ", Travell participates in an undercover operation conducted by the SGC to expose the rogue
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NID_(Stargate)" title="NID (Stargate)">
        NID
       </a>
       agents as thieves. In Travell's final appearance in "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Between_Two_Fires_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Between Two Fires (Stargate SG-1)">
        Between Two Fires
       </a>
       ", she offers Tollan ion cannon technology to Stargate Command, later discovered to be part of Tanith's extortion of the Curia.
      </li>
     </ul>

        Enemy Mine
       </a>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naqahdah" title="Naqahdah">
        naqahdah
       </a>
       mining operations on the Unas planet unwittingly encroached on holy Unas ground.  When Dion Johnstone was unavailable to play Chaka in "Enemy Mine", Patrick Currie (who had previously been cast to play
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fifth_(Stargate)" title="Fifth (Stargate)">

       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Peter_DeLuise" title="Peter DeLuise">

       </a>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shadow_Play_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Shadow Play (Stargate SG-1)">

       " to obtain more advanced military technology against Kelowna's two rival nations, but the SGC refuse to share their technology. Dreylock becomes Kelowna's new First Minister in season 7's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homecoming_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Homecoming (Stargate SG-1)">

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anubis_(Stargate)" title="Anubis (Stargate)">
        Anubis
       </a>
       . Dreylock subsequently allows Jonas Quinn, whom she previously regarded as a traitor, to remain on Langara. Since the forming of the planet's Joint Ruling Council in the aftermath of the Goa'uld invasion, Dreylock has become concerned with maintaining the uneasy peace between the three nations and again asks for Earth's help in season 7's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fallout_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Fallout (Stargate SG-1)">
        Fallout
       </a>
       ".

       <span id="Martin_Lloyd">

        Martin Lloyd
       </b>
       , played by
       <a href="/wiki/Willie_Garson" title="Willie Garson">
        Willie Garson
       </a>
       (seasons 45, 10)  A human from another planet who crashed on Earth after deserting from his military fighting a losing war with the
       <a href="/wiki/Goa%27uld" title="Goa'uld">
        Goa'uld
       </a>
       . Drugged with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" title="Pharmaceutical">
        pharmaceuticals
       </a>
       by his comrades, Martin loses his memories and becomes a paranoid conspiracy theorist who learns of the
       <a href="/wiki/Stargate_Program" title="Stargate Program">
        Stargate Program
       </a>
       . In season 4's "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Point_of_No_Return_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="Point of No Return (Stargate SG-1)">
        Point of No Return
       </a>
       ", O'Neill helps Martin to slowly regain his memory, and Martin chooses to remain on Earth. By season 5's "
       <a href="/wiki/Wormhole_X-Treme!" title="Wormhole X-Treme!">
        Wormhole X-Treme!
       </a>
       ", Martin has become so disgruntled with his life that he starts taking the drugs again. His latent memories inspire him to create a
       <a href="/wiki/Camp_(style)" title="Camp (style)">
        campy
       </a>
       science fiction television show,
       <i>
        Wormhole X-Treme!
       </i>
       , based on the real Stargate program and SG-1, and O'Neill helps Martin recover his memories once again. A ship approaches Earth to pick up Martin's former comrades, but Martin chooses to stay behind to continue working on
       <i>
        Wormhole X-Treme!
       </i>
       as a creative consultant. As becomes known in season 10's "
       <a href="/wiki/200_(Stargate_SG-1)" title="200 (Stargate SG-1)">
        200
       </a>
       ", Martin's show only aired for three episodes but had high DVD sales. Martin approaches the SGC to review a script for a television movie based on the series, and although the movie is eventually cancelled, the series is renewed, ultimately lasting ten years with Martin Lloyd as producer.
