        <h1 class="entry-title ">
         Tofu Puffs with Chinese Kale (Gai-Lan) and Carrot
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="posted-on">
          <i class="fa fa-calendar">
          </i>
          <a href="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/recipe-categories/meatless-recipes/tofu-puffs-with-chinese-kale-gai-lan-and-carrot/" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2011-02-09T07:10:26+00:00">
            February 9, 2011
           </time>
           <time class="updated" datetime="2014-10-01T10:30:34+00:00">
            October 1, 2014
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <i class="fa fa-user">
          </i>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/author/emily/">
            Emily
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta2 -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content2">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a46.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3755" height="546" src="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a46.jpg" title="Chinese Kale (Gai Lan) with Tofu Puffs and Carrot a4" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Guess what Ive found from my local
         <a href="http://www.harrisfarm.com.au/">
          Harris Farm Market
         </a>
         ? Mini tofu puffs!!!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         If you are puzzled with my excitement, please bear with me  Emily, the drama queen. Thats what happen when youre deprived from Asian groceries. Having lived in regional areas for some time, I find the supply of Asian ingredients has been intermittent. Unlike living in places with Chinatown, you dont get everything in one place and all the time.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I may try to find substitutions like cornflakes for panko breadcrumbs, but there are many things I still cant (or refuse to) make do. Tofu, for one. Anyway, I was so excited because of the small tofu puffs. You get the bigger ones occasionally but this is the first time Ive seen the small ones.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span id="more-3754">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a141.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3756" height="325" src="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a141.jpg" title="small tofu puffs a14" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         If you havent had these before, whats so great about these, you may wonder.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Regardless of their sizes, they are like edible sponges. Okay, maybe Im not selling this right  sponges arent exactly appetising.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         *Try again* When the tofu puffs are added in curries and stir fries, they can soak up all the flavours around them. Sounds good right? I love to put these in laksa and Thai red curry. You cant believe how awesome they taste after being left floating (yep, they float) in laksa soup and curries! The moment you sink your teeth in the gravy-contained puffs, the gravy gushes out to your palate which provides an instant hit of flavours.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a151.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3758" height="333" src="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a151.jpg" title="a15" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         {p/s: Although there are 5 garlic cloves in the photo, I ended up using 3 which turned out to be plenty.}
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         While Id love to make a curry dish or laksa for this, its best that I keep away from coconut milk for a couple days as you all know that I just had my carrot coconut curry yesterday. So Ive add them to my simple vegetarian stir fry instead.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a33.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3806" height="547" src="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a33.jpg" title="a3" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Tofu Puffs with Chinese Kale and Carrot (served with cooked brown rice)
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         (A fuss free cooking recipe)
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Serves 2 people
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           Prep time
          </strong>
         </span>
         : 10 minutes
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <strong>
           Cooking time
          </strong>
         </span>
         : 10 minutes
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         Ingredients:
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          250g (9 oz) Chinese Kale (aka Gai-lan or Chinese Broccoli)  about 3 stalks
         </li>
         <li>
          3 garlic cloves
         </li>
         <li>
          0.6 cm (1/4 inch) thick ginger
         </li>
         <li>
          1 carrot
         </li>
         <li>
          100g (4 oz) tofu puffs (or add any protein you like)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp vegetarian oyster sauce (or normal oyster sauce if you arent vegetarian)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 tbsp soy sauce
         </li>
         <li>
          5 drops of sesame oil
         </li>
         <li>
          3 tbsp water
         </li>
         <li>
          Some white pepper
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         Method:
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Mix 1 tbsp of water with vegetarian oyster sauce, soy sauce, white pepper and sesame oil in a small bowl. Set aside.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a131.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3759" height="663" src="http://www.fussfreecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/a131.jpg" title="vegetarian oyster sauce a13" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          Wash, trim and cut the kale into pieces; slice a carrot into matchsticks (check out
          <a href="http://cookincanuck.blogspot.com/2011/02/how-to-julienne-carrot-matchstick-style.html">
           here
          </a>
          to learn how); cut ginger into thin strips; Sliver the garlic about the same size as ginger.
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          Heat pan with 2 tbsps of oil over medium heat. When the oil is hot, add carrot, garlic and ginger and saute until the carrot is slightly tender.
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          Add tofu puffs and toss to coat with the ginger and garlic. Cook until they are VERY lightly charred.
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          Add kale with 2 tbsps of water. Toss to mix with the carrot-tofu mixture.
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          When the kale has slightly change its color to bright green, turn the flame to low and add the sauce mixture. Toss the ingredients to bind with the sauce. Its done when the sauce is heated.
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          Serve immediately with cooked brown rice.
         </li>
        </ul>
