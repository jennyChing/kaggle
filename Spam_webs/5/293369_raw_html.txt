           <h1 class="p-name">
            Apple Proclaims Erratic Heart Rate Readings Following Apple Watch Update Are Normal
           </h1>
           <div class="sharing share-inside">
            <div class="pw-widget" pw:counter="horizontal" pw:layout="horizontal" pw:url="http://hothardware.com/news/apple-proclaims-erratic-heart-rate-readings-introduced-with-apple-watch-update-are-normal">
             <a class="pw-button-googleplus pw-look-native">
             </a>
             <a class="pw-button-facebook pw-look-native">
             </a>
             <a class="pw-button-twitter pw-look-native">
             </a>
             <a class="pw-button-pinterest pw-look-native" pw:image="http://passets-cdn.pinterest.com/images/LogoRed.png">
             </a>
             <a class="pw-button-post-share">
             </a>
            </div>
            <script async="async" src="http://i.po.st/static/script/post-widget.js#publisherKey=ivlkpb0sg3anp6lr0ek0" type="text/javascript">
            </script>
           </div>
           <div class="cn-body e-content">
            <p>
             Leave it to
             <a href="http://hothardware.com/tags/apple">
              Apple
             </a>
             to write off what many see as a bug or a decrease in functionality as a feature. Maybe its part of the
             <a href="http://hothardware.com/news/apple-strengthens-reality-distortion-field-by-acquiring-augmented-reality-firm-metaio">
              reality distortion field
             </a>
             (RDF) that we briefly mentioned yesterday, but Apple is definitely stretching things quite a bit this time around.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             When the
             <a href="http://hothardware.com/news/canadian-man-receives-120-ticket-for-operating-apple-watch-while-driving">
              Apple Watch
             </a>
             first started arriving at customer doorsteps, users were able to obtain accurate and consistent heart rate readings. In fact, the Apple Watch was shockingly consistent, delivering readings every 10 minutes. However, following the release of the Apple Watch 1.0.1 update, customers began to notice that the readings were no longer recorded every 10 minutes, and in fact, there was actually no rhyme of reason as to when and how the readings were recorded.
             <br/>
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center;">
             <a href="/gallery/NewsItem/33867?image=big_applewatchupdate.jpg&amp;tag=popup" target="_blank">
              <img alt="applewatchupdate" src="http://hothardware.com/ContentImages/NewsItem/33867/content/small_applewatchupdate.jpg" style=""/>
             </a>
             <br/>
             <i>
              Apple Watch heart rate readings before (L) and after (R) the 1.0.1 software update (Source: twisted-pixel)
             </i>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Needless to say, users flocked to Apples Support Community to voice their frustrations. As of now,
             <a href="https://discussions.apple.com/thread/7050353?start=0&amp;tstart=0" target="_blank">
              the original thread
             </a>
             discussing the matter has grown to 197 replies and has been viewed nearly 15,000 times.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Rather than admit that this was a bug introduced with the 1.0.1 update or give the standard response that it is looking into the matter (as was the case with the recent
             <a href="http://hothardware.com/news/apple-posts-temporary-workaround-for-iphones-crippling-texting-bug">
              iPhone texting bug
             </a>
             ), Apple is doubling down and denying that this is an actual bug. In fact, its being called a feature of the Apple Watch.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Apples
             <a href="https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT204666" target="_blank">
              support page
             </a>
             that describes the functionality of the Apple Watchs heart rate sensor was recently updated this morning to state, In addition, Apple Watch attempts to measure your heart rate every 10 minutes, but won't record it when you're in motion or your arm is moving.
             <br/>
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center;">
             <img alt="watch heart rate monitor hero" src="http://hothardware.com/ContentImages/NewsItem/33867/content/watch-heart-rate-monitor-hero.jpg" style=""/>
             <br/>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Prior to this morning, the latter half of the statement was absent from Apples description of the heart rate sensor. Given that this is Apple were talking about, were likely never to receive a full-fledged response from Apple on why this feature has been neutered with the 1.0.1 update.
            </p>

            </div>
