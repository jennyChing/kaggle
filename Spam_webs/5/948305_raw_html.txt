         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Learn to Keep Moving
         </h1>
         <p class="p-meta">
          <span>
           In
           <a href="http://brktrail.com/category/pe/" title="View all posts in: PE">
            PE
           </a>
           by Hank Martin
          </span>
          <span>
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-08-29T06:30:59+00:00">
            August 29, 2014
           </time>
          </span>
          <span>
           <a class="meta-comments" href="http://brktrail.com/keep-moving/#comments" title="Leave a comment on: Learn to Keep Moving">
            2 Comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content content">
         <span class="x-dropcap">
          K
         </span>
         eep moving your feet, my brain gasps to my wavering body. Sweat trickles down my face, and burns my eyes before sliding off of me and onto the rocks below my feet. Already, at 8,000 feet the thinning air makes breathing difficult. I feel like I have ashthma. Ive spent most of my life living on the water at an elevation of 620 feet above see level. Right now, 8,000 ft is killing me. Two immense hands are squeezing my lungs and constricting my airway. My hands grab onto rock edges, and I pull my legs upward with my upper body. My legs need the help after all the hiking they have already done today. Another onslaught of sweat burns my eyes, causing me to blink furiously as thoughts of giving up fill my head.
         <blockquote class="x-blockquote x-pullquote left">
          One of these life lessons that is important to your personal education and well-roundedness is the lesson of persistence, sticking it out no matter the pain or desire to quit.
         </blockquote>
         Negativity can blindside you in a moment. One second you can be cruising along, happy the sun is shining and filled with more energy then you know what to do with, and the next you can be ready to curl up in the fetal position on the grass under a shady tree. Is this meant to be an inspiration for life? Am I writing to give you the courage to challenge yourself, and bring yourself to the point of pain so that you can lap up the pride that comes with summitting whatever mountain you find yourself climbing? Not really. Think of this as a chance to spend some time reflecting on a 7 word question that could change your life.
         <p>
          Flashback to several years ago, I had just arrived in Germany. I thought I was in shape. I thought I was athletic, but soon found out there are several different types of in shape just as several different types of bodies. For fit people you have the two basic types, a model body and a productive body. The model body is on the cover of Mens Health, hes the man with a rippling 8 pack and perfectly shaped muscles. He doesnt have useful muscle. Put this man before a mountain and hell fail. Why? Because he lacks both the persistence to succeed when faced with pain, and the useful muscle to actually complete the task.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now enters the useful body, the body not necessarily perfectly toned but made to dominate the world. The body capable of climbing every and any mountain thrown before him. Think of the model body as the politician and the useful body as the entrepreneur. The politician looks good standing in front of a crowd with his microphone, and his polished gestures make him look like a trained monkey (actually he is one if you remember that most politicians take body language lessons), but he cant help you.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats the entrepreneurs job. The entrepreneur may not always be dressed to the nines, have the most polished body language or impeccable manners but he gets shit done. He doesnt have to hide behind these things like the politicians. Moral of the story is that I was a politician I didnt have the persistence, pain threshold, or drive to be productive.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive come along way in a few years. The things I do now I would have never dreamed of doing before I came here. And the single greatest reason I have been able to accomplish these things has been my drive and persistence. Sometimes you will begin climbing a mountain and after only 20 minutes begin to feel like dying as the sun beats down on you, and your legs feel wobbly.
         </p>
         <p>
          You give up that time, you learn your lesson fast. Life teaches you the lesson you need to know, but are unable to find in other parts of the world. One of these life lessons that is important to your personal education and well-roundedness is the lesson of persistence, sticking it out no matter the pain or desire to quit.When you are one on one with nature there is no escaping your inadequacies like you can in the social world. Mother nature is not forgiving, if you fail to prepare then you prepared for failure. But in the world of man you can block out your flaws, you can sugar coat them with dress and a sly tongue. Only to a certain degree though, for the smart and able will see through your mask and uncover the worthlessness below. They are nature in human form, meant to keep you honest. They live freely, embracing opportunities to challenge who they are as people.
         </p>
         <p>
          We had left the tree line behind us and now we climb with our hands up the mountain. One wrong move and you will fall, limbs breaking against sharp-edged rocks and head pulvirized like meat under a meat softening mallet. Dont fall. Trust yourself. Believe in yourself, and have confidence that you can complete the task in front of you. Look up, dont focus on your feet but the top of the mountain above you. The second you look down, and hang your head toward the Earth, you are finished. Keep your chin up and think about nothing more then touching the top of the mountain youre summitting. So, with that being said, are you a politician or an entrepreneur?
         </p>

   jQuery(document).ready(function($) {

      $.fn.scrollBottom = function() {
        return $(document).height() - this.scrollTop() - this.height();
      };

      var windowObj            = $(window);
      var body                 = $('body');
      var bodyOffsetBottom     = windowObj.scrollBottom();
      var bodyHeightAdjustment = body.height() - bodyOffsetBottom;
      var bodyHeightAdjusted   = body.height() - bodyHeightAdjustment;
      var contentDock          = $('.x-content-dock');

      function sizingUpdate() {
        var bodyOffsetTop = windowObj.scrollTop();
        if ( bodyOffsetTop &gt; ( bodyHeightAdjusted * 0.05 ) ) {

            contentDock.toggleClass('x-content-dock-off').toggleClass('x-content-dock-on').css('right', '20px');

          e.preventDefault();
          contentDock.toggleClass('x-content-dock-off').toggleClass('x-content-dock-on').css('right', '-400px');
        });
      }

      windowObj.bind('scroll', sizingUpdate).resize(sizingUpdate);
      sizingUpdate();

    });
