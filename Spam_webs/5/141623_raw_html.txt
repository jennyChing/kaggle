       <img class="alignnone" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/boingboing.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/photostream-9.jpg"/>
       <p>
        Ben Marks of
        <a href="http://www.collectorsweekly.com/">
         Collectors Weekly
        </a>
        says: We just published an interview with Kirsten Hively, whose Project Neon documents about 800 neon signs in New York City (her
        <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/stat?id=5*EWppsT*Rw&amp;offerid=146261&amp;type=3&amp;subid=0&amp;tmpid=1826&amp;RD_PARM1=https%253A%252F%252Fitunes.apple.com%252Fus%252Fapp%252Fproject-neon%252Fid464751184%253Fmt%253D8%2526uo%253D4%2526partnerId%253D30" target="itunes_store">
         iPhone app
        </a>
        directs users to about 120 of them).
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Ive loved neon signs for a long time. When I would travel to cities like Portland or Chicago, I would take photos of neon signs and print them to hang up on my wall. Two years ago, I read that it was the 100th anniversary of the debut of the modern neon sign at the Paris Motor Show. It struck me then that I didnt have a single photo of neon signs in New York. I think I took it for granted that I could see those signs any day. But those classic signs arent necessarily permanent.
        </p>
        <p>
         At the time, I had just started a new job on the Upper East Side, in a neighborhood Id never spent much time in. Getting out of work at 5 p.m., it was pitch-black outside. So I thought, Maybe Ill go take photos of those nice signs near my officeat the Cork &amp; Bottle liquor store and Goldbergers Pharmacy. I figured Id walk around the neighborhood. I vaguely remembered another up on 86th Street, at Papaya King. After I found that one, I ended up walking for hours and hoursand taking so many photos. Later, I went home and put them up on my Flickr.
        </p>
        <p>
         A couple nights later, I went out to the Upper West Side and spent even more hours wandering around. I didnt get to everything, but I got obsessive about taking photos of neon signs and marking where they were. When I had searched for other peoples photos of neon signs online, often the caption wouldnt say where the image was taken. So I tried to be careful about always documenting the address of the place.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.collectorsweekly.com/articles/neon-lost-and-found-where-new-york-city-still-burns-bright/">
         Neon Lost and Found: Where New York City Still Burns Bright
        </a>
       </p>
