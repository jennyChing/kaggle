   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Two Cunts in a Kitchen
   </h1>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      <b>
       Two C's in a K
      </b>
      (for "
      <b>
       two cunts in a kitchen
      </b>
      "), is
      <a href="/wiki/Slang" title="Slang">
       slang
      </a>
      used within the
      <a href="/wiki/Advertising" title="Advertising">
       advertising
      </a>
      industry for a type of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_commercial" title="Television commercial">
       television commercial
      </a>
      and other advertising. Generally, the commercial shows two women in a domestic scene, discussing, using, or otherwise portraying the advertiser's product in a positive manner. This method has been typical for advertising targeted at women, such as commercials for household cleaning products,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      personal care products
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Feminine_hygiene" title="Feminine hygiene">
       feminine hygiene
      </a>
      products.
     </p>
     <p>
      A 1959 advertisement for Pursettes
      <a href="/wiki/Tampon" title="Tampon">
       tampons
      </a>
      is a typical example of "two C's in a K": It depicted two women discussing tampons over coffee, one saying to the other "Of course unmarried women can use them!".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
