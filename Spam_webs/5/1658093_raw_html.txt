           <p>
            If youre like me you love the look of Pottery Barn, West Elm and Restoration Hardware, but you hate the sticker price. Thats why I created my own furniture and put these plans together so you can too. Read on to find out how you can craft a dining bench at home with simple materials from your nearest big-box hardware store.
           </p>
           <p>
            I have always loved the look of a dining table with a bench on one side. I think it just helps keep the space less cluttered if you have an open dining area (like we do). All of those tall table backs can really start to close off the space, but a bench tucks neatly away.
           </p>
           <p>
            This bench is very similar in construction to the
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/2012/06/detailed-table-plans/">
             DIY Pottery Barn Hyde Dining Table Knockoff
            </a>
            . The legs are put together first, given shape with the long side aprons, and topped off (in this case) with 2 long planks. We used standard lumber from a big-box home improvement store so Im pretty sure all of the pieces are pine. After construction, it was sanded down and stained with 3 coats of Minwax (Jacobean color). I used a pre-stain first to try and help keep the coat even. I finished it off with 2 coats of polyurethane.
           </p>
           <p>
            This post is a long time coming and I apologize to those of you who have been waiting to build a bench with your . Here are the detailed plans to help you build a bench of your own.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             The plans were put together using Google SketchUp and while I tried my hardest to keep all of the measurements exact, sometimes the program just would not work with me. So please follow the measurements and do not rely solely on the photos.
            </em>
            <em>
             <br/>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Dimensions:
            </strong>
            <br/>
            52 wide x 14.5 deep x 18 tall
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Supplies List:
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            *2 1/2 Screws
            <br/>
            * 1  44 post 8 length (we used some left overs from the dining table)
            <br/>
            *8 of 1  14 8 lengthboard(use scraps from the table project if possible)
            <br/>
            * 1  22 8 length board
            <br/>
            *2 24 8 length board
            <br/>
            *1  28 8 length boards
            <br/>
            *Sandpaper (100, 150, and 220)
            <br/>
            *Wood putty (If you want to cover the screws. We left the screws exposed and they still look great)
            <br/>
            *Stain, varnish, paint or other finishing product
            <br/>
            *Paint brush
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Tools:
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            * measuring tape
            <br/>
            *square
            <br/>
            *pencil
            <br/>
            *hammer
            <br/>
            *safety glasses
            <br/>
            *hearing protection
            <br/>
            *mitre saw (or have the hardware store cut your wood for you)
            <br/>
            *drill
            <br/>
            *sander
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Cut List:
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
            A) 4  44 Posts @ 16.5 (Legs)
            <br/>
            B) 2  24 @ 10 (Leg Joiners)
            <br/>
            C) 2  24 @ 43 (Side Aprons)
            <br/>
            D) 4  14 @ 2 (End Apron Spacer)
            <br/>
            E) 2  24 @ 6 (End Aprons)
            <br/>
            F) 3  22 @ 10 (Tabletop Supports)
            <br/>
            G) 2  24 @ 14.5 (Breadboard Ends)
            <br/>
            H) 2  28 @ approx. 45 (Benchtop Pieces  Measure the bench before cutting these boards)
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Instructions:
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Cut out all boards except H. To get the best fit, you will measure and precisely cut out each of the boards H to fit the bench. Remember, use the 2 1/2 screws unless otherwise noted and always pre-drilland countersink your screws. Use glue on all joints to keep the table from separating later.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Step 1:
            </strong>
            Attach the end apron spacers (D)into the legs first, because the small space between the legs will make this step difficult later.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-1.png">
             <img alt="Building a farmhouse bench" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7667" height="360" src="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-1.png" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Step 2:
            </strong>
            Screw the leg joiner (B) to the legs (A) as shown below. Do this on both sets of legs. Leave 1 1/2 of space on the side of the leg joiners so the side aprons fit tightly.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-2.png">
             <img alt="DIY Dining Table Bench" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7668" height="360" src="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-2.png" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Step 3:
            </strong>
            Screw the side aprons (C) into the ends of the leg joiners (B) as shown below.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-3.png">
             <img alt="How to build a bench" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7669" height="361" src="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-3.png" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Check for square. Measure from one end diagonally across the table. Then measure on the other diagonal. If the measurements are the same, your table is square. If not, evaluate where you are off and make the necessary corrections.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Step 4:
            </strong>
            Screw the end aprons (E) into theend apron spacers as shown below.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-4.png">
             <img alt="Dining table bench plans" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7670" height="361" src="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-4.png" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Step 5:
            </strong>
            Screw in the tabletop supports (F) as shown below, spacing the supports approximately 10 apart.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-5.png">
             <img alt="DIY Dining Table Bench Plans" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7671" height="361" src="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-5.png" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Step 6:
            </strong>
            Now screw the breadboard ends into the tops of the legs as shown below. The edge of the breadboard is centered on the leg.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-6.png">
             <img alt="Making a Dining Table Bench" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7672" height="361" src="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-6.png" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Step 7:
            </strong>
            Take a length measurement between the breadboard ends. Be very precise and cut your first bench top piece(H).Place the board with one edge centered on the table and screw in place from the bottom through the supports (F). Measure the second bench top and put in place, screwing in through the supports.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-7A.png">
             <img alt="DIY Farm House Bench How To" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7676" height="361" src="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DIY-Bench-Step-7A.png" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Step 8:
            </strong>
            <span style="line-height: 1.6em;">
             Stand back and admire your beautiful bench.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Our bench and
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/2012/06/detailed-table-plans/">
             matching table
            </a>
            (pictured below)were stained using Minwax in Jacobean. There is one coat of wood stain primer, three coats of stain and three coats of polyurethane. We built it over a year ago and has held up very well so far.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/FinishedTableWatermark.jpg">
             <img alt="DIY Pottery Barn Dining Table and Matching Bench" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5802" height="398" src="http://littlegreenbow.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/FinishedTableWatermark.jpg" width="600"/>
            </a>
           </p>
