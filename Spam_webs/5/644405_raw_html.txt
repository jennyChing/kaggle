        <h2>
         54 Warning Signs That You Work In Social Media
        </h2>
        <div class="post_details clrfix">

        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-11792" height="200" src="http://socialfresh.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/social-media-gurus-300x200.jpg" title="social-media-gurus" width="300"/>
         I really hate when I have to deliver bad news to our readers, but the fact is, I know many of you are in danger of this illness. I personally have been battling against it fairly publicly, and yet I have noticed many of you might even have it worse.
        </p>
        <p>
         Those of you at standing desks right now might want to sit down.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some of you ARE in fact
         <strong>
          social media professionals
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         I know, it sounds horrible. And there really is no known cure to date. The best remedy right now, is to become Amish. And even that
         <a href="http://www.marketingdaze.com/2011/06/amish-finding-facebook/">
          seems to be slipping as a solution
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         So you might be asking yourself This is horrible, how can I tell if I have it? Well, luckily, there are some clear signals that you might be afflicted.
        </p>
        <p>
         These are a few of the warning signs
        </p>
        <h4>
         You might work in social media if
        </h4>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Your parents keep up with your life through your Twitter feed.
         </li>
         <li>
          You are actually using Google+.
         </li>
         <li>
          You have sent a DM to someone sitting within 5 feet of you.
         </li>
         <li>
          Its been years since someone mentioned news to you that you hadnt heard already.
          <a href="https://twitter.com/#!/dshanahan">
           Derek Shanahan
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You verbally hashtag real world conversations.
         </li>
         <li>
          You are the mayor of something other than your home. 
          <a href="https://twitter.com/#!/acappellamedia">
           John Hondroulis
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You judge anyone with a hotmail email address as not so hip.
         </li>
         <li>
          You own a t-shirt or jewelry with your Twitter handle on it.
         </li>
         <li>
          You look down on anyone that does not own an iPhone.
         </li>
         <li>
          You get distracted easi
          <a href="http://twitter.com/davedelaney">
           Dave Delaney
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You look down on anyone that does not own an android.
         </li>
         <li>
          You secretly judge blackberry owners.
         </li>
         <li>
          You run into people you have not seen for years and they know everything about your life through Facebook, Twitter and your blog.  Inspired by
          <a href="http://twitter.com/djwaldow">
           DJ Waldow
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You secretly judge QR codes that are on subway ads or in airplane magazines.  Inspired by
          <a href="http://twitter.com/unmarketing">
           Scott Stratten
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You sign up to social networks before there is any discernible value, just to be an early adopter.
         </li>
         <li>
          You have reached the friend limit on Facebook.
         </li>
         <li>
          You know that there is a friend limit on Facebook.
         </li>
         <li>
          Your mom just tells her friends that you work on the internet  Inspired by
          <a href="https://twitter.com/#!/davidspinks">
           David Spinks
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You checkin to a restaurant before actually speaking to anyone there.
         </li>
         <li>
          You not so secretly judge anyone following more people than are following them on Twitter.
         </li>
         <li>
          Your phone is usually face up on the bar or restaurant table when you are out.
         </li>
         <li>
          Your couch has Twitter, Facebook, Foursquare or Angry Bird pillows.
         </li>
         <li>
          Your world feels like its coming to an end when you get a low battery alert on your smartphone
          <a href="https://twitter.com/#!/Elysa">
           Elysa Rice
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You take photos thinking about how they will look on Facebook.
         </li>
         <li>
          You read whatever news you find on Facebook and Twitter.
         </li>
         <li>
          You are haunted by the Tweetdeck chirping sound. 
          <a href="http://twitter.com/nikisnotes">
           Nicole DAlonzo
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You secretly judge magazine and TV ads that promote their social profiles poorly.
         </li>
         <li>
          You secretly hate friends who have more Twitter followers than you.
         </li>
         <li>
          You have a backup plan for when Twitter goes down.
         </li>
         <li>
          When you have bad customer service, your first step is to find the companys Twitter handle.
         </li>
         <li>
          You complain about how bad Klout is while still signing in to check your score everyday.
         </li>
         <li>
          Your smartphone is your best friend.
         </li>
         <li>
          You hate when people use the word viral.
         </li>
         <li>
          You think of @
          <a href="http://twitter.com/GaryVee">
           GaryVee
          </a>
          every time you see an orange Crush soda.
         </li>
         <li>
          You read Mashable more than you read the USA Today.
         </li>
         <li>
          You know what a bookmarklet is.
         </li>
         <li>
          You have Google alerts setup for your own name.
         </li>
         <li>
          You are working on a strategy for people to like you. 
          <a href="http://twitter.com/therab">
           Ryan Boyles
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You love Twitter.
         </li>
         <li>
          You secretly hate Twitter.
         </li>
         <li>
          You respect Justin Beiber for his Twitter following and recently learned he plays music too.
         </li>
         <li>
          You assume someone is talking about social media instead of pending nuptials when they mention the word engagement. 
          <a href="https://plus.google.com/103482450749250646854">
           Dave Cutler
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You get bored reading news that islonger than 140 characters.
         </li>
         <li>
          All of the parties and events you go to are from Facebook invites.
         </li>
         <li>
          Spike Jones has ever made fun of you.  Inspired by
          <a href="https://plus.google.com/106107925365647314226">
           Jason Falls
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          No one in your family is capable of explaining to their friends what exactly it is that you do.
          <a href="http://www.facebook.com/mandila">
           Mandi Laine
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          Your significant other asks, Are you still working, or just tweeting? 
          <a href="http://twitter.com/therab">
           Ryan Boyles
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You never ask to redeem Foursquare specials because you hate explaining them to your server.
         </li>
         <li>
          You think that your friends that are not on Facebook dont have birthdays.
         </li>
         <li>
          Your use Pinterest to write your letter to Santa.  Inspired by
          <a href="http://pinterest.com/nikisnotes/">
           Nicole DAlonzo
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          You ask your coworkers and friends for a big favor  to help Retweet your latest clients Twitter campaign.
         </li>
         <li>
          You are fully aware that Auto DMs are what is really wrong with America.
         </li>
         <li>
          When you completely lose your voice, you use Twitter to ask those sitting with you to pass the butter, please #truestory 
          <a href="https://twitter.com/#!/lvm">
           Lea Marino
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          As much as you say you hate the term you secretly hope that someone calls you a guru 
          <a href="https://plus.google.com/112506923444042812138">
           Simon Salt
          </a>
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         Thanks to
         <a href="https://plus.google.com/101075569626939618739/posts/8KSuD9ufEF3">
          our Google+ fans
         </a>
         for the help.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have any additions,
         <strong>
          please add them below in the comments
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
