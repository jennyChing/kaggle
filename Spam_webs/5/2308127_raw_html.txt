      Nokia's Purity HD stereo headset by Monster goes Pro, gains Bluetooth, NFC and noise cancellation (update)
     </h1>
     <p class="byline">
      <span class="byline-container">
       <img alt="blogger-avatar" class="avatar" height="24" src="http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-global/dims/GLOB/5/24/24/http://www.blogcdn.com/www.engadget.com/media/2013/07/akatrent5.jpg" width="24"/>

        by

       <a data-image="http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-global/dims/GLOB/5/24/24/http://www.blogcdn.com/www.engadget.com/media/2013/07/akatrent5.jpg" href="/about/editors/joe-pollicino/">

      <a class="aol-share-permalink" data-bid="21" data-pid="20314864" href="mailto:yourfriend@email.com?subject=Nokia%27s%20Purity%20HD%20stereo%20headset%20by%20Monster%20goes%20Pro%2C%20gains%20Bluetooth%2C%20NFC%20and%20noise%20cancellation%20%28update%29&amp;body=www.engadget.com/2012/09/02/nokias-purity-hd-stereo-headset-by-monster-goes-pro-gains-blue/" title="Nokia's Purity HD stereo headset by Monster goes Pro, gains Bluetooth, NFC and noise cancellation (update)">

    <div class="copy post-body" data-author="joe-pollicino" data-mn-header="93319229" data-mn-onvis="93431222" data-mn-rail-topper="93310581" data-mn-sponsor-logo="" data-mobile-onvis="93431222" data-mobile-placement="93424580" data-original-publication-date="09-02-2012" data-postid="20314864" data-prop19="bh 940|bh-940|Bh940|head detection|HeadDetection|headphones|headset|mobilepostcross|monster|near field communication|NearFieldCommunication|NFC|noise cancellation|NoiseCancellation|nokia|nokie purity hd pro|NokiePurityHdPro|purity|Purity HD|purity hd pro|purity hd Pro headset by monster|PurityHd|PurityHdPro|PurityHdProHeadsetByMonster" data-sourceid="original_content" data-title="Nokia's Purity HD stereo headset by Monster goes Pro, gains Bluetooth, NFC and noise cancellation (update)" data-url="www.engadget.com/2012/09/02/nokias-purity-hd-stereo-headset-by-monster-goes-pro-gains-blue/" data-videopresence="video_youtube_entrybody" itemprop="text">
     <div class="article-content" data-swiftype-name="body" data-swiftype-type="text">
      <p class="image-container" style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2012/09/02/nokias-purity-hd-stereo-headset-by-monster-goes-pro-gains-blue/">
        <img alt="Nokia's Purity HD Headset by Monster goes Pro, ditches cord with Bluetooth in the process" data-src-height="300" data-src-width="465" src="http://www.blogcdn.com/www.engadget.com/media/2012/09/monsterpurityfamily465.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       If Nokia doesn't show off at least one
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2012/08/31/nokia-lumia-820-920-leak/">
        banana
       </a>
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2012/08/30/nokia-blurrycam/">
        -hued
       </a>
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/tag/lumia/">
        Lumia
       </a>
       smartphone come
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2012/08/15/nokia-microsoft-windows-phone/">
        September 5th
       </a>
       , we're going to be extremely confused. Say howdy to the Pro-monikered followup to its corded
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2011/10/26/nokia-unveils-purity-hd-stereo-headset-with-a-little-help-from-m/">
        Purity HD stereo headset
       </a>
       by Monster, which gains Bluetooth (no word on what version), NFC for pairing and active noise cancellation. Similar to its recently revealed
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2012/08/30/nokia-jbl-play-up-portable-speaker/">
        PlayUp speaker
       </a>
       , the company's teasing the Purity HD Pro as an audio accessory that'll "perfectly match your Lumia" in a choice of black, white, red and yellow. The Pro is essentially identical to its predecessor, but now the folding earcups also serve as a pseudo-on/off switch
       <strike>
        for its anc functionality
       </strike>
       for the unit itself. As you might expect, all that tech and
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2009/04/17/engadget-endorses-monster-cable-uh-hell-no/">
        Monster
       </a>
       branding will cost ya a spendy 299 (about $376) -- nearly as much as
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2012/08/02/parrot-zik-by-starck-review/">
        Parrot's Zik by Starck
       </a>
       .
       <strike>
        Further details are currently unavailable, except that
       </strike>
       the cans will reach store shelves near this year's close.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Update:
       </strong>
       You'll find a video hands-on by way of
       <em>
        The Nokia Blog
       </em>
       <a href="http://youtu.be/1jiRvKHskN4">
        here
       </a>
       . According a Nokia rep in that video, the earpads have a head detection sensor for automatically enabling ANC (similar to the Zik), while plugging in an included cable will let the headphones function sans Bluetooth. Furthermore, the company also noted to
       <em>
        TNB
       </em>
       that the battery should last about 24 hours for music or calls, with a week's worth of life on standby.
      </p>
