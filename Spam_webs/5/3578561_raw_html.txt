     <p>
      <b>
       William G.
      </b>
      "
      <b>
       Jerry
      </b>
      "
      <b>
       Boykin
      </b>
      was the United States Deputy
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undersecretary_of_Defense_for_Intelligence" title="Undersecretary of Defense for Intelligence">
       Undersecretary of Defense for Intelligence
      </a>
      under President
      <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">
       George W. Bush
      </a>
      from 2002 to 2007 and is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Right" title="Christian Right">
       conservative Christian
      </a>
      political activist. During his 36-year career in the military he spent 13 years in the
      <a href="/wiki/Delta_Force" title="Delta Force">
       Delta Force
      </a>
      , including two years as its commander, and was involved in numerous high-profile missions, including the 1980 Iran hostage rescue attempt, the 1992 hunt for
      <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Escobar" title="Pablo Escobar">
       Pablo Escobar
      </a>
      in Colombia, and the
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mogadishu_(1993)" title="Battle of Mogadishu (1993)">
       Black Hawk Down
      </a>
      incident in
      <a href="/wiki/Mogadishu" title="Mogadishu">
       Mogadishu
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">
       Somalia
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timep_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-timep-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He is an author and teaches at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hampden-Sydney_College" title="Hampden-Sydney College">
       Hampden-Sydney College
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">
       Virginia
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hsc.edu_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-hsc.edu-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He is currently executive vice president at the
      <a href="/wiki/Family_Research_Council" title="Family Research Council">
       Family Research Council
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=William_G._Boykin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      William G. "Jerry" Boykin was born on April 19, 1948 in
      <a href="/wiki/Wilson,_North_Carolina" title="Wilson, North Carolina">
       Wilson, North Carolina
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He attended New Bern high school and was the captain of the football team. He graduated from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virginia_Polytechnic_Institute_and_State_University" title="Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University">
       Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University
      </a>
      (Virginia Tech) with a bachelors degree in English in 1971.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_military_career">
       Early military career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=William_G._Boykin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early military career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As a young officer, he held positions in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._2nd_Armored_Division" title="U.S. 2nd Armored Division">
       2nd Armored Division
      </a>
      at Fort Hood, Texas, and in the
      <a href="/wiki/101st_Airborne_Division" title="101st Airborne Division">
       101st Airborne Division
      </a>
      in Vietnam, and served as a company commander in the
      <a href="/wiki/24th_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="24th Infantry Division (United States)">
       24th Infantry Division
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Mechanized_infantry" title="Mechanized infantry">
       Mechanized
      </a>
      ) at Fort Stewart, Georgia.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-waponov5_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-waponov5-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iirusa_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-iirusa-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      From 19781993 he was assigned in various capacities to the 1st Special Forces Operational Detachment  Delta, or
      <a href="/wiki/Delta_Force" title="Delta Force">
       Delta Force
      </a>
      . Lieutenant Colonel L.H. "Bucky" Burruss, helping with Delta Force selection at the time, recalled that Boykin "had a bad knee and I thought he would never make it.... I thought, I hate to see this guy busting his [butt], I don't see how he can make it on this bad road wheel, but he surprised us." In fact, a Fort Bragg
      <a href="/wiki/Psychologist" title="Psychologist">
       psychologist
      </a>
      almost ended Boykin's career, wanting to exclude him from the Delta Force because he was "too religious". However, he was finally accepted into the Delta Force at the age of 29. Burruss wrote at the time that "Jerry Boykin is a Christian gentleman of the highest order." Boykin believed God had a hand in things: "God led me into the Delta Force.... And He said to me, 'This is where you ought to be.'"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-waponov5_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-waponov5-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Overseas_deployments">
       Overseas deployments
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=William_G._Boykin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Overseas deployments">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      By 1980 he was the
      <a href="/wiki/Delta_Force" title="Delta Force">
       Delta Force
      </a>
      operations officer on the April 2425
      <a href="/wiki/Operation_Eagle_Claw" title="Operation Eagle Claw">
       Iranian hostage rescue attempt
      </a>
      . Boykin called it "the greatest disappointment of my professional career because we didn't bring home 53 Americans."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Despite this, his "faith was strengthened" believing he had witnessed "a miracle": "Not one man who stood with us in the desert and pleaded for God to go with us was killed or even injured that night."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-waponov5_5-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-waponov5-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1983 Major Boykin was in charge of a 'special' security detail consisting of OC Delta 9 and one OC CIA SA officer in
      <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">
       South Korea
      </a>
      to gain intelligence on the troop build up by
      <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">
       North Korea
      </a>
      on the
      <a href="/wiki/Korean_Demilitarized_Zone" title="Korean Demilitarized Zone">
       DMZ
      </a>
      and as additional security for President Reagan's visit.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1989, Boykin took part in
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_invasion_of_Panama" title="United States invasion of Panama">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Religious.2FPolitical_views_and_comments">

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

     </h3>
     <p>
      Boykin, a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Born-again_Christian" title="Born-again Christian">
       born-again Christian
      </a>
      has gained attention for his Christian views over the years and some of his public remarks which cast the
      <a href="/wiki/War_on_Terror" title="War on Terror">
       War on Terror
      </a>

     <p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="War_on_Terror_tactics">

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <p>
      The
      <i>
       New York Times
      </i>
      reported on March 18, 2006, that, when asked by
      <a href="/wiki/Under_Secretary_of_Defense_for_Intelligence" title="Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence">
       Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Cambone" title="Stephen Cambone">
       Stephen Cambone
      </a>
      to "get to the bottom" of abuses committed by an elite counterinsurgency task force, Boykin found no pattern to them, despite reporter alleged ample evidence to the contrary.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>

        </span>

     <p>

      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">
        The Guardian
       </a>
      </i>
      connected Boykin with secret Israeli counterinsurgency assistance in Iraq, allegedly including assassination squads.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>

      <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">

      </a>
      's former senior adviser and current Washington bureau chief for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salon.com" title="Salon.com">
       Salon.com
      </a>
      , claimed that towards the end of 2003, it was Boykin who, under Donald Rumsfeld's orders, advised then
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camp_X-Ray" title="Camp X-Ray">
       Camp X-Ray
      </a>
      head
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_General_(United_States)" title="Major General (United States)">
       Major General
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Miller_(MG)" title="Geoffrey Miller (MG)">
       Geoffrey Miller
      </a>
      in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guantanamo_Bay_detainment_camp" title="Guantanamo Bay detainment camp">
       Guantanamo
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Abu_Ghraib" title="Abu Ghraib">
       Abu Ghraib
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">
       Iraqi
      </a>
      prison system.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2003,
      <a href="/wiki/Seymour_Hersh" title="Seymour Hersh">
       Seymour Hersh
      </a>

      <i>
       New Yorker
      </i>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Forces" title="Special Forces">
       Special Forces
      </a>
      approach to fighting the War on Terror.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newyorker.com_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-newyorker.com-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/War_on_Terror" title="War on Terror">
       War on Terror
      </a>
      special operations and USA's
      <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Program" title="Phoenix Program">
       Phoenix Program
      </a>
      during the
      <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">
       Vietnam War
      </a>
      , he said: "I think were running that kind of program. Were going after these people. Killing or capturing these people is a legitimate mission for the department. I think were doing what the Phoenix Program was designed to do, without all of the secrecy."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">

        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
