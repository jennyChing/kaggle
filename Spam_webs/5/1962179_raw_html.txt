         </strong>
        </em>
        I dont know about you, but Im always sad to see the summer go. Like really,
        <em>
         really
        </em>
        sad. Im a summer girl, one hundred and ten percent. Winter makes me feel like I need to curl up and die. Not to be confused with Curl up &amp; Dye, a hair salon in what movie? Ten points to Gryffindor for the first one to get it right.
       </div>
       <div>
       </div>
       <div>
        <h6>
         <span style="color: #ff6600;">
          <em>
           Are you here from StumbleUpon? Thanks so much for visiting! If you like what you see, I have plenty more where this came from. Use the FOLLOW US buttons on my blog to
           <a href="http://feedburner.google.com/fb/a/mailverify?uri=petitelefantcom" rel="nofollow">
            <span style="color: #ff6600;">
             subscribe
            </span>
           </a>
           !
          </em>
          THANK YOU
          <em>
           !
          </em>
         </span>
        </h6>
       </div>
       <div>
        <em>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </em>
       </div>
       <div>
        <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/citrus-scrub.jpg">
         <img alt="how to citrus scrub" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10968 pin-it" height="445" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/citrus-scrub.jpg" title="citrus scrub" width="575"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div>
        <em>
         Anyway.
        </em>
       </div>
       <div>
        <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/lemons.jpg">
         <img alt="sliced lemons" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11135 pin-it" height="384" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/lemons.jpg" title="lemons" width="575"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div>
        I thought the best way to preserve my love of summer was to make a citrus salt scrub. Its a little bit like the
        <a href="http://petitelefant.com/how-to-make-homemade-sugar-scrub/">
         sugar scrub
        </a>
        I make in the winter. It smells like heaven in the shower, a little drop of citrus sunshine for your day.
       </div>
       <div>
        <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/citrus-scrub-ingredients.jpg">
         <img alt="ingredients for citrus scrub" class="aligncenter pin-it" height="575" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/citrus-scrub-ingredients.jpg" title="citrus scrub ingredients" width="384"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div>
        So heres how it rolls out. You need four ingredients, and theyre all
        <strong>
         completely natural
        </strong>
        .
        <em>
         Thats it
        </em>
        :
       </div>
       <div>
        <strong>
         Ingredients:
        </strong>
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1/2 cup sea salt
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 cup olive oil
         </li>
         <li>
          1 or 2 slices of lemon
         </li>
         <li>
          1 or 2 slices of orange
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
       <div>
        <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/citrus-scrub1.jpg">
         <img alt="how to citrus scrub" class="aligncenter pin-it" height="575" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/citrus-scrub1.jpg" title="citrus scrub" width="445"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div>
        <strong>
         Step 1:
        </strong>
        Add the olive oil and sea salt into a bowl or blender.
       </div>
       <div>
        (You can do this one of two ways: in a blender, or in a bowl. I chose to use the
        <a href="http://www.blendtec.com/products/view/6">
         Twister Jar
        </a>
        on my Blendtec* because it grinds up all the little tiny pieces, and I wanted to get all the little bits of orange and lemon peel. If you do it by hand in a bowl, chop up the peel really
        <em>
         really
        </em>
        well, or omit the peels completely.).
       </div>
       <div>
        I personally really like chunks of citrus peel in my citrus scrub. Makes it more,
        <em>
         citrus-y
        </em>
        .
       </div>
       <div>
        And exfoliate-ish.
       </div>
       <div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/blendtec.jpg">
          <img alt="blendtec blender" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10971 pin-it" height="575" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/blendtec.jpg" title="blender" width="384"/>
         </a>
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step 2:
         </strong>
         Chop the fruit
        </p>
        <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/citrus.jpg">
         <img alt="citrus fruit" class="aligncenter pin-it" height="384" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/citrus.jpg" title="citrus" width="575"/>
        </a>
        <p>
         I used about two slices of lemon and two slices of orange and popped them straight into the blender. Again, if youre not going to use a blender, chop the pieces up really
         <em>
          really
         </em>
         small. I used both the fruit and the peel, because I was using the blender, but if you arent using a blender squeeze the fruit as much as you can to get the juice out. You want all the juice you can get, and then chop up the peel and mix it up in a bowl.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/lemon.jpg">
          <img alt="slice a lemon" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10972 pin-it" height="367" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/lemon.jpg" title="lemon" width="575"/>
         </a>
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step 3:
         </strong>
         Mix
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/blender1.jpg">
          <img alt="blending fruit" class="aligncenter pin-it" height="575" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/blender1.jpg" title="blender" width="384"/>
         </a>
         Can you believe that?
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats all folks,
         <em>
          thats all she wrote
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <a href="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/how-to-citrus-scrub.jpg">
         <img alt="homemade citrus scrub" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10976 pin-it" height="575" src="http://petitelefant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/how-to-citrus-scrub.jpg" title="how to citrus scrub" width="439"/>
        </a>
        <p>
         You can use this citrus scrub one of two ways: you can put it next to your kitchen sink and use it after you wash your hands, or you can use it in the shower like I do. If you use it in the shower, mix it around with your hands and apply it all over your body with your hands or a washcloth. Rinse it off completely and pat yourself dry so you dont rub off all the olive oil.
        </p>
        <p>
         Have fun with this one, its double delicious.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
          Disclaimer:
         <a href="http://www.blendtec.com/">
          Blentec*
         </a>
         sent me a super rock star blender to try out and adopt into my home. I love it very, very much. 
        </em>
       </p>
