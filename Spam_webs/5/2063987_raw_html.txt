      <h1>
       <a href="http://www.apartmentbarcelona.com/blog/2014/09/12/top-8-markets-in-barcelona/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to The Top 8 Markets in Barcelona">
        The Top 8 Markets in Barcelona
       </a>
      </h1>
      <ul class="post-meta">
       <li class="date">
        September 12, 2014 4:16 pm
       </li>
       <li class="author">
        <a href="http://www.apartmentbarcelona.com/blog/author/siobhankeeley/" rel="author" title="Posts by Siobhan - Apartment Barcelona Writer">
         Siobhan - Apartment Barcelona Writer
        </a>
       </li>
       <li class="comments">
        <a href="http://www.apartmentbarcelona.com/blog/2014/09/12/top-8-markets-in-barcelona/#respond" title="Comment on The Top 8 Markets in Barcelona">
         0 comments
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <div class="entry">
       <div class="wdgpo wdgpo_standard_count">
        <g:plusone count="true" href="http://www.apartmentbarcelona.com/blog/2014/09/12/top-8-markets-in-barcelona/" size="standard">
        </g:plusone>
       </div>
       <p>
        With the many markets that exist in Barcelona, it can be difficult to decide which ones to visit during your stay here. If youre looking to sample some delicious chocolate or fresh fruit we know a place! Or perhaps youd like to admire some local artists and their fantastic paintings? We know a place for that too! The city really has it all, from food markets to flea markets, theres something for the whole family! Here are our favourite
        <strong>
         markets in Barcelona
        </strong>
        :
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         1. L
         <img alt="Boqueria Market Barcelona La Rambla" class="alignleft wp-image-8232 size-medium" height="199" src="http://www.apartmentbarcelona.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Boqueria-Market-Barcelona-La-Rambla-300x199.jpg" title="Boqueria Market Barcelona La Rambla" width="300"/>
         a Boqueria
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Eat, shop and gossip like the locals at this world-famous market, also known as El Mercat de Sant Josep de la Boqueria. This
        <strong>
         famous Barcelona market
        </strong>
        is ideally located on Las Ramblas, making it a must see for any visitor coming to the city.
       </p>
       <p>
        La Boqueria dates back hundreds of years and it was thought to be formed in the 1200s solely as a meat market. It wasnt until 1826 that the market became legally recognised and an official structure was built. Did you know that the roof that exists on the market today dates back to 1914?
       </p>
       <p>
        Whether youre looking for fish, fruits or tasty treats to satisfy your sweet tooth, La Boqueria never fails to disappoint! Tired after all that walking around? Why not grab a bite to eat at one of the many restaurants situated within the market.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Tip: come at the end of the day for a discount on their delicious fruit cups.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Location: Las Ramblas
        <br/>
        Metro: Liceu (Green Line L3)
        <br/>
        Open: Monday  Saturday 08:00  20:30
       </p>
       <h1>
       </h1>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         2. Sant Antoni Market
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        With quality foods at fabulous prices, this market is a firm favourite with the Catalan locals.
        <strong>
         Sant Antoni market in Barcelona
        </strong>
        is a great place to get away from the hoards of tourists and experience the local culture. You can even find a collectors book and coin market on Sundays from 8:00am, with a fantastic selection of magazines, comics, old books and postcards!
       </p>
       <p>
        Location: Ronda Sant Pau/Carrer Comte dUrgell
        <br/>
        Metro: Sant Antoni (Purple line L2)
        <br/>
        Open: Mondays  Saturdays 07:00  14:30; 17:00  20:30. Friday 07:00  20:30.
       </p>
       <h1>
       </h1>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         3. Fira de Nautumismo
         <img alt="Fira de Nautumismo Flea Market Barcelona" class="alignright wp-image-8234 size-medium" height="200" src="http://www.apartmentbarcelona.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Fira-de-Nautumismo-Flea-Market-Barcelona-300x200.jpg" title="Fira de Nautumismo Flea Market Barcelona" width="300"/>
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        The Fira de Nautumismo can be found in Plaa Reial in the Gothic Quarter, next to Las Ramblas. Here you can find rare and unique items from all around the world, so whether youre a collector or a bargain hunter, this
        <strong>
         Barcelona market
        </strong>
        is definitely worth a visit! Youll find a wide range of collectables from coins and stamps to antique jewellery and handmade gifts. Nearby the market, at the top of Ave. Portal de lAngel, there is also a small flea market consisting of craft and handmade gift stalls, ideal for picking up a unique souvenir.
       </p>
       <p>
        Location: Plaa Reial
        <br/>
        Metro: Liceu (Green Line L3)
        <br/>
        Open: Sundays 10:00  14:30
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         4. Fira Artesana &amp; Mercadillo de la Plaa de Sant Josep
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Located on Plaa del Pi, just off Las Ramblas, the Fira Artesana comprises of local producers from the Catalan region who come together monthly to sell their goods. You can expect to find a variety of edible goods here, from honey-infused cheeses to delicious homemade cupcakes! Whats not to like?
       </p>
       <p>
        The Mercadillo de la Plaa de Sant Josep is a tiny artists market, situated across from the Fira Artesana, that takes place every weekend. The 15 or so talented artists line up under the church to showcase their local works, from oil paintings to watercolours. If a portrait is what youre after, you can even catch some of the artists at the southern end of Las Ramblas in the evening.
       </p>
       <p>
        Location: Plaa del Pi and Plaa de Sant Josep Oriol.
        <br/>
        Metro: Liceu (Green Line L3)
        <br/>
        Open: The artists market is open every weekend. The Fira Artesana is open the 1
        <sup>
         st
        </sup>
        Friday and Saturday of every month.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         5. Mercat dels
         <img alt="Mercat Dels Encants Vells Barcelona Flea" class="alignleft wp-image-8237 size-medium" height="199" src="http://www.apartmentbarcelona.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Mercat-Dels-Encants-Vells-Barcelona-Flea-300x199.jpg" title="Mercat Dels Encants Vells Barcelona Flea" width="300"/>
         Encants Vells
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Encants Vells, located at Plaa de les Glries, is one of the oldest markets in Europe and is
        <strong>
         Barcelonas largest flea market
        </strong>
        . Here you can find around 500 traders selling a range of items, both old and new, from vintage clothing to home dcor. You can also catch public auctions on Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays between 7:15-9:00am. Who knows, you might even grab a bargain or two!
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Tip: arrive early as the best items will go fast!
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Location: Plaa de les Glries
        <br/>
        Metro: Glries, (Red Line, L1)
        <br/>
        Open: Monday, Wednesday, Friday and Saturday from 08:30
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         6. Mercat de la Concepci
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        This colourful
        <strong>
         Barcelona flower market
        </strong>
        is located near Passeig de Grcia, in the Eixample district, and is a must see for any gardening enthusiast. You can expect to find a wide variety of garden flowers and seeds to choose from, unlike any other market in Barcelona. Dont be put off if flowers arent really your thing, there is also a food market selling a range of fruits, vegetables, meats and fish.
       </p>
       <p>
        Location: C Arag, 311
        <br/>
        Metro: Passeig de Grcia (Green Line L3) or (Yellow Line L4)
        <br/>
        Open: 01 September  30 June
        <br/>
        Mondays: 08:00  15:00. Tuesday  Friday: 08:00  15:00. Saturday: 08:00  15:00
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         7. Mercat de Santa Caterina
        </strong>
        <img alt="Mercat Santa Caterina Best Markets in Barcelona" class="alignright wp-image-8213 size-medium" height="224" src="http://www.apartmentbarcelona.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Mercat-Santa-Caterina-Best-Markets-in-Barcelona1-300x224.jpg" title="Mercat Dels Encants Vells Barcelona Flea" width="300"/>
       </h2>
       <p>
        At a first glimpse you may notice the brightly coloured roof on this market, not a work of Gaud but actually a design drawn up in 1997 by local city planners and architects. Today you can find everything food related and more in the Santa Caterina Market, from quality meats including traditional Spanish ham to fresh fruit and veg  theres even a small Caprabo supermarket inside. The
        <strong>
         Santa Caterina Market in Barcelona
        </strong>
        is a great place to visit if youre looking for a more relaxed atmosphere than the likes of La Boqueria.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Tip: stop for a delicious bite to eat at the tapas bar opposite the main entrance.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Location: Francesc Camb, 16
        <br/>
        Metro: L4 Jaume I
        <br/>
        Opening hours : Monday, Tuesday &amp; Wednesday 07.30  14:30/15:30approx, Thursday &amp; Friday 07:30-20:30, Saturday from 07:30-15:30
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         8. Mercat Gtic
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Open every Thursday (except in August) from 9am to 8pm, this antique and secondhand
        <strong>
         Barcelona market
        </strong>
        is located in the Gothic Quarter of Barcelona. Although the market is quite small, it is well stocked and there are some bargains to be had! Amongst the secondhand items you can find valuable collectors items such as dolls and old ceramics.
       </p>
       <p>
        Location: Avinguda de la Catedral, 6
        <br/>
        Metro: Jaume I (Yellow Line L4)
        <br/>
        Every Thursday from 9am to 9pm  Closed throughout August
       </p>
