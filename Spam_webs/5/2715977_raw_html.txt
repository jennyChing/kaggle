          <p>
           The Michelin Red Guide has awarded stars to over 500 restaurants in Tokyo, Yokohama, Kamakura, and in the Kansai region, and listed hundreds more of other praiseworthy establishments that did not fit the star criteria. You will find the complete listing in the highly recommended
           <a class="o" href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/2067169823?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=eupedia-21&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1634&amp;creative=6738&amp;creativeASIN=2067169823" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Michelin Tokyo, Yokohama, Kamakura &amp; Shonan
           </a>
           and
           <a class="o" href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/2067169483?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=eupedia-21&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1634&amp;creative=6738&amp;creativeASIN=2067169483" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Michelin Kyoto, Osaka, Kobe &amp; Nara
           </a>
           . As for 2012, Yokohama and Kamakura do not yet have any three-stars restaurants and are therefore not listed here.
          </p>
          <p>
           Apart from three French restaurants in Tokyo, all the restaurants listed serve Japanese cuisine.
          </p>
