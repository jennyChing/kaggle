                     Olla de frijoles means "a pot of beans"
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-cfpCcu9Ziwg/VWZMetTiYSI/AAAAAAAAzYU/Ks33W_vB8xU/s1600/IMG_7610.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

                     <br/>
                     Frijoles are a staple in the Mexican kitchen.
                     <br/>
                     Frijoles are an essential part of Mexican food.
                     <br/>
                     Pinto beans are the most common beans used,
                     <br/>
                     although beans change from region to region.
                     <br/>
                     In Mexican cooking,
                     <br/>
                     there are three ever present ingredients:
                     <br/>
                     chilies, corn and beans.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     In every Mexican home,
                     <br/>
                     there is a pot of beans simmering on the stove-top,
                     <br/>
                     simmering in the oven,
                     <br/>
                     or a pot of beans in the refrigerator ready to be used.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Mexican cooks take the process of simmering a pot of beans very seriously.
                     <br/>
                     Each cook has their own unique way of preparing them.
                     <br/>
                     Everything from the bean variety,
                     <br/>
                     the type of pot used,
                     <br/>
                     to the seasonings differ greatly from cook to cook.
