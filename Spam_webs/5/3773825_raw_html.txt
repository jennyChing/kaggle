          <div class="ingredients p-bg-t p-bg-faded">
           <h5 class="heading p-col">
            Ingredients
           </h5>
           <ul class="content ingredients-list">
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1 pot roast, about 4 pounds (lean chuck, round, rump)
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon butter
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1 medium onion, sliced
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             8 ounces sliced mushrooms, optional
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup water or
             <a href="/od/howtocook/r/How-to-Make-a-Rich-Beef-Stock.htm">
              beef stock
             </a>
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup ketchup
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup dry red wine
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 teaspoon salt
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1/8 teaspoon pepper
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             1 clove garlic, minced
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tablespoons cornstarch
            </li>
            <li itemprop="ingredients">
             3 tablespoons water
            </li>
           </ul>
           <div class="content">
            <ul class="meta">
             <li>
              <strong>
               Prep Time
              </strong>
              :
              <meta content="PT15M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
              15 minutes
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Cook Time
              </strong>
              :
              <meta content="PT480M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
              480 minutes
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
           <div class="content">
            <ul class="meta">
             <li>
              <strong>
               Total Time
              </strong>
              :
              <meta content="PT495M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
              495 minutes
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Yield
              </strong>
              :
              <span itemprop="recipeYield">
               6 to 8 Servings
              </span>
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="preparation expert-content-text" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           <h5 class="heading-style-a p-col">
            Preparation
           </h5>
           <p>
            Trim visible fat from roast; place in slow cooker. Cover and set on low while preparing remaining ingredients.
           </p>
           <p>
            In a skillet, heat butter over medium-low heat. Add onion and mushrooms, if using; saut until tender.
           </p>
           <p>
            In a small bowl, combine cooked onion and mushrooms, water, ketchup, wine, mustard, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, and minced garlic. Pour mixture over meat.
           </p>
           <p>
            Cover and cook on Low about 7 to 9 hours.
           </p>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense1">
           </div>
           <div class="radlinks ads-half" data-character-limit="null" data-display-inline="true" data-display-label="false" data-num-links="5" id="radlinks3">
           </div>
           <p class="cb-split">
            An even longer cooking time will make meat more tender and easy to shred, if you plan to use it for sandwiches.
           </p>
           <p>
            Remove meat and slice, cut up, or shred.
            <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/howtocook/fl/How-to-Keep-Hot-Food-Warm.htm">
             Keep meat warm
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Turn slow cooker to HIGH.
           </p>
           <p>
            Dissolve cornstarch in 3 tablespoons of water; stir into cooker.
           </p>
           <p>
            Cover and cook on HIGH 15 to 20 minutes or until thickened.
           </p>
           <p>
            Serve sauce with pot roast.
           </p>
