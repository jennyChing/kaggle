             </p>
             <p>
              There is a new challenge going around on Facebook right now.
             </p>
             <p>
              People are asking you to list 10 books that have affected your life in some way.
             </p>
             <p>
              The rules are:
              <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
               Dont take more than a few minutes and dont think too hard. They dont have to be the right books or great works of literature, just ones that have affected you in some way. Tag some friends and youre done!
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              Ive been tagged by three people (thank you
              <a href="https://twitter.com/katenolan" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://twitter.com/katenolan', 'Kate Nolan');" target="_blank">
               Kate Nolan
              </a>
              ,
              <a href="https://twitter.com/wbsmith200" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://twitter.com/wbsmith200', 'Bill Smith');" target="_blank">
               Bill Smith
              </a>
              , and
              <a href="http://www.law.northwestern.edu/professional-life/career/contact/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.law.northwestern.edu/professional-life/career/contact/', 'Pascale Bishop');" target="_blank">
               Pascale Bishop
              </a>
              ) and Ive had a hard time with it. Because, you know, dont think too hard doesnt apply when youre asked to choose just 10 books.
             </p>
             <p>
              Argh!
             </p>
             <p>
              But then I read that September 6 is
              <a href="http://www.seacoastonline.com/articles/20140903-LIFE-409030311" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.seacoastonline.com/articles/20140903-LIFE-409030311', 'Read a Book Day');" target="_blank">
               Read a Book Day
              </a>
              and decided to not only take the challenge, but to post it here (albeit a couple of days early).
             </p>
             <p>
              You know, in honor of GO READ A BOOK (I type this as I settle down to watch episode 5 of season 3 of
              <a href="http://www.netflix.com/WiMovie/70180183?locale=en-US" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.netflix.com/WiMovie/70180183?locale=en-US', 'The Killing');" target="_blank">
               The Killing
              </a>
              on Netflix).
             </p>
             <h3>
              Read a Book Day
             </h3>
             <ol>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/What-Would-Google-Do-Reverse-Engineering-ebook/dp/B001NLKYT2/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409792557&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=what+would+google+do" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/What-Would-Google-Do-Reverse-Engineering-ebook/dp/B001NLKYT2/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409792557&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=what+would+google+do', 'What Would Google Do');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 What Would Google Do
                </strong>
               </a>
               . I read this when I was trying to decide how to transition
               <a href="http://armentdietrich.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://armentdietrich.com', 'Arment Dietrich');" target="_blank">
                Arment Dietrich
               </a>
               and what the web might do for the business. The result began with 
               <a href="http://spinsucks.com/communication/arment-dietrich-is-no-longer-a-pr-firm/" target="_blank">
                Arment Dietrich is No Longer a PR Firm
               </a>
                in 2010.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Night-Circus-Vintage-Magic-ebook/dp/B004J4WKTW/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793382&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=night+circus" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Night-Circus-Vintage-Magic-ebook/dp/B004J4WKTW/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793382&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=night+circus', 'The Night Circus');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 The Night Circus
                </strong>
               </a>
               . I read this in the middle of my quest to write fiction (a project that has been backburnerd, but will make a comeback) and thought the writing was spectacular. So good, in fact, it scared me a bit from my own project.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Light-Between-Oceans-M-Stedman-ebook/dp/B0064CL1T2/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793402&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=the+light+between+oceans" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Light-Between-Oceans-M-Stedman-ebook/dp/B0064CL1T2/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793402&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=the+light+between+oceans', 'The Light Between Oceans');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 The Light Between Oceans
                </strong>
               </a>
               . For some reason, this book totally got into my soul. I sobbed and sobbed while I read. And I cant get it out of my brain.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Daps&amp;field-keywords=harry%20potter&amp;sprefix=harry+%2Caps" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Daps&amp;field-keywords=harry%20potter&amp;sprefix=harry+%2Caps', 'Harry Potter');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Harry Potter
                </strong>
               </a>
               . (
               <a href="http://twitter.com/howiegoldfarb" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://twitter.com/howiegoldfarb', 'Howie Goldfarb');" target="_blank">
                Howie Goldfarb
               </a>
               , you zip it!) Yes, the entire series. I got into them 15 years ago, when Mr. D and I first became acquainted. I convinced him to read themand Im still convinced he did it just to impress meand then we had lots to talk about. Those books began a great relationship our marriage has with books.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/The-Lost-Son-Brent-Spencer/dp/1559702664/ref=sr_1_5?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409792288&amp;sr=8-5&amp;keywords=brent+spencer" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/The-Lost-Son-Brent-Spencer/dp/1559702664/ref=sr_1_5?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409792288&amp;sr=8-5&amp;keywords=brent+spencer', 'The Lost Son');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 The Lost Son
                </strong>
               </a>
               . My college advisorBrent Spencerwrote this book. I remember the first class I had with him. He taught us a new language. On the first day, he wrote on the board, Xter and said, You must learn my language. This means character. Ill never forget that.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Little-House-Collection-Full-Color/dp/0060754281/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=undefined&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=little+house+on+the+prairie" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Little-House-Collection-Full-Color/dp/0060754281/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=undefined&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=little+house+on+the+prairie', 'Little House on the Prairie');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Little House on the Prairie
                </strong>
               </a>
               . Im not sure how many times I read every book in that series (my mom can probably tell you), but I loved them as a kid. Itd be interesting to go back and read them again.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Groundswell-Winning-Transformed-Social-Technologies-ebook/dp/B004XOZ7K2/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793722&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=groundswell" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Groundswell-Winning-Transformed-Social-Technologies-ebook/dp/B004XOZ7K2/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793722&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=groundswell', 'Groundswell');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Groundswell
                </strong>
               </a>
               . The timing of this book was perfect because it was right when Robert Scoble was talking about this Twitter thing and many of us were trying to figure out what social media meant to us personally and professionally. I still refer back to it quite often, particularly when working with clients who are scared to jump into the fray.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Fountainhead-Ayn-Rand-ebook/dp/B002OSXDAU/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793781&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=the+fountainhead" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Fountainhead-Ayn-Rand-ebook/dp/B002OSXDAU/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793781&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=the+fountainhead', 'The Fountainhead');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 The Fountainhead
                </strong>
               </a>
               . When I was a junior in high school, I chose this as my freebie book. Ill never forget my English teacher telling my parents that it was a pretty heady book for a 16-year-old and he was impressed I not only read it, but totally understood it. Of course, I feel differently about it as an adult, but it was pretty mind blowing at that age.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Me-Talk-Pretty-One-Day-ebook/dp/B000SEJHRA/ref=sr_1_1_ha?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793807&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=me+talk+pretty+one+day" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Me-Talk-Pretty-One-Day-ebook/dp/B000SEJHRA/ref=sr_1_1_ha?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1409793807&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=me+talk+pretty+one+day', 'Me Talk Pretty One Day');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Me Talk Pretty One Day
                </strong>
               </a>
               . This was my introduction to David Sedaris and it was perfect. I remember I was in New York City, working on a celebrity chef program for The Catfish Institute and a colleague recommended it. I started reading it on the flight home and I laughed so hardand out loudthat I had to stop reading and put it in the overhead so I could stop embarrassing myself.
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Daps&amp;field-keywords=trixie%20belden&amp;sprefix=trixie+b%2Caps" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Daps&amp;field-keywords=trixie%20belden&amp;sprefix=trixie+b%2Caps', 'Trixie Belden');" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Trixie Belden
                </strong>
               </a>
               . Until I thought about Little House on the Prairie, Id totally forgotten about Trixie Belden! Im fairly certain I read every book in that series 1,000 times. It also is interesting that I
               <em>
                still
               </em>
               love detective stories (hello, The Killing!). Some things never change.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <h3>
              Your Turn
             </h3>
             <p>
              Ill officially tag people on Facebook when I post this there, but Id like to challenge each of you to think about the 10 books that have changed your life in some way.
             </p>
             <p>
              If youre so inclined, Id love to see the list in the comments here.
             </p>
             <p>
              If youve already taken the challenge, copy and paste it!
             </p>
             <p>
              And lets make a pact to get everyone to read a book on September 6!
             </p>

             </p>

                     <p>
                      No particular order, and there are others that influenced me greatly.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       A Wrinkle in Time
                      </em>
                      - Read the first time in fifth grade. Made me want to tell stories.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Hobbit/Lord of the Rings
                      </em>
                      - Umm, Tolkien. Enough said.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       The Saint Who Would Be Santa Claus
                      </em>
                      - A non-fiction book. Really shed light on the man that Santa Claus is based on and made faith seem alive. The real St. Nicholas was pretty intense. The fist fight was a hoot.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Confessions of an S.O.B
                      </em>
                      - Al Neuharth's book isn't necessarily the best example of leadership we can turn to, but he talks a lot about overcoming adversity and challenges. I think it made me mentally tougher.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Dracula
                      </em>
                      - A real vampire, no sparklies. More importantly, a pretty scary book and a novel that showed me classic literature doesn't have to be dry and boring.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Lord of the Flies
                      </em>
                      - I was in 5th or 6th grade when I read this and just remember the shock I was in at the thought of how those boys changed and what they did to one another.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Something Wicked This Way Comes
                      </em>
                      - As a young lad, it was a stark reminder - one that has stuck with me - that evil can strike anywhere and everywhere.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Stranger than Fiction: True Stories
                      </em>
                      - I'm not a big Chuck Palahnuik fan, but this collection of essays were a stark reminder that he is one heck of a writer and that non-fiction often stranger and more interesting than fiction.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Mere Christianity
                      </em>
                      - CS Lewis, who is an awesome fiction writer, wrote some of the most thoughtful and powerful Christian non-fiction, including this apologetics book.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Cop Hater
                      </em>
                      - In 1984 I was at a garage sale and on whim, picked up this paperback for 10 cents. The book itself wasn't that influential to me, but it launched me into a world of mystery and crime fiction, which I still read voraciously to this day. I get more enjoyment from mystery fiction than any genre, and all my published fiction falls under the crime genre.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <br/>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Bonus:
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Battlefield Earth
                      </em>
                      - The book I read, put down and thought, "If this can get published then I KNOW I can get published."
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       Proverbs/Psalms
                      </em>
                      - Yes yes...The Bible. I know. But Bill Graham once said he reads Proverbs daily to learn how to have relationships with other people and Psalms to learn how to have relationships with God. Too much good advice in these books to pass up.
                     </p>
