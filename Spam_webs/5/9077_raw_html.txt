         <span class="cb-title-fi">
          <h1 class="entry-title cb-entry-title cb-single-title" itemprop="headline">
           Itaipu Dam in Brazil
          </h1>
          <div class="cb-byline">
           <div class="cb-author cb-byline-element">
            <i class="fa fa-user">
            </i>
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/author/jack-bennet/">
             Jack Bennet
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="cb-date cb-byline-element">
            <i class="fa fa-clock-o">
            </i>
            <time class="updated" datetime="2014-09-01">
             September 1, 2014
            </time>
           </div>
           <div class="cb-category cb-byline-element">
            <i class="fa fa-folder">
            </i>
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/category/south-america-2/brazil-south-america-2/" title="View all posts in Brazil">
             Brazil
            </a>
            ,
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/category/south-america-2/" title="View all posts in South America">
             South America
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="cb-comments cb-byline-element">
            <span>
             <i class="fa fa-comment">
             </i>
             <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/itaipu-dam-brazil/#comments">
              3 Comments
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
          </div>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="cb-image" data-type="background" style="background-image: url(http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3345.jpg)">

        <div id="cb-parallax-bg">
        </div>

       <div class="wrap clearfix" id="cb-content">
        <div class="clearfix" id="main" role="main">
         <article class="clearfix post-413 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-brazil-south-america-2 category-south-america-2 tag-brazil-2 tag-foz-do-iguacu tag-iguassu tag-itaipu-dam tag-jack-bennet tag-paraguay tag-south-america" id="post-413" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting" role="article">
          <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
           <a id="dd_start">
           </a>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            As a budding engineer I had heard about the
            <a href="https://www.itaipu.gov.br/en/tourism/dam">
             Itaipu Dam
            </a>
            long before I went travelling to South America and it was certainly a place I wanted to visit while in Iguassu. It is a hydroelectric dam located on the border between Paraguay and Brazil and is the greatest hydroelectric power producing dam in the world at 98.6 TWh in 2013. The dam is jointly owned by both countries with 50% of the power going to each country; this is enough to power 75% of Paraguay and 17% of Brazil!
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The dam took 10 years to build and was completed in 1984. It is 196m tall and the volume of concrete used to build it was 15 times greater than that of the channel tunnel! The dam cost UK 11.8 billion (US $19.6 billion) and I feel it rightly deserves its place as one of the
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wonders_of_the_World">
             seven wonders of the modern world
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_415" style="width: 610px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3345.jpg">
             <img alt="Itaipu Dam from above in Brazil" class="wp-image-415" height="381" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3345-1024x649.jpg" width="600"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             The Itaipu Dam from above
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <span id="more-413">
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Unfortunately as a consequence of building this dam, the
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gua%C3%ADra_Falls">
             Guara Falls
            </a>
            , which was the worlds largest waterfall by volume, was drowned by the newly formed Itaipu reservoir. The Brazilian government decided to dynamite the rock face to facilitate safer navigation thus eliminating the possibility of restoring the falls in the future. Shortly before this happened, 80 people died when a bridge overlooking the falls collapsed as tourists sought a last glimpse of the falls.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_414" style="width: 460px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/1408858-bigthumbnail.jpg">
             <img alt="Guara Falls in Brazil" class="size-full wp-image-414" height="300" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/1408858-bigthumbnail.jpg" width="450"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             The once magnificent Guara Falls
            </p>
           </div>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            To reach the dam I could catch bus numbers 101, 102 or 104 which all left just down the road from
            <a href="http://www.tripadvisor.co.uk/Hotel_Review-g303444-d2157467-Reviews-Green_House_Hostel-Foz_do_Iguacu_State_of_Parana.html">
             my hostel
            </a>
            in Foz do Iguau, Brazil. The bus took about 30 minutes; I exited it at the entrance to the Itaipu National park and walked to the Itaipu Dam tour centre which was just alongside.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_427" style="width: 610px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Itaipu_Binacional.jpg">
             <img alt="Entrance to the Itaipu Dam" class="wp-image-427" height="301" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Itaipu_Binacional-1024x513.jpg" width="600"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Entrance to the Itaipu Dam
            </p>
           </div>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The dam is very popular so I would recommend pre-booking, I had pre-booked onto the
            <a href="https://www.itaipu.gov.br/en/turismo/special-tour">
             special tour
            </a>
            ; there was also a panoramic tour available but you dont see nearly as much on this. These tours were R$ 64 and R$ 26 Real respectively (17 and 7 GBP), running throughout the day, every day.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The tour took about 2 hours and 30 minutes with 7 different stops along the way. We watched a short film about the dam before setting off on the bus to our first stop which was a panoramic view of the dam. From here we could see a great view of the whole dam with huge spillways off to the left and lots of turbines further to the right. The water colour had changed to brown in the recent weeks before my visit due to immense rainfall upstream.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_416" style="width: 610px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3357.jpg">
             <img alt="Jack Bennet at the Itaipu Dam" class="wp-image-416" height="450" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3357-1024x768.jpg" width="600"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Itaipu Dam from afar, unfortunately the camera I had with me didnt allow for panoramic shots
            </p>
           </div>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The bus then drove us to the top of the dam which gave great views of the surrounding area and the huge reservoir next to us. We were told not to walk any further along the dam than we were as that would be trespassing on Paraguayan land for which we could get into trouble.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_417" style="width: 610px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3372.jpg">
             <img alt="On top of the Itaipu Dam" class="wp-image-417" height="450" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3372-1024x768.jpg" width="600"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Top of the Itaipu Dam with the Paraguayan border just ahead
            </p>
           </div>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Next stop was inside the dam, passing the huge ducts through which 700,000 litres of water flow every second, or half the flow of the Iguassu Falls within each one! The dam is hollow so as to reduce the amount of concrete needed in its production; this enabled us to have great views of the internal castings of the dam with bigdrops down to the dam base.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_419" style="width: 510px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3394.jpg">
             <img alt="Entrance to the innards of the Dam" class="wp-image-419" height="667" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3394-e1409591825792-768x1024.jpg" width="500"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Entrance to the innards of the Dam
            </p>
           </div>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_420" style="width: 610px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3399-e1409592163764.jpg">
             <img alt="base of Itaipu Dam" class="wp-image-420" height="446" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3399-e1409592163764-1024x761.jpg" width="600"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Long way down to the base of the Dam
            </p>
           </div>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The central command room station was the next place we headed to; we were able to cross a yellow strip on the floor which represented the border between Brazil and Paraguay. This line appeared to follow through to the control room itself where half of it controlled the Brazilian power and the other half Paraguayan power. Half of the power from the 20 generator units is generated at 50 Hz for Paraguay and the other half at 60 Hz for Brazil. As the output capacity of the Paraguayan generators far exceeds Paraguays requirements, most of their production is exported to the Brazilian side where its converted to 60 Hz.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_423" style="width: 610px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3415-e1409592432204.jpg">
             <img alt="Jack Bennet with one foot in Brazil, the other in Paraguay" class="wp-image-423" height="719" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3415-e1409592432204-854x1024.jpg" width="600"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             One foot in Brazil, the other in Paraguay
            </p>
           </div>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_422" style="width: 610px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3410-e1409592625895.jpg">
             <img alt="Itaipu Dam Control Room" class="wp-image-422" height="800" src="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3410-e1409592625895-768x1024.jpg" width="600"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             The control room
            </p>
           </div>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Inside of the control room we were able to see the huge turbines in actionwhichwas very noisy. Not far from here we went to our last stop which was a huge room above the turbines, the room was one kilometre long so workers used bikes or small electric cars to get around!
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_426" style="width: 610px">
            <a href="http://www.jacksjaunts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_3440-e1409592935412.jpg">

            </a>
