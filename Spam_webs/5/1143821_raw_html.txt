                        <h1 class="entry-title">
                         Could you turn down Rex and the Jets?
                        </h1>
                        <div class="metabar">
                         Posted
                         <time class="date time published updated sc" datetime="2011-07-28T10:25:00+00:00">
                          July 28, 2011
                         </time>
                         
                         <span class="post-comments sc">
                          <a href="http://www.nationalfootballpost.com/could-you-turn-down-rex-and-the-jets/#comments">
                           Add Comment
                          </a>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                       </header>
                       <!-- .entry-header -->
                       <div class="entry-content">
                        <div class="metamedia">
                        </div>
                        <p class="no-break">
                         <span class="st_email_hcount" st_title="Could you turn down Rex and the Jets?" st_url="http://www.nationalfootballpost.com/could-you-turn-down-rex-and-the-jets/">
                         </span>
                         <span class="st_linkedin_hcount" st_title="Could you turn down Rex and the Jets?" st_url="http://www.nationalfootballpost.com/could-you-turn-down-rex-and-the-jets/">
                         </span>
                         <span class="st_reddit_hcount" st_title="Could you turn down Rex and the Jets?" st_url="http://www.nationalfootballpost.com/could-you-turn-down-rex-and-the-jets/">
                         </span>
                         <span class="st_googleplus_hcount" st_title="Could you turn down Rex and the Jets?" st_url="http://www.nationalfootballpost.com/could-you-turn-down-rex-and-the-jets/">
                         </span>
                         <span class="st_twitter_hcount" st_title="Could you turn down Rex and the Jets?" st_url="http://www.nationalfootballpost.com/could-you-turn-down-rex-and-the-jets/" st_via="footballpost">
                         </span>
                         <span class="st_facebook_hcount" st_title="Could you turn down Rex and the Jets?" st_url="http://www.nationalfootballpost.com/could-you-turn-down-rex-and-the-jets/">
                         </span>
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         Here is what I would do if I were still playing in the NFL and testing the free agent market: call up my agent, tell him to work the phones and get Rex Ryan and the Jets on the line.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         It wouldn't matter if I were a top-tier free agent or a guy that covered kicks for a living. Because that is the coach I would want to play for.
                        </p>
                        <p class="co_image co_image_right inline_right">
                         <img alt="Rex Ryan " src="http://cdn.cloudfiles.mosso.com/c1910342/media_center/images/rendered/blog/wysiwyg/789891011102710JetsPractice.jpg"/>
                         Could you turn this guy down if he offered you a contract?
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         During my career, it was Bill Belichick and the Patriots. Everyone wanted to play for the Pats. They won and the players respected the coach. A pretty simple formula. And while New England is still one of the top destinations for free agents in this league, I think New York is taking over as the top spot.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         But it starts with Rex.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         Players in this league want to get paid in free agency. That is a given, however they also want to play for a coach that is going to challenge them, test them and treat them like pros.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         With the Jets head coach, you get all of that, plus the brash (or borderline cocky) attitude that comes with it.  Something that drives players. They want to be a part of that, experience the environment that Ryan provides and win football games.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         And talking with current and ex-players for Rex, they will tell you the same thing: you want to go to work when he is running the show.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         It is a topic that NBCs Chris Collinsworth talked about over at his website,
                         <a href="http://footballpros.com/content.php/1044-Is-The-Rex-Ryan-Factor-Kicking-in-with-Jets" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://footballpros.com/content.php/1044-Is-The-Rex-Ryan-Factor-Kicking-in-with-Jets', 'FootballPros.com']);" target="_blank">
                          FootballPros.com
                         </a>
                         , last night. And he brings up some great points: Santonio Holmes re-signing for less money, Mark Sanchez willing to restructure his deal to provide more cap space and the
                         <a href="http://www.nationalfootballpost.com/Serious-interest-Will-the-Jets-close-on-Asomugha.html" target="_self">
                          talk of top free agent Nnamdi Asomugha
                         </a>
                         landing with the Jets.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         Does this happen with another coach in New York? Easy to say it would, but Im not buying it. That is why I believe the Jets can entertain any free agent they want and also be selective with their roster. A huge advantage when it comes to targeting veteran players.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                         Lets see how it plays out with Asomugha this week. The CB may decide to take his skills somewhere else, but I know that I couldnt turn Rex down.
                        </p>
