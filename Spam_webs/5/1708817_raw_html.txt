     <p>
      <b>
       Messier 46
      </b>
      (also known as
      <b>
       M 46
      </b>
      or
      <b>
       NGC 2437
      </b>
      ) is an
      <a href="/wiki/Open_cluster" title="Open cluster">
       open cluster
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">
       constellation
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Puppis" title="Puppis">
       Puppis
      </a>
      . It was discovered by
      <a href="/wiki/Charles_Messier" title="Charles Messier">
       Charles Messier
      </a>
      in 1771. Dreyer described it as "very bright, very rich, very large." M46 is about 5,500
      <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">
       light-years
      </a>
      away.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-majaess2007_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-majaess2007-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There are an estimated 500 stars in the cluster, and it is thought to be some 300 million years old.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ScienceDaily-2014-12-17_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ScienceDaily-2014-12-17-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Planetary_nebula" title="Planetary nebula">
       planetary nebula
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/NGC_2438" title="NGC 2438">
       NGC 2438
      </a>
      appears to lie within the cluster near its northern edge (the faint smudge at the top center of the image), but it is most likely unrelated since it does not share the cluster's radial velocity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-majaess2007_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-majaess2007-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kiss2008_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-kiss2008-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is an example of a superimposed pair possibly similar to that of
      <a href="/wiki/NGC_2818" title="NGC 2818">
       NGC 2818
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-majaess2007_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-majaess2007-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mermilliod2001_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-mermilliod2001-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      M46 is located close by to another open cluster,
      <a href="/wiki/Messier_47" title="Messier 47">
       Messier 47
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ScienceDaily-2014-12-17_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ScienceDaily-2014-12-17-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      M46 is about a degree east of M47 in the sky, so the two fit well in a binocular or wide-angle telescope field.
     </p>
