        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Create A Garden Sensory Bin
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-before-post" style="display: none !important;">

        <p>
         Last month I wrote about
         <a href="http://www.besttoys4toddlers.com/home/5-ways-to-play-with-play-food" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.besttoys4toddlers.com/home/5-ways-to-play-with-play-food', '5 Ways to Play With Play Food over at Best Toys for Toddlers');">
          5 Ways to Play With Play Food over at Best Toys for Toddlers
         </a>
         and I remembered one of the fun sensory, imaginative play bins I made for the kids. The upcoming Spring and warm weather also make me think of gardening- so nows the perfecttime tomake this garden sensory bin activity.
         <span id="more-1847">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          For your convenience this post contains affiliate links.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ipurchased these clear
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002BDTETW/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B002BDTETW&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=umbtrecaf-20&amp;linkId=5C6I3BDUDLQZM3CO" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002BDTETW/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B002BDTETW&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=umbtrecaf-20&amp;linkId=5C6I3BDUDLQZM3CO', 'Sterilite 6-Quart Storage Totes');">
          Sterilite 6-Quart Storage Totes
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=umbtrecaf-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B002BDTETW" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         at Walmart. They fit perfectly into the flower planting box I bought at the Refton Mud Sale that we use for our sensory box. Its basically just a wooden box with some trim for legs. It doesnt even seem very sturdy but my kids climb in it and so far its held up great for the $7 I spent on it. You could also make one pretty easily. We like that it puts the totes up off the floor, sort of like a table, but the mess stays contained inside it (for the most part).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://amypessolano.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/make-a-garden-sensory-bin.jpg">
          <img alt="Make a garden sensory bin with play food." class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1913" height="523" src="http://amypessolano.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/make-a-garden-sensory-bin.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         For this Garden Sensory Bin youll need:
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00GTDI9S2/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00GTDI9S2&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=umbtrecaf-20&amp;linkId=5N2G3V6M3L7PPTI2" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00GTDI9S2/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00GTDI9S2&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=umbtrecaf-20&amp;linkId=5N2G3V6M3L7PPTI2', 'Potting soil');">
           Potting soil
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=umbtrecaf-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00GTDI9S2" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          or just plain dirt
         </li>
         <li>
          Play food fruits and vegetables. I like this
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00B2AYLYS/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00B2AYLYS&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=umbtrecaf-20&amp;linkId=ULC5CY6RERVUAJ4P" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00B2AYLYS/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00B2AYLYS&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=umbtrecaf-20&amp;linkId=ULC5CY6RERVUAJ4P', 'Learning Resources Fresh Picked Fruit And Veggie Tote');">

          .
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00B2AYLYS/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00B2AYLYS&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=umbtrecaf-20&amp;linkId=ULC5CY6RERVUAJ4P" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00B2AYLYS/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00B2AYLYS&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=umbtrecaf-20&amp;linkId=ULC5CY6RERVUAJ4P', '');">

        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          I also used some flowers and bunnies from a Lego Duplo Set and some Little People toys we already had.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://amypessolano.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/garden-sensory-bin.jpg">
          <img alt="Make a garden sensory bin" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1912" height="1102" src="http://amypessolano.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/garden-sensory-bin.jpg" width="735"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <p>
         I gave the kids the box of dirt and we pretended to plant and harvest our veggies. Then we used them for picnics, restaurants and tons of other imaginative play scenarios that they came up with. If its warm, you can just let them dig outside in the real dirt, but when I decided to this, the weather conditions were less than ideal. I had a leftover bag of potting soil, so we just went with it and the kids had a great time.
        </p>
        <p>
         While you play you can discuss the differences between fruits and vegetables, identify or sort them by color, or just talk about what you plan to grow in your garden when its time to plant.
        </p>
