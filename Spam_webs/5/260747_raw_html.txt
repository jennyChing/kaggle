       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Do you like
         <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autumn_leaf_color" target="_blank" title="Ky">
          ky
         </a>
         , sweet waterfalls and breathtaking landscapes? Then Nikkis not only the perfect place but it is also not very far from Tokyo, only two hours by train. Tourists often prefer to go to Hakone. That is maybe because there are many famous onsen there, a lake with a view on Fuji-san and numerous museums however if you love nature then Nikk is by far a better destination.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18710" style="width: 900px">
         <a href="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/lake-chuzenji-with-autumn-foliage.jpg">
          <img alt="Lake Chzenji ()" class="size-large wp-image-18710 " height="600" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/lake-chuzenji-with-autumn-foliage-900x600.jpg" width="900"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Lake Chzenji ().
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Nikkcan be thought as three places :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Nikk City: the train station, many great temples and a
          <em>
           holy
          </em>
          bridge. This is where most visitors go and where you should go as well if you want to see Japanese culture.
         </li>
         <li>
          Chunzenji Lake: easily accessible by bus (20-30 minutes but watch out  the road is sometimes super crowded!), it is also a very touristic area. We will go around this lake in this article.
         </li>
         <li>
          Beyond Chunzenji: a few kilometers further passed the lake, there is Yumato Onsen, Kinugawa Onsen (even better) and a beautiful swamp that we will visit in the next article.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Hangetsuyama ()
        </h2>
        <p>
         Lets start from the top, in the heights surrounding the lake. We are here at Hangetsuyama which is actually quite a normal place. However, in autumn, it is an incredible observation point.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18712" style="width: 900px">
         <a href="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/hangetsuyama.jpg">
          <img alt="Hangetsuyama" class="size-large wp-image-18712 " height="600" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/hangetsuyama-900x600.jpg" width="900"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Hangetsuyama ().
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18714" style="width: 900px">
         <a href="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/hangetsuyama-autumn-colors.jpg">
          <img alt="Hangetsuyama, Autumn Colors" class="size-large wp-image-18714 " height="600" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/hangetsuyama-autumn-colors-900x600.jpg" width="900"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Hangetsuyama ().
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         The RyzuFalls ()
        </h2>
        <p>
         The RyzuFalls are also in the heights around the lake but on the opposite side of Hangetsuyama. My previous visit being in winter, the waterfalls were really dull and sad looking but now it simply looks amazing!
        </p>

         <div class="col-xs-6">
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18708" style="width: 400px">

           </a>

            The Ryzu Falls ()
           </p>

          <p>
          </p>
         </div>

        <p>
         The name of this waterfall means
         <em>
          Dragons Head
         </em>
         but you really need a lot of imagination to see anything close to it.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18702" style="width: 900px">

        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          Are you still looking for the dragon? ^^
         </em>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18704" style="width: 900px">
         <a href="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/ryuzu-falls-autumn.jpg">

        </div>
        <p>
         By going down the river, we get finally back to Chuzenji. It is the ideal place for a break even though the restaurants are a bit too expensive for what they have to offer.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="autumn, japan, japanese, kanto, natural, nature, tochigi" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-18716 lazyload" data-srcset="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/nikko-lakeside-city-900x600.jpg, http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/nikko-lakeside-city-900x600@2x.jpg 2x" height="593" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/nikko-lakeside-city-900x600.jpg" width="890"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         The Kegon Waterfall ()
        </h2>
        <p>
         Lets now visit the plumbing of the area. This is the water faucet of Nikk: the very grand and famous Kegon Waterfall.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18668" style="width: 900px">
         <a href="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/lake-chuzenji-chuzenjiko.jpg">
          <img alt="Kegon Waterfall ()" class="size-large wp-image-18668 " height="600" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/lake-chuzenji-chuzenjiko-900x600.jpg" width="900"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Sacr fuite, dis-donc.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         It is a 97 meters high fall and one of the 3 longest falls of Japan. It is unfortunately famous for something else too
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18670" style="width: 900px">
         <a href="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/kegon-waterfall-rainbow.jpg">
          <img alt="Kegon Waterfall ()" class="size-large wp-image-18670 " height="600" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/kegon-waterfall-rainbow-900x600.jpg" width="900"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Kegon Waterfall ().
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          They would come here, going along the river and through the trees to end up throwing themselves from the top of the fall
         </em>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18672" style="width: 900px">
         <a href="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/river-of-the-kegon-waterfall.jpg">
          <img alt="Kegon Waterfall ()" class="size-large wp-image-18672 " height="600" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/river-of-the-kegon-waterfall-900x600.jpg" width="900"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Kegon Waterfall ().
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          to land here but without any suffering, thats for sure.
         </em>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18674" style="width: 900px">
         <a href="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/kegon-waterfall-pool.jpg">
          <img alt="Kegon Waterfall ()" class="size-large wp-image-18674 " height="600" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/kegon-waterfall-pool-900x600.jpg" width="900"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Kegon Waterfall ().
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Maybe is that a bad ending for a walk around those falls? Well yes but often the most beautiful places hide the most awful stories and this place is no exception. Lets go back to the car now and go beyond the heights around the lake to get to the marshlands, where the bears are having a feast
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://cdn.totorotimes.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
