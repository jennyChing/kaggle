        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Be One Step Ahead of the Social Customer Service Game
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-06-20T05:45:54+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          June 20, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://maximizesocialbusiness.com/author/sofie-de-beule/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Sofie De Beule
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://maximizesocialbusiness.com/how-to-always-be-one-step-ahead-of-the-social-customer-service-game-14355/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div id="triberr_endorsement">
        </div>
        <div class="pf-content">
         <div class="printfriendly pf-alignright">
          <a class="noslimstat" href="http://www.printfriendly.com/print?url=http%3A%2F%2Fmaximizesocialbusiness.com%2Fhow-to-always-be-one-step-ahead-of-the-social-customer-service-game-14355%2F" onclick="window.print(); return false;" rel="nofollow">
           <img alt="Print Friendly" src="//cdn.printfriendly.com/pf-button-both.gif" style="border:none;-webkit-box-shadow:none; box-shadow:none;"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://cdn.maximizesocialbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Be-One-Step-Ahead-of-the-Social-Customer-Service-Game-V1-copy.jpg">
           <img alt="Be-One-Step-Ahead-of-the-Social-Customer-Service-Game-V1 copy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16618" height="600" src="http://cdn.maximizesocialbusiness.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Be-One-Step-Ahead-of-the-Social-Customer-Service-Game-V1-copy.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Customers are everywhere, and social media is apparently their go-to source. But, are you handling them the right way?
         </p>
         <p>
          While the buzz around
          <a href="https://econsultancy.com/blog/65018-what-social-customer-service-is-really-worth-stats#i.1fj6id7199efhr" target="_blank" title="What social customer service is really worth: stats">
           delivering social customer service has been going on for years
          </a>
          now, the debate on how exactly people should approach social media as a customer-care channel has evoked a lot of criticism.
         </p>
         <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
          <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7284850702328431" data-ad-slot="1904859849" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
         <p>
          What leaves most customers frustrated is the lack of human connection when companies talk to their customers. Therefore, its crucial for businesses to
          <b>
           gain contextual knowledge
          </b>
          of their customers to deliver that important, consistent reply across all channels. This is where analytics comes in to help you further identify the needs of your customers.
         </p>
         <p>
          A lot of businesses simply arent
          <a href="http://maximizesocialbusiness.com/future-social-customer-service-seamless-customer-experience-12995/" target="_blank" title="The Future of Social Customer Service: Seamless Customer Experience">
           delivering quick and personalized responses
          </a>
          to customer complaints. While smaller companies  regardless of their size or social media budget  have especially grown into the habit of delivering great social customer service, theyre setting the example for multinational companies who leave complaints unattended and ignored.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, beyond actively answering customers complaints, theres a whole other scope on customer satisfaction, customer retention, and customer loyalty that needs to be attended to help you
          <a href="http://blog.hootsuite.com/jetstar-case-study/" target="_blank" title="What Successful Social Customer Service Looks Like: The Jetstar Case Study">
           gain a crucial edge over your competition
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          But how exactly can you maintain that edge over your competition?
         </p>
         <h3>
          1. Proactive Outreach
         </h3>
         <p>
          Dont let your customers come to you with complaints reach out to them before they make complaints. When it comes to social customer service, its not about being a great company, its about being the best company out there. To really have your head in the game, make sure you reach out to your customers first.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you already know who your customers are, and on which social channels theyre active, why not use this opportunity to make them feel cared for at the right place, at the right time?
         </p>
         <p>
          In order to provide your customers with relevant offers, dispose of robust analytics to form a well-rounded profile of the person thats on the other side of the screen. Are they using your product in the best possible way? Make sure you gain insights into the issues your customers are struggling with and make a firm statement by touching the right elements in your one-on-one, two-way conversation. Never forget that your competitor is just a quick online search away. Customers would easily ditch a brand to which theyre loyal if they can find an alternative that can
          <a href="http://maximizesocialbusiness.com/companies-fail-social-customer-service-12848/" target="_blank" title="How Most Companies Fail at Social Customer Service">
           ensure them a great, personalized customer service
          </a>
          . Dont give them reason to switch!
         </p>
         <h3>
          2. Respond Within 30 Minutes
         </h3>
         <p>
          Social media is a go-to source for many kinds of complaints. So, how would you feel if you had to wait hours for a reply regarding an urgent issue? The unfortunate thing with social media is  although its the perfect medium for real-time interaction  a lot of companies simply dont manage to provide a response within 30 minutes. However, many customers would go to great lengths to have
          <a href="https://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/article/ignored-side-social-media-customer-service/" target="_blank" title="The Ignored Side of Social Media: Customer Service">
           companies deliver on excellent customer service
          </a>
          . According to
          <em>
           Harvard Business Review
          </em>
          , 86% of people would even be willing to pay more for a great customer experience.
         </p>
         <p>
          Service companies need to
          <a href="http://maximizesocialbusiness.com/lowes-creating-engaging-customer-experience-10578/" target="_blank" title="How Lowes Is Creating an Engaging Customer Experience">
           leverage customer service through social media
          </a>
          . Dealing with a lot of customer complaints, expectation levels are high when it comes to delivering fast and smooth replies. People dont want to go through a lot of hassle by switching to other customer service channels, and receiving incomplete answers.
         </p>
         <h3>
          3. Make It Relevant, And Above All, Make It Personal
         </h3>
         <p>
          To elevate yourself above the competition,
          <a href="http://sproutsocial.com/insights/how-social-media-is-impacting-customer-service/" target="_blank" title="How Social Media Is Impacting Customer Service">
           give your customers more than expected
          </a>
          . Dont just simply strive for relevancy, strive for excellence. This doesnt imply you need to offer them a complex solution. Think about howto thank them even when they dont buy something. What can you give them that they cant get anywhere else? Make that an exercise for your business!
         </p>
         <p>
          For example, in many cases, generic responses are not done. While they can help you ease the workload when it comes to crisis situations, they often make you sound too robotic. To help bridge the gap between offline and online contact, make sure you tell your story in a personal way.
         </p>
         <p>
          Why not make them feel special by regularly asking them for feedback? In that case, make sure you actually incorporate that feedback into your business model to have the voice of your customers being heard.
         </p>
         <p>
          What brands have you seen offer exceptional customer service through social media?
         </p>
