         <p>
          Idont believe in New Years resolutions. If youve set your sites on conquering P-90X, getting a degree or being in the audience on the Ellen Show, rock on, especially if one of your resolutions is to meet Ellen. I love Ellen. Tell her I said hi.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://rippedjeansandbifocals.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/2015-New-Years-Resolutions1.jpg">
           <img alt="2015 New Years Resolutions" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3270" height="200" src="http://rippedjeansandbifocals.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/2015-New-Years-Resolutions1-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I dont make New Years resolutions because I feel like they set me up for failure. Probably because Im too ambitious in my goal-setting, such as the 2009 special: Ima go to the gym every day at 5:00 AM. As if.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-2926">
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="tm-tweet-clear">
         </div>
         <div class="tm-click-to-tweet">
          <div class="tm-ctt-text">
           <a href="https://twitter.com/share?text=New+Year%27s+resolutions+set+me+up+to+fail.+Example%3A+Ima+go+to+the+gym+every+day+at+5AM....&amp;url=http://rippedjeansandbifocals.com/things-i-could-stop-doing-in-2015-but-probably-wont/" target="_blank">
            New Years resolutions set me up to fail. Example: Ima go to the gym every day at 5AM.
           </a>
          </div>
          <p>
           <a class="tm-ctt-btn" href="https://twitter.com/share?text=New+Year%27s+resolutions+set+me+up+to+fail.+Example%3A+Ima+go+to+the+gym+every+day+at+5AM....&amp;url=http://rippedjeansandbifocals.com/things-i-could-stop-doing-in-2015-but-probably-wont/" target="_blank">
            Click To Tweet
           </a>
          </p>
          <div class="tm-ctt-tip">
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          I dont
          <em>
           not
          </em>
          make resolutions because I dont have room for improvement. Im a work in progress fo sho. Heres ten thingsId think about cutting out
          <em>
           if
          </em>
          I were the resolution-making type.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Eye rolling
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Im a grand-slam champion eye roller. If there were trophies for eye rolling, Id have them all. All the trophies, people. Ive mastered everything from the super-quick roll behind your back to the sweeping, dramatic roll-sigh combo that lets everyone in a five-mile radius know how put-out I am.
         </p>
         <div class="tm-tweet-clear">
         </div>
         <div class="tm-click-to-tweet">
          <div class="tm-ctt-text">
           <a href="https://twitter.com/share?text=If+there+were+awards+for+eye+rolling+I+would+have+all+the+trophies.+All+the+trophies%2C+people....&amp;url=http://rippedjeansandbifocals.com/things-i-could-stop-doing-in-2015-but-probably-wont/" target="_blank">
            If there were awards for eye rolling I would have all the trophies. All the trophies, people.
           </a>
          </div>
          <p>
           <a class="tm-ctt-btn" href="https://twitter.com/share?text=If+there+were+awards+for+eye+rolling+I+would+have+all+the+trophies.+All+the+trophies%2C+people....&amp;url=http://rippedjeansandbifocals.com/things-i-could-stop-doing-in-2015-but-probably-wont/" target="_blank">
            Click To Tweet
           </a>
          </p>
          <div class="tm-ctt-tip">
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          Ive gotten better at eye-rolling. Not in an Ive perfected eye-rolling way (although that, too) but Ive definitely gotten sneakier about it. Ive also developed a little more tolerance for BS and my threshold for raising my baby blues to the sky is a little higher.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Blowing off friends
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          
          <em>
           Lets grab coffee
          </em>
           or 
          <em>
           why dont we meet for that drink
          </em>
           are things said by mewell, lets be real, people. Not often enough. And when I do suggest coffee, I dont follow through. Im sincere in my 
          <em>
           hey lets
          </em>
           but the meet-ups never pan out.
         </p>
         <p>
          If I were a resolution-maker, Id carve out some realistically manageable time each month to be a better friend. Like most people, I use the so busy excuse. While I
          <em>
           am
          </em>
          busy, I could probably benefit from a little reprioritizing when it comes to the people in my life who have decided they find me tolerable. Its a small but esteemed group.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Using my bitch voice with my husband
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          No matter what words are being spoken the bitch voice instantly translates them to 
          <em>
           youre a dumbass and I cant believe Im even talking to you.
          </em>
           My bitch voice can make 
          <em>
           would you like some ice cream, darling
          </em>
           sound like 
          <em>
           die evil troll
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Dont get me wrong: having a bitch voice is a useful skill that I suspect most women possess but if I were making resolutions, Id stop using it with my husband. Yes, he does stuff that annoys me but no, its usually not on purpose. Sometimes, using my bitch voice on my husband is justified, but not usually.
         </p>
         <p>
          To read more about how Id like to ditch my bitch voice in 2015, click

           HERE.
          </a>

         </p>

         <p>
          <strong>
           Yelling at my kids
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Theres not a lot I can say about this one. I do it. I wish I didnt and I really try not tobut I still do.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Multi-tasking
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Yes, really. Most women wear multi-tasking like a badge of honor, but Ive come to the point in my life where Ive realized that doing seven things at once means that Im probably sucking at most of them. Sure, I can compose email, text, watch the news and listen to my kid tell me about his day. This doesnt make me Superwoman: it makes me the mom who made six typos, inadvertently texted her boss instead of her bestie about a bikini wax gone wrong and blew off her kid because she was on information overload.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rippedjeansandbifocals.com/places-we-go/ditching-the-multitasking-william-chris-vineyards/">
           RELATED POST  STOP AND SMELL THE GRAPES
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Nail biting
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Im a sporadic nail biter. I dont do it out of nervousness or boredom and every now and then, Ill have a nice looking set of nails, but Ill inevitably screw it up by chewing on one of themand then I feel compelled to mess up the rest, too.
         </p>
         <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Judging my body
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          I constantly look at myself and criticize what I see. My stomach is too pudgy, my nose too hooked, my hair too flat and omigosh is that really a zit in that wrinkle on my neck? Why the hell cant I find something that fights wrinkles that doesnt give me teenage acne? While I take reasonably good care of myself, the fact is that my body and I are no longer 25. Beating myself up over imperfections thatdont matter isnt going to turn back the clock. I wish I couldlearn to be a little moreaccepting of myself.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://rippedjeansandbifocals.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Body-Image1.jpg">
           <img alt="Body Image" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3088" height="300" src="http://rippedjeansandbifocals.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Body-Image1-229x300.jpg" width="229"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Spending too much time on social media
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Like many people, Isee social media as a blessing and a curse. It helps me keep up with people and events and helps me promote my writing. Most things are good in moderation, but have you ever said something like 
          <em>
           Im just gonna check Facebook real quick
          </em>
           and poof, three hours are sucked away? Yeahme either. Ahem.
         </p>
         <div class="tm-tweet-clear">
         </div>
         <div class="tm-click-to-tweet">

            Ima just gonna check FB real quickand then 3 hours later. #socialmediaoverload

         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Comparing myself to other people
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          One of the best pieces of advice I ever received was this: Run your own race, literally and figuratively.
         </p>
         <p>
          I was struggling to finish a 10K a few years ago when a short, thick, older woman sailed past me, completely destroying my mental mojo. She didnt
          <em>
           look
          </em>
          like a runner, therefore she should be slow. She certainly didnt belong in front of me, but on that day, she kicked my ass. Maybe she was having her best day ever. Maybe looks are deceiving. Maybe I was too big for my britches.
         </p>
         <p>
          This was a good reminder to keep my eyes on my own paper. To focus on my own goals and to not try and keep up with the Jones.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Being sanctimommy
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Are you a mom? Im judging you. If your kids look a little chunky and your grocery cart is full of Hot Pockets and Little Debbies? Im critiquing you for not taking time to plan better meals. Running errands in your Hello Kitty pajama bottoms? Im calling you lazy. And so on, you get the idea.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres the thingI make snap judgments based on a snippet of information. Maybe Im usually right but sometimes, we dont know what we dont know.
         </p>
         <p>
          One of my friends is an Army widow. She lost her husband in a plane crash a few days before Christmas a few years ago. Someone gave her a dirty look as she ducked her head and walked through a contingent of happy humans holding welcome home signs for service members at the airport. To the casual observer, she was unpatriotic, or a scrooge. To someone who knew her, she was just trying to make it through the day without losing it.
         </p>
         <p>
          Wedont know what wedont know. My friends story had a huge impact on my mom judging. We only see a small slice of the picture, so why dont we stop filling in the blanks? Althoughodds are when I see you in your Hello Kitty jammies at Wal-Mart, Ima still gonna callyou lazy.
         </p>
