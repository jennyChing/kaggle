        <p>
         <a href="http://lemoinefamilykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/fajitabowl.jpg">
          <img alt="fajitabowl" class="aligncenter wp-image-3810" height="450" src="http://lemoinefamilykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/fajitabowl-1024x1024.jpg" width="450"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I love me some fresh and flavorful Mexican! You all know that anytime you go out for Mexican, when you hear that sizzling pan go by you take a big ole whiff and a peek. Chicken fajitas is definitely one of the dishes I frequently order out because of the wonderful flavors without so much of the smothering of heavy sauce and cheese. While I dont have one of those fancy pants skillet pans that restaurants serve fajitas on, I still make a killer dish! These Chicken Fajita Bowls have all the elements in one lovely package! Sauted chicken, peppers and onions, some simple refried beans, brown rice and then to finish it off fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime. Perfection.
         <span id="more-3809">
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <p>
         I took chicken breasts and cut them into strips, coated them with a homemade Mexican seasoning and let it sit in the fridge overnight. The chicken is quickly cooked in a hot pan with just a little bit of oil to prevent sticking. The chicken is removed and the peppers and onions are cooked in the same pan to take on all the delicious flavor bits left by the chicken. The beans, well they are a total cheat here. I doctored up some canned fat free refried beans with diced canned chills and fresh cilantro. The base is simply brown rice seasoned with a little salt and tossed with more fresh cilantro. I absolutely love the addition of the lime at the very end, it just really brightens up the dish.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

             Chicken Fajita Bowl

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 lbs chicken breasts, cut into thin strips
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             4 bell peppers, cut into strips
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 red onion, sliced
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tsp salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 Tbsp dried oregano
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tsp garlic powder
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tsp onion powder
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tsp cumin
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 Tbsp chili powder
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 cans refried beans
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 small can diced green chilis
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-11" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 lb dry brown rice
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
             cilantro
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-13" itemprop="ingredients">
             limes, cut into wedges
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-14" itemprop="ingredients">
             vegetable oil
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             In a ziplock bag mix together the salt, oregano, garlic powder, cumin, onions powder and chili powder. Add in the cut chicken and mix around to coat evenly. Refrigerate overnight or at least a few hours.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Heat up a large saut pan, over medium high heat, add about a Tbsp of oil and the chicken, Stir occasionally to cook through. Remove from the pan and place aside.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add another Tbsp of oil to the pan and add the peppers and onions. Cook until a tender crisp, or further if you prefer them a little more cooked. Stir occasionally to pick up all the flavors left by the chicken.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             For the beans, in a pan over medium low heat, mix together the beans and green chilis. Warm through, shut the heat and stir in a small handful of chopped cilantro.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Cook the rice according to the packing. Season with salt and pepper. At the very end stir in some chopped cilantro.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Assembly time! Start with a big ole scoop of the rice, top with some of the delicious beans and then the chicken, peppers and onions. Garnish with some more cilantro {yes, I really love cilantro} and a couple lime wedges.
            </li>
           </ol>
