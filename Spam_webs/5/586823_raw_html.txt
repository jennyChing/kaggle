           <h1 class="post-title">
            Devils Ride : Kawasaki Z800
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://www.randomheads.com/author/motwanirajat/" rel="author" title="Posts by Rajat Motwani">
             Rajat Motwani
            </a>
             January 21, 2014
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              Japanese two wheeler giant Kawasaki has introduced its well-proven Z800 on the Indian streets. With a devilish headlamp and a wicked tail, there is nothing in this bike that makes it a gentlemans ride.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-1-1.jpg">
                <img alt="13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-1-1" class="alignright" height="404" src="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-1-1.jpg" width="308"/>
               </a>
              </strong>
              The naked Z800 beast being the 5th model of the company which will be imported as a CBU (Completely Built Unit) is built tubular backbone frame, as specified by Kawasaki. Powered by a 806cc liquid-cooled engine, it delivers a power of 113PS.This bike is precisely designed to maintain the perfect balance between the aspects of daily usage of the vehicle at the same time this machine can achieve sheer performance on the highways.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Shorter final gearing
              </strong>
              <br/>
              Smooth-shifting 6-speed transmission has a gear for every situation, from low-speed city traffic to high-speed highway use. A larger rear sprocket (43T&gt;&gt;45T) emphasises the Z800s already more powerful acceleration.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Highly communicative chassis
              </strong>
              <br/>
              While maintaining a highly sporty nature, the Z800s friendly character makes it an accomodating mount even for beginners. Increased road holding performance, and hence traction, on all types of road surfaces enables both sporty and friendly riding characteristics. Tubular backbone chassis design with engine sub-frame and new connecting pipe, and revised chassis rigidity offer great feedback to the rider, communicating what the bike is doing in all situations.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <a href="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-2.jpg">
               <img alt="13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-2" class="aligncenter" height="404" src="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-2.jpg" width="530"/>
              </a>
              <strong>
               41 mm inverted fork
              </strong>
              <br/>
              41mm KYB Inverted fork contributes to the Z800s quick, nimble handling, excellent road-holding ability and superb feedback. Optimized for the increased engine and revised frame rigidity the front fork is adjustable for rebound dumping and preloading.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Piggyback-reservoir-equipped rear shock
              </strong>
              <br/>
              The rear shock features an aluminum body with a piggyback reservoir for increased cooling performance. KYB rear shock is repositioned 20mm to the left side for better left-right balance.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Enhanced braking performance
              </strong>
              <br/>
              New opposed, 4-piston calipers gripping larger-diameter 310 mm petal discs deliver formidable stopping power with superb control and feel.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <a href="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-5-1.jpg">
               <img alt="13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-5-1" class="aligncenter" height="364" src="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-5-1.jpg" width="477"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Full digital instrumentation
              </strong>
              <br/>
              The Z800s futuristic looking instrument panel features a triple LCD screen which includes a level-meter-style tachometer, fuel gauge, remaining range and the Economical Riding Indicator.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Natural riding position
              </strong>
              <br/>
              The relationship between pegs, seats and handlebars was designed to enhance communication between rider and machine, giving the rider a confidence-inspiring feeling of control that perfectly complements its accurate and precise handling qualities. New seat design contributes to increased comfort for rider and passenger.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Buffed header pipes
              </strong>
              <br/>
              Elegant curves of the exhaust header pipes and a buffed finish complement the Z800s fierce street-fighter styling.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <a href="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-3-2.jpg">
               <img alt="13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-3-2" class="aligncenter" height="323" src="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_2-3-2.jpg" width="424"/>
              </a>
              <strong>
               Aggressive front cowl
              </strong>
              <br/>
              Compact headlamp cowl uses a multi-face design to create a more aggressive, glaring visage for the Z800. the fierce appearance is one of the key styling elements of the aggressive new design.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               LED taillight
              </strong>
              <br/>
              New taillight design features a Z pattern  a nice touch that complements the slim tail section of this latest Z-model.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <a href="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_1-2.jpg">
               <img alt="13_ZR800ABCD_F_1-2" class="aligncenter" height="323" src="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/13_ZR800ABCD_F_1-2.jpg" width="424"/>
              </a>
              <strong>
               Sharp under-cowl
              </strong>
              <br/>
              The under-cowl adds to the Z800s street-fighter image and along with the engine shrouds contributes to the aggressive, visceral design.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Optional ABS
              </strong>
              <br/>
              ABS Models feature a compact and lightweight Nissin motorcycle ABS unit. The unit is located under the riders seat, contributing to increased room for storage under the passenger seat.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <a href="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/z800_121002.jpg">
               <img alt="z800_121002" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-674" height="680" src="http://www.randomheads.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/z800_121002.jpg" width="1200"/>
              </a>
             </p>
