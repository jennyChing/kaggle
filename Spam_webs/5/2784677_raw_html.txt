        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: small;">
          I know this sounds simple, however, in reality, it takes a lot of work to actually under commit and over deliver. This is a saying that was drilled into my head when I first became the CFO of the company I worked for. Throughout my career, I have heard this phrase a lot, however, very few people actually do it.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: small;">
          Why is it so important? Setting expectations in your organization is critical. Not only should you under commit and over deliver to your customers, you should do it with your internal staff as well.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: small;">
          Unfortunately, the consequences of not living this mantra can be debilitating to an organization. Providing overly optimistic projections to investors or bankers can cause irreparable damage and make it difficult to regain trust and confidence. Telling employees there are going to be benefits that arent realistic can leave you scrambling trying to retain your best talent. And probably most importantly, not meeting deadlines or commitments to clients can put you out of business.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: small;">
          Although this is an easy concept to understand, make sure you live by it or you might be looking for someone else to under commit and over deliver to you at another company.
         </span>
        </p>
