            <h2>
             Luxembourg City, Luxembourg
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </h2>
            <div class="wpgeo_map wp_geo_map" id="wpgeo_map_4464_2" style="width:100%;height:300px;">
            </div>
            <p>
             I had never really known if Luxembourg actually existed. For me, growing up on the other side of the world, European geography always consisted of the beer country, the wine country, the pasta country, the tapas country and the other ones. Luxembourg, if it was ever mentioned, was spoken about in the same way that Narnia was  a magical fictional land that is virtually impossible to find.
            </p>
            <p>
             Even if you were to know what to look for, you would have to squint and zoom on a map to find Luxembourg. Eventually, its located, squeezed in between Germany, France and Belgium. It kind of looks like the gap thats left when you havent fit together two jigsaw pieces quite right.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-199_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13569" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-199_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-204_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13568" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-204_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             And, as it turns out, visiting the capital (also called Luxembourg) is a bit like going to that magical land I had always imagined. The city feels like it is built to be enjoyed more than lived in. The valleys with the rivers have charming little houses built along the banks; castles and churches line the clifftops; palaces and grand government buildings dominate the structure of the historic part of town; and even the busiest of streets have a pleasant mix of offices, shopping and recreation.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-208_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13567" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-208_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             It has a population of less than 100,000 people but feels much more like a city than the town the size would suggest. It spreads out  never too crowded, never too hectic, always scenic from every angle.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-215_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13565" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-215_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             In terms of history, Luxembourg survived the cycles of European conquests mainly because of its fortifications. Its considered to be one of the best examples of military architecture on the continent. Having walked up and down its hills a few times now, I can assure you that its location is protection enough. The steep cliffs and ravines that snake through and around the city make it a very strategic position.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-218_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13563" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-218_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-216_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13564" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-216_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             The valleys are also one of the main reasons why its such a pleasure to visit Luxembourg. Walking through the town  particularly the old quarters  is a pleasure on the eyes. The lack of the tourist hordes, who are presumably busy in the more famous neighbouring countries, makes it even more enjoyable.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-230_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13561" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-230_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: left;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-232_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13560" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-232_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: right;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <div style="clear: both;">
            </div>
            <p>
             There arent lots of sights to visit in Luxembourgs capital. There are quite a few interesting buildings but theres a similarity to many of them and, remember, the city is quite small so it doesnt take long to see them all.
            </p>
            <p>
             As far as food and drink go, there are lots of good options around the squares in the medieval part of the city but things become a bit bare if you wander too far away. I was a bit surprised at first at how long it took me to find a decent area for dinner.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-212_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="visiting luxembourg city, luxembourg's capital, unesco sites in luxembourg" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13566" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Luxembourg-2012-212_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             It seems that I am not the only one who has at some point wondered whether Luxembourg really exists. The foreign crowds just dont exist here but the locals dont seem to mind. As a city, its extremely strong economically but appears content and self-assured, with no grand ambitions to be more than it is. I guess thats easier to do when youre already the capital of the worlds only grand duchy.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/UNESCO_Logo.jpg">
              <img alt="UNESCO world heritage site" class="alignnone wp-image-2418" height="75" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/UNESCO_Logo-150x150.jpg" style="float: left;" title="UNESCO_Logo" width="75"/>
             </a>
             This is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
             <a href="http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/699" target="_blank" title="City of Luxembourg: its Old Quarters and Fortifications">
              For more info click here
             </a>
             .
             <br/>
             You can see all the
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/unesco-world-heritage-list/" title="The UNESCO World Heritage List">
              UNESCO World Heritage Sites Ive visited here
             </a>
             .
            </p>
