     <h1 class="ipsType_pagetitle">
      Packers Bolster Secondary by Selecting Quinten Rollins
     </h1>
     <div class="desc lighter blend_links">
      May 02 2015 01:00 AM | Views: 795 | 
	Sean Blashe
 in
      <a href="http://profootballspot.com/_/nfl/nfc-north/green-bay-packers/">
       Green Bay Packers
      </a>
     </div>
     <br/>
     <div>
      <div class="ipsPad ipsType_textblock">
       <img alt="" height="301" src="http://profootballspot.com/uploads/ea88ef0af5c5edc6c2b8efc6f94e309d.jpg" style="float: left;" width="600"/>
       <br/>
       <div class="teaser">
        <em>
         <i>
          The Green Bay Packers used their second round pick on the cornerback from Miami (OH). (Photo Credit: muredhawks.com)
         </i>
        </em>
       </div>
       Heading into the NFL draft, the Green Bay Packers two biggest needs were at inside linebacker and cornerback.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       With the Packers first round pick,
       <a class="bbc_url" href="http://profootballspot.com/_/nfl/nfc-north/green-bay-packers/damarious-randall-a-typical-ted-thompson-pick-r9002" title="">
        Green Bay selected Damarious Randall
       </a>
       who played safety at Arizona State. Early reports stated that Randall would likely be converted to a cornerback because the Packers did not have an immediate need at safety.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       After taking a defensive back in the first, it seemed likely that the Packers would look to a different position with their second pick. But Ted Thompson continued to be unpredictable and went with another defensive back with the 62
       <sup class="bbc">
        nd
       </sup>
       pick.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       With Green Bays second round pick, the Packers took
       <a class="bbc_url" href="http://profootballspot.com/_/nfl/nfc-north/green-bay-packers/packers-replacing-a-ball-hawk-with-a-redhawk-r8973" title="">
        Quinten Rollins
       </a>
       , a cornerback from Miami (OH). Rollins will be a work in progress, but he has the potential to blossom into a star for the Packers.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Collins is definitely a raw talent. He played basketball for four years for the RedHawks, but only played football in his senior season. But in that senior season, he recorded seven interceptions and defended 16 passes on his way to winning the Mid-American Conferences defensive player of the year.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Collins displayed great ball skills, but he will need some refinement in order to translate to a difference maker in the NFL. After losing both Tramon Williams and Davon House in free agency, Rollins has the potential to step into an impact role at some point in the season.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-family: arial">
        <span style="font-size: 14px">
         Green Bay still needs to address its need at inside linebacker, but for a team that lacked proven depth in the secondary, the Packers first two picks of the 2015 draft are sure to strengthen the teams roster.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
