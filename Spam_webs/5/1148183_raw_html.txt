    <div style=" padding-left:100px;padding-right:100px; font-size:16px; padding-top:10px; font-weight:600;">
     Whilst everyone knows that exotic locations yield fabulous materials such as gold, oil and diamonds, we prefer to think a little outside of the box and so have compiled a list of some of the greatest fictional metals that you certainly wont be able to get from any ore on the planet.
    </div>
    <div style="font-size:14px;padding-left:100px;padding-right:100px; text-align:left;">
     <p>
     </p>
     <div>
      <div class="theText" style="padding-top:20px;">
       <h2 style="margin-bottom:10px;">
        <b>
         Adamantium
        </b>
       </h2>
       <a onclick="getPage('http://marvel.wikia.com/wiki/Adamantium');">
        Adamantium
       </a>
       is primarily known to us as the virtually indestructible metal that coats
       <a onclick="getPage('http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolverine_(comics)');">
        Wolverines
       </a>
       skeleton and claws, making him super awesome. The process required to create adamantium is incredibly expensive and is therefore only available to those with almost inexhaustible funding. Lady Deathstrike has also had adamantium fused to her skeleton and possesses claws made from the metal. Other uses of note include Gambits staff, Bullseyes spine, as well as some strips of the stuff covering his bones
      </div>
      <div class="theImages">
       <img align="bottom" class="theImages" height="265px;" src="images/Fictional Metals/Adamantium.jpg"/>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
     </p>
     <div>
      <div class="theText">
       <h2 style="margin-bottom:10px;">
        <b>
         Carbonite
        </b>
       </h2>
       Han Solo is awesome, right? So...If we use logic here, that means that when Han Solo is encased in carbonite, that makes the carbonite awesome as well! Just in case you didnt know, in the Star Wars movie The Empire Strikes back, the character of Han Solo is frozen in Pure Awesome in order to be displayed as a trophy by his captor.
      </div>
      <div class="theImages">
       <img align="bottom" class="theImages" height="265px;" src="images/Fictional Metals/Carbonite.jpg"/>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
     </p>
     <div>
      <div class="theText">
       <h2 style="margin-bottom:10px;">
        <b>
         Mithril
        </b>
       </h2>
       Another famous fictional metal largely popularised in J. R. R. Tolkiens Middle-earth fantasy writings, the metal was said to have been mined by the Dwarves in the mines of Moria. Mithril was expensive before the Balrog was released and destroyed the only supply of mithril, but afterwards, it was rendered priceless. Its properties not only included looking similar to silver and being pleasing to look at, but it was nigh-on impervious, being the strongest of metals available and able to stop spears and arrows from piercing it. Pretty awesome.
      </div>
      <div class="theImages">
       <img align="bottom" class="theImages" height="265px;" src="images/Fictional Metals/Mithril.jpg"/>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
     </p>
     <div>
      <div class="theText">
       <h2 style="margin-bottom:10px;">
        <b>
         Necrodermis
        </b>
       </h2>
       Necrodermis is the self-repairing 
       <a onclick="getPage('http://wh40k.lexicanum.com/wiki/Living_metal');">
        living metal
       </a>
        that both the
       <a onclick="getPage('http://wh40k.lexicanum.com/wiki/C%27tan');">
        Ctan
       </a>
       and
       <a onclick="getPage('http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Necrons');">
        Necrons
       </a>
       are encased in, in the
       <a onclick="getPage('http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warhammer_40,000');">
        Warhammer 40,000
       </a>
       universe. Necrodermis not only repairs itself when its damaged, but is extremely durable and can alter its shape with ease. Good stuff to cover yourself in if you intend to wage war against the universe.
      </div>
      <div class="theImages">
       <img align="bottom" class="theImages" height="265px;" src="images/Fictional Metals/Necrodermis.jpg"/>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
     </p>
     <div>
      <div class="theText">
       <h2 style="margin-bottom:10px;">
        <b>
         Saronite
        </b>
       </h2>
       Predominantly used by
       <a onclick="getPage('http://www.wowwiki.com/Scourge');">
        the Scourge
       </a>
       in Warcraft/World of Warcraft lore.
       <a onclick="getPage('http://www.wowwiki.com/Saronite');">
        Saronite
       </a>
       is made from
       <a onclick="getPage('http://www.wowwiki.com/Saronite_Ore');">
        saronite ore
       </a>
       and is used to construct the vast majority of the Scourges war machines as well as implanting shards of saronite into lower minions, thus making them slightly more powerful. Incredibly strong and resilient, saronite displays a high resistance to most magics (including the holy power of
       <a onclick="getPage('http://www.wowwiki.com/Light');">
        the Light
       </a>
       ) and is therefore the metal of choice for the Scourge, but blacksmiths outside of the Scourge are also known to make weaponry and armour from saronite.
      </div>
      <div class="theImages">
       <img align="bottom" class="theImages" height="265px;" src="images/Fictional Metals/Saronite.jpg"/>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
     </p>
     <div>
      <div class="theText">
       <h2 style="margin-bottom:10px;">
        <b>
         Supermanium
        </b>
       </h2>
       Awesome in a purely comedic sense (see picture),
       <a onclick="getPage('http://dc.wikia.com/wiki/Supermanium');">
        Supermanium
       </a>
       has only been used to construct Supermans ridiculously-named Supermobile and the
       <a onclick="getPage('http://media.comicvine.com/uploads/1/14288/326777-38530-supermanium.jpg');">
        bars of a prison
       </a>
       used to contain Lex Luthor.
      </div>
      <div class="theImages">
       <img align="bottom" class="theImages" height="265px;" src="images/Fictional Metals/Supermanium.jpg"/>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
     </p>
     <div>
      <div class="theText">
       <h2 style="margin-bottom:10px;">
        <b>
         Vibranium
        </b>
       </h2>
       A rare metal of extraterrestrial origin,
       <a onclick="getPage('http://marvel.wikia.com/wiki/Vibranium');">
        vibranium
       </a>
       is  like adamantium  virtually indestructible and is the main constituent of that most iconic of superhero weaponry,
       <a onclick="getPage('http://marvel.wikia.com/wiki/Captain_America%27s_Shield');">
        Captain Americas shield
       </a>
       . Thanks to an unknown factor in the lab of the scientist that created it (the alloy that the shield was made from was somehow formed as the scientist was asleep from exhaustion); the material has never been replicated.
      </div>
      <div class="theImages">
       <img align="bottom" class="theImages" height="265px;" src="images/Fictional Metals/Vibranium.jpg"/>
