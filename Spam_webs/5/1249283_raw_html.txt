         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Kayaker Killed By Shark In Hawaii
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <div class="meta">
          <span class="meta_posted_by">
           Posted by:
          </span>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a href="http://sportsmasher.com/author/sportsmasher/" rel="author">
            SportSmasher
           </a>
          </span>
          ,
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2013-12-03T11:05:38+00:00">
           December 3, 2013
          </time>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://sportsmasher.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/20131203-105508.jpg">
           <img alt="20131203-105508.jpg" class="size-full aligncenter" src="http://sportsmasher.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/20131203-105508.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/12/02/us/hawaii-fatal-shark-attack/index.html?sr=fb120313sharkattack930a">
           CNN.com
          </a>
          <em>
           A man fishing from a kayak off a Hawaiian island has died after being bitten by a shark, authorities said Monday. The incident was the latest in an alarming spate of shark attacks in the state this year. The attack took place Monday morning half a mile off a point near Little Beach in Makena State Recreation Area on the island of Maui, the Department of Land and Natural Resources (DLNR) said. The victims companion, who was also on a kayak, told the department that his friend was fishing for baitfish with artificial lures when a shark bit one of his feet, which was dangling over the edge of the boat.The mans companion, who was about 500 yards away when the attack took place, paddled over to him, tied a tourniquet to try to stem severe bleeding and called on a tour boat in the vicinity for help, authorities said. The tour boat brought the man to shore, and he was then taken to the hospital. But authorities believe he died of his injuries during the boat journey, said Rod Antone of the Mayors Office of the County of Maui. The man was in his 40s, but authorities are unsure if he was a local resident or a tourist, Antone said. The identities of the man and his companion have not been disclosed.We offer our condolences to the family of the victim. Our thoughts and prayers are with them, said William J. Aila, Jr., the DLNR chairman. Authorities say they have closed the waters off Makena State Recreation Area following the attack. Beaches in the area remain open, but the DLNR said people are advised to stay out of the water. The area will reopen at noon Tuesday if no more sharks are seen in the vicinity, the department said.The attack is the 13th shark incident reported in Hawaii so far this year, and the eighth on Maui, authorities said. Thats well above the states average of four unprovoked shark attacks per year over the past 20 years. The 10 incidents reported in 2012 were unprecedented at the time, the DLNR said. We are not sure why these bites are occurring more frequently than normal, especially around Maui, Aila said. Thats why we are conducting a two-year study of shark behavior around Maui that may give us better insights. Aila said that authorities hope and expect that numbers of incidents will return to a more normal range in the near future. In August, a German tourist died after being bitten by a shark while she was vacationing in Hawaii.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          First off, we here at
          <a href="http://sportsmasher.com">
           SportSmasher.com
          </a>
          would like to extend our sympathy to this mans family, a shark attack must be a horrible way to lose a loved one.
         </p>
         <p>
          These kayakers were fishing for baitfish at the time of this attack, it is likely that a tiger shark was chasing those same baitfish, and when the shark saw the mans foot dangling over the side of his kayak it mistook the foot to be a fish and attacked. Unfortunately, the shark hit a major artery and the man passed away, likely due to blood loss.
         </p>
         <p>
          It just goes to show you that, when you are in an area that had a known population of sharks or other dangerous animals, you must take every precaution and you can never let your guard down. Something as seemingly harmless as dangling a foot in the water could have fatal consequences.
         </p>
