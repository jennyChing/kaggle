     <p>
      <b>
       Skulls Unlimited International, Inc.
      </b>
      is a commercial supplier of
      <a href="/wiki/Osteology" title="Osteology">
       osteological
      </a>
      specimens located in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City,_Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma City, Oklahoma">
       Oklahoma City, Oklahoma
      </a>
      . Skulls Unlimited provides a skull cleaning service, using
      <a href="/wiki/Dermestidae" title="Dermestidae">
       dermestid beetles
      </a>
      to strip the flesh from skulls and skeletons.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msnbc_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-msnbc-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The bones are later whitened using
      <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_peroxide" title="Hydrogen peroxide">
       hydrogen peroxide
      </a>
      . Skulls Unlimited processes approximately 25,000 skull specimens per year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Oklahoman_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Oklahoman-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Skulls_Unlimited_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the summer of 1986, Jay Villemarette found himself out of work after being laid off from his auto body job and started considering the possibility of working full-time with
      <a href="/wiki/Skull" title="Skull">
       skulls
      </a>
      . In June of that year he established Skulls Unlimited International.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chicago_Tribune_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Chicago_Tribune-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His fascination with skulls and bones began when he was seven years old, after he found a
      <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">
       dog
      </a>
      skull in the woods near his home. That interest, along with encouragement from his father,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outsidemag_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-outsidemag-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazette_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Gazette-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      led Villemarette to begin collecting other animal skulls. After graduating from
      <a href="/wiki/High_school" title="High school">
       high school
      </a>
      , he collected and sold
      <a href="/wiki/Skull" title="Skull">
       skulls
      </a>
      in his spare time
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Reuters-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      while working as an
      <a href="/wiki/Auto_mechanic" title="Auto mechanic">
       auto
      </a>
      body
      <a href="/wiki/Technician" title="Technician">
       technician
      </a>
      . As sales grew, Villemarette and his wife Kim began to clean skulls in their kitchen.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazette_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Gazette-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After relocating to a retail location for his business in 1992, Villemarette was able to employ his first full time
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Employees" title="Employees">
       employees
      </a>
      Skulls Unlimited remained at their South Shields address until June 2000, when they expanded into a custom built facility in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City,_OK" title="Oklahoma City, OK">
       Oklahoma City
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Museum_of_Osteology">
       Museum of Osteology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Skulls_Unlimited_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Museum of Osteology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 2004, Skulls Unlimited started construction on a new 12,000-square-foot (1,100m
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ) building that now houses Skulls Unlimited's corporate offices and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Museum_of_Osteology" title="The Museum of Osteology">
       The Museum of Osteology
      </a>
      . The museum opened to the public on October 1, 2010 and exhibits over 300 real skeletons and over 400 real skulls,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Journalrecord_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Journalrecord-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      focusing on the form and function of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skeletal_system" title="Skeletal system">
       skeletal system
      </a>
      .
     </p>
