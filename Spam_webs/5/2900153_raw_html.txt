          A Connecticut Baker is Under Fire for Designing a Pro 2nd Amendment Birthday Cake

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.guns.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/735048_597764990253304_866566299_n.jpg">
           <img alt="735048_597764990253304_866566299_n" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-47450" height="563" src="http://www.guns.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/735048_597764990253304_866566299_n.jpg" width="960"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.guns.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/image.jpeg">
           <img alt="image" class="alignright size-full wp-image-47448" height="526" src="http://www.guns.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/image.jpeg" width="350"/>
          </a>
          Jennifer Montalto, owner ofJenny-Lus bakery in Middlebury, CT is receiving some backlash for baking a pro 2nd Amendment cake for a local
          <em>
           Friends of NRA
          </em>
          birthday dinner.
         </p>
         <p>
          Elizabeth Mais wanted to order her husband Bill an AR-15 crafted cake for his 51st birthday, but she was turned down by a number of local bakers who didnt approve of her idea. Determined to find someone to create the highly detailed dessert, Mais came across Montalto who said she had no problem creating the sporting rifle cake with a Dont Tread on Me flagdrapedon top. I dont think about politics when Im doing my cakes, she said Tuesday. A friend contacted me about it because they couldnt find anyone to make it It was a challenge.
         </p>
         <p>
          After creating the cake Montalto received some hate online via
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/JennyLus.bakedgoods" target="_blank">
           Facebook
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/jenny-lus-middlebury" target="_blank">
           Yelp reviews
          </a>
          (which have since been removed). She was labeledinsensitiveand guilty ofridiculing the tragedy in Newtown.
         </p>
         <p>
          Shaken by the backlash Montalto stated, I cried for three days straight when everything happened. But in my mind, it wasnt the gun that killed people, it was a very sick individual.
         </p>
         <p>
          Montalto andJenny-Lus bakery has since received some positive attention from local gun owners  feel free to head over to her
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/JennyLus.bakedgoods" target="_blank">
           Facebook page
          </a>
          and show her some support.
         </p>
         <p>

           Patch
          </a>
          |
