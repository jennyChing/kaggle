                &lt;h1 style="text-align: center"&gt;Why Oprah (&amp; millions of her 
                fans) love Jenny McCarthy&lt;/h1&gt;
                
                &lt;p style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;font size="2" color="#006699"&gt;
                (and why it doesn&#39;t matter very much)&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                
                &lt;p style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;i&gt;&lt;font face="Georgia"&gt;
                &lt;font size="2"&gt;&quot;The rule that human beings seem to follow is to 
                engage the brain only when all else fails  and usually not even 
                then.&quot;-- &lt;/font&gt;&lt;/i&gt;&lt;font size="2"&gt;David Hull&lt;/font&gt;&lt;i&gt;&lt;a href="http://books.google.com/books?id=2SQvleDwn9YC&amp;pg=PA70&amp;lpg=PA70&amp;dq=david+hull+the+rule+human+beings+engage+brain&amp;source=bl&amp;ots=9RapPIS5e_&amp;sig=1_KerQKAZ3pJmygi3aafWlBWG7s&amp;hl=en&amp;ei=800ISq-SHZK8tgPHyuzkAQ&amp;sa=X&amp;oi=book_result&amp;ct=result&amp;resnum=1"&gt;&lt;font size="2"&gt;*&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/i&gt;&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                
                &lt;p style="text-align: center"&gt;&lt;font size="2" face="Georgia"&gt;&lt;i&gt;
                Most people would die sooner than think  in fact they do so.&quot;&lt;/i&gt;--Bertrand 
                Russell&lt;a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Biography/Quote"&gt;*&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;&lt;b&gt;May 11, 2009&lt;/b&gt;. Jenny McCarthy was 
                signed to a&lt;img border="0" src="graphics/jenmac.jpg" width="114" height="274" lowsrc="Jenny%20McCarthy" alt="Jenny McCarthy"&gt; multi-year contract with Oprah Winfrey&#39;s Harpo 
                Productions, Inc. McCarthy is to develop projects on different 
                platforms, including hosting a syndicated talk show.&lt;a href="http://www.slate.com/id/2217798/"&gt;*&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;Skeptics think of
                &lt;a href="http://www.antivaccinebodycount.com/Anti-Vaccine_Body_Count/Home.html"&gt;Jenny McCarthy as a 
                know-nothing anti-vaccination crusader&lt;/a&gt; who has deluded 
                herself into thinking she cured her son&#39;s autism. Her fans know 
                her as the mother warrior who has the courage to stand up to the 
                Establishment&#39;s deadly crusade to harm our children with 
                vaccines. Oprah is clearly a fan, not a skeptic. Why?&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;If you were to ask 
                Oprah the following question, how would she answer? &quot;A bat and a 
                ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1 more than the ball. 
                How much does the ball cost?&quot; Like most of you,&lt;a href="http://books.google.com/books?id=2SQvleDwn9YC&amp;pg=PA72&amp;lpg=PA72&amp;dq=kahneman+frederick+ball+bat&amp;source=bl&amp;ots=9RapPIQ7gT&amp;sig=uM4k32qGPzOnRb4Lnfd-wCMpGA8&amp;hl=en&amp;ei=-EYISoZTjPyzA7uaqPcB&amp;sa=X&amp;oi=book_result&amp;ct=result&amp;resnum=3"&gt;*&lt;/a&gt; 
                she would probably answer quickly: &quot;Ten cents.&quot; Those of you who 
                don&#39;t go with your gut instinct, but would think about the 
                problem before answering, might say to yourself or out loud: 
                Well, if the ball costs, say, a dime, then the bat would cost 
                $1.10 and together they&#39;d cost $1.20. If the ball costs 5 cents, 
                then the bat would cost $1.05 and together they&#39;d cost $1.10. 
                Some of you might even instinctively answer &quot;5 cents.&quot; Those of 
                you who are skeptics and critical thinkers, but did not 
                instinctively answer &quot;5 cents,&quot; will learn from this example of 
                what Kahneman and Frederick call Type 1 thinking. This is 
                non-reflective thinking. There are some people whose 
                non-reflective thinking is often accurate and reliable. They&#39;re 
                the kind of people Malcolm Gladwell promised to explain in &lt;i&gt;
                Blink &lt;/i&gt;(but didn&#39;t), though he was onto the answer in &lt;i&gt;
                &lt;a href="http://skepdic.com/refuge/outliers.html"&gt;Outliers&lt;/a&gt;
                &lt;/i&gt;(though I don&#39;t think he saw it): some people instinctively 
                use their
                &lt;a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prefrontal_cortex"&gt;
                prefrontal cortex&lt;/a&gt; to solve problems and they&#39;ve engaged in a 
                great deal of practice at making critical thinking instinctive.&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;
                &lt;img border="0" src="graphics/oprahw.jpg" width="140" height="211"&gt;Oprah, I think it can 
                be safely said, has proved that you don&#39;t need to rely on your 
                prefrontal cortex to achieve great things in this world. George 
                W. Bush proved the same thing. Their appeal is not to the 
                critical thinker, but to the feelings and emotions that 
                unconsciously drive the thinking of most people most of the 
                time. It is often said of George W. Bush that he thinks with his 
                gut. He was able to use fear  not only to stay in power, but to 
                rally millions of people behind a series of irrational behaviors 
                that his gut led him to. Oprah is also led by her gut or her 
                heart. The same is true of billions of other people. All 
                successful hucksters know this. Their success does not come from 
                appealing to the higher brain functions, but to the parts of the 
                brain governing instinctual survival behaviors and instinctual 
                emotional responses. The successful hucksters appeal to fear, 
                pity, vanity, and physical pleasure. They don&#39;t appeal to our 
                ability to understand probability or double-blind randomized 
                studies. They don&#39;t appeal to our ability to critically examine 
                all the relevant evidence for an issue. Why should they? Most of 
                us probably wouldn&#39;t pay attention. Hucksters intuitively 
                know that &lt;a href="http://skepdic.com/magicalthinking.html"&gt;
                magical thinking&lt;/a&gt;, the quintessential Type 1 thinking, drives 
                most of us most of the time.&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;Talk show hosts, 
                advertisers, and politicians have at least one thing in common: 
                they must appeal to Type 1 thinking if they are to survive. Talk 
                show hosts would have no audience if they stopped to reflect on 
                anything said on their shows. Their job is to keep it moving and 
                keep their guests moving. The greatest fear a talk show host or 
                guest can have is fear of silence. Jenny McCarthy will make a 
                perfect talk show host, if only because she seems to abhor 
                silence. You will not have to worry about her braking for a 
                moment of reflection. She&#39;ll stick to what her gut and her heart 
                drives her to say. Why wouldn&#39;t Oprah love her?&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;Oprah and Bush are 
                two of the most successful hucksters of our time, but let&#39;s 
                leave Bush (and, by&lt;img border="0" src="graphics/philmac.jpg" width="163" height="179" style="float: right" lowsrc="Phil%20McGraw" alt="Phil McGraw"&gt; association, Cheney) out of this for the 
                time being. Let&#39;s focus on Oprah and her attraction to such 
                people as Phil McGraw and Jenny McCarthy. As many of you know, 
                Oprah met McGraw when she was called to defend herself against 
                some cattle ranchers in Texas. (See&lt;i&gt;
                &lt;a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ISBN=1400054109/roberttoddcarrolA/"&gt;
                Sham&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/i&gt; for details.)&lt;i&gt; &lt;/i&gt;They sued her for defaming 
                their product. Before millions of viewers of her talk show, she 
                said she&#39;d eaten her last burger when told by some &quot;expert&quot; that 
                &quot;mad cow disease&quot; may be waiting for her in her next beefy meal. 
                McGraw acted as an advisor on jury selection or some such thing. 
                Oprah liked him and the rest is history. The very thought of 
                doing psychotherapy in public before a live audience as a form 
                of entertainment is a bit much for the rational mind to take, 
                but it&#39;s a natural for Oprah and her fans. You can&#39;t defend it 
                on an intellectual level. Its appeal is purely to the emotions, 
                like the Jerry Springer show. &lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;Why does Oprah like 
                Jenny McCarthy so much that she thinks she can be another Dr. 
                Phil, i.e., a successful entertainer? At least McGraw can 
                occasionally dress 
                up his entertainment as intellectually defensible by applying 
                some well-known psychological practices and terms. McCarthy&#39;s 
                intellectual credentials are not her strong suit, of course. Her 
                physical features, her non-stop mouth, her &quot;vivaciousness,&quot; if 
                you will, are her main qualities that promise success on the 
                Oprah train. But Oprah&#39;s had many guests who fit that bill, so 
                why Jenny? I think the main reason goes back to all those 
                letters sent to Oprah from parents of kids with autism who asked 
                her to please, please, please, save our children from more 
                devastation by speaking out against vaccines.&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;Here is what Barbara 
                Loe Fisher, who calls herself an &quot;autism mom,&quot; wrote on her blog 
                after Jenny McCarthy appeared on
                &lt;a href="http://skepdic.com/refuge/harm8.html"&gt;Oprah&#39;s show 
                in 2007&lt;/a&gt; as a &quot;warrior mom&quot; in the fight against vaccines as 
                the cause of autism:&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;blockquote&gt;
                  &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;When she was not 
                  yet a superstar or even broadcasting nationally, in early 1985 
                  a friend gave Oprah a copy of &quot;DPT: A Shot in the Dark,&quot; which 
                  was the first report of an association between autism and 
                  vaccination. &quot;We&#39;re waiting until it comes out in paperback,&quot; 
                  a producer told her friend. The paperback version was 
                  published in 1986 but, by then, Oprah had starred in &quot;The 
                  Color Purple,&quot; her show had been nationally syndicated and the 
                  vaccine safety debate had become a heated controversy. Over 
                  the last two decades, many parents of vaccine injured children 
                  have written letters to Oprah in hopes that she would do a 
                  show about vaccine risks.&lt;a href="http://adventuresinautism.blogspot.com/2007/09/jenny-mccarthy-on-oprah-vaccine-injury.html"&gt;*&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;/blockquote&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;Yes, and over those 
                past
                &lt;a href="http://skepdic.com/skeptimedia/autismthimerosal.html"&gt;
                two decades numerous scientific studies have been published on 
                the issue and the evidence has failed to support the notion that 
                vaccines are a significant risk factor for autism&lt;/a&gt;. But it 
                doesn&#39;t matter to Barbara Loe Fisher,&nbsp; Jenny McCarthy, or 
                Oprah Winfrey. Once their guts told them vaccines are a problem, 
                their brains were guided by
                &lt;a href="http://skepdic.com/confirmbias.html"&gt;confirmation 
                bias&lt;/a&gt;. They ignored or brushed off the scientific evidence 
                and found &quot;experts&quot; who agreed with them. Mostly, however, they 
                collected &lt;a href="http://skepdic.com/testimon.html"&gt;
                anecdotes&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;The letters from 
                parents about their &quot;vaccine injured children&quot; was not the first 
                time Oprah has been moved to promote junk science because her 
                gut told her to do so. (She&#39;s even promoted
                &lt;a href="http://skepdic.com/refuge/bunk34.html"&gt;critical 
                thinking&lt;/a&gt; because her gut told her to do it!)&nbsp; She did 
                it for road rage.&nbsp; A few vivid anecdotes are all it takes 
                for Oprah to turn a few stories about disturbed people and motor vehicles into a candidate for 
                admission into the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental 
                Disorders. She did it with breast implants. Jenny Jones and 
                Oprah had programs featuring women who&#39;d had breast implants 
                telling the stories of their horrific suffering from painful 
                disorders. The general public, governed by Type 1 thinking, 
                would reasonably conclude from these sad stories that there must 
                be strong evidence that breast implants caused these disorders. 
                There was no scientific study that had shown any such 
                connection, but it didn&#39;t matter. It didn&#39;t matter even when the 
                evidence came in and showed that the more than one million women 
                who had had breast implants did not suffer from any major 
                disease or disorder at a greater rate than women who had &lt;i&gt;not&lt;/i&gt; 
                had breast implants.&lt;a href="../refuge/funk2.html#implants"&gt;*&lt;/a&gt; 
                (Oprah is not unique here. Remember that the FDA banned silicone 
                implants. Unlike Oprah and millions of others, however, once the 
                evidence clearly showed that silicone implants do not cause all 
                the diseases and disorders that had been blamed on them, the FDA 
                admitted its error. It lifted the ban. Remember also that the 
                media played a major role in perpetuating the fear of breast 
                implants, the &quot;illness&quot; of road rage, and the hysteria around 
                childhood vaccinations, but they have played almost no role in 
                correcting any of these errors.)&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
                &lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;&lt;font size="3"&gt;Understanding why 
                Oprah loves Jenny McCarthy is as easy as understanding why Dick 
                &lt;a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/11/us/politics/11cheney.html?_r=1"&gt;Cheney loves Rush&lt;/a&gt; Limbaugh. Jenny McCarthy is loved by Oprah 
                because Jenny represents what Oprah represents to Type 1 
                thinking: an attractive, caring storyteller, someone with 
                compassion and courage, someone from Heartland (upstream from 
                Gutland) who is here to 
                help and protect us from harm. The fact that McCarthy doesn&#39;t 
                know what she&#39;s talking about when it comes to autism or 
                vaccines is as irrelevant as the fact that Cheney and Limbaugh 
                don&#39;t know what they&#39;re talking about when it comes to 
                9/11 and terrorism. (For details on the latter pair, see &lt;i&gt;
                &lt;a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ISBN=0525950621/roberttoddcarrolA/"&gt;
                The Science of Fear&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/i&gt;.) She&#39;s compelling to the 
                unreflective mind looking into the mirror. She&#39;s not a threat to 
                anyone who considers evidence and medical training essential to 
                the credibility of someone making claims about vaccines. On the 
                other hand, someone like
                &lt;a href="http://skepdic.com/skeptimedia/skeptimedia5.html"&gt;
                Mehmet Oz, another Oprah favorite&lt;/a&gt;, has the potential to do

                authority on medical matters. Let&#39;s hope he&#39;s not McCarthy&#39;s 
                first guest when the topic is vaccines. Even if he is, though, 
                neither he nor McCarthy could ever match the harm done by the 
                political merchants of fear whose motto seems to be: &lt;i&gt;the only 
                thing we need fear is the end of the fear of fear.&lt;/i&gt;&lt;/font&gt;&lt;p style="text-align: left"&gt;
