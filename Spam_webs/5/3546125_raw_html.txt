        <h1 style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Facebook introduces spherical video at the F8 Facebook Developer Conference 2015
         </strong>
        </h1>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="400" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/ngJI-9LXp1Y?autoplay=1&amp;rel=0&amp;controls=0&amp;showinfo=0" width="700">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Social media giant Facebook is currently testing what it calls spherical videos, part of a new breed of online video you can move through, very similar to 3D gaming videos.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Zuckerberg says Were at this interesting transition where were starting to see traditional video blend with even more immersive content.
         <br/>
         Company founder and CEO Mark Zuckerberg unraveled the new technology this morning during his keynote opening address at Facebooks annual F8 developer conference in San Francisco.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Mark Zuckerberg Facebook introduces spherical video at the F8 Facebook Developer Conference 2015" class=" wp-image-2163 alignleft" height="338" src="http://aleshadrew.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Mark-Zuckerberg-Facebook-introduces-spherical-video-at-the-F8-Facebook-Developer-Conference-2015.png" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         You can move around within it, and you can change your viewing angle. And thats possible because the videos are filmed on up to 24 different cameras at once
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its not something you just consume passively, youre actually interacting with it, and you feel like youre there. 
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          People already watch 3 billion videos a day in news feed
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="F8 Facebook 2015" class="alignleft wp-image-2152" height="263" src="http://aleshadrew.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/F8-Facebook-2015.png" width="475"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Youre going to be able to put on your head set and feel like youre really there.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The company is demonstrating the new video format for attendees at this weeks F8 conference by showing off the 24-camera-view spherical camera at Facebook headquarters in Menlo Park, California.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Zuckerberg describes the teleportation station Oculus as humorously and ironically named.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The plan, Zuckerberg says, is to eventually have these spherical videos available in the news feed.
         <br/>
         As soon as today, the company is asking third-party publishers to create their own spherical videos for sharing across Facebook.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
