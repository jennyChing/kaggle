     <p>
      <b>
       Angus Oblong
      </b>
      is the
      <a href="/wiki/Pen_name" title="Pen name">
       pen name
      </a>
      of an American writer and illustrator best known as the author of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Creepy_Susie_and_13_Other_Tragic_Tales_for_Troubled_Children" title="Creepy Susie and 13 Other Tragic Tales for Troubled Children">
        Creepy Susie and 13 Other Tragic Tales for Troubled Children
       </a>
      </i>
      (1999) and the 2001 television series it spawned,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Oblongs" title="The Oblongs">
        The Oblongs
       </a>
      </i>
      . The character Milowhich appeared in his
      <i>
       Creepy Susie
      </i>
      bookwas based on a young version of himself; the Milo that appeared on the television show
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Oblongs" title="The Oblongs">
        The Oblongs
       </a>
      </i>
      was a less exaggerated version of the character from the book.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Work">
       Work
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Oblong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Work">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Oblong's book,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Creepy_Susie_and_13_Other_Tragic_Tales_for_Troubled_Children" title="Creepy Susie and 13 Other Tragic Tales for Troubled Children">
        Creepy Susie and 13 Other Tragic Tales for Troubled Children
       </a>
      </i>
      , consists of 13 short stories of adult-oriented humor. Many of the characters from his TV series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Oblongs" title="The Oblongs">
        The Oblongs
       </a>
      </i>
      first appear in this book, including Milo and Creepy Susie. A self-published sequel,
      <i>
       13 More Tragic Tales for Ugly Children
      </i>
      , is available from his website.
     </p>
     <p>
      He has also
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-publish" title="Self-publish">
       self-published
      </a>
      three collections of drawings:
      <i>
       Angus Oblong Random Drawings Book One
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Book Two
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Book Three
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      His television series,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Oblongs" title="The Oblongs">
        The Oblongs
       </a>
      </i>
      , airing originally on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_WB_Television_Network" title="The WB Television Network">
       The WB
      </a>
      and in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_syndication" title="Television syndication">
       syndication
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">
       Adult Swim
      </a>
      . The complete series was released on DVD.
     </p>
     <p>
      Oblong wrote, directed, and produced
      <i>
       Deliriously Jen
      </i>
      , a television pilot that aired on
      <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">
       Comedy Central
      </a>
      and was shown at several film festivals in 2005.
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       The Victorian Hotel
      </i>
      , a play written by Oblong, and created in association with
      <a href="/wiki/Rogue_Artists_Ensemble" title="Rogue Artists Ensemble">
       Rogue Artists Ensemble
      </a>
      , featuring many of his characters as puppets, played October through December 2006 at the Powerhouse Theatre in
      <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">
       Santa Monica, California
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">
       Personal life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Angus_Oblong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Oblong was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">
       Sacramento, California
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and attended the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">
       University of California, Berkeley
      </a>
      for a couple of years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
       Los Angeles
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">
       California
      </a>
      at the request of his agent when a "bidding war" was underway over whether
      <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">
       FOX
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">
       The WB
      </a>
      , or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney-ABC_Television_Group" title="Disney-ABC Television Group">
       Disney
      </a>
      would produce what became
      <i>
       The Oblongs
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      When his work began garnering attention from the press, Oblong began wearing "thick clown makeup and a bulbous, red plastic nose" in public places, a practice he has continued into 2010, when
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/LA_Weekly" title="LA Weekly">
        LA Weekly
       </a>
      </i>
      named him one of the L.A. People 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
