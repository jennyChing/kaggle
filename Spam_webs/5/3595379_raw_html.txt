     <h1 class="entrytitle" id="post-154">
      Watermelon Juice Smoothie, The Perfect Summer Treat
     </h1>
     <div class="entrymeta">
      Posted by Levi on July 3, 2008  in
      <a href="http://coachlevi.com/category/nutrition/" rel="category tag">
       Nutrition Articles
      </a>
      <a href="http://coachlevi.com/nutrition/watermelon-juice-smoothie-recipe/#comments">
       <img alt="*" src="http://coachlevi.com/wp-content/themes/coachlevi2/images/comments.gif" title="comments"/>
       Comments(5)
      </a>
      and
      <a href="http://coachlevi.com/tag/juice/" rel="tag">
       juice
      </a>
      |
      <a href="http://coachlevi.com/tag/smoothie/" rel="tag">
       smoothie
      </a>
      |
      <a href="http://coachlevi.com/tag/watermelon/" rel="tag">
       watermelon
      </a>
     </div>
     <!-- end entrymeta -->
     <div class="entrybody">
      <p>
       <img alt="watermelon smoothie" src="http://coachlevi.com/images/watermelonsmoothie.jpg" style="float: left; padding: 6px;" width="250"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       There is no better way to celebrate summer than with a big slice of watermelon (especially with the 4th of July right around the corner.)  But if you are looking to spice things up,
       <strong>
        try this watermelon smoothie!
       </strong>
       It has that great watermelon flavor with a little extra kick.
      </p>
      <p>
       Plus it is packed with
       <strong>
        lycopene
       </strong>
       .  I dont know about you, but Id rather have a frosty glass of watermelon juice than tomato juice!
      </p>
      <p>
       (Lycopene is a bright red carotenoid pigment and phytochemical found in tomatoes and other red fruits, such as watermelon  and its one of the most potent antioxidants out there!)
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       Heres what youll need:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        2 cups Chopped Seedless Watermelon
       </li>
       <li>
        1 cup Crushed Ice
       </li>
       <li>
        1 tsp Honey
       </li>
       <li>
        1 tsp Lemon Juice
       </li>
       <li>
        1/4 tsp Black Pepper
       </li>
       <li>
        Watermelon wedge (for garnish)
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Step 1: Prepare.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
      <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
       <script type="text/javascript">
        &lt;!--

       </script>
       <script src="http://pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/show_ads.js" type="text/javascript">
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       First, chop watermelon into chunks.  Second, crush some ice.  (Or, what I prefer to do is freeze the watermelon chunks.  You can skip the ice in this case.)
      </p>
      <p>
       Removing the seeds isnt a bad idea, either, because even the little white ones can be annoying in a smooth drink like this.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Step 2: Blend.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Blend the watermelon and ice into a thick drink.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Step 3: Add extra flavor.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Add in the honey, lemon juice, and black pepper.  Make sure the pepper is finely ground or it will sink to the bottom.
      </p>
      <p>
       (Dont be concerned about the black pepper.  It actually adds a nice kick, but you dont really taste the pepper itself.)
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Step 4: Blend.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Now blend it all together.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Step 5: Garnish.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Pour the finished drink into a glass.
      </p>
      <p>
       Slide a watermelon wedge on the side of the glass to garnish.  (Just like youd do with a lemon wedge.)
      </p>
      <p>
       Then serve!
      </p>
