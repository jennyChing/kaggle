            <h1 class="documentFirstHeading" id="parent-fieldname-title">
             FSF Licensing &amp; Compliance Team
            </h1>
            <div id="viewlet-below-content-title">
             <div class="documentByLine" id="plone-document-byline">
              <span class="documentAuthor">
               by
               <a href="http://www.fsf.org/author/jgay">
                Joshua Gay
               </a>
               <a class="netscape4" href="http://www.fsf.org/contribs/jgay">
                Contributions
               </a>
              </span>
              <span class="documentModified">
               
               <span>
                Published on
               </span>
               Feb 10, 2005 04:06 PM
              </span>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="documentDescription" id="parent-fieldname-description">
             For over 20 years the FSF Licensing &amp; Compliance Lab has been the preeminent resource of free licensing for free software developers.
            </div>
            <div id="viewlet-above-content-body">
            </div>
            <div id="content-core">
             <div class="" id="parent-fieldname-text-5a4649c8df6c2815f0b872df8a6f90fa">
              <h2>
               Education &amp; Support
              </h2>
              <p>
               We have a number of online resources as well as community-based and paid
support.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                See our licensing recommendations, analysis, and FAQ
               </strong>
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                <a href="http://www.gnu.org/licenses/license-recommendations.html">
                 Guide to choosing a license for your own
    work
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                <a href="http://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-faq.html">
                 Comprehensive FAQ about the GNU
    Licenses
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                <a href="http://www.gnu.org/licenses/license-list.html">
                 List of other licenses and whether they are free, copyleft, or
    compatible with the
    GPL
                </a>
                .
               </li>
              </ul>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Learn why we must put an end to software patents
               </strong>
               :
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                <a href="http://endsoftpatents.org/">
                 Visit EndSoftPatents.org
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                <a href="http://groups.fsf.org/wiki/Group:EndSoftwarePatents">
                 Get Involved on the LibrePlanet.org End Software Patents
    Group
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                <a href="http://campaigns.fsf.org/cgi-bin/mailman/listinfo/esp-action-alert">
                 Join the action alert mailing
    list
                </a>
               </li>
              </ul>
              <p class="pullquote visualNoPrint">
               I must say that the
               <em>
                vast
               </em>
               majority of my questions have been answered by the thorough FAQs included around the FSF website. I needed them, for example, when I helped someone correctly GPL their software in under 10 minutes in time for a competition deadline! Thank you for all the resources you already provide.
              </p>
              <p>
               Keep up to date with the latest licensing issues by visiting our
               <a href="http://www.fsf.org/blogs/licensing/">
                License and Compliance blog
               </a>
               , or
subscribe to the
               <a href="/static/fsforg/rss/licensing.xml" title="Subscribe to an RSS feed of FSF news">
                Licensing and Compliance Blog RSS
feed
               </a>
               <a href="/static/fsforg/rss/licensing.xml" title="Subscribe to the licensing and compliance RSS feed of FSF news">
                <img alt="RSS
feed" src="/static/fsforg/css/img/rss.gif"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               Have a question that you couldnt find the answer to? For general free
software licensing questions please email
               <a href="mailto:licensing@fsf.org">
                licensing@fsf.org
               </a>
               . And of course, we would love any feedback you can provide -- good or bad! Our team of
               <a href="http://www.fsf.org/licensing/team">
                licensing volunteers
               </a>
               are committed to answering questions and providing quality educational resources to the free software community. We also offer paid support to proprietary software developers.
              </p>
              <h2>
               Copyright &amp; Compliance
              </h2>
              <ul>
               <li>
                The Free Software Foundation holds the copyright to many GNU
    packages, such as GCC and EMACS. When hackers contribute to these
    projects, we ask that they assign their copyright to enable us to
    enforce the license. For questions about assigning to the FSF,
    please contact us at
                <a href="mailto:assign@gnu.org">
                 assign@gnu.org
                </a>
                .
               </li>
              </ul>
              <div style="clear: right; float: right; text-align: center; font-style: normal; width: 20em; max-width: 90%; padding: 0px; margin: 0.3em 1em 1em; border: 0.3em solid rgb(52, 101, 164);">
               <strong>
                Become an FSF Corporate Patron
               </strong>
               <br/>
               <p>
                One of the benefits of becoming a
                <a href="https://www.fsf.org/patrons/benefits">
                 Corporate Patron of the FSF
                </a>
                is that you receive two complimentary hours of licensing and/or GPL consultation from the FSF's Compliance Lab (with a reduced rate for further consultation).
                <br/>
                To sign up as a patron, please write to
                <a href="mailto:patron@fsf.org">
                 patron@fsf.org
                </a>
                .
               </p>
              </div>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Free software is everywhere these days, inside our computers,
    phones, and even televisions. With so much free software being
    distributed every day, we have to remain vigilant against potential
    violations. You can help to make sure that free software is always
    free by
                <a href="http://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-violation.html">
                 reporting violations to our compliance lab
                </a>
                .
               </li>
               <li>
                The FSF can only enforce the license on works to which we hold the
    copyright, but we can still help bring about compliance even when
    the copyright lies elsewhere. If you need help with enforcement,
    please dont hesitate to contact us at
                <a href="mailto:license-violation@gnu.org">
                 license-violation@gnu.org
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                Many copyright holders seek monetary damages when their license is
    violated. We do not 
                <a href="http://www.fsf.org/licensing/compliance">
                 we only want violators to come back into
    compliance
                </a>
                , and help
    repair any harm done to the free software community by their past
    actions. Because of that, we contact violators directly, and
                <a href="http://www.fsf.org/licensing/dealt.html">
                 negotiate a strategy
                </a>
                with them that best accomplishes those goals.
               </li>
              </ul>
              <h2>
               Verification &amp; Certification
              </h2>
              <ul>
               <li>
                <a href="http://www.gnu.org/distros/free-distros.html">
                 Fully free GNU/Linux
    Distributions
                </a>
                . These
    distributions meet our guidelines for a fully free distro, and are
    fully committed to keeping their distributions free.
               </li>
               <li>
                You can help keep those distributions free and earn a
                <a href="http://www.gnu.org/help/gnu-bucks.html">
                 GNU
    Buck
                </a>
                by filing a bug
    regarding a licensing issue in one of our endorsed distributions and
    sending an email to report-nonfree@fsf.org.
               </li>
               <li>
                The
                <a href="http://directory.fsf.org/">
                 Free Software Directory
                </a>
                is our
    community curated listing of over 6500 free software packages. A
    great resource for finding and promoting software that respects its
    users.
               </li>
               <li>
                <a href="http://www.fsf.org/resources/hw/endorsement/respects-your-freedom">
                 Respects Your Freedom Hardware certification
    program
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                Looking to buy hardware that works well with free software? Check
    out
                <a href="http://h-node.org/">
                 h-node
                </a>
                , a community run project to catalog
    how well different pieces of hardware work with fully free software.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
