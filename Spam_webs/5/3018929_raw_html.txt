              Magic Mystery Bulb
             </h1>
             <div class="field-subtitle">
              <p>
               Transform an old lightbulb into a glowing April Fools prank.

              </noscript>
              <div class="field-title">
               <p>
                LED Prank Lamp
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="field-credit">
               <p>
                Son of Alan
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              The Addams Family's Uncle Fester is famous for popping lightbulbs into his mouth and making them glow. The TV trick inspired
              <a href="http://kipkay.com">
               Kip Kedersha
              </a>
              , a fan of the series, to build a prank lamp using an LED, batteries, and an old bulb. Create your own and light up a room on April 1.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               1)
              </strong>
              Wearing thick gloves, carefully twist a glass lightbulb out of its metal base and save both parts. Discard the filament.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               2)
              </strong>
              Solder a 2-inch wire to the base's inside center and another to the side. Stack and zip-tie two three-volt button batteries.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               3)
              </strong>
              To the short lead (anode)of a white LED, solder the center wire. Tape the other wire to the battery pack's negative end.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               4)
              </strong>
              Tape one end of a third wire to the battery pack's positive terminal. Solder the other end to the LED's long lead (cathode).
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               5)
              </strong>
              Coax the LED into the glass, fit the battery pack and wires into the base, and reconnect the bulb's two pieces with hot glue.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               6)
              </strong>
              Grasp the base, touching a wire that's hidden in your hand to its side and bottom. To the surprise of your pals, it should light up.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Time:
              </strong>
              1 hour
              <br/>
              <strong>
               Cost:
              </strong>
              $4
              <br/>
              <strong>
               Difficulty:
              </strong>
              1/5
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               WARNING:
              </strong>
              Broken glass can cause deep cuts and gashes. Do not screw the modified lightbulb into any socket.
             </p>
