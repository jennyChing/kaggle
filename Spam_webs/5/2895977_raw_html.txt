              <span>
               Social enterprise is the nexus of business and charity. At Haiti Babi, we often say that we are giving people a hand-up, not a hand-out. This distinction is a big deal to us, and it is part of what makes us a
               <strong>
                social enterprise
               </strong>
               .
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               At Haiti Babi, we create a product, which we sell to make money (business). We also want to empower moms in Haiti to
               <a href="haitibabi.org/about">
                care for their children
               </a>
               (mission/charity). So, we smush it all together and get something that is
               <strong>
                more meaningful
               </strong>
               than a traditional baby products company and
               <strong>
                more sustainable
               </strong>
               than traditional charity. We hire moms in Haiti to knit and crochet beautiful baby blankets and hats, which are sold in the US. The income generated from product sales goes to employ moms in Haiti so they can afford to care for their children.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               Giving someone a hand-out is easy. But when that hand-out is extinguished- the food has been eaten, the money spent, the clothes worn out- there is need for another hand-out.A never-ending cycle of poverty.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               But you only have to give someone a job once. And that job can create a steady stream of income for a Mom to buy her children food, pay rent on a safe house, buy clothes, and send kids to school. But above all, that job gives that Mom
               <a href="https://vimeo.com/112213829" target="_blank">
                pride
               </a>
               .
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <span>
                Heres the magic part:
               </span>
              </strong>
              <span>
               that Mom has kids. And now those kids are growing up with a Mom who is proud of herself. A mom who gets out of bed everyday, goes to work, and makes her life and the lives of her children better. Those kids are healthy because they are eating every day andgoing to the doctor when they need to, and they are now going to school to get an education. And that is
               <strong>
                breaking the cycleof poverty
               </strong>
               .
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               Thats sustainable change. That'ssocial enterprise.
              </span>
             </p>

                 <img alt="Fabiola" class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52a363f0e4b0f6c3609a0de8/t/546bde48e4b05a70ad80ade3/1416355402025/Fabiola" data-image-dimensions="1500x1000" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="546bde48e4b05a70ad80ade3" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52a363f0e4b0f6c3609a0de8/t/546bde48e4b05a70ad80ade3/1416355402025/Fabiola" data-type="image"/>
                </div>
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1416344917005_43764">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <p>
              <span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               According to the
               <a href="https://www.se-alliance.org/" target="_blank">
                Social Enterprise Alliance
               </a>
               , a social enterprise:
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <!--[if !supportLists]-->
              <span>
               1.
               <span style="font-size:7pt">
               </span>
              </span>
              <!--[endif]-->
              <span>
               Directly address an intractable social need and serves the common good
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <!--[if !supportLists]-->
              <span>
               2.
               <span style="font-size:7pt">
               </span>
              </span>
              <!--[endif]-->
              <span>
               Has commercial activity as a strong revenue driver
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <!--[if !supportLists]-->
              <span>
               3.
               <span style="font-size:7pt">
               </span>
              </span>
              <!--[endif]-->
              <span>
               Has the common good as its primary purpose
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <!--[if !supportLists]-->
              <span>
               4.
               <span style="font-size:7pt">
               </span>
              </span>
              <!--[endif]-->
              <span>
               Runs more efficiently than government
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <!--[if !supportLists]-->
              <span>
               5.
               <span style="font-size:7pt">
               </span>
              </span>
              <!--[endif]-->
              <span>
               Is more sustainable and creative than the non-profit sector, and
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <!--[if !supportLists]-->
              <span>
               6.
               <span style="font-size:7pt">
               </span>
              </span>
              <!--[endif]-->
              <span>
               Is more generous than business.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               Business as usual is hurting a lot of people, and our planet. And charity as usual isnt enough to solve the growing needs of our global community. Our traditional institutions are no longer sufficient. We live in a world of growing problems, and
               <strong>
                we need a change.
               </strong>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               Social enterprise is more than just businesses making donations. In social enterprise, business and mission are interwoven so they are bonded together, and they are stronger together. Creating social value and creating economic value happen simultaneously.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               Imagine what the future looks likewhen all enterprise is social enterprise.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               Want to learn more about social enterprise? Check out this
               <a href="http://zana.io/lessons/disruption-for-social-good/innovators/#1" target="_blank">
                video series
               </a>
               on Disruption for Social Good by Zana, or my all-time favorite
               <a href="http://www.ted.com/talks/dan_pallotta_the_way_we_think_about_charity_is_dead_wrong" target="_blank">
                TED Talk
               </a>
               by
               <a href="http://www.danpallotta.com/" target="_blank">
                Dan Palotta
               </a>
               .
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
