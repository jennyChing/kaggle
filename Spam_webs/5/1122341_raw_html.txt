        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         5 Athlete Secrets to Steal
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-02-18T07:00:10+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 18, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/author/healthperch/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Health Perch
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <h1 style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/header-1.png">
          <img alt="5 Athlete Secrets To Steal" class="aligncenter wp-image-524" height="554" src="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/header-1.png" width="922"/>
         </a>
        </h1>
        <h1 style="text-align: center;">
         How to champion for your health
        </h1>
        <p>
         When Olympic athletes compete for their country  they are at the top of their game. They are prepared not only physically, but mentally to compete on the world stage. We cant help but wonder how they got there. True, sports like cross-country and ice skating burn major caloriesa minimum of 510 and 425, respectivelybut is that all it takes to compete?
        </p>
        <p>
         In short, no. And after we did some digging, we found the best ways athletes physically and mentally stay on top of their game.Heres how you can medal in terms of your health, too.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <b>
          1. Eat clean
         </b>
        </h2>
        <p>
         A majority of Olympiansand athletes in generalfollow a strict, low-fat diet. With the exception of Usain Bolt, of course, who has admitted to fueling his run with McDonalds nuggets. (
         <a href="http://www.runnersworld.com/general-interest/usain-bolt-ate-1000-mcnuggets-at-2008-olympics" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.runnersworld.com/general-interest/usain-bolt-ate-1000-mcnuggets-at-2008-olympics', 'Seriously']);">
          Seriously
         </a>
         .)
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          However,
         </em>
         95% of those diets are made up of whole, quality foods with cheat days factored in,
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/07/30/olympic-diets_n_1717713.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/07/30/olympic-diets_n_1717713.html', 'says U.S. Olympic Committee Nutritionist Jennifer Gibson, RD']);">
          says U.S. Olympic Committee Nutritionist Jennifer Gibson, RD
         </a>
         . Its not as sexy as some new seed from the Amazon, she says, but it achieves balance.
        </p>
        <p>
         That means replacing processed foods with their more natural counterparts. Think whole apples instead of applesauce, homemade soup instead of canned, whole wheat grains instead of white, grass-fed beef instead of frozen patties, etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         Still need some inspiration? Check out these
         <a href="http://www.cookinglight.com/eating-smart/smart-choices/clean-eating-recipes-00412000069359/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.cookinglight.com/eating-smart/smart-choices/clean-eating-recipes-00412000069359/', '10 Clean Eating Recipes']);">
          10 Clean Eating Recipes
         </a>
         from
         <i>
          Cooking Light.
         </i>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <b>
          2. Exercise
         </b>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Its no secret that athletes spend a majority of their time in trainingup to eight hours a day, to be exact. But you dont have to dismiss the idea of achieving their same abs or arms because you can barely find the time to go to the bathroom.
        </p>
        <p>
         As with any goal, the key is to start small and gradually progress as you get more comfortable. Maybe thats taking a 10 minute walk on your lunch break or scheduling to stay late for your co-workers yoga class. Whatever it is, commit to the idea of moving more.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres also something to competition: Researchers from Michigan State University found that women working out with a friend worked out harder and longer without even realizing.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/illustration-1.png">
          <img alt="5 Athlete Secrets To Steal" class=" wp-image-526 aligncenter" height="576" src="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/illustration-1.png" width="819"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <b>
          3. Mental Toughness
         </b>
        </h2>
        <p>
         A study published in the journal of
         <em>
          Personality and Individual Differences
         </em>
         <em>
          found that athletes are mentally tougher than non-athletes. What makes someone mentally tough? Optimism, perseverance and resilience.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         A sure-fire way to adopt, or strengthen, these qualities is to say
         <i>
          I can do it
         </i>
         over
         <i>
          How much longer?
         </i>
         Researchers from the University of Kent at Medway find that this kind of motivational self-talk can help a person feel stronger, which, in turn, makes exercise easier.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <b>
          4. Book a massage
         </b>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Yep, athletes pamper themselves, too. Researchers from McMaster University in Canada find that deep massage after an intense workout causes muscles to enlarge and grow new mitochondriathe cells that convert nutrients into energy. On a more general note, The American Massage Therapy Association (AMTA) says massage can reduce pain, prevent injury, and reduce stress and recovery time. As if you needed an excuse to go to the spa!
        </p>
        <p>
         Find a therapist near you when you visit
         <a href="http://www.amtamassage.org/index.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amtamassage.org/index.html', 'the AMTA website']);">
          the AMTA website
         </a>
         . Or, if thats not ideal,
         <i>
          Elle
         </i>
         has amazing
         <a href="http://www.ellecanada.com/living/health/diy-massage-tips/a/24713" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ellecanada.com/living/health/diy-massage-tips/a/24713', 'at-home tips you can try']);">
          at-home tips you can try
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h2>
         <b>
          5. Quality Sleep
         </b>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Low light can disrupt the sleep hormone melatonin, says sleep expert Dr. Robert Oexman. And blackout curtains, available at places like Target, help to keep the room dark.
        </p>
        <p>
         Other ways to get Olympic shut-eye is to keep your room temperature to 67F and making a point to wake up early. This direct morning light exposure has been proven to align the daily, natural body clock and increase overall energy for the day, says Dr. Oexman.
         <b>
         </b>
        </p>

        </p>
