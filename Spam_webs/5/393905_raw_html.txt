        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Big Breakfasts Are Key to Weight Loss &amp; Disease Prevention
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-04-21T10:00:19+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 21, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://blog.grainberry.com/author/amy-forman/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Amy Forman
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://blog.grainberry.com/breakfast-ideas-for-weight-loss/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_255" style="width: 600px;">
         <a href="http://blog.grainberry.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/breakfast11.jpg">
          <img alt="Matt_Weibo/flickr" class="wp-image-255" height="450" src="http://blog.grainberry.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/breakfast11.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Matt_Weibo/
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/mattweibo/3128855154/in/photolist-5LucCw-8Ps5tz-8VtSb6-cLrrES-8pKbBj-2dz8Qe-ayDBDM-9oLR2j-998D3r-8piB53-5g52an-q7hSET-qiZ13h-ayGnzq-8bMx9C-bUgepf-6SbfvV-7aWp8u-ekfwNd-7x56X8-8cAJBs-63g76S-tTPeH-5djJhz-4FY2Be-8XBgX6-5NYdaE-cdxnqA-7cLEZg-grM7HU-AxBDq-e6uhui-dXJVKf-8ULT5T-evvnsD-5hgj4Y-8SiGA1-ayGuYw-4BdJVd-ayDFwg-2gqPnt-E88gq-brUF4U-o14io5-63bQqk-duB2Pg-6fbboj-daubEC-8V5hCk-dau9Ve">
           flickr
          </a>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         <em>
          Sitting down to a healthy morning meal is more than just a nice way to start the day  eating a hefty, high-energy breakfast is good for you in more ways than one.
         </em>
         <span id="more-253">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         This one goes out to all the mothers whove been demanding that their children eat breakfast, whether they want to or not. Science is now on their side  with more proof backing up their claim than ever before.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Breakfast &amp; Weight Loss
        </h3>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://ajcn.nutrition.org/content/early/2014/06/04/ajcn.114.083402" target="_blank">
          American Journal of Clinical Nutrition
         </a>
         recently revealed that people who eat a hearty breakfast actually burn more calories through physical activity than those who skip breakfast. In other words, those who ate breakfast tended to move more  and more efficiently  than those who didnt, and burned significantly more calories while doing so.
        </p>
        <p>
         According to the report, this effect lasted throughout the entire day, and the subjects remained more alert and more energetic after enjoying a balanced early-day meal.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Quality Counts
        </h3>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_257" style="width: 600px;">
         <a href="http://blog.grainberry.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/donuts_and_coffee.jpg">
          <img alt="Roy Patrick Tan/flickr" class="wp-image-257" height="437" src="http://blog.grainberry.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/donuts_and_coffee.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Roy Patrick Tan/
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/flyingroc/5450687951/in/photolist-9iEcCt-cZjcvd-e7DUGM-mtJNW-4gskAS-kvfKhk-FksT5-FksS1-7KzkHA-xxSZ9-eg6u5P-9RTjGd-5s6JCZ-rahDv3-n4SKYF-qYY8SK-qGEAdR-qYY8cX-q3keVB-qWPVqd-oWag9y-qGEzPV-ea9f6H-ax16EL-7Kvpgz-7qWFq4-5gmPNJ-bHnrhH-nVHRUi-dT15DZ-4LrEoi-9zLhf-5h5afA-4u52mF-bKd2aa-dWhTpv-9tiCgy-a1VK5Y-6RpUPX-9ArjZo-8rm4HH-4u959E-akJVMB-2ism9-6FkdKG-a91kx5-5bCVQ-eg8kFV-fr74sK-2HxJP">
           flickr
          </a>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         There are some requirements, of course  if you start each day with a mocha frappuccino and a hearty donut, you might be wondering why this isnt working for you. What you eat, of course, matters just as much as when you eat it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Authorities like
         <a href="http://www.wakehealth.edu/Health-Central/MCN/Breakfast-Tips.htm" target="_blank">
          Wake Forest Baptist Health
         </a>
         recommend combining fruits, grains, and protein, limiting processed foods, and eating (rather than drinking) your breakfast. All of these elements combined provide a healthy and balanced nutritional foundation for your day.
        </p>
        <p>
         Incorporating products like Grain Berry Cereals into your breakfast lets you swiftly check off your whole grains requirement using a tasty product with minimal processing.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Timing is Everything
        </h3>
        <p>
         Another study published in the
         <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24809437" target="_blank">
          <em>
           Journal of the American College of Nutrition
          </em>
         </a>
         found that even with well-established regiments like the Mediterranean Diet, timing is a critical factor.
        </p>
        <p>
         The study followed people adhering strictly to the Mediterranean Diet, eating the same food and the same number of calories each day, but at different times  one group ate the majority of their calories early in the day, while the other group spread their meals throughout it. Both groups saw decisive improvements to their weight and health, but the morning-eaters made significantly larger strides.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Health Benefits
        </h3>
        <figure class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_259" style="width: 180px;">
         <a href="http://blog.grainberry.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/handstand1.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="wp-image-259 size-medium" height="300" src="http://blog.grainberry.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/handstand1-180x300.jpg" width="180"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Carl Malamud/
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/publicresourceorg/494004916/in/photolist-8287pT-85Sx7k-85Sx74-82bf2s-8287rk-8287ra-82bf1j-8287qv-an4J3Q-byhsus-57hx6v-57q9m7-57q9ij-57ttxv-57xE5U-57tti4-57xDTG-57pjum-KDqUB-8TNuqC-KDEr3-aoGgAf-KDUs9-KE4Dr">
           flickr
          </a>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The benefits of eating breakfast span far beyond just weight loss. In the above study, committed breakfast eaters also saw a notable improvement in their insulin sensitivity. In the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition study referenced earlier, experts found that glycemia levels also remained steadier throughout the day after a balanced breakfast.
        </p>
        <p>
         Another study from
         <a href="http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2015/02/150224182541.htm" target="_blank">
          Science Daily
         </a>
         reported that diabetics who ate large, energy-rich breakfasts and low-energy dinners exhibited steadier blood sugar. If these three sets of data werent proof enough, a study from Harvard published in the journal
         <a href="http://circ.ahajournals.org/content/128/4/337.abstract" target="_blank">
          Circulation
         </a>
         found that eating breakfast is associated with a significantly lower risk of heart disease.
        </p>
        <p>
         The results couldnt be clearer  all of these studies suggest that eating a balanced breakfast not only helps you control and maintain health, but also prevents the onset of nutrition-related diseases like diabetes. The takeaway is simple: to maintain optimal health, make a healthy, well-rounded breakfast a fundamental part of your day.
        </p>
