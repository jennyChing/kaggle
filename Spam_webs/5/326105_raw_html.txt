          <img alt="IMG_5225" border="0" height="367" src="http://images.ohsheglows.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/IMG_5225_thumb.jpg" style="display: inline; border: 0px;" title="IMG_5225" width="550"/>

        <h3>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          Easy Salt &amp; Vinegar Roasted Chickpeas
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Adapted from
         <a href="http://ohsheglows.com/2010/07/30/homemade-salt-vinegar-chips/">
          Homemade Salt &amp; vinegar Chips and Green Beans
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          Ingredients:
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1.5-2 cups cooked chickpeas (one 15-oz can), drained and rinsed*
         </li>
         <li>
          2.5-4 cups white vinegar
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2-1 tsp fine grain sea salt, to taste
         </li>
         <li>
          2 tsp extra virgin olive oil
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Directions
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Line a baking sheet with tin foil or parchment paper.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Add chickpeas into pot and cover with vinegar. Add a dash of sea salt. Open a window and turn on range hood fan to air out the soon-to-be potent vinegar smell. &lt; you were warned!
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Bring to a boil and then remove from heat. Let sit in pot for 15-20 minutes, uncovered.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Preheat oven to 400F. Carefully drain chickpeas in a strainer and discard vinegar. Place chickpeas on lined baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil and sea salt. Massage with fingers until fully coated.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Roast for 25-35 minutes, stirring once half way through. Keep a careful eye on them after 20 minutes of cooking to ensure they dont burn. Enjoy as a
         <strong>
          high protein snack
         </strong>
         or as a side to a lunch or dinner. Cool on pan for 5 minutes. They will crisp up as they cool.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          *Note
         </strong>
         : I assume you could also use dry chickpeas and cook them in vinegar from scratch.
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          Also note
         </span>
         : When I brought the cooked chickpeas to a boil, some of the peas split open and some of the skins came off. Not to worry if this happens-it will turn out regardless!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://images.ohsheglows.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/IMG_5226.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_5226" border="0" height="366" src="http://images.ohsheglows.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/IMG_5226_thumb.jpg" style="display: inline; border: 0px;" title="IMG_5226" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I loved these! The salt &amp; vinegar taste was amazing and really came through.
        </p>
