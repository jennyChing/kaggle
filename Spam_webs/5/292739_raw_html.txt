            <p class="first-text" data-textannotation-id="340fac1d0e241c460b81307b06c2c983">
             For those who don't know (because there are quite a few...let's be honest, not everyone reads comic books)
             <em>
              Captain America #22
             </em>
             came out last week, and there was a bit of an uproar on social media that resulted in calls to
             <a href="https://twitter.com/search?f=realtime&amp;q=%23firerickremender&amp;src=tyah" target="_blank">
              fire the author
             </a>
             , Rick Remender.
            </p>
            <p class="has-media media-300 has-image" data-textannotation-id="a8d6cad39eda80f7d54473733d8345b1">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border lazy-image lazy-gif">
               <img alt="When Having Opinions Brings Rape Threats: A Captain America Story" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--7hdRQESN--/802842246372653454.gif" data-chomp-id="802842246372653454" data-format="gif" data-mp4-src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--1CYlkAew--/802842246372653454.mp4" data-webm-src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--G7tjmSWc--/802842246372653454.webm" height="150" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--ubB4G1ZX--/802842246372653454.jpg" width="245"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="b876a79a1c112b0894c3f1ad00d670b0">
             At the heart of the argument was that Remender wrote
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falcon_(comics)" target="_blank">
              Sam Wilson
             </a>
             (beloved by many after
             <em>
              Captain America: the Winter Soldier,
             </em>
             where he's played by Anthony Mackie) having
             <a href="http://www.examiner.com/article/why-marvel-needs-to-fire-rick-remender" target="_blank">
              drunk sex
             </a>
             with a woman that many readers understood to be 14 years old. That specific claim is not the case (the character is in fact 23) but even after that clarification, people were upset.
            </p>
            <div class="ad-unit js_ad-unit hide-contents ad-mobile js_ad-mobile">
             <div class="ad-mobile-inner">
              <p class="ad-label proxima">
               <small class=" proxima">
                Advertisement
               </small>
              </p>
              <div class="ad-container js_ad-container" data-zone-type="MOBILE" id="ad-container-34178264">
              </div>
              <p class="ad-label-bottom">
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <p class="has-media media-470 has-image" data-textannotation-id="c849f652a98730defb4e6e3efe311ba0">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border lazy-image lazy-gif">
               <img alt="When Having Opinions Brings Rape Threats: A Captain America Story" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--PsRoodyx--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_320/802842246567891086.gif" data-chomp-id="802842246567891086" data-format="gif" data-mp4-src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--TfEhBGw7--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_320/802842246567891086.mp4" data-webm-src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--vlEvt-fG--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_320/802842246567891086.webm" height="179" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--0r4VyWm3--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_320/802842246567891086.jpg" width="320"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="a2390101ac4435073d147e91372c6b75">
             Here's the thing: the scene depicted was not, in fact, statutory rape. Jet, the young woman in question, has been in a dimension where time moves faster than it does on Earth (comic books, remember?) and so rather than being in her early-to-mid teens she is in her early twenties. I'm not going to argue that point. If you would like a good break down of the timeline, I point you
             <a href="http://moneystcroix.tumblr.com/post/90703508863/re-rick-remender-alleged-statutory-rape-and-jet" target="_blank">
              here
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="4a8135f2c05e03e27736ab9a42a1a3a8">
             But you have to admit that it's confusing. The fact that there's a single solitary line clarifying her age wasn't quite enough to explain precisely what was going on. On top of that, Remender doesn't have the best
             <a href="https://twitter.com/soufex/status/486500730841223169" target="_blank">
              reputation
             </a>
             when it comes to writing female characters and having recently
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Women_in_Refrigerators" target="_blank">
              fridged
             </a>
             * Sharon Carter, he's
             <a href="http://www.comicvine.com/rick-remender/4040-44849/forums/rick-remender-why-i-will-stop-buying-every-book-he-667201/" target="_blank">
              compounded
             </a>
             the
             <a href="http://www.therainbowhub.com/why-rick-remender-needs-to-stop/" target="_blank">
              bad
             </a>
             blood
             <a href="http://www.reddit.com/r/comicbooks/comments/1sq6gd/thought_this_could_provoke_discussion_why_rick/" target="_blank">
              he's
             </a>
             built
             <a href="http://roboclaws.tumblr.com/post/82464101709/is-cap-america-by-rick-remender-bad" target="_blank">
              up
             </a>
             with the "non-core" (read: white male) readers.
            </p>
            <p class="has-media media-300 has-image" data-textannotation-id="67598a08afff250ea3de1dc4fc002a00">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border lazy-image lazy-gif">
               <img alt="When Having Opinions Brings Rape Threats: A Captain America Story" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--Kzqh6Npj--/802842246890649486.gif" data-chomp-id="802842246890649486" data-format="gif" data-mp4-src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--8sBZEjku--/802842246890649486.mp4" data-webm-src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--f5-SASZN--/802842246890649486.webm" height="180" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--rG8ZV6Zd--/802842246890649486.jpg" width="245"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="8cbb47e7916513b94d8e6af39828d7e8">
             Sam Wilson is the first African American superhero (
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Panther_(comics)" target="_blank">
              Black Panther
             </a>
             , who predates him by three years, is African). And Anthony Mackie is Sam Wilson's biggest fan. He's been lobbying to be in Marvel movies since the very beginning, he's clearly invested in the industry; he's one of the few people involved in comic book movies who's been
             <a href="http://theanthonymackie.tumblr.com/post/83077916391/there-should-be-a-wonder-woman-movie-i-dont-care" target="_blank">
              outspoken in his support
             </a>
