        <h1 class="page-title" id="page-title" property="dc:title">
         NATO would 'fully respect' Ukraine application
        </h1>
        <ul class="l-meta l-row-1">
         <li>
          <span class="meta--author" property="dc:creator">
           <a href="/contributor/dow-jones-newswires" title="Dow Jones newswires">

     <div about="/news/2014/8/29/international-news/nato-would-fully-respect-ukraine-application" class="ds-1col node node--news view-mode-bs_full node--bs-full node--news--bs-full clearfix" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">
      <div class="field--body">
       <p>
        Anders Fogh Rasmussen, the secretary general of the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation, said on Friday he would "fully respect" any potential decision by Ukraine to try to join NATO.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Mr Rasmussen, speaking after an emergency meeting between NATO and Ukraine's ambassador to the alliance, said that "each and every nation has a right to decide for itself" on which alliances it seeks, and he called on Russia to show similar respect.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        However, the proposal by Ukraine's government that the country repeal a law banning membership in military blocs and move toward NATO membership wasn't discussed at the meeting, Mr Rasmussen said.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Any formal application from Ukraine to join the alliance would take years at least. Ukrainian leaders have said for months that seeking to join NATO, a move seen likely to provoke Moscow, wasn't a top priority. But the recent reported incursion of more than 1,000 Russian troops into Ukraine apparently prompted the government to change course.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        "I'm not going to interfere with a political discussion in Ukraine," Mr Rasmussen said.
       </p>
