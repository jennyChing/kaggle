                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-0DaJDbddIwU/VS_Uvm4MENI/AAAAAAAAg80/r_P_1AviaxQ/s1600/IMG_4741.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     IN PARIS . . .
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7025068634913423240" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="1439" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-0DaJDbddIwU/VS_Uvm4MENI/AAAAAAAAg80/r_P_1AviaxQ/s1600/IMG_4741.jpg" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      Some thoughts, some photos, from my time in Paris . . .
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                     </div>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <img border="0" height="600" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-lqwmnFaFhkE/VS_UYVsTPTI/AAAAAAAAg3I/fQiJDNNN5Ns/s1600/IMG_4643.jpg" style="text-align: justify;" width="900"/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      There is just something about the pace of life out there across the Atlantic that gets me. I don't know what it is. I
                      <i>
                       also
                      </i>
                      think the fact that the more I go on about it the more pathetic it starts to become has
                      <i>
                       got
                      </i>
                      to be part of the fun for me. :) When I was asked to give a conference keynote at The Hive in Berlin, I knew that part of my trip was going to have to include a little walkabout in Paris. Just me, my thoughts, and my feet on those cobblestone streets. It's been a big couple of years for me, big and odd. Brandon and I agreed that a little me-time was in order, some time to sit with myself for a few days and think some things through. Once I had Paris figured out, a few other cities sort of fell into place--a signing in Amsterdam, a meet up in London--so it went from a speaking engagement plus mini-vacation to this monster of a trip that I just really loved the hell out of and feel so lucky to have gotten.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      I'm planning a few posts in the coming days on where to stay, where to shop, how to pack, etc etc, and I'll get to those soon. But first, a few-odd photos and thoughts from my time in Paris. It's going to be all over the place. But then you expect that from here by now. :)
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="600" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-VlE-u7JafNc/VS_UaGDNG9I/AAAAAAAAg3g/njcjpOVLJQ8/s1600/IMG_4650.jpg" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      I stayed in the 3rd, in the heart of the Marais, on Rue Charlot. This was my view every morning when I woke up in the loft.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="900" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-95ye-cBAdhI/VTD_YakW8RI/AAAAAAAAhl8/t-otR28XXn8/s1600/IMG_6678.JPG" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      It was pretty gosh darn romantic.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      And just around corner from the
                      <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/le-march%C3%A9-des-enfants-rouges-paris-2" style="font-weight: bold;">
                       Le March des Enfants Rouges
                      </a>
                      . A lot of produce and cheese shops and delis and a cous cous place with spicy meats that was always packed with locals for lunch. Out front you could find leather bags, scarves, books, more books, flowers . . . and of course the cheese guy was always closed during the hours in which I really wanted some cheese, but I did manage to snag me a wedge of comt eventually. :)
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="900" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-_G_0-T9VXXk/VTD_R34RhOI/AAAAAAAAhkE/6swJBDIJexs/s1600/IMG_5979.JPG" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="900" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-3aruLJA8yeg/VTD_dq6ucMI/AAAAAAAAhnc/haVr5NnQee0/s1600/IMG_7105.JPG" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="900" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-YEvhDvOH-zc/VTD_eYLExyI/AAAAAAAAhns/3sTC-80VDfk/s1600/IMG_7107.JPG" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      Locals actually do buy a baguette first thing in the morning and then wander around with it, snacking here and there as they go. Fun fact: Morning doesn't really start until around 11AM. How fantastic is that. And hey, funner fact: You don't refrigerate your eggs in France. They're stacked out on the shelves in the markets with the bread and jams and cereals, and once you bring them home you just keep them on your counter. They're good to be left out for a few weeks, according to a woman I met who I then grilled with all sorts of stupid grocery shopping questions, so long as you haven't washed the "scuzz" off the eggs first.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="900" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DvgrtV5bUBA/VTD_R5mSFEI/AAAAAAAAhkA/Mj-OfQP4kLQ/s1600/IMG_5980.JPG" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      I started each morning with a stop in at the corner cafe to do some people-watching for as long as I could bear to sit still. And then I walked my little toesies off. But I
                      <i>
                       loved
                      </i>
                      The Marais. The Marais was amazing, I could have spent all my days there and been happy as a clam. I'd love to stay in the neighborhood again the next time I visit. Cute shop chats next to bottles of wine, really good food, bookshops filled with the most beautiful books I've ever seen (the same books we have here, just
                      <i>
                       prettier
                      </i>
                      ), a few banks and pharmacies and tons of
                      <i>
                       really
                      </i>
                      great shopping. In fact, The Marais sort of reminded me a bit of Columbus Avenue between 66th and 72nd here in New York City, or any part of the West Village really, only with even better architecture. Swoon-y architecture. F-bomb architecture.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      And also everybody here is impossibly pretty.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="900" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-xbd6OfoJXro/VTD_d4k-_CI/AAAAAAAAhnk/gRp7uUqFBhw/s1600/IMG_7106.JPG" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="900" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-AwUa5TyVnd4/VTD_dCavyMI/AAAAAAAAhnU/TAyUDZFEPHI/s1600/IMG_7104.JPG" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      Paris and New York seem similar . . . ish. It's a lot like New York there, except
                      <i>
                       way
                      </i>
                      more civilized. The bathrooms! Even the toilets are more civilized in Paris! I'm sure they're covered in just as many germs, but you can't
                      <i>
                       see
                      </i>
                      them the way you can see them in New York. They stare back at you in New York. It's also somehow cheaper in Paris. Muuuch cheaper. (I'm sure the taxes make up for it.) A 50-cent bottle of water in Paris costs $2 here. I stopped at a real estate listing posted to the side of a building to play pretend, and I couldn't believe how affordable the apartments there are compared to here in the city. I'm like, come on now Brandon, why don't either of us speak French. And all the history in the buildings! I'm sure there are a few dives here and there, but every window I peeked in after dusk had these exposed beam ceilings, beautiful moulding details . . . geez.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <img border="0" height="1439" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Nsn9fDxGA68/VS_UbPN2YqI/AAAAAAAAg30/VVAVl5i6lFk/s1600/IMG_4658.jpg" width="900"/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>

It's sort of satisfying to know that you have your own private space bubble in Paris and you can expect to be left alone--you don't have to smile at anybody if you don't want to smile at anybody (ugh the number of times I'm told by randos on the street here that I should smile..)--but that the moment you need it, you can get help past the rude parts and find nothing but kindness underneath.                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">

A friend told me it was kind of like the Upper East Side of Paris, whatever that means. :) Tracing Hemingway's footsteps along the Left Bank was really wonderful. At one point I happened across a music video in-the-making starring a very bouncy pop star, and later on I passed Picasso's old apartment and got to listen in as a local woman translated the French placard by the door for her out-of-towner friend. Thank you kindly, French woman! I can't remember at all what the placard said!
