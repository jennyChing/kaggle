               <h1>
                Commerce secretary: US stands by multistakeholder process to preserve free, open Internet
               </h1>
               <div class="byline">
                <div class="byline">
                 October 14, 2014  | By
                 <a href="/author/dsarkar" rel="author" title="View author profile.">
                  Dibya Sarkar
                 </a>
                </div>

               <div class="body">

                <div class="maincolumn">
                 <p>
                  The United States will resist all efforts to give "any person, entity or nation" control of the Internet rather than the "global multistakeholder communities," said Commerce Secretary Penny Pritzker in a Oct. 13 speech.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "Next week, at the International Telecommunication Union Conference in Korea, we will see proposals to put governments in charge of Internet governance. You can rest assured that the United States will oppose these efforts at every turn," she
                  <a href="http://www.commerce.gov/news/secretary-speeches/2014/10/13/us-secretary-commerce-penny-pritzker-delivers-remarks-internet-co">
                   said in prepared remarks
                  </a>
                  to an Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers, or ICANN, meeting in Los Angeles.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  In recent weeks, U.S. government officials have said they expect several countries to try to manipulate or change the mandate of ITU, which is holding its meeting in Busan, Korea Oct. 18, to give governments greater authority over the Internet. A specialized U.N. agency, ITU allocates global radio spectrum and satellites and develops technical standards for networks and technologies to seamlessly communicate with one another.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  In her speech, Pritzker said that these countries that want governmental control over the Internet tend to "censor content and stifle the free flow of information."
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "We will be clear that these steps are contrary to our belief in the value of free speech  whether on the Internet, in society, in the public sphere  both here at home and abroad. We will remind all players  in each instance  that the multistakeholder model will preserve and protect a strong and resilient Internet," she said.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  In April, the United States announced it would not renew its contract with ICANN, which expires Sept. 30, 2015, to manage Internet addresses. The group is leading a process to oversee the transition to a nongovernmental third party that will oversee it. However, if a new governance model isn't in place a year from now then the U.S. could extend the contract past 2015 and for up to four years.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Pritzker said the Internet has grown and thrived because it has been participatory, adding that the multistakeholder process is a "bedrock principle for Internet governance." She said the Internet is also essential for free expression and exchange of ideas, which is why it should be protected and preserved.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "The world is watching ICANN, and some are waiting for us to fail," she said. "But we cannot  and must not  let that happen. We have to get this transition right. Make no mistake: I stand by ICANN. I am 'all in' when it comes to the global debate over Internet governance. And we will preserve and protect a free and open Internet."
                 </p>
