       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Outdoor DIY Marquee Sign for the Holidays! #damagefreeDIY
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-12-19T02:00:52+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 19, 2013
         </time>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="DIY Outdoor Holiday Marquee Sign" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8277" height="453" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-Outdoor-Holiday-Marquee-1.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Today I, along with the
         <a href="http://bit.ly/AtThePicketFence" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Command Brand
         </a>
         , am sharing a super cool outdoor holiday project! I have been so busy getting the
         <a href="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/2013/12/decorating-for-christmas-home-tour.html" target="_blank">
          INSIDE decorated
         </a>
         , that I must confess I had been a little neglectful of the outside!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Oh I had thrown some lights in the bushes and hung a wreath on the door, but I had still to create that one fun thing to make our yard stand out from the rest of the neighborhood. Until I came up with this idea
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="DIY outdoor holiday marquee sign" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8279" height="1020" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-holiday-lighted-marquee-sign.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-pin-color="white" data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonPin" data-pin-height="28" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.atthepicketfence.com%2F2013%2F12%2Foutdoor-diy-marquee-sign-for-the-holidays.html&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.atthepicketfence.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F12%2FDIY-holiday-lighted-marquee-sign.jpg&amp;description=DIY%20Outdoor%20Marquee%20Sign!%20Includes%20tutorial%20with%20step%20by%20step%20instructions.%20%20Could%20make%20one%20for%20any%20occasion!%20%20">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_white_28.png"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         an outdoor DIY marquee sign! Im going to show you how you can make one for yourself, in an afternoon. Heres what youll need
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: center;">
         <span id="more-8274">
         </span>
         SUPPLIES YOULL NEED:
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Sheet of plywood
         <em>
          (large enough to accommodate the word you choose. Mine was 4 feet X 24 by 1/2 thick)
          <br/>
         </em>
         Large wooden craft letters
         <em>
          (mine were 18 inches tall)
          <br/>
         </em>
         White spray paint and primer combination
         <br/>
         Gold spray paint
         <br/>
         Sets of large bulb outdoor approved lights
         <em>
          (I used 3 sets of 25 for my project)
         </em>
         <br/>
         1 inch wood drill bit
         <br/>
         2-3 small wood stakes
         <em>
          (I found mine in the garage sale sign section of my home improvement store)
          <br/>
         </em>
         <a href="http://www.command.com/wps/portal/3M/en_US/NACommand/Command/Products/Catalog/~/Command-Outdoor-Light-Clips?N=5924736+3294277839+3294529207&amp;rt=rud" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Outdoor Command Brand Light Clips
         </a>
         <br/>
         Nail gun and brads (or small nails and hammer), measuring tape, pencil, shop vac, sawhorses, safety glasses
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="supplies for diy lighted marquee" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8280" height="453" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/supplies-for-diy-lighted-marquee.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: center;">
         HOW TO MAKE YOUR OWN SIGN:
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="DIY outdoor lighted marquee sign tutorial " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8281" height="228" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outdoor-lighted-marquee-tutorial-sign-4.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="683"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         1. Paint plywood sheet with white spray paint and allow to dry thoroughly
         <br/>
         2. Paint letters with gold spray paint and allow to dry thoroughly.
         <br/>
         3. Arrange letters on plywood and measure to make sure they are centered and even.
         <br/>
         4. Nail letters to plywood using nail gun and brads or small finishing nails and hammer.
         <br/>
         5. Measure and mark out where you will drill your holes for lights. I spaced mine 2 1/2-3 inches apart.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outdoor-lighted-marquee-sign-tutorial-5.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY outdoor lighted marquee sign tutorial " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8282" height="228" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outdoor-lighted-marquee-sign-tutorial-5.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="683"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         5. Using firm pressure drill holes on marked locations. Step back and make sure holes are lined up and even.
         <br/>
         6. Use a shop vac, if you have one, while working to keep letters and work space free of sawdust for easy clean up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="DIY outside lighted marquee sign tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8290" height="453" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outside-lighted-marquee-sign-tutorial-7.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         7. Once you have your holes drilled and your work space cleaned up, begin to thread lights through holes. You will need to remove each bulb and push base up through hole, and place bulb back in base. Start at the top and work your way across your sign so that you end with your plug at the base of sign to attach to extension cord.
         <br/>
         8. Once you have all of your lights placed, turn your sign over to arrange cords neatly along back of sign using the Command Outdoor Light Clips.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outdoor-lighted-marquee-sign-tutorial-9.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY outdoor lighted marquee sign tutorial 9" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8283" height="1020" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outdoor-lighted-marquee-sign-tutorial-9.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         The Command OUTDOOR Light Clips work great for outdoor lighting projects because they hold strongly in temperatures from-20F to 125F! They also are provide damage free hanging and remove cleanly when you are finished with them.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="DIY outdoor lighted marquee sign tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8284" height="228" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outdoor-lighted-marquee-sign-tutorial-7.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="683"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         9. Place your Command light clips along the back of your sign, where you will gather up the light cords neatly, and allow the clips to set for one hour. Run clips along the bottom of your sign for excess lights and cords. This will keep them protected and off of the ground. If you need to reposition a clip, its super easy! Just pull the strip straight down and the clip will pop cleanly off. You can use a new strip and reuse the clip in the proper location.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="DIY outside lighted marquee sign tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8285" height="1020" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outside-lighted-marquee-sign-tutorial-10.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         10. For the final step, using your nail gun or finishing nails and a hammer attach stakes to the back of the sign, lining the top up evenly with the sign. Place your sign in your yard and hammer stakes into ground to properly secure your sign. Plug lights into a outdoor approved extension cord and watch your sign glow!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="DIY outdoor holiday lighted marquee sign" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8286" height="453" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outdoor-holiday-lighted-marquee-2.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="DIY outdoor lighted marquee sign" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8293" height="453" src="http://www.atthepicketfence.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/DIY-outdoor-lighted-marquee-sign-.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="680"/>
