    <h1>
     Lucy the Elephant, Americas oldest roadside attraction
    </h1>
    <div class="font_size_small single_date">
     Wednesday, September 10th, 2014. Filed under:
     <a href="http://culturetripper.com/architecture/" rel="category tag">
      Architecture
     </a>
     <a href="http://culturetripper.com/united-states/" rel="category tag">
      United States
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="the_content">
     <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_5449" style="width: 710px">
      <a href="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Lucy800.jpg">
       <img alt="Lucy the Elephant, New Jersey" class="wp-image-5449" height="526" src="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Lucy800.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Lucy the Elephant (1881) is located in Margate, New Jersey.
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Lonely Planet named the Jersey Shore a
      <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/travel-tips-and-articles/top-10-us-travel-destinations-for-2014" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lonelyplanet.com/usa/travel-tips-and-articles/top-10-us-travel-destinations-for-2014', 'Top 10 U.S. destination for 2014']);">
       Top 10 U.S. destination for 2014
      </a>
      . Whats the big attraction? Lucy the Elephant, of course!
     </p>
     <p>
      Located about four miles south of Atlantic City,
      <a href="http://www.lucytheelephant.org/?presets=preset5" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lucytheelephant.org/?presets=preset5', 'Lucy the Elephant ']);" title="Lucy the Elephant website">
       Lucy the Elephant
      </a>
      has stared outto seasince 1881, makingher Americas oldest roadside attraction.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_5462" style="width: 710px">
      <a href="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/LucyCouple800.jpg">
       <img alt="Lucy Couple " class="wp-image-5462" height="524" src="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/LucyCouple800.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       This piece of 19th century advertising architecture is located about 4 miles south of Atlantic City.
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      The six-storey novelty architecture was built by James V. Lafferty, Jr., a Philadelphia land speculator who needed a big advertising tool to lure buyers for his then-desolate dunelots
      <strong>
       .
      </strong>
      Lucy became a popular attraction for Victorian-era holidaymakers on the Jersey shore.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_5464" style="width: 710px">
      <a href="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/LucyLegStaff800.jpg">
       <img alt="Lucy Leg Door" class="wp-image-5464" height="525" src="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/LucyLegStaff800.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Staff and tours enter Lucy through a door in her leg.
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Decades of hurricanes off the Atlantic took their toll, though, and by the 1960s Lucy was facingthe wrecking ball. An expensive renovation in the early 70s brought Lucy back to her original glory and in 1976 she was designated a
      <strong>
       National Historic Landmark
      </strong>
      , ranking her alongside other sites of national significance like the Alamo.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_5465" style="width: 460px">
      <a href="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/LucyHowdah800.jpg">
       <img alt="Lucy Howdah" class="wp-image-5465" height="600" src="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/LucyHowdah800.jpg" width="450"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Lucys howdah offers panoramic views.
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Today visitors can climb Lucysnarrow staircases tomarvel at her ship-like interior and take in 360 views from the open-air howdah on her back. A peekthrough her porthole eyes is not just a window onto the beautiful South Jersey shore,it is a window on Americas past.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_5466" style="width: 710px">
      <a href="http://culturetripper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/LucyView800.jpg">

      </strong>

     <p>
      Lucy the Elephant is open seasonallyfor tours; check her
      <a href="http://www.lucytheelephant.org/?presets=preset5" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lucytheelephant.org/?presets=preset5', 'website']);" title="Lucy the Elephant website">
       website
      </a>
      for details.
     </p>
     <p>
      Margate is a quiet residential beach town about four miles south of Atlantic City, aneasy drive down Atlantic Avenue. If you dont have your own vehicle, I can recommendhiring a car and driverfrom
      <a href="http://www.ceasarlimousineinc.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ceasarlimousineinc.com/', 'Ceasar Limousine']);" title="Ceasar Limousine, New Jersey">
       Ceasar Limousine
      </a>
      <strong>
      </strong>
      (609-383-6069). Its great value for the money, especially if you have a group.
     </p>
     <p>
      From the Victorian architecture of Cape May to Wildwoods
      <a href="http://www.doowopusa.org/district/index.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.doowopusa.org/district/index.html', 'Doo Wop motel strip']);" title="Doo Wop architecture, Wildwood, NJ">
       Doo Wop motel strip
      </a>
      ,Atlantic City is a great base for exploring the history, architecture and beaches of southern New Jersey. For more fun things to do inand around Atlantic City, see
      <a href="http://www.atlanticcitynj.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.atlanticcitynj.com/', 'Visit AC']);" title="Visit Atlantic City tourism">
       Visit AC
      </a>
      .
