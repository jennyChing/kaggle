           3 Year Old Made Matcha Tiramisu
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry">
          <p class="post_meta">
           Posted on
           <strong class="published">
            October 14, 2012
           </strong>
           by
           <strong class="author">
            <a href="http://betterteas.com/author/osdperry/" rel="author" title="Posts by Peter">

          <div class="pd-rating" id="pd_rating_holder_6948208_post_2805">
          </div>
          <p>
           <br/>
           http://www.youtube.com/watch?feature=player_embedded&amp;v=rm9ouSapKKs
          </p>
          <p>
           I have been itching to make Matcha Tiramisu for some time. I am not sure if I ever had it, but my liking of Tiramisu makes me think this could be quite nice. Perhaps a more healthy version of Tiramisu
           <img alt=";)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://betterteas.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_wink.gif"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I want to keep it simple, as I am going to try making Tiramisu with my near 3 year old daughter. She is not one with patience! Fortunately I found the Home Baking blog with a wonderfully simply, no cooking recipe.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://betterteas.com/matcha-tiramisu/">
            See our Matcha Tiramisu recipe.
           </a>
          </p>
          <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
          </div>
          <p>
           Matcha is a green tea powder from Japan. For Matcha afficionado, there are 2 grades  food grade, which is often more like a powdered sencha, and a tea ceremony grade, which is made from the same premium tea leaves as gyokuro, called Tencha.
          </p>
          <p>
           For my own learning, I have tried a lot of matcha lately. The very common Matcha sold in little green can from Ujinotsuyu is unfortunately sand like when compared to any half decent matcha (more of this on another blog post later).
          </p>
          <p>
           So go and get some decent matcha, food grade is good enough for food and it is pretty expensive already. A little goes a long way, and you can make, besides this too-easy-too-evil Matcha Tiramisu, you can also try Matcha Latte, Matcha pancake and of course Matcha icecream
           <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://betterteas.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
           <br/>

           </a>
          </p>
