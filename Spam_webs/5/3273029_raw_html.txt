      <p>
       Optimize your time with these Study Tips for Final Exams
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       12. Make Flash Cards
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="bromate" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1919" height="390" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/bromate.png" width="840"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Flash cards are one of the best study tools you can use. Making flash cards requires integrating and summarizing content which in and of itself is a power study technique. They also engage all learning styles in their creation and execution. Visual learners will engage with the writing on the cards, auditory learners will engage by saying and repeating the content aloud, and kinesthetic learners will engage with the physical cards themselves.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Flash cards can be thrown in your bag for review while sitting on the toilet, waiting for a bus, eating lunch, watching TV, waiting for paint to dry, waiting for the Giants to win another Super Bowl you get the idea.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       <span id="more-1918">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Heck you could even make some copies and
       <a href="https://www.tilt.com/campaigns/new?sell=1&amp;utm_source=crushexams&amp;utm_campaign=blog">
        sell them on Tilt
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Stop reading (not really) and make some cards!
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       11. Make a schedule
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="all-the-things-meme-generator-schedule-all-the-things-fa8482" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1930" height="383" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/all-the-things-meme-generator-schedule-all-the-things-fa8482.jpg" width="510"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Even if you are not a list person,
       <a href="http://sass.queensu.ca/learningstrategies/decemberapril-exam-study-schedule/">
        studies have shown
       </a>
       that students that make a schedule are much more likely to succeed than those who dont.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       You dont need anything crazy here, just something to keep you accountable. Break down all of your classes and give each of them time on a calendar. By seeing it all laid out, your stress levels will start to come down immediately as your brain will have some reassurance that all subjects will at least get looked at.
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       10. Get an accountabilibuddy
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="accountabilibuddy" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1920" height="459" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/accountabilibuddy.png" width="612"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       An accountabilibuddy is someone who will hold you accountable to studying and doing well on your exams. Set up a system whereby you check in with each other and then grant permission for public shamings if one of you hasnt kept the agreement you made. In fact, you can even tilt a digital procrastination jar, whereby every time you dont have a good excuse for not studying and your accountabilibuddy catches you, you must add $5 to the tilt.
      </p>
      <p>
       You can feel good donating the tens of thousands of dollars to charity when exams are done or use it as a slush fund to buy some ice cold beverages.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you dont have an accountabilibuddy just ask me, I would be happy to help. You can YO me at kentf.
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       9. Known Unknowns
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="RUMSFELD" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1921" height="288" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/rumsfeld_1404848c.jpg" width="460"/>
      </p>
      <p class="wrapped">
       Donald Rumsfeld once said, There are known unknowns and then there are unknown unknowns. I think he was talking about studying for December finals here. The reason is that too many students focus on the wrong things! You want to focus on the known unknowns in your course. What are the concepts that you had the most trouble with? Are there entire chapters that dont make sense? Start there. Too often we look for some quick wins and start with material we know. Not smart. You have limited time, focus on those known unknowns. How many times did I say known unknowns in that paragraph? Doesnt matter, its an unknown unknown. Not meant to be known. Stop trying to figure this out! You have to study.
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       8. PMA, habits, and goals
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="pma" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1922" height="554" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/pma.png" width="840"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       PMA  or positive mental attitude  isnt just for new age cranks: the science behind it is sound. When you approach a problem with an attitude of success, you are more likely to succeed. Set a goal for yourself and activate your PMA while studying. This will be difficult as there are exactly 4,214,820 other things you would rather be doing, but the cold hard truth is that those things will have to wait.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       It takes about 30 days to form a habit, but you dont have 30 days. However, you can use little, mental hacks to trick your brain.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       1) Place study material in the fridge, bathroom, next to your alarm clock, on your keyboard anywhere you will randomly stumble upon it during the day. Seeing the material will act as visual cue and trigger your brain that you should be studying.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       2) Get all military like with your day. Create a strict, daily routine while you are studying. Eat the same breakfast, wake up at the same time, take breaks at the same time, etc. Reduce the number of decisions you need to make in a day so that the only important decision  should I study right now?  is an emphatic Yes!
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       7. Meditate
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="funny-meditating-dog4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1923" height="400" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/funny-meditating-dog4.jpg" width="340"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       We will do a more thorough post on meditation in coming months, but for now know this. Meditation is a powerful, secular way to reduce stress, improve memory and increase happiness in your life. Why wouldnt you give it a shot?
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       There are so many different ways to go about this that we will keep it simple today. Use an app like
       <a href="https://www.headspace.com/" target="_blank" title="">
        Headspace
       </a>
       or
       <a href="http://calm.com" target="_blank" title="">
        Calm.com
       </a>
       and add 30 minutes of meditation to your daily routine developed during step 8. 15 minutes in the morning and 15 minutes before bed is the perfect bookend to a productive day of studying.
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       6. Get out of the house
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="tumblr_m6oad71lAk1qlsnnh" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1924" height="282" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/tumblr_m6oad71lAk1qlsnnh.gif" width="500"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Students who study in groups or at the library do better than those who dont. Its that simple. Try and push yourself to get out of the house and study with others around you. Their intensity and presence will inspire you to keep going.
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       5. Sleep
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="1292439998" class="aligncenter wp-image-1925" height="365" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/1292439998.jpg" width="487"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Brain scientists are pretty sure that one of the reasons we sleep is to form and organize long term memories. Dont throw away a perfect study day just because you wanted to watch Sons of Anarchy or Girls. Motorcycles and naked people eating birthday cake can wait a few weeks. Get 7-8.5 hours of sleep to ensure maximum memory benefits.
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       4. Dont binge drink before exams
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="giphy" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1926" height="250" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/giphy.gif" width="500"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Alcohol affects your ability to form memories. You wouldnt go into an exam with a bunch of dull pencils and no pencil sharpener right? So why would you dull your senses while studying? There will be plenty of time to kill brain cells after you are done: assuming that you are of legal age in your state / province of course.
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       3. Find your learning style
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="communitystudy" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1927" height="281" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/communitystudy.gif" width="500"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       We briefly discussed learning styles earlier, but lets dive a little deeper. They typically break down into three groups:
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       1) Visual learners
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       2) Auditory learners
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       3) Kinesthetic learners
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Dont know your learning style? Find out here:
       <a href="http://www.educationplanner.org/students/self-assessments/learning-styles-quiz.shtml">
        http://www.educationplanner.org/students/self-assessments/learning-styles-quiz.shtml
       </a>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Depending on which group you fall into, here are a few study tips.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       For visual learners:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Write out your notes. Then write them again. And again. And again. Repetition works for you.
       </li>
       <li>
        Use flash cards, see #12
       </li>
       <li>
        Color code your notes with post-its, highlighers etc.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p dir="ltr">
       For auditory learners:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Try and get your hands on the audio of the most confusing lectures (ask the kids at the front with the recording device, or the NSA). Listen to them while walking around campus or at the gym.
       </li>
       <li>
        Read your notes out loud as often as possible
       </li>
       <li>
        Teach someone else your material. Call your family, your GF/BF/BFF, pooch, your accountabilibuddy and teach them the concepts you are having the most trouble with. Teaching someone else will give you a new perspective.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       For kinesthetic learnings
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Get outside as much as possible. Reward yourself with frequent walks, bike rides, or hikes. Anything to keep you active while still leaving enough time to study.
       </li>
       <li>
        Make flash cards.
       </li>
       <li>
        Make posters of exam material and plaster your walls with them.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2 class="">
       2. Write as many practice exams as possible
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="draw a sketch of the situation dr heckle funny wtf test answers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1928" height="450" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/draw-a-sketch-of-the-situation-dr-heckle-funny-wtf-test-answers.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Practice exams are pure gold when studying, especially if the prof has been teaching the class for a long time. Follow these steps once you get your hands on them.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       1) Dont take any practice tests until you have studied for at least 1 day.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       2) Take an older test first and save the newer tests for later.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       3) Focus your studies on the areas you had the most trouble with. Write out a list of the concepts that didnt stick the first time round. Then, as you begin to understand them, you will have the satisfaction of crossing them off.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       4) 2 days before your final, take the most recent and relevant practice exams and treat them like a real exam. Block yourself out, set a timer, dont cheat.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       5) Spend the last day reviewing where you had trouble in your real, fake exam
      </p>
      <h2 class="">
       1. Go to all review classes with the Professor or TA
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="tumblr_mlhf2ytmh61snjoxto1_500" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1929" height="319" src="http://blog.tilt.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/tumblr_mlhf2ytmh61snjoxto1_500.jpg" width="480"/>
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Really? No thank you! Those sessions are for nerds and they suck! WRONG. If coordinated, they can be the most effective study aid. You want to use this time to get the professor or TA to tell you exactly what you should and shouldnt be studying! Sometimes, theyll even reward you with bonus points or the answer(s) to a question or two.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Go through your notes and look for the most complex concepts. Then, during the review, make sure you ask the Prof or TA to work through the example. If they think it will confuse the class or that it requires too much setup, chances are they will give you a sly remark like,
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       I have a feeling you wont need to know this kind of question or Maybe we should do another question first. You now know this question isnt on the exam. Wash, rinse, and repeat.
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Get your friends to do the same, so that by the end of the session you will have a pretty good idea by process of elimination what will not be on the exam.
      </p>
      <hr/>
      <p dir="ltr">
       I would wish you luck, but with these stellar tips in hand, who needs luck right 
      </p>
      <p dir="ltr">
       Godspeed and goodnight,
      </p>
