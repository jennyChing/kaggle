            <div class="content">
             <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
             <p>
              <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-26179" height="250" src="http://www.yummly.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/grocery-store-thoughts-on-films.jpg" title="5 Facts the Food Industry Doesnt Want You to Know" width="250"/>
              If youve seen any documentaries about food 
              <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/63283/super-size-me" target="_blank">
               Super Size Me
              </a>
              and
              <a href="http://www.foodincmovie.com/" target="_blank">
               Food, Inc
              </a>
              among them  youre probably aware that the food world can sometimes be a scary place. Unsavory practices and unhygienic conditions are among a few of the unsettling realities of mass-produced food, but it doesnt end there. Here are 5 facts about the food industry that you should probably be aware of  not to scare you away from eating whatever you choose to eat, but to make your choices more informed!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-26184" height="250" src="http://www.yummly.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/Ground-Beef-times-union.jpg" title="5 Facts the Food Industry Doesnt Want You to Know" width="250"/>
               1. Not all ingredients appear on labels
              </strong>
              .
              <br/>
              How do
              <a href="http://www.yummly.com/dish/blog/2011/07/the-healthiest-fast-food-meals-at-the-drive-thru/" target="_blank">
               fast food
              </a>
              burger companies make sure their burgers are (mostly) e.coli free? They put their ground beef through a cleaning process done with
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia" target="_blank">
               ammonia
              </a>
              ! Its safe according to the USDA, but
              <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2009/12/31/us/31meat.html?pagewanted=all">
               incidents of meat infected with salmonella and e.coli
              </a>
              have brought the effectiveness of ammonia cleaning into question  not to mention the odor and taste of ammonia often present in freshly delivered batches of
              <a href="http://www.yummly.com/recipes/ground-beef" target="_blank">
               ground beef
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              Thats just one example of ingredients present in the food that dont appear anywhere on the label. Considering ingredient lists for most mass-produced food is already confounding, its mind-boggling to think what else might be in there.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-26191" height="250" src="http://www.yummly.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/end-aisle-display-creative-mag.jpg" title="5 Facts the Food Industry Doesnt Want You to Know" width="250"/>
               2. Visible shelf space is paid advertising
              </strong>
              <br/>
              Popular food manufacturers buy the most visible shelf spaces in order to position their products more advantageously. End-aisle displays and the most easily-reachable, frontally located shelf spaces are just another form of paid advertising. If you want to buy the less rigorously advertised, quality products, you can usually find them in the harder to reach locations with decreased visibility.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-26197" height="250" src="http://www.yummly.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/food-dyes-dr-pinna.jpg" title="5 Facts the Food Industry Doesnt Want You to Know" width="250"/>
               3. Ingredients dont have to be explained.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              What is 
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimson" target="_blank">
               Natural red 4
              </a>
              ? Its found in juice, ice cream, candies, and lipstick, so its probably some kind of berry or something right? Actually, its a
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carmine" target="_blank">
               red pigment
              </a>
              derived from
              <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cochineal" target="_blank">
               Cochineals
              </a>
               insects native to subtropical South America! If youre worried, calm down  since 2006, its been required for labels to specifically list carmine as an ingredient due to
              <a href="http://www.snopes.com/food/ingredient/bugjuice.asp" target="_blank">
               severe allergic reactions
              </a>
              that arose as a result of the bugs consumption.
             </p>
             <p>
              However, this issue illustrates a greater problem: think of the tons of inscrutable ingredients in foods that are never explained. Another example is cellulose. Its popping up in all kinds of foods, from ice cream to muffins  but what is it, actually?
              <a href="http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748703834804576300991196803916.html" target="_blank">
               Wood pulp.
              </a>
              The same pulp, in fact, that is used to make newspapers and other non-food items.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-26203" height="250" src="http://www.yummly.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/fruit-leather-weelicious.jpg" title="5 Facts the Food Industry Doesnt Want You to Know" width="250"/>
               4. Processing foods greatly reduces nutritional value
              </strong>
              <br/>
              Companies stretch their resources as thin as possible to increase profit margins. Why sell whole
              <a href="http://www.yummly.com/recipes/apple" target="_blank">
               apples
              </a>
              when you can sell 10x as many apple-flavored
              <a href="http://www.yummly.com/recipes/fruit" target="_blank">
               fruit
              </a>
              leathers? Or gallons of
              <a href="http://www.yummly.com/recipes/apple-juice" target="_blank">
               apple juice
              </a>
              ? This makes sense from a business perspective, but the foods nutritional value takes a major hit. The more food is processed, the less nutritional value it retains, and the more likely it is that the food contains non-nutritive additives.
             </p>
             <p>
              For example, most
              <a href="http://www.yummly.com/recipes/orange-juice" target="_blank">
               orange juice
              </a>
              isnt produced fresh squeezed the way you might expect. To preserve orange juice for year-round consumption, its stored in vats without oxygen  a process which often causes the juice to lose its flavor. In order to 
              <a href="http://yalepress.typepad.com/squeezed/science/" target="_blank">
               re-flavor
              </a>
               the juice, they add citrus-fragrances and chemicals to it, similar to those found in perfumes.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-26206" height="250" src="http://www.yummly.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/health-claims-grit.jpg" title="5 Facts the Food Industry Doesnt Want You to Know" width="250"/>
               5. Health claims are often unfounded
              </strong>
              <br/>
              When
              <a href="http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=5024420" target="_blank">
               Quaker Oats came to the FDA in the 90s
              </a>
              to get permission to print heart-healthy claims on their packaging, it ushered in a time of health advertising strongly backed by scientific evidence. As more companies jumped on the health bandwagon, the FDAs rules changed to allow 
              <a href="http://www.fda.gov/food/labelingnutrition/labelclaims/qualifiedhealthclaims/default.htm" target="_blank">
               qualified health claims
              </a>
                health claims with an asterisk. Companies were then allowed to make health claims like helps maintain a healthy heart with little or no scientific backing.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>

    function yummly_load_more_labs(post_type,skip,limit){

        var data = {
            "action":"yummly_get_labs_posts",
            "post_type":post_type,
            "skip":skip,
            "limit":limit
         };

        $.post(ajaxurl,data,function(result){
            var $load_more = $("#load-more");
            $load_more.toggleClass("loading");
            var result = JSON.parse(result);
            var handlebars_template;

            //console.log(result);
            /*
            *
            * when you get results back you need to
            * compare the result.count value
            * and the length of a $("WHATEVER").length of the items
            * already in the list to see if it is time to hide the
            * load more
            *
            */

            handlebars_template = post_type == "labs" ? labs_template : post_template;

            var len = result.posts.length;
            for (var i = 0; i &lt; len; i++) {
                var html = handlebars_template(result.posts[i]);
                $("#post-list").append(html);
            }

            var count;
            if(post_type == "post"){

            }else{
              count = $("#post-list &gt; li").length;
            }
            if($(".labs").length &gt; 0){
                count += 1;
            }
            if(count &gt;= result.total){
                $load_more.hide();
            }

        });

          "skip":skip,
          "limit":limit,

      if(taxonomy.hasOwnProperty("type")){

        data.taxonomy_slug = taxonomy.slug;
      }
      //console.log(data);
      //console.log("Outgoing data____________________");
      //console.log(data);

        // console.log(result);
        var result = JSON.parse(result);
        var len = result.posts.length;
        //console.log("Container____________________");
        // console.log($container.html());
        $container.html(" ");
        $('#load-more-posts').toggleClass("loading");

        for (var i = 0; i &lt; len; i++) {
          var html = handlebars_template(result.posts[i]);
          $("#load-more-container").append(html);
          $container.append(html);
