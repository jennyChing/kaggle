       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Tooty Fruity: Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato for #IceCreamWeek
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-08-20T07:00:27+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          August 20, 2014
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.crumbblog.com/author/superdisco" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Isabelle Boucher
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.crumbblog.com/2014/08/raspberry-ripple-buttermilk-gelato.html#comments">
           15 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Welcome to Day 3 of Ice Cream Week 2014! This year, the event is hosted by Kim of
         <a href="http://www.cravingsofalunatic.com/">
          Cravings of a Lunatic
         </a>
         and Susan of
         <a href="http://girlinthelittleredkitchen.com/">
          The Girl in the Little Red Kitchen
         </a>
         , and weve teamed up with 25 amazing bloggers to treat you to tons of amazing ice cream recipes. Ice Cream Week is shaping up to be the best week ever!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/polkaroo/14789160577" title="Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato by Isabelle Boucher, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato" class="aligncenter" height="640" src="https://farm4.staticflickr.com/3888/14789160577_70bf5b4dcb_z.jpg" width="427"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Most of you know Ill eat ice cream anytime, even in the middle of a blizzard, but it really does taste best during those unbearable dog days of summer. The kinds of dog days that this summer has totally failed to deliver, making it one of the crappiest summers on record, at least from an ice cream eating perspective.
         <span id="more-6001">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its been too cool, too rainy, too busy, too everything well, except for too hot and too lazy. Sigh.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/polkaroo/14789021890" title="Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato by Isabelle Boucher, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato" class="aligncenter" height="333" src="https://farm4.staticflickr.com/3919/14789021890_c155ec451f.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         On the bright side, this cool, wet, overcast summer has done wonders for my backyard raspberry patch, which delivered a bumper crop of sweet, plumpruby red berries most of which never actually made their way back to the kitchen because a certain someone just couldnt stop snacking on them by the handful. (What can I say? Im my mothers daughter, and the woman never meta raspberry bush she couldnt pick clean in 5 minutes flat.)
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/polkaroo/14972609361" title="Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato by Isabelle Boucher, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato" class="aligncenter" height="640" src="https://farm4.staticflickr.com/3848/14972609361_b9e82bb393_z.jpg" width="427"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Lucky for you guys, I did manage to save just enough to make the intensely redraspberry puree that windsits way through this Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato.
        </p>
        <p>
         This delightful gelato is as tasty as it is pretty the creamy off-white gelato base is subtly tangy and not too sweet, while the swirling scarlet ribbons of fresh raspberry are sweet-tart and intensely fruity. The combination is downrightrefreshing, especially since the lack of egg yolks in the base means the brightflavours of tangy buttermilk and fresh berries shine through perfectly unmuddled.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/polkaroo/14972614531" title="Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato by Isabelle Boucher, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Raspberry Ripple Buttermilk Gelato" class="aligncenter" height="333" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5591/14972614531_2c53ca2048.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It might just be my favourite non-chocolate ice cream flavour ever. Its honestly that good.
        </p>
        <p>
         And yes, you can totally make it with regular storebought raspberries if you dont have a thriving patch of raspberry bushes right outside your back door. Which is good, because my backyard berries are all gone for the season, and I have zero intentions of waiting a whole freakin year for another helping of this gelato.
        </p>
        <p>

            Cook time:
            <time datetime="PT10M" itemprop="cookTime">
             10 mins
            </time>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSHead">
            Total time:
            <time datetime="PT6H10M" itemprop="totalTime">
             6 hours 10 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSHead">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            4
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          Ribbons of ruby red raspberry swirl their way through a creamy off-white buttermilk gelato base for an ice creamy treat that's just as tasty as it is pretty.
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Lemon-Buttermilk Gelato:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cups heavy cream, divided
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 tbsp corn starch
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup buttermilk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup lemon juice
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tbsp lemon zest
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Pinch salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tbsp limoncello (optional)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Raspberry Ripple Sauce:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cups fresh raspberries
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tbsp vodka or raspberry liqueur
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Prepare the Gelato Base:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a small saucepan set over medium-high heat, heat 1 cup cream and sugar until wisps of steam start to rise from the surface.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a small mixing bowl, whisk together the cornstarch with the remaining  cup cream. Whisk into the hot cream, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 5 to 6 minutes, whisking constantly, or until the mixture thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Remove from heat and immediately stir in the buttermilk, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt and limoncello (if using).
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Transfer the custard into a mixing bowl and cover with plastic wrap, resting the wrap directly on the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Move to the refrigerator and chill for at least 2 hours (or preferably overnight).
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Make the Raspberry Swirl:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In small saucepan set over medium-high heat, stir together raspberries and sugar. Bring to a simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 10 minutes or until the raspberries break down and thicken. Strain through fine mesh sieve to remove the seeds. Stir in the vodka, then cover and chill until ready to assemble the gelato.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Freeze and Assemble the Gelato:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Using an ice cream maker, freeze the chilled custard as per the manufacturer's directions.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spoon alternating layers of ice cream and raspberry sauce into a freezer-safe container, then run a skewer or butter knife through the mixture to create a swirl. Transfer to the freezer to chill until firm but scoopable, about 4 hours.
           </li>
          </ol>

              Thanks for catching that, Janet. The water was a holdover from an earlier version, but I realised I could leave it out by dissolving the cornstarch in the cream instead.
              <br/>
              Its hard to troubleshoot just based on your description, but it sounds like you may have churned your ice cream a little too long. I usually find its best to stop as soon as the custard reaches the consistency of thick soft-serve, at which point you should scoop it into a container and put it in the freezer to finish firming up. (At least, this is true for both my machines  a Donvier hand-crank and a Cuisinart continuous churn. Your model might be different.)
              <br/>
              You didnt mention substitutions, so this is probably less likely of an explanation, but other factors that can affect the texture of the final product include reducing the sugar content, replacing the sugar with another sweetener, and/or reducing the fat content by using less cream or substituting a lower-fat cream. This recipe doesnt include any egg yolks or other emulsifiers to compensate, so those small changes will have a much bigger impact than they would in an American style cooked-custard type ice cream.
