       <article class="entry post clearfix">
        <h1 class="main_title">
         Great Lives Are Sitcoms
        </h1>
        <p class="meta-info">
         Posted  by
         <a href="http://howtosplitanatom.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by Steve Spalding">
          Steve Spalding
         </a>
         in
         <a href="http://howtosplitanatom.com/category/news/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Featured">
          Featured
         </a>
         |
         <a href="http://howtosplitanatom.com/news/great-lives-are-sitcoms/#comments" title="Comment on Great Lives Are Sitcoms">
         </a>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <a href="http://sbspalding.disqus.com/?url=http://howtosplitanatom.com/news/great-lives-are-sitcoms/">
          View comments
         </a>
        </noscript>
        <a class="dsq-comment-count" href="http://howtosplitanatom.com/news/great-lives-are-sitcoms/#disqus_thread" wpid="2221">
         Comments
        </a>
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         <img align="left" alt="I Love Lucy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2223" height="144" src="http://howtosplitanatom.com/wp-content/uploads/ilovelucytitlescreen.jpg" title="ilovelucytitlescreen" width="192"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets go back, way back and peer through the primordial ooze into the early 90s.
        </p>
        <p>
         Were going in search of one of the most closely guarded secrets of content production, a secret locked deep in the beating heart of
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TV_Land">
          TV Lands
         </a>
         evening lineup.
         <br/>
         <span id="more-2221">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ask yourself 
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <p>
           Why did you watch insipid, preachy pap like Full House and Family Matters and love every second of it?
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p>
           Why can you remember who said More Power! and 
           <em>
            Did
           </em>
           I do that? but cant even keep your kids birthdays straight?
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p>
           Why did some small part of you really want to ask Al Bundy for relationship advice or Tim The Toolman Taylor how to soup up your riding mower?
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Its because ladies and germs, Hollywood knows something that most modern content producers have forgotten  the power of a good story and how critical it is to own it.
        </p>
        <p>
         What do
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=afR5J7eskno">
          Bill Gates
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.cher.com/">
          Cher
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.43folders.com/">
          Merlin Mann
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.tomcruiseisnuts.com/">
          Tom Cruise
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://tastyblogsnack.com/">
          iJustine
         </a>
         all have in common? They all tell a great story. They live their careers like a treatment to a primetime sitcom. We want to watch them exist because in many ways they exist to have us watch them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Look at the celebrities in whatever microcosm you exist in and Ill bet youll find the same thing. They act out their careers like a stage performance, and feed their audiences exactly what their audiences want to be fed. Whether you like them, love them or hate them, its all a part of the same vaudeville act. You can
         <em>
          count
         </em>
         on them to give you just the right punch line and no matter how hard you cry out for them to change their wicked ways, in the back of your mind you know that if they did  you wouldnt
         <em>
          care
         </em>
         anymore.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          People like people who they can sum up on the back of a business card.
         </strong>
         This isnt to say that great content producers lack depth, but when you look at the myths of great leaders, celebrities, and just about anyone known on a wide scale youll find that their public personas consist of instance after instance of the same common threads, repackaged and resold for the entirety of their careers. Their personalities are bullet-points carved into concrete and are just as difficult to tip over.
        </p>
        <p>
         What does that mean for you?
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether you are
         <a href="http://howtosplitanatom.com/news/branding-slogans-and-getting-your-point-across/">
          branding your product
         </a>
         or your career, ask yourself what your life would look like on the back of a business card. What four or five points run true whether you looked at them now or six years from now? In fact, dont just think about it, go out and get an index card and see what you come up with.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once you find out what your show is about, start building the episodes. Push those points out into the world and make sure that everything you do has those fundamentals at its core. Cast your characters, find your setting and discover what moral you want people to take away from every 23 minute block.
        </p>
        <p>
         I still cant guarantee anyone will watch, but you have a much better chance than you did at the reality TV table for one.
        </p>
        <p>
         (
         <a href="http://howtosplitanatom.com/the-lab/">
          Forum
         </a>
         )
        </p>
