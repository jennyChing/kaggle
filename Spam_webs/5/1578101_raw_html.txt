       <h1>
        Breast Augmentation Procedure, Surgery Risks, and Safety
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="attribution">
         Posted by admin2 on
         <span class="date">
          08/18/14
         </span>
        </span>
        |
        <a class="comments" href="http://www.healthnwellnessblog.com/breast-augmentation-surgery-procedure-risks#exp-comments">
         0 Comments
        </a>
        |
        <span class="tags">
         Tags:

          treatment
         </a>

         <a href="http://www.healthnwellnessblog.com/tag/surgery">

         <a href="http://www.healthnwellnessblog.com/tag/breast-augmentation">
          breast augmentation
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.healthnwellnessblog.com/tag/breasts">
          breasts

         <a href="http://www.healthnwellnessblog.com/tag/cosmetic-surgery">

       <div class="bodycopy">
        <p>
         <a href="http://bayareabreastaugmentation.org/best-plastic-surgeons-san-francisco-ca/">
          <img alt="San Francisco Breast Augmentation " src="/files/San-Francisco-Breast-Augmentation.jpg" style="width: 250px; height: 208px; float: right; margin: 6px;"/>
         </a>
         Breast augmentation or breast enhancement is a popular surgical procedure among women of all ages. Apparently, there are many reasons for augmentation and even the end results vary on the basis of individual requirements. This procedure aims at improving the overall appearance of ones personality.
        </p>
        <p>
         The biggest concern of women after pregnancy is the stretched and expanded breasts tissues that do not look appealing. This is because of the hormonal and physical changes that take place in a womans body during pregnancy and lactation. Thus, breast augmentation is an excellent method to restore the natural structure of a womans breasts after delivering a child.
        </p>
        <p>
         Breast augmentation can help you restore your breasts tissues to their former selves. Believe it or not, it is a very effective procedure that helps regain youthfulness and attractiveness. However, it is imperative to choose the right breast implant according to the requirement if the patient to ensure natural outcomes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether you're seeking a
         <a href="http://bayareabreastaugmentation.org/">
          Bay Area breast augmentation surgeon
         </a>
         or just looking for information, below we share information about the breast augmentation procedure, surgery risks, and overall safety concerns among women.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Prenatal Breast Augmentation Surgery
        </h2>
        <p>
         As a matter of fact, there is also a huge group of women who prefer getting breast enhancement treatments even before they get pregnant. One of the simplest reasons for such decisions is the desire to have vivacious and attractive frame. While some women see unwanted changes after pregnancy, many are unhappy with their natural structure. They desire to have fuller breasts to get the perfect frame. The procedure of Breast augmentation has increasingly become popular among women looking for more enhanced options. It is a very effective method to gain fullness giving incredibly high rate of satisfaction.
        </p>
        <h3>
         What is the breast augmentation procedure?
        </h3>
        <p>
         The procedure for breast augmentation is simple and quick. A tiny inframammary, also known as IMF incision is made under both the breasts to make a deep pocket, which is about width of a quarter. This incision is very small and heals easily. Almost all the scars made on the skin heal well and fade away easily.
        </p>
        <p>
         The entire breast augmentation procedure last between 45 minutes to one hour. The patient is allowed to return home on the very same day. In less than three days post-surgery, you can start participating in majority of daily routine activities such as gardening, bathing, jogging, etc. However, it is always advisable to avoid heavy activities to prevent post-surgery complications.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Where is the incision made?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Typically, just an inch long incision is used to place the breast implants and it is made at the lower surface of the breasts for minimum visibility. Also, this incision heals quickly and fades away within couple of weeks time due to its tiny size. It also ensures that the patients do not have to suffer from other related problems such as capsular contraction or nipple sensitivity problems. The procedure of breast augmentation various per the surgeon performing the surgery. Some surgeons prefer to use different incisions compared to others. For instance, a surgeon who performs
         <a href="http://bayareabreastaugmentation.org/best-plastic-surgeons-sacramento-ca/">
          breast augmentation in Sacramento
         </a>
         might prefer to make the incision under the breast, while another surgeon for
         <a href="http://bayareabreastaugmentation.org/best-plastic-surgeons-san-jose-ca/">
          breast augmentation in San Jose
         </a>
         might opt for an alternative placement. Be sure to inquire with you breast augmentation surgeon about these details to get everything out on the table.
        </p>
        <h3>
         How to choose between silicone gel and saline breast implants?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Although, both the materials are tested and approved by FDA, most of the patients prefer silicone gel based implants for more natural appearance and durability. Silicone gel implants give more natural touch as well and show visible wrinkling that is sometimes seen in patients with very thin tissues. However, both the variety of implants is positioned below the muscle. If you wish to know more about them, then there are various informative websites such as
         <a href="http://plasticsurgery.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          PlasticSurgery.org
         </a>
         that can provide typical scientific information on both types of implants.
        </p>
        <h3>
         What are the risks associated with breast augmentation?
        </h3>
        <p>
         As far as the risks are concerned, about 1% of the total breast augmentation surgeries have been reported so far. A lot of women, around the world have been able to live a normal and confident life post breast augmentation surgery. Even if the patient has to suffer from postoperative hematoma, early recognition and timely treatment can fix the problem in a smooth manner.
        </p>
