    <section class="content fixed-content">
     <div class="row">
      <div class="small-12 medium-12 large-8 columns">
       <p>
        A couple of weeks ago, our columnist
        <strong>
         Susana Pereira
        </strong>
        <a href="http://visualoop.com/8546/visualizing-meteorites-fall" target="_blank" title="Visualizing meteorites fall">
         talked about two recent data visualizations projects
        </a>
        depicting the known/observed meteorites impacts on Earth. Those projects were among the entries submitted to the
        <a href="http://visualizing.org/contests/visualizing-meteorites" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Visualizing Meteorites Challenge
        </a>
        , promoted by
        <a href="http://visualizing.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Visualizing.org
        </a>
         and the winners were announced yesterday (11/06).
       </p>
       <div class="caption-image aligncenter">
        <a class="fancybox" href="http://visualizing.org/galleries/visualizing-meteorites" rel="nofollow">
         <img alt="Partial screen capture of the Visualizing Meteorites Challenge Winners gallery" class="size-large wp-image-9107" height="436" src="http://visualoop.com/media/2013/06/Visualizing-Meteorites-Challenge-Winners-680x490.png" width="750"/>
        </a>
        <div class="asset-source clearfix" id="figcaption_attachment_9107">
         <div class="img-title left">
          (image: Visualizing Meteorites Challenge Winners | Visualizing;or)
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        The first prize went to
        <a href="http://visualizing.org/users/roxana-torre" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Roxana Torre
        </a>
         Remember her? She presented
        <a href="http://visualoop.com/3152/how-livable-are-cities-by-roxana-torre" target="_blank" title="How livable are cities? by Roxana Torre">
         here on Visual Loop
        </a>
        another one of her visualization projects, not so long ago -, with her project
        <a href="http://visualizing.org/full-screen/56186" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Macrometeorites
        </a>
        .
        <strong>
         Carlo Zaponi
        </strong>
        s Bolides and
        <strong>
         Alexandra Muresan
        </strong>
        s Facts about Meteorites, Illustrated by Meteorites were the runner-ups . Roxana, Carlo, and Alexandra will each receive $3750, $500, and $250 respectively for their projects.
       </p>
       <p>
        Other projects worth mentioning are the ones developed by
        <strong>
         Christian Gross
        </strong>
        ,
        <strong>
         Kim Albrecht
        </strong>
        ,
        <strong>
         Tiffany Farrant-Gonzalez
        </strong>
        ,
        <strong>
         Sebastian Sadowski
        </strong>
        and
        <strong>
         Jonathan Hull
        </strong>
        , recognized by the jury as honorable mentions for visual appeal, creativity, or the offering of a unique perspective.
       </p>
       <p>
        All the 32 entries are available on
        <a href="http://visualizing.org/galleries/visualizing-meteorites" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Visualizing.orgs gallery
        </a>
        , but we picked a few of them to share with you:
       </p>
       <h3>
        Macrometeorites | Roxana Torre
       </h3>
       <div class="caption-image aligncenter">
        <a class="fancybox" href="http://visualizing.org/full-screen/56186" rel="nofollow">
         <img alt="Partial screen capture of the interactive data visualization, Macrometeorites" class="size-large wp-image-9109" height="386" src="http://visualoop.com/media/2013/06/Macrometeorites-680x490.png" width="750"/>
        </a>
        <div class="asset-source clearfix" id="figcaption_attachment_9109">
         <div class="img-title left">
          (image: Roxana Torre | Visualizing.org)
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <h3>
        Bolides | Carlo Zaponi
       </h3>
