         Austri Vind, OX2 to add 250 MW of wind in Norway
        </h2>

        </span>
        <span class="article-author">
         by
         <strong>
          <a href="/author/veselina-petrova-18" itemprop="author" rel="author" title="Veselina Petrova">

        <div class="article-image">
         <img alt="austri-vind-ox2-to-add-250-mw-of-wind-in-norway" class="img-responsive" src="http://cdn.aiidatapro.net/media/97/45/3f/t780x490/97453f43c005012a1272c2767cb1168ee17ff371.jpg"/>
         Wind farm in Norway. Author: John Christian Fjellestad. License: Creative Commons, Attribution 2.0 Generic
        </div>
        <p>
         Feb 5, 2015 (SeeNews) - Wind farm developer Austri Vind said Monday it will build 250 MW of wind parks in Norway in partnership with Swedish sector player OX2.
        </p>
        <p>
         The parties will develop and install three wind power facilities in the municipalities of Trysil and Amot as well as inEngerdaland Valer. Austri Vind and OX2 have already secured permits for the Raskiftet, Kvitvola and Kjolberget plants from the Norwegian Water and Energy Directorate, while a decision by the countrysOil and Energy Department is to be issued this year.
        </p>
        <p>
         The partners expect to complete the projects in the next few years and plan to start installing 100 MW of capacity in 2015. Austri Vind will take care of relations with permitting authorities and local shareholders, while OX2 will be in charge of the technical and financial aspects of the schemes. The latter will alsoact as construction contractor.
        </p>
        <p>
         OX2 has financed and constructed about 800 MW of wind farms in the Nordic region. Its partner Austri Vind has also developed several projects in Norway.
        </p>
