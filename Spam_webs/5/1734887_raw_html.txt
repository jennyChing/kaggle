          <h2 class="hed6">
           Porch.com remodels, eyes big expansion
          </h2>
          <div class="byline vspacing10">
           Posted by
           <a class="url fn" href="http://blogs.seattletimes.com/brierdudley/author/bdudley/" rel="author" title="Posts by Brier Dudley">
            Brier Dudley
           </a>
          </div>
         </header>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           Seattles Porch.com isfacing all sorts of established competitors in the online home-improvement space, butthe companysfounder isnttoo worried about its position.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-16501">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Just fivemonths after launching its service for exploring, sharing and referring home-improvement services,
           <a href="http://www.porch.com">
            Porch.com
           </a>
           is on a tear, hiring as fast as it can to fill a huge new office and build up its platform.
          </p>
          <p>
           Really over the last five months, weve gotten over that inflection point where the business is going to work, said Matt Ehrlichman, the companys 34-year-old founder and chief executive.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_16507" style="width: 630px">
           <img alt="Erlichman confers with colleagues in the new offices of Porch.com. Photo by John Lok, Times staff photographer." class="size-large wp-image-16507" height="413" src="http://seattletimes.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/brierdudley/files/2014/03/porchLOK2-620x413.jpg" width="620"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Ehrlichman confers with colleagues in the new offices of Porch.com. Photo by John Lok, Times staff photographer.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Now that the team believes the business will fly, its really about execution, its about staying focused and every day trying to go faster and do it better than its been done before, in every part of the business, he said.
          </p>
          <p>
           A breakthroughwas a deal with Lowes announced in January. It putPorch.com in stores and on the devices carried by Lowes employees, who direct customers to the site when theyre looking for professional help with home-improvement projects.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ehrlichman wouldnt share sales but said theyve accelerated since the Lowes deals. Its services are now highlighted in 139 stores, mostly in the South, but will be extended nationally later this year.
          </p>
          <p>
           Also boosting growth is new backing from investors, in addition to the
           <a href="http://blogs.seattletimes.com/brierdudley/2013/06/05/porch-jumps-into-home-improvement-space-with-6-25-million/">
            $6.25 million Ehrlichman raised initially
           </a>
           from angel investors after starting the company in 2012.
          </p>
          <p>
           The company has raised more since. Weve not announced details of that yet, he said. I would say that the company is exceptionally well capitalized at this point so right now were not raising more money at this point in time.
          </p>
          <p>
           Hell probably need every penny for its battle with Palo Alto, Calif.-based
           <a href="http://www.houzz.com">
            Houzz
           </a>
           , a similar home-improvement site that has raised about $50 million since 2010. Another well-financed competitor is
           <a href="http://blog.seattletimes.nwsource.com/brierdudley/2013/02/05/post_68.html">
            Zillow, which launched a home-improvement channel called Digs
           </a>
           last year.
          </p>
          <p>
           We are a newer kid on the block certainly in this space. There are companies that have been around 10 or 15 years and are at scale, Ehrlichman said. The reality of it, though, is that were [taking] an exceptionally different approach than all of those companies, starting with the data and through that information be able to provide insights that are unique to homeowners  not just help them get inspired and look at photos but to actually get their projects done.
          </p>
          <p>
           Similar to the way Zillow drew on public assessors to build its real-estate site, Porch combined mapping information and public records of building projects and contractor licenses to build a foundation of data on which its layering features and services.
          </p>
          <p>
           Already the site includes information about 1.5 million home-improvement professionals. They get free listings on the site and can pay to upgrade their accounts. Homeowners use the site for free.
          </p>
          <p>
           The plan is to connect with homeowners who have done projects and add their recommendations to its listings, so visitors to the site can find local referrals.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ehrlichman believes hes building what will be the next big software company in Seattle. Hes been down this path before with a company called Thriva, which he started while studying at Stanford. It was sold in 2007for $60 million to The Active Network, an event-organization software company based in San Diego.
          </p>
          <p>
           Porch.com was hatched when Ehrlichman tried to find referrals for pros to work on his own home project in Seattle.
          </p>
          <p>
           Hes simultaneously been remodeling the office. The company,which initially started in Ehrlichmans basement, last month moved into a 17,000-square-footoffice on Seattles Eastlake Avenue, where employees chipped in to paint and decorate the space.
          </p>
          <p>
           Porch grew from 25 to 111 employees over the past year, including the recent addition of six employees hireden massefrom the Jawfish Games studio in Seattle, whichfolded in January.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ehrlichman said hes budgeted for 180 employees this year but with the way things are going I wouldnt be surprised if we surpass that.
          </p>
          <p>
           Which means that Porchs remodelingproject,like many others, may just keep going on and on. The company already has taken an option on28,000 more square feet of adjacent, bare space formerly used by the Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation. No doubt it will share details of that project as well.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_16508" style="width: 630px">
           <img alt="Swings in the new offices of Porch.com. Photo by John Lok, Times staff photographer." class="size-large wp-image-16508" height="415" src="http://seattletimes.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/brierdudley/files/2014/03/porchLOK4-620x415.jpg" width="620"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Swings in the new offices of Porch.com. Photo by John Lok, Times staff photographer.
           </p>
          </div>
