   <section id="description">
    Do you have a Samsung Galaxy Note 4? Are you interested in Virtual Reality display? Then checkout this amazing virtual reality device from the same manufacturer as Note 4, Samsung Gear VR.

The Samsung GearVR is not a standalone device. It does not come with its own screen, but is a headset shell that is packed with the stereoscopic lens goodness needed to make the VR work. The key ingredient in unleashing the VR is the Samsung Galaxy Note 4. There is a compartment at the front of the device where the handset slips into.

You can experience a variety of VR experiences on an included 16 GB MicroSD card starter kit as well as the Oculus store on this amazing virtual reality device. However, Samsung hasnt confirmed whether or not youll be able to get all of the cards content on the Oculus store. 

You'll be able to find a lot of big changes if you are already an Oculus Rift user. First of all, the screen is a Galaxy Note 4  you attach to the headset, youve got a 1440p screen rather than 1080p. That means the so-called screen door effect, where you can see the pixels on your screen because theyre so close to your eyes, wont be quite as distinct with this headset. There is a downside as well as, The Galaxy Note 3 screen in Oculus second dev kit is set to refresh 75 times per second. The Note 4 can only handle 60, which means that youre slightly more likely to experience some nausea as you spend time in VR.

Also, since Samsung has the display made using mobile phone,Samsung cant rely on a persistent external camera to track your position in space while you where it. This means youre not going to be able to play the kinds of games built for Oculus that let you interact by moving your body for example, Roller Coaster ride game, leaning around to fly a ship or dodge bullets etc. as the gyroscopes can only really check the angle of your head. Another difference in this device is that there is no 6 degrees of freedom. Thus if you move forward or backward, the device won't recognize it and won't reciprocate it in the virtual reality display.

At the end we would say, the Samsung GearVR is better than expected. We have used phone-based VR in the past but this feels superior and built better. It's a fun add on for the Note 4. Also, at a price tag of $200 it can give a tough competition to counterparts such as Oculus Rift and Morpheus project from Sony.

Share your feedback for the device if you have used it or experienced it with us in comments below.
