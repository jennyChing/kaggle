        <p>
         The jury is still out whether Foursquare Inc. can figure out a way to monetize its hot location-based mobile service, but that hasnt stopped the World Economic Forum from including it among a class of
         <a href="http://www.weforum.org/en/media/Latest%20News%20Releases/NR_TP2011">
          31 companies
         </a>
         deemed pioneers in technology, the same honor it once bestowed on a young Google Inc.
        </p>
        <div class="mceTemp" style="text-align: left;">
         <dl class="wp-caption alignright caption-alignright" style="width: 262px;">
          <dt class="wp-caption-dt">
           <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-5" height="174" src="http://s.wsj.net/media/worldeconomicforum_D_20100901121232.jpg" width="262"/>
          </dt>
          <dd class="wp-caption-dd wp-cite-dd" style="text-align: right;">
           European Pressphoto Agency
          </dd>
          <dd class="wp-caption-dd" style="text-align: left;">
          </dd>
         </dl>
        </div>
        <p>
         Of the companies selected, 17 were from the U.S. Five Silicon Valley companies made the list, as did two from the Boston area. The forum recognized the companies among those poised to have a critical impact on the future of business, industry and society.
        </p>
        <p>
         This years class is composed almost entirely of venture-backed start-ups, with three New York-based companiesFoursquare, Knewton Inc. and SecondMarket Inc.making the prestigious list, matching the citys total for all previous years combined.
        </p>
        <p>
         I think the recognition is proof that New York is emerging as one of the hottest places in the world to start a company, SecondMarket Chief Executive Barry Silbert said.
        </p>
        <p>
         New York Mayor Michael Bloomberg also recognized his citys start-ups.
        </p>
        <p>
         Having three New York City-based start-up companies on the World Economic Forums 2011 list of Technology Pioneers is a testament to the world-class entrepreneurs launching businesses in New York City today, Bloomberg said in a statement. SecondMarket, Knewton, and Foursquare represent the innovation of New Yorkers and the diversity of New York Citys economy. Innovative ventures like these are helping to position New York as a global center for business entrepreneurship, and we congratulate them on the honor.
        </p>
        <p>
         Another feather in New Yorks cap is that local venture firm FirstMark Capital claims both Knewton and SecondMarket as portfolio companies.
        </p>
        <p>
         Knewton CEO Jose Ferreira said New York still has work to do before it can be considered a technology hotbed, but believes the Technology Pioneers award shows theres significant activity in the region.
        </p>
        <p>
         Having three New York companies win in one year is unprecedented, Ferreira said. Its a good story for New York and a sign of things to come.
        </p>
        <p>
         To be selected as a Technology Pioneer, a company must be involved in the development of a major technology or innovation and have the potential for long-term impact on business and society. In addition, it must demonstrate visionary leadership and show all the signs of being a long-standing and sustainable market leader  and its technology must be proven, according to the forum.
        </p>
        <p>
         Selected companies will be recognized during the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland, in January.
        </p>
        <p>
         More than 330 applications from around the world were submitted for consideration by 68 global technology experts. This year, the forum received the widest range ever of countries of origin, with 13 countries represented: Australia, Canada, India, Israel, Lithuania, the Netherlands, Nigeria, Peoples Republic of China, South Africa, Switzerland, Thailand, the U.K. and the U.S. Over half of the Technology Pioneers are from the U.S.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres the entire roster of companies: Adimab Inc., Aster Data Systems Inc., Atlassian Pty Ltd, Digital Lumens Inc., Ecovative Design LLC, Ferrate Treatment Technologies LLC, Flexoresearch Group, Foursquare Inc., GetJar Inc., Ion Torrent Systems Inc., Knewton Inc., Layar B.V, Medicine in Need, Molecular Partners AG, NetQin Mobile Inc., Neuronetics Inc., Novacem Ltd., On-Ramp Wireless Inc., OpenDNS LLC, OPOWER Inc., Ostara Nutrient Recovery Technologies Inc., Quintas Renewable Energy Solutions, ReputationDefender Inc., Scribd Inc., SecondMarket Inc., Spotify Ltd., TaKaDu, Tendril Inc., Topell Energy BV, Transonic Combustion Inc. and Vortex Engineering.
        </p>
        <p>
         The World Economic Forum is an independent, international organization incorporated as a Swiss not-for-profit foundation. The forums motto is entrepreneurship in the global public interest.
        </p>
        <p>
         Previous Technology Pioneers include 23andme Inc., (2008), Amyris Biotechnologies Inc. (2006), Bloom Energy Corp. (2010), Google Inc. (2001), Mozilla Corp. (2007) and Twitter Inc. (2010).
        </p>
        <p>
         Venture capitalists from across the world were also prominently featured on the Technology Pioneers selection committee. VC members on the committee included: Kevin Comolli, partner, Accel Partners; Bruce Golden, partner, Accel Partners; Bernard Liautaud, general partner, Balderton Capital; Sven Lingjaerde, general partner, Endeavour Vision; Amish Mehta, partner, Vector; Campbell Murray, managing director, Novartis Venture Funds; Alan Salzman, chief executive and managing partner, VantagePoint Venture Partners; Helmut Schuhsler, managing partner, TVM Capital; Oliver Sichel, partner, Sofinnova Partners; David Spreng, founder and managing partner, Crescendo Ventures; and Jim Tananbaum, managing director, Prospect Venture Partners.
        </p>

// performs callback when element becomes visible, then removes listeners
function whenElementVisibleDo(el, cb) {
  var handler = function() {
    if (isElementInViewportAtAll(el)) {
      cb();
      removeListeners(handler);
    }
  }
  attachListeners(handler);
}

// similar to http://stackoverflow.com/a/7557433/458614 but returns true if any part of the element is in the viewport, not just the whole thing
// should work on: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, IE8+, iOS Safari, Android2+, Blackberry, Opera Mobile, IE Mobile
// doesn't handle z-index, being outside of viewable area of parent element, and possibly other edge cases
// we check if either the top OR the bottom of the element is within the viewport, while either the left right or the element is also in the viewport
function isElementInViewportAtAll (el) {
    var rect = el.getBoundingClientRect();
    var height = window.innerHeight || document.documentElement.clientHeight;
    var width = window.innerWidth || document.documentElement.clientWidth;
    return (
      (
        (rect.top &lt;= height &amp;&amp; rect.top &gt;= 0) ||
        (rect.bottom &lt;= height &amp;&amp; rect.bottom &gt;= 0)
      ) &amp;&amp;
      (
        (rect.left &lt;= width &amp;&amp; rect.left &gt;= 0) ||
        (rect.right &lt;= width &amp;&amp; rect.right &gt;= 0)
      ) 
    );
}

function attachListeners(fn) {
  if (window.addEventListener) {
    window.addEventListener('DOMContentLoaded', fn, false);
    window.addEventListener('load', fn, false);
    window.addEventListener('scroll', fn, false);
    window.addEventListener('resize', fn, false);
  } else if (window.attachEvent) { // IE fallback
    window.attachEvent('DOMContentLoaded', fn);
    window.attachEvent('load', fn);
    window.attachEvent('onscroll', fn);
    window.attachEvent('onresize', fn);
  }
}
function removeListeners(fn) {
  if (window.removeEventListener) {
    window.removeEventListener('DOMContentLoaded', fn, false);
    window.removeEventListener('load', fn, false);
    window.removeEventListener('scroll', fn, false);
    window.removeEventListener('resize', fn, false);
  } else if (window.attachEvent) { // IE fallback
    window.detachEvent('DOMContentLoaded', fn);
    window.detachEvent('load', fn);
    window.detachEvent('onscroll', fn);
    window.detachEvent('onresize', fn);
  }
}

// adds a class when top of element is off screen, removes class again when scrolling back up to that point
function addClassWhenOffTop(el, classname) {
  var scrollTopWhenAdded;
  attachListeners(function() {
    if (scrollTopWhenAdded === undefined) {
      // class has not been added , only add for ads with size less than 400
      if ( (el.getBoundingClientRect().top) &lt; 0 &amp;&amp; ($(el).children()[0].height &lt; 400 ) ) {
        // it's off the top of the screen
        el.className += ' ' + classname;
        scrollTopWhenAdded = document.documentElement.scrollTop || document.body.scrollTop;
      }
    }
    else {
      if ((document.documentElement.scrollTop || document.body.scrollTop) &lt;= scrollTopWhenAdded) {
        // we've gone back up past it
        el.className = el.className.replace(new RegExp('\\b'+classname+'\\b'), ''); // remove class. in PHP double-quoted string have to do 4 backslashes to get 2
        scrollTopWhenAdded = undefined;
      }
    }
  });
}
