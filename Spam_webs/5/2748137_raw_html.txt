           <div class="entry-category-asian_food_culture entry-category-asian_food_news entry-category-asian_ingredients entry-author-andrea_nguyen entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a00d8341ef22f53ef01b8d0c0500e970c">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <h3 class="entry-header">
              David Tran on How Huy Fong Sriracha Sauce is Made
             </h3>
             <div class="entry-content">
              <div class="entry-body">
               <p>
                <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.vietworldkitchen.com/.a/6a00d8341ef22f53ef01b7c736d384970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false">
                 <img alt="Sriracha-tower-F-Ball" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341ef22f53ef01b7c736d384970b img-responsive" src="http://www.vietworldkitchen.com/.a/6a00d8341ef22f53ef01b7c736d384970b-500wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Sriracha-tower-F-Ball"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                America continues its lovefest with Sriracha chile sauce. Every few months, I hear about a new rendition. I've made my own, done tastings, and tried the original Sriracha sauce.
               </p>
               <p>
                Frank Ball and I started an email conversation long about the popularity of Sriracha chile sauce. Hed read VWK posts about the chile sauce and one day, sent along the above photo from a Bed Bath and Beyond in White Plains, New York. Frank is a former movie maker and author of a terrific
                <a href="http://amzn.to/1sHf7zP" target="_blank">
                 book
                </a>
                on professional kitchen tips for home cooks. Weve kept in touch since and this week, he emailed an interesting video made about the production of Huy Fongs Sriracha hot sauce.
               </p>
               <p>
                A quick recap: Huy Fong has been making various kinds of Southeast Asian-style chile sauces for decades in a more or less low-key manner. No publicity, no marketing. Owner David Tran was more or less silently manufacturing a lot of hot chile sauce in Los Angeles. He is Chinese-Vietnamese.
               </p>
               <p>
                In 2014 as things got super heated with his legal battle with the City of Irwindale where his factory is located, he broke his silence. He gave interviews all of a sudden and flexed his muscle. There was local, national and international coverage of the story.
               </p>
               <div class="photo-wrap photo-xid-6a00d8341ef22f53ef01bb07db0d3e970d" id="photo-xid-6a00d8341ef22f53ef01bb07db0d3e970d" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; width: 500px;">
                <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.vietworldkitchen.com/.a/6a00d8341ef22f53ef01bb07db0d3e970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false">
                 <img alt="David-Tran-Credit-Griffin-Hammond" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341ef22f53ef01bb07db0d3e970d img-responsive" src="http://www.vietworldkitchen.com/.a/6a00d8341ef22f53ef01bb07db0d3e970d-500wi" title="David-Tran-Credit-Griffin-Hammond"/>
                </a>
                <div class="photo-caption caption-xid-6a00d8341ef22f53ef01bb07db0d3e970d" id="caption-xid-6a00d8341ef22f53ef01bb07db0d3e970d">

                 <a href="http://srirachamovie.com/" target="_blank">
                  Srirachamovie.com
                 </a>
                </div>
               </div>
               <p>
                He appeared at Sriracha events like the Los Angeles Chinese American Museums Sriracha
                <a href="http://camla.org/past-exhibitions/#la-heat-taste-changing-condiments">
                 art exhibit called LA Heat
                </a>
                . Tran granted an
                <a href="http://www.npr.org/blogs/codeswitch/2014/05/12/311719864/sriracha-maker-says-factory-will-remain-in-california">
                 NPR interview
                </a>
                to reporter Karen Grigsby Bates. He opened up the Irwindale factory for public tours. He came out of the shadow to show the world that hes a businessman who runs a transparent operation. It's been a terrific American immigrant story.
               </p>
              </div>
              <a id="more">
              </a>
              <div class="entry-more">
               <p>
                Many people, especially young people, enjoy all the heat in Huy Fongs Sriracha sauce. Fashion and style site
                <a href="www.hypebeast.com" target="_blank">
                 Hype Beast
                </a>
                decided to go inside the factory and make a mini documentary. Its well done and Tran proves that he is savvy and understands his market. Lots of things are revealed about the production process, the color of the cap, and his approach to creating his rendition of Sriracha chile sauce.
               </p>
               <p>
                I dont know if this was scripted because the captioning does not totally match with what Tran actually says. It's a bit odd. In any event, this is an interesting video worth watching.
