        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         DIY Overhead Camera Mount
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-06-04T22:03:59+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          June 4, 2014
         </time>
         By:
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
           Katie
          </span>
         </span>

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_11.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6329">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6329 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="360" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_11.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I have to say I am mighty proud of myself for this project.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Several weeks ago I attended a Bloggers Retreat at Pondersa Ranch near Zions National Park. The entire week was absolutely amazing. Throughout the retreat there was a big emphasis on video and capturing your adventure. So we went on many adventures including horseback riding, four wheeling, and I even got to go on a helicopter ride. Now that was truly amazing! Anyways, we also had a couple classes on video, and how to do lighting, sound and editing. The weekend was indeed an awesome adventure and when I left I had a new excitement for videos and decided I wanted to take the leap and do more videos on my blog.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I do hands only videos at work (meaning an overhead shot and all you record is your hands, no front shot of your face) and I have really enjoyed doing them. I began to think about how I could do that at home. At work we have a really nice set up that probably cost several hundred dollars, if not more. But I need something on a smaller scale but still produced a high quality shot. After exploring online and seeing what others have done I came up with this set up.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         What you will need:
        </p>
        <ul style="text-align: left;">
         <li>
          1, 10 foot piece of 1/2 PVC Pipe
         </li>
         <li>
          2, 1/2 PVC Caps
         </li>
         <li>
          2, 1/2 PVC Elbows
         </li>
         <li>
          3, 1/2 PVC Non-threaded Tees
         </li>
         <li>
          1, 1/4-20 x 12 bolt
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I got my PVC pipe at Lowes, and the best part was that they cut it for me. So I didnt even have to mess with a hack saw. While I had a good idea of what I wanted I had them cut a couple extra pieces so I would be able to to place around with what I had envisioned. Here Is what I ended up having the Lowes gentleman cut the PVC pipe into.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_9.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6327">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6327 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="368" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_9.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: left;">
          3, 13 pieces
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: left;">
          2, 11 pieces
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: left;">
          1, 7 piece
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: left;">
          1, 3 piece
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Once I had all of my pieces and they were cut to the lengths I wanted it was time to begin assembly.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Have you ever noticed the little screw hole in the bottom of your camera?
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_12.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6330">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6330 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="374" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_12.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         That is so you can screw it onto a tripod. Well for all intensive purposes, that is what I am creating. So I needed to fin a bolt that would fit that hole so I had a way to attach my camera to the mount.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I took my camera with me to Lowes and found that the 1/4  20 x 1/2 bolt fit it perfectly. What all those numbers mean, I have no idea. I just know that it fit. Keep in mind that you dont want a pointy ended screw but a flat ended bolt.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_5.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6323">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6323 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="560" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_5.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I wanted that screw to go into one of the Tees. So I started by making a mark on the inside center with a black marker so I would know where to drill.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_1.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6319">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6319 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="342" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_1.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Using a 15/64 drill bit, I drilled a hole where that black dot was.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_2.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6320">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6320 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="413" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_2.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I found the 15/64 drill bit to be the perfect size. It allowed me to twist the bolt in and keep it nice and tight.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_7.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6325">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6325 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="442" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_7.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Twist the bolt in as far as it will go so the end is poking out of the tee.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_8.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6326">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6326 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="309" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_8.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         That is really the hardest part of the whole assembly. Next you simply push the pieces together.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_13.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6331">
          <img alt="Make an overhead camera mount with PVC pipe. This is an inexpensive camera hack that you won't know how you lived without. Step up your photography!" class="alignnone wp-image-6331 size-full" data-pinit="registered" height="644" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_13.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The tall 13 inch piece is standing up in the center can really be any length that you need. I had the gentleman at lowes cut me a 7 inch piece to go right there but I found for my camera I need the longer piece. Out of my left over pipe I actually cut me an even longer piece, closer to 17-18 inches for my DSLR. It really is personal preference and what works best for your camera.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_10.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-6328">
          <img alt="Camera Stand with PVC" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6328" data-pinit="registered" height="419" src="http://thecraftyblogstalker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Camera_Stand_10.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The very best part is all of the pieces only cost me about $6. Now I bet you can afford that!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         With my new set up I did a practice video. Now I know this is really pretty lame, so remember this was a test for my new overhead camera mount.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/_ytArq6HZ1A" width="500">
         </iframe>
        </center>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         So what do you think? Are you ready to do more videos too?
        </p>
