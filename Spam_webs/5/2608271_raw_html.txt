        <div class="entry-content col-sm-9">
         <div class="article-meta visible-xs visible-xxs">
          <p class="byline">
           <span class="by">
            By
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" data-omtag="news:article:author:top" href="http://www.realtor.com/author/erik-gunther/">
             Erik Gunther
            </a>
            <a data-omtag="news:article:author:twitter" href="http://twitter.com/egnonstop">
             <i class="fa fa-twitter">
             </i>
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="posted-on">
           <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2015-01-15T12:40:45+00:00">
            12:40 pm ET
            <br/>
            January 15, 2015
           </time>
           <time class="updated hidden" datetime="2015-01-15T12:40:45+00:00">
            January 15, 2015
           </time>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="image-wrapper no-caption">
          <figure>
           <a href="http://www.realtor.com/news/alabamas-expensive-house/" title="Alabamas Most Expensive House">
            <img alt="Birmingham 1" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="480" src="http://rdcnewscdn.realtor.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Birmingham-1.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
           <figcaption>
            <p class="credit">
            </p>
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </div>
         <p>
          <em>
           Each week, we look at the most expensive MLS-listed house in each state.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          This week, we kick off the series in
          <a href="http://www.realtor.com/realestateandhomes-search/Alabama" target="_blank">
           Alabama
          </a>
          home to the most rabid college football fans in the country. Its also home to an
          <a href="http://www.realtor.com/realestateandhomes-detail/7-Montagel-Way_Birmingham_AL_35242_M78634-85063?ex=AL516438891&amp;row=1" target="_blank">
           enormous Birmingham mansion
          </a>
          thats been on the market for almost five years.
         </p>
         <p>
          Listed for $9.9 million after a $4 million price cut just this week, the 50,000-square-foot behemoth is known as Americas largest home. Its notoriety and interesting guitar-shaped landscaping helpedit place sixthin our rankingsof the
          <a href="http://www.realtor.com/news/must-clicks-2014-most-viewed-home-listings/" target="_blank">
           most-viewed listings
          </a>
          of 2014.
         </p>
         <p>
          Even with the recent price reduction, the
          <a href="http://www.realtor.com/realestateandhomes-search/Birmingham_AL" target="_blank">
           Birmingham
          </a>
          home still lists for $4.5 million more than its nearest competitor in the state:a $5.4 million
