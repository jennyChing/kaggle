          <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Why merino wool is the perfect material for outdoor clothing" data-url="http://www.keller-sports.com/blog/why-merino-wool-is-the-perfect-material-for-outdoor-clothing/">
          </div>
          <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
          </div>
          <p>
           Warm and cold. Wet and dry.
          </p>
          <p>
           Anyone who goes outside needs flexible clothing.
          </p>
          <p>
           Thanks to its many practical properties
           <strong>
            merino wool
           </strong>
           is suitable for the manufacture of sport and
           <a href="http://www.keller-sports.com/outdoor/outdoorclothing/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.keller-sports.com/outdoor/outdoorclothing/', 'outdoor clothing']);" target="_blank" title="Keller Sports - Outdoor">
            outdoor clothing
           </a>
           , especially stop n go activities like skiing or snowboarding, a several day mountaineering expedition or a long walking holiday.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Merino wool is obtained from the coat of Merino sheep
           </strong>
           . The majority is produced in Australia and New Zealand, since the sheep there develop a very robust coat as a result of the weather conditions.
          </p>
          <p>
           Well explain what properties the wool displays and why it is so well suited for outdoor activities.
          </p>
          <h2>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Merino wool properties
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Breathable and thermoregulatory (even when wet), protects from the cold as well as extreme heat
           </li>
           <li>
            Wicks sweat away from the body (merino wool can hold around 30% of its own weight in moisture) and dries very quickly
           </li>
           <li>
            Smooth, soft and elastic
           </li>
           <li>
            Offers a natural sun protection factor
           </li>
           <li>
            Naturally renewable material
           </li>
           <li>
            Sterile (due to the animal protein fibres), self-cleaning and odour-free, so they do not need to be washed as often as traditional sports and functional clothing
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.keller-sports.de/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/merinowolle.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.keller-sports.de/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/merinowolle.jpg', '']);">
            <img alt="merinowolle" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7383" height="865" src="http://www.keller-sports.de/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/merinowolle.jpg" width="1200"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <div class="framebox">
           <h2>
            Washing merino wool the correct way
           </h2>
           <p>
            You can always hand wash, but in the mean time many washing machines ofer a
            <strong>
             30 wool washing programme with a low spin cycle
            </strong>
            , which is ideal for the washing of merino wool (we would also recommend an appropriate detergent). Treated wool also felts less and can be safely washed in a machine.
           </p>
           <p>
            Anyone who has a tumble dryer with the wool mark seal can directly dry their merino wool; otherwise we recommed that you
            <strong>
             leave your clothes out to dry so that they dont wear out
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Facts about Merino sheep
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            The whole wool coat of a sheep weighs up to 4kg, about half of that is suitable for use after cleaning  the best coats can even reach 10kg.
           </li>
           <li>
            In comparison to normal wool, merino is smoother and more robust (strictly speaking, with 15-25 microns it is the finest wool in the world) and is therefore really suitable as a base layer.
           </li>
           <li>
            The
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merino" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merino', 'Merino sheep']);" target="_blank" title="Merino Sheep">
             Merino sheep
            </a>
            emerged in the 14th Century as a result of a cross between an African and Asian sheep.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           <img alt="merino sheep" class="size-full wp-image-7388" height="960" src="http://www.keller-sports.de/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/merinoschafe.jpg" width="1280"/>
          </p>
          <p class="imgcredits">
           (Foto:
           <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/User:%C3%96kologix" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/User:%C3%96kologix', 'Flickr-kologix']);">
            Flickr-kologix
           </a>
           /
           <a href="http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/', 'CC-BY-SA-3.0']);">
            CC-BY-SA-3.0
           </a>
           )
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           In the past, merino wool was criticised by animal protection groups for the so called 
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mulesing" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mulesing', 'Mulesing']);" target="_blank" title="Mulesing">
            Mulesing
           </a>
           . Big brands such as
           <a href="http://www.keller-sports.com/brands/icebreaker/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.keller-sports.com/brands/icebreaker/', 'Icebreaker']);" target="_blank" title="Keller Sports - Icebreaker">
            Icebreaker
           </a>
           , Arcteryx, Mammut or
           <a href="http://www.keller-sports.com/brands/patagonia/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.keller-sports.com/brands/patagonia/', 'Patagonia']);" target="_blank" title="Keller Sports - Patagonia">
            Patagonia
           </a>
           strongly speak out against cruelty to animals, as do we when choosing our range.
          </p>
          <p>
           Our thoughts regarding merino wool for outdoor sport are completely positive.
          </p>
          <p>
           It is flexible, durable and natural.
          </p>
          <p>
           Perfect, dont you think?
          </p>
