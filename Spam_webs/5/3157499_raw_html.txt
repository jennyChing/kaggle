       <h1>
        <a href="http://www.blogonlyapartments.com/madrid-andreas-salome/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Madrid and Lou Andreas Salom">
         Madrid and Lou Andreas Salom
        </a>
       </h1>
       <p class="post-info">
        Feb 24th, 2011 by
        <a href="http://www.blogonlyapartments.com/author/apartmentblogger/" rel="author" title="Posts by apartmentblogger">
         apartmentblogger
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="entry">
       <div class="topsy_widget_data topsy_theme_blue" style="float: right;margin-left: 0.75em; background: url(data:,%7B%20%22url%22%3A%20%22http%253A%252F%252Fwww.blogonlyapartments.com%252Fmadrid-andreas-salome%252F%22%2C%20%22style%22%3A%20%22big%22%2C%20%22title%22%3A%20%22Madrid%20and%20Lou%20Andreas%20Salom%C3%A9%22%20%7D);">
       </div>
       <p>
        The other day we were imagining that moment many years ago in Viena when writer and pioneer of psychology
        <strong>
         Lou
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Andreas
        </strong>
        Salom explained to an impressionable Sigmund
        <strong>
         Freud
        </strong>
        how a city can also be psychoanalysed. For those who are unfamiliar with the St. Petersburg writer, Salom was one of the most fascinating and influential figures to emerge from a bygone generation of European culture that lived through the turn of the century and two world wars.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="lou andreas salome" height="565" src="http://blogimgs.only-apartments.com/images/only-apartments/1224/madrid-lou-andreas-salome.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="lou andreas salome" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Indeed, during two different centuries, Salom both inspired
        <strong>
         Nietzsche
        </strong>
        to ponder the overwhelming and vast abyss of life, and revealed to
        <strong>
         Rilke
        </strong>
        some of the most profound, fundamental mysteries of Russia. The result of the latters epiphany was The Book of Hours [1905], the Czech poets incomparable collection of one-line odes to the dark shadows, vast landscapes, and open horizons of his country  one of the most remarkable lyrical achievements of Western art.
       </p>
       <p>
        If they are considered as live beings which pass through different stages of growth and personality development, all cities have the potential to be psychoanalyzed  but few appear more ripe for psychoanalysis than
        <strong>
         Madrid.
        </strong>
        Its an exercise that should be approached seriously as a sort of game that is also a form of art.
       </p>
       <p>
        The dark, beautiful, and little-known Neo Roman, Bizantine stylecrypt in the
        <strong>
         Almudena
        </strong>
        cathedral would be a good place to start a journey back down into the roots of the city. Just as the
        <strong>
         crypt
        </strong>
        is located underneath the cathedral, connecting to it through the back of the apse, so too is the Andalucian history of
        <strong>
         Madrid
        </strong>
        accessed via a mythical path that is buried beneath five hundred years offacade that has not only wiped out practically all the cities visible vestiges but also, even worse, almost all memory of this past.
       </p>
       <p>
        But timeleaves its forgotten spots. The
        <strong>
         crypt
        </strong>
        lies just under the cathedral of
        <strong>
         Almudena
        </strong>
         the virgin patron of
        <strong>
         Madrid,
        </strong>
        whose nameis typicallyArabic, since it is derives from Al-Mudayna, meaning
        <em>
         walled city
        </em>
         just as Madrids name comes from the word Mayrat, meaning journey or direction of water. Just in front of the
        <strong>
         crypt
        </strong>
        sits the oldest ruin in the city, and inscribed over 108 meters of the flint wall is the citys beautiful motto: I was built on water, my walls are of fire.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Paul Oilzum Only-apartments Author" height="100" src="http://www.only-apartments.com/bloggers/images/users/187.jpg" style="float: right;" title="Paul Oilzum Only-apartments Author" width="100"/>
        <b>
         Paul Oilzum
        </b>
       </p>

         apartments in Madrid
        </a>
        go in search of yourself  you might find it walking these streets.
