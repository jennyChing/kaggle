          <h2>
           <a href="/Blogs/NET-POSTS/June-2014/Video-Nadal-Wins-Two-Amazing-Points-after-Falling.aspx">
            Video: Nadal Wins Two Amazing Points after Falling Down
           </a>
          </h2>
          <div style="margin-top:10px;border-bottom:2px solid #5bb249;">
          </div>
         </div>
         <p style="font-size:11px; font-weight:bold; padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:15px;">
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <a href="/Blogs/NET-POSTS/June-2014/Video-Nadal-Wins-Two-Amazing-Points-after-Falling.aspx">
          </a>
         </div>
         <p style="font-size:13px !important;padding-right:2%;">
         </p>
         <div class="video-container">
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           <iframe class="youtube-player" height="429" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/pWVlzDcnIlg?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" type="text/html" width="610">
           </iframe>
          </span>
         </div>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: small;">
          <strong>
           Rafael Nadal
          </strong>
          had to work his tail off to defeat
          <strong>
           Martin Klizan
          </strong>
          in four sets on Day 2 of the Championships  literally! Watch as Nadal wins two of the most amazing grass court points we have seen at Wimbledon in quite some time. On each point Nadal takes a spill in the grass, only to recover in rapid fashion to eventually steal each point from Klizan. Perhaps not the most delicate grass-court footwork, but an A+ for effort, and off the charts in terms of determination and fortitude, for Nadal.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           <span style="color:#b22222;">
            Video:
           </span>
           <a href="http://www.tennisnow.com/Blogs/NET-POSTS/June-2014/Video-Federer-Carves-up-a-Sweet-Volley-against-Lo.aspx" target='_"blank"'>
            Federer Hits Tasty Dropper vs. Lorenzi on Day 2
           </a>
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Who does that? Who falls in the grass and gets back up at full tilt, continuing to play as if nothing has happened? Answer: Only Nadal. What tenacious, ferocious stuff from the two-time Wimbledon champion. Its great news for Nadal and his fans that the Spaniard is more concerned with winning points and matches than protecting his body.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Much to the chagrin of a very in-form Martin Klizan, Nadal looks to be 100 percent committed to laying it all on the line at Wimbledon for the next two weeks.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          But enjoy these videos of Nadal falling down while you can, because by weeks end Nadal, who ended a three-match grass-court losing streak with his Day 2 victory, should have his footing all figured out.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Watch out, Wimbledon.
         </span>
