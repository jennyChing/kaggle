                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Best of New York City: My 3 Favorite Bars
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <b>
                      <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                       H
                      </span>
                      onestly, I may have bitten off more than I can
                      <strike>
                       drink
                      </strike>
                      chew with this one.
                     </b>
                     I mean, how does one even BEGIN to choose their favorite bar in New York City: a land of so, SO many good - nay! - GREAT ones? It is a particular torture to choose one haven of sin over another, but by god, I'll do it for YOU.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                       S
                      </span>
                      o here we go, cats and kittens! A native's guide to the three best bars in New York City, with four honorable mentions plus some additional reading material for overachievers:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-8_ASp1KFNJ4/TzsnmtIakuI/AAAAAAAABt4/FEU2TJG7OgE/s1600/Sweet+Afton.tiff" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-8_ASp1KFNJ4/TzsnmtIakuI/AAAAAAAABt4/FEU2TJG7OgE/s640/Sweet+Afton.tiff" width="531"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Laid-back cool at Sweet Afton. Photo via Sweet Afton.
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <b>
                      <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                       A
                      </span>
                      storia, Queens: Sweet Afton
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Sweet Afton is a total locals' pick: not only for the low-key atmosphere, but also because they serve classic bar fare using only the freshest ingredients from local purveyors, and all of their infused liquors and preserves are made in house.
                     </b>
                     Patrons and staff are equally mellow and laid-back, except for that couple who once propositioned Dan and me for a foursome circa New Year's Eve 2010. (Nothing to say here except: EW.) Anyway, as long as you stay away from those two, I guarantee you'll like it here, especially after you've tried the
                     <b>
                      Beer Battered Fried McClure's Brooklyn Pickles.
                     </b>
                     Wash it down with one of
                     <b>
                      their specialty cask ales, classic cocktails (I the Cucumber Collins), or well-edited selection of red and white wines
                     </b>
                     . If you're feeling especially zesty, try a Dirty Pickle Martini, which isn't nearly as obscene as it sounds.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-vciKGE4tn5Y/TzsnlskssII/AAAAAAAABtw/TTOPdPZB9ME/s1600/IMG_0033.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                          <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-vciKGE4tn5Y/TzsnlskssII/AAAAAAAABtw/TTOPdPZB9ME/s640/IMG_0033.jpeg" width="640"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         Sixties kitsch at Beauty Bar. Photo via Beauty Bar.
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <b>
                      <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                       G
                      </span>
                      ramercy, Manhattan:
                      <a href="http://thebeautybar.com/New_York/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                        Beauty Bar
                       </span>
                      </a>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Ladies! Have you ever asked yourself,
                      <i>
                       Self, Where can I get a reasonably priced brew and a kick ass manicure ALL IN ONE PLACE?
                      </i>
                     </b>
                     Sure you have - and you may have labored under the assumption that this heaven on earth, this unicorn of cantinas didn't exist. But IT DOES. And you know what else? Despite the glitter pink wall paint and the rows of 1960s hair dryers, straight guys totally hang out here. (Gay guys and gals do, too.) Anyway,
                     <b>
                      the vibe here is fun and frisky
                     </b>
                     : they have a very decent happy hour, and regular pints of beer aren't disturbingly expensive here, as they can be in so many other spots in Manhattan.
                     <b>
                      And if you want to dance your lady pants off, there is a sweet back room where the DJ will save your life.
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <br/>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-9o98iTEK3A8/TzsnlOYuR9I/AAAAAAAABto/fTamZb7PFgY/s1600/Hotel+Delmano.tiff" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                          <img border="0" height="390" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-9o98iTEK3A8/TzsnlOYuR9I/AAAAAAAABto/fTamZb7PFgY/s640/Hotel+Delmano.tiff" width="640"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         The swanky interior of Hotel Delmano, Brooklyn. Photo via Hotel Delmano.
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                       W
                      </span>
                      illiamsburg, Brooklyn:
                      <a href="http://www.hoteldelmano.com/" target="_blank">
                       <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                        Hotel Delmano
                       </span>
                      </a>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      The Hotel Delmano is a rare bird, indeed: a legal speakeasy that manages to pull off Roaring Twenties flair with complete sincerity and (dare I say it?) authenticity.
                     </b>
                     Yes, the barmen wear wingtips and suspenders. Yes, the walls are adorned with smokey mirrors and portraits of old people. Yes, the chandeliers are gilded and the bar is a long, curving cake of marble.Yet for all its
                     <i>
                      Gatsby-
                     </i>
                     esque opulence, Hotel Delmano strikes all the right notes:
                     <b>
                      the cocktails are delicate, subtle, and bursting with fresh and surprising flavors
                     </b>
                     . They also serve small plates of cheese and olives. A great place to go when you're feeling fancy and want to pretend your date is
                     <strike>
                      Al Capone
                     </strike>
                     <strike>
                      Zelda Fitzgerald
                     </strike>
                     not wearing pleated-front cargo shorts. P.S. They're pricey.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                       H
                      </span>
                      onorable Mentions:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                       <a href="http://cafebarastoria.com/" target="_blank">
                        <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                         Cafe Bar
                        </span>
                       </a>
                      </span>
                     </b>
                     // Indeed, a cafe + bar inAstoria, Queens. Great sidewalk scene in warmer months.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      The Charleston
                     </b>
                     // A hipster dive bar giving away free pizza with every drink purchased. No website, located at 174 Bedford Avenue near N. 7th Street in Williamsburg, Brooklyn.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://fryingpan.com/" target="_blank">
                      <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                       <b>
                        The Frying Pan
                       </b>
                      </span>
                     </a>
                     // Sail the drunken seas on this salvaged lightship on Pier 66, Manhattan.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      White Horse Tavern
                     </b>
                     // A classic pub with literary street cred: Dylan Thomas favored this place back in the day. No website, located at 567 Hudson Street at 11th Street, Manhattan.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                       A
                      </span>
                      dditional Reading Material for Overachievers:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/nights/5-nyc-beer-gardens-plus-1-on-the-way/" target="_blank">
                      <b>
                       <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                        5 NYC Beer Gardens (Plus 1 on the Way)
                       </span>
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     // My guide to, uh, 5 NYC Beer Gardens. P.S. The one on the way is now open.
