      <header class="entry-header">
       <div class="prev-next">
        <span class="next-link">
         <a href="http://freethoughtblogs.com/cuttlefish/2013/04/14/national-poetry-month-guest-poet-4-rikitiki/">

          A Thousand Words: Pensive
         </a>
        </span>
        <div style="clear: both">
        </div>
       </div>
       <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
        The Dinosaur Told Me
       </h1>
      </header>
      <hr/>
      <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
       <p>
        The dinosaur told me be careful
        <br/>
        The dinosaur told me beware
        <br/>
        The dinosaur told me its happened before,
        <br/>
        And the universe just doesnt care
       </p>
       <p>
        The dinosaur told me Extinction
        <br/>
        Is the safest, conservative bet
        <br/>
        The dinosaur told me dont think youre immune
        <br/>
        Just because it has not happened yet.
       </p>
       <p>
        The dinosaur gave me a warning
        <br/>
        She told me to share it with you
        <br/>
        It wont be an asteroid this time around
        <br/>
        Well be killed by the things that we do
       </p>
       <div id="div-gpt-ad-1421869288950-0" style="width: 100%; max-width:728px; height:90px; margin: 0 auto; clear: both;">
        <script type="text/javascript">
         googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1421869288950-0'); });
        </script>
       </div>
       <div style="clear: both">
       </div>
       <p>
        The dinosaur pointed to history
        <br/>
        And biology books on the shelves
        <br/>
        The dinosaur told me whats different this time
        <br/>
        Is, were doing it all by ourselves
       </p>
       <p>
        The dinosaur told me be careful
        <br/>
        The dinosaur told me its true
        <br/>
        The dinosaur said, its a fight to the death,
        <br/>
        And the enemy this time is you.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://freethoughtblogs.com/cuttlefish/files/2013/04/Dinosaur.jpeg">
         <img alt="Dinosaur" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-3710" height="256" src="//freethoughtblogs.com/cuttlefish/files/2013/04/Dinosaur-300x256.jpeg" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        (click to embiggen, a bit.)
       </p>
       <p>
        Today was a good day for dinosaurs. (NoteI am a big fan of Dana Hunters 
        <a href="http://freethoughtblogs.com/entequilaesverdad/2013/04/02/unidentified-flying-dinosaur-ufd-in-a-fruit-tree/">
         Unidentified Flying Dinosaur
        </a>
         series.) Today, while watching an American Kestrel, I was completely blown away when the tiny kestrel, skittish in the presence of my camera, nearly flew right into the talons of a huge Osprey (I was reminded of fighter planes escorting bombersthe relative size of these two is astonishing). I saw five different species of ducksmergansers, mallards, buffleheads, ring-necked ducks, and the first gorgeous wood ducks of the season (got good pics of all, too! Yes, I am obsessive, why do you ask?). And a beautiful prairie warbler, quite the tease, very keenly aware of where my camera was pointed, and pointedly staying one step ahead (well, mostly I got a couple of nice shots of him as well).
       </p>
       <p>
        And all of them are dinosaurs. Isnt that just astonishing?
       </p>
       <p>
        The dino in the pic above is one of my all time favorites, a black-crowned night heron. The first one I ever saw, I saw in Greece, at Lake Kerkini. This one, I saw well, lemme splain.
       </p>
       <p>
        I was getting my oil changed (well, the oil in my car), at an auto-service chain that will go unnamed for now, attached to a big-box store that will also go unnamed for now, that had apparently built on cheap land that had once been swamp. Or wetlands, if you want to be a tree-hugger. While my car was up on hydraulic lifts, I walked the perimeter of the parking lot; this shot was taken from the parking lot of a big-box store.
       </p>
       <p>
        Isnt it wonderful, what a zoom lens and cropping can do?
       </p>
       <p>
        What you dont see (mostly) is the horrendous treatment of the herons home. Discarded automobile tiresat least 8 that I saw. Bottles and cans too numerous to countmostly soda, but quite a bit more, including antifreeze and oil bottles. Insulated coffee mugs. Wheels from shopping carts. Hundreds of newspaper flyersno idea what was in the ink they used. An entire single-serving coffee machine, in pieces. Plastic bags by the score. Insulated foam containers. Not to mention, the runoff from the parking lot itself ran directly into the wetlands area, not into a storm sewerall the crap that leaks from cars on a regular basis was flowing right into that pond. Im certain Im leaving out as much as Im including.
       </p>
       <p>
        The red deally next to the heron? Near as I can tell, a plastic bread rack.
       </p>
       <p>
        Ill be writing to the owner of the big box store, and asking for responsible action. Failing that, I have plenty of pictures, and the addresses of the local papers.
       </p>
       <p>
        Weve used dinosaurs before, to warn us. The canary in a coal mine is a dinosaur warning system; weve seen the stomachs of starving albatrosses distended with plastics. Dinosaurs know extinction. Will we listen?
       </p>
