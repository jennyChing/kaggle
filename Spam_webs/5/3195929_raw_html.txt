     <div class="story-header-left twoCol">
      <h1 class="title" itemprop="name">
       Everything has changed since correction prediction: Acampora
      </h1>
      <div class="source" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <a href="/michelle-fox/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
        Michelle Fox
       </a>
       |
       <span class="twitter-url">
        <a href="http://twitter.com/MFoxCNBC">
         @MFoxCNBC
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <time class="datestamp" datetime="2014-06-24T08:41:29-0400" itemprop="datePublished">
       Tuesday, 24 Jun 2014 |  8:41  AM ET
      </time>
      <span class="sourcelogo" id="sourcelogo-cnbccom" itemprop="sourceOrganization">
       CNBC.com
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="story-header-right">
      <div>
       <div class="share-count-container hide">
        <div class="share-total">
        </div>
        <div class="share-text">
         SHARES
        </div>

    </div>
    <section class="cols2">
     <div class="unit col1">
      <article>
       <div class="story">
        <div class="content" id="article_body">
         <div class="group-container ">
          <div class="player embed-container cnbcvideo autoplay">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="player" data-src="http://player.cnbc.com/cnbc_global?byGuid=3000286905&amp;autoPlay=true&amp;endCardGuids=3000383239|3000383217|3000383353" frameborder="0" height="298" id="player_0_0" mozallowfullscreen="" msallowfullscreen="" oallowfullscreen="" scrolling="no" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="533">
            <p>
             Can't fight the tape: Acampora
            </p>
            <p>
             Dissecting current market volatility, with Ralph Acampora, Altaira; Phil Orlando, Federated Investors; Dani Hughes, Divine Capital; and CNBC's Dominic Chu.
            </p>
           </iframe>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            Ralph Acampora, known as "The Godfather of Technical Analysis," still thinks the stock market is extended and should be correcting, but he told CNBC's "
            <a href="/closing-bell/" target="_blank">
             Closing Bell
            </a>
            " that "everything has changed" since he predicted a 25 percent correction in May.
           </p>
           <p>
            "About 63 percent of the
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="599362" data-inline-quote-symbol=".DJI" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI" target="_blank">
             Dow
            </a>
            components are in strong, strong trends and no topping action, and about  8 out of 10
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="593933" data-inline-quote-symbol=".SPX" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX" target="_blank">
             S&amp;P
            </a>
            sectors are doing very, very well," he said Monday. "Of course the market is extended and should be correcting but I can't fight the tape and I love what I see."
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/06/23/dow-sp-headed-to-this-level-art-cashin.html" target="_self">
             Dow, S&amp;P headed to this level: Art Cashin
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="group-container last">
          <div class="embed-container image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
           <img alt="Ralph Acampora" height="298" src="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2013/10/22/101134066-7rmyc1fadqvemqhq20xh_r.530x298.jpg?v=1400522769" width="530"/>
           <meta content="http://fm.cnbc.com/applications/cnbc.com/resources/img/editorial/2013/10/22/101134066-7rmyc1fadqvemqhq20xh_r.jpg?v=1400522769" itemprop="image"/>
           <meta content="width='530'" itemprop="width"/>
           <meta content="height='298'" itemprop="height"/>
           <meta content="2013-10-22T15:34:56-0400" itemprop="datePublished"/>
           <meta content="2013-10-22T15:33:28-0400" itemprop="dateCreated"/>
           <meta content="2014-05-19T14:06:09-0400" itemprop="dateModified"/>
           <meta content="2013-10-22T15:33:28-0400" itemprop="uploadDate"/>
           <meta content="2099-12-31" itemprop="expires"/>
           <!-- TODO: defaulting to 2099 until we get the expiry date from toolbelt -->
           <meta content="jpg" itemprop="encodingFormat"/>
           <meta content="Source: Ralph Acampora | Twitter" itemprop="author"/>
           <meta content="CNBC" itemprop="publisher"/>
           <meta content="Ralph Acampora" itemprop="caption"/>
           <div class="attribution">
            Source: Ralph Acampora | Twitter
           </div>
           <div class="caption">
            Ralph Acampora
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="group" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            In May, Acampora told "
            <a href="/futures-now/" target="_blank">
             Futures Now
            </a>
            " that he had a "sick feeling" about the market, and predicted a 20-25 percent drop for the
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="21646410" data-inline-quote-symbol=".RUT2" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/.RUT2" target="_blank">
             Russell
            </a>
            ,
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="579435" data-inline-quote-symbol=".IXIC" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC" target="_blank">
             Nasdaq
            </a>
            and the
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="56179402" data-inline-quote-symbol="@1MD14M" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/%401MD14M" target="_self">
             S&amp;P MidCap
            </a>
            between then and October. He also predicted the
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="593933" data-inline-quote-symbol=".SPX" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX" target="_blank">
             S&amp;P 500
            </a>
            would fall 10-15 percent.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="label-read-more">
             Read More
            </span>
            <a href="/2014/05/15/i-have-sick-feeling-25-crash-is-ahead-acampora.html" target="_self">
             I have 'sick feeling' 25% crash is ahead: Acampora
            </a>
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Acampora, director of technical research at Altaira, said in this market, he'd look for stocks that are not overextended. That means turning to some "old" names like
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="25619" data-inline-quote-symbol="MSFT" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/MSFT" target="_blank">
             Microsoft
            </a>
            ,
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="18425" data-inline-quote-symbol="GE" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/GE" target="_blank">
             General Electric
            </a>
            ,
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="35171" data-inline-quote-symbol="TXN" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/TXN" target="_blank">
             Texas Instruments
            </a>
            ,
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="42624" data-inline-quote-symbol="CSCO" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/CSCO" target="_blank">
             Cisco
            </a>
            and
            <a class="inline_quotes" data-gdsid="21400" data-inline-quote-symbol="INTC" href="http://data.cnbc.com/quotes/INTC" target="_blank">
             Intel
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            "They're just starting to pick up, and I think they're playing a game of catch up," he said.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             By CNBC's Michelle Fox
            </em>
            <br/>
           </p>
