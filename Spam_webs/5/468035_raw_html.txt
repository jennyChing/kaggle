     <p>
      <b>
       Astoria
      </b>
      is the seat of
      <a href="/wiki/Clatsop_County,_Oregon" title="Clatsop County, Oregon">
       Clatsop County, Oregon
      </a>
      , United States.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-GR6-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Situated near the mouth of the
      <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">
       Columbia River
      </a>
      , the city was named after the American investor
      <a href="/wiki/John_Jacob_Astor" title="John Jacob Astor">
       John Jacob Astor
      </a>
      . His
      <a href="/wiki/American_Fur_Company" title="American Fur Company">
       American Fur Company
      </a>
      founded
      <a href="/wiki/Fort_Astoria" title="Fort Astoria">
       Fort Astoria
      </a>
      at the site in 1811. Astoria was incorporated by the
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Legislative_Assembly" title="Oregon Legislative Assembly">
       Oregon Legislative Assembly
      </a>
      on October 20, 1876.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-incorp_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-incorp-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Located on the south shore of the Columbia river, the city is served by the deepwater Port of Astoria. Transportation includes the
      <a href="/wiki/Astoria_Regional_Airport" title="Astoria Regional Airport">
       Astoria Regional Airport
      </a>
      with
      <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_30_in_Oregon" title="U.S. Route 30 in Oregon">
       U.S. Route 30
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_101_in_Oregon" title="U.S. Route 101 in Oregon">
       U.S. Route 101
      </a>
      as the main highways, and the 4.2-mile (6.8km)
      <a href="/wiki/Astoria%E2%80%93Megler_Bridge" title="AstoriaMegler Bridge">
       AstoriaMegler Bridge
      </a>
      connecting to neighboring
      <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">
       Washington
      </a>
      across the river. The population was 9,477 at the
      <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">
       2010 census
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010_census_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-2010_census-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

        <div class="magnify">

      </div>

     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Lewis_and_Clark_Expedition" title="Lewis and Clark Expedition">
       Lewis and Clark Expedition
      </a>
      spent the winter of 18051806 at
      <a href="/wiki/Fort_Clatsop" title="Fort Clatsop">
       Fort Clatsop
      </a>
      , a small log structure south and west of modern-day Astoria. The expedition had hoped a ship would come by to take them back east, but instead endured a torturous winter of rain and cold, then returned east the way they came. Today the fort has been recreated and is now a
      <a href="/wiki/Lewis_and_Clark_National_and_State_Historical_Parks" title="Lewis and Clark National and State Historical Parks">
       historical park
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Fur_Company" title="Pacific Fur Company">
       Pacific Fur Company
      </a>
      , a subsidiary of
      <a href="/wiki/John_Jacob_Astor" title="John Jacob Astor">
       John Jacob Astor
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/American_Fur_Company" title="American Fur Company">
       American Fur Company
      </a>
      , was created to begin fur trading in the
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Country" title="Oregon Country">
       Oregon Country
      </a>
      . Its primary fur-trading post
      <a href="/wiki/Fort_Astoria" title="Fort Astoria">
       Fort Astoria
      </a>
      was built in 1811, holding the distinction of being the first permanent U.S. settlement on the Pacific coast. It was an extremely important post for American exploration of the continent and was later used as an American claims in the
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon_boundary_dispute" title="Oregon boundary dispute">
       Oregon boundary dispute
      </a>
      with other European nations. British explorer
      <a href="/wiki/David_Thompson_(explorer)" title="David Thompson (explorer)">
       David Thompson
      </a>
      was the first European to navigate the entire length of the
      <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">
       Columbia River
      </a>
      in 1811. Thompson reached the partially constructed Fort Astoria at the mouth of the Columbia, arriving two months after the Pacific Fur Company's ship, the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tonquin" title="Tonquin">
        Tonquin
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      With the ongoing
      <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">
       War of 1812
      </a>
      , the Pacific Fur Company officers sold the company assets to their Canadian rivals, the
      <a href="/wiki/North_West_Company" title="North West Company">
       North West Company
      </a>
      in 1813. The house was restored to the U.S. in 1818, though the fur trade would remain under British control until American pioneers following the
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Trail" title="Oregon Trail">
       Oregon Trail
      </a>
      began filtering into the port town in the mid-1840s. The
      <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_1818" title="Treaty of 1818">
       Treaty of 1818
      </a>
      established joint U.S.  British occupancy of the Oregon Country. In 1846 the
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Treaty" title="Oregon Treaty">
       Oregon Treaty
      </a>
      divided the mainland at
      <a href="/wiki/49th_parallel_north" title="49th parallel north">
       49th parallel north
      </a>
      , the southern portion of Vancouver Island south of this line being awarded to the British.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Astoria_bridge.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2160" data-file-width="3840" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Astoria_bridge.jpg/220px-Astoria_bridge.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Astoria_bridge.jpg/330px-Astoria_bridge.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Astoria_bridge.jpg/440px-Astoria_bridge.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Astoria_bridge.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        AstoriaMegler Bridge
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Washington_Irving" title="Washington Irving">
       Washington Irving
      </a>
      , a prominent American writer with a European reputation, was approached by John Jacob Astor to mythologize the three-year reign of his Pacific Fur Company.
      <i>
       Astoria
      </i>
      (1835), written while Irving was Astor's guest, cemented the importance of the region in the American psyche.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In Irving's words, the fur traders were "Sinbads of the wilderness", and their venture was a staging point for the spread of American economic power into both the continental interior and into the Pacific.
     </p>
     <p>
      As the
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Territory" title="Oregon Territory">
       Oregon Territory
      </a>
      grew and became increasingly more colonised by Americans, Astoria likewise grew as a
      <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">
       port city
      </a>
      at the mouth of the great river that provided the easiest access to the interior. The first U.S. Post Office west of the
      <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">
       Rocky Mountains
      </a>
      was established in Astoria in 1847. In 1876, the community was incorporated by the state.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-incorp_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-incorp-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Astoria attracted a host of immigrants beginning in the late 19th century:
      <a href="/wiki/Nordic_countries" title="Nordic countries">
       Nordic
      </a>
      settlers, primarily Finns, and Chinese soon became significant parts of the population. The Finns mostly lived in Uniontown, near the present-day end of the AstoriaMegler Bridge, and took fishing jobs; the Chinese tended to do cannery work, and usually lived either downtown or in bunkhouses near the canneries. In 1883, and again in 1922, downtown Astoria was devastated by fire, partly because it was mostly wood and entirely raised off the marshy ground on pilings. Even after the first fire, the same format was used, and the second time around the flames spread quickly again, as collapsing streets took out the water system. Frantic citizens resorted to dynamite, blowing up entire buildings to stop the fire from going further.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:View_of_Astoria,_Oregon.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="420" data-file-width="863" height="107" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/View_of_Astoria%2C_Oregon.jpg/220px-View_of_Astoria%2C_Oregon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/View_of_Astoria%2C_Oregon.jpg/330px-View_of_Astoria%2C_Oregon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/View_of_Astoria%2C_Oregon.jpg/440px-View_of_Astoria%2C_Oregon.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>

       </div>
      </div>
     </div>

     <p>
      Astoria has served as a port of entry for over a century and remains the trading center for the lower Columbia basin, although it has long since been eclipsed by
      <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">
       Portland, Oregon
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">
       Seattle, Washington
      </a>
      , as an economic hub on the coast of the
      <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">
       Pacific Northwest
      </a>
      . Astoria's economy centered on fishing, fish processing, and lumber. In 1945, about 30 canneries could be found along the Columbia; however, in 1974
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bumble_Bee_(brand)" title="Bumble Bee (brand)">
       Bumblebee Seafood
      </a>
      moved its headquarters out of Astoria, and gradually reduced its presence until 1980 when the company closed its last Astoria cannery. The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timber_industry" title="Timber industry">
       timber industry
      </a>
      likewise declined; Astoria Plywood Mill, the city's largest employer, closed in 1989, and the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burlington_Northern_and_Santa_Fe_Railway" title="Burlington Northern and Santa Fe Railway">
       Burlington Northern and Santa Fe Railway
      </a>
      discontinued service in 1996.
     </p>
     <p>
      From 1921 to 1966, a
      <a href="/wiki/Astoria%E2%80%93Megler_Ferry" title="AstoriaMegler Ferry">
       ferry route across the Columbia River
      </a>
      connected Astoria with
      <a href="/wiki/Pacific_County,_Washington" title="Pacific County, Washington">
       Pacific County, Washington
      </a>
      . In 1966 the
      <a href="/wiki/Astoria%E2%80%93Megler_Bridge" title="AstoriaMegler Bridge">
       AstoriaMegler Bridge
      </a>
      was opened; it completed
      <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_101" title="U.S. Route 101">
       U.S. Route 101
      </a>
      and linked Astoria with Washington on the opposite shore of the Columbia, and replaced the ferries.
     </p>
     <p>
      Today, tourism, Astoria's growing art scene, and light manufacturing are the main economic activities of the city. It is a port of call for
      <a href="/wiki/Cruise_ship" title="Cruise ship">
       cruise ships
      </a>
      since 1982, after $10 million in pier improvements to accommodate cruise ships. To avoid Mexican ports of call during the
      <a href="/wiki/2009_flu_pandemic" title="2009 flu pandemic">
       Swine Flu outbreak of 2009
      </a>
      , many cruises were re-routed to include Astoria. The residential community
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_World_(cruise_ship)" title="The World (cruise ship)">
        The World
       </a>
      </i>
      visited Astoria in June 2009.
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition to the replicated Fort Clatsop, a popular point of interest is the
      <a href="/wiki/Astoria_Column" title="Astoria Column">
       Astoria Column
      </a>
      , a tower 125 feet (38m) high, built atop
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Coxcomb_Hill&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Coxcomb Hill (page does not exist)">
       Coxcomb Hill
      </a>
      above the town, with an inner circular staircase allowing visitors to climb to see a panoramic view of the town, the surrounding lands, and the Columbia flowing into the Pacific. The column was built by the
      <a href="/wiki/Astor_family" title="Astor family">
       Astor family
      </a>
      in 1926 to commemorate the region's early history.
     </p>
     <p>
      Since 1998, artistically-inclined fishermen and women from Alaska and the Pacific Northwest have traveled to Astoria for the
      <a href="/wiki/Fisher_Poets_Gathering" title="Fisher Poets Gathering">
       Fisher Poets Gathering
      </a>
      , where poets and singers tell their tales to honor the fishing industry and lifestyle.
     </p>
     <p>
      Astoria is also the western terminus of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TransAmerica_Trail" title="TransAmerica Trail">
       TransAmerica Trail
      </a>
      , a bicycle touring route created by the American Cycling Association.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Astoria is home to three United States Coast Guard ships: the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/USCGC_Steadfast_(WMEC-623)" title="USCGC Steadfast (WMEC-623)">
        Steadfast
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/USCGC_Alert_(WMEC-630)" title="USCGC Alert (WMEC-630)">
        Alert
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/USCGC_Fir_(WLB-213)" title="USCGC Fir (WLB-213)">
        Fir
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
       Geography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      According to the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">
       United States Census Bureau
      </a>
      , the city has a total area of 10.11 square miles (26.18km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ), of which, 6.16 square miles (15.95km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ) is land and 3.95 square miles (10.23km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ) is water.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">
       Climate
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Astoria,_Oregon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Astoria lies within the
      <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">
       Mediterranean climate
      </a>
      zone (
      <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Kppen climate classification">
       Kppen
      </a>
      <i>
       Csb
      </i>
      ), with very mild temperatures year-round, some of the most consistent in the
      <a href="/wiki/Contiguous_United_States" title="Contiguous United States">
       contiguous United States
      </a>
      ; winters are mild for this
      <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">
       latitude
      </a>
      (it usually remains above freezing at night) and wet. Summers are cool, although short heat waves can occur. Rainfall is most abundant in late fall and winter and is lightest in July and August. Snowfall is relatively rare, occurring in only three-fifths of years. Nevertheless, when conditions are ripe, significant snowfalls can occur.
     </p>
     <p>
      Astoria is tied with
      <a href="/wiki/Lake_Charles,_Louisiana" title="Lake Charles, Louisiana">
       Lake Charles, Louisiana
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur,_Texas" title="Port Arthur, Texas">
       Port Arthur, Texas
      </a>
      , as the most humid city in the contiguous United States. The average relative humidity in Astoria is 89% in the morning and 73% in the afternoon.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Annually, there is an average of only 4.2 days with temperatures reaching 80F (27C) or higher, and 90F (32C) readings are rare. Normally there are only one or two nights per year when the temperature remains at or above 60F (16C).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There are an average of 31 days with minimum temperatures at or below the freezing mark. The record high temperature was 101F (38C) on July 1, 1942. The record low temperature was 6F (14C) on December 8, 1972, and on December 21, 1990.
     </p>
     <p>
      There are an average of 191 days with measurable precipitation. The wettest year was 1950 with 113.34 inches (2,879mm) and the driest year was 1985 with 41.58 inches (1,056mm). The most rainfall in one month was 36.07 inches (916mm) in December 1933, and the most in 24 hours was 5.56 inches (141mm) on November 25, 1998.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NCDC-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The most snowfall in one month was 26.9 inches (68cm) in January 1950,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the most snow in 24 hours was 12.5 inches (32cm) on December 11, 1922.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">
       2010 census
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <p>
      As of the census

        </span>
        3
        <span>

      of 2010, there were 9,477 people, 4,288 households, and 2,274 families residing in the city. The population density was 1,538.5 inhabitants per square mile (594.0/km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ). There were 4,980 housing units at an average density of 808.4 per square mile (312.1/km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ). The racial makeup of the city was 89.2%
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">
       White
      </a>
      , 0.6%
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">
       African American
      </a>
      , 1.1%
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">
       Native American
      </a>
      , 1.8%
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">
       Asian
      </a>
      , 0.1%
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">
       Pacific Islander
      </a>
      , 3.9% from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">
       other races
      </a>
      , and 3.3% from two or more races.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">
       Hispanic
      </a>
      or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">
       Latino
      </a>
      of any race were 9.8% of the population.
     </p>
     <p>
      There were 4,288 households of which 24.6% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 37.9% were married couples living together, 10.8% had a female householder with no husband present, 4.3% had a male householder with no wife present, and 47.0% were non-families. 38.8% of all households were made up of individuals and 15.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.15 and the average family size was 2.86.
     </p>
     <p>
      The median age in the city was 41.9 years. 20.3% of residents were under the age of 18; 8.6% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 24.3% were from 25 to 44; 29.9% were from 45 to 64; and 17.1% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 48.4% male and 51.6% female.
     </p>

The average household size was 2.26 and the average family size was 2.93.

      <span class="mw-headline" id="Sister_cities">

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <p>
      Astoria has one
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sister_city" title="Sister city">
       sister city
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      as designated by
      <a href="/wiki/Sister_Cities_International" title="Sister Cities International">
       Sister Cities International
      </a>
      :
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <span class="flagicon">
        <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
         <img alt="Germany" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="1000" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/23px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/35px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/46px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png 2x" width="23"/>
        </a>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Walldorf" title="Walldorf">
        Walldorf
       </a>
       , Germany, which is the birthplace of Astoria's namesake,
       <a href="/wiki/John_Jacob_Astor" title="John Jacob Astor">
        John Jacob Astor
       </a>
       , who was born in Walldorf near Heidelberg on July 17, 1763. The sistercityship was founded on Astor's 200th birthday in 1963 in Walldorf by Walldorf's mayor Wilhelm Willinger and Astoria's mayor Harry Steinbock.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         26
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Warships_named_Astoria">
       Warships named
       <i>
        Astoria
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Astoria,_Oregon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Warships named Astoria">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Two
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Navy" title="US Navy">
       US Navy
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cruisers" title="Cruisers">
       Cruisers
      </a>
      were named USS
      <i>
       Astoria
      </i>
      :
      <a href="/wiki/USS_Astoria_(CA-34)" title="USS Astoria (CA-34)">
       a heavy cruiser (CA-34)
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/USS_Astoria_(CL-90)" title="USS Astoria (CL-90)">
       a light cruiser (CL-90)
      </a>
      . The former was lost in combat in August 1942 at the
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      Pacific ocean
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Savo_Island" title="Battle of Savo Island">
       Battle of Savo Island
      </a>
      , and the latter was scrapped in 1971 after being removed from active duty in 1949.
     </p>
