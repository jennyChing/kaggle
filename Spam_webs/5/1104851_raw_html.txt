       <h1 class="title">
        <a href="http://climbingnarc.com/2010/12/video-ish-friday-2010-reel-rock-film-tour-dvd/" rel="bookmark" title="Video-ish Friday:  2010 Reel Rock Film Tour DVD">
         Video-ish Friday:  2010 Reel Rock Film Tour DVD
        </a>
       </h1>
       <div class="post-meta">
        Posted
        <abbr class="date time published" title="2010-12-03T07:11:53+0000">
         December 3, 2010
        </abbr>
        at
        <abbr class="time published" title="2010-12-03T07:11:53+0000">
         7:11 am
        </abbr>
        
        <span class="post-comments comments">
         <a href="http://climbingnarc.com/2010/12/video-ish-friday-2010-reel-rock-film-tour-dvd/#comments">
          Comments { 8 }
         </a>
        </span>
        
       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         Looking for the perfect stocking stuffer for the climbing addict in your life?  Then look no further than the 2010 Reel Rock Film Tour which is now available on DVD and digital download.
        </p>
        <p>
         Unlike previous years which saw either
         <a href="http://biguprproductions.com" target="_blank">
          Big Up
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://senderfilms.com" target="_blank">
          Sender Films
         </a>
         debut a feature-length climbing movie like
         <em>
          <a href="http://climbingnarc.com/2009/10/review-big-up-productions-progression-dvd/" target="_blank">
           Progression
          </a>
         </em>
         or
         <em>
          <a href="http://climbingnarc.com/2008/10/the-sharp-end-dvd-review/" target="_blank">
           The Sharp End
          </a>
         </em>
         , this years tour featured 6 shorter films that I think did a good job appealing to a broad audience with their diversity.  If watching Daniel Woods, Paul Robinson or Chris Sharma throw themselves at the hardest climbs possible doesnt do it for you then maybe watching Lisa Rands step out of her comfort zone with Peter Croft on the beautiful Incredible Hulk is something that might interest you.  Otherwise there are plenty of laughs to be had watching the likes of James Pearson, Matt Segal, Cedar Wright and Heidi Wirtz epic in Australia and Tasmania, or you can watch Ueli Steck race his way up the Eiger literally one misstep from certain death.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2010 Reel Rock Tour Films
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           The Hardest Moves
          </strong>
           Daniel Woods &amp; Paul Robinson pushing to complete the hardest boulders ever scaled
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           First Round First Minute
          </strong>
           Chris Sharmas epic battle with the well-known Margalef testpiece
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           The Swiss Machine
          </strong>
           Ueli Stecks quest to take his one-man alpine speed game to the largest, highest walls in the world
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Origins: The Hulk
          </strong>
           Peter Croft &amp; Lisa Rands attempting a wild free ascent on the Incredible Hulk
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Down and Out, and Under
          </strong>
           Exploring wild climbing in Australia &amp; Tasmania with James Pearson, Matt Segal, Cedar Wright and Heidi Wirtz
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Fly Or Die
          </strong>
           Dean Potters extreme vertical exploration
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_10801" style="width: 550px">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/climbingnarc.com/wp-content/images/reel_rock_tour_jacket.jpg" rel="lightbox">
          <img alt="2010 Reel Rock Film Tour DVD Jacket" class="size-medium wp-image-10801 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/climbingnarc.com/wp-content/images/reel_rock_tour_jacket.jpg?resize=540%2C366" title="2010 Reel Rock Film Tour DVD Jacket"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          2010 Reel Rock Film Tour DVD Jacket
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         In addition to the 6 short films the DVD also includes the following bonus features:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Various Reel Rock Film Tour winners from over the years
         </li>
         <li>
          Beth Rodden &amp; Tommy Caldwell climbing in the Grand Canyon
         </li>
         <li>
          A brief clip of Ueli Steck and Alex Honnold climbing
          <strong>
           Separate Reality
          </strong>
          (5.12) in Yosemite, CA.  One of them does it with a rope and one doesnt.  Ill let you guess which is which.
         </li>
         <li>
          A nice piece following Daniel Woods and Paul Robinson at the 2010 Bouldering World Cup in Vail, CO which
          <a href="http://climbingnarc.com/2010/06/2010-teva-mountain-games-vail-bouldering-world-cup-final-results/" target="_blank">
           happened to be won by Woods
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="340" width="560">
          <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
          </param>
          <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
          </param>
          <param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/kwHwntiv-Pw?fs=1&amp;hl=en_US&amp;hd=1">
          </param>
          <param name="allowfullscreen" value="true">
          </param>
          <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="340" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/kwHwntiv-Pw?fs=1&amp;hl=en_US&amp;hd=1" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="560">
          </embed>
         </object>
        </p>
        <p>
         There are a few places to get the DVD for $29.95,
         <a href="http://www.bouldering.com/products/2010-Reel-Rock-Tour-DVD.html" target="_blank">
          one of which is Bouldering.com
         </a>
         where you can get free shipping and a free M-16 climbing brush if you order by Monday.  Otherwise
         <a href="http://www.bigupproductions.com/#/store/" target="_blank">
          Big Up
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://senderfilms.com/store.html#rrt" target="_blank">
          Sender Films
         </a>
         have the DVD and the downloads available at their respective stores.  The download will set you back $19.95 and 1.6 GB of hard drive space for the movie itself with the extras being an additional $6.95 and 1 GB.
        </p>
