    <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
     Ampersand Pumpkin (I may have lied to youaccidentally.)
    </h1>
   </header>
   <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
    <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     I did it guys.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     Im sorry, but I lied to you. I didnt mean to lie to you. It just
     <em>
      happened
     </em>
     .
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     When I posted the 7 simple &amp; frugal fall crafts, I said there probably wouldnt be many new fall craftsaround here  if any.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     And then I decided I needed a green pumpkinwith an ampersand symbol.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     Before I knew it, I was in the drivers seat and my car was practically steering itself to Michaels.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     <em>
      Story of my life.
     </em>
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     Hello Ampersand Pumpkin.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     <a href="http://rainonatinroof.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-crafts-ampersand-pumpkin-1.jpg">
      <img alt="Ampersand Pumpkin via RainonaTinRoof.com #fallcrafts" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7899" height="900" src="http://rainonatinroof.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-crafts-ampersand-pumpkin-1.jpg" width="600"/>
     </a>
     <span id="more-7757">
     </span>
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     Even though I caved and did a fall craft, luckily this is one of those pumpkins that will be around forever.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     Because its fake.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     Lets be honest, it may not always have an ampersand symbol, seeing as how I change things up often, but I didnt waste my time painting and poking holes in a pumpkin that will rot in a few weeks.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     I do wish though that they would start making these fake pumpkins with bigger stems. Someone needs to tell those people that bigger stems make a pumpkin prettier.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     I like big stems and I cannot lie.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     Preferably gold or glittered ones.
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: left;">
     <strong>
      Materials:
     </strong>
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      Pumpkin  Fake or real. Mine is a fake one from Michaels.
     </li>
     <li>
      Paint  I used
      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002BWOS2Q/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002BWOS2Q&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=raonatiro-20&amp;linkId=XSASWCCM25IC3PKD" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       this spray paint
      </a>
      *.
     </li>
     <li>
      Paper copy of an ampersand symbol  I just searched for one online, then saved it and printed it out.
     </li>
     <li>
      Scissors
     </li>
     <li>
      Decorative furniture tacks or thumb tacks  I used the furniture tacks because I had a lot laying around.
     </li>
     <li>
      Pencil
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Step 1:
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     Paint your pumpkin whatever color you want to. I went with green. It works with the rest of my decor. I dont really care if everyone else says that green is not a fall color and that I should be using orange, red, or yellow. Im a rebel like that.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Step 2:
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     After your pumpkin has dried, cut out your ampersand symbol. Place it on your pumpkin with a few of the tacks.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="http://rainonatinroof.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-crafts-2.jpg">
      <img alt="Ampersand Pumpkin via RainonaTinRoof.com #fallcrafts" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7897" height="667" src="http://rainonatinroof.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-crafts-2.jpg" width="500"/>
     </a>
    </p>
    <p>
     Take a pencil and trace around the ampersand. Be sure to trace on the inside of the symbol as well.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Step 3:
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     Take the ampersand off your pumpkin, but dont remove the tacks. Just tear it away from the tacks.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="http://rainonatinroof.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-crafts-3.jpg">
      <img alt="Ampersand Pumpkin via RainonaTinRoof.com #fallcrafts" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7898" height="667" src="http://rainonatinroof.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-crafts-3.jpg" width="500"/>
     </a>
    </p>
    <p>
     This just helps to give you a few anchor points. Fill in the symbol with the tacks.
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="http://rainonatinroof.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-crafts-ampersand-pumpkin-1.jpg">
      <img alt="Ampersand Pumpkin via RainonaTinRoof.com #fallcrafts" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7899" height="900" src="http://rainonatinroof.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/fall-crafts-ampersand-pumpkin-1.jpg" width="600"/>
     </a>
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     How easy was that?
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     Have you done any fall crafts with furniture or thumb tacks?
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center;">
     If you want more simple and frugal fall crafts, dont miss these
     <a href="http://rainonatinroof.com/2014/08/7-simple-frugal-fall-crafts/" target="_blank">
      seven fall craft ideas
     </a>
     or my
     <a href="http://rainonatinroof.com/2014/09/fall-home-tour-3/" target="_blank">
