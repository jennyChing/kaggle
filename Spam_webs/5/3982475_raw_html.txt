        <p class="p1">
         From summer 2015 Teignbridge is changing its waste and recycling services so it can collect even more recycling and food waste from your home every week. As long as that doesnt involve anything from your garden  which may include the back yard and even houseplants, presumably.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <span id="more-38623">
         </span>
         For the stunning sum of 35 a year, you can have your garden waste collected fortnightly in a subscription-only service. Its the exclusivity you pay for, and that chance to feel extra special.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         And if you opt in you get a sticker! Plus a newsletter (its the valued added stuff that softens the blow and makes the charge acceptable).
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Apparently, concessionary rates are available for those on Council Tax or Housing Benefit.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         If you refuse to opt in, you still get a new bin for food waste, to be collected weekly, itll slightly smaller than a recycling box with a handle and lockable lid.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         But before you get all uppity, you must realise, says Teignbridge, that councils are not legally obliged to collect garden waste free of charge, and weve been through difficult economic times.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         If you read that with a particular tone of sage-like condescension, then you are sure to be mollified and accept it with the same sagacity youve put up with for all the policies that youve faced under the austerity nomenclature.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         You can of course do your own composting, join a community compost group, or take your garden waste to the tip  we dont know if the tip will charge, yet.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Teignbridge residents will get a detailed information pack, (cue that condescension again) in the meantime, youre doing a great job of keeping us in the top 20 of all English authorities for recycling, so keep up the good work!
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Maybe its now appropriate to mis-quote Candide: lets not cultivate our garden  theres still time to concrete over it!
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
        </p>
