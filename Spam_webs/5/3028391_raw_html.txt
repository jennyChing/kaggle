     <h1 class="page-header" itemprop="name">
      "Rape Trees" Found Along Southern US Border
     </h1>
     <div itemprop="description">
      <article class="node node-article clearfix" id="node-11720">
       <header>
        <span class="submitted">
         By
         <span itemprop="author">
          <a class="username" href="/author/mariela-rosario" title="View user profile.">
           Mariela Rosario
          </a>
         </span>
         
         <span itemprop="dateCreated">
          March 11, 2009
         </span>
           4:00pm
        </span>
       </header>
       <div class="node-image-area float_left">
        <div class="node-image">
         <img src="/sites/default/files/styles/main-image-article/public/0311trees_article.JPG?itok=wE59ebBg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="field-name-field-photo-credit">
         <div class="field-item">
          Julian Cardona
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
        <div class="field-items">
         <div class="field-item even">
          <p>
           From beheading to kidnappings, there seems to be no limit to what the Mexican drug cartels are willing to do to assert their dominanceand they deal not only in drugs, but also in humans. The majority of the coyotes who help undocumented immigrants cross the border are affiliated with the cartels.
          </p>
          <p>
           Although many politicians would like to believe that the violence will stay to the south of the border, the reality is that it has already begun to affect South Western states. The revelation that Phoenix is now the "kidnapping capital" of the United States only affirms what many residents already believe.
          </p>
          <p>
           Now, a new method of marking territory has crossed over into the United States. "
           <a href="http://blogs.chron.com/txpotomac/2009/03/rape_victims_in_ice_detention.html" target="_blank">
            Rape trees
           </a>
           " are popping up in Southern Arizona and their significance is horrific.  These "rape trees" are places where cartel members and coyotes rape female border crossers and hang their clothes, specifically undergarments, to mark their conquest.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Sen. Jonathan Paton
           </strong>
           (R-Tucson), recently invited officials to describe the problems being faced in his home state to the Senate Judiciary Committee, which he chairs. Paton
           <a href="http://www.tucsoncitizen.com/ss/fromcomments/110843.php" target="_blank">
            said
           </a>
           violence along the border has escalated dramatically in the past year, "We want to go after these crimes," he insisted, "Its an unbelievable situation, and we cant allow that to go on in this country."
          </p>
          <p>
           For more info. visit
           <a href="http://www.truthout.org/article/price-admission-migrant-women" target="_blank">
            truthout.org
           </a>
           or
           <a href="/lifestyle/news-politics/steep-price-immigration-latin-american-women" target="_blank">
            read the follow up article at Latina.com.
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
