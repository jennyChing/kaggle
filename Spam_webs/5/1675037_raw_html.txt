            <div class="profile-intro">
             On more than 120 deep-sea expeditions, Robert Ballard has made many major natural discoveries, such as the deep-sea vents. Oh, and he found the Titanic.
            </div>
            <div class="section section--minor">
             <h3 class="h7 m5">
              Why you should listen
             </h3>
             <p>
              From an early age, Robert Ballard was intrigued by the deep. He's perhaps best-known for his work in underwater archaeology; in addition to
              <em>
               Titanic
              </em>
              , he has found the wrecks of the
              <em>
               Bismarck
              </em>
              , the USS
              <em>
               Yorktown
              </em>
              ,
              <strong>
               the nuclear sub
               <em>
                Thresher
               </em>
               (on
               <a href="http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/nova/transcripts/2602subsecrets.html" target="_blank">
                a top-secret mission for the Navy
               </a>
               -- for which the
               <em>
                Titanic
               </em>
               was his cover story)
              </strong>
              and John F. Kennedy's
              <em>
               PT-109
              </em>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              His contributions to our scientific knowledge of the ocean is just as awe-inspiring.
              <strong>
               He was in the first team of humans to view the deep-sea vents
              </strong>
              , and to understand how life can not only survive but thrive in these deep black waters, under extreme pressure and at extreme temperature.
             </p>
             <p>
              He's also a powerful storyteller and
              <strong>
               a passionate scientific educator
              </strong>
              . He founded the Institute for Exploration and has pioneered distance learning in classrooms around the world. Through his
              <a href="http://www.jason.org/public/home.aspx" target="_blank">
               JASON Project
              </a>
              , 1.7 million students a year join scientists virtually as they experience the thrill of exploration and discovery.
             </p>
             <h3 class="h7 m5">
              What others say
             </h3>
             <p>
              Mr. Ballard has long been blessed with the special luck of a successful explorer as well as the special knowledge of a leading scientist.New York Times
             </p>
