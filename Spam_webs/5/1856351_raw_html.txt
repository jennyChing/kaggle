    <h1>
     Can I Have A Solar Power System On My House And Still Be Connected To City Power?
    </h1>
    <br/>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Down Under, the most popular solar set-up is grid-connect. Why? Because it gives you the bountiful benefits of solar energy, while keeping connected to city power. Its the best of both worlds.
    </p>
    <p>
     What this means is your property is powered round-the-clock. You can tap into green energy when you want it, and access the grid when you need it. Of course, it also means your home isnt being powered by 100 percent green energy  and youll still have to pay increasing electricity costs.
    </p>
    <p>
     Lets take a closer look at this convenient set-up.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      What is grid-connect solar?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     It goes by a few names 
     <a href="http://www.3quotes.com.au/solar-power/cut-the-confusion-on-grid-off-grid-grid-tied-grid-connect.html">
      grid-connect, on-grid, grid-tied, grid-connect
     </a>
     . Notice a common theme? Yes, grid-connect is a solar photovoltaic (PV) system that is connected to the main power network in your area.
     <img alt="Grid connected solar power system" src="http://www.3quotes.com.au/content/images/grid-connected-solar-system.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: 270px; float: right;"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     When your system pulls power from the grid, youll see the charges on your next electricity bill.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      How does grid-connect solar work?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     In a grid-connect system, an inverter allows power to be sent to and drawn from the grid.
    </p>
    <p>
     When does electricity flow from your solar system to the grid? When its produced too much. That excess energy is transferred to city power for other residents to use. The bonus is that youre likely to receive a solar credit or other incentive for feeding electricity back to the grid.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      What happens at night?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     Are you ready for the great news? Because youre still hooked up to mains power, whenever theres a shortfall in your solar output, the system will be supplied with city power. So
     <a href="http://www.3quotes.com.au/solar-power/how-does-solar-power-work-at-night.html">
      after dark or during winter
     </a>
     , you wont have to worry about where to source energy to power your home.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      What happens during a blackout?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     And heres the not so great news. Because your home is connected to the grid, its vulnerable to power outages. And while some people mistakenly believe their solar system will replace mains power in an outage, the truth is it wont. Your system will be shut down to protect the safety of workers repairing the grid.
    </p>
    <p>
     While you can connect a back-up battery to your system for peace of mind, these are expensive, fiddly and bulky.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      Is it worth getting grid-connect solar?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     As you can see, there are some positives and pitfalls to installing a grid-connect solar. Take the time to consider whether a grid-tied system suits your energy needs and lifestyle  and chat to a solar expert about the options available for you.
    </p>
