        <header class="entry-header">
         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Doug Kessler: taking a stand  content marketing as evangelism
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          By
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.i-scoop.eu/author/iscoop/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
            <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
             J-P De Clerck
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="entry-comments-link">
           <a href="http://www.i-scoop.eu/doug-kessler-taking-stand-content-marketing-evangelism/#comments">
            <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="1407 http://www.i-scoop.eu/?p=1407">
             3 Comments
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="16232743" data-link="http://www.i-scoop.eu/doug-kessler-taking-stand-content-marketing-evangelism/" data-summary="" data-title="Doug Kessler: taking a stand  content marketing as evangelism">
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Everyone says
           <a href="http://www.i-scoop.eu/content-marketing/" title="Content marketing: the complete online guide">
            content marketing
           </a>
           is not about selling. Theyre wrong,
           <a href="http://www.velocitypartners.co.uk/who-we-are/doug-kessler/" target="_blank" title="Doug Kessler">
            Doug Kessler
           </a>
           says.
           <strong>
            Doug is Creative Director and co-founder of UK-based
            <a href="http://www.velocitypartners.co.uk" target="_blank" title="Velocity Partners">
             Velocity Partners
            </a>
            , a B2B marketing agency with a strong focus on content strategy. Doug is a displaced Yank who started his career at Ogilvy &amp; Mather, New York. Soap and fabric softener bored him rigid so he jumped ship to specialise in B2B.
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_412" style="width: 110px">
          <a href="http://contentmarketingexperience.i-scoop.eu/wp-content/uploads/sites/6/2014/07/Doug-Kessler.jpg">
           <img alt="Doug Kessler" class="size-full wp-image-412" height="118" src="http://contentmarketingexperience.i-scoop.eu/wp-content/uploads/sites/6/2014/07/Doug-Kessler.jpg" width="100"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Doug Kessler
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Content marketing is always about selling something critical, Doug emphasizes: your world view and your position on the critical issues that shape your market. Breakthrough content marketing always takes a stand. Its about steering well clear of the middle of the road (where the flattened squirrels are).
         </p>
         <p>
          At our 2014
          <a href="http://www.i-scoop.eu/content-marketing-experience-platform/content-marketing-conference-2014/">
           Content Marketing Conference
          </a>
          , the messages in Doug Kesslers great keynote were clear. Making a stand, among others, means evangelizing your perspective to create converts that will be much easier to sell to.
          <span id="more-1407">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Doug Kesslers content marketing advice
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            We asked Doug to share some practical content marketing tips and in the videos below he shares more views (watch them!).
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_411" style="width: 360px">
          <a href="https://twitter.com/TristanLavender/status/476290721486749696/photo/1" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Doug Kessler at the Content Marketing Conference Europe in Antwerp with a clear message - picture source" class="size-full wp-image-411" height="212" src="http://contentmarketingexperience.i-scoop.eu/wp-content/uploads/sites/6/2014/07/Doug-Kessler-at-the-Content-Marketing-Conference-Europe-in-Antwerp-with-a-clear-message-picture-source.jpg" width="350"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Doug Kessler at the Content Marketing Conference Europe in Antwerp  with a clear message 
           <a href="https://twitter.com/TristanLavender/status/476290721486749696/photo/1" target="_blank">
            picture source
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #ff6600;">
           <strong>
            Doug Kessler:
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ask yourself three questions:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           What content would I produce if I didnt need this job?
          </li>
          <li>
           What do I dare not say but really believe to be true?
          </li>
          <li>
           What could I say that would actively alienate our worst prospects while attracting our best?
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <em>
            We also asked him what is one of the most important ways that content marketing creates value for customers.
           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #ff6600;">
           <strong>
            Doug Kessler:
           </strong>
          </span>
          <strong>
           Buyers actually come to you for what you know more than for what you sell
          </strong>
          . (They just hope that what you know is built into what you sell). But if what you know is what they really, really want, content is a fantastic way to give it to them
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           The other value that content adds is that it increases peoples trust in you.
          </strong>
          And better outcomes come from a trust relationship. So the reassurance and confidence that content can instill is actually adding value.
         </p>
         <h2>
          More content marketing wisdom from Doug Kessler
         </h2>
         <p>
          Below are the two promised videos. Check them out. And also read our interview 
          <a href="http://www.i-scoop.eu/doug-kessler-cowardice-crippling-content-marketing-program/" title="Doug Kessler: cowardice is crippling to any content marketing program">
           Doug Kessler: cowardice is crippling to any content marketing program
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Content Marketing Experience Conference Europe 2014 interview part 1: on standing out with content marketing.
