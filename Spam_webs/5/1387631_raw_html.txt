                                   <h1 class="heading-story">
                                    Dozens killed in attacks on Nigerian villages
                                   </h1>
                                   <div>
                                    <h2 class="standfirst">
                                     Suspected Boko Haram fighters kill 48 people in north as US sends 80 army personnel to Chad to search for missing girls.
                                    </h2>
                                    <p class="meta">
                                     <time datetime="22 May 2014 05:31 GMT">
                                      22 May 2014 05:31 GMT
                                     </time>
                                     |
                                     <span id="article-topics">
                                      <a data-topic-name="War &amp; Conflict" href="/topics/categories/war-conflict.html">
                                       War &amp; Conflict
                                      </a>
                                      ,
                                      <a data-topic-name="Humanitarian crises" href="/topics/categories/humanitarian-crises.html">
                                       Humanitarian crises
                                      </a>
                                      ,
                                      <a data-topic-name="Africa" href="/topics/regions/africa.html">

                                      </a>
                                      ,
                                      <a data-topic-name="Nigeria" href="/topics/country/nigeria.html">
                                       Nigeria
                                      </a>

                                      <a data-topic-name="Boko Haram" href="/topics/organisations/boko-haram.html">

                                     </span>

                                 <div class="ArticleTopToolbar" id="Article-Top-Toolbar">

                                 <div class="article-body" id="article-body">
                                  <p>
                                   Suspected Boko Haram fighters have killed 48 villagers in northeasternNigeria near the town where the group kidnapped more than 270 schoolgirls.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   The three villages attacked overnight Tuesday and early Wednesday are nearthe town of Chibok, where the girls were abducted from their boarding schoolin a brazen April 15 assault that has ignited a global movement to securetheir freedom.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   The attacks came hours after twin car bombs exploded at a crowded bus terminal and market in Nigeria's central city of Jos, killing at least 122 people.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   Gunmen stormed the village of Alagarno late on Tuesday and stole food, razed homes and fired on fleeing civilians.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   "It was a sudden attack," said Alagarno resident Haruna Bitrus, in an account supported by other locals. "They began shooting and set fire to our homes. We had to flee to the bush," he added.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   Many of those who fled Alagarno ran to Chibok, where the armed group Boko Haram kidnapped 276 schoolgirls on April 14.
                                  </p>
                                  <hr/>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 16px; font-family: georgia, palatino;">
                                    <a class="internallink" href="http://www.aljazeera.com/indepth/features/2014/05/nigeria-life-schoolgirls-north-boko-haram-2014515111059956125.html" target="_blank">
                                     RELATED: Life for schoolgirls in the north
                                    </a>
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <hr/>
                                  <p>
                                   The nearby villages of Bulakurbe and Shawa were also attacked.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   There was no immediate claim of responsibility for eitherassault, but Boko Haram has either claimed or been blamed forscores of similar attacks in that part of Borno state, near thehilly border with Cameroon.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   President Barack Obama said on Wednesday that the
                                   <a class="internallink" href="http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2014/05/us-sends-troops-search-nigeria-girls-2014521215614820904.html" target="_blank">
                                    US had deployed
                                   </a>
                                   80 military personnel to neighbouring Chad to help in the search for the girls.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   He said the service members will help with intelligence, surveillanceand reconnaissance aircraft for missions over northern Nigeria. He said theforce would stay in Chad until its support is no longer necessary.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <strong>
                                    Children killed
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   Meanwhile in Jos,rescue workers with body bags combed the rubble for morebodies as scores of residents gathered at mortuaries and hospitals in thesearch for missing loved ones.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   Most victims were women and children who worked in the market as vendors,said Mohammed Abdulsalam of the National Emergency Management Agency.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   "Weexpect to find more bodies in the rubble," he said.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   Officials said that the bombs were concealed in a truck and a minibus. The second blast killed some of the rescue workers whorushed to the scene, which was obscured by billows of black smoke.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   The Nigerian government has been heavily criticised for failing to find the hundreds of girls who were taken from their school.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   Boko Haram has stepped up its use of explosives in attacks that are spreading far beyond its core area of operation, including two in Abuja last month.
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   Boko Haram, which claimed responsibility for the schoolgirls abduction in Chibok, has been trying to overthrow the government of Nigeria and establish an Islamic state. Thousands have been killed in the armed groups five-year uprising.
                                  </p>
