           <h1 class="title">
            Meet Hatch-chan, Japan's First Blogging Cat
           </h1>
           <!-- google_ad_section_end -->
           <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">
            <meta content="Meet Hatch-chan, Japan's First Blogging Cat" itemprop="headline"/>
            <meta content="Meet Hatch-chan, Japan's First Blogging Cat" itemprop="alternativeHeadline"/>
            <meta content="http://inventorspot.com/files/blog1/51TZZ0TJ4EL._SL500_AA240_.jpg" itemprop="image"/>
            <meta content="A black &amp; white cat named Hatch-chan has become a minor celebrity in Japan thanks to his popular blog. Yes, blog. Deal with it, LOLcats!" itemprop="description"/>
            <meta content="2014-07-07" itemprop="datePublished"/>
            <div class="node ntype-blog-2" id="node-12480">
             <!-- google_ad_section_start(weight=ignore) -->

              <div class="KonaBody">
               <span itemprop="articleBody">
                <!--paging_filter-->
                <p>
                 <span class="inline inline-left">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="164" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/prof-chara.jpg" title="" width="95"/>
                 </span>
                 Diary of a mad house cat?
                 <b>
                  Hatch-chan
                 </b>
                 , a six-year-old former stray cat, isn't so much mad as he is spoiled...
                 <i>
                  with attention
                 </i>
                 . And yes, he has a diary - an immensely popular
                 <a href="http://hatchannikki.blog107.fc2.com/" target="_blank">
                  blog
                 </a>
                 (in Japanese, yet) that gets
                 <b>
                  50,000 hits
                 </b>
                 a day. Dog-gone it, I feel inadequate!
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Hatch-chan didn't climb to the top of the Net on his own, of course. If it wasn't for his photographer owners he'd likely still be roaming around a park in Kobe, Japan, begging for his next meal.
                </p>
                <p>
                 But
                 <i>
                  no
                 </i>
                 - thanks to the promotional efforts, photographic skills and social networking savvy of Natsumi Fujiwara and her hubby, the two-toned tabby is the toast of the town.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <br/>
                 <span class="inline inline-middle">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-img_assist_custom" height="266" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/080407-1.img_assist_custom.jpg" title="" width="400"/>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <br/>
                 <span class="inline inline-left">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="80" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/prof-fuji.jpg" title="" width="80"/>
                 </span>
                 Nowadays Hatch-chan calls Osaka his home, and he has a nice-sized
                 <i>
                  (for Japan)
                 </i>
                 apartment to pussyfoot around in all day, looking out the window and doing the usual things house cats do.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Except of course, Fujiwara is around to document Hatch-chan's every move for each day's blog posting. Also for his calendar, fridge magnets, DVD and much, much more. It's like The Truman Show... with a kitty instead of a Carrey.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <br/>
                 <span class="inline inline-none">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="285" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/dvd-1.jpg" title="" width="202"/>
                 </span>
                 <span class="inline inline-none">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="284" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/dvd-2.jpg" title="" width="202"/>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <br/>
                 <span class="inline inline-left">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="170" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/hani.jpg" title="" width="170"/>
                 </span>
                 When Hatch-chan isn't busy chasing a ball of yarn or fawning for the camera
                 <i>
                  (or both)
                 </i>
                 , he's on tour with Fujiiwara at a department store, television show taping or a "meet the fans" event.
                </p>
                <p>
                 I watched one such
                 <i>
                  purr
                 </i>
                 -sonal appearance yesterday on the Japanese TV show
                 <b>
                  "Pets Pochi Tama"
                 </b>
                 , and it was a sight to see... the nonchalant Hatch-chan yawned, stretched and licked his, er, fur in front of an admiring crowd of fans brandishing cellphone cameras and full wallets. The latter were soon depleted by the relentless Hatch-chan merchandising machine.
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span class="inline inline-middle">
                  <img alt="" class="image image-_original" height="133" src="http://f00.inventorspot.com/images/shopcalendar.jpg" title="" width="275"/>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <br/>
                 It's the big time for sure, for this former stray. The only downside? He can only impress the female felines with his catnip-filled bank account - Hatch-chan was
                 <i>
                  neutered
                 </i>
                 in his early days as a stray and bears the characteristic triangular nip on his ear that signifies his cat-stration. Hmm, suddenly I don't feel quite so inadequate.
                 <i>
                  (via
                  <a href="http://hatchannikki.blog107.fc2.com/" target="_blank">
                   hatchan-nikki.com
                  </a>
                  )
                 </i>
                </p>
