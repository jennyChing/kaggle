             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               LOS ANGELES  They're both "
               <a href="https://twitter.com/BAHjournalist/status/573676781867155456" target="_blank">
                banged up
               </a>
               " after Thursday's crash.
              </p>
              <p>
               While Harrison Ford is "
               <a href="https://twitter.com/ChefBenFord/status/573649772583591937" target="_blank">
                battered, but OK
               </a>
               " and recovering from non-life-threatening injuries at a local hospital, the vintage plane he was piloting when it fell from the sky due to engine failure is still resting, broken, at a local golf course.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/03/05/harrison-ford-plane-crash/">
                 Harrison Ford 'battered, but OK' after high-impact plane crash at golf course
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               But being out of commission is not all the actor and the small Ryan Aeronautical ST3KR warbird aircraft have in common. Both entered this world in 1942.
              </p>
              <p>
               This flight wasn't the pair's first adventure, but it may be their last, as the WWII fighter plane is part of an overnight on-scene crash investigation, surrounded by glaring yellow caution tape on a grassy patch of Penmar Golf Course. Investigations like these can take months to a year to complete, with the aircraft sidelined as evidence in storage.
              </p>
              <p>
               Ford, 72, is a veteran pilot of private planes and helicopters, having started flight courses in the 1960s. But he only began flying solo at age 52.
              </p>
              <p>
               "Flying is like good music: It elevates the spirit and it's an exhilarating freedom," Ford
               <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/home/moslive/article-1251762/Harrison-Ford-I-love-flying-planes-riding-bikes-Thats-Ive-got-of-nine.html" target="_blank">
                explained
               </a>
               to the
               <em>
                Daily Mail
               </em>
               in 2010. "I was 52 when I started flying. ... Learning to fly was a lot of work, but the net result was a sense of freedom and a pleasure in seeing to the safety of myself and the people who fly with me."
              </p>
              <p>
               National Transportation Safety Board investigator Patrick Jones, during a Thursday evening press conference at the crash site, reiterated more than once that the pilot (he didn't mentioned Ford's name) was experienced, experienced, experienced.
              </p>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="harrison-ford-warbird" class="" data-caption="The wreckage of a plane piloted by Harrison Ford lies on Penmar Golf Course." data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="Splash News/Splash News/Corbis" data-credit-provider="" data-fragment="m!8004" data-image="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzAzLzA2LzY5L2hhcnJpc29uZm9yLjdjYjQ1LmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/b28c5dc7/5b1/harrison-ford-warbird.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="640" src="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzAzLzA2LzY5L2hhcnJpc29uZm9yLjdjYjQ1LmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/b28c5dc7/5b1/harrison-ford-warbird.jpg" title="harrison-ford-warbird"/>
               </p>
               <figcaption class="image-caption">
                <p>
                 The wreckage of a plane piloted by Harrison Ford lies on Penmar Golf Course.
                </p>
               </figcaption>
               <div class="image-credit">
                <p>
                 Image: Splash News/Splash News/Corbis
                </p>
               </div>
              </figure>
              <p>
               But experience isn't a safeguard against calamity; his high-impact golf course crash isn't even Ford's first brush with emergency landings. In 1999, the avid pilot made another emergency crash landing, that time in a helicopter, around Santa Clarita during a training flight due a failed flight maneuver, the
               <a href="http://www.airsafe.com/events/celebs/ford.htm" target="_blank">
                NTSB determined
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               To those who know him on film (
               <em>
                Indiana Jones, Star Wars, Air Force One
               </em>
               ), it should come as no surprise to learn he's a thrill-seeker offscreen.
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                <p>
                 Ford saved a hiker atop a 11,106-foot mountain in Wyoming, where he has a ranch, with his helicopter and flew her to a hospital in 2000.
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <p>
                 Ford veered into a telephone pole while speeding to work in a car in Orange County California, in 1964.
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <p>
                 Ford helped find a missing Boy Scout in Yellowstone National park, again by helicopter, in 2001.
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <p>
                 Ford piloted a plane that made yet another emergency landing, this time due to weather, at a Nebraska airport in 2000, damaging the aircraft's wings.
                </p>
               </li>
              </ul>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="harrison-ford-different-aircraft" class="" data-caption="Harrison Ford flies another one of his planes on July 29, 2014. " data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="Splash News/Corbis" data-credit-provider="" data-fragment="m!2350" data-image="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzAzLzA2LzY5L2hhcnJpc29uZm9yLjFlZTE5LmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/ccf955c1/b8c/harrison-ford-different-aircraft.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="640" src="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzAzLzA2LzY5L2hhcnJpc29uZm9yLjFlZTE5LmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/ccf955c1/b8c/harrison-ford-different-aircraft.jpg" title="harrison-ford-different-aircraft"/>
               </p>
               <figcaption class="image-caption">
                <p>
                 Harrison Ford flies another one of his planes on July 29, 2014.
                </p>
               </figcaption>
               <div class="image-credit">
                <p>
                 Image: Splash News/Corbis
                </p>
               </div>
              </figure>
              <p>
               But Ford's last mishap had nothing to do with adventure-seeking; he was
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/06/19/harrison-ford-broke-left-leg-star-wars/">
                breaking his left leg
               </a>
               in June on the set of
               <em>
                Star Wars: Episode VII
               </em>
               , a film in which he's reprising his role as Han Solo.
              </p>
              <p>
               There's no word on whether the crash will affect any of his upcoming projects, including a just-confirmed
               <em>
                Blade Runner
               </em>
               sequel. His June
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/harrison-ford/">
                injury altered production
               </a>
               on
               <em>
                Episode VII
               </em>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               All signs point to a full recovery, but no timetable has been revealed:
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                JUST IN: A statement from

               <p>

                 March 6, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                At the hospital. Dad is ok. Battered, but ok! He is every bit the man you would think he is. He is an incredibly strong man.
               </p>
               <p>
                 Chef Ben Ford (@ChefBenFord)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/ChefBenFord/status/573649772583591937" target="_blank">
                 March 6, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <h2>
               Details about Harrison Ford's wrecked plane
              </h2>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="harrison-ford-wwii-fighter-plane" class="" data-caption="Harrison Ford flies his Ryan Aeronautical ST3KR plane in September 2013. 
" data-credit-link="" data-credit-name="KAMINSKI/Splash News/Corbis" data-credit-provider="" data-fragment="m!d886" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzAzLzA2LzY5L2hhcnJpc29uZm9yLjY5OTUwLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/acfe9b45/9cc/harrison-ford-wwii-fighter-plane.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="640" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzAzLzA2LzY5L2hhcnJpc29uZm9yLjY5OTUwLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/acfe9b45/9cc/harrison-ford-wwii-fighter-plane.jpg" title="harrison-ford-wwii-fighter-plane"/>
               </p>
               <figcaption class="image-caption">
                <p>
                 Harrison Ford flies his Ryan Aeronautical ST3KR plane in September 2013.
                </p>
               </figcaption>
               <div class="image-credit">
                <p>
                 Image: KAMINSKI/Splash News/Corbis
                </p>
               </div>
              </figure>
              <p>
               Ford's fallen aircraft, the
               <a href="http://www.airport-data.com/aircraft/N53178.html" target="_blank">
                Ryan Aeronautical ST3KR
               </a>
               , was manufactured in 1942, and now is registered to MG Aviation in Delaware. It's a fixed-wing, single-engine plane that has separate bays for a pilot and a passenger, though Ford was alone during Thursday's crash.
              </p>
