        <h1>
         <a href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/2015/03/04/planning-for-business-success-getting-the-fundamentals-right/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Planning for business success: getting the fundamentals right">
          Planning for business success: getting the fundamentals right
         </a>
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="post-meta">
        <span>
         <i class="mini-ico-calendar">
         </i>
         <a class="published-time" href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/2015/03/04/planning-for-business-success-getting-the-fundamentals-right/" rel="bookmark" title="3:08 pm">
          March 4, 2015
         </a>
        </span>
        <span>
         <i class="mini-ico-user">
         </i>
         By
         <a class="author-link" href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/author/johndavies/">
          John Davies [Partner post]
         </a>
        </span>
        <span>
         <i class="mini-ico-comment">
         </i>
         With
         <a class="comments-link" href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/2015/03/04/planning-for-business-success-getting-the-fundamentals-right/#respond">
          no comments yet
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="tags">
         <i class="mini-ico-tag">
         </i>
         Tagged with:
         <a href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/tag/business-advice/" rel="tag">
          business advice

         <a href="http://www.businessadviceservice.com/tag/cashflow-management/" rel="tag">
          cashflow management
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="post-description">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         This is a post from our partner
         <a href="https://www.just-cashflow.com/home">
          Just Cashflow PLC
         </a>
         by John Davies, Director.
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/www.businessadviceservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/partner_justcashflow.png">
          <img alt="partner_justcashflow" class=" size-full wp-image-7691 alignright" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.businessadviceservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/partner_justcashflow.png?resize=200%2C89"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         I have always found it strange to compare starting a business with learning to drive. With one you have to study, take lessons and pass a test, while with the other you can just hang up a sign that says Open all Hours and off you go.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         A business and a car are both vehicles to help you get from A to B. If you dont know how to drive them properly, you will almost inevitably crash and often wreak havoc along the way.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         At Just Cash Flow PLC, we receive many excellent finance applications but we also talk to owners that clearly dontfully understand their own businesses and the numbers. A key part of our underwriting involves seeing how well anapplicantknows theirbusiness, financial position and future plans. If someone cant tell you the difference between a P&amp;L and a cash flowforecast it setsalarmbells ringing.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         One of the key questions we ask applicants is: Have you spoken to your accountant about this finance application and how you are planning to use these funds? Its often very clear to see those that have and those that have not.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         I strongly believe it is good practice for a business to first rehearse making the application with their accountant  who better to make sure they know the key business numbers?
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <strong>
          A university-led qualification?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Back in October I set a few hares running in an article where Icalled for a university led type of qualification for potential directors that included studying:
        </p>
        <p class="p3" style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Employment Responsibilities
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Finance
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Sales andMarketing
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Role and Responsibilities of Directors
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Planning
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         In the article I was responding to the question  What can businesses do to help themselves?
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         More than 30 years ago when I first started in business, I know I would have benefited from a better knowledge of key businessfundamentals; especially how managing your cash flow is all important.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         The fact is you dont know what you dont know.
        </p>
        <p class="p5">
         <strong>
          The pros and cons
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Response to the article fell neatly into two camps  those who thought it was an excellent idea and those who thought the last thing potential businesses needaremore barriers and bureaucracy.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Firmly in the yes camp was a lady I had great empathy with. She painfully remembered how, shortly after she started work, she saw the heartbreak caused by her employer going into administration and the redundancies that followed.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Among those not entirely in favour wasProfessor John Thanassoulis, Head of Executive Education at Warwick Business School, who felt entrepreneurs should be encouraged to have an idea and seek to make it a reality.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Placing bureaucratic hurdles in their way is not, in my view, a good idea, he said.
        </p>
        <p>
         A vibrant economy needs at least three things: entrepreneurs willing to start new businesses and take risks; informed and astute financial backers able to judge the quality of a company and offer useful guidance; and high quality corporate directors to oversee the strategic direction of a business.
        </p>
        <p>
         I think a National Directors Qualification would help with the second and third of these, but not the first.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <strong>
          Let creativity flourish
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         The Professor made some excellent points but I wasnt calling for more bureaucracy that would stifle small businesses and the next generation of entrepreneurs. Quite the opposite  lets give creative talent the foundations to build tomorrows successful businesses.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         I fully realise that a university-led qualification may prove too expensive or time consuming for some but it certainly got peoples attention. I was thinking along the lines of a distant learning course followed by a multiple choice type of exam.
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         There are plenty of good courses that teach business fundamentals. Also, you just have to type into any search engine cash flow, KPIs, finance, employment responsibilities etc. and there is a wealth of information to study and leave you better prepared to enter the business world.
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         <strong>
          Planning ahead
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         No matter how creative you are, enthusiastic, hardworking or bloody minded, you need to have an accurate plan and know how to adjust it if the desired result doesnt happen. Basics matter and learning this before starting your business would be a great investment.
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         The Professor also made the point about businesses needing to be able to access guidance from those with financial and business experience. I completely agree and there are already some excellent initiatives underway to facilitate this.
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         Just Cashflow PLC is a responsible specialist lender to UK businesses and I believe that responsibility stretches beyond simply making the right lending decisions. This is why it is important to raise the business education issue and why we actively support the ICAEW Business Advice Service and the GrowthAccelerator (now part of the Business Growth Service).
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <span class="s1">
          <a href="https://www.just-cashflow.com/home">
           Just Cash Flow PLC
          </a>
         </span>
         is a responsible specialist lender to UK Businesses that offers flexible funding solutions to help them grow. Director John Davies is a keen supporter of BAS and has strong views on the benefits of better guidance and education for businesses.
        </p>
