        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Raspberry Boccone Dolce (Chocolate and Raspberry Pavlova)
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         <abbr class="published" title="2015-02-12">
          February 12, 2015
         </abbr>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <div id="triberr_endorsement">
        </div>
        <p>
         I once queried an Italian speaking friend about the meaning of the Italian words, boccone dolce, wondering what aRaspberry Boccone Dolce (Chocolate and Raspberry Pavlova) was as thats the name Ive always known this recipe by. Ive been making this beautiful dessert for many, many years but had never thought to find out what Boccone Dolce meant before but truer words were never spoken. This dessert really is a sweet mouthful. Lovely clouds of meringue layered with chocolate, whipped cream and berries. Oh my.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-25198">
         </span>
         Also known as a Pavlova for the light layers of meringue mixed with whipped cream and berries, this dessert includes a layer of chocolate ganache; it is truly a stellar dessert for Valentines Day but its so easy we dont limit it to just one day of the year. Simply changing up the fruit can make it the perfect dessert for any holiday but seriously, why stop at holidays? I had never made it with anything but raspberries until one summer and must tell you that this
         <a href="http://www.creative-culinary.com/peach-melba-pavlova-with-caramel-sauce/">
          peach and caramel version
         </a>
         I served at a backyard barbecue was perfect with fresh summer peaches. I think my guests wanted to skip the burgers and head straight to dessert!So versatile and really so very, very good!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cf.creative-culinary.com/wp-content/uploads/raspberry-boccone-dolce-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Raspberry Boccone Dolce - The Perfect Pavlova from Creative-Culinary.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-25214" height="968" src="http://cf.creative-culinary.com/wp-content/uploads/raspberry-boccone-dolce-2.jpg" width="645"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Beautiful to look at (more so BEFORE you slice it!), wonderful to eat; its a dessert you will love to present but the truth isits pretty doggone easy too! Ive been swept up into the Macaron Madness a couple of times but making those lovely French meringue cookies has been an exasperation for me. So many factors influence their success and since I cant control humidity or altitude the results have been less than stellar. The truth is that Ive also never had one that seemed to make all of that effort worth itgive me this dessert in a heartbeat!
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Raspberry Boccone Dolce (Sweet Mouthful)
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://cf.creative-culinary.com/wp-content/uploads/raspberry-boccone-dolce-1.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.creative-culinary.com/easyrecipe-print/25198-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTimes">
          <div class="ERSTime">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Prep time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT20M" itemprop="prepTime">
             20 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTime ERSTimeRight">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Cook time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT50M" itemprop="cookTime">
             50 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTime ERSTimeRight">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Total time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT1H10M" itemprop="totalTime">
             1 hour 10 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClearLeft">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          A stunning dessert that is actually quite simple to prepare but will wow your guests!
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Barb
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6-8 Servings
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Meringues:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 eggs white at room temperature
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            pinch of salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp cream of tartar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups sugar
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Whipping Cream:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 cups whipping cream
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Tbsp vanilla extract
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Chocolate and Raspberry layers
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 ounces good chocolate, chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup half and half
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 to 1 pints fresh raspberries
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Preparation
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           To Make the Meringues:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Beat egg whites until frothy. Add salt and cream of tartar, beating until stiff. Gradually add sugar continuing to beat until the meringue is stiff and glossy.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Line cookie sheets with parchment paper and trace 3 circles with cake pan (I used the bottom of my springform pan), each 8 or 9 inches in diameter.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spread the meringue evenly over the circles and bake at 250 degrees for 35-50 minutes, or until the meringues are pale gold and still pliable. Turn off oven and leave in until ready to use or overnight.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            When ready to use, carefully peel the paper from the bottom.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           To Make the Pavlova:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Melt the chocolate with the half and half over low heat and whisk until smooth. Remove  cup chocolate and reserve for drizzling top.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Divide evenly on top of each meringue and smooth; refrigerate until chocolate has hardened a bit.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Beat the cream; gradually adding the sugar. Add the vanilla and beat until quite stiff.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place a small dollop of whipped cream on a serving plate and put one meringue on top.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spread approximately  of whipped cream over the chocolate. Top with  of the fresh berries.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Repeat with a 2nd and 3rd layer; pipe some whipped cream on top of berries and garnish with a few that have been reserved.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Warm reserved chocolate if necessary and drizzle over entire cake using either a fork or a ziplock bag with a tiny snip taken off of a corner.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           The longer you can let this dessert refrigerate before serving, the better. I recommend a 2 hour minimum but more is even better while making sure it is served the same day it is put together. That will give the soft and crunchy meringues time to absorb a bit of moisture and make cutting easier.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           BUT note...as beautiful as it is and as much as we love it, the beauty of the presentation is certainly not in the individual slices. No matter; I promise no one will complain as they lick their plates clean!
          </div>

        <h2>
         A trip WAY BACK!
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cf.creative-culinary.com/wp-content/uploads/raspberry-meringue-cake11.jpg">
          <img alt="Raspberry and Chocolate Pavlova" class="alignnone wp-image-25220 size-full" height="379" src="http://cf.creative-culinary.com/wp-content/uploads/raspberry-meringue-cake11.jpg" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I first published thisRaspberry Boccone Dolce (Chocolate and Raspberry Pavlova) on my blog back in the olden days of 1996. When my camera was a point and shoot and I took one quick snap before we devoured the food. I have loved the journey into photography and now look at some of these old photos with dismaybut I still want to keep them as a reminder and an encourager for me and maybe others!
        </p>
        <p>
         The good news thoughI obviously did not spend the time decorating much on the day of that barbecue and no matter; everyone LOVED the dessert. Ive since upped the volume on the meringue and the whipping cream but no decorating tips in your kitchen? Now worries, just dollop the whipped cream on with a spoon and smooth it a bit and youre covered!
        </p>
