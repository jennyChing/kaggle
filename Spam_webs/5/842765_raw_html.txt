       <h2 style="text-transform: none; font-size: 16px; letter-spacing: 1px; color: #ff6600;">
        <strong>
         Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary in India:
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        The Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary (NWS) is located in the Rewari district of Haryana State. It is approximately 50 km (31.06 miles) away from Rewari town, headquarters of Rewari district in Haryana.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         History of the NWS
         <br/>
        </strong>
        History of the NWS dates back to the period of 1987. Earlier to the formation of the NWS, the area is an old pasture land of Nawab of Dujana. It was managed as a Reserve Forest by the government of Haryana and declared as the Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary on 30
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        January 1987 with an aim to protect the species of Black Bucks living in the Reserve Forest. This sanctuary is named after 19th century Jat king Nahar Singh of Princely state of Ballabhgarh in Faridabad district.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Geographical Location of
        </strong>
        <strong>
         NWS
         <br/>
        </strong>
        The Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary is geographically situated on the south west of Rewari town in Haryana. The NWs covers an area of 2.11 sq km (0.81 sq miles) in Rewari district.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Flora and Fauna at
        </strong>
        <strong>
         NWS
         <br/>
        </strong>
        The Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary is considered as an eco-sensitive zone in Haryana. It is a home to different species of flora and fauna. Fauna of the sanctuary include different species of wild animals, birds and reptiles. Few of the important wild animals found in the sanctuary includes Black Bucks, Jackals, Foxes, Monitor Lizards and such other little animals.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Best time to visit NWS
        </strong>
        <strong>
         <br/>
        </strong>
        The Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary is open throughout the year. Visitors are advised to get in touch with officials of the NWS for complete information on entry timings and fee.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Accommodation
         <br/>
        </strong>
        Visitors can avail the accommodation facilities provided in the PWD Rest House at Rewari near the NWS by the government of Haryana. Visitors are advised to get in touch with the authorities of the NWS about accommodation, tariff and other facilities.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Contact Address of NWS
         <br/>
        </strong>
        Haryana Tourism Corporation Limited,
        <br/>
        SCO 17-19, Sector 17-B,
        <br/>
        Chandigarh,
        <br/>
        Haryana -160 017
        <br/>
        Phone: +91 17227 02955
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         How to reach
        </strong>
        <strong>
         the Nahar Wildlife Sanctuary
         <br/>
        </strong>
        <strong>
         By Road
        </strong>
        :The NWS is well connected by road from all places in Haryana. Bus Stand in Rewari is approximately 40 km (24.85 miles) away from the sanctuary. Bus and Taxi services are available to reach the sanctuary.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         By Rail
        </strong>
        :Nearest Railway Station is in Kosali, which is approximately 10 km (6.21 miles) away. Bus and Taxi services are available to reach the sanctuary.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         By Air:
        </strong>
        Nearest airport is in Delhi, which is approximately 100 km (62.13 miles) away. Bus and Taxi services are available to reach the sanctuary.
       </p>
