       <h1 class="entry-title">
        The benefits of triple glazing for your home
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="plain" href="http://blog.everest.co.uk/author/admin/">
           Everest Blog Team
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <div class="authorContext">
         <span class="author job">

         </span>

          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Triple glazing has long been the standard in colder countries like Scandinavia and Canada  and now the UK is starting to embrace this new technology.
          </strong>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         But why should you join the revolution and get triple glazing installed in your home? Weve outlined a few of the most important reasons below
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="/wp-content/uploads/r9frame.jpg">
          <img alt="Triple glazing breakout" class="size-medium wp-image-4440 aligncenter" height="239" src="/wp-content/uploads/r9frame-300x239.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Better U-values
        </h2>
        <p>
         The quality of insulation  or how much heat a material lets through  is measured in U-values. The higher the score, the more heat is allowed to escape through the surface.
        </p>
        <p>
         For example, a single-pane window would have a U-value higher than five, while a modern wall would typically score 0.3 or below.
        </p>
        <p>
         Current building regulations stipulate that new windows must have a U-value of 1.6  and double glazed windows typically come in at around that level.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.everest.co.uk/all-benefits/triple-glazing/">
          Triple glazed windows
         </a>
         take insulation to the next level. They typically score 0.8 on the U-scale, meaning that your window will be closer to a wall than a double glazed window in terms of heat retention  they are on average 35% more efficient than their double glazed counterparts.
        </p>
        <h2>
         A consistent temperature
        </h2>
        <p>
         Beyond the obvious advantage of greater efficiency, the fact that triple glazed windows retain heat that much better allows a consistency of temperature throughout your home  regardless of the number of windows in a room.
        </p>
        <p>
         Well-insulated walls in a dwelling without triple glazed windows can create a striking contrast in temperature, making a spot by the window feel that much colder. Triple glazing eliminates these cold spots, keeping your room as warm as possible without any extra effort on your part.
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Noise reduction
        </h2>
        <p>
         Those living in cities and busy towns can also take advantage of triple glazings other fantastic properties. In addition to improving your green credentials and temperature control, the extra pane of glass provides better protection from noise pollution, helping to keep disturbances to an absolute minimum.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, not only can Everest triple glazing help to cut your energy bills, but it can also eliminate cold spots, improve security and help cut back on unwanted noise.
        </p>
        <p>

          Do you have triple glazing? Are you thinking of getting triple glazing? Let us know your stories below
