           <h1>
            Killer whales surround 3 men on a dinghy
           </h1>
           <!-- &lt;h2&gt;&lt;/h2&gt; -->
           <div class="article-meta clearfix">
            <div class="meta-container clearfix">

          <div class="article-content clearfix">
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_133922" style="width: 630px">
            <img alt="Killer whales surround 3 men on a dinghy" class="wp-image-133922 size-full" height="432" src="http://cdn.grindtv.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Orcas-copy.jpg" width="620"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Killer whale named Bumper investigates Eric Martin and crew; photo by Alisa Schulman-Janiger
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            When Eric Martin, his son Cody, and David Lantos set out from the Southern California port of Redondo Beach in a 10-foot inflatable boat on Tuesday, they never imagined that theyd soon be surrounded by
            <a href="http://www.grindtv.com/search/killer+whale/" target="_blank">
             killer whales
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Or that the killer whales would develop a deep interest in their presence, and spend more than an hour mugging a boat so small that the Martins refer to it as the bathtub. (Mugging is when the mammals repeatedly swim up to a vessel.)
           </p>
           <p>
            <a class="videoLinkEmbed videoYouTube videoYouTube-utpJRejrZNI" data-autoplay="false" data-height="340" data-thumbnail="" data-videoid="utpJRejrZNI" data-width="620" href="//www.youtube.com/embed/utpJRejrZNI?feature=player_detailpage" style="display:none;" target="_blank" title="YouTube Embed">
             //www.youtube.com/embed/utpJRejrZNI?feature=player_detailpage
            </a>
            <script type="text/javascript">
             try{jQuery("a.videoLinkEmbed.videoYouTube-utpJRejrZNI").first().pbEmbed();}catch(e){}
            </script>
           </p>
           <p>
            It was as close of an encounter with orcas as can be experienced in the wild without physical contact, an experience Cody, 18, described as possibly the best moment of my life.
           </p>
           <p>
            This interaction occurred off the Palos Verdes Peninsula, involving four transient orcas cataloged as the CA51s, in such calm, clear water, that the trio was able to capture incredible footage from above and below the surface.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_133930" style="width: 630px">
            <img alt="killer whale" class="wp-image-133930 size-full" height="437" src="http://cdn.grindtv.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/4b-ORCAS-2015-01-06-Alisa-Schulman-Janiger-IMG_6674-c2-cr.jpg" width="620"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Killer whale named Orion approaches the dinghy; photo by  Alisa Schulman-Janiger
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            It was an experience Ill never forget, said Eric Martin, co-director of the Roundhouse Marine Studies Lab and Aquarium in Manhattan Beach. And to be honest, I didnt realize how small we looked until he saw an image captured by researcher Alisa Schulman-Janiger (image is atop this post).
           </p>
           <p>
            Schulman-Janiger, who runs the California Killer Whale Project, was aboard the
            <a href="http://2seewhales.com" target="_blank">
             Triumphant
            </a>
            , a
            <a href="http://www.grindtv.com/search/whale+watching/" target="_blank">
             whale-watching
            </a>
            boat out of Long Beach. She said this magical footage was the best she had ever seen of killer whales off California.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a class="videoLinkEmbed videoYouTube videoYouTube-Uv7pqqbXwQI" data-autoplay="false" data-height="340" data-thumbnail="" data-videoid="Uv7pqqbXwQI" data-width="620" href="//www.youtube.com/embed/Uv7pqqbXwQI?feature=player_detailpage" style="display:none;" target="_blank" title="YouTube Embed">
             //www.youtube.com/embed/Uv7pqqbXwQI?feature=player_detailpage
            </a>
            <script type="text/javascript">
             try{jQuery("a.videoLinkEmbed.videoYouTube-Uv7pqqbXwQI").first().pbEmbed();}catch(e){}
            </script>
           </p>
           <p>
            The underwater footage is serene, showing several close approaches and revealing vocalizations as the orcas communicate with each other. The topside footage provides a human element and reveals the type of chaos associated with being mugged by orcas in a dinghy. It also includes strange raspberry sounds made by the orcas at the surface.
           </p>
           <p>
            The CA51s are more commonly seen off Monterey, in Central California, but have visited Southern California for four consecutive years in January. (The three previous sightings were on New Years Day.)
           </p>
           <p>
            This family group included CA51, or Star, the mother; CA51C, or Bumper an 11-year-old male; CA51B, or Orion, a 16-year-old male; and CA51D, or Comet, a 4-year-old female.
           </p>
           <p>
            Like all transient orcas, the CA51s feed on other marine mammals, and during their
            <a href="http://www.grindtv.com/search/southern+california/" target="_blank">
             Southern California
            </a>
            visits they typically feed on California sea lions.
           </p>
           <p>
            The CA51s are famously boat friendly, which made for a precarious situation when Martin first saw them as he approached the Palos Verdes Peninsula in his dinghy, after the initial sighting by shore-based volunteer spotters working for the
            <a href="http://acs-la.org/seewhales2.htm" target="_blank">
             ACS-LA Gray Whale Census and Behavior Project
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            The orcas decided to investigate them as soon as they saw the bathtub in the distance. Eric Martin, who has been visited by these same orcas while he was aboard a larger boat, killed the motor, wisely, once they were close.
           </p>
           <p>
            We all thought they were just going to swim under the boat, he said. Little did I realize that the mother and Comet would decide to stop, face to face, just inches from David, who was holding the
            <a href="http://www.grindtv.com/tag/gopro/" target="_blank">
             GoPro
            </a>
            camera, and stare right at us.
           </p>
           <p>
            The orcas swam away briefly, and harassed some sea birds, before returning for more human interaction.
           </p>
           <p>
            Cody, in disbelief, told his father, I cant believe Im actually making eye contact with a killer whale.
           </p>
           <p>
            Said Eric, Just when you thought it couldnt get any better, they started vocalizing. You could hear them even at the surface. It was an unforgettable experience.
           </p>
           <p>
            Perhaps it was for the orcas as well. They were last seen Thursday off Orange County, heading slowly to the northwest.
           </p>
