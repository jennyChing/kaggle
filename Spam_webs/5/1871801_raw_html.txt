        <p>
         <em>
          This shop has been compensated by Collective Bias, Inc. and its advertiser. All opinions are mine alone. #PourMoreFun #CollectiveBias

        <p>
         Bring your summer gatherings to life with
         <a href="http://cbi.as/8hy1" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Kool-Aid Easy Mix
         </a>
         . From Chicken Tenders to Cherry Ice Cream, weve got you covered with fun, creative recipes both adults and children will enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-id="7019" href="http://livingthegourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Kool_Aid_Punch_5.jpg">
          <img alt="Living the Gourmet: Tropical Punch Spritzer" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7019" height="3121" src="http://livingthegourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Kool_Aid_Punch_5.jpg" width="2340"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Summer season, for all its lazy days, is more often than not an exercise in relaxed chaos. The warm weather brings with it poolside lounging and easy meals prepared over an open grill, but as the saying goes, it takes hard work and planning to find time to relax. That has hardly ever been truer, as the season so far has been a busy one where I am, and where I am, the season has not even started.
        </p>
        <p>
         Between juggling family get-togethers, hosting friends for semi-formal luncheons, and partaking of local kayaking events, relaxed summer chaos has been the pleasant order of the day  all while polishing my grill techniques; still thoroughly rusted by winter.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-id="7027" href="http://livingthegourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/1430750947.821526.IMG_6486.jpg">
          <img alt="Living the Gourmet: Kool-Aid Summer" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7027" height="3128" src="http://livingthegourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/1430750947.821526.IMG_6486.jpg" width="2346"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Through all of it, how are those of us more likely to be the host rather than the hosted supposed to indulge in our own bit of warm weather lounging?
        </p>
        <p>
         With invisible shortcuts, of course.
        </p>
        <p>
         Allow me to elaborate. Over the years, Ive found that one of the most tedious tasks the season may pose is aptly balancing the adults and the children, especially when it comes to seasonal fare. How well this balancing act is performed will largely determine how successful the gathering will be.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-id="7017" href="http://livingthegourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Kool_Aid_InStore1.jpg">
          <img alt="Living the Gourmet: Kool-Aid Easy Mix Ins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7017" height="2000" src="http://livingthegourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Kool_Aid_InStore1.jpg" width="2118"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Due to host my own family get-together, I was in the midst of plotting a balancing act of my own. During my last stop at Walmart for assorted supplies, I noticed the Kool-Aid Easy Mix display, and thats where it clicked. In that single instant, I realized I wouldnt have to worry about crafting separate dishes or strip down original recipes. Instead, I could craft something that the entire family would enjoy equally, albeit for very different reasons.
        </p>
        <p>
         To begin, I wanted something that was both finger food, as well as portable, basically something that could be carried around a yard, consumed standing up, or even packed up for a picnic or taken on a boat. I decided on oven fried chicken tenders.
        </p>
        <p>
         To accompany the tenders, I decided to do something that was both a little different, and a whole lot of fun. After infusing sour cream with grape Kool-Aid, I then mixed in dill, fresh lemon juice, and honey. The result was a sweet but slightly tangy dipping sauce, whose unmistakable base of grape sweetness clashes with the natural tang of the sour cream, with the entire experience mellowed by the addition of honey, while the dash of dill adds a vague elegance.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-id="7023" href="http://livingthegourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Kool_Aid_Chicken_Tenders_7.jpg">
          <img alt="Living the Gourmet: Chicken Tenders w/ Grape Dipping Sauce" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7023" height="3128" src="http://livingthegourmet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Kool_Aid_Chicken_Tenders_7.jpg" width="2346"/>
         </a>

            1 tablespoon vanilla
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            14 oz. condensed milk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 tablespoons Kool-Aid Easy Mix Cherry
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup maraschino cherries, halved
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            16 oz. whipping cream
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Kool-Aid Cherry Sauce:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon cornstarch
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup Kool-Aid Easy Mix Cherry
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tablespoons water
           </li>
          </ul>

           Instructions

           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a large bowl whip the whipping cream.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a separate bowl whisk together vanilla, salt, Cherry Kool Aid and the condensed milk.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Fold the whipped cream into the condensed milk mixture.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place into a container and place in the freezer for one hour before adding the halved cherries. Once the ice cream has chilled and thickened a little, stir in halved Maraschino cherries. Return to freezer for at least 5  6 hours.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Kool-Aid Cherry Sauce:
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a small saucepan over low heat, whisk cornstarch, Kool-Aid and water until the mixture thickens into a sauce. Drizzle over ice cream before serving. Top with whipped cream and a cherry.
           </li>
          </ol>
