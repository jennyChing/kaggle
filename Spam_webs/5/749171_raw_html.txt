        <span>
         Sculpture
        </span>
       </h1>
       <div class="archive-meta">
        <p>
         Its hard to define which LEGO models are sculptural and which are just a regular pile of bricks, but we know a good sculpture when we see one  even if its a funny cartoon character and not the Venus de Milo.
        </p>
       </div>

         <a href="http://www.brothers-brick.com/2015/07/13/sean-kenney-builds-7-25-foot-tall-eiffel-tower-out-of-lego/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Sean Kenney builds 7.25 foot tall Eiffel Tower out of Lego">
          Sean Kenney builds 7.25 foot tall Eiffel Tower out of Lego
         </a>
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.seankenney.com/">
          Sean Kenney
         </a>
         s latest Lego sculpture captures the magnificence of the Eiffel Tower in a giant model using 67,470 Lego bricks. At 7.25 feet tall, its even taller than the average NBA player! Check out the creation on
         <a href="http://mocpages.com/moc.php/415991">
          MOCpages
         </a>
         to see detail photos and behind-the-scenes shots of this iconic monument.
        </p>

      <article class="post-57108 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-lego category-models tag-movies tag-sculpture" id="post-57108">
       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         <a href="http://www.brothers-brick.com/2015/05/22/in-this-robbery-your-money-is-safe/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to In this robbery, your money is safe">
          In this robbery, your money is safe
         </a>
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Admittedly most of my knowledge about the Toy Story movies is from the LEGO sets, but you dont have to be a fan to recognise the Train Chase, by
         <strong>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/jaredchan/">
          </a>
          Jared Chan
         </strong>
         , as a neat little model.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/jaredchan/17297541164" title="Toy Story - Money Train Chase Coin Bank (2015) by Jared Chan, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Toy Story - Money Train Chase Coin Bank (2015)" height="427" src="//c2.staticflickr.com/6/5455/17297541164_d4d471284d_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The train is simply adorable and it hides a neat feature: it also serves as a piggy bank. I do have some doubts whether it can actually keep your money safe, however, no matter how strong the clutch between LEGO elements may be.
        </p>

      <article class="post-56151 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-lego category-models tag-character tag-movies tag-sculpture tag-superheroes" id="post-56151">
       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">

          Chappie: Dont laugh, Im being cool
         </a>
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         A slight digression  Ive been meaning to see the Chappie movie for a while now and finally got around to it last night. And of course like any good builder, I spent an inordinate amount of time during the movie mentally figuring out how I could build him when I got home. Ive seen a few other LEGO Chappies built already, but not a really big detailed one. It would have been my time to shine! And maybe even get blogged!
        </p>
        <p>
         So I was super stoked to try  I got home, broke out my Dark Blues, got the Orange antennae built and just gave up. It would have been too large and too complex and too difficult. But thankfully for everyone, Roman (
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/mypeanut/">
         </a>
         <b>
          th_squirrel
         </b>
         ) didnt give up as easily as I did. Much like Chappie himself, he kept on going and built a super awesome LEGO Chappie:
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/mypeanut/16662391940/" title="CHAPPiE by th_squirrel, on Flickr">
          <img alt="CHAPPiE" height="640" src="//farm9.staticflickr.com/8669/16662391940_28ef916f67_z.jpg" width="426"/>
         </a>
        </center>
        <p>
         This is just a thing of beauty. I also really love how Roman used pieces with the original stickers on them, it really adds that gangster touch to his LEGO Chappie.
        </p>

          CUB3  a rolling ball sculpture
         </a>
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Those who went to Brickworld in Chicago and Tampa last year probably remember this crazy cuboid contraption by Tyler (
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/legohaulic/">
          Legohaulic
         </a>
         ). Built using an abundance of Technic elements and flex tubing, this creation features conveyor belts that lift ball bearings and lets them drop through one of 4 spiraling and twisting tracks. Check out the masterfully produced video to see it in action.
