        <div id="content-area">
         <p>
          Drinking craft beer: awesome. Paying $10+ for a 6-pack: not so cool. You might associate homebrewing with hipsters, or assume anything that ferments in someones basement cant possibly taste any good, but brewing your own beer can save you a lot of money, taste genuinely good (certainly a million times better than a Bud/Miller/Coors/Whatever Lite), and is a lot of fun. Heres a list of the things youll need in order to get started*:
         </p>
         <p>
          *If you get a
          <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/click?id=isIkAyUyNbM&amp;subid=&amp;offerid=289463.1&amp;type=10&amp;tmpid=11998&amp;RD_PARM1=http%253A%252F%252Fwww.northernbrewer.com%252Fshop%252Fbrewing%252Fbeer-equipment-starter-kits" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           pre-packaged starter kit
          </a>
          , a lot of this will be included, but its still good to know what it is youre buying and what it all does.
         </p>
         <h2>
          1.Malt Extract Ingredient Kit ($25 to $40)
         </h2>
         <p>
          An ingredient kit will have all the grains, fermentables, hops, yeast and priming sugar youll need for 5 gallons of beer, along with instructions on when and how to put everything together.
          <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/click?id=isIkAyUyNbM&amp;subid=&amp;offerid=289463.1&amp;type=10&amp;tmpid=11998&amp;RD_PARM1=http%253A%252F%252Fwww.northernbrewer.com%252Fshop%252Fnut-brown-ale-extract-kit.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           A simple brown ale
          </a>
          is a good place to begin when youre just starting out. Once you have done a few batches, you can start to get a bit more creative by adding additional ingredients and/or tweaking the recipes to fit your taste (for example, I recently started with a wheat kit and added blueberries and fresh mint  it was delicious).
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/white_house_honey_ale_kit.jpg">
           <img alt="homebrew_kit" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5741" height="600" src="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/white_house_honey_ale_kit.jpg" width="800"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          2. Heat Source (Free to $60)
         </h2>
         <p>
          The top of your stove will work just fine for brewing, but you could also go for something like this
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Brinkmann-815-4001-S-Turkey-Fryer/dp/B002HEL5QA/bensbargaicenter" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           turkey fryer
          </a>
          if you want to brew outside. I suppose a grill would even work  basically anything that can hold (and get hot enough to boil) a 5 gallon stockpot.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/turkey.jpg">
           <img alt="homebrew_turkeycooker" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5744" height="1024" src="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/turkey.jpg" width="765"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          3. Stock Pot/Brewing Kettle (Around $30)
         </h2>
         <p>
          Speaking of stockpots, youll need one. This is what you cook the beer in. Most people start with a
          <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/click?id=isIkAyUyNbM&amp;subid=&amp;offerid=289463.1&amp;type=10&amp;tmpid=11998&amp;RD_PARM1=http%253A%252F%252Fwww.northernbrewer.com%252Fshop%252F5-gallon-stainless-kettle.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           5 gallon pot
          </a>
          , but they come as big as 25 gallons for the semi-professional brewers out there. If you buy one from a homebrew shop, it will be called a brewing kettle or something similar, but any old stock pot will work just fine. (FYI, they have them at thrift stores all the time).
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/kettles.jpg">
           <img alt="homebrew_kettle" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5745" height="382" src="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/kettles.jpg" width="700"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          4. Fermenter ($10 to $40)
         </h2>
         <p>
          The fermenter is the container the beer sits and ferments in (ie: turns alcoholic) for anywhere from one week to three months. The cheapest version is a
          <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/click?id=isIkAyUyNbM&amp;subid=&amp;offerid=289463.1&amp;type=10&amp;tmpid=11998&amp;RD_PARM1=http%253A%252F%252Fwww.northernbrewer.com%252Fshop%252F6-5-gallon-fermenting-bucket.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           plastic bucket with an airlock
          </a>
          and the standard version is a plastic or
          <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/click?id=isIkAyUyNbM&amp;subid=&amp;offerid=289463.1&amp;type=10&amp;tmpid=11998&amp;RD_PARM1=http%253A%252F%252Fwww.northernbrewer.com%252Fshop%252F5-gallon-glass-carboy.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           glass carboy
          </a>
          (pictured).
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/6-gallon-glass-carboy_2.jpg">
           <img alt="homebrew_carboy" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5746" height="700" src="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/6-gallon-glass-carboy_2.jpg" width="700"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          5. A Good Brewing Book ($10ish)
         </h2>
         <p>
          The more you know about the brewing process, the more youll be able to play around and experiment. The
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/The-Complete-Homebrewing-Third-Edition/dp/0060531053/bensbargaicenter" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Complete Joy of Homebrewing
          </a>
          is a good place to start  its a really broad guide that covers everything from equipment to basic instructions to recipes.
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Alaskan-Bootleggers-Bible-Leon-Kania/dp/0967452406/bensbargaicenter" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           The Alaskan Bootleggers Bible
          </a>
          is another fun one  super odd and goes over not just beer-making but how to make your own wine, cider,moonshine whiskey, bathtub gin
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/beer_bible.jpg">
           <img alt="beer_bible" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6825" height="600" src="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/beer_bible.jpg" width="418"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          6. A Capper and Empty Beer Bottles ($15 to $20)
         </h2>
         <p>
          Nobody wants a flat beer. The cheapest way to fizz your beer up is to start saving/collecting beer bottles (only the fancy beers will do; twist-off bottles wont work). Then buy a
          <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/click?id=isIkAyUyNbM&amp;subid=&amp;offerid=289463.1&amp;type=10&amp;tmpid=11998&amp;RD_PARM1=http%253A%252F%252Fhome-brewing.northernbrewer.com%252Fsearch%2523w%253Dcapper%2526asug%253D" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           capper
          </a>
          to put caps on your beer once you transfer it to bottles (which will create carbonation in a week or two).An alternative is to get a
          <a href="http://click.linksynergy.com/fs-bin/click?id=isIkAyUyNbM&amp;subid=&amp;offerid=289463.1&amp;type=10&amp;tmpid=11998&amp;RD_PARM1=http%253A%252F%252Fwww.northernbrewer.com%252Fshop%252Fbrewing%252Fkegging%252F5-gallon-keg-system-new-new-keg.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           kegging system
          </a>
          (around $200); it will eliminate the need for caps and bottles altogether.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/capper.jpg">
           <img alt="beer_capper" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6826" height="600" src="http://bensbargains.net/thecheckout/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/capper.jpg" width="800"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <address>
         </address>
         <p>
          If after all this you find yourself thinking, You know what, I think Id rather buy my beer and not mess with this. What am I going to do with all this crap if I dont end up liking brewing? The answer is Craigslist. Brewing gear is in high enough demand that you wont lose much should you decide to sell it after giving it a try. Basically, theres a lot of potentially awesome beer to gain and not a lot to lose, so go for it!
         </p>
        </div>
