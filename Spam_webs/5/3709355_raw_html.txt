         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Why Traveling as a Mountain Biker is Worth the Oversize Baggage Fees
         </h1>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          There is a moment on every trip when I wish I wasnt a mountain biker. Sometimes this moment comes when Im having a staredown with the toad-faced woman behind the American Airlines counter.
          <em>
           Will she notice that every single one of our bags is slightly overweight? That we have three carry-on items each? Is she bribe-able?
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="A typical scene... " class="size-full wp-image-1876" height="640" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/10344801_815467150175_411283296756695865_n.jpg" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Sometimes the moment arrives earlier, when I am trying to drag my bike bags onto a bus and then inevitably something falls out and thwacks me in the shin. Often, it happens even before that, during the packing process, when Macky and I reiterate the same argument we have been having ever since we started traveling together. It goes like this:
         </p>
         <p>
          Macky: Were almost done, I just need to get in a few more tires.
          <br/>
          Me: (sensing danger) How many tires?
          <br/>
          Macky: Just these. (Points to a pile of 30 tires.)
          <br/>
          Me: You dont need that many tires.
          <br/>
          Macky: Yes, I do.
          <br/>
          Me: No, you dont.
          <br/>
          Macky: (Proceeds to list all the ways he is capable of destroying a tire, which are numerous.)
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="whyMTBtires" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1877" height="612" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/whyMTBtires.jpg" style="border:none;" width="612"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          You get the picture. Traveling as a mountain biker is, at the outset, A GIGANTIC PAIN IN THE ASS. But, that said, I cant imagine traveling any other way.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres why:
         </p>
         <h2>
          Mountain biking gets you to amazing places.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Sure, sure, hiking gets you to pretty cool places, too. But when you hike to the top of a mountain, you encounter a problem: you have to WALK down the mountain. Seriously, walking downhill has got to be one of the stupidest things ever. If you dont know what I mean, you obviously havent ridden a mountain bike down a mountain. There is just no comparison.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1880" style="width: 1510px">
          <img alt="Riding in La Parva, Chile. (Photo: Dogman Photos) " class="size-full wp-image-1880" height="1000" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/whyMTBdogman.jpg" width="1500"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Riding in La Parva, Chile. (Photo: Dogman Photos)
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1881" style="width: 1510px">
          <img alt="Wellington, New Zealand" class="size-full wp-image-1881" height="1125" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/whyMTBnelson.jpg" width="1500"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Wellington, New Zealand
          </p>
         </div>
         <h2>
          You dont have to read those dumb how to stay fit on vacation articles.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Thats right, no hotel room burpees for you. Just grab your bike, go have fun all day and burn more calories than most people burn in a week (or a month or their entire lifetimes) Let other people worry about staying in shape. You just worry about having funand, um, not whacking your head on a rock.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="1974988_807519128075_462718796000458320_n" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1882" height="640" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/1974988_807519128075_462718796000458320_n.jpg" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <h2>
          You can eat whatever you want (and you will want to eat everything).
         </h2>
         <p>
          Prepare to sample every single local delicacy and then some, because you will be starving and probably eating upwards of 4,000 calories a day. Mind you, this can be a bit of a financial inconvenience, but with a little finagling (like eating baked beans straight out of the can, as shown below) it can be done. Just remember that you will have to eat at least every hour so dont go ANYWHERE without food.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1883" style="width: 2058px">
          <img alt="Budget lunch... " class="size-full wp-image-1883" height="1536" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/1026116_3893297748486_1217331686_o.jpg" width="2048"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Budget lunch
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <img alt="whymtbfoood" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1884" height="640" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/whymtbfoood.jpg" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <h2>
          And, most importantly, if you travel as a mountain biker, you will always have friends.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Making friends abroad is not easy. There are language barriers and cultural differences to take into account, plus the fact that people tend to be wrapped up in their own thing with their own schedules and their own friends. Its hard to break in. Mountain biking changes everything. The bike community has welcomed me without fail on three continents. I have found places to stay, people to ride with, dinner invitations and friends for life. The fact of the matter is that mountain bikers, no matter where they are from, are always interested in other mountain bikers. They want to hear about your trails and then they want to show you why theirs are better. While I loooove being able to eat constantly and stand on epic mountain tops, this is the real reason I bring my bike with me wherever I go.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1874" style="width: 1510px">
          <img alt="Bike buddies in Argentina..." class="size-full wp-image-1874" height="698" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/whyMTBarg.jpg" style="border:none;" width="1500"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Bike buddies in Argentina
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1873" style="width: 1510px">
          <img alt="And Peru...  (Photos by Jaime Zapata) " class="size-full wp-image-1873" height="964" src="http://www.freewheellifeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/whymtbperu.jpg" style="border:none;" width="1500"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           And Peru  (Photos by Jaime Zapata)
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <em>
           What about you? Have you traveled with your mountain bike? Are there other similar communities for travelers?
          </em>
         </p>
