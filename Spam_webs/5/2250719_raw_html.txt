           </span>
           <span class="crd ntb">

             0+ / 0-

          <div class="ct">
           <p>
            While I agree with some of the chart, there are (at least) a few errors in it that I was able to detect.
           </p>
           <p>
            Note that the deletion of funds for the home heating program was proposed by Obama.
            <a href="http://whitehouse.blogs.cnn.com/2011/03/08/democratic-reps-protest-obama-budget-cuts/">
             http://whitehouse.blogs.cnn.com/...
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            On ethanol subsidies, the Administration (through EPA) as a favor to the big ethanol producers backed a rule to permit greater use of ethanol in vehicles, from the current 10% to 15%.
            <a href="http://www.examiner.com/green-transportation-in-national/ethanol-support-divided-by-geography-and-not-politics">
             http://www.examiner.com/...
            </a>
            The House voted to block the increase. The voting was along geographic lines, farm state representatives voting nay because they did not want the new rule blocked. 206 Republicans and 79 Democrats voted (in the public interest) to kill the EPA increase. The effort in the Senate to kill the direct ethanol subsidy is being led by Ben Cardin (D-MD) and Tom Coburn (R-OK). It's not really an R versus D issue as far as I can tell. I predict that if the item comes up separately in the Senate, the vote will be along geographic lines.
           </p>
           <p>
            The $5.2 billion for raising the limit on itemized deductions for high-income taxpayers had bi-partisan support, and was driving much of the tax bill from last year. This is the alternative minimum tax issue, which was affecting middle class families (especially those with high mortgage payments). The history of the AMT is discussed here.
            <a href="http://www.hvpress.net/news/119/ARTICLE/10084/2011-03-09.html">
             http://www.hvpress.net/...
            </a>
            The AMT was originally designed to make sure really high income people could not use deductions to remove much of their tax liability. But the formula was never changed so inflation meant families with about $72k income. In many areas of the country that is a middle class family income. In some areas, it's at the lower end of the middle class. The bad thing that the GOP did was to hold the AMT hostage to get its way on the estate tax and other provisions. (Not all Democrats are for an estate tax. Tim Kaine, for one, opposes them. I agree with the general liberal view on this and think there should be a stiff estate tax.)
           </p>
           <p>
            I do not know the substance of the education programs on the chart that are said to be good, but just because they have buzz words like assistance and education in them does not mean they will work. Have you read any recent data on Head Start? It does not work.
            <a href="http://www.edweek.org/ew/articles/2010/01/14/18headstart.h29.html?tkn=NVNF5tWv7iyPs69q%2FjEdMCXxKujsUv2bTsNp">
             http://www.edweek.org/...
            </a>
            That's HHS saying this. After 45 years of pouring money into this glorified daycare, it can't be shown to have lasting effects.
           </p>
           <p>
            Rather than having a whole slew of federal programs of doubtful effectiveness, I would much rather guarantee a living minimum income to all and let individuals decide how best to spend the money. So, if Head Start is just an expensive and inefficient provider of child care, let a qualifying family use that money to buy child care and use the surplus for something else. I certainly have no faith that the bureaucracies behind these programs are necessarily acting with the public interest in mind.
           </p>
           <p>
            Finally, the chart's class warfare nomenclature is not helpful and just perpetuates the good/evil dualistic thinking in which both parties specialize.
           </p>
