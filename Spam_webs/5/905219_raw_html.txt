                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       Media partners or large gaming websites will be busy to release trailers and previews for the games since they have got dollars from developers. This can take many forms including pre-orders, page views, youtube views, comments etc. The games will be promoted throw all kinds of channels. T
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       hen the page views and unique visitors or other performance for the advertisement will be tracked. If the number presented is satisfactory, the developer would throw more money into advertising. On the contrary, they will take measures, for example, changing the game name or sound effects to let the game rush into the market again.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       It seems that the developers are happy enough only if the advertising effects indicates to be satisfactory such as the pv and uv is high, the advertising page was hit a lot, the video targeted a large number of people, etc. Although there are indeed many people got the game information, are you sure they will become the targeted players or they will take the further action? Maybe there are majority of people who
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       received
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       the game information
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       just -
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       "
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       passively" so they won't play games and they won't spent any, of course. There are also potential players but how to define "potential"?
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ATvQmh69JIo/VFn1PidG3jI/AAAAAAAAAcw/GY-KyMizOFI/s1600/advertising1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="advertising to browser games" border="0" height="295" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ATvQmh69JIo/VFn1PidG3jI/AAAAAAAAAcw/GY-KyMizOFI/s1600/advertising1.jpg" title="advertising to browser games" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       Never did i take action just because i have watched an advertisement. At most, from the repeated advertisements, i remembered the brands or products. What i think the main function of advertising is exposure which can just generate profit indirectly.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       In total, Advertising is not the only answer for browser games. It indeed helps but it's not worth throwing large amount of dollars on. It's better to spent the money that in the first place arranged for placing advertising on games themselves like reducing game price or imposing more benefits for player. The browser game developers and publishers shouldn't rely on completely on advertising since the audience is not precised but costly and the effect can't directly transferred into profit.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: arial, sans, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>

                       For more about browser games, please pay close attention to my blogs:
                       <a href="http://gameonwebs.blogspot.com/">
                        http://gameonwebs.blogspot.com
                       </a>
