         Ok, here are the trends:
1. Content is being made available outside of the Cable walled garden
2. Kids are watching most content via non-TV means...look at your children ad their friends, where are they getting content now. My kids get more than half from their laptop, tablet and AppleTV/Roku. Why care about the kids, because these are your paying customers with expectations in the future. Mine are now 18 and over so they are the new paying consumers.
3. New Advertising models with targeted ads make this more attractive because device are assigned to specific users making it easier to target.
4. Traditional Set Tops are being virtualized. The new emerging software technologies allow for this, this adds fuel to the fire. In fact TWC is rumored to be working on a virtual Set Top for the AppleTV (there are others doing similar things).
5. Broadband is more tuned for video content. The basic infrastructure is getting better at transporting video streams (multicast is still an open issue because of legacy equipment in the network, but streaming via HLS [Apple] and HTML5 [Google] are moving forward aggressively.

We're at the critical juncture where all the stars are aligning and monitization is emerging.

       <div class="classDivNewText" id="divNewTextTwo">
        <div class="classTextOpinionDetails">
         Cable companies are feeling the heat from online service providers like Netflix, Hulu, and other online content providers. While these services are a great supplement to cable companies, they will never be able to replace them. As of right now, though, the cable companies are their own worst enemy, but with a slight change of perspective they will be right back on top. But what should cable companies do to bridge the gap? The answer is simple: Offer customers access to their own content through streaming services online.

DISH Network already has the right idea with their DISH Anywhere service. You can log into your account and access live TV as well as programming recorded by your DVR. Comcast is also on the right track with their Xfinity TV services, but they dont yet offer access to live TV. The idea should really be a no-brainer, especially since most cable companies already double as internet providers anyway.

Cable companies also need to provide leaner subscription bundles with the ability to personalize content and offer a la carte premium services. I love Netflix and all the great content they have available, but at the end of the day, they are just another channel and to be honest there are many times I spend more times trying to find something to watch than actually watching something. So come on cable, get with the times and dont make yourself go the way of the dinosaur.
