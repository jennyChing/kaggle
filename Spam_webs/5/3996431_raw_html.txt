      <article class="post-5334 post type-post status-publish format-standard category-centsible-diy tag-bathroom tag-centsible-diy-2 tag-money tag-remodeling tag-sponsored entry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Mini Bathroom Remodel
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-07-19T06:00:36+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          July 19, 2011
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/author/kelly_w/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Kelly
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/mini-bathroom-remodel/#comments">
           3 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Renovating is not a quick process no matter how well your plans are laid, and my mini bathroom makeover this weekend was no exception. I planned a mini-makeover to help de-moldify the bathroom, and make it more liveable for the meantime since a full gut isnt in the budget, yet.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mini makeovers are a great way to change a space enough to suit you for now without having to spend a bundle. That can be as simple as a coat of paint, or some new linens on your bed. Weve done mini makeovers to several areas of our house including our kitchen, and theyve been a great way to hold back my inner Destructo from ripping everything apart. (ok that still happens sometimes, just call me the
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iyvPjn2m240" target="_blank">
          Tasmanian Devil
         </a>
         of remodeling).
        </p>
        <p>
         Our hall bath is our only functioning full bathroom. The master bathroom has a mini makeover that turned into a leaky issue, one that requires a full gut, and since that isnt quite in the budget yet, weve been using the hall bath for all 6 of us. Using only one of our two
         <a href="http://www.wdbathrooms.co.uk/bathrooms-suites.html" target="_blank">
          bathrooms
         </a>
         is definitely frustrating to say the least! Needless to say a 40 year old bathroom (blue since you know that was the thing back then to have colorful bathrooms-the other bath is an avocado mess) hasnt stood that well. Its in need of a full gut too, but in the meantime I figured a good wall washing, some mold resistant paint, and a new light and curtain would be enough to spruce the place up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Previous Work
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         My Dad had already done some work while we were in
         <a href="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/2011/06/san-francisco-bound/" target="_blank">
          San Francisco
         </a>
         (gotta love handy retired people!) including adding an awesome shower rod which makes the space in the shower more open and I dont have to stare at the rusted/painted rod anymore. He also caulked all along the tub and corners, replaced the shower head and the faucets, and added this super nifty shower caddy that actually doesnt move! (Ok so clearly he did a lot-but I paid him in hugs &amp; kisses from the grandkids so its a win all around) Taking
         <a href="http://www.wdbathrooms.co.uk/showering/showers.html" target="_blank">
          showers
         </a>
         has been a much more pleasant experience with those little touches but the mildew was kinda wrecking the whole calm and relaxed vibe.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-5342 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/IMG_2936-223x300.jpg" title="Shower caddy" width="223"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-5341 aligncenter" height="301" src="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/IMG_2934.jpg" title="Shower Curtain Rod" width="403"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The Plan
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         My plan was simple. Wash down the walls (that were mildewed and yucky) with a bleach/water solution (and I get that its not exactly a healthy thing but nothing else seems to work), and paint it all white with a special bathroom paint. While I was at it I decided to frost the window a la Young House Love since it would allow more sunlight and air to get in the bathroom and also help with the mildew issue. I liked the idea of having no curtain, but we couldnt leave it bare since you can see the sidewalk, sidestreet and neighbors from that window (flashing just isnt my thing). I decided while I was at it I would replace the light fixture that was literally rusty (ew).
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Execution
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Step one went fine. Wiping down the walls=easy and free. Step two was ok too. Picked up paint and got painting after spackling and sanding a few spots that desperately needed it. Im no pro at painting but Ive done enough of it to be ok at it. I painted the cabinet while I was at it (though you can still some scuff marks) and the back of the door since our doors are all hollow core with wood layered on top. (in other words hideous)
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-5343 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/IMG_2937-223x300.jpg" title="Cabinets" width="223"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-5337 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/IMG_2927-223x300.jpg" title="Back of door" width="223"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then I decided with some guidance from my hubby I wold replace the light fixture. Off went the power (and the entertainment for the kids-egads!), and I got to work. Only oops, the box in the wall was not at all the right kind of box, and the light fixture was going to hang sideways if we tried to hang it. Rather than admit defeat (we should have), we started to pull out the box, only surprise! (and not the good kind) the outlet was attached to a sistered board which yay! decided to come off.
        </p>
        <p>
         We were left with no choice but to make a bigger hole and pull out the smallish board (thankfully it bumped right up against the medicine cabinet about 6 inches below it so maneuvering it out wasnt a huge issue. Next we had to replace the drywall and the box before hanging the fixture. We managed to do all of the above, but the patch isnt ideal, and the light fixture isnt really hanging properly (poor design in some way because the screws and covers just dont fit together right).
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5338" style="width: 624px">
         <img alt="" class="size-large wp-image-5338 " height="458" src="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/IMG_2928-1024x764.jpg" title="Light fixture" width="614"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Pretty(and it's missing glass covers since I'm afraid they will fall off!)
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Lets end this on a high note though. After the light was installed I moved on to the window. Using a kit I purchased at Lowes (and possibly some curse words) and the help of my willing assistant (my 9 year old) I was able to frost the glass using the film I purchased. I loved the way it looked so much I opted not to frost the top of the window. You cant see anything from ground level with the bottom frosted and I love being able to see the tops of the trees and the night sky.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-5340 aligncenter" height="224" src="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/IMG_2933-300x224.jpg" title="Frosted window" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-5339 aligncenter" height="300" src="http://www.thecentsiblelife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/IMG_2929-223x300.jpg" title="Top of window frosted" width="223"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Total Cost
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         My cost for this mini remodel was $30 for paint and supplies for painting, $38 for the light fixture, $2.50 for the rack on the back of the door, and $30 for the privacy film (which I still have plenty of leftover for reuse in our master bathroom). Total: just over $100!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whats Next?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Well be calling in some help to get the light fixed, and add a ceiling fan to the bathroom to further help the mold/mildew issue. The issue is mainly that what seems like a simple project turns into a big one because the fixtures and things in our home are so outdated. (see also the vent cover which is totally not standard. My mini-remodel (once again) is going to be another project. Next time I mention wanting to spruce something up, please remind me of the 17 billion other projects I have already started. My husband will thank you.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>

           Kelly
