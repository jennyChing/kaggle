              <img alt="3-way home" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-18101" height="367" src="http://blog.waveavenue.com.s167390.gridserver.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/3wayhomefeaturingarockclimbingwall.jpg" width="550"/>

            <p>
             This two-storey private house was designed by
             <a href="http://www.naf-aad.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
              naf architect &amp; design
             </a>
             (tetsuya nakazono, akio nakasa).What makes this 3-way home in Tokyo, Japan fantastic is its very unique rock climbing wall that scales a central courtyard.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://blog.waveavenue.com.s167390.gridserver.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/3wayhomefeaturingarockclimbingwall2.jpg">

             </a>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              Cubic in form, the free-standing wooden structure is simple and unassuming in its exterior expression,
              <br/>
              exhibiting textured facades with rectangular windows interspersedly marking their surface. a large
              <br/>
              opening from the street level provides a sheltered car park which connects into the multi-storey courtyard
              <br/>
              at the core of the plan. treating the generous rooftop as an additional room, a two-meter high parapet
              <br/>
              circumscribes the terrace for both safety as well as to meet the local height regulation of 7 meters.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
