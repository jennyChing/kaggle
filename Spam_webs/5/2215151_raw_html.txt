                  &lt;div class="step-text"&gt;&lt;p&gt;Bring the milk to the boil, then remove from the&nbsp;heat and add the&nbsp;butter. Leave to cool until it&nbsp;reaches hand temperature. Put the flour,&nbsp;salt,&nbsp;sugar and yeast (see Tip on recipe) into a bowl.&nbsp;Make a well in&nbsp;the centre. Pour in the warm milk&nbsp;and butter mixture, then add the&nbsp;egg. Using a&nbsp;wooden spoon, mix well, then bring everything&nbsp;together&nbsp;with your hands until you have a sticky&nbsp;dough.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;/div&gt;
              &lt;/div&gt;
            &lt;/li&gt;
                      &lt;li&gt;
              &lt;div class="step-wrapper"&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-image"&gt;&lt;img src="http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/sites/bbcgoodfood.com/files/styles/bbcgf_step_by_step_image/public/step-2_1.jpg?itok=v1Qeqsg6" width="440" height="440" alt="" /&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-info"&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-number"&gt;Step 2&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-title"&gt;Knead the dough&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-text"&gt;&lt;p&gt;Tip on to a lightly floured surface and knead by&nbsp;holding the dough&nbsp;with one hand and stretching&nbsp;it with the heal of the other hand, then&nbsp;folding it&nbsp;back on itself. Repeat for 5 mins until smooth&nbsp;and elastic.&nbsp;Put the dough in a lightly oiled bowl.&nbsp;Cover with oiled cling film and&nbsp;leave to rise in a&nbsp;warm place for 1 hr or until doubled in size and&nbsp;a&nbsp;finger pressed into it leaves a dent.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;/div&gt;
              &lt;/div&gt;
            &lt;/li&gt;
                      &lt;li&gt;
              &lt;div class="step-wrapper"&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-image"&gt;&lt;img src="http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/sites/bbcgoodfood.com/files/styles/bbcgf_step_by_step_image/public/step-3_1.jpg?itok=Lsfd-PgQ" width="440" height="440" alt="" /&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-info"&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-number"&gt;Step 3&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-title"&gt;Add the fruit&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-text"&gt;&lt;p&gt;With the dough still in the bowl, tip in the&nbsp;sultanas, mixed peel, orange zest, apple and&nbsp;cinnamon. Knead into the dough, making sure&nbsp;everything is well distributed. Leave to rise&nbsp;for 1 hr more, or until&nbsp;doubled in size, again&nbsp;covered by some well-oiled cling film to stop&nbsp;the dough getting a crust.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;/div&gt;
              &lt;/div&gt;
            &lt;/li&gt;
                      &lt;li&gt;
              &lt;div class="step-wrapper"&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-image"&gt;&lt;img src="http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/sites/bbcgoodfood.com/files/styles/bbcgf_step_by_step_image/public/step-4.jpg?itok=LA13ckBe" width="440" height="440" alt="" /&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-info"&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-number"&gt;Step 4&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-title"&gt;Divide into buns&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-text"&gt;&lt;p&gt;Divide the dough into 15 even pieces (about&nbsp;75g per piece &ndash; see Tip on recipe). Roll each piece into&nbsp;a smooth ball on a lightly floured work surface.&nbsp;Arrange the buns on one or two baking trays&nbsp;lined with parchment, leaving enough space for&nbsp;the dough to expand. Cover (but don&rsquo;t wrap)&nbsp;with more oiled cling film, or a clean tea towel,&nbsp;then set aside to prove for 1 hr more.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;/div&gt;
              &lt;/div&gt;
            &lt;/li&gt;
                      &lt;li&gt;
              &lt;div class="step-wrapper"&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-image"&gt;&lt;img src="http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/sites/bbcgoodfood.com/files/styles/bbcgf_step_by_step_image/public/step-5_0.jpg?itok=K1dBrZGo" width="440" height="440" alt="" /&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-info"&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-number"&gt;Step 5&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-title"&gt;Pipe and bake&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-text"&gt;&lt;p&gt;Heat oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7. Mix the flour&nbsp;with about 5 tbsp water to make the paste for&nbsp;the cross &ndash; add the water 1 tbsp at a time, so&nbsp;you add just enough for a thick paste. Spoon&nbsp;into a piping bag with a small nozzle. Pipe a&nbsp;line along each row of buns, then repeat in the&nbsp;other direction to create crosses (see Tip on recipe).&nbsp;Bake for 20 mins on the middle shelf of the&nbsp;oven, until golden brown.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;/div&gt;
              &lt;/div&gt;
            &lt;/li&gt;
                      &lt;li&gt;
              &lt;div class="step-wrapper"&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-image"&gt;&lt;img src="http://www.bbcgoodfood.com/sites/bbcgoodfood.com/files/styles/bbcgf_step_by_step_image/public/step-6_0.jpg?itok=O8HRV7kW" width="440" height="440" alt="" /&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;div class="step-info"&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-number"&gt;Step 6&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-title"&gt;Brush with jam&lt;/div&gt;
                  &lt;div class="step-text"&gt;&lt;p&gt;Gently heat the apricot jam to melt, then sieve&nbsp;to get rid of any chunks. While the jam is still&nbsp;warm, brush over the top of the warm buns&nbsp;and leave to cool.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
                &lt;/div&gt;
              &lt;/div&gt;
            &lt;/li&gt;

       <section class="recipe-method separator separator-dashed-bottom col span6 tab" id="recipe-method">
        <h2 class="separator separator-dashed-bottom tab-header">
         Method
        </h2>
        <a class="open-step-by-step-btn btn" href="#">
         <span class="btn-save">
          Launch step-by-step
         </span>
         <i class="btn-save icon icon-frames-turquoise">
         </i>
        </a>
        <div>
         <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
          <div class="field-items">
           <div class="field-item even">
            <div class="item-list">
             <ol class="unstyled">
              <li class="separator separator-solid-grayoffwhite first" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               <span>
                Bring the milk to the boil, then remove from the
heat and add the butter. Leave to cool until it
reaches hand temperature. Put the flour, salt,
sugar and yeast (see Tip, below) into a bowl.
Make a well in the centre. Pour in the warm milk
and butter mixture, then add the egg. Using a
wooden spoon, mix well, then bring everything
together with your hands until you have a sticky
dough.
               </span>
              </li>
              <li class="separator separator-solid-grayoffwhite" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               <span>
                Tip on to a lightly floured surface and knead by
holding the dough with one hand and stretching
it with the heal of the other hand, then folding it
back on itself. Repeat for 5 mins until smooth
and elastic. Put the dough in a lightly oiled bowl.
Cover with oiled cling film and leave to rise in a
warm place for 1 hr or until doubled in size and
a finger pressed into it leaves a dent.
               </span>
              </li>
              <li class="separator separator-solid-grayoffwhite" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               <span>
                With the dough still in the bowl, tip in the
sultanas, mixed peel, orange zest, apple and
cinnamon. Knead into the dough, making sure
everything is well distributed. Leave to rise
for 1 hr more, or until doubled in size, again
covered by some well-oiled cling film to stop
the dough getting a crust.
               </span>
              </li>
              <li class="separator separator-solid-grayoffwhite" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               <span>
                Divide the dough into 15 even pieces (about
75g per piece  see Tip below). Roll each piece into
a smooth ball on a lightly floured work surface.
Arrange the buns on one or two baking trays
lined with parchment, leaving enough space for
the dough to expand. Cover (but dont wrap)
with more oiled cling film, or a clean tea towel,
then set aside to prove for 1 hr more.
               </span>
              </li>
              <li class="separator separator-solid-grayoffwhite" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               <span>
                Heat oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7. Mix the flour
with about 5 tbsp water to make the paste for
the cross  add the water 1 tbsp at a time, so
you add just enough for a thick paste. Spoon
into a piping bag with a small nozzle. Pipe a
line along each row of buns, then repeat in the
other direction to create crosses (see Tip below).
Bake for 20 mins on the middle shelf of the
oven, until golden brown.
               </span>
              </li>
              <li class="separator separator-solid-grayoffwhite last" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               <span>
                Gently heat the apricot jam to melt, then sieve
to get rid of any chunks. While the jam is still
warm, brush over the top of the warm buns
and leave to cool.
               </span>
              </li>
             </ol>

                        Made these yesterday and they turned out great!  I followed the suggestions to up the cinnamon, so I used 1 1/2 tsp which I though was just right after tasting one; after all, these aren't cinnamon buns.  I also don't drink full fat milk and I'm not going to buy a litre just for this, so I used 200 ml of 33% cream and he rest with skim milk. I wasn't too worried about my ratio as this is an enriched dough, so the extra fat wasn't going to hurt.  I also upped the sultanas a bit as well as the peel, as I like some bits in every bite.  My complaint is the supermarket is always stingy with it, hence making my own, and with this recipe, from now on.  When all was said and done I think each bun was around 80 g, which is big, but you only need one to toast!
                       </p>
                       <p>
                        I too questioned not putting the cinnamon in with the flour to get an even mix, because it won't - at least for me it didn't - evenly distribute during the folding in with the fruit without over handling the dough.                        </p>

I also took the other suggestion to make the cross with 2/3 flour to 1/3 confectioners sugar - which is basically a two to one ratio - so 1/4 cup of flour and two tablespoons of confectioners sugar was the perfect amount to dress all the buns.  Note that it will spread when baking, so a 1/4" piping will look just about right on these buns.  Lastly, yes, keep an eye on these babies, as you don't want to over bake them, as they are abit of work, but so worth it.
