         Sweet Maple Barbecue Chicken Kabobs | summer of the kabobs
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-05-29T06:08:55+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 29, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.adashofsanity.com/author/sandra/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Sandra
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="17445117" data-link="http://www.adashofsanity.com/2014/05/sweet-maple-barbecue-chicken-kabobs-summer-kabobs/" data-summary="" data-title="Sweet Maple Barbecue Chicken Kabobs | summer of the kabobs">
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Need some ideas for the grill this summer, check out my Grilled Kabob Collection on
         <a href="http://www.foodie.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.foodie.com/', 'Foodie.com']);">
          Foodie.com
         </a>
         .
         <br/>
         I will be adding to it all summer, so be sure to subscribe to get the latest kabob recipe.
        </p>
        <blockquote id="foodie-widget" lang="en">
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Check out
          <a href="http://www.foodie.com/collections/a-dash-of-sanity/grilled-kabob-recipes-bbqplanet" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.foodie.com/collections/a-dash-of-sanity/grilled-kabob-recipes-bbqplanet', 'Grilled Kabob Recipes | #BBQPlanet']);">
           Grilled Kabob Recipes | #BBQPlanet
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          by
          <a href="http://www.foodie.com/profiles/a-dash-of-sanity" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.foodie.com/profiles/a-dash-of-sanity', 'A Dash of Sanity']);">
           A Dash of Sanity
          </a>
          at
          <a href="http://www.foodie.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.foodie.com/', 'Foodie.com']);">
           Foodie.com
          </a>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <script language="JavaScript" src="http://www8.glam.com/js/widgets/glam_coll.act?afid=670016609;colid=6571683:Collection:3979053;authorid=6571683:Yuser:2295392;userid=6571683:Yuser:2295392;es=1;" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Continuing with the theme for this week of chicken kabobs; today I am sharing my favorite barbecue sauce used for grilling. And Ill let you in on a little secret it was made my accident!
         <br/>
         Dont you love it when those things happen and it turns out to be better than your original recipes?
         <br/>
         You see, I went to reach for the honey and grabbed the maple syrup instead, distracted by some shrilling screams coming from the backyard I started to pour in the syrup, then realized what I had done.
         <br/>
         Instead of starting over I decided to go with it and so glad I did. I love the touch of caramelized sweetness that this adds to my traditional sauce. It isnt overly sweet, like sometimes honey barbecues sauces can be.
         <br/>
         I already have this on the menu again for next week. From my accidental recipe kitchen to yours.
         <br/>
         Enjoy!
         <br/>
         XOXO San

             Sweet Maple Barbecue Chicken Kabobs | summer of the kabobs

            <div class="fl-l width-50">
             <p id="zlrecipe-prep-time">

              <span content="PT10M" itemprop="prepTime">
               10 minutes

              <span content="PT15M" itemprop="cookTime">
               15 minutes

             <p id="zlrecipe-total-time">
              Total Time:
              <span content="PT25M" itemprop="totalTime">
               25 minutes
              </span>

            <div class="zlclear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="img-desc-wrap">
            <p class="t-a-c hide-print">
             <img alt="Sweet Maple Barbecue Chicken Kabobs | summer of the kabobs" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://www.adashofsanity.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/sweet-maple-barbecue-chicken-kabobs-main.jpg" style="width: 600x400px;" title="Sweet Maple Barbecue Chicken Kabobs | summer of the kabobs"/>
            </p>
            <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
             <p class="summary italic">
              With a touch of caramelized sweetness, this takes your traditional barbecue sauce and elevates it to a whole new level.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             2lbs. chicken tenders
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 (packed) cup brown Sugar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup ketchup
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup red wine vinegar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 cup water
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
              heaping cup pure maple syrup, meaning a little over  cup
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 teaspoons ground mustard
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 teaspoons Paprika
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
             1-1/2 teaspoons kosher salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon black pepper
            </li>
            <img class="ingredient-image" src="http://www.adashofsanity.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/sweet-maple-barbecue-chicken-kabobs-bite.jpg"/>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-12" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Directions
           </p>
           <span class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             <span class="bold">
              Marinade:
             </span>
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Mix brown sugar, ketchup, red wine vinegar, water, pure maple syrup Worcestershire sauce, ground mustard, paprika, kosher salt and pepper in a large bowl.   Place chicken tenders in marinade.  Cover and let chicken marinade for at least 2-4 hours or overnight. I like to throw all the ingredients into a Ziplock and let it marinade this way.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             If you are using metal skewers skip this part, if you are using wood skewers follow the directions below.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Now I know there is a debate whether or not to soak your skewers and to each their own. But I soak mine, I have found that I get less wood pieces in the chicken and they dont go up in flames. They still may char a tad, but not following apart or on fire.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             I completely soak my skewers for 30 minutes, sometimes accidently longer, like the whole day.  Make sure the entire stick is completely dredge in water. Once they have soaked, remove and start to assemble the skewers.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             <span class="bold">
              Kabobs:
             </span>
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Remove the chicken from the fridge, one piece at a time grab a tender and lace through the skewer. I start at the chunkier end and do a weave motion. Or you can just slide the entire tender on the kabob and not make it all fancy like a wave. Either way, they will taste the same. Set kabob on a plate and repeat the process. Once completed drizzle extra marinade over the chicken, and let it sit while grill is heating up.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Heat gas or charcoal grill, remove chicken from marinade. Place chicken on grill over medium heat. Cover grill and cook 3 minutes, then turn over the kabob and cook another 3 minutes. At this point check the chicken. To know if the chicken is done the juices should be clear and the center of thickest part is cut (170F). If not done, continue to cook, at this point I leave the lid open and just turn the kabobs so they are cooked evenly on both sides. My chicken takes about 8-10 minutes.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Remove from grill and serve immediately. Or cover with foil and serve later.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Great to serve with grilled corn on the cob, cornbread  my favorite Sweet Jalapeno Cornbread and coleslaw.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-10" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Use the leftovers the next day for a grilled chicken salad.
            </p>
            <img class="instruction-image" src="http://www.adashofsanity.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/sweet-maple-barbecue-chicken-kabobs-group-close-up.jpg"/>
           </span>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-notes">
            Notes
           </p>
           <div id="zlrecipe-notes-list">
            <p class="notes">
             Total time does not include the time to marinade.
            </p>
