               <span class="reply">
                <a aria-label="Reply to Sarita" class="comment-reply-link" href="/blog/2012/11/20/wedding-dress-with-sleeves-lace-gown-bridal-sleeved-dresses/?replytocom=173106#respond" onclick='return addComment.moveForm( "div-comment-173106", "173106", "respond", "5993" )'>
                 Reply
                </a>
               </span>
              </footer>
              <div class="comment-content">
               <p>
                Most people dont have cute arms and shoulders.  One in a million does.  Ive seen more brides than not who look simply ridiculous baring the arms and shoulders.  Either the arms are fat, or unattractive, or badly shaped, or covered in acne, or have long tattooed sleeves, or any number of other reasons why most brides look bad with their arms and shoulders naked to the world.
               </p>
               <p>
                So why does the bridal industry offer only wedding dresses that expose the shoulders and arms?  How did this even get started?  Simple.  Its much more expensive for bridal dress manufacturers to create sleeves.
               </p>
               <p>
                It takes a rather large amount of time for a seamstress to create sleeves, so the manufacturer has to pay for that time.  It requires much more fabric to create sleeves, and usually very expensive fabric (you dont want sleeves looking ugly), and the manufacturer has to pay for that.  It requires more thread to make sleeves, and the manufacturer pays for that.  Then, as if that were not enough, when the dress is complete and is put out on the market, it might not sell.  The problem is that every womans arms and shoulders are different, and dresses are made standardized and easy to fit because its basically a tube  you either take it in or let it out.  Sleeves are not as easy as that.  Adjusting sleeves is not the same as merely taking in the bodice or letting it out.  That takes time and knowledge.
               </p>
               <p>
                Manufacturers have been having a very profitable field day since they made the bridal fashion one that requires exposing the arms and shoulders.  Theyre a happy bunch, and are not about to give that up so easily.  It will be a very, very sad day for manufacturers the day women insist on bridal fashion with sleeves.  For now, manufacturers and designers work hand in hand to keep womens arms and shoulders naked and their bank coffers quite full.
               </p>
