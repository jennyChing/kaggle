             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               You dont need a window for these views. Paris-based design company
               <a href="http://www.technicondesign.com/" target="_blank">
                Technicon Design
               </a>
               recently won an award for their IXION Windowless Jet Concept. The idea is to provide a 360-degree view using cameras mounted on the planes exterior to capture the scenery and then project that on high-res screen on the interior cabin walls and ceiling.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                SEE ALSO:
                <a href="http://news.discovery.com/autos/videos/the-future-of-transportation-video.htm" target="_blank">
                 What The Future Of Transportation Looks Like
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               And actually any scene could be displayed on the interior. Lets say the view is mostly clouds or ocean. How about displaying a rainforest? A flight through the Grand Canyon? A trip to the Moon?
              </p>
              <p>
               Solar panels on the exterior would help power the displays.
              </p>
              <p>
               Removing windows has its advantages, too. It reduces the materials and cost needed as well as reducing the weight of the plane. Not having windows allows for a greater flexibility of the interior design of the aircraft, too.
              </p>
              <p>
               The ethos of the project is simple, to challenge current thinking, and propose something a little different, but not just a fantasy. It has to be credible and relevant, yet provoke discussion, design director Gareth Davies said in a
               <a href="http://blog.technicondesign.com/2014/05/technicon-design-france-team-received.html" target="_blank">
                press release
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               See the video below.
              </p>
              <p>
               <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="281" src="//player.vimeo.com/video/78458486" width="500">
               </iframe>
              </p>
              <p>
               Other aircrafts are exploring similar ideas of transparent cabins. In 2012 at the
               <a href="http://news.discovery.com/tech/gear-and-gadgets/futuristic-airbus-transparent-concept-cabin-110616.htm" target="_blank">
                Paris Air Show
               </a>
               , Airbus presented an aircraft with an interior fuselage made of displays. And earlier this year, Boston engineering firm
               <a href="http://www.dezeen.com/2014/02/24/supersonic-private-jet-replace-windows-with-live-streaming-screens/" target="_blank">
                Spike Aerospace
               </a>
               unveiled a windowless supersonic jet with displays.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                SEE ALSO:
                <a href="http://news.discovery.com/tech/gear-and-gadgets/futuristic-airbus-transparent-concept-cabin-110616.htm" target="_blank">
                 Futuristic Airbus Plane Has Transparent Cabin And More
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               If these aircraft designs are any indication of the future of flight, we are looking forward to flying.
              </p>
