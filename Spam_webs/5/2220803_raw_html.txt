           <h2 class="slide-headline" itemprop="name">
            Seek Secondhand Gems
           </h2>
           <article class="slide-description">
            <p>
             Kids grow like weeds  especially in the first two years  so theres no need to invest in expensive clothing or shoes. Most secondhand stores are bursting with barely worn baby duds (some with the original tags!) and
             <a href="http://www.whattoexpect.com/toddler/cheap-toddler-clothes.aspx">
              cheap toddler clothes
             </a>
             . Before you buy, though, check each item in a well-lit area for stains, loose seams, and worn-out knees or elbows. Eyeing secondhand furniture, strollers, or toys? Make sure theyre sturdy, and dont forget to check the Consumer Product Safety Commission Web site  www.cpsc.gov  to be sure the product hasnt been recalled or deemed unsafe.

           <h2 class="slide-headline" itemprop="name">
            Get Your Breast Pump for Less
           </h2>
           <article class="slide-description">
            <p>
             One of the many benefits of breastfeeding is that you barely have to buy anything  except, of course, a
             <a href="http://www.whattoexpect.com/first-year/choosing-a-breast-pump.aspx">
              breast pump
             </a>
             . The good news: Insurance might have you covered. Before you invest in a quality pump, call your provider to find out if its included in your plan. One word of warning about using a secondhand breast pump: Dont. Even if the pump looks squeaky clean, some germs can live on surfaces for months or even years. For safetys sake, buy or rent your own.

           <h2 class="slide-headline" itemprop="name">
            Start a Babysitting Swap
           </h2>
           <article class="slide-description">
            <p>
             Even though new moms and dads would give anything for a night out, you dont need to shell out big bucks for a sitter. One option: Enlist trustworthy nearby parents in a babysitting swap. Start small (just one or two other families) and set up guidelines in advance: How many hours will parents contribute each month? Will awake hours carry the same weight as after-bedtime hours? How will you deal with values or rules that differ among households? Keep the lines of communication open and this arrangement might just last for years.

            Skip Pricey Salon Visits
           </h2>
           <article class="slide-description">

             Your much-deserved
             <a href="http://www.whattoexpect.com/first-year/week-45/mommy-rut.aspx">
              mommy time
             </a>
             doesnt have to cost much. Cancel your next visit with your colorist and try a do-it-yourself hair-coloring kit instead. The newest formulas are usually goof-proof and cost a fraction of a professional dye job. At-home manicures and pedicures are easier (and neater!) than ever, too, thanks to press-on polish strips that can be applied in minutes and require zero drying time. This beauty steal comes in basic colors, like reds and pinks, as well as bold patterns  think leopard and zebra prints  and will cost you considerably less than a professional polish job.

           <h2 class="slide-headline" itemprop="name">
            Shop Smart in the Produce Aisle
           </h2>
           <article class="slide-description">
            <p>
             Yes, organic fruits and veggies are best, but their high prices can be pretty hard to swallow. It pays, then, to know your Dirty Dozen: Apples, celery, strawberries, peaches, spinach, nectarines, grapes, bell peppers, potatoes, blueberries, lettuce, and kale are the crops most susceptible to pesticide contamination, so its worth the extra bucks to buy organic. But if any of the Clean 15 (produce
             <em>
              least
             </em>
             susceptible to pesticide contamination) is on your shopping list, no need to spring for organic. The Clean 15 includes onions, sweet corn, pineapples, avocados, asparagus, sweet peas, mangoes, eggplant, cantaloupe, kiwi, cabbage, watermelon, sweet potatoes, grapefruits, and mushrooms.
            </p>

           <h2 class="slide-headline" itemprop="name">
            Sign Up for Baby Rewards Programs
           </h2>
           <article class="slide-description">
            <p>
             Having a baby is rewarding  in more ways than one! Huggies and Pampers both offer points-based programs, so every box of diapers or wipes you purchase earns you points to put toward toys, books, baby supplies, and more. And loads of baby-supply companies (including Earths Best, Gerber, and Johnson &amp; Johnson) are happy to send you coupons if you join their sites. Sign up for Amazon Mom for additional savings on items you buy regularly, like diapers and baby wash. Baby-related purchases (clothing, furniture, toys  you name it) earn you up to a year of free two-day shipping on all qualifying Amazon purchases, whether youre buying a bouncy seat or a barbecue set.
            </p>

            Rotate Toys Among Families
           </h2>
           <article class="slide-description">
            <p>
             Babies dont need every new toy that hits the market  if youd like to keep your toy stash small
             <em>
              and
             </em>
             keep your cutie constantly entertained, consider rotating toys with neighbors who have similarly aged kids. Of course youll hold on to your tots favorites, but once a month, swap three
             <a href="http://www.whattoexpect.com/best-toys-for-babies.aspx">
              baby toys
             </a>
             he typically overlooks for new to you toys from down the block. If he falls head over heels for his neighbors neglected Gloworm, talk to that mom about making the switch permanent.

           </h2>

             <a href="http://www.whattoexpect.com/first-year/photo-gallery/playground-activities-for-babies.aspx#/slide-1">
              Playgrounds
             </a>
             offer exercise and socialization opportunities, and public libraries often have a roster of
             <a href="/toddler/photo-gallery/fun-family-outings.aspx">
              free activities
             </a>
             from infant story hours to toddler sing-alongs. Family-friendly restaurants or bookstores sometimes host a guitar player or puppeteer in an effort to drum up afternoon business, so check your newspaper or local listings online. If youre willing to spend a little money, community-based organizations, like the YMCA, often have low-cost programs for little ones, including tumbling classes and arts and crafts.
