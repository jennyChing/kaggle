       <h2 class="posttitle">
        Green Chile Steak Tacos
       </h2>
       <div class="postmeta">
        <em>
         posted by
        </em>
        Sommer 
        <a href="http://www.aspicyperspective.com/green-chile-steak-tacos/#comments">
         36 comments 
        </a>
       </div>
      </header>
      <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="17464635" data-link="http://www.aspicyperspective.com/green-chile-steak-tacos/" data-summary="" data-title="Green Chile Steak Tacos">
      </div>
      <p>
       Easy grilled steak tacos marinated in green chile relish, and piled with lean greentoppings like cilantro, scallions, and avocado!
       <span id="more-29749">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Green Chile Steak Tacos #tacos #steak #mexican" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29761" height="900" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/green-chile-steak-tacos-8-copy.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ive been in Austin, Texas the last couple days consuming tacos in large quantities with my new friends at
       <a href="http://oldelpaso.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Old El Paso
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       I know what youre thinking my job is
       <em>
        really
       </em>
       hard.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Taco Truck #austin #freshestbloggers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-30003" height="476" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/old-el-paso-taco-tour-30.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Im partnering with Old El Paso this year to create quick and fresh dinners your whole family will love.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="338" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/hldFsh-uxxI" width="600">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p>
       To kick off our year of Mexican inspired meals, we took a quick trip to Austin, Texas to go on a Taco Tour with author and taco expert
       <a href="http://www.mandorayo.com/" target="_blank">
        Mando Rayo
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Sommer and Mando" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29992" height="484" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/old-el-paso-taco-tour-27.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       We stayed at the unique
       <a href="http://www.sanjosehotel.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Hotel San Jose
       </a>
       on South Congress Avenue (i.e. SoCo.) Its a serene oasis right in the middle of all of Austins action.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Hotel San Jose - Austin Texas" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29993" height="760" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Hotel2.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Mando lead us to 4 of Austins 500+ taco stops for what he feels are the best tacos in town. Heres a map of where we went:
       <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/veracruz-all-natural-austin" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Veracruz All Natural
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://joesbakery.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Joes Bakery
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.tacodeli.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Tacodeli
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/al-pastor-restaurant-and-taco-stand-austin" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Al Pastor
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Austin Taco Tour Map " class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-29981" height="414" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-19-at-1.02.07-AM-600x414.png" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Each location offered a different variety of tacos, from breakfast migas tacos, to bacon and bean tacos, to grilled queso fresco tacos, to the classic al pastor style taco.
      </p>
      <p>
       Seriously, I could not get enough!
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Austin Texas Taco Tour" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29985" height="760" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/TacoStops.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <img alt="Old El Paso's #FreshestBloggers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29991" height="430" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/old-el-paso-taco-tour-26.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span style="color: #808080;">
        <a href="http://www.muybuenocookbook.com/" target="_blank">
         Yvette
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://sweetlifebake.com/" target="_blank">
         Vianney
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://hungryfoodlove.com/" target="_blank">
         Melissa
        </a>
        , Me,
        <a href="http://www.the-girl-who-ate-everything.com/" target="_blank">
         Christy
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.tasteandtellblog.com/" target="_blank">
         Deborah
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://gimmesomeoven.com" target="_blank">
         Ali
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.twopeasandtheirpod.com/" target="_blank">
         Maria
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="http://picky-palate.com" target="_blank">
         Jenny
        </a>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       I loved them all, but theres just something about a good fish taco that makes me sublimely happy. You know?
      </p>
      <p>
       And the salsas we tried made me want to cry. (Tears of joy, not pain.)
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Austin, Texas Taco Tour " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29986" height="760" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Tacos.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ever seen guys so serious about tacos?
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Taco Tour - Austin" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29988" height="482" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Tacos2.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       After our taco tour, and a little time to work up an appetite, we headed to
       <a href="http://silverwhiskcooking.com/" target="_blank">
        Silver Whisks
       </a>
       kitchen to do some cooking of our own.
      </p>
      <p>
       We spent the evening creating Mexican favorites with a bit of
       <em>
        panache,
       </em>
       likecreamy quesadillas, shrimp tostadas with garlic aioli, strawberry salsa, rainbow nachos, and deep fried sriracha lime ice cream cups.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Cooking Challenge" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29987" height="482" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Cooking.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Its easy to make marvelous Mexican food when youre starting with great products.
      </p>
      <p>
       Did you know Cooks Illustrated rated
       <a href="http://oldelpaso.com/products/Flour-Tortillas-10-count-for-Soft-Tacos--Fajitas/90f14090-ca3f-4601-b774-af6e58c2c1e9" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Old El Paso Flour Tortillas
       </a>
       #1 in taste? Just a little fun fact.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Old El Paso's #FreshestBloggers Taco Tour" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29990" height="450" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/old-el-paso-taco-tour-25.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       To see more of my trip photos, check out my
       <a href="http://instagram.com/spicyperspectiv" target="_blank">
        Instagram feed
       </a>
       and look for Old El Pasos hashtag #FreshestBloggers.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Food of Austin" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29989" height="768" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/MoreFood.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Obviously all this fun involvingamazing Tex Mex cuisine, particularly tacos, has only spurred on my serious taco cravings.
      </p>
      <p>
       These green chile steak tacos offer big bold flavor from
       <a href="http://oldelpaso.com/products/Green-Chiles-Chopped/037f2db4-5b77-4814-a5a6-e8eab4a3c092" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Old El Paso Green Chiles
       </a>
       . Thererea throw-back to family taco night, with fresh modern flavors and bold ingredients.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="How to Make Green Chile Steak Tacos #tacos #steak #mexican" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29755" height="907" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/green-chile-steak-tacos-2.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       I started by marinating the steak in a green chile relish mixed with zesty garlic and lime zest.
      </p>
      <p>
       Once the steak has soaked up all the chile goodness, grill the steakto a perfect medium-rare over a hot flame. The green chiles go into a skillet to create a caramelized green chile sauce.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Easy Green Chile Steak Tacos #tacos #steak #mexican" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29756" height="900" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/green-chile-steak-tacos-3.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Slice the steak and warm up some soft comforting
       <a href="http://oldelpaso.com/products/Flour-Tortillas-10-count-for-Soft-Tacos--Fajitas/90f14090-ca3f-4601-b774-af6e58c2c1e9" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Old El Paso tortillas
       </a>
       . Then place the sliced steak in the tortillas.
      </p>
      <p>
       Like this
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Simple Green Chile Steak Tacos #tacos #steak #mexican" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29757" height="900" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/green-chile-steak-tacos-4.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Pile on your favoritegreen toppings like creamy avocado slices, zippy green onions, and fresh chopped cilantro. Sprinkle with salty queso fresco and youve got a sizzling hot sensation with a clash of cool flavors.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Dinner Tonight: Green Chile Steak Tacos #tacos #steak #mexican" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29762" height="900" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/green-chile-steak-tacos-6.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Green chile steak tacos add a bit of
       <em>
        OLE
       </em>
       to the dinner table!
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Fab Green Chile Steak Tacos #tacos #steak #mexican" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-29760" height="900" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/green-chile-steak-tacos-7-copy.jpg" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <div class="recipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
       <meta content="http://www.aspicyperspective.com/green-chile-steak-tacos" itemprop="url"/>
       <img align="right" class="photo nopin" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://xnjm5arbt0-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/green-chile-steak-tacos-12-210x210.jpg"/>
       <span itemprop="name">
        <h2 class="fn">
         Green Chile Steak Tacos
        </h2>
       </span>
       <div class="time">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Yield:
         </strong>
         <span itemprop="recipeYield">
          10 tacos
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Prep Time:
         </strong>
         <meta content="PT20M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
         20 minutes
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cook Time:
         </strong>
         <meta content="PT15M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
         15 minutes
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="printbutton">
        <a class="print" href="http://www.aspicyperspective.com/green-chile-steak-tacos/print/" title="Print Recipe">
         Print Recipe
        </a>
       </div>
       <h3 id="ingredients">
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <div class="ingredients">
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 1/2 pounds sirloin steak, 1 inch thick
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          4.5 ounce can Old El Paso Chopped Green Chiles
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 garlic clove, minced
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 limes (1 zested)
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 cup chopped green onions
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 cup chopped cilantro
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 avocados
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1cup crumbled queso fresco
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 package Old El Paso Flour Tortillas (10)
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          Salt and pepper
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
       <h3 id="directions">
        Directions:
       </h3>
       <div class="instructions">
        <span itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         <ol>
          <li>
           Place the steak in a baking dish and top with Old El Paso chopped green chiles, garlic, the zest of one lime, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1/2 teaspoon black pepper. Rub the mixture over the steak on all sides and set aside.
          </li>
          <li>
           Preheat the grill to high. Allow the steak to marinate in the green chile relish, while you chop and prepare the remaining ingredients.
          </li>
          <li>
           Lift the steak out of the green chile relish and place it on the hot grill. Sear for 3-4 minutes per side. Take the steak off the grill and tent it with foil to keep warm. Then saut the remaininggreen chile relish in a small skillet until slightly charred, 5-8 minutes on medium-high. Scoop it out of the skillet and place the charred green chile relish on top of the steak.
          </li>
          <li>
           Slice the limes into wedges. Then place the tortillas on a microwave-safe plate and cover them with a damp paper towel. Heat for 45-60 seconds to warm through. Slice the steak into thin strips, against the grain.
          </li>
          <li>
           To serve: Place several slices of steak with relish on each warm tortilla, then top with chopped green onions, cilantro, sliced avocado, and crumbled queso fresco. Serve with lime wedges.
          </li>
         </ol>
        </span>
       </div>
