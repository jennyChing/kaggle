         <div class="challIntro">
          You never know the impact you will make on someone elses life. When I was a young man in my mid 20s, I had a brief conversation that transformed my life and has touched the lives of tens of thousands of people in the last 18 years.
         </div>
         <p>
          I was Deputy Sheriff in Western Kansas and about 7 years into my law enforcement career. A couple years earlier I had started going back to church, having made a mess of my life after leaving church a few years earlier. Although my life was much different, I still had many rough edges.
         </p>
         <div class="article-content">
          <p>
           Dean was an older man in his 50s (I was 26 or 27 at the time) who greeted everyone coming into the church on Sunday mornings.
          </p>
          <p>
           One Sunday as I was walking up, Dean asked me to consider serving in a leadership position within the church. I quickly dismissed his request by saying, Ive still got too many rough edges, I dont think Im the right man for the job.
          </p>
          <p>
           Deans response to me that day was very simple, but his words carried great weight and their impact sent my life in whole new direction. If not being ready is your excuse for not serving, you will never serve in any capacity, because there are always things that need to change in your life and you will never be ready. With that he handed me the bulletin for that days service and wished me well.
          </p>
          <p>
           That conversation didnt go away. Those words had gone straight to my soul. I knew God was saying something to me through the words of a wise friend.
          </p>
          <p>
           Shortly after that conversation, I left law enforcement and watched as my life went in a new direction. A few years later, I would be answering Gods call on my life to serve as a pastor to congregations in the United States. Within a few years, I would also become a pastor to other pastors who leading their own congregations.
          </p>
          <p>
           The encouragement to put my excuses aside and simply say yes to the things God was inviting me into, has allowed me to touch tens of thousands of lives on 3 continents.
          </p>
          <p>
           It was that same conversation that gave me the courage to leave traditional employment and launch out full-time as an entrepreneur. Over the years that conversation has prompted me to step out in faith time and time again.
          </p>
          <p>
           What I find most fascinating is that today, Dean is in his late 70s and I am now in my mid 40s, Dean doesnt even remember us having that conversation. I told him a few years ago, that there will be a long line of people waiting to shake his hand someday. They will be the lives that were touched, because he dared to challenge a young man who didnt feel worthy to take up the call on his life.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="postSegue">
          <p>
           There may be something stirring deep inside of you today. A step that you need to take, but a step that you dont feel worthy or qualified to take.
          </p>
          <p>
           Just like Dean told me years ago, there will always be reason youre not ready so dont make that your excuse.
          </p>
          <p>
           One of the things that has allowed me to touch so many lives around the world is my online business. It has given me a platform to connect with people and to support my ministry work financially.
          </p>
          <p>
           This video will show you how you can take that first step into a world that could potentially change your life and the lives of thousands of others for years to come. Watch it now.
          </p>
