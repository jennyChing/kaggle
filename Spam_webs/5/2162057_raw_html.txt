       <h1 class="normal">
        Whatsapp Blue Tick Can Now Be Removed
       </h1>
       <div class="share_sec">
        <div class="float-left">
         <span class="calander bold">
          13 Mar
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="share float-left">
         <!--&lt;span class="msg"&gt;62&lt;/span&gt; &lt;span class="fb"&gt;28&lt;/span&gt; &lt;span class="tw"&gt;50&lt;/span&gt;-->

       <aside class="btmrgn">
        Hope has been restored.  Enough said.
       </aside>
       <div class="image-section">
        <img alt="" class="widget_img" src="http://www.collegeokplease.com/upload/361/e212d343c053e3684d36ab9606abab23.jpg"/>
       </div>
       <p>
        A single tick indicates message sent, a double tick indicates message delivered and then came the bombarding blue tick that let others know that the message has been read (in most cases, the blue tick signifies message ignored).
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" height="350" src="/uploads/2014-11-24/1416809113.jpg" width="700"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Much to our despair, there was no undoing this disaster. The blue tick is the worst nightmare we could have.
       </p>
       <p>
        And here comes the ray of hope. IT CAN NOW BE REMOVED! :D
       </p>
       <p>
        Fair warning: No upgrade has come in the Android/Apple/Windows app store.
       </p>
       <p>
        Just follow these few steps and get rid of the monster tick.
       </p>
       <p>
        1. Delete the current Whatapp app.
       </p>
       <p>
        2. Check if your phone is running on Android 2.1 or higher version.
       </p>
       <p>
        3. Check your Settings if your Download from Unknown Source is enabled.
       </p>
       <p>
        4. Go to the Whatsapp official website and download the APK file.
       </p>
       <p>
        5. Install the 2.11.444 WhatsApp version to your phone.
       </p>
       <p>
        6. Open Whatsapp and go to Settings. (Settings on the app and not your phone settings)
       </p>
       <p>
        7. Go to Account &gt; Privacy.
       </p>
       <p>
        8. You will see a new option called Read Receipts. Uncheck that.
       </p>
       <p>
        9. Hello, Old Whatsapp.
       </p>

function CheckUserExist(myemail)
{
	var re = /^([\w-\.]+)@((\[[0-9]{1,3}\.[0-9]{1,3}\.[0-9]{1,3}\.)|(([\w-]+\.)+))([a-zA-Z]{2,4}|[0-9]{1,3})(\]?)$/;
	var myemail = $.trim($('#myemail').val());	
	if(!$.trim($('#myemail').val())){
		$('#myemail').css('color','red');
		flag = true;
	}else if(!re.test($.trim($('#myemail').val()))){
		$('#myemail').css('color','red');
		$('#myemail').addClass('erroremptyfield');
		$('.error_box').html('Enter valid email');
		flag = true;
	}else{
		$(".loadingimg").show();
		$.ajax({
			type: "POST",
			url: "http://www.collegeokplease.com/login/CheckUserExist/",
			data: "myemail="+myemail,
			success: function(result){                
				if(result==1)
				{
					//alert(result);
					$('#myemail').css('color','red');
					$('#email_exist').val(1);
					$('.error_box').html('Email already exists');
				}
				else
				{
					$('#myemail').css('color','');
					$('#email_exist').val('');	
				}
			},
			complete: function(){
				$(".loadingimg").hide();
			}
        });
	}	
}
