     <h2 class="page_title">
      Rhubarb Bundt Cake with Ginger Glaze
     </h2>
     <img alt="Rhubarb and Ginger Cake" class="nimbus_750_900 wp-post-image" height="640" src="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Rhubarb-Ginger-Cake.jpg" width="640"/>
     <div class="row single_meta">
      <div class="col-sm-8">
       <p>
        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/category/4-star/" rel="category tag">
         4 Star
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/category/cake/bundt/" rel="category tag">
         Bundt
        </a>

        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/category/capturing-cornwall/" rel="category tag">
         Capturing Cornwall
        </a>
        |                                         5th May 2015 | By
        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/author/choclette/" rel="author" title="Posts by Choclette">
         Choclette
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-sm-4">
       <p class="text-right">
        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/2015/05/rhubarb-bundt-cake-with-ginger-glaze/#comments">
         34 comments
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="16577022" data-link="http://tinandthyme.uk/2015/05/rhubarb-bundt-cake-with-ginger-glaze/" data-summary="" data-title="Rhubarb Bundt Cake with Ginger Glaze">
     </div>
     <p>
      It was a friends birthday a couple of weeks ago and we had been invited over for lunch. This was to be followed by a walk from Respryn Bridge at Lanhydrock to Duchy Nurseries so we could indulge ina cream tea. But first a cake was in order.
      <span id="more-5780">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      As it was a beautifully warm spring day, a light seasonal cake seemed appropriate. I had the first of my mothers rhubarb raring to go and the last of my
      <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/jumbles-with-a-white-chocolate-lemony-drizzle/" target="_blank">
       Spanish oranges
      </a>
      . Rhubarb pairs particularly well with both orange and ginger Ive found. In my pre Chocolate Log blog days, I used to make a rather gorgeous rhubarb and ginger cake, but its quite heavy on the flavouring and more of a cold day bake. Rather than use lots of ginger in the sponge, I thought a gingery glaze would do the trick instead.
     </p>
     <p>
      I used the restof the rhubarb to make these
      <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/waffles-with-rhubarb-and-rose-compote-and-rose-cream/" target="_blank">
       rhubarb and rose waffles
      </a>
      , which were a real winner.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5793" style="width: 650px">
      <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Yellow-Magnolia.jpg">
       <img alt="Yellow Magnolia" class="img-responsive wp-image-5793 size-full" height="478" src="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Yellow-Magnolia.jpg" width="640"/>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       Magnolias were out everywhere in the Fowey Valley, but this yellow specimen was particularly magnificent. #CapturingCornwall
      </p>
     </div>
     <p>
      Thebirthday boy was delighted with his cake and we all had a slice after a hearty bowl of soup for lunch. It turned out just as I hoped and was lightly fruity without being stodgy. Bursts of tart rhubarb exploded on the tongue and the touch of hot spiciness from the ginger glaze made a welcome contrast. Itset us up very nicely for our afternoon walk where spring flowers abounded. Magnolias are always splendid at this time of year in Cornwall but yellow ones are a little more unusual. We refuelled at theDuchy Nursery cafe where it was warm enough to sit outside and enjoy the picturesqueviews of Restormel Castle and the Fowey valley below. My kind of day.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Love-Cake-logo.jpg">
       <img alt="Love Cake" class="alignright size-full wp-image-5139 img-responsive" height="141" src="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Love-Cake-logo.jpg" width="212"/>
      </a>
      This may not be a particularly brightly coloured cake, but it is flecked with pink from the rhubarb and has a gingery coloured icing, so I am sending it to
      <strong>
       Love Cake
      </strong>
      over at
      <a href="http://www.jibberjabberuk.co.uk/2015/05/love-cake-may-2015.html" target="_blank">
       JibberJabber UK
      </a>
      , where the theme this month is
      <em>
       Colour Me Pretty
      </em>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

       <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="0">
        <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
         Ingredients
        </div>
        <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
          180g unsalted butter
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
          200g golden caster sugar (I used cardamom sugar as the flavour works well with fruit)
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
          175g flour (I used half wholemeal, half white)
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
          75g ground almonds
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
          scant 2 tsp baking powder
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
          1/4 tsp bicarbonate of soda
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
          3 large eggs
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="7">
          1 organic orange  zest &amp; juice
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="8">
          1 tbsp natural yoghurt
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="9">
          2 sticks rhubarb (200g) - chopped fairly small
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="10">
          50g icing sugar
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="11">
          1 tsp ground ginger
         </li>
        </ol>
       </div>
       <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="1">
        <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
         Instructions
        </div>
        <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
          Cream butter and sugar together until light and fluffy.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
          Grate in the orange zest and beat some more.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
          Beat in eggs, one at a time, alternating with a spoonful of the flour if necessary.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">
          Sift in the flour, almonds, baking powder and bicarb. Stir until just combined.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="4">
          Gently mix in the yoghurt and orange juice until just combined, then stir in the rhubarb.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="5">
          Spoon the batter into an oiled silicone bundt mould or tin. Bake at 180C for about 40 minutes or until the cake is well risen and an inserted skewer comes out clean. Allow to cool for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="6">
          Mix the icing sugar and ginger with just enough water to make a slightly runny icing. Drizzle over the cooled cake.
         </li>
        </ol>
