             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://zakkalife.blogspot.com/2009/10/how-to-make-mini-bithday-banners.html">
               How To Make Mini Birthday Banners
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUVAUVMAOI/AAAAAAAADto/giqvBHWdCmY/s1600-h/HPIM7544.JPG" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5396742823675166946" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUVAUVMAOI/AAAAAAAADto/giqvBHWdCmY/s400/HPIM7544.JPG" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 214px;"/>
              </a>
              Last weekend we celebrated my daughter's birthday so I've had birthday decorations on the brain.  This is one of the ideas that has been on my mind, mini birthday banners.  The letters in the birthday banner are only 1 1/2 inches high. Because the letters are tiny, the banner fits perfectly in a bedroom doorway.  You could also hang it on a bulletin board or the fridge.  Wouldn't you want to be surprised by one of these in your office or at home?
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUR0ddAAMI/AAAAAAAADtA/GXBExUS8KmQ/s1600-h/Birthday+Banner+11.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5396739321430540482" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUR0ddAAMI/AAAAAAAADtA/GXBExUS8KmQ/s400/Birthday+Banner+11.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 322px;"/>
              </a>
              <span style="font-weight: bold;">
               Supplies
              </span>
              :
              <br/>
              Small Letters - There's a couple of ways to go about this.  I cut mine out using a Cricut.  If you don't have a personal cutting machine another option is to buy small letters in the scrapbooking section. Another way to make the letters  is to type them out in Word to a size you want.  Print the letters on the back side of nice card stock and cut out the letters.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Small rectangles - the ones I used were 1/4" by 1 1/2"  Will depend on the size of your letters.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Extra small brads
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Small hole punch
              <br/>
              <br/>
              String
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Glue
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUTlzk2h7I/AAAAAAAADtI/eFr-J4ii5H4/s1600-h/Birthday+Banner+12.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5396741268694271922" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUTlzk2h7I/AAAAAAAADtI/eFr-J4ii5H4/s400/Birthday+Banner+12.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 278px;"/>
              </a>
              Glue each of your letters to the center of the rectangles.  Punch a hole on each side.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUT4YhGmgI/AAAAAAAADtQ/4s8zhpN3k6k/s1600-h/Birthday+Banner+13.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5396741587848305154" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUT4YhGmgI/AAAAAAAADtQ/4s8zhpN3k6k/s400/Birthday+Banner+13.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 258px;"/>
              </a>
              Overlap the holes of the two rectangles and secure with a brad.  Continue to do this until you spell out the entire phrase.  Tie string to the each end.  Hang for everyone to enjoy.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUYxjjxWmI/AAAAAAAADuA/Jbl4ePvKlQ8/s1600-h/Birthday+Banner+8.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
               <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5396746968111340130" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_kyYaMHB4tGw/SuUYxjjxWmI/AAAAAAAADuA/Jbl4ePvKlQ8/s400/Birthday+Banner+8.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 171px;"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              *Later in the week I'll be sharing how we celebrated my daughters birthday including decorations, food, and cake.
