           <a href="http://www.apieceofmonologue.com/2014/01/actors-samuel-beckett-plays-mckellen-dwan.html">
            Actors' Experiences of Samuel Beckett's Plays
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="post-header-line-1">
          </div>
          <div class="post-body entry-content">
           <h6>
            Holly Williams surveys actors' responses to some of Beckett's most difficult roles
           </h6>
           <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
            <tbody>
             <tr>
              <td style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ppHZcEq54EA/UtWqPD_6wWI/AAAAAAAAPMI/PcwaHWn9zns/s1600/ian-mckellen-beckett-godot.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ppHZcEq54EA/UtWqPD_6wWI/AAAAAAAAPMI/PcwaHWn9zns/s640/ian-mckellen-beckett-godot.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </td>
             </tr>
             <tr>
              <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
               Ian McKellen stars as Estragon (Gogo) in Samuel Beckett's
               <i>
                Waiting for Godot
               </i>
              </td>
             </tr>
            </tbody>
           </table>
           From
           <a href="http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/theatre-dance/features/up-to-their-necks-the-singular-joys-of-appearing-in-samuel-beckett-9051981.html" target="new">
            Holly Williams (
            <i>
             The Telegraph
            </i>
            )
           </a>
           :
           <br/>
           <blockquote>
            Samuel Beckett liked his actors to be up to their necks in it. In
            <i>
             Happy Days
            </i>
            , the protagonists head pokes out of a mound of earth; in
            <i>
             Play
            </i>
            , characters are incarcerated in urns; in
            <i>
             Endgame
            </i>
            , its dustbins. Sometimes he gets rid of their necks altogether: the actress in
            <i>
             Not I
            </i>
            must have her head strapped down, her body clamped still, so just her mouth can be lit. Demanding? Not half  and not just physically. Becketts bleak (if also blackly comic) texts also require actors to give up their innermost self; his favourite direction was dont act. [
            <a href="http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/theatre-dance/features/up-to-their-necks-the-singular-joys-of-appearing-in-samuel-beckett-9051981.html" target="new">
             Read More
            </a>
            ]
           </blockquote>
