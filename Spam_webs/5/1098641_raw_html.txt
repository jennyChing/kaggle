         <a href="http://lindawagner.net/blog/2014/09/paleo-bison-chili-kale/index.html" rel="bookmark" title="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale">
          Paleo Bison Chili With Kale
         </a>
        </h1>
        Tuesday, September 23rd, 2014
       </header>
       <section class="entry fix">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40477" height="533" onload="NcodeImageResizer.createOn(this);" src="http://lindawagner.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/MG_2690.jpg" title="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale" width="800"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://lindawagner.net/blog/2014/09/paleo-bison-chili-kale/index.html&amp;media=http://lindawagner.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/MG_2690.jpg&amp;description=Paleo Bison Chili With Kale')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         This chili is so easy to make and tastes amazing! Its super hearty and wonderfully filling on a cool autumn day. I chose to use Bison because it is a lean source of protein thats loaded with health benefits.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          Did you know that Bison is:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Lower in calories, fat,and cholesterol than chicken or fish.
         </li>
         <li>
          Higher in protein than beef.
         </li>
         <li>
          Nutrient-dense, flavor rich, outrageously lean, and high in antioxidant Omega 3 fatty acids and vitamin E. (
          <em>
           <a href="http://wildideabuffalo.com/healthier-red-meat/" target="_blank">
            source
           </a>
          </em>
          )
         </li>
         <li>
          High in beta-carotene. Beta-carotene is used in the body to make Vitamin A, a powerful anti-oxidant linked to preventing cancer and cardio-vascular disease.
         </li>
         <li>
          High inconjugated linoleic acid (CLA).
          <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
           In preliminary studies CLA, a fatty acid, has been linked to reducing the risk of cancer, diabetes and obesity. The benefits of CLA are only in free-range animals fed fresh green grass, not hay or grain. (
           <a href="http://www.bisonbasics.com/pastured/fully_grass_fed_and_finished.html" target="_blank">
            <em>
             source
            </em>
           </a>
           ) Always try to choose free-range grass fed meat and animal products!
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40476" height="800" onload="NcodeImageResizer.createOn(this);" src="http://lindawagner.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/MG_2687.jpg" title="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale" width="533"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://lindawagner.net/blog/2014/09/paleo-bison-chili-kale/index.html&amp;media=http://lindawagner.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/MG_2687.jpg&amp;description=Paleo Bison Chili With Kale')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          Carrots
         </strong>
         supply this dish with vitamin A, C, beta carotene,vitamin B1, vitamin B2, vitamin B6, vitamin K, biotin, fiber, potassium and thiamine.
         <a href="http://lindawagner.net/blog/2012/10/simple-roasted-veggies-for-weight-loss" target="_blank">
          Here are7 additional healthbenefits of carrots for human body
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          Cooked tomatoes
         </strong>
         are rich in vitamin C, lycopene, antioxidants, and have several anti-cancer properties. You can read more about the added health benefits of cooked tomatoes in
         <a href="http://www.news.cornell.edu/stories/2002/04/cooking-tomatoes-boosts-disease-fighting-power" target="_blank">
          this article from Cornell University
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          Cumin
         </strong>
         is a spice that can help control blood sugar levels and supports healthy digestion. Cumin is rich in magnesium which supportsheart health, controls blood pressure and aids the absorption of calcium. Cumin is also an excellent source of the essential mineral iron, with just 1 tablespoon supplying 20 percent of the daily value. (
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.livestrong.com/article/415653-ground-cumin-health-benefits/" target="_blank">
           source
          </a>
         </em>
         )
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          Kale
         </strong>
         . Ive talked about the
         <strong>
          <a href="http://lindawagner.net/blog/2012/10/does-kale-help-with-weight-loss" target="_blank">
           health benefits of kale
          </a>
         </strong>
         before but if you need a reminder: itsgreat for digestion, high in iron, contains vitamin K which can help protect against cancer, supports healthy bones, supports healthy liver function, anti-inflammatory, supports the cardiovascular system, supports healthy skin, the immune system, healthy vision, and healthy metabolism.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         With all of these amazing health benefits, you can actually feel good about what you are eating and serving for your family! Food as medicine!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40478" height="800" onload="NcodeImageResizer.createOn(this);" src="http://lindawagner.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/MG_2697.jpg" title="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale" width="533"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://lindawagner.net/blog/2014/09/paleo-bison-chili-kale/index.html&amp;media=http://lindawagner.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/MG_2697.jpg&amp;description=Paleo Bison Chili With Kale')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         Paleo Bison Chili With Kale
        </h2>
        <p>
         Youll need:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          1 pound ground grass fed bison
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          4 cloves garlic, minced I always use one of
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/B00EENF838?psc=1&amp;tag=vglnk-c407-20" target="_blank">
           these
          </a>
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          5 stalks celery, chopped
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          5 large carrots, peeled and chopped
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          1 large onion, diced
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          5 stalks kale  stems removed and leaves torn into pieces
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          2 tablespoons
          <a href="http://www.iherb.com/Frontier-Natural-Products-Organic-Chili-Powder-Salt-Free-Blend-2-08-oz-58-g/35929?gclid=CLGdpMic-MACFUs2aQod_TMAIQ&amp;gclsrc=aw.ds" target="_blank">
           chili powder
          </a>
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          1 teaspoon
          <a href="http://www.iherb.com/simply-organic-cumin-2-31-oz-65-g/31374" target="_blank">
           ground cumin
          </a>
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          1 large can (28 ounces) diced tomatoes
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          2 Tbs tomato paste
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          water
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          olive oil
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          sea salt and fresh cracked pepper
         </li>
         <li itemprop="ingredients">
          avocado as garnish  optional
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         In a large skilletover med-high heat, place a dash of olive oil, minced garlic, and bison. Cook, stirring frequently until bison is fully cooked.
        </p>
        <p>
         Next, use large pot or
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Dutch-Ovens-Cookware-Baking-Kitchen/b?ie=UTF8&amp;node=289818" target="_blank">
          dutch oven
         </a>
         over med-high heat to cook onion, celery, and carrots with a dash of olive oil. Add chili powder, cumin, about a tsp of sea salt, and stir well. Cook for a few minutes until onions begin to soften. Then add your cooked bison, canned tomatoes, tomato paste, and about 42 ounces of water (I used the empty can of diced tomatoes and filled it one and a half times with water as an easy measure). Taste and add more sea salt and cracked pepper to taste.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cover and let simmer for about 20mins.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remove from heat and stir in kale pieces. Serve as is or with avocado slices.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40479" height="800" onload="NcodeImageResizer.createOn(this);" src="http://lindawagner.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/MG_2700.jpg" title="Paleo Bison Chili With Kale" width="533"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://lindawagner.net/blog/2014/09/paleo-bison-chili-kale/index.html&amp;media=http://lindawagner.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/MG_2700.jpg&amp;description=Paleo Bison Chili With Kale')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         This is a great dish for picky eaters, meat &amp; potato people, or anyone else you want to ease into healthier eating!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         This dish is:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          low fat
         </li>
         <li>
          paleo
         </li>
         <li>
          carb free
         </li>
         <li>
          sugar free
         </li>
         <li>
          dairy free
         </li>
         <li>
          grain free
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         You could also make this dish vegan by subbing kidney beans and/or cooked quinoa for the bison meat. Feel free to add extra veggies that you may have on hand: bell peppers, butternut squash, zucchini, and/or cauliflower would all make a lovely addition!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I hope you and your family enjoy this healthy chili as much as I did!
        </p>
