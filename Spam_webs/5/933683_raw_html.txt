               <div>
                The Paper, the new Facebook application, come to the App Store with the proposal to unite the Timeline social networking with news and relevant issues, creating a sort of "final" reader content. Free, the app is only compatible with Apple gadgets running iOS 7.
               </div>
               <div>
               </div>
               <div>
                Basically, the Paper is a more beautiful, intuitive and complete Facebook. Without losing all their social nature, with access to friend requests, direct messages, notifications and updates to the feed, but with the "plus" a nice visuals and extra content.
               </div>
               <div>
               </div>
               <div>
                Almost everything a user does on the Facebook mobile can be done in the Paper, including post (managing pages is one of the few things that are not there). The difference is that the idea of Paper seems not only want to gather content in one place, but also allow users to have access to all without leaving your interface. So much so that the first login, the user is asked to customize your Paper, select the content categories in which most interested from a list with a large number of topics, from technology to sports.
               </div>
               <div>
               </div>
               <div>
                By default the app shows news feed Facebook first. To change the category, just slide your finger on the photo highlighted. Below are the links of these images and pictures of posts related to the subject. And like everything else on Facebook, you can enjoy, comment and share.
               </div>
               <div>
               </div>
               <div>
                Visually, and even in terms of features, the Paper reminds Flipboard, but with the difference only display content from Facebook. Even in the pages of categories, such as Sports, Technology, Fashion and others, the Paper shows no direct link to an article. First it shows a publication made in the social network that has that link. It is possible to enjoy, share and comment on any post, but read the articles without leaving the app by sliding your finger down on the screen up.
               </div>
