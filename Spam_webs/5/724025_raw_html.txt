           <em>
            <strong>
             Mobile phones, Internet access and social networking have opened many doors for teenagers to stay connected to one another.

             </a>

            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <a href="http://nobullying.com/combating-cyber-bullying-in-the-uk/" target="_blank" title="Cyberbullying Facts in the United Kingdom">
            <strong>
             Cyberbullying Facts in the United Kingdom
            </strong>
           </a>
          </h3>
          <p>
           The following numbers related to Cyber bullying Statistics are according to Liam Hackett (2013) in his Annual Bullying Survey taken from over 2,000 British teens.
          </p>
          <div id="UK_statistics1">
           <div class="left-col">
            <h3>
             In United Kingdom
            </h3>
            <p>
             According to Liam Hackett (2013) in his
             <br/>
             Annual Bullying Survey taken from over
            </p>
            <h2>
             2,000
            </h2>
            <p>
             British teens
            </p>
            <div class="seven-in-ten">
             <h2>
              69%
             </h2>
             <p>
              <strong>
               7
              </strong>
              in
              <strong>
               10
              </strong>
             </p>
            </div>
            <p>
             Young people aged
             <strong>
              13
             </strong>
             and
             <strong>
              22
             </strong>
             <br/>
             had experienced Cyber bullying
            </p>
           </div>
           <div class="right-col">
            <ul>
             <li>
              <strong>
               20%
              </strong>
              Of which had been very extreme.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               37%
              </strong>
              Of this experience bullying frequently.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               20%
              </strong>
              Also had underwent extreme cases &amp; were twice as likely to be bullied in Facebook than any other sites.
             </li>
             <li>
              With
              <strong>
               54%
              </strong>
              Had underwent extreme cases and were twice as likely to be bullied in Facebook than any other sites.
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>

           <h3>
            Cyber bullying Statistics 2013: What is Cyber Bullying?
           </h3>
           <p>
            Cyber bullying is bullying behavior (tormenting, threatening, harassment, etc.) that takes place through electronic mediums, including the Internet and mobile phones. This form of bullying can take on various forms, including:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Delivering threats or hurtful messages to someone via email or text message
            </li>
            <li>
             Spreading false rumors through text message, online boards or social networking sites.
            </li>
            <li>
             Leaving hurtful, harassing or threatening messages on web pages or social networking sites.
            </li>
            <li>
             Impersonating someone else online to harass or hurt another person.
            </li>
            <li>
             Spreading unflattering or sexually suggestive pictures of another person and spreading them via Internet or cell phones.
            </li>
            <li>
             Cyber bullying is something that affects teens of all races and genders. Recent statistics show that boys are more likely to receive threats from cyber bullies that girls, while girls are just as likely as boys to engage in cyber bullying or fall victim to cyber bullying.
            </li>
            <li>
             The act of cyber bullying itself is often fluid enough for the bully to become the victim and vice-versa. Often times, a target of bullying can easily become an aggressor, while someone who attempt to defend a target of bullying ends up becoming a target themselves.
            </li>
            <li>
             Cyber bullying is a form of teen violence that has lasting and even deadly repercussions for many teenagers. Its also a form of violence that most parents dont find out about until it is too late, since over half of young teens who experience or witnessed online bullying do not tell their parents.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Cyber bullying Statistics 2013: How to Stop &amp; Prevent Cyber Bullying
            </strong>
           </p>

             parents and authority figures can help reduce the prevalence of cyber bullying

            <a href="http://nobullying.com/dangers-sexting/" target="_blank" title="especially if the messages are threatening or sexual in nature">
             especially if the messages are threatening or sexual in nature
            </a>
            . There are other ways parents and
            <a href="http://nobullying.com/cyber-safety-tips-for-teens-and-pre-teens/" target="_blank" title="teens can help stop cyber bullying">
             teens can help stop cyber bullying
            </a>
            in its tracks:
           </p>

            <li>
             Teens should never share personal information online or meet people they only know online.
