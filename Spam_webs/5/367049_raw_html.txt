                     <center>

                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      Most of these will be items to buy, so get your lists ready and make some notes.
                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <b>
                       Pregnancy Tests: Snag it
                      </b>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <center style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       The Dollar Store has really accurate pregnancy tests
                      </center>
                      <center style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       Several of my readers have told me they saved a lot of money buying these tests
                      </center>
                      <center style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       They really are more accurate than some of the brand name tests you can pick up for $15 a piece.
                      </center>
                      <center style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       Don't believe me?
                      </center>
                      <center style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       Compare them with other brands
                       <a href="http://www.babyhopes.com/pregnancy-test-sensitivity-comparison.html" style="color: #999999; text-decoration: none;">
                        here
                       </a>
                      </center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <img src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JjObsAW5A7c/TzmEdayiTCI/AAAAAAAAM4g/EPWU0OLC_I8/s640/ds18.jpg"/>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <b>
                       Glow Sticks: Snag it
                      </b>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      Kids like glow sticks
                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <img src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-LoghiA1B-Ug/T1T6rOjmHhI/AAAAAAAANFI/oazokv0SaUE/s640/dt53.jpg"/>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <b>
                       Crayola explosion gear: Snag it
                      </b>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      Yesterday, I stopped in and found that our local store got a shipment of Crayola Glow Expressions kits.
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      They had sand art, glow stick kits (with 40 glow sticks included), and glow paint.
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      I snagged some to put in the kids Easter baskets.
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      Such a great deal since they are currently selling on-line for $10-$15 a piece.
                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <br/>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <b>
                       Puzzles: Snag it
                      </b>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       I give my snag it seal of approval on puzzles,
                       <b>
                        but
                       </b>
                       only on puzzles under 150 pieces.
                      </div>
                      <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       There are many big name character puzzles that would cost you much more at the regular store.
                      </div>
                      <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       I found that anything more than 150 pieces gets flimsy.
                      </div>
                      <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       So stay away from the 500 piece puzzles.
                      </div>
                      <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       Also, the board puzzles made for toddlers should be avoided.
                      </div>
                      <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       The pieces just don't fit together well and are just plain frustrating.
                      </div>
                      <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
                       If it's made by a big name company like Marvel or Disney, snag it, it will make for hours of fun
                      </div>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <img src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-VZarhCU0cPM/T1T6tQBXDxI/AAAAAAAANFQ/-f72IgQkj4s/s640/dt54.jpg"/>
                     </center>

                     </center>

                      </div>

                     </center>

                     </center>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 20px;">
