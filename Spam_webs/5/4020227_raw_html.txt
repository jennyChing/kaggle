             10 
        Habits of Highly Effective Brains

     </h1>

     <p>
      <font color="#000000" face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font face="Georgia, Times New Roman, Times, serif">
         By 
        Alvaro Fernandez
         <br/>
         Source:
         <font color="#0000FF">
         </font>
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
      <font color="#66CCFF" face="Georgia, Times New Roman, Times, serif" size="2">
       <font color="#0000FF">
        <b>
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/alvaro-fernandez/10-habits-of-highly-effec_b_77369.html" target="_blank">
          Huffington 
        Post
         </a>
        </b>
       </font>
      </font>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <strong>
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       If you are 
        reading this, the good news is that you have a brain inside your head. 
        And you have probably read about the emerging brain fitness movement: 
        frequent articles in the media, an ongoing PBS special, more and more 
        products and games.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Newsweek's
       <a href="http://www.mindpowernews.com/MarvelOrMess.htm" target="_blank">
        Sharon 
        Begley
       </a>
       recently wrote that "With the nation's 78 million baby 
        boomers approaching the age of those dreaded "where did I leave my 
        keys?" moments, it's no wonder the market for computer-based brain 
        training has shot up from essentially zero in 2005 to $80 million this 
        year, according to the consulting firm SharpBrains."
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Now, before 
        you embark on buying any of those programs, you should know that there 
        is a lot we can do without spending a dime. Based on dozens of interviews 
        with scientists and recent research findings, let's take a look at some 
        of the habits of Highly Effective Brains:
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3">
       <b>
        1. Learn 
        what is the "It" in "Use It or Lose It".
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       A basic understanding 
        will serve you well to appreciate your brain's beauty as a living and 
        constantly-developing dense forest with billions of neurons and synapses.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3">
       <b>
        2. Take 
        care of your nutrition.
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Did you know 
        that the brain only weighs 2% of body mass but consumes over 20% of the 
        oxygen and nutrients we intake? As a general rule, you don't need expensive 
        ultra-sophisticated nutritional supplements, just make sure you don't 
        stuff yourself with the "bad stuff".
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font size="3">
         3. 
        Remember that the brain is part of the body.
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Things that 
        exercise your body can also help sharpen your brain: physical exercise 
        enhances neurogenesis.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font size="3">
         4. 
        Practice positive, future-oriented thoughts until they become your default 
        mindset and you look forward to every new day in a constructive way.
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Stress and 
        anxiety, no matter whether induced by external events or by your own thoughts, 
        actually kills neurons and prevents the creation of new ones. You can 
        think of chronic stress as the opposite of exercise: it prevents the creation 
        of new neurons.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font size="3">
         5. 
        Thrive on Learning and Mental Challenges.
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       The point 
        of having a brain is precisely to learn and to adapt to challenging new 
        environments. Once new neurons appear in your brain, where they stay in 
        your brain and how long they survive depends on how you use them. "Use 
        It or Lose It" does not mean "do crossword puzzle number 1,234,567". 
        It means, "challenge your brain often with fundamentally new activities."
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font size="3">
         6. 
        We are (as far as we know) the only self-directed organisms in this planet. 
        Aim high.
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Once you 
        graduate from college, keep learning. The brain keeps developing, no matter 
        your age, and it reflects what you do with it.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font size="3">
         7. 
        Explore, travel.
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Adapting 
        to new locations forces you to pay more attention to your environment. 
        Make new decisions, use your brain.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font size="3">
         8. 
        Don't Outsource Your Brain.
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Not to media 
        personalities, not to politicians, not to your smart neighbor, not to 
        this blogger... Make your own decisions, and mistakes. And learn from 
        them. That way, you are training your brain, not your neighbor's.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font size="3">
         9. 
        Develop and maintain stimulating friendships.
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       We are "social 
        animals", and need social interaction. Which, by the way, is why 
        the Baby Einstein series has been shown not to be the panacea for children 
        development.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       <b>
        <font size="3">
         10. 
        Laugh. Often.
        </font>
       </b>
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Especially 
        to cognitively complex humor, full of twists and surprises. Better, try 
        to become the next Jon Stewart, and create your own unique humor.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
       Keep in mind 
        that what counts is not reading this article - or any other one - but 
        practicing a bit every day until small steps snowball into unstoppable, 
        internalized habits... so, pick your next battle and try to start improving 
        at least one of these 10 habits during the holidays!
      </font>
     </p>
