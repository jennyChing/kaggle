     <p>
      And finally the cakes! (Dont forget to check outmy
      <a href="http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/merida-cake-tutorial/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/merida-cake-tutorial/', 'Merida');" title="Merida Cake Tutorial">
       Merida
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/princess-rapunzel-cake-tutorial/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/princess-rapunzel-cake-tutorial/', 'Rapunzel');" title="Princess Rapunzel cake tutorial">
       Rapunzel
      </a>
      and
      <a href="http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/princess-tiana-birthday-cake/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/princess-tiana-birthday-cake/', 'Tiana');" title="Princess Tiana Birthday Cake">
       Tiana
      </a>
      Princess cakes as well)
     </p>
     <p>
      Today Im Sharing the Anna tutorial and tomorrow well do
      <a href="http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/frozen-cake-tutorial-elsa/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/frozen-cake-tutorial-elsa/', 'Elsa');" title="Frozen Princess Cake Elsa">
       Elsa
      </a>
      ! Making these cakes was so much fun! Looking back there are a few things Id change. The week of the party the baby got sick and I got behind.So I felt kind of rushed through the cakes, I MIGHT actually break my no repeat rule to add the final details I feel were missing from these.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <center>
      <a title="tutorial to create your own Anna princess cake for your Frozen birthday party">
       <img alt="tutorial to create your own Anna princess cake for your Frozen birthday party" height="700" src="//farm6.staticflickr.com/5519/14170694062_d6d7d8b039_o.jpg" width="460"/>
      </a>
     </center>
     <br/>
     <span id="more-10441">
     </span>
     <p>
      So Anna. The first question is WHICH dress to make. I debated doing her ball dress, BUT since we werent doing ELSAs ball gown I decided not to. Her hiking winter dress is actually shorter, showing her ankles and boots, but of course for this I made it full length. Its also full than it is in the movie, to leave enough cake to actually EAT!
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="princess-cake">
       <img alt="princess-cake" height="460" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7416/13986729350_fb1523e514_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Start with a
      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;pageMinusResults=1&amp;suo=1399952868624&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts#/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts&amp;field-keywords=clay%20extruder&amp;sprefix=clay+e%2Carts-crafts&amp;rh=i%3Aarts-crafts%2Ck%3Aclay%20extruder&amp;sepatfbtf=true&amp;tc=1399952870995&amp;linkId=RKKOAF3EGBUV7XLQ" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;pageMinusResults=1&amp;suo=1399952868624&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts#/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts&amp;field-keywords=clay%20extruder&amp;sprefix=clay+e%2Carts-crafts&amp;rh=i%3Aarts-crafts%2Ck%3Aclay%20extruder&amp;sepatfbtf=true&amp;tc=1399952870995&amp;linkId=RKKOAF3EGBUV7XLQ', '12 inch Anna doll');">
       12 inch Anna doll
      </a>
      , a Wonder Mold cake and a second cake, an 8 inch round. I do this because the wonder mold cake isnt tall enough for the dolls legs. Also my chocolate cake is very DENSE, its best for carving, so it tends to shrink a bit too, making it even SHORTER. So the extra height is key (unless you tear the dolls legs off)
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="carving-princess-cake-anna">
       <img alt="carving-princess-cake-anna" height="460" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7360/14170694392_bf8fdda2b7_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Use
      <a href="http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/ganache-3-frosting-truffles-pouring/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.imtopsyturvy.com/ganache-3-frosting-truffles-pouring/', 'Ganache');" title="Ganache  3 uses  frosting, truffles &amp; pouring">
       Ganache
      </a>
      to stick the cake layers together. Then wrap the dolls legs in plastic.
     </p>
     <ol>
      <li>
       to keep their legs together do they dont separate while going into the cake
      </li>
      <li>
       keep the cake food safe, and the doll clean.
      </li>
     </ol>
     <p>
      I cut a small hole into the cake first to guide the legs. Notice even with the extra height the cake still falls short of her hips, dont worry about it, I use the ganache to fill it in.
     </p>
     <p>
      Then start carving around your skirt, I find that carving it makes it look more skirt like and less cake-ish. Start small, you can always carve away more, you cant carve less once its gone. Once your happy with them carve the sharp edges to soften the curves.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <center>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cakes">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cakes" height="650" src="//farm6.staticflickr.com/5528/14150209636_3a44461ffe_o.jpg" width="460"/>
      </a>
     </center>
     <p>
      Once your done carving wipe away the crumbs and frost with ganache. Make sure to get into all the cracks and fill in the hip area. Smooth as good as you can, then let it set. Once its set you can rub any points and theyll melt and smooth away! I love working with ganache!
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <center>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-skirt">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-skirt" height="650" src="//farm3.staticflickr.com/2939/14150209606_b7f8f86a44_o.jpg" width="460"/>
      </a>
     </center>
     <p>
      Then cover your skirt with a purplish navy fondant, making sure to smooth into the cracks. Because there will be a cake I just wrapped it around and created a clean, straight, seam up the back. Trim around the base and around the waist, creating the pointed princess waist line
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-gumpaste">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-gumpaste" height="460" src="//farm3.staticflickr.com/2923/13986736808_3e4b52d24e_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Now for the shirt. We want to roll this SUPER thin so I do a half fondant half gumpaste mixture and dye it turqouise. Then use my
      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;pageMinusResults=1&amp;suo=1399952868624&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts#/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts&amp;field-keywords=clay%20extruder&amp;sprefix=clay+e%2Carts-crafts&amp;rh=i%3Aarts-crafts%2Ck%3Aclay%20extruder&amp;sepatfbtf=true&amp;tc=1399952870995&amp;linkId=UESWKDBUMB3KYQNH" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;pageMinusResults=1&amp;suo=1399952868624&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts#/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts&amp;field-keywords=clay%20extruder&amp;sprefix=clay+e%2Carts-crafts&amp;rh=i%3Aarts-crafts%2Ck%3Aclay%20extruder&amp;sepatfbtf=true&amp;tc=1399952870995&amp;linkId=UESWKDBUMB3KYQNH', 'pasta roller');">
       pasta roller
      </a>
      to get it super thin. Put your arms how you want them brush clear vanilla (or vodka  cheaper) on to the back of the gumpaste/fondant then lay itover the front and arms (sticky side down). cut away areas you dont need to help shape it
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-shirt">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-shirt" height="460" src="//farm3.staticflickr.com/2910/13986738687_3846a72681_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      wrap it around the arms, shoulders, neck, etc cut a straight light where the it meets under the arms to hid the lines. Dont forget to cut the nice neckline as well (I forgot before the picture but did it later).
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-top">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-top" height="460" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7343/14173412324_9c2a5e9cd8_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      for the bodice rather than try to cut it ON the doll and get it to stick I cut the neckline out first adding extra length in the straps and the sides for wrapping it. Then paint the back with the vanilla/vodka and place it on the doll. Be VERY careful when doing this, the sticky back side WILL stain your turqouise shirt if you get it out of place.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-bodice">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-bodice" height="460" src="//farm3.staticflickr.com/2910/14170058101_5c3ea921a0_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      press it into place in the front, cutting the waistline. Then wrap it around the back, pressing it into the doll so you dont get bubbles. Stretching it a bit where you need to, and cut a nice clean line down the back.
     </p>
     <p>
      Now the bare bones of the dress are done! time for the details!
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-stencil">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-stencil" height="460" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7431/13986711539_d417c6baa6_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      To get the details of the embroidered front of the bodice I used one of my favorite things,
      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;keywords=martha%20stewart%20adhesive%20stencil&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;qid=1399952723&amp;rh=i%3Aaps%2Ck%3Amartha%20stewart%20adhesive%20stencil&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;linkId=WJNV23ZWSZPFQJS2" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;keywords=martha%20stewart%20adhesive%20stencil&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;qid=1399952723&amp;rh=i%3Aaps%2Ck%3Amartha%20stewart%20adhesive%20stencil&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;linkId=WJNV23ZWSZPFQJS2', 'adhesive stencils');">
       adhesive stencils
      </a>
      ! There are a lot of options with small details
      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B007C7XT5M/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B007C7XT5M&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;linkId=N7GUYD7UMYIELUOB" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B007C7XT5M/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B007C7XT5M&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;linkId=N7GUYD7UMYIELUOB', 'this is the one');">
       this is the one
      </a>
      I used. Then I colored some icing green and burgundy. You can use buttercream if you have some, I opted to make a quick batch of royal icing.
     </p>
     <p>
      Place the stencil going down the center, add the green where you want it, not too thick, and then add a little bit of the burgundy. then pull off the stencil (just make sure, unlike me, you let your black fondant dry completely first or it will pull away a bit.).
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-gold">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-gold" height="460" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7404/14150209406_a222781f0b_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      I used a
      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;pageMinusResults=1&amp;suo=1399952868624&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts#/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts&amp;field-keywords=clay%20extruder&amp;sprefix=clay+e%2Carts-crafts&amp;rh=i%3Aarts-crafts%2Ck%3Aclay%20extruder&amp;sepatfbtf=true&amp;tc=1399952870995&amp;linkId=JBS6M7W77BPN6NCI" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/?_encoding=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;pageMinusResults=1&amp;suo=1399952868624&amp;tag=imtotu-20&amp;url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts#/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Darts-crafts&amp;field-keywords=clay%20extruder&amp;sprefix=clay+e%2Carts-crafts&amp;rh=i%3Aarts-crafts%2Ck%3Aclay%20extruder&amp;sepatfbtf=true&amp;tc=1399952870995&amp;linkId=JBS6M7W77BPN6NCI', 'clay extruder');">
       clay extruder
      </a>
      (that I ONLY use with fondant and gumpaste, NOT clay) for the edging. Soften some goldfondant by kneading some extra shortening into it, then extrude it through one of the smaller holes. Attach it to the edge of the bodice using the vanilla/vodka and then for a final step paint it with some gold airbrush paint!
     </p>
     <p>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-embroidery">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-embroidery" height="460" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7371/13986738587_66724b8e07_o.jpg" width="700"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Now for the bottom of the skirt, again you can hand paint but I used another self adhesive stencil and the green and burgundy royal icing again. I started by doing a center one, then working my way around both sides. The great thing about the adhesive stencil is it sticks inside the nooks and crannies of the skirt better than a traditional stencil would.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <center>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-caplet">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-anna-caplet" height="650" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7392/13986738507_d314325afe_o.jpg" width="460"/>
      </a>
     </center>
     <p>
      Finally roll out some burgundy/brown fondant. Cut along piece, rounding the edges, and let it dry a bit (flat or rounded) then attach it down the back to her shoulders. Once its all set roll out another small amt cut a circle with a hold in the center and wrap it around her shoulders. Then trip the neck and front.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <center>
      <a title="Frozen-princess-cake-anna">
       <img alt="Frozen-princess-cake-anna" height="650" src="//farm3.staticflickr.com/2934/13986711469_02ccd47911_o.jpg" width="460"/>
      </a>
     </center>
     <p>
      Here is themistake I made, I cut the fronts square, ISHOULD have cut them rounded! GAH! and I wish Id had the time to paint the edges a darker shade. Oh well, overall I LOVE how Anna turned out!
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <center>
      <a title="princess-cakes">
       <img alt="princess-cakes" height="650" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7437/14173364545_21ee3fcc2a_o.jpg" width="460"/>
      </a>
     </center>
     <p>
      I think they look GREAT together too! AND the birthday girl love love LOVED them so nothing else really matters!
     </p>
