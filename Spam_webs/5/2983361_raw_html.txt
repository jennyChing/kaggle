       <div class="headline_area">
        <h1>
         Knitting Scouts
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Welcome to the Illustrious Order of Cast On Knitting Scouts!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Wherein it is acknowledged that members are:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Not opposed to alcohol.
         </li>
         <li>
          Into badges.
         </li>
         <li>
          Mostly in agreement that there is no right way to knit.
         </li>
         <li>
          Committed to diligent positive and accurate presentation of knitting and knitters, to editors, producers, directors, and those generally presumed to be part of The Media in an effort to close the gap of
          <em>
           Public Knitting Literacy
          </em>
          .
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         If you think you have what it takes to be a Cast On Knitting Scout, right click on a badge, and grab one for your blog. (It is probable that youve earned one or two already, without knowing it). Share the tale of how you earned it, or why you think you deserve it, and do your part to 
         <a href="http://www.yarnharlot.ca/blog/archives/2007/02/16/represent.html">
          Represent
         </a>
          and close the gap of Public Knitting Literacy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Finally, if youve got an idea for a most excellent badge that doesnt take it self too seriously, and doesnt yet exist, please leave your suggestion in the comments below, and well see what we can do about it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/talking.jpg" title="talking.jpg">
          <img alt="talking.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/talking.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="talkknitting">
          <strong>
           The Proselytize Knitting Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          A requirement for all Knitting Scouts, the recipient must do his or her bit to present knitting in a positive light, whilst at the same time avoiding all references to hipness,  grandmothers, and yoga.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/macgyver1.jpg" title="macgyver1.jpg">
          <img alt="macgyver1.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/macgyver1.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="macgyver1">
          <strong>
           The MacGyver Badge (Level One)
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient must demonstrate clever use of a non-knitting tool in a knitting-related scenario. For instance, recipient has used paper clips as stitch markers, or successfully whittled and then utilized bamboo skewers as dpns.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/macgyver2.jpg" title="macgyver2.jpg">
          <img alt="macgyver2.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/macgyver2.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="macgyver2">
          <strong>
           The MacGyver Badge (Level Two)
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient must demonstrate clever use of a knitting tool in a non-knitting-related scenario. For instance, recipient has used a strand of Regia Bamboo to slice cheese, or repaired a small appliance with a metal knitting needle.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/macgyver3.jpg" title="macgyver3.jpg">
          <img alt="macgyver3.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/macgyver3.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="macgyver3">
          <strong>
           The MacGyver Badge (Level Three)
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient must demonstrate clever use of a knitting tool in a non-knitting-related scenario working towards the Greater Good. For instance, recipient used a Clover yarn cutter, a 30 Addi Turbo circular needle, and a copy of Elizabeth Zimmermans Knitting Without Tears to foster world peace.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/interweave.jpg" title="interweave.jpg">
          <img alt="interweave.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/interweave.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/vouge.jpg" title="vouge.jpg">
          <img alt="vouge.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/vouge.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/knitty1.jpg" title="knitty1.jpg">
          <img alt="knitty1.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/knitty1.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="beenpublished">
          <strong>
           The Ive Been Published in a Knitting Magazine badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient has been published in a print or online knitting magazine.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/interweave_rejected.jpg" title="interweave_rejected.jpg">
          <img alt="interweave_rejected.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/interweave_rejected.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/vouge_rejected.jpg" title="vouge_rejected.jpg">
          <img alt="vouge_rejected.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/vouge_rejected.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/knitty_rejected.jpg" title="knitty_rejected.jpg">
          <img alt="knitty_rejected.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/knitty_rejected.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="beenrejected">
          <strong>
           The Ive Been Rejected by a Knitting Magazine Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient has had a design or article rejected by a print or online knitting magazine.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/book_about_knitting.jpg" title="book_about_knitting.jpg">
          <img alt="book_about_knitting.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/book_about_knitting.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="writtenbook">
          <strong>
           The Ive Written a Knitting Book Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient has written and published a book in which knitting patterns and beautifully photographed knitting feature prominently; Or, the recipient has written and published a book in which he/she writes eloquently about the knitting experience. No extra credit, but proper snaps if the recipient actually makes any money at it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/broadcast.jpg" title="broadcast.jpg">
          <img alt="broadcast.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/broadcast.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="hostpodcast">
          <strong>
           The I Host a Knitting Podcast Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient hosts a podcast in which knitting is a primary focus. Presumes a thorough understanding of rss feeds, and skype recording with minimal audio disruption. Recipient must demonstrate ability to make oneself sound way smarter in a podcast than in person.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/asshole.jpg" title="asshole">
          <img alt="asshole" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/asshole.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="asshole">
          <strong>
           The I Can Be an Asshole When It Comes To Knitting Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          In which the recipient is so passionate, opinionated and entirely convinced of his/her own superior knowledge about all things knitting, that he/she may appear pompous, rude, or self-righteous.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/drinking.jpg" title="drinking.jpg">
          <img alt="drinking.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/drinking.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="kui">
          <strong>
           The Knitting Whilst Under the Influence Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          This applies to both actual knitting under the influence, as well as achieving moments of stunning intellectual clarity about ones knitting under the influence. Presumes talking about knitting whilst under the influence a given.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/math1.jpg" title="math1.jpg">
          <img alt="math1.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/math1.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="math1">
          <strong>
           The I Will Impress You With My Math Prowess Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient is a whiz at substituting yarns and calculating gauge, can space increases and decreases evenly and is fully comfortable with the basic math encountered in all knitting projects.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/math2.jpg" title="math2">
          <img alt="math2" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/math2.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="math2">
          <strong>
           The I Will Crush You With My Math Prowess Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          The recipient has applied the principles of higher mathematics to knitting including, but not limited to hyperbolic planes, Fibonacci sequences, Klein bottles, Moebius strips, fractals and Flying Spaghetti Monster hats.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/novelty_yarn.jpg" title="novelty_yarn.jpg">
          <img alt="novelty_yarn.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/novelty_yarn.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="noveltyyarn">
          <strong>
           The Inordinately Fond of Novelty Yarn Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          In which the recipient professes an arguably unhealthy affinity for yarn with slubs, sparkles, spangles, fur, feathery bits, and an unconscionable proportion of man-made fibre. Recipient makes no apology for the preference.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/medical1.jpg" title="med1">
          <img alt="med1" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/medical1.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="medical1">
          <strong>
           The Knitting Has Forced Me to Seek Medical Attention Badge (Level One)
          </strong>
         </a>
          In which the recipient has been forced to seek the advice of a medical doctor, nurse, or alternative healthcare professional for injuries sustained as a result of knitting.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/medical2.jpg" title="med2">
          <img alt="med2" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/medical2.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="medical2">
          <strong>
           The Knitting Has Forced Me to Seek Medical Attention Badge (Level Two)
          </strong>
         </a>
          In which the recipient has been forced to seek the advice of a medical doctor, nurse, or alternative healthcare professional, in a hospital emergency room, for injuries sustained as a result of knitting.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/08/unneccessaryobjects.jpg" title="unneccessaryobjects.jpg">
          <img alt="unneccessaryobjects.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/08/unneccessaryobjects.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="noapplication">
          <strong>
           The Ive Knit Items With No Conceivable Practical Application Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          Recipients are those special campers who have knit items which somehow missed the mark of their intended application. There are probably more who are deserving of this badge than one would expect.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/08/throughdivorce.jpg" title="throughdivorce.jpg">
          <img alt="throughdivorce.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/08/throughdivorce.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="throughdivorce">
          <strong>
           The Knitting Got Me Through My Divorce Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          Better for you than wine, easier to care for than a houseful of cats, knitting probably kept you busy, and definitely kept you sane, while you navigated your way back to single life. Youre better off without him/her, honey.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/divorce.jpg" title="divorce.jpg">
          <img alt="divorce.jpg" src="http://www.cast-on.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/divorce.jpg"/>
         </a>
         <a name="causeddivorce">
          <strong>
           The Knitting Caused My Divorce Badge
          </strong>
         </a>
          Maybe your spouse didnt fully appreciate your chosen lifestyle, or maybe you were caught in a lie about the frequency you were having S.E.X. outside the relationship, either way you can trace beginning of the end back to your obsessive need to knit.
