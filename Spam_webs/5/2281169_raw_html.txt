     <p>
      <b>
       Todd Morgan Beamer
      </b>
      (November 24, 1968  September 11, 2001) was an American software salesman and passenger aboard
      <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines_Flight_93" title="United Airlines Flight 93">
       United Airlines Flight 93
      </a>
      which was
      <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_hijacking" title="Aircraft hijacking">
       hijacked
      </a>
      as part of the
      <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">
       September 11 attacks
      </a>
      in 2001. He was one of the passengers who tried to reclaim the aircraft from the hijackers, leading them to crash it into a field in
      <a href="/wiki/Stonycreek_Township,_Cambria_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Stonycreek Township, Cambria County, Pennsylvania">
       Stonycreek Township
      </a>
      near
      <a href="/wiki/Shanksville,_Pennsylvania" title="Shanksville, Pennsylvania">
       Shanksville
      </a>
      , Pennsylvania.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBSNews_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-CBSNews-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Todd_Beamer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Beamer was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Flint,_Michigan" title="Flint, Michigan">
       Flint, Michigan
      </a>
      , to David Beamer, an IBM sales representative, and Peggy Jackson Beamer, a muralist,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the middle child of three and only son.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Beamer and his two sisters, Melissa and Michele, were raised "with a strong biblical value system and work ethic". The family relocated to
      <a href="/wiki/Poughkeepsie,_New_York" title="Poughkeepsie, New York">
       Poughkeepsie, New York
      </a>
      , and then to
      <a href="/wiki/Wheaton,_Illinois" title="Wheaton, Illinois">
       Wheaton, Illinois
      </a>
      , a suburb west of Chicago, where David worked at Amdahl, a computer technology company.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Beamer attended Wheaton Christian Grammar School, where he played baseball, basketball, and soccer.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He attended
      <a href="/wiki/Wheaton_Academy" title="Wheaton Academy">
       Wheaton Academy
      </a>
      , a Christian high school, from 1983 to 1985,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABCNews9.13.11_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ABCNews9.13.11-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      where he excelled in the same sports.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was elected class vice president in his junior year. After David was promoted to vice president of Amdahl's California headquarters, the family moved, and Beamer spent his senior year at Los Gatos High School, just south of San Jose, California.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Beamer attended
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fresno_State_University" title="Fresno State University">
       Fresno State University
      </a>
      , where he majored in physical therapy and played baseball, in the hopes of playing professionally, but injuries he suffered in an automobile accident ended these plans. He returned home to Illinois and transferred to
      <a href="/wiki/Wheaton_College_(Illinois)" title="Wheaton College (Illinois)">
       Wheaton College
      </a>
      , a Christian university, where he met
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lisa_Brosious" title="Lisa Brosious">
       Lisa Brosious
      </a>
      , his future wife, during a senior seminar class.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Their first date was November 2, 1991, a date whose 10-year anniversary they had been planning to celebrate at the time of his death.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He transferred to Wheaton College, where he majored initially in medicine before switching to business. He continued to play baseball and as a senior became captain of the basketball team.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He graduated in 1991.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WheatonCenter_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-WheatonCenter-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He subsequently worked for Wilson Sporting Goods while taking night classes at
      <a href="/wiki/DePaul_University" title="DePaul University">
       DePaul University
      </a>
      , earning an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M.B.A." title="M.B.A.">
       M.B.A.
      </a>
      in June 1993.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After graduating Beamer married Brosious on May 14, 1994 in Peekskill, New York, and they moved to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plainsboro,_New_Jersey" title="Plainsboro, New Jersey">
       Plainsboro, New Jersey
      </a>
      , where Beamer began working with
      <a href="/wiki/Oracle_Corporation" title="Oracle Corporation">
       Oracle Corporation
      </a>
      , selling systems applications and database software as a field marketing representative.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Within months, Beamer was promoted to account manager.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Beamer and Lisa taught Sunday school at Princeton Alliance Church for six years, and worked in youth ministry.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Beamer also played on the church softball team. He was a staunch fan of the
      <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">
       Chicago Cubs
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bulls" title="Chicago Bulls">
       Chicago Bulls
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bears" title="Chicago Bears">
       Chicago Bears
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2000 the Beamers moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Cranbury,_New_Jersey" title="Cranbury, New Jersey">
       Cranbury, New Jersey
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChristianityToday_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ChristianityToday-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      with their two sons.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChristianityToday_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ChristianityToday-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      Beamer's work forced him to travel up to four times a month, sometimes for as long as a week. In 2001, he earned a five-day trip to Italy with his wife for being a top sales performer. They returned home on Monday, September 10, at 5 pm (EST). While Beamer could have left that night for a Tuesday business meeting in California, he opted instead to spend time with his family. His wife was due the following January with their third child. He left home at 6:15 am the next morning, to take an early flight from Newark to San Francisco to meet with representatives of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Corporation" title="Sony Corporation">
       Sony Corporation
      </a>
      at 1:00pm, planning to return on a
      <a href="/wiki/Red-eye_flight" title="Red-eye flight">
       red-eye flight
      </a>
      that night.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostGazette10.28.01_6-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-PostGazette10.28.01-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      United Flight 93 was scheduled to depart at 8:00am, but the Boeing 757 did not depart until 42 minutes later due to runway traffic delays. Six minutes later,
      <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines_Flight_11" title="American Airlines Flight 11">
       American Airlines Flight 11
      </a>
      crashed into the World Trade Center's North Tower. 15 minutes later, at 9:03 am, as United Flight 175 crashed into the South Tower, United 93 was climbing to cruising altitude, heading west over New Jersey and into Pennsylvania. At 9:25 am, Flight 93 was above eastern Ohio, and its pilot radioed Cleveland controllers to inquire about an alert that had been flashed on his cockpit computer screen to "beware of cockpit intrusion." Three minutes later, Cleveland controllers could hear screams over the cockpit's open microphone. Moments later, the hijackers, led by the Lebanese
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ziad_Samir_Jarrah" title="Ziad Samir Jarrah">
       Ziad Samir Jarrah
      </a>
      , took over the plane's controls, disengaged the autopilot, and told passengers, "Keep remaining sitting. We have a bomb on board." Beamer and the other passengers were herded into the back of the plane. The curtain between first class and second class had been drawn, at which point Beamer saw the pilot and co-pilot lying dead on the floor just outside the curtain, their throats having been cut, as a flight attendant informed him. Within six minutes, the plane changed course and was heading for
      <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">
       Washington, D.C.
      </a>
      . Several of the passengers made phone calls to loved ones, who informed them about the two planes that had crashed into the World Trade Center. Beamer tried to place a credit card call through a phone located on the back of a plane seat, but was routed to a customer-service representative, who passed him on to GTE airphone supervisor Lisa Jefferson. With FBI agents listening in on their call, Beamer informed Jefferson that hijackers had taken over United 93, that one passenger had been killed, and mentioned the dead pilots. He also stated that two of the hijackers had knives, and that one appeared to have a bomb strapped around his waist. When the hijackers veered the plane sharply south, Beamer briefly panicked, exclaiming, "We're going down! We're going down!"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>

     <p>
      Following this, the passengers and flight crew decided to act.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">

         [

      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bingham" title="Mark Bingham">
       Mark Bingham
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tom_Burnett" title="Tom Burnett">
       Tom Burnett
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Glick" title="Jeremy Glick">
       Jeremy Glick
      </a>
      formed a plan to take the plane back from the hijackers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YahooNews_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-YahooNews-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>

      They were joined by other passengers, including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lou_Nacke" title="Lou Nacke">
       Lou Nacke
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rich_Guadagno" title="Rich Guadagno">

      </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alan_Beaven" title="Alan Beaven">

      </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linda_Gronlund" title="Linda Gronlund">
       Linda Gronlund
      </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Cashman" title="William Cashman">
       William Cashman
      </a>
      , along with flight attendants
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sandra_Bradshaw" title="Sandra Bradshaw">
       Sandra Bradshaw
      </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cee_Cee_Ross-Lyles" title="Cee Cee Ross-Lyles">
       Cee Cee Ross-Lyles
      </a>
      , in discussing their options and voting on a course of action, ultimately deciding to storm the cockpit and take over the plane.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">

        </span>

        <span>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChristianityToday_8-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-ChristianityToday-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>

      <a href="/wiki/Lord%27s_Prayer" title="Lord's Prayer">
       Lord's Prayer
      </a>
      and the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/23rd_Psalm" title="23rd Psalm">
       23rd Psalm
      </a>
      with Jefferson, prompting others to join in. Beamer requested of Jefferson, "If I don't make it, please call my family and let them know how much I love them." After this, Jefferson heard muffled voices and Beamer clearly answering, "Are you ready? Okay. Let's roll." These were Beamer's last words to Jefferson.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmNatBiography_3-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-AmNatBiography-3">

     <p>
      According to the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/9/11_Commission_Report" title="9/11 Commission Report">
        9/11 Commission Report
       </a>
      </i>
      , after the plane's
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voice_data_recorder" title="Voice data recorder">
       voice data recorder
      </a>
      was recovered, it revealed pounding and crashing sounds against the cockpit door and shouts and screams in English. "Let's get them!" a passenger cries. A hijacker shouts, "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allah_akbar" title="Allah akbar">
       Allah akbar
      </a>
      !" ("God is great"). Jarrah repeatedly pitched the plane to knock passengers off their feet, but the passengers apparently managed to invade the cockpit, where one was heard shouting, "In the cockpit. If we don't, we'll die." At 10:02 am, a hijacker orders, "Pull it down! Pull it down!" The 9/11 Commission later reported that the plane's control wheel was turned hard to the right, causing it to roll on its back and plow into an empty field in
      <a href="/wiki/Shanksville,_Pennsylvania" title="Shanksville, Pennsylvania">
       Shanksville, Pennsylvania
      </a>
      at 580 miles an hour, killing everyone on board. The plane was twenty minutes of flying time away from its suspected target, the White House or the U.S. Capitol Building in Washington, D.C. According to Vice President Dick Cheney, President George W. Bush had given the order to shoot the plane down had it continued its path to Washington.
