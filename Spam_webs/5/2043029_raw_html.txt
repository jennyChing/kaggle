              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               When researching
               <b>
                stylish
               </b>
               places to stay,
               <a href="http://www.tablethotels.com/">
                <b>
                 Tablet Hotels
                </b>
               </a>
               is a good resource.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               The
               <b>
                boutique
               </b>
               hotelson their list meet
               <b>
                high design
               </b>
               criteria in all different price ranges.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-WaOKKdZo2ps/TiHOM2f9QUI/AAAAAAAACcU/Ux9B_zrqaig/s1600/Picture+8.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: x-small;">
                Hotel Keppler, Paris
               </span>
              </div>
              Usually, I check Tablet's prices and then the price for the same hotel on the hotel's website and another source like
              <a href="http://www.kayak.com/">
               <b>
                Kayak
               </b>
              </a>
              (which compares other good sources likeTravelocity, Hotwire, Expedia, Hotels.com, and Priceline).
              <br/>
              <br/>
              About three years ago, my stylish friend, Netta recommended joining the
              <a href="http://www.tablethotels.com/plus">
               <b>
                Tablet Plu
               </b>
               s
              </a>
              program for the amazing
              <b>
               upgrades
              </b>
              like the next higher level accommodation. The Tablet Plusprogram includes 350 hotels costs $195/year (which pays for itself if you plan to stay in their hotels a number of nights). We have been happy with the program so far.
              <br/>
