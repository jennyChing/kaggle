         <h2 itemprop="name">
          10 Tips for Living Well With Urinary Incontinence
         </h2>
         <p>
          Millions of people live with bladder health problems from an overactive bladder, neurologic disorder, or other urinary system disorder. People with
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/managing-urinary-incontinence/">
           urinary incontinence
          </a>
          often refrain from certain activities and constantly worry about the possibility of leaking urine. But with some lifestyle changes  such as balancing your fluid intake, maintaining a healthy weight, exercising, and quitting smoking  you can stay active. Here are 10 tips that can help you manage the challenges of your condition and live well with urinary incontinence.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="2" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_01_full.jpg" data-title="Balance+Your+Fluid+Intake" id="slide-img-2" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_01_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_01_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Balance Your Fluid Intake
         </h2>
         <p>
          Many people who have urinary incontinence
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/incontinence/the-truth-behind-six-incontinence-myths.aspx">
           think they should drink less water
          </a>
          to decrease the risk of an accident. But this isnt exactly true. While drinking too much fluid can lead to bladder leakage, not drinking enough can also lead to leakage and bladder health problems. When you dont drink enough water, the urine becomes more concentrated and that can be irritating to the bladder and increase urgency, says Tomas Griebling, MD, MPH, vice chair of the department of urology at the University of Kansas Medical Center in Kansas City. So, its important to keep a healthy balance of fluids.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="3" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery-urinary-incontinence-managing-urinary-incontinence-02-full.jpg" data-title="Empty+Your+Bladder+Regularly" id="slide-img-3" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery-urinary-incontinence-managing-urinary-incontinence-02-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery-urinary-incontinence-managing-urinary-incontinence-02-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Empty Your Bladder Regularly
         </h2>
         <p>
          Timed voiding, or going to the bathroom on a regular basis  say, every two to three hours  is a good idea for people who have urinary incontinence or overactive bladder. Most people wait until their bladder feels full, but that method doesnt work for people with urinary incontinence, says Dr. Griebling.
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/incontinence/out-and-about-with-incontinence.aspx">
           Emptying your bladder before leaving the house
          </a>
           or office or restaurant  even if you dont think you need to go is important for preventing accidents.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="4" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_03_full.jpg" data-title="Locate+Bathrooms+" id="slide-img-4" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_03_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_03_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Locate Bathrooms
         </h2>
         <p>
          If you have urinary incontinence, its a good idea to know where the bathrooms are located at your destination, such as at a restaurant. If youre going to a museum or shopping center, find a directory or ask a staff person where the restrooms are when you arrive. If youre heading out to an unfamiliar public area, do some research beforehand to find public restroom locations. One very useful tool, which can be found on the
          <a href="http://www.nafc.org/bladder-bowel-health/find-a-bathroom/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           National Association for Continence
          </a>
          site, lets you search for public bathrooms by zip code.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="5" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_04_full.jpg" data-title="Keep+a+Bladder+Diary+" id="slide-img-5" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_04_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">

          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_04_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Keep a Bladder Diary
         </h2>
         <p>
          Successful treatment of urinary incontinence depends on identifying the
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/womens-health/incontinence/understanding/index.aspx">
           type of incontinence
          </a>
          , says Griebling. A bladder diary can be an excellent tool to help your doctor pinpoint which type of incontinence you have. A few days before your doctor visit, begin writing down what you drank, what time you drank it, how many times you urinated, and when you had leaking-urine episodes; include anything that may have led up to the accident, such as coughing, exercising, or a sense of urgency.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="6" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_05_full.jpg" data-title="Do+Pelvic+Floor+Exercises+" id="slide-img-6" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_05_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_05_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Do Pelvic Floor Exercises
         </h2>
         <p>
          Pelvic floor exercises, or Kegel exercises, can benefit both men and women who have urinary incontinence. To do a Kegel, squeeze or tighten your pelvic floor muscles  these are the same muscles you use to stop urinating  and then release. Doing these exercises regularly will strengthen the muscles that support the bladder and therefore help prevent leaking urine and the feeling of urgency that comes with overactive bladder.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="7" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_06_full.jpg" data-title="Exercise" id="slide-img-7" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_06_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_06_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Exercise
         </h2>
         <p>
          Some people who have urinary incontinence  particularly those with
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/womens-health/incontinence/managing-incontinence.aspx">
           stress incontinence
          </a>
          , which is affected by any activity that puts pressure on the abdomen  may shun exercise for fear of leaking urine. But since exercise is so important for your overall health, people who have incontinence should work with their doctors to modify their exercise routines. Find what works for you: If running leads to incontinence, switch to low-impact exercises such as swimming or biking.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="8" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_07_full.jpg" data-title="Maintain+a+Healthy+Weight" id="slide-img-8" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_07_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_07_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Maintain a Healthy Weight
         </h2>
         <p>
          You may be able to lower your risk of leaking urine by losing weight. Obesity and incontinence are often linked, likely because more weight increases abdominal pressure, which can cause urinary incontinence. Research has found that if women who are obese lose just 10 percent of their body mass, it can reduce the risk of incontinence episodes by 50 percent.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="9" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_08_full.jpg" data-title="Limit+Caffeine+and+Alcohol" id="slide-img-9" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_08_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_08_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Limit Caffeine and Alcohol
         </h2>
         <p>
          Cutting back on caffeine and alcohol is a good idea if you have urinary incontinence, particularly
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/womens-health/incontinence/understanding/index.aspx">
           urge incontinence
          </a>
          , which is caused by an overactive bladder. Caffeine is bothersome for many people, says Griebling. Alcohol and caffeine are diuretics, and they can be irritating to the bladder. I encourage patients to limit caffeine and alcohol intake to help reduce the urgency they may feel.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="10" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_09_full.jpg" data-title="Quit+Smoking" id="slide-img-10" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_09_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_09_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Quit Smoking
         </h2>
         <p>
          People who have
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/managing-urinary-incontinence/">
           urinary incontinence
          </a>
          should do what they can to stop smoking. Studies have found that people who smoke are more prone to incontinence, says Yvonne Koch, MD, an assistant professor of urology at the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine. Smoking damages the very small blood vessels in our bodies and causes tissue weakness. Furthermore, smoking can lead to coughing, which can cause urine leakage in people who have stress incontinence. Smoking has also been linked to bladder cancer.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="11" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_10_full.jpg" data-title="Discuss+Medications" id="slide-img-11" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_10_full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/photogallery_living_well_with_urinary_incontinence_10_full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Discuss Medications
         </h2>
         <p>
          Certain medications, such as diuretics, some blood pressure medications, and antidepressants, can cause urinary incontinence, so talk to your doctor about any prescription and over-the-counter drugs you are taking. Bring as many of your medications as possible with you to the doctor, says Griebling. With more information, your
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/womens-health/incontinence/diagnosing-urinary-incontinence.aspx">
           doctor can better identify your type of urinary incontinence
          </a>
          and what factors might be affecting you, which is important for treatment.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/managing-urinary-incontinence/">
           Learn more in our Managing Urinary Incontinence Guide
          </a>
          .
         </p>
        </li>
