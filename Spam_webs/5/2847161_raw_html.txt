                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     5 Fully-Beginner CrossFit Workouts
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4108128723998925820" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26254" style="box-sizing: border-box; float: none; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; margin-bottom: 1em; max-width: 100%; width: 630px;">
                       <img alt="Beginner Friendly CrossFit Workout" class="size-full wp-image-26254" data-jpibfi-indexer="0" height="400" src="http://cdn4.dailyburn.com/life/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Beginner-CrossFit_2.jpg" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; height: auto; max-width: 100%;" title="Beginner Friendly CrossFit Workout" width="620"/>
                       <br/>
                       <div class="pinit-overlay" data-jpibfi-indexer="0" style="border-width: 0px; box-shadow: none; box-sizing: border-box; height: 400px; left: 241.5px; margin: 0px; overflow: hidden !important; padding: 0px; position: absolute; top: 480px; width: 620px; z-index: 200;" title="Beginner Friendly CrossFit Workout">
                        <br/>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       Its nearly impossible to talk about fitness trends withoutCrossFitentering the conversation. Thishigh-intensity type workout programof constantly varying functional movements has swept the nation, and doesnt appear to be going anywhere. The great thing about CrossFit is that it can work for anyone, saysNick Lobotsky, CrossFit Level 1 trainer and full-time coach at
                       <a class="external" href="http://www.crossfitnyc.com/" style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; background-position: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-size: initial; box-sizing: border-box; color: #0c90ff;" target="_blank">
                        CrossFit NYC
                       </a>
                       . We have everyone from ex-football players to ballerinas to grandmothers who come in. And for good reason. All CrossFit workouts, or WODs (Workout Of the Day), are scalable to each individuals fitness ability.
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       From bodyweight-only routines to workouts with weights, your first WOD doesnt have to be scary. But it
                       <i style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                        should
                       </i>
                       challenge  and change  you. And the workouts below will do just that  without putting you on your back. But before we get into some of these heart-pumping beginner WODS, lets get familiar with the CrossFit lingo.
                      </div>
                      <ul style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; list-style: square;">
                       <li style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #00b4c4;">
                        <span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: black;">
                         <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          Box:
                         </b>
                         A CrossFit gym
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #00b4c4;">
                        <span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: black;">
                         <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          WOD
                         </b>
                         :
                         <i style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          Workout Of the Day
                         </i>
                         , as posted on CrossFit.com or determined by your coach/box (typically only about 20 minutes). If youre wondering why many WODs have names, its because these are workouts that come up over and over again: It makes them easier to remember.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #00b4c4;">
                        <span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: black;">
                         <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          AMRAP
                         </b>
                         :
                         <i style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          As Many Reps/Rounds As Possible
                         </i>
                         , in regards to number of reps or round in a timed workout, you want to complete as many as you can
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #00b4c4;">
                        <span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: black;">
                         <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          For Time
                         </b>
                         : Your goal is to finish the prescribed workout as quickly as you can
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #00b4c4;">
                        <span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: black;">
                         <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          Score
                         </b>
                         : The total number of reps/rounds completed in a workout; If youre scoring rounds, youll tack on the additional reps you completed if you were into the next round but didnt complete it (i.e., 8R + 12 would mean 8 rounds complete and 12 reps into the 9
                         <span style="box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 12px; line-height: 0; position: relative; top: -0.5em; vertical-align: baseline;">
                          th
                         </span>
                         round when time expired)
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #00b4c4;">
                        <span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: black;">
                         <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          Rxd
                         </b>
                         : This is written after your score if you did each exercise of the workout without any modifications, meaning you completed it as prescribed (i.e., 7R +16 Rx)
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #00b4c4;">
                        <span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: black;">
                         <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          CrossFit Games
                         </b>
                         :The Superbowl of CrossFitwhere the most elite in the sport come to compete in the summer and be crowned the Worlds Fittest Man and Woman (current title holders:Rich Froning(3-time winner) andSamantha Briggs)
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #00b4c4;">
                        <span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: black;">
                         <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                          CrossFit Open
                         </b>
                         : CrossFitters can register online, then compete in a form of CrossFit Games alone or at their box
                        </span>
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       While the true CrossFit experience will take place at your local box with a team of athletes sweating right alongside you, its possible to get in on the action just about anywhere on your own. These beginner-friendly yet challenging CrossFit workouts will get your feet wet, and might just inspire you to commit to the program.
                      </div>
                      <h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                        CrossFit WOD #1: Half Cindy
                       </b>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26294" style="box-sizing: border-box; float: none; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; margin-bottom: 1em; max-width: 100%; width: 630px;">
                       <img alt="Half Cindy CrossFit WOD" class="size-full wp-image-26294" data-jpibfi-indexer="1" height="533" src="http://cdn2.dailyburn.com/life/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Half-Cindy-CrossFit-WOD.jpg" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; height: auto; max-width: 100%;" title="Half Cindy CrossFit WOD" width="620"/>
                       <br/>
                       <div class="pinit-overlay" data-jpibfi-indexer="1" style="border-width: 0px; box-shadow: none; box-sizing: border-box; height: 533px; left: 241.5px; margin: 0px; overflow: hidden !important; padding: 0px; position: absolute; top: 1791.71875px; width: 620px; z-index: 200;" title="Half Cindy CrossFit WOD">
                       </div>
                       <div class="wp-caption-text" style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #666666; font-size: 1.4rem; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.5; padding: 0px;">
                        Photo:
                        <a class="external" href="https://www.pond5.com/" style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; background-position: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-size: initial; box-sizing: border-box; color: #0c90ff;" target="_blank">
                         Pond5
                        </a>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       While the full Cindy is 20 minutes, youll be happy youre only starting with 10. Because your body isnt used to the endurance needed for many WODs, you may find yourself completely unable to rise from the ground to do a push-ups after one round. By only doing half the time, youre diminishing returns, says Lobotsky. Youll quickly learn exactly what your body is able to do and how soon you hit exhaustion. And with that comes the importance of form.
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       As you start to get tired, your form may begin to deteriorate, so dont be ashamed to use modifications (if you dont, especially at the beginning, you may be a super hero). To modify this WOD, use a resistance band, wrapped around the bar for assisted pull-ups. Push-ups can be done on your knees (even that will become difficult). Keep count of your rounds and record it so you can track your progress.
                      </div>
                      <h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                        CrossFit WOD #2: CrossFit Total
                       </b>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26293" style="box-sizing: border-box; float: none; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; margin-bottom: 1em; max-width: 100%; width: 630px;">
                       <img alt="CrossFit Total WOD" class="size-full wp-image-26293 " data-jpibfi-indexer="2" height="533" src="http://cdn1.dailyburn.com/life/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/CrossFit-Total-CrossFit-WOD.jpg" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; height: auto; max-width: 100%;" title="CrossFit Total WOD" width="620"/>
                       <br/>
                       <div class="pinit-overlay" data-jpibfi-indexer="2" style="border-width: 0px; box-shadow: none; box-sizing: border-box; height: 533px; left: 241.5px; margin: 0px; overflow: hidden !important; padding: 0px; position: absolute; top: 2720.4375px; width: 620px; z-index: 200;" title="CrossFit Total WOD">
                       </div>
                       <div class="wp-caption-text" style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #666666; font-size: 1.4rem; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.5; padding: 0px;">
                        <br/>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       Do not let these heavy lifts intimidate you; they will only make you stronger. This WOD focuses on getting newbies accustomed the heavy lifting element of the sport. The workout isnt timed; its about learning how the weight affects your body and how much you can move safely. Dont try to max out intensity, says Lobotsky. I prescribe five back squats as opposed to the usual three so you get used to feeling that weight on your shoulders if youve never done it before.
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       Form is key for this WOD. If youre not sure how to do the lift, ask a trainer or someone who is well-versed in lifting for help. Lobotsky also recommends taking a video of yourself and posting it to social media or sending it to a fellow CrossFitter for pointers and suggestions. Staying safe is the number one priority.
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       NOTE: Because youre new to these lifts, this WOD is about becoming familiar with the feel and form. Once youre comfortable, youll perform the CrossFit total as its done in the Games  three attempts to successfully lift the heaviest load on each movement. Your heaviest lifts, which are usually the third attempt on each, get combined to generate your Total. And while there is still no time limit, you must complete all three attempts for one lift before moving onto the next.
                      </div>
                      <h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                        CrossFit WOD #3: Helen
                       </b>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26295" style="box-sizing: border-box; float: none; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; margin-bottom: 1em; max-width: 100%; width: 630px;">
                       <img alt="Helen CrossFit WOD" class="size-full wp-image-26295" data-jpibfi-indexer="3" height="533" src="http://cdn4.dailyburn.com/life/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Helen-CrossFit-WOD.jpg" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; height: auto; max-width: 100%;" title="Helen CrossFit WOD" width="620"/>
                       <br/>
                       <div class="pinit-overlay" data-jpibfi-indexer="3" style="border-width: 0px; box-shadow: none; box-sizing: border-box; height: 533px; left: 241.5px; margin: 0px; overflow: hidden !important; padding: 0px; position: absolute; top: 3793.15625px; width: 620px; z-index: 200;" title="Helen CrossFit WOD">
                        <br/>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       Sure, anyone can run. But dont underestimate this one. Dont go as fast as you can on the first run because youll exhaust yourself, Lobotsky warns. Youll die after one round. Endurance is important, and while it takes time to build, doing CrossFit will teach you a lot about how much
                       <a href="http://dailyburn.com/life/fitness/best-fitness-tests-crossfit-marines/" style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; background-position: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-size: initial; box-sizing: border-box; color: #0c90ff;" target="_blank">
                        your body can handle
                       </a>
                       . To modify this WOD, tryRussian kettlebell swings(the weight only comes up to parallel with your shoulders, as opposed to American where it comes overhead) if youre unable to safely swing the weight overhead. For pull-ups, wrap a resistance band around the bar for assistance, or you can do
                       <a class="external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LdmS3EsYtmM" style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; background-position: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-size: initial; box-sizing: border-box; color: #0c90ff;" target="_blank">
                        ring rows
                       </a>
                       if your strength isnt there yet.
                      </div>
                      <h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                        CrossFit WOD #4: Wall Ball, Burpees
                       </b>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26297" style="box-sizing: border-box; float: none; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; margin-bottom: 1em; max-width: 100%; width: 630px;">
                       <img alt="Wall Ball Burpees CrossFit WOD" class="size-full wp-image-26297" data-jpibfi-indexer="4" height="533" src="http://cdn3.dailyburn.com/life/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Wall-Ball-Burpees-CrossFit-WOD.jpg" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; height: auto; max-width: 100%;" title="Wall Ball Burpees CrossFit WOD" width="620"/>
                       <br/>
                       <div class="pinit-overlay" data-jpibfi-indexer="4" style="border-width: 0px; box-shadow: none; box-sizing: border-box; height: 533px; left: 241.5px; margin: 0px; overflow: hidden !important; padding: 0px; position: absolute; top: 4625.875px; width: 620px; z-index: 200;" title="Wall Ball Burpees CrossFit WOD">
                       </div>
                       <div class="wp-caption-text" style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #666666; font-size: 1.4rem; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.5; padding: 0px;">
                        <br/>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       Why the 21, 15, 9 rep scheme? There isnt a definitive reason why, but by the time you get to 9 reps, its guaranteed to feel as hard as 21 did. Plus, its worth noting that all 21, 15, and 9 can be broken down into rounds of 3, (21 would be 3 rounds of 7 reps, 15 would be 3 rounds of 5, and 9 would be 3 of 3). This helps if you need to break up the reps and take a breather  which is allowed and encouraged!
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       Wall ball-burpees is a good combination in the worst way possible, says Lobotsky. If you do it once, it feels like the wall ball would be all leg, and the burpee all arms, but both actually work all shoulder muscles, too, for a total-body effect. A few tips to help you get started: Be sure to use your hips to throw the ball as opposed to your shoulders  they wont last. And both catch and throw the ball at the highest point your hands can reach to help minimize exhaustion. As for burpees, try not to stop as much as possible. As soon as you stop, its hard to start again, warns Lobotsky.
                      </div>
                      <h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif;">
                       <b style="box-sizing: border-box;">
                        CrossFit WOD #5: Sit-ups, Lunges
                       </b>
                      </h3>
                      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_26296" style="box-sizing: border-box; float: none; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; margin-bottom: 1em; max-width: 100%; width: 630px;">
                       <img alt="Sit-ups Lunges CrossFit WOD" class="size-full wp-image-26296" data-jpibfi-indexer="5" height="533" src="http://cdn1.dailyburn.com/life/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Sit-ups-Lunges-CrossFit-WOD.jpg" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; height: auto; max-width: 100%;" title="Sit-ups Lunges CrossFit WOD" width="620"/>
                       <br/>
                       <div class="pinit-overlay" data-jpibfi-indexer="5" style="border-width: 0px; box-shadow: none; box-sizing: border-box; height: 533px; left: 241.5px; margin: 0px; overflow: hidden !important; padding: 0px; position: absolute; top: 5578.59375px; width: 620px; z-index: 200;" title="Sit-ups Lunges CrossFit WOD">
                       </div>
                       <div class="wp-caption-text" style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #666666; font-size: 1.4rem; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.5; padding: 0px;">
                        <br/>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       This is aninterval styleWOD, demanding you to push as hard as you can for three minutes, followed by two minutes of rest. While this does help build up
                       <a href="http://dailyburn.com/life/fitness/new-cardio-routines/" style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; background-position: initial; background-repeat: initial; background-size: initial; box-sizing: border-box; color: #0c90ff;" target="_blank">
                        cardio
                       </a>
                       , we use it more for training and endurance, so you can push back to fatigue in each interval, says Lobotsky. And while you wont fully be able to recover in the two-minutes (dont be alarmed its not supposed to happen), you should come close to matching your numbers each round. If this starts to feel too easy, scale it up by adding weight to the lunge or add another two rounds to make five rounds total.
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
                       One of the best features of CrossFit is that every workout that comes up can be modified. Rep schemes, weight, and time can all be altered for beginners. You have to work your way up in this sport. Youre not going to walk in and say hey Im going to lift 500 pounds, and do it, says Lobotsky. (Thats a good way to get hurt!) CrossFitters come in all shapes and sizes, and improvements all depend on the person. So dont be discouraged and instead focus on getting better each and every time you step into the box.
                      </div>
                      <div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: pt-sans, 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', 'Lucida Sans', Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin-bottom: 1.5em;">
