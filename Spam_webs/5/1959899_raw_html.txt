            Imagine arriving in Nice (the regional capital of the French Riviera) and only having 24 hours to see the city. What would your priorities be? What would zoom to the top of your must-see list? Is it even possible to get a feeling for such a multifaceted city in so short a time?
           </p>
           <p>
            Why not give it a try? I did. And it wasnt so bad (though I did want to stay).
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <strong>
              This is how I see a perfect day going in the Cote dAzur:
             </strong>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             A good start
            </strong>
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_34666" class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_34666" style="width: 346px">
            <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/2012/10/04/twice-as-nice-in-france/nice-france-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-34666">
             <img alt="" class=" wp-image-34666 " height="223" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2012/10/travel-blog-twice-nice-beach-480x319.jpg" title="Cote d'Azur - Nice, France" width="336"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_34666">
             The beautiful Cote d'Azur. (Photograph by Redswept, Flickr)
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            Whether youre on a budget or can afford to stay at the super-duper luxurious
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.hotel-negresco-nice.com/en/" target="_blank" title="Hotel Negresco site">
              Hotel Negresco
             </a>
            </strong>
            , make it a priority to indulge in a delicious breakfast at
            <a href="http://www.lepainquotidien.com/" target="_blank" title="Le Pain Quotidien site">
             <strong>
              Le
             </strong>
             <strong>
             </strong>
             <strong>
              Pain Quotidien
             </strong>
            </a>
            . Can you think of a better way to start your day than watching the city slowly wake up while you enjoy orange juice, muesli, freshly baked bread with homemade marmalade, and a steaming-hot espresso? After all, youll need your energy for the action-packed day that lies ahead.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Get an overview
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            After your
            <em>
             petit djeuner
            </em>
            , take in the atmosphere next door at the
            <strong>
             <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/2012/09/07/food-fridays-top-10-street-markets/" target="_blank" title="Intelligent Travel - Top 10 Street Markets - Cours Saleya">
              Cours Saleya
             </a>
            </strong>
            streetmarket, taking time to savor the colorful flower arrangements and tempting food stands.
           </p>
           <p>
            Buy ingredients for a picnic and take off toward the
            <strong>
             <a href="http://en.nicetourisme.com/things-to-do/92" target="_blank" title="Nice Tourism site - Colline du Chateau">
              Colline du Chteau
             </a>
            </strong>
            (Castle Hill)
            <strong>
            </strong>
            between the old town (
            <em>
             Vieux Nice
            </em>
            ) and the harbor. Even though the namesake castle is long gone, you can still check out the Roman ruins, a beautiful waterfall, amazing greenery, and a panoramic view of the city and the Mediterranean. Dont forget your camera!
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Feel the city pulse
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            The walk up to the Colline du Chteau involves some serious exercise (if you dont take the lift), so when your legs demand a rest, roll down to the beach for a bit of relaxation before hitting the city center. Visit
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.virtualtourist.com/travel/Europe/France/Provence_Alpes_Cote_dAzur/Nice-100528/Things_To_Do-Nice-Place_Massena-BR-1.html" target="_blank" title="Virtual Tourist - Place Messena">
              Place Massna
             </a>
            </strong>
            , the main square, with its characteristic black and white tiles and futuristic human sculptures by Spanish artist
            <a href="http://jaumeplensa.com/" target="_blank" title="Jaume Plensa site">
             Jaume Plensa
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_34651" class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_34651" style="width: 259px">
            <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/2012/10/04/twice-as-nice-in-france/2012-09-08_1445761-subscriber-false-marketing-false-newsletter-false-regysnewsletter-false-microtransactions-false/" rel="attachment wp-att-34651">
             <img alt="" class=" wp-image-34651 " height="333" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2012/10/travel-blog-twice-mattisse.jpg" title="Henri Matisse House - Nice, France" width="249"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_34651">
             Henri Matisse lived here while residing in Nice. (Photograph by Risa Bender, My Shot)
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            If shoppings your thing, pop into
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.galerieslafayette.com/magasin-nice/" target="_blank" title="Galeries LaFayette site">
              Galeries Lafayette
             </a>
            </strong>
            , a famous French department store, to check out all the latest trends. There are many restaurants in the area, but I recommend the excellent
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.boccaccio-nice.com/" target="_blank" title="Boccaccio site">
              Boccaccio
             </a>
            </strong>
            for fresh seafood. Remember to save room for dessert. Their crme brle is extra large and super tasty.(Nope, it doesnt come in a smaller serving. I asked.)
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Must-see museums
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Nice boasts two museums dedicated to famous French painters 
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.musees-nationaux-alpesmaritimes.fr/chagall/" target="_blank" title="Musees Nationaux site - Chagall">
              Chagall
             </a>
            </strong>
            <strong>
            </strong>
            and
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.musee-matisse-nice.org/" target="_blank" title="Musee de Matisse site">
              Matisse
             </a>
            </strong>
            (who lived in Nice for an extended period). Both display some memorable paintings by the masters and are must-sees for art aficionados.
           </p>
           <p>
            If Nice in 24 hours isnt a big enough challenge, take a taxi or train to nearby
            <a href="http://www.beyond.fr/villages/grasse-provence-france.html" target="_blank" title="Beyond France - Grasse">
             Grasse
            </a>
            , the words perfume capital. Here the
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.fragonard.com/parfums_grasse/GB/fragonard/grasse/" target="_blank" title="Fragonard site">
              Fragonard Historic Factory
             </a>
            </strong>
            (one of the oldest parfumeries in the village) offersfree admission and guided tours. If you have read the book
            <em>
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Perfume-Story-Murderer-Patrick-Suskind/dp/0375725849" target="_blank" title="Amazon - Perfume: The Story of a Murderer by Patrick Suskind">
              Perfume: The Story of a Murderer by Patrick Sskind
             </a>
            </em>
            (or seen
            <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0396171/" target="_blank" title="IMDB - Perfume">
             the movie based on it
            </a>
            ), then you might recall that part of the story takes place in this town.
            <strong>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Unwind
            </strong>
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_34652" class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_34652" style="width: 346px">
            <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/2012/10/04/twice-as-nice-in-france/travel-blog-twice-nice-street/" rel="attachment wp-att-34652">
             <img alt="" class=" wp-image-34652 " height="225" src="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/files/2012/10/travel-blog-twice-nice-street-480x321.jpg" title="Promenade des Anglais - Nice, France" width="336"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_34652">
             Quai des tats-Unis, an extension of the famous Promenade des Anglais. (Photograph by Paphio, Flickr)
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            When this eventful day finally comes to an end, take a leisurely stroll down the boardwalk
            <a href="http://www.tripadvisor.co.uk/Attraction_Review-g187234-d195658-Reviews-Promenade_des_Anglais-Nice_French_Riviera_Cote_d_Azur_Provence.html" target="_blank" title="TripAdvisor - Promenade des Anglais">
             Promenade des Anglais
            </a>
            .
            <strong>
            </strong>
            If you are traveling with children, look for the charming
            <a href="http://freephotooftheday.com/2009/02/23/two-story-carousel-nice-france/" target="_blank" title="Free Photo of the Day - Two-Story Carousel in Nice">
             double-decker carousel
            </a>
            nearby. Its more than 100 years old and still up and running.
            <strong>
            </strong>
            As soon as the
            <strong>
            </strong>
            hunger kicks in, head back toward
            <em>
             Vieux Nice
            </em>
            to choose among the many excellent seafood eateries and settle in for a fun night out on the town.
           </p>
           <p>
            Looking back at this immensely eventful day, one can conclude that 24 hours might be enough time to see many of the important landmarks Nice has to offer. But why limit yourself? Take the time to enjoy this beautiful city  in your own way and at your own pace.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <em>
              <strong>
               Hanna Snarberg
              </strong>
              (a Swede) and her partner, Alex (a Ruskie), share their wanderlust on their travel blog,
              <a href="http://samandthedunes.com/" target="_blank" title="Sam and the Dunes site">
               Sam and the Dunes
              </a>
              (Sam is their lovable pooch).
             </em>
            </em>
           </p>
