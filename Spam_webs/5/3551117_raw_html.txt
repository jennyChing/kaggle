      <h1 class="omc-post-heading-standard">
       Comic Review  Twisted Dark Volume 3
      </h1>
      <div class="omc-review-placement-t" id="omc-review-wrapper" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/Review">
       <span itemprop="itemreviewed" style="display:none">
        Comic Review  Twisted Dark Volume 3
       </span>
       <span itemprop="reviewer" style="display:none">
        Mia Faller
       </span>
       <div class="omc-review-criteria omc-criteria-percent">
        <span class="omc-criteria-percentage" style="width:100%">
        </span>
        <span class="omc-criteria-description">
         Story- 10
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="omc-review-criteria omc-criteria-percent">
        <span class="omc-criteria-percentage" style="width:100%">
        </span>
        <span class="omc-criteria-description">
         Art- 10
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="omc-review-criteria omc-criteria-percent">
        <span class="omc-criteria-percentage" style="width:100%">
        </span>
        <span class="omc-criteria-description">
         Overall- 10
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="omc-review-summary omc-final-score-percent">
        <div id="omc-short-summary">
         <p>
          <strong>
           Summary:
          </strong>
          A breath of fresh air flows through this gripping yet unnerving edition to the Twisted Dark series.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="omc-criteria-final-score">
         <span itemprop="rating">
          <h3>
           10
           <span>
           </span>
          </h3>
         </span>
         <h4>
          Mind Blowing!
         </h4>
        </div>
        <br class="clear"/>
       </div>
       <div class="omc-user-review-criteria" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/Review-aggregate">
        <span class="omc-user-review-description">
         <b>
          <span class="your_rating" style="display:none;">
           Your Rating
          </span>
          <span class="user_rating">
           User Rating
          </span>
         </b>
         :
         <span class="score">
          0
         </span>
         <em>
          (
          <span class="count">
           0
          </span>
          votes
         </em>
        </span>
        <span class="omc-user-review-rating">
         <span class="omc-criteria-star-under">
          <span class="omc-criteria-star-top" style="width:0%">
          </span>
         </span>
        </span>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Writer: Neil Gibson
       </li>
       <li>
        Illustrators: Seb Antoniou, Jake Elphick, Leonardo Gonzalez, Caspar Wijngaard, Hugo Wijngaard, Jan Wijngaard, Atula Sirwardane
       </li>
       <li>
        Cover Art: Leonardo Gonzalez
       </li>
       <li>
        Letterer: Jim Campbell
       </li>
       <li>
        Publisher: T Publications
       </li>
       <li>
        Release Date: April 23 2012
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <i>
        Twisted Dark Vol. 3
       </i>
       brings us back into the arena of the horrific yet thought provoking stories we saw in
       <a href="http://wordofthenerdonline.com/2015/01/comic-review-twisted-dark-vol-1/" target="_blank">
        <i>
         Twisted Dark Vol. 1
        </i>
       </a>
       <i>
        .
       </i>
       This volume has characters that span all socioeconomic classes, ages, and even times in history. In the Foreword, Gibson notes that in this volume, it was important for him to write the things he loves, regardless of how twisted or dark they may be. This volumesatiates the desires of his readers to a point that proves that Gibson can balancethe two.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Twisted Dark Vol. 3
       </i>
       gives us a look at how some people evolve into monsters, whether its through body image issues in the story Perfection, bad luck in Lifeboat or simply underestimating someones power (as seen in Growth.) We are treated to both new stories and different sides to stories from earlier volumes. We see how Mike (the cop who interrogates Helen in the story Smile from
       <a href="http://wordofthenerdonline.com/2015/01/comic-review-twisted-dark-volume-2/" target="_blank">
        <i>
         Twisted Dark Vol. 2
        </i>
       </a>
       ) reacts to his job, and again, we see how glamour and wealth that is so often glorified with the lifestyles of drug lords is met with danger and life changing consequences in Growth. The continuation of earlier stories gives exciting insight into the thoughts of characters that we many not have known well, and helps give a sense of realism to the overall stories. These characters have different ideas and live lives outside of the small glimpses we are given in each story.
      </p>
      <p>
       I also found it interesting that Gibson drew inspiration from actual life events in the stories The Bid and Lifeboat. While creating stories inspired by actual events is common, I found it particularly useful in cementing these stories into my mind while giving depth to the characters.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://wordofthenerdonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/20/TwistedDark3Panels1.jpg" rel="lightbox[113670]">
        <img alt="Twisted Dark Vol 3" class=" size-full wp-image-115874 alignright" src="//wordofthenerdonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/20/TwistedDark3Panels1.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       Campbells lettering in this volume shines bright. His talents are especially showcased in the story Silent Justice, where Campbell brings to life the story of a mute man who has connections to some characters weve grown to know over the previous two volumes. Gibsons dialogue is fluid and vast, showing a real talent for capturing the voice of each character.
      </p>
      <p>
       The artwork in this volume continues to showcase a wide range of talents and styles. In Lifeboat (illustrated by Caspar Wijngaard) shows a beautiful use of contrast to give depth and weight to the brief story. This volume treats us to wonderful attention to detail in The Bid and Peace and Quiet. The range of artistic talents is especially present in the liberties taken in respect to panel layout and perspective. In Hitting Back, we see how panel layout and 3D images can make a story pop off the page. Each storys artwork meshes beautifully with Gibsons dialogue and Campbells lettering (which thankfully is more integrated in this volume than it was in
       <i>
        <a href="http://wordofthenerdonline.com/2015/01/comic-review-twisted-dark-volume-2/" target="_blank">
         Twisted Dark Vol. 2
        </a>
        .
       </i>
       <i>
        )
       </i>
       Perhaps the most effective combination of dialogue and artwork in this volume is with the story Perfection where we see the horrific evolution of a woman who just wants to fit in. While her thought process is rationalized to us, we only see her as she sees herself, until the last panel. The effect is haunting.
       <a href="http://wordofthenerdonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/20/TwistedDark3Panels2.jpg" rel="lightbox[113670]">
        <img alt="Twisted Dark Vol. 3" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-115876" src="//wordofthenerdonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/20/TwistedDark3Panels2.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       What really ties the stories of
       <i>
        Twisted Dark Vol. 3
       </i>
       together (and gives them that sinister feeling that was so saturating in
       <a href="http://wordofthenerdonline.com/2015/01/comic-review-twisted-dark-vol-1/" target="_blank">
        <i>
         Twisted Dark Vol. 1
        </i>
       </a>
       <i>
        ,)
       </i>
       is that many of the stories end with that eerie, full page panel of a twisted smile. This continuation is both intimidating and familiar, reminding us that darkness is present in the most unexpected places. While I respect and appreciate what Gibson attempted to do in
       <a href="http://wordofthenerdonline.com/2015/01/comic-review-twisted-dark-volume-2/" target="_blank">
        <i>
         Twisted Dark Vol. 2
        </i>
       </a>
       <i>
        ,
       </i>
       I was relieved that Gibson got back to his roots in this volume. I am dying to see what happens in
       <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/23923311-twisted-dark-volume-4" target="_blank">
        <i>
         Twisted Dark Vol. 4
        </i>
       </a>
       <i>
        .
       </i>
       If you are someone who preferred the lighter style in
       <a href="http://wordofthenerdonline.com/2015/01/comic-review-twisted-dark-volume-2/" target="_blank">
        <i>
         Twisted Dark Vol. 2
        </i>
       </a>
       <i>
        ,
       </i>
       Gibson recommends
       <i>
       </i>
       <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18189781-twisted-light" target="_blank">
        <i>
         Twisted Light
        </i>
       </a>
       <i>
        .
       </i>
      </p>
