          A former FSU student accused Winston of rape in December 2012, but the allegation wasnt made public until November 2013 and a code of conduct investigation was opened soon afterward.
         </p>
         <p>
          FSU stated in a letter to supporters Friday it suspended the investigation when both parties did not agree to be questioned, but it resumed in June when Winstons accuser spoke with school officials.
         </p>
         <p>
          On its face, this decision seems motivated to protect FSUs interests and respond to media pressure, not to protect Mr. Winstons interests, Cornwell wrote. If there is a better explanation, Mr. Winston is entitled to hear it.

         <p>
          The U.S. Department of Education's Office for Civil Rights began investigating FSU in April to determine whether the school violated Title IX statutes while responding to the Winston rape allegations. FSUs open letter to supporters offered a 1,234-word account of its handling of the Winston investigation.
         </p>
         <p>
          The university's letterstated on Aug. 6, after multiple requests by FSU over the previous 20 months, the accuser's attorney agreed to make her available for an interview. Based on her statement, the university reopened a confidential Title IX investigation and sought a statement from Winston.
         </p>
         <p>
          Cornwell, citing this timeline, questioned why FSU did not follow its own policy regarding sexual harassment.
         </p>
         <div class="trb_gptAd trb_articleAd_bottomBanner" data-adloader-adtype="cube" data-adloader-position="pageBottomCube" data-adloader-size="300x250" data-role="adloader adloader_ad delayload delayload_done" data-state="adloader_unloaded" data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&amp;topOffset=1000000" id="">
         </div>
         <p>
          Mr. Winston requires a full explanation from FSU explaining its rationale for ignoring the OCR and its own policies after [the accuser] repeatedly rebuffed FSUs attempts to implement those policies, Cornwell wrote.
         </p>
         <p>
          Corwell also wrote that he looked forward to a prompt response from FSU explaining why the investigation was moving forward. He also noted in the letter it did not serve as Winston's response to the four allegations against him outlined in a letter the star quarterback received Friday.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the FSU code of conduct letter, Winston was given five class days to respond and arrange a meeting to agree to the format of his conduct hearing.
         </p>
