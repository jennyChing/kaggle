        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         cake batter fudge.
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-04-04T07:15:23+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          4 April, 2012
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://girlmeetslife.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Gracie
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://girlmeetslife.com/2012/04/cake-batter-fudge/#comments">
           135 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         Looking for an easy and delicious treat to make for your family for Easter?
         <br/>
         <em>
          Of course you are.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sick of all of my cake batter recipes?
         <br/>
         <em>
          Of course you arent.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Well good, because I have a treat that everyone will love, but it takes just about 5 minutes to make and requires NO (real) cooking.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="356" src="http://girlmeetslife.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/cbf.png" style="display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border: 0px;" title="" width="475"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          (inspired by
         </em>
         <a href="http://www.cdkitchen.com/recipes/recs/35/Quick-n-easy-Vanilla-Fudge93715.shtml">
          <em>
           Quick-n-easy Vanilla Fudge
          </em>
         </a>
         <em>
          )
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          ingredients:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          2 cups yellow cake mix (I used Betty Crocker) *
         </li>
         <li>
          2 cups powdered sugar
         </li>
         <li>
          1 stick butter
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 cup milk
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 cup rainbow sprinkles
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          directions:
         </strong>
         Place cake mix and powdered sugar in a large bowl and combine. Pour in the milk, top with stick of butter, and microwave for 2 minutes. Immediately begin mixing everything together  the butter may not be fully melted but will as you continue mixing. Lastly, mix in the rainbow sprinkles, then pour fudge into a greased round cake pan. Chill for at least 2 hours before cutting. Store in the refrigerator.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          * Ive had some people say that their fudge didnt harden well. The only thing I can think that might make a difference is the brand of cake mix you use. A few commenters have also noted that Funfetti mix DOES NOT harden!
          <br/>
         </em>
        </p>

           <p>
            Just made this and it worked wonderfully!  I adjusted the recipe to use all of the cake mix.  I had 3 cups of cake mix, so I increased everything by 50%: 3 cups of powdered sugar, 1.5 sticks of butter, and 1/4 cup + 2 Tbl of milk.
           </p>
           <p>
            I used Betty Crocker Butter Yellow cake mix and Lactose-free (and fat-free) milk.
           </p>
           <p>
            Turned out perfectly!  Definitely thickens a lot as you mix it together after microwaving the butterif its runny at that stage, youve done something wrong.  It also really helps to take the extra 5 seconds to cut up the butter sticks into about 4-5 pieces each, and spread them throughout the bowl so its easier to mix.
           </p>

            Thanks!
