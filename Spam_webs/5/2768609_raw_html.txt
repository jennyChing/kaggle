         <em>
          This post on games that teach children about money contains affiliate links
         </em>

        <p>
         <img alt="5 Games That Teach Children About Money" border="0" height="764" src="http://gaggleofgamers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/games-that-teach-children-about-money.png" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 0px auto 4px; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="5 Games That Teach Children About Money" width="604"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Games can be a fun way to teach your children about lots of different subjects including money. Money can be a difficult concept to understand. Try playing some of these games with your children to help them understand.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Monopoly or Monopoly Junior
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Monopoly Junior" border="0" height="256" src="http://gaggleofgamers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/monopoly-junior-game.jpg" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 0px auto 4px; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Monopoly Junior" width="290"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Monopoly is a classic game where you buy up properties and then earn commission when another player lands on your properties. The
         <a href="http://gaggleofgamers.com/2014/11/monopoly-junior/" target="_blank">
          Monopoly Junior
         </a>
         version is great for younger kids as the original version of Monopoly is quite long and involved. By playing this game, kids can learn the very basics of the concept of owning property.
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00EDBY7X8/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00EDBY7X8&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=smbore-20&amp;linkId=QFZD6GSP36RPUMVS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Monopoly Junior
         </a>
         is good for ages 5+.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Pay Day
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Pay Day" border="0" height="319" src="http://gaggleofgamers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/payday.png" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 0px auto 4px; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Pay Day" width="387"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00083HIJK/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00083HIJK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=gagglegamers-20&amp;linkId=2RPPMAL5F53UDB6F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Pay Day
         </a>
         was my favourite game as a kid and I still love it. Pay Day is another classic where the board is like a calendar month and as you go through out the month you collect money, pay money, buy and sell items and such. Along the way you pick up bills as well. My kids learned that there are lots of different types of bills that adults are responsible for. For added money lessons, have your child help be banker which involves not only making change and handing out pay but tracking loans and savings accounts in the imaginary bank.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Game of Life
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Game of Life" border="0" height="265" src="http://gaggleofgamers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/gameoflife.png" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 0px auto 4px; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Game of Life" width="386"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Another classic and favourite of mine. In the
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00CM2IFT2/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00CM2IFT2&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=gagglegamers-20&amp;linkId=Q36P4L5XPGRJ3CPG" target="_blank">
          Game of Life
         </a>
         you get a job and live your life and make decisions along the way that can affect your money situation. Do you buy fire insurance or not? Do you buy shares from the stock exchange and then do you play the stocks to see if you can win more?
        </p>
        <h2>
         Money Wise Kids
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Money Wise Kids" border="0" height="337" src="http://gaggleofgamers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/moneywise-kids.jpg" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 0px auto 4px; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Money Wise Kids" width="504"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I havent had a chance to try this game yet but it seems to be a good choice with high ratings and good comments. Available on
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00000IZHK/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00000IZHK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=gagglegamers-20&amp;linkId=YLHO3TV4DIBGJEWJ" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Amazon.com
         </a>
         (US) and
         <a href="http://www.amazon.ca/gp/product/B00000IZHK/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=15121&amp;creative=390961&amp;creativeASIN=B00000IZHK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=callisrambl-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Amazon.ca
         </a>
         (Canada), Money Wise Kids game allows kids to roll dice and earn money and eventually, exchanging smaller bills for larger ones. The game is actually two games in one for different levels. In the first game you are just working towards $100 but in the seconds game, you start with $100 and earn more as you go but also draw bills for food, medical care and taxes and make payments.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Money Bags: A Coin Value Game
        </h2>
        <p>
