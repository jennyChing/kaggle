          <!-- Post Content -->
          <div class="post_content">
           <p>
            I'm not a short girl. My Danish heritage has afforded a rather large frame of 5'10" however I am still completely unable to reach the top cabinet in our kitchen without standing on something. We have tall ceilings, and 39" upper cabinets and those things are tall! For two years I have dragged over a kitchen chair, or straight up hopped up on the countertop to reach that top shelf.
           </p>
           <p>
            I know what you're thinking, why on earth have I not bought a kitchen step stool yet? You know those things MEANT for standing on to reach a cupboard?! Well, I just never thought about it when I was someplace that sold step stools.
           </p>
           <p>
            That was until last week, when I was stuck at the
            <a href="http://www.storefrontlife.com/we-have-a-sink/" target="_blank" title="We have a sink!">
             Ikea kichen lineup
            </a>
            , and noticed their basic
            <a href="http://www.ikea.com/ca/en/catalog/products/60178887/" target="_blank" title="Ikea Bekvam">
             Bekvam
            </a>
            step stools situated behind the counter. For a whopping $16.99 I decided then and there that I would cure my inability to reach the top shelf! I wrote down the isle and bin number and when I (finally) reached the warehouse I added the stool to my cart, and was on my way to being able to reach whatever I wanted, whenever I wanted it. (It's the small things people.)
           </p>
           <p>
            I brought home my new prize, named him Sultan* and assembled it.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5656" style="width: 490px">
            <a href="http://www.storefrontlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/photo-41.jpg">
             <img alt="Stool" class="size-full wp-image-5656" height="640" src="http://www.storefrontlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/photo-41.jpg" width="480"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Sultan the Stool
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Hrm, its okay, but its a bit boring right?
           </p>
           <p>
            So I got out the c
            <a href="http://www.storefrontlife.com/painting-kitchen-cabinet-backs/" target="_blank" title="Painting Kitchen Cabinet Backs">
             abinet paint
            </a>
            , and gave Sultan a couple coats of Thai Teal, then a couple coats of poly.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5657" style="width: 490px">
            <a href="http://www.storefrontlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/photo-36.jpg">
             <img alt="Teal Sultan" class="size-full wp-image-5657" height="640" src="http://www.storefrontlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/photo-36.jpg" width="480"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Teal Sultan
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Isn't he more handsome now?
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5658" style="width: 490px">
            <a href="http://www.storefrontlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/photo-27.jpg">
             <img alt="Stool &amp; Cabinet" class="size-full wp-image-5658" height="640" src="http://www.storefrontlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/photo-27.jpg" width="480"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Stool &amp; Cabinet
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            He matches the cabinet backs, so he fits right in. For now I have made beside the coffee bar his new home, where he can quickly be accessed to reach a cookbook or a casserole dish.
           </p>
