         <h1 class="title entry-title">
          The Unbelievable Life and Death of Michael C. Ruppert
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="small">
          By
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://disinfo.com/author/majestic/" rel="author" title="Posts by majestic">
            majestic
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="small">
          on
         </span>
         <abbr class="date time published updated" title="2014-07-23T19:00:32-0400">
          July 23, 2014
         </abbr>
         <span class="small">
          in
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://disinfo.com/category/news/" title="View all items in News">
           News
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments comments">
          <a href="http://disinfo.com/2014/07/unbelievable-life-death-michael-c-ruppert/#comments">
           <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="121320 http://disinfo.com/?p=121320">
            <i class="icon-comment">
            </i>
            22
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <script class="lockerdome-js-lite" data-box="box-off" data-follow_up="share" data-href="http://disinfo.com/2014/07/unbelievable-life-death-michael-c-ruppert/" data-owner="7565172005012737" data-size="custom-medium" data-size_specifics="24" data-style="text" data-width="fixed-width" src="//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/widget_interest_1_0.js">
        </script>
        <section class="entry">
         <p>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Michael_Ruppert.jpg#mediaviewer/File:Michael_Ruppert.jpg">
           <img alt="Michael Ruppert.jpg" class="alignright" height="240" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/3/33/Michael_Ruppert.jpg" width="240"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Michael C. Ruppert receivesthe full posthumousbiography treatmentat
          <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2014/7/22/5881501/the-unbelievable-life-and-death-of-michael-c-ruppert" target="_blank">
           The Verge
          </a>
          , where Matt Stroud writes After decades of struggle, the notorious doomsayer finally found fame and recognition. Then he shot himself.:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p class="pgh-paragraph" id="paragraph1">
           <b>
            By the second Sunday of April this year
           </b>
           , Michael C. Ruppert was broke. The 63-year-old cop-turned-writer and firebrand gained fame by starring in
           <em>
            Collapse
           </em>
           , a 2009 documentary in which he predicts societys destruction. Publicity from the film was great  he went on a countrywide promotional tour  but compensation had fizzled out. By April, he was receiving just a couple hundred dollars per month in royalties to supplement his meager Social Security checks.
          </p>
          <p class="pgh-paragraph" id="paragraph4">
           In an effort to simplify his life, Ruppert had gradually sold, tossed out, or given away nearly all of his possessions, which included an arsenal of guns, countless books, and government documents. All that remained was a collection of sentimental knickknacks, along with clothes appropriate for a man in his 60s: button-up shirts, dark-colored slacks, a few flannels, a couple of L.L. Bean jackets, and a gray cowboy hat. Everything he owned fit into his burgundy 2000 Lincoln Continental.
          </p>
          <p class="pgh-paragraph" id="paragraph7">
           For the last eight years, Ruppert had largely lived off the goodwill of friends and followers. When he made public requests for money  either through his weekly podcast, 
           <a href="http://prn.fm/category/archives/lifeboat-hour/" style="color: #fb4834;">
            The Lifeboat Hour
           </a>
           , or in posts to more than
           <a href="https://www.facebook.com/michael.c.ruppert" style="color: #fb4834;">
            5,000 Facebook followers
           </a>
            he received checks in the mail. When he needed a place to stay, people opened their homes. And its no surprise why: to subscribers of his elaborate theories  that the CIA trafficked drugs; that the Bush administration was behind the 9/11 attacks; or that the human race will face extinction by 2030  Ruppert was a soldier who fought under the banner of truth. In exchange for exposing dark secrets, he was persecuted by authorities and shadowy organizations: hed received death threats  both explicit and covert, he said  because he knew too much.
          </p>
          <p class="pgh-paragraph" id="paragraph10">
           So when he made a plea for a place to stay early this year, a follower and friend named Jack Martin in Calistoga, California, offered up a modest trailer. Thats where, on the evening of April 13th, Ruppert committed suicide with a gunshot to the head. According to Rupperts friends, his suicide at first seemed sudden and unexpected  a brash decision during a dark moment. But looking back over his life and final days, Rupperts suicide resembles a grand finale  the end of a trail hed been following for decades.
          </p>
          <p class="pgh-paragraph" id="paragraph12">
           <strong>
            An only child, Rupperts family
           </strong>
           had close ties to the military and federal government. His father was a US Air Force pilot during the Second World War and later worked for Martin Marietta  which became Lockheed-Martin  as a liaison between the CIA, the US Air Force, and Martin for booster programs, Ruppert wrote in
           <a href="http://www.collapsenet.com/free-resources/collapsenet-public-access/item/681-michael-c-ruppert-autobiography" style="color: #fb4834;">
            a 2010 online autobiography
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p class="pgh-paragraph" id="paragraph13">
           Ruppert interviewed to be a CIA operative during his senior year at UCLA in 1973, he wrote, but turned down the subsequent offer. He became a cop with the Los Angeles Police Department (LAPD) and handled narcotics cases in some of the citys most dangerous neighborhoods. The hours were long, and the job eventually got to him. He would later write that his work with the LAPD saddled him with combat fatigue.
          </p>
          <p class="pgh-paragraph" id="paragraph14">
           It was during his tenure at the LAPD that Ruppert met a woman named Nordica Theodora Teddy DOrsay. Ruppert later wrote that their 15-month relationship determined the course of my life.
          </p>
          <p class="pgh-paragraph" id="paragraph15">
           Teddy wasnt a cop, but she and Ruppert met at a Marina del Rey cop bar in 1975. The two became infatuated with one another, and quickly moved in together. Early in their relationship, however, Ruppert became suspicious of Teddy. For a civilian, she was, he thought, a little too knowledgeable about guns and stakeouts and the day-to-day work of officers on a beat. She dropped names of LAPD cops as well as crime bosses and undercover agents
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          [continues at
          <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2014/7/22/5881501/the-unbelievable-life-and-death-of-michael-c-ruppert" target="_blank">
           The Verge
          </a>
          ]
         </p>
