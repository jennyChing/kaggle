       <p>
        STATEN ISLAND  The city plans to remove more than 1,000 trees damaged by
        <a href="http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/tags/hurricane-sandy" target="_self">
         Hurricane Sandy
        </a>
        still standing in parks around Staten Island.
       </p>
       <p>
        The
        <a href="http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/tags/parks-department" target="_self">
         Parks Department
        </a>
        put out a bid last week for a company to remove trees damaged by the storm 16 months ago in several parks around the borough, a spokeswoman for the agency said.
       </p>
       <p>
        In areas where storm waters surged, workers surveyed trees and the foliage they've been producing, the spokeswoman said.
       </p>
       <p>
        Last year, 1,073 trees in several borough parks did not leaf out, the spokeswoman said.
       </p>
       <p>
        The succesful bidder will be expected to survey the trees again in the spring and remove many of them.
       </p>
       <p>
        An estimated 10,192 trees were in city flood zones during the storm, Parks said. Citywide, 2,428 trees did not leaf out last year.
       </p>
       <div class="story-nsq-prompt">
       </div>
       <p>
        The deadline for the bid is March 25.
       </p>
