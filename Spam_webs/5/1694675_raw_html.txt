    <h1>
     <i>
      Nurhachius
     </i>
    </h1>
    <p align="left">
     <b>
      Name:
     </b>
     <i>
      Nurhachius
     </i>
     (Nurhaci).
     <br/>
     <b>
      Phonetic:
     </b>
     Nur-ha-chi-us.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Named By:
     </b>
     Wang Xiaolin, Alexander Kellner, Zhou
Zhonge and Diogenes de Almeida Campos - 2005.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Classification:
     </b>
     Chordata, Reptilia, Pterosauria,
Pterodactyloidea, Istiodactylidae.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Species:
     </b>
     <i>
      N. ignaciobritoi (type).
     </i>
     <br/>
     <b>
      Type:
     </b>
     Piscivore.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Size:
     </b>
     Estimated 2.4-2.5 meter wingspan.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Known locations:
     </b>
     China, Liaoning Province -
Jiufotang Formation.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Time period:
     </b>
     Aptian of the Cretaceous.
     <br/>
     <b>
      Fossil representation:
     </b>
     Partial skull and post
cranial remains.
    </p>
    <p align="left">
     <i>
      Nurhachius
     </i>
     is perhaps most closely related to
     <a href="http://www.prehistoric-wildlife.com/species/i/istiodactylus.html">
      <i>
       Istiodactylus
      </i>
     </a>
     even though the two
are geographically separated by several thousand miles. However
     <i>
      Nurhachius
     </i>
     has a different skull which is lower with
a slightly curved
lower jaw.
     <br/>
     <i>
      Nurhachius
     </i>
     is named after the first khan of the Qing dynasty, who once ruled from
the area where
     <i>
      Nurhachius
     </i>
     was found.
    </p>
