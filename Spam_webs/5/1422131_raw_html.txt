        <h1 class="article_title">
         Trends in Time - SIHH 2010
        </h1>
        <h2 class="article_standfirst">
         Making sense of the 2010 luxury watch novelties at the Salon International de la Haute Horlogerie and looking for a direction forward
        </h2>
        <p class="byline">
         By Ashok Soman
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="wide_image">
         <img src="http://i.luxury-insider.com/uploads/features/2010/new-luxury-watches-sihh-2010/Trend-Hunter-pg1-1.jpg" width="650px"/>
         <p>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="articleTools inline">
         <!-- AddThis Button BEGIN -->

          <a class="addthis_counter addthis_pill_style">

         <script src="http://s7.addthis.com/js/300/addthis_widget.js#pubid=luxuryinsider" type="text/javascript">

         <!-- AddThis Button END -->
        </div>
        <p>
         Vacheron Constantin, in the introduction to its notes on its own new "Patrimony Grandes Complication" states emphatically that "the trend for 2010 is an exclusive and sophisticated grey". Of all the statements issued by all the houses of high horology at the Salon International de la Haute Horlogerie in January this year, this one rings most true. Platinum is certainly the noblest of all shades of grey and the metal was in ample evidence at the watch fair.
        </p>
        <div class="image">
         <img alt="" height="554" src="http://i.luxury-insider.com/uploads/features/2010/new-luxury-watches-sihh-2010/Trend-Hunter-pg1-4.jpg?width=650" width="500"/>
         <p class="caption">
          Vacheron Constantin Patrimony Perpetual Calendar Chronograph 47292
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         On the face of the watch, otherwise known as the dial, shades of grey to match platinum were also revealed. At Vacheron Constantin, this Dress Grey sentiment can best be seen in the band's use of dark grey opaline dials within the Patrimony collection. Other brands are using the new material of the moment, anthracite to turn grey and in fact have been quietly doing so for the last couple of years. It is a matter of taste of course but grey does not play well with yellow gold or even rose gold. Stainless steel, palladium, white gold and stainless steel make better playmates.
        </p>
        <p>
         On the other end of the dial divide, if it can be called that, Van Cleef &amp; Arpels showed their artisinal flair with their Extraordinary Dials series. Building on their Jardins series from 2009, Van Cleef &amp; Arpels reminds the world that watchmaking is not merely about what happens beneath the dial and between the plates but also right on the face of time. The jewelers-by-tradition use everything from Japanese lacquer and three-dimensional enamel to stained glass and enamel/ precious stone mosaic. Despite this massive burst of color from Van Cleef &amp; Arpels here, the trend from Geneva (and as we have seen from Basel also but more on that later) is moving in the other direction.
        </p>
        <div class="image">
         <img alt="" height="554" src="http://i.luxury-insider.com/uploads/features/2010/new-luxury-watches-sihh-2010/Trend-Hunter-pg1-2.jpg?width=650" width="500"/>
         <img alt="" height="554" src="http://i.luxury-insider.com/uploads/features/2010/new-luxury-watches-sihh-2010/Trend-Hunter-pg1-3.jpg?width=650" width="500"/>
         <p class="caption">
          Van Cleef &amp; Arpels Extraordinary Butterflies (above) and Midnight Japanese Lacquer Sakura
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         As no one who attended the fair can fail to notice, the forces of neoclassicism are on the ascent, with more brands than you can shake a deployant clasp at producing no-frills, no-fuss options. At the same time, technical innovation has not been left wanting, with a number of supercomplications which we will discuss towards the end of this article.
        </p>
