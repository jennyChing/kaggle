        <h2 class="entry-title">
         Is El Salvador the New Costa Rica?
        </h2>
        <div id="where">
         <b>
          Where:
         </b>
         <a href="/city/San%20Salvador">
          San Salvador
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/country/sv">
          El Salvador
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="date">
         <abbr class="updated published">
          December 29, 2014 at 5:58 PM
         </abbr>
         |
         <span class="vcard">
          by
          <a class="url fn n author" href="/user/StevenD" rel="author" title="View all posts by StevenD">
           StevenD
          </a>
         </span>
         |
         <a href="/story/2014/12/29/0485/3492#comment-block">
          Comments (0)
         </a>
        </div>
        <p class="entry-summary">
        </p>
        <p>
         <img class="top" src="http://www.jaunted.com/files/6193/ElSalvimagrs_8.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Is El Salvador the new Costa Rica?
        </p>
        <p>
         The short answer is no. Let us explain.
        </p>
        <p>
         Costa Rica is a wonderful place to be. There is no doubt about that. Gorgeous beaches and eco-adventuring are just some of the things that travelers have come to love about the country. It's been going strong for decades.
        </p>
        <p>
         El Salvador is still only just beginning.
        </p>
        <p>
         The country suffered a terrible civil war that lasted 12 years and finally ended in 1992. Since then, El Salvador has been rebuilding its infrastructure with the aim of attracting more tourism. And you know what? It's working. Driving some of the growth are tax cuts for businesses investing in tourism, which in turn help make visiting and enjoying El Salvador accessible and affordable for many first-time tourists.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="story-body">
        <p>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="450" src="https://www.google.com/maps/embed?pb=!1m18!1m12!1m3!1d3967589.996066923!2d-88.90533639999998!3d13.802993950000003!2m3!1f0!2f0!3f0!3m2!1i1024!2i768!4f13.1!3m3!1m2!1s0x8f6327a659640657%3A0x6f9a16eb98854832!2sEl+Salvador!5e0!3m2!1sen!2sus!4v1419912997843" style="border:0" width="600">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         There are about 1.85 million visitors to the country each year, 37% of which are from North America, 5% from Europe, and the rest from South America, Caribbean, and beyond.
        </p>
        <p>
         With numbers like that, it's clear that El Salvador isn't simply hanging on Costa Rica's coattails. Additionally, in El Salvador the dependence on tourism isn't as blatantly visible as in Costa Rica. During our time in country, we didn't encounter any tour buses full of khaki-clad package tourists; instead we found a vibrant spirit of adventure with solo travelers, couples, and individual families looking for that somewhere special.
        </p>
        <p>
         Occasionally the media contains news of the crime in El Salvador, but reality is a little common sense goes a long way. Do some research about the areas you're off to visit, read first-hand travel reports like that of our friend
         <a href="http://first2board.com/pointssummary/2014/10/23/10-things-learned-recent-trip-el-salvador/">
          Jamison
         </a>
         (who recently flew to San Salvador, rented a car, and drove around the country on his own), and understand that crime could happen anywhere.
        </p>
        <p>
         The people of El Salvador greeted us with some of the most genuine spirit and warmth we've had the pleasure of enjoying. They're right up there with Fiji. They want you there. Just don't call them "the new Costa Rica."
        </p>
        <p>
         <img class="top" src="http://www.jaunted.com/files/6193/ElSalvimagrs_3.jpg"/>

         <i>
          Above: Montecristo National Park's Cloud Forest
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          We traveled to El Salvador as a guest of Corsatur and El Salvadors Ministry of Tourism, but all opinions and photos are completely our own.
         </i>
        </p>
