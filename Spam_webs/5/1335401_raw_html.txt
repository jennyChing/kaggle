       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Italiexican Risotto Chopped
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/indexBlog.php/italiexican-risotto-chopped/" rel="bookmark" title="11:58 am">

        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="15464070" data-link="http://thekitchenchopper.com/indexBlog.php/italiexican-risotto-chopped/" data-summary="" data-title="Italiexican Risotto Chopped">
        </div>
        <div class="pf-content">
         <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
          <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Italiexican-Risotto-Chopped-The-Kitchen-Chopper.jpg">
           <img alt="Italiexican Risotto Chopped   The Kitchen Chopper" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-17714" height="652" src="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Italiexican-Risotto-Chopped-The-Kitchen-Chopper-1005x1024.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           <span itemprop="name">
            Italiexican Risotto Chopped
           </span>
           <br/>
          </strong>
          By
          <span itemprop="author">
           KC the Kitchen Chopper
          </span>
          ,
          <p>
           <span itemprop="description">
            Last week I made a Soup Chopped with a combo of Mexican and Italian herbs, spices and veggies for Cinco de Mayo.  As many people pointed out the flavors of Mexico are not just popular around this time of year.  I agree.  But since were in the holiday spirit I figured why not put another recipe up for Cinco de Mayo.  I have some of the same veggies left, so in keeping with The Chopped Diet, heres a way to use them up.
            <span id="more-17695">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            No wasting of food when you can Chop a Chopped.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Hubby and I love risotto, but I only make it for special occasions.
           <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Seafood-Risotto-Chopped.jpg">
            <img alt="Seafood Risotto Chopped" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-7239" height="242" src="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Seafood-Risotto-Chopped-300x242.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Valentines Day for example is most often
           <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/indexBlog.php/seafood-risotto-chopped/">
            Seafood Risotto
           </a>
           Day.  Well Cinco de Mayo is a holiday, right?
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Mexitalian-Soup-Chopped-The-Kitchen-Chopper.jpg">
            <img alt="Mexitalian Soup Chopped   The Kitchen Chopper" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-17677" height="300" src="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Mexitalian-Soup-Chopped-The-Kitchen-Chopper-160x300.jpg" width="160"/>
           </a>
           So I thought I would take some of the same ingredients I used for my
           <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/indexBlog.php/mexitalian-soup-chopped/">
            Mexitalian Soup Chopped
           </a>
           and Choppedify a Risotto.  Choppedifying means taking a highly caloric recipe and adding lots of vitamin, mineral, and fiber rich veggies while still keeping the original intent intact.  In this case a grain heavy dish like risotto gets Choppedified by using half the amount of rice and chopping in veggies and beans for protein. Its still risotto, and still yummy, but with many fewer calories.
          </p>
          <p>
           Risotto is the perfect Template recipe.  I make the base the same way every time.  Oil, Onions, Garlic, Aborrio Rice, Wine and Broth with an oz. of Parmesan thrown in at the end.  Add Broth, Stir, Add broth, Stir, repeat for 22 Minutes and its done.  Not so difficult as some would have you think!
          </p>
          <p>
           Risotto is a blank canvas that you can augment with protein and veggies. You are only hampered by your imagination.
          </p>
          <p>
           Since Risotto is an Italian creation I had to start with that in the title. But like the Mexitalian Soup theres a bit of Mexico and Italy in the additional herbs, spices, veggies and beans.
           <br/>
           You can see the similarity between the ingredients in the two recipes.  Yet the two are very different results.
          </p>
          <p>
           This recipe is naturally Gluten Free. Keep it vegetarian and use Veggie Broth.
          </p>
          <p>
           So grab your big ole Soup Pot and lets get started!
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Prep Time:
           </strong>
           <meta content="PT10M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
           10 minutes
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Cook time:
           </strong>
           <meta content="PT40M&gt;40 minutes  &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Yield: &lt;/strong&gt;&lt;span itemprop=" itemprop="cookTime" recipeyield=""/>
           4-6 servings
          </p>
          <div itemprop="nutrition" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/NutritionInformation">
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Bowl/Pot Ingredients:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tablespoon Olive Oil
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Zucchini, Chopped
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup Frozen or Fresh Corn Kernels. No need to defrost.
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup Chopped Celery Root or Celery Stalk
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            5-6 Sun Dried Tomato Halves, Chopped. Packed in Oil.
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon ground Cumin
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon dried Oregano
           </span>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            <br/>
            Risotto/Pot Ingredients:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tablespoon Olive Oil
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Small or Medium Onion, Chopped.  White or Brown is fine. Purple is OK, but it tends to muddy the color.
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cloves Garlic, finely Chopped
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            2 teaspoons ground Cumin
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            2 teaspoons dried Oregano
           </span>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Now Add:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1.5 cups Aborrio Rice
           </span>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Now Add:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1/4 cup dry White Wine
           </span>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Now Add:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            5 cups of Broth.
            <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/indexBlog.php/turkey-stock-in-the-crock/">
             Chicken
            </a>
            or Veggie. Homemade or store bought.
           </span>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Now Add:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 15 oz. can Black Beans, drained and well rinsed
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 4 oz. can diced mild Green Chilies
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 oz. grated
            <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/indexBlog.php/the-chopped-diet-a-peek-a-week-4-weights-and-measures/">
             Parmesan Cheese
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Zing Factors:
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon
            <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/indexBlog.php/?p=2576">
             Turmeric
            </a>
            .  I didnt add the Turmeric until after the photos were taken. I wanted the colors to be true. I swear, I did add it though. Turned it a bright yellow color.
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Avocado, Chopped. Chop only 1/2 the avocado if youre serving fewer than 4. In that case, Lucky you, youll have Risotto leftovers!
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Broth Prep:
           </strong>
           While youre Chopping your Ingredients, get your broth into a saucepan and heat it up.  Keep it warm till youre ready to cook the Risotto.
           <br/>
           <span itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Bowl/Pot Ingredient Prep:
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Youll want to have these ingredients ready to go into the Pot all at once. So, except for the Broth and Olive Oil, add all these ingredients to a Bowl.  Heat your Soup Pot to Medium High. Add the Olive Oil, then add the Bowl Ingredients to the Pot. Stir to combine and coat with the Oil.  Reduce heat and saut for 10 minutes till ingredients are soft.  Remove to the Bowl and reserve.
            <br/>
            <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_1170.jpg">
             <img alt="IMG_1170" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-17717" height="200" src="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_1170-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Risotto/Pot Ingredient Prep:
            </strong>
            <br/>
            In the same Pot heated to Medium High, add the Olive Oil, Onion, Garlic, Cumin and Oregano. Reduce heat to low, cook for 5 minutes until the onion is translucent.  Add the rice and cook for another 3 minutes. Stirring often. This opens up the rice so it will release its starch.  This is what makes Risotto creamy, not cream or butter. Set your timer to 22 Minutes.
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Now Add:
            </strong>
            1/4 cup heated broth at time, stirring after each addition.  When the broth is absorbed, add another 1/4 cup. Repeat for 22 minutes. Make sure the mixture is softly simmering the whole time. You should use up most of the broth in those 22 minutes. The Risotto will be luscious and creamy.
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Now Add:
            </strong>
            The sauted Bowl Ingredients, Beans, Chilies, Cheese and Turmeric, if desired.
            <br/>
            Since my broth, black beans and parmesan were salted I didnt add any additional Salt.  Taste your Risotto and add salt accordingly.  Stir to combine and heat through.  Remove from heat.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Ladle into Bowls, top with the Avocado, if desired.  Then
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             Get Your Spoons Ready!
            </strong>
            <br/>
           </em>
           <a href="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Italiexican-Risotto-Chopped-The-Kitchen-Chopper1.jpg">
            <img alt="Italiexican Risotto Chopped  The Kitchen Chopper" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-17719" height="1024" src="http://thekitchenchopper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Italiexican-Risotto-Chopped-The-Kitchen-Chopper1-593x1024.jpg" width="593"/>
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
