        <p>
         <em>
          Balsamic vinegar and minced rosemary lend subtle notes that heighten the berry essence without overshadowing it.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Ultimate Strawberry Frozen Yogurt - The Lemon Bowl" class="aligncenter wp-image-13320 size-full" height="450" src="http://cdn2.thelemonbowl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Ultimate-Strawberry-Frozen-Yogurt-The-Lemon-Bowl.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As a kid,we always had yogurt on the table orin the fridge. A typical condiment in
         <a href="http://thelemonbowl.com/category/middle-eastern" target="_blank">
          Middle Eastern cuisine
         </a>
         , we always eatplain, whole milk yogurt or labneh, not to be confused with the thick and often fruityGreek yogurt.
        </p>
        <p>
         Rich, creamy and slightly tangy  plain is still my go-to yogurt of choice. We dollop it on savory dishes like
         <a href="http://thelemonbowl.com/2010/08/baked-stuffed-eggplant-with-beef-and-pine-nuts.html" target="_blank">
          Lebanese Baked Eggplant with Pine Nuts
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://thelemonbowl.com/2011/10/slow-cooked-lebanese-green-beans-and-beef.html" target="_blank">
          Slow Cooked LebaneseGreen Beans and Beef
         </a>
         and we also enjoy it for breakfast with a sprinkling of muesli and fresh fruit.
         <span id="more-13209">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Yogurt Culture Cookbook - The Lemon Bowl" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13321" height="600" src="http://cdn3.thelemonbowl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Yogurt-Culture-Cookbook-The-Lemon-Bowl.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Given my inherent love of all things yogurt, I was thrilled when my friends at
         <a href="http://www.stonyfield.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Yogurt Culture">
          Stonyfield
         </a>
         sent mea review copy of Cheryl Sternman Rules new book,
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Yogurt-Culture-Global-Creamiest-Healthiest/dp/0544252322" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Yogurt Culture">
          Yogurt Culture
         </a>
         . Within the first few pages I stumbled upon a gorgeous photo of labneh balls soakedin oil which immediately reminded me of my Jiddo who always kept labneh in his refrigerator.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Yogurt Culture Recipes - The Lemon Bowl" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13345" height="400" src="http://cdn3.thelemonbowl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Yogurt-Culture-Recipes-The-Lemon-Bowl.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As you browse through the cookbook, its practically impossible not to get hungry. A few recipes I cant wait to try include Coriander-Lime Grilled Chicken, Shiitake Frittata with Kale, Shallots and Labneh, Yogurt-Plumped Lamb Kabobs and Orange-Clove Lassis. Are you hungry yet?
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Balsamic Rosemary Strawberry Frozen Yogurt - The Lemon Bowl" class="aligncenter wp-image-13322 size-full" height="900" src="http://cdn4.thelemonbowl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Yogurt-Culture-Strawberry-Frozen-Yogurt-The-Lemon-Bowl.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         While I do hope you all pick up a copy of
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Yogurt-Culture-Global-Creamiest-Healthiest/dp/0544252322" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Yogurt Culture">
          this gorgeous book
         </a>
         , I am happy to share this Ultimate Strawberry Frozen Yogurt recipe featured in Yogurt Culture. With a few unexpected ingredients like balsamic vinegar and fresh rosemary, you can tell just by looking at it that it will taste delicious.
        </p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 pound strawberries, preferably organic
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup plus 2 tablespoons sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups plain whole-milk yogurt (not Greek) or 1 cups plain whole-milk Greek yogurt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon light corn syrup
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon finely minced fresh rosemary, plus (optional) additional minced rosemary for garnish
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            PREP THE BERRIES. In a food processor, pulse the strawberries, sugar, and vinegar in five 1-second bursts. Let stand at room temperature (go ahead and keep it in the food processor), covered, for 1 hour.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            MEANWHILE, PREP THE YOGURT. If using traditional yogurt, spoon it into a fine-mesh sieve set over a deep bowl. Refrigerate to drain off the whey for 1 hour only. Discard the whey or reserve it for another use. Add the yogurt to the food processor with the strawberries. (If using Greek yogurt, do not strain, but do not add the yogurt to the berries until after they have stood for 1 hour.)
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            PUREE AND CHILL. Add the corn syrup to the yogurt mixture. Process until nearly smooth. Transfer to a covered container and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            FREEZE. Freeze the mixture in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturers instructions. After 20 minutes, add the rosemary. Continue churning until the mixture has fully thickened and is nearly scoopable. You may eat it now, soft-serve style, but I recommend transferring it to a metal loaf pan to further chill and develop deeper flavor. Press a sheet of parchment directly on the surface of the frozen yogurt, then cover tightly with aluminum foil. Freeze for several hours.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            SERVE. Let the yogurt stand at room temperature at least 20 minutes before scooping with a hot, dry scoop. Garnish sparingly with rosemary.
           </li>
          </ol>
