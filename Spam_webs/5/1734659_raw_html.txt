         <p>
          Lets face it: running a WordPress blog or site is no easy game. You have to take care of all the content, marketing, site design, social media, and so forth. But before you do that, you have to make sure your WordPress site is running smoothly and your current hosting provider isnt giving you any headaches or issues.
         </p>
         <p>
          Today, most of the web hosting companies out there focus on providing affordable solutions. It costs you less than $5 to get an account with a company like
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/go/hostgator" target="_blank">
           Hostgator
          </a>
          or
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/go/bluehost" target="_blank">
           BlueHost
          </a>
          . And thats a great solution if youre just getting started. But for a medium-sized or business site going for high-quality hosting is a better alternative.
          <span id="more-7775">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          By quality hosting, I mean
          <i>
           managed
          </i>
          hosting, which is specifically created for WordPress users. This new breed of managed WordPress hosting is becoming an increasingly popular option among WordPress bloggers and developers. It all started with WordPress.com VIP, but now there are numerous players in the market.
         </p>

         <p>
          Managed WordPress hosting aims at providing faster loading times, better security, and expert WordPress support. But its a bit costlier than shared hosting companies. Their servers are configured to provide better and faster WordPress performance.
         </p>
         <p>
          In this article, I am going to provide some basic information on the 8 most trusted and best WordPress Managed Hosting Services, so you can make an educated decision about whats best for you and your business.
          <br/>
          <a name="hosting1">
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          What is Managed WordPress Hosting?
         </h2>
         <p>
          Managed hosting is specifically designed and optimized for WordPress websites. In other words, it takes care of all the technical aspects of WordPress for you, allowing you to focus on creating and sharing great content. You dont have to worry about site updates, site speed, up-time, or anything like that.
         </p>
         <p>
          Many hosting companies use their own CDN and server level caching, so you dont have to rely on cache plugins such as
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/setup-w3-total-cache-plugin/" target="_blank" title="How to Setup the W3 Total Cache Plugin">
           W3 Total Cache
          </a>
          or
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/how-to-setup-wp-super-cache-maxcdn-in-wordpress/" target="_blank" title="How to Setup the WP Super Cache Plugin and MaxCDN in WordPress">
           WP Super Cache.
          </a>
          This type of hosting is perfect for those who dont have any technical skills or time to manage the server.
          <br/>
          <a name="hosting2">
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Pros of Managed Hosting for WordPress
         </h2>
         <p>
          <b>
           Security
          </b>
           The main aim of premium managed hosting is to provide bulletproof security. They have a very tight layer of security on their servers, so you dont have to worry about hacking, DoS attacks, malware, or other security issues.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Expert Support
          </b>
           It wouldnt be fair to say that shared hosting providers have poor support. In fact, most companies have excellent staff who are well-trained and possess a good knowledge of WordPress. But they cant assist you with things like performance, site functionality, WordPress errors, etc. With managed hosting support, you will get expert WordPress assistance, always.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Speed
          </b>
           Speed plays a significant role in site rankings and there a lot of things you can do improve it like implement a caching plugin, use a content delivery network such as
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/go/maxcdn" target="_blank">
           MaxCDN
          </a>
          , and reduce image sizes.
         </p>
         <p>
          Most of the managed hosting companies out there enable content caching at the server level, so you dont have to rely on any external caching plugins.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre going with a normal plan, then you might need to use a content delivery network to improve site speed.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Daily Backups
          </b>
           They take a daily backup of your site including content, data, themes, and plugins, which you can then download from the dashboard. This can save you some money, especially if you are using a
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/wordpress-backup-plugins/" target="_blank">
           paid backup
          </a>
          or maintenance service such as VaultPress or BlogVault.
         </p>
         <p>
          They also create a restore point. In case something goes wrong, you can easily restore your site to a previous working version with just one click.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Automatic Updates
          </b>
           They will automatically update your WordPress Core files to the latest version, as soon as an update is released. You dont have to pay someone extra to update your site anymoreyour managed hosting provider does it for you.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           No Downtime
          </b>
           No matter how much traffic your site gets, it will never go down. That means, if you hit the homepage of Hacker News or Reddit, you site will still be up and running without any issues.
          <br/>
          <a name="hosting3">
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Cons of Managed Hosting for WordPress
         </h2>
         <p>
          <b>
           Expensive
          </b>
           There is a huge difference in the price of managed and shared hosting. An average shared hosting plan will cost you a mere $3.95 while a managed hosting plan will set you back $30.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Limitations
          </b>
           Basically, any plugin that consumes resources isnt allowed. They also do not allow any cache plugins including W3 Total Cache and WP Super Cache. Heres the
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/go/wpengine-disallowed-plugins" target="_blank">
           full list
          </a>
          of disallowed plugins shared by most managed hosts.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           cPanel
          </b>
           If you are moving from shared hosting to Managed hosting, then you might find it difficult as they have a very different kind of user portal.
          <br/>

          WordPress.com VIP isnt your normal hosting provider. Pricing starts at $5,000 per month and is fine-tuned for high traffic websites such as Time, GigaOM, MSNBC, and TechCrunch. The hosting is perfect fit for those who are looking for an enterprise level solution. Some features include:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            SaaS
           </strong>
            Turnkey infrastructure lets you build your site in confidence.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            CDN
           </strong>
            Security is a top priority.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Hourly backups
           </strong>
            So youll never lose a bit of data again.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Flat pricing
           </strong>
            Pay a set monthly rate and get unlimited traffic, bandwidth, and storage without paying a penny more.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            24/7 support
           </strong>
            For answering your most pressing middle-of-the-night WordPress questions.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <b>
           Pros:
          </b>
          Top-quality managed WordPress hosting from the people who made WordPress itself. Includes SaaS, CDN, backups, 24/7 support all for a flat price.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Cons:
          </b>
          Designed for the enterprise set with a price tag to match. Definitely not appropriate for new developers or even those who build smaller websites.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="cubutton" href="http://vip.wordpress.com" target="_blank">
           VISIT WORDPRESS.COM VIP 
          </a>
          <br/>
          <a name="hosting5">
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Do You Really Need Managed WordPress Hosting?
         </h2>
         <p>
          It directly comes down to you and your needs. If you have WordPress blog that gets a lot of traffic, managed hosting could be an attractive option. On the other hand, if you are just starting out and only looking for basic WordPress hosting, then you might be better off going with
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/go/siteground" target="_blank" title="SiteGround Hosting">
           SiteGround
          </a>
          or
          <a href="http://www.wpkube.com/go/bluehost" target="_blank">
           BlueHost
          </a>
          (shared hosting environment).
         </p>
         <p>
          You need to think about the big picture here. What do you want to achieve with your site? Whats your top priority? If you anticipate building a lot of traffic to your site very quickly, then youll definitely need to consider a managed host, since it will likely allow for greater traffic volume and offers greater stability for WordPress sites specifically. If you just want to dabble in building a site for the first time, a managed host might be overkill.
         </p>
         <p>
          Another thing to think about is the level of support and customization you require. While shared hosting offers one-click installations for your convenience, these can preload your site with settings you might not like, especially if you have some development know-how. Using a managed host gives you the control you need to make smart decisions during the installation process, particularly with regard to security. Again, it ultimately depends on what you want to do with your site that determines how you approach this hosting decision.
          <br/>
          <a name="hosting6">
          </a>
         </p>
