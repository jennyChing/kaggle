        <h2 class="post-title">
         <a href="http://afterthekidsleave.com/2013/05/23/do-it-yourself-makeover-the-dining-room-edition/" rel="bookmark" title="Do-it-yourself makeover: The dining room edition">
          Do-it-yourself makeover: The dining room edition
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="post-date">
          <a href="http://afterthekidsleave.com/2013/05/23/do-it-yourself-makeover-the-dining-room-edition/" title="Do-it-yourself makeover: The dining room edition">
           May 23, 2013
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="date-sep">
          /
         </span>
         <span class="post-author">
          <a href="http://afterthekidsleave.com/author/karensmartershift-com/" rel="author" title="Posts by Karen Irving">
           Karen Irving
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="date-sep">
          /
         </span>
         <a href="http://afterthekidsleave.com/2013/05/23/do-it-yourself-makeover-the-dining-room-edition/#comments">
          14 Comments
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="post-content">
        <p>
         Dear Wendy,
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://afterthekidsleave.com/2013/05/21/my-beautiful-mother-in-law/" target="_blank" title="My beautifulmother-in-law">
          Back on Tuesday, I mentioned a de-cluttering project
         </a>
         Ive been doing. I didnt want to disclose details just then, because Certain People read this blog, and this project was a surprise.
        </p>
        <p>
         As in, Hey, Mitch! While you were away on a three-day business trip, Rachel and I cleared out the dining room, turfed a bunch of stuff, painted the walls and trim, rehung a bunch of pictures, and rearranged the furniture. Hope you like it!
        </p>
        <p>
         It started on Sunday morning. Rachel and I were hanging out eating breakfast and chatting. I mentioned that it bugs me that we have some beautiful art that is really going to waste because its not displayed properly. (I might have mentioned something else about drowning in our own junk. The details are all a bit hazy now.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Somehow, this conversation led to, What colour would we paint our dreary beige dining room to highlight the beautiful winter landscape painting we inherited from Mitchells mum? The painting has been hung in a dark corner of that over-cluttered room for the past 4 years or so, and its always seemed such a waste. Its mostly done in black, greys, and whites; unexpectedly, the the artist, William Allister, used deep vermilion as an accent.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9290">
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno2.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-9290">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-winter-landscape-william-allister-oil"' class="wp-image-9290" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno2.jpg?resize=600%2C447"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The lighting here makes it look yellowish. Its not.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         It didnt take long for Rachel and me to decide what the painting needed: a bold red wall that would make it really pop.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <em>
          Rachel:
         </em>
         Lets do it. Well get paint today
         <br/>
         <em>
          Me: 
         </em>
         and we can paint it tomorrow and Monday
         <br/>
         <em>
          Rachel:
         </em>
         and have it ready when Dad comes home. Itll be
         <strong>
          AWESOME
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Note: we have just committed ourselves to repainting a room red. Around a painting. In a very limited time-frame. What could go wrong?
        </p>
        <p>
         Within the hour, we were browsing the paint selection at our local paint supplier. We chose a deep, rich red for the focal wall (Rhubarb by Behr, in case you want to go look it up); a very pale pinkish tone for the other three walls; and an almost-white for the trim.
        </p>
        <p>
         All Sunday afternoon, we prepped. First, we moved all the furniture, knick-knacks, doo-dads, and other assorted stuff into the living room. Honestly, the place looked like a somewhat sketchy antique shop.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9293">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno3.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-9293">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-living-room-filled-with-junk"' class="wp-image-9293" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno3.jpg?resize=600%2C366"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Anyone want to buy some usedI mean vintage candlesticks? We have tons.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Rachel used filler to repair all the various nicks and picture-hanger holes and such, while I washed down the walls and trim so the paint would adhere properly. Then we used painters tape to protect the trim. Knowing that we are both accident-prone, and paint would be involved, we pretty much taped everything in sight.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9294">
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno8-e1369257328789.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-9294">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-painters-tape"' class="wp-image-9294 size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno8-e1369257328789.jpg?resize=480%2C496"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Tape early and tape often, that was our motto.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         I should point out that Rachel is a championship taper, who just zipped along with her roll, while I struggled with mine. All her time spent installing kitchens came in handy here!
        </p>
        <p>
         Once wed taped everything that wasnt moving, we started painting the focal wallwhere the painting would hang. After two coats, we were surprised that it was still kind of patchy and uneven in placesbut the third time really was the charm. It came up smooth as silk, and the colour is gorgeous.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9295">
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno4-e1369257637168.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-9295">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-red-wall-with-painters-tape"' class="wp-image-9295 size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno4-e1369257637168.jpg?resize=450%2C640"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Note the strategic use of brown paper, which we taped down to the floor. And a good thing, too.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Our dining room is built to 1940s dimensions: that is, its a bit cramped. Even so, it took us until Monday evening to finish the final coat on all four walls (did I mention we had to wait two hours between coats?). We collapsed into bed, knowing that Tuesday would be crunch day: we had both baseboards and crown molding to do, and those are notoriously finicky.
        </p>
        <p>
         I woke to a dream: Mitchell had come home early, and he and Rachel had decided to complete the job without me. While I was busy upstairs, they hung
         <em>
          every picture we owned
         </em>
         on our beautiful new red wall; then one of them decided to use Polyfilla to create a 3-D artistic rendition of waves across the wallI woke myself up shouting, Who did this?!!
        </p>
        <p>
         Which is when I realized: I might be a little, um, over-invested in our project. Time to ramp down the anxiety levels a notch.
        </p>
        <p>
         We attacked the trim with renewed zeal Tuesday after lunch (I had errands and the gym in the morning), and (allowing for two hours between coats, etc.) we had the last of it done by early evening.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9296">
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno7.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-9296">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-painted-wall-with-painters-tape"' class="wp-image-9296 size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno7.jpg?resize=495%2C452"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          One of the non-red wallswith trim nicely drying.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Mitchells flight was due around 11 p.m., and we still had to get the pictures hung, the excess stuff bundled up and ready for a yard sale, donation, or the trashand we had to hang the pictures. At this point, we went into hyperdrive.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was like one of those TV reality shows, where they speed up the tape to show the participants beetling around placing furniture, banging nails into the walls, rolling up old carpeting and dumping it at the curband then, suddenly, we realized we weredone. It was complete.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9297">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno5-e1369260016510.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-9297">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-red-wall-painted-dining-room"' class="wp-image-9297 size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno5-e1369260016510.jpg?resize=480%2C624"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Time for the big reveal
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9298">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno6.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-9298">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-red-wall-with-pictures"' class="wp-image-9298 size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno6.jpg?resize=543%2C480"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Now, both the painting and some of our family pictures have a home. And it looks like we actually meant to put them there.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9299">
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno9.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-9299">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-dining-room-repainted"' class="wp-image-9299" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno9.jpg?resize=600%2C405"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          This is the other side of the room, facing the kitchen (left) and family room (right).
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_9300">
         <a href="http://afterthekidsleave.com/?attachment_id=9300" rel="attachment wp-att-9300">
          <img alt='alt="IMAGE-dining-room-reno"' class="wp-image-9300" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/afterthekidsleave.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/reno11.jpg?resize=600%2C448"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Here it is by daylight.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         I was a bit worried that the red would make the room even darker than it is, but its had the opposite effect. And Rachel and I had a blast doing this togetherin fact, weve set our sights on another room already.
        </p>
        <p>
         For now, though, Im going to go lie down. I think weve earned it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Love,
        </p>
        <p>
         Karen
        </p>
        <p>
         p.s. Mitchell was definitely surprised. And he loves it. So all round, a very successful mission.
        </p>
