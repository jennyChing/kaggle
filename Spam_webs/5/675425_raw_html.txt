     <p>
      <b>
       NerdTV
      </b>
      is a technology
      <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">
       TV show
      </a>
      from
      <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">
       PBS
      </a>
      . NerdTV is aired; instead each episode is released as a
      <a href="/wiki/MPEG-4" title="MPEG-4">
       MPEG-4
      </a>
      video file, freely downloadable and licensed under a
      <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">
       Creative Commons
      </a>
      license. Transcripts and audio-only versions of the released episodes are available as well.
     </p>
     <p>
      The show features
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_X._Cringely" title="Robert X. Cringely">
       Robert X. Cringely
      </a>
      interviewing famous and influential
      <a href="/wiki/Nerd" title="Nerd">
       nerds
      </a>
      . Each episode is about one hour and features a single guest from the world of technology. From September 6, 2005 to November 29, 2005, thirteen episodes comprising Season One were released on the
      <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">
       Internet
      </a>
      . Another twelve episodes have been promised for Season Two (in late summer after an initial delay
      <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.pbs.org/cringely/pulpit/pulpit20060804.html" rel="nofollow">
       [1]
      </a>
      ), along with a more consistent release schedule and better quality video files.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="NerdTV008_.E2.80.93_Avram_Miller">
       NerdTV008  Avram Miller
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=NerdTV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: NerdTV008  Avram Miller">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      This episode is one of the first where the subject (
      <a href="/wiki/Avram_Miller" title="Avram Miller">
       Avram Miller
      </a>
      ) is not an entrepreneur, which is to say he didn't create a company that was successful, though he did facilitate many successful startup companies through his investment portfolio while at Intel. The show follows his career in chronological including:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biotech" title="Biotech">
        Biotech
       </a>
       (although the term didn't exist yet) experiences with brain-wave analysis
      </li>
      <li>
       networked computer monitoring in the hospital environment in the mid-late 1960s
      </li>
      <li>
       starting &amp; running a company in Israel at the end of the
       <a href="/wiki/War_of_Attrition" title="War of Attrition">
        War of Attrition
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       working with
       <a href="/wiki/Ken_Olsen" title="Ken Olsen">
        Ken Olsen
       </a>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">
        Digital Equipment Corporation
       </a>
       around the time of
       <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">
        IBM
       </a>
       's launch of the PC
      </li>
      <li>
       to finally joining
