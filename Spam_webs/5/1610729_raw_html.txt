              <h1 id="page-title">
               Data Centers Coming to a Mall Near You
              </h1>
             </header>
             <div id="content">
              <div class="region region-content">
               <div class="block block-system no-title odd first last block-count-5 block-region-content block-main" id="block-system-main">
                <article class="node node-article node-promoted article odd node-with-picture node-full ia-l clearfix" id="node-1905" role="article">
                 <div class="node-inner">
                  <header class="node-header">
                   <p class="submitted">
                    Submitted by
                    <a class="username" href="/users/jennifer-d-bosavage" title="View user profile.">
                     Jennifer D. Bosavage
                    </a>
                    on
                    <time datetime="2014-11-04T05:15:24-0500" pubdate="pubdate">
                     November 4, 2014 - 05:15
                    </time>
                   </p>
                  </header>
                  <div class="node-content">
                   <div class="user-picture">
                    <a href="/users/jennifer-d-bosavage" title="View user profile.">
                     <img alt="Jennifer D. Bosavage's picture" class="image-style-thumbnail" src="http://www.solutionprovidersforretail.com/sites/default/files/styles/thumbnail/public/pictures/picture_671.jpg?itok=IonG3dzb" title="Jennifer D. Bosavage's picture"/>
                    </a>
                   </div>
                   <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden view-mode-full">
                    <div class="field-items">
                     <div class="field-item even">
                      <p>
                       Yesterday's Wall Street Journal had a story about department stores that have vacated small spaces, and savvy real estate companies are renting out the space to very unlikely tenants: data centers. According to the article,
                       <a href="http://online.wsj.com/articles/malls-fill-vacant-stores-with-server-rooms-1415042980">
                        Malls Fill Vacant Stores With Server Rooms
                       </a>
                       , some of these mall spaces have been empty for a long time; therefore, these new, high-tech tenants are being warmly welcomed.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       The data centers are the nuts and bolts  some might say heart and soul  behind e-commerce, so it's ironic that they are taking the former residences of brick and mortar retailers. In a way, it adds insult to injury: E-commerce has been a fierce competitor to some retailers, ultimately forcing them to close up shop. Now, those online retailers are supporting the data centers that are moving into their former competitors' storefronts. As e-commerce continues to grow, so does the business of being a data center.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Making sure the data center is properly designed is more important than ever before. If a server goes down, hundreds of thousands of dollars can be lost. However, experts will tell you that theres probably never a perfect solution for any one data center customer. There is always a trade-off.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Rarely, if ever, does an owner or a tenant enter a new data center arrangement saying, thisis absolutely perfect for our needs. said Chad Giddings, VP of Marketing at a QTS Data Centers. So, what tradeoffs do you make? You want more space, butthe connectivity is great. The power availability lacks some redundancy, but the office space isperfect. The security offered is excellent, but the space doesnt completely fit your needs.Unless your budget is absolutely unlimited, the data center you build is not going to be 100%perfect. But theres one thing you shouldnt compromise on these days, and thats scale. Youreally need to go big.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Of course, empty department stores arent optimal for all types of data centers, because they dont provide the type of built-in security its customers need. For example, other tenants, such as restaurants that use fire, would represent a potentially hazardous condition. But for data centers serving online retailers, brick and mortar real estate seems to fit like a glove.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                      </p>
                     </div>
                    </div>
