        <p>
         MANILA, Philippines  Contemporaries of student activist Leandro Lean Alejandro are planning to build a marker on the site where he was assassinated 26 years ago today, September 19.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Lean Alejandro Foundation, Project Nameless, and the Samasa Alumni Association are working to build a reminder of his heroism at No. 5 Rosal Street, Cubao, Quezon City.
        </p>
        <p>
         Alejandro was at the passenger seat of a car that was about to enter the former Bayan office at that address when suspected gunmen from the military shot him in 1987, during the turbulent years of the late President Corazon Aquinos administration.
        </p>
        <p>
         A folk hero to many activists of the late 70s and 80s from the University of the Philippines in Diliman, Alejandro is being remembered this week with a UP Repertory presentation of Lean, based on Gary Granadas Lean the Musical, at the Aldaba Recital Hall in UP Diliman.
        </p>
        <p>
         On Facebook, many of his colleagues status updates were in his memory:
        </p>
        <p>
         Boyette San Diegos is: Remembering Lean Alejandro. Lean had a very firm, bone-crushing handshake. Each time we met, we would have this little contest to see who can outlast each other's grip. He would always come out the winner and we'd end each bout with loud chuckles...while I recovered.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lawyer Raffy Aquinos is a
         <a href="http://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=203346756510385&amp;set=a.110782985766763.15956.100005051862932&amp;type=1&amp;theater" target="_blank">
          picture of a poster of the UP Repertory presentation
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Journalist Romel Lalatas is a quotation from Alejandro: "We must bring down the dictatorship because we have to. We must end violence at all costs because we desire peace. We must bring down the tyrant so that no tyrant shall ever rise up again and tyranny shall be torn down forever. And when the people have finally seized power in their hands, they must exercise it with reason, pity and understanding. Only thus could the new power stand a better chance of not getting corrupted by their own dominance."
        </p>
        <p>
         Gone too soon but he lived a life of courage and intellectual integrity, Lalata said.
        </p>
        <p>
         Benjamin Pimentels is a
         <a href="http://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151794726446929&amp;set=a.64091246928.76507.770811928&amp;type=1&amp;theater" target="_blank">
          picture he took of the place where Alejandro was shot
         </a>
         . Pimentel, a journalist based in California, visited the site when he was in the country a couple of months ago.
        </p>
        <p>
         Last year, on the occasion of Alejandros 25th death anniversary, InterAksyon came out with a number of articles about him:
        </p>
