            <p>
             To define the recording source for any recording application we may install
             <a href="http://apt.ubuntu.com/p/pavucontrol">
              <strong>
               pavucontrol
              </strong>
             </a>
             <a href="http://apt.ubuntu.com/p/pavucontrol">
              <img alt="Install pavucontrol" src="http://hostmar.co/software-small"/>
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             As soon as we record any audio stream the name of the recording application and the source from where it records will be shown in the
             <em>
              Recording
             </em>
             tab. We then may be able to change the source to
             <code>
              Monitor of &lt;your soundcard&gt;
             </code>
             to record the output of our soundcard:
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="enter image description here" src="http://i.stack.imgur.com/xSRS7.png"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             We may have to choose another audio profile in addition. This can be done from the
             <em>
              "Configuration"
             </em>
             -tab of
             <strong>
              pavucontrol
             </strong>
             , e.g. a
             <em>
              "Duplex"
             </em>
             sound profile for output of the microphone to our local audio sink:
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" src="http://i.stack.imgur.com/rCyfB.png"/>
            </p>

          <td class="answercell">
           <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
            <p>
             The only way I was able to do this on 12.04 was to load ALSA's module
             <code>
              module-loopback
             </code>
             as recommended by Charl Botha in
             <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RSeINGM68A8" rel="nofollow">
              this
             </a>
             screen-cast.
            </p>
            <p>
             The short story ( as explained there ):
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Get PulseAudio Volume Control (pavucontrol)
             </li>
             <li>
              On Input devices tab, see that you have "Monitor of Built-in Analog Stereo" -- this is a virtual recording device with which you can record whatever's coming out of your speakers.
             </li>
             <li>
              UPDATED: Now we just need to route your microphone to your speakers too. Do this by typing "pactl load-module module-loopback latency_msec=1" at the shell prompt.
             </li>
             <li>
              If you now record with audacity, or recordmydesktop, from the "Monitor of Built-in Analogy Stereo" (see Recording tab of pavucontrol after you start recording), you'll get your microphone as well as whatever's coming out of the speakers. This means that you can now easily record Skype meetings or private Google+ Hangouts.
             </li>
             <li>
              UPDATED: On the Recording tab, you can show all streams (combobox at the bottom) and then configure which microphone (if you have more than one) should loopback into the built-in analog stereo
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             I think this module load has the same effect as the "Duplex" config recommended by @Takkat, but did not worked on my machine.
            </p>
