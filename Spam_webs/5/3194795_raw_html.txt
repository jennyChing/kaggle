                    &lt;h1 title="Preventing Gum Disease in Wylie TX"&gt;Dentists serving patients in Wylie, TX share tips for preventing gum disease&lt;/h1&gt;
          &lt;div class="para"&gt; How is your "periodontium?" That word describes your gums, the spaces between teeth and gums (sulcus), the root surfaces of teeth (cementum), alveolar bone, and connective tissues. What do these components of your mouth have in common? The condition of each contributes to your gum health. Gum disease is a serious situation that can cost your appearance, quality of life, and overall wellness. Dr. Jason and Dr. Jean Larsen share this information on preventing gum disease to help patients in the Murphy and Wylie, TX area enjoy healthier lives.&lt;br&gt;
            &lt;br&gt;
            &lt;strong&gt;Symptoms of gum disease&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br&gt;
            &lt;br&gt;
            Gum disease begins as gingivitis. Gums look puffy and discolored. They bleed easily from brushing and flossing. Your mouth tastes funny, and breath becomes persistently foul.&lt;br&gt;
            &lt;br&gt;
            Without treatment, gum disease advances to periodontitis, the leading cause of tooth loss in adults. Gums recede, so teeth look long. Deep pockets form around roots, allowing bacteria to infiltrate further. As the disease attacks ligaments and bone, teeth become loose and fall out or require extraction. Without vital stimulation from tooth roots, jawbone deteriorates, leading to a sunken facial appearance.&lt;br&gt;
            &lt;br&gt;
            That's the bad news. The good news? Gum disease is largely preventable! Heredity, certain illnesses and medications, pregnancy, and other factors may increase your risk of developing gum disease, but is also quite treatable, especially in the early stages.&lt;br&gt;
            &lt;br&gt;
            &lt;strong&gt;Preventing gum disease&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br&gt;
            &lt;br&gt;
            The hygiene team at Parkside Dental is a great ally in ensuring your gum health. But we only get to examine and clean your teeth a few times a year. A consistent good home hygiene routine is your best defense against gingivitis and periodontitis.
            &lt;ul style="margin:20px"&gt;
              &lt;li&gt;Brush after eating or at least twice daily with fluoride toothpaste and a soft brush. Be sure to brush gums and tongue gently, to remove additional oral bacteria. Finish with an antibacterial mouth rinse.&lt;br&gt;
                &lt;br&gt;
              &lt;/li&gt;
              &lt;li&gt;Replace your toothbrush every 3-4 months.&lt;br&gt;
                &lt;br&gt;
              &lt;/li&gt;
              &lt;li&gt;Clean surfaces between teeth with dental floss, before turning in for the night. Many adults don't floss correctly. Dr. Larsen will be happy to demonstrate proper technique.&lt;br&gt;
                &lt;br&gt;
              &lt;/li&gt;
              &lt;li&gt;Eat a nutritious, balanced diet. Limit between meal snacks, sugary treats, soda, and sport drinks. Even diet beverages have plenty of acids that are not mouth-friendly.&lt;br&gt;
                &lt;br&gt;
              &lt;/li&gt;
              &lt;li&gt;Please don't smoke. It is a primary risk factor for gum disease and is detrimental to the health of your whole body. Ask Dr. Larsen for cessation tips, or visit http://www.cdc.gov/tobacco/campaign/tips/quit-smoking/.&lt;br&gt;
                &lt;br&gt;
              &lt;/li&gt;
              &lt;li&gt;Emotional stress often increases destructive habits like snacking, smoking, and bruxism (grinding or clenching teeth). Utilize healthy stress reduction techniques such as yoga, massage, meditation, physical exercise, and singing (yes, singing!), and try to get adequate sleep.&lt;br&gt;
                &lt;br&gt;
              &lt;/li&gt;
              &lt;li&gt;Keep your appointments at Parkside Dental, even when your schedule gets busy.&lt;br&gt;
                &lt;br&gt;
              &lt;/li&gt;
            &lt;/ul&gt;
            Your gum health matters . . . to the beauty of your smile, to your enjoyment of eating, to your social acceptance, and to your health. It also matters to us  Drs. Jason and Jean Larsen and the Parkside Dental team. Call 972-961-4244 to become a patient. &lt;br&gt;
