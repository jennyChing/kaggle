    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      <b>
       Zappa Records
      </b>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">
       independent
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">
       record label
      </a>
      based in California that currently specializes in reissuing the recorded output of
      <a href="/wiki/Frank_Zappa" title="Frank Zappa">
       Frank Zappa
      </a>
      . The label was founded in 1977 by
      <a href="/wiki/Frank_Zappa" title="Frank Zappa">
       Frank Zappa
      </a>
      and revived in 2006 by the Zappa Family Trust.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zappa_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      After leaving
      <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" title="Warner Bros. Records">
       Warner Bros. Records
      </a>
      , Zappa founded the label Zappa Records in 1977, and formed a deal with
      <a href="/wiki/Phonogram_Records" title="Phonogram Records">
       Phonogram Records
      </a>
      , which distributed the label's releases in the United States.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IconsOfRock_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-IconsOfRock-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its first release was the
      <a href="/wiki/Live_album" title="Live album">
       live album
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sheik_Yerbouti" title="Sheik Yerbouti">
        Sheik Yerbouti
       </a>
      </i>
      , followed by the dystopian
      <a href="/wiki/Rock_opera" title="Rock opera">
       rock opera
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Joe%27s_Garage" title="Joe's Garage">
        Joe's Garage
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IconsOfRock_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-IconsOfRock-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/L._Shankar" title="L. Shankar">
       L. Shankar
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Touch_Me_There" title="Touch Me There">
        Touch Me There
       </a>
      </i>
      was released on Zappa Records in 1979.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1981, after Phonogram refused to release the single "I Don't Wanna Get Drafted", which criticized
      <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">
       Jimmy Carter
      </a>
      's reintroduction of the military draft,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IconsOfRock_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-IconsOfRock-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Zappa founded
      <a href="/wiki/Barking_Pumpkin_Records" title="Barking Pumpkin Records">
       Barking Pumpkin Records
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2006, the label was revived with the release of
      <a href="/wiki/Dweezil_Zappa" title="Dweezil Zappa">
       Dweezil Zappa
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Go_with_What_You_Know" title="Go with What You Know">
        Go with What You Know
       </a>
      </i>
      and Frank Zappa's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Imaginary_Diseases" title="Imaginary Diseases">
        Imaginary Diseases
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Trance-Fusion" title="Trance-Fusion">
        Trance-Fusion
       </a>
      </i>
      on Zappa Records, followed by
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Dub_Room_Special_(soundtrack)" title="The Dub Room Special (soundtrack)">
        The Dub Room Special
       </a>
      </i>
      (2007),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/One_Shot_Deal" title="One Shot Deal">
        One Shot Deal
       </a>
      </i>
      (2008) and
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feeding_the_Monkies_At_Ma_Maison" title="Feeding the Monkies At Ma Maison">
        Feeding the Monkies At Ma Maison
       </a>
      </i>
      (2011).
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2012, the Zappa Family Trust regained control of Frank Zappa's recorded output and made a distribution deal with
      <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Enterprises" title="Universal Music Enterprises">
       Universal Music Enterprises
      </a>
      to reissue the recordings on the Zappa and Barking Pumpkin labels.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
