                    <p>
                     While it is common for larger companies (often with a human resources department) to apply pre-employment assessments, many smaller companies have yet to utilize this cost-efficient tool for hiring. What is great about assessments, especially when made with
                     <a href="http://www.testshop.com/tg-web/">
                      online exam software
                     </a>
                     , is that they require little time or money for the immense benefit of a more adequate selection of employees. Read below to discover how human resources departments utilize pre-employment assessment to choose the best possible employees for a job.
                    </p>
                    <!--more-->
                    <p>
                     <img alt="how_human_resources_uses_assessment" src="http://www.testshop.com/hs-fs/hub/95240/file-2490042470-jpg/how_human_resources_uses_assessment.jpg?t=1436462805301"/>
                    </p>
                    <h2>
                     The Practicality of Assessment
                    </h2>
                    <p>
                     Assessments are becoming a highly-utilized hiring tool because they are flexible and can measure whatever the administrator chooses. An assessment could measure employee motives, ethics, intelligence, work traits, personality traits, and skills  which can help determine and predict which candidates may be best suited for the job.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     There are several reasons why a company may choose to implement pre-employment testing as a part of the hiring process. A high-volume company may simply use assessment as a method of quickly selecting the most effective employees for the job. If there have been high turnover rates in the past, assessment may be used to improve employee retention and generally waste less money by making the hiring process more efficient and effective.
                    </p>
                    <h2>
                     Common HR Tests and What They Measure
                    </h2>
                    <p>
                     When an administrator uses
                     <a href="http://www.testshop.com/tg-products/">
                      test generator software
                     </a>
                     , they have the ability to
                     <a href="http://www.testshop.com/">
                      create a test
                     </a>
                     of any type they desire, measuring a wide variety of employee traits. For some positions - such as sales, personality may be a very important trait for an employer to gage. For high-profile companies, employee theft may be of high concern, meaning that the pre-employment assessment may include questions pertaining to an employees honesty and morals. Here are some of the more common assessment types used by human resources:
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Personality Tests
                     </strong>
                     : These assessments are usually based on psychological theories of human traits, such as Jungs Myers-Briggs Type Indicator Assessment. A personality test may measure character traits which could determine the potential behaviour of an employee and flag any undesirable traits such as stealing, neuroticism and improper attitudes towards safety. The item type used in personality tests is often multiple choice.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <strong>
                      Cognitive Ability Test
                     </strong>
                     : These assessments will measure an employees learning capacity, comprehension and reasoning style and may be used to determine if an employee has the proper essential workplace knowledge to be hired. These types of assessment may use multiple choice or true/false items.
                    </p>
                    <h2>
                     The Applications of Assessment for Hiring
                    </h2>
                    <p>
                     Before assessment can even be applied in the hiring process, the employer must define the competencies required for the job. Thus, the assessment process will usually occur towards the end of the hiring timeline. An employee will have their competencies assessed firstly through a review of their resume, and one to three interviews.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     While an employer may have a pretty good idea of who the candidate is at this point, only an assessment can truly review more detailed aspects like personality traits. The assessment assigned to the group of employee candidates will overlap with their competencies (job skills, previous experience)  but will still remain standardized. It is crucial that human resources employees know how to review assessment results properly, in order to use these findings in the final hiring process.
                    </p>
