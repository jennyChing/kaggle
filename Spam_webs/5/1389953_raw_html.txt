       <p>
        Sometimes, miracles happen when you least expect them.
       </p>
       <p>
        After eight and a half years of trying, Alicia Holmes got word that she was not only pregnantshe was already 26 weeks along! Because she was already so far along, she had missed the threshold for safely performing genetic testing.She wasnt worried thoughshe knew that this child was a gift.
       </p>
       <p>
        When Hayden was born months later, a test right after his birth confirmed that he had Down syndrome. The diagnosis was surprising, but instead of getting scared, Alicia decided to find support. She had always been told that it takes a village to raise a childshe just needed to find a special one that would embrace Hayden, who she lovingly called her superhero. She started doing research and looked into a few different organizations.
       </p>
       <p>
        She hesitantly decided to attend a Buddy Walk with Hayden. The event, designed to promote understanding and acceptance of people with Down syndrome, seemed like a great chance to get to know others with Down syndrome, but she wasnt 100% sure that this was the right community for her son yet. Alicia was determined to find a welcoming environment for her superhero, and she had to make sure that this was the right one. This was a chance to observe and get a feel for the group.
       </p>
       <p>
        When Alicia and her mother stepped into Oklahoma Citys baseball park, she felt like her eyes had been opened to a new world. Everywhere she looked, children, teenagers and adults with Down syndrome were walking around with their families without anyone giving them a second glance. No one was judging or scoffing. Everyone was accepting.
       </p>
       <p>
        Tears welled up in her eyes. Alicia knew at that moment that she had found her sons community. This was a place for him to belong without judgment. A place where people flocked to Hayden and wanted to be around him. A place where everyone knew he was, indeed, a superhero!
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         We have a three word motto: accept, respect, and include, Alicia said. Those three words summed everything up when we found this community. These children have just as much right to be respected as anyone else does.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        But belonging to the community wasnt enough for Alicia. She wanted to give back to the community that gave her son a home. She started a fundraising teamHaydens Superheroes.
       </p>
       <p>
        For a busy mom like Alicia, everydayhero made fundraising simple, fun and easy. She gets an alert every time someone donates to her campaign, letting her see how close she is to hitting her goal while on the go. When she goes on a walk with Hayden, she can track all of the miles that she has put into her fundraising journey.
       </p>
       <p>
        Even while busily fundraising, Alicia knew there was more she could be doing to help Hayden have the best life possible. Together with her newfound friends, she has gotten involved in efforts over the last year to remove the R word from all legal documentations. She takes it upon herself to ask others not to use the word, and hopes that in the future, others will follow her lead.
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Our new R word is RESPECT because everyone deserves it, she said. These children are human beings and deserve the respect that you demand for yourself.
        </p>
       </blockquote>

       <h5>
        About Alicia and Hayden Holmes
       </h5>
       <p>
        Alicia was 40 years old when she found out she was pregnant for the first time.  Nine months later, Hayden was born.  Since Hayden's birth, he's been a blessing and is the center of her families universe.
