           <h2 class="post_title">
            Garden Play: Using the Senses
           </h2>
           <p class="post_meta">
            <span class="meta_author">
             <em>
              posted by
             </em>
             <a href="http://gardentherapy.ca/author/admin/">
              Stephanie
             </a>
            </span>

           <div class="entry">

            <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
             <a data-pin-color="gray" data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fgardentherapy.ca%2Fgarden-play-using-senses%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fgardentherapy.ca%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2014%2F09%2FGetting-kids-interested-in-the-garden-through-engaging-thier-sense-of-sight-smell-taste-hearing-and-touch.-Lots-of-ideas-on-getting-them-outside.jpg&amp;description=Garden%20Play%3A%20Using%20the%20Senses" rel="nobox">

            <p>
             I have worked with kids in the garden for quite a while now and if there is one thing that is universally appealing to all children outdoors, it is the desire to play. I dont mean an organized game of tag, but instead a lighthearted, playful approach to the garden.
            </p>
            <div class="code-block code-block-1" style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
             <center>
              <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
              </script>
              <!-- In Post Display Ad -->
              <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2008610541549994" data-ad-slot="5302597541" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
              </ins>
              <script>
               (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
              </script>
             </center>
            </div>
            <p>
             <a href="http://gardentherapy.ca/garden-play-using-senses/getting-kids-interested-in-the-garden-through-engaging-thier-sense-of-sight-smell-taste-hearing-and-touch-lots-of-ideas-on-getting-them-outside/" rel="attachment wp-att-11383">
              <img alt="Getting kids interested in the garden through engaging their sense of sight, smell, taste, hearing, and touch. Lots of ideas on getting them outside!" class="aligncenter wp-image-11383" height="700" src="http://gardentherapy.ca/wp-content/uploads/pep-vn/c474100b/Getting-kids-interested-in-the-garden-through-engaging-thier-sense-of-sight-smell-taste-hearing-and-touch-Lots-of-ideas-on-getting-them-outside-7f.jpg" width="655"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Some children are at home up to theirelbows in the soil, while others may be a bit leery of getting dirty. Some have a curiosity for the creatures that inhabit the space and others may get the willies from all the creepy crawlies. Regardless of the differences in background, attitude, or experience, the garden can be a fun place to play. Some kids just need a little help getting interested.
            </p>
            <div class="code-block code-block-2" style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
             <center>
              <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
              </script>
              <!-- Second In Post Ad -->
              <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2008610541549994" data-ad-slot="5764878345" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
              </ins>
              <script>
               (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
              </script>
             </center>
            </div>
            <p>
             Break down barriers to experiencing the garden at a young age by engaging the senses.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              SIGHT:
             </strong>
             Kids can see all the grows in the garden, but they can also use their eyes to inspect it. Have them pick flowers and pull them apart to get a closer look. Collect bugs in a mason jar and feed them some leaves. Play I Spy and see how many new things they can observe in the outdoor space.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://gardentherapy.ca/garden-play-using-senses/tips-on-activities-and-games-to-get-kids-interested-in-the-garden-using-the-senses/" rel="attachment wp-att-11385">
              <img alt="Tips on activities and games to get kids interested in the garden using the senses." class="aligncenter wp-image-11385" height="700" src="http://gardentherapy.ca/wp-content/uploads/pep-vn/39682869/Tips-on-activities-and-games-to-get-kids-interested-in-the-garden-using-the-senses-7f.jpg" width="467"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              SMELL:
             </strong>
             The aroma of the garden in intoxicating! Try smelling different flowers to see how they differ from each other, or even try smelling different colours of flowers of the same kind and talk about what you learn.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              TASTE:
             </strong>
             Picking berries, fresh from the garden to eat must be the single most powerful way to get kids interested in the garden. I plant blueberries, grapes, strawberries, raspberries, and ground cherries all for the simple pleasure of snacking out in the garden. I never grow enough to bring indoors and all the kids who visit my house know how topick a bush clean in no time flat! Herb leaves offera fun tasting project as well. Pick a few different kinds of herbs and let kids try to identify them just from taste. get ready for some funny faces when chives or rosemary gets chomped. Their palettes will grow the more they snack. You may even get them to fall in love with kale!
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://gardentherapy.ca/garden-play-using-senses/getting-kids-to-harvest-berries-instills-a-love-for-the-garden-at-an-early-age-plus-many-more-ideas-on-engaging-the-senses-to-make-garden-a-playful-space/" rel="attachment wp-att-11384">
              <img alt="Getting kids to harvest berries instills a love for the garden at an early age  - plus many more ideas on engaging the senses to make garden a playful space." class="aligncenter wp-image-11384" height="700" src="http://gardentherapy.ca/wp-content/uploads/pep-vn/f43bfd37/Getting-kids-to-harvest-berries-instills-a-love-for-the-garden-at-an-early-age-plus-many-more-ideas-on-engaging-the-senses-to-make-garden-a-playful-space-7f.jpg" width="467"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              TOUCH:
             </strong>
             Feel the soft leaves of lambs ear, the spiky casing of a chestnut, the warmth river stones set in thesun, or the squishy guts of a freshly-harvested tomato. There is no end to whatlittle hands can get into in the garden.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              SOUND:
             </strong>
             Ask kids to identify the sounds they hear in the garden. Is there running water, buzzing bees, or chirping birds? How many different things make up the musical score which plays in the background? Ask kids to imitatethe sounds and find the source.
            </p>
            <p>
             Creative expression, learning, and confidence will all develop naturally from a comfortable place to play.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://gardentherapy.ca/crafty-garden-projects-kids/" target="_blank">
              Here are a few moreprojects
             </a>
             that may help to engage kids and warm them up to getting a little dirt under their fingernails!
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://gardentherapy.ca/crafty-garden-projects-kids/" rel="attachment wp-att-11401" target="_blank">
