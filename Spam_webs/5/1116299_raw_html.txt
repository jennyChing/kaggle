// function to populate width and height according to input value

	function getCustomValue(ele)

	{

		var myImage = new Image();

		myImage.name= document.getElementById('load_img').alt;

		myImage.src = document.getElementById('load_img').src;

		

		var custom_width = document.getElementById('custom_width'); 

		var custom_height = document.getElementById('custom_height'); 

		var ratio = myImage.width/myImage.height;



		if(custom_width == ele ){

			custom_height.value = Math.round(custom_width.value/ratio);

		}else if(custom_height == ele ){

			custom_width.value = Math.round(custom_height.value*ratio);

		}



		var html = '&lt;a href="'+document.getElementById('load_img').parentNode.href+'"&gt;'+

		'&lt;img width="'+((document.getElementById('custom_width').value == '' || document.getElementById('custom_width').value == 0)? myImage.width:document.getElementById('custom_width').value )+'" height="'+((document.getElementById('custom_height').value == '' || document.getElementById('custom_height').value == 0)? myImage.height : document.getElementById('custom_height').value )+'"  class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8538" src="'+myImage.src+'" alt="'+myImage.name+'" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';

		

		document.getElementById('custom_textarea').innerHTML = html;

	}

           <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
            Ensure Simple Logo Design To Represent Your Business
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://www.designhill.com/design-blog/author/campbelljof/" rel="author" title="Posts by Campbell Joef">
             Campbell Joef
            </a>
             July 21, 2014
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <div class="simplesocialbuttons">
              <div class="simplesocialbutton ssb-button-googleplus">
               <!-- Google Plus One-->
               <div class="g-plusone" data-href="http://www.designhill.com/design-blog/ensure-simple-logo-design-represent-business/" data-size="medium">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="simplesocialbutton ssb-button-fblike">
               <!-- Facebook like-->
               <div id="fb-root">
               </div>
               <div class="fb-like" data-href="http://www.designhill.com/design-blog/ensure-simple-logo-design-represent-business/" data-layout="button_count" data-send="false" data-show-faces="false">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="simplesocialbutton ssb-button-twitter">
               <!-- Twitter-->
               <a class="twitter-share-button" data-text="Ensure Simple Logo Design To Represent Your Business" data-url="http://www.designhill.com/design-blog/ensure-simple-logo-design-represent-business/" data-via="designhilldh" href="https://twitter.com/share" rel="nofollow">
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="simplesocialbutton ssb-button-pinterest">
               <!-- Pinterest-->
               <a data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.designhill.com%2Fdesign-blog%2Fensure-simple-logo-design-represent-business%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.designhill.com%2Fdesign-blog%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2014%2F07%2Findex.jpg&amp;description=logo" rel="nofollow">
                <img border="0" data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" src="&lt;a href="/>
                <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_gray_20.png" title="Pin It"/>
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              Your business benefits from logo design in many ways. The logo helps in making your business familiar in the eyes of consumers. McDonalds golden arches logo is an example of how customers know about a business by just seeing a logo. Nikes swoosh logo is another instance of a simple design helping people recognize a business.
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000; text-align: center;">
              <a href="http://www.designhill.com/design-blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/SIMPLE.jpg">
               <img alt="logo design" class="alignnone wp-image-6636" height="312" src="http://www.designhill.com/design-blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/SIMPLE.jpg" width="418"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              <strong>
               Symbols
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              Symbols are an effective way to make your logo distinctive and recognizable. But many designers fail to make good use of symbols and instead incorporate complicated images. Best logo symbols are the ones that are simple as well. Simple symbols are easily recognizable and memorable. They are also scalable and effective.
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000; text-align: center;">
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              <a href="http://www.designhill.com/design-blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/index.jpg">
               <img alt="logo" class="wp-image-6823 aligncenter" height="232" src="http://www.designhill.com/design-blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/index.jpg" width="604"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              <strong>
               Scalability
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              Scalability of your business is equally important in creating your
              <a href="http://www.designhill.com/logo-design" title="logo-design">
               <strong>
                logo design
               </strong>
              </a>
              . It means that a good logo design is the one that keeps its original size and shape of the design when printed in different surfaces and media. So, your logo design must look equally good on website, business card, billboards, brochures, promotional pens and other gifts etc. with no distortion of shape and size.
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              <strong>
               Black and White Test
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              While colors look great on a logo but its real test is when it is turned colorless for printing on certain surfaces. Your logo will be printed in black and white in newspapers, magazines and even on billboards etc. If the logo does not look good without colors, then the design cannot be called as complete or satisfactory. Since your marketing campaign includes newspapers and all those media that are usually not known for colored advertisements, make sure that design of your logo keeps its impression without colors as well.
             </p>
             <p style="color: #000000;">
              One of the advantages of simple shapes of logos is that they retain their eye-catching properties even in black and white. So, give emphasis on creating a simple looking logo design.
             </p>
