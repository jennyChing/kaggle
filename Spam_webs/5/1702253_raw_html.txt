        <center>
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="underwareness-logo" class="aligncenter wp-image-5268 size-full" height="210" src="http://uncreativemommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/underwareness-logo.png" width="428"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://uncreativemommy.com/dropyourpants-underwareness-depend/&amp;media=http://uncreativemommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/underwareness-logo.png&amp;description=#DropYourPants for #Underwareness with Depend')">
           </span>
          </span>

        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Ok, its time to get real again here on Uncreative Mommy, time to talk about things we wish we never had to.
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Bladder leakage
         </a>
         is the topic today. If you are a mom, you probably know all about it, the pee a little every time you sneeze, laugh, blink? I have known it all too well for the last 14 years and hate every time it happens. But guess what ladies, we arent alone, over 65 million Americans experience bladder leakage too. Its not just the elderly either, 12 million of them are under the age of 35. I never knew that. I guess the stigma keeps us from talking about it. But why should we be embarrassed? Its perfectly natural and normal.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Drop Your Pants  For Underwareness    Bladder Leakage Help and Awareness by Depend" class="aligncenter wp-image-5267 size-full" height="593" src="http://uncreativemommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Drop-Your-Pants-For-Underwareness-Bladder-Leakage-Help-and-Awareness-by-Depend.jpg" width="635"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://uncreativemommy.com/dropyourpants-underwareness-depend/&amp;media=http://uncreativemommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Drop-Your-Pants-For-Underwareness-Bladder-Leakage-Help-and-Awareness-by-Depend.jpg&amp;description=#DropYourPants for #Underwareness with Depend')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Depend
         </a>
         feels the same way I do, lets talk about it.
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Depend
         </a>
         even wants us to go further than talking. They have started a new movement to encourage people to NOT be ashamed of bladder leakage. It is the
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          #DropYourPants
         </a>
         for
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          #underwareness
         </a>
         movement. Yes, you heard (read?) me right..#DropYourPants!
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Depend
         </a>
         will be donate $1, up to $3 million, over the next three years to charities that advance the research, education and awareness of bladder leakage for:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Every pant drop, photo and video shared Using
          <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           #Underwareness and #DropYourPants
          </a>
          ; and
         </li>
         <li>
          Every personal video made about what Underwareness means to you that is uploaded to YouTube and tagged #Underwareness and #DropYourPants
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Great idea right? And a very worthy cause. Now I know what you are thinking, probably the same thing as I did until a few days ago.arent
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Depend
         </a>
         DIAPERS? No they arent. I thought the same thing for years, but they are actually briefs that look, fit and feel like real underwear and feature a cloth-like fabric for a sleek, ultra-smooth fit. You cant tell that someone is wearing them unless they tell you. I will never think of
         <a href="http://ooh.li/0acf0c0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Depend
         </a>
         the same anymore, will you?
        </p>
