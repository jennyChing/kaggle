        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         How to Become a Morning Person
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-31T09:21:01+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 31, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/author/d_emig/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Danielle Emig
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-header.jpg">
          <img alt="How to Become a Morning Person" class="aligncenter wp-image-1838 size-full" height="565" src="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-header.jpg" width="1068"/>
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Check out these advantages to waking up early,and discover some simple steps to make itjust a little bit easier.
        </p>
        <p>
         In a perfect world, we would all wake up naturally with the sun, and be refreshed, awake, and ready to tackle the day. But if you arent one, becoming a morning person is a struggle, no matter what ringtone the alarm is set to. Many of us intend to wake up early to check items off the to-do list, but end up hitting the snooze button until were running late.
        </p>
        <p>
         Becoming a morning person has its advantages. If you want to enjoy a complete breakfast, read the newspaper, or just take in the early morning, follow these steps to change your daily routine.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Take Advantage of the A.M.
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Do morning people lead better lives than the rest of us? Possibly. How you start the day sets the tone for your waking hours;
         <a href="http://www.livescience.com/20880-morning-people-happier.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.livescience.com/20880-morning-people-happier.html', 'research']);">
          research
         </a>
         suggests that morning people are happier and healthier. Hit that snooze button continuously? You may procrastinate for the rest of day, just as you did with your alarm.
        </p>
        <p>
         Waking up earlier allows us to enjoy more sunlight, which is especially important in the winter when the days are shorter and the workdays end well after dark.
         <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2290997/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2290997/', 'Research shows']);">
          Research shows
         </a>
         sunlight can boost mood, and the extra sun provides an added dose of
         <a href="http://www.rodalenews.com/research-feed/increase-vitamin-D" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.rodalenews.com/research-feed/increase-vitamin-D', 'vitamin D']);">
          vitamin D
         </a>
         , which many of us lack during the cold seasons.
        </p>
        <p>
         Have trouble sticking to a workout routine? Its easier to maintain it when youre an early riser and knock it out in the morning. That way, if plans come up during the day, theres no need to skip the gym for happy hour. Plus,
         <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/fitness-pictures/10-ways-to-get-motivated-for-your-morning-workout.aspx#01" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.everydayhealth.com/fitness-pictures/10-ways-to-get-motivated-for-your-morning-workout.aspx#01', 'bumping up the heart rate in the morning']);">
          bumping up the heart rate in the morning
         </a>
         preps you for the day ahead.
        </p>
        <p>
         For those with a family, loud neighbors, or other distractions, becoming an early riser provides more time to get things done while the house is still quiet. Imagine having the time to read that novel thats been sitting on the nightstand, clean the house so its spotless after work, or prep a healthy meal. Those that wake up earlier are
         <a href="http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1559-1816.2009.00549.x/abstract" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1559-1816.2009.00549.x/abstract', 'proven to be more proactive']);">
          proven to be more proactive
         </a>
         than those who consider themselves night owls. Plus, for wishful thinkers out there, many wealthy, successful people (think Bill Gates)
         <a href="http://www.entrepreneur.com/article/230918" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.entrepreneur.com/article/230918', 'have stated']);">
          have stated
         </a>
         they wake up three or more hours before work.
        </p>
        <p>
         And while its not everything, a
         <a href="http://www.byui.edu/Documents/Admin_Offices/Advising/PowerOfPersonalAppearance.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','download','http://www.byui.edu/Documents/Admin_Offices/Advising/PowerOfPersonalAppearance.pdf']);">
          polished appearance
         </a>
         can boost self-esteem and help make the right impression at work. Getting up earlier allows time to find an outfit and put yourself together so youre not rushing out the door with wrinkled pants or fussing with your hair during your commute.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-001.jpg">
          <img alt="Early Birds vs. Night Owls" class="aligncenter wp-image-1839 size-full" height="1934" src="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-001.jpg" width="1068"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 1: Start with small changes
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Setting your alarm clock for 6 a.m. when you normally wake up at 8 a.m. may be too aggressive to maintain. Set the clock 15 minutes earlier and go to bed 15 minutes earlier each week until you meet your desired rise time. The gradual difference will allow your body to get used to the schedule change and the small difference wont feel like a byproduct of jetlag. Just make sure to stick to the routineeven on weekendsor else youll jeopardize your
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/casey-cavanagh/morning-person_b_5502768.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.huffingtonpost.com/casey-cavanagh/morning-person_b_5502768.html', 'internal']);">
          internal
         </a>
         clock and waking up during the week will continue to be a challenge.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 2: Get ready for the morning at night
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         This routine starts with what you do before you close your eyes. Staring at a computer screen right before bed will hinder your sleep, since
         <a href="http://health.usnews.com/health-news/family-health/sleep/articles/2011/03/07/using-electronics-before-bed-may-hamper-sleep" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://health.usnews.com/health-news/family-health/sleep/articles/2011/03/07/using-electronics-before-bed-may-hamper-sleep', 'light exposure']);">
          light exposure
         </a>
         suppresses
         <a href="http://sleepfoundation.org/sleep-topics/melatonin-and-sleep" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://sleepfoundation.org/sleep-topics/melatonin-and-sleep', 'melatonin']);">
          melatonin
         </a>
         , a hormone known to promote sleep. Shut off all TVs and smart devices one hour before bed, and instead reach for a novel that will calm your mind. And if you do have to use a phone, like to set the alarm, make sure to dim the brightness.
        </p>
        <p>
         Having a routine before bedtime will let your body know its time to sleep. Start a relaxing nightly pattern, like sipping on decaffeinated tea and reading a book or taking a warm bath. Make the morning process easier by laying out the next days outfit the night before. If you have trouble making time for a good meal in the morning, try prepping breakfast in advancethink hard-boiled eggs and egg muffins.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 3: Make your bedroom a sleep sanctuary
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Its tempting, but try not to watch TV in bed. Keep movies, TVs, and computers in the living room and consider the bedroom a haven for relaxation and sleep. It should be calm, dark, and cool to allow comfortable sleep. The
         <a href="http://time.com/3602415/sleep-problems-room-temperature/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://time.com/3602415/sleep-problems-room-temperature/', 'ideal temperature']);">
          ideal temperature
         </a>
         for sleep is around 65 degrees Fahrenheit. (Set the thermostat a few notches higher if you like it warmer during the day.) The cooler temps will tell your brain its time for bed and
         <a href="http://healthcare.utah.edu/healthfeed/postings/2014/07/072814_article-sleep-cooler.php" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://healthcare.utah.edu/healthfeed/postings/2014/07/072814_article-sleep-cooler.php', 'research shows']);">
          research shows
         </a>
         that could be good for your metabolism. Have clutter laying around? It could be stressing you out. A
         <a href="http://www.prevention.com/health/sleep-energy/10-reasons-you-cant-sleep/6-your-bedroom-is-mess" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.prevention.com/health/sleep-energy/10-reasons-you-cant-sleep/6-your-bedroom-is-mess', 'clean, organized room']);">
          clean, organized room
         </a>
         provides a better sleeping space than a messy one.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 4: Place your alarm clock across the room
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Placing the alarm clock across the room (and not in arms distance from the bed) means you have to get up to turn it offbut just make sure you dont climb back under the covers. The movement may help energize you to get started with the day. Leave your bedroom soon after waking. Since youve made it a sleep sanctuary, its important to walk to another area of the house as soon as possible, like the kitchen or bathroom.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 5: Wake with the sun
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Our bodies were designed to be awake with the sun and sleep when its dark outside, but the artificial light in the office and curtained-off windows in your bedroom can alter this. Exposing yourself to sunlight, which is known as
         <a href="http://health.usnews.com/health-news/articles/2012/09/17/how-and-why-to-become-a-morning-person" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://health.usnews.com/health-news/articles/2012/09/17/how-and-why-to-become-a-morning-person', 'light therapy']);">
          light therapy
         </a>
         , tells your brain its morning and time to get going. Open up your curtains to let natural light in. If sunlight is limited, invest in a sun lamp that mimics natural sunlight, which
         <a href="http://www.columbia.edu/~mt12/blt.htm" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.columbia.edu/~mt12/blt.htm', 'research']);">
          research
         </a>
         shows can also improve seasonal depression. Try this technique the next time you experience
         <a href="http://www.ninds.nih.gov/disorders/brain_basics/understanding_sleep.htm#dreaming" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ninds.nih.gov/disorders/brain_basics/understanding_sleep.htm#dreaming', 'jet lag']);">
          jet lag
         </a>
         , since itll help reset your biological clock.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 6: Work out in the morning
         </strong>
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Not only will hitting the gym early in the day help you stick to a workout routine, but a morning sweat session can
         <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/fitness-pictures/10-ways-to-get-motivated-for-your-morning-workout.aspx#01" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.everydayhealth.com/fitness-pictures/10-ways-to-get-motivated-for-your-morning-workout.aspx#01', 'increase metabolism and energy']);">
          increase metabolism and energy
         </a>
         throughout the day, and you may be prompted to reach for healthier food options.
         <a href="http://well.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/12/15/phys-ed-the-benefits-of-exercising-before-breakfast/?_r=0" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://well.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/12/15/phys-ed-the-benefits-of-exercising-before-breakfast/?_r=0', 'Research']);">
          Research
         </a>
         also shows that exercising before breakfast burns more fat than doing so after breakfast or in the evening. But if youre a serious athlete, eat a light meal with carbs to improve performance. Cant spare an hour at the gym? Take a 10-minute
         <a href="http://www.nerdfitness.com/blog/2014/08/14/how-to-become-a-morning-person/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.nerdfitness.com/blog/2014/08/14/how-to-become-a-morning-person/', 'brisk walk']);">
          brisk walk
         </a>
         outside to get some fresh air and sunlight.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 7: Drink a glass of water
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         After getting a full nights rest (hopefully seven to eight hours) your body will be dehydrated from lack of
         <a href="http://www.entrepreneur.com/article/238219" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.entrepreneur.com/article/238219', 'water']);">
          water
         </a>
         , causing fatigue. Quickly replenish hydration levels first thing in the morning with a full glass of water. While youre at it, splash some cool water on your face for an added refresher.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 8: Start with a healthy breakfast
         </strong>
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Rushing out the door with a coffee and bagel in hand may not fill you up and can leave you with cravings later on. A persons
         <a href="http://www.realsimple.com/work-life/life-strategies/how-to-become-morning-person/protein" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.realsimple.com/work-life/life-strategies/how-to-become-morning-person/protein', 'metabolism and blood sugar']);">
          metabolism and blood sugar
         </a>
         are at their lowest in the morning after sleeping all night, so reenergize with a balanced breakfast that includes protein and whole grains. Rotate meals, find new ones, and eat foods you enjoy so breakfast becomes a moment to look forward to. Greek yogurt with berries or rolled oats with a side of coffee and splash of milk will help rev you up for the day ahead.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 9: Have something to look forward to
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Remember when you were a little kid and you jumped out of bed on Christmas morning or when you left for vacation? Plan at least one exciting date, happy hour with friends, or a fun night in each week so youll have something to look forward to each passing day. Getting up can be a struggle if the only agenda for the day is going to a stressful or boring job when youd rather stay in bed. Whether its a hobby like yoga, a cooking class, or a date, focus on what excites you rather than things that make you dread getting up.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Step 10: Avoid grogginess
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         If, even after eight hours of sleep, you wake up feeling groggy, it could be because youre interrupting your sleep cycle.
         <a href="http://www.webmd.com/sleep-disorders/guide/sleep-101" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.webmd.com/sleep-disorders/guide/sleep-101', 'REM']);">
          REM
         </a>
         , or rapid eye movement, stimulates the brain to learn. Its a deep stage of sleep where dreams typically happen, and if abruptly woken up during this stage, you will feel disoriented and groggy.
         <a href="http://www.newyorker.com/tech/elements/snoozers-are-in-fact-losers" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.newyorker.com/tech/elements/snoozers-are-in-fact-losers', 'Sleep inertia,']);">
          Sleep inertia,
         </a>
         a physiological state caused by waking up suddenly and too early, can affect people from two to four hours.
         <a href="http://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0079688" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0079688', 'Research shows']);">
          Research shows
         </a>
         that even if you feel awake, sleep inertia can affect cognitive skills, challenging productivity levels at work. Try a smartphone app for help: Using a smartphone set under your pillow, the app tracks sleep cycles and wakes you when youre in your lightest sleep phase.
         <a href="http://www.fitnessmagazine.com/health/sleep/become-a-morning-person/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.fitnessmagazine.com/health/sleep/become-a-morning-person/', 'Hitting the snooze button']);">
          Hitting the snooze button
         </a>
         will also disrupt this cycle. It takes about one hour to reach a deep sleep, and the fragmented dozing can actually leave you more tired.
        </p>
        <p>
         Even though
         <a href="http://health.usnews.com/health-news/family-health/sleep/articles/2011/08/15/more-evidence-that-alcohol-hinders-good-sleep" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://health.usnews.com/health-news/family-health/sleep/articles/2011/08/15/more-evidence-that-alcohol-hinders-good-sleep', 'alcohol']);">
          alcohol
         </a>
         may make us pass out at the end of the night, it inhibits the deep sleep needed to wake up feeling refreshed. Avoid booze before bed, and if you do enjoy a few cocktails during happy hour, make sure to drink plenty of water before sleeping to counter the effects.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-002.jpg">
          <img alt="Skip the Snooze" class="aligncenter wp-image-1840 size-full" height="2357" src="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-002.jpg" width="1068"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-003.jpg">
          <img alt="10 Things Successful People Do Before 8AM" class="aligncenter wp-image-1842 size-full" height="3224" src="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-003.jpg" width="1068"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Conclusion
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Waking up isnt easy, so reward yourself for doing so. Make it a point to use your morning to your advantage and get a jumpstart on a productive day.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.northwestpharmacy.com/healthperch/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/morning-person-embed.jpg">

         </a>
        </p>
