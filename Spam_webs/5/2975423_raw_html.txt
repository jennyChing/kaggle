         <div class="entry">
          <p>
           Just finished a massage. Not the last one of the day, or the week, or even the year, but of my career as a therapist.
          </p>
          <div class="widget ad_position widget-ad-position-A300 widget-fake">
           <div class="hdn-ad-position ad-A300 hdn-ap-visible-mobile">
            <div class="juice-ad-position juice-ad-position-A300">
             <script id="A300-0-65602700-1436916255" type="text/javascript">
              window.ad_position_widget.place_named_ad('A300', 'A300-0-65602700-1436916255');
             </script>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           The table is put away, and I had a long, friendly chat with my linen delivery guy announcing my retirement and arranging for the final pick-up.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its been quite a ride over 20 odd years of practice. Clients have come and gone. Many have come and stayed with me almost for the duration. Ive shared not only their aches and pains, bad backs, and wonky shoulders  but Ive watched and listened as their lives spun on. There were the celebrations, birthdays, and vacations . Then, there were the job losses, illnesses, and set-backs. For one-hour I was allowed to be a privileged visitor to their days.
          </p>
          <p>
           Deciding to pack it in wasnt easy, but it was time. Im grateful for it all, and ready to change and move on.
           <a href="http://blog.timesunion.com/holistichealth/files/2014/12/owning-it-coastline.jpg">
            <img alt="owning it coastline" class="alignright size-full wp-image-14577" height="127" src="http://blog.timesunion.com/holistichealth/files/2014/12/owning-it-coastline.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           How decisions get made  mine, and everyone elses  has always fascinated me. True there are commonalities in the process, but theres plenty of variety as well. Ive thought about it  a lot  and written about it too here in the blog over the last 8 years.
          </p>
          <p>
           Since were racing toward the end of one year, and getting ready to start a new one, lots of us are taking stock and at least contemplating the prospect of some kind of change. Maybe we seek better health through diet or exercise. There could be a new job on the horizon. Or perhaps its a relationship that needs to be strengthened  or possibly to end.
          </p>
          <p>
           Over my lifetime theres been lots of choices, lots of decisions, lots of pros and plenty of cons to consider. True, not all have worked out the way I would have liked, but I chose with intention, I owned my actions, and thats made the outcome ultimately comfortable.
           <a href="http://blog.timesunion.com/holistichealth/files/2014/12/owning-it-fork-in-road.jpg">
            <img alt="owning it fork in road" class="size-full wp-image-14579 alignleft" height="183" src="http://blog.timesunion.com/holistichealth/files/2014/12/owning-it-fork-in-road.jpg" width="275"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Along the way Ive picked up a tool or two to help me navigate my way through the complicated and often unsettling waters of change. Perhaps some will be useful to you as well.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             No choice is 100%.
            </strong>
            Saying yes to one option usually means saying no to something else. Waiting for the perfect solution may mean permanent indecision  which is a decision in itself. But having said that..
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Sometimes not making a decision is the right thing to do.
            </strong>
            Maybe more time will lead to more information. Maybe in time some things just work themselves out in a better way than we could even dream. Timing is important.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Know what you value
            </strong>
            . Other peoples values are not yours. Their choices will probably be different. That doesnt mean you are wrong  its just that you are not them. Learning what we truly care about is a life long process. It can and should affect everything we do and every decision we make.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Save your energy for the big stuff.
            </strong>
            It always helps to ask how important will this be in a week, in a year, in 10 years. Obviously the implications of buying the wrong car might be big, but certainly not in the same league as choosing the wrong life partner.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Most choices that dont work out arent fatal
            </strong>
            . The wrong decision may cost us time, money, embarrassment or tears  but seldom our life. Regroup and carry on!
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Trust your gut!
            </strong>
            Oh I know wed so like to believe that we are creatures of reason and logic, and to some extent we are. But it often happens that after listing all the pluses and minuses our innards are still giving us a very different message. Listen to, and give equal weight to that inner voice.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           So there you have it, and hopefully youll find it helpful. If youve wrestled with a decision (and who hasnt!) and would like to share a technique thats worked for you, please feel free to post a comment. Would love to hear from you!
          </p>
          <p>
           Wishing you all a peace-filled holiday, and a New Year full of possibilities.
          </p>
          <p>
           Judi England, RN, LMT (retired), Kripalu Yoga Teacher  yogajudi@aol.com  12/22/2014
          </p>
