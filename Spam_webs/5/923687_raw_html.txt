           <h2 class="articlePageTitle">
            How do banks make money?
           </h2>
           <p>
             Banks are just like other businesses. Their product just happens to be
            <a href="http://money.howstuffworks.com/">
             money
            </a>
            . Other businesses sell widgets or services; banks sell money -- in the form of loans,
            <a href="http://money.howstuffworks.com/question725.htm">
             certificates of deposit (CDs)
            </a>
            and other financial products. They make money on the interest they charge on loans because that interest is higher than the interest they pay on depositors' accounts.
           </p>
           <p>
            The
            <a href="http://money.howstuffworks.com/interest-rate.htm">
             <strong>
              interest rate
             </strong>
            </a>
            a bank charges its borrowers depends on both the number of people who want to borrow and the amount of money the bank has available to lend. As we mentioned in the previous section, the amount available to lend also depends upon the reserve requirement the Federal Reserve Board has set. At the same time, it may also be affected by the
            <a href="http://money.howstuffworks.com/fed3.htm">
             funds rate
            </a>
            , which is the interest rate that banks charge each other for short-term loans to meet their reserve requirements. Check out
            <a href="http://money.howstuffworks.com/fed.htm">
             How the Fed Works
            </a>
            for more on how the Fed influences the economy.
           </p>
           <p>
            Loaning money is also inherently risky. A bank never really knows if it'll get that money back. Therefore, the riskier the loan the higher the interest rate the bank charges. While paying interest may not seem to be a great financial move in some respects, it really is a small price to pay for using someone else's money. Imagine having to save all of the money you needed in order to
            <a href="http://home.howstuffworks.com/real-estate/buying-home/mortgage.htm">
             buy a house
            </a>
            . We wouldn't be able to buy houses until we retired!
           </p>
           <p>
            Banks also charge
            <strong>
             fees
            </strong>
            for services like checking,
            <a href="atm.htm">
             ATM
            </a>
            access and overdraft protection. Loans have their own set of fees that go along with them. Another source of income for banks is
            <strong>
             investments
            </strong>
            and
            <a href="http://money.howstuffworks.com/mortgage-backed-security.htm">
             <strong>
              securities
             </strong>
            </a>
            .
           </p>
