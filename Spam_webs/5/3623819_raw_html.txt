       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SHC5FEVI9FGRYG7" data-entry-url="/id/Survial-Kit/step2/null/" id="photoset-SHC5FEVI9FGRYG7">

         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SHC5FEVI9FGRYG7" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/F9M/ZFFM/I9FGRZ1T/F9MZFFMI9FGRZ1T.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/F9MZFFMI9FGRZ1T" data-notes="" href="/file/F9MZFFMI9FGRZ1T" id="photoset-link-FL0C614I9YBX6TL-0" style="">

          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">

And we opted for a trick birthday candle to add to the kit  the wick is infused with magnesium, which allows you to keep a flame even in the windiest of conditions. We then wrapped rubber bands around the cache. Melted, they make a useful adhesive or they can assist in a finger splint or tourniquet.
        </p>

        Step 3:
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SR0LGEOI9FGRYG8" data-entry-url="/id/Survial-Kit/step3/null/" id="photoset-SR0LGEOI9FGRYG8">

          </div>
         </a>
        </div>

       <div class="txt step-body">

Designed for this very reason, the pod is stuffed with helpful gear including 550 paracord, sharp eye knife, flint, 6 of Jute Firstarter Tinder, and duct tape. Also, a Tru-Nords Pocket Compass was next on the list. This moisture-sealed compass supplies a cobalt steel magnet and luminous paint dial for superior orientation, even in the dead of night. And for when the matches run out weve packed an additional stainless steel lighter to keep the fire going.
        </p>
