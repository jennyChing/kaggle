                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Carrot and turkey pasta
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6593538912500018766" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <i>
                      Enjoy this easy, healthy pasta recipe that features lots of good-for-you carrots, lean ground turkey and dried plums.
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-bl36Pz8Mx8U/VUuolthfnjI/AAAAAAAAEo4/a6GT_nFFfRE/s1600/carrot-turkey-pasta.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Carrot and turkey pasta" border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-bl36Pz8Mx8U/VUuolthfnjI/AAAAAAAAEo4/a6GT_nFFfRE/s1600/carrot-turkey-pasta.jpg" title="Carrot and turkey pasta"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I'm pretty psyched about this carrot and turkey pasta, peeps. Read that sentence again and you'll know why.
                     <b>
                      <a href="http://www.yankeekitchenninja.com/search/label/carrots">
                       Carrots
                      </a>
                     </b>
                     . As the
                     <i>
                      main
                     </i>
                     ingredient in a
                     <i>
                      pasta
                     </i>
                     dish.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     And -- wait for it -- it doesn't suck.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     So much so that it got two thumbs up from Mr. Ninj, and regular readers know that's like having your picky three-year-old eater declare that she is a devoted fan of lentils salads.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Now that you're already reeling from the fact that a carrot-based pasta is awesome, let me turn it up to eleven: There are also
                     <i>
                      prunes
                     </i>
                     in this
                     <b>
                      <a href="http://www.yankeekitchenninja.com/2010/07/pasta.html">
                       pasta
                      </a>
                     </b>
                     dish.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Whoops, sorry, I meant
                     <i>
                      dried plums
                     </i>
                     . Apparently prunes got a bad rep as prunes and have now been reinvented as dried plums. The plum formerly known as a prune.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Whatevs. I still love prunes. I mean
                     <i>
                      dried plums
                     </i>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     And I've got 'em in this pasta, adding a little touch of natural sweetness.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-8uudWUTwgdg/VUuoxR0--5I/AAAAAAAAEpA/SdlH8W77PYg/s1600/carrot-turkey-pasta-2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Carrot and turkey pasta" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-8uudWUTwgdg/VUuoxR0--5I/AAAAAAAAEpA/SdlH8W77PYg/s1600/carrot-turkey-pasta-2.jpg" title="Carrot and turkey pasta"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     This is definitely a super-easy, kid-friendly dinner that is perfect for
                     <b>
                      <a href="http://www.yankeekitchenninja.com/2010/07/easy-and-healthy-weeknight-dinners.html">
                       busy weeknight family dinners
                      </a>
                     </b>
                     . Plus, it reheats excellently as next-day lunch bag leftovers.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I love it when you get two meals from one, don't you?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Especially when it's a meal that's as healthy as this one. I mean, there are a boatload of carrots in this dish. And extra-lean ground turkey keeps it on the lighter side. And if you want to add a touch of creaminess, add a dollop of plain greek yogurt before serving.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Have I piqued your interest? Are you digging the idea of carrots and prunes in a pasta dish? Leave a comment: The Ninj wants to know.

                     <blockquote>
                      <div id="recipebox" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
                       <h3>
                        <span itemprop="name">
                         Carrot and Turkey Pasta
                        </span>
                       </h3>
                       (adapted from Better Homes and Gardens)
                       <br/>
                       Ingredients:
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        8 ounces elbow macaroni or other small pasta
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        1 pound extra-lean ground turkey
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        10-ounce bag shredded carrots
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        1 small red onion, diced
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        1 tablespoon minced garlic
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        1 teaspoon smoked paprika
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        15-ounce can no-salt-added tomato sauce
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        2/3 cup chopped dried plums
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        plain Greek yogurt
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       Directions:
                       <br/>
                       Cook pasta according to package directions. Drain.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       While pasta cooks, heat a little olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add turkey and cook until done (about 5 minutes). Remove the turkey from pan with slotted spoon and set aside. Drain any excess liquid from the pan, then heat a bit more olive oil in it and add the carrots, onion and all the spices. Cook, stirring often, about 6-8 minutes or until carrots begin to get tender. Add the cooked turkey, tomato sauce, cooked pasta, plums and 1/2 cup water. Cook until heated through. Serve topped with a dollop of yogurt, if desired.
