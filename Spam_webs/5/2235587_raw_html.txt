      <div id="faqs">
       <h3>
        Q: Is there downtime?
       </h3>
       <div>
        <p>
         Many patients return to work the next day and even go out to dinner that night. A few patients do experience some mild swelling or black and blue, so don' get it right before some important work or social function.
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3>
        Q: Does Ultherapy hurt?
       </h3>
       <div>
        <p>
         Ultherapy was originally taught using only mild analgesics such as Advil. We prefer giving our patients stronger oral sedation, so you will need to have someone to pick you up.
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3>
        Q: What kind of results should I expect and how long do they last?
       </h3>
       <div>
        <p>
         Our rule of thumb is that Ultherapy will give you 25-30% of a facelift for 20% of the price. Treatment results should last one to two years.
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3>
        Q: Is it true that it works on the same layer of tissue as a surgical facelift?
       </h3>
       <div>
        <p>
         You can actually see the layers of the face being treated during Ultherapy. The Ultrasound is focused to heat and tighten the SMAS layer, the same one that we lift for a facelift.
        </p>
