        <p>
         <a href="http://inmybag.net/inmybag/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-13-700x465.jpg">
          <img alt="Why-you-only-need-one-lens-13-700x465" class=" size-full wp-image-11367 aligncenter" height="465" src="http://inmybag.net/inmybag/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-13-700x465.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been a professional photographer for 6 years and like most photographers I have invested in a number of high quality lenses, but have recently concluded that this has been a waste of money.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Why you only need one lens to make world class images" class=" size-full wp-image-8647 aligncenter" height="469" src="http://inmybag.net/wp512/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-15.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its not that these are not great lenses, but I truly prefer to use a single lens.
        </p>
        <p>
         At first, I used other lenses and always changed lenses during the shoot. Once I started using my 50 mm prime lens, I slowly started using the others less and less, until now I shoot everything with that ones.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive hesitated from selling my other lenses because they can occasionally be useful for some jobs, but what 50 mm gives to me, its totally great. I feel comfortable with it, and now I know it so well, that I really do use it constantly.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, heres why I think that you only need one lens to make world class images:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Why you only need one lens to make world class images" class=" size-full wp-image-8640 aligncenter" height="499" src="http://inmybag.net/wp512/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-07.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         1) Consistency and cohesion:
        </h2>
        <p>
         A single lens gives your work a consistent and cohesive viewpoint and feel. I can say in some ways that my style is also the 50 mm, used for large shots, landscapes and portraits as well. This represents the perspective in my photos.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class=" wp-image-8643 size-full aligncenter" height="499" src="http://inmybag.net/wp512/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-10.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         2) Narrative:
        </h2>
        <p>
         Your story telling and the ability to construct a narrative is a far better use of your time than on which lens to use.
        </p>
        <p>
         What I do, before each shooting, is to research inspirational images, decide about location, make-up, models etc. Most of all, I decide about the style of the final images, the emotions I want to give, and, at the first place, the ATMOSPHERE and the STORY behind all the series, if its a fine art project or a fashion editorial, I do exactly the same.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its not important thinking about which light I will use, which lens, which kind of technical skills. For me, it goes beyond skills and techniques, its all about story and atmosphere. Obviously I treat light in different ways for each shooting, to reach the result I want.
        </p>
        <p>
         One thing is for sure, is that I am going to shoot it with my 50 mm lens.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class=" wp-image-8646 size-full aligncenter" height="499" src="http://inmybag.net/wp512/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-14.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         3) Do one thing well:
        </h2>
        <p>
         When you only use a single lens you truly push to find its limits far more than when you can just pop another lens on. You build a relationship which has far more depth.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats the point; this lens represent my perspective, my way to see everything through the camera. its like my vision as a photographer, not the vision of life and world you can catch from my finished photos, but the real vision I have when I look through it, with my camera in my hands. My camera just wouldnt be the same without this lens attached.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class=" wp-image-8638 size-full aligncenter" height="499" src="http://inmybag.net/wp512/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-05.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         4) Saves time:
        </h2>
        <p>
         It saves you time worrying about lens choice and longer term, saves you time that would have been spent cleaning your sensor from all those lens changes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class=" wp-image-8641 size-full aligncenter" height="499" src="http://inmybag.net/wp512/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-08.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         5) Normalization:
        </h2>
        <p>
         A 50mm lens mimics the perspective of the human eye, this consistent and familiar perspective helps the viewer feel comfortable and gives them the sense that they are actually there.
        </p>
        <p>
         I live in photos, every time I walk down the street, that Im in a shop, or when I watch a movie  I see photos everywhere. For a particular place, light, composition, atmosphere and with my 50 mm , if I want, I can exactly take that precise scene. Even when I work on more constructed shots, I build the set, I look around, just to search the perfect point of view for me, and Im sure that my 50 mm will catch exactly what I see with my eyes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Why you only need one lens to make world class images" class=" size-full wp-image-8639 aligncenter" height="499" src="http://inmybag.net/wp512/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-06.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         6) Cost:
        </h2>
        <p>
         Invest in one amazing lens and save the rest of your money.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class=" wp-image-8654 size-full aligncenter" height="499" src="http://inmybag.net/wp512/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Why-you-only-need-one-lens-16.jpg" width="750"/>
        </p>
