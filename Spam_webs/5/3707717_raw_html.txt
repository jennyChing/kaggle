         <h1>
          The Top 5 Comic Book Homes
         </h1>
         <div class="author">
          Weichert Realtors
         </div>
         <div class="published-date">
          August 18, 2014
         </div>
         <h2>
          Which of these comic cribs would you want to call home?
         </h2>
         <div class="clearfix" id="articlecontent">
          <img alt="" src="http://wdcassets.blob.core.windows.net/images/content/a22353dc-3003-4c4b-80d2-f74e5d951b0e/superhero.jpg"/>
          <p>
           When your schedule consists of trying to save or destroy the world, a nice place to rest your head at night is a priority.  After scanning the comic universe for the finest superhero and villain digs, we present our top 5.
          </p>
          <ol style="font-weight: bold;">
           <li value="5">
            The Baxter Building:
            <span style="font-weight: normal;">
             The Fantastic Four made the top 5 floors of this 35 story skyscraper their home.  Located at 42nd Street and Madison in
             <a href="http://www.weichert.com/NY/Newyork/Manhattan/Listings/">
              Manhattan
             </a>
             , Mr. Fantastic and crew were sitting on some prime
             <a href="http://www.weichert.com/NYC/">
              NYC real estate
             </a>
             .  However sharing the building with a paper recycling company would surely lead to some noise and odor issues.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li value="4">
            The Hall of Doom:
            <span style="font-weight: normal;">
             Resembling Darth Vader's helmet, the Hall of Doom served as home to the 13 member Legion of Doom.  The HoD was able to go underwater, fly into space and even time travel.  However its usual location in the less than desirable Slaughter Swamp just outside of Gotham City knocked it down a few notches.  Location, location, location.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li value="3">
            Wayne Manor:
            <span style="font-weight: normal;">
             The stately home of Bruce Wayne is the most traditional home on our list.  Built with some serious Wayne Enterprises cash the home features arguably the best superhero home base of all, the Batcave.  While the Batcave would offer endless entertainment, the rest of the Manor seems kind of impractical for just Bruce and Alfred.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li value="2">
            Castle Doom:
            <span style="font-weight: normal;">
             Home to dictator/genius inventor/sorcerer Doctor Doom, this home offered everything a super-villain could need.  Nuclear reactor ... check.  Time machine ... check.  Dungeons ... check.  It wasn't all world domination however.  The 110 room castle also featured an art gallery.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li value="1">
            The Fortress of Solitude:
            <span style="font-weight: normal;">
             The original man cave.  This icy crystal residence served as Superman's place to get away from it all.  It also allowed him to reconnect with his deceased father.  The arctic climate would turn many off but the minimalist design, privacy and sentimental nature make it our #1 comic book residence.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ol>
