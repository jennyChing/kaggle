          <div itemprop="description">
           <p>
            Gregorys exquisite
            <span class="caps">
             BLACK
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             BEANS
            </span>
            . Well I think so, my friends and family certainly enjoy them. I was born in Cuba and raised in Miami around a household that was always in the kitchen.  At first I had very little interest on what was on the stove and more on what I was going to eat.  But one day, as I watched my mother make her wonderful recipes, I decided to joined her. What happened next was my meals were as good as hers and some, with all due respect to Mom, were better.  I rarely go out to eat a Cuban meal since it is so simple to make at home. I find most restaurants enhance the recipes with pre-mixes of spices that contain
            <span class="caps">
             MSG
            </span>
            and are simply terrible, will not mention brand names.  Or  they enhance heavily with the wrong amount of spice (mostly Cumin) and call it Cuban food  wrong!  With Cuban cuisine it is not about heavy handed spices- but, how well you use them.  One in particular to carefully monitor is Cumin; taste the essence of the flavor and not the spice before your recipe. This I feel even with established Chefs that cook Cuban cusine is the most common mistake.
           </p>
           <p>
            I am extremely picky with my cooking, ingredients and protocol to serving the proper dish and the first recipe I achieved this with is my Cuban Black Beans.  I do not eat meat, poultry, pork but do cook them with enjoyment.  Mostly I am a fish-atarian. In my recipe I use only the basics, onions, green peppers, garlic, olive oil- the core in most Cuban dishes.  But, before you venture and make them note a few things about
            <span class="caps">
             CUBAN
            </span>
            cuisine It is not
            <span class="caps">
             SPICY
            </span>
            it is properly
            <span class="caps">
             SEASONED
            </span>
            , yellow rice or saffron rice does not belong on your plate with your black beans. Understand the essence of yellow rice and how to apply it- that is another recipe.
           </p>
           <p>
            <span class="caps">
             BUEN
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             APROVECHO

            !

         <h2 class="headline mb20 ">
          Instructions
         </h2>
         <div id="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          <ol>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             1
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              TOOLS
             </span>
            </span>
            <br/>
            1- 8qt Slow Cooker with Lid, cast iron &amp; enamel is best, I recommend
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              STAUB
             </span>
            </span>
            unconditionally!
            <br/>
            1  9.5 Stainless Steel Fry Pan, 7 ply is best I recommend Viking!
            <br/>
            Chef Knife or Santoku to chop/dice and prep the vegetables
            <br/>
            Paring Knife
            <br/>
            Measuring Spoons
            <br/>
            Food Mill
            <br/>
            Medium Size bowl
            <br/>
            Tongs
            <br/>
            Oh yes a nice piece of soft/textured French bread cut into slices for your tasting as you go. Yummy! To make the perfect black bean recipe shop at www.bluecashew.com !
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             2
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              PREPARATION
             </span>
            </span>
            (
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              THE
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              SOAKING
             </span>
            </span>
            OF
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              THE
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              BEANS
             </span>
            </span>
            )
            <br/>
            Clean/rinse the beans very carefully several times they might contain little pebbles.
            <br/>
            Once cleaned place the beans in the pot
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             3
            </span>
            Cut 1- Spanish Onion into quartersplace in the pot
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             4
            </span>
            Cut 1- Green Pepper remove seeds into quartersplace in the pot
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             5
            </span>
            1-Teaspoon of Ground Cuminplace in the pot
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             6
            </span>
            1-Teaspoon of Coarse Saltplace in the pot
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             7
            </span>
            1-Teaspoon of Black Pepperplace in the pot
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             8
            </span>
            1 1/2-Tablespoon of Olive Oilplace in the pot
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             9
            </span>
            4-Large Peeled Garlic Cloves  Use your paring knife and cut a slit into themplace in the pot
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             10
            </span>
            Add 3- Dried Bay Leaves
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             11
            </span>
            I like to use my hands, but spoons work as well, mix all together. The aroma is great!
            <br/>
            Fill the pot with water about 1 inch above the bean line, cover and soak overnight for at least 12 hours.
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             12
            </span>
            IN
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              THE
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              MORNING
             </span>
            </span>
            (
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              THE
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              FUN
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              BEGINS
             </span>
            </span>
            )
            <br/>
            Check the bean water line- water has been absorbed, add more, at least 1-inch above the bean line
            <br/>
            Bring to a high boil for about 5minutes, stir and reduce to low heat
            <br/>
            Cook uncovered for about 2 1/2 hours
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             13
            </span>
            Skim occasionally and stircheck the water line, never let it dry out, check bean tenderness.  If you need to add more water it must be warm/hot.
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             14
            </span>
            After 2 1/2 hours the vegetables (onion, green pepper, cloves, etc. have basically dissolved to nothing). Discard the bay leaves and remove the vegetables with your tongs and transfer to a bowl. Next transfer into a food mill- if you leave a few vegetables behind in the pot it is fine. Add some liquid of the beans to your food mill with the vegetables and create a nice paste- add back to the pot.
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             15
            </span>
            Taste the bean tenderness most should have cracked and they should be tender not
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              MUSH
             </span>
            </span>
            , not hardContinue to cook on low heat. Add your last 3-remaining bay leaves. Add some fresh cracked pepper  to taste. (Less is more, you can always add more)
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             16
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              FINAL
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              STEP
             </span>
            </span>
            .
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              MAKING
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              THE
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              SOFRITO
             </span>
            </span>
            !
            <br/>
            Fry Pan is now needed
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             17
            </span>
            Finely dice/cube your remaining onion,green pepper(seeds removed) and garlic cloves  set aside
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             18
            </span>
            Add about 1/4 cup of olive oil to the fry pan low heat until
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              FRAGRANT
             </span>
            </span>
            do not smoke it at all
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             19
            </span>
            Add the garlic, stir, do not let it brown
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             20
            </span>
            Add the onion, continue to stir until opaque and tender about 8-10 minutes
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             21
            </span>
            Add the green pepper cook until tender
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             22
            </span>
            Add pinch of salt to taste
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             23
            </span>
            Add pinch of black ground pepper
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             24
            </span>
            Add a teaspoon of apple cider vinegar (sometimes I use the cap of the bottle as the measurement)
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             25
            </span>
            Add the cumin1teaspoon
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             26
            </span>
            Stir  mix well making sure all flavors are combined
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             27
            </span>
            Remove from heat and add all contents to the beans that are still cooking at low temp. Continue to cook your beans for about another 1 1/2 low heat 
            <span class="caps">
             <span class="caps">
              COVERED
             </span>
            </span>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="number_divider">
             28
            </span>
            Your basically done The beans should be tender, the broth a bit thick
            <br/>
            Set aside serve and eat or store in the fridgeonly in glass containers.
           </li>
          </ol>
