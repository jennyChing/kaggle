       </h3>

       <p>
        A tree-filled courtyard is glimpsed through the shimmering glass-brick facade of this house in Hiroshima, designed by Japanese architect Hiroshi Nakamura.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Translucent Slip House by Carl Turner Architects
       </h3>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/624b5-8f136-5adc8-3a2c9-b83d9.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/300175/slip-house-carl-turner-architects/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/c3cec-ee7c2-20f69-3a492-40803.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="http://minimalissimo.com/2013/08/slip-house/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        Slip House is a unique entity on a street full of traditional English houses. The three story building is comprised of rectangular forms covered in translucent glass planks. Completed in 2012 by Carl Turner Architects, Slip House is designed with beauty and sustainability in mind. The structure features a green roof, rain water harvesting, and a ground source heat pump. Slip Houses smart design makes it one of the most energy efficient houses in the UK.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Translucent House San Fransisco
       </h3>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/4239d-09647-4ee68-22268-12f92.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="http://www.dwell.com/house-tours/article/just-redo-it?utm_medium=referral&amp;utm_source=pulsenews">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        What do you get when you give a couple of designers unlimited creative license on a very limited budget? For Andrew Dunbar and Zoee Astrakhan, the possibilities were limitless. They ended up with an incredible, translucent home!
       </p>
       <h3>
        Translucent Bamboo House
       </h3>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/0225b-5b888-b659a-c9430-ce21a.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="http://dornob.com/small-shoots-big-shades-beautiful-tropical-bamboo-home/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        A climate-specific design for Costa Rica by an architect for his mother, this is a unique dream home that combines local building traditions, modern techniques and an extreme sensitivity to connecting the interior with the wild and wonderful outdoors around the house. Her previous abode? A self-constructed work composed of scrap wood and plastic, making this an even more remarkably luxurious upgrade to jungle living!
       </p>
       <h3>
        House in Yamasaki by Tato Architects
       </h3>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/4e685-95af4-57a46-99f1d-4254a.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="http://www.dezeen.com/2012/11/12/house-in-yamasaki-with-rooftop-sheds-by-tato-architects/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/9c1eb-84e32-b6a37-67d27-54101.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="http://www.dezeen.com/2012/11/12/house-in-yamasaki-with-rooftop-sheds-by-tato-architects/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        This is a house in the northern part of Hyogo Prefecture for a couple and their two children. The construction site is a part of a place surrounded by mountains and the sky is overcast most of the days. The architect wanted to create a light, stable indoor climate and came up with a plan of three sheds of translucent house types arranged on a 1.8 m high, grey foundation platform.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Marcus Beach House by Bark Architecture
       </h3>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/1a2c5-4c425-b2288-a20bd-897bc.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="https://lunchboxarchitect.com/featured/marcus-beach-house/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="http://www.contemporist.com/2014/09/11/marcus-beach-house-by-bark-architects/mb_110914_03/"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="https://lunchboxarchitect.com/featured/marcus-beach-house/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        The Marcus Beach house celebrates a natural, coastal setting providing its occupants with an inextricable relationship to the landscape and sensitive surrounding environment. The dwelling explores lightness, filtering natural breezes, layers of transparency and integrating indoor / outdoor spaces within dynamic patterns of light and shadow, being a simple frame to enable a contemporary sustainable lifestyle to unfold.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Mme Experimental House by Kengo Kuma &amp; Associates
       </h3>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/aacb0-ab809-14d36-15201-de344.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/322830/meme-experimental-house-kengo-kuma-associates/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/71485-6a5f8-ef066-cc773-32aa1.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <small>
         <a href="http://www.archdaily.com/322830/meme-experimental-house-kengo-kuma-associates/">
          Image Source
         </a>
        </small>
       </p>
       <p>
        This experimental house is wrapped a wooden frame made of Japanese larch with a membrane material of polyester fluorocarbon coating. Inner part is covered with removable glass-fiber-cloth membrane. Between the two membranes, a polyester insulator recycled from PET bottles is inserted that penetrates the light. This composition is based on the idea that by convecting the air in-between, the internal environment could be kept comfortable because of the circulation.
       </p>

The house fulfils the modest brief for a beach retreat and in the future it will evolve into a permanent dwelling. The house effortlessly integrates within the existing streetscape whilst adapting to the changing character of the town.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Peninsula House by Sean Godsell Architects
       </h3>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/fd62f-be263-27d45-cff63-8aef1.jpg"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="https://s3.servicecentral.com.au/art_img/7b951-56b77-f9527-55ed3-1ce78.jpg"/>

       </p>
