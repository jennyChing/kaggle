          <h3>
           Key facts
          </h3>
          <p>
           Club: Birmingham City FC | Opening: 1906 | Capacity: 30,009 seats
          </p>
          <h3>
           History and description
          </h3>
          <p>
           St. Andrews Stadium opened on the 26th of December 1906 with a league match between Birmingham City and Middlesbrough. The stadium consisted of one covered main stand and open terraces on the other sides, with an estimated capacity of 75,000 spectators.
          </p>
          <p>
           Small improvements were made in the 1930s, mainly by increasing cover, and a record attendance was set on the 11th of March 1939 when 66,844 fans visited an FA Cup match against Everton.
          </p>
          <p>
           The stadium got heavily damaged in the Second World War, but got soon after rebuilt.
          </p>
          <p>
           St. Andrews Stadium got further redeveloped in the 1950s and 1960s, first with the construction of a new main stand in the early 1950s, then with another new stand at the Railway End, and finally with the erection of new roofs over the Kop and Tilton Road end.
          </p>
          <p>
           No changes were made in the following two decades, and by the late 1980s the stadium had slowly fallen into disrepair. Capacity had furthermore been decreased to just 26,000 due to safety measures.
          </p>
          <p>
           In the early 1990s, Birmingham City therefore embarked on a redevelopment program, which started with the demolition of the Kop and Tilton Road terraces. Two new all-seater stands were built in their place.
          </p>
          <p>
           Works were completed in 1999 with the opening of a new stand at the Railway End.
          </p>
          <p>
           In the mid 2000s, Birmingham City was involved in plans to replace St. Andrews with a 55,000-seater multi-purpose stadium, however these plans never materialised.
          </p>

          <h3>
           Getting there
          </h3>
          <p>
           St. Andrews Stadium is located about 1.5 miles east of Birminghams city centre. The slightly uphill walk from Birmingham New Street Rail Station or Moor Street Rail Station takes about 30 minutes.
          </p>
          <p>
           Alternatively,
           <a href="http://www.networkwestmidlands.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.networkwestmidlands.com']);" target="_blank">
            bus
           </a>
           17, 58, 59, 60, 97, and 97A connect the stadium with Moor Street Station (or Digbeth Road). The journey takes 15 to 20 minutes.
          </p>
          <p>
           On matchdays, there are also
           <a href="http://www.nationalrail.co.uk/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.nationalrail.co.uk']);" target="_blank">
            trains
           </a>
           that run to nearby Bordesley Rail station, which is a 10-minute walk from the stadium. On non-matchdays this station is out-of-use.
          </p>
          <p>
           St. Andrews lies just off the A45, which connects with the M42 in the east and the M38/M6 in the north. At the Bordesley Circus roundabout turn east onto Coventry Road.
          </p>
          <p>
           Address: St. Andrews Stadium, Birmingham, B9 4RL
          </p>

          <h3>
           Eat, drink, and sleep
          </h3>
          <p>
           St. Andrews Stadium is located in a rather quiet residential area with little around. As usual in England, there is always the odd pub, though you find many more options in Birminghams city centre, which is not far away. There are, for example, various eating and drinking options south of Birmingham New Street Station, from where one can walk or catch a bus to the ground.
          </p>
          <p>
           There is an
           <a href="http://www.booking.com/hotel/gb/ibis-birmingham-bordesley.html?aid=353837;label=standrews" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.booking.com']);" target="_blank">
            Ibis Hotel
           </a>
           a few minutes walking from St. Andrews, while
           <a href="http://www.booking.com/hotel/gb/royal-george.html?aid=353837;label=standrews" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.booking.com']);" target="_blank">
            Royal George Hotel
           </a>
           lies right at the back of the stadium. Both are reasonably priced and get acceptable reviews.
           <a href="http://www.booking.com/hotel/gb/the-moseley-arms.html?aid=353837;label=standrews" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.booking.com']);" target="_blank">

          <h3>
           Tickets
          </h3>
          <p>
           Tickets for Birmingham City games can be purchased
           <a href="https://www.eticketing.co.uk/bcfc/default.aspx" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.eticketing.co.uk']);" target="_blank">
            online
           </a>
           , by phone +44 (0) 844 557 1875, or in person at the Ticket Office at St. Andrews Stadium.
          </p>
          <p>
           If tickets remain, these can also be bought on the day of the match at the stadium. Birmingham City rarely sells out in the Championship.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ticket prices typically range from 25.00 for an upper-tier seat behind the goal to 30.00 for a seat at one of the long sides. Tickets are 5.00 cheaper if bought in pre-sale.
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           For more information email
           <a href="mailto:ticket.office@bhfc.com">
            ticket.office@bhfc.com
           </a>
           or call+44 (0) 844 557 1875.
          </p>
