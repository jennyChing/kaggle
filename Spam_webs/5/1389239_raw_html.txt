       <h2>
        <span class="editsection">
         <a href="/wiki/Minge?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: A Furry Little Friend">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="A_Furry_Little_Friend">
         A Furry Little Friend
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        A
        <b>
         Minge
        </b>
        is a small American pudendum, closely related to the European
        <a class="new" href="/wiki/Flange?action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Flange (page does not exist)">
         Flange
        </a>
        and to the Asian Fadge. It is covered in small dark hairs which create a distinctive muffled sound whilst the
        <b>
         Minge
        </b>
        is in motion.
       </p>
       <p>
        The Mexican Hairless (sometimes called the "
        <i>
         Shaven Haven
        </i>
        " Minge) is popular as a pet, closely followed by the Brazillian Ridgeback Flapper. Both are now native to North America, and have been introduced to parts of Europe (excluding
        <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
         France
        </a>
        ).
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span class="editsection">
         <a href="/wiki/Minge?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">
         Habitat
        </span>
       </h2>
       <figure class="article-thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 250px">
        <a class="image image-thumbnail" href="/wiki/File:LohanGymno.JPG">
         <img alt="LohanGymno" class="thumbimage " data-image-key="LohanGymno.JPG" data-image-name="LohanGymno.JPG" height="309" src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/uncyclopedia/images/d/da/LohanGymno.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20050831040901" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <figcaption>
         <a class="sprite info-icon" href="/wiki/File:LohanGymno.JPG">
         </a>
         <p class="caption">
          Rarely seen in daylight,
          <b>
           Minges
          </b>
          prefer to live within the shadows, though this has not affected their popularity as pets.
         </p>
        </figcaption>
       </figure>
       <p>
        <b>
         Minges
        </b>
        prefer warm, damp places, preferably shaded, though are able to exist in any climate. Some varieties are known to shelter in specially constructed "hammocks" created from many different types of material, though mostly cotton. Interestingly some species prefer to live in the open air, though they tend to do so in large groups, or
        <a href="/wiki/Nudist" title="Nudist">
         colonies
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span class="editsection">
         <a href="/wiki/Minge?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spread of the Minge">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Spread_of_the_Minge">
         Spread of the Minge
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        It is known that the
        <b>
         Minge
        </b>
        (and related species) have spread throughout the known world. The most notable of these are two dichotomous subspecies; the most prominent of these is the "
        <i>
         Mini Outback
        </i>
        ". It is most at home in the Australian Bush, sometimes confused with the
        <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
         Australian Bush Baby
        </a>
        (
        <i>
         Ozzi Minimuff
        </i>
        ), the miniature species of the Australian Bush, though it should be noted that these animals are inbreeds. The second species is the Indo-European Long Hair (
        <i>
         Hurisute Maximuff
        </i>
        ) closely Related to the Asian Fadge. Its vast territory goes from
        <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
         Eastern Europe
        </a>
        to
        <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
         Japan
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span class="editsection">
         <a href="/wiki/Minge?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mating Habits">
          edit
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Mating_Habits">
         Mating Habits
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        With the exception of the
        <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
         Brazillian
        </a>
        variety, the male is seldom seen, so little factual data exists on their mating habits. What is known, is that during the mating season (believed to follow some sort of lunar or tidal pattern) the
        <a href="/wiki/Female" title="Female">
         female
        </a>
        begins to emit a sweet smelling odour, similar to fermenting apples. This is a dangerous time for the
        <b>
         Minge
        </b>
        as it also attracts the Minge's main predator, the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dong" title="Dong">
         Dong
        </a>
        as well as
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trousersnakes" title="Trousersnakes">
         Trousersnakes
        </a>
        .
       </p>
