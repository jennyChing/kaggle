             <h1>
              Dont Be This Guy.
             </h1>
             <p>
              Dont be that guy. The one who gives away personal information on the web. There is one identity fraud victim every 3 seconds. Social networking sites make your personal information publically available, putting you at risk of identity theft. Take a moment and think before you post.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <font color="#a80101">
                Trend Micro Security helps you to stay safe online.
               </font>
              </b>
             </p>
             <h3>
              Security Tips to keep personal information private
             </h3>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Know when and where to share your personal information online  dont share your credit card details unless youre shopping online. Cybercriminals may be eavesdropping.
              </li>
              <li>
               Ask yourself before clicking on that Share button: can someone use this information against me? Protect yourself by only sharing information that you know cant be used against you.
              </li>
              <li>
               Customize your privacy settings and limit those who can view your posts to Friends only. This prevents anyone outside your Friends list from seeing your posts.
              </li>
              <li>
               Turn on your social networking profile security settings where applicable. This helps prevent unauthorized access to your account.
              </li>
              <li>
               Use a privacy scanner to make sure your social networking accounts are secure. This helps you find and fix any security gaps you may have missed when securing your social networking accounts.
              </li>
             </ul>
