             <h1>
              <b>
               WHERE WE STARTED
              </b>
             </h1>
             <ul>
              <li>
               <b>
                WHO DOESN'T WANT TO FLY LIKE A BIRD? That's what we thought so we built a prototype IPad version of AERO
               </b>
               . It was just a basic simulation of the concept, however, it still achievednearly 100,000 downloads, because it is super fun!
              </li>
              <li>
               <b>
                Bill Nye the Science Guy
               </b>
               volunteered his time to address several essential concepts in Albatross flight.We then spent 6 months building a full and accurate simulation of bird flight with a visualization of force vectors around the bird. "IT'S REALLY SCIENCE! YOUR A BIRD FOR CRYING OUT LOUD!!"
              </li>
             </ul>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="269237">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/000/269/237/b153f4c8e712e936afc1bf4510469f0c_original.jpg?v=1353296995&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;q=92&amp;s=427e241b81808c8d614029aa34101ba7"/>
              </figure>

             <ul>
              <li>
               <b>
                Last year, we launched a simulation of the game
               </b>
               in which we placed two Wiimotes inside of flappable wingsto create the experience of flying.
              </li>
              <li>
               We thought:
               <b>
                "Wouldn't it be awesome if you could FEEL what it's like to have wings?"
               </b>
               So we made some wings that utilize two nintendo Wii controllers and took AERO on the road! We went to Maker Faire, the USA Engineering Festival, and various schools throughout the year. The overwhelmingly positive response from students, parents, and teachers made it clear to us that we had to get AERO to everyone!
              </li>
             </ul>

             <h1>
              <b>
               WHERE WE NEED TO BE
              </b>
             </h1>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="273183">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/000/273/183/4b6b4482b1a47ab51ace3bd244e7f7f6_original.jpg?v=1353476159&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;q=92&amp;s=5038fa52a33fabbd6dd42cab7fe7323f"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              <b>
               WE WANT TO OFFER A FULL EXPLORATORY WORLD OF FLYING AND AERODYNAMIC LEARNING EXPERIENCES FOR THE IPAD.
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               In Depth - Physics of Flight Lessons:
              </b>
              AERO will have five special educational lesson levels that teach aerodynamicprinciples in detail.
              <b>
               Lessons will teach lift, drag, thrust, constant and terminal velocity, gravity, banking, turning, and more.
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Destination and Landing challenges:
              </b>
              Island destination courses, completing safe landings.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               RollerCoaster Ring challenges:
              </b>
              Rollercoaster rings and obstacle courses, racing against the computer,treasure hunts collecting a number of objects around the game environment, timed races around different trials, and learning to fly from different perspectives.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Fly into cumulus clouds:
              </b>
              Cumulus clouds are from a upward convection of air. Fly into them to reach hidden collectable stars.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Bank, Turning and Lift challenges:
              </b>
              Bank side to side around ocean buoys to complete an obstacle course.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Fish Diving:
              </b>
              Search and dive for fish!
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               A desalination Albatross can drink from the ocean!
              </b>
              Drink ocean water to survive and watch as you shot the salt out of your nose.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Bird Watcher Mode:
              </b>
              Fly the bird from the perceptive of the Bird Watcher, like flying a remote controlled airplane.
             </p>
             <div class="template asset" contenteditable="false" data-id="273725">
              <figure>
               <img alt="" class="fit" src="https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/000/273/725/9aac6ea8d628e4d64bb29d18cd8115d8_original.jpg?v=1353516931&amp;w=680&amp;fit=max&amp;auto=format&amp;q=92&amp;s=f83eeedce5950369c653c00a696bf006"/>
              </figure>
             </div>
             <p>
              <b>
               Vector Challenge:
              </b>
              Players are tasked with correctly naming and using the right forces to fly in the right direction.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Constant Velocity:
              </b>
              Players have toachieve a constant velocity for a certain amount of time. Can the player find all three methods to do so?
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Vertical Equilibrium:
              </b>
              Players must equalize the lift and gravity vectors to achieve vertical equilibrium. This reinforces earlier learning points.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Gravity Challenge:
              </b>
              Players are challenged to rotate the gravity force vector around the bird in various directions, reinforcing gravities pull towards the center of the earth.
             </p>

             <p>
              As a nonprofit, GameDesk is always looking for more funding in order to increase our development of educational games and research into education reform. While our development team has a proven record of creating fun and interactive games, we need help from backers in order to take the next steps and release a game to the public. If we reach our stretch goals, those challenges will be exponential as we develop for different platforms.
