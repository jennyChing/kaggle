   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Anima and animus
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
     <span class="mw-redirectedfrom">
      (Redirected from
      <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_(Jung)&amp;redirect=no" title="Anima (Jung)">
       Anima (Jung)
      </a>
      )
     </span>
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
     Jump to:
     <a href="#mw-head">
      navigation

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="hatnote">
      For the album by The Creatures, see
      <a href="/wiki/Anima_Animus" title="Anima Animus">
       Anima Animus
      </a>
      .
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       anima and animus
      </b>
      , in
      <a href="/wiki/Carl_Jung" title="Carl Jung">
       Carl Jung's
      </a>
      school of
      <a href="/wiki/Analytical_psychology" title="Analytical psychology">
       analytical psychology
      </a>
      , are the two primary
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphic" title="Anthropomorphic">
       anthropomorphic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Jungian_archetypes" title="Jungian archetypes">
       archetypes
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Unconscious_mind" title="Unconscious mind">
       unconscious mind
      </a>
      , as opposed to both the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theriomorphic" title="Theriomorphic">
       theriomorphic
      </a>
      and inferior function of the
      <a href="/wiki/Shadow_(psychology)" title="Shadow (psychology)">
       shadow
      </a>
      archetypes, as well as the abstract symbol sets that formulate the archetype of the Self. The anima and animus are described by Jung as elements of his theory of the
      <a href="/wiki/Collective_unconscious" title="Collective unconscious">
       collective unconscious
      </a>
      , a domain of the unconscious that transcends the personal
      <a href="/wiki/Psyche_(psychology)" title="Psyche (psychology)">
       psyche
      </a>
      . In the
      <a href="/wiki/Unconscious_mind" title="Unconscious mind">
       unconscious
      </a>
      of a man, this archetype finds expression as a feminine inner personality:
      <b>
       anima
      </b>
      ; equivalently, in the unconscious of a woman it is expressed as a
      <a href="/wiki/Masculinity" title="Masculinity">
       masculine
      </a>
      inner personality:
      <b>
       animus
      </b>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The anima and animus can be identified as the totality of the unconscious
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feminine" title="Feminine">
       feminine
      </a>
      psychological qualities that a man possesses or the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masculine" title="Masculine">
       masculine
      </a>
      ones possessed by a woman, respectively. It is an archetype of the collective unconscious and not an aggregate of father or mother, brothers, sisters, aunts, uncles, or teachers, though these aspects of the personal unconscious can influence the person for good or ill.
     </p>
     <p>
      Because a man's sensitivity must often be repressed, the anima is one of the most significant autonomous
      <a href="/wiki/Complex_(psychology)" title="Complex (psychology)">
       complexes
      </a>
      of all. It is said to manifest itself by appearing in dreams. It also influences a man's interactions with women and his attitudes toward them and vice versa for women and the animus. Jung said that "the encounter with the
      <a href="/wiki/Shadow_(psychology)" title="Shadow (psychology)">
       shadow
      </a>
      is the 'apprentice-piece' in the individual's development...that with the anima is the 'masterpiece'".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Jung viewed the anima process as being one of the sources of creative ability.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the book
      <i>
       The Invisible Partners
      </i>
      it is said that the key to controlling one's anima/animus is to recognize it when it manifests and exercise our ability to discern the anima/animus from reality.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Levels_of_anima_development">
       Levels of anima development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Levels of anima development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Jung believed anima development has four distinct levels, which he named
      <i>
       Eve
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Helen
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Mary
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Sophia
      </i>
      . In broad terms, the entire process of anima development in a man is about the male subject opening up to emotionality, and in that way a broader
      <a href="/wiki/Spirituality" title="Spirituality">
       spirituality
      </a>
      , by creating a new conscious
      <a href="/wiki/Paradigm" title="Paradigm">
       paradigm
      </a>
      that includes
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intuition_(psychology)" title="Intuition (psychology)">
       intuitive
      </a>
      processes, creativity and imagination, and psychic
      <a href="/wiki/Sensitivity_(human)" title="Sensitivity (human)">
       sensitivity
      </a>
      towards himself and others where it might not have existed previously.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Eve">
       Eve
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Eve">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The first is
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Eve" title="Eve">
        Eve
       </a>
      </i>
      , named after the
      <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Genesis" title="Book of Genesis">
       Genesis
      </a>
      account of
      <a href="/wiki/Adam_and_Eve" title="Adam and Eve">
       Adam and Eve
      </a>
      . It deals with the emergence of a man's object of desire.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Helen">
       Helen
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Helen">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The second is
      <i>
       Helen
      </i>
      , an allusion to
      <a href="/wiki/Helen_of_Troy" title="Helen of Troy">
       Helen of Troy
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">
       Greek mythology
      </a>
      . In this phase, women are viewed as capable of worldly success and of being self-reliant, intelligent and insightful, even if not altogether virtuous. This second phase is meant to show a strong schism in external talents (cultivated business and conventional skills) with lacking internal qualities (inability for virtue, lacking faith or imagination).
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mary">
       Mary
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mary">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The third phase is
      <i>
       Mary
      </i>
      , named after the
      <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">
       Christian
      </a>
      theological understanding of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virgin_Mary" title="Virgin Mary">
       Virgin Mary
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">
       Jesus'
      </a>
      mother). At this level, women can now seem to possess virtue by the perceiving man (even if in an esoteric and dogmatic way), in as much as certain activities deemed consciously unvirtuous cannot be applied to her.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Sophia">
       Sophia
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sophia">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The fourth and final phase of anima development is
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sophia_(wisdom)" title="Sophia (wisdom)">
        Sophia
       </a>
      </i>
      , named after the Greek word for
      <a href="/wiki/Wisdom" title="Wisdom">
       wisdom
      </a>
      . Complete integration has now occurred, which allows women to be seen and related to as particular individuals who possess both positive and negative qualities. The most important aspect of this final level is that, as the personification "Wisdom" suggests, the anima is now developed enough that no single object can fully and permanently contain the images to which it is related.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Levels_of_animus_development">
       Levels of animus development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Levels of animus development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Jung focused more on the man's anima and wrote less about the woman's animus. Jung believed that every woman has an analogous animus within her
      <a href="/wiki/Psyche_(psychology)" title="Psyche (psychology)">
       psyche
      </a>
      , this being a set of unconscious masculine attributes and potentials. He viewed the animus as being more complex than the anima, postulating that women have a host of animus images whereas the male anima consists only of one dominant image.
     </p>
     <p>
      Jung stated that there are four parallel levels of animus development in a woman.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Man_of_mere_physical_power">
       Man of mere physical power
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Man of mere physical power">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The animus "first appears as a personification of mere physical power - for instance as an athletic champion or
      <i>
       muscle man
      </i>
      , such as 'the fictional jungle hero
      <a href="/wiki/Tarzan" title="Tarzan">
       Tarzan
      </a>
      '".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Man_of_action_or_romance">
       Man of action or romance
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Man of action or romance">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In the next phase, the animus "possesses initiative and the capacity for planned action...the
      <i>
       romantic
      </i>
      man - the 19th century British poet
      <a href="/wiki/Percy_Bysshe_Shelley" title="Percy Bysshe Shelley">
       Shelley
      </a>
      ; or the
      <i>
       man of action
      </i>
      - America's
      <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Hemingway" title="Ernest Hemingway">
       Ernest Hemingway
      </a>
      , war hero, hunter, etc."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Man_as_a_professor.2C_clergyman.2C_orator">
       Man as a professor, clergyman, orator
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Man as a professor, clergyman, orator">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In the third phase "the animus becomes the
      <i>
       word
      </i>
      , often appearing as a professor or clergyman...the bearer of the word -
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lloyd_George" title="Lloyd George">
       Lloyd George
      </a>
      , the great political orator".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Man_as_a_helpful_guide_to_understanding_herself">
       Man as a helpful guide to understanding herself
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Man as a helpful guide to understanding herself">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      "Finally, in his fourth manifestation, the animus is the incarnation of
      <i>
       meaning
      </i>
      . On this highest level he becomes (like the anima) a mediator of...spiritual profundity".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Jung noted that "in mythology, this aspect of the
      <i>
       animus
      </i>
      appears as
      <a href="/wiki/Hermes" title="Hermes">
       Hermes
      </a>
      , messenger of the gods; in dreams he is a helpful guide." Like Sophia this is the highest level of mediation between the unconscious and conscious mind.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Anima_and_animus_compared">
       Anima and animus compared
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Anima and animus compared">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The four roles are not identical with genders reversed. Jung believed that while the anima tended to appear as a relatively singular female personality, the animus may consist of a conjunction of multiple male personalities: "in this way the unconscious symbolizes the fact that the animus represents a collective rather than a personal element".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The process of animus development deals with cultivating an independent and non-socially subjugated idea of self by embodying a deeper
      <i>
       word
      </i>
      (as per a specific existential outlook) and manifesting this word. To clarify, this does not mean that a female subject becomes more set in her ways (as this word is steeped in emotionality, subjectivity, and a dynamism just as a well-developed anima is) but that she is more internally aware of what she believes and feels, and is more capable of expressing these beliefs and feelings. Thus the "animus in his most developed form sometimes...make[s] her even more receptive than a man to new creative ideas".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Both final stages of animus and anima development have dynamic qualities (related to the motion and flux of this continual developmental process), open-ended qualities (there is no static perfected ideal or manifestation of the quality in question), and pluralistic qualities (which transcend the need for a singular image, as any subject or object can contain multiple
      <a href="/wiki/Jungian_archetypes" title="Jungian archetypes">
       archetypes
      </a>
      or even seemingly antithetical roles). They also form bridges to the next archetypal figures to emerge, as "the unconscious again changes its dominant character and appears in a new symbolic form, representing the
      <a href="/wiki/Self_in_Jungian_psychology" title="Self in Jungian psychology">
       Self
      </a>
      ".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      - the archetypes of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wise_Old_Woman/Man" title="Wise Old Woman/Man">
       Wise Old Woman/Man
      </a>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Jungian_cautions">
       Jungian cautions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anima_and_animus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Jungian cautions">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Jungians warned that "every personification of the unconscious - the
      <a href="/wiki/Shadow_(psychology)" title="Shadow (psychology)">
       shadow
      </a>
      , the anima, the animus, and the Self - has both a light and a dark aspect....the anima and animus have dual aspects: They can bring life-giving development and creativeness to the personality, or they can cause petrification and physical death".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      One danger was of what Jung termed "invasion" of the conscious by the unconscious archetype - "Possession caused by the anima...bad taste: the anima surrounds herself with inferior people".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Jung insisted that "a state of anima possession...must be prevented. The anima is thereby forced into the inner world, where she functions as the medium between the ego and the unconscious, as does the
      <a href="/wiki/Persona_(psychology)" title="Persona (psychology)">
       persona
      </a>
      between the ego and the environment".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Alternatively, over-awareness of the anima or animus could provide a premature conclusion to the individuation process - "a kind of psychological short-circuit, to identify the animus at least provisionally with wholeness".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Instead of being "content with an intermediate position", the animus seeks to usurp "the self, with which the patient's animus identifies. This identification is a regular occurrence when the shadow, the dark side, has not been sufficiently realized".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
