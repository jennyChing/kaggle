        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://cdn0.mynaturalfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Type-1-diabetes-cure.jpg">
          <img alt="Is There a Type 1 Diabetes Cure?" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18736" height="650" src="http://cdn0.mynaturalfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Type-1-diabetes-cure.jpg" width="450"/>

        <p>
         You can read more about our sons
         <a href="http://www.mynaturalfamily.com/our-story/sons-type-diabetes-diagnosis-story/" title="type 1 diabetes story">
          Type 1 Diagnosis Story here
         </a>
         . We have been able to find little conclusive evidence about a Type 1 Diabetes cure. I believe there is because a natural treatment for diabetes is going to be different for everyone. There is not going to be a one-size-fits-all treatment for everyone. Why? Because the cause is different for everyone and each individual person is going to respond to the treatments differently. Why else? Because there is not going to be a single, simple treatment. A type 1 diabetics entire body is affected and all the different pieces of the puzzle need to be treated. This is not something you are going to be able to go to the local health food store and get a few pills and fix. Its not going to be cured by diet and exercise, although will help the patient feel better and help keep their numbers in range. Its going to take professional long-term help and its not going to be inexpensive or easy.
        </p>

        <p>
         Do I believe its possible? Yes, in theory I believe its possible. Do I believe we will be able to cure our son? Im hoping but Im also realistic that a full cure is very unlikely but totally worth trying because even if we dont achieve a total cure, we will be helping him feel better and helping his body be much healthier than it would have.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know Ill get a lot of negative comments out there from people who dont believe theres a type 1 diabetes cure that if there was, everyone would know about it and everyone
         <a href="http://cdn0.mynaturalfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/diabetes-help-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Is There a Type 1 Diabetes Cure?" class=" wp-image-18587 alignleft" height="310" src="http://cdn0.mynaturalfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/diabetes-help-2.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
         would do it. They think its a waste of time to try and might tell me to not get my hopes up and my sons hopes up. But Im not a good person to say those kind of things to. Weve managed to get my husband off all medication and insulin. Sure, he still has Type II diabetes, but its managed. I been able to make myself almost
         <a href="http://www.mynaturalfamily.com/symptoms/rheumatoid-arthritis/rheumatoid-arthritis-natural-treatment-yes-really-can-free-ra/" title="Rheumatoid Arthritis Natural Treatment  Yes, You Really Can Be Free of RA!">
          symptom-free from RA
         </a>
         , which is an auto-immune disease that there is supposedly not a cure for. I will not give up trying to fix our health problems. Our health is too precious not to try. Im not going to give up and let this disease run my childs life.
        </p>
        <p>
         I also think that right now is the perfect time to try. When a child is first diagnosed they go through the honeymoon phase. This generally lasts from several weeks to a year or two after diagnosis. This is where there pancreas still somewhat functions. This helps them so they dont need as much insulin. The biggest short-term worry for Diabetes is going too low. This can easily happen if you take too much insulin or dont eat all the food you were dosed for. In the honeymoon phase, your pancreas can help control these things. Honeymoon is also hard though because you never know when your pancreas is going to kick in and that could make you go low too. Once you are out of the honeymoon phase, your pancreas shuts down and nothing works as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         So my theory is that if were going to be successful, now is the time, while hes in the honeymoon phase and his pancreas is still somewhat functioning. My first goal is just to keep him in the honeymoon phase as long as possible. Our naturopathic doctor says hes been very successful doing this.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn0.mynaturalfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/diabetes-help-3.jpg">
          <img alt="Is There a Type 1 Diabetes Cure?" class=" wp-image-18590 alignright" height="360" src="http://cdn0.mynaturalfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/diabetes-help-3.jpg" width="225"/>
         </a>
         <strong>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           Here are some things weve learned as of January 28, 2014:
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         He used to be a picky eater but I didnt fully realize it until after all this. I really wanted to give him healthy foods but he doesnt like any natural foods. Yes, here I am Miss Natural herself and I can barely get my son to eat anything natural!
         <img alt=":(" class="wp-smiley" src="http://cdn0.mynaturalfamily.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/frownie.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         Well, at least two of my daughters love healthy food . . . Hes always had a diet high in protein. Thats just what he likes. Weve actually had to make him eat carbs since his Diabetes diagnosis so that he could have some insulin with each meal. Otherwise, if he only has protein for breakfast, his blood sugar goes way up.
        </p>
        <p>
         His tummy and head still hurt a lot and he throws up water a lot. At first we thought that was due to his blood sugar levels going up and down so dramatically. Now we suspect its from getting the Lantus (his insulin medicine) too late in the day. So were experimenting with giving him the shot earlier to see if it helps.
        </p>
        <p>
         Weve found that if he eats more than 40 carbs in a meal, his behavior and blood sugar are crazy a few hours later. So were trying to make his meals between 20-40 carbs, but most of them are close to 40. It seems to be working well. Its helping him learn to make choices. He can have all the sugary stuff that everyone else eats as long as he doesnt have too much. Its been really useful to show him that for 40 carbs you can get a few pieces of candy, a huge pile of veggies or a whole bowl of noodles or pasta. For reference, he is at 14 units of Lantus (a once-a-day, long-acting insulin, 1 unit of insulin for every 50 above 150 (bolus) and 1 unit of insulin for every 8 grams of carbs).
        </p>
        <p>
         School lunch has been an issue. Since its such a huge deal for him to eat all his food that he says he will, I need to watch him eat. I dont want to worry about other children taking or giving him food or him not eating all his lunch. The school by law has to provide someone to give him insulin shots but asking someone to sit there and watch him eat is way too much.

Plus, school lunch is expensive and not heavy on proteins. So what weve settled on is after my sister drops off my daughter from kindergarten, I start cooking his lunch. Then we all pile in the car, drive to the school, and he runs out and meets us. We test his blood, give him a shot, he eats his food and hes back in time for lunch recess. Its a pain for me but it seems like the least pain as possible. Its nice to see him and know hes ok and talk about his day for a few minutes.

My life can be very hectic and my young children like to eat all day. So with our new schedule, we all eat a good, big breakfast then have lunch on time, we all have one snack then we arent hungry until dinner. We TRY to have dinner earlier so we are hungry for a protein/carb bedtime snack. As long as we stick to the schedule, all the children arent as whiny and begging for food all day, especially right before dinner.

Its because other parents worry about their children in the night a lot and have to wake up their child and test in the night. However, our son starts feeling really bad and wakes up and tests his blood by himself. If he is low, he wakes us up but if he isnt, he doesnt. It gives me peace of mind knowing there is a good chance he will wake up so Im not so worried about him just not waking up in the morning. I do have to remind myself though that it only takes one time of not waking up. This makes him scared to go to sleep and is what I think is causing a lot of his sleep issues. The only way I get through it is with lots prayers. We are also going to need to get an insulin pump soon.
