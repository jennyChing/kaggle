        <p>
         Australian cruising has had arecord-breaking run, reaching its target of one million localcruise passengerssix years ahead of target to become the world's fastest-growing passenger market.
        </p>
        <p>
         More than one million Australians (4.2 per cent of the nationalpopulation)took a cruise holiday in 2014. This is the world's largest share per population and the first time any region has surpassed four per cent, according to Cruise Lines International Association's latest industry report.
        </p>

        <p>
         "There is no other cruising nation with this level of market penetration," CLIA Australasia chairman Gavin Smith said at the launch of the report in Sydneyon Tuesday.
        </p>
        <figure class="media media--photo">
         <img alt="Australia has beaten France and the UK become the world's fastest-growing passenger market." src="/content/dam/images/g/h/9/y/5/b/image.related.articleLeadwide.520x294.gh9u44.png/1433400896387.jpg" title=""/>
         <figcaption class="media__caption">
          Australia has beaten France and the UK become the world's fastest-growing passenger market.
          <cite>
           Photo: James Morgan
          </cite>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="350" mozallowfullscreen="mozallowfullscreen" msallowfullscreen="msallowfullscreen" oallowfullscreen="oallowfullscreen" src="http://cf.datawrapper.de/VufOC/2/" webkitallowfullscreen="webkitallowfullscreen" width="100%">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         "Australia remains firmly in the spotlight."
        </p>
        <p>
         The latest passenger numbers representa 20.4 per cent increase on 2013 and a six-fold increase over the past decade.
        </p>
        <p>
         France was the only other country to achieve double digit growth last year, while the UK/Irish, Spanish and Italian markets shrank.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="370" mozallowfullscreen="mozallowfullscreen" msallowfullscreen="msallowfullscreen" oallowfullscreen="oallowfullscreen" src="http://cf.datawrapper.de/bK9EK/3/" webkitallowfullscreen="webkitallowfullscreen" width="100%">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         Australians now account for 4.5 per cent of the world's 22 million cruise passengers, making the countrythe fourth largest source market.
        </p>
        <p>
         North America still dominates with 54.2 per cent of passengers, followed by Germany (8 per cent) and UK/Ireland (7.4 per cent).
        </p>
        <p>
         The South Pacific is the most popular cruise destination for Australians, with nearly 400,000 passengers (39 per cent) choosing to holiday inthis region last year.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="460" mozallowfullscreen="mozallowfullscreen" msallowfullscreen="msallowfullscreen" oallowfullscreen="oallowfullscreen" src="http://cf.datawrapper.de/tCbnO/2/" webkitallowfullscreen="webkitallowfullscreen" width="100%">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         The Caribbean experienced the strongest growth, attracting 65 per cent more Australians than in 2013.
        </p>
        <p>
         New Zealand was one of the few cruise destinations to see a fall in Australian passengers. Although it remains the third most popular destination, it attracted 10 per cent fewer passengersthan in 2013.
        </p>
        <p>
         Due to thestronggrowthof the Australian cruise market, the local industry has revised its 2020 target, doubling its target passenger number from one million passengers to two million, Smith said.
        </p>
        <p>
         This would require annual average growth of 12.5 per cent over the next six years.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" mozallowfullscreen="mozallowfullscreen" msallowfullscreen="msallowfullscreen" oallowfullscreen="oallowfullscreen" src="http://cf.datawrapper.de/nLzrY/1/" webkitallowfullscreen="webkitallowfullscreen" width="100%">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         Accessibility and choice were key drivers of growth, according to Smith.
        </p>
        <p>
         "Australians no longer have to travel overseas to experience first class luxury cruises," he said.
        </p>
        <p>
         "They have more choice than ever before in finding a cruise product for their economic status, age or stage in life."
        </p>
