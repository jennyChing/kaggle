       <div class="entry-content" style="clear: both;">
        <p>
         Money may not be able to buy you happiness, but according to science it can at least make you less sad.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats the conclusion a team of researchers led by psychologist Kostadin Kushlev came to while
         <a href="http://spp.sagepub.com/content/early/2015/01/08/1948550614568161?papetoc" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          studying the census data of 12,291 Americans
         </a>
         while testing if higher income rates really led to further happiness.
        </p>
        <p>
         It may seem like a paradox but Kushlev and the researchers explained that happiness and sadness, are distinct emotional states, rather than diametric opposites. In other words, just because youre not happy doesnt necessarily mean you are sad.
        </p>
        <p>
         Think of it this way: If you have the cash on hand to buy a new pair of kicks youre probably not going to feel happy or sad. If you really want a new pair of shoes but dont have the money to afford the purchase you will probably feel sad.
        </p>
        <p>
         While money itself cant make you happy, what you buy with it might.
        </p>
        <div class="con-img">
         <a href="http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/seriouslyscience/2014/08/26/makes-happier-anticipating-experience-purchase/#.VMf6hmR4rp4" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          According to another group of researchers
         </a>
         , purchasing experiences like vacations, tickets to a concert or finally taking that skydiving trip will make you exponentially more happy than spending your hard earned cash on material goods.
         <p>
          Wondering how much cash you need to buy all those experiences you want? Heres how much you need to make in each state in order to never think about your
          <a href="http://www.epi.org/multimedia/unequal-states-interactive/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           credit limit again
          </a>
          :
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.epi.org/publication/income-inequality-by-state-1917-to-2012/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="600" src="http://wp.newrepublic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/B8Sdv_1IgAA97sL-1.png" style="-webkit-user-select: none; cursor: zoom-in;" width="800"/>
          </a>
         </p>
