        <h1 class="entry-title">
         DIY Taco Seasoning!
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">
         <a href="http://sweetcountrychaos.com/2014/06/diy-taco-seasoning.html#comments">
          5 Replies
         </a>
        </div>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="5660926" data-link="http://sweetcountrychaos.com/2014/06/diy-taco-seasoning.html" data-summary="" data-title="DIY Taco Seasoning! ">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://glutenfreeforjen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/008-copy-2.jpg">
          <img alt="008 copy 2" class="aligncenter wp-image-3355 size-large" height="416" src="http://glutenfreeforjen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/008-copy-2-1024x682.jpg" width="625"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Have you ever started to make dinner and realize that you dont have one
         <em>
          key
         </em>
         ingredient? That happened to me tonight. I started dinner at my usual time. I was craving some tacos because it has been a few months since having them (summer time I cook outside). I opened up my spice cabinet only to find I was missing Taco Seasoning! Ugh! Frantic I went to my computer to find out what all is IN taco seasoning. Luckily I had
         <em>
          most
         </em>
         ingredients for the recipe I found but had to substitute some. Wow! It was WAY better then what I buy! The best part- because of the spices I buy: I knew 100% for sure there was NO MSG and NO GLUTEN!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-3347">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I am in love with this seasoning. It took seconds to mix up and once and then I just added it to my meat with some water and it was ready to go!
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want the original recipe you can check it out
         <a href="http://allrecipes.com/recipe/taco-seasoning-i/?scale=100&amp;ismetric=0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          HERE
         </a>
         . Below is what I did and will continue making!
        </p>
        <p>
         What you will need?
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 tbsp Chili Powder
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 tsp garlic powder
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 tsp onion flakes
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 tsp crushed red pepper
         </li>
         <li>
          1/4 tsp dried oregano
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 tsp paprika
         </li>
         <li>
          1 1/2 tsp ground cumin
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 tsp ground white pepper
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Mix all ingredients in a small bowl.
        </p>
        <p>
         Brown 1.5lbs of hamburger and add the above to it. Mix in 1/2-3/4 cup of water. Let simmer until water has cooked off.
        </p>
        <p>
         ~Enjoy!~
        </p>
