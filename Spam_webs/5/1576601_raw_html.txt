       <div class="large-12 columns pointable chapter-nav-next" style="display:none">
        <h2>
         Next Chapter &gt;
        </h2>
       </div>
       <div class="chapter-content">
        <p>
         Purists, look away. Im pretty sure that by now you should all know what the name Rocky Auto is synonymous with. This is one place that isnt afraid to fettle with authenticity, taking a modern approach to the sort of Japanese vintage rides we all know and respect. Watanabes latest project couldnt possibly be a better ambassador for this way of thought and its one we got a little glimpse of back in February at the
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/tag/nostalgic-2-days-2013/">
          Nostalgic 2 Days
         </a>
         . Back then the car wasnt even fitted with an engine yet, but it didnt take much imagination to figure out that with a carbon fiber body
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-22/" rel="attachment wp-att-158065">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-22.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-22"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
          it would be running something pretty special for propulsion. The car isnt actually completed yet, but Watanabe-san was kind enough to quickly fit the wheels and a bunch of other interior parts the night before my visit and get it down from the lift so that I could feature it. And Im glad he did because this is one car Ive really been looking forward to admiring in all its glory. It follows much the same recipe as the
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2012/05/old-meets-new-rocky-autos-rb30-powered-fairlady-z/">
          RB30-powered carbon S30
         </a>
         Rocky Auto built back in 2009, except what makes it very special is that you just dont see these wild sort of conversions done to Hakosukas. Seeing that the car was only ever sold in Japan, it tends to be a more revered Nissan classic  one that people havent dared to meddle with too much. Most are usually more than content with going for a 2000 GT-R look and modding the L-series motor that usually powers them, except of course Watanabe had different ideas.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-23/" rel="attachment wp-att-158067">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-23.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-23"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The exterior itself is mostly wrapped in carbon-look vinyl but countless bits and pieces have been custom made out of carbon fiber. So the whole bumper-less front fascia is carbon, as are the headlight covers, the hood and the fender flares. It all combines to give the car a sort of Works look but with the obvious modern feel carbon fiber introduces to the equation.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-02/" rel="attachment wp-att-158039">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158039" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-02.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-02"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         There are a lot of cool details that further spice up the looks like these prohibitively expensive dry-carbon fender mirrors from 09 Racing
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-05/" rel="attachment wp-att-158042">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-05.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-05"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
          or the C-pillar air outlets, again made in carbon.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-13/" rel="attachment wp-att-158051">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-13.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-13"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Thanks to adjustable Rocky Auto coilovers the car has been significantly lowered onto a set of 15 Work Meister CR01s, measuring 9.5 inches across at the front. The lack of appropriately sized tires needed to achieve the look that Watanabe wanted, led him to stumble upon a set of old 225/50VR15 Pirelli P7 Cinturato. As you can see from the picture above the aged rubber hasnt particularly liked the excessive stretching its been subjected to and has begun to split along the rim.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-16/" rel="attachment wp-att-158057">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-16.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-16"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The 11-inch wide rears are fitted with Pirelli P700s
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-06/" rel="attachment wp-att-158043">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-06.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-06"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
          in slightly wider 255/50VR15 size. The custom offset of the wheels has been perfectly selected for an optimal fit. The last thing remaining to be done is lower the rear suspension
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-04/" rel="attachment wp-att-158041">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158041" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-04.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-04"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
          pretty much as far as it will go. With such a unique exterior treatment for a KGC10, its hard not to get curious about the cars driveline
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-11/" rel="attachment wp-att-158049">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-11.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-11"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
          which of course hasnt got a single period correct component in sight. Recall our little
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/a-private-collection-rocky-autos-secret-garage/">
          tour of Watanabes new garage
         </a>
         where we came across about seven R32 Autech Versions? Well this is why he has so many  they are donor cars for this rather rare and special version of Nissans RB26.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-10/" rel="attachment wp-att-158048">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-10.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-10"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The limited edition Autech R32 was never regarded as quite the special car as other limited edition versions of the Skyline, but the engine that powered it was quite interesting. After grabbing RB26 shortblocks from the Nissan production line Autech would disassemble the engines, and get to work on them. They bored out the blocks to accommodate the 1mm oversized 87mm pistons that increase capacity to 2,627 cc and balanced the crank, con rods and flywheel to guarantee smooth, vibration-free operation all the way to the redline. Furthermore the head was polished for extra flow, helping to extract 220hp.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-08/" rel="attachment wp-att-158045">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158045" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-08.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-08"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The engines are easily recognizable thanks the S/S plaque on the intake plenum, playing homage to Shinchiro Sakurai, Princes engineer responsible for developing the very first Skyline and later the Prince R380 race car.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-09/" rel="attachment wp-att-158046">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158046" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-09.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-09"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         To liberate a few more horsepower, Rocky Auto added Tomei Powered camshafts with slightly more aggressive duration and lift, and fine-tuned the overlap thanks to HKS adjustable cam gears. A full stainless steel exhaust system, including the six-into-one headers, helps give the RB26 a more vintage raspy tone and slightly increases throttle response. The engine is fitted with a lightweight Toda Racing flywheel and sends drive to a Nissan five-speed transmission.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-07/" rel="attachment wp-att-158044">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-07.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-07"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Watanabe is still tuning the suspension and will probably end up running slightly more negative camber than what the top mounts of the coilovers allow.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-03/" rel="attachment wp-att-158040">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-03.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-03"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         To pay homage to the successful 2000 GT-R race cars that dominated endurance races in Fuji Speedway in the early to mid seventies, an external front-mounted oil cooler was fitted  quite a contrasting addition against all the black shiny carbon fiber.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-12/" rel="attachment wp-att-158050">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158050" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-12.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-12"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         An aluminum Flex radiator of generous dimensions makes sure the RB26DE never runs into issues even if its future owner decides to run the car hard on track.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-14/" rel="attachment wp-att-158053">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158053" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-14.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-14"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         It might be a sore sight to digest for those that appreciate only period-correct alterations, but its not like Watanabe cares. While he would never dream of messing around with a real S20-powered GT-R, these relatively affordable base model Skylines offer a fun platform upon which to go wild on and often create rather fast and exciting cars to drive.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-15/" rel="attachment wp-att-158055">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158055" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-15.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-15"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Just like at the front, the rear end is pretty wild, with carbon fiber replacing the whole section, topped off with a carbon trunk and spoiler.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-21/" rel="attachment wp-att-158063">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-21.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-21"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The interior is the final area of the car that needs to be finished off and the day before my visit they had to interrupt fitting the A/C system to get the car ready for the shoot, which is why there are a bunch of wires and looms hanging down under the dashboard.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-18/" rel="attachment wp-att-158060">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158060" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-18.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-18"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         And talking of the dashboard you may have noticed that its a one off piece, made out of aluminum
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-19/" rel="attachment wp-att-158061">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158061" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-19.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-19"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
          and wrapped in carbon-look vinyl to match the theme of the car. The Carbon-look AutoMeter tachometer and speedometer
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-20/" rel="attachment wp-att-158062">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-158062" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-20.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-20"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
          join the four centrally mounted auxiliary gauges, all neatly positioned for clear consultation.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/08/kyusha-dreams-rocky-autos-carbon-hakosuka/rocky-auto-carbon-hakosuka-17/" rel="attachment wp-att-158059">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-17.jpg" title="Rocky-Auto-Carbon-Hakosuka-17"/>

                <p>
                 While the fake carbon vinyl can be removed, the point I feel is that  Rocky Auto is a taste maker and they have a reputation to uphold as  being a thought leader in their realm of vintage car restorations. Their  work is always incredible but there's a responsibility in being a taste  maker and trend setter. I just would have loved to see them come out  and do it 110%. They didnt cut corners anywhere else, so why not at the  least overlay the body with real carbon instead of using fake carbon  sticker. They were so close to hitting a home run with this car but this  shortcut was really just unfortunate with the fake carbon vinyl.
                </p>
                <p>
                 I  still love Rocky Auto and their work. I just don't understand the route  then went with faking the carbon on a car that otherwise promotes usage  of carbon fiber.
                </p>

For every CR-01 there is a knackered and dangerous old P7 Cinturato that should have formed half a coffee table.
