     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Hidden Ivies: Thirty Colleges of Excellence
       </b>
      </i>
      is a college educational guide published in 2000. It concerns
      <a href="/wiki/College_admissions_in_the_United_States" title="College admissions in the United States">
       college admissions in the United States
      </a>
      . The authors define both the title of this book as well as their goals in writing it on page one in the following manner: "Our mission in writing this book for students and parents is to create greater awareness of the small, distinctive cluster of colleges and universities of excellence that are available to gifted college-bound students."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-greene.27s_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-greene.27s-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the introduction, the authors further explain their aim by referring specifically to "the group historically known as the 'Little Ivies' (including
      <a href="/wiki/Amherst_College" title="Amherst College">
       Amherst
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bowdoin_College" title="Bowdoin College">
       Bowdoin
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Middlebury_College" title="Middlebury College">
       Middlebury
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Swarthmore_College" title="Swarthmore College">
       Swarthmore
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Wesleyan_University" title="Wesleyan University">
       Wesleyan
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Williams_College" title="Williams College">
       Williams
      </a>
      )" which the authors say have "scaled the heights of prestige and selectivity and also turn away thousands of our best and brightest young men and women."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-greene.27s_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-greene.27s-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-green_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-green-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The second edition includes the assessment of all the institutions considered "
      <a href="/wiki/Little_Ivies" title="Little Ivies">
       Little Ivies
      </a>
      " except
      <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_College" title="Connecticut College">
       Connecticut College
      </a>
      . Connecticut College is referenced on three occasions in the book for its affiliation and student exchange program with other Little Ivies, and the college is included in Appendix II as another college of excellence.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In this book, the authors (using the same criteria often used to evaluate
      <a href="/wiki/Ivy_League" title="Ivy League">
       Ivy League
      </a>
      schools) discuss thirty American schools that are small in size and are either
      <a href="/wiki/Liberal_arts_colleges_in_the_United_States" title="Liberal arts colleges in the United States">
       liberal arts colleges
      </a>
      or universities that emulate them.
      <i>
       <b>
        The Hidden Ivies: 50 Top Colleges - From Amherst to Williams - That Rival the Ivy League
       </b>
      </i>
      , the second edition of the guide published in 2009, evaluates fifty "renowned academic institutions."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Nine of the fifty schools in the second edition are located in the
      <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">
       Midwestern United States
      </a>
      , 24 in the
      <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">
       Northeastern United States
      </a>
      , 11 in the
      <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">
       Southern United States
      </a>
      , and six in the
      <a href="/wiki/Western_United_States" title="Western United States">
       Western United States
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
       Overview
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hidden_Ivies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Hidden Ivies
      </i>
      discusses the college admissions process and attempts to evaluate 50 colleges in comparison to Ivy League colleges. The schools are examined based on academics, admissions process, financial aid, and student experiences. The book argues the importance of a liberal arts education and goes on to inquire about the qualities of Ivy League schools in general, and how such qualities apply to higher education.
     </p>
