       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Customer Experience: The Next Big Competitive Advantage
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-01-13T08:30:18+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 13, 2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.steamfeed.com/author/annereuss/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Anne Reuss
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.steamfeed.com/customer-experience-the-next-big-competitive-advantage/#comments">
           15 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Lets discuss customer experience (CX). According to this post last week,
         <a href="http://www.customerthink.com/blog/the_biggest_danger_to_cx_is_simply_this_hype">
          CX is also in danger due tohype
         </a>
         . So Im reporting to duty to combat this! Plus, yesterdays community manager hangout about
         <a href="http://mycmgr.com/magical-customer-experience/">
          magical customer experiences
         </a>
         was encouraging.
        </p>
        <p>
         <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3926" style="width: 438px;">
          <a href="http://www.steamfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/medium_60492897221.jpg">
           <img alt="customer experience" class="size-full wp-image-3926" height="640" src="http://www.steamfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/medium_60492897221.jpg" title="medium_6049289722" width="438"/>
          </a>
          <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
           photo credit:
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/mordefroid/6049289722/">
            mordefroid
           </a>
           via
           <a href="http://photopin.com">
            photopin
           </a>
           <a href="http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nd/2.0/">
            cc
           </a>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         This is not a fancy term for customer service or a fad. Its a sleeper (not widely recognized  like the NIU Huskies football team in the early season). It influences business growth and results through increased conversions, lead generation, brand advocates and more. As
         <a href="https://twitter.com/jeanniecw">
          Jeannie Walters
         </a>
         pointed out yesterday during the hangout, customer service is reactive and customer experience is proactive. You make the first move.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im talking about every flipping interaction your potential leads, customers or buddies checking out your website (or store) experiences along the customer journey relevant to you and your business. When youre there or not. You want to mastersatisfaction plus some before, during and after the product/service lifecycle. Its a heavy topic but to give you an idea, here are some touchpoints to consider:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Amount of time it takes you to respond to feedback and questions from customers. Ensure a two way street for communication, especially in real time social media. Are youenthusiasticabout their happiness factor and eliminating any painful experiences?
         </li>
         <li>
          How easy is it to discover or use the contact form, checkout, download your content offerings or subscribe? If its a long and clicky process, fix it.
         </li>
         <li>
          Do you say thank you to your customers and brand advocates?Surprisethem once in a while? Keep the romance alive!
         </li>
         <li>
          Sometimes there are breakups. No need topiss them off by ignoring their request to cancel. But you could be kind and clever (i.e. I once saw a webpage asking me to select my reason why I wanted to leave underneath the line An angel just lost its wings today. Awww.
         </li>
         <li>
          Satisfy the customers needs beyond the product. (Dig
          <a href="http://www.hubspot.com/">
           Hubspot
          </a>
          and all their free ebooks beyond their inbound marketing software).
         </li>
         <li>
          Design of your 404 Error Message  Notice how some make you laugh? Or scold you. Like, shame on you for not figuring it out.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         It may strike you Im talking a bit about
         <a href="http://360connext.com/user-experience-v-customer-experience/">
          user experience instead of customer experience
         </a>
         ..andthats not completely wrong. But usability has to mirrorthe overall experience. In case some people need reminding. humans still dont have an app working on the computer for them!
        </p>
        <h2>
         The experience is as essential as the product
        </h2>
        <p>
         Heres a really great example I came across this week (I am dying to go in geek review mode but it will have to wait).
        </p>
        <p>
         So the G+ community
         <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/communities/111411798689384783793?cfem=1">
          oneQube beta testers
         </a>
         wasbirthed.I was so impressed with what
         <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/112467373963828278655/posts?cfem=1">
          Robert Moore
         </a>
         did when I joined. He announced
         <i>
          Starting today, I am going to post a oneQube Search of the Day challenge to get you diving into your followers with different search queries. Todays challenge is to see how many of your followers refer bacon!
         </i>
         See what he did there? Instead of setting up a place to only discuss bugs, share tips and gather feature requests he went the extra mile to keep us motivated to master oneQube. Id probably set aside a chunk of time in the week to play with it instead of effectively studying oneQube. And here I am writing about it because it left such an impression.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats gold.
        </p>
        <p>
         Be your customers friend. Were all in this together FTW.
        </p>
