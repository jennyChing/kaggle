        <div class="panel border_c">

         <div class="block-news" style="margin-left:17px;">
          <h2 class="subtitle">
           Properly Restart a Windows 8, 7, Vista, or XP Computer
          </h2>
          <p class="separator">
           <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-zqGh9XVn6Ls/VChfuURPMiI/AAAAAAAAAXU/dAljczwTbqk/s1600/fst054028-copy.jpg">
            <img alt="" border="0" data-orig-src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-zqGh9XVn6Ls/VChfuURPMiI/AAAAAAAAAXU/dAljczwTbqk/s1600/fst054028-copy.jpg" height="266" src="https://images-blogger-opensocial.googleusercontent.com/gadgets/proxy?url=http%3A%2F%2F4.bp.blogspot.com%2F-zqGh9XVn6Ls%2FVChfuURPMiI%2FAAAAAAAAAXU%2FdAljczwTbqk%2Fs1600%2Ffst054028-copy.jpg&amp;container=blogger&amp;gadget=a&amp;rewriteMime=image%2F*" width="400"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2 class="subtitle">
          </h2>
          <div class="firstBlock">
           <p>
            Did you know that theres a
            <em>
             right
            </em>
            way, and several
            <em>
             wrong
            </em>
            ways, to restart a computer? Its not an ethical dilemma  one method ensures that problems dont happen and a myriad of others are risky, at best.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           You certainly could restart your computer by powering it off and on, swapping out the AC power or battery, or hitting the reset button, but each of those methods is a bit of a surprise to your computers operating system.
          </p>
          <p>
           The result of that surprise could be nothing if youre lucky, but more likely it could cause issues from file corruption up to the very serious problem of a computer that wont even start!
          </p>
          <h3>
           How Do I Restart My Computer?
          </h3>
          <p>
           To safely restart a Windows computer, you can usually tap or click on the
           <em>
            Start
           </em>
           button and then choose the
           <em>
            Restart
           </em>
           option.
          </p>
          <p>
           As strange as it may sound, the exact method of restarting differs quite a bit between some versions of Windows.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Note:
           </strong>
           Its not just Windows computers that should but restarted in a certain way to avoid causing issues.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Important:
           </strong>
           Be very careful if you see the option to
           <em>
            reset
           </em>
           something. Restarting, also known as rebooting, is also sometimes called
           <em>
            resetting
           </em>
           . However, the term
           <em>
            resetting
           </em>
           is also often used synonymously with a factory reset, meaning a complete wipe-and-reinstall of a system, something very different than a restart and not something you want to take lightly.
          </p>
