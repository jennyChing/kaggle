              Google Has A Street View Camel
             </a>

             In an effort to get "more authentic"
             <a href="https://www.google.com/maps/@23.1480433,53.7327198,3a,75y,315.44h,83.74t/data=!3m5!1e1!3m3!1sfMKtEa9ywi1HQ5oD5_WLqg!2e0!3e5" target="_blank">
              Street View images of Abu Dahbi's Liwa Desert
             </a>
             (and these great press photos) Google strapped their Trekker camera to a 10-year-old camel named Raffia and dealt with the stink, spit, and bad attitude so you don't have to!
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="5f68780b8bb9deacc45e8f383e2c8970">
             <span class="clear-both flex-video widescreen">

            <p data-textannotation-id="9133491e5b4ecb62dc885afd6a1b0c79">
             The idea behind the exercise is supposedly to bring some tourist attention to this arid sea of beautiful nothingness. Joyce Baz, Google's Middle East and North Africa spokewoman says "Twenty to 30 per cent of the views come from outside the country. We think this really helps people check these locations out before they come to visit."
            </p>
