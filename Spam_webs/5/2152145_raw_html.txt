            <h2 class="title">
             <a class="journal-entry-navigation-current" href="/blog/2011/7/22/quotel.html">
              Quotel
             </a>
            </h2>
            <div class="journal-entry-tag journal-entry-tag-post-title">
             <span class="posted-on">
              Posted on
              <img alt="Date" class="inline-icon date-icon" rel="dark" src="/universal/images/transparent.png" title="Date"/>
              Fri, 22 Jul 2011
             </span>
             <span class="posted-by">

              <a href="/blog/author/kim">
               <img alt="Author" class="inline-icon user-registered-icon" rel="dark" src="/universal/images/transparent.png" title="Author"/>
               kim

             <span class="post-comments">

            <div class="body">
             <p>
              Back in November I
              <a href="http://www.desiretoinspire.net/blog/2010/11/3/modelina.html">
               blogged
              </a>
              the office of
              <a href="http://modelina-architekci.com">
               mode:lina
              </a>
              , an architecture firm based in Poland. They emailed us recently with one of their latest projects - an apartment designed for guests visiting the Pozna International Fair. It's called the QUOTEL (combination of the words "quote" and "hotel").
              <em>
               This specific customer defines the nature of this apartment - at the crossroads of  hotel features and relaxed homely atmosphere. The challenge was to use easily replaceable parts (here:IKEA) and create a space that definitely stands out among other similar offers on the market. Designers faithful to the principle of crossing-over, combined catalogue elements with custom-made ones to create an affordable and durable design giving the impression of tailored interior.
              </em>
              I dig the modern minimalism with great use of Ikea products, colour that adds some life, and the phrases on the walls are a fun, cheap and at times cheeky form of artwork. (Oh, and those floors!!)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              ***Totally off topic: My parents, twin sister and I are having a garage sale tomorow out in Cumberland (parents' house - they have the longest driveway, from 8am-noonish) so you can stop at the Cumberland Market, pick up some fruits and veggies, then drive up the road about 100 feet and come check out our junk!!
