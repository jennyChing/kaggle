     <h1>
      Getting around Chicago in 30 minutes or less
     </h1>
     <div class="meta-bar">
      Posted to
      <a href="http://flowingdata.com/category/visualization/mapping/" rel="category tag">
       Mapping
      </a>
      | Tags:
      <a href="http://flowingdata.com/tag/chicago/" rel="tag">
       Chicago
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://flowingdata.com/tag/interactive/" rel="tag">
       interactive
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="archive-featured-image" style="margin-top:18px">
      <img alt="Get there inThirty" class="attachment-medium wp-post-image" src="http://i0.wp.com/flowingdata.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/Get-there-inThirty.png?fit=620%2C9999"/>
     </div>
     <div class="entry">
      <p>
       When you're deciding on a place to live in a new place, it's always good to know what's in the area. After all, a house close to conveniences and things to do is usually more desirable than a house that is out of the way.
       <a href="http://inthirty.com/">
        InThirty
       </a>
       , by Brian Lange of
       <a href="http://datascopeanalytics.com/">
        Datascope Analytics
       </a>
       , provides some insight, starting with Chicago. Enter an address and see what libraries and parks (restaurants to come) are within 30 minutes of walking, biking, or public transit.
      </p>
      <p>
       It's stil fairly basic in what it does. You just get markers on the map for places that are within 30 minutes. The heat map on the layer underneath only changes as you change mode of transportation or between libraries and parks. Colors relative to your entered address could be more useful.
      </p>
      <p>
       Still though, like with
       <a href="http://flowingdata.com/2011/06/01/find-everywhere-you-can-go-in-15-minutes-or-less/">
        Mapnificent
       </a>
       , I like the idea of searching for things by travel time over distance. If a couple of places are 10 miles versus 20 miles away, I don't really care, if it takes the same amount of time to get there.
      </p>
      <p>
       [
       <a href="http://inthirty.com/">
        inThirty
       </a>
       | Thanks, Bryan]
      </p>
