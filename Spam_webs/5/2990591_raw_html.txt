      <h2>
       Caramel Peanut Butter Cookie Bars
      </h2>
      <div class="postmeta">
       <em>
        posted by
       </em>
       Dorothy Kern
       <em>
        under
       </em>
       <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/brownies/" rel="category tag">
        brownies
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/cookies/" rel="category tag">
        cookies
       </a>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style ">
      <a class="addthis_button_tweet" tw:count="horizontal" tw:via="crazyforcrust">
      </a>
      <a class="addthis_button_facebook_like" fb:like:layout="button_count" style="margin-right:15px;">
      </a>
      <a class="addthis_button_stumbleupon_badge" style="margin-right:10px;">
      </a>
      <a href="javascript:void((function(){var%20e=document.createElement('script');e.setAttribute('type','text/javascript');e.setAttribute('charset','UTF-8');e.setAttribute('src','http://assets.pinterest.com/js/pinmarklet.js?r='+Math.random()*99999999);document.body.appendChild(e)})());">
       <img alt="Pin It" onclick="doPinIt();" src="/images/pinit.png" style="margin: 0 0 0 10px;"/>

     <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
     <p>
      So you know all those caramel brownie recipes you see all over the place? These are like those, but instead of being brownies, theyre peanut butter cookie bars.
     </p>
     <p>
      Yeah, I love me too.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Caramel Peanut Butter Cookie Bars: soft caramel layered between layers of peanut butter cookie bars!" class="aligncenter wp-image-9573 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Caramel-Peanut-Butter-Cookie-Bars-6-of-6w.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      I have a confession to make: Im a helicopter mom. And Im not proud of it, not one little bit.
     </p>
     <p>
      If youre not familiar with the term helicopter mom it describes
      <em>
       those
      </em>
      parents (typically the mom, but often the dad or both parents). You know  the ones that are uber involved with every little thing in their childs life? Always at school, always solving their problems, always stunting their growth into capable adult humans who can be on their own, that sort of thing.
     </p>
     <p>
      I try really, really hard not to hover. I actually pride myself on not being at school at recess and lunch to make sure the girl drama doesnt get out of hand (yes, there are moms who do that). I tell her to work it out and I try and roll play with her. But then she cries and sobs and wails and I email the teacher.
     </p>
     <p>
      Her poor teacher. Ive already talked to Jordans 4th grade teacher more than I ever did her 3rd grade one. I tell myself thats because (1) I know her better, (2) Im not running and online blog conference and writing a book so Im actually present this year, and (3) I want Jordan to succeed, have friends, be liked, and not be bothered by 9 year old boys who lick necklaces and rub them all over her arms.
     </p>
     <p>
      But Im hovering. And I know I need to stop. 4th grade is a hard year  they have to learn to exist on their own.And thats hard for me because Ive been doing
      <em>
       all-the-freaking-things
      </em>
      for 9 years. Moms  you know what I mean right? Its time for me to let go a little. And that makes me want to cry and it makes me want comfort food.
     </p>
     <p>
      My comfort food is peanut butter. And caramel. Last week I needed some comfort dessert, and these bars were IT. Caramel Peanut Butter Cookie Bars  theres not much more comforting than that!
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Caramel Peanut Butter Cookie Bars: soft caramel layered between layers of peanut butter cookie bars!" class="aligncenter wp-image-9572 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Caramel-Peanut-Butter-Cookie-Bars-4-of-6.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Youve seen the brownie versions of this bar all over the place. A few years back a chocolate chip cookie one went around too. Ive tried to make both those versions before and its never worked. The caramel either bubbles out and burns or cooks into the bottom layer or gets so hard eating the bars requires dental work.
     </p>
     <p>
      But these? Theyre perfect. I found the perfect caramel recipe in a new cookbook I love, which Ill be formally reviewing in a few weeks. Its called
      <em>
       Extreme Brownies
      </em>
      , by Connie Weis. The caramel in the center is perfect  creamy, smooth, and it stays soft.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span id="more-9571">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Because brownie and chocolate chip cookie have been done before I thought Id do something different: peanut butter cookie bars! Didnt you know that
      <strong>
       <span style="color: #800000;">
        peanut butter + caramel
       </span>
      </strong>
      were the bees knees?
     </p>
     <p>
      A few notes about the recipe:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <span style="color: #800080;">
        Unwrap all the caramels
       </span>
       before you make the cookie dough.
      </li>
      <li>
       Start the caramel while the base cookie layer is baking. Its hard to time it all but you want the caramel to be done right about the time the base layer is done so you can
       <span style="color: #800080;">
        pour hot caramel on hot cookie bars
       </span>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Make sure to
       <span style="color: #800080;">
        keep the caramel layer in from the edges of the bars
       </span>
       , about 1/4-1/2. You want to try and seal the cookie layers together and if the caramel touches the pan itll bubble and stick.
      </li>
      <li>
       If you just drop pieces of cookie dough on top the caramel, its too hard to spread them without ruining the bottom layer.
       <span style="color: #800080;">
        Heres the trick:
       </span>
       pull of pieces of dough and flatten them between your hands, then lay them on the caramel. All the pieces dont have to completely seal to each other, but youll get more even coverage that way.
      </li>
      <li>
       Try not to eat these hot straight out of the oven. Youll burn your mouth. Its hard, but be patient and wait until they cool a bit.
       <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png"/>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      These are my most favorite bars yet. I couldnt keep away from them! Theyre perfect for when you need a stick-to-your-love-handles comfort food dessert.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Caramel Peanut Butter Cookie Bars: soft caramel layered between layers of peanut butter cookie bars!" class="aligncenter wp-image-9574 size-full" height="1388" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Caramel-Stuffed-Peanut-Butter-Cookie-Bars.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Plus, I must burn a lot of calories with all that helicoptering. Or so I tell myself anyway
     </p>
     <div class="recipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
      <meta content="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/2014/09/caramel-peanut-butter-cookie-bars/" itemprop="url"/>
      <div class="printbutton">
       <a class="icon-print" href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/2014/09/caramel-peanut-butter-cookie-bars/print/" title="Print Recipe">

      <h2 itemprop="name">
       Caramel Peanut Butter Cookie Bars
      </h2>
      <div class="time">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Yield:
        </strong>
        <span itemprop="recipeYield">
         24 Bars
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="summary" itemprop="description">
       <p>
        Peanut butter cookies sandwiched with gooey caramel are the best dessert! These are easy to make and the caramel stays soft for chewing!
       </p>
      </div>
      <img align="right" class="photo nopin" height="125" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Caramel-Peanut-Butter-Cookie-Bars-6-of-6w-200x200.jpg" width="125"/>
      <h3 id="ingredients">
       Ingredients:
      </h3>
      <div class="ingredients">
       <h4>
        For the Peanut Butter Cookie Bars:
       </h4>
       <ul>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         3/4 cup peanut butter
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         3/4 cup packed brown sugar
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/4 cup granulated sugar
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 large egg
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1teaspoon vanilla
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 tablespoon milk
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/4 teaspoon salt
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/2 teaspoon baking soda
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 3/4 cups all purpose flour
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h4>
        For the Caramel
       </h4>
       <ul>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/3 cup + 1 teaspoon heavy whipping cream
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 tablespoons unsalted butter
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 (11 ounce) bag Kraft Caramels (around 38-40), unwrapped
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 teaspoon vanilla extract
        </li>
       </ul>
      </div>
      <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
       Directions:
      </h3>
      <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
       <ol>
        <li>
         Preheat oven to 350F. Line a 9x13 pan with foil and spray with cooking spray. The foil is needed here because the caramel can get sticky!
        </li>
        <li>
         Cream butter, peanut butter, and both sugars in the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment. Add egg, vanilla, milk, and salt and baking soda. Mix until combined. Slowly add flour and mix until dough comes together.
         <br/>
         Press half of dough into the bottom of the prepared pan. The dough will be sticky - spray your hands with cooking spray first. It will be a very thin layer of dough!
        </li>
        <li>
         Bake the bottom layer of cookie dough for 10 minutes.
        </li>
        <li>
         While the dough is baking, make the caramel.
        </li>
        <li>
         Place the heavy whipping cream and butter in a heavy bottomed 2Qt sauce pan over medium-low heat. Add unwrapped caramels one at a time. Stir often until the caramel is melted and smooth. Sift the flour into the caramel (if you have a small mesh strainer, just place the flour in it and tap the side of the strainer until all the flour falls through). Stir until the flour is incorporated. Turn heat all the way to low until the bottom layer is done cooking, stirring often so it doesnt burn. Right before taking the cookie out of the oven, turn off the heat and stir in the vanilla.
        </li>
        <li>
         After 10 minutes, remove the bottom layer from the oven and pour the caramel over the top. Try to leave a 1/2 border around the cookie base. If the caramel touches the edges of the pan it will boil and stick.
        </li>
        <li>
         Break off pieces of the remaining dough and flatten between your hands. Lay on the top of caramel. Continue until the entire pan is covered, pressing the edges to seal them together. A little of the caramel may peek through, thats okay.
        </li>
        <li>
         Bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, until the bars start to get golden on the top. Cool completely before slicing. Store in an airtight container at room temperature. Do not refrigerate or caramel will get too hard to eat.
        </li>
       </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="source">
       <p>
        Caramel recipe From Extreme Brownies: 50 Recipes for the Most Over-the-Top Treats Ever by Connie Weis/Andrews McMeel Publishing, 2014
       </p>
