     <div class="article-block">
      <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 183px">
       <img alt="" class=" " height="152" src="http://pinellasrepublican.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Guju-Headshot-2.jpg" style="margin: 10px;" width="173"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Michael J. Guju, Esq., PCREC Chairman
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Its difficult to be both part of a story and also to report on one, but I will try.
      </p>
      <p>
       About a month ago, Rev. Nancy Davis, a member of the Pinellas County Republican Executive Committee (PCREC), had called to ask me to recommend someone to speak at the PCREC on the topic of Islamic terrorism. I immediately thought of an internationally known expert whom I have heard speak before  Dr. Jonathan Matusitz.
      </p>
      <p>
       I sent his bio and credentials to Rev. Nancy Davis, who forwarded them to the PCREC:
      </p>
      <p>
       Dr. Matusitz earned his doctorate in Communications from the University of Oklahoma in 2006. He is currentlyan
       <a href="http://communication.cos.ucf.edu/person/jonathan-matusitz/" target="_blank">
        associate professor
       </a>
       in the Nicholson School of Communication at the University of Central Florida (UCF). He studies globalization, culture, terrorism and health communication. On top of having 95 academic publications and over 100 conference presentations, he taught at a NATO-affiliated military base in Belgium in 2010. One of his particular emphases is the Islamic threat to America and to the world. Originally from Belgium himself, he immigrated to the United States in 2000. In 2012, he was honored with a prestigious teaching award by the College of Science at UCF.
      </p>
      <p>
       After the PCREC Chairman, Michael Guju, vets Dr. Matusitz, invitations are sent out to all PCREC members, listing the speech on Radical Islam in America as part of the June 10th event, during the Education Forum that usually precedes monthly PCREC meetings. See the flyer
       <a href="http://watchdogwire.com/florida/2013/06/09/republican-party-of-pinellas-county-stifles-free-speech/">
        here
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 179px">
       <img alt="" class=" " height="169" src="http://www.tampabay.com/resources/images/dti/rendered/2013/05/nal_chrislatvala051213_10754910_8col.jpg" style="margin: 10px;" width="169"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Chris Latvala, son of Republican state Sen. Jack Latvala, said on May 8 that he is considering running for FL House District 67 in 2014.
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       But four days before Dr. Matusitzs appearance, objections arise. Chris Latvala,son of State
       <a href="http://www.flsenate.gov/Senators/s20" target="_blank">
        Sen. Jack Latvala
       </a>
       (R, S-20) and a likely Republican candidate for the State House in 2014, publicly announces his displeasure in a
       <a href="https://www.facebook.com/chris.latvala/posts/10200467976555537">
        Facebook post
       </a>
       that according to grassroots activists oozes with calculated I am better than you piousness:
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <em>
        June 6 at 4:22pm: Im disappointed that the PCREC will be having a speaker Monday night talk about The Islamic Threat to America. I am a born again Christian saved by grace alone, through christ alone, but a local county republican meeting should not be an avenue to bash a religion that is practiced by many in Pinellas County including fellow Republicans. As a party our focus should be trying to win the vote of every voter regardless of their religious beliefs.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       Less than three hours later on the same day (June 6 at 7:09 p.m.), this quote becomes a centerpiece of a
       <a href="http://www.tampabay.com/blogs/baybuzz/pinellas-republicans-criticized-for-inviting-anti-islam-speaker/2125295">
        <i>
         Tampa Bay Times
        </i>
        article
       </a>
       <em>
        ,
       </em>
       chastising the Republicans for inviting a speaker who has been making the rounds within Floridas tea party chapters, insinuating that such appearances somehow disqualify Dr. Matusitz as a credible speaker. But Dr. Matusitz also speaks at Florida Republican Womens Clubs. What would
       <em>
        Tampa Bay Times
       </em>
       insinuate about that?
      </p>
      <p>
       Already on the following morning, June 7, CAIR (Council On American-Islamic Relations  an activist organization with ties to Muslim Brotherhood and an
       <a href="http://www.investigativeproject.org/1854/doj-cairs-unindicted-co-conspirator-status-legit">
        unindicted co-conspirator in a terrorist-funding trial
       </a>
       ) sends out an Action Alert email blast with the same pious quote and a link to the
       <i>
        Tampa Bay Times
       </i>
       story under the headline Fla. RepublicansCriticized for Inviting Anti-Islam Speaker.
      </p>
      <p>
       The next morning, June 8, the PCREC emails an announcement that Dr. Matusitzs speech has been postponed. In common speak, canceled.
      </p>
      <p>
       Immediately, Chris Latvala writes a
       <a href="http://www.facebook.com/chris.latvala/posts/10200476589530856" target="_blank">
        follow-up Facebook post
       </a>
       :
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <em>
        June 8 at 11:34am: I am very pleased that the PCREC Seminar/Educational Forum on The Islamic Threat to America that was to take place before this Mondays meeting has been postponed.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       This is how careers and reputations of decent citizens are tarnished by opportunists scoring politically correct points. Many courageous, principled educators and critics of radical Islam have already become casualties in the war of ideas with Islamism.
      </p>
      <p>
       Stephen Coughlin, the Pentagon specialist on Islamic law and Islamist extremism,
       <a href="http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2008/jan/4/inside-the-ring-8-34302/?page=all">
        has been fired
       </a>
       from his position on the militarys Joint Staff following a report by an Islamic activist, which described him as a Christian zealot and an extremist.
      </p>
      <p>
       Robert Spencer, the author of twelve books, including two
       <em>
        New York Times
       </em>
       bestsellers, who has led seminars on Islam and jihad for the United States Central Command, United States Army Command and General Staff College, the U.S. Armys Asymmetric Warfare Group, the FBI, the Joint Terrorism Task Force, and the U.S. intelligence community, had his
       <a href="http://www.catholicculture.org/news/headlines/index.cfm?storyid=16953">
        appearances canceled
       </a>
       under pressure from Islamic groups and is now classified by the Southern Poverty Law Center as a 
       <a href="http://www.islamophobiatoday.com/2013/02/06/remove-hate-group-leader-robert-spencer-as-catholic-deacon/">
        hate group leader
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        The cancellation of Dr. Matusitzs speech creates an eerie precedent for a similarly unfair marginalization.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       As the most recent example of Islamic pressure, CAIR just
       <a href="http://blogs.browardpalmbeach.com/pulp/2013/06/fau_roxanna_trinka_anti_muslim_cair.php">
        issued a demand
       </a>
       for Florida Atlantic University (FAU) to drop an advisor to the College of Engineering, who has expressed and supported extremist anti-Muslim views on her Facebook page. Fearing the consequences of CAIRs witch hunt, FAU submissively
       <a href="http://blogs.browardpalmbeach.com/pulp/2013/06/fau_roxanna_trinka_anti_muslim_cair.php">
        complied
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_14449" style="width: 279px">
       <a href="http://watchdogwire.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/florida/files/2013/06/Larissa_Group_of_Activists_and-_Speaker.jpg">
        <img alt="Larissa_Group_of_Activists_and _Speaker" class=" wp-image-14449 " height="190" src="http://watchdogwire.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/florida/files/2013/06/Larissa_Group_of_Activists_and-_Speaker.jpg" style="margin: 10px;" width="269"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Citizen activists at the Golden Corral presentation
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Thats usually how these things play out, and the atmosphere of terror and panic before a potential Islamic backlash grows thicker with every such incident. If terrorism is defined as the systematic use of terror, especially as a means of coercion, to achieve the state of fear and submission, how would one define CAIRs relentless pressure and the reaction to it on the part of Americas institutions?
      </p>
      <p>
       But hey, Chris Latvala got his grandstanding quote in the
       <em>
        Tampa Bay Times
       </em>
       . What a way to kick off a campaign run for the State House.
      </p>
      <p>
       Given CAIRs history of monitoring all suspicious activities and pressuring organizations to cancel speakers who expose the tactics and scope of penetration of radical Islam in the West, it was safe to assume that PCREC had received at least a few calls from offended Muslims. However, my inquiries at PCRECs main office confirmed that no such activity took place. The manager would only comment that the speech was canceled because some members were offended by the subject matter.
      </p>
      <p>
       According to my inside sources, the pressure on the new PCREC Chairman, Michael Guju, came mostly from State Senator Jack Latvala and his son, Chris.
      </p>
      <p>
       It appears that Florida Republicans no longer even need CAIRs help to censor information on radical Islam. They have learned to do it themselves, preemptively.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the words of one PCREC member, who wished to remain anonymous, Michael Guju is a really nice guy. Hes too nice.He can be easily bullied. Since when does the son of a Senator call the shots at PCREC?
      </p>
      <p>
       To quote
       <a href="http://www.politicalislam.com/" target="_blank">
        Dr. Bill Warner
       </a>
       , Director of the Center for the Study of Political Islam:
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <em>
        Being nicer may not be the way to win a civilizational war. Our society has become so wimpy and politically correct that there is no longer any righteous anger. We have become a nation of pleasant liars who are never angered at societys outrages. We are ever so polite to those who destroy our civilization. We are a nation of zombies, but polite zombies. There have been over 20,000 jihad attacks since September 11, 2001, but you dont want to mention that since someone might become uncomfortable or be offended.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       But this is where the story turns more optimistic.
      </p>
      <p>
       Jonathan Matusitz did deliver his speech on radical Islam that night  to a much larger audience  due to the efforts of many local volunteers.
      </p>
      <p>
       On June 8th, shortly after the PCRECs announced cancellation, Dr. Matusitz notified me of the new site of his presentation at the Golden Corral Restaurant, ten minutes down the road from the Republican event.
      </p>
      <p>
       Immediately, I sent out my own Action Alert to area activists, giving the new location and asking everyone to support free speech by showing up and bringing friends. In addition, I reported this debacle in
       <a href="http://watchdogwire.com/florida/2013/06/09/republican-party-of-pinellas-county-stifles-free-speech/">
        WatchDogWire-Florida
       </a>
       . Leaders of local activist groups shared my Watchdog Wire post with their members, urging them to go to the Golden Corral. Word spread like wildfire.
      </p>
      <p>
       In addition, I printed out a bunch of flyers with the new address and brought them to the PCREC meeting at the Feather Sound Country Club, to hand them out to those who may not have known about the cancellation. A few first-time PCREC attendees, to whom I spoke, turned right around and went to the Golden Corral.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_14445" style="width: 166px">
       <a href="http://watchdogwire.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/florida/files/2013/06/NANCY-WITH-FREEDOM-SIGN.jpg">
        <img alt="NANCY WITH FREEDOM SIGN" class=" wp-image-14445 " height="207" src="http://watchdogwire.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/florida/files/2013/06/NANCY-WITH-FREEDOM-SIGN.jpg" style="margin: 10px;" width="156"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Rev. Nancy Davis
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Rev. Nancy Davis, a member of the PCREC to whom I had originally recommended Dr. Matusitz as a speaker, came to the meeting at the Country Club with a handmade sign that read: I am for freedom of speech.
      </p>
      <p>
       Pastor Anthony McDaniel of
       <a href="http://www.abundantlifeministries.cc/" target="_blank">
        Abundant Life Ministries
       </a>
       , who was initially invited to lead the invocation at the PCREC, eventually declined the invitation and chose instead to attend Dr. Matusitzs speech, where he led the invocation.After learning about the cancellation, I
       <i>
        had
       </i>
       to be here to give my support to Jonathan, he told me later.
      </p>
      <p>
       Having arrived to the Republican meeting early, I walked into the empty ballroom and placed a flier on each empty chair in the room, plus one on the podium.
      </p>
      <p>
       Of course, some of those walking through the country clubs front door were there to eat dinner, enjoy cocktails, and rub elbows with the influential; they didnt have time for me. As it turned out, they were a minority.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the final count, only 45 people came to listen to the replacement speaker, whose topic was Know Your Government. The rest of the prospective attendees, apparently, already knew their government well enough to go to the Golden Corral.
      </p>
      <p>
       In contrast, a much smaller room where Dr. Matusitz gave his speech was overflowing with people, some of whom couldnt find a place to sit and remained outside in a show of support for the speaker. The restaurants employees counted 96 people in the audience  almost twice the size of the PCREC meeting.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_14448" style="width: 298px">
       <a href="http://watchdogwire.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/florida/files/2013/06/Sully_Crowd_Golden_Corral.jpeg">
        <img alt="Sully_Crowd_Golden_Corral" class=" wp-image-14448 " height="176" src="http://watchdogwire.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/florida/files/2013/06/Sully_Crowd_Golden_Corral.jpeg" style="margin: 10px;" width="288"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Overflow crowd at Dr. Matusitz presentation at the Golden Corral
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Was Dr. Matusitzs speech controversial? Not according to Lynn, an attendee who had originally planned to go to the PCREC for the first time. This is exactly the kind of information Americans need to know, she said after the event.I cant understand why the PCREC doesnt want us to learn more about Islamic terrorism.
      </p>
      <p>
       Jonathan Matusitz agrees. When asked during the Q and A how we can fight back against jihad and the growing Islamic threat to America, his response was: We need to educate the public, quickly. We need to defend our way of life. We need to elect leaders who will stand up to those who wish us harm.
      </p>
      <p>
       Let this serve as a lesson to the GOP establishment, who assume the role of gatekeepers in deciding for other members who is important enough to speak at Republican functions, or what speech is acceptable. On a local scale, this political firestorm reflects what is happening to the nation and the Republican leadership, which allows the unelected, instead of the rank and file members, to call the shots.
      </p>
      <p>
       This is just one more fight in the larger battle between grassroots Republicans and the establishment types, said one of the attendees named Cathi, adding that this showdown only proved that she had made the right choice when she left the Republican Party several years ago.
      </p>
      <p>
       Are you reading this, Florida Republicans?
      </p>
      <p>
