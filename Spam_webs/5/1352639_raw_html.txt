              <p>
               Those of you looking to buy Google's new Nexus 7 tablet may have a wait on your hands.
              </p>
              <p>
               Unveiled late last month, the
               <a data-is-autolink="true" href="/topics/tablets/">
                tablet
               </a>
               has slowly trickled into the retail market. Google was naturally first in line, offering the
               <a data-is-autolink="true" href="/products/google-nexus-7/">
                Nexus 7
               </a>
               as a preorder in advance of its official mid-July launch. Other retailers, such as GameStop, Staples, and Sam's Club, soon followed suit.
              </p>
              <p>
               But a lot of eager customers apparently scooped up those pre-sales, since the tablet is playing hard to get at most outlets.
              </p>
              <p>
               GameStop, which is selling the 16GB edition, is showing the
               <a data-component="externalLink" href="http://www.gamestop.com/android/tablets/nexus-7-16gb-from-google0153/102893" target="_blank">
                tablet as back-ordered
               </a>
               . Further details reveal that the gaming outlet has ordered more stock from the vendor.
              </p>
              <p>
               A spokeswoman for GameStop told CNET that the company expects to ship a new round of tablets to its stores on August 7. The retail outlets have been taking preorders based on this shipment. Until August 5, GameStop is offering a 30 percent increase on the value of any trade-in item for customers who purchase the Nexus 7.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a data-component="externalLink" href="http://www.staples.com/Google-Nexus-7-16GB-Tablet/product_949231?catalogId=10051&amp;fromUrl=home&amp;cmSearchKeyword=nexus+7&amp;cmArea=SEARCH&amp;langId=-1&amp;storeId=10001&amp;ddkey=http:StaplesSearch" target="_blank">
                Staples
               </a>
               ,
               <a data-component="externalLink" href="http://www.officedepot.com/a/browse/tablets/N=5+518425/?hijack=nexus%207&amp;type=Search" target="_blank">
                Office Depot
               </a>
               , and
               <a data-component="externalLink" href="http://www.samsclub.com/sams/shop/product.jsp?productId=prod7380213&amp;searchTerm=nexus%207" target="_blank">
                Sam's Club
               </a>
               all show the Nexus 7 as currently out of stock with no further information as to its availability. Google's own site is promising that the tablet will "ship soon," meaning a
               <a data-component="externalLink" href="https://play.google.com/store/devices/details?id=nexus_7_8gb&amp;feature=nexus-home-page" target="_blank">
                one- to two-week delay
               </a>
