             <h3>
              Content Overload: Break Free!
             </h3>
             <p>
              The number of people who claim to know how to teach you a five figure launch plan or how to gain your first billion subscribers or
              <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/85broads/2013/10/01/how-to-crowdfund-50000-in-your-spare-time/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.forbes.com/sites/85broads/2013/10/01/how-to-crowdfund-50000-in-your-spare-time/', 'how to crowd fund');" target="_blank">
               how to crowd fund
              </a>
              your way to a permanent vacation on a faraway beach is growing daily.
             </p>
             <p>
              I dont want yet another free download. My Dropbox has a hilariously dusty digital folder titled Self Study with 350 free (and not-so-free) e-learning programs Ive downloaded in the last five years  thats 70 per year if you were paying attention. Most languish unread.
             </p>
             <p>
              I dont want yet another no-commitment e-course from some guru, maven, ninja, or otherwise titled nincompoop.
             </p>
             <p>
              (Id use stronger words, but
              <a href="http://spinsucks.com/communication/sucks-bad-word/" target="_blank" title="Sucks is a Bad Word">
               was told that harsh language makes you get all bleary-eyed and clicky
              </a>
              .)
             </p>
             <p>
              I dont want your tw0-page long manifesto, mantra, chant, ancient witchdoctor script, or miracle elixir in a digital bottle. I can barely remember to call my family on their birthdays, so what makes you think Ill find marketing inner peace with  let alone remember to read  your mantra?
             </p>
             <h3>
              What is all this Free Content Really Doing for Us?
             </h3>
             <p>
              Can I get real with you? All this feel-good, self-gratifying, webinar-attending, free-ebook downloading, manifesto researching, blog-after-blog-after-blog-after-blog-reading activity that we do?
             </p>
             <p>
              It might enable us to
              <a href="http://sxsw.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://sxsw.com/', 'hold our own at the bar at SXSW');" target="_blank">
               hold our own at the bar at SXSW
              </a>
              or at a Chamber of Commerce Luncheon, but by Thors Hammer it sure isnt growing our bottom lines, our customer base, our time with the family we like, or our ability to vacation on some faraway beach.
             </p>
             <p>
              Is it?
             </p>
             <p>
              I mean, you wouldnt be sitting here reading this if you were where you wanted to be. I bet youre
              <a href="http://spinsucks.com/entrepreneur/time-management-get-things-done/" target="_blank" title="Time Management: Six Tips to Be More Productive and Get Things Done">
               procrastinating
              </a>
              right now. I can see it in your eyes and those tiny beads of sweat starting to gather on your forehead.
             </p>
             <p>
              Is that a to-do list on your desk? How many tasks is that? Did you even keep count past 100? Were 328 words in and you just got three more Gmail notifications, five new Facebook notifications, four new Twitter notifications, 20 Google+ notifications, and your iPhone is ringing.
             </p>
             <p>
              Stop. It. You deserve better than this. I might be sick of free webinars, but you know what Im really sick of?
             </p>
             <p>
              Im sick of people like you  brilliant, smart, funny, talented, and nice as you are  wasting their precious time sitting on the sidelines.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thinking youre not good enough.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thinking you need just a little bit more knowledge, a little more free content to really take it to the next step.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thinking you could really feel good if you ran your idea past one more so-called expert.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thinking you really ought to know what that guru knows because if your list were just a little larger, youd
             </p>
             <p>
              Youd what?
             </p>
             <p>
              Be more successful? More liked? Validated that your blog isnt awful, your business is successful, and the work you do matters?
             </p>
             <h3>
              Time to Make a Change
             </h3>
             <p>
              Do me a favor: I want you to look at your hilariously-far-from-
              <a href="http://www.43folders.com/43-folders-series-inbox-zero" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.43folders.com/43-folders-series-inbox-zero', 'inbox zero');" target="_blank" title="Inbox Zero">
               inbox zero
              </a>
              email (because I know you have it open right now) and I want you to pick five newsletters or expert dispatches or blogs delivered to your email that youve deleted week-after-week-after-week thinking, Maybe next week, theyll write the perfect answer for my problem.
             </p>
             <p>
              I want you to take your shaking mouse hand and do the mean, horrifying, anti-social, brow-sweat inducing thing and
              <a href="http://unbounce.com/email-marketing/ask-readers-to-unsubscribe/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://unbounce.com/email-marketing/ask-readers-to-unsubscribe/', 'UN-SUB-SCRIBE');" target="_blank">
               UN-SUB-SCRIBE
              </a>
              . And when you click that mouse button down like the champ you are, I want you to hear a stadium full of people chanting that.
             </p>
             <p>
              UN.
             </p>
             <p>
              SUB.
             </p>
             <p>
              SCRIBE.
             </p>
             <p>
              And then I want you to hear that stadium of people going nuts for you like you just threw the winning touchdown pass.
             </p>
             <p>
              Newsflash, you lovely, flawed, goofball of a human being: That kind of warm fuzzy feeling has to come from inside.
             </p>
             <p>
              Youre not accountable to your parents, anymore. Or your teachers. Or even your boss (if you still have one). You know who youre accountable to?
             </p>
             <p>
              You.
             </p>
             <p>
              And thats a terrifyingly beautiful thing. It means you get to
              <a href="http://spinsucks.com/communication/how-to-effectively-manage-client-expectations/" target="_blank" title="How to Effectively Manage Client Expectations">
               set the expectations
              </a>
              . And it means you can either expect yourself to be a great businessperson and work yourself hard on things that really matter to you.
             </p>
             <p>
              Or you can expect yourself to be a mediocre, somewhat successful businessperson, who often got distracted by webinars, living for other peoples approval, and mile-long to-do lists that had less than five tasks you genuinely cared about.
             </p>
             <p>
              Your fate is entirely in your hands.
             </p>
             <p>
              Act like it. You deserve it.
             </p>
             <p>
              Stop consuming. Start creating. Do it today.
             </p>
