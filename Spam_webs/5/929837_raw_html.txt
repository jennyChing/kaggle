       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Dorm Life Eats: Microwave Chocolate Cake
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2011-11-01T20:59:25+00:00">
         November 1, 2011
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://www.bakerita.com/dorm-life-eats-microwave-chocolate-cake/#comments">
          2 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          The start of my Dorm Life Eats series, a collection of recipes that are easy and cheap to prepare, begins with a delicious Microwave Chocolate Cake! This chocolate cake uses to Nutella to stay rich and fudgy and is so simple to make.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1041" height="381" src="//www.bakerita.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/IMG_1715-1024x651.jpg" title="IMG_1715" width="600"/>
         As most of you know, Im living in a dorm this year.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youve lived in a dorm at any point in your life, youll understand my struggles with trying to bake in a dorm kitchen. Its smelly, its gross, no one cleans up afterthemselves, and the appliances should have been replaced long ago. No ideal for someone who loves to bake.
        </p>
        <p>
         Because of this, Ive been creative in avoiding the oven and kitchen all together and Ive taken to doing something I never would have imagined myself doing  using the microwave to bake. Whaaat? I know. Stay with me here.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-1036">
         </span>
         This post will be the first in my Dorm Life Eats series. Most of these recipes will be either:
        </p>
        <p>
         1. No-bake
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Made in the microwave
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Very minimal oven/stove use
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Few ingredients
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Cheap ingredients! (Poor college student, remember?)
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.bakerita.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/IMG_17091.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1045" height="750" src="//www.bakerita.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/IMG_17091-819x1024.jpg" title="IMG_1709" width="600"/>
         </a>
         [Pre baking  er, microwaving; ignore the power cord in the back, focus on the flowers and the pumpkin.]
        </p>
        <p>
         I assume there are many folks in my position, and being the avid baker that I am, Im determined to make delicious treats inunconventionalways. Ive researched recipes andbrainstormingincessantly, and its definitely possible!
        </p>
        <p>
         So, while Ill still have tradition recipes from my visits home, Ill also be providing recipes for easy, dorm cooking with minimal costs involved. Get ready  even those of you with a readily available oven will be wanting to try these easy recipes.
        </p>
        <p>
         The first? A microwave cake that actually tastes
         <em>
          good!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Most of us know microwave cakes as a desperate, middle of the night substitute f
        </p>
        <div style="display: none;">
         <a href="http://start-success-up.com/thinspiration-revolutionary-weight-loss-method%09" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://start-success-up.com/thinspiration-revolutionary-weight-loss-method%09', ' Thinspiration Revolutionary Weight Loss Method! ');" title="	Thinspiration Revolutionary Weight Loss Method!	">
          Thinspiration Revolutionary Weight Loss Method!
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         or an actually good cake. This one, however, is moist, chocolatey, and you can barelydifferentiateit from a normal chocolate cake (but dont overcook it; this will be the death of your cake). The addition ofNutella doesnt really add to the flavor, but it makes the texture and moistness be out of this world.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its so easy to whip up, and is the perfect portion size of a dessert for two. My roommate and I devoured this thing on a chilly fall day and I know well be making it again soon! We ate it with a little extra Nutella, which is never a bad thing, obviously.
        </p>
        <p>
         Try it out, youll bepleasantlysurprised! And stay tuned for some delicious, easy, dorm-friendly recipes!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.bakerita.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/IMG_17281.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1047" height="598" src="//www.bakerita.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/IMG_17281-1024x1021.jpg" title="IMG_1728" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
        </p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon baking powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 tablespoons white granulated sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 egg
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 tablespoons cocoa powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 tablespoons Nutella
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 tablespoons milk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 tablespoons vegetable oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup chocolate chips (optional)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Directions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Combine all ingredients in a large coffee mug. Whisk well with a fork until smooth. Microwave on high for 1  3 minutes. (Time depends on microwave wattage. Check after 1 minutes, and then cook in 30 second increments from there. DO NOT OVERCOOK! Moister is better in this case. Mine took 3 minutes.)
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Serve in the mug, with some extra Nutella if desired. Dig in, and enjoy!
           </li>
          </ol>

          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           Note: Depending on the wattage of your microwave, you may have to increase cooking time.
