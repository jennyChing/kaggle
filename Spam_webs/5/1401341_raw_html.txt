         <p>
          In order to maximize your chances for both federal and state financial aid, file the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (
          <a href="https://www.estudentloan.com/financial-aid/fafsa">
           FAFSA
          </a>
          ) as soon as possible on or after January 1 for each academic year in which you will needassistance. For the 2015-2016 academic year, the FAFSA became available on January 1st, 2015. You may
          <a href="https://fafsa.ed.gov/" target="_blank">
           file theFAFSA
          </a>
          for the 2015-2016 academic year online as late as midnight Central Time June 30, 2016. You may file corrections or updates online up until midnight Central Time September 17, 2016. The school you attend may have a separate deadline as well. Be sure to check with your financial aid office to ensure you meet their requirements. Each state has separate deadlines and some require additional applications. Use the following table to learn about deadlines in your state.
         </p>

         <h4>
          FAFSA Deadline Tips
         </h4>
         <ul>
          <li>
           File as soon as possible after the FAFSA is made availableon or after January 1st of each academic year you are in college.
          </li>
          <li>
           If you (and your parents, if applicable) havent filed tax returns, use estimates. Your FAFSA can be updated later.
          </li>
          <li>
           The FAFSA is your application for many types of financial aid including federal student aid (including federal student loans), state-based aid, campus-based federal aid, and school-specific aid (e.g. endowed scholarships, school-funded work programs, etc).
          </li>
          <li>
           File the FAFSA even if you dont think youll qualify for assistance. The formula for determining eligibility is complex. Even if you dont qualify for need-based aid, you still need to file the FAFSA if you want to borrow a federal student loan.
          </li>
         </ul>
