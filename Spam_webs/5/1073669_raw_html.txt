                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats
                    </h3>
                    <div id="Date">
                     <script>
                      changeDate('Friday, January 23, 2015');
                     </script>
                    </div>
                    <br/>
                    <br/>
                    <br/>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6480024718962178004" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <img alt="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-M6Jr8vZ_3YY/VMIFJxHoZcI/AAAAAAAAHkw/gt0bfQZOi2U/s1600/diy-flower-party-hats.jpg" title="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats"/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I'm a fan of parties -
                     <i>
                      especially garden parties
                     </i>
                     - and if I ever throw one I like things to be pretty from start to finish.
                     <i>
                      Must be aninner girly girl thing?
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      <br/>
                     </i>
                     Party hats are totally underrated, and should be worn by adults WAY MORE OFTEN, and not as a joke! We're so quick to drop the small fun things from our childhood, let's stop that. It's the small things that make life that much sweeter.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     With that being said, at your next shindig - I demand that you have party hats! Not just any kind of party hats, but party hats with flair!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Supplies
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <u>
                      <br/>
                     </u>
                     Fresh Flowers
                     <br/>
                     Party Hat Template
                     <br/>
                     Scissors
                     <br/>
                     Glue or Tape
                     <br/>
                     Hot Glue
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Instructions
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      <u>
                       <br/>
                      </u>
                      Cut your party hat template out of your cardstock and glue or tape together into a cone shape.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.studiodiy.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/DIY-Flower-Party-Hat.jpg">
                        <img alt="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" height="400" src="http://www.studiodiy.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/DIY-Flower-Party-Hat-297x445.jpg" title="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" width="266"/>
                       </a>
                       <a href="http://www.studiodiy.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Flower-Party-Hats.jpg">
                        <img alt="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" height="400" src="http://www.studiodiy.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Flower-Party-Hats-297x445.jpg" title="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" width="266"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Cut all the stems as close to the base of the flower as possible. mine off the stems. Place a dot of hot glue on the bottom center of the flower. Carefully but firmly glue your flowers in a cascading effect slightly wrapping around the party hat one by one. Start from the bottom.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.studiodiy.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Ranunculus.jpg">
                        <img alt="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" height="400" src="http://www.studiodiy.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Ranunculus-297x445.jpg" title="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" width="266"/>
                       </a>
                       <a href="http://www.studiodiy.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Flowers-on-Party-Hats-DIY.jpg">
                        <img alt="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" height="400" src="http://www.studiodiy.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Flowers-on-Party-Hats-DIY-297x445.jpg" title="DIY Cascading Flower Party Hats" width="266"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Let the flowers set, and there you have yourself pretty hats for your party!
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
