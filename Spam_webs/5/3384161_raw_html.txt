        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Watermelon Blueberry Mojito  The Perfect Sundowner {no sugar added}
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-05-30T10:31:01+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 30, 2014
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.thecleandish.com/author/rose-2/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Rose
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.thecleandish.com/watermelon-blueberry-mojito-the-perfect-sundowner-no-sugar-added/#comments">
           21 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.thecleandish.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Watermelon-Blueberry-Mojito-Sugar-Free_14521.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Watermelon Blueberry Mojito - The Perfect Sundowner {no sugar added} | The Clean Dish" class="alignnone wp-image-4394 size-full" height="1025" src="http://www.thecleandish.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Watermelon-Blueberry-Mojito-Sugar-Free_14521.jpg" width="680"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thecleandish.com/watermelon-blueberry-mojito-the-perfect-sundowner-no-sugar-added/&amp;media=http://www.thecleandish.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Watermelon-Blueberry-Mojito-Sugar-Free_14521.jpg&amp;description=Watermelon Blueberry Mojito  The Perfect Sundowner {no sugar added}')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          This fruity-fresh Watermelon Blueberry Mojito is the perfect sundowner and has a major advantage over the classic mojito: You dont need to add any sugar as this cocktail is naturally sweet! Bottoms up!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-4376">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh sweet watermelons!!
        </p>
        <p>
         Nothing says summer-sunshine-picnic-on-the-beach more than a juicy slice of watermelon, am I right?
        </p>
        <p>
         Since the beach is a good 2 hour drive from where we live (trust me, I am NOT complaining! Its close enough for a day trip so I know were still better off than umm anybody living 2h+ away from the beach!!) I decided we might as well turn that slice of watermelon into a cocktail, sit on the front porch, watch the sun set and wind down with an icy cold fruity beverage in hand. Swatting mosquitoes with the other. Aw, the South! You gotta love it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mojito is my favorite mix drink as I always feel like its the cleanest of em all. Minus the sugar, of course. I remember the first time I made mojitos at home  glorious! I must have been about 19  but before I continue my trip down memory lane, let me remind you that its perfectly legal in Germany to have alcohol at that age! In fact, you can drink beer and wine as soon as you turn 14 if youre accompanied by a parent. Crazy, huh? Well, making and drinking alcohol (in moderation) is a huge part of our culture and I miss it!
        </p>
        <p>
         Back to my first homemade mojito: it was like a revelation! I suddenly understood that it doesnt take a fully stocked bar in a crowded club to have a mix drink. In fact, I realized it was even
         <em>
          cheaper
         </em>
         to make cocktails at home! Ok, I dont want to make it sound like a milestone but it was a pretty big deal for us back then!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.thecleandish.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Watermelon-Blueberry-Mojito-Sugar-Free_1474.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">

        <p>
         If you like mojitos and enjoy fruity drinks, Im gonna go ahead and say: youll love this version of a classic! Just make sure your watermelon is not past its prime. If the flesh is dark red and falls apart easily, you may want to wait until you have a watermelon that is not overly ripe as those tend to taste almost a tad bit fermented (not in a good way). A fresh watermelon will make your mojito more flavorful and enjoyable. Cheers!
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Watermelon Blueberry Mojito - The Perfect Sundowner {no sugar added)
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://www.thecleandish.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/450-Watermelon-Blueberry-Mojito-Sugar-Free_1452-300x300.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.thecleandish.com/easyrecipe-print/4376-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Rose | The Clean Dish
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            1
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup ice
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup watermelon juice (see notes)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2oz / 4 tablespoon white rum
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 sprig mint (10-12 leaves)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon lime juice, fresh squeezed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup blueberries
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Sparkling water
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Directions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add ice, watermelon juice, rum, half of the mint and lime juice to a cocktail shaker (or canning jar with lid) and shake well.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Transfer to a tall glass and top off with sparkling water.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Garnish with remaining mint, add blueberries and enjoy!
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           To make watermelon juice, separate flesh from rind, discard rind.
           <br/>
           Slice and cube watermelon and add to a blender. Process until smooth, then pour through a sieve, cheesecloth or nut bag. Straining is optional but recommended!
          </div>
