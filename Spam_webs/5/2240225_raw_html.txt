         5 Ingredient Baked Egg Boats
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ingredient Baked Egg Boats for Breakfast!" class="alignright size-full wp-image-14511" height="828" src="http://asideofsweet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Baked-Egg-Boats-Recipe-Bread-Egg-Brunch-226e.jpg" width="600"/>
         One of the best thing about being a blogger is the community of bloggers that Ive gotten to connect with. Im always surprised by how supportive and friendlyother bloggers are. People go out of the way to help each other, mentor beginning bloggers and pass onthe tricks that theyve learned along the way. Ive never felt like blogging is a competition, or that Im being judged for my blog not being good enough, or big enough. In all fairness, my harshest critic is probably myself!
        </p>
        <p>
         One of the blogging groups I belong to,
         <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/recipetin/food-bloggers-central-sharing-board/" target="_blank" title="Food Bloggers Central">
          Food Bloggers Central
         </a>
         , is hosting a Blog Hop! Were hosting a virtual recipe circle where each one of us has one of our recipes made and we make another awesome bloggers recipe.
        </p>
        <p>
         The happy task fell upon me to make one of Peter from
         <a href="http://www.feedyoursoul2.com/" target="_blank" title="Feed Your Soul Too">
          Feed Your Soul Too
         </a>
         s recipes. There were so many great classic recipes to choose from it was hard to narrow it down! Peter is from Chicago and specializes in easy and delicious mealsthat bring the whole family together. In addition to these baked egg boats, some other favorites of mine from Peters blog are his
         <a href="http://www.feedyoursoul2.com/2015/03/salmon-paella.html" target="_blank" title="Salmon Paella">
          salmon paella
         </a>
         and this
         <a href="http://www.feedyoursoul2.com/2015/02/greek-7-layer-dip.html" target="_blank" title="Greek 7 Layer Dip">
          Greek 7 Layer Dip
         </a>
         . You absolutely have to check his blog out!
         <span id="more-14504">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ingredient Baked Egg Boats for Breakfast!" class="alignright size-full wp-image-14507" height="397" src="http://asideofsweet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Best-Easy-Brunch-Recipes-Eggs-236.jpg" width="600"/>
         <img alt="5 Ingredient Baked Egg Boats for Breakfast!" class="alignright size-full wp-image-14506" height="397" src="http://asideofsweet.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Baked-Egg-Boats-Recipe-Breakfast-220.jpg" width="600"/>
         Bobbie from
         <a href="http://bamskitchen.com/dietary-restrictions/vegan/kimchi-pantry-fried-brown-rice/" target="_blank" title="Bam's Kitchen">
          Bams Kitchen
         </a>
         made one of my earliest food blog adventures,
         <a href="http://asideofsweet.com/edamame-and-kimchi-fried-rice-recipe/" target="_blank" title="Kimchi Fried Rice">
          Kimchi Fried Rice
         </a>
         (spoiler alert  my food photography has gotten
         <em>
          a lot
         </em>
         better in the last three years). It was so fun to see the nice update she gave to one of my old favorites!
        </p>
        <p>
         As for the recipe, these egg boats are
         <em>
          amazing
         </em>
         ! They are so easy to make and can be made with those random things you have in you fridge that just need to be used up! Any sort of cheese, any veggies, you name it! Its one of the genius sort of recipes that left me thinking, Why didnt I think of this!? Thanks so much
         <a href="http://feedyoursoul2.com" target="_blank" title="Feed Your Soul Too">
          Peter
         </a>
         !

          5 Ingredient Baked Egg Boats

         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          These baked egg boats are delicious and SO easy to make! They are easily customized for whatever you have in the fridge!
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Lightly adapted from Feed Your Soul Too
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSCategory">
           Recipe type:
           <span itemprop="recipeCategory">
            Breakfast
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            4-5
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            9 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             medium onion, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             colored pepper (I used  yellow,  red), diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup of cheddar cheese, divided
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Loaf of French bread
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Whisk the eggs. Add the onion, colored pepper and  cup of cheddar cheese.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Hollow out the loaf of bread. Make sure not to carve out too much to prevent leaking.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour in the egg mixture and sprinkle with remaining cheese.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake at 350F for 30-40 minutes, until egg is set. Slice and serve.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           <em>
            Tip:
           </em>
           If you get overzealous and carve out too much of your loaf, pour the contents out into a bowl and patch the leak with a smear of cream cheese.
          </div>
