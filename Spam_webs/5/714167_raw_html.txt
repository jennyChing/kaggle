       <h2 class="storytitle">
        <span>
         October 13th, 2008
        </span>
        <br/>
        <a href="http://www.harlandwestgate.com/TastyBeverage/blog/?p=20" rel="bookmark">
         Guava Juice
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="storycontent">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.harlandwestgate.com/TastyBeverage/img/Guava.JPG" rel="lightbox">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="100" src="http://www.harlandwestgate.com/TastyBeverage/img/guavathumb.jpg" title="Guava Juice" width="75"/>
         </a>
         <a class="gm-link" href="http://www.harlandwestgate.com/TastyBeverage/blog/?page_id=2&amp;center_lat=21.2718563&amp;center_lng=-157.7736969">
          <img alt="Geotag Icon" src="http://www.harlandwestgate.com/TastyBeverage/blog/wp-content/plugins/geo-mashup/images/geotag_16.png"/>
          Found in: Kahala Resort, Honolulu, HI.
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sure its pink, but since youre probably on a tropical island if youre drinking this, its ok.  I was first introduced to this nectar in Hawaii, where its just as common as water, except a few million times more delicious.  Its sweet without being syrupy, and its got just enough pulp so that you know it actually came from a fruit instead of somebodys chemistry lab, without making you feel like you should be chewing it (Im looking at you, Extra Pulp Orange Juice).
        </p>
        <p>
         The pulp itself is more like tiny soft beads than shredded rind (you again, Extra Pulp Orange Juice).  Whether you sip or gulp this beverage, the taste and texture combine to create the impression that you have a luau in your mouth.  While I havent tried this yet, I assume that if you were to find this crazy-amazing drink here on the mainland, you could close your eyes while drinking it and actually believe youre lying on a white-sand beach.
        </p>
        <p>
         So get thee to a gourmet market, and if you find some, please, please, please tell me.  Seriously.  Thats why this site has a Contact page.
        </p>
