        <h2>
         Influenza Activity + Surveillance
        </h2>
        <div class="content">
         <p>
          Surveillance of Influenza activity in Australia &amp; New Zealand
         </p>
         <p>
          This report provides a summary of surveillance data collected from around Australia and New Zealand. Regional reporting in some areas ceases when flu activity is low (out of season).
         </p>
         <p>
          Please note that the vastmajority of people do not get tested for influenza and that there may alsobe some delays in reporting confirmedinfluenza cases. Therefore data presented here maybe underestimating influenza activity.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Australia
         </h2>
         <h4>
          Commonwealth Department of Health &amp; Ageing
         </h4>
         <p>
          There have been a total of 15,174 laboratory confirmed notifications of Influenza in Australia at the end of 10 July 2015
          <br/>
           QLD: 4290
          <br/>
           NSW: 2987
          <br/>
           WA: 1721
          <br/>
           SA: 2966
          <br/>
           VIC: 2685
          <br/>
           ACT: 256
          <br/>
           NT: 88
          <br/>
           TAS: 181
         </p>
         <p>
          For more national data:
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a class="jce_file" href="http://www9.health.gov.au/cda/Source/CDA-index.cfm?CFID=2150133&amp;CFTOKEN=9727723710eb1a77-28680E71-D60E-BDAF-8B3EF9FA17C5355F&amp;jsessionid=72306d6e8e0ca9ba1e766f8496b547534333" target="_blank" title="National Notifiable Diseases Surveillance System">
            National Notifiable Diseases Surveillance System
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.health.gov.au/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-surveil-ozflu-flucurr.htm" target="_blank">
            Australian Influenza Report
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <h4>
          New South Wales
         </h4>
         <p>
          <strong>
           28 June 2015
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          All measures indicate that the influenza season has commenced, however hospital activity remains low to moderate. Influenza activity is expected to continue to increase from now until the season peaks.
          <br/>
          Hospital surveillance  presentations to NSW emergency departments for influenza-like illness (ILI) increased and were above the flu season threshold. Bronchiolitis presentations remained high.
          <br/>
          Laboratory surveillance  the proportion of respiratory samples positive for influenza was low (6.7%) but continues to increase, with influenza B viruses predominant. Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) and rhinovirus activity remain high.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more information:
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a href="http://www.health.nsw.gov.au/Infectious/Influenza/Pages/reports.aspx" target="_blank">
            NSW Health - Infectious Diseases Alert
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <h4>
          Queensland
         </h4>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5 July 2015
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Year to date (1 January to 5 July) there have been 4,131 notifications.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span>
           For more information:
          </span>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a class="jce_file" href="http://www.health.qld.gov.au/ph/cdb/cdsarunit.asp" target="_blank" title="QLD Health - Preventable Diseases">
            QLD Health - Preventable Diseases
           </a>
          </span>
          <a name="surveillance sa">
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           South Australia
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4 July 2015
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Influenza activity in South Australia is increasing.
          <br/>
          Three hundred and forty-eight cases of influenza were reported this week. Of these, 119 were characterised as influenza A and 229 were characterised as influenza B. Cases comprised of 154 males and 194 females, with a median age of 17 years. One hundred and nineteen (34%) notifications were for children aged less than 10 years and 34 (10%) notifications for persons aged 65 years or greater. Year-to-date there have been 2,770 cases of influenza, compared to 1,011 cases reported for the same period last year.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more information:
          <a href="http://www.sahealth.sa.gov.au/wps/wcm/connect/public+content/sa+health+internet/about+us/health+statistics/surveillance+of+notifiable+conditions" target="_blank">
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <span class="jce_file">
             Communicable Diseases Control Branch
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h4>
          Tasmania
         </h4>
         <p>
          <strong>
           28 June 2015
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          This report describes flu activity in Tasmania up to Sunday 28 June 2015
          <br/>
          Available data over this period indicate:
          <br/>
          Increased flu in the South of the State indicated that the 2015 winter flu season has started. A total of 160 notifications of flu have been notified since the start of 2015.
          <br/>
          Flu notifications and the amount of testing increased during June.
          <br/>
          Influenza A virus is responsible for the majority of recent influenza infections.
          <br/>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          For more information:
          <a href="http://www.dhhs.tas.gov.au/peh/communicable_diseases_prevention_unit" target="_blank">
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <span class="jce_file">
             Communicable DiseasesPrevention -
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a href="http://www.dhhs.tas.gov.au/peh/communicable_diseases_prevention_unit" target="_blank">
            FluTAS
           </a>
           <br/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <h4>
          Victoria
         </h4>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5 July 2015
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Influenza-like illness (ILI) activity increased above baseline levels (4.0) this week with 6.3 cases per 1000 patients seen, compared to 2.9 in the previous week. Data from the National Home Doctor Service (Victorian data) remained above the baseline threshold (10.0) with a proportion of 10.9.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           For more information:
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.vidrl.org.au/" style="font-size: 13px;" target="_blank">
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Victorian Infectious Diseases Reference Laboratory
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h4>
          Western Australia
         </h4>
         <p>
          <strong>
           28 June 2015
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Rates of influenza-like illness increased this week.
          <br/>
          ILI presentations to sentinel emergency departments (EDs) and sentinel general practitioners (GPs) increased this week.
          <br/>
          Hospitalisations with confirmed influenza have been relatively steady over recent weeks, at around 10 per 1000 admissions.
          <br/>
          Influenza virus detections, test positivity and notifications are have changed little in the past few weeks, with influenza B viruses (75%) and influenza A/H3N2 (20%) viruses dominating detections; influenza A/H1N1 is co-circulating at a low level (5%). The influenza B strains currently circulating are 90% Yamagata lineage, matching the trivalent influenza vaccine strain.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more information:
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a class="jce_file" href="http://www.public.health.wa.gov.au/3/487/3/virus_watch.pm" target="_blank" title="WA Virus Watch">
            WA Virus WAtch
           </a>

         <h2>
          Nationally
         </h2>
         <h4>
          Australian Sentinel Practices Research Network (ASPREN)
         </h4>
         <p>
          <em>
           ASPREN is a national syndromic surveillance program co-ordinated by the Discipline of General Practice at the University of Adelaide and The Royal Australian College of General Practitioners. One of the conditions under surveillance is influenza like illness (ILI). General practitioners participating in the ASPREN program contribute data on the proportion of consultations which are ILI related.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           28 June 2015
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Influenza-Like-Illness levels increased remaining above baseline
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           (Baseline ILL &lt; 4 in 1000 consultations, Normal 4&lt; ILL rate &lt;24).
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span>
           For more information:
          </span>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a class="jce_file" href="http://www.dmac.adelaide.edu.au/aspren/asprenMISServlet?page=index" target="_blank" title="ASPREN">
            ASPREN
           </a>
          </span>
          <span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <h4>
          Flu Tracking
         </h4>
         <p>
          <em>
           FluTracking is a pilot online health surveillance system which aims to detect epidemics of influenza. It is a joint initiative of The University of Newcastle, Hunter New England Area Health Service (NSW Health) and Hunter Medical Research Institute. Participation is voluntary and involves the completion of a weekly online survey during the influenza season. Data are collected on basic demographics, symptoms of ILI and absenteeism.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5 July 2015
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Low levels of influenza-like illness activity
         </p>
         <p>
          Across Australia, fever and cough was reported by 2.8% of vaccinated participants and 2.9% of unvaccinated participants. Fever, cough and absence from normal duties was reported by 1.8% of vaccinated participants and 1.6% of unvaccinated participants.
          <br/>
          For participants this week, 12500/20352 (61.4 %) have received the seasonal vaccine so far. Of the 4228 participants who identified as working face-to-face with patients, 3390 (80.2%) have received the vaccine.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 13px;">
           For more information, or to enrol:
          </span>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a href="http://www.flutracking.net/" target="_blank" title="Flu Tracking">
            Flu Tracking
           </a>
          </span>
