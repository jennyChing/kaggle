        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Eggless Turkey and Spinach Meatballs
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2015-01-27T11:53:07+00:00">
          January 27, 2015
         </span>
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/author/oriana-romero/" rel="author">
            Oriana Romero
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/eggless-turkey-and-spinach-meatballs/#comments">
           14 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
         <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="17257183" data-link="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/eggless-turkey-and-spinach-meatballs/" data-summary="" data-title="Eggless Turkey and Spinach Meatballs">
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           Eggless Turkey and Spinach MeatballsThese egg-free meatballs are very juicy, tender and packed full of flavor. Theyre easy to put together  Dinner in a flash!
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Egg-Free Turkey and Spinach Meatballs -2" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8681" data-jpibfi-description="Eggless Turkey and Spinach Meatballs - These egg-free meatball are very juicy, tender and packed full of flavor. They're easy to put together  dinner in a flash! http://mommyhoodsdiary.com" height="980" src="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Egg-Free-Turkey-and-Spinach-Meatballs-2-700x980.jpg" width="700"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          My kids Love. Love. Love meatballs. Any kind of meatballs! But making meatballs without eggs is quite challenging. They end up too dry and hard or too fragile and break when cooking. Let me tell you that I have been testing and re-testing this recipe for quite some time, and I finally nailed it!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          These meatballs are easy to make, quick, and bursting with yummy flavor.In other words  theyre so good!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Many of you have been asking me about Victorias egg allergy and I want to thank you allfor your kind messages. Its very gratifying to know that your little ones enjoy my recipes. I know that egg allergy can be hard. I mean, so many foods are made with eggs and egg products that it can be really hard to know whats OK and not OK to eat. Although, theres a growing awareness about food allergies sometimes I dont feel comfortable eating out, so developing these
          <a href="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/egg-free/" title="Egg-Free">
           eggless recipes
          </a>
          its one of my priorities.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Egg-Free Turkey and Spinach Meatballs -6" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8689" data-jpibfi-description="Eggless Turkey and Spinach Meatballs - These egg-free meatball are very juicy, tender and packed full of flavor. They're easy to put together  dinner in a flash! http://mommyhoodsdiary.com" height="500" src="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Egg-Free-Turkey-and-Spinach-Meatballs-6-700x500.jpg" width="700"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The thing I love about meatballs is that theyre so versatile. You can make them with
          <a href="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/black-bean-soup-with-smoked-pork-chop-venezuelan-style/" title="Black Bean Soup with Smoked Pork Chops {Venezuelan Style}">
           pork
          </a>
          , turkey, chicken, or beef. These meatballs are perfect over rice, some mashed potatoes, and of course over some noodles.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          As usual, I didnt waist the opportunity to hide/camouflage some veggies to add an extra bit of wonderfulness to these meatballs. This time was spinach, but other times Ive added red pepper, kale,
          <a href="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/quinoa-breakfast-bowl/" title="Quinoa Breakfast Bowl">
           quinoa
          </a>
          , etc
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Egg-Free Turkey and Spinach Meatballs -4" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8685" data-jpibfi-description="Eggless Turkey and Spinach Meatballs - These egg-free meatball are very juicy, tender and packed full of flavor. They're easy to put together  dinner in a flash! http://mommyhoodsdiary.com" height="980" src="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Egg-Free-Turkey-and-Spinach-Meatballs-4-700x980.jpg" width="700"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          This is an
          <a href="http://mommyhoodsdiary.com/easy-peri-peri-chicken-breasts/" title="Easy Peri Peri Chicken Breasts">
           easy
          </a>
          , comforting, budget-friendly meal that the whole family will love. You can also make the meatballs ahead of time or freeze them as needed!
          <br/>
         </p>

           <div class="ERSServes">
            Serves:
            <span itemprop="recipeYield">
             12 meatballs
            </span>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSIngredients">
           <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
            Ingredients:
           </div>
           <ul>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tablespoons buttermilk
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             4 saltine or soda crackers, crushed into fine crumbs
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup onion, finely chopped
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon dried oregano
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 cloves garlic, minced
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup chopped spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 teaspoon salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 teaspoon black pepper
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup Parmesan cheese, finely grated
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 pound extra lean ground turkey
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2  3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1  - 2 cups jarred tomato sauce
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon snipped fresh basil
            </li>
           </ul>
           <div class="ERSClear">
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSInstructions">
           <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
            Directions:
           </div>
           <ol>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             In a medium bowl combine buttermilk, crushed crackers, oinion, oregano, garlic, spinach, salt, pepper, and parmesan. Add ground turkey; mix well. Using fingers, gently mix all the ingredients until thoroughly combined.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Form into 1 1/2-inch balls.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Heat the oil in heavy large frying pan over medium high heat. Add the meatballs and saute until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Add the marinara sauce. Simmer over medium low until the sauce thickens slightly and the flavors blend, about 10 minutes. Season the sauce, to taste, with salt and pepper if necessary.
            </li>
           </ol>
           <div class="ERSClear">
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
           <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
            Notes
           </div>
           <div class="ERSNotes">
            The key to a light, juicy meatball is DON'T OVERMIX! Overworking the mixture can lead to dense meatballs.
           </div>
