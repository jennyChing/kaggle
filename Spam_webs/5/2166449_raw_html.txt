      <h1>
       A Good Pac-Man Is Hard To Find: Spadina Bust
      </h1>
     </header>
     <p class="dek">
     </p>
     <p class="byline">
      By
      <a class="url fn" href="http://torontoist.com/author/toronto_david/" rel="author" title="Posts by David Topping">
       David Topping
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="052608pac-man2_3.jpg" height="474" src="http://torontoist.com/attachments/toronto_david/052608pac-man2_3.jpg" width="640"/>
      <br/>
      <img alt="052608pac-man2_5.jpg" height="375" src="http://torontoist.com/attachments/toronto_david/052608pac-man2_5.jpg" width="640"/>
      <br/>
      <img alt="052608pac-man2_4.jpg" height="361" src="http://torontoist.com/attachments/toronto_david/052608pac-man2_4.jpg" width="640"/>
      <br/>
      We should have known! The culprits responsible for
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2008/05/pac_man_feasts_on_ttc_shelter.php">
       the Pac-Man recreation on a streetcar shelter outside Kensington Market
      </a>
      have stepped forward, and theyre neither advertisers nor
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2008/05/pac_man_feasts_on_ttc_shelter.php#comment-1370765">
       immoralists
      </a>
      : theyre Teeth (responsible for
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2008/04/vandalist_bear.php">
       this charming bear-woman
      </a>
      ) and
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/profile/Posterchild/posts">
       our very own
      </a>
      <a href="http://www.bladediary.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bladediary.com/', 'Posterchild');">
       Posterchild
      </a>
      (who is something of a
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2008/03/for_the_good_of.php">
       fan
      </a>
      of
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2007/12/one_more_poster_1.php">
       video games
      </a>
      ).
      <br/>
      The two collaborated to put up three nearly identical installations along Spadina: one at Spadina and Harbord (pictured above, at top); one at Spadina and Queen (above, bottom); and the one we spotted on Sunday, at Spadina south of Nassau (above, middle). The three ghosts, three cherries, and three Pac-Men were made out of scrap adhesive vinyl, took a grand total of five hours to build and install, and, says Posterchild, were totally Teeths idea. Says Posterchild: When Teeth told me about it, I said Thats brilliant! Lets do it!' And they did, and it was good.
      <br/>
      <em>
       All photos courtesy of Posterchild.
      </em>
     </p>
