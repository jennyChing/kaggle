         <h1 class="entry-title">
          <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/saints-row-4-a-review/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Saints Row IV: A Review">
           Saints Row IV: A Review
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="on-date">
           Posted on
           <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/saints-row-4-a-review/" rel="bookmark" title="7:41 pm">
            <time class="entry-date" datetime="2013-08-22T19:41:34+00:00">
             August 22, 2013
            </time>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="by-author">
           by
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/author/andrewkorkmas/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Andrew Korkmas">
             Andrew Korkmas
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Let me tell you a magical tale about the
          <em>
           Saints Row
          </em>
          series.
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000BLM5PG/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000BLM5PG&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boom0e0-20">
           The first
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=boom0e0-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000BLM5PG" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          was a generic
          <em>
           Grand Theft Auto
          </em>
          knock-off and thus isnt worth talking about.
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000ZKDOV2/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000ZKDOV2&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boom0e0-20">
           The second
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=boom0e0-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000ZKDOV2" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          saw how
          <em>
           Grand Theft Auto 4
          </em>
          was caught up in being realistic and decided to keep things less realistic and add in a bit of wackiness in both the narrative and gameplay options  for example, you could drive septic tanks and spew their contents on enemies.
         </p>
         <p>
          Then came
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004QEV0MI/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004QEV0MI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boom0e0-20">
           Saints Row: The Third
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=boom0e0-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B004QEV0MI" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          , a game so bonkers and fun, it was almost criminally so. This was the kind of game in which you could either strive to play through the story that parodies other popular video games and movies, play through the immense variety of different missions such as surviving a zombie outbreak or doing a variety of bizarre tasks for guy with a cat head  dont ask  or just go bananas and go on a killing spree as most people do in these kinds of games.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Saints-Row-iv.jpg">
           <img alt="Saints-Row-iv" class="size-full wp-image-5837 aligncenter" height="240" src="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Saints-Row-iv.jpg" width="427"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          At this point, Volition (the developers of
          <em>
           Saints Row
          </em>
          ) came up with the idea of making a dlc story chapter called Enter the Dominatrix which would be largely a parody of
          <em>
           The Matrix
          </em>
          and would revolve around your character being stuck in a fake reality where he had super powers and would try to escape. But then two things happened:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Though Steven Colbert was originally going be the president of US in the game, it was changed so that the player character would be instead. And
          </li>
          <li>
           THQ kinda went bankrupt. This lead publisher Deep Silver to acquire the rights to the
           <em>
            Saints Row
           </em>
           series and they decided that instead of making DLC, it would instead be the next iteration in the series.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Saints_Row_4_13633589119487.jpg">
           <img alt="Saints_Row_4_13633589119487" class="size-full wp-image-5838 aligncenter" height="232" src="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Saints_Row_4_13633589119487.jpg" width="412"/>
          </a>
          Youre probably wondering why I just spent a good two paragraphs detailing a bunch of things anyone could look up on Wikipedia. That question also has a two part answer:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           We all know you wouldnt have bothered to look any of this up. And
          </li>
          <li>
           After playing
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00BRQN2EM/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00BRQN2EM&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boom0e0-20">
            Saints Row IV
           </a>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=boom0e0-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00BRQN2EM" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
           , you can really tell it was once just DLC and not its own stand-alone entry in a series.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          After booting up the game, I marveled at the games graphics, textures and lighting. I did so because all three were beyond hideous. It doesnt matter whether its in-game or cut-scene graphics. Both make the game feel dated and the poor lighting and an abundance of ugly textures dont help the matter either.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5848" style="width: 256px">
          <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/SaintsRowIV.jpg">
           <img alt="Game cover from Wikipedia." class="size-full wp-image-5848" height="336" src="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/SaintsRowIV.jpg" width="246"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Game cover from
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:SaintsRowIV.jpg">
            Wikipedia
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </div>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          I dont particularly remember how good or bad
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004QEV0MI/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004QEV0MI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boom0e0-20">
           Saints Row: The Third
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=boom0e0-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B004QEV0MI" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          s graphics and textures were, so I cant make a direct comparison but its worth noting that it came out in 2010, not 2013. What mightve looked good or even passable back then does not necessarily look favorable three years later.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/saints-row-4-04.jpg">
           <img alt="saints-row-4-04" class="size-full wp-image-5839 aligncenter" height="206" src="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/saints-row-4-04.jpg" width="367"/>
          </a>
          The first mission had me infiltrating a terrorist compound with a group of ai partners in what is a parody of
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00B1E6FF8/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00B1E6FF8&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boom0e0-20">
           Zero Dark Thirty
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=boom0e0-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00B1E6FF8" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          . I only know that it was a parody of it because of the trophy that pops up at completion. This mission was extremely boring. It felt like it was being generic and as if it was a lame attempt to be ironic as if they dont understand the meaning of the word. The mission was also loaded with quick-time events, all of which are horribly implemented, again as if to trying to be ironic but also as if they completely missed the point of irony.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          After this, I was given the chance to create my own character using the notorious in-game character creation system. While I was enjoying this, I realized something that really made negative impact on my experience, and that is that with the exception of option to have your character be voiced by Nolan North (which is totally awesome and is also exactly what I did) the character creation system was relatively unchanged from that of
          <em>
           Saints Row: The Third
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Saints-Row-4-4.jpg">
           <img alt="Saints-Row-4-4" class="size-full wp-image-5840 aligncenter" height="235" src="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Saints-Row-4-4.jpg" width="413"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          Though not as bad as the first mission, the second didnt fare much better either. I was now president and had to protect the White House from an alien invasion.
          <strong>
           SPOILERS:
          </strong>
          It doesnt work. The protecting, not the invasion. The invasion part works relatively well.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          It was at this point where I realized something else,
          <em>
           Saints Row 4
          </em>
          isnt very funny. There had been maybe a light chuckle or two from me at this point, but I looked back on how the mere opening of
          <em>
           Saints Row: The Third
          </em>
          caused me to erupt in laughter almost entire way through.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/deadpool-a-review/">
           It has the same problem as the Deadpool game I reviewed not too long ago
          </a>
          . Yeah, there are some funny moments, but theres simply not enough of them. The moments could be wackier and theres far too much low-brow humor that revolves around swear words and fecal matter and the like. Also, there were still more pointless quicktime events to be found.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          When I finally got to the open-world city, my inner urge to create chaos was at an all-time high so I went on a killing spree. It was at this point where I started to notice that
          <em>
           Saints Row 4
          </em>
          is glitchy and not in the fun sort of way. The sound would drop out at random times and at others it would persist when it shouldnt. My mix-tape song kept going even after I got out of cars but then when I got back in a car the mix-tape refused to play.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          Whenever I got in hectic firefights, the sound would start to drop out and so most shots werent heard. It was also around this time where I realized that the vehicle controls are horrible. This is bizarre seeing as the vehicle controls in both
          <em>
           Saints Row 2
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           Saints Row: The Third
          </em>
          were among the best Ive ever seen in video-games. Why they felt the need to change them, I do not know but, then again, youll probably be super sprinting instead of using vehicles in this game.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <a href="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Saints-Row-4-drama.png">
           <img alt="Saints-Row-4-drama" class="size-full wp-image-5841 aligncenter" height="243" src="http://boompopmedia.com/index/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Saints-Row-4-drama.png" width="434"/>
          </a>
          Speaking of super powers, about two hours after playing the game, I finally got them. Yeah you heard me right. Two hours of gameplay to get to the big addition. But I suppose Ive spent enough of this review complaining so Ill just say the following, playing
          <em>
           Saints Row 4
          </em>
          with super powers is heck of a lot of fun.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          All the different melees you can do are sick. Jumping super high and chaining jumps to scale buildings along with super-speed adds to the free-flow style of play. You know, the style of play that the series is known for and it does make it stand out from its predecessor. It really does make me wonder why it takes so long to get to this.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          The addition of super powers was a great move and while you can still do a lot of the same fun stuff you could in the last game, this seems to come at the price of almost no comedic charm, vastly outdated visuals, glitches, and lots of God-awful quick-time events that never go away.
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <strong>
           Conclusion: 7.5/10
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p class="MsoNormal">
          <em>
           All of the images in this article are from the game Saints Row 4. Given the artistic criticism contained in this article, we will assume that our use of the images constitutes Fair Use. None of the above parties are affiliated with or endorse BoomPopMedia.
