          <h2>
           Gary Anderson Beats Stokes Phil Taylor To Win PDC Darts World Championship
          </h2>
          <p>
           Gary Anderson has won his first PDC World Championship after defeating Stoke-on-Trents Phil Taylor last night in a thrilling match which could have gone either way at the Alexandra Palace in London.
          </p>
          <p>
           The 16 time world champion Taylor was behind for most of the match after Anderson romped to an early 3-1 lead and saw his usual ability to hit the doubles fade, which would cost him dear.
          </p>
          <p>
           Taylor managed to level the score at 4 sets all after winning 9 out of a possible 10 legs. Anderson then watched in horror as his 3rd dart knocked his previous 2 darts out of the treble 20 to leave him with 0 score. Taylor then missed out on the chance for this first ever world championship 9 dart finish after he narrowly missed the double.
          </p>
          <p>
           Anderson took the next 2 sets to bring it to 6-4 but The Power wasnt giving up that easily and brought the match back to 6-6 to bring it to the deciding final set.
          </p>
          <p>
           Anderson, 44, won the first leg of the decider and when Taylor missed double 16 in the next, the Scot capitalised to win the 250,000 first prize.
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            Doubles cost me a couple of sets and he took advantage
            <br/>
             Phil The Power Taylor
           </p>
          </blockquote>
