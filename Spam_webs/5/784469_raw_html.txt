        <p>
         I used to hate oatmeal. I mean, really hate it. Gummy, goopy-looking mess of a bowl. I saw no appeal in it. But then again, all I knew was the instant oatmeal you found in the packages.
        </p>
        <p>
         We would buy the instant oatmeal in bulk size at Costco, and Dan would have one every day for breakfast. Soon owyn was eating them and as I had always made her homemade baby food, I wanted something better for her (aka, knowing what exactly was in her food.) I decided to look into making
         <a href="http://thecookiewriter.com/2014/01/homemade-oatmeal.html" target="_blank" title="Homemade Oatmeal">
          homemade oatmeal
         </a>
         and was SUPER surprised at how easy it is! I mean I was floored. From that moment on, it was homemade oatmeal all the way!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thecookiewriter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Homemade-Granola-Recipe-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Homemade Granola Recipe 2" class="size-full wp-image-2801 aligncenter" height="453" src="http://thecookiewriter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Homemade-Granola-Recipe-2.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         But sometimes you can only eat so much of one meal before you kind of get bored with it. Dan began making these awesome homemade instant oatmeal bowls, which I need to get around to publishing. I decided it was high-time to graduate to a more sophisticated oat recipe and found myself making a homemade granola recipe!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thecookiewriter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Homemade-Granola-Recipe-4.jpg">
          <img alt="Homemade Granola Recipe 4" class="size-full wp-image-2803 aligncenter" height="1020" src="http://thecookiewriter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Homemade-Granola-Recipe-4.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It was a lot of fun, and once again, so easy! The crunchy texture is perfect to pair with yogurt or other desserts, or you can go very simple like my daughter does and just add a touch of milk to the granola. Dan has become partial to adding a tablespoon or so of granola to his oatmeal in the morning for added crunch. Whatever you decide to do with your granola, just know you will be pleasantly surprised at how easy it is to make!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thecookiewriter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Homemade-Granola-Recipe-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Homemade Granola Recipe 1" class="size-full wp-image-2800 aligncenter" height="453" src="http://thecookiewriter.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Homemade-Granola-Recipe-1.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
         <br/>

           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups old-fashioned oats
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup almonds, chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup sunflower seeds
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. ground cinnamon
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp.ground ginger
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. nutmeg
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup coconut oil, melted
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup maple syrup (or  cup honey,  cup maple syrup for non-vegans.)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. vanilla
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 350F. Line baking sheet with parchment paper.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Mix together the oats, almonds, salt, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and sunflower seeds in a large bowl.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Combine the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla. Drizzle over dry ingredients and mix until well combined.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spread out onto the baking sheet and bake for 10 minutes. Give the ingredients a toss and cook for another 10-15 minutes, or until lightly golden.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Allow to cool on cookie sheet and once they have firmed up nicely, transfer to an airtight container.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">

         </div>

        <p>
         Another wonderful thing about this homemade granola recipe is howeasy it is to customize. I am sure you have seen hundreds of granola recipes out there, all with a unique twist on the last. With the pumpkin season upon us, do not be surprised if you begin seeing home autumn inspired granola recipes! Seriously though, I consider this a basic granola recipe so you can really spice things up from here.
        </p>
