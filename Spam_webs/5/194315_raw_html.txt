        <h3>
         Leviticus 5
        </h3>
       </td>
       <td bgcolor="eeeeee">
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="1">
          5:1
         </a>
        </b>
        And if a soul sin, and hear the voice of swearing, and is a witness,
    whether he hath seen or known of it; if he do not utter it, then he shall
    bear his iniquity.
       </td>
       <td bgcolor="eeeeee" rowspan="5">
        <font size="-1">
         <img src="../images/abs1.gif"/>
         <img src="../images/sex1.gif"/>
         (5:2-4) Things that make you unclean and guilty 
(like touching a dead bug or cow, or "the uncleanness of man" (?!), or 
swearing to do something good).
         <p>
          (5:2)
          <i>
           "If a soul touch any unclean
    thing, whether it be a carcase of an unclean beast, or a carcase
    of unclean cattle, or the carcase of unclean creeping things, and
    if it be hidden from him; he also shall be unclean, and guilty."
          </i>
         </p>
         <p>
          (5:3)
          <b>
           "Or if he touch the uncleanness of man ... he shall be guilty."
          </b>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          (5:4)
          <i>
           "Or if a soul swear, pronouncing with his lips to do evil, or to do good ...
he shall be guilty."
          </i>
          <br/>
         </p>
        </font>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="2">
          5:2
         </a>
        </b>
        Or
        <span class="a">
         if a soul touch any unclean thing, whether it be a carcase of an unclean beast, or a carcase
    of unclean cattle, or the carcase of unclean creeping things, and
    if it be hidden from him; he also shall be unclean, and guilty.
        </span>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="3">
          5:3
         </a>
        </b>
        <span class="a">
         Or if he touch the uncleanness of man
        </span>
        , whatsoever uncleanness it be that a man shall be defiled withal,
    and it be hid from him; when he knoweth of it, then
        <span class="a">
         he shall be guilty.
        </span>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="4">
          5:4
         </a>
        </b>
        <span class="a">
         Or if a soul swear, pronouncing with his lips to do evil, or to do good
        </span>
        ,
    whatsoever it be that a man shall pronounce with an oath, and it be hid from
    him; when he knoweth of it, then
        <span class="a">
         he shall be guilty
        </span>
        in one of these.
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="5">
          5:5
         </a>
        </b>
        And it shall be, when he shall be guilty in one of these things, that he
    shall confess that he hath sinned in that thing:
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="6">
          5:6
         </a>
        </b>
        And
        <span class="v">
         he shall bring his trespass offering unto the LORD
        </span>
        for his sin which
    he hath sinned,
        <span class="v">
         a female from the flock, a lamb or a kid of the goats
        </span>
        , for a
    sin offering; and the priest shall make an atonement for him concerning his
    sin.
       </td>
       <td bgcolor="eeeeee" rowspan="6">
        <font size="-1">
         <img src="../images/cr1.gif"/>
         <img src="../images/abs1.gif"/>
         <img src="../images/wom1.gif"/>
         (5:6-9) If you touch an insect, dead animal, or "the uncleanness of man" or if you swear to do something good 
or bad, kill a female lamb or goat for God (A female will do since it's a minor offense.) 
and sprinkle the blood around. If you don't have a lamb to kill, then you can wring off the head of a pigeon or dove.
         <p>
          (5:6)
          <i>
           "He shall bring his trespass offering unto the LORD ... 
a female from the flock, a lamb or a kid of the goats."
          </i>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          (5:7-8)
          <i>
           "If he be not able to bring a lamb, then he shall bring ... 
two turtledoves, or two young pigeons, unto the LORD ... and wring off his head."
          </i>
         </p>
         <p>
          (5:9)
          <i>
           "And he shall sprinkle of the blood of the sin offering upon the side of the altar; and the rest of
    the blood shall be wrung out at the bottom of the altar: it is a sin offering."
          </i>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img src="../images/contra1.gif"/>
          (5:10, 16, 18)
          <i>
           "The priest
    shall make an atonement for him for his sin which he hath sinned, and it
    shall be forgiven him."
          </i>
          <br/>
          <a href="../contra/blood.html">
           Does God desire animal sacrifices?
          </a>
         </p>
        </font>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="7">
          5:7
         </a>
        </b>
        And
        <span class="v">
         if he be not able to bring a lamb, then he shall bring
        </span>
        for his
    trespass, which he hath committed,
        <span class="v">
         two turtledoves, or two young pigeons, unto the LORD
        </span>
        ; one for
    a sin offering, and the other for a burnt offering.
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="8">
          5:8
         </a>
        </b>
        And he shall bring them unto the priest, who shall offer that which is for
    the sin offering first, and
        <span class="v">
         wring off his head
        </span>
        from his neck, 
    but shall not divide it asunder:
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="9">
          5:9
         </a>
        </b>
        <span class="v">
         And he shall sprinkle of
    the blood of the sin offering upon the side of the altar; and the rest of
    the blood shall be wrung out at the bottom of the altar: it is a sin
    offering.
        </span>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="10">
          5:10
         </a>
        </b>
        And he shall offer the second for a burnt offering, according to the
    manner:
        <span class="c">
         and the priest
    shall make an atonement for him for his sin which he hath sinned, and it
    shall be forgiven him.
        </span>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="11">
          5:11
         </a>
        </b>
        But if he be not able to bring two turtledoves, or two young pigeons, then
    he that sinned shall bring for his offering the tenth part of an ephah of
    fine flour for a sin offering; he shall put no oil upon it, neither shall he
    put any frankincense thereon: for it is a sin offering.
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="12">
          5:12
         </a>
        </b>
        Then shall he bring it to the priest, and the priest shall take his
    handful of it, even a memorial thereof, and burn it on the altar, according
    to the offerings made by fire unto the LORD: it is a sin offering.
       </td>
       <td bgcolor="eeeeee">
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="13">
          5:13
         </a>
        </b>
        And the priest shall make an atonement for him as touching his sin that he
    hath sinned in one of these, and it shall be forgiven him: and the remnant
    shall be the priest's, as a meat offering.
       </td>
       <td bgcolor="eeeeee" rowspan="3" valign="top">
        <font size="-1">
         <img src="../images/abs1.gif"/>
         <img src="../images/inj1.gif"/>
         <img src="../images/cr1.gif"/>
         (5:14-18)
         <br/>
         If you sin without knowing that you've done anything wrong, kill an unblemished ram for God.
         <p>
          (5:14-15)
          <i>
           "The Lord spake unto Moses, saying, If a soul ... sin through ignorance ... then he shall bring for his 
trespass unto the LORD a ram without blemish."
          </i>
         </p>
        </font>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="14">
          5:14
         </a>
        </b>
        And
        <span class="a">
         the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,
        </span>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="15">
          5:15
         </a>
        </b>
        <span class="a">
         If a soul
        </span>
        commit a trespass, and
        <span class="a">
         sin through ignorance
        </span>
        , in the holy things of the
    LORD;
        <span class="v">
         then he shall bring for his trespass unto the LORD a ram without
    blemish
        </span>
        out of the flocks, with thy estimation by shekels of silver, after
    the shekel of the sanctuary, for a trespass offering.
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="16">
          5:16
         </a>
        </b>
        And he shall make amends for the harm that he hath done in the holy thing,
    and shall add the fifth part thereto, and give it unto the priest:
        <span class="c">
         and the 
    priest shall make an atonement for him with the ram of the trespass offering, and it shall be
    forgiven him.
        </span>
       </td>
       <td bgcolor="eeeeee" rowspan="3">
        <font size="-1">
         (5:16)
         <i>
          "And the priest shall make an atonement for him with the ram of the trespass offering, and it shall be forgiven him."
         </i>
         <p>
          <img src="../images/abs1.gif"/>
          <img src="../images/contra1.gif"/>
          (5:17)
          <i>
           "If a soul sin ... though he wist it not, yet is he guilty, and shall bear his iniquity."
          </i>
          <br/>
          If you sin without knowing it, you are still guilty.
          <br/>
          <a href="../contra/ignorance.html">
           What should you do if you sin through ignorance?
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          (5:18)
          <i>
           "And the priest shall make an atonement for him concerning his ignorance wherein he erred and wist it not, 
and it shall be forgiven him."
          </i>
         </p>
        </font>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <b>
         <a name="17">
          5:17
         </a>
        </b>
        <span class="i">
         And if a soul sin
        </span>
        , and
    commit any of these things which are forbidden to be done by the
    commandments of the LORD;
        <span class="a">
         though he wist it not,
        </span>
        <span class="i">
         yet is he guilty, and shall 
    bear his iniquity.
        </span>
       </td>

    estimation, for a trespass offering, unto the priest:
        <span class="c">
