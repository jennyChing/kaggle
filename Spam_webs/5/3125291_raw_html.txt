           </span>
           Billion

He would go on to create one of the largest shipping and railroad empires the world had ever seen.

          </p>
          <h2>
           #2.Andrew Carnegie  $
           <span style="color: #339966;">
            309
           </span>
           Billion
          </h2>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5207" style="width: 749px">
           <img alt="the top 10 richest men in history" class=" wp-image-5207" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/quickshott.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/richest-8.jpg?resize=739%2C474"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            source
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Source Of Wealth:
           </strong>
           After Immigrating to the United States with his parents,Andrew Carnegie started out work as a telegrapher. By the 1860s, Carnegie had saved up enough money to begin investing in railroads, bridges and oil derricks, and had began accumulating further wealth as a bond salesman raiding money for American enterprise in Europe. He is most well known for the Carnegie steel company that he would eventually sell to J.P. Morgan in 1901 for $480 Million or $13.6 Billion with inflation.
          </p>
          <p>
           Aside from his vast wealth, Carnegie is known as one of the highest profile philanthropists in history after having donated almost 90% off his fortune to charities. He is credited with stimulating a wave of philanthropy in the U.S. stemming from his 1889 article titled The Gospel of Wealth in which he called on the rich to use their wealth to improve society.
          </p>
          <h2>
           #1.John D. Rockefeller $
           <span style="color: #339966;">
            336
           </span>
           Billion
          </h2>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5219" style="width: 634px">

             source
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Source Of Wealth:
           </strong>
           Rockefeller co-founded the Standard Oil Company in 1870 along with his brother William and other business partners. At his peak, Rockefeller controlled an astounding 90% of all oil in the United States. His fortune upon death in 1937 stood at $336 billion (with inflation), which at the time accounted for roughly 1.5% of the national economy.
          </p>
