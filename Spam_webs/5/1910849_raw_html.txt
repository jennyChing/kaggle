       <h1 class="entry-title">
        5 Vital Questions To Ask At Your First Prenatal Visit
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         To ensure that you and your baby will be safe throughout the birthing process, prenatal visits are both important and necessary. Typically taking place around the 8th week of pregnancy, your first prenatal visit will help your healthcare professional review your health history, detail a pregnancy plan and outline your estimated due date. To help get a solid grasp on your pregnancy, it can be helpful to come to your initial prenatal visit with a handful of questions youd like answered. Here are five of the top questions to ask at your first prenatal visit.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <img alt="prenatal visit" class="alignnone wp-image-7194 size-full" height="552" src="https://www.corcell.com/wp-content/uploads/prenatal-visit.png" title="questions to ask prenatal visit" width="825"/>
        </h2>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          1. What does prenatal care consist of?
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Many women are unaware of what actually takes place during prenatal visits. To get a feel for what goes on during prenatal care, ask your healthcare physician for a breakdown of the proposed schedule and prenatal tests that will be taking place during each visit. By understanding what is upcoming in your prenatal schedule, you can prepare for each visit with valuable questions and concerns about future treatments.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          2. What is your birthing philosophy?
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         One of the most important things to determine early on in your prenatal visits is your physicians approach to birthing and their approved situations for delivering newborns. Find out what your providers birth rates have been for situations such as Cesarean births, inductions past due dates and other unconventional birthing scenarios. While it still may be early to choose a side on the debates concerning pain medications, birth positions and breastfeeding, dont be afraid to start having the conversation with your physician.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          3. What to do if experiencing cramping and bleeding in the first trimester?
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         At your initial prenatal visit, find out what your provider considers to be an emergency. While some doctors may not be able to do anything for the first 20 weeks of pregnancy, a reassurance that the cramping and bleeding that you are experiencing is normal can go a long way. Cramping and bleeding during the early stages of pregnancy can be scary, which is why it is important to find a physician that can help quell your worries in times of need.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          4. Do I need to make any significant lifestyle changes?
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Although general pregnancy guidelines suggest that smoking, drinking and poor eating habits should be eliminated, your provider may have some other lifestyle changes that they suggest you follow. If you already exercise frequently, your physician can either approve your current workout regimen or determine an exercise schedule that is better for the health of you and your baby. Also, be sure to discuss any medications and supplements you are currently taking with your physician to see if they can be taken over the course of your pregnancy.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          5. What are the best ways to handle uncomfortablepregnancy symptoms?
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         One of the most difficult parts of pregnancy is dealing with the fatigue, morning sickness and other symptoms associated with giving birth to a newborn. Fortunately, your healthcare physician can assure you that everything is normal and offer assistance for methods for dealing with painful and distracting pregnancy symptoms.
        </p>
        <p>
         For any concerns you may be having about your pregnancy, prenatal visits are the ideal time to speak with a trained professional about potential issues and problems you are facing. For more information about questions to ask during your prenatal visits or to learn about the many benefits of
         <a href="https://www.corcell.com/">
          cord blood banking
         </a>
         , give us a call today at (702) 914-7250.
        </p>
