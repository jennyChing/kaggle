        <p>
         "
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/news/star-wars/" target="_hplink">
          Star Wars
         </a>
         " fans have more than just a new movie to look forward to next year. Disney says it will announce major plans for more "Star Wars" in the company's theme parks.
        </p>
        <p>
         Disney CEO Bob Iger said during an earnings call on Tuesday that the company is "
         <a href="http://www.reuters.com/article/2014/08/05/disney-results-starwars-idUSL2N0QB2J220140805" target="_hplink">
          developing designs for a far greater 'Star Wars' presence in the parks
         </a>
         ," according to multiple reports.
        </p>
        <p>
         When we grow Star Wars'' presence, which we will do significantly,
         <a href="http://variety.com/2014/biz/news/significant-star-wars-presence-planned-for-disney-theme-parks-1201276296/" target="_hplink">
          you will see better bets being made
         </a>
         that will pay off for us than were made in the past, Iger said, according to Variety.
        </p>
        <p>
         Details of the expansion will not be revealed until next year.
        </p>
        <p>
         Jay Rasulo, Disney's finance chief and senior executive vice president, said attractions based on major franchises draw guests.
        </p>
        <p>
         "They pull people from, 'Gee, I'm gonna go someday' to '
         <a href="http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/envelope/cotown/la-et-ct-star-wars-to-be-prominently-featured-at-disney-attractions-20140805-story.html" target="_hplink">
          I'm gonna go this year
         </a>
         ,'" Rasulo said, according to the Los Angeles Times.
        </p>
        <p>
         "Star Wars" already has a significant presence in Disney parks around the world. The "
         <a href="https://disneyworld.disney.go.com/attractions/hollywood-studios/star-tours/" target="_hplink">
          Star Tours
         </a>
         " simulator ride is in Disney's Hollywood Studios in Florida, Disneyland in California, Tokyo Disneyland and Disneyland Paris. The Florida and California parks also have an interactive show called "
         <a href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Jedi_Training_Academy" target="_hplink">
          Jedi Training Academy
         </a>
         ."
        </p>
        <p>
         In addition, Disney's Hollywood Studios plays host to "Star Wars Weekends," an annual event featuring shows, parades and a massive store called "Darth's Mall."
        </p>
        <p>
         Rumors of a major "Star Wars" expansion or even the creation of a "Star Wars Land" in one or more Disney parks have been a mainstay of the fan community for years, intensifying in 2012
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/10/30/disney-buys-lucasfilm-star-wars-7_n_2045632.html" target="_hplink">
          when the company acquired Lucasfilm
         </a>
         and announced new movies in the franchise.
        </p>
        <p>
         Last year, Disney
         <a href="http://www.theforce.net/story/front/Orange_Harvest_D23_Hints_At_Disneys_Plans_For_New_Star_Wars_Theme_Park_Experiences_153610.asp" target="_hplink">
          teased a "Star Wars" presence in its parks at the company's D23 expo
         </a>
         with crates marked "Orange Harvest"
         <a href="http://www.insidethemagic.net/2013/08/star-wars-attractions-teased-for-disney-parks-at-2013-d23-expo-imagineers-hint-at-whats-to-come/" target="_hplink">
          and partially visible blueprints
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         In other "Star Wars" news, Iger said Tuesday that "Episode VII" is on schedule for release on Dec. 18, 2015, but didn't discuss the injury to Harrison Ford that
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/07/07/harrison-ford-star-wars-delay_n_5563232.html" target="_hplink">
          reportedly delayed filming
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         "The footage weve seen so far is spectacular
         <a href="http://popwatch.ew.com/2014/08/05/disney-theme-parks-star-wars/" target="_hplink">
          and certainly worthy of the fan frenzy and excitement this movie is generating around the world
         </a>
         ," Iger said, according to Entertainment Weekly.
        </p>
