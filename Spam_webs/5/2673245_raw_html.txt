        <p>
         When it comes to owning some of the best watches, it usually boils down to
         <a href="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/best-affordable-watches/">
          what you can afford
         </a>
         and what exactly it is youll be getting for your money. Whether its a casual watch or a luxury watch, its all about how much youre willing to pay and how the watch you want fits with your personal needs.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the world of watches, complexity and price go hand in hand. So the more you are willing to pay for a watch, the more choices youll have. And believe us, there is an overwhelming amount of choices when it comes to some of the best watches from around the world.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are a few important questions youll want to ask yourself when buying a watch:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Whats your overall budget or how much are you willing to pay?
         </li>
         <li>
          How often will you be wearing it?
         </li>
         <li>
          What will you be wearing your watch for? (sports, special occasion, work)
         </li>
         <li>
          Do you want a high-tech gadget watch or a more traditional watch?
         </li>
         <li>
          Do you have a watch brand in mind?
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         For some of you, looking at the list of questions may have brought a chuckle to your belly. We understand that purchasing a watch isnt like buying a new car or a house. Most of us just buy our watches on impulse right there in the store. But keep in mind, a lot of the best watches are well out of the average consumers price range. Some costing more than cars and houses.
        </p>
        <p>
         No matter the price though, when you find yourself asking these questions, more may bubble up during the thought process. Like are you going to buy a quartz or mechanical watch? Do you want a digital or analog watch? Or maybe a combination of both digital and analog?
        </p>

         to fit your everyday needs and the type of lifestyle you have.
        </p>

         </li>
         <li>
          Fashion
         </li>
         <li>
          Luxury
         </li>
         <li>
          Sport
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Watches fall into five basic categories and there is considerable overlap within those classifications of the different watch styles.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Casual watches
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         A watch that is designed for everyday use and typically compliments your overall style and comfort. Casual watches are ideal timepieces that you wear on the weekend or at a job that has casual work dress.
        </p>
        <p>
         Casual watches are often inexpensive and quite affordable. And since these are the type of watches that you would wear on an everyday basis, its important to find one that is built with comfort in mind.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Fashion watches
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         When it comes to the latest trends, fashion watches are the timepieces that boast stylish materials that help them to stand out.Choosing a fashion watch is less about coordinating with your clothes and more about picking a style that is an extension of your personality.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fashion watches are meant to be stylish accessories as well as being a timepiece.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Dress watches
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         When youre looking for a more sophisticated approach to style, it is dress watches that provide you with the added level of class. When you think about dress watches, think formal and evening wear. Think weddings and dinner parties.
        </p>
        <p>
         A dress watch is meant to make a suit look better and add more to your glamorous style. Also, what is appropriate for a dress situation is very subjective. For some jobs, wearing a bright orange watch with a three piece suit would be very appropriate. For others, it wouldnt. But there are some factors to consider when selecting a watch to wear at work. If you are in a job that requires that you be able to easily read your watch, then the readability of the watch face is a consideration as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         The width of watch can affect how well it fits under your dress shirt sleeves. Or you might intentionally choose a larger watch so it stands out. Diamonds or gemstones might be appropriate for more formal situations. And the type of watch band is a consideration as well.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Sport watches
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         These
         <a href="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/best-selling-sport-watches-for-men/">
          types of timepieces compliment an active lifestyle
         </a>
         . Their designed to be tough yet still stylish. And most of all, they are built to be useful. Whether running, swimming or hiking, your sport watch should be an additional tool to your overall success.
        </p>
        <p>
         Water resistance, shock resistance, and features such as timers, alarms, and stopwatch functions are some of the considerations when looking for a watch to use during sporting activities. Sometimes a digital watch would be more helpful than an analog one. The bezel, the rotating dial surrounding the face of the watch, can be useful in some sporting situations.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Luxury watches
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Prestige and precision are two of the words that come to mind when speaking of luxury watches. These are the watches that require a bit of money to afford them. And the more money you invest, the more heads youll turn with these type of watches.
        </p>
        <p>
         When you
         <a href="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/best-selling-luxury-watches-for-men/">
          choose a high-end luxury watch
         </a>
         , you are choosing the highest quality materials, craftsmanship, and in some cases, exclusivity.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Should I buy a Quartz or Mechanical watch?
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         It all depends as there are differences between Quartz and Mechanical watches. Well explain the differences for each one so that you have a better understanding of which type of watch is for you.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          What is a quartz watch exactly?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         The majority of watches being manufactured today are quartz watches. Its most likely that if you own a watch, its probably a quartz watch. They are much more simplistic than mechanical watches but quartz technology helps to provide an extremely accurate measure of time.
        </p>
        <p>
         The quartz crystal within a watch is powered by the watchs battery. Together, the two create a frequency that maintains an extremely accurate measurement of time.
         <i>
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Quartz Watches:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Generally less expensive.
         </li>
         <li>
          Easy to maintain.
         </li>
         <li>
          Very accurate (typical accuracy is 15+/- seconds per month)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          So what is a mechanical watch?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Mechanical watches, also known as wind-up watches, keep accurate time by using a spring mechanism rather than a battery. Although mechanical watches are not as common as quartz watches, some of the best watches in the world are self-winding timepieces.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Mechanical Watches:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Battery free.
         </li>
         <li>
          Self-winding (automatic) watches are highly convenient.
         </li>
         <li>
          Prestige and traditional.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Features to look for in a watch
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/What-are-the-best-watches-image-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Watch complications" class="alignleft wp-image-120" height="212" src="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/What-are-the-best-watches-image-2.jpg" width="212"/>
         </a>
         There is an extraordinary variety of watches available today and timepieces can cost tens of thousands of dollars or twenty dollars. The interesting thing is that even an inexpensive watch can look great, be incredibly accurate, and be of surprising quality. Watch collectors often have a wide range of watches in their collection with some that are very valuable and some that are not, but are of quality and just plain fun.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are some things to look for in any watch that can increase your chances of having a great experience with a watch that will last you a long time.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Watch movement
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         A watchs movement provides the energy that keeps it going. Typically, a watch will have either a quartz or mechanical movement.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whatever the cost of the watch, it will have either a quartz movement or a mechanical movement. Mechanical movements were the way the first watches were powered. A spinning balance wheel powered by a tightly wound spring provides the power. The spring is wound automatically or by hand, essentially storing energy and releasing it as the spring unwinds.
        </p>
        <p>
         The energy feeds into a series of gears that convert energy into movement, with each gear turning one of the watchs hands. The escapement controls the pace at which the spring unwinds and uses pins, wheels, pivots, teeth, and its own spring to keep time. They are very complicated and often expensive.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Quartz movement in watches
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A quartz movement is powered by electricity, usually generated by a battery. It is called a quartz movement because a small quartz crystal is integrated into the electronics. Why? When you run a current through a quartz crystal, it oscillates very close to a perfectly constant frequency.
        </p>
        <p>
         The quartz crystal is typically shaped like a tuning fork and it vibrates at a frequency of 32,768 Hz. A better quartz watch by a known manufacturer may be accurate to within a few seconds a year.Quartz movements have been produced since the 1960s and can be extremely accurate, mass produced, and relatively inexpensive. Good movements can come from Japan or Switzerland.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           How mechanical watches work
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mechanical watches store energy in a spring which is wound automatically or by hand. The energy feeds into a series of gears that converts the energy into movement, with each gear turning one of the watchs hands.
        </p>
        <p>
         The escapement controls the pace at which the spring unwinds and uses pins, wheels, pivots, teeth, and its own spring to keep time. They are very complicated and hence, expensive.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <strong>
           Some countries specialize in watch movements
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Several countries have specialized in watch movements. Historically, most watch movements were designed and manufactured in Switzerland and Swiss watchmakers have set the standard for quality, especially for mechanical movements.
        </p>
        <p>
         But since the 1980s, Recently, Japanese-made movements have equaled to the Swiss in quality. Also, the quality of Chinese-made movements has increased in recent years.
        </p>
        <p>
         If a watch says Swiss-made, is has a special designation. A Swiss-made movement must have Swiss parts, be fully assembled in Switzerland and be placed in the case at a Swiss factory. The case itself does not need to be manufactured in Switzerland. That designation can dramatically increase the cost of a watch.
        </p>
        <p>
         Japanese quartz movements have a long and successful history and have been producing good watches since the 1960s. Japanese quartz watches have been the official timekeeping watch for many Olympic Games. They can be mass produced and therefore are very inexpensive.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          The watch case
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         The case of the watch can be made of a variety of materials including plastic, steel, stainless steel, and even gold, silver, or titanium. Obviously, the type of case material can add dramatically to the cost of a watch.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          The crystal
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/What-are-the-best-watches-image-3.gif">
          <img alt="The crystal inside a watch" class="alignleft wp-image-125" height="134" src="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/What-are-the-best-watches-image-3-300x300.gif" width="134"/>
         </a>
         The crystal of a watch is usually either acrylic, mineral glass, or synthetic sapphire, in increasing order of hardness and cost. Acrylic crystal is an inexpensive form of plastic that does scratch easily, but the scratches can be buffed out.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mineral glass is composed of several elements that do resist scratches somewhat. Scratches cannot be easily buffed out. Synthetic sapphire is three times harder than mineral crystals and 20 times harder than acrylic ones.
        </p>
        <p>
         To produce synthetic sapphires, a tiny seed of genuine sapphire is dipped into molten aluminum oxide and slowly (at a rate of 100 mm per hour) drawn out, producing lumps of synthetic sapphire of up to 200 kg in mass. For making watch crystals and windows, the chunks, called boules, are grown is a specific orientation so that the result, when polished, is crystal clear optical class. The extreme hardness of sapphire makes these crystals highly scratch resistant and the most expensive.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          The watch band
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Watch bands can be made of many different materials. Plastic, rubber, and various types of leathers are common. If you get a watch with a standard width band, then you can buy any band you like to use with the watch.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Complications
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         The features and functions of the watch are known as complications. Examples are a stopwatch, moon phase indicator, date display, and many more. Complications will increase the cost of the watch as well as the amount of power it requires.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Watch maintenance
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Maintaining your watch is easy and is an important part of keeping it in working order and to have it retain its value.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Typical battery life in watches
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Most quartz watches are battery powered. Batteries generally last two to five years before they need to be changed, otherwise quartz watches are very low maintenance.
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/hannahelliott/2014/01/28/the-best-of-the-sihh-geneva-watch-show/" target="_blank">
          Watches with fancy features
         </a>
         such as electric illumination, compasses or stopwatches may go through batteries more quickly.
        </p>
        <p>
         Quartz watches require batteries to be changed every one to two years. An exception is the Citizen Eco-drive that uses solar cells behind the watch face to charge a battery. It can operate continuously for 6 months on a fully charge battery and the battery is kept charged in any light.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          Crystal, case, and band
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Keep the crystal, case, and band clean with a polishing cloth. If your band is leather, you can periodically treat it with a leather conditioner.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Brands
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Do you have your heart set on
         <a href="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/what-are-the-best-watch-brands/">
          the best watch brand out there
         </a>
         ? Because if you do, chances are youll be paying top dollar for some of the more exclusive watches some of these brands produce. The great thing about understanding which brands are for you is the ability to know what price range you can expect.
        </p>
        <p>
         For instance, when you become familiar with brand like Seiko, you know that youll be able to find their dress watches ranging from $50 to a couple of hundred dollars. On the other hand, with a watch brand
         <a href="http://us.tagheuer.com/" target="_blank">
          like Tag Heuer
         </a>
         , you can expect to pay anywhere from almost a thousand to a couple of thousand dollars for a dress watch.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          What are Smartwatches?
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         With the rise in popularity of smartphones, it was only inevitable that technology would spread wrist watches. Now you have the ability to have
         <a href="http://watchwhatyoubuy.com/pebble-smartwatch-iphone-android-review/">
          the convenience of a smartwatch
         </a>
         right on your wrist.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <span style="color: #808080;">
          So what is a smart watch exactly?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p>
         A smartwatch is simply a PDA (Personal Digital Assistant) that you wear on your wrist. The functionality of these type of devices goes far beyond the typical timekeeping of a regular wrist watch. The technology behind a smartwatch is designed to provide the device with computerized functionality.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Water Resistance
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Watches are rated for pressure, not depth. A 5 ATM rating would mean it could survive up to about 165 feet (50 meters). That is suitable only for swimming in shallow, not deep, water. A 10 ATM watch can be used for snorkeling. It would not be useable for scuba diving. For that you need a watch rated 15 ATM or greater.
        </p>
