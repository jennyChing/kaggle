       <div class="thepost" id="post-170880">
        <div style="max-width:650px;width:100%;margin-bottom:0px;margin-top:-10px;min-height:24px;">

        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/170880/how-to-send-text-messages-from-your-pc-with-your-android-phone/" rel="bookmark" title="How To Send Text Messages From Your PC With Your Android Phone">
          How To Send Text Messages From Your PC With Your Android Phone
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="thecontent">
         <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
         <p>
          <img alt="texting-at-computer" border="0" height="300" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/texting-at-computer.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="texting-at-computer" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Youre sitting at your PC and you receive a text message, so you pick up your phone, unlock it, read the message on the small screen, and type one back using the touchscreen keyboard. Why not use your PC instead?
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre already sitting at your computer, you can take advantage of your computers keyboard to send and receive text messages. The utilities here are primarily for Android phones  Apple doesnt allow developers to do this with iPhones.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre in the US, you can also do this with Google Voice using any type of phone  see below for more information.
         </p>
         <div id="div-1342-1" style="margin-top:10px;padding-top:10px;border-top:1px solid #CCC;margin-bottom:10px;border-bottom:1px solid #CCC;">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           tmntag.adTag('div-1342-1');
          </script>
         </div>
         <h3>
          <a href="http://mightytext.net/install">
           MightyText
          </a>
         </h3>
         <p>
          MightyText offers an Android app that you can install on your phone. After linking the app with your phones Google account, you can log into MightyText from any browser using the same Google account. Theres also a Chrome extension that alerts you to new text messages as they come in.
         </p>
         <p>
          The MightyText website allows you to send and receive text messages via your browser. Text messages will be sent via MightyText to the MightyText app on your phone, where theyre sent out of the network as standard text messages. MightyText will also capture incoming text messages, forwarding them to the MightyText web app.
         </p>
         <p>
          This means you can install an app on your phone and then use your browser to send and receive text messages. Its a slick, polished solution for texting from Android phones via your browser.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="mightytext-in-browser" border="0" height="511" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/mightytext-in-browser.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="mightytext-in-browser" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <a href="http://airdroid.com/">
           AirDroid
          </a>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Install the AirDroid app on your Android device and it will function as a local web server. You can then access AirDroid in a browser if youre on the same local network as your phone. AirDroid provides a variety of ways to interact with your device  downloading and uploading files from your browser, managing your installed apps, and even sending and receiving text messages.
         </p>
         <p>
          Just install the app on your phone, launch it, type your phones address in your browser, accept the connection, and click the Messages icon on the AirDroid page. Youll be able to view and send text messages via your browser from the comfort of your keyboard.
         </p>
         <p>
          AirDroid doesnt require any accounts or other setup  its capable of functioning entirely locally without any third-party servers involved. As a result of this, you will need to re-establish the connection in your browser when you take your phone and laptop onto a different Wi-Fi network.
          <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/105813/control-your-android-from-a-browser-with-airdroid/">
           AirDroid does a lot of things
          </a>
           it can do more than MightyText, but MightyText feels much more optimized for sending and receiving text messages.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="airdroid-send-text-messages-via-your-browser" border="0" height="602" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/airdroid-send-text-messages-via-your-browser.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="airdroid-send-text-messages-via-your-browser" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <a href="http://www.google.com/voice">
           Google Voice
          </a>
          (US Only)
         </h3>
         <p>
          Google Voice isnt an Android-only solution. If youre in the USA, you can get a free Google Voice number and use Google Voice with any phone  even landline ones. Google Voice can function a your main phone number, allowing you to forward incoming calls and texts to other numbers.
         </p>
         <p>
          Google Voice also allows you to send and receive text messages via your browser from the Google Voice website. You can also install the
          <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/google-voice-by-google/kcnhkahnjcbndmmehfkdnkjomaanaooo?hl=en">
           Google Voice Chrome extension
          </a>
          to send and receive text messages from your browsers toolbar.
         </p>
         <p>
          Best of all, Google Voice is entirely free to sign up for. Sending text messages with Google Voice is also completely free, while you may be charged by your carrier to send text messages if you use the above services  this depends on what your carrier charges you for text messages.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you live in the USA and are willing to sign up for Google Voice, its a great option. Best of all, it can be used by anyone, even people who dont even own a cellphone. Everyone can use the Google Voice website. Apples iPhone has an official Google Voice app you can use, while Windows Phone users will have to look for third-party Google Voice apps.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="send-sms-from-google-voice-website" border="0" height="428" src="http://cdn3.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/650x428xsend-sms-from-google-voice-website.png.pagespeed.ic.KDoB1-5r9y.png" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="send-sms-from-google-voice-website" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          You could also try
          <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/164395/how-to-eliminate-sms-fees-and-text-for-free/">
           using a messaging service that replaces text messages
          </a>
          , allowing you to send messages for free  some such services offer desktop apps, too.
         </p>
