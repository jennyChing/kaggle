         <h1 class="title single-title entry-title">
          What to Do for the Perfect Luxury Weekend Getaway to Barcelona, Spain @VisitBCN_EN
         </h1>
         <div class="post-info">
          <span class="theauthor">
           <i class="icon-user">
           </i>
           posted by
           <a href="http://travelingpanties.com/author/kgoldstein818/" rel="nofollow" title="Posts by @Travelpanties">
            @Travelpanties
           </a>
           |
          </span>
          <span class="thetime updated">
           <i class="icon-calendar">
           </i>
           January 13, 2015 |
          </span>
          <span class="thecategory">
           <i class="icon-tags">
           </i>
           In
           <a href="http://travelingpanties.com/category/luxury-travel-destinations/spain/barcelona-spain/" rel="nofollow">
            Barcelona
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://travelingpanties.com/category/fabulous-places-to-go/" rel="nofollow">
            Fabulous Places to Go
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://travelingpanties.com/category/luxury-travel-tips/" rel="nofollow">
            Travel Tips
           </a>
           |
          </span>
          <span class="thecomment">
           <i class="icon-comments">
           </i>
           <a href="http://travelingpanties.com/2015/01/13/what-to-do-for-the-perfect-luxury-weekend-getaway-to-barcelona-spain/#comments" rel="nofollow">

        <!--.headline_area-->
        <div class="post-single-content box mark-links">
         <p>
          This past summer I headed out to Barcelona for an extremely short stopover before I departed for my
          <a href="http://travelingpanties.com/2014/10/10/welcome-travelingpanties-azamara-club-cruises-quest-review/" title="Welcome Travelingpanties: A Review of Azamara Club Cruises Quest Ship">
           Azamara Cruise
          </a>
          . I had very limited time but have always wanted to visit Barcelona so I made sure I did my research in advance and figured out all the must see places on my list in the most efficient way possible. I wanted to nail as many of Barcelonas quintessential must-sees in the little time that I had. I couldnt believe how many things I was able to squeeze into my short 2-day time frame. Here are my top things to see for the luxury traveler, especially if youre only in town for a short while. This Barcelona itinerarygave me an excellent taste of Spain and I left feeling like I got a good sampling of the best Barcelona has to offer! Yes, I realize several places I visited are very touristy, but when youre a first-time visitor like I was, you have to see them. Theyre the mandatory stops you must visit before going to snack on tapas and wine!
         </p>
         <p>
          The first stop on my Barcelona journey was Gaudis
          <strong>
           Casa Batll
          </strong>
          . This gem is a marvel of design. Naturally, it was designed by Gaudi for one of the citys famous aristocrats. Casa Batllis definitely one of Gaudis masterpieces. This place is worth a visitespecially at night. The lights outside are miraculous. And do yourself a favor and learn about all the intricate details by getting an audio guide. Its criminal not to. And rememberBuy your tickets online before you come to avoid a long line.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="582" src="https://fbcdn-sphotos-f-a.akamaihd.net/hphotos-ak-xpa1/v/t1.0-9/10544312_660450520711369_6351898608993861441_n.jpg?oh=1a4efbe4392a229e595e3341d6ae2a6e&amp;oe=55385476&amp;__gda__=1428530894_c8d268fe609703468155700d1d9c0d4b" width="582"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Next, wander over to
          <strong>
           Plaza Catalunya
          </strong>
          , one of the main tourist squares. Its surrounded by many monumental buildings but its pretty much just a busy plazalots of tourists posing with pigeons, etc. I actually got pooped on by a pigeon here! Ick! My grand welcome! Two of Barcelonas most famous streets start here:the Ramblaand the Passeig de Grcia. I would take a few photos here, look at the fountains and be on your way. But since its on the way to your next stop, might as well check this off the list!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="594" src="https://scontent-b-lga.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-xpa1/v/t1.0-9/1560761_660450724044682_772955191918247790_n.jpg?oh=e9fc0970fc836d8574c3047bef3ae2b4&amp;oe=55366008" width="594"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you head down
          <strong>
           La Ramblas
          </strong>
          , it will lead you to the citys most famous shopping district. This is probably the biggest tourist trap but certainly worth a jaunt through. Its the Champs-lyses/ Times Square of Barcelona. I loved wandering the streets here and checking out the shops and outdoor vendors. We especially loved sampling some cured meats and goodies at St. Josep La Boqueria. Its the largest food market in Barcelona! Ohh I must remind you, hold onto your purses and phones in this area. Its infamous for pickpockets! While I didnt personally (luckily) see anything, I have heard dozens of horror stories from friends.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="595" src="https://scontent-b-lga.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-xap1/v/t1.0-9/10556256_660452860711135_9127139961802536168_n.jpg?oh=d32822a6bde3b1afb4f34edc02eae70b&amp;oe=55266779" width="595"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          We kept walking and eventually wandered up
          <strong>
           Passeig de Gracia
          </strong>
          , which is home to many of Barcelonas posh shops. All the usual suspects are here if shopping is your intention.
         </p>
         <p>
          For dinner, we headed over to
          <a href="http://lolitataperia.com/" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            Lolita
           </strong>
          </a>
          , which is near thePoble Sec stop on the Metro line. I had hooked up with the editor at
          <strong>
           Barcelona Experience
          </strong>
          who recommended this restaurant. It was exactly what we were in the market for. When our taxi dropped us off, I wasnt sure what we were in for. Random side street and an empty restaurant. But we were starving so we took refuge at the bar and immediately ordered up two glasses of rose. It wasnt but maybe 20 minutes later that the place was packed! When we left there was a terribly long line. Turns out we had found a hotspot! And with good reason. Every morsel of food we ordered was authentic and delicious. Oh and our entire meal, even in Euros, only added up to about $70! And we had a feastand two drinks each!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="594" src="https://scontent-a-lga.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-xpa1/v/t1.0-9/10393724_660928067330281_7750808850272954455_n.jpg?oh=95a498a724ad9861da46a7c6270b1a16&amp;oe=556A4A4C" width="594"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="595" src="https://scontent-a-lga.xx.fbcdn.net/hphotos-xap1/v/l/t1.0-9/10440893_660455917377496_8629448319884426694_n.jpg?oh=c61f7f5a1f57a072d7069aff6b31eca8&amp;oe=5569DD71" width="595"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          After dinner we walked over to Barcelonas
          <strong>
           Magic Fountain
          </strong>
          anothericonic stop. I wasnt expecting much and was looking forwardto heading home after being up for over 24 hours but what we saw really amazed us. There is a night show with amazing synchronized water and music each evening. We had the best time. Prepare for massive crowds but its totally worth it.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="595" src="https://fbcdn-sphotos-h-a.akamaihd.net/hphotos-ak-xpa1/v/t1.0-9/10509550_660454780710943_1966258871148387637_n.jpg?oh=882cc76d0c1b34c563435d1295705d8c&amp;oe=5527FD75&amp;__gda__=1429674701_4f3547e52f4711a4e38537327b9792c0" width="595"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The next morning we awoke extremely early hellbent on making our way to
          <strong>
           Park Gell
          </strong>
          before it got feverishly hot and crowded. We were there when it first opened and had the entire thing practically to ourselves!Park Gell is a public park that was commissioned by Gaudi for the aristocracy (sensing a theme yet?). Its located on Carmel Hill, nestled in a mountain range. The views, the mosaics and the architecture will blow you away! Do get here early though to avoid the heaviest crowds.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="594" src="https://fbcdn-sphotos-f-a.akamaihd.net/hphotos-ak-xfp1/v/t1.0-9/10551089_660928980663523_2137963782828093056_n.jpg?oh=0c75f30000a42fb306a7d1430e10b3b6&amp;oe=5529CDD0&amp;__gda__=1428277593_7ef78aef876956866c9e1a775c13d085" width="594"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Luxury Travel Barcelona" class="alignnone" height="595" src="https://fbcdn-sphotos-g-a.akamaihd.net/hphotos-ak-xap1/v/t1.0-9/10579998_660928867330201_5266298946915586769_n.jpg?oh=f57e7de8d60a3244993fab10a28e084d&amp;oe=556630F4&amp;__gda__=1428402216_3c02538f3c67166113cbcb70bb983a66" width="595"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="595" src="https://fbcdn-sphotos-a-a.akamaihd.net/hphotos-ak-xap1/v/t1.0-9/1560737_660928510663570_2614002592804557220_n.jpg?oh=e3bafa1f54894fb0cecef4badf887828&amp;oe=5523FA00&amp;__gda__=1432832841_97ffe887ec079bb4a0da8d29acdaaaa1" width="595"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Next, we were off to Barcelonas crown jewel
          <strong>
           Sagrada Familia
          </strong>
          . This Roman Catholic churchwas of course also designed by none other than Gaudi. Its his unfinished masterpiece! Everything about this place is breathtaking. Definitely purchase your tickets before you come here too! There is timed entry and theyre very strict about scheduling but youll save yourself tons of time if you can skip the lines.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Luxury Travel Barcelona What to Do" class="alignnone" height="640" src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7492/16090185679_139a3bdfdc_z.jpg" width="424"/>
          <a href="http://travelingpantiescom.c.presscdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/15653889974_329afe56ca_z.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-13689">
           <img alt="15653889974_329afe56ca_z" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-13689" height="424" src="http://travelingpantiescom.c.presscdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/15653889974_329afe56ca_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Finally, with just a couple hours to spare, we raced over to the Picasso museum to squeeze in just a little bit of Pablo! The Picasso Museum houses one of the most extensive collections of the Spanish artist. The line here wasnt too bad and the museum wasnt too overwhelming so we finished just in time.
         </p>
         <p>
          I wish we had another week and I truly cannot wait to go back and see everything else the city has to offer! What are your favorite things to see and do in Barcelona? Let me know in the comments so I can add them to my Barcelona bucket list for when I return!
         </p>
         <p>
          For a few more luxe experiences in Barcelona be sure to check out this post by
          <a href="http://travelingpanties.com/2014/08/29/barcelonas-top-five-luxury-experiences-you-must-see/" title="Barcelonas Top Five Luxury Experiences You Must See">
           Barcelona Experience
          </a>
          !
         </p>
