             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              10 Things You Should Be Buying at Costco
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>

             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4110755496563281049">
              <i>
               (Hey, I need you to do me a favor!
               <a href="https://feedburner.google.com/fb/a/mailverify?uri=365daysofcrockpot/LVsg&amp;amp" target="_blank">
                Sign up for my free emails.
               </a>
               There are about 2-3 per week and you can cancel at anytime. Love ya!)
              </i>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              If you have a Costco membership you know how many great buys you can find at that giant warehouse store. You may have heard this before but not EVERYthing is a better deal at Costco than say in a regular grocery store or a Walmart or Target. But there are a few items that I've found that are a super better deal than anywhere else! If you have other items you'd like to add to the list, please
              <a href="http://www.365daysofcrockpot.com/2015/05/best-costco-deals.html#comment-form" target="_blank">
               make a comment below
              </a>
              ! I'd love to know what your favorite Costco finds are too :)
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nJ5pmo9q6FI/VVOkIqU1iNI/AAAAAAAAOEE/E_BMLHdPzyc/s1600/10%2Bthings%2Byou%2Bshould%2Bbe%2Bbuying%2Bat%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="10 of the best deals at Costco" border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nJ5pmo9q6FI/VVOkIqU1iNI/AAAAAAAAOEE/E_BMLHdPzyc/s640/10%2Bthings%2Byou%2Bshould%2Bbe%2Bbuying%2Bat%2Bcostco.jpg" title="10 things you should be buying at Costco" width="454"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <i>
               Note: these prices were in my local store in April 2015, your local Costco may be different than mine. Also, these are only good deals
              </i>
              if you actually consume the food before it goes bad!
              <i>
               If you can't do that then consider splitting with a friend or buying just the non perishable items or freezable items. Finally, please excuse some of the pictures, then aren't the best. I felt like a nerd taking pictures with my cell phone and tried to do it as discreetly and quickly as possible.
              </i>
              <br/>
              <i>
              </i>
              <br/>
              <a name="more">
              </a>
              <br/>
              1.
              <b>
               Romaine Lettuce.
              </b>
              You get 6 heads of romaine lettuce for only $3.29. When I shop at my local Smith's or Walmart you get a 3 count for about the same price. We eat a lot of salads at our house so we can usually get through all 6 heads before they go bad. If you know you won't be able to do that you may want to split the package with a neighbor. For our salads we love using
              <a href="http://amzn.to/1HdT6fA" target="_blank">
               a salad spinner.
              </a>
              You wash the leaves and then spin off any excess moisture. When you have the tools to eat healthier it makes it so much more likely to happen! Don't you agree?
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-d_O6Mt16isQ/VVN-RpxO_1I/AAAAAAAAOBw/2DXtTp7hybY/s1600/romaine%2B6%2Bcount%2Blettuce%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="romaine lettuce 6 count at costco is only $3.29---a really great deal if you love salads!" border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-d_O6Mt16isQ/VVN-RpxO_1I/AAAAAAAAOBw/2DXtTp7hybY/s640/romaine%2B6%2Bcount%2Blettuce%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" title="6 things you should be buying at costco" width="483"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              Recipes to make with romaine lettuce:
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.365daysofcrockpot.com/2011/12/slow-cooker-chicken-rio-salad.html" target="_blank">
               chicken rio salad with tomatillo ranch dressing
              </a>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.365daysofcrockpot.com/2012/05/recipe-for-slow-cooker-beef-enchilada.html" target="_blank">
               beef enchilada salad
              </a>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              2.
              <b>
               Milk.
              </b>
              2 gallons of milk for $3.79 is a darn good deal if you ask me! That's only $1.89 a piece which is probably the very good sale price at a regular grocery store. If you can't drink 2 gallons of milk before it goes bad I recommend freezing one gallon. About 3 or 4 days before you'll need the second gallon of milk put it in the refrigerator and let it thaw.
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-mjRRhtfZh5c/VVOBVQdwRvI/AAAAAAAAOB8/oGFairtb0WI/s1600/1%2Blowfat%2Bmilk%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="2 gallons of milk at costco for only $3.79 is a good deal!" border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-mjRRhtfZh5c/VVOBVQdwRvI/AAAAAAAAOB8/oGFairtb0WI/s640/1%2Blowfat%2Bmilk%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" title="6 things you should be buying at costco" width="483"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               <br/>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               3.
               <b>
                Fresh Meats (on occasion).
               </b>
               My friend told me that she had purchased stew meat from Costco to make
               <a href="http://www.365daysofcrockpot.com/2014/07/crockpot-freezer-meals.html" target="_blank">
                my freezer meals.
               </a>
               I had a hard time believing that it would be worth it because typically pre-cut stew meat can be almost twice the price as taking a roast and cutting it up yourself. The next time I was at Costco I checked the price and sure enough stew meat was only $3.99 a pound which is actually just as cheap or cheaper than buying a roast and cutting it up yourself. I do believe the prices vary a lot between stores and even in the same store on different occasions. The past 2 times I've been into Costco I've checked the stew meat price and it was much higher than $3.99.
               <b>
                So my message here is just check the meat section each time you go to Costco and see if you can find any great deals
               </b>
               . You know you can always freeze meat. I really like stew meat for so many crockpot meals. On another occasion I bought pork loin for only $1.59 a pound. I cut it into 2 pound increments and froze it. Then I just throw it in the slow cooker with some BBQ sauce and we have BBQ pork salads for dinner (in fact that's what's for dinner tonight). So easy and inexpensive.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-__bDA23VVTU/VVODVC72zyI/AAAAAAAAOCI/pQKYd_qRYbQ/s1600/beef%2Bstew%2Bmeat%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="beef stew meat at costco was only $3.99.  Check fresh meats section for screaming deals each time you go to Costco!" border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-__bDA23VVTU/VVODVC72zyI/AAAAAAAAOCI/pQKYd_qRYbQ/s640/beef%2Bstew%2Bmeat%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" title="6 things you should be buying at costco" width="484"/>
               </a>
              </div>

               4.
               <b>
                Bottled Water.
               </b>
               You can buy 40 (16 oz) water bottles at Costco for $3.39. I always like to go by the general rule that if you can buy bottled water for less than 10 cents a bottle that is a really good deal (these come out to 8.5 cents a bottle). I like to buy a case of these water bottles and throw it in the back of my van. Then when we have soccer or other sports we just grab what we need and it's always there.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-JPJrf1NePAU/VVOGrMNx4dI/AAAAAAAAOCU/Q3YeSuru8VU/s1600/kirkland%2Bsignature%2Bpremium%2Bdrinking%2Bwater%2Bbottles%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

               </a>

               <br/>

              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               5.
               <b>
                Fresh Spinach.
               </b>
               You can buy a huge 2.5 pound bag of spinach from Costco for only $4.29. That's much cheaper per pound than the small 10 ounce bags you buy at a normal grocery store. However, I must say that I NEVER can get through this monstrous bag before it goes bad. So this is what I do--I take the bag straight from the store and throw it in the freezer. That's it. Then I use the frozen spinach for my green smoothies in the morning! You could also steam the spinach and it would be good too.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-T17H-669G5E/VVOIP1c_bCI/AAAAAAAAOCg/hz4QPk_3bOM/s1600/spinach%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

               </a>
              </div>

               Recipes using spinach:

               6.
               <b>
                Papa Pita Greek Flat Bread.
               </b>
               Okay first off I just want to say how much I love these things. They are so so delicious! We love to brush them with olive oil and then put a little garlic salt on top and then bake them for 5 minutes and gobble them up! We also like to spread butter over the top and then sprinkle with cinnamon sugar and bake for 5 minutes. The taste and texture are spot on. I just love these pitas! You get a 12 count package for $2.79 which is less than the price of a 6 count bag at a normal grocery store. So totally a good deal! I keep mine in the fridge to keep them from getting moldy.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hvBIISMhrZ0/VVOM4D7F14I/AAAAAAAAODk/n27k3AJ_Dw8/s1600/papa%2Bpita%2Bbakery%2Bgreek%2Bpita%2Bflat%2Bbread%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

              7.
              <b>
               Bread (sometimes)
              </b>
              . Sometimes you can find bread for a really good deal at Costco. For example this local Prairie Grain Honey Whole Wheat Bread is so yummy! But at a normal grocery store it costs around $5 per load. Expensive. But at Costco you can get 2 loaves for $5. And you can always freeze bread for later if you can't eat both loaves quickly.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1kb18RBTuKU/VVOMlEMV7OI/AAAAAAAAODc/ow1nVjIHUTA/s1600/prairie%2Bgrain%2Bhoney%2Bwhole%2Bwheat%2Bbread%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

               8.
               <b>
                Cheese (the Kirkland brand).
               </b>
               The Kirkland brand of cheese at Costco is a screaming deal. It's only $2.29 per pound. At a normal grocery store the cheese would probably be closer to $3.33 per pound when it's on sale. The good thing about cheese is that you can grate it up and freeze it and it works perfectly in recipes.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-aulQGiJcYVE/VVOMbW71KZI/AAAAAAAAODA/f240s8gzl4E/s1600/kirkland%2Bsignature%2Bmild%2Bcheddar%2B2%2Blb%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               It's not always better to go bigger though. This 5 lb block of cheese is actually MORE expensive per pound than the 2 lb block of cheese. Weird. Moral of the story...pay attention to the little price per pound text.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-0CxGVI2Wop8/VVOMd8_6JKI/AAAAAAAAODM/Gt5zNN5BmxI/s1600/kirkland%2Bsignature%2Bmild%2Bcheddar%2B5%2Blb%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               9.
               <b>
                Frozen Berries (Kirkland Brand)
               </b>
               . I love these berries! We use them for smoothies and I like to eat them in Greek yogurt. They are frozen so you don't have to worry about them going bad. I feel like they are always flavorful (which isn't always the case with frozen berries). The berry medley at Walmart is about 24 cents per ounce whereas these berries are 18 cents per ounce. I do feel that these are better quality in addition to being cheaper.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-bg7W7rUOvX8/VVOMi9HnzyI/AAAAAAAAODU/dvaJ67K4Eao/s1600/kirkland%2Bsignature%2Bnature's%2Bthree%2Bberries%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="the best deals at costco: frozen kirkland berries" border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-bg7W7rUOvX8/VVOMi9HnzyI/AAAAAAAAODU/dvaJ67K4Eao/s640/kirkland%2Bsignature%2Bnature's%2Bthree%2Bberries%2Bfrom%2Bcostco.jpg" title="kirkland frozen berries are one of the best deals at costco" width="484"/>
               </a>

              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
               10.
               <b>
                Boneless, Skinless Chicken Thighs (Kirkland brand).
               </b>
               If you read the recipes on my blog you know I use boneless, skinless chicken thighs a TON! They work GREAT in the slow cooker. I always buy a big bag of them in the frozen section at Costco. They cost around $2 a pound. I like how they are frozen individually and they are pretty much trimmed of excess fat. And let me tell you a secret,
               <i>
                I never thaw my chicken before I put it in the crockpot
               </i>
               . You could probably find chicken thighs that are slightly cheaper on sale at a regular grocery store but I've found that it is really hit or miss. They aren't always easy to find. That's why I just get them at Costco every time.
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rNpv6cWCwEY/VVOWgjnjWmI/AAAAAAAAOD0/aGqSo9sjA6A/s1600/boneless%2Bskinless%2Bchicken%2Bthighs%2Bat%2Bcostco.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="finding the best deals at costco--chicken thighs" border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rNpv6cWCwEY/VVOWgjnjWmI/AAAAAAAAOD0/aGqSo9sjA6A/s640/boneless%2Bskinless%2Bchicken%2Bthighs%2Bat%2Bcostco.jpg" title="kirkland boneless skinless chicken thighs are a good deal at costco" width="532"/>
