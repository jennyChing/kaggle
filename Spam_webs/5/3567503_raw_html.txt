     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Meeting People Is Easy
       </b>
      </i>
      , first released on 30 November 1998, is a
      <a href="/wiki/Rockumentary" title="Rockumentary">
       rockumentary
      </a>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/Grant_Gee" title="Grant Gee">
       Grant Gee
      </a>
      following British
      <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">
       alternative rock
      </a>
      band
      <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">
       Radiohead
      </a>
      on their exhaustive world tour following the success of their 1997 album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/OK_Computer" title="OK Computer">
        OK Computer
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blasengame_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Blasengame-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The film was nominated for a
      <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">
       Grammy Award
      </a>
      in the "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Long_Form_Music_Video" title="Grammy Award for Best Long Form Music Video">
       Best Long Form Music Video
      </a>
      " category in 2000.
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meeting_People_Is_Easy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Meeting People Is Easy
      </i>
      takes place during the promotion of
      <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">
       Radiohead
      </a>
      's 1997 release
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/OK_Computer" title="OK Computer">
        OK Computer
       </a>
      </i>
      , containing a collage of video clips, sound bites, and dialogue going behind the scenes with the band on their world tour, showing the eventual burn-out of the group as the world tour progresses. The inaugural show of the OK Computer tour began on 22 May 1997 in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Spain" title="Barcelona, Spain">
       Barcelona, Spain
      </a>
      . Their final performance, 104 concerts later, was in New York's
      <a href="/wiki/Radio_City_Music_Hall" title="Radio City Music Hall">
       Radio City Music Hall
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blasengame_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Blasengame-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Unlike other music documentaries, the film does not focus on the band's friendship with one another, families, or anything else outside the direct production, promotion and touring of a record. Most of the film contains footage consisting of music writing, concerts, promotional material, and
      <a href="/wiki/Abstraction" title="Abstraction">
       abstract
      </a>
      video footage.
     </p>
     <p>
      During interviews, the rock group take on critics, record-label hype and American modern-rock radio, which Yorke compares to "a fridge buzzing". This coincides with the soundtrack of the film, with sounds that weave in and out of snatches of interviews, conversations, and songs. Along with this "radio wave" effect is a series of edits and quickly moving shots with stills, slow tracking shots, time-lapse photography, and colour/black-and-white film and video.
     </p>
     <p>
      The documentary opens with video taken from the back of a subway train, along with the track "Fitter Happier" from
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/OK_Computer" title="OK Computer">
        OK Computer
       </a>
      </i>
      . This cuts to the band members reading off several dozen radio intros. Interspliced with the promotional material is live footage taken from various venues around the world, including the song "
      <a href="/wiki/Karma_Police" title="Karma Police">
       Karma Police
      </a>
      " on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">
        Late Show with David Letterman
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Included in the documentary are various video clips of television appearances, newspaper articles, and other mentions of Radiohead in the media. In a clip from a
      <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">
       Sky News
      </a>
      programme, presenter
      <a href="/wiki/Kay_Burley" title="Kay Burley">
       Kay Burley
      </a>
      comments on the video for the band's song "
      <a href="/wiki/No_Surprises" title="No Surprises">
       No Surprises
      </a>
      ", saying "Music to cut your wrists to. [...] It's the most miserable-sounding tune I've ever heard." While watching the video, the programme's music reviewer quips "You'll probably quite enjoy it because he actually drowns at the end". The making of the video is then shown, where frontman
      <a href="/wiki/Thom_Yorke" title="Thom Yorke">
       Thom Yorke
      </a>
      is lip-synching while trapped in a tank of rising water.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The world tour that is the subject of the film has subsequently been admitted as a low point for the band, specifically Yorke, who had a near-breakdown. As "On Your Own Again" by
      <a href="/wiki/Scott_Walker_(singer)" title="Scott Walker (singer)">
       Scott Walker
      </a>
      plays on the soundtrack, Yorke is seen placing a note that reads "i am not here and this is not really happening" on his hotel windowpane, which looks out onto
      <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">
       midtown Manhattan
      </a>
      . This message was suggested by his friend
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Stipe" title="Michael Stipe">
       Michael Stipe
      </a>
      (singer of
      <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">
       R.E.M.
      </a>
      )
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      as a way of dissociating himself from everything around him, and was used as the central lyrics of "How to Disappear Completely", released on their following album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Kid_A" title="Kid A">
        Kid A
       </a>
      </i>
      . This would also form the background of Radiohead's recording sessions beginning in 1999 for their next albums
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Kid_A" title="Kid A">
        Kid A
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amnesiac" title="Amnesiac">
        Amnesiac
       </a>
      </i>
      , inspiring a different songwriting and recording style and the band's changed sound after
      <i>
       OK Computer
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meeting_People_Is_Easy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In an interview, director
      <a href="/wiki/Grant_Gee" title="Grant Gee">
       Grant Gee
      </a>
      explained the production process of filming
      <i>
       Meeting People Is Easy
      </i>
      . Gee explains that Radiohead "sat in these hotel suites for three days, four suites booked up simultaneously" doing interviews; 20-minute slots for interviews, a 40-minute slot if it was a bigger newspaper or magazine. To film and record every individual interview, Gee had to have been "running around, leaving a microphone in one room, going and filming something in another".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">
       Soundtrack
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meeting_People_Is_Easy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Although
      <i>
       Meeting People Is Easy
      </i>
      is not a traditional concert film as it often cuts away from songs before they are finished, it features clips of several recordings that are not found in other official releases:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       "Follow Me Around," played by Yorke on an
       <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_guitar" title="Acoustic guitar">
        acoustic guitar
       </a>
       to an empty auditorium, with the rest of the band later joining in. This track was known only from this film, and in 2000 during the band's Toronto concert it was performed live during the encore following a female fan's vocal requestThom responded to the fan, and then played Follow Me Around to an ecstatic audience.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Yorke has since performed the song several times at solo appearances, or included a few lines from the song in Radiohead performances, but the song has never been performed with the full band. The lyrics of later versions differ, referencing former UK Prime Minister
       <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">
        Tony Blair

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Release">
       Release
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meeting_People_Is_Easy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Meeting People Is Easy
      </i>
      was released on VHS on 30 November 1998 in the United Kingdom, later on 12 June 2000 on DVD. It was released in both VHS and DVD format on 18 May 1999 in the United States.
     </p>
     <p>
      In subsequent years EMI and other record labels had released countless DVDs to promote pop and rock acts, as the format has become dominant over VHS in most parts of the world. In 1999, the film became the first DVD ever released by Radiohead's record label
      <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">
       EMI
      </a>
      /
      <a href="/wiki/Parlophone" title="Parlophone">
       Parlophone
      </a>
      /
      <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">
       Capitol Records
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Several television channels broadcast
      <i>
       Meeting People Is Easy
      </i>
      after its release. In the UK,
      <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">
       Channel 4
      </a>
      broadcast the film on 6 May 1999.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the US, MTV broadcast a premiere of the film on 16 May 1999,
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sundance_Channel_(United_States)" title="Sundance Channel (United States)">
       The Sundance Channel
      </a>
      broadcast the documentary 9 times during May 2000.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

       Meeting People Is Easy

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_art">
       Cover art
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meeting_People_Is_Easy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cover art">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The home video packaging and cover art is by
      <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Donwood" title="Stanley Donwood">
       Stanley Donwood
      </a>
      and Thom Yorke, similar to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/OK_Computer" title="OK Computer">
        OK Computer
       </a>
      </i>
      -era artwork. The VHS box spine and the DVD booklet contain the following text:
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       "If you have been rejected many times in your life, then one more rejection isn't going to make much difference. If you're rejected, don't automatically assume it's your fault. The other person may have several reasons for not doing what you are asking her to do: none of it may have anything to do with you. Perhaps the person is busy or not feeling well or genuinely not interested in spending time with you. Rejections are part of everyday life. Don't let them bother you. Keep reaching out to others. When you begin to receive positive responses then you are on the right track. It's all a matter of numbers. Count the positive responses and forget about the rejections."
      </dd>
     </dl>
     <p>
      The back of the package has the lyrics for the aforementioned "Big Boots" intentionally misprinted:
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       "drift all you want fromocean to ocean.search the whole world. but drunken confessions and hijacked affairs will just make yumore Alone.If you come home ill bake youa Cake made of all their yeyesi wish you coould seeme dressd for the Kill.what a nastySURPRISE.unplug thephone.stop all the taps.itall comes flooding back.from poison cloud to poisoned dwarf&gt;&gt;&gt;what a nastydulkbhs SURPRISE.the wormsll come for you big boots"
      </dd>

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">
       Critical reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Meeting_People_Is_Easy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Critical reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Reception for
      <i>
       Meeting People Is Easy
      </i>
      was very positive. The film received an average rating of 8.2/10 on
      <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">
       Rotten Tomatoes
      </a>
      , giving it 95% freshness.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Reviewer Jessica Brandt of
      <i>
       theshrubbery.com
      </i>
      gave 5 out of 5 stars.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Film critic Daniel Fletcher named
      <i>
       Meeting People is Easy
      </i>
      one of his ten favourite films.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Troy Patterson, a critic for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">
        Entertainment Weekly
       </a>
      </i>
      , gave the film a B+, calling it "an expressive mood piece creepy with cosmopolitan paranoia and bracingly somber bombast".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bart Blasengame gave the film 4 out of 5 stars, stating: "Instead of taking the usual tour documentary approach and dwelling on individual concerts or behind-the-scenes banter between the band, Gee's film focuses on the absurdity of being an important rock band in the current musical landscape  the shallow marketing of the band, the endless stream of redundant interviews, the blinding photo shoots and awkward television appearances."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blasengame_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Blasengame-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Kevin Archibald of
      <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">
       IGN
      </a>
      gave the film a rating of 8/10.
     </p>
