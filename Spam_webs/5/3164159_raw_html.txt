         <div class="article__body">
          <p>
           Christopher Nolan has been looking for two women to infiltrate Bruce Wayne's inner circle for a whileand now he seems to have zeroed in on a handful of candidates, both famous and fameless.
          </p>
          <p>
           Nolan's
           <i>
            Bat
           </i>
           -films haven't been particularly kind to the women who appear in them, as both Katie Holmes and Maggie Gyllenhaal were dismissed after just one outing as Bruce Wayne's doomed love interest Rachel Dawes. Now Nolan is looking to fill two roles: another love interest and the villainess Talia al Ghul, daughter of Liam Neeson's Big Bad from
           <i>
            Batman Begins
           </i>
           and, in the comics, the mother of Bruce Wayne's son, Damien.
          </p>
          <p>
           Here are the actresses who've made Nolan's shortlist.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Anne Hathaway
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blastr.com/anne-hathaway-2.jpg">
            <img alt="anne-hathaway-2.jpg" class="migrated-image" height="408" src="/sites/blastr/files/images/assets_c/2011/01/anne-hathaway-2-thumb-330x408-55258.jpg" style="text-align: center; display: block; margin: 0 auto 20px;" width="330"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Credits:
           </b>
           <i>
            Alice in Wonderland
           </i>
           ,
           <em>
            Get Smart
           </em>
           ,
           <em>
            Brokeback Mountain
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Buzz:
           </b>
           Clearly one of the best young actresses of her generation, she's an Oscar nominee who can do both comedy and drama and isjudging by her rather revealing role in
           <em>
            Love and Other Drugs
           </em>
           fearless.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Odds:
           </b>
           3:2. Provided she doesn't price herself out of the running.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Jessica Biel
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blastr.com/Jessica_Biel_4.jpg">
            <img alt="Jessica_Biel_4.jpg" class="migrated-image" height="247" src="/sites/blastr/files/images/assets_c/2011/01/Jessica_Biel_4-thumb-330x247-55256.jpg" style="text-align: center; display: block; margin: 0 auto 20px;" width="330"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Credits:
           </b>
           <em>
            The A-Team
           </em>
           ,
           <em>
            The Illusionist
           </em>
           ,
           <em>
            Blade: Trinity
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Buzz:
           </b>
           Her constant second-banana-ness can't be doing her any favors, but she's a more physical actress than anyone else on this list, which could make her a formidable villain.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Odds:
           </b>
           5:1. Not sure if she can sell the internationality required for Talia.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Kate Mara
