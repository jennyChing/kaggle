             <p>
              Long Island City has the greatest concentration of art in NYC outside of Manhattan, and P.S. 1 is its most famous art institution and the biggest draw to the neighborhood.
             </p>
             <p>
              P.S. 1 Contemporary Art Center has won international acclaim for its art exhibits. Yet, for a major art institution, there's real zing in the corridors, not the plain white walls of most spaces. Come for the excellent art, and keep coming back to explore this former public school turned art world star.

It's a well-known sight from the elevated 7 subway, which runs behind 5 Pointz.

           <article class="sbs content widget expert-content">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <p>
              The Noguchi Museum is one of the best small museums in NYC. It is home to the art of Isamu Noguchi, a prominent modern art sculptor, renowned for stone work.
             </p>
             <p>
              The rock garden is a highlight, a precise corner perched in a ramshackle neighborhood.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Recommended Length of Visit
              </b>
              : 45 minutes
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Visiting Times
              </b>
              :
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Wednesdays-Fridays - 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.
              </li>
              <li>
               Saturday, Sunday - 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.
              </li>
              <li>
               Monday, Tuesday - Closed
              </li>
             </ul>
             <b>
              Cost
             </b>
             : $10/adults; $5/seniors, students.
             <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="3" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense1">
             </div>
             <div class="radlinks ads-half" data-character-limit="null" data-display-inline="true" data-display-label="false" data-num-links="5" id="radlinks3">
             </div>
             Free for children under 12. The first Friday of every month is pay what you wish.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <b>
              Address
             </b>
             :
             <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="externalLink" href="http://www.noguchi.org/">
              Noguchi Museum
             </a>
             , 32-37 Vernon Blvd (entrance at 9-01 33rd Rd), Long Island City (across from Costco and
             <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="2" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/thingtodo/ss/lic_art_6.htm">
              Socrates Sculpture Park
             </a>
             ), 718-204-7088
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <b>
              Directions by Car
             </b>
             :
             <ul>
              <li>
               <b>
                Triborough Bridge
               </b>
               : Exit at 31st St (first exit in Queens). Right onto 31st (under subway). Right on Broadway. Left on Vernon.
              </li>
              <li>
               <b>
                Grand Central (west)
               </b>
               : Exit at Hoyt Ave (last exit in Queens). Left onto 31st Street (under subway). Right on Broadway. Left on Vernon.
              </li>
              <li>
               <b>
                Parking
               </b>
               : Park on street.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <b>
              Subway
             </b>
             : N or W subway to Broadway, then walk west toward Manhattan for 10 blocks. Left on Vernon and museum is in two blocks across from Costco.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <b>
              Buses
             </b>
             : Q103 and Q104 to Broadway/Vernon.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <b>
              Sunday Bus From Manhattan
             </b>
             : $5 one-way, $10 round-trip (not including admission). Pick-up from the Asia Society (Park Ave and 70th St, Manhattan). Sundays at 12:30, 1:30, 2:30, and 3:30 p.m. Return trips at 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 p.m. 718-204-7088.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <b>
              Where to Eat
             </b>
             : The Noguchi Museum cafe serves sandwiches and snacks.

             <p>
              On the East River waterfront, the Socrates Sculpture Park hosts outdoor sculpture by contemporary artists and puts on community events. The events make the area come alive.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Recommended Length of Visit
              </b>
              : 30 minutes
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Visiting Times
              </b>
              : Open all year, 7 days a week, 10 a.m. to sunset.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Cost
              </b>
              : Free
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Address
              </b>
              :
              <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="externalLink" href="http://www.socratessculpturepark.org/">
               Socrates Sculpture Park
              </a>
              , Broadway at Vernon Boulevard, Long Island City, NY 11106, 718-956-1819
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Directions by Car to Socrates Sculpture Park
              </b>
              :

              </li>
             </ul>
             The Museum of the Moving Image is a jewel of Queens. It has more than enough movie and film exhibits to entertain and educate a group for a couple hours, but it's not overwhelming like the super-museums in Manhattan. Kids, parents, and hipsters will love the hands-on exhibits.

             <p>
              The Fisher Landau Center for Art houses a private collection of contemporary art work, from 1960 to present day. Highlights include art by Jasper Johns and Kiki Smith.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Recommended Length of Visit
              </b>
              : 30 minutes
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <b>
               Visiting Times
              </b>
              :
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Thursday to Monday - noon to 5 p.m.
              </li>
              <li>
               Tuesday and Wednesday - Closed
              </li>
             </ul>
             <b>
              Cost
             </b>
             : Free
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <b>
              Address
             </b>
             : 38-27 30th Street, Long Island City, New York 11101, 718-937-0727
             <p>

              </b>

              <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="externalLink" href="http://www.flcart.org/">

           <article class="sbs content widget expert-content">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <p>
              The only museum dedicated to African art in New York City, the Museum for African Art is planning to relocate to a new building in Manhattan. While still based in Sunnyside, Queens, the Museum has curtailed its exhibits. Only offices and a gift shop are currently open. The Museum continues to stage exhibits at other locations.
             </p>
             <p>
              The Museum is in a Sunnyside warehouse, separated from the rest of the Long Island City art world by rail yards.
             </p>
             <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="3" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense1">
             </div>
             <div class="radlinks ads-half" data-character-limit="null" data-display-inline="true" data-display-label="false" data-num-links="5" id="radlinks3">
             </div>
             <p class="cb-split">
              It's only minutes away by the 7 train, but walking can be tricky. From the
              <a data-component="link" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="/od/thingtodo/ss/lic_art_7.htm">
               Museum of the Moving Image
              </a>
              take Steinway/39th Street.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Visiting Times
              </b>
              :
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Monday, Thursday, Friday - 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.
              </li>
              <li>
               Saturday, Sunday - 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.
              </li>
              <li>
               Tuesday, Wednesday - Closed
              </li>
             </ul>
             <b>
              Cost
             </b>
             : Free to visit gift shop
             <br/>

               <b>

               </b>
               , just after Midtown Tunnel toll plaza, exit Borden Ave. Left on Borden. Left onto Van Dam St. Right on Queens Blvd. Left on 35th. Right on 43rd Avenue. Museum is at 36th St.
              </li>
              <li>
               <b>
                LIE-West

               , exit Van Dam St. Right on Van Dam. Right on Queens Blvd and then left on 35th. Right on 43rd Ave. Museum is at 36th St.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <b>
              Subway
             </b>
             : #7 subway to 36th St. Walk north on 36th.
