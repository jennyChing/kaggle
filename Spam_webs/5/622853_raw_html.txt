           <p>
            The executive leading search giant Googles fibre optic roll out in three US cities says it is a myth that internet users do not want or cannot use faster broadband speeds than are now available.
           </p>
           <p>
            Kevin Lo, who led Googles foray into building its own fibre network in Kansas City in 2010, said entrepreneurs would rise to the occasion" to build applications capable of taking advantage of broadband speeds up to a gigabit, 200 times the average download speeds available in Australia today.
           </p>
           <p>
            Theres huge consumer demand out there for fast internet and we believe that faster internet speeds will lead to what we refer to as the next chapter of the internet," Mr Lo said at the Broadband World Forum conference in Amsterdam on Tuesday.
           </p>
           <p>
            Just like the last step function improvements in speed going from dial-up to broadband brought about all the current applications we use today  from streaming video, photo sharing, video conferencing online  were confident that the next 100x improvement in speeds will lead to innovation."
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="cq-article-content-media section">
          </div>
          <div class="cq-article-content-paras section">
           <p>
            The utility of fast broadband has been one of contentious debate in Australia and globally.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Coalition government has based its policy to slim down the national broadband network roll out partly on the notion that Australians do not need to access speeds of 100 megabits per second or a gigabit in the short-term.
           </p>
           <p>
            Internet services company Akamai says Australian users now have an average broadband speed of 4.8 megabits per second and just 5 per cent of the country has double that speed despite the NBN roll out.
           </p>

            Weve been surprised with the impact
           </h2>

          <div class="cq-article-content-paras section">
           <p>
            Steve Unger, the chief technology officer of UK communications watchdog Ofcom, said on Tuesday that increasing customers broadband speeds beyond 10 megabits per second did not automatically change the number or type of online applications they used.
           </p>
           <p>
            But Mr Lo said businesses were likely to offer new applications that we cant even imagine at this point in time" in markets where broadband speeds and adoption were high.
           </p>
           <p>
            He said Google decided to build its own fibre network in 2010 to explore how users would take advantage of faster internet speeds, through Google and other services.
           </p>
           <p>
            The venture, which Google says will be profitable, is now operational in two US cities, providing 1 gigabit upload and download speeds  10 times faster than those available on the NBN today  and a free service providing 5 megabits per second for eligible customers.
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="cq-article-content-media section">
          </div>
          <div class="cq-article-content-paras section">
           <p>
            Mr Lo pointed to the success of Google Fibers first installation in Kansas City, Missouri, which had increased attention from technology start-ups, investors and not-for-profit groups since activating services.
           </p>
           <p>
            The play has caused dominant cable television and broadband providers in the towns receiving Google Fiber to reduce their prices in competition. Mr Lo said access to increases in broadband speeds had also prompted ratings agency Fitch to upgrade Kansas Citys rating to neutral this year.
           </p>
           <p>
            Weve been surprised with the impact that fibre connectivity and fast internet can really have on a community," he said.
           </p>
           <p>
            
            <i>
             James Hutchinson travelled to Amsterdam as a guest of Alcatel-Lucent.
            </i>
           </p>
