       <h1>
        <a href="http://www.blogonlyapartments.com/gerome-madrid/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Jean-Len Grme in Madrid">
         Jean-Len Grme in Madrid
        </a>
       </h1>
       <p class="post-info">
        Jan 28th, 2011 by
        <a href="http://www.blogonlyapartments.com/author/apartmentblogger/" rel="author" title="Posts by apartmentblogger">
         apartmentblogger
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="entry">
       <div class="topsy_widget_data topsy_theme_blue" style="float: right;margin-left: 0.75em; background: url(data:,%7B%20%22url%22%3A%20%22http%253A%252F%252Fwww.blogonlyapartments.com%252Fgerome-madrid%252F%22%2C%20%22shorturl%22%3A%20%22http%3A%2F%2Fbit.ly%2Ff0nkS8%22%2C%20%22style%22%3A%20%22big%22%2C%20%22title%22%3A%20%22Jean-Le%C3%B3n%20G%C3%A9r%C3%B4me%20in%20Madrid%22%20%7D);">
       </div>
       <p>
        Grme was born in Vesoul in 1824. He is a classic representative of the French academic formalism. His works of clean, perfect lines with a huge technical quality, made Parisian bourgeoisie and aristocracy go crazy for him in the nineteenth-century. They saw art as a mere expression of beauty and perfection to serve the needs of a class without questioning or showing negative aspects of society, like poverty. Academicism was art that dominated the nineteenth century and was imposed by the Academy of Fine
        <strong>
         Arts
        </strong>
        in Paris, as opposed to naturalism that showed the most unpleasant aspects of reality.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="gerome madrid" height="748" src="http://blogimgs.only-apartments.com/images/only-apartments/1145/gerome-madrid.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="gerome madrid" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        In his studies at the Academy Julian in Paris he met and worked closely with the realist painter Paul Delachoche (whose real name was Hippolyte Delaroche) whom he accompanied on his trip to Italy, where incidentally won an award for his
        <strong>
         painting
        </strong>
        <em>
         Fighting cocks
        </em>
        that gave him prestige and popularity. In this
        <strong>
         painting,
        </strong>
        which shows a singular eroticism, there is a handsome naked young boy who stirs the roosters in the fight, while a young woman dressed in veils that reveal her naked body observes complacent. This symbolism of the naked bodies and cockfight synthesizing sexual desire will mark his work.
       </p>
       <p>
        However, Grme evolved as he got to known other worlds. In 1850 he began a series of trips to Turkey, Egypt and the bank of the Danube river -lands that were regarded as distant and exotic by Europeans because of their customs and way of life- which affected his pictorial themes, appearing images that reflect the
        <strong>
         culture
        </strong>
        of those lands with eroticism, representing female nude in positions that reflect sexual desire, many of them being considered as scandalous by critics, public and the church, the same who had applauded his paintings of dogs, wars or portraits of prominent characters of the time.
       </p>
       <p>
        In 1853 la bote  joined the Grme. It was a study group of the Rue Notre Dame des Champs in Paris. This place soon became a meeting place for other artists, intellectuals, writers and actors of Europe. Meetings and long conversations with the writer George Sand, who entertained the audience in a small theater, and the novelist Thophile Gautier and Ivan Turgenev or musicians Hector Berlioz, Johannes Brahms will be the layout of a new life and the beginning of their criticism of impressionism, denouncing it as a decadent fashion.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Nancy Guzman Only-apartments Author" height="100" src="http://www.only-apartments.com/bloggers/images/Anonimous.png" style="float: right;" title="Nancy Guzman Only-apartments Author" width="100"/>
        <b>
         Nancy Guzman
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        If you want to know deeply this prolific artist, do not miss the exhibition at the Museo Thyssen  Bornemisza in Madrid. Then you can go shopping and eat
        <em>
         tapas
        </em>
        , to end a pleasant day resting in
        <a href="http://www.only-apartments.com/apartments-madrid.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="apartments in Madrid">
         apartments in Madrid
        </a>
        ?
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.only-apartments.com/authors/nancy_guzman" rel="nofollow" target="authors" title="Nancy Guzman Only-apartments Author">
         Contact Me

       </p>
