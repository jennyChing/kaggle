       <h1 class="title">
        Westminster revolt on powers "up and running"
       </h1>
       <div id="content-area">
        <div class="node node-type-news node-promoted build-mode-full clearfix" id="node-14691">
         <div class="meta">
          <span class="submitted">
           Sat, 13/09/2014 - 14:04
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <div class="content">
          <p>
           Seizing on the comments by Tory MP Christopher Chope on BBC Radio 4's 'Week in Westminster' this morning - in which he said that there may be enough Tory MPs to block any additional powers for the Scottish Parliament if there was a No vote in the referendum - SNP Westminster leader Angus Robertson MP said:
          </p>
          <p>
           "The Westminster revolt against any more powers for Scotland is up and running, and exposes the utter deceit at the heart of the No campaign that additional powers would follow a No. The only way Scotland can get the powers we need to create more jobs and build a fairer society is to vote Yes. A No vote would put Scotland's future in the hands of Westminster Tories - and they are already preparing the ground to renege on anything Cameron, Miliband and Clegg are saying. And this distrusted Westminster trio are only saying it out of blind panic to try to keep their jobs - there is no genuine commitment to Scotland. We need to vote Yes as the only way to put Scotland's future in Scotland's hands."
          </p>
          <p>
           ENDS
          </p>
          <p>
           Notes:
          </p>
          <p>
           An extract of Christopher Chopes comments on Radio 4s Week in Westminster is below:
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Christopher Chopes (CC):
           </b>
           If there is a no vote then it will be the status quo
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Presenter:
           </b>
           Well it wont be will it
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            CC:
           </b>
           Well it will be because we cant change the constitution without the approval of the UK parliament and that seems to have been lost in this debate.
           <br/>
           If for example the UK parliament had wanted to discuss Devo Max then the thing to have done would have been to put forward legislation on Devo Max two or three years ago so it was on the statute book and then the Scots could have been given the choice in the referendum between independence and Devo Max that would have been approved by parliament and could have.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Presenter:
           </b>
           So are you saying that if David Cameron announces, (with the support of Nick Clegg and Ed Miliband) the powers that were outlined earlier this week by Gordon Brown you sense that there might be enough of your colleagues in Westminster to prevent it going ahead
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            CC:
           </b>
           Well in isolation Im sure there would be, because those powers would be powers for us to Scotland but there wouldnt be any rebalance of the constitution and no discussion on some of the more basic issues that were raised in the MacKay report.
          </p>
         </div>
