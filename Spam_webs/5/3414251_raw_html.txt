            <span class="blog-byline">

            <div class="node node-blog" id="node-162358">
             <div class="content">
              <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
               <div class="field-items">
                <div class="field-item even">
                 <p>
                  Annotations throw a wrench in dating materials, and Timothy Leary liked to annotate... everything. Aware of his demise after being diagnosed with cancer in 1995, he wrote notes and signed printed matter, clippings and correspondence from his personal files. Although he authored the autobiographies
                  <a href="http://nypl.bibliocommons.com/search?t=smart&amp;search_category=keyword&amp;q=High Priest" rel="nofollow">
                   <em>
                    High Priest
                   </em>
                  </a>
                  and
                  <a href="http://nypl.bibliocommons.com/search?t=smart&amp;search_category=keyword&amp;q=flashbacks a personal and cultural history of an era" rel="nofollow">
                   <em>
                    Flashbacks
                   </em>
                  </a>
                  , it will be his annotations that provide the more immediate, intimate flashback.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  For example, Leary was suspended from the University of Alabama in 1942 (he was reinstated October 1944), prompting him to enroll in the short-lived
                  <a href="http://www.astpww2.org/" rel="nofollow">
                   Specialized Army Training Program
                  </a>
                  offered at Ohio  State University. He enlisted in the Army on 16 May 1942 at the University of Alabama and began active duty 4 January 1943. His enlisted records show that he held inactive service from 16 May 1942 to 3 January 1943, before receiving this train voucher from Fort Eustin, Virginia for travel to Washington DC:
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="Travel voucher" class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="193" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img029.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="Travel voucher" width="449"/>
                   <span class="caption caption caption">
                    Travel voucher
                   </span>
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <!--break-->
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt=" Opened with apprehension by me and Shane-Michael-Trembly Cpl Timothy Leary, 9-12-95" class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="193" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img030.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title=" Opened with apprehension by me and Shane-Michael-Trembly Cpl Timothy Leary, 9-12-95" width="449"/>
                   <span class="caption caption caption">
                    Written on back of US War Department transportation order issued 3 May 1943: Opened with apprehension by me and Shane-Michael-Trembly Cpl Timothy Leary, 9-12-95
                   </span>
                  </span>
                  Some of his annotations are dated contemporary with the documents, providing much insight.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="" class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="449" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img018.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="" width="342"/>
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="Examples of Timothy Leary practicing his signature on 7 September 1956 on the front and back of memorandum, possibly Ed Glaser &amp; Associates, dated a few days earlier. Leary served as a consultant psychologist for the firm during the 1950s." class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="449" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img019.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="Examples of Timothy Leary practicing his signature on 7 September 1956 on the front and back of memorandum, possibly Ed Glaser &amp; Associates, dated a few days earlier. Leary served as a consultant psychologist for the firm during the 1950s." width="339"/>
                   <span class="caption caption caption">
                    Examples of Timothy Leary practicing his signature on 7 September 1956 on the front and back of memorandum, possibly Ed Glaser &amp; Associates, dated a few days earlier. Leary served as a consultant psychologist for the firm during the 1950s.
                   </span>
                  </span>
                  Unfortunately, dates may not be evident for some notes. For example, this letter and check from the US Treasurer was kept in the following envelope:
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="Veterans Administration Hospital document transferring assets of Timothy Leary, Sr. (d. 23 December 1956) to son." class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="356" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img027.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="Veterans Administration Hospital document transferring assets of Timothy Leary, Sr. (d. 23 December 1956) to son." width="342"/>
                   <span class="caption caption caption">
                    Veterans Administration Hospital document transferring assets of Timothy Leary, Sr. (d. 23 December 1956) to son.
                   </span>
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt='"My Paternal Inheritance"' class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="172" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img028.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title='"My Paternal Inheritance"' width="450"/>
                   <span class="caption caption caption">
                    "My Paternal Inheritance"
                   </span>
                  </span>
                  It is not the original envelope for its contents, but can be dated at and after the death of his father on 23 December 1956 or his residence at 1230 Queens Road, Berkeley,  California, whichever is later.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  While Im on the topic of dates, I came across his diplomas from the University of Alabama and Washington State  University, where he received his BA and MS degrees, respectively.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="" class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="418" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img024.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="" width="449"/>
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="" class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="336" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img025.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="" width="450"/>
                  </span>
                  Published and online sources quote him receiving the degrees
                  <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timothy_Leary" rel="nofollow">
                   one to two years earlier
                  </a>
                  . This is an example of incorrect dates mistakenly published, whether from an editorial typo or other error, but the misinformation continues to be quoted. This is an opportunity to emphasize the importance of primary research.
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  Forensic Files--More Dates!
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  The archivist searches through recorded media, traditionally paper-based, for official and legitimate historical evidence. This is the essential definition of archives, records with evidential value. The Leary papers have many opportunities for fact-checking.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Like this:
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="Telephone receipt from Motel Acapulco, Mexico" class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="223" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img020.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="Telephone receipt from Motel Acapulco, Mexico" width="450"/>
                   <span class="caption caption caption">
                    Telephone receipt from Motel Acapulco, Mexico
                   </span>
                  </span>
                  This puts Leary in Acapulco, Mexico, receiving a phone call from Cuernavaca,  Mexico on 3 Aug 1960, days before his first ingestion of the psychotropic fungi, psilocybin mushrooms. Yes, this is not a big revelation, but its good to see documentation for his life-changing trip.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Of course, much of his personal papers contain less official recordings. His manuscripts are written off the record, but will have important value in primary research. This type of evidence may not verify a date, but sheds light into his character and relationships.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="" class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="450" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img021.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="" width="345"/>
                  </span>
                  Timothy Leary married his first wife, Marianne Busch, on 14 April 1945. Marianne would commit suicide ten years later on 22 October 1955, his 35th birthday. This note appears to be a checklist with the fifth item stating, "find out about depressive period."
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  A couple years after her death and a few years before he first consumed a psychedelic drug, he drafted this:
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span class="inline inline inline-middle inline-middle">
                   <img alt="" class="image image image-img_assist_custom" height="304" src="http://cdn-prod.www.aws.nypl.org/sites/default/files/img022.img_assist_custom_0.jpg" title="" width="448"/>
                  </span>
                  While much of his personal reminisces are drafts of his autobiographies and other writings, the purpose of this note is not yet clear, as it was loose among papers dating around the same year. It still provides insight into how he perceived himself and his interest in the study of human nature a few years before his introduction to psychedelics (coined by psychiatrist
                  <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/society/2004/feb/26/mentalhealth.guardianobituaries" rel="nofollow">
                   Humphry Osmond
                  </a>
                  at a New York Academy of Sciences meeting in 1957, to describe mind-manifesting drugs).
                 </p>
                </div>
               </div>

                   Reading these blogs about the Leary papers at the NYPL is fascinating.
Especially seeing the actual documents and reading the excellent annotations.

The record of a phone call from Cuernavaca received by Leary at his motel in Acapulco days
before his life-changing magic mushroom trip demonstrates the importance of an archive.
One can presume the call was from someone telling him of the procurement of the mushrooms and arranging a date to take them once he arrived in Cuernavaca.   

Viewing this small piece of paper one gets a palpable sense of what must have been
the state of mind of the person holding it:  anticipation and anxiety that anyone would
have in advance of their first psychedelic journey.   The month is Aug. 1960; the Kennedy-Nixon presidential campaign is in full swing, and at that time the only intoxicant Leary
had ever used was alcohol.  Big changes were coming for both Leary and the country.
