          Guss World Famous Fried Chicken is Heading to Knoxville So You Can Get Some Memphis Quality Southern Eats
         </h1>

        <div class="post-text-area text-area">
         <p>
          On February 18th,
          <a href="http://gusfriedchicken.com/" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            Guss World Famous Fried Chicken
           </strong>
          </a>
          , a franchise originally located in the West Tennessee area, announced on its Facebook page that in 2015 a franchise will be opening in Knoxville, along with seven other new franchises in Los Angeles, Philadelphia, Atlanta, Ft. Worth, Kansas City, Chicago and St. Louis.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre from the greater-Memphisarea (as many UTK students are,) you know exactly how exciting this news is. Someof your favorite Memphis foodis finally going to be available to you
          <em>
           year round
          </em>
          . No need for a weekend drive or school breaks to get your fried chicken fix. As a Memphian myself, I understand the hype.
         </p>
         <p>
          UTK students from other areas maybe thinking, Okay, more fried chicken, thats great and all. But I already have Canes, Zaxbys, and lets not forget the original Guss- Guss Good Time Deli. Whats the big deal?
         </p>
         <p>
          Let me enlighten you, you poor soul.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_156130" style="width: 990px">
          <a href="http://ut.spoonuniversity.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/50/2015/02/333-.jpg">
           <img alt="Photo courtesy of Flicker user Darren &amp; Brad" class="lazy" data-original="http://ut.spoonuniversity.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/50/2015/02/333--1024x768.jpg" height="735" src="http://spoonuniversity.com/wp-content/themes/spoon/images/loading.svg" width="980"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo courtesy of Flicker user Darren &amp; Brad
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Guss has been in business for over 60 years. Their original location is located in a small town called Mason, Tennessee, which is a little east of Memphis. The restaurantstarted as a family restaurant and continues to be family-based to this day. So you know theres love in the food from step one.
         </p>
         <p>
          Guss isnt fast food. The fried chicken restaurants you know and love in Knox now dont compare to the homestyle taste of Guss Fried Chicken. Good food takes time, andwith Guss, its worth the wait. The recipe is a secret, but anyone could recognize theunique spicy flavor that Guss has made its trademark. Whether you choose a breast, thigh, wing or leg, you can still expect the signature crispy crunch that comes with their chicken.
         </p>
         <p>
          You know Guss is a traditional southern dining establishment when theres more than just fries and cole slaw as side options. With sides includingmac and cheese, baked beans, potato salad, fried rice and even whole jalapeos, you can eat there multiple times and never have the same meal twice.
         </p>
         <p>
          The price is perfect and reasonable for college students, too. With their plate option, you can get a meal with three chicken wings, baked beans, cole slaw and bread for under $6. Did I mention they also serve alcohol for in store customers? You actually cant get much better than that.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_156131" style="width: 990px">
          <a href="http://ut.spoonuniversity.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/50/2015/03/444-.jpg">
           <img alt="Photo courtesy of Flickr user Evan Blaser" class="lazy" data-original="http://ut.spoonuniversity.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/50/2015/03/444--1024x636.jpg" height="609" src="http://spoonuniversity.com/wp-content/themes/spoon/images/loading.svg" width="980"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo courtesy of Flickr user Evan Blaser
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          If you arent excited for a Guss Fried Chicken in Knoxville yet, then Im not sure what else can excite you. Maybe trying the restaurant yourselfwill make everything clear to you. Though an official opening date and location have yet to be annouced, we will all be watching
          <strong>
           <a href="https://www.facebook.com/GusFriedChickenMemphis" target="_blank">
            their Facebook page
           </a>
          </strong>
          for announcements like a hawk (or chicken?).
         </p>
         <p>
          Speaking for myself and all otherMemphians attending UTK, I say, come at us, Guss. Youll be glad you decided to franchise with us.
         </p>
