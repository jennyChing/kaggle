         <p>
          <!-- Go to www.addthis.com/dashboard to customize your tools -->
          <script src="//s7.addthis.com/js/300/addthis_widget.js#pubid=ra-53fec806754e8d85">
          </script>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           1. The Annapurna Region
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Nepal is home to the Annapurna Region which is known for having some of the worlds best trekking routes. Theres also the Lake City of Pokhara and shorter treks. The Annapurna Region compiles the wettest, driest, and windiest places in Nepal.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/trekking/annapurna_basecamp_trekking.htm" target="_blank" title="Annapurna Region">
           <img alt="The Annapurna Region" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-487" height="659" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-Annapurna-Region.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           2. The High Peaks
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Seven of the worlds highest peaks are situated within Nepal. All of the peaks reach over 20,000 ft. You may not actually be on top of the world, but it will sure feel like you are.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/peak_climbing/index.htm" target="_blank" title="Peak Climbing in Nepal">
           <img alt="The High Peaks" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-488" height="660" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-High-Peaks.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           3. The Bird Watching
          </strong>
          <br/>
          With over 856 known species, Nepal is a bird watchers paradise. Thats almost 10% of the worlds total bird population. Chances are youll see several birds during your stay.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/adventure-sport/bird_watching.htm" target="_blank" title="Bird watching in Nepal">
           <img alt="The Bird Watching" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-489" height="444" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-Bird-Watching.jpg" width="789"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           4. The Multitude of Adventures
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Nepal is the perfect destination for adventure seekers. They have mountaineering, trekking, rafting, wildlife safaris, hot air balloons, paragliding, bungee jumping, ultra lights, and more. Good luck choosing.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/adventure-sport/index.htm" target="_blank" title="Adventure Sports in Nepal">
           <img alt="The Multitude Of Adventures" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-490" height="642" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-Multitude-Of-Adventures.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           5. World Heritage Sites
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Nepal is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites which further adds to the cultural charm. Make sure to check out the various temples, monasteries, inns, and more to experience Old World culture.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/nepal_info/UNESCO_world_heritage_sights_nepal.htm" target="_blank" title="World Heritage sight in Nepal">
           <img alt="World Heritage Sites" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-491" height="660" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/World-Heritage-Sites.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           6. Value for Money
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Nepal is surprisingly affordable. This is particularly true if you compare Nepals hiking trails to others such as Kilimanjaro or the Inca Trail. With the food pricing, you could live off of about $10 a day. And you can find a decent, clean hotel room for about $18.50.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/nepal_info/about_nepal.htm" target="_blank" title="About Nepal">
           <img alt="Value For Money" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-492" height="599" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Value-For-Money.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           7. The Lakes
          </strong>
          <br/>
          The various lakes of Nepal are known not only for their outstanding beauty but also for their spiritual importance. There are over 200 lakes in Nepal with glacier origin. Who needs the sea when you have all these lakes?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/tours/kathmandu_chitwan_pokhar_8D_tour.htm" target="_blank" title="Lakes in Nepal.">
           <img alt="The Lakes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-493" height="660" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-Lakes.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           8. The Elevation Change
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Nepal is the only country in the world where the elevation will change from 60 meters to over 8,000 meters above sea level. Its quite the experience.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/trekking/index.htm" target="_blank" title="Trekking in Nepal.">
           <img alt="The Elevation Change" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-494" height="657" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-Elevation-Change.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           9. Diverse Cultures
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Over 35 different ethnic groups call Nepal home. This means there are multiple religions, languages, musical influence, and foods. Nepal is a wonderful place to immerse you into different cultures and leave your comfort zone. And Nepal is the only place in the world where people worship the living goddess, Kumari.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/tours/nejpal_cultural_11D_tour.htm" target="_blank" title="cultural tour in Nepal.">
           <img alt="Diverse Cultures" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-495" height="661" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Diverse-Cultures.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           10. Nepalese Food
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Although Nepalese dishes do result from heavy South Asian influences like China and India, the food is usually healthier with more lean meats and veggies. Other common ingredients include lentils, tomatoes, cumin, potatoes, yogurt, and garlic. And you can eat overlooking views like this!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Nepalese-Food.jpg">
           <img alt="Nepalese Food" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-496" height="660" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Nepalese-Food.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           11. Nepals Kathmandu Valley
          </strong>
          <br/>
          The Kathmandu Valley is located in the foothills of the Himalayas and is composed of seven different Monument Zones. These zones include urban centers with palaces, temples, and the oldest known Buddhist monument thought to be built in the third century B.C.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/Nepal/tours/cultural_nepal_kathmandu-4D_tour.htm" target="_blank" title="Explore Kathmandu Valley">
           <img alt="Nepals Kathmandu Valley" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-497" height="660" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Nepals-Kathmandu-Valley.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           12. The Serenity
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Nepal has been regarded as a sacred place for hundreds of years. The tranquility of the area will have a positive effect on anyone that is able to experience it. The people, the buildings, and the land are all symbols of serenity. In Nepal, you can truly escape.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://samratnepal.com/mountain-flight/index.htm" target="_blank" title="Serenity from Mountain ">
           <img alt="The Serenity" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-498" height="659" src="http://samratnepal.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-Serenity.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
         </p>
