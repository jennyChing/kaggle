      <p>
       Having passed Julys midpoint, the world is officially on the down-slope of 2014s
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/edge-of-tomorrow">
        unusually
       </a>
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/captain-america-2/">
        solid
       </a>
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/x-men-days-of-future-past/">
        summer
       </a>
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/dawn-of-the-planet-of-the-apes/">
        blockbuster
       </a>
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/snowpiercer/">
        season
       </a>
       . As box office trackers tally the seasons winners and critics begin the debate on what was actually worthwhile, we find ourselves in the mood to look back  way back.
      </p>
      <p>
       Every year, dozens of films are overshadowed by more popular, more profitable, more talked-about, and more awarded features. This doesnt mean these movies were bad  only that they lacked that certain spark that creates a culture-defining work. Screen Rants nostalgia for the waning season has left us wondering at all the titles that could have been contenders last year.
      </p>
      <p>
       Far removed from their release dates, some titles can gain a new appreciation among critics and general audiences alike. In the home market, 2013s most underratedcould finallyfind the popularity that they cry out for. In that spirit, we have cobbled together a list of
       <strong>
        15 movies from 2013 that disappointed critically or at the box office, but deserve viewership nonetheless (in no particular order)
       </strong>
       .
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span style="color: #ff9900">
        <strong>
         1.
         <em>
          The Secret Life of Walter Mitty
         </em>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <span class="responsiveImg img_article_content" data-alt="2013 Secret Life of Walter Mitty 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" data-image="wp-content/uploads/2013-Secret-Life-of-Walter-Mitty.jpg" data-size="0:slir/w380-h200-q90-c380:200/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-size2x="0:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-src-base="http://static.srcdn.com/" style="display:block; padding-bottom: 52.6315789474%;">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="2013 Secret Life of Walter Mitty 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" src="http://static.srcdn.com/slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/wp-content/uploads/2013-Secret-Life-of-Walter-Mitty.jpg" title="2013 Secret Life of Walter Mitty 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look"/>
        </noscript>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Despite an evocative promotional campaign, well-recognized lead, and a feel-good message,
       <em>
        <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/the-secret-life-of-walter-mitty/">
         The Secret Life of Walter Mitty
        </a>
       </em>
       (
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/secret-life-of-walter-mitty-movie-reviews-2013/">
        read our review
       </a>
       ) didtepid business in the United States. Though the film made a profit through the international box office, it remained something of an oddball disappointment.
      </p>
      <p>
       While it may have ended up slightly more generic than advertised, the flick is also an emotionally rousing ride with eye-popping visuals. Ben Stillers direction is confident and often creative, and the plot builds to a genuinely emotional climax that could have come off as schmaltzy in lesser hands. Any seekers of inspiring cinema would do well to consider
       <em>
        The Secret Life of Walter Mitty
       </em>
       .
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span style="color: #ff9900">
        <strong>
         2.
         <em>
          Saving Mr. Banks
         </em>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <span class="responsiveImg img_article_content" data-alt="2013 Saving Mr Banks 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" data-image="wp-content/uploads/2013-Saving-Mr-Banks.jpg" data-size="0:slir/w380-h200-q90-c380:200/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-size2x="0:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-src-base="http://static.srcdn.com/" style="display:block; padding-bottom: 52.6315789474%;">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="2013 Saving Mr Banks 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" src="http://static.srcdn.com/slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/wp-content/uploads/2013-Saving-Mr-Banks.jpg" title="2013 Saving Mr Banks 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look"/>
        </noscript>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Though its been characterized as awards bait,
       <em>
        <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/saving-mr-banks/">
         Saving Mr. Banks
        </a>
       </em>
       (
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/saving-mr-banks-reviews-2013/">
        read our review
       </a>
       )comesoff more like a somewhat idiosyncratic passion project. The story of Walt Disneys attempts to woo author P.L. Travers
       <em>
        Mary Poppins
       </em>
       novels,
       <em>
        Saving Mr. Banks
       </em>
       traces the prickly negotiations between the two, along with flashbacks fleshing out Travers childhood.
      </p>
      <p>
       Sure, the film can wax a little bit hagiographic when it comes to Disney. However, Tom Hanks is consistently amusing as the overenthusiastic studio head, whose verve for the project is infectious. Combine all this with meticulous period detail and an exacting performance from Emma Thompson, and
       <em>
        Saving Mr. Banks
       </em>
       certainly deserves a new audience.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span style="color: #ff9900">
        <strong>
         3.
         <em>
          Stoker
         </em>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <span class="responsiveImg img_article_content" data-alt="2013 Stoker 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" data-image="wp-content/uploads/2013-Stoker.jpg" data-size="0:slir/w380-h200-q90-c380:200/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-size2x="0:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-src-base="http://static.srcdn.com/" style="display:block; padding-bottom: 52.6315789474%;">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="2013 Stoker 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" src="http://static.srcdn.com/slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/wp-content/uploads/2013-Stoker.jpg" title="2013 Stoker 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look"/>
        </noscript>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       A methodical, painterly exercise in dread,
       <em>
        <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/stoker/">
         Stoker
        </a>
       </em>
       (
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/stoker-movie-reviews-2013/">
        read our review
       </a>
       ) wasprobably not what fans of director Chan-Wook Park expected out of his English-language debut. Used to the bombast of titles such as
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/oldboy/">
        <em>
         Oldboy
        </em>
       </a>
       or
       <em>
        Sympathy for Mr. Vengeance
       </em>
       (and perhaps forgetting the similarly paced
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/thirst/">
        <em>
         Thirst
        </em>
       </a>
       ), viewers could be forgiven for being put off by
       <em>
        Stoker
       </em>
       s chilly meticulousness.
      </p>
      <p>
       At the same time, the film is genuinely unnerving, featuring an entirely unpredictable cast of characters who all seem to be approaching some event horizon of madness. Especially impressive is Mia Wasikowska as India Stoker, who ramps up to full-blown psychosis in a way thats both believable and disturbing.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span style="color: #ff9900">
        <strong>
         4.
         <em>
          Its a Disaster
         </em>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <span class="responsiveImg img_article_content" data-alt="2013 Its a Disaster 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" data-image="wp-content/uploads/2013-Its-a-Disaster.jpg" data-size="0:slir/w380-h200-q90-c380:200/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-size2x="0:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-src-base="http://static.srcdn.com/" style="display:block; padding-bottom: 52.6315789474%;">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="2013 Its a Disaster 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" src="http://static.srcdn.com/slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/wp-content/uploads/2013-Its-a-Disaster.jpg" title="2013 Its a Disaster 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look"/>
        </noscript>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Beginning as an extremely uncomfortable comedy of manners,
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/its-a-disaster/">
        <em>
         Its a Disaster
        </em>
       </a>
       soon becomes something much darker. David Cross and Julia Stiles star as a couple who arrive at a long-awaited brunch  just before a massive terrorist attack dooms the entire West Coast.
      </p>
      <p>
       Mining surprisingly strong comedy from the coping mechanisms of a group preparing to die, the movie manages to also directly engage with the pathos of the situation. It all ends on a joke so twisted and shameless that viewers will likely split the difference between gasping and bursting into wild laughter.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span style="color: #ff9900">
        <strong>
         5.
         <em>
          Don Jon
         </em>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <span class="responsiveImg img_article_content" data-alt="2013 Don Jon 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" data-image="wp-content/uploads/2013-Don-Jon.jpg" data-size="0:slir/w380-h200-q90-c380:200/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-size2x="0:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-src-base="http://static.srcdn.com/" style="display:block; padding-bottom: 52.6315789474%;">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="2013 Don Jon 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" src="http://static.srcdn.com/slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/wp-content/uploads/2013-Don-Jon.jpg" title="2013 Don Jon 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look"/>
        </noscript>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Joseph Gordon-Levitts feature film-directing debut (
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/don-jon-reviews-movie-2013/">
        read our review
       </a>
       ) is a strange beast, combining what could have been the premise of a raunchy comedy with a surprisingly mature examination of modern romance. Gordon-Levitt stars as the titular New Jersey ladies man whose long-term addiction to pornography has left him with warped sexual standards.
      </p>
      <p>
       A premise that could have been used for a series of cheap gags is instead used for a warm-hearted, uncompromising look at sex and dating in the 21st century. It helps that everyone in
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/don-jon">
        <em>
         Don Jon
        </em>
       </a>
       s cast is on point here  especially Gordon-Levitt himself, who brings an edge of unctuousness to his natural charm.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span style="color: #ff9900">
        <strong>
         6.
         <em>
          Mud
         </em>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <span class="responsiveImg img_article_content" data-alt="2013 Mud 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" data-image="wp-content/uploads/2013-Mud.jpg" data-size="0:slir/w380-h200-q90-c380:200/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-size2x="0:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-src-base="http://static.srcdn.com/" style="display:block; padding-bottom: 52.6315789474%;">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="2013 Mud 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" src="http://static.srcdn.com/slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/wp-content/uploads/2013-Mud.jpg" title="2013 Mud 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look"/>
        </noscript>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Like many of the entries on this list,
       <em>
        <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/mud/">
         Mud
        </a>
       </em>
       (
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/mud-reviews-2013-movie/">
        read our review
       </a>
       )suffers largely because it presents something rather different than the previous work of its creator  in this case, Arkansas-based director Jeff Nichols.
       <em>
        Mud
       </em>
       contains little of the festering violence of
       <em>
        Shotgun Stories
       </em>
       or the nearly unbearable dread of
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/take-shelter/">
        <em>
         Take Shelter
        </em>
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Instead, the film offers a unique combination of crime thriller and tall-tale
       <em>
        bildungsroman
       </em>
       , clearly drawing inspiration from
       <em>
        Huckleberry Finn
       </em>
       . Its a gentler sort of movie than Nichols previous efforts, but just barely. Though it moves at a more languid pace than one would expect,
       <em>
        Mud
       </em>
       ends up a solidly shot, evocative piece of adventure cinema.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span style="color: #ff9900">
        <strong>
         7.
         <em>
          Prisoners
         </em>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/prisoners/">
        <em>
         <span class="responsiveImg img_article_content" data-alt="2013 Prisoners 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" data-image="wp-content/uploads/2013-Prisoners.jpg" data-size="0:slir/w380-h200-q90-c380:200/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-size2x="0:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,481:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,768:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/,1024:slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/" data-src-base="http://static.srcdn.com/" style="display:block; padding-bottom: 52.6315789474%;">
          <noscript>
           <img alt="2013 Prisoners 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look" src="http://static.srcdn.com/slir/w570-h300-q90-c570:300/wp-content/uploads/2013-Prisoners.jpg" title="2013 Prisoners 15 Movies from 2013 that Deserve a Second Look"/>
          </noscript>
         </span>
        </em>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        <a href="http://screenrant.com/tag/prisoners/">
         Prisoners
        </a>
       </em>
       (
       <a href="http://screenrant.com/prisoners-movie-reviews-2013/">
        read our review
       </a>
       )sets itself apart by creating a familiar story that proceeds to step over the usual boundaries expected of its genre. Whereas other movies about vigilante justice stumble over themselves to justify the morality of their protagonists actions,
       <em>
        Prisoners
       </em>
       takes the opposite tack  instead suggesting that every act of retaliation and violence is inherently self-defeating.
      </p>
      <p>
       This lack of moral anchor can make the movie difficult to engage with, but its worth it
       <em>
        Prisoners
       </em>
       is a pretty great thriller. Not only does the film reveal its grand design with tense economy, it also centers on Hugh Jackmans career-best performance as a supposedly good man driven to do unforgivable deeds.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       
      </p>
