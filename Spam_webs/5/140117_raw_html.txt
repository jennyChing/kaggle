        <h1 class="post-tile entry-title" itemprop="name">
         Tour the Solar System at the Speed of Light (Video)
        </h1>
        <div class="mom-post-meta single-post-meta">
         <span class="author vcard">
          Posted By:
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.fromquarkstoquasars.com/author/scottfqtq/">
            Scott Wilson
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span>
          on:
          <time class="updated" datetime="2015-02-03T15:16:36+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           February 03, 2015
          </time>
         </span>
         <span>
          In:
          <a href="http://www.fromquarkstoquasars.com/category/solar-system/" title="View all posts in Solar System">
           Solar System
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.fromquarkstoquasars.com/category/video/" title="View all posts in Video">

         </span>
         <div class="post-tools">
          <a class="print" href="javascript:window.print()" rel="nofollow">
           <i class="fa-icon-print">
           </i>

          </a>
          <a class="email" href="mailto:?subject=Tour the Solar System at the Speed of Light (Video)&amp;body=Tour the Solar System at the Speed of Light (Video) http://www.fromquarkstoquasars.com/watch-tour-solar-system-speed-light/" rel="nofollow">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          As astoundingly fast as light moves through a vacuum, its still a pretty pokey speed limit for getting around in the Universe. It would take four years (and some change) to get to the local planning department over at Alpha Centauri. Wake me up when were there!
         </p>
         <p>
          Its one thing to run the numbers on this phenomena and marvel at the great expanse of space, but another to actually
          <em>
           see
          </em>
          it happening, in real-time.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thanks to animator
          <a href="http://aswinehart.com/">
           Alphonse Swinehart,
          </a>
          you can do just that in this recently released video. According to his description, This animation illustrates, in real-time, the journey of a photon of light emitted from the sun and traveling across a portion of the solar system.
         </p>
         <p>
          Swinehart arbitrarily adjusted the alignment of planets and asteroids to ensure that the cameras viewpoint would take in all of the relatively few scenic opportunitiesalong the route out to Jupiter, a 45-minutes ride so featureless that it will have you yearning for a few acres of cornfields to stare at out of the window along the way.
         </p>
         <p>
          Grab a beverage and a snack and get settled in for the ride  and be glad Swinehart didnt tack on the extra half-hour or so it would have taken to get all the way out to Saturn.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="324" src="//player.vimeo.com/video/117815404" width="600">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://vimeo.com/117815404">
           Riding Light
          </a>
          from
          <a href="http://vimeo.com/aswinehart">
           Alphonse Swinehart
          </a>
          on
          <a href="https://vimeo.com">
           Vimeo
          </a>
          .
         </p>
