                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   Sunday, March 11, 2012
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-aTTPDUAa2JY/T11Z7Q7v7PI/AAAAAAAAAwg/tXaRTv5ytmk/s320/4435700783475da10052.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     The Southwest Airlines Experience
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5851647268027463167" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-aTTPDUAa2JY/T11Z7Q7v7PI/AAAAAAAAAwg/tXaRTv5ytmk/s1600/4435700783475da10052.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="216" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-aTTPDUAa2JY/T11Z7Q7v7PI/AAAAAAAAAwg/tXaRTv5ytmk/s320/4435700783475da10052.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Over the years, wheelchair-related technology has come a
long way. The bells and whistles
available for wheelchair users now spans the spectrum from solid insert tires (
                      <i>
                       no more flats!
                      </i>
                      ) to advanced seating
recliners (
                      <i>
                       perfect for napping during
boring meetings
                      </i>
                      ) and even add-on cell phone chargers (
                      <i>
                       can you hear me now?
                      </i>
                      ).
And now, thanks to Southwest Airlines, my wheelchair features something never
offered before; cruise control.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      When Casey and I landed at BWI last week, we looked out the
window to see something very disturbing  the airline baggage handlers were
breaking my wheelchair. Despite
having taped a sign around my wheelchair controller/joystick that read: DO NOT
TOUCH four times in large bold letters, the baggage handlers from Southwest
Airlines went on to touch and break my chair.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Talk about going above and beyond!
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      So, now that the controller/joystick is broken, my
wheelchair keeps driving  even when I take my hand off it. Hence, I have cruise control (
                      <i>
                       great party trick, but bad for safety
                      </i>
                      ).
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      As disappointed (
                      <i>
                       major
understatement
                      </i>
                      ) as it is to have a broken wheelchair, we were equally disappointed
by how rudely we were treated by the Southwest Airlines customer service representative. After waiting nearly 30 minutes for her
to meet us at the gate, she arrived only to tell us that this was basically our
fault for not drawing up an operator manual for the handlers. She then gave me a blank stare and said
everything was all good because Southwest would cover the repair costs.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      What the what?
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      I tried to assure her that it wouldnt be all good if I cruise
controlled my way off a Metro platform, in front of a bus or over a small
child, but she didnt seem to appreciate my point.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      While my wheelchair will get fixed, this has been a truly
unfortunate situation for everyone involved. Ive been terribly inconvenienced and the airline is losing
money (
                      <i>
                       and face
                      </i>
                      ).
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Oh how different this could have been had the airline invested
in better training programs to teach baggage handlers how to operate/transport
wheelchairs and to sensitize customer service representatives regarding how
critical mobility devices are to members of the disabled community.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Who knows, maybe my wheelchair wouldnt have been broken or
maybe we would have been treated with compassion and respect.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Breaking wheelchairs is bad for business. The repair costs far exceed whatever is
paid for airfare and the poor customer service leads to terrible word-of-mouth
and doesnt generate customer loyalty.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      If Southwest Airlines is as forward-thinking and committed
to customer service as it would have us believe, they will take steps to
improve. In order to help raise
the bar regarding disability awareness and customer service, I would like to
officially offer to address Southwests management and employees. By doing so in a positive and uplifting
way, we could work together to improve the customer experience of disabled
passengers while improving the companys overall business.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      According to its website, the mission of Southwest Airlines
is dedication to the highest quality of Customer Service delivered with a
sense of warmth, friendliness, individual pride and Company Spirit.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Guess that means Ill be hearing from them soon...
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
