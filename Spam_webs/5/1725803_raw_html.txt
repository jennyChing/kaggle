             <h5>
              Jeremy Renner
             </h5>
             <h6>
              Gary Webb and Producer
             </h6>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <strong>
               <span>
                JEREMY RENNER (Gary Webb)
               </span>
              </strong>
              <span>
               starred in
               <em>
                The Hurt Locker
               </em>
               , which won six Academy Awards including Best Picture and Best Director (Kathryn Bigelow). For his portrayal of Sgt. William James, he received many accolades, including the Breakthrough Actor Award at the Hollywood Film Festival; BAFTA, Screen Actors Guild, Gotham, Independent Spirit, and Critics Choice Movie Award nominations; and his first Academy Award nomination, in the Best Actor category.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               The following year, he was again an Academy Award nominee, this time as Best Supporting Actor for his performance as James Coughlin in
               <em>
                The Town
               </em>
               , directed by Ben Affleck. Mr. Renner also received Screen Actors Guild, Golden Globe, and Critics Choice Movie Award nominations. With his fellow actors from the film, he was honored by the National Board of Reviews award for Best Acting by an Ensemble.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               Moviegoers worldwide also know him for his starring roles in blockbuster film series. He played Hawkeye in Joss Whedons
               <em>
                The Avengers
               </em>
               , the third-highest-grossing movie of all time, and is reteaming with the filmmaker for
               <em>
                Avengers: Age of Ultron
               </em>
               . As William Brandt, he joined Tom Cruise for Brad Birds
               <em>
                Mission: Impossible  Ghost Protocol
               </em>
               , and will do so again in a sequel to be directed by Christopher McQuarrie. He inaugurated the role of Aaron Cross in Tony Gilroys
               <em>
                The Bourne Legacy
               </em>
               , and will reprise the role in a movie to be directed by Justin Lin.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               His breakthrough movie role, for which he earned an Independent Spirit Award nomination, was as Jeffrey Dahmer in
               <em>
                Dahmer
               </em>
               , for writer/director David Jacobson. Mr. Renners other films include David O. Russells smash
               <em>
                American Hustle
               </em>
               , for which he shared a Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture with his fellow actors from the movie; James Grays
               <em>
                The Immigrant
               </em>
               ; Tommy Wirkolas
               <em>
                Hansel &amp; Gretel: Witch Hunters
               </em>
               ; Andrew Dominiks
               <em>
                The Assassination of Jesse James by the Coward Robert Ford
               </em>
               ; Juan Carlos Fresnadillos
               <em>
                28 Weeks Later
               </em>
               ; Charles Olivers
               <em>
                Take
               </em>
               , opposite Minnie Driver; Niki Caros
               <em>
                North Country
               </em>
               , with Charlize Theron; Clark Johnsons
               <em>
                S.W.A.T.
               </em>
               ; and Van Fischers
               <em>
                Neo Ned
               </em>
               , for which he was named Best Actor at the Palm Beach International Film Festival. He previously starred for
               <strong>
                Kill the Messenger
               </strong>
               director Michael Cuesta in
               <em>
                12 and Holding
               </em>
               , which was nominated for the Independent Spirit Awards John Cassavetes Award.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               His initial acting background was in theater; he starred in and co-directed a production of
               <em>
                Search and Destroy
               </em>
               , produced by Barry Levinson, which received stellar reviews.
              </span>
             </p>

             <h5>
              Rosemarie DeWitt
             </h5>
             <h6>
              Sue Webb
             </h6>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <strong>
               ROSEMARIE D
              </strong>
              <strong>
               E
              </strong>
              <strong>
               WITT (Sue Webb)
              </strong>
              grace, style, and charm are known to moviegoers, television viewers, and theatergoers. Her upcoming movies include Jason Reitmans
              <em>
               Men, Women &amp; Children
              </em>
              , alongside Adam Sandler, Emma Thompson, and Jennifer Garner; and Gil Kenans
              <em>
               Poltergeist
              </em>
              , an update of the horror classic, with Sam Rockwell.
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               Her performance in
               <em>
                Your Sisters Sister
               </em>
               , alongside Emily Blunt and Mark Duplass for writer/director Lynn Shelton, brought her a Spirit Award nomination. With her fellow actors, she shared the Gotham Award for Best Ensemble Cast.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               In the feature film
               <em>
                Rachel Getting Married
               </em>
               , written by Jenny Lumet and directed by Jonathan Demme, she played the title character opposite Anne Hathaway. For her portrayal, Ms. DeWitt was named the years Best Supporting Actress by the Toronto, Vancouver, and Washington D.C. Film Critics groups; and received Spirit and Gotham Award nominations. She subsequently reteamed with Mr. Demme on his staging of Beth Henleys play
               <em>
                Family Week
               </em>
               , at NYCs Lucille Lortel Theatre.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               Among her other films are Lynn Sheltons
               <em>
                Touchy Feely
               </em>
               ; Ry Russo-Youngs
               <em>
                Nobody Walks
               </em>
               , with John Krasinski and Olivia Thirlby; Peter Hedges
               <em>
                The Odd Life of Timothy Green
               </em>
               , with Jennifer Garner; Gus Van Sants
               <em>
                Promised Land
               </em>
               , also for Focus Features, opposite Matt Damon and Mr. Krasinski; John Wells
               <em>
                The Company Men
               </em>
               , opposite Ben Affleck; and Ron Howards
               <em>
                Cinderella Man
               </em>
               , which told the story of her real-life grandfather James J. Braddock, portrayed by Russell Crowe in the film.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               Ms. DeWitts television credits include starring opposite Toni Collette on all three seasons of
               <em>
                United States of Tara
               </em>
               and playing opposite Jon Hamm in a recurring role on
               <em>
                Mad Men
               </em>
               . In the fall of 2014, she will be seen starring in the HBO miniseries
               <em>
                Olive Kitteridge
               </em>
               , directed by Lisa Cholodenko and based on Elizabeth Strouts Pulitzer Prize-winning novel of the same name, with Frances McDormand, Bill Murray, and Richard Jenkins.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               Her stage work includes playing Masha in Chekhovs
               <em>
                Three Sisters
               </em>
               (at the Williamstown Theatre Festival); starring in the revival of John Patrick Shanleys
               <em>
                Danny and the Deep Blue Sea
               </em>
               and in
               <em>
                Swimming in the Shallows
               </em>
               (both at Second Stage); originating the role of Fanny in Craig Lucas
               <em>
                Small Tragedy
               </em>
               (at Playwrights Horizons), for which she shared an Obie Award with her fellow actors from the ensemble; and
               <em>
                The Butter and Eggman
               </em>
               (at the Atlantic Theatre Company),
               <em>
                Dream Girl
               </em>
               (at the Zipper Theater),
               <em>
                St. Scarlet
               </em>
               (at the Ontological Theater),
               <em>
                Dead Reckoning
               </em>
               (at the Cherry Lane Theatre), and
               <em>
                Frame 312
               </em>
               (at the Eugene ONeill Center).
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               Ms. DeWitt is a graduate of Hofstra University with a degree in Creative Studies, and she also studied at the Actors Center in New York.
              </span>

            <div>
             <h5>
              Ray Liotta
             </h5>
             <h6>
              John Cullen
             </h6>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <strong>
               <span>
                RAY LIOTTA (John Cullen)
               </span>
              </strong>
              <span>
               has chosen diverse and challenging roles both comedic and dramatic in the more than 60 feature films to his credit.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoHeader">
              <span>
               The New Jersey native began acting while a student at the University of Miami. He earned critical, audience, and industry attention with his Golden Globe Award-nominated performance opposite Melanie Griffith and Jeff Daniels in Jonathan Demmes
               <em>
                Something Wild
               </em>
               . He next starred in Robert M. Youngs acclaimed
               <em>
                Dominick and Eugene
               </em>
               , opposite Tom Hulce, and Phil Alden Robinsons beloved
               <em>
                Field of Dreams
               </em>
               , in which Mr. Liotta portrayed the iconic baseball player Shoeless Joe Jackson.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoHeader">
              <span>
               His most widespread acclaim came next, with his portrayal of real-life mobster Henry Hill in Martin Scorseses
               <em>
                Goodfellas
               </em>
               , in which he starred with Robert De Niro and Academy Award winner Joe Pesci.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoHeader">
              <span>
               Among Mr. Liottas other films are Jonathan Kaplans
               <em>
                Unlawful Entry
               </em>
               , for which he received an MTV Movie Award nomination; Jessie Nelsons
               <em>
                Corrina, Corrina
               </em>
               , alongside Whoopi Goldberg, Tina Majorino, and Don Ameche; James Mangolds
               <em>
                Cop Land
               </em>
               , reteamed with Robert De Niro, and
               <em>
                Identity
               </em>
               ; Ridley Scotts
               <em>
                Hannibal
               </em>
               , opposite Anthony Hopkins; Ted Demmes
               <em>
                Blow
               </em>
               , with Johnny Depp; David Mirkins
               <em>
                Heartbreakers
               </em>
               ; Nick Cassavetes
               <em>
                John Q
               </em>
               ; Walt Beckers
               <em>
                Wild Hogs
               </em>
               ; Wayne Kramers
               <em>
                Crossing Over
               </em>
               ; Jody Hills
               <em>
                Observe and Report
               </em>
               , with Seth Rogen; Miguel Artetas
               <em>
                Youth in Revolt
               </em>
               ; Shawn Levys
               <em>
                Date Night
               </em>
               , with Steve Carell and Tina Fey; Jacob Estes
               <em>
                The Details
               </em>
               ; Ariel Vromens
               <em>
                The Iceman
               </em>
               , with Michael Shannon; Andrew Dominiks
               <em>
                Killing Them Softly
               </em>
               ; Frank Miller and Robert Rodriguezs
               <em>
                Sin City: A Dame to Kill For
               </em>
               ; Joe Carnahans
               <em>
                Smokin Aces
               </em>
               and
               <em>
                Stretch
               </em>
               ; and, also for Focus Features, Derek Cianfrances
               <em>
                The Place Beyond the Pines
               </em>
               , opposite Bradley Cooper.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoHeader">
              <span>
               He produced and starred in the police drama
               <em>
                Narc
               </em>
               , directed by Joe Carnahan. The film received critical acclaim, with his performance as Henry Oak earning Mr. Liotta an Independent Spirit Award nomination.
               <strong>
               </strong>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoHeader">
              His unforgettable guest appearance on the top-rated television drama
              <em>
               ER
              </em>
              brought him an Emmy Award and a Prism Award. His performance as Frank Sinatra in Rob Cohens telefilm
              <em>
               The Rat Pack
              </em>
              garnered him a Screen Actors Guild Award nomination.
             </p>

               <span>

               </span>
              </strong>
              <span>
               is an actor, writer, producer, and director.
              </span>

              <span>

               <span>

               </span>

               </em>
               <span>

               </em>

                </em>

               <em>

               </em>

              </span>

The film received the top award at the Seattle International Film Festival, as well as the Bronze Award at the Tokyo International Film Festival, and Mr. Nelson was nominated for the Independent Spirit Awards Someone to Watch Award.

             <h5>
              Barry Pepper
             </h5>
             <h6>
              Russell Dodson
             </h6>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <strong>
               <span>
                BARRY PEPPER (Russell Dodson)
               </span>
              </strong>
              <span>
               is one of Hollywoods most talented actors. Ever since he gained worldwide critical and audience attention for his portrayal of Private Jackson in Steven Spielbergs classic, Academy Award-winning feature
               <em>
                Saving Private Ryan
               </em>
               , he has been sought for compelling roles by a variety of filmmakers.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               Mr. Pepper was born on Vancouver Island, the gem of the Canadian West Coast, where his ancestors had settled in the early 1800s. His family later relocated to a farm on a small Gulf Island off the West Coast of British Columbia. He discovered acting through his high school drama teacher, Mrs. Salter, and enrolled in the Vancouver Actors studio, training under the gifted Mel Tuck, who opened his eyes to the possibilities of a career in the performing arts. He worked in Vancouvers film and television industry before packing everything he owned into his car and driving to Los Angeles, where was soon hired for
               <em>
                Saving Private Ryan
               </em>
               .
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               His starring role as the New York Yankees Roger Maris in the critically acclaimed telefilm
               <em>
                61*
               </em>
               , directed and executive-produced by Billy Crystal, earned him Golden Globe, Critics Choice, and Emmy Award nominations. A decade later, Mr. Pepper would win an Emmy Award for portraying the iconic Robert F. Kennedy in the miniseries
               <em>
                The Kennedys
               </em>
               , directed by Jon Cassar, as well as a Gemini Award.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               He executive-produced and starred in the title role of the telefilm
               <em>
                3: The Dale Earnhardt Story
               </em>
               , a biopic of the NASCAR legend, receiving a Screen Actors Guild Award nomination; and also executive-produced and starred in the feature
               <em>
                The Snow Walker
               </em>
               , which earned nine Genie Award (Canadas Oscars equivalent) nominations including Best Picture, Best Actor, and Best Director (Charles Martin Smith).
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               Among his other films are Tommy Lee Jones
               <em>
                The Three Burials of Melquiades Estrada
               </em>
               , for which Mr. Pepper was an Independent Spirit Award nominee; George Hickenloopers
               <em>
                Casino Jack
               </em>
               , for which he received the Hollywood Spotlight Award from the Hollywood Film Festival; Frank Darabonts
               <em>
                The Green Mile
               </em>
               ; Joel and Ethan Coens
               <em>
                True Grit
               </em>
               ; Clint Eastwoods
               <em>
                Flags of Our Fathers
               </em>
               ; Spike Lees
               <em>
                25

                </sup>

               </em>

               <em>
                The Lone Ranger
               </em>

               <em>
                Enemy of the State
               </em>
               ; Ric Roman Waughs
               <em>
                Snitch
               </em>
               ; Gabriele Muccinos
               <em>
                Seven Pounds
               </em>
               ; Randall Wallaces
               <em>
                We Were Soldiers
               </em>
               ; and Jon Gunns
               <em>
                Like Dandelion Dust
               </em>
               , for which he was voted Best Actor at the Las Vegas International Film Festival and New York VisionFest.

               <span>

              </strong>
              <span>
               has garnered acclaim for his unique ability to mesmerize audiences with his stunning character portrayals.
              </span>

             <p class="MsoNormal">
              Mr. Williams is best known for his starring role as Omar on the contemporary dramatic series
              <em>
               The Wire
              </em>
              , which ran for five seasons on HBO and is one of the most praised programs of recent years. He was an NAACP Image Award nominee for the portrayal.
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal">
              <span>
               More recently, he has starred as Chalky White on all four seasons of HBOs acclaimed period drama
               <em>
                Boardwalk Empire
               </em>
               , and will be in the shows fifth and final season in the fall of 2014. In 2012, Mr. Williams shared with his fellow actors from the program the Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series; in 2014, he received an NAACP Image Award nomination for his portrayal.

                Community

                The Philanthropist

              <span>

After numerous appearances in music videos and as a background dancer on concert tours with Madonna and George Michael, he decided to seriously pursue acting. He participated in several productions of the famed La MaMA Experimental Theatre; the prestigious National Black Theatre Company; and the storied Theater for a New Generation, directed by Mel Williams.
              </span>
