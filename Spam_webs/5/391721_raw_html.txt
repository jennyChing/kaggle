   function render_heritageaction_scorecard_boxes(){

      (function($){
        $(document).ready(function(){

          $('.js-scorecard-info').hover(
            function() {
              cong = $(this).data('cong');
              $('.'+cong).clone().appendTo(this);
              if((window.innerWidth / 2) &gt; this.offsetLeft)
              { 
                $(this).addClass('scorecard-tag__left');
              }
              $.event.trigger({
                type: "scorecard-hover-open",
                member_name: $(this).data('member-name'),
                member_mcid: $(this).data('member-mcid'),
                time: new Date()
              });
            },
            function() {
              cong = $(this).data('cong');
              $.event.trigger({
                type: "scorecard-hover-close",
                member_name: $(this).data('member-name'),
                member_mcid: $(this).data('member-mcid'),
                time: new Date()
              });
              $(this).find('.'+cong).remove();              
            }
          );

          $(".js-scorecard-info").on('click', ".heritage-scorecard-member-button", function(){
            $.event.trigger({
              type: "scorecard-hover-button-click",
              member_name: $(this).data('member-name'),
              member_mcid: $(this).data('member-mcid'),
              time: new Date()
            });
          });

          
          $(".scorecard-tag-content").live('click',function(e){             
             if(e.target.nodeName == 'A'){ return; } // dont hide hover for outbound link

                type: "scorecard-hover-close",
                member_name: $(this).data('member-name'),
                member_mcid: $(this).data('member-mcid'),
                time: new Date()
              });   
              $(this).parent().find('.' + $(this).data('cong')).remove();                        
             }
             
          });
        

        })
      })(jQuery);

         Well played, McConnell
        </h1>
        <h2 class="subtitle">
        </h2>

          </span>

         <p>
          Ive often called
          <span class="js-scorecard-info" data-cong="M000355" data-member-name="Sen. Mitch McConnell (R-KY)">
           <span class="scorecard-name">
            Sen. Mitch McConnell (R-KY)
           </span>
           <span class="scorecard scorecard-tag">
            63%
           </span>
          </span>
          RedStates Leading Supervillain, and its not been unwarranted either, but damn, I respect this.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           BATON ROUGE, La. (AP)  Republicans have promised
           <span class="js-scorecard-info" data-cong="C001075" data-member-name="Sen. Bill Cassidy (R-LA)">
            <span class="scorecard-name">
             Rep. Bill Cassidy (R-LA)
            </span>
            <span class="scorecard scorecard-tag">
             71%
            </span>
           </span>
           a seat on the Senates energy committee if he defeats Democratic
           <span>
            Sen. Mary Landrieu (D-LA)
           </span>
           in Louisianas runoff election next month, potentially undercutting one of her main arguments for re-election.
          </p>
          <div id="div-gpt-1613311184-5" style="text-align:center; margin-bottom:20px;">
           <script type="text/javascript">
            googletag.cmd.push(function () { googletag.display('div-gpt-1613311184-5'); });
           </script>
          </div>
          <p>
           Landrieu has repeatedly touted the importance of her seat on the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee in her campaign for a fourth term representing a state with a robust oil and gas industry.
          </p>
          <p>
           While she will no longer serve as the committees chair in the GOP-led Senate in the next Congress, Landrieu has campaigned hard on her place as the committees senior Democrat  and Cassidys inability to get a seat on the panel as a freshman senator.
          </p>
          <p>
           But incoming Senate Majority Leader
           <span class="js-scorecard-info" data-cong="M000355" data-member-name="Sen. Mitch McConnell (R-KY)">
            <span class="scorecard-name">
             Sen. Mitch McConnell (R-KY)
            </span>
            <span class="scorecard scorecard-tag">
             63%
            </span>
           </span>
           said in a statement released to The Associated Press that he will appoint Cassidy to the committee should Cassidy win his Dec. 6 runoff election against Landrieu.
          </p>
          <p>
           Im confident Dr. Cassidy will use this position to succeed where Sen. Landrieu failed, McConnell said in a statement touting Cassidys support for the Keystone XL pipeline and opposition to Obama administration efforts to place new restrictions on carbon emissions.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Not gonan lie, folks. Thats the first time McConnell has said something that brought a tear to my eye. Of course, Landrieus re-election bid is looking more and more like a campaign to nowhere with the DSCC pulling out money and the NRSC pouring more money in. Just last night, I saw about 20 campaign ads over the course of a few hours and none of them were pro-Landrieu. And, while the Democrats are looking to hold a vote on the Keystone XL Pipeline to try and save her job, McConnell has proven once again that he can win elections.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>

          </a>
          Also, 54 seats is looking pretty delightful.
         </p>
