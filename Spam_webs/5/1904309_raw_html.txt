                               Tomatillo Dressing (+ site updates)

                            <div id="postcontent">
                             I would like to label last night and today as, Erin tried to do some awesome, cool things but then everything got royally %$@#&amp;% upd. You may notice things look a bit different around here. Naturally Ella is going through some updates that will hopefully make finding content a bit easier. For a couple years Ive been slowly taking the site away from just being a blog and moving it into a resource site for eating whole foods. Im also taking into consideration that a good majority of you are currently reading this on your phone and iPad. All changes for the better (and I like to use my site as a test subject before I inflict this pain onto clients)So first of all, thank you. Thank you for coming back here, making my recipes, and starting a dialogue. Yall rock something fierce. Secondly, I appreciate your patience during this time of adjustment. There is still a lot of content to update, pages that need finished, and enhancements to the site so it isnt slow as molasses. As a one woman team, Im slowly cranky everything out. I really thought that the site would be ready today but as with most new technology, there are bugs, failures, and frustration.Things to look for in the future:
                             <br/>
                             1. Updated Seasonal Pages that include more items
                             <br/>
                             2. Update to the bulk bin pages to include Spices, Herbs, and Nuts/Seeds
                             <br/>
                             3. A resource for quick, delicious meals
                             <br/>
                             4. More series to inspire you to have fun in the kitchen
                            </div>
                            <p>
                             Ive also started a newsletter that once it gets started, will be focused on spotlighting one ingredient every couple of weeks. The newsletter will be chocked full of information and recipe ideas from around the web.
                            </p>
                            <p>
                             I think thats all the news for now. Awesome things are coming, it just takes a bit of trial and error!
                            </p>
                            <p>
                             Of course, with all the craziness, I felt like I needed to share a recipe to keep things some-what sane. This dressing is simple and a fun take on a vinaigrette. Ive seen tomatillos all over the farmers market and Ive been itching to start playing with them outside of salsa. This dressing pairs well with grilled vegetables and all kinds of greens! I whipped up a charred corn and kale salad to go along with the dressing!
                             <br/>
                            </p>
                            <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
                             <link href="http://32lxcujgg9-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/IMG_8300-666x1000.jpg" itemprop="image"/>
                             <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
                              Tomatillo Dressing
                             </div>
                             <div class="ERSClear">
                             </div>
                             <div class="ERSTopRight">
                              <div class="ERSSavePrint">
                               <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
                                <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://naturallyella.com/easyrecipe-print/15533-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                                 Print
                                </a>
                               </span>
                              </div>
                             </div>
                             <div class="divERSHeadItems">
                              <div class="ERSAuthor">
                               Author:
                               <span itemprop="author">
                                Erin Alderson
                               </span>
                              </div>
                              <div class="ERSServes">
                               Serves:
                               <span itemprop="recipeYield">
                                1 cups
                               </span>
                              </div>
                             </div>
                             <div class="ERSIngredients">
                              <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
                               Ingredients
                              </div>
                              <ul>
                               <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                                 lb tomatillos
                               </li>
                               <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                                 small white onion
                               </li>
                               <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                                1 jalapeo, seeds removed
                               </li>
                               <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                                 cup cilantro
                               </li>
                               <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                                 cup olive oil
                               </li>
                               <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                                 cup lime juice
                               </li>
                               <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                                 teaspoon sea salt
                               </li>
                               <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                                2-3 tablespoons honey
                               </li>
                              </ul>
                              <div class="ERSClear">
                              </div>
                             </div>
                             <div class="ERSInstructions">
                              <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
                               Instructions
                              </div>
                              <ol>
                               <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                                Preheat oven to 425 and cover a baking tray with parchment paper.
                               </li>
                               <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                                Bring a pot of water to a boil. Drop tomatillos into the boiling water for 30 seconds. Remove from water and peel off outer skins. Slice tomatillos in half and place on baking tray. Cut onion and jalapeo into rough slices and toss with tomatillos. Roast for 20-25 minutes until tomatillos are soft.
                               </li>
                               <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                                Place roasted tomatillo mixture into a blender and add cilantro, olive oil, lime juice, salt, and honey. Puree until dressing is smooth. Taste and adjust flavors as you like.
                               </li>
                               <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                                Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3-4 days.
                               </li>
                              </ol>
