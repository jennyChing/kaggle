         Hey everyone! Im not sure if Im the only one, but when it comes to cooking, I like making things that require a maximum of 2 pots, preferably just one or two silicone spatulas anda singlechopping board to wash. I absolutely cannot stand having a huge pile of wash up to do (especially when you have cats running around and rushing to the sink to drink straight from the tap!). Insert the
         <span style="color: #99cc00;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            Gluten Free, Vegan
           </strong>
          </em>
         </span>
         <span style="color: #339966;">
          <strong>
           One Pan Mexican Quinoa
          </strong>
         </span>
         recipe that Levan and I have seen all over Pinterest.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/QUINOA1.jpg">
          <img alt="Healthy 100% Vegan One-Pan Mexican Quinoa. #vegan #recipe #quinoa | MyWifeMakes.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-1275" height="750" src="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/QUINOA1.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         Nothing could be easier than whipping something up in one pot, all in under 30 minutes. Even the quinoa cooks in the same pot! Dare I say, even Levan could probably successfully make this quinoa recipe  The original recipe comes from
         <a href="http://cooklikeachampion.com/2014/02/one-pan-mexican-quinoa/">
          Cook Like a Champion
         </a>
         , and I have tweakedit to our own taste. I added simple mirepoux to the base recipe, a handful of spinach into the mix, more cayenne and cuminfor an intense spicy kick, and then finally topped it all off with a homemade
         <span style="color: #3366ff;">
          <strong>
           Cashew Sour Cream
          </strong>
         </span>
         and crispy oven baked Mountain Bread. Mountain Bread is a vegan/gluten free/yeast free flatbread that we get in Australia, but feel free to replace it with oven baked tortillastrips!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa2.jpg">
          <img alt="Healthy 100% Vegan One-Pan Mexican Quinoa. #vegan #recipe #quinoa | MyWifeMakes.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-1276" height="750" src="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa2.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa3.jpg">
          <img alt="Healthy 100% Vegan One-Pan Mexican Quinoa. #vegan #recipe #quinoa | MyWifeMakes.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-1277" height="750" src="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa3.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         Let me tell you this: once you try
         <span style="color: #3366ff;">
          <strong>
           cashew sour cream
          </strong>
         </span>
         , youll most definitely
         <strong>
          NOT
         </strong>
         miss the dairy stuff. I was astonished at how similar it tasted to dairy sour cream, even though it only required a handful of ingredients to make. I can imagine using it in vegan stroganoff, potato salad, and a whole load of other vegan dishes. It only keeps for
         <strong>
          a week
         </strong>
         in the refrigerator, but I can assure you that the entire batch will probably be wolfed down in less than a day. Using a
         <a href="http://www.mywifemakes.com/2014/10/21/9-reasons-to-buy-a-vitamix/">
          <strong>
           Vitamix
          </strong>
         </a>
         also ensures an incredibly smooth texture,
         <em>
          but do remember to make the FULL BATCH instead of a half batch.
         </em>
         Initially I tried making a half-recipe but then soon came to the realization that it wasnt blending smoothly because there just wasnt enough of it in the blender!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa4.jpg">
          <img alt="Healthy 100% Vegan One-Pan Mexican Quinoa. #vegan #recipe #quinoa | MyWifeMakes.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-1278" height="750" src="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa4.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa5.jpg">
          <img alt="Healthy 100% Vegan One-Pan Mexican Quinoa. #vegan #recipe #quinoa | MyWifeMakes.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-1279" height="750" src="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa5.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         As for the quinoa recipe  well, a trillion re-pins cant be wrong now can it? It was really really delicious and thoroughly perfumed with the taste of mexico. When combined with crispy mountain bread strips and cool cashew sour cream, it was like eating the lightest, healthiest taco youve ever had. I reckon someone should rename the quinoa recipe to Taco Quinoa! Do give it a go, you most probably already have all the ingredients you need in your pantry. One Pan Mexican Quinoa 
         <strong>
          Inexpensive, delicious, healthy
         </strong>
         andsomething Ill definitely be keeping in my repertoire from now on.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa62.jpg">
          <img alt="Healthy 100% Vegan One-Pan Mexican Quinoa. #vegan #recipe #quinoa | MyWifeMakes.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-1280" height="773" src="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quinoa62.jpg" width="515"/>
         </a>
        </p>

         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          One Pan Mexican Quinoa + Cashew Sour Cream
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://d31zbndex795j6.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/image-2.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.mywifemakes.com/easyrecipe-print/1274-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            My Wife Makes
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            <strong>
             For the Quinoa:
            </strong>
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon Olive Oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cloves Garlic, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 stalk Celery, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 small Carrot, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Spring Onions, chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 Jalapeos, seeded and finely chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup Uncooked Quinoa, rinsed well and drained
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cups vegetable broth
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 (15 ounce) can Black Beans, drained and rinsed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 (14.5 oz) can Diced Tomatoes, with juice
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup Frozen Corn
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon Cumin
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon Cayenne
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 teaspoons Sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon kosher salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup chopped fresh cilantro
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Avocado, peeled and cubed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup Baby Spinach
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Juice of 1 Lime
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            <br/>
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            <strong>
             For Cashew Sour Cream:
            </strong>
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup Raw Cashews (soaked overnight in water and then drained)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon Apple Cider Vinegar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Juice of 1 Lemon
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon Salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon Nutritional Yeast
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <strong>
             For Quinoa:
            </strong>
            Heat the oil in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the garlic, celery, carrot, spring onoin and jalapeos and saut for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Stir in the quinoa, broth, beans, tomatoes, corn, cumin, cayenne, sugar and salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cover and let cook for 20-25 minutes, until the liquid is fully absorbed. Remove from heat, stir in cilantro, avocado, spinach and lime juice. Season to taste. Top with cashew sour cream and oven-crisped tortilla strips.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <strong>
             For Cashew Sour Cream:
            </strong>
            Combine ingredients in a blender and blend till very smooth. Keep in air tight container in the fridge for up to 1 week.
           </li>
          </ol>
