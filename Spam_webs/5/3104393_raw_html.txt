     <p>
      <b>
       Woy Woy
      </b>
      is a coastal town and a southern
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Central_Coast_suburbs" title="List of Central Coast suburbs">
       suburb
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Central_Coast_(New_South_Wales)" title="Central Coast (New South Wales)">
       Central Coast
      </a>
      region of
      <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">
       New South Wales
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
       Australia
      </a>
      , located on the southern reaches of
      <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Water" title="Brisbane Water">
       Brisbane Water
      </a>
      79km (49mi) north of
      <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">
       Sydney
      </a>
      . It is an important population centre within the
      <a href="/wiki/City_of_Gosford" title="City of Gosford">
       City of Gosford
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Local_government_areas_of_New_South_Wales" title="Local government areas of New South Wales">
       local government area
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Woy Woy is located in the northern half of the Woy Woy Peninsula, a densely populated estuarine peninsula that also includes the districts of
      <a href="/wiki/Umina_Beach,_New_South_Wales" title="Umina Beach, New South Wales">
       Umina Beach
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ettalong_Beach,_New_South_Wales" title="Ettalong Beach, New South Wales">
       Ettalong Beach
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Blackwall,_New_South_Wales" title="Blackwall, New South Wales">
       Blackwall
      </a>
      , in addition to several small sub-districts.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Woy Woy Peninsula is the most populous area of the Central Coast. The historical and commercial core of Woy Woy is located around the railway station at the northern tip of the peninsula while its residential districts merge imperceptibly southwards with Umina and Ettalong. (Woy Woy officially ends at Veron Road and Gallipoli Avenue; and Umina begins beyond this.)
     </p>
     <p>
      Woy Woy is considered a
      <a href="/wiki/Commuter_town" title="Commuter town">
       dormitory town
      </a>
      of Sydney.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Woy_Woy,_New_South_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_name">
       Origin of name
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Woy_Woy,_New_South_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origin of name">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The double name is a corruption of the indigenous term apparently taken from the local
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darkinjung_people" title="Darkinjung people">
       Darkinjung
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_Australians" title="Indigenous Australians">
       Aboriginal
      </a>
      people, and reputedly means 'big lagoon' or 'much water', referring to the deep tidal channel adjacent to the town centre.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was originally known as Webb's Flat, named for James Webb, the first European settler of the Brisbane Water region in 1823, and was first explored by a party led by
      <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_New_South_Wales" title="Governor of New South Wales">
       Governor
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Phillip" title="Arthur Phillip">
       Arthur Phillip
      </a>
      in 1789.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gos-hist_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-gos-hist-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Small_coastal_settlement">
       Small coastal settlement
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Woy_Woy,_New_South_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Small coastal settlement">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Woy Woy developed rapidly as a small coastal resort north of Sydney with the construction in 1888 of the northern railway.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Electrification of the Main Northern rail line running through Woy Woy to Gosford in 1960 prompted rapid residential development in and around Woy Woy in the 1960s and 1970s as its relatively low-priced properties became an important part of the Sydney commuter belt, with rail journey times of just over an hour to reach
      <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">
       Sydney
      </a>
      's central business district.
     </p>
     <p>
      During the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">
       Second World War
      </a>
      , an airfield was constructed on the Woy Woy Peninsula as a satellite field of Schofields airfield. Schofields was used by the Royal Navy Fleet Air Arm (RN FAA) during WW2. Woy Woy airfield had a single runway sealed north/south. There were 4 pens for medium bombers, probably Grumman Avengers. The airfield was last used in 1946, the land subsequently developed for residential use.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      December 1948, there were private enterprise plans announced to develop 1259acres of light industrial land, 800acres of accompanying housing, a railway connection with the existing main line and the conversion of the
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      era airstrip at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ettalong_Beach" title="Ettalong Beach">
       Ettalong Beach
      </a>
      into an airport. If the concept had proceeded all costs would have been covered by the developer, including water, sewerage and other amenities. At the time it was predicted that up to 50,000 jobs would have been developed within 10 to 25years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The $24million Peninsula Leisure Centre opened on 29 October 2005 after significant delays.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Schools">
       Schools
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Woy_Woy,_New_South_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Schools">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Woy Woy has one Catholic school, St John the Baptist Primary School, located in Dulkara Road, Woy Woy. Schooling offered by the Sisters of St. Joseph commenced in Woy Woy in 1922 at the original St John the Baptist Church (Parish hall since 2007) located on the corner of Blackwall and Victoria Roads, Woy Woy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There are also two public primary schools, Woy Woy South and Woy Woy, with the senior campus of
      <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Water_Secondary_College" title="Brisbane Water Secondary College">
       Brisbane Water Secondary College
      </a>
      also located within Woy Woy.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Business">
       Business
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Woy_Woy,_New_South_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Business">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Woy Woy comprises 34,000 square metres of commercially zoned land of which 60% consists of retail businesses together with a mix of professional services and major supermarkets including Deepwater Plaza and Peninsula Plaza. Woy Woy represents the commercial heart of the Peninsula and vital commuter transport hub. The town centre is enhanced by the Woy Woy waterfront precinct and Woy Woy Channel with ferry links to Davistown.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Woy Woy commercial centre is represented by the Peninsula Chamber of Commerce which is an affiliated member of the
      <a href="/wiki/NSW_Business_Chamber" title="NSW Business Chamber">
       NSW Business Chamber
      </a>
      . Woy Woy is recognised as the commercial hub of the Peninsula accommodating numerous legal practices, medical centres, banks and professionals. It is largely focused around the Woy Woy Rail Station (major transport hub) and bus interchange with a heavy retail concentration at Deepwater Plaza shopping centre on western edge of the commercial centre and Peninsula Plaza adjacent to the heritage listed Woy Woy Library. Small cafs and restaurants have clustered along the Woy Woy waterfront adjacent to the heritage listed Bayview Hotel and Woy Woy Hotel on Brickwharf Road. The traditional main street (Blackwall Road) has struggled in recent years from pressure from the major shopping centres and the resurgence of the thriving Umina Beach town centre.
     </p>

     <p>
      As a regional area, job losses have had an impact on the local economy - a recent example being the closure of the F.C. Nichols
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abattoir" title="Abattoir">
       abattoir
      </a>

       drought

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">
       Culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Woy_Woy,_New_South_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Culture">
        edit

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      An annual Brisbane Water
      <a href="/wiki/Oyster" title="Oyster">
       Oyster
      </a>
      Festival is held on the first Sunday in November at Ettalong Beach after being relocated from the Woy Woy waterfront in 2005 due to space restrictions. The event outgrew the location with over 20,000 people attending annually. The "Brisbane Water" Oyster Festival was inaugurated in 2000 and is organised by the Peninsula Chamber of Commerce.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThePeninsular_OysterFestival_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ThePeninsular_OysterFestival-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Woy Woy Little Theatre Company
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      perform four plays every year at The Peninsula Theatre, the sister theatre to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laycock_Street_Theatre" title="Laycock Street Theatre">
       Laycock Street Theatre
      </a>
      in Gosford. Performances are generally based on strict plays rather than devised or improvised theatre forms and have been doing so since 1962.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After their retirement, the parents and younger brother of comedian
      <a href="/wiki/Spike_Milligan" title="Spike Milligan">
       Spike Milligan
      </a>
      (19182002) moved to Woy Woy; as a result, Spike spent some time in the town and was occasionally jocularly referred to as "the boy from Woy Woy". Woy Woy was the home to the now defunct festival known as "Spike Fest", which celebrated Milligan's life and works. Milligan famously named Woy Woy "the largest above ground cemetery in the world" when visiting in the 1960s. He made numerous references to Woy Woy in the radio series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Idiot_Weekly" title="The Idiot Weekly">
        The Idiot Weekly
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In July 2007, a new cycle bridge near Woy Woy was named the "Spike Milligan Bridge"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bridge_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bridge-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Another internationally known resident of Woy Woy was
      <a href="/wiki/Olive_Riley" title="Olive Riley">
       Olive Riley
      </a>
      (18992008), of the Woy Woy Community Nursing Home, who became recognised as the world's oldest known blogger.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-olive_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-olive-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      From February 2007, aged 107, she started an internet blog and also appeared in a number of
      <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">
       YouTube
      </a>
      videos.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Her last post was made on 26 June 2008, two and a half weeks prior to her death on 12 July, aged 108.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Woy_Woy_railway_station,_New_South_Wales" title="Woy Woy railway station, New South Wales">
