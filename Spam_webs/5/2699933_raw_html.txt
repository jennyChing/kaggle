        <h1 class="entry-title">
         What is Whey Protein
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="author vcard">
          Article by
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://fitblogger.ca/author/guest-blogger/" rel="author">
            Guest Contributor
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <em>
           <a href="http://fitblogger.ca/wp-content/images/2011/09/Fitblogger.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-12674" height="199" src="http://fitblogger.ca/wp-content/images/2011/09/Fitblogger-300x199.jpg" title="" width="300"/>
           </a>
           Appears courtesy of
           <a href="http://teamintrafit.wordpress.com">
            Team Intrafit.
           </a>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Many people who begin a fitness program find themselves asking what is
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whey_protein">
           whey protein
          </a>
          ? Protein is essential for all living things and whey protein has gotten the reputation of being the king of all proteins for us humans.
         </p>
         <p>
          During the mid 1900s, researchers were experimenting with proteins from plant and animal sources. They discovered that some proteins have all the essential amino acids to support growth and health while others do not. After years of testing and retesting, they determined that whey protein scored the highest in
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein_sequencing">
           amino acid composition
          </a>
          , but more importantly it also had a high
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_value">
           biological value
          </a>
          (a measure of how much of a protein is actually used by the body).
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.wheyoflife.org/benefits.cfm">
           Whey protein has been clinically proven to:
          </a>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Help build muscle
          </li>
          <li>
           Improve physical performance
          </li>
          <li>
           Sharpen your mind
          </li>
          <li>
           Help your body ward off disease
          </li>
          <li>
           Enhance circulatory system function
          </li>
          <li>
           Improve overall health
          </li>
          <li>
           Has potent anti-aging effects
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h2>
          3 different types of whey protein
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Concentrate whey protein:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Protein varied from 25-89% protein
          </li>
          <li>
           Higher levels of
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_activity">
            bioactive
           </a>
           compounds
          </li>
          <li>
           Contains low level of fat and cholesterol
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Isolate whey protein:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Processed to remove the fat and lactose
          </li>
          <li>
           Usually lower in bioactivated compounds
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Hydrolyzed whey protein:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Hydrolysis process which breaks down the protein chains into small fractions called peptides
          </li>
          <li>
           Easily digested and less potential for allergic reactions
          </li>
          <li>
           Bitter taste
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          *Your bodys activity level, and the type of activity you perform will dictate what type of protein you need.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Benefits of Whey Proteins Key Amino Acids
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Branched-chain_amino_acid">
           BCAAS (Branched-Chain Amino Acids)
          </a>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Increases endurance
          </li>
          <li>
           Prevents fatigue
          </li>
          <li>
           Improves mental performance
          </li>
          <li>
           Increases energy levels
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenylalanine">
           Phenylalanine:
          </a>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Maintains nervous system health
          </li>
          <li>
           Relieves depression
          </li>
          <li>
           Elevates mood
          </li>
          <li>
           Decreases pain
          </li>
          <li>
           Boosts memory
          </li>
          <li>
           Suppresses appetite
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tryptophan">
           Tryptophan
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Relieves mild to moderate depression
          </li>
          <li>
           Relieves insomnia and promotes restful sleep
          </li>
          <li>
           Promotes weight loss by suppressing appetite
          </li>
          <li>
           Reduces overall sensation of pain (i.e. migraine headaches, general muscle pain)
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arginine">
           Arginine:
          </a>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Increases growth hormone levels
          </li>
          <li>
           Protects against heart disease
          </li>
          <li>
           Lowers blood pressure
          </li>
          <li>
           Improves poor circulation
          </li>
          <li>
           Aids in the production of creatine, an important source of energy
          </li>
          <li>
           Lowers ammonia levels, which can be increased in athletes due to frequent exercise
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glutamine">
           Glutamine
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Boosts immune system function
          </li>
          <li>
           Maintains muscle mass
          </li>
          <li>
           Prevents muscle catabolism (breakdown)
          </li>
          <li>
           Enhances glycogen storage
          </li>
          <li>
           Aids recovery from exercise
          </li>
          <li>
           Promotes healing
          </li>
          <li>
           Increases growth horrmone levels
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          The following is an interesting case study on the effect of protein-supplement timing (when is the best time to take my protein). The following information is how it appears at
          <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19478342">
           http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19478342.
           <br/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Effect of protein-supplement timing on strength, power, and body-composition changes in resistance-trained men.
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed?term=%22Hoffman%20JR%22%5BAuthor%5D">
           Hoffman JR
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed?term=%22Ratamess%20NA%22%5BAuthor%5D">
           Ratamess NA
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed?term=%22Tranchina%20CP%22%5BAuthor%5D">
           Tranchina CP
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed?term=%22Rashti%20SL%22%5BAuthor%5D">
           Rashti SL
          </a>
