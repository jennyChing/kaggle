         Yesterdays (October 8) selloff was broad-based and ugly, with just about every industry sector getting hit hard (utilities were an exception).
        </p>
        <p>
         The NASDAQ Composite ($COMP) and small-cap Russell 2000 Index ($RUT), the leading market averages in recent months, were hit the hardest with losses of -2.0% and -1.7% respectively.
        </p>
        <p>
         The NASDAQ is now the
         <b>
          only index still trading above the 50-day moving average
         </b>
         (but not by much).
        </p>
        <p>
         Individual leadership stocks, typically small to mid-cap stocks with a strong history and outlook of earnings growth, were hit hard as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whenever the major indices undergo a price correction, one of the most important factors we analyze is
         <strong>
          how well leading stocks hold up and show relative strength
         </strong>
         to the broad market.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, where does this leave us as we enter the new week?
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          <img alt="Market Timing Buy Signal Reversing" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3224" height="300" src="http://www.morpheustrading.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/cliffhanger-233x300.jpg" width="233"/>
          How To KnowWhen The Tide Is Turning
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         If leading stocks show relative strength by mostly ignoring weakness in the S&amp;P, Dow, and NASDAQ during broad-based price corrections, its a positive sign that tells us its safe to carry on entering new trades of these
         <strong>
          leading growth stocks, the best stocks to buy in a healthy market.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         However, when top stocks begin succumbing to the weight of the broad markets downward pressure, it quickly grabs our attention and tells us its
         <strong>
          time to lay off the gas pedal
         </strong>
         and take a more proactive stance with regard to managing existing positions for
         <strong>
          maximum profits and minimal losses.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          So, Is The Tide Turning?
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Yesterday, market leader
         <strong>
          LinkedIn Corporation ($LNKD)
         </strong>
         sliced through key support of its 50-day moving average on volume that surged to roughly 300% its average daily level. This volume spike tells us banks, hedge funds, mutual funds, and other institutions were driving the selling. Not a good sign for bulls.
        </p>
        <p>
         Other leading stocks that sold off on heavy volume yesterday included:
         <strong>
          Qihoo 360 Technology ($QIHU), Vipshop Holdings ($VIPS), Soufun Holdings ($SFUN), Pandora Media ($P), Mercadolibre ($MELI), Priceline.com ($PCLN), and Amazon.com ($AMZN).
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Although $PCLN and $AMZN had a rough day, both stocks are still trading above their respective 50-day moving averages (an intermediate-term line in the sand for many retail and institutional traders/investors).
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Balancing On The Cliff
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         The broad market rally is just barely hanging on. If leading stocks begin cracking below their 50-day moving averages en masse, our proprietaryMarket Timing Model will be forced to shift from buy to sell mode.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre new to the role that market timing rules play into our overall ETF and stock picking selection, please
         <a href="http://www.morpheustrading.com/blog/market-timing" target="_blank" title="A Market Timing System That Works On Any Market In The World">
          click here
         </a>
         to learn the general concept of our system for market timing, then check out
         <a href="http://www.morpheustrading.com/blog/market-timing-system" target="_blank" title="5 Modes Of Our Market Timing System">
          this article
         </a>
         that explains the five, rule-based modes of our model for timing the stock market.
         <br/>
         <a name="toes">
         </a>
         <br/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Keeping On Our Toes
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Yesterday was a busy day for subscribers of
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="https://www.morpheustrading.com/services/swing-trading-newsletter" target="_blank" title="The Wagner Daily signup page">
            The Wagner Daily
           </a>
          </em>
         </strong>
         , our nightly swing trading newsletter (which includes access to our market timing methodology).
        </p>
        <p>
         On the individual stock side, we sold Bitauto ($BITA) and LifeLock ($LOCK) for
         <b>
          decent share price gains of 36.7% and 13.8% respectively.
         </b>
         LinkedIn ($LNKD) hit our stop and we sold for an average loss of just 2.7%. In case you missed it, you may want to check out
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.morpheustrading.com/blog/buy-pullback-lifelock-lock/" target="_blank" title="Trading Strategy Video Detailing Buy Entry Into LifeLock ($LOCK)">
           How We Bought A Pullback In LifeLock ($LOCK) For A 15% GainSo Far
          </a>
          ,
         </em>
         a trading strategy video from a few weeks ago that explains why we bought $LOCK when we did.
        </p>
        <p>
         As for the ETF side, we sold two existing ETF trades yesterday to minimize losses. On
         <strong>
          SPDR Biotech ($XBI)
         </strong>
         and
         <strong>
          Direxion Small-Cap Bull 3x ($TNA)
         </strong>
         , we lost 3.6% and 6.3% respectively. However, these losses were much smaller than the
         <strong>
          44% gain we secured in Guggenheim Solar ETF ($TAN)
         </strong>
         last week (check back on this blog for an educational technical review of this $TAN trade by the end of this week.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          <img alt="Profitable Stock Trading Is All About Math" class=" wp-image-3225 alignright" height="205" src="http://www.morpheustrading.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/deduce.jpg" width="256"/>
          How To Be Profitable In A Reversing Market? Simple Math
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Many traders, particularly newbies, are obsessed with the accuracy of win rates (percentage of winning trades vs. losing trades) when analyzing how well a trading system is likely to perform over the long-term.
        </p>
        <p>
         However, the reality is that a traders long-term profitability depends largely on other factors, such as the dollar amount of the average winning trade compared to the dollar amount of the average losing trade.
        </p>
        <p>
         If, for example, a trader has a win rate of 70%, but allows their average losing trade to be 300% larger than their average winning trade, he/shewill be net negative over the long-term.
        </p>
        <p>
         Conversely, if a trader has a win rate of just 50%, but allows the average winning trades to ride to being just double (200%) the size of an average losing trade,
         <strong>
          the trader will become net profitable over the long-term.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Lately, weve been closing our swing trades with about a 50% win rate (maybe even slightly lower), but our average gain has been MUCH larger than the average loser.
        </p>
        <p>
         With the three stock trades we closed yesterday ($BITA, $LOCK, and $LNKD), our average winner was a share price gain of 25.3%, while the sole losing trade was just 2.7%.
        </p>
        <p>
         That makes for a
         <strong>
          whoppingreward-risk ratio of more than 9 to 1
         </strong>
         (anything ratio above 3 to 1 is generally considered to be quite good).
        </p>
