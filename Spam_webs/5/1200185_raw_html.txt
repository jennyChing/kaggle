         Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-01-16T09:00:36+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 16, 2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

            Jodi
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">

           26 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <h2>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="stretch yourself header" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4940" height="140" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/stretch-yourself-header.png" width="517"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/stretch-yourself-header.png&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          Welcome to Sew Fearless! This post is part of the Stretch Yourself Series hosted by
          <a href="http://madmim.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://madmim.com/', 'Mad Mim']);" target="_blank">
           Mad Mim
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://onelittleminuteblog.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://onelittleminuteblog.com/', 'One Little Minute']);" target="_blank">
           One Little Minute
          </a>
          . The topic today is
          <strong>
           STRETCH
          </strong>
          <strong>
           LACE
          </strong>
          and I get to have the pleasure of joining in on the fun. Be sure to check out my fellow Guest Contributor of the Day,
          <a href="http://verypurpleperson.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://verypurpleperson.com/', 'Novita of Very Purple Person']);" target="_blank">
           Novita of Very Purple Person
          </a>
          too!
         </em>
         <br/>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Lace Drape Cardigan by SewFearless.com [includes tips on how to sew stretch lace]" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4948" height="398" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1230729-598x398.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1230729-598x398.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
         I went to the store with thinking about a funky printed draped cardigan thingy, but once this ethereal drapey-ness caught my eye all other plans went flying out the window. Never mind that I have never sewn lace before.
         <em>
          It. Needed. To. Happen.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Lace Drape Cardigan by SewFearless.com [includes tips on how to sew stretch lace]" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4950" height="598" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1230737-398x598.jpg" width="398"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1230737-398x598.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         And happen it did, but not before there was a good amount of dragging of feet out of fear that it wouldnt turn out quite right and not being sure of how to tackle the whole thing. All thats over now. boo-freakin-ya!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Lace Drape Cardigan by SewFearless.com [includes tips on how to sew stretch lace]" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4951" height="598" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1230741-398x598.jpg" width="398"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1230741-398x598.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Pattern:
          <a href="http://www.simplicity.com/p-1560-misses-tops.aspx" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.simplicity.com/p-1560-misses-tops.aspx', 'Simplicity 2603']);" target="_blank">
           Simplicity 2603
          </a>
          , View D (sleeves extended)
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Size: Medium, let out to Large for hips at side seams
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Material:
          <a href="http://www.joann.com/sew-classic-knits-stretch-lace-ivory-fabric/zprd_11794260a/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.joann.com/sew-classic-knits-stretch-lace-ivory-fabric/zprd_11794260a/', 'Sew Classic Knits Ivory Stretch Lace from Joanns\n']);" target="_blank">
           Sew Classic Knits Ivory Stretch Lace from Joanns
           <br/>
          </a>
         </strong>
         (p.s. my bump is a bout 4 weeks bigger than this now, much much bigger)
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Whats all this have to do with sewing with knits and the rest of the Stretch Yourself series? Well stretch lace and knits share many of the same properties, and, like knits, it turns out the stretch lace isnt as scary as one would think. Ive even done the experimentation and research so you dont have to. Lets do this thing!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Tips and Tricks for working with Stretch Lace - sewfearless.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4963" height="336" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Tips-and-Tricks-for-working-with-Stretch-Lace.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Tips-and-Tricks-for-working-with-Stretch-Lace.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <span id="more-4869">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         Choosing the Right Project
        </h2>
        <p>
         Although it wasnt included in the fabric recommendations, I used a pattern designed for knits for this stretchy fabric. I like to livedangerouslylike that. However, it is always a good idea to compare your fabrics stretch to the stretch guide on the envelope. I didnt.
         <em>
          oops
         </em>
         .
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="comparing to stretch guide - sewfearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4931" height="398" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_12312881-598x398.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_12312881-598x398.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         As you can see here, the lace doesnt meet the recommended stretch requirements. Not even remotely.Consequently the top was just a bit too small for my measurements. Fortunately, I was able to rescue the project from near disaster by letting out the arm/side seam as much as I could. Learn from my mistake! Use knit patterns designed for little-to-no stretch, or compensate by going up in size.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Cutting it Out
        </h2>
        <p>
         If these tips for cutting out your stretch lace sound familiar, that is because they are the same tips given for working with knits. Treat your fabric well and it will treat you well. (Hopefully.)
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="lay out lace on a large surface while cutting - sewfearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4968" height="398" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1229773-598x398.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1229773-598x398.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Find yourself a large smooth surface area to work on.
          </strong>
          I often find myself laying out my stretch fabrics on floors, because they give me more space and my fabric wont stretch as it drapes off the edge of a table. (Oh, to have a gigantic cutting table!! sigh.)
         </li>
         <li>
          Once your fabric is laid out,
          <strong>
           give it a chance to relax.
          </strong>
          The fabric stretched out on the bolt, or while folded in your stash, it needs a chance to recover from the abuse. You dont want it to relax
          <em>
           after
          </em>
          your pieces are cut out! Lay it out as smoothly and as straight as possible and then let it sit for a couple of hours or even overnight. That being said, I never seem to have the patience (or the floor space!) for that. I think I might have given this fabric an hour to sit. It worked out just fine.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Use
           <a href="http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/11/diy-pattern-weights-ribbon-wrapped-washers/" title="DIY Pattern Weights  Ribbon Wrapped Washers">
            pattern weights
           </a>
           instead of pins to hold the pattern pieces in place.
          </strong>
          The pins wont do much good with all the holes in the fabric anyways. Dont have any pattern weights? In the past, I have used (full) cans, cups, play blocks, and silverware.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Needles &amp; Thread
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Needle Choice for Stretch Lace - sewfearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4874" height="398" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231282-598x398.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231282-598x398.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Needle  I used a Shmetz Stretch 75/11 needle
          </strong>
          for this delicate fabric and it worked perfectly. Choosing the right needle for the job can be bewildering at first. There are as many kinds of needles as there are kinds of projects! This
          <a href="http://www.schmetzneedles.com/learning/pdf/schmetz-needle-chart.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','download','http://www.schmetzneedles.com/learning/pdf/schmetz-needle-chart.pdf']);" target="_blank">
           Guide to Needles by Shmetz [PDF]
          </a>
          is helpful. Dont get too bogged down in the details though, if your needle is working well (minimal fabric damage, no skipped stitches) than you have the right needle for the job! Test before you sew, and keep trying a different needles until you find one that works well enough for you.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Thread
          </strong>
           As long as you are using a good stretch stitch, your standard
          <strong>
           all-purpose thread
          </strong>
          will work just fine.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Stitches and Seam Finishes
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="finished seam on stretch lace - sewfearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4937" height="398" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231313-598x398.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231313-598x398.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I spent a considerable amount of time before beginning testing out stitches and seam finishes. The seams would be somewhat visible from the outside and I didnt want sloppy seam allowances to ruin the look of the garment.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Stretch Stitch - SewFearless.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-4928" height="359" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_12313151-398x598.jpg" width="239"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_12313151-398x598.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I used my machines 
         <strong>
          stretch stitch
         </strong>
          for sewing together all of my seams and hems in this project. If you dont have a specific stretch stitch, a narrow zig-zag will do.With the seams sewn, all that remains is what to do with the seam allowances. Here are the options I came up with
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Stretch Lace Seam Finish Options - sewfearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4926" height="392" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/seam-finishing-options-598x392.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/seam-finishing-options-598x392.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.themotherhuddle.com/how-to-finish-seams-with-a-sewing-machine/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.themotherhuddle.com/how-to-finish-seams-with-a-sewing-machine/', 'Zig-Zag']);" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             Zig-Zag
            </strong>
           </a>
           : This is by far the simplest option. Perfectly functional, but looks a bit too homemade for my tastes.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://blog.makezine.com/craft/sewing-101-french-seams/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://blog.makezine.com/craft/sewing-101-french-seams/', 'French Seam']);" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             French Seam
            </strong>
           </a>
           : This is a
           <em>
            super
           </em>
           classy finish, and would be very d0able for straight seams. I decided it was too tricky to use for the armholes or along the neck seam in this project.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0033PCT2Q/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=sewfear-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0033PCT2Q" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0033PCT2Q/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=sewfear-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0033PCT2Q', 'Dritz Seams Great Seam Finish']);" target="_blank">
             Dritz Seams Great Seam Finish
            </a>
           </strong>
            This is translucent bias cut tape covers the seam allowance for a subtle, non-bulky, soft finish. I found it tricky for me to apply neatly, maybe with more practice this could be a good option.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Bias Bound
           </strong>
            Another option, similar to the Seams Great, is to
           <a href="http://www.coletterie.com/tutorials-tips-tricks/tutorial-bias-bound-seams" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.coletterie.com/tutorials-tips-tricks/tutorial-bias-bound-seams', 'bind the seam allowance with bias tape']);" target="_blank">
            bind the seam allowance with bias tape
           </a>
           (or
           <a href="http://sewaholic.net/seam-finishes-bound-edges/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://sewaholic.net/seam-finishes-bound-edges/', ' seam binding']);" target="_blank">
            seam binding
           </a>
           ). This method could be good for adding stability to seams that need it (like the shoulder seam), but it was too bulky for this delicate fabric.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Overlock Stitch[
            <em>
             Recommended Method
            </em>
            ]
           </strong>
            If you haveaccess to a serger or a standard sewing machine with a overlock stitch, this would the method I would recommend.
          </li>
         </ol>
        </ol>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Seam Finish Options for Stretch Lace - SewFearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4927" height="398" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231280-598x398.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231280-598x398.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Adding 
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000YYZQIS/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=sewfear-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000YYZQIS" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000YYZQIS/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=sewfear-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000YYZQIS', 'stay tape']);" target="_blank">
          stay tape
         </a>
          to seams gives extra strength and stability where needed. I used it for the neck/shoulder seam in this project.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Stay Tape - SewFearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4929" height="359" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231284-598x359.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231284-598x359.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its subtle but it makes a big difference.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="stay tape in lace shoulder seams - sewfearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4936" height="398" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231311-598x398.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231311-598x398.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         The Wonders of Wonder Tape
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Lace Hems with Wonder Tape - sewfearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4935" height="398" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231305-598x398.jpg" width="598"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1231305-598x398.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <br/>
         Ive seriously fallen in love with
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000YZAQJQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=sewfear-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000YZAQJQ" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000YZAQJQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=sewfear-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000YZAQJQ', 'Wonder Tape']);" target="_blank">
          Wonder Tape
         </a>
          a double-sided wash-away adhesive tape. It was my secret weapon for this project.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Wonder Tape worked double duty as a stabilizer and as a pin substitute.
          </strong>
          I would have had a difficult time holding this holey fabric together with pins, but Wonder Tape kept everything in place. It also stabilized the fabric and prevented fabric distortion while sewing.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           1/4 inch Wonder Tape made 5/8 hems ridiculously easy
          </strong>
          , and in this hemming heavy project that was a sanity saver.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Wonder Tape Hemming Trick for Stretch Lace and Knits - SewFearless.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4934" height="598" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Page_1-462x598.jpg" width="462"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Page_1-462x598.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Easy right? Ive used this method on knits as well. Works like a charm every time.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          FYI: If you dont like the look of a plain ol double hem and dont need the stretch in the hem, there are other options to try, like a
          <strong>
           <a href="http://stitchcraftonline.com/blog/?p=42" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://stitchcraftonline.com/blog/?p=42', 'rolled hem']);" target="_blank">
            rolled hem
           </a>
           or the
           <a href="http://grosgrainfabulous.blogspot.com/2010/10/technique-tuesday-finishing-lace.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://grosgrainfabulous.blogspot.com/2010/10/technique-tuesday-finishing-lace.html', 'Grosgrains method']);" target="_blank">
            Grosgrains method
           </a>
           of wavy satin stitches
          </strong>
          .
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Lace Drape Cardigan by SewFearless.com [includes tips on how to sew stretch lace]" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4946" height="598" src="http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1230725-398x598.jpg" width="398"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sewfearless.com/2013/01/16/stretch-yourself-working-with-stretch-lace/&amp;media=http://sewfearless.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Andy_Bonjour_1230725-398x598.jpg&amp;description=Stretch Yourself: Working With Stretch Lace')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Not so bad right? It turns out that stretch lace is nothing to getsqueamishabout! Youve totally got this!
