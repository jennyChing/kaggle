       <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="15186113" data-link="http://insights.dice.com/2014/07/01/americans-chocolate-alcohol-tv-smartphones/" data-summary="" data-title="Americans Would Give Up Chocolate, Alcohol, TV for Smartphones">
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://insights.dice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Screen-Shot-2014-07-01-at-10.21.23-AM.png">
         <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-07-01 at 10.21.23 AM" class="aligncenter wp-image-120484 size-full" height="303" src="http://insights.dice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Screen-Shot-2014-07-01-at-10.21.23-AM.png" width="592"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        A new report suggests that Americans love their smartphones so much, theyd sooner give up alcohol, chocolate, television, shopping and even their car than part with their iPhone or
        <a href="http://www.dice.com/job/results?caller=basic&amp;q=Android&amp;x=all&amp;p=/?icid=dicenews">
         Android
        </a>
        device.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://newsroom.bankofamerica.com/sites/bankofamerica.newshq.businesswire.com/files/press_kit/additional/2014_BAC_Trends_in_Consumer_Mobility.pdf" target="_blank">
         The report
        </a>
        (PDF), commissioned by
        <a href="http://www.dice.com/jobsearch/company/DiceId_10118499/10118499/?icid=dicenews" target="_blank">
         Bank of America
        </a>
        , surveyed 1,000 adults in the United States. Roughly 85 percent of those surveyed checked their smartphone at least a few times a day, while 35 admitted that they did so constantly; only 13 percent said they hardly ever consulted their device.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <a href="http://www.dice.com/job/results?caller=searchagain&amp;q=Mobile+Developer&amp;x=all&amp;p=/?icid=dicenews">
          Click here for mobile developer jobs.
         </a>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        When questioned further, Americans ranked their phone as more vital to their lives than their social networking profiles, coffee, microwave, television, laptop or PC, while rating it just as important as their deodorant or car. Fortunately, those surveyed ranked their toothbrush and the Internet as far more critical to their daily lives, because you need to draw the line somewhere.
       </p>
       <p>
        Still not fully convinced that Americans
        <em>
         really
        </em>
        like their smartphones? Around 45 percent of those surveyed said theyd give up alcohol before they gave up their mobile device; 34 percent said the same for chocolate; 22 percent would abandon shopping; 16 percent would chuck television and movies from their lives; and 8 percent would surrender their car.
       </p>
       <p>
        Bank of America commissioned the survey in order to find out more about how people use their smartphones in a banking context. Given the data, the bank probably doesnt have to worry overmuchit seems that Americans rely on their smartphones for pretty much everything.
       </p>
