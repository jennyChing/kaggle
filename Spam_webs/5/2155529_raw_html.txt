     <h1 itemprop="headline">
      Icelands Secret Swimming Pool in the Valley
     </h1>
    </header>
    <div class="meta left">
     <!-- Author avatar -->
     <p class="avatar">
     </p>
     <!-- Author name -->
     <p class="author">
      By
      <a href="http://www.messynessychic.com/author/vanessa/" rel="author">
       MessyNessy
      </a>
     </p>
     <!-- Date published -->
     <p class="date">
      6th May, 2015
     </p>
     <!-- Comment count -->
     <a class="comments" href="#comments">
      <span class="fa fa-comment">
      </span>
      <fb:comments-count href="http://www.messynessychic.com/2015/05/06/icelands-secret-swimming-pool-in-the-valley/">
      </fb:comments-count>
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="content left" itemprop="articleBody">
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <img alt="Seljavallalaug4" class="size-full wp-image-65051" height="617" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Seljavallalaug4.jpg" width="767"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      This one isnt in the guide books or on the tourist trail, but out there in the nowhere. Icelands oldest swimming pool, built into the mountainside in a narrow valley, has been gathering hot water trickling down fromEyjafjallajkull (I cant pronounce it either) sinceit was constructed in 1923. Today it is abandoned and covered with thick ice throughout the winter until once every summer when a few faithful volunteers clearfor theadventurous travellers who care to find it
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Seljavallalaug2" class="size-full wp-image-65054" height="527" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Seljavallalaug2.jpg" width="798"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Seljavallalaug" class="size-full wp-image-65053" height="541" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Seljavallalaug.jpg" width="727"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Seljavallalaug is Icelands best kept secret, promising a breathtaking dip in the middle of the mountains that youll never forget. There are changing room facilities here so you can change and keep your belongings in the simple whitebuilding by the pool.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Seljavallalaug1" class="size-full wp-image-65057" height="541" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Seljavallalaug1.jpg" width="726"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      The protected 25-metre pool is located next to a stream at the bottom of the valley not farfrom Seljavellir, down an uneven trail that looks like it leads to nowhere. You cant see it until you get to it and will have to jump over the little stream.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <img alt="OLYMPUS DIGITAL CAMERA" class="size-large wp-image-65056" height="1240" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Isl_Seljavallalaug-930x1240.jpeg" width="930"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      But withdaylightbeing somewhat of a luxury in Iceland, Id suggest setting out on your trail early on.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <img alt="14941820840_61c0830fb8_k" class="size-large wp-image-65059" height="618" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/14941820840_61c0830fb8_k-930x618.jpg" width="930"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      And watch out for that old Icelandic humor.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="3366212693_5c8d660cc1_b" class="size-large wp-image-65060" height="698" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/3366212693_5c8d660cc1_b-930x698.jpg" width="930"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Dont worry, this next photograph has nothing to do with the pools sanitation standards!(Butperhaps not entirely accidental in their order on my part)!
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="ashpool" class="size-large wp-image-65055" height="620" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ashpool-930x620.jpg" width="930"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Access to Seljavallalaug became more difficult after the 2010 volcanic eruptions when the pool was filled with ash. But once again, faithful locals gathered to clean it with loaders andexcavators.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <img alt="icelandpool1" class="size-full wp-image-65065" height="444" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/icelandpool1.jpg" width="803"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      The colour of the water does of course vary throughout the year, depending on when it was last maintained, but most visitors will vouch for the naturalwaterbeing completely safe to swim in, and even good for the skin.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <img alt="5979280920_78a3772a4f_o" class="size-full wp-image-65063" height="466" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/5979280920_78a3772a4f_o1.jpg" width="700"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Seljavallalaug was originally builtto provide the locals with a place where they could learn how to swim, because at the beginning of the 20th century, most Icelanders didnt know how despite many of them living off fishing.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="15734205723_3ff6128e62_b" class="size-large wp-image-65058" height="620" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/15734205723_3ff6128e62_b-930x620.jpg" width="930"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Today, you cant graduate school in Iceland unless youve passed a swim test and in 1990, a new modern swimming pool was built for the area somewhat closer to civilisation. ButSeljavallalaug is a protected site and more than 90 years after it was built, it remains open for people to go swimming undisturbed in the old pool free of charge at their own risk of course.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="icelandpool" class="size-full wp-image-65064" height="533" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/icelandpool.jpg" width="796"/>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Find the
      <a href="https://www.google.com/maps?ll=63.416416,-19.723981&amp;z=7&amp;t=m&amp;hl=en-US&amp;gl=IS&amp;mapclient=embed&amp;cid=13173919015778478161" target="_blank">
       <strong>
        location here
       </strong>
      </a>
      .
     </p>
