Being Uber-Productive


 The new entrepreneurs are expected to be fast. Fast to learn, fast to do, fast to decide. After all, entrepreneurs succeed when they do things faster and better than a large corporation.


 That means being uber-productive. This year I experimented with uber-productivity. I made a new year resolutions a year ago. Not so naive about my self-discipline any more, I did not kid myself that I would just do it. I had to pre-do to make sure that I actually do it. Let me explain.


Pre-Doing Your Goals, or Beta-Testing Your Life


 When I set a big goal, like change my life, or be more productive, I normally failed. Predictably so. Until I started using a tool, well familiar in the startup world. You can call it beta testing. Or just pre-doing. When a startup company launches, they are usually so far from achieving their goal that they have to launch before they are ready. Otherwise they would never launch. Their money would run out, the teams enthusiasm would drain and the investors would lose interest. Thats why a startup launches a beta, something that looks like their product and almost works, but not quite. Same idea goes for some Kickstarter projects - they pre-sell something that is not quite done yet. So pre-doing works for companies with small resources and a lot of enthusiasm. Why couldnt it work for people with little patience and a lot of optimism about life around New Year? That was me last year. So I wanted to pre-do my goal of being uber-productive.


Productivity Test in Village, Then in City


 I started by doing what I do best - visualize what I will do. I visualized a productive live for me in a series of infographics (see above). That was the pre-doing part. Then I followed the graphics like a mind map. I went to an Indian village (Indian, not Native American) with limited internet access, put the graphics on my walls and followed them like a map. I worked. And did nothing else. It worked. So I pre-did my goal for 3 months. But village life made it easy to be productive - other than snakes and lizards there werent many distractions. So the experiment had to continue in a more mind-grabbing place. A metropolis. There I went for another 7 months. As life distractions piled on, I had to make additional graphics to stay focused. But after life again was minimized to its bare and beautiful necessities, the last 3 months I spent like this. And as I write this, I can say that I have pre-done my last New Year resolution, so next year looks promisingly productive.


We Really Have About 9 Years


 Is it worth it to go through this just to be productive? After I deducted everything that one has to do in life, like sleeping, commuting, working and eating - I put a number on the time we all have left of our lives to do what we want. 9 years. Even still in my twenties, I am in a hurry to make those 9 years count.

     <h1 class="title">
      Simple Life As an Entrepreneur
     </h1>
    </a>
    <div class="text">
     <p>
      If you were to visualize a productive life, it would look something like this.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="How To Have Simple Life as an Entrepreneur" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/79056e114e45eb8de9e1712c9f6879b1/tumblr_inline_my4uqw8OKQ1rivytv.jpg"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <!-- more -->
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://notes.fundersandfounders.com/post/67357540439/how-to-get-up-early">
       <img alt="How To Get Up Early" src="http://31.media.tumblr.com/3e83b3e030d9f0d8a965922fe0945fc7/tumblr_inline_my4uscpqZs1rivytv.png"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://notes.fundersandfounders.com/post/66805283931/howto-start-the-day-first-things-people-do">
       <img alt="how to start the day" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/8783626684e964c329777cbe30611be3/tumblr_inline_myte4iLLZk1rivytv.png"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://notes.fundersandfounders.com/post/68882521386/how-to-work-fast">
       <img alt="How To Work Fast" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/ea589e2fb0349c858c4ae1ba30364a34/tumblr_inline_my4usw47gY1rivytv.png"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://notes.fundersandfounders.com/post/68997537863/how-to-think-faster">
       <img alt="How To Think Fast" src="http://41.media.tumblr.com/700457e1665b5136a9357ea4adc00aab/tumblr_mxarn0lkGf1s6bw99o1_1280.png"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <h2>
      Being Uber-Productive
     </h2>
     <p>
      The new entrepreneurs are expected to be fast. Fast to learn, fast to do, fast to decide. After all, entrepreneurs succeed when they do things faster and better than a large corporation.
     </p>
     <p>
      That means being uber-productive. This year I
      <a href="http://anna.vc/post/64187007171/how-to-be-productive" title="How to Be Productive - The Mindmap of 35 Habits of the Uber-Productive">
       experimented with uber-productivity
      </a>
      . I made a new year resolutions a year ago. Not so naive about my self-discipline any more, I did not kid myself that I would just do it. I had to pre-do to make sure that I actually do it. Let me explain.
     </p>
     <h2>
      Pre-Doing Your Goals, or Beta-Testing Your Life
     </h2>
     <p>
      When I set a big goal, like change my life, or be more productive, I normally failed. Predictably so. Until I started using a tool, well familiar in the startup world. You can call it beta testing. Or just pre-doing. When a startup company launches, they are usually so far from achieving their goal that they have to launch before they are ready. Otherwise they would never launch. Their money would run out, the teams enthusiasm would drain and the investors would lose interest. Thats why a startup launches a beta, something that looks like their product and almost works, but not quite. Same idea goes for some
      <a href="http://www.kickstarter.com/" title="Kickstarter - the world's largest funding platform for creative projects">
       Kickstarter
      </a>
      projects - they pre-sell something that is not quite done yet. So pre-doing works for companies with small resources and a lot of enthusiasm. Why couldnt it work for people with little patience and a lot of optimism about life around New Year? That was me last year. So I wanted to pre-do my goal of being uber-productive.
     </p>
     <h2>
      Productivity Test in Village, Then in City
     </h2>
     <p>
      I started by doing what I do best - visualize what I will do. I visualized a productive live for me in a series of infographics (see above). That was the pre-doing part. Then I followed the graphics like a mind map.
      <a href="http://anna.vc/post/46606700818/where-on-earth-is-anna">
       I went to an Indian village
      </a>
      (Indian, not Native American) with limited internet access, put the graphics on my walls and followed them like a map. I worked. And did nothing else. It worked. So I pre-did my goal for 3 months. But village life made it easy to be productive - other than snakes and lizards there werent many distractions. So the experiment had to continue in a more mind-grabbing place. A metropolis. There I went for another 7 months. As life distractions piled on, I had to make additional graphics to stay focused. But after life again was minimized to its bare and beautiful necessities, the last 3 months I spent like this. And as I write this, I can say that I have pre-done my last New Year resolution, so next year looks promisingly productive.
     </p>
     <h2>
      We Really Have About 9 Years
     </h2>
     <p>
      Is it worth it to go through this just to be productive? After I deducted everything that one has to do in life, like sleeping, commuting, working and eating - I put a number on the
      <a href="http://notes.fundersandfounders.com/post/60376706416/how-much-time-we-have" title="How Much Time Left - Infographic">
       time we all have left
      </a>
      of our lives to do what we want. 9 years. Even still in my twenties, I am in a hurry to make those 9 years count.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/118c0ff535b4b602227e6f0c95dc9715/tumblr_inline_my78w65VKU1rivytv.jpg"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <em>
       How I have been working for the last 3 months
      </em>
     </p>
