         <h1 class="storytitle">
          Haunted Hotels
         </h1>
         <div class="meta">
          Posted by
          <a href="http://daringdestinations.com/author/editor/" rel="author" title="Posts by editor">
           editor
          </a>
          on March 30, 2013
         </div>
         <div class="storycontent">
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_244" style="width: 197px">
           <a href="http://daringdestinations.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/ghost.png">
            <img alt="haunted hotel travel destinations" class="size-full wp-image-244" height="215" src="http://daringdestinations.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/ghost.png" width="187"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Ghosts and haunted hotels
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           The
           <a href="http://daringdestinations.com/haunted-destinations/" title="Haunted Destinations">
            haunted destinations
           </a>
           page includes all sorts of paranormal places, but this page is specifically for hotels where you can stay the night and wait for a ghostly encounter. Of course there is no guarantee youll see anything spooky. At least you can tell your friends you braved a night in these haunted places.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-239">
           </span>
          </p>
          <h3>
           USA
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.stanleyhotel.com/">
            The Stanley Hotel
            <br/>
           </a>
           Famous for its role in the Stephen King book The Shining, the real-life hotel is up in the Colorado Rocky Mountains and is known to be genuinely haunted.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.hotelprovincial.com/">
            Hotel Provincial
           </a>
           <br/>
           In the heart of New Orleans, the Hotel Provincial has had its share of ghostly tales told by the guests.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crescent-hotel.com/">
            The Crescent Hotel
           </a>
           <br/>
           Head to Arkansas for this paranormal hotpsot, and spend some time in the dining room. Thats where the most sightings happen.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.myrtlesplantation.com/">
            Myrtles Plantation
           </a>
           <br/>
           Another southern USA location in Louisiana, the home of a 200-year old plantation mansion. They claim to be one of the most haunted places in America.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.queenmary.com/">
            The Queen Mary
           </a>
           <br/>
           This is a boat as well as a hotel, so its a fun place to stay in any case. They offer a scary ghost tour but its better to see the ship on your own for more authentic experiences.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.tarrytownhouseestate.com">
            Tarrytown House
           </a>
           <br/>
           Near where the story Sleepy Hollow was set, this hotel offers luxury accommodations and potential scares in New York state.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.national-hotel.com/">
            The 1859 Historical National Hotel
           </a>
           <br/>
           The historic gold rush in California has certainly left some spirits in its wake, and the National Hotel is home to a few of them.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Europe
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.castlestuart.com/">
            Castle Stuart
           </a>
           <br/>
           It shouldnt be a surprise that some ancient castles would have a spirit or two. Castle Stuart in Scotland is particularly well-known for being haunted.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Canada
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.fairmont.com/banff-springs/">
            Fairmont Banff Springs
           </a>
           <br/>
           This is Canadas version of the Stanley Hotel, also up in the Rockies. Several gruesome deaths could be the cause of the ghost sightings.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.fairmont.com/empress-victoria">
            Fairmont Empress
           </a>
           <br/>
           Out in British Columbia, this hotel is apparently home to several different spirits.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.hihostels.ca/ontario/1166/HI-Ottawa_Jail/index.hostel">
            HI Ottawa Jail
           </a>
           <br/>
           Not only is this hotel haunted, its a budget hostel in an old jail building. Maybe youll meet some inmates who never really left.
          </p>
