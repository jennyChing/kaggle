              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="The 2015 Ford Mustang GT kicks ass" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--EFBVzggs--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/ite2stp9keypsspa5u1g.jpg" data-chomp-id="ite2stp9keypsspa5u1g" data-format="jpg" height="357" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--EFBVzggs--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/ite2stp9keypsspa5u1g.jpg" width="636"/>

            <p class="first-text" data-textannotation-id="0a476df3b77eca967e72586737d1074c">
             I'll cut right to the chase.
             <a href="http://www.roadandtrack.com/car-reviews/first-drives/2015-ford-mustang-gt-first-impressions" target="_blank">
              The 2015 Ford Mustang GT is excellent.
             </a>
             We had this white car, a GT Premium with the Performance Pack, in our PCOTY posse last week in Ohio and Detroit. Everyone loves it. Well, except Robin.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="0de6c0aa72bff66522e214d810c9bd28">
             Here's what I said over at R&amp;T:
            </p>
            <blockquote data-textannotation-id="fefd658917a16fac5f4565e214334ea4">
             <p data-textannotation-id="451435ffeb7fc500be4f38975f918c0a">
              I have an '06 Mustang GT convertible. I love that car, even though it feels like it was smashed together with a nail gun compared with the post-'09 editions. And now it might be time to part ways with it, because the 2015 Mustang GT is pretty special.
             </p>
             <p data-textannotation-id="eebe78e0d36c8349e91e756825fe5c46">
              In Ohio's Hocking Hills, where we took our 2014 Performance Car of the Year candidates for real-world drive impressions, it was comfortable, fast, involving, predictable, and composed. The car has matureda lot. It's not just 5.0 muscle and tire smoke; there's real agility to exploit. Mind you, I drove the GT in a group that also included the Porsche 911 GT3, the Lexus RC F, and the Italian-superhero Ferrari 458 Speciale. It confidently, casually held its own in that heady company.
             </p>
             <p data-textannotation-id="418f00ac4a02dbdd4c4d7ad9ffc70ba9">
              I'd take it over the BMW M235i without a second thought. The Mustang, you see, is livelier and more engaging, gleefully invalidating many import-car arguments. That's a handy thing, since it's now being sold where the import cars actually come from. All I thought when I was behind the wheel was that if the regular GT is this good, the Shelby GT350 will have rivals smashing office furniture next year.
             </p>
             <p data-textannotation-id="d8a013da9b217870619cc1167d043292">
              Oh, and the line lock? It ruins tires exactly as advertised. Ford didn't just build a better pony car, they went and built a great car, period.
             </p>
             <p data-textannotation-id="71362f37d4cf69b4dd056ccce7a37cbd">
              Still needs a set of Magnaflows, though.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
