       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Top Ten Biggest Bad-Ass Moments for Women in Comics
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        Posted on
        <span class="date published time" title="2008-11-06T12:00:00-0400">
         11/06/08
        </span>
        at
        <span class="published time" title="2008-11-06T12:00:00-0400">
         12:00 PM
        </span>
        in
        <span class="categories">
         <a href="http://ifanboy.com/category/articles/" rel="category tag" title="View all Articles in Articles">
          Articles
         </a>
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          soniaharris
         </span>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <font size="2">
          <br/>
          <font size="1">
           <span style="font-style: italic;">
            (Spoiler alert: If you havent read these stories, this will ruin them for you. Go read them first if you want to be surprised.)
           </span>
          </font>
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_elektrabad_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
          <img align="right" alt="Elektra" border="0" height="123" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_elektrabad_sm.jpg" style="width: 200px; height: 123px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="200"/>
         </a>
         1. Elektra
         <font size="2">
          in
         </font>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/0785127771/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
          <font size="2">
           <span style="font-weight: bold;">
            Elektra: Assassin
           </span>
          </font>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <font size="2">
          You know I love this book, so Ill try not to go on. Breaking though the drug haze and escaping from the mental institution is incredible to watch, you can feel the wrench as she fights within herself to pull her mind back to coherence. This is the first clue that Elektra is more powerful than anyone anticipated, particularly her enemies. By the second chapter, shes killing SHIELD agents in droves, deflecting bullets, slicing and dicing, throwing them back with her silent shout (which hits like a mack truck). Ultimately she saves the world, maneuvering her man into the presidency and beating back the forces of the Beast. There is barely a moment in this book which isnt bad-ass, its impossible to pick only one.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_phoenix_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
           <img align="right" alt="Phoenix" border="0" height="140" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_phoenix_sm.jpg" style="width: 140px; height: 140px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="140"/>
          </a>
          2. Phoenix
         </span>
         <font size="2">
          in the
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/0785122133/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
           <span style="font-weight: bold;">
            Dark Phoenix Saga
           </span>
          </a>
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          </span>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="2">
          The journey from insipid and unsure Marvel Girl to a planet-eating level of violent power is a marvelous thing to behold. There was something intensely satisfying in watching Jean Grey gradually discover the various aspects of her power, which is seemingly without limits, until she finally embraces it in all of its aspects. While her character has gone through many changes in direction, and historical rethinks, in this book at least, her power is all her own and she revels in it most when she devours an entire planet and everything on it. For me, this is a perfect example of personal continuity; Im always going to see Phoenix as part of Jean Grey because thats how it was written then, and thats how I like it. Something external didnt possess her, the phoenix effect is part of her, and an expression of her own power.
          <br/>
          <br style="font-weight: bold;"/>
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           <img align="right" alt="Dara" border="0" height="140" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_dara_sm.jpg" style="width: 190px; height: 140px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="190"/>
           3. Dara
          </span>
         </font>
         <font size="2">
          in
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1582409765/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
            The Sword
           </a>
           : Volume 2
          </span>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="2">
          This is the bad-ass moment which inspired this list. For their part in shaping my life, the top two positions have to go to Elektra and Phoenix, but this month in
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           The Sword
          </span>
          , Dara really blew my mind. Throughout their work, The Luna Brothers have impressed me with their depiction of women and examination of the gender experiences. Now in
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           The Sword
          </span>
          , theyve created a woman who is already a hero in overcoming the limitations of a crippling accident. But with her discovery of this sword (the depository of ancient powers of healing and strength), and undertaking the mission to avenge her families murder, she becomes a true warrior. In issue 12 this month her legs are chopped off by her adversary. Instinctively she grabs the sword, seeking its healing powers. But the sword begins to heal up the stumps of her legs, the skin quickly growing over the blood. She flings the sword away, rips the skin off, pulls her dismembered legs to their place and only then picks up the sword again. Its pretty impressive.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_ww_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
           <img align="right" alt="Wonder Woman" border="0" height="165" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_ww_sm2.jpg" style="width: 140px; height: 165px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="140"/>
          </a>
          4. Wonder Woman
         </span>
         <font size="2">
          in
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          </span>
         </font>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1401207685/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
          <font size="2">
           <span style="font-weight: bold;">
            Kingdom Come
           </span>
          </font>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <font size="2">
          Until
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           Kingdom Come
          </span>
          , I thought that Wonder Woman was simply an out-dated concept. She was obviously intended to give the author an excuse to depict a scantily clad woman tying people up. For me, she lacked intensity, and although I always loved her back story as an Amazon princess warrior, I just wasnt really feeling it. But in
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           Kingdom Come
          </span>
          she really stepped up. At one point she tells a placatory Superman
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           If they want to act like
          </span>
          <span style="font-weight: bold; font-style: italic;">
           warriors
          </span>
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           
          </span>
          <span style="font-weight: bold; font-style: italic;">
           Ill
          </span>
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           show them
          </span>
          <span style="font-weight: bold; font-style: italic;">
           war
          </span>
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           .
          </span>
          , this is the book where I first believed in a really pissed off Wonder Woman. Superman might be the central hero in this book, but its Wonder Woman who brings the battle to the story. When she dons her battle armor and flies above the clouds with Batman, ultimately to disable the nuclear weapons, she is fearsome and decisive, and every bit Amazon warrior royalty.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_carrie_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
           <img align="right" alt="Carrie" border="0" height="140" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_carrie_sm.jpg" style="width: 140px; height: 140px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="140"/>
          </a>
          5. Robin
         </span>
         <font size="2">
          in
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          </span>
         </font>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1563893428/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
          <font size="2">
           <span style="font-weight: bold;">
            The Dark Knight Returns
           </span>
          </font>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <font size="2">
          This little girls got balls. Carrie Kelley jumps right in and becomes Robin all on her own, with not a single bit of encouragement from anyone. She sees the need, and decides to do something about it. Basically, she saves the big guys life and does it without help or encouragement from anyone. Carrie is The Dark Knights polar opposite in every way, and she fills in the gaps for him, with her youth, energy and enthusiasm. In the end she works with Alfred as the secret engineer to Batmans triumph, to the rebirth of a city that they witness, and she is at his right hand all the way. The fact that shes a girl, not a woman, only reinforces the fact that there is not a moment when shes fighting that she isnt flying in the face of her natural instincts to cower and hide. The level of bravery here cant be underestimated.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
         </span>
         <font size="2">
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_jakita_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
            <img align="right" alt="Jakita" border="0" height="140" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_jakita_sm2.jpg" style="width: 123px; height: 140px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="140"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </font>
         <font size="2">
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           6. Jakita Warner
          </span>
         </font>
         <font size="2">
          in
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1563897644/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
           <span style="font-weight: bold;">
            Planetary
           </span>
          </a>
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           : Volume 2
          </span>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="2">
          In the first issue of
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           Planetary
          </span>
          , Jakita jumped out of a helicopter, landed with an earth shattering bang, and her role as the mover and shaker on the team was firmly established. This is truly a superwoman, shes tough, brave and incredibly strong. In Volume 2 of
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           Planetary
          </span>
          Ellis creates a scenario straight out of a 1960s monster movie; Giant mutated ants are attacking, and only Jakita can fight these massive behemoths. This isnt a job shes forced into, but one she actively seeks out. Wrestling these beasts down and punching them through buildings is her idea of a good time and she relishes the experience. Her teammates know this is her deal, and they leave her to deal with problem quickly and effectively.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_tulip_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
           <img align="right" alt="Tulip" border="0" height="160" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_tulip_sm.jpg" style="width: 140px; height: 160px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="140"/>
          </a>
          7. Tulip
         </span>
         <font size="2">
          in
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1563896176/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
           <span style="font-weight: bold;">
            Preacher
           </span>
          </a>
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           : Volume 8
          </span>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="2">
          Throughout the series, Tulip is every bit her mans equal partner, fighting against his need to shelter her from danger. She is strong, skilled, and smart, saving Jessies life again and again. Pitting herself against the most horrible enemies, she isnt fearless, but her fury carries her pretty far. The moment in the series that really gave me pause, the moment when I felt like Tulip showed her grit, was when she walked away from Cassidy. Despite what could only be the worst hangover imaginable she leaves (6 months of brain-destroying quantities of valium and alcohol, plus the knowledge that the love of your life is dead, has got to make a lady feel pretty wobbly). When she hears Cassidys blustering threats, and simply tells him,
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           Cassidy. Really. This is me.
          </span>
          you know thats it, shes done with this and he needs to just move out her way.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_grace_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
           <img align="right" alt="Promethea" border="0" height="171" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_grace_sm.jpg" style="width: 140px; height: 171px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="140"/>
          </a>
          8. Promethea
         </span>
         in
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/140120094X/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           Promethea
          </span>
         </a>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          : Volume 3
         </span>
         <br/>
         While on her tour through the metaphysical levels of the Kaballah/tarot/planets/the afterlife, Sophie leaves Stacia and Grace in charge of the embodiment of Promethea on earth. Stacia/Grace is a much wilder, better dressed, more bloodthirsty, baser version of Promethea than Sophies (as she says herself 
         <span style="font-style: italic;">
          as for that
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           other
          </span>
          Promethea, the
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           nice
          </span>
          one Im afraid shes elsewhere
         </span>
         ). In her final battle with the Goetia (demons inhabiting the mayor) she exhibits her most fearsome side, the side that revels in her role of wild warrior princess. Unexpectedly, its not her sword-wielding persona (though she does do that part really well), but her willingness to embrace her demons which is truly chilling. She literally and metaphorically consume the Geotia, making the demons her own, a part of her, and shes victorious at last.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_tara_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
           <img align="right" alt="Tara" border="0" height="140" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_tara_sm.jpg" style="width: 140px; height: 140px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="140"/>
          </a>
          9. Tara Chace
         </span>
         in
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1932664890/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           Queen &amp; Country
          </span>
         </a>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          : Volume 2
         </span>
         <br/>
         Taras a tough, gritty government agent, so her being a bit of a bad-ass is a given. Her intense commitment to this job is almost a prerequisite to her success and survival, so its not surprising to see her deal with any given situation. The shock is always in her joy, her love of what could almost seem like an untenable career. Clearly she gains tremendous satisfaction from her ability to deal with the untenable, and its when she is fighting for her life that she seems most present in her life. When shes captured by inept agents in Operation: Stormfront, despite suffering the after-effects of a car-wreck which just killed her colleague, she smiles up at her would-be rapist/kidnappers and says
         <span style="font-style: italic;">
          Your muzzle control sucks mate.
         </span>
         before shooting 3 of her 4 captors with their own gun.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <a href="#" onclick="window.open('/spaw/img_popup.php?img_url=http://www.ifanboy.com/images/ifanboy/sh_kitty_bg.jpg','Image','width=500,height=300,scrollbars=no,toolbar=no,location=no,status=no,resizable=yes,screenX=120,screenY=100');return false;">
           <img align="right" alt="Kitty" border="0" height="140" hspace="0" src="http://ifanboy.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/sh_kitty_sm.jpg" style="width: 140px; height: 140px; padding-left: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px;" vspace="0" width="140"/>
          </a>
          10. Kitty Pryde
         </span>
         in
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/0785122540/?tag=ifanboycom-20">
           Astonishing X-Men
          </a>
          : Volume 4
         </span>
         <br/>
         I never really liked Kitty, from the minute she appeared, she seemed ineffectual and gimmicky. It seemed kind of like when all the cute little babies grew up on
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          The Cosby Show
         </span>
         , and suddenly they got a new one who was all tiny and adorable. Maybe its because I was about 12 when she was, or because I was also a dorky, awkward, Jewish kid who didnt feel quite at home anywhere, but I wanted her to step up in a way that never seemed to happen. When
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          Astonishing X-Men
         </span>
         started, I was following the artist and writer, because Id sworn off X-Men comics long ago. But Whedon did something incredible. He made Pryde an adult, but still herself. She had massive resources, depths of strength and that could carry her team. In the final story, its Pryde that saves the earth and her determination and sacrifice was fantastic to witness.
        </p>
        <p>
         <br style="font-style: italic;"/>
         <br/>
        </p>
