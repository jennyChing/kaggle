         <p class="m12">
          While there are some excellent books on writing, the web is also filled with terrific sites on the topic. Here are 151 of the very best writing websites, and theyre organised for you by subject and genre.
         </p>
         <div id="textpreview">
          <p>
           Structure and Plot
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.screenwritingtricks.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Screenwriting Tips for Authors
            </a>
            is the blog of screenwriter and novelist Alexandra Sokoloff, and she comprehensively breaks down the three-act structure for writers.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://marthaalderson.com/plot-whisperer-blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;" target="_blank" title="The Plot Whisperer">
             The Plot Whisperer
            </a>
            blog is all about plotting.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.thewritersjourney.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Storytech Literary Consulting
            </a>
            is a site by the author of
            <i>
             The Writers Journey
            </i>
            . This is a book for writers based on Joseph Campbells
            <i>
             The Hero with a Thousand Faces
            </i>
            , and the site offers a number of resources related to structure.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.plot-generator.org.uk/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Plot Generator
            </a>
            can be a fun way to jumpstart your plot.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.literatureandlatte.com/scapple.php" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Scapple
            </a>
            is a mindmapping software from the same company that developed Scrivener.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://storymind.com/blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Storymind
            </a>
            is a site that sells software related to story creation, but their blog is free and includes excellent articles about narrative, structure and more.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.lifewrite.com/html/class.htm" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Free Writing Class from Steven Barnes
            </a>
            is based on the screenplay course Barnes has taught for years, but as he points out in his introduction, writing is writing, and this is an excellent way for a writer in any medium to learn structure.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Characterisation
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://fuckyeahcharacterdevelopment.tumblr.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             FYCD
            </a>
            is a blog that focuses on all aspects of character development and answers questions as well.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.babycenter.com/baby-names" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Baby Name Center
            </a>
            will help you get the crucial right name for your character. You can search for names by origin, meaning, number of syllables and more.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://charactertherapist.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Character Therapist
            </a>
            takes a psychological approach to readers questions about character development.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.rangen.co.uk/chars/motivegen.php" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Motive Generator
            </a>
            offers prompts to give your characters purpose.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Researching Agents and Writing Queries and Synopses
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.queryshark.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Query Shark
            </a>
            offers contributors the opportunity to have their novel queries critiqued and read critiques of others queries.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://evileditor.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Evil Editor
            </a>
            breaks down synopses and explains pitfalls writers should avoid.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.writersdigest.com/editor-blogs/guide-to-literary-agents" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Chuck Sambuchinos Guide to Literary Agents
            </a>
            is a blog that mostly covers agents and what they are looking for but sometimes touches on other aspects of writing and the publishing business as well.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://agentquery.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Agent Query
            </a>
            is a free searchable database of literary agents that not only allows you to search by genre but includes information such as agent interests and whether they are currently accepting new clients. The site also includes a social networking aspect.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://jetreidliterary.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Janet Reid, Literary Agent
            </a>
            writes another excellent blog. Crime fiction is her specialty, but many of her posts concern all types of fiction.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Publishing and Business
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.publishersweekly.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Publishers Weekly
            </a>
            is the bible of the publishing world with a lot of free material on its site and free e-newsletters.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.sfwa.org/other-resources/for-authors/writer-beware/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Writer Beware
            </a>
            compiles information about scam agents and publishers across genres for writers in any country.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.publishersmarketplace.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Publishers Marketplace
            </a>
            keeps track of publishing deals.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.mediabistro.com/galleycat/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Galley Cat
            </a>
            is a blog about the publishing industry.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.alanrinzler.com/blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Book Deal
            </a>
            is written by a consulting editor who looks at the industry and the many changes happening there.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://janefriedman.com/blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Jane Friedman
            </a>
            focuses on digital changes in publishing and how authors can navigate them.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.thecreativepenn.com/blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Creative Penn
            </a>
            is a blog that approaches writing as an entrepreneurial venture.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://publishingperspectives.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Publishing Perspectives
            </a>
            takes on the global publishing world.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://bestsellerlabs.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Bestseller Labs
            </a>
            offers tips from bestselling writer Jonathan Gunson on how to get published and how to get more readers.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://writersandauthors.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Writers and Authors
            </a>
            offers tips on building an audience and provides a space for writers to network.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://pred-ed.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Preditors &amp; Editors
            </a>
            is an old and trusted site that contains resources about publishers, agents, scams, workshops and more.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.burryman.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Burry Man Writers Center
            </a>
            has a focus on Scotland but is a global site with many resources on the business of writing as well as other aspects.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://kriswrites.com/freelancers-survival-guide-table-of-contents/#sthash.xEiVt4tM.dpbs" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Freelancers Survival Guide
            </a>
            is an entire free book online about how to survive as a freelancer.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Editor and Agent Blogs
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://blog.nathanbransford.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Nathan Bransford
            </a>
            is an author and former literary agents who has one of the best sites on the web about writing and publishing.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.rachellegardner.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Rachelle Gardner
            </a>
            is a literary agent who maintains a terrific site with advice on writing and publishing.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://agentinthemiddle.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Agent in the Middle
            </a>
            is a blog by agent Lori Perkins.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://jennybent.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Bent on Books
            </a>
            includes posts from all agents at the Jenny Bent Agency.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://nelsonagency.com/pub-rants/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Pub Rants
            </a>
            is agent Kristen Nelsons blog with excellent advise on the business side of publishing.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Self-Publishing
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://jakonrath.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             A Newbies Guide to Publishing
            </a>
            is the blog of writer J.A. Konrath; he is considered one of the leading voices in self-publishing.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.thebookdesigner.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Book Designer
            </a>
            has had a long career in book design, printing and typesetting, and he turns that into excellent advice for the self-publisher.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.theindependentpublishingmagazine.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Independent Publisher Magazine
            </a>
            takes a global perspective on self-publishing.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.publetariat.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Publetariat
            </a>
            is a news hub and online community for self-publishers.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Creativity, Inspiration and Writing Prompts
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://thewritepractice.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Write Practice
            </a>
            focuses on daily practices to build skill and creativity.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://writingprompts.tumblr.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Writing Prompts
            </a>
            are the prompts that a teacher uses in class, and here, he shares them with readers.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://procrastinatingwritersblog.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Procrastinating Writers
            </a>
            is for writers who struggle with blocks and motivation.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://thecourage2create.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Courage to Create
            </a>
            details one mans struggle to finish his first novel.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://danishapiro.com/category/blog/moments-of-being/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Dani Shapiro
            </a>
            is an author who muses on her blog about the challenges and epiphanies of the writing life.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://writersinspire.wordpress.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Great Writers Inspire
            </a>
            is primarily designed as a resource for students and teachers, but may also be of interest to novelists wishing to ground themselves in some of the great works of literature.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://theodoragoss.com/blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Theodora Goss
            </a>
            is a fantasy writer who blogs about her inspirations and how to balance writing with the other demands of life.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://itstartswith.com/blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             It Starts With
            </a>
            uses psychology to inspire and motivate writers.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://positivewriter.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Positive Writer
            </a>
            focuses on self-doubt and confidence-building for writers.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.inspiredbylifeandfiction.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Inspired by Life and Fiction
            </a>
            brings together ten novelists who write about the things that stir their imaginations.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://thewritersalleys.blogspot.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Writers Alley
            </a>
            features writers from around the world offering inspiration with a Christian slant.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.theprocrastiwriter.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Procrastiwriter
            </a>
            gives advice on how to avoid procrastination and fit writing into a busy life.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.advicetowriters.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Advice to Writers
            </a>
            inspires via a quote of the day from famous writers.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.pantheon.org/mythica.html" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Encyclopedia Mythica
            </a>
            is an online encyclopedia of myth, folklore and religion that can be a wonderful resource for story ideas in all genres.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://writers-den.pantomimepony.co.uk/writers-plot-ideas.php" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Writers Plot Idea Generator
            </a>
            also has exercise prompts, character profile generators, plot twist generators, first lines and more.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://750words.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             750 Words
            </a>
            is a site inspired by the book
            <i>
             The Artists Way
            </i>
            that gives writers the opportunity to do the
            <a href="http://www.nownovel.com/blog/morning-pages-how-to-make-them-work/">
             morning pagesexercise
            </a>
            privately and online with a progress tracker.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Workshops and Forums
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.critters.org/index.php" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Critters Workshop
            </a>
            began as a free workshop for science fiction, fantasy and horror writers, but now it covers all genres.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.absolutewrite.com/forums/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Absolute Write forums
            </a>
            cover novels as well as other forms of writing, and are an invaluable source of information from other professionals and aspiring writers about the publishing business, the craft of writing and more.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="https://www.awpwriter.org/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Association of Writers &amp; Writing Programs
            </a>
            has a database of hundred of programmes and conferences for writers plus news, contests, articles and more.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           General Writing Advice
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.nownovel.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Now Novel
            </a>
            (thats us!) is not only an online novel-writing process, it also provides handy writing advice and great resource roundups like this one.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.writersdigest.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Writers Digest
            </a>
            is one of the best and oldest resources for writers.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://absolutewrite.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Absolute Write Blog
            </a>
            is related to the forum of the same name and offers a similar scope of information in the form of longer-form posts.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://nanowrimo.org/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             NaNoWriMo
            </a>
            challenges writers to complete a 50,000 word first draft in a month during November.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.booklifenow.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Booklife Now
            </a>
            is a general guide to both the craft of writing and the publishing industry across genres.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://terribleminds.com/ramble/blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Terrible Minds
            </a>
            is the popular blog of writer Chuck Wendig who shares his observations on publishing and writing technique.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.advancedfictionwriting.com/blog/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Advanced Fiction Writing Blog
            </a>
            is where Randy Ingermanson, inventor of the snowflake method of writing, blogs about technique and the business of writing.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://litreactor.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Litreactor
            </a>
            offers online classes, essays on craft and more.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.novelrocket.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Novel Rocket
            </a>
            is a site that teaches visitors how to write a novel through guest posts and author interviews.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://writersinthestormblog.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;" target="_blank" title="Writers in the Storm blog">
             Writers in the Storm
            </a>
            features professional writers across a variety of genres blogging about their craft and specialties.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.firstnovelsclub.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             First Novels Club
            </a>
            is a blog with a particular focus on childrens and YA writing, but it looks at other types of fiction as well.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.shewrites.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             She Writes
            </a>
            offers women writers a social network, community blog and more.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://www.writingforward.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Writing Forward
            </a>
            covers grammar, creativity and more.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://lisagardner.com/writers-toolbox" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             The Writers Toolbox
            </a>
            is written by a best-selling suspense novelist who offers articles on the writing process.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://blog.janicehardy.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Fiction University
            </a>
            features experienced writers as guest bloggers.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="http://warriorwriters.wordpress.com/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">
             Kristen Lambs Blog
            </a>
            covers everything from dialogue to social media for writers and more.
           </li>

            <a href="http://bksp.org/" style="font-weight: bold; color: #e95e0f;">

            </a>
            has many resources including a subscription-only forum for writers.
