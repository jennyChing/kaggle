      <h2>
       <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/2012/08/chocolate-chip-blueberry-breakfast-quinoa/" rel="bookmark">
        Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa.
       </a>
      </h2>
      <div class="postmeta">
       August 16, 2012
       <div class="ccount">
        <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/2012/08/chocolate-chip-blueberry-breakfast-quinoa/#comments">
         116 comments 
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
     <p>
      Thisssss is what we call a copout.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-3.jpg">
       <img alt="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26500" height="444" src="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-3.jpg" title="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" width="668"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Its incredibly similar to my favorite
      <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/2012/04/breakfast-quinoa/" target="_blank">
       normal breakfast quinoa
      </a>
      that I only recently shared, but this has chocolate. Which in itself deserves its own post, of course.
     </p>
     <p>
      Because look at those cute little chocolate chips getting all melty in that bowl!
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-1-2.jpg">
       <img alt="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26495" height="818" src="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-1-2.jpg" title="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" width="668"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Oh oh oh AND I did toast the quinoa first in coconut oil, which is a new thing Im doing and totally loving. And this one time, I also swirled in some roasted blueberries that I had leftover from, um margaritas. Im just glad I repurposed them.
     </p>
     <p>
      But I love it both ways: fresh blueberries for a burst and roasted for more flavor. I just cant decide.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-1.jpg">
       <img alt="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26496" height="444" src="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-1.jpg" title="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" width="668"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      This whole breakfast is sort of an ode to my favorite breakfast as a kid, which was Quaker instant oatmeal. You know, the kind in that package? But I had to have two packages instead of one, and it had to be cooked in milk instead of water. And it had to be the PERFECT consistency, or else I would start all over. My favorite flavor was peaches and cream, but honestly? My actual favorite flavor was strawberries and cream, you could just never find it back then. It would randomly pop up in a variety pack and the rest of the choices would be horrific. But peaches and cream was always available, so I went with that.
     </p>
     <p>
      I even ate those suckers dry. Yes, I totally ate dry packets of instant oatmeal. Which was like, instant oats and sugar and dehydrated fruit which Im pretty sure were just apples with fake peach flavoring on them. So so so good though.
     </p>
     <p>
      And sometimes? I still buy it. I buy it as a treat and eat it on the weekends, or on days when Im feeling particularly nostalgic for childhood or home. And when I say home, I mean home thats still only ten minutes away. But whatevs.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-2-2.jpg">
       <img alt="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26497" height="817" src="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-2-2.jpg" title="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" width="668"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      As I was walking through the cereal aisle the other day, I noticed the completely crazy range of flavors that oatmeal comes in now. If I had those choices? Id go nutso. Like lose my fahreaking mind as a tween. Right there on the shelf sat chocolate chip instant oat packets and I was sort of torn. I mean peaches and cream and strawberries and cream are one thing, but chocolate chips? I can just add my own. And saturate them in coconut milk.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-2.jpg">
       <img alt="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26498" height="444" src="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-2.jpg" title="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" width="668"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      So I did. Dont mess with me man.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-3-2.jpg">
       <img alt="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26499" height="832" src="http://www.howsweeteats.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/CCQuinoa-3-2.jpg" title="Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa I howsweeteats.com" width="668"/>
      </a>
     </p>

      <span class="item">
       <h2 class="fn">
        Chocolate Chip Blueberry Breakfast Quinoa
       </h2>
      </span>
      <div class="time">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Yield:
        </strong>
        <span class="yield">
         serves 2
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Prep Time:
        </strong>
        <span class="preptime">
         5 minutes
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Cook Time:
        </strong>
        <span class="cooktime">
         20 minutes
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Total Time:
        </strong>
        <span class="duration">
         25 minutes
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="left">
       <h3 style="clear:left">
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <div class="ingredient">
        <p>
         1/2 cup dry quinoa, rinsed and patted dry
        </p>
        <p>
         1/2 tablespoons coconut oil
        </p>
        <p>
         3/4 cup canned lite coconut milk + more for drizzling
        </p>
        <p>
         2 teaspoons vanilla extract
        </p>
        <p>
         1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
        </p>
        <p>
         a pinch of salt
        </p>
        <p>
         1/4 cup mini chocolate chips
        </p>
        <p>
         1/2 cup fresh blueberries
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="right">
       <h3 style="clear:left">
        Directions:
       </h3>
       <div class="instructions">
        <p>
         Heat a small saucepan over medium heat and coconut oil. Once melted, add in quinoa and shake the pan to coat, letting toast for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
        </p>
        <p>
         Combine coconut milk, cinnamon and vanilla in the saucepan with quinoa and bring to a boil. Reduce to a simmer, cover, and let cook for 15 minutes until quinoa can be fluffed with a fork.
        </p>
        <p>
         Divide quinoa into two bowls then sprinkle with chocolate chips. Let sit for 2-3 minutes so the chips get melty. Add in a few drizzles of coconut milk, then blueberries.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="source">
       <p>
        [adapted from
        <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/2012/04/breakfast-quinoa/" target="_blank">
         my original breakfast quinoa
        </a>
        ]
       </p>
      </div>

     </p>
     <p>
      Also good? Roasted strawberries stirred in. Dooooo it.
     </p>
