         <h3 class="entry-header">
          Quick and easy black beans and rice recipe {vegan}
         </h3>
         <div class="top-buttons">
          <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="11278682">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <div class="entry-body">
           <p>
            <span style="color: #996633;">
             A week of recipes designed for easy cooking, in or out of a burrito. Viva Mexico Week, Day One.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://ninecooks.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451fa5069e2014e89be8006970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
             <img alt="Black-beans-and-rice" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83451fa5069e2014e89be8006970d" src="http://ninecooks.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451fa5069e2014e89be8006970d-800wi" title="Quick and easy black beans and rice comes together in just 10 minutes."/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Except for the little sprigs of cilantro (from my herb garden), every ingredient in this quick and easy rendition of classic black beans and rice came right from the pantry. The
            <em>
             quick
            </em>
            comes from pre-cooked rice, which could be leftover rice from Chinese takeout or from a big batch you've made in the rice cooker. Canned black beans and store-bought
            <a href="http://www.goya.com/english/product_subcategory/Condiments/Marinades-Cooking-Ingredients" target="_self">
             sofrito
            </a>
            speed things up, too. Sometimes black beans and rice are served side by side (as in
            <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2007/08/dried-black-bea.html" target="_self">
             moros y cristianos, "moors and christians"
            </a>
            ), but I figure it's all going to get tossed together in your tummy, so why not cook everything together from the outset? You can double or triple this recipe, or make a huge batch for a crowd, to serve with grilled chicken or fish, or rolled up in a burrito.
           </p>
          </div>
          <!-- .entry-body -->
          <a id="more">
          </a>
          <div class="entry-more">
           <p>
            <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://ninecooks.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451fa5069e201a3fd325850970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
             <img alt="Skillet black beans and rice." border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83451fa5069e201a3fd325850970b img-responsive" src="http://ninecooks.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451fa5069e201a3fd325850970b-800wi" title="Skillet black beans and rice."/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <div class="hrecipe">
            <h3>
             <span class="fn">
              Quick and easy black beans and rice
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             From the pantry, you'll need:
             <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2007/06/canola_oil.html" target="_self">
              canola oil
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2010/08/onions-recipe-clam-bacon-and-caramelized-onion-pita-pizza.html" target="_self">
              onion
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2009/05/garlic-recipe-tortilla-espanola.html" target="_self">
              garlic
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2009/11/cumin-recipe-mushroom-bhaji.html" target="_self">
              cumin
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2006/08/chili_powder.html" target="_self">
              chili powder
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2007/11/green-chiles.html" target="_self">
              canned green chile peppers
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2009/08/long-grain-white-rice-recipe-jambalaya.html" target="_self">
              long-grain white rice
             </a>
             ,
             <a href="http://www.theperfectpantry.com/2008/07/canned-black-be.html" target="_self">
              canned black beans
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Serves 4-6.
            </p>
            <h4>
             Ingredients
            </h4>
            <p>
             <span class="ingredient">
              2 tsp canola oil
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              1 small onion, peeled and chopped
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              1 clove garlic, peeled and chopped
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              1 tsp ground cumin
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              1 tsp ground chili powder (I like
              <a href="http://www.penzeys.com/cgi-bin/penzeys/p-penzeyschili3000.html" target="_self">
               Penzeys Chili 3000
              </a>
              )
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              2 oz canned roasted green chile peppers (half a small can)
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              2 cups
              <em>
               cooked
              </em>
              white rice
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              1/4 cup
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Goya-Sofrito-12-Ounce-Jars-Pack/dp/B0004MXOFM/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1309176403&amp;sr=8-1" target="_self">
               red sofrito
              </a>
              (available in supermarkets, in the Latino foods section)
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              1 15-oz can black beans, rinsed and drained
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              1/4 tsp kosher salt
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span class="ingredient">
              1/4 tsp black pepper
             </span>
            </p>
            <h4>
             Directions
            </h4>
            <p>
             In a large nonstick frying pan, heat the oil over medium-low heat. Add the onion, and cook 2-3 minutes, until soft and translucent. Stir in the garlic, cumin, chili powder and green chile peppers, and cook for 2 minutes.
            </p>
            <p>
             If the rice is cold, break it up with your hands as you add it to the pot. Stir well to combine the rice and onion mixture; cook, stirring, for 2-3 minutes until the rice is warmed through. Add in the sofrito and black beans, and stir gently, until the sofrito is incorporated and the beans evenly distributed throughout the rice.
            </p>
            <p>
             Season with the salt and pepper. Taste, and add more if needed. Serve warm, or cool completely and refrigerate. You can make this up to 2 days in advance; add a few drops of water and reheat in the microwave.
            </p>
