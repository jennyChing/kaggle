      <p>
       Although there are plenty of airports offering straightforward complimentary wireless, theres a growing minority that tease your bits with an introductory period of minutes, only to cut the connection for an alternate paid option. (Im looking at you
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/countries/germany-travel-information/">
        Germany
       </a>
       .) Many times this paid wifi requires you to have a local mobile number or is simply too expensive to justify watching another
       <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pKE7e1A4QWI" target="_blank">
        video of a pug dancing in a tutu
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Whatever the case may be, turning most time-based Internet restrictions in airports,
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/category/travel/lodging/">
        hotels
       </a>
       , or elsewhere into unlimited access generally requires nothing more than a few clicks with the right software.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
        <strong>
         How Time Restricted Wireless Connections Work
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Condensing down the
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/category/travel/tech/">
        technical
       </a>
       details into a small byte, when you log on to a given network, it makes note of your devices MAC address, a set of letters and numbers that uniquely identify your network card. The MAC address from your phone, tablet, or laptop can then be used for authentication and how the airport network tracks your time online.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="romanian clock" class="alignnone" height="517" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/Europe/Romania/Sibiu/i-HnZW8z9/0/690x690/P1310542_3_4c-L.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        Think of the MAC address as your face and the restricted wireless connection like a limited all-you-can-eat buffet. You can consume all the calories you want for 30 minutes a day. But if you try to leave lunch and come back for dinner without paying, the hipster hostess recalling your face reminds you theres no such thing as a free lunch (and dinner).
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       In the digital world however appearances are easily morphed, without going to the extremes of
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00340I4KG/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00340I4KG&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=foxnomad-20&amp;linkId=ZVKL4DJQWXWHZWF2" target="_blank">
        John Travola, Nicholas Cage
       </a>
       , or Joan Rivers.
      </p>
      <div style="float: right;">
       <object height="183" width="325">
        <param name="movie" value="//www.youtube.com/v/UH0udzZVWU4?version=3&amp;hl=en_US">
        </param>
        <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
        </param>
        <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
        </param>
        <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="183" src="//www.youtube.com/v/UH0udzZVWU4?version=3&amp;hl=en_US" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="325">
        </embed>
       </object>
      </div>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
        <strong>
         Will Spoof For Internetz
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       MAC addresses are hard-coded into network interfaces but the free programs
       <a href="http://www.softpedia.com/get/Network-Tools/Misc-Networking-Tools/Mac-Makeup.shtml" target="_blank">
        Mac Makeup
       </a>
       (Windows) and
       <a href="https://github.com/halo/LinkLiar" target="_blank">
        LinkLiar
       </a>
       (Mac OS X) use software magic to make them variable. This is akin to putting on a convincing rubber face mask for the buffet example above.
      </p>
      <p>
       While you cant change your face, its possible to alter your appearance just well enough to fool the host or hostess.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
        <strong>
         Step-By-Step MAC Spoofing Using LinkLiar
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       To demonstrate how straightforward spoofing your MAC address is, heres exactly how to do it using LinkLiar for OS X. (Windows users, the process is basically the same using Mac Makeup.)
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <strong>
        1. Download LinkLiar
       </strong>
        Click over to either
       <a href="http://download.cnet.com/LinkLiar/3000-2085_4-75852722.html" target="_blank">
        CNETs LinkLiar page
       </a>
       or
       <a href="https://github.com/halo/LinkLiar" target="_blank">
        GitHubs download page
       </a>
       to grab the half megabyte application file.
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <strong>
        2. Install LinkLiar
       </strong>
        As you would any standard Mac app but keep in mind once done, it will be hanging out in your System Preferences, not Applications folder.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="linkliar mac os x" class="alignnone" height="637" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/photos/i-znqqFq3/0/O/i-znqqFq3.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <strong>
        3. Open System Preferences
       </strong>
        Click the purple LinkLiar icon. Youll be prompted that System Preferences must be closed, then reopened. Click OK. Once System Preferences comes back up, click the LinkLiar icon again.
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <strong>
        4. Spoof Your Wireless Connection
       </strong>
        When LinkLiar fires up, it shows your network interfaces with their current MAC addresses shaded in gray. Click your wifi adapter (this would work for Ethernet connections as well), then toggle the fake MAC address by clicking the little wheel. Hit
       <em>
        Apply
       </em>
       at which point youll be prompted to type in your administrator password.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="linkliar osx" class="alignnone" height="270" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/photos/i-FjDz8pG/1/O/i-FjDz8pG.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <strong>
        5. Stay In System Preferences For A Moment
       </strong>
        Otherwise you may have to restart this process. In the main System Preferences pane, click
       <em>
        Network
       </em>
       , which will open up the Networking Preferences window. In this new window, in the left sidebar click in this order:
       <em>
        Wi-Fi &gt; Assist Me &gt; Diagnostics &gt; Continue &gt; Continue &gt; Allow &gt; Quit
       </em>
      </p>
      <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
       <strong>
        6. Clear Your Browser Cache
       </strong>
        Heres
       <a href="https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/how-clear-firefox-cache" target="_blank">
        how in Firefox
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95582?hl=en" target="_blank">
        Chrome
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="https://kb.wisc.edu/page.php?id=15141" target="_blank">
        Internet Explorer
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="https://discussions.apple.com/thread/4448839?tstart=0" target="_blank">
        Safari
       </a>
       . (Alternatively open up a new private browsing window.)
      </p>
      <p>
       This entire process will take you less than a minute after you get the hang of it and once completed, will give your laptop a new lease on wifi life. You can repeat the steps above as needed pretty much without limit  just be sure to make your next connection  but if you dont heres
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2011/11/10/black-hat-travel-hacking-how-to-miss-flights-for-free/">
