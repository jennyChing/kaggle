                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.avisiontoremember.com/2015/07/simple-organization-for-sewing-room.html">
                      Simple Organization for a Sewing Room Cutting Table
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3903346161826151543" itemprop="articleBody">
                     I don't know if you remember awhile back,
                     <a href="http://www.avisiontoremember.com/2014/04/craft-room-reveal.html" target="_blank">
                      I posted my craft room
                     </a>
                     .  It worked really well at that point, but lately, not so much.  My quilting rulers, rotary blades, pins, pencils, scissors, making pens, and all those little sewing gadgets that I have at my cutting table have been cluttered.  I sit down a pair of scissors and 2 seconds later they are buried under piles of fabric and I can't find them anymore!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Please tell me I'm not the only one!?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I've had on my mind for the last few months that I need to do something.  It's not efficient.  I was wasting time, energy, and getting frustrated.  So when I walked into the Dollar Spot section in
                     <a class="g-profile" href="https://plus.google.com/110542030981995160584" target="_blank">
                      +Target
                     </a>
                     and saw these cute paper holders ($3/each), and pen holders ($1 each), I knew that they would work perfectly for my quilting rules and other cutting accessories.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UZ190rHGr-8/VZr6kip-kBI/AAAAAAAAR9g/sQC229tp1Jo/s1600/image1%25281%2529.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Simple and Cheap Sewing Room Organization" border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UZ190rHGr-8/VZr6kip-kBI/AAAAAAAAR9g/sQC229tp1Jo/s640/image1%25281%2529.JPG" title="Simple and Cheap Sewing Room Organization" width="512"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     As soon as I got home, I hurried to my garage and looked for board that would work and had my husband cut it down to size.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Then I spray painted the board to match my walls and trim.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Then I used wood screws and secured the board to the studs in the wall.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-9O2RlqXcL8M/VZr4vCar5lI/AAAAAAAAR9I/-LTiCsmFzUg/s1600/FullSizeRender%25281%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="How to Secure paper organizers to wall for quilting rulers" border="0" height="480" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-9O2RlqXcL8M/VZr4vCar5lI/AAAAAAAAR9I/-LTiCsmFzUg/s640/FullSizeRender%25281%2529.jpg" title="How to Secure paper organizers to wall for quilting rulers" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     To secure the baskets to the board I used picture hanging hooks. I also used the little c hooks for the pencil holders
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-jkNQzmXKQbo/VZr4u96JDiI/AAAAAAAAR9E/Ru58EHPMiA0/s1600/FullSizeRender%25282%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Pen Holder used for Sewing Room Organization" border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-jkNQzmXKQbo/VZr4u96JDiI/AAAAAAAAR9E/Ru58EHPMiA0/s640/FullSizeRender%25282%2529.jpg" title="Pen Holder used for Sewing Room Organization" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then I also screwed some magnetic pieces to the board (I found them on Ebay) and painted them the same color as the board.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I just love how simple (and cheap!) this board ended up being. Plus, it will keep me a lot more organized, which equals being more efficient, which gives me more time to spend with my little kiddos.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     #win-win
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cPfwl80vjYs/VZr4wE-KfyI/AAAAAAAAR9U/6Z6_Hkxu0n4/s1600/FullSizeRender.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Cutting Table Sewing Organization" border="0" height="342" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cPfwl80vjYs/VZr4wE-KfyI/AAAAAAAAR9U/6Z6_Hkxu0n4/s640/FullSizeRender.jpg" title="Cutting Table Sewing Organization" width="640"/>
                      </a>

                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   6.29.2015
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DWXhLwVJnas/VYX1Ggi37hI/AAAAAAAAR5g/irQDBq1Y22U/s640/IMG_4495.JPG" itemprop="image_url"/>
                    <meta content="1510156664287988361" itemprop="blogId"/>
                    <meta content="9131852816503347097" itemprop="postId"/>
                    <a name="9131852816503347097">
                    </a>
                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.avisiontoremember.com/2015/06/baby-receiving-blanket-with-satin.html">
                      Baby Receiving Blanket with Satin Binding in 10 Minutes Flat! The Quickest Receiving Blanket Ever
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-9131852816503347097" itemprop="articleBody">
                     This is such a simple and quick quilt to sew! It also is fairly inexpensive, depending on how expensive your fabric is that you have chosen to work with.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Plus, you can complete it in 10 minutes. Seriously, It is that quick. That's what makes it the perfect baby shower gift for someone in a time crunch (like I always seem to be)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DWXhLwVJnas/VYX1Ggi37hI/AAAAAAAAR5g/irQDBq1Y22U/s1600/IMG_4495.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DWXhLwVJnas/VYX1Ggi37hI/AAAAAAAAR5g/irQDBq1Y22U/s640/IMG_4495.JPG" width="512"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     First you will need:
                     <br/>
                     1 package of Satin Blanket Binding
                     <br/>
                     1 Yard of Knit Fabric-Cut into 36" square
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-lEBOFuOQkkA/U-4P-30MHyI/AAAAAAAAHCU/9ECK7HhZaNo/s640/blogger-image-2144237673.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-lEBOFuOQkkA/U-4P-30MHyI/AAAAAAAAHCU/9ECK7HhZaNo/s640/blogger-image-2144237673.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-QD-TkDdEXyY/U-4QF3hW0nI/AAAAAAAAHCc/jrKK8Adg1Rs/s640/blogger-image--1769033526.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-QD-TkDdEXyY/U-4QF3hW0nI/AAAAAAAAHCc/jrKK8Adg1Rs/s640/blogger-image--1769033526.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Sew the binding to the knit fabric. There are a ton of really great tutorials and videos online of how to do binding. Doing satin binding is a little bit easier than regular binding because you can sew both the top and the bottom on at the same time. Satin binding has already been folded over perfectly and there is a nice crease down the middle. So shove that crease all the way over to the edge of your knit fabric. If you want you can use wonder clips, alligator hair clips, straight pins, or whatever else you use to secure your fabric in place. Now top stitch that binding to your knit fabric.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both;">
                      <a href="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-Wda7X-ypEv8/U-4QL0j5K-I/AAAAAAAAHCk/rTPddE_eMyE/s640/blogger-image-1411385344.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-Wda7X-ypEv8/U-4QL0j5K-I/AAAAAAAAHCk/rTPddE_eMyE/s400/blogger-image-1411385344.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     When you get to your corner, sew all the way to the edge, then pull your blanket out of the machine, and gently fold/crease your binding so that it will go down the next side of the blanket. Make sure that your point on the outside of the blanket and the inside corner all line up.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both;">
                      <a href="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-84SB-2V9WHM/U-4P6QbOdDI/AAAAAAAAHCM/8B1SKAZJ62I/s640/blogger-image-1847890406.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-84SB-2V9WHM/U-4P6QbOdDI/AAAAAAAAHCM/8B1SKAZJ62I/s400/blogger-image-1847890406.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     And now you are all finished and have an absolutely adorable baby quilt. It really should be quick. It really is the perfect last minute baby shower gift. Quick, cost effective, and pretty darn cute!
