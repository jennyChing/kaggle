     <p>
      <b>
       Heterobranchia
      </b>
      , the
      <i>
       heterobranchs
      </i>
      (meaning "different-gilled snails"), is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taxonomic" title="Taxonomic">
       taxonomic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">
       clade
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Snail" title="Snail">
       snails
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Slugs" title="Slugs">
       slugs
      </a>
      , which includes
      <a href="/wiki/Marine_(ocean)" title="Marine (ocean)">
       marine
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Aquatic_animal" title="Aquatic animal">
       aquatic
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Terrestrial_animal" title="Terrestrial animal">
       terrestrial
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gastropod" title="Gastropod">
       gastropod
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mollusk" title="Mollusk">
       mollusks
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Heterobranchia is one of the main clades of gastropods. Currently Heterobranchia comprises three informal groups: the
      <a href="/wiki/Lower_Heterobranchia" title="Lower Heterobranchia">
       Lower Heterobranchia
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Opisthobranchia" title="Opisthobranchia">
       Opisthobranchia
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Pulmonata" title="Pulmonata">
       Pulmonata
      </a>
      , also known as the lower heterobranchs, the opisthobranchs and the pulmonates.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bouchet-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Diversity">
       Diversity
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heterobranchia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Diversity">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The three subdivisions of this large clade are quite diverse:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Lower_Heterobranchia" title="Lower Heterobranchia">
        Lower Heterobranchia
       </a>
       includes shelled marine and freshwater species.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Opisthobranchia" title="Opisthobranchia">
        Opisthobranchia
       </a>
       are almost all marine species, some
       <a href="/wiki/Gastropod_shell" title="Gastropod shell">
        shelled
       </a>
       and some not. The internal organs of the opisthobranchs have undergone detorsion (unwinding of the viscera that were twisted during
       <a href="/wiki/Torsion_(gastropod)" title="Torsion (gastropod)">
        torsion
       </a>
       ).
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Pulmonata" title="Pulmonata">
        Pulmonata
       </a>
       includes the majority of land snails and slugs, many freshwater snails, and a small number of marine species. The mantle cavity of the Pulmonata is modified into an air-breathing organ. They are also characterized by detorsion and a symmetrically-arranged nervous system. The pulmonates almost always lack an
       <a href="/wiki/Operculum_(gastropod)" title="Operculum (gastropod)">
        operculum
       </a>
       and are
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hermaphroditic" title="Hermaphroditic">
        hermaphroditic
       </a>
       .
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">
       Taxonomy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heterobranchia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Older_taxonomy">
       Older taxonomy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heterobranchia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Older taxonomy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The families currently included in Heterobranchia have historically been placed in many different parts of the taxonomic class of gastropods. Earlier authors (such as
      <a href="/wiki/John_Edward_Gray" title="John Edward Gray">
       J.E. Gray
      </a>
      , 1840) considered Heterobranchia to consist of only marine gastropods, and conceptualized it as a borderline category, intermediate between the
      <a href="/wiki/Opisthobranchia" title="Opisthobranchia">
       Opisthobranchia
      </a>
      &amp;
      <a href="/wiki/Pulmonata" title="Pulmonata">
       Pulmonata
      </a>
      , and all the other gastropods.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The (sometimes recognized) category
      <a href="/wiki/Heterostropha" title="Heterostropha">
       Heterostropha
      </a>
      within the Heterobranchia, which includes such families as
      <a href="/wiki/Architectonicidae" title="Architectonicidae">
       Architectonicidae
      </a>
      , the sundial or staircase snails, is primarily characterized by a shell which has a heterostrophic
      <a href="/wiki/Protoconch" title="Protoconch">
       protoconch
      </a>
      , in other words the apical whorls are coiled in the opposite plane to the adult whorls. The classification of this group was revised by
      <a href="/wiki/Winston_Ponder" title="Winston Ponder">
       Ponder
      </a>
      &amp; Warn in 1988.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      According to the older
      <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy_of_the_Gastropoda_(Ponder_%26_Lindberg,_1997)" title="Taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Ponder &amp; Lindberg, 1997)">
       taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Ponder &amp; Lindberg, 1997)
      </a>
      the Heterobranchia were ranked as a superorder.
     </p>

     <p>
      Heterobranchia is currently one of the main clades of gastropods. For a detailed taxonomy, see
      <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy_of_the_Gastropoda_(Bouchet_%26_Rocroi,_2005)#Clade_Heterobranchia" title="Taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Bouchet &amp; Rocroi, 2005)">
       Taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Bouchet &amp; Rocroi, 2005)#Clade Heterobranchia

     </p>
