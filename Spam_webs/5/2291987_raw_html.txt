       <h1 class="entryviewheading">
        Sling Back - A Universal Wire Retractor
       </h1>
       <div class="entryviewheadersub">
        <span class="hiddentext">
         Published
        </span>
        Friday, December 18, 2009 12:56 PM
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div align="center">
        <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.blogiversity.org/blogs/frankthetank/slingback.jpg"/>
       </div>
       <br/>
       Ever since the iPod came out people have been wondering what to do with their headphone cord. You can wrap it, fold it, or twist it but this usually ends up damaging the cord or crimping the wire. So the best thing for a wire it to be wound around something without being twisted. Companies sell headphone that come with their own cord retractor but I don't want their headphones I want to use mine! Enter the Sling Back, a universal wire retractor for your headphone or whatever other cord you may have. The Sling Back works with any chord that is 1/8" inches in diameter or less. I had this very idea several years ago and here it is. The product is in pre-production over at Quirky.com. You can pre-order one and when they get to their pre-sale goal they will start producing and shipping. You can get one of these guys for only $9.99 which I think is a great deal so go and check it out.
