      <h1 class="title">
       <a href="http://www.4tunate.net/2010/09/everybody-needs-a-little-time-away/" rel="bookmark" title="Everybody Needs A Little Time Away">
        Everybody Needs A Little Time Away
       </a>
      </h1>
      <div class="entry">
       <div id="fcbk_share">
        <div class="fcbk_like">
         <div id="fb-root">
         </div>
         <script src="http://connect.facebook.net/en_US/all.js#appId=224313110927811&amp;xfbml=1">
         </script>
         <fb:like font="" href="http://www.4tunate.net/2010/09/everybody-needs-a-little-time-away/" layout="button_count" send="false" show_faces="false" width="450">
         </fb:like>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/225801048_2ef439c60c2.jpg">
         <font size="3">
         </font>
        </a>
        <a href="http://4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/225801048_2ef439c60c3.jpg">
         <img align="right" alt="225801048_2ef439c60c" border="0" height="328" src="http://4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/225801048_2ef439c60c_thumb.jpg" style="border-right-width: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; margin-right: 0px" title="225801048_2ef439c60c" width="251"/>
        </a>
        <font size="3">
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         <em>
          <strong>
           Even lovers need a holiday far away from
           <strike>
            each other
           </strike>
           their children.
          </strong>
         </em>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         So I totally just butchered a
         <a href="http://chicagotheband.com/">
          Chicago classic
         </a>
         , but hear me out for a few minutes.
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         I believe taking a break from parenting once in a while benefits not only you as the parent, but your entire family.
         <em>
          Spending a short time away with your mate alone  all the better.
         </em>
         Perhaps this comes across as completely selfish, or even uncompassionate towards my young, however, I think the benefits are completely contrary.
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         During the last few years, Brad and I at times have experienced trying, uncertain, troublesome, surprising, consuming, and dramatic circumstances in our 7 years of marriage. I know we are not alone in this journey, nor have we been given a load that we cannot bear. We have MUCH to be grateful for daily.
         <strong>
          We have grown together through many of these trials, and were thankful for how God has used them in our lives to draw us closer not only to one another, but to Him.
         </strong>
         It has caused us to not rely solely on one another, but to focus on the One who controls every minute detail of our lives.
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         However, sometimes the details in life distract us from the purpose in the difficulty. We too often have missed the mark as a couple.
         <strong>
          Instead of allowing trials strengthen our faith, weve fallen into patterns of discontentment, discouragement, complacency, and impatience
         </strong>
         . Its humbling to admit, and I am responsible for well over half of the blame.
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         <strong>
          All of this to say, Ive never felt more secure in our marriage.
         </strong>
         We are blessed with a solid foundation, and a cherish our lifelong
         <a href="http://4tunate.net/2009/08/covenant-relationship/">
          covenant relationship
         </a>
         . We love with a love that is deeper than the ocean and I am pleased to still be married to my best friend on earth.
         <em>
          However
         </em>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <font size="3">
           Marriage can be intense.
          </font>
         </em>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <font size="3">
           Parenting is intense.
          </font>
         </em>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         <strong>
          <em>
           Parenting can make marriage even more intense.
          </em>
         </strong>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         We consume ourselves in caring for our children, meeting their needs, sacrificing our own desires around the clock. 365 days a year.
         <strong>
          If were not careful our marriage gets the leftovers if theres anything left over to give at the end of a long day.
         </strong>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         Not only does this deliver the message to our husbands that hes last on the list, it communicates to our children that they are the utmost important. The pedestal is reserved for their occupancy.
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         <em>
          <strong>
           Where does this place our marriage? More importantly, where does this rank our relationship with Jesus?
          </strong>
         </em>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         I heard a staggering statistic today that marriage lasts an average of 7 years, and 200,000 marriages are dissolved within the first 2 years of marriage.
         <em>
          Sobering. Shocking. Sorrowing.
         </em>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         <strong>
          <em>
           So will taking a break away for a couple of days guarantee a blissful marriage?
          </em>
          <em>
           Maybe not.
          </em>
         </strong>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         <strong>
          <em>
           Does it demonstrate that your marriage is valuable, delicate, a priority, and worth fighting for?
          </em>
          <em>
           Quite Possibly.
          </em>
         </strong>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         I know that getting away takes financial means, and its simply not always feasible.
         <em>
          What about an extended date? 24 hours away?
         </em>
         It doesnt have to be extravagant or expensive.
         <em>
          (We used our already paid for
          <a href="http://4tunate.net/2009/10/did-i-fail-to-mention-our-trip-to-walt/">
           airline tickets that were about to expire
          </a>
          and got a family rate from my brother at a hotel, but you dont even have to leave your home state.)
         </em>
         In addition, it doesnt require an insane amount of planning
         <em>
          (minus finding childcare for four 3 year old boys).
         </em>
         <strong>
          There is no price tag on the opportunity to spending uninterrupted time relaxing, refreshing, and renewing.
         </strong>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         <em>
          <strong>
           Trust me, Im still coming down off the mountain with the man who still has my heart.
          </strong>
         </em>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font face="Loved by the King" size="6">
         <strong>
          QuatroMama
         </strong>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font size="3">
         <em>
          (A sincere thank you to Ryan, Emily, Melissa, and Kara for supporting our marriage and loving our kids by caring for them while we were away. We couldnt have done this without you!)
         </em>
        </font>
       </p>

          <p>
           When I was pregnant with my first, I remembering sitting in my supervisors office and asking him if he had any parenting advice.  He and his wife had been married nearly 30 years and had raised two terrific boys.  I expected him to pass along some sage advice about firmness or consistency or some other aspect of discipline.  Instead he told me to make sure that no matter what, my husband and I find time to slip away without the children at least once a year.  Even if it was just overnight down the road.  I thought he was confused, that he thought I was asking for marriage advice, not parenting advice.
          </p>
          <p>
           By golly, he was right, though.  Weve snuck away together every year since our oldest one born.  Some years its been lengthy and extravagant, other years its been a quick, cheap getaway.  It gives us the energy to come back and parent with purpose.  More importantly, it helps us remember why we fell in love in the first place, and why we wouldnt want anything to ever destroy that love.
          </p>
