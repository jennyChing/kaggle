          Can you sue the Almighty's publishers?
         </h1>

         <div class="entry-content">
          <div class="entry-body">
           <p>
            If you can't sue the Lord for libel, what are your options? A Michigan man is about to find out. Bradley LaShawn Fowler, 39, has filed lawsuits in a Michigan federal court against Zondervan Publishing and Thomas Nelson Publishing, claiming some editions of the Bibles those companies put out call homosexuality sinful, which has led him to suffer discrimination, emotional pain and mental instability.
           </p>
          </div>
          <!-- .entry-body -->
          <a id="more">
          </a>
          <div class="entry-more">
           <p>
            "Defendant willfully caused me to endure acts of hate, discrimination, and loss of sleep, appetite, by structuring their New King James Bible to reflect Gods distaste of homosexuals," Fowler wrote in his complaint against Thomas Nelson filed this week.
           </p>
           <p>
            "By designing this product to promote hate and violence toward homosexuality, because such product is promoted as being the authentic word of God, it is a design defect," says Fowlers lawsuit.
           </p>
           <p>
            Fowler is seeking $60 million from Zondervan, alleging their Bibles refer to homosexuality as a sin have made him an outcast from his family and contributed to physical discomfort and periods of "demoralization, chaos and bewilderment." He is seeking $10 million from Thomas Nelson.
           </p>
           <p>
            The suit against Zondervan claims 1982 and 1987 editions of the publishers Bible declare homosexuality to be wrong in 1 Corinthians 6:9:
            <a name="N_00484_2">
            </a>
            "Do you not know that the wicked will not inherit the kingdom of God? Do not be deceived: Neither the sexually immoral nor idolaters nor adulterers nor male prostitutes nor homosexual offenders."
           </p>
           <p>
            Fowler claims the term was edited out of the 1989 and 1994 editions, but consumers were not informed.
           </p>
           <p>
            "This misrepresentation is a willful and deliberate tort. Fraudulently imposing a written defamation or libel in order to prevent me from marrying someone of the same sex in this state," his lawsuit states. "This obvious coerced method of mind control and social dictatorship violates the religious [sacred] laws which prevent anyone from adding to the Biblical scriptures or from taking any words away from the text."
           </p>
           <p>
            Fowler levels similar allegations against Thomas Nelson regarding the companys earlier versions of the New King James Bible.
           </p>
           <p>
            The intent of the publisher was to promulgate a point of view to cause "me or anyone who is a homosexual to endure verbal abuse, discrimination, episodes of hate, and physical violence ... including murder," the lawsuit states.
           </p>
           <p>
            Fowler said the editions of the Bibles he cites have destroyed his relationship with his family who refuses to support him because the Bible says homosexuality is a sin.
           </p>
           <p>
            What do you think? Should the publishers of Bibles be held accountable for the pain inflicted by what many readers consider to be Gods word?
           </p>

             At first glance this struck me as a frivolous lawsuit; if you don't like what the Bible says, well, tough. The first amendment guarantees the right to offend. But then I looked more closely through my various translations, and the issue makes a bit more sense. My NKJV (Nelson) translation has a tiny footnote next to the word "homosexuals"; it says, "that is, catamites." And when I look up "catamite" in a dictionary, I find the word does not mean what we typically associate with "homosexual"; it means a young man who participates in pederasty (sex with older men), and there is a sort of inference that this is done for money or other rewards. If this is the case, that the precise translation of the Greek is "catamites," then the translation as "homosexuals" is inaccurate. And while I don't believe there's a case to be made against the Bible's actual content, there is a case to be made against a publisher who mistranslates the Greek text. At the least it sounds like negligence, and at the worst it sounds like an attempt to advance a political agenda that's not justified by the text. This could become a very interesting case.

            <p>
             This is a legal case which will be handled in court, and I'm not a lawyer so I'm flying blind insofar as the legalities are concerned.
            </p>
            <p>
             But there is common sense.  Modern Bible versions are based on translation of ancient Hebrew and Greek texts we can safely assume are in the public domain.  Thomas Nelson and Zondervan are not creating Bible content.
            </p>
            <p>
             How can Mr Fowler's "pain and suffering"  stem from the Bible?  The Bible has to be communicated to a person, who choses how to respond to its message.  Mr Fowler's relatives may respond in ways Mr Fowler doesn't like, but Ellen Degeneres' or Bill Maher's response to Bible content might be more to his liking.  There are also Christians who might stop short of shunning a gay relative
            </p>
            <p>
             Most important, how much latitude do we have to communicate ideas among us, or hold beliefs?.  If we can sue publishers and authors, that puts us on a slippery slope.  Fortunately, my layman's take strongly suggests this is a garden variety nuisance lawsuit.
            </p>
            <p>
             I'm most curious about why Mr Fowler's attorney agreed to file this lawsuit.  There are plenty of Christian ministers and ministries to target that are hostile to homosexuality.
            </p>
            <p>
             But not with deeper pockets than Thomas Nelson and Zondervan.
