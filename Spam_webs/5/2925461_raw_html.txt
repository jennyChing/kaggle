     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        C.S.A.: The Confederate States of America
       </b>
      </i>
      is a 2004
      <a href="/wiki/Mockumentary" title="Mockumentary">
       mockumentary
      </a>
      directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Willmott" title="Kevin Willmott">
       Kevin Willmott
      </a>
      . It's a
      <a href="/wiki/Fiction" title="Fiction">
       fictional
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Tongue-in-cheek" title="Tongue-in-cheek">
       tongue-in-cheek
      </a>
      account of an
      <a href="/wiki/Alternate_history" title="Alternate history">
       alternate history
      </a>
      in which the
      <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">
       Confederates
      </a>
      won the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">
       American Civil War
      </a>
      , establishing the new
      <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">
       Confederate States of America
      </a>
      (that incorporates the former United States as well). The film primarily details significant political and cultural events of Confederate history from its founding until the early 2000s. This viewpoint is used to satirize real-life issues and events, and to shed light on the continuing existence of discrimination in
      <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_the_United_States" title="Culture of the United States">
       American culture
      </a>
      .
      <i>
       C.S.A
      </i>
      was released on
      <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">
       DVD
      </a>
      on August 8, 2006.
     </p>
     <p>
      Willmott, who had earlier written a
      <a href="/wiki/Screenplay" title="Screenplay">
       screenplay
      </a>
      about
      <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism_in_the_United_States" title="Abolitionism in the United States">
       abolitionist
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/John_Brown_(abolitionist)" title="John Brown (abolitionist)">
       John Brown
      </a>
      , told interviewers he was inspired to write the story after seeing an episode of
      <a href="/wiki/Ken_Burns" title="Ken Burns">
       Ken Burns
      </a>
      '
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Civil_War_(TV_series)" title="The Civil War (TV series)">
        The Civil War
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was produced through his
      <a href="/wiki/Hodcarrier_Films" title="Hodcarrier Films">
       Hodcarrier Films
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="American_Civil_War">
       American Civil War
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

     <p>
      In an alternate United States history reality, after the Union victory in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Battle_of_Antietam" title="The Battle of Antietam">
       The Battle of Antietam
      </a>
      , Union President
      <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">
       Abraham Lincoln
      </a>
      issued a revolutionary proclamation entitled the "Emancipation Proclamation" (which announces that all slaves are permanently free), butin this fictional realitythe proclamation failed. Confederate President
      <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Davis" title="Jefferson Davis">
       Jefferson Davis
      </a>
      took this opportunity to counteract the Emancipation Proclamation and sent politician
      <a href="/wiki/Judah_P._Benjamin" title="Judah P. Benjamin">
       Judah P. Benjamin
      </a>
      to persuade the military and financial aid of
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">
       United Kingdom
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Second_French_Empire" title="Second French Empire">
       France
      </a>
      to the Confederacy in their fight against the Union. Benjamin also created the "Southern Cause of States' Rights" which proclaims Southerners have the right to have private property and doesn't proclaim it being the salvation of slavery. Eventually after the success of Benjamin's gambit, the Confederateswith the aid of British and French forceswere able to win the
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">
       Battle of Gettysburg
      </a>
      , capture
      <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">
       Washington, D.C.
      </a>
      and take over the
      <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">
       White House
      </a>
      a few months later, but by then the now deposed President Lincoln escaped. Union General
      <a href="/wiki/Ulysses_S._Grant" title="Ulysses S. Grant">
       Ulysses S. Grant
      </a>
      surrenders to Confederate General
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_E._Lee" title="Robert E. Lee">
       Robert E. Lee
      </a>
      on April 9, 1864, effectively ending the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">
       American Civil War
      </a>
      . The hunt for the now deposed President Lincoln (disguised in
      <a href="/wiki/Blackface" title="Blackface">
       blackface
      </a>
      ) and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abolitionist" title="Abolitionist">
       abolitionist
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Harriet_Tubman" title="Harriet Tubman">
       Harriet Tubman
      </a>
      was undertaken, and both are eventually captured, which became the prime subject of
      <a href="/wiki/D._W._Griffith" title="D. W. Griffith">
       D. W. Griffith
      </a>
      's fictional classic
      <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">
       silent film
      </a>
      called
      <i>
       The Hunt for Dishonest Abe
      </i>
      released in 1915 and shows a humorous reenactment of Lincoln and Tubman's failed escape attempt. Abraham Lincoln was quickly tried for war crimes against the Confederacy and was imprisoned in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fortress_Monroe" title="Fortress Monroe">
       Fortress Monroe
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">
       Virginia
      </a>
      , where he watched the execution of Tubman from his cell. In 1866, Lincolnfrail and gaunt from his two-year sentenceis fully pardoned by the now President Davis and exiled to
      <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
       Canada
      </a>
      , where he remains until he dies in June 1905 at the age of 96. Shortly before his death, Lincoln laments not having made the Civil War a battle to end slavery. As the years would past, the C.S.A. identified the American Civil War as "the War of Northern Aggression".

      <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">

      <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">
       East Germany
      </a>
      before its annexation by the
      <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
       Soviet Union
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Rosa_Parks" title="Rosa Parks">
       Rosa Parks
      </a>
      is identified as a Canadian terrorist and a member of the
      <a href="/wiki/John_Brown_(abolitionist)" title="John Brown (abolitionist)">
       John Brown
      </a>
      Underground. The
      <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II_assassination_attempt" title="Pope John Paul II assassination attempt">
       failed assassination attempt
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">
       Pope John Paul II
      </a>
      occurs in
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      instead of
      <a href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Square" title="St. Peter's Square">
       St. Peter's Square
      </a>
      , with the assailant being a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Baptist" title="Southern Baptist">
       Southern Baptist
      </a>
      who is subsequently executed for the crime.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">
       Gulf War
      </a>
      has
      <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">
       Kuwait
      </a>
      as CSA territory. In 1995,
      <a href="/wiki/Timothy_McVeigh" title="Timothy McVeigh">
       Timothy McVeigh
      </a>
      blows up the
      <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Memorial" title="Jefferson Memorial">
       Jefferson Memorial
      </a>
      instead of
      <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_bombing" title="Oklahoma City bombing">
       the Murrah Building in Oklahoma City
      </a>
      ; his execution is broadcast on
      <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">
       pay-per-view
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>

        </span>
        5
        <span>

        </span>

      </sup>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001-present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001-present)">
       War in Afghanistan
      </a>
      and subsequent American interventions in the
      <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">
       Middle East
      </a>
      are known as the 1st and 2nd
      <a href="/wiki/Crusades" title="Crusades">
       Crusades
      </a>
      , with the goal of eradicating the "
      <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">
       Muslim
      </a>
      Menace" by overthrowing the Islamic governments, taking over their
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_production" title="Oil production">
       oil production
      </a>
      , and converting their populace to Christianity.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Main_cast">
       Main cast
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=C.S.A.:_The_Confederate_States_of_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Main cast">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Rupert Pate as Sherman Hoyle, a Confederate American historian who speaks highly of the Confederate American values.
      </li>
      <li>
       Evamarii Johnson as Patricia Johnson, an African-Canadian historian whose viewpoints focus on the slaves and minorities oppressed by the Confederate regime.
      </li>
      <li>
       Larry Peterson as Senator John Ambrose Fauntroy V, descendant of Confederate senator John Ambrose Fauntroy I and Democratic candidate for the presidency in 2002.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Charles_Frank" title="Charles Frank">
        Charles Frank
       </a>
       as the mockumentary's narrator.
      </li>
     </ul>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=C.S.A.:_The_Confederate_States_of_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The film received generally positive reviews, garnering a 78% approval rating via
      <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">
       Rotten Tomatoes
      </a>
      . Most critics were intrigued by the film's premise, but some found the execution to be lacking primarily due to a low budget. The film grossed US$744,165 worldwide in limited release.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
