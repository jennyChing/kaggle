       <p>
        <b>
         Houses
        </b>
        in
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion" title="Oblivion:Oblivion">
         Oblivion
        </a>
        are buildings in which you may sleep and safely store items in
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Containers#Safe_Containers" title="Oblivion:Containers">
         non-respawning containers
        </a>
        . Each
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Cities" title="Oblivion:Cities">
         major city
        </a>
        (except
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Kvatch" title="Oblivion:Kvatch">
         Kvatch
        </a>
        ) has a house available for you to purchase. Some of the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Official_Plug-ins" title="Oblivion:Official Plug-ins">
         official plug-ins
        </a>
        also add new homes, and there are even a few
        <a href="#Free_.22Houses.22">
         free homes
        </a>
        that become accessible after completing associated quests. However, only the houses purchased inside major cities count towards your "Houses Owned"
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Journal#Accomplishments" title="Oblivion:Journal">
         statistic
        </a>
        . There is no limit to the number of houses you can own. Once a home is purchased and has been entered, the door is permanently unlocked (although there is a glitch associated with the
        <a href="#Bravil">
         Bravil house
        </a>
        ). Although it is impossible to lock the door, no NPCs will enter your house when you are not there, nor will any NPCs steal items from your house.
       </p>
       <p>
        The benefits of owning a house may not be immediately evident. There are
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Sleeping_and_Eating" title="Oblivion:Sleeping and Eating">
         free beds
        </a>
        available in the game, any items left in a pile on the floor are perfectly safe, and there are even some containers outside houses that do not respawn. One main benefit of owning houses is that they allow you to be organized, giving easy access to your belongings and storage areas. Houses provide you with a regular place to sleep near your stored items, and support
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Roleplaying" title="Oblivion:Roleplaying">
         roleplaying
        </a>
        situations calling for a home. Fast-traveling from within the purchasable homes in every city (except Anvil) is another benefit, especially for
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Vampirism" title="Oblivion:Vampirism">
         vampires
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Note:
        </b>
        Each price listed on this page is a
        <i>
         Base Cost
        </i>
        , meaning that each player will nearly always have to pay more than the amount listed, as detailed in
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Commerce" title="Oblivion:Commerce">
         Commerce
        </a>
        .
        <b>
         Warning:
        </b>
        Immediately selling the upgrade papers after having bought them from the merchant (without ever exiting the conversation) prevents the new upgrades from ever appearing in the house. Furthermore, the upgrades will not reappear in the merchant's sales list, since you "gave" away the no-cost notes.
       </p>

       <h2>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="City_Houses">
         City Houses
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oblivion:Houses&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: City Houses">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Once you've learned that a house is for sale, buying that house becomes a quest. The quest is completed when the house and all available upgrades have been purchased. There is no need to carry the house key or the receipts from the upgrades. Upgrades are available for each house, adding furniture and other items. These upgrades can easily cost more than the house itself, by the time you've bought them all. One unusual property of these houses is the fact that you can fast travel from the interior of all these houses, except
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Benirus_Manor" title="Oblivion:Benirus Manor">
         Benirus Manor
        </a>
        .
       </p>

       <p>
        Upon purchasing Benirus Manor in Anvil, you will notice that it is in disrepair inside and out; however, after you complete
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Where_Spirits_Have_Lease" title="Oblivion:Where Spirits Have Lease">
         Where Spirits Have Lease
        </a>
        , the house will be restored to its former beauty. During the quest, ghosts can be found within the house. Even after its restoration, it's a little dark inside, but it has a balcony, a loft office upstairs overlooking the sitting area, thirty-three containers in the basement, and a secret chamber (related to the quest). Note that you should
        <i>
         not
        </i>
        use this house for storage before completing the quest, as any items in the house will be lost when the house is repaired. Once you've completed the quest however, all the containers are safe to use. Note that the remains of the ghosts you encounter on the quest stay in the house for a while but do go away eventually. Benirus Manor is the only house for sale in the cities that uses a quest to renovate/furnish it, and not purchasable upgrades. See the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Benirus_Manor" title="Oblivion:Benirus Manor">
         Benirus Manor
        </a>
        article for additional information.

         doesn't
        </i>
        have a lit fireplace.

       <p>
        The house in
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Cheydinhal" title="Oblivion:Cheydinhal">
         Cheydinhal
        </a>
        is located at the far southwestern tip of the city. If you stand just outside the Mages Guild, you can see it directly in front of you across the plaza. It's bigger than all the cheaper ones and looks to be in very good condition - pricey too, though. Once fully furnished, it will look great inside.
       </p>
       <p>
        The main benefit of this house is that there are thirteen containers on the second floor: seven crates, barrels, and chests in a pile in front of the staircase, two cupboards on the other side of the staircase, and four bureaus and chests in the bedroom. Unlike many other houses where you have to run around the entire house to empty your loot, these thirteen containers are all laid out conveniently. This along with many, many shelves have led some to the conclusion that it is one of the best "real" houses in the game ignoring price, second only to Rosethorn Hall. For only 15,000 septims, it is a good alternative. The ground floor of the interior does not extend as far to the north as the exterior suggests.
       </p>

        </a>
        is the house for sale in
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Chorrol" title="Oblivion:Chorrol">

        </a>

        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Chorrol_Mages_Guild" title="Oblivion:Chorrol Mages Guild">

        </a>
        . It features a sitting area, study, kitchen area, and dining area on the ground level. It also has two upper levels; one houses a servant's quarters as well as a bedroom, while the other is a master suite. Completing
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:A_Shadow_Over_Hackdirt" title="Oblivion:A Shadow Over Hackdirt">
         A Shadow Over Hackdirt
        </a>
        grants a disposition of 100 with
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Seed-Neeus" title="Oblivion:Seed-Neeus">
         Seed-Neeus
        </a>
        which helps with prices. Note that, while one upgrade available for purchase is "House Servants Quarters", no help-for-hire is available for this house. See the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Arborwatch" title="Oblivion:Arborwatch">
         Arborwatch
        </a>
        article for additional information.

       <p>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Shack_for_Sale" title="Oblivion:Shack for Sale">
         Shack for Sale
        </a>
        in the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Imperial_City" title="Oblivion:Imperial City">
         Imperial City
        </a>
        is located in the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Waterfront_District" title="Oblivion:Waterfront District">
         Waterfront District
        </a>
        , and is nothing more than a small pitiful shack, but does have a bed where you can sleep and level up. The upgrades are rather pathetic as well, but you will likely need the storage extension, because the basic house comes with zero containers. The Kitchen Area upgrade also adds some storage. The crate outside the house is
        <i>
         not
        </i>
        safe for storage. It is also perfect for people with large bounties and a need to avoid all guards until you can meet up with a Doyen. You can fast-travel to
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Dzonot_Cave" title="Oblivion:Dzonot Cave">
         Dzonot Cave
        </a>
        just west of the Temple District, and from there it is only a short swim to your house. See the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Shack_for_Sale" title="Oblivion:Shack for Sale">
         Shack for Sale
        </a>
        article for additional information.
       </p>

       <p>
        The house in
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Leyawiin" title="Oblivion:Leyawiin">
         Leyawiin
        </a>
        is in the east of town next to the castle, where many other homes are. It is somewhat bigger than the cheaper shacks, but looks very rundown and ugly, especially next door to the huge and beautiful house of the wealthy Rosentia Gallenus.
       </p>
       <p>
        The interior of the home is rather bland and unimpressive, even after all interior upgrades have been acquired. Considering the price of the property and its size (barely larger than the Bravil shack yet 3,000 gold more expensive), it is debatable whether this house is worth the 7,000 gold you will have to spend on it.
       </p>

       <p>
        The house in Skingrad, called Rosethorn Hall, is in the eastern part of the town and is the grandest (and most expensive) home that can be bought. Rosethorn Hall was formerly owned by
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Vandorallen_Trebatius" title="Oblivion:Vandorallen Trebatius">
         Vandorallen Trebatius
        </a>
        , but he is "evicted" when you buy the house.
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Eyja" title="Oblivion:Eyja">
         A housekeeper
        </a>
        is available
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Helping_Hands" title="Oblivion:Helping Hands">
         for hire
        </a>
        upon purchase of the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Rosethorn_Hall#House_Servants_Quarters" title="Oblivion:Rosethorn Hall">
         Servants Quarters
        </a>
        upgrade. See the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Rosethorn_Hall" title="Oblivion:Rosethorn Hall">
         Rosethorn Hall
        </a>
        article for additional information, including the three quests related to this house.
       </p>

       <p>
        This is the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Battlehorn_Castle_(place)" title="Oblivion:Battlehorn Castle (place)">
         castle
        </a>
        from the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Fighter%27s_Stronghold" title="Oblivion:Fighter's Stronghold">
         Fighter's Stronghold
        </a>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Official_Plug-ins" title="Oblivion:Official Plug-ins">
         official plug-in
        </a>
        . The associated
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Battlehorn_Castle_(quest)" title="Oblivion:Battlehorn Castle (quest)">
         quest
        </a>
        is required to claim it as your home. It is designed for warriors and combat-based characters. The castle has three "hidden" doors. The easiest can be found by exploring the basement - a door at the back of the wine cellar leads to a hidden pillar door on the main floor. The second is a hidden pillar door at the back of the private quarters. This door leads to a small "storage vault" with three chests, two barrels, a box, and a jewelry case. In the ceiling is a trapdoor that leads to the roof where you will find a steel bow, arrows, and some mugs, presumably for drinking and shooting from the roof of your castle. There is a journal on the top of a bookcase that leads you to the third secret door, behind the archery target in the weapons room in the basement. Use the unlit wall candelabra to the right to open the door. Travel through another hidden pillar door to fight the skeleton and lich that inhabit the castle's
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Unwanted_Tenants" title="Oblivion:Unwanted Tenants">
         grotto
        </a>
        .
       </p>

       <p>
        This is not a house in the traditional sense, but
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Deepscorn_Hollow_(place)" title="Oblivion:Deepscorn Hollow (place)">
         Deepscorn Hollow
        </a>
        , from the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Vile_Lair" title="Oblivion:Vile Lair">
         Vile Lair
        </a>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Official_Plug-ins" title="Oblivion:Official Plug-ins">
         official plug-in
        </a>
        . A
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Deepscorn_Hollow_(quest)" title="Oblivion:Deepscorn Hollow (quest)">
         quest
        </a>
        must be completed to actually own it. It is tailored for "evil" or "criminal" characters. This house is for those that wish to live like the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Dark_Brotherhood" title="Oblivion:Dark Brotherhood">
         Dark Brotherhood
        </a>
        but do things their own way. The "lair" is in a very hard to reach area of the map in a swampy area, and is decorated with interesting and morbid items. When the upgrades are completed, you have the perfect house for a
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Vampire" title="Oblivion:Vampire">
         vampire
        </a>
        assassin. The Shrine of Sithis can cure illnesses if your Infamy is too high to use temples, and the Font of Renewal can cure vampirism. It offers many poisonous plants, some unique. You can even send out a
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Dark_Minion" title="Oblivion:Dark Minion">
         Dark Minion
        </a>
        to "kill in the name of Sithis" for you, storing the items of the victim in the lair's chest, though this will earn you an infamy point. It is possible to obtain and use all the lair's benefits except the Shrine of Sithis and sending the Dark Minion out without earning any infamy points. The
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal" title="Oblivion:Gray Cowl of Nocturnal">
         Gray Cowl of Nocturnal
        </a>
        may be used to circumvent these restrictions, but a character concerned with keeping his or her infamy at zero is unlikely to have obtained the cowl.
       </p>

       <p>
        This is not a house in the traditional sense; this is
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Dunbarrow_Cove_(place)" title="Oblivion:Dunbarrow Cove (place)">
         Dunbarrow Cove
        </a>
        from the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Thieves_Den" title="Oblivion:Thieves Den">
         Thieves Den
        </a>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Official_Plug-ins" title="Oblivion:Official Plug-ins">
         official plug-in
        </a>
        . This "house" is particularly well suited for thief-type characters. It may be slightly difficult to reach if your character is heavily burdened. To obtain this as a base of operations, you must complete a
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Dunbarrow_Cove_(quest)" title="Oblivion:Dunbarrow Cove (quest)">
         quest
        </a>
        and purchase all the upgrades. It offers five merchants, one of whom is a Spell Merchant and one of whom is a Fence; they also offer training in
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Speechcraft" title="Oblivion:Speechcraft">
         Speechcraft
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Mercantile" title="Oblivion:Mercantile">
         Mercantile
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Security" title="Oblivion:Security">
         Security
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Marksman" title="Oblivion:Marksman">
         Marksman
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Sneak" title="Oblivion:Sneak">
         Sneak
        </a>
        . The cave contains both
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Cairn_Bolete_Cap" title="Oblivion:Cairn Bolete Cap">
         Cairn Bolete Cap
        </a>
        and
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Wisp_Stalks" title="Oblivion:Wisp Stalks">
         Wisp Stalks
        </a>
        - useful in making both healing potions as well as damaging poisons. Outside the cave entrance and along the coast nearby are
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Sacred_Lotus" title="Oblivion:Sacred Lotus">
         Sacred Lotuses
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Water_Hyacinth" title="Oblivion:Water Hyacinth">
         Water Hyacinths
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Lady%27s_Mantle" title="Oblivion:Lady's Mantle">
         Lady's Mantles
        </a>
        , and some
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Aloe_Vera" title="Oblivion:Aloe Vera">
         Aloe Vera
        </a>
        plants. All of these are very useful in making potions and poisons. The upgrades for this "house" are all fixed prices; haggling is not necessary. The cove also comes with pirates who you can direct to go out and "plunder"; they will give you a share of the profit after they return.
       </p>

       <p>
        This is not a house in the traditional sense; it is
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Frostcrag_Spire_(place)" title="Oblivion:Frostcrag Spire (place)">
         Frostcrag Spire
        </a>
        from the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Wizard%27s_Tower" title="Oblivion:Wizard's Tower">
         Wizard's Tower
        </a>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oblivion:Official_Plug-ins" title="Oblivion:Official Plug-ins">
         official plug-in
        </a>
        . It is particularly well-suited for mage-type characters and characters that prefer solitude, but it also for any character that does not want to join the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Mages_Guild" title="Oblivion:Mages Guild">
         Mages Guild
        </a>
        but wants access to
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Spell_Making" title="Oblivion:Spell Making">
         custom spells
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Enchanting" title="Oblivion:Enchanting">
         custom enchantments
        </a>
        . Some players may want the tower simply for the
        <a href="/wiki/File:OB-place-Cheydinhal_05.jpg" title="File:OB-place-Cheydinhal 05.jpg">
         view
        </a>
        . To use all available features of this "house", you must complete the associated
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Frostcrag_Spire_(quest)" title="Oblivion:Frostcrag Spire (quest)">
         quest
        </a>
        . Even with no upgrades, the tower contains a wide variety of plants to harvest, including those only available in
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Planes_of_Oblivion" title="Oblivion:Planes of Oblivion">
         Oblivion
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Paradise" title="Oblivion:Paradise">
         Mankar Camoran's Paradise
        </a>
        .
       </p>

       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Priory_of_the_Nine">
         Priory of the Nine
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oblivion:Houses&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Priory of the Nine">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <div class="thumb tright">
        <div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;">
         <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="thumbimage" height="135" src="/w/images/thumb/8/88/OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg/180px-OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg" srcset="/w/images/thumb/8/88/OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg/270px-OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg 1.5x, /w/images/thumb/8/88/OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg/360px-OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg 2x" width="180"/>
         </a>
         <div class="thumbcaption">
          <div class="magnify">
           <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg" title="Enlarge">
            <img alt="" height="11" src="/w/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          The Priory of the Nine Restored
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        Once you have completed the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Knights_of_the_Nine" title="Oblivion:Knights of the Nine">
         Knights of the Nine
        </a>
        quest line, you can safely use the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Priory_of_the_Nine_(place)" title="Oblivion:Priory of the Nine (place)">
         Priory of the Nine
        </a>
        as a house. There are multiple available beds to sleep in and all the chests and containers in the Priory are non-respawning. However, do
        <b>
         not
        </b>
        use any of the second floor chests other than in the bedroom before completing the associated quest line: as the Priory is being cleaned up by the other knights, several containers vanish completely. After the Priory is restored, it is safe to store items in any container for the remainder of the game. Beware though; the other knights may take books to read and ingredients to eat, especially if you drop them on the floor. You can also use the crates outside for safe storage.
       </p>

       <h2>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Free_.22Houses.22">
         Free "Houses"
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oblivion:Houses&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title='Edit section: Free "Houses"'>
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         Note:
        </b>
        Free "Houses" must provide the player with a place to sleep and all containers in the sleeping quarters must be
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Containers#Safe_Containers" title="Oblivion:Containers">
         safe (non-respawning)
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h4>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Room_in_the_Aleswell_Inn">
         Room in the
         <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Aleswell_Inn" title="Oblivion:Aleswell Inn">
          Aleswell Inn
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oblivion:Houses&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Room in the Aleswell Inn">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h4>
       <div class="thumb tright">
        <div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;">
         <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:OB-interior-Aleswell_Inn.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="thumbimage" height="135" src="/w/images/thumb/5/50/OB-interior-Aleswell_Inn.jpg/180px-OB-interior-Aleswell_Inn.jpg" srcset="/w/images/thumb/5/50/OB-interior-Aleswell_Inn.jpg/270px-OB-interior-Aleswell_Inn.jpg 1.5x, /w/images/thumb/5/50/OB-interior-Aleswell_Inn.jpg/360px-OB-interior-Aleswell_Inn.jpg 2x" width="180"/>
         </a>
         <div class="thumbcaption">
          <div class="magnify">
           <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:OB-interior-Aleswell_Inn.jpg" title="Enlarge">
            <img alt="" height="11" src="/w/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          The player's room at the Aleswell Inn
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        After you have completed
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Zero_Visibility" title="Oblivion:Zero Visibility">
         Zero Visibility
        </a>
        , you will get a free room at the Aleswell Inn as a reward. The drawer within that room can be safely used for storage (but the other containers in the building cannot). There is also a small bedroll in the room that you can sleep on. One thing you should keep in mind however is that some of the inn's regulars enjoy hanging out in your room, thus often making it tricky for you to enter or exit.
       </p>
       <h5>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">
         Notes
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>

          edit

          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h5>
       <ul>
        <li>
         The Aleswell residents may take stolen property out of your drawers.
        </li>
       </ul>

       </div>
       <h4>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="White_Stallion_Lodge">
         <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:White_Stallion_Lodge" title="Oblivion:White Stallion Lodge">

        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       <div class="thumb tright">
        <div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;">

          <img alt="" class="thumbimage" height="135" src="/w/images/thumb/e/e0/OB-place-White_Stallion_Lodge.jpg/180px-OB-place-White_Stallion_Lodge.jpg" srcset="/w/images/thumb/e/e0/OB-place-White_Stallion_Lodge.jpg/270px-OB-place-White_Stallion_Lodge.jpg 1.5x, /w/images/thumb/e/e0/OB-place-White_Stallion_Lodge.jpg/360px-OB-place-White_Stallion_Lodge.jpg 2x" width="180"/>

          </div>
          White Stallion Lodge
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        If you travel to
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Leyawiin" title="Oblivion:Leyawiin">
         Leyawiin
        </a>
        you will hear rumors about
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Mazoga_the_Orc_(person)" title="Oblivion:Mazoga the Orc (person)">
         Mazoga
        </a>
        . Her questline can be started by speaking to
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Count_Marius_Caro" title="Oblivion:Count Marius Caro">
         Count Marius Caro
        </a>
        at
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Leyawiin#Castle_Leyawiin" title="Oblivion:Leyawiin">
         Leyawiin Castle County Hall
        </a>
        . One reward for completing the second of the two quests is the key to the White Stallion Lodge. You can sleep in the lodge and all the containers in the building can safely be used for storage. Your friendly Orcish roommate will only eat the food, and will often leave to hunt Black Bow Bandits. You can follow her to
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Rockmilk_Cave" title="Oblivion:Rockmilk Cave">
         Rockmilk Cave
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Undertow_Cavern" title="Oblivion:Undertow Cavern">
         Undertow Cavern
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Telepe" title="Oblivion:Telepe">
         Telepe
        </a>
        to fight together.
       </p>

        The
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Thieves_Guild" title="Oblivion:Thieves Guild">
         Thieves Guild
        </a>
        Guildmaster's Quarters contain a bed and multiple containers, all of which are safe for storage. These quarters are only accessible after you have pulled off
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:The_Ultimate_Heist" title="Oblivion:The Ultimate Heist">
         The Ultimate Heist
        </a>
        at the end of the Thieves Guild quest line. At that point, this room becomes accessible via Dareloth's basement in the
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Waterfront_District" title="Oblivion:Waterfront District">
         Imperial City Waterfront District
        </a>
        .
