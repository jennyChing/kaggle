      <div id="article-header">
       <h1 itemprop="headline">
        Man jailed for drugs, but tests show substance was vitamins
       </h1>
       <div id="byline">
        <a class="primary_container " href="http://minnesota.publicradio.org/collections/issues/" id="primary_container">
         Issues
        </a>
        The Associated Press

								

														
        <span class="dateline" itemprop="dateline">
         Mankato, Minn.
        </span>
        
        <time datetime="2015-02-16" itemprop="datePublished" pubdate="">
         Feb 16, 2015
        </time>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div data-canonical="http://www.mprnews.org/story/2015/02/16/charges-dismissed-against-man-jailed-for-drug-possession-tests-show-it-was-vitamins" id="article-share">
       <a class="share twitter" data-tweeter="mprnews" href="javascript:void(0);" id="share-track-tw" title="Share via Twitter">
       </a>
       <a class="share facebook" href="javascript:void(0);" id="share-track-fb" title="Share via Facebook">

      <div class="share-align" id="article-body" itemprop="articleBody">
       <p>
        A Mankato man was jailed for months while he waited for the state crime lab to process suspected drugs that turned out to be vitamins.
       </p>
       <p>
        Joseph Burrell was arrested in November and charged with two felony counts of drug possession. His bail was set at $250,000. The 31-year-old Burrell says he's not happy it took so long for the Bureau of Criminal Apprehension to process the evidence.
       </p>
       <p>
        Blue Earth County assistant attorney Chris Rovney says a field test was positive for amphetamine. A more accurate lab test done by BCA showed the bag of powder found in Burrell's car didn't contain anything illegal.
       </p>
       <div class="adaptTarget adaptBaseAndMedium half" data-adapt="mpr-ad-1">
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://bit.ly/17gVbK9">
         The Free Press says
        </a>
        charges were dismissed just as Burrell was about to go to trial early this month. He had just completed a stay at a drug treatment center when he was stopped.
       </p>
