             </h3>

               Bollywood dance numbers nearly redeem a bone-brained action thriller
              </em>
             </p>

           <h2 class="main_article_deck">
            India's Fox Star Studios produces an official remake of Tom Cruise and Cameron Diaz's 'Knight and Day'
           </h2>
           <div class="article_body">
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span>
               Hrithik
              </span>
              Roshan
             </strong>
             <span>
              is believable as a brilliant international jewel thief  but a slack
              <strong>
               Katrina
              </strong>
             </span>
             <strong>
              Kaif
             </strong>
             <span>
              <strong>
              </strong>
              lends little to the proceedings  in
              <em>
               Bang Bang!
              </em>
              , the official Fox Bollywood remake of its 2010
              <strong>
               Tom Cruise
              </strong>
              and
              <strong>
               Cameron Diaz
              </strong>
              film
              <em>
               Knight and Day
              </em>
              .
             </span>
            </p>
            <div class="recommended_articles">
             <div id="grv-personalization-24">

             <strong>
              Nair
             </strong>
             <span>
              , working from
              <strong>
               Patrick ONeill
              </strong>
              s original story, put a global Indian spin on the action movie.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span>
               Read more
              </span>
             </strong>
             <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/imax-screen-bollywood-remake-bang-737271">
              Imax to Screen Bollywood Remake 'Bang Bang!' in India
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Opening the film in a record 245 theaters in North America and on Imax screens across India, Fox Star Studios has high expectations; the violent Bollywood actioner is up against the more prestigious
             <em>
              Haider
             </em>
             and is bound to dominate a holiday weekend traditionally kicked off by non-violent Mahatma Gandhis Oct. 2 birthday.
            </p>
            <p>
             Roshan has a multitude of charms, including fierce dancing talent, bright green eyes and a killer physique, but try as he might, he doesnt match Cruises energy in the original. Here, hes on the run against some flamboyant baddies, including Hamid Gul (
             <strong>
              Jaaved Jaffrey
             </strong>
             ), a ludicrously tattooed criminal, and his even more lethal boss, Omar (
             <strong>
              Danny Denzongpa
             </strong>
             ). Seems the fabled Kohinoor diamond has gone missing from the Tower of London, and a web of corrupt Indian officials and villains will do anything to recover it.
            </p>
            <p>
             Even the sillier action films out there attempt to follow some kind of logic, but this one has sequences that begin in a Prague casino, move to a
             <span>
              Phuket
             </span>
             villa and back to Prague in the same scene. Blaring music,
             <span>
              slow-mo
             </span>
             fist fights and endless car chases go on and on  and wasnt it just last week that
             <strong>
              Denzel Washington
             </strong>
             walked away from the exact same explosion?
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              View more
             </strong>
             <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/video-fox-releases-knight-day-720563" target="_blank">
              Fox Releases 'Knight and Day' Bollywood Remake Teaser
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Roshan is supposedly the first Bollywood actor to use a Flyboard water jetpack in an action sequence, evading bad guys in an ocean shootout. In another scene, he expertly maneuvers an Formula One race car through the streets of Abu Dhabi.
            </p>
            <p>
             Kaif spins around on Roshans motorcycle to shoot at pursuers, and Roshan sprints between two approaching trains, in some of the scenes designed to be exact copies of
             <em>
              Bang Bang!
             </em>
             s Hollywood counterpart. But the director has explained the film is not meant to be identical, especially considering that he has to fill almost an hour of extra running time.
            </p>
            <p>
             What do viewers get for that additional investment of time? More flipped-over cars, more speeches from its hammy villains, and more bickering between the two leads. If it wasnt for Roshans awe-inspiring dance performances (be sure to hang on until the title track at the end), one could easily write off the film altogether.
            </p>
            <p>
             By the end of
             <em>
              Bang Bang!
             </em>
             , one is left to contemplate the idea that there is little point in circling the world at the risk of great physical danger  simply for the sake of somebody elses shiny rock.
            </p>
