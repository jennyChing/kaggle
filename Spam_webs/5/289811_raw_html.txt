     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">
       Hinduism
      </a>
      is explained through what
      <a href="/wiki/Swami_Vivekananda" title="Swami Vivekananda">
       Swami Vivekananda
      </a>
      described as "the accumulated treasury of spiritual laws discovered by different persons in different times."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindu_scriptures" title="Hindu scriptures">
       scriptures
      </a>
      were transmitted orally, in verse form to aid memorization, for many centuries before they were written down.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISBN_0-911206-15-9-E5-V1-3_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ISBN_0-911206-15-9-E5-V1-3-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      While many of these texts are in
      <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">
       Sanskrit
      </a>
      , several others have been composed in, or translated into other
      <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_India" title="Languages of India">
       Indian languages
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="N">
       N
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Hindu_scriptures&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: N">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Nalayira Divya Prabandham (
      <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">
       Tamil
      </a>
      :   ) is a collection of 4,000 Tamil verses (Naalayira in Tamil means 'four thousand') composed before 8th century AD,[1] by the 12 Alvars, and was compiled in its present form by Nathamuni during the 9th 10th centuries. The work is the beginning of the canonization of the 12 Vaishnava poet saints, and these hymns are still sung extensively even today. The works were lost before they were collected and organized in the form of an anthology by Nathamuni.
     </p>
