                                  <h1 class="post-title text-center">
                                   Vallejo City Attorney Sends Out Cease and Desist Letters to Medical Marijuana Dispensaries
                                  </h1>
                                  <div class="post-meta text-center">
                                   <ul class="list-inline">
                                    <li class="post-meta-author">
                                     by
                                     <a href="http://medicalmarijuana411.com/author/daily-dose/">
                                      Daily Dose
                                     </a>
                                    </li>
                                    <li class="post-meta-date">
                                     March 18, 2015
                                    </li>
                                    <li class="post-meta-category">
                                     in

                                  <div class="entry">
                                   <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="13131248" data-link="http://medicalmarijuana411.com/vallejo-city-attorney-sends-out-cease-and-desist-letters-to-medical-marijuana-dispensaries/" data-summary="" data-title="Vallejo City Attorney Sends Out Cease and Desist Letters to Medical Marijuana Dispensaries">
                                   </div>
                                   <p>
                                    Vallejo, California  City Attorney Claudia Quintana confirmed Monday night that her office has sent cease and desist letters out to several of Vallejos medical marijuana dispensaries.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    Quintana said that 44 letters have been sent to managers, landlords and owners in regard to 23 MMDs operating within the city of Vallejo.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    She also stated that the city will continue to send out letters as additional MMDs are identified.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    According to the letter, the operation of a MMD in Vallejo is considered a nuisance by city law, which the city has the authority to abate.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The city demands that you cease and desist the medical marijuana operation (at the location of the MMD), the letter warns. The city will hold all responsible parties, including the operator, the owner and managers liable for conducting and allowing the existence of this nuisance.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    If legal action is initiated due to your disregard of this request, the city will petition the court for an award of the citys attorneys fees and police investigation costs, the letter states.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    In 2011, city voters approved Measure C, which imposes a business license tax rate of 10 percent on the sales of medical marijuana products within the city.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The city council in January voted to shut down all MMDs in the city  even if they were paying the Measure C tax  and to stop collecting the tax altogether.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The council also voted to have city staff bring back a regulatory scheme to allow a limited number of MMDs in the city.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    While the city has, in recent months, refrained from enforcing this prohibition against existing MMDs, the citys policy direction has changed and enforcement is now imminent, the letter also states.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    Last Tuesday the city council unanimously approved a resolution of intention to amend the citys budget to add two positions and salaries to enforce against MMDs.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    A resolution of intention serves as a first reading  not an actual policy change  at the time of adoption.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    According to city staff, a deputy city attorney and legal secretary are required to carry out enforcement of shutting down all MMDs inside city limits.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The budget impact for the 2014-2015 fiscal year would be around $66,00, if a hire date of April 1 is approved. According to the same staff report, $30,000 would be given to the police department for enforcement activity between approval by the council and June 30.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The citys fiscal year begins July 1 and ends the following June 30.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    City staff estimates that the enforcement project will be in place for two years, with the deputy city attorney position costing the city more than $150,000 each year, while the legal secretary position will require $110,000 per year.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    Vallejo Police Capt. Jim OConnell said Monday that any actions that the police might take are highly dependent on the outcome of the next city council meeting on March 24.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The council is expected to decide on whether to fund the positions.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    Our position is to support the city and the citys attorneys office, OConnell added.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The City of Vallejo is also allowing local residents to weigh in on the medical marijuana dispensary issue by releasing the proposed ordinance via the Open City Hall forum on the citys web page.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    Vallejo residents have until 10 a.m. on Thursday to voice concerns, comments, and ask questions about the ordinance which, if approved, would allow two MMDs in the city through discretionary regulatory permits.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    According to city staff, the recommended approach is not to amend local zoning but allow two MMDs to be granted limited immunity from local civil prosecution, if they comply with specific requirements in the regulatory ordinance, while generating $400,000 to $700,000 of revenue in taxes and fees.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    Staff is recommending that the police department administer the permit, instead of the planning department, since the police have much more experience interpreting complex civil and criminal laws.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The two MMDs would be required to be at least 600 feet from any school, park, recovery center or other marijuana business, be prohibited from dispensing to non-members, manufacturing of concentrated cannabis and drinking or other controlled substances would be prohibited on-site.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    The MMDs could only be open from 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. and are subject to police department inspection, at any time, with no minors, consumption of marijuana or alcohol on-site.
                                   </p>
                                   <p>
                                    According to staff, cultivation would be prohibited, as well.
                                   </p>
