             <h2 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
              <a href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/modern-film-editing-tips-from-the-pros/" rel="bookmark" title="Modern Film Editing Tips From The Pros">
               Modern Film Editing Tips From The Pros
              </a>
             </h2>
             <div class="entry-meta entry-meta-above">
              By
              <span class="entry-author vcard" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
               <a class="author-link fn nickname url" href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/author/filmeditingpro/" itemprop="url" title="View all posts by Film Editing Pro">
                <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
                 Film Editing Pro
                </span>
               </a>
              </span>
              
              <a class="entry-comments" href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/modern-film-editing-tips-from-the-pros/#comments" title="Modern Film Editing Tips From The Pros  Comments">

            <a class="post-thumbnail post-thumbnail-above-content" href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/modern-film-editing-tips-from-the-pros/">
             <img alt="Modern Film Editing Tips From The Pros" height="213.5" itemprop="image" src="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/A18779-610x213.jpg" width="610"/>
            </a>
            <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
             <p>
              The amount of video being produced and made available online and through other channels is increasing exponentially  and a
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               <strong>
                lot
               </strong>
              </span>
              of it is terrible.
             </p>
             <p>
              For those of us who take pride in putting out quality content and film, it is becoming increasingly crucial to separate yourself from the mountains of slop out there and cut through all the noise to be noticed.
             </p>
             <p>
              Threeways to easily make your work stand out from the crowd are with:
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Quality cinematography
              </li>
              <li>
               Engaging storytelling
              </li>
              <li>
               Skillful editing
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              Well-known film editor Walter Murch has some poignant thoughts on the topic.
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               Easier access [to filmmaking &amp; editing tools] does not automatically make for better results. The accompanying sense that anyone can do it can easily produce a broth spoiled by too may cooks. All of us today are able to walk into an art store and buy inexpensive pigments and supplies that the Renaissance painters would have paid fortunes for. And yet, do any of us paint on their level today?
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              Check out this short video from Mr. Murch. Its only about 7 minutes longand contains some really useful nuggets of wisdom that can help us all improve the quality of our work.
             </p>
             <p>
              Enjoy!
             </p>

             </p>

             <h2 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
              <a href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/the-indian-film-industry-past-and-present-and-how-to-get-a-job-in-it/" rel="bookmark" title="The Indian Film Industry Past and Present (And How to Get a Job in It!)">
               The Indian Film Industry Past and Present (And How to Get a Job in It!)
              </a>
             </h2>
             <div class="entry-meta entry-meta-above">
              By
              <span class="entry-author vcard" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
               <a class="author-link fn nickname url" href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/author/filmeditingpro/" itemprop="url" title="View all posts by Film Editing Pro">
                <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
                 Film Editing Pro
                </span>
               </a>
              </span>
              
              <a class="entry-comments" href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/the-indian-film-industry-past-and-present-and-how-to-get-a-job-in-it/#comments" title="The Indian Film Industry Past and Present (And How to Get a Job in It!)  Comments">
               0 Comments
              </a>
             </div>
             <!-- .entry-meta -->
            </header>
            <a class="post-thumbnail post-thumbnail-above-content" href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/the-indian-film-industry-past-and-present-and-how-to-get-a-job-in-it/">
             <img alt="The Indian Film Industry Past and Present (And How to Get a Job in It!)" height="213.5" itemprop="image" src="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IndianFilmIndustry_Feature-610x213.jpg" width="610"/>
            </a>
            <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
             <p>
              Today were going to take a look at the Indian film industry  a large and expanding industry producing an enormous variety of high-quality movies. In this multi-part article, well take a look at the following topics:
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               <a href="#history">
                The history of the Indian film industry
               </a>
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="#regions">
                The different Indian film regions
               </a>
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="#filmmaker">
                How to become a filmmaker in India
               </a>
              </li>
              <li>
               <a href="#editor">
                How to become a film editor in India
               </a>
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              Feel free to jump to any section that is of particular interest to you!
              <br/>
              <a id="history">
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <b>
               History of Movie Making in India
              </b>
             </h2>
             <p>
              It is popularly believed that the very first full-length Indian motion picture was a silent film by the legendary filmmaker Dadasaheb Phalke, called Raja Harishchandra, in the year 1913. That is why, last year, the centenary celebrations of the Indian film industry were held.
             </p>
             <p>
              However, recent research shows that this history goes even further back  to 1899, when a little known photographer called H.S.Bhatavdekar made short film on a wrestling match in Bombay. Opinions differ on who was the first Indian filmmaker  was it Phalke or Bhatavdekar ?
             </p>
             <p>
              But regardless, the story of Indian film making has been one of relentless success.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <b>
               Stunning and Glorious Success
              </b>
             </h3>
             <p>
              It is estimated that the Indian film industry produces as many as 1,250 films each year, made in 27 different languages. This is thrice the number produced by Hollywood  albeit nowhere near the global reach, soft power and incredible financial clout of Hollywood. Bollywood still has a long way to go in its quest to be the entertainment capital of the world, but is making rapid strides in that direction, nonetheless.
             </p>
             <p>
              Bollywood films are highly popular worldwide, and are shown in multiplexes in almost every major city in the world  producing a total estimated gate receipts of just over $2 billion, which is estimated to grow to $3 billion by 2016.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <b>
               The Early Years
              </b>
             </h3>
             <p>
              After Dadasaheb Phalkes breakthrough film, many more Indians took to film making, producing movies that mirrored the sensibilities of a people still under the rule of the British empire. The films were mostly based on Hindu mythological stories, and tickets were very reasonably priced  less than a cent in todays terms. This led to a rapid growth in popularity of full-length motion pictures as a popular medium for entertainment.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <b>
               The Bollywood Phenomenon
              </b>
             </h3>
             <p>
              Bollywood, as we understand it today, began only after Indias independence in 1947, with the migration of several hundred thousand Hindu Punjabis from the newly created nation of Pakistan, into Bombay. The early Punjabis, who literally built Bollywood, were penniless, but full of great dreams.
             </p>
             <p>
              Filmmakers like Prithviraj Kapoor, and his more famous son Raj Kapoor, Guru Dutt  the tragic filmmaker behind critically acclaimed commercial flops such as Pyaasa and Kaagaz Ke Phool, Mehboob Khan, who directed the greatest Indian film ever made, Mother India  laid the foundation of the Hindi film industry and set it up for the stunning success that followed.
             </p>
             <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1061" style="width: 536px;">
              <img alt="Mother India" class="size-full wp-image-1061" data-lazy-src="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mother_India.jpg" height="357" src="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="536"/>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="Mother India" class="size-full wp-image-1061" height="357" src="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Mother_India.jpg" width="536"/>
              </noscript>
              <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
               A scene from Mother India (1957) directed by Mehboob Khan
              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <h3>
              <b>
               Star Power
              </b>
             </h3>
             <p>
              Bollywood is nothing without its stars  who are hero-worshiped all over the country  and men like Dev Anand, Dilip Kumar, Raj Kapoor were the original Bollywood superstars, followed by Rajesh Khanna and the world famous Amitabh Bachchan. Last 20 years have seen the rise of the phenomenal trio of Khans  Shah Rukh, Salman and Aamir, who have defined Bollywood as we know it today.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <b>
               The Multiplex Factor
              </b>
             </h3>
             <p>
              The biggest thing to have happened to Bollywood recently, has been the multiplex. Symbols of Indias rising economic power, several hundred multiplexes have sprung up all over the country, offering a safe and comfortable option for watching movies for the entire family.
             </p>
             <p>
              With tickets priced 10 times higher than those offered by the traditional single screen theaters, multiplexes have resulted in a tremendous spurt in revenues for Indias film industry  making the business of movie making consistently profitable, even in times of national economic slowdowns.
             </p>
             <p>
              As they say in India, a poor filmmaker is a rare filmmaker!
              <br/>
              <a id="regions">
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <b style="line-height: 16.799999237060547px;">
               Diversity of the Indian Film Industry (Its More Than Just Bollywood)
              </b>
             </h2>
             <p>
              Contrary to public perception, Hindi is not the only language spoken in India, and Bollywood is not Indias only film industry. As a matter of fact, last year saw 1,250 movies made in India, and a vast majority of them had nothing whatsoever to do with Bollywood!
             </p>
             <h3>
              <b>
               Indias Regional Film Industries
              </b>
             </h3>
             <p>
              India is a huge multilingual country, with as many as 27 popular languages and dialects, and with each major language having a state or a province of its own. With a majority of Indians being absolutely crazy about movies, it is only to be expected for each region in India to have its own film industry. We discuss the four most popular regional film industries in India  that are so not Bollywood, thank you very much!
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               1.Tamil Film Industry
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              Based in the city of Chennai, featuring films made in the Tamil language, the Tamil film industry enjoys great clout in Indian cinema. The very first full-length Tamil film was a silent movie,
              <i>
               Keechaka Vadham
              </i>
              , made in 1916. The first talkie made in Tamil was the phenomenally successful
              <i>
               Kalidas
              </i>
              (1931).
             </p>
             <p>
              Since then, buoyed by a film crazy public and a huge Tamil diaspora, spread all over the world, Tamil cinema has gone from strength to strength. No write-up on Tamil cinema is complete without a reference to the legendary superstar of the Tamil film industry, the incredible Rajnikanth. Rajnikanth is a larger than life figure, and almost a demigod to tens of millions of Tamilians all over the world. His movies transcend cinema and are cultural and national events.
             </p>
             <p>
              Most Rajnikanth movies are over-the-top, flashy, fun and really make no sense whatsoever!!
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               2.Telugu Film Industry
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              In terms of revenues generated, and the sheer number of movies made, the Telugu film industry is the second biggest in India after Bollywood. The state of Andhra Pradesh, where Telugu movies are made, is known for its craze for cinema, and has the most cinema halls in India, 2809 out of a total Indian strength of 10,167.
             </p>
             <p>
              The most popular star of Telugu cinema has been the late N.T.Rama Rao, so popular that he beat Indias grand old political party, the Congress, to set up the government in Andhra Pradesh, by a huge popular mandate. Recent popular stars in Telugu cinema include Chiranjeevi, Nagarjuna and the young Ravi Teja.
             </p>
             <p>
              The Ramoji Film City, located in Hyderabad, the capital of Andhra Pradesh, is said to be the largest film production facility in the world.
             </p>
             <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1057" style="width: 536px;">
              <img alt="Ramoji Film City" class="size-full wp-image-1057" data-lazy-src="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Ramoji_Film_City.jpg" height="357" src="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="536"/>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="Ramoji Film City" class="size-full wp-image-1057" height="357" src="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Ramoji_Film_City.jpg" width="536"/>
              </noscript>
              <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
               A movie set in Ramoji Film City
              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <p>
              <strong>
               3.Kannada Film Industry
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              Based in Bangalore and catering to people speaking the Kannada language, the Kannada film industry, while not as big as its Tamil and Telugu counterparts, is still very popular. This industry boasts of some of the most critically acclaimed filmmakers in India such as Girish Kasaravalli, P. Sheshadri, Girish Karnad and Puttanna Kanagal.
             </p>
             <p>
              The greatest star of Kannada cinema has been the late Rajkumar, who lorded over this industry for several decades before his death.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               4.Bengali Film Industry
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              The Bengali film industry, based in the city of Kolkata (earlier known as Calcutta), is much more famous for its past than for its present. The present state of this industry has been quite terrible, with the quality of movies made rather horrible. This is sad because in the past, Bengali cinema gave us one of the greatest filmmakers in the world  Satyajit Ray, who made movies that won great critical acclaim all over the world such as
              <i>
               Pather Panchali
              </i>
              (1955),
              <i>
               Aparojito
              </i>
              (1956) and
              <i>
               Charulatha
              </i>
              (1964).
             </p>
             <p>
              Ray was presented with an honorary Oscar by the Academy of Motion Pictures and Sciences, for outstanding lifetime achievement in film making, just before his death in 1992.
              <br/>
              <a id="filmmaker">
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <b style="line-height: 16.799999237060547px;">
               How To Be A Filmmaker In India?
              </b>
             </h2>
             <p>
              The film industry in India has a reputation for widespread nepotism, where the easiest way to get a break as a filmmaker is to be a family member or a friend of some powerful producer or popular actor. So if you are a young person in India, with dreams of becoming a top filmmaker someday, and have no family connections in the industry or any real money to speak of  should you be disheartened, and give up all hope?
             </p>
             <p>
              Our answer is an unequivocal NO. There are many just like you who have made it big in the Indian film industry  powered by nothing but their talent, determination, hard work and of course, a bit of luck.
             </p>
             <p>
              We have prepared a short guideline that might help you get your first break in the Indian film industry, and then, perhaps go on to be the top filmmaker that you have always dreamed of becoming. Read on.
              <a class="more-link" href="https://www.filmeditingpro.com/the-indian-film-industry-past-and-present-and-how-to-get-a-job-in-it/">
               Read more 
              </a>
             </p>
