                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-37Pd9_FTLUw/U4T_ySkuZiI/AAAAAAAAXiU/m_SkbRLI4dc/s1600/egg+salad+with+avacado.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="avocado spinach lentil and egg salad recipe" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-37Pd9_FTLUw/U4T_ySkuZiI/AAAAAAAAXiU/m_SkbRLI4dc/s1600/egg+salad+with+avacado.jpg" title=""/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      On my work days it usually gets to around 2pm and I think 'oh gosh! I've not eaten and I'm starving!' having a child makes you appreciate any alone time &amp; the importance of getting as much done as possible. I'm usually so in 'the zone' that I loose track of time until my tummy growls at me and I think 'what can I make that's really fast and really filling?' usually my go-to is pasta pesto but it often leaves me feeling a bit rubbish (I always make too much and end up eating at least two bowls) so last work day I made it my goal to try something different.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      With limited ingredients I felt a bit stumped for inspiration but I thew these items together and was really pleasantly surprised. I'm not usually a salad eater but whenever I do have them I think 'why don't I have these more often?' same applies to soup.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      There really is no recipe to follow as such, it's just lentil, spinach leaves, avocado and boiled eggs, oh and a sprinkling of olive oil and a bit of salt too. Enjoy!
                      <br/>
