      <h1 class="entry-title">
       Combat Water Discolouration with Plumbers in Whitley Bay
      </h1>
      <div class="entry-meta">
       <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
        Posted on
       </span>
       <a href="http://www.whiteknightmaintenance.co.uk/blog/combat-water-discolouration-plumbers-whitley-bay/" rel="bookmark" title="4:57 pm">
        <span class="entry-date">
         October 14, 2014
        </span>
       </a>
       <span class="meta-sep">
        by
       </span>
       <span class="author vcard">
        <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.whiteknightmaintenance.co.uk/blog/author/whiteknightm/" title="View all posts by White Knight Maintenance">
         White Knight Maintenance
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <!-- .entry-meta -->
      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        Drinking water is normally clear but occasionally it can turn white, brown, yellow, orange or grey; as
        <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/local/tyne/hi/people_and_places/newsid_8350000/8350832.stm">
         happened in 2009
        </a>
        when a water main burst in Huene Park of Alnwick interrupted water supply and discoloured water in towns across Northumberland. Whitley Bay was not spared. Fortunately the engineers on the job were quick to respond and everything quickly returned to normal.
       </p>
       <p>
        Water discolouration is not uncommon but in most cases it is transient and not harmful. If homeowners keep the faucets open for a few minutes the clear water starts flowing again in a few minutes. Only in rare cases does one have to call
        <a href="http://www.whiteknightmaintenance.co.uk/plumbing.html">
         plumbers in Whitley Bay
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Causes of Water Discolouration
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Water can change colour for many reasons. The most common are:
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Essential maintenance work from the water company
        </li>
        <li>
         A burst in water main or pump
        </li>
        <li>
         Resumption of water supply after maintenance
        </li>
        <li>
         Chance in valve settings in supply main
        </li>
        <li>
         Usage of water by third parties such as drain cleaning services and fire fighters
        </li>
        <li>
         Installation of a new supply in neighbourhood
        </li>
        <li>
         Condition of the service pipe connecting a homeowners property to the water main
        </li>
        <li>
         Poor condition of internal plumbing
        </li>
        <li>
         Use of internal booster pumps in a highrise nearby
        </li>
        <li>
         Rust in cast iron pipes; thousands of kilometers of old water supply in England is made from cast iron
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Health Risks
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Discoloured water is not appealing to drink but this does not mean it is necessary a health threat. However it is advisable to talk to the water supply company before consuming discoloured water. It may or may not contain harmful substances.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Techniques to Fix
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        The least expensive, most convenient, and fastest technique to fix water discolouration is to phone ones water supply company. In most cases the company will apologise for not informing them beforehand  if a supplier is replacing pipes or cleaning them it is its responsibility to warn customers. This is what normally happens.
       </p>
       <p>
        In a few rare cases the cause of discolouration lies closer at home: a failing hot water tank or old pipes. In this scenario, one can either pull out his toolbox from garage or contact a plumbing solutions provider in Whitley Bay. It is a good idea to consult certified plumbers.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         How to Hire Plumbing Solutions Provider to Tackle Water Discolouration?
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Whitley Bay does not have a dearth of plumbers. Some are good and others are not so good. Therefore, it is a good idea to keep a few things in mind when hiring a plumber.
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Certifications:
         </strong>
         It is advisable to ask if the plumber in question has the required certification to undertake the job at hand.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Residential or Commercial:
         </strong>
         Residential and commercial plumbing are different beasts altogether. Commercial plumbing differs from residential plumbing in many ways. Therefore it is smart to hire plumbers with relevant experience.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Experience:
         </strong>
         Novices can or cannot be good. It is safer to hire a plumbing solutions service whose professionals have at least four years of experience.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        To conclude, water can change colour occasionally. Every instance of water discolouration should not be a cause for panic because the problem is often from the side of the water supply company and short lived. In some cases plumbing at home or office is responsible and requires expert and professional assistance.
       </p>
      </div>
