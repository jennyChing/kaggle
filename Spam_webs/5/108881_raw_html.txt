         </h2>
         <header class="details clearfix">

         <div class="single-content">
          <p>
           For those of you know personally know me know I have a semi-secret love of tattoos  and now the rest of you know too! I have
           <a href="http://photos-h.ak.fbcdn.net/hphotos-ak-snc1/hs111.snc1/4652_509416380475_214401980_30773038_4387437_n.jpg">
            one in solid turquoise
           </a>
           , and am always up in a debate of if i will get another and what it will be. I have 2 small ones in mind that I might get someday.. so stay tuned. But no really it will be a few years.. but you can still stay tuned :)  I am very opinionated when it comes to tattoos. This is my problem: I really do not like tattoos that are outlined in black it seem like it is a coloring book image or you are in the 1st grade and need to have thick black lines to help you know where to color. Now I am not against shading and contrast dont get me wrong.. I just dont want to pay lots of money for a tattoo that looks like a kid outlined it in sharpie first.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           So in saying all that, you can realize how happy I was to find Amanda Wachob on
           <a href="http://elseachelsea.typepad.com/frolic/2010/01/amanda-wachob.html">
            {frolic}
           </a>
           today! Amanda Wachob has been tattooing since 1998 and currently works in NYC. She pushes the boundaries of the tattoo medium to blur the line between fine art and body art.
