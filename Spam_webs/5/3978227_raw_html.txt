        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Vintage National Geographic
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-16T15:04:06+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          Monday 16 March, 2015
         </time>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <img alt="Vintage National Geographic" class="attachment-blog-featured wp-post-image" height="1431" src="http://www.missmoss.co.za/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/vintage-nat-geo-01.jpg" width="1080"/>
        <p>
         not to be confused with National Geographics
         <a href="http://natgeofound.tumblr.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          own tumblr
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://vintagenatgeographic.tumblr.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Vintage National Geographic
         </a>
         insteadfeatures scanned in pages from vintage copies of NationalGeographic going back to the 1800s. youll recognise that familiarhalftoneprinting look that jumps off these old clippings, and its almost like you can smell the pile of old magazines that you used to page through at grandmas house.
         <br/>
         <span id="more-37953">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         i have a stack of these old mags too, i wish there was some way to preserve them for all the internet to see  much like the online
         <a href="http://books.google.com/books/about/LIFE.html?id=R1cEAAAAMBAJ&amp;redir_esc=y" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Life archives
         </a>
         ? that would be cool. they even have the
         <a href="http://vintagenatgeographic.tumblr.com/post/39897747481/very-first-page-of-the-first-issue-of-national" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          first page
         </a>
         of the first issue of National Geographic dating back to 1888.
        </p>
        <p>
