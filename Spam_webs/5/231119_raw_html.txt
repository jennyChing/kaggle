         <h1>
          Are Digital Cameras Becoming Obsolete?
         </h1>
         <ul class="in">
          <li class="auth">
           by
           <a href="http://www.inspirefirst.com/author/mikearnill/" rel="author" title="Posts by mikearnill">
            mikearnill
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Oct 22nd, 2013
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.inspirefirst.com/category/gadgets/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Gadgets">
            Gadgets
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div class="icont">
          <div class="soclink">
           <a href="http://www.facebook.com/sharer/sharer.php?u=http://www.inspirefirst.com/2013/10/22/digital-cameras-obsolete/" rel="nofollow external" style="margin-right:20px" target="_blank" title="Share this article on Facebook">
            <img alt="" height="20" src="http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/themes/inspirefirst/images/sb.gif" width="56"/>
           </a>
           <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="horizontal" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.inspirefirst.com/2013/10/22/digital-cameras-obsolete/&amp;media=http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/digital-cameras.jpg&amp;description=Are Digital Cameras Becoming Obsolete?" rel="nofollow external">
            <img border="0" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
           </a>
           <a class="butsplit" href="https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?original_referer=http://www.inspirefirst.com&amp;source=tweetbutton&amp;text=Are Digital Cameras Becoming Obsolete?&amp;url=http://www.inspirefirst.com/2013/10/22/digital-cameras-obsolete/&amp;via=inspirefirst" rel="nofollow external">
            <img alt="" height="20" src="http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/themes/inspirefirst/images/tweet-button.png" width="59"/>
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.stumbleupon.com/submit?url=http://www.inspirefirst.com/2013/10/22/digital-cameras-obsolete/" rel="nofollow external">
            <img alt="" height="18" src="http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/themes/inspirefirst/images/stumble-button.png" width="74"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <div id="wix">
          </div>
          <div itemprop="description">
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            For well over two centuries, people have used a device to capture moments on film. As technology progressed, the wait time for pictures reduced, and the image quality has gotten better. Traditional film has given way to digital cameras, but in recent years, more people have used smartphones to replace their digital cameras. Are digital cameras becoming obsolete?
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Size
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img height="202" src="http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/digital-cameras-300x202.jpg" width="300"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="none" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.inspirefirst.com/2013/10/22/digital-cameras-obsolete/&amp;media=http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/digital-cameras-300x202.jpg&amp;description=Are Digital Cameras Becoming Obsolete?" rel="nofollow external">
             <img class="pinimg" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            Digital cameras are big and bulky. Theyre a burden to carry around and keep track of. Smartphones are much more convenient. Theyre small and compact, designed to fit in your pocket. If youre going to go on a road trip and need a device to take cameras with, theres really no competition.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            Smartphones are almost always on you, as well. The bulky size of a camera tends to mean you only bring it with you when planned  great moments for photos arent always planned events. Getting that perfect picture sometimes needs a spur of the moment tool.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Battery Life
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            <img class="lazy" data-src="http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/camera-battery-life.jpg" height="215" src="http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/themes/inspirefirst/images/loader.gif" width="300"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="none" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.inspirefirst.com/2013/10/22/digital-cameras-obsolete/&amp;media=http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/camera-battery-life.jpg&amp;description=Are Digital Cameras Becoming Obsolete?" rel="nofollow external">
             <img class="pinimg" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            Most digital cameras have a short battery life. Once the camera is dead, it can take hours to recharge. The battery life is much longer in smartphones. There are several third-party accessories available to extend the battery life of your phone, allowing you to take many more pictures. While you can buy extra battery packs for cameras, you have to make sure that theyre charged before leaving. With phone accessories, you can charge on the go.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Storage
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            While most cameras support memory cards, they just cant compete with smartphones. Phones in the
            <a href="http://uk.blackberry.com/software/smartphones/blackberry-10-os.html" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="BB10">
             BB10
            </a>
            family, for instance, feature at least 16 GB of storage, expandable up to 64 GB, allowing for a total of 80 GB of storage. If you somehow manage to fill that up, since smartphones are connected to cellular networks, you can upload pictures, send them to your e-mail address, or remotely store them. Digital cameras just cant compete.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Features
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img class="lazy" data-src="http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Digital-Camera-Features-300x187.gif" height="187" src="http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/themes/inspirefirst/images/loader.gif" width="300"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="none" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.inspirefirst.com/2013/10/22/digital-cameras-obsolete/&amp;media=http://www.inspirefirst.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Digital-Camera-Features-300x187.gif&amp;description=Are Digital Cameras Becoming Obsolete?" rel="nofollow external">
             <img class="pinimg" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            This is the area where smartphones cant yet compete. With smartphones, youre offered many options, such as adding filters, captions, cropping, rotating, HDR, and Panorama, among others. Digital cameras offer many more features than smartphones can offer. With some digital cameras, you can purchase additional lenses, allowing you to take sharper pictures. You can also take pictures in many different styles such as HDR, time-lapse, tilt shift, and more. The pictures can then be uploaded to a computer for further manipulation.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Shareability
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            This is one category that digital cameras may never be able to compete with smartphones in. With smartphones, you can share images with anyone worldwide, whether in a text message, an email, or through social media outlets such as Twitter, Instagram and Facebook. With digital cameras, you can send images to digital picture frames and e-mail addresses, but only if youre on a Wi-Fi connection.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify">
            So, are digital cameras becoming obsolete? That depends on what you use your camera for. Smartphones are great for capturing quick images that can be shared with anyone at any time. Professional photographers, however, will stick with digital cameras. The plethora of features and options available on digital cameras arent rivaled by smartphones just yet, and may never be.
           </p>
