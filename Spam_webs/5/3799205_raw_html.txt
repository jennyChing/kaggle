             <h1 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
              iCloud Drive- Top 10 Reasons to Choose
             </h1>
             <p class="post-excerpt">
              <span class="swt-snippet">
               iCLoud drive is a new integration in Apple iCloud service. Apple users must know the benefits of using this service compare to previous iCloud.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="toolset">
              <a href="#" id="couchMode" title="Couch mode for comfortable reading">
               <span id="counchBtn">
               </span>
               couch mode
              </a>
              <a href="#" id="printStory" onclick="window.print()" title="Print story without the clutter">
               <span id="printBtn">
               </span>
               print story
              </a>
             </p>
             <div class="post-content">
              <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1503855684972952475" itemprop="articleBody">
               <div class="MsoNormal">
                The concept cloud storage is not new with popular services like Google Drive. Dropbox or Onedrive. Although Apple has released their own cloud storage system called iCloud Drive much later on; it certainly created a buzz among Apple users.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                The major reason behind this cherishment is to have cloud storages totally compatible with Apple Ecosystem. But, is this the only reason behind choosing iCloud Drive? Let`s explain what we can expect more from this service.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <h2>
                 Top 10 Reasons to use iCloud Drive
                </h2>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                </div>
                <ol>
                 <h3>
                  1. Easier to Explore
                 </h3>
                </ol>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 <div style="text-align: center;">
                  <div style="text-align: left;">
                   You can easily access all of your iCloud files from a browser like window. The finder like window
                   <strong>
                    iCloud Drive
                   </strong>
                   display folder, files and apps that uses it to store files within a grid view. You can notice any folder and apps easily by icon visible with it.
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   It has also include a fast and powerful search box, so youll find desired files or folders while youre typing in that box. This search box is really useful while your Drive gets fill up with tons of contents like music, video and documents. In the finder window you can also view by date, name and even tags.
                  </div>
                 </div>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="video-container">
                  <iframe height="320" src="https://docs.google.com/file/d/0B_GE4yXfEFy8YmJSbHYzTlh3aWs/preview" width="440">
                  </iframe>
                 </div>
                 <h3>
                  2. Cross platform Accessibility
                 </h3>
                 You can access iCloud Drive files from your iPhone and iPad running on iOS, Mac on OS X Yosmite or even from your windows PC. That means all files visible in your
                 <a href="http://www.iphoneups.com/2015/02/icloud-drive-for-mac-setup-and-explore.html">
                  iCloud Drive folder for Mac
                 </a>
                 or Windows are also accessible on your iOS devices.
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 Think about a movie or music you have downloaded from your iPhone and listen/watch it in your Windows PC or Mac and Vise Versa. You will see iCloud Drive option in OS X Yosmite finder sidebar.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Notice that there are some default folders you already created for the apps that support iCloud Drive such as Pages, Numbers or Keynotes. You can create new folders, move files from your PC and within Drive by just dragging them into different folders.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 Read Also:
                 <a href="http://www.iphoneups.com/2015/01/icloud-drive-apps-3-ways-to-explore.html">
                  How to access iCloud Drive from your iPhone or iPad running on iOS8 or later.
                 </a>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 Apple introduced an updated version of
                 <a href="http://support.apple.com/downloads/DL1455/en_US/icloudsetup.exe">
                  iCloud for Windows
                 </a>
                 that need to be installed in PC to access this service. After installing, simply open File Explorer (in Windows 8) or Windows Explorer (in Windows 7) and you will see a new folder (or folders) called iCloud Drive.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-DcJL3HPBaro/VNS7a9qWKeI/AAAAAAAABMo/WqSC1HvheB8/s1600/iclouddrivewindows.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img alt="iCloud Drive for Windows" border="0" height="584" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-DcJL3HPBaro/VNS7a9qWKeI/AAAAAAAABMo/WqSC1HvheB8/s1600/iclouddrivewindows.png" title="iCloud Drive for Windows" width="640"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 Any Documents, photos and other files can be stored directly on iCloud Drive by simply dragging from your PC. This files can be further accessible on your iOS or Mac device
                </div>
                <h3>
                 3.  No Boundary of Space and Device
                </h3>
                Have it ever happened to you while you forget to pick the USB drive that contains a very urgent official presentation? In that case, you can use iCloud Drive like a portable hard drive, files that used to be on your computer are now on your iPhone, iPad or Mac, making them spontaneously accessible.
                <br/>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 <br/>
                 You make a PowerPoint presentation in your Windows PC and present it in seminar from iPhone using iCloud Drive which certainly blows away the complexity (like carrying portable hard drive or USB drive) when it comes to accessibility across platforms. With browser accessibility, you can even grab that presentation file on a desert island from someones else`s computer.
                </div>
                <h3>
                 4. Protect Valuable Contents
                </h3>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 You probably keep some very important official documents, presentations and photos on your iPhone, iPad or PC which are vulnerable to theft, virus attack or hard drive crash. Your wedding photos or pictures of your child`s birthday may be stored in your PC`s hard drive. You definitely thought about losing all these information at any point of life.
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 <br/>
                 But, what happens when your files, photos and everything else are secured by some top companies of the world like Apple and let you access these contents from anywhere and anytime from online browser. The iCloud Drive is such an option which can be explore from browser from https://www.icloud.com.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 As iCloud provides a web-based interface with iCloud Drive option along with a desktop applications for Windows or OS X operating system and with the facility to easily explore and share folders with others. This web-based access eliminated the fear of losing your important files stored in physical devices.
                </div>
                <h3>
                 5. Better Integration
                </h3>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 Dropbox or Google Drive are dominating Apple devices long before iCloud Drive. So, why users should switch from previous Cloud services to the news one. The major benefit of iCloud Drive is that it is built on Apple operating systems like iOS, OS X Yosemite. Each files stored here can gain access from Apps compatible to these operative systems.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 To make it clear, we must know that old iCloud files can only be accessible from applications that are used to store them. On the contrary, iCloud drive integrates many applications including Windows compatible ones.
                </div>
                <h3>
                 6. Using Multiple Backup
                </h3>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 The iCloud Drive also makes it simple to back up your stored content. Anything you copied here get compiled in Yosemite or Windows PC hard drive as well.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 This copy option on more device certainly better the backup, so if your Mac or iPhone get stolen, you still have access to your files from your Windows backup.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 Dragging a file in iCloud Drive actually take up space of your Mac/Windows PC (all devices that have the drive enabled) as well as the drive itself (where you get 5GB free space).
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Taking up twice the space (one virtual and one physical) may seem unnecessary but it is actually helpful. Because there is potential risk of file get accidentally deleted from the web interface which can be retrived from the hard drive backup.
                </div>
                <h3>
                 7.  Access from Multiple Apps
                </h3>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 When it comes to apps accessibility, iCloud Drive is very different from the old school iCLoud where the files saved to iCloud were neither visible nor accessed directly. Only applications that created those files were able to open it.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 For example, a document saved to iCloud that was created by Pages could only be opened by Pages even if it was saved as a document format which should be able to open by other applications like MS word. But now we open these types of files directly from the cloud using any compatible application on iOS.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 However, there is no dedicated area to access iCloud Drive on iOS devices; it only appears within apps that have been updates to support it. You can open files that are compatible with the app. and upload news files to it.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 Each Compatible app has its own folder in iCloud Drive and if you space a file to your deice from within those apps, it will be added into relevant folder. You can organize these folders easily on your Mac or Windows PC.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 There are other benefits to the multiple apps accessibility. If you are working on an official document (MS Word doc) in your home PC running on Windows 7 and could not finish it on time, there is still an option to continue working if you save it in iCloud Drive that allows to finish this work in your office Mac using Pages.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Once any edit has done in the Drive, edits will sync across all devices.All these features of iCloud Drive will change the way many users engage their devices and it will kill the stigma that iPhone is not a work machine or productivity tool.
                </div>
                <h3>
                 8. Safer Storage of Content
                </h3>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 iCloud Security has been right at the forefront of the news in recent months, thanks to numerous celebrities having photographs stolen from their iCloud accounts. While users are right to be concerned.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Apple itself came forward to reassure people that no accounts were actually 'hacked'-instead the people involved had week passwords that were too easy to work out or guess. One the 'hackers' had worked out the passwords, it was just a matter of taking what they wanted.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 Of course, this could happen to anyone, which is why it`s important to stay as secure as possible in using
                 <a href="http://www.iphoneups.com/2015/02/how-to-secure-icloud-drive-to-safeguard.html">
                  iCloud Two Step Verification
                 </a>
                 can help with this-even if someone knows your password, you`ll have to confirm a login on a trusted device, before they can access your details.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 The iCloud Drive also includes dozens of critical security features specifically designed to keep data safe in encrypted transfer among multiple compatible devices.
                </div>
                <h3>
                 9. Sending Larger Attachments
                </h3>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 Apart from storing files, iCloud Drive comes with interesting features like Mail Drop. Sending email with larger files, especially photos, is frustrating because the mail server provides file size limit for attachments.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 To work around this problem, iCloud Drive can be used with your Mail apps in running on the latest version of OS X.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 After writing your mail, just select "use mail drop" to attach files. Mail will upload the file to your iCloud Drive storage instead of sending the whole message through your email service.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 If the recipient is using Mail for OS X 10, downloading the attachment will happen automatically,
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 For a different e-mail client or a previous version of OS X, the email will contain a link to download the file. Mail Drop is able to manage files and folders of documents up to 5 GB in size, and security for all files are ensure as usual with encrypted transition.
                </div>
                <h3>
                 10. Attractive Upgrades
                </h3>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 For every user iCloud Drive offers 5 GB free storage space, you can upgrade to upper storage plans according to your needs up to 1TB. This 1 TB option is useful when you require serious amount of storage like backing up loads of files like photos, videos stored in several devices.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Most of the users choose to upgrade to 200 GB of space for just $3.99 a month which is enough space to backup personal files.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 See More:
                 <a href="http://www.iphoneups.com/2015/01/icloud-drive-how-worth-it-is-compare-to.html">
                  Storage Plan of iCloud Drive compared to other cloud platforms.
                 </a>
                </div>
                <h3>
                 Conclusion
                </h3>
                Overall, iCloud Drive is a promising first step towards a complete online service Apple storage and synchronization. But, of course, it need to be improved if its intention is to challenge rivals such as Google Drive, Dropbox and OneDrive.
                <br/>
                <div>
                 <br/>
