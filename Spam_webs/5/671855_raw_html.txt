      <div class="entry-content">
       <span itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         This week had its ups and downs with the most obvious being the latter, a twin-suicide attack on the Iranian Embassy in Beirut. We brought in guests to inform us about it as well as give us a robust range in perspectives on the matter, from rapper Chyno who just released a song about suicide-bombing to Molly Crabapple, a visiting American artist. On the bright side, Adrian and Matias tested out brand new vintage mics flown in from Sweden in their Radio Beirut studio.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe frameborder="no" height="166" scrolling="no" src="https://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=https%3A//api.soundcloud.com/tracks/121775851&amp;color=ff6600&amp;auto_play=false&amp;show_artwork=true" width="100%">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         First we welcomed
         <strong>
          Habib Battah
         </strong>
         , investigative journalist and author of
         <a href="http://www.beirutreport.com/" target="_blank">
          Beirutreport.com
         </a>
         , who informed us about the Iranian embassy bombing this past week. The twin suicide-bomb that demolished the embassy killing 23 people and leaving 146 injured, is one of the first recent tragedies that has spawned an actual investigation by the country. This is an important sign to notice, as other bombings in Lebanon have been swept under the rug with no investigations or further inquiries, especially regarding the culprits.
        </p>
        <p>
         He also discussed the growing violence against journalists in Lebanon. Recently, Battah was photographing
         <a href="http://www.beirutreport.com/2013/08/beiruts-lost-roman-hippodrome-unearthed.html" target="_blank">
          ancient ruins of a Hippodrome
         </a>
         that was set to be filled in and destroyed to make way for a new building. While taking pictures on his phone Habib was assaulted and forced to delete the pictures, in hopes of keeping the general public unaware of the secret demolition of Lebanons 2 thousand-year old structure. Because of wide-spread censorship like this, investigative journalism does not have much of a presence in Lebanon.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Kameh
         </strong>
         is originally a duo headed by Hussein and Ziad, but joined in the studio with Ghiath on percussions and Hisham on oud. On the show Matias asks why they attach themselves to an American-style descriptor such as Southern but they countered him with the fact that some of the members are from
         <em>
          Southern Lebanon
         </em>
         , which they consider a very defining feature of their music especially regarding inspiration and language, #takethat. Id describe it as Bob-Dylan style vocals with beautiful Arabic features in the music due to the oud and the cajon. Guitarist Ziadsums it up as blues jazz with reggae. When asked how the situation affects their music, they replied that Mondays bomb literally affected their music because their own recording studio is located in the neighborhood of the attack.
        </p>
        <div class="viddid viddid_thumb" id="yt_v-t3EhdUCP4" onclick="myFunction('v-t3EhdUCP4')">
         <img src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/v-t3EhdUCP4/0.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Reine Farhat
         </strong>
         , the Arabic editor of
         <a href="http://www.wamda.com/" target="_blank">
          Wamda
         </a>
         told us the truth about startups in the Middle East. No one says its easy, not even in Silicon Valley, so speaking honestly starting a new company is difficult but possible  mostly in part to thanks to organizations like Wamda that have created a platform to encourage entrepreneurs, link investors with startups, and publish relevant daily news on the tech scene in the Middle East.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_17593" style="width: 210px">
         <a href="http://2famous.TV/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Chyno-Molly-pedros-1-of-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Molly Crabapple drawing Chyno at Radio Beirut." class="size-medium wp-image-17593" height="300" src="http://2famous.TV/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Chyno-Molly-pedros-1-of-1-200x300.jpg" width="200"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Molly Crabapple drawing Chyno at Radio Beirut.
         </p>
        </div>
        <strong>
         Molly Crabapple
        </strong>
        , illustrator, artist, and writer from New York City hung out to sketch performers on our show and chat with us. Previously, Molly visited Guantanamo Bay as one of the only artists given access to report on it. She reflected on her first day in Lebanon when, on a mission sketching refugees in Tripoli, she heard about the bombs in Beirut. I get there and weve already heard about the suicide bombing at the Iranian Embassy and behind me are these explosions so I shrink behind a car and everyone starts laughing because it turns out theyre fireworks. Her work from LEbanon will appear on medium.com.
        <p>
         And rapper
         <strong>
          Chyno
         </strong>
         sat with us to talk about how eerily relevant his recent single has been in light of Mondays bomb attack on the Embassy. The song O.P.P which was released at the end of October captures the inner-struggle that pushes a person to finally become a suicide bomber.
        </p>
        <div class="viddid viddid_thumb" id="yt_CYuzBzGYScU" onclick="myFunction('CYuzBzGYScU')">
         <img src="http://img.youtube.com/vi/CYuzBzGYScU/0.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         People are telling me Oh youre relevant now which doesnt exactly make me happy but it does make people reflect on the music for a second, Chyno admitted. The videos director,
         <strong>
          Pedros Temizian,
         </strong>
         also joined us to discuss how the video came to be. Chyno reveals Pedros always wanted to do a hip hop video so I said lets do this and gave him a list of songs to choose. He chose O.P.P, showcasing Temizians tendency towards darkness.
        </p>

            </p>
