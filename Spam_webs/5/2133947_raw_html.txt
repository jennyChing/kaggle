        <p>
         Sure, birds of a feather flock together, but they become even more similar over time. We'rebuilt to conform to our social groups: Back when we were evolving into humans, fitting in with a tribe was a matter of life and death, after all. Here's how you're becoming your BFFs identical twin.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. You thought SoulCycling was the actual worst, until she was like, "You've got to try SoulCycle!"
          <br/>
         </strong>
         If a friend becomes obese, it nearly triples
         <em>
          your
         </em>
         risk of becoming obese. On the flip side, if your friend (or even your friend's friend) loses weight, you're more likely to shed pounds. Weight gain and loss spreads through friend groups because it shifts each person's idea about which eating and exercise routines are normal. Dangerously, teens and young women with eating disorders are likely to adopt each other's unhealthy body image ideals.
        </p>
        <div class="ad-gpt-breaker-container">
         <div class="ad-gpt-breaker ad-article-breaker-mobile">
          <div class="ad-article-breaker-text">
           Advertisement - Continue Reading Below
          </div>
          <div class="gpt-tag" id="gpt-ad-vertical-top-mobile">
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2.  Her dark humor used to crack you up, but now it's dragging you down.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Feelings are contagious. If your friend is down in the moment, you'll likely "catch" her bad attitude.And overall, you're about 15 percent more likely to be happy if one of your friends is happy. A generally unhappy friend
         <em>
          decreases
         </em>
         your happiness by 7 percent. Pharrell's friends must be really chipper.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. You share
          <em>
           all
          </em>
          of her Facebook statuses. (Even the political ones you used to roll your eyes at.)
          <br/>
         </strong>
         We're more willing to entertain beliefs that are different from our own if a friend is the one espousing them rather than a cable-news talking head. That can open up your horizons in an enriching way, but a friend whose core values and ambitions don't match your own can cause you to "drift" off the course that is best for you. (Remember the celebrity Kabbalah craze?)
        </p>
        <div class="embedded-recirc--container norewrite">
         <a class="embedded-recirc--link link">
          <div class="embedded-recirc--image-container">
           <img class="embedded-recirc--image" nopin="nopin"/>

            We Recommend
           </div>

         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Two wines and a cocktail after work is a typical Tuesday night for her. What, is that, like, a lot?
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Trying to kick bad habits? It's not just your personal problem: A friend who starts smoking increases your chances of taking up cigarettes by 36 percent. If your close friend is a daily drinker, you're 50 percent more likely to drink heavily yourself. Slowly back away from the LiLo in your group or lure them to a juice-only outing at Whole Foods.
        </p>
        <div class="ad-gpt-sharethrough">
         <div class="gpt-tag" id="ad-gpt-sharethrough">
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. She invested in some Kiehl's lotion. You mooched for a while, and then invested in some Kiehl's lotion.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Don't blame advertisers for telling you what to buy. It's actually your friends who, often through social media, point you in the direction of brands and products to try out. It's fine to follow a friend's lead (Jessica Alba reportedly taught her BFF Jamie King how to be an environmentally-conscious new mom, for example), but remember to keep cultivating your unique mind and sense of style  and to be a good influence on
         <em>
          her
         </em>
         .
