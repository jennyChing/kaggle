               <p>
                Few things are more annoying to an Android smartphone or tablet owner than wanting to upgrade their device to a newer version of Android but being blocked from doing it by their vendor and/or carrier. Sure you could unlock your device and load an alternative bootloader if you knew exactly what you were doing.
                <a href="http://www.cyngn.com/" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
                 Cyanogen
                </a>
                , the company behind the leading alternative Android firmware
                <a href="http://www.cyanogenmod.org/" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
                 CyanogenMod
                </a>
                has released the first easy-to-use Android bootloader to the
                <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.cyanogenmod.oneclick" style="line-height: 1.5em;">
                 Google Play Store: CyanogenMod Installer
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <div class="ad-inpage-video-top" data-ad="inpage-video-top" id="inpage-video-top-55a57c54d2064">
                <script>
                 window.console &amp;&amp; console.log &amp;&amp; console.log("ADS: queuing inpage-video-top-55a57c54d2064 for display");

               <figure class="image alignRight image-thumbnail shortcode-image">
                <span class="img ">
                 <img alt="CyanogenMod Installer" class="" height="170" src="http://zdnet1.cbsistatic.com/hub/i/r/2014/10/04/9eeb4537-4c11-11e4-b6a0-d4ae52e95e57/resize/170x170/ab8d15ce4984ded265a79c96ed1c6a92/cyanogenmod-installer.png" width="170"/>
                </span>
                <figcaption>
                 <span class="caption">
                  Say howdy to the easy-to-use Cyanogen alternative Android ROM bootloader. (Credit: Cyanogen)
                 </span>
                </figcaption>
               </figure>
               <p>
                While it's not as simple as click, download, and boot your new Android, the CyanogenMod Installer and its accompanying Windows program, makes upgrading Android devices easier than ever before.
               </p>
               <p>
                At this time there are only a few dozen devices supported by the Installer. These are all members of the
                <a href="http://wiki.cyanogenmod.org/w/CyanogenMod_Installer">
                 Google Nexus, Samsung Galaxy, and HTC One lines
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                You will also need a
                <a href="http://beta.download.cyanogenmod.org/install">
                 Windows PC for the CyanogenMod Installer
                </a>
                .This assistant program works with Windows Vista, 7, and 8. It does not work with Windows XP. There are
                <a href="http://www.cyanogenmod.org/blog/cyanogenmod-installer-release">
                 plans for a Mac OS X Installer
                </a>
                , but none for Linux at this time.
               </p>
               <p>
                With the pair of programs, you can upgrade the supported devices from their existing version of Android to the
                <a href="http://www.cyanogenmod.org/blog/cyanogenmod-10-2-m1-release">
                 CyanogenMod 10.2 M1 Release
                </a>
                . This is a version of
                <span class="link" section="shortcodeLink">
                 <a href="/article/top-5-features-in-android-4-3-jelly-bean/">
                  Android 4.3
                 </a>
                </span>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                For now, there is no version of CyanogenMod for
                <span class="link" section="shortcodeLink">
                 <a href="/article/the-five-things-you-need-to-know-about-android-4-4-kitkat/">
                  Android 4.4 "KitKat"
                 </a>
                </span>
                . The
                <span class="link" section="shortcodeLink">
                 <a href="/article/cyanogenmod-goes-from-hobby-android-to-business-android/">
                  newly minted Android company
                 </a>
                </span>
                is working on this.
               </p>
               <p>
                "We will continue to work on the 10.2 code branch, in parallel to all of our Android 4.4 efforts," the company said."For those that will inevitably ask, we have not made any decisions with respect to what devices will make the leap to the KitKat code base and likely won't have that information for a few weeks."
               </p>
               <p>
                Before jumping into upgrading your device, do keep in mind that this is potentially dangerous for your device. With more than nine million downloads, CyanogenMod may be the most popular alternative firmware. However, most of the people who've used it until now have been Android developers or power users.
               </p>

               <p>
                So, to lessen the chances that you'll brick your device, or at least be in a better position to recovery it if you do brick it, be sure to take the following steps.
               </p>
               <p>
                First, back up your data. Next (no, really) make sure you backed up your data and that you can read the backup.
               </p>
               <p>
                Then, make sure your device's battery is fully charged. After that connect your device directly to the Windows PC with a high-quality USB cable. Do
