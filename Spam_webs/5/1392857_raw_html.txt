        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #808000;">
          <strong>
           Barcelona
          </strong>
         </span>
         , a Gothic and Modernist marvel on the Mediterranean Sea, is a city well known for its quirky, cosmopolitan, cool character. Whether its by unearthing Barcelonas past in the hidden courtyards of the Barri Gtic, or by enjoying the charms of the modernist city, you will discover many interesting things that make Barcelona one of Spains most livable and energetic cities and one of the most interesting cities in Europe. So, here are 10 facts you might not know that will get you started.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           1. Its founding origins are still in dispute
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         There are two Spanish legends concerning the founding of Barcelona. One legend says that it was founded by Hercules, 400 years before the building of Rome. But, according to the other legend, the city was founded in the 3rd century BC by the Carthaginian Hamilcar Barca, father of Hannibal. He named the cityBarcinoafter his family, the Barca family of Carthage. Which one do you believe?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Barcelona, Spain" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4990" height="316" src="http://www.globotreks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/Barcelona-Beach.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="Barcelona Beach" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           2.
          </span>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           Barcelona is considered
          </span>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           the best beach city in the world by
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.nationalgeographic.com/" target="_blank">
           <em>
            <span style="color: #808000;">
             National Geographic
            </span>
           </em>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           , but
          </span>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           their beaches werent used for leisure until 1992
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Today there are seven beaches with a total of 4.5 km of coastline, but before 1992, Barcelonas seaside was completely run over by industries and there existed no beach for the use of the citys populace and tourism.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         It wasnt until the citys redevelopment for the 1992 Olympic Games that the city moved their industries and converted the seaside area into an exemplary leisure area for locals and tourist alike; including the improvement of two beaches and the creation of five new beaches.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         According to
         <a href="http://dsc.discovery.com/" target="_blank">
          <em>
           Discovery Channel
          </em>
         </a>
         ,
         <em>
          Barceloneta beach
         </em>
         is the best urban beach in the world and the third best beach in the world.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           3. Barcelona is to thank for World Book Day
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         La
         <em>
          Diada de San Jordi
         </em>
         (St. Georges Day) is one of the biggest, most widely celebrated festival days in Barcelona which takes place on April 23rd and is a celebration of love and literacy.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         On this day, it is a tradition to present your near and dear ones with roses and books. Inspired from this custom, UNESCO declared the day as the
         <em>
          International Day of Books
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Barcelona, Spain" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4991" height="357" src="http://www.globotreks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/Las-Ramblas.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="Las Ramblas" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           4. La Rambla is not just one street its five streets
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <em>
          La Rambla
         </em>
         is probably the most famous street in Barcelona, but in reality it consists of 5 boulevards or ramblas joined to make one long promenade. It starts from
         <em>
          Placa Catalunya
         </em>
         and end at the Colombus statue by the waterfront. For this reason, this 2 kilometer long boulevard is also commonly known in plural as
         <em>
          Las Ramblas
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Thousands of people stroll on Las Ramblas every day and night, where they watch the performing street artists, shop, or sit for a nice meal or coffee.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Las Ramblas is the major center of activities in the city and is undoubtedly the most famous promenade of Barcelona, and probably in all Spain.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           5. It is Europes largest metropolis on the Mediterranean coast
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         In addition, Barcelona is the second largest city in Spain, after Madrid, and the sixth-most populous urban area in the European Union after Paris, London, Ruhr area, Madrid and Milan.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           6. It is the first city to win a RIBA Royal Gold Medal for its architecture
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The Royal Gold Medal for architecture is awarded annually since 1848, by the
         <em>
          Royal Institute of British Architects
         </em>
         on behalf of the British monarch, in recognition of an individuals or groups substantial contribution to international architecture. In 1999, Barcelona became the first and only city, to this date, to receive this honor  instead of an individual architect. And it goes without saying that it is well deserved.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Barcelona, Spain" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4992" height="354" src="http://www.globotreks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/La-Merce.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="La Merce" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           7. Fiestas de la Merc is the most important festival in Barcelona
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The main event of
         <em>
          La Merc
         </em>
         is held on the 24th of September each year and the Festival is held in honour of the Cities Patron Saint and Protector, The Virgin de La Merce. It is an official city holiday since the year 1871.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           8. Flamenco is not well known in Barcelona
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Even though Spain is well known for its flamenco dancing, this popular dance is not traditional or well known in Barcelona or Catalua. Catalans prefer the more contemporary rock-n-roll scene.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Barcelona, Spain" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4993" height="300" src="http://www.globotreks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/Eixample.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="Eixample Cerda" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           9. The hallmark grid pattern of the city came from a revolutionary utopian master plan
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Most of the city of Barcelona was designed byIldefons Cerd i Sunyerfor the 19thcentury extension of the city. The plan, calledEixample, intended to create the perfect environment for Barcelonas fast expanding population. He considered traffic and transport along with sunlight and ventilation for the design of his characteristic octagonal blocks, where the streets broaden at every intersection making for greater visibility, better ventilation, and greater open spaces. In the end, the plan wasnt built following all the utopian characteristics he envisioned in his plan.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Barcelona, Spain" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4994" height="339" src="http://www.globotreks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/Sagrada-Familia1.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="Sagrada Familia" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #808000;">
           10. Gaudwas not the original architect of Sagrada Familia
          </span>
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Even though
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.globotreks.com/destinations/10-gaudi-buildings-barcelona/">
           La Sagrada Famlia
          </a>
         </em>
         is one of the major works of
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.globotreks.com/destinations/10-gaudi-buildings-barcelona/">
           Anton Gaud
          </a>
         </em>
         , he was not the first architect to be appointed to work on it. Architect
         <em>
          Francisco de Paula del Villar
         </em>
         was the first one commissioned to design the church. Construction of the crypt of the church was begun March 19, 1882, following
         <em>
          Villars
         </em>
         design. It wasnt until 1883 when
         <em>
          Anton Gaud
         </em>
         began working on the project. On March 18, 1883,
         <em>
          Villar
         </em>
         retired from the project, and
         <em>
          Gaud
         </em>
         assumed responsibility for its design, which he changed radically.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         So that is a quick glimpse at Barcelonas interesting facts. This city is definitely worth visiting to discover even more secrets for yourself.
        </p>
