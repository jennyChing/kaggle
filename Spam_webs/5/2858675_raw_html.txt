           <p>
            Submit!
           </p>
           <p>
            Um, no thanks, Im not really a submissive person.
           </p>
           <div class="header-unit sej-unit" style="text-align:center;padding:10px;clear:both">
            <!-- SEJ_320x50_In_Post_Mobile -->
            <div id="div-gpt-ad-1397407000033-15">
             <script type="text/javascript">
              googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1397407000033-15'); });
             </script>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            Read more!
           </p>
           <p>
            WaitI have to read more than what Ive already read? I dont need this product that bad.
           </p>
           <p>
            Buy now!
           </p>
           <p>
            You need me to buy RIGHT NOW? Im a little nervous about that. Maybe I should reconsider.
           </p>
           <p>
            CTAs are big opportunities in tiny, little button format. They are the gatekeepers that allow your users to access those magical parts of your website where the conversions happen. But theyre sensitive little thingsif you miss the mark with appearance, language or positioning, youre essentially waving those conversions goodbye.
           </p>
           <p>
            Since CTAs are so important , you have to treat them as more than just a simple button. You should consider how your buttons should look, what they should say, and where they should be placed so that theyll appeal to as many users as possible.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Appearance
           </h2>
           <p>
            In order for your visitors to convert, theyll have to
            <strong>
             interact
            </strong>
            with at least one CTA on your site. And in order for visitors to interact with a CTA, it has to be
            <strong>
             visually noticeable
            </strong>
            . Thats why appearance-based considerations like color, shape and size are really important when designing your call to action buttons:
           </p>
           <h2>
            Color
           </h2>
           <p>
            Color is probably your biggest opportunity to make your CTA
            <strong>
             stand out
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            There are two approaches you can take to choosing a color  the complementary color approach, or the color psychology approach
           </p>
           <h3>
            Complementary Colors
           </h3>
           <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_109554" style="width: 327px">
            <a href="http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Color-Wheel.png" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Color-Wheel.png', '');">
             <img alt="Color Wheel" class="wp-image-109554 " height="317" src="http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Color-Wheel-380x380.png" width="317"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo Credit: Wikimedia Commons
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Using
            <strong>
             Complementary colors
            </strong>
            (colors that are directly across from each other on the color wheel) is a great way to make sure your CTA stands out, but still fits in with your website. Complementary colors
            <strong>
             contrast
            </strong>
            enough to stand apart from each other, but they still work well as a
            <strong>
             pair
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Choose a complementary color that contrasts from the rest of the page for your CTA. That way, it pops out from the rest of the elements and is the first thing your users will see.
           </p>
           <p>
            Lets say, for instance, that your site is designed in shades of blue. If you look directly across the color wheel from the two blue shades, youll see two orange shades. Orange is blues complementary color; therefore, warm orange is the best color to contrast (but not conflict) with your cool blue layout.
           </p>
           <p>
            Obviously, there are more shades than the 12 shown in this color wheel. If youre looking to match a
            <strong>
             specific color hue
            </strong>
            , check out
            <a href="http://colorschemedesigner.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://colorschemedesigner.com/', 'ColorSchemeDesigner.com');" target="_blank">
             ColorSchemeDesigner.com
            </a>
            , which shows you color combinations that work well together. Just choose the main color of your website, and this software will find your perfect match.
           </p>
           <h3>
            Color Psychology
           </h3>
           <p>
            Or, if youd rather appeal to the mind over the eyes, consider incorporating
            <a href="http://www.webpagefx.com/blog/web-design/psychology-of-color-infographic/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.webpagefx.com/blog/web-design/psychology-of-color-infographic/', 'color psychology');" target="_blank">
             color psychology
            </a>
            (Full disclosure: link goes to infographic on my site).
           </p>
           <p>
            Research has found different colors can create different
            <strong>
             emotional responses
            </strong>
            in viewers. You can use colors that create the positive emotional responses you want associated with your brand. By enabling these experiences, youre increasing the chances that visitors will
            <strong>
             positively perceive
            </strong>
            your brand and convert.
           </p>
           <p>
            If your company has a logo/brand color palette, chances are your colors were already strategically chosen to portray certain brand characteristics. You can never go wrong using one of
            <strong>
             your brands colors
            </strong>
            for your CTAs, as long as it effectively stands out on the page.
           </p>
           <p>
            If your brand doesnt have a logo or color scheme yet, dont worry. You can use your website and your CTAs as a chance to start that color association with your customers.
           </p>
           <p>
            First, take some time to
            <strong>
             identify the ideal experience
            </strong>
            youd like your customers to have with your brand. How do you want people to feel when theyre on your site? What words do you want them to use when describing your brand? How would you describe the benefits that your company can offer its customers?
           </p>
           <p>
            Maybe youre a bank or law firm, looking to provide security. Or maybe you sell a line of organic food products, designed to provide a healthy snack while still sustaining the environment. Whatever your brands selling point, theres a color to convey it:
           </p>
           <h4>
            Red
           </h4>
           <p>
            The color red is capable of evoking strong emotions in its viewers, and is most known for its
            <strong>
             passion
            </strong>
            and
            <strong>
             intensity
            </strong>
            . Its also been found that red increases heart rate and stimulates appetite. The energy of red is often used to create
            <strong>
             urgency
            </strong>
            , often seen in clearance sales, and is very effective for
            <strong>
             impulsive
            </strong>
            shoppers.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_109557" style="width: 770px">
            <a href="http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Verizon-CTA.png" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Verizon-CTA.png', '');">
             <img alt="Verizon CTAs" class="size-large wp-image-109557" height="505" src="http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Verizon-CTA-1024x681.png" width="760"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Screenshot Taken 6/12/2014 from Verizonwireless.com
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Verizon effectively establishes urgency for this online exclusive offer by incorporating red elements,like the Get the iPhone 4s CTA.
           </p>
           <h4>
            Orange
           </h4>
           <p>
            Shades of orange can reflect
            <strong>
             excitement
            </strong>
            and
            <strong>
             enthusiasm
            </strong>
            . Orange represents a friendly, confident brand, although orange can sometimes signify
            <strong>
             aggression
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_109560" style="width: 700px">
            <a href="http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Payless-CTA.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Payless-CTA.jpg', '');">
             <img alt="Payless Shoes CTAs" class="size-full wp-image-109560" height="397" src="http://cdn.searchenginejournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Payless-CTA.jpg" width="690"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Screenshot Taken 6/12/2014 from http://www.payless.com/womens-shoes/
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Payless Shoe Sourceincorporates orange in a fun way as a highlight feature for its CTAs, where users can scroll over a white CTA and see it turn orange before they click on it. The orange is bold and exciting, and works well to get viewers excited for new shoes.
           </p>
           <h4>
            Yellow
           </h4>
           <p>
            Yellow is the happy color. Yellow is known to increase
            <strong>
             cheerfulness
            </strong>
            and
            <strong>
             optimism
            </strong>
            , and it stimulates our mental and nervous system processes. Yellow encourages
            <strong>
             communication
            </strong>
            and is often used to
            <strong>
             grab attention
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
