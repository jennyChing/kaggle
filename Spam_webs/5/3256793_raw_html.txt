     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Doom
       </b>
      </i>
      (stylized as
      <i>
       <b>
        DOOM
       </b>
      </i>
      and originally known as
      <i>
       <b>
        Doom 4
       </b>
      </i>
      ) is the upcoming reboot of the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Doom_(series)" title="Doom (series)">
        Doom
       </a>
      </i>
      series by
      <a href="/wiki/Id_Software" title="Id Software">
       id Software
      </a>
      following the release of its last title,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Doom_3" title="Doom 3">
        Doom 3
       </a>
      </i>
      , in 2004. It will be released on
      <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">
       Microsoft Windows
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">
       PlayStation 4
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">
       Xbox One
      </a>
      in 2016. After years in development with almost no news from id Software, it was restarted completely in 2011 with
      <a href="/wiki/Bethesda_Softworks" title="Bethesda Softworks">
       Bethesda Softworks
      </a>
      serving as the publisher for the game.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doom_(2016_video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The game takes place on Mars, where the UAC Corporation have been discovering artificial symbols that marked Mars and bring demons from hell.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">
       Gameplay
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doom_(2016_video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Gameplay">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">
       first-person shooter
      </a>
      which has "badass demons, big effing guns, and moving really fast" as key principles, according to id Software executive producer Marty Stratton.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The game features different weapons, which can be collected and switched freely by players throughout the game. Many classic weapons such as the super shotgun and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BFG-9000" title="BFG-9000">
       BFG-9000
      </a>
      make a return. In addition, melee-weapons like the chainsaw, which can cut enemies in half, are also featured. Many enemies from the original games like the Revenant, Mancubus, and Cyberdemon return as well, some of them redesigned.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As the combat system of the game puts emphasis on momentum and speed, the game allows players to perform movements like splinting and double-jumping. A combat system known as "push forward combat" is featured, which discourages players from taking cover behind obstacles or resting to regain health.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As a result, the game will not feature any cover mechanics or a
      <a href="/wiki/Health_(gaming)#Regenerating_health" title="Health (gaming)">
       health-regeneration system
      </a>
      . Players can collect health and armour pick-ups scattered throughout levels, or kill enemies to regain health. A new mechanic introduced in
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      is the melee execution system. It allows players to perform a melee-takedown when players deal enough damages to enemies. Enemies available for melee-takedown will be highlighted.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Multiple multiplayer modes, such as traditional four-player
      <a href="/wiki/Deathmatch" title="Deathmatch">
       deathmatch
      </a>
      , domination, freeze tag, and clan arena, are included in the game.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gem_e3_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-gem_e3-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Power-ups and teleports are also to be featured in the game. Certain game modes feature
      <a href="/wiki/Asymmetry" title="Asymmetry">
       asymmetrical
      </a>
      structures, in which players can pick-up a pentagram in the map that allows them to transform into and fight as one of the monsters featured in the game. The game will also include an built-in tool titled "
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      SnapMap" which allows players to construct custom maps, create new game modes and share them with the online community.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Development">
       Development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doom_(2016_video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="As_Doom_4">
       As
       <i>
        Doom 4
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doom_(2016_video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: As Doom 4">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The game was announced as in production on May 7, 2008,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ign_announce_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ign_announce-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      after
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_D._Carmack" title="John D. Carmack">
       John Carmack
      </a>
      , then lead developer at
      <a href="/wiki/Id_Software" title="Id Software">
       id Software
      </a>
      , hinted it at
      <a href="/wiki/QuakeCon" title="QuakeCon">
       QuakeCon
      </a>
      on August 3, 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carmackrage_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-carmackrage-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Id Software's CEO
      <a href="/wiki/Todd_Hollenshead" title="Todd Hollenshead">
       Todd Hollenshead
      </a>
      suggested that, like
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Doom_II:_Hell_on_Earth" title="Doom II: Hell on Earth">
        Doom II: Hell on Earth
       </a>
      </i>
      , it will take place on Earth,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and will feature gameplay more akin to the original
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      games rather than the horror styled gameplay of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Doom_3" title="Doom 3">
        Doom 3
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On August 1, 2008, John Carmack said that
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      will look three times better than
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rage_(video_game)" title="Rage (video game)">
        Rage
       </a>
      </i>
      does, intended to run at 30 frames per second; on Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3; rather than 60 that
      <i>
       Rage
      </i>
      targets.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In its Windows version,
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      was intended to run at 60 frames per second with state-of-the-art hardware.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2009, he revealed that the multiplayer component is being developed separately and will run at 60 frames per second.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Carmack stated in 2011 that "you can't have 30 guys crawling all over you at 60 frames per second at this graphics technology level because it's painful. -- So, [in single-player] we can have 30 demons crawling all over you on there."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In April 2009, Hollenshead said
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      was "deep in development". When asked if
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      would be "a sequel? A reboot? A prequel?", his response was: "It's not a sequel to
      <i>
       Doom 3
      </i>
      , but it's not a reboot either.
      <i>
       Doom 3
      </i>
      was sort of a reboot. It's a little bit different than those."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GameSpotApr09Doom4_20-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-GameSpotApr09Doom4-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On June 23, 2009,
      <a href="/wiki/ZeniMax_Media" title="ZeniMax Media">
       ZeniMax Media
      </a>
      , best known for
      <a href="/wiki/Bethesda_Softworks" title="Bethesda Softworks">
       Bethesda Softworks
      </a>
      , acquired id Software and announced that all future id games will be published by Bethesda Softworks, including
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      in addition to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rage_(video_game)" title="Rage (video game)">
        Rage
       </a>
      </i>
      and future
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Quake_(series)" title="Quake (series)">
        Quake
       </a>
      </i>
      titles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Carmack said at Quakecon 2011 that once
      <i>
       Rage
      </i>
      shipped its development team would move to
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      to speed up on that project.
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      might also feature dedicated servers unlike
      <i>
       Rage
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Carmack.27s_Keynote_at_QuakeCon_2011_22-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Carmack.27s_Keynote_at_QuakeCon_2011-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      At the 2011 QuakeCon, Carmack mentioned that new
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      will be using a new scripting language that is based on C++, and called it "super-script". This so-called "super-script" is a subset of C++; with features like scheduling and
      <a href="/wiki/Type_safety" title="Type safety">
       type safety
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Carmack.27s_Keynote_at_QuakeCon_2011_22-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Carmack.27s_Keynote_at_QuakeCon_2011-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In February 2012, some alleged screenshots were released on
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Official_Xbox_Magazine_UK" title="Official Xbox Magazine UK">
        Official Xbox Magazine UK
       </a>
       '
      </i>
      s website,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but the images were discredited by id Software's creative director Matthew Hooper.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In November 2013, Carmack left id Software to commit to his work at
      <a href="/wiki/Oculus_Rift" title="Oculus Rift">
       Oculus VR
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In April 2013,
      <a href="/wiki/Kotaku" title="Kotaku">
       Kotaku
      </a>
      published an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expos%C3%A9_(journalism)" title="Expos (journalism)">
       expos
      </a>
      describing
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      as trapped in "
      <a href="/wiki/Development_hell" title="Development hell">
       development hell
      </a>
      ". Citing connections to id, the article claims that
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      has suffered under mismanagement, and that development was completely restarted in 2011. Inside sources described the pre-2011 version, which was to portray the uprising of hell on Earth, as heavily scripted and cinematic, comparing it to the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty" title="Call of Duty">
        Call of Duty
       </a>
      </i>
      franchise. The pre-2011 version was criticized as mediocre, but the sources also described the new version as "lame" and a "mess".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kotaku.com_26-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-kotaku.com-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Id's Tim Willits said during Quakecon 2013: "Every game has a soul. Every game has a spirit. When you played
      <i>
       Rage
      </i>
      , you got the spirit. And [
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      ] did not have the spirit, it did not have the soul, it didnt have a personality."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="As_Doom">
       As
       <i>
        Doom
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doom_(2016_video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: As Doom">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      After the game was sent back to the drawing board, Willits revealed in August 2013 that the next game in the
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      franchise was still the team's focus although he did not make it clear if the game was still meant to be titled
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_28-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-mtv-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On February 19, 2014, Bethesda revealed that access to a beta version of
      <i>
       Doom 4
      </i>
      , renamed
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      , will be available for those who pre-order
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Wolfenstein:_The_New_Order" title="Wolfenstein: The New Order">
        Wolfenstein: The New Order
       </a>
      </i>
      on any of the platforms.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On June 10, 2014, a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teaser_trailer" title="Teaser trailer">
       teaser trailer
      </a>
      of
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      was presented at
      <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Entertainment_Expo_2014" title="Electronic Entertainment Expo 2014">
       Electronic Entertainment Expo 2014
      </a>
      as well as at QuakeCon's website and at a new official
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      website. A more expansive trailer was unveiled at QuakeCon 2014 on July 17, 2014, where a closed presentation was made mainly to silence ongoing rumors of the project being in jeopardy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quakecon_31-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-quakecon-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      From what was seen in the QuakeCon presentation, the new
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      is to feature mechanics such as melee combat, finishing moves, and the ability to rip someone's arm off and use it to open a biometric security door.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quakecon_31-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-quakecon-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Also said to be featured in the game are double jumping and freedom of movement such as vaulting and
      <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_climbing_terms#mantel" title="Glossary of climbing terms">
       manteling
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On July 19, 2014, in light of
      <a href="/wiki/Crytek" title="Crytek">
       Crytek
      </a>
      's financial difficulties, it was announced that Tiago Sousa, head R&amp;D graphics engineer at Crytek, was leaving to join the
      <i>
       Doom
      </i>
      and idTech 6 engine team.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On May 18, 2015, a brief teaser trailer was released to promote gameplay being shown at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E3_2015" title="E3 2015">
       E3 2015
      </a>
      on June 14, 2015; the trailer depicted the double-barreled shotgun, and the Revenant, a monster returning to the game.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On June 14, 2015, around 15 minutes of gameplay footage were shown at E3 and the release date was revealed to be Q1/Q2 2016.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-releasedate_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-releasedate-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The initial reception of the trailer is positive, despite receiving criticisms by some critics as they considered the game "too violent".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Pete Hines from Bethesda Softworks responded by saying that the game is designed to allow players to apply violence on demons instead of humans. Hines added that "If you're not into violent, bloody games...
      <i>
       Doom'
      </i>
      s probably not a game for you."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
       <a href="#cite_note-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
