         impacted crop

        <span class="type">

       <div class="content">
        <p>
         An impacted
         <a class="populated" href="/title/crop" title="crop">
          crop
         </a>
         is when food gets stuck inside a bird's crop, hardens, and blocks digestion.
        </p>
        <p>
         This can happen for a few reasons:
        </p>
        <p>
         1. It can happen if a mother bird doesn't shell seed properly and eats them whole, then regurgitates it for their young. Young birds cannot digest whole seeds, leading to crop impaction.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. It can happen if a bird overeats and can't digest all the food its ingested in time before the food begins to harden.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. It can happen if a bird eats something indigestible that blocks up the crop, resulting in what's in the crop hardening.
        </p>
        <p>
         Treatment varies bird to bird. With larger birds, such as chickens, you can sometimes help them drink water and massage the impaction away. Smaller birds, like
         <a class="populated" href="/title/parakeet" title="parakeet">
          parakeet
         </a>
         s or
         <a class="populated" href="/title/cockatiel" title="cockatiel">
          cockatiel
         </a>
         s, will need to go to a vet immediately. I've heard that some people feed their hens
         <a class="populated" href="/title/apple+cider+vinegar" title="apple cider vinegar">
          apple cider vinegar
         </a>
         to get the birds to vomit the impaction up, but I've also heard that this is a bad idea that can go terribly wrong if the
        </p>
