        <a href="http://www.odditycentral.com/pics/banana-oxidation-art-is-just-bananas.html" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Banana Oxidation Art Is Just Bananas">
         Banana Oxidation Art Is Just Bananas
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="post-detail">
        <span class="post-info">
         By  Spooky on  August 8th, 2011 Category:
         <a href="http://www.odditycentral.com/category/art" rel="category tag">
          Art
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.odditycentral.com/category/pics" rel="category tag">

       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         Australian artist
         <strong>
          Jun Gil Park
         </strong>
         has found a way of turning regular bananas into awe-inspiring works of art by drawing on them with a toothpick.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive seen some pretty amazing banana artworks since I started Oddity Central, like the
         <a href="http://www.odditycentral.com/pics/the-banana-wall.html" target="_blank">
          banana wall
         </a>
         , or
         <a href="http://www.odditycentral.com/pics/the-banana-boats-of-jacob-dahlstrup.html" target="_blank">
          Jacob Dahlstrubs banana boats
         </a>
         , but Jun Gil Parks oxidation art just makes me go bananas. Using a simple toothpick he scratches the designs into the banana, and the harder he presses/scratches, the darker the bruised part gets. It usually takes about five minutes for the oxidation to start showing, and after a day or two it gets really dark.
        </p>
        <p>
         Youre probably wondering how Jun Gil Park came up with this simple-yet-impressive technique of turning bananas into organic works of art. It was pretty simple actually- he was just sitting at the table one day, talking to his family, holding a toothpick in his mouth, when he noticed a banana in front of him. He began scribbling on it as they talked and noticed what was happening to the banana. Thats when he decided he should try something more detailed next time.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-25914" height="412" src="http://www.odditycentral.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/Jun-Gil-Park-550x412.jpg" title="Jun Gil Park" width="550"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-25913">
         </span>
         Im just blown away by this guys talent, too bad bananas dont last very long, although Im sure there must be a way to preserve them.
        </p>
