      <h1>
       <a href="http://www.needyl.com/knitting/mens-cabled-hat-pattern/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Mens Cabled Hat Pattern">
        Mens Cabled Hat Pattern
       </a>
      </h1>
      <h3>
       Published by Shana | Filed under
       <a href="http://www.needyl.com/free/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Free">
        Free
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.needyl.com/knitting/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Knitting">
        Knitting
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.needyl.com/patterns/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Patterns">
        Patterns
       </a>
      </h3>
      <p>
       With how brutally cold its been, its depressing to think we are just a month into winter. It seems like Kansasians have given into the permanence of the cold. Random comments about how nice it is outside can be overheard when the thermometer inches above the 20 degree mark. Its possible the lack of sun has made us delirious.
      </p>
      <p>
       Needless to say, it is time for heavy duty knit ware. Lace and silk are nice and all, but these balmy 5 degree days demand wool and cables. Designed the below for the fella. I wanted a guy-friendly design that wasnt just ribbing and a rolled up cuff so he can double up on his ears. I really love the outcome, and it was a really fast knit (2 days of fairly regular knitting).
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 475px">
       <img alt="man's hat pattern" height="349" src="http://needyl.com/sites/default/files/img_thumbnails/166_511.jpeg" title="knit hat pattern for men" width="465"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        OMG, my ears have never been so warm.
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       <span id="more-166">
       </span>
      </p>
      <h1>
       A Hat Fit For a Fella Pattern
      </h1>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/badges/redirect?p=a-hat-fit-for-a-fella" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.ravelry.com']);">
        <img alt="" src="http://api.ravelry.com/badges/projects?p=a-hat-fit-for-a-fella&amp;t=.gif" style="border: none;"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h2 class="western">
       Size
      </h2>
      <p>
       Adult Womens/Mens
      </p>
      <h2 class="western">
       Gauge
      </h2>
      <p>
       18 sts = 4 inches in stockinette
      </p>
      <h2 class="western">
       Materials
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        One skein (4 oz, 190 yards) Lambs Pride Worsted weight. 85% wool, 15% mohair
       </li>
       <li>
        Size 8 (5 mm) 32 circular needles for magic loop method or DPNs, depending upon preference
       </li>
       <li>
        Cable Needle
       </li>
       <li>
        Darning needle
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2 class="western">
       Abbreviations
      </h2>
      <p>
       K  Knit
      </p>
      <p>
       P  Purl
      </p>
      <p>
       K2tog  knit 2 together
      </p>
      <p>
       Ssk  Slip 2 stitches knitwise and knit those two sts tog
      </p>
      <p>
       Sl, k2tog, psso  Slip on st to the right needle, k2tog, pass slip stitch over
      </p>
      <p>
       CL  Cable Left  Slip 3 sts to cable needle, hold to front, k3 from left needle, k3 from cable needle
      </p>
      <p>
       CR  Cable Right  Slip 3 sts to cable needle, hold to back, k3 from left needle, k3 from cable needle
      </p>
      <h2 class="western">
       Directions
      </h2>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       Cable cast on 80 (90) sts
      </p>
      <h3 class="western">
       Ribbing
      </h3>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P2 (3), k3, p2 (3), k3, p3, k3* Repeat from * to * around until ribbing measures 2.5 (3) inches.
      </p>
      <h3 class="western">
       Body of Hat
      </h3>
      <p>
       Work the following pattern three times
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Work 6 (7) rows of *P2 (3), k3, p2 (3), k9* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
       </li>
       <li>
        *P2 (3), k3, p2 (3), CR, k3* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
       </li>
       <li>
        Work 6 (7) rows of *P2 (3), k3, p2 (3), k9* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
       </li>
       <li>
        *P2 (3), k3, p2 (3), k3, CL* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Mens only.
       </strong>
       Replace the final round of the third repeat with:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        *P2, ssk, k1, k2tog, p2, k3, CL* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <div>
       <em>
        Please note  this line has been discussed multiple times in the comments. Please read those if you have questions. I am unable to provide more assistance on this free pattern, so if you have more questions, I recommend the forums at Ravelry.com.
       </em>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 475px">
       <img alt="Cable hat for men" height="349" src="http://needyl.com/sites/default/files/img_thumbnails/166_512.jpeg" title="Cable hat for men" width="465"/>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Yes, I realize I say this is a mans hat, yet here I am wearing it. This became acceptable about the time shoulder pads came into style.
       </p>
      </div>
      <h3 class="western">
       Crown
      </h3>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P2, k3, p2, ssk, k5, k2tog* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P2, k3, p2, k7* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P2, k3, p2, ssk, k3, k2tog* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P2, k3, p2, k5* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P1, ssk, k1, k2tog, p1, k5* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P1, k3, p1, k5* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P1, k3, p1, ssk, k1, k2tog* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P1, k3* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P1, k3, p1, sl1, k2tog, psso* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P1, k3, p1, k1* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P1, sl1, k2tog, psso, p1, k1* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       *P1, k1* Repeat from * to * to end of round.
      </p>
      <p style="font-weight: normal;">
       Ssk around.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-weight: normal;">
        Cut yarn and pull end through remaining sts
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Weave in ends.
      </p>

           Work the final round of the last repeat as:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <p>
             *P2, ssk, k1, k2tog, p2, k3, CL* Repeat from * to * to end of round. -
             <strong>
              This round is actually a decrease round (or you can think of it as the first round of the crown). The goal is to get your number of stitches down to the women's size so that the crown directions fit. You should have 80 sts after this round.
             </strong>
            </p>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           You do not need to move the start of the round.
          </p>
