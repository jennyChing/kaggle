        JobSeekers Guide to What to Wear to an Interview (Men)

        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/HowtoDressforJobInterview.jpg">
          <img alt="http://www.artofmanliness.com/2008/01/04/how-to-shave-like-your-grandpa/" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-946" height="850" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/HowtoDressforJobInterview.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.mindtools.com/CommSkll/FirstImpressions.htm" target="_blank" title="First Impressions">
          First impressions
         </a>
         play one of the most important factors of an interviewers determination of your character.  Knowing what to wear to an interview will definitely give you a professional appearance, and it can actually give you more confidence as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         When you are actively
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/help-me-find-a-job-the-most-epic-guide-in-history/" target="_blank" title="searching for a job">
          searching for a job
         </a>
         , its even more important to look the part and make the perception that you are likeable, hard-working, trustworthy, and of course, the perfect candidate for the job.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats why we consulted with a number of stylists, tailors, haberdashers, barbers, and more to put together the ultimate guide for men on what to wear to a job interview.  Its all here in this 4,000+ word guide.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Importance of Appearance in the Job Interview
        </h4>
        <p>
         Dont underestimate the importance of appearance. A
         <a href="http://www.salisbury.edu/careerservices/DataPort/NACE/NACE2006YearJobs.pdf" target="_blank" title="Study Nace">
          2006 Job Outlook study by NACE
         </a>
         found that employers were most influenced by a candidates overall grooming and how they dress.  When deciding how to dress for your next job interview, its important to take note of how the hiring manager might feel about your choices as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/CandidateAppearanceInfluence-e1402976738772.png">
          <img alt="Candidate Apperance Influence" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-798" height="272" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/CandidateAppearanceInfluence-e1402976738772.png" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         What to Wear to an Interview
        </h2>
        <p>
         Deciding what to wear is a big decision  a decision that can impact if you get the job or not.  Typically, my advice is to always dress up for the interview, whether that be a shirt and tie or a suit.
        </p>
        <p>
         My reasoning for this is twofold.  I always want to establish a sense of confidence and accomplishment when I enter an interview, but even if youve overdressed, the hiring manager is bound to respect you because it looks like you are taking this job interview seriously.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Assess the Companys Culture Before the Interview
        </h4>
        <p>
         With this said, company culture plays a huge role in determining what you should wear to a job interview as well.  If you show up in a suit to an interview and everyone is wearing flip flops and shorts, you could possibly lose out on the job because they wont think you will fit in with their company culture.
        </p>
        <p>
         Before deciding on what you are going to wear for your new job, it might be a good idea to see what others wear day to day within the company.  Are they always in a suit and tie?  Is it a more casual office with your potential coworkers wearing jeans and polos?  It might not be a good idea to show up in a suit and tie to a company that wears shorts and flip flops or even workout gear like the guys at Beachbody.
        </p>
        <p>
         What should I wear to my job interview?
        </p>
        <p>
         Examples:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Chick-Fil-A  Shirt and tie.
         </li>
         <li>
          Bank  Suit and tie
         </li>
         <li>
          Life is Good  Probably one of their T-shirts
         </li>
         <li>
          Gym  Depends on culture.  Gym clothes, shirt and tie, or suit and tie
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Typically, you will want to dress one level higher than the everyday dress at the place of work.  If they wear slacks and a button down shirt, wear a suit or shirt and tie.  If the office is more casual, it would be smart to dress in business casual.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/DressforCompanyCulture-e1403058730944.jpg">
          <img alt="Dress for Company Culture" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-928" height="428" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/DressforCompanyCulture-e1403058730944.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/InterviewHairstyles.jpg">
          <img alt="Interview Hairstyles" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-887" height="100" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/InterviewHairstyles.jpg" width="850"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Your hair says a lot about you. No matter if its short, long, or non-existent, when its time for your interview you should try to tidy it up a little bit.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/JobInterviewHairStyles1.jpg">
          <img alt="Job Interview Hair Styles" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-916" height="200" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/JobInterviewHairStyles1.jpg" width="750"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Choosing the Right Hairstyle for the Interview
        </h2>
        <p>
         Short, well styled hair just screams business man, and thats probably why they named it the businessmans haircut.
        </p>
        <p>
         A short taper cut is the most recommended haircut for job interviews, but its not 100% necessary to follow the standard advice.  Many of the stylist we talked to recommended finding the right
         <a href="http://www.fashionbeans.com/2013/the-right-haircut-for-your-face-shape/" target="_blank" title="face shape">
          cut for your face shape
         </a>
         .  And hell, if longer hair makes you feel more confident, then go for it!
        </p>
        <p>
         The main goal of an interview is to look professional.  If you plan on rocking long hair, make sure that it is clean and styled neatly.  You want to look like a reliable employee.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/InterviewShave.jpg">
          <img alt="Interview Shave" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-889" height="100" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/InterviewShave.jpg" width="850"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Facial Hair
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/ShaveBeardJobInterview.jpg">
          <img alt="Shave Beard Job Interview" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-917" height="200" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/ShaveBeardJobInterview-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Most career experts agree that the
         <a href="http://www.artofmanliness.com/2008/01/04/how-to-shave-like-your-grandpa/" target="_blank" title="clean shaven">
          clean shaven
         </a>
         look is the best option for an interview, and they have a good point.  It shows that you are serious about the interview.  Beards can make you look lazy if they are unkept, even if youre an all-star. Going the clean shaven route is definitely the safer option.
        </p>
        <p>
         While the experts mostly agree on this one, keep your beard short and well groomed with clean lines if you just dont want to part with your manliness. Personally, I went the bearded route for my last interview.  I have a baby face, and I wanted to appear slightly older to ensure that my young looks didnt trump my experience.  It worked, because I did get the job!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/InterviewSuit.jpg">
          <img alt="Interview Suit" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-892" height="100" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/InterviewSuit.jpg" width="850"/>
         </a>
        </p>

        <h2>
         Choosing Your Interview Suit
        </h2>
        <p>
         When it comes time to buy a suit, you should focus on three things: fit, style, and fabric.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fit is one of the most important aspects of choosing a suit.  Suit manufacturers have taken a one size fits all approach in order to save money, which means that most are big and boxy to accommodate all different body types.
        </p>
        <p>
         Of course you will want to make sure that the suit hangs right, that it is easy to wear, and that it is comfortable, but its the fit that really makes you look sharp.  Most people think a tailor is expensive, but you might be surprised.  Anytime you purchase a suit, you should consider finding a tailor to make some adjustments to make the suit fit you.  You dont want the suit to wear you.  You want to look good and be confident.
        </p>
        <p>
         The jacket should fit you snugly around the chest and arms.  Ensure that you have freedom to move your arms, and that the shoulder pads end right at the taper of your shoulders.  The sleeves of the jacket should show approximately 1/2 of your shirt cuffs and be long enough to cover your rear end; although, it should not extend past your knuckles when your arms are relaxed near your sides.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/NicolasRichard-e1401510776436.jpg">
           <img alt="Nicolas Richard" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-861" height="128" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/NicolasRichard-e1401510776436.jpg" width="128"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          How do you know a suit works for you? There are two main things to check.
         </p>
         <p>
          First, check the shoulders, the end of the shoulder pad should be just above the end of your shoulder.
         </p>
         <p>
          Second, check the fit around the waist. When closed the jacket shouldnt allow you to fit more than a fist between your torso and the jacket. Everything else can be altered, in particular the length of the sleeve which has a great visual impact.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now, how do you find one ? Youll have to try at least three of them, preferably in different price ranges. This way youll get a feel of what a well built suit feels like.
         </p>

        <h3>
         Your Suits Style
        </h3>
        <p>
         Whats in style today, may not be in style tomorrow.  Luckily, the classic suit is still a timeless piece that has stood the test of time.  We recommend something both classic and versatile enough to wear for different occasions.
        </p>
        <p>
         Opt for a single breasted two or three button suit jacket with a notched lapel and regular flap pockets.  Depending on your frame, you will need to choose a single or double back vent.  Larger guys should go with a double back vent to allow for greater movement while skinnier guys may want to go with the single back vent. Skinny lapels also seem to be in at the moment.  This looks fine on most men, but can look rather silly on bigger guys.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Two or Three Button Suit?
        </h4>
        <p>
         Two and three button suits are the most common styles youll find. Two-button suits are a classic style that works on almost everyone. Three-button works suits work best on taller guys with longer torsos.  Unless your really tall, the two button suit is a good choice.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Your Suits Fabric
        </h3>
        <p>
         Weave, color, and pattern are the most important factors when selecting your
         <a href="http://www.7regentlane.com/blog/suit-fabrics-101-2/" target="_blank" title="fabric">
          suits fabric
         </a>
         . Natural fibers such as wool are best, and if youre choosing something that is hybrid or blend, make sure the synthetic fiber make-up is 40% or less.  Wool is the most popular and versatile fabric, and allows for some breathing in the summer, and is a little warmer in the winter.
        </p>

More than any other suit, the navy is perhaps the most powerful and versatile asset in any mans arsenal  even more than charcoal, which can create a really monochrome palette on a fair skinned guy with light or gray hair and eyebrows. The navy adds just enough of a contrasting pop of color.

         How to Tie a Tie

        <p>

        <p>
         The Four in Hand knot is one of the most basic knots it your tie tying arsenal and for good reason.  Its simple.  Its classic.  The knot is not too big or too small.  The Four in Hand knot is the perfect knot for a day at the office or a night on the town.  Do you need to learn how to tie a tie for your job interview?  Heres a handy little video to show you how.

        <h4>
         Getting the Right Size Pants
        </h4>
        <p>
         Remember, comfort is key when choosing a pair of work pants.  Its difficult to think on your feet and feel confident if youre wearing an uncomfortable pair of pants, but having the right fit will make you look a lot better than you think.
        </p>
        <h5>
         Waist
        </h5>
        <p>
         Your pant size is generally the size in inches of your waist; although, you will see a slight difference in sizing from different manufacturers.  Find a pair that fits your form, but not uncomfortably.  You should be able to easily button your pants, but you need to ensure that they do fit and are not falling off your arse.
        </p>
        <h5>
         Rise
        </h5>
        <p>
         Rise is measured from the top of the waistband to the seam of the crotch.  This is what affects comfort the most, and can audibly alter a mans voice from sexy baritone to Mickey Mouse.
        </p>
        <p>
         Typically, there are three types:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Low-rise: sits at or below your hips, a few inches below your belly button (Typically skinny guys)
         </li>
         <li>
          Mid-rise: sits at the hips or slightly above (Guys with a little meat on their bones)
         </li>
         <li>
          High-rise: anything that sits at your true waist and hits your belly button (Guys with tree trunks for thighs)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         You may have to experiment to find the right rise in order to find the most comfortable pair of pants.
        </p>
        <h5>
         Break
        </h5>
        <p>
         Break relates to the length of the pants and how the end of the pants leg falls over the shoe.  For business pants, it is common for the break to form an almost horizontal line where the pant leg falls just over the laces of the shoe and angles slightly lower to the back of the shoe.
        </p>

        </p>

         Lets be honest.  Most people overlook the belt. Its an afterthought really.  Black belt? Check.  Brown belt? Check.  Were good!  Until I realized that the buckle was ripping off the leather on the black belt, and my brown belt looked like it was an antique that should have been stored in the Smithsonian.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Choosing the Right Belt
        </h3>
        <p>
         Choosing the right belt is easier than you think.  Youll need to consider style, the buckle, color, and length.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/JobInterviewBelts.jpg">
          <img alt="Job Interview Belts" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-905" height="112" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/JobInterviewBelts-300x112.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h4>
         Belt Style
        </h4>
        <p>
         There are typically four different types of dress belts:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Tongue Buckles:  the tongue buckle consists of a belt that slides trough a metal ring, and then the tongue is slipped through a open hole in the belt itself to hold the belt.
         </li>
         <li>
          Hook Buckles::  the hook buckle consists of a belt that is attached by a flat metal plate attached by hooking the hook on the back of the belt to the front of the belt.
         </li>
         <li>
          Sliding Latch Buckles: the sliding latch buckle consists of a belt that slides through a metal plate which has a latch that presses the belt into place when fastened.
         </li>
         <li>
          Braided belts:  consists of a braided woven leather rather than a flat piece of leather.  Braided belts use a typical tongue that slides through the strands of the braided belt.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h4>
         The Belt Buckle
        </h4>
        <p>
         There are a number of different buckle types based on the styles listed above.
        </p>
        <p>
         Which type of belt is best for a job interview?
        </p>
        <p>
         Really any of the four can work as long as you stay with a nice clean look.  Bigger buckles are more casual so its best to stay with a smaller buckler for the interview.
        </p>
        <p>
         When choosing the color of your belt buckle, you should consider any jewelry you would wear (Such as watches / wedding rings). Try your best to match your buckle color to your accessories.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Belt Color
        </h4>
        <p>
         Your belt should match your shoes.  Period.
        </p>
        <p>
         A more refined gentleman would probably say that all leathers should match.
        </p>
        <p>
         Brown Shoes = Brown Belt
        </p>
        <p>
         Black Shoes = Black Belt
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have a high gloss shoe shine, then your belt should have a glossy finish as well.  The opposite is true as well.  If you have a matte finish on your shoes, go for the same in the belt.
        </p>

         Belt Length
        </h4>
        <p>
         You will want your belt to have a few inches of leather remaining once the belt is fastened completely. If its too long and wraps around your side, you tend to look like a moron.  Dont do that.

         There are six parts of a shoe that you should be aware of:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Shoe Color  Black shoes are the traditional job search attire, but brown lace-ups look good with a navy suit.
         </li>
         <li>
          The Material  Leather with a high gloss will pull of a great shine and compliment your outfit quite nicely.
         </li>
         <li>
          The Laces  Although slip-on shoes are both comfortable and a breeze to put on, lace-up shoes just look better.  Opt for a pair of lace up shoes.

Personally, I go with the squared off toe because my foot is so wide that I would have to jump up three sizes to fit into a pair with elongated toe.  It may not look as classy, but either will work.

         <li>
          The Sole  You dont want to wear platform shoes, but a thick sole is the hallmark of a good shoe. Look for a leather sole with at least 1/4 thickness of the sole.
         </li>
         <li>
          The Broguing  The decorative stitching, or broguing,  on a shoe makes the shoe look more casual.  The less decorations the better.
         </li>

         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Accessories can finish off an outfit as long as they are not overdone.  Things such as jewelry or a nice briefcase offer a nice polish that can really bring your look together.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Jewelry
        </h2>
        <p>
         A wedding ring, if youre married, and a nice watch are typically the only jewelry you need on your interview.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the US, it is okay to wear a class ring to the interview.  As Ive learned in Houston, some schools have a very strong ties with fellow alumni, and this connection could actually help you get the job (Texas A&amp;M grads, Im talking about you).
        </p>
        <p>
         Stay away from cufflinks unless you are interviewing for an executive position.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Briefcase
        </h2>
        <p>
         A nice leather briefcase or messenger bag that matches either your black or brown shoes is an excellent choice.  If you dont have one, a leather binder or notebook will work quite well.  Youll need something to carry your materials and of course some paper to take notes.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Other Frequently Asked Questions
         <br/>
         <h2>
         </h2>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/InterviewWearFAQ.jpg">
          <img alt="Interview Wear FAQ" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-952" height="100" src="http://www.jobkaster.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/InterviewWearFAQ.jpg" width="850"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h4>
         Should Interview Clothing be Dry Cleaned?
        </h4>
        <p>
         As long as everything looks good and smells clean, you shouldnt have to get it professionally dry cleaned.  Just make sure it was washed sometime in the last 3 months.
        </p>
        <p>
         Steaming your suit is the best way to remove wrinkles.  Your shirt should most definitely be ironed and your shoes freshly polished.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Can I Wear Cologne to my Interview?
        </h4>
        <p>
         After interviewing hundreds of HR Reps, one of the number one complaints heard was that the candidate was wearing too much cologne or perfume.  Because of this, it is recommended to not wear cologne on your interviews, but I go against the grain on this one.  I typically wear much less as to not be overbearing, but I still want to smell nice.  If you are going to wear cologne, just dab a small amount your chest at the base of your throat.  This will allow the cologne to commingle with your pheromones for the perfect smell.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Conclusion
        </h2>
        <p>
         Research has proven that the way you dress affects the interviewers perception of you at job interviews.
        </p>
        <p>
         Our experts ultimate recommendation is to wear a navy suit, white or light blue shirt, and a solid colored tie.  Youll want to pair that with matching black or brown shoes and belt. Lastly, get a professional haircut, and a clean shave, and you should be good to go.  Rock that interview with the confidence of a well dressed man.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have any questions or comments, please leave them below, and we can add to this resource!
        </p>
