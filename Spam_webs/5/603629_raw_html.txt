       <h1>
        <a href="http://drivinginertia.com/586/biosphere-2-is-dying/">
         Biosphere 2 is Dying
        </a>
       </h1>
       <div class="meta">
        <div class="date">
         <span class="day">
          7
         </span>
         Dec
         <br/>
         <strong>
          2011
         </strong>

        <div class="metaRight">
         Posted in:
         <a href="http://drivinginertia.com/category/arizona/" rel="category tag">
          Arizona
         </a>
         <br/>
         By
         <a href="http://drivinginertia.com/author/paul-and-lisa/" rel="author" title="Posts by Paul and Lisa">
          Paul and Lisa
         </a>
         <img src="http://drivinginertia.com/wp-content/themes/wpanniversary/images/ico_comments.png"/>
         <a href="http://drivinginertia.com/586/biosphere-2-is-dying/#comments">
          <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="586 http://drivinginertia.com/?p=586">
           9 Comments
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        If you grew up when we grew up, chances are you learned about the
        <a href="http://www.b2science.org/">
         Biosphere 2
        </a>
        project in science class. Maybe you even read about it in
        <em>
         Ranger Rick
        </em>
        magazine. It was a hugelyambitiousproject  recreate the earth (aka Biosphere 1) so that people could live inside entirely self-sufficient. Some called it a failure because people only lived in there for two entire years, but they were calorie andoxygendeprived. However, many scientists call it a success because of the amount of knowledge gained by theexperiment. We vote it as a success.
       </p>
       <p>
        It was interesting to visit. In the magazines growing up, the entire structure radiated newness and cleanliness. Now, there are rusty spots, the paint is pealing away  its dying. Biosphere 1 is kicking Biosphere 2s ass.
       </p>
       <p>
        However, it was most interesting to hear the insider stories. Rumor has it that two factions formed during the long mission and there are still members of the groups that refuse to speak to each other! And supposedly there was at least one Biosphere hook-up  two of the original team were married after the mission according to our guide. Also according to our guide, the second experiment ended because the knowledge they were gaining was the same as the knowledge gained during the first experiment. Because of this, it was cut short.
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biosphere_2#Second_mission">
         Wikipedia, however, tells a different story
        </a>
        : federalmarshalsserved the management team with a retraining order, removing them from the site. This pushed the mission into chaos.
       </p>
       <p>
        The structure is now being used for other experiments, notably the
        <a href="http://leo.b2science.org/">
         LEOexperiment
        </a>
        that is going on now that hopes to learn more about soil formation and the interaction between rain and soil. Its nowwhollyowned by the University of Arizona, and its evidently pretty expensive to keep up 
        <a href="http://www.b2science.org/visitor/admission">
         tickets for the tour are $20
        </a>
        .
       </p>
