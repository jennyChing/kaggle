        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Copywriting Q&amp;A: How to Write a Great About Us Page
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-02-23T08:00:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 23, 2015
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://filthyrichwriter.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Nicki Krawczyk
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://filthyrichwriter.com/copywriting-qa-how-to-write-a-great-about-page/#comments">
           2 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <em>
          <img alt="How to Write an About Us Page" class="alignnone wp-image-3337 size-full" height="420" src="http://filthyrichwriter.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Dollarphotoclub_49980700.jpg" width="750"/>
          <br/>
          Theres one page on every companys site that many people visitbut that the company virtually forgets about. And this one page can mean the difference between customers trusting the company and making purchases versus, well, not. Its the About Us page and if you want to learn how to write a great one, read on
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Todays question comes from John C. who asks,
         <strong>
          My company is redoing our corporate site and they gave me the Our Company page to write. Im pretty new so maybe this is kind of a throwaway task for me, but is there anything I can do to impress them?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its understandable to think that an Our Company or About Us page is a throwaway project, since thats how many companies treat it. But, oh how wrong they are!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          About Us pages are actually some of the most-visited pages on a companys website
         </strong>
         , especially among people who havent interacted much with that company before. (Read: people who havent purchased yet.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Potential future customers people visit an About Us page to
         <strong>
          determine if this is a company they can trust and theyd feel comfortable purchasing from
         </strong>
         , and potential job applicants view the About Us page to
         <strong>
          see if its the kind of company thats worth applying to
         </strong>
         . Both of these are very important audiences, and all of their needs are very important to meet.
        </p>
        <p>
         In truth, the About Us page is one of the most important pages on a site, so when its your job to write it, you have a great opportunity to shine!
        </p>
        <p>
         Before we get into how to write them, I just want to make a note about what theyre called.
         <strong>
          Some companies call them About Us pages, while others call them Our Company, Our Team, or even Who We Are
         </strong>
         . For the most part, theyre all the same thing. The only difference might come in if a company is separating the content onto two pages; say, instead of one About Us page, they have an Our Team page and an Our Mission page. For the sake of brevity, Im going to continue to just refer to it as an About Us page.
        </p>
        <p>
         The About Us page has a couple of key missions:
         <strong>
          to communicate the companys mission and values, to establish its trustworthiness, to highlight any key players on the team, and to give some idea as to its personality.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         When I say highlight the companys mission and values, understand that I dont just mean literally writing out a mission statement and some bullet points for values. I mean
         <strong>
          writing about who they are, why they do what they do, and why they see that as important in the world
         </strong>
         . This is really the companys opportunity to tell its story.
        </p>
        <p>
         If it has a good origin story/history, this is a great place to put it here, too. People want to purchase from and work with
         <strong>
          companies that truly believe in what they do and believe in helping people
         </strong>
         with their products or services. The About Us page is the place to convey that.
        </p>
        <p>
         The element of trustworthiness is actually going to come about as a natural byproduct from conveying the mission and vision. You can also help to
         <strong>
          increase the trustworthiness by including real pictures of staff and, when applicable, their stories
         </strong>
         . Seeing the real people behind a company helps people believe in its inherent trustworthiness.
        </p>
        <p>
         If there are
         <strong>
          people at the company who have made an impact on the world
         </strong>
         (founded other companies, written books, given TED speeches, etc.), this can also be a great place to highlight them. Now, you obviously want to feature them with some humility, but their impressive resumes tend to lend some of that status to the company they work for. Think: If this company has
         <em>
          that person
         </em>
         working for them, it must be pretty good!
        </p>
        <p>
         And finally, this is the place to
         <strong>
          convey the companys personality through photos and through your copy
         </strong>
         . Potential job applicants especially want to know if a company is creative, laidback, focused, straightforward, uncompromising, irreverent, challenging or any other possible trait. This is a great place to give an idea of the companys culture, yes, but even more than that, its personality.
        </p>
        <p>
         Putting all of these elements together helps to paint a vivid picture of your company, and
         <strong>
          helps the many people who visit this page understand more about them and decide to interact with them much faster
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Your turn! Have you seen any especially good About Us pages? What made them good? Let us know in the comments below!
        </p>

              </a>
              and
              <a href="http://www.marieforleo.com/about/" rel="nofollow">
               http://www.marieforleo.com/about/
              </a>

Nearly every website you visit is going to have an About Us page (and if they dont, they should), so Id recommend spending a little time just hopping around on the internetgo down some rabbit holes and then check out their About Us pages. And, of course, if you find any especially good ones, please write back and let us know!
