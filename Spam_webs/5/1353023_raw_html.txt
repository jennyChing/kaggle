     <h1>
      White House knew of destroyed emails two months before Congress
     </h1>
     <div class="author">
      By
      <a href="/rick_moran">
       Rick Moran
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="article_body top">
      <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
       <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        Congressional investigators have released tesimony showing that an attorney for the IRS told the Treasury Department counsel of the destroyed emails, who then passed the information on to the White House. The conversations took place in April - two months before the IRS told Congress of the destruction of evidence.
       </span>
      </span>
      <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
       <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/federal-eye/wp/2014/09/09/irs-chiefs-legal-adviser-spread-word-of-missing-e-mails-to-treasury-lawyer/?wpisrc=nl_fed&amp;wpmm=1">
         Washington Post:
        </a>
       </span>
      </span>
      <blockquote>
      </blockquote>
     </div>
     <div class="article_box_ad">
      <script type="text/javascript">
       &lt;!--
				google_ad_client = "pub-4560167926987914";
				google_ad_width = 300;

      </script>
     </div>
     <div class="article_body bottom">
      <blockquote>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Republican lawmakers have suggestedthat the agencycollaboratedwith the Treasury Department and the executives office to control the damage ofthe IRS targeting scandal.TheWhite House learned about the e-mail issues in April, two months before the IRS informed Congress about the matter.
        </span>
       </span>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          In closed-door testimony with House Oversight and Government Reform Committee staff, IRSattorney Catherine Duval said she told Treasury counsel
          <a href="http://www.law.georgetown.edu/faculty/stott-bumsted-hannah.cfm" target="_blank">
           Hannah Stott-Bumsted
          </a>
          about the missinge-mails, which were requested by congressional committees. A Fox News affiliate first revealedthe conversation in
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g6h2Y_LWv9A&amp;feature=youtu.be" target="_blank">
           a report Monday
          </a>
          .
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          Duvall and Stott-Bumsted worked together at aD.C. law firm before joining the government. Theyre also friends, according to Duvall.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          I was seeking to have a conversation with a colleague, Duvall said. I think that talking to appropriate colleagues about issues that youre working on can sharpen your thinking.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          Duvall added: I dont think that Commissioner Koskinen would be surprised that I was talking to Ms. Stott-Bumsted. But Commissioner Koskinen would not know about particular conversations or the content of particular conversations.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          Duvalls testimony provides a starting point for congressional investigators who want to determine which e-mails couldprovide evidence of wrongdoing, specifically collaboration between the White House, the Treasury Department and the IRS.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          Koskinen has testified that he did not directanyone to talk to Treasury or the White House about the missing e-mails. He practically challenged Rep.Darrell Issa (R-Calif.), who heads the House oversight panel, to prove otherwise.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          When askedwhether Koskinen caused anyone at the White House, Treasury Department or the IRS inspector generals office to find out about the missing e-mails, the IRS chief said: I did not, and if you have any evidence of that, Id be happy to see it.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Duvall's attempt to spin the conversation as being innocent falls flat. The White House had to be intensely interested in information from the IRS scandal, particularly any information that would be damaging. It's hard to believe that the Treasury lawyer - who had a direct conduit to the White House - wouldn't use that connection to give the White House a heads up on something as potentially explosive as thousands of missing emails from a principle target of the investigation.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Koskinen himself may not be responsible for the transfer of information. But he now has the evidence he requested that such a transfer took place. What's he going to do about it?
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Don't hold your breath if you're expecting any disciplinary action against Duvall.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Hat Tip: Ed Lasky
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
     </div>

       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Koskinen himself may not be responsible for the transfer of information. But he now has the evidence he requested that such a transfer took place. What's he going to do about it?
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Don't hold your breath if you're expecting any disciplinary action against Duvall.
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
