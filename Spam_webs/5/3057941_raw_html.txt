      <h1>
       Take Time Out for YOU: Beating work blues
      </h1>
      <span class="date">
       February 14, 2012
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="post-image">
      <a href="http://unashamedlycreative.com.au/2012/02/take-time-out-for-you/">
       <img alt="10 tips poster web" class="attachment-full-thumb wp-post-image" height="448" src="http://unashamedlycreative.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/10-tips-poster-web.jpg" width="317"/>
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="post-entry">
      <p>
       Its fair to say that we all work pretty hard these days, right? And that leads to the occasional bout of work blues.
      </p>
      <p>
       Were constantly connected to work through emails on our phones. Were always chasing down leads or ideas or working on grand plans on the outskirts of what we are trying to achieve.
      </p>
      <p>
       But you have to take time out for you, else you become some husk, some shell of what you need to be and let me tell you, both your productivity and your emotional, mental or physical health could suffer.
      </p>
      <p>
       How do you stop the niggling feeling of what you need to do pressing on you when you try to relax?
      </p>
      <h3>
       I havent exactly worked out how to beat work blues entirely, but here is what I have been trying of late:
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Put that music on loud and walk fast:
        </strong>
        I dont do exercise. When I was in my twenties, this wasnt a problem because I walked everywhere I went. I lost that somewhere along the line (I suspect it was about the same time I started dating people who owned cars) together with my flat stomach. However I have rediscovered the joy of walking to meetings or catch ups, or even just taking ye olde Sansa out for a walk after dinner. It has really, really helped clear my head and help me disconnect my work day (which is in my living room) from my evenings off (which is also usually held in my living room).
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Desk Disconnect:
        </strong>
        Its incredibly easy to get welded to your desk, to eat at it, to sit at it all day and all night. This is why I now make myself get up and go and do something else at regular(ish) intervals. If I finish writing something or the CD needs changing in the background, I take these opportunities to go and do something else for ten minutes. It really does help! My house is certainly cleaner, thats for sure!
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Fear the Creeper:
        </strong>
        When I find myself on Facebook and Twitter and flicking in between, this is time for another break. As addictive, fun and productive feeling as these activities are, its time to remember that beyond the odd link for business, being a major player in the world of Facebook does not a career make. So if you, like me, is finding the need to connect in this way, why not try writing that blog post you have been putting off, or take your scratch pad and plan out that idea or campaign you havent had the time to do yet instead? I find my to do lists are better now that I choose to use
        <a href="https://idonethis.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://idonethis.com/', 'https://idonethis.com/']);">
         https://idonethis.com/
        </a>
        because I get a sense of achievement, but the black art book also helps. Or hell, just put on some very cheddar music and dance with your dog, broom, toddler or mannequin!
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Screw Work:
        </strong>
        The To Do List grows and grows until you feel like youre underneath it trying to breathe, or every minute you spend doing something else means a minute you havent done towards cracking your work into the right shape- sound familiar? The reality is though the human brain is not designed to be connected to the work world 247. Your eyes cannot handle staring at a screen all day, your back isnt designed to be sitting at a desk all the time, your mind loses focus the more it doesnt get to concentrate on what it is doing. Do yourself a favour by taking a break, having a day off, enjoying your mornings or evenings because you will feel better and be more productive.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Follow a Passion:
        </strong>
        If you throw yourself into a creative project or cause, it can really help you gain perspective. As hard as your life may seem at times, being around inspiring people or people who face situations worse than your own can really reset your whoa as me clock pretty damn quick and ignite something a lot more positive. Joining this
        <a href="http://www.meetup.com/Think-Act-Change/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.meetup.com/Think-Act-Change/', ' bunch of spunkrats']);" target="_self">
         bunch of spunkrats
        </a>
        or working on your own idea at
        <a href="http://www.planbig.com.au" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.planbig.com.au', 'www.planbig.com.au']);">
         www.planbig.com.au
        </a>
        could be just the injection of happy you need!
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Be Creative:
        </strong>
        It can be tempting to over think things, not allow time for creativity, to be caught up in day to day life but the process is only as hard as you make it, trust me-
        <a href="http://unashamedlycreative.com.au/2011/06/no-excuses-no-none/" target="_self">
         no excuses!
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        The standard list of what you should and shouldnt do (which I havent quite cracked as routine yet):
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Cut out caffeine (makes the motor rev)
       </li>
       <li>
        Reduce your alcohol intake (makes the motor rev the day after)
       </li>
       <li>
        Eat better
       </li>
       <li>
        Try something new (head
        <a href="http://www.openshed.com.au/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.openshed.com.au/', 'here']);" target="_self">
         here
        </a>
        , check out stuff, try it and feel awesome doing it)
       </li>
       <li>
        Play with a pet (thank you
        <a href="http://unashamedlycreative.com.au/gibson-t-dog-morale-officer/" target="_blank" title="Gibson T Dog  Morale Officer">
         Gibson
        </a>
        for being so truly wagtastic on hard days!)
       </li>
       <li>
        Listen to music via possibly the best internet streaming idea ever invented, music by mood on
        <a href="http://www.stereomood.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.stereomood.com/', 'Stereomood.']);" target="_self">
         Stereomood.
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       The bottom line, take time out for YOU!
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="http://unashamedlycreative.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/10-tips-poster-web.jpg">
        <img alt="beating work blues" class="size-medium wp-image-1261 alignleft" height="300" src="http://unashamedlycreative.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/10-tips-poster-web-212x300.jpg" title="beating work blues" width="212"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       If you are feeling stressed and need more help, also contact the super awesome people at
       <a href="http://www.mentalhealth.asn.au/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mentalhealth.asn.au/', 'Mental Health Association NSW.']);" target="_blank">
