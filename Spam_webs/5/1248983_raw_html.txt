       <h1>
        Green Deal cashbacks see surge in applications in first week
       </h1>
       <div class="name">
        Energy efficiency news  by Thomas McMullan
       </div>
       <div class="date">
        18th June 2014
       </div>
       <div class="summary" id="ctl00_MainContent_ctl00_divSummary">
        Recent changes to the Green Deal are showing early signs of success, with an estimated 2.61 million issued to households in cashbacks during the renovated schemes first week.
       </div>
       <div id="ctl00_MainContent_ctl00_NewsContentLoader">
        The
        <strong>
         Department for Energy and Climate Change
        </strong>
        has reported that 1736 applications have been received for the
        <strong>
         Green Deal Home Improvement Fund
        </strong>
        , which now offers households in England and Wales up to 7600 cashback for
        <a href="http://greenwisebusiness.co.uk/energy-efficiency.aspx" title="click for latest energy efficiency news">
         energy efficiency
        </a>
        upgrades.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        The Green Deal, which launched last year and has been described by the Government as the "biggest home improvement programme since the second world war", has got off to a dismal start, falling to its lowest ebb in February when just 1,754 Green Deal Plans were progressed. Industry has been urging the Government to throw a lifeline to the Green Deal.
        <div>
         <br/>
        </div>
        <div>
         The new 7600 incentive offered to households is split into a number of parts; 1000 is given for installing two measures from an approved list of renovations, 100 is available as a refund for the initial assessment and 6000 is on offer for the expensive procedure of installing solid wall insulation. Those whove bought their property within 12 months of application to the scheme are also eligible for a further 500.
        </div>
        <div>
         <br/>
        </div>
        <div>
         "Weve changed the Green Deal to make it simpler and faster for people to make their homes more energy efficient," Energy and Climate Change Secretary, Ed Davey, said. "I want as many people as possible to benefit from this unmissable offer. It wont be around forever, so sign up now and get your home ready for the winter."
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Today's figures have been welcomed by organisations looking to grow the number of households taking up energy efficiency measures.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          "Step in the right direction"
         </strong>
        </div>
        <div>
         Philip Sellwood, chief executive, Energy Saving Trust, said itsEnergy Saving Advice Service had had "a big uplift in calls from householders" since the cashback had been launched.
        </div>
        <div>
         <br/>
        </div>
        <div>
         "Theres certainly a long way to go but this is a step in the right direction. Ultimately its got to be as straightforward as signing up for Sky or Virgin. Its not about finance deals, its about people living in warmer, cosier and healthier homes that are cheaper to run."
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Richard Twinn, policy and public affairso at the UK Green Building Council, added: "Its promising to see a huge amount of interest in the new Green Deal incentives within the first week. 2.6 million represents more than half of what was spent during the original cashback scheme which was available for over a year  clearly demonstrating that the word is out.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         "But we must not be too quick to celebrate the success of the new fund  it still remains to be seen if this level of enthusiasm can be maintained. Without a long term demand driver such as Stamp Duty, its unlikely this is the beginning of a truly mass market for energy efficiency."
         <br/>
         <br/>
         The changes to The Green Deal were launched on Monday June 9. A national network for Green Deal installers said at the time that it had been seeing "a significant increase in enquiries" following the announcement.
