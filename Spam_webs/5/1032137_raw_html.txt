     <h1>
      Kratom  New Wonder Drug Or Dangerous Substance
     </h1>
     <small class="meta">
      <time datetime="%3$s" pubdate="">
       Apr-22-2015
       <br/>
      </time>
     </small>
     <figure class="post-image">
      <img alt="Kratom" class="attachment-post-image wp-post-image" height="300" src="http://www.serenityrecovery.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/640px-Mitragyna_speciosa111-500x300.jpeg" width="500"/>
     </figure>
     <h2>
      What Is Kratom?
     </h2>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       Kratom is a the new all natural drug on the scene. Its possible benefits have been seen in peoples all over Southeast Asia. Slowly, it is being introduced here in the United States. But some are worried of its possible effects. Most people however are wiling to wait and see if this new drug has any risks at all, or if it could be even good for you.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       Kratom is a tree that is in the same family as the coffee tree. It is native to the Southeast Asia region, where its leaves are used for there medicinal properties. It is typically chewed in that part of the world to help uplift mood, and treat health problems. In certain parts of the region, like Thailand, it has been banned for several decades. The ban on it began due to its popularity, and the fact that it was keeping them from making tax revenue off their distribution of
       <a href="http://www.serenityrecovery.com/drug-info/heroin-a-rising-tide-of-overdose/" title="Heroin  The Rising Tide Of Overdose">
        opium
       </a>
       . The effects and safety of kratom have not been well-studied, however what is known comes from generations of use by the local people.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       In a majority of Southeast Asian countries, kratom is not seen as a drug, and carries no stigmas. Users dont usually start chewing the plant until the age of 25, and have been known to chew it the rest of their lives. The average number of leaves chewed can be anywhere from 10 to 60 per day, and almost 70% of men in southern Thailand chew kratom daily. It has been used as a therapeutic cultural medicine for diarrhea, a treatment for opium dependence, and rarely as a way to increase the duration of sex.
      </span>
     </p>
     <h3>
      Could Kratom Be Bad For You?
     </h3>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       While no proof, as far as clinical trials are concerned, exists that kratom can relieve opioid dependence, it has been seen as a sedative itself. In low doses it can be a stimulant, allowing you to be more alert, and to have more physical energy. At higher doses though it is a sedative with euphoric effects. It will decrease sensitivity to pain, put you in a sense of calm and relaxation, or even put you into a pleasant dream-like state.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       However, many adverse effects have been reported as well. Side effects from long-term kratom use have been darkening of the skin, constipation, loss of appetite, and weight loss. In certain cases there has even been reports of bowel obstruction. When users have quit using kratom, they have reported withdrawal symptoms. These were either irritability, diarrhea, and runny nose. These symptoms of withdrawal tended to be short-lived and mild though. One reason many people might experience these effects is due to dosage. At higher levels of use the effects can be more sever, even leading to psychosis and hallucinations. High dosage can usually occur from one of two things: Either you have taken too much, or the particular batch of krotam that you are taking is more potent then normal.
      </span>
     </p>
     <h3>
      Is Krotam Even Legal?
     </h3>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       While krotam is still legal in the United States, some lawmakers have proposed, or even passed, legislation due to the plant. In Indiana, state legislatures passed a law adding two of the main alkaloids found in kratom illegal, and on their list of controlled substances. While this law passed, kratom is still legal in the state. Other states have proposed similar bills by either adding certain compounds found in kratom to their lists of controlled substances, putting an age limit on use, or labeling it as a synthetic cannabinoid.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       Most advocacy groups have argued against criminalization of kratom. Stating that there has not been enough research and study on the plant to warrant strait prohibition. Few records show any health or social negatives from using kratom, but law officials are still trying to take action against it before proof can be given one way or another. While very little to no research exists on the negative effects of kratom, it might be best to wait until it is. While there may be positive effects found from the plant, there still could be many negatives. It might be best to just wait and see before using kratom.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      If you have found yourself addicted to kratom, please
      <a href="http://www.serenityrecovery.com/contact/">
       call us
      </a>
      . Our qualified staff is here to help and assist you in your recovery.
     </p>

       Wikipedia
      </a>

     </p>
