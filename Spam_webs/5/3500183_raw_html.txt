              Many people will say that attending community college is either
              <em>
               the absolute best
              </em>
              or the
              <em>
               absolute worst decision
              </em>
              of their life. There are very few levelheaded opinions that measure the benefits and costs of attending a community college. I will try to avoid the obvious,and try to focus on my own experiences attending a community college.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <em>
                BENEFIT 1  Easier to Develop Relationships with Teachers
               </em>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              The real benefit of small class sizes is that it makes it easier to develop meaningful relationships with your teachers. Being close to your teachers will not only help you focus on the material, but it will also help you gain insight on the type professional work that the teacher did before they started teaching. After attending office hours, I found out that one of my community college teachers used to work for AT&amp;T. He provided me with some invaluable advice on what he would have done differently as he got his degree in business. I learnt more talking to him during office hours than I did sitting in his class.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <em>
                BENEFIT 2  You Have Extra Time
               </em>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              I acknowledge that having extra time can be botha benefit and a cost. This is all dependent on whether you spend your extra time being productive. For some reason, teachers will often say that, For every hour you spend in class, you will have to spend two hours outside of class studying. In my own personal experience, this is absolutely
              <em>
               not true
              </em>
              so long as you efficiently use your studying time. Regardless, people complain that they have so much free time that they become bored. Dont let yourself be bored!! Find productive ways to spend your extra time; finda job you love, volunteerwith an organization youre passionate about, workout, or read books that you're interested in. Its really, really, really important
              <em>
               not
              </em>
              to cut out your gaming, Netflix watching, spending time looking at mason jars on Pinterest, or any unproductive hobbies that you have. Find a balance between your hobbies and productive activities.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <em>
                COST 1  No Campus Life
               </em>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              This isnt always true for every community college campus. However, in my first year of attending community college I didnt join clubs and didnt take an initiative to meet people on campus. Therefore, I hard a hard time making friends and felt like I was missing out on the freshmen experience. During my second year I joined clubs and made an initiative to make friends and felt less lonely. If youre an extrovert I would suggest talking to people in your classes! If that sounds too awkward, join a club and meet people with common interests. Make your own campus life with the people you meet.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <em>
                COST 2  The Stigma
               </em>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              In my experience, the
              <em>
               worst
              </em>
              part of attending community college is the stigma behind it. Many people make harsh generalizations about you simply on your decision to attend a community college. When I was a senior in high school and would tell people that I was attending a community college I would get one of two responses.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Response 1: why? I thought you were smart!!
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              This response is an assumption that everyone who attends community college is stupid. Since these people conveniently neglect the benefits of attending a community college, I suggest that you conveniently neglect their ignorance.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Response 2: that is SUCH A SMART decision.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              People who would say this are usually well intentioned and want you to feel better with attending a community college. I felt like this response isa little patronizing. I didnt want people to feel sorry for me, but rather just acknowledge that Im not going down the same academic path that they are.
             </p>
             <p>
              Its easy to initially feel insecure about attending a community college, especially as a graduating senior. In my experience, the benefits of attending a community collegeclearly outweighedthe cost.
             </p>

                    Why Attending a Community College is the Right Choice
                   </a>

                  <!-- Excerpt -->
                  <div class="summary-excerpt">
                   <p>
                    <span style="font-size:18px">
                     For high school students, its the time of the year where people start to decide which university theyre going to attend. Many schools create the infamous
                    </span>
                    <em>
                     decision day
                    </em>
                    <span style="font-size:18px">
                     Facebook page for students to publicly announce which university theyre going to attend for the next four years. As someone who decided to attend a community college;
                    </span>
                    <em>
                     I hated the end of the year,
                    </em>
                    <span style="font-size:18px">
                     because it made me feel insecure about attending a community college. Now that Ive transferred from my community college to the university of my dreams, I now realize that attending a community college was one of the
                    </span>
                    <em>
                     best decisions
                    </em>
                    <span style="font-size:18px">
                     I have ever made.
                    </span>
                   </p>

                  <div class="summary-excerpt">
                   <p>
                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">

                    </span>
                    <em>
                     not
                    </em>
                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">
                     listen to them. However, look at the statistics; the overall transfer rate at Stanford is 1.9% (Note: This number includes
                    </span>
                    <em>
                     all
                    </em>
                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">
                     transfer students...not just community college transfers). S
                    </span>
                    <em>
                     tatistically

                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">
                     it's really, really, really,
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">
                     <em>
                      hard
                     </em>
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">
                     to get into Stanford from a Community College.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">
                     But it is
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size:15.3333330154419px">
                     <em>
                      NOT IMPOSSIBLE
