       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 1: How it Works
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="S10ZWB9GHFK7QWH" data-entry-url="/id/Gluphite-Make-A-Key-Chain-Pencil/step1/How-it-Works/" id="photoset-S10ZWB9GHFK7QWH">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-S10ZWB9GHFK7QWH" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FH3/VBI8/GHFK7QWS/FH3VBI8GHFK7QWS.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FH3VBI8GHFK7QWS" data-notes="" href="/file/FH3VBI8GHFK7QWS" id="photoset-link-F67EYFHGHFK7QX2-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="">
           <img alt="Picture of How it Works" class="photoset-photo id_FH3VBI8GHFK7QWS " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FH3/VBI8/GHFK7QWS/FH3VBI8GHFK7QWS.MEDIUM.jpg" style="height:500px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        Gluphite is a simple mixture of Gorilla Glue and carbon graphite. It hardens into a fairly stout plastic that has some interesting qualities including working well as a pencil body and lead.
        <br/>
        <br/>

       <a id="stepanchor-step2">
       </a>
       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 2: Materials And Mixing
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SU7MZU0GHFK7QX0" data-entry-url="/id/Gluphite-Make-A-Key-Chain-Pencil/step2/Materials-And-Mixing/" id="photoset-SU7MZU0GHFK7QX0">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SU7MZU0GHFK7QX0" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FUQ/PWE3/GHFK7QZF/FUQPWE3GHFK7QZF.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FUQPWE3GHFK7QZF" data-notes="" href="/file/FUQPWE3GHFK7QZF" id="photoset-link-FPBEXQVGHFK7R1J-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="">
           <img alt="Picture of Materials And Mixing" class="photoset-photo id_FUQPWE3GHFK7QZF " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FUQ/PWE3/GHFK7QZF/FUQPWE3GHFK7QZF.MEDIUM.jpg" style="height:500px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        <strong>
         Materials
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Gorilla Glue or Elmers white glue
        <br/>
        Carbon Graphite available as lubricating graphite from a hardware store.
        <br/>
        Corn starch
        <br/>
        100% silicone caulk
        <br/>
        Plastic mixing cup and mixing stick
        <br/>
        Measuring spoon
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Mixing Gluphite
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Mix 3 measures of Carbon Graphite by volume with enough water to form a thick paste. Add 2 measures of white glue and mix well. Spoon the mix onto some polyethylene plastic on a flat surface. Spread it out 3/16" to 1/4" thick at a diameter large enough for the pencil.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Unfortunately I have not found a way to get it to set up faster, so it usually takes 3 or 4 days to totally harden up. While drying, it will shrink and warp a bit.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Because it has so much carbon which strengthens the plastic, it also works quite well as a pencil lead. It is hard enough to not rub off on your hands but soft enough to make smear less lines on paper that are light but very readable.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
