      <h1>
       See the Golden Gate Bridge From Golden Gate Park
      </h1>

       Beyond its acclaimed art collection, the parks de Young Museum offers a unique glimpse of the San Francisco icon

      <div class="byline">
       A lush green strip that covers more than 1,000 acres, from the center of San Francisco to its far western edge, Golden Gate Park was once a great sand bank of windswept, rolling dunes.
      </div>
      <div class="chapter-body">
       <p>
        Now, its home to lakes, meadows, museums, monuments and sports fieldswhich is a far better trade. While it doesnt count the
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/california/business-san-francisco/golden-gate-bridge-13932803">
         Golden Gate Bridge
        </a>
        among its attractions, you can squeeze in a view while youre visiting the park, if you follow this advice:
       </p>
       <p>
        The
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/california/business-san-francisco/de-young-museum-bouquets-to-art-2013-304458217" target="_blank">
         de Young Museum
        </a>
        was founded in
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/california/business-san-francisco/golden-gate-park-263202898">
         Golden Gate Park
        </a>
        in 1895, and was renovated to great acclaim in 2005. This copper-clad museum showcases collections of 17th through 20th century American art, as well as art of the Pacific, Africa and Native Americans. Make sure that when you visit you allocate plenty of time to see it all, even if it means grabbing nourishment in the de Young Caf, which features ingredients grown within 150 miles of the museum.
       </p>
       <div class="extra green">
        <h2>
         <span class="icon-Icon-Extras">
         </span>
         INSIDER TIP
        </h2>
        The
        <a href="https://deyoung.famsf.org/" target="_blank">
         de Young Museum
        </a>
        is well worth the $10 general admission fee, but admission is free on the first Tuesday of the month. This isnt a secret to locals, so to get the best views from the observation deck, head straight there when the museum opens.
       </div>
       <p>
        The one place in the museum that requires a bit of planning for premium viewing is the Hamon Education Tower Observation Deck, on the ninth floor. Spiraling out of the museums roof, the deck has floor-to-ceiling windows that provide a birds-eye, 360-degree view of Golden Gate Park and the western end of
        <a href="http://www.mapquest.com/us/ca/san-francisco">
         San Francisco
        </a>
        . Cast your gaze north to catch the uppermost spires of the beloved Golden Gate Bridge.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The bridge opened in 1937. Until the museums 2005 redesign, there were no views of the bridge from Golden Gate Park.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        For premium views, avoid days when San Franciscos famed fog blankets the city, but also remember that the deck closes one hour prior to museum closing hours.
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        </span>
       </p>
