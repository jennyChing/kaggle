        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          The writing journey is all about discovering what works bestof for each of us as individual, and very unique, writers. Learning from others isvaluable in helping us glean tips and fit together the puzzle pieces that willform our own writing processes. Today, Id like to share with you ten of thesites that inspire, educate, and help me refine my processplus, theyre run by a bunch of super awesome folks!
          <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-NbhFfynOGl4/UYFcOsmrN6I/AAAAAAAAEkY/QIz4Uce4Q48/s1600/10-of-my-favorite-writing-craft-sites.jpg">
           <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="424" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-NbhFfynOGl4/UYFcOsmrN6I/AAAAAAAAEkY/QIz4Uce4Q48/s640/10-of-my-favorite-writing-craft-sites.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <b>
           1.
          </b>
          <a href="http://jodyhedlund.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">
           Jody Hedlund
          </a>
          :Her spot-on insights into the publishing and marketing processes never fail tooffer me something to chew on. Plus, she has the best post titles in thebusiness!
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           2.
          </b>
          <a href="http://nailyournovel.wordpress.com/" target="_blank">
           Nail YourNovel
          </a>
          : Book doctor Roz Morris, author of the wonderful
          <i>
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004LROOEQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004LROOEQ&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=httpwwwkmweil-20" target="_blank">
            Nail Your Novel
           </a>
          </i>
          , offers an always refreshingtake on the nitty-grits of storytelling.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           3.
          </b>
          <a href="http://thebookshelfmuse.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">
           The Bookshelf Muse
          </a>
          : Run by Angela Ackerman and Becca Puglisi, authors of
          <i>
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00822WM2M/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00822WM2M&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=httpwwwkmweil-20" target="_blank">
            The Emotion Thesaurus
           </a>
          </i>
          , this site is best known for its insanelyhandy lists of synonyms for everything from facial features to colors to (youguessed it!) emotions.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           4.
          </b>
          <a href="http://porteranderson.com/blog/" target="_blank">
           Porter Anderson
          </a>
          : His Writing on the Ether series is a fun, quirky, and alwaysinsightful look into the goings-on of the book world.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           5.
          </b>
          <a href="http://www.thecreativepenn.com/" target="_blank">
           The Creative Penn
          </a>
          : Joanna Penns site is fast becoming the go-to stop for independentauthors who are trying to understand all the ins and outs of the system. She offersgreat advice on publishing and marketing and a super-helpful transparency abouther own journey as an indie.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           6.
          </b>
          <a href="http://mysterywritingismurder.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">
           Mystery Writing Is Murder
          </a>
          : Elizabeth Spann Craig dishes out both genre-specific andgeneral advice, along with a great round-up of writing-related Twitter posts.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           7.
          </b>
          <a href="http://thewritepractice.com/" target="_blank">
           The WritePractice
          </a>
          : This lovely site, founded by the always gracious Joe Bunting, offerssound advice and the forum-like opportunity to put writing lessons intopractice.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           8.
