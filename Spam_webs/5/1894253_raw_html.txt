              <div class="" id="watch-description-text">
               <p id="eow-description">
                Overcoming addiction can be difficult. Many people can tell you how to overcome addiction, but what does research say?
                <br/>
                find out here:
                <a class="yt-uix-redirect-link" dir="ltr" href="http://truthofaddiction.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://truthofaddiction.com/">
                 http://truthofaddiction.com/
                </a>
                This will not only show you how to break a porn addiction, but also how to stop an addiction that may be lesser-known or misunderstood.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Click the link below to discover common misconceptions
                <br/>
                <a class="yt-uix-redirect-link" dir="ltr" href="http://truthofaddiction.com/10-lies/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://truthofaddiction.com/10-lies/">
                 http://truthofaddiction.com/10-lies/
                </a>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Do you need help overcoming addiction? There are many places you can turn to. Rehabs, detox centers, the 12 steps, AA and some online programs.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                The problem is that many of these methods are not backed up by sufficient evidence or research. Ever since the 1950s addiction treatment has remained relatively the same even though scientists have made huge strides into the field of addiction treatment.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                How to overcome an addiction is not really what we should be focusing on, but instead how to manage addiction. There are simple techniques you and anyone can do at home that have shown to boost success.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                But first we should clear up some misconceptions about addiction.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                A misconception has been circulating for the last 50 years: that addiction only affects those that are weak willed or morally wrong. This is far from the truth. People may think that addicts just need to muster up enough will-power so that they can overcome their addiction, whatever it may be.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                You may also hear people say that they have an addictive personality or pernicious character flaw. This isn't true either.
                <br/>
                As addiction worsens, it affects the brain in a way that really helps to explain why addicts are do what they do. Why they often break promise after promise and why many of them appear to be weak willed or unmotivated, yet they are actually quite motivated to obtain their drug of choice.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                The addicted brain is distinctly different from the non-addicted brain  Addiction is tied to changes in brain structure and function (Science Magazine, 1997)."
                <br/>
                <br/>
                "...The common view that drug addicts are resistant because of pernicious personality characteristics that are part of their condition. Denial is often regarded to be a trait of 'chemical dependency.' In fact, extensive research has revealed relatively few consistent personality characteristics among drug users, nor do studies of defense mechanisms suggest any unique pattern associated with addictive behavior" (Miller, William R. / Motivational Interviewing)
                <br/>
                Conventional treatment options such as AA, the 12 steps or acute treatment are all designed to treat a morally wrong "defect in character," as AA calls it.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                These methods are only one dimensional.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                We need to consider much more...
                <br/>
                These facilities do not address the biochemical issue in the brain. Although a great thing about these therapy groups is the support system that one can gain.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                If you want to overcome an addiction, a holistic and multidimensional method should be used along with evidence-based materials to help individuals break free from that addiction forever.
