         What is an Earthship?
        </h1>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>

       <div class="container">
        <div class="row-fluid">
         <div class="span8 content panel-panel">
          <div class="panel-panel-inner">
           <div class="field-featured-image">
            <img class="medium" src="http://www.openethical.com/sites/default/files/styles/medium/public/243748_10150952887799724_2090619504_o.jpg?itok=xOxjGCr2"/>
           </div>
           <div class="field-body">
            <p>
             Earthships are radically sustainable houses first designed and built by
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Reynolds_(architect)">
              Mike Reynolds
             </a>
             over 40 years ago. They can be made almost anywhere and have minimum impact on the environment. Please see
             <a href="http://earthship.com/">
              Earthship Biotecture
             </a>
             for more information.
            </p>
            <p>
             We are grateful for the images supplied courtesy of
             <a href="http://earthship.com/">
              Earthship Biotecture
             </a>
             - check out their
             <a href="https://www.facebook.com/earthship">
              facebook page
             </a>
             for more.
            </p>
            <h3>
             An off the grid home
            </h3>
            <p>
             Fully functional Earthships do not need to be connected to the grid. They are completely self -sustaining. They generate their own electricity, collect their own water, treat their own sewage and can even accommodate indoor vegetable gardens! Read more about
             <a href="http://earthship.com/retrofit-your-building" title="retrofitting your home">
              retrofitting your building
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" class="full media-element file-default large" src="http://www.openethical.com/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/774256_10151276921624724_339592653_o.jpg?itok=H4C91sgu" title=""/>
            </p>
            <h3>
             Built using recycled materials
            </h3>
            <h4>
             Tyres
            </h4>
            <p>
             The main structural walls are made out of used tires. These are packed full of earth to create a thermic mass that retains heat in the winter and cools the room in the summer.
            </p>
            <h4>
             Bottles and cans
            </h4>
            <p>
             Non-structural walls can be filled out using bottles and cans. This reduces the need for cement and / or bricks and has the added benefit of creating beautiful light patterns.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" class="full media-element file-default large" src="http://www.openethical.com/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/470452_10151226829469724_1508213856_o.jpg?itok=OGFk2go3" title=""/>
            </p>
            <h3>
             Self-heating and cooling
            </h3>
            <p>
             The thermic mass created by the thick walls and / or positioning against a hillside, together with exposure to sunlight and carefully planned ventilation, keeps the house warm in winter and cool in summer  whatever the climate!
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" class="full media-element file-default large" src="http://www.openethical.com/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/471549_10150917121289724_2109547567_o.jpg?itok=n0FuvA00" title=""/>
            </p>
            <h3>
             Generate your own electricity
            </h3>
            <h4>
             Solar panels
            </h4>
            <p>
             Photovoltaic cells can easily provide all of a familys power requirements.
            </p>
            <h4>
             Wind
            </h4>
            <p>
             In windy areas it can be cheaper to supplement photovoltaic cells with a series of wind turbines.
            </p>
            <h4>
             The control room
            </h4>
            <p>
             Electricity generated by the sun and wind is managed in the control room. Here a battery is used to store energy and electricity is converted from AC to DC current. All of the ventilation systems are also managed here.
            </p>
            <h3>
             Collect your own water
            </h3>
            <p>
             The roof is designed as a water collecting system. Water runs into the main tank and is then distributed throughout the house. Grey water from the shower and the taps is then filtered using plants and used for the toilet.
            </p>
            <h3>
             Treat your own sewage
            </h3>
            <p>
             Black water from the toilet runs out through a series of pits and is cleaned using a filtering system and more plants. No sewage escapes into the environment.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="" class="full media-element file-default large" src="http://www.openethical.com/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Food.jpg?itok=v5gHoQt6" title=""/>
            </p>
            <h3>
             Grow your own food
            </h3>
            <p>
             The light filled hallways make for excellent greenhouses and can enable food production for the entire household.
            </p>
