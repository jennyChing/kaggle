         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Brush With Greatness
         </h2>
         <p>
          With the overwhelmingly vast array of hairbrushes available today, finding the shape, size, and bristles that are right for you can leave you pulling your hair out. Whether you have waist-length waves or a perfectly sleek pixie cut, there's a hairbrush that can make styling your strands much easier. These tips come straight from the industry's best stylists  they'll equip you with all the knowledge you need to accomplish your mane mission.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="2" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/mason-pearson-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Oval+Brush" id="slide-img-2" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/mason-pearson-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="slideshow-sponsored-link img">
          </div>
          <span class="eh-media-credit" itemprop="author">
          </span>
          <span class="slide-pinterest">
          </span>
          <meta content="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/mason-pearson-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          <meta content="" itemprop="caption"/>
         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Oval Brush
         </h2>
         <p>
          This tool employs a combination of nylon and boar bristles for styling during a blow-dry or to generally detangle and smooth strands. The mixture of bristles and shape of the brush also make it a great scalp stimulator. Keep this brush style in your arsenal for smoothing and shine and to distribute product, says celebrity stylist
          <a href="http://www.tedgibsonbeauty.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
           <strong>
            Ted Gibson
           </strong>
          </a>
          , who's worked with Angelina Jolie and Anne Hathaway.
         </p>
         <p>
          Also, this is the only brush you need to style bangs, whether you have side-swept or blunt fringe, says Eva Scrivo, owner of Eva Scrivo Salon and the author of
          <em>
           Eva Scrivo on Beauty.
          </em>
          "This brush produces a shiny finish that hugs the head," she says, "and is the solution to the 'mall bang.'"
         </p>
         <p>
          Try Mason Pearson's iconic
          <a href="http://shop.nordstrom.com/s/mason-pearson-popular-mixture-nylon-boar-bristle-brush-for-long-coarse-to-normal-hair/3257677" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
           <strong>
            "Popular Mixture" nylon and boar bristle model
           </strong>
          </a>
          ($170).
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="3" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/conair-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Ceramic+Round+Brush" id="slide-img-3" src=""/>
          <noscript>

         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Ceramic Round Brush
         </h2>
         <p>
          "Ceramic round brushes are designed to add smoothness while creating shape during your styling," says
          <a href="http://canalesalon.com/?page_id=18" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
           <strong>
            James Williams
           </strong>
          </a>
          , head stylist at the Canale Salon in Beverly Hills, Calif.  he's worked on the famed manes of Amanda Bynes and Kyra Sedgwick. Use this brush style to create lift for thick to normal hair textures.
         </p>
         <p>
          Celebrity stylist
          <a href="http://www.sarahpotempa.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
           <strong>
            Sarah Potempa
           </strong>
          </a>
          recommends selecting a brush with boar bristles and a ceramic coating on the inside of the core, where the bristles are attached. "Because the ceramic heats up on the inside, if you allow the hair to 'set' while rolled in the brush, you'll get more volume," she explains.
         </p>
         <p>
          Try
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.drugstore.com/conair-brush-tourmaline-ceramic-red-round-brush-medium/qxp161182" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
            Conair's Tourmaline Ceramic Medium Round Porcupine Brush
           </a>
          </strong>
          ($9.99).
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="4" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/fekkai-round-brush-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Round+Wood+Brush+" id="slide-img-4" src=""/>

         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Round Wood Brush
         </h2>
         <p>
          "This brush can handle any hair texture, length, or density," says David Babaii, who has worked on Liv Tyler's and Kate Hudson's hair. Babaii explains that this style brush is the key to a shiny, sleek blow-dry, if you keep the brush moving and concentrate the heat on the ends.
         </p>
         <p>
          It's a perfect "finishing" brush  that is, your hair should be 80 percent dry before you use it, says Matt Fugate, a stylist at Sally Hershberger Salon in New York City.
         </p>
         <p>
          Try
          <a href="http://www.fekkai.com/styling-tools/large-round-brush/00730870717295,default,pd.html" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
           <strong>
            Frederic Fekkai's Large Round Brush
           </strong>
          </a>
          ($65).
         </p>
        </li>

         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Vented Brush
         </h2>
         <p>
          These inexpensive plastic brushes are used to speed drying on any length hair. Since these have openings or vents to allow air to flow through, you can dry much faster. And this is an essential brush for anyone with limp, flat hair. "This is the ideal tool to move roots around and create volume while rough-drying  excellent prep for using your wooden round brush," Fugate says. A smaller brush, he says, will make it easier to create lift and smoothness.
         </p>
         <p>
          Try
          <a href="http://www.bigdaddybeauty.com/p-3915-spornette-metro-antistatic-tunnel-vent-hair-brush-9020mt.aspx" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
           <strong>
            Spornette's Anti-Static Metro Tunnel Brush
           </strong>
          </a>
          ($10).
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="6" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/sarah-potempa-brush-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Square+Paddle+Brush+" id="slide-img-6" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/sarah-potempa-brush-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg"/>

         </span>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Square Paddle Brush
         </h2>
         <p>
          Ideal for daily detangling and to distribute oils along the hair shaft, these brushes are typically made of wood or plastic, with a cushioned rubber pad and synthetic bristles.
         </p>
         <p>
          Paddle brushes, which are good for all hair types, are strong enough to tame thick, curly locks and gentle enough for fine hair. Be advised, however: "This brush is not used for finishing," Fugate says. "It's for prepping hair only."
         </p>
         <p>
          Scrivo recommends using this brush to comb through thick hair when wet, a job that can be challenging with a comb.
         </p>
         <p>
          Try
          <a href="http://www.sarahpotempa.com/shop/products/power-paddle-brush/" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
           <strong>
            Sarah Potempa's "Power" Paddle Brush
           </strong>
          </a>
          ($25).
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="7" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/sally-beauty-brush-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Teasing+Brush" id="slide-img-7" src=""/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/sally-beauty-brush-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg"/>

         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Teasing Brush
         </h2>
         <p>
          This skinny, specialized tool provides a huge payoff, especially when you're styling your hair for a special occasion. The tight bristles push hair toward the roots, enabling you to create lasting volume. It works for any hair type, says Potempa. "With fine hair, work on larger sections, and with thick hair, take small sections. Brush only in a down, rather than an up and down, direction to prevent breakage."
         </p>
         <p>
          To add more volume to your roots after styling, gently backcomb the underside of your hair. "After you've created the effect you're looking for, use the bristles to smooth out the top to hide the evidence of your backcombing," Williams says.
         </p>
         <p>
          Try
          <a href="http://www.sallybeauty.com/tease-brush/SBS-217027,default,pd.html" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
           <strong>
            Brush Strokes' Wood Tease Mixed Boar/Porcupine Bristles Teasing Brush
           </strong>
          </a>
          ($4.99).
         </p>
        </li>
        <li itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
         <span class="slide-img-container">
          <img alt="" class="slide-img" data-id="8" data-src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/marilyn-tuxedo-brush-basics-dg-pg-full.jpg" data-title="Hourglass+Round+Brush" id="slide-img-8" src=""/>

         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Hourglass Round Brush
         </h2>
         <p>
          This shapely brush creates a smooth shine and is best for curly and wavy strands. These brushes are especially effective for finer hair, as it smooths the cuticle without adding tension, which can leave hair limp. The longer your hair, the larger the brush should be.
         </p>
         <p>
          "Round brushes like these, with dual bristles, are best to create volume, to detangle, and to use in heat styling," Gibson says. To smooth frizzy waves, Potempa recommends placing the brush on top of the section of hair you're drying, and aiming your blow-dryer down as you brush.
         </p>
         <p>
          Try the
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.ulta.com/ulta/browse/productDetail.jsp?productId=VP10719#sku2115356" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
            Marilyn Tuxedo Pro Brush
           </a>
          </strong>
          ($23).
         </p>
        </li>

          Oval Paddle Brush
         </h2>
         <p>

"It allows you to brush hair without having it snagged so easily," he says. It can be used for detangling hair while wet, brushing hair while rough-drying, and smoothing hair before wrapping it around a straightening iron.
         </p>

          Try
          <a href="http://www.walmart.com/ip/Goody-Finish-Gel-Cushion-Brush/14658248" rel="nofollow" target="_new">

           </strong>
          </a>
          ($6.99).
         </p>

          Rectangular Styling Brush

"The brush mimics the head shape and creates amazing shine to the hair without much effort," Fugate explains.
