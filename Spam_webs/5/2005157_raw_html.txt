        How-To Geek
       </h1>
       <!-- abovepost -->
       <div class="thepost" id="post-3331">
        <div style="max-width:650px;width:100%;margin-bottom:0px;margin-top:-10px;min-height:24px;">
         <div style="clear:both;">
         </div>
        </div>
        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/3331/install-windows-live-essentials-to-windows-7/" rel="bookmark" title="Install Windows Live Essentials In Windows 7">
          Install Windows Live Essentials In Windows 7
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="thecontent">
         <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
         <p>
          So youre starting out fresh with the new Windows 7 operating system but notice there are some of your favorite Windows applications missing. Today we take a look at the Windows Live Essentials installer for getting your favorite Microsoft applications set up.
         </p>
         <p>
          With the Windows 7 release Microsoft left out several previously bundled applications like Mail, Messenger, and Photo Gallery. These apps are now branded as their Live Essentials suite and are available as a separate download. This allows Microsoft to update and improve on these features without causing disturbance to the core operating system.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Installing Live Essentials
          </strong>
         </p>
         <div id="div-1342-1" style="margin-top:10px;padding-top:10px;border-top:1px solid #CCC;margin-bottom:10px;border-bottom:1px solid #CCC;">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           tmntag.adTag('div-1342-1');
          </script>
         </div>
         <p>
          To start getting those MS apps you love back go to Start \ Getting Started \ Get Windows Live Essentials
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="start" border="0" height="561" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/start2.png" style="border-right-width: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px" title="start" width="523"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          You will be brought to the Windows Live page where you can select your language and download the Windows Live Setup executable.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2-site" border="0" height="323" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/2site.png" style="border-right-width: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px" title="2-site" width="354"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Choose from the list of MS programs to install. Messenger, Mail, Photo Gallery, Movie Maker and more are all available to download.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="4-components" border="0" height="487" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/4components.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="4-components" width="611"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          A progress screen is displayed while your selections are installed. The amount of time it takes will vary depending on which and how many programs you choose.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="5 - progress" border="0" height="474" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/5progress.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="5 - progress" width="617"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          After the programs are installed you will see them in the Start menu under Windows Live.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="7 on menu" border="0" height="218" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/7onmenu.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="7 on menu" width="282"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Windows Live Applications
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Windows Live Mail replaces Outlook Express and Windows Mail that was included with Vista. It is a great full featured email application with Calendar, RSS Feeds, and has the ability to check multiple web accounts. If you dont want to pay for Outlook this is a great way to handle your email needs.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Mail" border="0" height="601" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/Mail.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="Mail" width="651"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Windows Live Photo Gallery provides a free way to store, organize, and share all of your digital photos. It also lets you easily edit photos, view in a slideshow presentation, and share them on the web.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Photos" border="0" height="516" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/Photos.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="Photos" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Of course you know Windows Live Messenger for instant messaging, exchange pictures, and play games with other MSN users.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="msgr" border="0" height="629" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/msgr.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="msgr" width="636"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Movie Maker beta replaces Windows Movie Maker that was include with XP and lets you import photos, video clips, and music into your own personal video.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="mmk" border="0" height="486" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/mmk.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="mmk" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          With these popular and commonly used applications Microsoft will be able to improve and update them on a more regular basis without making the user run through a lot of update and patches. This also makes for an easier installation and give you more choice in what you
          <em>
           do or do not
          </em>
          want to install. Make sure to also check out our articles on how to
          <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/the-geek-blog/backup-your-windows-live-writer-settings/">
           Backup Windows Live Writer Settings
          </a>
          and using
          <a href="http://blogs.howtogeek.com/mysticgeek/2009/01/26/help-protect-your-children-with-windows-live-family-safety-filter/">
           Windows Live Family Safety Filter
          </a>
          to help protect your children on the PC.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="sshot-2009-08-12-[01-01-08]" border="0" height="85" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/sshot20090812010108.png" style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" title="sshot-2009-08-12-[01-01-08]" width="94"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://download.live.com/">
           <strong>
            Download Windows Live Essentials Installer
           </strong>
          </a>
         </p>
