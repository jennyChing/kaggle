  <p>
   That's a length spread of 3 orders of magnitude.  Each is 10 times
longer.  The box is 1000 times longer than the grain of sugar.
   <br/>
  </p>
  <p>
   Now we can zoom in...
   <br/>
  </p>
  <center>
   <b>
    What if a grain of sugar were as big as the box?
   </b>
  </center>
  We could stand next to it.  Could use it as a bed or a desk.  Dust
mites would look like hand-sized turtles.  Blood cells would be little
red and white marbles.  Bacteria would look like little grains of
sugar.  You could swim in a raindrop.

  <small>
   A verbal description - picture your desk, and on it a grain of sugar,
a marble, a baseball, and a large beachball.  Each is 10 times wider
than the previous.  There is a spread of 1000 in size, the beachball
is 1000 times wider than the grain of sugar.  Now, imagine we zoom
in so that the grain of sugar seems as large as the beachball.  Then
the little parasitic mites which live on your skin look like turtles,
blood cells are little red and white marbles, and bacteria look like
grains of sugar.  And we can repeat this, ...
