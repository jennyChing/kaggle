        <h1>
         How to Get Your First House Sitting Job? Here are 6 tips to get you started:
        </h1>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Polish your profile with video and appropriate photos:
          </strong>
          There is no better way for a homeowner to get a feeling for who you are than video. It doesnt have to be a cinema quality production. It is
          <a href="http://www.wikihow.com/Make-a-YouTube-Video" target="_blank">
           easy to create
          </a>
          and edit a short, clear video outlining who you are and your special skills or experience. Thats all you need. Photos are a must. But specific photos: Pictures with you and your pet or a friends pet, shots of you gardening or repairing something in the house.The best photos dont look staged. Keep it natural and genuine looking.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Make sure your about is targeted on important details:
          </strong>
          why you want to house sit, your love of animals, properties you have owned or been responsible for, job responsibilities or skills that would make you a good house sitter (bonded, property management, contractor).
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Get professional references
          </strong>
          : Having professional references is important to make the short list for a house sit. A homeowner is more likely to hire a sitter with references instead of one without.What do you do if you dont have experience? We used our Airbnb profile. We had earned great references from our hosts that directly related to being respectful guests. References from landlords, employers or volunteer efforts are also great to prove you are responsible and trustworthy. Apply for an assignment in your hometown. This is a great way to get started and earn a reference. You can meet the homeowners face to face before the assignment. This is very appealing to homeowners because they can get to know potential house sitter better than interviewing by phone or email.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Be available to communicate face to face
          </strong>
          : Skype is perfect and easy for long distance face to face chat. We chatted on Skype 3 times with our homeowners. It was so much more personal and better to convey feelings and opinions. Of course, if you can meet in person that is even better when possible.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Speak up about your special expertise and experience:
          </strong>
          We have had lots of experience raising and caring for dogs. List your special pet or homeowner experience; giving meds to pets, behavior problem experience. Include home ownership experience; handy with tools, experienced in home repair, specialized knowledge and passions you have.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Get the police check
          </strong>
          : Some applicants do have police checks and some dont. Having one is just another way to prove you are trustworthy.
         </li>
        </ul>
