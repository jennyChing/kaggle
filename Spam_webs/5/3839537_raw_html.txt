        <div class="editors-post-body">
         <p>
          <em>
           Every other Thursday,
           <a href="/users/70454_gena_hamshaw">
            Gena Hamshaw
           </a>
           of the blog
          </em>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.choosingraw.com/">
            Choosing Raw
           </a>
          </em>
          <em>
           shares satisfying, flavorful recipes that also happen to be vegan.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           Today: Yes, you can make vegan pesto. Just grab yourself some
           <a href="/blog/6779-vegan-pesto-yes-you-can#nooch">
            nutritional yeast
           </a>
           , and you'll be a believer.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <img alt="vegan pesto " height="413" src="https://d2k9njawademcf.cloudfront.net/indeximages/32919/full/NV2.jpeg?1369863698" width="620"/>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          In my home, the advent of summer is always marked by an abundance of fresh pesto. It crowds my fridge, and soon after that, it starts to crowd my freezer. Pesto is such a staple in my kitchen that I sometimes forget that not all pesto is created vegan: the classic recipe, of course, contains Parmesan cheese. If youre experimenting with vegan recipes but you love pesto as dearly as I do, fear not. Vegan pesto is within your reach.
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <a name="nooch">
          </a>
          The trick to a perfect vegan pesto is nutritional yeast. This magical ingredient is a staple of vegan diets (vegans lovingly refer to it as nooch), but its often ignored by everyone else. This is a shame, because nutritional yeast is versatile and nutritious. It also has a cheesy, nutty flavor that makes a great substitute for Parmesan cheese.
         </p>
         <p class="p2">
          <img alt="Nooch" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5470/8883035591_ced5ecf7bc_z.jpg" width="620"/>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          I love nutritional yeast so much that Ive been known to eat it by the spoonful, but it has more sophisticated uses: its great sprinkled on
          <a href="/blog/4068-when-a-vegan-comes-to-dinner">
           pasta
          </a>
          or
          <a href="/blog/5481-cooking-whole-grains">
           warm grains
          </a>
          , mixed into
          <a href="/blog/5751-the-many-faces-of-cashew-cream">
           cashew cream
          </a>
          for a cheesy vegan pasta sauce, or as a topping for popcorn.
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          If none of those ideas strike your fancy, trust me when I say that nutritional yeast in vegan pesto is a game changer. I use the following vegan pesto in potato salads, pasta dishes, and spread on sandwiches or in wraps. Its always a tremendous hit. And if the idea of any food nicknamed nooch freaks you out, dont worry -- lots of pesto recipes, including this delicious
          <a href="/recipes/18134-mint-pistachio-pesto">
           Mint-Pistachio Pesto
          </a>
          , are naturally vegan. Just make sure theyre dairy-free!
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <img alt="vegan pesto " height="413" src="https://d2k9njawademcf.cloudfront.net/indeximages/32920/full/NV3.jpeg?1369863722" width="620"/>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <a href="/recipes/22291-simple-vegan-pesto">
           Simple Vegan Pesto
          </a>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           Makes1 generous cup
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <strong>
           2 cups tightly packed fresh basil
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           1/2 cup walnuts or pine nuts
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           1 to 2 cloves garlic, roughly chopped (to taste)
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           1/2 cup extra-virgin olive oil
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           Sea salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           1 tablespoon lemon juice
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           3 tablespoons nutritional yeast
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <a href="/recipes/22291-simple-vegan-pesto">
           See the full recipe (and save and print it) here.
          </a>
         </p>

              I think tofu cheese is pretty good especially for pizza. Bled tofu, cashews, a bit of oil, a bit of soymilk (or any other kind you like), lemon and then whatever herbs you like. I usually prefer basil. I'm sure you could even bled the tofu with the vegan pesto. You blend it so it's got a kind of crumbly texture. Make sure it doesn't get to smooth.
