         Maybe youve heard of this story before, and maybe you havent: Back in 2006, an anonymous Blogger by the name of jpeg appeared, claiming to be somebody within the game industry. He made various posts about both his personal life and his job (like any normal Blogger), though never anything terribly specific. That all changed in early 2007, when he made a post about
         <em>
          NiGHTS: Journey of Dreams
         </em>
         . He spoke on a great many things; he confided that he did not have much faith in the game, despite what he considered the best efforts of Sonic Team. He knew of the games troubled development, which he used as a segway to discuss the internal workings of Sega and how it related to Sonic Team, claiming that he knew Sonic Team, personally, on a professional level.
        </p>
        <p>
         Given the deliberately anonymous nature of his blog, though, not many people knew whether to believe him.
        </p>
        <p>
         A month after writing his post about
         <em>
          NiGHTS: Journey of Dreams
         </em>
         , jpeg deleted all of his blog posts and bid farewell to his Blogspot users. Thankfully, however, the internet never forgets: after a little bit of figurative
         <a href="http://www.theghz.com/forum/viewtopic.php?p=93440#p93440" target="_blank">
          legwork
         </a>
         from Green Hill Zone forums user FlashTHD, an
         <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20070408161400/http://bossrush.blogspot.com/2007/03/nights-2.html" target="_blank">
          archived version
         </a>
         of the original blog post regarding
         <em>
          Journey of Dreams
         </em>
         has been recovered. By now, you are probably wondering why an unconfirmed blog post from an anonymous person on the vast expanse of the internet is so important. As it turns out, one possible reason all previous posts had been removed from the blog is because jpeg left a little hint at the end of the post about
         <em>
          NiGHTS
         </em>
         :
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          
          <em>
           Sonic Team are a relic from a past age who have overstayed their welcome. For the same marketing reasons that their name was initially kept (and has since backfired) they should be dissolved  it may already be too late for NiGHTS (and I really hope it isnt and they manage to turn it around, which with Iizuka-sans passion for the project is a possibility), but for the good of the companys image; for the future of Segas other prestigious IPs and lastly for its iconic mascot it needs to be done before they release
           <strong>
            another Sonic game with a transforming Evil Night Sonic who looks like a werewolf and can stretch his arms out Dhalsim-style.
           </strong>
           It will happen, mark my words.
          </em>
          
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Over a year before anybody had ever spoken the word Werehog, this blog user had tipped us off to the existence of Sonic Unleashed. Obviously, this most likely constituted an illegal breach of NDA, resulting in all posts on the blog being removed.
        </p>
        <p>
         Today, his
         <a href="http://bossrush.blogspot.com/index.html" target="_blank">
          blogspot account
         </a>
         is still active, containing only his Goodbye post. The sidebar of his blog now contains a typical 
         <em>
          My opinions are my own and do not reflect upon my employer, and I will not discuss my job with you
         </em>
          disclaimer, but most importantly, reveals the blog owners true name: Ben Andac. Ben Andac,
         <a href="http://www.linkedin.com/in/benandac" target="_blank">
          as it turns out
         </a>
         , worked as a Game Evaluator and an Admissions and Recruitment Executive for both Sega Europe and Sony Computer Entertainment Europe. Bens job was most likely to evaluate the quality of products coming out of the publisher and potentially scout for new recruits to develop software. Ben now works for online gaming website
         <a href="http://www.idlethumbs.net/about/staff.php?id=8" target="_blank">
          Idle Thumbs
         </a>
         , writing editorials and reviews. (
         <strong>
          TRISTAN EDIT:
         </strong>
         Since this story has been published, the Blogspot account linked above has been made completely inactive.)
        </p>
        <p>
         By confirming Bens identity as an employee at Sega Europe, re-reading the March 2007 blog regarding the development of
         <em>
          NiGHTS: Journey of Dreams
         </em>
         shines a spotlight directly on a side of Sega rarely seen by the public. In particular, it reveals an internal strife between Sonic Team and Sega of Japan, highlighted by the fact that
         <em>
          NiGHTS: Journey of Dreams
         </em>
         was originally developed as an Xbox 360 and Playstation 3 title  going so far as to say he (Ben Andac) played an Xbox 360 build of
         <em>
          NiGHTS
         </em>
         at one point. Upon witnessing the overnight success of the Wii, Sega of Japan apparently forced Sonic Team to retool the game for Nintendos lower-spec platform, something its producer (Takashi Iizuka) was not happy about. Despite the shift in platform, the games fast-approaching release date and limited development budget remained firmly unchanged, giving Sonic Team roughly one year to rebuild
         <em>
          NiGHTS
         </em>
         exclusively for the Wii (when most games typically take two years or more to develop).
        </p>
        <p>
         As it turned out, according to Mr. Andac, after Sega had restructured itself to re-integrate external development houses like Smilebit and AM2 back in to the main Sega offices, Sonic Team was kept external strictly as a sweatshop to milk the
         <em>
          Sonic
         </em>
         cashcow, assembly-line style. Sega of Japan had intended
         <em>
          NiGHTS: Journey of Dreams
         </em>
         as a brief rest from the never-ending onslaught of
         <em>
          Sonic
         </em>
         game after
         <em>
          Sonic
         </em>
         game. In Bens own words: 
         <em>
          Theres no doubt that Sonic Team have lost their quality touch. They are worse than talentless: they are without passion. Bored, weary, closed-minded and out of touch with any sense of what makes games good anymore.
         </em>
          Quite simply put, after making so many of them, Sonic Team is tired of
         <em>
          Sonic
         </em>
         games  something Ben cites as a reason for Yuji Nakas resignation in 2006.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the end, what this blog really represents is that, even within their own ranks, members of Segas staff are unhappy with the direction Sonic Team as a development studio has headed. While it was easy to assume such, we now have confirmation of internal strife between Sonic Team and their parent subsidary, Sega of Japan. In relation, this goes a long way to explain the shortcomings of games produced by Sonic Team: They are forced to work on a franchise they no longer care about, and when they do get a chance to stretch their legs, it is under tight restrictions.
        </p>
        <p>
         Stay tuned to TSSZ as we cover all aspects of Sonic the Hedgehog and his related franchises.
        </p>
