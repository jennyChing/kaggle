                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      <strike>
                       Ten
                      </strike>
                      Eleventhings to do with a friend from out of town when you're married with children:
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       The older you get, the less you see those friends you really and truly shared your childhood with, either they slip away and fade like so many things from youth, or you see each other infrequently because life gets in the way. When you do finally meet up, here are some things you may be interested in doing.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <a name="more">
                      </a>
                      <div>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        1. Eat too much Mexican. Pretend you understand enough Spanish to pronounce wordslike ceviche, chiles rellenos, cerveza (your husband and friend, both beer drinkers, know this term immediately),  and pepitas, look like a fool when you don't. Curse your 7th grade Spanish teacher, the children's television system for not understanding how important having bilingual cartoon characters was in the 1980's (hello Dora), and the college counselor who told you Japanese would serve you well, sumimasen and dozo yoroshiku. No, you don't remember what they mean.Then stick to tacos because you have no idea what those other foods are. Besides, you're too old to try new things. Feel a bit of relief when you see your spell check doesn't know these words either because they are underlined in red as if misspelled, though you've copied them from a Mexican restaurant site.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        2. Drink several alcoholic beverages. Feel relief when you look at the menu and see the one identifiable alcoholic drink, Sangria, realize you like it and stick with it. Red is good, white is better...just like wine! Know you are too old for tequila and as such, stay away from it in all forms including mixed in with other things and straight up in shot glasses. There will be no more dancing on tables, you're 40 unlike your friend who lied to her newest boyfriend and said she was 35, fessed up (sort of) and said she was 38, had a birthday which would have made her 39 (but in fact, she was 40). Laugh and call her 3940 and vow to get tattoos with those exact numbers. Shit, you're too old and not nearly drunk enough for tats. Instead, you'll play a number in the lottery, 3940 sounds as lucky as anything else.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       3. Pull out your smart phones, compare mutual Facebook friends, discuss them in depth by figuring out who looks good (aka better than you) and find some other reason to bitch about them, who looks worse than you (try to say some other kind word like, 'gee, don't they have cute kids.' If they were mean to you, thank the gods of Karma for getting them back in your name), and who unfriended either or both of you and offer up theories that can never be proven. Marvel at who is divorced, who is successful and who is totally different than you thought they'd be, including yourselves.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       4. Talk about how great you each look in spite of the fact that you're now two, middle-aged woman. Complain about the things you hate about yourselves (acne that still clings, the dark skin over an upper lip due to hormones which make you look like a fifteen-year-old prepubescent boy) and validate each other's concerns while trying to offer comfort or make jokes because really things aren't so bad, you could look like those Facebook friends who haven't aged as well. Pull out phones again and reexamine unlucky 'friends.'
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BAGPuVJ8yeU/VWtwaqU6Z2I/AAAAAAAAA54/pUJOYawTPFM/s1600/photo%2B%252824%2529.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                         <img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BAGPuVJ8yeU/VWtwaqU6Z2I/AAAAAAAAA54/pUJOYawTPFM/s1600/photo%2B%252824%2529.JPG"/>
                        </a>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       5. Discuss status of singlehood or marriedom and compare notes. She can go back to school or travel, you have a home, a husband and kids. Realize the grass is always greener, and while you each respect each other's choices and lives, you are okay with where you are and what lies ahead.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       6. Introduce said friend to kids who have aged significantly since your last meeting and some who weren't even born. Let her see how crazy, weird and amazing kids are as they reveal both the embarrassing (how your eight-year-old believes your boobs go down to your vig, her word for vagina, and watch with horror as your two-year-old runs around naked sticking his finger in off-limits places) and the silly (your husband hopes to someday own a firetruck, something he tried to convince his family to go in on with him as an investment when he was drunk at his 40th birthday).
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       7. Drive to a remote area to smoke a cigarette because she still does and you haven't in years. Wish you hated it, but realize you still miss the habit (though you'll never take it up again because your a mom with asthma scared shitless of wrinkles) and comfort yourself with the promise of the once-every-five-year butt, eat a mint on the way home, almost get sick because of some sort of chemical in said mint, get annoyed that the mint, rather than alcohol or the cigarette, will be the thing to make you throw up. Pray you don't hurl because you haven't done it in ages and really hate vomiting. Spray perfume to mask the scent. Hope the kids and husband don't smell it on you. If they do, you'll blame your friend and she'll have your back. She always does.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       8. Discuss how old parents are getting or how dead and realize how quickly life goes by. Really miss the days of a youth that passed far too quickly. Discuss the million memories the two of you haven't thought of in ages and remember how important your friendship is. Vow five years will never go by again. Vow to Skype because the kids really love your friend almost as much as you do.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       9. Watch as said friend gives all your kids little souvenirs from her conference (the reason she was able to visit), plays with them, loves them and think someday she'll make an amazing mother if she decides that is something she wants.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       10. Write a post about your time together and cry while doing so because you really miss her and wish she lived closer. Begin planning for that trip when you'll be able to visit her, of course you may have a husband and children in tow, but you're sure she won't mind.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       11. Gather your kids so she can take pictures of them to show her parents, who were like your parents growing up, laugh when it is a total shit show. Force her to take pictures with you so you can preserve this moment and have something for the blog, let the kids take selfies. Send her off with hugs as you thank god you have had the same best friend for 32 years. Remember, when her mother said, "If you have one true friend in your life consider yourself lucky." Understand this
                       <i>
                        is
                       </i>
                       true and you
                       <i>
                        are
                       </i>
                       lucky.
                      </span>

               + config.blogId + '&amp;postID=' + comment.id;
        } else if ('deleteclass' == key) {
          return comment.deleteclass;
        }
        return '';
      };

      var replybox = null;
      var replyUrlParts = null;
      var replyParent = undefined;

      var onReply = function(commentId, domId) {
        if (replybox == null) {
          // lazily cache replybox, and adjust to suit this style:
          replybox = document.getElementById('comment-editor');
          if (replybox != null) {
            replybox.height = '250px';
            replybox.style.display = 'block';
            replyUrlParts = replybox.src.split('#');
          }
        }
        if (replybox &amp;&amp; (commentId !== replyParent)) {
          document.getElementById(domId).insertBefore(replybox, null);
          replybox.src = replyUrlParts[0]
              + (commentId ? '&amp;parentID=' + commentId : '')
              + '#' + replyUrlParts[1];
          replyParent = commentId;
        }
      };

      var hash = (window.location.hash || '#').substring(1);
      var startThread, targetComment;
      if (/^comment-form_/.test(hash)) {
        startThread = hash.substring('comment-form_'.length);
      } else if (/^c[0-9]+$/.test(hash)) {
        targetComment = hash.substring(1);
      }

      // Configure commenting API:
      var configJso = {
        'maxDepth': config.maxThreadDepth
      };
      var provider = {
        'id': config.postId,
        'data': items,
        'loadNext': paginator,
        'hasMore': hasMore,
        'getMeta': getMeta,
        'onReply': onReply,
        'rendered': true,
        'initComment': targetComment,
        'initReplyThread': startThread,
        'config': configJso,
        'messages': msgs
      };

      var render = function() {
        if (window.goog &amp;&amp; window.goog.comments) {
          var holder = document.getElementById('comment-holder');
          window.goog.comments.render(holder, provider);
        }
      };

      // render now, or queue to render when library loads:
      if (window.goog &amp;&amp; window.goog.comments) {
        render();
      } else {
        window.goog = window.goog || {};
        window.goog.comments = window.goog.comments || {};
        window.goog.comments.loadQueue = window.goog.comments.loadQueue || [];
        window.goog.comments.loadQueue.push(render);
      }
    })();
// ]]&gt;
