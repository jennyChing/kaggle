       <p>
        The current events in the Ukraine are quite disturbing as we see some very proactive behavior taking place with little resistance from world leadership.  If you are a student of world history, you are beginning to see a pattern of behavior that continues to repeat itself over and over again.  This pattern is one where a proactive leader uses sheer charisma and behavior to pretty much do as he/she wants with nothing but a strategy of appeasement to stand in their way.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-2803">
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://sam-smith.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/wpid-Photo-20140318072702.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="202" id="blogsy-1395145796071.2354" src="http://sam-smith.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/wpid-Photo-20140318072702.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        <span style="line-height: 1.3em;">
         Leadership is not a given commodity, but one that is earned through action.  Those that choose to legislate  and talk
        </span>
        <span style="line-height: 1.3em;">
         without providing action and guidance soon find themselves in a position of chaos with little to do but stand by and watch.  I have been recently reading the words of Jesus leading up to His death on calvary and time and time again Jesus provides parables and stories that provide guidance in times such as this
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          23 Jesus replied, The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified. 24 Very truly I tell you, unless a kernel of wheat falls to the ground and dies, it remains only a single seed. But if it dies, it produces many seeds. 25 Anyone who loves their life will lose it, while anyone who hates their life in this world will keep it for eternal life. 26 Whoever serves me must follow me; and where I am, my servant also will be. My Father will honor the one who serves me.
         </em>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="line-height: 1.3em;">
         <strong>
          <em>
           27 Now my soul is troubled, and what shall I say? 'Father, save me from this hour'? No, it was for this very reason I came to this hour. 28 Father, glorify your name! Then a voice came from heaven, I have glorified it, and will glorify it again.  John 12:23-28
          </em>
         </strong>
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://sam-smith.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/wpid-Photo-201403180727021.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="249" id="blogsy-1395145796069.9739" src="http://sam-smith.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/wpid-Photo-201403180727021.jpg" width="350"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        In ministry leadership, we look to God's direction as we navigate the challenges of leadership.  The old adage tough times call for tough people comes to mind as we address each challenge thrown into our path.  God didn't say life was easy and He is not going to just fix everything.  We must be proactive and continue to work hard and do our very best.  We are planting seeds to find support and raise the monies necessary to support our work.  That is a tough challenge in of itself, and its just the beginning.  We then must deal with the issues of poverty related to logistics, execution, cultures, and governmental restrictions in order to execute our service to the poor.
       </p>
       <p>
        Leadership is not the gathering of seeds, but planting of these seeds so that there are
        <a href="http://www.medicalministrytrips.org" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.medicalministrytrips.org', 'multiple opportunities');" target="_self" title="MMI">
         multiple opportunities
        </a>
        to sprout to provide the nourishment to thrive and lead the organization to optimize its potential.  Leading is not meant to be easy, God is not looking to us to just stand by and be complacent.  He is providing challenges to make times hard, these challenges are used to define our faith, commitment, character, and passion to serve Him.  It's not for the faint of heart, and its not meant to be easy.  It is only when a leader can grasp this concept that s/he can endure.  It's really up to uswe can lead, or be lead.
       </p>
