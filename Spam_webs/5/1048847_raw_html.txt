             For male Serengeti lions like Hildur and C-Boy, teamwork is essential. Here, Hildur shakes the rain from his mane. He and C-Boy work together to retain control over two prides: the Vumbi, consisting of five adult lionesses, and Simba East, a pride now also with five.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              See more
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/08/serengeti-lions/nichols-photography">
               pictures
              </a>
              from the August 2013 feature
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/08/serengeti-lions/quammen-text">
               story
              </a>
              The Short Happy Life of a Serengeti Lion.
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a class="thumb" href="//images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/720/cache/algae-bloom-lake-erie-essick_72092_600x450.jpg">
           </a>
           <div>
            <p class="wallpaper_link">
             <a href="/wallpaper/photography/photos/best-pod-october-2013/algae-bloom-lake-erie-essick/">
              Get Wallpaper
             </a>
            </p>
            <h2>
             Algae Bloom, Ohio
            </h2>
            <p class="credit">
             Photograph by Peter Essick
            </p>
            <p>
             Fertilizer runoff causes toxic algal blooms. This one covered a third of Lake Erie in 2011.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              See more
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/05/fertilized-world/essick-photography">
               pictures
              </a>
              from the May 2013 feature
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/05/fertilized-world/charles-text">
               story
              </a>
              "Fertilized World."
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a class="thumb" href="//images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/724/cache/ice-diamond-balog_72496_600x450.jpg">
           </a>
           <div>
            <h2>
             Ice Diamond, Iceland
            </h2>
            <p class="credit">
             Photograph by James Balog
            </p>
            <p>
             Destined to melt, an 800-pound chunk of ice glows in moonlight on a wintry Icelandic beach. The ice washed up in a lagoon formed by a receding glacier. Photographer James Balog calls such pieces ice diamonds, finding beauty as well as tragedy in disappearing glaciers.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              See more
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/10/glacial-meltdown/balog-photography">
               pictures
              </a>
              from the October 2013 feature
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/10/glacial-meltdown/kunzig-text ">
               story
              </a>
              Meltdown.
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a class="thumb" href="//images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/720/cache/golden-gate-bridge-morell_72093_600x450.jpg">
           </a>
           <div>
            <h2>
             Golden Gate Bridge, California
            </h2>
            <p class="credit">
             Photograph by Abelardo Morell
            </p>
            <p>
             Morell, recently retired as a professor of photography at the Massachusetts College of Art and Design, created this image of the Golden Gate Bridge using a camera obscura and the ground as a canvas.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              See more
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/10/morell-parks/morell-photography">
               pictures
              </a>
              from the October 2013 feature
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/10/morell-parks/oneill-text">
               story
              </a>
              "Visions on Earth."
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a class="thumb" href="//images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/725/cache/fjord-norway-haarberg_72511_600x450.jpg">
           </a>
           <div>
            <h2>
             Fjord, Western Norway
            </h2>
            <p class="credit">
             Photograph by Erlend Haarberg
            </p>
            <p>
             Western Norways fjordslike Nryfjorden, northeast of Bergen and a UNESCO World Heritage sitelook like fingers of the sea intruding into the land. But they are also receptacles for fresh water pouring off the peaks and ridges that surround them.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              See more
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/11/coastal-norway/haarberg-photography/">
               pictures
              </a>
              from the November 2013 feature
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/11/coastal-norway/klinkenborg-text">
               story
              </a>
              Follow the Water.
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a class="thumb" href="//images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/720/cache/iceberg-greenland-balog_72094_600x450.jpg">
           </a>
           <div>
            <h2>
             Iceberg, Greenland
            </h2>
            <p class="credit">
             Photograph by James Balog
            </p>
            <p>
             Solid turns to liquid as a 15-story iceberg erodes in the warming seas of the North Atlantic.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              See more
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/10/glacial-meltdown/balog-photography">
               pictures
              </a>
              from the October 2013 feature
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/10/glacial-meltdown/kunzig-text">
               story
              </a>
              "Meltdown."
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a class="thumb" href="//images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/721/cache/lions-pride-serengeti-nichols_72101_600x450.jpg">
           </a>
           <div>
            <p class="wallpaper_link">
             <a href="/wallpaper/photography/photos/best-pod-october-2013/lions-pride-serengeti-nichols/">
              Get Wallpaper
             </a>
            </p>
            <h2>
             Lions, Serengeti
            </h2>
            <p class="credit">
             Photograph by Michael Nichols
            </p>
            <p>
             Dusk is a busy time for the Vumbi pride. As the moon rises, the lionesses rouse themselves from their afternoon naps, tussle in the grass, and set out on the evening hunt. Nichols made this photo using natural light; soon after, he switched to infrared.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              See more
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/08/serengeti-lions/nichols-photography">
               pictures
              </a>
              from the August 2013 feature
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/08/serengeti-lions/quammen-text">
               story
              </a>
              "Serengeti Lions."
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a class="thumb" href="//images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/724/cache/las-calaveras-cenote-nicklen_72498_600x450.jpg">
           </a>
           <div>
            <p class="wallpaper_link">
             <a href="/wallpaper/photography/photos/best-pod-october-2013/las-calaveras-cenote-nicklen/">
              Get Wallpaper
             </a>
            </p>
            <h2>
             Snorkeler, Mexico
            </h2>
            <p class="credit">
             Photograph by Paul Nicklen
            </p>
            <p>
             A snorkeling tourist floats in a cenote called Las Calaverasthe skullsnear Tulum. Local Maya got their drinking water here until about 30 years ago, when divers found bones. Archaeologists have recorded the remains of more than a hundred people, usually shrouded by the waters primordial darkness.
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              See more
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/08/sacred-cenotes/nicklen-schwarz-photography">
               pictures
              </a>
              from the August 2013 feature
              <a href="http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/2013/08/sacred-cenotes/guillermoprieto-text">
               story
              </a>
              Secrets of the Maya Otherworld.
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
         </ul>

        $('.adunit').each(function() {
            var $element = $(this);
            var $parent = $element.closest('.advertisement');
            if ($parent.length == 0 || $parent.css('display') !== 'none') {
                $element.addClass('visible');
            }
        });
        $('.adunit.visible').dfp({

                } else {

    /**
     * Add a script inside the interstitial div, that initialize
     * the slot to show the GPT ad; this function also save the
     * slot's instance in window, to refresh the ad every time
     * this function is called.
     * ad-gallery-container is the div's id
     */
    function interstitialAd(adunit, adsize, divId) {
        var gtag = document.createElement('script');
        gtag.type = 'text/javascript';
        gtag.innerHTML = 'googletag.cmd.push(function() {' +
            'if(window.adInterstitial == undefined){' +
                'var slot1 = googletag.defineSlot("2994/' 
                    + adunit + '", [' + adsize[0] + ', ' 
                    + adsize[1] + '],"'+divId+'");' +
                'window.adInterstitial = slot1;' +
            '}' +
            'else{' +
                'slot1 = window.adInterstitial;' +
            '}' +
            'slot1.addService(googletag.pubads());googletag.'+
                'enableServices();googletag.display("'+divId+'");});';
        //add the script tag inside the div
        $('#'+divId).append(gtag);
        //refresh only the instance of the interstitial ad
        googletag.pubads().refresh([window.adInterstitial]);
