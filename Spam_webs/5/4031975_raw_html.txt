           <h1 class="post-title">
            Shadow coffee
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://coffeebean.guru/author/coffeebean/" rel="author" title="Posts by coffeebean.guru">
             coffeebean.guru
            </a>
             February 2, 2015
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_728" style="width: 729px">
              <img alt="Photo: Mario F. Gomez - La Esperanza, Huila" class="lazy size-full wp-image-728" data-original="http://coffeebean.guru/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/coffee_flowers_in_shade-e1422842112437.jpg" height="449" src="http://coffeebean.guru/wp-content/plugins/jquery-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="719"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
              </p>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="Photo: Mario F. Gomez - La Esperanza, Huila" class="size-full wp-image-728" height="449" src="http://coffeebean.guru/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/coffee_flowers_in_shade-e1422842112437.jpg" width="719"/>
              </noscript>
              <center>
               Photo: Mario F. Gomez  La Esperanza, Huila
              </center>
             </div>
             <p>
              Shadow coffee is coffee grown under a shade canopy (shade provided by mixed native trees). Arabica coffee is traditionally grown in shade in many (but not all) parts of Mexico, Central America, Colombia, Peru, and Venezuela, and in some other parts of the world, including India and some regions of Indonesia and Africa. Elsewhere arabica coffee is traditionally grown in full sun, or near full sun.
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_729" style="width: 210px">
              <img alt="Blue-crowned_Motmot" class="lazy wp-image-729 size-medium" data-original="http://coffeebean.guru/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Blue-crowned_Motmot-200x300.jpg" height="300" src="http://coffeebean.guru/wp-content/plugins/jquery-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="200"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
              </p>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="Blue-crowned_Motmot" class="wp-image-729 size-medium" height="300" src="http://coffeebean.guru/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Blue-crowned_Motmot-200x300.jpg" width="200"/>
              </noscript>
              Blue crowned Motmot
             </div>
             <p>
              As these shade canopies provide a habitat for migrating song birds in Central America, coffees grown under such a native canopy is sometimes marketed as bird friendly.The avifauna of Colombia include a total of 1871 species, of which 74 are endemic, 9 have been introduced by humans and 87 are rare or accidental.
             </p>
             <p>
              Shade grown coffees most definitely are much easier on the environment than sun grown coffees, and the better tasting Central American coffees are best grown in shade.
             </p>
