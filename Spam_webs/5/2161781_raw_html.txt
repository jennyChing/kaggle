             <p>
              <iframe border="0" frameborder="0" height="360" scrolling="no" src="https://ll.ms-studiosmedia.com/events/2015/1501/Windows10CP/live/Windows10CP.html?title=Windows10CP-mscom" style="overflow: hidden" width="640">
              </iframe>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               MASHABLE LIVE BLOG:
               <a href="http://mashable.com/2015/01/20/microsoft-windows-10-live-blog/">
                Microsoft's Windows 10 consumer event
               </a>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://mashable.com/category/microsoft/">
               Microsoft
              </a>
              is gearing up for its biggest update to Windows in years.
             </p>
             <p>
              Though we've seen hints of the company's plan for
              <a href="http://mashable.com/category/windows-10/">
               Windows 10
              </a>
               including a new Start menu and rumors of a
              <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/12/29/internet-explorer-vs-spartan/">
               new browser
              </a>
               Wednesday's event will be about much more than the desktop.
             </p>
             <div class="see-also">
              <p>
               See also:
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/01/20/windows-10-event-what-to-expect/">
                What to expect at Microsoft's big Windows 10 event
               </a>
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
              The latest version of the operating system will also be integrated into the Windows Phone and Xbox platform and, possibly, an all-new piece of hardware.
             </p>
             <p>
              Those who want to follow every second of what looks to be a jam-packed event can follow Microsoft's livestream beginning at noon ET to keep tabs on the latest news and updates from CEO Satya Nadella and other top executives. The livestream is above, or on Microsoft's site
              <a href="http://news.microsoft.com/windows10story/" target="_blank">
               here
              </a>
              . If you can't tune in live, Microsoft will post the full video at the same link in the hours following the event.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Mashable
              </em>
              will also be on the ground in Redmond, Washington, reporting live from the event, so be sure to follow our
              <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/01/20/microsoft-windows-10-live-blog/">
               live blog
              </a>
              for real-time updates and analysis from Microsoft's headquarters. (We've got the livestream over on that page, too.)
             </p>
             <p>
              Though we saw glimpses of the long awaited Windows 10 at a business-focused
              <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/09/30/windows-10-hands-on/">
               preview event
              </a>
              in September, many details  such as the user interface improvements for touch devices and the mechanics of its Continuum feature  have remained under wraps until now.
             </p>
