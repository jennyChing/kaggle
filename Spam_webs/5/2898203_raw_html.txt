         <p>
          Do you think that kids find comics engaging and enjoy looking at them more than they do books? If so, then you might also believe that comics can be effective educational tools for teaching and promoting literacy.
          <strong>
           Reading with Pictures
          </strong>
          , a nonprofit organization and website, not only believes that to be true butadvocates the use of comics in the classroom to promote literacy and improve educational outcomes for all students.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://connectwithyourteens.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Reading-with-Pictures.png">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
            <img alt="Reading with Pictures logo" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2095" height="300" src="http://connectwithyourteens.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Reading-with-Pictures-192x300.png" width="192"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://connectwithyourteens.net/using-comics-to-promote-literacy/&amp;media=http://connectwithyourteens.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Reading-with-Pictures-192x300.png&amp;description=Using Comics to Promote Literacy')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.readingwithpictures.org/" target="_blank">
           Reading with Pictures
          </a>
          works with and/or serveseducators, parents, writers, cartoonists, publishers, academics and librarians. Everyone has a part in developing, researching, promoting, or even just learning about using comics to promote literacy.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           We work with academics to cultivate groundbreaking research into the proper role of comics in education. We collaborate with cartoonists to produce exceptional graphic novel content for scholastic use. Most importantly, we partner with educators to develop a system of best practices for integrating comics into their curriculum. At Reading With Pictures, we get comics into schools and get schools into comics.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Reading with Pictures has created The Graphic Textbook, a textbook for grades 3-6, with chapters on Social Studies, Math, Language Arts and Science. The Graphic Textbook was financed through a Kickstarter campaign and there will be a teachers edition with standards correlated lesson plans. The textbook will be ready for use in the fall of 2013. You can read more details about
          <a href="http://www.readingwithpictures.org/the-graphic-textbook/" target="_blank">
           The Graphic Textbook on the Reading with Pictures website
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://connectwithyourteens.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Graphic-textbook.jpg">
