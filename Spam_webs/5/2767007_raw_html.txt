        <h1 class="name post-title entry-title" itemprop="itemReviewed" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Thing">
         <span itemprop="name">
          The Coming of the Apart Hotel
         </span>
        </h1>
        <div class="clearfix">
        </div>

         <blockquote>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>
            Guest Post by Nassar Khalil, Principle Consultant, Nassar Khalil and Associates
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.nassarKhalil.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Nassar Khalil &amp; Associates">
             www.nassarKhalil.com
            </a>
           </strong>

         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          From it humble beginnings as inter-connecting rooms in mainstream hotels to cater for the needs of families with young children in holiday resorts, the family suite has come a long way since the 40s with a series of transformations leading to the extended stay hotel concept first tried in North America in the early 70s with the Marriot Corporation acquiring and promoting the first major portfolio in 1987.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Today, Hotel Apartments and
          <a href="http://www.valueapartments.co.uk/serviced-apartments-a-great-alternative-to-hotel-accommodation/" title="Serviced Apartments  A great alternative to hotel accommodation">
           Apart-Hotels
          </a>
          are widely regarded as a formidable alternative to the conventional model. Interest in the model has quadruple in the past 20 years with major hotel groups such as Intercontinental Hotels in the form of Staybridge Suites and Hilton in the form of Homewood Suites making their presence in the sub-sector extensively noticed and their global intentions widely represented.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Apart-Hotels are now part of the hospitality landscape in many popular cities across all continents. In fact, they have long since represented the majority of hotel properties in the Middle East and Asia. Although investors were initially slow in realising the potential in Europe due to broadly held views that suites and penthouses in hotels are reserved for the wealthy in five star surroundings, the focus has truly shifted. This is partly due to the rise in multiple hotel online booking platforms and the instant exchange of guest experiences and recommendations on review sites such as
          <a href="http://www.tripadvisor.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Tripadvisor.Com
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.holidaycheck.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Holidaycheck.com
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Furthermore, online marketing channels and tools have made it possible and cost effective for hoteliers to market new concepts or hybrids to millions providing a plethora of options for hotel guests to choose from and given service, value and design have been the three main factors considered by the majority of todays savvy hotel bookers when
          <a href="http://www.valueapartments.co.uk/hotel-accommodation-a-perfect-choice-for-holidaymakers-and-adventurers/" title="Hotel Accommodation  A perfect choice for holidaymakers and adventurers">
           booking a hotel
          </a>
          , it is no surprise that Apart-Hotels are exceeding investor expectations in both occupancy and RevPAR.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           In a recent sample survey carried out in North America in which the feedback of 2000 hotel guests was canvassed,
           <span class="highlight">
            89.2% of the sample said they would opt to stay in an Apart-Hotel instead of a typical hotel
           </span>
           if given the choice within their budget.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Although the survey confirmed what investors and hoteliers have come to realise in Europe, this is by no means a new trend elsewhere and justifiably so.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Here are a few facts about Apart-Hotels
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <ul class="bd_star_list">
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            With space at a premium in a typical hotel bedroom and bathroom, you get ample space in an apart-hotel apartment with a separate sitting area and fitted kitchen.
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Unlike in a typical hotel, there is no need to pay for hotel breakfast or queue for a breakfast table. You eat what you want when you want at a price you can afford.
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Contrary to hotel norms, there is no need to book additional rooms and there are no supplementary charges for additional sleepers making an apart-hotel cost effective for families and small groups.
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Not allowed in a standard hotel room, you have the flexible of preparing your own food in your apartment for travellers on a tight budget
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            The refrigerator in your apartment is not a mini-bar designed to make you part with your money, it is a fridge provided for your comfort and convenience
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            You get the independence of a home in a hotel environment
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            No nasty surprises on your bill at check-out. With no additional cost zones to subsidise, the price you expect to pay at check-in is the price you will pay at check-out
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            You are able to welcome visitors to your apartment without compromising the privacy of your bedroom
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            A hotel apartment is designed to accommodate both short and long stay bookings without the need to commit to an AST type contract with a large upfront deposit.
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Apart-hotels very often have guest discount arrangements with a number of restaurants, cafes, retail outlets, etc within half a mile radius offering much more for less.
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The list goes on. That noted, there is no doubt the conventional hotel will keep it niche and popularity in it various facets but with Apart-Hotels providing so much more for less, the European hotel landscape is set to change forever.
         </p>
