       <p style="text-align: left;">
        Sometimes it pays to revisit some basics the basics like effective
        <strong>
         <em>
          hamstring stretching
         </em>
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        This is an area that a surprising number of runners always seem to get get slightly wrong!
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        In this quick video above, I explain and demonstrate the difference between a stretch which brings on an intense feeling behind the knee, and a true stretch of the hamstring muscles. I call this exercise a
        <em>
         <strong>
          bent knee hamstring stretch
         </strong>
        </em>
        , or
        <em>
         <strong>
          reverse hamstring stretch
         </strong>
        </em>
        as you push back into the stretch from a flexed knee.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Sciatic Tension vs Hamstring Stretch
       </h2>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        The intense feeling or burn behind the knee that many runners associate with feeling a hamstring stretch is often actually the feeling of
        <strong>
         increasing tension on the sciatic nerve
        </strong>
        as you lean forward rather than the targeted hamstring muscles themselves.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        Instead we should be looking to create a stretching feeling in the muscle belly of each of those hamstring muscles
        <em>
         (middle of the posterior thigh)
        </em>
        , using the hamstring stretch in the video. Try it!
        <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.kinetic-revolution.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
       </p>
