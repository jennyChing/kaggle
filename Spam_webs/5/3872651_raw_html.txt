                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.littlebitfunky.com/2014/07/how-to-make-string-art-40-ideas-20.html">
                      how to make string art {40 ideas &amp; 20 minute crafter}
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7052980738780263199" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HUlVJvxpfpg/U9I0m43SxgI/AAAAAAAAVkk/kWluul7D3bk/s1600/image_14.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HUlVJvxpfpg/U9I0m43SxgI/AAAAAAAAVkk/kWluul7D3bk/s1600/image_14.jpeg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     We're in the trenches of summer right now...somewhere between "let's make the most of every day" and "how many more days until school starts?". A couple of weeks ago I promised the boys "one fun thing a day" for each day that remains in their summer...so far we've done things like playground runs, ice cream stops, cooking, and random other various things....many of which I think up at the last minute based on time, money and coupons available! This one was a little more planned, Moses really loved it and Aaron enjoyed as well!
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-kxRXVh2gprQ/U9I0ijU4BaI/AAAAAAAAVkA/186YRqyfjnA/s1600/image.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-kxRXVh2gprQ/U9I0ijU4BaI/AAAAAAAAVkA/186YRqyfjnA/s1600/image.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Here are some of the tools you will need:
                     <br/>
                     scrap wood, a print out of the letter you want to do that matches the size of your scrap wood, pliers, a hammer, small nails (make sure they have a head on them, from Lowe's), a ruler, a pencil and thin string (found at Joann's).
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-nET2_P3gHl0/U9I0ngnqa8I/AAAAAAAAVks/p3tkJUQGRpQ/s1600/image_2.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-nET2_P3gHl0/U9I0ngnqa8I/AAAAAAAAVks/p3tkJUQGRpQ/s1600/image_2.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     The first thing we did was to mark a dot every half an inch or so using the ruler and pencil.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DGlHlepTz8U/U9I0oTs_yGI/AAAAAAAAVk0/oEp8ZQe4fPk/s1600/image_3.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DGlHlepTz8U/U9I0oTs_yGI/AAAAAAAAVk0/oEp8ZQe4fPk/s1600/image_3.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then using the pliers to hold the nail (and keep them all the same height) hammer a nail into each dot you've marked.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-xFZRjGE1e2c/U9I0pH9qCVI/AAAAAAAAVk4/kIvm7CW7OpQ/s1600/image_4.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-xFZRjGE1e2c/U9I0pH9qCVI/AAAAAAAAVk4/kIvm7CW7OpQ/s1600/image_4.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Eventually, you will get to this point.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-VOYZLuGldQ4/U9I0p2SRLcI/AAAAAAAAVlA/XSw-koEBhhs/s1600/image_5.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-VOYZLuGldQ4/U9I0p2SRLcI/AAAAAAAAVlA/XSw-koEBhhs/s1600/image_5.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then peel off your paper...
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BPsXBqP5X8o/U9I0q8coCRI/AAAAAAAAVlI/GtQANp4dWZc/s1600/image_6.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BPsXBqP5X8o/U9I0q8coCRI/AAAAAAAAVlI/GtQANp4dWZc/s1600/image_6.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Get your string out, tying it to your first nail (pick one).
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-iCNV0PpJgas/U9I0s2OlC4I/AAAAAAAAVlY/Bvdh0pRmwSI/s1600/image_7.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-iCNV0PpJgas/U9I0s2OlC4I/AAAAAAAAVlY/Bvdh0pRmwSI/s1600/image_7.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     And go to town zig zagging like crazy. Moses really enjoyed this part. I worked with Aaron (5) to do this, I told him to pick a nail for me to end up on and I would zig-zag until I got there.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-aHlHvGnSyQA/U9I0tXs91QI/AAAAAAAAVlg/hlvPrekx8Ck/s1600/image_9.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-aHlHvGnSyQA/U9I0tXs91QI/AAAAAAAAVlg/hlvPrekx8Ck/s1600/image_9.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Keep going! The more you do, the bolder your design will be.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--W6baOw-PxU/U9I0hvKG-0I/AAAAAAAAVj4/VG9iJ3y26Yk/s1600/image_10.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--W6baOw-PxU/U9I0hvKG-0I/AAAAAAAAVj4/VG9iJ3y26Yk/s1600/image_10.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     To end it, make your way back to the nail you started on and knot off the string. I also tied the beginning end of the string to the end end of the string. You could also add a dab of glue to hold in in place.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nl5xap7E8tc/U9I0j7-cBaI/AAAAAAAAVkI/V_YuRV41SQE/s1600/image_11.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nl5xap7E8tc/U9I0j7-cBaI/AAAAAAAAVkI/V_YuRV41SQE/s1600/image_11.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     The result is pretty fun! The whole project cost less than $5!
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-WQbDSfQcqzo/U9I0lI3LbcI/AAAAAAAAVkQ/sZa0vI5YSa8/s1600/image_12.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-WQbDSfQcqzo/U9I0lI3LbcI/AAAAAAAAVkQ/sZa0vI5YSa8/s1600/image_12.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     When you're done you can hang them! You can also do a whole word in the same way.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-mYIa4oUI1Ow/U9I0mvLpVOI/AAAAAAAAVkc/Ekx6N7OmcFc/s1600/image_13.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-mYIa4oUI1Ow/U9I0mvLpVOI/AAAAAAAAVkc/Ekx6N7OmcFc/s1600/image_13.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Have fun making your own!

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.littlebitfunky.com/2014/07/how-to-sew-simple-skirt-project-for.html">
                      How to sew a simple skirt! {a project for the weekend}
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-357047420768741038" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dgocO_seV8E/U783S8NCZ6I/AAAAAAAAVbc/b0y73M8w7PA/s1600/skirt.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dgocO_seV8E/U783S8NCZ6I/AAAAAAAAVbc/b0y73M8w7PA/s1600/skirt.jpg" width="426"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     So I was perusing the aisles of Joann Fabrics the other day, searching for the most random kind of fabric for the most random project ever, which I'll show you another day, when this remnant caught my eye. I saw it and instantly saw the skirt it was meant to be. I love it when that happens, don't you? Even if I didn't know the whole process when I bought the fabric, I just knew that it was meant for better things than the remnant bin. So here is me, encouraging you, to just BUY that fabric you love already and don't be afraid to just make something up as you go! Sometimes you just have to live a little.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     So I saw this fabric, and knew it would be a skirt and then came home and made it up as I went. Feel free to change how I do things, I just thought it might be encouraging and fun to show you the results of "just make it up as you go". Ready?
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SUCddisD4_k/U783H46CfqI/AAAAAAAAVZo/HSydffzsxoQ/s1600/image.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SUCddisD4_k/U783H46CfqI/AAAAAAAAVZo/HSydffzsxoQ/s1600/image.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     So here was my remnant. It ended up being about a 50 inches long and about 44in wide If it helps you decide to buy a remnant or not, hold the fabric up around your waist and make sure it's as long as you need to be plus a few inches so you have room to play. You also want to make sure it goes around your waist at least 1 1/2 times or more. The selvedges on this piece are on the right and left. Since I wanted the pattern to be horizontal that worked for me. If I had wanted the stripes to be vertical I would have had to "square up" the edges and made sure I had a as close to a square as possible.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     You will also need some elastic. I used 1 1/4in because I thought the waist would be cute a little chunky.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-iY_DKNeIjfE/U783HOGr8ZI/AAAAAAAAVZc/vSpVv5MD7gI/s1600/image_1.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-iY_DKNeIjfE/U783HOGr8ZI/AAAAAAAAVZc/vSpVv5MD7gI/s1600/image_1.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     But...since I wanted horizontal...I folded the fabric by bringing the selvedges together (right sides together) and sewed along that edge.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-eT7GnlJhAWQ/U783M5DJvFI/AAAAAAAAVag/OyD6VQPGcMc/s1600/image_2.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-eT7GnlJhAWQ/U783M5DJvFI/AAAAAAAAVag/OyD6VQPGcMc/s1600/image_2.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then, I folded it in half the other way, so the wrong sides were together and the un-hemmed edges met. I know that is confusing but if you look closely you will see the edge on the left is a fold and the edges on the right are the un-hemmed ones.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-VXgmYl-ssnw/U783NpDM6OI/AAAAAAAAVak/OJnuiYUJGLQ/s1600/image_3.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-VXgmYl-ssnw/U783NpDM6OI/AAAAAAAAVak/OJnuiYUJGLQ/s1600/image_3.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     See...un-hemmed edges.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-x1xEEOY3ils/U783OGjiC2I/AAAAAAAAVaw/ji5DRmsSY2Y/s1600/image_4.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-x1xEEOY3ils/U783OGjiC2I/AAAAAAAAVaw/ji5DRmsSY2Y/s1600/image_4.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     A folded edge. The skirt will be reversible, that's a bonus right?
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-cZDZPW_ooPA/U783PFPugVI/AAAAAAAAVa4/W9vIHmCho9o/s1600/image_5.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-cZDZPW_ooPA/U783PFPugVI/AAAAAAAAVa4/W9vIHmCho9o/s1600/image_5.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Now, head on over to your iron, fold back the outer layer, iron a hem as shown above. Then tuck that layer out of the way and do the same with the outer layer.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1XtWogl1arU/U783P4-yoWI/AAAAAAAAVa8/JPTPh9w66vY/s1600/image_6.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1XtWogl1arU/U783P4-yoWI/AAAAAAAAVa8/JPTPh9w66vY/s1600/image_6.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Here's how I did that...pinned one end to my ironing board (already folded in) and pulled with my left arm and ironed with my right. :)
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ekE2JDHdVFY/U783SBwz6nI/AAAAAAAAVbQ/YUOmosDN738/s1600/image_8.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ekE2JDHdVFY/U783SBwz6nI/AAAAAAAAVbQ/YUOmosDN738/s1600/image_8.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then line up the ironed hems and pin.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-cVp7gwM3Lmg/U783ShK7uwI/AAAAAAAAVbY/ImxYw3sAOr8/s1600/image_9.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-cVp7gwM3Lmg/U783ShK7uwI/AAAAAAAAVbY/ImxYw3sAOr8/s1600/image_9.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Start at the selvedge seam so you can line them up and work around. Then sew around the edge, leaving an opening large enough to get your elastic in.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--c61pILOhr4/U783Gi0j7TI/AAAAAAAAVZY/xmmhy16WuIk/s1600/image_10.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--c61pILOhr4/U783Gi0j7TI/AAAAAAAAVZY/xmmhy16WuIk/s1600/image_10.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     See the opening? :)
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-NrI5DkWZCm4/U783IcKltWI/AAAAAAAAVZs/B8070OVt_2k/s1600/image_11.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-NrI5DkWZCm4/U783IcKltWI/AAAAAAAAVZs/B8070OVt_2k/s1600/image_11.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then gauge about how wide you need to make your "elastic pocket" and add about a half an inch or so.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-xKxIg8IPLs4/U783Jefhg9I/AAAAAAAAVZ0/8tfSRSj-_cA/s1600/image_12.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-xKxIg8IPLs4/U783Jefhg9I/AAAAAAAAVZ0/8tfSRSj-_cA/s1600/image_12.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then sew all the way around again, closing this one and staying parallel to your first sewn line.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-KXoCSd_BB6I/U783KwSP_jI/AAAAAAAAVaI/2sYt7cvnD28/s1600/image_14.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-KXoCSd_BB6I/U783KwSP_jI/AAAAAAAAVaI/2sYt7cvnD28/s1600/image_14.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then feed your elastic through, the "safety pin" method is my favorite. Make sure it doesn't get twisted.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VXUXMfTnQsI/U783LvCfaVI/AAAAAAAAVaQ/W2U3qQ2x6C8/s1600/image_15.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VXUXMfTnQsI/U783LvCfaVI/AAAAAAAAVaQ/W2U3qQ2x6C8/s1600/image_15.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then sew then ends together, pull it through and close up the opening. :) And that is IT!
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-q6wCQfMKKCI/U783MOdOIAI/AAAAAAAAVaU/7N3mHn0XBPw/s1600/image_16.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-q6wCQfMKKCI/U783MOdOIAI/AAAAAAAAVaU/7N3mHn0XBPw/s1600/image_16.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     One super cute skirt, done in about twenty minutes! No pattern, no plan, just a love for fabric. I hope this encourages you to just jump in and try! :) Have fun!

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.littlebitfunky.com/2014/05/20-minute-crafter-how-to-sew-hooded.html">
                      20 minute crafter {how to sew a hooded beach/pool towel (by sewing two straight lines!)}
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1644932628571447553" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-UozwFLCBdx0/U4fPoG1VZ_I/AAAAAAAAUv0/An0r8SL5bpw/s1600/image_5.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-UozwFLCBdx0/U4fPoG1VZ_I/AAAAAAAAUv0/An0r8SL5bpw/s1600/image_5.jpeg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     We live at the pool in the summer and the last few years the boys had a couple of pool robes (made from towels and a simplicity kids robe pattern) but this year, they no longer fit and I honestly had no interest in making new ones. So...I thought I would make them a couple of hooded towels instead! The best part is that they take just a few minutes and a few dollars to make! And AND you only have to sew TWO STRAIGHT LINES! You can do this!!!
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-PH_LfEH3aow/U4fPg7OalsI/AAAAAAAAUvc/Mc0MyoWZ-y8/s1600/image_2.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-PH_LfEH3aow/U4fPg7OalsI/AAAAAAAAUvc/Mc0MyoWZ-y8/s1600/image_2.jpeg" width="398"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Clearly he is pleased about his new towel. Can't you tell?
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-9RRkHwJiruk/U4fPaiBo-DI/AAAAAAAAUvE/iHBlHfNYw0k/s1600/IMG_1097.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-9RRkHwJiruk/U4fPaiBo-DI/AAAAAAAAUvE/iHBlHfNYw0k/s1600/IMG_1097.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     You will need a beach towel (I got mine at TJMaxx for $8) and a coordinating hand towel (or a regular towel if you plan on making multiples) cut into a 9in by 18in rectangle (with a finished edge as one of your long edges) see photo above. Fold it in half as shown above.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-24nxYxQpE2c/U4fPbweOY9I/AAAAAAAAUvM/oA_yqGOyx1w/s1600/IMG_1098.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-24nxYxQpE2c/U4fPbweOY9I/AAAAAAAAUvM/oA_yqGOyx1w/s1600/IMG_1098.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Sew along the edge opposite of your finished edge with "right" sides together. I did a straight stitch and then a wide zig-zag stitch on top to keep fraying to a minimum.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-y1DaxYvwnQg/U4fPZc8lUZI/AAAAAAAAUu8/g8T2TEfA-9s/s1600/IMG_1099.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-y1DaxYvwnQg/U4fPZc8lUZI/AAAAAAAAUu8/g8T2TEfA-9s/s1600/IMG_1099.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Find the center of your pool towel and pin it to mark it. Turn your little hood right side out and pin it on the center of your towel as shown below (right sides together).
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-yc2z4AmDfVA/U4fPqxBIRWI/AAAAAAAAUv8/oC93SRXLeEI/s1600/towel+center.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-yc2z4AmDfVA/U4fPqxBIRWI/AAAAAAAAUv8/oC93SRXLeEI/s1600/towel+center.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Now sew along this edge, again I did a straight stitch and then zig-zagged over it to prevent fraying. Now you CAN be done because I did promise you only two straight lines. See how easy that was? Feeling extra saucy now because you did so awesome? Then maybe consider the following...
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cHB8Qbhh45k/U4fSZVz5rRI/AAAAAAAAUwI/c_QIscnfppk/s1600/image_1.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cHB8Qbhh45k/U4fSZVz5rRI/AAAAAAAAUwI/c_QIscnfppk/s1600/image_1.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Add a couple of "handles" to the top corners to make your towel extra snuggle-able!
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-XTrjfnKVHuU/U4fPj26MwXI/AAAAAAAAUvk/nz-j9CRBas8/s1600/image_3.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-XTrjfnKVHuU/U4fPj26MwXI/AAAAAAAAUvk/nz-j9CRBas8/s1600/image_3.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Just grab some scrap ribbon, pin it as shown above (to the two corners on the same side as your hood).
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3qm1z-qN5i0/U4fPmq5ssgI/AAAAAAAAUvs/7YwT1AoPEsQ/s1600/image_4.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3qm1z-qN5i0/U4fPmq5ssgI/AAAAAAAAUvs/7YwT1AoPEsQ/s1600/image_4.jpeg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     And straight stitch and zig-zag again! And now not only are you done, but you're a rock star! Won't these be the perfect gift for birthdays this summer? Add some goggles and call it done!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Have fun making some! :)

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.littlebitfunky.com/2014/05/20-minute-crafter-easy-and-inexpensive.html">
                      20 minute crafter {easy and inexpensive teacher gift!
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3416107229123679498" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-sGHftgd7snc/U3SSyHeybtI/AAAAAAAAUtQ/93jwmxfxQjw/s1600/photo+3.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-sGHftgd7snc/U3SSyHeybtI/AAAAAAAAUtQ/93jwmxfxQjw/s1600/photo+3.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     It's that time of year again! School is almost out, summer is neaaaaaarly here and we are ready for it! We've had a lovely bunch of teachers this year but we are all ready for a break from schedules and rules and leaving the house every day. We are ready for the pool and popsicles and hot sun!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     But before that can happen it's time to say goodbye to those teachers! This gift is easy and can be started and finished in ONE evening! Anyone can do this! I love to give a gift the boys have helped with a little. For this project I had the boys write a simple "thank you" in their own handwriting. Want to give it a try?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     You will need:
                     <br/>
                     some solid color hand towels (lighter ones will make it easier for you)
                     <br/>
                     a clover iron on transfer pencil (found at Joanns for a couple bucks)
                     <br/>
                     embroidery floss
                     <br/>
                     a needle
                     <br/>
                     an embroidery hoop
                     <br/>
                     a small piece of interfacing
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-jWFiQWdMf5g/U3SSoTRWTdI/AAAAAAAAUs4/Qzd3l15ZEws/s1600/photo+1.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-jWFiQWdMf5g/U3SSoTRWTdI/AAAAAAAAUs4/Qzd3l15ZEws/s1600/photo+1.JPG" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     You can make these as easy or as complex as you would like. For Aaron's I only did the words and a little smiley face he added.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-a_MSdIpLQ-E/U3SSsoc_0aI/AAAAAAAAUtA/7BeIoAdcJN4/s1600/photo+2+copy.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-a_MSdIpLQ-E/U3SSsoc_0aI/AAAAAAAAUtA/7BeIoAdcJN4/s1600/photo+2+copy.JPG" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     For Moses we added his hands. You can do this using my tutorial
                     <a href="http://www.littlebitfunky.com/2010/02/20-minute-crafter-how-to-make-simple.html">
                      HERE
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-b59oyUlhmBc/U3SSZ50LgNI/AAAAAAAAUsA/vEitFgXNkvw/s1600/IMG_9965.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-b59oyUlhmBc/U3SSZ50LgNI/AAAAAAAAUsA/vEitFgXNkvw/s1600/IMG_9965.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Start by having your child write a simple note. Then go over their handwriting with a black sharpie.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-9jGpy6FfNCA/U3SSX7Wc99I/AAAAAAAAUr4/EUYG4ChI8Ok/s1600/IMG_9966.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-9jGpy6FfNCA/U3SSX7Wc99I/AAAAAAAAUr4/EUYG4ChI8Ok/s1600/IMG_9966.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     For Aaron's I had to cut up his words and tape them together in a more cohesive way so they would fit into the space I had. I then ran it through our copy machine, shrinking his image because he wrote huge. For Moses, I had to enlarge his because he wrote tiny. You can also do this on your computer.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DoMjqAEidPw/U3SSa1ht9gI/AAAAAAAAUsI/opmpGTr0Nvk/s1600/IMG_9967.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DoMjqAEidPw/U3SSa1ht9gI/AAAAAAAAUsI/opmpGTr0Nvk/s1600/IMG_9967.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then on the back of your prepared image...
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-3JXYNs2UgGk/U3SSdLD_WXI/AAAAAAAAUsQ/r_6B0JP935I/s1600/IMG_9968.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-3JXYNs2UgGk/U3SSdLD_WXI/AAAAAAAAUsQ/r_6B0JP935I/s1600/IMG_9968.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     ...trace the words. Make dark lines with your pencil, going over it a couple times. This will help with the transfer.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-df01LnGgHd8/U3SSfPNJHsI/AAAAAAAAUsc/EXgfe1kZXa0/s1600/IMG_9969.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-df01LnGgHd8/U3SSfPNJHsI/AAAAAAAAUsc/EXgfe1kZXa0/s1600/IMG_9969.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Here it is! Ready to be ironed.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fgc2FP7EPY0/U3STl2cb7RI/AAAAAAAAUtY/KfPB0qJMTgQ/s1600/IMG_9970.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fgc2FP7EPY0/U3STl2cb7RI/AAAAAAAAUtY/KfPB0qJMTgQ/s1600/IMG_9970.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Grab your towels and a piece of scrap wood that is not painted (or some other HARD surface you can think of).
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6nSbX65QeAE/U3ST0quEoAI/AAAAAAAAUtc/s9nD7OhOQVA/s1600/IMG_9971.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6nSbX65QeAE/U3ST0quEoAI/AAAAAAAAUtc/s9nD7OhOQVA/s1600/IMG_9971.jpg" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then following the instructions on the pencil iron your image onto your towel (you could also make a hoop if you'd like!). I place the wood under the towel so I can get a good hard press.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Then you are ready to embroider! For my tutorial on super basic embroidery go
                     <a href="http://www.littlebitfunky.com/2012/03/20-minute-crafter-super-basic.html">
                      HERE.
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--0l6S1qkIuc/U3SSkauZkNI/AAAAAAAAUsw/h1RI1tM99nY/s1600/photo+1+copy.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--0l6S1qkIuc/U3SSkauZkNI/AAAAAAAAUsw/h1RI1tM99nY/s1600/photo+1+copy.JPG" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     When you are done you can iron on a lightweight interfacing on the back to hide the stitches and make the towel more durable for using.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Q2FTzD5fxpc/U3SSvyLzidI/AAAAAAAAUtI/pOeJ4Z0VuYE/s1600/photo+2.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Q2FTzD5fxpc/U3SSvyLzidI/AAAAAAAAUtI/pOeJ4Z0VuYE/s1600/photo+2.JPG" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I used the towels to wrap up bottles of hand soap that I got for pennies using ePantry! If you use my referral (www.epantry.com/referrer/5682/) you can get $10 off your first order and then refer others to get $10 in store credit each time! Handsoap comes in handy for teacher and hostess gifts! Sorry for the commercial but it's worth it to earn FREE cleaning supplies, which you can do with just a couple of successful referrals each month!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Have fun making some awesome teacher gifts!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     xo
