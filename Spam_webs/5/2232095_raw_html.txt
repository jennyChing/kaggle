         <p>
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/foodnservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/tomato-stuff-tarts-final.png">
           <img alt="Spinach and Feta stuffed Heirloom tomato tarts recipe. A great vegetarian recipe to enjoy!" class="aligncenter wp-image-3847" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/foodnservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/tomato-stuff-tarts-final.png?resize=500%2C617"/>

         <h1 style="text-align: center;">
          Spinach And Feta Stuffed Heirloom Tomato Tarts Recipe
         </h1>
         <p>
          If you love farm fresh ingredients or vegetarian recipes, then youre going to absolutely LOVE this Heirloom tomato tarts recipe I have for you! The tart shells are super rustic looking in appearance, and I think it adds a certain feel from the
          <span id="more-3846">
          </span>
          mixture of heirloom tomatoes and feta cheese. Such a perfect combination of flavors together. Also, the recipe is super easy for anyone to tryeven the novice in the kitchen!
          <br/>
          Ive also been working with
          <a href="http://www.poldersoldworldmarket.com/p/home.html" target="_blank">
           Polders Old World Market
          </a>
          kitchen utensils in preparing the food and usage in my photography.
          <a href="http://www.poldersoldworldmarket.com/p/home.html" target="_blank">
           Polders O.W.M
          </a>
          . has the single most gorgeous hand carved artisan kitchen utensils that Ive seen anywhere. Period! In this recipe, Ive been able to utilize both items of their Spurtle and the their hand carved measuring cups.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/foodnservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/tomato-tarts.png">
           <img alt="Heirloom tomato tarts recipe! I love a great vegetarian recipe!" class="aligncenter wp-image-3860" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/foodnservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/tomato-tarts.png?resize=565%2C378"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive been using Polders hand carved measuring cups with everything that Ive been making lately! The handles are super easy to use, grip, and perfect for the little ones to help out in the kitchen when helping to measure ingredients!
          <a href="http://oldworldmarket.storenvy.com/collections/757344-scoops-measuring-cups/products/8084979-measuring-cups-wood-scoop-set-hand-carved-artisan-chefs-tools-1-4-1-3-a" target="_blank">
           The measuring cups come in 3 different sizes: 1/2, 1/3, and 1/4 cup measuring cups
          </a>
          . They do great in the dishwasher, I just prefer to wash them by hand. I feel they tend to stay more seasoned from the ingredients.
         </p>
         <p>
          I feel in love with this recipe. It really is more simple than what you may think. For the crust, I completely made my own, and you follow the same recipe here for my
          <a href="http://foodnservice.com/apple-dumpling-recipe/" target="_blank" title="Apple Dumpling Recipe">
           Mini Apple Dumplings recipe
          </a>
          or you can buy the pre-made pie shell sheets.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres the recipe, and enjoy!
          <br/>
         </p>
         <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
          <link href="http://foodnservice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/tomato-stuff-tarts-final-243x300.png" itemprop="image"/>

             David Murphy

            <span itemprop="recipeCategory">

            Serves:
            <span itemprop="recipeYield">
             6
            </span>
           </div>

          <div class="ERSIngredients">
           <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
            Ingredients
           </div>
           <ul>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             12 Heirloom tomatoes
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 Bag of Organic Baby Spinach
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 Tsp Fresh minced garlic
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             3 Tablespoons of Feta Cheese (crumbled)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             Sea Salt &amp; cracked pepper to taste
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             Pie Shell
            </li>
           </ul>
           <div class="ERSClear">
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSInstructions">
           <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
            Instructions
           </div>
           <ol>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Preheat oven to 375
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Cut of tops of heirloom tomatoes and hallow out the centers with a pairing knife
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Sautee garlic and baby spinach together on a low heat. The spinach will cook super fast, so be cautious in not over cooking it.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Drain baby spinach in a small colander.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Fill each tomato with baby spinach, and then place in a hand formed tart shell. I used a mini-cheesecake pan that holds up to 12, but you can also use a mini muffin tray OR just hand form them onto a piece of parchment paper on a cookie sheet.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Top each tomato tart with feta cheese on top and then place in the oven for approximately 15 minutes, or until crust is a light golden brown color.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Allow to cool and serve!
            </li>
           </ol>
