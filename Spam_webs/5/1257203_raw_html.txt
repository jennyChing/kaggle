     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Berserk
       </b>
      </i>
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">
        Japanese
       </a>
       :
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">
        
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">
        Hepburn
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Beruseruku
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">
       manga
      </a>
      series written and illustrated by
      <a href="/wiki/Kentaro_Miura" title="Kentaro Miura">
       Kentaro Miura
      </a>
      . Set in a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval">
       medieval
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
       Europe
      </a>
      -inspired
      <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_world" title="Fantasy world">
       fantasy world
      </a>
      , the story centers on the characters of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guts_(Berserk)" title="Guts (Berserk)">
       Guts
      </a>
      , an orphaned
      <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">
       mercenary
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Griffith_(Berserk)" title="Griffith (Berserk)">
       Griffith
      </a>
      , the leader of a mercenary band called the Band of the Hawk. Themes of isolation, camaraderie, and the question of whether
      <a href="/wiki/Human_nature" title="Human nature">
       humanity
      </a>
      is fundamentally
      <a href="/wiki/Problem_of_evil" title="Problem of evil">
       good or evil
      </a>
      pervade the story, as it explores both the best and worst of human nature. Both the manga and anime are noted for their heavy violence.
     </p>
     <p>
      Miura premiered a prototype of
      <i>
       Berserk
      </i>
      in 1988. The first volume of the manga was published on November 26, 1990 by
      <a href="/wiki/Hakusensha" title="Hakusensha">
       Hakusensha
      </a>
      in its
      <a href="/wiki/Hakusensha#Imprints" title="Hakusensha">
       Jets Comics
      </a>
      collection. In 1992, after the publication of three more volumes,
      <i>
       Berserk
      </i>
      began serialization in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Young_Animal" title="Young Animal">
        Young Animal
       </a>
      </i>
      . The series was adapted into a twenty-five episode
      <a href="/wiki/Berserk_(TV_series)" title="Berserk (TV series)">
       anime series
      </a>
      covering the series' first story arc by
      <a href="/wiki/OLM,_Inc." title="OLM, Inc.">
       Oriental Light and Magic
      </a>
      from October 7, 1997 to March 31, 1998. A series of films,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Berserk:_The_Golden_Age_Arc" title="Berserk: The Golden Age Arc">
        Berserk: The Golden Age Arc
       </a>
      </i>
      , were released beginning in 2012.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote">
      Further information:
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Berserk_characters" title="List of Berserk characters">
       List of Berserk characters
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guts_(Berserk)" title="Guts (Berserk)">
       Guts
      </a>
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">
        
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <i>
        Gattsu
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      is a wandering warrior and former Raid Leader of the renowned mercenary unit known as the "Band of the Hawk"
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">
        
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <i>
        Taka no Dan
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      , who hunts demonic creatures called Apostles, humans who have made a deal with a group of archdemons known as "The God Hand" that entails sacrificing whoever or whatever is closest to them in exchange for incredible supernatural power. Guts himself bears a Brand of Sacrifice from this very ritual; he is one of the only people to have ever survived it, but still has demons ceaselessly drawn to him wherever he goes, with the Brand becoming more painful and bloody the closer and more powerful his adversaries are.
     </p>
     <p>
      After a troubled childhood fighting in his adoptive father's mercenary band, Guts is recruited to the Hawks by their charismatic leader Griffith. Griffith, Guts and the Hawks play a pivotal role in ending the long war that has ravaged the country of Midland and are renowned as heroes; Griffith, however, in his ambition to become a ruler, seduces the King's daughter, leading to him being imprisoned and tortured for a year until he is rescued by his comrades. With his body horribly mutilated, rendering him a mute, immobile cripple, Griffith summons the God Hand and agrees to sacrifice his friends and mercenary group in a demonic ritual, and continue his dreams of world conquest through rebirth as a member of the God Hand itself; only Guts and his lover Casca escape alive.
     </p>
     <p>
      After being separated from Casca, who lost her memories due to the trauma of the ritual, Guts begins hunting down Apostles in search of revenge against Griffith, later reuniting with Casca just in time to rescue her from being burned as a witch. While Guts and Casca travel to the Elf country of Elfhelm seeking a safe haven, accompanied by a new group of companions they have befriended during their travels, a reincarnated Griffith creates a second Band of the Hawk and leads the fight against invading forces from the east, eventually establishing his rule over Midland and founding a new, massive capital, Falconia, where the humans take refuge from the ever increasing attacks of demons, unaware that their leader is one himself.
     </p>

     <p>

      </a>

        Young Animal
       </a>
      </i>

      <i>

        tankbon
       </a>
      </i>
      of the manga have been published in Japan. The manga has published on an irregular schedule since late 2006.

       Digital Manga Publishing
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Gl%C3%A9nat_(publisher)" title="Glnat (publisher)">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Susumu_Hirasawa" title="Susumu Hirasawa">

      </a>

He received the offer to compose music for the series due to Miura being a fan of his works and listening to them while he works on Berserk, that also led to Miura almost never requesting a specific composition to Hirasawa. Hirasawa has said that Berserk does not feel like a self-restrained story, and that composing for the series is similar to the sensation of unlocking one of his inner restraints, leading to compositions on a "Berserk" style.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_merchandise">
       Other merchandise
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other merchandise">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In addition to video games and game guidebooks,
      <i>
       Berserk
      </i>
      has spurred on a range of different
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merchandise" title="Merchandise">
       merchandise
      </a>
      , from lighters to keyrings to
      <a href="/wiki/Kubrick_(toy)" title="Kubrick (toy)">
       Kubrick
      </a>
      . Statues and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Action_figures" title="Action figures">
       action figures
      </a>
      are produced by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Art_of_War_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Art of War (company) (page does not exist)">
       Art of War
      </a>
      . A Berserk trading card game was released by
      <a href="/wiki/Konami" title="Konami">
       Konami
      </a>
      in Japan.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A figure of Guts was released by Max Factory in its Figma line in January 2012. Guts was a combatant in the popular web series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Death_Battle" title="Death Battle">
        Death Battle
       </a>
      </i>
      , where he was pitted against the antagonist of the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Soul_(series)" title="Soul (series)">
        Soulcalibur
       </a>
      </i>
      video games,
      <a href="/wiki/Nightmare_(Soulcalibur)" title="Nightmare (Soulcalibur)">
       Nightmare
      </a>
      , and won.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        ]
       </span>

     <p>
      As of June 2013, the manga has sold 24 million volumes in Japan and 7 million overseas.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">

        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Tezuka_Osamu_Cultural_Prize" title="Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize">
       Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize
      </a>
      in 2002.

       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
