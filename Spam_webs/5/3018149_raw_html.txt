         <h1 class="post-title">
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/2014/09/sweet-barn-wedding-story/">
           Sweet Barn Wedding with the Most Incredible Story Behind the Pictures
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="post-meta">
          September 12, 2014 |
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/category/inspiration/" rel="category tag">
           INSPIRATION
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/category/real-weddings/" rel="category tag">
           REAL WEDDINGS
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/category/real-weddings/rustic-weddings/" rel="category tag">
           Rustic Weddings
          </a>
         </div>
         <!--  Claire  &lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt; | &lt;a href="http://bridalmusings.com/2014/09/sweet-barn-wedding-story/#comments"&gt;&lt;span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="61952 http://bridalmusings.com/?p=61952"&gt;| 3 Comments&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/small&gt;-->
         <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
         <p>
          So were not going to lie.
         </p>
         <p>
          When it comes to swooning overreal weddings, (while we love hearing what brides and grooms have so say about their big days,) all too often, we skip the story and head straight to the pictures. Admit it, you do the same, right!
         </p>
         <p>
          But this time, we urge you to read the words behind these beautiful pictures of Christian and Saras wedding, by the wonderful
          <a href="http://www.lydiajane.com/" target="_blank">
           Lydia Jane Photography
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Because this is a lesson inwhat weddings are really all about.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-9.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 9" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61961" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-9.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          With carefulplanning and a great photographer, your wedding will look like the dream day. But what you often dont see in the pictures are the thoughtful gestures, minor mishaps and dramatic moments.
         </p>
         <p>
          This stunning bride in her
          <a href="http://www.reemacra.com/" target="_blank">
           Reem Acra
          </a>
          gown (which she bought from a
          <a href="http://www.churchstreetbridal.com/" target="_blank">
           charity shop
          </a>
          !), the beautiful weather (a sweat-inducing 90 degree day  in October!), the wonderful parents who helped create the perfect day (but left the wedding in an ambulance) and the sweet, normally shy groom, (who stepped in at exactly the right moment).
         </p>
         <p>
          In three words, Christian and Sara describe their chic barn wedding as sweet, rustic, and joyful. But we say its a feat of style, creativity, and most importantly, love.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-2.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61954" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-2.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 1" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61953" height="476" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-1.jpg" width="634"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-5.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 5" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61957" height="947" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-5.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-6.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 6" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61958" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-6.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Inspiration
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           My color palette was based around a mustard yellow color that I love, and warm neutrals (greys, creams, etc.).
           <span class="Apple-converted-space">
           </span>
           I already knew that I wanted to be married at the
           <a href="http://www.theglasgowfarm.com/" target="_blank">
            Glasgow Farm
           </a>
           , so that location played a huge role in my inspiration.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I was also inspired by herbs and wildflowers, vintage French kitchens, and several songs: Five Years Time by Noah and the Whale and Not With Haste by Mumford &amp; Sons.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           We are both musicians (I am a music teacher) and I got a lot of inspiration from the feeling of certain music.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-24.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 24" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61976" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-24.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Advice for Other Couples
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           My wedding day is proof that two timeless wedding adages are true: First, that something will always go wrong, and second, that as long as you end up married to the love of your life  it is all okay.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           If you looked just at pictures, you would think everything went off with a hitch.
           <span class="Apple-converted-space">
           </span>
           And truly, all of the things that I had been expecting to go wrong like food, favors, place cards, etc., went perfectly. However, several big things happened that were totally unexpected and, if you had told me beforehand that they were going to happen, I would have thought would ruin my wedding day.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           First of all, it was 90 degrees in October. This was some kind of cosmic joke from the universe, because I had been adamant that I didnt want to sweat on my wedding day. One of my clearest memories from the outdoor ceremony is sweat running down my legs.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Secondly, I had strep throat and a fever the entire weekend. I was one month into my first year of teaching elementary school, and all of those precious little germy children had given me a lovely strep throat infection.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I didnt know it until I went to the doctor a few days after the wedding. All weekend whenever I complained of a sore throat or feeling tired and woozy, everyone assured me it was nerves and stress.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           My bridesmaids kept me doped up on Tylenol all weekend and that plus adrenaline got me through.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-25.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 25" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61977" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-25.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Finally, my parents left my wedding reception in an ambulance. About an hour before our scheduled departure, my mom had a severe allergic reaction to, we think, the silk in my dads tie.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Her face got swollen and puffy, she went pale and limp, and actually lost her eyesight for a while. It was absolutely terrifying.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           The wonderful DJ and wedding coordinator kept most guests occupied on the dance floor, while my siblings and I stood around her on the patio holding ice chips to her forehead. The ambulance came and they went to the hospital while the party went on.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           After they left we halfheartedly did the cake-cutting and my brother gave a beautiful toast in my dads place. I held it together until we arrived at our hotel room later that night, when my husband let me just cry it out in his arms. Thankfully, she was completely fine and we got to see her the next morning looking healthy again.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           When I think back on these events, it is kind of miraculous to me that I have fond and positive memories of my wedding day.But none of these things ruined it for me. The love that was all around us on that day, from our community and for each other, trumped everything else.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           People had a wonderful time, everything looked beautiful, and I got to marry my best friend.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           So, my advice to future brides? Plan. Be organized. Dream. Get excited. But please know (and I, of all people, can say this): It is going to be a wonderful day. No. Matter. What. Happens.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           The Beatles had it right (at least when it comes to weddings) that, love is all you need.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-26.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 26" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61978" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-26.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-3.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61955" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-3.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-13.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 13" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61965" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-13.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Flowers
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           Flowers were something I had less of a vision for, so I was pretty loose in my descriptions of what I wanted.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I definitely wanted lavender and herbs like rosemary, and I knew I wanted some kind of cream rosespreferably the small tea roses and
          </em>
          <em>
           ranunculus.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I dont know how much of that got completely realized, but since I didnt have a specific vision, I trusted my florist to find one. She did a great job.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-7.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 7" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61959" height="420" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-7.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-8.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 8" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61960" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-8.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-11.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 11" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61963" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-11.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-12.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 12" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61964" height="947" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-12.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-14.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 14" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61966" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-14.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Attire
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           I wore a gorgeous
           <a href="http://www.reemacra.com/" target="_blank">
            Reem Acra
           </a>
          </em>
          <em>
           designer dress that I purchased at a bridal consignment shop for a fraction of its original price. This shop is actually a nonprofit that donates proceeds to a womens shelter, so even my purchase was a donation!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I got it fixed up and altered by a wonderful woman in Richmond. My total price after alterations for the dress was $325. The dress would have originally been $2,000!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I knew my group of bridesmaids had a wide variety of styles and personalities, and that none of us are exactly rolling in it. So I gave them a color palette and some inspiration boards and helped them find dresses that they lovedall under $100 dollars. I was going for more of a feeling than a set style: cream, taupe, lace, satin, soft, and romantic.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I wanted the groomsmen in grey, but I liked the thrown-together look (and again, didnt want them to have to spend a tonne of money, since most of us were either in college or had just graduated).
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           If they already had a grey suit I told them to wear that; if not, they found one at Mens Wearhouse.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-27.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 27" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61979" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-27.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-28.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 28" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61980" height="947" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-28.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Ceremony
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           We crafted our own vows, which was so special.
           <span class="Apple-converted-space">
           </span>
           We combined the traditional wording with some extra thoughts of our own.
           <span class="Apple-converted-space">
           </span>
           We had scripture read by friends and my brother and sister sang the hymn Be Thou My Vision, which was beautiful.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-29.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 29" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61981" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-29.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-31.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 31" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61983" height="947" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-31.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-34.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 34" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61986" height="947" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-34.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Photography
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           I was actually a wedding photographer for 3-4 years in college, so this was an important decision for me because I knew the intimate role that they play in your wedding day.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          I knew
          <a href="http://www.lydiajane.com/" target="_blank">
           Lydia
          </a>
          personally through mutual friends and had watched her business grow over the years as more and more people discovered how awesome she is.
          <span class="Apple-converted-space">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I chose her because I knew her relaxed, sarcastic, funny, and sweet personality would fit with the laid-back feeling I wanted for our wedding day.
           <span class="Apple-converted-space">
           </span>
           I also knew that I would be able to trust her implicitly to get the job done and take amazing pictures.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           On the wedding day, I didnt think about the photography once. It was partially a conscious choice to let her do her job and trust that I had hired her for a reason, and partially because she was there for every big moment.I was not disappointed.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Our wedding videographer was a friend who I knew was an incredible artist, and was just breaking into the wedding film scene.
           <span class="Apple-converted-space">
           </span>
           She has a creative eye and actually grew up on the farm where we got married, so I knew she would make its beauty come alive.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-33.jpg">

          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Refreshments
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           Our caterer was actually a food truck from Richmond that served incredible wood oven-fired pizza made from mostly local ingredients.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           They served pizza like the Fig &amp; Pig, which had fig preserves, gorgonzola cheese, and proscuitto. Incredible.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           We had appetizers like antipasti and bruscetta, and they even had gluten-free pizzas available (which is great because I am wheat intolerant). It was a little unconventional, but we LOVE pizza, so it was the totally natural choice for us. We also served wine and beer.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           <span class="s1">
            Our desserts were all lovingly handmade by friends and family  mostly all of my aunts.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            There were cakes, cookies, pies, bars, browniesyou name it.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            For our cake cutting we actually cut a heart-shaped gluten-free pumpkin bar.
           </span>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           <span class="s1">
            We had a coffee, tea, and hot chocolate bar set up, but then it was 90 degrees outside 
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
             s
            </span>
            o that didnt really get used!
           </span>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-20.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 20" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61972" height="947" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-20.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-18.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 18" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61970" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-18.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Decor &amp; Details
         </h2>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           <em>
            For the tabletops, we collected tonnes of glass bottles and jars and two of my friends filled them with sprigs of flowers and herbs before the wedding.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           <em>
            We used burlap table runners (recycled from a friends wedding) and silver platters borrowed from family and/or found at thrift shops.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           <em>
            At each place setting we had a menu, mustard yellow napkin (sewn by my mother!) and a sprig of rosemary tied together with twine. My aunt harvested all of the rosemary from a bush at her house and brought it with her!
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           <em>
            My dad built the ceremony backdrop and my mom sewed the fabric to cover it, and my dad built the place card display.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            I was inspired by herb-drying racks and so we hung each place card with a little bag full of dried lavender and stamped with a custom stamp from Etsy.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           <em>
            Guests threw the lavender on us when we left instead of rice.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            The stamp had our initials and a robot  my husband is a huge nerd so I had to throw something like that in somewhere.
           </em>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           <span class="s1">
            Our wedding favors were twofold.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            We collected hundreds of mugs in the neutral color scheme from thrift shops and tied a tag to each one that said Thank You!.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            Guests were encouraged to take one of those home.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
           </span>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           <span class="s1">
            We also had mixed CDs available to take home with songs that friends and family wrote, either previously or specifically for our wedding.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            It was incredibly special for us.
           </span>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-21.jpg">

          </a>
         </p>

          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-16.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 16" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61968" height="420" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-16.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>

          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-16.jpg">

          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Music
         </h2>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           <span class="s1">
            We were both music majors in college (we met in conducting class!) so music was very important to us.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
           </span>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           <span class="s1">
            I walked down the aisle to a
            <a href="www.rorytyerband.com" target="_blank">
             song my brother wrote
            </a>
            called This is How it Feels to Come Home. Our recessional was Not With Haste by Mumford &amp; Sons.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
           </span>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           <span class="s1">
            Our first dance was to First Day of My Life by Bright Eyes.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            My father and I danced to When You Come Back Down by Nickel Creek.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
            My husband and his mother danced to In My Life by The Beatles.
            <span class="Apple-converted-space">
            </span>
           </span>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <em>
           <span class="s1">
            We entered the reception to the Star Wars theme song.
           </span>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-39.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 39" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61991" height="947" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-39.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Favourite Moment
         </h2>
         <p>
          <em>
           When I think about what made our wedding special, I think of all the labors of love that went into it from our family and friends. My bridesmaids and groomsmen helped set up the reception, my parents did so many DIY projects, my extended family stepped in and helped so much. It was truly a community effort to give us the wedding of our dreams and I am so, so grateful.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           One of my favorite moments was when my dad saw me for the first time. We have a close relationship and he is a total weeper, so I knew he would lose it as soon as he saw meand he did.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Another favorite was when we said our vows. Just the feeling of saying them aloud and knowing that our community of friends and family was listening and agreeing to support us in our marriage was priceless.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           The moment that I will remember the most (and if you havent read the advice section yet, stop and read that first before reading this!) was after my mom and dad left the reception in an ambulance.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           We were about to do toasts and Christian and I had gotten up to thank our guests. I felt like I needed to address what had just happened with my mother, so I told the guests that everything was okay, she was going to the hospital, etc.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           We are people of faith and so I told our guests, I know not all of you share our faith, but I want to take a moment and pray for my mom together. I was about to start praying on the microphone when I just lost it. I knew as soon as I started talking I would begin to cry. I silently handed it over to Christian (who does not like to talk in public as much as I do) and without missing a beat, he prayed a beautiful prayer for my mother.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           It was in that moment that I knew he was my husbandthat we were a team. I knew I could rely on him and that we were strong together and for one another. It was a beautiful reminder of what the wedding day was really about.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-38.jpg">
           <img alt="Sweet Barn Wedding | Lydia Jane Photography | Bridal Musings Wedding Blog 38" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-61990" height="419" src="http://bridalmusings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Sweet-Barn-Wedding-Lydia-Jane-Photography-Bridal-Musings-Wedding-Blog-38.jpg" width="630"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Wow, such a beautiful wedding.
         </p>
         <p>
          But what great advice from Christian and Sara  and proof that you shouldnt put pressure on yourself to create the perfect day, because even behind the most gorgeous wedding pictures, is plenty of drama, mishaps, and perspiration!
         </p>

          </a>
          and of course Christian and Sara for sharing their rustic barn wedding with us.
