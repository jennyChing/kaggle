         <b>
          Want to find a mate? Location, location, location!
         </b>
         <a class="inline ">
          <span>
           Top/Best/Most
          </span>
         </a>

          Monday, October 27, 2014
         </span>
         <br/>

       <div>
        <font color="">
         <br/>
         Americans either don't want to get married or are having trouble finding a mate. Whatever the reason, the percentage of Americans who have never been married is at a record high, 20 percent, according to the
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.pewsocialtrends.org/interactives/marriage-market/">
          <span>
           Pew Research
          </span>
         </a>
         Center.
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.pewsocialtrends.org/interactives/marriage-market/">
          <span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Thats 1 in 5 Americans and doesn't even count those who are divorced or widowed. That 20 percent is made up of adults who have NEVER been married.
         <br/>
        </font>
       </div>
       <div>
        <div>
         <div>
          <div>
           <img align="right" alt="" border="0" src="http://r.m.yahoo.com/api/res/1.2/IAh3xXLmQq59soB7K7NPdw--/Zmk9ZmlsbDtoPTEyO3E9SGlnaDt3PTEy/https://s.yimg.com/vs/images/link.png"/>
           <a href="http://3221818.r.msn.com/?ld=b2Scn1mv1mSprqQq715LQctDVUCUzr2dkRyREw_gXPYfJ85BclI7_vdikEI_kYKgyMwhKmDGb0hD6lk90zl_dgc3Us4wJc4CW3-134UyQqfkXaq7ZjaY1K7B5InRn6AyJNK78wSQ&amp;u=investors.changingtechno.com%2f%3futm_source%3dBing%26utm_medium%3dCPC%26utm_campaign%3dBing%2520Ads">
            <span class="ad-title">
             <font color="">
              New Stock Pick - CHGT
             </font>
            </span>
           </a>
           <div>
            <font color="#8d8c8d">
             <span class="ad-grey">
              <font color="">
               investors.changingtechno.com
              </font>
             </span>
            </font>
           </div>
           <br clear="all"/>

        <br/>
       </div>
       <div>
        <font color="">
         <br/>
         There have been studies on whether people even want to get
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.examiner.com/article/more-couples-skipping-marriage-and-choosing-to-live-together">
          <span>
           married
          </span>
         </a>
         . For one thing, the average wedding in the U.S. now reportedly tops
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.costofwedding.com/%20">
          <span>
           $25,000
          </span>
         </a>
         .
         <br/>
         <br/>
         But for those who do, if youre looking for Mr. or Mrs. Right, you may want to pack your bags.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Pew looked at the ratio of single men to single women between the ages of 25 and 34 in Americas cities. "For those in the 'marriage market,' location matters," the report claims. Not only did Pew run the numbers on the male-to-female ratio, it dug deeper to look at where you have the best chance of finding someone single with a job. "Finding a spouse with a steady job is a high priority for 78 percent of never-married women who may want to get married in the future (and 46 percent of men), though the pool of employed young men has shrunk," researchers said. That pool may have shrunk, but it's still a bigger pool than the pool of available young women with jobs.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Some surprises. For those women thinking Alaska is brimming with available guys, Pew said in Anchorage there are only 117 single young men for every 100 single young women. There are more women there than youd think. On the other hand, do you think "All the good ones are taken" in New York City? Nope. Single men outnumber single women in the Big Apple, and I assume a few of them are "good ones."
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Many cities have equal numbers of available guys and gals: Akron, Baton Rouge, Birmingham, Charlotte, and Nashville to name a few. (By the way, Pew only looked at the ratio of men to women. Given the current rollout of legalized gay marriage, perhaps a same-sex eligibility map will follow.)
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Here are the best places to head with your U-Haul if you want to find an eligible mate. The best cities for women are the worst for men, and vice versa.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         FOR WOMEN:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         #3 - Clarksville, Tennessee. Take the last train to this city made famous by the 1960s pop/rock group The Monkees, where there are 188 available young men to every 100 available young women. Perhaps this is because Clarksville is close to Ft. Campbell and the 101st Airborne. Lots of men in uniform.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         #2 - Mansfield, Ohio. This rust belt town is home to the Klondike Bar. What would you do for a husband? Move here! Single young men outnumber single young women by more than 2-to-1.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         #1 - Hanford/Corcoran, California. Don't let the fact Charles Manson lives here scare you off. Hey, by the way, I hear he's single! This area of California's Central Valley is known for agriculture and oil and its prison, and that means lots of men. Lots and lots of men. Pew said there are 236 available men between the ages of 25 and 34 for every 100 single women in the same age group.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         FOR MEN:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         #3 - Madera, California. This community is also in the Central Valley, and Pew found there are 121 single young women for every 100 single young men. You know, Madera is only 50 miles away from Hanford/Corcoran, so those two areas need to do some speed dating social media meet ups!
         <br/>
         <br/>
         #2 - A tie! Parkersburg, West Virginia, where the slogan is "A Beautiful Place to Call Home," and Lewiston, Maine, where the slogan is, "The Sky's the Limit!" Maybe both should change their slogans to "Men Wanted." Each city has 124 single young gals for every 100 single young guys.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         #1 - Rocky Mount, North Carolina. Not really sure why the male-to-female ratio is out of whack here in the birthplace of Thelonious Monk, but the women there would sure like to jazz the place up with more men. In this city with a growing arts scene, there are 1.4 young single women for every available young guy.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         BUT WHAT ABOUT JOBS?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Where are there the most employed single young people of the opposite sex?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Ladies, head to Fayetteville, Arkansas (124 single young guys with jobs for every 100 gals), San Luis Obispo, California (125 per 100), and once again take that last train to Clarksville, Tennessee (145 per 100!).
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Men, no city in the Pew study showed more women having jobs than available men, but a couple cities come close. In Racine, Wisconsin, there are 95 young single women with jobs for every 100 single young men. But clawing its way to the top is good ol' Lewiston, Maine, with 99 women with a job per 100 young single guys. What kind of jobs do the women in Lewiston have? According to its "
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.ci.lewiston.me.us/DocumentCenter/View/">
          <span>
           relocation guide
          </span>
         </a>
         ," the biggest employment sectors are education, health services, and retail.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Men, move to Maine and meet a woman who can teach you, heal you, and dress you. You're welcome.
         <br/>
