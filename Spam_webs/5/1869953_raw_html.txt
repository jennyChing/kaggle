           <a href="http://www.crayonphysics.com/">
            Crayon Physics
           </a>
           quickly traveled around the interweb as a small physics sandbox game. Back then, there wasnt too much game to it; it was simply fun to play around with.
          </p>
          <p>
           Then Crayon Physics Deluxe was announced  a sequel that sought to extend and perfect the game. News and screenshots have been flying around for a while, but earlier this month it was finally released in full glory.
          </p>
          <p>
           Yes, the game is retail, normally selling at around 20 bucks a pop. But for the occasion, a special birthday deal was announced. Counting on the good of your heart, you can pick your own price for the game. That is, until 15 January (closing in fast).
           <br/>
           <span id="more-29563">
           </span>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Crayon Physics Deluxe
          </h3>
          <p>
           The concept of the game is rather simple; collect the stars with a little red ball. You cant control the ball itself, but you can manipulate the level environments. With a digital crayon in hand, you can set off to solve the puzzles. Theres nothing more entrancing than seeing your drawings come to life.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center; ">
           <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="385" width="480">

            </param>
            <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/avkacGQKWec&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;rel=0" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
            </embed>
           </object>
          </p>
          <p>
           The interesting part of Crayon Physics Deluxe is that there are countless ways to solve each puzzle. There is no right way to beat it, there are dozens! The only thing you need to complete this game is a vast and lively imagination.
          </p>
          <p>
           In Crayon Physics Deluxe, there are over 70 original levels. Add to that a level designer, so in a way, the fun never has to stop. Complete with relaxing music, Crayon Physics Deluxe is an instant classic for puzzlers and physics enthusiasts alike.
          </p>

          <h3>
           Pay What You Want (till 15 January)
          </h3>
          <p>
           With the games launch fresh in memory, the developer has set up a special birthday deal. Normally costing around twenty bucks, you can now purchase the game for whatever amount you feel like paying. Look into your heart (and your wallet), and pick the price yourself!
          </p>
          <p>
           Other developers have tried it in the past, including 2DBoy with the much famed
           <em>
            World of Goo.
           </em>
           Even a number of musicians managed to pull it off with success.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="crayon physics deluxe" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/Screen-shot-2010-01-09-at-20.30.40.png?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Do note that this limited deal is only applicable until the 15th of January. That puts quite the time pressure on you. So
           <a href="http://www.crayonphysics.com/">
            go to the site
           </a>
           , and download the demo. Fiddle around a bit before you decide how much you want to spend.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Cool! So I can practically get it for free?
          </h3>
          <p>
           No. I mean, yes, you
           <em>
            can
           </em>
           , but you wont. Heres why.
          </p>
          <p>
           When you buy a game from EA, Ubisoft, or another big game producer, a big part of the money is lost to the middleman. The part that actually reaches the developer(s) is often quite small. But there is no big firm behind Crayon Physics. Not even a team. The game is produced by one guy only; Petri Purho, a long-haired dude from Finland.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="crayon physics deluxe download" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/gdc_purho_art_jpg_595x325_crop_upscale_q85.jpg?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           You see, Petri Purho worked two years to complete this game, and although the choice is yours, Im sure you agree he deserves a little credit. Paying $1 (like many do) is not smart, its just disrespectful. Im not saying you cant profit from the sale, thats the enticement of the whole deal! Just make your choice wisely.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.crayonphysics.com/">
            Crayon Physics Deluxe
           </a>
           runs on Windows only.
          </p>
