         <section>
          <h2 class="hide">
           How to Get the New iWork Apps for Free in Mac OS X Mavericks
          </h2>
          <p>
           If you didn't already know, Apple is giving away their newest operating system,
           <a href="http://mac-how-to.wonderhowto.com/how-to/create-bootable-install-usb-drive-mac-os-x-10-9-mavericks-0149169/">
            Mavericks
           </a>
           , for most of your computers. While the folks over at Microsoft surely despise this tactic, those of use Mac users still running Snow Leopard surely do appreciate it.
          </p>
          <p>
           While one free thing is cool, how about more free things? Apple has already been giving away the iWork apps for
           <a href="http://ios.wonderhowto.com/how-to/get-your-free-ilife-iwork-ios-apps-after-restoring-from-backup-0149021/">
            newly purchased iOS devices
           </a>
           , but what about us Mac users? If you already own the iWork suite, upgrading is free, but if you've never owned it, things get tricky, but not impossible. You can still get iWork for free, you just have to trick the Mac App Store into thinking you already have it.
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="52082252docPartGal880019" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <img alt="How to Get the New iWork Apps for Free in Mac OS X Mavericks" height="532" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/74/86/63518755902813/0/get-new-iwork-apps-for-free-mac-os-x-mavericks.w654.jpg" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;" width="532"/>
             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p>
           Before you begin, make sure that you have
           <a href="http://mac-how-to.wonderhowto.com/how-to/create-bootable-install-usb-drive-mac-os-x-10-9-mavericks-0149169/">
            Mac OS X Mavericks 10.9
           </a>
           installed first, otherwise it's pointless, because the new iWork apps
           <a href="http://support.apple.com/kb/SP676?viewlocale=en_US" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            only work with it
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </section>
         <section>
          <h2 class="step">
           Step 1: Download the Trial Version of iWork
          </h2>
          <p>
           In order to fool the Mac App Store, you'll need to download an old trial version of iWork, such as this
           <a href="http://mac.softpedia.com/get/Business/Apple-iWork-06.shtml" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Apple iWork '09 Suite version
           </a>
           . Download it and install it onto your computer, no need to open it or anything.
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="1893521docPartGal880005" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
              <img alt="How to Get the New iWork Apps for Free in Mac OS X Mavericks" height="532" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/84/67/63518750846856/0/get-new-iwork-apps-for-free-mac-os-x-mavericks.w654.jpg" style="max-width:532px;height:auto;" width="532"/>
             </figure>
            </div>
           </div>
          </figure>
         </section>
         <section>
          <h2 class="step">
           Step 2: Install the iWork Suite
          </h2>
          <p>
           Once it's done downloading, install it onto your Mac. For some, you might receive the warning below, which says it can't be opened.
          </p>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="25673683docPartGal880009" role="group">
           <div class="gallery-layout">
            <div class="gallery-layout-container">
             <figure class="gal-mgb" data-index="0">
