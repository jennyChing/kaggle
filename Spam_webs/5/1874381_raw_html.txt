       <span class="media-body">
        <p>
         <span>
          <span>
           Concrete is one of the most used building products in the world, and its also one of the
          </span>
         </span>
         <span>
          heaviest and hardest to handle. Builders that must order cement usually order more than is needed to complete their task.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          After all, coming up short could be devastating to the pour as well as to the budget. All too often excess cement is disposed of, wasting the embedded carbon footprint of cement.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          Now there's a way to use excess cement on site, recovering both the cost and carbon invested in the product.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          New reusable light weight concrete block molds allow for the recovery of leftover cement on the job site while producing a value added product.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          In most cases the landscaping blocks produced on the job site out of waste cement end up being used on the same site during the final landscaping portion of the project.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          For any regular user of concrete, new product can help you develop more sustainable construction practices and get more out the budget.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          Popularity is also growing among the "Do It Yourselfers" as they can now make their own custom colored blocks or add monograms to the block face to make a
         </span>
         truly unique block for their hardscaping project.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://formablok.com" target="_blank">
          Formablok
         </a>
         molds are a new product invented and manufactured in Canada while being sold locally as well as internationally.
        </p>
        <p>
         Read more about
         <a href="http://www.proudgreenhome.com/research/566/Concrete-Forms">
          concrete forms
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </span>
