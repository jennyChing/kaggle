This natural phenomenon, occurring in latitudes north of the Arctic Circle, happens when the sun stays above the horizon for 24 hours. Slumber may not come as easy, but it'll hardly seem a priority once you realize that you no longer have to wait until the waking hours to take in the beauty of Norway's jagged fjords and dense hardwood forests.

Your adventure doesn't have to stop because of the lack of sunlight. Further yet is Troms, where you'll find 18th-century wooden homes sitting along the ocean's edge under the uninterrupted sunbeams and green peaks jutting skyward in the lit horizon.
