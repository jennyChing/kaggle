         <h1>
          6 Majors that Payoff and the Jobs that Write the Check
         </h1>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="article-container" id="TNArticleBodySection">
        <div id="article-body">
         <p>
          <img alt="female holiding college degree looking at a screen with job offers" class="media-image media-image-left" src="http://artstg.icbdr.com/sites/all/files/iStock_000031283418XSmall.jpg" style="line-height: 1.5; margin: 4px 10px; width: 337px; float: left; height: 356px" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
          With the cost of education increasing each year, and start-up success stories of millionaire twenty-something dropouts on countless magazine covers, many wonder if a college degree is even worth it anymore. However, there are still some very lucrative majors out there. Many even cover the cost of attending college with one year's salary and no advanced degree. Here are the majors that pay out and the jobs they can help you land.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <p>
          While there are multiple types of degrees in engineering, petroleum engineer tops the best paying position according to
          <a href="http://www.payscale.com/college-salary-report-2014" target="_blank">
           PayScales's 2013-2014 College Salary Report
          </a>
          . Petroleum engineers work for oil companies and they develop methods to extract petroleum products from the earth. While an advanced degree is not essential, many petroleum engineers have one. Fieldwork and travel can be a big part of this position. The pay begins at around $103,000, with an average mid-career salary of $160,000. The annual compensation for this position has increased 40.18 percent nationwide over the past decade.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <p>
          The third highest position on the PayScale's Report is also in engineering and it is nuclear engineering. Nuclear engineers look for ways to capture the energy from a disintegrating atom. Having a strong background in math and science is essential for this job. The average salary in this position is $67,600, with a mid-career salary of $117,000, and about 30 percent of those working in this field have master's degrees. The emphasis on a more green economy has bolstered the opportunities and job openings in this area.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <p>
          Other high-scoring mentions inengineering:
         </p>
         <br/>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Chemical Engineering - $68,200. While it starts off higher than nuclear engineering, it has a mid-career salary of $115,000
          </li>
          <li>
           Aerospace Engineering - $62,800. Mid-career - $109,000
          </li>
          <li>
           Electrical Engineering - $64,300. Mid-career - $106,000
          </li>
          <li>
           Computer Engineering - $65,300. Mid-career - $59,800
          </li>
          <li>
           Mechanical Engineering - $60,900. Mid-career - $99,700
          </li>
          <li>
           Materials Science and Engineering - $62,700. Mid-career - $99,500
          </li>
         </ul>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Mathematics
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          There's been a shift recently with the increase in dependence on technology and the ability to generate more data. It has brought about increasing opportunities in actuarial mathematics. The ability to analyze statistical data and apply them, to derive things like probability tables and forecasting risk and liability, has become important to industries like insurance.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, actuaries do more than just work in the insurance field. They can find out the effect of legislation on things like business and chart probabilities of natural disasters. The starting median salary is $58,700 and the average mid-career salary is $120,000. The job outlook for this position categorizes it as one of rapid growth and opportunity.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          Statistics is also an area of rapid growth and high salary for many of the same reasons that actuaries are seeing an increase in their value in the marketplace. The median starting salary for this position is $52,500 and mid-career salary average of $98,900.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center">
          Looking for a job in IT? Check out these openings for
          <a href="http://workat.careerbuilder.com/search/software-engineer-jobs/ST101000000000000000/" target="_blank">
           Software Engineers
          </a>
          !
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center">
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Management Information Systems/Business
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          This specialized business degree boasts an impressive average starting salary of $60,300. From 2012 to 2013, the average salary paid to college graduates of this major saw an increase of 7.9 percent, one of the largest among the highest paying majors.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          An MIS career focuses on matching and analyzing resources for companies. Graduates with this degree can find careers as IT consultants, Information Systems managers ($115,780, with a forecasted 18 percent growth level in opportunities from 2010-2020), Business Intelligence analysts ($78,160, with a forecasted 22 percent growth level) or network administrators (median income $69,160, with forecasted growth of 28 percent),
          <a href="http://mays.tamu.edu/info/what-is-mis/" target="_blank">
           among other positions
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Computer Science
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          While still a very lucrative career with a median starting salary of $59,800 and a mid-career median salary of $102,000, according to data released by the National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE), recent graduates who had majored in computer science saw a 2.5 percent decrease in salaries from 2012 to 2013. The degree of computer science is a larger category, and while computer science majors are faring well in the job market, information science (IS) degrees are taking a hit. IS degrees are considered a division within computer science so the decline in the IS salary is what is causing the decline in the computer science salary.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          With a degree in computer science, you can be a software engineer, which starts out with a slightly higher salary than the average computer science job at $60,500, with a (current) mid-career salary of $99,300. Other possibilities include a systems engineer and software developer. The job outlook for this major is good because employment opportunities are projected to increase 19 percent from 2010-2020.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Logistics/Materials Management
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          This major prepares graduates to plan and direct resources such as materials and equipment. While some two-year colleges offer this as a focus in a business management program, graduates with this four-year degree can expect to make, on average, $59,500. Graduates in logistics/materials management can go on to become logisticians.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          The median pay for this position is $70,800 and the job outlook for 2010-2020 is well above average (14 percent) at 26 percent. Other opportunities include industrial production managers ($87,160) or operation research analysts ($70,960).
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Physics
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          The scientific study of matter and energy is also among the highest paying majors at an average starting salary of $53,100 and a mid-career salary of $101,000 on average.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          In addition to working in the lab or teaching, many physics majors use their deductive reasoning and math skills in test engineering, technical advising, research, rocket science and more. Finding a career in physics depends largely on your focus in school. Two exciting areas physicists are finding work in is the environmental science field and the petroleum industry. A strong basis in physics allows for a great understanding of the earth's core and atmosphere.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <p>
          One thing is clear when reviewing the top earning college majors: engineering, science and technology top the list. If you are good in science and mathematics, your salary will reflect it.
         </p>
         <br/>
         <p>
          According to last year's analysis of government data, 53 percent of graduating college students haven't gainfully employed. Selecting a major that is both high paying and in-demand may be a way to safeguard your future and make sure your degree doesn't go to waste.
         </p>
