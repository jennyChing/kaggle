         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Fire-breathing, Neck-snapping: The UltimateCapri
         </h1>
         <hr/>
         <dl>
          <dt>

          </dt>
          <dd>
           <a class="url fn" href="http://www.speedhunters.com/author/2281433439/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jonathan Moore">

       <div class="large-12 columns pointable chapter-nav-next" style="display:none">
        <h2>
         Next Chapter &gt;
        </h2>
       </div>
       <div class="chapter-content">
        <p>
         Theres naturally a direct link between the cars that I had as toys and model kits as a child in the 80s and the cars that still stop me dead in my tracks today. I loved my 1/43rd rallycars (Audis, Saabs, Lancias), my 1/32nd Peugeot 205 T16, Matchbox Porsche K3s and fleet of F1 cars. They were all cars that Id never seen in real life, but even as a kid I just
         <em>
          knew
         </em>
         they were special. What I really held up on a pedestal were my fathers Tamiya model kits, painstakingly put together and liveried. Four cars always stood out. A Jaguar XJR9-LM. A Martini-liveried Porsche 935/77. The silver wings of a Ferrari 312 T3 F1.
        </p>
        <p>
         And then there was the Zakspeed Capri.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-002.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110180" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-002.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         My father owned a 2.0s MkIII Capri in the 80s, in which we used to bomb around Europe (as much as you could with a family of four crammed in and a frame-tent the size of Versailles in the boot). In my head, there was a direct relationship between our lithe white coup, with its go-fast S stripe down the side (surprisingly, its number plate popped back into my head as I was writing this!), and the brutal German Group 5 machine which sat on the shelf at home.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-003.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110181" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-003.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I tried modifying a regular Capri model kit to look like the Zakspeed car, but it just didnt work. Nothing could look as aggressive or as striking as the real thing. I couldnt imagine ever seeing one in real life, especially as they had only once crossed the Channel to race at Donington in 1980, when I was still using stabilisers, let alone holding a steering wheel.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-004.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110182" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-004.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I might have had to wait 25 years, but it was more than worthwhile. This 1981 Group 5 Zakspeed Ford Capri Turbo is sitting in the Zakspeed teams factory in Niederzissen. Its been the subject of a painstaking seven-year restoration by original Zakspeed engineer Marco Fichter, which now sees the car in immaculate, as-new condition. Its a beautiful racing car. It is also a bellowing monster: all flame, wings and raw visceral speed.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-005.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110183" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-005.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Ford were no stranger to aiming punches at those that considered themselves a strata above in automotive terms. Racing saloons were a given, but Ford Formula 1 engines had taken on and beaten Ferrari in the 60s, and Ford had then stepped into the ring to take on the same marque at Le Mans with the GT40  and won. Into the 70s, Ford engines still dominated F1, though saloon cars continued to be bread and butter for the firm, run out of the Cologne operation in Germany.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-006.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110184" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-006.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The wow factor of the Capri coup had been the perfect weapon to keep Ford in the headlines during the 70s, and the famous Cologne Capri MkIs battled BMWs, Alfas and Opels across Europe. But even a global company like Ford couldnt avoid the 70s fuel crisis: midway through the decade Cologne was closed down, signalling the end of Fords in-house European racing facility. As the decade moved on, independent teams continued to campaign Capris and Escorts, and there was one team that crossed over with the factory era: Zakspeed. Theyd developed their own highly-tuned BDA-engined Escort RS1600 MkI in cooperation with Ford, representing the Blue Oval in both the European Touring Car Championship and the national Deutsche Rennsport Meisterschaft.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-007.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110185" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-007.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The DRM. Theres something in the water in Germany that means that the country is addicted to having the highest powered, most over the top racers possible in their own national series. Modern DTM racers are the natural descendants of this series, which ran between 1972 and 1985. DRM started off as a pure touring car championship, but then the organisers decided to open up the championship to the fearsome new turbocharged Group 5 silhouette racers, perhaps as a result of a heavy night and half-closed eyes when signing off the regs for 1977.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-008.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110186" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-008.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Not only did it prosper, but it even hoovered up more cars than the international series the fourth generation FIA Group 5 rules had been intended for, the World Championship For Makes. Porsche 935 battled BMWs 320i, Lancias brutal Beta Montecarlo and
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/tag/kremer/" target="_blank" title="Kremer Racing">
          Kremers 911 on steroids, the K3
         </a>
         , as the 80s rolled into view.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-009.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110187" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-009.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         It was the perfect championship for German teams. If theres one thing the Germans like its the excuse of enormous power in extreme cars, and Zakspeed had been more than happy to oblige Ford with the classic black, red and yellow, Mampe-liveried (among others) Group 2/5 Escort MkII. With the MkIII Capri due to be released in Europe in 78, Ford wanted the car back in competition  and racing at the highest level possible. So how about Group 5? Zakspeeds telephone rang again.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-010.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110188" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-010.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The result was the Zakspeed Capri Turbo. Boy racer against the elite. But it was definitely not David versus Goliath, not with what went into this car.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-011.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110189" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-011.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Group 5 rules dictated that the lines of the production model needed to be maintained above the wheel arches, the suspension layout had to be unchanged and the engine taken from a production unit in the manufacturers line. Aside from that Make it wide. Make it low. Make it fast.
         <em>
          Very
         </em>
         fast. This gave Zakspeed team chief Erich Zakowski and Ford Europes Chief Engineer Thomas Ammerschalger a free brief to create a far more purebred racing car than had been allowed with the MkI, and the raked windscreen and long lines of the Capri was an ideal platform for creating a car to fight the BMWs and Porsches on the same terms.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-012.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110190" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-012.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         As with most other Group 5 monsters, that meant the A and C pillars were the only things taken from the production car, aside for the odd ancillary like the rear lights.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-013.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110191" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-013.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The Capri was chopped and lowered to the extent that the entire car barely came up to the sill of the original, and big wings and fenders added.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-014.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110192" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-014.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         And when I say big, I mean huge. Group 5 was all about overwhelming quantities of aero, maxing out the car to the extreme dimensions of the rules: Bszoku style draws directly from this era of racing car. When you look at the 1978 Zakspeed Escort from a three-quarter angle, you can also see how much the Capri drew from Zakspeeds experience with that car: the fenders and wing are very similar.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-015.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110193" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-015.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The wheels are BBS split rims, with magnesium centres and alloy outers: 16 at the front and enormous 19 rims at the back, further adding to the Capris aggressive, nose-down poise.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-016.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110194" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-016.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The chassis used proven spaceframe technology, as with most cars of the time, with a welded tubular rollcage: 80 metres of aluminium tubing went into its construction, and the entire structure weighed just 75kg. Scale models were used in the pre-production phase to find the optimal chassis stiffness and balance. The roof section with pillars from a donor MkIII was then welded to the resulting space frame.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-017.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110195" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-017.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Thin-gauge aluminium sheeting was used to create the cockpit floor and bonded to the spaceframe  so thin that the slightest pressure would rupture it, meaning built-in air jacks werent a luxury but a necessity unless you wanted to jack the cockpit up into the roof.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-018.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110196" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-018.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Kevlar-reinforced plastic was used for all the major body panels, creating a massive weight saving, and a plethora of Dzus fasteners secured everything in place.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-019.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110197" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-019.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         This created a virtual model kits worth of body panels, and gave rise to the classic dismantled Capri marketing photograph  which Ive tried to homage here!
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-020.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110198" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-020.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Although the front lights werent dictated in the rules (hence the slab front of the 935), Zakspeed stuck with the stock slatted grill and iconic quad-lights, further increasing the Group 5 cars link with the road-going market.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-021.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110199" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-021.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         As with modern racers, the driver is positioned as far to the rear as possible, with the wheel and main instrument cluster adjustable to suit. Ludwig managed to get one concession to comfort  the cut-out to the right of the bucket seat to give him more room for shifting. In front, the switchgear was kept to an absolute minimum, with the focus on a small set temperature gauges covering the critical functions.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-022.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110200" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-022.jpg" title="Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-022"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Two things stand out on the dash, literally: oversized dials for controlling turbo boost (top) and brake balance (bottom). What a refreshing change from over-cluttered modern dashes, where you have to focus on which dial to go for whilst under pressure. Big top one:
         <em>
          make fast faster
         </em>
         . Big bottom one:
         <em>
          make different slows
         </em>
         . Perfect!
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-023.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110201" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-023.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         In the rear is the 100-litre FIA-spec ATL fuel cell along with the battery, the four four fuel pumps and the dry sump.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-024.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110202" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-024.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Everything was of the highest standard. Wiring through the car was aerospace standard: the Zakspeed name flowed through the Capri, but Ford investment provided that extra boost and half-mil silver-wire cabling was used. More fractional weight savings, but every gram would count. The Capri Turbo would weigh in at just 790kg.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-025.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110203" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-025.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         So it was light, but was it fast? The engine might have seemed teeny tiny in the cavernous nose, but when you examine it you realise just what insanity is at work. The block is mounted well back in the chassis and slightly offset to help optimise weight distribution, but whats up front gives the game away.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-026.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110204" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-026.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Initially the Capri was entered into the DRM Division 2 class (for sub 2-litre NA engines or 1.4-litre turbos) with a Kent-based unit: a poor little 60s-spec inline-four BDA was rebuilt by Zakspeed and smothered with bullying twin KKK turbochargers, Garret intercoolers and Bosch fuel injection.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-027.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110205" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-027.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The power was delivered through a Getrag-sourced, five-speed gearbox: 380bhp and a top speed of 170 mph was hardly a bad result  but the pressure on the small engine caused numerous problems, particularly broken pistons, camshafts and cracked rotors.
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/2013/01/citroen-supercar/" target="_blank" title="Citroen DS3 Supercar">
          Its a common problem even today  vis the modern rallycross Supercars
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-028.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110206" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-028.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         These issues were got round by moving to a single but larger KKK turbocharger and using twin intercoolers. A blow-off valve controlled the level to keep things within the regulation limits. The Garrett intercoolers were cast by Zakspeed, and the dual layout adds that extra kick of cooling before the air is charged into the turbo: originally there were a pair because of the two turbos, but it was found that the layout worked well even with a single turbo to feed.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-029.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110207" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-029.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         By the end of 1980, the Division 2 car was delivering over 450bhp at 9200rpm. One thing youll notice here is that theres an important part of the power delivery system missing
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-030.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110208" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-030.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         as it was on the workbench awaiting reassembly!
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-031.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110209" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-031.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The primitive ECU (by modern standards) and ignition management are located under a service hatch by the transmission tunnel, with the fuel injection provided by Bosch. Accessibility was an important part of the build, and all major components can be accessed directly or via removable panels.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-032.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110210" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-032.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The exhaust exits out of the left side, which makes for great gobs of fire being thrown out under braking  the tubes have been flattened down into rounded rectangles to work with the flat underbody, but still they were vulnerable to damage.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-033.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110211" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-033.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Another problem to tackle was heat. The Capri is covered in heat-dissipating devices: a chrome-nickel alloy exhaust manifold helped up front, and then there were the bespoke, curving side radiators in the front of the rear fenders  these were Citron units bent into shape  and separate oil coolers the rear axle, the gearbox and engine oil.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-034.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110212" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-034.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The front suspension followed Formula 1 convention of using isosceles triangle aluminium wishbones in conjunction with screw-adjustable ball-joints. Lightweight McPherson struts worked in combination with Bilstein shocks: single-gas-padded tubes with concentric titanium springs supporting adjustable aluminium spring plates. Compression and rebound damping and ride height control was all controllable.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-035.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110213" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-035.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The rigid live rear axle was made from a light alloy, though the material went through some iterations to find something that could withstand the power, and the housing was set up with positive camber and tie bars to control toe. The rear shocks used the same set-up as at the front.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-036.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110214" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-036.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Twin cast-aluminium Girling brake callipers on vented and perforated disks were used on each front wheel: water cooling could be added if necessary.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-037.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110215" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-037.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         At the rear, larger single-calliper disks provided the braking power.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-038.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110216" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-038.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The first car, in classic Mampe colours, was run out halfway during the 1978 DRM season, and immediately wowed the German public  it was dubbed the Wunderwagen by the press  and scored four poles and a win. In 79, its second season, three Capris were entered and they swept the Division 2 title, winning the majority of races  but the Klaus Ludwigs Division 1 935 was still winning outright. It was time to get a bigger boat. And a new pilot for the bigger boat.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-039.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110217" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-039.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The result was the 1980 Division 1 (for 2-litre and over NA cars, or 1.5-litre and over turbos) version, in combination with legendary driver Klaus Ludwig jumping ship to Zakspeed (via a court case). The engine was bored out to 1.7 litres and the twin-turbos came back; the front splitter and rear wing got even more extreme.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="youtube">
          <object height="745">
           <param name="movie" value="//www.youtube.com/v/JkvtCR4XDzg?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark&amp;hd=1">
           </param>
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" height="745" src="//www.youtube.com/v/JkvtCR4XDzg?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark&amp;hd=1" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" wmode="opaque">
           </embed>
           <param name="wmode" value="opaque">
           </param>
          </object>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         A savage 600hp beast, the Division 1 car put the opposition to the sword. 10 seconds faster than the Division 2 model, its advanced aero, 186mph top speed and power-to-weight advantage put it right up there with the 800hp Porsches.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-040.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110218" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-040.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         The over-wide wing was soon protested and banned, so Zakspeed simply deployed more engineering skill. The underside of the car was revised with an advanced F1-style, full-length ground effect venturi tunnel with flexible side skirts. This was serious stuff. The rear diffuser slot created negative pressure, pulling the Capri down through Germany and towards Australia. The side skirts would soon wear down, decreasing the effect, but it still made the car almost untouchable in early laps. However, Ludwig had been stripped of the points for his wide-wing wins, so those pesky Porsches still took the title
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="youtube">
          <object height="745">
           <param name="movie" value="//www.youtube.com/v/qAIX48KfnCA?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark&amp;hd=1">
           </param>
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" height="745" src="//www.youtube.com/v/qAIX48KfnCA?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark&amp;hd=1" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" wmode="opaque">
           </embed>
           <param name="wmode" value="opaque">
           </param>
          </object>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         In 1981, Manfred Winklehock took to Division 1 with a LiquiMoly-sponsored car and blitzed the class; Klaus Ludwig switched back to a Wrth Division 2-spec Capri with the advanced aero fitted and took 11 wins to Winklehocks six  stealing the overall title.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-041.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110219" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-041.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         This is his car from that year, with the livery reversed from the white and red on black of 80 to this stunning black and red on white.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="youtube">
          <object height="745">
           <param name="movie" value="//www.youtube.com/v/d-NdKKjHBN8?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark&amp;hd=1">
           </param>
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" height="745" src="//www.youtube.com/v/d-NdKKjHBN8?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark&amp;hd=1" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" wmode="opaque">
           </embed>
           <param name="wmode" value="opaque">
           </param>
          </object>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         It was the final chance for the Capri: from 82, Group C cars became top dog, though Division 1 Capris continued to compete until the end of 83. The car lived on, used as the basis for the IMSA Mustang in the USA, plus the engine formed the basis for the unit Zakspeed would use for their foray into Formula 1:. Well have more on that in a Zakspeed shop tour coming up later this month.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-042.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110220" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-042.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         One of the most striking things about the Zakspeed Capri was its range of liveries: all the cars raced with striking but simple geometric designs that were full of character, easily identifiable and perfectly complement the long shape of the Capri Turbo.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-043.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110221" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-043.jpg" title="Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-043"/>
         </a>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Zakspeeds restoration of Ludwigs car has been accurate to the 80s in almost every way. The spaceframe was crack-tested and repaired as necessary, and a new subframe manufactured. All the mechanicals are as per the original, and the body panels have been remade using the original wooden jigs  but the difference is that this time round carbon-fibre has been used for all major panels and the front wing is the wider 1982 Division 1 spec.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="youtube">
          <object height="745">
           <param name="movie" value="//www.youtube.com/v/ScNCjLFDHyM?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark&amp;hd=1">
           </param>
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" height="745" src="//www.youtube.com/v/ScNCjLFDHyM?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark&amp;hd=1" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" wmode="opaque">
           </embed>
           <param name="wmode" value="opaque">
           </param>
          </object>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The effort put in by Zakspeed was given its ultimate reward by the car being invited to the 2012 Goodwood Festival Of Speed, with Klaus Ludwig himself taking the Capri up the famous hill. Its also competed in the hands of current Zakspeed owner and son of Erich, Peter Zakowski: he ran at the Oldtimer Grand Prix at the Nrburgring. Enjoy this video for some glorious on-board action from this very car: hes still hunting K3s, just like in the old days.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <a href="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-044.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-110222" src="http://www.speedhunters.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Zakspeed_Ford_Capri_Turbo-044.jpg" title="The 2012 Essen Motor Show"/>
         </a>
        </figure>

         Me, I still have all my old toy cars in boxes in the garage. Im a terrible one for hoarding. Oh, and now a Wrth Zakspeed Capri slot-car that is very much a more recent purchase. And. Ah. Two more in the post. Sometimes writing articles like this can be a very expensive business
