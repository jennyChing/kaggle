          <div class="post-inner group">
           <h1 class="post-title">
            How To Play (And Patch) The Ultima Games Today!
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://ultimacodex.com/author/ultimacodex/" rel="author" title="Posts by WtF Dragon">
             WtF Dragon
            </a>
             June 18, 2011
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry ">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <p>
              Andrew writes in, asking a question that realistically I should have already published an answer to:
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               I want to play the Ultima games from beginning to end, but I have no clue how to apply the upgrade patches!
              </p>
              <p>
               Im not an expert or even a novice code writer, so please excuse me if I ask you to tell me what to do step by step.
              </p>
              <p>
               If it helps, can you start with just
               <em>
                Ultima 2
               </em>
               ?
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              Im going to put my reply in an article, because that way I can add it to the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/faq/">
                FAQ
               </a>
              </b>
              as a link. If any of you have suggestions which you feel are better than mine, by all means give them in the comments.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Note:
              </b>
              For the purposes of this write-up, I am assuming that everyone is trying to run the PC/DOS versions of the earlier games.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Akalabeth
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              Your best bet for playing
              <em>
               Akalabeth
              </em>
              (or
              <em>
               Ultima 0
              </em>
              ) is
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/akalabeth/aklabeth/">
                Paul Robsons remake
               </a>
              </b>
              of it. Or you can grab the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/akalabeth/akalabeth-for-iphone/">
                iPhone version of the game
               </a>
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              Alternatively, if you want to run the original game in
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com/">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              , you should grab
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/akalabeth/akalabeth-patched-executable/">
                this patched executable
               </a>
              </b>
              which will enable the Ultima Collection version of the game to run smoothly under the DOS emulation software.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 1
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              There isnt much you need to do  or can do  for
              <em>
               Ultima 1
              </em>
              ; there is only a single
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima1/ultima-1-graphics-patch/">
                graphics patch
               </a>
              </b>
              available for the game, which is recommended as it corrects a known glitch in the original display driver executable.
             </p>
             <p>
              It is best to run
              <em>
               Ultima 1
              </em>
              in
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com/">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 2
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              There are two different approaches you can take to get
              <em>
               Ultima 2
              </em>
              up and running. One method would be to use
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima2/ultima-2-for-windows/">
                <em>
                 Ultima 2
                </em>
                for Windows
               </a>
              </b>
              , which is a front-end for the original game data files. As such, it
              <em>
               should
              </em>
              work with the various patches that comprise the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima2/ultima-2-upgrade/">
                <em>
                 Ultima 2
                </em>
                Upgrade
               </a>
              </b>
              (but that is untested). And realistically, Im not 100% sure that the Windows front-end works under modern Windows environments, or on 64-bit systems.
             </p>
             <p>
              So, the other option is to run
              <em>
               Ultima 2
              </em>
              in
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com/">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              , and just apply the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima2/ultima-2-upgrade/">
                <em>
                 Ultima 2
                </em>
                Upgrade
               </a>
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 3
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              There are two different approaches you can take to get
              <em>
               Ultima 3
              </em>
              up and running. One method would be to use
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima3/ultima-3-for-windows/">
                <em>
                 Ultima 3
                </em>
                for Windows
               </a>
              </b>
              , which is a front-end for the original game data files. As such, it
              <em>
               should
              </em>
              work with the various patches that comprise the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima3/ultima-3-upgrade/">
                <em>
                 Ultima 3
                </em>
                Upgrade
               </a>
              </b>
              (but that is untested). And realistically, Im not 100% sure that the Windows front-end works under modern Windows environments, or on 64-bit systems.
             </p>
             <p>
              So, the other option is to run
              <em>
               Ultima 3
              </em>
              in
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com/">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              , and just apply the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima3/ultima-3-upgrade/">
                <em>
                 Ultima 3
                </em>
                Upgrade
               </a>
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              A third option,
              <b>
               <a href="http://sergorn.wordpress.com/2010/08/06/exodus-ultima-iii/">
                which Sergorn Dragon recommends
               </a>
              </b>
              , is to pay $5 to LairWare and grab their
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima3/ultima-3-for-mac/">
                OS X port of
                <em>
                 Ultima 3
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              . If you can get a good Mac emulator up and running (or an OS X virtual machine), or if you have an Apple computer already, this is easily the best way to go, as LairWares version of the game is easily the best out there.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 4
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              The best way to play
              <em>
               Ultima 4
              </em>
              is using
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima4/xu4-ultima-4-recreated/">
                <em>
                 xu4
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              . There is really no better way to do it. You might also want to grab
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima4/ultima-4-hythloth-fix/">
                this fix for Hythloth
               </a>
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              I believe that
              <em>
               xu4
              </em>
              also renders moot the need for the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima4/ultima-4-upgrade/">
                <em>
                 Ultima 4
                </em>
                Upgrade patch
               </a>
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 5
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              It is best to run
              <em>
               Ultima 5
              </em>
              in
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com/">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              . There are a number of patches available for the game as well, including the comprehensive
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima5/ultima-5-upgrade/">
                <em>
                 Ultima 5
                </em>
                Upgrade
               </a>
              </b>
              , a
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima5/ultima-5-music-patch/">
                music patch
               </a>
              </b>
              , a patch that lets you use
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima5/numlock-patch/">
                the numeric keypad
               </a>
              </b>
              properly, and (of course) a patch
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima5/runeless-font-for-ultima-5/">
                to remove the runic font
               </a>
              </b>
              used at various points in the game.
             </p>
             <p>
              Alternatively, if you have an advanced TI calculator,
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima5/ultima-5-for-ti-calculators/">
                theres a faithful port of the game
               </a>
              </b>
              for that platform as well.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 6
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              Im not 100% sure, but I dont think you can yet complete the game using
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima6/nuvie/">
                <em>
                 Nuvie
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              . As such, it is probably best to run
              <em>
               Ultima 6
              </em>
              in
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com/">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              There isnt much in the way of patching needed to play
              <em>
               Ultima 6
              </em>
              . If you configure it to use the PC speaker,
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima6/u6-speaker-patch/">
                this timing patch
               </a>
              </b>
              might be of use if you are noticing odd sound errors, and there are also patches for
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima6/ultima-6-mt-32-drivers/">
                MTU-32 and Soundblaster
               </a>
              </b>
              sound, and for
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima6/ultima-6-adlib-patch/">
                AdLib
               </a>
              </b>
              sound. Use as necessary.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Savage Empire &amp; Martian Dreams
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              Since
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima6/nuvie/">
                <em>
                 Nuvie
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              does not yet support these two games fully, it is best to run
              <em>
               Savage Empire
              </em>
              and
              <em>
               Martian Dreams
              </em>
              in
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com/">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              . There are no patches needed for either game.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima Underworld &amp; Ultima Underworld 2
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              The best way to play the two Underworld games is to just
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.gog.com/en/gamecard/ultima_underworld_1_2/?pp=e1a864f0b77f6c89794827a9035355dc8d052622">
                buy them from Good Old Games
               </a>
              </b>
              ; they will run under pretty much any modern version of Windows. The GOG version should already incorporate the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/underworld/uw1/underworld-patch/">
                official patch for the first game
               </a>
              </b>
              and
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/underworld/uw2/ultima-underworld-2-patch/">
                the second game
               </a>
              </b>
              . If you want a bit of mood music in the first game,
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/underworld/uw1/uw1-general-midi-patch/">
                there is a patch which adds a MIDI soundtrack
               </a>
              </b>
              , which I can only assume works with the GOG versions of the game.
             </p>
             <p>
              If, on the other hand, you dont want to buy the games off of Good Old Games, it is best to run
              <em>
               Ultima Underworld
              </em>
              and
              <em>
               Ultima Underworld 2
              </em>
              in
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com/">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              . If youre playing the CD-based versions of the games, you shouldnt need the official patches; if youve got the game on ancient 3.5 diskettes, you may need those patches.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 7 &amp; Serpent Isle
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              A good and easy way to play
              <em>
               Ultima 7
              </em>
              and
              <em>
               Serpent Isle
              </em>
              is with
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima7/exult/">
                <em>
                 Exult
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              , which is a mature cross-platform engine for the original game data files that incorporates a number of graphical scalers (so you can run the game at higher resolutions), bug fixes, and enhancements. If you use
              <em>
               Exult
              </em>
              , theres no need to install any other patches.
             </p>
             <p>
              If, however,  you want to play the games in their pure form, they are well-supported by
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              As far as patching the games goes, you shouldnt need much in the way of patches to run the game under DOSBox. If, however, you want a bit of additional atmosphere in the game, there is a patch that
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima7/ultima-7-general-midi-patch/">
                adds support for MIDI music in
                <em>
                 Ultima 7
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              (and
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/serpentisle/serpent-isle-general-midi-patch/">
                a similar patch for
                <em>
                 Serpent Isle
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              ). There is also an
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima7/ultima-7-update/">
                official patch from Origin Systems for
                <em>
                 Ultima 7
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              , which might help if you find that
              <em>
               Ultima 7
              </em>
              crashes.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 8
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              A good and easy way to play
              <em>
               Ultima 8
              </em>
              is with
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima8/pentagram/">
                <em>
                 Pentagram
                </em>
               </a>
              </b>
              , which is a cross-platform engine for the original game data files that incorporates a number of graphical scalers (so you can run the game at higher resolutions) and bug fixes. If you use
              <em>
               Pentagram
              </em>
              , theres no need to install any other patches.
             </p>
             <p>
              If you have a version of
              <em>
               Ultima 8
              </em>
              that is on 3.5 diskettes, however, you may want to install
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima8/ultima-8-official-patches/">
                the official patch
               </a>
              </b>
              for the game from Origin, before firing up
              <em>
               Pentagram
              </em>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              And if you want to play the game in its pure form, they are well-supported by
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.dosbox.com">
                DOSBox
               </a>
              </b>
              . If you want to go this route, you may find the official patches from Origin Systems that
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima8/ultima-8-adlib-patch/">
                add AdLib support
               </a>
              </b>
              and
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima8/ultima-8-official-patches/">
                fix a few bugs and improve gameplay
               </a>
              </b>
              to be of use.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Ultima 9
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Ultima 9
              </em>
              , interestingly, will run even under Windows 7 (32-bit and 64-bit). To get it working, you just need to install
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima9/ultima-9-official-patch/">
                the official v1.18 patch
               </a>
              </b>
              and the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima9/ultima-9-unofficial-patch/">
                unofficial v1.19f patch
               </a>
              </b>
              . You may also want to install the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima9/forgotten-world/">
                <em>
                 Forgotten World
                </em>
                v1.19g patch
               </a>
              </b>
              , which corrects a couple more bugs in the game.
             </p>
             <p>
              And if you want to improve the look and feel of
              <em>
               Ultima 9
              </em>
              somewhat, there is also the
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.ultimaaiera.com/ultima9/beautiful-britannia/">
                <em>
                 Beautiful Britannia
                </em>
                R1
               </a>
              </b>
              patch.
             </p>
             <p>
              Be mindful of the fact that when you have the game fully patched, it will run pretty darn well for the most partbut you will encounter bugs in Moonglow, possibly in Yew, and possibly when trying to manually sail the boat around. You will also want to
              <b>
               <a href="http://codex.ultimaaiera.com/wiki/Cheating_in_Ultima_IX">
                enable the flying cheat
               </a>
              </b>
              , because on most modern systems you will encounter the floating runes and sigils bug after cleansing each shrine.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               One final note:
              </b>
              Keep your eyes on
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.gog.com/en/frontpage/?pp=e1a864f0b77f6c89794827a9035355dc8d052622">
                Good Old Games
               </a>
              </b>
              as the summer progresses; it has
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.wcnews.com/news/update/10902">
                been confirmed
               </a>
              </b>
              that more Ultima titles (beyond the two Underworld games) will be released through that service over the coming months. Which games, exactly, I am not yet sure of.
             </p>

              <p>
               Dont overlook emulation.  It might be worth checking out the gold remakes of Ultima 3 and 4 for the C64.  I personally prefer the Atari 800(xl) version of Ultima 3, as I think it had the best music of them all (and the red/blue graphics were just weird).  Ultima 2 plays nicest on the Atari ST.
              </p>
              <p>
               Overall, Id recommend:
              </p>
              <p>
               Akalabeth: Go oldschool  emulate the original Apple 2 release
              </p>
              <p>
               Ultima: PC version, or emulate the C64 remake. The Atari 800 release lets you play with a joystick  thats almost worth checking it out right there
              </p>
              <p>
               Ultima II: The Atari ST version runs well under the GEM interface and even allows mouse control
              </p>
              <p>
               Ultima III: Atari 800xl.  Best music and the red water lends an otherworld atmosphere
              </p>
              <p>
               Ultima IV:  Tossup between the C64 gold remake, or the PC version with all the bells and whistles (music, VGA graphics).
              </p>
              <p>
               Ultima V: PC version with MIDI patch, unless your MIDI table sounds like crap with the included .XMI files (mine does)  in that case, the C128 version has good graphics, good music and runs well.  The Atari ST version also has good music, but suffers from a slightly smaller view window.
              </p>

              <p>
               I just tried Ultima 5 on a 64-bit machine on Win7. Just running these EXEs in DOS will not upgrade the system.
              </p>
              <p>
               1. Ultima has be in a DOS-sey file folder. No spaces! No foldername greater than 8! I have my Ultima in C:GamesUltima.
               <br/>
               2. You will have to pull the upgrade files into DosBox too (from c:gamesultima). This will mimic a pre-64bit machine.
               <br/>
               3. Even then, an error is possible: it wants a file called csdpmi5b. Just download this from a reputable site and put it in c:gamesultima.
               <br/>
               4. The sound system is fine just with windows. THAT part was easy.
              </p>
              <p>
               When playing U5 I recommend doing it in a window, not full screen. Get a cheat sheet of commands and put that off to one side. Also, a pdf of that cloth map.
              </p>
