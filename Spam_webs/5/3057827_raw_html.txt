       <h1 class="p-name">
        25 Reasons Why Garlic is Believed to Have Magic Powers!
       </h1>
       <p>
        Posted by
        <span class="p-author h-card">
         Matthew Garlic Shaker/ www.garlicshaker.com
        </span>
        on
        <time class="dt-published" datetime="2014-10-22T00:00:00+0000">
         October 22, 2014
        </time>
       </p>
       <div class="e-content BlockContent">
        <p>
         <img src="http://cdn3.bigcommerce.com/s-vtol2s/product_images/uploaded_images/twenty-five-reasons-why-garlic-is-believed-to-have-magic-2powers.jpg?t=1422925689" style="width: 660px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         1.
         <a href="http://www.garlicshaker.com/blog/25-reasons-why-garlic-is-believed-to-have-magic-powers/" style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,Verdana,Tahoma,sans-serif; font-size: 15px; background-color: initial;">
          Garlic
         </a>
         scares away vampires!
Obviously magical.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. It protects against both physical
vampirism and psychic vampirism.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Garlic is believed to be masculine in
nature with its astrological planet being in Mars, its element is Fire, and its astrological
sign is Aries.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. It is widely used in the Wiccan
religion for charms and spells that enhance speed, strength, endurance,
protection, and to repel evil. Soldiers have often carried it into battle for
these reasons.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. It is believed if you have a garlic
clove, when you pass over water, you are safe from drowning.
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Putting a garlic clove under a childs pillow
while it sleeps is believed to protect it from evil.
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Braids of garlic are believed repel
thieves from a home and keep away envious people.
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Sailors and pirates carried
garlic when at sea because they believed it protected them from sinking.
        </p>
        <p>
         9. Knights during the middle ages wore garlic for self-defense against their enemies.
        </p>
        <p>
         10. Roman centurions and soldiers
believed that
         <a href="http://www.garlicshaker.com/garlic-recipes/">
          eating garlic
         </a>
         gave them super human courage in battle.
        </p>
        <p>
         11. Ancient brides in various cultures
kept cloves of garlic in their pocket for good luck and to ward off evil on
their wedding day. The tradition still continues in some cultures.
        </p>
        <p>
         12. Prior to modern science garlic was
believed to be magic because it is effective at fighting against bacteria,
fungi, and viruses.
        </p>
        <p>
         13. Ancient Egyptians are believed to be
the first people to cultivate garlic. They did so because they believed it was
sacred.
        </p>
        <p>
         14.
         <a href="http://www.garlicshaker.com/the-garlic-plant/">
          Garlic
         </a>
         is believed to be an essential
ingredient in love potions.
        </p>
        <p>
         15. It is still believed that hanging a
garlic braid over a bedroom door will help draw a lover into the boudoir.
        </p>
        <p>
         16. Garlic is often said to have
aphrodisiac powers.
        </p>
        <p>
         17. Hanging garlic is believed to bring
good luck.
        </p>
        <p>
         18. Some Ancient Arabic cultures believed
garlic was good for commerce and prosperity.
        </p>
        <p>
         19. Garlic was believed to be so powerful in the ancient world that pyramid builders of Egypt were paid partially with garlic.
        </p>
        <p>
         20. It is believed that wiping a knife
with fresh garlic juice can empower the knife to be used against dark forces.
        </p>
        <p>
         21. Garlic has commonly been used for
exorcisms.
        </p>
        <p>
         22. A clove of garlic can be added to a Voodoo mojo bag and will strengthen its magical powers.
        </p>
        <p>
         23. Wearing garlic is believed to protect
against bad weather.
        </p>
        <p>
         24. It is believed in eastern Europe that
placing garlic in the mouth of the deceased will prevent the soul from re-entering the
body and keep wandering evil spirits from entering and re-animating the body.
        </p>
        <p>
         25. Unfaithful husbands in the ancient
world would use garlic after visiting their mistresses to deceive their wives of their infidelity.
        </p>
