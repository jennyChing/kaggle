    <p style="color: #444; margin-left:65px;">
     Who doesn't want to create lightsaber effects. In this tutorial I'll show you how.
    </p>
    <br/>
    <script type="text/javascript">
     &lt;!--
google_ad_client = "pub-3627254476693042";

    <br/>
    <br/>
    <p>
     First get an image you can get one here: http://www.baudalign.com/lee/pics/quigon.jpg
Its Qui-Gon without out a lightsaber effect.
    </p>
    <p>
     Ok first create a new black layer. Then in the mode section put it in screen.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_3.png"/>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_4.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     After that get your beizer tool and create a selection of the lightsaber.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_5.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Then click on Create Selection From path.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_6.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Next fill in the selection with white and then go to Select&gt;None.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_7.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     After that duplicate that layer twice.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_10.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Click on the first black layer and go to Filters&gt;Blur&gt;Gaussian Blur
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_9.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Put them both on 5.0
    </p>
    <p>
     Go to the second black layer and apply a Gaussian blur of 20.0
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_11.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Go to the last black layer and apply a Gaussian blur of 40.0
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_12.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Now click on the top layer and put merge down. Then put the mode on screen again.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_13.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Now click on the top layer again and merge it down and then put it on screen.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_14.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Heres the fun part. Go to Layer&gt;Colors&gt;Color Balance
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_15.png"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     Move the slides to get the color you want. There is a button that says PRESERVE LUMINOSTY its your choice to select it or deselect it. I usually deselect it but its your choice.
    </p>
    <p>
     Heres how mine turned out.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" class="submission_image" src="uploads/148_169_16.png"/>
    </p>
