       <h1 class="entry-title">
        POLITICS: On the GOP voting against the Senate Veteran Bill.
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2015-02-07T08:55:20+00:00">
         February 7, 2015
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://www.patheos.com/blogs/wwjtd/author/jberhard/" rel="author" title="JT Eberhard">
           JT Eberhard
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://www.patheos.com/blogs/wwjtd/2015/02/politics-on-the-gop-voting-against-the-senate-veteran-bill/#respond">
          <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="30595 http://admin.patheos.com/blogs/wwjtd/?p=30595">
           Leave a Comment
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         The Republicans have voted against pretty much every bill that would support veterans to have hit the floor of Congress, the most recent being the Senate Veteran Bill. One of my veteran friends recently posted this to facebook:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="color: #141823;">
          That awkward moment when Republicans nearly unanimously vote down a Veteran support bill that would have streamlined the VA healthcare process (something DIRELY needed, I can tell you), improved treatment of sexual trama in military members, and many, many other amazing things. That awkward moment when the Democrats nearly unanimously voted yay. That super awkward moment when the Republicans point to the debt that was accumulated over the war THEY started and threw me into as the reason they cant afford to clean up the mess afterwards.
         </p>
         <p style="color: #141823;">
          I think the Republicans might have to forfeit the stance that theyre the patriotic side of things and that they support the troops. Oh well, even if they dont admit it, the VSO, American Legion, VFW, Paralyzed Veterans of America, and IAVA have certainly done it for them. They sent scathing letters to the Congressmen. Time to put your money where your mouth is.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p style="color: #141823;">
         We could sure afford the war. But the things we promised to the people fighting it? Yeah, sorry. The moneys just not there.
        </p>
       </div>
