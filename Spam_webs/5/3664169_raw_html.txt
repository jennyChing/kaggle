       <h5>
        November 18, 2013
       </h5>
       <h6>
        By
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/115786134267802127131?rel=author" target="_blank">
         Adina Berzofsky
        </a>
       </h6>
       <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/blog/23-tips-for-avoiding-fraud-this-holiday-season/fraud_header/" rel="attachment wp-att-179683 slb_group[179682] slb slb_internal">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-179683" height="299" src="http://www.lifeshield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fraud_header.jpg" title="fraud_header" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         No one wants to become the victim of a holiday crime, but burglary, robbery, and online fraud rates always increase this time of year. Criminals and con artists are always looking new ways to separate you from your money, and the holidays provide unique opportunities for crime. Travel, parties, shopping for gifts make us more vulnerable to theft and fraud. Here are LifeShield Securitys 23 common sense tips for avoiding fraud this holiday season:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. If an online deal seems too good to be true, it probably is.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          2. Never enter your credit card information on any website that is not secure. The URL should start with https and you should see a closed padlock icon somewhere on your web browser.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          3. If you arent sure about choosing a specific company or service,
          <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/home-security-reviews/">
           check reviews on multiple review sites
          </a>
          to read about the real-life experiences of others.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/blog/23-tips-for-avoiding-fraud-this-holiday-season/fraud_receipts/" rel="attachment wp-att-179684 slb_group[179682] slb slb_internal">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-179684" height="254" src="http://www.lifeshield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fraud_receipts.jpg" title="fraud_receipts" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Keep your receipts. All of them, especially digital receipts sent via email.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          5. If you make a purchase using someone elses computer or tablet, do not save any of your information on their device.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          6. Dont friend strangers on your private social media accounts, and remember that public posts can be seen by anyone in the world.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          7. Use public Wi-Fi connections with caution. Sending passwords and personal data over unsecured lines can give bad guys an opportunity to hack your accounts.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/blog/23-tips-for-avoiding-fraud-this-holiday-season/fraud_burglar/" rel="attachment wp-att-179685 slb_group[179682] slb slb_internal">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-179685" height="265" src="http://www.lifeshield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fraud_burglar.jpg" title="fraud_burglar" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          8. If a telemarketer calls you, never ever EVER give them personal information or a credit card over the phone. Ask them to send you something in the mail, do your research and find out if the organization is legitimate, then call them back or mail in a payment.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          9. Check your bank statements and credit card receipts for fraud.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          10. Never reply to spam emails that you didnt sign up for, or return phone calls to unrecognized organizations. These are scams used frequently by thieves.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          11. Download with Caution! Even holiday apps, wallpapers, and screensavers for your devices can actually be malicious and steal your data. Dont use them unless youre sure they are safe.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/blog/23-tips-for-avoiding-fraud-this-holiday-season/fraud_charity-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-179687 slb_group[179682] slb slb_internal">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-179687" height="246" src="http://www.lifeshield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fraud_charity1.jpg" title="fraud_charity" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          12. Giving to charity is important, but make sure your money is going to the right place. Use a verification site, such as Charity Navigator, to make sure its a real charity.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          13. Use Craigs List and the classifieds with caution. Never meet up with a buyer or seller alone, dont give them your personal information, and always meet in a safe, public area.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          14. Only buy gift cards at stores and trusted online sites. You might be buying inactive cards or cards which have already been used and are worthless.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/blog/23-tips-for-avoiding-fraud-this-holiday-season/fraud_box/" rel="attachment wp-att-179688 slb_group[179682] slb slb_internal">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-179688" height="305" src="http://www.lifeshield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fraud_box.jpg" title="fraud_box" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          15. Use caution when throwing away packaging. A giant flat screen TV box at your curb is like a flashing sign to inform burglars about what is waiting for them inside your home.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          16. Install security cameras outside your home. Surveillance cameras deter crime and let you see what is happening near your home at all times.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          17. Be careful using group discount websites. You may find that the hot deal you are about to purchase isnt really that great.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/blog/23-tips-for-avoiding-fraud-this-holiday-season/fraud_birthday/" rel="attachment wp-att-179692 slb_group[179682] slb slb_internal">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-179692" height="263" src="http://www.lifeshield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fraud_birthday.jpg" title="fraud_birthday" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          18. Never use your real birthday when registering on websites, instead choose a date that you will remember. It can help prevent you from being a victim of identity theft.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          19. Trying to stick to a budget this holiday season? Dont forget to factor in shipping costs, tax, set up fees, and other extras.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          20. Verify any businesses you plan to use on review websites. Make sure they do not have many complaints.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          21. Keep track of packages being shipped to you. Packages can be stolen within minutes in some unsafe neighborhoods.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/blog/23-tips-for-avoiding-fraud-this-holiday-season/fraud_cc/" rel="attachment wp-att-179689 slb_group[179682] slb slb_internal">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-179689" height="243" src="http://www.lifeshield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fraud_cc.jpg" title="fraud_cc" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          22. Always make online purchases with a credit card. You will have better consumer protection, and youll have the ability to dispute the charges if the company defrauds you.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          23. Always read the fine print. Youd be amazed at what some companies hide in their Terms and Conditions.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         So how do you protect yourself? Its simple if you use common sense. If you do your research, plan in advance, and trust your instinct, you should be able to avoid most common holiday scams. Keep your home locked up safe and secure, and
         <a href="http://www.lifeshield.com/security-system/">
          arm your security system day and night
         </a>
         , even when you have guests visiting your home. You can enjoy the best parts of the holiday season and avoid the pitfalls with a little effort and a watchful eye.
        </p>
        <p>
