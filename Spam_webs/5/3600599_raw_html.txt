      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <em>
        On a bitterly cold December night, a paranormal investigations team has set their sights on the bloody Hillcrest Sanatorium to answer the age old question: Is there life after death? With rumors of hauntings and local children gone missing, they may just get their answer the hard way when members of the team mysteriously disappear, leaving behind unnerving evidence.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        <em>
         Sanatorium
        </em>
       </strong>
       is one of those films that you want to hate, but simply cant. What do I mean? Well, even though every trope in the horror movie genre has been used in the making of this film  found footage flick, creepy kids, ghosts, token pregnant chick, a case of possession, etcetera  somehow its still scary as hell and quite entertaining. Okay, sure its pretty predictable, but Sanatorium has blended together all the elements of a scary movieand somehow made it better than okay in the long run.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The story follows a group of paranormal investigators who setoff to film their 100th episode of their hit show. However, as soon as they have their eyes on Hillcrest Sanatorium, things already seem odd. Their investigation coincides with the anniversary of the big bad ghosts suicide (which was apparently a homicide/suicide) and all hell breaks loose as a result of that. Of course, its predictable, as Ive stated earlier, but Ill be lying if I said I didnt get a few jolts of: Oh my  Damn it! in, upon viewing the flick.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The acting was better than I expected it to be (total bonus), and the directing wasnt too shabby either (even though its a found footage film). Basically, people who enjoy watching
       <strong>
        <em>
         Ghost Hunters
        </em>
       </strong>
       or otherparanormal investigation reality shows and whos been waiting for proper proof of ghostsbut never found it, will be in for a treat with Sanatorium. Oh, and uber-kudos to the director (
       <strong>
        Brant Sersen
       </strong>
       ) for not showing the ghosts in full view. That made it extra chilling.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       I quite liked Sanatorium, and Im sure that horror enthusiasts thats in the mood for something creepy will feel the same way. Take note that the shaky camera might cause queasiness (it always does with me), but otherwise,itsdefinitely worth a watch.
      </p>
