           Patagonia

           visit Patagonia
          </strong>
          to explore the most beautiful places, the immense size of the area and the harsh weather conditions make you feel that you are on a land of undisturbed beauty. There are many places in Patagonia that is worth visiting, and we show you some of the most dramatic landscapes from the area through our black and white photographs. Our photos follow an itinerary from the Southto the North, starting at the end of the world in Ushuaia. Enjoy the pictures of these dramatic landscapes, which surely will make you feel like visiting Patagonia.

         <h2>
          Useful Information aboutPatagonia
         </h2>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Where is Patagonialocated?
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Patagonia is situated in the Southern end of South America. This vast region is shared between Argentina and Chile, although most of the land belongs to the Argentineans.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           How to get to Patagonia
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          The best way to get toPatagonia isto fly to Ushuaia (or some place in the far South) from Buenos Aires and make your way North stopping at the most beautiful places. If you want to visit the most Southern part of the region, thelong busride from the capital wouldtake several days, crossing the border between Argentina and Chile several times and it wouldturn out to be an infinite journey.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           What tosee and do in Patagonia
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Our photos above show youthe places in Patagonia that we would recommend youto visit. Starting in
          <strong>
           Ushuaia
          </strong>
          , a very peculiar city, known as the most Southern town in the world, you should visit the
          <strong>
           Tierra del Fuego National Park
          </strong>
          with beautiful lakes and green forests, and enjoy a boat trip in the
          <strong>
           Beagle Strait
          </strong>
          where apart from the landscape, you can see quite a lot of wildlife: lion seals and penguins amongst others. Then head North and cross the border to Chile till you get to Puerto Natales, where you can do a 3 or more day trek in the
          <strong>
           Torres del Paine National Park
          </strong>
          . Here you can see the famous towers, probably the most dramatic mountain landscape of the whole Patagonia region. Continuing your way North, another place that you have to visit in Patagonia is the
          <strong>
           Perito Moreno Glacier
          </strong>
          , which can be accessed from
          <strong>
           El Calafate
          </strong>
          . Although you might start to get tired of the hikes, you cant miss out on
          <strong>
           El Chaltn
          </strong>
          , which is the hiking capital of the country. You can do several trails from here, the most populars are the one that goes to the lagoon close to
          <strong>
           Cerro Torre
          </strong>
          and you definitely have to see
          <strong>
           Mount Fitz Roy
          </strong>
          , a peak of such a unique shape. If you still have energy, you can explore the
          <strong>
           Los Alerces National Park
          </strong>
          close to Esquel, which is quite off the beaten path, but the colors of the rivers and lakes will make this park one of the top places in your Patagonia itinerary. Bariloche, refered to as the capital of the region, has to be your next stop, where you can rent a car and visit the
          <strong>
           Nahuel Huapi National Park
          </strong>
          and the
          <strong>
           Route of the 7 Lakes
          </strong>
          . The final stop of your visit to Patagonia can be Pucn, from where you can climb the
          <strong>
           Villarrica Volcano
          </strong>
          if you feel brave or you can just enjoy the views of the volcano from either Pucn or Villarrica.
         </p>
