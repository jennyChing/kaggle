        <p>
         Now that Shane is in school five days a week, its more important than ever to keep his little body fueled. He takes after me and gets cranky if hes even a little hungry- plus I also worry that he wont be able to focus in class if his tummy is rumbling.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img alt="Shane Kindergarten" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-12211" height="727" src="//www.notquitesusie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Shane-Kindergarten-575x727.jpg" width="575"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Since he is in PM kindergarten, we eat both breakfast and lunch before he goes to class, giving us plenty of opportunity to fill his belly. However, a lot of the time we are out and about in the mornings- whether we are going to our music class at Gymboree, story time at the library, or just running errands. That means that I dont always have a whole lot of time to make something for lunch before its time to pack up and go, so we are always looking for fast and easy lunch solutions that are kid-friendly.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of our new favorite lunches are these pizza sandwiches. They use Oscar Mayers new Old World Style Classic Pepperoni and are so easy to put together- Shane can even help!
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img alt="Easy Pizza Sandwiches- Kid friendly and quick!" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-12212" height="401" src="//www.notquitesusie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Easy-Pizza-Sandwiches-Kid-friendly-and-quick-575x401.jpg" width="575"/>
        </p>
        <h2 align="center">
         Easy Pizza Sandwiches
        </h2>
        <h4>
         Ingredients:
        </h4>
        <p>
         Bread or Roll of your choice
        </p>
        <p>
         Oscar Mayer Old World Style Classic Pepperoni
        </p>
        <p>
         Kraft sliced mozzarella
        </p>
        <p>
         Sprinkle of dried oregano
        </p>
        <p>
         Pizza sauce
        </p>
        <h4>
         Directions:
        </h4>
        <p>
         Place all ingredients onto roll on a baking sheet- personalize quantities to your taste
        </p>
        <p>
         Place in oven and broil open-faced, watching closely, until cheese
         <em>
          begins
         </em>
         to melt
        </p>
        <p>
         Remove, let cool, and enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         Optionally, you can include a small cup with more pizza sauce for dipping!
        </p>
        <p>
         There are no complicated steps and you dont even have to wait for the oven to preheat, so its the perfect lunch for a busy weekday!
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img alt="Oscar Mayer Old World Style Pepperoni and Salami" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-12213" height="416" src="//www.notquitesusie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Oscar-Mayer-Old-World-Style-Pepperoni-and-Salami-575x416.jpg" width="575"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Oscar Mayer has several great new Old World Style options that kids and adults will both like, including Classic Salami, Cracked Black Pepper Salami, Classic Pepperoni, and Turkey Pepperoni. All of them come in sandwich-ready slices, so you could easily mix up your sandwiches- whether they are pizza sandwiches or not. Each of the items in the Old World Style line has up to 8 grams of protein per serving, helping keep little bellies full for longer- making for the perfect ingredient for school lunches!
        </p>
        <p>
         You can find Oscar Meyer products at your local grocery store in the refrigerated meat section. They
         <em>
          just
         </em>
         came out in August, so if your store doesnt have them yet, keep checking back!
        </p>
        <p align="center">
