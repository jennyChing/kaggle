          <p>
           Lets be clear, this is a post mainly for
           <em>
            Starcraft
           </em>
           fans. Anyone else who stumbles upon this will most likely leave scratching their heads as to how anyone could get so into a ten year old video game. But in fact,
           <em>
            Starcraft
           </em>
           has become something of a professional sport in Korea, with its top players getting sponsorship deals and becoming national celebrities.
          </p>
          <p>
           I had vaguely heard about this kind of gaming hero worship over there, but I didnt realize the full extent of it until I started watching the televised matches. These kids are literally revered as gods over there. In Korea, Stork, Flash, Jaedong, Savior and GGPlay are as well known as Jordan, Woods, Phelps and A-Rod are here. To be a pro
           <em>
            Starcraft
           </em>
           player requires over 300 clicks and button presses a minute, or five a second, which is ****ing insane, in case you cant tell. These kids might not be atheletes, but theres no denying they have skills.
          </p>
