     <p>
      <b>
       Wilhelmus Simon Petrus Fortuijn
      </b>
      , known as
      <b>
       Pim Fortuyn
      </b>
      (
      <small>
       Dutch:
      </small>
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">
        [pm frtyn]
       </a>
      </span>
      <small class="nowrap metadata">
       (
       <a href="/wiki/File:Pim_Fortuyn.ogg" title="File:Pim Fortuyn.ogg">
        <img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/>
       </a>
       <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5a/Pim_Fortuyn.ogg" title="Pim Fortuyn.ogg">
        listen
       </a>
       )
      </small>
      ; 19 February 1948 6 May 2002) was a Dutch politician, civil servant, sociologist, author and professor who formed his own party,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pim_Fortuyn_List" title="Pim Fortuyn List">
        Pim Fortuyn List
       </a>
      </i>
      (Lijst Pim Fortuyn or LPF) in 2002.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Margry_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Margry-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Fortuyn provoked controversy with his stated views about
      <a href="/wiki/Multiculturalism_in_the_Netherlands" title="Multiculturalism in the Netherlands">
       multiculturalism
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Immigration_to_the_Netherlands" title="Immigration to the Netherlands">
       immigration
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_the_Netherlands" title="Islam in the Netherlands">
       Islam in the Netherlands
      </a>
      . He called Islam "a backward culture", and is quoted as saying that if it were legally possible, he would close the borders for Muslim immigrants.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-controversy_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-controversy-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was labelled a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Far_right" title="Far right">
       far-right
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Populism" title="Populism">
       populist
      </a>
      by his opponents and in the media, but he fiercely rejected this label.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Fortuyn explicitly distanced himself from "far-right" politicians such as the Belgian
      <a href="/wiki/Filip_Dewinter" title="Filip Dewinter">
       Filip Dewinter
      </a>
      , the Austrian
      <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B6rg_Haider" title="Jrg Haider">
       Jrg Haider
      </a>
      , or Frenchman
      <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marie_Le_Pen" title="Jean-Marie Le Pen">
       Jean-Marie Le Pen
      </a>
      whenever compared to them. While he compared his own politics to centre-right politicians such as
      <a href="/wiki/Silvio_Berlusconi" title="Silvio Berlusconi">
       Silvio Berlusconi
      </a>
      of Italy, he also admired former Dutch Prime Minister
      <a href="/wiki/Joop_den_Uyl" title="Joop den Uyl">
       Joop den Uyl
      </a>
      , a social democrat, and Democratic U.S. president
      <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">
       John F. Kennedy
      </a>
      . Fortuyn also criticised the
      <a href="/wiki/Polder_model" title="Polder model">
       Polder model
      </a>
      and the policies of the outgoing government of
      <a href="/wiki/Wim_Kok" title="Wim Kok">
       Wim Kok
      </a>
      and repeatedly described himself and
      <a href="/wiki/Pim_Fortuyn_List" title="Pim Fortuyn List">
       LPF
      </a>
      's ideology as
      <a href="/wiki/Pragmatism" title="Pragmatism">
       pragmatic
      </a>
      and not
      <a href="/wiki/Populism" title="Populism">
       populistic
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Fortuyn was
      <a href="/wiki/Coming_out" title="Coming out">
       openly
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homosexual" title="Homosexual">
       homosexual
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Fortuyn was
      <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Pim_Fortuyn" title="Assassination of Pim Fortuyn">
       assassinated
      </a>
      during the
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_general_election,_2002" title="Dutch general election, 2002">
       2002 Dutch national election campaign
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/Volkert_van_der_Graaf" title="Volkert van der Graaf">
       Volkert van der Graaf
      </a>
      . In court at his trial, Van der Graaf said he murdered Fortuyn to stop him from exploiting Muslims as "scapegoats" and targeting "the weak members of society" in seeking political power.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">
       Early life and education
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pim_Fortuyn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Fortuyn was born on 19 February 1948 in
      <a href="/wiki/Driehuis" title="Driehuis">
       Driehuis
      </a>
      , as the third child to a Catholic family. In 1967 he began to study
      <a href="/wiki/Sociology" title="Sociology">
       sociology
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Amsterdam" title="University of Amsterdam">
       University of Amsterdam
      </a>
      but transferred after a few months to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit" title="Vrije Universiteit">
       Vrije Universiteit
      </a>
      in Amsterdam. In 1971 he ended his study with the
      <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">
       Academic degree
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Doctorandus" title="Doctorandus">
       Doctorandus
      </a>
      . In 1981 he received a doctorate in
      <a href="/wiki/Sociology" title="Sociology">
       sociology
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Groningen" title="University of Groningen">
       University of Groningen
      </a>
      as a Doctor of Philosophy.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
       Career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pim_Fortuyn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Fortuyn worked as a lecturer at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nyenrode_Business_Universiteit" title="Nyenrode Business Universiteit">
       Nyenrode Business Universiteit
      </a>
      and as an associate professor at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Groningen" title="University of Groningen">
       University of Groningen
      </a>
      , where he taught
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marxist" title="Marxist">
       Marxist
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Sociology" title="Sociology">
       sociology
      </a>
      . He was a Marxist at the time. Later, he joined the
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Netherlands)" title="Labour Party (Netherlands)">
       Labour Party
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1989 Fortuyn became director of a
      <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_the_Netherlands" title="Politics of the Netherlands">
       government
      </a>
      organisation administering student transport cards. In 1990 he moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">
       Rotterdam
      </a>
      . From 1991 to 1995, he was an extraordinary professor at the
      <a href="/wiki/Erasmus_University_Rotterdam" title="Erasmus University Rotterdam">
       Erasmus University Rotterdam
      </a>
      , appointed to the Albeda-chair in "employment conditions in public service".
     </p>
     <p>
      When his contract ended, he made a career of public speaking and writing books and press columns, gradually becoming involved in politics. Fortuyn was openly gay, and said in a 2002 interview that he was Catholic.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">
       Political career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pim_Fortuyn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:149px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pim_Fortuyn_1991.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="256" data-file-width="147" height="256" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/ba/Pim_Fortuyn_1991.jpg" width="147"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pim_Fortuyn_1991.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Pim Fortuyn in 1991
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In 1992 Fortuyn wrote "Aan het volk van Nederland" (To the people of the Netherlands), declaring he was the successor to the charismatic but controversial 18th-century Dutch politician
      <a href="/wiki/Joan_van_der_Capellen_tot_den_Pol" title="Joan van der Capellen tot den Pol">
       Joan van der Capellen tot den Pol
      </a>
      . A one-time
      <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Netherlands" title="Communist Party of the Netherlands">
       communist
      </a>
      and former member of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social-democratic" title="Social-democratic">
       social-democratic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Netherlands)" title="Labour Party (Netherlands)">
       Labour Party
      </a>
      , Fortuyn was elected "
      <a href="/wiki/Lijsttrekker" title="Lijsttrekker">
       lijsttrekker
      </a>
      " of the newly formed
      <a href="/wiki/Livable_Netherlands" title="Livable Netherlands">
       Livable Netherlands
      </a>
      party by a large majority on 26 November 2001, prior to the
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_general_election,_2002" title="Dutch general election, 2002">
       Dutch general election of 2002
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      On 9 February 2002, he was interviewed by the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volkskrant" title="Volkskrant">
        Volkskrant
       </a>
      </i>
      , a Dutch newspaper (see below). His statements were considered so controversial that the party dismissed him as
      <i>
       lijsttrekker
      </i>
      the next day. Fortuyn had said that he favoured putting an end to Muslim immigration, if possible. Having been rejected by Livable Netherlands, Fortuyn founded his own party
      <b>
       LPF
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Pim_Fortuyn_List" title="Pim Fortuyn List">
       Pim Fortuyn List
      </a>
      ) on 11 February 2002. Many Livable Netherlands supporters transferred their support to the new party.
     </p>
     <p>
      Heading the list of the
      <a href="/wiki/Livable_Rotterdam" title="Livable Rotterdam">
       Livable Rotterdam
      </a>
      party, a local issues party, he achieved a major victory in the
      <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">
       Rotterdam
      </a>
      district council elections in early March 2002. The new party won about 36% of the seats, making it the largest party in the council. For the first time since the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">
       Second World War
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Netherlands)" title="Labour Party (Netherlands)">
       Labour Party
      </a>
      was out of power in
      <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">
       Rotterdam
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Fortuyn's victory made him the subject of hundreds of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interviews" title="Interviews">
       interviews
      </a>
      during the next three months, and he made many
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/News_release" title="News release">
       statements
      </a>
      about his
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political_ideology" title="Political ideology">
       political ideology
      </a>
      . In March he released his book
      <i>
       The Mess of Eight Purple Years
      </i>
      (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/De_puinhopen_van_acht_jaar_Paars" title="De puinhopen van acht jaar Paars">
        De puinhopen van acht jaar Paars
       </a>
      </i>
      ), which he used as his
      <a href="/wiki/Political_agenda" title="Political agenda">
       political agenda
      </a>
      for the upcoming
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_general_election,_2002" title="Dutch general election, 2002">
       general election
      </a>
      . Purple is the colour to indicate a coalition government consisting of left parties (red) and conservative-liberal parties (blue). The Netherlands had been governed by such a coalition for eight years at that time.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Assassination">
       Assassination
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pim_Fortuyn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Assassination">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Palazzo_di_Pietro_Rotterdam.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2473" data-file-width="1680" height="324" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Palazzo_di_Pietro_Rotterdam.jpg/220px-Palazzo_di_Pietro_Rotterdam.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Palazzo_di_Pietro_Rotterdam.jpg/330px-Palazzo_di_Pietro_Rotterdam.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Palazzo_di_Pietro_Rotterdam.jpg/440px-Palazzo_di_Pietro_Rotterdam.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Palazzo_di_Pietro_Rotterdam.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Fortuyn's house in
        <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">
         Rotterdam
        </a>
        where he lived from 1998 until his death
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Pim_Fortuyn" title="Assassination of Pim Fortuyn">
       Assassination of Pim Fortuyn
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      On 6 May 2002, at age 54, Fortuyn was
      <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Pim_Fortuyn" title="Assassination of Pim Fortuyn">
       assassinated
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Hilversum" title="Hilversum">
       Hilversum
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/North_Holland" title="North Holland">
       North Holland
      </a>
      , by
      <a href="/wiki/Volkert_van_der_Graaf" title="Volkert van der Graaf">
       Volkert van der Graaf
      </a>
      . The attack took place in a parking lot outside a radio studio where Fortuyn had just given an interview. This was nine days before the
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_general_election,_2002" title="Dutch general election, 2002">
       general election
      </a>
      , for which he was running. The attacker was pursued by Hans Smolders, Fortuyn's driver, and was arrested by the police shortly afterward, still in possession of a
      <a href="/wiki/Handgun" title="Handgun">
       handgun
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Months later, Van der Graaf confessed in court to the first notable political assassination in the Netherlands since 1672 (excluding WW II events).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was sentenced to eighteen years in prison and automatically paroled in 2014, after having served twelve years.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Pim_Fortuyn" title="Assassination of Pim Fortuyn">
       assassination
      </a>
      shocked many residents of the Netherlands and highlighted the cultural clashes within the country. Various conspiracy theories arose after Pim Fortuyn's murder and deeply affected Dutch politics and society.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Politicians from all parties suspended campaigning. After consultation with LPF, the government decided not to postpone the elections. As Dutch law did not permit modifying the ballots, Fortuyn became a posthumous candidate. The
      <a href="/wiki/Pim_Fortuyn_List" title="Pim Fortuyn List">
       LPF
      </a>
      made an unprecedented debut in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_the_Netherlands" title="House of Representatives of the Netherlands">
       House of Representatives
      </a>
      by winning 26 seats (17% of the 150 seats in the house). The LPF joined a cabinet with the
      <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Appeal" title="Christian Democratic Appeal">
       Christian Democratic Appeal
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_for_Freedom_and_Democracy" title="People's Party for Freedom and Democracy">
       People's Party for Freedom and Democracy
      </a>
      , but conflicts in the rudderless LPF quickly collapsed the cabinet, forcing new elections. By the following year, the party had lost support, winning only eight seats in the
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_general_election,_2003" title="Dutch general election, 2003">
       2003 elections
      </a>
      . It won no seats in the
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_general_election,_2006" title="Dutch general election, 2006">
       2006 elections
      </a>
      , by which time the
      <a href="/wiki/Party_for_Freedom" title="Party for Freedom">
       Party for Freedom
      </a>
      , led by
      <a href="/wiki/Geert_Wilders" title="Geert Wilders">
       Geert Wilders
      </a>
      , had emerged as a successor.
     </p>
     <p>
      During the last months of his life, Fortuyn had become closer to the Catholic Church. To the surprise of many commentators and Dutch TV hosts, Fortuyn insisted on Fr. Louis Berger, a
      <a href="/wiki/Curate" title="Curate">
       parish priest
      </a>
      from
      <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">
       The Hague
      </a>
      , accompanying him in some of his last TV appearances. According to the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">
        New York Times
       </a>
      </i>
      , Berger had become his "friend and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sacrament_of_penance" title="Sacrament of penance">
       confessor
      </a>
      " during the last weeks of his life.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Fortuyn was initially buried in
      <a href="/wiki/Driehuis" title="Driehuis">
       Driehuis
      </a>
      in the Netherlands. He was re-interred on 20 July 2002, at
      <a href="/wiki/San_Giorgio_della_Richinvelda" title="San Giorgio della Richinvelda">
       San Giorgio della Richinvelda
      </a>
      , in the
      <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Pordenone" title="Province of Pordenone">
       province of Pordenone
      </a>
      in Italy, where he had owned a house.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Views_on_Islam_and_immigration">
       Views on Islam and immigration
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pim_Fortuyn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Views on Islam and immigration">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In August 2001, Fortuyn was quoted in the
      <i>
       Rotterdams Dagblad
      </i>
      newspaper saying, "I am also in favour of a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_war" title="Cold war">
       cold war
      </a>
      with Islam. I see Islam as an extraordinary threat, as a hostile religion."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the TV program,
      <i>
       Business class
      </i>
      , Fortuyn said that Muslims in the Netherlands did not accept Dutch society.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      He appeared on the program several times. It was moderated by his friend Harry Mens. Since his death, commentators have suggested Fortuyn's words were interpreted rather harshly, if not wrongly. For instance, he said that Muslims in the Netherlands needed to accept living
      <i>
       together
      </i>
      with the Dutch, and that if this was unacceptable for them, then they were free to leave. His concluding words in the TV program were "...I want to live together with the Muslim people, but
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/it_takes_two_to_tango" title="wikt:it takes two to tango">
       it takes two to tango
      </a>
      ."
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rotterdam_kunstwerk_beeld_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2599" data-file-width="1633" height="350" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Rotterdam_kunstwerk_beeld_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg/220px-Rotterdam_kunstwerk_beeld_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Rotterdam_kunstwerk_beeld_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg/330px-Rotterdam_kunstwerk_beeld_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Rotterdam_kunstwerk_beeld_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg/440px-Rotterdam_kunstwerk_beeld_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rotterdam_kunstwerk_beeld_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        After his death a statue was placed at his home in
        <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">
         Rotterdam
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      On 9 February 2002, additional statements made by him were carried in the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volkskrant" title="Volkskrant">
        Volkskrant
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-controversy_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-controversy-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He said that the Netherlands, with a population of 16 million, had enough inhabitants, and the practice of allowing as many as 40,000 asylum-seekers into the country each year had to be stopped. (This figure was higher than the actual numbers, and immigrants were decreasing at the time.).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He claimed that if he became part of the next government, he would pursue a restrictive immigration policy while also granting citizenship to a large group of illegal immigrants.
     </p>
     <p>
      He said that he did not intend to "unload our Moroccan hooligans" onto the Moroccan
      <a href="/wiki/Hassan_II_of_Morocco" title="Hassan II of Morocco">
       King Hassan
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dl120510_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-dl120510-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Hassan had died three years earlier.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He considered Article 7 of the constitution, which asserts
      <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_speech" title="Freedom of speech">
       freedom of speech
      </a>
      , of more importance than Article 1, which forbids discrimination on the basis of religion, life principles, political inclination, race, or sexual preference. Fortuyn distanced himself from
      <a href="/wiki/Hans_Janmaat" title="Hans Janmaat">
       Hans Janmaat
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Centre_Democrats_(Netherlands)" title="Centre Democrats (Netherlands)">
       Centrum Democraten
      </a>
      , who in the 1980s wanted to remove all foreigners from the country and was repeatedly convicted for discrimination and
      <a href="/wiki/Hate_speech" title="Hate speech">
       hate speech
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Fortuyn proposed that all people who already resided in the Netherlands would be able to stay, but he emphasized the need of the immigrants to adopt Dutch society's consensus on human rights as their own.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dl120510_18-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-dl120510-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He said "If it were legally possible, I'd say no more Muslims will get in here", claiming that the influx of Muslims would threaten freedoms in the liberal Dutch society. He thought Muslim culture had never undergone a process of modernisation and therefore still lacked acceptance of democracy and women's, gays', lesbians' and minorities' rights. He feared Muslims would try to replace the Dutch legal system with the
      <a href="/wiki/Sharia" title="Sharia">
       shari'a law
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      He said he was concerned about intolerance in the Muslim community. In a televised debate in 2002, "Fortuyn baited the Muslim cleric by flaunting his homosexuality. Finally the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Imam" title="Imam">
        imam
       </a>
      </i>
      exploded, denouncing Fortuyn in strongly
      <a href="/wiki/Homophobia" title="Homophobia">
       anti-homosexual
      </a>
      terms. Fortuyn calmly turned to the camera and, addressing viewers directly, told them that this is the kind of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trojan_horse" title="Trojan horse">
       Trojan horse
      </a>
      of intolerance the Dutch are inviting into their society in the name of
      <a href="/wiki/Multiculturalism" title="Multiculturalism">
       multiculturalism
      </a>
      ."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      When asked by the Dutch newspaper
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volkskrant" title="Volkskrant">
        Volkskrant
       </a>
      </i>
      whether he hated Islam, he replied:
     </p>
     <table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
      <tr>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
        I don't hate Islam. I consider it a backward culture. I have travelled much in the world. And wherever Islam rules, it's just terrible. All the hypocrisy. It's a bit like those old
        <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Reformed_Church" title="Dutch Reformed Church">
         reformed protestants
        </a>
        . The Reformed lie all the time. And why is that? Because they have standards and values that are so high that you can't humanly maintain them. You also see that in that Muslim culture. Then look at the Netherlands. In what country could an electoral leader of such a large movement as mine be openly homosexual? How wonderful that that's possible. That's something that one can be proud of. And I'd like to keep it that way, thank you very much.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
         <a href="#cite_note-22">
          <span>
           [
          </span>
          22
          <span>
           ]
          </span>
         </a>
        </sup>
       </td>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
      </tr>

     <p>
      Fortuyn used the word
      <i>
       achterlijk
      </i>
      , literally meaning "backward", but commonly used as an insult in the sense of "retarded". After his use of "achterlijk" caused an uproar, Fortuyn said he had used the word with its literal meaning of "backward".
     </p>
     <p>
      Fortuyn wrote
      <i>
       Against the Islamization of Our Culture
      </i>
      (1997) (in
      <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">
       Dutch
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Fortuynism">
       Fortuynism
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pim_Fortuyn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fortuynism">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Ideology" title="Ideology">
       ideology
      </a>
      or political style that is derived from Pim Fortuyn, and in turn the LPF, is often called
      <b>
       Fortuynism
      </b>
      . Observers variously saw him as a political protest targeting the alleged
      <a href="/wiki/Elitism" title="Elitism">
       elitism
      </a>
      and bureaucratic style of the Dutch
      <a href="/wiki/Purple_(government)#The_Netherlands_and_Belgium" title="Purple (government)">
       purple coalitions
      </a>
      or as offering an appealing political style. The style was characterized variously as one "of openness, directness and clearness",
      <a href="/wiki/Populism" title="Populism">
       populism
      </a>
      or simply as charisma. Another school holds Fortuynism as a distinct
      <a href="/wiki/Ideology" title="Ideology">
       ideology
      </a>
      , with an alternative vision of
      <a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">
       society
      </a>
      . Some argued that Fortuynism was not just
      <i>
       one
      </i>
      ideology, but contained
      <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">
       liberalism
      </a>
      , populism and
      <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">
       nationalism
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-m213214_24-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-m213214-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      During the 2002 campaign, Fortuyn was accused of being on the "extreme right", although others saw only certain similarities.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      While he employed anti-immigration rhetoric, he was neither a radical
      <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">
       nationalist
      </a>
      nor a defender of traditional authoritarian values. On the contrary, Fortuyn wanted to protect the socio-culturally liberal values of the Netherlands, women's rights and sexual minorities (he was openly
      <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">
       homosexual
      </a>
      himself), from the "backward" Islamic culture.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rvh49_26-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-rvh49-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He held liberal views favouring the
      <a href="/wiki/Drug_policy_of_the_Netherlands" title="Drug policy of the Netherlands">
       drug policy of the Netherlands
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_marriage_in_the_Netherlands" title="Same-sex marriage in the Netherlands">
       same-sex marriage
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Euthanasia_in_the_Netherlands" title="Euthanasia in the Netherlands">
       euthanasia
      </a>
      , and related positions.
     </p>
     <p>
      The LPF also won support from some ethnic minorities; one of Fortuyn's closest associates was of
      <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">
       Cape Verdean
      </a>
      origin, and one of the party's MPs was a young woman of
      <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">
       Turkish
      </a>
      descent.
     </p>
     <p>
      His ideology can be comprised in the following positions:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andeweg.2C_R_p.49_27-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Andeweg.2C_R_p.49-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Civil_liberties" title="Civil liberties">
        Civil liberties
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberalism" title="Classical liberalism">
        Classical liberalism
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Criticism_of_Islam" title="Criticism of Islam">
        Criticism of Islam
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Deregulation" title="Deregulation">
        Deregulation
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Direct_democracy" title="Direct democracy">
        Direct democracy
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Euroscepticism" title="Euroscepticism">
        Euroscepticism
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_speech" title="Freedom of speech">
        Freedom of speech
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Laissez-faire" title="Laissez-faire">
        Laissez-faire
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/LGBT_rights_by_country_or_territory" title="LGBT rights by country or territory">
        LGBT rights
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Republicanism" title="Republicanism">
        Republicanism
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Secularism" title="Secularism">
        Secularism
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Separation_of_church_and_state" title="Separation of church and state">
        Separation of church and state
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Small_government" title="Small government">
        Small government
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>

     <table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
      <tr>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">

          <span>

      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Algemeen_Dagblad" title="Algemeen Dagblad">
        Algemeen Dagblad
       </a>
      </i>
      newspaper about
      <a href="/wiki/Demonization" title="Demonization">

      </a>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>

        <span>
         ]

      wrote:

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">
       Legacy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pim_Fortuyn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legacy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Beeld_Pim_Fortuyn_Rotterdam.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="1944" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Beeld_Pim_Fortuyn_Rotterdam.jpg/220px-Beeld_Pim_Fortuyn_Rotterdam.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Beeld_Pim_Fortuyn_Rotterdam.jpg/330px-Beeld_Pim_Fortuyn_Rotterdam.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Beeld_Pim_Fortuyn_Rotterdam.jpg/440px-Beeld_Pim_Fortuyn_Rotterdam.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Beeld_Pim_Fortuyn_Rotterdam.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Pim Fortuyn monument in
        <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">
         Rotterdam
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Fortuyn changed the Dutch political landscape and political culture.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The 2002 elections, only weeks after Fortuyn's death, were marked by large losses for the liberal
      <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_for_Freedom_and_Democracy" title="People's Party for Freedom and Democracy">
       People's Party for Freedom and Democracy
      </a>
      and especially the
      <a href="/wiki/Social_democracy" title="Social democracy">
       social democratic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Netherlands)" title="Labour Party (Netherlands)">
       Labour Party
      </a>
      (whose parliamentary group was halved in size); both parties replaced their leaders shortly after their losses. The election winners were the
      <a href="/wiki/Pim_Fortuyn_List" title="Pim Fortuyn List">
       Pim Fortuyn List
      </a>
      , and the
      <a href="/wiki/Christian_democracy" title="Christian democracy">
       Christian democratic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Appeal" title="Christian Democratic Appeal">
       Christian Democratic Appeal
      </a>
      . Some
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (April 2012)">
          who?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      commentators think that Fortuyn's perceived martyrdom created greater support for the LPF, which seems likely given its quick later decline.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Netherlands has made its asylum policy more strict. Some
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (April 2012)">
          who?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      have objected to what they think is a harsher political and social climate, especially towards immigrants and Muslims.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Contemporary Dutch politics is more polarized than it has been in recent years, especially on the issues for which Fortuyn was best known. People debate the success of their
      <a href="/wiki/Multiculturalism" title="Multiculturalism">
       multicultural society
      </a>
      , and whether they need to better
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assimilation_(sociology)" title="Assimilation (sociology)">
       assimilate
      </a>
      newcomers. The government's decision to expel numerous asylum seekers whose applications had failed was controversial.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Fortuyn had advocated an amnesty for asylum seekers already residing in the Netherlands.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Coalition_government" title="Coalition government">
       coalition
      </a>
      cabinet of
      <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Appeal" title="Christian Democratic Appeal">
       Christian Democratic Appeal
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Pim_Fortuyn_List" title="Pim Fortuyn List">
       Pim Fortuyn List
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_for_Freedom_and_Democracy" title="People's Party for Freedom and Democracy">
       People's Party for Freedom and Democracy
      </a>
      fell within three months, due to infighting within the LPF. In the following elections, the LPF was left with only 8 seats in parliament (out of 150) and was not included in the new government. Political commentators speculated that discontented voters might vote for a non-traditional party, if a viable alternative was at hand. In recent times the right-wing
      <a href="/wiki/Party_for_Freedom" title="Party for Freedom">
       Party for Freedom
      </a>
      , which has a strong stance on immigration and integration, won 9 (out of 150) seats in the 2006 elections and 24 in 2010.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2004, in a TV show, Fortuyn was chosen as
      <a href="/wiki/De_Grootste_Nederlander" title="De Grootste Nederlander">
       De Grootste Nederlander
      </a>
      ("Greatest Dutchman of all-time"), followed closely by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_I_of_Orange" title="William I of Orange">
       William of Orange
      </a>
      , the leader of the independence war that established the precursor to the present-day Netherlands.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The election was not considered representative, as it was held by viewers' voting through the internet and by phoning in.
      <a href="/wiki/Theo_van_Gogh_(film_director)" title="Theo van Gogh (film director)">
       Theo van Gogh
      </a>
      had been murdered a few days before by a Muslim, which likely affected people's voting in the TV contest for Fortuyn. The program later revealed that William of Orange had received the most votes, but many could not be counted until after the official closing time of the television show (and the proclamation of the winner), due to technical problems. The official rules of the show said that votes counted before the end of the show would be decisive, but it was suggested that all votes correctly cast before the closing of the vote would be counted. Following the official rules, the outcome was not changed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

        <a href="/wiki/Hilversum" title="Hilversum">
         Hilversum
        </a>
        where Fortuyn was assassinated
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Monument_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1120" data-file-width="1848" height="133" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Monument_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg/220px-Monument_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Monument_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg/330px-Monument_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Monument_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg/440px-Monument_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Monument_Pim_Fortuyn.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Epitaph
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Fortuyn's political career and popularity suggested a change in the Dutch people's views of their society as tolerant with integrated multiple cultures.
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "First of all, one can conclude that criticism on political correctness and on the ideal of the multicultural society has broken through for real relatively late.... In the end it was Pim Fortuyn, the electoral success of the LPF and namely the murder on Fortuyn which led to the definitive breakthrough."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
        <a href="#cite_note-36">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         36
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Although Fortuyn did not advocate
      <a href="/wiki/Racial_segregation" title="Racial segregation">
       segregation
      </a>
      , he brought it up as a debatable issue.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Right-wing_politics" title="Right-wing politics">
       Right-wing politicians
      </a>
      gained power after Fortuyn's death, such as former
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Justice_(Netherlands)" title="Ministry of Justice (Netherlands)">
       Minister for Integration &amp; Immigration
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Rita_Verdonk" title="Rita Verdonk">
       Rita Verdonk
      </a>
      and the prominent
      <a href="/wiki/Criticism_of_Islam" title="Criticism of Islam">
       critic of Islam
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_the_Netherlands" title="House of Representatives of the Netherlands">
       Member of the House of Representatives
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Geert_Wilders" title="Geert Wilders">
       Geert Wilders
      </a>
      . These politicians often focused on the debate over
      <a href="/wiki/Cultural_assimilation" title="Cultural assimilation">
       cultural assimilation
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integration_law_for_new_immigrants_to_the_Netherlands" title="Integration law for new immigrants to the Netherlands">
       integration
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Posthumous_sexual_accusations">
       Posthumous sexual accusations
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pim_Fortuyn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Posthumous sexual accusations">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 2005, three years after Fortuyn's death, Dutch journalist
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_R._de_Vries" title="Peter R. de Vries">
       Peter R. de Vries
      </a>
      obtained and publicized a secret report of the intelligence department of the Rotterdam police.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
       <a href="#cite_note-37">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        37
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Fortuyn and several other members of his party had been the subject of an investigation by the intelligence services. According to De Vries, an anonymous informant had claimed that Fortuyn had engaged in sex with minors,
      <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">
       Moroccan
      </a>
      youths;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
       <a href="#cite_note-38">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        38
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      they would be aged between 16 and 21, this was legal under Dutch law. The Ministry of the Interior informed parliament that the report contained factual inaccuracies, and that the trustworthiness of the original source could not be verified.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
       <a href="#cite_note-39">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        39
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
