        <div id="article-body" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          LONDON (MarketWatch) -- Stocks in Europe rose on Wednesday for the fifth straight session, led by gains by oil producers including Royal Dutch Shell and airlines such as Air France-KLM.
         </p>
         <div class="inset inset-html">
         </div>
         <p>
          The pan-European Dow Jones Stoxx 600 index (SXXP) rose 0.5% to 211.75.
         </p>
         <p>
          Equities have rallied sharply since March on optimism that the economic backdrop is stabilizing, and the Stoxx 600 is currently showing a gain for 2009.
         </p>
         <p>
          John Haynes, a strategist at Rensberg Sheppard Investment Management in London, said that the worst option, as far as perceptions of the future are concerned, has been taken off the table.
         </p>
         <p>
          "The next 12 months or so are going to see uninspiring economic conditions, but that's better than deeply depressed," he added.
         </p>
         <p>
          Any improvement in economic conditions usually leads to higher demand for oil, and oil producers were performing well on Wednesday -- with shares of ENI (ENI) up 2.3%, as light-sweet-crude futures rose $1.35 to $61.44 a barrel.
         </p>
         <p>
          Royal Dutch Shell
          <span class="quote down bgQuote" data-bgformat="" data-channel="/quotes/zigman/379078/delayed">
           <a class="qt-chip trackable" data-fancyid="XNYSAmericanDepositoryReceiptStockRDSA" data-track-mod="MW_story_quote" href="/investing/stock/rds.a">
            RDS.A,
            <span class="bgPercentChange">
             -0.09%
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          (RDSA) climbed 1.5% after an upgrade at J.P. Morgan to neutral. The broker said that underperformance for the shares for the year to date already prices in most macro-related concerns and weaker exploration and production volumes.
         </p>
         <p>
          On a regional level, the German DAX 30 index (1876534) rose 1.6% to 5,038.94 and the French CAC-40 index (PX1) advanced 0.9% to 3,303.37, while the U.K. FTSE 100 index (UKX) slipped 0.3% to 4,468.41.
         </p>
         <p>
          U.S. stocks were higher in early trading before heading back toward the flat line at midday.
          <a class="icon " href="http://www.marketwatch.com/story/stocks-gain-as-cyclicals-take-over-lead-role">
           Read more in Market Snapshot.
          </a>
         </p>
         <h6>
          Airlines higher
         </h6>
         <p>
          Shares of Air France-KLM (AF) jumped 11.4% in Paris.
         </p>
         <p>
          The airline posted a smaller annual operating loss than expected of 129 million euros, raised its cost-savings target by 200 million euros to 600 million euros this year and signed a deal to operate transatlantic routes with Delta Air Lines Inc.
          <span class="quote up bgQuote" data-bgformat="" data-channel="/quotes/zigman/463579/composite">
           <a class="qt-chip trackable" data-fancyid="XNYSStockDAL" data-track-mod="MW_story_quote" href="/investing/stock/dal">
            DAL,
            <span class="bgPercentChange">
             +2.14%
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          <a class="icon none" href="/story/air-france-klm-delta-reach-venture-pact">
           See full story.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Other airlines gaining ground included British Airways (BAY), up 1.9%, and Deutsche Lufthansa (LHA), up 3.9%.
         </p>
         <p>
          Shares of Swiss private bank Julius Baer (BAER) rose 1.4%. It intends to split its private-banking and asset-management businesses into two independent companies with their own individual listings on the Swiss stock market.
         </p>
         <p>
          Broker changes helped shares in some firms to rise. Nokia Corp.
          <span class="quote up bgQuote" data-bgformat="" data-channel="/quotes/zigman/162154/composite">
           <a class="qt-chip trackable" data-fancyid="XNYSAmericanDepositoryReceiptStockNOK" data-track-mod="MW_story_quote" href="/investing/stock/nok">
            NOK,
            <span class="bgPercentChange">
             +0.15%
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          (NOK1V) shares advanced 1% after it was upgraded to buy from hold at Deutsche Bank, with the broker saying Nokia will become a growth stock again.
         </p>
         <p>
          Bank of Ireland (BKIR) shares surged 21.5%.
         </p>
         <p>
          It was upgraded to neutral from reduce by Nomura after the group reported better-than-expected capital ratios on Tuesday. But the broker still sees uncertainty around further dilution risks from the government's implementation of the National Asset Management Agency.
         </p>
         <div class="inset inset-html">
         </div>
         <p>
          However, shares of German insurance giant Allianz SE
          <span class="quote up bgQuote" data-bgformat="" data-channel="/quotes/zigman/52834150/delayed">
           <a class="qt-chip trackable" data-fancyid="XTSEStockAZ" data-track-mod="MW_story_quote" href="/investing/stock/az?countrycode=ca">
            AZ,
            <span class="bgPercentChange">
             +2.56%
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          (ALV) declined 1.9% after it was cut to reduce from neutral by Nomura.
         </p>
         <p>
          The broker said the company's personal-and-casualty business is coming under increasing pressure; its U.S. life business is facing significant challenges from litigation and regulatory change; and the company's valuation of 9 times estimated 2009 earnings is above that of rivals at 7 times.
         </p>
         <p>
          Vodafone Group
          <span class="quote up bgQuote" data-bgformat="" data-channel="/quotes/zigman/29728828/composite">
           <a class="qt-chip trackable" data-fancyid="XNASAmericanDepositoryReceiptStockVOD" data-track-mod="MW_story_quote" href="/investing/stock/vod">
            VOD,
            <span class="bgPercentChange">
             +0.33%
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          (VOD) shares fell 2.9%, extending losses from Tuesday when the wireless giant reported annual results.
         </p>
         <p>
          "Underlying results show strong pressure on group revenue growth and on margins due to the economic-recession impact on telecom spending worldwide, high competition across all geographies and strong regulatory pressure," said analysts at S&amp;P Equity Research after downgrading the firm to sell from hold.
         </p>
