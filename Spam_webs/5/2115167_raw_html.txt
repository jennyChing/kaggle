           The San Antonio Spurs have been one of the most successful franchises in the NBA for the past 16 years and in the history of the NBA.  During the history of the Spurs franchise (37 years) they have only missed the playoffs 4 times, have 4 NBA Titles, are currently on a 16 year streak of not only never missing the playoffs but doing so while winning at least 50 games every year. They have one of the best coaches to ever coach the game, arguably the best PF to ever lace them up, and fans who love them more than barbacoa and Big Red (and believe me that's huge in San Antonio,
           <a href="http://www.mysanantonio.com/news/article/S-A-to-celebrate-barbacoa-and-Big-Red-4488806.php">
            see for yourself
           </a>
           ). I have them listed as one of the
           <a href="http://webbrosports.sportsblog.com/post/551338/top_5_nba_franchises_of.html">
            top 5 NBA franchises of all time
           </a>
           .
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            With all their success the question has to be asked, how do they always do it and in a small market?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            <b>
             Force
            </b>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://blog-blogmediainc.netdna-ssl.com/SportsBlogcom/filewarehouse/85109/68b05af465771982b33d880f7810ecfe.jpg">
            <img src="https://blog-blogmediainc.netdna-ssl.com/SportsBlogcom/filewarehouse/85109/68b05af465771982b33d880f7810ecfe.jpg" style="max-width: 500px;" unselectable="on"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Many people don't know the story of how Gregg Popovich came to be the head coach of the Spurs. At the start of the 1996-1997 season, the Spurs started off to a horrific 3-15. Then General Manager and Vice President, Gregg Popovich, fired head coach Bob Hill and named himself head coach. With star center David Robinson sidelined with a back injury, the Spurs only won 17 games. Popovich's move did not go over well with San Antonians at first but four championships and the best active winning percentage can change a lot of minds. In hindsight, every Spurs fan is glad Pop took the team into his hands. Clearly it was the right choice.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Luck
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://blog-blogmediainc.netdna-ssl.com/SportsBlogcom/filewarehouse/85109/d923c51672df4bb98447f6bd1da8a0ef.jpg">
            <img src="https://blog-blogmediainc.netdna-ssl.com/SportsBlogcom/filewarehouse/85109/d923c51672df4bb98447f6bd1da8a0ef.jpg" style="max-width: 500px;" unselectable="on"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Everyone needs a little bit of luck to win in the NBA but looking at how the stars aligned for the Spurs on certain events is quite mind boggling. As mentioned, in the 96-97 season, David Robinson missed nearly the entire season with a back injury and things looked bad for San Antonio as they only won 17 games. However, that back injury ended up being the best thing to ever happen in the history of the franchise. The Spurs were a top 3 lottery team in the 1997 NBA draft with clear #1 choice Tim Duncan entering the draft. Going into the lottery, the Boston Celtics were the favorites to win the first overall pick, but again, the stars aligned for the Spurs and they landed the number one spot. They drafted Tim Duncan. Everyone knows the rest.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Front Office
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://blog-blogmediainc.netdna-ssl.com/SportsBlogcom/filewarehouse/85109/f6cf9f3bcfa0b116fde6a5fe856ac617.jpg">
            <img src="https://blog-blogmediainc.netdna-ssl.com/SportsBlogcom/filewarehouse/85109/f6cf9f3bcfa0b116fde6a5fe856ac617.jpg" style="max-width: 500px; cursor: default;" unselectable="on"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <i>
            RC Buford- General Manager
           </i>
          </p>
          <p>
           Drafting Tim Duncan was the luck of the draw, but building a team around him was a product of having the right people in the organization making the right moves. In 1999, the Spurs took a player from Argentina 57th overall by the name of Emmanuel Ginobili. Manu, as his fans call him, made the all-rookie 2nd team in 99-00, won the sixth man award in 07-08, was selected to the All-star team twice (05, 11) and has averaged 14.7 ppg in his career. Ginobili is considered one of the biggest draft steals of all time. Subsequently, in the 2001 draft, the Spurs took 18 year old Frenchman Tony Parker with the 28th pick. Parker was on the NBA all-rookie 1st team in 01-02, has made six all star games, and is the only active PG in the league to win a Finals MVP (2007). These are just two examples of how the Spurs front office has made the right moves the majority of them time. They seem to be able build a successful team every year while staying under the luxury tax.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Loyal Players
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://blog-blogmediainc.netdna-ssl.com/SportsBlogcom/filewarehouse/85109/c6dfded56db137051c15f64681b4baa4.jpg">
            <img src="https://blog-blogmediainc.netdna-ssl.com/SportsBlogcom/filewarehouse/85109/c6dfded56db137051c15f64681b4baa4.jpg" style="max-width: 500px; cursor: default;" unselectable="on"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           With the mindset of today's players of chasing money and always look for the "sexier" place to play, the core players of the Spurs have stayed loyal to their team. Duncan, Parker, and Ginobili have all taken pay cuts over the years to keep the big three together and sign players around them. I like to compare the Oklahoma City Thunder situation with James Harden a couple years ago with the Spurs and Manu. Where James Harden would not take a cut in pay to keep the big three of him, Russell Westbrook, and Kevin Durant, Ginobili took less money and it resulted in two NBA Championships. The loyalty was repaid, recently, as he was signed to a two year $14.5 million deal during the off season. Many say the Spurs overpaid him but I believe they paid him for past loyalty, and that's something you don't see in the game today.
          </p>
          <p>
           The San Antonio Spurs have found the combination to winning and others in the NBA are noticing. There are currently
           <a href="http://webbrosports.sportsblog.com/post/562006/former_spurs_now_head_coaches.html">
            seven head coaches
           </a>
           in the league who have either played for the Spurs or coached under GreggPopovich. With the recent addition of Kawhi Leonard and Tony Parker still having good years left at 32, the Spurs will be a force to reckon with for awhile. After the big three of Duncan, Parker, and Manu retire, there isn't a doubt in my mind the Spurs will keep going strong and remain one of the NBA's most storied franchises.
           <br/>
          </p>
