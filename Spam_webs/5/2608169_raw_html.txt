        <p>
         <em>
          This coconut key lime bread is soft and fluffy, coconut-y with the perfect hints of citrus. Its like whoa!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Coconut Key Lime Bread" class="aligncenter wp-image-3919 size-full" height="851" src="http://cf.blahnikbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Coconut-key-lime-bread.jpg" title="Coconut Key Lime Bread" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         It is May folks! And I have a very tropical bread for you today. Because what better way to get ready for the summer than enjoying flavors like coconut and lime? This coconut key lime bread is deliciously moist, coconut-y and perfectly citrus-y. Its like whoa! Seriously!
        </p>
        <p>
         But Id expect nothing less since the recipe for this coconut key lime bread is from the new e-cookbook of a talented baker and friend. Steph of
         <a href="http://girlversusdough.com">
          Girl Versus Dough
         </a>
         created 30+ mouth-watering quick bread recipes and collected them all in a beautiful photographed e-book,
         <a href="http://www.girlversusdough.com/quick-bread-love/">
          Quick Bread Love
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-3905">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Coconut Key Lime Bread" class="aligncenter wp-image-3918 size-full" height="860" src="http://cf.blahnikbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Coconut-key-lime-bread_1.jpg" title="Coconut Key Lime Bread" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://www.girlversusdough.com/quick-bread-love/">
          e-book
         </a>
         has recipes from savory breads to muffins to this tropical darling here. When I first saw the book, I couldnt close my mouth. Each page has a gorgeous photograph accompanying the recipe, making you drool as you bake! You will seriously want to buy this e-book and start making the hard choices of which bread to bake first.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.e-junkie.com/ecom/gb.php?cl=273747&amp;c=ib&amp;aff=273907">
          <img alt="Quick Bread Love eCookbook" class="aligncenter" height="250" src="http://www.girlversusdough.com/affiliates/300-250-GVD.jpg" title="Quick Bread Love eCookbook" width="300"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It was very hard to decide what to make first. There was a savory sun-dried tomato spinach bread that caught my eye and this
         <a href="http://www.girlversusdough.com/2014/05/01/peaches-and-cream-streusel-bread/">
          peaches and cream streusel bread
         </a>
         makes me hungry but my coconut loving self eventually won and I tried this coconut key lime bread. Im so glad I did. I tell you my friends, its the best combination EVER! This bread is so quick and easy to make. One mixing bowl action here. Filled with coconut flakes in each bite and the zesty hints of key limes, this soft and fluffy sweet bread is going to disappear in no time. Id advise just making two loaves to be safe like I did
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://cf.blahnikbaker.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png"/>
         Give one to a loved one, like your mother-in-law!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Coconut Key Lime Bread" class="aligncenter wp-image-3917 size-full" height="886" src="http://cf.blahnikbaker.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Coconut-key-lime-bread_2.jpg" title="Coconut Key Lime Bread" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As you go into the weekend, be sure to add this recipe to your must bake recipe. You can get Stephs e-book,
         <a href="http://www.girlversusdough.com/quick-bread-love/">
          Quick Bread Love
         </a>
         here.

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           INGREDIENTS
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the bread
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup granulated sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             6 tablespoons vegetable oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup canned coconut milk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon lime zest
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cups all-purpose flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoons baking powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup sweetened coconut flakes
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the glaze
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup powdered sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 teaspoons key lime juice
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the topping
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tablespoons sweetened coconut flakes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 teaspoon lime zest
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           DIRECTIONS
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Lightly grease a 8-by-4-inch loaf pan and set aside.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a medium bowl, whisk together the sugar, oil and eggs until combined. Whisk in coconut milk and lime zest. In a separate small bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder and salt. large bowl or bowl of stand. Add the dry ingredients to the sugar mixture. Stir until combined. Fold in the coconut flakes.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spoon batter into prepared pan and bake for 50 minutes to 1 hour until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let cool in pan for 10 minutes before turning out onto a cooling rack to cool completely.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            To make glaze: Mix all the ingredients until smooth in a small bowl. Drizzle over cooled cake. Top with coconut flakes and lime zest.
           </li>
          </ol>

            <div class="comment-content" itemprop="commentText">
             <p>
              I made this when I was exhausted (I started baking at 11 p.m.) so the results are 100% my fault.
             </p>
             <p>
              1. I dont think the buttermilk worked as a coconut milk substitute. I think it is too heavy for this quickbread. I cant really say that with full confidence because of the next things that went wrong:
              <br/>
              2. I took the cake out too early! It wasnt done after an hour, and I thought I didnt have toothpicks (new apartment), so I used a fork instead. Dont do that. The batter doesnt stick to a metal fork the way it does the absorbent, natural surface of a toothpick  it will always come out cleaner than a toothpick.  Therefore I took it out. It looked done and was the same golden brown as the picture. The next day, it was doughy, dense and underbaked in the center.
              <br/>
              3. The flavors were AMAZING. The frosting was tangy and sweet. The coconut was good on top, but I think it would be be better toasted.
             </p>
             <p>
              So yeah, maybe if I had tented the top and left it in longer, the results would be different. Maybe the buttermilk make it dense and doughy. Who knows.
