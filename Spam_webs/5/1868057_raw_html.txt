       <div class="meta-title">
        <h2>
         Creating brand personality on social media
        </h2>
        <p class="meta-details">
         Posted by Hitesh Parekh in Blog
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="post-content">
        <div id="getsocialmain">
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://globalwebforcesocial.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/socialmedia.jpg">
           <img alt="social media" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1074" height="380" src="http://globalwebforcesocial.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/socialmedia.jpg" width="715"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Getting people to follow your company on social media is difficult because most users want to connect with other users, not brands. They appreciate the human aspect and the kind of connection social media helps to facilitate on the Internet. Despite this, many brands still succeed in their social media campaigns, and this is due to one significant characteristic they each share: brand persona.
         </p>
         <p>
          By giving your brand its own personality, you are
          <span class="GINGER_SOFTWARE_mark" id="53237b1f-f1fd-4f21-8d54-2091cd64c4ad">
           humanising
          </span>
          it and making it easier to communicate the message you wish your brand to convey. Instead of just being another company on the Internet, you are providing users a more personal experience that allows them to relate to your brand and to become more interested in your story. There are three simple steps you can follow to start
          <span class="GINGER_SOFTWARE_mark" id="3d7e3bbc-3631-4206-888a-ec1c3a15b1ed">
           humanising
          </span>
          your brand on social media.
          <b>
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           1. Practise transparency.
          </b>
          Instead of making what you do look perfect all the time, give your followers insight into how your service works. Show them the good, the bad, and the ugly every now and then. This will make them feel as though they have a greater connection with your brand and will make them more responsive towards it.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           2. Showcase your staff and workplace.
          </b>
          Including photos of real people in your company in your posts instantly welcomes your followers to your brand.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           3. Provide entertainment.
          </b>
          Give users something to get excited about now and then. Make your campaigns fun and interesting by combining a mix of photos and well-written, witty brand messages thats sure to stir up buzz.
         </p>
         <p>
          Above all, practise consistency. This does not mean posting the same messages again and again. Rather, its all about knowing what kind of voice you want your brand to have and to make your posts parallel with it. Creating content thats consistent with your brand and aligned with the interests of your audience will draw more attention and interaction from your target market.
         </p>
         <p>
          Make use of analytics to get a better understanding of your target audience.
          <span class="GINGER_SOFTWARE_mark" id="6d00d630-6489-42b5-8379-fa7ae2def280">
           Utilise
          </span>
          these tools for each platform:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/help/336893449723054/?ref=u2u" target="_blank" title="Facebook Page insights">
           Facebook Page Insights
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="www.google.com.au/analytics" target="_blank" title="Google Analytics">
           Google Analytics
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://analytics.twitter.com" target="_blank" title="Twitter Card Analytics">
           Twitter Card Analytics
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://business.pinterest.com/en/pinterest-analytics" target="_blank" title="Pinterest for Business Analytics">
           <span class="GINGER_SOFTWARE_mark" id="39477a2b-34d5-420d-a185-d243ce3809d5">
            Pinterest
           </span>
           for Business Analytics
          </a>
          (if applicable)
         </p>
         <p>
          Once you have determined the age range, gender ratio, the product or service youre looking to sell, the social platform/s of your choosing, and the type of content that works best on these platforms, you are ready to design relevant content and create your brands social media voice.
         </p>
         <p>
          Planning your strategy before posting
          <span class="GINGER_SOFTWARE_mark" id="d40f8a9a-c7bd-454c-b6f4-159d280ce2ff">
           on
          </span>
          your companys social media accounts will allow you to represent it properly and to use the tone and the same kind of language to reflect your brands persona. It will also make it easier for you to fall into a regular pattern where you continue to come up with fresh content thats useful to your readers, and is reflective of your brands image.
         </p>
        </div>
