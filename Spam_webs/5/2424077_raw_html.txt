     <p>
      <b>
       Sterling Vineyards
      </b>
      , is a
      <a href="/wiki/Winery" title="Winery">
       winery
      </a>
      near
      <a href="/wiki/Calistoga,_California" title="Calistoga, California">
       Calistoga
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">
       California
      </a>
      , owned by international alcohol conglomerate
      <a href="/wiki/Diageo" title="Diageo">
       Diageo
      </a>
      . The winery achieved international recognition when its
      <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">
       wine
      </a>
      won first place in the Ottawa Wine Tasting of 1981.
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
       Description
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sterling_Vineyards&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Visitors to the winery take a short cable car ride from the main entrance to the villa and distillery which sits upon a hill looking over the Napa Valley.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Sterling Vineyards farms 1,200 acres (486ha) of vines in various parts of
      <a href="/wiki/Napa_Valley_AVA" title="Napa Valley AVA">
       Napa Valley
      </a>
      . Production emphasizes
      <a href="/wiki/Bordeaux" title="Bordeaux">
       Bordeaux
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Burgundy_wine" title="Burgundy wine">
       Burgundy
      </a>
      -style wines, and is split in several tiers: the entry-level "Napa Valley" wines for which it is most famous, "Reserve" wines (their highest level), "Single Vineyard" wines (specialty wines for enthusiasts), "Vintner's Collection" (value wines for restaurants and mass distribution), and "Cellar Club" (limited production and eclectic wines for their wine club members).
     </p>
     <p>
      Sterling Vineyards is a popular destination for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wine_tourism" title="Wine tourism">
       tourists
      </a>
      , in part for an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aerial_tram" title="Aerial tram">
       aerial tram
      </a>
      that shuttles visitors from a
      <a href="/wiki/Parking_lot" title="Parking lot">
       parking lot
      </a>
      to the winery, which sits on a volcanic hill 300 feet (91m) above the valley floor. The building is designed to appear like the white villages of the
      <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">
       Greek
      </a>
      island
      <a href="/wiki/Mykonos" title="Mykonos">
       Mykonos
      </a>
      , and incorporates
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Church_bells" title="Church bells">
       bells
      </a>
      from St. Dunstans's in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London,_England" title="London, England">
       London, England
      </a>
      , a church destroyed in
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cellarnotes_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-cellarnotes-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">

     <p>
      English expatriate Peter Newton founded the company in 1964, with the first year of production in 1969. He planted Merlot and Chardonnay, now very common in Napa Valley, at a time most vineyards were focusing on Cabernet Sauvignon. Under long-time
      <a href="/wiki/Winemaker" title="Winemaker">
       winemaker
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Ric_Forman" title="Ric Forman">
       Ric Forman
      </a>
      (later of
      <a href="/wiki/Abreu_Vineyards" title="Abreu Vineyards">
       Abreu Vineyards
      </a>
      ), they released California's first
      <a href="/wiki/Vintage" title="Vintage">
       vintage
      </a>
      -dated Merlot.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-year_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-year-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Coca_Cola_Company" title="The Coca Cola Company">
       The Coca Cola Company
      </a>
      bought the winery in 1977, in a short-lived attempt to enter the wine market.
      <a href="/wiki/Seagram" title="Seagram">
       Seagram
      </a>
      bought the winery in 1982, and was in turn purchased by
      <a href="/wiki/Diageo" title="Diageo">
       Diageo
      </a>
      in 2001.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cellarnotes_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-cellarnotes-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
