       <h3>
        <span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Seducer_and_Golden_Saint_Weapons">
         Dark Seducer and Golden Saint Weapons
        </span>
        <span class="mw-editsection">
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          [
         </span>
         <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shivering:Weapons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Dark Seducer and Golden Saint Weapons">
          edit
         </a>
         <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
          ]
         </span>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        Each weapon comes in a normal and a worn version. The worn versions cause less damage, are lighter, have less health, and are worth less than their normal counterparts. Most of these weapons ignore
        <a href="/wiki/Oblivion:Resist_Normal_Weapons" title="Oblivion:Resist Normal Weapons">
         normal weapon resistance
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Most
        <a href="/wiki/Shivering:Golden_Saint" title="Shivering:Golden Saint">
         Golden Saints
        </a>
        use bows and swords (though at lower PC levels they will use blunts), and most
        <a href="/wiki/Shivering:Dark_Seducer" title="Shivering:Dark Seducer">
         Dark Seducers
        </a>
        use bows and maces (swords at lower PC levels). The quality of weapon they use is dependent on the level of your character, with the Worn versions found at the lowest levels. Depending on your choices in the main quest, you will rarely see soldiers arm themselves with the alternative form of their melee weapons: blades for Dark Seducers, blunt weapons for Golden Saints. There is a reason why they generally disfavor the weapons, though: they are substantially less powerful than their usual arms. For example, the Golden Saints' favored Golden Longswords are the equivalent of Ebony Longswords, but their Golden Maces only have the same quality of Silver Maces. The same arrangement is true for Dark Seducers, with their favored Dark Maces being of Ebony quality while their Longswords match that of Silver.
       </p>
       <p>
        The worn versions of each weapon and the plain
        <a href="#Dark_Arrow">
         Dark Arrows
        </a>
        and
        <a href="#Golden_Arrow">
         Golden Arrows
        </a>
        are exclusively available during the quest
        <a href="/wiki/Shivering:Retaking_The_Fringe" title="Shivering:Retaking The Fringe">
         Retaking The Fringe
        </a>
        . Which ones you will find depend upon whether you have previously chosen Dementia or Mania, and upon the orders given to your troops during the quest.
       </p>

Grummites that use bows and arrows can be found in any type, so Grummite arrows are always available. Refer to the
