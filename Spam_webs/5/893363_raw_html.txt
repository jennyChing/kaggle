     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Lullabies to Paralyze
       </b>
      </i>
      is the fourth
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">
       studio album
      </a>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/Queens_of_the_Stone_Age" title="Queens of the Stone Age">
       Queens of the Stone Age
      </a>
      , released on March 21, 2005.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The album debuted at #5 on the
      <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">
       <i>
        Billboard
       </i>
       200
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Updates_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Updates-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and sold 97,000 copies in America during its first week of release,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Updates_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Updates-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      eventually topping over 342,000 copies as of March, 2007 according to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nielsen_Soundscan" title="Nielsen Soundscan">
       Nielsen Soundscan
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The album has been certified gold in the UK, where it has sold over 100,000 units. It is also the band's first album to be released after
      <a href="/wiki/Nick_Oliveri" title="Nick Oliveri">
       Nick Oliveri
      </a>
      was fired from the band.
      <a href="/wiki/Josh_Homme" title="Josh Homme">
       Josh Homme
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Lanegan" title="Mark Lanegan">
       Mark Lanegan
      </a>
      are the only members from the previous album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Songs_for_the_Deaf" title="Songs for the Deaf">
        Songs for the Deaf
       </a>
      </i>
      , to play on this album and it's the first album to feature drummer
      <a href="/wiki/Joey_Castillo" title="Joey Castillo">
       Joey Castillo
      </a>
      and guitarist
      <a href="/wiki/Troy_Van_Leeuwen" title="Troy Van Leeuwen">
       Troy Van Leeuwen
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
       Background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lullabies_to_Paralyze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The album title
      <i>
       Lullabies to Paralyze
      </i>
      was intended to bridge
      <i>
       Lullabies
      </i>
      with its predecessor
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Songs_for_the_Deaf" title="Songs for the Deaf">
        Songs for the Deaf
       </a>
      </i>
      by naming it after a line in "Mosquito Song", the final track on
      <i>
       Songs for the Deaf
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vertigo_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Vertigo-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The "deluxe limited edition" of the album includes a bonus track and a bonus
      <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">
       DVD
      </a>

     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Nick_Oliveri" title="Nick Oliveri">

      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Lanegan" title="Mark Lanegan">

      </a>
      eventually clarified in several interviews was never the case.

     <p>
      The album received generally positive reviews, although slightly lower than its
      <a href="/wiki/Songs_for_the_Deaf" title="Songs for the Deaf">
       predecessor
      </a>
      , the average score being 78 out of 100 on
      <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
       Metacritic
      </a>
      based on 31 professional reviews.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Kevin Forest Moreau awarded it 3rd best album of the year 2005,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" title="Billboard magazine">
       Billboard magazine
      </a>
      ranked it 7th best album,
      <a href="/wiki/Magnet_(magazine)" title="Magnet (magazine)">
       Magnet magazine
      </a>
      ranked it 9th, and
      <a href="/wiki/Filter_(magazine)" title="Filter (magazine)">
       Filter magazine
      </a>
      considered it 10th best album of the year 2005.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      JustPressPlay ranked it #31 on its list of the Top 100 Albums of the 2000s
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and named "Tangled Up in Plaid" the 19th best song of the decade.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Lullabies To Paralyze was certified gold in the UK on April 15, 2005 with sales exceeding 100,000 copies.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="LP_versions">
       LP versions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lullabies_to_Paralyze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: LP versions">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The album had two LP pressing runs. The first pressing was released by
      <a href="/wiki/AntAcidAudio" title="AntAcidAudio">
       AntAcidAudio
      </a>
      and contains a different cover to the CD version. The sides are named
      <i>
       Once
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       You
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Were
      </i>
      &amp;
      <i>
       Lost
      </i>
      , after a line from "Someone's in the Wolf", with each containing a mixture of the CD's album and bonus tracks.
     </p>
     <p>
      The second pressing is a reissue by Dutch record label Music On Vinyl, released on September 22, 2011. The reissue is different from the first pressing in that it does not have its sides named after the line from "Someone's in the Wolf," and it uses the cover art from the CD release. The reissue omitted bonus tracks "Infinity" and "Precious and Grace" from the track listing, and listed "Like A Drug" as the last track. Side four contains an etching of one of the album's artwork.

       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington,_DC" title="Washington, DC">

      </a>
      on March 27, 2005.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">

          original research?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>

     <p>
      The final item on the song listing (located behind the CD in the normal version, or inside the booklet in the deluxe edition), below "Long Slow Goodbye" reads:
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       "The Fun Machine took a shit and died  Was lost or misplaced. (There is a reward for the return of said tapes)"
      </dd>
     </dl>
     <p>
      During that time, the band assumed that the tapes of that song were either lost or stolen. A live version of this song can be found on the DVD release
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Over_the_Years_and_Through_the_Woods" title="Over the Years and Through the Woods">
        Over the Years and Through the Woods
       </a>
      </i>
      . Homme commented, "The tapes got lost. Actually, they were just at another studio, but we falsely accused everyone in the world of theft."

       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>

        </span>

        <span>
