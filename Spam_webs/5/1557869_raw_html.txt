       <div id="paragraphs1">
        If there is one thing people love more than dogs, its dogs in costume. No wonder the annual Howl-O-Ween dog pageant and contest has been one of the most popular themes on the calendar for First Friday Main Line.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        When the event returns for 2014 on Oct. 3, there are both happy and bittersweet reasons why this parade of pups is one not to miss.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Taking place at 6 p.m. at Cricket and Lancaster avenues in Ardmore, the contest will feature several categories to inspire local canine couturiers, offering valuable prizes donated by local merchants.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Joining the panel of guest judges is a notable name: new Lower Merion Township Manager Ernie McNeely.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        And now the bittersweet. Octobers First Friday Main Line will be the last in a nearly nine-year run that, since 2006, has lived up to founder Sherry Tillmans twin goals to get Main Line residents together, enjoying their local downtown, and to help them discover the arts in unexpected places.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Im going on hiatus, Tillman said in a recent interview at her craft and gift shop, Past*Present*Future on Lancaster Avenue in Ardmore. The change of course is coming for a lot of reasons.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Its been eight and a half years since that First Friday Main Line trolley first ran, shuttling event-goers to venues from Ardmore to Haverford to Bryn Mawr. While the scope of the events has shifted, focusing on downtown Ardmore in recent years, Tillman has continued to be its primary organizer and jack-of-all trades, finding and matching artists with venues from car dealer showrooms to storefronts to local cafes, setting up block parties, and planning major events like Howl-O-Ween, Septembers bluesfest and Junes antique and sports car show.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        I want to create again myself, Tillman said of her own grounding in art. She also wants to try a different tack to engage the community.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Beginning in 2015, I want to transition to two art events per year, Tillman said. The focus will be different. Tillman describes what she has in mind as the public experiencing art, instead of just viewing it; playing a participatory role.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        She added that her own change of roles does not mean that popular events like Howl-O-Ween, the blues party or the car show might not continue. They are so well-established, she said, that another organization, such as the Ardmore Business Association or downtown business authority The Ardmore Initiative might be poised to stage them as stand-alone events.

       <div id="paragraphs2">
        Clearly, a factor is the change coming to the downtown. With major redevelopment on Lower Merion Townships Cricket Avenue parking lot slated to break ground by the end of 2014, access for larger events will be affected during a construction period. But she doesnt close that door. Well see what happens when Cricket reopens, she suggested.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Tillman said she will maintain the First Friday Main Line website to list and support other local events. She will also preserve its status as a non-profit organization.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Its been wonderful, she said of the past 8  years and the continuing interest by a number of local businesses to host events. Im so happy that I did it, and that the community embraced [the concept].
        <br/>
        <br/>
        For now, Tillman is focused on making Octobers First Friday Main Line one to remember.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Howl-o-Ween activities will get under way with registration beginning at 5:30 p.m. Oct. 3. Thanks to support from sponsors, entry is free this year. However, donations will be welcome to support this years featured charity, Main Line Animal Rescue. Judging will start promptly at 6:15 p.m.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Categories for awards will include Most Original, Scariest, Funniest and the new category of Most Unusual Theme.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        The category has been added, Tillman explained, because What weve discovered over the years is that families do this together. Pets and their humans  adults and kids alike  want to get into the act, coming in costume and with props.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        In addition to McNeely, judges this year include founding and longtime First Friday Main Line volunteer and dachshund devotee Carla Zambelli; Linward Robinson, associate professor and director of Harcum Colleges Animal Center Management program; and MLARs Steve Boulden.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Our local businesses have been so generous in providing prizes, Tillman said. Mapes Hardware is giving a big tub of dog supplies, while Harrys Treasures and Collectibles, The Mud Room pottery studio, Viva Video! and Past*Present*Future and more are donating items. Nemours-Dupont Pediatrics in Bryn Mawr is presenting sponsor.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        After the dog pageant wraps up, there will be much more on the First Friday line-up, including another Cricket Avenue Block Party from 7:30 to 9:30 p.m. Magdaliz Roura and her Latin ensemble, Cristol, will be the featured performers with a repertoire of folk and traditional music from Latin America and the Caribbean.
