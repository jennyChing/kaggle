              <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
               5 Essential Elements to Starting Your Mindfulness Meditation Practice
              </h1>
              <div class="post-meta the-icons pmeta-alt">
               <span class="post-author vcard">
                By
                <a href="http://blogs.psychcentral.com/mindfulness/author/goldstein/" rel="author" style="text-decoration:none;">
                 Elisha Goldstein, Ph.D.
                </a>
                <br/>
                ~ 3 min read
               </span>
              </div>
              <br/>
             </div>
             <div class="post-content">
              <div class="entry-content">
               <p>
                <img alt="deck" class="alignright size-full wp-image-3942" height="268" src="http://blogs.psychcentral.com/mindfulness/files/2013/10/deck.jpg" width="240"/>
                No matter how much we talk about it, read about it, or study it, putting a mindfulness practice into practice can be challenging. But what are we to do? Science continues to reveal that an active practice has
                <a href="http://blogs.psychcentral.com/mindfulness/2013/10/the-heart-and-science-of-how-to-make-mindfulness-work/">
                 important health benefits,
                </a>
                relational benefits and even corporate benefits (increased productivity and reduced healthcare costs). Sometimes all we need is a simple road map to get us started or restarted if its been some time since we practiced.
               </p>
               <p>
                Here are 5 essential elements to creating a mindfulness meditation practice in daily life.
               </p>
               <h3>
                <strong>
                 Prepare Yourself
                </strong>
               </h3>
               <div align="center">
                <div id="mj_adblock">
                 <script type="text/javascript">
                  //&lt;![CDATA[ 
 OAS_RICH('Right2'); 
 //]]&gt;
                 </script>
                </div>
               </div>
               <p>
                <span id="more-3936">
                </span>
                Before even attempting to do any practice its important to understand that your practice is
                <em>
                 not a performance
                </em>
                . Each practice doesnt need to be evaluated about whether it was a good meditation or a bad meditation. This performance-based mindset misses the point entirely.
                <em>
                 If there is any goal at all to the practice its simply to learn.
                </em>
               </p>
               <p>
                For example, if someone is using their breath as an object of attention, the goal is not to stay on the breath for a long period of time, its to learn about what its like to settle attention on the breath. If the mind wanders a lot, then you learn how busy the mind is. If it wanders a lot on a particular topic, you learn to what degree that topic is on your mind. If it is on your mind a lot you learn that whatever it is, it needs attending to and you can later make the choice to focus on it.
               </p>
               <p>
                Everyones mind wanders, even people who have been meditating for 50 years. Its part of what the brain does. In fact, you could make the argument that the more it wanders the more you have an opportunity to train the mind to see choice points to gently bring it back. What you practice and repeat becomes a habit and so youre strengthening the habit of choice.
               </p>
               <p>
                Heres a
                <a href="http://elishagoldstein.com/videos/breath-as-an-anchor/" target="_blank">
                 short breathing practice
                </a>
                from
                <i>
                 <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1451623895/ref=as_li_tf_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1451623895&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=mindfulmoment-20">
                  The Now Effect
                 </a>
                 <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=mindfulmoment-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1451623895" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                 
                </i>
                to get you started.
               </p>
               <h3>
                Bring Heart into It
               </h3>
               <p>
                There is sometimes confusion in how people teach mindfulness practice, but in the way that Ive come to understand it is that its simply not as effective unless youre bring your heart into it. The quality of attention has relaxed curiosity and tenderness to it. Its as if were bowing with respect to the life being lived whether the attention is on the breath, the body, or any sense perceptions.
               </p>
               <p>
                When there is pain involved, there is an awareness of the pain and the attention has this quality of wanting to be supportive in some way. It is a quality of care and self-compassion.
               </p>
               <div align="center">
                <div id="mj_adblock">
                 <script type="text/javascript">
                  //&lt;![CDATA[ 
 OAS_RICH('x02'); 
 //]]&gt;
                 </script>
                </div>
               </div>
               <p>
                In other words, youre doing this practice ultimately because you care about yourself and possibly because you know that doing this practice will also be a gift to those around you.
               </p>
               <h3>
                Forgive Yourself
               </h3>
               <p>
                Youre going to be completely imperfect at this like the rest of us. If time goes by and you forget to practice, practice Forgive and Invite. Forgive yourself for the time gone by, investigate what took you off course, and then in that space of awareness invite yourself to begin again.
               </p>
               <p>
                This is a very forgiving practice; you can always begin to be present to your life again. It only takes a moment.
               </p>
               <h3>
                Thank Yourself
               </h3>
               <p>
                Perhaps even the most important part of this practice is to thank yourself each time you do it. When the time is up, you acknowledge yourself for making the effort to take time out of daily busy-ness for your own learning, health and well-being.
               </p>
               <p>
                This imprints in your memory that you care enough about yourself to pay attention to you! That self-compassionate caring type of energy is healing. What would the days, weeks, and months ahead be like if you had more of that energy circulating through your mind and body?
               </p>
               <p>
                Keep coming back to these four essential elements of a mindfulness practice.
               </p>
               <h3>
                Find a Buddy
               </h3>
               <p>
                You could do the previous four elements of practice on its own, but it sure helps when we have people to do it with. Maybe you know someone who has had the interest to start a practice or you go on meetup.com and check to see if there are any groups in your area. Or maybe you look at an app like
                <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/app/zen-timer-meditation-timer/id337472899?mt=8" target="_blank">
                 Insight Timer
                </a>
                that has worldwide online and in-person groups to check in with. Again, a buddy is not essential to start, but it can help you feel more connected to others who are aligned with this interest of yours. Ultimately that really helps motivation.
               </p>
               <p>
                Now if youre ready, carve out 10 minutes (on this link youll also find a 5-minute and 3-minute version of this)
                <a href="http://elishagoldstein.com/videos/10-minute-body-scan/" target="_blank">
                 , to start your practice with
                </a>
                <a href="http://elishagoldstein.com/videos/10-minute-body-scan/" target="_blank">
                 a body scan
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                As always, please share your thoughts, stories and questions below. Your interaction creates a living wisdom for us all to benefit from.
               </p>
               <p>

                 <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic-75728905/stock-photo-an-attractive-young-woman-and-man-doing-yoga-on-a-jetty-with-the-blue-ocean-and-another-island.html" target="_blank">
                  Couple meditating image
                 </a>
                 available from Shutterstock.
                </small>
               </p>
