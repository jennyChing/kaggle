        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Karen May Sorensen is an American artist who has been suffering from schizofrenia ever since she was 19 years old.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         44-year-old Karen, who has an official blog where she writes about her disease, admits that she can only paint in the morning hours. This is the only time when she is lucid and calm enough to draw.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Even though she has to take strong, daily medication, Karen often has crisis such as catatonia. She explains the episodes herself: It really doesnt matter how much medication Im on, occasionally I will loose the ability to speak. There are ways to communicate, but never with words. And even on oodles of medication Ive gotten to the point where I could not speak or move, frozen in place. 
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.weirdlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/234.jpg" rel="prettyphoto[3695]">
          <img alt="2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3696" height="819" src="//www.weirdlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/234.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Karen also talks about her life and friends on her website: Most of the time my life is predictable. But I seem not to react well to stress. These new, weird thoughts are a sign of my mind breaking down. My schizophrenic best friend who hears voices has a lot of weird thoughts. Like constantly, she obsesses over weird thoughts. Weird thoughts torment her. Why must the weirdness be tormenting?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Karen has happy marriage for many years  or, at leazt, as happy as it can be. When her medication dose is lower, she begins to talk nonsense, be impatient and constantly having weird thoughts about herself.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         One time, she says, she told her husband I think Im made of sugar and onions. And yes, she really believed it, just as she believes sometimez that she should die or that everybody hates her.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         However, Karen is one of the very few schizofrenic patients who can live a normal life. As she mentions on her blog, her schizofrenic friends are either institutionalized in mental hospitals or living alone in a house, going crazier and crazier each day.
        </p>
