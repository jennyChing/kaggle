                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-YQLo_UEc-cc/UYsjxkeVCrI/AAAAAAAAL7E/KbfF2BUJgH4/s1600/blackberryorangepopsicles2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-YQLo_UEc-cc/UYsjxkeVCrI/AAAAAAAAL7E/KbfF2BUJgH4/s1600/blackberryorangepopsicles2.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Mmm...mmm.. mmmm...
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     It is H-O-T in Arizona- simple, refreshing treats are needed!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Why are they so mini? I have a toddler- the big popsicles are just a bit too much for her, but the ice cubed size popsicles are perfect! An added bonus? She is teething and LOVES them!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Now, if you would like to make them a bit more adult you can add a splash of vodka or rum for a BBQ treat (and if you have a teething toddler, theadditionis a good one- for you, not them, of course ;)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Blackberry Orange Mint Popsicles- Makes about 12 ice cube popsicles
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <br/>
                     </b>
                     <b>
                      Ingredients/Materials:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <br/>
                     </b>
                     1.5 cups freshly squeezed orange juice
                     <i>
                      (approximately 4 oranges)
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     1/2 cup of blackberries
                     <br/>
                     1/2 cup of water
                     <br/>
                     Small handful of chopped mint leaves
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Ice Cube Tray
                     <br/>
                     AluminumFoil
                     <br/>
                     Popsicle sticks
                     <i>
                      (I got the mini ones at Michaels)
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Method:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <br/>
                     </b>
                     Crush the blackberries (a mortar &amp; pestle or a fork will do)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-nB7r5zqS08Q/UYsj3DltvRI/AAAAAAAAL7Y/Gz0uAzLo-oQ/s1600/smashedblackberry.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-nB7r5zqS08Q/UYsj3DltvRI/AAAAAAAAL7Y/Gz0uAzLo-oQ/s1600/smashedblackberry.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Mix ingredients together, pour into ice cube tray.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Lay tin foil on top of ice cube tray and make small slits with a knife to insert the popsicle sticks.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Freeze andEnjoy!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     You can add sugar or agave nectar if you like, but trust me- the sweetness is there! These popsicles are so easy to make- and without the dyes, additives and sugars of the store bought ones.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I have many different varieties- all using fruit for sweetness. I think I am going to make a apple-spinach-blueberry one and Kalia will never know the difference!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-voXHjscI0vQ/UYsjxVp-xxI/AAAAAAAAL64/5fOu5PhFN70/s1600/blackberryorangepopsicle.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-voXHjscI0vQ/UYsjxVp-xxI/AAAAAAAAL64/5fOu5PhFN70/s1600/blackberryorangepopsicle.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     You probably wouldn't notice but I am telling you- I have small white paint marks on my thumb and finger and you can see a bandaid from a DIY injury. My hands were not made for foodie pics!
