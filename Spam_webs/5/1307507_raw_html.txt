              <em>
               Disclosure: This review was made possible by

             <p>
              I was sitting at work one afternoon when I realized that aunt flo was late and I sent my husband a message so that he could swing by the store on the way home to pick up a pregnancy test. I remember waiting until the next morning to take it because I was nervous that it would come out negative as it had in the past. So the next morning, I peed on a stick and it quickly came up positive. I remember a rush of emotions come over me. I was excited and anxious at the same time. It had been 10 years since I had a baby in the house. I remember calling the doctors office one afternoon and scheduling my first prenatal visit.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Babys First Photo
             </h2>
             <p>
              The first prenatal visit went great and they scheduled me for an ultrasound. When the day came for the ultrasound, my husband and I was excited to see ourbaby for the very first time. I was called back to the ultrasound room and I remember seeing our baby on the techs screen. Our baby was perfect. It is amazing how a baby grows after conception.
             </p>
             <h2>
              FeelingMovement For the First Time During Pregnancy
             </h2>
             <p>
              I remember that I was about 12 weeks along, when I first felt my baby move. Those kicks, rolls, and punches would get stronger as the days went by. Soon daddy was able to feel him move too. It is an amazing feeling.
             </p>
             <h2>
              The Big Gender Reveal
             </h2>
             <p>
              My pregnancy was flying by and before I knew it we were sitting at the doctors office for our gender ultrasound. Once the ultrasound technician confirmed the gender of our baby, she let my husband take a picture of the screen and we had a bit of fun with our friends on Facebook before we announced that we were expecting a boy.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Hes Here!
             </h2>
             <p>
              As my due date grew closer and closer, we were getting more and more excited to meet our baby boy. The day finally arrived and we drove to the hospital to prepped for my c-section. I was wheeled into the operating room and after they gave me my spinal block in my back my husband was allowed to join me. It seemed like forever as I waited for my baby to cry. All of a sudden, I heard him cry and I knew that he arrived safe and sound. They took him over to the warming bed to get him cleaned up and get his vitals. He was so tiny and beautiful.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Breastfeeding Him for The Very First Time
             </h2>
             <p>
              I was able to try breastfeed him for the very first time about an hour after I had him. He latched on and nursed for the very first time. We successfully breastfed for 18 months before I ended up weaning him.
             </p>
             <h2>
              More First Memories After My Son Was Born
             </h2>
             <p>
              As you can see, pregnancy is a fun and exciting time and filled with many first moments that you will want to capture. I remember taking a ton of photos after he was born because I wanted to cherish them forever since he was my last baby. Once your child is born, they will also have many first moments during the first year. Here are a few of my sons first memories:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               I grasped my sons hand and held it after they placed him next to me on the bed so that they could wheel me to my recovery room
              </li>
              <li>
               Saying I love you to my son for the very first time
              </li>
              <li>
               Those tiny cries as the nurses got his vitals
              </li>

              </li>

               daddy holding him for the first time
              </li>

                pampers swaddlers
               </a>
               )
              </li>
              <li>
               first dirty diaper
              </li>
              <li>
               first outfit change because they messed up their clothes
              </li>
              <li>
               those first visitors
              </li>
              <li>
               the first night at the hospital
              </li>
              <li>
               going home
              </li>
              <li>
               keeping mom and dad up all night long
              </li>
              <li>
               babys first smile
              </li>
              <li>
               learning how to coo and babble
              </li>
              <li>
               finding their hands
              </li>
              <li>
               finding their toes
              </li>

              </li>

               learning how to talk

               and the list goes on and on.
              </li>

             <p>
              I cant believe that this time 4 years ago, I still had 15 weeks to go in my pregnancy. Even though I was completely miserable during my third trimesters, after my son was born I immediately missed feeling him move inside of my belly.
             </p>
