      <h1>
       Alexandra Levits 10 Tips to Fireproof Your Bridges When Exiting a Job
      </h1>
      <div class="meta">
       <em>
        Posted on:
       </em>
       March 4th, 2014
       <em>
        by
       </em>
       bobmorris
      </div>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        <a href="http://bobmorris.biz/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Levit.png">
         <img alt="Levit" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-25889" height="200" src="http://bobmorris.biz/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Levit-127x200.png" width="127"/>
        </a>
        According to the US Bureau of Labor and Statistics, the average young American has more than
        <em>
         seven
        </em>
        different employers before the age of 26; the typical 20-something changes jobs every 16 months.
        <strong>
         Alexandra Levit
        </strong>
        is the author of
        <em>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/They-Teach-Corporate-College-Revised/dp/1601630581/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1392393450&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=alexandra+levit%27s+they+don%E2%80%99t+teach+corporate+in+college.+2009" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/They-Teach-Corporate-College-Revised/dp/1601630581/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1392393450&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=alexandra+levit%27s+they+don%E2%80%99t+teach+corporate+in+college.+2009', 'They Dont Teach Corporate in College']);" target="_blank">
           They Dont Teach Corporate in College
          </a>
         </strong>
         : A Twenty-Somethings Guide to the Business World
        </em>
        ,  10th Anniversary Edition (Career Press, 2014). Millennials are eager to succeed. But in order to move on  and UP  they need to make sure they leave the right way.
       </p>
       <p>
        Here are her 10 Tips:
       </p>
       <p>
        1.
        <strong>
         Tell your supervisor first.
        </strong>
        You want your boss to hear the news from you, not from someone else in the department.
       </p>
       <p>
        2.
        <strong>
         Give two weeks notice.
        </strong>
        Stay for the entire two weeks, unless the company requests that you leave sooner.
       </p>
       <p>
        3.
        <strong>
         Be modest.
        </strong>
        Dont alienate your colleagues by bragging or chattering incessantly about your awesome new gig.
       </p>
       <p>
        4.
        <strong>
         Dont insult anyone or anything.
        </strong>
        Whether its true or not, show that you regret leaving such wonderful people behind.
       </p>
       <p>
        5.
        <strong>
         Stay on top of your responsibilities.
        </strong>
        Remember that youre accountable for you work until 5 pm on your last day.
       </p>
       <p>
        6.
        <strong>
         Continue to adhere to office protocol.
        </strong>
        You worked hard for that professional persona, so leave them with the right impression.
       </p>
       <p>
        7.
        <strong>
         Review the employee handbook.
        </strong>
        Understand what youre entitled to in regard to benefits and compensation for unused sick or vacation days.
       </p>
       <p>
        8.
        <strong>
         Organize your files.
        </strong>
        Make it easy for your colleagues to find materials, so that they can transition your workload seamlessly and wont need to call you at your new job.
       </p>
       <p>
        9.
        <strong>
         Do a great job training your replacement.
        </strong>
        Your current organization has paid your salary for a year or more. You owe it to them to leave your job in good hands.
       </p>
       <p>
        10.
        <strong>
         Dont take anything that doesnt belong to you.
        </strong>
        This includes office supplies and work product that was not developed by you personally.
       </p>
       <p>
        *     *     *
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Alexandra Levit
        </strong>
        s goal is to help people succeed in meaningful jobs, and to build relationships between organizations and top talent. A former nationally syndicated columnist for the Wall Street Journal and a current writer for the New York Times, Alexandra has authored several books, including the bestselling
