        <h1 class="headline">
         Grand Marnier Souffls
        </h1>
        <div class="article-excerpt">
         Souffls can be individual-sized, made in small ramekins, or big enough to serve a crowd. Ours are the former. You can prepare them up to 4 hours in advance through Step 4, then hold them in the refrigerator (uncovered) until youre ready to bake. In the case of early prep, add 5 to 7 minutes to cooking time.

          <img alt="Grand Marnier Souffls" class="attachment-post-thumb wp-post-image" height="336" src="http://d1ujpofy5vmb70.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Grand-Marnier-Souffls-300x336.jpg" width="300"/>
         </div>
         <!--post-image-->
         <p>
          By Jill Silverman Hough
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Serves:
          </strong>
          6
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Hands-on Time:
          </strong>
          30 minutes
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Total Time:
          </strong>
          55 minutes
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           INGREDIENTS:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Neutral-flavored cooking spray (such as safflower oil or grape seed oil)
          </li>
          <li>
           5 large eggs, separated, plus
          </li>
          <li>
           2 large egg whites
          </li>
          <li>
           1 cup whole milk
          </li>
          <li>
           1 1/2 tsp pure orange extract
          </li>
          <li>
           3 tbsp organic unsalted butter
          </li>
          <li>
           3 tbsp white whole-wheat flour
          </li>
          <li>
           1/3 cup plus 1 1/2 tsp organic evaporated cane juice, divided
          </li>
          <li>
           1/4 tsp sea salt
          </li>
          <li>
           3/4 tsp cream of tartar
          </li>
          <li>
           1/4 cup Grand Marnier or other orange-flavored liqueur
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           INSTRUCTIONS:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Preheat oven to 375F. Mist 6 8-oz ramekins with cooking spray and arrange on a rimmed baking sheet. In a medium bowl, whisk together egg yolks, milk and orange extract; set aside.
          </li>
          <li>
           In a large saucepan on medium, melt butter. Whisk in flour, 1/3 cup cane juice and salt. Cook, whisking occasionally, for 5 minutes, until slightly browned. Slowly whisk in milk mixture and cook, whisking constantly, until smooth and custard-like,about 1 minute. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.
          </li>
          <li>
           Meanwhile, using a mixer on high speed, beat egg whites until foamy. Add cream of tartar and continue beating until stiff peaks form.
          </li>
          <li>
           Stir Grand Marnier and about 1 cup egg white mixture into flour mixture. Fold in remaining whites in 3 additions and then divide batter among prepared ramekins.
          </li>
          <li>
           Sprinkle top of each souffl with 1/4 tsp of remaining cane juice. Bake until nicely browned on top and a toothpick inserted into center comes out almost entirely clean, about 25 minutes. Serve immediately.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          <em>
           Nutrients per serving (1 souffl):calories: 220, total fat: 11 g, sat. fat: 6 g, monounsaturated fat: 4 g, polyunsaturated fat: 1 g, carbs: 20 g, fiber: 0 g, sugars: 17 g, protein: 8 g, sodium: 176 mg, cholesterol: 174 mg
          </em>
         </p>
