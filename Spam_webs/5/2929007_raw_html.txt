     <p>
      <b>
       Estelle Bennett
      </b>
      (July 22, 1941  February 11, 2009)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTobit_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYTobit-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      was a member of the
      <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">
       girl group
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/The_Ronettes" title="The Ronettes">
       The Ronettes
      </a>
      , along with her sister
      <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Spector" title="Ronnie Spector">
       Ronnie Spector
      </a>
      (the 'Ronnie' of the band's name) and cousin
      <a href="/wiki/Nedra_Talley" title="Nedra Talley">
       Nedra Talley
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Estelle and her sister, Veronica, grew up New York. Children of a white father and black mother, were reported to have been bullied at school,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      because of their mixed race appearance.
     </p>
     <p>
      She attended George Washington High School in Manhattan where she was valedictorian. Known as studious and interested in fashion, she went on to study at Manhattan's Fashion Institute of Technology.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The first incarnation of what was to become the Ronettes appeared in when Estelle was 14.
     </p>
     <p>
      After a number of unsuccessful attempts the trio reinvented themselves as the Ronettes. Signed up by a then 23 year old Phil Spector, Ronnie was made lead, with Estelle and Nedra as backing .
     </p>
     <p>
      She was frequently romantically associated with a number of contemporary singers including Mick Jagger, George Harrison, Johnny Mathis and George Hamilton.
     </p>
     <p>
      After the Ronettes' 1966 break-up, she recorded a single for Laurie Records, "The Year 2000/The Naked Boy." She then quit the music business and had rarely been seen since. She married the group's road manager Joe Dong, and they had a daughter, Toyin.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Her mental health deteriorated in the following years. It was reported that, during a visit to fellow former Ronette, Nedra, she slept through her baby daughter crying.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She was reported to have had periods of homelessness when she would approach strangers in the street of New York, telling people that she would be singing with the Ronettes in a jazz club.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2007, when the group was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, it was decided that she was too fragile to perform with them, and spoke only a brief two sentences during her acceptance speech, "I would just like to say, thank you very much for giving us this award. I'm Estelle of the Ronettes, thank you." She did, however, come back out on stage for a final bow with the rest of the Ronettes after the performance of "Be My Baby".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTobit_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYTobit-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sisario2_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-sisario2-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bennett died of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colon_cancer" title="Colon cancer">
       colon cancer
      </a>
      aged 67 in
      <a href="/wiki/Englewood,_New_Jersey" title="Englewood, New Jersey">
       Englewood, New Jersey
      </a>
      . Her body was discovered on February 11, 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTobit_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYTobit-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A week after her death it was revealed that she had suffered from
      <a href="/wiki/Anorexia_nervosa" title="Anorexia nervosa">
       anorexia nervosa
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Schizophrenia" title="Schizophrenia">
       schizophrenia
      </a>
      in the years after the Ronettes break up and that later on she had been homeless in
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTobit_1-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYTobit-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sisario2_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-sisario2-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
