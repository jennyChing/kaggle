       <p>
        We as a race are inquisitive creatures- we just arent content by being somewhere, we want to know how we got there. This characteristic is what led Socrates to prison and Galileo to be tried for heresy. Why did he feel the need to see whether the earth revolved around the sun or if the planets all danced to hey Macarena?

       </p>

        Why are visitors prompted to click on the CTA button of one landing page and not the other?
       </p>

       <h3>
        The Psychological Guide to Landing Page Conversions
       </h3>
       <p>
        How your visitor behaves on your landing page is directly related to how his mind and body responds to the combination of design and copy (landing page) that youve put in front of him- hence, the title.
       </p>
       <p>
        What were about to embark upon right now is an interesting journey- a guide into the psychology of conversions; a write up thats going to let you in on all the psychological secrets to conversions- what is that makes your visitors tick and click your CTA button and why does that button being red factors into anything.
       </p>
       <p>
        For your ease and mine, I have divided the guide into two main parts. The first will shed some light on the actual psychological phenomena that guide customer behavior while the second will teach you how to use this knowledge and practically apply it into your landing pages to score conversions-
        <i>
         really high conversions!
        </i>
       </p>
       <h3 style="background-color: #141b22; color: white; padding: 10px 10px 10px 10px;">
        The Theory
       </h3>
       <p>
        Online marketing has evolved and so have the consumers, a shiny banner ad may have worked a few years ago but it no longer does the same trick- why?
        <a href="https://blog.newscred.com/article/sales@newscred.com/b30b982d32d10e0d61216ea3b454b11c" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         Because the average consumer thats surfing the web has become much smarter
        </a>
        , with years of research they have now become immune to a lot of BS marketing tactics like giving away free mp3 players and email spam.
       </p>
       <p>
        And while flashy ads dont work anymore and email filters protect us from spam, theres one thing that hasnt changed and wont really change for all the years to come- human or in this case customer psychology- the triggers that cause your customer to behave in a certain way.
       </p>
       <p>
        Human actions arent arbitrary, we dont just do something just because, theres a whole set of brain and body functions that go behind any decision that we make- whether thats batting our eyelashes or filling up a lead capture form.
       </p>
       <p>
        The knowledge of what drives or triggers customer behavior is pure gold if used in your marketing tactics, whether thats designing your landing page or setting up your entire content marketing strategy.
       </p>
       <p>
        So, without further ado lets just see what exactly these psychological phenomena are.
       </p>
       <h4>
        The Deictic Gaze
       </h4>
       <p>
        Look at the picture below and tell me where your gaze goes?
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="look left meme" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9623 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/look-left-meme.jpg" height="365" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        To the right? Of course, it did- because your brain is just wired that way. When we see someone, anyone looking at something our brain acts reflexively and starts looking at that thing too.
       </p>
       <p>
        Whether thats looking at gunk stuck on the side of the road or a CTA button that this woman is looking at.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="girl gazing right" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9625 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/girl-gazing-right.jpg" height="411" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <h4>
        The Primacy Effect
       </h4>
       <p>
        The
        <a href="https://changingminds.org/explanations/theories/primacy_effect.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         primacy effect
        </a>
        dictates that when given the choice between a list of things, our brains are hardwired to remember the first few things, more than the later things.
       </p>
       <p>
        Also, our brain regards things at the beginning to be of more importance than the things that come later on. One major reason why the primacy effect works is because an individual or in this case your customer pays more attention likely at the start and then they start drifting off because their brain gets bored or gets tired later on.
       </p>
       <p>
        In other words, positive reinforcement works great as far as your landing page campaigns are concerned.
       </p>
       <h4>
        The Von Restorff Effect
       </h4>
       <p>
        Customers tend to remember things that stand out, things that are different than the rest.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="odd one out" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9627 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/odd-one-out.jpg" height="472" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        The burnt one caught your attention didnt it?
       </p>
       <p>
        Why? Because its different from the red ones, because it contrasts and because it stands out and attracts you.
       </p>
       <p>
        Basically you remember items that are different from the rest of the bunch in size, color and other characteristics.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="odd one out" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9643 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/odd-one-out1.jpg" height="492" width="702"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        See what I mean?
       </p>
       <h4>
        Informational Social Influence
       </h4>
       <p>
        Informational social influence aka
        <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Informational_Social_Influence" target="_blank">
         social proof
        </a>
        is a phenomenon that dictates that people adopt the actions of others in order to conform to their choices or the right choices.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="conformity" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9629 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/conformity1.jpg" height="592" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Social proof has very much to do with herd behavior. Our brain goes where other people are going, the brains decision making center, the pre-frontal cortex has a direct link to the area responsible for rewards and social learning, seeing someone elses choice activates that center and causes us to follow their lead.
       </p>
       <p>
        So, basically monkey see monkey do but in a much more elegant way.
       </p>
       <h4>
        Loss Aversion
       </h4>
       <p>
        We are scared to lose something, so basically we value not losing something more over actually gaining something. And because we are so scared of losing something we do anything in our power to stop this.
       </p>
       <p>
        Maybe even subscribe to a white paper just because we couldnt afford losing any more of our valuable time and money.
       </p>
       <h3 style="background-color: #141b22; color: white; padding: 10px 10px 10px 10px;">
        The Practical
       </h3>
       <p>
        The aforementioned theory would really be of no help to you if you dont have in front you a practical pathway to help put these theories to test and milk all the benefits that you can from them.
       </p>
       <p>
        Lets start with them one by one like we did before and as an added advantage lets sprinkle in some real life examples of the theories success right alongside.
       </p>
       <h4>
        Directional cues for your landing pages
       </h4>
       <p>
        The deictic gaze theory explicitly states that our eyes immediately go to where someone else is looking, so using directional cues on your landing pages to point to the sweet spots on the pages really help.
       </p>
       <p>
        With the help of a directional cue like an arrow or a pathway or even a humans line of sight you as a marketer are essentially able to take your visitor by the hand and guide them towards your CTA button, towards the conversions that you need, that you deserve.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="directional" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9630 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/directional.jpg" height="365" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        You look at the lead capture form and CTA button, dont you.
       </p>
       <p>
        A study performed
        <a href="https://usableworld.com.au/2009/03/16/you-look-where-they-look/" target="_blank">
         UsableWorld
        </a>
        used an image of a baby and his gaze to see whether or not this would impact where a visitor would look upon arriving on the page, the study was done using 106 people.
       </p>
       <p>
        The first picture is of a heat map of the page where the baby is looking straight ahead, the red spots show how much time a visitor spent on a particular spot.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="heat tracking study" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9631 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/heat-tracking-study.jpg" height="504" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Now look at the heat map when the baby is facing the copy, where the website owner wanted to get his visitors attention.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="heat mapping study two" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9632 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/heat-mapping-study-two.jpg" height="508" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        See the difference?
       </p>
       <h4>
        A sensational headline
       </h4>
       <p>
        Your landing page headline needs to rock your visitors world, why? Because primacy effect clearly states that the first thing that your visitor sees which is of course your headline needs to be memorable and attractive, or else theyll simply lose interest and move along without even bothering with the rest of your page; copy, form and all.
       </p>
       <p>
        The headline that you choose to put on your landing page should really pop. It should be able to grab the attention of your visitors within a millisecond.
       </p>
       <p>
        Take the unique selling point of your respective service or product and nail it perfectly on your landing page to get the most out of your headline. Using your keywords in the headline also helps you attract your target audience.
       </p>
       <p>
        Try taking inspiration from this.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Pulp Fingers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9633 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Pulp-Fingers.jpg" height="517" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <h4>
        A contrasting call to action button
       </h4>
       <p>
        Your CTA button is the star of your landing page; if its not placed in a prominent position well then whats the point of you even building a landing page because according to the Von Restorff Effect your visitors are only going to pay attention if it stands out for them.
       </p>
       <p>
        Your CTA needs to be prominent, it needs to leap off from the page so that your visitors know exactly where they have to click to get what youre offering.
       </p>
       <p>
        You need to make it noticeable, make it clear and make it attractive.
       </p>
       <p>
        Like so,
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="voice bunny-CTA" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9634 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/voice-bunny-CTA.jpg" height="369" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Testing is very important here, you need to test the your CTA button; try varying positions, different colors, different sizes and shapes and select the one that pulls in the most conversions.
       </p>
       <h4>
        Powerful testimonials
       </h4>
       <p>
        Nothing sells a product better than a loyal customer whos all praises about your amazing product. Factoring in the whole social proof theory this is simply because customers often think alike, which is why they trust each others opinion.
       </p>
       <p>
        Including legit testimonials on your landing page really helps get conversions, make sure that the testimonials are honest and descriptive, simply including a customer quote saying Amazing Service doesnt really persuade your visitors.
       </p>
       <p>
        Social media likes and tweets hits the bulls eye with the whole herd behaviour thing and makes your new prospects more likely to sign up for your service or buy your product.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="basecamp testimonails" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9635 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/basecamp-testimonails.jpg" height="697" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <h4>
        Loss Aversion
       </h4>
       <p>
        Try telling your customers what theyll be losing if they dont sign up for your service and see the conversions flood in.
       </p>
       <p>
        We hate losing stuff and we love free stuff- combine both these factors and come up with copy that doesnt simply give your visitors something for free but has some value attached with it.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="loss bounce" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9636 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/loss-bounce.jpg" height="228" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Say Well take care of Shipping instead of free shipping on your CTA button and see how your visitors respond.
       </p>
       <p>
        Armed with these psychological triggers and their practical implementation there is no chance that your landing page will ever strike out.
       </p>
