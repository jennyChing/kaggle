           <p class="intro">
            The pictures on your website are speaking thousands of words to your customers and potential customers, and you might not always like what theyre saying. Even if youve spent hours choosing just the right look for those photos and illustrations, youll want to revisit those images if its been a couple of years (or more) since you picked them out. You can save yourself from the dreaded outdated photograph by learning how to choose timeless stock art.
           </p>
           <h2>
            What makes an image look dated?
           </h2>
           <p>
            First you want to think about these six things that lock an image into a particular time period:
           </p>
           <div class="quote-box">
            <p class="quote-text">
             "Even something as timeless as a suit can look dated if the cut is wrong, the shirt is the wrong color or the tie is the wrong width."
            </p>
           </div>
           <h4>
            Clothing, hairstyles and makeup
           </h4>
           <p>
            Pillbox hats are adorableon Jackie Kennedy in 1963. Plaid flannel shirts are as hot right now as ever, but not long ago they screamed either lumberjack or grunge. And the girl with the bun loosely gathered on top of her head? Tomorrow that pic will be so Brooklyn-five-minutes-ago. Even something as timeless as a suit can look dated if the cut is wrong, the shirt is the wrong color or the tie is the wrong width.
           </p>
           <p>
            While clothing and hairstyle trends do come around again, it doesnt happen fast. Timeless images use subtle and natural styling. So when you are choosing an image, remember that a pencil skirt will last decades whereas a bubble skirt is already over. Look for neutral colors and classic clothing. Plain jeansnot too skinny, not too looseand a ponytail, on a woman, will always be stylish. A soul patch is not. Hint: anything you can find in your teens closet will probably look dated by next season (if not next week).
           </p>
           <h4>
            Colors
           </h4>
           <p>
            You know how you can walk into a house and know that the avocado green refrigerator is a relic? Colors tell us a lot about somethings age without us even having to think about it. If you have any doubts, check out Pantones
            <a href="http://www.pantone.com/pages/Pantone/Pantone.aspx?pg=19758&amp;ca=1">
             40 Years of Color
            </a>
            . We are not suggesting that you avoid images with color in them (not at all), but if you find yourself choosing image after image in turquoise, know that you are miring yourself in the
            <a href="http://www.pantone.com/pages/Pantone/Pantone.aspx?pg=20705&amp;ca=1">
             2010 color of the year
            </a>
            , and if chocolate is your accent color, just pretend you intended to go retro.
           </p>
           <p>
            To get the look that lasts forever, remember that subtle blues and grays have withstood the test of time.
           </p>
           <h4>
            Technology
           </h4>
           <p>
            Technology changes so fast its impossible to keep ahead of it. Your best bet, unless you are a tech blogger, is to steer clear of pictures that have gadgets like cellphones and MP3 players in themtheyll be out of date before you can even get them posted. The best photographers will update their stock images with the latest gadget, so if you have to have images of the iPhone on your site, choose a favorite contributor and stalk their profile page for new uploads.
           </p>
           <h4>
            Skylines
           </h4>
           <p>
            Skylines seem iconic, dont they? Enduring, everlasting, strong. Until a new signature building goes up or a national disaster happens. If we could only count the number of hours filmmakers spent editing images of the World Trade Center out of movies after 9/11.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you want to include an image of a building, showcase your own storefront. That way its natural and easy to post a new pic when you refresh your paint job or get a new sign.
           </p>
           <h4>
            Lighting
           </h4>
           <a href="http://www.istockphoto.com/stock-photo-22119222-loving-couple.php" target="_blank">

           <p>
            Even subtle clues like lighting tell a viewer whether a picture is current or out of date. A backlit flare is generally joyful and looks fantastic. But if you want a picture thats going to last a long time, look for one with what photographers call a High Keybright images with not very much contrast and few shadows.
           </p>
           <h4>
            The latest visual effect
           </h4>
           <p>
            It can be really tempting to take advantage of trends in photography. Even though tilt-shift images came back in style a few years ago, they still look really fresh.
           </p>
           <p>
            But it wont be long before they all start to look the same. The same can be said for HDR.
           </p>
           <p>
            Just know that when you are choosing a visual effect because it looks eye-catchingly brand new, it might someday (sooner than youd like) pinpoint itself in time. And if you have an in-house graphic designer, he or she can take a timeless image and give it any of the oh-so-now effects you desire. Then he or she can update the same image next year with the latest and greatest.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Timeless elements in any image
           </h2>
           <p>
            So weve proven how fickle trends can be, does that mean its impossible to pick a photo or illustration that will endure? Absolutely not. When youre looking for an image thats truly timeless, look for something that transcends eras. There are two ways to do this:
           </p>
           <h4>
            Emotional connection
           </h4>
           <a href="http://www.istockphoto.com/stock-photo-6613919-old-man-with-beard-amp-long-white-hair.php" target="blank">
            <img alt="old man with beard &amp; long white hair - Stock Image" src="http://i.istockimg.com/generic_image_view/217510/217510" style="float:right; margin-left:20px;"/>
           </a>
           <p>
            This image of an old man breaks several rules outlined above, but its still timeless. Thats because it engages the reader with the mans humanity. The fact that he looks like he is about to start a conversation with you triggers that emotional connection to the man, his fascinating and open face, and also the image. Long after the shirt hes wearing is out of style and a different level of focus is trending, this mans eyes will still be engaging.
           </p>
           <p>
            Pro Tip: When looking for an image that will stand the test of time, have a group of people write down words that the picture evokes. If you have one or two, the image isnt going to stand up for long. A great image offers many possible stories and interpretations that can be read and adjusted even as the picture ages.
           </p>
           <h4>
            Metaphor
           </h4>
           <p>
            Another way to trick viewers into getting past that bias of style is to use an image that is metaphorical or conceptual rather than a direct illustration. For example, illustrating a post about accumulating wealth with a stack of money is a direct illustration of the idea whereas an image of a squirrel gathering nuts for the winter adds a new depth to the story its illustrating. The best photographers do this already with their images. All you have to do is find one that speaks to your content.
           </p>
           <p>
            Were not saying you cant go retro with your images. But when youre developing the unique aesthetic of your blog or website, make certain you take time to think carefully about the images youll be including, what you want them to say and how long they can exist online before youll wish youd replaced them.
           </p>
