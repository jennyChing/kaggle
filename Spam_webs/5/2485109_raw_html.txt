         <p>
          By
          <a href="http://www.globalresearch.ca/autism-bomb-bayer-herbicide-causes-autism-even-at-trace-levels/5417852" target="_blank">
           Christina Sarich
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Bayers Liberty Link GMO crops made to withstand glufosinate ammonium (GLA) herbicide are linked directly to autism-like symptoms, according to a new study. Even in low doses, both pre and post-natal exposure to GLA caused symptoms in laboratory mice.
         </p>
         <p>
          Published in
          <strong>
           <a href="http://journal.frontiersin.org/Journal/10.3389/fnbeh.2014.00390/full" target="_blank">
            Frontiers of Behavioral Neuroscience
           </a>
          </strong>
          , the study outlines how GLA, one of the most widely used herbicides in agriculture is harming neurological health.
         </p>
         <p>
          Pointing to the findings of
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.mdpi.com/1099-4300/15/4/1416" target="_blank">
            previous research
           </a>
          </strong>
          linking herbicides and autism, as well as with Parkinsons and Alzheimers, the study explains that pesticide and herbicide exposure weakens the basic structure of the brain.
         </p>
         <p>
          The developmental impact of GLA was examined by exposing female mice to low dose GLA during both pre- and postnatal periods and analyzed potential developmental and behavioral changes of the offspring during infancy and adulthood.
         </p>
         <p>
          A host of neurobehavioral tests revealed some unsettling results:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           . . .significant effects of GLA maternal exposure on early reflex development, pup communication, affiliative behaviors, and preference for social olfactory cues, but emotional reactivity and emotional memory remained unaltered. These behavioral alterations showed a striking resemblance to changes seen in animal models of Autistic Spectrum Disorders. At the brain level, GLA maternal exposure caused some increase in relative brain weight of the offspring. In addition, reduced expression of Pten and Peg3  two genes implicated in autism-like deficits  was observed in the brain of GLA-exposed pups at postnatal day 15.
          </p>
          <p>
           Our work thus provides new data on the link between pre- and postnatal exposure to the herbicide GLA and the onset of autism-like symptoms later in life. It also raises fundamental concerns about the ability of current safety testing to assess risks of pesticide exposure during critical developmental periods.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Bayer Crop Science
          <a href="https://www.bayercropscience.us/products/traits/libertylink" target="_blank">
           will tell you
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           High-performance LibertyLink traits are widely available across crops  canola, cotton, corn and soybeans  including leading brands such as FiberMax and Stoneville cotton, InVigorcanola and more than 100 brands of corn and soybeans, including HBK soybeans.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          This means that GLA is in use on thousands of acres of crops across the United States and in Canada.
         </p>
         <p>
          The
          <a href="http://www.responsibletechnology.org/gmo-dangers/65-health-risks/1notes" target="_blank">
           Institute for Responsible Technology
          </a>
          has stated that:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Twice the number of chickens died when fed Liberty Link corn. The death rate for chickens fed Chardon LL GM corn for 42 days was 7%, compared to 3.5% for controls. GM-fed chickens also had more erratic body weight and food intake, and less weight gain overall. But these results were dismissed without follow-up.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Were the results of this study ignored like so many other GMO tests because the makers were already well aware that GLA was toxic and deathly?
         </p>
         <p>
          Bayer is working to defeat GMO labeling as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          How convenient for Bayer, since it
          <a href="http://pharma.bayer.com/scripts/pages/en/news_room/news_room/news_room84.php" target="_blank">
           makes pharmaceuticals which also treat neurological impairment
          </a>
          like Alzheimers disease. Its also convenient for Titan pharmaceuticals, which makes drugs to
          <a href="http://www.titanpharm.com/about.htm" target="_blank">
           treat Parkinsons
          </a>
          . A new appointee to the board at Titan includes one former Bayer executive. And of course,
          <a href="http://houston.legalexaminer.com/fda-prescription-drugs/fda-reviews-drug-to-treat-condition-linked-to-ssri-use/" target="_blank">
           Bayer makes drugs
          </a>
          like selective seratonin re-uptake inhibitors (SSRIs) that treat autism as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its a tangled web.
         </p>
