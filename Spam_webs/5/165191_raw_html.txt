        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Why I hate the Mirena IUD
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-12-14T21:12:11+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 14, 2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://asmomseesit.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Mom
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://asmomseesit.com/hate-mirena-iud/#comments">
           27 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="17514191" data-link="http://asmomseesit.com/hate-mirena-iud/" data-summary="" data-title="Why I hate the Mirena IUD">
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          How the Mirena IUD went from a seemingly sound decision to something that destroyed my relationship and made me lash out at everyone.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://asmomseesit.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/mirena-iud.jpg?97fe1d">
          <img alt="Mirena IUD" class="aligncenter wp-image-5425" height="196" src="http://asmomseesit.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/mirena-iud.jpg?97fe1d" width="360"/>
          <br/>
         </a>
         I have two kids that are 10 years apart. I love my kids, but there are times I feel overwhelmingly exhausted. Ive been a single mom since I was 22-years-old and though I once felt it was a horribly selfish thing to say, I like that Im gradually finding more time to myself. Im able to travel more, the kids are more fun as they get older, and I get more time for friends and even for relationships.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span id="more-5424">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         After a series of bad relationships and the very sad ending of a long-term relationship, I wasnt ready to meet anyone. I wasnt looking and I didnt want to go through anymore heartbreak. Plans be damned, I started dating a guy with whom Id been friends for two years and it didnt take long before I found that I was happier than Id been in years, or likely ever. After a few months of dating, we decided that a more convenient and reliable birth control solution would work best for us. I spoke with my doctor, we decided on the Mirena IUD.
        </p>
        <h2>
         The start of the downward Mirena spiral
        </h2>
        <p>
         The day of the
         <a href="http://asmomseesit.com/mirena-iud-as-birth-control/">
          Mirena insertion
         </a>
         , I felt brilliant for having made such a responsible decision. The day after the Mirena insertion, I had a break down; an intense, sobbing, drama-filled breakdown. I thought it was just me, as I have a history with
         <a href="http://asmomseesit.com/what-its-like-being-bipolar/">
          bipolar
         </a>
         . Then, things gradually started to get worse.
        </p>
        <p>
         My sudden mood changes became worse and more frequent, I was lashing out at the kids and at Doug and my depression became worse. I made a point to talk to my doctor and increased my bipolar medication. Things got better, then got far worse. It felt like nothing helped and my relationship was deteriorating. It saddened me to slowly lose him but it was like I was powerless to change the way I was feeling.
        </p>
        <p>
         Finally, I made the decision to have it removed. The soonest appointment was 3 weeks away, but it felt like an eternity. My relationship with Doug ended because, according to him, the moods created too much stress for him and he wasnt sure he felt the same way about me. It was the most difficult thing Ive ever heard in my life but I didnt blame him. All I could hope for was that things would change once the Mirena was removed.
        </p>
        <h2>
         The final days of the Mirena
        </h2>
        <p>
         Removal was easy and painless. By later that day, I felt a little better. In the days to come, I felt calmer, happier, there was a sense of relief and I felt more clear headed. Eventually, Doug and I reignited our relationship and Im so happy to report that were now engaged to be married.
        </p>
        <p>
         I would caution anyone thinking about Mirena to consider the repercussions, especially if youve a history of depression. While it has worked for many people and Ive heard many positive experiences with Mirena, mine definitely wasnt one of them. It saddens me so greatly to think about what I almost lost, but Im thankful that I was able to quickly and easily take care of many of the issues I was having just by having the Mirena removed.
        </p>
