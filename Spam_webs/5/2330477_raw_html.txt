     <article data-streamid="7.1401142" data-streamtags='["NYDN.Entertainment.d"]' itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="a-module" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.984322!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_970/assault-horizon-2-web.jpg" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
       <figure class="a-image h">
        <img alt="'Ace Combat: Assault Horizon' looks like a thrill ride, but crashes and burns at times as a gaming challenge." src="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.984322.1322628298!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_970/assault-horizon-2-web.jpg"/>
        <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">
         NAMCO
        </span>
        <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">
         'Ace Combat: Assault Horizon' looks like a thrill ride, but crashes and burns at times as a gaming challenge.
        </h2>
        <a class="a-enlarge" href="#">
        </a>
       </figure>
      </div>
      <p>
       <em>
        <strong>
         LOVED IT:
        </strong>
        Tremendous production value, nice cinematic presentation, solid multiplayer options
       </em>
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       <em>
        <strong>
         HATED IT:
        </strong>
        Gameplay summons little skill, short campaign, dimwitted A.I.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       <em>
        <strong>
         GRAB IT IF:
        </strong>
        You want to watch a video game instead of play it
       </em>
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       There are explosions. There are epic gunfights. And there are monstrous battles.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       But in "Ace Combat: Assault Horizon," none of it is truly any fun.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       Namco's venerable aerial adventure game takes things in a different direction, aiming for a more cinematic, less obtuse approach. The resulting game is truly a beauty to behold, but it lacks any and all challenge to actually play.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       Yes, "Assault Horizon" does a great job of drawing the sky and putting you into the cockpit of a fighter jet. But too often, you'll feel less like a pilot and more like you're simply along for the ride.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       None of this is the world's fault. Developer Project Aces crafts some tremendous-looking environments, and everything has a sharp look. Things look even better when you slip into dogfight mode, the camera closing in on the detailed jet fighters themselves. And when you down an opposing jet, it certainly appears to be fulfilling; sometimes, the ensuing crashes result in mini-in-game cutscenes show off a flame-engulfed craft crash-landing.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       The thing is, "Assault Horizon" seems intent on letting you admire all this spectacle while never truly providing a challenge. The dogfights look beautiful, but they play far too easily. It won't take long before you realize that once you get behind an enemy fighter, you can simply mash the machine gun for awhile, then fire off a few missiles, then mash the machine gun even more, then fire off a few missiles, to earn victory.
      </p>
      <div class="a-module" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.984323!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_970/ace-combat-web.jpg" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
       <figure class="a-image h">
        <img alt="'Ace Combat: Assault Horizon' looks like a thrill ride, but crashes and burns at times as a gaming challenge." src="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.984323.1322635802!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_970/ace-combat-web.jpg"/>
        <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">
        </span>
        <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">
         'Ace Combat: Assault Horizon' looks like a thrill ride, but crashes and burns at times as a gaming challenge.
        </h2>
        <a class="a-enlarge" href="#">
        </a>
       </figure>
      </div>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       You can fly conservatively throughout "Assault Horizon," never truly taking a risk -- never doing any of those amazing things that you've seen on TV.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       This lather-rinse-repeat simplicity may actually draw a few fans of old-school patterned gaming, but it's hard to get into a solid aerial groove. Helicopter missions and on-rails sections of the game break up the aerial monotony, but they also lack any degree of difficulty.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       The on-rails levels are by far the worst. Granted, it's never been easy to create a level of difficulty in an on-rails section in any shooter, but in "Assault Horizon," I merely fired my chopper machine gun aimlessly, never once worried about what might happen.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       In the end, "Assault Horizon" isn't so much a bad game as it is a shallow one. Contrary to the success of the "Call of Duty" and "Battlefield" series, explosive, cinematic visuals cannot completely carry a game. Even in those titles, a skeleton story provided intrigue, and the gameplay was well-honed.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       Project Aces forgets that in this game. If you want to play a fighter game, consider the older "Ace Combat 6: Fires of Liberation."
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       If you want to take part in a "cinematic experience," for whatever that's worth, then give "Assault Horizon" a chance.
      </p>
      <p class="DNTEXT">
       <em>
        Reviewed on Xbox 360
       </em>
      </p>
