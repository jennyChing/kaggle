         <h1 class="title">
          Easy Crochet Edging Pattern
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="small">
          By
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/author/petalstopicotsgmail-com/" rel="author" title="Posts by Kara">
            Kara
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="small">
          on
         </span>
         <abbr class="date time published" title="2014-07-07T00:30:00-0400">
          July 7, 2014
         </abbr>
         <span class="small">
          in
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/category/crochet-love/">
           Crochet Love
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <section class="entry">
         <div class="pf-content">
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/edging-1-of-3.jpg">
            <img alt="Easy Crochet Edging" class="aligncenter wp-image-10239 size-full" height="366" src="http://www.petalstopicots.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/edging-1-of-3.jpg" title="Easy Crochet Edging" width="550"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Thiseasy crochet edging pattern is a super quick way to add a personal and handmade touch to any piece of fleece or fabric  perfect for a last minute gift or for making items and loveys to donate! If the fabric already has a blanket stitch around it, just work into the tops of the stitches. If there is no stitching around it, no worries at all  check out my post
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/2012/04/quick-and-easy-crocheted-blanket-edging-patterns/">
            here on how to prep your fabric to crochet a border
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Got your fabric? Ready to get started?
           </em>
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Easy Crochet Edging
          </h1>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/edging-3-of-3.jpg">
            <img alt="Easy Crochet Edging Pattern" class="aligncenter wp-image-10240 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.petalstopicots.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/edging-3-of-3-300x300.jpg" title="Easy Crochet Edging Pattern" width="300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Heres what you need:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            worsted weight yarn
           </li>
           <li>
            H hook
           </li>
           <li>
            fleece or fabric blanket
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Click
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/resources/crochet-abbreviations/">
            here
           </a>
           to reference
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/resources/crochet-abbreviations/" title="Crochet Abbreviations">
            Crochet Abbreviations
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Round 1:
           </strong>
           Join yarn in any blanket stitch along edge of fabric (see
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/2012/04/quick-and-easy-crocheted-blanket-edging-patterns/">
            here
           </a>
           to add your own), ch 4 (counts as 1 dc and 1 ch), skip next blanket stitch, *1 dc in next blanket stitch, ch 1, skip next blanket stitch, rep from * around, working [1 dc, ch 1, 1 dc], ch 1, skip next blanket stitch around corners (
           <em>
            Note: you may need to adjust how you work your corners depending on the degree of curve you have to your blankets corners
           </em>
           ), join.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Round 2:
           </strong>
           Ch 1 (does not count as stitch), work 1 sc in first st, 1 sc in next ch-1 sp, *1 sc in next st, 1 sc in next ch-1 sp,rep from * around, join.
          </p>
          <p>
           Here is a stitch diagram of the basic repeat in case you like a more visual pattern. Click
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/resources/stitch-chart-symbols/">
            here
           </a>
           to reference
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/resources/stitch-chart-symbols/">
            Crochet Stitch Symbols
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/simple-edging-diagram.png">
            <img alt="crochet edging stitch diagram" class="aligncenter wp-image-10302 size-full" height="150" src="http://www.petalstopicots.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/simple-edging-diagram.png" title="crochet edging stitch diagram" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/2012/04/quick-and-easy-crocheted-blanket-edging-patterns/" title="Quick and Easy Crocheted Blanket Edging Patterns">
            <img alt="DSC_0191-square" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-10305" height="300" src="http://www.petalstopicots.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/DSC_0191-square-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Click
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/2012/04/quick-and-easy-crocheted-blanket-edging-patterns/" title="Quick and Easy Crocheted Blanket Edging Patterns">
            here
           </a>
           for more
           <a href="http://www.petalstopicots.com/2012/04/quick-and-easy-crocheted-blanket-edging-patterns/" title="Quick and Easy Crocheted Blanket Edging Patterns">
            quick and easy (and free!) blanket edging patterns
           </a>
           .
          </p>
