          </h1>

          <p>
           <img alt="DSC_0091" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10503" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/theweekendinparis.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/DSC_0091.jpg?resize=725%2C483"/>
           The Palais Galliera Paris is also known as the Muse de la Mode de la ville de Paris. It isthe City of Lights fashion museum. Today, historic garments by fashion icon Jeanne Lanvin (the oldest French fashion house still in operation) are being exhibited in the beautiful buildinguntil August 23, 2015.
           <span id="more-10497">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="DSC_0087" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10502" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/theweekendinparis.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/DSC_0087.jpg?resize=725%2C483"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Themuseum wascommissioned by the Duchesse de Galliera in the late 1800s to house her vast collection which ironically was never shown there. The sitewas not completed before her death, but thankfully architect Paul-Ren-Lon Ginain finished it in 1894.
          </p>
          <p>
           The haute couture art of dress designby Jeanne Lanvin was born out of her inspirations from her travels. She kept extensive notes and swatches of ethnic fabrics and incorporated her vision of the well-dressed woman into her designs. Regarded as anexpertin the use ofpatterns and colors, she also was known for her mastery of intricate embroidery, topstitches, cut-outs and unique use of jewels to adorn the gowns.
          </p>
          <p>
           Her style is classical French with an Art Deco twist. Ribbons, crystals, beads and silk tassels were the finishing touches that set her designs apart. The current exhibition displays over a hundred itemsfrom1867-1946 organized into collections:
          </p>
          <p>
           Lanvin Blue and the Color Range, Lanvin Craftsmanship, Black and White, The Dress of Style, Hats and the Childrens Departments, Embellishment and Embroidery, The Exhibition of 1025, Exotic and Ethnic Inspirations, Religious and Medieval Influence, Going Geometrical, Bridal Gowns, and last but not least Glitter and Romance.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-04-13 at 11.45.47 AM" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10505" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/theweekendinparis.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Screen-Shot-2015-04-13-at-11.45.47-AM.png?resize=324%2C579"/>
           My personal favorite was the dramatic My Fair Lady dress. It is a 1939 bias-cut ribbon in black organza with a black tulle base and a large black taffeta bow. How I wish we were allowed to take photographs, but on the other hand, they would not have done the clothes justice. You simply must see them in person to appreciate the details in the design, fabrics and finishes.
          </p>
          <p>
           To understand the world of Lanvin, the museum offers a free iPhone app you can download before visiting the museum which will allow you to learn a bit of the history of thehouse of Lanvin. While in the museum, follow along with the app to read English explanations and backgrounds about each piece on view. To download,
           <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/jeanne-lanvin-exhibition-at/id972036619?mt=8">
            click HERE
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="DSC_0085 - Version 2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10501" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/theweekendinparis.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/DSC_0085-Version-2.jpg?resize=725%2C1088"/>
           Make sure you walk the grounds surrounding the museum. The architecture is wonderful and around the back you will have gorgeous up close views of the Eiffel Tower, an added bonus!
          </p>
