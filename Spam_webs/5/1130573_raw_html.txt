        <p>
         Our
         <strong>
          Market Tour
         </strong>
         always start at the meeting point, Cooking Lisbon in Lisbons downtown. At 10 am we startet with presentations of each other and together with our new friends we went to Mercado da Ribeira (one of the most famous markets in Lisbon).
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Market Tour in Lisbon, Vegetables" class="alignnone wp-image-382" data-id="382" height="307" src="http://cookinglisbon.com/wp-content/uploads/lisbon-market-tour-vegetables1.jpg" title="Market Tour in Lisbon, Vegetables" width="605"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         During the transportation we speak about local habits, portuguese culture,
         <strong>
          gastronomy
         </strong>
         , wines, among many other ocasional subjects that
         <strong>
          travelers
         </strong>
         want to know.
        </p>
        <p>
         On the
         <strong>
          Market Tour
         </strong>
         we choose first the fresh fish, to have the best quality possible of the ingredients and since we dont have any restrictions, we choose a fish called
         <strong>
          Corvina
         </strong>
         (
         <i>
          Argyrosomus regius ,
         </i>
         from the family of Sciaenidae with big dimensions  2 metres and 50 kg ).
        </p>
        <p>
         The Market Tour at Cooking Lisbon has four courses during the Cooking Class (which is included). During the five hours of the activity people get in touch with portuguese habits, meet the locals and knowledge.
        </p>
        <div id="fb-root">
        </div>
        <p>

        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          During the class people can learn how to make a starter / soup, the fish dish (today : Corvina ), a meat dish and of course one dessert, everything with good wines ( fresh white wine and red).
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         This fish was prepared on a wok from Silampos called Cataplana ( Algarve Model ), which it is possible to find worldwide.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Wok and the Fresh Fish
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          After the fish dish made on the Cooking Class from the Market Tour, it was time to prepare the meat dish, always with fresh ingredients.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          <img alt="Corvina on Oak, Fresh Fish" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-383" data-id="383" height="300" src="http://cookinglisbon.com/wp-content/uploads/corvina-fresh-fish-market-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
          <img alt="Peas and Eggs, Market Tour" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-384" data-id="384" height="300" src="http://cookinglisbon.com/wp-content/uploads/peas-market-tour-eggs-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          We choose Peas with Poached Eggs, since we bought the peas on Mercado da Ribeira.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         We still had time for the dessert, the famous sweet clouds made with milk, cinnamon, sugar and totally hands on mode for the people who mades our cooking classes or market tours.
        </p>
