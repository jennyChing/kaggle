       <h2 class="entry-title">
        Why should they get history wrong? The Authors Collection.
       </h2>
       <h3 class="entry-date">
        June 5, 2014
       </h3>
       <h3 class="entry-author">
        <a href="http://venturegalleries.com/author/davidstokes/" rel="author" title="Posts by David R. Stokes">
         David R. Stokes
        </a>
       </h3>
       <div class="full-content group">
        <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_51633" style="width: 576px">
         <a href="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Neville-Chamberlain-and-Winston-Church.jpg">
          <img alt="Winston Churchill, left, and Neville Chamberlain played major roles in two World Wars." class=" wp-image-51633 " height="448" src="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Neville-Chamberlain-and-Winston-Church.jpg" width="576"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
          Winston Churchill, left, and Neville Chamberlain played major roles in two World Wars.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         HAVING MISSED the broadcasts when they were presented in prime time last week, Ive finally gotten around to working through the multi-episode series, The World Wars, on the
         <i>
          History Channel
         </i>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Im about halfway through.
        </p>
        <p>
         As an avid student of 20
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         century geopolitical history, Im sort of torn as I watch the great story and stories unfold. First, I enjoy dramatic portrayals of great events, while recognizing that what is shown on the screenwhether big or smallwill inevitably include a measure of license. Yes, I am one of those viewers who tend to talk back to the narrator, injecting my own commentary.
        </p>
        <p>
         Karen simply loves it when I do that.
        </p>
        <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_51634" style="width: 275px">
         <a href="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/david-r-stokes_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="David R. Stokes" class="size-full wp-image-51634" height="197" src="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/david-r-stokes_opt.jpg" width="275"/>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
          David R. Stokes
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         But heres the thingI find myself annoyed when something is presented as true history yet gets basic facts glaringly wrong. Its one thing to give one side of something in dispute, but quite another to get basic chronology wrong. Ive seen several examples of this, but Ill give you two for now.
        </p>
        <p>
         First, in the introductory episode called Trial by Fire, the writers have Vladimir Lenin taking control in Russiaand pulling his nation out of the First World War (a move that helped Germany) via the Treaty of Brest Litovsk
         <i>
          before
         </i>
         Americas entrance into the war.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yikes!
        </p>
        <p>
         The facts are that America joined the conflict in April 1917. Lenin came to power later that year (October/November 1917, depending on the calendar you use). The treaty with Germany was then signed the next yearin March 1918.
        </p>
        <p>
         The second glaring thing I saw had to do with Winston Churchill and Neville Chamberlain. In the episode called, A Rising Threat, it shows Chamberlain bringing Churchill into his government (as First Lord of the Admiralty) long before the Nazis invaded Poland.
        </p>
        <p>
         Double yikes!!
        </p>
        <p>
         The actual timeline is well known. First, Hitler invaded Poland on September 1, 1939. Then, a few days later, Chamberlain announced that Britain was at war with Germany. It was
         <i>
          after
         </i>
         this that he summoned Churchill and gave him his job back (Winston had been First Lord during World War I).
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, these may seem small, but they arent. They show a carelessness that is simply unnecessary. I wonder what the historians who appear on the series must think. Im sure the insertion of their commentary was an editorial matter for the producer(s). But if I were Douglas Brinkley, or Paul Reid, or H. W. Brandsor any of their British counterparts, Id be a little ticked.
        </p>
        <p>
         I really cant figure out why the producer(s) allowed such basic glaring mistakes to be presented.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, the series otherwise is actually pretty good. I think it captures the personalities of the good and evil giantsthe men who made the history.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats valuable.
        </p>
        <p>
         I am enjoying the seriesmostly. But have to give it only two out of four stars for accuracy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its almost like I half expect to see Pearl Harbor happen on a
         <i>
          Saturday
         </i>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/David-R.-Stokes/e/B001KICQ5S/ref=sr_tc_2_0?qid=1401879350&amp;sr=1-2-ent">

         </a>
        </p>

         </strong>
