     <div itemprop="articlebody">
      <p>
       Several world organisations have raised their voices in alarm because of a new case of dolphin abusein Taiji, Japan.
      </p>
      <h2>
       International pressure against Taiji
      </h2>
      <p>
       Scubacrowd supports the petition placed by activist Jade Jones, to return the calf albino dolphin that was captured on the 17
       <sup>
        th
       </sup>
       of January with its mother, in the coast of Japan. Both animals were surrounded by nets and boats, cornered and led to the infamous Taiji cove, where they remained for more than 20 hours captive, together with over 250 dolphins. Whil fishermen manipulated the calf, the mother was jumping and calling desperately. Half an hour later, the mother disappeared to the bottom of the cove, and was not seen again.
      </p>
      <p>
       This practice is performed as part of the selection for the aquarium industry. Some dolphins are selected to entertain tourists, others are massacred for their meat, or returned to the ocean exhausted.
      </p>
      <p>
       Albino Dolphins are very rare, and hard to find, so they represent a big attraction in Theme Parks. That is why this baby dolphin was the first one to be separated from the pack. It was transported to the Whale Museum in Taiji where it is being exhibited in captivity.
      </p>
      <p>
       As we all know, dolphins are social animals that live in big packs, and they need not only their mothers, but their entire community to fully develop. In Scubacrowd we find this case abhorrent, as well as the general practice of removing a wild animal from its habitat for mass entertainment.
      </p>
      <h3>
       Sign the petition
      </h3>
      <p>
       You can also contribute to help save this baby dolphin. If you want to support the cause and help put international pressure against the Taiji Museum,
       <a href="https://www.change.org/es/peticiones/devuelvan-al-mar-la-cr%C3%ADa-de-delf%C3%ADn-albino?alert_id=BMEZpRrCCF_IrLmFsNyBN&amp;utm_campaign=46504&amp;utm_medium=email&amp;utm_source=action_alert" rel="nofollow">
        sign this petition
       </a>
