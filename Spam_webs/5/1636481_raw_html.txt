        Avoid these marketing no-no's at all costs
       </div>

        <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          In todays sophisticated and digitalized marketplace, people have more options and more opportunities to test and compare products and services before they make a decision. When you develop a marketing strategy, you need to take into account that your target market has more choices than ever. You have to adapt to a new style of marketing that is more authentic and more people-oriented.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <img alt="10 Old Marketing Techniques You Should Get Rid Of" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32914" height="450" src="http://www.wix.com/blog/file/2013/07/marketing-techniques-6.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Its time to stop using any of these outdated marketing tools in your modern-day business because they have long stopped bringing any results and could be potentially damaging to your brand. Check out these marketing no-nos:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            Stop fabricating
           </strong>
           . In the past, online consumers were way more gullible; you could easily funnel them to your landing pages by playing on their fears and creating a false need. Todays consumer can see right through half-truths in marketing messages and go directly to sites that offer real information, products or services.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <img alt="10 Old Marketing Techniques You Should Get Rid Of" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32910" height="450" src="http://www.wix.com/blog/file/2013/07/marketing-techniques-2.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            Avoid telemarketing like it was fire ants
           </strong>
           . You wont even get one bite and youll embarrass yourself. Chances are your call will initially be screened thanks to caller ID and many times will go unanswered. Nobody wants to be read from a script about a new and exciting exclusive offer available only through you.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <img alt="10 Old Marketing Techniques You Should Get Rid Of" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32911" height="450" src="http://www.wix.com/blog/file/2013/07/marketing-techniques-3.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            Say no to direct mailing
           </strong>
           (random mass snail mailing). Its completely ineffective unless you know everyone on your mailing list and their buying habits, which is highly unlikely. If you do send snail mail, make sure its in the form of a letter, not a postcard and be sure to offer something of value. Giving a free sample and a coupon still works, but not for every type of customer or business.
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            Stop sending out spam
           </strong>
           . Its crucial that your emailing list be streamlined to contain only members of your target audience and send out only specific, customized and segmented emails.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <img alt="10 Old Marketing Techniques You Should Get Rid Of" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32909" height="430" src="http://www.wix.com/blog/file/2013/07/marketing-techniques-.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            Dont go with intuition instead of relying on hard data and stats.
           </strong>
           Those who do not test and analyze are destined for failure. In order to produce results you need to focus on that which has proven to be successful, and you cant do that without controlled testing of promotions and thorough evaluations of your available stats.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <img alt="10 Old Marketing Techniques You Should Get Rid Of" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32912" height="450" src="http://www.wix.com/blog/file/2013/07/marketing-techniques-4.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           You must
           <strong>
            communicate with your audience,
           </strong>
           dont just show them adverts. If you focus too much on selling, you wont get very far.Open a dialogue with your audience and connect with them.
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            Never underestimate the customers need to be reach you
           </strong>
           . Many clients will not even consider your business seriously if you dont display at least two contact options on your website. By allowing them to get in touch easily you are creating reliability and loyalty.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <img alt="10 Old Marketing Techniques You Should Get Rid Of" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32913" height="450" src="http://www.wix.com/blog/file/2013/07/marketing-techniques-5.jpg" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            Dont try to be the first in the phone book
           </strong>
           , or even second. unless your target audience is 65+.
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           Writing
           <strong>
            crappy keyword-stuffed content
           </strong>
           instead of providing worthy and useful information. Long gone are the days when search engines couldnt tell good content from bad. Remaining in the top 10 search results requires top quality content with value being placed on the number of shares your articles have achieved as opposed to the number of backlinks as in the past.
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            No fake testimonials
           </strong>
           . It is essential to offer third party credibility. People need to know someone they trust recommends you as well.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
         </p>

         </p>
