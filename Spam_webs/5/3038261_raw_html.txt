        <section class="body article">
         <p>
          Duke will add a 6'7" stretch four to its roster next season, without having to spend a scholarship spot.
          <b>
           <a href="http://247sports.com/Player/Justin-Robinson-78029">
            Justin Robinson
           </a>
          </b>
          has the genes, the talent, and he even has the grades, to gain early admittance into Duke University as part of the Class of 2019 and will walk-on to the basketball team next year after getting an early acceptance notice on December 12th. If you're wondering about his genes, his dad is none other than two-time NBA champion David Robinson.
         </p>
         <p>
          "I'm happy to get early decision from Duke since it is so amazing academically," Justin said via text. "It's been a long time dream for me. The opportunity to be a preferred walk-on is exciting and I'm looking forward to being a part of the Duke Basketball tradition."
         </p>
         <p>
          The elder Robinson has been seen on Duke's campus multiple times over the last few years, speaking to Duke Basketball campers in 2012 when Justin was a camper.
          <a href="/tu/MTIxNDA1dGlueTI0Nw==" rel="nofollow">
           When "The Admiral" spoke, he noted how highly
          </a>
          he regards
          <b>
           <a href="http://247sports.com/Coach/Mike-Krzyzewski-1509">
            Mike Krzyzewski
           </a>
          </b>
          , who once coached Robinson on the 1992 Dream Team.
         </p>
         <p>
          Robinson called Coach K one of his heroes during his speech and shared kind words about the Duke program.  Coach K relentlessly pursues perfection every day, said Robinson.  Perfection is impossible but the result is excellence That is how Duke Basketball became great.
         </p>
