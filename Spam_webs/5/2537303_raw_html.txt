                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-kM-702-LsFA/U5xxqh0v8MI/AAAAAAAABEM/9xSqnmL5wnA/s1600/ultimate+road+trip.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="480" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-kM-702-LsFA/U5xxqh0v8MI/AAAAAAAABEM/9xSqnmL5wnA/s1600/ultimate+road+trip.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>

                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       This summer, many families will be hitting the road and driving to their summer vacation destination. Have a fun and relaxing road trip by including
                       <b>
                        The Ultimate (Disney) Road Trip Survival Kit
                       </b>
                       . Having one of these will ensure lots of entertainment and the miles will just fly by.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                     </div>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-STwurZTLsWk/U5xy5XEgcZI/AAAAAAAABEY/6Dmt_vxgBSc/s1600/207.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-STwurZTLsWk/U5xy5XEgcZI/AAAAAAAABEY/6Dmt_vxgBSc/s1600/207.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       For our family, road trips are a way of life. With both of our families out of state, our kids have been taking road trips since birth. As they have gotten older though, we have found that we need more in the car to keep them occupied.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-icerxVyiXEE/U5xzTZc_SEI/AAAAAAAABEs/EjBqijX_rLs/s1600/232.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-icerxVyiXEE/U5xzTZc_SEI/AAAAAAAABEs/EjBqijX_rLs/s1600/232.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       Last summer, as we were preparing for our road trip to Disney (from NY that's 20+ hours) with a 6 and 2 year old, I wanted to make something for the car that would keep them both occupied and entertained at the same time. This is how The Ultimate (Disney) Road Trip Survival Kit came to be. I use the word Disney in parenthesis because this kit can be used for any road trip.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <a href="https://images-blogger-opensocial.googleusercontent.com/gadgets/proxy?url=http%3A%2F%2Fecx.images-amazon.com%2Fimages%2FI%2F51yOkpCCp4L.jpg&amp;container=blogger&amp;gadget=a&amp;rewriteMime=image%2F*" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="265" src="http://ecx.images-amazon.com/images/I/51yOkpCCp4L.jpg" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       First start by purchasing a car storage bin. We bought this
                       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B005NMP6IG/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B005NMP6IG&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=homiswhethe09-20">
                        Diono Travel Pal Car Storage
                       </a>
                       <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=homiswhethe09-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B005NMP6IG" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                       from Amazon. What I like about this one is it has a lot of pockets and some zippered compartments for small things. It is also big enough to hold a wide variety of items.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       You'll also need ziploc bags, a binder and a package of sheet protectors to get you started. Also, we had a
                       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002VOS168/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B002VOS168&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=homiswhethe09-20">
                        Taby Tray
                       </a>
                       <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=homiswhethe09-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B002VOS168" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                       for our toddler son to do his activities. Our daughter used the binder to lean on, but you could also have a small lap desk in the car.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-I9TTCevx-5U/U5x0tuDLAKI/AAAAAAAABFQ/WTRHCR-_PfE/s1600/P1050590.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-I9TTCevx-5U/U5x0tuDLAKI/AAAAAAAABFQ/WTRHCR-_PfE/s1600/P1050590.JPG" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://ecx.images-amazon.com/images/I/51yOkpCCp4L.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       </span>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      Now it's time to fill up the bin. You want to choose different kind of items so the kids feel like they are not doing the same type of thing over and over again. Also, put some activities in here that will get the whole family in on the fun.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-PPnMlJHi_U0/U5xzz8mosGI/AAAAAAAABEw/awaV6ggh-xA/s1600/road+trip+1-5.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-PPnMlJHi_U0/U5xzz8mosGI/AAAAAAAABEw/awaV6ggh-xA/s1600/road+trip+1-5.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       1. Activity Binder
                      </b>
                      - Some careful planning on my part and searching Pinterest led to our family's activity binder. I printed out pages from various websites for my kids to do in the car. I then put each the pages in sheet protectors. What's great about that is that the kids could use dry erase markers and use the pages again and again. I included pages such as bingo, word searches, license plate game, I Spy, coloring pages, and Road Trip ABC
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      Here are some of the sites I visited:
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       <a href="http://www.playdoughtoplato.com/2013/04/02/road-trip-activity-pack-2/" target="_blank">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                         http://www.playdoughtoplato.com/2013/04/02/road-trip-activity-pack-2/
                        </span>
                       </a>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <a href="http://www.disboards.com/showthread.php?t=2191711&amp;page=2" target="_blank">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                         http://www.disboards.com/showthread.php?t=2191711&amp;page=2
                        </span>
                       </a>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <a href="http://www.crayola.co.uk/free-coloring-pages.aspx" target="_blank">
                        <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">

                      <li>

                        <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                         http://www.housingaforest.com/road-trip-bingo/
                        </span>
                       </a>
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ueBPzduXXcI/U5x0djcpUXI/AAAAAAAABFA/v5SwJt_XiBw/s1600/P1050593.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      I also printed some pages from
                      <a href="http://disneyjunior.com/make" target="_blank">
                       Disney Junior
                      </a>
                      such as mazes and coloring pages.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-K5egtx8oxzA/U5x0lI6G1-I/AAAAAAAABFI/D5PMp1wwjMY/s1600/P1050594.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-K5egtx8oxzA/U5x0lI6G1-I/AAAAAAAABFI/D5PMp1wwjMY/s1600/P1050594.JPG" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      I found some new pages on
                      <a href="http://3boysandadog.com/2014/06/fun-road-trip-ideas-for-kids/" target="_blank">
                       Three Boys and a Dog
                      </a>
                      for this year's binder.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       2. Crayons and coloring books
                      </b>
                      - Buy the triangle crayons so they won't roll. Put them into a small baggie (one for each kid) to save space.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       3. Dry Erase board and markers
                      </b>
                      - These can be found at the dollar store. The markers are also good when using the Activity Binder.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       4. Music CDs
                      </b>
                      - A fun road trip always has some great tunes. We included some family friendly CDs and a homemade Disney Road Trip Mix.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Qpj_Rr4UliU/U5xzQyuLuuI/AAAAAAAABEk/cgzMo2R8anc/s1600/IMG_1017.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Qpj_Rr4UliU/U5xzQyuLuuI/AAAAAAAABEk/cgzMo2R8anc/s1600/IMG_1017.JPG" width="240"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       5. DVDs
                      </b>
                      - We purchased a dual-screen DVD player to have in the car. The one we got is great because you can easily move it from one car to another, and it allows you to watch the same DVD on both screens, or to play separate DVDs in each. I took some of their favorite DVDs and put them into a CD holder to save space. Each has a headphone jack, so the kids could watch their movies and we could listen to music at the same time.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      * We designated after eating times for DVD watching , otherwise that's all that they would do.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-yRB7BXYbD80/U5xz7ql5PsI/AAAAAAAABE4/396GAlDKIvY/s1600/road+trip+6-10.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-yRB7BXYbD80/U5xz7ql5PsI/AAAAAAAABE4/396GAlDKIvY/s1600/road+trip+6-10.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       6. Electronics
                      </b>
                      - We bring along the Kindle Fire for the 7 year old and the Leapfrog Pad for the toddler. Both are allotted a set amount of time to play.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       7. Books
                      </b>
                      - Provided your child doesn't get car sick, books are a great way to pass the time. You could also pre-load some books on a Kindle to save room. You could also listen to an audio book as a family.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       8. Puzzles and Games
                      </b>
                      - Many of your kids favorite games come in travel sized. You can also include puzzles. A good tip would be to add a magnet to the back of each piece and have them assemble it on a small baking sheet.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      *My daughter came up with bringing the Headbanz game. Great idea!
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       9. Etch a Sketch or Magnadoodle
                      </b>
                      - These are fun for all ages. Both our kids have a small one in the kit.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       10. Stickers and Magnets
                      </b>
                      - Our kit has a reusable sticker pad. If you are bringing a small cookie sheet for puzzles you could also pack magnet sets.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LLttERyGTTg/U5x1TeLxoWI/AAAAAAAABFY/Mn9Nmf8peZI/s1600/P1050592.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LLttERyGTTg/U5x1TeLxoWI/AAAAAAAABFY/Mn9Nmf8peZI/s1600/P1050592.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      Make sure everything is within arms reach. Everything in this kit is adaptable depending on the ages of your kids. Use these suggestions as a guideline so you have all of your bases covered.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                      Our new and improved 2014 kit will be hitting the road very soon. We're hoping that everything we packed will keep our kids occupied as we make the 20 hour trip to Disney!
