            <p>
             The
             <a href="/mlb/team/_/name/chw/chicago-white-sox">
              Chicago White Sox
             </a>
             pulled off Part 2 of their pitching staff coup Tuesday, trading for starter
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/29166/jeff-samardzija">
              Jeff Samardzija
             </a>
             in a deal with the
             <a href="/mlb/team/_/name/oak/oakland-athletics">
              Oakland Athletics
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             The
             <a href="/chicago/mlb/story/_/id/12002705/chicago-white-sox-acquire-jeff-samardzija-david-robertson" target="_blank">
              move comes on the heels
             </a>
             of the White Sox signing former
             <a href="/mlb/team/_/name/nyy/new-york-yankees">
              New York Yankees
             </a>
             closer
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/29172/david-robertson">
              David Robertson
             </a>
             via free agency Monday.
            </p>
            <p>
             <aside class="inline editorial float-r" data-behavior="article_related">
              <h1>
               Editor's Picks
              </h1>
              <ul>
               <li>
                <h2>
                 <a class="" href="/chicago/mlb/story/_/id/12005339/jeff-samardzija-happy-back-home-ready-help-chicago-white-sox-win-now">
                  More from ESPN.com
                 </a>
                </h2>
                <p>
                 Jeff Samardzija is happy to be back home in Chicago and confident the White Sox can "win now," Jon Greenberg writes.
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <h2>
                 <a class="" href="/blog/chicago/white-sox/post/_/id/22493/samardzija-deal-much-more-than-a-homecoming">
                  More from ESPN.com
                 </a>
                </h2>
                <p>
                 Jeff Samardzija signing with the White Sox is much more than just a homecoming, Doug Padilla writes.
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <a class="img-link" href="/blog/keith-law/insider/post?id=3052">
                 <img src="http://a.espncdn.com/combiner/i?img=/photo/2014/0718/mlb_u_samardzija_d1_576x324.jpg&amp;w=130&amp;h=130&amp;scale=crop&amp;location=origin"/>
                </a>
                <h2>
                 <a class="insider" href="/blog/keith-law/insider/post?id=3052">
                  Law: White Sox fill two major holes on roster
                 </a>
                </h2>
                <p>
                 Keith Law puts the White Sox's acquisitions of Jeff Samardzija (via trade) and David Robertson (free agency) into perspective.
                </p>
               </li>
              </ul>
              <div class="button button-alt sm btn-related">
               2 Related
              </div>
             </aside>
            </p>
            <p>
             "Being a Chicago guy, that's one of the craziest things I've thought about," Samardzija said Tuesday after Oakland dealt him to Chicago's South Side. "I'm sitting here thinking, 'Now, do I really have to go get my cleats on and go play for the
             <a href="/nfl/team/_/name/chi/chicago-bears">
              Bears
             </a>
             ?' If I could skate, maybe the
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/nhl/team/_/name/chi/chicago-blackhawks">
              Hawks
             </a>
             . My jumper's garbage, so the
             <a href="/nba/team/_/name/chi/chicago-bulls">
              Bulls
             </a>
             are out of the question."
            </p>
            <p>
             Samardzija, the third major player dealt by the Athletics during an offseason roster purge, likely will be placed in the White Sox rotation behind staff ace
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/30948/chris-sale">
              Chris Sale
             </a>
             and in front of
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/32106/jose-quintana">
              Jose Quintana
             </a>
             . Chicago also received minor league pitcher
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/31770/michael-ynoa">
              Michael Ynoa
             </a>
             in the deal.
            </p>
            <p>
             "I think we're going to be the most competitive rotation in the league, and I mean that internally," Samardzija said. "The best starting rotations are made when there's three or four guys that want to be the best and they want to go out there and clinch that three-game series or win that Sunday sweep game."
            </p>
            <p>
             The White Sox sent infielder
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/32146/marcus-semien">
              Marcus Semien
             </a>
             , right-hander
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/33148/chris-bassitt">
              Chris Bassitt
             </a>
             , catcher
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/30600/josh-phegley">
              Josh Phegley
             </a>
             and first baseman
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/31933/rangel-ravelo">
              Rangel Ravelo
             </a>
             to the A's.
            </p>
            <p>
             "Jeff is one of the elite right-handed starting pitchers in baseball, and we believe his addition to our roster gives us the potential for a formidable starting rotation," White Sox general manager Rick Hahn said in a statement. "His competitiveness fits well in our clubhouse, on the mound and in our city."
            </p>
            <p>
             Samardzija moved into the
             <a href="/mlb/team/_/name/chc/chicago-cubs">
              Chicago Cubs
             </a>
             ' rotation in 2012 and has gone 26-42 as a starter with a 3.86 ERA. He went a combined 7-13 last season with a 2.99 ERA with the Cubs and A's. He was 5-6 in 16 starts in the American League, posting a 3.14 ERA.
            </p>
            <p>
             He is expected to make between $9 million and $10 million in 2015, his final year of arbitration eligibility. The former Notre Dame wide receiver will be a free agent after the 2015 season.
            </p>
            <p>
             Samardzija wasn't so sure he would want to miss free agency.
            </p>
            <p>
             "You're so close to it, you really want to experience it," he maintained. "But like I said before, when the situation's right, it's right. When the numbers are right, they're right."
            </p>
            <p>
             White Sox outfielder
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/32068/adam-eaton">
              Adam Eaton
             </a>
             took to Twitter to express his excitement over the team's acquisitions.
            </p>
            <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
             <p>
              How do you spell his name..? Samardzija...? Heard he's good.
              <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/ComeOnOver?src=hash">
               #ComeOnOver
              </a>
              <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/SouthSide?src=hash">
               #SouthSide
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
               Adam Eaton (@AdamSpankyEaton)
              <a href="https://twitter.com/AdamSpankyEaton/status/542159875414163456">
               December 9, 2014
              </a>
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js">
            </script>
            <p>
             The 23-year-old Ynoa went 4-2 with a 5.52 ERA and 64 strikeouts in 31 relief appearances for Class A Stockton in 2014.
            </p>
            <p>
             Semien appeared in 64 games with the White Sox last season, hitting .234 with 10 doubles, 6 home runs, 28 RBIs and 30 runs scored.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bassitt made his debut for the White Sox last season, going 1-1 with a 3.94 ERA and 21 strikeouts over six appearances.
            </p>
            <p>
             Since the end of the season, Oakland also has traded third baseman
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/29563/josh-donaldson">
              Josh Donaldson
             </a>
             to Toronto and first baseman/outfielder
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/28844/brandon-moss">
              Brandon Moss
             </a>
             to Cleveland.
            </p>
            <p>
             Robertson agreed to a four-year deal with the White Sox on Monday that is worth $46 million, a source confirmed to ESPN.com's Jerry Crasnick. The club's bullpen was among the worst in the majors last season, plagued by injuries and struggles.
            </p>
            <p>
             Robertson, who went 4-5 with 39 saves and a 3.08 ERA after taking over the closer role in New York last year from
             <a href="http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/3240/mariano-rivera">
              Mariano Rivera
             </a>
             , will be the new closer for the White Sox.
            </p>
