       <p>
        <a href="http://social-savvy-pets.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/facebook-googleplus-blog.jpg">
         <img alt="Should Facebook or Google Plus Replace Your Blog?" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-215" height="169" src="http://social-savvy-pets.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/facebook-googleplus-blog-300x169.jpg" title="Should Facebook or Google Plus Replace Your Blog?" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        The question of whether Facebook can replace building a blog seems to be a recurring theme. And with the addition of Google Plus to the social media scene, many are now wondering whether Google Plus could replace the blog.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Use Facebook or Google Plus in Place of a Blog?
       </h2>
       <p>
        In both cases, the simple answer is yes, either Facebook or Google Plus could replace your blog and allow you to keep in touch with and continue to build your network. However, the question is more complicated than whether it is possible.
       </p>
       <p>
        Perhaps a more applicable question to ask is whether Facebook or Google Plus should replace your blog.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Should Facebook or Google Plus Replace Your Blog?
       </h2>
       <p>
        In my opinion, no. I dont believe it is a good idea to replace your blog with either Facebook or Google Plus. Though I like Facebook and use it regularly, it works better as a means of amplifying my blog and does not serve as a replacement.
       </p>
       <p>
        The same is true of Google Plus. Though Google Plus is a newcomer to the scene, I believe it could potentially play a big part in promoting your business and amplifying your blog message. However, I do not like the idea of replacing my blog with Google Plus either.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Why Blog Instead of Using Facebook or Google Plus?
       </h2>
       <p>
        There is one very simple reason for building your own blog instead of relying solely on Facebook or Google Plus. Your blog is exactly that 
        <strong>
         your
        </strong>
        blog. Assuming that you own your own domain, which I strongly recommend, no one can take your blog away from you, change the terms of service or discontinue your service without reason. The same cannot be said of Facebook or Google Plus.
       </p>
       <p>
        Even though you retain administrative rights to your Facebook page and have the ability to manage both your Facebook account and your Google Plus account, the real estate still does not belong to you. No matter how you look at it, Facebook or Google owns the real estate that holds your Facebook or Google Plus account. That leaves you at their mercy.
       </p>
       <h2>
        The Changing Face of Social Media
       </h2>
       <p>
        The other reason that I prefer to continue building my blog rather than giving over all my efforts to Facebook or Google Plus is the fact that social media as a whole is still evolving. Do any of you remember My Space? It was a powerful social media force not that long ago but now is overshadowed by Facebook and Twitter.
       </p>
       <p>
        Google Plus is another case in point. Introduced just a few short weeks ago, its popularity is said to be growing faster than Facebook and Twitter at the current time. But still, the future of Google Plus is in question. Will it replace Facebook as the most important social media force in existence? Only time will tell.
       </p>
       <p>
        Regardless of whether Google Plus overcomes Facebook or whether Facebook retains its hold on the social media world, the blog will still continue. I, for one, intend to continue building my blog, using social media in whatever form it takes to promote and market my blog and my business.
       </p>
       <p>
        What about you? Do you think the blog will survive? Do you think Facebook will replace blogging? Or Google Plus? Please share your thoughts.
       </p>
