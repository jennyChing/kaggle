       <h2>
        <strong>
         Worthwhile Excursions Away from the Bustle of Tokyo
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Whilst downtown Tokyo offers a seemingly endless array of sightseeing possibilities, sadly not enough visitors get the chance to broaden their horizons and travel beyond the city limits. Included below are but a small selection of less travelled destinations on the fringes of Tokyo (all within 3 hours of Shinjuku Station) waiting to be explored. Most wont be found in your guide books but nonetheless make worthwhile day trips, providing an opportunity to check out some stunning rural landscapes.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         1. Hossawa Falls, Hinohara Village (Tokyo)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Hossawa-Falls-Hinohara-Village.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Hossawa Falls Hinohara Village" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7121" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Hossawa-Falls-Hinohara-Village.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        Many tourists get the impression that Tokyo is nothing more than a concrete jungle with little in the way of unspoiled ecosystems; thankfully places such as the
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/hossawa-falls/" title="Hossawa Falls">
         Hossawa Falls
        </a>
        prove the contrary. This four level 60 metre (195 foot) waterfall is even included in the One Hundred Waterfalls of Japan. The waterfall is located in the scenic Hinohara Village and is only a 10 minute walk from the bus stop. Weekends can sometimes get a little hectic so best advice is to shoot for an early bus to avoid the crowds. For those feeling a little more energetic a trip to the falls can be combined with a hike to nearby
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/hiking/mt-sengenrei/" title="Mt. Sengenrei">
         Mount Sengenrei
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        Take a Chuo Line train towards Ome and transfer at Haijima for Musashi-Itsukaichi Station. From the station take a 20 minute bus ride and get off at the
        <em>
         Hossawa no taki iriguchi
        </em>
        bus stop.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         2. Sayama Hills, Higashimurayama City (Tokyo / Saitama Prefecture)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tamako-Lake-overlooking-Sayama-Hills.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Tamako Lake overlooking Sayama Hills" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7224" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tamako-Lake-overlooking-Sayama-Hills.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        As the closest of the sightseeing locals, Sayama Hills located in the commuter town of Higashimurayama feels more like rural Japan than outer suburbia. Four smaller parks combine to make up the expansive 23 hectare woodlands including two reservoirs,
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/tamako-lake/" title="Tamako Lake">
         Tama Lake
        </a>
        and Sayama Lake which supply drinking water to the capital. While Sayama Park is a popular family picnic area there are plenty of secluded pockets ideal for nature walks, bird watching and mountain biking amongst the mixed species forests.
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        The Metropolitan Sayama Park is a 3 minute walk from the south exit of Seibu Yuenchi Station while transferring to the quaint Seibu Yamaguchi Line to Seibu Stadium-mae provides access to Sayama Lake.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         3. Nagatoro River, Nagatoro Village (Saitama Prefecture)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Nagatoro-River-Nagatoro.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Nagatoro River Nagatoro" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7130" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Nagatoro-River-Nagatoro.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        Situated in the picturesque Chichibu District of Saitama Prefecture the Nagatoro River, is synonymous for traditional longboats and whitewater rafting, whisking adventurer seekers alongside its precipitous cliffs. The township of
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/nagatoro-town/" title="Nagatoro Town">
         Nagatoro
        </a>
        has been designated a prefectural nature park which means there are plenty of family friendly activities including nature walks, visiting the Mt. Hodosan Shrine or taking a 5 minute cable car ride up its namesake mountain.
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        From Ikebukuro take a Seibu limited express train bound for Seibu Chichibu Station. Make a short walk to Ohanabatake Station and transfer to the Chichibu Main Line for Nagatoro Station.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         4. Showa Park, Tachikawa City (Tokyo)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Showa-Memorial-Park-Tachikawa.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Showa Memorial Park Tachikawa" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7113" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Showa-Memorial-Park-Tachikawa.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/showa-kinen-park-autumn-foliage/" title="Showa Kinen Park Autumn Foliage">
         Showa Memorial Park
        </a>
        is a 165 hectare National Government Park about a 40 minute train ride from Shinjuku Station. The park is great to visit year round with seasonal flowers adding some colour even through the winter months. Noteworthy is the crisscrossed network of cycling paths and bikes including tandems are available for hire. In addition the park includes several playgrounds including a Childrens Forest and rainbow pool, BBQ facilities and for couples looking for something quieter there is always pedal boating on the artificial lake.
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        From Shinjuku take a Chuo Line train bound for Ome and get off at Nishi Tachikawa Station which closest to the park. Entry fee adults 410 yen / children 80 yen.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         5. Tokyo Citizens Forest, Hinohara Village (Tokyo)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tokyo-Citizens-Forest-Hinohara-Village.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Tokyo Citizen's Forest, Hinohara Village" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7185" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tokyo-Citizens-Forest-Hinohara-Village.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        With all the stress that comes with living in Tokyo its nice to know there is a place set aside to revitalise ones health. The 197 hectare Tokyo Citizens Forest created in 1995 is a good example of a sustainably managed recreational area near Tokyo that caters for a cross-section of visitors. The popular 20 minute walk to the Otaki Waterfall has even been designated one of Japans growing number Forest Therapy Roads. With a little more time its possible to make a longer three and a half hour hike which dips over nearby
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/hiking/mt-mito/" title="Mt. Mito">
         Mount Mito
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        Take a Chuo Line train towards Ome and transfer at Haijima for Musashi-Itsukaichi Station. From the station its a 60 minute ride to Kazuma bus stop, from here a free shuttle bus runs up to the main car park.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         6. Hakuusan Torii Kannon, Naguri Village (Saitama Prefecture)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i2.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Hakuunsan-Torii-Naguri-Saitama-Prefecture.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Hakuunsan Torii Naguri Saitama Prefecture" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7122" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Hakuunsan-Torii-Naguri-Saitama-Prefecture.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        The temples and statues which make up the Hakuunsan White Cloud Mountain
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/hakuunsan-torii-kannon/" title="Hakuunsan Torii Kannon">
         Torii Kannon
        </a>
        in Naguri Village are the life work of sculptor Yataro Hiranuma who spent over 30 years amassing the collection. With no shortage of forests and landscaped gardens to admire the area remains a drawcard for tourists and hikers alike. From the car park the round trip to the main temple and back takes a leisurely 90 minutes. While the mountain is most popular during the autumn foliage season the diverse plant life means the opportunity to see flowers in bloom throughout the year.
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        Easiest way to visit is by car as public transport is limited. From Ikebukuro take a Seibu Ikebukuro Express train to Hanno Station. Transfer for a bus bound for Naguri shako or Yunosawa and get off at of
        <em>
         Renkeibashi Teiryuusyo
        </em>
        which is a 5 minute walk from the Torii Kannon. Entry fee adults 200 yen / children 100 yen.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         7. Nasu Highlands (Tochigi Prefecture)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Nasushiobara-Tochigi-Prefecture.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Nasushiobara Tochigi Prefecture" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7119" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Nasushiobara-Tochigi-Prefecture.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        As the most northerly region in Kanto, the Nasu highlands are a bit of a stretch for a day trip and probably best broken over two days. The area is famed for its hot spring resorts but its the fresh mountain air and cooler temperatures in summer that ensure a steady flow of visitors. Mount Nasudake is a popular hiking destination with a cable car ferrying day tippers within 45 minutes of the summit. Little less strenuous diversions include the
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/momijidani-suspension-bridge-shiobara-onsen/" title="Momijidani Suspension Bridge">
         Momijidani Suspension Bridge
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/sesshoseki-murder-stone-nasu-onsen/" title="Sesshoseki Murder Stone">
         Sesshoseki Murder Stone
        </a>
        and for families at the Nasu Animal Kingdom, children can hand feed the resident capybaras.
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        The Nasushiobara region can be reached by local or rapid trains along the JR Utsunomiya Line from Tokyos Ueno Station with a one way trip taking around three hours. Nasu Animal Kingdom entry fee adults 1,800 yen / children 1,000 yen (additional 700 yen all-you-can-eat BBQ).
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         8. Fujido Cave, Ueno Village (Gunma Prefecture)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i1.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Fujido-Cave-Gunma-Prefecture.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Fujido Cave Gunma Prefecture" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7112" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Fujido-Cave-Gunma-Prefecture.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        While not as well-known as Tokyos own
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/nippara-limestone-cave-spelunking-in-tokyo/" title="Nippara Limestone Cave: Spelunking in Tokyo">
         Nippara Cave
        </a>
        , the Fujido Limestone Cave in
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/ueno-village/" title="Ueno Village Gunma Prefecture">
         Uneo Village
        </a>
        , Gunma Prefecture remains Kantos largest. The cave stretches over two kilometres and maintains a constant 11 degrees throughout the year making it the perfect place to head to in summer. It may not provide the spelunking challenges of some larger caves, however the 40 minutes or so it takes to walk through includes some striking stalactite and stalagmite formations. Entry fee adults 600 yen / children 400 yen.
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        Easiest way to visit the caves is by car as public transport is limited. From Shinjuku take a JR Shonan-Shinjuku Line train (via Takasaki Line) to Shinmachi Station and transfer for a 2 hour bus ride to
        <em>
         Uenomura Yakuba
        </em>
        (Ueno Village town hall). From there, its just over an hours walk. Entry fee adults 600 yen / children 400 yen.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         9. Okutama Lake, Okutama Town (Tokyo)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i2.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Lake-Okutama-Tokyo.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Lake Okutama Tokyo" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7125" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Lake-Okutama-Tokyo.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        The small township of Okutama lies within Chichibu-Tama-Kai National Park on the western fringe of Tokyo. The area is a haven for nature starved Tokyoites with various outdoor activities including hiking, camping and fishing available. The nearby
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/sightseeing/lake-okutama-ogochi-dam-tokyo/" title="Lake Okutama (Ogochi Dam) Tokyo">
         Lake Okutama
        </a>
        supplies the vast amount of Tokyos drinking water and is only a 15 minute bus ride from Okutama Station. For those with more time can try walking the Okutama Mukashi-michi, a 10 km trail from Okutama Station to the Ogouchi Dam. From the dam wall nice views are afforded through the valley and at the
        <em>
         Mizu-to-Midori-no-Fureaikan
        </em>
        museum you can learn about the culture and history of the Okutama area. At the end of the day relax at the Batoukan hot spring which is located beside the floating pedestrian bridge.
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        Take a Chuo Line train for Ome and transfer for Okutama. At Okutama Station take a Nishi Tokyo bus bound for Lake Okutama. Batoukan hot spring 1,000 yen.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         <em>
          <em style="color: #0092B2">
           #
          </em>
         </em>
         10. Tokyo Peace Pagoda, Okutama Town (Tokyo)
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i1.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tokyo-Peace-Pagoda-Mount-Odera.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="Tokyo Peace Pagoda Mount Odera" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7184" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/ridgelineimages.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tokyo-Peace-Pagoda-Mount-Odera.jpg?resize=900%2C596"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        Tokyo has a Peace Pagoda? This is one sightseeing place youre definitely not going to find in your guide book. It comes as a surprise to many Japanese that Tokyo does in fact have its own peace pagoda on Mount Odera overlooking Okutama Lake. This Buddhist stupa opened in 1974 with much fanfare but unfortunately over the years fell into a state of disrepair. Thankfully all is not lost as restoration works are underway in earnest that will hopefully return the glistening white
        <a href="http://ridgelineimages.com/haikyo/tokyo-peace-pagoda/" title="Tokyo Peace Pagoda">
         Tokyo Peace Pagoda
        </a>
        back to its former self. Hiking up to the pagoda takes around an hour and provides some scenic views over the lake and valley below.
        <br/>
        <span style="display: none;">
         .
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="shortcode-highlight">
         Access:
        </span>
        <!--/.shortcode-highlight-->
        Access: Take a Chuo Line train for Ome and transfer for Okutama. At Okutama Station take a Nishi Tokyo bus bound for Miyamabashi which is near the trail head intersection.
       </p>
