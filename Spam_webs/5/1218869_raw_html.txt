     <p>
      <a href="http://www.anglicare-tas.org.au/" target="_blank">
       Anglicare
      </a>
      , a Tasmanian welfare agency, has
      <a href="http://www.themercury.com.au/article/2012/04/22/320931_most-popular-stories.html" target="_blank">
       submitted a proposal
      </a>
      to the Australian governments inquiry into problem gambling that wouldrequire pokies (the Aussie version of a slot machine)players to stand up while playing. We dont want to punish people, but there are things we can do to assist problem gamblers to get a break in play, said
      <strong>
       Chris Jones
      </strong>
      , the CEO of Anglicare. If they want a break, they can sit elsewhere, but they dont need to take a seat in front of a machine.
     </p>
     <p>
      Anglicare also suggested allowing more natural light into poker machine areas and bans on food and drink in all gaming areas. Freakonomics readers, what do you think? Will these nudges work?
