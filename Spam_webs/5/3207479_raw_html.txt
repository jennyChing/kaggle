         Summer Cake Recipe: Edd Kimbers Mixed Berry Traybake
        </h1>
        <aside class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-author-link">
          <a href="http://lilinhaangel.com/author/lilinhaangel/" rel="author" title="Posts by Lilinha Espindula">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         We love baking and enjoyingfreshly baked cakes with a cup of tea, I dont usually add fresh fruit to cakes, but we tried a new recipe and loved it.
        </p>
        <p>
         We recreated Edd Kimbers Mixed Berry Traybake and loved the combination of crunchy Jordans Super Berry Country Crisp with fresh seasonal berries, yummy!
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18662" style="width: 600px;">
         <a href="http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/059.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt=" Edd Kimbers Mixed Berry Traybake" class="size-large wp-image-18662" height="600" src="http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/059-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://lilinhaangel.com/2014/07/summer-cake-recipe-edd-kimbers-mixed-berry-traybake/&amp;media=http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/059-600x600.jpg&amp;description=Summer Cake Recipe: Edd Kimbers Mixed Berry Traybake')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Edd Kimbers Mixed Berry Traybake
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Jordans Country Crisp Super Berry are made with crunchy clusters, baked with British wholegrain oats and generously mixed with blackcurrants, cranberries and blueberries.
        </p>
        <p>
         They added a crunchy texture to the cake. I must confess Ive never added cereal or granola to cakes before, but it was a hit with the whole family. From now on, we will be adding granola and fresh fruit to our recipes!
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18670" style="width: 600px;">
         <a href="http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/099.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Mixed Berry Traybake" class="size-large wp-image-18670" height="600" src="http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/099-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://lilinhaangel.com/2014/07/summer-cake-recipe-edd-kimbers-mixed-berry-traybake/&amp;media=http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/099-600x600.jpg&amp;description=Summer Cake Recipe: Edd Kimbers Mixed Berry Traybake')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Mixed Berry Traybake
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         This month, Jordans Cereal is, very excitingly, unveiling its very first show garden at RHS Hampton Court Palace Flower Show, from 8
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         to 13
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         July.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          
         </strong>
         The Jordans Wildlife Garden has been created by award winning garden designer, Selina Botham, to reflect Jordans commitment to planting wild flowers and habitats for wildlife on British farms.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18663" style="width: 600px;">
         <a href="http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/060.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Mixed Berry Traybake with Jordans Super Berry Country Crisp" class="size-large wp-image-18663" height="600" src="http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/060-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://lilinhaangel.com/2014/07/summer-cake-recipe-edd-kimbers-mixed-berry-traybake/&amp;media=http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/060-600x600.jpg&amp;description=Summer Cake Recipe: Edd Kimbers Mixed Berry Traybake')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Mixed Berry Traybake with Jordans Super Berry Country Crisp
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         With a colourful variety of features from edible wild flowers, trees and hedges to oats, fruit and nuts  all of which can be foraged from the countryside  the garden provides a natural larder to share as a shelter for birds, bees and butterflies.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its unveiling celebrates the belief that great tasting food comes from working closely with nature and is set to inspire gardeners everywhere to support British wildlife.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_18665" style="width: 600px;">
         <a href="http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/065.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Mixed Berry Traybake" class="size-large wp-image-18665" height="600" src="http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/065-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://lilinhaangel.com/2014/07/summer-cake-recipe-edd-kimbers-mixed-berry-traybake/&amp;media=http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/065-600x600.jpg&amp;description=Summer Cake Recipe: Edd Kimbers Mixed Berry Traybake')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Mixed Berry Traybake
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         In celebration of the edible wild flowers, oats, fruits and nuts in their Wildlife Garden, Great British Bake Off Winner Edd Kimber has created a series of foraged food recipes inspired by the garden.
        </p>
        <p>
         The range of three exciting new recipes includes a Mixed Berry Traybake with Rosehip Syrup, a Cherry Plum Crumble with Cobnuts and an Alpine Strawberry Fool.
        </p>

         </div>
         <div class="blog-yumprint-recipe-contents">
          <div class="blog-yumprint-photo-middle" style="background-image: url(http://lilinhaangel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/059-600x600.jpg)">
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-info-box">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-infobox-section">
            <div class="blog-yumprint-infobox-section-title">
             Prep Time
            </div>
            <div class="duration blog-yumprint-infobox-section-data" datetime="PT15M" itemprop="prepTime">
             15 min
             <span class="value-title" title="PT15M">
             </span>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="blog-yumprint-infobox-section">
            <div class="blog-yumprint-infobox-section-title">
             Cook Time
            </div>
            <div class="duration blog-yumprint-infobox-section-data" datetime="PT40M" itemprop="cookTime">
             40 min
             <span class="value-title" title="PT40M">
             </span>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="0">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Ingredients
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             225g unsalted butter
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             225g caster sugar
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             1 tsp vanilla extract
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             4 large eggs
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             300g self-raising flour
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
             1/2 tsp baking powder
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
             4 tbsp milk
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="7">
             200g mixed berries (raspberries, blackberries,
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="8">
             strawberries)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="9">
             100g Jordans Super Berry Granola
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="1">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Optional ingredients
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             75g icing sugar
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             Juice of 1/2 lemon
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             2 tsp of Rosehip Syrup
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="2">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Instructions
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
             For the traybake, grease a deep 9x13 rectangular baking tin and line with a strip of parchment paper, leaving about a 2-inch overhang along the long sides. This will make removing the cake a lot easier later on. Preheat the oven to 180C, fan oven 160C, Gas Mark 4.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
             For the sponge mixture, place the butter and sugar into a large bowl and using an electric mixer beat until light and fluffy, for about five minutes. Mix in the vanilla extract and then one at a time beat in the eggs, beating each egg until fully combined before adding the next. Mix the flour and baking powder together and then in three additions add to the egg mixture, mixing until just combined. Add the milk and mix to combine.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
             Scrape the batter into the prepared tin and spread into an even layer. Scatter over the berries and Jordans Super Berry Granola and bake in the preheated oven for about 40 minutes until golden brown, or when a toothpick inserted into the middle of the sponge comes out clean. Allow the cake to cool in the tin before drizzling with the rosehip syrup.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">
             If you want to add a bit of decoration to the traybake you can mix 75g icing sugar with the juice of 1/2 lemon, until you have a smooth, just pourable mixture. You can also add a couple of teaspoons of rosehip syrup to this if you like. Drizzle over the sponge to give a lovely finish to the cake.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-note-section" yumprintsection="3">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Notes
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-notes">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="0">
              If you are unable to buy rosehips, rose syrup is readily available from good delis and specialist food shops
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="1">
              Blueberries make a lovely alternative to blackberries, so feel free to go with whatever berry you can get your hands on. We used raspeberries, blueberries and blackberries in our Berry Traybake.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
