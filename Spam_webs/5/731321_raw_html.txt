  <meta content="This drawing was the first thing I made after Ana and I decided to make over her living room together. She was at my Christmas party and had seen my laundry room make-over with the zig-zag wall and liked it.
She is also a fan of the white tongue and groove walls you see everywhere on Pinterest. They are pretty great!
It seemed like a great solution for her entertainment wall. If we went that route, we would be able to re-build the TV nook and make the cabinets under the TV taller so they would have room for all the AV equipment.
You can see in the before picture below, that the sub woofer was next to the TV before and the front center speaker was on the shelf above the TV. We wanted to clean up that look.
I found some new doors at a reasonable price here. Ana bought these handles for the doors.
On the drawing, I added a panel across the top of the cabinet doors where the speaker could sit behind a cut-out with speaker fabric.
I suggested a 1x6 frame around the whole entertainment center wall and then a 1x4 to flank each side of the TV nook. I did this to create some definition and at the same time we avoided any seams in the tongue and groove boards.
&nbsp;
As for the overall design, Ana asked me to design the space around the one piece of furniture she wanted to keep: Their coffee table. Isn't it awesome?
She had also already found a sofa that she loved and she sent me a picture of it. I incorporated this into a mood board which I sent her. Other than that she just told me, I want turquoise and orange. Go!
Sources (that we ended up not using):&nbsp; Grass cloth&nbsp; Sofa console&nbsp; Bicycle pillow (discontinued), Blue vintage pillow, Chunky throw,&nbsp; Conserve water art,
Sources for the rest that we did use are at the bottom of this post.
I had my handyman bid on the wall job and a couple of weeks later I stopped by and he had put up all of the left side of the tongue and groove as you can see here. He had wired for the new sconces and re-built the TV nook with the board going across for the speaker.
Another couple of weeks later the tongue and groove wall had been finished and Ana's painter had come by and sprayed all but the mantle top white, white, white. He had also painted the walls Agreeable Gray by Sherwin Williams and the ceiling had received a fresh coat of (ultra) white as well.&nbsp;
In the meantime I had emailed Ana a couple of sconce choices:
Sources:1&nbsp; 2&nbsp; 3&nbsp; 4&nbsp; 5
We both immediately liked number 1 best for the space. It's the Industrial Atom from Lamps Plus. I think it ties in the Spanish rustic feel of the house with the bit of cottage beachy twist of this room.
Next, we started looking at some mirror options.
I found several which we narrowed down to these three and I made a mock-up of each one for her to better decide:Mirror 1Mirror 2Mirror 3
Of course that needs to be a gif, right?
They are all great, but we both liked the sun star burst mirror the best for this space.
For behind the TV nook, I had originally suggested grass cloth, but we later decided to make a golden strie effect. Yes, this is me bringing back faux painting. You heard it here first.
First we painted a yellow color (I later re-did this in a much lighter yellow (Valspar's napkin yellow) because this was too dark), then on top of that we used a golden glaze paint that I dragged on with a wide brush, making a strie effect on the wall.
While that was drying we pulled out the artwork that she bought from the mood board and I found some more art around the house. That antique map of Uruguay her husband had found while living there, was quite the find for this space I think.
I laid it all up on the floor first.
I added symmetry to the gallery wall by putting the largest frame in the middle, at the bottom, then equally sized white frames in the outer corners. Then I added a bit more free-style to it by adding 3D objects like the letters and the scull. The circle elements are worth their weight in gold too, I think.
We hung the map first, then the two square white frames to get the width of the gallery.
Next we added the upper white frames along an imaginary bottom line, leaving space between the two rows.
Then filled in with the rest of the pieces.
Here you can see how the gallery wall is made up of two rows that are aligned at the bottom and there is a &quot;triangle&quot; that make the eye move around between the scull, the mirror and the &quot;c W&quot;.
A few days later the new shaker doors were installed and then I came by with bags of accessories. I styled it all up and took some pictures.
That's how we took Ana's living room from this:

             <br/>
             We both immediately liked number 1 best for the space. It's the
             <a class="PageLink" href="http://www.lampsplus.com/products/industrial-atom-11-inch-high-edison-bronze-wall-sconce__x8817.html" target="_blank">
              Industrial Atom
             </a>
             from Lamps Plus. I think it ties in the Spanish rustic feel of the house with the bit of cottage beachy twist of this room.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Next, we started looking at some mirror options.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             I found several which we narrowed down to these three and I made a mock-up of each one for her to better decide:
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-a2.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <a class="PageLink" href="http://www.pier1.com/Starburst-Elements-Mirror/2713591,default,pd.html?cgid=round-mirrors" target="_blank">
              Mirror 1
             </a>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-a3.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <a class="PageLink" href="http://www.homedecorators.com/P/Sole_Mirror/530/" target="_blank">
              Mirror 2
             </a>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-a4.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <a class="PageLink" href="http://www.pier1.com/Champagne-Burst-Mirror/2685486,default,pd.html?cgid=round-mirrors" target="_blank">
              Mirror 3
             </a>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Of course that needs to be a gif, right?
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Ana mirror selection" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Ana-mirror-selection.gif"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             They are all great, but we both liked the sun star burst mirror the best for this space.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             For behind the TV nook, I had originally suggested grass cloth, but we later decided to make a golden strie effect. Yes, this is me bringing back faux painting. You heard it here first.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             First we painted a yellow color (I later re-did this in a much lighter yellow (Valspar's napkin yellow) because this was too dark), then on top of that we used a
             <a class="PageLink" href="http://www.lowes.com/pd_99192-4-007.0099192.004_0__?productId=3087561" target="_blank">
              golden glaze paint
             </a>
             that I dragged on with a wide brush, making a strie effect on the wall.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-4.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             While that was drying we pulled out the artwork that she bought from the mood board and I found some more art around the house. That antique map of Uruguay her husband had found while living there, was quite the find for this space I think.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             I laid it all up on the floor first.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-5.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             I added symmetry to the gallery wall by putting the largest frame in the middle, at the bottom, then equally sized white frames in the outer corners. Then I added a bit more free-style to it by adding 3D objects like the letters and the scull. The circle elements are worth their weight in gold too, I think.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-6.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             We hung the map first, then the two square white frames to get the width of the gallery.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-7.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Next we added the upper white frames along an imaginary bottom line, leaving space between the two rows.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-8.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Then filled in with the rest of the pieces.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="#Behind the #design of Ana s #living #room" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Behind-the-design-of-Ana-s-living-room-9.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Here you can see how the gallery wall is made up of two rows that are aligned at the bottom and there is a "triangle" that make the eye move around between the scull, the mirror and the "c W".
             <br/>
             <br/>
             A few days later the new shaker doors were installed and then I came by with bags of accessories. I styled it all up and took some pictures.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             That's how we took Ana's living room from this:
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Ana #living #room #makeover #before #after" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Ana-living-room-before-after-1.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             to this:
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="Ana #living #room #makeover #before #after" src="/images/Blog2014/07July/Ana-living-room-before-after-5.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
