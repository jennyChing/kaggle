     <h1 class="post-title mob-marg-b-25 med-marg-b-50 big-marg-b-50" data-js="postTitle" itemprop="name" tabindex="0">
      The True Story of the Kids Who Beat MITs Best Robots, Coming Soon to Theaters
     </h1>
     <figure class="wp-caption landscape alignnone" data-js="" id="attachment_1664047">
      <a href="http://www.wired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/four.jpg">
       <img alt="Lorenzo Santillan, Luis Aranda (standing), Oscar Vazquez, and Crisitan Arcega. " class="size-660-single-full wp-image-1664047" data-pin-description="The True Story of the Kids Who Beat MITs Best Robots, Coming Soon to Theaters" height="495" src="http://www.wired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/four-660x495.jpg" width="660"/>
      </a>
      <figcaption class="wp-caption-text link-underline">
       Lorenzo Santillan, Luis Aranda (standing), Oscar Vazquez, and Crisitan Arcega.
       <img class="photo" data-pin-description="The True Story of the Kids Who Beat MITs Best Robots, Coming Soon to Theaters" src="https://www.wired.com/wp-content/themes/wired/assets/images/gallery-cam@2x.png"/>
       courtesy Luis Aranda
      </figcaption>
     </figure>
    </header>
    <section class="post-container clearfix relative" data-js="post" id="start-of-content" tabindex="0">
     <article class="content link-underline relative body-copy" data-js="content" itemprop="articleBody">
      <p>
       Ten years ago, contributing editor Joshua Davis received a press release that gave him pause. Clumsily formatted and full of typos, the email described an underwater robot competition, funded by NASA, in which four high school students in Phoenix, Arizonathree of them undocumented immigrants from Mexicobeat MIT to win gold. Davis picked up the phone to learn more.
      </p>
      <p>
       <figure class="wp-caption landscape alignleft fader" data-js="fader" id="attachment_1664053">
        <a href="http://www.wired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/wired-13.04.jpg">
         <img alt='"La Vida Robot" (issue 13.04)' class="size-315-single-full wp-image-1664053" height="257" src="http://www.wired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/wired-13.04-315x257.jpg" width="315"/>
        </a>
        <figcaption class="wp-caption-text link-underline">
         <a href="http://archive.wired.com/wired/archive/13.04/robot.html">
          La Vida Robot
         </a>
         (issue 13.04)
        </figcaption>
       </figure>
       That reportorial instinct turned into the WIRED story 
       <a href="http://archive.wired.com/wired/archive/13.04/robot.html">
        La Vida Robot
       </a>
        (issue 13.04), which mentioned that three of the young men didnt qualify for federal student loans because of their immigration status. WIRED readers eventually raised more than $90,000 in scholarships for Oscar Vazquez, Cristian Arcega, Luis Aranda, and Lorenzo Santillan.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now the story of those robot-builders has a new chapter.
       <em>
        Spare Parts
       </em>
       , starring George Lopez and Carlos PenaVega, opens in January, and Davis is publishing a
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Spare-Parts-Undocumented-Teenagers-American/dp/0374534985">
        book by the same title
       </a>
       updating the kids story. What happened to these guys fascinates me, Davis says. It changed their lives. How? Heres a preview.
      </p>
      <p>
       <figure class="wp-caption alignright fader" data-js="fader" id="attachment_1664049">
        <a href="http://www.wired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/spare-parts-cover.jpg">
         <img alt="Davis' new book, also called &lt;em&gt;&lt;figcaption class=" link-underline="" src="http://www.wired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/spare-parts-cover-315x472.jpg" wp-caption-text=""/>
        </a>
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Spare-Parts-Undocumented-Teenagers-American/dp/0374534985">
         Spare Parts
        </a>
        , updates the four men's story in the decade following their historic robotics competition win.
       </figure>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Lorenzo Santillan
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Santillan, whom Davis describes as the mechanical genius who also loved cooking, an out-of-the-box thinker, used the WIRED scholarship money to attend culinary school. Now he and Luis Aranda run a catering firm in Phoenix. Hes also a line cook at a local restaurant and moonlights as a mechanic.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Luis Aranda
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Aranda was a US citizen when the group won the contest; today he runs a catering company with Santillan and works as janitorial supervisor for the courts in Phoenix.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Oscar Vazquez
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Thanks to the scholarship funds provided by WIRED readers, Vazquez graduated from Arizona State Universitybut afterward he couldnt resolve his immigration status and returned to Mexico. Then Senator Dick Durban learned of Vazquezs plight and helped him get amnesty. Vazquez returned to the US, enlisted in the Army, and saw combat in Afghanistan. Today hes a mechanical foreman for BNSF railroad in Montana. The movie coming out is pretty surreal and exciting, he says. Im looking forward to seeing it.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Cristian Arcega
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       He was the genius kid, supersmart, Davis says. Arcega couldnt finish college, in part because of money; a ballot measure raised tuition for undocumented immigrants. For a time, Arcega worked at Home Depot and now helps with a neighbors business. Besides that, most of my time is spent working on product ideas, Arcega says.
      </p>
      <figure class="wp-caption landscape alignnone fader" data-js="fader" id="attachment_1664051">
       <a href="http://www.wired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/spare-parts-screen.jpg">
        <img alt="A still from the new movie, also called Spare Parts, based on Joshua Davis' 2005 WIRED story: David Del Rio as Arcega, Jos Julin as Santillan, Carlos PenaVega as Vazquez, and Oscar Gutierrez as Aranda. " class="size-660-single-full wp-image-1664051" height="355" src="http://www.wired.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/spare-parts-screen-660x355.jpg" width="660"/>
       </a>
       <figcaption class="wp-caption-text link-underline">
        A still from the new movie, also called
        <em>
         Spare Parts
        </em>
        , based on Joshua Davis 2005 WIRED story: David Del Rio as Arcega, Jos Julin as Santillan, Carlos PenaVega as Vazquez, and Oscar Gutierrez as Aranda.
       </figcaption>
      </figure>
