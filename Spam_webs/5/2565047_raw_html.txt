   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Stop-time
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
     Jump to:
     <a href="#mw-head">

     </a>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="hatnote">
      This article is about music.  For the memoir, see
      <a href="/wiki/Stop-Time" title="Stop-Time">
       Stop-Time
      </a>
      .
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stop-time_cadential_pattern.png">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="637" data-file-width="1673" height="84" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Stop-time_cadential_pattern.png/220px-Stop-time_cadential_pattern.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Stop-time_cadential_pattern.png/330px-Stop-time_cadential_pattern.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Stop-time_cadential_pattern.png/440px-Stop-time_cadential_pattern.png 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stop-time_cadential_pattern.png" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Stop-time cadential pattern.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berlin_1-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-Berlin-1">
          <span>
           [
          </span>
          1
          <span>
           ]
          </span>
         </a>
        </sup>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Tap_dance" title="Tap dance">
       tap dancing
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">
       jazz
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">
       blues
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       stop-time
      </b>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/Accompaniment" title="Accompaniment">
       accompaniment
      </a>
      pattern interrupting, or stopping, the
      <a href="/wiki/Meter_(music)" title="Meter (music)">
       normal time
      </a>
      and featuring regular
      <a href="/wiki/Accent_(music)" title="Accent (music)">
       accented
      </a>
      attacks on the first
      <a href="/wiki/Beat_(music)" title="Beat (music)">
       beat
      </a>
      of each or every other
      <a href="/wiki/Bar_(music)" title="Bar (music)">
       measure
      </a>
      alternating with
      <a href="/wiki/Rest_(music)" title="Rest (music)">
       silence
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Solo_(music)" title="Solo (music)">
       solos
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Stop-time appears infrequently in
      <a href="/wiki/Ragtime" title="Ragtime">
       ragtime
      </a>
      music.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The characteristics of stop-time are heavy accents, frequent rests, and a stereotyped
      <a href="/wiki/Cadence_(music)" title="Cadence (music)">
       cadential
      </a>
      pattern.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berlin_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Berlin-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <b>
       Stop-timing
      </b>
      may create the impression that the
      <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">
       tempo
      </a>
      has changed though it has not.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Stop-time is, according to Samuel A. Floyd Jr., "a musical device in which the forward flow of the music stops, or seems to stop, suspended in a
      <a href="/wiki/Rhythm" title="Rhythm">
       rhythmic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Unison" title="Unison">
       unison
      </a>
      , while in some cases an
      <a href="/wiki/Improvisation" title="Improvisation">
       improvising
      </a>
      instrumentalist or singer continues
      <a href="/wiki/Solo_(music)" title="Solo (music)">
       solo
      </a>
      with the forward flow of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metre_(music)" title="Metre (music)">
       meter
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">
       tempo
      </a>
      . Such stop-time moments are sometimes repeated, creating an illusion of starting and stopping, as, for example, in
      <a href="/wiki/Scott_Joplin" title="Scott Joplin">
       Scott Joplin
      </a>
      s '
      <a href="/wiki/The_Ragtime_Dance" title="The Ragtime Dance">
       The Ragtime Dance
      </a>
      ' and
      <a href="/wiki/Jelly_Roll_Morton" title="Jelly Roll Morton">
       Jelly Roll Morton
      </a>
      s '
      <a href="/wiki/King_Porter_Stomp" title="King Porter Stomp">
       King Porter Stomp
      </a>
      '."
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#What_information_to_include" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
         <span title="A more detailed citation is required. (May 2010)">
          full citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Joplin's "Stoptime Rag" (1910) employs stop-time throughout; it even lacks his characteristic four-bar introduction. Stop-time in Joplin's rags is characterized by directions in the music for performers to stomp their foot to the beat. Joplin's "Ragtime Dance" contains the direction,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      "Notice: to get the desired effect of 'stop time', that the pianist will please
      <i>
       stamp
      </i>
      the heel of one foot heavily upon the floor at the word 'stamp'. Do not raise the toe from the floor while stamping."
     </p>
     <p>
      Stop-time is common in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African-American_popular_music" title="African-American popular music">
       African-American popular music
      </a>
      including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">
       R&amp;B
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">
       soul music
      </a>
      , and led to the
      <a href="/wiki/Break_(music)" title="Break (music)">
       break
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">
       hip hop
      </a>
      .
     </p>
