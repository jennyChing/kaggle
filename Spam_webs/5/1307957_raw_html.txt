        <p>
         Having a full arsenal of quality freelance tools is essential to all online entrepreneurs. They can help us land clients, bill customers, network, monitor feedback, manage our time. The list goes on and on. While I love trying out the latest and greatest, there are a few tools that have stuck with me through the years.
        </p>
        <p>
         1.
         <a href="http://mailchimp.com/" target="_blank">
          MailChimp
         </a>
          You have got to build that list. Start collecting the emails of potential clients from Day One. MailChimp offers a free option for doing just that, and I have been using their service for years. Even if you dont have a free report to hand out yet, allow interested parties to sign up for updates, periodic discounts, etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         2.
         <a href="http://www.hootsuite.com" target="_blank">
          HootSuite
         </a>
          I consider my social network an extension of my list. It gives me another avenue to stay in contact with those that may not be ready to hand over their email address. I have tried out a few social media tools over the years, but HootSuite is the one that works best for me. You can monitor Facebook Pages and Profiles, Twitter, LinkedIn, etc. You can also schedule updates ahead of time.
        </p>
        <p>
         3.
         <a href="http://theworkathomewife.com/recommends/mozy" target="_blank">
          Mozy
         </a>
          This little freelance tool has saved my life. I experienced a huge computer crash last year that inevitably took my desktop back to Day One, straight from the factory settings. This could have had a big impact on my business if I hadnt been prepared. Luckily, I had Mozy. I went online, clicked my mouse, all of my files and contacts were restored with the click of a button. If your computer crashed this afternoon, what would you lose? How long would it take you to reproduce all of your documents? This is a no-brainer, and for many of us it is free.
        </p>
        <p>
         4.
         <a href="http://www.google.com/alerts" target="_blank">
          Google Alerts
         </a>
          This is a great way to monitor your brand. Set up an alert for your URL or business name and you will be notified by email or feed reader when Google comes across anything online. Use quotation marks around your business name to keep the clutter out.
        </p>
        <p>
         5.
         <a href="http://theworkathomewife.com/quickbooks" target="_blank">
          QuickBooks
         </a>
          It is imperative that you have a system to track your income and expenses. You also need to send professional-looking invoices to your clients. If you are a regular reader, you know I have tried a few different business accounting systems. I havent found a suitable replacement for QuickBooks, thus far. I started with QuickBooks Simple Start Edition. I recently upgraded to QuickBooks Online Essentials.
        </p>
        <p>
         6.
         <a href="http://www.paypal.com" target="_blank">
          PayPal
         </a>
          You need to get paid. I have seen a few payment portals come and go over the years, but PayPal has stuck around. Sign up for a Premier or Business account, not Personal. This will allow you to accept credit card payments, accept payments via your website, setup recurring payments for retainer clients and accept International payments.
        </p>
        <p>
         7.
         <a href="http://theworkathomewife.com/proposalkit" target="_blank">
          Proposal Kit
         </a>
          These freelance tools were just introduced to me, but I wish I had found them in my startup days. I spent a pretty penny on attaining proposal and contract templates. Proposal Kit has a package available for only $67. These are professional-looking proposals and contracts with industry-specific content. The $67 package includes 120 samples with over 1300 pages of content. Some are for product sales, some for services, some for technical projects, some for non-technical projects, some for grants, some for contracts, some non-profit related, and many for specific industries. You get ALL of these same samples regardless of which Proposal Pack you order. Amazing! I love it. And I see people asking for contracts and proposals every day.
        </p>
        <p>
         What are your favorite freelance tools?
        </p>
