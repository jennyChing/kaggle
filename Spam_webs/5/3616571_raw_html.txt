      <div class="intro">
       <h2 class="hide">
        Cheat Sheet
       </h2>
       <h1 itemprop="name">
        Bartending For Dummies
       </h1>
       <div class="product-info">
        From
        <span class="product-title">
         <a href="/store/product/Bartending-For-Dummies-5th-Edition.productCd-1118791266.html" title="Information about this product: Bartending For Dummies, 5th Edition">
          Bartending For Dummies, 5th Edition
         </a>
         <span class="product-authors">
          by Ray Foley
         </span>
        </span>
       </div>
       <p itemprop="description">
       </p>
       <p>
        Good bartenders pride themselves on being knowledgeable about cocktail recipes and proficient in the art of properly serving their guests and patrons. With a healthy repertoire of popular mixed drink recipes and the right ingredients at your disposal, you can be the life of any gathering.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="series-article_top">
      </div>
      <div class="series-article">
       <h2>
        Classic Cocktail Recipes That Are Great for All Occasions
       </h2>
       <div class="article-body p402_premium" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         The cocktail recipes listed here are classics for a reason: They taste good. Whether youre a professional bartender or just someone who likes to throw cocktail parties at home, you should know how to make these mixed drinks recipes like you know how to make toast.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Bacardi Cocktail
        </h2>
        <p>
         Bacardi cocktail is the only cocktail issued a copyright from the United States government.
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           2 oz. Bacardi Light Rum
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1 oz. Roses Lime Juice
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1/2 tsp Sugar
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1/2 oz. Rose Grenadine
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Mix in a shaker with ice and strain into a chilled martini glass.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Brandy Alexander
        </h2>
        <p>
         A sweet and creamy delight. A favorite to all until Baileys came around.
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1-1/2 oz. Brandy or Cognac
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1/2 oz. Dark Crme de Cacao
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1 oz. Sweet Cream or Ice Cream
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Shake all ingredients with ice. Strain into a Margarita glass.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Bloody Mary
        </h2>
        <p>
         This cocktail is probably the most famous of the Hair of the Dog morning-after concoctions.
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1-1/4 oz. Vodka
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           2-1/2 oz. Tomato Juice
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           dash Worcestershire Sauce
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           dash Tabasco Sauce
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           dash Salt and Pepper
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Pour Vodka over ice in a tall glass. Fill with Tomato Juice. Add a dash or two of Worcestershire Sauce and Tabasco Sauce. Stir and garnish with a Celery Stalk. For those who enjoy their Bloody Marys extremely spicy, add more Tabasco or even Horseradish.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Cosmopolitan
        </h2>
        <p>
         The most popular drink of the new millennium.
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           2 oz. Vodka
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1/2 oz. Cointreau
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           Juice of 1/2 Lime
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1/2 oz. Cranberry Juice
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Shake with ice and strain into a chilled martini glass.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Manhattan
        </h2>
        <p>
         If you make this cocktail correctly, youll never hear your guests utter this classic line from
         <i>
          The Simpsons:
         </i>
         What did I do to deserve this flat, flavorless Manhattan?
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           2 oz. American or Canadian Whisky
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           splash Sweet or Dry Vermouth
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           dash Angostura Bitters
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Stir ingredients in a rocks glass. Garnish with a Cherry.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Margarita
        </h2>
        <p>
         A great drink for a hot summer day.
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1 oz. Tequila
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1 oz. Cointreau or Triple Sec
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1 oz. Sweet &amp; Sour Mix or Lime Juice
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Shake ingredients with ice or blend ingredients with crushed ice. Serve in a salt-rimmed glass. Garnish with a Lime Wheel.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Martini
        </h2>
        <p>
         A simple and elegant classic.
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           2 oz. Gin
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           dash Extra Dry Vermouth
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Shake or stir Gin and Vermouth with ice. Strain and serve in a cocktail glass straight up or over ice. Garnish with a twist or an Olive.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Tequila Sunrise
        </h2>
        <p>
         You will have a warm summer glow after this one!
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           2 oz. Patrn Tequila
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           3 oz. Orange Juice
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1/4 oz. Grenadine
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Pour Grenadine into a tall glass first. Then add Tequila and fill with ice and Orange Juice. Garnish with an Orange Slice
        </p>
        <h2>
         White Russian
        </h2>
        <p>
         If youre worried about calories, you can use whole milk or half-and-half rather than cream, but cream tastes better. Skim and 2-percent milk produce unsatisfactory results. The Dude in
         <i>
          The Big Lebowski
         </i>
         settled for nondairy creamer powder in one scene; that option is for emergencies only.
        </p>
        <ul class="level-one">
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1-1/2 oz. Vodka
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1/2 oz. Kahla
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="first-para">
           1/2 oz. Cream
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Shake ingredients and serve over ice in a rocks glass.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>

       <h2>
        A Basic Bar Setup for a Limited Budget
       </h2>
       <div class="article-body p402_premium" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         Setting up a home bar can become quite costly. The variety of liquors available today is mind-boggling. How do you pare down your shopping list to the essentials? Some bartenders may scoff at the idea of not having both American and Canadian whiskies in a bar. And do you really need both dry and sweet vermouth? The options can be overwhelming. For simple entertaining, even if you're on a limited budget, start with the following items:
        </p>
        <table border="0">
         <tbody>
          <tr>
           <td width="250">
            One 750 ml bottle of gin
           </td>
           <td>
            One 750 ml bottle of whiskey (Bourbon, Canadian, and so on)
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <td width="250">
            One 750 ml bottle of rum
           </td>
           <td width="250">
            One 750 ml bottle of white domestic wine
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <td width="250">
            One 750 ml bottle of tequila
           </td>
           <td width="250">
            One 750 ml bottle of red domestic wine
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <td width="250">
            One 750 ml bottle of vodka
           </td>
           <td width="250">
            Twelve 12-oz. bottles of beer (domestic or imported)
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <td width="250">
            Three 750 ml bottles of the liqueurs and cordials of your choice (such as Irish cream, coffee liqueur, Grand Marnier, triple sec, Cointreau, Sambuca, white or green crme de menthe, Galliano, B&amp;B, Frangelico, amaretto, peach schnapps, and so on)
           </td>
           <td width="250">
           </td>
          </tr>
         </tbody>
        </table>
       </div>

       <h2>
        Necessary Supplies for Your Bar
       </h2>
       <div class="article-body p402_premium" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         Bartending, even at home, requires lots of supplies, mixers, and garnishes. In addition to beer, wine, liquors, and liqueurs, a decent home bar should have the following supplies if you plan on serving cocktails. You need one bottle of the following mixers for every five guests:
        </p>
        <table>
         <tr>
          <td>
           Cola or diet cola
          </td>
          <td>
           Lime juice
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr>
          <td>
           Cranberry juice

         You also need these fruits, garnishes, and seasonings:
        </p>

           Lemon twists
          </td>
          <td>
           Angostura bitters
