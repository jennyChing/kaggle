     <p>
      <b>
       Debra "Deb" Morgan
      </b>
      (spelled
      <b>
       Deborah
      </b>
      in the novels) is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">
       fictional character
      </a>
      created by
      <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Lindsay_(writer)" title="Jeff Lindsay (writer)">
       Jeff Lindsay
      </a>
      for his
      <i>
       Dexter
      </i>
      book series. She also appears in the
      <a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">
       television series
      </a>
      , based on Lindsay's books, portrayed by
      <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Carpenter" title="Jennifer Carpenter">
       Jennifer Carpenter
      </a>
      . In Lindsay's novels, she first appeared in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Darkly_Dreaming_Dexter" title="Darkly Dreaming Dexter">
        Darkly Dreaming Dexter
       </a>
       ,
      </i>
      and has been featured in every novel since.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Character_history">
       Character history
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Morgan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Character history">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Morgan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Debra was born to
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Dexter_characters#Doris_Morgan" title="List of Dexter characters">
       Doris
      </a>
      (Kathrin Lautner Middleton) and
      <a href="/wiki/Harry_Morgan_(Dexter)" title="Harry Morgan (Dexter)">
       Harry Morgan
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/James_Remar" title="James Remar">
       James Remar
      </a>
      ) on December 7, 1979.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She later became foster sister to
      <a href="/wiki/Dexter_Morgan" title="Dexter Morgan">
       Dexter Morgan
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_C._Hall" title="Michael C. Hall">
       Michael C. Hall
      </a>
      ). A tough, foul-mouthed tomboy, she craved her father's attention and envied Dexter for the amount of time Harry spent with him, unaware that Harry was training him to be a vigilante
      <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">
       serial killer
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      At the age of 12, her mother died of cancer. From then on, she decided to become a detective, like her father. Debra learned to shoot taking her father's gun. Dexter found out and told Harry, who punished her. Hurt, Debra told Dexter that she wished that Harry had never brought him home. She was immediately remorseful for saying it, however, and apologized.
     </p>
     <p>
      She was distraught at her father's death and, inspired by his career, joined the Miami-Metro Police Department. She spent three years in patrol and then another two years in vice before being promoted by
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Dexter_characters#Tom_Matthews" title="List of Dexter characters">
       Captain Tom Matthews
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Pierson" title="Geoff Pierson">
       Geoff Pierson
      </a>
      ) (a friend of her father), becoming a Homicide officer at the start of the first season.
     </p>
     <p>
      Debra is characterized as smart and capable, yet unsure of herself, and so she relies upon Dexter's seemingly limitless expertise on murderers to solve difficult cases. Initially assigned to Vice, she is desperate to be transferred to Homicide. After Matthews promotes her, she starts to grow self-confident, relying less on Dexter's abilities and more on her own.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Season_one">
       Season one
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Morgan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Season one">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Newly promoted to the homicide department, Debra's first case is a series of murders committed by "The Ice Truck Killer", who preys on prostitutes and leaves their dismembered bodies in refrigerated trucks. She works hard on the case, but her progress is hampered by her own insecurity and her antagonistic relationship with Lieutenant
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maria_LaGuerta" title="Maria LaGuerta">
       Maria LaGuerta
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Lauren_V%C3%A9lez" title="Lauren Vlez">
       Lauren Vlez
      </a>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      She starts dating, and eventually falls in love with,
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Dexter_characters#Brian_Moser" title="List of Dexter characters">
       Rudy Cooper
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Christian_Camargo" title="Christian Camargo">
       Christian Camargo
      </a>
      ); unbeknownst to her, he is actually the Ice Truck Killer, and is only dating her in order to get close to Dexter. At the end of the season, Rudy proposes to her and she accepts. Rudy  who is actually Brian Moser, Dexter's biological brother  then kidnaps her to reveal himself to Dexter. He binds her to a table in the same manner that Dexter kills his victims, while Dexter and Brian discuss her fate. Dexter ultimately saves her life, and reluctantly kills Brian to keep her safe.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-born_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-born-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>

      Vogel tells Debra that she had no other choice but to protect Dexter, and shows her recorded interviews with Harry expressing his guilt over training Dexter to kill; in these interviews, Debra learns the truth about her father's death. She reaches out to Dexter, asking him to take a drive with her so they can talk. Once on the road, Debra seizes the steering wheel and plunges the car into the bay, intent on killing herself and her brother. She is rescued, however, and saves Dexter's life by swimming to the car and pulling him out.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Debra goes to a resentful Dexter for help when Vogel is kidnapped. While the two of them track Vogel down, Debra tells Dexter that she had saved his life because she couldn't imagine hers without him in it. They find Vogel at the mercy of one of her former patients, serial killer A.J. Yates (
      <a href="/wiki/Aaron_McCusker" title="Aaron McCusker">
       Aaron McCusker
      </a>
      ); Dexter kills him to protect Debra. Dexter and Debra admit they need each other, and they reconcile.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Debra and Dexter are both non-lethally poisoned by Hannah, who has escaped from prison.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Debra demands that Dexter get her out of their lives, and starts surveilling her. When Dexter appears reluctant, Debra goes to her boss Jacob Elway (
      <a href="/wiki/Sean_Patrick_Flanery" title="Sean Patrick Flanery">
       Sean Patrick Flanery
      </a>
      ) and offers to apprehend Hannah for a substantial reward. She tries to capture Hannah, who says she did not kill her because she didn't want to hurt Dexter. Debra reluctantly drops the case, and even allows Hannah to hide from the police in her apartment.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Debra stops by her old precinct, and Batista offers to reinstate her as a detective. She wants the job, but she knows that she cannot do it while harboring a fugitive and turning a blind eye to Dexter's crimes. To further complicate the situation, she and Quinn, who is now in a relationship with Batista's sister Jamie (
      <a href="/wiki/Aimee_Garcia" title="Aimee Garcia">
       Aimee Garcia
      </a>
      ), share a kiss.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Dexter tells her that he, Hannah and Harrison are going to move to
      <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
       Argentina
      </a>
      . Debra is saddened, but gives him her blessing when she sees how happy he is with his new family. The following day, she takes the job at Miami Metro, and renews her relationship with Quinn.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In her first case, Debra helps Dexter catch Oliver Saxon (Darri Ingolfson), a serial killer known as "the Brain Surgeon". Dexter ultimately decides not to kill him, however, and asks her to arrest Saxon. Debra arrives just as Saxon is killing Deputy U.S. Marshall Cooper (
      <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Johnson" title="Kenny Johnson">
       Kenny Johnson
      </a>
      ), and draws her weapon. Saxon gets the drop on her, however, and shoots her in the abdomen.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the series' final episode, "
      <a href="/wiki/Remember_the_Monsters%3F" title="Remember the Monsters?">
       Remember the Monsters?
      </a>
      ", Debra is taken to the hospital, where she tells Quinn she loves him and wishes Dexter a happy life. Moments later, however, she suffers a massive stroke that leaves her in a
      <a href="/wiki/Persistent_vegetative_state" title="Persistent vegetative state">
       persistent vegetative state
      </a>
      . After killing Saxon, Dexter goes to see her in the hospital as it is being evacuated in the midst of a hurricane. He tearfully removes her life support, carries her body to his boat, and buries her at sea.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Romantic_relationships">
       Romantic relationships
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Morgan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Romantic relationships">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Debra is known to have very bad relationships with men. Her first boyfriend on the series is a mechanic named Sean who turns out to be married.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
       <a href="#cite_note-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She then has a relationship with Rudy Cooper, who ends up kidnapping her and trying to kill her twice.
     </p>
     <p>
      In Season two, she starts going out with a man named Gabriel whom she met at the gym, and whom she suspects (incorrectly) of trying to use her notoriety following the Ice Truck Killer case to jumpstart his writing career.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
       <a href="#cite_note-37">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        37
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      After that ends, she goes out with Frank Lundy, a man 25 years her senior who has to leave Miami at the end of season two, although she resumes her relationship with him in season four. She then starts a relationship with her Confidential Informant Anton Briggs. She breaks up with him to resume her relationship with Lundy, who is murdered. In the season five premiere she sleeps with her partner,
      <a href="/wiki/Joey_Quinn" title="Joey Quinn">
       Joey Quinn
      </a>
      , who tries unsuccessfully to move the relationship into something more than purely sexual. By the end of season 6, she comes to believe that she is in love with her adoptive brother, Dexter  only to find out that he is a serial killer. In season 7, she has feelings for true crime writer Sal Price, but he is murdered as well.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_from_the_novels">
       Differences from the novels
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Morgan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Differences from the novels">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the books, the character's name is spelled "Deborah", and she is described as "voluptuous," in contrast to her slim appearance on the TV show. In the novel
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Darkly_Dreaming_Dexter" title="Darkly Dreaming Dexter">
        Darkly Dreaming Dexter
       </a>
      </i>
      , Deborah finds out that Dexter is a
      <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">
       serial killer
      </a>
      , and appears to accept it, although she is sometimes torn between her love for her brother and her duty as a cop. In the TV series, Debra remains unaware of Dexter's "hobby" until the final episode of season six, when she witnesses Dexter killing Travis Marshall.
     </p>
     <p>
      At the conclusion of
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dexter_is_Delicious" title="Dexter is Delicious">
        Dexter is Delicious
       </a>
       ,
      </i>
      Deborah is pregnant by her boyfriend, Kyle Chutsky, and in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Double_Dexter" title="Double Dexter">
        Double Dexter
       </a>
      </i>
      we learn that she gave birth to a son, Nicholas, and raises him alone.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Debra_Morgan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Jennifer Carpenter's portrayal of Debra Morgan has impressed some critics, with Australian journalist
      <a href="/wiki/Jack_Marx" title="Jack Marx">
       Jack Marx
      </a>
      describing her portrayal of "cool and clumsy" Debra as "so perfect that many viewers appear to have mistaken the character's flaws for the actor's."
     </p>
     <p>
      For her portrayal of the character, she has been nominated for seven
      <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award" title="Saturn Award">
       Saturn Awards
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_on_Television" title="Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress on Television">
       Best Supporting Actress on Television
      </a>
      , in 2007, 2008, 2010, 2011, 2012, and 2013, winning in 2009 for her performance in season three. She has also been nominated four times as part of the ensemble cast for the
      <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Outstanding_Performance_by_an_Ensemble_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series">
       Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series
      </a>
      in 2009, 2010, 2011, and 2012.
     </p>
     <p>
      In a review of
      <i>
       Dexter
      </i>
      , Internet critic
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremy_Jahns&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeremy Jahns (page does not exist)">
       Jeremy Jahns
      </a>
      said that she "outshined everyone in the entire show", adding more positive review of the character and the portrayal.
     </p>
