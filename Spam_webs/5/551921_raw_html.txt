         INTERVIEW: A Sit Down With Richard Hatch, Star Of LOADOUT GOING IN HOT
        </span>
       </h1>
       <div class="summary">
        <img class="picture" id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_pictureIMG" src="http://www.comicbookmovie.com/images/articles/112447.jpg" style="border-width:0px;"/>
        <h2>
         <span id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_summaryLBL">
          CBM was given the chance to interview
          <i>
           Battlestar Galactica
          </i>
          actor Richard Hatch, regarding his new short film project by Wayside Creations for debut on Machinima Prime; adapting the popular online F2P game--Loadout. Hit the jump for more!
         </span>
        </h2>
        <div class="authorSocial">
         Follow E.F. "Manny":
         <div class="links">
          <a href="/profiles/?u=efcamachopmp">
           <img alt="View CBM Profile" src="/images/social/CBMBTNS.png"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.facebook.com/emanuelfcamacho" id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_authorFacebookLNK" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Follow on Facebook" src="/images/social/facebookBTNS.png"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.twitter.com/emanuelfcamacho" id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_authorTwitterLNK" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Follow on Twitter" src="/images/social/twitterBTNS.png"/>
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="author">
         By
         <span id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_authorLBL">
          E.F. "Manny" Camacho
         </span>
         -
         <span class="date" id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_dateLBL">
          12/16/2014
         </span>
        </div>
       </div>
       <img class="banner" id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_bannerIMG" src="http://www.comicbookmovie.com/images/articles/banners/112447.jpg" style="border-width:0px;"/>
       <div class="body">
        <span id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_contentLBL">
         <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; line-height: 1.428571em; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'Droid Sans', sans-serif; font-size: 14px; color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           CBM had a great opportunity to interview Richard Hatch, who many of you will know from his role as Captain Apollo in the original
           <em>
            <strong>
             Battlestar Galactica
            </strong>
           </em>
           series who would later play another pivotal character by the name of Tom Zarek in the rebooted series. Hatch is no stranger to film and televisionhaving a career that spans over 40 years. For most of the readers here, that 40 years condenses down to his Sci-Fi and Horror work. In this interview I get to talk to him about a short film he's starring in (releasing December 17th) that is a Wayside Creations adaptation for Machinima Prime of the popular online free-to-play FPS
           <em>
            <strong>
             Loadout
            </strong>
           </em>
           . titled
           <em>
            <strong>
             Loadout: Going in Hot
            </strong>
           </em>
           .
           <br/>
           <br/>
           I got to sit down with Richard and talk about everything from his time with both BSG series, to his role as Gaz in this short film. I ran out of time because of how deeply focused we were in the conversation (as that time melted away) unrealized. However, Richard generously offered to answer some of my additional questions offline.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           I thoroughly enjoyed talking to Richard about
           <strong>
            <em>
             Loadout: Going in Hot
            </em>
           </strong>
           ,
           <strong>
            <em>
             Battlestar Galactica
            </em>
           </strong>
           <em>
            ,
           </em>
           <strong>
            <em>
            </em>
           </strong>
           and
           <strong>
            <em>
             Star Trek: Axanar
            </em>
           </strong>
           I hope you enjoy the interview.
           <br/>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <br/>
           When we first connected into the conference call we exchanged pleasantries and he let me know he was recovering from a minor flu. He was hopeful to kick it completely before leaving for Biloxi, MS for the weekend; on his way to an event called Geek-O-Nomicon. I'm familiar with the event and a specific world record they are trying to breakMost Steampunk Cosplays gathered in one location. I of coursed asked if he would be participating.
           <br/>
          </div>
          <blockquote>
           <div style="text-align: justify;">
            <strong>
             Richard Hatch [chuckles]
            </strong>
            : "I'm actually in a steampunk show called: Cowboys and Engines with Malcolm Mcdowel. We filmed it last year, It's actually going to debut at the landmark theater out on Pico in Los Angeles on January 14th. And their going to screen some of the presentation here [Geek-O-Nomicon]."
           </div>
          </blockquote>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            CBM Manny
           </strong>
           : "I love your work, I've been a fan since I was a kid watching the original Battlestar Galactica."
           <br/>
          </div>
          <blockquote>
           <div style="text-align: justify;">
            <strong>
             RH
            </strong>
            :
            <em>
             "Oh Thank you!"
            </em>
           </div>
          </blockquote>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            CBM Manny
           </strong>
           : "Funny enough I was exposed to Battlestar before Star Wars and I loved it so much it started my fixation on science fiction at age 4."
           <br/>
          </div>
          <blockquote>
           <div style="text-align: justify;">
            <strong>
             RH
            </strong>
            :
            <em>
             "That's great! Did you like Star Wars too?"
            </em>
           </div>
          </blockquote>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            CBM Manny
           </strong>
           : "I did (still do), but I was a weird kid, started reading Frank Herbert and Kenneth Johnson material before anything else because of BSG."
           <br/>
          </div>
          <blockquote>
           <div style="text-align: justify;">
            <strong>
             RH
            </strong>
            :
            <em>
             "I appreciate that. I love that. It's so great! You were assimilated early!"
            </em>
           </div>
          </blockquote>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            CBM Manny
           </strong>
           : "Actually as early as a few months old in a stroller at Comic-Con!" [Laughs]
           <br/>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; line-height: 1.428571em; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'Droid Sans', sans-serif; font-size: 14px; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-align: justify;">
         </div>
         <blockquote>
          <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; line-height: 1.428571em; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'Droid Sans', sans-serif; font-size: 14px; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            RH [Laughs]
           </strong>
           :
           <em>
            "My God...wow! Then I'm with the right guy, in the right place! I'm in good hands."
           </em>
          </div>
         </blockquote>
         <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; line-height: 1.428571em; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'Droid Sans', sans-serif; font-size: 14px; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-align: justify;">
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           CBM Manny
          </strong>
          : "Indeed! We got you covered here at CBM. Now?
          <span style="line-height: 1.428571em;">
           I never had a chance to meet you, even though we've been to some conventions together, but we were both working and never got a chance to cross paths. But
          </span>
          
          <span style="line-height: 1.428571em;">
           I did get a chance to do some light project and production work for the SyFy network when BSG was in production and I got to see Edward James Olmos (a lot) a few times while in the production offices. Having worked with him closely yourself and being one of the stars of the original series
          </span>
          c
          <span style="line-height: 1.428571em;">
           an you tell me about some of your experience on both BSG's and your take on how Apollo was then vs recently and your take on Tom Zarek?
          </span>
          <br/>
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; line-height: 1.428571em; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'Droid Sans', sans-serif; font-size: 14px; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-align: center;">
          <span style="line-height: 1.428571em;">
           <img alt="" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7526/15411859173_3bfc55af79_z.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 339px;"/>
          </span>
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; line-height: 1.428571em; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'Droid Sans', sans-serif; font-size: 14px; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-align: justify;">
         </div>
         <blockquote>
          <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; line-height: 1.428571em; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'Droid Sans', sans-serif; font-size: 14px; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-align: justify;">
           <strong>
            RH
           </strong>
           :
           <em>
            "Well, all I can say is (you know) the original battlestar was the biggest most expensive production in TV historyat the time. and, they were trying to do a theatrical style series on the level [...] (you know) the scope of Star Wars. Only it was a very different story. In factI think the battlestar story is a phenomenal story. I love this epic journey, surviving a holocaust and trying to survive space and find a new homeland. It's a very archetypal and iconic type of story. Having somebody like Loren Green [Adama] play the (you know) Moses and the israelites, leading us to a new land. (I thought) was really extraordinary and to have grown up with Loren Green on Bonanza and to have him play my father, was...was probably a dream come true. He was such an extraordinarily down to earth, generous human being. He was the father all of us wished we had or would love to have. Like I said, that was an amazing experience for me and we worked 18 hour days, seven days a week.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            It was a year and a half filming that first season. It was a pretty dynamic, pretty intense year and a half of filming. We bonded as a crew, as a group of actors, and it was one of those kinds of extended families we [actors] rarely get to be a part of...because we tend to come and go(you know)from show to show. We rarely stay in one place long enough. It was (you know) going on the set the first day. Seeing film crews from all over the world. I had no idea how big this show was.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            We were on the cover of every major magazine. Time. Newsweek. It was extraordinary. I have to say: I was kind of overwhelmded or I thought what the hell have I got myself into. I was honestly embarrassed to have my name first in the credits over Loren Green. Even though my agent had negotiated for the moon and somehow got it. I felt like apologizing to everybody, because I was humbled (to tell you the truth).
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Because:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            1) I was starring in this great epic.
            <br/>
            2) I have a great respect for those people that put their time in and had the success Green had.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Anyway, it didn't matter. He was gracious about it, he had no problem with any of it, and we all got along famously.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            It was just...just a really, really, really great shoot. And I have to say: not every production runs smoothly. Not every production has a number of people that don't rank or ego gets in the way...
           </em>
