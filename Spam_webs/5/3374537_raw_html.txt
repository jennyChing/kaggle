        <h1>
         The real-life Limitless pill? Drug helps adults learn as fast as children by making the brain more 'elastic'
        </h1>

         <li>

        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          It may sound like something from 2011 thriller Limitless, but researchers have discovered a pill that helps adults learn new skills as quickly as children.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          A professor at Harvard rewired the brain of a visually impaired women to process images by giving her Alzheimers drug donepezil.
          <br/>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          The pill boosts chemicals in the brain, such as serotonin and acetylcholine, which are both found in high concentrations in the brains of young children.
         </font>
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="artSplitter">
         <img alt="In 2011 thriller Limitless, Bradley Cooper, pictured centre, takes a pill that opens up closed regions of his brain.          <p class="imageCaption">
          In 2011 thriller Limitless, Bradley Cooper, pictured centre, takes a pill that opens up closed regions of his brain. This boost his intelligence, motor skills and ability to learn new languages
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          These chemicals naturally reduce with age.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          Children under seven develop new skills rapidly because their brains go through whats called critical periods of development.
         </font>
        </p>
