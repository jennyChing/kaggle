                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     HP fanless Chromebox has a fan
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6548556480449048877">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ZAXTeR2TQPs/U7ThJCnVjZI/AAAAAAAAH6Y/nMgEu_Sb4yg/s1600/HP.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="160" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ZAXTeR2TQPs/U7ThJCnVjZI/AAAAAAAAH6Y/nMgEu_Sb4yg/s1600/HP.png" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Fanless is the new
                     <i>
                      it
                     </i>
                     word. HP's Chromebox
                     <a href="http://www8.hp.com/us/en/ads/chromebox/overview.html" target="_blank">
                      <b>
                       is being marketed
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     as fanless when it's really not. Yep, a blatant lie.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     We didn't cover it previously since, obviously, a 17W CPU can't be cooled passively in such a small chassis, but Lon Seidman
                     <a href="http://youtu.be/6_bP45QpW-U" target="_blank">
                      <b>
                       just played with it
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     and confirms the fan's noise.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Update: Picture from HP's own
                     <a href="http://www.hp.com/hpinfo/globalcitizenship/environment/productdata/Countries/_MultiCountry/disassembly_deskto_2014516234521185.pdf" target="_blank">
                      <b>
                       disassembly guide
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     (PDF).
