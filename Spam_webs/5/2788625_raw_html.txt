        <p>
         For years I have tried to describe small sponge cakes as Fairy Cakes rather then Cupcakes, I am beginning to realise though that there can be a difference. I do love a Fairy cake, that tiny morsel of cakey perfection. In this case however, the cakes really are cupcakes. They certainly are cup sized, as I used muffin liners in a muffin tin.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="very simple cupcakes" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-193" height="290" src="http://judemagee.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/P1150086-343x290.jpg" width="343"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I was making the cakes to accompany the Victoria Sandwich for the Cake Stall at Ham Fair. Once again I used a simple Vanilla Victoria Sandwich recipe, but this time divided it between 18 cake cases. My argument for making such large cakes is that they could remain more moist and therefore fresher. As with my Classic Victoria Sandwich I had to consider icing, to just sprinkle icing sugar would have ensured that the cakes looked very dull and unappetising. They need a little zing, so I decided on buttercream, but not too much.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I was pleased with the cakes, they had a certain uniformity and looked neat and regimented on their gold cake tray. I do hope they didnt have to sit in the sunshine for too long!
        </p>

           <span itemprop="author">
            Jude Magee

           <span itemprop="recipeCuisine">

          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            18
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8oz/225g softened unsalted butter
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8oz/225g caster sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8oz/225g self raising flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp baking powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 large eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Muffin Cake Cases
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Muffin Tin (for 18 cakes)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Buttercream
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4oz/120g butter
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8oz/225g icing sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1tsp vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1tbsp water
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cupcakes
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Put the softened butter into a bowl and mix to ensure it is really soft
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the caster sugar and beat into the butter for a few minutes
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Beat the eggs separately and then add gradually to the cake mixture
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Sift the flour with the baking powder and fold into the cake mixture
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Divide the mixture evenly between the cake cases (I found a dessert spoon worked well for this size cake)
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake in a pre-heated oven (180) for about 15-20 minutes
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Allow the cakes to cool completely before icing them
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Buttercream
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Beat the butter in a clean bowl (this is easier with a stand mixer or electric whisk!)
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Gradually add the icing sugar (it is worth covering the bowl with a clean tea towel, if you use a stand mixer, this prevents icing sugar flying everywhere)
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Use the water to slacken the icing slightly
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            fill a piping bag and pipe little florets onto the cupcakes
           </li>
          </ol>
