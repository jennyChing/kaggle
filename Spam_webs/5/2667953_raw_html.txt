     <h1>
      Baked Tortilla Chips
     </h1>
     <p>
      Still working on the ice cream. I keep forgetting and use the ingredients I bought for other things.  Its not easy having prego brains.
     </p>
     <p>
      After making those little tortilla strips for the
      <a href="http://perrysplate.blogspot.com/2008/07/vegetarian-tortilla-soup.html">
       Vegetarian Tortilla Soup
      </a>
      , I liked them so much I made them into full-sized corn chips. Id made some spinach dip, didnt have any bread on hand and was extremely low on tortilla chips. So, I whipped some of these up. Honestly, I think I like them better than store-bought fried tortilla chips. Theyre sturdier and have a distinct flavor that I like. Theyre not fried and theyre much cheaper than buying them, which is even better.
     </p>
     <p>
      I baked mine until they were a medium brown color. They didnt taste like they were over done, and baking them that long ensured they were crisp enough and didnt have chewy parts.
     </p>
     <div style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://bp2.blogger.com/_JHaAQ4Kxyf4/SHzMWUC3BLI/AAAAAAAACsw/ehNeiS2s32A/s1600-h/08.07.14+004.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5223274351552824498" src="http://bp2.blogger.com/_JHaAQ4Kxyf4/SHzMWUC3BLI/AAAAAAAACsw/ehNeiS2s32A/s400/08.07.14+004.jpg" style="cursor: pointer;"/>
      </a>
      <div style="text-align: left;">
       <span style="font-weight: bold;">
        Baked Tortilla Chips
       </span>
       <p>
        small corn tortillas
        <br/>
        olive oil
        <br/>
        salt (coarse Kosher if possible)
       </p>
       <p>
        Preheat oven to 400 degrees. Using a pizza cutter, slice tortillas into quarters. Arrange them in a single layer on a greased cookie sheet (try not to overlap them too much). Drizzle oil over the top and sprinkle with salt. Bake for 10-12 minutes or so, or until theyre as dark as you want them.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size:85%;">
         Nats Notes:
         <br/>
         1. If you wish to flavor them, sprinkle a little cumin, chili powder, garlic or anything else youd like over the top along with the salt. Cinnamon and sugar would be delicious if you wanted them to be sweet!
        </span>
       </p>
