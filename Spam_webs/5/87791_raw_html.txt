          Buy it where you burn it
         </span>
        </p>
        <!--				      &lt;div&gt;Be aware that firewood can harbor insects and diseases that threaten California's valuable forest resources. Transporting firewood can move these pests to new locations. &lt;/div&gt;&lt;br /&gt;-->
        <div>
         Be aware that firewood can harbor insects and diseases that threaten California's valuable forest resources. Transporting firewood can move these pests to new locations.
        </div>
        <br/>
        <div>
         <strong>
          WHAT CAN I DO?
         </strong>
        </div>
        <br/>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Leave firewood at home
          </strong>
          - do not transport it to campgrounds or parks
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Use firewood from local sources
          </strong>
          - that means the wood was cut within 50 miles of where you'll have your fire
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Bring only what you'll need, and burn responsibly
          </strong>
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           <a href="faq.html">
            What is the problem with moving firewood?
           </a>
          </strong>
          <em>
           Questions and Answers
          </em>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <!--&lt;a href="#" class="butmore alignright"&gt;Read More&lt;/a&gt;-->
       </div>
       <img class="imgslide" src="images/logos/CA-FTF-Final-12-302w.png" title="California Firewood Task Force Logo -- Buy It Where You Burn It"/>
      </div>
      <div class="ui-tabs-panel" id="panel2">
       <div class="contentslide">
        <p>
         <span class="titlefront-tab">
          Protect Trees - Don't Move Firewood
         </span>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Buy firewood where you'll burn it.
         </li>
         <li>
          Buy only what you'll need and burn responsibly.
         </li>
         <li>
          Tell your friends not to move firewood.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <div>
         When you move firewood, you can accidentally move insects and diseases that kill trees.
        </div>
        <br/>
        <div>
         Many communities around the USA and Canada have seen this happen in their own backyards - what starts as a few dead trees can quickly turn into a whole neighborhood or forest riddled with dying trees.
        </div>

       California Firewood Task Force
      </span>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <a href="http://firewoodscout.org/about/" target="_blank">
       <img alt="Visit the Firewood Scout Pilot Program Website" class="imgframe alignright" height="146" src="images/FirewoodScoutProgram.png" title="Visit the Firewood Scout Pilot Program Website" width="325"/>
      </a>
      <p>
       California is participating in the kickoff of the national 
       <a href="http://firewoodscout.org/about/" target="_blank">
        Firewood Scout
       </a>
        pilot program this summer in an effort to assist consumers in buying firewood local to where they will be burning it. The online Firewood Scout program will be especially useful in its mobile form, as on-the-go travelers will be able to use their smartphones to identify where local firewood distributors are in a given area. The website will also offer information on invasive species of concern in California as well as a set of questions consumers can ask to help assure the wood being purchased is low-risk for insects and diseases. Movement of firewood long distances can help spread harmful invasive species. Firewood Scout will help keep wood local and keep residents and visitors of California Buying It Where They Burn It.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you are a firewood vendor who sells to the public and you are interested in participating in the Firewood Scout program, please contact California Firewood Task Force Vice Chair Katie Harrell at
       <a href="mailto:kpalmieri@berkeley.edu">
        kpalmieri@berkeley.edu
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <span class="titlefront">
       California's Trees
      </span>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <a href="links.html#C">
       <img alt="Campground Resource Links (Photo by Ed Williams)" class="imgframe alignleft" height="186" src="images/CampgroundLinks-SoupSpringsSunset-EdWilliams-web.jpg" title="Campground Resource Links (Photo by Ed Williams)" width="326"/>
      </a>
      <p>
       California's trees are at risk from insects and diseases that can move on firewood. In San Diego County alone, tens of thousands of oak trees have been killed due to the Goldspotted Oak Borer, an insect researchers believe was initially brought to California on infested firewood. Other dangerous tree pests, such as the Emerald Ash Borer, have been found on firewood at California's borders.
      </p>

       <span class="titlefront">
        The problem with moving firewood
       </span>
       <img alt="Buy It Where You Burn It" class="imgframe alignright" src="images/CDFA_choppingfirewood_l64w.jpg"/>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Why is moving firewood such a bad idea?
        </strong>
        Tree-killing insects and diseases can lurk in firewood. These insects and diseases can't move far on their own, but when people move firewood they can jump hundreds of miles. New infestations destroy our forests, property values, and cost huge sums of money to control.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>

          Sacramento
