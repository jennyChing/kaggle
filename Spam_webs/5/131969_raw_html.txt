            <div class="entrytitle" style="background-color:transparent;">
             <h1 style="background-color:transparent;font-size:26px;line-height:35px;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;">
              <a href="http://kopiaste.org/2011/02/croissants/" rel="bookmark" style="background-color:transparent;color:rgb(0, 0, 0);" title="Link to Croissants">
               Croissants
              </a>
             </h1>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="entrybody" ezoic="wrapper" style="-webkit-box-sizing: content-box; background-color: transparent; background-origin: padding-box; border-spacing: 0px 0px; clear: none; font-family: Verdana, Georgia, 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; font-weight: normal; line-height: 17px; list-style-image: none; list-style-position: outside; list-style-type: disc; overflow: auto; overflow-x: auto; overflow-y: auto; text-indent: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; visibility: visible; word-wrap: normal;                height: auto; background: transparent; position: static; top: auto; right: auto; left: auto; bottom: auto; padding-bottom: 6px;">
            <div class="pf-content" style="background-color:transparent;">
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <a href="http://kopiaste.org/2011/02/croissants/homemade-croissants/" rel="attachment wp-att-14362" style="background-color:transparent;border-bottom-color:rgb(51, 51, 51);border-bottom-style:none;border-bottom-width:0px;color:rgb(127, 154, 66);padding-bottom:1px;">
               <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-14362" data-orig-height="450" data-orig-width="600" src="http://cdn-0.kopiaste.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/Homemade-croissants-600x450.jpg" style="background-color:transparent;width:580px;height:450px;" title="Homemade croissants"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              I think that you will agree with me that there isnt any one who will say that they do not love croissants, especially if they are home made.
             </p>
             <span class="ezoic-ad banner-1" style="display:inline-block;width:468px;min-height:80px;float:none;margin:2px 0px !important;">
              <span class="ezoic-ad" id="div-gpt-ad-kopiaste_org-banner-1-0" style="width:468px;position:relative;z-index:501;display:block;">
               <script data-cfasync="false" style="display:none;" type="text/javascript">
                eval(ez_write_tag([[468,60],'kopiaste_org-banner-1']));
               </script>
              </span>
             </span>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              I used to think that it was impossible to make them but I challeged myself and after a couple of attempts and watching a few tutorials on
              <!-- INFOLINKS_OFF -->
              <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RxCE963K-FY" style="minimum_paddings:Array;">
               You-Tube
              </a>
              <!-- INFOLINKS_ON -->
              I finally succeeded to make some.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Before going to the recipe let me share with you some information I read about these flaky, buttery bread. I am sure that most of you think that croissants are French pastry however there are several different stories. During the middle ages in Austria in 1683 they used to make crescent shaped bread as a worship to the moon.  It is believed that croissants were made for the first time in Vienna after the defeat of the Turks who tried to siege the city. In order to celebrate, the bakers shaped the kipferl bread into crescent shape, same as the symbol on the turkish flag.  According to others, Marie Antoinette, who was Austrian, introduced them to the French in 1686 and finally according to others an Austrian artillery officer named August Zang, founded a Viennese Bakery, in Paris, around 1838  9, where the croissant was born.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <span class="ezoic-ad medrectangle-1" style="display:inline-block;width:300px;min-height:270px;float:left;margin:2px 28px 4px 1px !important;">
               <span class="ezoic-ad" id="div-gpt-ad-kopiaste_org-medrectangle-1-0" style="width:300px;position:relative;z-index:501;display:block;">
                <script data-cfasync="false" style="display:none;" type="text/javascript">
                 eval(ez_write_tag([[300,250],'kopiaste_org-medrectangle-1']));
                </script>
               </span>
              </span>
              Whichever the truth is, we all love these delicious bread, especially the children.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Croissant pastry is similar to puff pastry with the only difference that yeast is added to the dough.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <!-- INFOLINKS_OFF -->
              <a href="http://kopiaste.org/2009/07/mpiftekia-hamburgers-with-vlita-and-garlicky-cucumber-salad/6168-revision-5/" rel="attachment wp-att-6380" style="minimum_paddings:Array; line-height:17px; ">
              </a>
              <!-- INFOLINKS_ON -->
              <a href="http://kopiaste.org/2011/02/croissants/croissants-baked/" rel="attachment wp-att-14364" style="background-color:transparent;border-bottom-color:rgb(51, 51, 51);border-bottom-style:none;border-bottom-width:0px;color:rgb(127, 154, 66);padding-bottom:1px;">
               <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14364" data-orig-height="461" data-orig-width="615" src="http://cdn-0.kopiaste.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/Croissants-baked.jpg" style="background-color:transparent;width:580px;height:461px;" title="Croissants baked"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Making croissants is not a very easy task and it is time consuming, considering that it is a two day procedure but it is worth the effort.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              You can leave them plain or add some filling if you like. I filled mine with couverture chocolate, some with nutella, but my favourite were with homemade apple jam.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <!-- INFOLINKS_OFF -->
              <a href="http://kopiaste.org/2009/07/mpiftekia-hamburgers-with-vlita-and-garlicky-cucumber-salad/6168-revision-6/" rel="attachment wp-att-6381" style="minimum_paddings:Array; line-height:17px; ">
              </a>
              <!-- INFOLINKS_ON -->
              <a href="http://kopiaste.org/2011/02/croissants/collage-croissants-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-14365" style="background-color:transparent;border-bottom-color:rgb(51, 51, 51);border-bottom-style:none;border-bottom-width:0px;color:rgb(127, 154, 66);padding-bottom:1px;">
               <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14365" data-orig-height="862" data-orig-width="765" src="http://cdn-2.kopiaste.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/Collage-croissants-2.jpg" style="background-color:transparent;width:580px;height:862px;" title="Collage croissants 2"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Croissants
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Preparation time, first stage:
              </strong>
              1 hour
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Rising time
              </strong>
              : 1 hour
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Preparation time, second stage
              </strong>
              : 1 hour
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Cooking time
              </strong>
              : 12  15 minutes
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Makes
              </strong>
              : about 30 croissants (depending on size)
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Ingredients:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               For the dough
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              1 kilo all purpose flour (divided)
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              1/4 cup olive oil
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              42 grams fresh yeast (2 packets dry yeast 16 grams)
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
               cup demerera sugar (divided)
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
               tsp salt
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              2 cups lukewarm milk + 1/8 cup milk or water
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              250 grams cold butter
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              some extra flour, to flour your working surface
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Egg wash
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              1 egg yolk, mixed with 2 tbsp milk
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Filling (optional)
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Couverture chocolate, jam, nutella
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Directions:
              </strong>
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Stage 1:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              In a bowl add the fresh yeast, 1 tbsp sugar, salt and 2 tablespoons flour and dissolve the yeast with 1/8 cup lukewarm water or milk. Mix to combine and cover the bowl with cling film and a kitchen towel and set aside to bubble and double in size.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Add the remaining flour in the mixer bowl, add the remaining sugar, olive oil, yeast mixture and using the dough hook mix, adding the milk gradually until the dough does not stick to the bowl.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Cover with cling film and a towel and set aside until it doubles in volume.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Place the butter between two sheets of cling film and with the rolling pin, pound it to flatten it as much as you can and then roll it as thin as you can. Place it on a tray and refrigerate.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Divide the dough in two parts. (I only used half of it so you may make half quantity of dough or use double amount of butter).
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Flour your working surface or use a silicone mat. Roll out the dough into a rectangular which should be bigger than the butter. Remove the first piece of cling film from the butter and invert it on the dough, leaving about an inch on each side. Fold the sides and then fold the dough into three plies.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Wrap the dough with cling film and refrigerate until the next day.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              <strong style="background-color:transparent;font-weight:bold;">
               Stage 2:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              To make the croissants, place the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface. Cover it again with cling film and pound it lightly horizontally and vertically until it becomes double in size and then work with your rolling pin to make the dough as thin as you can (about  cm).
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Using a sharp knife of a pizza wheel, cut stripes of 10 cm (4 inches) width and from each stripe cut triangles, every 7.5 cm (3 inches).
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Place each triangle between cling film and using the rolling pin, make each piece even thinner.  On the wide side add any filling you like and roll.  Take the two edges and curve them towards the centre to form a crescent.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Preheat oven to 190
              <sup style="background-color:transparent;font-size:11px;line-height:14px;">
               o
              </sup>
              C /375
              <sup style="background-color:transparent;font-size:11px;line-height:14px;">
               o
              </sup>
              F.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Place on a baking tin lined with parchment paper and allow for 30 minutes to rise.
             </p>
             <p style="background-color:transparent;margin:0px 0px 0px 0px;padding:0px 0px 10px 0px;">
              Brush with the egg wash and bake for 12 -15 minutes or until golden brown. Keep an eye on them in the oven as these can brown quickly.
             </p>
