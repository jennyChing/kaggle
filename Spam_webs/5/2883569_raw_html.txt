            <p>
             <a href="http://blog.becauseiamagirl.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Malala-book1.jpg">
              <img alt="Malala Yousafzai sits in her garden reading a book" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-12386" height="300" src="http://blog.becauseiamagirl.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Malala-book1-272x300.jpg" width="272"/>
             </a>
             On October 9th 2012, Malala Yousafzai was shot by a member of the Taliban while she was travelling to school in Pakistan. She was targeted because of her committed campaigning for the right of
             <a href="http://blog.becauseiamagirl.ca/from-head-to-toe-the-anatomy-of-girls-education/">
              all girls to get an education
             </a>
             . The world watched in amazement as she recovered from the malicious attack.
            </p>
            <p>
             July 12th, 2013 was Malala Yousafzais 16th birthday. Most girls have a party with some cake. But not Malala. Shes led 600 of the brightest youth from around the world at the FIRST EVER youth takeover of the United Nations General Assembly.
            </p>
            <p>
             The occasion was marked by Malala giving her FIRST public address since the attack. Her speech was bold, passionate and inspiring. This quote in particular gave us chills: Malala Day is not my day; today is the day of every woman, every boy and every girl who have raised their voice for their rights.
            </p>

            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              History in the making
             </b>
            </p>
            <p>
             With Malala at the helm, current world leaders stepped aside and allowed the leaders of tomorrow the platform to deliver a youth-led resolution at the UN to help all children around the world access their right to an education.
            </p>
            <p>
             Canada was represented by two youth delegates at the historic UN event. Chrystal, 18, and Fahmeeda, 16, attended the youth takeover with Malala. Both girls are members of
             <a href="http://becauseiamagirl.ca/page.aspx?pid=3845">
              Plan Canadas Girls Speaker Bureau
             </a>
             , and dedicated supporters of Plans
             <b>
              Because I am a Girl
             </b>
             initiative.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              The Malala effect
             </b>
            </p>
            <p>
             The UN Youth Takeover also kick-started Plans countdown to the 2nd annual
             <a href="http://becauseiamagirl.ca/page.aspx?pid=4430">
              International Day of the Girl
             </a>
             on October 11. The lead-up to the event will help engage Canadians to support girls right to an education.
            </p>
            <p>
             Will Malalas speech ignite a new beginning for education, shining the spotlight on the need for all children  especially girls  to get an education? We certainly hope so.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              What can you do to support Malala and girls around the world?

               Join Malala and Raise Your Hand NOW!
              </a>
             </b>
