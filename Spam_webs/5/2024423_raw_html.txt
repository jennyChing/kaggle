               China to Take the Reins in Funding Regional Infrastructure Projects
              </font>
             </b>
            </font>

             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               By Frank Holmes
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               This Tuesday marked the last day that countries could submit their applications to become founding members of the new China-led Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB). As of this writing, a little over 40 nations have either already been approved or have applied for membership, including strong U.S. allies such as Britain, Germany and Australia.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               Notable absentees, as you can see below, are the U.S. and Japan.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">

             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               <img align="baseline" alt="" border="0" hspace="0" src="http://67.19.64.18/news/2015/4-1fh/image001.png"/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               Conceived to serve as an alternative to Western-dominated sources of credit such as the World Bank, International Monetary Fund (IMF) and Asian Development Bank, the AIIB will aim to invest in regional infrastructure projects ranging from energy to transportation to telecommunications.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               The new development bank, which is expected to launch later this year, will have $100 billion in capital to begin witha massive mountain of money, to be sure, but it falls far short of the estimated trillions that will be necessary to fund Asias astronomical infrastructure demand.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               Chinas creation of its own global bank highlights the countrys desire to wield more control over funding such projects. It currently commands only 5.17 percent of the vote in the World Bank and 3.81 percent in the IMF.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               <img align="baseline" alt="" border="0" hspace="0" src="http://67.19.64.18/news/2015/4-1fh/image002.jpg"/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               And so the
               <a href="http://www.usfunds.com/investor-library/frank-talk/currency-wars-heat-up-as-central-banks-race-to-cut-rates/#.VRr4af50zcs">
                <font color="#0000ff">
                 currency wars
                </font>
               </a>
               continue to heat up. Chinas move demonstrates its ongoing efforts to establish the yuan as a global reserve currency on par with the U.S. dollar. Its no secret that the country wants the yuan to become part of the IMFs Special Drawing Right (SDR), a composite currency unit that now consists of the dollar, Japanese yen, British pound sterling and euro. The founding of the AIIB might very well bring the country closer to realizing these goals.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal">
               <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
                A-Shares Headed Higher
                <o:p>
                </o:p>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               Chinese stocks are currently having a moment. Mainland A-shares, as measured by the benchmark Shanghai Composite Index, are up an incredible 92 percent for the 12-month period on the back of strong recent performance in the financial, property and infrastructure industries.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               Theres generally a high correlation between the A-share market and China and Hong Kong, but the A-shares have outperformed by a wide margin over the past year.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               <img align="baseline" alt="" border="0" hspace="0" src="http://67.19.64.18/news/2015/4-1fh/image003.png"/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               Last Wednesday the index fell a slight 0.8 percent, ending a 10-day rally that contributed 12 percent, its longest winning streak in 23 years.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               Chinese policymakers have recently eased quota controls for foreign investors in mainland stocks and bonds, as they promote the yuan to be accepted as an SDR. The potential for greater inflows into the market should help the Shanghai Composite head even higher.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               Our China Region Fund (USCOX) has participated in this rally through the Morgan Stanley China A Share Fund and a closed-end fund.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt">
              <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal">
               <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
                Read more about China:
                <o:p>
                </o:p>
               </span>
              </b>
             </p>
             <ul style="MARGIN-TOP: 0in" type="disc">
              <li class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1">
               <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
                <a href="http://www.usfunds.com/investor-library/frank-talk/mind-blowing-china-consumes-more-gold-than-the-world-produces/#.VRqyxv50zcs">
                 <font color="#0000ff">
                  China Consumes More Gold Than the World Produces
                 </font>
                </a>
                <o:p>
                </o:p>
               </span>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial">
               Whats not so well-knownbut just as amazingis that Chinas supply of the precious metal per capita is actually low compared to neighboring Asian countries such as Taiwan and Singapore.
              </span>
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>

              <li class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1">

                 <font color="#0000ff">
                  China Just Crossed a Landmark Threshold
                 </font>
                </a>
                <o:p>
                </o:p>
               </span>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial">
               One of the most headline-worthy developments is Chinas $16.3-billion infrastructure initiative intended to revive trading routes along the centuries-old Silk Road. Thousands of miles of railways, roads and pipelines will link Beijing to major markets all over Asia, Africa and Europe.
              </span>
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
             </p>
             <ul style="MARGIN-TOP: 0in" type="disc">
              <li class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1">
               <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">
                <a href="http://www.usfunds.com/investor-library/frank-talk/?startRow=31&amp;nextNID=BF62EFAD-CAE1-E91B-83120F4642A3D482#.VRnB9_50zcs">
                 <font color="#0000ff">
                  China Wants to Conduct the Worlds High-Speed Rail Market
                 </font>
                </a>
                <o:p>
                </o:p>
               </span>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in">
              <span style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%; FONT-FAMILY: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial">
               In recent months, Chinese Premier Li Keqiang has emerged as the nations top salesman for what he calls the New Silk Roadmiles upon miles of high-speed transportation connecting all corners of the world. His plan might very well become one of Chinas most lucrative exports and culturally significant contributions to the world: fast, efficient and reliable railways.
               <o:p>
               </o:p>
              </span>
