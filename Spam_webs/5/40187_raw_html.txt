              <h1 class="blog-title">
               <a href="http://templeofnoise.com/ginger-wildheart-courtney-love-share-new-single-honour/">
                Ginger Wildheart &amp; Courtney Love Share New Single Honour
               </a>
              </h1>
              <div class="blog-info-wrapper gdl-item-border">
               <div class="blog-date">

              <div class="blog-content">
               <p>
                According to Rolling Stone, the rock worlds greatest duet is Queen and David Bowies Under Pressure. A fine choice no doubt, but is it made legendary by the fact that it was a duet and the way Freddie and Daves voices worked together, or more because it has one of the great basslines ever committed to tape? Come back John Deacon, youd have never allowed the Adam Lambert atrocity would you?
               </p>
               <p>
                The same list also includes Temple Of The Dogs Hunger Strike (but cmon, its practically a Chris Cornell solo track), and the genuinely classic Kenny and Dolly duet Islands In The Stream. Its the latter that brings home the power of a duet, as its all about how the voices intertwine, each a separate instrument, allowing call and response. In the last 20 years the track that encapsulated it best for me wasnt even a proper duet, but Acquiesce, a B-side (of Some Might Say) shoulda been an A-side allowed the Gallagher brothers egos and voices to work together within the same track brilliantly, really for the 1st and only time.
               </p>
               <p>
                Ginger Wildheart, no stranger to the B-side shoulda been an A-side (check out any number from Bad Time To be Having A Bad Time, Shut Your Fucking Mouth And Use Your Fucking Brain, Bang! or Girlfriend Clothes to name just a handful), always knew the secrets were in the harmonies. His intertwining with brother in sonics CJ was the secret to the devotion The Wildhearts fostered in their fanbase.
               </p>
               <p>
                Well, hes at it again, and this time with a genuine icon generously sharing the spotlight. Having been playing in her touring band for the last 18 months, Ginger and Courtney Love collaborated on a track for Gingers ambitious
                <a href="http://g-a-s-s.co/main" target="_blank">
                 G-A-S-S
                </a>
                project, Honour.
               </p>
