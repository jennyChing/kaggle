            <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
             Curiosity Got the Better of Me.
            </h1>
            <div class="post-meta">
             By
             <span class="author vcard">
              <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.skepticink.com/azatheist/author/azatheist/">
               Arizona Atheist
              </a>
             </span>
             
             <time class="updated" datetime="2014-12-16T02:34:10+00:00">
              Dec 16, 2014
             </time>
             
             <a href="http://www.skepticink.com/azatheist/2014/12/16/curiosity-got-the-better-of-me/#comments">
              No comments
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="post-content cf">
            <p>
             Not long ago I decided to purchase the updated book
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/True-Reason-Confronting-Irrationality-Atheism/dp/0825443385" target="_blank">
              <i>
               True Reason: Confronting the Irrationality of the New Atheism
              </i>
             </a>
             (Kregel, 2013). Readers might remember that just a few months ago I had written an in-depth response to an earlier version of this book, titled,
             <a href="http://www.skepticink.com/azatheist/2014/05/25/true-reason-christian-responses-to-the-challenge-of-atheism-a-refutation-part-1/" target="_blank">
              <i>
               True Reason: Christian Responses to the Challenge of Atheism
              </i>
             </a>
             (Patheos Press, 2012).
            </p>
            <p>
             After finishing my response to the 2012 edition I found out that an updated printing became available about a year later. I viewed what I could of the updated version online and concluded that my review of the 2012 edition would be sufficient to respond to both editions. But doubts plagued me. I wondered how much of an update they did to the book. With all of the issues I found there was much room for improvement. Eventually, my curiosity got the better of me and I bought the new version. I just finished the book today and I was not impressed in the least.
            </p>
            <p>
             Most of the essays appeared to have been rewritten to improve the grammar but most of the arguments were the same. Other than this, I noted a number of differences between the two editions: The inclusion of two new chapters: One by Lenny Esposito titled Atheism and the Argument from Reason, and one by David Marshall and Timothy McGrew titled Faith and Reason in Historical Perspective; the formatting was nicer and better organized; the writing was improved; and in a few essays there were minor changes, but nothing that I found that would cause me to have to revise my response to the 2012 edition. I stand by my statement at the end of my response to the previous 2012 edition: my review will suffice as a handy refutation of both editions (minus the two new chapters in the updated edition). But as it happens, Ive already responded to one of the newer essays a few years ago.
            </p>
            <p>
             The essay by Marshall and McGrew is one that I was very curious about. I read what little I could of the essay online and from the limited view allowed to me it appeared to be an improved and revised version of an essay David Marshall has written in the past,
             <a href="http://www.skepticink.com/azatheist/2012/08/12/dazed-and-confused-david-marshalls-irrational-take-on-faith-and-reason-2/" target="_blank">
              and one which I have responded to in great detail
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             It purports to make the case that reason has always had a central place in Christian thought throughout history and Marshall cites numerous Christian theologians, philosophers, and scientists to support his thesis. The only problem? Each of his quotes have been taken out of context  in some cases, egregiously. When I saw that Marshall had enlisted the help of someone with actual academic credentials I was curious if he had improved upon his essay.
            </p>
            <p>
             I was wrong. Very, very wrong.
            </p>
            <p>
             The Chapter in
             <i>
              True Reason
             </i>
             is a hacked up version of Marshalls previous essay he titled Faith and Reason. Marshall provided dozens of quotes in his essay, but in the book version he provides only a handful of Christians to support his argument. And most of the quotes provided I have already sourced and found them to be taken out of context.
            </p>
            <p>
             Given that I had already responded to this essay I was curious if Marshall might have responded to me in this updated version, and he did  sort of. This is the passage in question from the 2013 edition of
             <i>
              True Reason
             </i>
             from the essay Faith and Reason. Marshall is discussing Justin Martyr and provides a quote he claims proves that Martyr values reason and evidence and attempts to respond to some atheists who have objected in online discussions that Justin gives only lip service to the integration of faith and reason. (151)
            </p>
            <p>
             While Marshall does not mention me by name and he says 
             <i>
              atheists
             </i>
               plural  and to my knowledge I am the only atheist who has quoted Richard Carrier from
             <i>
              Not the Impossible Faith
             </i>
             (2009) in response to Marshalls argument regarding Justin Martyr.
            </p>
            <p>
             Marshall provides next what he believes is a passage that is proof positive of this and argues that, unlike what Richard Carrier argues, Martyr does not rely just on scripture as the justification for his beliefs. He quotes Martyr from his
             <i>
              First Apology
             </i>
             :
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              For we have come, not to flatter you by this writing, nor please you by our address, but to beg that you pass judgment, after an accurate and searching investigation, not flattered by prejudice or by a desire of pleasing superstitious men, nor induced by irrational impulse or evil rumors which have long been prevalent, to give a decision which will prove to be against yourselves. For as for us, we reckon that no evil can be done us, unless we be convicted as evil-doers or be proved to be wicked men; and you, you can kill, but not hurt us.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             In reference to this passage Marshall writes, The inquiry requested in this great passage is not a Bible study: it is judicial and historical. The question is whether Christians are evil men, whether they in fact commit the crimes they are accused of. (152)
            </p>
            <p>
             For anyone with even an ounce of critical thinking ability they ought to be able to spot Marshalls egregious blunder. Atheists, when they talk of Christians not being guided by reason or evidence, are referring to Christians and their religious beliefs.
             <i>
              Not
             </i>
             every day matters, like a court case, which is what Martyr is referring to! So of course, he wouldnt reference the bible when discussing this subject!
            </p>
            <p>
             I
             <a href="http://www.skepticink.com/azatheist/2012/12/23/the-truth-behind-the-new-atheism-the-definitive-refutation-addendum/" target="_blank">
              responded
             </a>
             to this nonsensical argument
             <i>
              three years ago
             </i>
             ! Marshall apparently has never read this scathing rebuttal. There I said,
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              This is crazy. As I said before, Marshall finds a passage speaking of an investigation and he jumps all over it like a bitch in heat. What Justin is talking about is an investigation into the alleged
              <i>
               crimes
              </i>
              of Christians,
              <i>
               not
              </i>
              evidence for their beliefs!
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             I followed up further:
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              As Ive shown, it was Marshall who interpreted Justin incorrectly. After all, Marshall has just proven true what Sam Harris has written. He said, Tell a devout Christian that his wife is cheating on him, or that frozen yogurt can make a man invisible, and he is likely to require as much evidence as anyone else, and to be persuaded only to the extent that you give it. Tell him that the book he keeps by his bed was written by an invisible deity who will punish him with fire for eternity if he fails to accept every incredible claim about the universe, and he seems to require no evidence whatsoever.
             </p>
             <p>
              Exactly. Did Justin give any evidence for his
              <i>
               religious beliefs
              </i>
              ? No. Did he give reasons for other beliefs aside from his religious ones? Yes. Thats the point. Its not that Christians
              <i>
               never
              </i>
              rely on evidence in their lives, but that when it comes to
              <i>
               their faith
              </i>
              they fail to rationally investigate their reasons for belief. Marshall has just proven Harris point without even meaning to.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             Marshall did
             <i>
              absolutely nothing
             </i>
             to respond to this logical, factual objection, even though its been out there for a number of years. I would implore Marshall to keep up, but I doubt that would help. Hes behind the game as usual.
            </p>
            <p>
             The other Chapter by Lenny Esposito about the Argument from Reason is one of the very few Christian arguments Ive neglected to address in my writings. I never thought it was a good argument and many years ago I read Richard Carriers
             <a href="http://infidels.org/library/modern/richard_carrier/reppert.html" target="_blank">
              response to it
             </a>
             and believed there wasnt any point to looking any further into it.
            </p>
            <p>
             I am pleased that there will be no need for me to revise my rebuttal to
             <i>
              True Reason
             </i>
             and I very much hope my response to the book is being referenced often, as this appears to be a fairly popular book, as it has a vast majority of highly positive reviews on Amazon.com. These Christian authors are trying to pull the wool over their readers eyes and I hope many curious readers will find my response helpful.
            </p>
           </div>
