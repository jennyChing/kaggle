             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Theres no shortage of dogs who will gladly join you in every adventure, but these 20 prove especially up to the task. If you're looking for a dog who can keep pace, you should start with this list.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/lubrador-active_ph.jpg?itok=JEhghhPp"/>

            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              20. Lagotto Romagnolo
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Hailing from the Romagna subregion of Italy, the
              <a href="http://www.lagottous.com/" target="_blank">
               Lagotto Romagnolo
              </a>
              was named as a lake dog and traditionally used as a gun dog. With a natural instinct for retrieving, these dogs make for the ideal hunting companions. Their most interesting occupation, though, is truffle hunting.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/tibetan-terrior-active_ph.jpg?itok=mQaL58Km"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Flickr/acidpix
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              19. Tibetan Terrier
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Don't let their size fool you.
              <a href="http://www.ttca-online.org/" target="_blank">
               Tibetan Terriers
              </a>
              , originally bred and raised in monasteries 2,000 years ago, make for excellent outdoor companions. Kept as good-luck charms and watchdogs, and originally used for herding sheep as well as retrieving items that fell below mountainsides, Tibetan Terriers are agile, excel in the snow, and love to climb.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/bernese-mountain-dog_ph.jpg?itok=9BZCmytA"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              teekaygee/Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              18. Bernese Mountain Dog
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Originally bred as a drafting and droving dog, the huge Bernese Mountain Dog will gladly take on mountain walks by your side - it
              <a href="http://www.active.com/outdoors/articles/10-Best-Dog-Breeds-for-Adventure-Junkies" target="_blank">
               will even carry a pack for you
              </a>
              . A great dog for hikers, especially if you live in a colder climate and dont want to bother with a leash, since the Bernese is very easily trained.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/pembroke-corgie_ph.jpg?itok=PIt6nvyR"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Flickr/Ron Armstrong
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              17. Pembroke Welsh Corgi
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Welsh for "dwarf dog," the
              <a href="http://www.pwcca.org/" target="_blank">
               Pembroke Welsh Corgi
              </a>
              is small but no lap dog. Bred for herding, these creatures love to stay active and do well in the outdoors. Despite their short legs, corgis are surprisingly quick and athletic.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/portugese-water-active_ph.jpg?itok=XZQAA0lx"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Flickr/Ron Armstrong
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              16. Portuguese Water Dog
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Bred to herd fish, retrieve broken nets, and swim as couriers,
              <a href="http://www.pwdca.org/" target="_blank">
               Portuguese water dogs
              </a>
              excel in the surf. Given their working history, they also stay close to their ownersinside and outside. With a strong retrieving instinct and love for the water, these dogs make for the ultimate water companions.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/german-shorthaired_ph.jpg?itok=5c2EAhtp"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Andre Blais via Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              15. German Shorthaired Pointers
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              What sets the German Shorthaired Pointer apart is its intelligence and versatility. According to the American Kennel Club, this breed excels at
              <a href="http://www.akc.org/breeds/german_shorthaired_pointer/index.cfm" target="_blank">
               trailing, retrieving and pointing
              </a>
              . Its also comfortable hunting animals like pheasant, quail, grouse, waterfowl, raccoons, possum, and deer. No matter what your hunting style is, this dog can do it all.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/shetland-sheepdog_ph.jpg?itok=h-kv8ZgM"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Flickr/Ron Armstrong
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              14. Shetland Sheepdog
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              One of the most intelligent breeds,
              <a href="http://www.assa.org/" target="_blank">
               Shetland Sheepdogs
              </a>
              are intelligent, playful, and trainable. And with a strong herding instinct, they enjoy chasing and herding animals. They also love to run in wide-open spaces, but keep them clear of roadstheir herding instinct can lead them astray.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/vizsla-active_ph.jpg?itok=qEkMYT-v"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              AnetaPics via Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              13. Vizsla
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              If you live in the city or want to skip the leash, vizslas make the perfect companion. Their exceptional trainability helps them keep focused in busier areas, and experienced runners will appreciate the vizslas athleticism.
              <a href="http://www.runnersworld.com/running-tips/breed-apart" target="_blank">
               According to
               <i>
                Runners World
               </i>
              </a>
              , this breed excels at speed, endurance, navigating obstacles, and even jumping.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/dalmation-active_ph.jpg?itok=rlphdSB5"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Lottie and lily via Flickr
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              12. Dalmation
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Dalmatians can keep pace with horses - they can keep pace with you. These large and lean dogs were classically employed to run ahead of firefighting carriages and clear the way, but theyre just as happy running through the park. Great if youre looking for
              <a href="http://www.runnersworld.com/running-tips/breed-apart">
               a more protective breed
              </a>
              to accompany your long-distance jogs.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/doberman-pinscher_ph.jpg?itok=VZ-UshHC"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              AnetaPics/Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              11. Doberman Pinscher
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              The Doberman pinscher might have an intimidating reputation, but it's really
              <a href="http://gofygure.hubpages.com/hub/Most-Athletic-Dog-Breeds-For-an-Active-Owner" target="_blank">
               an affectionate friend and guardian
              </a>
              . Of course, it's also an excellent fit for active typesfearless but obedient, up for jogs and hikes, and as low-maintenance as they come.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/australian-cattle-dog_ph.jpg?itok=1ODsRLav"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Dalibor Sosna/Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              10. Australian Cattle Dog
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              The Australian cattle dog is a true herding dog: whip-smart, energetic, strong, and protective, not to mention it has some dingo in its blood. With that kind of heritage, you can expect this breed to keep going all day. It loves wide-open spaces but will also
              <a href="http://www.akc.org/breeds/australian_cattle_dog/index.cfm" target="_blank">
               form a tight bond
              </a>
              with its owners, making it a great choice for active families.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/jack-russell-terrier_ph.jpg?itok=n0BZWKw_"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              woodygraphs/Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              9. Jack Russell Terrier
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Small but mighty, Jack Russell Terriers have
              <a href="http://www.pawnation.com/2012/05/16/best-dog-breeds-for-running/" target="_blank">
               boundless energy
              </a>
              and can run for surprisingly extended periods of time. If you're looking for a more compact long-distance running mate, this adventurous breed should be just right.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/rat-terrier_ph.jpg?itok=CksjhNV7"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Flickr/LifeSupercharger
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              8. Rat Terrier
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              An American dog breed with a hunting and farm-dog history,
              <a href="http://www.ratterrierclub.com/" target="_blank">
               Rat Terriers
              </a>
              are cherished as family pets and for their pest control skills. Often mistaken for Jack Russell terriers, these dogs are equally at home on the sofa as on the hiking trail. Highly nimble and obedient, they excel in agility and obedience training.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/rhodesian-ridgeback_ho.jpg?itok=RpEFvZmN"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Tatiana Katsai/Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              7. Rhodesian Ridgeback
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Rhodesian Ridgebacks were originally
              <a href="http://www.akc.org/breeds/rhodesian_ridgeback/index.cfm">
               bred to hunt lions
              </a>
              in Africa, so they are extremely athletic, yet surprisingly good with people. They have an efficient stride and a short, low-maintenance coat that helps when running in the heat.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/aussie-shephard-active_ph.jpg?itok=cbFuaW8L"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Utekhina Anna via Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              6. Australian Shepherd
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              The Australian Shepherd is another busybody
              <a href="http://www.livestrong.com/slideshow/557713-the-best-dog-breeds-for-running/#slide-5">
               herding breed
              </a>
              that sets itself apart with its people skills and protective nature. This breed would make a wonderful addition to an active family, and a good companion for anyone who likes to explore different kinds of terrain.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/weimaraner-active_ph.jpg?itok=FkQNrmIU"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Sari ONeal/Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              5. Weimaraner
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              The Weimaraner is an ideal jogging companion, able to expertly navigate tricky terrain. It's also a good pointing dog for hunters. Plus, its "ghostly" grey coat takes well to hot climates and needs minimal brushing. This breed is
              <a href="http://animal.discovery.com/breed-selector/dog-breeds/sporting/weimaraner.html" target="_blank">
               known to be rambunctious
              </a>
              and extremely active, so youd be well advised to provide an open running area in addition to exercise.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/siberian-husky_ph.jpg?itok=wG-GA5_L"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              ots-photo/Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              4. Siberian Husky
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              The Husky is a beautiful and independent breed that
              <a href="http://www.active.com/outdoors/articles/10-Best-Dog-Breeds-for-Adventure-Junkies" target="_blank">
               requires an active lifestyle
              </a>
              . After all, this is a breed capable of pulling a sled Iditarod distances. An ideal dog if you live in cold weather and want a capable companion on ultra long hikes, trail runs and camping trips - the tough Siberian Husky will keep you safe too.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="slide pg-slide">
            <div class="img-wrap">
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/labrador-active_ph.jpg?itok=FsMC0ypN"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>
              Kirk Geisler via Shutterstock
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              3. Labrador Retriever
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Eager and energetic Labs are "weatherproof, waterproof, and tenaciousthe epitome of a hunter," says
              <a href="http://www.outdoorlife.com/photos/gallery/hunting/2011/10/best-sporting-dog-breeds-all-your-hunting-needs?photo=5#node-1001349450" target="_blank">
               Outdoor Life
              </a>
              . Regularly named as the
              <a href="http://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/dogs/The-Most-Popular-Dog-Breeds-in-the-US.html" target="_blank">
               most popular dog in the U.S.
              </a>
              , great with families, and an excellent athlete, it seems the Labrador retriever can do no wrong.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>

             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">

There's no better outdoor companion than that.
