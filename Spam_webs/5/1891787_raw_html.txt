    <p>
     There are so many
     <strong>
      fun facts about
      <a href="/travel/guides/">
       Spain
      </a>
     </strong>
     that it is hard to know where to start. Here is a selection of our favorite
     <strong>
      <a href="/culture/mexico/society/holidays/">
       fun
      </a>
      facts about Spain
     </strong>
     that the average person might now know.
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      When your
      <strong>
       car is towed away
      </strong>
      , a message left on the street so you know where it was taken.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="/en/learn-spanish-in-spain/madrid">
       Madrid
      </a>
      is in the physical center of the country and the plaza
      <a href="/culture/spain/places/routes/madrid.austrias.asp">
       <em>
        Puerta del Sol
       </em>
      </a>
      , in
      <strong>
       Madrid
      </strong>
      , is the exact center of the country.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="/en/why-study-spanish-in-spain">
       Spain
      </a>
      has a
      <strong>
       very low birthrate
      </strong>
      . Most attribute this to high unemployment, low wages and steep housing costs.
     </li>
     <li>
      Do not be alarmed by a
      <strong>
       dirty floor in a bar
      </strong>
      . It is completely acceptable and normal to throw things on the ground in bars. Most of the time a dirty floor means a good bar!
     </li>
     <li>
      Sunflower seeds are big deal in Spain. More importantly, Spaniards are really talented at opening and eating them at a shocking speed.
     </li>
     <li>
      The locals of Spain have
      <strong>
       lunch at 2 pm and dinner at around 9 to10 pm
      </strong>
      read more about
      <a href="/culture/spain/society/food/eating-customs.asp">
       Spanish Eating Customs
      </a>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      Spanish food is not spicy and very seldom hot. Learn more about the
      <a href="/culture/spain/society/food/mediterranean-diet.asp">
       Mediterranean diet
      </a>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      Spain has the
      <strong>
       second highest number of bars per inhabitants
      </strong>
      , the only country that has more bars than Spain is Cyprus. Bars are an important part of life; learn more about
      <a href="/en/why-study-spanish-abroad">
       Spanish
      </a>
      eating customs.
     </li>
     <li>
      <strong>
       <em>
        Tortillas
       </em>
      </strong>
      in Spain are not the same as
      <em>
       tortillas
      </em>
      elsewhere. You will not find corn or flour
      <em>
       tortillas
      </em>
      in
      <a href="/culture/recipes/">
       Spanish dishes
      </a>
      . In Spain,
      <a href="/culture/recipes/tortilla/">
       <em>
        tortilla espaola
       </em>
      </a>
      refers to a very popular and delicious egg and potato dish. Spaniards use the word
      <em>
       tortitas
      </em>
      to refer to flour/corn tortillas.
     </li>
     <li>
      Most households buy fresh bread every day. Traditionally, they are long baguettes called
      <em>
       barras
      </em>
      or
      <em>
       pistolas
      </em>
      . Bread is present (and required) at almost every meal.
     </li>
     <li>
      Spain is home to
      <strong>
       the worlds largest tomato fight
      </strong>
      :
      <a href="/culture/spain/society/holidays/tomatina.asp">
       <strong>
        <em>
         La Tomatina
        </em>
       </strong>
      </a>
      , celebrated every year in a village of
      <a href="/en/learn-spanish-in-spain/valencia">
       Valencia
      </a>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      The Spaniard Catherine of Aragon married Henry VIII of England. She was the first of his six wives and the mother of Queen Mary I.
     </li>
     <li>
      Gibraltar was ceded by Spain to Britain in 1713, controlled the entrance to the Mediterranean.
     </li>
     <li>
      In 1588 the
      <a href="/culture/spain/history/spanish-empire.asp">
       <strong>
        Spanish Armada
       </strong>
      </a>
      , consisting of one hundred and fifty ships, sailed to England to invade, but it was defeated by the English navy and stormy weather.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="/culture/spain/languages/euskera.asp">
       <strong>
        Euskera
       </strong>
      </a>
      , spoken by the Basque population in northern Spain and southern France, is one of
      <strong>
       the oldest living languages in the world
      </strong>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      Spain remained neutral in
      <strong>
       both world wars
      </strong>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      <strong>
       Food is important
      </strong>
      in Spain and often a topic of conversation. Additionally, they often go on walks after a meal to help them digest their meal.
     </li>
     <li>
      <a href="/culture/spain/sports/soccer-in-spain.asp">
       <strong>
        Football/Soccer
       </strong>
      </a>
      is the
      <strong>
       countrys most important sport
      </strong>
      . Spain won its first
      <a href="/culture/spain/sports/spanish-soccer-team.asp">
       World Cup football
      </a>
      title in 2010, which made the country the 8th country to have ever won.
     </li>
     <li>
      For 40 years, between 1936 and 1975,
      <em>
       Franco
      </em>
      ruled Spain as a dictator. After his death, the rightful
      <a href="/culture/spain/history/the-bourbons.asp">
       King Juan Carlos
      </a>
      had a key role in returning the country to a
      <a href="/culture/spain/history/spanish-democracy.asp">
       democracy
      </a>
      with a new
      <a href="/culture/spain/history/constitution.asp">
       constitution
      </a>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      <strong>
       Same-sex marriage in Spain
      </strong>
      became legal on July 3, 2005.
     </li>
     <li>
      Tomatoes, potatoes, avocadoes, tobacco, and cacao (for chocolate) were all imported into Europe by Spain.
     </li>
     <li>
      Though Spain is more famous for its
      <a href="/culture/spain/society/food/wines-rioja.asp">
       red wine
      </a>
      than white, the majority of its vineyards have white grapes. The fortified wine sherry comes from the city of Jerez in Andalusia. In
      <a href="/en/learn-spanish-in-spain">
       Spanish
      </a>
      , sherry is simply called
      <em>
       'vino de Jerez'
      </em>
      (Jerez wine).
     </li>
     <li>
      The Spanish (in particular those from Cadiz) claim that they invented
      <strong>
       fried fish
      </strong>
      . In the 18th century, Britain had ties with
      <a href="/en/learn-spanish-in-spain/cadiz">
       Cadiz
      </a>
      and therefore it is believed that the English took the idea of fish 'n' chips from Spain. However, other sources claim the Portuguese and Belgians invented it.
     </li>
     <li>
      Spain is the
      <strong>
       number one producer of olive oil in the world
      </strong>
      with 44% of the world's olive oil production. That is more than twice that of Italy and four times that of Greece.
     </li>
     <li>
      Spain is one of the world's biggest producers of saffron, an important ingredient in
      <a href="/culture/recipes/paella/">
       paella
      </a>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      The
      <a href="/en/learn-spanish-in-spain/madrid-city">
       Madrid
      </a>
      -
      <a href="/en/learn-spanish-in-spain/barcelona-city">
       Barcelona
      </a>
      is one of the city pairs with
      <strong>
       most flights per week
      </strong>
      in the world!
     </li>
     <li>
      The Madrid subway is the
      <strong>
       second largest underground system in Europe
      </strong>
      and the sixth largest system in the world. It has 141 miles of track and is still growing.
     </li>
     <li>
      Spain is the third country in the world, after the US and Brazil, with the most
      <strong>
       plastic surgery interventions
      </strong>
      per capita in the world.
     </li>
     <li>
      Spain is the number one country in regards to
      <strong>
       organ donation in the world
      </strong>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      <em>
       Mahou-San Miguel
      </em>
      is the leading brand of Spanish Beer.
     </li>
     <li>
      <em>
       Seat
      </em>
      is the only Spanish car brand.
     </li>
     <li>
      Presenting oneself at a party before 12 is early; Spaniards normally
      <strong>
       start the night around 12:30-2:00 am
      </strong>
      .
     </li>
     <li>
      When meeting someone, it is customary to give one kiss on each cheek.
     </li>
     <li>
      Older women often flaunt expensive fur coats during the winter.
     </li>
     <li>
      There are a lot of
      <a href="/en/learn-spanish-in-spain/madrid-city">
       holidays
      </a>
      and vacation days in Spain and Spaniards really respect their free time and
      <a href="/culture/spain/society/customs/siesta.asp">
       siestas
      </a>
      . They often talk about the quality of life which has a lot to do with how much free time one has.
     </li>
     <li>
      The
      <a href="/culture/spain/history/spanish-empire.asp">
       Spanish Empire
      </a>
      was once one of the world's most powerful.
     </li>
    </ul>
