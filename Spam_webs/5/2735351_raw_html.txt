          <p>
           Copying and pasting are probably some of the most common functions you perform on your computer. Weve shown how a
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/hold-thought-5-reasons-need-clipboard-manager/" title="Hold That Thought: 5 Reasons You Need A Clipboard Manager">
            clipboard manager improves these functions
           </a>
           , but copy-pasting brings another common annoyance: special formatting. Youve surely experienced this: youll copy some text (perhaps a heading from a website) and want to paste it into a Word document, but it keeps its color, size, and other features when you do.
          </p>
          <p>
           Were going to take a look at ways to alleviate this issue. They range from bare-bones to automated; go for whichever works for you!
          </p>
          <h2>
           Let Notepad Intervene
          </h2>
          <p>
           Windows
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/notepad-not-doing-the-trick-try-out-the-lightweight-alternatives-xint-subpad/" title="Notepad Not Doing The Trick? Try Out The Lightweight Alternatives Xint &amp; Subpad">
            Notepad and its alternatives
           </a>
           are about as basic as a computer program can get. Because of this, they dont carry the capacity for special formatting like dedicated word processors (such as Microsoft Word or the
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/libreoffice-free-office-suite-windows-linux-mac/" title="LibreOffice  A Free Office Suite For Windows, Linux &amp; Mac">
            free LibreOffice Writer
           </a>
           ) do.
          </p>
          <p>
           Our simplest solution is to paste any text that you want to strip of formatting into Notepad first, then copy it again. Once you grab Notepads version, youll have only the text and wont have to worry about any colors or alternative fonts sticking around. Try
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/be-more-productive-with-launchy-keystroke-launcher/" title="How To Be More Productive with Launchy Program Launcher">
            using Launchy
           </a>
           or pinning Notepad to your taskbar to be sure you can access it whenever you need to paste something.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="01-Notepad-Strip-Formatting" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01-Notepad-Strip-Formatting.png?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Use Offices Special Paste
          </h2>
          <p>
           A lot of pasting occurs into
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/whats-new-in-office-2013-your-unofficial-overview/" title="Microsoft Office 2013 : Unofficial Guide">
            Microsoft Office products
           </a>
           , and if youre typing a paper,
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/5-creative-uses-of-powerpoint-presentations-you-havent-explored-yet/" title="5 Creative Uses Of PowerPoint Presentations You Haven't Explored Yet">
            coming up with a slideshow
           </a>
           , or preparing a graphic in Publisher, the extras are probably unwanted. Thankfully, with an extra click, you can skip the formatting when you paste in Office.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="02-Office-Paste-Options" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/02-Office-Paste-Options.png?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <div>
           <div class="ad-unit-post" id="adsense2">
            <small>
             <a href="https://support.google.com/adsense/troubleshooter/1631343?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.makeuseof.com%2Ftag%2F5-ways-strip-formatting-copy-paste-text%2F&amp;client=ca-pub-9894449314507324" style="color:LightGray">
              Ads by Google
             </a>
            </small>

          <p>
           You have a few ways of clearing text formatting. First, once you paste, a small pop-up will appear and give you three options:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Keep Source Formatting
            </strong>
            will preserve the text as you copied it. (Shortcut: Press
            <strong>
             CTRL+K
            </strong>
            , to select this after pasting).
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Merge Formatting
            </strong>
            will force the text youre pasting to match the text around it. (Shortcut: Press
            <strong>
             CTRL+M
            </strong>
            , to choose this after pasting).
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Keep Text Only
            </strong>
            will only take the text and give it no other formatting. (Shortcut: Press
            <strong>
             CTRL+T
            </strong>
            , to do this quickly after pasting).
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           When this bubble appears, you can evenchoose which option to set as default. If youd like, you can also accomplish this using the
           <em>
            Paste Special
           </em>
           option under
           <em>
            Paste
           </em>
           on the left side of the
           <em>
            Home
           </em>
           ribbon. Its effectively the same option, though it gains more functionality if youre pasting more than just text.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="03-Office-Paste-Special" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/03-Office-Paste-Special.png?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Try a Dedicated Program
          </h2>
          <p>
           The above two solutions require you to manually ensure your text is stripped of its formatting, but
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/fastkeys-customizable-automation-software-to-do-almost-anything-windows/" title="FastKeys: Customizable Automation Software To Do Almost Anything [Windows]">
            for those who like to automate
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.stevemiller.net/puretext/">
            PureText
           </a>
           for Windows is a free tool that essentially does the copy-paste-copy in Notepad for you. Grab either the
           <a href="http://www.stevemiller.net/downloads/puretext_3.0_32-bit.zip">
            32-bit
           </a>
           or the
           <a href="http://www.stevemiller.net/downloads/puretext_3.0_64-bit.zip">
            64-bit
           </a>
           download and
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/extract-files-zip-rar-7z-common-archives/" title="How To Extract Files From ZIP, RAR, 7z &amp; Other Common Archives">
            unzip it
           </a>
           (PureText doesnt need to be installed). You wont know its workingother than a small
           <em>
            PT
           </em>
           icon
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/manage-and-tidy-the-windows-7-system-tray/" title="Manage And Tidy The Windows 7 System Tray">
            running in your system tray
           </a>
           . Right-clicking the icon will let you open the few program options.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="04-PureText-Options" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/04-PureText-Options.png?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           By default, the shortcut is
           <strong>
            Windows + V
           </strong>
           <em>
            ,
           </em>
           which is probably fine if you havent
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/how-to-launch-any-windows-app-at-the-touch-of-a-button/" title="How To Launch Any Windows App At The Touch Of A Button">
            set your own keyboard shortcut
           </a>
           on that combination. If you need something different, PureText lets you set it to pretty much anything you want.
          </p>
          <p>
           The only other options let you automatically paste the text that the program converts (which you obviously want enabled to streamline the process), play a sound upon pasting (this is annoying and youll want to shut it off), and run the program at startup. If youll be using PureText regularly, this is a good idea; just remember that
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/make-windows-start-faster-10-non-essential-startup-items-can-safely-remove/" title="Make Windows Start Faster: 10 Non-Essential Startup Items You Can Safely Remove">
            too many startup programs can backfire
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <h2>
           Utilize Browser Extensions
          </h2>
          <p>
           In addition to PureText, which works across Windows, you can install a
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/best-chrome-extensions/" title="The Best Chrome Extensions">
            dedicated browser extension
           </a>
           in either Chrome or Firefox, if you prefer.
          </p>
          <p>
           For Firefox,
           <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-us/firefox/addon/copy-plain-text-2/">
            Copy Plain Text 2
           </a>
           will do the trick. After installing it, youll have a new
           <em>
            Copy As Plain Text
           </em>
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/10-best-shortcuts-add-right-click-menu/" title="10 Best Shortcuts To Add To Your Right-Click Menu">
            option on your right-click menu
           </a>
           that lets you grab anything in your browser without the formatting. If you prefer keyboard shortcuts, the extension also makes
           <strong>
            CTRL + Shift + U
           </strong>
           perform the copy.
          </p>
          <p>
           A few tweaks can be made, including removing extra space and changing special characters to regular text (such as  to r). If you want to go all-in, Copy Plain Text 2 can also override the standard copy command completely. Be careful with this, though!
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="05-Firefox-Paste-Formatting-Extension" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/05-Firefox-Paste-Formatting-Extension.png?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Chrome users will want to take advantage of
           <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/copy-as-plain-text-amazin/mkkcgjeddgdnikkeoinjgbocghokolck?hl=en-US">
            Copy as plain text
           </a>
           , a counterpart to the Firefox extension. Its slim and only allows you to copy plain text by right-clicking and choosing the new option; you wont find any keyboard shorcuts with this one, which may be a deal-breaker.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Remember Keyboard Shortcuts
          </h2>
          <p>
           Frankly,
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/3-reasons-install-lots-browser-extensions-opinion/" title="3 Reasons Why You Shouldn't Install Lots Of Browser Extensions [Opinion]">
            browser extensions are a heavy-handed solution
           </a>
           for this problem, as Chrome and Firefox already include shortcuts for pasting regular text out of the box. In both browsers, simply press
           <strong>
            CTRL + Shift + V
           </strong>
           to paste text without the extras, no matter where you copied it from!
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="06-Chrome-Keyboard-Shortcut-Formatting-Strip" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/06-Chrome-Keyboard-Shortcut-Formatting-Strip.png?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Elsewhere in Windows, the shortcut
           <strong>
            CTRL + Space
           </strong>
           will clear formatting from selected text. Its not a universal shortcut (it isnt working in
           <a href="https://markdownpad.com/">
            MarkdownPad
           </a>
           , the program I use for writing), but it will work in Office.
          </p>
          <h2>
           What About Mac and Linux?
          </h2>
          <p>
           You can still easily strip the formatting using a different OS.
          </p>
          <p>
           In
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/service/mac/" title="Mac OS X">
            Mac OS X
           </a>
           :
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            You can use
            <strong>
             Shift + Option + Command + V
            </strong>
            to paste without formatting (or with whatever format the pasted text is placed into). The Chrome shortcut from Windows is the same:
            <strong>
             Command + Shift + Option + V
            </strong>
            , and you can install the extensions if you want.
           </li>
           <li>
            Using TextEdit,
            <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/closer-textedit-word-processing-program-mac/" title="A Closer Look At The TextEdit Word Processing Program [Mac]">
             the Mac equivalent to Notepad
            </a>
            , you can copy and paste text as in the first method outlined above. You may need to choose
            <strong>
             Format
            </strong>
            <em>
             &gt;
            </em>
            <strong>
             Make Plain Text
            </strong>
            (
            <strong>
             Command + Shift + T
            </strong>
            ); to keep formatting out by default, go to
            <strong>
             Preferences
            </strong>
            and under
            <em>
             Format
            </em>
            , choose
            <strong>
             Plain Text
            </strong>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            Install a clipboard manager, such as the free
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/flycut-clipboard-manager/id442160987">
             Flycut
            </a>
            , which allows you to paste unstyled text.
           </li>
           <li>
            If youd like to force pasted text to be unstyled system-wide, head to
            <strong>
             System Preferences
            </strong>
            <em>
             &gt;
            </em>
            <strong>
             Keyboard
            </strong>
            <em>
             &gt;
            </em>
            <strong>
             Keyboard Shortcuts
            </strong>
            <em>
             &gt;
            </em>
            <strong>
             Application Shortcuts
            </strong>
            and click the plus sign below to add a shortcut. For the
            <em>
             Application
            </em>
            box, select
            <strong>
             All Applications
            </strong>
            , and in the
            <em>
             Menu Title
            </em>
            box, type
            <em>
             Paste and Match Style
            </em>
            . Finally, in the
            <em>
             Keyboard Shortcut
            </em>
            box, type
            <strong>
             Command + V
            </strong>
            and youll see the key combination in the box. Click
            <strong>
             Add
            </strong>
            , and youre all set!
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <img alt="07-Paste-Key" class="aligncenter" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/07-Paste-Key.png?f35dd4"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           For
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/service/linux/" title="Linux">
            Linux
           </a>
           :
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            In recent versions of Linux, you can use
            <strong>
             CTRL + Shift + V
            </strong>
            to paste text without formatting.
           </li>
           <li>
            Like Windows, you can paste into a text editor (try
            <a href="https://wiki.gnome.org/Apps/Gedit">
             Gedit
            </a>
            if you need one) to strip the formatting before pasting elsewhere.
           </li>
           <li>
            The above browser extensions will work in Linux as well.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           Youre a Pasting Wizard
          </h2>
          <p>
           As weve seen, it doesnt really matter whether you copy the text without the formatting or if you wait until pasting it to strip it. Whichever methods work best for you (theres a lot of them!) will do just fine, and youll save time instead of having to manually format everything.
          </p>
          <p>
           If youre on Windows, the best combo is probably to install PureText and use that universal shortcut whenever you need a plain paste. Barring that, Chrome and Firefoxs shortcut and special pasting in Office on special occasions should mean youre taken care of.
          </p>
          <p>
           Want to take your copy-pasting even further? Check out
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/ten-incredibly-useful-clipboard-management-utilities/" title="10 Incredibly Useful Clipboard Management Utilities [Windows]">
            ten alternative clipboard managers
           </a>
           for increased functionality.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Whats your favorite way to strip out text formatting?
           </strong>
           Are any of these methods new to you? Paste a comment below and let me know!
          </p>
