                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     World's Easiest Languages To Learn
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2531394034755003189">
                     To follow up on one of our most popular posts,
                     <a href="http://blog.leximo.org/2009/03/worlds-hardest-languages-to-learn.html">
                      World's Hardest Languages to Learn
                     </a>
                     , I decided to do a quick survey or what people think is the easiest language in world to learn.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Their responses where quite surprising, there was a clear consensus that
                     <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish%20language" id="aptureLink_jACGzEa0ze">
                      Spanish
                     </a>
                     is the easiest language to learn. Although this was a small sample size, I'm still surprised with the answers.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     If there's another language you think is easy to learn, feel free to respond in the comments.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     With out further ado, here are the survey responses.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote style="font-style: italic;">
                      Born in
                      <a href="http://maps.google.com/maps?om=0&amp;iwloc=addr&amp;f=q&amp;ll=27.98141%2C-82.451141&amp;hl=en&amp;z=13&amp;ie=UTF8" id="aptureLink_pc9BR5Lvmt">
                       Tampa, Florida
                      </a>
                      speaking
                      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English%20language" id="aptureLink_eJsncChz1B">
                       English
                      </a>
                      and started studying
                      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish%20language" id="aptureLink_ErRwfkbFtt">
                       Spanish
                      </a>
                      back in the days when middle school was still called Junior High!!!!
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Spanish is the easiest language to learn because each letter sounds the same at all times in all various contexts.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      It took me five years of formal study in a school language lab and three weeks of living in
                      <a href="http://maps.google.com/maps?om=0&amp;iwloc=addr&amp;f=q&amp;ll=-35.675147%2C-71.542969&amp;hl=en&amp;z=3&amp;ie=UTF8" id="aptureLink_qqJaqyqThP">
                       Chile
                      </a>
                      until I was conversationally fluent and bi-literate (can read, write and speak it).
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Tips:
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <ol>
                       <li>
                        Start young and start your children even younger - experts agree that native or near-native pronunciation is the product of early language learning.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        With Spanish, once you master the sounds of the consonants, the consonant blends and the vowels, the rest is simply putting it together because, unlike English, what you see is what you get with Spanish!
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Practice rolling your Rs so you sound like a native with that one simple trick.  It's a trilling of the tongue up on the roof of your mouth.  Do an internet search for other clever ways to practice that rolling of the Rs!
                       </li>
                      </ol>
                      <br/>
                      Happy Educating!  Sea feliz educando!
                     </blockquote>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     -Beth Butler
                     <br/>
                     Founder of The Boca Beth Program
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Raise A Bilingual Child the Fun &amp; Easy Way!

                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <blockquote style="font-style: italic;">
                      Not meaning to be flip, the easiest language for me to learn was "
                      <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FdiR95cB9i8" id="aptureLink_4umpwIUpb7">
                       animal
                      </a>
                      ", especially canine.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      I didn't get my first dog until I was in my 30's and found that I was able to communicate with him right away -- without any books, tapes, videos,
                      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosetta%20Stone%20%28software%29" id="aptureLink_yVhgilI4sn">
                       Rosetta Stone
                      </a>
                      , etc.  Among other things, he told me when he needed to go out, wanted to be fed, to fill his water bowl and play with him.  And when I responded to him correctly, he rewarded me with love and licks.  What a great way to learn a new language!  And, amazingly, he understood English!  Asking him "do you want to go out?", he always responded with a canine "yes."
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Further, I came to understand that dogs were able to understand many human languages.  Quick illustration -- a friend found a dog in a park having a collar with a tags and brought it home, seeking to find its human from there.  She remarked that although the dog had tags that it appeared not to be trained because every time she asked/commanded it to sit, it wouldn't.  Observing the situation, I asked what made her think that the dog's human spoke English?  And, so with a lit light bulb above her head, she commanded the dog to sit in Spanish, which the dog promptly did!
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      With regards -- and a woof to you!
                     </blockquote>

                      </a>
                      a couple of years later and was discussing plumbing systems after a week. Spanish is one of the easiest languages in the world as far as as vocab and grammar is concerned. Where pronunciation does not affect meaning (say
                      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese%20language" id="aptureLink_GtFBAOl2NV">
                       Chinese
                      </a>
                      or African languages), proper pronunciation is not key to comprehension.
                     </blockquote>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     -JK
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Well there you have it, let us know what other languages are easy to learn in the comments below.
