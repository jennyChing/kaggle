              <div class="field-title">
               <p>
                <a href="/article/Recipes/Korean-Fried-Chicken">
                 Korean Fried Chicken
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="field-credit">
               <p>
                Andr Baranowski
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="field-body">
               <p>
                Double-frying chicken wings gives them a delicate, crackly crust that is the hallmark of this popular Korean specialty, made famous in this country at the Los Angeles restaurant Kyochon.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
             SERVES 2-4
             <h4>
              INGREDIENTS
             </h4>
             <p>
              Canola oil, for frying
              <br/>
              5 cloves garlic
              <br/>
              1 (1") piece peeled ginger
              <br/>
              3 tbsp. soy sauce
              <br/>
              3 tbsp. gojujang (Korean chile paste)
              <br/>
              1 tbsp. rice vinegar
              <br/>
              1 tbsp. Asian sesame oil
              <br/>
              1 tbsp. honey
              <br/>
              &frac23; cup flour
              <br/>
              1 tbsp. cornstarch
              <br/>
              16 chicken wings (about 1 lbs.)
             </p>
             <h4>
              INSTRUCTIONS
             </h4>
             <p>
              1. Pour oil into a 6-qt. pot to a depth of 2". Heat over medium-high heat until a thermometer reads 350. Chop garlic and ginger in a food processor. Add soy, gojujang, vinegar, sesame oil, and honey; puree. Put sauce into a bowl.
             </p>
             <p>
              2. Whisk flour, cornstarch, and &frac23; cup water in another bowl. Add chicken; toss. Working in 3 batches, fry chicken until golden, 6-8 minutes. Drain on paper towels. Return oil to 350. Fry chicken until crisp, 6-8 minutes more. Drain again. Toss chicken in sauce.
             </p>
