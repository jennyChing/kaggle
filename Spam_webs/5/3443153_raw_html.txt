          It is often said we are what we eat. Continuing from our first post onhealthy life how to be smarter and
          <a href="https://hoperaw.com/blog/our-daily-opinion/tag/focus/">
           focus
          </a>
          : How attainable is living well? Living well with the choices we make in the food we eat is not as expensive as we think. Lets look at what it can cost us to have three meals a day without being deliberate at living well through your choices.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-4348">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          For many of us who live here, our lives have become very busy it would seem. We will not have the time to stop to make a home-cooked meal. Most times our three meals are eaten on the road in our cars or some restaurant. I would safely average that the cost per person for three meals a day is around $18 in total when buying from a restaurant, from my own personal investigation, or for three people we are looking at $54 a day.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Comparing Cost of Living Well
           </em>
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          When comparing the cost for a meal at a restaurant to cooking at home, I have discovered that it costs considerably less. Take for an instance when we go to the supermarket and are buying food items at the cost of $250. Such items include, but are not limited to,wild caught salmon, chicken breast, chicken liver, pork, jasmine rice, whole wheat flour, cornmeal, unbleached flour, brown sugar, whole grain oats, coconut oil, green peas, spinach, avocado, cabbage, onions, tomatoes, potatoes, plantains, carrots,healthy snacks, spices, and seasonings among other things. All these items cost us around $250.
         </p>
         <p>
          When you calculated the cost of daily eating at a restaurant for three people in two weeks, the cost is $756 compared to $250 cooking at home for three, including carrying lunch to work. We have personally tried this and it works even though we havent started out intentionally. We also didnt have a rigid plan in place, because there are times I felt like not cooking and Some Sundays we wouldnt cook anything but breakfast.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Meal Types for Living Well
           </em>
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          The type of meals we would cook would be like plantain oats porridge, salmon with plantains, oven sirloin chops, apple banana oat pancakes and chicken sandwiches. Because I have personally done this myself, the experiences ofeatingmy home cooked meals cannot be compared to eating at a restaurant. A home cook meal in my experience has outdone any restaurant that I have ever gone to. I see that in cost, taste, quality, measure, health and the overall reaction of my body.
         </p>
         <p>
          It can be argued that there are restaurants that provide really tasty and healthy choices. My answer would be yes I know, but still it cannot be matched, especially against theprice. I realize that eating at a restaurant can be very convenient for us, but a home-cooked meal will always be the best. I can hear the thoughts youre saying, But I really dont have the time to prepare home cook meal now, even though I would love to practice living well and making sure my family is maintaining healthy living lifestyles.
         </p>
         <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
         </div>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Living Well is Attainable with Discipline
           </em>
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Living well is attainable if we just slow down, practice good time management, downsizing and sticking to the plan. What we dont realize is if we practice living well, it gives us the extended boost to our lives. How many times have we stopped and thought about how we manage our time to see how we aredoing? If we really look into our time management we will all realize that we are lacking because some of the things we find ourselves caught up with most of the time just isnt worth our time.
         </p>
         <p>
          First I will say slow down and take a deep breath if youre running behind; no need to rush. You just need take stock of your routine and see whats on your shelves that are obsolete and need to go. What are the overstock items you need to downsize? What are thebadlyplaced items and needing to reschedule how they are placed on the shelves of yourlife?Having done your stock count, this is where you realize I have a few more hours free.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Keeping Up with Living Well
           </em>
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Having realized that you have a few hours free, this is where you put measures in place to keep up living well and maintaining a balanced lifestyle. I would make meal plans ahead of time. Even though I dont write it down, I have an idea of what I want to cook for different times of the day, but I will say to you write it down and stick to your plan.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its not hard to come up with a meal plan; just look at what you have and decide how creative you can get. Look up the recipes that match your items and seek ways of how to enhance the taste, quality and look of the food you want to prepare. It also helps to present a variety of different meals, even if you are using the same ingredients.
         </p>
         <p>
          Take someone such as my roommate who likes plantains, so I will try to use plantains in many ways whether I am using it forbreakfast, lunch or dinner. You save a lot of time and
          <a href="https://hoperaw.com/blog/our-daily-opinion/tag/money/">
           money
          </a>
          cooking one-pot meals like soup or pasta
         </p>
