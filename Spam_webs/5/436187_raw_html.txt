        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Dr Pepper Pulled Pork
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.itisakeeper.com/6993/slow-cooker-summer-recipes-dr-pepper-pulled-pork/#comments">
           6 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14151811" data-link="http://www.itisakeeper.com/6993/slow-cooker-summer-recipes-dr-pepper-pulled-pork/" data-summary="" data-title="Dr Pepper Pulled Pork">
        </div>
        <p>
         This shop has been compensated by Collective Bias, Inc. and its advertiser. All opinions are mine alone. For more ideas on how to create your own #BackyardBash, check out the tumblr page at
         <a href="http://www.drpepperbash.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          www.drpepperbash.com
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         Get your party started this summer with one of my favorite
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.itisakeeper.com/6503/slow-cookin-summer-series-easy-recipes-for-a-slow-cooker/" title="Slow Cookin Summer Series: Easy Recipes for a Slow Cooker">
           slow cooker summer recipes
          </a>
         </strong>
         
         <a href="http://www.drpepperbash.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Dr Pepper
         </a>
         Pulled Pork!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Slow Cooker Summer Recipes Dr Pepper Pulled Pork ~ www.itisakeeper.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6999" src="http://www.itisakeeper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Slow-Cooker-Summer-Recipes-Dr-Pepper-Pulled-Pork-www.itisakeeper.com-6-737x1024.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This past weekend was my in-laws wedding anniversary. Theyve been married for 56 years. Amazing, right?!? To help celebrate this milestone, we had a little backyard get-together at our house.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was actually a dual celebration. We just finished having a patio installed in our backyard. This was the perfect opportunity to show off our new patio to our family!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Slow Cooker Summer Recipes Dr Pepper Pulled Pork ~ www.itisakeeper.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6995" src="http://www.itisakeeper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Slow-Cooker-Summer-Recipes-Dr-Pepper-Pulled-Pork-www.itisakeeper.com-2-1024x682.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Since my father-in-law loves pulled pork, I made Dr Pepper Pulled Pork BBQ Sandwiches with a finger-lickin good homemade BBQ sauce, my favorite
         <a href="http://www.itisakeeper.com/494/creamy-coleslaw-and-remembering-binghams/" target="_blank" title="Creamy Coleslaw and Remembering Binghams">
          creamy coleslaw
         </a>
         and macaroni salad. I also had bowls of Cheeze-Its for everyone to munch on while I was putting the finishing touches on dinner. And, I served up plenty of Dr Pepper to wash everything down!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Slow Cooker Summer Recipes Dr Pepper Pulled Pork ~ www.itisakeeper.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6996" src="http://www.itisakeeper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Slow-Cooker-Summer-Recipes-Dr-Pepper-Pulled-Pork-www.itisakeeper.com-1-808x1024.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The pulled pork was easy to make. I started with my favorite
         <a href="http://www.itisakeeper.com/3811/slow-cooker-pulled-pork-bbq/" title="Slow Cooker Pulled Pork BBQ">
          pulled pork recipe
         </a>
          since its always a huge hit whenever I make it  and added a can of Dr Pepper to the slow cooker for a fun twist.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         Then I made the homemade BBQ sauce to go on top. Again, I started with my favorite recipe and added some Dr Pepper extra flavor.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Slow Cooker Summer Recipes Dr Pepper Pulled Pork ~ www.itisakeeper.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6997" height="1024" src="http://www.itisakeeper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Slow-Cooker-Summer-Recipes-Dr-Pepper-Pulled-Pork-www.itisakeeper.com-4-682x1024.jpg" title="Slow Cooker Summer Recipes Dr Pepper Pulled Pork ~ www.itisakeeper.com" width="682"/>
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         I loved how the Dr Pepper transformed the flavor and gave it a very subtle cherry flavor. This stuff is crazy-good and so easy to make yourself!
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         For dessert, we grilled up smores. My son loves to burn his marshmallows beyond recognition so the metal marshmallow sticks by Coleman are perfect since they dont burn.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         I found everything I needed at my local Wal-Mart.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Slow Cooker Summer Recipes Dr Pepper Pulled Pork ~ www.itisakeeper.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6998" src="http://www.itisakeeper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Slow-Cooker-Summer-Recipes-Dr-Pepper-Pulled-Pork-www.itisakeeper.com-5-1024x1024.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         My favorite way to eat pulled pork is to start with a generous helping of pork on a soft sandwich roll. Then slather it with extra BBQ sauce and place a heaping pile of coleslaw right on top! Its the perfect texture and flavor combination  the succulent pork, the sweet and zesty sauce and the crisp and creamy coleslaw. Its a little piece of heaven on a bun. You can easily see why its one of my favorite slow cooker summer recipes!
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Slow Cooker Summer Recipes Dr Pepper Pulled Pork ~ www.itisakeeper.com" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-7000" src="http://www.itisakeeper.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Slow-Cooker-Summer-Recipes-Dr-Pepper-Pulled-Pork-www.itisakeeper.com-7-1024x893.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;">
         The handy printable recipe is below!

         <h2 dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;">
          Slow Cooker Summer Recipes: Dr Pepper Pulled Pork Sandwiches
         </h2>
         <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;">
          <em>
           Compliments of Its a Keeper
          </em>
          <br/>
          <em>
           www.itisakeeper.com
          </em>
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;">
          Ingredients:
         </p>
         <p>
          1 (7 pound) bone-in pork butt
          <br/>
          Spice rub (recipe below)
          <br/>
          1 (12 oz) can Dr Pepper
          <br/>
          8 sandwich rolls
          <br/>
          Spicy BBQ Sauce (recipe below)
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Rub entire roast with spice rub and place fat side up in slow cooker. Pour Dr Pepper into slow cooker. Cover and cook on high for 7-8 hours or until meat falls off the bone.
          </li>
          <li>
           Remove pork from slow cooker and place on a large rimmed baking sheet. Using two forks, shred pork, removing excess fat and bone.
          </li>
          <li>
           Place a helping of pork on roll and top with Spicy BBQ Sauce.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Spice Rub
          </strong>
          <br/>
          2 Tbsp dark brown sugar
          <br/>
          1 tsp paprika
          <br/>
          1 tsp chili powder
          <br/>
          3/4 tsp ground cumin
          <br/>
          1/2 tsp ground chipotle chili pepper
          <br/>
          1/4 tsp ground ginger
          <br/>
          1/2 tsp salt
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Combine all ingredients in a small bowl and mix to combine.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Spicy BBQ Sauce
          </strong>
          <br/>
          1 large onion, diced
          <br/>
          1 Tbsp vegetable oil
          <br/>
          12 oz tomato paste
          <br/>
          1 (12 oz) can Dr Pepper
          <br/>
          6 Tbsp apple cider vinegar
          <br/>
          4 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
          <br/>
          4 Tbsp molasses
          <br/>
          4 tsp prepared mustard
          <br/>
          2 cups ketchup
          <br/>
          2 tsp chili powder
          <br/>
          2 tsp dry mustard
          <br/>
          1 tsp garlic powder
          <br/>
          1/2 tsp all spice
          <br/>
          1/4 tsp ground red pepper
          <br/>
          2 tsp salt
          <br/>
          3 tsp liquid smoke
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           In a large pot, saute onion in vegetable oil over medium heat until softened but not browned.
          </li>
          <li>
           Add all other ingredients and cook for 10-20 minutes, or until sauce thickens to desired consistency.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           NOTES:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           I have substituted two pork tenderloins in place of the pork butt and it has turned out wonderful!
          </li>
          <li>
           I have also substituted two double chicken breasts in place of the pork and its wonderful, too!
          </li>
          <li>
           You can make the sauce ahead of time and reheat it when youre ready to serve it.
          </li>
          <li>
           You can also add the shredded pork to the sauce and heat it together, if youd like.
          </li>
         </ul>
