              <h3>
               <img alt="Top Wines of 2014" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-94484" height="600" src="http://cdn.vinology.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/blind-wine-class-black-and-white-1.png" width="960"/>
              </h3>
              <h2>
               Twenty Five of theTop Wines of 2014
              </h2>
              <p>
               <em>
                The top wines of 2014 from our wine tasting classes. These are the bottles that excited and challenged us this year. And these arent just the expensive bottles, either:thereare plenty here in the $20 price range, andonly a fewtopping $100. All but oneof the bottles are still available (as of this update).We included the current price ranges for your benefit.
               </em>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #25. Locations NV Corse, Corsica
              </h3>
              <p>
               A vermentino with textural complexity and beautiful quince and almond notes. One of be most interesting whites of the year. This bottle was discovered byAlana Zerbe and was a huge hit in one of herExotica classes.
               <em>
                $16 to $20
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #24. Ronchi Di Cialla 2011 Cialla Bianco, Colli Orientali del Friuli
              </h3>
              <p>
               Technically an orange wine and primarily Ribolla Gialla. Fermented on the skins and just a touch oxidized, this bottle bursts with orange marmalade, wild strawberry, onion skin, and toasted hazelnuts. FounderPaolo Rapuzzi passed away this past summer. He will be greatly missed. $23 to $32
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #23. Giribaldi 2006 Barbaresco
              </h3>
              <p>
               There are about a hundred and ten bottles of Barbaresco and Barolo that are far more expensive than this bottle. ThisBarbaresco offers a kingly degree of varietal correctness and just enough oak dressing to put this squarely into ourmodern nebiolo throne.
               <em>
                $27 to $34
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #22. Selbach-Oster 2012 Zeltinger Schlossberg Riesling, Sptlese Trocken
              </h3>
              <p>
               The most important wine style that Americans have not discovered yet. Lets change that. Lets dance with joy and sing in a graveyard. Lets eat oysters and osso bucco and drink a Sptlese Trocken. Lets not do all of that at once.
               <em>
                $23to $29
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #21. Mas Doix 2009
               <i>
                Salanques
               </i>
               Priorat
              </h3>
              <p>
               The most important wine region that still remains inaccessible to the average wine drinker. This is the bottle that seems to explain it all without becoming too simplistic or too scary. Sophisticated red fruit swallowed bya savage roarof mineral that softensinto a suave gooseberry and leather crescendo.
               <em>
                $25 to $42
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #20. Domaine Vincent Paris 2007 Granit 30 Cornas
              </h3>
              <p>
               Showing how beautiful and delicate Syrah can be. Almost Burgundian in its complexity and refinement, this bottle offers the benchmarks of game and dark fruit with an abundance of minerality. A wine for gray days and Sylvia Plath poetry.
               <em>
                $29 to $39
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #19. La Follette 2009 Chardonnay Manchester Ridge Vineyard
              </h3>
              <p>
               Chardonnay is dead, long live Chardonnay. This is the rareargument that Cali Chards still deserve to be considered fine wine.
               <a class="internal-link post-link post-29285" href="http://www.vinology.com/la-follette-2009-chardonnay-manchester-ridge-vineyard/" title="La Follette 2009 Chardonnay Manchester Ridge Vineyard, Mendocino Ridge  (California)">
                Full wine review here.
               </a>
               <em>
                $36 to$44
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #18. Clos de los Siete 2009 Red.
              </h3>
              <p>
               Rich with slippery blackberry and fatty fig, the fruit flavors are followed by smoked vanilla and mocha. A note of fresh sage turns to ripe tannins and more complex fruit flavors that twist toward a tightening finish of mineral and burnt marshmallow. Intense and beautiful and full of everlasting joy. A wonderful bottle of wine.
               <a class="internal-link post-link post-29282" href="http://www.vinology.com/clos-de-los-siete-2009/" title="Clos de los Siete 2009, Mendoza (Argentina)">
                Full wine review here.
               </a>
               <em>
                $15 to $21
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #17. Locations NV F-2
              </h3>
              <p>
               Dave Phinney has twobottles on this list (
               <em>
                Corse
               </em>
               and this bottle), and earns the title of Winemaker of the Year. This bottle is the reason why. Despite being under $20, the F-2shows a level of complexity and depth that only the hardest of hard working winemakers can muster. His bottleDepartment 66 2011 LAgent Grenache
               <em>
                almost
               </em>
               made the list, but with only 30 cases made, it wastoo rare. That said, it isadeep pool of fruit with something scary swimming under the glassy surface. If you can find it, buy it.
               <em>
                $14 to $16 (and $60+ for the LAgent)
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #16.Zanzibar 2008 Sandra Red
              </h3>
              <p>
               Columbia Valley is our New World Wine region of the year, and this bottle is one of the main reasons. Very rarely does a wine at this price point manage to make its own statement on the origin of things. Notes of earl grey tea and jasmine come together in a rattling and rusty cage adorned with a plump pillow of cedar chips and black berry jam.
               <em>
                $23 to $26
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #15. Celler de lEncastell 2007 Roquers de Porrera Priorat
              </h3>
              <p>
               There are three bottles from Priorat on our list this year, and for good reason. It is our Old World wine region of the year, and one we visited with a group of students.
              </p>
              <p>
               One of the greatest winemaking villagesin Priorat is Porrera. This bottling shows how the intense minerality can cut through a stranglehold of delicious fruit. Ill let Steve Tanzer take this one home:
              </p>
              <div class="et_quote">
               <div class="et_right_quote">
                Lively and precise on the nose, offering ripe red fruit, floral qualities and a slow-building herbal essence. Much darker in profile on the palate, offering dense cherry and plum flavors and exotic medicinal herb and mineral qualities. The minerality adds urgency to the sweet, dark fruit-dominated finish, which clings tenaciously.
               </div>
               <span class="et_quote_sign">
               </span>
              </div>
              <p>
               <em>
                $25 to$35 (Auction only)
               </em>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #14. Gramercy Cellars 2009 Inigo Montoya Tempranillo
              </h3>
              <p>
               It is a rare that upholds the standards of an ancient winemaking culture but also maintains its own identity. This bottle puts Columbia Valley Tempranillo on the map. It has the structure and core elements of a Reserva Rioja, in the style of Muga. However, it keeps its New World fruit sensibility and that frosty tone I love about Walla Walla.
               <em>
                $45 to $55
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #13. Tikves 2011 Barovo, Macedonia
              </h3>
              <p>
               This bottle puts Macedonia into the spotlight as the up and coming wine country. Made from two indigenous varietalsKratosija and Vranec. Kratosija one of which is very likelyan original cloneof Americas beloved Zinfandel (and Italys Primitivo) and introduces a whole new side to that popular grape.
               <em>
                $16 to $20
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #12. Cristom 2006 Pinot Noir, Louise Vineyard, Eola Amity Hills
              </h3>
              <p>
               If a cranberry could shatter, and each crack a doorway into a world of full of cranberries, then you probably should probably stop taking drugs. Or have another bottle of this classic Eola Pinot. This is simply the finest Pinot Noir made today.
               <em>
                $29 to $59
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #11.Domaine Des Ardoisieres 2010 Argile Blanc, Savoie
              </h3>
              <p>
               The flavors and scents of this wine are truly transformative, pushing to a poetic beauty thats staggering. Ill try to make sense of it in words, but this wine truly defies both words and logic.
               <a class="internal-link post-link post-17418" href="http://www.vinology.com/domaine-des-ardoisieres-2010-argile-blanc-savoie/" title="Domaine Des Ardoisieres 2010 Argile Blanc, Savoie">
                Full wine review here.
               </a>
               This was offered by Bacchus Selections when I was their National Sommelier and it no longer available, except in wine classes.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #10. Cantina Terlan 2011 Vorberg Pinot Bianco Riserva, Alto Adige
              </h3>
              <p>
               Lemon sorbet and beeswax are the core notes in this bottle that manages the deft feat of being both linear and lush. This is a quiet wine that needs careful reflection. A varied palette of flavors and textures are at play in this wine, far above any other white wine tasted this year.
               <em>
                $19 to $36
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #9.Celler Cal Pla 2009 Planots Porrera, Priorat
              </h3>
              <p>
               Wild and undomesticated under a sophisticated layer of French oak. This bottle growls and speaks its own language, or possibly Catalan.
               <em>
                $86 to $95
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #8. Andrew Will 2010 Champoux Vineyard Red, Horse Heaven Hills
              </h3>
              <p>
               This bottle from Andrew Will Vineyards is impeccability tailored in a Bordeaux style. Subtle and complex, it stitches a carnal bed of fruit into a Downton Abbey level of structural perfection.
               <em>
                $50 to $60
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #7.Huerta de Albala 2006 Taberner #1
              </h3>
              <p>
               The greatest unknown in a list of unknowns. IfChateauneuf du Pape and Barossa Valley had a love child, it would be this bottle. Primarily Syrah with the balance of Cab and Merlot, this smells like sweat, smoked meat, and patchouli. Thats a BBQ I dont want to be at, butin a wine its magical.$49 to $93
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #6.Cos 2007 Maldafrica
              </h3>
              <p>
               A very reliable test for the cult status of any wine is to drop a millimeter onto Zach Morris tongue (the Wine School instructor, NOT the fictional TV character). If his eyes turn glassy and he starts talking about how many vintages he has in his wine cellar, the test is positive. This Cabernet from Cos keeps him rambling incoherently for nearly an hour.
               <em>
                $31 to $43
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #5.Chateau Montelena 2007 Estate Cabernet Sauvignon, Calistoga
              </h3>
              <p>
               The Montelena Estate Cabernet is sinewy with tight and clear blackberry cut with burnt cedar. This is the first growth of Napa Valley and requires at least five years of cellaring.
               <em>
                $95-$150
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #4.Feudi di San Gregorio 2007 Serpico
              </h3>
              <p>
               An exemplary bottle of Aglianico from Campania.Aromas of crushed violets, cherry and licorice give way to burnt cedar and mineral. The palate offers dark fruit, wet stone, and cocoa that moves deeply into mineral until it veers toward fresh sage and dried fruit.
               <a class="internal-link post-link post-90158" href="http://www.vinology.com/feudi-di-san-gregorio-2007-serpico/" title="Feudi di San Gregorio 2007 Serpico">
                Full wine review here.
               </a>
               
               <em>
                $39 to $83
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #3.Contino 2007 Gran Reserva Rioja
              </h3>
              <p>
               The experience begins with a captivating aroma of cola, rust, and blackberry. The attack offers lean but substantial tannins that leads into notes of blackberry cobbler, burning tobacco and ocean air.
               <a class="internal-link post-link post-31614" href="http://www.vinology.com/contino-2007-gran-reserva-rioja/" title="Contino 2007 Gran Reserva Rioja">
                Full wine review here.
               </a>
               <em>
                $44to $95
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #2.Chappellet 2009 Pritchard Hill Cabernet Sauvignon, Napa Valley
              </h3>
              <p>
               Lush texture and a dense core of thick black fruit are balanced by a freshness that balances between mountain air and wild fennel. The benchmark for Napa mountain wine.
               <em>
                $85 to $120
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <h3>
               #1. M. Chapoutier 2006 Le Pavillon Ermitage
              </h3>
              <p>
               Whatever your religion, you can add one more demigod or angel to the pantheon. This wine is an unknowable force of grace and power. Its far and above the best wine we drank this year at the Wine School (and thats a year that included an 86 Margaux and a 03Lafite Rothschild).
               <em>
                $149 to $174
               </em>
              </p>
             </div>
