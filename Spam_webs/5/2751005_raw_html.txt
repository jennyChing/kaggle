     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The Ultimate Collection
       </b>
      </i>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Box_set" title="Box set">
       box set
      </a>
      by American recording artist
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">
       Michael Jackson
      </a>
      . It was released on November 16, 2004 by
      <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">
       Epic Records
      </a>
      and consist of four audio
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_Disc" title="Compact Disc">
       CDs
      </a>
      and one
      <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">
       DVD
      </a>
      . Selling over a million copies,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAA_certification_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-RIAA_certification-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the DVD marked the first appearance of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Live_in_Bucharest:_The_Dangerous_Tour" title="Live in Bucharest: The Dangerous Tour">
        Live in Bucharest: The Dangerous Tour
       </a>
      </i>
      , which was later released as an individual title on July 25, 2005. The album has been certified Platinum by RIAA for the shipments of at least 1 million units in the USA.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Album_information">
       Album information
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Ultimate_Collection_(Michael_Jackson_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Album information">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Much of the music is drawn from the height of Jackson's career, particularly from the principal seven albums:
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Off_the_Wall_(album)" title="Off the Wall (album)">
        Off the Wall
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Thriller_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Thriller (Michael Jackson album)">
        Thriller
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Bad_(album)" title="Bad (album)">
        Bad
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dangerous_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Dangerous (Michael Jackson album)">
        Dangerous
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/HIStory:_Past,_Present_and_Future,_Book_I" title="HIStory: Past, Present and Future, Book I">
        HIStory: Past, Present and Future, Book I
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Blood_on_the_Dance_Floor:_HIStory_in_the_Mix" title="Blood on the Dance Floor: HIStory in the Mix">
        Blood on the Dance Floor: HIStory in the Mix
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Invincible_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Invincible (Michael Jackson album)">
        Invincible
       </a>
      </i>
      . Notable tracks on the box set include the first release of the
      <a href="/wiki/Demo_(music)" title="Demo (music)">
       demos
      </a>
      of songs such as "
      <a href="/wiki/P.Y.T._(Pretty_Young_Thing)" title="P.Y.T. (Pretty Young Thing)">
       P.Y.T. (Pretty Young Thing)
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/Shake_Your_Body_(Down_to_the_Ground)" title="Shake Your Body (Down to the Ground)">
       Shake a Body
      </a>
      ", "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheater_(Michael_Jackson_song)" title="Cheater (Michael Jackson song)">
       Cheater
      </a>
      " and the original demo of "
      <a href="/wiki/We_Are_the_World" title="We Are the World">
       We Are the World
      </a>
      " featuring Jackson as a soloist. The set also contains 8 unreleased songs, including "In the Back", "Beautiful Girl", "The Way You Love Me" and "We've Had Enough".
     </p>
     <p>
      The song "The Way You Love Me" was updated for the posthumous album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Michael_(album)" title="Michael (album)">
        Michael
       </a>
      </i>
      with the title "
      <a href="/wiki/(I_Like)_The_Way_You_Love_Me" title="(I Like) The Way You Love Me">
       (I Like) The Way You Love Me
      </a>
      ", and was released as a single on July 8, 2011.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Rarities">
       Rarities
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Ultimate_Collection_(Michael_Jackson_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rarities">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       The Ultimate Collection
      </i>
      box set also contains songs previously out of print:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       "
       <a href="/wiki/Enjoy_Yourself_(The_Jacksons_song)" title="Enjoy Yourself (The Jacksons song)">
        Enjoy Yourself
       </a>
       " is 15 seconds longer than the album version.
      </li>
      <li>
       The full version of "
       <a href="/wiki/You_Can%27t_Win_(song)" title="You Can't Win (song)">
        You Can't Win
       </a>
       " was only available on the 12-inch single.
      </li>
      <li>
       "Someone in the Dark" was first released as a two-part song on the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/E.T._the_Extra-Terrestrial_(album)" title="E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (album)">
         E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial
        </a>
       </i>
       double LP, and re-issued on the 2001 special edition of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Thriller_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Thriller (Michael Jackson album)">
         Thriller
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Both the early version of "
       <a href="/wiki/Dangerous_(Michael_Jackson_song)" title="Dangerous (Michael Jackson song)">
        Dangerous
       </a>
       " and "Monkey Business" were previously available only on the rare 2001 special edition acetate of the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Dangerous_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Dangerous (Michael Jackson album)">
         Dangerous
        </a>
       </i>
       album.
      </li>
      <li>
       "Someone Put Your Hand Out" is a
       <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">
        Pepsi
       </a>
       -supported
       <a href="/wiki/Cassette_single" title="Cassette single">
        cassette single
       </a>
       released in
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       to promote the
       <a href="/wiki/Dangerous_World_Tour" title="Dangerous World Tour">
        Dangerous World Tour
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/On_the_Line_(song)" title="On the Line (song)">
        On the Line
       </a>
       " was part of the "
       <a href="/wiki/HIStory/Ghosts" title="HIStory/Ghosts">
        Ghosts
       </a>
       " boxset released in 1997, but containing an earlier fade out.
      </li>
      <li>
       "
       <a href="/wiki/You_Are_Not_Alone" title="You Are Not Alone">
        You Are Not Alone
       </a>
       " is 15 seconds longer than the album version, featuring more vocals.
      </li>
      <li>
       "
       <a href="/wiki/We_Are_Here_to_Change_the_World" title="We Are Here to Change the World">
        We Are Here to Change the World
       </a>
       " is a previously unreleased song from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">
        Disney
       </a>
       short film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Captain_EO" title="Captain EO">
         Captain EO
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Fourteen previously unreleased songs and demos are included, such as "Beautiful Girl," "Cheater" and the demo to "We Are the World".
      </li>
     </ul>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Someone_Put_Your_Hand_Out">
       Someone Put Your Hand Out
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Ultimate_Collection_(Michael_Jackson_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Someone Put Your Hand Out">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      "Someone Put Your Hand Out" (on disc three of
      <i>
       The Ultimate Collection
      </i>
      ) is a song originally written by Michael Jackson with Teddy Riley in 1987 for the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bad_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Bad (Michael Jackson album)">
        Bad
       </a>
      </i>
      album and re-written in April 1992, having failed to make it on to Jackson's 1991 album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dangerous_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Dangerous (Michael Jackson album)">
        Dangerous
       </a>
      </i>
      . It was released May 1992 in
      <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
       Europe
      </a>
      as an exclusive
      <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">
       Pepsi
      </a>
      promotional single, to promote Jacksons upcoming
      <a href="/wiki/Dangerous_World_Tour" title="Dangerous World Tour">
       Dangerous World Tour
      </a>
      . 500,000 (source needed)(copies were made available by collecting a winning token from certain Pepsi products. It was also released as part of an exclusive Pepsi promotional pack in June 1992 throughout Europe and the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       United Kingdom
      </a>
      . The promotional pack included a Michael Jackson poster, a promotional sticker, a press release about Jacksons upcoming Dangerous World Tour and a
      <a href="/wiki/Cassette_single" title="Cassette single">
       cassette single
      </a>
      of "Someone Put Your Hand Out". In 1993 the song was also planned to be released in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      as a promotional
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CD" title="CD">
       CD
      </a>
      for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Walkman" title="Sony Walkman">
       Sony Walkman
      </a>
      . Customers who purchased a special Michael Jackson signature Sony Walkman would receive a bonus CD featuring "Someone Put Your Hand Out" as well as other rare Jackson songs and remixes of his hits. However, this promotion was withdrawn following the
      <a href="/wiki/1993_child_sexual_abuse_accusations_against_Michael_Jackson" title="1993 child sexual abuse accusations against Michael Jackson">
       1993 child sexual abuse accusations against Michael Jackson
      </a>
      .
     </p>
