       <div class="post-1602 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-uncategorized category-vintage-look tag-557 tag-fashion tag-new-year tag-new-years-resolution tag-resolutions tag-style tag-vintage" id="post-1602">
        <h2>
         Why You Should Resolve to Wear More Vintage in 2015
        </h2>
        <div class="wpfblike" style="height: 40px;">
         <fb:like action="like" colorscheme="light" href="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/blog/why-you-should-resolve-to-wear-more-vintage-in-2015" layout="default" send="false" show_faces="true" width="400">
         </fb:like>
        </div>
        <p>
         We all know the first week in January is a struggle. Everyone plans to diet and take more exercise after the indulgences of Christmas, but it isnt easy. There are still unfinished boxes of chocolates and half a Christmas cake left in the cupboard and were all feeling a bit fed up about returning to work and the weather, so new years resolutions really only seem to kick in about now.
        </p>
        <p>
         Feeling uninspired about dieting and getting down the gym? (me too). In that case I have a new resolution for you:
         <strong>
          wear more vintage this year
         </strong>
         . Here are some reasons why wearing vintage will make you feel fantastic:
        </p>
        <h2>
         1. Vintage makes you feel special
        </h2>
        <p>
         Its oh-so-easy to fall into the habit of wearing clothes because theyre comfortable. We all love to wear jeans and a T-shirt sometimes, but every day? Each morning when I do the school run the mums look as much in uniform as the kids. Jeans, winter boots and some kind of dark jumper, or jeans, flip flops and t-shirts in the summer. Theres nothing wrong with that of course, but wouldnt it be nice to stand out a little? For someone to comment on how much they love your jacket? Or the earrings youre wearing?
        </p>
        <p>
         In that case I recommend adding some vintage to your wardrobe! You dont need to completely re-style yourself to start wearing vintage, you can mix and match vintage with modern clothes for a really great look. How about a cute
         <a href="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/vintage/Womens/1960s/page1.html" target="_blank">
          60s minidress
         </a>
         over leggings and winter boots? Or a
         <a href="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/vintage/Womens/Blouses/page1.html" target="_blank">
          bold printed 1950s blouse
         </a>
         peeking out from underneath your jumper?
        </p>
        <p>
         It can even be as simple as adding a
         <a href="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/vintage/Womens/Bags/page1.html" target="_blank">
          quirky vintage bag
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/vintage/Womens/Jewellery/page1.html" target="_blank">
          necklace
         </a>
         to clothes you already have. Youll be amazed at the difference it makes!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_1610" style="width: 460px">
         <a href="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/view/7872/60s+Contrast+Bib+Dress.html" target="_blank">
          <img alt="1960s minidress" class="wp-image-1610 size-large" height="600" src="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Ex7872189-061014010539PM-768x1024.jpg" width="450"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          This 60s mini dress would look great with leggings and some knee-high black boots
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         2. Vintage is oh-so-affordable
        </h2>
        <p>
         Let me just get one thing straight  buying vintage isnt the same as buying from a charity shop. You dont expect a t-shirt for 10p. If you want to get quality vintage clothes from 40, 50, 60 years ago you will have to pay for them, but nowhere as much as you would if you were buying the equivalent item new now.
        </p>
        <p>
         Quality of clothes was generally better back in the 1950s and 60s: fabrics were nicer and a lot of items were tailor made. This means youre getting a much better quality of clothes than you might do if you spent the same amount in one of the big fast fashion high street shops. Can you imagine a dress from Tesco still being worn in 50 years time? Seems unlikely doesnt it? Thats the difference in quality Im talking about.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_1612" style="width: 460px">
         <a href="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/view/6757/Brown+Gold+Satin+50s+Dress.html" target="_blank">
          <img alt="1950s evening dress" class="wp-image-1612 size-large" height="600" src="http://www.revivalvintage.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Ex2675739-301114030259PM-768x1024.jpg" width="450"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Look at the beautiful brocade fabric used in this 1950s evening dress
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         3. Vintage is unique
        </h2>
        <p>
         The big high street giants (Next, Topshop, ASOS, H&amp;M etc) literally make thousands of each design each season, so the odds of turning up to a wedding wearing the same dress as another guest are pretty high. You dont get that with vintage. Fewer copies of each design were made, and that plus the ravages of time means that youre very unlikely to see anyone wearing the same vintage dress as you!
        </p>
        <p>
         Plus, when asked where you got your dress from its lovely to be able to say its a vintage 1950s dress secure in the knowledge that they cant get straight online and buy exactly the same dress (in the sales when you bought it full price).
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          There are lots of other reasons to wear vintage (its recycling after all and great for the environment!), but the main one is that itll make you feel great! So embrace vintage for 2015! If you have any questions or need any tips just let us know, well be happy to try and help!
         </em>
        </p>
