          <h1>
           3 AWESOME SMARTPHONE PHOTO FEATURES TO MAKE YOUR VACATION BETTER
          </h1>
          <div class="metadata">
           <div class="published">
            Jul 3 2014 5:07 AM
           </div>
          </div>
         </header>
         <div class="sharebar-wrapper">
          <div class="sharebar">
           <!-- AddThis Button BEGIN -->
           <div addthis:title="3 AWESOME SMARTPHONE PHOTO FEATURES TO MAKE YOUR VACATION BETTER" addthis:url="http://www.blacks.ca/inspiration/3-smartphone-features-for-vacation" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style">
            <a class="addthis_button_facebook_like" fb:like:layout="button_count">
            </a>
            <a class="addthis_button_tweet">
            </a>
            <a class="addthis_counter addthis_pill_style">
            </a>
           </div>
           <!-- AddThis Button END -->
          </div>
         </div>
         <p class="taxonomy-field">
          <span class="name">
           Pick  Section:
          </span>
          <a href="/inspiration/section/seasonal">
           Seasonal
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img height="1200" src="/inspiration/Media/Default/SmartphoneVacation/16x20_summercollage.jpg" width="1200"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Your smartphone has three awesome, under-appreciated photo features, making it an ideal companion on your upcoming vacation.
         </p>
         <p>
          There are lots of good reasons
          <strong>
           not
          </strong>
          to bring your smartphone on vacation or leave it shut off, especially if youre traveling outside Canada for your trip: it might get lost or stolen, theres uncertainty over roaming rates, spotty connectivity, and it could just be a distraction from enjoying all the wonderful sights, sounds, and tastes youll experience.
         </p>
         <p>
          But heres why you should bring your smartphone along: its the best friend a photographer could have.
         </p>
         <!--more-->
         <h1>
          PANORAMIC PHOTOS
         </h1>
         <p>
          <img height="489" src="/inspiration/Media/Default/SmartphoneVacation/photosphere-create.jpg" width="1200"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          While some traditional cameras offer panoramic images, theyre few and far between compared to most modern smartphones having panoramic features baked right in. If youre thinking of using a DSLR for this, youll often require additional software to digitally glue the pieces of your pano together (not fun!). Smartphonography to the resuce! Simply select the panoramic mode on your phone and then using either a sideways sweeping motion to capture the scene in front of you, or a slightly staggered one-shot-at-a-time approach (every camera app is a little different). The benefit of a
          <a href="http://beta.blacks.ca/products/prints/panoramic">
           panoramic picture
          </a>
          is being able to capture an enormously wide scene in one image  with some apps you can even capture an astounding 360!
         </p>
         <p>
          For more info and apps:
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
          </span>
          
          <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
          </span>
          Check out
          <a href="http://www.google.ca/maps/about/contribute/photosphere/" target="_blank">
           Photosphere for Android
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
          </span>
          
          <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
          </span>
          Download
          <a href="http://photosynth.net/create.aspx" target="_blank">
           Photosynth for Windows Phone or iOS
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
          </span>
          
          <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
          </span>
          Download
          <a href="http://appworld.blackberry.com/webstore/content/25014206/?countrycode=CA&amp;lang=en" target="_blank">
           360 Panorama for BlackBerry 10
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h1>
          GEOTAGGING
         </h1>
         <p>
          <img height="1053" src="/inspiration/Media/Default/SmartphoneVacation/ArubaMap.jpg" width="1083"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Another amazing feature of your smartphone is the built-in GPS! And no, were not talking about live maps or driving directions (though those can also come in quite handy on vacation too).
         </p>
         <p>
          Did you know that your phone records the exact location coordinates of every single picture you take? Services like Instagram, Google Maps, Flickr, Adobe Lightroom, iPhoto and others can make use of this info, called "geotags". You can use geotags to plot all the photos from a given place or landmark on a map, letting you see what other people have taken in that same spot!
         </p>
         <p>
          Looking at geotagged photos is a wonderful way to glean inspiration from other photographers or just to research a spot in advance to decide if you want to go. Geotags are also helpful to remember precisely where you took a photo so that you dont have to constantly jot down notes in your phone.
         </p>
         <p>
          The most jaw-dropping addition to any Photobook you make of your travels is definitely a map that shows where all your photos were taken! Your friends will be amazed, trust us!
         </p>
         <h1>
          POSTCARDS
         </h1>
         <p>
          You can send custom, personalized postcards right from your smartphone! Why buy a tacky postcard at a gift shop when you can make your own personalized version? Whether its a virtual card you send electronically or a physical card in the mail, you can do both right on your smartphone. If youre worried about roaming rates, find the closest coffee shop with free WiFi and connect before you send your card.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img height="800" src="/inspiration/Media/Default/SmartphoneVacation/photopop.jpg" width="1200"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          For physical cards, theres no better option than the ridiculously amazing
          <a href="http://beta.blacks.ca/Blacks_photo_app">
           BLACKS App
          </a>
          (yes, we're a little biased). You can make a unique six-image accordion-style card  when was the last time you saw a postcard that had six different pictures? This makes it easy to tell the story of your trip as well as add custom text. And because your card is printed and shipped in Canada to Canadian addresses, you dont have to worry about international mail rates or slow delivery if you order from outside the country. Heck, mail one to yourself so you have a souvenir waiting in your mailbox when you get home!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img height="640" src="/inspiration/Media/Default/SmartphoneVacation/triple-apps.jpg" width="1400"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          For virtual/digital postcards, there are a ton of great apps for dressing up your photos in unique, elegant ways that you can email to your friends or post on social media. Two of our favourites are
          <a href="http://madewithover.com/" target="_blank">
           Over
          </a>
          and
          <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/ca/app/rhonna-designs/id667819594?mt=8" target="_blank">
           Rhonna Designs
          </a>
          . Both apps allow you to overlay your photo with a variety of quirky, modern designs and fonts that look amazing.
         </p>
