      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       In Defense Of Scarlett Johansson Starring In 'Ghost In The Shell'
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->
      <div class="mninst">
      </div>
      <div class="comment_links">
       <a class="scroll_to post_your_comment" href="#comment_reply">
        Comment Now
       </a>
       <div class="comments_follow followable_block">
        <div class="followable_block initializing" data-object-json="{&quot;title&quot;:&quot;In Defense Of Scarlett Johansson Starring In 'Ghost In The Shell'&quot;,&quot;avatar_src&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;follow_count&quot;:&quot;&quot;}" data-target-id="blogAndPostId/blog/post/2595-14754" data-target-name="" data-target-type="article" data-tracking-link-name="articleLink_ArticleCommentsFollow" data-type-advoice="false">
         <div class="follow_unireg">
          <a class="follow_unireg_link" href="javascript://follow">
           <span class="option_follow">

     <div class="article_body article" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         Under normal circumstances,
         <a href="http://variety.com/2015/film/news/scarlett-johansson-signs-on-to-star-in-dreamworks-ghost-in-the-shell-exclusive-1201320788/" target="_blank">
          the news
         </a>
         that
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/scarlett-johansson/">
          Scarlett Johansson
         </a>
         being cast as the lead in DreamWorks upcoming live-action adaptation of
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         would be a winfor the film, for Ms. Johansson, and for the industry as a whole. The actress had a terrific 2014, co-starring in
         <a href="/companies/walt-disney/">
          Walt Disney's
         </a>
         <span class="quotecard_hook" data-exchange="NYSE" data-naturalid="fred/company/1314" data-quotes-closing="116.44" data-quotes-now="118.05" data-ticker="DIS" data-type="organization">
          <span class="wrapper">
           <a href="/companies/walt-disney/">
            Walt Disney's
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <em>
          Captain America: The Winter Soldier,
         </em>
         starring in A24s
         <em>
          Under the Skin
         </em>
         , and headlining Luc Bessons
         <em>
          Lucy
         </em>
         for Universal/
         <a href="/companies/comcast/">
          Comcast
         </a>
         <span class="quotecard_hook" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-naturalid="fred/company/1046" data-quotes-closing="63.18" data-quotes-now="63.7" data-ticker="CMCSA" data-type="organization">
          <span class="wrapper">
           <a href="/companies/comcast/">
            Comcast
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         Corp.to $458 million on a $40m budget. Oh, and she also starred in Spike Jonzes
         <em>
          Her
         </em>
         (a December 2013 gem which made most of its money in 2014) and cameoed in Jon Favreaus
         <em>
          Chef
         </em>
         purely out of the goodness of her heart.It is important and newsworthy that a female actor who has shown real box office drawing power in a genre film getting rewarded with a leading role in another big-budget action film. But the catch is that
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         is based on a Japanese graphic novel and Japanese animated feature. Ms. Johansson will apparently be playing a character named Kusanagi. So on the surface, the
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         remake seems to be a case of American/white actors starring in a story not just originating from Japanese source material but involving characters who for all-intents-and-purposes are supposed to be Japanese in story set in a futuristic Japan. So its good news,
         <a href="http://www.hitfix.com/news/ghost-in-the-shell-casts-scarlett-johansson-as-their-lead-what-were-they-thinking?postId=593849&amp;socialTitle=&amp;utm_campaign=vrl&amp;utm_content=377-option-b&amp;utm_medium=fnpg-hitfix&amp;utm_source=facebook.com&amp;utm_term=1420493707983567" target="_blank">
          bad
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.hitfix.com/news/ghost-in-the-shell-casts-scarlett-johansson-as-their-lead-what-were-they-thinking?postId=593849&amp;socialTitle=&amp;utm_campaign=vrl&amp;utm_content=377-option-b&amp;utm_medium=fnpg-hitfix&amp;utm_source=facebook.com&amp;utm_term=1420493707983567" target="_blank">
          news
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://tinypic.com?ref=66khgp" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Image and video hosting by TinyPic" border="0" src="http://i59.tinypic.com/66khgp.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         For a moment lets put aside whether or not the world needs a live-action
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         movie (Im not a fan of the animated filmbut its been awhile) and whether or not Rupert Sanders (
         <em>
          Snow White and the
          <a href="/companies/huntsman/">
           Huntsman
          </a>
          <span class="quotecard_hook" data-exchange="NYSE" data-naturalid="fred/company/2081" data-quotes-closing="21.14" data-quotes-now="21.56" data-ticker="HUN" data-type="organization">
           <span class="wrapper">
            <a href="/companies/huntsman/">
             Huntsman
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </em>
         ) should be the guy to direct it. We have one very positive development perhaps negated or at least mitigated by one somewhat negative development. We have a female movie star getting the same kind of opportunities to headline a major action picture that is afforded to (usually white) male action stars (or often mostly unknown white male actors) as a matter of course.Scarlett Johansson getting a big-ticket leading role of this nature should not be taken for granted even while any number of male-centric would-be blockbusters are headlined by glorified unknowns every single year.
         <em>
          Lucy
         </em>
         s $44 million debut weekend is the kind of opening that any number of male would-be stars would kill for outside of safe franchises. Johanssons opportunity may be so obviously deserved as to be comical, but it still matters and it is still worth celebrating in an industry where Hollywood reacts to Shailene Woodleys
         <em>
          Divergent
         </em>
         being a hit by letting Theo James potentially
         <a href="http://www.hitfix.com/news/divergents-theo-james-taking-over-for-kate-beckinsale-in-underworld-franchise">
          take over Sonys
          <em>
           Underworld
          </em>
          franchise
         </a>
         from Kate Beckinsale.
        </p>
        <p>
         But can I align my excitement over a big-budget female-centric sci-fi action fantasy with the fact that
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         appears to be a Japanese-set adventure starring (at this juncture) white actors? First and foremost, Scarlett Johansson is a big movie star and for a film that will cost what
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         will probably cost, you arguably need a put butts in the theater seats movie star to justify said investment. Sadly Devon Aoki or Rinko Kikuchi dont fill the bill at this juncture. As I discussed with Ridley Scotts
         <em>
          Exodus
         </em>
         , which by the way just crossed $200 million worldwide despite flopping in America, the need to cast a star of Christian Bales stature as Moses did not necessarily negate the possibility of casting ethnically-correct actors in pretty much any other role in the film. Thus could arguably be the case for a Japan-set
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         which cast Johansson as an American and otherwise filled the cast with Japanese movie stars. Think
         <em>
          47 Ronin
         </em>
         (which co-starred Keanu Reeves), but hopefully much cheaper.
        </p>
        <p>
         All of that is based on the notion that
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         will be a movie technically set in Japan with characters who are supposed to be living in Japan. While
         <em>
          Edge of Tomorrow
         </em>
         for examplewas based on a Japanese novel entitled
         <em>
          All You Need Is Kill
         </em>
         , there was no attempt to trumpet that fact in the marketing while selling a movie starring
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/tom-cruise/">
          Tom Cruise
         </a>
         and Emily Blunt. If
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         follows suit, I would argue the theoretical offense is perhaps lesser. It would simply be a big-budget American sci-fi movie based on a Japanese graphic novel. It would be akin, although obviously not identical in a cultural sense, to the recent Ken Watanabe-starring
         <em>
          Unforgiven
         </em>
         remake or the various American
         <em>
          Godzilla
         </em>
         movies. Its not a cure-all situation, but I would argue its different than what amounts to glorified yellow-face, where Johansson and her fellow non-Japanse actors bounce around Japan while partaking in a Japanese adventure andtheoretically playing Japanese characters.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now it is possible that I am bending over backward to justify something that would normally make me roll my eyes because it also represents something that I would normally applaud. In a perfect world, we would have more than enough global movie stars of all races and ethnicities so that DreamWorks could green-light a big-budget adaptation of
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         and fill out the cast with Japanese actors. And if the likes of Rila Fukushima and Tao Okamotoarent given the kind of opportunities in which to become movie stars then they will never be bankable enough to snag roles like this. I would in that sense argue that the problem is a lack of diversity among genuine movie stars as opposed to the fact that one of the few box office-draw actresses who could headline such a film by herself happens to be white. At the risk of being accused of selective outrage, I take less offense at Scarlett Johansson (a proven movie star with real box office pull) starring as the lead in
         <em>
          Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         than I do with Rooney Mara (great actress, but not a movie star) playing Native American Tiger Lilly in Warner Bros. (Time Warner Inc.)
         <em>
          Pan
         </em>
         prequel that already stars Hugh Jackman.
        </p>
        <p>
         So Yay! toScarlet Johansson starring in her own big-budget sci-fi action spectacular after proving her box office muscle and/or reaffirming her thespian bonofides in the likes of
         <em>
          Captain America: The Winter Soldier
         </em>
         ,
         <em>
          Lucy
         </em>
         , and
         <em>
          Under the Skin
         </em>
         . But Nay?! tothe film in question being
         <em>
          The Ghost in the Shell
         </em>
         , which is a perhaps unnecessary remake of an Asian film that will star Ms. Johansson as a character who was Japanese in the source material. Can I, as someone who has complained about this kind of thing over the years, merely ignore what I dont like about this specific casting to celebrate what I feel is worth celebrating? Or maybe Im just a raging hypocrite who would rather just celebrate a female-centric action blockbuster at the expense of ignoring/justifying a pretty blatant case of racebending. But just in case, lets end this on something I think we can all agree with: It is deeply pathetic that in 2015 the gender representation in mainstream cinema is so uneven that a mega movie star like Scarlett Johansson getting the lead in a big-budget sci-fi spectacle counts as a progressive development, one groundbreaking enough that I am willing to hold my nosein order to celebrate.
        </p>

          </a>
          .
         </em>
        </p>
