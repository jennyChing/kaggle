         <h1>
          Crab Apple Crazy  A Collection of Recipes Around the Web
         </h1>
         <p>
          I have two crab apple trees in my yard. They are loaded to the point of exploding! Time to find some crab apple recipes so they dont go to waste. A crab apple is two inches around or smaller. They are more tart than standard apples. I use my crab apples just like regular apples in recipes and they always turn out just as tasty.
          <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/crab-apple-recipes/crab-apple/" rel="attachment wp-att-4732">
           <img alt="crab apple" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4732" height="338" src="http://preparednessmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/crab-apple.jpg" width="450"/>
          </a>
          Last year I made a couple big batches of my favorite apple pie filling and canned it up. Weve been eating on them all year. I have two jars left so its time to get busy. By now you know that I love to buy in bulk. My crab apples are even better  theyre free! Now, what to do with all those apples?
         </p>
         <h2>
          How to Freeze
         </h2>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           Whole crab apples, crab apple puree and crab apple juice will freeze well.
          </span>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           To freeze whole crab apples, rinse them and remove stem and blossom ends. Freeze in a single layer on a cookie sheet. When frozen, transfer to freezer bags and use within three months. Juice and
          </span>
          puree should be put into glass or plastic freezer storage containers with airtight lids.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Crab Apple Recipes From Around the Web
          <span id="more-4731">
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          So get out there, find a crab apple tree and make one of these one of these recipes. Once you look for a tree, youll find them everywhere.
          <a href="http://nchfp.uga.edu/how/can_02/crab_apple_spiced2.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://nchfp.uga.edu/how/can_02/crab_apple_spiced2.html', 'Make Spiced Crab Apples');" target="_blank" title="National Center for Home Food Prreservation">
           Make
           <em>
            Spiced Crab Apples
           </em>
          </a>
          , leave the stems on and can them using the water bath method. These
          <em>
           <a href="http://dinnerwithjulie.com/2010/09/08/slow-roasted-crabapples-pickled-chutney/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://dinnerwithjulie.com/2010/09/08/slow-roasted-crabapples-pickled-chutney/', 'Slow Roasted Crab Apples');" target="_blank" title="Dinner With Julie">
            Slow Roasted Crab Apples
           </a>
          </em>
          look fabulous  also look at her recipes for
          <em>
           Slow Cooker Crab Apple Chutney
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           Spiced Pickled Crab Apples
          </em>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.mennonitegirlscancook.ca/2008/08/crab-apple-butter.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mennonitegirlscancook.ca/2008/08/crab-apple-butter.html', 'Crab Apple Butter');" target="_blank" title="Mennonite Girls Can Cook">
            Crab Apple Butter
           </a>
          </em>
          is another tasty and easy way to use your crab apple abundance. Heres another recipe from Lovely Greens 
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.lovelygreens.com/2011/10/spiced-crab-apple-butter_04.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lovelygreens.com/2011/10/spiced-crab-apple-butter_04.html', 'Spiced Crab Apple Butter');" target="_blank" title="Lovely Greens - Spiced Crab Apple Butter">
            Spiced Crab Apple Butter
           </a>
          </em>
          The
          <a href="http://www.uaf.edu/files/ces/publications-db/catalog/hec/FNH-00109.pdf" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'download', 'http://www.uaf.edu/files/ces/publications-db/catalog/hec/FNH-00109.pdf');" target="_blank" title="Crab Apple Recipes">
           University of Alaska  Fairbanks
          </a>
          put together a collection of recipes and other tips for freezing, drying and storing crab apples. These include making
          <em>
           syrup, fruit leather, apple butter, jelly and muffins.
          </em>
          And here is my favorite
          <strong>
           Apple Pie Filling
          </strong>
          made with crab apples. I like to use
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B009C9BSGK/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B009C9BSGK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=prepamama-20" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B009C9BSGK/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B009C9BSGK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=prepamama-20', 'Clear Jel');">
           Clear Jel
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=prepamama-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B009C9BSGK" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          as my thickening agent for pies because it tolerates the high temperatures used during the canning process. I also like to use this handy
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004NPM3NU/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B004NPM3NU&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=prepamama-20&amp;linkId=A62X4P3YDD6F4NDR" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004NPM3NU/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B004NPM3NU&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=prepamama-20&amp;linkId=A62X4P3YDD6F4NDR', 'Apple Corer &amp; Slicer');">
           Apple Corer &amp; Slicer
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=prepamama-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B004NPM3NU" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          to really get it done quickly. This one tool saves me a ton of time in the kitchen each fall as I prepare my apple pie filling for the year.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
         </p>
         <div class="zlrecipe-container-border" id="zlrecipe-container-7">
          <div class="serif zlrecipe" id="zlrecipe-container" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
           <div id="zlrecipe-innerdiv">
            <div class="item b-b">
             <div class="zlrecipe-print-link fl-r">
              <a class="butn-link" href="javascript:void(0);" onclick="zlrPrint('zlrecipe-container-7', 'http://preparednessmama.com/wp-content/plugins/ziplist-recipe-plugin/'); return false" title="Print this recipe">

              Crab Apple Pie Filling

            </div>
            <div class="zlmeta zlclear">
             <div class="fl-l width-50">
              <p id="zlrecipe-rating" itemprop="aggregateRating" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/AggregateRating">

               <span class="rating rating-4">
                <span itemprop="ratingValue">

             <div class="zlclear">
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="img-desc-wrap">
             <p class="t-a-c hide-print">
              <img alt="Crab Apple Pie Filling" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://preparednessmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/crab-apple-pie-filling.jpg" title="Crab Apple Pie Filling"/>
             </p>
             <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
              <p class="summary italic">
               Spoon 1 quart of Crab Apple Pie Filling into a pastry lined 9 inch pie plate. Add 1 tablespoon of butter. Cut slits into top crust, seal and flute edge. Bake in 375 degree oven for 45 minutes or until pastry is golden. Makes 8 servings
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
             Ingredients
            </p>
            <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 12 pounds of apples, washed and cored -  no need to peel
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 5 1/2 cups sugar
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 1 1/2 cups Clear Jel
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 1 tablespoon cinnamon (but I always put more)
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg (again more to taste)
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 1/8 teaspoon ground cloves (to taste)
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 5 cups apple juice
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 2 1/2 cups cold water
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
              - 3/4 cup lemon juice
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
             Instructions
            </p>
            <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Cut apples into 1/2 inch slices, place in ascorbic acid solution if you wish. Measure 33 cups
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              In a large pot, heat 1 gallon of water to boiling.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Add 6 cups apple slices, return to boiling and boil for 1 minute
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Using a slotted spoon, transfer apples to a large bowl, cover
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Repeat with remaining apples. Measure 24 cups
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              In a 4 qt dutch oven combine sugar, Clear Jel, cinnamon, nutmeg and cloves
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Stir in apple juice and the cold water
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens and boils.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Add lemon juice, boil one minute, stirring constantly
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Pour over apples, stirring to coat
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-10" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
