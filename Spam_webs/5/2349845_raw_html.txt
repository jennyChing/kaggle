        <p>
         In all the times I have made this fabulous version of french toast, I have never once made it for breakfast. My kids adore it (and all the pumpkin treats that fall brings) but they always seem torequest itfor
         <em>
          dinner
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Which is fine with me!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Pumpkin French Toast" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-45457" data-pin-description="pumpkin french toast ~ http://iambaker.net" height="526" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/pumpkin-french-toast.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         My husbands brothershared some freshly made maple syrup and that is what we used on the french toast. If you can ever splurge and use real maple syrup (no high fructose corn syrup allowed) I recommend it.
        </p>
        <p>
         I also use a 12 grain bread in this recipe. The main reason being that it is what we prefer to keep in the house. But I also like how well it stands up to the pumpkin seasoning and that the small grains add some texture to the egg soaked bread.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Pumpkin French Toast" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-45459" data-pin-description="pumpkin french toast ~ http://iambaker.net" height="484" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/pumpkin-french-toast-1.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 cup heavy cream
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 cup whole milk
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 cup pumpkin puree
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon pumpkin pie spice
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             4 eggs
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             16-20 pieces bread
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, pumpkin and pie spice until smooth.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Dip each side of the bread and cook on a hot, greased griddle or skillet. I have mine set to 300 degrees F.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Flip the bread over after approximately 2 minutes.
            </li>
           </ol>

         <img alt="Pumpkin French Toast" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-45460" data-pin-description="pumpkin french toast ~ http://iambaker.net" height="600" src="http://cf.iambaker.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/pumpkin-french-toast-2.jpg" width="414"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you prefer you can use all whole milk or even skim milk. After we started drinking raw milk a few years ago, I feel like I am drinking water when I drinkskim. That is totally a matter of preference and you are welcome to make an adjustment that fits your likes.
        </p>
        <p>
         We are a one loaf family, meaning that we can can easily polish off an entire loaf in one meal. This recipe is designed to accommodatethat! If you need to halve the recipe it can be done with success.
        </p>
