            <p>
             The beauty of being a professional athlete, to me, is getting paid to have fun, Davis told RedBull.com recently. You never look at it as a job.
            </p>
            <p>
             For some pro athletes, the game becomes a bottom-line business, driven by stressors to succeed and rigors of the grind outweighing the joy of playing a game every day. Not so for Davis, who stays grounded through a quest for fun and a real relaxed, chill personality.
            </p>
            <p>
             Ive loved the game of basketball since I was 3 years old, and its just been my friend ever since, my love, Davis said during a shoot in a New Orleans gym last month. And I dont plan on giving it up anytime soon. The game of basketball has  given me everything Ive got. Its all I know.

             Davis unique feature as a youth has become arguably the NBAs most iconic piece of facial hair. (James Hardens beard is also in the conversation). The New Orleans Pelicans big man was teased through high school for his unibrow. But as a star at the pinnacle of his sport, he embraces it as a big part of his public image.
            </p>
            <p>
             And now, thats how people recognize me, he said, smiling.
            </p>
            <p>
             Davis brow has taken on a life of its own. Its become a symbol of strength, his superpower characteristic, a shield of sorts for him. In Red Bull parlance, Davis derives his wings from the power of the brow.

           <div class="content-text">
            <p>
             When Davis enters a gym, he soaks it all in: the atmosphere, the intensity of the game and all the sweat, blood thats gonna be on the floor. He doesnt care whos in the game, cheering from the stands or watching on TV. He keeps a singular goal once he gets between those lines.
            </p>
            <p>
             My focus is on winning this basketball game [and] making sure that Im on my game, my teammates are on their game and we're all focused to get that win tonight. Everything that we love to do, everything we were born to dowere gonna leave right here on this floor. It definitely gets me pumped up.
            </p>
            <p>
             In his third professional season, the former No. 1 overall draft pick continues adjusting to life in the NBA. An 82-game seasonreplete with long, draining road tripsspent wearing the oppositions bulls-eye each night can take its toll, and the lifestyle isnt all glitz and glamour as it can appear to fans.
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              You come into the paint, 20 times out of 20, youre not gonna see the ball go in the rim.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             For Davis, the biggest sacrifice has been not seeing his family as much. He said, Youre so used to being with your family on Thanksgiving, Christmas, all these holidays, and now, youre spending Christmas on the road.
            </p>
            <p>
             Your teammates arent cooking; theyre worried about the same things you are. So now youre eating hotel food. Thats a sacrifice that you have to take in order to get where you want to be.
            </p>
            <p>
             Davis is the type of player you love to have on your teambut hate playing against. When hes protecting the basket, good luck. Pelicans fans know hes the leagues best rim protector, and he calls his relationship with the rim very close.
            </p>
            <p>
             I make sure nobody bothers it, when they go up for layups and dunks, said Davis, whos in the middle of a breakout season. You come into the paint, 20 times out of 20, youre not gonna see the ball go in the rim.
            </p>

            <p>
             His teammates have a rare luxury. If they blow a defensive assignmentor simply get burned guarding their manthey have an enforcer behind them that few NBA teams have. Chances are, the man wearing No. 23hes from Chicago, rememberis lurking and ready to swat away attempts from all comers.
            </p>
            <p>
             Every game, I tell my teammates: No matter what happens, you got Anthony Davis back there. And Im gonna try my best to block every shot, get every rebound. Im gonna be right there, waiting. I got your back, added Davis, the
             <a href="http://stats.nba.com/leaders/#!?StatCategory=BLK" target="_blank">
              <strong>
               league leader in blocks per game
              </strong>
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             His defensive prowess no longer overshadows Davis offensive repertoire. Hes the leagues
             <a href="http://stats.nba.com/leaders/#!?StatCategory=PTS" target="_blank">
              <strong>
               fourth-leading scorer
              </strong>
             </a>
             and
             <a href="http://stats.nba.com/leaders/#!?PerMode=Totals&amp;StatCategory=FG_PCT" target="_blank">
              <strong>
               eighth in field-goal percentage
              </strong>
             </a>
             . Hes on track to break Wilt Chamberlains player efficiency rating mark (31.8), set during the 1962-63 season. Immortals like Michael Jordan and LeBron James never have been as efficient as Davis has been this season.
            </p>
            <p>
             Just a big kid, excelling at a game hes always loved. Anthony Davis wouldnt have it any other way.
            </p>
