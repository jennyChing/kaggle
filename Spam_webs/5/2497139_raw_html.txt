        &lt;h2 class="pane-title"&gt;Preparation&lt;/h2&gt;
    
  
  &lt;div class="pane-content"&gt;
    &lt;div itemprop="instructions"&gt;&lt;div class="field-instructions"&gt;&lt;div class="field-items"&gt;&lt;div class="field-item even"&gt;&lt;p&gt;1. Pour oil into a 5- to 6-quart pan over medium-high heat; when hot, add onion, bell pepper, and garlic and stir often until limp, 5 to 7 minutes.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;2. Rinse chicken. Add broth, tomatoes, enchilada sauce, and chicken to pan; bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer, stirring occasionally, until chicken is no longer pink at the bone (cut to test), about 40 minutes.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;3. Meanwhile, in a bowl, mix flour, cornmeal, baking powder, salt, and jalapeos. In a small bowl, whisk butter into milk; stir into flour mixture until well blended. Drop batter in tablespoon portions into simmering chicken mixture; cover and simmer gently until dumplings are cooked all the way through (cut to test), 10 to 12 minutes.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;4. Ladle chicken, dumplings, and sauce equally into wide, shallow bowls and serve immediately.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;  &lt;/div&gt;
