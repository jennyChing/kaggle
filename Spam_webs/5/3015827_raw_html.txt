          <p style="margin:0 3% 0 3%;text-align:center;font-size:94%">
           <i>
            <b>
             The Plumbob
            </b>
           </i>
           <br/>
           <a class="image image-thumbnail" href="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/sims/images/c/cb/Sims3plumbob.png/revision/latest?cb=20120816220732">
            <img alt="Sims3plumbob" class="lzy lzyPlcHld " data-image-key="Sims3plumbob.png" data-image-name="Sims3plumbob.png" data-src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/sims/images/c/cb/Sims3plumbob.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/80?cb=20120816220732" height="163" onload="if(typeof ImgLzy==='object'){ImgLzy.load(this)}" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" width="80"/>
            <noscript>
             <img alt="Sims3plumbob" class="" data-image-key="Sims3plumbob.png" data-image-name="Sims3plumbob.png" height="163" src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/sims/images/c/cb/Sims3plumbob.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/80?cb=20120816220732" width="80"/>
            </noscript>
           </a>
           <br/>
           <small>
            This appears above the active Sim.
           </small>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The
          <b>
           Plumbob
          </b>
          <small>
           (sometimes spelled differently 
           <a href="#Spelling_differences">
            see below
           </a>
           )
          </small>
          is used in most titles of
          <a href="/wiki/The_Sims_(series)" title="The Sims (series)">
           <i>
            The Sims
           </i>
           series
          </a>
          (including the
          <a class="extiw" href="http://mysims.wikia.com/wiki/" title="w:c:MySims">
           <i>
            MySims
           </i>
           series
          </a>
          ) to identify the character selected and to which the player can give commands. It is also used to show the
          <a href="/wiki/Mood" title="Mood">
           mood
          </a>
          of playable Sims.      
The plumbob has become an important symbol to the franchise, and it is widely recognized by players around the world as one of the game's identities. Several game covers, logos, and special products feature plumbobs in their designs.
         </p>

         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Sims">
           The Sims
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Plumbob?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit The Sims section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          In
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/The_Sims" title="The Sims">
            The Sims
           </a>
          </i>
          , the plumbob is a fairly accurate reflection of the Sim's
          <a href="/wiki/Mood" title="Mood">
           mood
          </a>
          . If the Sim is in a very good mood, the plumbob will be a bright, deep green. As the Sim's mood gets lower, the green will become paler until it becomes a sort of neutral color. A Sim in a bad mood will have a red plumbob, and the red will get brighter and deeper as the Sim's mood gets worse.
         </p>
         <p>
          If the Sim has a date
          <a href="/wiki/Downtown_(The_Sims)" title="Downtown (The Sims)">
           Downtown
          </a>
          , in
          <a href="/wiki/Old_Town" title="Old Town">
           Old Town
          </a>
          or on
          <a href="/wiki/Vacation_Island" title="Vacation Island">
           Vacation Island
          </a>
          , the date will have a small blue plumbob (known as a DateBob) which will reflect their mood. The deeper the blue, the better their mood.
          <a href="/wiki/Fame" title="Fame">
           Famous
          </a>
          Sims will have a StarBob appear over their head, taking the form of a solid gold star. The StarBob only appears when they are inactive and will be replaced by the regular plumbob when control is switched to them. The Starbob makes it easy to identify which Sims are famous.
         </p>

          </caption>

         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Sims_2_and_The_Sims_Stories">
           The Sims 2 and The Sims Stories
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Plumbob?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit The Sims 2 and The Sims Stories section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          In
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/The_Sims_2" title="The Sims 2">
            The Sims 2
           </a>
          </i>
          , the plumbob is white if the
          <a href="/wiki/Sim" title="Sim">
           Sim
          </a>
          's
          <a href="/wiki/Aspiration_(The_Sims_2)" title="Aspiration (The Sims 2)">
           aspiration
          </a>
          bar is in platinum, which produces a very high
          <a href="/wiki/Mood" title="Mood">
           mood
          </a>
          regardless of the Sim's
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motives" title="Motives">
           motives
          </a>
          . Otherwise, it is bright green if the Sim's mood is high, and orange or bright red if it is low. If the Sim's mood is somewhere in between, it will be a shade of green-yellow reflecting the approximate level.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="/wiki/Perma-platinum" title="Perma-platinum">
           Perma-platinum
          </a>
          can be achieved through completing the Sim's
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifetime_Want" title="Lifetime Want">
           Lifetime Want
          </a>
          if the player has
          <i>
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University" title="University">
            University
           </a>
          </i>
          through
          <i>
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bon_Voyage" title="Bon Voyage">
            Bon Voyage
           </a>
          </i>
          , however if the player has
          <i>
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FreeTime" title="FreeTime">
            FreeTime
           </a>
          </i>
          perma-platinum can be reached by maximizing the lifetime aspiration bar.
         </p>
         <p>
          In
          <i>
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nightlife" title="Nightlife">
            Nightlife
           </a>
          </i>
          , the members of a group in an
          <a href="/wiki/Outing" title="Outing">
           outing
          </a>
          or a
          <a href="/wiki/Dating" title="Dating">
           date
          </a>
          partner will have a small blue plumbob over their heads. This plumbob doesn't reflect their mood, however. In
          <i>
           Open for Business
          </i>
          , business
          <a href="/wiki/Employee" title="Employee">
           employees
          </a>
          have a small spherical plumbob over their heads, which could be green, yellow, or red depending on their mood and motives. It is important to watch the color of employees' plumbobs so as to know when to give them a break, otherwise they might quit.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <blockquote class="note">
          The
          <b>
           plumbbobToggle
          </b>
          cheat can be used to hide the plumbobs, which can be useful for moviemaking or snapshots. This cheat, however, doesn't hide the small outing and employee plumbobs.
         </blockquote>

         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Sims_3">
           The Sims 3
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Plumbob?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit The Sims 3 section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          In
          <i>
           The Sims 3
          </i>
          with
          <i>
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Sims_3_World_Adventures" title="The Sims 3 World Adventures">
            The Sims 3 World Adventures
           </a>
          </i>
          the plumbob is green if everything's right, yellow if the Sim isn't good, and red if the Sim has multiple and/or potent bad moodlet(s) or low needs. There is a new meaning of the yellow color introduced by
          <i>
           World Adventures
          </i>
          . It is the subject of speculation, some think it means the Sim is in an "adventurous" mood, but most think that it is just the mood between green and red, as in
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/The_Sims_2" title="The Sims 2">
            The Sims 2
           </a>
          </i>
          .
          <span title="This section should be re-written to clarify its meaning">
           <sup>
            [
            <a href="/wiki/The_Sims_Wiki:Templates#Articles_needing_improvement" title="The Sims Wiki:Templates">
             clarification needed
            </a>
            ]
           </sup>
          </span>
         </p>
         <table class="plaintable">

         <p>
          Contrary to initial belief, a Sim's icon background changes color depending on their need levels specifically, not their mood level, unless the mood is lower than the needs. This is for the player to actively fulfill the Sim's needs or allow the Sim to autonomously fulfill them and keep the Sim's mood elated and from shifting moods. The colors shift from the mean of each need bar, from green (Good Mood), Yellow (Not very good) to red (Bad Mood).

         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Controlling_device">
           Controlling device
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Plumbob?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit Controlling device section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          In the
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/The_Sims_2_(PSP)" title="The Sims 2 (PSP)">
            The Sims 2
           </a>
          </i>
          for PlayStation Portable storyline, (events which occur after
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/The_Sims_2" title="The Sims 2">
            The Sims 2
           </a>
          </i>
          ) the player's Sim will encounter
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Dominic_Newlow" title="Dr. Dominic Newlow">
           Dr. Dominic Newlow
          </a>
          aka Dr. Dominion (the antagonist) who created a device that can create the "Green Diamond" to control other people including the player's Sim. The ending of the game
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/fourth_wall" title="wikipedia:fourth wall">
           breaks the fourth wall
          </a>
          , as he reveals that the "Green Diamond" is a mind control device used by an "Outside Force" (player).
         </p>
         <p>
          During normal gameplay other than the storyline, there doesn't seem to be any plumbob at the top of the playing Sim, but it is still controlled by the player. It seems that the player controls the Sims using the plumbob (selected Sims will have a plumbob over their heads and become controllable) which became the game mechanics until the revelation of an outside force at the end.
         </p>
         <p>
          When the game ends, the character talks to
          <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Rossum" title="Isaac Rossum">
           Isaac Rossum
          </a>
          about the "Green Diamond", but they end up agreeing that all these things about the "Outside Force" can't be true and that mad scientists can cause peoples' brains to blow up.
         </p>
         <p>
          The plumbbob once again assumes the role of a mind control device in
          <i>
           The Sims 3: Showtime
          </i>
          . Players who control a
          <a href="/wiki/Genie" title="Genie">
           genie
          </a>
          can take command of a Sim by ensorcelling them, causing a purple plumbob to appear over the targeted Sim for approximately four hours. Whether this purple plumbob is the source of the genie's control over the Sim or just a signal showing the genie's control over them is unclear, but due to the plumbob's background as a mind control device the latter is quite likely.
         </p>

         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">
           Trivia
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Plumbob?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit Trivia section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <ul>
          <li>
           In
           <i>
            <a class="extiw" href="http://spore.wikia.com/wiki/Main_Page" title="w:c:spore:Main Page">
             Spore
            </a>
           </i>
           , sometimes the plumbob will appear in the creatures' speech bubbles in the Tribal, Civilization, and Space Stages, as a reference to
           <i>
            The Sims
           </i>
           series, as they are both developed by Maxis.
           <ul>
            <li>
             Also in
             <i>
              Spore
             </i>
             , the
             <i>
              Galactic Adventures
             </i>
             expansion pack's adventure editor has a stained glass window effect that clearly has a plumbob on it.
            </li>
           </ul>
          </li>
          <li>
           In
           <i>
            <a href="/wiki/The_Sims_3:_Island_Paradise" title="The Sims 3: Island Paradise">
             The Sims 3: Island Paradise
            </a>
           </i>
           , when unlocking the
           <a href="/wiki/Hidden_island#Plumbob_Island" title="Hidden island">
            Plumbob Island
           </a>
           in
           <a href="/wiki/Isla_Paradiso" title="Isla Paradiso">
            Isla Paradiso
           </a>
           , there's a treasure chest that contains a painting called "Evolution of the Plumbob". The painting apparently shows the development design of the Plumbob from
           <i>
            The Sims
           </i>
           through
           <i>
            The Sims 3
           </i>
           .
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Spelling_differences">
           Spelling differences
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Plumbob?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit Spelling differences section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Several different spellings for this object's name appear throughout
          <i>
           The Sims
          </i>
          series, including in games, on official websites and pages, and on fan sites. For instance, in
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/The_Sims" title="The Sims">
            The Sims
           </a>
          </i>
          the name is spelled "PlumbBob," and in
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/The_Sims_2" title="The Sims 2">
            The Sims 2
           </a>
          </i>
          it is spelled "Plum Bob". SimGuruSarah has stated that the spelling used by the development team for
          <i>
           The Sims 4
          </i>
          is "plumbob", though she notes that other spellings are sometimes used accidentally.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-0">
           <a href="#cite_note-0">
            [1]
           </a>
          </sup>
          It is worth noting that this object takes its name from the
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plumb_bob" title="wikipedia:Plumb bob">
           plumb bob
          </a>
          , a weighted object hung from a string, which is used as a vertical reference line; the real-life object's name is often spelled "plumb bob" or "plumb-bob."
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Geometric_Information">
           Geometric Information
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Plumbob?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit Geometric Information section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p>
          The plumbob is displayed as a
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexagonal_bipyramid" title="wikipedia:Hexagonal bipyramid">
           hexagonal bipyramid
          </a>
          , making it a dodecahedron. It has 12 isosceles triangles, 18 edges, and 8 vertices.
         </p>
