      <p>
       When Jeter was selected by the Yankees with the sixth overall pick of the 1992 amateur draft, no one could have foreseen the career that awaited him. Five teams passed on Jeter that June: the Houston Astros, Cleveland Indians, Montreal Expos, Baltimore Orioles, and Cincinnati Reds. They selected Phil Nevin, Paul Shuey, B.J. Wallace, Jeffrey Hammonds, and Chad Mottola, respectively. While Johnny Damon, Jason Kendall, and Preston Wilson were among the other notable first round selections, Jeter was obviously the class of the draft. Ral Ibaez in the 36th round, 1,006th overall, signed by the Seattle Mariners, wasnt a bad find either.
      </p>
      <p>
       After a cup of coffee in 1995, Jeter kicked off his Hall of Fame career in style, winning the AL Rookie of the Year honors in 1996, as well as his first World Series. Helping to launch off the Yankee Dynasty, Jeter would add three more World Series rings to his collection before the calendar turned to 2001. Just to be safe, the Yankees added another World Series victory in 2009 and the longtime shortstop will do everything he can to add one more ring to his collection during the 2014 season.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://itsaboutthemoney.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/02.derek-jeter.jpg">
        <img alt="Jeter has never played a position other than shortstop." src="http://itsaboutthemoney.net/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/02.derek-jeter.jpg" style="width: 300px; height: 360px; float: left;"/>
       </a>
       While Derek Jeters defense has been a topic of some
       <a href="http://grantland.com/features/the-tragedy-derek-jeter-defense/">
        debate
       </a>
       , never quite as good as his reputation and fame would imply, it is impressive that Jeter has never played an inning at any other position. Jeter has either been on the field at shortstop or serving as the designated hitter.
      </p>
      <p>
       Jeter is the last remaining player from the Yankees teams of the 1990s. Hes retiring one year after Mariano Rivera and Andy Pettitte. Three years after Jorge Posada. Eight years after Bernie Williams.
      </p>
      <p>
       In his 19 years with the Yankees,w Jeter has hit .312/.381/.446. Although his last consecutive seasons with an .840 OPS or greater were 2006 and 2007, Jeter has just two full season with an OPS lower than .771.
      </p>
      <p>
       A member of the 3,000 hit club, one
       <a href="https://twitter.com/mtmeyers/status/433681784769744896">
        estimate
       </a>
       suggests Jeter would need to hit approximately .210 in the 2014 season over about 600 plate appearances for his career batting average to dip below .300. Outside of his injury-plagued 2013 season and his fifteen game call-up in 1995 Jeter has never posted a batting average lower than .270. If hes healthy enough to play its likely he will hit more than well enough to end his career with a batting average north of .300.
      </p>
      <p>
       While his numbers in 2,602 regular season games are impressive, Jeter has another chapter in his career: 16 postseasons. Jeter, Mr. November, has been no slouch: hes hit .308/.374/.465 in 158 postseason contests. Should the Yankees play in four playoff games in his final year, either in the Wild Card game or ALDS or some combination of both, Jeter could finish his career with an entire season worth of postseason stats. Its almost hard to believe.
      </p>
      <p>
       Derek Jeter has never won an MVP award, but hes finished in the top three among vote getters three times. Hes appeared in the All-Star Game 13 times. Hes never won a batting title but has hit at least .340 in a season twice and led the league in hits in 1999 and 2012. Hes stolen at least 20 bases eight times and hit at least 10 home runs sixteen times.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2014, Jeter, like Mariano Rivera in 2013, will try to prove himself one last time. He has to show that he can go out on top, willingly leaving the game, not being forced out due to injury or ineffectiveness. Hell do so with Carlos Beltran, Jacoby Ellsbury, Brian McCann, and Masahiro Tanaka as new teammates. Hell do so with Alex Rodriguez serving the longest suspension this side of Pete Roses lifetime ban. Derek Jeter will play out 2014, wait five years, and join the
       <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_New_York_Yankees_in_the_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame">
        fifty-one
       </a>
       other Yankees (21 inducted as such) in baseballs Hall of Fame in Cooperstown, NY.
      </p>
      <p>
       For a little more pop culture trivia, four months after Jeter was drafted, the sixth episode of the sixth season of Full House, 
       <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0584133/">
        Educating Jesse
       </a>
       , featured John Stamos reciting Walt Whitman. Stamos character, Jesse Katsopolis, returned to high school to finish his degree and attempted to impress his former English teacher. The poem he recited?
       <a href="http://www.bartleby.com/142/193.html">
        O Captain! My Captain!
       </a>
      </p>
