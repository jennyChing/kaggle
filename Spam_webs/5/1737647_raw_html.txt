          Giant Post-it world map - how we made it
         </a>
        </h1>
        <span class="submitted">
         Published on 19 Jun 2013 by Steven Wong
        </span>
        <!-- Google Code for Remarketing Tag -->
        <!--Remarketing tags may not be associated with personally identifiable information or placed on pages related to sensitive categories.

        <div class="field field-name-field-b-image field-type-image field-label-hidden">
         <div class="field-items">
          <div class="field-item even">
           <img alt="Giant Post-it world map" height="300" src="http://www.seduniatravel.com/sites/default/files/styles/620_300/public/blog/world-post-it-map-small.jpg?itok=k7qFR84s" width="620"/>

        <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
         <div class="field-items">
          <div class="field-item even">
           <p>
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.seduniatravel.com/sites/default/files/blog/world-spot-it-map-large.jpg">
               Click here for larger image 
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Sedunia Travel recently acquired the rest of the 13th floor of our building, which gave us much needed room to grow our team. But this also meant a lot of bare walls. Now looking at empty walls all day is not very inspiring so what better way is there for us to be a bit creative than to decorate it! And what is more appropriate for a travel agency than a world map. So a decision was made and the only thing cheap and easy enough to use was Post-it notes.
           </p>
           <p>
            I can't decide if this was an OCD's worst nightmare or dream - all those perfectly lined squares.
           </p>
           <p>
            Here is how we did it.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Step 1: Find an empty wall
           </h2>
           <p>
            <img alt="Empty white wall" src="/sites/default/files/blog/emtpy-wall.jpg" style="width: 620px; height: 465px;"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Look how boring and miserable this large empty wall is! We had about 5.5m x 2.5m to decorate.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Step 2: Plan the map
           </h2>
           <p>
            <img alt="Planning the world map" src="/sites/default/files/blog/planning-the-world-map.jpg" style="width: 620px; height: 332px;"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Firstly we had to decide how many notes across by how many down that can fit the wall and have the correct ratio to feature the whole map. We settled for 70 notes across and 36 notes.
           </p>
           <p>
            Next step was to print a grid on some acetate so we could overlay that over a world map. A quick photocopy and we had the world over a grid.
           </p>
           <p>
            With the help of some white correction ink and black marker, our pixelated plan all worked out. Oh and we also had to buy about RM70 worth of sticky notes.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Step 3: Stick it on the wall
           </h2>
           <p>
            <img alt="Making the map" src="/sites/default/files/blog/making-the-map.jpg" style="width: 620px; height: 765px;"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Over the course of 3 lunches and after work hours we started to build it. The hardest part was ensuring each note prefectly lined up with each other.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Yay: all done
           </h2>
           <p>
            <img alt="The Post-it world map all completed" src="/sites/default/files/blog/all-done.jpg" style="width: 620px; height: 400px;"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.seduniatravel.com/sites/default/files/blog/world-spot-it-map-large.jpg">
               Click here for larger image of the finished world map 
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Some stats for you:
           </p>
           <ul class="normal-size">
            <li>
             A total of 848 Post-its used
            </li>
            <li>
             North America: 200; South America: 62; Europe: 122; Africa &amp; Middle East: 122; Australasia: 35; Asia: 305
            </li>
            <li>
             70 across (532cm) and 36 down (273cm)
            </li>
            <li>
             Took about 7 hours
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            It will all fall down eventually so what should we build next?
           </p>
