function word_count(){
    jQuery(".word").keydown(function (e) {
        // Allow: backspace, delete, tab, escape, enter and .
        if (jQuery.inArray(e.keyCode, [46, 8, 9, 27, 13, 110, 190]) !== -1 ||
             // Allow: Ctrl+A
            (e.keyCode == 65 &amp;&amp; e.ctrlKey === true) || 
             // Allow: home, end, left, right, down, up
            (e.keyCode &gt;= 35 &amp;&amp; e.keyCode &lt;= 40)) {
                 // let it happen, don't do anything
                 return;
        }
        // Ensure that it is a number and stop the keypress
        if ((e.shiftKey || (e.keyCode &lt; 48 || e.keyCode &gt; 57)) &amp;&amp; (e.keyCode &lt; 96 || e.keyCode &gt; 105)) {
            e.preventDefault();
        }
    });
    
    jQuery('.word').on('keyup',function(){
        student_type($(this));
    });
}

        nativec =9,delux = 12 ; esl = 18;

        nativec =13,delux = 16 ; esl = 22;
    }
    
    if(word &lt; 1000 &amp;&amp; word != 0){
        var word = 1000;
    }
    if(isNaN(word)){
        var word = 0;
    }
    
    obj.closest('.pcb-inner').find('.nativec').html('&pound; '+(nativec * word/1000).toFixed(2));
    obj.closest('.pcb-inner').find('.delux').html('&pound; '+(delux * word/1000).toFixed(2));

          <div class="entry-title">
           Possible pitfalls of a PhD proposal
          </div>
         </header>
         <!-- .entry-header -->
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           For some students, writing a good PhD proposal can seem like an unnecessary precursor to actually doing a PhD; this thinking often results in poor PhD proposals. It is useful to change perspective and look at a PhD proposal as a chance to demonstrate your passion and understanding of the subject you wish to explore. Many students, that do meet the minimum criteria to be accepted as PhD candidates, actually lose their place due to a weak PhD proposal. If you are applying for a
           <a href="http://proofreadmyessay.co.uk/services/academic-proofreading/phd-proofreading">
            PhD
           </a>
           , it is a good idea to keep an eye out for the possible pitfalls of writing your proposal.
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Ensure that you clearly state your research concept, question, or problem. It will need to be persuasive and address a recognisable gap existing in the available literature.
           </li>
           <li>
            Give yourself enough time to produce the questions you will be addressing early on, these can prove just as significant as the consequent results.
           </li>
           <li>
            It is important that you check with the department you are applying to that there are staff available, and interested, in supervising your research. If no one can supervise your project, then writing your PhD proposal will be a waste of time. Contact potential supervisors ahead of time and give them a professional version of your PhD proposal for feedback.
           </li>
           <li>
            Your proposal needs to be well structured. When a proposal is full of ramblings and is poorly executed, this can indicate to the department you are applying to that your research will be conducted in a similar fashion.
           </li>
           <li>
            Make sure that the breadth of your project is sensible. Remember that there are considerable limits to the length and complexity that your project can have, to be completed to a good standard within the three years allocated.
           </li>
           <li>
            Ensure that you express your passion for your subject and topic question. This needs to be done through the coherency and conclusions you give in your proposal.
           </li>
           <li>
            Have your PhD proofread by a professional; like us!
           </li>
          </ol>
