         <p>
          <a href="http://img.wonkette.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Today-We-Are-All-On-The-Beach.jpg" rel="prettyphoto[524414]">
           <img alt="Today, We Are All On The Beach" class="size-medium wp-image-525170 alignright" height="300" src="http://img.wonkette.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Today-We-Are-All-On-The-Beach-218x300.jpg" title="Today, We Are All On The Beach" width="218"/>
          </a>
          Here we go again with another appalling Wonkette Sci-Blog. Fix yourselves a Brain Stimulating Beverage and come on in.
         </p>
         <p>
          Earlier this year, scientists affiliated with five American universities released a study titled
          <a href="http://pus.sagepub.com/content/early/2013/04/01/0963662513480091.abstract" target="_blank">
           An attack on science? Media use, trust in scientists, and perceptions of global warming.
          </a>
          The researchers surveyed a representative sub-sample of U.S. media consumers, in 2008 and 2012, about their belief (or lack thereof) in global warming and the types of media they preferred. In addition, they categorized coverage of Anthropogenic Global Warming (AGW, or climate change) among all the major media sites, from CNN to Fox to MSNBC to the
          <em>
           Wall Street Journal
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           New York Times
          </em>
          . What they found no surprises here is that theres a strong positive correlation with exposure to conservative media and climate change denial. Its not exactly Hot News that people who watch Fox or listen to Limbaugh exclusively are the biggest skeptics of global warming  read the comments in any AGW story on Yahoo or Fox, or listen to the average caller on CSPANs
          <em>
           Washington Journal
          </em>
           if you dare. The real news here is the lengths to which right wing media go to create doubt and craft controversy over years of internationally published scientific findings that have survived skeptical peer review, data validation and experimental verification and all lead to the conclusion thats accepted by
          <a href="http://iopscience.iop.org/1748-9326/8/2/024024/article" target="_blank">
           98% of all the climate science professionals worldwide.
          </a>
          <span id="more-524414">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          The methods that organs like Fox use to achieve this goal are well established and finely honed: Highlight minor conflicts in published studies to claim theres a lack of scientific consensus, have paid
          <a href="http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052970203388804576612620828387968.html" target="_blank">
           flacks
          </a>
          from
          <a href="http://www.sourcewatch.org/index.php?title=Manhattan_Institute_for_Policy_Research" target="_blank">
           fossil fuel industry-funded think tanks
          </a>
          appear uncredited as Climate Experts, and run far more stories designed to disprove rather than prove global warming. If this sounds much like the strategy that the tobacco companies used to block regulatory actions and limit their liability in cancer suits, thats because it is. Go pick up
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1608193942/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1608193942&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=wonkette0f-20" target="_blank">
           Merchants of Doubt,
          </a>
          and youll see that its pretty much the same nefarious libertarian cast of characters from the Tobacco Wars who are behind AGW denialism.Cold War eraspooksused to call this kind of domestic propagandaoperation
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mighty_Wurlitzer_%28media%29" target="_blank">
           The Mighty Wurlitzer
          </a>
          , and its no coincidence that Roger Ailes was a media fixer for the criminal Nixon Administration.
         </p>
         <p>
          The other shoe that drops in the study is this: regular consumers of conservative media experience adramatic decline in the level of trust they place in scientists. Previous studies have found that trust in scientistsamong the public is key to being able to accept the facts of global warming. The exact opposite effect  greater trust in scientists  was seen in people who watched what were designated liberal outlets  CNN and MSNBC for example. The authors state that the sustained right wing war on climate change has
          <em>
           served to marginalize scientists in general and climate scientists in particular.
          </em>
          Probably the most egregious example is Mark Steyns disgraceful post in
          <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/corner/309442/football-and-hockey-mark-steyn" target="_blank">
           National Review Online
          </a>
          comparing
          <a href="http://www.meteo.psu.edu/holocene/public_html/Mann/index.php" target="_blank">
           Dr. Michael E. Mann,
          </a>
          the Director of the Earth System Science Center, to a
          <em>
           serial child molester!
          </em>
          Dr. Mann is
          <a href="http://arstechnica.com/science/2013/07/hockey-stick-graph-climate-researchers-defamation-suit-to-go-forward/" target="_blank">
           taking those bastards to court
          </a>
          for defamation of character and should win, especially as Steyn has accused him of fraudulently altering his data  just about the worst accusation you could make about a scientist. Getting a peek behind the curtain at NRO during the discovery phase of this trial should be entertaining, indeed.
         </p>
         <p>
          If this lack of trust phenomenon were limited to climate scientists it would be bad enough. Theres a heavy element of political tribalism in folks who watch Fox News for instance and, once you accept the
          <em>
           opinions
          </em>
          of
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sean_Hannity" target="_blank">
           angry, mal-informed jackasses
          </a>
          over learned professionals
          <em>
           facts
          </em>
          then theres little mechanism left in place to stop it. Witness the widespread conviction on the right that Nobel-winning economists
          <a href="http://krugman.blogs.nytimes.com/" target="_blank">
           Krugman
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.josephstiglitz.com/" target="_blank">
           Stiglitz
          </a>
          are in reality foolish hacks,
          <a href="http://www.algore.com/" target="_blank">
           Al Gore
          </a>
          claimed he invented the Internet,
          <a href="http://gohmert.house.gov/" target="_blank">
           Louis Gohmert
          </a>
          isnt a blithering idiot, that sending money to
          <a href="http://www.bennyhinn.org/" target="_blank">
           <em>
            The Man in the Brown Suit
           </em>
          </a>
          can purchase you a place in Heaven, and that
          <a href="http://kfmonkey.blogspot.com/2009/03/ephemera-2009-7.html" target="_blank">
           <em>
            Atlas Shrugged
           </em>
          </a>
          isnt a monumental pile of B-movie word guano written by a psychotic Russian speed freak.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tempting as it is to mock a group of people who reject significant elements of modern science from the Enlightenment on up in favor of a laughably lightweight and failure ridden political philosophy, dont. After 20 years of propaganda immersion they really believe all this hogwash, theyve taken over one of the two major political parties in the U.S. and theyve gerrymandered themselves into districts where they routinely elect legislators whose understanding of modern science at least as bad as  or worse than  their own. They elected George W. Bush as President.
          <em>
           After
          </em>
          they knew all about him.
         </p>
         <p>
          AGW is real. Our world is changing. 2012 was one of the
          <a href="http://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/news/ncdc-announces-warmest-year-record-contiguous-us" target="_blank">
           10 warmest years on record.
          </a>
          Arctic sea ice is
          <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/world/2013/aug/06/noaa-report-arctic-ice-climate-change" target="_blank">
           disappearing at an unprecedented pace
          </a>
          . Heat waves have created havoc in
          <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/capital-weather-gang/wp/2013/08/09/austria-sets-new-all-time-high-temperature-as-european-heat-wave-hits-peak/" target="_blank">
           Europe
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.theatlanticcities.com/technology/2013/01/nasas-alarming-map-worst-australian-heat-wave-record/4475/" target="_blank">
           Australia
          </a>
          and the
          <a href="http://www.accuweather.com/en/weather-news/heat-to-roil-more-fire-weather/14619950" target="_blank">
           US West
          </a>
          .
          <a href="http://www.npr.org/2013/08/08/210062400/rain-pounds-waynesville-missouri" target="_blank">
           Torrential rainfall
          </a>
          has caused catastrophic levels of flooding every year and
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_Sandy" target="_blank">
           massive 100-year storms
          </a>
          are showing up every other year.
          <a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2013-08-06/investors-see-climate-change-as-risk-that-influences-decisions-report.html" target="_blank">

          </a>

          <a href="https://www.genevaassociation.org/media/616667/GA2013-PR-09.pdf" target="_blank">
           insurance companies
          </a>
          and are adjusting their plans accordingly.
         </p>

At a time when we need to make important, complex, evidence-based decisions, it wuld be nice to have a well-informed electorate and clear-eyed lawmakers, but instead we have a reflexively partisan voting bloc supporting a senator who
          <a href="http://www.inhofe.senate.gov/newsroom/press-releases/inhofe-statement-at-epw-hearing-on-climate-change-its-happening-now" target="_blank">
           makes up his own facts
          </a>
          and believes AGW is the
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B007D2LK4S/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B007D2LK4S&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=wonkette0f-20" target="_blank">
           greatest hoax ever perpetrated on the American people
          </a>
          , a congressman and member of the
          <em>
           House Science Committee
          </em>
          who thinks that evolution, the big bang theory and embryology are
          <a href="http://wonkette.com/486046/rep-paul-broun-takes-bible-based-stand-against-hell-spawned-lies-of-science" target="_blank">
           Lies from the Pit of Hell
          </a>
          and a congresswoman and member of the House
          <em>
           Intelligence
          </em>

          <a href="http://wonkette.com/453097/michele-bachmann-says-cancer-sex-drug-making-girls-suffer-mental-retardation" target="_blank">

          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0307473996/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0307473996&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=wonkette0f-20" target="_blank">
           <em>
            On The Beach.
           </em>
          </a>
          A particular passage, which Ill share with you, has stuck with me and still seems eerily appropriate after all these years:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Newspapers, he said. You could have done something with newspapers. We didnt do it. No nation did, because we were all too silly. We liked our newspapers with pictures of beach girls and headlines about cases of indecent assault, and no government was wise enough to stop us from having them that way. But something might have been done with newspapers, if wed been wise enough.
           <br/>
           She did not fully comprehend his reasoning. Im glad we havent got newspapers now, she said. Its been much nicer without them.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
