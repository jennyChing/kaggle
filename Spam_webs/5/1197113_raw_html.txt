         <p>
          So youve decided that you would like to integrate your story into your online marketing. Congratulations! Now, where to begin? The world of online marketing can get pretty confusing. To illustrate this, take a look at an industry snapshot compiled by
          <a href="http://cdn.chiefmartec.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/marketing_technology_jan2015.png" target="_blank">
           Chiefmartech.com
          </a>
          :
          <br/>
          <a href="https://launchpodium.com/launchpodium/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/marketing_technology_jan20153.png" target="_blank">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="255" src="//launchpodium.com/launchpodium/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/marketing_technology_jan20153.png" width="340"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Yikes! Our goal with this section is to break down the challenging elements and give you simple ways to manage them.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Here is a brief outline of the progression of the journey:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Decide on an interesting story you would like to tell
          </li>
          <li>
           Decide how you want to tell it
          </li>
          <li>
           Evaluate the outlets for content i.e. podcasts, case studies, blogs, images
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          From there, a whole new world of decision-making comes into play.
         </p>
         <p>
          This includes:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Evaluating various technologies
          </li>
          <li>
           Coming up with your newsletter template and content plan
          </li>
          <li>
           Assessing advertising opportunities and costs
          </li>
          <li>
           Evaluating the need to hire someone to manage your online presence
          </li>
          <li>
           Enlisting a developer to manage your website
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          This seems like a lot! There are a few key factors that can make things difficult when planning an online marketing strategy: complexity, user control and art &amp; science. By looking more deeply into these elements,we are able to better understand the way the internet works and formulate a strategy.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Complexity
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           The Hard Part:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          New technology is being introduced at a break-neck speed. We barely have time to familiarize ourselves with our upgraded phone before the new model comes into the market!
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           The Internet makes the entire world available at our fingertips. There are over 30 trillion web pages and over 2 billion users and the numbers continue to grow each day.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Internet users access these webpages on five major browsers installed on three leading operating systems. Each system has its own style and flow that makes the information look and act differently.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is a lot different than the old-fashioned method of obtaining information in newspapers and magazines!
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Make It Work:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Ok, so youve seen the hard part. Now what? Follow these simple guidelines to harness the complexity of the Internet and make it work for you:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Choose one topic and be the expert
          </li>
          <li>
           Create and open conversation with your audience instead of barking information into the cybersphere
          </li>
          <li>
           Decide on your format early on and stick to it. Examples of formats are videos, blogs, podcasts, white papers, and infographics
          </li>
          <li>
           Be everywhere that your audience is. This means meeting them on your website, social media pages, newsletters and through advertisements
          </li>
          <li>
           Publish one good thing per week and your audience will grow
          </li>
          <li>
           Get familiar with data so you can better understand what is working and what isnt
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           User Control
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <div class="one-half first">
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Hard Part:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           In the early days of marketing, the marketers were in complete control of the customer experience. They would make a product look wonderful, tell everyone it was wonderful and, if they did a good job, people would buy it. Today, this is not the case. Users are in complete control of the experience and one bad review on
           <a href="http://www.yelp.com/" target="_blank">
            Yelp
           </a>
           can seriously hinder a businesss success.
          </p>
          <p>
           In addition to platforms like
           <a href="http://www.yelp.com/" target="_blank">
            Yelp
           </a>
           , there are other tools like Social Commerce, Livecasting, and Social Bookmarking that we can do in places like
           <a href="https://www.flickr.com/" target="_blank">
            Flickr
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://vine.co/" target="_blank">
            Vine
           </a>
           and
           <a href="https://www.pheed.com/" target="_blank">
            Pheed
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="one-half">
          <a href="https://launchpodium.com/launchpodium/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/a-balance-of-science-and-art.jpg" target="_blank">
           <img alt="" height="490" src="//launchpodium.com/launchpodium/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/a-balance-of-science-and-art.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="clear">
          <p>
           Did you know that over 500 million Tweets are sent every minute? Thats a lot of characters!For a better idea of the scale of online data,
           <a href="http://www.domo.com/blog/2014/04/data-never-sleeps-2-0/" target="_blank">
            check out this link by Domo
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Make It Work:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Here are some easy ways to benefit from the control of your customers:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Listen to them and acknowledge their voice
           </li>
           <li>
            Find your authentic voice and make a connection with them
           </li>
           <li>
            Show, dont tell
           </li>
           <li>
            Ask questions that provoke action
           </li>
           <li>
            Invite and reward them for their participation and contribution
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            Art &amp; Science
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Hard Part:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The main challenge of sharing your stories online is that you are required to use both sides of your brain. On the right, we have our creativity and storytelling. On the left, we must tap into our logical and analytical brain. Its crucial that we use these two sides constantly!
          </p>
          <p>
           According to a study by
           <a href="http://www.awarenesshub.com/#&amp;panel1-1" target="_blank">
            Awareness Hub
           </a>
           , 53% of social media marketers dont measure their success. Whats the point of telling your awesome stories if you never check to see if anyone is responding to them?
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Make It Work:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Dont worry. Its easy to avoid neglecting one of these tools. Heres how:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Divide your efforts into two parts: creation and distribution. Make sure you dont have one without the other
           </li>
           <li>
            Identify the areas of creativity where you write and edit copy, graphics and videos and work together with writers and designers
           </li>
           <li>
            Identify the key distribution areas where you post and measure data and work together with engineers and analysts
           </li>
           <li>
            Make sure you have a well-rounded team of creators and engineers that understands the overarching language of your brand
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           You might be having mixed feelings right now but there is no reason to be afraid. By utilizing these techniques and integrating them into your daily practices, you will find yourself befriending the complexity of the Internet.
          </p>
          <p>

By analyzing this data, you will know where to focus your creative energy and truly launch your story into the world.
