         <h1>
          9 stunning cars showcased at this years Goodwood Festival of Speed
         </h1>
        </div>
        <span class="post-byline">
         By
         <a href="http://motorburn.com/author/andywalker/" rel="author" title="Posts by Andy Walker: staff reporter">
          Andy Walker: staff reporter

         on 2 July, 2014
        </span>
        <span class="twitter-byline">
         <a href="http://www.twitter.com/https://twitter.com/reddevilandy10" target="blank">

        </div>
        <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
        </div>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://grrc.goodwood.com/section/festival-of-speed/" target="_blank">
          Goodwood Festival of Speed
         </a>
         is debatably Englands premier motorsport show, taking place every year since 1993 in West Sussexs height of summer. The Goodwood House and its stoned walls flank the iconic hillclimb, a strip of tarmac used decades before the event became a social and motoring highlight. The tradition of returning every June/July to British motorsports spiritual home is now held in high esteem by all in the motoring world.
        </p>

        <p>
         Its popularity is unquestionable. Personalities and legends from F1 champions
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Surtees" target="_blank">
          John Surtees
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" target="_blank">
          Emerson Fittipaldi
         </a>
         to NASCAR great
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Petty" target="_blank">
          Richard Petty
         </a>
         and Le Mans master
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Derek_Bell_(racing_driver)" target="_blank">
          Derek Bell
         </a>
         all graced the lawns this year.
        </p>
        <p>
         In addition, the 150 000 spectators got up close and extremely personal with some rare and rapid vehicles.
        </p>
        <p>
         All this hype makes it a perfect place for the odd car launch or two.
        </p>
        <p>
         This years edition of the motoring festival saw an unprecedented number of new cars. From supercars, to prototypes and concepts, to more practical drives, Goodwood surely had them all.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Range Rover Sport Stealth SVR
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="349" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/xV07UXpfmpY?feature=oembed" width="620">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         A product of Jaguars new
         <a href="http://newsroom.jaguarlandrover.com/en-in/jlr-corp/news/2014/06/jlr_john_edwards_announces_launch_of_special_operations_120614/" target="_blank">
          Special Vehicle Operations
         </a>
         , the 
         <a href="http://newsroom.jaguarlandrover.com/en-in/land-rover/news/2014/06/rr_rrs_svr_prototype_at_goodwood_250614/" target="_blank">
          fastest and most powerful
         </a>
         production Land Rover ever made is a vehicle traipsing the thin tightrope between simplicity and absurdity. The Union Jack-adorned prototype is faster than most hot hatches and some supercars, spitting out 550 horsepower and boasting a 0 to 100km/h time of under five seconds.
        </p>
        <p>
         Add a few suspension tweaks, an eight-speed performance transmission from ZF and a much lower stance, and the SVR (according to test drivers) performs more like a sports car than an SUV. Although it may be slightly over the top, and sport AMG-levels of brashness, one cant deny that this car sounds like a hive of knife-wielding hornets. While it is an SUV, we doubt customers will want to get this Range Rover muddy.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Citroen C4 Cactus
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="citroen c4 cactus" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86258" height="401" src="http://motorburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/citroen-c4-cactus.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mundane would be an unfair way to describe the unusually named Cactus. Citroens latest crossover-slash-hatchback-slash-styling experiment didnt take to the track in any form at Goodwood, but the car garnered more than a few interested glances.
        </p>
        <p>
         Instead of loudly lauding how much power it has, Citroens central focus for the Cactus is economy, simplicity and practicality. Fuel economy is a claimed 2.58L/100km while it sports running costs 20% lower than other hatches in the segment. The car should shake up the hatchback segment nicely from September this year, but just dont expect the Cactus to be too sharp through the corners.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Nissan 2020 Gran Turismo Vision Concept
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="nissan 2020" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86259" height="430" src="http://motorburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/nissan-2020.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Back to the spectacular. Many marques exhibited Gran Turismo concepts that are about as sublime as they are ludicrous.
         <a href="http://nissannews.com/en-US/nissan/usa/releases/nissan-and-playstation-reveal-future-vision" target="_blank">
          Polyphony Digitals program
         </a>
         encourages users to develop custom concepts for use within the Gran Turismo gaming environment, paying homage to the PlayStation games 15th anniversary. Nissan, however, has gone one step further by bringing the four-wheel drive, shark-like 2020 Vision into tangible reality.
        </p>
        <p>
         The gaping front airdam and the sleekest of lines makes this car both aggressive and elegant. Of course, we may never see the 2020 Vision roar and pounce like its predatory facade would suggest, but design cues may actually hint at the new GT-R design direction  Nissans supercar contender slated for future release.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Koenigsegg Agera One:1
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Koenigsegg Agera One" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86256" height="430" src="http://motorburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Koenigsegg-Agera-One.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Koenigsegg will never make an ordinary car. Ever. And this is about as special as vehicles come.
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bugatti_Veyron" target="_blank">
          Bugattis 1000-horsepower Veyron
         </a>
         was special, but the Agera One:1 is just something
         <em>
          more
         </em>
         . Dubbed as a megacar by its manufacturer, the Agera has 1 megawatt (1340 horsepower) of grunt on tap and can get to the astronomical landmark of 400km/h from a standstill in just 20 seconds. The Veyron, in comparison, takes 55 seconds.
        </p>
        <p>
         The manic twin-turbocharged 5.0-litre V8 also makes it the
         <a href="http://www.koenigsegg.com/models/one1/" target="_blank">
          first production car ever
         </a>
         to achieve a 1:1 kilowatt-to-kilogram power-to-weight ratio. It can also run on biofuel, remarkably, making it more green than most supercars on the market. Its price also makes it one of the most expensive, costing a cool US$4.25-million or around the same as a seaside villa.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. Volkswagen GTI Roadster Concept
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="vw gti roadster" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86271" height="430" src="http://motorburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/vw-gti-roadster.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Volkswagen usually lets one of its luxury marques build the breathtaking machinery while it takes care of practical buyers needs. Bentley, Audi and Bugatti have all released some stunners this decade, but the Wolfsburg-manufacturer has just showcased what is possibly its most beautiful car ever, thanks to another Gran Turismo-inspired venture.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its called the
         <a href="http://www.gran-turismo.com/us/news/00_1883038.html" target="_blank">
          GTI Roadster
         </a>
         , and we hope VW starts building it tomorrow. Its an open-top, two-seater work of sheet metal art with subtle VW-esque design cues. Massive 20 wheels, an oversized rear wing and a wrap-around windscreen completes the racy profile. A 3.0-litre bi-turbo engine produces power to match the looks  a full 496 horsepower  while a top speed of 309km/h is achieved through VWs 4MOTION all wheel drive system. It sounds like a vehicle that could easily slot in above the Scirocco in VWs product line.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          6. Aston Martin DP-100 Vision Gran Turismo Concept
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="aston martin dp100" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86257" height="430" src="http://motorburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/aston-martin-dp100.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The worst looking of the Gran Turismo cars on offer, the
         <a href="http://www.astonmartin.com/en/live/news/2014/06/27/aston-martin-unveils-virtual-dp-100-racer-for-gran-turismo-6" target="_blank">
          DP-100
         </a>
         is however, no less impressive. The mid-engined, 800-horsepower V12 is about as powerful as supercars come, while the orange and baby blue paint job elegantly doffs its cap to Astons famed racing pedigree.
        </p>
        <p>
         According to the designers, it took around six months to style and engineer, while the cars body panels can literally adapt physically to the aerodynamic load exerted. Whether Aston Martin will ever decide to build such a monster is beyond guess, but the car will be available to drive, digitally at least, when
         <a href="http://www.gran-turismo.com/us/news/?t=gt6" target="_blank">
          Gran Turismo 6
         </a>
         launches in a few weeks.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          7. McLaren 650S GT3
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="McLaren_650S_GT3-034" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86260" height="430" src="http://motorburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/McLaren_650S_GT3-034.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         McLaren was another manufacturer not far from its home event either. The company, while showcasing some of its iconic Formula 1 cars, had
         <a href="http://www.cars.mclaren.com/sites/mclaren/featured-articles/mclaren-650s-gt3-revealed-at-fos.html" target="_blank">
          time to unveil
         </a>
         its new McLaren-orange 650S GT3 (in case the standard 333km/h 650S is too slow).
        </p>
        <p>
         The GT3 is however made for genuine racers in mind, quite literally. Only 15 are currently scheduled for production, powered by the marques iconic 3.8-litre V8. Drivers also get reworked aerodynamics, a wider track and massive rear wing. Although its unlikely well see this monster on the road anytime soon, it should replace the highly successful 12C GT3 in sports car championships around the world. We may even see it race at Le Mans next year, which would be the first time McLaren took part in the event since 1988.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          8. Infiniti Q50 Eau Rouge Concept
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="infiniti eau rouge" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86261" height="430" src="http://motorburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/infiniti-eau-rouge.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Infinitis
         <a href="http://infinitinews.com/en-US/infiniti/usa/releases/infiniti-q50-eau-rouge-prototype-to-make-its-first-dynamic-appearance-at-the-goodwood-festival-of-speed" target="_blank">
          new supercar-killer
         </a>
         , the Q50 Eau Rouge Concept, looks less dramatic than its performance would suggest. Named after one of motor racings most feared corners,
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" target="_blank">
          Spa Francorchamps
         </a>
         Eau Rouge has claimed the lives of many drivers and vehicles in the past. The Q50 not only pays homage to them, but to racing around the iconic circuit.
        </p>
        <p>
         The car itself employs some Formula 1 gadgetry thanks to their partner and current F1 Champions,
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" target="_blank">
          Infiniti Red Bull Racing F1 team
         </a>
         . Its 3.8-litre, twin-turbo V6 pushes out 568-horsepower, which is not far off the current breed of F1 cars. In fact, RBR head
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Horner" target="_blank">
          Christian Horner
         </a>
         had the chance to pilot the Q50 up the Goodwood hill, to rapturous applause aided by the Eau Rouges roar.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          9. Bentley Continental GT3-R
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="bentley gt3-r" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-86263" height="430" src="http://motorburn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/bentley-gt3-r.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.bentleymedia.com/release-557.htm" target="_blank">
          Billed
         </a>
         by Bentley as a new chapter in performance focussed luxury, the company surely undersells the abilities of this insane piece of engineering. What is definitely the fastest accelerating Bentley ever produced, the GT3-R has a pumped up 4.0-litre twin-turbo V8 producing around 580 horsepower. Its also features two seats less than the standard Continental, and weighs 100kg less than its ordinary V8 brother. Somehow, the GT3-R still weighs a paunchy 2.1 tons.
        </p>
        <p>
         With all that belly to lug around, one would think it to be as sluggish as a sloth, but it reaches 100km/h from a standstill in 3.6 brief seconds, while doing so with pure Bentley grace. Only 300 bespoke examples will be made, unfortunately, but judging by Goodwoods reception of the vehicle, we could see luxury-orientated marques experiment even more with hot variants of their cars in the future.
        </p>
