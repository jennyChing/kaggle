             <h2>
              Vintage Postcard  Silver Lining Motel
             </h2>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.catobear.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/silverlining.jpg">
               <img alt="Silver Lining Motel" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3195" height="385" src="http://www.catobear.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/silverlining.jpg" width="610"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Look for the
              <br/>
              Silver Lining Motel
              <br/>
              on U.S. #1, 1/2 mile south of Vero Beach, Fla. and 1 mile north of McKee Jungle Gardens. Heated  Air-Conditioned  TV in Rooms  10 Motel Rooms back from Highway on Spacious Landscaped Grounds. Phone J.O. 2-4666.
             </p>
             <p>
              Pub. by Florida Motel Industry Printing Services, P.O. Box 1032, Orlando, Florida.
             </p>
             <p>
              Made by Dexter West Nyack N.Y.
             </p>
             <p>
              This lovely vintage postcard for the Silver Lining Motel has the AAA logo. It was mailed using a 3 cent purple liberty stamp from Fort Pierce, Florida on Oct 25, 1961. It was mailed to Mr. Jack Perkins, Wayne, Maine.
             </p>
             <p>
              Now, Ill pause here before I share the message on the back. And I am not going to be writing the full message out on this one. Two sentences are so racially objectionable that they will not be included. In todays world, it seems absolutely shocking that someone would have written these things on the back of a postcard, not only for the recipient but also the mail carrier and postal workers and rest of the world to see.
             </p>
             <p>
              Dear Jack,
             </p>
             <p>
              Do you like keeping house? How is Bobby cut? [?] I hear it is cold
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               there
              </span>
              . Went to Daytona Beach this am are nowhere
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               tan
              </span>
              here. [sentences removed] Temperature here was 80 to-day. Must be nice in winter months. I am wearing sleeveless dresses Dixie gets so hot but it is real good. Going to Miami tomorrow. Stay here tonite. The surf is beautiful. As ever, Jen &amp; Vance (
              <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
               Rush!
              </span>
              ) Keep water in furnace half full thanks.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.mckeegarden.org/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mckeegarden.org/', 'McKee Botanical Garden']);" target="_blank" title="McKee Botanical Garden">
               McKee Botanical Garden
              </a>
              is still open for business. Ive been up and down the street (digitally) using both Google Maps and Google Earth. Google Earth allows for historical images as well, and shows a building at this location as late as 2004. It could be the location (the dirt lot across the street from Sewell Hardware):
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.catobear.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/silverlininglot.jpg">
               <img alt="silverlininglot" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3198" height="432" src="http://www.catobear.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/silverlininglot.jpg" width="610"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              Note the half-moon shape of the little island out in front, where the old neon could have been.
             </p>
             <p>
              But waitwhats this? Bonus! Heres a
              <em>
               second
              </em>
              Silver Lining postcard, this time a daytime shot! Woot! Can you believe your luck?
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.catobear.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/silverlining2.jpg">
               <img alt="silver lining motel" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3234" height="390" src="http://www.catobear.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/silverlining2.jpg" width="610"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Look for the
              <br/>
              Silver Lining Motel
              <br/>
              on U.S. #1, 1/2 mile south of Vero Beach, Fla. and 1 mile north of McKee Jungle Gardens. Heated  Air-Conditioned  TV in Rooms  Connecting Rooms. Back from Highway on Spacious landscaped grounds.
              <br/>
              Phone 562-9813
              <br/>
              JACK &amp; JOYCE SMITH, Owner-Management
             </p>
             <p>
              Pub. by OBrien Color Studios, Daytona Beach, Florida
              <br/>
              #1380
             </p>
             <p>
              So this card is a little newer. Expanded phone number. Description slightly changed (also some capitalization changed). The addition of the owners/managers. Slight detail changes in the motel, toonotice the new electric poles? The old awning over the front is gone, too. On the main sign, heated has been replaced with air conditioned.
             </p>
             <p>
              If you like this post, check out this one:
             </p>

               Dolphin Motel, another Vero Beach beauty
              </a>
             </p>
