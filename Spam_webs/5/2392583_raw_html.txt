     <p>
      <b>
       Drumright
      </b>
      is a city in
      <a href="/wiki/Creek_County,_Oklahoma" title="Creek County, Oklahoma">
       Creek
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Payne_County,_Oklahoma" title="Payne County, Oklahoma">
       Payne
      </a>
      counties in the
      <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">
       U.S. state
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">
       Oklahoma
      </a>
      . It began as an
      <a href="/wiki/Oil_boom" title="Oil boom">
       oil boom
      </a>
      town. However, the population has declined as oil production has waned in the area. The population was 2,907 at the 2010 census, almost unchanged from 2,905 at the
      <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" title="2000 United States Census">
       2000 census
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Drumright and nearby
      <a href="/wiki/Cushing,_Oklahoma" title="Cushing, Oklahoma">
       Cushing
      </a>
      were at the center of a large, productive oilfield in the 1910s and 1920s.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Drumright,_Oklahoma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Drumright_1920.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="542" data-file-width="856" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Drumright_1920.jpg/220px-Drumright_1920.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Drumright_1920.jpg/330px-Drumright_1920.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Drumright_1920.jpg/440px-Drumright_1920.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Drumright_1920.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Historical Drumright (1920)
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The town sprang up nearly overnight in 1912, after wildcatter
      <a href="/wiki/Tom_Slick" title="Tom Slick">
       Tom Slick
      </a>
      struck oil on the farm of Frank Wheeler, causing a rush of speculators, oilfield workers, and merchants into the area. A post office was established in the community on December 28, 1912. Local landowners James W. Fulkerson and Aaron Drumright platted a townsite, which was initially called Fulkerson, The town was renamed for Aaron Drumright, a farmer and later local businessman whose farm was part of the townsite.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EOHC-Drumright_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-EOHC-Drumright-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Oil workers flooded into town so quickly that they lived in tents or shacks made
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=From_box_cars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="From box cars (page does not exist)">
       from box cars
      </a>
      , causing the community to be known locally as "Ragtown." Hotels and boarding houses were constructed next, as well as amenities like gambling dens, dance halls, and roadhouses, where the workers could spend their money. Drumright incorporated as a town on May 27, 1913. In 1914, the city built a two-story building of stone to serve as an elementary and high school. It was called Washington School, and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places (NRIS 81000462). Two banks opened in the town during 1914. Drumright was designated a first-class city after an election on April 18, 1916. The 1920 census reported a population of 6,460.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EOHC-Drumright_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-EOHC-Drumright-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Oil Fields and Santa Fe Railway built a track from rom Frey Junction (south of Oilton) to Drumright in 1915. The following year the Atchison, Topeka &amp; Santa Fe Railroad (AT&amp;SF) built a line north from Shamrock to Drumright. The AT&amp;SF also acquired the Oil Fields and Santa Fe Railway.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EOHC-Drumright_4-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-EOHC-Drumright-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1919 a riot broke out in Drumright during a strike by telephone workers. The town's mayor and chief of police were locked in the town jail by rioters. The Governor of Oklahoma sent six militia units to town to restore order.
     </p>
     <p>
      Beginning with the
      <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">
       Depression
      </a>
      of the 1930s, the town declined as oil production waned, and a large refinery at the edge of town closed in the 1950s.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">
       Tornadoes
      </a>
      have caused loss of life and property damage in Drumright on at least two occasions: on April 2, 1956, when five people were killed and several homes, a school, and the public library were damaged; and on June 8, 1974, when 12 people were killed, a nursing home was destroyed, and about 100 homes were damaged or destroyed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EOHC-Drumright_4-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-EOHC-Drumright-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      of 2000, there were 2,905 people, 1,209 households, and 790 families residing in the city. The
      <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">
       population density
      </a>
      was 411.8 people per square mile (159.1/km). There were 1,378 housing units at an average density of 195.4 per square mile (75.5/km). The racial makeup of the city was 84.44%
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">
       White
      </a>
      , 0.93%
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">

      </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">
       other races
      </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">
       Latino
      </a>
      of any race were 0.90% of the population.
     </p>
     <p>
      There were 1,209 households out of which 29.9% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 47.5% were
      <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">
       married couples
      </a>
      living together, 14.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 34.6% were non-families. 31.0% of all households were made up of individuals and 16.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.34 and the average family size was 2.92.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the city the population was spread out with 25.5% under the age of 18, 8.0% from 18 to 24, 25.2% from 25 to 44, 20.3% from 45 to 64, and 20.9% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 38 years. For every 100 females there were 87.5 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 81.2 males.
     </p>
     <p>
      The median income for a household in the city was $27,292, and the median income for a family was $34,761. Males had a median income of $30,069 versus $20,123 for females. The
      <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">
       per capita income
      </a>
      for the city was $14,511. About 13.7% of families and 17.3% of the population were below the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">
       poverty line
      </a>
      , including 19.7% of those under age 18 and 11.0% of those age 65 or over.
     </p>

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      </a>
      has opened in a historic building that once served as a school for refinery workers.
