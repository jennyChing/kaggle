      <b>
       St Albans
      </b>
      <span class="nowrap">
       <span class="IPA nopopups">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">
         /
         <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
          <span title="'s' in 'sigh'">
           s
          </span>
          <span title="'n' in 'no'">
           n
          </span>
          <span title="'t' in 'tie'">
           t
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
          <span title="// primary stress follows">
           
          </span>
          <span title="// 'au' in 'fraud'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'l' in 'lie'">
           l
          </span>
          <span title="'b' in 'buy'">
           b
          </span>
          <span title="/n/ 'on' in 'button'">
           n
          </span>
          <span title="'z' in 'Zion'">
           z
          </span>
         </span>
         /
        </a>
       </span>
      </span>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
       Lat.
      </a>
      :
      <i>
       Villa Sancti Albani
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       Villa Albani
      </i>
      ) is a city and
      <a href="/wiki/Unparished_area" title="Unparished area">
       unparished area
      </a>
      in southern
      <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">
       Hertfordshire
      </a>
      , England, around 19 miles (31km) north of central
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      . It forms the main urban area of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_and_District_of_St_Albans" title="City and District of St Albans">
       City and District of St Albans
      </a>
      . It was the first major town on the old
      <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">
       Roman
      </a>
      road of
      <a href="/wiki/Watling_Street" title="Watling Street">
       Watling Street
      </a>
      for travellers heading north and became the Roman city of
      <a href="/wiki/Verulamium" title="Verulamium">
       Verulamium
      </a>
      . It is an historic
      <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">
       market town
      </a>
      and is now a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dormitory_town" title="Dormitory town">
       dormitory town
      </a>
      within the
      <a href="/wiki/London_commuter_belt" title="London commuter belt">
       London commuter belt
      </a>
      .

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Toponymy">
       Toponymy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Toponymy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Saint_Alban" title="Saint Alban">
       Saint Alban
      </a>
      , the first British Christian martyr, was possibly beheaded in AD 308 by
      <a href="/wiki/Maximian" title="Maximian">
       Maximian
      </a>
      on the orders of Emperor
      <a href="/wiki/Diocletian" title="Diocletian">
       Diocletian
      </a>
      , who denounced Christianity and had ordered the deaths of all subjects and allies of the Roman Empire who refused to give up the faith.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Saint Alban consequently gave the town its modern name.
     </p>
     <p>
      St Albans has two official
      <a href="/wiki/Demonym" title="Demonym">
       demonyms
      </a>
      : Verulamian and Albanian. St Albans was a settlement of pre-Roman origin named
      <i>
       Verlamion
      </i>
      (or Verulam) by the
      <a href="/wiki/Belgae" title="Belgae">
       Ancient British
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Catuvellauni" title="Catuvellauni">
       Catuvellauni
      </a>
      tribe.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/History_of_St_Albans" title="History of St Albans">
       History of St Albans
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stalbans-tower.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1446" data-file-width="714" height="344" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Stalbans-tower.jpg/170px-Stalbans-tower.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Stalbans-tower.jpg/255px-Stalbans-tower.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Stalbans-tower.jpg/340px-Stalbans-tower.jpg 2x" width="170"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stalbans-tower.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The early 15th century Clock Tower
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The St Albans area has a long history of settlement. The Celtic
      <a href="/wiki/Catuvellauni" title="Catuvellauni">
       Catuvellauni
      </a>
      tribe had a settlement at Prae Hill a mile or so to the west. The Roman city of
      <a href="/wiki/Verulamium" title="Verulamium">
       Verulamium
      </a>
      , second-largest town in Roman Britain after
      <a href="/wiki/Londinium" title="Londinium">
       Londinium
      </a>
      , was built alongside this in the valley of the
      <a href="/wiki/River_Ver" title="River Ver">
       River Ver
      </a>
      a little nearer to the present city centre. The town was burned by
      <a href="/wiki/Boudica" title="Boudica">
       Boudicca
      </a>
      , Queen of the Iceni tribe, in 60-61 AD in her fight against Roman rule.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After the Roman withdrawal the town became the centre of the territory or
      <i>
       <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Regio" title="Regio">
        regio
       </a>
      </i>
      of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon" title="Anglo-Saxon">
       Anglo-Saxon
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Waeclingas" title="Waeclingas">
        Waeclingas
       </a>
      </i>
      tribe, being called
      <i>
       Verlamchester
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       Wclingacaester
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The medieval town grew on the hill to the east of this around the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benedictine" title="Benedictine">
       Benedictine
      </a>
      foundation of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Albans_Abbey" title="St Albans Abbey">
       St Albans Abbey
      </a>
      . This is the spot where tradition has it that
      <a href="/wiki/Saint_Alban" title="Saint Alban">
       St Alban
      </a>
      , the first British
      <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">
       Christian
      </a>
      martyr, was beheaded some time before AD 324. It was at one time the principal
      <a href="/wiki/Abbey" title="Abbey">
       abbey
      </a>
      in England and the first draft of
      <a href="/wiki/Magna_Carta" title="Magna Carta">
       Magna Carta
      </a>
      was drawn up there, reflecting its political importance. The Abbey Church, now
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_Cathedral" title="St Albans Cathedral">
       St Albans Cathedral
      </a>
      (formally
      <i>
       the Cathedral &amp; Abbey Church of St Alban
      </i>
      but still known locally as
      <i>
       The Abbey
      </i>
      ) became the parish church when it was bought by the local people in 1553, soon after the priory was
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_monasteries" title="Dissolution of the monasteries">
       dissolved
      </a>
      in 1539. It was made a cathedral in 1877 when the City Charter was granted. There is evidence that the original site was somewhat higher up the hill than the present building and there had certainly been successive abbeys before the current building was started in 1077.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_School_(Hertfordshire)" title="St Albans School (Hertfordshire)">
       St Albans School
      </a>
      , a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_school_(UK)" title="Public school (UK)">
       public school
      </a>
      which, since 1871, occupies a site to the west of the Abbey and which includes the 14th century
      <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Gateway,_St._Albans" title="Abbey Gateway, St. Albans">
       Abbey Gateway
      </a>
      , was founded in AD 948 and is the only school in the English-speaking world to have educated a
      <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">
       Pope
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Pope_Adrian_IV" title="Pope Adrian IV">
       Adrian IV
      </a>
      ). It numbered amongst its buildings until comparatively recently a converted former hat factory, a link with the city's industrial past. Nearby
      <a href="/wiki/Luton" title="Luton">
       Luton
      </a>
      was also a notable centre for the hat making industry.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:StAlbans-PSX1.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/StAlbans-PSX1.JPG/220px-StAlbans-PSX1.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/StAlbans-PSX1.JPG/330px-StAlbans-PSX1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/StAlbans-PSX1.JPG/440px-StAlbans-PSX1.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:StAlbans-PSX1.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        St Albans: French Row (to the left), Market Place (to the right), St Peter's Street and the tower of St Peter's Church (centre)
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The road between the Abbey and the school, running down to the
      <a href="/wiki/River_Ver" title="River Ver">
       River Ver
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Verulamium_Park" title="Verulamium Park">
       Verulamium Park
      </a>
      (on part of the site of Roman Verulamium), is called Abbey Mill Lane. On this road are the palaces of the Bishops of
      <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_St_Albans" title="Bishop of St Albans">
       St Albans
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Hertford" title="Bishop of Hertford">
       Hertford
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Ye_Olde_Fighting_Cocks" title="Ye Olde Fighting Cocks">
       The Fighting Cocks
      </a>
      , one of the oldest public houses in England, is at the Verulamium Park end of this road. Also on the River Ver, at the St Michael's Village end of the park, is
      <a href="/wiki/Kingsbury_Watermill" title="Kingsbury Watermill">
       Kingsbury Watermill
      </a>
      , which is now maintained as a museum with a waffle house attached.
     </p>
     <p>
      Two battles of the
      <a href="/wiki/Wars_of_the_Roses" title="Wars of the Roses">
       Wars of the Roses
      </a>
      took place in or near the town. The
      <a href="/wiki/First_Battle_of_St_Albans" title="First Battle of St Albans">
       First Battle of St Albans
      </a>
      was fought on 22 May 1455 within the town of St Albans itself, and the
      <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_St_Albans" title="Second Battle of St Albans">
       Second Battle of St Albans
      </a>
      was fought on 17 February 1461, just to the north.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The growth of St Albans was generally slow before the 20th century, reflecting its status as a rural
      <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">
       market town
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/Christian_pilgrimage" title="Christian pilgrimage">
       Christian pilgrimage
      </a>
      site, and the first coaching stop of the route to and from
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      - a fact which also accounts for its numerous inns, many dating from
      <a href="/wiki/Tudor_period" title="Tudor period">
       Tudor
      </a>
      times. In the inter-war years it became a popular centre for the
      <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">
       electronics
      </a>
      industry. In the post-
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      years it was expanded significantly as part of the post-War redistribution of population out of
      <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">
       Greater London
      </a>
      that also saw the creation of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_town" title="New town">
       new towns
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The city today shows evidence of building and excavation from all periods of its history and is a tourist destination. Notable buildings include the Abbey and the early 15th century Clock Tower (pictured). The clock tower is one of only two similar towers in England; it is also the site of an
      <a href="/wiki/Eleanor_cross" title="Eleanor cross">
       Eleanor cross
      </a>
      , which was pulled down in 1703, it had suffered years of neglect, and had been struck and badly damaged by a carriage, it was replaced by the town pump. A fountain was erected in its place in 1874, now relocated to Victoria Square. Originally the fountain was situated in front of St Albans Clock Tower on the High Street. However as traffic increased it became too obstructive and was moved in the 1920s.
     </p>
     <p>
      Running into St Albans from the south is Holywell Hill (generally pronounced "holly-well hill"), its name taken from the story of St Alban. Legend has it that the Romans were looking for a traitor that had been injured and Alban found the traitor and helped him to recover; somehow, after the traitor had recovered and left, the Romans found out that Alban had helped the traitor to escape and had him beheaded. The severed head rolled down the hill from the execution site and into a well at the bottom, thus Holy-well Hill.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:StAlbansRomanWall-PS01.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/StAlbansRomanWall-PS01.JPG/220px-StAlbansRomanWall-PS01.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/StAlbansRomanWall-PS01.JPG/330px-StAlbansRomanWall-PS01.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/StAlbansRomanWall-PS01.JPG/440px-StAlbansRomanWall-PS01.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:StAlbansRomanWall-PS01.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Remains of Roman wall
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The mixed character of St Albans and proximity to London has made it a popular filming location. The Abbey and Fishpool Street areas were used for the pilot episode of the 1960s' ecclesiastical TV comedy
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/All_Gas_and_Gaiters" title="All Gas and Gaiters">
        All Gas and Gaiters
       </a>
      </i>
      . The area of Romeland, directly north of the Abbey Gateway and the walls of the Abbey and school grounds, can be seen masquerading as part of an Oxford college in some episodes of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Inspector_Morse_(TV_series)" title="Inspector Morse (TV series)">
        Inspector Morse
       </a>
      </i>
      (and several local pubs also appear). Fishpool Street, running from Romeland to St Michael's village, stood in for
      <a href="/wiki/Hastings" title="Hastings">
       Hastings
      </a>
      in some episodes of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Foyle%27s_War" title="Foyle's War">
        Foyle's War
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Life_Begins_(TV_series)" title="Life Begins (TV series)">
        Life Begins
       </a>
      </i>
      was filmed largely in and around St Albans. The Lady Chapel in the Abbey itself was used as a location for at least one scene in
      <a href="/wiki/Sean_Connery" title="Sean Connery">
       Sean Connery
      </a>
      's 1995 film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/First_Knight" title="First Knight">
        First Knight
       </a>
      </i>
      , whilst the nave of the Abbey was used during a coronation scene as a substitute for
      <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">
       Westminster Abbey
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Johnny_English" title="Johnny English">
        Johnny English
       </a>
      </i>
      starring
      <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Atkinson" title="Rowan Atkinson">
       Rowan Atkinson
      </a>
      . The 19th century gatehouse of the former prison near the
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_City_railway_station" title="St Albans City railway station">
       mainline station
      </a>
      appeared in the title sequence of the TV series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Porridge_(TV_series)" title="Porridge (TV series)">
        Porridge
       </a>
      </i>
      , starring
      <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Barker" title="Ronnie Barker">
       Ronnie Barker
      </a>
      . The 2001 film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Birthday_Girl" title="Birthday Girl">
        Birthday Girl
       </a>
      </i>
      starring
      <a href="/wiki/Ben_Chaplin" title="Ben Chaplin">
       Ben Chaplin
      </a>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Clock_Tower">
       The Clock Tower
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Clock Tower">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Between 1403 and 1412 Thomas Wolvey, formerly the Royal Mason, was engaged to build "Le Clokkehouse" in the Market Place. It is the only extant mediaeval town belfry in England. The tower's design was based on the Clock House at Westminster Palace that the architect
      <a href="/wiki/Henry_Yevele" title="Henry Yevele">
       Henry Yevele
      </a>
      (Wolvey's master) built in 1365. The Clock Tower was used to sound the curfew until 1863. The Tower was also used as a semaphore station from 1808 to 1814 during the
      <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">
       Napoleonic Wars
      </a>
      . The architect
      <a href="/wiki/George_Gilbert_Scott" title="George Gilbert Scott">
       George Gilbert Scott
      </a>
      restored the structure of the tower in 1865-6; he also added the gothic spire and parapets.
     </p>
     <p>
      The original bell, named for the Archangel Gabriel (cast round the bell is the Latin rhyme "From Heaven I come/Gabriel my name"), is still in use, though chimed rather than rung; it last rang out for
      <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">
       Queen Victoria
      </a>
      's funeral in 1901. It sounds F-natural and weighs one ton.
     </p>
     <p>
      Gabriel sounded at 4 am for the Angelus and at 8 or 9 pm for the curfew. A small bell, dated 1729, was moved in the Clock Tower from the market place nearby, where it opened business until 1855.
     </p>
     <p>
      The ground floor of the tower was a shop until the 20th century. The first- and second-floor rooms were designed as living chambers. The shop and the first floor were connected by a flight of spiral stairs. Another flight rises the whole height of the tower by 93 narrow steps and gave access to the living chamber, the clock and the bell without disturbing the tenant of the shop.
     </p>
     <p>
      The old clock may have been removed in the 18th century and replaced by a pendulum clock. The present clock incorporates a four-legged gravity escapement invented by
      <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Beckett,_1st_Baron_Grimthorpe" title="Edmund Beckett, 1st Baron Grimthorpe">
       Lord Grimthorpe
      </a>
      , the local horologist and restorer of the Abbey who designed
      <a href="/wiki/Big_Ben" title="Big Ben">
       Big Ben
      </a>
      's mechanism.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Borough_of_St_Albans">
       Borough of St Albans
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Borough of St Albans">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      St Albans was an
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_borough" title="Ancient borough">
       ancient borough
      </a>
      created following the dissolution of the monastery in 1539.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It consisted of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_parish" title="Ancient parish">
       ancient parish
      </a>
      of St Albans (also known as the Abbey parish) and parts of
      <a href="/wiki/St_Michael,_Hertfordshire" title="St Michael, Hertfordshire">
       St Michael
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/St_Peter,_Hertfordshire" title="St Peter, Hertfordshire">
       St Peter
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Municipal_corporation" title="Municipal corporation">
       municipal corporation
      </a>
      was reformed by the Municipal Corporations Act 1835 and the boundary was adjusted to additionally include part of the parish of
      <a href="/wiki/St_Stephen,_Hertfordshire" title="St Stephen, Hertfordshire">
       St Stephen
      </a>
      . In 1887 the borough gained
      <a href="/wiki/City_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="City status in the United Kingdom">
       city status
      </a>
      , following the elevation of St Albans Abbey to cathedral, and the boundary was adjusted to include part of the parish of
      <a href="/wiki/Sandridge" title="Sandridge">
       Sandridge
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The Local Government Act 1894 divided parishes that were partly within municipal boroughs. The parts of St Michael, St Peter and Sandridge within the borough became the new parishes of St Michael Urban, St Peter Urban and Sandridge Urban. The part of St Stephen within the borough was absorbed by the parish of St Albans. The parishes that were formed outside the borough, that is St Michael Rural, St Peter Rural, Sandridge Rural and the reduced St Stephen, became part of
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_Rural_District" title="St Albans Rural District">
       St Albans Rural District
      </a>
      in 1894.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1898 the parish of St Albans absorbed St Michael Urban, St Peter Urban and Sandridge Urban so the parish and borough occupied the same area. In 1901 the population of the borough was 16,019, growing to 18,133 in 1911. St Albans expanded in 1913 by gaining parts of Sandridge Rural (241 acres), St Michael Rural (138 acres), St Peter Rural (992 acres) and St Stephen (335 acres). In 1921 the population of the enlarged borough was 25,593, growing to 28,624 in 1931. It expanded again in 1935 as part of a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_review_order" title="County review order">
       county review order
      </a>
      gaining more of St Michael Rural (890 acres), St Peter Rural (436 acres) and St Stephen (712 acres).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The population of the borough was 44,098 in 1951 and 50,293 in 1961.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The borough was abolished on 1 April 1974. It became an
      <a href="/wiki/Unparished_area" title="Unparished area">
       unparished area
      </a>
      within the
      <a href="/wiki/City_of_St_Albans" title="City of St Albans">
       City of St Albans
      </a>
      non-metropolitan district. City status transferred to the entire new district by
      <a href="/wiki/Letters_patent" title="Letters patent">
       letters patent
      </a>
      dated 9 July 1974.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government">
       Local government
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Local government">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Unparished_area" title="Unparished area">
       unparished area
      </a>
      of St Albans consists of the eight wards of Ashley, Batchwood, Clarence, Cunningham, Marshalswick South, St Peters, Sopwell and Verulam. As the only unparished part of the wider
      <a href="/wiki/City_of_St_Albans" title="City of St Albans">
       City of St Albans
      </a>
      , it does not have a
      <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">
       parish council
      </a>
      . Since June 2013 the City Neighbourhood Committee has functioned in place of a parish council, a role previously provided by the City Forum.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The neighbourood committee has a budget and decision making powers for small parks, playgrounds, open spaces, war memorials, allotments and public conveniences.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      St Albans is located in the middle of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_and_District_of_St_Albans" title="City and District of St Albans">
       City and District of St Albans
      </a>
      . The local authority for the whole district is
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_City_Council" title="St Albans City Council">
       St Albans City Council
      </a>
      , which also calls itself
      <i>
       St Albans City and District Council
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       St Albans District Council
      </i>
      . The irregular sounding name reflects the diverse nature of the area covered, consisting of eight civil parishes including two main urban areas and several villages. Typically districts with the status of cities covering large areas are known simply as Cities, for example the
      <a href="/wiki/City_of_Canterbury" title="City of Canterbury">
       City of Canterbury
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/City_of_Carlisle" title="City of Carlisle">
       City of Carlisle
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Some local government services are provided by
      <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire_County_Council" title="Hertfordshire County Council">
       Hertfordshire County Council
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="UK_Parliament">
       UK Parliament
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: UK Parliament">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="St Albans (UK Parliament constituency)">
       St Albans (UK Parliament constituency)
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      St Albans is a parliamentary constituency represented in the House of Commons of the Parliament of the United Kingdom. Established in 1885, it is a county constituency in Hertfordshire, and elects one Member of Parliament (MP) by the first past the post system of election.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">
       Transport
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Transport">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Two railway stations serve the town, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Albans_City_station" title="St Albans City station">
       St Albans City station
      </a>
      which is situated 0.5 miles east of the city centre, and the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Albans_Abbey_station" title="St Albans Abbey station">
       St Albans Abbey station
      </a>
      , which is situated approximately 0.6 miles south of the city station.
     </p>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_City_railway_station" title="St Albans City railway station">
       St Albans City railway station
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_Abbey_railway_station" title="St Albans Abbey railway station">
       St Albans Abbey railway station
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The City station is part of the main Bedford to Brighton line, and the Abbey station is a single track line to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watford_Junction_station" title="Watford Junction station">
       Watford Junction station
      </a>
      .
     </p>

      </span>

     <p>
      Clarence Park also plays host to St Albans Cricket Club.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">

        29
        <span>

        </span>

In 2008 the club's 1st XI won the Hertfordshire League Title. In the previous two seasons, the first XI came 5th (2011) and 4th (2012) in division one.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_League">
       Rugby League
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_Centurions" title="St Albans Centurions">
       St Albans Centurions
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rugby_League" title="Rugby League">
       Rugby League
      </a>
      Club have their ground at Toulmin Drive, St Albans. They play in the London Premier League. In 2007 and again in 2010 'The Cents', as they are known, won 'the triple' - topping the league, and becoming the Regional and National Champions of the Rugby League Conference Premier Divisions.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_Union">
       Rugby Union
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Old_Albanian_RFC" title="Old Albanian RFC">
       Old Albanian RFC
      </a>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">
       rugby union
      </a>
      club that plays at the Old Albanian sports complex. They play in
      <a href="/wiki/National_League_1" title="National League 1">
       National League 1
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/English_rugby_union_system" title="English rugby union system">
       the third tier of the English rugby union system
      </a>
      . Saracens A team and OA Saints Women's Rugby team also play here. This complex hosts the offices of the
      <a href="/wiki/English_Premiership_(rugby_union)" title="English Premiership (rugby union)">
       Aviva Premiership
      </a>
      club
      <a href="/wiki/Saracens_F.C." title="Saracens F.C.">
       Saracens
      </a>
      (and have recently moved their home ground to
      <a href="/wiki/Barnet_Copthall" title="Barnet Copthall">
       Barnet
      </a>
      ).
      <a href="/wiki/St_Albans_RFC" title="St Albans RFC">
       St Albans RFC
      </a>
      play at Boggymead Spring in Smallford. Verulamians RFC (formerly Old Verulamians) play at Cotlandswick in
      <a href="/wiki/London_Colney" title="London Colney">
       London Colney
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Skateboarding">
       Skateboarding
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Skateboarding">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      St Albans is home to one of the country's oldest and finest indoor
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skateparks" title="Skateparks">
       skateparks
      </a>
      , the Pioneer Skatepark in Heathlands Drive, next to the former fire station. Its ramps are available to all
      <a href="/wiki/Skateboarding" title="Skateboarding">
       skateboarders
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Inline_skates" title="Inline skates">
       inliners
      </a>
      . A new outside mini ramp was built in March 2005. A second outdoor mini ramp was opened at Easter 2009.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Links_with_other_sports">
       Links with other sports
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Links with other sports">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      St Albans is additionally home to a community of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traceurs" title="Traceurs">
       traceurs
      </a>
      from around Hertfordshire.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      St Albans was once home to the then most prestigious
      <a href="/wiki/Steeplechase" title="Steeplechase">
       steeplechase
      </a>
      in England. The Great St Albans chase attracted the best horses and riders from across Britain and Ireland in the 1830s and was held in such high esteem that when it clashed with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1837_Grand_National" title="1837 Grand National">
       1837 Grand National
      </a>
      the top horses and riders chose to bypass
      <a href="/wiki/Aintree" title="Aintree">
       Aintree
      </a>
      . Without warning the race was discontinued in 1839 and was quickly forgotten.
     </p>
     <p>
      St Albans was once home to
      <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Ryder" title="Samuel Ryder">
       Samuel Ryder
      </a>
      , the founder of the Ryder cup. He ran a very successful packet seeds business in the 1890s which at one time he ran from a packing warehouse on Holywell Hill (now
      <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_Rouge" title="Caf Rouge">
       Caf Rouge
      </a>
      ). His interest in golf and sponsorship led to his donation of the now famous
      <a href="/wiki/Ryder_Cup" title="Ryder Cup">
       Ryder Cup
      </a>
      . He is buried in Hatfield Road Cemetery, where in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Olympics_2012" title="London Olympics 2012">
       July 2012
      </a>
      the
      <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics_torch_relay" title="2012 Summer Olympics torch relay">
       Olympic Torch Relay
      </a>
      passed by to honour him.
     </p>

     <p>
      St Albans has many state primary and secondary schools, and number of independent schools.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Law School of the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hertfordshire" title="University of Hertfordshire">
       University of Hertfordshire
      </a>
      used to be based in Hatfield Road in St Albans until it moved to the university's De Havilland campus in Hatfield in 2011. Hertfordshire County Council purchased the site. The interior of the former Law School building has since been refurbished and now forms part of Alban City School, a state-funded Free School for primary aged children, which started taking reception class children in September 2012.
     </p>

      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">
       In popular culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Albans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: In popular culture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       The 1957
       <a href="/wiki/April_Fools%27_Day" title="April Fools' Day">
        April Fools' Day
       </a>
       spoof edition of BBC documentary series
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Panorama_(TV_series)" title="Panorama (TV series)">
         Panorama
        </a>
       </i>
       , which dealt with the fictitious Swiss
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spaghetti_tree" title="Spaghetti tree">
        spaghetti harvest
       </a>
       , was filmed partly at the (now closed) Pasta Foods factory on London Road, St Albans.
      </li>
      <li>
       The 2001 film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Birthday_Girl" title="Birthday Girl">
         Birthday Girl
        </a>
       </i>
       , featuring
       <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Kidman" title="Nicole Kidman">
        Nicole Kidman
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Ben_Chaplin" title="Ben Chaplin">
        Ben Chaplin
       </a>
       , is set in St Albans.
      </li>
      <li>
       A number of places across the world are named after the City of St Albans, most notably in Australia, New Zealand and the United States.
      </li>
      <li>
       St Albans was the name of a planet in the cult science-fiction television series
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Firefly_(TV_series)" title="Firefly (TV series)">
         Firefly
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       In September 2007, St Albans replaced
       <a href="/wiki/Mayfair" title="Mayfair">
        Mayfair
       </a>
       as the most expensive square on a special UK
       <i>
        Here and Now Edition
       </i>
       <a href="/wiki/Monopoly_(game)" title="Monopoly (game)">
        Monopoly
       </a>
       board, having won an internet vote.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Enter_Shikari" title="Enter Shikari">
        Enter Shikari
       </a>
       's song "All eyes on the Saint" (B-side of "
       <a href="/wiki/Juggernauts_(song)" title="Juggernauts (song)">
        Juggernauts
       </a>
       " single) tells the story of St Alban.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
        <a href="#cite_note-38">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         38
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       The BBC used the Main Gate House of the former St Albans Prison in Victoria Street as the main gate of "Slade Prison" in the sitcom
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Porridge_(TV_series)" title="Porridge (TV series)">
         Porridge
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
        <a href="#cite_note-39">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         39
         <span>
          ]
         </span>

       </sup>
      </li>
