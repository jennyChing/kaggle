            The last thing anyone wants when traveling is to fall into a tourist trap.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Youre all set to immerse yourself in an exotic culture when suddenly you realize youve been standing in line behind a man wearing a fanny pack for two hours. What happened?!
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            In an attempt to separate the cant misses from the cant stand its,
            <em>
             Lonely Planet
            </em>
            polled travelers on 10 of Europes most famous landmarks.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Most faired reasonably well with icons like The Parthenon (Athens), The Coliseum (Rome), and the Eiffel Tower (Paris) garnering overwhelming positive reviews.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            However, some famous sights like Stonehenge (Wiltshire, England) and the Leaning Tower (Pisa) received only tepid support.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Still others, like Irelands Blarney Stone and The London Eye were declared full-on tourist traps, over-hyped and over-crowded.
           </p>
