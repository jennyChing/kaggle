        <h1>
         England are dreaming if they don't think they are under pressure at the World Cup, claims Mitchell Johnson
        </h1>
        <ul class="mol-bullets-with-font">
         <li class="">
          <font style="font-size:1.4em;">
           <strong>
            England lost to Australia in the final of the tri-series
           </strong>
          </font>
         </li>
         <li class="">
          <font style="font-size:1.4em;">
           <strong>
            England captain Eoin Morgan claims England are not under pressure
           </strong>
          </font>
         </li>
         <li class="">
          <font style="font-size:1.4em;">
           <strong>
            Mitchell Johnson says every team at the World Cup is under pressure
           </strong>
          </font>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p class="author-section byline-plain">
         By
         <a class="author" href="/home/search.html?s=&amp;authornamef=Paul+Newman+for+MailOnline" rel="nofollow">

        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          They may try to insist he is just like any other opponent but England are simply kidding themselves if they think Mitchell Johnson does not pose a particular threat to them at this World Cup.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Johnsons spectacular return for Australia in their Tri-series final victory turned the clock back a year and provided a warning to England that he intends to remain their nemesis now and, more worryingly, in the Ashes this summer.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Certainly Eoin Morgans bizarre attempt to play down Johnsons significance after Englands third defeat to Australia already on this tour suggested that, deep down, the wounds inflicted upon them last winter are still gaping open.
         </font>
        </p>
        <div class="artSplitter mol-img-group">
         <div class="mol-img" style="height: auto;">
          <img alt="Mitchell Johnson celebrates taking the wicket of England captain Eoin Morgan" class="blkBorder img-share" height="418" id="i-7a7c50babcf38285" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/02/02/253E2B2300000578-0-image-a-14_1422866829857.jpg" width="634"/>
         </div>
         <p class="imageCaption">
          Mitchell Johnson celebrates taking the wicket of England captain Eoin Morgan
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="artSplitter mol-img-group">
         <div class="mol-img">
          <img alt="Johnson poses a major threat to England during the World Cup" class="blkBorder img-share" height="453" id="i-54a18e727c9a52cf" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/02/02/253E5E4F00000578-0-image-a-15_1422866857867.jpg" width="634"/>
         </div>
         <p class="imageCaption">
          Johnson poses a major threat to England during the World Cup
         </p>
        </div>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Surely better to acknowledge the success Johnson has had against England and show him a bit more respect rather than claim, unconvincingly, that he barely featured in the pre-match chat and then didnt threaten or swing the ball.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Johnson smiled when told that the new England one-day captain believed all the pressure will be on Australia when the teams meet again in a far more significant match when they open the World Cup in Melbourne on February 14.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Theyre dreaming if they think theyre not under pressure, said Johnson after his three wicket burst effectively settled Sundays final. I think every teams under pressure. Its a World Cup. Its a knock out situation and you have to try to win all the games that you play in. Thats our plan. Yes, there might be a bit more pressure on us being at home in a World Cup but were ready for that.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          The bad news for England and other contenders is that Johnson felt he was easing his way back when he ripped the heart out of Morgans middle order and seemingly softened England up for Glenn Maxwell to cash in at the other end.
         </font>
        </p>
        <div class="artSplitter mol-img-group">
         <div class="mol-img">
          <img alt="England batsman Eoin Morgan is bowled by  Johnson during the tri-series final" class="blkBorder img-share" height="436" id="i-fd35ca9466f6296b" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/02/02/253D7DA400000578-0-image-a-20_1422867124248.jpg" width="634"/>
         </div>
         <p class="imageCaption">
          England batsman Eoin Morgan is bowled by  Johnson during the tri-series final
         </p>
        </div>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          I felt a little bit rusty and nervous coming into it, said Jonson after his first game this year. I was just really keen to get out there and play a game of cricket, but I felt pretty comfortable once I was out there.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          My pace didnt feel too bad. I looked up at the screen a couple of times just to see how fast I was, as you do. But I had to stay away from looking up too much and just focus on what I needed to do. Out of the hand it felt really good most of the time but the more games I play the more I will get into that rhythm.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Johnson, who touched 90 miles per hour but no quicker in Perth, can clearly still be an intimidatory presence in white ball cricket as well as red as his wickets on Sunday seemed to show. James Taylor was caught playing away from his body, Moeen Ali was undone by a short snorter and Morgan inexplicably left a straight one. Meanwhile Maxwells innocuous off-spin claimed four wickets as England tried to make hay at the other end.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          I think you can intimidate in one-day cricket at times, insisted Johnson. Using the short ball definitely worked for me against England. Its just being able to adapt. Sometimes it wont work so much and Ill have to keep trying or go to Plan B. Im a fast bowler who likes to bowl fast and I dont mind bowling the short ball. I try that in all formats.
         </font>
        </p>
        <div class="artSplitter mol-img-group">
         <div class="mol-img" style="height: auto;">
          <img alt="Michael Clarke says he would be happy to play under Steve Smith on his return from a back injury" class="blkBorder img-share" height="421" id="i-209dac904ed1489b" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/02/02/253489CF00000578-0-image-a-22_1422867370373.jpg" width="634"/>
         </div>
         <p class="imageCaption">
          Michael Clarke says he would be happy to play under Steve Smith on his return from a back injury
         </p>
        </div>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          His return is one of many reasons why Australia will be expected to win the cup on home soil and it may prove particularly timely as James Faulkner could soon be ruled out of the tournament with the side injury suffered on Sunday.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Faulkner again showed what a threat he is with the bat with a rapid half century at the WACA which did much to take the game away from England while his death bowling was crucial when Australia again proved victorious in Hobart.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Certainly Faulkner would prove a bigger miss than sidelined captain Michael Clarke who, interestingly, said here that he would be happy to play under Steve Smith on his return from back and hamstring problems.
         </font>
        </p>
        <div class="artSplitter mol-img-group">
         <div class="mol-img" style="height: auto;">
          <img alt="James Faulkner could be ruled out of the World Cup with a side injury" class="blkBorder img-share" height="400" id="i-160756ada59c426a" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/02/02/253CB95200000578-0-image-a-24_1422867693244.jpg" width="634"/>
         </div>
         <p class="imageCaption">
          James Faulkner could be ruled out of the World Cup with a side injury
         </p>
        </div>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Clarke is said to remain on course to meet Australias fitness deadline of their second World Cup match against Bangladesh but his return could cause more problems than it solves because he is said to be at loggerheads with chief selector Rod Marsh and perhaps even his own team.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          The uncertainty over Faulkner and Clarke are the only clouds on Australias horizon as they look to be a powerful, potent one-day outfit full of fast scoring batsmen and attacking bowlers. England will have to play exceptionally well at the MCG if it is to prove fourth time lucky for them on this tour.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Before then England have been given this week off before they travel here to Sydney on Friday to begin their preparations for the cup in earnest. In the mean time it may be good for them if they stopped being in denial over Johnson.
         </font>
        </p>
