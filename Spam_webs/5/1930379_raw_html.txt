                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     DIY Customized Herb Pots with Elmer's Single Painters  ( #ExpressYourself )
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2968409778945023385">
                     I love my garden, but I am running out of room for all my favorite herbs. With the way I love to cook, can I ever have too many?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I wanted to decorate my front porch withdecorativepots of herbs for a while now, but have you seen the price of paintedTerracotta pots in stores? Yikes! This was one project I knew I could tackle myself, but the
                     <b>
                      #ExpressYourself
                     </b>
                     campaign and
                     <b>
                      <a href="http://www.elmers.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                       Elmer's
                      </a>
                      Single Painters Paint Markers
                     </b>
                     gave me the final nudge to just try it. I couldn't wait to see how easy (or not?) it would be.
                     <br/>
                     <span style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.917969); color: #222222; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-xGIsDtn_OGA/T7HtK-V_nEI/AAAAAAAAITg/Kdw4vNJgpWg/s640/IMG_2387.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="458" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-xGIsDtn_OGA/T7HtK-V_nEI/AAAAAAAAITg/Kdw4vNJgpWg/s640/IMG_2387.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.917969); color: #222222; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                      <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     We had the perfect place all picked out to put them. I wanted them right next to my lonely rosemary and mint pots.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Our local Walmart carries the
                     <b>
                      Elmer's Painters Paint Markers
                     </b>
                     in a wide variety of colors and tips. Not only can you find packages with multiple colors, but now you can buy the colors individually for just $1.97 each! Ours were located with the craft supplies section with the craft paints.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-0pT8ld6NUIc/T681ozhOQdI/AAAAAAAAILg/rWswEoPVAqs/s912/2012-05-12_20-17-11_327.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="358" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-0pT8ld6NUIc/T681ozhOQdI/AAAAAAAAILg/rWswEoPVAqs/s640/2012-05-12_20-17-11_327.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I fell in love with themetalliccolors, silver and gold, and also grabbed white and black paint pens as well. You can see our entire shopping experience in my
                     <a href="https://plus.google.com/photos/109798473145464946773/albums/5742774956371497985?authkey=CPqhx7z1sbTe6AE" target="_blank">
                      Google+ Album
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ns6jV-d2Lz8/T7HdVwt4jhI/AAAAAAAAIRY/7nlDOLlWo2w/s640/IMG_2447.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="458" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-ns6jV-d2Lz8/T7HdVwt4jhI/AAAAAAAAIRY/7nlDOLlWo2w/s640/IMG_2447.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Just shake the pen and hold the tip down till the paint begins flowing. I started mine out on paper first. After that, they are ready to go.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     These pretty pots are simple and inexpensive to make. I purchased a variety of brushes, sponges and a roller, but a simple small paint brush worked the best for me.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <u>
                       <i>
                        You Will Need:
                       </i>
                      </u>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     Clean terra cotta pots
                     <br/>
                     Acrylic Outdoor Paint - in colors of your choice
                     <br/>
                     Paint brush
                     <br/>
                     Elmer's Painters Paint Markers for decorations and writing
                     <br/>
                     Drop cloth or newsprint to put down
                     <br/>
                     Painters Tape
                     <br/>
                     Stencils or designs to use
                     <br/>
                     Pencil
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I started by taping off the rims and painting the pots one of three colors, red, orange or green. It was a beautiful day and they dried in under an hour while I was busy in the house.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Then I removed the painters tape and blocked off the body of the pot so I could paint the rim, inside and out. I chose a dark and neutral brown for both the rim and the bases.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rOFOui0gFWQ/T7HrGKIWfMI/AAAAAAAAITY/YP0cQJj68FU/s800/2012-05-141.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rOFOui0gFWQ/T7HrGKIWfMI/AAAAAAAAITY/YP0cQJj68FU/s640/2012-05-141.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I found a fun little design online, cut it out with my exacto knife and traced it with a pencil. Using the Elmer's Painters I could trace the design and fill in the gaps. The paint flows freely and did an excellent job of covering the paint underneath with a single stroke. I loved that it didn't require multiple applications or a long wait between use.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-oGdDPxjkr3U/T7HoNmFxFcI/AAAAAAAAIS8/ka5ik4ZxiiU/s800/2012-05-14.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-oGdDPxjkr3U/T7HoNmFxFcI/AAAAAAAAIS8/ka5ik4ZxiiU/s640/2012-05-14.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     With the first pot I embellished, my hand wasn't as steady. When I went back to outline the design, my black lines were too thick. The pens dried so quickly, I didn't have to wait to go back over them with more gold. Again, it covered perfectly allowing me to hide the flaws easily. Does that look better?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1c6wCu9XI88/T7HeMtd1kZI/AAAAAAAAISQ/hnCvvmi98TM/s512/IMG_2469.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1c6wCu9XI88/T7HeMtd1kZI/AAAAAAAAISQ/hnCvvmi98TM/s640/IMG_2469.JPG" width="456"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I decided to write the herb names directly on the pot rim. I liked the simplicity and color on the body of the pot. The Paint Markers write exceptionally well.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-vbBO_rhX3uo/T7HeWB5y_LI/AAAAAAAAISY/xnrC0rtj_qw/s640/IMG_2470.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="458" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-vbBO_rhX3uo/T7HeWB5y_LI/AAAAAAAAISY/xnrC0rtj_qw/s640/IMG_2470.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     For a final touch I painted some inexpensive wooden spoons to match the pots. I added the herb names, pronunciation and plant genus to the spoons with the Elmer's Painters Pens. I wish I had also purchased some fine tipmetallicpens, but overall it came out well.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-GUX37p-AlIY/T7HeiOXPEyI/AAAAAAAAISg/0CwliSUAhiI/s512/IMG_2471.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-GUX37p-AlIY/T7HeiOXPEyI/AAAAAAAAISg/0CwliSUAhiI/s640/IMG_2471.JPG" width="456"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I love how they turned out! I have the expensive custom look at a fraction of the cost and loved every moment spent making them.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-qu6zx9aqfug/T7HeoizAECI/AAAAAAAAISo/AV1H41ASnWU/s512/IMG_2473.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-qu6zx9aqfug/T7HeoizAECI/AAAAAAAAISo/AV1H41ASnWU/s640/IMG_2473.JPG" width="426"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I am going to enjoy these pots on our front porch, but I might be making a few more to give away as gifts. I will need to go back and expand my collection of Elmer's Single Painters.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
