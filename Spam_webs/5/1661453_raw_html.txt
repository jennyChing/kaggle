        <div class="post-heading">
         <h1>
          General Chiropody /Podiatry
         </h1>
        </div>
        <p>
         Circle Podiatry is your premium brand that provides true value for your money. If you are looking for a cheap quick fix, then unfortunately Circle Podiatry is not for you.
        </p>
        <p>
         We come alongside you and work together to achieve a longer term / permanent resolve to your problems. You will be provided with tailored solutions that are absolutely specific to your needs as an individual, your feet are unique to you so all our treatment options are not available on a general/mass level. Rather on first contact we go through the end results right from the start, and we will hold you accountable to doing your part, and will go the whole journey with youto achieve the best results for you
        </p>
        <p>
         If the condition or the problem you have is not listed here, just call our reception and we will be able to advise you.
        </p>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="Ingrowing-toe-nail1-262x200" class=" wp-image-2102 alignleft" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Ingrowing-toe-nail1-262x200.jpg" style="font-size: 13px;" width="210"/>
           Ingrown toe nail
          </h3>
          <p>
           Ingrown toenail is a painful condition characterized by the nail digging into the surrounding skin, leading to inflammation and possible infection of the toe. This is a serious condition for people with impaired circulation, diabetes or other systemic diseases.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="plantar-fascitiis-262x200" class=" wp-image-2108 alignleft" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/plantar-fascitiis-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Plantar fasciitis/heel pain
          </h3>
          <p>
           Plantar fasciitis is an inflammation of the long band of connective tissue running from the heel to the ball of the foot. Heel spur syndrome is a bony overgrowth on the heel bone. Plantar fasciitis and heel spur syndrome affect women more than men. They can cause the bottom of the heel and arch to become painful.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="tn__MG_2333-150x150" class="size-full wp-image-2103 alignleft" height="150" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/tn__MG_2333-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Arthritis
          </h3>
          <p>
           Arthritis is a disabling disease. It is characterised by inflammation of the cartilage and the lining of the bodys joints. Symptoms include swelling in one or more joints, recurring pain or tenderness in any joint, redness or heat in a joint, limitation in motion, early morning stiffness, and skin changes including rashes or growths.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="foot doctor london" class="wp-image-2106 alignleft" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/athletes-foot-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
           Athletes Foot
          </h3>
          <p>
           Athletes foot is a common fungal infection of the skin and nails characterized by itching, scaling, redness, and the formation of small blisters. In general the lesions start between the toes and can extend to the borders and bottom of the foot. In some cases as seen in clinic, for you as a man, it can cause further discomfort as usually trousers are worn from feet upwards- It is advised to get your feet checked as soon as you present with any of the above mentioned symptoms to avoid embarrassing complications!
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="foot podiatrist london" class="wp-image-2104 alignleft" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Bunion-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
           Bunions
          </h3>
          <p>
           A bunion is an enlargement at the base of the big toe caused by a misalignment of the joint. It may be swollen, tender and painful with the wearing of footwear. You can use our professional help to slow down the rate of deterioration/pain/discomfort using conservative methods such as orthotic intervention rather than going down the route of surgery, due to the risks associated with any surgical procedures. If allowed to progress, then your only remaining option may end up being a surgical procedure in which case we can advise you.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="MG_3070-262x200" class="size-full wp-image-2105 alignleft" height="200" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/MG_3070-262x200.jpg" width="262"/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Biomechanics
          </h3>
          <p>
           Podiatric Biomechanics is that specialism concerned with the preservation, restoration, and development of the function of the foot and its associated structures. Your foot plays a crucial part in the process of walking, and often suffers as a result of poor function. Painful foot problems that may result from inefficient function include common things like corns and callus, painful toenails, pain in the ball of the foot, in the arch, the ankle, or in the heel.
          </p>
          <p>
           Not only painful foot problems; less obvious, but very important, is the fact that inefficient foot function has an influence on other parts of the body.Just like an unevenly loaded the cargo ship, a small structural or functional imbalance in your foot may often cause no foot pain at all. It may however have an effect that may cause problems in the ankles, knees, hips, back, neck and even the jaw-all parts of the body that are connected to the feet by the skeleton. This effect may show as pain, instability, restricted movement, or in some cases just fatigue. Our biomechanical examinations are fully comprehensive and bespoke to you. In most cases we may combine the use of video gait analysis and or pressure plate analysis depending on your presenting conditions/problems.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="professional foot  podiatrist  london" class="wp-image-2109 alignleft" height="140" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Hard-corn-262x200.jpg" width="183"/>
           Corns or Calluses
          </h3>
          <p>
           A callus or corn is a buildup of skin that forms at points of pressure or over bony prominences. Calluses form on the bottom side of the foot. Corns form on the top of the foot , in-between the toes and any area of high pressure/friction.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="london podiatry centre" class="size-full wp-image-2107 alignleft" height="150" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/MG_3118-262x2001-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
           Diabetic Footcare
          </h3>
          <p>
           In the UK alone, there are currently 3.5 million people with diabetes and a further 7 million of us either at high risk of developing it or even walking around unaware we already have it. The second problem is most people just like you dont understand the link between Diabetes and Podiatry and even those with diabetes often dont fully really recognise the devastating long term effects of the disease pertaining to their lower limbs.One truly gruesome thought is the fact that every 20 seconds, someone somewhere in the world will be having a Diabetes related lower limb amputation. As a diabetic using professional help can greatly help you keep doing the things you love and also help to improve /maintain your quality of life. To discover how 2 minutes a day can help you keep your feet for life.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="flat-feet-262x200" class="alignleft wp-image-2110" height="140" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/flat-feet-262x200.jpg" width="183"/>
           Flat Foot
          </h3>
          <p>
           Flat foot is a structural deformity resulting in the lowering of the arch of your foot. This is sometimes referred to as fallen arches. If you have a flat foot or a highly arched foot that is painful, then you will need professional treatment. You will most likely have problems such as ankle, knee or lower back pain.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="foot-and-ankle-injuries-262x200" class="alignleft wp-image-2111" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/foot-and-ankle-injuries-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Foot and ankle injuries
          </h3>
          <p>
           Foot and ankle emergencies happen every day. Broken bones, dislocations, sprains, contusions, infections, and other serious injuries can occur at any time. Early attention is vitally important for you. Whenever you sustain a foot or ankle injury, you should seek immediate treatment from a podiatrist to avoid further complications.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="Superficial-fungal-nail-infection-262x200" class="alignleft wp-image-2113" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Superficial-fungal-nail-infection-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
           Fungal nail infection
          </h3>
          <p>
           It is not just a cosmetic problem. It is an infection of the bed and plate underlying the surface of a nail. Fungal nail infection, a condition called onychomycosis (ON-i-ko-my-KO-sis), is caused primarily by organisms called dermatophytes. It is extraordinarily common. Once these tiny organisms find their way under your nail, they begin to multiply. Ironically, when the fungus finds its way under your nail, the nail itself provides a protective environment for the fungus to thrive. Your toenails are most vulnerable to infection, since they spend much of their day surrounded by dark, warm, and often moist shoes and socks.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="gait-analysis-262x200" class="alignleft wp-image-2112" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/gait-analysis-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
           Gait analysis
          </h3>
          <p>
           When you are walking or running, your foot needs to be flexible enough to absorb the shock of hitting the ground thousands of times a day. Its got to be firm enough not to collapse as your bodys weight moves over it with each step. No wonder that often things start to go wrong with foot function, and pain or disability results. Usually its not until heavy or unusual demands are made on your feet by work, sports, injury, or simply by the passage of time, that potential weaknesses are revealed. Through our gait analysis, we can study and analyze how your joints and muscles behave when your body moves and any abnormalities that may present.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="Hammered-toe-262x200" class="alignleft wp-image-2114" height="112" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Hammered-toe-262x200.jpg" width="147"/>
           Hammer Toe
          </h3>
          <p>
           A hammertoe is a contraction deformity, resulting in bony prominences on the feet. If you present with this kind of physical changes within your feet, we will use biomechanical means to help you.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="mike-150x150" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2115" height="150" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/mike-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
           Neuroma
          </h3>
          <p>
           A neuroma is the swelling of nerve that is a result of a compression or trauma. They are a swelling within the nerve that may result in permanent nerve damage. The most common site for a neuroma is on the ball of the foot. The most common cause of neuroma in ball of the foot is the abnormal movement of the long bones behind the toes called metatarsal bones. Burning pain, tingling, and numbness in one or two of the toes is a common symptom.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <a href="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/IMG_20150417_020517-e1435194273499.jpg">
            <img alt="Orthotics" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2375" height="300" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/IMG_20150417_020517-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
           Orthotics / insoles
          </h3>
          <p>
           The most common treatment for biomechanical problems is the use of special shoe inserts known as orthoses, or more commonly, orthotics. They assist the feet to function more efficiently. They do this by controlling joint movement, and by altering some joint angles to prevent excessive motion.Orthotics and children Practically all foot imbalances that podiatrists see stem from childhood. Bunions, for example, thought to be caused by poor fitting footwear are, in fact, primarily caused by abnormal foot mechanics and aggravated by shoes. These imbalances are recognized as early as the age of three or four. If a child has feet that flatten or roll in excessively (called hyper-pronation) chances are that they will experience more serious problems in later life.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="pregnancy-and-the-feet-262x200" class="alignleft wp-image-2116" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/pregnancy-and-the-feet-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
           Pregnancy and the foot
          </h3>
          <p>
           When you are pregnant, the excessive weight gain combined with hormonal release relaxes certain ligaments in the body. As the fetus grows larger, the centre of gravity changes from the lower back to the front of the body. In the later stages of pregnancy, you will tend to turn out your feet for stability. This puts undue stress on the tendons, muscles, and ligaments of the foot, which may cause pain or mechanical problems such as bunions, tendonitis, ingrown toenails, and other inflammatory conditions.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="stress-fractures-262x200" class="alignleft wp-image-2118" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/stress-fractures-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Stress fracture
          </h3>
          <p>
           Stress fracture in an incomplete crack in the bone caused by overuse. A foot that is not structurally sound is prone to developing stress fractures. Even a normal foot can easily develop a stress fracture from repetitive use; symptoms may include pain, redness, and swelling.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="verrucae-262x200" class="alignleft wp-image-2120" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/verrucae-262x200.jpg" width="210"/>
           Plantar warts (plantar verruca)
          </h3>
          <p>
           Warts are one of several soft tissue conditions of the foot that can be quite painful. They are caused by a virus, which generally invades the skin through small or invisible cuts and abrasions. They can appear anywhere on the skin, but, technically, only those on the sole are properly called plantar warts.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="et-box et-shadow">
         <div class="et-box-content">
          <h3>
           <img alt="footwear-advice-262x2001" class="alignleft wp-image-2119" height="160" src="http://www.circlepodiatry.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/footwear-advice-262x2001.jpg" width="210"/>
           Footwear advice
          </h3>
          <p>
           Your shoe size is not a static measurement  sizes vary with style and manufacturer. Once you have a shoe that fits and flatters, you have to maintain it. Specialists recommend that you make it a habit to examine your shoes every time you put them on, for comfort, health reasons, and appearance.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
