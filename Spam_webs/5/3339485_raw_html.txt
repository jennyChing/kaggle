             <h1>
              GeneSure - PGD Analysis
             </h1>
             <h3>
              Imagine if you could double your chance of a healthy baby?
             </h3>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Geneas full in-house GeneSure technology offers a faster way to a baby. And its more affordable than you think.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              We all want a healthy baby, one who grows into a healthy child. Unfortunately, in some cases its not always how things turn out. Certain genetic conditions can cause birth defects or start to show up as a child develops and certain factors, such as knowing you have a family history of a genetic disease or being of advanced maternal age, can also increase the likelihood of your baby being affected. Geneas world leading GeneSure Preimplantation Genetic Diagnosis (PGD) is a way of reducing or removing that risk.
             </p>
             <div>
              <hr/>
              <p style="margin-left: 40px;">
               <span style="color:#0099cc;">
                GeneSure can offer you:
               </span>
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                <p style="margin-left: 40px;">
                 <span style="color:#0099cc;">
                  A faster way to a baby
                 </span>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <p style="margin-left: 40px;">
                 <span style="color:#0099cc;">
                  Double the success rate of PGD at other clinics
                 </span>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <p style="margin-left: 40px;">
                 <span style="color:#0099cc;">
                  Best chance of a healthier pregnancy
                 </span>
                </p>
               </li>
               <li>
                <p style="margin-left: 40px;">
                 <span style="color:#0099cc;">
                  The most affordable PGD available
                 </span>
                </p>
               </li>
              </ul>
              <hr/>
              <p>
               In Australia in 2012 (the most recent figures available), Genea was responsible for almost 50 per cent of the babies born using this technology. We led the way in investigating and developing PGD in Australia and our expertise and techniques are amongst the best in the world. We first launched chromosome testing of embryos in 1995 and expanded that treatment to offering single gene testing in 1998. You can learn more about Geneas world leading PGD expertise on our
               <a href="/my-fertility/why-genea/pgd-success-rates">
                PGD success rates
               </a>
               page.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="/Genea/media/Genea/Fertility%20images/Anzard%20graphs%20-%20Sucess%20rates%20by%20age/ANZARD-graph-PGD800x756.png" target="_blank">
                <img alt="PGD at Genea Vs other Australian and New Zealand Clinics" src="/Genea/media/Genea/Fertility%20images/Anzard%20graphs%20-%20Sucess%20rates%20by%20age/ANZARD-graph-PGD800x756.png"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <h2>
               How can GeneSure help me have a healthy baby?
              </h2>
              <p>
               In a standard IVF treatment, every time a cycle produces more than one embryo, our Embryologists must make a choice about which embryo will be transferred. That choice is determined by the development and appearance of the embryos, usually over a five day period to work out which one has the best chance of achieving a pregnancy.
              </p>
              <p>
               With Geneas GeneSure technology, our scientists analyse your embryos using advanced scientific techniques to assess their genetic make-up and therefore rule out embryos that contain an obvious genetic abnormality. This assessment can involve either a count of the chromosomes in each embryo and/or a molecular examination for a particular gene or mutation, these factors ultimately decide the health and chance of success of your pregnancy and any resulting baby.
              </p>
              <p>
               PGD with Geneas GeneSure technology can increase the chances of a genetically normal embryo being selected, implanting successfully and the chance of carrying and delivering a healthy baby.
              </p>
              <p>
               If youre considering IVF, PGD could be an important part of the equation towards you achieving your dream faster.
              </p>

             <h2>
              Can we benefit from PGD with Geneas GeneSure?
             </h2>
             <p>
              PGD is recommended for you if you:
             </p>
             <div>
              <div id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_acc">
               <input id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_acc_AccordionExtender_ClientState" name="p$lt$WebPartZone8$ZoneMainContent$pageplaceholder$p$lt$WebPartZone5$ZoneMainContentAfter$AccordionLayout2$acc_AccordionExtender_ClientState" type="hidden" value="-1"/>
               <div class="PaneHeader" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane1_header">
                or your partner are affected by or carry a known genetic disease
               </div>
               <div class="PaneContent" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane1_content" style="display:none;">
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 If you know you carry a serious genetic condition, genetic testing of your embryos before implantation is an effective and logical way to avoid passing that condition onto your future children.
                </p>
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 For example, when two cystic fibrosis carriers conceive a child, there is a 25 per cent chance that the baby will have cystic fibrosis, a 50 per cent chance that the baby will be a carrier and a 25 per cent chance that the baby will be unaffected. IVF with PGD allows the couple to produce a number of embryos, but to transfer only those that are either unaffected by the condition or carriers.
                </p>
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 Genea has developed the largest range of tests in Australia - for more than 200 inherited diseases. Custom tests are designed just for you and can be developed for any single gene disorder.
                </p>
               </div>
               <div class="PaneHeader" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane2_header">
                are of advanced maternal age
               </div>
               <div class="PaneContent" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane2_content" style="display:none;">
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 Typically, as a womans age increases, selecting a healthy embryo becomes more of a challenge using the IVF technique alone as an embryos appearance doesnt always tell the whole story.
                </p>
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 Its an unfortunate fact of reproductive science that the frequency of chromosomal abnormalities - thats when your embryo is missing a chromosome or carrying an extra one - increases with maternal age. These chromosomal abnormalities can cause your embryo to fail to implant or miscarry or they might result in a baby born with developmental problems or a serious condition such as Down syndrome.
                </p>
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 A form of PGD called
                 <a href="/my-fertility/im-a-patient/pgd-genetic-testing/what-does-pgd-involve">
                  Comparative Genome Hybridisation (CGH)
                 </a>
                 lets us screen your embryos to find ones that have healthy chromosome numbers. The good news is that our PGD results show that following the transfer of a healthy embryo, pregnancy rates among older women do not differ greatly to those achieved by women in younger age groups. CGH can provide you with the best and fastest chance of a healthy baby, particularly when time really matters.
                </p>
               </div>
               <div class="PaneHeader" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane3_header">
                have had recurrent miscarriages
               </div>
               <div class="PaneContent" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane3_content" style="display:none;">
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 Random chromosome problems are thought to be the cause of the majority of miscarriages - approximately seven out of 10 first trimester losses are caused by chromosome problems. What happens is that a genetically abnormal embryo implants in the uterus, but is destined to fail. Unfortunately miscarriages are not uncommon with approximately one in five pregnancies ending in a miscarriage. However, due to advanced maternal age or a number of other possible factors, some women are more prone to
                 <a href="/my-fertility/i-need-help/female-trouble-conceiving/miscarriage">
                  miscarriage
                 </a>
                 . If youve experienced multiple miscarriages, considering PGD as part of your treatment may be the best way to achieve your goal and GeneSure may be an option for you.
                </p>
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 Our scientists use Comparative Genome Hybridization (CGH) to screen your embryos to discover which ones have the correct number and sequence of chromosomes, to avoid the genetic faults that could be causing them to miscarry. We then only go on to transfer genetically healthy embryos which gives you the best chance of successful implantation and pregnancy.
                </p>
               </div>
               <div class="PaneHeader" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane4_header">
                have had a number of failed embryo transfers
               </div>
               <div class="PaneContent" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane4_content" style="display:none;">
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 Perhaps the problem youre facing is that your IVF cycles are not working because your embryo fails to implant after the transfer. If this has happened a couple of times, your Fertility Specialist may recommend PGD - Geneas GeneSure offers a higher chance of success per embryo transfer.
                </p>
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 To overcome this problem, Genea scientists will use
                 <a href="/my-fertility/im-a-patient/pgd-genetic-testing">
                  Comparative Genome Hybridization (CGH)
                 </a>
                 to screen your embryos for chromosome abnormalities before implantation. Only embryos that have the correct number and sequence of chromosomes will be selected for transfer to give you the best chance of successful implantation and an ongoing pregnancy.
                </p>
               </div>
               <div class="PaneHeader" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane5_header">
                want to prevent the transmission of a sex-linked genetic disease
               </div>
               <div class="PaneContent" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane5_content" style="display:none;">
                <p>
                 Using PGD to select the sex of your embryo is only allowed in Australia if the testing is being used to avoid passing on a specific sex-linked genetic disorder to a child.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The National Health and Medical Research Councils Assisted Reproductive Technology Guidelines restrict the use of PGD for sex selection in Australia. (
                 <a href="http://www.nhmrc.gov.au/guidelines/publications/e78" target="_blank">
                  See sections 11 and 12 of the ART Guidelines
                 </a>
                 ).
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 For more information on this please
                 <a href="/my-fertility/i-need-help/getting-started/ask-our-fertility-advisor">
                  contact us
                 </a>
                 .
                </p>
               </div>
               <div class="PaneHeader" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane6_header">
                are likely to develop multiple embryos of equal appearance.
               </div>
               <div class="PaneContent" id="p_lt_WebPartZone8_ZoneMainContent_pageplaceholder_p_lt_WebPartZone5_ZoneMainContentAfter_AccordionLayout2_pane6_content" style="display:none;">
                <p>
                 <span style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px;">
                  If youre having IVF because of changes in the male partners
                  <a href="/my-fertility/i-need-help/male-trouble-conceiving/male-tests">
                   sperm analysis
                  </a>
                  or Fallopian tube obstruction on the womans side, its likely that the female partners ovarian function may be very good and a high number of embryos may be produced in one IVF cycle.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p style="color: rgb(115, 115, 115); font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial;">
                 In this instance, PGD can be used to screen those embryos to determine which ones are also chromosomally normal  leading to you having the truly best embryo transferred first and achieve success faster.
                </p>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              To ensure more people can access GeneSure - Australias most successful PGD technology - Genea is offering a unique pricing option. Testing through Geneas exclusive genetic technology suite will start at $750 per embryo tested.
             </p>
             <p>
              GeneSure can increase the chance of a genetically normal embryo being selected, implanting successfully and your chance of carrying and delivering a healthy baby in a single cycle. If you would like more information please call and speak with one of our
              <a href="/my-fertility/i-need-help/getting-started/ask-our-fertility-advisor">
               Fertility Advisors
              </a>
              or discuss your options with your
              <a href="/my-fertility/doctors-and-clinics/find-a-doctor">
               Fertility Specialist
              </a>
              .
             </p>
