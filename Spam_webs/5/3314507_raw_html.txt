					&lt;h1 class="entry-title"&gt;Debtor, Consumer, or Investor: What Kind of Worshiper are You?&lt;/h1&gt;
					&lt;p class="headline_meta"&gt;by &lt;span class="author vcard fn"&gt;Andy Wood&lt;/span&gt; on &lt;abbr class="published" title="2014-08-18"&gt;August 18, 2014&lt;/abbr&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
					&lt;p class="headline_meta"&gt;in &lt;span&gt;&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/lifevesting-alter-egos/consumers/" rel="category tag"&gt;Consumers&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/lifevesting-cycle/enlarging-your-capacity/" rel="category tag"&gt;Enlarging Your Capacity&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/life-currency/esteem/" rel="category tag"&gt;Esteem&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/laws-of-lifevesting/" rel="category tag"&gt;Five LV Laws&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/life-currency/" rel="category tag"&gt;Life Currency&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/lifevesting-alter-egos/" rel="category tag"&gt;LV Alter-egos&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/lifevesting-cycle/" rel="category tag"&gt;LV Cycle&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/lifevesting-alter-egos/pleasers/" rel="category tag"&gt;Pleasers&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/category/laws-of-lifevesting/principle-of-increase/" rel="category tag"&gt;Principle of Increase&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
				&lt;/div&gt;
				&lt;div class="format_text entry-content"&gt;
&lt;div class="wwsgd" style="display:none;"&gt;&lt;p style='border:thin dotted black; padding:3mm;'&gt;If you're new here, you may want to subscribe to my &lt;a href='http://feeds.feedburner.com/LifeVesting/'&gt;RSS feed&lt;/a&gt;.  Or you can select your feed type by clicking on the "Subscribe" button on the right.  Thanks for visiting!&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://lifevesting.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/stained-glass-2.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9218" src="http://lifevesting.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/stained-glass-2.jpg" alt="stained glass 2" width="536" height="402" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;How was church today?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Thats a pretty common question in my family. With four households all involved in some sort of ministry, all living and attending four different worship venues, its not unusual for me to ask.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;But its also important for me to remember that Im asking a consumer question.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Im basically asking somebody in my family to evaluate their &lt;em&gt;experience&lt;/em&gt;. To interpret an &lt;em&gt;event&lt;/em&gt;. Yes, to tell me &lt;em&gt;what they got out of it&lt;/em&gt; or whether they liked the goings-on down at the church house.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Is that wrong? Not necessarily. But its a pitifully limited  and limiting  question.&lt;span id="more-9216"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;Did &lt;/em&gt;you go to church today?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;We dont ask that one as often, but sometimes it floats by one of us also. Woven into the question is that were supposed to be good Christians and good Christians go to church.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;But its also important to remember that this is a debtor question.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Were basically asking, Did you fulfill your obligation to get together with other Christians and worship God?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Anything wrong with that? No. But again, its a pitifully limited  and limiting  question.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Anytime you gather with other believers for worship, there are three lenses through which you can view it. You can be a debtor, a consumer, or an investor. Each has its place, because each is built on a measure of truth.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Worshipping as a Debtor&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The Bible makes it clear in case your Grandma didnt. Youre not supposed to skip church. not forsaking our own assembling together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another; and all the more as you see the day drawing near (Hebrews 10:25).&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;See there? Black-and-white. You owe it to yourself, you owe it to God, and you owe it to your local congregation to be there, to engage, to connect. After all, Jesus gave His all for you, now youre obligated to worship Him.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Just one problem with a debtors perspective. Okay, more than one. Worship as a debtor leads to a sense of drudgery. Its a checkbox and youre the pen. And once checked, its tempting to assume youre relieved of other duties until next week.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Second, forced gratitude is no gratitude. Remember when Aunt Jane gave you clothes for your birthday and you wanted toys instead? Remember your parents insisting on you saying Thank You to Aunt Jane? Remember how thankful you &lt;em&gt;really &lt;/em&gt;were? But you checked the box.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Third, it limits worship to what you do at the church house. And thats way too limiting for true worship.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;One other thing about debtor worship  the debt never, ever gets paid. You never graduate, never are excused from your duty, and can never fully repay for all you have been given (as if it were a loan or something).&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Worshipping as a Consumer&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Let me let you off the hook a little with this one. Its perfectly OK to receive spiritual or soul value from a worship experience. Id be a little suspect if you &lt;em&gt;didnt&lt;/em&gt; identify something you gained as a result of a genuine corporate experience.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Worship consumers take that a bit further. They make receiving something from the service a criteria, or form of judgment. (Pardon me dirty word I mean evaluation.) The service has to have your kind of music, your kind of preaching, your preferred method of response, and of course, your required programming for the kids. You can flex to accommodate other tastes or the preacher being off a time or two. But hey, you have needs, the first of which is to &lt;em&gt;feel &lt;/em&gt;something that a worship service is supposed to deliver. And if you dont get something out of it, whats the point?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Right?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Skipping the obvious rant this kind of thinking can generate, let me just offer one observation. Know whats wrong with consumer worship? Its like spiritual cotton candy. Once you taste and see that church is good, its over. Congratulations. Youve consumed.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Worshipping as an Investor&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I have fallen in love with Psalm 145 over the last several months. Its a psalm about worship. About how great our God is, and how, as David recognized the beauty of the Lord, he committed himself to worship God with all his heart.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;At one point, David says this:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="padding-left: 30px;"&gt;&lt;em&gt;One generation shall praise Your works to another&#8230;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;em&gt; Men shall speak of the power of Your awesome acts&#8230;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;em&gt; They shall eagerly utter the memory of Your abundant goodness,&lt;/em&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;em&gt; And will shout joyfully of Your righteousness (Psalm 145:4-7).&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The idea here is that these things are going to happen, one way or another. God is so great and awesome, it will be impossible for one generation &lt;em&gt;not&lt;/em&gt; to declare His greatness, His awesome acts, His abundant goodness and His righteousness. The only question is, will we join in the testimony? Will we declare to future generations the greatness of our God? Will we keep the memory alive of how God has been abundantly good, not just to the nations or in the past, but to us?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;That, my friend, is a different kind of approach. It is worshipping God from the perspective of an investor. Worship investors, having received abundantly from an all-powerful and good God, feel compelled to tell others of His faithfulness &#8211; &lt;em&gt;not just about how good a service is or was, but how great their God is.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Investor worship is praise on a mission. Not content to vent about life at the church house, worship investors, out of the overflow of their gratitude, take their declarations of Gods worth to the streets. They tell future generations. They share their experiences of Gods goodness with others.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The shout joyfully&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Tell eagerly&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Speak glowingly&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Praise faithfully.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;And they do it outside the church walls.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Now, get this David says this &lt;em&gt;is &lt;/em&gt;going to happen. Somebody &lt;em&gt;will&lt;/em&gt; worship as an investor. The only question is whether you and I will join in the celebration.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Know whats awesome about worshipping as an investor? Its a gift that keeps on giving. It lives on because it multiplies. Your testimony  your declaration  potentially becomes someone elses experience as well.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&nbsp;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;So next time the church bells are ringing in your heart (for most people, thats Sunday morning), go ahead and do your duty if thats how you need to see it. Enjoy the blessings of the fellowship or the sermon or whatever. Nothing inherently wrong with any of that. But if you want the day to live on, somewhere along the way, ask yourself the question: &lt;em&gt;Who can I share this with? &lt;/em&gt;Or maybe, &lt;em&gt;What is it about my God that is so amazing, I just have to share it?&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;You may just find a whole new generation of people who want what you have.&lt;/p&gt;
