            <h1 class="page-title">
             Skiing at Easter: Why It Rocks and Where You Should Go
            </h1>
            <div class="page-meta-wrapper">
             <span>
              By
              <span class="author">
               <a href="http://welove2ski.com/author/peakretreats/" rel="author" title="Posts by Xavier Schouller">
                Xavier Schouller
               </a>
              </span>
              <span class="meta-sep">
               |
              </span>
              on March 12, 2015
              <span class="meta-sep">
               |
              </span>
              <a class="link-comments" href="http://welove2ski.com/skiing-at-easter#comments">
               8 Comments
              </a>
             </span>
             <div class="categories">
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/brand-publisher" rel="category tag">
               Brand Publisher
              </a>
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/editors-picks" rel="category tag">
               Editor's Picks
              </a>
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/where-to-ski" rel="category tag">
               Where To Ski
              </a>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="page-content content">
             <div class="page-featured-image">
             </div>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27898" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27898" height="428" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/gorilla-imagesshutterstock.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  Gorilla Images/Shutterstock.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Xavier Schouller, MD of Portsmouth-based ski-drive specialist
               <a href="http://wl2s.co/peakretreats" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://wl2s.co/peakretreats', 'Peak Retreats');" target="_blank">
                Peak Retreats
               </a>
               , knows a thing or two about skiing. He grew up in France, first put on skis as a child in Val Cenis, and has been flying down the mountains in the both the Alps and the Pyrenees for the past 42 years. He has two young children at primary school.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              The secret to good Easter skiing trip isnt solely a question of aiming high. Ski areas which have above-average snowfall are also worth considering  such as the Grand Massif. Its close to Mont Blanc and (along with Avoriaz) is first in line when storms from the northwest hit the Alps. It has its own snowy micro-climate as a result.
             </p>
             <p>
              You should also look for those resorts with
              <strong>
               north-facing slopes
              </strong>
              like
              <a href="/val-cenis">
               Val Cenis
              </a>
               because the snow on them stays colder for longer. A glacier is always useful Easter-skiing insurance as well. In France, both
              <a href="/tignes">
               Tignes
              </a>
              and
              <a href="/les-deux-alpes">
               Les Deux Alpes
              </a>
              have one.
             </p>
             <p>
              Recent investments in snow-cannons mean that
              <strong>
               many resorts have snow left as late as May
              </strong>
              . When the lifts finally close they have to send piste-bashers out to actually remove snow as otherwise the farmers would go mad  as was the case in Val Cenis for a few years!
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Skiing at Easter has many advantages over mid-winter holidays
              </strong>
              . Here are a few of them:
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27897" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27897" height="321" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/gorillaimagesshutterstock.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  Gorilla Images/Shutterstock.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h3>
              The Weather Is Sunnier
             </h3>
             <p>
              This means you have longer days, can cut down on
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/ski-base-layers" title="Ski Base Layers: The Golden Rule for Keeping Warm">
               bulky layers of ski clothing
              </a>
              , and its a better time to learn to ski  especially for families with young children.
             </p>
             <p>
              Its much more fun having a lesson when youre warm  inevitably theres a lot of standing around during the initial learning stages and a harsh winters day when the thermometer plummets way below zero this can be a real challenge.
             </p>
             <h3>
              You Can Eat Lunch Al Fresco
             </h3>
             <p>
              Bring out your sunglasses and T-shirts at lunchtime, be generous with the sun cream, and take in the gorgeous mountain views on a restaurants lovely sunny terrace. If you are on a tight budget,
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/the-ultimate-ski-sandwich">
               picnic lunches
              </a>
              become a delight rather than a financial necessity.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Theres a Higher Number of Blue Sky Days
             </h3>
             <p>
              Generally thats true, but remember that there are also snowy days  and occasionally some of the heaviest snow of the season comes in April. Best case scenario is to wake up to a clear blue sky each morning after a night of fresh snow!
             </p>
             <h3>
              The Lifts Are Open Later
             </h3>
             <p>
              Daylight hours are lengthier too, so you can have a relaxed lunch and ski for that little bit longer. The Easter fortnight is popular in some resorts, but slopes are still positively empty compared with peak winter dates.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Prices Are Lower
             </h3>
             <p>
              This is not a peak time so prices are lower, especially when compared with the
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/half-term-stress-skiing-school-holidays" title="Half Term Blues Be Gone: How to Survive the Stress of Skiing in the School Holidays">
               February or Christmas holidays
              </a>
              . Mid-March is usually as cheap as mid-January and in April some resorts even drop their lift pass prices for the Easter ski holiday weeks. You can also pick up some pretty good bargains in ski shop end-of-season-sales.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Driving Out Is Easier
             </h3>
             <p>
              As you reach the mountains youre less likely to come across roads closed due to too much snow, and you might not even need snow chains (although I always recommend people take them, just in case).
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              But which resorts should you go for? These are my picks for Easter skiing
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              La Rosiere
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 2650m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27884" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27884" height="292" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/OT-La-Rosiere.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT La Rosiere
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="/la-rosiere">
               La Rosiere
              </a>
              , together with the linked resort of
              <a href="/la-thuile">
               La Thuile
              </a>
              on the Italian side of the Petit St Bernard pass, is something of a secret snow pocket. Due to a micro-climate created by the Mont Blanc Massif, both resorts habitually get better snow-cover than many of their neighbours.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              Some great nursery slopes for beginners at Les Eucherts with three free ski lifts. The village has received the highest official
              <em>
               Famille Plus Montagne
              </em>
              award.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying There
             </h3>
             <p>
              Les Balcons apartments have attractive rustic style interiors and are ski-in ski-out. Theyre located in the quiet Les Eucherts area.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Les Deux Alpes
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 3600m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27886" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27886" height="378" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/OT-Les-Deux-Alpes.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT Les Deux Alpes.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The Jandri Express jumbo gondola whisks you up from the village of
              <a href="/les-deux-alpes">
               Les Deux Alpes
              </a>
              to an underground funicular station at 3200m for the final ride to the top of the glacier.
             </p>
             <p>
              Some of the easiest skiing is up here, which means that complete beginners can learn high up on snow-sure slopes with lovely views, rather than on the sometimes slushy lower nursery slopes of many other resorts in spring time.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              Beginners have a choice of learning either on the glacier with wide easy runs or on the nursery slopes next to the village. There are five free lifts on the lower slopes.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying There
             </h3>
             <p>
              The family-run Cote Brun hotel offers half board accommodation and is located at the foot of the pistes. It has a large south-facing terrace and a spa area.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Montgenevre
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 2800m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27888" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27888" height="361" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/Montgenvrecommons.wikimedia.org_.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo: commons.wikimedia.org
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="/montgenevre">
               Montgenevre
              </a>
              is snowier and a lot more charming than its Italian neighbours in the Milky Way  such as Sestriere and
              <a href="/sauze-doulx">
               Sauze dOulx
              </a>
               and there are some excellent places to stay. The ski area suits intermediates and families best of all.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              Beginners have a good selection of gentle nursery slopes in Montgenevre, with reliable snow at the foot of the north-facing area. Theres also an open-air ice rink in the village.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying There
             </h3>
             <p>
              Hameau des Airelles are four-star apartments in a traditional-style residence right by the slopes. It has an indoor/outdoor swimming-pool and a spa.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Peisey Vallandry
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 3250m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27889" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27889" height="227" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/peissey-vallandr.com_.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT Peissey-Vallandry.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The back door village of the giant Paradiski area is half-way between
              <a href="/la-plagne">
               La Plagne
              </a>
              and
              <a href="/les-arcs">
               Les Arcs
              </a>
              . You can choose to stay either in Plan Peisey, which is the departure point of
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/where-to-ski/ski-lifts" title="The 12 Most Amazing Ski Lifts in The World">
               the giant Vanoise Express cable-car
              </a>
              , or in Vallandry at the edge of the forest.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              This is a good place for families to base themselves and both resorts have nursery slopes higher up in the tree-line, which are accessed by a chair-lift.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying There
             </h3>
             <p>
              Arollaie apartments are at the foot of the Vanoise Express cable-car, so theyre convenient for skiing the entire area. Theres a pool table and a heated indoor swimming-pool here.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Val Thorens
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 3230m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27780" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="How to Find the Best Snow in a Ski Resort | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27780" height="482" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/IMG_0325.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:   Welove2ski.com
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Set at 2300m, the resort of
              <a href="/val-thorens">
               Val Thorens
              </a>
              is the highest in Europe  and in five separate places, the lift system rises to 3000m or above. Many of the pistes face north, and next door youll also find
              <a href="/les-menuires">
               Les Menuires
              </a>
              and
              <a href="/meribel">
               Mottaret
              </a>
              , which are part of the same Three Valleys ski area. Together, they offer one of the most extensive and reliable Easter playgrounds in the Alps.
             </p>
             <p>
              There are drawbacks. Because of its setting in an steep-sided bowl, Val Thorens is a high-rise, high-density place, and with all the slopes above the treeline, it can feel a little bleak when the clouds come down. But that hasnt held it back recently: theres been a lot of investment here, not just in hotels, lifts and apartments, but restaurants, too. The resort is now the winter home of three Michelin-starred chefs.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              The resort has a purpose-built 10-hectare area with four covered magic carpets, aimed at beginners, and you dont have to buy a lift pass to use it. Meanwhile at the Galerie Commerciale de Caron, theres a big sports centre with two pools, a soft-play area and trampolines.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying There
             </h3>
             <p>
              Le Hameau du Kashmir is recently built and has its own pool, games room and an excellent restaurant, Le Karmin.
              <br/>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Samoens
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 2480m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27890" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27890" height="415" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/samoens-village-winter.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT Samoens.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The Grand Massif, which
              <a href="/samoens">
               Samoens
              </a>
              is part of, is one of the largest ski areas in France and boasts a super-snowy microclimate  thanks to its proximity to Mont Blanc.
             </p>
             <p>
              Samoens isnt a ski-in ski-out resort like neighbouring
              <a href="/flaine">
               Flaine
              </a>
               its a proper town with bags of character and a life outside skiing.
             </p>
             <p>
              Yes, you have to take a cable-car up into the ski area at the beginning of the day and another one down again at the end of the day, but how many resorts can guarantee being able to ski back down to the village at Easter anyway?
             </p>
             <p>
              The nursery slopes are north-facing and reinforced by snow-cannons when needed.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              Theres a nursery slope at Samoens 1600, with seven lifts and a new travelator. The beginner sector is separated from the rest of the ski area, making it extra safe for little ones.
             </p>
             <p>
              Dont miss the hidden fun zone called La Fort Myztrieuze de ZiziPanpan (ZiziPanPans Mysterious Forest), which includes a mini-zipwire.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying There
             </h3>
             <p>
              La Reine des Pres apartments are a 400-metre walk from the cable-car. It has an indoor swimming-pool and a spa.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Sainte Foy
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 2620m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27891" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27891" height="362" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/OT-St-Foy.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT Saint Foy.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The chalet-style village blends old with new and is close to
              <a href="/tignes">
               Tignes
              </a>
              ,
              <a href="/val-disere">
               Val dIsere
              </a>
              and
              <a href="/les-arcs">
               Les Arcs
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              The ski area is known for its off-piste  and locals head over here from the bigger resorts nearby on powder days. This is because
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/sainte-foy" title="Sainte Foy, France">
               Sainte Foy
              </a>
              has mainly north-west facing slopes, so the powder stays fresh for days.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              There are some gentle green and blue runs through the trees, and its a wonderful resort for beginners and families as its a lot less crowded than the better-known resorts.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying Here
             </h3>
             <p>
              Etoile des Cimes apartments are in the centre of the village and at the foot of the slopes. Theres an indoor swimming-pool in the building.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Tignes
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 3456m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27899" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27899" height="377" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/OT-Tignes.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT Tignes.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The snow-sure resort has summer skiing on its glacier, and is one of Europes 20 highest ski areas, so it makes an ideal destination for Easter holidays.
             </p>
             <p>
              In good weather, intermediates will have a ball in
              <a href="/tignes">
               Tignes
              </a>
              . The wide pistes are served by fast lifts that get you to and from next-door
              <a href="/val-disere">
               Val dIsere
              </a>
              . Several British ski schools operate in the resort.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              Tignes-les-Boisses (also known as Tignes 1800) provides a quieter base than the more mainstream Tignes-le-Lac or Val Claret. Theres a new beginners area and, once theyre more confident, novices can easily take the gondola into the rest of the ski area.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying Here
             </h3>
             <p>
              Kalinda Village has extremely comfortable apartments that are bigger than average. The residence houses an indoor swimming-pool and spa, mini-market and bakery, creche, ski school office and a ski hire shop.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Val Cenis
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 2800m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27893" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27893" height="297" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/val-cenis-OT.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT Val Cenis.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              This little-known resort of
              <a href="/val-cenis">
               Val Cenis
              </a>
              was one of the very few with a high percentage of slopes open that first week of the Christmas holidays, when a lot of other places were struggling. It has north-facing slopes, which are part of the reason.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              There are ten green runs, as well as some long blues. Prices are very reasonable here, as the resort is in the Maurienne Valley which is off the beaten track.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying Here
             </h3>
             <p>
              Les Chalets de Flambeau are attractive apartments right by the slopes, chair-lift and the ski school meeting place.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Vaujany
             </h2>
             <h3>
              Top 3330m
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27894" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Easter | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27894" height="279" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/vaujany-OT.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT Vaujany.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Little
              <a href="/vaujany">
               Vaujany
              </a>
              is an example of a satellite resort with access to a very high-altitude ski area  in this case that of
              <a href="/alpe-dhuez">
               Alpe dHuez
              </a>
              . A vast cable-car transports you into the heart of the ski area in next to no time.
             </p>
             <h3>
              For Families
             </h3>
             <p>
              The small farming village has hardly any traffic and is completely unspoilt, with a pretty church and chalet-style buildings. At Easter the village can be completely spring-like, with hens and goats roaming around, but youre whisked straight back into winter when you take the cable-car into the ski area.
             </p>
             <p>
              The resorts facilities include a sports centre with a 25m swimming-pool, sauna, steam room, fitness hall and an ice-skating rink.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Staying Here
             </h3>
             <p>
              Les Balcons de Vaujany apartments are inside four chalets right by the cable-car. They have been built in the typical mountain style of the area.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              When Is Your Favourite Time Of Year To Go Skiing?
             </h2>
             <p>
              Do you agree with me that Easter is one of the best times to go skiing or snowboarding, especially for beginners and families?  Let me know about your spring skiing experiences in the comments below.
             </p>
