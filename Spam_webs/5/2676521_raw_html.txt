           <p>
            Another one of our iOS developers is here to talk about Apples keynote. With us we have Vasiliy Makarov, also known as DrMoriarty on the Chupamobile community. He is the developer of
            <a href="http://www.chupamobile.com/ios-full-applications/e-course-maker-ios-starter-kit-1052?utm_source=chupa_blog&amp;utm_medium=post&amp;utm_campaign=interview_17sept" target="_blank">
             E-Course Maker iOS Starter Kit
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.chupamobile.com/ios-full-applications/radio-application-for-npo-2113?utm_source=chupa_blog&amp;utm_medium=post&amp;utm_campaign=interview_17sept" target="_blank">
             Radio application for NPO
            </a>
            . Keep reading to find out more about him.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.chupamobile.com/ios-full-applications/e-course-maker-ios-starter-kit-1052?utm_source=chupa_blog&amp;utm_medium=post&amp;utm_campaign=interview_17sept" target="_blank">
             <img alt="e-course" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2180" height="290" src="http://www.chupamobile.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/650x290xe-course.jpg.pagespeed.ic.kXnkeJ1OR-.jpg" width="650"/>
            </a>
            <strong>
             Chupamobile: Hi Vasiliy! Its nice to have you here. What can you tell us about yourself?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Vasiliy:
            </strong>
            Hello! My names Vasiliy Makarov. Im an iOS developer since 2009.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             C: What are your first impressions after having watched the keynote?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             V:
            </strong>
            I think that Apple every time takes more and more from Android. Several years ago some Android phones looked like iPhones. But now the iPhone6 looks like top Android phone.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             C: Higher screen resolution, better battery, bigger screen do you think the iPhone 6 and 6 Plus are finally different than the other iPhone generations? Why?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             V:
            </strong>
            The progress is obvious. Definitely the iPhone could never be like it once was. I think that there are two major things to be done- make the iPhone different from the previous one, and make the iPhone different from all other kind of phones. I think that this autumn Apple accomplished the first one, but Im not so sure about the second one.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             C: What do you think of the new graphics API,
             <em>
              Metal
             </em>
             , already introduced on the last June WWDC and repurposed now? Will it impact the market and change the feeling of playing on a smartphone? Or do you think playing on the phone will never be as exciting as playing on a real games console? Tell us your opinion about the future of smartphone gaming!
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             V:
            </strong>
            Very interesting question. Ive made casual games for iOS for several years now and every year I thought that I could predict the next year trend. I was wrong each time. I think that gaming on a huge TV with console is much more exciting than playing on the bigiPhone6 Plus screen. But definitely Im not the target audience for that kind of games. Im a hardcore gamer.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             C: Do you have the feeling the Apple Watch is going to open a new market for apps or you think is just a temporary trend? Do you see a real future in Apple Watch?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             V:
            </strong>
            I dont have an opinion about that. I wont buy Apple Watch and cant say anything about its future.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             C: So you don
            </strong>
            <strong>
             
            </strong>
            <strong>
             t you plan to work on Apple Watch applications?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             V:
            </strong>
            I think that smartphones are the ideal form of mobile applications. And we can discuss a lot about the screen size, DPI and the processor speed. As for Apple Watch, I think it cant replace a mobile phone or have any notable part of mobile market.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             C: Now it
            </strong>
            <strong>
             
            </strong>
            <strong>
             s time to get your apps compatible with iOS 8, are your products ready for it?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             V:
            </strong>
            Im still working on it. But this jump is much more easy than upgrading from iOS6 to iOS7.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             C: One last question: We
            </strong>
            <strong>
