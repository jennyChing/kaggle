      <h1 class="brandpost-font" itemprop="name">
       Samsung Mega Phablet Joins the Ever-Growing Galaxy Lineup
      </h1>
      <div class="article-meta">
       <div class="article-byline">
        By
        <a href="/author/Matthew-OConnell/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
         <span itemprop="name">
          Matthew OConnell
         </span>
        </a>
        , BrandPost
       </div>
       <ul class="fmenu">
        <li itemprop="datePublished">
         Aug 20, 2013 10:03 AM
        </li>
        <li>
         <a class="icn-link icn-email ir" href="#email" id="email-icon">
          e-mail
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a class="icn-link icn-print ir" href="javascript://" onclick="window.print()">
          print
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </div>
      <span class="brandpost-font" itemprop="articleBody text">
       <section class="page">
        <p>
         [
         <em>
          This sponsored article was written by IDG Creative Lab, a partner of Qualcomm.
         </em>
         ]
        </p>
        <p>
         After months of anticipation, Samsung has put a firm release date on the
         <a href="http://www.informationweek.com/hardware/handheld/samsung-galaxy-mega-phablet-debuts/240160154?cid=RSSfeed_IWK_ALL">
          Galaxy Mega Phablet
         </a>
         . The large phone-tablet hybrid was first announced
         <a href="http://www.techhive.com/article/2033824/samsung-mega-sizes-the-phablet-with-a-6-3-inch-display.html">
          back in April
         </a>
         , but it was only Monday that we finally learned exactly when we would be able to get our hand(s) on it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Samsungs incredibly popular Galaxy line-up has been steadily receiving new additions all year. The flagship S4 has spawned new variations including a more
         <a href="http://www.pcworld.com/article/2041695/rugged-galaxy-s4-active-joining-the-samsung-family.html">
          rugged waterproof version
         </a>
         , a
         <a href="http://www.pcworld.com/article/2038903/google-releasing-a-samsung-galaxy-s4-with-stock-android-os.html">
          Google edition
         </a>
         with a stock Android OS, the
         <a href="http://www.techhive.com/article/2041549/samsung-puts-galaxy-camera-on-a-diet-to-make-s4-zoom-smartphone.html">
          Zoom Edition
         </a>
         with a more powerful camera, and an
         <a href="http://www.techhive.com/article/2040283/samsung-unveils-galaxy-s4-mini-with-43inch-screen-and-dualcore-processor.html">
          S4 Mini
         </a>
         , not to mention the Galaxy Note and Galaxy Tab.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Mega phablet bridges the size gap between tablet and smartphone  perhaps the only remaining hole in Samsungs Galaxy inventory. With the S4 Minis display reportedly measuring 4.3 inches, the Galaxy S4 smartphone screen coming in at 5 inches, the Galaxy Note II measuring 5.5 inches, and the Galaxy Tab 3 coming in both 8 and 10.1-inch options, the Galaxy Megas 6.3-inch 720x1280 HD display hits that sweet spot right in the middle.
        </p>
        <p>
         Like its companion gadgets, the Galaxy Mega will utilize Samsungs popular software and powerful hardware. Air View, Multi Window, S Translator, ChatOn, and WatchOn will all come standard, operating on the Android 4.2.2 Jelly Bean OS. It will come with either 8GB or 16GB of internal storage, and also include a microSD slot for expandable memory. The Galaxy Mega will run on the same 1.7 GHz
         <a href="http://www.pcworld.com/partner/qualcomm/home/">
          Qualcomm Snapdragon
         </a>
         processor that powers the HTC First and HTC Mini.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Galaxy Mega might seem to round out Samsungs Galaxy smartphone offering, but may also just be the next addition in a long, evolving line of products. While tablets and smartphones have carved out their markets by offering distinct benefits, phablets walk the line in the middle, earning both praise and criticism. It may be due to their nature  larger than an average phone, smaller than a normal tablet. Some people enjoy having a compromise of two devices in one unit, preventing them from needing to buy both. Others find the hybrid approach to be anything but the Goldilocks of gadgets. Regardless, Samsung has a lot of fans, and anticipation for any new Galaxy device is always palpable.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Galaxy Mega Phablet officially releases this Friday, August 23
         <sup>
          rd
         </sup>
         . It will initially sell for $149.99 with a two-year contract through Sprint, US Cellular, and AT&amp;T, or for $24/month through
         <a href="http://www.techhive.com/article/2044469/atandt-and-verizon-scramble-to-compete-with-t-mobiles-jump-device-upgrade-plan.html">
          AT&amp;Ts Next Plan
         </a>
         . Pick it up in Nova Black or Polaris White.
        </p>
        <p>

function selectTSOAds(ads,max) {
	if (ads.length &lt;= max) {
		return ads;
	} else {
		var uniq = {};
		var found = 0;
		var selectedAds = [];
		while (found &lt; max) {
			var ad = ads[Math.floor(Math.random()*ads.length)];
			if (uniq[ad.id] != null) {
				continue;
			} else {
				uniq[ad.id] = true;
				found++;
				selectedAds.push(ad);
			}
		}
		return selectedAds;
	}
	return null;
}
