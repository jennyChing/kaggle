        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Celebrate 2015 Robbie Burns Day in Toronto
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-date">
          <a href="http://dehaanservices.ca/?p=4241" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-01-23T22:46:30+00:00">
            January 23, 2015
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://dehaanservices.ca/?author=2" rel="author">
            Mike DeHaan
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Where can you celebrate the 2015 Robbie Burns Day in Toronto? Or enjoy a Burns Supper in Toronto?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_4242" style="width: 295px;">
          <a href="http://dehaanservices.ca/?attachment_id=4242" rel="attachment wp-att-4242">
           <img alt='"Robbie Burns painted by Alexander Nasmyth" image owned by Scottish National Portrait Gallery' class="size-full wp-image-4242" height="380" src="http://dehaanservices.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/B84_Robbie_Burns.jpg" title='"Robbie Burns painted by Alexander Nasmyth" image owned by Scottish National Portrait Gallery' width="295"/>
          </a>
          <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
           "Robbie Burns painted by Alexander Nasmyth" image owned by Scottish National Portrait Gallery
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         When is Robbie Burns Day?
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         We honour the national poet of Scotland, Robbie Burns, on the anniversary of his birthday: January 25, 1759. The traditional Burns Supper includes piping in, reciting Burns Ode To, and eating a haggis.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         (
         <em>
          <strong>
           Added
          </strong>
         </em>
         Feb. 5, 2015): Learn to prepare and cook your own haggis at Mackenzie House Museum in Toronto on Sat. March 14 from 10am-1pm. Maggie Newell will share her expertise in the culinary arts of the period. Be ready for next years Robbie Burns Day! Heres the link to the
         <a href="http://www1.toronto.ca/wps/portal/contentonly?vgnextoid=8ccf1d18ed7a0410VgnVCM10000071d60f89RCRD" target="_blank" title="Link to Mackenzie House events page">
          Mackenzie House events page
         </a>
         ; but the event probably wont be listed after the date has passed. (And now back to Robbie Burns Day 2015).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Yes, a Burns Supper is one of many annual Toronto events;
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         Where does Toronto Celebrate Robbie Burns Day in 2015?
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The
         <a href="http://www.standrews-society.ca/event/burns-statue-event/" target="_blank" title="Link to St. Andrews Society of Toronto Robbie Burns event">
          St. Andrews Society of Toronto celebrates the 2015 Robbie Burns Day
         </a>
         meets at Allen Gardens at 11am on Sun. Jan. 25. Gather by the Burns statue on the east side of the park, nearer Sherbourne St. The Society offers piping, haggis and other delectable fare (at a pub, so be prepared to pay your way after the free outdoor ceremonies).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Allen Gardens is in downtown Toronto at 19 Horticultural Ave. The park is bounded by Carlton, Sherbourne, Gerrard and Jarvis. (Horticultural Ave. comes north from Gerrard St.).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Among the Toronto museums,
         <a href="http://www1.toronto.ca/wps/portal/contentonly?vgnextoid=8ccf1d18ed7a0410VgnVCM10000071d60f89RCRD" target="_blank" title="Link to Robbie Burns day at Mackenzie House in Toronto">
          Mackenzie House concentrates on Robbie Burns
         </a>
         for Sunday afternoon from noon to 4pm on Jan. 25. They offer shortbread and a taste of haggis, with music, for the standard under-$10 admission fee. Mackenzie House is at 82 Bond St. (Thats a few short blocks east of Yonge, south of Dundas Square, but west of Church and well north of Shuter).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Since I advocate free and frugal events, lets only mention that Casa Loma will charge a lot of money for a 4-course Burns Supper on Sat. Jan. 24. Johnny Walker sponsors this whiskey tasting meal, but the Liberty Group still charges a hefty price tag. Maybe thats just to make sure that only true fans will attend; hmm, theres a challenge for you.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Other Toronto restaurants also host a Burns Supper theme this weekend; just run a search combining Robbie Burns and restaurants in Toronto.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         Avoiding Robbie Burns in Toronto
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         You could take a nature walk at Todmorden Mills on Robbie Burns Day. Their 
         <a href="http://www1.toronto.ca/wps/portal/contentonly?vgnextoid=93221d18ed7a0410VgnVCM10000071d60f89RCRD" target="_blank" title='Link to "Winter in the Valley" at Todmorden Mills in Toronto'>
          Winter in the Valley
         </a>
          event runs for an hour from 1:30pm on Sun. Jan. 25. For under $10, you can learn how the Don Valleys wildlife survives the winter; and sample hot cider.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Todmorden Mills, another Toronto museum, is at 67 Pottery Road, just east of the Don River and on the south side of the road. You cant miss seeing the chimney! Pottery Road links Broadview (at Mortimer) and Bayview (south of Moore Park).
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #993300;">
          Looking for More about Robbie Burns?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Get the
         <span style="color: #008000;">
          <strong>
           free
          </strong>
         </span>
         Kindle edition of Poems and Songs of Robert Burns from
         <a href="http://www.amazon.ca/Poems-Songs-Robert-Burns-ebook/dp/B0082RI1SQ/" target="_blank" title='Link to "Poems and Songs of Robert Burns" at Amazon Canada'>
          Amazon Canada
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_noss?url=search-alias%3Daps&amp;field-keywords=B0082RI1SQ" target="_blank" title='Link to "Poems and Songs of Robert Burns" at Amazon USA'>
          Amazon USA
         </a>
         for free!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Or buy the amazingly frugal paperback version of Haggis: the Recipe Book: 30 Ways with Haggis from
         <a href="http://www.amazon.ca/Haggis-Recipe-Book-30-Ways/dp/0956212646/" target="_blank" title='Link to "Haggis: the Recipe Book: 30 Ways with Haggis" at Amazon Canada'>
          Amazon Canada
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #993300;">
          Looking for More Annual Events or a Holiday in Toronto Canada?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         My blog tracks many of the annual Toronto events that I find interesting, free or frugal.
        </p>
