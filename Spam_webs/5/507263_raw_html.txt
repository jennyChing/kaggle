               <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
                Snowflake Christmas Tree Skirt
               </h1>
               <div class="post-meta the-icons pmeta-alt">
                <span class="post-author vcard">
                 <i class="fa fa-user">

              <div class="post-content">
               <div class="entry-content">
                <p>
                 Weve always had a real Christmas tree and for years I used to place it in a large plant pot, packed with stones to stop it from falling sideways. A few years ago we bought a Christmas-tree stand which is a lot easier to use.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/SnowflakeChristmasTreeSkirt2.jpg">
                  <img alt="Snowflake Embroidered Christmas Tree Skirt | Hellie's Corner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4777" height="524" src="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/SnowflakeChristmasTreeSkirt2.jpg" width="450"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 The tree usually stands in the hall, but earlier in the year we decided that that particular spot would work well as a place for my desk and sewing table. So the Christmas tree has been relocated to the living room. The tree looks great, but now I can see the stand, so the move has forced me to make a skirt for it  a job Id been intending to do for a few years!
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/ChristmasTreeStand1.jpg">
                  <img alt="Before - Christmas Tree Stand | Hellie's Corner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4748" height="411" src="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/ChristmasTreeStand1.jpg" width="450"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 I decided to make a patchwork-circle design with six panels, using a piece of green cotton fabric with a white snowflake print on it and a second piece of plain-green brushed cotton. I used a piece of plain-purple cotton material for the lining.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Once Id established the diameter of the circle I would need, I drew up a pattern using some old newspaper.
                </p>
                <p>
                 I wanted to embroider a snowflake design on each of the plain-green pieces, so I roughly designed a snowflake on a piece of plain paper to act as a guide. I then measured and markedwith a pencil, where I wanted the snowflake to be on the material and placeed the material in a medium-sized embroidery hoop.
                </p>
                <p>
                 I love using free-motion embroidery, so after drawing each of the snowflakes in pencil onto the material, I set my machine up by dropping the feed dogs (consult your sewing machine manual to see how to do this) and embroidered a different snowflake onto each of the panels.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/FreeMotionEmbroideredSnowflake.jpg">
                  <img alt="Free Motion Embroidered Snowflake | Hellie's Corner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4749" height="492" src="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/FreeMotionEmbroideredSnowflake.jpg" width="400"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/FreeMotionEmbroideredSnowflake1.jpg">
                  <img alt="Three Free Motion Embroidered Snowflake | Hellie's Corner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4753" height="695" src="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/FreeMotionEmbroideredSnowflake1.jpg" width="700"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 Once I had embroidered all three of the snowflakes, I assembled the skirt. Firstly, with right sides facing, I attached the front to the lining at the neck edge. Then, with the skirt turned the right way round, I pinned around the three remaining edges, pinned cord ties to close the open edge of the skirt together and then sewed a hem around the pinned edges.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <a href="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/SnowflakeChristmasTreeSkirt1.jpg">
                  <img alt="Snowflake Embroidered Christmas Tree Skirt | Hellie's Corner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4750" height="337" src="http://www.helliescorner.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/SnowflakeChristmasTreeSkirt1.jpg" width="500"/>
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 I fitted the skirt onto the stand, resting the waist part just above the screws that hold the Christmas tree in place, then tightened the ties to keep it closed. The top of the skirt sits below the top of the tube that the tree trunk sits in, so we can still water the tree every now and again.
                </p>
