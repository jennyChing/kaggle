         White Chicken Chili  4 WW Points Plus
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-09-20T01:09:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          September 20, 2013
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         </div>
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AIgihqkaJxE/UjuVURmvUII/AAAAAAAAJx0/rDg4lOy03WY/s1600/White+Chicken+Chili.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img border="0" src="http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/White-Chicken-Chili2.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          Grumpy is not a chicken kinda guy. If I make chicken once a week it can be too often for him. However, Im not a beef kinda girl. I just never cared for it. I do my best to make recipes that are good for us and if I can sneak chicken in it Im a lot happier.
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.grumpyshoneybunch.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          This White Chicken Chili is a great weeknight meal and just one cup of it is 4 weight watcher points plus (if your counting that is). I went with 1-1/2 cups for 7 points. Im looking forward for leftovers for lunch tomorrow. I made sure to get another serving out before Grumpy finished it up on me! He really liked this recipe but he thought it should be a little thicker. Next time I will mash an extra cup of beans in the recipe to thicken.
         </p>

         <div class="therecipewizprintwrapper" id="White-Chicken-Chili">
          <div class="hrecipe" id="therecipewiz" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/Recipe">
           print recipe
           <div class="item" id="rechead">
            <img class="photo" itemprop="photo" onclick="window.open('http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/White-Chicken-Chili2.jpg','Recipe Image' );" src="http://52.24.202.75/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/White-Chicken-Chili2.jpg" width="100px"/>
            <div class="recabout">
             <div class="title fn" itemprop="name">
              White Chicken Chili
             </div>
             <div class="byline">
              September-19-2013
             </div>
             <div class="summary" itemprop="summary">
              slightly adapted from Cooking Light.
              <br/>
              Weight Watcher Points Plus = 4 for 1 cup of chili
             </div>
             <div class="clear">
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div id="recbody">
            <div class="subtitle">
             Ingredients
            </div>
            <ul>
             <li>
              1 tablespoon   olive oil
             </li>
             <li>
              1 lb   chicken breast, cut into bite sized pieces
             </li>
             <li>
              1 teaspoon   adobo with pepper (or salt)
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 cup   onion slices, cut vertically
             </li>
             <li>
              2 teaspoons   cumin
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 teaspoon   corriander
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 teaspoon   oregano
             </li>
             <li>
              2 (4 oz each)   cans of green chilies
             </li>
             <li>
              2-15 oz cans   cannellini beans, rinsed and drained
             </li>
             <li>
              1 cup   water
             </li>
             <li>
              1  14 ounce can   chicken broth
             </li>
             <li>
              1/4 cup    cilantro
             </li>
             <li>
              1   lime (6 wedges)
             </li>
            </ul>
            <div class="clear">
            </div>
            <div class="subtitle">
             Instructions
            </div>
            <div itemprop="instructions">
             Heat a deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add oil. Sprinkle chicken with 1/2 teaspoon adobo seasoning (or salt). Add chicken; saut 4 until no longer pink. Add onion and next 5 ingredients; saut 3-4 minutes. Add all but 1 cups beans, water, 1/2 teaspoon adobo seasoning, 1 can chiles, and broth; bring to a boil.
             <br/>
             Mash 1 cup beans and 1 can chiles in a bowl. Add to soup; simmer 5 minutes. Serve with cilantro and lime.
            </div>
            <div class="clear">
            </div>
            <div class="subtitle">
             Details
            </div>
            <p>
             Ready in: 20 mins Yield: 6 servings
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
