       <div class="entry-content c3-9">
        <p>
         Here is great reporting
         <a href="http://www.haaretz.com/print-edition/news/merkel-chides-netanyahu-for-failing-to-make-a-single-step-to-advance-peace-1.345539">
          at Haaretz
         </a>
         on the Israeli Prime Minister calling Germany after the Security Council vote last week condemning settlements and expressing his dismay at Germanys assent. German Chancellor Angela Merkel wasnt buying. When will such a bald description of Israels western-rogue status find its way into American papers? (an understanding, by the way, that Seham
         <a href="http://mondoweiss.net/2011/02/netanyahu-seems-as-delusional-as-qadhafi.html">
          expressed here yesterday
         </a>
         with slashing insight). Also, contrast Merkels stellar performance here with the passive high-faluting rhetoric of our president, hobbled by the lobby and the whole delegitimization narrative that prevents any real understanding.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Netanyahu told Merkel he was disappointed by Germanys vote and by Merkels refusal to accept Israels requests before the vote, the source added. Merkel was furious.
         </p>
         <p>
          How dare you, she said, according to the official. You are the one who disappointed us. You havent made a single step to advance peace.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-37148">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          The prime minister assured Merkel that he intended to launch a new peace plan that would be a continuation of his Bar-Ilan University speech, given in June 2009, in which he agreed to establishing a Palestinian state, the official revealed.
         </p>
         <p>
          I intend to make a new speech about the peace process in two to three weeks, Netanyahu told Merkel.
         </p>
         <p>
          The German chancellor and her advisers, who have been repeatedly disappointed by Netanyahus inaccurate statements and failure to keep promises, did not believe a word of what the prime minister told her, the source said.
         </p>
         <p>
          Haaretzs check with a number of Israeli sources indicates that the prime minister and his advisers are desperately looking for a way to jumpstart the peace process, in view of Israels growing international isolation.
         </p>
        </blockquote>

              </p>

He said she made the same criticism in Netanyahus presence on her trip to Israel last month.
              </p>

               Merkel and Netanyahu spoke two days after Germany supported a draft resolution in the United Nations Security Council that would have declared Israels settlement construction in the West Bank and East Jerusalem to be illegal and demanded a halt to such activity. The U.S. vetoed the draft.
              </p>
              <p>
               It is not our practice to comment on stories in the media, Mark Regev, Netanyahus spokesman, said in a statement.
              </p>
              <p>
               Netanyahu called Merkel to express his disappointment over Germanys support of the measure, Haaretz said. The newspaper described Merkels response as furious, reporting that she responded by expressing her disappointment in Netanyahu.
              </p>
              <p>
               The U.S. was alone in opposing the measure on the 15-member council, the UNs principal policy-making panel. It was the Obama administrations first veto of a UN resolution and marked the 10th time in the past 11 years that the U.S. has voted against a text considered to be critical of Israel.
              </p>

               A quick google search will reveal that Merkel has been constantly pressuring Bibi about the settlements, about Bibis not making any effort at all toward peace since at least 2007. She has also been disappointed in the US not pressuring Bibi in behalf some movement towards peace.
