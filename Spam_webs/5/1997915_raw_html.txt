         <p>
          <a href="http://www.bigprofile.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/la-fi-tn-facebook-to-crack-down-on-antiwomen-h-001-300x210.jpeg">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-2239" height="189" src="http://www.bigprofile.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/la-fi-tn-facebook-to-crack-down-on-antiwomen-h-001-300x210.jpeg" title="Facebook " width="270"/>
          </a>
          Facebook announced a new review policy to limit branded ads appearing on pages containing offensive content last week in the wake of recent domestic violence controversy. This policy will be implemented through the Facebook staff manually checking content on the network to ensure they are appropriate for branded ads to appear on them. Inappropriate pages where Facebook ads will be restricted from include those containing violent, graphic or sexual content. Not only will Facebook prevent future use of branded ads on offensive pages but it will remove ads from any existing pages by the end oft his week.
         </p>
         <p>
          Some of the brands involved in stopping or suspending ads alongside offensive content have yet to register a vote of confidence in these changes. Brands such as Sky and Marks and Spencer claim they are working closely with Facebook and monitoring the situation in order to understand the measures that need to be taken to prevent this from reoccurring.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ensuring ads do not appear on illegal websites will help to give advertisers increased confidence in advertising online. However, it is sill important to acknowledge that this problem is worldwide and impossible to always be in complete control of.
          <em>
          </em>
         </p>
