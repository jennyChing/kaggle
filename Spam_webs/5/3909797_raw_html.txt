       <h1 class="entry-title">
        From Idle to Idol: How American Idols Comeback is Inspiring Marketers, Creators, and Business Leaders
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2014-01-28T10:01:54+00:00">
         January 28, 2014
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="https://contentequalsmoney.com/author/daniel/" rel="author">
           Daniel
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div id="leftcontainerBox" style="border:1px solid #808080;background-color:#F0F4F9;position:fixed; top:40%; left:30px;">
         <div class="buttons">
          <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" src="https://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=https%3A%2F%2Fcontentequalsmoney.com%2Ffrom-idle-to-idol-how-american-idols-comeback-is-inspiring-marketers-creators-and-business-leaders%2F&amp;layout=box_count&amp;show_faces=false&amp;action=like&amp;font=verdana&amp;colorscheme=light" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:60px; height:65px;">
          </iframe>
         </div>
         <div class="buttons">
          <a class="twitter-share-button" data-count="vertical" data-text="From Idle to Idol: How American Idols Comeback is Inspiring Marketers, Creators, and Business Leaders" data-url="https://contentequalsmoney.com/from-idle-to-idol-how-american-idols-comeback-is-inspiring-marketers-creators-and-business-leaders/" href="https://twitter.com/share">
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="buttons">
          <g:plusone href="https://contentequalsmoney.com/from-idle-to-idol-how-american-idols-comeback-is-inspiring-marketers-creators-and-business-leaders/" size="tall">

         </div>
         <div class="buttons" style="padding-left: 0px;">
          <script data-counter="top" data-url="https://contentequalsmoney.com/from-idle-to-idol-how-american-idols-comeback-is-inspiring-marketers-creators-and-business-leaders/" type="in/share">
          </script>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div style="clear:both">
        </div>
        <p>
         Lets face it: if you were surprised to hear that American Idol Season 13 premiered last week, you werent alone. Though it easily ranked as the evenings most watched program, Idol isnt the ratings juggernaut it once was. As a television ratings and box office junkie, Ive followed Idol through its 
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_Star#Television">
          death star
         </a>
          days when it
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2010/02/18/sports/olympics/18ratings.html">
          beat the Olympics
         </a>
         to its least watched finale last season.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="idol" class="aligncenter wp-image-13767" height="400" src="https://contentequalsmoney.com/wp-content/uploads/media/2014/01/idol-1024x746.jpg" width="549"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Therefore, I wasnt surprised when I saw headlines announcing that Season 13s premiere set a series low at
         <a href="http://insidetv.ew.com/2014/01/16/american-idol-ratings-13/">
          15 million
         </a>
         viewers.
         <i>
          Yikes.
         </i>
         What I didnt expect, however, were the astonishing Live +3 Ratings, which calculate the viewers who watch a program through TiVo, DVR, or another recording device within 3 days of the original airing.
        </p>
        <p>
         What was so astonishing? The Live +3 Ratings for Idol show a
         <a href="http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2014/01/20/live-3-ratings-american-idol-premiere-draws-higher-percentage-gains-narrows-gap-vs-last-year/230662/">
          15 percent
         </a>
         gain in the 18-49 adults demographic and
         <a href="http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2014/01/20/live-3-ratings-american-idol-premiere-draws-higher-percentage-gains-narrows-gap-vs-last-year/230662/">
          32 percent
         </a>
         more viewers from last years finale. Simply put: word of mouth was so strong following the Season 13 premiere that Idol gained an additional third of its original viewership, making it the
         <a href="http://www.thefutoncritic.com/ratings/2014/01/22/abc-delivers-4-of-the-weeks-top-20-non-sports-tv-shows-in-adults-18-49-39511/20140122abc05/">
          most watched
         </a>
         non-sports program in 2014.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are two primary questions this raises:
        </p>
        <ol start="1">
         <li>
          What did American Idol do to achieve such a notable ratings comeback?
         </li>
         <li>
          What does this mean for marketers, content creators, and business leaders?
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h2>
         For Idol, Old is the
         <i>
          New
         </i>
         New
        </h2>
        <p>
         Ever since the original trio of Randy, Paula, and Simon left the judges table, American Idol has experienced something of a free fall. Year after year, wed tune in for changes that promised a fresher show. But new contestants, new judges, new mentors, new rules, and a new format pushed viewers away. Idol was stuck in quicksand, and the more producers struggled to improve the show, the deeper it sank.
        </p>
        <p>
         Before Season 13 began, producers promised something new: a return to the old Idol. Viewer surveys following last season revealed that overstuffed results shows, drawn out middle rounds, and drama between judges Maria Carey and Nicki Minaj hurt the show. Idol producers
         <a href="http://tv.yahoo.com/news/tca-american-idol-listened-viewers-changed-format-ep-224906107.html">
          listened to viewers
         </a>
         and implemented changes that bring the show back to its roots. These changes include, but are not limited to:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <b>
           Three judges  in the backseat.
          </b>
          In recent seasons, fighting between the judges has overshadowed the contestants. The focus should be in the front, says Season 13 judge
          <a href="http://www.redding.com/news/2014/jan/22/american-idol-hopes-lucky-13th-season/">
           Keith Urban
          </a>
          , promising a likeable panel that takes a secondary role in the show. Gone are the days of a crowded 4-judge panel.
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Paula Abdul.
          </b>
          No, Paula isnt
          <i>
           actually
          </i>
          back. Rather, her friendly, nurturing spirit that helped create a family-friendly show is brought to the screen. Producers promise a warm and fuzzy Idol that will bring families together in front of the television once again, resulting in bigger ratings.
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Shorter results shows.
          </b>
          The original results shows were just 30 minutes long. As Idol grew, however, FOX wanted to capitalize on the large audience, expanding shows to an hour and sometimes an hour and a half in length just to reveal which contestant was headed home. Prior to Season 13,
          <a href="http://www.adweek.com/news/television/fox-trims-american-idol-results-show-153429">
           FOX announced
          </a>
          a return to 30 minutes results shows, eliminating needless filler.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="idol2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13766" height="200" src="https://contentequalsmoney.com/wp-content/uploads/media/2014/01/idol2.jpg" width="400"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         But thats just beginning. Now that the Idol has returned to the original spirit that made it so likeable, a flurry of other changes will freshen the show.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Per Blankens and Trish Kinane are
          <a href="http://www.redding.com/news/2014/jan/22/american-idol-hopes-lucky-13th-season/">
           two producers
          </a>
          replacing long-time Idol director Nigel Lythgoe. Blankens and Kinane are responsible for Swedish Idols European comeback.
         </li>
         <li>
          More
          <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/news/american-idol-to-return-with-new-changes/">
           current songs
          </a>
          on the repertoire will attract a younger audience. Gone are Idols outdated 70s Songs theme nights.
         </li>
         <li>
          A new 
          <a href="http://www.thewrap.com/american-idol-producer-talks-small-changes-new-rounds-mentor-role-expanded/">
           Rush Week
          </a>
           shortens the notoriously slow middle rounds. Instead of beginning the live shows in early March, the competition will reach full swing in February.
         </li>
         <li>
          Gimmicky sob stories and hilariously bad auditions have been
          <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/idol-worship/american-idol-7-changes-season-670552">
           eliminated
          </a>
          . Sure, there will be laughs and heartstrings pulled during the audition episodes, but they will feel more authentic than over the top.
         </li>
         <li>
          A
          <i>
           Hunger Games
          </i>
          inspired 
          <a href="http://www.americanidol.com/videos/season_xiii_auditions/enter-the-chamber">
           Chamber
          </a>
           will greet contestants before they audition before the judges. The Chamber contains a hidden camera to catch the contestants authentic and unfiltered moments.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Do You Really Know Your Audience?
        </h2>
        <p>
         While knowing your audience might seem like Business and Marketing 101, forgetting your target demographic is a pitfall that many brands fall into, including American Idol. Changes in past Idol seasons focused on creating a provocative, edgier show to keep up with X-Factor and The Voice, turning off viewers that fell in love with the shows
         <a href="http://www.deseretnews.com/article/865594206/Family-friendly-judges-may-breathe-life-back-into-American-Idol.html">
          family friendly
         </a>
         atmosphere.
        </p>
        <p>
         When the competition does something new, its time to amplify what makes your brand unique, not try to outdo the competition. Theres no better way to do this than
         <a href="http://www.concentriccontent.com/content-and-the-client-listening-to-your-audience/">
          listening to your audience
         </a>
         , honoring your roots, and remembering your target demographic.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="idol3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13765" height="409" src="https://contentequalsmoney.com/wp-content/uploads/media/2014/01/idol3.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Understanding the Evolution of Content
        </h2>
        <p>
         While competitors X-Factor and The Voice invested in an aggressive social media strategy to market their content, American Idol tried to keep its footage exclusively on television. Though this seemed like a good idea on paper, Idol fell behind the changing media landscape. In Season 13, for the first time ever, Idol is
         <a href="http://digitalstrategyplanner.com/american-idol-winners-youtube-recap/">
          posting recaps and exclusive content
         </a>
         on its YouTube channel and website immediately after the episode airs. The official
         <a href="https://twitter.com/AmericanIdol">
          Idol Twitter account
         </a>
         posts live tweets in sync with the televised content, harnessing the power of
         <a href="http://www.brandchannel.com/home/post/2013/04/16/Dual-Screen-Engagement-041613.aspx">
          dual screen
         </a>
         engagement.
        </p>
        <p>
         Similar to Idol, businesses and brands must develop a
         <a href="https://contentequalsmoney.com/developing-a-content-strategy/">
          content strategy
         </a>
         to remain current in todays competitive market or theyll fall behind the competition. Its not necessarily the content itself that changes. Rather, its the medium and manner in which content is presented. By trying to keep content exclusively broadcast on television, Idol eventually hurt itself. Is your brand being smart about social media and content marketing?
        </p>
        <h2>
         What Idol is Teaching Business Leaders and Marketers
        </h2>
        <p>
         After 5 consecutive seasons of ratings declines, American Idols magic was firing on all cylinders during the Season 13 premiere. Finally, everything came together. Genuine judges, likeable talent, and a focus on finding a superstar brought back the spirit that made Idol a powerhouse in the first place  and ratings are beginning to show it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a reason your business is unique. Even if you stray from your roots, theres always a route back home. American Idol made a comeback by:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <b>
           Remembering its target demographic.
          </b>
          Is your brand keeping its eye on the prize?
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Adapting to todays social landscape.
          </b>
          Are you invested in a
          <a href="https://contentequalsmoney.com/content-for-you/">
           content strategy for you
          </a>
          and
          <a href="https://contentequalsmoney.com/content-for-your-clients/">
           your clients
          </a>
          ?
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Hiring new producers to give the show a fresh feel.
          </b>
          Are you
          <a href="http://contentequalsmoney.com/what-is-insourcing/">
           insourcing
          </a>
          to get outside expertise with the commitment of an in-house team?
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <b>
          Update:
         </b>
         The latest edition of American Idol is up
         <a href="http://www.thefutoncritic.com/ratings/2014/01/23/wednesdays-broadcast-ratings-american-idol-continues-its-reign-for-fox-889403/broadcast_20140122/">
          3 percent
         </a>
         to last Thursdays episode. The comeback continues
        </p>
