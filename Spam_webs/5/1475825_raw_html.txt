    <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-leftRailSpanner" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Red Tea Poached Pears
     </h1>
     <img height="516" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Red-Tea-Poached-Pear-790x516.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         February 28, 2014
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="654" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/red-tea-poached-pear/red-tea-poached-pear-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/red-tea-poached-pear-2" rel="attachment wp-att-5097">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I doubt there is anything more elegant than a poached pear. I just love them because, well, most fruit-based desserts are more
         <em>
          everything else
         </em>
         other than fruit, such as pies or cobbler (not that I dont indulge in all that butter and sugar). But poached pears are about pears, plain and simple. I poached these in red tea only because I wanted to use something other than wine. The mulled rooibos tea worked out perfectly! Spicy, rich and full of flavor the syrup is divine; tasty on pears and your lover!
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            4 Bosc pears
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            6 Cups Water
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            6 Tablespoons Rooibos Tea, loose leaf
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Sugar
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            20 pink Peppercorns
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cinnamon stick
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Vanilla Bean, halved and scraped
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Star Anise
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Bay Leaf
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 orange slices
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 lemon peel
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Cloves, whole
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            3 allspice berries
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/red-tea-poached-pear/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            4 poached pears
           </span>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT1H30M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            1 hour, 30 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT5M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            5 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Inactive time
           <div style="float: right">
            10 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <meta content="PT1H15M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Cook time
           <div style="float: right">
            1 hour, 15 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Recipe Type:
           </h2>
           Dessert
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Technique:
           </h2>
           Poaching
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Diet:
           </h2>
           <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/diet/vegan/">
            Vegan
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. Warm 6 cups of water in a saucepan over a medium-high heat.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="475" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/red-tea-poached-pear/red-tea-spices/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/red-tea-spices" rel="attachment wp-att-5092">
          <img alt="Red Tea &amp; Spices" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5092" height="285" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Red-Tea-Spices-600x285.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. When bubbles have formed in the pan but before it begins to boil, add in the red rooibos tea.Cover, remove from heat, and let steep for 10 minutes. Strain the tea using a sieve and add it back to the saucepan.This works for regular tea making too; its better with water that hasnt boiled.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/red-tea-poached-pear/roobios-red-tea/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/roobios-red-tea" rel="attachment wp-att-5095">
          <img alt="Roobios Red Tea" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5095" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Roobios-Red-Tea-600x399.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Add 1/2 cup sugar, 20 pink peppercorns, 1 cinnamon stick, 1 vanilla bean (halved &amp; scraped), 1 star anise, 1 bay leaf, 2 orange slices (I cut mine in half), 1 lemon peel, 2 cloves, and 3 allspice berries to the tea. I f*cking love how that looks. After taking this photo, I just stared at it for about 5 minutes. I wanted to dive in!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="670" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/red-tea-poached-pear/mulled-red-tea/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/mulled-red-tea" rel="attachment wp-att-5090">
          <img alt="Mulled Red Tea" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5090" height="402" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Mulled-Red-Tea-600x402.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Peel the pears, trimming the bottoms so they stand upright. Then place them in the saucepan. If youre peeling many pears at once, immerse them in a large bowl of cold water with lemon juice. The acidulated water will keep the pears from discoloring until they are ready to be poached. Um, my word editor keeps wanting to replaced acidulated with ejaculated! WTF.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="548" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/red-tea-poached-pear/poached-pear/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/poached-pear" rel="attachment wp-att-5096">
          <img alt="Poached Pear" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5096" height="329" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Poached-Pear-600x329.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Set saucepan over a high heat and bring to a boil. Reduce to a low simmer, occasionally stirring. Cook until a pairing knife easily pierces pears, about 15 minutes. Remove from pan and let the pears chill in the liquid.
        </p>
        <p>
         NOTE: Use perfectly ripe pears for any poached pear recipe; if theyre overripe, they will be too soggy, and if theyre underripe, they wont poach well. Bosc pears, which hold their shape well, have a sweet, tart flavor, but you can also use Anjou or Bartlett pears.
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Using a slotted spoon, transfer pears to a plate. Then store pears in an airtight container until ready to serve.
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Pour poaching liquid through a sieve set over a bowl. Compost solids, and return liquid to saucepan. Place over medium-high heat, and cook until liquid has been reduced to a syrup that coats the back of a wooden spoon, about 45 minutes. Let cool.
        </p>
        <p>
         8. When ready to serve, arrange pears on a platter or on individual plates, and drizzle poaching liquid over them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy these fuckers, I know I did.
        </p>

        </p>
