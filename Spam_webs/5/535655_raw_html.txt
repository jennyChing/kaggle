       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Blueberry Scones
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2014-09-12T08:23:08+00:00">
         September 12, 2014
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="10767095" data-link="http://www.createdby-diane.com/2014/09/blueberry-scones.html" data-summary="" data-title="Blueberry Scones">
        </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="Homemade blueberry scone with yummy blueberry curd from @createdbydiane" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-11839" height="720" src="//www.createdby-diane.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Homemade-blueberry-scone-with-yummy-blueberry-curd-from-@createdbydiane-480x720.jpg" width="480"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its no secret I love blueberries,
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive made blueberry scones many times,
        </p>
        <p>
         not sure why I never took photos and blogged them,
        </p>
        <p>
         so today is the day!
        </p>
        <p>
         I love using fresh blueberries,
        </p>
        <p>
         if they arent available frozen blueberries are fine, I prefer to freeze them myself so Im sure they have a great taste, I find frozen blueberries taste like they coat them in sugar before freezing sometimes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-11806">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Blueberry Scones from @createdbydiane" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11836" height="663" src="//www.createdby-diane.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Blueberry-Scones-from-@createdbydiane.jpg" width="442"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You most definitely will want to make some
         <a href="http://www.createdby-diane.com/2014/09/blueberry-curd.html" target="_blank">
          <span style="color: #3366ff;">
           <strong>
            Blueberry Curd
           </strong>
          </span>
         </a>
         to go on top of these beauties, so click on over for that recipe.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="blueberry scones #recipe @createdbydiane" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-11840" height="663" src="//www.createdby-diane.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/blueberry-scones-recipe-@createdbydiane.jpg" width="442"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I sprinkled sugar on the tops of these, and they crusted nicely and tasted DELICIOUS!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="how to make homemade blueberry scones" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11835" height="663" src="//www.createdby-diane.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/how-to-make-homemade-blueberry-scones.jpg" width="442"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Paired with the Blueberry Curd, its hard to resist eating ALL of them!
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
        </p>

          Blueberry Scones

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup fresh blueberries (frozen will work too, if you can't get fresh)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cups flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoons baking powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup cold unsalted butter
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup heavy cream
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1-2 tablespoons sugar (for tops of scones)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 400 degrees
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In medium bowl blend flour, brown sugar, baking powder and salt.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cut in cold butter with pastry cutter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Mix in heavy cream, eggs, vanilla extract and blueberries.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Knead out on floured surface.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Roll out dough to  to 1 inch thick, cut into desired sharp.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            I cut this batch into 9 squares
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Sprinkle sugar on the tops of the scones for an extra sweet crunch.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake for 12-15 minutes or until toothpick comes out clean,
           </li>
          </ol>

           <div class="comment-content">
            <p>
             I love making scones, but yes, they can be sticky, Flour your hands and dont try to overwork the dough. Make sure the butter and milk is ice cold. After you have cut in the butter and aded milk and eggs, just turn the bowl of batter on a floured surface and fold over a couple times. The  heat of your hands will melt the butter and the batter will get stickier. So handle it just until it comes together. If this is the first time youve made scones, dont be disappointed, its like making biscuits. A few tries and youll get the hang of it. I make scones all the time and recall in the beginning how I struggled. Freeze the berries for 10 minutes or so, or even longer an hour, and that will help them not release the juice if your berries are over ripe as well.
             <br/>
             here are a few posts with more info and photos that may help.
             <br/>
             If you still struggle, try this method
             <a href="http://www.createdby-diane.com/2013/02/sprinkle-scones.html" rel="nofollow">
              http://www.createdby-diane.com/2013/02/sprinkle-scones.html
             </a>
             <br/>
             Scones with fruit tend to be wetter,
             <br/>
             in this post, you can see I just roll the batter in flour and press it down, try not to knead it like you would bread, but more pat it into place and just be sure all the ingredients are mixed before you pour it onto you floured surface
             <br/>
             <a href="http://www.createdby-diane.com/2013/06/nectarine-and-white-chocolate-chip-scones.html" rel="nofollow">
              http://www.createdby-diane.com/2013/06/nectarine-and-white-chocolate-chip-scones.html
             </a>
             <br/>
             You can also flour wax paper and then just fold the wax paper over and not handle the dough if that seems easier. like in this post
             <a href="http://www.createdby-diane.com/2014/03/coffee-scones-with-coffee-icing.html" rel="nofollow">
