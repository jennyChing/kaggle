          <img alt="easy diy paper flower crafts with how-to " class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6916 frizzly-group-6915" height="270" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/easy-diy-paper-flowers-e1290287587308.jpg" title="easy-diy-paper-flowers" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          <span style="font-size: x-large;">
           DIY Paper Flowers
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="drop_cap">
          <span style="color: #5badad;">
           J
          </span>
         </span>
         ust last week, I was driving in Hillsboro Village (a cute shopping and dining area in Nashville) when I looked over at one of my favorite stores (Social Graces) to see these
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          darling white paper flowers
         </span>
         covering their windows. I almost had a wreck. I
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          <strong>
           HAD
          </strong>
         </span>
         to find out how they made those.
         <span style="color: #5badad;">
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            Lucky me
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
         there was a craft night just a few days later where Erin Duffy of Social Graces taught us how to make them. Now, I get to teach you! Thanks, Erin!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/socialgracespaperflowers.jpg">
          <img alt="paper flowers nashville's social graces" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6929 frizzly-group-6915" height="610" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/socialgracespaperflowers-e1290289775325.jpg" title="socialgracespaperflowers" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         These paper flowers were strung together and hung in the window. I think they would make fabulous garlands for an outdoor party or even made in holiday colors for inside decorations. Match them with your party colors or use white for a wedding! There really is no right or wrong, just learn the steps, get creative, and have fun. Add in your own element like rimming the edges in glitter or using different sheets of patterned paper.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          <strong>
           What youll need:
          </strong>
         </span>
         <br/>
         Scissors
         <br/>
         Thick Cardstock (I used white, but get creative and use colors or even old book pages)
         <br/>
         Brads (regular and mini size)
         <br/>
         Hot glue
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/gettingstarted.jpg">
          <img alt="diy paper flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6921 frizzly-group-6915" height="273" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/gettingstarted-e1290288048642.jpg" title="gettingstarted" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Using a thick cardstock (I used 80 pound linen white cardstock), cut out of 5 sheets (I stacked and cut through all of them at once) a petal shape.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/gluedownpetals.jpg">
          <img alt="diy paper flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6922 frizzly-group-6915" height="304" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/gluedownpetals-e1290288291411.jpg" title="gluedownpetals" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Use a regular size metal brad at one end of the petals. I used a hole punch to make the hole first. Then spread out your petals (I had 5 petals, this number works best for this step). Then use a touch of hot glue under each petal so they stay spread out.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/step2paperflowers.jpg">
          <img alt="easy diy paper flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6923 frizzly-group-6915" height="229" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/step2paperflowers-e1290288481909.jpg" title="step2paperflowers" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Make your next layer of petals. With this set, make a few more (6 to 8). Use a regular or mini size brad just like you did with the first set. Insert it in one end of the paper petals and spread out the petals.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/step3.jpg">
          <img alt="paper flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6924 frizzly-group-6915" height="211" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/step3-e1290288687398.jpg" title="step3" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Get creative with this layer of petals by rolling them up so that when they unfold they have a real flower petal look. Or another fun trick is folding them back and forth (accordion style  or paper fan style). Or consider tearing the edges for a more vintage look. Get creative! Then, put a dab of hot glue down on your first brad and stick the next sets brad on top to glue them together.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/step4.jpg">
          <img alt="paper flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6925 frizzly-group-6915" height="229" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/step4-e1290288882988.jpg" title="step4" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just like you did with the first set of petals, use a dab of glue around each petal so it stays spread out. For the 3rd (and last set), fold 2 sheets of your paper into quarters and cut small petals out (between 6 and 8). Using a small brad, just like with the first two sets, insert the brad and spread the petals out.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/littlepetals.jpg">
          <img alt="diy paper flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6926 frizzly-group-6915" height="230" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/littlepetals-e1290289078312.jpg" title="littlepetals" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Glue down the 3rd brad to the 2nd brad with a dab of hot glue. Then for a cute topper, I rolled strips of paper up like above and glued them down. Below youll see two finished flowers. The 2nd one was made using the accordion style folding for the 2nd set of petals.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/finishedflowers.jpg">
          <img alt="finished paper flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6927 frizzly-group-6915" height="229" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/finishedflowers-e1290289280136.jpg" title="finishedflowers" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here I used old book pages to make a paper flower. It was a bit more difficult as the pages were not thick cardstock. It did not hold its shape quite as well though I still like how it turned out. These would be neat for a vintage outdoor party.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/bookpages.jpg">
          <img alt="book pages paper flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6928 frizzly-group-6915" height="229" src="http://www.pizzazzerie.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/bookpages-e1290289434754.jpg" title="bookpages" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
