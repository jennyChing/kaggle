         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Poulet Yassa (Senegalese Chicken)
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-12-13T09:00:17+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           December 13, 2013
          </time>
           by
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/author/lancaster-anettagmail-com/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
            <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
             Anetta
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          
          <span class="entry-comments-link">
           <a href="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/poulet-yassa-senegalese-chicken/#comments">
            14 Comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
         <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="16431957" data-link="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/poulet-yassa-senegalese-chicken/" data-summary="" data-title="Poulet Yassa (Senegalese Chicken)">
         </div>
         <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Poulet Yassa (Senegalese Chicken)" data-url="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/poulet-yassa-senegalese-chicken/">
         </div>
         <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
         </div>
         <p>
          When I first heard about this recipe, I was really skeptical about the amount of onion called for in the marinade and sauce. I mean, seriously four onions? Good grief!
         </p>
         <p>
          Im glad I took a leap of faith and tried it out, because this chicken is delicious and the onions totally make the dish.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-5.jpg">
           <img alt="Poulet Yassa (Senegalese Chicken)" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2355" height="1024" src="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-5-682x1024.jpg" width="682"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Youll want to plan a head when you make this, because the chicken needs to marinatein the onion-lemon-vinegar mixture at least 8 hours, or overnight. The lemon juice and vinegar totally take the bite out of the onions, then it all cooks down into a completely delectable sauce that is to-die-for.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Poulet Yassa (Senegalese Chicken)" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2351" height="533" src="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-1.jpg" width="800"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          As if by magic, some of the onions just melt right into the sauce, while other pieces remain intact to give the dish a bit of texture. Its so, so good.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-7.jpg">
           <img alt="Poulet Yassa (Senegalese Chicken)" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2357" height="1024" src="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-7-682x1024.jpg" width="682"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I served mine with fluffy Basmati rice, but a side of sauteed leafy greens (spinach, kale, etc.) would be lovely.
         </p>
         <p>
          I wanted to make sweet potatoes to go with it, but it turns out that the husband hates sweet potatoes. He also let me know that hes told me that several times, yet I keep making them. so evidently I just havent been listening to him. Whoops.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-9.jpg">
           <img alt="Poulet Yassa (Senegalese Chicken)" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2359" height="1024" src="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-9-682x1024.jpg" width="682"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The best way to cut your chicken breasts into two thin pieces is to place your hand on the top of the breast, then use your other hand to cut through the breast while keeping the blade parallel to your work surface. Alternatively, you could pound out the chicken breast into one large flat piece, then cut that in half down the middle.
         </p>
         <p>
          Either way, the point is to get the chicken breasts to be in two thin pieces, rather than one large piece. It will cook faster and more evenly this way.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-4.jpg">
           <img alt="Poulet Yassa (Senegalese Chicken)" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2354" height="1024" src="http://thewanderlustkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Poulet-Yassa-4-682x1024.jpg" width="682"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h1>
          Heres your recipe!
         </h1>
         <p>
         </p>

            </span>

          <div class="ERSIngredients">
           <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
            Ingredients
           </div>
           <ul>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup peanut oil (or vegetable oil)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             3 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut in half horizontally to make two thin fillets
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             4 large yellow onions, roughly chopped
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             8 Tbsp. lemon juice
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             8 Tbsp. cider vinegar
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 bay leaf
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             4 cloves garlic, minced
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 Tbsp. Dijon or stone-ground mustard
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped (optional)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              tsp. cayenne pepper
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              tsp. black pepper
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tsp. salt
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 Tbsp. peanut or vegetable oil
            </li>
           </ul>
           <div class="ERSClear">
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSInstructions">
           <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
            Instructions
           </div>
           <ol>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Combine all of the ingredients except for the last 2 Tbsp. of oil in a large zip-close bag and allow chicken to marinate in the refrigerator 8 hours, or overnight. When ready to cook, remove the chicken from the bag, but save the marinade.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Heat a large saute pan over medium-high heat and add 2 Tbsp. peanut or vegetable oil once hot. Saut chicken for two minutes on each side. Remove, and set aside on a plate.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Use a slotted spoon to scoop the onions out of the marinade bag, add to the hot pan and cook for 5 minutes. Add the remaining marinade and bring to a boil. Cook at a boil for ten minutes.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Continue to cook the marinade until it becomes a sauce (about 20 minutes). Reduce heat to medium, then return the chicken and drippings to the sauce, cover and simmer until chicken is cooked through (about another 10-15 minutes). Serve with rice.
            </li>
           </ol>
