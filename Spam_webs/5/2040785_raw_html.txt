        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Why Do We Continue to Discriminate Against Men with Long Hair?
        </h1>
       </div>
      </header>
      <div class="entry-media">
       <figure class="base-image">
        <img alt="vrigin-620x349" class="attachment-big-size wp-post-image" height="400" itemprop="image" src="//kikiandtea.com/wp-content/uploads/vrigin-620x3491.jpg" width="800"/>
       </figure>
      </div>
      <div class="wrapper">
       <div class="grids">
        <div class="grid-8 column-1">
         <div class="single-box clearfix entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           When I saw this story in the SMH this morning 
           <a href="http://www.smh.com.au/travel/travel-news/longhaired-virgin-steward-wins-back-his-job-20130115-2crno.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.smh.com.au/travel/travel-news/longhaired-virgin-steward-wins-back-his-job-20130115-2crno.html', 'Long-haired Virgin steward wins back his job');" target="_blank">
            Long-haired Virgin steward wins back his job
           </a>
            I thought to myself,
           <em>
            yes, finally a win for men with long hair
           </em>
           . As a man who likes to wear his hair long, Ive long fought against any form of male hair discrimination.Unfortunately, when I came to read the article, I realised there was a twist to the story. It seems the reason thatMr Taleski won his case against Virgin Airlines was becausehe felt compelled to wear his hair long because
           <em>
            he was suffering from a body-image disorder
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_12712" style="width: 310px">
           <a href="http://kikiandtea.com/2013/01/why-do-we-continue-to-discriminate-against-men-with-long-hair/olympus-digital-camera-27/" rel="attachment wp-att-12712">
            <img alt="JJ's sleek and practical  ponytail..." class="size-medium wp-image-12712 " height="225" src="//kikiandtea.com/wp-content/uploads/JJ-Male-Tail21-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            JJs sleek and practical ponytail
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Now I dont want to dismiss this as a valid reason for a man wanting or needing to wear his hair long.
           <a href="http://kikiandtea.com/2012/12/love-yourself-and-a-manifesto-or-two/" target="_blank">
            Body image issues
           </a>
           are complex and understandable. But for once, Id really like to read a story where a man with long hair wins an anti-discrimination case simply because its wrong todiscriminateagainst a man who chooses to wear his hair long.
          </p>
          <p>
           As Ive written before on KiKi &amp; Tea,
           <a href="http://kikiandtea.com/2012/03/all-men-have-long-hair-in-defence-of-the-male-ponytail/" target="_blank">
            all men have long hair
           </a>
           . In our natural state, theres no difference between men and women in this regard. If we all stop having ourhaircut, we all end up with long hair. Thats a simple fact. I also understand and acknowledge that there are valid reasons why long hair isnt allowed in some workplaces, especially for safety reasons. All Im asking is that when there are rules about how people should wear their hair in the workplace, that they are applied equally to both men and women.
          </p>
          <p>
           Virgin Airlines has apersonal grooming manual called
           <em>
            The Look Book
           </em>
           . In the section in
           <em>
            The Look Book
           </em>
           for females it describes a ponytail as 
           <em>
            sleek, practical and shows off healthy hair to its full advantage
           </em>
           . So why cant this description be applied to men as well? Why one rule for men and a different rule for women? I dont get it! This is discrimination, pure and simple.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_12711" style="width: 630px">
           <a href="http://kikiandtea.com/2013/01/why-do-we-continue-to-discriminate-against-men-with-long-hair/vrigin-620x349/" rel="attachment wp-att-12711">
            <img alt="A page from Virgin's The Look Book, which dictates hair length, general grooming and dress code." class="size-full wp-image-12711" height="349" src="//kikiandtea.com/wp-content/uploads/vrigin-620x349.jpg" width="620"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            A page from Virgins The Look Book, which dictates hair length, general grooming and dress code.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           In the court case, a Virgin manager said
           <em>
            The Look Book
           </em>
           simply reflected how a typical guest expects a male employee to look. The manager conceded though that the manual reflected the most conservative interpretation of what the typical guest would expect.
          </p>
          <p>
           Well, I dont care if this is what the most conservative opinion is. Any form of discrimination based on gender is wrong and should be stopped. If it is appropriate for women to wear long hair in the workplace  even if it must be worn in asleek and practical ponytail  then it should be equallyappropriatefor men to do the same. Why the gender-specific difference in your grooming guide, VirginAirlines? Sounds like discrimination in my book!
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Do you think it is unfair for men and women to be treated differently when it comes to personal grooming in the workplace. Or do you think its OK for different rules to be applied to men and women?
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #808080;">

             <a href="http://www.smh.com.au/travel/travel-news/longhaired-virgin-steward-wins-back-his-job-20130115-2crno.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.smh.com.au/travel/travel-news/longhaired-virgin-steward-wins-back-his-job-20130115-2crno.html', 'The Sydney Morning Herald');" target="_blank">
              <span style="color: #808080;">
               The Sydney Morning Herald
              </span>
             </a>
             .
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
