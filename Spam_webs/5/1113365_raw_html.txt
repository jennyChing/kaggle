        <div class="post-27923 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-general category-healthcare-marketing category-social-media" id="post-27923">
         <h2 class="posttitle">
          The Healthy Way Johns Hopkins Hospital Treats Social Media
         </h2>
         <div class="entry">
          <img alt="johns-hopkins-approachable-social-media" class="display_none wp-post-image" height="256" src="http://www.mdgadvertising.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/blog_johns-hopkins-approachable-social-media.jpg" title="johns-hopkins-approachable-social-media" width="475"/>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.mdgadvertising.com/blog/?p=27923">
            <img alt="healthcare social media" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-27924" height="256" src="http://www.mdgadvertising.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/blog_johns-hopkins-approachable-social-media.jpg" title="johns-hopkins-and-social-media-marketing" width="475"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           The healthcare industry is rather conservative, which has made it more difficult for healthcare marketers to get involved in the less-traditional world of social media. Fortunately, any initial hesitation is rapidly subsiding as more and
           <strong>
            more healthcare brands and organizations are seeing the way social media is being warmly embraced by patients and bringing valuable opportunities for consumer communication.
           </strong>
           One strong social advocate is Stacy Poliseo, whose role as the social media voice for
           <a href="http://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/">
            Hopkins Medicine
           </a>
           has given her incredible insight into making social media a worthwhile part of a healthcare organization.
           <em>
            <a href="http://socialmediatoday.com/joan-justice/1510631/big-brand-theory-how-johns-hopkins-hospital-became-approachable-social-media">
             SocialMediaToday.com spoke with the social specialist
            </a>
           </em>
           to learn how she approaches social media for a healthcare leader like Johns Hopkins.
          </p>
          <p>
           According to Ms. Poliseo, the Hopkins approach to social media marketing is to share the depth of the institution in both cutting-edge research and personal patient care. She strives to update the international professional community while reaching out to the local audience. With patients, her primary goals are to humanize the brand, join in on the conversation, and listen to the patient voice in an effort to show how Hopkins is a supportive resource they can trust.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-27923">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           One of her social media successes at Johns Hopkins was a campaign that promoted
           <a href="http://www.healthcarecommunication.com/Main/Articles/Johns_Hopkins_Medicine_opens_its_new_clinical_buil_11502.aspx">
            the new Sheikh Zayed Tower and The Charlotte R. Bloomberg Childrens Center
           </a>
           . Since these buildings were far from finished at the time of the campaign, Ms. Poliseo relied on an innovative Facebook application to create a building-centric page directly on the Johns Hopkins page. This additional page featured details,
           <a href="http://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/the_johns_hopkins_hospital/about/new_facilities/video_tour.html">
            a video tour
           </a>
           , a construction timeline, and a countdown clock to teach visitors all about the new buildings.
          </p>
          <p>
           This page drove over 20 percent of new Facebook likes, said Ms. Poliseo. Patients wrote congratulation posts and we generated a lot of excitement about the buildings. It was a great example of the public integrating with Hopkins Medicine.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ms. Poliseo may be a social marketing pro, but she admits to learning two important lessons about
           <a href="http://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/social_media/">
            social media marketing at Hopkins
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <div id="blog-sm-advertisement">
           <div id="blog-sm-advertisement-top">
           </div>

           The other is not to assume anything about your social media audience, but instead get to know them. It may be
           <strong>
            surprising to see how social media spans all demographics,
           </strong>
           but its important to become familiar with each audience in order to engage with them most effectively.
          </p>

           My advice would be to listen, learn and adapt,she said.
