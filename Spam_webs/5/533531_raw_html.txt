       <h3>
        <a href="http://thepennysaved.com/2008/05/13/gas-and-money-saving-tips-1-10-of-50/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Gas and Money Saving tips 1-10 (of 50!)">
         Gas and Money Saving tips 1-10 (of 50!)
        </a>
       </h3>
      </div>
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        So I have a massive list of tips Ive compiled going from the obvious to the not-quite-so-obvious.  So here to start things off, is 1-10.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         1) Dont speed
        </strong>
        <br/>
        When you speed not only does your drag increase (thinks back to my mechanical engineering classes that I mostly slept through).  Not only that, but at higher RPMs even in high gears you are burning a lot more fuel.  If youre on the highway, slowing down from 85 to 70 depending on your car can decrease your fuel consumption by over 20%.  I am terrible about this.  Im working on it.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         2) Dont accelerate too hard
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Once again, high RPMs = large amounts of gas being burned.  Im also terrible at this.  I am also working on this.
        <img alt=";)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://thepennysaved.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_wink.gif"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         3) De-Junk-ify your car
        </strong>
        <br/>
        The more the car weighs the more fuel it uses.  Only bring stuff you really need.  Like golf clubs.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         4) Turn that A/C off (or use less)
        </strong>
        <br/>
        When the air conditioner is on it puts extra load on the engine forcing more fuel to be used (by about 20%).  Roll down the windows, after all, being in touch with with the cool breeze is nicer anyway, right? Edit: rate of diminishing return, drag gets worse as you speed up so above low speeds, roll the windows up and switch to AC.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         5) Long trip? Cruise control it up
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Avoid accelerating and decelerating if possible. See #2.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         6) Waiting on a Train? How about at Sonic? Turn the car off
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Save some otherwise wasted fuel.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         7) Keep your tires inflated
        </strong>
        <br/>
        My cousin and I just did a test on this on his Lumina and it really does save you several MPG if they are properly inflatedin his case from low to slightly high, 3 MPG, no joke.  On my Z thats $.57 per gallon!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         8) Buy the lowest grade (octane) of gasoline that you can (safely)
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Check your owners manual for this information. IF youre not pinging, youre good to go.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         9) Pay cash at places that charge extra for credit cards
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         10) Get gas from places like Walmart and King Soopers that are offering loss leader deals
        </strong>
        <br/>
        You can often get gas for a lot cheaper.  I am saving about 20 cents a gallon at Kings by my house. Sweet.
       </p>
