        <p>
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVOnNvVZI/AAAAAAAADKI/D77iqr_6e0A/s1600-h/beef.jpg">
          <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5361488328429360530" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVOnNvVZI/AAAAAAAADKI/D77iqr_6e0A/s400/beef.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 340px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         For several years I have been buying my beef directly from the local farmer.Ive had people ask me if its less expensive to buy beef this way. Yes, definitely, if you can budget in the initial payment, youll definitely reap the benefits.
        </p>
        <div>
        </div>
        <div>
         I live in a rural town, so the farmer is literally only a few miles away. I pay him market price for the live animal, he transports it to our local packing house, then I pay them for the processing.
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVOZ6aacI/AAAAAAAADKA/Ra5la-jXIr0/s1600-h/beef1.jpg">
          <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5361488324858636738" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVOZ6aacI/AAAAAAAADKA/Ra5la-jXIr0/s400/beef1.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 271px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
          There are several benefits to this:
         </span>
        </p>
        <div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          1) Its less expensive
         </div>
         <div>
          2) It tastes better
         </div>
         <div>
          3) Its better for you
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Yes, buying a side of beef is definitely a big chunk out of your wallet all at once, but overall its a tremendous cost savings. If you cant afford a side of beef or you dont have that many mouths to feed, consider going halves with another couple or family. Our local farmer has a loyal customer base of people just like me. He keeps a list of names of who wants what and is able to piece together an order based off of his customers. So if I only want a half, he may have another customer who wants a half or two that want quarters. In my particular case, my neighbor and I share a steer. She has 6 kids and I have 4, so we each get a side.
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVN97U0fI/AAAAAAAADJ4/eZAPybqmGzk/s1600-h/beef2.jpg">
           <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5361488317346271730" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVN97U0fI/AAAAAAAADJ4/eZAPybqmGzk/s400/beef2.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 318px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <div>
          Now on to the cost savings. Ive broken it out below to give you an idea of how much you can save:
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Though I only get half of this, let me show you how this calculates out. We did split this with our neighbor. The steers live weight was 1,454 pounds. After processing they give you what is called their hanging weight. Im sure you can figure out what that means :) Our hanging weight was 780 pounds.
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Obviously you lose a lot of weight after blood, bones and hide have been removed. After hanging for ten days, the beef is processed according to how you want it cut. For example, in this order I didnt get any prime rib roasts, instead I opted for rib eye steaks. Getting certain cuts will eliminate the possibility of other cuts. Weve had ours cut several different ways over the years.
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVNtwvBDI/AAAAAAAADJw/QO2FlDxWEvg/s1600-h/beef3.jpg">
           <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5361488313006883890" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVNtwvBDI/AAAAAAAADJw/QO2FlDxWEvg/s400/beef3.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 282px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <div>
          When all was said and done the processed and packaged weight came to 516 pounds. Market price at the time that we purchased the beef was $0.85/pound. The farmer always gives me a discount if I pay him cash. The processing cost was $446.40. So lets see how that comes out:
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          1,454 x $0.85 = $1,235.90
         </div>
         <div>
          less discount for paying cash  $135.90
         </div>
         <div>
          <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
          </span>
         </div>
         <div>
          Cash to farmer $1,100.00
         </div>
         <div>
          Processing $446.40
         </div>
         <div>
          <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
           Total $1,546.40
          </span>
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          $1,546.40 divided by 516 pounds = $3.00/pound
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          So now at first glance you are probably thinking I can get hamburger on sale for $1.89/lb!
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Yes you can. But what about steaks, roasts, and other cuts? Heres some examples for you. I was grocery shopping at WalMart and wrote down a few prices in the meat department:
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
           Prices are per pound:
          </span>
         </div>
         <div>
          
         </div>
         <div>
          ground beef, 85% lean $3.04
         </div>
         <div>
          T-bone steak $9.98
         </div>
         <div>
          Porterhouse $9.98
         </div>
         <div>
          Rib Eye $9.98
         </div>
         <div>
          Chuck roast $3.58
         </div>
         <div>
          rump roast $2.98
         </div>
         <div>
          Sirloin steak $5.18
         </div>
         <div>
          Round steak $3.98
         </div>
         <div>
          Sirloin tip roast $2.68
         </div>
         <div>
          Cube steak $3.48
         </div>
         <div>
          Stew meat $3.18
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Theres only one cut in that list that is less than my per pound price. I wasnt able to get every cut that I have, they didnt have any flank steak or brisket, so not sure if the grocery store price on those.
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVNUhf-sI/AAAAAAAADJo/Y1Bq72TAiH8/s1600-h/beef4.jpg">
           <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5361488306232097474" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_ywvV-d3ePCk/SmfVNUhf-sI/AAAAAAAADJo/Y1Bq72TAiH8/s400/beef4.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <div>
          Now to give you an idea of how much beef we get with an order like this, here are SOME of the quantities of the different cuts. This is for the whole order:
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          ground beef  196 lbs
         </div>
         <div>
          chuck roast, 3.5 lb  5 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          soup bones, 3 lb  7 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          rolled rump roast, 3 lb  2 each
         </div>
         <div>
          rolled sirloin tip roast, 3 lb  2 each
         </div>
         <div>
          stew meat, 2 lb  12 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          round steak, 1 lb  3 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          round roast, 3 lb  2 each
         </div>
         <div>
          sirloin steak  8 each
         </div>
         <div>
          T-bone steak, 2 per pkg  11 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          Porterhouse, 2 per pkg  4 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          Rib eye steak, 2 per pkg  12 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          Cube steaks  7 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          Chuck steak  10 pkgs
         </div>
         <div>
          brisket  2
         </div>
         <div>
          flank  1
         </div>
         <div>
          oxtail  1
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
           Now just based on steaks alone, weve saved $6.98 per pound!
          </span>
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Other benefits I mentioned above are that the beef tastes better and its better for you. There is no comparison between beef bought at the grocery store and the beef I get from the farmer. The flavor is so much better. Hamburgers and steaks are phenomenal!
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          I feel better eating it because I know where the steer spent its days, what pasture he grazed in and what barn he entered every night. Ive been in that barn! I know there arent any hormones pumped into him and I have peace of mind knowing that he wasnt treated badly or crammed into a tiny pen with tons of other steers all his life.
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          When I pick up my side of beef I get 5 big boxes, the contents fill my chest freezer and I usually have to find extra room in my kitchen freezer as well. It lasts me about 7 or 8 months, and thats of course supplementing our meals with chicken and pork as well.
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          If you can find a source for beef near you, its worth it. Find someone to go in on it with you, or check with the farmer and see if they have customers that are waiting to share a beef. Even if you have to drive 1-2 hours, its worth it. The flavor, the peace of mind and the cost savings all speak for themselves!
         </div>
