      <p>
       <a href="http://wersm.com/category/facebook" target="_blank" title="facebook on wersm">
        Facebook
       </a>
       is always on the move, changing this, making this better, creating new features, etc Funny enough,
       <a href="http://wersm.com/category/facebook" target="_blank" title="facebook on wersm">
        Facebook
       </a>
       has been really
       <strong>
        quiet in terms of design
       </strong>
       lately. In fact, not much has changed visually for
       <a href="http://wersm.com/category/facebook" target="_blank" title="facebook on wersm">
        Facebook
       </a>
       pages over the last 12 months.
      </p>
      <p>
       But just to be sure, we have an updated
       <strong>
        cheat sheet
       </strong>
       for you, so you can be sure all your images will look great on your
       <a href="http://wersm.com/category/facebook" target="_blank" title="facebook on wersm">
        Facebook
       </a>
       page!
      </p>
      <h2>
       Also Read:
       <a href="http://wersm.com/is-facebooks-new-algorithm-making-it-harder-for-pages/" target="_blank" title="Is Facebooks New Algorithm Making It Harder For Pages?">
        Is Facebooks New Algorithm Making It Harder For Pages?
       </a>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The nice guys over at
       <a href="http://www.techwyse.com" target="_blank" title="techwyse canada">
        TechWyse
       </a>
       created this infographic detailing
       <strong>
        the most updated specifications for Facebook pages and ads image sizes.
       </strong>
       Even if things have not change that much, it is always a good idea to check that all your posts and ads will look their best on
       <a href="http://wersm.com/category/facebook" target="_blank" title="facebook on wersm">
        Facebook
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       Looking Back On Some Facebook Basics:
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Upload your images at the
        <strong>
         recommended size
        </strong>
        for great visual results
       </li>
       <li>
         If you dont upload an image with the correct dimensions,
        <strong>
         Facebook will centre part the image
        </strong>
       </li>
       <li>
         Remember that you can
        <strong>
         upload your own image when sharing a link or a video
        </strong>
       </li>
       <li>
         F
        <strong>
         acebook ads are now bigger
        </strong>
        , think about it when designing your visuals
       </li>
       <li>
        
        <strong>
         Keep text on image under 20%
        </strong>
        in case you want to promote/boost a post
       </li>
      </ul>
