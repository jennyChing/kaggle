        <h1 class="pagetitle">
         Super Bowl Advertising  Creative, Captivating or Controversial?
        </h1>
        <div class="entry">
         <p>
          Super Bowl ads get plenty of press and are often watched more closely than the game itself. But for both small businesses and large companies, the Super Bowl ad creative frenzy also provides some valuable insights that can be applied to everyday business situations.
         </p>
         <h2>
          What Is Super Bowl Advertising?
         </h2>
         <p>
          Super Bowl Sunday is a worldwide cultural phenomenon that recently enjoyed its 49th annual observance  matching American professional football playoff winners vying for the championship of the National Football League. Because the television broadcast reaches so many viewers  recently over 100 million  of all ages and sexes, the event has become a hallmark event for observing marketing in action.
         </p>
         <p>
          Even for the growing number of viewers that might not even like football, Super Bowl advertising continues to be a popular sideshow that is often talked about as much as the main event. The Super Bowl ads are always analyzed and surveyed in detail  in many cases, new and successful advertising trends are established. Its not just about football! In many respects, the Super Bowl is an advertising laboratory for testing new marketing themes and ideas.
         </p>
         <h2>
          What Makes Super Bowl Ads So Captivating?
         </h2>
         <p>
          Super Bowl ads are regularly treated as the royalty of the marketing world. An advertisers dream is for a companys advertising to generate hype and publicity. Because stale and repetitive ads often miss the mark, new and fresh Super Bowl ads frequently stand apart because of being the first of its kind  for example, Apples 1984 Super Bowl ad is still talked about as a key creative moment for advertising and marketing. Steve Jobs seemed to sense that the ad would be an important step in Apples attempt to crush the PC oligarchy. The ad also was followed by impressive sales for the new Mac computer, with $150 million in 100 days.
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Super Cost of Super Bowl Ads
         </h2>
         <p>
          A frequent water-cooler topic before and after the Super Bowl involves advertising costs  how much is a Super Bowl ad? The cost of a Super Bowl ad this year was about $4.5 million for a 30-second commercial. Keep in mind that this represents only network fees. These high-profile ads are also expensive to produce because of celebrity appearances, music licensing and other production costs. Unlike most television ads that result in viewers leaving the room during commercials, Super Bowl viewers stick around for the ads. For example, less than 1% left during commercial breaks for the 2013 Super Bowl.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Content and Controversies of Super Bowl Ad Creative
         </h2>
         <p>
          Super Bowl ad content is often guided by several products that seem to dominate  chips, cars and beverages are three of the most consistent regulars. A heavier-than-normal dose of humor and warmth is usually involved as is the presence of puppies and Clydesdales.
         </p>
         <p>
          In most Super Bowl broadcasts, one or more controversies usually emerge. The objectification of women seems to creep through in a few ads  2015s crew of ads included some balance with appropriate attention to reducing violence against women and a marketing message that emphasized doing things like a girl.
         </p>
         <p>
          A regular concern for many parents is the impact of Super Bowl ads on children. One practical solution is for parents to use the potentially controversial ads as educational opportunities  for example, explaining sexist behavior can usually be facilitated with one or more Super Bowl ads.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Evaluating the Effectiveness of Super Bowl Ads
         </h2>
         <p>
          Given the high cost of a Super Bowl ad, participating companies should understandably evaluate if Super Bowl advertising is cost-effective or not. A recent Stanford University study concluded that Super Bowl ads are mostly effective. However, a key exception is when two or more similar brands advertise during the game  competing brands tend to be ineffective with their Super Bowl ads.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is a marketing lesson to be learned for your companys advertising strategies  how can you do a better job of standing apart from your competition? Additionally, how can you integrate more creative storytelling into your marketing efforts and engage your target audience more effectively? If you are looking for practical and cost-effective advertising strategies, Video Caddy can help.
         </p>
         <p>
          What do you think of Super Bowl ads? Do they deserve all of the hoopla? Are they overpriced? Please share your thoughts by sharing via the social media buttons and leaving a comment below.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
            Video Caddy

         </p>
