/**
 * Gets the "real" position of an element
 * Courtesy Peter-Paul Koch 
 * http://www.quirksmode.org/js/findpos.html
 */         
function findPos(obj) {
    var curleft = curtop = 0;
    if (obj.offsetParent) {
        do {
            curleft += obj.offsetLeft;
            curtop += obj.offsetTop;
        } while (obj = obj.offsetParent);
        return [curleft,curtop];
    }
}

      getPremiumNotice().style.left = realPos[0] + 25;

      getPremiumNotice().style.display = "block";

      getPremiumNotice().style.display = "none";

                 Got an acre? S.W. Minnesota winery growing, needs more grapes
                </h1>
                <!--subtitle-->

                 By Julie Buntjer

                 <p>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  An excavator and a dirt mover began clearing the way Thursday morning for a new, 5,000-square-foot reception hall and wine cellar on the grounds of a Round Lake, Minn., winery.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Owners Scott and Jenny Ellenbecker and their recently-hired winemaker, Sam Jennings, hope construction of Round Lake Vineyards &amp; Winery will be complete by the time they begin bottling wine in midwinter.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Grapes from 16 acres of the vineyard were crushed on site this fall, not long after the fermentation tanks -- ordered direct from Italy -- were delivered.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "Everything is in our own tanks and it's in the process of becoming our own wine," said Scott Ellenbecker, adding that he plans to do a couple of soft launches of wines in early 2015.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Jennings joined the vineyard in mid-September, and brings with him the experience of working for both small and large wineries. He hails from Washington state, and previously worked for Master Brands.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The trio will work together to produce an array of red and white wines from the grapes grown in the 18-acre vineyard. Grape varieties grown on the site northeast of the city of Round Lake (on the western shores of the actual lake) include Bluebell, Brianna, Frontenac Gris, King of the North, La Crescent, Louis Swenson, Marquette, Petite Amie, Sabrevois, St. Pepin and Somerset Seedless.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "We have seven whites and four reds that we grow on the property," Scott said. "They range anywhere from a grape that makes a nice, dry wine to a sweeter wine.
                 </p>
                 <div class="articleEmbeddedAdBox" style="width:336px">
                  <hr class="articleAdRule"/>
                  <div class="articleAdHeader">

                 <p>
                  Jennings' connections and expertise have added another element to the business -- purchasing grapes from vineyards in Washington and California to process into wines.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "We want Minnesota-grown fruit as much as possible, but we can't grow everything in Minnesota," Scott said. "We're not just bringing in any old fruit -- it's been custom-selected by Sam to be quality fruit. It's hard to find really good grapes and Sam worked really hard to find the right place."
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "We're having apple wine, too," Jennings joked.
                 </p>
                 <div class="articlePosition3" style="width:300px;">
                  <div class="articleImageBox" style="width:300px">
                   <span class="articleImage">
                    <a href="/portlet/article/html/imageDisplay.jsp?contentItemRelationshipId=6359632" target="_new">
                     <img alt="Workers move dirt at the site of the new wine cellar and reception hall being constructed at Round Lake Vineyards &amp; Winery." border="0" height="201" src="http://extras.mnginteractive.com/live/media/site569/2014/1103/20141103__2-110214.B.DG.ROUNDLAKEWINERY2_300.jpg" title="Workers move dirt at the site of the new wine cellar and reception hall being constructed at Round Lake Vineyards &amp; Winery." width="300"/>
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   <div class="articleImageCaption" style="width:100%">
                    Workers move dirt at the site of the new wine cellar and reception hall being constructed at Round Lake Vineyards &amp; Winery.
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                 The vineyard worked out an agreement with Ocheda Orchard of rural Worthington.
                 <p>
                  "We have a few tons of apples to crush in the next week," Scott said. "It's great to have an orchard a few miles down the road."
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Between all of the wine varieties, Round Lake Vineyards &amp; Winery is ramping up to a 3,500-case winery -- considered small in Minnesota, where the largest vineyards produce up to 10,000 cases.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "We have plans to (grow) over the next few years as we get more growers online," Scott said.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  FERMENTATION, TASTING ROOMS
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The fermentation room, and an adjacent tasting room (slated to open May 1) were constructed several years ago at the vineyard, which is also home to the couple's business, Ellenbecker Communications. The new facility under construction will house a 1,500-square-foot wine cellar, with the remaining 3,500 square feet to include a reception hall, catering kitchen and restrooms.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "Tasting will be in the winery building during the week, and weekends we'll have music and tasting on the green closer to the lake," Scott said.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The reception hall will be ready for summer 2015 bookings, and the Ellenbeckers plan to open the area for weddings, receptions and special events.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "Couples can get married in the vines and come here for the reception afterwards," said Scott, adding that the reception hall will seat about 200 people. "There's a certain kind of person that wants to be married at a vineyard by a lake. There's certain people who want to marry in the church and have a reception at the convention center. There's the barn at Pioneer Village -- it's options."
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "We've traveled to a lot of wineries and talked to them about how they do it," added Jenny. "It's beautiful out here. Why not?"
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  To prepare for construction of the wine cellar and reception hall, the Ellenbeckers had to remove 17 large ash and maple trees. The wood from those trees was taken to a mill in Steen, where it will be turned into lumber for use in the interior design of the new building.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Special doors will be made from the wood, and the lees -- the dark-pigmented remnants of the grapes during the winemaking process -- will be blended with the wood to create a unique stain on the boards.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  GOT AN ACRE? GROW GRAPES FOR THEM
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The Ellenbeckers planted their first grape vines in 2007 and have steadily added acres to reach the 18 acres in production right now. They have about 10 more acres available for expansion, but hope to lure more people from the area to grow grapes -- even a plot as small as an acre -- to help them reach their expansion goals.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Their first grapes were harvested in 2009, and from then until 2013, they marketed their grapes to other wineries in the region. Jenny said they could keep up to 200 gallons on-site for their own production and experimentation.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Now, with 16 acres producing enough to begin their small-scale winery, Scott said Round Lake Vineyards &amp; Winery has completed the necessary paperwork and is a Minnesota-bonded winery.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "We're now going through the process of getting a liquor license," he said. The license will allow them to sell beer and hard liquors, in addition to their wines, during events.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  With the catering kitchen, Scott said people will be able to choose their favorite caterer for events. Plans are also in the works to offer a food trailer open on weekends during the summer.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "There's a new opportunity for things to do -- entertainment -- next summer in the area," Scott said. "There will be a lot of food, fun and wine ... and yard games.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  "We need to put a boat dock in, and hopefully campers across the lake will come over and spend some time -- pull up and taste some wine and take a few bottles with them," he added.
                  <span fd-id="default" fd-type="end">
