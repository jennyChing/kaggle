          <p>
           There has always been a fuss about how to get those smooth underarms with discoloration. Women try every trick in the book to attain the same. There are many who are prone to ingrown hairs as well. These ingrown hairs cause problems like redness, irritation and swelling in many. As a result, you have to make sure that when you remove hair, using whatever method, you get rid of every one of them. There are several steps that you can follow to help you deal with ingrown hair and also attain smooth underarm skin.
          </p>
          <p>
           Razor Sharp
          </p>
          <p>
           For those of you using a razor to remove underarm hair might want to change your razor frequently and not use the same one for long periods of time just to make sure that your underarms are not raided with bacteria or razor bumps after you have shaved. This is a common mistake that most people make. Also, clean your armpits before you shave them so that they are free of the dirt and other particles accumulated during the day. This will allow the razor to glide on your skin and remove all the hair easily.
          </p>
          <p>
           Invest in an Epilator
          </p>
          <p>
           Waxing is a painful experience for many. If you do not wish to go through that pain every time to get rid of underarm hair then you might want to invest in an epilator. An epilator removes the hair from the roots as well slowing the growth of new hair. However, if the underarm hair is long then you should first shave it off because an epilator tends to break the hair if they are long. Using an epilator a few days after shaving your underarms will ensure all the hair is out leaving it smooth and clear.
          </p>
          <p>
           Use a Scrub
          </p>
          <p>
           Exfoliation is one of the best ways to unclog your pores and your underarms need them just as much as your face does. It effectively helps you rid dead skin cells and in-growths which act as breeding grounds for bacteria. You can buy an exfoliating scrub that is easily available in the market or make you own scrub at home to put on a loofah and gently rub on your underarms. This will definitely give you smooth underarms.
          </p>
          <p>
           Fabrics Friction
          </p>
          <p>
           Rough fabrics, especially winter woollen clothes, tend to cause itching and inflammation on your skin due to friction. This friction leaves those repulsive brown or grayish patches on your skin making it look unattractive. Those with sensitive skin are most prone to this problem. You can apply a lotion to ease of this inflammation. Wearing softer fabrics might do a lot good in such a case. If you are feeling too cold then you can slip in a t-shirt inside your woollen clothes.
          </p>
