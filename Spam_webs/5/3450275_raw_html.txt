       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://unbrandedtruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/IMG_0740.jpg" rel="lightbox[798]" title="IMG_0740">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-800" height="252" src="http://unbrandedtruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/IMG_0740.jpg" title="IMG_0740" width="700"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Unbranded Truth recently visited the Wolves Caf, which is a coffee and cake shop that doubles as a community centre, art space, music venue and bar based in Johannesburg. They put up a new exhibition up every month. In October they are having the whimsical illustrations of Graeme Jenner and in November they will have some new work by Elise Wessels.
       </p>
       <p>
        This is where we first came across Rikus Ferreiras work. We were ecstatic to be able to chat with this multi-faceted Cape Town based artist. Rikus is a go-getter with a string of awards and accolades that include the New York Type Directors Club Award for Typographic Excellence and the D&amp;DA Silver Award. He has also taken part in a number of group exhibitions locally and abroad. We talked about his career, his source(s) of inspiration, his recent exhibition at Wolves Caf and his future exhibitions. Here is how it went
       </p>
       <p>
        Ofentse Mohitshane: Who is Rikus Ferreira?
       </p>
       <p>
        Rikus Ferreira: I am an illustrator, comic artist and graphic designer living and working in Cape Town. I have won numerouslocal and international awards, and I have exhibitedmy work both locally and abroad. In 2010 I spent some time at the Cite des Arts In Paris on an artists residency, and early 2012 I was invited to do a workshop at the International Comics Festival in Angoulme, in the south of France. Currently, I am a lecturer in graphic design at the Stellenbosch University.
       </p>
       <p>
        OM: Tell us about the collection that was showcased at Wolves Cafe
       </p>
       <p>
        RF: In a time where everything is digital, we have iPads, digital books, and the Internet; books are fast becoming something from the past. I find this fascinating, and this is in essence what informs my work. I like the physical aspect of books; the tactile qualities of paging through a book, and the fact that a book is almost like an interactive sculpture. I also love the fact that each book (especially old ones) comes with its own history; it has been through many hands and homes. It meant a lot to different people. So, what I do is to find old books, and rework them. I change the old story, change their history, their narrative, to create a new narrative. I tear pages out, and paint over the existing story, to create a new story. In essence the old text/story informs my new narrative  I select some words/sentences from the old printed text, and let that inform my new story. I also like the fact that a book is made up of lots of pages, so in these works I play with that idea of layering, I cut through the top pages to see through to the ones underneath, and let this layering add to the narrative. I like that these works are tactile, textured and multi-layered.
       </p>
       <p>
        OM: Who or what inspires you?
       </p>
       <p>
        RF: Reality and everyday things inspire me. I love real stories, and find it fascinating that we as humans are able to relate to each others stories, no matter how far removed it is from our own.
       </p>
       <p>
        OM: When and where is your next exhibition?
       </p>
       <p>
        RF: I am planning to take part in a group show towards the end of the year at Salon91 in Cape Town, and then there are plans for another solo show there (Salon91) next year some time.
       </p>
       <p>
        OM:Whats next for you? What are you busy with?
       </p>
       <p>
        RF: Im working on some book projects (similar to the work from the Wolves exhibition). Then Im also working on a new short comic story for an Italian magazine.
       </p>
