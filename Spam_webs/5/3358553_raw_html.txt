         <h1 style="text-align: center">
          The Quote From John F. Kennedy That Stopped Me In My Tracks
         </h1>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          As I sat down to start blogthis post to share mythoughtson the Motivation Manifesto by Brendon Burchard.
         </p>
         <p>
          JFKs quote stopped me from even opening the book up to the first page.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Motivation Manifesto opens up with two quotes, one from Albert Einstein and the other from John F. Kennedy.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you didnt recognize the quote it was the title of this blog post:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #993300">
            Conformity is the jailer of freedom and the enemy of growth
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          I had never heard that quote before reading the Motivation Manifesto, but each time I see that quote
         </p>
         <p>
          I say to myself, that is so true!
         </p>
         <p>
          Maybe you disagree, I dont knowlet me know in the comments if you agree or not.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            I believe if youre not chasing YOUR own DREAMS, then you are conforming and letting everyone else choose your fate
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Society, family, friends, people you dont even know mold you into something you dont really want to be.
         </p>
         <p>
          Maybe you had BIG dreams to grow up to change the world, but after a while the world starts beating you down or so you think.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats why if you hang around a lot of self-development people you might have heard them say
          <em>
           <strong>
            you gotta have child-like faith and just go make it happen.
           </strong>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Sometimes things are hard to comprehend, but if we have child-like faith and just roll with it, they turn out pretty awesome and if they dont.
         </p>
         <p>
          most likely we had a kick ass time along the way or learn a valuable life lesson that we can apply and grow from.
         </p>
