        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         White Chocolate Craisin 
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-11-29T05:00:46+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          November 29, 2012
         </time>
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          by:
          <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
           Chef in Training
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.chef-in-training.com/2012/11/white-chocolate-craisin-cheesecake-pudding-cookies/#comments">
           25
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-color="white" data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" data-pin-zero="true" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.chef-in-training.com%2F2012%2F11%2Fwhite-chocolate-craisin-cheesecake-pudding-cookies%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.chef-in-training.com%2Fblog%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2012%2F11%2FIMG_7035-1.jpg&amp;description=White%20Chocolate%20Craisin%20Cheesecake%20Pudding%20Cookies" rel="nobox">
          <img data-jpibfi-description="" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png?c62cc9" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Being a food blogger has its perks. In my cupboards, you will find a wide variety of spices, seasonings, mixes and extracts. Sometimes I will just go open the cupboard and stare at my extracts until something pops out at me. This particular time, the Cheesecake extract stood out to me.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Christmas season is upon us. Craisins, chocolate and cheesecake are some of my favorite flavors/desserts of the holidays. I knew they would compliment each other perfectly in, you guessed it, a pudding cookie. I adapted the original
         <a href="http://www.chef-in-training.com/2011/07/pudding-cookies/">
          <strong>
           Chocolate Chip Pudding Cookie
          </strong>
         </a>
         recipe on my blog and the end result was fantastic.
        </p>
        <p>
         In my church, I am called to work with the youth and I love it! They love it too because I bring them yummy goodies :)I brought these cookies into class and they all loved them. My husband also raved about them. So there you have it. These cookies are kid and adult approved.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20852" data-jpibfi-description="" height="1142" src="http://www.chef-in-training.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/IMG_7035-1.jpg?c62cc9" title="White Chocolate Craisin Cheesecake Pudding Cookies... These are the perfect treat for the holidays! #cookie #recipe" width="750"/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          White Chocolate Craisin Cheesecake Pudding Cookies
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img data-jpibfi-description="" itemprop="image" src="http://www.chef-in-training.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/IMG_7035-1.jpg?c62cc9" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.chef-in-training.com/easyrecipe-print/20851-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          These are the perfect treat for the holidays!
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSCategory">
           Recipe type:
           <span itemprop="recipeCategory">
            Dessert
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            3 dozen cookies
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup butter, softened
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 (3.4 oz) box cheesecake instant pudding mix, dry, do not prepare
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp. cheesecake flavoring
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp. baking soda
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup craisins
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup white chocolate chips
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 350 F.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a medium bowl, stir together flour and baking soda and set aside.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a separate large bowl, cream butter and sugars together.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add in dry cheesecake instant pudding mix and beat until well blended.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add eggs and cheesecake flavoring and mix well. (if you don't have cheesecake flavoring, vanilla will work as well)
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add flour mixture slowly until well incorporated.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add chocolate chips and craisins and stir in to cookie dough.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Roll into 1 balls and place on greased baking sheet.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake at 350 F for 8-12 minutes.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="endeasyrecipe" style="display: none" title="style002">
          3.3.3070
         </div>
        </div>
        <br/>
        Enjoy
        <p>
         (Recipe base from my
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.chef-in-training.com/2011/07/pudding-cookies/">
           Chocolate Chip Pudding Cookie
          </a>
          s
         </strong>
         )
        </p>
        <p>
         While I came up with the recipe on my own, I am getting in the habit of googling recipes before I post them to see if the recipe already exists. While searching I found this recipe at
         <a href="http://annadoll.wordpress.com/2012/11/17/white-chocolate-and-cranberry-cheesecake-cookies/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://annadoll.wordpress.com/2012/11/17/white-chocolate-and-cranberry-cheesecake-cookies/', 'Annadoll']);">
          <strong>
           Annadoll
          </strong>
         </a>
         that is similar to mine. While our recipes are slightly different, I want to give her a shout out too :)
        </p>

           <p>
            I am 54 years old and have been making pudding cookies since I was in high school and possibly before. My mother and I used to make them together. In fact I dug out the original pudding box that we got the recipe from, the other day to show my 16 year old son. He tried to google some of the numbers from the box to see if he could figure out its age. No luck but I am guessing its from the 1970s.  I have adapted all of my cookie recipes to include pudding. I am somewhat famous (or infamousLOL) for my cookies in Las Vegas. Everyone loves them! I was surprised when I searched Pinterest the other day for pudding cookies just how many are out thereI used to tell people my recipe was a secret!!!.
