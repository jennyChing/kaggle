       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Angela Pia (Pious Angel): a Vintage Dessert
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         Posted on
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-10-07T13:02:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 7, 2011
         </time>
         Written by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://boulderlocavore.com/author/boul7429/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Toni Dash
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://boulderlocavore.com/angela-pia-pious-angel-a-vintage-dessert/#comments">
           34 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-unVz8K61hpM/To7zsr4Xx2I/AAAAAAAACJs/LzPu_mwMl4I/s1600/Boulder+Locavorevintangela+pia+0031.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" height="640" src="//boulderlocavore.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Boulder+Locavorevintangela+pia+0031.jpg" width="428"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           After making a
           <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
            <a href="http://www.boulderlocavore.com/2011/09/gratitude-and-grasshopper-pie.html">
             Grasshopper Pie
            </a>
           </strong>
           from a family recipe for a vintage affair a few weeks back, I was inspired to pull out my Grandmothers recipe box which is filled with her hand written recipes, and those from her friends as well as my Great Grandmother.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           It was a time before canned soup casseroles with three ingredients were in vogue and everything was a whole food for the most part.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           I was thumbing through the dessert sections and landed on a few that felt unique or seasonal I wanted to try and pass along.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           My first choice proved that in the 60s (my guess at the recipe vintage) cocktail hour was clearly NOT just for before dinner.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           The dessert named 
           <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
            Angela Pia
           </strong>
           or translated from Italian to be 
           <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
            Pious Angel
           </strong>
           was not something I could pass up.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           I had scanned the ingredients before making but it was not until I was putting it together that the strong waft of the liquor gave way to suggestions on serving sizes!
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           It was the name that caught my attention but I wont be desserting and driving (
           <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
            actually in the tasting it was much more mild, really producing flavor from the brandy and rum but no teetering!)
           </em>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ADMGSF-lr9U/To70CS1XEtI/AAAAAAAACJw/avMOZA_gGtI/s1600/scan0001.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" height="371" src="//boulderlocavore.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/scan00014.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           My grandparents would have lived in San Francisco at the time.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Anyone who has lived in Northern California in the last century would have heard of famous newspaper
           <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
            columnist Herb
           </strong>
           <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
            Caen
           </strong>
           .
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           His columns in the San Francisco chronicle began in the late 1930s and spanned almost 60 years, always the talk of the breakfast or dinner table in my grandparents household.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           When I saw his recommendation on this recipe slip I was sure it must have been a hit at the time.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           I did some sleuthing on the pre-printed recipe I found that had seemingly been torn from a larger document.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           What I pieced together with some internet help is that the original dessert was served at a restaurant named
           <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
            Pietro 311
           </strong>
           on Washington Street in San Francisco.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           At the time this would have been in the Produce District, I suspect leaving the clearly adored restaurant a surprise due to its unlikely location combined with its quality cuisine.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           The area where it stood is now the Embarcadero Center for those knowledgeable to the Bay Area.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-AczZ0v0QPrQ/To71hbGsPII/AAAAAAAACJ0/fmyZuvgxDW8/s1600/Boulder+Locavorevintangela+pia+0013-1.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" height="640" src="//boulderlocavore.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Boulder+Locavorevintangela+pia+0013-1.jpg" width="448"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           I found a few references to the restaurant in chat sessions from the past decade.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           They all shared a common palpable nostalgia and fondness for how special the restaurant and its cuisine were.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           This dessert was always mentioned so must have really something in the era (before Jello Pudding Parfaits hit the shelves!).
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
            <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
             ANGELA PIA (Pious Angel)
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
          <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
           <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            I moved the preparation steps around from the original recipe not wanting the whipped egg whites or cream to sit and deflate.
            <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
            </span>
            This all came together easily though Id suggest reading all instructions and preparing every step ready before beginning.
           </span>
          </span>
         </em>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
          <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
           <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            Serves: 6-8
           </span>
          </span>
         </em>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-family: Symbol; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Symbol; mso-fareast-font-family: Symbol;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           3 eggs, separated
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-family: Symbol; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Symbol; mso-fareast-font-family: Symbol;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            cup granulated sugar
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-family: Symbol; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Symbol; mso-fareast-font-family: Symbol;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           1 ounce brandy
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-family: Symbol; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Symbol; mso-fareast-font-family: Symbol;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           1 ounce rum
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-family: Symbol; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Symbol; mso-fareast-font-family: Symbol;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           1 cup whipping cream
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-family: Symbol; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Symbol; mso-fareast-font-family: Symbol;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           1 package Knox gelatin
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-family: Symbol; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Symbol; mso-fareast-font-family: Symbol;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           1 teaspoon vanilla
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.25in;">
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l1 level1 lfo2; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            1.
           </span>
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Set a saucepan filled with water over medium high heat on the stove to come to a low boil.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l1 level1 lfo2; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            2.
           </span>
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Add the packet of gelatin to  cup of water in a metal bowl (which will go over the saucepan of hot water) for 5 minutes.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l1 level1 lfo2; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            3.
           </span>
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           While the water is coming to a low boil and gelatin is soaking, add egg yolks to a different bowl and whisk or beat with a mixer until fully mixed.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Gradually add the sugar and continue beating until fully mixed into a light yellow, thick mixture.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Add the brandy and rum and mix thoroughly.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l1 level1 lfo2; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            4.
           </span>
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           If 5 minutes has elapsed, place the bowl with gelatin over the hot water and whisk until it is fully dissolved.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Remove from heat to cool for a few minutes.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Pour into yolk mixture and stir to fully combine.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l1 level1 lfo2; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
          <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">
           <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
            <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
             5.
            </span>
            <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </em>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Beat the egg whites until stiff but not dry.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
            Note:
           </strong>
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
            I did this step during the time the gelatin was processing.
           </em>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; mso-list: l1 level1 lfo2; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">
          <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
           <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            6.
           </span>
           <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Beat whipping cream until it forms peaks and add the vanilla.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-list: l1 level1 lfo2; text-indent: -0.25in;">
         <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
          <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">
           <span style="mso-list: Ignore;">
            <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
             7.
            </span>
            <span style="font-size-adjust: none; font-stretch: normal; font: 7pt/normal;">
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </em>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Fold together the egg whites and whipping cream mixture.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Fold those into the yolk mixture until all are fully combined.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Put into small cocktail glasses or sherbet cups to chill or freeze at least two hours before serving (
           <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
            I froze mine longer and they were light and easy to eat).
           </em>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BIx_g_I32rM/To72MhFEfvI/AAAAAAAACJ4/k00JPoVp-Pw/s1600/+Boulder+Locavore+vintage+angela+pia+5.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" height="428" src="//boulderlocavore.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/+Boulder+Locavore+vintage+angela+pia+5.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           This dessert is light with a texture of frozen whipped topping and a lovely flavor of the vanilla and brandy.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           To me it looked naked in the freezer and I thought about adding a sour cherry sauce or something to it but in the tasting it does not require a thing; it is angelic on its own.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Maybe a garnish..!
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-623Kvb0rrRA/To72uRAnyeI/AAAAAAAACJ8/p7Qv2gL9DR8/s1600/Boulder+Locavorevintangela+pia+0023.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" height="428" src="//boulderlocavore.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Boulder+Locavorevintangela+pia+0023.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Another thing of the time that jumped out at me is that this recipe
           <strong style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
            uses raw eggs
           </strong>
           .
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           Salmonella was present in the 1960s and I did NOT get the impression that dessert was made famous by causing deaths.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           However we live in a more cautious time so I also did research about using the raw eggs, contacting farmers and chefs I know as well.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           To be the most cautious I would recommend using pasteurized eggs which should remove any risk from eating raw eggs.
           <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
           </span>
           The statistics on contracting Salmonella are pretty slight but of course follow your own judgment.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-nDj_0ObDxOg/To73QPOB2lI/AAAAAAAACKA/f4AH9EcNFM8/s1600/Boulder+Locavorevintangela+pia+0002.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" height="640" src="//boulderlocavore.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/Boulder+Locavorevintangela+pia+0002.jpg" width="428"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div align="left" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">
          <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
           <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
            Post Script.
            <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
            </span>
            Following the posting of this article I received two emails that I could not resist sharing.
            <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
            </span>
            After my research travels on the internet and encountering many misty nostalgic references to Pietro 311, I felt there are others who would relish this background history as I do.
            <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">
            </span>
            My great thanks to Francis for taking the time to write.
           </span>
          </span>
         </em>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Hello. My cousin, Stephanie Alioto, was married to Pietro Pinoni, who owned and operated Pietros 311 restaurant at 311 Washington Street in San Francisco, where I was a frequent diner. I loved the Angela Pia desert, which means pious angel but which was named after his sister-in-law, Angelina Alioto Figone. And the reason the restaurant was in the produce district at the time was because Pietros father-in-law, my Grand Uncle Ignatius Aliotos fish processing plant, Consolidated Fish Co., was just up the street, and my Grandfathers plant, San Francisco International Fish Co., was a couple of blocks away where what is now the redwood grove of the Transamerica building at the corner of Washington and Montgomery Streets.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Pietro started his restaurant in the late 50s at 311 Washington St., then when that lease was not renewed, he moved up a block, but kept the 311 name until redevelopment took that building for the new apartments and high-rise buildings sometime in the mid- to late 60s. Pietro then moved the restaurant across to Marin County, but that was short-lived. He retired to make wine, write a book on wine making, and travel.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           How long did you all live in SF? Do you remember the Tortola on Polk Street? The predecessor, Garabaldis Tamale Parlor, was started by my Great-grandfather; operated by my grandparents, and then by my father until about 1978, when it closed, as none of us wanted to go into the restaurant business.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           At one time, a group of us made a list of t he old SF restaurants (and businesses) that were such a part of the city and are now gone  e.g., Mayes Oyster House, The Old Poodle Dog, Jacks, Paolis, The Temple Bar, El Matador, Original Joes on Broadway, Vanessis, Venetos, The White House, J. Magnin, Roos Brothers, etc. I am sure you remember all of them.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
        </div>
        <div style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
          <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
           Regards,
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
