             A recent study by scientists at McMaster University in Canada found that honeybees effectively steer nave recruits away from dangerous flowers by the way they
             <a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science?_ob=ArticleURL&amp;_udi=B6W9W-4WTYY12-1&amp;_user=10&amp;_rdoc=1&amp;_fmt=&amp;_orig=search&amp;_sort=d&amp;_docanchor=&amp;view=c&amp;_acct=C000050221&amp;_version=1&amp;_urlVersion=0&amp;_userid=10&amp;md5=bd22dc681388669f79c9c42dc0b793fc" target="_blank">
              dance directions
             </a>
             , according to Animal Behavior.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Bees normally do a waggle dance upon returning to the hive from a successful hunt for foodthey wiggle in a figure eight pattern
             <a href="http://www.livescience.com/animals/050527_bee_dance.html" target="_blank">
              gesturing the direction of the food source
             </a>
             according to its position relative to the sun, LiveScience explains.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             There are
             <a href="http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/nova/bees/dances.html" target="_blank">
              two types of bee dance
             </a>
             , according to PBSs NOVA; the waggle dance is used when food is farther than 35 yards away, and gives direction, and the round dance is used when food is closer to the hive, and helps give the other bees the scent of the nearby flower. The dances intensity increases with the abundance of the food source.
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sidebar sidebar_full">
            <div class="sidebar_text">
             See the
             <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nga4Z_HRUsU" target="_blank">
              "waggle dance"
             </a>
             in action in this YouTube video.
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="resource_section">
            <div class="resource_text">
             In this most recent study, however, scientists found that the bees waggled less when returning from a hunt where they encountered flowers in which the researchers had planted dead bees to signal danger, according to BBC. Thus, in addition to pointing out abundant food sources, the signaling
             <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/earth/hi/earth_news/newsid_8176000/8176878.stm" target="_blank">
              bees help others avoid dangerous places
             </a>
             by not directing them there.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Alerting bees of danger is becoming increasingly important to the survival of a hive, as bee populations worldwide are on the decline. Bees are listed as one of "
             <a href="http://www.findingdulcinea.com/features/science/environment/Five-Animals-We-Need-to-Survive.html">
              Five Animals We Need To Survive
             </a>
             ." Some trends, such as
             <a href="http://www.findingdulcinea.com/news/environment/2009/june/Urban-Beekeeping-Illegal-in-NYC--But-Growing-in-Popularity-Nationwide.html">
              urban beekeeping
             </a>
             , may also help to encourage growth of the bee population.
            </div>
