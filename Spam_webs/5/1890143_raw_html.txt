For those who may not be familiar slope-intercept form it is y = mx + b where m is the slope and b is the

                 <div class="MsoNormal">

models. For example: if you bought a car at $81,292 and after 10
years it is only worth $33,121 to find out how much it will be worth after 13
years you can use slope-intercept to get a depreciation formula. The two points
are at 0 years it is 81,292 (0, 81,292) and at 10 years it is 33,121 (10 ,
33,121) substituting these numbers into our formulas you first find the slope
which will represent the rate of depreciation.

                   (33,121  81,292) /
(10-0) = -48,171 / 10 = -4817.1

0(4817.1) = b= 81292
                  </b>

                  <b>

                  </b>
