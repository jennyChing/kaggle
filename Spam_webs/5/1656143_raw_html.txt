     <h1 class="entry-title">
      Beautiful Words about Beautiful Flowers: Our Favorite Flower Poetry
     </h1>
     <div class="meta right">
      <time class="published" datetime="2014-03-21T12:46:57+00:00">
       March 21, 2014
      </time>
     </div>
    </header>
   </div>
   <div class="wrap">
    <div class="container" role="document">
     <div class="content row">
      <main class="main " role="main">
       <div class="entry-content col-md-offset-1 col-md-10 col-lg-offset-2 col-lg-8">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <img alt="Favorite Flower Poetry" class="aligncenter" height="150" src="http://a1128.g.akamai.net/7/1128/497/0001/origin-d5.scene7.com/is/image/ProvideCommerce/PoetryDay-header_blog140321" width="610"/>
         We simply cant get enough floral photography, but we also love to read beautiful poetry and prose about flowers and nature. In just a few powerful words, our mind creates its own image and invigorates our senses, whisking us away to that endless flower field or basking in the sun with our hands in the garden.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres just something so intimate about letting our imaginations run freely after diving into a poem, so we wanted to share our favorite flower poetry with you. Hopefully these beautiful words inspire your own imaginations and possibly a poem of your own!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Carol Brown" height="500" src="http://a1128.g.akamai.net/7/1128/497/0001/origin-d5.scene7.com/is/image/ProvideCommerce/PoetryDay-1_blog140321" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Beauty in nature
         <br/>
         Flower blooms so succulent
         <br/>
         Hummingbirds flutter
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Carol Brown
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         One little flower
         <br/>
         Nods in the delicate breeze
         <br/>
         As the crickets hum
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Jeanine DeJesus
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vassilis Comporozos" height="500" src="http://a1128.g.akamai.net/7/1128/497/0001/origin-d5.scene7.com/is/image/ProvideCommerce/PoetryDay-5_blog140321" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The flower is
         <br/>
         the stems cry of beauty
         <br/>
         to the universe.
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Vassilis Comporozos
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Whats in a name? that which we call a rose
         <br/>
         By any other name would smell as sweet.
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Excerpt from Romeo and Juliet(2.2.45-7)
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         This old world that were livin in
         <br/>
         Is might hard to beat.
         <br/>
         You get a thorn with every Rose
         <br/>
         But  aint the roses sweet?
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Frank Stanton
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Silently a flower blooms,
         <br/>
         In silence it falls away;
         <br/>
         Yet here now, at this moment, at this place,
         <br/>
         The world of the flower, the whole of
         <br/>
         the world is blooming.
         <br/>
         This is the talk of the flower, the truth
         <br/>
         of the blossom:
         <br/>
         The glory of eternal life is fully shining here.
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Zenkei Shibayama
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         You love the roses  so do I.I wish
         <br/>
         The sky would rain down roses, as they rain
         <br/>
         From off the shaken bush. Why will it not?
         <br/>
         Then all the valley would be pink and white
         <br/>
         And soft to tread on.They would fall as light
         <br/>
         As feathers, smelling sweet; and it would be
         <br/>
         Like sleeping and like waking, all at once!
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -George Eliot,Roses
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="William Blake" height="500" src="http://a1128.g.akamai.net/7/1128/497/0001/origin-d5.scene7.com/is/image/ProvideCommerce/PoetryDay-3_blog140321" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         To see the world in a grain of sand
         <br/>
         and heaven in a wildflower,
         <br/>
         hold infinity in the palm of your hand
         <br/>
         and eternity in an hour.
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -William Blake
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The bud may have a bitter taste,
         <br/>
         But sweet will be the flower.
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -William Cowper
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; color: #333; line-height: 24px;">
           THE ROSE
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The lily has a smooth stalk,
         <br/>
         Will never hurt your hand;
         <br/>
         But the rose upon her brier
         <br/>
         Is lady of the land.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres sweetness in an apple tree,
         <br/>
         And profit in the corn;
         <br/>
         But lady of all beauty
         <br/>
         Is a rose upon a thorn.
        </p>
        <p>
         When with moss and honey
         <br/>
         She tips her bending brier,
         <br/>
         And half unfolds her glowing heart,
         <br/>
         She sets the world on fire.
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           <br/>
           -Christina Rossetti
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; color: #333; line-height: 24px;">
           Sunny Flowers
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Joy is seeing
         <br/>
         sunshines reflection
         <br/>
         in Mother Natures flowers.
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Gail Sobotkin
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Gail Sobotkin" height="500" src="http://a1128.g.akamai.net/7/1128/497/0001/origin-d5.scene7.com/is/image/ProvideCommerce/PoetryDay-2_blog140321" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; color: #333; line-height: 24px;">
           Sunny Side Up
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Two flowers
         <br/>
         natures very own eggs
         <br/>
         served sunny side up.
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Gail Sobotkin
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         As a flower in the garden
         <br/>
         Bending toward the sun,
         <br/>
         Unfolds its tiny petals
         <br/>
         One, by one, by one
         <br/>
         So faith expands its beauty
         <br/>
         Until at last it grows
         <br/>
         Into lifes lasting flower
         <br/>
         The hearts fair perfect rose.
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -Rebecca Helmann
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 18px; color: #333; line-height: 24px;">
           Flowers by the Sea
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         When over the flowery, sharp pastures
         <br/>
         edge, unseen, the salt ocean
         <br/>
         lifts its formchicory and daisies
         <br/>
         tied, released, seem hardly flowers alone
         <br/>
         but color and the movementor the shape
         <br/>
         perhapsof restlessness, whereas
         <br/>
         the sea is circled and sways
         <br/>
         peacefully upon its plantlike stem
         <br/>
         <span style="font-weight: bold;">
          <span style="font-style: italic;">
           -William Carlos William
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
