        <p>
         Lets not bury the lead here. At a moment when people across the country are reckoning with the deadly reality of police violence and the terror it imposes on black communities, the New York Police Department fractured the leg of a player in the National Basketball Association. The NYPD had an interaction with Thabo Sefolosha of the Atlanta Hawks, and they broke his damn leg.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sefoloshas damaged fibula comes after a season when NBA players
         <a href="http://www.thenation.com/article/power-political-athletes-puncture-privilege">
          spent last winter
         </a>
         making statements against police violence, after the killings of Michael Brown and Eric Garner. It also comes at a time when police brutality is under an exacting microscope following
         <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2015/apr/09/walter-scott-shooting-america-racism">
          the execution of Walter Scott
         </a>
         by Officer Michael Slager in North Charleston, South Carolina. In the blinkered reality of the sports world, the big story is that the damage to Sefolosha has happened right when the Atlanta Hawks are about to enter the playoffs with the best record in the Eastern Conference, jeopardizing what has been a dream season. Now, unless they make a deep playoff run, it will be remembered as a dream trapped between nightmares; a squad whose season began under a cloud of racist controversy, with the ugly
         <a href="http://www.thenation.com/article/out-executing-nba-game-plan-atlanta-hawks-and-levenson-e-mail">
          leaked interactions
         </a>
         between owner Bruce Levenson and general manager Danny Ferry, and now ends under a similarly colored cloud.
        </p>
        <p>
         How in the hell did the NYPD come to injure Thabo Sefolosha? One moment fellow NBA player Chris Copeland and Copelands girlfriend Katrine Saltara were being stabbed at a trendy Chelsea nightclub (both are in stable condition), then Sefolosha and his Atlanta Hawk teammate Pero Antic were being arrested for obstruction and then a broken leg. As for how Sefoloshas fibula was fractured, there is the police version of what went down and then there is Thabos version. Stunningly, several outlets including ESPN first printed the polices version as fact. If nothing else, the death of Walter Scott should be a lesson to all of us that there is a chasm between what the police can say happened and the reality of a situation.
        </p>
        <p>
         The police version, to quote ESPNs original article, was, Sefolosha sustained the injury while resisting arrest outside a Manhattan night club early Wednesday morning. Sefolosha was arrested along with teammate Pero Antic for interfering with local polices efforts to set up a crime scene following the stabbing of Indiana Pacers forward Chris Copeland.
        </p>
        <p>
         A great deal of credit should go to the author of this piece, Kevin Arnovitz, who
         <a href="http://espn.go.com/nba/story/_/id/12656394/atlanta-hawks-forward-thabo-sefolosha-broken-leg-indefinitely">
          changed
         </a>
         the wording in the article after a social media pushback. But the original text should be a reminder, especially this week of all weeks, that we should never take police versions as synonymous with reality. Sure enough, Sefolosha and Pero Antic deny this version of events. Their only statement

              Bernie Sanders Speaks
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             As members of the Atlanta Hawks, we hold ourselves to a high standard and take our roles as professionals very seriously. We will contest these charges and look forward to communicating the facts of the situation at the appropriate time. We apologize to our respective families, teammates, and the Hawks organization for any negative attention this incident has brought upon them. We are unable to provide further comment as this is an ongoing legal matter.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            We do
            <a href="http://www.tmz.com/2015/04/09/thabo-sefolosha-video-of-combative-arrest-headlocked-and-taken-down/">
             have a videotape
            </a>
            of what took place*, but all it reveals is multiple police officers jumping the rail-thin 67 220 pound Sefolosha. Ironically, or tellingly, his fellow-arrestee, Pero Antic, has an appearance well describe as
            <a href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/22/PeroAntic.jpeg">
             ornately terrifying.
            </a>
            Tattooed, bald, seven feet tall and over 260 pounds, he is a Macedonian guy who happens to be white. Sefolosha is a Swiss guy who happens to be black. The terrifying seven-footer walked away and the guy from Switzerland was jumped. Whether or not racial bias was involved, the optics of this are very familiar to anyone who has followed the methodologies of the NYPD.
           </p>
           <p style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #bf0e15; font-weight: bold; font-size: 14px; text-align: center;">
            <a href="https://ssl.palmcoastd.com/06601/apps/ORDOPTION1LANDING?ikey=I**ARL" style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #bf0e15; font-weight: bold; font-size: 14px; text-align: center; text-decoration: none;" target="_blank">
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            In time, we will find out what happened. Sefolosha has the deep pockets and the lawyers to either wrest some justice out of this situation or, if it is determined that he did obstruct justice, make it go away. But in the league, it will be seen as bigger than one case, one confrontation, one injury. This is the year when the NBA intersected with the #BlackLivesMatter movement. Perhaps after a several month lull, players or the NBPA** will have something to say, if not about Walter Scott, then about Thabo Sefolosha. The message from NBA players last winter was that what happened to Michael Brown or Eric Garner could happen to me. Well, police violence has come home. This is bigger than the damage the Hawks have suffered to their title dreams. Its about whether fame and fortune can buy safety in the United States, if one also happens to be living while black.
           </p>

	   event.preventDefault();
	   $(this).closest('.article-footer').find('.commentContainer').slideToggle('slow');
		 $('html, body').animate({
				scrollTop: $($(this).closest('.article-footer').find('.commentContainer')).offset().top
			},2000);
	 });
	 
	var list;
	$('body').on('click','a.comment-reply-link',function(event){
	$( ".comment-status" ).remove();
	var comment_link_respond = $(this);		
	comment_link_respond.closest('.comment-form').find('.comment-status').html('');
	list = $(this).closest('article').attr('id');

	var PIDS = list.split('-');
	$(this).closest('.comments-area').find('.comment-respond').find('#comment_parent').val(PIDS[PIDS.length-1]);
	
	});
	
	$('body').on('click','.cancel-comment-reply-link',function(event){
	$( ".comment-status" ).remove();
	list=null;
	});
	
	
	$('body').on('click','.submit',function(event){
		event.preventDefault();
		$( ".comment-status" ).remove();
	var commentform=$(this).closest('.comment-form'); // find the comment  form
	    commentform.prepend('&lt;div class="comment-status" &gt;&lt;/div&gt;'); // add info panel before the form to provide feedback or errors
        var statusdiv=commentform.find('.comment-status'); // define the infopanel
				var checkspace = $(this).closest('.comment-form').find("textarea#comment").val();
		if (checkspace.replace(/\s/g, '').length &amp;&amp; $(this).closest('.comment-form').find("textarea#comment").val()!='') {
			statusdiv.html(' ');
			//serialize and store form data in a variable
			var formdata=commentform.serialize();
			//Add a status message
			//Extract action URL from commentform
			var formurl=commentform.attr('action');
			
				statusdiv.html('&lt;p&gt;Processing...&lt;/p&gt;');
				 $(this).attr('disabled','disabled');
				var e = $(this);
				$.ajax({
						type: 'post',
						url: formurl,
						data: formdata,
					  error: function(XMLHttpRequest, textStatus, errorThrown)
						{
							statusdiv.html(' ');
							 $('.submit').removeAttr('disabled');
							statusdiv.html('&lt;p class="error_cmnt" &gt;You might have left one of the fields blank, or be posting too quickly&lt;/p&gt;');
						},
						success: function(data, textStatus){
							var chkstatus_comment = $($.parseHTML(data)).filter(".chk_comment_class").attr('comment-status');
							
								if (chkstatus_comment=='spam') {
									statusdiv.html('&lt;p class="error_cmnt" &gt;You have been blocked from commenting by &lt;i&gt;The Nation\'s&lt;/i&gt; moderators.&lt;/p&gt;');
								}
								else{
									$('.submit').removeAttr('disabled'); 
									$("textarea#comment").val('');
									statusdiv.html('&lt;p class="success_cmnt" &gt;&lt;/p&gt;');
									if(e.closest(".comments-area").has("ol.comment-list").length &gt; 0){
										if(list != null){
											e.closest(".comments-area").find('#'+list).after(data);
										}
										else{
											e.closest(".comments-area").find('ol.comment-list').prepend(data);
										}
									}
									else{
										e.closest(".comments-area").prepend('&lt;ol class="comment-list custom_comments"&gt; &lt;/ol&gt;');
										e.closest(".comments-area").find('ol.comment-list').html(data);
									}
							}
						}
						
				});
				return false;
			statusdiv.html('');
		}else{
			statusdiv.html('&lt;p class="error_cmnt" &gt;Please enter text in comment box.&lt;/p&gt;');
		}
        }); 
});
