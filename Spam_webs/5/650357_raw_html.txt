       <p>
        <strong>
         What is Hummus?
        </strong>
        Hummus is simply a frugally fantastic and delicious dip or spread made from cooked chickpeas! Its cheap, high in protein, and easy to make in under 10 minutes. Hummus (also spelled hamos, houmous, hommos, hommus, hummos, hummous or humus, WHEW!) is a popular food throughout the Middle Eastern world.
       </p>
       <p>
        Traditionally, hummus is served as a dip with pita bread cut into wonderful wedges. It also pairs well as a vegetable dip for carrots, snow peas, celery, mushrooms, radishes and much more! Hummus makes a great appetizer at parties, is delicious as a meat alternative in sandwiches or wraps, and is an amazing side dish served with grilled meats (chicken or salmon) and a salad.
       </p>
       <p>
        Since I love sharing frugal, delicious, and healthful foods with you, here are three recipes for making heart-healthy protein-packed hummus. Ive included an
        <strong>
         Authentic Hummus Recipe
        </strong>
        , a
        <strong>
         Lighter Hummus Recipe
        </strong>
        (with dairy), and a
        <strong>
         Low Fat Vegan Recipe
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <center>
        <img alt="humus_veggies_snack.JPG" class="alignnone size-full" height="360" src="http://www.squawkfox.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/07/humus_veggies_snack.JPG" width="480"/>
       </center>
       <h2>
        Recipe: Authentic Hummus
       </h2>
       <p>
        One of the tricks to making gob smacking humus is keeping the ingredients fresh. Ive included directions for using both canned chickpeas (also called garbanzo beans) and
        <a href="http://www.squawkfox.com/2008/02/19/how-to-soak-and-cook-dried-beans/">
         dried beans
        </a>
        .
       </p>

       <h3>
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <ul>
        <li>
         1 can (19 oz/540 mL) chickpeas, drained and rinsed. (OR soak and cook 6 oz of
         <a href="http://www.squawkfox.com/2008/02/19/how-to-soak-and-cook-dried-beans/">
          dried chickpeas
         </a>
         )
        </li>
        <li>
         1/4 cup (50 mL) lemon juice
        </li>
        <li>
         1/4 cup (50 mL) olive oil
        </li>
        <li>
         2 tbsp (25 mL) tahini
        </li>
        <li>
         2 cloves garlic, chopped
        </li>
        <li>
         1/2 tsp (2 mL) ground cumin
        </li>
        <li>
         1/4 tsp (1 mL) each salt and pepper
        </li>
        <li>
         3 tbsp water (if desired)
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h3>
        Instructions:
       </h3>
       <p>
        In a food processor, blend chickpeas. Add lemon juice, olive oil, tahini, garlic, cumin, salt and pepper and blend. Add a little water to thin if desired. Scrape into serving bowl. Cover and refrigerate for up to 3 days.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Tip:
        </strong>
        Tahini is a sesame seed paste (or spread) found in grocery or ethnic speciality stores. Tahini adds a few extra calories to the hummus, but its the best for an authentic hummus recipe. For a lighter hummus, feel free to omit the tahini.
       </p>

       <h3>
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <ul>
        <li>
         3/4 cup (175 mL) fat-free plain yogurt
        </li>
        <li>
         1 can (19 oz/540 mL) chickpeas, drained and rinsed. (OR soak and cook 6 oz of
         <a href="http://www.squawkfox.com/2008/02/19/how-to-soak-and-cook-dried-beans/">
          dried chickpeas
         </a>
         )
        </li>
        <li>
         2 tbsp (25 mL) lemon juice
        </li>
        <li>
         1 tsp (5 mL) ground cumin
        </li>
        <li>
         1/4 tsp (1 mL) salt
        </li>
        <li>
         pinch cayenne pepper
        </li>
        <li>
         2 cloves garlic, minced
        </li>
        <li>
         1 tbsp (15 mL) olive oil (optional)
        </li>
        <li>
         pinch paprika
        </li>
        <li>
         3 tbsp water (if desired)
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h3>
        Instructions:
       </h3>
       <p>
        In food processor, blend yogurt, chickpeas, lemon juice, sesame oil, garlic, cumin, salt and cayenne pepper until smooth. Add water to thin, if desired. Scrape into bowl. Cover and refrigerate for up to 3 days.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Tip:
        </strong>
        To lessen the liquidity of the hummus, try draining the water from the yogurt. Line a small sieve with cheesecloth and set over bowl. Add yogurt and drain in refrigerator until reduced by half, about 2 hours. Add this reduced yogurt to the hummus.
       </p>
