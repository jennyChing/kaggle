      <h1 class="entry_title">
       Why Apples iPad is Set To Dominate the Tablet Market
      </h1>
      <div class="singlepostmeta" style="font-size:14px">
       <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-68 photo" height="68" src="http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/191e14ab1f495fbd09a81d2820008719?s=68&amp;d=monsterid&amp;r=g" srcset="http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/191e14ab1f495fbd09a81d2820008719?s=136&amp;d=monsterid&amp;r=g 2x" width="68"/>
       <strong>
        About Michael John Scott
       </strong>
       <br/>
       Mr. Scott is a political junkie, and animal lover.  He is also a U.S. Army veteran, career law enforcement executive and university professor. In addition he happens to own MadMikesAmerica which means he can write anything he wants, and often does.
       <br/>
       <a href="http://madmikesamerica.com/author/madmikesamerica/">
        View all posts by Michael John Scott 
       </a>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
      </div>
      <h3>
       Im an Apple devotee. I have 2 iMacs, a MacBook Air, an iPhone, and an iPad. It looks like I might be upgrading my iPad as soon as the new ones are released to the market. I became an Apple fan after my Dell PC crashed following just a year and a half of use, and that wasnt the first time my PC had crashed, but it was going to be the last.
      </h3>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_106120" style="width: 610px">
       <a href="http://madmikesamerica.com/2013/10/why-apples-ipad-is-set-to-dominate-thetablet-market/imac27inch_35440705_01_1_620x433/" rel="attachment wp-att-106120">
        <img alt='27" iMac.  Picture courtesy of CNET.' class="size-full wp-image-106120" height="403" src="http://madmikesamerica.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/imac27inch_35440705_01_1_620x433.jpg" width="600"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        27 iMac. Picture courtesy of CNET.
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       I was tired of the constant calls for anti-viral, anti-spyware, anti-malware software, and I was tired of always having to clean the cache, remove the cookies and tune the machine to get the maximum benefit. I knew there had to be a better way, so I took my PC to the local dump, and ordered my first iMac from Amazon.
      </p>
      <p>
       When it arrived two days later I was thrilled, and a wee bit nervous. This was a new operating system, and a whole new world of computing. Could I adapt? Could I make the switch from the clunky Windows OS to Apples OSX? Well, within a week, I had made that switch and was convinced that this was the best $1200 I had spent in a long, long time, and I never, ever looked back. No spyware, no malware, no constant, time consuming upgrades, and an always up, immediate boot, fast, one piece computer.
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        (NEWSER) The iPad is poised for a bright futureand it just might be indomitable, writes Farhad Manjoo in the
        <a href="http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702304682504579154011806712216?mod=WSJ_hps_MIDDLENexttoWhatsNewsThird" target="_blank">
         Wall Street Journal
        </a>
        . At the very least, it should continue to control a healthy portion of the tablet market. But after
        <a href="http://www.newser.com/story/176332/apple-unveils-slim-ipad-air.html">
         this weeks presentation
        </a>
        , Manjoo is willing to wager that the competition might become a sideshow: With tablets looking like the new PCs, Apple could establish a self-perpetuating, super-profitable juggernaut, becoming the Microsoft, Intel, and Dell of tomorrows computer business.
       </p>
       <p>
        Yesterday, Apple unveiled a $299 iPad mini, helping establish enough pricing diversity both to bring in more customers and stay top-of-the-line. Meanwhile, Apples unified platform, iOS, is a huge attraction for software developers compared to Googles perilously fragmented Android. Make an iPad app, and its viable on all modelswhereas an Android app has to take into consideration all the variations on various hardware. And theres a cycle at work: More people buy iPads, so developers build more apps, so more iPads sell. Its the secret of Windows success, now powering Apples post-PC hegemony. Click for the
        <a href="http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702304682504579154011806712216?mod=WSJ_hps_MIDDLENexttoWhatsNewsThird" target="_blank">
         full piece
        </a>
        .
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Two years after I bought my first iMac, I bought another one, with a larger screen, and faster processor. Following on the heels of that purchase came the MacBook Air, the iPhone, and finally the iPad Mini. Remember once you go Apple youll never go back. Yes. They are slightly more expensive than PCs but you get what you pay for.
      </p>
