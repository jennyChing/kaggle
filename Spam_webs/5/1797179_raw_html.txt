            Top 10 Blacklisted Apps in the Enterprise (CNET Tech Republic, Gina Smith)
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             Here's my latest piece from TechRepublic. A lot of fun doing this one!
            </p>
            <p>
             Excerpted below.
            </p>
            <h1>
             The top 10 apps being blacklisted in the enterprise
            </h1>
            <div id="siu-horizontal">
             <div>
              <div>
               By
               <a href="http://www.techrepublic.com/search?q=gina+smith" rel="author">
                Gina Smith
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div>
             <div>
              <div>
               May 17, 2012, 1:44 PM PDT
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              Takeaway:Whether theyre regarded as productivity killers or security risks, many apps have made it onto company blacklists.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thanks to the invasion of personal apps, services, and hardware in enterprise, it shouldnt surprise anyone that smart IT pros out to protect data are conducting a wholesale blacklisting of the apps they find most threatening to data security.
             </p>
             <p>
              Zenprise, a mobile device management company that specializes in BYOD, recently released its
              <a href="http://www.zenprise.com/mdm-cloud-report-q1-2012" target="_blank">
               Zenprise MDM Cloud Report
              </a>
              . The report includes the top 10 apps that enterprises are blacklisting (or attempting to blacklist) in the United States and globally. The list is theirs. The snide commentary, mine. So here they are. The 10 most blacklisted apps this month, in descending order.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Note: This list is also available as a
               <a href="http://www.techrepublic.com/photos/10-apps-that-top-enterprise-blacklists/6364407" target="_blank">
                photo gallery
               </a>
               .
              </em>
             </p>
             <h2>
              1: Angry Birds
             </h2>
             <p>
              Would someone please muster up the courage to tell the CEO to stop with the slingshot already?
             </p>
             <h2>
              2: Facebook
             </h2>
             <p>
              Imagine an update like, I am sitting here listening to our blowhard manager give a talk that could fell a tree.
             </p>
             <h2>
              3: Google Play
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" height="328" src="http://i.techrepublic.com.com/gallery/6364411-620-547.jpg" title="Google Play" width="372"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Buying apps on company time? And potentially with company money? Hard stop.
             </p>
             <h2>
              4: Dropbox
             </h2>
             <p>
              Worst case: Employee walks off with your company data and Dropbox is installed on four of her computers. Nuff said.
             </p>
             <h2>
              5: Skype
             </h2>
             <p>
              Now this is what you want. Folks texting and calling relatives all over the world. It puts a whole new spin on the idea of personal calls at work, doesnt it?
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.techrepublic.com/blog/10things/the-top-10-apps-being-blacklisted-in-the-enterprise/3228?tag=main" target="_self">
               MORE HERE ....
              </a>

                By
                <a href="/search?q=gina+smith" rel="author">

                May 10, 2012, 4:41 AM PDT

              <p>
               Takeaway: IT pros react harshly to Yahoo CEO Scott Thompsons false claims of a
               <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_science" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Computer science">
                computer science
               </a>
               degree, adding fuel to investor Daniel Loebs pressure for a Yahoo board CEO ouster.
              </p>
              <p>
               As Yahoo investor Dan Loeb continues to urge Yahoos YHOO board of directors to fire new CEO Scott Thompson - Loeb on Wednesday sent yet another letter to the press and the beleaguered companys board - IT pros we contacted reacted pretty vehemently to what has emerged as Thompsons falsified claims of holding a degree in computer science from
               <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.stonehill.edu" rel="homepage" target="_blank" title="Stonehill College">
                Stonehill College
               </a>
               in Easton, MA.
              </p>
              <p>
               Yahoo execs called the computer science degree claim an inadvertent error in a
               <a href="http://www.mercurynews.com/business/ci_20583023/investor-who-revealed-scott-thompsons-false-degree-claim?source=rss" target="_blank">
                San Jose Mercury News
               </a>
               interview.
              </p>
              <p>
               Loeb, according to records, runs a hedge fund that owns outright nearly 5.9 percent of Yahoos stock. In his latest letter, Loeb wrote that Thompsons fantasy degree was in no way an inadvertent error. The evidence shows us he had been using false credentials for years. Loeb and his fellows are demanding an immediate ouster.
              </p>
              <p>
               IT pros I contacted reacted hotly on the issue.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/116778611088000767597/about" target="_blank">
                John Livingston
               </a>
               , an IT pro for the American Red Cross in Savannah, Georgia, wondered if someone is willing to lie on (his) resume, what else would (he) be willing to lie about?
              </p>
              <p>
               Such prevarication just makes it harder for people looking for IT jobs, Livingston added, saying that though anyone can easily lie about extra degrees and certifications.
              </p>
              <p>
               Tech pro
               <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/104717270922252773157/posts" target="_blank">
                Paul Miller
               </a>
               agreed that ethics is the bigger issue here. Nonetheless, he said, The irony of Silicon Valley (venture capitalists) and (hedge) fund managers asserting moral outrage is patently absurd. The giant elephant in the room is that this was perpetuated for years and  no one noticed until some guy with an agenda began to dig up dirt. The fact that someone can claim a degree (he or she) does not have - and that there is little way to tell them from the (rest) of herd who has earned a degree, without researching an educational background, tells how little these degrees mean.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/105025452481231203230/about" target="_blank">
                Andrew Terry
               </a>
               , an IT consultant in the London area, also sided with Thompsons detractors. He pointed out that theres a big difference between what most of us think of as resume padding (versus) adding qualifications (one) doesnt have.
              </p>
              <p>
               If I found out someone lied on the resume about something as important as a degree, (the employee) would be fired. I personally worked my ass off for my computer science degree, said
               <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/103844700626502953893/about" target="_blank">
                Peter Schmidt
               </a>
               , a CTO at a Boston-based aviation firm. Thompsons bald-faced lying angers me.
              </p>
              <p>
               Other IT pros were a little easier on Thompson, though all 12 tech pros this reporter interviewed expressed distress at the prevarication.
              </p>
              <p>
               Isnt a degree really just a glorified piece of paper? Yes and no, IT pros said. I dont have a degree, said
               <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/104284294038834749792/about" target="_blank">
                Dan Phillips
               </a>
               , who is in tech support and network security in Ontario, Canada. What I do have is nearly two decades of real world IT experience and that gets me in the door every time.
              </p>
              <p>
               Not to knock all computer science students, but I wont hire anyone straight out of school because they just wont have the knowledge thats needed for some high end IT jobs, Phillips said. Certifications are nice but you tend to have to

                MORE HERE ...
               </a>
              </p>

            Anna's Hummingbird (I identified the shiny whizzy bird in my lavender! YAY)
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             <a href="http://sfpsociety.org/SFbirds.html" target="_self">
              Thanks to this site
             </a>
             -- I finally identified the name and type of hummingbird in my Northern California coastal backyard. Constantly in the lavender. Well, turns out it's
             <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anna%27s_Hummingbird" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Anna's Hummingbird">
              Anna's Hummingbird
             </a>
             Calypte ...
            </p>
            <p>
             Here are the high points! From the site:
            </p>
            <p>
             Family: Trochilidae, Hummingbirds view all from this family
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Description ADULT MALE Hood usually appears uniformly dark (almost black in harsh light), but when light catches it a metallic rose-red iridescence is revealed. ADULT FEMALE Has metallic greenish yellow upperparts. Underparts grayish white with yellowish green feathering on flanks and a few dark spots (iridescent in the right light) on throat. Tail has striking white tips on outer three feathers and a dark subterminal band. IMMATURE Similar to adult female; immature males gradually acquire adult's head coloration.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Dimensions Length: 3 1/2-4" (9-10 cm)
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Habitat Common and widespread within its restricted west coast range. Resident year-round in many parts, especially in coastal districts, but often found at higher altitudes, and further inland, during breeding season than in winter. Favors deserts, scrub-covered slopes, urban parks, and gardens.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Observation Tips Easiest to observe at feeders throughout the year, and in city parks and ornamental gardens in winter.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Range Western Canada, Northwest, Alaska, California, Texas, Southwest
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Voice Utters a sharp tchik call.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Discussion Males are amazingly colorful when seen in the right light and are pugnacious when defending territory against rivals. Sexes are strikingly dissimilar.
             <br/>
             <br/>
            </p>

            Rexxfield Founder Michael Roberts: On Defamation (civilination.org)
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
           <div class="entry-body">
            <div>
             <div>
              <h1>
               Rexxfield Founder Michael Roberts Shares His Personal Story of
               <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defamation" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Defamation">
                Defamation
               </a>
               and Explains How His Company
               <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helping_behavior" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Helping behavior">
                Helps
               </a>
               Other Victims Regain Their Good Name
              </h1>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div>
             <div>
              <div>
               Posted on
               <a href="http://www.civilination.org/blog/rexxfield-founder-michael-roberts-shares-his-personal-story-of-defamation-and-explains-how-his-company-helps-other-victims-regain-their-good-name/" rel="bookmark" title="3:24 am">
                April 10, 2012
               </a>
               by
               <a href="http://www.civilination.org/blog/author/dre/" title="View all posts by Andrea Weckerle">
                Andrea Weckerle
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              <a href="http://civilination.org/blog/rexxfield-founder-michael-roberts-shares-his-personal-story-of-defamation-and-explains-how-his-company-helps-other-victims-regain-their-good-name/picture-1-18/" rel="attachment wp-att-3955">
               <img alt="" height="300" src="http://civilination.org/components/com_wordpress/wp/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/Picture-11-199x300.png" title="Picture 1" width="199"/>
              </a>
              Michael Roberts is the Founder of
              <a href="http://www.rexxfield.com/">
               Rexxfield
              </a>
              , a company that assists and supports individuals who have been the victims of online lies, defamation, and privacy invasion by rendering all reasonable assistance in order to have deceptive materials retracted or hidden from the public domain and the victims good name and reputation restored.
             </p>
             <p>
              He understands first-hand what being a victim feels like, having been on the receiving end of character assassination and defamatory attacks by his ex-wife, who is now in prison for a murder conviction, and others associated with her case or seeking to capitalize upon it.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               CiviliNation:
              </strong>
              Tell us about your company Rexxfield and what let to its creation.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Michael Roberts:
              </strong>
              To understand why Rexxfield was created, you first need to understand what happened in my personal life, which is a long, convoluted and incredibly complicated story.
             </p>
             <p>
              In December 2000, my now ex-wife Tracey Richter murdered 20-year-old Dustin Wehde in what was described by one prosecutor as an
              <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Execution-style_murder" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Execution-style murder">
               execution-style killing
              </a>
              . She claimed self-defense, and despite contradictions in her testimony and evidence to the contrary, I believed her  perhaps because at the time I couldnt emotionally fathom the fact that she actually murdered someone in cold blood. Then in early 2004, after I discovered her affair with another man, she attempted to kill me, first by drugging me and then, when I was semiconscious, by suffocating me. By the grace of God I survived the ordeal but to this day Im still struggling from the injuries sustained from the attempt on my life. Thankfully, the Iowa
              <a class="zem_slink" href="http://maps.google.com/maps?ll=38.89325,-77.0249722222&amp;spn=0.01,0.01&amp;q=38.89325,-77.0249722222 (United%20States%20Department%20of%20Justice)&amp;t=h" rel="geolocation" target="_blank" title="United States Department of Justice">
               Department of Justice
              </a>
              , crime victims compensation fund paid for my associated medical expenses.
             </p>
             <p>
              I finally wised up, saw her for the person she really is, and filed for divorce. But notwithstanding the evidence that she had killed one man and attempted to murder another, the Family Court judge in the subsequent divorce case gave primary care of our children to my ex-wife time and time again. This just goes to show that domestic relations courts are seriously flawed and that some judges make highly questionable decisions that have serious and negative repercussions on innocent lives.
             </p>
             <p>
              Finally, in the summer of 2011, 10 years after killing Dustin Wehde, Tracey was finally arrested and charged with first-degree murder. She was convicted unanimously by a jury of 12 on 7 November and
              <a href="www.fathersfight.org">
               is now in prison serving a life sentence without the possibility of parole
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              Fortunately, after her incarceration, I was able to obtain primary custody of our children.
             </p>
             <p>
              What does all of this have to do with the creation of Rexxfield? Everything, I would say.
             </p>
             <p>
              Right after I filed for divorce in 2004, my ex-wife began a relentless Internet smear campaign against me, my business, and any individual that offered any kind of support to me, whether financial, emotional, vocational, or otherwise.
             </p>
             <p>
              My business at the time was
              <a href="http://www.mile2.com/">
               mile2
              </a>
              , and the relentless attacks on my reputation and, by association, my business, brought mile2, which designed, developed and delivered information security training and information assurance services, to its knees. I was forced to sell for a fraction of what it wouldve otherwise been worth had it not been for these attacks. Even then the flaming aspersions continued until July 30, 2011, the day Tracy was arrested on first-degree murder charges.
             </p>
             <p>
              She is actually now a 3 times convicted felon; in 2009 she also received felony convictions for both perjury and fraud by trials in Iowa and Nebraska, respectively.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.rexxfield.com/">
               Rexxfield
              </a>
              was founded in 2008 in response to understanding what character assassination and unfounded attacks on businesses can do. I had experienced first-hand the devastation that these types of attacks can cause and that, in most cases, neither law enforcement nor the civil authorities and judiciary can relate to this issue.
             </p>
             <p>
              Instead, what Ive frequently found is that the very entities that are supposed to help people who have been wronged seem to take the position that sticks and stones will break your bones, but names will never hurt you. In my case, I had to fully prove that all of these anonymous attacks in fact originated from my ex-wife before anyone would even listen to me. So, in the process of having to convince the legal system to take my situation seriously, I developed some proprietary techniques, technologies, and methods for getting behind the cloak of anonymity in ways that ultimately helped inexorably link Tracey to the poison-penned attacks.
             </p>
             <p>
              Unfortunately my breakthroughs were too little and too late to prevent the damage to my own name and business, but as I started blogging about my experiences I found myself being inundated with desperate cries for help. These requests ranged from tearful calls from parents of cyber bulling victims to the
              <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Chief executive officer">
               CEOs
              </a>
              of multi-billion dollar companies. Realizing the desperate need for these types of services, Rexxfield was born..
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               CiviliNation:
              </strong>
              What kinds of people typically seek Rexxfields services?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Michael Roberts:
              </strong>
              Calls for help are incredibly diverse. Some are relatively simple and yet emotionally devastating because they come from teenaged cyber bullying victims, and others originate from from powerful people in multinational corporations. We also receive many calls from frustrated law-enforcement officials who are trying to investigate serious crimes. Ive helped law enforcement investigate in cases of rape, robberies and even death threats against police officers who are living in fear despite their station in life. In all of these situations, without exception, the
              <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_service_provider" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Internet service provider">
               Internet service providers
              </a>
              were not willing to reasonably cooperate with the investigations - I have found Google and
              <a class="zem_slink" href="http://facebook.com/" rel="homepage" target="_blank" title="Facebook">
               Facebook
              </a>
              to be quite notorious in this area.
             </p>
             <p>
              I was recently invited to teach some of my methods to members of a joint federal and state task force whose mandate is protecting children from exploitation. I showed them some of the techniques we have developed and how the task force can implement those techniques into their own investigations. The group then shared some horror stories with me, including a case involving Facebook where a teenage rape victim was involved in a chat through Facebook less than half-an-hour before the crime took place. In that case it took months before Facebook complied with the requests for the originating
              <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_address" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="IP address">
               IP address
              </a>
              , by which time the evidence had perished because the ISP providing the connectivity to the suspect had subsequently purged the log file records. And to think the loss of this information could have been easily avoided with the proper cooperation.
             </p>
             <p>
              In addition to helping private individuals and businesses, I am also assisting members of State House of Representatives in drafting new laws that will force Internet service providers to retain this perishable evidence for at least 2 years, because currently there are no laws addressing this obvious need. Obviously this needs to be balanced with peoples desire for privacy, so I want to make clear that through these proposed laws we are not asking to give Big Brother the keys to everybodys Internet activities; on the contrary, we want the Internet service providers to remain the sole custodian of these confidential records and to only provide information in instances of legitimate civil and criminal warrants or subpoenas. In other words, we simply want to help avoid what happened in the rape case I described above.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               CiviliNation:
              </strong>
              How have the attacks against you helped positively influence your work at Rexxfield?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Michael Roberts:
              </strong>
              Had I not gone through this fiery trial, I wouldve been like so many other people and considered the issue of online attacks and character assassination a mere trifle not worthy of serious attention. I would probably have also dismissed the victims who issue anguished cries for help as thin-skinned weaklings, as seems to be the reaction by most people who have not experienced this tragedy firsthand and simply refuse to see what is happening online.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               CiviliNation:
              </strong>
              What is your response to people who claim that online reputation attacks against adults are rare and not something that most people need to worry about?
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Michael Roberts:
              </strong>
              I would encourage such people to take a course in critical thinking, sympathy, empathy, and to open their eyes. Ive found that until somebody has experienced this issue first-hand, or even second-hand through someone they love, they simply cannot relate to the devastation it causes.
             </p>
             <p>
              Anonymous free speech is a wonderful privilege and should be preserved. In many cases horrible problems have been avoided by the ability to communicate anonymously, such as in situations involving whistle blowing of white-collar crimes, community awareness of when sexual predators move into the neighborhood, and many other alerts that are of great community benefit.
             </p>
            </div>
