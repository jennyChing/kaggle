&lt;p&gt;Like most kids growing up in the late 60's early 70's,  I spent my fair share of time reading comic books.  I can't remember a lot about what was in them, but I do remember several of the ads.  Of course, there was the classic Charles Atlas ad where the beach bully kicked sand on the skinny boy and his girlfriend.&lt;br /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;But the ad that I remember the best was the "&lt;strong&gt;Magnificent Marvelous Money Machine&lt;/strong&gt;".  It was a wooden block with some rollers in which you would put a one dollar bill in one side and a five dollar bill would come out the other.  Being very young and naive, my mom had a tough time convincing me that it was just a trick and something like that didn't really exist.&lt;br /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;Sorry mom, but you were wrong on this one.
