         <div class="arl-entry" id="articles374">
          <div class="votes_small" id="bx_articlessmall374">
           <div class="votes_gray_small" style="width:55px;">
            <div class="votes_active_small" id="bx_articlessmall374Slider" style="width:0px;">

          <div class="arl-info">
           <div class="arl-caption">
            <a class="arl-caption" href="http://socialpolitan.org/fiction-writing-craft/m/articles/view/How-to-Revise-a-Novel-Rules-for-the-Rewrite">
             How to Revise a Novel  Rules for the Rewrite
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="arl-date">
            16.07.2012 09:54
                    	Comments: 0
                    	Categories:
            <a href="http://socialpolitan.org/fiction-writing-craft/m/articles/category/Fiction Editing">
             Fiction Editing
            </a>
            Tags:
            <a href="http://socialpolitan.org/fiction-writing-craft/m/articles/tag/jim sellers">
             jim sellers
            </a>
            <a href="http://socialpolitan.org/fiction-writing-craft/m/articles/tag/how to">
             how to
            </a>
            <a href="http://socialpolitan.org/fiction-writing-craft/m/articles/tag/revise">
             revise
            </a>
            <a href="http://socialpolitan.org/fiction-writing-craft/m/articles/tag/novel">
             novel
            </a>
            <a href="http://socialpolitan.org/fiction-writing-craft/m/articles/tag/rewrite">
             rewrite
            </a>
            <a href="http://socialpolitan.org/fiction-writing-craft/m/articles/tag/editing">
             editing
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="arl-text">
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              Every writer knows that their story has to go through its edits and  revisions, just as a parent knows that their perfect child will become  an adult only after he has struggled through the torturous and  horrifying scenario of adolescence. With that surrender to the certainty  of your fate as a writer, I have collected some of the fundamental  guides uncovered through my exhaustive research into
              <strong>
               <a type="amzn">
                how to revise a novel
               </a>
              </strong>
              . Here they are in no specific order:
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               A writer or an editor be,
              </strong>
              never  the twain should work in your head at the same time. The writer is a  positive, sensitive, creative sort who tells stories about people  (fictional or non) who he loves. An editor is a critical, heartless and  unsympathetic person who finds flaws. The two cannot work together.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Your idea and story are just right 
              </strong>
              now change  them. Love your story at the concept stage; love it while youre writing  the first draft. Then rewrite with no attachment or emotional  commitment. If you cant clear your mind and look at the manuscript with  fresh eyes, put it away.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Rewrite the entire manuscript
              </strong>
              . After reading, making  notes and suffering through feedback and critique, avoid the temptation  of merely editing the text on your computer. The typical error of  finding words, either out of place in a sentence or missed, is a  phenomenon of the word processor. This error will be less likely to  happen if you are rewriting from scratch. This also forces you to read  and hear every line as opposed to the skimming we tend to do over the  text when we already know it.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Edit backwards
              </strong>
              . Read and edit the text of a  manuscript paragraph by paragraph, or bit by bit from the end of the  story to the beginning. This avoids getting caught up in the story and  helps you focus on the text.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Understand the critique.
              </strong>
              I received some scathing  comments from critics about my story, which hurt because I had built up a  following and had many supporting comments. I did not want to place one  critics opinion over the praise of many but I wanted to understand  what he didnt like about the story, so I asked. As it turns out he was  angry at something else and my story was how he vented it. He apologised  and offered me some constructive comments, which I took to heart.  Ultimately, I benefited from something I was initially going to blow off  and resent.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Dont be afraid to start further back.
              </strong>
              If your story  needs a major rewrite, go back to the outline and work forward from  there. You have the convenience of knowing what is happening in the  story so your characters and plot are less likely to change on you.
             </span>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Nothing is sacred
              </strong>
              . Your novel/story is made of people,  cities, cars, planes, buildings, money, emotion, pain, the Earth, the  Universe, other worlds. Yet it is all clay, everything is changeable to  better suit the story. Dont be afraid to alter anything unless it is an  historical fact (event, date, person) or it is the central element of  the story.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Dont do donts
              </strong>
              . Watch for anything that is  dishonest or borrowed, either from clich or from popular culture unless  you want to pin the dialogue to a certain era. Dont avoid contact or  conflict; make the argument and friction real, no euphemisms. Dont lie,  do your research. If you are telling a story about university  professors or taxi drivers, find out what they do and how they talk and  dont pretend to know anything unless you have checked it out. Fiction  is only enjoyed when it is believable. Dont be redundant: illuminating  lights under the dark, night sky next to the noisy loudspeakers and the  wet water.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Search for weak words.
              </strong>
              This can be rough. After you  have finished writing the draft do a Control F or Command F for Mac  users, and type in had. Notice how many times the word shows up. If  not had, then would or just ly. Get rid of as many as possible.  Every passive word or adverb you use makes the whole thing weak.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              <strong>
               Watch for inconsistencies of tense and Point of View.
              </strong>
              One  thing I noticed is how hard it is to do a third person limited POV from  a childs viewpoint. The narrator cant be too smart but cant talk  like a child either. This is why most stories involving child  protagonists tend to be narrated by an adult speaking from memory.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              This list can go on but this should help. I would add two things to this list:
             </span>
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
               <strong>
                Revision
               </strong>
               is a key part of writing your story where you can discover or rediscover the depth and quality of your characters.
              </span>
             </li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
             <li>
              <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
               The first revision is never the last.
              </span>
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: small;">
              If you found this article on how to revise a novel interesting or helpful, please share it with your social media friends!
             </span>
            </p>
