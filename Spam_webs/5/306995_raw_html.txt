           Five Documentaries You NEED To Watch On Netflix As Of 11/13/13
          </h2>

          <p>
           <img alt="de" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5881" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/de.jpg?resize=480%2C360"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Documentaries offer you something very few fictional films can. You walk away from them smarter than you were when you showed up. That is how documentaries work. They often find a single subject, and focus their energy on informing those who dont seem to know enough about it. While it is oft misunderstood that horror films are my favorite, they are a close second, but documentaries take the cake for me. If you went through my Netflix account right now, you would see that I have given five-star ratings to documentaries far more than any genre of film on there. Mainly because I often walk away from documentaries slightly changed. I will now share with you some documentaries that may make you feel the same way, and you can watch any of them right now. Presenting: five documentaries you NEED to watch on
           <a href="http://movies.netflix.com/WiHome">
            Netflix,
           </a>
           right now, as of November 13, 2013.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-5875">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            A BAND CALLED DEATH
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="band-called-death" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5876" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/band-called-death.jpg?resize=520%2C293"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Did you know punk was actually and unofficially started in 1974 by a three young black guys out of Detroit who formed a band named Death? I know you didnt. The whole point of this staggering documentary is that NO ONE knew. Well, one person. His name was David Hackney, and he formed the band.
          </p>
          <p>
           Not only did this open my eyes to how we stereotype cultures based on music, but it introduced me to a band whose music I have had a slight obsession with ever since. From the spastic chord progressions to the insane timing changes, Death was too ahead of their time, and this film proves that it took us almost forty years to catch up.
          </p>
          <p>
           Also, try not to get chills when you hear how David knew people would come looking for the music, so he stored it in his brothers attic and made him promise to release it when the time comes. Well, the time comes DURING the documentary, and it is a stunning thing to witness. Dont even get me started on the sons band and the musical legacy. Like I said, if you are a music fan like me, this movie will give you chills.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            FOR ALL MANKIND
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="mankind" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5877" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/mankind.jpg?resize=520%2C292"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I will give you this disclaimer right now. You dont have to like wrestling to be blown away by this documentary. I will admit that I am not, and never really have been, a fan. You know what I am a fan of, though? Ambition and passion, and you know who has that, maybe more than most people on this Earth? Former wrestler, best-selling author, and philanthropist, Mick Foley. This guy has so much passion, it almost killed him. Many times over.
          </p>
          <p>
           Yet NOTHING ever stopped him.
          </p>
          <p>
           That is the point of this film. You see him come up as a no one, fighting his way through wrestling circuits in smaller towns. He slowly got renowned for being able to take a beating, and it seemed he was always pushing that further and further, Yet still, nothing ever stopped him.
          </p>
          <p>
           He knew what he wanted to do, what he needed to do it, and he stopped at nothing to realize all of his dreams. Hell, even the fact that once he had become a superstar, they tried to get him a ghost writer for his first few books, and he refused, writing them all himself and ending up on the best sellers list, many times over is enough to awe most people. This is THE film you watch if you want to be inspired. Be forewarned, though. This dude did some GNARLY shit, and it gets quite bloody at times.
          </p>
          <p>
           Oh, and he gets an ear ripped off and they show it.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            EXIT THROUGH THE GIFT SHOP
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="bandksyd" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5878" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/bandksyd.jpg?resize=520%2C273"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Also known as that movie about Banksy. But that, my friends, is the beauty of this film. It is NOT about Banksy at all. No, instead, it is about how gullible we all are as bottom feeder art fans. It is about how the hipster scene has ruined all the credibility that once belonged to all subcultures. It is about being manipulated, played with, and playing along with it without even knowing.
          </p>
          <p>
           Yes, Banksy is all running the whole show here, but in typical Banksy fashion, this shit is a mindfuck. You THINK this is a movie about a dude who wants to follow Banksy, but then you find out that same guy now wants to make art, and rips off Banksy, but Banksy kind of helps, while pointing out the irony of the whole situation, constantly.
          </p>
          <p>
           Thing is, what if I told you the WHOLE thing was a setup? That Mr. Brainwash was just a further extension of the Banksy idea? What if I told you this film was laughing at you, right to your face, and you didnt even know it?
          </p>
          <p>
           I will admit, this film is SO meta, it took me a long time to get it, but once I did, I realized it was one of the most amazing things ever committed to film. Go watch it, see for yourself. We are all sheep, but most of us dont even know it.
          </p>
          <p>
           Bahhhhh.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            THE IMPOSTER
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="imposter" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-5879" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/imposter.jpg?resize=520%2C292"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Not only one of the best documentaries ever made, but I would go so far as to say one of the most compelling films, EVER MADE. Maybe not the film itself, but the story they tell in the film. This is the kind of movie that has so many twists and turns, you will find yourself sitting there, on more than one occasion, with your mouth hanging open. I know I have said that before about The Imposter, and I stand behind it.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Imposter is about a missing boy who shows back up after years, and his family openly embraces him, even though he is OBVIOUSLY a different person. Yes, seriously, and by the end of the film, you really cannot tell who is the more evil, devious one. The lying man who pretends to be a missing kid, or this strange family who are obviously trying to hide something by embracing him.
          </p>
          <p>
           Man, I get chills just TALKING about this movie.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            THIS FILM IS NOT YET RATED
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="fol" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-5880" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fol.jpg?resize=520%2C770"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Everything in Hollywood is corrupt. Everything. There is no single, shadowed corner that doesnt have some nefarious hand reaching into it. This Film Is Not Yet Rated is a movie about the rating system used in U.S cinema. The movie wanted to know WHO chooses ratings for movies, and WHO chooses those people who choose. The thing is, you would think it a simple question with simple answers, but such is not the case.
          </p>
          <p>
           Just like anything in Hollywoodland, it is all politics, the people with the money hold the power, and if the ratings board doesnt like you or your film, despite the content, you could be fucked. Yes, I am using that word on purpose.
          </p>
          <p>
           Also note, they address how insane levels of violence seem okay, but a breast or a same-sex kiss can ruin a movies chances. Very eye-opening. Ironically enough, a movie the ratings board did everything it could to try to get blocked from release.
          </p>
          <p>
           In an awesome bid of irony, they even rated the film NR. How very meta.
          </p>
          <p>
           Anyway, go peep those, and feel free to take to the comments and let me know some documentaries on Netflix right now that you love. Who knows, I could end up writing about a movie you suggest. If that happens, you get a shout-out, which is one notch up from a dry handjob.
          </p>

            <p>
             The Cove
             <br/>
             Im not sure if this is still on Netflix, but if it is, its a must-see.
             <br/>
             A group of activists go to Taijii, Japan, where they use high-tech technology to uncover the secrets of the Japanese dolphin killings. Its extremely eye-opening and sad, but its hard not to look away. Get the tissues ready and learn something new.
            </p>
            <p>
             The American Scream
             <br/>
             As an avid horror fan, and someone who visits Halloween attractions and haunted houses every year, I had to watch this one. Its about three families who live in the same town who, every year, build elaborate haunted houses on their properties. The amount of dedication, attention to detail, and time spent working on their projects is inspiring to say the least. This is the classic tale of Americans chasing their dreams, and for some, that dream comes true.
