        <h1 class="title">
         <a href="http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2014/08/dev_hynes_saman_2.html">
          Dev Hynes &amp; Samantha Urbani claim they were assaulted by security after Blood Orange's Lollapalooza performance
         </a>
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="body">
        <p>
         <em>
          Urbani and Hynes during Blood Orange Lollapalooza set (photo by @
          <a href="http://instagram.com/p/rL0jJuAytt/">

         <strong>
          Dev Hynes
         </strong>
         and
         <strong>
          Samantha Urbani
         </strong>
         are claiming the were assaulted by security guards at
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2014/08/lollapalooza_20_23.html">
           Lollapalooza
          </a>
         </strong>
         following their
         <strong>
          Blood Orange
         </strong>
         performance at the festival on Friday (8/1). Via Hynes' Twitter feed last night:
        </p>
        <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
         <p>
          Samantha and I just got assaulted by the security. We are about to press charges
         </p>
          Devont Hynes (@devhynes)
         <a href="https://twitter.com/devhynes/statuses/495388014236147712">
          August 2, 2014
         </a>
        </blockquote>
        <br/>
        <script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js">
        </script>
        <br/>
        <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
         <p>
          They grabbed her I asked what they were doing and they grabbed my neck and threw me to the ground, then two others joined in on me
         </p>
          Devont Hynes (@devhynes)
         <a href="https://twitter.com/devhynes/statuses/495388198856839169">
          August 2, 2014
         </a>
        </blockquote>

        Both Hynes and Urbani were wearing homemade "Stop Police Brutality" t-shirts, and his bore the names of victims Eric Garner, Jordan Davis, and Trayvon Martin. Garner died July 17 after being put in a chokehold by a New York police officer and his death was officially
        <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/08/01/eric-garner-homicide_n_5642481.html">
         ruled a homicide
        </a>
        on Friday.
        <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
         <p>
          I gave a speech on racism and police brutality and then I am jumped by three security guards and my gf is assaulted.
          <a href="https://twitter.com/lollapalooza">
           @lollapalooza
          </a>
         </p>
          Devont Hynes (@devhynes)
         <a href="https://twitter.com/devhynes/statuses/495390892661174273">
          August 2, 2014
         </a>

         </p>
          samantha urbani (@SamandudeBITCH)
         <a href="https://twitter.com/SamandudeBITCH/statuses/495403450763857923">
          August 2, 2014
         </a>
        </blockquote>

        <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
         <p>
          Thanks 4support guys, rly. We're shaken upset sad etc but ok. Just want 2keep working against violence &amp;abuse of power. Speak up &amp;take care.
         </p>
          samantha urbani (@SamandudeBITCH)
         <a href="https://twitter.com/SamandudeBITCH/statuses/495411740461780992">
          August 2, 2014

        Lollapalooza has now issued a statement:
        <blockquote>
         Late Friday night, we learned of an incident involving an artist and a security guard on site. Since then, we have been in contact with those involved and the authorities, as we work together to resolve the situation. As always, our top priority is to ensure the safety of everyone at the festival.
        </blockquote>
