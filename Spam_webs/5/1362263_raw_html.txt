        <h2 class="sectiontitle">
         2 Cops Gunned Down
        </h2>
        <div class="imgright" style="width: 184px;">
         <div class="imgmain">
          <a href="/img/iw2jwrzxt-3igu4k/NYPD-Cops">
           <img alt="NYPD Cops" height="180" src="http://img.wikinut.com/img/iw2jwrzxt-3igu4k/jpeg/180x300/NYPD-Cops.jpeg" title="NYPD Cops" width="180"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="imgtitle">
          <a href="/img/iw2jwrzxt-3igu4k/NYPD-Cops">
           NYPD Cops
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         December 20, 2014, two NYPD officers named Wen Jian Liu and Rafael Ramos were shot and killed as they sat in their patrol car in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn. When I first saw the breaking news of this tragic incident flashed on my TV, I instantly thought about the murder of 22-year-old rookie police officer Edward "Eddie" R. Byrne back in 1988. Four drug dealers from Queens murdered him while he sat in his marked patrol car on 107th Avenue and Inwood Street in South Jamaica, Queens. The murder of Officer Byrne prompted nationwide outrage. People and law enforcements got tired of the senseless violence in the streets and it led to a concentrated crackdown on drug dealers and kingpins during the crack cocaine era of the 1980s and early Nineties.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         I send my sincere condolences to the families of the two murdered police officers. Their murders had me shocked. Senseless violence against police officers is wrong. Their lives matters too. It does not help improve relationships between community and cops. An eye for an eye leaves the world blind!
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Image credit
         <br/>
         <i>
          www.jpupdates.com
         </i>
        </p>
        <a id="section-1" name="The-Shooter">
        </a>
        <h2 class="sectiontitle">
         The Shooter
        </h2>
        <div class="imgright" style="width: 184px;">
         <div class="imgmain">
          <a href="/img/w1jer_zhbxcrvgxo/Ismaaiyl-Brinsley">
           <img alt="Ismaaiyl Brinsley " height="180" src="http://img.wikinut.com/img/w1jer_zhbxcrvgxo/jpeg/180x300/Ismaaiyl-Brinsley.jpeg" title="Ismaaiyl Brinsley " width="180"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="imgtitle">
          <a href="/img/w1jer_zhbxcrvgxo/Ismaaiyl-Brinsley">
           Ismaaiyl Brinsley
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         The gunman was 28-year-old Ismaaiyl Brinsley. He had a criminal past in Georgia and Ohio, and expressed his anger at law enforcements and government on his Instagram account. Ismaaiyl traveled to New York City after shooting his ex-girlfriend, Shaneka Thompson in Owings Mills, Maryland. Then he executed Officers Liu and Ramos in retaliation for the deaths of Michael Brown and Eric Garner. He allegedly ran to a nearby subway station where he committed suicide.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         I feel Ismaaiyl Brinsley acted in cowardice and used the deaths of Michael Brown and Eric Garner as an excuse for his slayings. Killing cops are not going to bring Michael Brown and Eric Garner back to life. It is only going to unleash more problems between community and cops. We should put more emphasis on repairing the divide between community and cops.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Image credit
         <br/>
         <i>
          www.theglobaldispatch.com
         </i>
        </p>
        <a id="section-2" name="We-need-Compassion">
        </a>
        <h2 class="sectiontitle">
         We need Compassion
        </h2>
        <p>
         I conclude this article with a quote from award winning TV host Scott Nevins. He addressed the assassination of the two NYPD police officers on his Facebook:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Two cops shot dead in Brooklyn by an out of town gang member who drove to NYC with the purpose of killing Police officers. This is disgusting and horrific, but please stop trying to blame this on Mayor Bill de Blasio, Al Sharpton, President OBAMA or the peaceful protestors who had NOTHING to do with the incident. Both sides are acting like extremists now and it's sickening. Right now, what we need is compassion for the families of the fallen police officers and peace, not useless blabbering and finger pointing.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Sources
         <br/>
         amNewYork
         <br/>
         Daily News
        </p>
