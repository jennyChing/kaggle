        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-11-29T09:58:37+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          November 29, 2013
         </time>
         By:
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
           Tamara Kelly
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.mooglyblog.com/glittery-crochet-stars-in-2-rounds/#comments">
           40 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pf-content">
         <p>
          Disclaimer: This post includes affiliate links.
         </p>
         <p>
          This weeks free crochet pattern is perfect for the weekend after Thanksgiving  easy and relaxing, perfect for whipping up between turkey leftovers and naps. And theyre so fast and fun (only 2 rounds!) youll want to keep making them right on through the holidays! Introducing the free crochet pattern for Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Free #Crochet Pattern and Photo Tutorial. These take less than 10 yds of yarn!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10037" height="600" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Glittery-Crochet-Stars-All.jpg" width="430"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-10030">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Lion Brands Martha Stewart Crafts Glitter Ribbon inspired this pattern initially. I love it because it lets you make glittery, sparkle filled projects without covering yourself and your house and your kids and your dog etc in actual glitter (you know what Im talkin about)!
         </p>
         <p>
          But after Id run though a couple skeins of it I decided to see how the star pattern would look in a finer yarn. One skein of Vannas Glamour and I couldnt decide which I like more  the big chunky stars with their bold sparkle, or the little delicate ones with a more subtle glitter effect. Another great option would be Lion Brand Bon Bons  the holiday pack is perfect, and would make a ton of crochet stars in a whole bunch of colors! Either way, your trees, gifts, and garlands will be loaded with twinkle! (And how cute would the little ones be, appliqued on your little ones holiday outfit?!)
         </p>
         <p>
          So as soon as youve picked your yarn, youll need to choose which version of the Glittery Crochet Star pattern to make first  5 point or 6 point! (Personally I like to switch back and forth, but its okay to have a favorite!)
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Free #Crochet Pattern and Photo Tutorial. These take less than 10 yds of yarn!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10038" height="600" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Glittery-Crochet-Stars-Closeup.jpg" width="431"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 40px;">
           <span style="font-family: clicker script;">
            Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/glittery-crochet-stars-in-2-rounds" target="_blank">
           Add this pattern to your Ravelry Queue and Favorites
          </a>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           8  9 yds of any weight yarn of your choice (
           <a href="http://amzn.to/IqGgRn" target="_blank">
            Martha Stewart Crafts Glitter Ribbon
           </a>
           in Sterling and Yellow Gold shown and
           <a href="http://amzn.to/IqGtEa" target="_blank">
            Vannas Glamour
           </a>
           in Platinum shown; photo tutorial features Martha Stewart Crafts Glitter Ribbon in Blue Sapphire)
          </li>
          <li>
           The hook recommended on the label of the chosen yarn (for the Martha Stewart Glittern Ribbon a US-M/N, 9.0 mm; for the Vannas Glamour a US-G, 4.0/4.25 mm)
          </li>
          <li>
           Yarn needle for weaving in ends
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="Symbol Key for the Glittery Crochet Stars on Moogly" class="size-full wp-image-10105 aligncenter" height="350" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Star-Chart-Key-finished.jpg" width="294"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5 Point Glittery Crochet Star
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Round 1: Starting with a
          <a href="http://www.mooglyblog.com/the-magic-circle/" target="_blank" title="The Magic Circle">
           magic circle
          </a>
          , ch 2 (does not count as a st) and dc 10 into the ring. Join to 1st dc with a sl st. (10 dc)
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Free #Crochet Pattern and Photo Tutorial. These take less than 10 yds of yarn!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10039" height="350" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Glittery-Crochet-Stars-Round-1.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Round 2: *Ch 2, dc in the next st. Ch 3, work 2 sc around the post of the dc st. Sl st in the next st. Repeat from * 4 times, finishing the last repeat with a sl st in the 1st st of the first Round. Break yarn and weave in ends.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Free #Crochet Pattern and Photo Tutorial. These take less than 10 yds of yarn!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10041" height="350" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Glittery-Crochet-Stars-Step-2.1.jpg" width="500"/>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Free #Crochet Pattern and Photo Tutorial. These take less than 10 yds of yarn!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10042" height="350" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Glittery-Crochet-Stars-Step-2.2.jpg" width="500"/>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Free #Crochet Pattern and Photo Tutorial. These take less than 10 yds of yarn!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10043" height="350" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Glittery-Crochet-Stars-Step-2.3.jpg" width="500"/>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Free #Crochet Pattern and Photo Tutorial. These take less than 10 yds of yarn!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10040" height="350" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Glittery-Crochet-Stars-Round-2.4.jpg" width="500"/>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Free #Crochet Pattern and Photo Tutorial. These take less than 10 yds of yarn!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10036" height="362" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Glittery-Crochet-Star-Finished.jpg" width="500"/>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds - 5 pt chart" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10103" height="500" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/5-pt-chart-finished.jpg" width="486"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           6 Point Glittery Crochet Star
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Round 1: Starting with a magic circle, ch 2 (does not count as a st) and dc 12 into the ring. Join to 1st dc with a sl st. (12 dc)
         </p>
         <p>
          Round 2: *Ch 2, dc in the next st. Ch 3, work 2 sc around the post of the dc st. Sl st in the next st. Repeat from * 5 times, finishing the last repeat with a sl st in the 1st st of the first Round. Break yarn and weave in ends.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds 6 pt chart" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10104" height="500" src="http://www.mooglyblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/6-pt-chart-finished.jpg" width="474"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Et voila! You pick the yarn, you pick the number of points, and youll make the perfect Glittery Crochet Stars in 2 Rounds! Perfect as a present topper, beautiful hanging from the tree, and little will they know that your Martha Stewart touch took only a few minutes! Happy holidays to all!
         </p>
