       <h2 class="title">
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/rosemary-and-garlic-butter-rolls">
         Rosemary and Garlic Butter Rolls
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="meta noprint">
        Filed under:
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes/appetizers" rel="category tag">
         Appetizers
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes/appetizers/bread" rel="category tag">
         bread
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes/sides/bread-sides-recipes" rel="category tag">
         bread
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes/bread-recipes" rel="category tag">
         Bread
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes" rel="category tag">
         Recipes
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/category/recipes/sides" rel="category tag">
         Sides
        </a>
         Tags:
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/butter" rel="tag">
         butter
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/dinner-rolls" rel="tag">
         dinner rolls
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/garlic-and-rosemary-butter-rolls" rel="tag">
         Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/tag/instant-yeast" rel="tag">
         instant yeast
        </a>
          Kay @ 4:22 pm
       </div>
       <div class="article">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4574105262/" title="May2-2010-01">
          <img alt="May2-2010-01" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4063/4574105262_2c09109b7e.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         My sister stopped by this weekend to drop off the Electrolux Assistant mixer she had hidden in her basement for the past 5 years. Can you believe someone owning a machine like this and not using it? Craziness.
        </p>
        <p>
         It wasnt until she caught me talking to one of our other sisters (I have 5 of em!) about the KitchenAid she had bought thatit dawned on her she too had a stand mixer lying around doing nothing particularly useful. Brand new (well, 5 years old, but never been used). With a meat grinder attachment. And she gave it to me. Oh man, did I mention I love my big sis?
        </p>
        <p>
         In the short time Ive had this mixer now, I found out that really bad things happen when I get cravings and have a powerful machine like this at my disposal. Bad things in a good way. Bad things in a double batch of rosemary and garlic butter rolls in only two days kind of way. Lord have mercy on my thighs. If this is wrong, I dont want to be right!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-7481">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ingredients:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         2 tsp instant yeast (or 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast)
         <br/>
         1 stick butter (soft)
         <br/>
         1/4 cup warm water
         <br/>
         1 cup milk
         <br/>
         2 tbsp sugar
         <br/>
         1 large egg
         <br/>
         1 tsp salt
         <br/>
         3 cups flour
         <br/>
         1 rosemary sprig
         <br/>
         2 garlic cloves
         <br/>
         coarse sea salt
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Directions:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The yeast. I opted for instant yeast, mainly because it only requires one rise. But active dry yeast works perfectly as well, just keep in mind that the dough has to rise twice then.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570471889/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4050/4570471889_4963b9a63a.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         First, mix 2/3 stick of butter with 1 cup of milk.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4571108326/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3489/4571108326_93b83837e9.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Lightly beat a large room-temperature egg.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570471751/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3460/4570471751_a1faaf6983.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Keep 3 cups of flour nearby.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570471691/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4050/4570471691_f0e78b1afd.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Technically I wouldnt have to add instant yeast to water. I could just mix it in with the flour. However, Im a beast of habit and this is what works for me. Dump 2 tsp instant yeast (or 2 + 1/4 tsp active dry yeast) in a bowl and add 1/4 cup of warm water. Not hot, just warm. Stir and let the yeast sit there for 5 minutes.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570471635/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3437/4570471635_b5cd4538f0.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Im using the stand mixer, but you can also do this by hand. Youll need stamina. And a pair of hands that dont tire easily because youll be kneading for 10 minutes straight to get the best, fluffiest dough. If you have a hand mixer with dough hooks, use it until the dough forms to a ball and then work the dough with your hands.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <br/>
         Pop the milk &amp; butter mix in the microwave for a minute at full power. It doesnt have to be hot, just luke-warm. Transfer the yeast water to your mixer bowl. Add 2 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp salt, pour in the egg and add 1 cup of flour.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4571142318/" title="May2-2010-07">
          <img alt="May2-2010-07" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4061/4571142318_3878442835.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now pour in the milk with butter. The butter will have melted a bit.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570505421/" title="May2-2010-07b">
          <img alt="May2-2010-07b" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3507/4570505421_6cd7216847.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mix until the flour is incorporated before adding another cup of flour. Repeat this process until all the flour is incorporated. As soon as the wet and dry ingredients are combined, turn off the mixer and let the dough rest for 5 minutes before starting the kneading process. Unless youre doing this by hand, then you start kneading straight away and only use extra flour if the dough starts sticking to your hands. The dough will look really sticky at first, but that will resolve itself during kneading as the gluten are developed.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570471495/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4005/4570471495_7e5f99342b.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I let the machine knead for 6 to 7 minutes, by the end of which the dough will have formed into a ball. If your dough looks impossibly sticky in the final few minutes, add a tbsp flour at a time. But remember, it should still be sticky to the touch! Cover the bowl and let the dough rest for 10 minutes.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570471423/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4052/4570471423_24a2f156db.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          If you used active dry yeast you now transfer the dough to a lightly oiled bowl, apply some oil to the top of the dough to prevent it from drying out and instead of the 10 minutes Im giving it now, let it rise for about an hour, until it doubled in size. Proceed with the recipe as listed below after the first rise.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <br/>
         Lightly butter a baking tray. Or cast iron skillet if you want to get all fashionably old-fashioned on me.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570471069/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4065/4570471069_b6b6e27171.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         After 10 minutes I transferred the dough to a lightly floured cutting board. It should be a really supple and slightly sticky dough.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4571107812/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4003/4571107812_ac91d715d9.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Flatten it with your hand and using a sharp knife divide the dough into 9 pieces. Or 12, if you want them smaller.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4571107744/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4038/4571107744_974f2e8429.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         In stead of rolling the dough in your hand, you simply fold the sides to the bottom and shape them in the palm of your hand. Transfer them to the baking tray but give them a little room to rise. Cover them with a towel and let them rise for an hour to an hour and a half. Until they doubled in size.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570471233/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3521/4570471233_173ee06ba3.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          That gives us time to do the compound butter. I love rosemary but dont like it all burnt and crunchy on top of my rolls. So I put the flavor inside the butter, that way the flavor gets everywhere.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Lets get all medieval and break out the mortar and pestle. Provided you have one.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4571107580/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3431/4571107580_519fd7c1e5.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         In order to get the flavor inside the butter, you have to release some of the oils. Strip the rosemary sprig and add 2 garlic cloves. Now smash the whole shebang,
         <em>
          reallly
         </em>
         bruise the rosemary. Add the remaining 1/3 stick of butter and lightly mix it. Leave it be for an hour or so, while the dough rises.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4571107442/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3440/4571107442_3b39bf5a15.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then comes the moment you lift the towel and find this underneath. Can I hearsome
         <em>
          ahhhhs
         </em>
         and ohhhh
         <em>
          s
         </em>
         , please?
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4571107212/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3419/4571107212_7507ee2f9c.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats your cue to nuke the flavored butter, this will intensify the flavor and will make it easier to brush on top of the rolls. Give it 30 seconds at full power.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570470735/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4042/4570470735_5408e0a33d.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Brush it all over the dough and dont be shy, theyre not called butter rolls for nothing! I dont mind a few lonesome rosemary leaves here and there, as long as its not too much.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570470655/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4007/4570470655_21a7953095.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Last but not least, sprinkle a generous amount of coarse sea salt all over them yummy rolls.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4570470573/" title="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls">
          <img alt="Garlic and Rosemary Butter Rolls" border="0" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3490/4570470573_5e75c60f68.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Bake them in a preheated oven at 350F (175C) for 20 minutes. Until all puffy and golden brown.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <br/>
         The best dinner rolls are not sweet: theyre pillow light, soft, puffy, savory and buttery, all things good. And these rolls are. Oh boy, they are!
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25706352@N08/4573470697/" title="May2-2010-21">
          <img alt="May2-2010-21" border="0" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4007/4573470697_5dc1acd8ae.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh, and serve them with
         <a href="http://www.kayotic.nl/blog/easy-butter" style="text-decoration: none;" target="_blank">
          home made butter
         </a>
         if you
         <em>
          really
         </em>
         want to make an impression.
        </p>
        <table class="hrecipe">
         <tr>
          <td class="fnname" colspan="2">
           Rosemary and Garlic Butter Rolls
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr class="ingredients">
          <td class="hrlabel">
           Ingredients
          </td>
          <td class="hritem">
           <ul class="ingredients">
            <li class="ingredient">
             2 tsp instant yeast (or 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast)
             <br/>
             1 stick butter (soft)
             <br/>
             1/4 cup warm water
             <br/>
             1 cup milk
             <br/>
             2 tbsp sugar
             <br/>
             1 large egg
             <br/>
             1 tsp salt
             <br/>
             3 cups flour
             <br/>
             1 rosemary sprig
             <br/>
             2 garlic cloves
             <br/>
             coarse sea salt
            </li>
           </ul>
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr class="instructions">
          <td class="hrlabel">
           Directions
          </td>
          <td class="hritem">
           <ol class="instructions">
            <li>
             Mix 2/3 stick of butter with 1 cup of milk and lightly beat a large room-temperature egg. Dump 2 tsp instant yeast (or 2 + 1/4 tsp active dry yeast) in a bowl and add 1/4 cup of warm water. Not hot, just warm. Stir and let the yeast sit there for 5 minutes. Pop the milk &amp; butter mix in the microwave for a minute at full power. It doesnt have to be hot, just luke-warm. Transfer the yeast water to your mixer bowl. Add 2 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp salt, pour in the egg and add 1 cup of flour. Pour in the milk with butter. The butter will have melted.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Mix until the flour is incorporated before adding another cup of flour. Repeat this process until all the flour is incorporated. As soon as the wet and dry ingredients are combined, turn off the mixer and let the dough rest for 5 minutes before starting the kneading process. Unless youre doing this by hand, then you start kneading straight away and only use extra flour if the dough starts sticking to your hands. The dough will look really sticky at first, but that will resolve itself during kneading as the gluten are developed.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Let the machine knead for 6 to 7 minutes, by the end of which the dough will have formed into a ball. If your dough looks impossibly sticky in the final few minutes, add a tbsp flour at a time. But remember, it should still be sticky to the touch! Cover the bowl and let the dough rest for 10 minutes. When using active dry yeast you let the dough rise for the first time now.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Lightly butter a baking tray. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured cutting board, flatten it with your hand and using a sharp knife divide the dough into 9 pieces. Or 12, if you want them smaller. Fold the sides to the bottom and shape them in the palm of your hand. Transfer them to the baking tray but give them a little room to rise. Cover them with a towel and let them rise for an hour to an hour and a half. Until they doubled in size.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Strip the rosemary sprig and add 2 garlic cloves. Now smash the whole shebang, reallly bruise the rosemary. Add the remaining 1/3 stick of butter and lightly mix it. Leave it be for an hour or so, while the dough rises. As soon as the dough has doubles in size, microwave the butter for 30 seconds at full power, brush it all over the rolls and sprinkle them a generous amount of coarse sea salt. Bake them in a preheated oven at 350F (175C) for 20 to 25 minutes. Until all puffy and golden brown.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </td>
         </tr>
