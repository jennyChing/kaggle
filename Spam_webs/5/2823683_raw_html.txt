         Which yeast to use for fermentation of alcohol base?
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="postAuthor">
        By
        <a href="http://alcoholbase.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by admin">
         admin
        </a>

       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Alcohol base
         </strong>
         to make alcoholic beverages is mostly produced from sugar. Sugar have no nutrients what so ever and a high alcohol fermentation and a pure fermentation must have both micro and macro nutrients. This is best meet by using a Turbo Yeast.
        </p>
        <p>
         The first Turbo Yeast was invented by Gert Strand about 25 years ago. The Turbos that Gert Strand supplies today is still the best  there is nothing better. There is 3 of those that is suited for fermenting alcohol base.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Alco Base Turbo Yeast 20%
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://distillery-yeast.com/shop/en/alco-base-ingredients-40/alco-base-turbo-yeast-20-in-25-kg-sacks-per-kg-1843.html" target="_blank" title="Alco Base Turbo Yeast 20%">
          <strong>
           Alco Base Turbo Yeast 20%
          </strong>
         </a>
         is the best turbo for alcohol base because no other yeast can ferment a glucose based alcohol base to 20% ABV or a sucrose based to 19%. In fact, it ferments to 22% but this result is unreliable  it sometimes drops to 20% so Gert Strand only claims 20%. What is needed is temperature control, max 25-26C, no stirring needed. Higher temperature
         <strong>
          will
         </strong>
         result in a stuck fermentation or a lot of off smell and off taste produced  or both.
        </p>
        <p>
         As described on alcobase.com, adsorbents can be added from start of fermentation to make the
         <strong>
          alcohol base
         </strong>
         even purer.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Alco Base Turbo Yeast 20%
         </strong>
         dosage is 5.4 gram per liter for 16-20% ABV (alcohol by volume) and 3.6 gram per liter for alcohol base up to 15% ABV.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Puraferm Alco Base Turbo Yeast 16%
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://distillery-yeast.com/shop/en/alco-base-ingredients-40/puraferm-alco-base-turbo-yeast-1846.html" target="_blank" title="Puraferm Alco Base Turbo Yeast 16%">
          <strong>
           Puraferm Alco Base Turbo Yeast 16%
          </strong>
         </a>
         is designed to produce an  ultra-clean wash
         <strong>
          up to 15%
         </strong>
         but is not capable of reaching higher than 16% ABV.  The low congener producing strain used would produce a superior wash to Alco Base Turbo Yeast 20%
         <strong>
          at 15% ABV
         </strong>
         , but again this limits the applications compared to 20% ABV. But with post-treatments with adsorbents like activated carbon powder type A and activated carbon powder type B, bentonite and activated silicon dioxide the Alco Base Turbo Yeast 20% fermented alcohol base could be brought more in line  with this in terms of quality.
        </p>
        <p>
         For production of vodka spirit
         <strong>
          Puraferm Alco Base Turbo Yeast 16%
         </strong>
         produce the purest mash possible for premium luxury vodkas.
        </p>
        <h3>
         TT Turbo Yeast 25 kg Sacks
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://distillery-yeast.com/shop/en/distillery-ingredients-32/tt-turbo-yeast-25-kg-sacks-bulk-per-kg-1816.html" target="_blank" title="TT Turbo Yeast 25 kg Sacks">
          <strong>
           TT Turbo Yeast 25 kg Sacks
          </strong>
         </a>
         is Gert Strands temperature tolerant distillery Turbo Yeast. It is also sold in consumer sachets as Black label Turbo Yeast to hobby distillers.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          TT Turbo
         </strong>
         is  designed to produce a clean wash up to 15% ABV  it can be used to ferment up to  17 or 18% ABV at its maximum recommended dosage 3.6 gram/liter, but there would be an over-kill  of nutrient compared to
         <strong>
          Alco Base Turbo Yeast 20%
         </strong>
