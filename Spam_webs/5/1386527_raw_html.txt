         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
           History
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Hank_Scorpio?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit History section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Scorpio, despite being an evil genius, proved to be an ideal boss, welcoming the
          <a href="/wiki/Simpson_family" title="Simpson family">
           Simpson family
          </a>
          with a beautiful home and a friendly, relaxed, non-authoritative employer attitude. However, being an evil genius is still his priority, and over the episode he delivers a video threat to the United Nations, in which he destroys the Queensboro Bridge with a giant satellite laser device, but is annoyed that some of them seemed to think the bridge collapsed on its own. Unlike many supervillains, Scorpio does care for theemployees who live in his company town,Cypress Creek, even going so far offering his employees health and free dental care (including to life partners)along with regularly scheduledfun runs. Later, he tries to kill
          <a href="/wiki/Mr._Bont" title="Mr. Bont">
           Mr. Bont
          </a>
          , a secret agent, who cleverly escapes but is tackled by Homer before being rapidly executed in turn by Scorpio's guards. When Homer comes to Scorpio and tells him that he wants to take his family back to
          <a href="/wiki/Springfield" title="Springfield">
           Springfield
          </a>
          , a battle between Globex and the U.S government is taking place, and Homer sympathizes with him having problems with the government. Though Scorpio says he would like to have Homer stay, he told Homer to do what he felt he should do, and tells Homer he would do him a favor if he were to kill some people before grabbing a flamethrower and killing several soldiers while laughing sadistically.
         </p>
         <p>
          Scorpio later succeeds in taking over the East Coast of the United States, seen firstly as a newspaper headline "Supervillain Seizes East Coast" which lands on The Simpsons' doorstep. He sends Homer a letter saying that
          <a href="/wiki/Project_Arcturus" title="Project Arcturus">
           Project Arcturus
          </a>
          couldn't have succeeded without him, and gives him the
          <a href="/wiki/Denver_Broncos" title="Denver Broncos">
           Denver Broncos
          </a>
          as a gift, though he knew Homer wanted the Dallas Cowboys, he said it was a start. He later invites Homer if he finds himself in the East Coast.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-0">
           <a href="#cite_note-0">
            [1]
           </a>
          </sup>
          Homer and his family never knew that Hank Scorpio is an international criminal.
         </p>
         Scorpio was recognized by the Springfield Wall of Fame as being a philanthropist.

         <h3>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Bongo_Comics">
           Bongo Comics
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Hank_Scorpio?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit Bongo Comics section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p>
          In the comic "Sandwiches are Forever", it's revealed that Hank Scorpio was imprisoned in a Turkish prison for trading ray guns.
          <a href="/wiki/Homer_Simpson" title="Homer Simpson">
           Homer Simpson
          </a>
          was thrown into the same cell as he was after trying to sneak a sandwich out of Turkey. After the guards take a bite out of the sandwich and died of food poisoning, Homer and Hank escaped the prison.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the comic "Lisa Goes to Camp", he has a son often referred to as "
          <a href="/wiki/Gold_Star" title="Gold Star">
           Gold Star
          </a>
          ." His son displays many similar characteristics such as a desire to take over the world. He dresses identically to his father.
He was Hinted in The Simpsons Hit and Run video game on Level 6, Bart buys a supervillain car from Kearney. Kearny mentions that this villain had also "CONQURED THE EAST COAST."
         </p>
         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_Laughter">
           Behind the Laughter
          </span>
          <span class="editsection">
           <a href="/wiki/Hank_Scorpio?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit Behind the Laughter section">
            <img class="sprite edit-pencil" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D"/>
            Edit
           </a>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Hank Scorpio is a parody of British Billionaire Richard Branson. Scorpio's character parodies evil villains from
          <i>
           James Bond
          </i>
          movies, especially Ernst Stavro Blofeld. Unlike the Bond villains, however, Scorpio genuinely seems to care about the well-being of his peopleeven going so far as to have a heart-to-heart talk with Homer about his family's decision to move while in the middle of a battle in Scorpio's lair and giving a Homer the Denver Broncos as thanks for his help, semi-fulfilling Homer's lifelong dream of owning the Dallas Cowboys he told Scorpio earlier.
         </p>
         <p>
          The episode "
          <a href="/wiki/You_Only_Move_Twice" title="You Only Move Twice">
           You Only Move Twice
          </a>
          " has a Bond-esque closing song (a direct parody of the theme to
          <i>
           Goldfinger
          </i>
          ), which plays during the end credits.
         </p>
         <p>
          The entire "Hammock District" routine was heavily ad-libbed by Al Brooks, which also explained why Homer was unable to follow Hank Scorpio during the routine and only catch up at the end.
         </p>
         <p>
          He was supposed to be the villain of
          <a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons_Movie" title="The Simpsons Movie">
           The Simpsons Movie
          </a>
          but for whatever reason this idea was dropped, and he was replaced by new character
          <a href="/wiki/Russ_Cargill" title="Russ Cargill">
           Russ Cargill
          </a>
          who coincidentally (or perhaps not) is also voiced by Albert Brooks.
         </p>
