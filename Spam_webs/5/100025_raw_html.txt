               <span class="meta-date">

              <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7812727028682926782" itemprop="articleBody">
               <center style="text-align: justify;">
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 In case you didnt notice, Facebook has updated its news
feeds in its mission to make itself the go-to information portal on the
internet for the latest news on current events.
                 <o:p>
                 </o:p>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/--SkKzcDv56E/Up6f8M9fzwI/AAAAAAAAG2A/gnNfccUcEXM/s1600/FB+feedsw.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/--SkKzcDv56E/Up6f8M9fzwI/AAAAAAAAG2A/gnNfccUcEXM/s1600/FB+feedsw.jpg"/>
                 </a>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 This means that Facebook will now push for high quality
content and downgrade less important posts, such as memes when deciding on
which items to show on users news feeds. Moreover, a related section has been
added which will show up to three related stories once a user clicks or likes a
post.
                 <o:p>
                 </o:p>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal">
                 There are also reports hinting that Facebook might also add
a new feature that will push posts towards the top of feeds whenever a comment
is added. Facebook is aware though that such a feature can make certain posts
look spam, so Facebook said that its team is testing this functionality.
                 <o:p>
                 </o:p>
