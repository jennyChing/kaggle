       <p>
        As loyal Crunchy Betty readers, youre more aware than most people about what products you put on your body and what they contain. So you know from experience that you can create handmade products that are just as good, or better, than their store bought alternatives.
       </p>
       <p>
        And as G.I. Joe said  knowing is half the battle!
       </p>
       <p>
        I truly believe that we can live cleaner, greener, more creative and less expensive lives by making things ourselves. How? By rediscovering the tips, skills and recipes from our past! Why struggle to figure out something myself when people have been creating and using lovely beauty and health products for generations?
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres a recipe I love because it uses something that is so abundant in nature  daisies!
       </p>
       <h1>
        Why Daisies?
       </h1>
       <p>
        Daisies are easy to identify, grow everywhere, are quick to pick, and actually have a medicinal use  applied externally theyre great for bumps &amp; bruises!
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5701" style="width: 560px">
        <a href="http://crunchybetty.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/daisydrops-1.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="size-large wp-image-5701 pib-hover-img" height="392" src="http://crunchybetty.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/daisydrops-1-550x392.jpg" title="daisydrops-1" width="550"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Aw, I even had my own picture of daisies to show off here. (Leslie)
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Youve probably seen expensive tubes of Arnica cream for treating bruises. Arnica works brilliantly, but it doesnt grow in England. And rather than buying expensive specialty ingredients, why not use something thats in our backyards?
       </p>
       <p>
        According to my old
        <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=113543" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         herb
        </a>
        books, daisies are good for sprains, bruises, aches &amp; pains. Daisies are antiinflammatory, a wound-healer, and reduce pain. In modern life, I think this salve would be brilliant for bruises, sports injuries, sore muscles, arthritis and of course dry skin! Just please dont apply on broken skin. This salve is for closed wounds and bruises, etc.
       </p>
       <p>
        I love this project because it combines a vintage remedy with some outdoor foraging and some not-too-taxing time in the kitchen. There are two steps  make a daisy infused oil, then turn it into a salve. You could just use the daisy oil as a bruise healer, but its a bit messy to carry in your handbag.
       </p>
       <p>
        Note:As always,use common sense with this project. If you or your family is allergic to the daisy family, this isnt the project for you! Always test any of your homemade projects on a small patch of your inner arm and wait 24 hours to see if you have any reaction before proceeding, just in case!
       </p>
       <h1>
        Make Daisy Infused Oil
       </h1>
       <p>
        Its really easy to infuse any
        <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=113543" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         herb
        </a>
        into an oil. Gather a lot of the plant, preferably on a sunny day. I used 2 big handfuls of daisy flowers.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://crunchybetty.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/IMG_3156.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5703 pib-hover-img" height="200" src="http://crunchybetty.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/IMG_3156-300x200.jpg" title="IMG_3156" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Find a jar that fits the amount of daisies you have collected. We want the jar to be completely full of daisies and oil. Put the daisies in the jar, cover completely with olive oil, cover the jar with a lid. Let the jar sit in a sunny place for 2 weeks.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://crunchybetty.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/IMG_3161-300x200.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5704 pib-hover-img" height="200" src="http://crunchybetty.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/IMG_3161-300x200.jpg" title="IMG_3161-300x200" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        After 2 weeks, strain out the daisy flowers and save the oil. Really squeeze the daisies to get every bit of oil out. You can use the oil as-is, or you can proceed to
       </p>
       <h1>
        Make Daisy Salve
       </h1>
       <h3>
        Daisy Salve for Bumps &amp; Bruises
       </h3>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Daisy-infused oil
        </li>
        <li>
         Lavender
         <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=113543" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          essential oil
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         Beeswax
        </li>
        <li>
         Assorted pots or containers for the finished salve
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        How to:
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         Measure your daisy-infused oil.
        </li>
        <li>
         Math time! (Dont run away. Its easy, I promise.) Work out the proportions of oil and wax needed. For every 4 parts oil, you need 1 part beeswax. So, for example, if you have 1 c. of daisy oil you need 1/4c. of beeswax. I used 200ml of oil and 50 ml of beeswax.
        </li>
        <li>
         In a bain marie (or double boiler), combine the daisy oil and beeswax. Heat gently and stir until completely melted.
        </li>
        <li>
         Consistency test We need to check how hard the finished salve will be. Theres no right or wrong answer, its a personal preference. It could be soft and slightly oily, or set like a lip balm, or very hard and waxy. Think about how youll use it. I carry mine in my handbag, so I like it to set quite firm  but so that it will still melt when I rub my finger over the salve.
        </li>
        <li>
         Do the consistency check by taking a small spoonful of the liquid and placing it in the freezer for 2-3 minutes. Remove from freezer and check the consistency with your fingers. If its too hard, add more oil. If its too soft, add more beeswax.
        </li>
        <li>
         Remove from heat. Add 10 drops lavender
         <a href="http://www.mountainroseherbs.com/index.php?AID=113543" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          essential oil
         </a>
         (amount for 1c. of daisy oil).
        </li>
        <li>
         Pour into clean pots,and let them cool. If you want a smooth surface on your salve,dont touch the pots while theyre cooling! Once cool, cover tightly. Daisy salve should last at least one year or more.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://crunchybetty.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/IMG_3184.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5705 pib-hover-img" height="200" src="http://crunchybetty.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/IMG_3184-300x200.jpg" title="IMG_3184" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h1>
        How to Use Daisy Salve
       </h1>
       <p>
        I love my daisy salve! I split mine between a bigger jar for home,and several smaller containers to keep in my handbag. I use it for everything. Just rub some onto your fingers and then onto the affected area. I use my daisy salve for bruises, sprains, sore muscles, cuticle moisturizer and sometimes a lip balm.
