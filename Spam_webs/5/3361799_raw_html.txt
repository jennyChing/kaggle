        12 Things You Didnt Know About Joyce Banda Of Malawi

      <div class="wp-content entry">
       <p>
        Shes one of the two African female presidents. Her presidency has been compared to that of Nigerian president, Jonathan Goodluck. She was globally ranked at position 71 as the worlds most influential female leader, and position 1 in Africa by the Forbes magazine. If you cant figure out who is being referred in the above statement, then youre yet to learn much about African current history.
        <strong>
         Joyce Banda
        </strong>
        , the second female
        <a href="http://buzzsouthafrica.com/president-of-south-africa/" title="7 Fascinating Facts You Didnt Know About President of South Africa">
         president in Africa
        </a>
        and the first female president of Malawi, is one of the extremely few formidable female leaders whose influence is not only felt in Africa, but also with the rest of the world.
        <span style="text-decoration: underline">
         Joyce Banda
        </span>
        isone of the acclaimed African female leaders who have defied the African stereotypes by fighting their way to acquire the top posts in African countries. But behind this powerful female president, there are 12 things that most people dont know. Lets find out what they are:
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span style="text-decoration: underline">
         Joyce Banda of Malawi: 12 Things You Didnt Know About Her
        </span>
       </h2>
       <h3>
        1. She was once a victim of an abusive marriage:
       </h3>
       <p>
        Her first marriage to Roy Kachale, with whom they have three children, was an abusive one as described by her. In fact, thats what motivated her to get into politics. Shes now married to her second husband, Richard Banda with whom they have a stable marriage.
       </p>
       <h3>
        2. She once lived in Kenya:
       </h3>
       <p>
        After getting married to Roy Kachale in 1975, she moved to Kenya to live with her first husband who at that time used to work in Nairobi. She divorced him in 1981 before going back to Malawi.
       </p>
       <p>
        3. Shes the first female president and, at the same time, the first female vice president of Malawi. Before she became the first female president in Malawi, she was the first female vice president under president Mutharika  2009 to 2012.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_949" style="width: 1034px">
        <a href="http://buzzsouthafrica.com/wp-content/uploads/Joyce-Banda1.jpg">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="President Bingu wa Mutharika's runningma" class="wp-image-949" data-recalc-dims="1" src="//i1.wp.com/buzzsouthafrica.com/wp-content/uploads/Joyce-Banda1.jpg?resize=1024%2C641"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://buzzsouthafrica.com/joyce-banda/&amp;media=http://buzzsouthafrica.com/wp-content/uploads/Joyce-Banda1.jpg&amp;description=12 Things You Didnt Know About Joyce Banda Of Malawi')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         nymag.com
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3>
        4. She was fired from vice presidency:
       </h3>
       <p>
        The former Malawian president fired her, but she continued to serve as the vice president as mandated by the constitution. She was even expelled from Democratic Peoples Party (DPP), which is the ruling party. This was after she had refused to endorse Peter Muthariki ( president Mutharikis brother) as the partys presidential candidate in the next general election, 2014.
       </p>
       <h3>
        5. She supports gay rights:
       </h3>
       <p>
        President Joyce Banda is among the few African presidents who supports gay rights. Being the president of a country that was so much against homosexuality, Joyce Banda was quick to repel all laws against same-sex marriages immediately she got into power.
       </p>
       <h3>
        6. She had a sour relationship with the former president:
       </h3>
       <p>
        President Mutharikis relationship with his vice president
        <strong>
         Joyce Banda
        </strong>
        , was a tense one. According to President Banda, President Muthariki refused to keep his promise of supporting her for presidency in the 2014 general election but instead opted to support his brother. This is what brew the animosity between the two.
       </p>
       <div class="buzz_advert buzz_advert-2">
        <style type="text/css">

       <h3>
        7. Shes not the elected President of Malawi:
       </h3>
       <p>
        President Banda succeeded the former president after he died. She was appointed as the president of Malawi as mandated by the Malawian constitution, which allows the vice president to take over the presidency when the president is incapable of governing a country. She will be running for presidency in 2014 for the first time.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_950" style="width: 1034px">
        <a href="http://buzzsouthafrica.com/wp-content/uploads/Joyce-Banda.jpg2_.jpg">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Joyce Banda.jpg2" class="wp-image-950" data-recalc-dims="1" src="//i1.wp.com/buzzsouthafrica.com/wp-content/uploads/Joyce-Banda.jpg2_.jpg?resize=1024%2C743"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://buzzsouthafrica.com/joyce-banda/&amp;media=http://buzzsouthafrica.com/wp-content/uploads/Joyce-Banda.jpg2_.jpg&amp;description=12 Things You Didnt Know About Joyce Banda Of Malawi')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         blackbluedog.com
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3>
        8. There was an assassination attempt on her in 2011:
       </h3>
       <p>
        On November 19th 2011, there was an assassination attempt on her. She only survived because the attackers picked the wrong car.
       </p>
       <h3>
        9. She managed to revive the Malawian economy within a year since she stepped into power:
       </h3>
       <p>
        Her agenda was to create more employment opportunities for Malawian citizens as well as eradicate poverty by creating wealth. According to a vast majority of political analysts in Malawi, she has managed to accomplish much of what she had stated in her agenda.
       </p>
       <p>
        10. She jump-started her career as a secretary before working her way to the top. She has a bachelors degree from Columbus University in Early Childhood Education and a degree in gender studies from Atlantic International university. Apparently, shes doing a masters degree in Leadership from Royal Road University, Canada. In addition to that, she recently received an honorary doctorate from Jeonju University.
       </p>
       <h3>
        11. She refused to meet Madonna:
       </h3>
       <p>
        Recently, Madonna made an announced visit to African nation where she wrote a shady letter to President Banda requesting to meet with her. President Banda turned down the request and even went ahead to disparage Madonnas attempts to make an unannounced visit to Malawi before proceeding to the village to dance with poor people.
       </p>
       <h3>
        12. She Initiated several grass root projects
       </h3>
       <p>
        She has initiated several grass root projects about policy change for women and children in Malawi. This includes the
        <span style="text-decoration: underline">
         Joyce Banda Foundation For Better Education
        </span>
        , Young Women Leaders Network and the National Association of business Women. In 1997, she awarded an African price of leadership for sustainable hunger end alongside the president of Mozambique, Joaquim Chissano.
       </p>
