               </span>

             <div style="margin:15px;">
              <center>
               <script type="text/javascript">
                &lt;!--

             <p>
              Here is a list of animals that are the rarest in the world, and that should help people if we think that our children want to see.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <font size="2">
                Javan Rhinoceros
               </font>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://lh6.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPTagdgtI/AAAAAAAAHeU/502NoLYFVPU/s1600-h/Javan%20Rhinoceros%5B5%5D.jpg">
               <img alt="Javan Rhinoceros" border="0" height="321" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPUVtzZvI/AAAAAAAAHeY/S8RZ4SiI2BY/Javan%20Rhinoceros_thumb%5B3%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Javan Rhinoceros" width="484"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              Indonesia and Vietnam
              <br/>
              Number remaining:
              <strong>
               fewer than 60
               <br/>
              </strong>
              Perhaps the planet's rarest large mammal. Its horn is prized by poachers, and its forests are prized by developers. Both could spell doom for the species.
             </p>
             <span class="fullpost">
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Vaquita
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <a href="http://lh5.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPVAGNq3I/AAAAAAAAHec/LaMWVXm1cCI/s1600-h/Vaquita%5B7%5D.jpg">
                 <img alt="Vaquita" border="0" height="238" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPV2dtwFI/AAAAAAAAHeg/tuHQohLn-NU/Vaquita_thumb%5B8%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Vaquita" width="482"/>
                </a>
                <br/>
               </strong>
               Gulf of California
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                200 to 300
               </strong>
               <br/>
               One of the rarest cetaceans in the world, the
               <a href="http://www.itsnature.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/phocoena-sinus.jpg" target="_blank">
                Vaquita
               </a>
               is endangered by both its limited range and the ease with which it gets caught in fishing nets.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Cross River Gorilla
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPXRQD_TI/AAAAAAAAHek/dAO9C5mW6G0/s1600-h/Cross%20River%20Gorilla%5B6%5D.jpg">
                <img alt="Cross River Gorilla" border="0" height="321" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPYgOCmOI/AAAAAAAAHeo/oVLuyQAvyM4/Cross%20River%20Gorilla_thumb%5B4%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Cross River Gorilla" width="484"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               Nigeria and Cameroon
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                fewer than 300
               </strong>
               <br/>
               Thought to be extinct in the 1980s, the species is holding on, for now. Hunted for bush meat and crowded out by development, it may not last long.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Sumatran Tiger
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh5.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPabnERXI/AAAAAAAAHes/lh9zljtyFu0/s1600-h/Sumatran%20Tiger%5B5%5D.jpg">
                <img alt="Sumatran Tiger" border="0" height="321" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPbVWzZ6I/AAAAAAAAHew/xJQzI1Ps-1g/Sumatran%20Tiger_thumb%5B3%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Sumatran Tiger" width="484"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               Sumatra, in Indonesia
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                fewer than 600
               </strong>
               <br/>
               This small tiger has lived only in Sumatra for a million years, making it hard to escape human expansion. Most survivors dwell in reserves, but about 100 live beyond the borders of the protected areas.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Golden-Headed Langur
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh6.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPdUSVYxI/AAAAAAAAHe0/vwlVgBtymow/s1600-h/Golden-Headed%20Langur%5B5%5D.jpg">
                <img alt="Golden-Headed Langur" border="0" height="321" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPen0a9lI/AAAAAAAAHe4/U1kb-WC7oK0/Golden-Headed%20Langur_thumb%5B3%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Golden-Headed Langur" width="484"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               Vietnam
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                fewer than 70
               </strong>
               <br/>
               All but wiped out, this primate was placed under protection in 2000. It is still in grave danger, but in 2003 its numbers rose for the first time in decades.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Black-Footed Ferret
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh3.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPfQVGInI/AAAAAAAAHe8/FJf0KjslI8A/s1600-h/Black-Footed%20Ferret%5B7%5D.jpg">
                <img alt="Black-Footed Ferret" border="0" height="424" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPgZep9iI/AAAAAAAAHfA/kx4BFZHQ2rk/Black-Footed%20Ferret_thumb%5B12%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Black-Footed Ferret" width="364"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               North American Great Plains
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                about 1,000
               </strong>
               <br/>
               The continent's only native ferret is one of its most endangered mammals. In 1986, there were only 18 individuals left, but the species is clawing back.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Borneo Pygmy Elephant
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh3.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPiOtjXQI/AAAAAAAAHfE/K9zjhIzWAks/s1600-h/Borneo%20Pygmy%20Elephant%5B5%5D.jpg">
                <img alt="Borneo Pygmy Elephant" border="0" height="321" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPjfgxYcI/AAAAAAAAHfI/dmWWInunT8k/Borneo%20Pygmy%20Elephant_thumb%5B3%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Borneo Pygmy Elephant" width="484"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               Northern Borneo
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                about 1,500
               </strong>
               <br/>
               Shorter than the Asian elephant by about 20 in. (50 cm), the Borneo pygmy elephant is also more docile. Palm plantations have reduced its range, leaving it crowded for space.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Giant Panda
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh3.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPkXNiQDI/AAAAAAAAHfM/ziMwFDPJLeg/s1600-h/Giant%20Panda%5B6%5D.jpg">
                <img alt="Giant Panda" border="0" height="535" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPlU99i5I/AAAAAAAAHfQ/3WjqOTl56Vw/Giant%20Panda_thumb%5B9%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Giant Panda" width="362"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               China, Burma, Vietnam
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                fewer than 2,000
               </strong>
               <br/>
               Loss and fragmentation of habitat are to blame for the panda's perilous state. Captive breeding and species protection are helping the panda hang onbarely.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Polar Bear
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPmBK4mTI/AAAAAAAAHfU/tblg7j1SzFo/s1600-h/Polar%20Bear%5B5%5D.jpg">
                  <img alt="Polar Bear" border="0" height="321" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPmi0BgVI/AAAAAAAAHfY/lp2fBzpShHQ/Polar%20Bear_thumb%5B3%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: block; border-left-width: 0px; float: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; border-right-width: 0px" title="Polar Bear" width="484"/>
                 </a>
                 <br/>
                </font>
               </strong>
               The circumpolar Arctic
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                fewer than 25,000
               </strong>
               <br/>
               Human development and poaching have long threatened the polar bear, but climate change and the loss of sea ice are now pushing it onto the critical list.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <font size="2">
                 Mekong Giant Catfish
                </font>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPnXfzIxI/AAAAAAAAHfc/5mObPyd9eTU/s1600-h/giant-mekong-fish-dog-eating-catfish%2001%5B5%5D.jpg">
                <img alt="giant-mekong-fish-dog-eating-catfish 01" border="0" height="323" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPoFGAObI/AAAAAAAAHfg/IeY8oSy7kKo/giant-mekong-fish-dog-eating-catfish%2001_thumb%5B3%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="giant-mekong-fish-dog-eating-catfish 01" width="484"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               Mekong region of Southeast Asia
               <br/>
               Number remaining:
               <strong>
                hundreds
               </strong>
               <br/>
               Prized for its enormous size (the largest ever caught was 646 lb., or 293 kg), it is now protected in Thailand, Laos and Cambodia, but fishing goes on.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://lh3.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPo8eEakI/AAAAAAAAHfk/Q8yRJt7j-4A/s1600-h/Mekong%20Giant%20Catfish%5B7%5D.jpg">
                <img alt="Mekong Giant Catfish" border="0" height="410" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_JwTvDWpU4xI/TddPpiB-OFI/AAAAAAAAHfo/LIbnVAe_Ymc/Mekong%20Giant%20Catfish_thumb%5B10%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="border-right: 0px; border-top: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-left: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-bottom: 0px" title="Mekong Giant Catfish" width="262"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <font size="1">
                 <a href="http://www.time.com/time/photogallery/0,29307,1888702_1863807,00.html" target="_blank">
                  source
                 </a>
                </font>
               </em>
              </p>
