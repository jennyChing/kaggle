     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Pannonian Sea
      </b>
      was a shallow ancient sea located where the
      <a href="/wiki/Pannonian_Basin" title="Pannonian Basin">
       Pannonian Basin
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">
       Central Europe
      </a>
      is now. The Pannonian Sea existed during the
      <a href="/wiki/Miocene" title="Miocene">
       Miocene
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">
       Pliocene
      </a>
      epochs, when a three to four kilometre depth of marine sediments were deposited in the Pannonian Basin.
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pannonian_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Pannonian Sea, for most of its history, was part of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paratethys_Sea" title="Paratethys Sea">
       Paratethys Sea
      </a>
      . A Miocene uplift of the Carpathian Mountains isolated the sea from the rest of Paratethys (about 10 million years ago).
     </p>
     <p>
      During its first historical phase, the Pannonian Sea had a western connection with the
      <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">
       Mediterranean Sea
      </a>
      through the territories of the modern
      <a href="/wiki/Ligurian_Sea" title="Ligurian Sea">
       Ligurian Sea
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">
       Bavaria
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Basin" title="Vienna Basin">
       Vienna Basin
      </a>
      . Through the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%90erdap" title="erdap">
       erdap
      </a>
      Strait, the Pannonian Sea was linked to the Paratethys in the
      <a href="/wiki/Wallachia" title="Wallachia">
       Wallachian
      </a>
      -
      <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">
       Pontic
      </a>
      Basin. The Pannonian Sea was also attached to the
      <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">
       Aegean Sea
      </a>
      through the modern
      <a href="/wiki/Pre%C5%A1evo_Valley" title="Preevo Valley">
       Preevo Valley
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Due to its diverse history the salinity of the sea often shifted. The decrease of salinity resulted an endemic fauna.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Pannonian Sea existed for about 9 million years. Eventually, the sea lost its connection to the Paratethys and became a lake permanently (
      <i>
       Pannonian Lake
      </i>
      ). Its last remnant, the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Slavonian_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Slavonian Lake (page does not exist)">
       Slavonian Lake
      </a>
      , disappeared in the
      <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">
       Pleistocene
      </a>
      era. The remnants of the former islands of the Pannonian Sea are the modern
      <a href="/wiki/Pannonian_island_mountains" title="Pannonian island mountains">
       Pannonian island mountains
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Mecsek" title="Mecsek">
       Mecsek
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Papuk" title="Papuk">
       Papuk
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Psunj" title="Psunj">
       Psunj
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Krndija" title="Krndija">
       Krndija
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Dilj" title="Dilj">
       Dilj
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Fru%C5%A1ka_Gora" title="Fruka Gora">
       Fruka Gora
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Vr%C5%A1ac_Mountains" title="Vrac Mountains">
       Vrac Mountains
      </a>
      ).
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">
       In popular culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pannonian_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In popular culture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1979, the Serbian musician
      <a href="/wiki/%C4%90or%C4%91e_Bala%C5%A1evi%C4%87" title="ore Balaevi">
       ore Balaevi
      </a>
      released a single titled
      <i>
       <span lang="sr" xml:lang="sr">
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Panonski_Mornar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Panonski Mornar (page does not exist)">
         Panonski Mornar
        </a>
       </span>
      </i>
      (Pannonian sailor).
     </p>
