        <h1>
         How to ensure your new Kurt Geiger heels feel at home
        </h1>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://loblerdelaney.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/kurtgeiger19.jpg">
          <img alt="kurt geiger" class="aligncenter wp-image-18498" height="911" src="http://loblerdelaney.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/kurtgeiger19.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://bit.ly/19GQSd9">
          <em>
           <span style="color: #ff00ff;">
            Kurt Geiger Bond
           </span>
          </em>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         Its always difficult moving into a new season. Whilst youve loved those practical winter boots and all-weather wellies, spring is beckoning promising open toes, pointy flats and sandals with gay abandon. I often wonder how my old shoes feel when I brush them aside to the dustier parts of the shoe rack, let alone how the new heels on the block feel being tossed in amongst the old laggers whove been ruling the shoe roost for the past few seasons. Ive got my eye on some Kurt Geiger heels for spring so, I got to thinking, how do I minimise the stress a new shoe feels when its introduced to its new home? Here are 5 top tips to make that transition happy and peaceful for shoes and owner alike.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         1. On first arrival unpack them in a quiet place and take time out to let the little fellas take in their new environment.
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         2. A bit like welcoming any new family member, its important to be inclusive. Perhaps hold a little welcome meet &amp; greet (champagne &amp; canapes optional)
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <a href="http://loblerdelaney.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/kurtgeiger23.jpg">
          <img alt="kurt geiger" class="aligncenter wp-image-18502" height="534" src="http://loblerdelaney.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/kurtgeiger23.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         3.Get them used to the usual family life early on let the whole family bond
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <a href="http://loblerdelaney.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/kurtgeiger9.jpg">
          <img alt="kurt geiger" class="aligncenter wp-image-18489" height="692" src="http://loblerdelaney.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/kurtgeiger9.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         But a note of caution some family members like their own personal space, take a careful approach
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <a href="http://loblerdelaney.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/kurtgeiger7.jpg">
          <img alt="kurt geiger" class="aligncenter wp-image-18487" height="534" src="http://loblerdelaney.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/kurtgeiger7.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         4. Dont give them any special treatment although every Kurt Geiger heel is a special heel, they still need to stay grounded in their new environment. Yes, they may have hit pay-dirt and been placed next to a
         <a href="http://bit.ly/1FSJpDt">
          <span style="color: #ff00ff;">
           Louboutin
          </span>
         </a>
         or a
         <span style="color: #ff00ff;">
          <a href="http://bit.ly/1Bm4G2p" style="color: #ff00ff;">
           Valentino
          </a>
          Rockstud
         </span>
         , but every shoe has its own merit.
        </p>
