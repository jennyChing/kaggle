          <h2>
           <b>
            <i>
             Pinnable Image Half Double Crochet Hat Pattern:
            </i>
           </b>
          </h2>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           <a href="http://i2.wp.com/oombawkadesigncrochet.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/free+crochet+pattern+beanie+oombawka+design+pinterest+version.jpg?resize=448%2C640">
            <img alt="Free Crochet Hat Pattern" border="0" class="aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/oombawkadesigncrochet.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/free+crochet+pattern+beanie+oombawka+design+pinterest+version.jpg?resize=448%2C640" style="border: 0px;"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <p>
           This free crochet hat pattern is a basic hat pattern  this design uses soft worsted weight yarn  with wraps per inch of 10 (10 wpi). Similar weight yarn can be substituted  some examples include Caron Simply Soft, Vannas Choice and Loops &amp; Threads Impeccable.
          </p>
          <p>
           Please check your gauge as it is easy to allow your stitches to become more relaxed as you crochet the repeat rounds.
          </p>
          <p>
           This hat is crocheted in continuous rounds/spirals  you do not need to slip stitch to join or chain-1. You can use a stitch marker (safety pin, bobby pin, bit of scrap yarn, twist tie, etc. ) to mark the first (or last) stitch of the round and move the stitch marker up each round as you progress, to mark your place.
          </p>
          <p>
           There is no seam in this hat  it is neat and tidy.
          </p>
          <p>
           If you prefer using the slip stitch to join and chain-1 (or 2) and working in regular rounds  you can  just try to keep those slip stitches small. This will add a seam to the hat (from the slip stitch and chain stitches) and make the hat a little bit larger, but it will still work.
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            I would like to thank Louise of
            <a href="http://garngrammatik.dk/gratis-haekleopskrifter/nemme-haekleopskrifter/haeklet-halvstangmaske-hue-alle-stoerrelser/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Garn Grammatik
            </a>
            for kindly providing us with the translation of this entire pattern in Danish! Thank you Louise! You can meet and visit with Louise here:
            <a href="http://garngrammatik.dk/gratis-haekleopskrifter/nemme-haekleopskrifter/haeklet-halvstangmaske-hue-alle-stoerrelser/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Garn Grammatik.
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Danish Version of this pattern:
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://oombawkadesigncrochet.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Halvstangmaske-hue-i-alle-strrelser_Danish-version.pdf">
             Halvstangmaske hue  i alle strrelser_Danish version
            </a>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <b>
            Version 1
           </b>
            This hat will reach to the base of your ears  if you want a shorter hat please see
           <b>
            Version 2
           </b>
           below.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Full Length Half Double Crochet Hat Pattern
          </h2>
          <p>
           <b>
            Recommended Yarn:
           </b>
           Red Heart Soft Yarn (WW)  10 wpi
           <br/>
           <b>
            Hook:
           </b>
           5.50 mm
           <br/>
           <b>
            Gauge:
           </b>
           14 stitches in 4 inches; 12 rows in 4 inches
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Abbreviations Used:
           </strong>
           R  round, hdc  half double crochet, st  stitch, sts  stitches, x (as in 8x)  the number of times to repeat the instructions in brackets, [8]  the number of stitches in the round, F/O  finish off/fasten off, sl st  slip stitch
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Tutorials you may find helpful:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://oombawkadesigncrochet.com/2014/11/how-to-make-a-magic-ring.html">
            Adjustable Loop/Magic Ring Tutorial
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://oombawkadesigncrochet.com/2014/09/invisible-join-technique.html">
            Invisible Join/Invisible Finish (when you finish off)
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           This version is designed to reach the base of the ear.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Preemie (fits 12 circumference  4.5 height)
           </b>
           <br/>
           R1: 8 hdc in adjustable loop [8]
           <br/>
           R2: 2 hdc in each st around [16]
           <br/>
           R3: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in the next st) 8x [24]
           <br/>
           R4: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 2 sts) 8x [32]
           <br/>
           R5: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 3sts) 3x, 1 hdc in each remaining st [35]
           <br/>
           R6: 1 hdc in each st around [35]
           <br/>
           R7-13: repeat R6 [35]
           <br/>
           R14: 1 sl st (loosely) in each st around, F/O [35]
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Newborn (fits 13.5 circumference  5 height)
           </b>
           <br/>
           R1: 8 hdc in adjustable loop [8]
           <br/>
           R2: 2 hdc in each st around [16]
           <br/>
           R3: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in the next st) 8 x [24]
           <br/>
           R4: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 2 sts) 8x [32]
           <br/>
           R5: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 3 sts) 8x [40]
           <br/>
           R6: 1 hdc in each st around [40]
           <br/>
           R7-R15: repeat R6 [40]
           <br/>
           R16: 1 sl st (loosely) in each st around, F/O [40]
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            0-3 months (fits 16 circumference  6 height)
           </b>
           <br/>
           R1: 8 hdc in adjustable loop [8]
           <br/>
           R2: 2 hdc in each st around [16]
           <br/>
           R3: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in the next st) 8 x [24]
           <br/>
           R4: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 2 sts) 8x [32]
           <br/>
           R5: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 3 sts) 8x [40]
           <br/>
           R6: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the 4 sts) 8x [48]
           <br/>
           R7: 1 hdc in each st around [48]
           <br/>
           R8-18: repeat R7 [48]
           <br/>
           R19: 1 sl st (loosely) in each st around, F/O [48]
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            3-6 months (fits 17 circumference, 6.25 height)
           </b>
           <br/>
           R1: 8 hdc in adjustable loop [8]
           <br/>
           R2: 2 hdc in each st around [16]
           <br/>
           R3: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in the next st) 8 x [24]
           <br/>
           R4: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 2 sts) 8x [32]
           <br/>
           R5: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 3 sts) 8x [40]
           <br/>
           R6: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 4 sts) 8x [48]
           <br/>
           R7: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in each of the next 5 sts) 4x, 1 hdc in each remaining st [52]
           <br/>
           R8: 1 hdc in each st around [52]
           <br/>
           R9-R19: repeat R8 [52]
           <br/>
           R20: 1 sl st (loosely) in each st around, F/O [52]
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            6-12 months (fits 18 circumference, 6.75 height)
           </b>
           <br/>
           R1: 8 hdc in adjustable loop [8]
           <br/>
           R2: 2 hdc in each st around [16]
           <br/>
           R3: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in the next st) 8 x [24]
           <br/>

This technique eliminates the chain-1 and slip stitch to join bit and the seam you get when you use that methodif you prefer using the slip stitch to join and chain-1 though, you can, just try to make the slip stitch as small as possible  it will add a bit of extra size to the hat and a seam but it will still work out :) Rhondda

             <div class="comment-content" itemprop="commentText">
              <p>
               Hi Sandra,
              </p>
              <p>
               R3: (2 hdc in 1st st, 1 hdc in the next st) 8x [24]
              </p>
              <p>
               This means:
               <br/>
               Round 3: (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st) repeat this sequence 8 times around the hat  so repeat the entire sequence of instructions enclosed in the brackets around the hat 8 times (8 repeats). So in long form:
               <br/>
               (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st)
               <br/>
               (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st)
               <br/>
               (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st)
               <br/>
               (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st)
               <br/>
               (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st)
               <br/>
               (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st)
               <br/>
               (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st)
               <br/>
               (work 2 hdc sts in the 1st st, work 1 hdc in the next st)
               <br/>
               The [24] is the total stitches for the round so 24 stitches in this round.
              </p>
              <p>
               Hope this helps :)
               <br/>
               Rhondda
              </p>
