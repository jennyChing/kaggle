      <h2>
       <a href="http://www.omega-level.net/2011/11/14/lucasfilm-registers-the-domain-star-wars-identities-what-could-it-be/">
        LucasFilm Registers The Domain Star Wars: Identities. What Could It Be?!
       </a>
      </h2>
      <div class="post-author-date">
       November 14th, 2011 by
       <span class="bold txt-olred">
        Caffeine Powered
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="the-content">
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22431" height="300" src="http://www.omega-level.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/George-Lucas-Before-Full-Douche..png" title="George Lucas - Before Full Douche." width="450"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        LucasFilm has registered the domain Star Wars: Identities, prompting geeks like me to breath a sigh of relief. You see,
        <em>
         creating new aberrations and slights on God
        </em>
        is less painful to me than seeing him meddle with the OT. Still though, what could it be?
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-22430">
        </span>
        <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: 11px; font-weight: bold;">
         <a href="http://www.slashfilm.com/domain-registration-rumors-star-wars-identities-lucasfilm-phantoms-paramount/#more-115804" target="_blank">
          Slashfilm
         </a>
         :
        </span>
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Sometimes studios register domains on the off chance a project gets made. In other cases, domain registration can reveal a projects title or existence way before its ready for public knowledge. For example, the title of the new James Bond film Skyfall was revealed a month before the official announcement and Marvel has domains for movies like Doctor Strange and Ant-Man years before those projects are likely to be made.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres your context, heres your news. Lucasfilm has registered several domains revealing a project called Star Wars: Identities.
        </p>
        <p>
         [cont]
        </p>
        <p>
         The more interesting piece of news is the LucasFilm registration. What is Star Wars: Identities? Though the Fusible article surmises it could be the title of a new movie or TV show, its much more likely some kind of new game. Purely speculating, it sounds like the name of a role playing game where someone can be whatever kind of Star Wars identity theyd like: Jedi, Sith, Stormtrooper, Wookie, etc. Thats just a guess, but it makes more sense than saying its the title of the live action TV series.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        Any guesses?
       </p>
