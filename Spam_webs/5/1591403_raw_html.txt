           <h1 class="entry-title">
            Choose the Right Exterior for Your Commercial Building
           </h1>
           <div class="entry-meta">
            <span class="sep">
             Posted on
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.burbachexteriors.com/blog/choose-the-right-exterior-for-your-commercial-building/" rel="bookmark" title="9:05 am">
             <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-12-03T09:05:40+00:00" pubdate="">
              December 3, 2014
             </time>
            </a>
            <span class="by-author">
             <span class="sep">
              by
             </span>
             <span class="author vcard">
              <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.burbachexteriors.com/blog/author/jaren/" rel="author" title="View all posts by jaren">
               jaren
              </a>
             </span>
            </span>
           </div>
           <!-- .entry-meta -->
          </div>
          <!-- .entry-header -->
          <div class="entry-content">
           <p style="text-align: center">
            <img alt="Commercial Building" class="aligncenter wp-image-213" height="349" src="http://www.burbachexteriors.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Commercial-Building.jpg" width="640"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Commercial buildings are like a home everyone shares. Whether its an apartment building, public library or business, the exterior of a commercial building helps define the community around it. What do you want your
            <a href="http://www.burbachexteriors.com/blog/a-quick-guide-to-different-types-of-siding/" target="_blank">
             commercial buildings exterior
            </a>
            to say?
            <br/>
            Consider the purpose of the building, but also consider the structures around it. Do you want your building to blend in seamlessly with its surroundings, or to stand out and be noticed? In deciding how you want your building to look, you must consider the various choices of exterior building materials. Concrete, wood, metal, masonry, siding and stucco are popular choices for businesses, each offering their own unique advantages.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Wood
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Wood makes for a beautiful exterior, but like all beauty, it fades over time. Proper maintenance, including power washing, staining, sealing and painting, can help extend its life. Wood is more expensive and requires more work, but when done correctly, presents a handsome exterior.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Siding
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Siding is common in residential structures, is also an option for commercial buildings. Vinyl is popular  its low-maintenance, low-cost and comes in an endless variety of colors. Aluminum and cement fiber siding are other options, the latter being the most expensive, but also the most durable.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Stucco
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Stucco is a tough, economical material resistant to weather and impact. Its good at minimizing street noise and its low-maintenance One of the big draws for stucco is that it
            <strong>
             can
            </strong>
            be applied in endless styles and colors.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Brick
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Brick has lovely curb appeal, bringing a timeless look to your building while providing a helping of aesthetic texture. Its strong and requires almost no maintenance. Since it is purely cosmetic, you dont have to consider whether it is structurally adequate, since it isnt load-bearing. Brick red is a common color, but remember that these building blocks are available in a wide variety of colors, including white, gray and shades of brown.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Metal
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Metal sheets are an enduring exterior, providing strength and requiring little maintenance. Theyre fire-resistant, termites are never a problem and the thicker it is, the less likely to dent it is and the less noise the occupants will have to hear. Todays metal exteriors are no longer limited to industrial parks or garages  your architect or builder can show you some truly stunning finishes. Metal exteriors can be a prime example of strength meets beauty.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             Concrete
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Concrete is the most durable exterior finish, resisting weather and fire, providing a near-soundproof environment and effectively keeping out vermin. Its easy to make a concrete finish blend in with its surroundings, but if you have something aesthetically pleasing in mind, use an architect or contractor who knows how to make concrete look inviting.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Renovation by Burbach specializes in commercial structures. We guide customers and developers through the decisions necessary in selecting an exterior finish for your building. Aesthetics, cost, durability and energy efficiency are just some of the considerations that come in to play. Rely on the professionals at Renovation by Burbach to make their years of experience building commercial structures in the northern Utah area work for you.
           </p>
