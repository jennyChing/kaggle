     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Adventures of the Little Koala
       </b>
      </i>
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
        
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <i>
        Koara Bi Kokki
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">
       anime
      </a>
      TV series produced by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tohokushinsha_Film_Corporation" title="Tohokushinsha Film Corporation">
       Tohokushinsha Film Corporation
      </a>
      . It aired originally in Japan on
      <a href="/wiki/TV_Tokyo" title="TV Tokyo">
       TV Tokyo
      </a>
      from October 4, 1984 through March 28, 1985, and then aired in the United States on
      <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">
       Nickelodeon
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Dubbing_(filmmaking)" title="Dubbing (filmmaking)">
       dubbed
      </a>
      in English from June 1, 1987 until April 2, 1993. It also aired in Greece, Italy, France, the United Kingdom, in the
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      -speaking world and other countries, but its biggest success by far was in the United States on Nickelodeon. Production of the English and French versions of the series was done by the Canadian studio
      <a href="/wiki/Cookie_Jar_Group#Cinar" title="Cookie Jar Group">
       Cinar Films
      </a>
      . The storyline revolved around Roo-bear Koala and his friends and their
      <a href="/wiki/Utopia" title="Utopia">
       utopian
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Village" title="Village">
       village
      </a>
      in a fictional version of
      <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">
       New South Wales
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
       Australia
      </a>
      , within the shadow of a real rock formation known as
      <a href="/wiki/The_Breadknife" title="The Breadknife">
       The Breadknife
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_background">
       Historical background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adventures_of_the_Little_Koala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historical background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1984, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tama_Zoo" title="Tama Zoo">
       Tama Zoo
      </a>
      in western
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">
       Tokyo
      </a>
      welcomed its first koala, and the government of Australia sent six koalas to Japan as a token of goodwill. As a result, according to
      <i>
       The Anime Encyclopedia
      </i>
      by Jonathan Clements and Helen McCarthy, Japan went into a koala frenzy and anything to do with koalas became very popular. It was during this "koala-mania" that
      <i>
       The Adventures of the Little Koala
      </i>
      was made, as was another anime on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fuji_TV" title="Fuji TV">
       Fuji TV
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Animation" title="Nippon Animation">
       Nippon Animation
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       Fushigi na Koala Blinky
      </i>
      , which would later be broadcast alongside
      <i>
       Little Koala
      </i>
      on Nickelodeon's Nick Jr. block in 1988, as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Noozles" title="Noozles">
        Noozles
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production_Notes">
       Production Notes
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adventures_of_the_Little_Koala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production Notes">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Japanese animation studio
      <a href="/wiki/Topcraft" title="Topcraft">
       Topcraft
      </a>
      , best known for providing animation duties on many of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rankin-Bass" title="Rankin-Bass">
       Rankin-Bass
      </a>
      ' American animated TV specials as well as the feature film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Last_Unicorn" title="The Last Unicorn">
        The Last Unicorn
       </a>
      </i>
      , worked on
      <i>
       The Adventures of the Little Koala.
      </i>
      Topcraft provided animation assistance for
      <a href="/wiki/Hayao_Miyazaki" title="Hayao Miyazaki">
       Hayao Miyazaki
      </a>
      's 1984 feature film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Nausica%C3%A4_of_the_Valley_of_the_Wind_(film)" title="Nausica of the Valley of the Wind (film)">
        Nausica of the Valley of the Wind
       </a>
      </i>
      ; in fact, some of the animators who worked on
      <i>
       Little Koala
      </i>
      also worked on
      <i>
       Nausicaa
      </i>
      . After
      <i>
       Nausicaa
      </i>
      , a number of Topcraft animators went on to work for Miyazaki's
      <a href="/wiki/Studio_Ghibli" title="Studio Ghibli">
       Studio Ghibli
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Some notable animators who worked on this series included
      <a href="/wiki/Katsuhisa_Yamada" title="Katsuhisa Yamada">
       Katsuhisa Yamada
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hidekazu_Ohara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hidekazu Ohara (page does not exist)">
       Hidekazu Ohara
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media_release">
       Home media release
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adventures_of_the_Little_Koala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Home media release">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1989,
      <a href="/wiki/Family_Home_Entertainment" title="Family Home Entertainment">
       Family Home Entertainment
      </a>
      released the show on a single VHS cassette entitled
      <i>
       The Adventures of the Little Koala and Friends: Laura and the Mystery Egg
      </i>
      containing four episodes without their title cards which are "Laura Finds an Egg", "Conquering Mt. Breadknife", "Saving the Eucalyptus", and "Mommy Can Fly". As of May 11, 2014, neither
      <a href="/wiki/DHX_Media" title="DHX Media">
       DHX Media
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/CBS_Home_Entertainment" title="CBS Home Entertainment">
       CBS Home Entertainment
      </a>
      announced plans for a DVD release of the series.
     </p>
