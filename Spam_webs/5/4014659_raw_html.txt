             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://ifitshipitshere.blogspot.gr/2012/03/imaginative-and-innovative-architecture.html">
               Imaginative Architecture. The Fiction Series by Photographer Filip Dujardin.
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <script src="http://w.sharethis.com/button/sharethis.js#publisher=0d650ffe-1ec7-4395-883b-24e806d21a65&amp;type=website" type="text/javascript">
              </script>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DC0LmxZ9aPw/T2ADl_0XAZI/AAAAAAACHrg/U9lfniOqgCY/s1600/Filip%2BDujardin%2Bhero1.jpg">
               <img alt="" border="0" height="530" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5719575478081618322" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DC0LmxZ9aPw/T2ADl_0XAZI/AAAAAAACHrg/U9lfniOqgCY/s640/Filip%2BDujardin%2Bhero1.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-size: 85%;">
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                above: one of Filip Dujardin's architecture composite photographs
               </span>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Fast Company ran a
              <a href="http://www.fastcodesign.com/1669210/a-wonderfully-surreal-world-where-buildings-are-shaped-like-guns-and-french-fries">
               post
              </a>
              the other day on their
              <a href="http://www.fastcodesign.com/">
               Co.Design site
              </a>
              , inspired by the wonderful design blog,
              <a href="http://www.dezeen.com/2012/02/25/city-portraits-by-victor-enrich/">
               Dezeen
              </a>
              , about the work of
              <a href="http://victorenrich.com/">
               Victor Enrich
              </a>
              . Enrich is a Spanish photographer who photoshops together various pieces of architecture together to create surreal buildings shaped like french fries and guns, an example of which is shown below.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-j6nGTPS8vwU/T2AAWj_SjUI/AAAAAAACHrI/8zgwXKOHtc4/s1600/victor%2Benrich%2Bdefense%2Bfastcodesign.jpg">
               <img alt="" border="0" height="640" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5719571914378349890" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-j6nGTPS8vwU/T2AAWj_SjUI/AAAAAAACHrI/8zgwXKOHtc4/s640/victor%2Benrich%2Bdefense%2Bfastcodesign.jpg" width="640"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-size: 85%;">
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                above: Victor Enrich's Defense, a gun shaped building, is one of the images in Co.Design's article.
               </span>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              This instantly reminded me of the work of Belgium commercial photographer
              <span style="font-weight: bold;">
               Filip Dujardin
              </span>
              , who basically does the same sort of digital collage, only in a less whimsical or humorous fashion and with a better eye for composition.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Since many of my readers also browse
              <a href="http://www.fastcompany.com/">
               FastCo.
              </a>
              and
              <a href="http://www.dezeen.com/">
               Dezeen
              </a>
              , I thought they might like to see another - and in my opinion, better- version of similarly constructed photographs.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Shown here are the 22 images (including the one at the top of the post) from the Fiction series by Filip Dujardin.
