                 THE NIGHT I SPENT IN JAIL
                </a>
               </h2>
               <p class="blog-date">
                <span class="date-text">
                 2/4/2015

                 <script>
                  (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({}); &lt;/script&gt; &lt;/div&gt; &lt;/div&gt;  &lt;div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;"&gt; And it was the most incredible night of my life thus far and here's the story:&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;I just turned 33 a little while ago. People have walked all over me for most of my life. When I was a child I was picked on, rarely included (sometimes I was and it was great), teased, but not only rarely did it cross into bullying. My parents didn't allow to really watch TV and they heavily monitored what I watched. I was allowed to watch Cosby Show and cartoons and I Love Lucy, but only before noon on weekends. I was sheltered from violence and sex and it really hurt my social life. Kids thought I was weird for not knowing what the shows Seinfeld and Friends were.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;They did allow to read whatever I wanted though (they didn't seem to realize how trashy books could get) and I read a lot of trashy mystery novels with tons of teen sexy fun time and senseless violence.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;As I got older I got involved with people who were bad for me. They never cared about what I wanted. They used me as more of a therapist, for pot and for money. They liked how I made them feel, but listening to me was too much when I had my own set of problems. I was diagnosed severely bipolar and had severe anxiety and due to a misdiagnoses and being given the wrong meds I had a combination severely extreme manic episode/nervous breakdown which resulted in having the cops called on me and having them escort me to a hospital. My life changed forever that night (the cops were very fair though. They could have taken me to jail for what I did, but they went easy on me).&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;Anyway, this ruined my relationship with my parents. They took away my car keys, my debit card (even though I was 22 and had earned that money myself), they shamed me for spending my hard earned money the way I saw fit and never trusted me ever again. Even though I was incredibly responsible, they never forgave me for not being like them.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;They shipped me off to another state to a mental hospital, un-escorted. They didn't come with me because they said that was a waste of time. I was the only patient I knew there to arrive alone. Well, the first place didn't "fix me" to their satisfaction, so they shipped me off somewhere else.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;All that place did was tell me how normal I was and said I could go. I begged them to let me stay, because they were better to me than my parents. My parents never wanted to hear about my problems, still don't. They make me feel ashamed when I'm happy and bully me when I'm depressed. All they talk about is how difficult it is to have a difficult child who has mental illnesses. They're inconsiderate pussies.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;Anyway, about jail. I landed in jail when a guy was beating me and I defended myself.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;But here's the twist: The cops were great to me. They listened to me, they let me talk and instead of shaming me like everyone else, they made me feel good. They complimented me. They said I had balls, that I did the right thing.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;I did everything you're not supposed to do in jail, I learned later on. I joked around with the cops and made them laugh (I lucked out because they never read me my rights, so what I said I don't believe could be held against me, but I could be wrong about that).&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;They were really nice to me, they didn't make me feel crazy. They were polite, I didn't feel like criminal. They treated me like a human being.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;That night I spent in jail made me realize that I will no longer stand for people treating me like garbage and using me and neglecting me. I've still got problems and stuff, but spending the night in jail made me realize I'm not crazy, that the other people in my life just abused me.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; And, quite frankly, I'm not taking any form of abuse ever again. (I'm not going to do anything violent or anything, I'm just going to walk away from anybody who treats me like shit and never look back). I'm perfectly open to constructive criticism overall but I am no longer letting people make feel worthless. I've got a new life to begin and I'm so fucking happy and excited.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;P.S. I forgot to add a big old fashioned "FUCK YOU MOM AND DAD FOR NEVER ONCE GIVING ME A SINCERE APOLOGY FOR TREATING ME LIKE SHIT"&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;I may love you guys, but I don't especially like you (and you're pretty boring).&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;The whole time I was in jail, the cops told me to stop saying sorry. That I had nothing to be sorry for, because I had done almost nothing wrong. That I just had a bad day and every once in a while, people have bad days.&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;(Oh yeah and my case was dismissed)&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; &lt;span style=""&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;br&gt; ***Please understand that I am working on improving my relationship with my parents, we are trying to improve things.&lt;br&gt; &lt;br&gt; NOTE: PLEASE DO NOT BABBLE ON LIKE I DID WHILE IN JAIL. &nbsp;YOU HAVE THE RIGHT TO REMAIN SILENT, I'M VERY LUCKY I DIDN'T GET IN SERIOUS TROUBLE FOR NOT REALIZING WHAT MY RIGHTS WERE. &lt;/div&gt;  &lt;div&gt; &lt;div id="665744105234042391" align="center" style="width: 100%; overflow-y: hidden;" class="wcustomhtml"&gt; &lt;script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js"&gt;
