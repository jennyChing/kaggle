           <h1 class="post-title single">
            <a href="http://mccaccountants.co.uk/advice/review-ir35-hmrc-says-nothing-effectiveness-according-mcc-accountants/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Review of IR35 by HMRC says nothing about its effectiveness according to MCC Accountants">
             Review of IR35 by HMRC says nothing about its effectiveness according to MCC Accountants
            </a>
           </h1>
           <div class="meta single">
            <span class="meta-author">
             <a href="http://mccaccountants.co.uk/author/mistoria/" rel="author" title="Posts by Mish Liyanage">
              Mish Liyanage
             </a>
             
            </span>
            <span class="meta-date">
             January 20, 2015
            </span>
            <span class="meta-comments">
             
             <a href="http://mccaccountants.co.uk/advice/review-ir35-hmrc-says-nothing-effectiveness-according-mcc-accountants/#comments" title="Comments for Review of IR35 by HMRC says nothing about its effectiveness according to MCC Accountants">
              0 Comments

           <p>
            <strong>
             IR 35 Rule
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1364" style="width: 310px">
            <img alt="IR 35" class="wp-image-1364 size-medium" height="187" src="http://mccaccountants.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/HMRC_2293694b-300x187.jpg" width="300"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             IR 35
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Contractors remain effectively in the dark about how effective HMRCs implementation of IR35 has been since the administration review in 2012 commissioned by the Office of Tax Simplification (OTS) and overseen by the IR35 Forum.
           </p>
           <p>
            In the HMRC IR35 Forum Administration Review, the authorities talkmuch abouthow theystill have to improve, but provides no data about how effective the measures introduced in May 2012 have been.
           </p>
           <p>
            HMRC continues to believe that contractors are, for the large part,unaware of IR35 and that there is still considerable work to do to educate independent professionals about the legislation.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             MCC Accounatnts Response  how to show the taxman you mean business
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Mish Liyanage, Managing Partner of MCC states that Review of IR35 administration by HMRC says nothing about its effectiveness. Contractors remain effectively in the dark about how effective HMRCs implementation of IR35 has been since the administration review in 2012 commissioned by the Office of Tax Simplification (OTS) and overseen by the IR35 Forum.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            In the IR35 Forum Administration Review, HMRC talks much about how it still has to improve but provides no data about how effective the measures introduced in May 2012 have been.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             How does IR35 work?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            The team that looks after intermediaries legislation (IR35s official tag) targets specific individuals or agencies it believes are inside IR35.
           </p>
           <p>
            Whilst there are no doubt signals the team looks for, anyone can become the subject of such an investigation. Once identified, the taxman will begin to create an overall picture of the way you trade.
           </p>
           <p>
            One of the many factors they take into account is the relationship that exists between client and contractor. Moreover, they will try to ascertain whether there was a pre-existing relationship before the engagement began.
           </p>
           <p>
            Each element of your business goes under the taxmans microscope. If the way you work builds a picture that resembles an employee/employer relationship, youre sailing too close to the wind.
           </p>
           <p>
            How to ensure you look like a contractor, not an employee
           </p>
           <p>
            If youre an umbrella contractor, the boffins whove set up the way they pay you should have the answers for you. If said employer isnt compliant, it shouldnt be in business, after all.
           </p>
           <p>
            For those contracting through their own Limited Company, theres plenty you can do to make sure IR35 teams have no doubt about your trading status,
            <em>
             i.e.
            </em>
            you
            <em>
             are
            </em>
            a bona fide limited business.
           </p>
           <p>
            A few of the things you can do to give the taxman a positive perspective of the way you trade are:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <em>
              Do
             </em>
             work for more than just one client;
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Do
             </em>
             engage an accountant with unquestionable knowledge of the way contracting works  true whether youre worried about IR35 or not;
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Do
             </em>
             market your business;
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Do
             </em>
             order bespoke business stationary, branded, of course;
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Do
             </em>
             engage in relevant industry forums and communities as your brand;
            </li>
            <li>
             <em>
              Do
             </em>
             try to build relationships with peers: one of the most failsafe ways to prove that youre a business is to be able to substitute yourself with
             <em>
              n.other
             </em>
             if you, for whatever reason, cannot undertake the current task.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            To clarify this contractor conundrum further,
            <a href="http://www.freelancerfinancials.uk.com/guides/ir-35-compliance/what-ir-35/">
             the complete IR35 guide
            </a>
            explains our take on the subject in greater depth. It
            <strong>
             will
            </strong>
            help you ensure that even if the taxman does come a-calling, youre ready for him.
           </p>
           <p>
            If youre already under investigation, keep working. However,
            <strong>
             do
            </strong>
            try to find an accountant if youve not already done so. They may not be able to throw you a lifeline this time, but will show you how best to deal with your current circumstances and avoid them in the future
           </p>
           <p>
            MCC Accountants are accountants based in Salford that specialise in small business accounting and tax advice offering services such as financial, payroll and company support. If you have any clarifications on IR35 and whether that it will effect your small business and you would like some advice we will be happy to help, call us on 0161 707 1500 for more information
           </p>
