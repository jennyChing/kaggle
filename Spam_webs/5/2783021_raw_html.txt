        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Rosemary Butternut Squash Skillet Pie
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-11-10T06:00:28+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          November 10, 2014
         </time>
         
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/rosemary-butternut-squash-skillet-pie/#comments">
           15 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14695787" data-link="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/rosemary-butternut-squash-skillet-pie/" data-summary="" data-title="Rosemary Butternut Squash Skillet Pie">
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          This rosemary butternut squash skillet pie is like a mix between a squash gratin and pie. It comes together in minutes and bakes up as a festive fall side dish.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         If things pan out like theyre supposed to, Im going to be on a tropical island for two weeks later this month, including over Thanksgiving. Its hard to really have anything negative to say about a trip to the tropics as the temperatures plunge and my skin turns pastier by the day, but when it coincides with the best eating day of the entire year, theres a bit of sadness buried inside me somewhere.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im sure it will quickly go away when Im poolside with a delicious alcoholic beverage in hand though.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Rosemary butternut squash skillet pie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10448" height="848" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Rosemary-butternut-squash-skillet-pie-1-optimized.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         So, to make up for a missed holiday, Im eating like its Thanksgiving for the next two weeks starting with this butternut squash skillet pie.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Buttternut squash skillet pie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10449" height="800" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Rosemary-butternut-squash-skillet-pie-2-optimized.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The idea of using a pancake mix outside of breakfast has always been a bit weird to me. Weird and very southern American. Im about as far from Southern as they come yet here I am with a cast iron skillet and side dish that uses pancake mix.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-10446">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Rosemary butternut squash pie" class="alignnone wp-image-10450 size-full" height="800" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Rosemary-butternut-squash-skillet-pie-3-optimized.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This pie is like a gratin meets bread pudding meets side dish. Its pretty much the perfect addition to your Thanksgiving table and it couldnt be easier to make; mix everything together, pour into a skillet and bake. Its a savory flavor explosion and while Im not sure it outdoes a tropical island in the middle of winter, its close.
         <br/>
        </p>

         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          This rosemary butternut squash skillet pie is like a mix between a squash gratin and pie. It comes together in minutes and bakes up as a festive fall side dish.
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Gina Matsoukas
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6-8
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 cups thinly sliced butternut squash
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 small yellow onion, thinly sliced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup extra virgin olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup whole milk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tablespoons heavy cream
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tablespoons chopped rosemary
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 clove garlic, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup grated parmesan
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup pancake/baking mix (Bisquick or something similar)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            salt and pepper
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add butternut squash and onion to a large bowl.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Whisk together the eggs, olive oil, milk, heavy cream, rosemary and garlic in another bowl.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add parmesan and pancake/baking mix to the bowl with the butternut squash and toss to combine.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour the liquid mixture into the large bowl with the butternut squash and mix until combined.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the salt and pepper to taste and mix again.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Transfer the batter to a
            <a href="http://amzn.to/10I5uUh" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             10 inch cast iron skillet
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place skillet in the oven and bake for about 30 minutes until cooked through and the top starts to turn golden brown.
           </li>
          </ol>
