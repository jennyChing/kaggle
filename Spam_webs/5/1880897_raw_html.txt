        <span class="next-link">
         <a href="http://freethoughtblogs.com/pharyngula/2014/07/11/ah-sweet-schadenfreude/">
          Ah, sweet Schadenfreude
         </a>
         
        </span>
        <span class="previous-link">
         
         <a href="http://freethoughtblogs.com/pharyngula/2014/07/11/todd-akin-is-sorry-that-he-was-sorry/">
          Todd Akin is sorry that he was sorry
         </a>
        </span>
        <div style="clear: both">
        </div>
       </div>
       <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
        Anyone can be director of an institute of pseudoscience, I guess
       </h1>
      </header>
      <hr/>
      <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
       <p class="lead">
        So the
        <a href="http://edroso.tumblr.com/post/91358173948/this-insane-letter-links-to-this-insane-video">
         Washington Times is disseminating quackery
        </a>
         nothing new about that, I guess. But this is pretty awful stuff.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://freethoughtblogs.com/pharyngula/files/2014/07/jennylies.jpg">
         <img alt="jennylies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-17112" height="449" src="//freethoughtblogs.com/pharyngula/files/2014/07/jennylies.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        You have to suffer to find out what she is talking about:
        <a href="http://www.hsionlineorders.net/Obama/PT-PP/Transcript/?pco=LHSIQ711&amp;efo=HSI140519A&amp;xco=XHSIQ711">
         this link takes you to a canned presentation
        </a>
        . Youve seen these before, I presume. They use a custom video player with all the controls stripped out, so once it gets started, you have to go through the whole thing, and you cant skip ahead. And it goes on and on, hammering away at how evil modern medicine is and how it will kill you and stirring up fear and anger before it gets to the point: they have a cure for cancer, but Obama and the medical establishment are hiding it from you, and you can buy it from them. Wheee. So predictable.
       </p>
       <div id="div-gpt-ad-1421869288950-0" style="width: 100%; max-width:728px; height:90px; margin: 0 auto; clear: both;">
        <script type="text/javascript">
         googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1421869288950-0'); });
        </script>
       </div>
       <div style="clear: both">
       </div>
       <p>
        Their miracle cure is a fermented wheat germ extract, marketed as a dietary supplement. Its called H-86 or Avemar. Contrary to their claims that Obama and the FDA are trying to silence its existence because they want you to die a horrible painful death, its an object of active research. Ive seen one clinical trial that showed a significant improvement in survival time for melanoma patients; there are a number of papers that investigate it and try to identify the mechanism (it may stimulate apoptosis in some cancer cells).
       </p>
       <p>
        So its one among many. Its got some promising preliminary results  but those are dime a dozen, Im afraid, and many drugs that are initially interesting dont pan out  and scientists are exploring it and publishing on it. It doesnt magically vaporize tumors. The melanoma patients eventually died, but their average survival was prolonged. Thats the reality of cancer research.
       </p>
       <p>
        But I was intrigued by the Health Sciences Institute. Thats a name with authority, and it sounds like a real thing. When you dig a little deeper, though, it seems to be little more than a
        <a href="http://hsionline.com/about-jenny-thompson/">
         website and Jenny Thompson
        </a>
        , who has a bachelors degree in journalism and has appointed herself director of her very own sciencey institute of research and medicine.
       </p>
       <p>
        Ive been missing a trick. I need to declare Pharyngula to be a research think tank, start issuing dozens of e-alerts every month, and promote more FUD to draw in the massive donations so I can afford to advertise in the Washington Times, or the Daily Mail, or whatever hack rag with no standards will accept my money.
       </p>
       <p>
        I just need a good title. Pharyngula is probably too obscure. Give me some ideas for convincing masks for pseudoscience, so I can get on that gravy train.
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Jenny Thompson is a very naughty person
        </strong>
        . I did not sign up for anything on her website, and I didnt even leave my email address anywhere therebut she went ahead and signed me up for her HSI e-alerts anyway. Thats OK, though, Ive also put her HSI email address in my spam filters, so were even.
       </p>
