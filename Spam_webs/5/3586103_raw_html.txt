         <div class="post-title">
          <h1 class="title" itemprop="headline">
           <a href="https://www.ybinteractive.com/blog/10-mistakes-can-kill-ecommerce-site/">
            10 Mistakes that Can Kill Your Ecommerce Site
           </a>
          </h1>
         </div>
         <!-- end:Post Title -->
         <div class="post-meta">
          <ul>
           <li>
            Posted by
            <a href="https://www.ybinteractive.com/blog/author/apollino" rel="author">
             Anthony Pollino
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span>
             In
             <a href="https://www.ybinteractive.com/blog/category/e-commerce/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in E-Commerce">
              E-Commerce
             </a>
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <!-- end:Post Meta -->
         <div class="post-entry" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           As events like the
           <a href="http://pghmakerfaire.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://pghmakerfaire.com']);">
            Pittsburgh Maker Faire
           </a>
           and
           <a href="http://www.afairinthepark.org/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.afairinthepark.org']);">
            A Fair in the Park
           </a>
           demonstrate, Pittsburgh is a crafty city. With entrepreneurs and craftsmen constantly emerging in our local area, smart ecommerce website design in Pittsburghis becoming more critical for starting businesses. Both to gain client trust and stay ahead of the competition, there are a number of design elements your site should have as well as a number of mistakes your site should avoid on your ecommerce website.
          </p>
          <h2>
           1) Insufficient Product Information
          </h2>
          <p>
           People want to know what they are buying. Because customers cannot handle the products in person, they are relying on you to supply information for them. Give as much product details as possible, including, size, weight, material, origin of manufacturer, color (and color options), etc. Accompanying descriptions of fit, feel, or look may also have greater influence on customers than supplying purely technical details.
          </p>
          <h2>
           2) Poor Product Pictures
          </h2>
          <p>
           The product picture is one of the most telling components within any products profile. At a glance, a customer will form a positive or negative opinion on the product based on this one element alone. Make sure that the product is well-displayed so that customers can get an accurate idea of how it will look in their homes, as they are wearing it, etc. Also be sure to provide multiple views of the product with different angles and close-ups of details.
          </p>
          <h2>
           3) Missing/Hidden Contact Information
          </h2>
          <p>
           When people input credit card information on a site, they want to know that they are not being scammed. Placing your contact information in a conspicuous spot on your web pages will help gain your clients trust. In their minds, having this information easily accessible makes the company accountable. There is someone they can reach in case something goes wrong or if something needs to be changed with the order.
          </p>
          <h2>
           4) Long Checkout Process
          </h2>
          <p>
           The more time there is between selecting a product and closing the transaction, the more time customers have to preemptively feel buyers remorse and back out of the sale. When dealing with a long or tedious checkout, that feeling combined with the new frustration of an annoying checkout may be enough to convince the customer to forgo the purchase entirely. Keep checkout as simple and streamlined as possible. Save profile updates and questionnaires for afterward.
          </p>
          <h2>
           5) Requiring an Account
          </h2>
          <p>
           First, this could contribute to too long of a checkout. Second, in todays environment of database hacking, information security is a significant question in some peoples minds. Requiring an account may drive them to another supplier they know will not hang on to their information.
          </p>
          <h2>
           6) Not Allowing Account Creation
          </h2>
          <p>
           In opposition to the previous point, some people prefer to have their information stored so that they do not have to re-enter addresses and credit card information on the sites they frequent. People who visit your site multiple times may be annoyed that they must input the same data every time. To address both this and the previous mistake, ecommerce websites should have account creation optional, but not mandatory.
          </p>
          <h2>
           7) Implementing a Confusing Navigation
          </h2>
          <p>
           When customers are puzzling through clunky navigation, they arent engaging with your products. The longer it takes them to move between pages, the less interest they will have in the products themselves, and the faster they will leave your site entirely. Simplify your navigation and make it as logical as possible. As a backup, make sure you also have an accurate search tool.
          </p>
          <h2>
           8) Leaving Out Reviews
          </h2>
          <p>
           Social signals are huge in closing a sale. Seeing social media shares, customer ratings, and user comments is important in assuring potential buyers that they are getting the quality product they are looking for. Often reviews address user concerns better than your own product descriptions do.
          </p>
          <h2>
           9) Using Unoriginal Product Descriptions
          </h2>
          <p>
           While skimpy product descriptions will be unappealing to users, unoriginal product descriptions (such as from a manufacturers description) will look bad in the eyes of search engines. The original publisher of a piece of content will get the credit for it. To reduce duplicate results, search engines will push down or remove pages with duplicate content from the rankings. With enough duplicate content, your website may even receive a penalty. Additionally, you can bet other retailers will be relying on manufacturer descriptions, so with unoriginal content, you are trying to outcompete sites with the same information that they have. Be sure to not only provide detailed product descriptions, but make sure theyre also original.
          </p>
          <h2>
           10) Not Displaying Related Products
          </h2>
          <p>
           An easy way to increase sales on ecommerce sites is to show related products. When people look at items on ecommerce websites, theyre already looking to close. The chances are high that theyre receptive to more product offers. For example, if a customer is looking for a food mixer, show attachments that could go with the product or cookbooks designed for the product. This feature can be used to either automatically populate related products, or website owners can manually add products in that software may not necessarily connect.
          </p>
          <p>
           What ecommerce design mistakes have you seen?
          </p>
