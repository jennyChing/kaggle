        <h1>
         The smallest Computer in the World
        </h1>
        <!-- ORIG 
    &lt;iframe src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http://www.voodish.co.uk/articles/the-smallest-computer-in-the-world/&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=false&amp;width=450&amp;action=like&amp;colorsscheme=light" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowtransparency="true" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:530px; height:24px"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;

        <div class="entry" style="position:relative; z-index:2;">
         <div id="imgLeft2">
          <a href="http://www.voodish.co.uk/articles/the-smallest-computer-in-the-world/">
           <img alt="pt112x.jpg" src="http://www.voodish.co.uk/articles/v-content/uploads/2007/11/pt112x.thumbnail.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          Only slightly larger than an RJ45 connector (35mm19mm19mm), the picotux 100 is the worlds smallest Linux computer.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a id="more-298">
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Inside, there is an ARM7 CPU at 55 MHz running uClinux kernel 2.4.27 and Busybox 1.0. Two communication interfaces are provided, 10/100 Mbit half/full duplex Ethernet and a serial port with up to 230.400 baud. Five additional lines can be used for either general input/output or serial handshaking.
         </p>
