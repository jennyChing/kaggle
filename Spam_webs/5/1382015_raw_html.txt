                         <p>
                          PHOENIX - A brawl on the field has forced two Valley high schools to forfeit their final football games of the season.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          The beef centered around a special needs player, Vincent Stover, who is autistic and on the Mountain Ridge football team.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          After a good lead Thursday night against North Canyon High School, the coach put Vincent in the game on defense.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          A video clip of the game shows North Canyon hike the ball and Vincent gets tackled into his own end zone.
                         </p>
                         <div class="OUTBRAIN p402_hide" data-ob-template="ABC15" data-src="http://www.abc15.com/news/region-phoenix-metro/north-phoenix/football-brawl-ends-badly-for-two-valley-teams" data-widget-id="MB_2" id="mM-OUTBRAIN-INLINE-STORY">
                         </div>
                         <script>
                          ScrippsAdUtils.detectShowOutbrain('mM-OUTBRAIN-INLINE-STORY', '[MOBILE]', true);
                         </script>
                         <p>
                          "The tight end pushed me so hard I fell to the ground," Vincent recalls.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          One of his teammates saw the tackle and took off after the other player.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Vincent said both benches cleared after that.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          "I didn't know what was going on, I looked back, and a fight happened," he said.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Vincents mom, Terrie Silva, said she understands young hormones can get the best of teenagers, especially if their team is losing, but she says the whole incident is unacceptable.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          "There's no excuse to take it out on a player whether he has what he has or not," Silva said.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Silva said the other coaches know Vincent is a special needs player who has been on the team for three years.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          The Paradise Valley School District says they didnt know.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          Both schools sent these statements:
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          <strong>
                           Paradise Valley Unified School District:
                          </strong>
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          On Oct. 30 during the North Canyon High School vs. Mountain Ridge High School varsity football game, a Mountain Ridge player became aggressive toward several North Canyon players after North Canyon tackled one of their players during a play. This resulted in players from both schools rushing to the field and engaging in inappropriate conduct.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          The Arizona Interscholastic Association (AIA) sanctions football, and AIA rules prohibit players from leaving the bench or engaging in inappropriate conduct. Both teams must forfeit the game and record a loss. Both districts are meeting early next week to discuss the incident and will then meet with AIA officials to find out whether there will be any additional consequences.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          <strong>
                           Deer Valley Unified School District:
                          </strong>
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          On October 30, during a varsity football game between Mountain Ridge High School (MRHS) and North Canyon High School (NCHS), an incident occurred involving aggressive behavior. A MRHS player was tackled by a NCHS player after a game official ended the play.
                         </p>
                         <p>
                          A MRHS player then tackled the NCHS player who took part in the initial tackle after the play had concluded. These incidents led to players for both schools rushing to the field and engaging in inappropriate conduct. Football is an Arizona Interscholastic Association (AIA) sanctioned sport and all players are required to follow the rules or regulations outlined by the AIA. All MRHS players involved in this matter will be prohibited from paying in the next AIA sanctioned activity or event
                         </p>
