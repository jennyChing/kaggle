     <p>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       richpeoplethings shapes surfboard from recycled cork stoppers
       <br/>
      </strong>
      all images courtesy of richpeoplethings
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       <a href="http://www.richpeoplethings.org/" target="_blank">
        richpeoplethings
       </a>
      </strong>
      is a social enterprise located in murcia, spain that was born from the idea of suggesting creative alternatives to an otherwise difficult current situation. the company states,
      <em>
       our mission is to create value-added products from raw material that would otherwise be value-less waste. by the name of our company we also want to break taboos and cliches; why when we talk about richness we think about money?
      </em>
      <strong>
       with this goal in mind, the brand has developed a project titled connecting the corks.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <div class="video-container">
      <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="545" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/FF3gOOn8ddM" width="818">
      </iframe>
     </div>
     <br/>
     video courtesy of richpeoplethings
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       following the objective to offer clean alternatives, recycle, and to make use of components that would otherwise be wasted, the initiative has led to a surfboard of cork stoppers.
      </strong>
      based on their observations, the designers note that
      <em>
       just in spain, more than 2500 tons of cork stoppers are thrown away every year. the incredible fiscal and structural properties of the cork make it ideal to build surfboards from it surfboard and to stand the marine environment.
      </em>
      as a result, throughout the construction of each product, adhesives such as pine resin bees wax provide the base while natural fibers and bioresin are applied as finishes.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="richpeoplethings cork surfboard designboom" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-241667" height="500" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/richpeoplethings-cork-surfboard-designboom-05.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      detail of the structure
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       the enclosed corks have their personality and place in the board.
      </strong>
      they are placed together six by six, building hexagons in a manner similar to the formations of bees and their honeycombs.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="richpeoplethings cork surfboard designboom" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-241672" height="500" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/richpeoplethings-cork-surfboard-designboom-11.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      the cork pattern results in a perforated pattern
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="richpeoplethings cork surfboard designboom" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-241673" height="500" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/richpeoplethings-cork-surfboard-designboom-12.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      the board on the water
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="richpeoplethings cork surfboard designboom" height="500" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/richpeoplethings-cork-surfboard-designboom-04.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      the board is illuminated by light
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="richpeoplethings cork surfboard designboom" height="500" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/richpeoplethings-cork-surfboard-designboom-06.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      board in progress
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="richpeoplethings cork surfboard designboom" height="500" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/richpeoplethings-cork-surfboard-designboom-08.jpg" width="818"/>
