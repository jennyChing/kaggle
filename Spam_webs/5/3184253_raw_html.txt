      <p>
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <em>
         <strong>
          Argentina vs. Iran
         </strong>
        </em>
       </span>
       : Many assumed this game would be a cakewalk for the Argentines, but it was far from that. For 90 minutes, both Argentina and Iran had their chances, but neither could convert. Argentina had the better of the chances, but they didnt have anything to show for it. Argentina would keep knocking at the door, and in the 90+1 they finally got the breakthrough they so craved. Lionel Messi cut in from the top of the box and curled one in to give Argentina the win, three points, and secure passage to the knockout rounds. For Iran, theyll have to beat Bosnia and need Nigeria to lose for them to have any chance of advancing.
       <span id="more-642">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <em>
         <strong>
          Ghana vs. Germany
         </strong>
        </em>
       </span>
       : When Germany and Ghana locked horns on Saturday, it surely didnt disappoint. The game overall was absolutely phenomenal. The first half, however, was pretty abysmal. For 45 minutes, no goals were scored, nobody threatened, and the fans grew restless. I dont know what kind of half time speech each side received, but they came out firing in the second half. Germany opened the scoring in the 51 with a Mario Gotze header. The Ghanaians would not be held down for long, scoring a mere three minutes later through another header, this time by Andre Ayew. Less than ten minutes after equalizing, Ghana delivered another shockwave and took the lead against the Germans. This time it 2010 World Cup hero/villain Asamoah Gyan who put the Black Stars ahead. Ghana would have a few more quality opportunities, but couldnt convert. Unfortunately for them in the 71 German veteran Miroslav Klose scored to bring the Germans level. It was his 15
       <sup>
        th
       </sup>
       goal all-time and ties up with the most goals at the World Cup with Ronaldo. The game would end 2-2 and rightfully so. The game was very fair and balanced and both teams definitely earned a share of the spoils. Group G has now been busted wide open and everyone is eager to see how America performs tomorrow.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <em>
         <strong>
          Nigeria vs. Bosnia &amp; Herzegovina
         </strong>
        </em>
       </span>
       : The final game of the day pitted two teams in a must win scenario. If either team had any hope of reaching the knockout rounds they needed a win. Bosnia had the better of the chances even scoring a goal that was disallowed for offside (It wasnt offside in the slightest, the refs really butchered that one). Nigeria would make the most of the referees gift scoring in the 29. From that moment on, Nigeria realized they needed to play airtight defense to hold on and receive maximum points. Towards the end of the game, Bosnia had many chances to score, but Nigeria were able to hang on for the victory.
      </p>
