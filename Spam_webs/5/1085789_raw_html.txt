             <p style="text-align: left;">
              Welcome to Christmas Week aka Holiday Baking Week aka lets stuff our faces with butter, sugar, chocolate and candy week because its December and calories dont count week.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              Seriously, they dont, at least if I keep telling myself that over and over it could potentially be true (plus I havent been to the gym in 3 weeks, so I need it to be so).
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              Also welcome to Susans birthday celebration week; so kind of Kim to kick off this giant celebration in my honor isnt it? I kid, its really not for me, its for you, because lets be honest I bet you still have holiday baking to finish up on that long list of yours.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a href="http://girlinthelittleredkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Monster-Cookie-Cake.jpg">
               <img alt="Monster Cookie Cake |The Girl In The Little Red Kitchen" class="aligncenter wp-image-13097872329 size-full pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://girlinthelittleredkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Monster-Cookie-Cake.jpg" data-pin-url="http://girlinthelittleredkitchen.com/2014/12/monster-cookie-cake/" height="850" src="http://girlinthelittleredkitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Monster-Cookie-Cake.jpg" width="567"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              While yes, technically recipes youll find today through Friday are holiday centric  today  its all about me.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              So I made myself a birthday cake, which can absolutely double as a fun and easy Christmas cake too. Especially if you are a fellow December birthday child. I feel you.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              Why cookie cake?
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              I will tell you why.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              First off  best of both worlds  cookie + cake. Second, you can eat it out of the pan. Third, you can drizzle caramel sauce all over the top, finish with a scoop of ice cream and sprinkles.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              You have to have the sprinkles.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              Lastly, its easy. Baking should all be about the ease during the holidays. It should be a no-brainer and fun and one you can mix together with kids.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              So grab a friend or 3 and dig into this cookie cake straight out of the oven, no need to let this baby cool down. Oh but dont do what I did and stuff it all into a tiny skillet, size up just a bit so you dont spend an hour baking something that should take 30 minutes.
              <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://girlinthelittleredkitchen.com/2014/12/monster-cookie-cake/" src="http://girlinthelittleredkitchen.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>

              <div class="ERSIngredients">
               <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
                Ingredients
               </div>
               <ul>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                 1 cup unbleached all-purpose flour
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                  cup rolled oats
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                  teaspoon baking powder
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                  teaspoon salt
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                  cup unsalted butter, room temperature
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                  cup light brown sugar
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                  cup cane sugar
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                 2 tablespoons natural peanut butter
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                 1 large egg, room temperature
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                 1 cup peanut, peanut butter or regular M&amp;M's
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
                 optional: vanilla ice cream, caramel sauce, whipped cream and sprinkles to top
                </li>
               </ul>
               <div class="ERSClear">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="ERSInstructions">
               <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
                Instructions
               </div>
               <ol>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Heat your oven to 350 degrees and have an 8 or 9 in cast iron skillet near by.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 In a medium bowl combine the flour, oats, baking powder and salt.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 In the bowl of your stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment cream the butter and sugars for 1-2 minutes. Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat until combined.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Mix in the peanut butter.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Beat in the flour in two parts until combined, making sure not to over mix the dough.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Stir in the M&amp;M's.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Scoop the dough into your skillet, smoothing into an even layer.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. The middle should be a little gooey but not completely under baked. If it seems like the cake is still raw, continue to bake in 5 minute increments.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Let cool slightly so you can handle the skillet before serving.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 If using, when ready to serve top with caramel sauce, a scoop of ice cream, whipped cream and sprinkles.
                </li>
               </ol>
