         <p dir="ltr">
          Ten years from now, ships will be equipped with smart workspaces and the use of
          <a href="http://www.augmentedrealitytrends.com/category/augmented-reality">
           augmented reality
          </a>
          technology will be prevalent. This is what Rolls-Royce foresees as the future of the bridges of ships. The company is working with the Technical Research Centre of Finland (VTT) and has created innovative designs for future release. These series of designs suggest that the future workstations will be able to recognize automatically the navigator who has sat in front of the workstations. So, seafarers in the near future will be able to use augmented reality in a major way.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          <strong>

         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          <strong>
           How will AR be used in Ships?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <div class="code-block code-block-5" style="margin:8px 0px;">
          <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <!-- artrends - 728x15 link ads -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-9569358427962532" data-ad-slot="3150122358" style="display:inline-block;width:728px;height:15px">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
         <p dir="ltr">
          As proposed by Rolls-Royce, the bridge windows could be transformed into augmented reality displays. These displays could show information on navigation, locations of other ships in the nearby area and the routes they are taking; alerts on the weather could also be displayed in these windows.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          It is being hoped that by 2025, sea navigators who are travelling on cargo ships, tugs or platform supply vessels will be able to use such technology while navigating their vessels.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          <strong>
           The Design Process
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          Rolls-Royce along with the Technical Research Centre of Finland (VTT) consulted captains of ships as well as other crewmembers while the design process was on, to get some help. Their suggestions were taken the most efficient deigns could be created.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          According to the program manager, Maaria Nuutinen, exhaustive psychological and operative study of tasks has been combined successfully with experience-driven design and the foreseeing of future technological solutions for this project. This combination in turn, has been packaged to create impressive,
          <a href="http://www.augmentedrealitytrends.com/augmented-reality/augmented-reality-puts-high-tech-twist-on-art.html">
           high-tech
          </a>
          videos. They hope that these videos will inspire the players in this field to take on solutions which are based on user-experience.
         </p>
         <p dir="ltr">
          Apart from the use of augmented reality, the team comprising of VTT and Rolls-Royce also foresee the extensive use of remote-controlled technology. The use of this technology will be more as the ultimate goal is the use of fully self-sufficient vessels. With remote-controlled technology, some functions could be tackled right from the bridge of the ship or even from any control room located on land.
         </p>
         <p>
          So, the future of the ships is most likely to be controlled in a large way by augmented reality and remote-controlled technology.
         </p>
