    <div class="container content">
     <div class="row">
      <div class="span11 header-text">
       <h1>
        Aeropress Brew Guide
       </h1>
      </div>
     </div>
    </div>
   </div>
   <div class="container" id="brew_instructions">
    <br/>
    <div class="row">
     <div class="content span12 offset1">
      <div class="row">
       <div class="span3">
        <h4>
         At a Glance
        </h4>
        <ul class="at_a_glance with_bullets">
         <li>
          Total brew time: 2:00
         </li>
         <li>
          Yield: 1 short mug
         </li>
         <li>
          Mugs Per Craft Coffee Bag: 7
         </li>
         <li>
          Cup characteristics: Thick and complex
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
       <div class="span6">
        <h4>
         What You Need
        </h4>
        <div class="row">
         <div class="span3">
          <ul class="at_a_glance with_bullets">
           <li>
            AeroPress
           </li>
           <li>
            AeroPress filter
           </li>
           <li>
            15 grams (2-3 tbsp) Craft Coffee beans
           </li>
           <li>
            240 mL water, plus additional for rinsing
           </li>
           <li>
            Kettle (gooseneck preferred)
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="span3">
          <ul class="at_a_glance with_bullets">
           <li>
            Stirring utensil
           </li>
           <li>
            Timer
           </li>
           <li>
            Mug
           </li>
           <li>
            Burr grinder (recommended)
           </li>
           <li>
            Scale (recommended)
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="row">
       <div class="span9">
        <h4>
         Before You Brew
        </h4>
        <ul class="before_you_brew with_bullets">
         <li>
          Make sure your AeroPress is clean and completely dry before using, or else the plunger tip may not be able to form a perfect seal inside the chamber.
         </li>
         <li>
          For best results, we strongly recommend starting with whole bean coffee and grinding with a burr grinder immediately before brewing. If you grind your coffee too soon, it will quickly lose many of the compounds that give it such delightful aromas and flavors.
         </li>
         <li>
          While it may seem complicated, we think using a scale actually makes brewing easier by taking any guesswork out of the equation.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="row">
       <div class="span9">
        <br/>
        <h4>
         Step by Step
        </h4>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-1" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-1-67c22192df998987392b04c2ba441a0b.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 1
         </h5>
         <p>
          Heat your water to 200 degrees by bringing it to a boil and letting it sit for 60 seconds.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-2" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-2-4e3487ea3ce3a3bb50411e0e57292869.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 2
         </h5>
         <p>
          Place the filter inside the cap and rinse with hot water for 5 seconds. Rinsing the filter helps seal it inside the cap and also gets rid of any papery taste.
         </p>
         <p>
          Discard the rinse water.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-3" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-3-15c95661b7870191e0ccdfff9d28c9a0.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 3
         </h5>
         <p>
          If using pre-ground coffee skip to Step 4. Otherwise, weigh out your whole bean coffee and grind it on a setting slightly finer than medium. See our
          <a href="/how-to-make-coffee/grind-coffee-guide">
           guide on how to grind your coffee
          </a>
          for reference.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-4" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-4-a0c73ab633bfa3d5dce6093ecb5df84d.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 4
         </h5>
         <p>
          Assemble your AeroPress in the inverted position with the cap off. Inverting the standard position keeps coffee from leaking through the filter before it's ready. Pour your ground coffee into the AeroPress chamber and gently shake it back and forth to settle the grounds.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-5" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-5-66cda4257b8cd83393a4c053e6fdc184.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 5
         </h5>
         <p>
          Time: 0:00-0:45
         </p>
         <p>
          Start your timer and pour half of your water (120 grams) evenly over the grounds. Let it sit until your timer reads 45 seconds. This step is called the bloom. Hot water forces the coffee to release trapped gases, leading to expansion of the coffee bed, bubbling at the surface, and wonderful aromas for you to enjoy.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-6" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-6-ebc4a9fb385d714ae8ac887ac98d3947.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 6
         </h5>
         <p>
          Time: 0:45-1:00
         </p>
         <p>
          Fill the Aeropress all the way to the top, or until your scale reads 240 grams.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-7" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-7-80370375239194c3ff80a0119a6e044f.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 7
         </h5>
         <p>
          Time: 1:00-1:30
         </p>
         <p>
          Stir gently for 5 seconds, then attach the cap (with filter) securely to the top of the chamber.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-8" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-8-dcf216ccc14d9a06fb01bf70085c27ad.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 8
         </h5>
         <p>
          Time: 1:30-1:40
         </p>
         <p>
          Once your timer reads 1:30, carefully flip the entire AeroPress over and place it on top of your mug. Before brewing for the first time, practice this motion with just water inside the chamber to get the hang of it. You want to find a smooth, quick motion that feels comfortable.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="well">
         <img alt="Craft-coffee-aeropress-brew-guide-9" class="img-rounded" height="394" src="//dl6drjrldv42d.cloudfront.net/assets/brewguide/aeropress/Craft-Coffee-Aeropress-Brew-Guide-9-3bee6e1823bcc6dcb733d0526bf7ee7a.jpg" width="700"/>
         <h5>
          Step 9
         </h5>
         <p>
          Time: 1:40-2:00
         </p>
         <p>
          Press the plunger down slowly but steadily for about 20 seconds until you hear a hissing sound - thats air escaping from the chamber. Remove the cap and discard the grounds and filter.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
