        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         If we ran Hollywood: we cast our favourite books
        </h1>
       </div>
      </header>
      <div class="entry-media">
       <figure class="base-image">
        <img alt="featured" class="attachment-big-size wp-post-image" height="250" itemprop="image" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/featured.png" width="650"/>

         <div class="single-box clearfix entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           All month weve talked about the choices made by screenwriters and directors when adapting some of our favourite books, but now its our turn. The TFS staff has dug into our fantasy casting closets and come out with some dynamite ideas for adaptations of books that are close to our hearts. If we ran Hollywood, this is who we would cast.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Ada //
           <em>
            The Ecstasy Club
           </em>
           by Douglas Rushkoff
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="ecstasy-club" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54428" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/ecstasy-club.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Keeping up my reputation as a purveyor of questionable tastemy favourite book is a fictional work by media theorist Douglas Rushkoff, entitled
           <em>
            The Ecstasy Club
           </em>
           . A group of young idealists in 90s San Francisco set up camp in an abandoned factory as they try to find spiritual enlightenment and a place to throw nightly raves. In my dream ensemble cast, James Franco would be Zach, the charistmatic cyberdelic cult leader; Justin Timberlake as Parrot, the morally ambiguous economics genius and business representative of the club; Eva Amurri as the bewitching trust-fund hippie; and Mena Suvari as the ex-reality show troublemaker.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Alexis //
           <em>
            Bossypants
           </em>
           by Tina Fey
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="bossypants" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54429" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bossypants.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I dont read much, but when I do its books that were adapted to TV shows. I think the only book I own that hasnt been adapted is Tina Feys brilliant biography
           <em>
            Bossypants
           </em>
           . I would cast Jennifer Lawrence as Tina Fey in a heartbeat. What people dont know is that JLaw is hilarious.
           <a href="http://twitpic.com/cozoi6" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://twitpic.com/cozoi6', 'She photobombed Sarah Jessica Parker at the Met Gala');" target="_blank">
            She photobombed Sarah Jessica Parker at the Met Gala
           </a>
           and called out BFF Emma Stone on the
           <a href="http://youtu.be/pXlAkIuUrZM?t=1m20s" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://youtu.be/pXlAkIuUrZM?t=1m20s', 'Emmy Red Carpet');" target="_blank">
            Emmy Red Carpet
           </a>
           . She loves a well-placed pop culture reference and I think with Tina Feys words mixed with JLaws natural comedic timing, she could easily pull it off.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Daniel //
           <em>
            Death of Bunny Munro
           </em>
           by Nick Cave
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="bunny-munro" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54430" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bunny-munro.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           While reading Nick Caves
           <em>
            Death of Bunny Munro
           </em>
           I couldnt help but picture Bob Hoskins in the titular role. Bunny Junior would be the most difficult to cast, but the best performance from a child actor I can recall would be Kodi Smit-McPhees turn in the Road (this is imaginary, so whatever, we used a time machine.) There are so many minor parts to cast, so Ill try to narrow it down: Nick Frost as Geoffrey, Bunnys boss whose love of tasteless jokes pales in comparison to Munros personification of the Scum Manifesto. Ben Mendelsohn as Poodle, a Britishized version of his hillbilly from
           <strong>
            The Place Beyond the Pines
           </strong>
           . Libby, Bunnys wife, would be wonderful yet rather small role in the hands of Maggie Smith. Id be interested to see either Molly Parker or Carey Mulligan in a duel role as both the waitress and the seductress who share the name River, this might help enhance the erotic dread and deja vu that encompasses Bunnys sex life. As for one of the most memorable clients on Bunnys journey, I believe Brenda Blethyn would be a fantastic Mrs. Brooks, bringing out Bunnys remorseful soft side. While were at it, give Cave himself the role of the devil costume wearing serial killer, or at least the saxophonist Munro meets backstage. Im sure Kylie Minogue would love to play herself.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Danita //
           <em>
            The Heart is a Lonely Hunter
           </em>
           by Carson McCullers
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="loney-hunter" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54431" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/loney-hunter.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The Heart is a Lonely Hunter
           </em>
           by Carson McCullers was made into a film in 1968 starring Alan Arkin. Its an okay adaptation but it hasnt aged well. I just think its about time for a remake, especially with all of the sudden interest in southern Gothic stories. Its a dreary novel, but a beautiful one. The story centers around a deaf-mute named John as he moves into a boarding house and becomes friends with a teenage girl named Mick.
          </p>
          <p>
           It seems obvious because he would be great in anything, but Id love to see Daniel Day Lewis play John Singer. Hed probably just about break your heart in the role. Also important is Johns friend, Spiros. All Im thinking is Philip Seymour Hoffman. And for Mick, I see Saoirse Ronan. She could capture the toughness and vulnerability of Mick all in one foul swoop. Micks parents would be played by Michael Shannon and Jessica Chastain  I loved them together in
           <strong>
            Take Shelter
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Darja //
           <em>
            Alice in Wonderland
           </em>
           by Lewis Carrol
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="alice-in-wonderland" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54432" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/alice-in-wonderland.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Oh my. Where to begin? Obviously there have been movies made, however if I were to cast this one I would go in a slightly different direction. The Red Queen and Alice would be Michelle Yeoh and Zhang Ziyi, because theyre badass and could have epic fight scenes. The rabbit could be Will Farrel, because he has that pep to his step, the cat would be Tom Hardy, for no particular reason other than me wanting to see him spinning around in a cat-suit, and Benedict Cumberbatch would be one of the butterflies. All in velvet costumes.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Dayna //
           <em>
            Survivor
           </em>
           by Chuck Palahniuk
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="survivor" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54433" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/survivor.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Ive read my fair share of books, but one that I found myself coming back to is
           <em>
            Survivor
           </em>
           by Chuck Palahniuk. Its the story of Tender Branson, who goes from chubby cult member to buff religious celebrity, and has been considered for a film adaptation more than once. If, or when, Survivor gets the film treatment, my fantasy casting would start with Christian Bale to play Branson, who proved he can quickly transform his body between
           <strong>
            The Machinist
           </strong>
           and
           <strong>
            Batman Begins
           </strong>
           . Bransons quirky psychic friend, Fertility, would be played by Jennifer Lawrence. Laura Fraser would play Bransons burnt-out caseworker and Jeremy Piven as his obnoxious agent.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Jordan //
           <em>
            Nineteen Eighty-Four
           </em>
           by George Orwell
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="1984" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54434" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/1984.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Although George Orwells
           <em>
            Nineteen Eighty-Four
           </em>
           already has two big-screen versions (and Terry Gilliams
           <strong>
            Brazil
           </strong>
           ), rumours keep swirling about a new adaptation. The books sales jumped after the NSA controversy this summer and dystopian sci-fi is still a popular subgenre, so perhaps the timing is right. If I were casting a new version of Orwells classic, I would have Ben Whishaw as Winston Smith, Felicity Jones as Julia and either David Tennant or Gary Oldman as OBrien. Although Michael Caine would make an excellent Charrington, it would also be cool to see veteran actor John Hurt  who played Winston in Michael Radfords 1984 film  in that role.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Laura //
           <em>
            Just Kids
           </em>
           by Patti Smith
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="just-kids" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54435" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/just-kids.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Most of my favourite books have already been made into movies so for my entry, I picked a recent favourite  Patti Smiths
           <em>
            Just Kids
           </em>
           . The book is an autobiography detailing her friendship with Robert Mapplethorpe from the 60s to his death in 1985. Though the book is nonfiction, the story practically begs to be made into a film. In my mind, Rooney Mara could really shine in the role as Patti Smith. Shes got the edgy, androgynous look and acting chops to pull it off. As Robert Mapplethorpe, I think James Franco would be amazing. You just have to Google Mapplethorpe to find people already making the comparison. Francos mysterious sexuality, good looks, oddness and commitment to any role he plays would make him a perfect fit. And in my ideal world, the movie would be directed by Anton Corbijn, who directed the great 2007 Ian Curtis biopic Control. In Corbijns hands, Just Kids would surely be a great take on this great book.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Liam //
           <em>
            The Lord of the Rings
           </em>
           trilogy by J.R.R. Tolkien
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="lotr" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54436" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/lotr.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Heres my alternative cast list for the greatest epic fantasy bromance of all time:
          </p>
          <p>
           Frodo  David Tennant
           <br/>
           Sam  Simon Pegg
           <br/>
           Merry  Alan Cumming
           <br/>
           Pippin  Mark McKinney
           <br/>
           Saruman  Peter OToole
           <br/>
           Gollum  Ben Whishaw
           <br/>
           Elrond  David Bowie
           <br/>
           Galadriel  Helen Mirren (yes, shes older, but she could pull it off.)
           <br/>
           Boromir  Russel Crowe
           <br/>
           Arwen  Jessica Chastain
           <br/>
           Aragorn  Daniel Day-Lewis
           <br/>
           Legolas  Tilda Swinton (wouldnt she make a bad-ass elf warrior?!)
           <br/>
           Gimli  Albert Finney
           <br/>
           Gandalf  Ian McKellen (I cant think of anybody who would do better than McKellen. There is no one else.)
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Mark //
           <em>
            Less Than Zero
           </em>
           by Bret Easton Ellis
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="less-than-zero" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54437" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/less-than-zero.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           There was already an adaptation made of Bret Easton Elliss zeitgeist-y debut novel
           <em>
            Less Than Zero
           </em>
           a couple of years after it was published. But with the exception of a powerful early performance from Robert Downey Jr., the movie was a fraud, eliminating the bleak atmosphere and darker elements of the book in favour of a glossy teen soap opera. For a much-needed edgier remake, it would be interesting to see what someone like Zac Efron could do as the neutral protagonist, Clay. Brie Larson (
           <strong>
            21 Jump Street
           </strong>
           ,
           <strong>
            Rampart
           </strong>
           ) could bring some life to Clays flaky ex, Blair, and wouldnt it be cool to see Canadian wunderkind Xavier Dolan replace RDJ as the vulnerable Julian? Provided he could master the American accent, of course.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Mike //
           <em>
            The Catcher in the Rye
           </em>
           by J.D. Salinger
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="catcher-in-the-rye" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54438" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/catcher-in-the-rye.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           One of my favourite books as a teenager was
           <em>
            The Catcher in the Rye
           </em>
           . Even though author J.D. Salinger has refused to sell the rights and stated his disapproval of a film adaptation ever being made, I can always dream. Casting would be a bit tricky for such a beloved novel. I could see actors such as Anton Yelchin or Logan Lerman being able to tackle the role of the naive and alienated protagonist, Holden Caulfield. Holdens precocious, yet innocent sister Phoebe would be played by someone like Mackenzie Foy or Kiernan Shipka. For the role of the young, handsome and understanding Mr. Antolini, Holdens favourite teacher, I could see an actor like Jake Gyllenhaal fit perfectly. The best role, though, would have to be a 1950s-set New York City.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Nadya //
           <em>
            Please Dont Kill the Freshman: A Memoir
           </em>
           by Zoe Trope
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="please-dont-kill-freshman" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54440" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/please-dont-kill-freshman.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Please Dont Kill the Freshman: A Memoir,
           </em>
           isnt my favourite book, but it definitely had an everlasting effect on me. Zoe Trope speaks in fragmented, thoughtful patterns about high school and home life. It still surprises me that it hasnt had an adaptation yet.
          </p>
          <p>
           As Zoe, I picture blogger Ruby Book. Not only does Ruby actually submit real pages of her diary to Rookie Mag, but she just simply fits the role of a moody, outspoken, and daring young writer. As her best friend Linux Shoe, I picture Jeffrey Licon. In
           <strong>
            Mysterious Skin
           </strong>
           , he seemed really soft spoken and loyal.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Prerana //
           <em>
            The Sense of an Ending
           </em>
           by Julian Barnes
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="sens-of-an-ending" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54441" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/sens-of-an-ending.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           My favourite novel is a relatively recent one 
           <em>
            The Sense of an Ending
           </em>
           by Julian Barnes (which, granted, should probably never be made into a film). Adrian Finn is the most mystifying character. Hes brilliant and makes narrator Tony all but fall in love with him. Im drawn towards a younger version of Aaron Taylor-Johnson, who I think has the understated charm and brooding appeal to pull off the role. Tony Webster, on the other hand, is Adrians antithesis  passive observer as opposed to existential explorer. I would cast Skandar Keynes for this role, partly because he portrays frustration and angst reasonably well.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Sean //
           <em>
            This Cant Be Happening at MacDonald Hall
           </em>
           by Gordon Korman
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="mcdonald-hall" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54442" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/mcdonald-hall.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Im going to go with a childhood favourite of mine, which would be, Canadian young adult author, Gordon Kormans 1978 debut novel
           <em>
            This Cant Be Happening at MacDonald Hall
           </em>
           , which focuses on a pair of friends in a boarding school outside of Toronto. I would cast relative unknowns as the lead characters Bruno and Boots, as well as the other child characters, however I think a marquee name would be used for MacDonald Hall headmaster William R. Sturgeon. Nicknamed The Fish for his stern fishy stare, I think Canadian character actor Stephen McHattie would be absolutely perfect in the role.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Trista // The Harry Bosch Series by Michael Connelly
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="concrete-blonde" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54443" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/concrete-blonde.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Harry Bosch is a hard-boiled LA homicide detective with a heart of gold and a passion for the truth. Connelly is very prolificthere are 18 Harry Bosch novelsand two of his books have already been made into films (2002s
           <strong>
            Blood Work
           </strong>
           starring Clint Eastwood and 2011s
           <strong>
            The Lincoln Lawyer
           </strong>
           starring Matthew Conaughey). If I were a Hollywood exec looking to adapt a Harry Bosch book, I would start with
           <em>
            The Concrete Blonde.
           </em>
           With a serial killer and a cop on trial, its ripe for adaptation.
          </p>
          <p>
           Casting Harry Bosch would be tough. Described as tall, wiry and aging (when he meet Bosch hes in his 40s), I think the only person in current Hollywood who could carry this would be Bryan Cranston. For the other recurring characters in his life, I would cast Viola Davis as Kizmin Rider, Tyler Perry as Jerry Edgar, Diane Lane as Rachel Walling and Eva Mendez as Eleanor Wish. (Of course, if we had a time machine, this role would be best played by Charles Bronson circa 1987.)
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Will //
           <em>
            Paul Is Undead: The British Zombie Invasion
           </em>
           by Alan Goldsher
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="paul-is-undead" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-54444" height="350" src="http://thetfs.ca/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/paul-is-undead.png" width="620"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           If I had to fantasy cast one of my favourite books, I would have to go with the Alan Goldsher novel, 
           <em>
            Paul Is Undead: The British Zombie Invasion
           </em>
           . The book follows the career of the zombie Beatles, which manages to take two of my favourite things, and mash them together. Casting the Beatles was actually less difficult than it sounds. For John Lennon, I went with Aaron Taylor-Johnson, who has already played Lennon in
           <strong>
            Nowhere Boy
           </strong>
           . Paul McCartney  Dane DeHaan, since hes a bit of a babyface. Ringo Starr  Jamie Bell. I think it was the ears that did it. Finally, George Harrison  Jim Sturgess, who has also been around the Beatles with his role in
           <strong>
            Across The Universe
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <em>
            Whats your fantasy casting for your favourite book? Tell us in the comments!
           </em>
          </h3>
