     <p>
      <b>
       Richard Semler "Dick" Barthelmess
      </b>
      (May 9, 1895  August 17, 1963) was an American film actor. He was nominated for the
      <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">
       Academy Award for Best Actor
      </a>
      in 1928.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Barthelmess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Barthelmess was born in New York City in 1895 as the son of Caroline Harris, a stage actress,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and Alfred W. Barthelmess.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His father died when the boy was a year old.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Through his mother, he grew up in the theatre, doing "walk-ons" from an early age. In contrast to that, he was educated at Hudson River Military Academy at
      <a href="/wiki/Nyack,_New_York" title="Nyack, New York">
       Nyack
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College_(Connecticut)" title="Trinity College (Connecticut)">
       Trinity College
      </a>
      at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hartford" title="Hartford">
       Hartford
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">
       Connecticut
      </a>
      . He did some acting in college and other amateur productions. By 1919 he had five years in stock company experience.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
       Career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Barthelmess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Russian actress
      <a href="/wiki/Alla_Nazimova" title="Alla Nazimova">
       Alla Nazimova
      </a>
      , a friend of the family, was taught English by Caroline Barthelmess.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Nazimova convinced Richard Barthelmess to try acting professionally, and he made his debut screen appearance in 1916 in the serial
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Gloria%27s_Romance" title="Gloria's Romance">
        Gloria's Romance
       </a>
      </i>
      as an uncredited extra. He also appeared as a supporting player in several films starring
      <a href="/wiki/Marguerite_Clark" title="Marguerite Clark">
       Marguerite Clark
      </a>
      . His next role, in
      <i>
       War Brides
      </i>
      opposite Nazimova, attracted the attention of director
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Wark_Griffith" title="David Wark Griffith">
       D.W. Griffith
      </a>
      , who offered him several important roles, finally casting him opposite
      <a href="/wiki/Lillian_Gish" title="Lillian Gish">
       Lillian Gish
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Broken_Blossoms" title="Broken Blossoms">
        Broken Blossoms
       </a>
      </i>
      (1919) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Way_Down_East" title="Way Down East">
        Way Down East
       </a>
      </i>
      (1920).
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Barth07.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="223" data-file-width="267" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/57/Barth07.jpg/200px-Barth07.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/5/57/Barth07.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/5/57/Barth07.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Barth07.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        with
        <a href="/wiki/Lillian_Gish" title="Lillian Gish">
         Lillian Gish
        </a>
        in
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Way_Down_East" title="Way Down East">
          Way Down East
         </a>
        </i>
        (1920)
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Barthelmess soon became one of
      <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">
       Hollywood
      </a>
      's highest paid performers, starring in such classics as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Patent_Leather_Kid" title="The Patent Leather Kid">
        The Patent Leather Kid
       </a>
      </i>
      (1927) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Noose_(film)" title="The Noose (film)">
        The Noose
       </a>
      </i>
      (1928); he was nominated for Best Actor at the first
      <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">
       Academy Awards
      </a>
      for his performance in both these films. In addition, he won a Special Citation for producing
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Patent_Leather_Kid" title="The Patent Leather Kid">
        The Patent Leather Kid
       </a>
      </i>
      . He founded his own production company, Inspiration Film Company, together with Charles Duell and Henry King. One of their films,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tol%27able_David" title="Tol'able David">
        Tol'able David
       </a>
      </i>
      (1921), in which Barthelmess starred as a teenage mailman who finds courage, was a major success.
     </p>
     <p>
      With the advent of the sound era, Barthelmess' fortunes changed. He made several talkie films, most notably
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Son_of_the_Gods" title="Son of the Gods">
        Son of the Gods
       </a>
      </i>
      (1930),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Dawn_Patrol_(1930_film)" title="The Dawn Patrol (1930 film)">
        The Dawn Patrol
       </a>
      </i>
      (1930),
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Last_Flight_(film)" title="The Last Flight (film)">
        The Last Flight
       </a>
      </i>
      (1931), and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Cabin_in_the_Cotton" title="The Cabin in the Cotton">
        The Cabin in the Cotton
       </a>
      </i>
      (1932),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Airport_(film)" title="Central Airport (film)">
        Central Airport
       </a>
      </i>
      (1933), and a supporting role as
      <a href="/wiki/Rita_Hayworth" title="Rita Hayworth">
       Rita Hayworth
      </a>
      's character's husband in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Only_Angels_Have_Wings" title="Only Angels Have Wings">
        Only Angels Have Wings
       </a>
      </i>
      (1939).
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Post-acting_career">
       Post-acting career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Barthelmess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-acting career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Barthelmess failed to maintain the stardom of his silent film days and gradually left entertainment. He enlisted in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy_Reserve" title="United States Navy Reserve">
       United States Navy Reserve
      </a>
      during
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      , and served as a lieutenant commander. He never returned to film, preferring instead to live off his investments.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Death">
       Death
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Barthelmess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Barthelmess died of cancer in 1963, aged 68, and was interred at the
      <a href="/wiki/Ferncliff_Cemetery" title="Ferncliff Cemetery">
       Ferncliff Cemetery
      </a>
      and Mausoleum in
      <a href="/wiki/Hartsdale,_New_York" title="Hartsdale, New York">
       Hartsdale, New York
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_family">
       Marriage and family
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Barthelmess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Marriage and family">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      He married
      <a href="/wiki/Mary_Hay_(actress)" title="Mary Hay (actress)">
       Mary Hay
      </a>
      , a stage and screen star. They had one daughter, Mary Barthelmess, before divorcing.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1927, Barthelmess became engaged to Katherine Young Wilson, a Broadway actress.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, the engagement was called off, possibly due to his affair about this time with the journalist
      <a href="/wiki/Adela_Rogers_St._Johns" title="Adela Rogers St. Johns">
       Adela Rogers St. Johns

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">
       Legacy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Barthelmess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legacy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Barthelmess is a founder of the
       <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Motion_Picture_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences">
        Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.22founders.22_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-.22founders.22-10">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         10
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       For his contribution as an actor, Richard Barthelmess was awarded a star on the
       <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">
        Hollywood Walk of Fame
       </a>
       .
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Barthelmess is among the second group (1957) recipients of the
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.eastmanhouse.org/museum/awards.php" rel="nofollow">
        George Eastman Award
       </a>
       , given by
       <a href="/wiki/George_Eastman_House" title="George Eastman House">
        George Eastman House
       </a>
       for distinguished contribution to the art of film (1957).
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Composer Katherine Allan Lively dedicated her piano composition,
       <i>
        Within the Walls of China: A Chinese Episode
       </i>
       , to Barthelmess in the sheet music published in 1923 by G. Schirmer, Inc.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         11
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       An article in
       <i>
        The Music Trades
       </i>
       reported that Mrs. Lively was inspired by a viewing of the film,
       <i>
        Broken Blossoms
       </i>
       , and performed the piece for Mr. Barthelmess and his friends in New York in the summer of 1922
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         12
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
