       <p>
        Before we became famous travel bloggers, (well at least to my parents)we were broke and living in my parents trailer. We had returned from cycling the continent of Africa. A year earlier we made the decision to become an adventure couple for every man and knew that we needed to make a splash on the world stage. So we signed up for the worlds longest cycling race,from Cairo, Egypt to Cape Town, South Africa.
       </p>
       <p>
        Yesterday, I wrote about the long journey of how we knew we
        <a href="http://theplanetd.com/take-risks-it-leads-to-great-things/">
         wanted to make travel a full time career
        </a>
        , but we didnt know how to do it. We took many twists and wrong turns, over nearly a decade,but it eventually led us here. With each risk we took, new opportunities came our way. And even though the journey to becoming travel bloggers took nearly 10 years, we wouldnt have had it any other way.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://theplanetd.com/images/Travel-Bloggers.jpg">
         <img alt="Travel Bloggers" class="aligncenter wp-image-44574" height="1103" src="http://theplanetd.com/images/Travel-Bloggers.jpg" width="735"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://theplanetd.com/take-risks-it-leads-to-great-things/">
         When we left off yesterday,
        </a>
        I stopped at the point where Dave and I came back from 7 months of travel in Asia. We no longer had our jobs or connections in Toronto, but miraculously, things worked out. As a matter a fact, better opportunities fell into our laps than the jobs we had before leaving. Dave had offers for several major motion pictures and I received a regular gig as in house Make Up artist for a major Canadian TV network. Things were looking good. But we knew it was temporary because we knew that we wanted to travel.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 1327px">
        <img alt="Dave on the cover of Professional Lighting Magazine" height="1200" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-WJB2KVT/0/X3/Dave%20lighting-X3.jpg" width="1317"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Dave on the cover of Professional Lighting Magazine
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        For the next couple of years, we worked hard and focused on our film careers, but still took some trips to places like Peru, Europe and Mexico. Wanderlust was still within us, but we needed a plan. We continued to try new adventures at like rock climbing mountain, biking, and snowboarding. But we were growing discouraged. Work was starting to take over our lives and we felt restless. Would we ever fulfill our dream of being professional travelers or were we doomed to work at jobs we didnt love trapped in aperpetual cycle of trying to stay one step ahead of our bills?
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Read now: How to Get Paid to Travel
          <a href="http://theplanetd.com/how-to-get-paid-to-travel/" target="_blank">
           How to Get Paid to Travel
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #993300;">
         2007
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Near the end of 2006, weknew that it was now or never, if we wanted to finally be full time adventurers we would have to do something drastic. We made a toast on Dec 31 2006 to change our lives once and for all by this time next year.2007 started off with high hopes. It was two weeks later that we saw an article on the Tour dAfrique and we decided that was the adventure for us. What better way to set us apart from other couples than to cycle 12,000 km from Cairo, Egypt to Cape Town, South Africa. We signed up and told all our co-workers and bosses that by this time next year wed be leaving our jobs and cycling Africa.
       </p>
       <h2>
        We had big dreams, this was it, we were never going back.
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 1610px">
        <img alt="Our bikes at sunset." height="1200" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-QmLb4tg/0/X3/Dave%20and%20Deb-2-X3.jpg" width="1600"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Our bikes at sunset.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        We hired a publicist to get us some TV and radio appearances and her connections got us a series of articles in the Toronto Star. With the media exposure and the amazing one of a kind idea we had for a TV show once the race was over, we were sure wed be on our way.
       </p>
       <p>
        We cycled hard for four months and I managed to take the womens title andDave won EFI. We managed to do some interviews along the way, we visited a couple ofprojects on behalf of Plan Canada and we even wrotearticles for the Toronto Star. We had more media exposure than we ever had in our lives. We were positive wed have a TV show once we got back to Canada.
       </p>
       <h1>
        <span style="color: #993300;">
         The Trailer Park
        </span>
       </h1>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="402" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/photos/i-ZmSR7Sk/0/X3/i-ZmSR7Sk-X3.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        When we returned, we stayed with our families for as we figured out our future. We went tomeetings with our publicist and production companies to sell our idea. We did some media appearances and interviews and felt very optimistic for the future. After a month or so though we saw that it was going to be a very long process and we were broke.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 730px">
        <img alt="Looking and feeling broke." height="960" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-pxhFRQx/0/X2/Dave%20and%20Deb-3-X2.jpg" width="720"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Looking and feeling broke.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        All the money we saved for the Tour dAfrique the year before was gone and we came home with zero dollars in our savings account. Rather than dip into our retirement fund, we realized that we would have to go back to work. This time, things looked bleak. We didnt have money for first and last months rent (unless we dipped into a borrowing account or credit cards) and we didnt want to stay in Toronto for long anyway. We still hadnt given up on our dream yet. But, both of us had officially been replaced in our film jobs. The business in Toronto was pretty slow at the time so we couldnt even take daily calls. We werent quite sure what we were going to do for money.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #800000;">
         The Trailer and New Jobs
         <br/>
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Luckily my parents had a 5th wheel trailer that they used to take to Texas for the winter. They didnt need it so offered to let us park it at a trailer park just outside of Toronto so wed have cheap accommodation while we got ourselves back on track. We set up shop in Milton Ontario and put the word out that we were home. We then started researching adventures, adventurers, photographers, writers. We researched anything that had to do with travel and decided that even though our TV show didnt work out, we were going to find another way to make our travel careers possible.
       </p>
       <p>
        Even when things seem terrifying the rewards always outweigh the risks. Previous experience had shown us that if you stay focused, things have a way of working out. We were willing to make sacrifices to make things work. We werent embarrassed that we were living in a trailer and we were willing to do any job that came our way. A year ago, Dave was the head of lighting on big feature films, now he was willing to do whatever was needed to rebuild our bank account. Nobody had work for him on a big movie, but he did hear of a production lighting houseowned by the lead singer of
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triumph_(band)">
         Triumph
        </a>
        .It wasnt glamorous, but the money was good and we didnt have a lot of expenses anyway, so it helped rebuild our savings.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 1610px">
        <img alt="Back to work I go.." height="1200" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-TDH3fJj/0/X3/Dave%20and%20Deb-12-X3.jpg" width="1600"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Back to work I go..
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        YTV was done with me after leaving twice already, but out of the blue I got a call from a person I worked with for only a few days several years ago on the movie Chicago. We hit it off then and she just landed a gig doing makeup for the TV series
        <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1101009/">
         Urban Legends
        </a>
        . She had to leave early to do another movie and wanted to know if I could replace her. Oh Yeah, I was getting paid at least three times the money YTV was paying and it was a fun and easy gig that would take me through the summer.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 1610px">
        <img alt="Deb's Makeup artistry at work on Urban Legends" height="1200" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-TPMdB3S/0/X3/Dave%20and%20Deb-5-X3.jpg" width="1600"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Debs Makeup artistry at work on Urban Legends
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Soon Dave started getting calls again for movies.He was willing to rough it for a bit and then was rewarded with three movies back to back. Boondock Saints,Scott Pilgrim vs the World, and Defendor with Woody Harrelson.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #993300;">
         A New Plan
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        We started to see our bank accounts replenish and we started a new plan. Clearly our idea of hosting a TV series wasnt going to pan out at the rate we were going. We had our publicist continue to work on our behalf to try to sell our idea and we had a production company signed. All we needed was a distributor. When we received a $10,000 bill from our publicist. We decided that it was time to let her go. We negotiated paying her only half since most of the invoice consisted of lunches and business meetings. We told her, I think you could have done a lot of that business by phone. She agreed that it was a bit much for two regular folks like us, so she agreed to let us only pay half.
       </p>
       <p>
        We do credit a lot of our initial success on having a publicist, but there was no way we could continue.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_43201" style="width: 410px">
        <img alt="We hired a Publicist..." class="size-full wp-image-43201" height="190" src="http://theplanetd.com/images/cropped-GOLDFENIX-LOGO-SMALL-e13355495633221.jpg" width="400"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         We hired a Publicist
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Since TV wasnt an option at this point we started looking into other ways to make travel work. We started this blog for the TDA as a way to keep followers of the race informed of our progress through continent. So we decided to pursue travel blogging. If we were ever going to get a TV show, we were going to have to raise our profile. For the next year we worked full time at our jobs in TV and spent every spare moment we had working on our blog. It paid off because soon our readership grew and we decided that we had enough readers and followers to maybe make a living online.
       </p>
       <p>
        By living very frugally for that year, we put money away again to travel. We didnt do anything that would cut into our budget. We ate at home, our activities included going for bike rides, or visiting friends and family. The rest of the time was spent learning as much as we could about the b
        <a href="http://theplanetd.com/tbex-keynote-the-evolution-of-travel-blogging/">
         usiness of travel blogging.
        </a>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 1610px">
        <img alt="Spending time in Goa, India" height="1067" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-bGD4JgC/0/X3/Dave%20and%20Deb-4-X3.jpg" width="1600"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Spending time in Goa, India
        </p>
       </div>
       <h2>
        <span style="color: #800000;">
         ThePlanetD Begins
        </span>
       </h2>
       <p>
        By October 2009 we left for India with a dream of making money off of ThePlanetD. We didnt have as much money to last this time though. We really could only make it through about 4 months of travel before wed either have to start using credit or start making money. We hoped to start making money.
       </p>
       <p>
        During our travels we worked hard at social media, photography and blogging and our readership grew.But we still werent making any money. It wasnt until 3 months in when we were getting worried that we sold an ad on our blog. A company wanted to buy 4 text links for a year for something ridiculously low like $40 a month. However, it was money that was needed and they were going to pay us up front for the year for all four posts. That was $1980.00. That amount of money goes a long way in India and we were thrilled. We made a sale and we saw light at the end of the tunnel.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 1610px">
        <img alt="Celebrating our first sale in Sri Lanka" height="1200" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-x6c6Rv3/0/X3/Dave%20and%20Deb-1-X3.jpg" width="1600"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Celebrating our first sale in Sri Lanka
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        We lasted a few more months, but we had to come home one last time. If you read
        <a href="http://theplanetd.com/take-risks-it-leads-to-great-things/">
         yesterdays post
        </a>
        and then todays youll see that this is our 5th time coming home after a long travel. Only this time, we had no intention of starting all over again. We were going to keep the momentum going and continue to work on our travel blog and refuse to put down any roots.
       </p>
       <p>
        We did some house sitting for friends, stayed with family, and managed to book a few press trips. Money was tight and we were stressed out, so when Dave got a call to do a 3 week TV pilot late summer 2010, he took it. Funny enough, the movie business was booming and everyone was working. His pilot was so small, nobody wanted to work with him because they could do feature films instead. Since I was still a member of the
        <a href="https://www.iatse873.com/">
         same Union
        </a>
        as a make-up artist, I was eligible to work for Dave in the lighting department. So the final film Dave ever worked on in Toronto, had me working for him.
        <strong>
         We went out with a bang together.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        The money was good and with the two of us workinglike mad, we had double the money coming in. A friend let us stay at her house while she spent a few weeks up North and everything worked out.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 970px">
        <img alt="Getting advice form Journeywoman really helped us out." height="602" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-fSSrbTz/0/X3/dave%20deb%20and%20evelyn-X3.jpg" width="960"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Getting advice form Journeywoman really helped us out.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        I remember having lunch with
        <a href="http://journeywoman.com/">
         JourneyWoman
        </a>
        at the time and we told her we were working on a film. She said, never feel bad about having to take work, you do what you need to do. And we took that to heart. That TV show helped us build another little nest egg to get us through the year. We also had a good discussion with her about how we can start to make a living out of travel blogging. Back then, nobody really knew how, and everyone was trying to figure it all out. Can you believe that was only 2010?
       </p>
       <p>
        At this time, companies were starting to offer travel in return for blog posts so we were able to go back to Asia with our flights paid for by a third party. We then hustled our butts off to get some more sponsored trips so that we didnt have to spend too much money and we made it through the next year making money by selling ads. In truth, we were really just getting by because we didnt have to pay for our travel and we had some savings. It definitely wasnt a comfortable way to live. But it was exciting.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 1610px">
        <img alt="Making great friends." height="1067" src="http://travelphotography.theplanetd.com/Dave-and-Deb-The-Planet-D/i-R6WN5Bs/0/X3/Dave%20and%20Deb-1-X3.jpg" width="1600"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Making great friends.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Soon we started learning how to
        <a href="http://theplanetd.com/travel-business-pro-blogger/">
         make money with our travel blog
        </a>
        and how to start building campaigns. But from the time we started our blog for the Tour dAfrique in 2008 to early 2011, we had no idea how to make a living from travel.
       </p>
       <p>
        All we knew was that we wanted to make a living and that if we sacrificed enough and kept trying new and different things, we would make it work.
        <strong>
         And we did!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        We now make more money that we did in the film business and we now are living or dream of working together, travelling together, and being together. We really do pinch ourselves each day. I cannot believe the journey. We had been dreaming of this moment since 2002. We made our first pay cheque in 2010 and started to makea decent living in 2011. Since then, our business has grown beyond our wildest dreams.
       </p>
       <p>
        Dreams dont come easy and many risks were taken. We started over again and again. We went from having money to having no money again and again. We went from high paying jobs, to low paying jobs. I went back to waitressing and and Dave worked in warehouses.. We went from living in a big beautiful house to living in a trailer and sleeping on friends couches. We never once questioned what we were doing, it was all part of the plan and we knew that sacrifices would have to be made. When I look back, it was an amazing journey and a really fun ride. I wouldnt change a thing.
       </p>
       <p>
        And you know what? The fun is just starting
       </p>

             I wouldnt recommend it. If you are being pitched Text links that are strictly for link juice where the client is asking for specific keywords, the answer is bad idea. It was a big thing years ago, (people are doing it less now). We didnt realize at the time (5 years ago) but people were wanting us to use keywords like cheap flights, top hotels etc. They were booking engines so we didnt really think there was anything wrong with it, but it was not a good thing to do. Especially now that Google has caught on to this game. However, if a company approaches you as an influencer to write a sponsored post about a brand you use and believe in or an experience that you have done, that is fine. It offers value to your readers and fits with your website. Hope this helps!
