        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Ultraviolet: In this future, there is only one woman
        </h1>
        <!-- autor, fecha y comentarios -->
        <div class="sub-title">
         <div class="autor-fecha">
          <i class="fa fa-calendar-o">
          </i>
          August 2, 2006
          <i class="fa fa-comment-o">
          </i>
          <a href="http://planetjinxatron.com/ultraviolet-future-one-woman/#respond">
           No Comments
           <span class="screen-reader-text">
            on Ultraviolet: In this future, there is only one woman
           </span>
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <!-- .sub-title -->
        <div class="sharing-buttons-top">
         <div class="addthis_sharing_toolbox">
         </div>
        </div>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <!-- Subtitle widget area -->
       <div class="zg-wrapper-widget-area">
       </div>
       <!-- .sub-title-widget-area -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="l-pic" height="449" src="/images/2014/ultraviolet.jpg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I knew
         <a href="http://imdb.com/title/tt0370032/">
          <em>
           Ultraviolet
          </em>
         </a>
         would be bad, but I thought it would be funny bad.  Instead, it was 88 minutes feels like 200 minutes bad.  I know some people who enjoyed it as campy action fun, but I was just bored.  I also dont much care for the poster, which is a strange look at the powerful woman who is the size of a twig image.
        </p>
        <p>
         But heres the more interesting question: what does it mean when there is only one woman?
        </p>
        <p>
         Violet, played by
         <a href="http://imdb.com/name/nm0000170/">
          Milla Jovovich
         </a>
         , is the only woman among all of the vampires.  We never explicitly see a woman among the large number of faceless soldiers she kills, so most people would assume they are men.  Her nemesis is a man, her one true ally is a man, and the child that is her hope for a better future is male.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are women in the general public, whom we only see as a backdrop for the action.  The only other woman in the film who speaks is a courier, who gets 2 sentences and is never seen again.
        </p>
        <p>
         When I saw
         <a href="http://imdb.com/title/tt0121765/">
          <em>
           Star Wars Episode 2: Attack of the Clones
          </em>
         </a>
         , I was struck by how few women were Jedi.  One woman on the Jedi council, 4 or 5 more in the big fight at the end.  Annoying, but I knew how to interpret it.  The filmmakers just didnt give a damn.
        </p>
        <p>
         In
         <em>
          UItraviolet
         </em>
         , Violet is effectively the only woman in the world.  I have no idea what to make of this.  Is she the only woman badass enough to survive infection with the virus that made her a vampire?  Are women only worth viewing if they are the ultimate, most kick-ass people  and since women are weak, there is only one woman like that?  Were the filmmakers just trying to highlight her isolation?
        </p>
        <p>
         At the beginning of the film, Violet says I was born into a world that you may not understand.  No kidding.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          This was my first movie review post published on Heroine Content, a feminist and anti-racist movie blog that ran from July 2006 to May 2012.
         </em>
        </p>
