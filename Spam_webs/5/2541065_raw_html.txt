       <section class="text-description">
        <p class="p1">
         Azharuddin Abdul Rahman, director-general of the country's Department of Civil Aviation (DCA), said: "We officially declare Malaysia Airlines flight MH370 an accidentand that all 239 of the passengers and crew onboard MH370 are presumed to have lost their lives."
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         Now that the announcement has been made, families will be able to claim compensation in accordance with standards of annexes 12 and 13 in the International Civil Aviation.
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         Following the announcement, Malaysia Airlines will immediately proceed with the compensation process for victims' next-of-kin, Mr Rahman said.
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         A search is continuing in the Indian Ocean for the jet, as well as a Malaysian criminal investigation.
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         Mr Rahman added: "Both investigations are limited by the lack of physical evidence at this time, particularly the flight recorders.
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         "Therefore, at this juncture, there is no evidence to substantiate any speculations as to the cause of the accident.
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         "This declaration is by no means the end."
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         Most of the 227 passengers on board the plane were Chinese.
        </p>

        <p class="p2">
         Chinese Premier Li Keqiang told reporters: "We hope the Malaysian side honours its promises and fully investigates the incident, settling claims and making peace with the families, especially continuing to make all efforts to find the missing plane and its passengers."
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         The news comes as the final 54 minutes of communication aboard the stricken aircraft were revealed today by the Telegraph.
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         The transcript begins at 12.25am and ends at 1.19am with pilot Fariq Abdul's final words: "All right, good night".
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         The search for stricken flight MH370 resumed in October after it was halted for four months so crews could map the seabed of the search zone, which measures approximately 23,000 square miles, off the coast of western Australia.
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         The airliner lost contact with air traffic controllers on March 8 while carrying 239 people on a flight between Kuala Lumpur and Beijing.
        </p>

         The aircraft last made contact 120 nautical miles off the east coast of the Malaysian town of Kota Bharu.
        </p>

         No debris has yet been recovered.

         Just months laterMalaysia Airlines flight MH17 crashed.
