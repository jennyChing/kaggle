            <h2 class="blog-title">
             <a class="blog-title-link blog-link" href="http://www.droneguru.co/drone-news/gopro-to-create-quadcopter-drone">
              GoPro To Create Quadcopter Drone
             </a>
            </h2>
            <p class="blog-date">
             <span class="date-text">
              5/27/2015
             </span>
            </p>
            <p class="blog-comments">
             <a class="blog-link" href="http://www.droneguru.co/drone-news/gopro-to-create-quadcopter-drone#comments">
              0 Comments
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <div class="blog-separator">
           </div>
           <div class="blog-content">
            <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:justify;">
             Today at the Code Conference, GoPro CEO Nick Woodman announced the company is currently creating a quadcopter drone to compete with the drone companies DJI and 3D Robotics, and it will ship in early 2016. Currently GoPro is the camera of choice to attach to drones being used for aerial photography.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             "
             <span style="text-align: start;">
              It seemed natural to us that were in the GoPro enabling business and drones are the ultimate GoPro accessorythat was all the rationale we needed to say OK. Its core enough to our business to make sense to make our own, said Woodman on stage at the Code Conference.
             </span>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              DON'T MISS:
              <a href="http://www.droneguru.co/drone-news/lily-camera-the-aerial-photography-drone-that-follows-you" title="">
               Lily, the First Throw and Go Drone.
              </a>
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <span style="text-align: start;">
              <br/>
             </span>
             Woodman also mentioned that GoPro is a "consumer-focused company" so expect a price tag somewhere between $500 and $1,000. For those who already have an aerial photography setup, Woodman said that GoPro would continue to work with other drone manufacturers to ensure GoPro compatibility.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             The drone market is becoming saturated with high quality choices already available, so it will be interesting to see how the new GoPro Quadcopter Drone stacks up against the
             <a href="http://amzn.to/1Fc1FDK" target="_blank" title="">
              <strong>
               DJI Phantom 3
              </strong>
             </a>
             and the
             <a href="http://amzn.to/1EyWT2v" target="_blank" title="">
              <strong>
               3D Robotics IRIS+
              </strong>
             </a>
             . Stay tuned for more information as it becomes available, and don't forget to check out our
             <a href="/reviews---aerial-photography-drones.html" title="">
              <strong>
               Drone Reviews
              </strong>
             </a>
             .
