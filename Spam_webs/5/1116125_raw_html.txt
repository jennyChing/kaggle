                     <h4>
                      Trying the Recipes
                     </h4>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     The kids and I spent a fun morning shopping for the ingredients we needed to make
                     <b>
                      <i>
                       North Indian Chicken Curry
                      </i>
                     </b>
                     - a thick and earthy yogurt based curry dish. The kids loved learning about the spices, smelling them and creating this dish with me. It was also easy - make the marinade, throw in the chicken the night before and the rest comes together in little time or fuss at dinner time..
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I have to say that I've tried three recipes so far and all are quite spicy (and I love spicy)! We now cut out any serrano peppers that are listed and often cut the powdered chilis to just a 1/2 tsp or less....this way the kids will enjoy these great recipes and your guests won't be sweating by the end of the meal!

                      <span style='font-family: "GaramondPremrPro-Bd","serif"; font-size: 22.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: GaramondPremrPro-Bd;'>
                       North
Indian Chicken Curry
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align: none; mso-pagination: none; text-align: center; text-autospace: none;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt;">
                        Tools:
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <span style="font-size: 6pt;">
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt;">
                       Youll need a food
processor; a large and a small mixing bowl; a whisk; a fork; a heavy-bottomed, 6-quart
/ 6-L saut pan with a lid; tongs; and a large plate.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt;">
                        Yield:
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt;">
                       810 servings
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <b>
                       Ingredients:
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 medium yellow or red onion, roughly chopped
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 (4-inch / 10-cm) piece ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      10 cloves garlic, peeled
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      2 cups / 470 mL plain, unsweetened yogurt (whole, lowfat, or
nonfat)
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon plus
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 pinch salt, divided
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 tablespoon turmeric powder
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 tablespoon
                      <i>
                       Garam Masala
                      </i>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 tablespoon red chile powder or cayenne pepper
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      2 teaspoons ground coriander
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      2 teaspoons roasted cumin, ground
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1/3 cup / 10 g
                      <i>
                       kasoori methi
                      </i>
                      (dried fenugreek
leaves), lightly hand crushed to release flavor
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      4 pounds / 1.81 g skinless whole bone-in chicken, cut into
810 pieces (including cutting each breast in ), or 2 pounds / 910 g boneless
chicken
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      6 tablespoons / 90 mL
                      <i>
                       ghee
                      </i>
                      or vegetable oil, divided
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 teaspoon cumin seeds
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      2 (3-inch / 8-cm) sticks cinnamon
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      5 whole cloves
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      5 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      2 black cardamom pods
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      1 large yellow or red onion, thinly sliced
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      3 large tomatoes, diced
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      24 fresh Thai, serrano, or cayenne chiles, stems removed
and thinly sliced
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      2 heaping tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Brown or white basmati rice,
                      <i>
                       Roti,
                      </i>
                      or
                      <i>
                       Naan
                      </i>
                      for
serving
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <b>
                       Directions:
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst" style="mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -.25in;">
                      <br/>
                      <ol>
                       <li>
                        In the bowl of a food processor, grind the
chopped onion, the ginger, and the garlic into a smooth paste. Transfer to a
large mixing bowl and add the yogurt, the 1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon of the
salt, the turmeric, the
                        <i>
                         Garam Masala,
                        </i>
                        the red chile powder, the
coriander, the cumin, and the
                        <i>
                         kasoori methi.
                        </i>
                        Whisk until well blended.
Kasoori methi
                        <i>
                         can be found in Indian grocery stores. Omit if you
                        </i>
                        <i>
                         cannot
find it. Do not use fresh leaves or fenugreek seeds, as they will alter the
taste.
                        </i>
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Prepare the chicken by poking holes in it with a
fork to help it absorb the yogurt marinade. Carefully add the pieces of chicken
to the large mixing bowl and gently stir until all the pieces are evenly coated.
Cover and refrigerate for 2 hours to overnight.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        In a heavy-bottomed, 6-quart / 6-L saut pan
over mediumhigh heat, warm 3 tablespoons / 45 mL of the
                        <i>
                         ghee.
                        </i>
                        Using
tongs, carefully transfer the pieces of chicken to the saut pan, reserving the
marinade for later use. Cook, turning once to ensure even cooking, for a total
of 4 minutes. Transfer to a large plate, and transfer the remaining liquid in
the pan to a small mixing bowl for later use.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Return the saut pan to mediumhigh heat and
warm the remaining 3 tablespoons / 45 mL of
                        <i>
                         ghee.
                        </i>
                        Add the cumin seeds,
cinnamon, cloves, and green and black cardamom and cook for 2 minutes. Add the
sliced onion and the remaining pinch of salt and cook, stirring constantly and
scraping the pan to prevent burning, for 3 minutes, until the onion is slightly
browned.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Add the tomatoes to the saut pan and cook,
uncovered and stirring occasionally (breaking down the tomatoes with the back
of the spoon), for 11 minutes, until they are fairly smooth. Add the reserved
marinade and cooking liquid from Step 3 and the fresh chiles to the saut pan
and cook for 2 minutes.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Reduce the heat to mediumlow and slowly,
carefully return the chicken to the saut pan. Partially cover the pan and
cook, stirring occasionally to ensure even cooking, for 35 to 40 minutes (20
minutes if using boneless chicken), until the chicken is cooked through.
                        <i>
                         Be
sure not to overcook the chicken.
                        </i>
                        Remove from the heat.
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        Transfer to a serving bowl. Garnish with the
cilantro and serve with the brown or white basmati rice,
                        <i>
                         Roti,
                        </i>
                        or
                        <i>
                         Naan.
                        </i>
                       </li>
                      </ol>
