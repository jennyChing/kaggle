        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         7 Best Superfoods To Improve Your Memory
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         In this modern era, people are constantly bombarding their minds with loads and loads of information such as studies, assignments, new projects and other work-related information. A healthy memory is more important now than ever before. People are increasingly turning to gadgets such as smartphones and computers to keep track of most things in their lives. This dependence can be reduced by adopting healthy eating habits. Certain foods provide your brain with essential nutrients to enable sharpened reflexes, and a better attention span and memory.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Here are the 7 best superfoods to improve your memory:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Walnuts
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="walnuts help to improve memory" class="alignright size-full wp-image-143" height="200" src="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/file/2014/06/walnuts-help-to-improve-memory.jpg" width="300"/>
         Walnuts can enhance your thinking power and memory. Among all nuts, they are the richest source of omega-3 fatty acids. These boost the working of neurotransmitters, thus boosting memory and cognitive skills. Walnuts also contain magnesium and vitamin B6 which improve memory.
        </p>
        <p>
         Walnuts also promote blood flow, allowing efficient delivery of oxygen to the brain. They have anti-inflammatory nutrients which help fight inflammation and protect the brain and body from degenerative conditions.
        </p>
        <p>
         With all the benefits that walnuts have to offer, you may agree with me that going nuts is not always a bad thing. So next time you feel like snacking, go nuts! (by eating a handful of these tasty nuts)
         <span id="more-142">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Quinoa
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="quinoa seeds help to improve memory" class="alignright size-full wp-image-144" height="199" src="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/file/2014/06/quinoa-seeds-help-to-improve-memory.jpg" width="300"/>
         Research has shown that quinoa speeds up information dispensation and improves memory. It is an excellent source of complex carbohydrates, fiber and folic acid. It is also a natural source of zinc, iron and protein. It actually contains all the essential amino acids hence it is a complete protein. It also contains selenium and vitamin E which reduce the risk of heart disease and diabetes. Quinoa can easily become your best brain food because, other than its nutritional value, it is easy to prepare and can be paired with any meal.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Blueberries
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="blueberries" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-151" height="180" src="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/file/2014/06/blueberries1-300x180.jpg" width="300"/>
         These widely available fruits enhance learning capacity and reduce stress. They help keep your memory sharp even during old age. They contain flavonoids which improve communication between neurons, which in turn improves memory, learning, decision-making and other cognitive functions. Flavonoids also provide protection against mental disorders like Parkinsons disease and Alzheimers disease.
        </p>
        <p>
         Boost your memory by eating at least one half-cup of fresh, frozen or freeze-dried blueberries daily.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          More:
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <span style="color: #3366ff;">
            <a href="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/10-healthy-snacks-can-enjoy/" title="10 Healthy Snacks You Can Enjoy">
             <span style="color: #3366ff; text-decoration: underline;">
              10 Surprisingly Healtyh Snacks You Can Enjoy
             </span>
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Oily fish
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="kipper-slices-with-lemon-and-lettuce" class="alignright wp-image-147 size-full" height="388" src="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/file/2014/06/kipper-slices-with-lemon-and-lettuce.jpg" width="300"/>
         When it comes to fish, the best brain food is oily fish. They include herring, sardines and wild salmon. They are a rich source of naturally-occurring omega-3 fatty acids such as EPA and DHA. The two are the most effective omega-3 fats contained in the fish. They occur in a ready-made form, enabling the body to use them easily. They are linked to lower risk of dementia, improved focus and memory.
        </p>
        <p>
         Eating oily fish lowers cell inflammation that brings about loss of memory, and boosts cognitive performance and behavioral function. Consider having a 4-ounce serving of these fish, two to three times a week. (Heres a list of oily fish: Anchovies, bloater, carp, eel, herring, kipper, mackerel, pilchards, salmon, sardines, sprats, swordfish, trout, tune (when fresh) and whitebait)
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Spinach
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_145" style="width: 310px">
         <a href="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/file/2014/06/spinach-and-basil-smoked-salmon-roll.jpg">
          <img alt="spinach and basil smoked salmon roll" class="size-full wp-image-145" height="200" src="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/file/2014/06/spinach-and-basil-smoked-salmon-roll.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Spinach and salmon roll, 2 foods what improve your memory, in 1 dish!
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Spinach is rich in potassium, which maintains the brains electrical conductivity. This enables production of lightning-quick signals between neurons, hence improving thinking and memory capacity.
        </p>
        <p>
         Spinach is packed with antioxidants which protect brain cells from damage. It is a good source of magnesium, folate and vitamins E and K. You should therefore make spinach as well as other green vegetables part of your daily diet.
        </p>
        <h3>
         6. Tomatoes
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="tomatoes help to improve your memory" class="alignright size-full wp-image-146" height="265" src="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/file/2014/06/tomatoes-help-to-improve-your-memory.jpg" width="300"/>
         Tomatoes have a very powerful antioxidant known as lycopene, which protects brain cells from damage by free radicals which contributes to the development of dementia. Daily consumption of lycopene boosts memory. Tomatoes can also help protect your brain from strokes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Be it tomato soup or tomato salad, tomatoes can be added to any meal. They should be eaten on a daily basis.
        </p>
        <h3>
         7. Acorn Squash
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="cooked acorn squash helps to improve memory" class="alignright size-full wp-image-149" height="200" src="http://www.thehealthadventure.org/file/2014/06/cooked-acorn-squash-helps-to-improve-memory.jpg" width="300"/>
         Acorn Squash is a rich source of folic acid. Folic acid plays an important role in increasing the speed of transmission of information through nerve cells and improves memory.
        </p>
