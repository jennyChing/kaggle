          <img alt="Cash Crops" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-8343" height="2334" src="http://infobeautiful3.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/11/1276_Cash-Crops_6thNov.png" width="1276"/>

        <div class="post-content">
         <div class="post-content-inner">
          <p>
           Yep Cannabis makes serious dough. Curious why so many US states  Washington, Oregon, Alaska, Colorado  have decriminalised or even legalized the drug?
          </p>
          <p>
           Because state legislators are getting high on another kind of green.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Colorado State Taxes - Information is Beautiful" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-8317" height="370" src="http://infobeautiful3.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/11/colorado_state_taxes_2.png" width="520"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           How long before other cash-strapped governments around the world follow suit?
          </p>
          <h2>
           Our methodology
          </h2>
          <p>
           We scored some data on yearly harvested area, production weight and wholesale value for the most common food crops from the
           <a href="http://faostat3.fao.org/home/E" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://faostat3.fao.org']);" target="_blank">
            UN Food and Agriculture Organization
           </a>
           , and used it to calculate yield per km2.
          </p>
          <p>
           Then we went sniffing around in various
           <a href="http://www.unodc.org/wdr2014/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.unodc.org']);" target="_blank">
            UN World Drug Reports
           </a>
           and performed some backroom calculations to cook up some broadly comparable figures for illegal drugs  cocaine, heroin and the mighty marijuana.
          </p>
          <p>
           Data on illicit substances is, like the peeps who sell them, more than a bit sketchy. For example, the UN data on dried coca leaf production (the raw material for cocaine powder) is based on only the
           <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-latin-america-28035308" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.bbc.co.uk']);" target="_blank">
            top three coca-producing countries
           </a>
            Bolivia, Colombia and Peru. And the price and purity of coke varies, so we made some assumptions here.
          </p>
