        <header class="visible-desktop">
         <h1 class="nolinks btitle-replacable">
          6 Ways to Up-cycle Household Items into Fire Pits
         </h1>
         <span class="meta">
          <span class="info">
           <span class="category">
            <a href="/lifestyle/family" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Family">
             Family
            </a>
            <a href="/lifestyle" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Lifestyle">
             Lifestyle
            </a>
           </span>
           by
           <a href="/author/abigail-clark" rel="author" title="Posts by Abigail Clark">
            Abigail Clark
           </a>
           <span class="total-share-text">
            <span class="separator">
             |
            </span>
            638 Shares
           </span>
          </span>
          <span>
           <span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </header>
        <div class="google-text-ad">
        </div>
        <div class="div-gpt-ad-content-top adslot visible-desktop" data-dfp="Lifehack_Content_Top_640x180" id="div-gpt-ad-content-top-1" style="width:640px; height:180px;">
        </div>
        <div class="adslot hidden-desktop" data-dfp="Lifehack_Content_Mobile_320x100" id="div-gpt-ad-content-mobile-1" style="width:320px; height:100px; margin-bottom: 25px;">
        </div>
        <div class="post-content">
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you think the green recycling movement is a recent invention, then youve never met a redneck!
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           REDNECK RECYCLING: Consists of recycling, reforming and reusing of everything they get their hands on.
          </li>
          <li>
           Redneck Recycling is not: Separating glass, plastic, paper and aluminum.
          </li>
          <li>
           Reuse, Reduce and Recycle in Redneck terms = Recoup, Rig,Repurpose
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Since most rednecks love nothing more than sitting outside after a long day relaxingwith a beer next to the fire, its no wonder that making DIY recycled fire pits from repurposed scrap materials has practically become an art form in redneck culture. As Jeff Foxworthy might say, you know youre a redneck when your backyard barbecue is made from an abandoned shopping cart . . . or an old tractor tire rim, barrel, or washing machine tub!
         </p>
         <p>
          Looking for an inexpensive way to create the perfect DIY backyard fire pit? Learn the redneck secrets for turning junk into one of these backyard gems.
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Barrel Pit
         </h2>
         <p>
          This fire pit is made from an old barrel and old horseshoes. If you have these two redneck staple pieces lying around your yard (or know of a scrapyard where you can pick them up for next to nothing), youre well on your way to making a portable outdoor fire pit to light up your nights. Find an old grill top in your rummaging? Then get ready for some redneck barbecue!
         </p>
         <h3>
          Materials
         </h3>
         <p>
          Steel barrel
         </p>
         <p>
          4 old horse shoes
         </p>
         <p>
          Salvaged grill top (optional)
         </p>
         <p>
          Metal saw and welding tools
         </p>
         <p>
          Basic cleaning solution
         </p>
         <h3>
          How to Make
         </h3>
         <p>
          <strong>
          </strong>
          Once youve found the perfect barrel, clean it out before proceeding. Since steel barrels are generally used to carry oil or other gunky stuff, you want to make sure all residue is removed first before lighting a fire in it. Use the cleaner for this and rinse thoroughly.
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Measure 18  24 inches from the bottom of the barrel and mark this height at several points around the outside of the barrel.
          </li>
          <li>
           Cut the barrel using a metal saw and then weld around the rim of the barrel so edges are not sharp. Dont know a thing about cutting and welding metal, or dont have the tools? You may want to use the services of a metal welder, hopefully in the form of a redneck friend willing to work for free or for just the cost of materials. A professional welders services for this type of project can range anywhere from $20 to $80.
          </li>
          <li>
           If youre the one doing the work, next, cut vent openings around the bottom section of the barrel (see image) about 7-8 inches apart. Vent openings release excess heat and help prevent wood pieces from overheating and shooting out too much spark.
          </li>
          <li>
           Weld around the cuts to soften the edges.
          </li>
          <li>
           Position horseshoes in parallel pairs around the rim of the barrel and weld each into place to create an imaginary cross.
          </li>
          <li>
           Fill with wood and kindling and enjoy! If you have an old grill top laying around, once the flames have died down to hot coals, place it on top of the horseshoes for an instant barbecue.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Ah, the simple life!
         </p>
         <p>
          Source Credit  http://www.fireplacemall.com
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Repurposed Grill Fire Pit
         </h2>
         <p>
          If lounging in a lawn chair under the stars with your feet stretched out before a roaring fire sounds like a little slice of heaven, heres how to turn this redneck fantasy into redneck reality! Got an old grill tucked away in the back of the shed? Then youre all set.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Materials
         </h3>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Grill bottom
          </li>
          <li>
           Metal Saw
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          How to Make
         </h3>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Remove grill lid and other attached parts, including handles and legs.
          </li>
          <li>
           Reattach handles and legs, using each as a leg for the refashioned fire pit.Reattach only end of the handles and turn the other end down to act as a leg.
          </li>
          <li>
           The fire pit should sit at least 6 inches off the ground. If needed, cut old grill legs to size.
          </li>
          <li>
           Add kindling and fire starter.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Now, the fun part: Stretch out those tired dogs and relax!
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Wheelbarrow Fire Pit
         </h2>
         <p>
          Got an old wheelbarrow? Then youve got yourself a fire pit! Simply add wood and light. If the wheels still work, you can move the fire pit around the yard to keep you warm wherever you go. You can also keep it stationary by blocking the wheels.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Materials
         </h3>
         <p>
          Old Wheelbarrow
         </p>
         <p>
          Working wheels (If you want it to be portable)
         </p>
         <p>
          Cement Bricks
         </p>
         <h3>
          How to Make
         </h3>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Clean your wheelbarrow of any old debris
          </li>
          <li>
           Check the wheels. If you dont want your wheelbarrow to be moved around, wheel to desired location and block in place using the cement bricks. A big old rock will do in a pinch, too!
          </li>
          <li>
           Fill with kindling and scrap wood.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Get ready for a barrow full of redneck fun!
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Shopping Cart Pit
         </h2>
         <p>
          Well lookie here, an abandoned shopping cart with no identifying store name on it whatsoever. Bring it to the dump? Not if youre a redneck! If you can get your hands on some wire mesh, a little scrap metal, and you have some basic know-how of metal welding, the ultimate conversation fire pit could be yours!
         </p>
         <h3>
          Materials
         </h3>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Discarded shopping cart with chrome finish
          </li>
          <li>
           27 x 8 steel lath (wire mesh) to line all of the four sides of the cart and basket
          </li>
          <li>
           Steel drip edge flashing
          </li>
          <li>
           Steelcorner bead
          </li>
          <li>
           3 cookie sheets or flat scrap sheet metal
          </li>
          <li>
           Hinges (To connect the spark screen to the shopping cart)
          </li>
          <li>
           Handle
          </li>
          <li>
           1 pr Steel hurricane ties
          </li>
          <li>
           Nuts and bolts
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          How to Make
         </h3>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Remove all rubber and plastics parts from the cart, including the rubber bumpers on the corners of the basket.This is redneck must!
          </li>
          <li>
           Using the Steel drip edge flashing, build a snug frame for the bottom of the basket. This will help to keep embers from rolling out of the basket.
          </li>
          <li>
           Cut the edges off two cookie sheets (or use scrap sheet metal). Measure and cut so they fit inside the frame as a pan on the bottom of the cart.
          </li>
          <li>
           Line the cart with steel lath (wire mesh). Use large washers and bolts to attach the lath to the sides. For less than $10, a 27 x 8 sheetwill be enough for all 4 sides plus one piece left over to spark screen cover.
          </li>
          <li>
           To make the spark screen cover, cut to match the top of the cart (as a lid). Frame with steel corner bead, using nuts and bolts on all four sides to keep everything in place.
          </li>
          <li>
           Attach a handle to the far end of the lid; attaches hinges on the end closest to the basket.
          </li>
          <li>
           Use hurricane ties to secure the hinges in place.
          </li>
          <li>
           Pretty cool, huh? Now lift the lid and load your cart with kindling.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          If the wheels are still working, this is a completely mobile redneck work of fire pit art. You can even use the undercarriage to store extra wood!
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Old Pot Pit
         </h2>
         <p>
          Grandmas old cast iron or copper pot not getting much use anymore? Put your inner redneck to work and turn that unused cauldron into a fire pit with lots of charm.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Materials
         </h3>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Grandmas old, large cast iron or copper cauldron or pot
          </li>
          <li>
           Bricks or stones
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          How to Make
         </h3>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Place the pot on a non-flammable surface. Concrete, gravel or dirt works fine.
          </li>
          <li>
           Surround the pot with large stones or bricks to prevent the pot from moving around or tipping over.
          </li>
          <li>
           Load with kindling and light.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          If Grandmas got an old grill grate shes willing to part with, youre in business! No more stew, thoughget ready to barbecue!
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Steel Roofing Fire Ring
         </h2>
         <p>
          Have some strips of roofing metal left over from your last repair job? Well, dontcha ya know it, you are in luck. Its finally time to build that backyard fire ring!
         </p>
         <h3>
          Materials
         </h3>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Left over corrugated steel or pole barn roofing
          </li>
          <li>
           Bricks, stone, etc.
          </li>
          <li>
           Metal roofing screws (bolt style with washer)
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          How to Make
         </h3>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Choose the location in your yard where the fire ring will be permanently installed.
          </li>
          <li>
           Clear grass from the area to leave level dirt.
          </li>
          <li>
           Create a loop with the roofing metal in the desired size of the ring.
          </li>
          <li>
           Fasten with roofing screws and washers.
          </li>
          <li>
           Stack bricks or rocks around ring high enough to cover the width of the ring.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Thats pretty darn elegant, if we do say so ourselves. If you like the redneck ethos for recycling wherever you can, but dont necessarily like the redneck look, this project may be you!
         </p>
         <p>
          Use these simple steps to bring out your inner redneck and throw an outdoor party that will wow your friends. Cold beer, warm fire Ah, the simple joys of the redneck life!
