         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           We cant be productive little worker bees all the time. Sometimes we need to unwind, put our feet up, relax a little, possibly vaporise some cacodemons with a plasma rifle. Heres 20 ways to do, so using the magic of Adobe Flash software.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           Be a little careful, though  prolonged playing these games in the office may lead to being asked not to come back to the office any more.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           Believe us, we know. We only wrote this story so we could claim wed been doing research for the last three days.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           This is in no particular order  they are all good. If we've missed any of your favourites, let us know below.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.officegamespot.com/flashgames/doom-triple-pack.htm" target="_blank">
             1. Doom/Hexen/Heretic triple pack
            </a>
            (pictured above)
           </strong>
           <br/>
           Doom. One of the greatest games of all time, now available on Flash. We dont need to sell it to you; it's Doom.
           <strong>
            Warning
           </strong>
           : it is bloody and brutal, and not suitable for children.
          </p>
         </div>

          <p>
           Those of you who grew up with the original will struggle at first, as the alt key no longer triggers sidestepping  you use the A and D keys instead, and turn with the arrows. Dont worry, youll get used to it.
          </p>
          <p>
           Also comes with Hexen and Heretic, the swords-and-sorcery (and blood and guts) follow-ups that used the same engine.
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.gamertech.com/games/game.php?urll=http://www.glenrhodes.com/wolf/myRay.html" target="_blank">
             id Softwares earlier game, Wolfenstein 3D, is also available on Flash
            </a>
           </strong>
           , although its controls are a colossal pain in the etc.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.candystand.com/play/the-space-game" target="_blank">
             2. The Space Game
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://preview.telegraph.co.uk/telegraph/multimedia/archive/01512/The-Space-Game_1512822c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Yeah, its a bit of a boring name. But The Space Game is a marvellous little strategy game.
          </p>
          <p>
           Mine asteroids in deep space while fending off pirate attacks  simple. But the balancing acts between power, defence and mining, and the juggling of resources, makes it engrossing, and powerfully addictive. Dont start playing at work.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.notdoppler.com/magicpen.php" target="_blank">
             3. Magic Pen
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/magicpen_1512809c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           In this sweet, innovative puzzle game, you have to push a crudely drawn ball to collect flags. To do it, you draw your own objects, which fall under gravity and push the ball around. It sounds strange  and it is  but its remarkably intuitive.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.thewayoftheninja.org/n_downloads.html" target="_blank">
             4. N
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/ninja_1512819c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           A frantic ninja leap-em-up in which your tiny little stick figure man hurls his way between various platforms before  in our experience  falling rapidly to his inevitable death. Its engrossing, but its tricky. Very, very tricky.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.handdrawngames.com/DesktopTD/Game.asp" target="_blank">
             5. Desktop Tower Defence
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/towerdefence_1512823c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           There are hundreds of tower defence strategy games on the internet, but this is among the most popular. The concept is the usual: build gun turrets to defend your base against swarms of invading enemies.
          </p>
          <p>
           More unusually, the battle seems to be taking place on top of someones desk. Shades of Micro Machines, there.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.fugly.com/flash/819/flash-tetris.html" target="_blank">
             6. Tetris
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/tetris_1512821c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Its Tetris. Do we need to paint you a picture? Bricks fall down, you rotate them to fit, you make lines, the lines disappear. Russian music plays in the background. Its Tetris. You know Tetris.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.sticksports.com/cricket/" target="_blank">
             7. Stick Cricket
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/cricket_1512800c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           If Football Manager is the game most frequently cited in divorce proceedings, Stick Cricket is probably the game most frequently cited on P45s (or pink slips, if youre American. Is there a Stick Baseball?).
          </p>
          <p>
           Its all about timing and reactions, which is why the author of this piece is so monumentally bad at it.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.y8.com/games/Puzzle_Bobble" target="_blank">
             8. Puzzle Bobble
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/puzzlebobble_1512815c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Gamers of a certain age will remember the splendid Puzzle Bobble arcade machines. This Flash take on it will have the same nostalgic effect on those people as an episode of Round The Twist, or a Wham bar.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its a bit like an upside-down Tetris. Fire the bubbles up to match three or more of a colour and watch them disappear.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.channel4.com/4homes/play-win/games/mansion-impossible/" target="_blank">
             9. Mansion Impossible
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/mansion_1512810c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Buying houses and selling them at a profit might not sound like a game  if anything, it sounds like the lifestyle of the sort of coin-eyed guffawing Toby you try to avoid at parties  but it works surprisingly well.
          </p>
          <p>
           Click on em while theyre cheap, watch the price shoot up, and when you think its peaked, sell it on. You start with 100,000 and your goal is to buy the 10 million mansion at the right end of town. Get yourself on the property ladder at last.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.mariogamesflash.com/super_mario_world_flash_game.html" target="_blank">
             10. Super Mario World
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/mario_1512817c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Another one that needs little introduction. Guide a fat Italian plumber through a weird cartoon world, eat coins, jump on mushrooms and rescue a princess. Obviously.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.wickedpissahgames.com/games/gravitypods/GravityPods.html" target="_blank">
             11. Gravity Pods
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/gravity_1512811c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           A simple concept  use a gun to fire a pellet into a target. But the pellets path is deflected by little gravity pods. Use them to guide the pellet around barriers and into the target.
          </p>
          <p>
           We got stuck on Level 9, but we had other games to review. Were sure you can do better.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www2.b3ta.com/realistic-internet-simulator/" target="_blank">
             12. Kill the Popups
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/killpopup_1512808c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Also known as the Realistic Internet Simulator, and brought to you by the good, if profoundly twisted, folks at
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www2.b3ta.com/" target="_blank">
             b3ta
            </a>
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           You may not know it, but youve played this game before  every time you click on some suspect link on the web, which then floods your desktop with pop-up ads. This is just that, turned into a game. The soundtrack, entertainingly, is the phrase Kill the Popups sung gloomily over and over again to the tune of Ride of the Valkyries.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.bored.com/boomshine/" target="_blank">
             13. Boomshine
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/boomshine_1512796c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           An odd little puzzler, if thats the right word, in which you trigger a chain reaction of explosions in some dots that float around the screen.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.adobe.com/macromedia/holiday2004/" target="_blank">
             14. Penguin jumping
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/penguin_1512813c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Only briefly diverting, but pleasingly surreal. Send a penguin onto a diving board, make him perform a triple-somersault-with-pike or whatever, and get judged on height, rotation and angle of entry by a baby seal, a walrus and a (presumably extremely lost) polar bear.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.play.vg/games/142-Tennis%20Game.html" target="_blank">
             15. Tennis
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/tennis_1512824c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Former owners of the NES, that most ancient of games consoles, will enjoy this tennis game. Its essentially a straight copy of the old Nintendo tennis game. What you do is, right, you hit the ball with the racquet and make it go over the net. Youll soon pick it up.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.clinical-virology.org/killerflu/killerflu.html" target="_blank">
             16. The Killer Flu Game
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/flu_1512803c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Released by the UK Clinical Virology Network, this is intended to educate us all about the spread of pandemic (swine) flu and everyday seasonal flu. Naturally we dont care about that, we just want to spread contagion like a latter-day plague rat, but maybe were learning something while we do it.
          </p>
          <p>
           For a more lethal version, try
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.addictinggames.com/pandemic2.html" target="_blank">
             Pandemic
            </a>
           </strong>
           . It doesnt limit itself to causing the sniffles  you brew up a really nasty virus and spread it worldwide. Unleash your inner pestilence. Go on.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.freerice.com/index.php" target="_blank">
             17. Free Rice
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/freerice_1512802c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Its just Balderdash or Call My Bluff, really  it asks you what a word means, and you have to pick from four possibilities, three of which are false. It gets harder and harder, until youre trying to guess what erose means (jagged, apparently).
          </p>
          <p>
           But for every right answer you get, the site donates 10 grains of rice to the UN World Food Program. So youre ending world hunger as you learn. Sort of.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.sockandawe.com/" target="_blank">
             18. Sock and Awe
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/sockandawe_1512816c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Ever wanted to throw a shoe at former US President George W Bush, but not been in a suitable Baghdad press conference? Nows your chance. Almost 100 million shoes have hit poor Dubyas puzzled-looking face since this simple little game was created.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.helicoptergame.net/" target="_blank">
             19. The Helicopter Game
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/helicopter_1512807c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           As simple as it gets  press the button to make your helicopter go up; let go and it will fall. It automatically flies forwards. Make it avoid obstacles and see how far you can go. If youre anything like us, the answer is not very far.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://jiggmin.com/play_game.php?game=the_Game_of_Disorientation" target="_blank">
             20. The Game of Disorientation
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01512/disorientation_1512801c.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           All you have to do is walk to a target. But the spinning camera and weird zooms make it baffling. Dont play if you are prone to seasickness.
          </p>

         Read Next Widget

        var readNextWidget = (function(){

            var settings = {
                articleImage: "",
                articleTitle: "",
                articleUrl: "#",
                articleId: "",
                currentArticleId: "",
                articleList: [],
                articleListToSkip: [],
                readNextCookieName: "tmg_read_next",
                openButton: "#readNextOpen a",
                closeButton: "#closeBtn a",
                articleContainer: "#readNextArticleContainer",
                widgetContainer: "#readNextWidget",
                displayThumbnail: false,
                panelOpen: false,
                autoOpen: true
            };

            var renderTemplate = function() {
                var template = '&lt;div id="readNextWidget" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextOpen"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div id="readNextOpenInner"&gt;&lt;a href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Next Story&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="icn"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +

                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleInnerWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;p id="readNextLabel"&gt;MOST VIEWED IN NEWS&lt;/p&gt;';
                        
				if(settings.articleImage &amp;&amp; settings.articleImage!="null" &amp;&amp; settings.displayThumbnail){
					template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageThumbnail" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;img src="'+ settings.articleImage +'"/&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';
				}
                        
				template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageTitle" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;' + settings.articleTitle + '&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +

                        '&lt;div id="closeBtn"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="#"&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +

                        '&lt;iframe style="border:none;position:absolute;top:0;left:0; height:100%;width:100%;z-index:-1;" id="iefix" src="about:blank"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' + //fix for IE bug
                        '&lt;/div&gt;';
                return template;
            };


            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }

                        if($(window).scrollTop() + $(window).height() == $(document).height()) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }
                    });
                }
            };

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();

    };
