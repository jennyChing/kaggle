        <div class="article-content rich-text">
         <h5>
          Image: YouTube
         </h5>
         <p>
          First off, if the combination below (
          <a href="http://www.xlr8r.com/news/2014/07/watch-eproms-menacing-new-video-" target="_blank">
           an
           <em>
            XLR8R
           </em>
           premiere
          </a>
          )doesn't make the listener/viewer super-eager to click through to the full world of
          <a href="https://soundcloud.com/eprom" target="_blank">
           West Coast beatsmith Eprom
          </a>
          , don't be deterred. With more room to stretch out, dude's post-trapsludge-electro becomes a rather more satisfying experience, albeit just as fuck-all odd and sketchy. To be honest, I'm still getting aquainted, starting with
          <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/60KWbHt1yMaH7GDj6p5l3X" target="_blank">
           2013's
           <em>
            Halflife
           </em>
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" height="359" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//player.vimeo.com/video/101402434" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="640">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          If "Center of the Sun" feels like being buried alive beneath pixels as dense as diamonds, Eprom's collaboration with Amsterdam producer Akka below feels more like drifting down through the atmosphere beneath the wide canopy of a parachute, supported by pixels as dense as air.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/j7_rqdXlvaY" width="640">
