           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Theres a great point during the process of becoming a horror movie fan where you begin to get this urge to see
            <em>
             everything.
            </em>
            Whatever you hear aboutand in starting to join the horror community, you hear about it more and moreyou want to track down whatever it is. After that passes, theres still the urge to see all the greats, the classics and maybe even hear about why they worked so well.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The books well be taking a look at are great for new fans and hardcore fans alike. Some are fun reference books and some are more analytical, others still provide a bit of both by looking over individual films, franchises or careers. Each of them offers a different kind of experience, a different kind of insight, but all are well worth the read.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <strong>
             <em>
              Fangorias 101 BestHorror Movies Youve Never Seen
             </em>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            This is a great book for someone who has seen all the major franchises and the big classics butwants to take that next step. It was a great help to me starting out and it was because of this resource that I wound up seeing some of my favorite genre films, including
            <em>
             Ginger Snaps, Castle Freak, The Devils Backbone, The Ugly
            </em>
            and so many more. These are some of the more well known titles that Im sure a lot of people have at least heard of, but there are plenty of other, more obscure titles in here that turned out to be impressive gems.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <a href="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/fangoria-logo.jpg">
             <img alt="Fangoria logo" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22557" height="330" src="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/fangoria-logo.jpg" width="800"/>
            </a>
            <strong>
             <em>
              Dark Visions: Conversations With the Masters of the Horror Film
             </em>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Stanley Wiaters
            <em>
             Dark Visions
            </em>
            is such an important book for anyone who either wants to make horror movies or just wants to know more about the people who put these things together. Its a book comprised solely of interviews with some of the genres biggest heavy hitters. Clive Barker, John Carpenter, Sam Raimi, Tom Savini, Robert Englund and so many more give compelling interviews, talking about not only their own careers but what they think makes the genre as a whole so compelling and important.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <a href="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-04-at-4.37.32-PM.png">
             <img alt="Dark Visions" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22558" height="400" src="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-04-at-4.37.32-PM.png" width="800"/>
            </a>
            <strong>
             <em>
              The Films of John Carpenter
             </em>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Hes made some of the biggest hits in horror history, from
            <em>
             Halloween
            </em>
            and
            <em>
             The Thing
            </em>
            to pieces like
            <em>
             The Fog
            </em>
            and
            <em>
             Prince of Darkness.
            </em>
            Not to mention work hes done outside the genre, like the unbeatable
            <em>
             Escape from New York
            </em>
            and
            <em>
             Big Trouble in Little China.
            </em>
            This book is an insightful look into the directors career, examining the origins of each film as well as analyzing them individually to get a good look at both what worked and what didnt.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <a href="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/john-carpenter-wide.jpg">
             <img alt="horror director John Carpenter" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16311" height="400" src="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/john-carpenter-wide.jpg" width="800"/>
            </a>
            <strong>
             <em>
              Crystal Lake Memories: The Complete History of
             </em>
             Friday the 13
             <sup>
              th
             </sup>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            This one may have just been turned into a massive documentary, but that doesnt mean the book itself isnt worth reading. There are many interviews here that are not featured in the documentary and, more than that, theres something about the book as a whole that just feelsspecial. This is not only the best book chronicling the history of a single franchise, but is also one of the most honest chronicles of the hardships of independent filmmaking.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <a href="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/crystal-lake-memories.jpg">
             <img alt="Crystal Lake Memories" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22559" height="400" src="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/crystal-lake-memories.jpg" width="800"/>
            </a>
            <strong>
             <em>
              Going to Pieces: The Rise and Fall of the Slasher Film
             </em>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            Like the previous entry, this one was also turned into an important documentary.
            <em>
             Going to Pieces
            </em>
            provides a complete chronicle of one of the most infamous movements in horror history. There are a lot of books on slashers out there, Ill admit, but this is both the most insightful and the most comprehensive. It looks at not only why the slasher movement came about when it did, but why it fizzled out. Its an impressive tome that no slasher fan should miss.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <a href="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/going-to-pieces.jpg">
             <img alt="Going to Pieces: Rise and Fall of the Slasher Film" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22560" height="400" src="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/going-to-pieces.jpg" width="800"/>
            </a>
            <strong>
             <em>
              Chain Saw Confidential
             </em>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <em>
             Chain Saw Confidential
            </em>
            is a book by original Leatherface actor Gunnar Hansen that is the most brutally honest, realest account of the making of the original
            <em>
             Texas Chain Saw Massacre
            </em>
            that any fan could ever get their hands on. It allows fans to see the classic with new eyes to find out what a hard, grueling and frequently near-fatal shoot it actually was.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <a href="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/gunnar-hansen-confidential1.png">
             <img alt="Chainsaw Confidential by Gunnar Hansen" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22561" height="400" src="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/gunnar-hansen-confidential1.png" width="800"/>
            </a>
            <strong>
             <em>
              Men, Women and Chainsaws
             </em>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <em>
             Men, Women and Chainsaws
            </em>
            is one of the most essential books on horror ever. It is an amazing debate and analysis on the place of gender in the horror film. Are horror movies inherently misogynist, sexist, or are they feminist in that they depict a woman in a position of empowerment. Even for those not interested in deep analysis of the genre, the book addresses many questions that horror fans get asked on a regular basis.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            <a href="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Stretch-on-mountain-with-chainsaw.jpg">
             <img alt="Stretch with chainsaw on the mountain" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22231" height="400" src="http://wickedhorror.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Stretch-on-mountain-with-chainsaw.jpg" width="800"/>
            </a>
           </p>
