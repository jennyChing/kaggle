         <h2>
          <a href="/users/modhomeecteacher/posts/8316-how-to-scan-your-favorite-dress-to-make-wallpaper">
           How To Scan Your Favorite Dress to Make Wallpaper
          </a>
         </h2>
         <div class="meta">
          <strong class="author">
           By:
           <a href="/users/modhomeecteacher">
            Modhomeecteacher
           </a>
          </strong>
          <em class="date">
           Apr 26, 2010
          </em>
         </div>
         <p>
          <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
           <img alt="created at: 04/26/2010" class="lazyload" data-cfsrc="//images.curbly.com/0JbX7ElbmTmiAaeMAzQcMI2FNQI=/567x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8700/wallpaperprojects.jpg" data-pinsrc="http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8700/wallpaperprojects.jpg" data-src="//images.curbly.com/0JbX7ElbmTmiAaeMAzQcMI2FNQI=/567x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8700/wallpaperprojects.jpg" height="575" src="//images.curbly.com/0awxQMll5-AVQXdI_vHJ2J2sesw=/567x0/smart/filters:strip_icc():blur(2):quality(10)/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8700/wallpaperprojects.jpg" width="567"/>
           <meta content="//images.curbly.com/0JbX7ElbmTmiAaeMAzQcMI2FNQI=/567x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8700/wallpaperprojects.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          The first time I used fabric to make "wallpaper" was when I was working on a
          <a href="http://www.apartmenttherapy.com/ny/jan-jumpstart-2008-east/jan-jump-winners-most-likely-to-be-copied-042477">
           nightstand
          </a>
          for
          <a href="http://www.apartmenttherapy.com/ny/sales-events-calendar/blueprint-at-the-january-jumpstart-project-035231">
           ApartmentTherapy's January Jumpstart project
          </a>
          . I realized I could photocopy the fabric I was using to upholster the outside, and adhere the paper version of it on the inside of the nightstand.
          <span class="pagebreak">
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="centered">
          <div style="margin: 80px auto; display: table;">
           <div id="div-gpt-ad-1392309948904-13">
            <script type="text/javascript">
             ybotq.push(function() {
  googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1392309948904-13'); });
});
            </script>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p id="jump">
          Never did I imagine making enough pages to wallpaper an entire wall out of my favorite fabric.
          <a href="http://www.shelterpop.com/2010/02/18/3-diy-wallpaper-projects-with-wow-factor/">
           ShelterPop's review
          </a>
          of Derek and Lauren's book,
          <a href="http://search.barnesandnoble.com/Wallpaper-Projects/Derek-Fagerstrom/e/9780811867061/?itm=3&amp;usri=wallpaper+projects">
           Wallpaper Projects: 50 Craft and Design Ideas for Your Home, from Accents to Art
          </a>
          , reminded me of all the fabulous things that you can create with wallpaper. Derek and Lauren are regular contributors to
          <a href="http://www.designspongeonline.com/">
           DesignSponge
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <span itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
           <img alt="created at: 04/26/2010" class="lazyload" data-cfsrc="//images.curbly.com/kQHDhjekWpkqukhfjDrYDalFOz0=/567x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8704/fabricwallpaper.jpg" data-pinsrc="http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8704/fabricwallpaper.jpg" data-src="//images.curbly.com/kQHDhjekWpkqukhfjDrYDalFOz0=/567x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8704/fabricwallpaper.jpg" height="360" src="//images.curbly.com/gjxODIlgN7qGEDg6YwVhyt8bK_c=/567x0/smart/filters:strip_icc():blur(2):quality(10)/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8704/fabricwallpaper.jpg" width="567"/>
           <meta content="//images.curbly.com/kQHDhjekWpkqukhfjDrYDalFOz0=/567x0/filters:no_upscale()/http://assets.curbly.com/photos/0000/0007/8704/fabricwallpaper.jpg" itemprop="contentUrl"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Here's what you need to make wallpaper pieces from your favorite dress or skirt:
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <br/>
          </strong>
          favorite fabric
         </p>
         <p>
          white printer paper
         </p>
         <p>
          computer with scanner, printer, color photocopier
         </p>
         <p>
          level
         </p>
         <p>
          pencil
         </p>
         <p>
          metal ruler
         </p>
         <p>
          wallpaper paste and brush
         </p>
         <p>
          plastic float
         </p>
         <p>
          sponge
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          See how to do this, and more, on
          <a href="http://www.shelterpop.com/2010/02/18/3-diy-wallpaper-projects-with-wow-factor/">
           ShelterPop
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          *When I used photocopies to do a decoupage project, I sprayed them with a water based acrylic sealer before submerging them in water to keep the color from running. I'm not sure how wallpaper paste will affect color copies.
         </p>
