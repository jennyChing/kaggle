                      <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death" linkindex="67" rel="wikipedia nofollow" title="Death">
                       death
                      </a>
                      ; therefore,a coroner could not write thaton the certificate.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      At that time, we knew so little about SIDS. statistics showed it was usually a male, usually happening in the spring, and usually around 3:00am. We also knew that WV had the second highest incidence of SIDS. only VA had higher statistics because
                      <a class="zem_slink" href="http://www.dixiechicks.com/" linkindex="68" rel="homepage nofollow" title="Dixie Chicks">
                       DC
                      </a>
                      was lumped in with their totals. Some families has such a large number of SIDS, that the children were not name until 2 or 3 years of age. and, parents didn't bond with their children until they were named.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      The grant was to investigate the childrenwe termed "near" SIDS cases. We offered a free clinic for parents and near SIDS
                      <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infant" linkindex="69" rel="wikipedia nofollow" title="Infant">
                       infants
                      </a>
                      . We gave them free round-trip transportation, food, physical and psychological
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      examinations. Further, we gave them free medication, free mattress monitors and an alarm system. All they really needed to do was to arrive at our free clinic once a month. Some came to the clinic.some did not. The clinical nurse, Jodi, and I went to visit the homes of the no-shows.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Some homes were closed to us....no strangers permitted. Shotguns were produced to chase us off the property. Some mothers were interested, but the child's father refused. Ok, so the WV home visits happened almost 20 years ago. Some of these visits also happened in the deepest recesses of WV: the hollars.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Ok, so 20 years has passed, and my town has almost 50,000 people. I'm not in a hollar. Aren't we more educated? Aren't wemore informed? What's happened to common sense? We give free pre-natal care.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      What about post-natal care? Mandatory
                      <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parenting" linkindex="70" rel="wikipedia nofollow" title="Parenting">
                       parenting
                      </a>
                      classes?
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      All in all, I do a good job at work. for your family and for your child(ren). Why aren'tthose mothersdoing a good job?
