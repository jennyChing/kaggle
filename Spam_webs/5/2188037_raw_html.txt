      <h2 class="posttitle">
       How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples
      </h2>
      <div class="postmeta">
       <em>
        posted on
       </em>
       October 14, 2014
       <span class="break">
        //
       </span>
       <div class="ccount">
        <a href="http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/#comments">
         8
         <em>
          comments
         </em>
        </a>
       </div>
       <span class="break">
        //
       </span>
       <a class="zilla-likes" href="#" id="zilla-likes-11729" title="Like this">
        <span class="zilla-likes-count">
         0
        </span>
        <span class="zilla-likes-postfix">
        </span>
       </a>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
       <img alt="IMG_0222" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11750" height="900" src="http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0222.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/&amp;media=http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0222.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples')">
       </span>
      </span>
      <br/>
      Well, howdy folks!! I am busy in northern Washington on a blog trip just enjoying the chilly fall weather and gorgeous changing leaves! Youll be able to read a lot more about my stay in the next few weeks, so stay tuned. But in the mean time, this trip is making me fall more and more in love with the Fall season. Every season is my favorite for different reasons, but I have a special place in my heart for Fall. Mainly because I love the cooler temperatures, the pumpkin recipes, the heartier chilis and stews, Halloween, Thanksgiving andcaramel apples!
     </p>
     <p>
      <em>
       You had to have seen that coming.
      </em>
     </p>
     <p>
      Growing up, my Mom never made caramel apples with me. Probably because I hated apples {and most fruit} with a passion. It wasnt until years and years later that I embraced that food group. Luckily, my children have not inherited my strange waysthat I know of. If youre struggling to get your kids to eat fruit, just dip it into some caramel, roll it in some Oreos and drizzle it all with chocolate. Its REALLY healthy.
      <span id="more-11729">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Ok maybe not. But!! My kiddos certainly ate all the caramel apple slices we gave them. Can you blame them? These dudes are gorgeous and are covered with all sorts of goodies! Socant say that I do. Incase you have never made caramel apples before, there are a few little tips and tricks you need to be successful. I cover them all below in the step-by-step photos, so get a-scrolling.
     </p>
     <p>
      Heres how to make them:
     </p>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
       <img alt="IMG_0107_edited-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11757" height="900" src="http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0107_edited-1.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/&amp;media=http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0107_edited-1.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples')">
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Youre going to want to stock up on copious amounts of sweet treats. Most of these coatings are totally optional so pick what youd prefer. I wanted to show you lots of options, so I went a little overboard.
     </p>
     <p>
      Im so nice like that!
     </p>
     <p>
      Some of this stuff will be used as garnishes and some will be used in the actual caramel to coat the apples.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
       <img alt="IMG_0116" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11731" height="400" src="http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0116.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/&amp;media=http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0116.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples')">
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      One of the very first things we need to do is unwrap a bunch of caramels. Im using two 11-oz. bags; enough to coat 12 apples. I employed my husband and daughter for this job.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
       <img alt="IMG_0122" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11732" height="900" src="http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0122.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/&amp;media=http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0122.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples')">
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      I told her to work harder and she laughed at me.whatever.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
       <img alt="IMG_0130" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11733" height="900" src="http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0130.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/&amp;media=http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0130.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples')">
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Id also recommend getting your toppings all laid out and ready to go. I have nuts, chocolate chips, mini M&amp;Ms, crushed oreos, coconut, graham cracker crumbs and marshmallows. Just about anything you like could get stuck onto a caramel apple as long as its cut into small pieces.
     </p>
     <p>
      I used paper plates for easy clean up but that is optional too.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
       <img alt="IMG_0138" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11734" height="900" src="http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0138.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/&amp;media=http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0138.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples')">
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Ok, so the caramels should be unwrapped and put to the side while we prep the apples.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
       <img alt="IMG_0139" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11735" height="900" src="http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0139.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/&amp;media=http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0139.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples')">
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Pull your 12 apples out of the fridge and wash them well. I used organic apples because they dont have that wax coating on them. The wax coating can prevent the caramel from sticking so for this recipe specifically, grab yourself some organic apples. I used golden delicious and braeburn but honestly, any good looking apples that you like will work.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
       <img alt="IMG_0153" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11737" height="900" src="http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0153.jpg" width="600"/>
       <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.laurenslatest.com/make-gourmet-caramel-apples/&amp;media=http://www.laurenslatest.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/IMG_0153.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Gourmet Caramel Apples')">
       </span>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Dry the apples off really well and pull the stems out of each one.
     </p>
     <p>
      <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">

      <span class="item">
       <h2 class="fn">
        Gourmet Caramel Apples
       </h2>
      </span>
      <div class="summary" style="clear:left">
       <p>
        Grab a few simple pantry ingredients and whip up a gorgeous batch of gourmet looking {but totally easy!} caramel apples at home!
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="time">
       <p>
        <em>
         Yield:
        </em>
        <span class="yield">
         12 caramel apples
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Prep Time:
        </em>
        <span class="preptime">
         40 minutes
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Cook Time:
        </em>
        <span class="cooktime">
         0 minutes
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Total Time:
        </em>
        <span class="duration">
         40 minutes
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="ingredients">
       <h3 style="clear:left">
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <div class="ingredient">
        <p>
         12 large cold organic apples, any kind you like
         <br/>
         2 bags caramels, unwrapped {11 oz. bags}
         <br/>
         2-3 tablespoons cold water
        </p>
        <p>
         any desired coatings/toppings such as:
         <br/>
         shredded coconut
         <br/>
         mini chocolate chips
         <br/>
         graham cracker crumbs
         <br/>
         mini M&amp;M's
         <br/>
         mini marshmallows
         <br/>
         crushed cookies
         <br/>
         chopped nuts
        </p>
        <p>
         variety of chocolate chips such as:
         <br/>
         white
         <br/>
         milk
         <br/>
         semi sweet
         <br/>
         dark
         <br/>
         peanut butter
         <br/>
         butterscotch
        </p>
        <p>
         festive sprinkles, optional
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="directions">
       <h3 style="clear:left">
        Directions:
       </h3>
       <div class="instructions">
        <p>
         Wash cold apples well and dry with clean kitchen cloth. Remove stems and push craft sticks {or cleaned sticks from the garden} 1 inch into the apples to secure them into place. Set aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         Unwrap caramels and place into a large bowl with water. Set aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         Place any desired toppings into dishes or onto paper plates.
        </p>
        <p>
         To heat chocolate, place chocolate chips into heat-proof containers. Turn crockpot settings to high. Bring small pot of water to boil. Pour water into crockpot and reduce temperature to warm. Place containers of chocolate into hot water to melt and keep warm.
        </p>
        <p>
         Back to the bowl of caramels and water: microwave 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally to melt caramel completely.
        </p>
        <p>
         To make caramel apples, take a cold, clean and dry apple and dip it into the warm caramel. Turn apple completely around in the hot caramel to coat evenly. Let the excess drip off the apple for a good 30 seconds before dipping into prepared toppings. Place apple onto sheet of wax paper. Continue dipping and coating apples until all are covered. Feel free to refrigerate 5-10 minutes to solidify caramel faster. {Totally optional step!} After 3 or 4 apples, you may have to reheat the caramel if you notice its getting thicker and thus harder to get a thin, even coat on your apples.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remove from fridge and drizzle melted chocolate over the tops of each apple using a squeeze bottle or piping bag. Top with sprinkles. Once chocolate is set, cut into slices and serve. OR wrap up in cellophane bags as gifts.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
