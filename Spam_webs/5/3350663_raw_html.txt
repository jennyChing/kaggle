     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Fortress
       </b>
      </i>
      is the second studio album by Canadian band
      <a href="/wiki/Protest_the_Hero" title="Protest the Hero">
       Protest the Hero
      </a>
      which was released on January 29, 2008 on
      <a href="/wiki/Vagrant_Records" title="Vagrant Records">
       Vagrant Records
      </a>
      in the US and
      <a href="/wiki/Underground_Operations" title="Underground Operations">
       Underground Operations
      </a>
      in Canada.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
       Overview
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortress_(Protest_the_Hero_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Fortress
      </i>
      was recorded at Silo Studios in
      <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">
       Hamilton, Ontario
      </a>
      during the summer of 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The album contains 10 tracks broken into three movements, separated by piano interludes, and is approximately 41 minutes in length. The band has stated that the sound is similar to
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kezia_(Protest_the_Hero_album)" title="Kezia (Protest the Hero album)">
        Kezia
       </a>
      </i>
      but with a "less poppy and more technical metal" influence.
      <i>
       Fortress
      </i>
      does not revolve around a single concept, unlike their previous album. Instead, it is more akin to "goddess worship in the tradition of
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_Graves" title="Robert Graves">
       Robert Graves
      </a>
      ," according to their bassist and lyricist Arif Mirabdolbaghi:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "It has to do with goddess worship, and how there has been this degendering of the Lord and Savior, and the suppressed feminine. A lot of it is based in Genghis Khan and old Irish Mythology, about the rise and fall of the Goddess of the forest. [The theme] can be more appreciated by more people. If I had to reduce it to its simplest form, the concept is about the re-emerging of goddess worship and the erosion of faith in scientific process."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         3
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Also, states lead singer Rody Walker:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "This album is nothing to be intellectualized. All talk of wonder, pathos and optimism aside. I feel its a very natural progression for us. A natural progression into further obscurity. I am of the belief a lot of the people who work for us were hoping for a stab at a more commercially viable album, however we wrote what we wanted without linear boundaries and created something less commercial than ever. Some fans of the band will hate it. And some people who hate the band will love it. All others can rot."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         4
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>

      </a>
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template">
       <span style="white-space: nowrap;">

     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Fortress
      </i>
      , like its predecessor
      <i>
       Kezia
      </i>
      , achieved universal acclaim with high regards from many major publications, receiving a comprehensive score of 86 on
      <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
       Metacritic
      </a>
      making it the 12th best reviewed album on the site for 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Protest_the_Hero:_Fortress_on_Metacritic_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Protest_the_Hero:_Fortress_on_Metacritic-18">

         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Tyler Patrick Munro of SputnikMusic states: "The album improves on everything established on
      <i>
       Kezia
      </i>
      (right down to the much more natural sounding piano codas), and it does so without the sometimes blatant repetition of its predecessor." He cites the track "Bone Marrow," stating: "While 'Bone Marrow' places more emphasis on the underpinning synths of the first two tracks, its heavy orchestration still takes a backseat to its varying structure, which fluidly transits from hyper-melodic up-tempo to ball-crushing gutturals and chugged syncopation, all of which are made twice as effective by the interwoven bass slapping," and compares the style of the opener "Bloodmeat" to the dissonance of
      <a href="/wiki/The_Dillinger_Escape_Plan" title="The Dillinger Escape Plan">
       The Dillinger Escape Plan
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Converge_(band)" title="Converge (band)">
       Converge
      </a>
      .

      </i>

      <i>
       Kezia.
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sputnikmusic_review_of_Fortress_by_John._A._Hanson_20-0">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-gap">
       Pre-gap
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortress_(Protest_the_Hero_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pre-gap">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      There are 7 songs on this album that contain pre-gaps. The pre-gap breakdown is as follows.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Track -1, before "Bloodmeat," -0:53. Can be found by rewinding from the CD from the beginning. This pre-gap is not detected by iTunes.
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -3, before "Bone Marrow," -0:18. Added to the length of "The Dissentience" when uploaded to iTunes. Actual length of "The Dissentience" is 4:05.
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -4, before "Sequoia Throne," -0:55. Added to the length of "Bone Marrow." Actual length of "Bone Marrow" is 4:35.
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -5, before "Palms Read," -0:04. Added to the length of "Sequoia Throne." Actual length of "Sequoia Throne" is 3:08.
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -6, before "Limb From Limb," -1:22. Added to the length of "Palms Read." Actual length of "Palms Read" is 3:44.
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -8, before "Wretch," -0:44. Added to the length of "Spoils." Actual length of "Spoils" is 2:59.
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -9, before "Goddess Bound," -0:01. Added to the length of "Wretch." Actual length of Wretch is 4:11.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Tracks -1, -4 and -8 contain piano instrumentals played by guitarist Luke Hoskin.
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -3 serves as an intro to "Bone Marrow."
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -5 is an audio sample from a song by
       <a href="/wiki/Tom_T._Hall" title="Tom T. Hall">
        Tom T. Hall
       </a>
       called "
       <a href="/wiki/The_Year_That_Clayton_Delaney_Died" title="The Year That Clayton Delaney Died">
        The Year That Clayton Delaney Died
       </a>
       " from the album
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/In_Search_of_a_Song" title="In Search of a Song">
         In Search of a Song
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Track -9 is an audio sample from the movie
       <a href="/wiki/Starship_Troopers" title="Starship Troopers">
        Starship Troopers
       </a>
       . This 2 second clip contains one of the characters from the movie saying "You wanted to see the galaxy?"
      </li>
     </ul>
