       <h2 id="post-362734">
        Katee Sackhoff Stars in R-Rated 14-Minute 'Power Rangers' Short
       </h2>
       <p class="date">
        by
        <a href="http://www.firstshowing.net/author/anderton/" rel="author" title="Posts by Ethan Anderton">
         Ethan Anderton
        </a>
        <br/>
        February 24, 2015
        <br/>
       </p>
       <!--INFOLINKS_ON-->
       <div align="center" class="image" style="text-align:center;">
        <a class="watch" href="http://www.firstshowing.net/2015/katie-sackhoff-stars-in-r-rated-14-minute-power-rangers-short/">
         <span>
          <!--Watch-->
         </span>
         <img alt="Power Rangers" border="0" height="168" src="http://media2.firstshowing.net/firstshowing/img9/powerrangers-helmet-ground-tsr.jpg" width="350"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        Previously we've seen a couple of impressive short films from producer
        <strong>
         Ari Shankar
        </strong>
        from the comic book universe. Joe Lynch directed
        <a href="http://www.firstshowing.net/2013/joe-lynchs-new-short-film-truth-in-journalism-has-a-marvel-twist/">
         <em>
          Truth in Journalism
         </em>
        </a>
        , a take off on the Spider-Man character Venom, and Mike Pecci directed
        <em>
         The Dead Can't Be Distracted
        </em>
        , which was a
        <a href="http://www.firstshowing.net/2013/watch-impressive-trailer-for-punisher-fan-film-marvel-has-shut-down/">
         bootleg version of The Punisher
        </a>
        . Now Shankar is back with
        <strong>
         <em>
          Power/Rangers
         </em>
        </strong>
        , a gritty, R-rated, 14-minute short film directed by
        <strong>
         Joseph Kahn
        </strong>
        (
        <em>
         Torque
        </em>
        ) that adapts "Mighty Morphin' Power Rangers." The film certainly puts an interesting twist on the mythology of the Japanese imported action adventure, and it's much different from the
        <a href="http://www.firstshowing.net/2014/lionsgates-new-power-rangers-movie-set-for-a-july-2016-release/">
         eventual big screen take on the way
        </a>
        . Plus, it stars
        <strong>
         James Van Der Beek
        </strong>
        and
        <strong>
         Katie Sackhoff
        </strong>
        , which is just completely surreal.
        <em>
         Watch
        </em>
        !
        <span id="more-362734">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align:center;">
        Here's Joseph Kahn &amp; Adi Shankar's short film
        <strong>
         <em>
          Power/Rangers
         </em>
        </strong>
        (via
        <a href="http://www.hitfix.com/motion-captured/world-exclusive-what-are-katee-sackhoff-and-james-van-der-beek-doing-in-joseph-kahns-powerrangers">
         HitFix
        </a>
        ):
       </p>
       <p>
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="337" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/vw5vcUPyL90" width="599">
        </iframe>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align:center;">
        And if you're wondering just why Shankar decided to make this movie, here you go:
       </p>
       <p>
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="337" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/dDKR_2HqymU" width="599">
        </iframe>
       </p>
       <p>
        Interestingly enough, Kahn wasn't even a fan of
        <em>
         Power Rangers
        </em>
        in the least, but he was intrigued by doing a completely different version of something he viewed as a silly property. He says, "I think the trick that I really wanted to do with this was to make that dark and gritty version that everybody keeps talking about, but really do it. Really see if I could totally accomplish it with essentially a really incredible incredibly silly property. So it's pretty self-indulgent, but that doesn't make it any less of an impressive short film and something fans may have been anxious to see realized.
       </p>
       <p>
        However, if you're hoping Lionsgate will hire Kahn for the
        <a href="http://www.firstshowing.net/2014/lionsgates-new-power-rangers-movie-set-for-a-july-2016-release/">
         developing big screen version
        </a>
        , don't count on it. First of all, Lionsgate would never go in this direction for a tentpole adaptation of
        <em>
         Power Rangers
        </em>
        . Second of all, Kahn just isn't interested now that he's done this short, "The irony here is that I wouldnt even want to make
        <em>
         Power Rangers: The Movie
        </em>
        for real. Like if I had to make a Power Rangers movie, this is it. Its 14 minutes long and its violent and this is what I have in me. If they offered me the 200 million version, the PG-13 version, I literally wouldnt do it. Its just not interesting to me. But at least we got this short.
        <strong>
         Cool?
        </strong>
       </p>
