            <p data-textannotation-id="233089c5ead324695d9407dc1190b2a3">
             Before getting into the nitty gritty details of a budget, its important to realize that not all budgets work for everyone. Thats no reason to give up though!
             <a href="http://blog.readyforzero.com/how-to-create-a-budget/" target="_blank">
              Budget planning
             </a>
             can benefit from careful consideration of all variables involvedincluding your unique perspective and personal strengths. Your finances are personalized and your budgeting strategy will be too. Here are some ways to go about discovering what budgeting techniques will help you to manage your finances most effectively:
            </p>
            <h3 data-textannotation-id="b7e94874afe61527d133ce268e3c66ad">
             Assess Your Financial Strengths and Put Them Into Action
            </h3>
            <p data-textannotation-id="4a51fc53ecc4169d920bd91afc394ec9">
             When I realized just
             <a href="http://blog.readyforzero.com/spend-less-save-more/" target="_blank">
              how many budgeting strategies
             </a>
             there were to try out, I decided to focus on how I might be able to fit in some of my personal strengths.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="a4a5c8c226b9b0ccaa6e8844a930147a">
             For instance, I usually put a lot of momentum behind a project in the beginning so I wanted to find a budgeting technique that was easy to implement. Im also most productive when I first wake up, and more likely to finish problems if I make them a part of my daily ritual. These may seem like small things, but can actually be incredibly effective when put to good use.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="e57569922a50212a78a75303ad996e62">
             What
             <a href="http://lifehacker.com/when-are-you-most-productive-during-the-day-1222393450" x-inset="1">
              time
             </a>
             are you most productive? Do you work better alone or with someone else? Are you more successful with "a little every day" or "a lot all at once" strategies. Basically, look at where you can
             <em>
              easily
             </em>
             see yourself bettering your budget. Everyone has their sweet spot for productivity, and strengths that support it. What you find to be lesss challenging is just as important to consider as what you
             <em>
              do
             </em>
             find challenging.
            </p>

            <h3 data-textannotation-id="f127c4a6c6396e2fe5feb64762298778">
             Pinpoint Your Biggest Financial Weaknesses and Give Them Extra Attention and TLC
            </h3>
            <p data-textannotation-id="96e7eea111e810e20e3096b7953b8bef">
             As mentioned above, I found spreadsheet based budget-tracking to be difficult so I tried to pinpoint why exactly. First, I narrowed it down to three general "weaknesses":
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li data-textannotation-id="8252ec2741a12706142e3dbfdb1f0a5a">
              <strong>
               Distraction:
              </strong>
              Id inevitably become distracted, too busy, or simply bored with the number input.
             </li>
             <li data-textannotation-id="429e0a886ee18590ee9f05d4f116bcf9">
              <strong>
               Frequency of mistakes:
              </strong>
              Id forget to save a receipt which resulted in holes of mystery spending in the budget.
             </li>
             <li data-textannotation-id="f03aa318d9dc74d952bbb59522261b50">
              <strong>
               Negative emotional response:
              </strong>
              I became discouraged and fed up when faced with the above spending holes.
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p data-textannotation-id="0cfa387cc49431f7a209480eb0867062">
             Next, I looked at ways to address these personal weaknesses and develop useful ways to combat them. I personally find looking at only numbers to be less motivating and so I find it harder to focus on spreadsheets. So I started using charts and graphs to visualize the breakdown. This led me to be less discouraged with my progress. I started taking photos of my receipts with my phone so that I wouldnt lose them. Essentially, I improvised on the things I found to be most challenging about budgeting, and worked to create a structure to solve them.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="ac8357ddd2ecff49517d39913c1a1449">
             If youre struggling with a budget, it can be extremely helpful to look at the specifics that are making it difficult. Those are the areas that could likely use a little bit of adjustment or attention.
            </p>
            <h3 data-textannotation-id="5c3406d43c46843a38fc65044951acad">
             Look at Your Greatest Personal Motivators
            </h3>
            <p data-textannotation-id="24616aea5f01f188283c775fa45d4ce4">
             If asked to draw conclusions about myself, I might say that Im more on the artsy side of the spectrum. I love words, writing and reading. So when it came to motivators in my budgeting plan I looked at how I could incorporate my natural inclinations with my budgeting techniques.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="8e8a6c84adfbfb36fe2da4875a7bb239">
             I found that I often lost interest when I wasnt inspired. To overcome the apathy, that meant taking time to read budgeting articles when I was feeling lackluster and seeking inspiration from other peoples written success stories.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="e5fc525d4cf01deb3bd055bd9b3578e9">
             Aligning some of your natural interests with your goals can actually be a great way to work them into your lifestyle. Are you motivated by seeing the actual transfer of cash, or by balanced numbers on a page? Regular visual reminders of successes, or the feeling you have after youve reached an earned longer-term goals? Try and find some easy motivators to help regularly inject energy into your budgeting plan. It can be extremely useful in sustaining a budget long term.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="ea090873604b317a352b555d26775f68">
             And last but not least
            </p>
            <h2 data-textannotation-id="b4f8d8f1065857977aa94bb547d570ac">
             Keep your learning style in mind
            </h2>
            <p data-textannotation-id="0501c537e7dacbd4b7a46bdb9d21c4d0">
             "One size does not fit all" is a pretty valuable phrase to keep in mindparticularly when it comes to creating your budgeting strategy. Some people are more visual than others and some people respond more strongly to auditory cues. Learning styles are a great place to look when youre looking to explore or incorporate a personalized strategy into your long-term budgeting plan:
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="226bc701fc1f014590efc2b9ee550a61">
             <strong>
              Visual Learner
             </strong>
            </h4>
            <p data-textannotation-id="90a9022bf583bc3fcc6f5824ee26d54d">
             You learn best by visualizing things. You might find graphs or charts useful when breaking down your spending.
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="81c9d5a7e8b6b88821975de2719a487d">
             <strong>
              Auditory Learner
             </strong>
            </h4>
            <p data-textannotation-id="4708097ef19e3d4a3c742ee56fb5977e">
             You find music or sound a beneficial aid to learning. You could consider setting specialized tones along with budgeting reminders on your phone, or creating a specialized playlist you put on in the background every time you balance your budget.
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="e681e6d17a6d41d3eec7f71c5acab1b2">
             <strong>
              Physical Learner
             </strong>
            </h4>
            <p data-textannotation-id="90cc3901eed3bcc7db17901eee5ee1c3">
             Sense of touch or physical movement is important. Envelope saving, where you can see and touch exactly where your money or savings are being put could be powerful.
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="77f3d860d8dd36af76c7e77e01e9bdec">
             <strong>
              Logical Learner
             </strong>
            </h4>
            <p data-textannotation-id="57400a06c8bcd264b7bdcfe7382baa61">
             Logical thinking or reasoning helps you to learn best. Luckily for you, a lot of financial advice is structured around this kind of learning. Spreadsheets, number crunching tactics and bigger financial picture approach could add strength to your budget.
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="7e1f283f3654af6929c826c7665f37b7">
             <strong>
              Social Learner
             </strong>
            </h4>
            <p data-textannotation-id="72a1062e316678eb4107ccb9e3e8499a">
             Learning in a group setting is powerful for a social learner. Having an accountability buddy might be useful and inspire you to stick more closely to your budget!
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="db8df4c6ad0cc9b6056180805b0b3e4a">
             Everyone has their own style and their own set of experiences and preferences. I wasnt a spreadsheet budgeterbut thats not to say you wont find them helpful. But sticking to a budget that doesnt fit with your lifestyle is incredibly difficult to stick with! Try out different techniques, adjust and readjust every few weeks or months. As you find your preferred budgeting style, it will become easier to make it a part of your regular routine.
