      <h1>
       Nissan e-NV-200 launched in Japan
      </h1>
      <div class="clearfix">
       <span class="pull-left">
        By
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/author/stephen-clemenger/" rel="author">
         Stephen Clemenger
        </a>
        - June 22, 2014
       </span>
       <a class="picture-count" href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures">
        17 Pictures
       </a>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="inline-item">
      <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures">
       <img alt="The e-NV-200 being introduced in Yokohama by Nissan planning chief Andy Palmer and Executive Vice President Takao Katagiri (Photo: Meiko Katahira)" class="hero-img" src="http://images.gizmag.com/hero/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos-30@2x.jpg"/>
      </a>
      <div class="caption caption--hero">
       <p>
        The e-NV-200 being introduced in Yokohama by Nissan planning chief Andy Palmer and Executive Vice President Takao Katagiri (Photo: Meiko Katahira)
       </p>
       <a class="gallery-link" href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures">
        Image Gallery
       </a>
       (17 images)
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="article-body">
      <p>
       In October this year, Nissan will enter the commercial zero-emissions vehicle market when its
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv200-production/31064/" target="_blank">
        e-NV200
       </a>
       van goes on sale in Japan. The company officially introduced the vehicle during a launch display event at Nissan HQ in Yokohama, earlier this month. Gizmag dropped in for a closer look.
      </p>
      <ul class="thumbnails">
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures#1">
         <img alt="Nissan launched the e-NV-200 at it`s Yokohama HQ alongside its other EV the LEAF (Photo: Meiko Katahira)" src="http://images.gizmag.com/gallery_tn/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos.jpg" title="Nissan launched the e-NV-200 at it`s Yokohama HQ alongside its other EV the LEAF (Photo: Meiko Katahira)"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures#2">
         <img alt="The e-NV-200 makes an ideal camping vehicle (Photo: Meiko Katahira)" src="http://images.gizmag.com/gallery_tn/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos-5.jpg" title="The e-NV-200 makes an ideal camping vehicle (Photo: Meiko Katahira)"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures#3">
         <img alt="The front 3/4 view shows the close resemblance to the e-NV-200 has to the LEAF (Photo: Meiko Katahira)" src="http://images.gizmag.com/gallery_tn/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos-8.jpg" title="The front 3/4 view shows the close resemblance to the e-NV-200 has to the LEAF (Photo: Meiko Katahira)"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures#4">
         <img alt="The rear has a clear lens look but almost identical to the gasoline NV-200 (Photo: Meiko Katahira)" src="http://images.gizmag.com/gallery_tn/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos-10.jpg" title="The rear has a clear lens look but almost identical to the gasoline NV-200 (Photo: Meiko Katahira)"/>
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Nissan's new EV uses technology borrowed from its first zero-emissions vehicle, the
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/tag/nissan-leaf/" target="_blank">
        LEAF
       </a>
       . The e-NV200's styling has been changed from its gasoline powered brother in an effort to both differentiate it as the electric version and convey a family-friendly look.
      </p>
      <p>
       The e-NV200 will be available as a five or seven seat wagon as well as a standard van, with or without rear side windows. Nissan says this will enable the e-NV-200 to appeal to commercial as well recreational users. The interior then can be optioned to your required number of seats which are all fold-able, including the front passenger seat.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div class="inline-item">
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures#11" target="_blank">
        <img alt="" src="http://images.gizmag.com/inline/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos-19@2x.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Nissan says it has focused on technology that reduces energy consumption to enable the van to deliver a range of approximately 185 km (115 mi) and a top speed of 123 km/h (76 mph). This technology includes an accelerator pedal that reduces excessive pressure and also emits a clicking sound to warn of this excess. Also available as standard is an air con system that uses smart phone technology to either pre-cool or preheat the cabin. On cold days though, the standard fitment of a heated front seat together with a heated steering wheel, might be just enough to keep the cold at bay.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div class="inline-item">
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures#9" target="_blank">
        <img alt="" src="http://images.gizmag.com/inline/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos-17@2x.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       At the Yokohama launch display, Nissan was keen to highlight the van's potential uses within the light commercial vehicle market. Many of these displays emphasized the vehicle's ability to act as a mobile power source. With two power outlets and a maximum total output of 1500 kWh, electricity is available for both internal and external use. Nissan is also promoting the idea that the e-NV-200 can provide all the power to your office (or home) in emergencies.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div class="inline-item">
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures#12" target="_blank">
        <img alt="" src="http://images.gizmag.com/inline/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos-22@2x.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The e-NV200 has an external length of 4560 mm, a width of 1755 mm, a height of 1850-5 mm and the internal volume figures remain almost the same from the gasoline NV-200. As in the LEAF, Nissan has installed the battery pack under the floor and mounted it centrally. This positioning not only maximizes interior space to 3600 liters but also gives the e-NV200 a good stable platform to carry loads of up to 600 kg.
      </p>
      <p>
       Power is supplied by a 24 kWh battery pack to an 80 kW motor driving the front wheels. A full charge (in Japan) will take 8 hours from a normal outlet or just 30 minutes from a special quick charge installation, giving you an 80 percent charge.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div class="inline-item">
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/nissan-e-nv-van-launch/32474/pictures#14" target="_blank">
        <img alt="" src="http://images.gizmag.com/inline/nissane-nv200vanlaunchphotos-25@2x.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Prices in Japan on launch are expected to be from 3,880,440 to 4,786,580 yen ($38,071 to $46,962) covering 8 variations of body and trim level. These figures do include consumption tax, but exclude government eco-car subsidies.
      </p>
      <p>
       Product page:
       <a href="http://www.nissan.co.jp/ENV200/">
        Nissan
       </a>
       (Japan)
      </p>

Tesla actually killed off the 40 kWh model S because nobody picked it over the 60 and 85 kWH versions.&lt;/p&gt;
