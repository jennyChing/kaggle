        <p>
         <img alt="Homemade Dog Treats: 5 Trendy Recipes To Try This Fall" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-6588" height="270" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Homemade-Dog-Treats-5-Trendy-Recipes-To-Try-This-Fall-300x270.jpg" width="300"/>
         Making your own dog treats can be a fun experience for both you and your pet. Not only do you get to customize the treats to your dogs specific taste preferences, but you can also save money when you make your own treats at home. In addition, you get to know exactly what is going into the treats, which ensures wholesome and healthy ingredients without preservatives and additives. Before heading into the kitchen however, youll want to check with your vet first to see if there are any special dietary needs or ingredients you should avoid for your specific dog and breed.
        </p>
        <p>
         Please note: many commercial dog treats use wheat, soy, or corn flour to give the treats form because these flours are plentiful and inexpensive. A natural dog diet requires little carbohydrates, and does not actually include flour at all. As such, many dogs cannot process flour well and may develop sensitivities or digestion issues. Consider choosing alternative flours such as pea flour, chickpea flour, coconut flour, lentil flour, or tapioca. They are much more nutritional and easily digestible for dogs (that goes for humans too, so feel free to experiment with some of your own cooking). In the recipes below we have simply used flour as a general ingredient. You may choose which kind you would like to use for your dog. Always check with your veterinarian first to see which ingredients he or she would recommend.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Peanut Butter and Banana Treats
        </h1>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="image (2)" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6637" height="210" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/image-2-300x210.jpeg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Most dogs love peanut butter, so this treat should be a hit. This treat also comes with a secret ingredient: Parsley (or you can substitute mint) for a natural breath freshener.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          You will need:
         </b>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 banana
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup flour
         </li>
         <li>
          2/3 cup rolled oats
         </li>
         <li>
           cup parsley, chopped
         </li>
         <li>
          3 tablespoons peanut butter
         </li>
         <li>
          1 egg
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Preheat oven to 300F. Mash banana in a bowl using a fork or potato masher. Add flour, oats, parsley, peanut butter, and beaten egg and mix well. Set aside for five minutes to allow oats to absorb some of the mixture.
        </p>
        <p>
         Roll mixture into 24 balls of about 1 tablespoon of dough each (can be bigger or smaller depending on how large you want the treats to be). Press each ball into a 1  inch to 2 inch coin shape and place on parchment-lined baking sheet. Bake until firm and golden brown, approximately 40-45 min.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cool completely. Store in airtight container or freeze until ready to eat.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-6586">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h1>
         Dog-Friendly Carrot Cake
        </h1>
        <p>
         <img alt="image (4)" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6639" height="240" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/image-4-300x240.jpeg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This treat is a perfect option for your dogs birthday celebrations.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          You will need:
         </b>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          2 cups shredded carrots
         </li>
         <li>
           cup unsweetened applesauce
         </li>
         <li>
          2 tsp cinnamon
         </li>
         <li>
          2 eggs
         </li>
         <li>
          3 cups flour
         </li>
         <li>
           cup oats
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Frosting:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
           cup unsweetened applesauce
         </li>
         <li>
          8 oz cream cheese
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Preheat oven to 350F. Mix eggs, applesauce, and carrots and set aside. In a separate bowl, mix the cinnamon, flour, and oats. Slowly mix the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients (mixture will be thick). Bake the mixture in a greased cake pan for 30 minutes and allow it to cool before frosting. Simply blend the applesauce and cream cheese and frost when the cake is cool.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Apple Cheddar Biscuits
        </h1>
        <p>
         <img alt="image (3)" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6638" height="300" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/image-3-264x300.jpeg" width="264"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          You will need:
         </b>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          2 cups flour
         </li>
         <li>
           cup rolled oats
         </li>
         <li>
          1/3 cup shredded cheddar cheese
         </li>
         <li>
           cup grated parmesan cheese
         </li>
         <li>
          1/3 cup unsweetened applesauce
         </li>
         <li>
          2 tablespoons olive oil
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Preheat oven to 350F. In a large bowl, mix all ingredients plus about 3 tablespoons of water to form a dough. Roll dough to  inch thickness and cut out biscuits with cookie cutter (this is when a bone-shaped cookie cutter might come in handy).
        </p>
        <p>
         Space biscuits about 1 inch apart on parchment covered baking sheet. Bake for 30 minutes, or until golden brown and firm. Store in airtight container or freeze until ready to eat.
         <b>
         </b>
        </p>
        <h1>
         Turkey Treats
         <b>
         </b>
        </h1>
        <p>
         <img alt="image" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6635" height="300" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/image-240x300.jpeg" width="240"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          You will need:
         </b>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 cup flour
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup corn meal
         </li>
         <li>
           teaspoon sage
         </li>
         <li>
           teaspoon baking powder
         </li>
         <li>
          1 5-ounce can of flaked turkey (can use tuna or salmon as substitute)
         </li>
         <li>
           cup canola oil
         </li>
         <li>
          2/3 cup water
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Preheat oven to 320F. Mix flour, corn meal, sage, and baking powder. Use a food processor or blender to puree the turkey, then add oil and water and mix until smooth. Add to the dry ingredients and knead until smooth and well mixed. Roll dough to  inch thickness and cut with cookie cutters.
        </p>
        <p>
         Place biscuits on greased cookie sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown. Store in refrigerator or freeze until ready to eat.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Doggie Ice Cream
        </h1>
        <p>
         <img alt="image (1)" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6636" height="291" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/image-1-300x291.jpeg" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Fun fact: dogs do not sweat in the same way humans do (panting is their main source of body temperature control). Those summer months can take a toll on our furry friends, especially in warmer climates, so a frozen treat is the perfect way to award your dog after an especially hot day.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          You will need:
         </b>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          32 ounces yogurt (look for a kind that has live bacteria and no sugars or artificial sweeteners)
         </li>
         <li>
          1 cup peanut butter
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Melt the peanut butter in a microwave safe bowl. Mix melted peanut butter with yogurt and pour into cupcake liners or ice cube trays. Place in freezer and wait until solid.
        </p>
        <h1>
         <img alt="liver treat 11" class="alignright wp-image-6096" height="179" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/liver-treat-11-300x224.jpg" width="240"/>
         Homemade Liver Treats
        </h1>
        <p>
         Most dogs cant resist liver! This recipe is quick and simple:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          You will need:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          One package of chicken or beef liver
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Boil the liver to cook it thoroughly. Make sure not to use any garlic or onion powder like some recipes recommend; neither garlic nor onion is good for your dog and lets face it he doesnt need extra flavor anyway.After it has boiled thoroughly (this will depend on the size of the liver), drain it and cook it in the oven at 250 until slightly soft or crispy.Then freeze or refrigerate for future use!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="chicken jerky" class="alignright size-full wp-image-6097" height="176" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/chicken-jerky.jpg" width="176"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         Chicken/Turkey Jerky
        </h1>
        <p>
         If you have leftover turkey after your Thanksgiving meal, consider using what is left to make some jerky.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          You will need:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Leftover turkey scraps
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         White meat works best because it is lean and dries out easiest. Dark meat just takes longer and anything that is super fatty like the skin wont work at all plus it can become rancid if left out too long.Cut the pieces the size that you desire and place in the oven at 250 and allow to cook until crispy (usually an hour or more). Freeze or refrigerate the treats to keep them fresh.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Pumpkin/Peanut Butter Treats
        </h1>
        <p>
         <img alt="image (5)" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-6634" height="300" src="http://www.thedogtrainingsecret.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/image-5-269x300.jpeg" width="269"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          You will need:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          2  cups of wheat flour (remember to cook healthy of your dog too!)
         </li>
         <li>
          2 eggs  can of plain canned pumpkin (make sure there are no spices included *nutmeg can kill dogs)
         </li>
         <li>
          3 tablespoons of peanut butter
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Preheat oven to 350.Whisk ingredients together in a bowl and use a little water to make the dough workable if needed but the dough should be dry and stiff to make good dog biscuits. Roll the dough out to about  inch think and cut into  inch pieces.Bake in oven until crisp (about 45 minutes).
        </p>
