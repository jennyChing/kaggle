     <p>
      <b>
       Sir Kenneth Charles Branagh
      </b>
      (
      <span class="nowrap">
       <span class="IPA nopopups">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">
         /
         <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
          <span title="// primary stress follows">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'b' in 'buy'">
           b
          </span>
          <span title="'r' in 'rye'">
           r
          </span>
          <span title="// short 'a' in 'bad'">
           
          </span>
          <span title="'n' in 'no'">
           n
          </span>
          <span title="// 'a' in 'about'">
           
          </span>
         </span>
         /
        </a>
       </span>
      </span>
      <span class="Unicode" title="English pronunciation respelling">
       <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Wikipedia:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i>
         <b>
          <span class="smallcaps">
           <span class="SMALLCAPS" style="FONT-VARIANT:SMALL-CAPS;">
            <span class="NOCAPS" style="TEXT-TRANSFORM:LOWERCASE;">
             BRAN
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </b>
         -
        </i>
       </a>
      </span>
      ; born 10 December 1960)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is a Northern Irish actor,
      <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
       director
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">
       producer
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">
       screenwriter
      </a>
      from
      <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">
       Belfast
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">
       Northern Ireland
      </a>
      . He has directed or starred in several film adaptations of
      <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">
       William Shakespeare
      </a>
      's plays, including
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_(1989_film)" title="Henry V (1989 film)">
        Henry V
       </a>
      </i>
      (1989) (for which he was nominated for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">
       Academy Awards
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">
       Best Actor
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Director" title="Academy Award for Best Director">
       Best Director
      </a>
      ),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Much_Ado_About_Nothing_(1993_film)" title="Much Ado About Nothing (1993 film)">
        Much Ado About Nothing
       </a>
      </i>
      (1993),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Othello_(1995_film)" title="Othello (1995 film)">
        Othello
       </a>
      </i>
      (1995),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(1996_film)" title="Hamlet (1996 film)">
        Hamlet
       </a>
      </i>
      (1996) (for which he was nominated for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">
       Academy Award
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Adapted_Screenplay" title="Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay">
       Best Adapted Screenplay
      </a>
      ),
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love%27s_Labour%27s_Lost_(2000_film)" title="Love's Labour's Lost (2000 film)">
        Love's Labour's Lost
       </a>
      </i>
      (2000), and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/As_You_Like_It_(2006_film)" title="As You Like It (2006 film)">
        As You Like It
       </a>
      </i>
      (2006).
     </p>
     <p>
      He has also starred in numerous other films and television series including
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Fortunes_of_War_(TV_series)" title="Fortunes of War (TV series)">
        Fortunes of War
       </a>
      </i>
      (1987), Woody Allen's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_(film)" title="Celebrity (film)">
        Celebrity
       </a>
      </i>
      (1998),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Wild_Wild_West" title="Wild Wild West">
        Wild Wild West
       </a>
      </i>
      (1999),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Road_to_El_Dorado" title="The Road to El Dorado">
        The Road to El Dorado
       </a>
      </i>
      (2000),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_(2001_film)" title="Conspiracy (2001 film)">
        Conspiracy
       </a>
      </i>
      (2001),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">
        Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets
       </a>
      </i>
      (2002),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Warm_Springs_(film)" title="Warm Springs (film)">
        Warm Springs
       </a>
      </i>
      (2005),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Valkyrie_(film)" title="Valkyrie (film)">
        Valkyrie
       </a>
      </i>
      (2008),
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wallander_(British_TV_series)" title="Wallander (British TV series)">
        Wallander
       </a>
      </i>
      (2008present), and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/My_Week_with_Marilyn" title="My Week with Marilyn">
        My Week with Marilyn
       </a>
      </i>
      (2011) as
      <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier" title="Laurence Olivier">
       Sir Laurence Olivier
      </a>
      (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">
       Academy Award
      </a>
      nomination for
      <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">
       Best Supporting Actor
      </a>
      ). He has directed such notable films as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dead_Again" title="Dead Again">
        Dead Again
       </a>
      </i>
      (1991) in which he also starred,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swan_Song_(film)" title="Swan Song (film)">
        Swan Song
       </a>
      </i>
      (1992) (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">
       Academy Award
      </a>
      nominated for
      <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Live_Action_Short_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Live Action Short Film">
       Best Live Action Short Film
      </a>
      ),
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankenstein_(1994_film)" title="Frankenstein (1994 film)">
        Mary Shelley's Frankenstein
       </a>
      </i>
      (1994) in which he also starred,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Magic_Flute_(2006_film)" title="The Magic Flute (2006 film)">
        The Magic Flute
       </a>
      </i>
      (2006),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sleuth_(2007_film)" title="Sleuth (2007 film)">
        Sleuth
       </a>
      </i>
      (2007), the blockbuster superhero film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Thor_(film)" title="Thor (film)">
        Thor
       </a>
      </i>
      (2011), the action thriller
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Jack_Ryan:_Shadow_Recruit" title="Jack Ryan: Shadow Recruit">
        Jack Ryan: Shadow Recruit
       </a>
      </i>
      (2014) in which he also co-stars, and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Cinderella_(2015_film)" title="Cinderella (2015 film)">
        Cinderella
       </a>
      </i>
      (2015).
     </p>
     <p>
      He also narrated the
      <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">
       BBC
      </a>
      documentary miniseries'
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Walking_with_Dinosaurs" title="Walking with Dinosaurs">
        Walking with Dinosaurs
       </a>
      </i>
      (starred in 1999) (as well as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Ballad_of_Big_Al" title="The Ballad of Big Al">
        The Ballad of Big Al
       </a>
      </i>
      ),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Walking_with_Beasts" title="Walking with Beasts">
        Walking with Beasts
       </a>
      </i>
      (2001) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Walking_with_Monsters" title="Walking with Monsters">
        Walking with Monsters
       </a>
      </i>
      (2005).
     </p>
     <p>
      Branagh has been nominated for five
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">
       Academy Awards
      </a>
      , five
      <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">
       Golden Globes
      </a>
      , and has won an
      <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">
       Emmy
      </a>
      and three
      <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_of_Film_and_Television_Arts" title="British Academy of Film and Television Arts">
       BAFTAs
      </a>
      . He was appointed a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knights_bachelor" title="Knights bachelor">
       knight bachelor
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/2012_Birthday_Honours" title="2012 Birthday Honours">
       2012 Birthday Honours
      </a>
      and was knighted on 9 November 2012.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenneth_Branagh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Branagh, the middle of three children, was born and raised in
      <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">
       Belfast
      </a>
      , the son of working-class
      <a href="/wiki/Protestantism_in_Ireland" title="Protestantism in Ireland">
       Protestant
      </a>
      parents Frances (ne Harper) and William Branagh, a plumber and joiner who ran a company that specialised in fitting partitions and suspended ceilings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the age of nine, he relocated with his family to
      <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Berkshire" title="Reading, Berkshire">
       Reading, Berkshire
      </a>
      , to escape
      <a href="/wiki/The_Troubles" title="The Troubles">
       the Troubles
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was educated at Grove Primary School,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Whiteknights Primary School, then Meadway School,
      <a href="/wiki/Tilehurst" title="Tilehurst">
       Tilehurst
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      where he appeared in school productions such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Toad_of_Toad_Hall" title="Toad of Toad Hall">
        Toad of Toad Hall
       </a>
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Oh,_What_a_Lovely_War!" title="Oh, What a Lovely War!">
        Oh, What a Lovely War!
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At school, he acquired an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_accent" title="English accent">
       English accent
      </a>
      to avoid
      <a href="/wiki/Bullying" title="Bullying">
       bullying
      </a>
      . On his identity today he has said, "I feel Irish. I don't think you can take Belfast out of the boy," and he attributes his "love of words" to his Irish heritage.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He is known to have attended the (amateur) Reading Cine &amp; Video Society (now called Reading Film &amp; Video Makers)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      as a member and was a keen member of
      <a href="/wiki/Progress_Theatre" title="Progress Theatre">
       Progress Theatre
      </a>
      for whom he is now the patron.
     </p>
     <p>
      Branagh went on to train at the
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Dramatic_Art" title="Royal Academy of Dramatic Art">
       Royal Academy of Dramatic Art
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
       Career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenneth_Branagh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Stage_work">
       Stage work
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenneth_Branagh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stage work">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Branagh achieved some early measure of success in his native Northern Ireland for his role as Billy, the title character in the BBC's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Play_for_Today" title="Play for Today">
        Play for Today
       </a>
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      trilogy known as the
      <i>
       Billy Plays
      </i>
      (198284), written by
      <a href="/wiki/Graham_Reid_(writer)" title="Graham Reid (writer)">
       Graham Reid
      </a>
      and set in Belfast.
     </p>
     <p>
      He received acclaim in the UK for his stage performances, first winning the 1982
      <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award" title="Laurence Olivier Award">
       SWET
      </a>
      Award for Best Newcomer, for his role as Judd in
      <a href="/wiki/Julian_Mitchell" title="Julian Mitchell">
       Julian Mitchell
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Another_Country_(play)" title="Another Country (play)">
        Another Country
       </a>
      </i>
      , immediately after leaving
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RADA" title="RADA">
       RADA
      </a>
      . Branagh was part of the 'new wave of actors to emerge from the Academy. Others included
      <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Pryce" title="Jonathan Pryce">
       Jonathan Pryce
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Juliet_Stevenson" title="Juliet Stevenson">
       Juliet Stevenson
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Alan_Rickman" title="Alan Rickman">
       Alan Rickman
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Anton_Lesser" title="Anton Lesser">
       Anton Lesser
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Payne" title="Bruce Payne">
       Bruce Payne
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Fiona_Shaw" title="Fiona Shaw">
       Fiona Shaw
      </a>
      . In 1984 he appeared in the
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">
       Royal Shakespeare Company
      </a>
      production of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_(play)" title="Henry V (play)">
        Henry V
       </a>
      </i>
      , directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Noble" title="Adrian Noble">
       Adrian Noble
      </a>
      . The production played to full houses, especially at the Barbican in London. It was this production that he adapted for the film version of the play in 1989. He and
      <a href="/wiki/David_Parfitt" title="David Parfitt">
       David Parfitt
      </a>
      founded the
      <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_Theatre_Company" title="Renaissance Theatre Company">
       Renaissance Theatre Company
      </a>
      in 1987, following success with several productions on the London 'Fringe', including Branagh's full-scale production of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">
        Romeo and Juliet
       </a>
      </i>
      at the Lyric Studio, co-starring with
      <a href="/wiki/Samantha_Bond" title="Samantha Bond">
       Samantha Bond
      </a>
      . The first major Renaissance production was Branagh's Christmas 1987 staging of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Night" title="Twelfth Night">
        Twelfth Night
       </a>
      </i>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Riverside_Studios" title="Riverside Studios">
       Riverside Studios
      </a>
      in Hammersmith, starring
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_Briers" title="Richard Briers">
       Richard Briers
      </a>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Malvolio" title="Malvolio">
       Malvolio
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Frances_Barber" title="Frances Barber">
       Frances Barber
      </a>
      as Viola, and with an original score by actor, musician and composer
      <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Doyle" title="Patrick Doyle">
       Patrick Doyle
      </a>
      , who two years later was to compose the music for Branagh's film adaptation of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_(1989_film)" title="Henry V (1989 film)">
        Henry V
       </a>
      </i>
      . This
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Night" title="Twelfth Night">
        Twelfth Night
       </a>
      </i>
      was later adapted for television.
     </p>
     <p>
      Branagh became a major presence in the media and on the British stage when Renaissance collaborated with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birmingham_Rep" title="Birmingham Rep">
       Birmingham Rep
      </a>
      for a 1988 touring season of three Shakespeare plays under the umbrella title of
      <i>
       Renaissance Shakespeare on the Road
      </i>
      , which also played a repertory season at the
      <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Theatre_(London)" title="Phoenix Theatre (London)">
       Phoenix Theatre
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      . It featured directorial debuts for
      <a href="/wiki/Judi_Dench" title="Judi Dench">
       Judi Dench
      </a>
      with
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Much_Ado_About_Nothing" title="Much Ado About Nothing">
        Much Ado About Nothing
       </a>
      </i>
      (starring Branagh and Samantha Bond as Benedick and Beatrice),
      <a href="/wiki/Geraldine_McEwan" title="Geraldine McEwan">
       Geraldine McEwan
      </a>
      with
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/As_You_Like_It" title="As You Like It">
        As You Like It
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jacobi" title="Derek Jacobi">
       Derek Jacobi
      </a>
      directing Branagh in the title role in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">
        Hamlet
       </a>
      </i>
      , with
      <a href="/wiki/Sophie_Thompson" title="Sophie Thompson">
       Sophie Thompson
      </a>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Ophelia" title="Ophelia">
       Ophelia
      </a>
      . Critic
      <a href="/wiki/Milton_Shulman" title="Milton Shulman">
       Milton Shulman
      </a>
      of the London
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evening_Standard" title="Evening Standard">
        Evening Standard
       </a>
      </i>
      wrote: "On the positive side Branagh has the vitality of
      <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier" title="Laurence Olivier">
       Olivier
      </a>
      , the passion of
      <a href="/wiki/John_Gielgud" title="John Gielgud">
       Gielgud
      </a>
      , the assurance of
      <a href="/wiki/Alec_Guinness" title="Alec Guinness">
       Guinness
      </a>
      , to mention but three famous actors who have essayed the role. On the negative side, he has not got the magnetism of Olivier, nor the mellifluous voice quality of Gielgud nor the intelligence of Guinness."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A year later in 1989 Branagh co-starred with
      <a href="/wiki/Emma_Thompson" title="Emma Thompson">
       Emma Thompson
      </a>
      in the Renaissance revival of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Look_Back_in_Anger" title="Look Back in Anger">
        Look Back in Anger
       </a>
      </i>
      . Judi Dench directed both the theatre and television productions, presented first in
      <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">
       Belfast
      </a>
      then at the
      <a href="/wiki/London_Coliseum" title="London Coliseum">
       London Coliseum
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyric_Theatre_(London)" title="Lyric Theatre (London)">
       Lyric Theatre
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2002, Branagh starred at the
      <a href="/wiki/Crucible_Theatre" title="Crucible Theatre">
       Crucible Theatre
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">
       Sheffield
      </a>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_III_(play)" title="Richard III (play)">
       Richard III
      </a>
      . In 2003 he starred in the
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">
       Royal National Theatre
      </a>
      's production of
      <a href="/wiki/David_Mamet" title="David Mamet">
       David Mamet
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Edmond_(play)" title="Edmond (play)">
        Edmond
       </a>
      </i>
      . Branagh directed
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Play_What_I_Wrote" title="The Play What I Wrote">
        The Play What I Wrote
       </a>
      </i>
      in England in 2001
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and directed a Broadway production in 2003.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      From September to November 2008, Branagh appeared at
      <a href="/wiki/Wyndham%27s_Theatre" title="Wyndham's Theatre">
       Wyndham's Theatre
      </a>
      as the title character in the
      <a href="/wiki/Donmar_Warehouse" title="Donmar Warehouse">
       Donmar West End
      </a>
      revival of
      <a href="/wiki/Anton_Chekhov" title="Anton Chekhov">
       Anton Chekhov
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Ivanov_(play)" title="Ivanov (play)">
        Ivanov
       </a>
      </i>
      in a new version by
      <a href="/wiki/Tom_Stoppard" title="Tom Stoppard">

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">

     <p>
      In April 2015, Branagh announced his formation of Kenneth Branagh Theatre Company. With the company, he announced he will present a season of five shows at London's Garrick Theatre from October 2015 - November 2016. The shows will be The Winters Tale, Harlequinade, The Painkiller, Romeo and Juliet and The Entertainer. Branagh will direct all but the Entertainer, in which he will star. Branagh will also star in The Winter's Tale, Harlequinade and The Painkiller. Kenneth Branagh Theatre Company includes Judi Dench, Lily James, Richard Madden and Rob Brydon.
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.officialtheatre.com/garrick-theatre" rel="nofollow">
       Kenneth Branagh Season
      </a>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Film_work">

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       </a>

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Branagh is known for his film adaptations of
      <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">
       William Shakespeare
      </a>
      , beginning with
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_(1989_film)" title="Henry V (1989 film)">
        Henry V
       </a>
      </i>
      (1989), followed by
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Much_Ado_About_Nothing_(1993_film)" title="Much Ado About Nothing (1993 film)">
        Much Ado About Nothing
       </a>
      </i>
      (1993),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(1996_film)" title="Hamlet (1996 film)">
        Hamlet
       </a>
      </i>
      (1996),
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love%27s_Labour%27s_Lost_(2000_film)" title="Love's Labour's Lost (2000 film)">
        Love's Labour's Lost
       </a>
      </i>
      (2000) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/As_You_Like_It_(2006_film)" title="As You Like It (2006 film)">
        As You Like It
       </a>
      </i>
      (2006).
      <i>
       As You Like It
      </i>
      premiered in theatres in Europe, but was sent directly to television in the U.S., where it aired on
      <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">
       HBO
      </a>
      in August 2007. He was rumored to have been under consideration for the role of
      <a href="/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi" title="Obi-Wan Kenobi">
       Obi-Wan Kenobi
      </a>
      in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Wars_prequel_trilogy" title="Star Wars prequel trilogy">
       Star Wars prequel trilogy
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Notable non-Shakespeare films in which Branagh has appeared include
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dead_Again" title="Dead Again">
        Dead Again
       </a>
      </i>
      (1991) and
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankenstein_(1994_film)" title="Frankenstein (1994 film)">
        Mary Shelley's Frankenstein
       </a>
      </i>
      (1994), both of which he also directed,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Wild_Wild_West" title="Wild Wild West">
        Wild Wild West
       </a>
      </i>
      (1999),
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rabbit-Proof_Fence_(film)" title="Rabbit-Proof Fence (film)">
        Rabbit-Proof Fence
       </a>
      </i>
      (2002) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Valkyrie_(film)" title="Valkyrie (film)">
        Valkyrie
       </a>
      </i>
      (2008). He starred as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">
       Gilderoy Lockhart
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">
        Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets
       </a>
      </i>
      (2002). He also played the Minister, Dormandy (a parody of
      <a href="/wiki/Postmaster_General_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Postmaster General of the United Kingdom">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">
       Honours
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kenneth_Branagh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Honours">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Branagh has been nominated for five
      <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">
       Academy Awards
      </a>
      , the first man to be nominated for five different categories. His first two nominations were for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_(1989_film)" title="Henry V (1989 film)">
        Henry V
       </a>
      </i>
      (one each for directing and acting). He also received similar
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award" title="BAFTA Award">
       BAFTA Award
      </a>
      nominations for his film work, winning one for his direction. His first BAFTA TV award came in April 2009, for Best Drama Series (Wallander). Branagh's two other Academy Award nominations were for the 1992 film
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Short_subject" title="Short subject">
       short subject
      </a>
      <i>
       Swan Song
      </i>
      and for his work on the screenplay of
      <i>
       Hamlet
      </i>
      in 1996. His most recent is for his portrayal of Lord
      <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier" title="Laurence Olivier">
       Laurence Olivier
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Week_With_Marilyn" title="My Week With Marilyn">
        My Week With Marilyn
       </a>
      </i>
      . Branagh has co-starred several times with actress
      <a href="/wiki/Emma_Thompson" title="Emma Thompson">
       Emma Thompson
      </a>
      , to whom he was married from 1989 to 1995. They appeared together in
      <i>
       Look Back in Anger
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Henry V
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Much Ado About Nothing
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dead_Again" title="Dead Again">
        Dead Again
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Peter%27s_Friends" title="Peter's Friends">
        Peter's Friends
       </a>
      </i>
      . More recently, they both appeared in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Boat_That_Rocked" title="The Boat That Rocked">
        The Boat That Rocked
       </a>
      </i>
      , though with no shared scenes.
     </p>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NICVA" title="NICVA">
       NICVA
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Letters" title="Doctor of Letters">

      </a>
      from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen%27s_University_of_Belfast" title="Queen's University of Belfast">
       Queen's University of Belfast
      </a>
      in 1990. He is also a patron for the charity Over The Wall.

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">
       Order of the British Empire
      </a>

      , he is one of only two non-American actors to be nominated for Oscars for acting, writing, and directing, and one of eight actors to have achieved this honour. The other six are
      <a href="/wiki/Orson_Welles" title="Orson Welles">
       Orson Welles
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">
       Woody Allen
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Warren_Beatty" title="Warren Beatty">
       Warren Beatty
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">
       George Clooney
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/John_Huston" title="John Huston">
       John Huston
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/John_Cassavetes" title="John Cassavetes">
       John Cassavetes
      </a>
      .
     </p>
