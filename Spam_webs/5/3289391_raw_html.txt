Yes, it is my home state, but it is also right in the center of the mapand for a state that is centered in the middle of the country, Americans sure overlook it a lot. Many regard Kansas as a barren, flat wasteland that makes for a really boring drive on the way to Colorado. I see it a little differently. Next time you find yourself making that long, flat drive, try to incorporate one of these five micro adventures.

Find a young and rowdy onepreferably handsome as welland tell him youve heard that crop touring is the thing to do. Hell grab a bunch of his friends and take you on a tour of the land. others are clear, a few require a good deal of walking, others none at all. Whatever suits your excursion, you'll find places to explore, paddle, or simply enjoy the sunespecially in eastern Kansas where there are rivers a'plenty (you might have to share a few with wave runners!).                <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpMiddle">
                <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                 Kansas is right in the center of "Tornado Alley." To some I realize this sounds terrifying. But there is nothing like being
surrounded by lightning on all sides and searching for funnels in the flashes.
There is a joke among Kansans that you know if someone is not from Kansas if
they run to the basement when the tornado sirens go off. Youll find most of
the neighborhood out on their porches until the storm gets really close.
                </span>
                <br/>
                <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                 <br/>
                </span>
                <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                 Looking to photograph the experience? Here's a tip: Wait until the storm passes. Usually you have an
