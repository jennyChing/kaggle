   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Genetically modified virus
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      A
      <b>
       genetically modified virus
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Virus" title="Virus">
       virus
      </a>
      that has gone through genetic modification for various
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biomedical" title="Biomedical">
       biomedical
      </a>
      purposes.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="General_usage">
       General usage
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genetically_modified_virus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General usage">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Genetic modification involves the insertion or deletion of genes. When genes are inserted, they usually come from different species, which is a form of
      <a href="/wiki/Horizontal_gene_transfer" title="Horizontal gene transfer">
       horizontal gene transfer
      </a>
      . In nature this can occur when exogenous DNA penetrates the cell membrane for any reason but usually for domination of other diseases.
     </p>
     <p>
      To do this artificially may require attaching the genes to a
      <a href="/wiki/Virus" title="Virus">
       virus
      </a>
      or just physically inserting the extra DNA into the nucleus of the intended host with a very small syringe, or with very small particles fired from a
      <a href="/wiki/Gene_gun" title="Gene gun">
       gene gun
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, other methods exploit natural forms of gene transfer, such as the ability of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Agrobacterium" title="Agrobacterium">
        Agrobacterium
       </a>
      </i>
      to transfer genetic material to plants,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      or the ability of
      <a href="/wiki/Lentivirus" title="Lentivirus">
       lentiviruses
      </a>
      to transfer genes to animal cells.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Lithium-ion_batteries">
       Lithium-ion batteries
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genetically_modified_virus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lithium-ion batteries">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In materials science, a genetically modified virus has been used to construct a more environmentally friendly
      <a href="/wiki/Lithium-ion_battery" title="Lithium-ion battery">
       lithium-ion battery
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Gene_therapy">
       Gene therapy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genetically_modified_virus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gene therapy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Gene_therapy" title="Gene therapy">
       Gene therapy
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      uses genetically modified viruses to deliver genes that can cure disease into human cells. Although gene therapy is still relatively new, it has had some successes. It has been used to treat
      <a href="/wiki/Genetic_disorder" title="Genetic disorder">
       genetic disorders
      </a>
      such as
      <a href="/wiki/Severe_combined_immunodeficiency" title="Severe combined immunodeficiency">
       severe combined immunodeficiency
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Heart_pacemaker">
       Heart pacemaker
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genetically_modified_virus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Heart pacemaker">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 2012, US researchers reported that they injected a genetically modified virus into the heart of guinea pigs. This virus inserted into the heart muscles a gene called
      <a href="/wiki/T-box" title="T-box">
       Tbx18
      </a>
      which enabled heartbeats. The researchers forecast that one day this technique could be used to restore the heartbeat in humans who would otherwise need
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_pacemaker" title="Electronic pacemaker">
       electronic pacemakers
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cancer_treatment">
       Cancer treatment
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genetically_modified_virus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cancer treatment">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 2004, researchers reported that a genetically modified virus that exploits the selfish behaviour of cancer cells might offer an alternative way of killing tumours.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Since then, several researchers have developed genetically modified
      <a href="/wiki/Oncolytic_virus" title="Oncolytic virus">
       oncolytic viruses
      </a>
      that show promise as treatments for various types of
      <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">
       cancer
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid16507823_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pmid16507823-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Rabbits">
       Rabbits
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genetically_modified_virus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rabbits">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In Spain and Portugal, by 2005 rabbits had declined by as much as 95% over 50 years due diseases such as
      <a href="/wiki/Myxomatosis" title="Myxomatosis">
       myxomatosis
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rabbit_haemorrhagic_disease" title="Rabbit haemorrhagic disease">
       rabbit haemorrhagic disease
      </a>
      and other causes. This in turn caused declines in predators like the
      <a href="/wiki/Iberian_lynx" title="Iberian lynx">
       Iberian lynx
      </a>
      , a critically endagered species.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ward_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ward-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-decpaper_19-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-decpaper-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2000 Spanish researchers investigated a genetically modified virus which might have protected rabbits in the wild against myxomatosis and rabbit haemorrhagic disease.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However there was concern that such a virus might make its way into wild populations in areas such as Australia and create a population boom.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ward_18-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ward-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Rabbits in Australia are considered to be such a pest that land owners are legally obliged to control them.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="GMO_lentivirus">
       GMO lentivirus
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genetically_modified_virus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: GMO lentivirus">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A scientist claims she was infected by a genetically modified virus while working for Pfizer. In her federal lawsuit she says she has been intermittently paralyzed by the Pfizer-designed virus. "McClain, of Deep River, suspects she was inadvertently exposed, through work by a former Pfizer colleague in 2002 or 2003, to an engineered form of the
      <a href="/wiki/Lentivirus" title="Lentivirus">
       lentivirus
      </a>
      , a virus similar to the one that can lead to acquired immune deficiency syndrome, or AIDS."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The court found that McClain failed to demonstrate that her illness was caused by exposure to the lentivirus,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but also that Pfizer violated whistleblower laws.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
