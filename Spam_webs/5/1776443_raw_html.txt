                     <a href="http://www.sending-postcards.com/2015/07/red-sand-beach-of-kaihalulu-bay-maui.html">
                      Red Sand Beach of Kaihalulu Bay | Maui
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      <a href="http://www.sending-postcards.com/2015/07/red-sand-beach-of-kaihalulu-bay-maui.html#comment-form">

                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/alex-and-mina/19449148092" title="Red Sand Beach // Kaihalulu Beach by alex + mina, on Flickr">

                      <br/>
                      <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                       <br/>
                       <div style="text-align: justify;">
                        The hike to the
                        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sand_Beach">
                         Red Sand Beach of Kaihalulu Bay
                        </a>
                        , near Hana, is short but unconventional. A dirt path on private property brings you to a
                        <i>
                         very
                        </i>
                        narrow ridge, which leads you around a point, to the bay. The red volcanic rock/sand of the bay stands in vivid contrast to the almost comically blue water. We visited on an overcast day when the waves were loud and big.
                       </div>
                       <div style="text-align: justify;">
                        <br/>
                       </div>
                       <div style="text-align: justify;">
                        This was our final destination on our
                        <a href="http://www.sending-postcards.com/2015/05/road-to-hana-part-i.html">
                         Road to Hana
                        </a>
                        trip. We didn't want to drive the winding road back in the dark, so we marveled that this place even exists one more time before heading back to Kihei.
                       </div>
