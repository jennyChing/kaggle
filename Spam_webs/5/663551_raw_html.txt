         <article class="post-18572 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-photography category-random tag-conceptual tag-design-free-thursday tag-kids tag-photography tag-vintage clearfix" id="post-18572" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting" role="article">
          <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="600" src="http://assets.yellowtrace.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Smoking-Kids-by-Frieke-Janssens_yellowtrace_01.jpg" title="" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18574" height="600" src="http://assets.yellowtrace.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Smoking-Kids-by-Frieke-Janssens_yellowtrace_02.jpg" title="" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18575" height="600" src="http://assets.yellowtrace.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Smoking-Kids-by-Frieke-Janssens_yellowtrace_03.jpg" title="" width="650"/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18576" height="600" src="http://assets.yellowtrace.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Smoking-Kids-by-Frieke-Janssens_yellowtrace_04.jpg" title="" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Smoking Kids is a compelling and slightly disturbing personal project by Brussels based photographer
            <a href="http://frieke.com/">
             Frieke Janssens
            </a>
            .She was inspired to create this series after watching a
            <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x4c_wI6kQyE">
             YouTube video
            </a>
            of a chainsmoking Indonesian toddler. This is what Frike has to say about her project.
           </p>
           <hr/>
           <h2>
            The video highlighted the cultural differences between the east and west, and questioned notions of smoking being a mainly adult activity. Adult smokers are the societal norm, so I wanted to isolate the viewers focus upon the issue of smoking itself. I felt that children smoking would have a surreal impact upon the viewer and compel them to truly see the acts of smoking rather than making assumptions about the person doing the act.
           </h2>
           <hr/>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18577" height="600" src="http://assets.yellowtrace.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Smoking-Kids-by-Frieke-Janssens_yellowtrace_05.jpg" title="" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18578" height="600" src="http://assets.yellowtrace.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Smoking-Kids-by-Frieke-Janssens_yellowtrace_06.jpg" title="" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18579" height="600" src="http://assets.yellowtrace.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Smoking-Kids-by-Frieke-Janssens_yellowtrace_07.jpg" title="" width="650"/>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Frike feels that the culture of smoking has a retro feel, hence her photos capture a time period of
            <a href="http://www.amctv.com/shows/mad-men">
             Mad Men
            </a>
            , when smoking on a plane or in a restaurant was not uncommon. Her photographs also capture the fine line between the beauty and ugliness of smoking. And in case you are horrified about this whole thing, no real cigarettes were used on set,assuring the safety of the children. Instead, chalk and sticks of cheese were used as props, while candles and incense provided the wisps of smoke.
           </p>
