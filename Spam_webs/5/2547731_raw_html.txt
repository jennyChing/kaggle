        <header class="entry-header">
         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          How mobile is impacting business everywhere
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          By
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/author/conversionation/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
            <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
             J-P De Clerck
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="entry-comments-link">
           <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/mobile-impacting-business-everywhere/#respond">
            <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="101 http://www.informationdynamix.com/?p=101">
             Leave a Comment
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="How mobile is impacting business everywhere" data-url="http://www.informationdynamix.com/mobile-impacting-business-everywhere/">
         </div>
         <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_105" style="width: 265px">
          <a href="http://www.idc.com/getdoc.jsp?containerId=prUS24143513" target="_blank">
           <img alt="IDC mentioned the quick spread from developed markets to emerging markets in 2013 research - chart below" class=" wp-image-105" height="214" src="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IDC-mentioned-the-quick-spread-from-developed-markets-to-emerging-markets-in-2013-research-chart-below.gif" width="255"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           <a href="http://www.idc.com/getdoc.jsp?containerId=prUS24143513" target="_blank">
            IDC mentioned the quick spread from developed markets to emerging markets in 2013 research
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           According to new research from
           <a href="http://blogs.forrester.com/satish_meena/14-08-25-expect_35_billion_global_smartphone_subscribers_by_2019" target="_blank">
            Forrester
           </a>
           , there will be 3.5 billion global smartphone subscribers by 2019. The impact will be felt everywhere but mainly in emerging economies. Data and vertical examples.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          We have witnessed how the adoption of smartphones and tablets  impacting consumer behavior and offering new business opportunities  deeply affected organizations in, among others, North America and Europe, the regions driving smartphone adoption in previous years.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, the regional focus is changing and the growth of smartphone adoption will mainly happen in three other regions,
          <a href="http://blogs.forrester.com/satish_meena/14-08-25-expect_35_billion_global_smartphone_subscribers_by_2019" target="_blank" title="Expect 3.5 Billion Global Smartphone Subscribers By 2019">
           Forrester states
          </a>
          .
          <span id="more-101">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Focus on emerging markets
         </h2>
         <p>
          Asia Pacific will be the fastest-growing region, followed by MEA and Latin America, all parts of the globe where many emerging economies are located. Its a shift other research firms started noticing before too, as the IDC chart shows.
         </p>
         <p>
          On top of smartphones, tablets are conquering the hearts and wallets of consumers in many countries where adoption has been lagging behind. In the UAE, for instance, the tablet market has overtaken the PC market for the first time,
          <a href="http://gulfnews.com/business/technology/tablets-overtake-computer-sales-for-first-time-in-uae-1.1374034" target="_blank" title=" Tablets overtake computer sales for first time in UAE">
           according to IDC (quoted by Gulf News)
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          A few days ago, IDC also released its Annual Middle East and Africa Government CIO Survey 2014. Just one quote from the press release: Nearly two-thirds of MEA government IT decision makers expect to see an increase in the ratio of their organizations mobile workforce.
          <a href="http://www.idc.com/getdoc.jsp?containerId=prAE25048214" target="_blank" title="'Third Platform' Technologies to Feature Prominently as Pressure on MEA Government Sector Intensifies ">
           More here
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Finally, in
          <a href="http://www.idc.com/getdoc.jsp?containerId=prAE25050014" target="_blank" title="Africa: A Key ICT Investment Destination ">
           other new research
          </a>
          , where IDC emphasized the important role of Africa as a key ICT investment destination, mobile was mentioned as an essential driver (countries such as Kenya are interesting examples as well cover in another post).
         </p>
         <p>
          The list of research goes on and we could certainly mention government as an industry where mobile is becoming increasingly important.
          <strong>
           Indeed, as mentioned, the impact is felt everywhere: in several business functions, different parts of the world and across industries. So lets look at a few, starting with
           <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/healthcare-information-technology/" title="Healthcare and technology: global information challenges">
            healthcare
           </a>
           .
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Mobile and healthcare: challenge and opportunity
         </h2>
         <p>
          In a previous blog post, covering some conclusions from the annual Meeker report, we emphasized
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/healthcare-industry-digital-technology-evolutions-from-the-meeker-report/" title="Healthcare industry: digital technology evolutions from the Meeker report">
           how the uptake of mobile devices  including sensors  in general is a key transformational factor in healthcare evolutions
          </a>
          in the US.
         </p>
         <p>
          The changing behavior of the mobile consumer and overall consumerization partially strengthened the need to focus on the
          <a href="http://www.i-scoop.eu/customer-centric-thinking-the-channel-agnostic-customer/" target="_blank">
           channel-agnostic
          </a>
          and demanding patient first, with smartphones and tablets being the Swiss knives of the anywhere, anytime, anything generation. Mobile access to healthcare has become one of many drivers in healthcare innovation in emerging economies too.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_104" style="width: 310px">
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/The-mobile-health-intentions-of-smartphone-users-source.gif">
           <img alt="The mobile health intentions of smartphone users - source" class="size-full wp-image-104" height="232" src="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/The-mobile-health-intentions-of-smartphone-users-source.gif" width="300"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The mobile health intentions of smartphone users 
           <a href="http://healthpopuli.com/2010/11/16/another-bullish-forecast-for-mobile-health/" target="_blank">
            source
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Furthermore its not just about the patients themselves.Only a few years ago,
          <a href="http://www.networkworld.com/article/2288429/data-center/top-challenges-facing-healthcare-cios.html" target="_blank" title="Top Challenges Facing Healthcare CIOs">
           Network World
          </a>
          mentioned
          <strong>
           mobile health and BYOD as one of the top challenges facing healthcare CIOs as 1) mobile devices are loved by medical professionals and 2) mobile health offered tremendous opportunities in (quote) the developing world.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Obviously, there are challenges too. Just think about secure access to patient records and patient acceptation, even if in 2010,
          <a href="http://healthpopuli.com/2010/11/16/another-bullish-forecast-for-mobile-health/" target="_blank" title="Another bullish forecast for mobile health">
           research indicated smartphone users have strong mobile health intentions
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          Retail banking and emerging markets: mobile as the omnichannel enabler
         </h2>
         <p>
          Another industry where mobile is among the main game-changers is
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/financial-services-industry/" title="The financial services industry: information challenges and evolutions">
           financial services
          </a>
          , more specifically retail banking. Earlier this year,
          <a href="http://www.ovum.com/research/retail-banking-it-priorities-for-2014-and-spending-forecasts-to-2018/" target="_blank" title=" Retail Banking IT Priorities for 2014 and Spending Forecasts to 2018">
           Ovum
          </a>
          was one of many to identify online/digital banking as one of the key retail banking evolutions, with spending on channels such as mobile growing even faster.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_102" style="width: 310px">
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Growth-of-mobile-device-shipments-in-the-UAE-source-IDC.gif">
           <img alt="Growth of mobile device shipments in the UAE - source IDC" class="size-full wp-image-102" height="268" src="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Growth-of-mobile-device-shipments-in-the-UAE-source-IDC.gif" width="300"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Growth of mobile device shipments in the UAE  source
           <a href="http://www.idc.com" target="_blank">
            IDC
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Again looking at MEA and  more specifically  MENA, we see that mobile banking is growing very fast across the region. Obviously, there are differences. In the GCC (Gulf) countries, for instance, mobile banking goes hand in hand with a young, tech-savvy and mobile population that increases spending on consumer electronics in general. In many African countries, on the other hand, the boom in mobile banking is more related with the low number of retail bank branches. On top of these regional drivers, there are consumer-related drivers whereby
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/bridging-customer-experience-gap-disconnected-information-reality/" title="Bridging the customer experience gap in a disconnected information reality">
           customer experience
          </a>
          and communication requirements are far higher among the mobile connected: the omnichannel customer experience.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Taking into account all these regional differences and looking at where mobile adoption  and especially tablet and smartphone adoption  are growing fastest, its striking to see how in these markets the use of mobile in healthcare, banking and many more industries is increasing.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its probably not a coincidence that in many of these industries, where mobile and digital play an increasing role, ever more vendors and businesses, active in them, are increasingly focusing on developing and high-growth markets.
         </p>
         <p>
          An example? Just look at how
          <a href="http://online.wsj.com/articles/citigroup-considers-sale-of-retail-banking-business-in-japan-1408464316" target="_blank" title="Citigroup Considers Sale of Retail-Banking Business in Japan">
           Citigroup
          </a>
          wants to pull out its retail banking operations out of Japan, a saturated and mature country, in order to focus more on highest growth potential countries. Guess where these are and compare with the countries where IT spending and mobile penetration are growing fastest.
         </p>
