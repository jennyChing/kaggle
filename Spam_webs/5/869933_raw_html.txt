        <p>
         <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
          Breathtaking images of 'wave clouds' were captured by a helicopter pilot as they rolled off the sea and inland, completely engulfing a beachfront city.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          The surreal event was captured by helicopter pilot Mike Schaeffer who was just finishing a tour of the coastline in Panama City Beach, Florida when he spotted the weather phenomenon - called a
         </font>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          KelvinHelmholtz instability.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          The cloud swept across the sands creating a tsunami effect and over the top of the roofs of the beachfront blocks of condos earlier this month.
          <br/>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p style="font-weight: bold;">
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          Scroll down for video
         </font>

         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          It normally occurs in regions with vast plains where winds quickly change speed creating turbulence but appeared in the beachfront city on February 5.
          <br/>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          A fast-moving, lighter
         </font>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          density cloud slides on top of a slower, thicker layer, dragging out the surface and creating the wave effect.

         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          The
 Cloud Appreciation Society explains the quirky weather phenomenon. They
 are the result of turbulence in a layer of Cirrus cloud where air 
currents exist of differing speeds or directions - making the cloud 
resemble a wave rolling along the top of the water.
         </font>
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          The incredibly uncatchy name is a
 combination from Lord Kelvin - a Scottish baron who along with a German
 physicist Hermann Helmholtz - came up with an explanation for the freak
 occurrence.
          <br/>
         </font>
        </p>
