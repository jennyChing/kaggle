                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         <img alt="topless barber" src="http://ibizbook.com/live/images/62/topless-barber.jpg" width="100%"/>
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         That would be a cosmetology license, according to police in the town of Longmont, CO.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         How did the, um, bust go down? Not as a crime in progress. Suzette Hall, aka Suzette Dawe, had bounced a few checks intended to cover her chair rent at a salon near downtown Longmont. As police investigated that incident, her name rang a bell.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         In 2008, Hall/Dawe was also found without the appropriate licensing credentials. As a result of that investigation, one detective recalled, she was ordered to stop using unauthorized tools for manicure use and also to cease from engaging in permanent makeup services.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         Fast forward to 2013, as state officials tell the cops that she still doesn't have a license to work.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         A
                         <a href="http://www.timescall.com/top-stories/ci_24625028/longmont-police-topless-barber-arrested-practicing-without-cosmetology">
                          local newspaper reported
                         </a>
                         that witnesses -- to what wasn't clear -- told police that Hall/Dawe was operating a salon called Rebel Beauty and charging $45 for topless haircuts. Supposedly, she also advertised those services on Craigslist.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         A now-former business partner, uncomfortable with being associated with such practices, discovered to her dismay that Hall/Dawe was also apparently soliciting sex with men who frequented the business. The unlicensed cosmetologist reportedly admitted to her partner that she took payment for those services and also performed "house calls."
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         Hall/Dawe's husband advised authorities that she told him she'd applied for a license, which was in the mail, and that she'd also sought"a nude license for hairstylists," which the state says doesn't exist. And won't, from the sounds of things.
                        </p>
                        <p style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; text-align: justify;">
                         At least Hall/Dawe hasn't had the same trouble -- yet -- as a Boulder woman who
                         <a href="http://www.dailycamera.com/ci_22885737/boulder-couple-involved-topless-gardening-fight-investigated-over">
                          gained notoriety
                         </a>
                         for her topless gardening habits. Her husband subsequently was investigated for making threats against President Obama.
                        </p>
