         Grilled Asian Lamb Ribs
        </a>
       </h1>
       <div class="meta">
        <span class="date">
         <time class="published" datetime="2015-04-08">

      <div class="body entry-content">
       <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1428534657590" id="item-5525b282e4b0d5d2f33921c1">
        <div class="row sqs-row">
         <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
          <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-81abb71b33465e745bf3">
           <div class="sqs-block-content">
            <p>
             The first time I experienced lamb ribs was at Memphis' famed Rendezvous restaurant and since then there has been no turning back. While not readily available at your local grocer, it's worth a call to a butcher to score a couple racks for use in this recipe from
             <span style="font-size:13.3333330154419px">
              Chef Sharon Nahm of
             </span>
             <span style="font-size:13.3333330154419px">
             </span>
             <span>
              <a href="http://www.eosanfrancisco.com/" target="_blank">
               E&amp;O
              </a>
              in the San Francisco Bay area.
             </span>
             <span style="font-size:13.3333330154419px">
             </span>
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1428523326934_123106">
           <div class="sqs-block-content">

               </noscript>
               <img alt="Grilled Asian Lamb Ribs" class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54b2faf2e4b0c67d15add912/t/5525b4c1e4b0fb9afcd51777/1428534470847/Grilled+Lamb+Ribs" data-image-dimensions="840x600" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="5525b4c1e4b0fb9afcd51777" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/54b2faf2e4b0c67d15add912/t/5525b4c1e4b0fb9afcd51777/1428534470847/Grilled+Lamb+Ribs" data-type="image"/>

           <div class="sqs-block-content">
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size:10pt">
               GRILLED ASIAN LAMB RIBS
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size:10pt">
               Ingredients
              </span>
             </strong>
             <span style="font-size:10pt">
              :
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size:10pt">
              2 Lbs Lamb spareribs, Denver Style Cut
              <br/>
              1 cup Sweet soy sauce
              <br/>
               cup Tamarind puree
              <br/>
               cup Korean hot pepper paste (Kochujang)
              <br/>
               cup Hoisin Sauce
              <br/>
               cup Fish Sauce
              <br/>
              2 Tbsp Sambal Oelek
              <br/>
              1 Tbsp Green Jalapeo pepper, seeded, chopped
              <br/>
               cup Ginger, peeled and chopped
              <br/>
              3 Tbsp Garlic, chopped
              <br/>
               cup Honey
              <br/>
              2 tsp Ground Coriander
              <br/>
              1 tsp Ground black pepper
             </span>
             <br/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size:10pt">
              <strong>
               Instructions
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Prepare the grill for indirect cooking, placing the charcoal on one side of the grill and leaving the other cool. Preheat the grill to approximately 250 degrees. Season the ribs lightly with salt and pepper. Place the ribs on the cool side of the grill, cover the grill, and allow them to cook for approximately 3 hours until tender.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size:10pt">
              In a medium sized pot, combine the sweet soy, tamarind puree, hoisin sauce, fish sauce, honey, and Korean hot pepper paste. Mix well and place on medium heat on the stove top. In a blender, place the garlic, ginger, jalapeo, sambal, and coriander. Puree until smooth, add into the pot with the rest of the ingredients. Bring the mixture to a simmer, and cook for 10-15 minutes. The mixture will slightly thicken and the flavors will come together. Remove from the heat and let cool to room temperature. Set aside amount of sauce needed for the ribs. The remainder can be tightly sealed and refrigerated for up to 2 weeks.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size:10pt">
              Brush with sauce on both sides of the ribs and continue to cook and sauce until the ribs develop a nice thick glaze. Remove and serve.
             </span>
            </p>

            <p>
             <span style="font-size:12.6666669845581px">
              Travel to any part of the world and you are certain to find barbecue in some way, shape or form. Today we travel to South Africa with Chef
             </span>
             <span style="font-size:12.8000001907349px">
              Hugo Uys, an expert in braii (South Africa's version of barbecue) with his recipe for traditional
             </span>
             <span style="font-size:12.8000001907349px">
              lamb sosaties -- a kebob comprised of marinated lamb and apricots.
             </span>
            </p>

           <div class="sqs-block-content">
            <p>
             <span style="font-size:12.8000001907349px">
              According to Uys, the sosaties are even better when paired with asmokey
             </span>
             <span style="font-size:12.8000001907349px">
              and fruity South African red wine such as the Brampton Old Vine Red ($14.99) which helps
             </span>
             <span style="font-size:12.8000001907349px">
              cut
             </span>
             <span style="font-size:12.8000001907349px">
              through the dense fat and release enhanced flavors.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              GRILLED LAMB SOSATIES
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size:9.5pt">
              <strong>
               <span>
                Ingredients
               </span>
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <span>
               4 pounds lamb cut into 1 inch chunks
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               2 large red onions, chopped
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               2 tablespoons olive oil
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               2 tablespoons coriander
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               4 bay leaves
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               1 teaspoon cinnamon
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               1 tablespoons curry powder
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               1 teaspoon allspice
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               1 tablespoon turmeric
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               2 teaspoons sea salt
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               2 teaspoons black pepper
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
                cup dark brown sugar
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               5 ounce ginger, grated
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
                cup red wine vinegar
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
                cup Masala wine
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
                cup smooth apricot jam
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               2 tablespoons tamarind paste
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               8 garlic cloves, grated
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               8 ounce dried apricots
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span>
               Red onions cut in 1 inch x 1 inch cubes
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <strong>
               <span>
                Directions
               </span>
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <span>
               Roast coriander seeds till slightly brown  the grind coriander into a
              </span>
              <span>
               powder
              </span>
              .
              <span>
               Saut the onions and garlic in olive oil over medium heat until
              </span>
              <span>
               translucent. Add coriander powder, cinnamon, curry powder, allspice,
              </span>
              <span>
               turmeric, salt and black pepper. Turn heat down to low and add ginger, sugar, vinegar, wine, apricot jam
              </span>
              ,
              <span>
               and tamarind paste. Simmer for +/- 8 minutes.
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size:9.5pt">
              Remove from heat and let it cool down to room temperature.
              <span>
               Add lamb chunks and marinade into re-sealable bags and massage meat well.
              </span>
              <span>
               Place in refrigerator overnight.
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size:9.5pt">
              Prepare grill for medium-high heat cooking. Skewer the lamb with apricots and red onions, and repeat.
             </span>
             <span>
              Grill the skewers until desired temperature.
             </span>
             <br/>
             <!--[if !supportLineBreakNewLine]-->
             <br/>
             <!--[endif]-->
            </p>
            <p>
             <!--EndFragment-->

               Wines of South Africa
              </a>
             </em>
            </p>
