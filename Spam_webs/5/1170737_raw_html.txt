         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          10 Island Cities around the World
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          Last updated on December 12, 2014
          <span class="entry-categories">
           in
           <a href="http://www.touropia.com/category/beaches-islands/" rel="category tag">
            Beaches and Islands

        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          A couple of weeks ago I wrote about the ultimate Robinson Crusoe feel on several
          <a href="http://www.touropia.com/uninhabited-islands-around-the-world/">
           uninhabited islands
          </a>
          around the world. The islands listed in this post were also once uninhabited but after being settled became so heavily urbanized that the built-up areas eventually took over the entire island, forming
          <strong>
           island cities
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-2085">
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>

         <div class="topitem" id="item_lindau">
          <span class="topnumber">
           10
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Lindau
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Lindau" src="http://resources.touropia.com/gfx/d/island-cities-around-the-world/lindau.jpg" title="Lindau" width="100%"/>
          <span class="photo-credit">
           wikipedia/
           <a href="http://commons.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:LindauInsel.jpg">

           </a>

         <p>
          The historic city Lindau is located near the meeting point of the Austrian, German and Swiss borders in the eastern part of Lake Constance (Bodensee). The city is connected with the mainland by bridge and railway and has about 3,000 inhabitants. Full of medieval and half-timbered buildings, the island city is quite a popular tourist attraction.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a alt="explore Germany" href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/germany/">
           Germany Guide
          </a>
         </p>

         <div class="topitem" id="item_santa_cruz_del_islote">
          <span class="topnumber">
           9
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Santa Cruz del Islote
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Santa Cruz del Islote" src="http://resources.touropia.com/gfx/d/island-cities-around-the-world/santa_cruz_del_islote.jpg" title="Santa Cruz del Islote" width="100%"/>
          <span class="photo-credit">
           photo/
           <a href="http://tolucaribe.hostei.com">
            ToluCaribe.com
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          Located off the Caribbean coast of Colombia, Santa Cruz del Islote is unofficially the worlds most
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_islands_by_population_density">
           crowded island
          </a>
          . It has some 90 houses and a population of around 1200 people crammed on an island of about 1 hectare. The islanders bury their dead in a nearby island because there is no space for a cemetery. They play football on the neighboring Mucura key, because the only public square on Santa Cruz is about half the size of a tennis court.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a alt="explore Colombia" href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/colombia/">
           Colombia Guide
          </a>
         </p>
         <div class="topitem" id="item_isola_dei_pescatori">
          <span class="topnumber">
           8
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Isola dei Pescatori
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Isola dei Pescatori" src="http://farm1.static.flickr.com/139/324872935_e07feeb9d5_z.jpg?zz=1" title="Isola dei Pescatori" width="100%"/>
          <span class="photo-credit">
           flickr/
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/27753684@N00">
            _ Night Flier _
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          Isola dei Pescatori (Fishermens Island) is the most northerly of the three principal Borromean Islands in Lago Maggiore. With a population of about 50, it is the only one island to be inhabited all year round.  A narrow street running along its spine is joined by cobbled alleys to the promenade which encircles the island. The promenade is frequently flooded and the houses built against it are constructed to allow for this. While the traditional occupation of fishing still exists, its picturesque charms has made tourism the most important source of income for the islanders.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a alt="explore Italy" href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/italy/">
           Italy Guide
          </a>
         </p>
         <div class="topitem" id="item_mexcaltitan">
          <span class="topnumber">
           7
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Mexcaltitan
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Mexcaltitan" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3069/2600936826_e5913074bc_z.jpg?zz=1" title="Mexcaltitan" width="100%"/>
          <span class="photo-credit">
           flickr/
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/31723929@N00">
            adam79
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          Mexcaltitn is a small man-made island city off the Pacific coast of Mexico. The town sits low in the marshy, mangrove-lined channels that surround it, and during the June to October rainy season, water floods the streets and everyone rows from place to place in boats. Some experts believe that Mexcaltitn may actually be the legendary Aztln, the ancestral homeland of the Aztec people. Today its foremost a shrimping town, with shrimps spread out to dry on any available surface throughout the town.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a alt="explore Mexico" href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/mexico/">
           Mexico Guide
          </a>
         </p>
         <div class="topitem" id="item_trogir">
          <span class="topnumber">
           6
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Trogir
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Trogir" src="http://resources.touropia.com/gfx/d/island-cities-around-the-world/trogir.jpg" title="Trogir" width="100%"/>
         </div>
         <p>
          Located close to the city of Split, Trogir is one of the best preserved medieval towns in Europe. Tiny medieval streets wind through the enchanting island city revealing hidden restaurants and eye-catching galleries. A wide seaside promenade snakes around the town, culminating in a charming port full of sailboats. A pleasing blend of Romanesque and Gothic architecture, Trogir boasts a spectacular Venetian Cathedral of St. Lawrence, a town hall and a medieval fortress.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a alt="explore Croatia" href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/croatia/">
           Croatia Guide
          </a>
         </p>
         <div class="topitem" id="item_nesebar">
          <span class="topnumber">
           5
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Nesebar
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Nesebar" src="http://resources.touropia.com/gfx/d/island-cities-around-the-world/nesebar.jpg" title="Nesebar" width="100%"/>
          <span class="photo-credit">
           flickr/
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/63465428@N00">
            Boby Dimitrov
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          Often referred to as the Pearl of the Black Sea, Nesebar is a rich island city defined by more than three millennia of ever-changing history. The ancient part of the town is situated on a island connected to the mainland by a narrow man-made causeway, and it bears evidence of occupation by a variety of different civilizations over the course of its existence. Nesebar is sometimes said to be the town with the highest number of churches per capita and represent the rich architectural heritage of the Eastern Orthodox.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a alt="explore Bulgaria" href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/bulgaria/">
           Bulgaria Guide
          </a>
         </p>
         <div class="topitem" id="item_flores">
          <span class="topnumber">
           4
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Flores
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Flores" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3197/3010151488_a41fde8af0_z.jpg?zz=1" title="Flores" width="100%"/>
          <span class="photo-credit">
           flickr/
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/31479545@N00">
            Javier Aroche
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          Flores is a located on Lake Petn Itz and connected to land by a causeway, on the other side of which lie the twin towns Santa Elena and San Benito. It was here, on the island of Flores, that the last independent Maya state held out against the Spanish conquerors.  Their city, Noh Petn (literally City Island) was eventually destroyed in 1697 when the Spanish attacked by boats.
         </p>
         <p>
          For many tourist, the main reason to visit Flores is its proximity to the famous Maya ruins of Tikal. But the island city itself is a great destination, filled with colonial, red-roofed buildings, narrow cobblestone streets, a historic church and many hotels and restaurants. Most will find that this island city is more than just a take-off point, but a memorable attraction in itself.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a alt="explore Guatemala" href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/guatemala/">
           Guatemala Guide
          </a>
          |
          <a href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/guatemala/flores/">
           Flores Guide
          </a>
         </p>
         <div class="topitem" id="item_male">
          <span class="topnumber">
           3
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Mal
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Mal" src="http://resources.touropia.com/gfx/d/island-cities-around-the-world/male.jpg" title="Mal" width="100%"/>
          <span class="photo-credit">
           flickr/
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/26079103@N00">
            m o d e
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          Mal is the capital and most populous city in the Maldives. Over 100,000 people are crammed onto the small island. Since there is no surrounding countryside, all infrastructure has to be located in the city itself. Water is provided from desalinated ground water while electric power is generated in the city using diesel generators. Solid waste is transported to nearby islands, where it is used to fill in lagoons. The larger airport island nearby was built in this way.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a alt="explore Maldives" href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/maldives/">
           Maldives Guide
          </a>
         </p>

          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          Manhattan is one of New Yorks five boroughs and is what people most often think of when they picture New York City. Manhattan is actually a city island and includes most of the best known attractions in New York. The word Manhattan comes from the Lenape who inhabited the area before the Europeans and is translated as island of many hills. In 1625 the Dutch built a Fort on Manhattan Island, which marked the birth of New York City. Today Manhattan is one of the most densely populated island cities in the world, with a 2008 population of 1,634,795 people living in a land area of 59.47 km (22.96 square miles).
         </p>
         <p>

           United States Guide

          <a href="http://www.touropia.com/explore/usa/new-york-city/manhattan/">
           Manhattan Guide
          </a>
         </p>
         <div class="topitem" id="item_venice">
          <span class="topnumber">
           1
          </span>
          <span class="toptitle">
           Venice
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="image_640">
          <img alt="Venice" src="http://resources.touropia.com/gfx/d/island-cities-around-the-world/venice.jpg" title="Venice"/>
          <br/>
          <span class="photo-credit">
           photo:
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/ellimac/">
            ellimac
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          World famous for its canals, Venice is built on an archipelago of 117 islands which are connected by 455 bridges. In the old center, the canals serve the function of roads, and almost every form of transport is on water or on foot.
         </p>
         <p>
          The island city is slowly sinking however and during the high tides in autumn and winter, the Piazza San Marco, the lowest area of the island, becomes totally flooded with water. Over the last 1,000 years, it has sunk by around 7 centimeters (2.8 inches) for every century, while recent reports have stated that in the last century alone, the city of Venice has lowered by around 24 centimeters (9.4 inches). This may have more to do with the rising of the sea levels in the Adriatic than with Venice sinking into its own foundations. One
          <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4458910.stm">
           proposed solution
          </a>
          is to lift the city to a greater height above sea level by pumping water into the soil underneath the island city.
         </p>
