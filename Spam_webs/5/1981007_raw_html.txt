          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The headline read:
           <span style="color: #0000ff;">
            <a href="https://medium.com/@ashwin_ramesh/startup-marketing-on-a-0-budget-1276605a0ef2" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://medium.com/@ashwin_ramesh/startup-marketing-on-a-0-budget-1276605a0ef2', 'Startup Marketing on a $0 Budget']);" style="color: #0000ff;">
             Startup Marketing on a $0 Budget
            </a>
            
           </span>
           .
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           As someone who does marketing for startups and fast-growing companies, I had to check it out. Imagine that, marketing with spending a dime!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The list compiled Ashwin Ramesh includes activities such as answering questions on Quora, leaving comments on blogs, participating in forums, blogging, building a free tool , and monitoring mentions of competitors on Twitter.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           At first glance,most of the items either struck me as busy-work or non-marketing activity. Building a free tool, for example, is a wonderful idea but its not really marketing. The same goes for monitoring social media activity. Is that marketing?
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           There are many, manythings that startups can do to wave the flag but what falls into the marketing bucket?
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           One of the biggest challenges for many startups is marketing is, in some ways, a nebulous activity for them. With expertise in engineering and development, marketing is a strange creature that uses a different language. And, even worse, marketers are slick-talking beasts that promise the world.at a price.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           For startups, this is a confusing landscape. Its like going to the Mandarin for dinner with a buffet featuring dozens of choices. Everything looks good but what do you pick to eat?
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           For startups, marketing is everything and anything. The options listed by Ramesh are, in theory, marketing because they can spread the word.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           But the key question that startups must answeris whether marketing is going to move the sales needle. At the end of the day, marketing has a job to do: raise awareness to consumers move into the sales funnel.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           If a marketing activity isnt doing that, its not worth doing.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           While there are some items on Rameshs list that have good marketing potential (e.g. blogging), I would argue there are many items that will not deliver a return on investment for the time and effort required. They will keep a startup busy but thats about it.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Instead, startup marketing is about focusing on thingsthat resonate, even if means have a limited marketing program. For example, I would much rather see a startup focus on having anactive, insight blog that drives thought leadership and brand awareness, rather than trying to multiple marketing tactics.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           In other words, you are looking for bang for the buck. As a startup with limited resources (time, people, money), its about marketing that matters.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           In many cases, its a quality versus quantity equation, or less being more. Startups can get more awareness and traction by doing well at a few things as opposed to mediocre at many things.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The most important consideration about startup marketing is having a strategic plan, regardless of whether a startup is bootstrapped or well-financed. Its aboutknowingyour target audiences, and then aligning strategy, tactics and resources to connect with them.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           This is a better approach than doing this, that and everything. From the outside looking in, it looks like a startup is actively marketing, but they really doing is spinning their wheels by spending time on things that have no ROI.
          </p>
          <hr/>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <em>
            For start-ups and fast-growing companies looking to
            <span style="color: #0000ff;">
             <a href="http://www.markevans.ca/services" style="color: #0000ff;">
              jump-start their marketing
             </a>
            </span>
            , I offer strategic planning, core messaging, product marketing and content development.
           </em>
          </p>
