            Not only is Maui Crafts Guild the only artist-owned and operated cooperative gallery on Maui, but it also features 100% locally made handcrafted art since their opening in 1982. Each of the incredibly talented Maui artisans featured at the Maui Crafts Guild are committed to the creation of high-quality fine crafts. This Maui gallery offers artisan items you will not see anywhere else in Hawaii or the world. The
            <i>
             guild
            </i>
            is composed of woodworkers, print makers, textile artists, ceramists, glass artists, photographers, basket weavers, jewelers and more. Guild membership requirements strictly enforce that each artists must be a current full-time resident of Maui, and have had residency for at least 6 months before membership consideration. Inside knowledge states a little-known cool fact that more than one of these extremely talented masterminds has lived on Maui for over 30 years. Just sayin, that
            <i>
             San Francisco Summer of Love
            </i>
            turned into the
            <i>
             Endless Maui Summer of Love
            </i>
            gotta love North Shore life on Maui. When on island, you must check out the Maui Crafts Guild in Paia. Here, you will find amazing gifts and creations from artists in Maui. Dont forget to look for the banana bark fiber art landscapes by Mathew Westcott- they are out of this world.
           </p>
           <div id="link-callout">
            For more information about the Maui Crafts Guild, please visit:
            <a href="http://www.mauicraftsguild.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mauicraftsguild.com', 'www.mauicraftsguild.com');" style="color: #0266b4;" target="_blank">

If youre in Wailea for the day, do not miss out on this luxurious and tropical art extravaganza. Local art featured includes: Hand-painted Silks by textile artist Brenda Kennerly, Hawaiian Wood Sculptures by Arthur Dennis Williams, Ocean Photography by the fabulous Cesere Brothers, Tropical Oil Paintings by the beautiful Anna Keay, Island-inspired Ceramics by Curt Stevens, unique jewelry by Kathy Uu, and much more.

           <h3>
            4. Shane Robinson Fine Art at Andaz Maui at Wailea
           </h3>
           <img alt="Shane Robinson" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2228" height="453" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-04-11_0001.jpg" width="680"/>
           <img alt="Shane Robinson" class="alignright size-full wp-image-2231 hide-on-mobile" height="610" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-04-11_0003.jpg" width="300"/>
           <p>
            Originally born in Puerto Rico, Shane Robinson earned a B.F.A. in Studio Ceramics, Sculpture and Drawing from the University of Iowas School of Art and Art History. Now living and working in Mauis charming north shore town of Haiku, Shane has mastered the art of several different artistic genres, including Intentional Camera Movement (ICM) Abstract Photography, Digital Art and Acrylic on Panel and Canvas.
           </p>
           <p>
            As part of the gorgeous new Andaz Maui at Wailea Resort, Shane has created a series of original art utilizing his skills in abstract fine art photography. Other than cropping or rotating, each piece is created entirely in-camera through a single shot. When hes not busy fine tuning his artistic skills through a lens or paintbrush, Shane enjoys building furniture as well as exploring the digital world, in which he has successfully created iPad apps and iPhoneography pieces. His art can be seen throughout permanent collections in Hawaii, and visitors are encouraged to browse his
            <a href="http://www.shanerobinson.com/andaz-maui-gallery-wailea/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.shanerobinson.com/andaz-maui-gallery-wailea/', 'gallery');" target="_blank">
             gallery
            </a>
            at the Andaz Maui, open 24 hours a day.

           <h3>
            <b>
             5. The Flavor Gallery  Mickey Eskimo Fine Art
            </b>
           </h3>
           <img alt="Mickey Eskimo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2262" height="452" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-04-14_0009.jpg" width="680"/>
           <p>
            <img alt="Mickey Eskimo" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2264 hide-on-mobile" height="756" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-04-14_0011.jpg" width="300"/>
            Originally born in Vienna, Austria, Mickey Eskimo arrived on the Hawaiian shores of Oahu as a professional windsurfer in the early 1980s. Within a few years, Mickey migrated to the North Shore of Maui for the windsurfing craze of the 80s, and to make Maui his home base. At the time, Mickey Eskimo wasnt just an Austrian Windsurfing pioneer, he was also a very sought after textile and graphic design artist for water sport retail brands. Windsurfing became an international professional talent for Mickey Eskimo, and so did travel due to his varied expertise and background. Being a man of artistic awareness and international appeal, Mickey continued to create new ideas and artistic ventures including his own unique form of collecting and collaging.
           </p>
           <p>
            Mickey Eskimos art
            <i>
             could be
            </i>
            described as collaging, but that would be too juvenile. It
            <i>
             could be
            </i>
            called mixed media, but that wouldnt even begin to sum it up. Mickeys fine art is always a
            <i>
             rare collection of rarities
            </i>
            . He somehow finds the most obscure pieces of the past; rare Hawaiiana, vintage comics, and newspaper cut outs from major historical events. He seems to magically devise his one-of-a-kind Maui art pieces and prints with a sense of ease in color matching and composure. Mickey Eskimo has also received Hollywood acclaim for his latest accessory venture- local-style graphic trucker hats with Hawaiian-style phrases.
            <i>
             Shoots
            </i>
           </p>

Owner-artists Anna Keay and her husband John Lindquist, have successfully created a gallery which fully embodies what they call where the ocean meets the flower. This fabulous art gallery in Lahaina features Anna Keays brilliant tropical oil paintings with photographic precision, John Lindquists life-like blown glass sculptures, ocean jewelry by Debra Mack, and more. When strolling Front Street in Lahaina, definitely put a star on your map for the Kai Pua Art Studio &amp; Gallery. View live glass blowing, and have one on one conversations with Kai Puas featured artists. This fabulous Maui gallery definitely offers something for everyone.

           <h3>
            <b>
             7. Hui Noeau Visual Arts Center &amp; Gallery
            </b>
           </h3>
           <img alt="Hui Noeau" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2232" height="453" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-04-11_0004.jpg" width="680"/>
           <p>
            <img alt="Hui Noeau" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2234 hide-on-mobile" height="449" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-04-11_0006.jpg" width="300"/>
            Next time youre exploring Up Country Maui, take a drive up Baldwin Avenue from the Town of Paia. Midway between Paia Town and Makawao, youll find the historic Estate of Kaluanui on the left side of the road, built in 1917 by Hui founder Ethel Baldwin. Since 1934, the Hui Noeau has been a gathering of minds for Maui arts and culture. The mission of the Hui is to unlock creativity through exceptional visual arts education. They most definitely do this by offering a collective space for artist studios, weekly classrooms, a gift shop, and exhibition galleries. The Hui is a living legend for Maui resident artists, art students, traveling artists, art instructors, art buyers and lovers. This haven of creativity offers all the best for those looking for a unique and artistic experience on the Island of Maui.
           </p>
           <div id="link-callout">
            For more information about the Hui Noeau, including upcoming exhibitions and events, please visit:
            <a href="http://www.huinoeau.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.huinoeau.com/', 'www.huinoeau.com');" style="color: #0266b4;" target="_blank">

Set on a cliff above the Pacific Ocean near the remote Kahakuloa Village, visitors have a rare chance to experience the vibes of Old Hawaii, peace and serenity. Featuring works from over 100 local Hawaii artists, the Kaukini Gallery is truly one-of-a-kind. The setting is sublime with 360-degree island views; the West Maui Mountains, lush green valleys, and deep blue azure ocean scenes as far as the eye can see. Visit this sweet and quaint Maui gallery for a collection of ceramics, paintings, woodwork, prints, and jewelry made in Hawaii.

            <b>
             9. The Village Galleries
            </b>
           </h3>
           <img alt="Village" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2246" height="645" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-04-11_0017.jpg" width="680"/>
           <img alt="Village" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2247 hide-on-mobile" height="525" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-04-11_0018.jpg" width="300"/>
           <p>
            Established in 1970, the Village Galleries are a Maui legend when it comes to art collections. They are Mauis oldest gallery presenting original art by Hawaiis most sought after artists. They have two locations in Lahaina, and one at the Ritz-Carlton in Kapalua. Village Galleries represents approximately 100 artists in Hawaii with various talents and specialty skills using mediums such as Mixed Media, Oil, Acrylic, Giclee, Watercolors, Copper Sculpture, Raku, Pastels, Woodworking, Bronzing, Printmaking and more.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Village Galleries also hosts an Annual Maui Plein Air Painting Invitational Event. It is notably one of the most successful events of this category in the United States; attracting Plein Air painters from around the world. Each year, 25 artists are chosen, and can be seen around the Island of Maui creating inspiring paintings in the outdoors.
           </p>

           <h3>
            <b>
             BONUS #2: Viewpoints Gallery
            </b>
           </h3>
           <img alt="Viewpoints Gallery" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2499" height="533" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-05-21_0019.jpg" width="680"/>
           <p>
            <img alt="Viewpoints Gallery" class="alignright size-full wp-image-2500 hide-on-mobile" height="532" src="http://www.prideofmaui.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/2014-05-21_0020.jpg" width="300"/>
            Located on the slopes of Mt. Haleakala, the Viewpoints Gallery is the only fine arts cooperative on Maui. Its perched in a restored courtyard in the infamous Makawao Town; full of art galleries, restaurants, gift shops, yoga and wellness businesses. Showcasing over 20 established local artists, this Maui gallery is well worth a visit for art lovers and collectors. Viewpoints Gallery features original fine art: one of a kind prints, watercolors, fiber art, sculptures, photography, ceramic and glass art, gifts, and much more. When visiting the Viewpoints Gallery in Makawao, seek out work by artists such as Connie Adams with her floral, gold leafed, Asian-style paintings, oil landscapes by George Allen, glass art by Karuna Santoro, sculptures by Tim Garcia, and the inspiring cultural-figurative paintings by Suzy Papanikolas. One of the best things about the Viewpoints Gallerys artist community is that they consistently offer rotating art shows and events, and participate in yearly projects that benefit local Maui non-profits. Viewpoints artists were also the first collective on Maui to support the East Maui Watershed Partnerships Malama Wao Akua, an annual art contest that celebrates and highlights Mauis native flora and fauna. (www.eastmauiwatershed.org)
