         <div class="content-scroll" id="featured-content">
          <span class="author-byline">
           by
           <strong>
            <a href="http://escapologist.com.au/author/early-to-rise/" rel="author" title="Posts by Early to Rise">
             Early to Rise
            </a>
           </strong>
           <span class="published-date">
            <i class="glyphicon glyphicon-calendar" title="Published date">
            </i>
            <a href="http://escapologist.com.au/2015/04/three-steps-crush-2015/" title="3:43 pm">
             <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-04-28T15:43:13+00:00">
              April 28, 2015
             </time>
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
          <p>
           Every three  months I fly out to Denver (from wherever Im gallivanting around the world)  for our Quarterly Planning meeting at the Early to Rise office. I join Matt  Smith, ETRs Publisher, and Jeff Schneider, our COO, for an intense morning  where we review our progress and plan our processes for the next three months.
          </p>
          <p>
           Our 90-day planning  sessions constitute our short-term thinking. I dont recommend you stop there.  I want you to think bigger and go further. I want you to
           <a href="http://escapologist.com.au/category/lifestyle/personal-development/" title="More on personal development">
            plan your life
           </a>
           by  creating a foundation for success. This foundation is called your Vision, and  its even bigger than goal setting. Having this long-term vision will make 2015  more successful.
          </p>
          <p>
           Grab a pen  and paper, because Im going to help you decide where you want your life and  business to be five years from now. By creating this vision, you create a  destination. The vision will help you make the right decisions and dictate your  actions to make 2015 an awesome year
          </p>
          <p>
           I want to  give credit to the originator of this method,
           <strong>
            Ari Weinzweig, the owner of  the famous Zuckermans Deli business in Ann Arbor, Michigan
           </strong>
           . He shared  these strategies in an article in Inc. Magazine and it was a game-changer for  my life and business.
          </p>
          <p style="font-size:22px;">
           Ari  recommends these 3 steps for creating your vision:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Step #1  Pick a time frame
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           It could be one  year, five years, or 10 years, but Ari recommends five. That really allows you  to think big without it seeming too abstract.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Step #2  Use the questions below to help you write a first draft
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Ari  recommends that you write from the future, as though you had already achieved  your vision. Do not reject any ideas. Instead, come up with a Big Audacious  Vision and be as clear, concise, and specific as possible. For example, heres  the first paragraph I wrote in the vision for one of my fitness information  publishing businesses:
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The year is 2020 we have 25 publishing clients in over a dozen  countries, each making a minimum of $250,000 in gross revenue per year with  some clients doing over $3 million gross revenue. Our clients are like  family, not only to us but also to each other
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           It sounds  like fantasy, but its my vision of reality. Thats why your vision must be  written from the future, as if one day you will truly be saying this as I  truly expect to say that about that business.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Step #3 Get feedback, re-write, and then share
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Share your  vision with those who will implement it  and move to the next step, which is  the how of strategic planning that will lay the path for reaching that  vision. Answer these:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            What does  your organisation/family look like?
           </li>
           <p>
           </p>
           <li>
            How do you  measure success? (Be specific)
           </li>
           <p>
           </p>
           <li>
            What do you  refuse to do?
           </li>
           <p>
           </p>
           <li>
            What is your  Mission and Movement?
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           I also want  to insert a few business questions from my friend Bedros Keuilian. He shared  these 3 powerful personal questions at a Mastermind Meeting.
          </p>
          <p style="font-size:22px;">
           Bedros asks himself these questions every week:
          </p>
          <p>
           1) What am I  doing to get more clients this week?
          </p>
          <p>
           2) What are you doing to keep your customers? (Bedros says it is seven times  easier to keep a customer, than to get a new one).
          </p>
          <p>
           3) What value am I adding to my circle of influence? (Clients, business  partners, and people that work for me).
          </p>
          <p>
           And finally,  he asks himself, 
           <strong>
            <em>
             Have I made more deposits than Ive taken withdrawals  this week?
            </em>
           </strong>
            Those are all great questions you can add to a weekly  review of your progress.
          </p>
          <p>
           Now back to  my questions
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            How do you  feel about your business/family/health?
           </li>
           <li>
            What are the three  most important things to your business, family, and health?
           </li>
           <li>
            What kind of  people will you need to connect with?
           </li>
           <li>
            What are  everyday tasks that go about improving your business, family, and health?
           </li>
           <li>
            What do  people in your community say about your business, family, and health?
           </li>
           <li>
            What is your  endgame? Is it working six hours per day, making six-figures, and focusing more  on family than growing your business to seven-figures? Thats fine. Be  specific. Know specifically where you want to go dont just write down, make  more money.
           </li>
           <li>
            And what  resources do you need to achieve all of these changes? Its probably not as  much money as you think.
           </li>
           <li>
            Finally, what  really makes you happy in life? What brings you the greatest joy? For me,  nothing makes me happier than the simple things in life. Hanging out with  Bedros at Mastermind meetings, hitting the gym with my buddy Jay Ferruggia,  having dinners with friends, reading, and watching my dog run really fast.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Make your own  list of what makes you really happy and brings you great heart-bursting joy,  and then never lose sight of that. Yes, its important for us to keep pushing  and to change the world, and to persevere towards our grand missions in life but  you need to know what you are willing to do and what you will not do.
          </p>
          <p>
           This will  help you stay on track to making extraordinary breakthroughs in your business  while giving you the peace of mind in knowing that you are experiencing the  joyful, simple things in life that mean the most to you.
          </p>
          <p>
           Now that  youve written down your answers for all of these, write out your vision for  where you want you, your business, your family, and your health to be in five years  from now. Write a summary as if you were writing from the future, having  already achieved your vision. You will soon have a powerful statement that is  going to guide you to more success in the next five years than youve had in  the past 25.
          </p>
          <p>
           This vision  will help you have your best year ever in 2015.
          </p>
          <p>
           Craig Ballantyne
           <br/>
           For
           <em>
            The Escapologist
           </em>
          </p>
