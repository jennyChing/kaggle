function formatDate(d, f) {
    var d = new Date(d);
    var months = ['January', 'February', 'March', 'April', 'May', 'June', 'July', 'August', 'September', 'October', 'November', 'December'];
    var days = ['Sunday', 'Monday', 'Tuesday', 'Wednesday', 'Thursday', 'Friday', 'Saturday'];
    return f.replace(/(yyyy|mmmm|mmm|mm|dddd|ddd|dd|hh|nn|ss|a\/p)/gi,
          function($1) {
              switch ($1.toLowerCase()) {
              case 'yyyy': return d.getFullYear();
              case 'mmmm': return months[d.getMonth()];
              case 'mmm':  return months[d.getMonth()].substr(0, 3);

              }
          }
      );
}

            <br/>
            <font face="Verdana">
             The VW Group's
            </font>
            <a href="http://www.carscoops.com/2014/02/carscoops-to-z-guide-to-2014-geneva.html">
             <font face="Verdana">
              Geneva Motor Show
             </font>
            </a>
            <font face="Verdana">
             announcements
            </font>
            <a href="http://www.carscoops.com/2014/03/New-2015-Audi-TT-Coupe.html">
             <font face="Verdana">
              continue with the release
             </font>
            </a>
            <font face="Verdana">
             of the new T-Roc concept for a playful small crossover with removable targa tops, positioned between
             <a href="http://www.carscoops.com/2012/10/new-volkswagen-taigun-is-concept-for-up.html">
              the upcoming Up! SUV
             </a>
             and the existing compact Tiguan.
            </font>
            <a name="more">
            </a>
            <p>
             <font face="Verdana">
              VW won't confirm a production model, but it won't deny it either at this point, stating that the T-Roc gives us a look at a possible future SUV.
             </font>
            </p>
            <p>
             <font face="Verdana">
              The 4,179 mm long, 1,831 mm wide and 1,501 mm tall (164.5 x 72.1 x 59.1 inches respectively)
             </font>
             <a href="http://www.carscoops.com/2014/02/vw-new-t-roc-crossover-concept-sounds.html">
              <font face="Verdana">
               T-Roc is
              </font>
             </a>
             <font face="Verdana">
              competitor to the Nissan Juke and
             </font>
             <a href="http://www.carscoops.com/2014/03/2015-jeep-renegade-more-of-it-surfaces.html">
              <font face="Verdana">
               the new Jeep Renegade
              </font>
             </a>
             <font face="Verdana">
              , but instead of a conventional five-door body, it has a three-door, targa-top design with seating for four passengers.
             </font>
            </p>
            <p>
             <font face="Verdana">
              According to the Germans, "the middle section of the roof consists of two halves that with just a few flicks can be taken off and stowed in the boot".
             </font>
            </p>
            <p>
             <font face="Verdana">
              Like so many other the VW Group production and concept cars aimed at the masses these days, the T-Roc also rides on the MQB platform. VW says the concept tips the scales at 1,420kg (3,130 pounds) when fitted with an 181hp (184PS) and 380Nm 2.0-liter turbocharged diesel engine hooked up to a 7sp twin-clutch DSG transmission and 4MOTION all-wheel drive system with Haldex coupling.
             </font>
            </p>
            <p>
             <font face="Verdana">
              With this powertrain setup, the T-Roc is capable of completing the standard sprint to 100km/h (62mph) from standstill in a swift 6.9 seconds, going up to a top speed of 210 km/h (130mph), and returning a fuel consumption of 4.9 l/100 km (58.6mpg UK or 48.0 mpg US) in the European combined cycle with 129 g/km of CO2.
             </font>
            </p>
            <font face="Verdana">
             VW also mentions that the T-Roc is equipped with three different driving modes: 'Street', 'Off-road' and 'Snow', which alter the parameters of the 4Motion drive train, 7-speed DSG (dual-clutch gearbox), ABS, Hill Start Assist and Hill Descent Control.
            </font>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 85%">
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: verdana">
                By John Halas
               </span>
              </b>
             </span>
            </p>
