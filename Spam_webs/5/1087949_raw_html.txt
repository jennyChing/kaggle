       <h1>
        Online PC Support Scams: Turning the Tables
       </h1>
       <p class="details">
        May 9, 2013 | BY
        <a href="https://blog.malwarebytes.org/author/jeromesegura/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jrme Segura">
         Jrme Segura
        </a>
       </p>
       <div class="social">
        <a class="tumblr" href="http://www.tumblr.com/share?v=3&amp;u=https%3A%2F%2Fwp.me%2Fp2plUD-fW&amp;t=Online+PC+Support+Scams%3A+Turning+the+Tables" target="_blank">
         <img src="https://blog.malwarebytes.org/wp-content/themes/malwarebytes-unpacked%202.0.1/images/tumblr.png"/>
        </a>
        <a class="google" href="https://plus.google.com/share?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwp.me%2Fp2plUD-fW" target="_blank">
         <img src="https://blog.malwarebytes.org/wp-content/themes/malwarebytes-unpacked%202.0.1/images/google.png"/>
        </a>
        <a class="reddit" href="http://www.reddit.com/submit?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwp.me%2Fp2plUD-fW&amp;title=Online+PC+Support+Scams%3A+Turning+the+Tables" target="_blank">
         <img src="https://blog.malwarebytes.org/wp-content/themes/malwarebytes-unpacked%202.0.1/images/reddit.png"/>
        </a>
        <a class="twitter" href="http://twitter.com/home?status=Online+PC+Support+Scams%3A+Turning+the+Tables+https%3A%2F%2Fwp.me%2Fp2plUD-fW" target="_blank">
         <img src="https://blog.malwarebytes.org/wp-content/themes/malwarebytes-unpacked%202.0.1/images/twitter.png"/>
        </a>
        <a class="facebook" href="http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?u=https%3A%2F%2Fwp.me%2Fp2plUD-fW&amp;t=Online+PC+Support+Scams%3A+Turning+the+Tables" target="_blank">
         <img src="https://blog.malwarebytes.org/wp-content/themes/malwarebytes-unpacked%202.0.1/images/fb.png"/>
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="row">
      <div class="span8">
       <p>
        You may recall a
        <a href="http://blog.malwarebytes.org/intelligence/2013/04/phone-scammers-call-the-wrong-guy-get-mad-and-trash-pc/">
         post
        </a>
        I wrote back in April about fake Microsoft phone support calls. I had received a call from scammers whose job was to trick me into buying a bogus program for only $299. When they saw I was not willing to pay, they got mad and deleted documents and pictures off my (virtual) machine before cutting me off in a very rude way. Well, this time we meet again, but on different terms: I am the one calling them and I make sure Im collecting as much evidence as possible before waving good bye.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
         <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="370" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/s60jLxInYb4?version=3&amp;rel=0&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="604">
         </iframe>
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span id="more-988">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Update
        </strong>
        : GoDaddy has taken action to remove ownership of that domain name.
       </p>
       <p>
        It all started with a pop up, warning me that my computer was infected.I decided to call to find out more about this scam. Unfortunately, I got a voice mail and was a little disappointed. I still left a few messages so perhaps they would call me back. Anyway, the next morning I tried again and got through that time. The man on the phone was quite nice (all things considered) and did not even bother with the sales pitch: just what I like,straightto the point. Our first step was to launch a legitimate program (TeamViewer) so that he could remotely take control of my PC and run a program to scan for viruses. Im really excited to see what its going to find!
       </p>
       <p>

       <p>
        To say these are false positives is an understatement. These entries are made up since I am running a clean system (Virtual Machine). I know this is a critical step and hes probably going to destroy evidence of the bogus program he just installed and ran.
