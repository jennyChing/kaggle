           <h2>
            System Boilers
           </h2>
           <p>
            The system boiler works in a pretty similar means to a conventional boiler. These types of boiler are much quicker to install due to the fact they that many of the components are already pre-built in to the boiler itself. If you go for this type of boiler you will not require a independent feed or extension cylinder.
           </p>
           <p>
            Furthermore, considered one of the best benefits is that these systems allow for the back up of an immersion heater which an be added to the system. This clearly offers security of supply in case of a boiler malfunction. You can also negate the requirement for a loft based cold water tank in the event you install a mains fed hot water cylinder .
           </p>
           <h2>
            The Combination Boiler
           </h2>
           <p>
            Any combi or combination boiler, as its also called, does indeed offer you totally dependable warm water, although the water could take one or two seconds to heat up to a hot temperature  a small price to pay! It does not need an individual cold water cistern, or indeed a hot water cylinder therefore this kind of boiler is just the ticket in the event you have a house or flat that is on the smaller side.
            <br/>
            Moreover, because they donthave anywhere near as many components, the install is quick and painless. In addition they have a tendency to be smaller sized therefore there will be a reduction of space to boot. Really the only (insignificant) draw back is you may notice a tiny decrease of water pressure in the event hot water is being run by two taps together.
           </p>
           <h2>
            The Standard Heat-Only Boiler
           </h2>
           <p>
            This class of boiler is usually known as a standard or heat only boiler. This type of boiler will transport the hot water, not just to your tank, but also around your radiators and complete system. The benefits of having a heat only standard boiler running is that they are capable of providing a continual flow of hot water from a lot more than one tap at a time.
           </p>
           <p>
            The draw back may be the bulk of the actual boiler itself. They can be a lot larger and there is, in addition, the need for tanks for both hot and cold water, though the majority homes locate these in the attic so its no normally a problem. Hot water is restricted in terms of the amount the cylinder can contain and they also require controls that you can program.
           </p>

           Dorset /drst/ (or archaically, Dorsetshire), is a county in South West England on the English Channel coast. The ceremonial county comprises the non-metropolitan county, which is governed by Dorset County Council, and the unitary authority areas of Poole and Bournemouth. Covering an area of 2,653 square kilometres (1,024 sq mi), Dorset borders Devon to the west, Somerset to the north-west, Wiltshire to the north-east, and Hampshire to the east. The county town is Dorchester which is in the south. After the reorganisation of local government in 1974 the countys border was extended eastward to incorporate the Hampshire towns of Bournemouth and Christchurch. Around half of the population lives in the South East Dorset conurbation, while the rest of the county is largely rural with a low population density. During the Second World War, Dorset was heavily involved in the preparations for the invasion of Normandy and the large harbours of Portland and Poole were two of the main embarkation points.
