        <div class="headline_area">
         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe
         </h1>
         <p class="headline_meta">
          <abbr class="published" title="2012-09-05">
           September 5, 2012
          </abbr>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="format_text entry-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/MendedWardrobe_text.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22345" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/MendedWardrobe_text.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/MendedWardrobe_text.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           By Lindsey of
           <a href="http://cafejohnsonia.com" target="_blank">
            Caf Johnsonia
           </a>
           .
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Sigh. Its true. We live in a throw-away clothing culture. We pick up inexpensive pieces, fully aware that theyll wear out and need to be replaced next season. But with new interest in slower, sustainable living, choosing clothes that last, and maintaining them so theyll last even longer, is gaining momentum. Which means patches are back!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4689.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4689.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4689.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/finishedpatches2-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="625" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/finishedpatches2-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/finishedpatches2-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          And I say hooray. The thought of wearing a favorite piece of clothing over years instead of months is wonderfully appealing. And if your pockets are feeling empty after back-to-school shopping, the idea of dungarees that will handle a thousand hours on the playground, sounds especially smart.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingsupplies-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingsupplies-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingsupplies-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          So heres a useful patching guide that will aid even the non-sewers among us. In this post, Ill cover three patch types: A simple no-sew patch in both fabric and leather. A contrasting under patch that will add a nice texture  using iron-on fusible web and a few decorative hand-sewn stitches. And third, a patch that works with knits (like sweaters) and can be hand-sewn in minutes.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-22223">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4751.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22355" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4751.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4751.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          OVER PATCHES
         </p>
         <p>
          To start, youll need some type of iron-on interfacing. This helps strengthen the fabric as well as adhere it to the area being patched. Its basically a sheet of fabric glue that melts between your piece of clothing and patch when you iron it. I recommend
          <a href="http://www.joann.com/heat-n-bond-ultra-hold-iron-on-adhesive-17-x12-/prd49052/" target="_blank" title="heat n' bond">
           Heat n Bond
          </a>
          . Because it can be used on lower heat settings, doesnt require steam, and works with a wide variety of fabrics, including synthetics. Its also much stronger than traditional fusible web. Youll want to cut it exactly the right size, using the patch as a pattern. Remove any stray threads.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Secret #1:
          </strong>
          If you need a really precise edge, skip the scissors and use a rotary cutter with a straight edge tool instead. Rotary cutters are also good for thick, tough-to-cut materials, such as leather.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4766.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4766.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4766.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The rough side of the Heat n Bond goes against the fabric.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4766.jpg">
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4770.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22357" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4770.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4770.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Iron until it is melted and has completely adhered. Let cool and remove the backing. Position the patch where you want it on the pants or jacket or whatever youre patching. It might be pretty easy to tell where the patch should go, but just in case, try on the clothing so you can place it correctly.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/ironoutside-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/ironoutside-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/ironoutside-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/ironunderside-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/ironunderside-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/ironunderside-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          You will want to iron the outside and inside of the clothing to make sure the interfacing has totally melted.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Secret #2:
          </strong>
          Corners and thicker areas are the trickiest parts. Double-check that these are totally adhered. If theyre not, add more heat.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/leatherpatch3-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/leatherpatch3-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/leatherpatch3-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre making a patch with leather instead of fabric, replace the iron-on interfacing with a
          <a href="http://www.joann.com/dritz-liquid-stitch/xprd274584/" target="_blank" title="liquid stitch">
           strong fabric glue
          </a>
          . Cut the shape you want (perhaps hearts for some little girl jeans : ). I cut the basic rectangle from leather with the rotary cutter and rounded the edges with sharp scissors.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/leatherpatch2-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/leatherpatch2-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/leatherpatch2-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Place glue on the underside of the leather and place it on the pants.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4630.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4630.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4630.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Press firmly and let dry. Easy as can be.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          UNDER PATCHES
         </p>
         <p>
          Sometimes you may want to use an under patch, like when holes develop in the knees of your favorite jeans.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/rotarycutter+yellowfabric-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22288" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/rotarycutter+yellowfabric-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/rotarycutter+yellowfabric-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Pick a fun, contrasting fabric that will peek through the holes and a coordinated thread.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/heat-n-bond-rough-side-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/heat-n-bond-rough-side-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/heat-n-bond-rough-side-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans4-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans4-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans4-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Cut a piece of Heat n Bond the same size as your fabric patch,  leaving a hole in the center that will align with the hole in the jeans.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans4-1-of-1.jpg">
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans3-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans3-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans3-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Pin it directly behind the holes in the knees and iron as directed.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans2-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans2-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans2-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Secret #3:
          </strong>
          I wanted to make the fabric patch extra strong to ensure it would hold up longer, so I used Heat n Bond to attach some sturdy twill to the back of the fabric.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans5-1-of-1.jpg">
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4789.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4789.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/untitled-4789.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
           <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans5-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/holesinjeans5-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          As a final step, I used embroidery floss to make some stars that match the fabric. The hand-sewn stitches arent just cute, they also help keep the patch firmly in place.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sweaterbefore.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="357" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sweaterbefore.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sweaterbefore.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          KNITS
         </p>
         <p>
          When patching a knitted clothing item, such as a sweater, you wont want to use the heat n bond or glued or under-patching techniques above. Instead, youll want to pick a sturdy fabric that coordinates with the sweater and use a simple stitch to sew it on.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/placingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-11.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22341" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/placingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-11.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/placingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-11.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          For the elbow patch pictured, heavy upholstery felt was used. Regular felt strengthened with interfacing will work too. Or even leather would also make a handsome patch.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Secret #4:
          </strong>
          Sometimes, patches on knits are used to reinforce instead of repair. But, if you are using the patch to cover a hole, you will need to
          <a href="http://ladyott.com/2012/04/18/how-to-mend-holes-in-woolens/" target="_blank">
           mend the hole
          </a>
          a bit first so it doesnt keep unraveling under the patch.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the example pictured, there is no hole to mend. The patches are being added because theyre cute. : )
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/feltpinnedtosweater-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/feltpinnedtosweater-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/feltpinnedtosweater-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Mark where the patch should go and pin it in place.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1-2.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1-2.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1-2.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22337" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/sewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Use embroidery floss to make
          <a href=" http://www.fiber-images.com/Free_Things/Reference_Charts/free_reference_charts_handsewing_stitches.html" target="_blank">
           quick stitches
          </a>
          all the way around the patch, making sure to tie strong knots at the beginning and end. Simple as that.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/finishedsewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" height="700" src="http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/finishedsewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1.jpg" title="Clothes Patching Guide via DesignMom.com" width="500"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.designmom.com/2012/09/living-well-4-secrets-to-patching-clothes/&amp;media=http://www.designmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/finishedsewingfeltpatchonsweater-1-of-1.jpg&amp;description=Living Well: 4 Secrets to a Well-Mended Wardrobe')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Happy patching! And heres hoping youll be wearing that favorite sweater for many, many years.
         </p>

                  I have been reading these great comments but Margo you have the answer. I have mended clothes for my self and for customers for 30 years. The iron on type of patches, I believe, do come off unless they are stitched down. With jeans or work trousers I used to use a lighter fabric to cover the hole on the inside and machine down then a neater patch of matching fabric on the outside. Cutting an oval [round corners on a square] shape makes it easier to sew by machine. Patching fabric can be from an old garment or fabric scraps. Try a charity shop. Living in a small town I often saw my handiwork as the workmen got on with their day.
                  <br/>
                  When my son was at school I wouldnt send him in mended clothes until I realized that at least it showed I cared enough to bother patching rather than having holes in his pants knees.
                  <br/>

                   <p>
                    I find that I have better luck avoiding the tummy area holes if I wash my t-shirts (especially the thin ones) in lingerie bags. A pain? you bet but they stay hole-free much longer. Also, look at the quality of what you are buying in the first place. Thick, quality t-shirt material will last longer, require less patching/mending, and then when you do  fix a little hole, it will be worth it because the rest of the garment will last also.
                    <br/>
                    Finally the dryer kills clothes. I hate to say it, but it does cost in terms of wear and tear (as well as the environment)  I found this when I lived in Lithuania for a bit and was forced to hang all my clothes to dry (no dryer there!). At first I hated it, then I grew accustomed to it, and then I found that my clothes were lasting far longer! I have never gone back to using the dryer on most sweaters, t-shirts, jeans at least not the good ones! The house ones, yes, I do put in the dryer. Its that old ounce of prevention thing, I guess.

I find the cheap knee high ones in multiple colors.  I just cut a circle about a half inch larger all around than the hole and out all three layers together and iron from the inside.  It can be tricky keeping the pantyhose material from rolling so I use ball point pins to hold the edges.  The patch stretches with the fabric and its almost perfectly invisible!  Works great!
           </p>
