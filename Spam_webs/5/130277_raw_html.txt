      <h1>
       <a href="http://www.thecrepesofwrath.com/2011/08/03/vietnamese-coconut-pork/">
        Vietnamese Coconut Pork
       </a>
      </h1>
      <h2>
      </h2>
     </hgroup>
     <div class="entry">
      <p>
       Im off to
       <a href="http://www.blogher.com/blogher-11" target="_blank">
        BlogHer 2011
       </a>
       tomorrow, courtesy of
       <a href="http://www.blogher.com/knorr-four-recipe-contest" target="_blank">
        Knorr
       </a>
       ! If you will be attending, please come up to me and say hi! You can also come watch me cook my
       <a href="http://thecrepesofwrath.com/2011/07/08/weeknight-chicken-mushroom-pho/" target="_blank">
        Weeknight Chicken &amp; Mushroom Pho
       </a>
       live at the Knorr Kitchen Booth (in the exhibit hall, booth #115) at 11 AM on Saturday, August 6th! Im very excited, and, of course, very nervous, but either way, Im thrilled to be a part of this competition and I cant wait to attend my first BlogHer conference.
      </p>
      <p>
       I wanted to cook something comforting, seeing as how I will be gone for four days without Kramer, which rarely happens. Im really going to miss him! We never spend much time apart, so its always weird to head off to the airport without him. I figured that this dish would be a great way to make us both feel a bit better. I hope that the idea of coconut pork doesnt scare you, because this is really a fantastic recipe. The flavors that come through are really the ginger, Chinese 5 spice, garlic, and shallot  the coconut is mostly absorbed by the pork, making for some melt-in-your-mouth, fall-apart-tender pieces of pork. The sauce is absolutely delicious, too, and goes great on top of the rice on its own. This dish is pretty much fool proof: just put it all in the pot, let it cook for 2 hours, and ta-da! Youve got dinner on the table. I hope that everyone has a great week  please send good vibes my way for the cooking competition  I need all the help I can get!
      </p>
      <p>
       <span id="more-3652">
       </span>
       <br/>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_1.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_1.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Your ingredients.
      <p>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_2.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_2.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Mince your garlic.
      <p>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_3.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_3.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      And your shallot.
      <p>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_4.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_4.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Combine it with your ginger, Chinese 5 spice, soy sauce, red pepper flakes, and sugar.
      <p>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_5.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_5.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Heat your oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot, and add in the meat. Cook until browned all over, in batches, if necessary.
      <p>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_6.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_6.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_7.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_7.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Add in your shallot, garlic, soy sauce, ginger, Chinese 5 spice, soy sauce, red pepper flakes, and sugar. Stir to combine, add in the coconut milk, then cover and cook over medium-low heat for an hour and a half.
      <p>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_8.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_8.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      For the last half hour, stir, and uncover to allow the sauce to reduce a bit.
      <p>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_9.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Vietnamese Coconut Pork" src="/images/2011/08/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork/Vietnamese_Coconut_Pork_9.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Serve over rice and your favorite vegetables and enjoy!

          <time datetime="" itemprop="totalTime">

        <div class="ERSClear">
        </div>
        <div class="ERSHead">
         Serves:
         <span itemprop="recipeYield">
          4
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="ERSClear">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="ERSIngredients">
        <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
         Ingredients
        </div>
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 pounds pork, cubed into bite-sized pieces
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon ground black pepper
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon kosher salt
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          5 cloves garlic, finely minced
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 large shallot, finely minced
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 teaspoon granulated sugar
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon ground ginger
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon Chinese 5 spice
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           teaspoon red pepper flakes
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          juice of  a lemon
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 can low-fat coconut milk
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          rice, for serving
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          steamed vegetables, for serving
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          scallions, sliced thinly, for garnish
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div class="ERSClear">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="ERSInstructions">
        <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
         Instructions
        </div>
        <ol>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Heat your oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add in the cubed pork, toss with salt and pepper, and working in batches, if necessary, and cook until browned all over. Combine your minced garlic, minced shallot, soy sauce, granulated sugar, ground ginger, Chinese 5 spice, red pepper flakes, and lemon juice in a bowl, then pour over the browned pork. Stir to combine, then add in the coconut milk, and stir to combine. Cover, reduce the heat to medium-low, and cook for 1 hours, stirring every so often to prevent burning.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          After 1 hours, remove the lid of the pot, stir, and allow to cook for another 30 minutes or so, allowing the sauce to reduce a bit. Taste and adjust seasoning as necessary. Serve over rice, with your favorite steamed vegetables, and garnish with a few thinly sliced scallions.
         </li>
        </ol>
