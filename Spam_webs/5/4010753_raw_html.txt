             <h1 class="post-title">
              <a href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/cooking/2011/10/corn-cheese-chowder/" title="Corn &amp; Cheese Chowder">
               Corn &amp; Cheese Chowder
              </a>
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-tasks">
              <span class="author">
               Posted by Ree
              </span>
              <a class="comment-number-wrap" href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/cooking/2011/10/corn-cheese-chowder/#comments">
               <div class="comment-number">
                242
               </div>
               <span>
                Comments
               </span>
              </a>
              <a class="print-icon" href="#recipe-form-171474" id="print-recipe-link-171474">
               Print Recipe
              </a>
             </div>
            </header>
            <div class="post-padding">
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274947569/" title="TPW_4555 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4555" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6106/6274947569_7b391e3c38_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Last week, I whipped up three yummy soups, all perfect for Halloween night and all guaranteed to:
             </p>
             <p>
              1. Give your trick-or-treaters a nice warm head start before they take off on their quest to fill their bags to the brim with candy
              <br/>
              2. Possibly cut down on the number of pieces of candy your child will consume while he is trick-or-treating
              <br/>
              3. I cant think of a third thing.
             </p>
             <p>
              Todays recipe is a scrumptious, thick, delicious corn chowder made extra luscious with cheese. I served it in a bread bowl because corn chowder in a bread bowl is one of lifes great extravagances.
             </p>
             <p>
              (In a moment of soupy serendipity, at the exact time I was cooking my Halloween soups last week [Broccoli Cheese among the three varieties] A Cozy Kitchen posted this beautiful
              <a href="http://tastykitchen.com/blog/2011/10/broccoli-cheddar-soup/" target="_blank">
               broccoli-cheese
              </a>
              bread bowl on the Tasty Kitchen Blog. Ill post my broccoli-cheese version later this weekbut that one looks utterly divine.)
             </p>
             <p>
              And now. For zee chowder, baby.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275447828/" title="TPW_4212 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4212" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6112/6275447828_90831db0a2_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              The Cast of Characters: Butter, chopped onion, bacon pieces (either uncooked or cooked), fresh corn kernels, diced bell pepper (I used yellow, orange, and red; pretty!), sliced green onions, grated Monterey jack, grated pepper jack (or you can do any cheese combo!), flour, and
             </p>
             <p>
              NOT PICTURED because Im rusty with Cast of Characters photos and because Im an airhead: salt, pepper, chicken broth and half-and-half.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274926589/" title="TPW_4257 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4257" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6038/6274926589_b535ea0fcc_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              In a good-sized pot, saute the chopped onions in butter.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274927995/" title="TPW_4259 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4259" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6044/6274927995_88e85f6f18_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Add the bacon and saute it for a couple of minute, or until the onions and bacon become best friends forever.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275454750/" title="TPW_4260 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4260" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6035/6275454750_67c5e5fd52_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Next, throw in the diced bell peppers
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275455472/" title="TPW_4262 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4262" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6116/6275455472_92771b0654_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              And saute them around for a couple of minutes.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275455924/" title="TPW_4263 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4263" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6224/6275455924_1a4c46be45_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Next goes the corn.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275457000/" title="TPW_4264 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4264" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6120/6275457000_4603868540_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Cook it for a couple of minutes.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275457714/" title="TPW_4267 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4267" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6116/6275457714_6cc3b987ff_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              And now. This is important. Use your freakiest, most disturbing-looking alien hand you havewell, on hand. And with that hand, sprinkle in some flour.
             </p>
             <p>
              (If you do not have a freaky, disturbing-looking alien hand, you can have mine.)
             </p>
             <p>
              (No, really. You can.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275458298/" title="TPW_4268 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4268" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6117/6275458298_36666f6ffd_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Stir to incorporate the flour, then pour in the broth.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274933025/" title="TPW_4269 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4269" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6111/6274933025_2e5d138231_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Stir this around and let it thicken for a couple of minutes
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274933577/" title="TPW_4271 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4271" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6044/6274933577_28594b562c_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Then pour in the half-and-half. You can also use milk. You can also use half milk and half half-and-half.
             </p>
             <p>
              Either way, were getting ready to add enough cheese to render this choice irrelevant.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274937785/" title="TPW_4323 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4323" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6041/6274937785_182e7c94d5_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Now its just a matter of letting it simmer and thicken for 10-15 minutes or so.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275461970/" title="TPW_4317 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4317" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6053/6275461970_b27c4ab696_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Yum!
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274936963/" title="TPW_4319 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4319" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6213/6274936963_27c43a082a_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Now just dump in a bunch of green onions
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275461324/" title="TPW_4316 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4316" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6019/6275461324_1271c2f3ee_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              And a bunch of cheese.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275469052/" title="TPW_4533 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4533" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6039/6275469052_56c4cc475a_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Stir it around, check the seasonings, and add some black pepper.
             </p>
             <p>
              Now you can just stick on the lid and turn off the stove for a little while; itll stay warm for awhile.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275464244/" title="TPW_4516 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4516" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6091/6275464244_caa9683481_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Behold zee boule.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274940743/" title="TPW_4519 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4519" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6230/6274940743_315db58407_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Lop off the top, then pick out the innards, leaving a small margin of bread in tact.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274941981/" title="TPW_4524 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4524" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6223/6274941981_2f371b1ddc_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Gnaw on these if youre hungry, or stick em in a baggie and save emor feed em to the ducks.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274944123/" title="TPW_4535 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4535" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6119/6274944123_9eb4b79876_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Then grab a ladle
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6275471018/" title="TPW_4539 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4539" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6119/6275471018_5801e8738c_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              And fill up the hollowed-out boule with the creamy, wonderful, slightly crunchy goodness.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274946877/" title="TPW_4551 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4551" class="width_630 frame-img" height="945" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6232/6274946877_6e6732e388_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              This is a rather large boule, I will say that. But some stores sell them smaller than this (or, if not, Ill show you a trick with my next soup post.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/6274947569/" title="TPW_4555 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_4555" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6106/6274947569_7b391e3c38_o.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              To die for. If you have corn-loving trick-or-treaters in your house, theyll love this.
             </p>
             <p>
              Enjoy!
             </p>

              <h4 class="recipe-sub-head">
               Preparation Instructions
              </h4>
              <div itemprop="instructions">
               <p>
                In a large pot, melt butter over medium-high heat. Cook onions for a couple of minutes. Add bacon and cook for another minute or so, then add diced bell peppers and cook for a couple of minutes. Finally, add corn and cook for a minute.
               </p>
               <p>
                Sprinkle flour evenly over the top and stir to combine. Pour in broth and stir well. Allow this to thicken for 3 or 4 minutes, then reduce heat to low. Stir in half-and-half, then cover and allow to simmer/thicken for 15 minutes or so.
               </p>
               <p>
                Stir in cheeses and green onions. When cheese is melted and the soup is hot, check seasonings. Add salt and pepper as needed.
               </p>
               <p>
                Ladle into hollowed out boules and serve immediately.
               </p>
