      <h1>
       Diamond Certification  How Diamonds Are Certified And Why You Should Care
      </h1>
     </div>
     <div id="post">
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.jamesallen.com/diamond-pendants/" rel="nofollow">
         <img alt="Diamond Certification - How diamonds are certified and why you should care" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-365" src="http://diamondreviews.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/Diamond-Certification-How-diamonds-are-certified-and-why-you-should-care.jpg" style="float: left;margin: 0 5px 0 0"/>
        </a>
        <i>
         Diamond certification
        </i>
        is the process by which diamonds are measured, graded, and certified. If you have any experience shopping for diamonds, you will probably have seen a diamond certificate, or at least some of the grading information that comes from those certificates.
        <u>
         Diamond certification
        </u>
        involves a lot of different information, and some of these details have a much more direct effect than others on the value of the diamond in question. Heres what you really need to know, why you need to know it, and how youll benefit from that knowledge.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <a href="http://www.jamesallen.com/diamonds/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         <span style="font-size: 18px">
          Click Here And Browse Through First-Class Diamonds
         </span>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         Diamond Certification  The Basics
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Although it can be done in slightly different ways, the basic process of diamond certification involves having a gemologist assess an individual diamond. There are two basic types of information found on diamond certificates: quantitative information, such as measurements of the weight and other physical dimensions of the diamond, and qualitative information presented in the forms of grades in three major areas of
        <a href="http://diamondreviews.org/diamond-guide/diamond-cut/" target="_blank">
         cut
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://diamondreviews.org/diamond-guide/diamond-color-and-clarity/" target="_blank">
         clarity and color
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Cut, clarity and color are three of the fabled four Cs of diamond certification and appraisal, and these factors have a significant impact on the market value of a diamond. A diamond with better grades in these areas will be valued more highly than one with lower grades. The fourth C is carats. Carats are a unit of measurement used to express the actual weight of a diamond; large, high-carat diamonds are significantly more expensive than smaller ones. The four Cs are the most important things youll find on a diamond certificate.
       </p>
       <p>
        Diamond certification is performed by several different organizations, the largest of which are the Gemological Institute of America (GIA) and the American Gem Society (AGS). Most diamond certificates will have either the AGS or GIA seals on them, and these seals are proof that the diamonds in question have been independently inspected and certified, and that you can safely use the information contained therein in order to make an educated decision on the actual value of the diamond.
       </p>
       <p>
        Having diamond certification provided by a reputable institution is important because it ensures that youre getting this crucial information from an unbiased third party, as opposed to just taking the vendors word for it.
       </p>
       <h3>
        <strong>
         Diamond Certification  Why you Should Care
        </strong>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.jamesallen.com/diamond-pendants/" rel="nofollow">
         <img alt="Diamond Certification - How diamonds are certified and why you should care" class="alignright size-full wp-image-366" src="http://diamondreviews.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/Diamond-Certification-How-diamonds-are-certified-and-why-you-should-care2.jpg" style="float: right;margin: 0 0px 0 5px"/>
        </a>
        When buying a diamond, its obvious that you should be able to look at and assess the quality of the diamond yourself. Quite simply, if it doesnt look good, then it isnt worth buying, and this is a decision that any buyer can make independently.
       </p>
       <p>
        Diamond certification matters because it gives you a reliable way to determine how much a diamond should cost relative to others. Take the clarity grade, for example. Depending on the
        <a href="http://diamondreviews.org/diamond-cuts/" target="_blank">
         shape of the diamond
        </a>
        , the difference between a diamond with a clarity rating of VS1 and one of the same shape with a rating of SI2 may be virtually impossible to detect with the naked eye, and so, from an aesthetic standpoint, the two diamonds are essentially the same for your purposes. The diamond with an SI2 rating, though, should be less expensive even though the practical difference between the two diamonds is negligible.
       </p>
       <p>
        This type of difference provides you with an opportunity to save money and get better value, and its an opportunity that wouldnt be available without proper diamond certification. This is the main reason why diamond certification should matter to buyers.
       </p>
