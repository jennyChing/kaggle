       <h1>
        <a class="subj-link" href="http://oriannakitty.livejournal.com/204910.html">
         kids, "pro-lifers", and partying like a mathematician
        </a>
       </h1>
       <div class="aboutentry">
        April 9th, 2009 (05:09 pm)
       </div>
       <div class="entrylinks">
        <a href="http://oriannakitty.livejournal.com/204910.html?mode=reply#add_comment" title="reply">
         <img alt="reply" border="0" src="http://l-stat.livejournal.net/img/style/3column/leavecomment.gif" title="reply"/>
        </a>
        <a href="http://oriannakitty.livejournal.com/204910.html" title="link">
         <img alt="link" border="0" src="http://l-stat.livejournal.net/img/style/3column/permalink.gif" title="link"/>
        </a>
        <a class="js-lj-share" data-title="kids,%20%22pro-lifers%22,%20and%20partying%20like%20a%20mathematician" data-url="http%3A%2F%2Foriannakitty.livejournal.com%2F204910.html" href="http://www.livejournal.com/update.bml?repost_type=c&amp;repost=http://oriannakitty.livejournal.com/204910.html&amp;nodraft=1" title="Share">
         <img alt="Share" border="0" src="http://l-stat.livejournal.net/img/style/3column/share.gif" title="Share"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="entrytext">
        <p>
         While waiting in the long line of cars to pick up Benjamin from school, I saw a HUGE SUV with "pro-life" stickers plastered all over it, and the lady driving it threw some trash out the window that floated over into the school's playground.  Two little girls playing on the swings saw it and ran over to pick it all up and throw it away.  I was watching all this happen as if it was a metaphor for how Christian Republican Pro-lifers don't care about EXISTING life, and only care about potential life and afterlife.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         When I finally got to the front of the line, I discovered that Benjamin had a guitar lesson that day after school and that I should have come later because of this, so I waited around for it to finish and was entertained by the energetic childrens. There was a TON of laughing and crazy noises coming from the classroom next to us and one of the kids asked "is that the MATH club??" and the teacher responded "YES! They are partying like MATHEMATICIANS!!!"... I was highly amused.
        </p>

       <div class="entrytext">
        <p>
         So yesterday I helped pass on the sacred tradition of going through other people's garbage in search of free awesome stuff. Actually, all I did was (while bringing Benjamin to a park) I saw some furniture at the side of the road that I could use, and we pulled over to get it. But afterwards, I started babbling about freegans and about all the great stuff I found in the past (like the time we got a working piano and painted it psychedelic colors, and the time we found a huge box of bananas and had a BANANA PARTY where we made banana bread, banana pancakes, etc). Then he was like "Sounds like FUN" and said he wanted to go dumpster-diving with me sometime! Horray for awesome kids!
