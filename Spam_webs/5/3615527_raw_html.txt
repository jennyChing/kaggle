     <p>
      A
      <b>
       concentration camp
      </b>
      is a place which a
      <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">
       government
      </a>
      uses to keep people who are either against that government or who it thinks are too dangerous to remain free. Sometimes these are called
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internment_camp" title="Internment camp">
       internment camps
      </a>
      , where a large number of people are put in
      <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">
       prison
      </a>
      without a
      <a href="/wiki/Trial" title="Trial">
       trial
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The people who are locked away in such a prison, are not usually yet found
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guilty" title="Guilty">
       guilty
      </a>
      of a
      <a href="/wiki/Crime" title="Crime">
       crime
      </a>
      , but may be
      <a href="/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">
       politically
      </a>
      against the leaders of a region, people who are of a certain
      <a href="/wiki/Race_(sociology)" title="Race (sociology)">
       race
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">
       religion
      </a>
      , or non-military
      <a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_war" title="Prisoner of war">
       prisoners of war
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_concentration_camps">
       History of concentration camps
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=1" title="Change section: History of concentration camps">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Change section: History of concentration camps">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Many countries have used concentration camps often during wars or times of trouble and fighting.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">
       United States
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=2" title="Change section: United States">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Change section: United States">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The first modern concentration camps in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      were created in 1838. At this time, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_government" title="United States government">
       United States government
      </a>
      , led by
      <a href="/wiki/Martin_Van_Buren" title="Martin Van Buren">
       President Van Buren
      </a>
      , decided to remove the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_North_America" title="Indigenous peoples of North America">
       native
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Cherokee" title="Cherokee">
       Cherokee
      </a>
      population from their lands and place them all in concentration camps.
     </p>
     <p>
      During the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">
       American Civil War
      </a>
      in the1860s,
      <a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_war" title="Prisoner of war">
       soldiers who been captured
      </a>
      were sometimes placed in camps, which were very crowded and had very bad conditions. These were meant to be prisoner of war camps with good conditions, but many men died from sickness or
      <a href="/wiki/Starvation" title="Starvation">
       hunger
      </a>
      . At
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andersonville_prison&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andersonville prison (not yet started)">
       Andersonville prison
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">
       Confederate
      </a>
      prisoner of war camp,about 12,000 men died (out of about 45,000 who were in prison there). This camp was not meant to be so bad, and the men in charge were later put on
      <a href="/wiki/Trial" title="Trial">
       trial
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Death_penalty" title="Death penalty">
       killed
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/War_crime" title="War crime">
       war crimes
      </a>
      . The
      <a href="/wiki/Union_(American_Civil_War)" title="Union (American Civil War)">
       Union
      </a>
      's prisoner of war camps also had very bad conditions. After hearing what Andersonville prison was like, guards at Camp Chase in
      <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">
       Columbus, Ohio
      </a>
      , and at the "40 Acres of Hell" in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" title="Chicago, Illinois">
       Chicago, Illinois
      </a>
      , treated Confederate prisoners badly to get revenge for Andersonville.
     </p>
     <p>
      During the so-called
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Indian_Wars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Indian Wars (not yet started)">
       Indian Wars
      </a>
      (in the 1870s and later), the United States made many enemies who were
      <a href="/wiki/Native_American" title="Native American">
       Native Americans
      </a>
      . The U.S. government forced these people to leave their land and move onto "reservations." (These were called reservations because some land had been put aside, or "reserved," for the Native Americans.) Native Americans were not allowed to leave their reservations. On some reservations, many people, especially children, died from hunger and sickness.
     </p>
     <p>
      During
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      , the United States placed many
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">
       Japanese
      </a>
      -Americans in
      <a href="/wiki/Japanese_American_internment" title="Japanese American internment">
       internment camps
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="British_Empire">
       <b>
        British Empire
       </b>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=3" title="Change section: British Empire">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Change section: British Empire">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Around 1900, the
      <a href="/wiki/British" title="British">
       British
      </a>
      used concentration camps in the
      <a href="/wiki/Second_Boer_War" title="Second Boer War">
       Second Boer War
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
       South Africa
      </a>
      . The families of South African men fighting against the British were put in camps to stop them from giving food and help to the fighters. Their houses and farms were also burned. At least 30,000 people, mostly children, died in these camps from sickness or hunger.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">
       Spain
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=4" title="Change section: Spain">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Change section: Spain">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In the late 1800s,
      <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">
       Cuba
      </a>
      was a Spanish
      <a href="/wiki/Occupied_territory" title="Occupied territory">
       territory
      </a>
      , controlled by
      <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
       Spain
      </a>
      . When Cuban people tried to
      <a href="/wiki/Rebellion" title="Rebellion">
       rebel
      </a>
      and fight for
      <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">
       independence
      </a>
      in 1896 and 1897, Spain created concentration camps and sent many Cuban people to live in them. This was called the "Reconcentrado" Policy.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Nazi_Germany">
       Nazi Germany
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=5" title="Change section: Nazi Germany">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Change section: Nazi Germany">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      During World War II,
      <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">
       Nazi Germany
      </a>
      created many concentration camps,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slave" title="Slave">
       slave
      </a>
      labor camps, and extermination camps (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_camp" title="Death camp">
       death camps
      </a>
      ). Nazi Germany's leader,
      <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">
       Adolf Hitler
      </a>
      , thought that certain groups of people (like
      <a href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">
       Jews
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">
       Slavs
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Roma_people" title="Roma people">
       Roma people
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disabled" title="Disabled">
       people with disabilities
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homosexuals_in_Nazi_Germany" title="Homosexuals in Nazi Germany">
       homosexuals
      </a>
      ) were inferior (not as good as other people). He decided that everyone in these groups should be killed. He also wanted to get rid of people who he thought might challenge or fight the Nazi government. These people included
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Socialist" title="Socialist">
       socialists
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist" title="Communist">
       communists
      </a>
      , people of certain
      <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">
       religions
      </a>
      , and members of resistance movements.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Nazis sent many of these people to concentration camps to work as slave labor.
      <span>
       After a few years, some camps were set up just to kill people. These are now called "extermination camps" or "death camps." At these camps, people were killed in
       <a href="/wiki/Gas_chamber" title="Gas chamber">
        gas chambers
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Shooting" title="Shooting">
        shot
       </a>
       , worked to death, and marched to death. Many people also died from disease and starvation in the camps.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      More than half of the people who died in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holocaust" title="Holocaust">
       Holocaust
      </a>
      died at Nazi concentration camps. Just in the
      <a href="/wiki/Auschwitz_concentration_camp" title="Auschwitz concentration camp">
       Auschwitz camps
      </a>
      , at least 1.1 million people died (about 1,000,000 Jews and about 75,000 non-Jewish people, like
      <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">
       Poles
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Towards the end of World War II, the Nazis killed up to 20,000 people a day in the camps' gas chambers.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">
       Italy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=6" title="Change section: Italy">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Change section: Italy">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
       Italy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">
       Benito Mussolini
      </a>
      's government also created concentration camps in Italy and its
      <a href="/wiki/Occupied_territory" title="Occupied territory">
       occupied territories
      </a>
      . Most of the people sent to these camps were
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slovenes" title="Slovenes">
       Slovenes
      </a>
      , Jews, and Roma people.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Independent_State_of_Croatia">
       Independent State of Croatia
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=7" title="Change section: Independent State of Croatia">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Change section: Independent State of Croatia">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jadovno_concentration_camp" title="en:Jadovno concentration camp">
       Jadovno
      </a>
      extermination camp was the first of twenty-sixconcentration camps established by theUstaeregime and located in the Independent State of Croatia(ISC) during the World War II.
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jasenovac_concentration_camp" title="en:Jasenovac concentration camp">
       Jasenovac concentration camp
      </a>
      was the largest extermination andconcentration camp. The largest number of victims were ethnicSerbs, seen as the main racial enemy of the ISC. The camp also heldJews,Roma, Croats and a number of Yugoslav Partisans.
      <sup>
       [1].
      </sup>
      Other notorious extermination andconcentration camps include: Uvala Slana on the island of Pag and
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jastrebarsko_concentration_camp" title="en:Jastrebarsko concentration camp">
       Jastrebarsko
      </a>
      - set up specifically for children.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_Union">
       Soviet Union
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=8" title="Change section: Soviet Union">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Change section: Soviet Union">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Prison camps were used in
      <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
       Russia
      </a>
      for many years, especially in places in the
      <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">
       Arctic
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">
       Siberia
      </a>
      , a long way from the main cities. However, starting in the 1920's under the
      <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
       Soviet Union
      </a>
      , many more people were sent to labor camps. These camps, "zona" in Russian, are sometimes called "gulags"  an acronim for "Prison Camp Management Headquarters". Anyone who was seen as a threat to the government was sent to the gulag. In 1939, there were millions of people working as slaves in these camps.
     </p>
     <p>
      When
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Josef_Stalin" title="Josef Stalin">
       Josef Stalin
      </a>
      and the Soviet Union
      <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Poland_(1939)" title="Invasion of Poland (1939)">
       invaded Poland
      </a>
      at the start of World War II, at least 1.5 million Poles were
      <a href="/wiki/Deportation" title="Deportation">
       deported
      </a>
      at gunpoint. They were forced to get into cattle wagons, which took them to concentration camps in Siberia. Whole families were deported to the camps, including children and the elderly.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Russian writer
      <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Solzhenitsyn" title="Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn">
       Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn
      </a>
      wrote
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Gulag_Archipelago" title="The Gulag Archipelago">
        The Gulag Archipelago
       </a>
      </i>
      about his experiences in a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet" title="Soviet">
       Soviet
      </a>
      labor camp.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Latin_America">
       Latin America
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;veaction=edit&amp;vesection=9" title="Change section: Latin America">
        change
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-divider">
        |
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Concentration_camp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Change section: Latin America">
        change source
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      During the 1970s and 1980s, many military dictatorships in
      <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">
       Latin America
      </a>
      <span>
       set up concentration camps to imprison,
       <a href="/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">
        torture
       </a>
       , and kill their political opponents (people who disagreed with them). For example,
      </span>
      the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinochet" title="Pinochet">
       Pinochet
      </a>
      military regime in
      <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
       Chile
      </a>
      used concentration camps and torture centers, like
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colonia_Dignidad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colonia Dignidad (not yet started)">
       Colonia Dignidad
      </a>
      (or Villa Baviera), in
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Parral&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Parral (not yet started)">
       Parral
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
       Chile
      </a>
      .
     </p>
