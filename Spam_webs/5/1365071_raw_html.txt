       <p>
        <strong>
         The Barony of Lyondemere
        </strong>
        is the local chapter of the
        <b>
         <a href="http://www.sca.org" target="_blank">
          SCA
         </a>
        </b>
        , the Society for Creative Anachronism. The SCA is a non-profit 501(c) society dedicated to research and re-creation of elements of the Middle Ages and Renaissance history. The Barony is also the parental group for the Noe College, the SCA branch at Cal State University of Long Beach (CSULB).We are also a part of the
        <a href="http://www.sca-caid.org/" target="_blank">
         Kingdom of Caid
        </a>
        , one of nineteen kingdoms or major geographical units of the SCA. There are an estimated 35,000+ people involved in the SCA around the world.The Barony of Lyondemere hosts up to an average of 10-15 or more
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/" title="Meetings">
         meetings
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/arts-sciences/" title="Arts &amp; Sciences">
         workshops
        </a>
        (such as cooking, dancing, costuming, arts), and
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/martial-arts/" title="Martial Arts">
         fighter practices
        </a>
        a month, and between 3-5 major events a year.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/council-planning-meetings/" title="Council &amp; Planning Meetings">
         <b>
          Council Meetings
         </b>
        </a>
        are held once a month, during which we discuss business items.
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/arts-sciences/" title="Arts &amp; Sciences">
         <b>
          Arts and Sciences
         </b>
        </a>
        vary in topic from month to month, but are a mix of hands-on and lecture-type workshops, varying from beginner to advanced skill sets in different arts and sciences forms.
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/martial-arts/" title="Martial Arts">
         <strong>
          Fighter practice
         </strong>
        </a>
        (Wednesdays) encompasses both
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/martial-arts/" title="Martial Arts">
         <b>
          Armored Combat
         </b>
        </a>
        (sometimes called Heavy Weapons) and
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/martial-arts/" title="Martial Arts">
         <b>
          Rapier Fencing
         </b>
        </a>
        . Armored Combat is an unchoreographed martial art, recreating swordplay of the middle ages, using rattan swords, shields, and a variety of other secondary weapons. Rapier Fencing is medieval fencing in the round.
        <a href="http://www.lyondemere.org/meetings/martial-arts/" title="Martial Arts">
         <strong>
          Archery practices
         </strong>
        </a>
        (target archery) are held on Thursdays and Sundays.
       </p>
