          <div class="entry_widget_large entry_widget_left" id="asset-13122306">
           <span class="adv-photo-large">
            <img alt="turkey creek.jpg" class="lazy" data-original="http://media.al.com/living_impact/photo/13122306-large.jpg" src="https://blog.advance.net/static/common/img/blank.gif" style="display: block;"/>
            <span class="photo-data">
             <span class="caption">
              It's hot and Alabamians are flocking to their favorite local swimming hole to cool off, much in the way that our forebears did. Turkey Creek Nature Preserve north of Birmingham is just such a popular spot to beat the heat. (File photo)
             </span>
             <span class="byline">
             </span>
            </span>
           </span>
          </div>
          <p>
           Alabama is, by any stretch of the imagination, a water-loving state.
          </p>
          <p>
           There is an almost
           <a href="http://www.al.com/living/index.ssf/2013/07/alabamas_coolest_swimming_hole.html">
            limitless list of places
           </a>
           in the Yellowhammer State where people can take a dip in a river, creek, state park or beach.
          </p>
          <p>
           The spots themselves are the source of much regional pride, with their precise locations being passed down from generation to generation as a part of family lore.
          </p>
          <p>
           Some of the spots are off the beaten path, known by word of mouth and local reputation. Others are more well-known across the region through advertising and state funds.
          </p>
          <p>
           And as much as they are all different, they all share a few things in common. And fun, sheer old-fashioned fun, is the best characteristic they all share.
          </p>
          <p>
           So, as the mercury starts to rise we recommend you hit the road for the nearest water hole and take a dip. Well be right there with you.
          </p>
          <p>
           And as usual, please let us know if you have any other suggestions.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            <span style="font-size: 1em;">
             CHICKASABOGUE CREEK
            </span>
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Prichard
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           About: Chickasabogue Park is an 1,100-acre outdoor facility and wildlife refuge that provides a wide variety of outdoor activities in a natural setting while protecting the environment and preserving the diversity of plants and animals indigenous to the area.
          </p>
          <p>
           How to get there: From Mobile, go north on I-65 to exit 13. Follow signs to the park.
          </p>
          <p>
           Phone: 251-574-2267
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            LAKE SHELBY
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Gulf Shores
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           About: Lake Shelby is a roughly 750 acre, freshwater lake within spitting distance of the Gulf of Mexico. The lakes are fed by underground springs. The ponds of the are technically freshwater but there is enough salt in the water from the Gulf to support a number of saltwater fish,
          </p>
          <p>
           The lake is part of Gulf State Park, a 6,150 acre park that features two miles of sugar white sands on the Gulf of Mexico.
          </p>
          <p>
           How to get there: Gulf State Park and Shelby Lake are located on Alabama 181 in Gulf Shores, approximately 1 mile west of U.S. 59.
          </p>
          <p>
           Telephone: Toll-Free: 1-800-252-7275
          </p>
          <p>
           On the Web:
           <a href="http://www.alapark.com/GulfState/PicnicArea/">
            www.alapark.com/GulfState/PicnicArea/
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            CRYSTAL SPRINGS LAKE
            <br/>
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Wellington
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           About: Long popular with locals in and round Calhoun County, Crystal Springs Lake is a favorite spot to take a dip to beat the oppressive Alabama summer heat.
          </p>
          <p>
           The facility also features a volleyball court, caf and snack bar. It opens a 9 a.m. on Saturday and quickly gets full of folks looking to cool down.
          </p>
          <p>
           How to get there:  From Dothan, follow U.S. 431 south about 14 miles.
          </p>
          <p>
           Phone: 256-613-5608
          </p>
          <p>
           On the Web:
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            CHIMNEY ROCK ON LAKE MARTIN
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Dadeville
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           About: For years people have flocked to the local landmark known as Chimney Rock and its neighbor, Acapulco Rock. Its a popular meeting place for folks who know that the outcropping in the manmade lake is a great place to take a swim in the summertime.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            KINLOCK FALLS
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Lawrence County
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           About: A part of the Bankhead National Forest, Kinlock Falls is a natural waterfall that draws fans from all over. A large pool at the base of the falls is perfect, locals say, for jumping into the cool water.
          </p>
          <p>
           Phone: N/A
          </p>
          <p>
           On the Web:
           <a href="http://www.roamschool.com/Alabama/kinlock.html">
            www.roamschool.com/Alabama/kinlock.html
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            TURKEY CREEK NATURE PRESERVE
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Jefferson County
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           About:  During the early days of Alabama statehood, the preserve played a key role in the birth of industry in this area by the works of early entrepreneur and industrialist David Hanby. The Hanby family ran a grist mill and a small iron forge on the banks of Turkey Creek. David Hanby's forge made horseshoes for the Confederacy until he was killed by Union Soldiers on April 19th, 1865.
          </p>
          <p>
           How to get there: From Birmingham, take I-20 E to the Tallapoosa St (Exit No. 128). Keep right at the fork to merge onto Highway 79. Continue for approximately 11.9 miles and turn left onto Narrows Road (at intersection of Highway 75). Turn right onto Turkey Creek Road.
          </p>
          <p>
           Phone: 205-680-4116
          </p>
          <p>
           On the Web:
           <a href="http://www.bsc.edu/sec/ecoscape/turkeycreek.cfm">
            www.bsc.edu/sec/ecoscape/turkeycreek.cfm
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            CHEWACLA STATE PARK
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Auburn
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           About: Chewacla State Park boasts 696 scenic acres that are deceptively close to nearby I-85. In addition to swimming on the 26 acre lake, playgrounds, hiking trails and a modern campground with picnic areas. Cabins are also available.
          </p>
          <p>
           How to get there: Located four miles south of Auburn just off I-85 Exit 51.
          </p>
          <p>
           Phone:  334-887-5621
          </p>
          <p>
           On the Web:
           <a href="http://www.alapark.com/Chewacla/">
            www.alapark.com/Chewacla/
           </a>
          </p>
