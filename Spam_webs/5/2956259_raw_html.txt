              <p>
               <em>
                <strong>
                 DeMarco Murray is definitely one of the most important decisions to make for the Dallas Cowboys this offseason. Could they let him become a free agent?
                </strong>
               </em>
              </p>
              <hr/>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               DeMarco Murray had quite the season in 2014. He led the league in rushing with 1,845 yards (4.7 yards per carry), along with 13 touchdowns. Murray was a major part of Dallas offense that was
               <a href="http://www.nfl.com/stats/categorystats?tabSeq=2&amp;offensiveStatisticCategory=GAME_STATS&amp;conference=ALL&amp;role=TM&amp;season=2014&amp;seasonType=POST&amp;d-447263-s=TOTAL_YARDS_GAME_AVG&amp;d-447263-o=2&amp;d-447263-n=1" target="_blank">
                ranked
               </a>
               top 10 in the NFL.
              </p>
              <p>
               Thanks to his incredible season, Murray was named a Pro Bowler for the second consecutive season and was a first-team All-Pro for the first time in his four-year career.
              </p>
              <p>
               But still, the Dallas Cowboys will let him
               <a href="http://www.foxsports.com/southwest/story/dallas-cowboys-demarco-murray-dez-bryant-jerry-jones-franchise-tag-free-agency-022815" target="_blank">
                test the market
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               The Cowboys have one franchise tag to use by March 2 (next Monday) and vice president Stephen Jones
               <a href="http://www.foxsports.com/southwest/story/dallas-cowboys-dez-bryant-jerry-jones-franchise-tag-stephen-jones-022815" target="_blank">
                said
               </a>
               they will use that on wide receiver Dez Bryant, who had 1,320 receiving yards this season and 16 touchdowns, which led the NFL.
              </p>
              <p>
               While Jones doesnt want Murray to leave, the talks have slowed down at this point and wants him to see what else is out there before returning to negotiations on a long-term deal.
              </p>
              <blockquote>
               <p>
                [Murray] is probably going to, you know, get some peace and see whats out in the market and then well talk and see. I do believe deep down hed love to stay in Dallas.
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               While Murray has shown to be a risk in the past with his injury problems (11 combined missed games in his first three seasons), he missed no games last season despite injuring his hand. Murray is worth the investment and can become one of the leagues top backs for years to come behind the Cowboys offensive line should he stay there.
              </p>
              <p>
               While Murray is a better long-term investment, the Cowboys have been
               <a href="http://www.nfl.com/news/story/0ap3000000474780/article/adrian-peterson-linked-to-cowboys-colts-cardinals" target="_blank">
                linked
               </a>
               to Vikings running back Adrian Peterson, who isnt very happy with his current situation in Minnesota. If Murray does choose to walk, Dallas could always attempt to trade for Peterson should an opportunity become available.
              </p>
              <p>
               Jones isnt too worried about the possibility of Murray leaving.
              </p>
              <blockquote>
               <p>
                Weve had some really good football players go into free agency: Jay Novacek and Darren Woodson and, you know, they still sign with the Cowboys. So, just because youre a free agent doesnt mean youre not necessarily going to end up back in Dallas.
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               Murray helped Dallas to a 12-4 record and just their second playoff win since 1997.
              </p>
              <p>
               Teams and players can begin negotiating contracts on March 7, but will not be allowed to ink any deals until March 10. With Murray being just one of many good available free agents this summer, we will see how badly a team wants to steal him away from the Cowboys.
              </p>
