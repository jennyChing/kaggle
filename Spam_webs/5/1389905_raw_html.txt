     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Story">
       Story
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirai_Robo_Daltanious&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Story">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      It is the year 1995. Earth has been conquered by an alien army from the star cluster known as the Akron. The cities of Earth have been destroyed, and the remaining survivors live in harsh shanty towns and villages. Kento, a war orphan, hides within a cave along with his companions in order to escape some bandits.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the cave, they find the secret base of Doctor Earl, who was an inhabitant of the planet Helios, a planet conquered by the Akron. Doctor Earl then fled to Earth, bringing with him the greatest achievement in Helian technology: the super robot Atlaus, whose power is increased when combined with the intelligent lion robot, Beralios. Doctor Earl entrusts the fight for Earth to Kento, who happens to be a descendant from the long disappeared Helian royal line.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">
       Concept
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirai_Robo_Daltanious&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Concept">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Tadao_Nagahama" title="Tadao Nagahama">
       Tadao Nagahama
      </a>
      had a heavy influence in the artistic style and direction of the show. The style resembles that of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voltes_V" title="Voltes V">
       Voltes V
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combattler_V" title="Combattler V">
       Combattler V
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The main attraction
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_robot" title="Super robot">
       super robot
      </a>
      , Daltanious, was formed with 3 components: Atlaus (the main robot), Gunper (the space ship), and Beralios, the mechanical lion which was recovered after the first battle with Akron. The robot is named after the main hero of the
      <a href="/wiki/The_Three_Musketeers" title="The Three Musketeers">
       Three Musketeers
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%27Artagnan" title="D'Artagnan">
       D'Artagnan
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Daltanious is 56 meters tall and weighs 678 tons. Daltanious was the first combining super robot to have an animal component, as well as the first to have a lion's head on its chest; the lion's head would be incorporated into several
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brave_Series" title="Brave Series">
       Brave Series
      </a>
      robots, most notably
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GaoGaiGar" title="GaoGaiGar">
       GaoGaiGar
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <p>
      The Japanese
      <i>
       Mirai Robo Daltanious
      </i>
      series was originally planned to be adapted by
      <a href="/wiki/World_Events_Productions" title="World Events Productions">
       World Events Productions
      </a>
      as one act of the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voltron:_Defender_of_the_Universe" title="Voltron: Defender of the Universe">
        Voltron: Defender of the Universe
       </a>
      </i>
      series in the United States and abroad. The intention was for Voltron to have 3 series components made up of
      <i>
       Daltanious
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Dairugger XV
      </i>
      (the "Vehicle Voltron"), and
      <i>
       Albegas
      </i>
      ("Gladiator Voltron"). When requesting master tapes from
      <a href="/wiki/Toei_Animation" title="Toei Animation">
       Toei Animation
      </a>
      for translation purposes, the
      <a href="/wiki/World_Events_Productions" title="World Events Productions">
       World Events Productions
      </a>
      producers requested the "[The] ones with the lion." Mistakenly, Toei then proceeded to ship World Events copies of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Beast_King_GoLion" title="Beast King GoLion">
        Beast King GoLion
       </a>
      </i>
      , another "combining-robot"
      <i>
       anime
      </i>
      featuring lion-shaped fighters. However, the World Events producers greatly preferred the
      <i>
       GoLion
      </i>
      series over
      <i>
       Daltanious
      </i>
      , and the
      <i>
       GoLion
      </i>
      episodes went on to become the most popular portion of the original
      <i>
       Voltron
      </i>
      run.

       ]
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">
       Video games
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The series was introduced into
      <a href="/wiki/Banpresto" title="Banpresto">
       Banpresto
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Robot_Wars" title="Super Robot Wars">
        Super Robot Wars
       </a>
      </i>
      series as part of
      <i>
       Super Robot Wars Destiny'
      </i>
      s line-up in 2003. It was also featured in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Robot_Wars_GC" title="Super Robot Wars GC">
        Super Robot Wars GC
       </a>
      </i>
      in 2004 and
      <i>
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Super_Robot_Wars_XO&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Super Robot Wars XO (page does not exist)">
        Super Robot Wars XO
       </a>
      </i>
      in 2006.
