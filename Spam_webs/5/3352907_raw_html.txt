       <h1 class="entry-title">
        All You Need to Know About Windscreen Wipers
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.patchwayautoparts.co.uk/blog/all-you-need-to-know-about-windscreen-wipers/" rel="bookmark" title="9:56 am">
         <span class="entry-date">
          October 27, 2014
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.patchwayautoparts.co.uk/blog/author/admin/" title="View all posts by admin">
          admin
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         I am sure I am not the only one who takes windscreen wipers for granted. In spite of their immense importance, many car drivers only seem to appreciate them when they get faulty and fail to work effectively. Driving- especially in fogs, rains and dust- can be extremely dangerous when the wipers are faulty or dirty. To make sure that your wipers are always in great condition, I have given here simple fault detection, maintenance and replacement guide that will hopefully be of help to you.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.patchwayautoparts.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/wiper_blades.jpg">
          <img alt="wiper_blades" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-57" height="175" src="http://www.patchwayautoparts.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/wiper_blades.jpg" width="491"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Lifespan
        </h2>
        <p>
         The lifespan of windscreen wipers depends on many factors. The main ones are level of usage and weather conditions. Persistent rains, fog or dust force the wipers to work more. The more they wipe the windshield the faster they wear down.
         <br/>
         How Do You Know Its Time To Replace Your Windscreen Wipers?
         <br/>
         Knowing when wipers need replacement will go a long way in ensuring that your driving is safe in all weather conditions. Personally, any time I start seeing streaks on the window I know the wipers time is up and replacement is due. Another sign is formation of paste when the wipers are in use. Any time these two signs start showing, I immediately replace them. However, there other times when I just adjust the wipers instead of replacing them. Examples are when the blades start skipping across the shield, fail to remove visible debris or start banging on the trim of the windscreen.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Tips for Effective Maintenance
        </h2>
        <p>
         Wipers reduce in effectiveness the more they are used. To make them last longer, I only use them when there is real need for their use. I also try to prevent their overwork by removing any tough stains that might be difficult for the wipers to remove. These include dirt and bird droppings. For these two, I often use clean water while for snow and ice I use a scraper. When the weather is snowy I make sure the windshield wipers are lifted away from the car particularly at night. For day-to-day maintenance, I use a cloth dipped in mineral spirits or rubbing alcohol to wipe the edges of wipers blades.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Choosing a Good Wiper
        </h2>
        <p>
         When wipers expire and need replacement, I prefer the rubber ones to silicone wipers. Although the silicone ones often last longer than the rubber ones, they are less efficient and effective in cleaning. For wiper blades, I go for flat blades that were on the car when it was new.
        </p>
       </div>
