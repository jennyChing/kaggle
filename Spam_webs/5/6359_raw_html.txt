                <div class="header">
                 <h2 class="slide-title">
                  This Is Gonna Hurt
                 </h2>
                 <div class="dek">
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="image">
                 <img src="http://www.bicycling.com/sites/bicycling.com/files/styles/slideshow-desktop/public/slides/intervals_1_1_0.jpg?itok=I7faBmfL" width="739"/>
                 <div class="photo-credit">
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="below-slide-wrap">
                 <div class="pager">
                  1 / 6
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="text">
                 <br/>
                 Intervals. The very word triggers groans of dread from even the most training-obsessed cyclists. But these short, misery-inducing efforts offer a huge fitness return for a comparatively small time investment. Even 20- to 30-second micro-intervals have been shown to increase V02 max,
                 <a href="http://www.bicycling.com/training-nutrition/training-fitness/burn-more-fat" target="_blank">
                  burn fat
                 </a>
                 , and
                 <a href="http://www.bicycling.com/training-nutrition/chris-carmichael/do-tour-build-endurance" target="_blank">
                  improve endurance
                 </a>
                 . And they work fast. Just two weeks of
                 <a href="http://www.bicycling.com/training-nutrition/training-fitness/get-fast-now" target="_blank">
                  interval training
                 </a>
                 can enhance performance, says Paul Laursen, PhD, of the University of Queensland in Australia.
                 <p>
                 </p>
                 Choose one of the following intervals and add it to a ride no more than twice a week. Warm up with easy pedaling for at least 15 minutes. Cool down as needed.
                 <br/>
                </div>
