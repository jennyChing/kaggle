         <p>
          Although cannabis remains illegal on the federal level in the United States, some individual states have legalized cannabis for valid medical purposes (and two states, Washington and
          <a href="http://www.leafly.com/colorado">
           Colorado
          </a>
          , have legalized cannabis both medically and recreationally).
         </p>
         <p>
          Here's a list of which states authorize medical marijuana for valid, qualified patients. Each entry includes a link to the state's qualifying conditions, as well as a rundown of which medical conditions and symptoms must be verified by a physician in order to get authorized as a valid medical marijuana patient. Please note that some states allow physicians to approve other medical conditions not listed on a case-by-case basis, so check with your doctor.
         </p>

         <h2>
          Washington
         </h2>
         <p>
          Although Washington voters have passed Initiative 502, which will result in alegal recreational cannabis market, it will not debut until late spring/early summer 2014. Until that time, cannabis can still only be acquired by valid medical marijuana patients.
         </p>
         <p>
          Qualifying conditions include:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Cancer
          </li>
          <li>
           HIV/AIDS
          </li>
          <li>
           Multiple sclerosis
          </li>
          <li>
           Epilepsy or other seizure disorder
          </li>
          <li>
           Spasticity disorders
          </li>
          <li>
           Intractable pain
          </li>
          <li>
           Glaucoma
          </li>
          <li>
           Crohn's disease
          </li>
          <li>
           Hepatitis C
          </li>
          <li>
           Diseases, including anorexia, which result in nausea, vomiting, wasting, appetite loss, cramping, seizures, muscle spasms, or spasticity
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          For a complete list of qualifying conditions and guidelines, please refer to the
          <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=69.51a&amp;full=true#69.51A.005">
           Washington state legislature regarding medical cannabis
          </a>
          .
