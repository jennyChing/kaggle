           <p>
            It has been 17 years since the last true film in the
            <strong>
             <em>
              Alien
             </em>
            </strong>
            franchise (not counting 2004's
            <strong>
             <em>
              Alien vs. Predator
             </em>
            </strong>
            ), but a new game dropping Tuesday is offering a deeper dive into that universe than even the movies.
           </p>
           <p>
            The long-awaited
            <strong>
             <em>
              Alien: Isolation
             </em>
            </strong>
            is a horror survival game in which you play as Amanda Ripley, the daughter of Ellen Ripley, the character played by
            <strong>
             Sigourney Weaver
            </strong>
            in the classic horror/action films. In
            <em>
             Isolation
            </em>
            Amanda is an engineer who's determined to find theflight recorder from her mothers ship, 15 years after it disappeared.
           </p>
           <p>
            The massive game reportedly takes about 20 hours to play through, and gets some voiceover work from Weaver herself, whose likeness appears in some mini-game flashbacks within the game.
           </p>
           <p>
            Gaming site
            <a href="http://www.dealspwn.com/alien-isolation-ps4-gameplay-opening-scenes-182375">
             Dealspwn
            </a>
            shared a video of about 10 minutes of footage from the game (above), and it looks as beautiful, cinematic and dread-inducing as any Alien film.
           </p>
           <p>
            The reviews seem to agree that this is a scary as game. For example:
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             <span>
              The Escapist: "Alien: Isolation can be frustrating, but it's mostly terrifying in a near-perfect way. The Alien is scarier than it's been since Ridley Scott first showed it to the world, and the atmosphere is thick enough to cut."
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span>
              <a href="http://www.ign.com/articles/2014/10/03/alien-isolation-review">
               IGN
              </a>
              : "I cant stress this enough. From the DOS-based futuristic computers and their scan-lined CRT monitors to the fear-enhancing, violin-screeched orchestral score, Isolation clearly did its homework in the A/V department."
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span>
              <a href="http://kotaku.com/alien-isolation-the-kotaku-review-1641975245">
               Kotaku
              </a>
              : "Regardless of how it happens, rest assured that the alien will kill you. Often. That is what the bulk of
             </span>
             <em>
              Alien: Isolation
             </em>
             <span>
              consists of: Being mercilessly murdered, over and over again, by a horrifying, eight-foot-tall monster from outer space."
             </span>
            </p>
           </blockquote>
