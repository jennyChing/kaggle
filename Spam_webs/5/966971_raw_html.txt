         5 Valentines Day Alternatives To Fresh Cut Roses
        </h1>

        <img alt="Post image for 5 Valentines Day Alternatives To Fresh Cut Roses" class="post_image alignnone" height="387" src="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Heart-shaped-succulents.jpg" width="580"/>

        <p>
         When I think Valentines Day, I think a lot of things. I think pure true love, kindness, beauty and roses. Fresh cut roses.
         <span id="more-98586">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I love the beauty fresh cut flowers bring into a home, with their vibrant colors and magical scent. But it always upsets me how quickly they die. And while I may love displaying dried flowers in my home more than many, sometimes I just want something that will live a little longer! So for this Valentines Day, weve partnered with the plant-life experts at
         <a href="http://shopterrain.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://shopterrain.com', 'Terrain');" target="_blank">
          Terrain
         </a>
         to share 5 alternatives to fresh cut roses  each of which will live much, much longer!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Heart-shaped Jasmine
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Heart-shaped-jasmine.jpg">
          <img alt="Heart-shaped jasmine" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-98590" height="870" src="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Heart-shaped-jasmine.jpg" width="580"/>
         </a>
         Well isnt this beauty just perfect for Valentines Day? A fragrant bloom with petite white flowers, this jasmine has been specifically trained to grow in the unique shape of a heart! You can purchase one of these in a beautiful birch pot at
         <a href="http://www.shopterrain.com/valentines-day-blooming/potted-jasmine-heart-wreath/productOptionIDs/5f63e4f8-60b3-4ee7-9cbc-15481ea61cb2" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.shopterrain.com/valentines-day-blooming/potted-jasmine-heart-wreath/productOptionIDs/5f63e4f8-60b3-4ee7-9cbc-15481ea61cb2', 'Terrain');" target="_blank">
          Terrain
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Caretaker tip: Jasmine prefers bright light conditions with moist soil.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Stargazer Lily
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Stargazer-lily.jpg">
           <img alt="Stargazer lily" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-98593" height="870" src="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Stargazer-lily.jpg" width="580"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Another gorgeous plant with a beautiful fragrance, this lily has a bright pink bloom with white edging. You can transfer this to the garden in a few months  it will bloom mid to late summer, and grow and average of 3-4 feet tall!
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Caretaker tip: Lilies prefer bright light conditions with moist soil.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Gardenia Topiary
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Gardenia-topiary.jpg">
           <img alt="Gardenia topiary" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-98589" height="870" src="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Gardenia-topiary.jpg" width="580"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         This plant has an extremely fragrant bright white bloom, and a beautifully unique braided, tree-like shape.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Caretaker tip:
         </em>
         <em>
          Gardenias prefer bright light conditions with moist soil.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Heart-shaped Succulent
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Heart-shaped-succulents.jpg">
          <img alt="Heart-shaped succulents" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-98591" height="387" src="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Heart-shaped-succulents.jpg" width="580"/>
         </a>
         Terrain had
         <em>
          just
         </em>
         gotten this in when I arrived in their store the other morning  how unique a gift would this be? Multiple styles of mini succulents are configured into a heart shape  some of them have the potential to flower, too!
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Caretaker tip: Succulents prefer bright light conditions, but only need to be watered once a week.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. Potted Mini Rose
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Potted-mini-roses.jpg">
          <img alt="Potted mini roses" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-98592" height="870" src="http://blog.freepeople.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Potted-mini-roses.jpg" width="580"/>
         </a>
         Instead of giving fresh cut roses, how about potted mini roses?! This way, theyll last so much longer. With a mildly fragrant bloom and a potential to keep to about 12 inches tall, these guys are available at Terrains locations in red, pink, white, and yellow.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Caretaker tip: Roses prefer bright light conditions with moist soil.
         </em>
        </p>
