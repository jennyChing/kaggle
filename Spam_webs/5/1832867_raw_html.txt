     <h1>
      iPhone 5 Battery Replacement Program
     </h1>
     <div class="box" id="content">
      <div id="section">
       <div class="column first">
        <div class="sidebar">
         <div class="padtop30">
         </div>
         <img align="center" alt="" src="https://ssl.apple.com/support/assets/images/products/exchange_repair/iphone5.png" style="width:130px; display: block; margin: 0 auto;"/>
         <div class="padtop10">
         </div>
         <select id="country_nav" style="display: block; margin: 0 auto;">
          <option selected="selected">
           Please select a Country
          </option>
          <option value="/support/iphone5-battery/other_countries.html">
           Other countries
          </option>
         </select>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="column last">
        <div id="welcome">
         <p>
          Apple has determined that a very small percentage of iPhone 5 devices may suddenly experience shorter battery life or need to be charged more frequently. The affected iPhone 5 devices were sold between September 2012 and January 2013 and fall within a limited serial number range.
         </p>
         <p>
          If your iPhone 5 is experiencing these symptoms and meets the eligibility requirements noted below, Apple will replace your iPhone 5 battery, free of charge.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Eligibility
         </h2>
         <p>
          If your iPhone is in working order and exhibits the symptoms noted above, use the serial number checker below to see if it is eligible for this program.
         </p>
         <form id="ihc_form">
          <input id="ihc_textbox" type="text"/>
          <input id="ihc_submit" type="submit" value="Submit"/>
          <span style="font-size: 10px; height:27px; vertical-align:baseline; ">
           Finding your iPhone serial number is easy.
           <a href="http://support.apple.com/kb/HT4061" target="_blank">
            See how.
           </a>
          </span>
         </form>
         <div id="ihc_msgs">
          <div class="ihc_msg" id="ihc_E00">
           The iPhone 5 serial number you entered is eligible for this program. Please choose one of the service options below.
          </div>
          <div class="ihc_msg" id="ihc_E01">
           The serial number you entered is not eligible under this program because either:
           <br/>
           - It's not one of the serial numbers in the affected range
           <br/>
           - Our records show that your battery has already been replaced. If you paid for a battery replacement service,
           <a href="#cas,prkeys:133779,cid:SC0998,sid:99989">
            check with Apple
           </a>
           to see if you are eligible for a refund.
          </div>
          <div class="ihc_msg" id="ihc_E99">
           Were sorry, there was a problem processing your request. Please try again in a few minutes.
          </div>
          <div class="ihc_msg" id="ihc_FE01">
           Please enter your serial number.
          </div>
          <div class="ihc_msg" id="ihc_FE02">
           This is not a valid Apple Product serial number. Please re-enter the number one more time. If you still have problems,
           <a href="#cas,prkeys:PL133">
            contact us
           </a>
           .
          </div>
          <div class="ihc_msg" id="ihc_FE03">
           Were sorry, there was a problem processing your request. Please try again in a few minutes.
          </div>
          <br/>
          <h2>
           Replacement process
          </h2>
          <p>
           Choose one of the service options below to have your battery replaced. Your iPhone will be examined prior to any service to verify that it is eligible for this program and in working order.
          </p>
          <p>
           Please call your service provider to confirm that battery replacement service is available on the day you visit them.
          </p>
          <ul class="square">
           <li>
            Apple Authorized Service Provider - Find one
            <a href="/support/iphone5-battery/locate/">
             here
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            Apple Retail Store - Make an appointment
            <a href="/retail/geniusbar/">
             here
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            Apple Technical Support -
            <a href="#cas,prkeys:133779,cid:SC0105,sid:21085">
             Contact us
            </a>
            .
           </li>
          </ul>
          <br/>
          <p>
           To prepare your iPhone 5 for the battery replacement process, please follow the steps below:
           <br/>
          </p>
          <ul class="square">
           <li>
            <a href="http://support.apple.com/kb/HT5262" target="_blank">
             Back up your data
            </a>
            to iTunes or iCloud
           </li>
           <li>
            Turn off
            <a href="http://support.apple.com/kb/PH2702" target="_blank">
             Find my iPhone
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            Erase data and settings in Settings &gt; General &gt; Reset &gt; Erase all Content and Settings
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Note: If your iPhone 5 has any damage such as a cracked screen which impairs the replacement of the battery, that issue will need to be resolved prior to the battery replacement. In some cases, there may be a cost associated with the repair.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <h2>
           Additional Information
          </h2>
          <p>
           Apple may restrict or limit repair to the original country of purchase.
          </p>
          <p>
           If you believe your iPhone 5 was affected by this issue, and you paid to replace your battery, you can
           <a href="#cas,prkeys:133779,cid:SC0998,sid:99989">
            contact Apple about a refund
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           This worldwide Apple program doesn't extend the standard warranty coverage of the iPhone 5.
          </p>
          <p>
           The program covers affected iPhone 5 batteries for 3 years after the first retail sale of the unit.
          </p>
