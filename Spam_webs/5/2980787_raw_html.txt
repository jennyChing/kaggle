           <div class="entry" id="article-entry" itemprop="articleBody">
            <div class="updated-time">
             <p>
              Last Updated Sep 13, 2010 11:41 PM EDT
             </p>
            </div>
            You've probably heard of the
            <a href="http://www.freshman15.com/">
             <strong>
              Freshmen 15
             </strong>
            </a>
            , but
            <a href="http://i.bnet.com/blogs/4500189000_f96a17765f.jpg">
             <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-3058" height="350" src="http://i.bnet.com/blogs/4500189000_f96a17765f.jpg" style="border: 5px solid black; margin: 5px;" title="50 Weight Loss Tips for College Students" width="256"/>
            </a>
            lots of other college students
            <strong>
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weight_loss">
              gain weight
             </a>
            </strong>
            too.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            If you don't like the numbers flashing on your scale, here are 50
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.mayoclinic.com/health/weight-loss/MY00432">
              weight loss
             </a>
            </strong>
            tips:
            <br/>
            <ol>
             <li>
              Don't use a cafeteria tray - you can pile too much food on it. Only eat what you can carry to the table with your own two hands.
             </li>
             <li>
              Avoid the cafeteria's self-serve ice cream. A Dairy Queen self-serve ice cream cone is 280 calories.
             </li>
             <li>
              Avoid yogurt toppings. A medium scoop of yogurt is 113 calorie, but that doesn't include all the brownie chunks, sprinkles, gloppy fruit sauce and M&amp;M's that college students dump on it.
             </li>
             <li>
              Stop binge drinking. Five 8-ounce margaritas equal up to 2,500 calories. Five 12-ounce
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.suite101.com/content/college-students-binge-drinking-a46079">
                beers
               </a>
              </strong>
              equal up to 800 calories. Five shots of liquor add up to 1,000 calories.
             </li>
             <li>
              Don't play
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.bpong.com/">
                Beer Pong
               </a>
              </strong>
              ,
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hpi1aVZxucI">
                Flip Cup
               </a>
              </strong>
              and other drinking games -- you'll drink more than you think.
             </li>
             <li>
              Post this warning on your dorm mini fridge: To loose one pound of   weight, you must burn 3,500 calories more than you consume.
             </li>
             <li>
              Don't eat anything straight out of a bag or carton.
             </li>
             <li>
              Avoid dorm vending machines. You don't need a Kit Kat bar at 1 a.m.
             </li>
             <li>
              Visit
              <strong>
               <a href="http://rachelvsramen.blogspot.com/2010/08/cold-noodle-and-sesame-chicken-salad.html">
                Rachel &amp; Ramen
               </a>
              </strong>
              for healthy cooking tips from a student attending
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.wm.edu/">
                College of William &amp; Mary
               </a>
              </strong>
              .
             </li>
             <li>
              Start a
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.realsimple.com/health/nutrition-diet/healthy-eating/keep-food-journal-10000000965749/index.html">
                food journal
               </a>
              </strong>
              and write down everything you eat.
             </li>
             <li>
              Use an online
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.thecaloriecounter.com/">
                calorie counter
               </a>
              </strong>
              .
             </li>
             <li>
              Wear a
              <strong>
               <a href="http://walking.about.com/cs/measure/bb/bybpedometer.htm">
                pedometer
               </a>
              </strong>
              , which counts your footsteps.
             </li>
             <li>
              Air pop popcorn instead of using caloric microwave popcorn.
             </li>
             <li>
              Avoid mega, super and large sizes.
             </li>
             <li>
              Drink 100% fruit juices. Orange, grapefruit and pineapple juices are healthier than apple, grape or pear juice.
             </li>
             <li>
              Get enough sleep.
             </li>
             <li>
              Drink lots of water.
             </li>
             <li>
              Bored with water? Try flavored water, seltzer water or unsalted club soda.
             </li>
             <li>
              Never eat second helpings.
             </li>
             <li>
              Use a salad plate for your dinner plate.
             </li>
             <li>
              Eat breakfast. Eating in the morning makes you less hungry later and reduces the temptation to pork out at lunch.
             </li>
             <li>
              Eating breakfast will jump start your metabolism and give you more energy during the day.
             </li>
             <li>
              Avoid fast food. Duh.
             </li>
             <li>
              Only eat a serving size:
             </li>
             <li>
              A serving size of meat is about the size and thickness of a deck of cards.
             </li>
             <li>
              A serving size of cheese (one ounce) is about the size of four dice.
             </li>
             <li>
              One teaspoon of butter is about the size of your thumb tip.
             </li>
             <li>
              Get emotional support. Have your friends support you if you're trying to lose weight.
             </li>
             <li>
              Set realistic goals. It's better to aim to lose one or two pounds a week rather than decide you want to lost 30 pounds as soon as possible.
             </li>
             <li>
              Write down your food goals and how you hope to achieve them.
             </li>
             <li>
              Eat slower. You're more likely to stop eating when you're full if you eat slowly.
             </li>
             <li>
              Get off your butt. Sign up for an intramural squad.
             </li>
             <li>
              Visit the gym. College health centers are often gorgeous and they are usually FREE. Try to exercise for at least 30 minutes a day.
             </li>
             <li>
              Eat
              <strong>
               <a href="http://cspinet.org/nutritionpolicy/healthy_school_snacks.html">
                healthy snacks
               </a>
              </strong>
              like:
             </li>
             <li>
              Edamames - delicious soybeans you can heat up in microwave.
             </li>
             <li>
              Quarter cup of nuts.
             </li>
             <li>
              Raw veggies and hummus.
             </li>
             <li>
              Frozen grapes, frozen blueberries, frozen strawberries.
             </li>
             <li>
              Graham crackers.
             </li>
             <li>
              Pretzels.
             </li>
             <li>
              Peanut butter with apple slices.
             </li>
             <li>
              Frozen fruit bars with no sugar.
             </li>
             <li>
              Rice cakes made from whole grain rice.
             </li>
             <li>
              Forget weight-loss pills. They can be dangerous and the effects are short lived.
             </li>
             <li>
              Eat less pasta.
             </li>
             <li>
              Don't assume that you can eat more because you're drinking a diet soda.
             </li>
             <li>
              Ditto for sugar substitutes.
             </li>
             <li>
              Don't eat late at night.
             </li>
             <li>
              Set a time to weigh yourself each day or week and stick to that time.
             </li>
             <li>
              Don't hate yourself for messing up from time to time. It's going to happen.
             </li>
            </ol>

             <em>
              Lynn O'Shaughnessy is the author of

               The College Solution
              </a>
              and she also writes for
              <a href="http://www.thecollegesolutionblog.com/" target="_blank">
               TheCollegeSolutionBlog
              </a>
             </em>
             .
            </strong>
