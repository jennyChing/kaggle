       <h1>
        Next-generation Chevrolet Volt Gets Lighter, Goes Further
       </h1>
       <div id="articles">
        <div class="byline">
         By
         <a href="/users/aaront" title="View user profile.">
          Aaron Turpen
         </a>
         , Tue, November 25, 2014
        </div>
        <div class="all-attached-images">
         <div class="image-attach-body image-attach-node-5239" style="width: 251px;">
          <a href="/image/volt-plugged-in">
           <img alt="Volt plugged in" class="image image-news3 " height="251" src="http://www.futurecars.com/sites/default/files/images/2012Volt-pluggedinAOA-600x450.news3.jpg" title="Volt plugged in" width="251"/>
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          In brief:
         </strong>
         The next-generation Chevrolet Volt, to be introduced at the Detroit show in January, will have more range, fewer batteries.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Vehicle in the news
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Year, Model:
         </strong>
         Volt
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Manufacturer:
         </strong>
         Chevrolet
        </p>
        <h2>
         The word
        </h2>
        <p>
         General Motors has announced that the cell chemistry in the batteries used in the Voltec system has been revised and will provide more power per pound that the current-generation batteries do. Working with LG Chem, the new batteries have an increased storage capacity of 20 percent overall (by volume). This allows them to reduce battery cell count from 288 to 192 for the Volt while retaining its current storage capacity, resulting in a 30 pound weight reduction.
        </p>
        <p>
         Other improvements to the electronics and controls in the Voltec system, GM says, have also resulted in better range for the car. Although the company has not released official numbers for the next-gen Volt, they say that the electric range increases are "significant."
        </p>
        <h2>
         And so ...
        </h2>
        <p>
         The official estimates and more information about the next-generation Chevy Volt will be unveiled at the North American International Auto Show in Detroit this January.
        </p>
        <p>
         Photo credits:
         <a href="http://www.carnewscafe.com/2014/11/13/next-generation-chevy-volt-fewer-batteries-lighter-weight-extended-range/">
          CarNewsCafe.com
         </a>
        </p>
