         <div class="date-outer">
          <h2 class="date-header">
           <span>
            Monday, October 17, 2011
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="date-posts">
           <div class="post-outer">
            <div class="post hentry">
             <a name="2575857820455098166">
             </a>
             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://wishingpenny.blogspot.com/2011/10/sopapilla-cheesecake-recipe-yum.html">
               Sopapilla Cheesecake ~ Recipe ~ Yum!
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header-line-1">
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-5bmFSQFCsbw/TpyxX8pmHrI/AAAAAAAACfI/phfnPMFOjcM/s1600/cheesecake.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-5bmFSQFCsbw/TpyxX8pmHrI/AAAAAAAACfI/phfnPMFOjcM/s320/cheesecake.jpg" width="320"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              This is the picture that tormented me all week. It started with just a tinywhisper and ended up screaming at me until I sent the honey to stop off and grab some cream cheese on the way home from work.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              If you find yourself ( more often than you probably should ) at
              <a href="http://pinterest.com/jamaise_wilson/">
               Pinterest
              </a>
              , chances are you've seen this picture too. And, chances are it's tormented you as well.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The original source of this torment can be found at
              <a href="http://myellowumbrella.blogspot.com/2010/10/sopapilla-cheesecake.html">
               My Yellow Umbrella
              </a>
              - blame her, not me! :)))
              <br/>
              <br/>
              It's easy to wip up - just be careful, it's richer than Oprah. And if you're having a serious craving, be warned that it takes awhile to cool.
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-NZriFp1O0UQ/Tpy37rj3rRI/AAAAAAAACfY/XfnVC8CcD3I/s1600/yum.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-NZriFp1O0UQ/Tpy37rj3rRI/AAAAAAAACfY/XfnVC8CcD3I/s1600/yum.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <br/>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
              </div>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <span class="Apple-style-span" style="background-color: #f9f9f9; color: #555555; font-family: 'Droid Sans', 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 16px;">
               Ingredients:
               <br/>
               -3 cans pillsbury butter crescent rolls - they now make crescent sheets!
               <br/>
               -2 (8oz) packages cream cheese (softened)
               <br/>
               -1 cup sugar
               <br/>
               -1 teaspoon vanilla
               <br/>
               -1/4 cup butter (melted)
               <br/>
               -Cinnamon &amp; sugar
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Instructions:
               <br/>
               Unrolled and spread 1 &amp; 1/2 can crescent rolls on bottom of un-greased pan. Combine softened cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla ( food processor was great here). Spread mixture over crescent rolls. Unroll and spread remaining crescent rolls over mixture. Spread melted butter over the top and sprinkle with cinnamon and sugar. Bake at 350 degrees for 20-30 minutes.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span class="Apple-style-span" style="background-color: #f9f9f9; color: #555555; font-family: 'Droid Sans', 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 16px;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span class="Apple-style-span" style="background-color: #f9f9f9; color: #555555; font-family: 'Droid Sans', 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 16px;">
               I melted my butter to liquid, but I think it would work better if butter was softened to spreadable - not pourable.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
