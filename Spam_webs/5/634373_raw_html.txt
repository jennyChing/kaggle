     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        DeepStar Six
       </b>
      </i>
      is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_film" title="Science fiction film">
       science fiction
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">
       horror film
      </a>
      about the struggles of the crew of an underwater military outpost to defend their base against the attacks of a sea monster (possibly a giant
      <a href="/wiki/Eurypterid" title="Eurypterid">
       eurypterid
      </a>
      ). It was released in January 1989. The film's main actors and supporting players included
      <a href="/wiki/Greg_Evigan" title="Greg Evigan">
       Greg Evigan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Taurean_Blacque" title="Taurean Blacque">
       Taurean Blacque
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Everhard" title="Nancy Everhard">
       Nancy Everhard
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Cindy_Pickett" title="Cindy Pickett">
       Cindy Pickett
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Ferrer" title="Miguel Ferrer">
       Miguel Ferrer
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Matt_McCoy_(actor)" title="Matt McCoy (actor)">
       Matt McCoy
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=DeepStar_Six&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      DeepStar Six is a prototype deep-sea colony funded by the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">
       United States Navy
      </a>
      with a collection of 11 military and civilian crew members in the last week of their six month tour. The brainchild of Dr. Van Gelder, the base is allowed to conduct its experiment on underwater colonization provided that he also oversee the installation of a nuclear missile storage platform nearby. Nearing the last days of his deadline, Van Gelder's plans are threatened when geologist Burciaga (
      <a href="/wiki/Elya_Baskin" title="Elya Baskin">
       Elya Baskin
      </a>
      ) discovers a massive cavern system at the installation site. Van Gelder orders the submarine crew to use depth charges to collapse the cavern, to the dismay of Dr. Scarpelli (
      <a href="/wiki/Nia_Peeples" title="Nia Peeples">
       Nia Peeples
      </a>
      ), who wants to preserve the cavern to study the potentially primordial ecosystem she suspects lies inside.
     </p>
     <p>
      The detonation causes the site floor to partially collapse into a massive fissure. Submarine pilots Osborne (Ronn Carroll) and Hodges (
      <a href="/wiki/Thom_Bray" title="Thom Bray">
       Thom Bray
      </a>
      ) send an unmanned probe to explore, but lose contact and venture in after it. Upon finding the probe, they detect a large sonar contact moments before being attacked and killed by an unseen force. The aggressor attacks the Sea Track observation pod, crippling it and sending it teetering on the edge of the ravine. Joyce Collins (
      <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Everhard" title="Nancy Everhard">
       Nancy Everhard
      </a>
      ) is trapped in the pod with Burciaga, who succumbs to his injuries. Captain Laidlaw (
      <a href="/wiki/Taurean_Blacque" title="Taurean Blacque">
       Taurean Blacque
      </a>
      ) and submarine pilot McBride (
      <a href="/wiki/Greg_Evigan" title="Greg Evigan">
       Greg Evigan
      </a>
      )--who is also Collins'
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paramour" title="Paramour">
       paramour
      </a>
      --attempt a rescue. After barely escaping the mysterious sonar contact, they dock with the pod and rescue Collins, but the unstable hatch door closes on Laidlaw. Mortally wounded, he floods the compartment, forcing McBride and Collins to return to DeepStar Six without him.
     </p>
     <p>
      The survivors prepare to return to the surface after the Navy accepts their emergency situation, but the missile platform must be secured before they can leave it unattended. Without Laidlaw, fastidious facility technician Snyder (
      <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Ferrer" title="Miguel Ferrer">
       Miguel Ferrer
      </a>
      ) is forced to rely on preset computer protocol to secure the platform. When prompted by the computer to explain the reason, Snyder reports "aggression," due to the attacking creature. The computer interprets Snyder's definition of "aggression" as a military assault and instructs him to detonate the missiles remotely. Snyder complies and the ensuing detonation creates a shockwave that virtually destroys DeepStar Six and cripples the cooling system for the bases' nuclear reactor. With failed life support, they begin repairs to restore power and pressure for the decompression procedure.
     </p>
     <p>
      Engineer Jim Richardson (
      <a href="/wiki/Matt_McCoy_(actor)" title="Matt McCoy (actor)">
       Matt McCoy
      </a>
      ) ventures outside in a
      <a href="/wiki/JIM_suit" title="JIM suit">
       JIM suit
      </a>
      to repair external damage for the air support system, but his work lights attract the creature and it attacks him. The crew manage to retrieve his suit and haul him through the airlock, but the creature forces its way inside and bites through the armored suit, cleaving him in half and entering the dive center. The team retreats as the creature consumes the panic-stricken Scarpelli. Arming themselves with shotguns and shark-darts (harpoons with explosive carbon-dioxide cartridges), they move back inside the dive room to finish their work. They succeed, but the creature charges and Van Gelder accidentally backs into Snyder's harpoon and is killed by explosive gas expansion before they escape to the medlab. Snyder's mental state, already stressed from the claustrophobic environment, begins to unravel with guilt and the fear that the others blame him for everything that has transpired. After a hallucination of a vindictive Van Gelder, Snyder jumps into the escape pod and launches for the surface. Because he has not undergone decompression, the pressure changes from the ascent cause him to burst.
     </p>
     <p>
      McBride braves the flooded base and swims to the mini-sub with the intention of connecting it to the decompression chamber and converting it into an escape vessel. During his absence, the creature bursts into the lab and forces Dr. Norris (
      <a href="/wiki/Cindy_Pickett" title="Cindy Pickett">
       Cindy Pickett
      </a>
      ) to attack with an overcharged
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defibrillator" title="Defibrillator">
       defibrillator
      </a>
      . As Collins and McBride escape into the decompression chamber, Norris electrocutes herself and the creature. Once the decompression is complete, they flee the base in the sub minutes before the reactor explodes. The sub breaches the ocean surface and they inflate the emergency life raft. They are interrupted as the creature bursts from the water and attacks the submarine, trapping McBride inside. McBride opens the submarine's fuel tanks into the sea and ignites it with a flare gun. The submarine explodes, killing the creature in the process. Collins is distraught until McBride surfaces beside the raft, having dived under prior to the blast. They begin paddling across the ocean, waiting for a Navy rescue team to arrive.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=DeepStar_Six&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Producer Cunningham developed the idea in 1987 with the express purpose of being the first release on the slate of upcoming underwater action/sci-fi films.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FangoDeepstar_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FangoDeepstar-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Originally,
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_Harmon" title="Robert Harmon">
       Robert Harmon
      </a>
      was going to direct the film. However, when he left, Cunningham stepped in to direct the film with a budget of $8,000,000.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FangoDeepstar_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-FangoDeepstar-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The creature was initially designed by
      <a href="/wiki/Chris_Walas" title="Chris Walas">
       Chris Walas
      </a>
      , who then turned his production designs over to FX head
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Shostrom" title="Mark Shostrom">
       Mark Shostrom
      </a>
      . Shostrom made slight alterations and changed the creature's color scheme.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FangoDeepstar_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-FangoDeepstar-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Release">
       Release
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=DeepStar_Six&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       DeepStar Six
      </i>
      was poorly received by critics and viewers. It has a 23% score on Rotten Tomatoes (2.5 out of 10). The film was released by
      <a href="/wiki/TriStar_Pictures" title="TriStar Pictures">
       TriStar Pictures
      </a>
      in the United States on January 13, 1989. It opened on 1,117 screens and debuted in eighth place with a weekend total of $3,306,320. Its final box office total was $8,143,225.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       DeepStar Six
      </i>
      was the first release of several underwater-themed feature movies released during 1989, including
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Leviathan_(1989_film)" title="Leviathan (1989 film)">
        Leviathan
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Evil_Below" title="The Evil Below">
        The Evil Below
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Lords_of_the_Deep" title="Lords of the Deep">
        Lords of the Deep
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Rift_(1989_film)" title="The Rift (1989 film)">
        The Rift
       </a>
      </i>
      (
      <i>
       Endless Descent
      </i>
      ) and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Abyss" title="The Abyss">
        The Abyss
       </a>
      </i>
      . With the exception of
      <i>
       The Abyss
      </i>
      , none of these films were box office hits.
     </p>
