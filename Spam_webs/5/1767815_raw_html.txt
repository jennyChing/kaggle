             <p>
              <strong>
               Do you face this daily struggle?
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              The one where 24 hours to cross out everything on your to-do list is way too little?
             </p>
             <p>
              It is a tremendous energy drain. But its not your fault. You just want to please everybody.
             </p>
             <p>
              <span id="more-29504">
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Could you be trying to do too much?
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              You feel embattled, like an eternal fighter. You never thought youd face such formidable issues (
              <em>
               or enemies!
              </em>
              ) that make your life so difficult.
             </p>
             <p>
              Your jabs, uppercuts, and kicks are losing strength, and these enemies are strong.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               But you can defeat them. Trust me.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              Lets reveal how you can end your fight against your three worst time management issues. Just recognize them and execute your strategies against them.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Issue #1
              <span style="color: #ff6600;">
               :
              </span>
              You Sabotage Your Own Schedule
             </h2>
             <p>
              How many times have you missed an appointment with your doctor, lawyer or accountant?
              <em>
               I bet your answer is close to zero.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Generally, you respect other peoples
              <a href="http://addicted2success.com/life/8-time-wasters-that-hold-us-back-and-what-to-do-instead/">
               precious time
              </a>
              . So, why do you put yourself at the bottom of the list when your precious time is in question?
             </p>
             <h6>
              You sabotage your own schedule in the following ways:
             </h6>
             <ul>
              <li>
               You snooze five times through your alarm before getting up in the morning.
              </li>
              <li>
               On social media, you check your friends pictures instead of doing something that will help you reach your daily goals.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              How highly do you regard yourself?
              <em>
               <strong>
                <a href="http://addicted2success.com/success-advice/7-qualities-of-the-highly-successful/">
                 High achievers
                </a>
                have self-integrity.
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Make a daily effort to also consider the tasks that are concerning you.
              </strong>
              That doesnt mean you should ignore other peoples requests. Just put your one thing first and take care of the rest afterwards.
             </p>
             <p>
              Do not go forward with anything else until that personal stuff is complete. Dont turn yourself down!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               By failing to prepare, you are preparing to fail. 
               <strong>
                Benjamin Franklin
               </strong>
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Issue #2
              <span style="color: #ff6600;">
               :
              </span>
              You Treat Your Body Like A Punching Bag Or A Dustbin
             </h2>
             <p>
              During your
              <a href="http://addicted2success.com/success-advice/9-time-management-tips-to-maximize-productivity/">
               time management struggles
              </a>
              , do you treat your body like a temple or like a dump?
             </p>
             <p>
              If you want to get things done, you cant ignore your body. When you dont eat healthily or regularly, it doesnt have fuel to perform.
             </p>
             <p>
              When you sleep four hours or ignore your bodys symptoms, you are shutting down your required energy level to accomplish your daily tasks. Be honest with yourself and evaluate if you are treating your body with love and respect.
             </p>
             <h6>
              Take a piece of paper and write an honest assessment of the following:
             </h6>
             <ul>
              <li>
               What you eat and how frequently you eat
              </li>
              <li>
               The number of hours you sleep
              </li>
              <li>
               Your physical concerns (pain, injuries) that you have not taken care of yet
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Then, write a plan to fix the problems you listed. And most importantly, respect your plan!
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Issue #3
              <span style="color: #ff6600;">
               :
              </span>
              You Give People Unrestricted Access to Yourself
             </h2>
             <p>
              Nowadays, a constant and insane pressure is on us to always be accessible. You are afraid that you might miss something if you dont answer the phone now. You are scared that your client will get mad at you for not replying to his email within two hours. But by giving people unrestricted access to you, they take control of your time.
             </p>
             <p>
              Remember those days when we didnt have text messaging, the Internet, or voice mail? People would wait and try again later back then.
             </p>
             <p>
              Each time you answer a call, an email, etc., you stop your work, you break the flow of your creativity and you kill your focus.
              <strong>
               <em>
                Make people follow your rules.
               </em>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              Pat Hiban, a serial and successful entrepreneur, asks his employees to pay him $75 every time they want access to him when hes not available. Lee Milteer, a mindset coach, is hardly reachable. All her calls and emails go through her assistant first to evaluate the urgency of each request.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               The more accessible you are, the less people will value your time. Period.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="drucker" class="lazy aligncenter wp-image-29506 size-full" data-original="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/drucker.png" height="380" src="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/plugins/jquery-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" width="650"/>
             </p>
             <noscript>
              <img alt="drucker" class="aligncenter wp-image-29506 size-full" height="380" src="http://addicted2success.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/drucker.png" width="650"/>
             </noscript>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Forget cell phones were ever invented, and shut down all distractions.
              <em>
               Its Time To Knock Out Your Time Management Issues!
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Life is short, but that doesnt mean you should treat your body like crap and be eternally accessible in the pursuit of success, productivity or time management.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Relax!
              </strong>
              Take some time to think and reevaluate your priorities and your schedule. Be honest with yourself, dare to change your bad habits and get rid of those three issues.
             </p>
             <p>
              Sure, those changes might take some time. And you will face resistance. But keep fighting it, and you will hear the bell ring. You will win the fight!
             </p>
             <h6>
              So tell me, what will you do about your time management enemies in the next couple of days?
             </h6>
