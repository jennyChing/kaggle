      <td height="3" width="476">
       <p align="center">
        <font face="Arial" size="5">
         Vegetative
    Propagation of Perennials
        </font>
       </p>
      </td>
     </tr>
    </table>
   </center>
  </div>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Achillea
    </strong>
    : Divide clumps every 3-5 years;
spring. Take cuttings in early summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Artemesia
    </strong>
    : Divide every 2-3 years; early
spring as new growth is emerging. Decline after 5 years. Stem cuttings in early summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Astilbe
    </strong>
    : Divide clumps every 3-4 years;
spring. 2-3 eyes per division.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Babysbreath
    </strong>
    : Difficult to divide because
of taproot. Cuttings in early summer; treat with a rooting chemical and put under a mist.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Ballonflower
    </strong>
    : Flesh root can be diivided
in spring, but with difficulty; doesn't require frequent moving. Cuttings treated with a
rooting chemical start easily under mist in early summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Bellflower
    </strong>
    : Divide every few years in
spring; 2-3 inch tip cuttings after flowering (
    <em>
     C. glomerata
    </em>
    ).
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Bergenia
    </strong>
    : Divide after 3-4 years of
flowering; spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Blanketflower
    </strong>
    : Divide in early spring;
every 2-3 years to prevent overcrowding. New growth that appears away from the old crown
can be dug and transplanted; root cuttings can also be used.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Bleeding Heart
    </strong>
    : Divide every 3-5 years;
early spring or early September. Fleshy roots are tender; handle with care. Cut into
sections with 2-3 or 3-5 eyes per division. Cuttings can be taken in early summer. Root
cuttings can be taken after flowering; should be three inches or more in length.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Boltonia
    </strong>
    : Divide in spring; tip cuttings
can be taken in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Brunnera
    </strong>
    : Divide in spring; can take 1 1/2
inch root cuttings in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Butterfly Flower
    </strong>
    : Difficult to divide; tap
root; tip cuttings in early summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Candytuff
    </strong>
    : Divide ib early spring;
cuttings taken in early summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Chrysanthemum
    </strong>
    : Take plantlets from base of
mother plant every year or two; spring before May 15th. Discard old plants; lose vigor
with age. Three inch cuttings can be taken in late spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Columbine
    </strong>
    : Carefully divide tender roots
every 3-5 years; late August- early September or early spring. Older clumps cab be divided
but the resulting plants are less vigorous and shorter lived than seedlings. Self sow in
moist soil; transplant seedlings in the spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Coral Bells
    </strong>
    : Divide natural sections every
4-5 years; spring. Stem cuttings in early June. Leaf cuttings, taken in mid summer, with
part of the stem attached, root easily.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Coreopsis
    </strong>
    : Divide clumps every 3-4 years
or when overcrowded; spring or fall. Basal softwood cuttings can be made in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Croccus
    </strong>
    : Dig and reset when overcrowded;
early October.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Cushion Spurge
    </strong>
    : Division in the spring
should be done carefully so there is minimum damage to the thick fleshy roots; tip
cuttings can be taken after flowering.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Daffodil
    </strong>
    : Dig and reset every 2-3 years to
prevent overcrowding; September.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Daylily
    </strong>
    : Divide when overcrowded; late
summer- early fall; at least 3 stems per division. Crown should be 1" below soil
level.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Delphinium
    </strong>
    : Divide in early spring;.plants
lose vigor after 2-3 years. Cuttings in early summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Echinacea (Purple Coneflower)
    </strong>
    : Divide in
early spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Erigeron (Fleabane)
    </strong>
    : Divide clumps in
spring; tip cuttings in the spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Eryngium (Sea-holly)
    </strong>
    : Can be divided but
not easy; tap root.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Feverfew
    </strong>
    : Divide regularily; spring.
Cuttings in spring or summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Flax
    </strong>
    : Divide small clumps in spring; large
clumps are hard to divide. Cuttings in mid summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Flowering Onion
    </strong>
    <strong>
     (Allium)
    </strong>
    :
Dig and reset bulbs when overcrowded; fall.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Gas Plant
    </strong>
    : Difficult to divide;
carrot-like tap root.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Geranium (Crane's Bill
    </strong>
    ): Divide every 3-5
years to renew vigor; spring best but can be done in September. Cuttings in summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Globe Thistle
    </strong>
    : Can be easily divided in
the spring, in spite of their carrot-like roots. Root cuttings in the spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Goat's Beard
    </strong>
    : Clumps may be divided in the
spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Golden Marguerite
    </strong>
    : Divide every 2-3 years
or when overcrowded; early spring. Cuttings can be taken in mid summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Goldenrod
    </strong>
    : Divide every 3-4 years; early
spring. Basal cuttings in fall; tip cuttings in spring. Self seeds; may become a nuisance.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Grape Hyacinth
    </strong>
    : Dig and reset when
overcrowded; late September- early October.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Heliopsis
    </strong>
    : Divide every 2-3 years; spring.
Stem or basal cuttings in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Hollyhock Mallow
    </strong>
    : Divide in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Hosta
    </strong>
    : Divide every 5-8 years; just after
emergence. 2-3 eyes per division; eyes just below soil surface.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Hyacinth
    </strong>
    : Dig and reset every 2-3 years or
when overcrowded; late September.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Bearded Iris
    </strong>
    : Divide every 2-3 years; late
July- August. Use rhizome segment with leaf attached.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Siberian and
    </strong>
    <strong>
     Spuria Iris
    </strong>
    :
Divide rhizomes every 5-10 years; don't allow to dry out when transplanting. August
(Siberian) and September or early spring (Spuria).
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Jacob's Ladder
    </strong>
    : Divide in early spring or
late summer; stem cuttings in mid summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Joe-pye Weed
    </strong>
    : Divide in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Lady's Mantle
    </strong>
    : Divide in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Liatris
    </strong>
    : Divide tuberous roots in early
spring; every 3-4 years.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Lilies
    </strong>
    : Reset hardy bulbs every 3-4
years; late August.
    <strong>
    </strong>
    Small bulbs, bulbils and scales will flower in 2-3
years after planting.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Lungwort
    </strong>
    : Divide roots just after
flowering when overcrowded; mulch transplants for winter.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Lupines
    </strong>
    : Divide or take hardwood cuttings
in spring; don't transplant well.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Maltese Cross
    </strong>
    : Divide every 3-5 years;
early spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Marsh Marigold
    </strong>
    : Divide in early spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Meadow Rue
    </strong>
    : Divide in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Meadowsweet
    </strong>
    : Divide in spring; need
frequent division to prevent over crowding. Daughter plants easy to move.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Missouri Primrose
    </strong>
    : Divide when
overcrowded; spring. Prefers to be undisturbed.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Monarda
    </strong>
    : Divide every 3-5 years to
maintain vigor; spring just as new growth emerges. New shoot cuttings; spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Monkshood
    </strong>
    : Divide in spring; slow to
increase in size.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Mountain Bluet
    </strong>
    <strong>
     (Centaurea)
    </strong>
    :
Divide every 2-3 years; spring or fall.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Obedient Plant
    </strong>
    : Divide every 2-3 years;
early spring. Cuttings in late spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Oriental Poppies
    </strong>
    : Dislikes being moved.
Divide in late August as plants start to regrow; every 4-6 years. Mulch for 1st winter.
2" pencil diameter root cuttings can be taken; fall or spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Ostrich Fern
    </strong>
    : Divide large plants in
spring, before growth starts.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Painted Daisies
    </strong>
    : Divide every 2-3 years;
spring or late August. Stem cuttings; spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Pasque Flower
    </strong>
    : Seldom needs dividing.
Divide root; early spring. 2' small root pieces will produce plants.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Penstemon
    </strong>
    : Don't require frequent
division. Divide; early spring or fall. Stem cuttings from side shoots; mid summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Peony
    </strong>
    : Divide clumps in late August- early
September; 3-5 eyes per division. Top eye no more than 2" deep. If divided in spring,
may not bloom the same year.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Perennial Aster
    </strong>
    : Divide every two years in
spring; lift clump and replant the vigorous outside growth and discard older center
section. Tip cuttings in spring or early summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Phlox
    </strong>
    : Divide every 4-5 years to prevent
overcrowding; early spring before growth begins. 3-5 vigorous shoots with roots per
division. Stem cuttings; spring. 2" root cuttings; fall.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Ratibida (Prairie Coneflower)
    </strong>
    : Divide in
the spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Rudbeckia
    </strong>
    : Divide every 3-5 years; spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Russian Sage
    </strong>
    : Tip cuttings can be taken in
summer; maintain humidity with plastic tent.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Salvia (Sage)
    </strong>
    : Divide in the spring; slow
to recover because of the long stringy root system. Softwood cuttings work well.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Sea Lavender (Statice)
    </strong>
    : Can remain
undisturbed for long periods; divide in early spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Shasta Daisy
    </strong>
    : Divide every other year;
early spring. Stem cuttings; fall.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Showy Sedum
    </strong>
    : Divide clumps; early spring.
2-3" stem cuttings; early summer.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Sneezeweed
    </strong>
    : Divide every 2-3 years to
prevent over crowding; early spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Snowdrop Anemone
    </strong>
    : Infrequent division
needed. Divide roots in early spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Spiderwort
    </strong>
    : Divide every 2-3 years;
spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Tulips
    </strong>
    : Dig and reset bulbs every 2-3
years; late September-early October. Set small bulbs 3-4" deep; Larger bulbs
5-6" deep. Need at least 3 weeks to root; larger the bulb the better it will flower.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Veronica
    </strong>
    : Divide clumps after 2-4 years of
flowering; spring or early fall. Stem or basal cuttings; early summer
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Viola
    </strong>
    : Divide after 3-4 years of
flowering; early spring. Offsets can be taken in spring.
   </font>
  </p>
  <p>
   <font face="Arial" size="2">
    <strong>
     Virginia Bluebells:
    </strong>
    Divide tuberous roots
in early fall; never let dry out. Plant 1" below soil level.
   </font>
  </p>
