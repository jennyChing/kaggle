           <h2>
            <a href="http://www.terroirist.com/2014/09/daily-wine-news-dark-moments/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Daily Wine News: Dark Moments">
             Daily Wine News: Dark Moments
            </a>
           </h2>
           <h3>
            Posted by
            <span class="post_author">
             <a href="http://www.terroirist.com/author/david-white/" rel="author" title="Posts by David White">
              David White
             </a>
            </span>
            |  Posted in
            <span class="post_cat">
             <a href="http://www.terroirist.com/category/winenews/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Wine News">
              Wine News
             </a>
            </span>
            |  Posted on 09-25-2014
           </h3>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <div class="entry">
          <p>
           <img alt="215px-Merlot_Grape" class="alignright size-full wp-image-10040" height="161" src="http://www.terroirist.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/215px-Merlot_Grape.jpg" width="215"/>
           In some dark moments, I have fantasized about bombing the whole vineyard with chemicals or planting genetically modified supergrapes. So far I have resisted. But this years failure just about broke my heart. In his latest letter from Europe, Robert Camuto
           <a href="http://www.winespectator.com/blogs/show/id/50575">
            shares some lessons
           </a>
           he has learned trying to grow grapes and make wine.
          </p>
          <p>
           Americas 70 million millennials are a major focus for top wine executives, and according to the Press Democrats Bill Swindell, those executives
           <a href="http://www.pressdemocrat.com/home/2875200-181/wine-execs-concerned-about-growth">
            are worried about
           </a>
           competition from craft beer, cider, and specialty spirits.
          </p>
          <p>
           As the 2014 harvest in Champagne slowly draws to an end, many growers and houses are happy with this years crop, as a warm, dry September helps boost the grape quality. In Wine-Searcher,
           <a href="http://www.wine-searcher.com/m/2014/09/all-smiles-in-champagne-as-sun-shines-on-vintage">
            Caroline Henry shares this wonderful news
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           In Grape Collective, Jameson Fink
           <a href="https://grapecollective.com/articles/speakeasy-frank-morgan-drink-what-you-like">
            chats with
           </a>
           Frank Morgan of Drink What You Like. (Frank is my go-to source on all things Virginia wine, so if youre not familiar with his blog, check it out!)
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.snooth.com/great-wines/wine-drinking-songs/">
            In Snooth
           </a>
           , Claudia Angelillo lists five wine-tastic tunes.
          </p>
          <p>
           Alder Yarrow
           <a href="http://www.vinography.com/archives/2014/09/california_law_and_wine_ups_an.html">
            brings attention to
           </a>
           a really dumb law.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://palatepress.com/2014/09/wine/women-italian-wine-never-give/">
            In Palate Press
           </a>
           , Elisabetta Tosi profiles Lara Albertini, Claudia Donegaglia, and Valeria Carastro, three women of Italian wine who never give up.
          </p>
          <p>
           The grape could be aggressively astringent, but todays bottles are easier to approach, with vibrant, ripe fruit that does not sacrifice its dry, chewy character.
           <a href="http://www.bostonglobe.com/lifestyle/food-dining/2014/09/23/winemakers-tame-tannat-uruguay-irascible-grape/icD5mf69YvACSU2ysbt6WI/story.html">
            In the Boston Globe
           </a>
           , Ellen Bhang contends that Tannat is steaks best friend.
          </p>
         </div>
