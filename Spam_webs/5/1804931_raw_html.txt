               <p>
                <strong>
                 Pad Thai
                </strong>
                - This Pad Thai recipe is how you actually find it in Bangkok and comes from testing hundreds of different variations from food carts all over the city. Pad Thai is the ultimate street food. While "street food" may sound bad, food cart cooks are in such a competitive situation, with such limited space, ingredients and tools they need to specialize in a dish or two just to stay in business. The best of these cooks have cooked the same dish day-after-day, year-after-year, constantly perfecting it.
               </p>
               <p>
                Great Pad Thai is dry and light bodied, with a fresh, complex, balanced flavor. It should be reddish and brownish in color. Not bright red and oily like I've seen in the US. The ingredients listed below can be somewhat intimidating but many are optional. If you would like to make authentic Pad Thai, just like in Thailand, use all the ingredients.
               </p>
               <p>
                Pad Thai is another perfect vegetarian dish, just omit shrimp and substitute soy sauce for fish sauce. Add more tofu if you like.
               </p>
               <p>
                From street carts, you can also often find an older, more traditional version of
                <a href="pad-thai-street-food" title="Street food Pad Thai">
                 Pad Thai made with dried shrimp
                </a>
                .
               </p>

                      <div class="rec_tipsi">
                       <h3>
                        Tips and Techniques
                       </h3>
                       <ul>
                        <li>
                         By far, the trickiest part is the soaked noodles. Noodles should be somewhat flexible and solid, not completely expanded and soft. When in doubt, undersoak. You can always add more water in the pan, but you can't take it out.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         Shrimp can be substituted or omitted.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         In this recipe, pre-ground pepper, particularly pre-ground white pepper is better than fresh ground pepper. For kids, omit the ground dried chili pepper.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         Tamarind adds some flavor and acidity, but you can substitute white vinegar.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         The type of super firm tofu or pressed called for this recipe can be found at most oriental groceries in a plastic bag, not in water. Some might be brown from soy sauce, but some white ones are also available. Pick whatever you like.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         If you decide to include banana flower, cut lengthwise into sections (like orange sections). Rub any open cut with lime or lemon juice to prevent it from turning dark.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         The original
                         <strong>
                          Pad Thai
                         </strong>
                         recipe calls for crushed roasted peanuts.Thailand is hot and humid and storage conditions are often sub-optimal, so a certain fungus can grow on peanuts. This fungus is linked to cancer, so many people in Thailand avoid eating peanuts.
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         One of the big challenges with Pad Thai's measurements is that the flavor densities and characteristics of the 3 core flavor ingredients: fish sauce, tamarind and lime juice vary greatly from brand to brand and purchase to purchase. Plus the salt content of your fish sauce, dried shrimp and preserved turnips will likely differ from ours. You will need to taste this as you're making it and keep the 3 flavors, salty, sweet and sour, in balance to your liking.
                        </li>
                       </ul>

                   <div class="unboldedLinks method">
                    <h3 class="methodTitle">
                     Method
                    </h3>
                    <h3>
                     Prepping
                    </h3>
                    <p>
                     Start with soaking the dry
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     in lukewarm or room temperature water while preparing the other ingredients. Getting the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     just right is the trickiest part of making
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/recipe/pad-thai" target="_blank">
                      Pad Thai
                     </a>
                     . Make sure that the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     are submerged in plenty of water. Check out Tips and Substitutions for in depth explanations. By the time you are ready to put ingredients in the pan, the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     should be flexible but not mushy. Julienne
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/tofu" target="_blank">
                      tofu
                     </a>
                     and cut into 1 inch long matchsticks. When cut, the super
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/firm-tofu" target="_blank">
                      firm tofu
                     </a>
                     /
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/pressed-tofu" target="_blank">
                      pressed tofu
                     </a>
                     should have a mozzarella cheese consistency. You can fry the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/tofu" target="_blank">
                      tofu
                     </a>
                     separately until golden brown and hard, or you can fry with other ingredients below.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Cut the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/chinese-chives" target="_blank">
                      Chinese chives
                     </a>
                     into 1 inch long pieces. Set aside a few fresh chives for a garnish. Rinse the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/bean-sprouts" target="_blank">
                      bean sprouts
                     </a>
                     and save half for serving fresh. Mince shallot and
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/garlic" target="_blank">
                      garlic
                     </a>
                     together.
                    </p>
                    <h3>
                     Cooking
                    </h3>
                    <p>
                     Use a
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     . If you do not have a
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     , any big
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b0009w38qw/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b0009w38qw&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      pot
                     </a>
                     will do. Heat it up on high heat and pour oil in the
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     . Fry the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/peanuts" target="_blank">
                      peanuts
                     </a>
                     until toasted and remove them from the
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     . The
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/peanuts" target="_blank">
                      peanuts
                     </a>
                     can be toasted in the pan without oil as well. Add shallot,
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/preserved-turnip" target="_blank">
                      preserved turnip
                     </a>
                     ,
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/garlic" target="_blank">
                      garlic
                     </a>
                     and
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/tofu" target="_blank">
                      tofu
                     </a>
                     and stir them until they start to brown. The
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     should be flexible but not expanded at this point.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Drain the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     and add to the
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     . Stir quickly to keep things from sticking. Add
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/tamarind" target="_blank">
                      tamarind
                     </a>
                     , sugar,
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/fish-sauce" target="_blank">
                      fish sauce
                     </a>
                     and
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/chili-pepper" target="_blank">
                      chili pepper
                     </a>
                     . Stir. The heat should remain high. If your
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     is not hot enough, you will see a lot of juice in the
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     at this point. Turn up the heat, if it is the case.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Make room for the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/egg" target="_blank">
                      egg
                     </a>
                     by pushing all
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     to the side of the
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     . Crack the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/egg" target="_blank">
                      egg
                     </a>
                     onto the
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/b000nd3gqy/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=utf8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeasin=b000nd3gqy&amp;linkcode=as2&amp;tag=ttlinks-20" target="_blank">
                      wok
                     </a>
                     and scramble it until it is almost all cooked. Fold the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/egg" target="_blank">
                      egg
                     </a>
                     into the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     . The
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     should soft and chewy. Pull a strand out and taste. If the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     are too hard (not cooked), add a little bit of water. When you get the right taste, add
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/shrimp" target="_blank">
                      shrimp
                     </a>
                     and stir. Sprinkle white pepper around. Add
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/bean-sprouts" target="_blank">
                      bean sprouts
                     </a>
                     and chives. Stir a few more times. The
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/noodles" target="_blank">
                      noodles
                     </a>
                     should be soft, dry and very tangled.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Pour onto the serving plate and sprinkle with
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/ground-pepper" target="_blank">
                      ground pepper
                     </a>
                     and
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/peanuts" target="_blank">
                      peanuts
                     </a>
                     . Serve hot with the
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/banana-flower" target="_blank">
                      banana flower
                     </a>
                     slice, a wedge of
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/lime" target="_blank">
                      lime
                     </a>
                     on the side, raw
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/chinese-chives" target="_blank">
                      Chinese chives
                     </a>
                     and raw
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/bean-sprouts" target="_blank">
                      bean sprouts
                     </a>
                     on top.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     As always, in Thailand, condiments such as sugar,
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/chili-pepper" target="_blank">
                      chili pepper
                     </a>
                     , vinegar and
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/fish-sauce" target="_blank">
                      fish sauce
                     </a>
                     are available at your table for your personal taste. Some people add more
                     <a href="http://www.thaitable.com/thai/ingredient/chili-pepper" target="_blank">
                      chili pepper
                     </a>
                     or sugar at the table.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     See recipe for
                     <a href="/thai/recipe/pad-thai-street-food" title="pad thai with dried shrimp">
                      Pad Thai with dried shrimp
                     </a>
                     .
                    </p>
