                    <div class="post-header">

                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7674822249363473346" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--apxLvoNVEA/VSb1QDKQ0cI/AAAAAAAAArM/0y3emxpykKI/s1600/LIGHT%2BBULB.png.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--apxLvoNVEA/VSb1QDKQ0cI/AAAAAAAAArM/0y3emxpykKI/s1600/LIGHT%2BBULB.png.jpg" width="320"/>

                     Monday, April 13
                     <sup>
                      th
                     </sup>
                     , is Thomas Jeffersons
birthday! The American Founding Father, third United States President and
principal author of the Declaration of Independence was born in 1743, and died
coincidentally on July 4
                     <sup>
                      th
                     </sup>
                     , 1826.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      In honor of what would have been his 272
                      <sup>
                       nd
                      </sup>
                      birthday, here are 272 fun Thomas Jefferson facts!
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      Just kidding. Theres only three.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst">
                      1. Jefferson is believed to be the first
president to have a pet that lived in the White House. He was very fond of
birds, particularly mockingbirds. His
                      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dick_the_Mockingbird">
                       pet mockingbird Dick
                      </a>
                      often sat upon his shoulder as Jefferson would hum to him while working.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpMiddle">
                      <br/>
                      <a name="more">
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpMiddle">
                      2. He was known to love ice cream, and in
fact was a big reason why it is popular in the U.S. today. Jefferson is
credited with the first known recorded American ice cream recipe, which can be
found
                      <a href="http://www.monticello.org/site/research-and-collections/ice-cream">
                       right
here
                      </a>
                      !
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpLast">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpLast">
                      3. Jefferson was an early archaeologist. He
ordered
                      <a href="http://www.americanscientist.org/issues/pub/jeffersons-old-bones/1">
                       40-million-year-old
mastodon bones
                      </a>
                      to be sent to him at the White House, where he laid them out
in an attempt to build a skeleton in what is now known as the East Room. Talk
about an odd request.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal">
                      So, there you have it. Happy birthday, T-Jeff! Your legacy
will live on forever.
                     </div>
