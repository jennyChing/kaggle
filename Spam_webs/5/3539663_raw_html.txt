         Tomatoes in the RefrigeratorA Bad Idea
        </a>
       </h1>

       <p>
        <img align="right" alt="Tomatoes in the RefrigeratorA Bad Idea" height="170" hspace="5" src="http://www.tomatocasual.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/09/tomatoes_in_frig.thumbnail.jpg" title="Tomatoes in the RefrigeratorA Bad Idea" width="150"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        By Michelle Fabio
       </p>
       <p>
        Perhaps you just want to get a few more days out of those tomatoes so you put them in the refrigerator for storage.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Stop!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Take them out and set them on your counter (stacking them in bowls encourages bruising), stem side up and out of direct sunlight.
       </p>
       <p>
        Now theyre happy
       </p>
       <p>
        Why?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Refrigeration can cause what is called a chilling injury in a tomato because it just doesnt do well in temperatures below 45F (7C).
        </strong>
        <br/>
        <span id="more-168">
        </span>
        <br/>
        At that point, several bad things can happen from loss of flavor, color, composition, and increased likelihood of decay or spoilage microorganisms.
       </p>
       <p>
        Some say that already red, ripe tomatoes can be stored in the refrigerator without loss of flavor, so if you must have really cold tomatoes, use them as quickly as possibly as changes will kick in after just a day or so.
       </p>
       <p>
        On the other hand, tomatoes that arent yet ripe should never be put in the refrigerator as youre only stopping the ripening process.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         There is one big exception to the no-refrigerator rule, though. If youve cut a tomato but arent going to use all of it (some people tell me this could happen), wrap the remainder in plastic wrap and store it in the refrigeratorbut use it quickly because itll get mushy and flavorless in no time.
        </strong>
       </p>
