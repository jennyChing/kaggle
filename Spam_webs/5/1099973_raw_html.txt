        President Obama raised an additional
        <a href="https://econsultancy.com/blog/63672-seven-lessons-obama-s-digital-team-learned-from-a-b-testing-emails/" target='blank"'>
         $60 million for his campaign
        </a>
        using A/B testing on his landing page.
       </p>
       <p>
        Yes,
        <i>
         60 million dollars
        </i>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        This is why A/B testing is the most used method for improving conversion rates. Its why
        <a href="http://www.pardot.com/ideas/abcs-ab-testing/" target="_blank">
         Google ran more than seven thousand A/B tests in one year alone
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        A/B testing is what increases your conversion rate optimization (CRO).
       </p>
       <h3>
        What is A/B Testing?
       </h3>
       <p>
        A/B testing allows you to compare two different versions or variations of a page to see which one performs better. The tests help you determine which version of the page engages your reader most based on data  which increases your conversion rates.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres a visual that explains the idea.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="A.B testing explained" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11707 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/A.B-testing-explained.jpg" height="354" width="708"/>
       </p>
       <h3>
        Why Should You A/B Test?
       </h3>
       <p>
        A/B testing allows you to get the most bang for your advertising buck. While you need to spend money to get paid traffic (like with a PPC campaign), it doesnt cost much to run A/B tests. In fact, Instapage lets you run A/B tests on your landing pages
        <i>
         without any additional cost
        </i>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        However, paid traffic may not always get you conversions if your page isnt optimized. The right A/B tests will do the trick every time, helping you identify the elements your audience best responds to.
       </p>
       <p>
        Before you start picking out which elements to A/B test, you need to make sure you have the right foundation to begin your testing. To get you started, here are six crucial things to do before you run your first A/B test.
       </p>
       <h3>
        6 Things To Do Before You Start A/B Testing
       </h3>
       <h4>
        1. Imagine you have amnesia
       </h4>
       <p>
        Yes
        <em>
         amnesia.
        </em>
        In order to stop yourself from making any assumptions about your landing page, pretend you have amnesia. Start with a blank slate.
       </p>
       <p>
        You know better than to make assumptions about your audience, so why would you make assumptions about what elements they will respond to? If you want the most conversions, you want to be data-driven. Test everything  not just the elements you think your audience will focus on.
       </p>
       <h4>
        2. Set an end point
       </h4>
       <p>
        Marketers are in a hurry to do A/B tests, because the sooner they run a test, the sooner they get feedback, the sooner they can increase their conversions. Makes sense.
       </p>
       <p>
        However, to run a successful A/B test, it is important to establish an end date
        <em>
         before
        </em>
        you begin. Establish the duration of your tests beforehand to make sure youre getting accurate results.
       </p>
       <h4>
        3. Dont rely on best practices
       </h4>
       <p>
        Get inspiration from current trends and best practices, but dont just do what others have done before. Experiment! After all its called A/B
        <i>
         testing
        </i>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Just because Tim increased conversions for his ecommerce site by changing his button to red doesnt mean a red CTA is going to work for your real estate landing page. Test a button color yourself.
       </p>
       <p>
        The landing page world is still pretty new. Who knows? Maybe your next page will set the trend for the next big wave of them.
       </p>
       <h4>
        4. Dont split your focus
       </h4>
       <p>
        Test one element at a time. That way you dont split your focus, and your results are clear and easy to interpret.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you changed your CTA button size and your headline in one variation, how do you know which one increased conversions? You dont. Takethe time you need to create different variations to guarantee youre making the right changes.
       </p>
       <h4>
        5. Understand basic statistics
       </h4>
       <p>
        Think back to your college statistics class for a moment, and rejoice! Turns out it actually
        <em>
         is
        </em>
        useful for something. Do you remember the concept of statistical significance? No? Well, its a simple concept imperative to figuring out how reliable your A/B test is.
       </p>
       <p>
        While an A/B test will show you which variation won, statistical significance lets you know whether you can rely on that A/B test.
       </p>
       <p>
        To confirm statistical significance, you need to make sure your sample size wasnt too small. For example, if you saw a lift in conversions by testing a long/short form and your sample size was just 6 people, you cant guarantee those results.
       </p>
       <p>
        Even if the results showed that the short form won, it just means that 4 or 5 out of the 6 people preferred the short form. This is not enough data to generalize results. Dont change your form based on such little data. Test a bigger sample size.
       </p>
       <p>
        Theres no perfect number to test, because its different for everyone. If you really want to get crazy with the math, though, you can find your p-values and
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_significance">
         review your stats here.
        </a>
       </p>
       <h4>
        6. Define your success metric
       </h4>
       <p>
        Your success metric depends on the type of landing page youre testing. Is it more shares you want or more clickthroughs? More sales or more signups?
       </p>
       <p>
        You cant be successful without knowing what success actually looks like. You must establish this before you begin testing.
       </p>
       <h3>
        What elements to test for your next A/B test?
       </h3>
       <p>
        You can A/B test variants of each of your landing page elements. This gives you the best chance to increase conversions.
       </p>
       <p>
        Now that youve got a strong foundation to start your tests with, lets go through some case studies to give you ideas for testing the different elements of your landing page.
       </p>

       <p>
        <img alt="danish company" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11721 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/danish-company.jpg" height="345" width="708"/>

        A test on a bigger versus smaller button resulted in the bigger button getting 10.56% more conversions.

        Removing the signup form from the
        <a href="www.abtests.com/test/258001/landing-for-vendio " target='blank"'>
         Vendio landing page

        resulted in 60% increase in signups.

       <p>
        <img alt="vendio first" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11725 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/vendio-first.jpg" height="345" width="708"/>

       </p>
       <p>

        </a>
        .

        where conversions increased by 42%.

        <a href="http://www.conversionvoodoo.com/blog/2011/11/expedia-deletes-one-field-from-their-registration-process-increases-profit-12m/ " target='blank"'>
         Expedias landing page form

        This was the test.
       </p>

        <img alt="yobongo test" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11733 lazy" data-original="https://www.instapage.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/yobongo-test.jpg" height="345" width="708"/>

        <a href="visualwebsiteoptimizer.com/split-testing-blog/ecommerce-optimization-customer-reviews-increases-sales/ " target='blank"'>
