       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         A Photo Essay: After The Scottish Referendum
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-09-19T15:52:50+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          September 19, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            David Bennett
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/after-the-scottish-referendum/#comments">
           17 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/dreams-of-yesterday.jpg">
          <img alt="dreams-of-yesterday" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1921" data-wp-pid="1921" height="400" src="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/dreams-of-yesterday.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         As you can imagine, the Scottish referendum has been on our minds here in Edinburgh. Everyone has been talking about whether they would vote for Scotland to be an independent country  to break away from the UK.
        </p>
        <p>
         And now that it is over and the people have voted to stay within the UK, we can get back to thinking about work.
        </p>
        <p>
         I say that because in some ways it feels as though it was an historic time with an historic decision and then, nothing. Just a vote to go on as before.
        </p>
        <p>
         It feels in some ways like a non-decision.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some people are relieved, some are delighted, and some are disappointed. I dont think there are any sore losers. Well maybe a few.
        </p>
        <p>
         Everyone says there is a lot wrong in British politics. No one is sure how things will play out. Will Scotland get more powers? Will they be of substance? Will other regions of the UK ask for similar powers?
        </p>
        <p>
         What will the political landscape of the UK look like in ten years from now?
        </p>
        <p>
         So I thought I would give you a photographic glimpse into Edinburgh today, the day after the referendum.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yesterday I took this photo of the wire screen erected in the centre of Edinburgh on which people had written their hopes and dreams for a Scottish constitution.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/what-do-we-want.jpg">
          <img alt="what-do-we-want" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1925" data-wp-pid="1925" height="400" src="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/what-do-we-want.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         And today I took the photo that is at the top of this article. It seems symbolic that some of the hopes are now lying on the ground.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Outside The Scottish Parliament
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/belgian-tv.jpg">
          <img alt="belgian-tv" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1920" data-wp-pid="1920" height="400" src="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/belgian-tv.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         There are TV crews from all over world outside the Parliament today. This is a Belgian crew from Brussels. They told me that the issues were important to their viewers because of the not too dissimilar situation with Flemish and French speaking regions of Belgium.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/interview.jpg">
          <img alt="interview" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1922" data-wp-pid="1922" height="400" src="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/interview.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         And here is an interviewer from Al Jazeera TV interviewing a local man.
        </p>
        <p>
         I cannot help but wonder at the extent to which the cultural differences can be overcome in an interview like this.
        </p>
        <p>
         Are the worlds that the interviewer and the person being interviewed occupy, worlds apart or can they cut through that to share an understanding? I guess it is a matter of degree and that the answer is to some extent yes, and to some extent no.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/tva-interview.jpg">
          <img alt="tva-interview" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1928" data-wp-pid="1928" height="400" src="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/tva-interview.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Meanwhile, on the Mound in the centre of the city by the Scottish National Gallery, a crew from TVA  a French language television network from Canada  were interviewing French tourists.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Quebec referendum must make the Scottish experience especially relevant for them.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Talk Of Betrayal
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/talk-of-betrayal.jpg">
          <img alt="talk-of-betrayal" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1924" data-wp-pid="1924" height="400" src="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/talk-of-betrayal.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/talk-of-betrayal-closeup.jpg">
          <img alt="talk-of-betrayal-closeup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1923" data-wp-pid="1923" height="400" src="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/talk-of-betrayal-closeup.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         One of the things that I think is significant is how willing people are to talk about how they voted.
        </p>
        <p>
         It is quite unlike my experience of discussing politics in England, where people want to know they are among friends before they will say they are a Labour supporter or a Tory supporter.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have spoken to quite a few people and it feels very comfortable discussing similarities and differences.
        </p>
        <p>
         So this was the only protest I saw today. If you are not from Scotland you might think the men here are in National dress or fancy dress. Its a bit of both. Some people wear kilts all the time.
        </p>
        <p>
         But here there was a political statement being made. The talk of betrayal, however, was not against other Scots but against the bias in the media.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/haar-on-the-Jawbone-Walk-in-Edinburgh.jpg">
          <img alt="haar-on-the-Jawbone-Walk-in-Edinburgh" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1930" data-wp-pid="1930" height="667" src="https://more.flyingtwigs.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/haar-on-the-Jawbone-Walk-in-Edinburgh.jpg" width="1000"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ill finish with a photograph I took on Jawbone Walk on the Meadows  a park almost in the centre of Edinburgh  yesterday. The wet mist or
         <em>
          haar
         </em>
         as it is known, has been hanging around for a few days.
        </p>
        <p>
         I joked with my wife Tamara this morning that if the vote of the referendum had been YES, it would have been sunny today.
        </p>

           <p>
            David,
           </p>
           <p>
            Your article today reminds me of why I love the internet.  It is exciting to hear, first hand, from someone living in Scotland about news that might be distorted by the time it reaches TV in North America.  I love what you said about being able to discuss politics openly with people who have different views.  That has become quite difficult in the U.S.
           </p>
           <p>
            And we have had problems at voting booths in South Florida where they created long lines and huge hassles for some people in certain areas, possibly to stop them from voting, in a previous presidential election.
           </p>
           <p>
            As for the Al Jazeera reporter speaking to someone from a different culture, I would say that anyone who becomes a reporter out in the front of the news is not your average citizen of that culture.  Reporters have a view of life from the other side that many of us never really get.
           </p>
           <p>
            Thanks for sharing a wonderful perspective on a huge political event in Scotland.
            <br/>
            It seems to have been handled in a highly civilized way.
           </p>
           <p>
            Warmly,
           </p>

            Dr. Erica
