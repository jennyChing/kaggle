       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="description">
       <p>
        Twoyears ago, Jessica Schiller got the phone call that changed her life. Her father had suffered multiple heart attacks and needed emergency surgery. She was shell shockedher father had always been perfectly healthy and nothing like this had ever happened in her family.

         <h5>
          The Spark
         </h5>
         <p>
          Running was never something Jessica was particularly interested in but when a tragedy struck the Schillers, she decided it was time to make a change and hit the pavement.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li>
         <img class="meta-icon" src="https://qludn19a1ws1wby8q1l5qshc-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/us/wp-content/themes/edh-portal-2014/assets/img/sidebar-action.png"/>
         <h5>
          The Action
         </h5>
         <p>
          Jessica has always had a love of community service and with her new found love of running, she thought combining the two would be perfect.  That is when she decided to start raising funds for the charities that mattered most to her.
         </p>
        </li>
        <li>
         <img class="meta-icon" src="https://qludn19a1ws1wby8q1l5qshc-wpengine.netdna-ssl.com/us/wp-content/themes/edh-portal-2014/assets/img/sidebar-result.png"/>
         <h5>
          The Result
         </h5>
         <p>
          Having now run countless races with her family cheering her on, Jessica has not only been a great example of a healthy lifestyle, but she's raised money for Susan G. Komen, Ann &amp; Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital of Chicago and American Heart Association along the way.
         </p>
        </li>

     <div class="main-col">
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        <span id="more-934">
        </span>
        Although surgery helped get her father back on his feet, his doctors explained that there were several factors that led to his heart attack. First, it had a genetic componenthis father had also suffered from heart disease, and had ended up dying from complications of it. Secondly, regular exercise and healthy eating hadnt necessarily always been the familys number one priority, and the neglect over the years added up.
       </p>
       <p>
        A flurry of thoughts crossed Jessicas mind. Should she avoid the reality of the situation? After learning that heart disease is the number one killer of Americans, she realized that she had to do something. She
        <a href="https://biggest-loser-runwalk-chicago.everydayhero.com/us/jessica-schiller">
         joined the millions of people fundraising
        </a>
        around the world for causesand you can, too, by
        <a href="https://www.everydayhero.com/us/">
         signing up for everydayhero today
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Because genetics played a key role in her fathers heart attack, it was a wake up call for Jessica. She knew that she had to change her lifestyle to avoid going down the same path. When some friends signed up for a 5k, she agreed to join in rather than shrug off the event as she would have in the past. At first, it wasnt easyafter all, shed never been a runner. She struggled to get out of bed on those blustery Chicago mornings when her entire body felt sore and tired. Yet, the thought of seeing her father lying in that hospital bed kept her going.
       </p>
       <p>
        When she crossed the finish line at that first 5k, she was overcome with emotion. So many people had helped carry her through that run: her father, her family and her friends who had helped motivate her to train. Instead of feeling exhausted, she was exhilarated. Crossing that finish line wasnt the end of a raceit was just one step towards a new, healthier life.
       </p>
       <p>
        She decided to sign up for another race. This time, though, she wanted to run a race for a cause. Community service had always been one of her passions. Whether leading a donation drive at work for the Chicago Food Repository or finding volunteers for the local womens shelter, she was always busy finding a way to help others. Now, she had the opportunity to unite her lifelong love of service with her newfound interest in running.
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Why not combine my love for running with my love for community service? she thought.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        Before she knew it, Jessica was crossing finish lines at dozens of races around Chicago, keeping her heart healthy, while also contributing money to charities for everything from breast cancer awareness to feeding the hungry.
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         I raise money to give backnot just to charities, but to the running community, Jessica said. Running has given me so much, so I want to do my part to get others involved and share the love.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        She decided to sign up for The Biggest Loser Half Marathon. While registering, she had the option to
        <a href="https://biggest-loser-runwalk-chicago.everydayhero.com/us/jessica-schiller">
         select a charity
        </a>
        to fundraise for through everydayhero. For Jessica, it was an easy choice. It was her fathers heart attack that got her started down the road to health, so the American Heart Association was a perfect fit.
       </p>
       <p>
        Knowing that she is running for a cause she cares deeply about has made Jessicas training completely different this time around. It certainly has had its share of challenges. She has battled injuries and cold Chicago winters while working towards her goal of 13.1 miles. But, in just a few weeks, all of her hard work will pay off on race day. When she crosses the finish line, it wont just be another race medal to add to her collection. It will be for all of the people that the American Heart Association helped and the new lease on life that her commitment to healthy activity had given her.
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Running has become more than just a way to stay healthy for meits become a lifestyle, she said. Running has changed my life.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
