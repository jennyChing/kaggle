      <h1>
       10 Craziest Things You Can Buy in China
      </h1>
      <p class="meta">
       <span class="author">
        <a href="/authors/?id=7">
         Andrew Handley
        </a>
       </span>
       <time datetime="2013-02-19">
        February 19, 2013
       </time>
      </p>
      <style>
       .promote, .contributor {display:none;}
      </style>
      <div class="ga more" id="div-gpt-ad-1358325249632-6">
       <script type="text/javascript">
        var width = window.innerWidth || document.documentElement.clientWidth;

      <p>
       If you want to step outside your comfort zone, go skydiving. If you want to forget you ever had a comfort zone, buy a ticket to China. If you already live in China, youre two sentences ahead of me and just a few yuan away from owning some of the most bizarre items ever available on the legal free market. Here are ten of the craziest things you can buy in China. And if you know of anything that can top the items on this list, feel free to put it in the comments.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        10
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Live Crabs From Vending Machines
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-79" border="1" height="398" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-79.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2010 the Twin Lakes Crab Co., a Chinese crab supplier, decided that sometimes a grocery store just isnt convenient enough. So they built a vending machine that dispenses
       <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/japanrealtime/2010/10/26/video-hard-sell-for-china-vending-machine/">
        live crabs
       </a>
       to be installed in subway stations in the city of Nanjing. Picture a typical snack vending machine, then replace all the chips and cookies with aerated plastic boxes containing living, moving crabs and youll have a perfect image of what this looks like.
      </p>
      <p>
       The crabs are kept at a constant 5 degrees Celsius (41 F), which is cold enough to put them in a temporary stasis, but not so cold it kills them. They sell for the equivalent of about two US dollars, and the bottom row of the machine also offers bottles of ginger vinegara combo sort of like ketchup and french fries.
      </p>
      <div class="adman" style="left: 147px;">
       <p>
        <!-- LV_PERM_Article_middle -->
       </p>
       <div id="div-gpt-ad-1358325249632-5" style="height:280px;">
        <script type="text/javascript">
         googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1358325249632-5');
        </script>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        9
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Panda Tea
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-1-56" border="1" height="399" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-1-56.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Lets just go ahead and say it: Panda Tea is a drink made from panda poop, and its the single most expensive tea in the worldone dried kilo of the tea will run you about US $77,000. Why would anybody want to drink it? The theory is that pandas really only use about 30 percent of the bamboo they eat, eliminating the rest of the unprocessed bamboo in their
       <a href="http://www.weirdasianews.com/2012/02/03/panda-poo-worlds-expensive-tea/">
        fecal matter
       </a>
       . Its believed that, among other nutrients, bamboo contains antioxidants that can prevent cancer, so panda tea is marketed as an anti-cancer tonic and a weight loss aid.
      </p>
      <p>
       The facility is located in the Sichuan Province, and the owner of Panda Tea, Yanshi An, started his company with 11 tons of dung that he bought from a nearby panda sanctuary.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        8
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Food Prepared, Cooked, And Served By Robots
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-2-52" border="1" height="374" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-2-52.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       From noodles to spicy chicken, everything tastes better when its prepared by the cold, metallic dicing hooks of a robot. This isnt just one isolated example, eitherits three isolated examples, showing a growing trend in robotic food service.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2011, inventor Cui Runquan created
       <a href="http://www.geekosystem.com/meet-chef-cui/">
        Chef Cui
       </a>
       , a humanoid robot that prepares shaved noodles, a popular noodle variety in China in which the noodles are shaved by hand off a block of dough and then boiled. The robots are being mass produced and sold for the equivalent of US $2,000, and over 3,000 have already been sold.
      </p>
      <p>
       A fast food chain in Shanghai is also using robotsbut this time as the
       <a href="http://www.weirdasianews.com/2011/02/27/robot-chefs-gastronomic-nongalloping-gourmets/">
        actual chefs
       </a>
       . The obvious benefit is the efficiency: one robot can wash a dirty pot, combine the ingredients, cook the dish, and have the finished order on a plate in only three minutes. The downside? Yet one more integration that will make it easier for robots to kill
       <a href="http://listverse.com/2012/12/13/10-reasons-to-be-terrified-of-robots/">
        us all
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Finally, if cooking wasnt enough, you can also go to the Dalu Rebot Restaurant and have your food brought to you by a
       <a href="http://web.orange.co.uk/article/quirkies/Robots_wait_tables_at_new_restaurant">
        robot server
       </a>
       . The six robots follow a rotation that allows them to serve the restaurants twenty-one tables before returning to the kitchen to refill their trays.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        7
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Canned Air
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-3-53" border="1" height="425" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-3-53.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       If you want a breath of fresh air in China, its going to cost you about five yuan. Thats how much Chen Guangbaio is selling his new line of canned air for, which is literally a soda can filled with
       <a href="http://www.stuff.co.nz/world/asia/8236408/Canned-air-for-sale-in-China-as-smog-returns">
        air
       </a>
       . This product is part publicity stunt and part environmental statement on Chinese air pollution, which is now so bad that the haze is visible from space in some areas of the country (seriously).
      </p>
      <p>
       For the purest air, you can pick up a flavor called Pristine Tibet, and if youre feeling nostalgic you can buy Revolutionary Yahan or Post-Industrial Taiwan flavored air.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        6
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Traffic Jam Stand-Ins
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-4-44" border="1" height="402" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-4-44.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       For a country with 1.3 billion people, its not surprising that China sometimes has some pretty long traffic jamssometimes
       <a href="http://news.xinhuanet.com/english2010/china/2010-08/23/c_13457295.htm">
        lasting for weeks
       </a>
       . But a few entrepreneurs have turned lemons into the Chinese version of lemonade by offering a service that provides a person who will actually sit in your car
       <a href="http://www.theglobeandmail.com/globe-drive/green-driving/news-and-notes/beating-the-traffic-jam-blues/article568199/">
        for you
       </a>
       while its stuck in traffic.
      </p>
      <p>
       It works like this: If you find yourself stuck in a gridlock, you can call the service, tell them where you are, and wait for two men to arrive on a motorcycle. The stand-in will sit in your car, and the motorcycle driver will take you anywhere you need to go. The service is mostly offered around Wuhan in Central China, which typically has some of the worst traffic in the country.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div class="ga" id="div-gpt-ad-1366669464627-0" style="padding-top: 35px;">
       <script type="text/javascript">

       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        5
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Dwarf Tours
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Screen Shot 2013-02-19 At 6.07.38 Pm" border="1" height="329" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Screen-Shot-2013-02-19-at-6.07.38-PM.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Kingdom of the Little People is sort of like a cross between a theme park and the Shirelocated in the Yunnan Province, it encompasses 13,000 acres set aside to build a miniature world that will be populated exclusively by dwarfs and open for tours like a
       <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2010/03/04/world/asia/04dwarfs.html">
        safari
       </a>
       . Dont bother reading that sentence again; its even more offensive the second time.
      </p>
      <p>
       The brainchild of entrepreneur Chen Mingjing, the Kingdom of the Little People is still being built, but already has over 30 tiny cottages to house the dwarfs and a parody presentation of Swan Lake, which is now open to the public. Although a lot of people are offended by the idea, Chen claims that it will provide people with permanent job opportunities when they would otherwise struggle in a difficult economy, and many of the residents of the Kingdom are proud to be part of the community.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        4
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Obama Fried Chicken
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-5-37" border="1" height="336" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-5-37.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       From morally ambiguous dwarfism to vaguely racist copyright infringement, China truly has it all. In 2011, a businessman in Beijing opened Chinas first OFC
       <a href="http://www.eurweb.com/2011/09/what-tha-ofc-obama-fried-chicken-opens-in-china/">
        Obama Fried Chicken
       </a>
       . The restaurant uses a KFC-styled banner and logo complete with a cartoonishly grinning caricature of Barack Obama and sells fried chicken.
      </p>
      <p>
       Believe it or not, this isnt actually the first Obama Fried Chicken to grace the planetor the first time China has used Obamas image to sell chicken. The first OFC opened in
       <a href="http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/MTV-Cuts-Obama-Fried-Chicken-From-Rap-Video-70618747.html">
        Brooklyn
       </a>
       in 2009, but closed down a short time later. And in a completely unrelated stunt, the official KFC in China released
       <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5peC9wFu64w">
        this ad
       </a>
       in 2011 which features an actor who looks like Obama giving a speech before being crushed by a giant chicken sandwich. Theres some sort of message herebut were totally not spelling it out.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        3
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Live Turtle Key Rings
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-6-24" border="1" height="405" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-6-24.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Arguably one of the oddest things you can buy in China is a tiny live animal on a
       <a href="http://www.weirdasianews.com/2011/03/26/chinese-vendors-selling-small-animals-keyrings/">
        key ring
       </a>
       . The animalsusually a miniature Brazilian turtle or a kingfishare enclosed in a small bag or plastic bubble that is filled with a nutrient-rich liquid thats supposed to be able to feed the creature for up to three or four months.
      </p>
      <p>
       Of course, the sealed container raises a lot of questions about how the animal is supposed to get oxygen, and several animal welfare services in China have understandably raised a public outcry that call these trinkets a particularly severe form of animal abuse. One of the reasons people buy them is for good luck, but supposedly many people will buy one just to set the animal free.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        2
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        White People
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-7-15" border="1" height="402" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-7-15.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       At the risk of sidestepping premise for a second here, you cant actually buy white people in Chinabut you can
       <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2010/BUSINESS/06/29/china.rent.white.people/index.html">
        rent them
       </a>
       , which is close enough. Chinese businessmen will often rent white actors to stand beside them at important events and, well, thats about it.
      </p>
      <p>
       The idea is that Western businesses are successful (Chinas words, not mine), and so for a Chinese businessman to be seen with a guy who could be an overseas business partner is a symbol of status and prestige. Sometimes the actor just stands there, sometimes he gives a speech, and sometimes hell be given a small role to play, complete with fake business cards. One actor named Jonathan Zatkin was paid to give a speech for the opening of a jewelry store and describe how wonderful it was to work with the company for 10 years.
      </p>
      <p>
       According to Zatkin, The requirements for these jobs are simple. 1. Be white. 2. Do not speak any Chinese, or really speak at all unless asked. 3. Pretend like you just got off of an airplane yesterday.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a name="item-">
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="itemheading">
       <span class="itemnumber">
        1
       </span>
       <div class="itemtitle">
        Tea Picked By The Mouth Of A Virgin
       </div>
       <div class="itemmore">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="Url-8-9" border="1" height="391" hspace="4" src="http://listverse.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/url-8-9.jpg" vspace="4" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Heres a fun sentence: The Jiuhua tea plantation in the Henan Province hires virgins with C-cup breasts to pick tea by grabbing the leaves with their lips and then dropping them into a wicker basket nestled between their breasts.
      </p>
      <p>
       The women can not touch the leaves or the basket with their hands, and in addition to specifically requesting C-cup breasts, the plantation also requires that the women have no visible scars or
       <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-1378870/PG-Lips-Chinese-tea-plantation-seeks-virgins-pick-leaves-MOUTHS.html#axzz2KjEW5GVc">
        wounds
       </a>
       . According to the spokesperson for the company, this odd requirement comes from a legend about how the tea used to be picked by the mouths of fairies. With this method, the tea is supposed to be infused with the virility and purity of the virgins, which is then passed on to the person who drinks the tea.
      </p>
