     <p>
      <b>
       Adobe Photoshop
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Raster_graphics_editor" title="Raster graphics editor">
       raster graphics editor
      </a>
      developed and published by
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Systems" title="Adobe Systems">
       Adobe Systems
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">
       Windows
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/OS_X" title="OS X">
       OS X
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Photoshop was created in 1988 by Thomas and John Knoll. Since then, it has become the
      <i>
       de facto
      </i>
      industry standard in raster graphics editing, such that the word "photoshop" has become a verb as in "to photoshop an image," "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photoshopping" title="Photoshopping">
       photoshopping
      </a>
      ," and "
      <a href="/wiki/Photoshop_contest" title="Photoshop contest">
       photoshop contest
      </a>
      ," etc. It can edit and compose
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raster_image" title="Raster image">
       raster images
      </a>
      in multiple layers and supports
      <a href="/wiki/Mask_(computing)" title="Mask (computing)">
       masks
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Alpha_compositing" title="Alpha compositing">
       alpha compositing
      </a>
      and several
      <a href="/wiki/Color_model" title="Color model">
       color models
      </a>
      including
      <a href="/wiki/RGB_color_model" title="RGB color model">
       RGB
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/CMYK_color_model" title="CMYK color model">
       CMYK
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Lab_color_space" title="Lab color space">
       Lab color space
      </a>
      (with capital L),
      <a href="/wiki/Spot_color" title="Spot color">
       spot color
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Duotone" title="Duotone">
       duotone
      </a>
      . Photoshop has vast support for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graphic_file_format" title="Graphic file format">
       graphic file formats
      </a>
      but also uses its own
      <code>
       PSD
      </code>
      and
      <code>
       PSB
      </code>
      file formats which support all the aforementioned features. In addition to raster graphics, it has limited abilities to edit or render text,
      <a href="/wiki/Vector_graphics" title="Vector graphics">
       vector graphics
      </a>
      (especially through
      <a href="/wiki/Clipping_path" title="Clipping path">
       clipping path
      </a>
      ),
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3D_graphics" title="3D graphics">
       3D graphics
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Video" title="Video">
       video
      </a>
      . Photoshop's featureset can be expanded by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photoshop_plug-in" title="Photoshop plug-in">
       Photoshop plug-ins
      </a>
      , programs developed and distributed independently of Photoshop that can run inside it and offer new or enhanced features.
     </p>
     <p>
      Photoshop's naming scheme was initially based on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Version_number" title="Version number">
       version numbers
      </a>
      . However, in October 2004, following the introduction of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Creative_Suite" title="Creative Suite">
       Creative Suite
      </a>
      branding, each new version of Photoshop was designated with "CS" plus a number; e.g., the eighth major version of Photoshop was Photoshop CS and the ninth major version was Photoshop CS2. Photoshop CS3 through CS6 were also distributed in two different editions: Standard and Extended. In June 2013, with the introduction of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Creative_Cloud" title="Creative Cloud">
       Creative Cloud
      </a>
      branding, Photoshop's licensing scheme was changed to that of
      <a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_service" title="Software as a service">
       software as a service
      </a>
      and the "CS" suffixes were replaced with "CC". Historically, Photoshop was bundled with additional software such as
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_ImageReady" title="Adobe ImageReady">
       Adobe ImageReady
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Fireworks" title="Adobe Fireworks">
       Adobe Fireworks
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Bridge" title="Adobe Bridge">
       Adobe Bridge
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Device_Central" title="Adobe Device Central">
       Adobe Device Central
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adobe_Camera_RAW" title="Adobe Camera RAW">
       Adobe Camera RAW
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Alongside Photoshop, Adobe also develops and publishes
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photoshop_Elements" title="Photoshop Elements">
       Photoshop Elements
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Photoshop_Lightroom" title="Adobe Photoshop Lightroom">
       Photoshop Lightroom
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photoshop_Express" title="Photoshop Express">
       Photoshop Express
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photoshop_Touch" title="Photoshop Touch">
       Photoshop Touch
      </a>
      . Collectively, they are branded as "The Adobe Photoshop Family". It is currently a licensed software.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">
       Early history
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early history">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Photoshop was developed in 1987 by the American brothers Thomas and John Knoll, who sold the distribution license to Adobe Systems Incorporated in 1988.
      <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Knoll" title="Thomas Knoll">
       Thomas Knoll
      </a>
      , a PhD student at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">
       University of Michigan
      </a>
      , began writing a program on his
      <a href="/wiki/Macintosh_Plus" title="Macintosh Plus">
       Macintosh Plus
      </a>
      to display
      <a href="/wiki/Grayscale" title="Grayscale">
       grayscale
      </a>
      images on a monochrome display. This program, called Display, caught the attention of his brother
      <a href="/wiki/John_Knoll" title="John Knoll">
       John Knoll
      </a>
      , an
      <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic" title="Industrial Light &amp; Magic">
       Industrial Light &amp; Magic
      </a>
      employee, who recommended that Thomas turn it into a full-fledged image editing program. Thomas took a six-month break from his studies in 1988 to collaborate with his brother on the program. Thomas renamed the program ImagePro, but the name was already taken.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-history-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Later that year, Thomas renamed his program Photoshop and worked out a short-term deal with scanner manufacturer Barneyscan to distribute copies of the program with a
      <a href="/wiki/Image_scanner" title="Image scanner">
       slide scanner
      </a>
      ; a "total of about 200 copies of Photoshop were shipped" this way.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      During this time, John traveled to
      <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">
       Silicon Valley
      </a>
      and gave a demonstration of the program to engineers at
      <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">
       Apple
      </a>
      and Russell Brown, art director at
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Systems" title="Adobe Systems">
       Adobe
      </a>
      . Both showings were successful, and Adobe decided to purchase the license to distribute in September 1988.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-history-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      While John worked on
      <a href="/wiki/Plug-in_(computing)" title="Plug-in (computing)">
       plug-ins
      </a>
      in California, Thomas remained in
      <a href="/wiki/Ann_Arbor,_Michigan" title="Ann Arbor, Michigan">
       Ann Arbor
      </a>
      writing code.
      <i>
       Photoshop
      </i>
      1.0 was released on 19 February 1990 for Macintosh exclusively.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-About-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Barneyscan version included advanced color editing features that were stripped from the first Adobe shipped version. The handling of color slowly improved with each release from Adobe and Photoshop quickly became the industry standard in digital color editing. At the time Photoshop 1.0 was released, digital retouching on dedicated high end systems, such as the
      <a href="/wiki/Scailex_Corporation" title="Scailex Corporation">
       SciTex
      </a>
      , cost around $300 an hour for basic photo retouching.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="File_format">
       File format
       <span id="PSD">
       </span>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: File format">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Photoshop files have default file extension as
      <b>
       .PSD
      </b>
      , which stands for "Photoshop Document." A PSD file stores an image with support for most imaging options available in Photoshop. These include layers with
      <a href="/wiki/Mask_(computing)" title="Mask (computing)">
       masks
      </a>
      , transparency, text,
      <a href="/wiki/Alpha_compositing" title="Alpha compositing">
       alpha channels
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Spot_color" title="Spot color">
       spot colors
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Clipping_path" title="Clipping path">
       clipping paths
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Duotone" title="Duotone">
       duotone
      </a>
      settings. This is in contrast to many other file formats (e.g., .JPG or .GIF) that restrict content to provide streamlined, predictable functionality. A PSD file has a maximum height and width of 30,000 pixels, and a length limit of 2 Gigabytes.
     </p>
     <p>
      Photoshop files sometimes have the file extension
      <b>
       .PSB
      </b>
      , which stands for "Photoshop Big" (also known as "large document format"). A PSB file extends the PSD file format, increasing the maximum height and width to 300,000 pixels and the length limit to around 4
      <a href="/wiki/Exabyte" title="Exabyte">
       Exabytes
      </a>
      . The dimension limit was apparently chosen arbitrarily by Adobe, not based on computer arithmetic constraints (it is not close to a power of two, as is 30,000) but for ease of software testing. PSD and PSB formats are documented.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Because of Photoshop's popularity, PSD files are widely used and supported to some extent by most competing software. The .PSD file format can be exported to and from Adobe's other apps like
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Illustrator" title="Adobe Illustrator">
       Adobe Illustrator
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Premiere_Pro" title="Adobe Premiere Pro">
       Adobe Premiere Pro
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_After_Effects" title="Adobe After Effects">
       After Effects
      </a>
      , to make professional standard DVDs and provide
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-linear_editing" title="Non-linear editing">
       non-linear editing
      </a>
      and special effects services, such as backgrounds, textures, and so on, for television, film, and the web. Photoshop's primary strength is as a
      <a href="/wiki/Raster_graphics" title="Raster graphics">
       pixel-based
      </a>
      image editor, unlike
      <a href="/wiki/Vector_graphics" title="Vector graphics">
       vector-based
      </a>
      image editors. Photoshop also enables vector graphics editing through its Paths, Pen tools, Shape tools, Shape Layers, Type tools, Import command, and Smart Object functions. These tools and commands are convenient to combine pixel-based and vector-based images in one Photoshop document, because it may not be necessary to use more than one program. To create very complex vector graphics with numerous shapes and colors, it may be easier to use software that was created primarily for that purpose, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Illustrator" title="Adobe Illustrator">
       Adobe Illustrator
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/CorelDRAW" title="CorelDRAW">
       CorelDRAW
      </a>
      . Photoshop's non-destructive Smart Objects can also import complex vector shapes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Pen_tool">
       Pen tool
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pen tool">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Photoshop includes a few versions of the
      <i>
       pen
      </i>
      tool. The pen tool creates precise paths that can be manipulated using anchor points.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cs3userguide_25-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-cs3userguide-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <i>
       free form pen
      </i>
      tool allows the user to draw paths freehand, and with the
      <i>
       magnetic pen
      </i>
      tool, the drawn path attaches closely to outlines of objects in an image, which is useful for isolating them from a background.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Measuring_and_navigation">
       Measuring and navigation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Measuring and navigation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The

     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cropping">
       Cropping
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cropping">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h4>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       crop
      </i>
      tool can be used to select a particular area of an image and discard the portions outside the chosen section. This tool assists in creating a focus point on an image and excluding unnecessary or excess space.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Cropping allows enhancement of a photos composition while decreasing the file size. The "crop" tool is in the tools palette, which is located on the right side of the document. By placing the cursor over the image, the user can drag the cursor to the desired area. Once the Enter key is pressed, the area outside the rectangle will be cropped. The area outside the rectangle is the discarded data, which allows for the file size to be decreased.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The "crop" tool can alternatively be used to extend the canvas size by clicking and dragging outside the existing image borders.
     </p>

     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Marquee">
       Marquee
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Marquee">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h4>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       marquee
      </i>
      tool can make selections that are single row, single column, rectangular and elliptical.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cs3userguide_25-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-cs3userguide-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      An area that has been selected can be edited without affecting the rest of the image. This tool can also crop an image; it allows for better control. In contrast to the
      <i>
       crop
      </i>
      tool, the "marquee" tool allows for more adjustments to the selected area before cropping. The only
      <i>
       marquee
      </i>
      tool that does not allow cropping is the elliptical. Although the single row and column
      <i>
       marquee
      </i>
      tools allow for cropping, they are not ideal, because they only crop a line. The
      <i>
       rectangular marquee
      </i>
      tool is the preferred option. Once the tool has been selected, dragging the tool across the desired area will select it. The selected area will be outlined by dotted lines, referred to as "marching ants". These dotted lines are called "marching ants", because the dashes look like ants marching around the selected area. To set a specific size or ratio, the tool option bar provides these settings. Before making a selecting an area, the desired size or ratio must be set by adjusting the width and height. Any changes such as color, filters, location, etc. should be made before cropping. To crop the selection, the user must go to image tab and select crop.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>

        </span>

     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Lasso">
       Lasso
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Lasso">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h4>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       lasso
      </i>
      tool is similar to the "marquee" tool, however, the user can make a custom selection by drawing it freehand.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cs3userguide_25-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-cs3userguide-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There are three options for the "lasso" tool  regular, polygonal, and magnetic. The regular "lasso" tool allows the user to have drawing capabilities. Photoshop will complete the selection once the mouse button is released. The user may also complete the selection by connecting the end point to the starting point. The "marching ants" will indicate if a selection has been made. The "polygonal lasso" tool will draw only straight lines, which makes it an ideal choice for images with many straight lines. Unlike the regular "lasso" tool, the user must continually click around the image to outline the shape. To complete the selection, the user must connect the end point to the starting point just like the regular lasso tool. "Magnetic lasso" tool is considered the smart tool. It can do the same as the other two, but it can also detect the edges of an image once the user selects a starting point. It detects by examining the color pixels as the cursor move over the desired area. A pixel is the smallest element in an image. Closing the selection is the same as the other two, which should also should display the "marching ants" once the selection has been closed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       quick selection
      </i>
      tool selects areas based on edges, similarly to the
      <i>
       magnetic lasso
      </i>
      tool. The difference between this tool and the
      <i>
       lasso
      </i>
      tool is that there is no starting and ending point. Since there isnt a starting and ending point, the selected area can be added onto as much as possible without starting over. By dragging the cursor over the desired area, the
      <i>
       quick selection
      </i>
      tool detects the edges of the image. The "marching ants" allow the user to know what is currently being selected. Once the user is done, the selected area can be edited without affecting the rest of the image. One of the features that makes this tool especially user friendly is that the SHIFT key is not needed to add more to the selection; by default, extra mouse clicks will be added to the selection rather than creating a new selection.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Magic_Wand">
       Magic Wand
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Magic Wand">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>

     <p>
      The
      <i>
       magic wand
      </i>
      tool selects areas based on pixels of similar values. One click will select all neighboring pixels of similar value within a tolerance level set by the user. If the
      <i>
       eyedropper
      </i>
      tool is selected in the options bar, then the magic wand can determine the value needed to evaluate the pixels; this is based on the sample size setting in the
      <i>
       eyedropper
      </i>
      tool. This tool is inferior to the quick selection tool which works much the same but with much better results and more intuitive controls. The user must decide what settings to use or if the image is right for this tool.

       <a href="#cite_note-33">

     <h4>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Eraser">
       Eraser
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Eraser">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h4>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       Eraser
      </i>
      tool erases content based on the active layer. If the user is on the text layer, then any text across which the tool is dragged will be erased. The eraser will convert the pixels to transparent, unless the background layer is selected. The size and style of the eraser can be selected in the options bar. This tool is unique in that it can take the form of the paintbrush and pencil tools. In addition to the straight eraser tool, there are two more available options  background eraser and magic eraser. The
      <i>
       background eraser
      </i>
      deletes any part of the image that is on the edge of an object. This tool is often used to extract objects from the background. The
      <i>
       magic eraser
      </i>
      tool deletes based on similar colored pixels. It is very similar to the
      <i>
       magic wand
      </i>
      tool. This tool is ideal for deleting areas with the same color or tone that contrasts with the rest of the image.

       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>

Magazines use Photoshop and many of its tools in order to enhance the imagery and text in their publications. Many Health and Beauty Magazines employ in-house designers to retouch photos of models to enhance their beauty. They add finishing touches to the imagery by using tools that balance color and add drop shadows, among other edits.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="CS2">
       CS2
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: CS2">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Photoshop CS2, released in May 2005, expanded on its predecessor with a new set of tools and features.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It included an upgraded Spot Healing Brush, which is mainly used for handling common photographic problems such as blemishes, red-eye, noise, blurring and lens distortion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      One of the most significant inclusions in CS2 was the implementation of Smart Objects, which allows users to scale and transform images and vector illustrations without losing image quality, as well as create linked duplicates of embedded graphics so that a single edit updates across multiple iterations.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Adobe responded to feedback from the professional media industry by implementing non-destructive editing as well as the producing and modifying of 32-Bit High Dynamic Range (HDR) images, which are optimal for 3D rendering and advanced compositing. FireWire Previews could also be viewed on a monitor via a direct export feature.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Photoshop CS2 brought the Vanishing Point and Image Warping tools.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Vanishing Point makes tedious graphic and photo retouching endeavors much simpler by letting users clone, paint and transform image objects while maintaining visual perspective.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Image Warping makes it easy to digitally distort an image into a shape by choosing on-demand presets or by dragging control points.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The File Browser was upgraded to Adobe Bridge, which functioned as a hub for productivity, imagery and creativity, providing multi-view file browsing and smooth cross-product integration across Adobe Creative Suite 2 software.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Adobe Bridge also provided access to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adobe_Stock_Photos" title="Adobe Stock Photos">
       Adobe Stock Photos
      </a>
      , a new stock photography service that offered users one-stop shopping across five elite stock image providers to deliver high-quality, royalty-free images for layout and design.
     </p>
     <p>
      Camera Raw version 3.0 was a new addition in CS2, and it allowed settings for multiple raw files to be modified simultaneously.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In addition, processing multiple raw files to other formats including JPEG, TIFF, DNG or PSD, could be done in the background without executing Photoshop itself.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Photoshop CS2 brought a streamlined interface, making it easier to access features for specific instances. In CS2 users were also given the ability to create their own custom presets, which was meant to save time and increase productivity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2_45-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS2-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Creative_Suite" title="Adobe Creative Suite">

     <p>
      Photoshop CS5 was launched on 12 April 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54">
       <a href="#cite_note-54">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        54
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In a video posted on its official
      <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">
       Facebook
      </a>
      page, the development team revealed the new technologies under development, including three-dimensional brushes and warping tools.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55">
       <a href="#cite_note-55">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        55
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In May 2011, Adobe Creative Suite 5.5 (CS5.5) was released, with new versions of some of the applications. Its version of Photoshop, 12.1, is identical to the concurrently released update for Photoshop CS5, version 12.0.4, except for support for the new subscription pricing that was introduced with CS5.5.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56">
       <a href="#cite_note-56">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        56
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      CS5 introduces new tools such as the Content-Aware Fill, Refine Edge, Mixer Brush, Bristle Tips and Puppet Warp.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5_57-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The community also had a hand in the additions made to CS5 as 30 new features and improvements were included by request.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5_57-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      These include automatic image straightening, the Rule-of-Thirds cropping tool, color pickup, and saving a 16-bit image as a
      <a href="/wiki/JPEG" title="JPEG">
       JPEG
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5_57-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Another feature includes the Adobe Mini Bridge, which allows for efficient file browsing and management.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5_57-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5_57-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The new Shadow Catcher tool can be used to further enhance 3D objects.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5_57-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      For motion graphics, the tools can be applied to over more than one frame in a video sequence.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5_57-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

     <p>
      CS5 and CS5 Extended were made available through Adobe's online store, Adobe Authorized Resellers and Adobe direct sales.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5_57-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS5-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>

        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>

      <a href="/wiki/Windows_Vista" title="Windows Vista">
       Windows Vista

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="CS6">
       CS6
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Photoshop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: CS6">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]

     <p>
      Photoshop CS6, released in May 2012, added new creative design tools and provided a redesigned interface
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSCS6_59-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-PSCS6-59">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        59
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      with a focus on enhanced performance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6_Beta_60-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6_Beta-60">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        60
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      New features have been added to the Content-Aware tool such as the Content-Aware Patch and Content-Aware Move.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6_Beta_60-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6_Beta-60">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        60
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Adobe Photoshop CS6 brought a suite of tools for video editing. Color and exposure adjustments, as well as layers, are among a few things that are featured in this new editor. Upon completion of editing, the user is presented with a handful of options of exporting into a few popular formats.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6_61-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6-61">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        61
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      CS6 brings the "straighten" tool to Photoshop, where a user simply draws a line anywhere on an image, and the canvas will reorient itself so that the line drawn becomes horizontal, and adjusts the media accordingly. This was created with the intention that users will draw a line parallel to a plane in the image, and reorient the image to that plane to more easily achieve certain perspectives.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6_61-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6-61">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        61
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      CS6 allows background saving, which means that while another document is compiling and archiving itself, it is possible to simultaneously edit an image.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6_61-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6-61">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        61
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      CS6 also features a customizable auto-save feature, preventing any work from being lost.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6_61-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Adobe_Photoshop_CS6-61">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        61
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The price for CS6 is US$699 and the extended version is US$999. Students, however, even those who are homeschooled, can receive a significant discount on Photoshop.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62">
       <a href="#cite_note-62">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        62
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Windows_XP" title="Windows XP">
       Windows XP
      </a>
      (even on native x64 for
      <a href="/wiki/Windows_XP_Professional_x64_Edition" title="Windows XP Professional x64 Edition">
       Windows XP x64
      </a>
      ); thus, the last version that works on Windows XP is 13.0.1. Adobe also announced that it will quit selling perpetual licenses to new creative suite packages, but will continue to support Photoshop CS6 for
      <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">
       OS
      </a>
      compatibility and will provide bug fixes and security updates as necessary.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63">
       <a href="#cite_note-63">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        63
        <span>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="CC">
       CC
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

       </span>

     <p>
      Photoshop CC (14.0) was launched on 18 June 2013. As the next major version after CS6, it is only available as part of a Creative Cloud subscription, the full version of which costs $49 every month. Major features in this version include All-new Smart Sharpen, Intelligent Upsampling, and Camera Shake Reduction for reducing blur caused by camera shake. Editable Rounded Rectangles and an update to Adobe Camera Raw (8.0) were also included.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64">
       <a href="#cite_note-64">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        64
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Since the initial launch, Adobe has released two additional feature-bearing updates. The first, version 14.1, was launched on 9 September 2013. The major features in this version were Adobe Generator, a Node.js-based platform for creating plug-ins for Photoshop. Photoshop 14.1 shipped with two plug-ins, one to automatically generate image assets based on an extension in the layer name, and another to automatically generate assets for Adobe Edge Reflow.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65">
       <a href="#cite_note-65">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        65
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Version 14.2 was released on 15 January 2014. Major features include Perspective Warp, Linked Smart Objects, and 3D Printing support.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66">
       <a href="#cite_note-66">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        66
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

CC 2014 features improvements to content-aware tools, two new blur tools (spin blur and path blur) and a new focus mask feature that enables the user to select parts of an image based on whether they are in focus or not. Other minor improvements have been made, including speed increases for certain tasks.

     <p>
      Photoshop CC 2015 was released on 15 June 2015. Adobe added several features including Adobe Stock, which is a library of custom stocks. They also added the ability to have more than one layer style, for example in the older versions of Photoshop you could only have one shadow for a layer, but in CC 2015 you can have up to ten. Other minor features like Export As, which is a form of the Save For Web in CC 2014 were also added. CC 2015 also marks the 25th anniversary of Photoshop.
