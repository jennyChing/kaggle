      <p>
       <img alt="" height="388" src="/images/uploads/2009/11/colmans_569_0.jpg" width="569"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Vegetarians look away now: Karmarama has recreated the famous Lord Kitchener poster in 16 types of meat in an ad for Colman's Mustard
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" height="516" src="/images/uploads/2009/11/colmans_mustard_kitchener_300x2201_1.jpg" width="379"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The whole thing apparently took over 18 hours of cooking and was pieced together by food stylist Peta O'Brian, who also worked on Fallon's Skoda Cake ad.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" height="388" src="/images/uploads/2009/11/meat1_0.jpg" width="569"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The ad features the following:
       <br/>
       Breaded Ham
       <br/>
       Chorizo
       <br/>
       Beef Carpaccio
       <br/>
       Bresola
       <br/>
       Ginger Pig Bacon
       <br/>
       Beef Skirt
       <br/>
       Roast Duck
       <br/>
       Chicken Breast
       <br/>
       Brisket
       <br/>
       Pepperami
       <br/>
       Fillet Steak
       <br/>
       Sausages
       <br/>
       Beef Steak
       <br/>
       Bacon Joint
       <br/>
       Leg of Lamb
       <br/>
       Plus duck wings for the ears.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" height="388" src="/images/uploads/2009/11/meat3_0.jpg" width="569"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The ad will run in men's mags to promote "Colman's as the best accompaniment to meat".
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" height="388" src="/images/uploads/2009/11/meat4_0.jpg" width="569"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" height="388" src="/images/uploads/2009/11/meat6_0.jpg" width="569"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       We can't decide if it's grotesque, a colossal waste of good food or sheer genius. Or all of the above.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
