          <h1>
           The 10 Best Video Game Weapons Ever to Kick Ass
          </h1>
          <p>
           Posted by
           <a href="http://gearcrave.com/author/jared-newman/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jared Newman">
            Jared Newman
           </a>
          </p>
         </header>
         <div class="post-content">
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="428" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/ashpd1.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           Try as we might to deny it, video games certainly have their fair share of violence. But its all in good fun, as proven by the wild, crazy and downright cool weapons weve been able to play with over the years. Sure, weve seen a few friendly sites build similar lists, but last weeks
           <a href="http://www.gearcrave.com/2009-01-22/portal-gun-is-huge-success/">
            Portal Gun replica
           </a>
           got us excited enough to take our own crack at it. Can you blame us? Here they are, GearCraves ten best video game weapons in the history of gaming.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <span id="more-11510">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Ten: F.E.A.R.s Nail Gun
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="fearnail" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11511" height="428" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/fearnail.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           In a testament to the beautiful merger of video games and physics, the Nail Gun in F.E.A.R. let players pin enemies to the wall, apparently for no good reason other than to allow for Internet
           <a href="http://www.gamespot.com/pages/forums/show_msgs.php?topic_id=25399183&amp;page=0">
            bragging rights
           </a>
           . The sequels coming next month, so youll get another chance at this sadistic killing machine if you missed it the first time around.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Nine: Dooms BFG 9000
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="bfg9000" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11512" height="356" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/bfg9000.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           Yeah, yeah, you called this one from a mile away. Its not like the BFG 9000 can be excluded from the list because of predictability. This big freakin gun floats a massive green orb towards enemies and, on impact, spreads to any poor sap nearby. Dont bother fighting a room full of Cacodemons without it.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Eight: Unreal Tournaments Redeemer
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="381613-redeemer_2003" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11513" height="200" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/381613-redeemer_2003.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           Controlling the Redeemer was always an imprecise science. Once you fired the one-shot mini-nuke, the camera would switch to the missles perspective, and youd have to wind it around corners to reach your target. Fortunately, it didnt matter if your aim wasnt perfect, because even the splash damage was impossible to survive.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Seven: Halo: Combat Evolveds Pistol
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="halo-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11514" height="419" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/halo-1.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           Its no surprise that Bungie nixed the pistol as it functioned in the original Halo. It was just too deadly and accurate, to the point where no other weapon mattered. In other words, it was a great gun, and some gamers were understandably upset with its no-scope replacement, which was useless without another weapon in hand. Maybe this was the UNSCs biggest military blunder.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Six: No More Heroes Blood Berry
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="no_more_heroes" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11515" height="321" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/no_more_heroes.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           Most guys would love to own a Lightsaber, but that would require traveling to a long time ago, in a galaxy far, far away. Travis Touchdowns beam katana was won through an Internet auction, and there are no Jedi skills required to operate it. Lets hope one shows up on Ebay.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Five: Mega Mans Thunder Beam
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="megaman1nes" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11516" height="401" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/megaman1nes.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           Picking one selection from Mega Mans ever-expanding arsenal was no easy task. Ultimately, it came down to usability. Not only was Elec Mans weapon the bane of Ice Mans existence, it was shockingly effective on the Rock Monster and Dr. Wily as well. For every sequel that followed, I wished for a weapon with such brutality and versatility.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Four: Ninja Gaidens Dragon Sword
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="ninja-gaiden-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11517" height="321" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/ninja-gaiden-2.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           In its early years, Ryu Hayabusas Dragon Sword appeared to be a simple melee weapon, but it really came into its own during the series recent round of remakes. The concept of sharpness is so brilliantly conveyed as this mythic sword slices through the limbs and heads of your foes. The best part? Ryu always gives the blade a quick flick to shake off the excess gore before sheathing it.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Three: Ratchet &amp; Clanks Morph-O-Ray
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="morphoray" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11518" height="410" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/morphoray.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           This weapon turns your enemy into a chicken. Comedic value aside, think of the implications. Its one thing to be dead, but becoming a barnyard animal is so much crueler. Everything you love and lived for becomes a distant memory as you strut about, clucking and laying eggs. This is what anti-game pundits should really be worrying about.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->
           <!--[endif]-->
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number Two: Turok 2s Cerebral Bore
          </h1>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           <img alt="cerebralbore" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11519" height="428" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/cerebralbore.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
           Certainly one of the most hated weapons in first-person shooter history, the Bore was nearly impossible to escape once an opponent was lined in its crosshairs. Once fired, the floating contraption would home in on the enemys head, digging out comical amounts of brain goo. Years later, I still remember that dreaded whirr, mostly when Im at the dentist.
          </p>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Number One: Contras Spread Gun
          </h1>
          <p>
           <img alt="contra" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11520" height="199" src="http://www.gearcrave.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/contra.jpg" width="570"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           It was often a point of contention among NES gamers whether the Laser or the Spread Gun was the superior Contra weapon. This is my feature, so Im putting a foot down; shooting five bullets at once is definitely preferable. Dont believe me? Try playing for real, without the 30 lives code, using only that dinky little laser beam to defend you. The spreads wall of bullets will start looking like a healthier alternative right quick.
          </p>

          </p>

              February 5, 2009 at 4:21 pm
             </a>
            </div>
            <p>
             While this is a nice, well written list, I think a few more weapons should have been added.
            </p>
            <p>
             1. The Gravity Gun (HL2)  You can use almost ANYTHING as a weapon, nuff said.
            </p>
            <p>
             2. The Laptop Gun (Perfect Dark N64)  Not only was this a powerful machine gun, but you could also throw it and turn it into a sentry gun.
            </p>
            <p>
             3. The Farsight XR-20 (Perfect Dark N64)  In most video games, playing as a sniper will get you called "cheap", due to your ability to kill someone with one shot. If that "cheap" then the Farsight is "blatant cheating" x50. Not only could you kill someone with one hit, but you could also shoot through ANY wall and the attached scope gave you X-RAY vision. Combine that with a secondary mode that makes it AUTO AIM, and you have one of the best sniper rifles in video game history.
            </p>
            <p>
             4. The Shredder (Turok 2)  One of the most popular weapons in gaming, is rightfully the shotgun. Unsurprisingly, like most FPS's Turok 2 contains one as well. The standard shotgun, while an effective weapon, is still your generic shotgun. Once upgraded however, Turok brings forth the (in my mind) best shotgun in video game history. First off, you no longer fired those dinky little shot balls, you now fired freaking LASERS out of the barrel. While this alone is enough to make it one of my favorite shotguns in gaming, they throw in a nice little bonus in as well. After leaving the gun, every laser beam that does not hit a target will rebound off the walls a few times before dissipating. Make note that the lasers do not slow down while doing so. This was one of my favorite weapons in Turok 2, though it is often forgotten because of the Cerebral Bore.
            </p>
            <p>
             5.  Metal Blade (Mega Man 2)  While the Thunder Beam was a good weapon in it's own right, ask almost any classic Mega Man fan what the best weapon in a classic Mega Man game is, and this weapon is going to be popping up a lot. Not only was it the most powerful weapon in the game, it affected almost every robot, it could be fired through walls, it could be fired in any direction, it would be fired over 100 times with out recharging, AND it was super effective on 3 different Robot Masters, including (surprisingly enough) Metal Man himself. There has yet to be a weapon of this caliber in a Mega Man game since.
            </p>
            <p>
             That wraps up my thoughts on this article.
