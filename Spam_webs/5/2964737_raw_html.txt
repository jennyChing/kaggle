      <h1 class="title">
       Packing Food for a Trip to the Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness
      </h1>
      <div class="post-thumbnail alignleft">
       <img alt="Packing Food for a Trip to the Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness" height="135" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/blueberry-pancake-2010.jpg" width="135"/>
       <span class="post-overlay">
       </span>
      </div>
      <!-- end .post-thumbnail -->
      <p>
       Over the past five years, my husband and I have been continuously improving on the food we bring for our 7-9 day trips to the Boundary Waters. Most of it is very lightweight and can be eaten as-is, or by adding boiling water. If you are going on a trip with lots of hiking or portaging, I would omit a few of these items, which I will mention as I go. This is the food we took on a leisurely canoe trip with very few portages, so some of these items are a bit of a luxury.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_410" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/spam-and-tuna-boundary-waters-backpacking-food/" rel="attachment wp-att-410">
        <img alt="Spam and tuna in bags!" class="size-full wp-image-410" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/spam-and-tuna-boundary-waters-backpacking-food.jpg" title="spam and tuna boundary waters backpacking food" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Spam and tuna in bags!
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       The SPAM and tuna shown above, for example, could all be left behind if weight were a serious concern. There are a variety of freeze-dried meals available in stores such as REI or Gander Mountain that have dehydrated meat in them, but these meals tend to run about $8-$10 each. Saving money is more of a concern for us than the added weight, so we bring all of this. The SPAM is excellent fried up for breakfast, or added to a rice meal. We eat the tuna on crackers (do not bring crackers if space is a concern) or add mayo from the little condiment packets you can take from stores and eat it in tortilla wraps.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_411" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/fried-spam-backpacking-food-boundary-waters/" rel="attachment wp-att-411">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-411" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/fried-spam-backpacking-food-boundary-waters.jpg" title="fried spam backpacking food boundary waters" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Fried SPAM is seriously tasty
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_412" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/tuna-and-spam-in-baggie-bwca-backpacking/" rel="attachment wp-att-412">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-412" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/Tuna-and-SPAM-in-baggie-BWCA-backpacking.jpg" title="Tuna and SPAM in baggie BWCA backpacking" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Tuna and SPAM crammed into a space-saving baggie
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       I highly recommend packing similar items in baggies like this. It makes it easier to rummage through your food back, saves space, and you can use the baggies to pack out garbage as your food gets eaten. Remember  cans and glass containers are not allowed in the Boundary Waters, so its necessary to find food items in bags such as this.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_413" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/chicken-in-a-bag-backpacking-food/" rel="attachment wp-att-413">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-413" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/Chicken-in-a-bag-backpacking-food-.jpg" title="Chicken in a bag backpacking food!" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Chicken in a BAG!
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       This chicken-in-a-bag was a very special find for us. For several years we have discussed how fantastic it would be if we could only have chicken in a bag. We happened across these gems quite accidentally in Target. They were excellent added to rice.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_414" style="width: 310px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/boundary-waters-beverages/" rel="attachment wp-att-414">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-414" height="400" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/boundary-waters-beverages-.jpg" title="boundary waters beverages" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Coffee to be put in a baggie
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       These were our beverages for the week. In my haste to take a photo of our beverages, I left the instant coffee in a glass container  I will transfer it to a plastic one before we head out. The Tang and chai are quite unnecessary, but a fun treat. The small packets on the bottom right are various flavors of Crystal Light and various knock-offs. These are great for backpacking because youre not lugging around the unnecessary weight of sugar like were doing with the Tang and chai.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_415" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/cashews-peanuts-and-sunflower-seeds-backpacking-food/" rel="attachment wp-att-415">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-415" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/cashews-peanuts-and-sunflower-seeds-backpacking-food.jpg" title="cashews, peanuts, and sunflower seeds backpacking food" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Cashews, peanuts, and sunflower seeds
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Some snacks. We tend to eat plain nuts rather than trail mix. We get our fruit intake by finding wild blueberries and raspberries (hopefully!). Im also rather fond of wild cherries.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_416" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/backpacking-food-beef-jerkey-and-chocolate-covered-espresso-beans/" rel="attachment wp-att-416">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-416" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/backpacking-food-beef-jerkey-and-chocolate-covered-espresso-beans.jpg" title="backpacking food beef jerkey and chocolate covered espresso beans" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Beef jerky and chocolate-covered espresso beans
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       More treats. The chocolate-covered espresso beans will certainly melt, but still taste great in a big block.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_417" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/bwca-freeze-dried-ice-cream-backpacking-food/" rel="attachment wp-att-417">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-417" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/bwca-freeze-dried-ice-cream-backpacking-food.jpg" title="bwca freeze-dried ice cream backpacking food" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Freeze-dried ice cream!
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       This is our one indulgence of expensive freeze-dried food. I turned 25 on this trip, so in lieu of cake we had some freeze-dried ice cream. Its fantastic, we had this last year on our trip as well.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_418" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/birthday-ice-cream-2010/" rel="attachment wp-att-418">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-418" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/birthday-ice-cream-2010.jpg" title="birthday ice cream 2010" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Happy birthday to me!
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_419" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/breakfast-in-the-boundary-waters/" rel="attachment wp-att-419">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-419" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/breakfast-in-the-boundary-waters.jpg" title="breakfast in the boundary waters" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        various breakfast items
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       To make the hash browns, you just add water and fry em up! The peanut butter is for bagels (another space wasting treat). Lawrys seasoning and minced onions make many meals better, including hash browns. Dont forget the tiny ketchup packets!
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_420" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/boundary-waters-meals/" rel="attachment wp-att-420">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-420" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/boundary-waters-meals.jpg" title="boundary waters meals" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Excellent alternative to expensive freeze-dried schtuff
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       These look fun. Butternut squash fusilli and buffalo mac n cheese. Both would be great with the addition of that chicken-in-a-bag! Both of these just need water added, which can be found in abundance in the lakes we canoe through. An article about the importance of purifying/boiling water can be found earlier in my blog postings.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_421" style="width: 310px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/fish-fixings-bwca-meal/" rel="attachment wp-att-421">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-421" height="400" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/fish-fixings-bwca-meal.jpg" title="fish fixings bwca meal" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        The makings for some tasty fish!
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       The best case scenario with all of these fish fixings is that we
       <em>
        catch so many fish we run out of breading and have to use lemon pepper seasoning.
       </em>
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_422" style="width: 310px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/bwca-shore-lunch-soup/" rel="attachment wp-att-422">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-422" height="400" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/bwca-shore-lunch-soup.jpg" title="bwca shore lunch soup" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Shore Lunch makes a variety of soups
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Every year we bring a Shore Lunch wild rice soup, it only seems fitting for the Boundary Waters. The issue with this bag of soup, however, is that it literally makes 8 hearty servings. Its essential to keep this in mind when whipping it up, otherwise you will end up with way more food than you can possibly eat. Here is an example of me cooking an absurd quantity of soup.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_423" style="width: 310px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/way-too-much-soup-2010/" rel="attachment wp-att-423">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-423" height="400" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/way-too-much-soup-2010.jpg" title="way too much soup 2010" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        What was I thinking?
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_424" style="width: 310px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/pancakes-in-the-bwca/" rel="attachment wp-att-424">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-424" height="400" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/pancakes-in-the-bwca.jpg" title="pancakes in the bwca" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Just add water and fry
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       The greatest way to eat pancakes in the Boundary Waters is with the addition of freshly picked blueberries or raspberries. Here is what they look like while hanging out on their little blueberry bushes.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_425" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/wild-blueberries-2010/" rel="attachment wp-att-425">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-425" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/wild-blueberries-2010.jpg" title="wild blueberries 2010" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Wild Blueberries
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_426" style="width: 410px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/blueberry-pancake-2010/" rel="attachment wp-att-426">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-426" height="300" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/blueberry-pancake-2010.jpg" title="blueberry pancake 2010" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Pancakes with wild blueberries
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_427" style="width: 310px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/instant-mashed-potatoes-boundary-waters/" rel="attachment wp-att-427">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-427" height="400" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/instant-mashed-potatoes-boundary-waters.jpg" title="instant mashed potatoes boundary waters" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Instant mashed potatoes in a variety of flavors
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Instant mashed potatoes are a great addition to any meal, and are also superb fried up into little potato pancake things for breakfast.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_428" style="width: 310px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/instant-rice-backpacking-meals-bwca/" rel="attachment wp-att-428">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-428" height="400" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/instant-rice-backpacking-meals-bwca.jpg" title="instant rice backpacking meals bwca" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        More instant rice than what was actually brought
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Instant rice. Great with spam or bagged chicken. Tabasco sauce is generally a nice addition. The Uncle Bens rice already has water in it and is a bit heavier, but is nice in case its raining and we dont want to go out to get water. It simply fries up. A lazy mans meal at its finest.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_429" style="width: 310px">
       <a href="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/packing-food-for-a-trip-to-the-boundary-waters-canoe-area-wilderness/bwca-packs/" rel="attachment wp-att-429">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-429" height="400" src="http://wildernesswaypoint.com/wp-content/uploads/bwca-packs.jpg" title="bwca packs" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        packs of food
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Now, if we trimmed out a bunch of our fun indulgences we could fit all the food into the yellow bag. Fortunately, we have the space available for both of these bags and as a result we will feast like kings (and queens). These bags are great because they have little loops at the top, which makes hanging them in trees for the night easy. Remember to hang your bags of foodand any odorous items, including your cooking clothes in a tree at least 10 feet up and hanging out on a limb, about 5 feet down.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
