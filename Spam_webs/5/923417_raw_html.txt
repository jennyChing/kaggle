        <h1 class="entry-title">
         DIY  weather map
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">
         <a href="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/diy-weather-map/#comments" title="Comment on DIY  weather map">
          4 Replies
         </a>
        </div>
        <!-- .comments-link -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="weather 5" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2158" height="640" src="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/weather-5.jpg" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         LOOK! I found someone who can
         <em>
          actually
         </em>
         sew to show us how to make kids Christmas presents for (nearly) nothing!
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="www.etsy.com/shop/rachelbeejewellery">
          intimidatingly handy-with-a-needle Rachel Morrish
         </a>
         has agreed to share a series of posts for the blog while Im
         <del>
          recovering from shell-shock
         </del>
         taking a wee spell of maternity leave. I knew something great would come of my sleep deprivation.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youve got any other ideas for Christmas presents nows the time to share them. Im making
         <a href="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/a-3rd-birthday-plan-the-diy-play-kitchen-and-other-ideas/">
          the story tape
         </a>
         for Js main gift (each of the important people in his life are recording themselves reading their favourite childrens story) and
         <a href="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/the-diy-play-kitchen-update/">
          the play kitchen
         </a>
         for Christmas but need stocking filler suggestions pleeeeease.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Rachel Morrish:
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <a href="https://twitter.com/rachelmorrish">
           Twitter
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="www.etsy.com/shop/rachelbeejewellery">
          <em>
           Etsy shop
          </em>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Over to Rachel.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How to make a weather map
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         You will need:
        </p>
        <p>
         a piece fabric, i had a piece of linen left over from another project, size wise up to you
         <br/>
         fabric pen or permanent marker
         <br/>
         felt to make the weathercolours depend upon what you make
         <br/>
         filler stuff
         <br/>
         scissors
         <br/>
         needle and embroidery silks
         <br/>
         2 sticks (i use old ones from plants)
         <br/>
         ribbon
         <br/>
         velcro stickers
        </p>
        <p>
         so first of all, I got a very simple map of the UK and simple drew it onto the fabric
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="weather 1" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2154" height="800" src="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/weather-1.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I decided on my weather people sun, rainbow, cloud, wind, umberella, snowflake anddid a simple sketch and then cut out some shapes
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="weather 2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2155" height="1024" src="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/weather-2.jpg" width="768"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Simply using blanket stitch, I created the little characters and filled with the toy filler stuff top tip: do the faces first, so much easier
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="weather 3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2156" height="480" src="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/weather-3.jpg" width="480"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then I attached velcro to each character and placed the other velcro onto the map in significant places.i did birmingham, london, llandudno, totnes, st andrews, dublin (all places that mean something to our family). Secure the velcro with a few stitches too:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="weather 4" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2157" height="1024" src="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/weather-4.jpg" width="768"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Attach the map to sticks at the top and bottom and then tie on some ribbon:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="weather 5" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2158" height="640" src="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/weather-5.jpg" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         and now every morning do a weather forecast!
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          By the way, if you think THATS good, you should
          <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/rachelbeejewellery">
           check out her jewellery on Etsy
          </a>
          
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="rachelbeelogo" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2160" height="246" src="http://www.freeourkids.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/rachelbeelogo.jpg" width="230"/>
        </p>
       </div>
