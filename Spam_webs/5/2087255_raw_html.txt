         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
           History
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          After being exposed to high levels of radiation during a particle fusion experiment, Dr.
          <a href="/wiki/Magma" title="Magma">
           Mike Morgan
          </a>
          , Dr.
          <a href="/wiki/Freon" title="Freon">
           Mary Michaels
          </a>
          , and Dr.
          <a href="/wiki/2-D_Man" title="2-D Man">
           Stuart Lowe
          </a>
          underwent dramatic genetic changes. The accident altered their DNA and bestowed them with superpowers, so the three decided to use their abilities for good.
         </p>
         <p>
          The government financed and established their base of operation  the
          <a href="/wiki/Trio_Tower" title="Trio Tower">
           Trio Tower
          </a>
          . The Terrific Trio vowed to fight the crime in
          <a href="/wiki/Gotham_City" title="Gotham City">
           Gotham City
          </a>
          , although
          <a href="/wiki/General_Norman" title="General Norman">
           General Norman
          </a>
          of the United States Army did not trust them at all, preferring to kill them outright rather than support them.
         </p>
         <p>
          Six months later, the Trio was ready to clean up the city, and their first heroic exploit was thwarting the robbery of the
          <a href="/wiki/T7_Chip" title="T7 Chip">
           T7 Chip
          </a>
          from
          <a class="new" href="/wiki/M_Technologies?action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="M Technologies (page does not exist)">
           M Technologies
          </a>
          . Afterward, they were introduced to the public and became tremendously popular. Thereafter, the Trio would be summoned to intervene in various situations, such as a robbery or a hostage takeover.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Terrific Trio were not only popular among the crime-fighting community, but the general public adored them and couldn't get enough Terrific Trio merchandise such as T-shirts, toys, and posters. Despite the Trio's popularity, there were several people in Gotham who were suspicious of the team, including General Norman and
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bruce_Wayne" title="Bruce Wayne">
           Bruce Wayne
          </a>
          . Even with the Trio on the job, Wayne continued to send his protg,
          <a href="/wiki/Batman_(Terry_McGinnis)" title="Batman (Terry McGinnis)">
           Terry McGinnis
          </a>
          , to patrol Gotham every night. The Trio experienced a rude awakening when a little girl expressed extreme fright at the mere sight of Magma, despite Magma having saved her from kidnappers. To make matters worse, the police rudely snubbed him afterwards.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Trio's future started to unravel when their best friend,
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Hodges" title="Dr. Hodges">
           Dr. Hodges
          </a>
          , ascertained that their genetic condition was unstable. Hodges informed General Norman of this, who relished the opportunity to assault Trio Tower in an attempt to eliminate the Trio. Magma had become impatient with his condition and was anxious to find a cure, but Dr. Hodges wouldn't accept his help. So, Magma broke into Hodges'
          <a class="new" href="/wiki/-R_Research_Center?action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="-R Research Center (page does not exist)">
           laboratory
          </a>
          and stole the Trio's latest DNA scan. Moments after learning about their condition, the Trio was attacked by the military. The Trio fought their way out of the Tower, evading death.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Trio went on to review the data on the particle fusion experiment, and discovered that Dr. Hodges knew about its danger. Confronted, Hodges didn't admit his guilt, but the Trio remained unconvinced. So, they decided to duplicate the accident to finish what Hodges had started. At that point, Hodges finally confessed orchestrating the accident in a foolish attempt to kill Michael and seduce Mary for himself, and begged them to shut down the device, claiming that the radiation would devastate the entire city. However, at that point, the Trio were emotionally destroyed from Hodges' betrayal and the military, law enforcement and public turning against them. As a result, the Trio no longer cared about public safety or the consequences of their actions. Batman stepped in and a fight ensued. Batman activated the emergency ventilation system, trapping Freon and 2-D Man inside it. Batman then turned a fire hose on Magma, solidifying him.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heroes_0-0">
           <a href="#cite_note-Heroes-0">
            [1]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </p>
         <p>
          Apparently, after this, the Terrific Trio fell from grace, seeing as
          <a href="/wiki/Ratboy" title="Ratboy">
           Ratboy
          </a>
          , a self-proclaimed "Pack Rat", had a Magma action figure among his collection of unwanted items.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
           <a href="#cite_note-1">
            [2]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </p>

         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Background_information">
           Background information
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          The Terrific Trio is an obvious pastiche of the
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fantastic_Four" title="wikipedia:Fantastic Four">
           Fantastic Four
          </a>
          , with Magma being a counterpart of
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thing_(comics)" title="wikipedia:Thing (comics)">
           The Thing
          </a>
          and 2-D Man a counterpart of
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mr._Fantastic" title="wikipedia:Mr. Fantastic">
           Mister Fantastic
          </a>
          . Freon, despite being the sole female member of the team like the
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invisible_Woman" title="wikipedia:Invisible Woman">
           Invisible Woman
          </a>
          , has powers more akin to those of the
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Torch" title="wikipedia:Human Torch">
           Human Torch
          </a>
          , only ice-based instead of fire-based. Also, General Norman somewhat resembles
          <a class="extiw exitstitial" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Fury" title="wikipedia:Nick Fury">
           Nick Fury
          </a>
          (and to some extent, Adolf Hitler), although Norman is a biased and self-centered person who cares nothing for the heroes and would rather kill them for no reason than trust them (much like
          <a href="/wiki/General_Hardcastle" title="General Hardcastle">
           General Hardcastle
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Wade_Eiling" title="Wade Eiling">
           General Eiling
          </a>
          ), in contrast to Fury, who is himself a superhero and a member and close friend of the Avengers.
         </p>
