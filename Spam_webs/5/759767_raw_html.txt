                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_EFZJo5o978/U8-INEeC_jI/AAAAAAAAAnM/z1-SXMjhSjU/s1600/Spring-Temple.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Worlds 10 most Enormous Sculptures and Monuments
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5887202998574527809" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_EFZJo5o978/U8-INEeC_jI/AAAAAAAAAnM/z1-SXMjhSjU/s1600/Spring-Temple.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="213" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_EFZJo5o978/U8-INEeC_jI/AAAAAAAAAnM/z1-SXMjhSjU/s1600/Spring-Temple.jpg" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        People want to visit
the world in order to getting relax and to feel free from their daily hectic
routine and also to get closer to culture and traditions of every corner of the
world. Most considerable places in the world for tourists are beaches, seas,
cities, castles and museums. Statues are also one of the foremost captivations
for visiting. Statues are formed by tremendous human art and some of the
statues represent their nation. People travel thousand miles in order to watch
stunning statues.
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        This world is full of
colossal monuments and statues which are built on the basis of historical facts,
idols and occasions. Here are the top 10 statues and monuments of the world.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <a name="more">
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Murugan
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        Murugan is the second
tallest statue of Hindu Deity in the world as well as tallest statue of Hindu
Deity in Malaysia. This 42.7 meters tall statue took 3 years for its construction.
It is situated on the appearance of Batu Caves in Selangor. It was made up in
2006 with 250 tons of steel, 1550 cubic meters of concrete and 300 liters of
gold paint. It is most visited statue of the world.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Spring
temple Buddha
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        The Spring temple
Buddha is situated in Lushan country, Henan, China. This statue was built from
1997 to 2008 and its height is 128 meters. The peak on which statue stands was restructured
in October 2008, and now its height has become 208 meters. Its a fabulous
statue which represents Vairocana Buddha and captures attention of many people
a lot.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Cristo
de la Concordia
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        Cristo de la Concordia
is 40.44 meters tall statue of Jesus Christ, located in Cochabamba, Bolivia.
Construction of the statue was started on 31
                        <sup>
                         st
                        </sup>
                        July 1987 and was
completed on 4 November 1997. You can reach at this temple by cable cars or by
climbing 2000 steps. This tremendous statue is only accessible on Sundays.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Ten
directions Samantabhadra Bodhisattva
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        Ten directions
Samantabhadra is placed on a four headed and six-tusked elephant. This statue
is located in Mount Emei, Sichuan, China and is a ten-headed, 48 meters tall.
This striking statue was constructed in 2006.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Rodina-Mat
zovyot
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        Rodina-Mat zovyot is
also called the Motherland Calls. Its
a heavy weighted sculpture 0f 7900 tons. This excellent statue was created in 1967
and when it was newly constructed, it was the tallest statue of the world.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Great
Buddha of Thailand
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        The second name of
Great Buddha is Big Buddha. It is 92 meters tall statue placed in Ang Thong,
Thailand and is the tallest one in Thailand. Its foundation took a long 18
years as it construction period was 1990 to 2008. It is made up of cement and a
coat of gold paint.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Cross
of the Valle de los Caidos
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        Valle de los Caidos
consists of an underground church and a massive monument sited in Madrid Spain.
It was designed by Pedro Muguruza and Diego Mendez according to the Spanish architecture
of 20
                        <sup>
                         th
                        </sup>
                        Century. Its height is 152 meters.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Rear Front
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        Erected in 1979, the
Rear Front depicts a worker passing sword to a fidget. It is a very unique
statue which catches many people.
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Guan
Yin of the South Sea of Sanya
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        Guan Yin is the fourth
tallest statue on Earth and is 108 meters tall. It was constructed in six
years. The Statue is consist of three features having different postures, one
of them faces inland and the other two face South China Sea.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <u>
                         <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                          Leshan
Giant Buddha
                          <o:p>
                          </o:p>
                         </span>
                        </u>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif; line-height: 115%;">
                       Last but not the
least, Leshan Giant Buddha was directed by Buddhist monastic named Haitong.
Its a very old sculpture constructed in 700s. This eye-catching statue is 71
meters tall and placed in Leshan, China.
                      </span>
                     </div>
