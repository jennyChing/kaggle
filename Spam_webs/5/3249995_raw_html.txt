          The strangest moon in the Solar System (Synopsis)
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="byline vcard author">

       <div class="main">
        <div class="content entry-content">
         <blockquote class="graf--blockquote graf--startsWithDoubleQuote">
          <p>
           <em class="markup--em markup--blockquote-em">
            The dance between darkness and light will always remainthe stars and the moon will always need the darkness to be seen, the darkness will just not be worth having without the moon and the stars. 
           </em>
           C. JoyBell C.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Yet what do you do when you discover a moon that
          <em>
           itself
          </em>
          straddles the border between light-and-dark? It might sound like something out of a fantasy novel, but it isnt: its exactly what we get when it comes to the second of Saturns moons ever discovered: Iapetus.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_32386" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2015/02/Iapetus_equatorial_ridge.jpg">
           <img alt="Image credit: NASA / JPL-Caltech / Space Science Institute / Cassini." class="size-medium wp-image-32386" height="339" src="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2015/02/Iapetus_equatorial_ridge-600x339.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Image credit: NASA / JPL-Caltech / Space Science Institute / Cassini.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          While some of its mysteries remain elusive, such as why its orbit is so severely tilted or what caused its giant equatorial ridge, one mystery has finally been solved: why one hemisphere is so bright and reflective, while the other is so dark.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_32385" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2015/02/411068main_PIA11690_full.jpg">
           <img alt="Image credit: NASA / JPL, via http://spacefellowship.com/news/art16887/reddish-dust-and-ice-migration-darken-saturn-s-moon-iapetus.html." class="size-medium wp-image-32385" height="294" src="http://scienceblogs.com/startswithabang/files/2015/02/411068main_PIA11690_full-600x294.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Image credit: NASA / JPL, via http://spacefellowship.com/news/art16887/reddish-dust-and-ice-migration-darken-saturn-s-moon-iapetus.html.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Come find out about
          <a href="https://medium.com/starts-with-a-bang/the-strangest-moon-in-the-solar-system-bdc675ec411c" target="_blank">
           the strangest moon in the Solar System
          </a>
          !
         </p>
