         </span>
         <span content="" id="wpLogin" itemprop="wpLogin">
         </span>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Hello, my name is Jeannette and Im a peanut butter addict. Seriously I can eat the stuff straight out of the jar on a spoon without even thinking twice. And you all already know how much I love sweets! So when I can combine the best of both worlds for an amazing yummy dessert that is simple enough for even me to make yet tastes like a million bucks, Im one happy mama! So, if you need a sweet treat this summer to complete a cookout, a family get together or just to get through an afternoon with crazy kids, be sure to check out this super yummy, amazingly simple peanut butter pie recipe!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie1.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5948" height="300" src="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie1.jpg" title="PeanutButterPie1" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           What You Will Need
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          1/2 Cup Lowfat Milk
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          1 Cup of Powdered Sugar
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          8 ounces of Cream Cheese
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          1/2 Cup Peanut Butter
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Cool Whip to Taste
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          1 Pre Made graham cracker Pie Crust
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie2.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5949" height="300" src="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie2.jpg" title="PeanutButterPie2" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           How to Make It
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Start by disolving your cup of powered sugar in your milk. You will need to stir it a little bit to get it to dissolve completely and it will end up a little bit thick. This is what you want to happen!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie3.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5950" height="300" src="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie3.jpg" title="PeanutButterPie3" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Set your mixture aside and soften your cream cheese in the microwave. About 45 seconds does the trick if it has been in the refrigerator. Now, mix in your cream cheese and peanut butter and mix well. A blender is really recommended for this step but you can do it with a spoon if a blender is not available.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButter5.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5952" height="300" src="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButter5.jpg" title="PeanutButter5" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Now you have your base and are ready to mix in your Cool Whip. I like to eyeball this part and just add enough to soften the taste and produce the right amount of filling for my pie. On average, I use about a half of a small container of Cool Whip but you can use as much or little as you like to create a pie that has a stronger or lighter taste.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie4.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5951" height="300" src="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie4.jpg" title="PeanutButterPie4" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Your pie filling is now complete! Pour it into your graham cracker pie crust and place in the freezer for 1 hour to set. Then move it to the refrigerator so that it does not freeze.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie5.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5953" height="300" src="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie5.jpg" title="PeanutButterPie5" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Once the pie is completely set, cut into slices and top with chocolate syrup to serve! Youll have a creation that is delicious, beautiful and a sure fire hit for the entire family!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie6.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5954" height="300" src="http://jmanandmillerbug.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/PeanutButterPie6.jpg" title="PeanutButterPie6" width="400"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          Enjoy!
         </p>
