         Beer Battered French Fries Recipe
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Are you ready for this Sundays big game? I am now with this melt in your mouth, super savoryBeer Battered French Fries Recipe..Thats right, potatoes tossed in a seasoned beer batter and then fried to perfection.In case youre thinking that this recipe requires peeling a potato, cutting it into fries and soaking them. Youre wrong.. I use a little secret weapon called:
         <a href="http://gametimegrub.net" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Alexia Frozen Fries
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span id="more-27442">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Battered French Fries Recipe - starts with frozen fries to save time and work. They turn out so crispy and perfectly seasoned. Serve with tartar sauce on the side and you got a winning dish." class="aligncenter wp-image-27450 size-full" height="517" src="http://livingsweetmoments.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/alexia-smart-classics.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Since Alexia offers such great quality fries I decided to use them instead of going through all that trouble of peeling and cutting potatoes. I couldnt decided between the crinkle cut fries or the sweet potato kind so I chose both. I was inspired to create this recipe from a fish and chips dish I tried at Lincoln Rd. the other day. The fish was friedin a beer batter and I wanted to impart the same flavor and texture to the fries. Its perfect for game day. I serve it with tartar sauce and a giant pickle because the balance of brininess and creaminess are the perfect combination with the crunchy fries.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Battered French Fries Recipe - starts with frozen fries to save time and work. They turn out so crispy and perfectly seasoned. Serve with tartar sauce on the side and you got a winning dish." class="aligncenter wp-image-27444 size-full" height="532" src="http://livingsweetmoments.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/beer-battered-french-fries-recipe-2.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         If youre inviting people over to watch the game, serve it with a few beers and your guests will be very happy. Make an extra batch in case they ask for more. I would also serve some hot sauce on the side for a spicier kick.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Battered French Fries Recipe - starts with frozen fries to save time and work. They turn out so crispy and perfectly seasoned. Serve with tartar sauce on the side and you got a winning dish." class="aligncenter wp-image-27445 size-full" height="532" src="http://livingsweetmoments.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/beer-battered-french-fries-recipe-3.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Seriously, is there something better than a beer battered fry? You cant have just one.. They are seasoned to perfection with a nice crunch. The sweet potato and crinkle fries give a different flavor and texture dimension. The sweet potato has a deep sweet taste that can withstand the creamy sauce. The Crinkle fries on the other hand, are wider and meatier, making them perfect for battering and dipping.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Battered French Fries Recipe - starts with frozen fries to save time and work. They turn out so crispy and perfectly seasoned. Serve with tartar sauce on the side and you got a winning dish." class="aligncenter wp-image-27443 size-full" height="532" src="http://livingsweetmoments.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/beer-battered-french-fries-recipe-1.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The process of making them is very simple. 4 easy steps and youre done. I suggest keeping them in the oven before guests arrive and frying them in batches so the temperature of the oil doesnt drop rapidly. You want your potatoes to fry up evenly and crispy.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Battered French Fries Recipe - starts with frozen fries to save time and work. They turn out so crispy and perfectly seasoned. Serve with tartar sauce on the side and you got a winning dish." class="aligncenter wp-image-27448 size-full" height="800" src="http://livingsweetmoments.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/beer-battered-french-fries-recipe-in-process.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         For thisBeer Battered French Fries Recipe I start out with frozen potatoes and just coat them with flour. Then dip them in the batter and fry. When they come out of the batter I simply add some salt, pepper and chopped parsley. Thats it! Serve it alongside with your favorite tartar sauce and youre ready to watch your favorite team win.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         If your friends are on opposed teams you can divide them into crinkle and sweet potato teams or you can add some toothpicks in your favorite teams colors. I honestly dont even know who is playing so I chose a multicolored pattern.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Battered French Fries Recipe - starts with frozen fries to save time and work. They turn out so crispy and perfectly seasoned. Serve with tartar sauce on the side and you got a winning dish." class="aligncenter wp-image-27446 size-full" height="532" src="http://livingsweetmoments.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/beer-battered-french-fries-recipe-4.jpg" width="800"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I really hope you can give thisBeer Battered French Fries Recipe a try and if you do, let me know how they come out.
        </p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Bag of Alexia Fries (any flavor), frozen - Do not thaw
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Bottle of Beer
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cup of powdered Pancake Mix
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Teaspoon of Garlic Powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon of Paprika
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon of Powdered Mustard
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Salt and Pepper to taste
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Vegetable oil for frying
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Flour for coating
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Parsley, chopped for garnish
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Mix the frozen potatoes with the flour until they are completely coated. Toss out the excess flour
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a bowl whisk together the pancake mix, garlic powder, paprika, mustard and salt. Add the beer and whisk until completely mixed and no lumps form
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Working in batches, throw the potatoes inside the batter and shake off the excess using a slotted spoon
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Carefully insert the battered potatoes inside a big pot of oil over medium heat. Stir to make sure the potatoes do not stick together. Fry until they are brown and crispy. About 3-4 minutes
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place the fried potatoes on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb the excess oil
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add salt, pepper and parsley
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Enjoy!
           </li>
          </ol>
