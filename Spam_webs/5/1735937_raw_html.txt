     <h1>
      14 stunning landscapes youll only find in New Zealand
     </h1>
     <div class="post-inner clearfix">
      <div class="post-meta">
       <div class="post-meta-author">
        by
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/author/hal-amen/">
         Hal Amen
        </a>
       </div>

        April 29, 2015
       </div>
       <div class="post-avatar">
        <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-100" height="100" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/authors/hal-amen.jpg" width="100"/>

       <div class="post-share-wrapper post-share--vertical" data-post-id="354486" data-sticky="" data-sticky-max-width="991" data-sticky-offset-top="69" data-sticky-parent-contain="" data-sticky-parent-selector=".page-section">

      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        WHATEVER KIND OF TERRAIN youre into, chances are you can find it in New Zealand  dramatic coastal cliffs, alpine lakes and peaks, surfable beaches, active volcanoes and geothermal features, lush rainforest and old-growth forest, walkable glaciers, underground cavernsits all here. But what really sets New Zealand apart is the fact that all of the above is in such close proximity, and is so easily accessible. You can go surf to summit in a single day, drive from snowy mountain passes to temperate rainforest. What that means is you get to pack an incredible amount of adventure into every trip.
       </p>
       <p>
        When you make it down to New Zealand, or what the indigenous Maori people call Aotearoa, meaning land of the long white cloud, here are 14 stunning and unique landscapes to look for.
       </p>

       <p>
        The southwestern corner of New Zealands South Island is dominated by the enormous Fiordland National Park, a dramatic landscape of fiords with narrow fingers of sea cutting inland, flanked by cliffs thousands of feet high 
        <a href="https://ad.atdmt.com/c/go;p=11042207334194;a=11042207350690;ev.a=1">
         Milford Sound
        </a>
        is the most majestic of these fiords. Its glacially carved walls reach as high as 4,000 feet, and after heavy rain hundreds of waterfalls cascade down their sides. Boat cruises of all sizes can give you a good look at Milford Sound  for a more intimate experience, arrange an overnight boat trip or go by kayak.
       </p>

       <p>
        Even if
        <a href="https://ad.atdmt.com/c/go;p=11042207334194;a=11042207350692;ev.a=1">
         Mt. Taranaki
        </a>
        werent one of the most symmetrical conical volcanoes in the world, its location would make it seem so. The 8,261ft stratovolcano stands alone above the forested plains of the North Island region of Taranaki, striking quite a profile no matter where youre viewing it from  a distant horizon, an airplane window, or the slopes of an adjacent hill. Climbing is best attempted in summer, when theres minimal snow cover on the summit.
       </p>

       <p>
        A couple hours south of Auckland lies the small village of Waitomo and one of the biggest tourist attractions in the North Island: the
        <a href="https://ad.atdmt.com/c/go;p=11042207334194;a=11042207350687;ev.a=1">
         Waitomo Caves
        </a>
        . The cave system is awesome in itself, with underground rivers that you can tube or boat on, huge caverns, and rappelling / spelunking opportunitiesbut the real draw is the creatures down there waiting for you.          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/christoph_straessler/">
          Christoph Strssler
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_354714" style="width: 950px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Lake Tekapo lupines" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/lake-tekapo-lupines-940x529.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-large wp-image-354714 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/lake-tekapo-lupines-940x529.jpg" height="529" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/christoph_straessler/">
          Christoph Strssler
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Right in the middle of the South Island is a lowland area called the Mackenzie Basin, and within it sit three roughly parallel glacially fed lakes: Tekapo, Pukaki, and Ohau. Their respective primary sources are the Godley, Tasman, and Hopkins Rivers, which transport finely ground silt from the glaciers of the Southern Alps. These tiny particles give the lakes a distinctive powder-blue hue and, combined with the lupines that grow along their shores, the effect is particularly photogenic. If youre driving from
        <a href="https://ad.atdmt.com/c/go;p=11042207334194;a=11042207350693;ev.a=1">
         Queenstown to Christchurch
        </a>
        , the lakes make for a scenic drive, and/or great stops along the way.
       </p>
       <h2>
        13. Aoraki / Mt. Cook National Park
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_354715" style="width: 950px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Aoraki / Mt. Cook" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/mt-cook1-940x623.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-large wp-image-354715 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/mt-cook1-940x623.jpg" height="623" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/kewl/">

        </p>
       </div>

        </noscript>
        <img class="size-large wp-image-354716 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/mt-cook2-940x627.jpg" height="627" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/aschaf/">
          Andrea Schaffer
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_354717" style="width: 950px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Aoraki / Mt. Cook" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/mt-cook3-940x626.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-large wp-image-354717 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/mt-cook3-940x626.jpg" height="626" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/aschaf/">
          Andrea Schaffer
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        New Zealands highest peak (12,218 feet) gives its name to the national park that covers a section of the Southern Alps where most of the countrys tallest mountains are found.
        <a href="https://ad.atdmt.com/c/go;p=11042207334194;a=11042207350695;ev.a=1">
         Aoraki / Mt. Cook
        </a>
        itself is made up of three separate summits and is sandwiched between the Tasman and Hooker Glaciers. Just a few miles south of the mountain lies Mount Cook Village, your base of operations for exploring this incredible New Zealand landscape. Try the Hooker Valley Track, an easy 3-hour hike that gets you right into this scenery (and over three swing bridges).
       </p>
       <h2>
        14. Piha Beach
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_354718" style="width: 950px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Piha Beach" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/piha-beach1-940x552.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-large wp-image-354718 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/piha-beach1-940x552.jpg" height="552" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/12552120@N05/">
          Seth Linn
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_354719" style="width: 950px">

        </noscript>

       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_355681" style="width: 950px">

        <img class="size-large wp-image-355681 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/piha-replace-940x529.jpg" height="529" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/kiwi/">
          Kiwi Flickr
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="https://ad.atdmt.com/c/go;p=11042207334194;a=11042207350694;ev.a=1">
         Piha
        </a>
        is situated 45 minutes west of Auckland, which makes it one of the most popular beach getaways for residents of New Zealands largest city. Still, it feels remote thanks to the drive through lush rainforest and the steep hills that hem in the mile-and-a-half stretch of black sand. There are two surf beaches, a sheltered lagoon, the distinctive Lion Rock that divides South and North Piha, and the nearby Kitekite Falls. As with pretty much every corner of New Zealand, theres a whole lot to explore.
       </p>
