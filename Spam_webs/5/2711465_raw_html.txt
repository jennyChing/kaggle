         <h1 class="articletitle">
          Building an emotional connection through storytelling still reigns supreme
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="author">
         <img class="avatar" src="http://45.55.255.229/O6ATJEx_YpvJAKF8xGRw8qtsRjs=/34x34/smart/http://45.55.255.229/user/image/738793/shahnaznewmuseum2015-png"/>
         <span>
          By
          <span class="name">
           Shahnaz  Mahmud
          </span>
          on Nov 06, 2014
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="bodycopy">
         <p>
          Perhaps it was by design that the "Brands and Master Storytellers" session during Day One of
          <a href="http://ad-tech.com/ny" rel="nofollow">
           ad:tech New York
          </a>
          was held in the Hollywood room. The focus, of course, was on building an emotional connection through the very powerful medium of video and film.
         </p>
         <p>
          No matter how shiny your new gadgets are, its the art of storytelling that will resonate most successfully with consumers.
         </p>
         <p>
          Terry Rieser, partner and COO of
          <a href="http://www.content.tagcreativeny.com" rel="nofollow">
           Tag Creative
          </a>
          , presented a highly successful case study of YouTube sensation
          <a href="http://michellephan.com" rel="nofollow">
           Michelle Phan
          </a>
          ,who turned entrepreneur by offering makeup tutorials. In 2010,
          <a href="http://www.lancome-usa.com/on/demandware.store/Sites-lancome_us-Site/default/Search-Show?cgid=special_offers&amp;cm_mmc=LabeliumSearch-_-GoogleBrand-_-BrandHP-_-lancome&amp;bookmark=376846" rel="nofollow">
           Lancome
          </a>
          made her their official video makeup artist when she featured some of their products in her videos. But Rieser brought forth the
          <a href="http://www.lorealparisusa.com/?cid=lorus_rm_Google_Brand+Brand_Loreal&amp;gclid=CJDl4ZjJ5MECFUUV7Aod8jsAng" rel="nofollow">
           LOreal
          </a>
          partnership, in which they launched a new cosmetic line just for Phan, EM by
          <a href="http://www.emcosmetics.com/on/demandware.store/Sites-em-cosmetics-Site/default/Default-Start?cm_mmc=LabeliumSearch-_-Google-_-Brand-_-em%20by%20michelle%20phan&amp;gclid=CI-7saXJ5MECFQ9p7Aod-VAAnQ" rel="nofollow">
           Michelle Phan
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          The rich media was a short film on Phans own story to stardom, borne out of a desire to help others with makeup applications. Phan was immediately appealing to LOreal because she was authentic and catered to that elusive 18-35 demographic. She already had a built-in audience of 7 million followers. The goal, said Rieser, was to engage a large global audience and turn them into beauty consumers. Storytelling was the foundation, told through makeup, she noted. Rieser talked about LOreal as a brand unafraid to break the rules and recognize this as an evolving, living thing. And it was a first for the cosmetics behemoth to let the audience dictate some of the moving parts, like packaging, at the insistence of Phan.
         </p>
         <p>
          Jared Anderson, director of Jarden Media Ventures, also showcased a very powerful video. Professional climber
          <a href="http://paigeclaassen.com" rel="nofollow">
           Paige Claassen
          </a>
          , sponsored by
          <a href="http://www.backcountry.com/marmot?CMP_ID=PD_GOc001BR&amp;mv_pc=r101&amp;mkwid=sZZzhsALC_dc%7Cpcrid%7C51641269364&amp;gclid=CNSjyd_J5MECFXEQ7AodcmEAoA" rel="nofollow">
           Marmot
          </a>
          , wanted to do something good for the nonprofits she was involved with. The result? The company created videos of her traveling around the world doing special climbs to raise money for the charities. That translated into more than 189 million impressions, with 90 percent of that earned or organic, and it created a great deal of valuable long-form video, Anderson pointed out. The cause marketing factor was also a great benefit.
         </p>
         <p>
          To be effective in storytelling, taking the direction mobile is going in, Douglas Dicconson, co-founder and managing partner of
          <a href="http://www.cinelan.com" rel="nofollow">
           Cinelan
          </a>
          , said the power is in short form nowadays. Three minutes, to be precise. Two projects,
          <a href="http://www.focusforwardfilms.com" rel="nofollow">
           Focus Forward Films
          </a>
          in collaboration with
          <a href="http://www.ge.com/?gclid=CIv1hoTK5MECFeRj7AodxWkApA" rel="nofollow">
           GE
          </a>
          and
          <i>
           <a href="https://wetheeconomy.com" rel="nofollow">
            We the Economy
           </a>
          </i>
          , showcase several shorts with documentary filmmakers. The first focuses on the power of ideas and invention. The second, well, thats pretty self-explanatory. Dicconson was highlighting modern-day filmmaking that can make the greatest impact through these short bites.
         </p>
         <p>
          Cinelans distributor,
          <a href="http://www.jungroup.com" rel="nofollow">
           Jun Group
          </a>
          , was there to support his assertions. Mitchell Reichgut, CEO, noted that the companys focus on mobile went from 30-70 percent last year.
          <i>
           We the Economy,
          </i>
          as an example, is on pace to hit over 1,000,000 views, post its Oct. 21, 2014 release.
         </p>
         <p>
          Visual, emotional storytelling - and in the shorter form - then, is what marketers should strive for.
         </p>

         </p>
