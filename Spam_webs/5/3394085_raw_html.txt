     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Torre dei Lamberti
      </b>
      is a 84m high tower in
      <a href="/wiki/Verona" title="Verona">
       Verona
      </a>
      , northern
      <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
       Italy
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Construction of the tower was started in 1172. In May 1403 the top of the tower was struck by lightning, but the restoration works didn't start until 1448 and took 16 years. During that time, the tower was enlarged: The more recent sections can be recognized today by the use of different materials (such as
      <a href="/wiki/Marble" title="Marble">
       marble
      </a>
      ). The large clock was added in 1779.
     </p>
     <p>
      The tower has two bells: the
      <i>
       Marangona
      </i>
      signals fires, work times, and the hours of the day, while the largest, called
      <i>
       Rengo
      </i>
      , is used to call the population to arms or to invoke the city's councils.
     </p>
