         <h1 class="entry-title cb-entry-title cb-single-title" itemprop="headline">
          50 Bloggers + One Dress + Instagram = Sold Out! UPDATE: Bloggers Did Not Disclose the Collab
         </h1>
         <div class="cb-byline">
          <div class="cb-author cb-byline-element">
           <i class="fa fa-user">
           </i>
           <a href="http://heartifb.com/author/kphilipk/">
            Kristen Philipkoski
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="cb-date cb-byline-element">
           <i class="fa fa-clock-o">
           </i>
           <time class="updated" datetime="2015-04-06">
            April 6, 2015
           </time>
          </div>
          <div class="cb-category cb-byline-element">
           <i class="fa fa-folder">
           </i>
           <a href="http://heartifb.com/category/business-of-bloging/" title="View all posts in Business of Blogging">
            Business of Blogging
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://heartifb.com/category/tools/" title="View all posts in tools">
            tools
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="cb-comments cb-byline-element">
           <span>
            <i class="fa fa-comment">
            </i>
            <a href="http://heartifb.com/2015/04/06/50-bloggers-one-dress-instagram-sold-out/#comments">
             21 Comments
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
        </span>
       </div>
       <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         <a href="http://ddwqcu8pss-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/lord-taylor.jpg">
          <img alt="lord taylor" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-132452" height="450" src="http://ddwqcu8pss-flywheel.netdna-ssl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/lord-taylor.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Lord &amp; Taylor paid 50 bloggers to wear the same print handkerchief dress and post photos of themselves wearing it on their Instagram account. The brand wanted to promote its new
         <a href="http://www.lordandtaylor.com/webapp/wcs/stores/servlet/en/lord-and-taylor/search/whats-new--1/contemporary-womens-design-lab-clothing?sre=PA_DLAB_MOD1_L1_WMNS_STAC" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lordandtaylor.com/webapp/wcs/stores/servlet/en/lord-and-taylor/search/whats-new--1/contemporary-womens-design-lab-clothing?sre=PA_DLAB_MOD1_L1_WMNS_STAC', 'Design Lab collection');">
          Design Lab collection
         </a>
         festival-ready designs a notch or two less boho than Free People.
        </p>
        <p>
         The scheme worked: The dress sold out in less than a week. Lord &amp; Taylor CMO Michael Crotty
         <a href="https://www.yahoo.com/style/50-bloggers-wear-the-same-dress-sells-out-within-115319182708.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.yahoo.com/style/50-bloggers-wear-the-same-dress-sells-out-within-115319182708.html', 'tells');">
          tells
         </a>
         Yahoo! News:
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          The goal was to make her stop in her feed and ask why all her favorite bloggers are wearing this dress and what is Design Lab?
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Askand buy!they did. Most posts got more than 1,000 likesLuanna Perez-Garreaud (@
         <a href="http://instagram.com/luanna90" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://instagram.com/luanna90', 'luanna90');">
          luanna90
         </a>
         ) got
         <a href="https://instagram.com/p/0x-HEVIyx6/?taken-by=luanna90" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://instagram.com/p/0x-HEVIyx6/?taken-by=luanna90', '82,000');">
          82,000
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         The campaigns success underscores the power of the Instagram + fashion bloggers formula. With 300,000 million active users and 25 times higher engagement than any other social media platform, the apps power to sell is becoming obvious to even the least social-savvy brands. And without the ability to link directly to product, campaigns like this (as well as statistics like those produced by Fohr Card), are providing numbers to back up the hype.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          UPDATE:
         </strong>
         However,
         <a href="http://www.racked.com/2015/4/6/8352599/lord-and-taylor-bloggers" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.racked.com/2015/4/6/8352599/lord-and-taylor-bloggers', 'reports');">
          reports
         </a>
         this morning reveal that not one of the 50 bloggers disclosed that the Instagram posts were sponsored, which is required by the FTC (read the rules
         <a href="https://www.ftc.gov/sites/default/files/attachments/press-releases/ftc-staff-revises-online-advertising-disclosure-guidelines/130312dotcomdisclosures.pdf" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.ftc.gov/sites/default/files/attachments/press-releases/ftc-staff-revises-online-advertising-disclosure-guidelines/130312dotcomdisclosures.pdf', 'here');">
          here
         </a>
         ). Its shocking, really, that no one at the brand and none of the bloggers would notice the oversight. The rules have existed since 2009 and were strengthened in 2013.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://marketingland.com/did-lord-taylors-instagram-influencer-campaign-cross-the-line-123961" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://marketingland.com/did-lord-taylors-instagram-influencer-campaign-cross-the-line-123961', 'Marketing Land');">
          Marketing Land
         </a>
         was first to
         <a href="http://marketingland.com/did-lord-taylors-instagram-influencer-campaign-cross-the-line-123961" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://marketingland.com/did-lord-taylors-instagram-influencer-campaign-cross-the-line-123961', 'report');">
          report
         </a>
         on the apparent violation, explaining that the FTC demands that disclosure must be clear and conspicuous. Lord &amp; Taylor has not yet commented on the debacle, but well keep you posted!
        </p>
       </section>
