             <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
              <div class="field-items">
               <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                <p>
                 Early January 2000 was a weird, different time: We had just survived the big scare of Y2K, but we had no idea what other crazy stuff was on the horizon.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Namely,
                 <a href="http://www.billboard.com/artist/432017/nsync/chart" target="_blank">
                  <strong>
                   *NSYNC
                  </strong>
                 </a>
                 's iconic "Bye Bye Bye," which officially
                 <a href="http://www.mtv.com/news/1432527/nsync-lines-up-release-dates-in-wake-of-settlement/" target="_blank">
                  dropped
                 </a>
                 as a radio single 15 years ago this week.
                </p>
                <p>
                 That's right: Awkwardly doing the "Bye Bye Bye" arm moves to show that you can, in fact, dance is a tradition that's been going on for
                 <em>
                  fifteen years
                 </em>
                 .
                </p>
                <iframe frameborder="0" height="200" src="//giphy.com/embed/cazcsyz5IBqEM" width="400">
                </iframe>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                  What have we forgotten about
                  <a href="http://www.billboard.com/artist/1490153/justin-timberlake/chart" target="_blank">
                   Timberlake
                  </a>
                  &amp; Co. as puppets in the years since? Well...
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 1. In "Bye Bye Bye's" episode of
                 <em>
                  Making the Video
                 </em>
                 , JC Chasez explained why, exactly, Chris and Joey had to
                 <em>
                  run atop a moving train
                 </em>
                 . "The song is a love song, but it's about kissing somebody off after they burned you," JC
                 <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5qqzCbAE2lY" target="_blank">
                  explained
                 </a>
                 . "Now they want you back? Sorry! So that's what the video is about, but this is more of a freaky control situation."
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: center;">
                 <a href="http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6193459/n-sync-new-album-lance-bass" target="_blank">
                  'N Sync Just Dropped A Brand-New Album -- But No One Told The Band
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 2. "Bye Bye Bye" was nominated for Video of the Year at the 2000 MTV Video Music Awards, but -- injustice! -- it didn't win, losing to
                 <strong>
                  Eminem
                 </strong>
                 's "The Real Slim Shady." "Bye Bye Bye"
                 <em>
                  did
                 </em>
                 win Best Pop Video, so the string-less dancing was honored in some way.
                </p>
                <p>
                 3. Someone on Team *NSYNC loved Kim Smith (
                 <em>
                  Friday Night Lights
                 </em>
                 ). In addition to playing the puppeteer in the "Bye Bye Bye" video, she also appeared in their vid for "It's Gonna Be Me." In
                 <em>
                  Making the Video
                 </em>
                 , Chris Kirkpatrick made this valid point about Smith to Joey Fatone: "Did you see how fine that girl is that's chasing us? Why were we running?"
                </p>
                <p>
                 4. The iconic moves were choreographed by Darrin Henson, whom early '00s music fans will recognize from the
                 <em>
                  Darrin's Dance Groove
                 </em>
                 infomercials. (Show of hands if you subsequently ordered the DVD and came to the harsh realization that you were not as good of a dancer as Justin Timberlake.)
                </p>
                <p>
                 5. The song, off
                 <em>
                  No Strings Attached
                 </em>
                 , peaked at No. 4 on the
                 <a href="http://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100" target="_blank">
                  Billboard Hot 100
                 </a>
                 . On
                 <em>
                  Total Request Live,
                 </em>
                 it remained the No. 1 video for 25 days. What a time!
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                  Throwback Thursday demands you re-watch the guys lose their strings below.
                 </span>
                </p>
                <iframe frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Eo-KmOd3i7s?wmode=transparent" style="font-size: 14px;" width="560">
