             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               Though Texas Sen. Ted Cruz announced his 2016 run on Twitter, it seems the presidential hopeful may have overlooked one key element of his digital strategy: the ever-important domain name.
              </p>
              <p>
               Rather than providing relevant information on the first major candidate to officially announce a 2016 run, TedCruz.com heralds support for President Obama and the need for immigration reform. Ouch.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/03/13/ted-cruz-nasa-comments/">
                 Scientists to Senator Ted Cruz: NASA needs to study Earth
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               <a href="tedcruzforamerica.com" target="_blank">
                TedCruzForAmerica.com
               </a>
               meanwhile redirects to Healthcare.gov.
              </p>
              <p>
               Cruz's official campaign has been relegated to
               <a href="http://www.tedcruz.org/" target="_blank">
                TedCruz.org
               </a>
               for the time being.
              </p>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="Screen Shot 2015-03-23 at 9.08.09 AM" class="" data-caption="Screenshot of the TedCruz.com website." data-credit-link="http://www.tedcruz.com/" data-credit-name="TedCruz.com" data-credit-provider="" data-fragment="m!4533" data-image="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzAzLzIzLzA5L1NjcmVlblNob3QyLmFjZTVmLnBuZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/e2f7bde5/ff1/Screen-Shot-2015-03-23-at-9.08.09-AM.png" data-micro="1" data-width="1019" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzAzLzIzLzA5L1NjcmVlblNob3QyLmFjZTVmLnBuZwpwCXRodW1iCTEyMDB4OTYwMD4/e2f7bde5/ff1/Screen-Shot-2015-03-23-at-9.08.09-AM.png" title="Screen Shot 2015-03-23 at 9.08.09 AM"/>
               </p>
               <figcaption class="image-caption">
                <p>
                 Screenshot of the TedCruz.com website.
                </p>
               </figcaption>
               <div class="image-credit">
                <p>
                 Image:
                 <a href="http://www.tedcruz.com/" target="_blank">
                  TedCruz.com
                 </a>
                </p>
               </div>
              </figure>
              <p>
               TedCruz.com was created in 2004 and hasn't been updated since April 2014, but the message still throws relevant shade on Cruz's upcoming campaign. TedCruzForAmerica.com was just created on Monday.
              </p>
              <p>
               Cruz announced his candidacy just after midnight on Monday on Twitter, hours before a planned launch of his campaign at Liberty University in Lynchburg, Virginia.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                I'm running for President and I hope to earn your support!
                <a href="http://t.co/0UTqaIoytP" target="_blank">
                 pic.twitter.com/0UTqaIoytP
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                 Ted Cruz (@tedcruz)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/tedcruz/status/579857596191899648" target="_blank">
                 March 23, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               Cruz is not alone on overlooking the importance of the domain name. Wisconsin Rep. Paul Ryan, who has already said he has
               <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/politics/2015/01/12/paul-ryan-says-is-not-running-for-president-in-2016/" target="_blank">
                no intention to run in 2016
               </a>
               , has his own domain name issues.
              </p>
              <p>
               The former Republican VP candidate doesn't own PaulRyan.com, which redirects instead to a music site.
              </p>
              <p>
               Gov. Jeb Bush said in December that he is "
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/12/16/jeb-bush-pac-president/">
                actively exploring
               </a>
               " a bid for 2016, but he is out of luck if he wants to use the
               <a href="http://www.jebbushforpresident.com/" target="_blank">
                JebBushForPresident.com
               </a>
               domain name. A gay couple from Portland, Oregon owns that one.
              </p>
              <p>
               The pair
               <a href="http://www.politico.com/blogs/media/2015/02/gay-couple-who-own-jeb-site-its-an-opportunity-202349.html" target="_blank">
                purchased the domain
               </a>
               in 2008 after they heard of some of his harsh views on gay rights issues.
              </p>
              <p>
               The Democrats seem to have get the whole domain thing down. HillaryClinton.com is currently functioning as a holding site, owned by "
               <a href="http://hillaryclinton.com/" target="_blank">
                Friends of Hillary
               </a>
               ." Elizabeth Warren, seen by many as another Democratic frontrunner owns the rights to ElizabethWarren.com.
              </p>
