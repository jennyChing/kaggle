        <h3>
         Bedside cabinet using reclaimed wood
        </h3>
        <h1>
         There are plenty of ways to use a reclaimed wood pallet for 
		furniture. In this project you can make a couple of bedside cabinets 
		using a wood pallet - or you can pop into your local
         <a href="http://www.builders.co.za" target="_blank">
          Builders Warehouse
         </a>
         and grab some PAR pine to make one. If you want to age the piece so that 
		it replicates the look of old wood, you will find plenty of techniques 
		in the Home-Dzine
         <a href="../decorating/2012technique-index.htm">
          Decorating
         </a>
         section.
        </h1>
        <!-- Please call pinit.js only once per page -->
        <script async="" data-pin-height="32" data-pin-hover="true" data-pin-shape="round" defer="" src="//assets.pinterest.com/js/pinit.js" type="text/javascript">
        </script>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img alt="bedside table cabinet nightstand reclaimed timber wood pallet" src="../2014/jun/99.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img alt="bedside table cabinet nightstand reclaimed timber wood pallet" src="../2014/jun/98.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.instructables.com" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" class="auto-style1" src="../2014/jun/00credits.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img src="../2014/jun/00need.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Wood pallet* you may need a couple to have a choice of wood to choose 
		from
         <br/>
         Piece of 3mm plywood or hardboard for drawer base
         <br/>
         Wood nails
         <br/>
         Wood glue
         <br/>
         Antique wax
         <br/>
         <strong>
          <em>
           OPTIONAL: Wood stain / sealer / varnish
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          TOOLS:
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Crowbar
         <br/>
         Claw hammer
         <br/>
         Jigsaw, circular saw or handsaw
         <br/>
         Orbital sander + 80-, 120- and 180-grit sanding pads
        </p>
        <p>
         <img src="../2014/jun/00how.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Not all wood pallets are going to be in good condition. If you need 
		to remove mould or stains from the wood use
         <a href="http://www.plascon.co.za/products/product.asp?SectionID=1&amp;RangeID=10&amp;ProductID=117" target="_blank">
          Plascon Woodcare Weathered Wood Restorer
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://www.plascon.co.za/products/product.asp?SectionID=1&amp;RangeID=10&amp;ProductID=116" target="_blank">
          Plascon Woodcare Cleaner &amp; Brightener
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Use a claw hammer to remove all nails from the boards before you do any 
		cutting or assembly.
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/108.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The entire assembly is designed around two rectangular frames at the 
		top and bottom of the bedside cabinet. The sides, back and top are all added onto this frame. It is easier 
		to make the frame according to the sizes of wood slats you have on your 
		pallet, so you can design a bedside cabinet that works well with what 
		you have.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          GOOD TO KNOW:
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          The wood must be completely dry to avoid shrinkage after you 
		make the cabinet.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/107.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Select the nicest board for the sides, top and bottom. You preferably 
		don't want any boards that are badly ripped or have holes. In order to determine the size of the rectangles you need layout the 
		boards and measure the total width. This will determine the size 
		of the top and bottom frames. Cut all the boards to the same 
		length/height. Allow extra for a piece of wood at the front of each 
		rectangle to hide the frame and finish off nicely. Sand all the boards before you assemble. Use 80- and then 
			120-grit sanding pads if you want a rustic or rough finish, or 
			finish off with 180-grit for a smooth finish. Sand the top of the 
			bottom frame with 180-grit and apply antique wax so that the drawer 
			will slide smoothly in and out.
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/106.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          ABOVE: Rectangular frame with top board fitted. The front 
		strip still needs to be added.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Make the top and bottom rectangles and then cut and attach the top 
		boards using nails. Bear in mind that the rectangles won't be visible 
		once the cabinet is finished, so you can use rough boards for these. 
		They do, however, need to be cut to size to make matching rectangular 
		shapes.
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/104.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          ABOVE: Sides and back wrapped around the top and bottom 
		rectangular frames. Here you can see that a board has been added to the 
		front of the top frame to finish off.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Make sure all the pieces are straight and square before nailing in 
		place. You can always fix up any small uneven edges with a sander.
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/105.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          ABOVE: View from the top. You can see that not all the boards 
		are completely flat, but that lends itself to a rustic finish. If you 
		want a neater finish make sure that you use boards that are not warped 
		or bowed.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/109.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Make the drawer from the wood that you have and add a 3mm piece of 
		plywood or hardboard for the base. There are two sections for the drawer 
		front and you need to allow for this, so that both panels can be glued 
		together and then nailed to the sides.
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/102.jpg" width="450"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         After measuring up for the size of the drawer you need to make, cut 
		a groove 5mm deep and 30mm up on the front and back for fitting the 
		plywood or hardboard base. The hardwood base needs to be the same length as the drawer sides 
		PLUS 5mm each front the front and back grooves. Glue the base into the slot on the back and then attach the sides 
		using nails.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          GOOD TO KNOW:
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          If you don't have a circular saw to do this you can modify by 
		adding small brackets underneath the base to hold in place.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/103.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Cut out a heart shape in the top panel for the drawer front. You can 
		use a hole saw and drill, or use a 10mm wood bit and then insert your 
		jigsaw blade to cut out. Glue the two panels for the drawer front together. Once glued use 
		nails to attach to the sides of the drawer.
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/101.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Finishing
         </strong>
         <br/>
         There are various options for finishing off the bedside cabinet. Take 
		a look at the
         <a href="http://www.plascon.co.za/products/product.asp?SectionID=1&amp;RangeID=10http://www.plascon.co.za/products/product.asp?SectionID=1&amp;RangeID=10" target="_blank">
          Plascon Woodcare
         </a>
         range of products to select your preferred finish.
        </p>
        <p class="banner">
         <img src="../2014/jun/100.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         After seeing this project, Home-Dzine reader Alice couldn't wait to 
		get started on her own reclaimed pallet bedside table.
         <strong>
          BELOW:
         </strong>
         Here is her completed bedside table.
        </p>

         <img alt="diy reclaimed wood pallet bedside table" src="../2014/jul/427.jpg"/>
        </p>
