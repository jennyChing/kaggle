&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Youdontneed to search far into history to &lt;a title="start seeing traces of Discrimination Against Women" href="http://nobullying.com/the-real-deal-on-racial-discrimination/" target="_blank"&gt;start seeing striking evidence of discrimination against women&lt;/a&gt;. To start, just the study of human history and civilization is marked with the masculine prefix that designates male superiority. The battle for equality goes on and with it, &lt;a href="http://nobullying.com/violence-against-women-act/" target="_blank"&gt;millions of women are fighting&lt;/a&gt; for their right to be looked at and treated, regardless of their defining genitalia.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;|&lt;strong&gt;SEE ALSO: &lt;a href="http://nobullying.com/cyberbully-2015/" target="_blank"&gt;The Cyberbully Movie Review&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;|&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;&lt;strong&gt;History of Discrimination against Women: Past and Present&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://nobullying.com/equality-act-2010/" target="_blank"&gt;The road to equality between the sexes&lt;/a&gt; has been a long and bumpy one, with many obstacles and turns throughout its course. Just in the past 50 years a profound number of changes have taken place, and this has led many men and women alike to mistakenly believe that discrimination against women is something already in our collective past. Unfortunately, this is not the case.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;It is true that in many western cultures women hold an almost even footing with men. Women hold public office, perform advanced surgical procedures and run corporations just as men do, but in each of these roles women often earn less, face obstacles their male counterparts would never be confronted with, and are present in much smaller numbers than men.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Still considered a minority group in many contexts, &lt;a href="https://www.google.com.eg/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=1&amp;cad=rja&amp;uact=8&amp;ved=0CB0QFjAAahUKEwjrrIDe5pHGAhVEmtsKHbtXANs&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fmic.com%2Farticles%2F33643%2Fwomen-make-up-half-the-population-so-why-do-they-only-hold-20-percent-of-elected-offices&amp;ei=x9J-VautLMS07ga7r4HYDQ&amp;usg=AFQjCNGSqZrBa8xz6ZFgEWIliU1PygpiuQ&amp;sig2=76adBS7JXDkYZ713xi6T4A&amp;bvm=bv.95515949,d.ZGU" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.google.com.eg/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=1&amp;cad=rja&amp;uact=8&amp;ved=0CB0QFjAAahUKEwjrrIDe5pHGAhVEmtsKHbtXANs&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fmic.com%2Farticles%2F33643%2Fwomen-make-up-half-the-population-so-why-do-they-only-hold-20-percent-of-elected-offices&amp;ei=x9J-VautLMS07ga7r4HYDQ&amp;usg=AFQjCNGSqZrBa8xz6ZFgEWIliU1PygpiuQ&amp;sig2=76adBS7JXDkYZ713xi6T4A&amp;bvm=bv.95515949,d.ZGU', 'women make up approximately 51 percent of the worlds population');" target="_blank" class="external" rel="nofollow"&gt;women make up approximately 51 percent of the worlds population&lt;/a&gt;. This statistic shows that it is not because of their being fewer women in the world than men that &lt;a href="http://nobullying.com/racial-inequality/" target="_blank"&gt;this inequality exists&lt;/a&gt;. Instead, it is a long-held culture of discrimination against women that has created the binary between the genders.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Are you familiar with how to change &lt;a href="http://nobullying.com/gender-roles/" target="_blank"&gt;that perspective regarding discrimination against womenin the near future!&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;History of Discrimination against Womenin Developing Nations&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In developing nations where school attendance is not so highly regulated as it is in westernized countries, young girls account for 60 percent of the out-of-school population of children. This is due to the emphasis that many parents in developing nations place on the welfare of their sons. The young man is looked at as an asset in the family. The young woman is a supporter, someone who can stay home and help with household tasks while the men earn income and develop their education.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Girls are bought and sold as young brides without any consent on their own part. The rightful property of their father or brother, there are many girls who are left to the bidding of the family members with no protectors or supporters who have their best interests at heart.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In China, girls are given up for adoption at a rate much higher than men as families prefer to raise a son over a daughter in a highly regulated nation where they are limited to only one child.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://nobullying.com/gender-roles/" target="_blank"&gt;Gender discrimination&lt;/a&gt; is not always as evident in western nations like the United States. Throughout most of Europe and North America, women are full-fledged citizens with all the rights that men have. However, this does not change the fact that women are often treated differently than men. To understand the more subtle yet widespread discrimination against women happening today, you have to know where to look for it.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;Forms ofDiscrimination against Women&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Discrimination against women presents itself in a variety of ways, including:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
&lt;li&gt;In the workforce&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;In legal proceedings&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;In the home&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;In the medical field&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;In marketing tactics&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;In the education system&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;In the church&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Discrimination is something that women face in every country around the world. In the United States, &lt;a href="https://www.google.com.eg/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=2&amp;cad=rja&amp;uact=8&amp;ved=0CCsQFjABahUKEwiNvtby6ZHGAhUhGtsKHe9BAC4&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fprezi.com%2Fmemrjisuoeth%2Fsexual-discrimination-of-women%2F&amp;ei=GNZ-VY3SF6G07Abvg4HwAg&amp;usg=AFQjCNEA-LbDl3X69u_BmHRIGdkovxIQ8A&amp;sig2=JQoPwDWSmPI94W0jd2ncPA&amp;bvm=bv.95515949,d.ZGU" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.google.com.eg/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=2&amp;cad=rja&amp;uact=8&amp;ved=0CCsQFjABahUKEwiNvtby6ZHGAhUhGtsKHe9BAC4&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fprezi.com%2Fmemrjisuoeth%2Fsexual-discrimination-of-women%2F&amp;ei=GNZ-VY3SF6G07Abvg4HwAg&amp;usg=AFQjCNEA-LbDl3X69u_BmHRIGdkovxIQ8A&amp;sig2=JQoPwDWSmPI94W0jd2ncPA&amp;bvm=bv.95515949,d.ZGU', 'four in every ten women have faced or will face discrimination personally');" target="_blank" class="external" rel="nofollow"&gt;four in every ten women have faced or will face discrimination personally&lt;/a&gt;. Often this discrimination presents itself in the workplace, as women face barriers that center on childbearing and family obligations that many men do not.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The onset of discrimination history against womendoesnt start at the onset of a womans career, however. Gender discrimination starts young. It is present when girls are encouraged to sit still and be polite while boys are excused for boisterous behavior on account of their rowdy gender. Gender discrimination is present in schools where girls are asked to wear skirts and blouses, only to be chastised as sexual objects just a few years lateroften much earlier than any young woman is familiar with what this objectification points to.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Raising a daughter in this culture of discrimination is tiring and challenging, but it is possible. It is best to encourage girls to grow up informed of the common forms of discrimination they are likely to face, and to develop strategies from a young age to take responsibility and stand strong in the face of adversity.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;Discrimination against Womenin the Workplace&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a title="Discrimination against women is perhaps most common in the workforce" href="http://nobullying.com/discrimination-at-work/" target="_blank"&gt;Discrimination against women is perhaps most common in the workforce&lt;/a&gt;, where many women become personally affected by prejudice or expectations against them.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In the United States &lt;a href="https://www.google.com.eg/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=2&amp;cad=rja&amp;uact=8&amp;ved=0CCMQFjABahUKEwjWxq7d65HGAhUha9sKHUNlAFY&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fprezi.com%2Fvoxr8kc9r5ia%2Fwomens-rights%2F&amp;ei=BNh-Vda0IqHW7QbDyoGwBQ&amp;usg=AFQjCNElz1RvWcwL02lMx_NRs-ZDIm8ciQ&amp;sig2=gwnGtCSka_d-HG8Sa62uNA&amp;bvm=bv.95515949,d.ZGU" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.google.com.eg/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=2&amp;cad=rja&amp;uact=8&amp;ved=0CCMQFjABahUKEwjWxq7d65HGAhUha9sKHUNlAFY&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fprezi.com%2Fvoxr8kc9r5ia%2Fwomens-rights%2F&amp;ei=BNh-Vda0IqHW7QbDyoGwBQ&amp;usg=AFQjCNElz1RvWcwL02lMx_NRs-ZDIm8ciQ&amp;sig2=gwnGtCSka_d-HG8Sa62uNA&amp;bvm=bv.95515949,d.ZGU', 'women make approximately $0.72 for every $1.00 a man makes');" target="_blank" class="external" rel="nofollow"&gt;women make approximately $0.72 for every $1.00 a man makes&lt;/a&gt;. Men tend to start their career at a slightly higher pay rate, are more likely to be given raises, and are less likely to take time off of work to raise a family.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Women, on the other hand, are more likely to accept a low to moderately paying job and stay there without asking for regular raises or negotiating their salary. Certain researchers have looked at loyalty as a common trait among women in the workplace, suggesting that women are less likely to leave their place of employment due to a strong sense of nurturing and involvement in their current place of work.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Whatever the reason, it is statistically proven that the average woman will make a significant amount less than the average man over the course of her lifetime.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Women are often encouraged to embark on a certain career path due to her gender, and discouraged from attempting to play a role that has typically been reserved for a man. Nursing, education and childcare are&lt;a href="http://nobullying.com/gender-bias/" target="_blank"&gt; often thought of as good roles for females,&lt;/a&gt; leaving women very little space in more male-dominated fields like construction, finance and business.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In politics, women make up a small sliver of the total elected officials in the United States. Women account for less than a quarter of Congressional seats, making up about 18 percent of the 535 seats in Congress. There are only 20 female senators out of the 100 total, and only five female Governors across all 50 states.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;When running for political office women often face discriminatory questioning that detracts from their political viewpoints, being forced to respond to questions about mothering and their wardrobe at a much higher rate than men, despite the large percentage of fathers that hold elected office.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;OvercomingDiscrimination against Women&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Discrimination against women is something that many people have grown accustomed to, and that is a problem. Accepting and forgiving discriminationeven in small amountswill lead to continued discrimination throughout future generations.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;a title="Parents can address the subject of discrimination" href="http://nobullying.com/discrimination-and-bullying-laws-by-state/" target="_blank"&gt;Parents can address the subject of discrimination&lt;/a&gt; with their young daughters by opening a dialogue about what makes them feel different than the boys they know. Children are incredibly insightful about their bodies and social roles. Discussing potential forms of discrimination that are present in your daughters life can help her develop strategies to cope and more efficiently manage her role as a developing woman.&lt;/p&gt;
