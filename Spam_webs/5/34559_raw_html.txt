              Gaming is in its golden age, and big and small players alike are maneuvering like kings and queens in A Game of Thrones. Register now for our

              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/header/" rel="attachment wp-att-489567">
               <img alt="Fry meme header" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-489567" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/header2.jpg" height="453" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" title="Fry meme header" width="604"/>

             <p>
              You might have seen
              <a href="http://www.boredpanda.com/movie-poster-cliches/" target="_blank" title="Bored Panda: 13 popular movie poster cliches">
               this collection of movie poster clichs

              <a href="http://christophecourtois.blogspot.com/" target="_blank" title="Christophe Courtois blog">

             </p>
             <p>
              For simplicitys sake, Ive mostly limited this to titles from the current generation of hardware (Xbox 360, PlayStation 3, and Wii). Otherwise, wed be here all day.
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              Face-to-face
             </h3>
             <p>
              TV screenwriter Eddy Canfor-Dumas says, Conflict is drama. And he would know because he wrote
              <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0419372/" target="_blank" title="IMDb: Supervolcano">
               Supervolcano
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              Even non-narrative games like Pac-Man center around two forces in opposition, and the designers of game covers can express this in any number of ways. You cant beat the classics, however, and sometimes its enough to just have two characters demonstrating their antipathy by getting into one anothers face.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offender
              </strong>
              : Developer/Publisher Rockstars Bully: Scholarship Edition mixes up the formula with a difference in height between the two people staring each other down.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/headtohead/" rel="attachment wp-att-491428">
               <img alt="Face-to-face" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491428" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/headtohead1.jpg" height="528" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Face-to-face" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Face-to-face" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491428" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/headtohead1.jpg?resize=500%2C528" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Face-to-face"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              Guns pointed up
             </h3>
             <p>
              Modern video games are just straight-up full of guns, so it makes sense that many covers focus on their characters relationship to their hardware. Its hard to show people holding guns without making them look too threatening, however, so the workaround is apparently to show them casually pointing their weapon into the air.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offender
              </strong>
              : See that woman on the box for House of the Dead: Overkills Extended Cut? Shes pointing
              <em>
               two
              </em>
              guns up.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/gunsup/" rel="attachment wp-att-491427">
               <img alt="Gun pointed up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491427" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/gunsup.jpg" height="357" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Gun pointed up" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Gun pointed up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491427" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/gunsup.jpg?resize=500%2C357" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Gun pointed up"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              In the shadows
             </h3>
             <p>
              In this age of gritty reboots and cynicism, some video games have gotten pretty dark, and their covers reflect this in the most literal way possible. Apparently, the quickest way to convey to a prospective player that your game is for-reals serious is to design a cover in which your hero glowers in a poorly lit room.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offender
              </strong>
              : Limbos and Alan Wakes shadow-intensive covers make sense, considering the darkness inherent in their design. But why does a boxing game like Electronic Arts Fight Night: Champion need to go all monochromatic?
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/shadows/" rel="attachment wp-att-491429">
               <img alt="In the shadows" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491429" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/shadows.jpg" height="580" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="In the shadows" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="In the shadows" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491429" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/shadows.jpg?resize=500%2C580" style="border:1px solid black;" title="In the shadows"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              Screaming
             </h3>
             <p>
              Good visual art expresses emotion, and I cant think of a quicker way to do so than to just have a picture of a person screaming.
             </p>
             <p>
              Screaming probably appears on so many game covers because it can mean so many things, like anger, pain, fear, or aggression. But mostly, it gets us a bunch of pictures of people with their mouths open.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offender
              </strong>
              : Dead Space: Extractions screaming heroine was so memorable that gaming blog
              <a href="http://www.joystiq.com/2009/07/01/dead-space-extraction-box-art-is-terrified-and-screaming/" target="_blank" title="Joystiq: Dead Space Extraction box art is terrified and screaming">
               Joystiq
              </a>
              devoted an entire day to sneaking her into every image they posted on its site.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/screaming/" rel="attachment wp-att-491451">
               <img alt="Screaming" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491451" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/screaming.jpg" height="309" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Screaming" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Screaming" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491451" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/screaming.jpg?resize=500%2C309" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Screaming"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              Explosion in the background
             </h3>
             <p>
              A games box is often the first impression a title makes on us, and it only has a few seconds to let us know that its contents are fun and exciting. Some can accomplish this with brand recognition and scantily clad warrior princesses, but it never hurts to goose up the excitement a little.
             </p>
             <p>
              And in many cases, the shortest route to excitement is blowing some shit up.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offender
              </strong>
              : Developer Infinity Wards Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3 flips the script by featuring the only non-orange explosion in this image.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/explosion/" rel="attachment wp-att-491425">
               <img alt="Explosion in the background" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491425" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/explosion.jpg" height="472" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Explosion in the background" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Explosion in the background" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491425" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/explosion.jpg?resize=500%2C472" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Explosion in the background"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              Face in your face
             </h3>
             <p>
              Some games discard the old reliable guns and explosions in favor of something a little more character-focusedand nothing focuses more on a character than an extreme closeup of their face.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offenders
              </strong>
              : Super Meat Boy, SpongeBob Squarepants, Billy the Puppet from Saw, and God of War IIIs Kratos are so in-your-face that their covers dont have room for anything else.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/faces/" rel="attachment wp-att-491426">
               <img alt="Face in your face" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491426" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/faces.jpg" height="710" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Face in your face" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Face in your face" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491426" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/faces.jpg?resize=500%2C710" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Face in your face"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              From the waist up
             </h3>
             <p>
              If all those faces in your face made you uncomfortable, this batch of game art will be a little easier on your territorial bubble. It may not help that so many of the people on these games appear to be threatening you, though.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offenders
              </strong>
              : This may be the preferred format for superhero games. Wolverine, Spider-Man, Batman, Thor, and Green Lantern all appear from the waist up.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/waistup/" rel="attachment wp-att-491430">
               <img alt="From the waist up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491430" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/waistup.jpg" height="408" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="From the waist up" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="From the waist up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491430" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/waistup.jpg?resize=500%2C408" style="border:1px solid black;" title="From the waist up"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              Look at my back
             </h3>
             <p>
              Some characters are just rude. Here we are, just trying to pick out a game, and they cant even be bothered to face us. You cant take it personally all the time; occasionally, the subjects have their hands full with some kind of giant monster or an explosion. But others couldnt care less and look back over their shoulders as if asking if we like the view.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offender
              </strong>
              : Developer Capcoms Devil May Cry 4 manages to sneak in a back shot of series hero Dante, who appears behind Nero, the games new character. Nero, for his part, appears from the waist up (see above).
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/backs/" rel="attachment wp-att-491423">
               <img alt="Look at my back" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491423" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/backs.jpg" height="595" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Look at my back" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Look at my back" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491423" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/backs.jpg?resize=500%2C595" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Look at my back"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              3 people in a row
             </h3>
             <p>
              Three, as we know,
              <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPzAjiLr5Zw" target="_blank" title="YouTube: Three is a magic number">
               is a magic number
              </a>
              , which is probably why so many game boxes feature three characters standing in a row. Some of them mix it up by just putting three giant heads or torsos in the sky, but theyre not fooling anyone.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offender
              </strong>
              : Publisher Sonys PlayStation Move Heroes gets a special mention because it has not one, but two sets of three characters arranged in a row.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/3-people/" rel="attachment wp-att-491422">
               <img alt="Three people standing in a row" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491422" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/3-people1.jpg" height="708" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Three people standing in a row" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Three people standing in a row" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491422" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/3-people1.jpg?resize=500%2C708" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Three people standing in a row"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              Hero in the middle
             </h3>
             <p>
              Main characters are at the center of the action, so why not get superliteral and put them in the center of the cover? Also, make sure its just them, so nobody is confused over who the game is about.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offenders
              </strong>
              : War games like the Call of Duty and Battlefield franchises all favor this look.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/centeredsquare/" rel="attachment wp-att-491424">
               <img alt="Hero in the middle" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491424" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/centeredsquare1.jpg" height="516" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Hero in the middle" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Hero in the middle" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491424" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/centeredsquare1.jpg?resize=500%2C516" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Hero in the middle"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              The White Void
             </h3>
             <p>
              A lot of cover artists are into minimalism. Thats what I tell myself, anyway; the other possibility is that the designers of these covers just forgot to add a background.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Notable offenders
              </strong>
              : Its easier to name platforms and companies for this one than specific games. Wii titles, Electronic Arts sports franchises, and Red Faction: Armageddon publisher THQ love the White Void.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/whitesquare/" rel="attachment wp-att-491431">
               <img alt="The white void" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491431" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/whitesquare.jpg" height="504" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="The white void" width="500"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="The white void" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-491431" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/whitesquare.jpg?resize=500%2C504" style="border:1px solid black;" title="The white void"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <h3>
              In conclusion
             </h3>
             <p>
              If youve been looking closely throughout this article, you might have seen a few games pop up in more than one category. One title in particular contains four of the elements Ive discussed here, and for that, I am declaring it the Ultimate Cliche Box Art.
             </p>
             <p>
              Cover tropes: This is your king.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align:center;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2012/07/15/you-cant-un-see-these-11-video-game-box-art-cliches/call_of_duty_modern_warfare_3_cover-900x900/" rel="attachment wp-att-491513">
               <img alt="Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3" class="aligncenter wp-image-491513" data-src="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/call_of_duty_modern_warfare_3_cover-900x900.jpg" height="536" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3" width="400"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3" class="aligncenter wp-image-491513" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/call_of_duty_modern_warfare_3_cover-900x900.jpg?resize=400%2C536" style="border:1px solid black;" title="Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
             </p>
