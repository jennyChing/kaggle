        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Grow a Bumper Crop of Basil in Containers
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="author vcard">
          By
          <a class="url fn n" href="http://preparednessmama.com/author/admin/" title="View all posts by Shelle">
           Shelle
          </a>
          <span class="bl_sep">
           |
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="bl_categ">
          <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/category/gardening-2/" rel="tag">
           Gardening
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/category/herbs-medicinal-magic/" rel="tag">
           Herbs - Medicinal Magic
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <div class="fb-social-plugin fb-follow" data-font="lucida grande" data-href="https://www.facebook.com/shelle.wells" data-layout="box_count" data-width="450">
         </div>
         <div class="fb-social-plugin fb-like" data-font="lucida grande" data-href="http://preparednessmama.com/growing-basil/" data-layout="button_count" data-ref="above-post" data-width="250">
         </div>
         <h1>
          Basil and containers go together
         </h1>
         <p>
          I have basil growing in pots on my deck. I find that they get bigger when I can keep an eye on them.  Gardening on the deck also keeps pests (like those pesky rabbits) out of my plants.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/growing-basil/growing-basil-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-6870">
           <img alt="Grow a bumper crop of basil this year | PreparednessMama" class="alignleft wp-image-6870" height="375" src="http://preparednessmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Growing-basil-1024x768.jpg" width="500"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The kind of pot you use doesnt really matter, basil just needs at least 8 inches of depth to grow and the container needs proper drainage. Remember, this flavorful herbdoesnt like to have waterlogged roots. When it has too much water the plant will not produce.
         </p>
         <p>
          Use a good potting soil, not garden soil, and place your plants in full sun. They are going to need at least 6 hours of direct sunlight each day. Water every other day when its below 100 degrees and once daily when the temperatures go higher. Your plants may even benefit from some afternoon shade in extreme heat.
         </p>
         <p>
          Once a month, mix up an
          <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/diy-manure-tea/" target="_blank" title="DIY Manure Tea  Putting Manure to Work in Your Garden">
           organic fertilizer
          </a>
          and give your plants a good soak.
          <span id="more-6816">
          </span>
         </p>
         <!--Ad Injection:random-->
         <!--TBN BTF 300x250 pos 4 STARTS-->
         <div id="tbn-32b89c47-2161-4224-9b7e-a0021a7d9778">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           window.$tbn = window.$tbn || {"d":function(){}}; $tbn.d("32b89c47-2161-4224-9b7e-a0021a7d9778");
          </script>
         </div>
         <!--TBN BTF 300x250 pos 4 ENDS-->
         <p>
          If there is any hint of frost coming, bring your basil plants inside or cover them with sheets to keep the frost off the leaves. Use rocks or other easy to grab items to hold the sheet down. Frost will kill this tender plant FAST, so if you want to continue your harvest, dont skip this step.
         </p>
         <h2>
          The key to growing basil incontainers
         </h2>
         <p>
          The biggest trick to growing a bumper crop of basil is pruning it  always pinch it back to the next place where there are two leaves sprouting, and those will grow into two more stems. Start pruning when the plant gets about 6 inches tall. That will cause it to grow bushy instead of tall. Bushy = more leaves.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/growing-basil/growing-basil-pinching/" rel="attachment wp-att-6871">
           <img alt="The key to growing basil is pinching (then of course eating it) | PreparednessMama" class=" wp-image-6871 alignleft" height="600" src="http://preparednessmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/growing-basil-pinching-1024x1024.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          You can prune two to three times a week, preferably in the evening, if it is going to be really hot, so the stems dont bake. Once you start doing this regularly, you will know when your plant is ready for a pruning.
         </p>
         <p>
          Do not let the plant get flowers, which will signal it to stop leaf production and work on seeds. If you are keeping up on your pinching regimen, the flowers should not be a problem.
         </p>
         <p>
          Of course, do what you can to keep insects away.If pests are bugging your plants,try these
          <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/garden-pest-control/" target="_blank" title="The Gardeners Arsenal  10 Things to Spray, Sprinkle &amp; Brew for Garden Pest Control">
           natural remedies
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          UPDATE: This year I am trying straw bale gardening and have several basil plants growing in it. They are ding the best of any basil plant I have ever grown!
         </p>
         <h2>
          What will you do with your bumper basil crop?
         </h2>
         <p>
          Once you harvest your basil leaves, they will keep for about a week in the refrigerator. Heres what I do  wash them in cold water and place them in a plastic zip bag with some extra water. Store this is the produce section of the frig and remove what you need. This is also a great way to get enough leaves for a big basil cooking project.
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are just a few suggestions for using those wonderful basil leaves 
         </p>
         <!--Ad Injection:bottom-->
         <div style="">
          <center>
           <!--TBN BTF 728x90 pos 2 STARTS-->
           <div id="tbn-2bed5ecc-ba35-418d-8108-ba2c803cd11a">
            <script type="text/javascript">
             window.$tbn = window.$tbn || {"d":function(){}}; $tbn.d("2bed5ecc-ba35-418d-8108-ba2c803cd11a");
            </script>
           </div>
           <!--TBN BTF 728x90 pos 2 ENDS-->
          </center>
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Make fresh
           <a href="http://allrecipes.com/recipe/pesto/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://allrecipes.com/recipe/pesto/', 'pesto');" target="_blank" title="Pesto Recipe - AllRecipes">
            pesto
           </a>
           (every night!)
          </li>
          <li>
           Crush the basil leaves and
           <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/freeze-herbs-in-olive-oil-173648" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.thekitchn.com/freeze-herbs-in-olive-oil-173648', 'freeze them in ice cube trays');" target="_blank" title="Freeze herbs in Ice Cube Trays / Olive Oil">
            freeze them in ice cube trays
           </a>
           with olive oil
          </li>
          <li>
           Use fresh leaves on salads and sandwiches
          </li>
          <li>
           Make
           <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/how-to-make-basil-simple-syrup-and-what-to-do-with-it/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://ohmyveggies.com/how-to-make-basil-simple-syrup-and-what-to-do-with-it/', 'basil syrup');" target="_blank" title="Oh My Veggies - Simple Basil Syrup">
            basil syrup
           </a>
           for tea, pop-cycles and as a drizzle on fresh fruit
          </li>
          <li>
           Make
           <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/paula-deen/strawberry-basil-lemonade-recipe.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/paula-deen/strawberry-basil-lemonade-recipe.html', 'strawberry basil lemonade');" target="_blank" title="FoodNetwork - Strawberry Basil Lemonade Recipe">
            strawberry basil lemonade
           </a>
           , yum!
          </li>
          <li>
           Make an
           <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/calendula-oil-benefits/" target="_blank" title="Growing and Using Calendula  The Benefits of Calendula Oil">
            infused oil
           </a>
           , the process is similar to this post about calendula
          </li>
          <li>
           Make flavored vinegar
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://preparednessmama.com/dehydrating-herbs/" target="_blank" title="5 Ways to Dehydrate Herbs">
            Dehydrate your basil
           </a>
           leaves and powder them for later use
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Grow a bumper crop of basil by giving your plants proper soil, water and by pinching them regularly. Which shouldnt be too hard, since there is so much you can do with it. Share your basil ideas below.
         </p>
