        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Voices of Independent Writers
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://billhiatt.com/?page_id=132" title="About Me">
          Meet the host of Voice of Independent Writers
         </a>
         <br/>
         Information about books by the host can be found
         <a href="http://billhiatt.com/?page_id=5" title="Living with Your Past Selves">
          here
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://billhiatt.com/?page_id=432" title="Echoes from My Past Lives">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          This is a place for independent writers to promote their work and give you, the readers of this blog, a better idea of what is available. Click on the replies above or
          <a href="http://billhiatt.com/?p=278#comments" title="Independent Writer Promotions">
           click here
          </a>
          to see what various independent writers have to say about their work
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Writers, please reply to this post with your book information. For best results, include the name of your book (well, duh!), an indication of what genre it is, a sales pitch for it, any interesting information you want to share about yourself as a writer, and links to any sites from which your book may be purchases or other promotional sites (like your own author website, for example).
        </p>
        <p>
         Please note that it may take a little while for your comment to appear. (I have gotten enough spam recently that I have changed the settings to require all comments to be moderated before appearing.)
        </p>
        <p>
         OF course, I reserve the right not to include any material that I deem inappropriate. In particular, since I write YA literature, I want to do my best to keep the site teen friendly; for example, you cant advertise erotica here! Generally, use common senseif you wouldnt let a teenage son or daughter of yours read the book, dont post about it here. However, I am open to all appropriate genres. Actually, if enough different writers use this space, I will probably try to organize the material by genre eventually, but right now you can post for any genre right here.

           <p>
            Every train journey to and from work would always see me with my latest  horror find perched in my lap, trying to finish just that one more page before I had to relinquish my seat and reluctantly put my book away.
           </p>
           <p>
            Inevitably, the books that engrossed me for my meagre train journeys, while those around me plugged themselves into the latest from the top ten or something worthy from the Booker list well mine, to be honest, had either Zombies, Vampires or Werewolves in them and if I was really lucky, all three not very worthy or highbrow I know, but for me whenever I opened those pages and let reality fall away, it was like being a teenager again watching an old Saturday night horror flick and I loved it.
           </p>
           <p>
            Just like those old movies, sometimes they were good and sometimes they were not but even the bad ones were enjoyable in their own strange way. It was after finishing one of those well that wasnt the book I thought it was going to be novels, that I decided that I could probably give this a go myself so the next day I worked out a character list and their story arc and just started.
           </p>
           <p>
            So here I am over a year later with my Lanherne Chronicles trilogy now on Amazon and a prequel on the way. For my books I created a world seven years after the Zombie apocalypse but for a change I turned away from the usual gun toting American back drop (where lets face it the dead wouldnt really stand a chance) and set it in the UK around a small group of survivors who have made a Carmelite convent their home.

           <p>
            HEART OF THE WITCH
           </p>
           <p>
            Zerrick Dhur holds a dangerous secret. As the Pastors son, it is his duty to uphold Iahmels ways, the ways of the church. But he practices magic, feels it singing to him through the jungle outside the colonial town walls. As witch burning fever strikes his home town, Zerrick must decide whether to stay true to his fathers words, or follow the calling of his heart. In his journey, darkness and madness threatens. An untrained witch can lose his or her grip on reality.
           </p>
           <p>
            He meets several other lost souls in his search to tame the wild magic. A young woman with visions. An old native scout. A hidden city of magic users. As he draws nearer to the truth, he fears he will also have to face his greatest nightmare, the Ravenger, a demon of unspeakable power.
           </p>
           <p>
            In the end, Zerrick must decide whether witchcraft will lead him to goodness . . . or to evil.

           <p>
            <a href="http://justafterwords.files.wordpress.com/2012/12/wpid-1339973628314.jpg" rel="nofollow">
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            how best to tell you something about me? not an easy task, and replacing the bio that was here
           </p>
           <p>
            well, lets give it a go.
           </p>
           <p>
            i studied writing at university (poetry, to be exact. earning two terminal degrees). to be the next poet laureate was always my dream. to bring poetry back to the hearts and minds of an indifferent society seemed beyond hope. but, hey, its worth a try.
           </p>
           <p>
            after graduation i was tossed out into a world who, for the most part, only recalls poetry as an unremarkable six week period in junior high school. but i was undeterred poetry would once again be at the forefront of society.
           </p>
           <p>
            then reality set in.
           </p>
           <p>
            i have found publication for my work in various journals and magazines. my first  publication was at the age of thirteen, in omni magazine. this was in 1983. (wow, i am getting on in years)
           </p>
           <p>
            ive had my ups and downs. and through it all, have had my poetry. but i almost lost my own hope, and faith. as this is a bit autobiographical, ill tell you the down fall. i caught my ex-wife having an affair. days later, my son died from complications from a seizure he had.
           </p>
           <p>
            ah, but i handled the stress poorly, and almost lost my soul. eight of the darkest years followed those two events. my rage was fueled by anger, alcohol, drugs, and a stint in gaol. but the dream was still there.
           </p>
           <p>
            during those eight years i began to produce new poetry. i regained my voice, and vision. and helped ignite a spark of hope in a few others.
           </p>
           <p>
            join me on this journey. this blog is one venue for my work. i still find my way into print. and my book can be had on Amazon.
           </p>
           <p>
            join me.
           </p>
