         <p>
          As promised, here are photos from my entry to the Providence Festival of Trees gingerbread competition! I had a great time decorating this house and I feel pretty good about it considering it was my first gingerbread house ever!
         </p>
         <p>
          I made a template from foam core and assembled it first so I would knew where all the pieces went and to get an idea of how it looked once it was put together. It was like baking a giant jigsaw puzzle! I used all the pieces as templates for making the gingerbread pieces before baking.
         </p>

         <p>
          I used necca wafers to make the roof and asked (forced) my hubby to help me glue them on with royal icing
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://artisancakecompany.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
          It was the most time consuming part of the decorating process aside from actually baking all that gingerbread! The snow is melted marshmallows. I had to resist using my
          <a href="/2010/12/steampunk-gingerbread-house/">
           LMF fondant
          </a>
          on this house, but I wanted to keep it simple in terms of techniques.
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://artisancakecompany.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
          Made a very nice snowy look on the whole scene! A nice dusting of powdered sugar and disco dust set it all off
          <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://artisancakecompany.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
         </p>

          <a href="http://artisancakecompany.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/12/gingerbread_house_roof.jpg" rel="lightbox-7">

         </p>

         </p>
