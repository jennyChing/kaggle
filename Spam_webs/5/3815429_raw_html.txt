        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         21 Wonderful Places to Visit Before they Disappear
        </h1>
       </div>
      </header>
      <div class="entry-media">
       <figure class="base-image">
        <img alt="places to visit Dead Sea" class="attachment-big-size wp-post-image" height="185" itemprop="image" src="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Dead-Sea.jpeg" width="273"/>

          <p>
           We may be living in one of the last generations that can still visit some of the most beautiful places on Earth. Here is our list of 21 wonderful places to visit before they disappear!
          </p>
          <p>
           Places like animals and plant species are becoming endangered and soon some of the worlds most beautiful places will disappear altogether. Climate change coupled with pollution and global warming is affecting oceans, reefs, beaches and even cities causing drastic changes. The time is near when the most beautiful places on earth will soon disappear from our world forever! So its high time for you to rearrange your travel plans with these places to visit before they no longer exist.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Olympia, Greece
          </h2>
          <p>
           <a href="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Ancient-Olympia-Greece.jpg">

           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The ancient city of Olympia has been inhabited since prehistoric times and is the site of the first ever Olympic Games in 776 BC. It is one of Greeces top archaeological sites.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           In recent years the hot and dry summers have led to rampant wildfires, which have burnt surrounding areas close to the ruins. With the rise in temperatures and low rainfall in the area, its better to visit it sooner than later.
          </p>
          <h2>

          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/glacier-national-park.jpg">
            <img alt="places to visit Glacier National Park, Montana, USA" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2462" height="421" src="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/glacier-national-park.jpg" width="960"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Montanas rugged national park is a famous outdoor park with over 700 miles of hiking trails spanning beautiful lakes and alpine meadows.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           This Glacier National Park may no longer retain its name as of the 150 colossal glaciers that existed in the national park 100 years ago, only 27 have survived the test of time. According to scientists the surviving glaciers have a lifespan of 15 years; if the global warming of the planet continues at its present rate. Many species inhabiting the park require cold water, which means that the ecosystem of the park will drastically change once the glaciers are gone.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Taj Mahal, Agra, India

          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/India_Agra_Taj_Mahal.jpg">

           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           It is the most elaborate mausoleum, which was built in the 17
           <sup>
            th
           </sup>
           century in the memory of Mughal emperor Shah Jahans favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The city welcomes almost three million visitors per year, which may not be possible in the next five years as UNESCO and some preservation groups are urging India to close Taj Mahals visit. Air pollution, poor restoration, population explosion and huge influx of tourists have eroded the structures exterior. Until the palace is restructured, the days of close visit to the monument may be numbered.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Venice, Italy
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Amazon-Rainforest-Brazil.jpg">

          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The famous city in Italy is famous for being the Floating City.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Venice has been sinking on its wooden foundations for long. The city has subsided around 120 mm during the twentieth century and is also under the threat of frequent floods with the rising sea levels (which have risen almost 110mm during the same time period). Climatologists are devising several methods to save the city but are under dispute over the success of these methods.
          </p>
          <h2>

          <p>
           <a href="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/salar-de-uyuni-bolivia.jpg">
            <img alt="places to visit salar-de-uyuni-bolivia" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2476" height="1212" src="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/salar-de-uyuni-bolivia.jpg" width="1600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Salar de Uyuni in Bolivia has been famous amongst travellers for having the most mesmerizing salt flats on the planet. They also have half of the worlds lithium reserves, which are being actively extracted by the Bolivian government.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           As the demand for lithium batteries, used in smartphones is increasing, Bolivias untouched salt flats would soon become history.
          </p>

           Madagascan Rainforest

          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/madagascar-rainforest-compressed.jpg">
            <img alt="places to visit madagascar rainforest-compressed" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2473" height="400" src="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/madagascar-rainforest-compressed.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Madagascar, famous from the movies, is the place of worship for wildlife lovers inhabiting more than 50 species of lemur, two-thirds of worlds chameleons and giraffe weevil.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           While the documentaries show thick forests, the same is no longer true. Almost 90% of the islands original forests are gone and it is sad that many of the endemic species inhabiting Madagascar will be lost even before they are discovered.
          </p>

          <p>

           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>

          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Coral bleaching and ocean acidification are posing huge threat to its enchanting reefs. Also the rise in human population and tourism is changing the ecological face of this uninhabited island.

However its hard to predict when the government would finally extinguish the methane fuelled inferno or how long will the fire last by itself, but its certainly a sight you must see while you can.

           </strong>

          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The basin is under constant threat of deforestation, mining and illegal wildlife trade due to which environmentalists believe that a significant portion of the forests plants and animals could disappear by 2040.

           <strong>

           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>

           </strong>
          </p>

          <p>
           <strong>

           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Alaskas northern regions have seen a significant rise in temperature due to global warming in the recent years. Global warming heats up the Arctic at twice the rate than the rest of the world. This has melted the permafrost of the region causing an increase in coastal erosion, which will impact the polar bears and other inhabitants.

          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>

          </p>

           Patagonian Ice Fields, Argentina
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Patagonian-Ice-Fields-Argentina.jpg">

           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>

          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>

Due to rising temperatures and declining precipitation, many of the glaciers have been retreating fast. Its best to include the southern glaciers in your places to visit list now, before they are lost forever in the sea.

The UN Environmental Programme has predicted that Maldives will be the first nation to be lost to the ocean by the end of twenty-first century if sea levels continue to rise at their present rate.

          <h2>
           Great Barrier Reef, Australia
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Great-Barrier-Reef-Australia.jpg">
            <img alt="places to visit Great Barrier Reef, Australia" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2470" height="768" src="http://womenpulse.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Great-Barrier-Reef-Australia.jpg" width="1024"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Great Barrier Reef is one of the most bio diverse places in the planet and is the home to over 2500 coral reefs, 600 islands and over 1500 species of fish. It is so large that it can be seen from space!
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it going to disappear?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Australias great reef has lost about half of its coral reefs over the last thirty years because of increased tropical storms, coral bleaching and ocean acidification. If the sea temperatures and carbon pollution levels continue to rise, the coral reefs would face irreparable damage by 2030. What nature created in 8000 years may disappear within our lifetimes and hence me one of the places to visit on your next travel plans.
          </p>

          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Why is it famous?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Famous for its glaciers, the Alps sit at a lower altitude than the Rocky Mountains.
