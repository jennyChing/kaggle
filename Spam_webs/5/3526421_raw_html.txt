       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Culture in M&amp;A: Its Important, Now What?
       </h1>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
       <div class="beforepostmeta">
        August 12, 2013
        <span>
         |
        </span>
        Categories:
        <a href="http://mthink.mercer.com/category/infographics/" rel="category tag">
         Infographics
        </a>
        <span>
         |
        </span>
        Tags:
        <a href="http://mthink.mercer.com/tag/culture/" rel="tag">
         Culture
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://mthink.mercer.com/tag/ma/" rel="tag">
         M&amp;A
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Addressing culture is important in business transactions, no matter what the deal type or strategy. But organizations continue to struggle with turning an understanding of cultural differences into concrete actions that will help to successfully execute a transaction.
         <a href="http://www.mercer.com/culture-important-in-ma">
          New research from Mercer
         </a>
         , conducted among 68 major organizations from around the world that have completed multiple mergers and acquisitions, reveals how organizations can best address culture issues and drive workforce behaviors to achieve deal synergies.
        </p>
        <p>
         Despite knowing that corporate culture is important, organizations still struggle with how best to integrate culture into a definitive plan, says Chuck Moritt, senior partner in Mercers global M&amp;A consulting business.
        </p>
        <p>
         The keys to addressing culture in M&amp;A transactions are to begin the process early, engage leaders, and use data to inform integration planning, he notes. The final step before actual integration is to choose wisely  making targeted investments that will have the greatest impact in the near term.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>

           Learn more about culture issues in M&amp;A deals.
          </i>
         </b>
        </p>
