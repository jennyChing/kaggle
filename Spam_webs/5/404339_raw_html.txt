         <div class="title">
          <a href="/blog/babes-in-toyland-dctech-startup-style">
           Babes In Toyland - #DCTech Startup Style
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="category">
          April 29, 2014
         </div>
         <br/>
         <div class="story">
          <p>
           <img alt="" src="/upload/2014/nicey.png" style="width: 580px; height: 193px;"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           DC Tech is no Silicon Valley, in fact it's no Boston, nor New York, or Austin Texas. As a region the DC Metroplex is consistently ranked as the #8 startup community based on Venture Funding by the National Venture Capital Association (by the way with all the hoopla and noise thee past five years, we've slipped about 3 slots from 15 years ago). I'd like to suggest a reason we as a community are falling behind.... and it is an aversion to disgreement. Many founders in this town only want to hear good news. We're falling behind because we can't handle the truth.
          </p>
          <h2>
           You Can't Handle The Truth
          </h2>
          <p>
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/9FnO3igOkOk" width="560">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you're a business person and you want to surround yourself with yes men or women, zombies, clones, you're destined to fail. You've earned your failure. If all you want to hear are the positive stories, the good news, and filter out the cautionary tails, you are destined to repeat the failures of the past. If you don't want someone to say, hey look out, there may be rocks up ahead, you're sailing in dangerous waters.... even if it turns out the rocks have been removed... then go ahead and sail into the smooth deep waters... even if they're rocky shoals.
          </p>
          <p>
           Last weekend, I was reading an interesting thread in DCTechFacebook Group regarding a post by Bill Flook on the problem with intentional startup vagaries and a lack of transparency. Comments started showing up in response to someone named Robert. I could see responses to old Rob but couldn't see the any original comments to which these folks were responding. So in search of Robbie, I posted the following:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <span style="color: rgb(20, 24, 35); font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);">
             Wow! Funniest part of this thread about vagueness is the lack of transparency. People are referring to some "Mythical Ghost" named Robert and yet I must be blocked from seeing Robert's comments, I must not have the magic "ghost visibility" glasses that allow me to read this ghosts posts. Funny and difficult to think that there's a real startup founder that is afraid of a debate or having his information read. Never knew a great founder that was so wimpy they'd hide their thoughts.. I'm sure I'm missing nothing of any value.
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           .... and all of a sudden a ghost appeared with the following comment:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <span data-reactid=".j.1:3:1:$comment755104024535033_755983184447117:0.0.$right.0.$left.0.0.0:$comment-body.0.$text0:0:$0:0" style="color: rgb(20, 24, 35); font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);">
             Hi
            </span>
            <span data-reactid=".j.1:3:1:$comment755104024535033_755983184447117:0.0.$right.0.$left.0.0.0:$comment-body.0.$text0:0:$0:0" style="font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);">
            </span>
            <a class="profileLink" data-hovercard="/ajax/hovercard/hovercard.php?id=644060742&amp;extragetparams=%7B%22hc_location%22%3A%22ufi%22%7D" data-reactid=".j.1:3:1:$comment755104024535033_755983184447117:0.0.$right.0.$left.0.0.0:$comment-body.0.$range0:0" dir="ltr" href="https://www.facebook.com/ghellman" style="color: rgb(59, 89, 152); cursor: pointer; text-decoration: none; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);" target="_blank">
             Glen Hellman
            </a>
            <span data-reactid=".j.1:3:1:$comment755104024535033_755983184447117:0.0.$right.0.$left.0.0.0:$comment-body.0.$end:0:$0:0" style="font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);">
             ,
            </span>
            <span data-reactid=".j.1:3:1:$comment755104024535033_755983184447117:0.0.$right.0.$left.0.0.0:$comment-body.0.$end:0:$0:0" style="color: rgb(20, 24, 35); font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);">
             I am certainly not one to hide from a discussion about the Washington region's technology community.
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           Then another poster commented on why someone may want to block someone, possibly giving Robert an alibi and I responded to that poster with:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <span style="color: rgb(20, 24, 35); font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);">
             It takes a weak ego and a lack of curiosity to block people because you don't want to see what they are saying. It takes an incredibly insecure person to block someone so he can't read what you have to say in a forum like DCTech. A person who can't handle social media heat has no business calling themselves a business person. Sticks and stones may break your bones and names can never harm you. DCTech Facebook is a names sight....the real world of business is sticks and stones.
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           If you can't handle the heat in a Facebook Forum, you better go work in a nunnery because the business world is not about making friends. Well Bobby must have gotten angry when he read my critique and instead of responding with a counterpoint or contributing some learning he responded with:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <span style="color: rgb(20, 24, 35); font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);">
             Glen Hellman as soon as I get home you are going right back to status "blocked". There is no reason for you to attack someone for deciding to filter YOU out.
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           OMG, I'm in kindergarten. Excuse me Teacher, Mrs Maguilicuty, Robert's taking his ball and going home! Here's a guy who just said he'd never hide from a discussion about DCTech going back into hiding. Well you know me, Mr Sensitive, I had to drive that one last point so I thanked Bobby for his contribution with:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <span style="color: rgb(20, 24, 35); font-family: Helvetica, Arial, 'lucida grande', tahoma, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12px; line-height: 15.359999656677246px; background-color: rgb(246, 247, 248);">
             Robert, For someone who says he's "not one to hide from a discussion," you sure spend a lot of time hiding.
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <h2>
           You Want Me On That Wall
          </h2>
          <h3>
           You need me on that wall
          </h3>
          <p>
           You may not like hearing someone who disagrees with you but you know whatDCTech? You want me on that wall. You want someone who's cares enough to call bullshit when he thinks he sees bullshit and is willing to listen and learn from a response. You want someone who's willing to push a community towards growth. You want me on this wall.
          </p>
          <p>
           I have strong political views, yet when I watch the news, I don't just watch MSNBC or Fox News. I watch them both. Then I watch John Stewart on Comedy Central to get the facts... the real news, the truth. I don't just listen to people who agree with me.... I already agree with me and I know what I think.
          </p>
          <p>
           Go ahead you kindergarten babies and stick your head in the sand. Hide from opposing opinions, critics, and dissent. Pretend you're perfect... live in your pretend perfect world. I'd rather live in the real world where I compete for business. I rather have people take me down in the simulation of DCTech then get my ass beat in the real world of competitors.
          </p>
          <p>
           Lastly, you complement me by blocking me. You're blocking me says that I matter, and that your ego is too weak to handle me. In turn my not blocking you says, 1) I'm open to your criticism 2) I don't just listen to what I want to hear, and 3) I'm a life-time learner. You can't hurt me with your arguments, with your nay saying, with your disparagement. You can only help make me better. But then again, I guess I'm secure enough that I can handle the truth... or even someone else's false vision of the truth and I'm smart enough to tell the difference.
          </p>
          <p>
           Grow Up! Put on your big boy pants! Or why don't you take thee to a nunnery... where everyone is loved.
          </p>
          <p>
           ----------------------------------
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Note:
           </strong>
           <em>
            In my social media world, I've only ever blocked one person, My cx-wife. That's it. You can say anything you want to me or about me...I'm me, and not who you say I am. You can't hurt me, unless I hurt myself.
           </em>
          </p>
