       <div class="content">
        <p class=" leadin">
         All you want to do is kick off your shoes and relax when you arrive in your hotel room. But before you flop down on the bed, make time to do these eight simple things before anything else. Your health and safety may depend on it.
        </p>
        <div class="fare_ticker_holder">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.smartertravel.com/photo-galleries/editorial/eight-things-you-should-do-before-anything-else-in-your-hotel-room.html?id=651&amp;photo=64069&amp;max_photos=7&amp;source=44525" target="_blank">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="blog_image blog_image_1">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <small>
           (Photo:
           <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic.mhtml?id=110083901&amp;src=id" target="_blank">
            Hotel Bed
           </a>
           via Shutterstock)
          </small>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Check for Bedbugs
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Scrutinize your mattress, bed frame, and headboardyou're looking for small brown stains. Bedbugs are hard to see (they hide during the day and can run away quickly when disturbed), but they leave behind brown bloodstains when they process the blood of their victims. Be sure to check under the sheets and mattress pad, too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.smartertravel.com/photo-galleries/editorial/eight-things-you-should-do-before-anything-else-in-your-hotel-room.html?id=651&amp;photo=64068&amp;max_photos=7&amp;source=44525" target="_blank">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="blog_image blog_image_2">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <small>
           (Photo:
           <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic.mhtml?id=119046136&amp;src=id" target="_blank">
            Wiping Light Switch
           </a>
           via Shutterstock)
          </small>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Sanitize
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Your room may
         <em>
          look
         </em>
         clean, but that doesn't mean it's sanitary. Hotel cleaners may scrub the bathroom, but do they take the time to wipe down common safe houses for germs, such as remote controls, light switches, and doorknobs? Give these items a quick cleaning with an antibacterial wipe to stay healthy during your visit.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.smartertravel.com/photo-galleries/editorial/eight-things-you-should-do-before-anything-else-in-your-hotel-room.html?id=651&amp;photo=64067&amp;max_photos=7&amp;source=44525" target="_blank">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="blog_image blog_image_3">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <small>
           (Photo:
           <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic.mhtml?id=98553110&amp;src=id" target="_blank">
            Man Searching Under Bed
           </a>
           via Shutterstock)
          </small>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Check Hiding Spots
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Before letting your guard down in your locked hotel room, check these hiding spots to make sure you really are alone: under the bed, in the closet, and in the shower. An intruder could have slipped in before you and could be lying in waitor something more gruesome. You think I'm being alarmist? Maybe, but Snopes lists a number of
         <a href="http://www.snopes.com/horrors/gruesome/bodybed.asp" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          actual incidents
         </a>
         in which guests checked in to a hotel, complained about a horrible odor in their room, and later discovered a human body hidden under the bed or mattress. They had literally been sleeping on top of a corpse. Now who's the alarmist?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.smartertravel.com/photo-galleries/editorial/eight-things-you-should-do-before-anything-else-in-your-hotel-room.html?id=651&amp;photo=64066&amp;max_photos=7&amp;source=44525" target="_blank">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="blog_image blog_image_4">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <small>
           (Photo:
           <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic.mhtml?id=92965054&amp;src=id" target="_blank">
            Thermostat
           </a>
           via Shutterstock)
          </small>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Make Sure Temperature Controls Work
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Your room temperature may seem fine now, but it could become unbearably hot or cold once you're trying to sleep. If you wait until nighttime to discover that your climate controls don't work, you may be stuck if there's no maintenance staff on call to fix the problem and no vacant room for you to switch to.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.smartertravel.com/photo-galleries/editorial/eight-things-you-should-do-before-anything-else-in-your-hotel-room.html?id=651&amp;photo=64065&amp;max_photos=7&amp;source=44525" target="_blank">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="blog_image blog_image_5">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="speed_bump_container">
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          <small>
           (Photo:
           <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic.mhtml?id=38821432&amp;src=id" target="_blank">
            Hotel Bed Area
           </a>
           via Shutterstock)
          </small>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Remove the Comforter
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sheets and pillowcases: easy to wash. Bedspreads? Not so much. So some hotels just  don't. Even between guests. According to Reneta McCarthy, a former housekeeping manager for a major American hotel chain, it is possible that a hotel bedspread
         <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2011/TRAVEL/02/25/hotel.hygiene/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          might only be changed four times a year
         </a>
         . And you're not even safe with a duvet that has a removable cover: McCarthy says that if there is a top sheet between the duvet and the bed, the cover might not be washed between guests. So before you get into bed, take off the dirty bedspread!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.smartertravel.com/photo-galleries/editorial/eight-things-you-should-do-before-anything-else-in-your-hotel-room.html?id=651&amp;photo=64064&amp;max_photos=7&amp;source=44525" target="_blank">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="blog_image blog_image_6">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <small>
           (Photo:
           <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic.mhtml?id=158901410&amp;src=id" target="_blank">
            Alarm Clock
           </a>
           via Shutterstock)
          </small>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Unplug or Reset the Alarm Clock
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         An absent-minded or mean-spirited guest may have set the bedside alarm clock for 4:00 a.m. Make sure the alarm has been turned off when you arrive in your room, lest you be awoken or startled by an unexpected noise. Unfamiliar clocks may be tough to figure out, so if you have a cell phone, you can use that as an alarm clock instead. In that case, just unplug the bedside clock completely.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.smartertravel.com/photo-galleries/editorial/eight-things-you-should-do-before-anything-else-in-your-hotel-room.html?id=651&amp;photo=64063&amp;max_photos=7&amp;source=44525" target="_blank">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="blog_image blog_image_7">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <small>
           (Photo:
           <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic.mhtml?id=152094905&amp;src=id" target="_blank">
            'Do Not Disturb' Sign
           </a>
           via Shutterstock)
          </small>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Put the 'Do Not Disturb' Sign on the Door
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Unless you want housekeeping or a turndown service barging in on you unexpectedly, put the "Do Not Disturb" sign on your door as you enter. Just remember to take it off the doorknob in the morning if you want your room cleaned.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.smartertravel.com/photo-galleries/editorial/eight-things-you-should-do-before-anything-else-in-your-hotel-room.html?id=651&amp;photo=64062&amp;max_photos=7&amp;source=44525" target="_blank">
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="blog_image blog_image_8">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <small>
           (Photo:
           <a href="http://www.shutterstock.com/pic.mhtml?id=81324916&amp;src=id" target="_blank">
            Exit Map
           </a>
           via Shutterstock)
          </small>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Study the Fire Escape Map
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         It's unlikely that you'll ever need it, but you need to have a plan for how you will get out of the hotel in the case of an emergency. Take a minute to familiarize yourself with the fire escape route from your roomit could save your life.
        </p>
