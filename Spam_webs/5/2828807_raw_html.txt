      <div class="content">
       Even the game designers get it wrong sometimes.
       <p>
        <b>
         "This always works in the movies"
        </b>
        <br/>
        The
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Monster+Manual" title="Monster Manual">
         MM
        </a>
        says that staking a vampire
        <a class="populated" href="/title/send+them+to+the+next+dimension" title="send them to the next dimension">
         kills them
        </a>
        , but there are no
        <a class="populated" href="/title/called+shot" title="called shot">
         called
shot
        </a>
        rules and
        <a class="populated" href="/title/undead" title="undead">
         undead
        </a>
        aren't subject to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/coup-de-grace" title="coup-de-grace">
         coup-de-grace
        </a>
        . So you can't
actually
        <a class="populated" href="/title/stake" title="stake">
         stake
        </a>
        them.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "We don't need thumbs here in the Abyss"
        </b>
        <br/>
        By the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Monster+Manual" title="Monster Manual">
         MM
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/errata" title="errata">
         errata
        </a>
        , all
        <a class="populated" href="/title/outsiders" title="outsiders">
         outsiders
        </a>
        with an
        <a class="populated" href="/title/intelligence" title="intelligence">
         Int
        </a>
        of 6 or higher are
        <a class="populated" href="/title/proficient" title="proficient">
         proficient
        </a>
        with all
        <a class="populated" href="/title/martial+weapon" title="martial weapon">
         martial weapon
        </a>
        s. This includes
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Hellhounds" title="Hellhounds">
         Hellhounds
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Howlers" title="Howlers">
         Howlers
        </a>
        , and
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Nightmares" title="Nightmares">
         Nightmares
        </a>
        , among other
        <a class="populated" href="/title/quadrapeds" title="quadrapeds">
         quadrapeds
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "You backwoods Dwarves still dig, what ever for?"
        </b>
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Wall+of+Iron" title="Wall of Iron">
         Wall of Iron
        </a>
        produces real iron as an instant spell (so it can't be
        <a class="populated" href="/title/dispel" title="dispel">
         dispel
        </a>
        led and is
        <a class="populated" href="/title/non-magical" title="non-magical">
         non-magical
        </a>
        ) and by the price guidelines on
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Player%2527s+Handbook" title="Player's Handbook">
         PHB
        </a>
        96 &amp;
114 and the Avaibility guidelines on
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Dungeon+Master%2527s+Guide" title="Dungeon Master's Guide">
         DMG
        </a>
        137, you can
        <a class="populated" href="/title/hire+a+wizard" title="hire a wizard">
         hire a wizard
        </a>
        to
cast a spell that produces roughly 1800
        <a class="populated" href="/title/gp" title="gp">
         gp
        </a>
        worth of iron for only 500
        <a class="populated" href="/title/gp" title="gp">
         gp
        </a>
        in any small town or larger settlement. And yet iron mining
supposedly exists in the game world.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Like, you know, like that.. an stuff... anat, right?"
        </b>
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Simulacrum" title="Simulacrum">
         Simulacrum
        </a>
        produces a creature that has among other traits 51-60% of
the "speech" of the original. Does that mean that it leaves random
words out of ... or do it just use small words when talk?
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "All the commoners round here always die off at the same time every
year"
        </b>
        <br/>
        The
        <a class="populated" href="/title/extreme+heat" title="extreme heat">
         extreme heat
        </a>
        /
        <a class="populated" href="/title/extreme+cold" title="extreme cold">
         extreme cold
        </a>
        rules render much of the real world
uninhabitable as everyone in
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Fairbanks" title="Fairbanks">
         Fairbanks
        </a>
        in winter or
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Phoenix" title="Phoenix">
         Phoenix
        </a>
        in summer
takes enough
        <a class="populated" href="/title/subdual+damage" title="subdual damage">
         subdual damage
        </a>
        to render them unconscious in just a few
hours.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "But I'm as strong as Heracles, made my save, and was carrying a Spade
of Digging.."
        </b>
        <br/>
        Regardless of size, strength, tools on hand or reflex save result,
anyone caught in the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/bury+zone" title="bury zone">
         bury zone
        </a>
        of an
        <a class="populated" href="/title/avalanche" title="avalanche">
         avalanche
        </a>
        is
        <a class="populated" href="/title/pinned" title="pinned">
         pinned
        </a>
        and must
        <a class="populated" href="/title/wait+to+be+rescued" title="wait to be rescued">
         wait
to be rescued
        </a>
        rather than being able to dig their own way out.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "So that's what those pointy bits do..."
        </b>
        <br/>
        The
        <a class="populated" href="/title/20th+level" title="20th level">
         20th level
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Expert" title="Expert">
         Expert
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Trapmaker" title="Trapmaker">
         Trapmaker
        </a>
        with
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Search" title="Search">
         Search
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Profession%253A+Trapmaker" title="Profession: Trapmaker">
         Profession: Trapmaker
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Craft%253A+Trapmaking" title="Craft: Trapmaking">
         Craft: Trapmaking
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Knowledge%253A+Famous+Traps" title="Knowledge: Famous Traps">
         Knowledge: Famous Traps
        </a>
        and
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Knowledge%253A+Magical+Traps" title="Knowledge: Magical Traps">
         Knowledge: Magical
Traps
        </a>
        all maxed out at 23 ranks, cannot attempt to find a trap if the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Search" title="Search">
         Search
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/DC" title="DC">
         DC
        </a>
        is
over 20, but the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/1st+level" title="1st level">
         1st level
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/rogue" title="rogue">
         rogue
        </a>
        with none of those skills can.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "I know he fled into the woods, but first, we'll check at the inn"
        </b>
        <br/>
        A
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Successful+Wilderness" title="Successful Wilderness">
         Successful Wilderness
        </a>
        lore role to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Track" title="Track">
         Track
        </a>
        lets you track somebody for
up to 1 mile indoors, and in fact you get to retry more frequently
indoors than outdoors.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "................................ouch."
        </b>
        <br/>
        Since according to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Player%2527s+Handbook" title="Player's Handbook">
         PHB
        </a>
        121,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/free+action" title="free action">
         free action
        </a>
        s can only be performed while
taking another
        <a class="populated" href="/title/action" title="action">
         action
        </a>
        , and since you can only take an action on your
        <a class="populated" href="/title/initiative" title="initiative">
         initiative
        </a>
        , and since by Table 8-4 on
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Player%2527s+Handbook" title="Player's Handbook">
         PHB
        </a>
        128, "Speak" is a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/free+action" title="free action">
         free
action
        </a>
        : By a strict reading of the rules, you must wait until your
next action to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/cry+out+in+pain" title="cry out in pain">
         cry out in pain
        </a>
        after
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Being+hit+in+Combat" title="Being hit in Combat">
         being hit in combat
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Wall of Orca"
        </b>
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Running+away+from+someone" title="Running away from someone">
         Running away from someone
        </a>
        ? Need to block a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/passage" title="passage">
         passage
        </a>
        ? Well, have we got
a solution for you!
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Summon+Monster+V" title="Summon Monster V">
         Summon Monster V
        </a>
        allows you to summon a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/celestial+orca" title="celestial orca">
         celestial
orca
        </a>
        . The critter has (on average) 85
        <a class="populated" href="/title/hit+points" title="hit points">
         hit points
        </a>
        and it can
        <a class="populated" href="/title/thrash" title="thrash">
         thrash
        </a>
        around and attack anyone trying to get through. Much better than a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/wall+of+stone" title="wall of stone">
         wall of stone
        </a>
        . (And, since it just goes back to its
        <a class="populated" href="/title/home+plane" title="home plane">
         home plane
        </a>
        when it
"dies," you don't really have to feel all that bad.)
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "I'm not exactly feeling myself today, and neither am I"
        </b>
        <br/>
        A
        <a class="populated" href="/title/small" title="small">
         small
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/spellcaster" title="spellcaster">
         spellcaster
        </a>
        with a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/tiny" title="tiny">
         tiny
        </a>
        familiar can use
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Polymorph+Self" title="Polymorph Self">
         Polymorph Self
        </a>
        to
turn back into himself. And he can use
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Share+Spells" title="Share Spells">
         Share Spells
        </a>
        to have his
familiar also turn back into "himself" at the same time. There aren't
many spells that specifically allow you to break the normal
constraints English grammar has on
        <a class="populated" href="/title/reflexive+pronoun" title="reflexive pronoun">
         reflexive pronoun
        </a>
        s.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Wow, I didn't realize dragons beer-goggled that heavily"
        </b>
        <br/>
        The various "half-"
        <a class="populated" href="/title/template" title="template">
         template
        </a>
        s can be applied to any
        <a class="populated" href="/title/corporeal" title="corporeal">
         corporeal
        </a>
        creature
(
        <a class="populated" href="/title/alignment" title="alignment">
         alignment
        </a>
        permitting). This includes
        <a class="populated" href="/title/ooze" title="ooze">
         ooze
        </a>
        s,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/plants" title="plants">
         plants
        </a>
        and even
        <a class="populated" href="/title/constructs" title="constructs">
         constructs
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "I never Meta-magic I didn't like."
        </b>
        <br/>
        Silent
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Power+Word%252C+Kill" title="Power Word, Kill">
         Power Word, Kill
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        Still
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Burning+Hands" title="Burning Hands">
         Burning Hands
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        <i>
         How?
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Transmute Rock to Stone"
        </b>
        <br/>
        Type
        <a class="populated" href="/title/elemental" title="elemental">
         elemental
        </a>
        provides
        <a class="populated" href="/title/immunity" title="immunity">
         immunity
        </a>
        only to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/poisons" title="poisons">
         poisons
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/sleep" title="sleep">
         sleep
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/paralysis" title="paralysis">
         paralysis
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/stunning" title="stunning">
         stunning
        </a>
        and
        <a class="populated" href="/title/critical+hits" title="critical hits">
         critical hits
        </a>
        . So it is still possible for a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Gorgon" title="Gorgon">
         Gorgon
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Medusa" title="Medusa">
         Medusa
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Cockatrice" title="Cockatrice">
         Cockatrice
        </a>
        or
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Basilisk" title="Basilisk">
         Basilisk
        </a>
        to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/petrify" title="petrify">
         petrify
        </a>
        an Earth Elemental.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Trees; you can chop 'em down, but you can't change 'em with magic"
        </b>
        <br/>
        As per
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Monster+Manual" title="Monster Manual">
         MM
        </a>
        6, plants are immune to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/polymorphing" title="polymorphing">
         polymorphing
        </a>
        . Presumably this
includes
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Polymorph+any+Object" title="Polymorph any Object">
         Polymorph any Object
        </a>
        , even though the spell description uses
"vegetable" as a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/classifier" title="classifier">
         classifier
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "So much dropping a GP off the Empire State building"
        </b>
        <br/>
        An
        <a class="populated" href="/title/arrow" title="arrow">
         arrow
        </a>
        fired from a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/bow" title="bow">
         bow
        </a>
        or flung via
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Telekinesis" title="Telekinesis">
         Telekinesis
        </a>
        deals 1
        <a class="populated" href="/title/d6" title="d6">
         d6
        </a>
        or more
damage, yet the same arrow dropped from a great height can never deal
any damage, since it weighs less than one
        <a class="populated" href="/title/pound" title="pound">
         pound
        </a>
        (
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Dungeon+Master%2527s+Guide" title="Dungeon Master's Guide">
         DMG
        </a>
        89, column 2)
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "I command you to fall down and crush my enemies"
        </b>
        <br/>
        Conversely, a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Greater+Earth+Elemental" title="Greater Earth Elemental">
         Greater Earth Elemental
        </a>
        that falls 10' does 270
        <a class="populated" href="/title/d6" title="d6">
         d6
        </a>
        points of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/crushing" title="crushing">
         crushing
        </a>
        damage to everything under it (conceivably a 35' x 10' area). Since the first ten feet of a drop is
        <a class="populated" href="/title/subdual+damage" title="subdual damage">
         subdual damage
        </a>
        , to which elementals are
        <a class="populated" href="/title/immune" title="immune">
         immune
        </a>
        , they can make this ten-foot drop without so much as a scratch .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Azathoth's Gibbering Explained??"
        </b>
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Deities" title="Deities">
         Deities
        </a>
        are capable of casting
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Contact+Other+Plane" title="Contact Other Plane">
         Contact Other Plane
        </a>
        to converse with
themselves - but they "resent such intrusion" and even have a flat
        <a class="populated" href="/title/percentage+chance" title="percentage chance">
         percentage chance
        </a>
        of lying to themselves.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Mad cat!"
        </b>
        <br/>
        A house cat, accoring to the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Dungeon+Master%2527s+Guide" title="Dungeon Master's Guide">
         MM
        </a>
        , can deal 3 damage with two claws and
a bite. (Minimum
        <a class="populated" href="/title/damage" title="damage">
         damage
        </a>
        is 1
        <a class="populated" href="/title/HP" title="HP">
         HP
        </a>
        for an
        <a class="populated" href="/title/attack" title="attack">
         attack
        </a>
        .) And a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/peasant" title="peasant">
         peasant
        </a>
        has
        <a class="populated" href="/title/1d4" title="1d4">
         1d4
        </a>
        HP. So don't tick off
        <a class="populated" href="/title/kitty" title="kitty">
         kitty
        </a>
        !
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "It's Big, It's Heavy, It's Wood!"
        </b>
        <br/>
        Since a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/club" title="club">
         club
        </a>
        costs 0
        <a class="populated" href="/title/gp" title="gp">
         gp
        </a>
        , a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Limited+Wish" title="Limited Wish">
         Limited Wish
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/spell" title="spell">
         spell
        </a>
        can create an
        <a class="populated" href="/title/infinite" title="infinite">
         infinite
        </a>
        number of clubs, causing the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/plane" title="plane">
         plane
        </a>
        to collapse into a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/black+hole" title="black hole">
         black hole
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Why yes, I did bring weapons for the entire army"
        </b>
        <br/>
        Since the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/sling" title="sling">
         sling
        </a>
        has both cost 0 and weight 0, and you can generally
find ordinary stones for
        <a class="populated" href="/title/free" title="free">
         free
        </a>
        , there is no mechanical reason not to
carry a few million around in case you need to arm a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/peasant+uprising" title="peasant uprising">
         peasant uprising
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Dagger, Greatclub it's about the same"
        </b>
        <br/>
        The
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Dungeon+Master%2527s+Guide" title="Dungeon Master's Guide">
         MM
        </a>
        errata failed to amend the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Tarrasque" title="Tarrasque">
         Tarrasque
        </a>
        's
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Swallow+Whole" title="Swallow Whole">
         Swallow Whole
        </a>
        ability to
mention the usual
        <a class="populated" href="/title/stipulation" title="stipulation">
         stipulation
        </a>
        that you can only cut your way out with
a "light
        <a class="populated" href="/title/slashing" title="slashing">
         slashing
        </a>
        or
        <a class="populated" href="/title/piercing" title="piercing">
         piercing
        </a>
        weapon".
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "If the horses ever catch on, we're in trouble"
        </b>
        <br/>
        A
        <a class="populated" href="/title/whip" title="whip">
         whip
        </a>
        cannot even deal
        <a class="populated" href="/title/subdual+damage" title="subdual damage">
         subdual damage
        </a>
        to any creature with a +3 or
better
        <a class="populated" href="/title/natural+armor" title="natural armor">
         natural armor
        </a>
        bonus. This includes all
        <a class="populated" href="/title/horses" title="horses">
         horses
        </a>
        , which makes you
wonder about
        <a class="populated" href="/title/coachmen" title="coachmen">
         coachmen
        </a>
        and the use of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/riding+crops" title="riding crops">
         riding crops
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "It's all in the trunk"
        </b>
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Dire+Elephant" title="Dire Elephant">
         Dire Elephant
        </a>
        s, as described in
        <a class="populated" href="/title/MotW" title="MotW">
         MotW
        </a>
        , have a +23
        <a class="populated" href="/title/skill+bonus" title="skill bonus">
         skill bonus
        </a>
        to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Climb" title="Climb">
         Climb
        </a>
        ,
and are listed as having speed 40 ft., Climb 10.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "It's Not So Good Up Close When They're Standing Still"
        </b>
        <br/>
        Although it ignores armor (as it normally requires only a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/ranged+touch+attack" title="ranged touch attack">
         ranged touch
attack
        </a>
        to hit), and is specifically noted as creating a "
        <a class="populated" href="/title/magical" title="magical">
         magical
        </a>
        arrow",
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Melf%2527s+Acid+Arrow" title="Melf's Acid Arrow">
         Melf's Acid Arrow
        </a>
        cannot be used to perform a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Coup+de+Grace" title="Coup de Grace">
         Coup de Grace
        </a>
        against an
        <a class="populated" href="/title/adjacent" title="adjacent">
         adjacent
        </a>
        foe in the manner a normal bow can (
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Player%2527s+Handbook" title="Player's Handbook">
         PHB
        </a>
        133). For
that matter, neither can a sling, although a hand crossbow works just
fine.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Never trust a used weapon salesman"
        </b>
        <br/>
        Despite the really cool part about the ability not allowing a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/saving+throw" title="saving throw">
         saving
throw
        </a>
        , that's just
        <a class="populated" href="/title/clever+advertising+intended+to+distract+you" title="clever advertising intended to distract you">
         clever advertising intended to distract you
        </a>
        from
the fact that
        <a class="populated" href="/title/the+complete+destruction+effect" title="the complete destruction effect">
         the complete destruction effect
        </a>
        of a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Mace+of+Smiting" title="Mace of Smiting">
         Mace of Smiting
        </a>
        only functions when you deal a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/critical+hit" title="critical hit">
         critical hit
        </a>
        to a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/construct" title="construct">
         construct
        </a>
        - which are
not subject to the effects of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/critical+hit" title="critical hit">
         critical hit
        </a>
        s.
       </p>
       <p>
        And watch out for those
        <a class="populated" href="/title/vorpal+weapons" title="vorpal weapons">
         vorpal weapons
        </a>
        too, they have to be fabricated using
        <a class="populated" href="/title/death+spell" title="death spell">
         a spell
        </a>
        that doesn't exist in 3rd edition.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Butter-fingered Balors"
        </b>
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Balor" title="Balor">
         Balor
        </a>
        's
        <a class="populated" href="/title/vorpal+sword" title="vorpal sword">
         vorpal sword
        </a>
        is only a +1 weapon... This means that it's
almost entirely useless vs most other
        <a class="populated" href="/title/demon" title="demon">
         demon
        </a>
        s and
        <a class="populated" href="/title/devil" title="devil">
         devil
        </a>
        s. Why don't
they simply get a +5
        <a class="populated" href="/title/keen" title="keen">
         keen
        </a>
        or
        <a class="populated" href="/title/defending+weapon" title="defending weapon">
         defending weapon
        </a>
        ? Is that so that they
don't accidently cut themselves?
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "He's just faking it..."
        </b>
        <br/>
        Despite being blind,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Grimlock" title="Grimlock">
         Grimlock
        </a>
        s have a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Spot+check" title="Spot check">
         Spot check
        </a>
        of +3.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Class-ical Blunders"
        </b>
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Apprentice-level" title="Apprentice-level">
         Apprentice-level
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/paladin" title="paladin">
         paladin
        </a>
        s have
        <a class="populated" href="/title/lay+on+hands" title="lay on hands">
         lay on hands
        </a>
        as a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/class+ability" title="class ability">
         class ability
        </a>
        , but
have an
        <a class="populated" href="/title/effective+level" title="effective level">
         effective level
        </a>
        of 0.
       </p>
       <p>
        Wizards, druids, and monks do not have
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Unarmed+Strike" title="Unarmed Strike">
         Unarmed Strike
        </a>
        as a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/weapon+proficiency" title="weapon proficiency">
         weapon
proficiency
        </a>
        and the druid loses all abilities for 24 hours if she
tries to punch someone, or if she
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Wild+Shape" title="Wild Shape">
         Wild Shape
        </a>
        s into an animal and bites
them.
       </p>
       <p>
        A
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Cleric" title="Cleric">
         Cleric
        </a>
        with the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Water+Domain" title="Water Domain">
         Water Domain
        </a>
        can
        <a class="populated" href="/title/turn" title="turn">
         turn
        </a>
        Fire Creatures like a normal
cleric turns
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Undead" title="Undead">
         Undead
        </a>
        . So Clerics with the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Water+Domain" title="Water Domain">
         Water Domain
        </a>
        can turn Red
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Dragons" title="Dragons">
         Dragons
        </a>
        . In fact, the only dragons that cannot be turned are white,
the weakest of all colors.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "Forget stone, build my castle out of dwarves!"
        </b>
        <br/>
        The
        <a class="populated" href="/title/disintegrate" title="disintegrate">
         disintegrate
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/spell" title="spell">
         spell
        </a>
        cast on an object will destroy
        <a class="populated" href="/title/10+cubic+feet" title="10 cubic feet">
         10 cubic feet
        </a>
        of
matter, no
        <a class="populated" href="/title/saving+throw" title="saving throw">
         saving throw
        </a>
        allowed. The same spell will only affect one
        <a class="populated" href="/title/dwarf" title="dwarf">
         dwarf
        </a>
        , who the caster must hit first with a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/touch+attack" title="touch attack">
         touch attack
        </a>
        roll, and
still gets a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/saving+throw" title="saving throw">
         saving throw
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         "In the eye of the beholder"
        </b>
        <br/>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Hill+Giant" title="Hill Giant">
         Hill Giant
        </a>
        s are described as "oddly
        <a class="populated" href="/title/simian" title="simian">
         simian
        </a>
        ", who "seldom wash" and
carry "filthy, and
        <a class="populated" href="/title/stinky" title="stinky">
         stinky
        </a>
        " possesions.
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Cloud+Giant" title="Cloud Giant">
         Cloud Giant
        </a>
        s are "handsome" and
"
        <a class="populated" href="/title/well-defined" title="well-defined">
         well-defined
        </a>
        " who wear "the finest clothing available." Hill Giants
have a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/charisma" title="charisma">
         charisma
        </a>
        of 17; Cloud Giants have a 13.
       </p>

      <div class="content">
       <p>
        An
        <a class="populated" href="/title/interesting" title="interesting">
         interesting
        </a>
        collection of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/e-mail+humor" title="e-mail humor">
         e-mail humor
        </a>
        , that points out some inconsistencies in a ruleset.  But then, it also makes fun of itself, by laughing at the wrong things.   Some of these catches are definite issues, but that's why the book is called the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Dungeon+Master%2527s+Guide" title="Dungeon Master's Guide">
         Dungeon Master's
        </a>
        <i>
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Dungeon+Master%2527s+Guide" title="Dungeon Master's Guide">
          Guide
         </a>
        </i>
        .  DMs are supposed to make rulings up as they go to maintain consistency.  But to address some of the comments that seemed most bogus:
       </p>
       <dl>
        <dt>
         <b>
          The extreme heat/extreme cold rules render much of the real world uninhabitable as everyone in Fairbanks in winter or Phoenix in summer takes enough subdual damage to render them unconscious in just a few hours.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Yup.  I've been in Phoenix in the summer.  And, if it weren't for
         <a class="populated" href="/title/air+conditioning" title="air conditioning">
          air conditioning
         </a>
         and drinking lots of water, odds are, I would've passed out had I tried to adventure around in that weather.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          Simulacrum produces a creature that has among other traits 51-60% of the "speech" of the original. Does that mean that it leaves random words out of ... or do it just use small words when talk?
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Simulacrum gives you a copy that can't talk as well as the original.  It just doesn't use big words.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          The 20th level Expert Trapmaker with Search, Profession: Trapmaker, Craft: Trapmaking, Knowledge: Famous Traps and Knowledge: Magical Traps all maxed out at 23 ranks, cannot attempt to find a trap if the Search DC is over 20, but the 1st level rogue with none of those skills can.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Yup.  Maybe instead of being such a specialist, he should've spent one level learning how to spot a magical trap.  But heck, I know Ph.D.s in the computer sciences that can't figure out how to schedule a meeting in
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Outlook" title="Outlook">
          Outlook
         </a>
         , which any first level
         <a class="populated" href="/title/secretary" title="secretary">
          secretary
         </a>
         can do.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          Running away from someone? Need to block a passage? Well, have we got a solution for you! Summon Monster V allows you to summon a celestial orca. The critter has (on average) 85 hit points and it can thrash around and attack anyone trying to get through. Much better than a wall of stone. (And, since it just goes back to its home plane when it "dies," you don't really have to feel all that bad.)
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Of course, you'd be even better off with
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Stone+Shape" title="Stone Shape">
          Stone Shape
         </a>
         , since its an
         <a class="populated" href="/title/instantaneous" title="instantaneous">
          instantaneous
         </a>
         <a class="populated" href="/title/transmutation" title="transmutation">
          transmutation
         </a>
         , and
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Dispel+Magic" title="Dispel Magic">
          Dispel Magic
         </a>
         won't just blow it away.  But then,
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Douglas+Adams" title="Douglas Adams">
          Douglas Adams
         </a>
         has already used the falling giant whale trick.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          The various "half-" templates can be applied to any corporeal creature (alignment permitting). This includes oozes, plants and even constructs.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         "Can" just implies the possibility.  Is there any reason my universe can't have a half-dragon ooze, who gained half-dragoness by absorbing the essence of the
         <a class="populated" href="/title/avatar" title="avatar">
          avatar
         </a>
         of
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Tiamat" title="Tiamat">
          Tiamat
         </a>
         after it was killed by the avatar of
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Bahamut" title="Bahamut">
          Bahamut
         </a>
         ?  There's no
         <a class="populated" href="/title/intercourse" title="intercourse">
          intercourse
         </a>
         requirement.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          A house cat, according to the MM, can deal 3 damage with two claws and a bite. (Minimum damage is 1 HP for an attack.) And a peasant has 1d4 HP. So don't tick off kitty!
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Yup.  The day after I got this in email, I was cutting my parents' Maine coon's claws.  He decided he didn't like it, so bit the fleshy part of my hand.  Using
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Improved+Grapple" title="Improved Grapple">
          Improved Grapple
         </a>
         , he then executed, as a
         <a class="populated" href="/title/free+action" title="free action">
          free action
         </a>
         , a
         <a class="populated" href="/title/rend" title="rend">
          rend
         </a>
         attack.   He then released me and ran.   This was a good thing, since I was now at zero hit points and staggered.  If I'd tried to do anything strenuous, I'm sure I would have passed out.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          A whip cannot even deal subdual damage to any creature with a +3 or better natural armor bonus. This includes all horses, which makes you wonder about coachmen and the use of riding crops.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         I'll admit to not being an
         <a class="populated" href="/title/equestrian" title="equestrian">
          equestrian
         </a>
         , but whips and crops
         <i>
          sting
         </i>
         .  The horse
         <i>
          thinks
         </i>
         it takes damage, so it adjusts.  Kind of like the
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Disable" title="Disable">
          Disable
         </a>
         power, now that I think of it.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          Dire Elephants, as described in MotW, have a +23 skill bonus to Climb, and are listed as having speed 40 ft., Climb 10.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Haven't read
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Masters+of+the+Wild" title="Masters of the Wild">
          Masters of the Wild
         </a>
         yet, but I'd bet that the Dire Elephant, as a prehistoric type of elephant, has claws, instead of
         <a class="populated" href="/title/vestigal" title="vestigal">
          vestigal
         </a>
         toenails.  Claws for climbing.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          Although it ignores armor (as it normally requires only a ranged touch attack to hit), and is specifically noted as creating a "magical arrow", Melf's Acid Arrow cannot be used to perform a Coup de Grace against an adjacent foe in the manner a normal bow can (PHB 133). For that matter, neither can a sling, although a hand crossbow works just fine.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Yup.  A
         <a class="populated" href="/title/coup+de+grace" title="coup de grace">
          coup de grace
         </a>
         is a full round action, where you very carefully aim a shot, then fire.
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Melf%2527s+Acid+Arrow" title="Melf's Acid Arrow">
          Melf's Acid Arrow
         </a>
         creates an arrow
         <i>
          that is flying at the target
         </i>
         , whereas slings, with their spinning, are a bit difficult to make a precise shot.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          Despite the really cool part about the ability not allowing a saving throw, that's just clever advertising intended to distract you from the fact that the complete destruction effect of a Mace of Smiting only functions when you deal a critical hit to a construct - which are not subject to the effects of critical hits.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Follow the
         <a class="populated" href="/title/Rule+of+Specificity" title="Rule of Specificity">
          Rule of Specificity
         </a>
         - more specific rules overrule generic rules.
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          Wizards, druids, and monks do not have Unarmed Strike as a weapon proficiency and the druid loses all abilities for 24 hours if she tries to punch someone, or if she Wild Shapes into an animal and bites them.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Monks do.  Why should wizards?  How many wizards really train in how to hurt someone with their bare hands?  
And druids lose their abilities if they
         <i>
          wield
         </i>
         a prohibited weapon.  How do you
         <i>
          wield
         </i>
         a fang?
        </dd>
        <dt>
         <b>
          A Cleric with the Water Domain can turn Fire Creatures like a normal cleric turns Undead. So Clerics with the Water Domain can turn Red Dragons.
         </b>
        </dt>
        <dd>
         Yup.  And, in general, it would take a 12th level
         <a class="populated" href="/title/cleric" title="cleric">
          cleric
         </a>
         , rolling a twenty on their
         <a class="populated" href="/title/turn+check" title="turn check">
          turn check
         </a>
         , to turn a
         <i>
          young adult
         </i>
         <a class="populated" href="/title/dragon" title="dragon">
          dragon
         </a>
         .  A 12th level party should be able to handle a young adult dragon, don't you think?
        </dd>
       </dl>
