        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Blog/SmileyFace.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         It all started in 2004, when she got engaged to fellow advertising executive David Hernandez andthey bought a tumbledown lakefront resort in Elkhorn, Wisconsin- 90 miles from their Chicago home.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_1.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Even though it needed tons of work, they thought it would make the perfect location for their wedding ceremony and, later, for weekend getaways.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Hammock.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Developed in the mid-1920s, the 25-acre piece of lake property they purchased served as home to many different businesses over the years. A speakeasy, a brothel, a modest vacation destination, and finally- a Catholic priest-run retreat for the Latvian community.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Wandawega_Lake_Resort.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         But to David, (whos half-Latvian) the rustic camping resort was sacred ground. His extended family had vacationed there every summer since he was born, and each nook and cranny of the property held special childhood memories. Going there felt like a fantasy, David says. It was a different world.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_24.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         When David initially took Tereasa out to see the place (several years before they got engaged) they left with a parting request to the elderly priest who owned it at the time: 
         <em>
          If you ever decide to sell this place, call us first.
         </em>
         
        </p>
        <p>
         The good news call came five years later, and the couple snatched up all 25 acres. On it, sat four buildings (and all the furniture inside of them), an archery range, a basketball court, shuffleboard courts, a garage, two piers, and some horseshoe pits.
        </p>
        <p>
         But years of deferred maintenance had left almost everything in a fragile state. Leaky, sagging roofs; critter squatters; and mildewed drapes were just a few of things that needed to be dealt with. So from the time they closed on the place in February of 2004, until their wedding date six months later, tons of friends pitched in on weekends to help them whip it back into shape.
        </p>
        <p>
         Their marriage celebration also revealed the potential of their new purchase. With 25 bedrooms divided among the main lodge
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designtripper.com/2010/09/stay-camp-wandawega/lodgeinterior-2385e" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_Lodge.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         an old hotel
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_23.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         and three small cabins
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://roamandhome.com/category/stay/usa/camp-wandawega" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_22.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://elkhorn-wisconsin.olx.com/wandawega-three-bedroom-cabin-w5453-lake-view-dr-in-elkhorn-iid-193412591#pics" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_25-1.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Camp Wandawega makes one heckuva place to entertain. Tereasa says, Its like summer camp for adults!.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tereasas decorating strategy is what she calls found, flea, or free, and she really has fun with the camping theme.She reupholsters chairs with wool blankets
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_Chair_2.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         and cable-knit varsity sweaters
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_Chair.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         She decorates walls with vintage, fish-shaped jello molds
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jacobhand.com" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_28-1.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         and old school art
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jacobhand.com" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_29-1.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         and colorful fishing lures
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_31.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         She also took this rundown kitchen from drab to fab on a $5000 budget
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_Kitchen.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         And check out this shot of the
         <em>
          amaaaazing
         </em>
         collection of Fiestaware they discovered inside a boarded-up wall in the pantry
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designtripper.com/2010/09/stay-camp-wandawega/fiestawareshelves-2933" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Fiestaware.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          SCORE!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are some shots from around the dark green-painted cabin that came with the property
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_6.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Before
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_10.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_11.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         And the light and airy afters
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_8.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Cedar_Cabin_9.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_19.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are some photos from the 3-bedroom, red and white cabin that overlooks the lake
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://elkhorn-wisconsin.olx.com/wandawega-three-bedroom-cabin-w5453-lake-view-dr-in-elkhorn-iid-193412591#pics" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_25-1.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It was also on the property when they bought it, but Tereasa has given it a sweet &amp; sunny facelift over the past few years
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_20.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_21.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designtripper.com/2010/09/stay-camp-wandawega" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_27.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont you just love those vintage rackets and that pretty painted porch floor? *swoon*
        </p>
        <p>
         The tiny green and white cottage (now nestled next to the green cottage) was a
         <em>
          big time
         </em>
         labor of love too
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_Very_Modest.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Plucked from a weed-tangled truck yard in Beardstown, Illinois for $500, the crusty little cabin could barely stand when they purchased it
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_Moved.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Tereasa had had her eye on it since she was knee-high to a grasshopper, so David and his brother hauled it 300 miles over to their property on Lake Wandawega
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_Moving.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The white circle on the map below shows where it sits now
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Wandawega.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         (Take a virtual tour of the whole place at
         <a href="http://wandawega.com/" target="_blank">
          Wandawega.com
         </a>
         !)
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://roamandhome.com/category/stay/usa/camp-wandawega" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_22.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         When Tereasa got around to researching her little cottage-that-could, she discovered that it had several prior lives. In the 1920s, it was an overnight rest stop for motoring tourists. After that, a rod-and-gun club used it for Friday night card games. Then in the 1950s, it served as the office for a trucking company. Eventually it became a storage shed and when Tereasa and David bought it, it was just sitting there, abandoned.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres nothing lonely about it today though
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_4.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Tereasa%20Surratt/Camp_Cottage_2.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         (
         <em>
          Photos by Aimee Herring
         </em>
         )
        </p>
        <p>
         and would you believe Tereasa loved it back to life in just three months and with a budget of $300!?
        </p>
        <p>
         I dream about moving an old cottage to a lake someday too, so of course this part of David and Tereasas Wandawega story was
         <em>
          extra
         </em>
         intriguing to me!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="//i268.photobucket.com/albums/jj29/LaylaPalmer/Blog/SmileyFace.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         ___________________________
        </p>
        <p>
         Tereasa wrote a beautiful book about her cottage-moving/restoring experience, and we thought wed give away a signed copy here on our blog this week. (T
         <em>
          hank you for joining in on the fun, Tereasa!
         </em>
         )
        </p>
        <p>
