          <p>
           As Christians all around the world watched Dr.
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariam_Yahia_Ibrahim_Ishag">
            Meriam Ibrahim Ishag
           </a>
           bravely endure unthinkable horrors of her captivity, release, re-arrest and now tenuous future, Americans in particular can take away one critical piece of knowledge. We absolutely do not want any form of the barbaric Sharia law introduced into our U.S. courts.
          </p>
          <p>
           Except its too late.
          </p>
          <p>
           Thanks to efforts by the
           <a href="https://www.aclu.org/religion-belief/bans-sharia-and-international-law">
            American Civil Liberties Union
           </a>
           (ACLU), Sharia law is well on its way to becoming the new standard in civil court cases. As usual, the ACLU is playing the guilt card and working against the intent of our First Amendment rights of free speech.
          </p>
          <p>
           Attempting to get the jump on Sharia, many states have already passed laws prohibiting the use of foreign religious law in their courts. Yet despite strong voter support for these measures, the ACLU is fighting to get them all overturned. Oklahoma was one such state and  sure enough  in 2013 a
           <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/law/2013/08/16/oklahoma-ban-on-sharia-law-unconstitutional-us-judge-rules/">
            federal court struck
           </a>
           down their efforts, ignoring 70% of the populations wishes that the U.S. Constitution take precedence.
          </p>
          <p>
           The ACLU claims it is necessary to
           <a href="https://www.aclu.org/blog/religion-belief/debunking-mythical-sharia-threat-our-judicial-system">
            consider religious law
           </a>
           (Sharia) when negotiating adoptions, custody of children, executing a will and/or settling disputes over private property rights, to name a few. What the ACLU fails to mention is that within Sharia law, women are considered property and thus have no rights, which means they have no say in court.
          </p>
          <p>
           Proponents of Sharia law also seem to forget that when foreigners come to America, they are supposed to assimilate into our culture, not the other way around.
          </p>
          <p>
           Consider the case of the
           <a href="http://www.jihadwatch.org/2010/07/sharia-in-new-jersey-muslim-husband-rapes-wife-judge-sees-no-sexual-assault-because-husbands-religio">
            2008 arranged marriage
           </a>
           in Morocco between S.D., a 17 year-old girl, and M.J.R., a man she did not know. Born to a Muslim father, S.D. was by default forced into Islam and had no choice but to accept her fate of the prearranged marriage.
          </p>
          <p>
           The teenager was introduced to the man at their wedding and one month later they moved to New Jersey so that M.J.R. could pursue his career in accounting.
          </p>
