         <div class="asset-content entry-content">
          <div class="asset-body">
           We've all done it, trying to force something into a slot the wrong way or, if you're extracommitted, acompletelydifferent shaped slot.
           <div>
            <br/>
           </div>
           <div>
            Downtime thinks this is just further proof we have evolved from primates. Because, just as an ape would, when it doesn't fit we lose our temper, more often than not breaking something in the process.
            <div>
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div>
             Well the new Buffalo USB Hub is here to stop you breaking stuff, youruffian.
            </div>
            <div>
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div>
             It takes USB cords even if you try and shove them in the wrong way up. Clever, huh?
            </div>
            <div>
             <br/>
            </div>
            <div>
             <img alt="buffalo-usb-hub.jpg" class="mt-image-center" height="150" src="http://www.computerweekly.com/blogs/it-downtime-blog/buffalo-usb-hub.jpg" style="text-align: center; display: block; margin: 0 auto 20px;" width="450"/>
             It's about to go on sale in Japan for a little over 10 and, as yet, there is no word on when it will be released worldwide. In the meantime, sit on your hands and try not to break any other cables and ports.
            </div>
