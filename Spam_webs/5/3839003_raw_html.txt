                           <div id="__w2_ws0mFab_container">
                            Stumbleupon at it's core is a social networking website that helps you discover interesting things online. With that said, as a "discovery engine" there aren't many that make discovery of the web based on your preferences quite like Stumbleupon does.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            With that said there are alternatives and other social networking websites similar to Stumbleupon from the standpoint of being a social network and providing interesting content that are also helpful in discovering new content online that is interesting, entertaining and worthwhile too.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            However, there are only a handful that I feel truly come close to resembling a true discovery engine online..
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            Those that I feel are worthy of such endorsements are among the following:
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            <b>
                             Reddit
                            </b>
                            : One of the most interesting and engaging behind Stumbleupon with a diverse crowd and a host of topics to choose from that cater to truly any genre, preference, interest and age group. If there was a website that one could meaningfully compare to Stumbleupon without calling it another Stumbleupon it would have to be Reddit.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            <b>
                             Pinterest
                            </b>
                            : It's a close call to me regarding Pinterest and the next website that I will mention. Pinterest in terms of discovery is off the charts. You could spend weeks on Pinterest and continue to discover things that you hadn't seen before online and even more so.. it's engaging content that adds to your existing knowledgebase which is very appealing to me and likely others too.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            <b>
                             Tumblr
                            </b>
                            : This social website continues to attract people from all corners of the web. There's so much to see and discover on Tumblr it's unreal. Brands of all kinds, big and small all want to be a part of this social website. For a visual microblogging platform, it goes without saying that there's something for everyone here. Regardless of whether you're talking about photos, animated gifs, video clips or your traditional text blocks.. there's a post for nearly any subject that you can imagine on this platform.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            <b>
                             Twitter
                            </b>
                            : For what it's worth and in spite of it's longevity, to me this was the first Stumbleupon or discovery engine of it's kind. The massive discovery on Twitter is truly unrivaled. It's audience is unmatched and even spending just a few minutes on Twitter leads to discovery that you don't necessarily find on other social network platforms. Twitter and Stumbleupon may be different by name, but in terms of being a great discovery engine, Twitter gets the job done just as well as Stumbleupon or any look alike ever would.
                            <br/>
                            <br/>
                            Surely there are others that may come to mind for such a list as this, but as I mentioned before, there are truly only a handful in my mind that resemble a genuine discovery engine of interesting and engaging topics online.
