       <h2>
        Choosing Your Accent Colour
       </h2>
       <div class="entry">
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         Accent colours form an important part of my interior design schemes. I often advise the simplest way to create a harmonious scheme is by staying neutral but livening up the neutral or monochrome tones with a bright, strong accent colour. By juxtaposing against the neutral canvas you will create a star-piece in a room, especially if its one specific item of furniture created in your chosen accent colour.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         <a href="http://blog-static.hellomagazine.com/celiasawyer/files/2014/05/celia11.jpg" rel="follow">
          <img alt="celia1" class="aligncenter wp-image-241" height="298" src="http://blog-static.hellomagazine.com/celiasawyer/files/2014/05/celia11.jpg" width="440"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         Below: A recent London mansion I designed. The accent colour is Ox Blood and is incredibly striking against the neutral elements of the headboard, bedside tables and other contrasting features.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         Another example of this can be found in this central London luxury home. See how the accent colour is not only used on the chaise, but runs through the scheme with cushions and coffee table accessories.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         <a href="http://blog-static.hellomagazine.com/celiasawyer/files/2014/05/2052__05.jpeg" rel="follow">
          <img alt="2052__05" class="aligncenter wp-image-238" height="193" src="http://blog-static.hellomagazine.com/celiasawyer/files/2014/05/2052__05.jpeg" width="453"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         Similarly, lime green has been worked through this modern kitchen (below). Take note of the colourful chairs and accessories, contrasting against the white and grey kitchen.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         <a href="http://blog-static.hellomagazine.com/celiasawyer/files/2014/05/2052__13.jpeg" rel="follow">
          <img alt="2052__13" class="aligncenter wp-image-239" height="265" src="http://blog-static.hellomagazine.com/celiasawyer/files/2014/05/2052__13.jpeg" width="525"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         Purples and aubergines are two of my favourite accent colours; I use specific hues in my schemes as much as possible. In this family TV room I offset the purple chairs with artwork and objet dart that complement the purple accent colour of choice. The purple chairs really stand out against the cream walls and carpet, giving the room that colour burst it needs!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         <a href="http://blog-static.hellomagazine.com/celiasawyer/files/2014/05/photo-3-3.jpeg" rel="follow">
          <img alt="photo 3-3" class="aligncenter wp-image-240" height="345" src="http://blog-static.hellomagazine.com/celiasawyer/files/2014/05/photo-3-3.jpeg" width="460"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         An example of getting the look with accessories can be found
         <a href="http://www.zarahome.com/webapp/wcs/stores/servlet/ProductPage?storeId=84009906&amp;langId=-1&amp;catalogId=80209906&amp;categoryId=806027&amp;productId=3692019&amp;keyWord=Velvet%20Cushion" rel="follow">
          here
         </a>
         ; This great velvet cushion by Zara Home comes in many colours to suit anyones scheme.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify">
         Celia Sawyer x
