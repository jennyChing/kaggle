                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Flowering dogwood tree versus kousa dogwood tree
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8647194251942900526">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="abstract">
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Vv1iJJkgj1Q/T0uLZNaZMWI/AAAAAAAAAVg/EyVabLU3HXY/s1600/Dogwood+in+bloom.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="" border="0" height="320" nda="true" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Vv1iJJkgj1Q/T0uLZNaZMWI/AAAAAAAAAVg/EyVabLU3HXY/s320/Dogwood+in+bloom.JPG" title="flowering dogwood (white blooms); tulip tree (large tree in background)" width="240"/>
                       </a>
                       <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                        <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
                         When I moved to my current home, I was pleased to see the previous homeowner incorporated both a flowering dogwood tree and a kousa dogwood tree in the landscape design. I have come to enjoy one variety over the other. Can you guess which one?
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                        <span style="font-size: 11pt; mso-bidi-font-style: italic;">
                         <strong>
                          Flowering dogwood
                         </strong>
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                        <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
                        </span>
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <div style="border: currentColor;">
                        <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                         <span style="font-size: 11pt; mso-bidi-font-style: italic;">
                          Flowering dogwood (
                          <em>
                           Cornus
                           <place w:st="on">
                            <state w:st="on">
                             florida
                            </state>
                           </place>
                          </em>
                          ) blooms in early spring. Often, the first sign of spring color in a wooded landscape comes from flowering dogwood. Notice in the picture above of a flowering dogwood how the branches project out horizontally from the trunk, while many other trees have diagonally upward projecting branches. The roots of flowering dog run close to the ground surface; so close that care must be taken not to nick the roots with the lawnmower. This ornamental tree grows 30 to 35 feet tall.
                         </span>
                        </span>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gf1PXGuoSqg/T33ci9u8rzI/AAAAAAAAAiw/2yp69r7tTyg/s1600/Dogwood+florida+fruit.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="" border="0" height="240" nda="true" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gf1PXGuoSqg/T33ci9u8rzI/AAAAAAAAAiw/2yp69r7tTyg/s320/Dogwood+florida+fruit.JPG" title="flowering dogwood fruit is small red berry" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="border: currentColor; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <div style="border: currentColor;">
                        <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                         <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
                          Birds and squirrels enjoy the trees red fruit, which is about the size of a kernel of corn. In the fall, the leaves of this deciduous tree turn reddish-orange.
                         </span>
                        </span>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-lPacuNnQ0K0/T33ci1Fck6I/AAAAAAAAAjQ/V9MfAP48WW8/s1600/Dogwood+florida+trunk.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="" border="0" height="240" nda="true" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-lPacuNnQ0K0/T33ci1Fck6I/AAAAAAAAAjQ/V9MfAP48WW8/s320/Dogwood+florida+trunk.JPG" title="flowering dogwood tree bark" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="border: currentColor; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                        <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
                         The bark of flowering dogwood is scaly.
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <div style="border: currentColor;">
                        <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                         <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
                          <strong>
                           Kousa dogwood
                           <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;">
                           </span>
                          </strong>
                         </span>
                        </span>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                        <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-i0JyefsugOg/T33cjjOklWI/AAAAAAAAAjI/SatRt0MP-WM/s1600/dogwood+kousa.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="" border="0" height="298" nda="true" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-i0JyefsugOg/T33cjjOklWI/AAAAAAAAAjI/SatRt0MP-WM/s400/dogwood+kousa.JPG" title="kousa dogwood" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="border: currentColor; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                        <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
                         Kousa dogwood (
                         <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;">
                          <em>
                           Cornus kousa
                          </em>
                         </span>
                         ), which grows to about 20 feet tall, blooms in late spring. The branches of kousa dogwood are closer together than the flowering dogwood. Stems, or suckers, grow straight up from branches, out from the trunk and even from the roots. There are more blooms on the kousa dogwood than on the flowering dogwood.
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="border: currentColor; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-AxYnCHKY0Os/T33cjaKg5cI/AAAAAAAAAjA/arKxFf5jCQ4/s1600/dogwood+Kousa+fruit.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="" border="0" height="320" nda="true" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-AxYnCHKY0Os/T33cjaKg5cI/AAAAAAAAAjA/arKxFf5jCQ4/s320/dogwood+Kousa+fruit.JPG" title="kousa dogwood fruit is a spiny, round red ball" width="298"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="border: currentColor; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">
                       T
                       <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: x-small;">
                        <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
                         he spiny red fruit of kousa dogwood is about 1/2 to 1 inch in diameter. Once the fruit falls to the ground, it commences to decay making it a messy tree to have near a walkway. Flies are attracted to sweet fruit.
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </div>
