          On top of this, evolution has been observed in the lab (see the links below)!  Normally, E. coli can't use citrate as a food source.  After growing in the presence of citrate for thousands of generations, the E. coli developed a biochemical method for breaking down citrate!
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Finally, I've found that most Creationist arguments are based on a logical fallacy called the False Dichotomy.  Basically, the argument relies on the false assumption that there can ONLY be either Creation OR Evolution.  They completely neglect a third (or fourth, fifth, etc) possibility.  True, we don't have any idea what that third possibility is, but in science, conclusions are never "either, or."  There's always the possibility of another explanation completely out of left field (it's what makes science really exciting).
          <br/>
          <br/>
          In other words, many Creationists believe that by poking holes in Evolution, they are making an argument FOR Creation.  If your opponent does this, you can call them out for using a false dichotomy and that evidence *against* Evolution isn't evidence *for* Creationism (possibly invalidating many of their points).  Likewise, you have to be careful of using the fallacy yourself - arguments against Creationism aren't the same as arguments for Evolution.
         </span>
