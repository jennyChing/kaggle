     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Salad That Regrows Itself
     </h1>
     <img height="526" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Veggies-That-Regrow-Themselves-2-790x526.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         February 26, 2014
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/salad-regrows/veggies-that-regrow-themselves-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/veggies-that-regrow-themselves-2" rel="attachment wp-att-5073">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         What if you never had to buy salad fixings again. Youll get pretty darn close using this healthy recipe. Made using fresh organic vegetables,
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/blog/2014/02/food-that-magically-regrows-itself-from-kitchen-scraps/" title="Food That Magically Regrows Itself from Kitchen Scraps">
          these ingredients can easily regrow themselves
         </a>
         with no degree from Hogwarts required. Its that simple!
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 head of romain lettuce, chopped
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 celery ribs, sliced
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 green onion, sliced with greens chopped
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            3 mushrooms, sliced
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            4 Cherry Tomatoes
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Tablespoons
            <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/greek-yogurt-ranch-dressing/">
             Greek Yogurt Ranch Dressing
            </a>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            handful of croutons (optional)
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/salad-that-regrows-itself/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            1 serving
           </span>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT5M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            5 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT5M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            5 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. This recipe was inspired by fruits and vegetables easily regrown from their kitchen scraps. Want to add more veggies than this recipe asks for? The image below shows you all of the possibilities. And be sure to check out the
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/blog/2014/02/food-that-magically-regrows-itself-from-kitchen-scraps/" title="Food That Magically Regrows Itself from Kitchen Scraps">
          full infographic for complete growing instructions
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="1000" grub-popupphotopath="/blog/2014/02/food-that-magically-regrows-itself-from-kitchen-scraps/food-that-magically-regrows-itself-1000x1000/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/food-that-magically-regrows-itself-1000x1000" rel="attachment wp-att-4965">
          <img alt="Food-That-Magically-Regrows-Itself" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-4965" height="600" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Food-That-Magically-Regrows-Itself-1000x1000-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Combine all of the chopped veggies in a large mixing bowl and add 1 tablespoon of the
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/greek-yogurt-ranch-dressing/" title="Greek Yogurt Ranch Dressing">
          ranch dressing
         </a>
         . Using two large wooden spoons, toss the salad until all ingredients are lightly coated. Add a pinch of salt and pepper, if desired, and lightly toss once more.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="712" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/greek-yogurt-ranch-dressing/greek-yogurt-ranch-dressing-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/greek-yogurt-ranch-dressing-2" rel="attachment wp-att-5037">
          <img alt="Greek Yogurt Ranch Dressing-2" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5037" height="427" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Greek-Yogurt-Ranch-Dressing-2-600x427.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Plate the salad with croutons and drizzle it with the remaining salad dressing, then serve.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="727" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/salad-regrows/salad-that-regrows-itself-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/salad-that-regrows-itself-2" rel="attachment wp-att-5076">
          <img alt="Salad That Regrows Itself-2" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5076" height="436" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Salad-That-Regrows-Itself-2-600x436.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
