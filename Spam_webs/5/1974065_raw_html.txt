          <p>
           At the Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC) later this month, Apple is expected to show off iOS 7, the latest version of its popular mobile operating system. What makes this launch special is that it's the first to be led by the company's design guru, Jonathan Ive. According to several recent reports, Ive has quite a few changes in store when the software officially launches. That's actually good news. Although iOS is still a favorite among smartphone users around the world, the operating system has been looking outdated compared with many of the latest competing mobile platforms. Under the supervision of Ive, there's a good chance that Apple will breathe new life into it with some truly new features that customers will find appealing. This eWEEK slide show presents a wish list of what we'd like to see in iOS 7. If Apple can deliver at least some of these suggested new features, iOS 7 could be a huge success. Take a look at the design changes and new features we'd like to see in iOS 7.
