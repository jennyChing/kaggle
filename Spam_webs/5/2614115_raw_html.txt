      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Vice podcast co-host and artist David Choe." src="/content/dam/images/3/6/z/p/z/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.36zry.png/1398115456537.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Vice podcast co-host and artist David Choe.

      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       Sometime last week, it emerged that street artist and Facebook investment millionaire David Choe had told a story in which he all but admitted to raping a masseuse while bragging about it on the podcast he co-hosts with actress Asa Akira.
      </p>
      <p>
       In his description of the event - which was first covered by
       <a href="http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.xojane.com%2Fsex%2Fdavid-choe-alleged-rape-rapist&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFgFiV0VB0cVEsB7oCXHR2LBiwcQA" target="_blank">
        XO Jane
       </a>
       and then picked up by
       <a href="http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fgawker.com%2Fsuccessful-street-artist-talks-about-how-he-maybe-raped-1564734308%2Fall&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHrnxjRqB-S4WArL-Ry_LhGy3NQvQ" target="_blank">
        Gawker
       </a>
       -Choe talks about visiting a straight massage parlour (that is, one which doesnt also function as a place to purchase the services of sex workers) and getting an erection halfway through. He says he struggled with how best to act on it, deciding that it was too creepy to ask the woman if she minded him masturbating while she continued the massage.In the end, he decides to just start masturbating in front of her. Never ask for permission, only forgiveness etc. Later, Choe describes taking the womans hand and putting it on his penis while he helps her take over. Of this moment, he says, Its like, shes definitely like not into it, but shes not stopping it either. He then forces her to perform oral sex on him, comes in her mouth and then unsuccessfullytries to convince her to have genital intercourse with him. You can read about the
       <a href="http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.xojane.com%2Fsex%2Fdavid-choe-alleged-rape-rapist&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFgFiV0VB0cVEsB7oCXHR2LBiwcQA" target="_blank">
        whole interaction here
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Whatever it is that Choe thinks hes describing can be boiled down to one thing - rape. When his co-host Akira tells him as much he skirts around the issue, finally concluding that while he admits its rapey behaviour, he is not a rapist. His reasoning? She said yes with her eyes.
      </p>
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Vice podcast co-host and artist David Choe." src="/content/dam/images/3/6/z/q/0/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.36zry.png/1398115456537.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Vice podcast co-host and artist David Choe.
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       But did it really happen? After XOJane and Gawker picked up the story, Choe released a statement claiming that everything he said was made up. Worse, now everyone is being mean to him. According to Choe, it sucks to be called a rapist, especially because he hates rapists. He hates rapists so much, that he thinks rapists should be raped and murdered.
      </p>
      <div class="ad" id="adspot-300x250-pos3">
       <small>
        Advertisement
       </small>
       <script type="text/javascript">
        titan.requestConditionalAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       What Choe claims to be is an artist and a storyteller. And like so many artists and storytellers who muck about in the swamps of controversial material, he cant help it if you dont get it. As he explains:
      </p>
      <p>
       Just like many of my paintings are often misinterpreted, the same goes with my show...Its my version of reality, its art that sometimes offends people. Im sorry if anyone believes that the stories were fact. They were not!
      </p>
      <div class="cT-imageLandscape">
       <img alt="Vice podcast co-host and artist David Choe." src="/content/dam/images/3/6/z/q/1/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.36zry.png/1398115456537.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        Vice podcast co-host and artist David Choe.
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       But what kind of subversive art Choe was attempting to create with a story that, like so many others, basically employs the rhetoric of a rapists justifications in a culture that all too often tries to make victims complicit in their own attacks? At the time of recording, he gave no indication that what he was saying was made up, preferring it seems to enjoy the thrill of saying things that might make his co-host uncomfortable. At one point, he even suggests that talking about the assault was turning him on. It seems unfathomable that he could now claim to be surprised that people concluded he was a rapist because he offered as fact an anecdote which positioned him as raping a member of the service industry. If Choe was looking for a way to wriggle out of this - indeed, if he were really the clever and subversive provocateur he seems to consider himself - he could have tried a lot harder than, I made it up because art.
      </p>
      <p>
       <iframe height="349" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ZKgOtFUyDBs" width="90%">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p>
       Some people have a zero tolerance approach to comedy about rape, and thats fine. Im not one of them. I believe that intelligent content can be created around the subject of sexual assault, and I also believe that, when done well, it can be hilarious, challenging and socially transformative. The best rape comedy Ive ever seen was Adrienne Truscotts recent show Asking For It: A One Lady Rape About Comedy Starring Her Pussy And Little Else.
      </p>
      <p>
       With deft skill and genuine bravery, Truscott explodes the defensive barriers thin-skinned comics and artists have erected to protect themselves from public criticism regarding their own lazy (and often violent) comedy. With relentless energy, she cleverly and raucously unpacks the layers of a rape culture which, among other things, dictates that comedy is sacred but womens bodies arent. Its brilliant and subversive and at no point does it take for granted the likelihood that its subject matter might be personally familiar to a number of its audience members.
      </p>
      <p>
       But there are few things trickier to nail than the use of rape as an artistic device. Despite the fervent insistence by purveyors of the trend that their work is edgy and confronting, the vast majority of artistic riffs on rape are basically a paint-by-numbers lesson in how to be boringly offensive (and offensively boring). As the comedian Patton Oswalt wrote in
       <a href="http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.pattonoswalt.com%2Findex.cfm%3Fpage%3Dspew%26id%3D167&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHiql_29OCFkBmcNNn3fTU2CuZ-Pg" target="_blank">
        an essay last year
       </a>
       , Any edgy or taboo subject can become just as hackneyed as an acceptable or non-controversial one if theexact same approachis made every time.
      </p>
      <p>
       Thinking about the difference between Truscotts artistry and Choes lack of it, Ive become convinced that we need to redefine what is currently referred to as rape comedy, rape jokes and now rape art. These labels are still too broad to articulate what it is that Choe (and
       <a href="http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.thedailybeast.com%2Farticles%2F2012%2F07%2F11%2Fwhy-daniel-tosh-s-rape-joke-at-the-laugh-factory-wasn-t-funny.html&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNF7jYGGh0y6D8HKwY4ukvhvUiCsQQ" target="_blank">
        Daniel Tosh
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fglobalcomment.com%2Fsam-morrils-rape-jokes-not-so-funny%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHyBLLOuFVS3KSBEsWZ5WzfBfzZ3w" target="_blank">
        Sam Morrill
       </a>
       and
       <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Ftwitter.com%2FEddieIfft%2Fstatus%2F224948300615401474&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFN9Ds-sBFVdYLKnC-ZvKfYo4KTrw" target="_blank">
        Eddie Ifft
       </a>
       and so on and so on) are really trading in. By minimising the impact of sexual violence, these jokes and stories dont position themselves with the survivors but with the perpetrators.
      </p>
      <p>
       Daniel Toshs response to a heckler who disagreed that rape was funny that Wouldnt it be funny if that girl got raped by, like, five guys right now? isnt a joke but an act of allegiance with the kind of people who think that rape isnt just funny, its a suitable form of punishment. As far as Im aware, we call those kinds of people rapists.
       <a href="http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.thedailybeast.com%2Farticles%2F2012%2F07%2F11%2Fwhy-daniel-tosh-s-rape-joke-at-the-laugh-factory-wasn-t-funny.html&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNF7jYGGh0y6D8HKwY4ukvhvUiCsQQ" target="_blank">
        As Elissa Bassist writes here
       </a>
       , When you reiterate these half-truths (there are girls in the world getting raped by like five guys right now), they authenticate themselves, as if by magic. To promote the insidiousrape is hilariousis to join the crime at its own filthy level.
      </p>
      <p>
       In fact, almost all of the most offensive, upsetting and above alllazyjokes that are
       <a href="https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.facebook.com%2Fsam.morril%2Fposts%2F10100191249112069&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHjsfN_gPAGe_pKCkMWYanOeVzjhA" target="_blank">
        defended by stroppy male comics
       </a>
       as too out there for the Normals to cope with are actually little more than dog whistles to the kind of people who think rape is okay, perhaps even deserved. People like Choe, Tosh, Morrill, Ifft et al dont explore the complexities of rape culture and turn it into biting social satire. They create narratives and punchlines that positions rapists as the heroes of the story. This isnt rape comedy or rape art - its rapist comedy and rapist art and we should be calling it that. At least then it will be made very clear exactly whose side these so-called funny men are on.
      </p>
