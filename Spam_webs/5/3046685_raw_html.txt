        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Pumpkin chocolate chip cookies are classic! But what makes them even better? Baking them with a sweet cream cheese inside the cookie! Its a heavenly little surprise in every bite. Now, Ive had so much fun this season working with pumpkin and finding new ways to use it. Some of my favorites have been my
         <a href="http://www.yourcupofcake.com/2014/09/pumpkin-nutella-muffins.html">
          Pumpkin Nutella Muffins
         </a>
         that I still dream about, my
         <a href="http://www.yourcupofcake.com/2014/09/pumpkin-oreo-cupcakes.html">
          Pumpkin Oreo Cupcakes
         </a>
         because Ores are incredible and adding pumpkin just made sense
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.yourcupofcake.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
         and the
         <a href="http://www.yourcupofcake.com/2014/10/pumpkin-heath-bar-cookies.html">
          Pumpkin Heath Bar Cookies with Cream Cheese Frosting
         </a>
         that were so easy, gorgeous and delicious! But of all my new recipes one went insanely viral, and that was my

            <div class="b-b h-1 strong" id="zlrecipe-title" itemprop="name">
             Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Cookies Stuffed with Cream Cheese
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="zlmeta zlclear">
            <div class="fl-l width-50">
            </div>
            <div class="fl-l width-50">
             <p id="zlrecipe-yield">
              Yield:
              <span itemprop="recipeYield">
               30 cookies, depending on the size
              </span>
             </p>

           <div class="img-desc-wrap">
            <p class="t-a-c">
             <img alt="Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Cookies Stuffed with Cream Cheese" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://www.yourcupofcake.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pumpkin-Cookies-stuffed-with-Cream-Cheese.jpg" style="width: 130px;" title="Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Cookies Stuffed with Cream Cheese"/>
            </p>

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <span id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <div class="ingredient-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0">
             Cookies:
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 box spice cake mix (or yellow cake mix plus 1 Tablespoon pumpkin spice-it's what I did)
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             15 oz. pumpkin puree
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 C. oil
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tsp. vanilla extract
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 C. mini chocolate chips
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient-label" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6">
             Cream Cheese Filling:
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
             8 oz cream cheese
            </div>
            <div class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
             3/4 C. powdered sugar
            </div>
           </span>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Directions
           </p>
           <span class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees and line cookie sheets with parchment paper.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             2. Sift cake mix into a large bowl to remove any lumps and add pumpkin puree, oil and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             3. Stir in mini chocolate chips.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             4. In a stand mixer (or using a handheld beater) combine cream cheese and powdered sugar.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             5. Use a cookie scoop (or a regular spoon-see notes below recipe) and fill it 1/3 of way with cookie dough, then place a little bit of cream cheese filling over the dough and then top it off with more dough.  Place the full dough ball onto your cookie sheet and repeat until all the dough is used!
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             6. Bake for about 14 minutes or until the top of the cookies "spring" back like a cake or muffin would.
            </div>
            <div class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             7. Let cool. Like anything pumpkin, the flavors actually taste better the next day so these are great cookies to make the day before an event :)
            </div>
           </span>
