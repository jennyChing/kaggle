     <p>
      The
      <b>
       <a class="extiw" href="//commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Kama" title="commons:Category:Kama">
        kama
       </a>
      </b>
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         or 
       </span>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      (sometimes referred to as the
      <i>
       kai
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       double kai
      </i>
      .
      <i>
       Kama
      </i>
      made with intentionally dull blades for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Kata" title="Kata">
        kata
       </a>
      </i>
      demonstration purposes are referred to as
      <i>
       kata kai
      </i>
      ) is a traditional
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">
       Filipino
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japanese
      </a>
      farming implement similar to a
      <a href="/wiki/Sickle" title="Sickle">
       sickle
      </a>
      used for reaping crops and also employed as a weapon. The kama is often included in weapon training segments of
      <a href="/wiki/Martial_arts" title="Martial arts">
       martial arts
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Silat" title="Silat">
       silat
      </a>
      and in some
      <a href="/wiki/Chinese_martial_arts" title="Chinese martial arts">
       Chinese martial arts
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kama_(weapon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Before being improvised as a weapon, the kama was widely used throughout
      <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
       Asia
      </a>
      to cut crops, mostly rice. It is found in many shapes and forms in
      <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">
       Southeast Asia
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and is particularly common in
      <a href="/wiki/Martial_arts" title="Martial arts">
       martial arts
      </a>
      from
      <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">
       Indonesia
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
       Philippines
      </a>
      . It is also used in Chinese martial arts but not often. From one or both of these areas, the kama was brought to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">
       Okinawa
      </a>
      and incorporated into the martial art of
      <a href="/wiki/Okinawan_martial_arts#Early_martial_arts" title="Okinawan martial arts">
       te
      </a>
      (hand) and later
      <a href="/wiki/Karate" title="Karate">
       karate
      </a>
      (empty hand). It also spawned the use of the
      <a href="/wiki/Kusarigama" title="Kusarigama">
       kusarigama
      </a>
      and the Kyoketsu Shoge.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Technique">
       Technique
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kama_(weapon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Technique">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The kama can be used singly or in pairs. Both the point and sharpened edge of the metal blade are called into use, Okinawan
      <a href="/wiki/Kata" title="Kata">
       kata
      </a>
      suggesting that it could also be used to block, trap and disarm an opponent's weapon. The point at which the blade and handle join in the "weapon" model normally has a nook with which a staff can be trapped. The edge of a traditional rice sickle, such as one would purchase from a Japanese hardware store, continues to the handle without a notch, as this is unneeded for its intended use. The hard edge of the blade would be kept razor-sharp to enable efficient cutting of crops, though this is sometimes a cause of training accidents by unskilled wielders, for whom blunt training versions of the weapon are created.
     </p>
     <p>
      The kama is used today in only few martial arts. The kama can be used as a weapon in karate
      <a href="/wiki/Kata" title="Kata">
       kata
      </a>
      competitions but competitors often simply adapt their empty hand routine while holding kama with little actual kama technique, or employ elaborate weapon-juggling routines that differ radically from the focused Okinawan forms.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">
       In popular culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kama_(weapon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Ryu Hayabusa uses a pair of kama held together by chains, called the Vigoorian Flails, in the modern
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Ninja_Gaiden" title="Ninja Gaiden">
         Ninja Gaiden
        </a>
       </i>
       game series
      </li>
      <li>
       In the role-playing game
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Legend_of_the_Five_Rings" title="Legend of the Five Rings">
         Legend of the Five Rings
        </a>
       </i>
       , the Mantis Clan Champion Yoritomo was trained since birth to fight using large dual kamas.
      </li>
      <li>
       In Battle Royale, antagonist Mitsuko Souma is assigned the kama as a weapon.
      </li>
      <li>
       In the Warcraft universe, Akama's weapons of choice are a pair of kamas.
      </li>
      <li>
       A weapon type found in the game
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Warframe" title="Warframe">
         Warframe
        </a>
       </i>
       , which comes in both a single and dual form. It is however erroneously called a hatchet.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Strider_Hiryu" title="Strider Hiryu">
        Strider Hiryu
       </a>
       uses a futuristic-looking kama to climb walls.
      </li>
      <li>
       Kama are a special monk weapon in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dungeons_and_Dragons" title="Dungeons and Dragons">
        Dungeons and Dragons
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Madara Uchiha, a major antagonist in the anime
       <a href="/wiki/Naruto" title="Naruto">
        Naruto
       </a>
       , makes use of a kama along with a
       <a href="/wiki/Gunbai" title="Gunbai">
        gunbai
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       In the anime
       <a href="/wiki/Black_Butler" title="Black Butler">
        Black Butler
       </a>
       , trainee reapers use a kama until they are full-fledged reapers and can change their death scythes to suit their tastes.
      </li>
      <li>
       In the Riot online game
       <a href="/wiki/League_of_Legends" title="League of Legends">
        League of Legends
       </a>
       the champion Akali uses two kamas.
      </li>
      <li>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Randy_Cunningham:_9th_Grade_Ninja" title="Randy Cunningham: 9th Grade Ninja">
        Randy Cunningham: 9th Grade Ninja
       </a>
       , Randy Cunningham uses kamas to destroy robots.
      </li>
      <li>
       In the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">
         Law &amp; Order
        </a>
       </i>
       episode "
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Loco_Parentis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Loco Parentis (page does not exist)">
        Loco Parentis
       </a>
       ", a kama is the murder weapon.
      </li>
     </ul>
