         <p>
          <em>
           This post is brought to you by
           <a href="http://ad.doubleclick.net/ddm/clk/282734995;109601397;u">
            TINCUP Whiskey
           </a>

         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           If youve ever been to Colorado, you know that theres a lot to be proud of as a resident: They have thesecond-most craft breweries of any state, a booming music scene thats afforded us artists such as India Arie, and Earth, Wind &amp; Fire, some extremely
           <i>
            progressive
           </i>
           legislature and a comedy subculture arguably richer than anywhere in the country, as evidenced below.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           These are just a few of the reasons why it pays to be a Colorado local:
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           <b>
            TJ Miller music videos about your city.
           </b>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           Theres little to be said of TJ, other than he just may be the most riotous Colorado-native in his game, and he completely hit the nail on the head with his absurd single Denver. We suggest it as the soundtrack to this piece.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="400" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/mqkxQQygzXg" width="640">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Little Man Ice Cream
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           Little Mans name is a bit deceiving. Theres nothing little about the punch this storied local creamery packs in itswild flavors. Lemon orSnozberry Sorbet (for the lactose intolerant and vegans), Mexican Hot Chocolate and Salted Oreo are all among the most talked-about cool-down methods for Coloradans when those 300 days of sunshine break 80 degrees. Even better, when Dairy Queen and Baskin Robbins stop serving their plastic bags of soft serve around 9 or 10pm, Little Man keeps scooping their delicious signature flavors all the way up until 1am. Lines are never too long, and the scoopers are always friendly. Seriously, why cant you get this stuff everywhere?
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-16-at-10.59.05-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-16 at 10.59.05 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-223084" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-16-at-10.59.05-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           I Heart Denver
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           An incredible resource for the creative community, the I Heart Denver Store encourages artists to consign their apparel, stickers, postcards, crafts, and hundreds of other goods, with 70% of profit made returned to the artist. Its an incredible model, and an easy way to collect quality items from local, small-batch vendors of all types. A little touristy? Maybe, but arguably the hippest way to sell a city that weve seen yet.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-6.51.13-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-05-13 at 6.51.13 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-220944" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-6.51.13-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Big Blue Bear
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           Also known by its official moniker, I See What You Mean, the mischievous Big Blue Bear has turned into more than just an art installation at Denvers convention center. The cultural icon is almost as popular as the Nuggets in the Rocky Mountain state, and miniatures of the sky-colored Caniform are one of the most popular souvenirs for visitors to remember their trip by. Keeping Colorado proud and curious, the bear inspires even the locals to stop by and peer in the windows of the convention center. Next time youre in town, pay a visit to Big Blue and youll see what we mean.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-16-at-11.07.37-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-16 at 11.07.37 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-223085" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-16-at-11.07.37-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Jumpstreet
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           Seriously: wall-to-wall trampolines. Local geniusTim Crawford, explains that he grew up never knowing of a place where kids could bounce off the walls without driving their parents crazy. Now they have that place, and the parents can even join them!
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           Jumpstreet opened its first location in Thornton, CO in 2007 and has been helping make Colorado more of a destination ever since.Especially for me.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-5.26.30-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-05-13 at 5.26.30 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-220932" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-5.26.30-PM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           OLDeMAN Granola
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p class="p1">
          <span class="s1">
           OLDeMAN Granola is feed of the Gods. The slow-baked taste of real butter and brown sugar makes all of the difference, and I swear I could live for three days on a couple of bags of OLDeMAN. Founders Kelly and Becca were abroad in Africaand developed the recipe as a way to share their adventures in food with their children back in the states. In addition to making a far superior product to the competition, they feel that its their obligation to give back from the blessings [they] have received.Olde Man Granola is currently involved in supporting six charities and four local community organizations.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-4.38.30-PM.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-05-13 at 4.38.30 PM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-220929" height="400" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Screen-Shot-2014-05-13-at-4.38.30-PM.png" width="641"/>
          </a>
