        <p>
         It is very common for WIndows OS to become sluggishly after some time, but when it reaches the point where you cant get anything to run properly, you know that it is time to do a re-installation. For my Windows 7 machine, after countless installation/uninstallation of various applications (all for testing purpose), it starts to give way and behaves abnormally. Apps start to run sluggishly and I cant even get the Windows update to function. The best choice for me is to reinstall the OS.
        </p>

        </div>
        <p>
         However, most people dont like (or even hate) to do a reinstallation because it means that they have to reformat the hard drive and probably lost some of their precious data. The fact is, you dont really have to reformat your hard drive to reinstall the OS. Even after the reinstallation, your data will still be intact. Heres how you can do it:
         <br/>
         <span id="more-25018">
         </span>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Note:
         </strong>
         <br/>
         1. Reinstallation of Windows 7 will wipe the system folder, Program Files and My Document folders. All the applications that you have previously installed will be lost. However, any other user-created folders in the C: drive will still be intact.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. As usual, do a backup of all your data before you attempt to follow this tutorial.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Make sure you have the installer DVD, or the USB drive in hand.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Reinstall Windows 7 without reformatting the hard drive
        </h3>
        <p>
         1. Turn on your computer and load up Windows.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Put in the installer DVD (or plug in the installer USB).
        </p>
        <p>
         3. A window will popup to ask if you want to Run setup.exe or open the folder. Select Run setup.exe. If this popup window didnt appear, open your Windows Explorer, navigate to the DVD drive and run the
         <em>
          setup.exe
         </em>
         file.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="windows-setup" class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-25068" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-setup.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="windows-setup" width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="windows-setup" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25068" height="301" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-setup.png" title="windows-setup" width="336"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         4. The Windows 7 installation page will appear. Click Install Now.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="windows-install-now" class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-25069" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-install-now.jpg" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="windows-install-now" width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="windows-install-now" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25069" height="430" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-install-now.jpg" title="windows-install-now" width="577"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         5. On the next page, select the option Go online to get the latest update for installation.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="windows-get-update" class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-25070" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-get-update.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="windows-get-update" width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="windows-get-update" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25070" height="438" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-get-update.png" title="windows-get-update" width="577"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         6. Accept the license term and click Next.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="windows-license" class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-25071" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-license.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="windows-license" width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="windows-license" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25071" height="439" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-license.png" title="windows-license" width="577"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         7. Select Custom.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="windows-custom-installation" class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-25072" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-custom-installation.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="windows-custom-installation" width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="windows-custom-installation" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25072" height="433" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-custom-installation.png" title="windows-custom-installation" width="577"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         8. Choose the partition that you are going to reinstall the Windows OS. This is usually the C: Drive. It will then show a prompt saying that your existing files will be backup to the
         <em>
          windows.old
         </em>
         files. Click Ok.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="windows-install-location" class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-25073" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-install-location.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="windows-install-location" width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="windows-install-location" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25073" height="431" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-install-location.png" title="windows-install-location" width="577"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         9. Lastly, you just let the installation do its work. Once it is done, it will reboot and prompt you to setup your account. After you have login to your account, you will find that all your data will still be intact. At this point, you might want to reinstall all the usual software.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="windows-installing" class="lazy aligncenter size-full wp-image-25074" data-src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-installing.png" height="1" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/plugins/mte-image-lazy-loading/images/grey.gif" title="windows-installing" width="1"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="windows-installing" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-25074" height="275" src="https://www.maketecheasier.com/assets/uploads/2010/11/windows-installing.png" title="windows-installing" width="574"/>
        </noscript>
        <h3>
         What to do after a reinstallation?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Now you have a clean system, it is best to do a system backup and create a system restore point. In the future when your Windows messed up again, you can simply restore to this clean state.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats it.
        </p>
