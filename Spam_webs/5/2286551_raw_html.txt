                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   Monday, January 12, 2009
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">

                   <div class="post hentry uncustomized-post-template" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_ExNKC0WSh-c/SWt6GGOK8eI/AAAAAAAAABI/bX7TiHsU1Ws/s320/Stephen+Dunn.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>
                    <meta content="4112204366956454376" itemprop="blogId"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Stephen Dunn
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6635554000221183184" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_ExNKC0WSh-c/SWt6GGOK8eI/AAAAAAAAABI/bX7TiHsU1Ws/s1600-h/Stephen+Dunn.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5290456432444633570" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_ExNKC0WSh-c/SWt6GGOK8eI/AAAAAAAAABI/bX7TiHsU1Ws/s320/Stephen+Dunn.jpg" style="margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt; float: left; cursor: pointer; width: 155px; height: 202px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family:georgia;">
                      Stephen Dunn was born in New York City in 1939.
                     </span>
                     <span style='font-size: 12pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family:georgia;">
                      Awarded the Pulitzer Prize for
                      <span style="font-style: italic;">
                       Different Hours
                      </span>
                      (Norton, 2000), Dunn's other honors include the Academy Award for Literature, the James Wright Prize, and fellowships from the National Endowment for the Arts and the New Jersey State Council on the Arts.  Dunn has worked as a professional basketball player, an advertising copywriter, and an editor, as well as professor of creative writing. His most recent book is
                      <span style="font-style: italic;">
                       What Goes On - Selected and New Poems: 1995 - 2009
                      </span>
                      (Norton, 2009).
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-family:georgia;">
                      Dunn lives in Frostburg, Maryland.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-family:georgia;">
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      AND SO
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      And so you call your best friend
                      <br/>
                      who's away, just to hear his voice,
                      <br/>
                      but forget his recording concludes
                      <br/>
                      with "Have a nice day."
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      "Thank you, but I have other plans,"
                      <br/>
                      you're always tempted to respond,
                      <br/>
                      as an old lady once did, the clerk
                      <br/>
                      in the liquor store unable to laugh.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-style: italic;">
                       Always tempted
                      </span>
                      , what a sad
                      <br/>
                      combination of words. And so
                      <br/>
                      you take a walk into the neighborhood,
                      <br/>
                      where the rhododendrons are out
                      <br/>
                      and also some yellowy things
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      and the lilacs remind you of a song
                      <br/>
                      by Nina Simone. "Where's my love?"
                      <br/>
                      is its refrain. Up near Gravel Hill
                      <br/>
                      two fidgety deer cross the road,
                      <br/>
                      whitetails, exactly where
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      the week before a red fox
                      <br/>
                      made a more confident dash.
                      <br/>
                      Now and then the world rewards,
                      <br/>
                      and so you make your way back
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      past the careful lawns, the drowsy backyards,
                      <br/>
                      knowing the soul on its own
                      <br/>
                      is helpless, asleep in the hollows
                      <br/>
                      of its rigging, waiting to be stirred.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        When was this poem composed? How did it start?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       It was written three years ago at Yaddo, and started much as you now see it, the major difference being that it didnt begin with And so.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        How many revisions did this poem undergo? How much time elapsed between the first and final drafts?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       Not many revisions, which is unusual for me. It came rather fast. Certainly I had a viable draft after a mornings work. Maybe about two months later, with just minor tinkerings, I felt that I had it.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        Do you believe in inspiration? How much of this poem was received and how much was the result of sweat and tears?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       As I said, very little sweat and tears with this one, though I cant say that it was inspired by anything Im aware of. I do believe in inspiration, though more often than not inspiration comes from what Ive found myself saying and doing than by anything that preceded the poem.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        How did this poem arrive at its final form? Did you consciously employ any principles of technique?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       It arrived at its final form after I saw that I used And so twice in the body of the poem.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       When I decided to then begin the poem with those words, the poem had found its structure. The recurrence of And so became an organizing principle.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        How long after you finished this poem did it first appear in print? How long do you let a poem sit before you send it off into the world? Do you have any rules about this or does your practice vary with every poem?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       I sent it to APR with a bunch of other poems about four months later. It appeared in print about a year after. When I was younger I used to rush the sending out of poems. Now Im in no hurry.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        Could you talk about fact and fiction and how this poem negotiates the two?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       All Ill say is that nothing in this poem actually happened, though things like it have occurred at various times of my life.
                       <i style="">
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </i>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        In this poem, as in many of your poems, you manage to include a range of disparate material in a way that seems organic, not at all disjunctive. How did it first occur to you that a best friend, a red fox, and Nina Simone belonged in the same poem together?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       It only occurred to me after the fact. As Stevens says in a poem, things occur as they occur.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        Is this a
                        <span class="yshortcuts">
                         narrative poem
                        </span>
                        ?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       Id say its a poem of sensibility with narrative elements. Its less interested in its story than it is in how its details might reveal a consciousness in the act of discovering itself.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        Do you remember who you were reading when you wrote this poem? Any influences youd care to disclose?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       No.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        Do you have any particular audience in mind when you write, an ideal reader?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       The most astute reader that I can imagine, someone who might appreciate how a poem might be stitched together, who might perceive its moves and its orchestration.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        Did you let anyone see drafts of this poem before you finished it? Is there an individual or a group of individuals with whom you regularly share work?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       Larry Raab was at Yaddo with me, but I dont remember his input on this poem.
                       <span style="">
                       </span>
                       I show him everything that I write, and hes enormously helpful. My wife Barbara Hurd sees everything too. They often hasten a poems progress.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        How does this poem differ from other poems of yours?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       Im not sure.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        What is American about this poem?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       I dont know, but it aint German.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <i style="">
                       <span style="color:black;">
                        Was this poem finished or abandoned?
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style="color:black;">
                       This one, I think, felt more finished.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; line-height: 14.4pt;">
                      <span style=";font-family:Arial;font-size:10;color:black;">
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </p>
