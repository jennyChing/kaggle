        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Easy Sweet Potato Rolls
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-10-31T11:19:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          2014/10/31

        <p>
         Ive never been able to tackle a homemade cinnamon roll. Probably because instead of following someone elses recipe, I try to make my own. Thats the one bad thing about being a crazy recipe developer. Im sure there are a gazillion great cinnamon roll recipes out there, but I like to put my own spin on things.
        </p>
        <p>
         With that in mindI decided to play around with some Rhodes Rolls dough and make a splash. Sweet Potato Rolls are almost homemade, in that while it doesnt require you to make dough from scratch, it still requires hands on. Which is something I love about this recipe. Its easy- but its not too easy. You can say you made them, and know that they didnt take all day to make. You can keep that part to yourself.
        </p>
        <p>
         And hey-Wisconsin momsdid you know that Rhodes Rolls dough is made right here in Wisconsin? In fact, its only about a 20 minute drive from where I live, which is so cool. I love that when I buy an item like Rhodes Rolls dough in my local grocery store that I can know that Im helping to support jobs in nearby communities.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-010.jpg">
          <img alt="Rhodes Rolls #Rhodes #ad" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-12916" height="618" src="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-010-700x618.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont usually do step-by-step instructions, but thought that it might be a good idea for this recipe. I didnt photograph the whole dough rising bit, but I used Rhodes frozen rollsthe kind that you have to let rise before using. This recipe uses a whole package of the frozen rolls, but you could easily cut it in half.
        </p>
        <p>
         So- first, follow package instructions and let the dough rise. I did the quick start method. Make sure you turn the stove off after it preheats in this step (according to package directions), or youll end up ruining a good batch of dough. Just a friendly reminder, because I know someone who didnt turn the oven off. Not naming names.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once dough is ready, line a big section of table or counter with parchment paper. Dust the parchment paper with a little bit of flour.
        </p>
        <p>
         Next, dump the dough onto the parchment paper. Working from the middle towards the edges, press the dough down into a large rectangle. I would liken the desired thickens to be that of sugar cookie dough, but you can certainly make it thinner. I added flour to the top of minejust a littleand put parchment paper on top of it while working the dough into shape.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes.jpg">
          <img alt="rhodes" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-12908" height="341" src="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-700x341.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Once the dough has been shaped, its time to make the filling. This is a very simple filling to make. Simply microwave 2 large sweet potatoes until soft, scoop the potatoes out, and mix together with 1/2 cup of butter and 1 cup of brown sugar. See? Easy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-001.jpg">
          <img alt="Rhodes Rolls #Rhodes #ad" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-12909" height="402" src="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-001-700x402.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then, spread the filling over the dough using the back of a spoon or a spatula.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-002.jpg">
          <img alt="Rhodes Rolls #Rhodes #ad" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-12910" height="359" src="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-002-700x359.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Roll the dough up. Working with parchment paper really comes in handy here, because it helps lift the dough and filling.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-004.jpg">
          <img alt="Rhodes Rolls #Rhodes #ad" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-12911" height="466" src="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-004-700x466.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Slice the finished roll into 1 inch strips and line baking sheet with Sweet Potato Rolls. Mine made 18.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-006.jpg">
          <img alt="Rhodes Rolls #Rhodes #ad" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-12912" height="465" src="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-006-700x465.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         In a preheated oven, bake at 350 for 30-35 minutes on middle rack.
        </p>
        <p>
         Serve with glaze and enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-007.jpg">
          <img alt="Rhodes Rolls #Rhodes #ad" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-12913" height="832" src="http://allshecooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/rhodes-007-700x832.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
        </p>

          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients

          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 package Rhodes frozen rolls dough
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 sweet potatoes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup melted butter

           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 teaspoons cinnamon
           </li>
          </ul>

         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Let dough rise according to package directions. Line counter with parchment paper and press dough dough into large rectangle- dough should be about the thickness of sugar cookie dough.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Microwave sweet potatoes and then remove peelings. Mix sweet potatoes together with melted butter and brown sugar. Spread mixture over dough.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Roll dough up and then slice into one-inch slices. Line Sweet Potato Rolls onto parchment paper lined baking sheet and bake at 350 degrees for 30-35 minutes or until lightly browned and not doughy.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Top with glaze (recipe below) and serve.
           </li>
          </ol>
