        supplies needed:

        <br/>

        <br/>
        12v socket $11.00 (walmart)
        <br/>
        cooler $8.00 (walmart)
        <br/>
        nuts/bolts $2.00 (walmart)
        <br/>
        wire plugs $2.00 (walmart)
        <br/>
        super glue $1.00 (dollar store)
        <br/>
        <br/>
        TOTAL: $84.00
        <br/>
        w/tax around $90.00

        tools needed:
        <br/>

        <br/>

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 1: Cutting wires and holes
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="ET9836ODX4EYMW0QKS" data-entry-url="/id/Building-a-micro-solar-generator/step1/Cutting-wires-and-holes/" id="photoset-ET9836ODX4EYMW0QKS">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-ET9836ODX4EYMW0QKS" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FA5/7J27/0S9EYMW0QF6/FA57J270S9EYMW0QF6.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FA57J270S9EYMW0QF6" data-notes="[]" href="/file/FA57J270S9EYMW0QF6" id="photoset-link-FM5WSCKF0212IAD-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="Picture of Cutting wires and holes" class="photoset-photo id_FA57J270S9EYMW0QF6 " data-notes="[]" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FA5/7J27/0S9EYMW0QF6/FA57J270S9EYMW0QF6.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
          </div>
          <i class="notes-icon">
          </i>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        I made no exact measurements for wire length, just guestimation. using a tape measurer i centered the panel on the lid and used the 12v socket for the hole pattern on the side. On the lid I drilled 5 holes, 4 for the panel to mount to the lid and 1 for the panel wire.

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 2: Assembly
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="ENKVPC77Z5EYMW0R0Z" data-entry-url="/id/Building-a-micro-solar-generator/step2/Assembly/" id="photoset-ENKVPC77Z5EYMW0R0Z">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-ENKVPC77Z5EYMW0R0Z" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FLF/A046/DI0EYMW0QM2/FLFA046DI0EYMW0QM2.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FLFA046DI0EYMW0QM2" data-notes="[]" href="/file/FLFA046DI0EYMW0QM2" id="photoset-link-F3FO9HTF0211KMK-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="Picture of Assembly" class="photoset-photo id_FLFA046DI0EYMW0QM2 " data-notes="[]" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FLF/A046/DI0EYMW0QM2/FLFA046DI0EYMW0QM2.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
          </div>
          <i class="notes-icon">
          </i>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        First I bolted the panel to the lid and ran the wires throw, because of the uneven surface on the underside larger holes were made to make access to the nuts to be placed in the hollow cavity of the lid.
        <br/>
        Next the 12v socket was threaded into place.
        <br/>
        super glue was applied to the nuts and the 12v socket threading to insure vibration would not cause any thing to come loose.
        <br/>
        After the glue set I crimped the wires from the panel and the 12v outlet to quick disconnect tabs.
        <br/>
        the final step was dropping in the battery and plugging in the wires.
