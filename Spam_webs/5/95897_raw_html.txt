       <div data-action="Contextual" data-category="Article" data-track-tags="a" id="article-body">
        <p>
         Was that planned?
        </p>
        <p>
         At
         <em>
          The Giver
         </em>
         premiere in NYC on Aug. 11, the film's stars
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrities/katie-holmes">
           Katie Holmes
          </a>
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          Odeya Rush
         </strong>
         and
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrities/taylor-swift">
           Taylor Swift
          </a>
         </strong>
         chose shades of pinks and reds.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/pictures/summer-movie-preview-2014-201465">
          PHOTOS:
          <em>
           The Giver
          </em>
          and more summer movies to see
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Holmes wore an ethereal Zac Posen dress in pale pink. The gathered bodice and pleated chiffon skirt showed off her lean figure while the draped detail on the back added drama. The face of Bobbi Brown, 35, played up her radiant complexion with a smoky eye and shiny pink lips. Holmes swept her hair into a high top knot with extra height in the front.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-moms/pictures/hands-on-celebrity-parents-201448">
          PHOTOS: Katie and more hands-on celeb parents
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Newcomer Rush, 17, wore a gorgeous red Georges Hobeika Couture gown with an embellished bodice. With her hair swept into an elaborate braided chignon, Rush played up her eyes with a dramatic cat eye and bright pink lips. "We actually looked at a picture of
         <strong>
          Marilyn Monroe
         </strong>
         as sort of an inspiration and then figured out a way to make the look younger and more modern," celeb makeup artist Beau Nelson tells
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.usmagazine.com">
           Us Weekly
          </a>
         </strong>
         of the look he created with Chanel makeup. "By using shimmery shadows and pink lips instead of matte red the look felt updated and fresh as opposed to retro, but still had a classic Hollywood glamour feel."
        </p>
