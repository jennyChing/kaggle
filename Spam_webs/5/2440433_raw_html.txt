           <h2>
            <span style="color: #000000">
             <strong>
              Miami Heat Offseason: Compete or Rebuild?
             </strong>
            </span>
           </h2>
           <p>
           </p>
           <h3>
            <span style="color: #000000">
             Taking a look into theMiami Heat offseason and recommendations to build it back into a contender.
            </span>
           </h3>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span style="color: #000000">
             <strong>
              Analysis
             </strong>
             : The Heat, much like their MLB city counterparts, built their championship winning team through free agency, then once the money caught up with them, they were dismantled by the dollar. Lebron returned to Cleveland so they went back to the free agency well, adding Luol Deng, Josh McRoberts, Danny Granger, and others to replace his production. That predictably backfired and the team missed out on the playoffs in the awful Eastern Conference. The Heat are now old and going nowhere quickly. Theyll need to decide this offseason whether or not to rebuild, in a similar fashion as the Celtics recently did with their aging vets, or continue adding veteran talent to compete with their current players. Multiple first round picks through the year 2021 have been traded away, which will make a rebuild that more difficult.
            </span>
           </p>
