              <div class="mf-single-article-headerleft">
               <h1 class="mf-single-article-title">
                An Open-Source, Online Hive to Help Save Bees
               </h1>
               <p class="mf-single-article-title-byline">
                By
                <span class="mf-single-article-author">
                 <a href="http://modernfarmer.com/author/sam-brasch/">
                  Sam Brasch
                 </a>
                </span>
                <span class="mf-single-article-date">
                 on March 27, 2014
                </span>
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="mf-single-article-headerright">
               <div class="mf-article-share">
                <span class="share-text">
                 Share
                </span>
                <ul class="unstyled mf-single-article-share fix">
                 <li>
                  <a class="mf-ico invert twitter" href="https://twitter.com/share?text=via%20@modfarm&amp;url=http://modernfarmer.com/2014/03/hive-crowd-source-fight-bees/" target="_blank" title="Tweet this">

                  <a class="mf-ico invert fb" href="https://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?u=http://modernfarmer.com/2014/03/hive-crowd-source-fight-bees/&amp;t=An Open-Source, Online Hive to Help Save Bees" target="_blank" title="Share on Facebook">
                   <i class="fa fa-facebook">

                 <li>
                  <a class="mf-ico invert gplus" href="https://plusone.google.com/_/+1/confirm?hl=en&amp;url=http://modernfarmer.com/2014/03/hive-crowd-source-fight-bees/" target="_blank" title="Share on Google+">

                   </i>
                  </a>
                 </li>
                </ul>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="mf-single-article-mainimg-wrap">
              <img alt="" class="mf-single-article-mainimg" src="http://cdn.modernfarmer.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/beehive-hero.jpg"/>
             </div>
             <div class="mf-single-article-pad">
              <p class="mf-single-article-excerpt">
               To save the bees, enlist the human-hive mind.
              </p>
              <p>
               Thats the gist of Open-Source Bee Projects (OSBP) new project. Earlier this month, the international collaborative of hackers opened a
               <a href="http://www.indiegogo.com/projects/open-source-beehives" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.indiegogo.com/projects/open-source-beehives', 'fundraising effort');">
                fundraising effort
               </a>
               they hope will connect backyard beehives to the Internet. And no, this is not about bringing Netflix to insects. OSBP thinks that a global set of sensors could give scientists new insight into the possible causes of Colony Collapse Disorder (CCD)  the elusive condition threatening bees and the crops they pollinate.
              </p>
              <div class="mf-pullquote-sc mf-pullquote-sc-left">
               <p class="mf-pullquote-sc-quote">
                If enough people are monitoring their hives, we can gather independent data and come to our own conclusions.
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               If enough people are monitoring their hives, we can gather independent data and come to our own conclusions, says Aaron Makaruk, a co-founder the Open Tech Collaborative in Denver, Colorado, one of three groups involved in the effort. And hopefully we will be able to connect to universities and research laboratories to format the data in a way thats useful to them.
              </p>
              <p>
               In other words, a cheap sensor could turn backyard beekeepers into an army of citizen-scientists.
               <a href="http://colonymonitoring.com/cmwp/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://colonymonitoring.com/cmwp/', 'Others have had the idea');">
                Others have had the idea
               </a>
               , but Makaruk says open-sourcing the sensors  and giving anyone the right to manufacture or even sell the things  is key to getting them to the masses.
              </p>
              <p>
               That idea is a neat fit with the OSBPs last open-sourced effort to develop two digital hive designs  the Colorado Top Bar and the Warr. The hives can be cut from a single piece of wood and fitted together in a half hour. They hope the addition of sensors would turn each of those hives into a worldwide network of controlled experiments and give beekeepers crucial insights into the health of their honey-makers.
              </p>
              <div class="mf-post-video pl-video vimeo">
               <img alt="" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="PGlmcmFtZSBzcmM9Imh0dHA6Ly9wbGF5ZXIudmltZW8uY29tL3ZpZGVvLzg4NjM4Mjk3IiB3aWR0aD0iMTAwJSIgaGVpZ2h0PSIxMDAlIiAgZnJhbWVib3JkZXI9IjAiIHdlYmtpdEFsbG93RnVsbFNjcmVlbiBtb3phbGxvd2Z1bGxzY3JlZW4gYWxsb3dGdWxsU2NyZWVuIHdtb2RlPSJ0cmFuc3BhcmVudCI+" data-lazy-type="iframe" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
               <noscript>
                <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="100%" mozallowfullscreen="" src="http://player.vimeo.com/video/88638297" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="100%" wmode="transparent">
                </iframe>
               </noscript>
              </div>
              <p>
               The OSBP tailored its fund raising incentives toward that vision. A $15 dollar donation gets you a download kit for the Top Bar or the Warr you can have printed at your local maker space. (Those files will be available for free once the campaign ends on April 10th, but without the warm-fuzzies of giving). $600 gets you one of the beehives flat-packed and shipped to your front door
               <em>
                plus
               </em>
               first dibs on the sensors.
              </p>
              <p>
               Right now, those sensors  called Smart Citizen Kits feed data on air composition, light intensity, temperature and sound levels to a single online platform.Makaruk hopes that someday, his team will be able to detect one more variable: the presence of neonicotinoids, a pesticide
               <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/environment/georgemonbiot/2013/aug/05/neonicotinoids-ddt-pesticides-nature" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.theguardian.com/environment/georgemonbiot/2013/aug/05/neonicotinoids-ddt-pesticides-nature', 'some suspect');">
                some suspect
               </a>
               to be the cause behind Colony Collapse Disorder.
              </p>
              <p>
               The University of California has built a
               <a href="https://github.com/opensourcebeehives/Coordination/wiki/Surface-Plasmon-Resonance-Sensors" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://github.com/opensourcebeehives/Coordination/wiki/Surface-Plasmon-Resonance-Sensors', 'such a device');">
                such a device
               </a>
               , but it is made of pricey technology. Makaruk hopes a version by and for the masses would tip the scales, forcing the U.S. to follow the E.U. in banning the chemicals.
              </p>
              <p>
               We want to the empower the average citizen to know whats in their food and their water, he explains. These sensors are becoming more and more affordable all the time and so this is one step on the road where we could help develop a technology and make it accessible to people.
              </p>
