          <div class="perc-blog-wrapper">
           <h2 class="perc-blog-title">
            Maryland is helping its veterans find cybersecurity jobs
           </h2>
           <div class="perc-blog-dateByline-container">
            <div class="perc-blog-byline">
             <span class="perc-blog-author-label">
              by
             </span>
             <span class="perc-blog-author-name">
              Kris Kurcoba
             </span>
            </div>
            <div class="perc-blog-date">
             Mon Dec 8, 2014 at 02:30 PM
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="perc-blog-post">
            <div class="rxbodyfield">
             <p>
              <strong>
               <img alt="Kris Kurcoba" height="260" imgtype="_full" src="/Assets/uploads/images/Kris%20Kurcoba%20Picture.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="250"/>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              Veterans Day has come and gone in the U.S. and with it the plethora of stories that are always found on the Internet at that time with career counseling experts imploring companies to hire our retiring warriors to fill
              <a href="http://careers.eliassen.com/">
               IT consultant
              </a>
              roles that require personnel who are technologically savvy and decision makers who excel at functioning under pressure.
             </p>
             <p>
              While such a perspective is certainly admirable, what gets my attention - and holds it - is when an organization makes a long-term commitment toward helping our nations veterans.
             </p>
             <!-- morelink -->
             <p>
              Such is the case with the
              <a href="http://business.maryland.gov/">
               Maryland Department of Business and Economic Development
              </a>
              , which recently added a Veteran Outreach Coordinator to link the states veterans with the booming opportunities available in the regions cybersecurity industry.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://mdbiznews.business.maryland.gov/category/innovation-and-tech/cybersecurity-innovation-and-tech/">
               Nigel LeBlanc
              </a>
              , a 15-year member of the U.S. Air Force, is now serving on DBEDs Cyber Development team and helping veterans find cybersecurity jobs.
             </p>
             <p>
              Upon taking the job, LeBlanc said, Veterans have the ethics, the knowledge of how to work in teams and how to operate very well under pressure. Veterans have the technical aptitude and the experience needed (to fill the workforce shortage).
             </p>
             <p>
              Marylands DBED deserves our kudos for taking an active step in addressing an issue that gets a lot of lip service every 12 months, but is never truly addressed.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Are you a veteran who is a cybersecurity expert? Or a veteran with technology skills looking for a consulting opportunity?
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Share your story with us in the comments section below!
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
