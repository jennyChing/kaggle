   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Flotsam, jetsam, lagan and derelict
   </h1>

     <p>
      In
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritime_law" title="Maritime law">
       maritime law
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       flotsam
      </b>
      ,
      <b>
       jetsam
      </b>
      ,
      <b>
       lagan
      </b>
      and
      <b>
       derelict
      </b>
      are specific kinds of
      <a href="/wiki/Shipwreck" title="Shipwreck">
       shipwreck
      </a>
      . The words have specific nautical meanings, with legal consequences in the
      <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">
       law
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Admiralty_law" title="Admiralty law">
       admiralty
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Marine_salvage" title="Marine salvage">
       marine salvage
      </a>
      :
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <i>
        Flotsam
       </i>
       is floating wreckage of a ship or its cargo.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         2
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Jetsam
       </i>
       is part of a ship, its equipment, or its cargo that is purposely cast overboard or jettisoned to lighten the load in time of distress and is washed ashore.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         3
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Lagan
       </i>
       (also called
       <b>
        ligan
       </b>
       )
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         4
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       is goods or wreckage that is lying on the bottom of the ocean, sometimes marked by a
       <a href="/wiki/Buoy" title="Buoy">
        buoy
       </a>
       , which can be reclaimed.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Derelict
       </i>
       is cargo that is also on the bottom of the ocean, but which no one has any hope of reclaiming (in other maritime contexts,
       <i>
        derelict
       </i>
       may also refer to a drifting abandoned ship).
      </li>
     </ul>
