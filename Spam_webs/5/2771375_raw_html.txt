         <p>
          From new nail technology to anti-aging products for your locks, make room on your vanity for the latest and greatest beauty innovations that are sure to become life-changers.
         </p>
         <h4>
          LONGER-LASTING NAILS
         </h4>
         <p>
          <img alt="beauty breakthroughs" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-300240" src="http://ca.glam.com/files/2013/07/BeautyBreakthroughs_Layouts_1.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          Getting salon-slick nails at home continues to get better  and easier. First up, watch for the CND Vinylux launching this summer. Created by the same company that brought us Shellac (the two-week wear in-salon, gel-polish hybrid), CND Vinylux is colour that lasts for a week, but it requires no UV or LED light (it cures in natural light). Perhaps the biggest bonus? It can be removed like regular polish so no time is wasted in a gel soak-off session. Other hot polish trends: Dior and Deborah Lippmann both launched rock-hard gel topcoats that add that high-wattage, brilliant, gel-like shine and chip-free wear to your regular polish. Also on deck? Extreme nail textures like chalkboard paint, fuzzy topcoats and polka dot prints are dressing up digits in new ways.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Glam picks: Ciat Chalkboard Manicure Nail Set, $25;
           <a href="http://www.sephora.com/chalkboard-manicure-P379405?skuId=1488568" target="_blank" title="ciate chalkboard">
            sephora.com
           </a>
          </strong>
          . Dior Gel Coat, $25;
          <a href="http://www.dior.com/beauty/int/en/makeup/nails/nail_lacquers/topcoatslacquerssbl/y0028400/py0028400-skuf002840000-cnaillacquers.html" target="_blank" title="dior">
           dior.com
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h4>
          BEAUTY AND THE BEES
         </h4>
         <p>
          <img alt="BeautyBreakthroughs_Layouts_2" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-300241" src="http://ca.glam.com/files/2013/07/BeautyBreakthroughs_Layouts_2.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          Catherine, the Duchess of Cambridge may do her own makeup, but she never skimps on visits to her dermatologist Deborah Mitchell.  The London-based skin care gurus elite clientele (she has been catering to Royal complexions for years  the Duchess of Cornwall, Camilla Parker Bowles is said to be another devoted client) return time and again for her signature Bee Venom facial. The key ingredient bee venom, (sting-free, of course) contains enzymes and peptides that produce an almost Botox-like effect on facial muscles, so skin looks plump and full. This star anti-aging ingredient is now trickling down to launches on counter. And its not just bee venom earning bragging rights  honey is also a proven moisturizing and antioxidant ingredient that is making its way into more and more skin care serums.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Glam picks: Rodial Bee Venom Super Serum, $200;
           <a href="http://www.murale.ca/9758-Rodial_Bee-Venom-Serum.aspx" target="_blank" title="Murale">
            Murale
           </a>
           . The Body Shop HoneyMania Body Butter, $20; at The Body Shop stores mid-August.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h4>
          ANTI-AGING FOR YOUR HAIR
         </h4>
         <p>
          <img alt="BeautyBreakthroughs_Layouts_3" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-300242" src="http://ca.glam.com/files/2013/07/BeautyBreakthroughs_Layouts_3.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          A ritual of oils, serums and masks isnt just for your complexion anymore  now hair care brands are infusing anti-aging ingredients like collagen directly into shampoos, conditioners and styling products to keep hair looking youthful. Collagen promises to plump hair from the outside in so that strands look thicker, while new serum treatments soothe and balance the scalp and provide an optimal environment for hair growth.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Glam picks:
           <a href="http://www.organixhair.com/category-title/750" target="_blank" title="Organix">
            Organix Hair Thick &amp; Full Collagen &amp; Biotin shampoo
           </a>
           , $6; at select mass-market retailers.
           <a href="http://www.kerastase-usa.com/Replenish/regimen-replenish,default,sc.html" target="_blank" title="Kerastase">
            Krastase Initialiste Advanced Scalp and Hair Concentrate
           </a>
           , $55; at select salons.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h4>
          SLEEPING MASKS
         </h4>
         <p>
          <img alt="BeautyBreakthroughs_Layouts_4" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-300243" src="http://ca.glam.com/files/2013/07/BeautyBreakthroughs_Layouts_4.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          Much like the
          <a href="http://ca.glam.com/love-bb-creams-get-to-know-dd-creams/" target="_blank" title="bb and cc creams">
           BB creams and CC creams
          </a>
          that have made their way from Asia to Canada, the next skincare craze hitting North American shelves by way of Korea are sleeping masks. These new, ultra-moisturizing skincare staples are applied on top of your regular nighttime treatments and aim to give skin an intense hydration boost while you sleep  so you wake up looking extra smooth and plump.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Glam picks: Dr. Jart+ Water Fuse Water-Max Sleeping Mask, $48;
           <a href="http://www.sephora.com/water-fuse-water-max-sleeping-mask-P374570?skuId=1421981" target="_blank" title="dr jart">
            sephora.com
           </a>
           .
           <a href="http://www.en.vichy.ca/Aqualia-Thermal/Night-Care/AQUALIA-THERMAL-NIGHT-SPA-p994.aspx" target="_blank" title="Vichy">
