         <h1 class="post-title">
          Stage one makeover: DRZ 400 better beater
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="clear">
        </div>
        <div class="post-content-content">
         <p>
          It had to happen. I knew I couldnt ride it around long and not do something to it. So Im into round one of the makeover, its on the road and I reckon its looking pretty respectable. For a beater.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-597">
          </span>
          <br/>
          I ordered a new set of Acerbis plastics from eBay pretty much the same week I bought it, but they just lay there as other crap kept me occupied until I sat down in the shed one day, put some music on and thought, right lets get at this and see what I can do.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;">
           I replaced the front shrouds and rear panels but had some issues with the tail that required serious stanley blade work. The guard had what looked like a fuller tail section than what fit, hack hack and trim trim until it slid in nicely.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          That tailpiece was annoying the crap out of me though. I mean look at it.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://dailybikers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/PC213980.jpg">
           <img alt="OLYMPUS DIGITAL CAMERA" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1228" height="768" src="http://dailybikers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/PC213980-1024x768.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its the moby dick of guards and it had to go.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;">
           I scored this nice
           <a href="http://www.bikebiz.com.au/products/DRC-Edge%252d2-Tail-Light-Holder-Alloy-DRZ400.html" target="_blank">
            tail eliminator
           </a>
           and a set of LED blinkers for the rear from BikeBiz for a little over $160 shipped and I was determined to actually do this myself and make it work.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Tail tidy bikebiz" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1230" height="1024" src="http://dailybikers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/IMG_4911-768x1024.jpg" width="768"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Turns out everything was pretty straight forward. Two wires for the left, two for the right and two for the stop light.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="IMG_4921" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1231" height="768" src="http://dailybikers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/IMG_4921-1024x768.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Stripped and joined all the wires up and turned it on. Huzzah, everything worked. Jammed that new rear fender in and got it all screwed up in a few hours.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="IMG_4923" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1232" height="1024" src="http://dailybikers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/IMG_4923-768x1024.jpg" width="768"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its amazing how much cleaner it actually looks stripped back to basic black without any bubbled stickers and crap too. Once I started looking at them I realised that they only made it look worse so I just stripped the whole lot off and left it all basic black, new motard beak, shrouds, rear panels and a fender and it looks almost respectable.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;">
           I actually quite like the way it looks, I feel a bit like Ryan Gosling in
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1817273/" style="font-size: 14px;" target="_blank">
           The Place Beyond the Pines
          </a>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;">
           riding it, while robbing banks on the back wheel.
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1239" style="width: 478px">
          <img alt="628x471" class="size-full wp-image-1239" height="471" src="http://dailybikers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/628x471.jpg" width="468"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Ryan rockin it on a DRZ
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          But, those wheels hmm they still bug me. I believe they are original Suzuki RGV 250 wheels, or possibly a GS 500, but made in Japan so from a road bike, but ugh, they just look fugly. I think the bike needs spoke wheels from a SM to complete the look. Keeping an eye on eBay for any bargains.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;">
           The other thing it really needs (I never researched this properly of course, just bought the first freaking DRZ I looked at) is a full system, preferably a
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qQmp6hTWlT8" style="font-size: 14px;" target="_blank">
           Yoshimura
          </a>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;">
           . From my experience, Yoshi and Suzuki are meant for each other.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Im just being a bit tight about the investment, I really wished I had of hung out for a better beater. Haha
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;">
           Those mirrors are on the way out too, as soon as I get
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.highwaydirtbikes.com/HDB_Shop/index.php?app=ccp0&amp;ns=catshow&amp;ref=NexGen_00" style="font-size: 14px;" target="_blank">
           this shipment
          </a>
          <span style="font-size: 14px;">
           from Highway Dirt Bikes in the States, itll have a set of fat bars and funky flip out mirrors. Ive run into a bit of a postage problem with USPS and a lack of a clear way to communicate with them so right now I am just completely in the dark as to when they might turn up.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          So there ya have it folks.
         </p>
         <p>
          BD (Before Dan).
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="IMG_0086" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-1234" height="764" src="http://dailybikers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/IMG_0086-1024x764.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          AD (After Dan)
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="IMG_4927" class="aligncenter" height="768" src="http://dailybikers.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/IMG_4927-1024x768.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Slightly better beater.
         </p>
         <p>
          Stay tuned for stage two.
         </p>
