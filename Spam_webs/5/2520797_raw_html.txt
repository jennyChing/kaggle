                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/--603Z31iMAs/U_6rJLlMuwI/AAAAAAAABBU/40jlgeMv15s/s1600/1.JPG" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     D.I.Y. Studded Shirt
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1736693941691174741" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      Hi! I want to share with you my
                      <b>
                       latest D.I.Y. project.
                      </b>
                      The reason why I decided to ruin my favourite
                      <b>
                       jeans dress
                      </b>
                      was that it already was half damaged. I spilled the orange juice on it and I was not able to get rid of that spot. Ok, I said! "Time for my new diy project!" I had 50
                      <span style="color: #c27ba0;">
                       <span style="font-weight: bold;">
                        golden studs
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      left (that I ordered from Amazon for really cheap) and I wanted to make it a little bit "punk". I was kind of lucky with the dress cut because I was able to keep some of the fabric on the bottom and
                      <b>
                       tie a knot.
                      </b>
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/--603Z31iMAs/U_6rJLlMuwI/AAAAAAAABBU/40jlgeMv15s/s1600/1.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="570" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/--603Z31iMAs/U_6rJLlMuwI/AAAAAAAABBU/40jlgeMv15s/s1600/1.JPG" width="800"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Firstly I cut the dress to get rid of the damaged part.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hOr8ZsLnBO8/U_6rJJX7tPI/AAAAAAAABBY/odeN1xnfeV0/s1600/2.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="570" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hOr8ZsLnBO8/U_6rJJX7tPI/AAAAAAAABBY/odeN1xnfeV0/s1600/2.JPG" width="800"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Then I was considering where to put the studs and how many. (I really didn
                      <span style="background-color: white; color: #545454; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: xx-small; line-height: 18.200000762939453px;">
                       '
                      </span>
                      t want it to look overstudded).
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-7-FxpUq6QQ4/U_6rJhhOcbI/AAAAAAAABBc/G2WGxBxeojs/s1600/3.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="533" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-7-FxpUq6QQ4/U_6rJhhOcbI/AAAAAAAABBc/G2WGxBxeojs/s1600/3.JPG" width="800"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      I removed all the buttons and placed studs instead. My plan and intention was wear this shirt always over a top so I really don
                      <span style="background-color: white; color: #545454; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: xx-small; line-height: 18.200000762939453px;">
                       '
                      </span>
                      t need the buttons. If you guys want to wear it alone, you might want to keep the buttons or replace them with something more stylish (different buttons or broche etc.) - it
                      <span style="background-color: white; color: #545454; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: xx-small; line-height: 18.200000762939453px;">
                       '
                      </span>
                      s up to you.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-BQFjSLDr4MI/U_6rKiGpLhI/AAAAAAAABB0/-q2HylB3eUM/s1600/5.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="533" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-BQFjSLDr4MI/U_6rKiGpLhI/AAAAAAAABB0/-q2HylB3eUM/s1600/5.JPG" width="800"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Measure twice before you cut! You can use a pencil to plan where you are going to place the studs. Especially on the collar, every little mistake would be obvious.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3qBtUe9FtUU/U_6rJnhHnUI/AAAAAAAABBo/-f_iPhTMyLI/s1600/4.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="500" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3qBtUe9FtUU/U_6rJnhHnUI/AAAAAAAABBo/-f_iPhTMyLI/s1600/4.JPG" width="800"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Continue and place studs wherever you want. I just replaced the previous buttons and added studs on the collar, that
                      <span style="background-color: white; color: #545454; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: xx-small; line-height: 18.200000762939453px;">
                       '
                      </span>
                      s it.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-t2CZhNHpgls/U_6rMDkqlMI/AAAAAAAABB8/tgSyeWxH3xQ/s1600/6.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="533" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-t2CZhNHpgls/U_6rMDkqlMI/AAAAAAAABB8/tgSyeWxH3xQ/s1600/6.JPG" width="800"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      And this is my finished masterpiece.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-_DISHq6oJJg/U_6rNLIyuTI/AAAAAAAABCI/qYPXqMhhPJE/s1600/7.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="533" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-_DISHq6oJJg/U_6rNLIyuTI/AAAAAAAABCI/qYPXqMhhPJE/s1600/7.JPG" width="800"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cgwQaJtQKGI/U_6srFmSEWI/AAAAAAAABCY/EkVICIzRVNQ/s1600/8.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="510" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cgwQaJtQKGI/U_6srFmSEWI/AAAAAAAABCY/EkVICIzRVNQ/s1600/8.jpg" width="800"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      The dress was originally from Gate.
                     </div>
