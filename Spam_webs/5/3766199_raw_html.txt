          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Sweet Potato Nachos7" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-159" height="433" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sweet-Potato-Nachos7.jpg" width="650"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           My body confuses me. Take my current smoothie addiction, for example. I went almost all of last summer without drinking a single one, and then got hit with the biggest craving for them in the middle of January  the coldest time of the year.Youre looking at a girl who was sitting next to a fireplace sipping on frozen fruits and ice just so she could get her fix. My cravings make no sense.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           In an ideal world, Id eat seasonally from things I grew in my own garden  berries in the summer and squash in the fall.But in the real world, I live in a condo that wouldnt be able to support a garden even if I was willing to put in the work, and I drink blueberry smoothies in the winter and munch on roasted sweet potatoes in the the summer, or in the spring in this case
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Sliced Sweet Potato" class="aligncenter wp-image-155 size-full" height="650" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sliced-Sweet-Potato.jpg" width="433"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Sweet potatoes are perfection. Not only are they delicious, but they also happen to be packed with plenty of vitamins and minerals, meaning you can bury them under cheese and avocado and still end up with a dish you can feel good about eating. Why cheese and avocado? Because those are the best toppings for nachos!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Sweet Potato Nachos6" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-150" height="650" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sweet-Potato-Nachos6.jpg" width="433"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           I lovenachos  absolutely adore. The crunch of the chips, the gooeyness of the cheese, the perfectly harmonious meshing of the flavours be still my pounding heart. But I also love to eat healthy [most of the time], meaning that I had to say goodbye to my habit of eating cheese and chips for lunch 4 times a week and find a better alternative.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Enter baked sweet potato rounds.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Roasted Sweet Potatoes" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-158" height="433" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Roasted-Sweet-Potatoes.jpg" width="650"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Have I mentioned theyre perfection? And a great alternative to chips? They may not provide the same kind of crunch, but they
           <em>
            do
           </em>
           have a pillowy quality thats absolutelyto die for; and they have no problem supporting a healthy helping of cheese
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Sweet Potato Nachos" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-154" height="650" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sweet-Potato-Nachos.jpg" width="433"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           But lets talk toppings, since we know thats where all the fun is.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           I have to confess that, while I love Mexican, Im an absolute
           <em>
            wimp
           </em>
           when it comes to hot and spicy foods. Anything beyond mild salsa is more than I can handle, and dont even talk to me about cayenne and jalapeos unless you want to send me running full tilt in the opposite direction  its
           <em>
            that
           </em>
           bad. Im also a wimp when it comes to horror movies, but thats neither here nor there.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Thats why I stuck to the basics with my nachos. I started with a neutral base of baked sweet potato rounds, black beans, and cheese
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Sweet Potato Nachos2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-156" height="650" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sweet-Potato-Nachos2.jpg" width="433"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Added a sprinkle of green onions and diced avocado
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Sweet Potato Nachos3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-152" height="650" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sweet-Potato-Nachos3.jpg" width="433"/>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           And finished everything off with a spoonful of Greek yogurt before digging in
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Sweet Potato Nachos5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-151" height="650" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sweet-Potato-Nachos5.jpg" width="433"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Delicious
           <em>
            and
           </em>
           painless  what more could you ask for? And the best thing about a dish like this is that its fully customizable depending on your tastes. Not a fan ofbeans? Swap them out for some cooked chicken or beef instead! Toppings too simple for you? Add some sliced peppers, salsa, cilantro, guacamole, olives, or corn. The possibilities are endless, which is why you should make this dish again and again  to find out which combination is your favourite!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="Sweet Potato Nachos4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-153" height="650" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sweet-Potato-Nachos4.jpg" width="433"/>

          <h2 class="Recipe_title">
           Recipe
          </h2>
          <div class="recipe_metadata">
           <div class="recipe_title">
            Sweet Potato Nachos
           </div>
           <div class="recipe_info">
            <ul>
             <li>
              Prep Time: 15 minutes
             </li>
             <li>
              Cook Time: 45 minutes
             </li>
             <li>
              Difficulty: Easy
             </li>
             <li>
              Servings: 4
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
           <div class="ingredients_wr">
            <div class="ingredients">
             <div class="recipe_sub_title">
              Ingredients
             </div>
             <p>
              2 large sweet potatoes, sliced 1/4 inch thick
              <br/>
              1 Tbsp. olive oil
              <br/>
              1 tsp. paprika
              <br/>
              1 tsp. garlic powder
              <br/>
              salt and pepper, to taste
              <br/>
              1 cup canned black beans, rinsed
              <br/>
              1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
              <br/>
              1/4 cup chopped green onions
              <br/>
              1 avocado, diced or smashed
              <br/>
              Greek yogurt or sour cream
              <br/>
              Salsa
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="ingredients_img">
             <a href="http://www.savorystyle.com/blog/food/sweet-potato-nachos" title="Sweet Potato Nachos">
              <img alt="Sweet Potato Nachos7" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="433" src="http://www.savorystyle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Sweet-Potato-Nachos7.jpg" width="650"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <div class="clear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="preparation_instructions clear">
            <div class="recipe_sub_title">
             Preparation Instructions
            </div>
            1) Preheat oven to 425F and lightly spray a baking sheet with cooking spray.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            2) Wash and dry your sweet potatoes, then use a vegetable peeler to peel them. Slice them into 1/4 inch rounds and place in a large bowl. Drizzle with olive oil, using your hands to ensure that each round is evenly coated. Season with paprika, garlic powder, and salt and pepper, tossing well.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            3) Place rounds in a single layer on your baking sheet. Bake for 40 minutes, turning them over halfway through to ensure they don't burn.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            4) Remove from oven and transfer rounds to a cast iron skillet or oven safe dish, allowing the edges to slightly overlap. Top with beans and cheese before returning to the oven for an additional 5 minutes, or until cheese is melted.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            5) Remove from oven and sprinkle with chopped green onions. Serve with avocado, Greek yogurt or sour cream, and salsa.
           </div>
