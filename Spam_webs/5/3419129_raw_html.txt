            <p>
             The festival of
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/89435/jewish/Rosh-Hashanah-Unwrapped.htm" title="Rosh Hashanah Unwrapped">
               <b>
                Rosh Hashanah
               </b>
              </a>
             </b>
             the name means
             <b>
              
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/90203/jewish/The-48-Hour-Brain.htm">
               Head of the Year
              </a>
              
             </b>
             is observed for two days beginning on
             <b>
              <a href="/calendar/view/day_cdo/aid/150067/jewish/Tishrei-1.htm" title="Tishrei 1">
               1 Tishrei
              </a>
             </b>
             , the first day of the Jewish year. It is the anniversary of the creation of
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/175439/jewish/Adams-Birthday.htm">
               Adam and Eve
              </a>
             </b>
             , the first
             <b>
              <a href="/parshah/article_cdo/aid/92136/jewish/Man-and-Woman.htm">
               man and woman
              </a>
             </b>
             , and their first actions toward the realization of
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/4764/jewish/The-Man-In-Man.htm" title="The Man In Man">
               mankinds role
              </a>
             </b>
             in
             <span class="glossary_item" glossary_item="17588" onclick="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.CompleteShow(this);" onmouseout="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Hide(this);" onmouseover="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Show(this);">
              Gd
             </span>
             s world.
            </p>
            <p>
             <span class="glossary_item" glossary_item="9553" onclick="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.CompleteShow(this);" onmouseout="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Hide(this);" onmouseover="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Show(this);">
              Rosh Hashanah
             </span>
             thus emphasizes the
             <b>
              <a href="/search/keyword_cdo/kid/1213" kid="1213">
               special relationship
              </a>
             </b>
             between Gd and humanity: our dependence upon Gd as our creator and sustainer, and Gds
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/6196/jewish/Our-Father-Our-King.htm">
               dependence
              </a>
             </b>
             upon us as the ones who make His presence known and felt in His world. Each year on Rosh Hashanah, all inhabitants of the world pass before Gd like a flock of sheep, and it is decreed in the heavenly court
             <i>
              who shall
              <b>
               <a href="/search/keyword_cdo/kid/1434" kid="1434">
                live
               </a>
              </b>
              , and who shall die... who shall be impoverished, and who shall be
              <b>
               <a href="/search/keyword_cdo/kid/1665" kid="1665">
                enriched
               </a>
              </b>
              ; who shall fall and who shall
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/4460/jewish/Rabbi-Amnon.htm" title="Rabbi Amnon">
               <b>
                rise
               </b>
              </a>
              .
             </i>
             But this is also the day we proclaim Gd
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/4710/jewish/Insights.htm" title="Insights">
               King of the Universe
              </a>
             </b>
             . The Kabbalists teach that the continued existence of the universe is dependent upon the
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/3082/jewish/The-Waking-of-Creation.htm" title="The Waking of Creation">
               renewal of the divine desire
              </a>
             </b>
             for a world when we accept Gds
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/174392/jewish/The-King-and-I.htm">
               kingship
              </a>
             </b>
             each year on Rosh Hashanah.
            </p>
            <p>
             The central observance of Rosh Hashanah is the sounding of the
             <b>
              <i>
               <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/315559/jewish/Shofar.htm">
                shofar
               </a>
              </i>
             </b>
             , the rams horn, which also represents the trumpet blast of a peoples coronation of their king. The
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/4712/jewish/Stories.htm" title="Stories">
               cry
              </a>
             </b>
             of the
             <i>
              <span class="glossary_item" glossary_item="15546" onclick="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.CompleteShow(this);" onmouseout="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Hide(this);" onmouseover="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Show(this);">
               shofar
              </span>
             </i>
             is also a call to
             <b>
              <a href="/search/keyword_cdo/kid/1244" kid="1244">
               repentance
              </a>
             </b>
             , for Rosh Hashanah is also the anniversary of
             <b>
              <a href="/parshah/article_cdo/aid/2581/jewish/Gds-Business.htm" title="Gd's Business">
               mans first sin and his repentance thereof
              </a>
             </b>
             , and serves as the first of the 
             <span class="glossary_item" glossary_item="2711" onclick="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.CompleteShow(this);" onmouseout="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Hide(this);" onmouseover="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Show(this);">
              Ten Days of Repentance
             </span>
              which culminate in
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/4687/jewish/Yom-Kippur.htm" title="Yom Kippur">
               Yom Kippur
              </a>
             </b>
             , the Day of Atonement. Another significance of the
             <i>
              shofar
             </i>
             is to recall the
             <b>
              <a href="/parshah/article_cdo/aid/58838/jewish/The-Binding-of-Isaac.htm">
               Binding of Isaac
              </a>
             </b>
             which also occurred on Rosh Hashanah, in which a ram took
             <span class="glossary_item" glossary_item="16359" onclick="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.CompleteShow(this);" onmouseout="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Hide(this);" onmouseover="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Show(this);">
              Isaac
             </span>
             s place as an offering to Gd; we evoke
             <span class="glossary_item" glossary_item="14443" onclick="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.CompleteShow(this);" onmouseout="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Hide(this);" onmouseover="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Show(this);">
              Abraham
             </span>
             s readiness to sacrifice his son, and plead that the merit of his deed should stand by us as we
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/90202/jewish/The-Master-Key.htm">
               pray
              </a>
             </b>
             for a year of
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/4767/jewish/A-Glass-of-Milk.htm" title="A Glass of Milk">
               life, health and prosperity
              </a>
             </b>
             . Altogether, we listen to
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/4392/jewish/Sounding-of-the-Shofar.htm" title="Sounding of the Shofar">
               one hundred
               <i>
                shofar
               </i>
               blasts
              </a>
             </b>
             over the course of the Rosh Hashanah services.
            </p>
            <p>
             Additional Rosh Hashanah observances include: a) Eating a piece of
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/160979/jewish/Sweet-Stings.htm" title="Sweet Stings">
               apple dipped in honey
              </a>
             </b>
             , to symbolize our desire for a
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/175444/jewish/Holding-G-d-in-Her-Hands.htm">
               sweet
              </a>
             </b>
             year, and other
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/315455/jewish/Cooking-the-Year.htm">
               special foods
              </a>
             </b>
             symbolic of the new years blessings. b)
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/90039/jewish/Bless-You.htm">
               Blessing one another
              </a>
             </b>
             with the words
             <i>
              Leshanah tovah tikateiv veteichateim,
             </i>
             May you be inscribed and sealed for a good year. c)
             <b>
              <a href="/holidays/JewishNewYear/template_cdo/aid/4385/jewish/Minchah-and-Tashlich.htm" title="Minchah and Tashlich">
               Tashlich
              </a>
             </b>
             , a special prayer said near a body of
             <b>
              <a href="/library/article_cdo/aid/3154/jewish/The-Memory-of-Water.htm" title="The Memory of Water">
               water
              </a>
             </b>
             (an ocean, river, pond, etc.), in evocation of the verse, And You shall cast their sins into the depths of the sea. And as with every major Jewish holiday, after candlelighting and prayers we recite
             <i>
              <span class="glossary_item" glossary_item="14622" onclick="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.CompleteShow(this);" onmouseout="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Hide(this);" onmouseover="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Show(this);">
               kiddush
              </span>
             </i>
             and make a blessing on the
             <i>
              <span class="glossary_item" glossary_item="14610" onclick="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.CompleteShow(this);" onmouseout="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Hide(this);" onmouseover="Co.Tools.Content.Glossary.Show(this);">
               challah
              </span>
             </i>
             .
            </p>
