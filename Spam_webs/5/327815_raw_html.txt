       <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
        Gamifying and scientifying your sex life, badly
       </h1>
      </header>
      <hr/>
      <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
       <p class="lead">
        Theres a new app called
        <a href="http://spreadsheetsapp.com/">
         Spreadsheets
        </a>
        . This is not new; there are millions of apps, and 95% of them are crap. Spreadsheets purports to use the accelerometer and microphone in your smartphone to measure your sexual performance  a kind of
        <a href="http://www.fitbit.com/">
         fitbit
        </a>
        for sex (do not tell my wife, shes already slightly obsessed with her fitbit stats).
       </p>
       <p>
        I find the whole idea a little weird, and have zero interest in the thing, but whatever floats your boat, k? But heres what I find offensive and stupid: calling the noise from these smartphone stats
        <a href="http://www.nerve.com/love-sex/which-states-have-the-longest-and-shortest-sex">
         a study of sex duration in America
        </a>
        . Its basically a sex toy that will be used sporadically and idiosyncratically, and youre not going to get anything that could be called information out of it. Case in point: look at the data on intercourse duration.
       </p>
       <div id="div-gpt-ad-1421869288950-0" style="width: 100%; max-width:728px; height:90px; margin: 0 auto; clear: both;">
        <script type="text/javascript">
         googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1421869288950-0'); });
        </script>
       </div>
       <div style="clear: both">
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://freethoughtblogs.com/pharyngula/files/2014/02/sexduration.jpg">
         <img alt="sexduration" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-15187" height="569" src="//freethoughtblogs.com/pharyngula/files/2014/02/sexduration-500x569.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        That makes no sense. Why would you even expect variation to fall in the arbitrary boundary lines of the states? For instance, the part of Minnesota where I live is, culturally and geographically, very similar to the Dakotas, yet somehow Im supposed to believe that theres some kind of remarkable transition in sexual behavior over there? Why? Show me the variance in the data. Give me a somewhat finer grained breakdown. What these data show is that what theyre measuring is patternless and random.
       </p>
       <p>
        The one message I take from that figure is this: dudes, your app doesnt work.
       </p>
