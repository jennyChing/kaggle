        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Lending Club and BancAlliance Take On Big Banks Together
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-02-11T14:57:23+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 11, 2015
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://lendingclubipo.com/author/jondyer9/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Jonathan Dyer
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p class="p1">
         <span class="s1">
          After announcing partnerships with Google and Alibaba since their blockbuster IPO last December, Lending Club once again expanded their reach this week.
          <span class="Apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          The
          <a href="https://www.lendingclub.com/" target="_blank">
           peer to peer lending giant
          </a>
          announced a deal with BancAlliance, which is made up of around 200 community banks.
          <span class="Apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          According to
          <a href="http://www.wsj.com/articles/lending-club-small-u-s-banks-plan-new-consumer-loan-program-1423458187" target="_blank">
           <span class="s2">
            The Wall Street Journal
           </span>
          </a>
          , the deal will send loan customers Lending Clubs way through the banks associated with the BancAlliance.
          <span class="Apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          Additionally the alliance has agreed to purchase a certain number of loans from Lending Club as part of the partnership.
          <img alt="" class=" aligncenter" height="397" src="http://si.wsj.net/public/resources/images/MI-CH718_LENDIN_9U_20150208161807.jpg" width="459"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <span class="s1">
          The dealbrings the much-touted efficiency of the Lending Club platform to small banks that have suffered from their lack of resources in recent years.
          <span class="Apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          In fact research shows that small banks have been toppled handily by the big banks in the consumer loan market since the mid-1990s.
          <span class="Apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          By partnering with Lending Club, the members of the BancAlliance will be able to grow their loan portfolios while minimizing the types of risks that could wipe out institutions of their size.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p1">
         <span class="s1">
          On the wave of partnership news, Lending Clubs stock closed up 6% on Monday following the BancAlliance announcement.
          <span class="Apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          Surprisingly, despite their last two partnerships with titans of their industries, another
          <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/moneybeat/2015/02/10/lending-clubs-new-deals-fuel-investor-excitement/" target="_blank">
           <span class="s2">
            Wall Street Journal article
           </span>
          </a>
          speculates that the BancAlliance deal could be the biggest yet for Lending Club.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
