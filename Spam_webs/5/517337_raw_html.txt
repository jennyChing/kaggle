      <p>
       The
       <i>
        <a href="/index.php/PowerPedia:Stirling_Engine" title="PowerPedia:Stirling Engine">
         Stirling engine
        </a>
       </i>
       was invented in 1816 by the Rev. Robert Stirling who sought to create a safer alternative to the steam engines of the time, whose boilers often exploded due to the high pressure of the steam and the primitive materials of the time.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline">
        Comments
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       "There are lots of little advantages that can be played with Stirling engines and phase change materials, but in general there is nothing there for utilization that will boost us to a higher level of energy utilization.
      </p>
      <p>
       "What the world could use to good advantage is a cheap Stirling design.  Don't know of any great ones.  I only know of Dean Kaman's design with Decca Engineering (I think it is called, or Deka?) in Manchester, NH.  Cheap and simple, but not cheap and simple enough."
      </p>
      <p>
       --
       <a href="/index.php/Congress:Advisor:Kenneth_M._Rauen" title="Congress:Advisor:Kenneth M. Rauen">
        Ken Rauen
       </a>
       , PES Network Inc, Science Advisor (Feb. 9, 2006)
      </p>
