      <h1>
       5 Erotic Movies from Europe
      </h1>
     </figcaption>
     <img alt="" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/themes/fullscreen-2015/images/blank.gif"/>
    </figure>
    <div class="content-section-holder clear">
     <div id="content">
      <div class="entry entry_single">
       <img alt="tilda swinton and Ewan McGregor in young adam movie 2003" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70396" height="1474" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-01.jpg" width="2000"/>
       <div class="adv-single-top-leaderboard-box">
        <div class="adv-single-top-leaderboard">
         <div class="bsarocks bsap_b48753df2718669f8dec2f2fc8fc228a" id="bsap_1275142">
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p class="warning">
        <strong>
         Warning:
        </strong>
        Contains content with sexual references.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span class="intro">
         It is an established truth that European cinema, compared to others, has a different attitude to depictions of erotic themes and sex. In the USA, for example, screen violence is all fine and dandy. But nudity, and particularly
         <em>
          thrusting
         </em>
         , is seen as a big no-no by the
         <a href="http://www.mpaa.org/" target="_blank">
          MPAA
         </a>
         .
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        Many erotic-themed films, when released stateside, have been stamped with the dreaded NC-17 rating. This effectively ruins any chance of a film making money. Whereas in Europe, scenes with intercourse can produce a discussion on aestheticsand subject matterwhile often causing a sensation at festivals. Ultimately, there is a great philosophical and cultural difference. European movies, especially in the 2000s, have continued what is a rich and daring tradition.
       </p>
       <h6>
        Tilda Swinton and Ewan McGregor star in David Mackenzies Young Adam.
       </h6>
       <img alt="ADAM (Ewan McGregor9 WITH lover in young adam 2003" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70397" height="996" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-02.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <img alt="adam lying on floor while talking to lover in young adam 2003" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70395" height="844" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-03.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        The famous custard-and-ketchup scene between Joe (McGregor) and Cathy (Emily Mortimer, pictured) caused a stir on release back in 2003.
       </h6>
       <h2>
        Young Adam
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0533284/" target="_blank">
         David Mackenzie
        </a>
        s film of Alexander Trocchis 1954 novel, 
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Young_Adam" target="_blank">
         Young Adam
        </a>
        , featured a notorious scene, in which Ewan McGregors drifter and wannabe writer, Joe, throws custard and tomato ketchup over the semi-naked body of his girlfriend, Cathy (played with daring commitment by Emily Mortimer). An erotic thriller set on a coal barge in 1950s Glasgow sounds highly unlikelyand unsexybut Young Adam is an underrated gem. Its also notable for the musical score by
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talking_Heads" target="_blank">
         Talking Heads
        </a>
        legend,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Byrne" target="_blank">
         David Byrne
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <img alt="romantic couple, silhouette, 9 songs movie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70399" height="898" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-05.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <img alt="couple on the beach, lying down and hugging in 9 songs" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70400" height="1080" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-04.jpg" width="1835"/>
       <h6>
        Michael Winterbottoms explicit drama was a daring moment in British cinema.
       </h6>
       <h2>
        9 Songs
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0935863/" target="_blank">
         Michael Winterbottom
        </a>
        s proto-hipster relationship drama featured trendy indie rock bandsincluding Franz Ferdinandand hardcore sex scenes with real actors, not porno performers. 9 Songs, to quote British critic Derek Malcolm, looks like a porn movie, but feels like a love story. Ten years on, 9 Songs can be seen as a very unusual entry in the filmography of Winterbottom (admittedly an always eclectic director), and a time when British cinema ventured to imitate a more European attitude.
       </p>
       <img alt="woman lying in bed with hairy armpits in anatomy of hell" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70407" height="762" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-06.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <img alt="woman lying in bed in anatomy of hell movie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70408" height="767" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-07.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Catherine Breillats work is controversial, sometimes revolting, but always fascinating.
       </h6>
       <h2>
        Anatomy of Hell
       </h2>
       <p>
        There are
        <em>
         auteurs
        </em>
        that stoke debate, and then theres
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Breillat" target="_blank">
         Catherine Breillat
        </a>
        . Filming her own novel, the intriguingly titled 
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Pornocracy-Semiotext-Native-Catherine-Breillat/dp/1584350474" target="_blank">
         Pornocracy
        </a>
        , the French directors sexually explicit picture starred real-life sleaze merchant Rocco Siffredi, and Amira Cesar. Anatomy of Hell went down like a lead balloon over in the USA, but Breillat is considered a fascinating director in Europe, whose feminist explorations of sexualityno matter how extremehave earned her a mighty reputation.
       </p>
       <img alt="two men dancing with woman in a hotel bedroom in Nymphomaniac movie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70405" height="844" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-08.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <img alt="couple having an erotic moment in bedroom, Nymphomaniac movie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70406" height="1124" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-09.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Charlotte Gainsbourg stars as Joe in Lars von Triers epic investigation of psycho-sexuality.
       </h6>
       <h2>
        Nymphomaniac Vol 1 &amp; 2
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="www.imdb.com/name/nm0001885/" target="_blank">
         Lars von Trier
        </a>
        can push buttons like nobody else. Starring Charlotte Gainsbourg and Stacey Martin (in what is an extraordinary debut), Nymphomaniac was released in two parts and ran almost four hours. As with all von Trier flicks, Nymphomaniac was equally hailed as a masterpiece and a load of rubbish. The truth lies somewhere in between.
       </p>
       <img alt="man looking at sexy woman on sofa, venus in fur movie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70404" height="840" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-11.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <img alt="man ready to kiss woman in venus in fur" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-70410" height="852" src="http://illusion.scene360.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/erotic-movies-europe-12.jpg" width="1500"/>
       <h6>
        Mathieu Almaric and Emmanuelle Seigner headline Roman Polanskis playful theatre adaptation.
       </h6>
       <h2>
        Venus in Fur
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Polanski" target="_blank">
         Roman Polanski
        </a>
        adapted the hit
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venus_in_Fur" target="_blank">
         Broadway play
        </a>
        by David Ives for the screen, which in turn took as its source of inspiration the classic 1870 novella, 
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venus_in_Furs" target="_blank">
         Venus in Furs
        </a>
        , by Leopold Sacher-Masoch. Starring Emmanuelle Seigner and Mathieu Almaric, a dissatisfied theatre director searching for an actress to headline his new production, meets the initially unsuitable Wanda (Seigner). A playful and cleverly deconstructed battle-of-the-sexes saga ensues. The film received mixed reviews from the critics, but is well worth checking out.
       </p>
