        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-family: arial black,avant garde; font-size: 14px;">
           Our Friend, Florida Frenz wrote this letter when she was twelve. A younger girl wrote letters to Florida, asking her for advice. Floridas views on friendship reflects her perspective as a girl with autism. We thought that the advice Florida gave her was so good that we wanted to share it with you. You can read more of her letters on our
           <span style="color: #0000ff;">
            <a href="http://mosswoodconnections.com/activities/new-socialemotional-page/">
             <span style="color: #0000ff;">
              social-emotional page.
             </span>
            </a>
           </span>
           <br/>
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          <em>
           Everyone should read her book: How to Be Human; Diary of an Autistic Girl.
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Florida-Frenz-Author-Picture.jpg">
          <img alt="Florida Frenz Author Picture" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-997" height="155" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/130x155xFlorida-Frenz-Author-Picture.jpg.pagespeed.ic.6T6m-7fHVb.jpg" width="130"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          <strong>
           <em>
            Here is Floridas advice on friendship. (I am happy to report that Florida is a thriving high school student with a wonderful circle of friends.):
           </em>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Rachel-
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Some friends arent meant to have a ton of friends. When I was your age I became quite sad when I just couldnt figure out how to be the popular girl in my group. Having a few good friends wasnt enough. Also, I wanted a very close girlfriend A.K.A a best friend, forever. Since then, Ive found neither but Im still happy. Why? With being popular there would be a sacrifice of your personal space and free time. Instead it would be filled with playdates and phone calls which in small quantities are fun, but in the quantities popular girls get are overwhelming. As for best friends forever, its sooo nice to have a few close friends but 1 best friend forever could get boring. If you think about people as stories than youd want a few great stories you know well, a lot of good stories you dont know as well, mixed in occasionally with a new, unknown story. If you had just 1 best friend forever than it would be like knowing 1 story so well that you could chant it in your sleep. Yet if your to popular than you hear too many stories and forget them all. Its good to have the perfect balance in the middle of those where you may not be the most popular, but youre happy.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           Florida
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <em>
          <span style="font-family: helvetica;">
           (Update: Since Florida wrote this letter she has made many close friendships including that elusive BFF.)
          </span>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="Friendship Meter 2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7202" height="729" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/506x729xFriendship-Meter-2.jpg.pagespeed.ic.uvpm0tTgJX.jpg" width="506"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-size: 12pt; color: #800080;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            When a younger child with special needs started writing to Florida for advice on how to handle otherness, Florida responded with some priceless recommendations based on her own experiences:
           </strong>
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: 'book antiqua', palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Dear Rachel,
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: 'book antiqua', palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          When I learned I was Autistic, I cried about it for many months. It was good to know why I was different and why I had adults helping me and not other children around. Still it was sad knowing that I possessed a disability that I hadnt asked anyone for and that would make me unable to do some things for the rest of my life. Its so unfair to have to work so much harder on so many things!
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: 'book antiqua', palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Grrr! That still makes me sad and frustrated now.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: 'book antiqua', palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          The good thing is most of the time, I really dont think about having Autism now, because I realized that constantly grieving wouldnt get me anywhere and that our strengths are more important to making us successful than most of our weaknesses. Be sure to work on your weaknesses, too, though.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: 'book antiqua', palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           Florida
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="Florida Frenz Postcard" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1017" height="460" src="http://mosswoodconnections.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/460x460xFlorida-Frenz-Postcard-e1393178704105.jpg.pagespeed.ic.MGN6g2TGUd.jpg" width="460"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            Florida gives advice on handling hurt feelings:
           </strong>
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          Dear Rachel,
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          I used to get hurt feelings from people around me, but not understand they were hurt or why. Later
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          (hours, days, weeks, even months afterwards), I would suddenly explode about something I usually could
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          handle, and Shelah (my therapist) would guess something was up. When youre upset over something, its
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          even harder to process your feelings and put words to your emotions. Shelah often had to let me explain the
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          full story and then suggest many different feelings I could have before I could say how I felt and why.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          Eventually, I learned to do this for myself. The key was constantly being open and honest with myself.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          Being like that takes practice, but if you work on constantly processing your feelings, then one day, youll be
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
          able to do it on your own.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">
           Florida
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt; color: #800080;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            And my personal favorite- How to distract teachers:
           </strong>
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Dear Rachel,
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Since you asked, heres my list of Slick Tricks for Distracting Teachers:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          1) THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT ONE!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          THE CHAT BOMB!
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          This can work miracles when applied right. Often we use this in my English class and reduce the work period to a half hour or less! Heres how to do it.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Ask the person a complicated question, not one like, Whats your favorite color? Because that has a simpleanswer. If you ask a complicated question, youll get acomplicated answer, which might inspire more questionsfrom you which will get a conversation going. Once thathappens, the chat bomb has been released.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          2) THE BATHROOM
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          It isnt uncommon for people to spend as much as ten minutes in the bathroom, so if you just need a little break, just
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          lie, saying you need to go. This should give you a safe ten to fifteen minutes. However, if you take twenty in the bathroom,
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          people will get suspicious and youll get into trouble. Also, you cant just spend that time wandering around
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          randomly. You need to literally walk to the bathroom and shut the stall door. People will get suspicious if they see a random
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          kid wandering around during class time and youll be in trouble. The bathroom technique is best used when youre upset and you dont want others to see you cry. Simply excuse yourself for the bathroom and cry behind a closed stall.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          3) LOSING OR DROPPING SOMETHING
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Pretend to lose something and spend a bit of time searching for it. This isnt the best or the funnest, but will still
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          work. Also you can purposefully-accidentally-on-purpose drop your pencil and pretend that you cant reach it, no matter
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          how hard you try. Eventually someone will come along and help you, but its still worth a try.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Remember, these tricks are precious. Use them sparingly. You MAY get into trouble using any of the tricks above too much.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
          Try the Chat Bomb first.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-family: georgia, palatino; font-size: 12pt;">
           Florida
         </span>
        </p>
