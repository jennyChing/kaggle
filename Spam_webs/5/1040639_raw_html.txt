          <p>
           As we get towards the end of March, its time to spring clean your bike and go cycling! With longer days and better weather in front of us, cycling in the great outdoors is the perfect way to enjoy it. There really is no better way to keep in shape both physically and mentally. Leaping straight onto a bike which may have just spent the last few months gathering dust in a garage or shed, is not the best idea though. Some basic maintenance before taking it out is advised. Heres how to spring clean your bike and go cycling.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Spring Clean Your Bike and Go Cycling
          </h3>
          <p>
           Perhaps the first thing to be done before you go cycling, is to give the bike a quick clean and wash down. Warm, soapy water should do the trick, and its just the thing to remove dust, dirt, cobwebs and anything else that might be stuck to it. Dont forget to clean the chain. We will be looking at this later anyway.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_3942" style="width: 235px">
           <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/0857331183/ref=as_li_tf_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1634&amp;creative=6738&amp;creativeASIN=0857331183&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=davestravelpages-21">
            <img alt="Cycling book" class="size-full wp-image-3942 " height="400" src="http://www.davestravelpages.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/bike-book1.jpg" title="Bike Book" width="225"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            This bicycle maintenance book represents the best 20 pence I ever spent at a car boot sale! I highly recommend getting one.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Does the bike look as good as new, or at least as close as its going to get? Good, lets check out the tyres. If your bicycle has been left standing for a long period of time, you can guarantee that the tyres will be flat! This is only to be expected, and does not necessarily mean you have a puncture. Pump them up, and leave them for five or ten minutes. Its a good excuse to go and make a cup of tea or coffee! If they are still inflated after that time, result. If they go flat again, its time to fix it. You can follow this link to read about how to
           <a href="http://www.davestravelpages.com/bicycle-maintenance/60-how-to-change-a-bicycle-inner-tube.html">
            replace a bicycle inner tube
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           We havent quite finished with the tyres and wheels. Have a look at the tyres, and see if you notice any signs of cracks in the rubber. The reason for this, is that older, cheaper tyres do perish slightly over time. If the cracking is severe, it makes sense to buy some new ones. Now take a look at the wheel in general, and run your fingers over the spokes. Broken spokes will need to be replaced, and if this is a task you feel is out of your range, take it to a bike shop. (Make it a privately owned one though rather than a chain store. Lets keep local businesses alive!) If your spokes are fine, ensure that the quick release skewers or bolts which go through the hub are tight. Finally, spin the wheels to make sure they go around unimpeded.
          </p>
          <p>
           Next up is the chain. Chances are, that this will look a little rusty and make a bit of a grinding noise as you turn it. This is what oil is for, so use some! If you think that the chain needs replacing, then do so. Again, if its beyond you, take the bike into a local shop. Most bike shops will offer servicing.
          </p>
          <p>
           With tyres inflated, wheels that go around, and a chain to drive it, we need to start thinking about how to stop. Youve got it, lets test the brakes! Lift the front wheel off the ground with the handlebars, and spin the wheel. Apply the front brakes. If the wheel stops, the brakes work. Quite simple really! Spin the wheel again, just to make sure that the brakes are not sticking. Repeat this with the rear wheel. You may find that the brakes need adjusting. I wont go into it here, but either search about it online or buy a bike book. Whatever you do, dont go cycling with brakes that do not work properly. Its just a recipe for disaster.
          </p>
          <p>
           There are one or two other things to do before you go cycling on your bike. Tighten any nuts that may need it. Spray some oil on nut heads and moving parts. Make sure the seat is the right height and is tightened. Ensure the handlebars are tightened. And thats pretty much it. Its really not rocket science! A basic spring clean of your bike wont take more than 20 minutes, but it is an important 20 minutes, especially if the bike has been left standing for any period of time. Keeping on top of bicycle maintenance on a regular basis will also ensure that the bike is in good enough condition to go cycling on throughout the year. Now, go outside and start pedalling!
          </p>

           <a href="http://www.davestravelpages.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/dave-salt.jpg">
            <img alt="Dave Briggs" class=" wp-image-1631 alignleft" height="158" src="http://www.davestravelpages.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/07/dave-salt-300x225.jpg" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px;" title="Dave Briggs" width="210"/>

          <p dir="ltr">
           Hi, Im Dave Briggs, and welcome to my travel blog Daves Travel Pages. Back in 1997, I decided that the 9-5 life wasnt for me, and I headed out on my first around the world trip.
          </p>
          <p dir="ltr">
           Since then, I have driven a car around Australia, ridden trains through India, cycled around New Zealand, backpacked through Asia, taken chicken buses through Central America, rode a horse around Easter Island, cycled from England to Cape town, been a nightclub bouncer in Sweden, and spent 18 months bicycle touring from Alaska to Argentina. Did I forget anything? Yes! Thats why I keep this adventure travel blog  Its as much to remind me what I have done, as it is to inspire you to go out and do stuff!
          </p>
          <p>
           Who am I? Im nobody special. I work, I save, I travel. Thats it really. Over the years, I have endured some not great jobs, in order to have some of the most meaningful adventures. Recently though, I have developed my freelance writing and travel blog to the extent that I can now support my travels.
          </p>
          <p>
           Currently based in Athens, I am blogging about Greece whilst planning the next adventure. Bicycle touring around the world? A 4WD trip through Africa? Sign up to the monthly newsletter below, and I will keep you posted.
          </p>
