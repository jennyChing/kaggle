      <h3 class="title-post">
       Karmas a Bitch: Neighbor Trying to Shut Down 12-Year-Olds Lemonade Stand Gets Into Ironic Legal Trouble
      </h3>
      <div class="header-bottom">
      </div>
      <!-- header-bottom -->
     </header>
     <div class="entry-content clearfix">
      <div style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
       <img src="http://capitalismisfreedom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/bar2.jpg"/>
      </div>
      <div style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
       <!-- /169723673/topbanner -->
       <div id="div-gpt-ad-1432056361744-0">

      <p>
       <a href="http://capitalismisfreedom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/lemonADE.jpg">
        <img alt="lemonADE" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20470" height="361" src="http://capitalismisfreedom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/lemonADE.jpg" width="620"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Those who live by bureaucracy, die by bureaucracy.
      </p>
      <p>
       Remember T.J. Guerrero, the 12-year-old entrepreneur who has faced years of official complaints from a
       <a href="http://www.theblaze.com/stories/2014/08/25/one-man-has-waged-a-two-year-battle-to-shut-down-this-kids-lemonade-stand/" target="_blank">
        crotchety neighbor bent on shutting down the boys lemonade stand
       </a>
       ?
      </p>
      <p>
       In the week since Guerreros story went viral, the preteens business has boomed, the
       <a href="http://www.tampabay.com/news/humaninterest/donations-pour-in-for-dunedin-boy-and-his-lemonade-stand-neighbor-under/2195203" target="_blank">
        Tampa Bay Times reported
       </a>
       , while the crotchety neighbor, 61-year-old Doug Wilkey, is facing ironic legal trouble: It seems that while Wilkey was trying to get T.J.s illegal business shut down, he was running his own unlicensed business out of his house.
      </p>
      <p>
       Wilkey had listed his Dunedin, Florida, home address as the principal business address for Bayport Financial Services, a tipster told city officials.
      </p>
      <div style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">

       </ins>
       <script>
        (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       Wilkey was apparently skipping out on a $45-a-year business tax license and other paperwork, and Dunedins planning directorGreg Rice told the Times that Wilkey could start facing $250 daily fines if he doesnt come into compliance.
      </p>
      <p>
       The irony is [Wilkeys] not following the rules either, or doesnt seem to be, Rice said.
      </p>
      <p>
       Wilkey has tried to stay out of the spotlight as the local story reached the national stage, ordering reporters off of his property on Thursday, the Times noted.
      </p>
