        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Smashed Sweet Potato Guacamole Bites
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-01-26T06:00:14+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 26, 2015
         </time>
         
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/smashed-sweet-potato-guacamole-bites/#comments">
           34 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14695787" data-link="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/smashed-sweet-potato-guacamole-bites/" data-summary="" data-title="Smashed Sweet Potato Guacamole Bites">
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          These smashed sweet potato guacamole bites have crispy sides with soft centers. Topped with a crispy piece of salty bacon, theyre the perfect game day bite.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         So were apparently getting anywhere from 18-36 inches of snow today and tomorrow. Even the low end of that forecast sucks. 18 inches pretty much guarantees power outages around here and even though we have a generator and a wood stove, neither of those things will bring me the internet when it goes out. I mean, who cares about the lights, Id rather have theability to waste my entire day away on Facebook and Netflix.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Smashed sweet potato guacamole bites" class="alignnone wp-image-14781 size-full" height="840" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Smashed-sweet-potato-guacamole-bites-1-optimized.jpg" title="Smashed sweet potato guacamole bites" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         But thats all the shit talking Im going to do about this storm becauseyou know what its done?
        </p>
        <p>
         Its gotten people to shut the hell up about deflated balls and the Super Bowl while they freak out about stocking up on eggs, milk and bread instead.
        </p>
        <p>
         Hall-e-freakin-lujah.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Sweet potato guacamole bites" class="alignnone wp-image-14782 size-full" height="776" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Smashed-sweet-potato-guacamole-bites-2-optimized.jpg" title="Sweet potato guacamole bites" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you havent been around for any other Super Bowl season, please refer to this
         <a href="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/edamame-dip/" target="_blank">
          edamame dip
         </a>
         for my thoughts on the sport of football. Its pretty succinct.
        </p>
        <p>
         The one and only good thing about the Super Bowl, is and always will be, the food.
        </p>
        <p>
         A day full of eating buffalo, baconized everything and cheesy dips galore is really hard to pass up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-14779">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Smashed sweet potato guacamole bacon bites" class="alignnone wp-image-14783 size-full" height="750" src="http://www.runningtothekitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Smashed-sweet-potato-guacamole-bites-3-optimized.jpg" title="Smashed sweet potato guacamole bacon bites" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         These sweet potato guacamole bites are definitely on the healthier side of the Super Bowl food spectrum but that doesnt mean they dont have some serious game popability. Their crispy edges, soft middles, spicy guac centers and salty bacon top make them perfect for throwing back while you watch half-illiterate fat men run around a field and horribly lip-synced half-time shows.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">

         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          These smashed sweet potato guacamole bites have crispy sides with soft centers. Topped with a crispy piece of salty bacon, they're the perfect game day bite.
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Gina Matsoukas
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6-8
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 medium sweet potatoes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            extra virgin olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            salt and pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 avocados
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            juice of  a lime
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 clove garlic, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             jalapeno, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup chopped cilantro
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 slices bacon, cooked
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Clean sweet potatoes leaving skin on. Place in a large pot and cover with water. Boil potatoes for about 20 minutes until tender.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 450 degrees.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove potatoes from water, slice into thick rounds (about  inch) and place on a greased baking sheet.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Using a fork, gently smash the tops of the potatoes.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Drizzle the potato rounds with olive oil and season liberally with salt and pepper.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place potatoes in the oven for 20-22 minutes until the edges are crispy and starting to turn golden brown.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            While potatoes cook, combine the avocados, lime juice, garlic, jalapeno and cilantro in a small bowl. Mash together until smooth.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Chop the bacon into  inch pieces and set aside.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove the potatoes from the oven, top with a spoonful of the guacamole and a piece of bacon.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Serve warm.
           </li>
          </ol>
