               He specifically called Murdoch out for his tweet praising
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/01/10/brother-paris-policeman/" target="_blank">
                Ahmed Merabet
               </a>
               , the Muslim police officer who was killed during the
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/01/07/charlie-hebdo-paris-attack/">
                attack on the
                <em>
                 Charlie Hebdo
                </em>
                offices
               </a>
               in Paris.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                <a href="https://twitter.com/rupertmurdoch" target="_blank">
                 @rupertmurdoch
                </a>
                Quit back peddling you racist piece of shit
               </p>
               <p>
                 Aziz Ansari (@azizansari)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/azizansari/status/554475045334958082" target="_blank">
                 January 12, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               Ansari wasn't the only celebrity to speak out against Murdoch. Author
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/jk-rowling/">
                J.K. Rowling
               </a>
               tweeted that she would give up her Christian upbringing if it meant not being responsible for Rupert Murdoch.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                I was born Christian. If that makes Rupert Murdoch my responsibility, I'll auto-excommunicate.
                <a href="http://t.co/Atw1wNk8UX" target="_blank">
                 http://t.co/Atw1wNk8UX
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                 J.K. Rowling (@jk_rowling)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/jk_rowling/status/554229281929564160" target="_blank">
                 January 11, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               <strong>
                UPDATE, Jan. 14:
               </strong>
               Murdoch tried to clarify his remarks on Twitter on Wednesday, but Ansari was ready with a comeback.
              </p>

               <p>
                Certainly did not mean all Muslims responsible for Paris attack. But Muslim community must debate and confront extremism.
