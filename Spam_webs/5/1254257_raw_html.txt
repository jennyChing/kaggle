        <p>
         In late November, the folks turning LBJ Freeway into
         <a href="http://transportationblog.dallasnews.com/2012/11/with-new-videos-showing-completed-designs-lbj-express-wants-you-to-know-theres-a-light-at-the-end-of-the-tunnel.html/" target="_blank">
          the toll-laned LBJ Express unveiled three animated renderings of the $2.7(ish)-billion redo
         </a>
         due to hit the checkered flag by December 2015(ish). That was just a small taste to get you hooked on the good stuff: Above is the just-finished product, a five-minute virtual drive along LBJ all the way from IH-35E past N. Central Expressway.
        </p>
        <p>
         Like itll take ever take you just five minutes to drive from Stemmons to Central.
        </p>
        <p>
         The video, says LBJ Express spokesman Andy Rittler, really shows the size and breadth of this project. Its the first time you see the full expanse, from the Loop 12 area as well as the 35-635 interchange. You can see how large that is when you pan out. Once youve bailed out just before Greenville Avenue, says Rittler, You really get to see what type of an impact this has on North Dallas.
        </p>
        <p>
         But dont get too giddy: The 635 redo is only about 40 percent done, and, per Rittler, they havent even begun to dig out those managed toll lanes thatll be sunk in the middle. The bulk of the excavation will take place closer to the end of the summer, he says. When done, crews will have removed 8.7 million cubic feet worth of dirt  enough, says Rittler, to fill Cowboys Stadium two and a half times.
        </p>
        <p>
         The new virtual fly-through shows you things you might have missed before  like, say, toll entrances off of 35E. Rittlers provided a stick map below to show you exactly where those gantries will be located when the things done.
        </p>
        <p>
         Those toll lanes off Stemmons wont be open till April 2015. But come December of this year, crews should be done with the easternmost segment of the project  from just around Preston to Greenville, including the managed toll lanes.
        </p>
        <p>
         But remember, its a phased approach, says Rittler. Once we get these main lanes on the outside built, we have to demolish all of the old highway  the whole thing  and all of the bridges across it. Then we have to do the digging. And in the case of Webb Chapel, Josey, Midway and Valley View, we have to cut the roads in the half to get the lanes underneath the cross streets.
        </p>
        <p>
         Which means another year or so of inconvenience, as Rittler puts it, based on the fact we have the frontage roads still under construction, as well as the main lanes. But once we do those, the large majority of the construction will out of the way of commuters  the toll lanes. Well be building in the center, and traffic will not be obstructed. So thats good news for drivers, at least from that standpoint.
