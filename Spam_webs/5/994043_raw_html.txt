												   &lt;div class="twitter-follow-header"&gt;&lt;div class="tweet fa fa-twitter"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="https://twitter.com/@EdWhelanEPPC"&gt;@EdWhelanEPPC&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;

										&lt;section&gt;&lt;/section&gt;

					   &lt;p style="background:white"&gt;As I noted in my update to my &lt;a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/bench-memos/393074/chief-judge-kozinski-responds-ed-whelan"&gt;Q&amp;A&lt;/a&gt; with Ninth Circuit chief judge Alex Kozinski, a Republican appointee to the Ninth Circuit informed me last Friday that he or she had never heard of the clerks office supposed &lt;a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/bench-memos/392889/hijinks-ninth-circuit-clerks-office-ed-whelan"&gt;longstanding practice&lt;/a&gt; of assigning expedited cases to the available panel with the most senior presiding judge. &lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p style="background:white"&gt;I doubt very much that this supposed practicewhich purportedly helps to explain some of Judge Stephen Reinhardts seemingly amazing propensity to be selected to sit on ideologically charged caseswas in fact uniformly applied. Im not in a position to conduct a thorough review of the clerks calendaring practices, but I have immediately run across an apparent anomalyin the appeal of the anti-Prop 8 ruling, as it happens.&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p style="background:white"&gt;In August 2010, a motions panel &lt;a href="http://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/general/2010/08/16/order_motion_stay.pdf"&gt;ordered&lt;/a&gt; that the anti-Prop 8 appeal be expedited and be calendared during the week of December 6, 2010, at [the courthouse] in San Francisco. From the &lt;a href="http://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/calendaring/2010/11/29/sf12_10e.pdf"&gt;calendar&lt;/a&gt; for that week, we learn that the panel in San Francisco with the most senior presiding judge included &lt;a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mary_M._Schroeder"&gt;Mary M. Schroeder&lt;/a&gt;, who joined the Ninth Circuit in 1979 (Reinhardt joined it in 1980) and did not leave active service until 2012. Why, then, did the anti-Prop 8 appeal not get assigned to her panel?&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p style="background:white"&gt;More generally, insofar as this supposed practice is meant to explain Reinhardts unusual case assignments, it ought also to have resulted in even more appealing case assignments for the active judges senior to Reinhardti.e., Kozinski, Harry Pregerson, and (until 2012) Schroeder. But the concerns that Ive heard about unusual Ninth Circuit assignments focus specially on Reinhardt.&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p style="background:white"&gt;Also, while Im grateful that Kozinski responded to my inquiries, his responses strongly suggest a real lack of interest on his part in judicial administration. As I discussed in this &lt;a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/bench-memos/390294/re-somewhat-higher-one-787-trillion-ed-whelan"&gt;post&lt;/a&gt;, &lt;span style="letter-spacing: 0.1pt;"&gt;this&lt;span class="apple-converted-space"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;i&gt;Texas Law Review&lt;/i&gt;&lt;span class="apple-converted-space"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="http://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=957650"&gt;&lt;span style="color:#333333"&gt;article&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;span class="apple-converted-space"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;from 2000, titled Neutral Assignment of Judges at the Court of Appeals, surveys assignment systems in the federal courts of appeals and concludes that all systems permit a certain level of discretion and human intervention that, in the wrong circumstances, could be abused.&lt;span class="apple-converted-space"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="apple-converted-space"&gt;&lt;span style="letter-spacing: 0.1pt;"&gt;It finds specific faults with the Ninth Circuits case-assignment procedures. But heres how my exchange with Kozinski went:&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;blockquote&gt;
&lt;p style="background:white"&gt;[EW:] What steps, if any, have you or your predecessors aschief judge taken to ensure that no individual in theclerks office has discretion to assign a known case to aknown panel?&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p style="background: white;"&gt;[AK:] We train and supervise our staff and trust them to followinstructions.&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p style="background: white;"&gt;[EW:] (The law-review article that I refer to in this&lt;span class="apple-converted-space"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/bench-memos/390294/re-somewhat-higher-one-787-trillion-ed-whelan"&gt;&lt;span style="color:#333333"&gt;post&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;might be of interest to you.)&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p style="background: white;"&gt;[AK:] No.&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;/blockquote&gt;

&lt;p style="background:white"&gt;As Kozinski himself has just &lt;a href="http://billingsgazette.com/news/state-and-regional/montana/montana-s-th-circuit-judge-sid-thomas-to-become-next/article_624b2c54-1238-5727-8eb0-9eb50b10cd92.html"&gt;noted&lt;/a&gt;(in an article about his incoming successor as chief judge), chief judges &lt;span style="background-image: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-size: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial; background-position: initial; background-repeat: initial;"&gt;dont get picked because of [their] administrative skills.&lt;/span&gt; In a well-functioning judicial bureaucracy with a well-designed case-assignment system, it might well be sensible merely to train and supervise our staff and trust them to follow instructions. But Kozinskis head-in-the-sand approach leaves lots of room for concern that the Ninth Circuits case-assignment system may have been abused for ideological purposes.&lt;p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
