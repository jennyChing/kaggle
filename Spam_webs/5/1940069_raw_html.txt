       <h1 class="entry-title">
        100% whole wheat zucchini chocolate chip bread
        <span class="subtitle">
         : when the horn of plenty becomes the horn of too much
        </span>
       </h1>

      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        Paula Deen does it.
       </p>
       <p>
        Allrecipes.com does it.
       </p>
       <p>
        Taste of Home, cdkitchen, and food.com all do it.
       </p>
       <p>
        Even LiveStrong does it.
       </p>
       <p>
        But sometimes, King Arthur Flour just does it better.
       </p>
       <p>
        Or at least with more fiber, vitamins, and minerals.
       </p>
       <p>
        And it is?
       </p>
       <p>
        Zucchini chocolate chip bread.
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/recipes/100-whole-wheat-zucchini-chocolate-chip-bread-recipe">
         100% Whole Wheat Zucchini Chocolate Chip Bread,
        </a>
        to be exact.
       </p>
       <p>
        Quick breads  you know, those batters that you just stir together, pour into a pan, and bake  are the ideal place to substitute whole wheat flour for all-purpose.
       </p>
       <p>
        This is especially true for quick breads made with fruit (or, in this case, zucchini): whole grains need more liquid than all-purpose flour. The liquid in fruit helps soften wheats bran, making it less likely to produce a crumbly loaf.
       </p>
       <p>
        Unlike yeast bread, these baking powder breads dont rise mightily; they just casually poke their heads up over the rim of the pan.
       </p>
       <p>
        And thats just fine; no one expects your typical
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/recipes/banana-bread-recipe">
         banana bread,
        </a>
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/recipes/cornbread-recipe">
         cornbread,
        </a>
        or
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/recipes/low-fat-date-nut-bread-recipe">
         date-nut loaf
        </a>
        to look like the Goodyear Blimp.
       </p>
       <p>
        A gently curved top is just fine, thank you; no rainbows required.
       </p>
       <p>
        And thats one big reason whole wheat flour is perfect in these breads. With the bran in whole wheat doing its best to destroy a rising loafs gluten, it takes some skill to make a
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/recipes/100-whole-wheat-sandwich-bread-recipe">
         100% whole wheat sandwich loaf
        </a>
        rise to towering heights.
       </p>
       <p>
        Not so
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/recipes/heavenly-healthy-banana-bread-recipe">
         100% whole wheat banana bread.
        </a>
        Or this whole wheat zucchini chocolate chip bread, both of which receive a quick-n-easy tag on our
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/recipes/100-whole-wheat-zucchini-chocolate-chip-bread-recipe">
         new recipe site
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Its all about managing expectations, right?
       </p>
       <p>
        When you bake this bread, expect a moist, pleasantly dense, flavorful loaf, packed with zucchini and chocolate.
       </p>
       <p>
        You wont be disappointed  we promise.
       </p>
       <p>
        So long as youre not looking for that famous blimp
       </p>
       <p>
        Preheat the oven to 350F; lightly grease a
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/shop/items/9-x-5-loaf-pan">
         9 x 5 loaf pan.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48972" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6529.jpg" title="IMG_6529"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Put the following in a mixing bowl:
       </p>
       <p>
        2 large eggs
        <br/>
        1/3 cup molasses or honey
        <br/>
        1/2 cup vegetable oil
        <br/>
        1/3 cup brown sugar
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/shop/items/vanilla-bean-crush-8-oz">
         vanilla extract
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Beat until well combined.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48974" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6530.jpg" title="IMG_6530"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Add the following:
       </p>
       <p>
        2 cups (8 ounces)
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/shop/items/king-arthur-white-whole-wheat-flour-5-lb">
         King Arthur White Whole Wheat Flour
        </a>
        or
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/shop/items/king-arthur-premium-100-whole-wheat-flour-5-lb">
         Premium Whole Wheat Flour
        </a>
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon salt
        <br/>
        1/2 teaspoon baking soda
        <br/>
        1/2 teaspoon baking powder
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon ground
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/shop/items/vietnamese-cinnamon-2-oz">
         cinnamon
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Mix until well combined.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48976" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6531.jpg" title="IMG_6531"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Add the following:
       </p>
       <p>
        2 cups (8 to 12 ounces) shredded, unpeeled zucchini (about 1 small/medium zucchini)*
        <br/>
        1 cup chocolate chips (or raisins, or dried cranberries, if you dont like the chocolate/zucchini combination)
        <br/>
        3/4 cup chopped walnuts, optional
       </p>
       <p>
        *Zucchini weight varies a lot, depending on how juicy it is and how tight you pack it. Anything between 8 and 12 ounces will be fine.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48978" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6532.jpg" title="IMG_6532"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Stir just until combined.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48980" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6534.jpg" title="IMG_6534"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Pour the batter into the prepared pan.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48984" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_65361.jpg" title="IMG_6536"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Sprinkle the top with
        <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/shop/items/sparkling-white-sugar-1-lb">
         coarse white sparkling sugar,
        </a>
        if desired. Its pretty, tasty, and adds lovely crunch to each slice.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48986" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6540.jpg" title="IMG_6540"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Bake the bread for 55 to 60 minutes.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48988" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6542.jpg" title="IMG_6542"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        When done, a toothpick or cake tester inserted into the center will come out clean, save for perhaps a smear of chocolate.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48990" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6547.jpg" title="IMG_6547"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Remove the bread from the oven, and let it cool for 10 to 15 minutes before turning it out of the pan onto a rack.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48992" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6635.jpg" title="IMG_6635"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Cool completely before slicing; if you slice the bread warm, the chocolate chips smear and look kinda messy.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-48994" src="/blog/files/2011/08/IMG_6634.jpg" title="IMG_6634"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        See the sugar on the crust? Moist, soft bread; crunchy sugar; dark chocolate and 100% whole wheat. YES!
       </p>
       <p>
        One final hint: If possible, wait a day to serve this bread. An overnight rest softens the bran further, ensuring crumble-free slices.
       </p>
       <p>
        Read, bake, and review (please) our recipe for

           <strong>
            Sorry to hear of the difficulty.  When a loaf sticks, it could be for a couple of reasons.  The more common reason is that the loaf was too warm to unmold.  I prefer to always cool the loaf in the pan on a cooling rack for 10-15 minutes before attempting to unmold.  Another cause can be how the pan has been previously used.  If your pan does double duty for meat loaf (the meat proteins change the way the pan will release baked goods),  you can expect baked goods to stick a little more than usual.  You might try lining the length of the pan with a strip of parchment paper next time.  Frank @ KAF.
