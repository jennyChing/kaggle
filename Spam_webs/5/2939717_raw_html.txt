            Bowling For Soup Throws a Birthday Bash at HOB
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="meta">
           <span class="cat_icon">
            <a href="http://www.rattleandhumsports.com/category/the-front-row/" rel="category tag">
             The Front Row
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="comm_icon">
            <a href="http://www.rattleandhumsports.com/bowling-soup-throws-birthday-bash-hob/#commentspost" title="Jump to the comments">

          <a addthis:title="Bowling For Soup Throws a Birthday Bash at HOB " addthis:url="http://www.rattleandhumsports.com/bowling-soup-throws-birthday-bash-hob/" class="addthis_button" href="//addthis.com/bookmark.php?v=300">
           <img alt="Bookmark and Share" height="16" src="//cache.addthis.com/cachefly/static/btn/v2/lg-share-en.gif" style="border:0" width="125"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_26873" style="width: 360px">
          <img alt="Bowling for Soup" class="size-full wp-image-26873" height="339" src="http://www.rattleandhumsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/wpid-bowling-for-soup-anniversary-drum.jpg-e1402339806135.jpeg" width="350"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Bowling For Soup celebrated 20 years together at the House of Blues in Dallas
           <br/>
           Photo by Chuck Cox
           <br/>
           Rattle and Hum Sports
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           By Chuck Cox
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Special Contributor
         </p>
         <p>
          While the cowboy was busy riding away in front of more than 100,000 fans in Arlington on Saturday night, that little old band from Denton, by way of Wichita Falls, was celebrating a pretty big milestone of its own at House of Blues.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://bowlingforsoup.com/main.php" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://bowlingforsoup.com/main.php', 'Bowling For Soup']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Bowling For Soup
          </a>
          had some of its closest friends over for a 20th anniversary show. And even without the slew of No. 1 hits the great
          <a href="http://www.georgestrait.com/index.asp" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.georgestrait.com/index.asp', 'George Strait']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           George Strait
          </a>
          has under his belt, Bowling For Soup has enjoyed longevity several other bands that started in the 1990s have never known.
         </p>
         <p>
          Whats their secret? Fun. Its really as simple as that. Bowling For Soup has always been  and always will be  an absolute blast. They are four normal guys who grew up in the 1980s loving music, who just so happen to make incredibly catchy party tunes and rock anthems. The kind of guys you want to hang out and drink a beer or two with.
         </p>
         <p>
          The band, which is known for its unscripted banter between songs, gave the large crowd exactly what it came for  and then some. The show, which included a seven-minute video of other bands congratulating BFS on 20 years together at the outset, clocked in just tad shy of three hours.
         </p>
         <p>
          Believe me, I appreciated the effort. But with two opening acts, that meant I was standing for almost six hours. And, hey, Im old. Thats a long time to stand up.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_26871" style="width: 310px">
          <img alt="Bowling For Soup" class="size-full wp-image-26871" height="242" src="http://www.rattleandhumsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/wpid-bowling-for-soup-jaret.jpg-e1402340084429.jpeg" width="300"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Bowling For Soup lead singer Jaret Reddick did a great impression of Shaggy during the anniversary set.
           <br/>
           Photo by Chuck Cox
           <br/>
           Rattle and Hum Sports
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          However, the chance to spend the evening helping BFS celebrate two decades of being together was well worth two sore feet. Plus, the band dipped extensively into its sizable catalog to throw in some rarities, like Emily, Thirteen, Cody and 2113. The bands original drummer, Lance Morrill, played on Cody.
         </p>
         <p>
          And the band certainly didnt skimp on the big songs either, faithfully playing Almost, High School Never Ends, (Ohio) Come Back to Texas, My Wena, Punk Rock 101 and The Bitch Song.
         </p>
         <p>
          The band even made note of the Strait show, doing a couple of lines of Amarillo By Morning and All My Exes Live in Texas. That was extremely cool.
         </p>
         <p>
          BFS closed things out with Belgium, a cover of Bryan Adams Summer of 69 and 1985. During the Belgium breakdown, lead singer Jaret Reddick did a dead-on impression of reggae singer Shaggy before touching on hair metal classics Every Rose Has Its Thorn and I Remember You, along with rap classics Jump Around and Its Tricky. Guitarist Chris Burney got in on the act, too, with some rapping skills.
         </p>
         <p>
          It was my 13th Bowling For Soup show, including one of its 15th anniversary shows at Dans Silverleaf in Denton. I seriously dont ever get tired of seeing the band, because it is always such a fun night of music.
         </p>
         <p>
          Regular BFS openers
          <a href="http://www.dollyrots.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.dollyrots.com/', 'The Dollyrots']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           The Dollyrots
          </a>
          started the party off with a tight, rocking 35-minute set that included the bands staples Brand New Key and Because Im Awesome. It was a perfect way to start off the show.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_26872" style="width: 310px">
          <img alt="Bowling for Soup" class="size-full wp-image-26872" height="199" src="http://www.rattleandhumsports.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/wpid-bowling-for-soup-chris.jpg-e1402340576541.jpeg" width="300"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Bowling For Soup lead guitarist Chris Burney performing at the House of Blues
           <br/>
           Photo by Chuck Cox
           <br/>
           Rattle and Hum Sports
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Next up was
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/nerfrock" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.facebook.com/nerfrock', 'Nerf Herder']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Nerf Herder
          </a>
          . This was a band I knew very little about  other than it did the instrumental theme to the
          <em>
           Buffy the Vampire Slayer
          </em>
          television show. Named for a line in
          <em>
           Star Wars Episode IV: The Empire Strikes Back
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          As one might expect, it was pretty geek-tacular. The band was really a lot of fun (theres that word again) in celebrating its nerdom. Lead singer Parry Gripp said the band normally plays in front of 10 people and that the show was like a years worth of concerts.
         </p>
         <p>
          Nerf Herder flawlessly played the Buffy theme, along with the awesome Van Halen, 5,000 Ways to Die and Raining Tacos. For you stars fans, the latter should be very familiar. Its played in the American Airlines Center when fans win free tacos.
         </p>
         <p>
          Another cool touch was Nerf Herder coming back out to play Gripps Here Comes Bowling For Soup to bring the headliners out on stage. That songs recording is typically played in the house before BFS comes on stage.
         </p>
         <p>
          All in all, it was a really fun (albeit long) night. Cheers, BFS. Heres to another 20 years.
         </p>
         <p>

          </span>
         </p>
