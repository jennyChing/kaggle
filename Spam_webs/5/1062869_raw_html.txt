                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6109/6339993221_6565fca975.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Nashville's Parthenon and Centennial Park
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2152353386023254215" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      It has been awhile since Jim and I have had a day out to ourselves, but we had been planning yesterday for several weeks.  Right after we discovered that Kaleighs Crohns/Colitis was back, and more severe than in the past, I found a day of seminars in Nashville.  These were for patients, caregivers, or medical professionals wanting to learn what is new in Crohns/Colitis care. They were being held in a hotel in the Franklin area.  We made arrangements for someone to be here with the kids, and we took off at 7:00 in the morning.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      I have read everything I could find about the disease, and the newest treatments, but I thought it might give us a chance to talk to someone else before we give our treatment decision to Kaleighs doctor on Monday.   Im not sure why I thought we needed to hear more,  really.  I learned right after her diagnosis that it is sometimes best not to know everything, but to just deal with things when/if they happen.  I have heard some terrible stories online, and even talked personally with parents who were struggling through some of the worst effects of this disease with their children.   I guess I really just wanted to see if there were any other treatment options before we start the Remicade, which really scares me.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      Well, we arrived at the hotel about 15 minutes before the seminars were to start, and the parking lot was packed full.  I dont mean that every parking spot was full-I mean every parking spot, and spots that werent even meant for parking.  Maneuvering through the lot was tricky, and we werent the only ones driving around looking for a spot to park.  After about five trips all the way through, and after checking to see if there were other nearby lots where we could park, we gave up and drove away.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      I think we were both relieved, in a way.   Like I said, most of what we would have heard, we already knew anyway.  And I really do trust Kaleighs doctor to do what she knows is best right now.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      So instead of wasting a three hour drive, and a perfectly good chance to have some time together, we decided to find something to do in Nashville.  First we ran over to the Cool Springs Mall to walk around a bit.  This mall is beautiful, and very upscale. It is in the Franklin/Brentwood area where a lot of the country music stars live, and it was shined to perfection.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      Then we drove up to the Vanderbilt area to walk around Centennial Park.   I love this park, and  took the opportunity to get some pictures.  It has a beautiful, full size replica of the Parthenon, that gives Nashville the name Athens of the South.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6339993221/" title="centennialparknashville-13 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-13" height="333" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6109/6339993221_6565fca975.jpg" width="500"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6340736122/" title="centennialparknashville-10 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-10" height="500" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6032/6340736122_e959d1bb45.jpg" width="333"/>
                      </a>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      Nashvilles original Parthenon was built out of wood, brick, and plaster as part of the 1897 fair.  It was never intended to be a permanent display, but in 1920, work began to rebuild it with concrete on the same foundation.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6340722184/" title="centennialparknashville-4 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-4" height="333" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6214/6340722184_2be0b5d991.jpg" width="500"/>
                      </a>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6339976357/" title="centennialparknashville-5 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-5" height="500" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6033/6339976357_9105d01124.jpg" width="333"/>
                      </a>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6339981303/" title="centennialparknashville-7 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-7" height="500" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6214/6339981303_7698fcc446.jpg" width="333"/>
                      </a>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <br/>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      We didnt go inside the museum  this time, but probably will when we have the kids with us.  Inside is an art gallery and a huge statue of Athena which is said to be the largest inside sculpture in the western world.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      This park is beautiful, and the fall colors made it that much more gorgeous.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <br/>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6340766016/" title="centennialparknashville-21 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-21" height="333" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6048/6340766016_dbd910074d.jpg" width="500"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6340762648/" title="centennialparknashville-20 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-20" height="500" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6103/6340762648_a42c6a9a25.jpg" width="333"/>
                      </a>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6340755370/" title="centennialparknashville-18 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-18" height="500" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6091/6340755370_1ca0f9a9e6.jpg" width="333"/>
                      </a>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tlsuttles/6340747656/" title="centennialparknashville-15 by tsuttles, on Flickr">
                       <img alt="centennialparknashville-15" height="500" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6222/6340747656_bdeb67ff50.jpg" width="333"/>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p class="MsoNormal">
                      After Centennial Park,  we drove up to Opry Mills Mall for lunch.  We didnt realize that the mall was still shut down because of the flooding in May, 2010.  I had heard that it was flooded pretty badly, and that it was taking some time to get it back open.  But I had no idea how bad it really was.
