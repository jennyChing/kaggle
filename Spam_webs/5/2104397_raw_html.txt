          Perfectly Peanut Butter Oat Bars (Better than Clif Bars!)
         </h1>
         <a class="comments" href="http://www.godairyfree.org/recipes/peanut-butter-oat-bars#comments">

         <div class="post-content description " itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           <!-- In_Post_300x250 -->
          </p>
          <div class="alignleft" id="div-gpt-ad-1420569555603-0" style="width:300px; height:250px;">
           <script type="text/javascript">
            googletag.cmd.push(function() {
googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1420569555603-0'); });
           </script>
          </div>
          Upon hearing that March 1st was Peanut Butter Lovers Day, this peanut butter lover started eagerly jotting down ideas of what to make. Though many desserts popped into mind, I wanted something everyday and on the wholesome side. These peanut butter oat bars fit the bill and use ingredients that I keep on hand in abundance: lightly salted peanut butter, oats, flax seeds, and good-quality lite coconut milk.
          <p>
           Using my dads favorite cookie flavor as my guide, cinnamon raisin oatmeal, I whipped up a batch of thick, chewy, and positively scrumptious squares that somehow reminded us of a Clif Bar combo (Peanut Butter meets Oatmeal Raisin)  only way, way better.Unlike Clif Bars, these peanut butter oat bars have a simple pure ingredient list, lower sugars, more nutty goodness, and are completely soy-free.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Perfectly Peanut Butter Oat Bars Recipe - like Clif Bars only WAY better! Naturally vegan, gluten-free, nutritious treat, and spiked with cinnamon and raisins." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-206509" height="1067" src="http://www.godairyfree.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Perfectly-Peanut-Butter-Oatmeal-Raisin-Bars-Recipe-naturally-vegan-gluten-free-and-nutritious.jpg" width="800"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I decided to experiment with a couple of drizzle options when making these peanut butter oat bars, one low sugar maple-cinnamon peanut butter icing (pictured above) and one traditional peanut butter icing (thickly piped below). Though they are soft and pipe-able as shown in my images, both set up when chilled, offering a little extra Clif Bar-like appeal.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Perfectly Peanut Butter Oat Bars Recipe - like Clif Bars only WAY better! Naturally vegan, gluten-free, nutritious treat, and spiked with cinnamon and raisins." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-206508" height="1067" src="http://www.godairyfree.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Perfectly-Peanut-Butter-Oatmeal-Raisin-Bars-Recipe-naturally-vegan-gluten-free-and-nutritious-treat.jpg" width="800"/>
           <em>
            This post is sponsored by
            <a href="http://www.sodeliciousdairyfree.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.sodeliciousdairyfree.com', 'So Delicious Dairy Free');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             So Delicious Dairy Free
            </a>
            and highlights the versatility of their
            <a href="http://bit.ly/1FAxAjL" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://bit.ly/1FAxAjL', 'Lite Culinary Coconut Milk');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Lite Culinary Coconut Milk
            </a>
            , which is equivalent to light canned coconut milk, but higher quality in both ingredients and packaging (BPA-free carton with convenient resealable spout!
           </em>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Special Diet &amp; Ingredient Notes:Peanut Butter Oat Bars
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="So Delicious Lite Culinary Coconut Milk - a better, creamy option for dairy-free and to canned coconut milk" class="alignright size-full wp-image-206517" height="415" src="http://www.godairyfree.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/So-Delicious-Lite-Culinary-Coconut-Milk-a-better-creamy-option-for-dairy-free-and-to-canned-coconut-milk.jpg" width="300"/>
           By ingredients, this recipe is dairy-free / non-dairy, egg-free, gluten-free, tree nut-free, soy-free, vegan / plant-based, and vegetarian.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            For peanut-free
           </strong>
           peanut butter oat bars, use almond butter (if tree nuts are okay for you) in place of the peanut butter. For nut-free, use your favorite seed butter. Keep in mind that sunflower seed butter will yield the most peanutty results, but it may react with the baking soda to produce a greenish hue. This is totally safe, just not the most appealing!
          </p>
          <p>
           Since some ingredients can effect the results of these peanut butter oat bars more than others, I thought I would address them:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Peanut Butter:
           </strong>
           I did use an all-natural peanut butter when creating this recipe 
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00FGEPIMK/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00FGEPIMK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=godairyfree-20&amp;linkId=Q662OAB7M34DFWES" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00FGEPIMK/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00FGEPIMK&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=godairyfree-20&amp;linkId=Q662OAB7M34DFWES', 'Trader Joes Creamy Salted Peanut Butter');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Trader Joes Creamy Salted Peanut Butter
           </a>
           . It is a very soft peanut butter, almost a little runny at room temperature, and quite spreadable when refrigerated.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Dairy-Free Milk:
           </strong>
           Using just milk alternative or juicecan make bars more muffin-like. As mentioned, I use
           <a href="http://bit.ly/1FAxAjL" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://bit.ly/1FAxAjL', 'lite coconut milk');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            lite coconut milk
           </a>
           , which adds just enough liquid and richness to keep the recipe very bar-like, dense and rich. Yet it is a pure ingredient that adds less than .5 gram of fat per bar! In a pinch, you could substitute
           <a href="http://bit.ly/1AHyF96" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://bit.ly/1AHyF96', 'coconut milk creamer');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            coconut milk creamer
           </a>
           or
           <a href="http://bit.ly/1zOls9f" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://bit.ly/1zOls9f', 'dairy-free milk beverage');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            dairy-free milk beverage
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Sweetener:
           </strong>
           I test baked goods with both
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search/ref=as_li_qf_sp_sr_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=aps&amp;keywords=organic%20brown%20sugar&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=godairyfree-20&amp;linkId=XLIKNCWPEPQMDPT7" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/search/ref=as_li_qf_sp_sr_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=aps&amp;keywords=organic%20brown%20sugar&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=godairyfree-20&amp;linkId=XLIKNCWPEPQMDPT7', 'organic brown sugar');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            organic brown sugar
           </a>
           and
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search/ref=as_li_qf_sp_sr_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=aps&amp;keywords=coconut%20sugar&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=godairyfree-20&amp;linkId=ZN23VLXUDF5GFJ57" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/search/ref=as_li_qf_sp_sr_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=aps&amp;keywords=coconut%20sugar&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=godairyfree-20&amp;linkId=ZN23VLXUDF5GFJ57', 'coconut sugar');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            coconut sugar
           </a>
           , the latter produces a mellower, slightly less sweet flavor. If using coconut sugar, I recommend blending it with the oats and flax seeds into a powder and adding with the dry ingredients. Brown sugar gives a more classic taste. If you want to use brown sugar, but want it a little less sweet, just lightly pack the sugar.
           <br/>
          </p>

             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 cup regular rolled oats (
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search/ref=as_li_qf_sp_sr_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=aps&amp;keywords=gluten-free%20oats&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=godairyfree-20&amp;linkId=3GSUQNBX2MFWPUUG" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/search/ref=as_li_qf_sp_sr_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=aps&amp;keywords=gluten-free%20oats&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=godairyfree-20&amp;linkId=3GSUQNBX2MFWPUUG', 'certified gluten-free');" target="_blank">
               certified gluten-free
              </a>
              , if needed), divided
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 tablespoons flax seeds
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 teaspoons ground cinnamon
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 teaspoon baking soda (high altitude: use  teaspoon over 3000 feet)
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               teaspoon salt
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 cup lightly salted peanut butter
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               cup packed brown sugar or coconut sugar
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               cup lite coconut milk (I use
              <a href="http://bit.ly/1FAxAjL" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://bit.ly/1FAxAjL', 'So Delicious Dairy Free Lite Culinary Coconut Milk');" target="_blank">
               So Delicious Dairy Free Lite Culinary Coconut Milk
              </a>
              )
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 tablespoon maple syrup
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               tablespoon vanilla extract
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               cup raisins
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              Peanut butter icing drizzle (optional, see notes below)
             </li>
            </ul>
            <div class="ERSClear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSInstructions">
            <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
             Instructions
            </div>
            <ol>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Preheat your oven to 350F and line an 8x8-inch baking dish (see note in post above) with parchment paper or grease and flour it.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Place  cup of the oats and the flax seeds in a spice grinder or food processor and whiz into flour, about 60 seconds.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Add the ground oat mixture to a medium bowl with the remaining whole oats, cinnamon, baking soda, and salt.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              In a mixing bowl, blend the peanut butter, sugar, coconut milk, maple syrup and vanilla, with a hand mixer or by hand, until combined and smooth. Stir in the oat mixture, it should be the consistency of a thick but slightly sticky cookie dough. Fold in the raisins.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Scrape the dough into your prepared baking dish, and with lightly damp hands, firmly and evenly press the mixture in.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Bake for 20 minutes, or until lightly brown around the edges and beginning to pull away from the sides ever so slightly. Let cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before cutting (can even chill first).
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              If using parchment paper, pull the bars out onto a cutting board to cut (otherwise cut in the pan). Cut into 16 bars (cut in half, cut each half in half, turn the pan 90 and repeat). They are a little crumbly to cut but actually hold together quite well, particularly if stored in the refrigerator.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              If opting for an icing drizzle, add one of the options below to a plastic bag and seal. If too runny, chill the icing in the baggie for 10 minutes. Snip a small corner from the bottom of the bag, and pipe the icing onto the bars as desired.
             </li>
            </ol>
            <div class="ERSClear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
            <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
             Notes
            </div>
            <div class="ERSNotes">
             <strong>
              Low Sugar Maple Cinnamon Icing:
             </strong>
             Whisk together 2 tablespoons lightly salted peanut butter, 1 tablespoon coconut oil, 2 teaspoon maple syrup,  teaspoon ground cinnamon, and optionally add up to 10 drops alcohol-free stevia, to taste.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Simple Peanut Butter Icing:
             </strong>
             Whisk together 2 tablespoons lightly salted peanut butter, 2 tablespoons powdered sugar, and 2 teaspoons coconut oil.
            </div>
