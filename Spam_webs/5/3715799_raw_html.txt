             <p>
              Hi,
             </p>
             <p>
              Your case sounds complicated, and you should have an attorney.  If you have not yet bet arraigned and you qualify you can be appointed counsel.  If you do not qualify you should hire an attorney.  Drug and gun cases are complicated and often have valid motions to suppress evidence and or statements.   For example, in your case, they would have to prove that you were actually living at the residence to somehow link you to the gun and ammunition.  They will also need to prove that they did a valid search of the area where they found the gun and the ammunition.  The same applies for the marijuana and the stopping of the car.
             </p>
             <p>
              As for your sentence - it's tough to tell from what you said, but it is possible that you will face a minimum mandatory sentence on some of the charges...depending on the amount of drugs seized.
