     <h2>
      Leading: Making your copy a comfortable read
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i style="color:#666;">
       March 4, 2015
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <div class="a2a_kit a2a_kit_size_32 addtoany_list a2a_target" id="wpa2a_1">
      <a class="a2a_button_facebook" href="http://www.addtoany.com/add_to/facebook?linkurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.extravision.com%2Fblog%2Fleading-making-your-copy-a-comfortable-read%2F&amp;linkname=Leading%3A%20Making%20your%20copy%20a%20comfortable%20read" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Facebook">
      </a>
      <a class="a2a_button_twitter" href="http://www.addtoany.com/add_to/twitter?linkurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.extravision.com%2Fblog%2Fleading-making-your-copy-a-comfortable-read%2F&amp;linkname=Leading%3A%20Making%20your%20copy%20a%20comfortable%20read" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Twitter">
      </a>
      <a class="a2a_button_google_plus" href="http://www.addtoany.com/add_to/google_plus?linkurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.extravision.com%2Fblog%2Fleading-making-your-copy-a-comfortable-read%2F&amp;linkname=Leading%3A%20Making%20your%20copy%20a%20comfortable%20read" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Google+">
      </a>
      <a class="a2a_dd addtoany_share_save" href="https://www.addtoany.com/share_save">
      </a>
      <script type="text/javascript">

     <p>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       So youve proof read, edited and re-edited your copy, but rather than rush it online and consider it job done, your next step should be to make it as comfortable reading as possible so people stick with and concentrate on your message.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       Equally as important as to how many
       <a href="http://www.extravision.com/blog/how-many-characters-wide-should-your-content-be/" title="How many characters wide should your content be?">
        <span class="s2">
         characters wide your columns are
        </span>
       </a>
       , is the consideration given to the vertical space between lines, this is known in type setting as the leading.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       The term leading comes from the days of setting type by hand, lead strips were placed between blocks of type to increase the vertical height and as a result, legibility.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       Correct leading isnt just about being aesthetically pleasing, anything that distracts the reader from a natural rhythm of reading makes it harder for them to relax and to concentrate, and its more likely that people will tire from and abandon your fantastic copy.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       A smooth read helps the meaning of your message sink in.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       <b>
        Getting the leading right
       </b>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       As a simple rule, if lines are too closely set, a readers eye will take in the neighbouring above and below lines at the same time, impairing reading speed.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       If the leading is too high and the lines have too much vertical space, its more difficult for a readers eye to link up to the next line, lines appear isolated and your article will be tiring to read.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <span class="s1">
       See the following examples of too closely set, about right and too generous leading 
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
      <a href="http://s3.extravision.com/ev/images/leading-large.gif" target="_blank" title="Enlarge example">
       <img alt="Leading example" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2369" height="780" src="http://www.extravision.com/wp-content/uploads/leading.gif" width="640"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p class="p1">
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       <b>
        Considerations for implementing online
       </b>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       <b>
        CSS
       </b>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       The property to use is
       <a href="http://www.w3schools.com/cssref/pr_dim_line-height.asp">
        <span class="s2">
         line-height
        </span>
       </a>
       which can be applied to the body, the p tag and any other standard HTML element.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       <b>
        Inline CSS/email support
       </b>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       All modern mobile email clients and most desktop clients have good support for the line-height property; however some issues may arise when trying to use this property to compress the leading in Outlook 2007. If you require Outlook 2007 support, use px values that are greater than the font-size used in your copy.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       <b>
        Responsive design
       </b>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       One fixed line-height/font-size combination is unlikely to be the optimal fit for every screen size/device your content will be viewed on, so be sure to test and use media queries to optimise your contents legibility across different screen sizes.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       References
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="p2">
      <span class="s1">
       Mller-Brockmann, J. (1985). Grid Systems in graphic design. Stuttgart: Hatje [u.a.].
      </span>
     </p>
