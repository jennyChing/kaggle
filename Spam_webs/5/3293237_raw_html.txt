             <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
              9 Ways DC Could Respond To Spider-Man Joining The MCU
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-info">
              <ul class="td-category">
               <li class="entry-category">
                <a href="http://chillpaper.com/entertainmentt/">

             <a data-caption="" href="http://chillpaper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/1602575-mailstorm_1024x780.jpg">
              <img alt="" class="entry-thumb td-modal-image" height="533" itemprop="image" src="http://chillpaper.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/1602575-mailstorm_1024x780-700x533.jpg" title="1602575-mailstorm_1024x780" width="700"/>

             </div>
             <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="9 Ways DC Could Respond To Spider-Man Joining The MCU" data-frizzlyposturl="http://chillpaper.com/9-ways-dc-respond-spider-man-joining-mcu/" type="hidden" value=""/>
             <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="9 Ways DC Could Respond To Spider-Man Joining The MCU" data-url="http://chillpaper.com/9-ways-dc-respond-spider-man-joining-mcu/">
             </div>
             <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="9 Ways DC Could Respond To Spider-Man Joining The MCU" data-url="http://chillpaper.com/9-ways-dc-respond-spider-man-joining-mcu/">
             </div>
             <p>
              <img alt="DC" class="size-full wp-image-845912" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/dc-comics.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Ever since the Marvel Cinematic Universe turned into a global phenomenon and Warner Bros. set about rebooting its most significant properties (namely Superman and Batman) with the intention of creating DCs very own movie-verse, the two studios have been locked in a game of one-upmanship. This week, Marvel just took the fight to DC.
             </p>
             <p>
              The revelation that a new Spider-Man story will be told for the third time in just 15 years is not the headline today  the main scoop is that Marvel has scored a huge victory by making a deal with Sony to have Spider-Man become part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <div class="td-a-rec td-a-rec-id-content_inline ">
              <script src="http://www.adcash.com/ad/display.php?r=421985" type="text/javascript">
              </script>
             </div>
             Anew Spider-Man (Andrew Garfield will most likely not reprise his role, perhaps the biggest downside to this new Marvel/Sony deal) will appear in Captain America: Civil War next year, with a standalone Spidey movie coming in 2017 that Marvel will co-produce with Sony.
             <p>
              For Marvel Studios, this is a huge coup  the Spider-Man films have proven enormously successful so far, hence why Sony wants to retain ultimate control over the character rights but for Warner Bros. and DC, its a big blow. It means Warner now need a big revelation of their own to compete with the Marvel news, or at least something to bring attention back to their DC movie-verse.
             </p>
             <h2>
              9. Bring Those Release Dates Forward
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Warner Brothers" class="size-full wp-image-829825" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/batman-superman-dawn-of-justice1.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Filming on Batman VSuperman: Dawn Of Justice concluded in 2014, yet youwont see the film until spring 2016. With Marvel now releasing a couple of movies a year at the same time as it expands into television, theres no chance anyones going to forget about that studio; meanwhile, Warner Bros. hasnt had a release out ofits own DC movie universe since 2013s Man Of Steel.
             </p>
             <p>
              Man Of Steel is, FYI, the onlyDC Cinematic Universemovie from Warner Bros, compared to the ten Marvel has already released (itll be 12 by the time Batman VSuperman, only the second DC movie-verse film, comes out). Marvels established dominance over the major forms of media at the moment makes Warner Bros. and DC look like pretendersso why not take the Spider-Man news as an incentive to bring that Batman VSuperman release forward to 2015 as a statement, and get a shift on with production of those other films?
             </p>
             <p>
              DC cant possibly lag behind any longer, not if it expects to stay in this fight with Marvel.
             </p>
             <h2>
              8. Release A Trailer For Batman V Superman
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Warner Bros." class="size-full wp-image-727572" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/batman-vs-superman-batsuit.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Seeing as Batman VSuperman wrapped last year, youd think Warner Bros would be doing more to get some momentum going on a film that many are sceptical about at best. Marvel managed to give fans at Comic-Con a sneak peak at James Gunns Guardians Of The Galaxy after itd just started shooting  why is it that all fans have got from Warner Bros. on Batman VSuperman so far is a couple of gloomy, unsatisfying images?
             </p>
             <p>
              This is the ultimate superhero team-up  before Marvel came along, making whacky comic book character mainstream, Batman and Superman ruled as two defining icons of geekdom and American culture on the whole for decades. A trailer for the already-shot film would make the news of Spider-Man coming to Marvel seem minor by comparison.
             </p>
             <h2>
              7. Bring MorePopular Characters Into The Fold
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="The CW" class="size-full wp-image-836625" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Arrow-Oliver-Queen.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              The attention is on Marvel right now because one of its most famous characters has come home could DC be bold enough to throw another major character, or characters, into the mix of its own planned movie universe? Its not totally unlikely, though it could seem like a knee-jerk reaction, considering the DC movie-verse line-up until 2020 is already pretty packed.
             </p>
             <p>
              Highly ambitiously so, in fact  at the moment the plan is to give justover a dozen rebooted DC characters by just 2017; whether Warner Bros can afford to take such a creative and financial gamble within the space of two years is debatable. Yet rumours are constantly flying about other characters that might be set to join, and who knows, maybe the announcement that TVs Green Arrow is coming into the Justice League fold or that Guillermo del Toro is coming to Warner Bros to make that Justice League Dark film could be the story to bring the attention back to DC.
             </p>
             <h2>
              6. Officially Announce That Rumoured Standalone Batman Film
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Warner Bros." class="size-full wp-image-724905" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Batman-superman-batmobile.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              The news that Ben Affleck would take over the mantle of Bruce Wayne/Batman from Christian Bale was met with such ire, its a wonder DC and Warner Bros. didnt relent to the pressure and just let him go (thats a joke of course  probably noactor would have inspired much confidence in fans after Bales interpretation). If DC/Warner Bros. really want to make this Spider-Man news go away, all they have to do is make it official that there will be a standalone Batfleck film coming.
             </p>
             <p>
              Its been rumoured that Afflecks Batman will be getting his own movie (following Batman VSuperman) for a while now, and the news that this newBatman is getting his own movie would probably be enough to make the internet explode. Youknow its probably on the cards anyway, all Warner Bros. have to do is get it out to the press.
             </p>
             <h2>
              5. Focus Public Attention On Those Upcoming Unique And ExcitingProjects
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="DC Comics" class="size-full wp-image-636001" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Suicide-Squad.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Right now is a time for action for Warner Bros.:they want to compete against Marvels already well-established Cinematic Universe, and they want to do it fast, but with another Spider-Man movie on its way in 2017, before any standalone Batman or Superman films will make it to cinemas, it might not be fast enough. How much audience goodwill (read: pocket money) is there going to be left for another studio doing virtually the same thing?
             </p>
             <p>
              With Spider-Man set to be a major box office property again in just two years, Warner Bros. and DC may nothave time to waste on stories that audiences have already seen. Warners/DC could recognise the potential fatigue, forget about more standalone Superman and Batman films and focus on getting the publics attention for theirother, more unique projects instead.
             </p>
             <p>
              Suicide Squad, for one, seems like a step in the right direction  its got agreatcast, soundsdeliciously dark, and is like nothing Marvel currently has planned. DC couldstart highlighting just what its doing differently to Marvel, as opposed to what its doing the same.
             </p>
             <h2>
              4. Start The Crisis Storyline
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="DC Comics" class="size-full wp-image-734466" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/infinite-crisis-superboy-prime-punches-reality.png" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Or, if DC and Warner Bros. are feeling particularly brave, they could make an attempt at the Crisis storyline, introducing the concept of multiple universes and therefore the idea that these new iterations of DC characters are part of the same multiverse as ones youve already seen on the big screen in the past. Itd be taking the more grounded feel away from the new DC universe and putting the upcoming slate of films properly in the realm of science fiction.
             </p>
             <p>
              Itd be a big step thatd potentially alienate fans of the Nolan DC movies, but Marvel only got to where it is today by taking risks. The Spider-Man news today suggests Marvel is happy to ground itself on contemporary Earth for the foreseeable future, rather than taking it into other dimensions (at least further than Guardians Of The Galaxy and the Thor movies). The DC movies getting meta-tastic with the Crisis storyline would make the MCU look conventional.
             </p>
             <h2>
              3. Plan To AimMore DC Movies At Teens
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Sony Pictures" class="size-full wp-image-864940" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/The-Amazing-Spider-Man-Andrew-Garfield.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Alternatively, the Spider-Man news could encourage DC not to do something creatively risky, but to follow Marvels lead and tap into the market Marvels going to take with its addition of the web-slinger; the teen market.
             </p>
             <p>
              Its looking pretty dark over in the DC movie-verse right now. The fact they got David Ayer, a writer-director behind gritty, very much adult movies like Training Day, Harsh Times and Fury, to make Suicide Squad is an indication of where that movie will be heading, while the tone of Man Of Steel didnt exactly suggest this universe was going to be all that family-friendly.
             </p>
             <p>
              At the moment, Warner Bros looks to be creating a comic book world for adults. Warner Bros could start planning to get a bit brighter and aim its DC films at the market Marvel wants from Spider-Man, if it hasnt already attempted to alter the darkerfeel of the Superman and Batman stories for Batman V Superman.
             </p>
             <h2>
              2.Casting Announcements
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="DC" class="size-full wp-image-832870" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Green-Lantern-2020-casting.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Nothing gets fans going quite like casting news  see what happened when the announcements were made for who was starring in Suicide Squad? Fansstill dont know wholl play Green Lantern, for example, or what kind of villains will be played by what kind of actors in Aquaman, The Flash or Shazam.
             </p>
             <p>
              If the rumours are true that itll be Peter Parker, and not the mixed race Miles Morales, wholl be taking on Spider-Man duties for Marvel, its possible the creatives and execs behind the movie have missed a trick. Before Marvel announced its Phase Three plans, DC had already told the world how it was giving a woman (Gal Gadot, as Wonder Woman), a man of mixed Native American heritage (Jason Momoa, as Aquaman) and a self-identified queer actor (Ezra Miller, as The Flash) their own standalone superhero movies.
             </p>
             <p>
              It was a bold move, considering most superheroes up to this point had been almost exclusively portrayed by hetero white men. If DC wants to capitalise on the fan disappointment that it will once again be Parker and not Morales wearing the Spider-suit, it and Warner Bros. could always stand to diversify further and grab headlines with its casting and superhero choices.
             </p>
             <h2>
              1. Just Pack It In
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Warner Bros" class="size-full wp-image-871214" height="300" src="http://cdn3.whatculture.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/3114445-supernoooo_640-e1419106181685.jpg" width="600"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Man Of Steel cost Warner Bros. $225 million and made $668 million at the box office. Factor in the mighty cost of the marketing done on this film the general rule when taking marketing costs on Hollywood films into account is to take the production budget and double it and youre looking at something that performed only marginally well in cinemas.
             </p>
             <p>
              For an indication of what the success of Man Of Steel looks like, consider that How to Train Your Dragon 2s $618 million haul last year was considered a disappointment. Warner Bros. is already faced with bad buzz and underwhelming box office figures, while Marvel has gone from strength to strength, up to and including the news that Spider-Man, a hot property with a huge audience already attached, is now on its books.
             </p>
             <p>
              The DC movie-verse looks like an expensive, bank-breaking folly waiting to happen likely the smart move to make from a business sense would be for WB and DC to let the idea of a new comic book universe go.
             </p>
             <p>
              Realistically, of course, this isnt a move theyre likely to make; expect the team to stubbornly go as far with this idea as they can (before a lack of audience interest puts a stop to the whole endeavour anyway).
             </p>
