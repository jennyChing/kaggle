         <div class="articleFull">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            Questions on Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning could    be the game changer in the first day first session of CAT 2014. While Quant    and especially Arithmatic was found moderate by the test takers, the DI    and LR parts in respective section were found much difficult. The test takers    after completing their exam came out cursing these sections and said Quant    was easier, Verbal a bit difficult but DI and LR were the most difficult and    time consuming parts.
           </font>
          </p>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
           </font>
          </div>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            The first session on first day of CAT 2014 with number of changes    and modifications began at 9.30 AM on Nov 16, 2014 across the country. Candidates    had to report for the exam by 7.30 AM, 2 hours before the exam.
           </font>
          </p>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
           </font>
          </div>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            As expected, although there were number of questions both in    Quant and Verbal sections with changed pattern, the difficulty level ranged    from moderate to difficult. Candidates found the test doable overall.    The modified question types could be rated as surprise elements. Candidates    who were fear struck and were expecting unprecedented changes in the difficulty    level in the exam were found the DI as the most difficult part in CAT 2014 first    day first session.
           </font>
          </p>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
           </font>
          </div>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            Quant and DI section, the first of the two sections in CAT    2014 contained 50 questions of moderate to difficult level. Quant was moderate    but DI was very difficult and time consuming. Out of the 50 questions    in this section share of questions on Quant was 34 and of DI was 16 questions.    Candidates who could crack 28 question in this section are expected to score    a high percentile. Number system, PnC, Arithmetic were the major source topics    in this section.
           </font>
          </p>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
           </font>
          </div>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            The Second Section comprising questions on Verbal    Ability and Logical Reasoning in the first session of first day of CAT 2014    had 34 questions on Verbal Ability including three questions on critical reasoning     Facts Inference, Judgement (FIJ). There were 16 questions on Logical    reasoning in the exam.The section was not very difficult and therefore might    have a higher cut-off percentile. Options in questions on RC passages    were very close ones.
           </font>
          </p>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
           </font>
          </div>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            Questions on LR were very difficult and consumed most of the    time of the candidates. Most of the candidates were found complaining    that Logical Reasoning questions took them for a ride and all their preparation    was of not much help
           </font>
          </p>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
           </font>
          </div>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">

           </font>

          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            CAT aspirants, going to take the test on Nov 22, 2014 should    go through more questions on FIJ, RC passages, practice DI questions more to    ace the test.
           </font>
