         <p>
          <strong>
           Thinking Of Going Self-Employed? Then Read On
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you have had enough of working for someone else and are seriously considering becoming self-employed, these interesting statistics may help you to decide whether or not to set up business on your own:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           3 million people in the United Kingdom currently work for themselves.
          </li>
          <li>
           The average age of people that are self-employed is 47 years old.
          </li>
          <li>
           Self-employed people are far more likely to be male than female, in fact, around 70% of self-employed people are male.
          </li>
          <li>
           Around 18% of self-employed people live in London
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          The top 3 trades for self-employment in the United Kingdom are:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Carpenters
          </li>
          <li>
           Electricians
          </li>
          <li>
           Builders
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          But what are the top 3 reasons for people choosing to become self-employed in the first place?
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Work when you want  it is true that when you are self-employed, you can choose which hours that you work. However, this does not mean that self-employed people work fewer hours. Whilst the average number of hours worked per week is 38, self-employed people are likely to work longer hours.
          </li>
          <li>
           You can earn more money  on average, freelancers earn up to 45% more than people in traditional employment. Self-employed people can also deduct business expenses that employees cannot, meaning that self-employed people can actually keep more of what they earn.
          </li>
          <li>
           You are your own boss  by being self-employed, you are no longer told what to do by a higher authority, so you can control how you do your work. Obviously, customers or clients have some say, but how you go about your work is completely up to you.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          What do you need to do to be self-employed?
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Register as self-employed  The first think that you must do is let the HMRC know that you are self-employed as soon as possible. This can be done online by registering for business taxes. Self-employed people must fill out self-assessment tax returns every year.
          </li>
          <li>
           Get a UTR number  this is your unique taxpayer reference number. Once you have registered as self-employed, this will be sent to you in a letter.
          </li>
          <li>
           Find yourself an accountant. This is where we come in
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          At U-Tax Accountancy Services, we provide you with a friendly service. Rather than confusing you with complicated jargon, we like to be straight to the point to help you make the right decisions. Our staff are available to you all year to provide personal help and assistance when you need it. We are highly experienced in working with self-employed people. 3 years ago, we completed 849 self-assessment tax returns, whilst last year completed 4589 and have therefore gained considerable experience in this field.
         </p>
         <p>
          As part of our accountancy packages, we are happy to help you with annual accounts, self-assessment tax returns, corporation tax, and correspondence with HMRC as well as giving general tax advice.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more information on self-employment and filling out self-assessment tax returns, you can visit the U-Tax Accountancy Services website.
         </p>
