                <h1 class="entry-title cb-entry-title cb-single-title" itemprop="headline">
                 Lucerne in the Mist
                </h1>
               </span>
              </div>
             </header>
             <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
              <p>
               <a href="http://media.wild-about-travel.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Lucerne-in-the-mist1.jpg" target="_blank">
                <img alt="Lucerne-in-the-mist" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-7240" height="632" src="http://media.wild-about-travel.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Lucerne-in-the-mist1.jpg" title="Lucerne-in-the-mist" width="640"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Lucerne
               </strong>
               in the mist A muffled atmosphere.
               <br/>
               It reminded me of the the
               <strong>
                Charles Bridge
               </strong>
               in
               <span style="color: #ff6600;">
                <strong>
                 <a href="http://www.wild-about-travel.com/2010/10/remembering-prague-1980-part1/" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #ff6600;">
                   Prague
                  </span>
                 </a>
                </strong>
               </span>
               , on a foggy November in 1980. It made me think at
               <strong>
                George
               </strong>
               <strong>
                Simenon
               </strong>
               s novels, often set in a small town of Northern Belgium, where the haze is haunting and hides mysteries and meanness.
              </p>
              <p>
              </p>
