      <h1>
       How I Cut 87% Off My Heating Bills Last February
      </h1>
      <div class="post-22770 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-blogs category-lifestyles category-paul-wheaton category-sustainable-living" id="post-22770">
       <div id="content_ad">
        <!-- Medium-Rectangle -->
        <div id="div-gpt-ad-1332429463846-10" style="width:300px; height:250px;">
         <script type="text/javascript">
          googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1332429463846-10'); });
         </script>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         By PAUL WHEATON
        </p>
        <p>
         For years I have talked to people about how to
         <a href="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/10.0" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/10.0', 'save energy']);" target="_blank">
          save energy
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         And whenI try to describe a few things about heat yourself instead of thewhole house, it seems that there has been a huge gap between the stuffin my head and what others are willing to believe.So last year I experimented. By February, I cut my electric bill by87%. Imconfident that this year I might be able to push it past 90%.
        </p>
        <p>
         First, I have to express that Im not one of those polar people thatgo swimming in icy waters. I have learned that even when bundled up,I get uncomfortable in rooms with a temperature below 65 degrees F. Iguess Im a wimp. So this isnt about being tough. This is about
         <a href="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/8480_0/meaningless-drivel/science-denial" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/8480_0/meaningless-drivel/science-denial', 'science']);" target="_blank" title="Read the Permie blog about science denial">
          science
         </a>
         and understanding the
         <a href="http://www.permies.com/Permaculture/Notes-On-Heating" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.permies.com/Permaculture/Notes-On-Heating', 'differentkinds of heat']);" target="_blank" title="Read the Permie notes on heating">
          differentkinds of heat
         </a>
         .The key is to heat myself instead of the whole house. Here is mylame drawing of me, at my desk, and I am heating the whole house:
         <br/>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="113" src="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-images/4906_1585/whole-house.gif" title="Heating the whole house." width="284"/>
         <br/>
         Andhere is the same image, but Ive turned the house thermostat downto 50 and I have a personal heater at my feet:
         <br/>
         <img alt="" height="113" src="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-images/4906_1586/space-heater.gif" title="Using a space heater at my feet." width="284"/>
         <br/>
         Myshins cooked and my fingers were numb with cold.A bit of optimization led to a better solution:
         <br/>
         <img alt="" height="113" src="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-images/4906_1587/attempt-1.gif" title="Optimized heating." width="284"/>
         <br/>
         Thisturned out to be an excellent solution. I was plenty warm in aroom that was 50 degrees. My collection of personal heaters consumedabout 235 watts  far less than what the personal heater at my feetused. I started playing with ways to lower that and got that down to82.5 watts:
         <br/>
         <img alt="" height="113" src="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-images/4906_1588/attempt-2.gif" title="Heating with all the right supplies." width="284"/>
         <br/>
         Andnow for the stars of this show. 82.5 watts comes from these fourthings:
        </p>
        <p>
         A
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000PWJP7C/rs12-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000PWJP7C/rs12-20', 'dog bed warmer']);" target="_blank">
          dog bed warmer
         </a>
         (15 watts):
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000PWJP7C/rs12-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000PWJP7C/rs12-20', '']);" target="_blank">
          <img alt="personal heater dog bed warmer" height="180" src="http://ecx.images-amazon.com/images/I/41EYYs4XeML._SL500_AA300_.jpg" width="180"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         A
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000GBLXL8/rs12-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000GBLXL8/rs12-20', 'heated keyboard']);" target="_blank">
          heated keyboard
         </a>
         (25 watts):
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000GBLXL8/rs12-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000GBLXL8/rs12-20', '']);" target="_blank">
          <img alt="personal heater heated keyboard" height="180" src="http://ecx.images-amazon.com/images/I/313CHg0dXJL._SL500_AA300_.jpg" width="180"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         A
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B002DHPZGM/rs12-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B002DHPZGM/rs12-20', 'heated mouse']);" target="_blank">
          heated mouse
         </a>
         (2.5 watts):
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B002DHPZGM/rs12-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B002DHPZGM/rs12-20', '']);" target="_blank">
          <img alt="personal heater heated mouse" height="180" src="http://ecx.images-amazon.com/images/I/51T9ZPwBRhL._SL500_AA300_.jpg" width="180"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         A standard 40 watt incandescent light bulb:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" height="214" src="http://www.richsoil.com/images/incandescent-bulb-214.png" title="Personal heater: 40 watt light bulb." width="128"/>
         <br/>
         The
         <a href="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/2833_0/alternative-energy/heat-byproduct-of-incandescent-bulbs" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/2833_0/alternative-energy/heat-byproduct-of-incandescent-bulbs', 'heatfromthe incandescent light bulb']);" target="_blank" title="Read about incandescent blubs on the permaculture forum">
          heatfromthe incandescent light bulb
         </a>
         was key. I have to admit that Iprobably looked a little goofy, but the important thingis that it worked, and it worked well. There is now plenty of room tomake it all pretty for folks that want pretty, and I have loads ofideas for that space.
        </p>
        <p>
         The light bulb hung in a chick brooder lamp thingy about three inchesover my head. It warmed my bald spot and my nose. And was positionedin such a way that the light was not in my eyes.The other three contraptions gave off heat that would rise. Just atiny bit of heat right where I needed it. I can even say that I letthe room temperature drop below 50 and I was still warm as toast.
        </p>
        <p>
         For a lot of people, this can turn into $1000 of savings over thewinter. And $1000 is worth looking goofy while sitting under a chickbrooder. And that includes turning the thermostat up a few times overthe winter because you have guests. For other people, it is muchmore than the thousand dollars. Thats a thousand dollars worth ofdirty electricity from coal plants or hydro systems that are killingfish and filling with
         <a href="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/2867_0/green-building/making-durable-blocks-from-silt" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/2867_0/green-building/making-durable-blocks-from-silt', 'silt']);" title="Learn how to make blocks from silt on the Permies forum">
          silt
         </a>
         behind the dams.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have a lot more to say on lots and lots of details about this. Youcan read my discussion about this as I tried things last winter in athread called
         <a href="http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/4906_0/alternative-energy/making-the-best-of-electric-heat" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.permies.com/permaculture-forums/4906_0/alternative-energy/making-the-best-of-electric-heat', 'makingthebest of electric heat']);" target="_blank" title="Read the thread on the permaculture forum">
          makingthebest of electric heat
         </a>
         , or you can read the summary I wrotelast winter called
         <a href="http://www.richsoil.com/electric-heat.jsp" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.richsoil.com/electric-heat.jsp', 'Icut 87% off of myelectric heat bill']);" target="_blank" title="Read the summary on richsoil.com">
          Icut 87% off of myelectric heat bill
         </a>
         .
        </p>
