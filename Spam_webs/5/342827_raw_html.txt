       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Google Earth Sleuthing  Burning Ship?
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2005-10-27T09:45:18+00:00">
         October 27, 2005
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.gearthblog.com/images/burningship.jpg">
          <img align="right" height="200" hspace="8" src="http://www.gearthblog.com/images/burningshipi.jpg" title="Select for bigger" vspace="8" width="219"/>
         </a>
         Its amazing what the combination of Google Earths huge resource of satellite and aerial photos and the Internet can do.  A few months ago someone noticed a very small satellite photo of a remote area of ocean near Iceland where a boat or ship appeared to be burning with some other boats nearby.  earthling_andre posted a
         <a href="http://bbs.keyhole.com/ubb/download.php?Number=52776" title="GE File">
          placemark
         </a>
         <img border="0" height="17" src="http://www.gearthblog.com/images/gelogoicon.gif" title="Google  Earth File.  You must have GE installed." width="17"/>
         and his
         <a href="http://bbs.keyhole.com/ubb/showflat.php/Cat/0/Number/52776/an//page/0/vc/1">
          thoughts
         </a>
         at the Google Earth Community.
         <br/>
         A bunch of people speculated, and then a few weeks later someone named solskin posted that the satellite photo was indeed showing a burning boat, and the one occupant was rescued by the two boats in the scene.  He posted a link to a
         <a href="http://www.lhg.is/displayer.asp?cat_id=4&amp;module_id=220&amp;element_id=1435">
          news story in icelandic
         </a>
         , which was followed a few days later by an English translation
         <a href="http://bbs.keyhole.com/ubb/showthreaded.php/Cat/0/Number/105766/page/0/vc/1">
          found here
         </a>
         .  Apparently two helicopters also came out to help as well.
         <br/>
         What I find interesting is that a scene of drama is captured in a random satellite photo, discovered by someone browsing, and the mystery is solved by people all over the world.  Amazing!
        </p>
