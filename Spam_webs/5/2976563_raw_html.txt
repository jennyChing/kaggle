         <div class="wwsgd" style="display:none;">
          <div style="padding:10px 10px 0;border:1px solid #ddd;font-size:.9em;font-family:'Century Gothic', helvetica, sans-serif !important;line-height:1.5em;">
           <p>
            Are you looking for a little instant ease when it comes to kids home for summer vacation? I totally get you. Check out
            <a href="http://lifeasmom.com/the-summer-survival-guide" target="_blank">
             the Summer Survival Guide
            </a>
            . It's chock-full of great ideas to organize your summer and enjoy great times with yours kids.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          <em>
           The lunch hour is no longer 60 minutes. But, it can still be tasty and fun  with a little pre-planning and some make-ahead tips.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-27173" height="333" src="http://lifeasmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/make-ahead-school-lunches-copy.jpg" title="make-ahead school lunches" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          When I was a child, the lunch hour really was a lunch
          <em>
           hour
          </em>
          . The teachers had time to drive to a restaurant and order a sit-down meal. Kids could walk home and have lunch with whichever parent or caregiver might be home at that time.
         </p>
         <p>
          It was only those of us who lived far from school who brown-bagged it or bought the cafeteria lunch. We had plenty of time to eat and then go play on the playground.
         </p>
         <p>
          My how times have changed! Nowadays were all in a hurry. Many schools have shortened the lunch break, some to as short as 35 minutes. And kids who want to go home for lunch? I dont think that happens anymore, does it?
         </p>
         <p>
          School lunches, even in a homeschool setting, can be quick affairs. The day is so packed with activities that theres not time for the leisurely noontime meal of yesteryear. But, we want our kids to have good, nourishing meals at midday.
         </p>
         <p>
          Since were so
          <em>
           busy
          </em>
          , it can be hard to pull something together in the morning rush to get to school. Even though we homeschool, my day is pretty much packed, and I dont have a lot of time for meal prep throughout the day.
         </p>
         <p>
          The answer is to make ahead as much of the lunch as you can. Then, you can pack the lunch box and be on your way or pull it from the fridge at meal time.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Here are some steps to making it easy:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h2>
          1. Consider the proteins.
         </h2>
         <p>
          I like my kids to have a portion of protein at each meal. Good make-ahead proteins include:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Hard cooked egg  refrigerate in a dish.
          </li>
          <li>
           Yogurt cups  divide a larger container into small portions and refrigerate.
          </li>
          <li>
           Cheese slices or sticks  package in portion sizes and refrigerate.
          </li>
          <li>
           Bean salad  mix a batch and then divide into individual servings and refrigerate.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://goodcheapeats.com/2012/07/grilled-chicken-for-the-freezer/" target="_blank">
            Grilled and sliced chicken or meat
           </a>
            once cooled, package and refrigerate or freeze.
          </li>
          <li>
           Peanut butter or sunbutter sandwiches  can be made the night before and kept in the fridge.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://goodcheapeats.com/category/soups/" target="_blank">
            Soups and chilis
           </a>
            make ahead and freeze in individual portions. Reheat in the morning and pour into heated thermos.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h2>
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12532" height="375" src="http://lifeasmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/12/Grapefruit1.jpg" title="Grapefruit" width="500"/>
         </h2>
         <h2>
          2. Have some fruit and veg.
         </h2>
         <p>
          If were going to get eight servings of fruits and vegetables in a day, they need to be the majority in every meal. Here are some ideas to get you thinking:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://goodcheapeats.com/2010/02/crockpot-applesauce/" target="_blank">
            Applesauce
           </a>
            make a big batch and divide into small containers with lids. Freeze until the day of packing.
          </li>
          <li>
           Fruit salad  refrigerate cut-up fruit in individual containers.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://goodcheapeats.com/2010/04/cutting-grapefruit-sections/" target="_blank">
            Grapefruit sections
           </a>
            cut the fruit a night or two in advance and store in the refrigerator.
          </li>
          <li>
           Apple slices  Dip in lemon juice to prevent browning. Store in airtight containers for a day or two in advance.
          </li>
          <li>
           Carrot sticks and other crudite  slice up a variety of vegetables and have kids help you assemble snack bags for the week.
          </li>
          <li>
           Bagged raisins or other dried fruit  divide a larger package into snack sized bags.
          </li>
          <li>
           Fruit leathers  storebought or
           <a href="http://foodformyfamily.com/recipes/pumpkin-pie-fruit-rollups" target="_blank">
            homemade
           </a>
           .
          </li>
          <li>
           Green salad  make a large salad on Sunday night and divide into multiple containers to chill until ready to serve. Repeat on Wednesday night for the remainder of the week.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h2>
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26396" height="375" src="http://lifeasmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/water-bottles-in-fridge.jpg" title="water bottles in fridge" width="500"/>
         </h2>
         <h2>
          3. Keep the water bottles filled.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Storing empty water bottles is a pain. Caps and bottles separate and fall out of the cupboard. But, refilling them and storing them in them in the fridge? That will save you precious minutes when its time to pack lunches.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-27172" height="375" src="http://lifeasmom.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/basket-of-snack-bags.jpg" title="basket of snack bags" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <h2>
          4. Add a little treat.
         </h2>
         <p>
          Quick breads, scones, muffins, and cookies not only pack well for lunch, but they are also very freezer-friendly. Bake up a storm over the weekend, wrap well for freezing, and stash in the icebox. Pull out what you need when you pack lunches; they should thaw by serving time.
         </p>
         <p>
          For crackers, trail mix, and pantry-stable snacks, divide large batches, either homemade or storebought, into snack bags and store in a basket in the cupboard.
         </p>

         <h2>
          5. Include a warm fuzzy.
         </h2>
         <p>
          My 2nd grade teacher, Mrs. Marcy, was my very favorite. She was so encouraging, and she often gave out warm fuzzies, little tokens of affection and encouragement.
         </p>
         <p>
          Include a warm fuzzy in your childs lunch to remind him that you think of him during the day. It can be a small toy or a little note. Print out these fillable
          <a href="http://lifeasmom.com/?attachment_id=27176" target="_blank">
           Lunch Box Love Notes
          </a>
          and tuck one in each lunch box throughout the week.
         </p>
         <p>
          Click on the blue field and type whatever you want: words of encouragement, Scripture, or favorite things about your child.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you want to be more spontaneous, print out several sheets and cut them apart. Store them in the kitchen with a box of markers for late night or early morning love notes. The personal touch looks pretty cool.
         </p>
