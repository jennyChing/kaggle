           <a href="http://www.bizzyblog.com/2008/06/17/ap-explains-why-it-will-ignore-iraq-now-that-news-is-better/" rel="bookmark">
            AP Explains Why It Will Ignore Iraq Now That News Is Better
           </a>
          </h1>
          <div class="meta">
           Filed under:
           <a href="http://www.bizzyblog.com/category/msm-biz-bias/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in MSM Biz/Other Bias">
            MSM Biz/Other Bias
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.bizzyblog.com/category/taxes-government/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Taxes &amp; Government">
            Taxes &amp; Government
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.bizzyblog.com/category/us-military/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in US &amp; Allied Military">
            US &amp; Allied Military
           </a>
            Tom @ 11:05 pm
          </div>
          <div class="story-content">
           <p>
            An unbylined Associated Press report yesterday, at least
            <a href="http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/25196847/">
             as carried at MSNBC
            </a>
            , acknowledges improvement, and then explains why its not going to get much future coverage from the wire service as long as things stay that way:
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             BAGHDAD  Signs are emerging that Iraq has reached a turning point. Violence is down, armed extremists are in disarray, government confidence is rising and sectarian communities are gearing up for a battle at the polls rather than slaughter in the streets.
            </p>
            <p>
             Those positive signs are attracting little attention in the United States, where the war-weary public is focused on the American presidential contest and skeptical of talk of success after so many years of unfounded optimism by the wars supporters.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            But is the public tuning out because they are war-weary, or are they tuning out because are so tired of hearing and seeing over-the-top negative stories? Significant levels of positive news have been lacking from Iraq for so many years that the likely expectation is that any news will be bad news.
           </p>
           <p>
            Jim Tarantos more succinct take at the Wall Street Journals Best of the Web
            <a href="http://online.wsj.com/article/SB121372460122881495.html?mod=Best+of+the+Web+Today">
             is this
            </a>
            :
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             Dont expect to read any more about it (good Iraq news) from the AP. After all, youre just not interested.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            Once again, AP decides what the relatively disengaged will see based on its own set of priorities, which apparently do not include a responsibility to inform the public in a consistent matter.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             Cross-posted at
             <a href="http://newsbusters.org/blogs/tom-blumer/2008/06/17/ap-explains-why-it-will-ignore-iraq-now-news-better">
              NewsBusters.org
             </a>
             .
            </em>
           </p>
