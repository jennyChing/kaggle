         Dowsing Rod in case your gadgets didnt detect Wi-Fi
        </a>
       </h2>
       <span class="subtext">

     <div class="storycontent">
      <span class="news-body-text">
       <span id="ls_contents-0">
        <p>
         <img alt="wifi dowsing rod _01" src="http://www.instablogsimages.com/images/2009/09/18/wifi-dowsing-rod-_01_MGT7T_58.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p>
         Exploring both old and new technologies in order to generate fresh relationships between function and behavior, designer
         <a href="http://www.miket.co.uk/">
          Mike Thompson
         </a>
         appears to question common codes of conduct. Drawing inspiration from the Dowsing Rod, a stick used to locate hidden water wells, buried metals or ores and gemstones, the Dutch designer has come up with an innovative device called Wi-Fi Dowsing Rod that help the users in detecting the internet and remain connected. Looking like a Y-shaped branch, the wireless internet detector appears to revitalize the conventional practice
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dowsing">
          Dowsing
         </a>
         or doodlebugging.
        </p>
        <br/>
