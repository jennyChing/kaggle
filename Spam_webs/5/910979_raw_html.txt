        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2015/01/29/turn-based-gang-warfare-streets-of-chaos/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Turn-Based Gang Warfare: Streets Of Chaos">
          Turn-Based Gang Warfare: Streets Of Chaos
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="entry">
         <aside class="byline">
          <p>
           By
           <a href="mailto:adam@rockpapershotgun.com">
            Adam Smith
           </a>
           on January 29th, 2015 at 10:00 am.
          </p>
         </aside>
         <div class="social-buttons">
          <h4>
           Share this:
          </h4>
          <ul class="social-icons">
           <li>
            <a class="social-facebook" href="http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?u=http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2015/01/29/turn-based-gang-warfare-streets-of-chaos//">
             Facebook
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a class="social-twitter" href="http://twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=Turn-Based Gang Warfare: Streets Of Chaos http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2015/01/29/turn-based-gang-warfare-streets-of-chaos/">
             Twitter
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a class="social-reddit" href="http://www.reddit.com/submit?url=http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2015/01/29/turn-based-gang-warfare-streets-of-chaos//">
             Reddit
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <p>
          <img alt="An alternate set of posters for Lars Von Trier's Nymphomaniac" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/images/15/jan/streets.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Streets of Chaos [
          <a href="http://simprosestudios.com/?q=chaos">
           official site
          </a>
          ] is a turn-based strategy game in which you fight for control of a randomly generated city. The city is represented as a grid and to conquer it youll recruit gang members and send them out to perform missions on the various blocks, ranging from extortion and hacking to finger-clicking dance-offs. Or possibly violent combat with chains and baseball bats that you can choose to imagine as dance-offs should you be so inclined.
         </p>
         <p>
          When I saw the Steam page, I was immediately reminded of an old favourite and, indeed, Streets of Chaos is loosely inspired by the classic strategy PC game
          <a href="http://www.gog.com/game/chaos_overlords">
           Chaos Overlords
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-266505">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Chaos Overlords isnt a name to inspire fond reminiscence and homage in the way that many others do. Its no UFO or Fallout. In fact, itd probably fail the Kickstarter Test, which is a system Ive just invented whereby the nostalgic qualities of a game can be measured by the amount of money itd haul in during its first 24 hours on a crowdfunding platform. The fact that original designer John K Morris has been working on a sequel, irregularly, since 2006 suggests theres a possibility that a Chaos Overlords Reborn campaign is a possibility  the game isnt entirely forgotten  but Streets of Chaos might be the closest thing we see for a while.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its been out since the beginning of last year but just popped up on Steam for 1.99 and after a brief play this morning, I reckon Ill get more enjoyment out of it than a couple of Greggs pasties. Memories of Chaos Overlords came flooding back as soon as I saw the map but Streets lacks one important element of the game that inspired it  the potato-headed portraits for your characters may allow for more variety, but theyre not a patch on the brilliant cartoon gangs of the original.
         </p>
         <p>
          I dont remember how well Chaos Overlords worked as a game but it used its theme effectively enough to stay in my mind for almost two decades. A little flavour goes a long way. If Chaos Overlords gangs were the delicious Flaming Hot variety of your crisp of choice, Streets of Chaos lumpen-headed chaps are the Ready Salted packet hanging limply behind the wall of a flat-roof boozer.
         </p>
         <p>
          Despite that, Ill still end up sinking another couple of hours into it tonight, no doubt, because I want to see how the mechanics hang together. And I mean the strategic systems rather than the T-Birds. No more dance-offs.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://simprosestudios.com/?q=chaos">
           Streets of Chaos is available now
          </a>
          , as is
          <a href="http://simprosestudios.com/?q=chaos">
           a demo
          </a>
          , and  and
          <a href="http://www.gog.com/game/chaos_overlords">
           Chaos Overlords is available at GOG.com
          </a>
          . The latter isnt supported on Win 8 and the former is
          <a href="http://store.steampowered.com/app/346320/">
           cheaper through Steam
          </a>
          than its own website.
         </p>
        </div>
