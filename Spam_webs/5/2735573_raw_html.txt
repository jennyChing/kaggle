        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
         Vodka Gummy Bears
        </h1>
        <meta content="http://mixthatdrink.com/vodka-gummy-bears/" itemprop="url"/>
        <meta content="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-spoon.jpg" itemprop="image"/>
        <meta content="When you soak gummy bears in vodka, they absorb the alcohol and turn into a yummy fruity treat with a kick." itemprop="description"/>
        <p class="entry-meta">
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Vodka Gummy Bears" data-url="http://mixthatdrink.com/vodka-gummy-bears/">
        </div>
        <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
        </div>
        <p>
         When you soak gummy bears in vodka, they absorb the alcohol and turn into a yummy fruity treat with a kick. Since I first published this, Ive found a much
         <strong>
          quicker, simpler
         </strong>
         way to do this. It takes less than a day instead of the five or more days I recommended before, and the gummy bears get much bigger.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13850" height="900" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-pin-600x900.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         How to make Vodka Gummy Bears
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Time to complete: 20 hours.
         </strong>
         Thats 10-15 minutes to put the gummies in bowls, and the rest is just letting them soak.
        </p>
        <p>
         Youll need:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Vodka
         </li>
         <li>
          Gummy bears or worms
         </li>
         <li>
          A bowl
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         You dont need an expensive vodka  something mid-range is fine because youll barely taste it at all anyway.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13851" height="400" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-finished-600x400.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Related:
         <a href="http://mixthatdrink.com/starburst-vodka-tutorial/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Starburst Vodka Tutorial">
          Starburst Vodka Tutorial
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 1: Put your gummies in bowls
        </h2>
        <p>
         This is pretty straightforward. I kept out a dry gummy of each type so I could compare their size to the ones that were soaking.
         <strong>
          Note:
         </strong>
         the bowl on the right is rummy cola gummies, which ended up being so good that I gave them their own post [coming soon].
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13853" height="400" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-start-600x400.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 2: Pour the vodka
        </h2>
        <p>
         Next, pour enough vodka into each bowl to cover the gummies well.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13854" height="400" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-soaking-600x400.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 3: Wait patiently
        </h2>
        <p>
         At this point, youre done until the vodka has all been soaked up. You can cover the bowls to keep the fruity smell from filling up the room, but you
         <strong>
          dont need to cover or refrigerate them
         </strong>
         . In the earlier version of this tutorial, I said you did, but I was wrong. It actually works much faster if you just let them sit in a bowl.
        </p>
        <p>
         After about four hours, I fished out a gummy of each type, and put them beside the dry one. They were noticeably bigger, especially the worm, but I thought they could get bigger still. I ended up letting it go overnight. The next morning when I got up, about 20 hours had passed since the beginning of the experiment, and I fished out another gummy for comparison. You can see the difference for yourself:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13855" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-comparison-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13856" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-comparison-2-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Related:
         <a href="http://mixthatdrink.com/salted-caramel-vodka-recipe/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Salted Caramel Vodka Recipe">
          Salted Caramel Vodka Recipe
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Serving Drunken Gummy Bears
        </h2>
        <p>
         Once these are done, they get slippery and a little bit slimy. To serve, I recommend you spoon them out onto a flat dish or surface. As you do so, youll find some of them have gotten stuck together. Just wedge your spoon gently in between them and theyll pop apart. Offer guests spoons or appetizer forks to stab them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13857" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-spoon-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13858" height="400" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-plated-600x400.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Vodka Gummy Bears Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13859" height="400" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/vodka-gummy-bears-plated-2-600x400.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         There will be a little bit of juice left over at the bottom of the bowl you soaked them in.
        </p>
        <h2>
         The taste
        </h2>
        <p>
         These taste just like the candies, except with a little of that vodka burn.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Alcohol content
        </h2>
        <p>
         Theres been a lot of speculation about how much (or little) vodka is in these, so I weighed them dry and post-soaking. This wont be exact, but it should be somewhat useful. Dry, the bears weigh 2 grams and the worms weigh 5 grams. Soaked, the bears gained 2 grams of alcohol and the worms took on 3 grams. Other gummies you use may vary. In ounces, this means that each bear contains about 0.071 ounces of vodka and each work contains 0.106 ounces. So to equal a standard drink (1 1/2 ounces), you need about 21 bears or about 14 worms.
        </p>
