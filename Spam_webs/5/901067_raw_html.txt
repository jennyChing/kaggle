       </a>
      </h1>
     </div>
     <div class="byline">
      by Dave Llorens
     </div>
     <div class="entry-content">
      <p class="no-break">

       <span class="st_linkedin_large" st_title="The Pennsylvania Sunshine Solar Program  Updated Incentives" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-trends/the-pennsylvania-unhine-program-updated/">

      <p align="center">
       <img alt="Flickr/AMagill" class="size-full wp-image-1969 " height="377" src="http://solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/08/100Cash.jpg" title="100Cash" width="566"/>

       Photo: Flickr/AMagill
      </p>
      <p>
       Its appropriate that Pennsylvania native Ben Franklin is on the $100 bill, especially if youre thinking about going solar. Whether you live inbig Pennsylvania cities like Pittsburgh and Philadelphia, or smaller cities like Allentown or Scranton, Pennsylvania has just let loose some solar rebate love for you, and its a pretty sizable incentive.
      </p>
      <p>
       Weve just updated
       <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/pennsylvania/">
        the Pennsylvania solar page
       </a>
       , so you can see the eligibility requirements and some sample costs of a 3Kw to 5Kw system.
      </p>
      <p>
       Bottom line: The
       <a href="https://www.portal.state.pa.us/siteminderagent/forms/login.fcc?TYPE=33554433&amp;REALMOID=06-5621a676-0624-4642-bc94-9f3366619130&amp;GUID=&amp;SMAUTHREASON=0&amp;METHOD=GET&amp;SMAGENTNAME=-SM-a5n7I1ZY3D%2bwkCzJCdeyStrXsKqO%2f4pJ%2bNtN4mLOZcp%2b7m%2fBMQbCZmnm2BjDAlFD&amp;TARGET=-SM-http%3a%2f%2fwww%2eportal%2estate%2epa%2eus%2fportal%2fsso%2fSSOLogin%2easpx">
        Pennsylvania Sunshine Solar program
       </a>
       could take care of up to 35% of your installed cost. Thats in addition to the Federal governments 30% tax credit (which is supposed to be calculated after the Penn rebate is subtracted.)
      </p>
      <p>
       Remember that the program has just started. As it becomes more popular, the rebate will decrease (step down), so get in as early as you can by getting
       <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/see-your-solar-savings/">
        approved by solar installer in your area
       </a>
       . Then you can start not only saving money, but also trees, polar bears, and, you know, the planet. Big deal.
      </p>
