     <p>
      <b>
       Tony Robbins
      </b>
      (born
      <b>
       Anthony J. Mahavorick
      </b>
      ; February 29, 1960) is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Motivational_speaker" title="Motivational speaker">
       motivational speaker
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FORTUNE_magazine_article_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FORTUNE_magazine_article-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      personal finance instructor,
      <a href="/wiki/Coaching#Life_coaching" title="Coaching">
       life coach
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Self-help" title="Self-help">
       self-help
      </a>
      author. He became well known from his popular
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infomercials" title="Infomercials">
       infomercials
      </a>
      and self-help books:
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Unlimited_Power" title="Unlimited Power">
        Unlimited Power
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Unleash the Power Within
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Awaken the Giant Within
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2007, he was named in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">
        Forbes
       </a>
      </i>
      magazine's "Celebrity 100" list.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       Forbes
      </i>
      estimated that Robbins earned approximately $30 million USD in that year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Robbins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Robbins was born Anthony J. Mahavorick in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Hollywood,_California" title="North Hollywood, California">
       North Hollywood, California
      </a>
      , on February 29, 1960. His father was a parking garage attendant.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fortune_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-fortune-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Robbins is the eldest of three children. His parents divorced when he was 7. His mother remarried several times. He is of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croat" title="Croat">
       Croat
      </a>
      descent and adopted his stepfathers surname Robbins later in life.
     </p>
     <p>
      Jim Robbins, a former semi-professional baseball player, legally adopted Tony.
     </p>
     <p>
      Robbins was raised in
      <a href="/wiki/Azusa,_California" title="Azusa, California">
       Azusa
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Glendora,_California" title="Glendora, California">
       Glendora, California
      </a>
      , and attended
      <a href="/wiki/Glendora_High_School" title="Glendora High School">
       Glendora High School
      </a>
      . He worked as a
      <a href="/wiki/Handyman" title="Handyman">
       handyman
      </a>
      to help provide for his siblings. He was elected student body president in his senior year, and grew 10 inches in high school, a growth spurt later attributed to a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pituitary_tumor" title="Pituitary tumor">
       pituitary tumor
      </a>
      . He has said his home life was "chaotic" and "abusive", and that his mother chased him out of the house with a knife when he was 17, and he never returned.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fortune_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-fortune-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Robbins worked as a janitor and did not attend college.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fortune_4-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-fortune-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
       Career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Robbins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Robbins began his career promoting seminars for
      <a href="/wiki/Jim_Rohn" title="Jim Rohn">
       Jim Rohn
      </a>
      . According to Robbins, Rohn taught him that happiness and success in life are not the result of what we have, but rather of how we live. What we do with the things we have makes the biggest difference in the quality of life.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robbins_1987_p.409_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Robbins_1987_p.409-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Later Robbins began his own work as a self-help coach. He taught
      <a href="/wiki/Neuro-linguistic_programming" title="Neuro-linguistic programming">
       neuro-linguistic programming
      </a>
      (NLP) and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ericksonian_Hypnosis" title="Ericksonian Hypnosis">
       Ericksonian Hypnosis
      </a>
      after training with NLP co-founder
      <a href="/wiki/John_Grinder" title="John Grinder">
       John Grinder
      </a>
      . In 1983, Robbins learned to
      <a href="/wiki/Firewalking" title="Firewalking">
       firewalk
      </a>
      from
      <a href="/wiki/Tolly_Burkan" title="Tolly Burkan">
       Tolly Burkan
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sternfield_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Sternfield-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and began to incorporate it into his seminars.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Robbins_1992_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Robbins_1992-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Robbins promoted his services as a "peak performance coach" through his books and TV
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infomercials" title="Infomercials">
       infomercials
      </a>
      , gaining strong public recognition and lucrative sales. He gained wide exposure through infomercials advertising his
      <i>
       Personal Power
      </i>
      series of self-help audiotapes.

     <h2>

      </span>

     <p>
      Robbins has published three best-selling books,
      <i>
       Unlimited Power
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Awaken the Giant Within
      </i>

      <i>
       MONEY Master the Game: 7 Simple Steps to Financial Freedom.
      </i>

     <p>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Unlimited_Power" title="Unlimited Power">
        Unlimited Power
       </a>
      </i>
      , published in 1987, discusses the topics of health and energy, overcoming fears, persuasive communication, and enhancing relationships.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>

      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Dale_Carnegie" title="Dale Carnegie">

      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/How_to_Win_Friends_and_Influence_People" title="How to Win Friends and Influence People">
        How to Win Friends and Influence People
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Norman_Vincent_Peale" title="Norman Vincent Peale">
       Norman Vincent Peale
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       The Power of Positive Thinking,
      </i>
      <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_Hill" title="Napoleon Hill">
       Napoleon Hill
      </a>

      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Think_and_Grow_Rich" title="Think and Grow Rich">
        Think and Grow Rich
       </a>
       ,
      </i>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Maxwell_Maltz" title="Maxwell Maltz">
       Maxwell Maltz
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Psycho-Cybernetics" title="Psycho-Cybernetics">
        Psycho-Cybernetics
       </a>
       .
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Awaken the Giant Within
      </i>
      , published in 1991, was an expansion of his personal development techniques and strategies taught through a motivational self-help type approach.
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition, in 1994 he published the daily instruction book,
      <i>
       Giant Steps,
      </i>
      in a small pocket size.
     </p>
     <p>
      In November 2014 he released a new book.
      <i>
       MONEY Master the Game: 7 Simple Steps to Financial Freedom
      </i>
      is based primarily on research from practitioners including Nobel Laureates and self-made billionaires. It is intended to demystify money. It addresses money's benefits, limits, acquisition, and stewardship.
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       MONEY Master the Game
      </i>

      <i>
       Wall Street Money Machine
      </i>

      <a href="/wiki/Tacoma,_Washington" title="Tacoma, Washington">

Cook's lead attorney said "This is a landmark decision by the 9th Circuit Court because of the high profile players and issues at stake. The Court is telling individuals that, no matter who you are, the protection of copyrightable material will be preserved." Robbins was forced to pay the entire amount.

        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Robbins and his first wife were divorced in June 2001. Later that same year he married aspiring actress Bonnie Humphrey, now known as Sage Robbins.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
       <a href="#cite_note-42">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        42
        <span>

        </span>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Spiritual_beliefs">
       Spiritual beliefs
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Robbins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Spiritual beliefs">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Robbins alluded to his personal belief in a higher power in
      <i>
       Unlimited Power
      </i>
      , giving the grand complexity of life as evidence of an
      <a href="/wiki/Teleological_argument" title="Teleological argument">
       intelligent designer.
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
       <a href="#cite_note-43">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        43
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His spiritual beliefs are also reflected in his personal life purpose: "To humbly serve the Lord by being a loving, playful, powerful, and passionate example of the absolute joy that is available to us the moment we rejoice in God's gifts and sincerely love and serve all of his creations."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
       <a href="#cite_note-44">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        44
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Health_issues">
       Health issues
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Robbins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Health issues">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Robbins has stated that he has
      <a href="/wiki/Acromegaly" title="Acromegaly">
       acromegaly
      </a>
      , a condition in which the
      <a href="/wiki/Anterior_pituitary" title="Anterior pituitary">
       anterior pituitary
      </a>
      gland produces excess
      <a href="/wiki/Growth_hormone" title="Growth hormone">
       growth hormone
      </a>
      (GH) after
      <a href="/wiki/Epiphyseal_plate" title="Epiphyseal plate">
       epiphyseal plate
      </a>
      closure at
      <a href="/wiki/Puberty" title="Puberty">
       puberty
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Plaskin2013_45-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Plaskin2013-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As is often the case, this appears to have been linked to a tumor in his
      <a href="/wiki/Pituitary_gland" title="Pituitary gland">
       pituitary gland
      </a>
      ; doctors recommended surgery for the brain tumor at age 31, but Robbins did not go through with the procedure. He has incorporated his personal experiences related to this ordeal into his public speaking.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schnall_46-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Schnall-46">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        46
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
