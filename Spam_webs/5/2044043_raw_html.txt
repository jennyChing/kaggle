            LOL: Scott Pilgrim vs. The Matrix
           </a>
          </h1>
          <p class="post-info">
           Posted on Monday, August 23rd, 2010 by
           <a href="http://www.slashfilm.com/author/adam-quigley/" rel="author" title="Posts by Adam Quigley">
            Adam Quigley
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="top-bot">

        <p>
         <img alt="scottpilgrim_matrix" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-84588" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/bitcast-a-sm.bitgravity.com/slashfilm/wp/wp-content/images/scottpilgrim_matrix.jpg?resize=550%2C229" title="scottpilgrim_matrix"/>
        </p>
        <div class="addthis_jumbo_share" style="float: right; margin-left: -20px;margin-right: -15px">
        </div>
        <p>
         With
         <strong>
          <em>
           Scott Pilgrim vs. the World
          </em>
         </strong>
         being
         <a href="http://www.slashfilm.com/2010/08/22/box-office-the-expendables-stays-1-scott-pilgrim-drops-to-10/">
          the financial disaster
         </a>
         that it is, the blame is likely to be placed largely on the marketing. Where did the trailers go so wrong?
        </p>
        <p>
         But more importantly, a question youve clearly been wondering ever since
         <em>
          Pilgrim
         </em>
         bombed: If
         <strong>
          <em>
           The Matrix
          </em>
         </strong>
         had been anything like
         <em>
          Scott Pilgrim vs. the World
         </em>
         , would it still have found success?
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats not at all the question posed by the video
         <strong>
          <em>
           Scott Pilgrim vs. The Matrix
          </em>
         </strong>
         , a craftily edited mash-up of
         <em>
          Scott Pilgrim
         </em>
         trailer audio and footage from
         <em>
          The Matrix
         </em>
         trilogy, but what the hell, it should give you guys something to talk about in the comments. Watch the trailer, complete with perfectly utilized Woah usage, after the break.
         <span id="more-84586">
         </span>
        </p>
