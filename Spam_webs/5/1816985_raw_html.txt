&lt;h1 class="pageTitle"&gt;Those Who Love&lt;div class="pull-right"&gt;&lt;g:plusone&gt;&lt;/g:plusone&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/h1&gt; &lt;div class="entry-content clearfix"&gt;
&lt;div id="content"&gt; 
       &lt;div class="node"&gt;
   &lt;h2 class="author"&gt;by Sara Teasdale&lt;/h2&gt;
      &lt;div class="share-box pull-right"&gt;
                        &lt;ul class="clearfix"&gt;
                            &lt;li class="clearfix"&gt;
                                &lt;a class="addthis_button_email"&gt;&lt;span class="icon-envelope"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;Email&lt;/a&gt;
                                &lt;a class="addthis_button_print pull-right"&gt;&lt;span class="icon-print"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;Print&lt;/a&gt;
                            &lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;/div&gt;
      &lt;div class="content-poetry"&gt;
      &lt;p&gt;Those who love the most,&lt;br /&gt;
Do not talk of their love,&lt;br /&gt;
Francesca, Guinevere,&lt;br /&gt;
Deirdre, Iseult, Heloise,&lt;br /&gt;
In the fragrant gardens of heaven&lt;br /&gt;
Are silent, or speak if at all&lt;br /&gt;
Of fragile inconsequent things.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;And a woman I used to know&lt;br /&gt;
Who loved one man from her youth,&lt;br /&gt;
Against the strength of the fates&lt;br /&gt;
Fighting in somber pride&lt;br /&gt;
Never spoke of this thing,&lt;br /&gt;
But hearing his name by chance,&lt;br /&gt;
A light would pass over her face.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/div&gt;
