        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2009/12/31/2010-year-of-the-tiger-tank/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to 2010: Year Of The Tiger Tank">
          2010: Year Of The Tiger Tank
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="entry">
         <aside class="byline">
          <p>
           By
           <a href="mailto:timfstone@gmail.com">
            Tim Stone
           </a>
           on December 31st, 2009 at 2:23 pm.
          </p>
         </aside>
         <div class="social-buttons">
          <h4>
           Share this:
          </h4>
          <ul class="social-icons">
           <li>
            <a class="social-facebook" href="http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?u=http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2009/12/31/2010-year-of-the-tiger-tank//">
             Facebook
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a class="social-twitter" href="http://twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=2010: Year Of The Tiger Tank http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2009/12/31/2010-year-of-the-tiger-tank/">
             Twitter
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a class="social-reddit" href="http://www.reddit.com/submit?url=http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2009/12/31/2010-year-of-the-tiger-tank//">
             Reddit
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/jetthunder01.jpg">
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/tiger026.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23225" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/tiger026.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          In wilderness survival situations a dearth of hope can kill as quickly as a dearth of drinking water or plump companions. Remembering this, I survived a recent family gathering by mentally compiling a list of the sims and wargames I was most looking forward to in 2010.
          <span id="more-23218">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           While Auntie Judith was telling me about her hysterectomy, I was picturing Wildebeest Gamesmountaineering sim
          </em>
          <a href="http://www.wildebeestgames.com/">
           <em>
            Vertigo
           </em>
          </a>
          <em>
           .
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="385" width="480">
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
           </param>
           <param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/qJVYpQAjgzc&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;">
           </param>
           <param name="allowfullscreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/qJVYpQAjgzc&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
           </embed>
          </object>
         </p>
         <p>
          Detailsare scarce at present, but the preview videos suggests very classy clambering. Gawp as the ragdoll Hillary stretches for hand and footholds! Nail-bite as the weary-o-meters on each of his wiry limbs fill! Gasp as he falls from the face and dangles over the abyss (even if the abyss isnt particularly abyss-like at present). If Leeds-based Mr Wildebeest can conjure a random rock face generator and do justice to his title with some truly knee-jellying drops,Ill be all over this like edelweiss.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           While Uncle Terry was extolling the virtues of National Socialism, I was dreaming of Combat Mission: Normandy.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/CMNormandy02.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23227" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/CMNormandy02.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its been a long patch-cobbled road but
          <a href="http://www.battlefront.com/index.php?option=com_content&amp;task=blogcategory&amp;id=31&amp;Itemid=80">
           Shock Force
          </a>
          is finallyworthy ofbearing the CM name. Yep, there are still issues  areas where it is weaker than its three wonderful predecessors, areas where the differing levels of abstraction
          <a href="http://forums.gamesquad.com/blog.php?b=460">
           clash distractingly
          </a>
           but if youre after intricate, gripping combat simulation, you owe it to yourself to at least try
          <a href="http://www.battlefront.com/index.php?option=com_content&amp;task=blogcategory&amp;id=42&amp;Itemid=358">
           the latest demo
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          At some point in the coming year CMSF will be trading its Abrams for Shermans, its M4 carbines for M1s. The Normandy game will be cramped by CMx1 standards, simulating just a few months of conflict and a few hundred square miles of terrain. Therell be no British or Commonwealth forces either (theyll arrive in a later module). The good news is BF appear to be working hard to win back remaining dissidents. In addition to a revised Quick Battle system that permits cherry-picked forces, we should be getting bridges, hidden trenches, threat misidentification, and sophisticated hedgerow modelling. Shawn of Borg spotting, and spiced with buddy aid, hedgecutter tanks, and real-time play, battles in the Bocage should be absorbing affairs.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           While cousin Simon attempted to give me the Good News, I contemplated
          </em>
          <a href="http://www.matrixgames.com/products/377/details/Command.Ops:.Battles.from.the.Bulge">
           <em>
            Command Ops: Battles From The Bulge
           </em>
          </a>
          <em>
           .
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/bftb02.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23232" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/bftb02.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Like
          <a href="http://www.panthergames.com/Games/index.html">
           its predecessors
          </a>
          , this WW2 operational wargame wears bog-standard battledress. The mappy, counter-strewn screenshots suggest stodge, turn-based slowness, and inaccessibility, when in fact the reality(if the past is any guide) will be a game that is as fluid and pacey, as it is clever and convincing. Panthers secret? Turnless play, resourceful adversaries, and AI-controlled subordinates smart enough to
          <em>
           interpret
          </em>
          player orders rather than slavishly follow them. You dont rubber-band hordes of units in a Airborne Assault/Command Ops wargame and send them swanning towards the enemy. You select an HQ, give it a nuanced order and let it choose its own routes, allocate its own reserves, and arrange its ownartillery support. Forces can be marshalled at any level. Micromanagement is an option ratherthan anobligation.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tantalising engine improvements such as automated bridge building, changing ground conditions, and new bypass and ignore stragglers commands, wont mean a lot to the uninitiated but you can read about them
          <a href="http://www.matrixgames.com/forums/tm.asp?m=1964851&amp;mpage=1&amp;key=) ">
           here
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.matrixgames.com/forums/tm.asp?m=1723551">
           here
          </a>
          if you like. Id grumble about the absence of 3D R.U.S.E./
          <a href="http://www.matrixgames.com/products/370/details/World.War.II:.General.Commander">
           WW2 General Commander
          </a>
          -style maps if I hadnt already done it countless times before.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           While Uncle Percy was downing the last of the perry, I was debating whether to add
          </em>
          <a href="http://www.histwar.com/">
           <em>
            HistWar: Les Grognards
           </em>
          </a>
          <em>
           to my mental manifest.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/HISTWAR01.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23238" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/HISTWAR01.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Itll take more than a
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2009/11/30/napoleonic-irrigation-histwar-demo-out/">
           shambolic demo
          </a>
          littered with dodgy French-English translation to put me off Jean-Michel Maths Napoleonic opus. Actually, the demo isnt quite the disaster area it first appears. Following the suggestions in
          <a href="http://www.histwar.com/forum/index.php/topic,3265.0.html">
           this thread
          </a>
          , Ive managed to wring some moderately entertaining scraps out of the thing. Still, its good to hear that a second more polished taster is on the way.
         </p>
         <p>
          For those whove glanced at HistWar and seen only a rather ugly Total War aspirant, be aware that the game offers much that TW doesnt. For starters, armies are modelled as tiered entities rather than amorphous blobs. Orders once issued must percolate down, a slow, fraughtprocess that involves aides-de-camp galloping hither and thither. Youve also got friendly fog of war. Your picture of the battle is determined by what your commander-in-chief avatar can actually see, and the reports  some old, some inaccurate  that arrive fromfar-flung cornersof the battlefield.
         </p>
         <p>
          <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="385" width="480">
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
           </param>
           <param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/3glIue148jI&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;">
           </param>
           <param name="allowfullscreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/3glIue148jI&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
           </embed>
          </object>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3glIue148jI&amp;feature=related">
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           While Auntie Ruth launched into her Still not married! routine, I submerged myself in thoughts of
          </em>
          <a href="http://silent-hunter.uk.ubi.com/silent-hunter-5/">
           <em>
            Silent Hunter 5
           </em>
          </a>
          <em>
           .
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/sh501.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23237" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/sh501.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The Royal Navy and WWI have got shafted again, but its hard to stay mad at Ubisoft Romania when theyre dangling delights like fully modelled U-boat interiors in front of us. While early glimpses suggest the modelling wont be a patch on
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/user/TomiU99#p/a/u/0/6ihHHtcOIXQ">
           Tomi 099s work
          </a>
          , the thought of dashing the length of a busy boat in response to an excited Smoke on the horizon! call, or watching in horror as brine foams through a breached bulkhead, tempts. The talk of AI subs, milch cows and  fingers-crossed  wolfpacks is also heartening. After hours of lonely freelancing, what could befiner than rendezvousing with a few fellow sea-lupines for a spot of communal convoy slaughter.
         </p>
         <p>
          One hopes that the lads and lasses from Bucharest are familiar with
          <a href="http://www.thegreywolves.com">
           SH3 mega-mod GWX
          </a>
          and not too proud to borrow freely from it. Promisingly they do appear to have abandoned one of the sillier aspects of SH4s U-Boat Missions expansion. Run into a spot of bother in the Denmark Strait and you wont be able to call up the Bismark or Graf Spee for assistance.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           While a kneeling Uncle Bob did his Jimmy Krankie impersonation, I heard
          </em>
          <a href="http://www.thunder-works.com/">
           <em>
            Jet Thunder
           </em>
          </a>
          <em>
           echoing round the hills at San Carlos Water.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/jetthunder01.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23239" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/jetthunder01.jpg"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/jetthunder01.jpg">
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Im including this Falklands War flight sim here more out of hope than expectation. The work of a small, self-financed team of Anglo-Argentinian amateurs, its been lurking on the edges of radar screen for years now. The theme still feels as fresh and enticing as ever (the Falklands Conflict is one of those very rare military campaigns where the actions of one or or two pilots might have changed history) but with each passing month without progress reports or a demo, the chances well ever see it, appear to recede.
         </p>
         <p>
          <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="385" width="480">
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
           </param>
           <param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/Pqglea5Q1dc&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;">
           </param>
           <param name="allowfullscreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/Pqglea5Q1dc&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
           </embed>
          </object>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           While Auntie Margaret inserted the third of her holiday snaps DVDs into the player, I remembered
          </em>
          <a href="http://www.scourgeofwar.com/">
           <em>
            Scourge of War
           </em>
          </a>
          <em>
           .
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/scourge01.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23236" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/scourge01.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its superb predecessor is going for
          <a href="http://store.steampowered.com/app/25840/">
           a mere 4 on Steam
          </a>
          at the moment. For that kind of money you might not expect to get one of the most entertaining AIs in wargaming, ludicrous replayability, and the sort of subtle soft factor modelling that has you screaming at the recklessness/reticence of CPU-controlled colleagues at regular intervals. As in HistWar and BftB, Scourge Of War represents armies as many-tentacled beasts. While one tentacle might be probing in one spot, another might be recoiling somewhere else, manoeuvring for an attack, or tightening its grip on a gain. The co-ordination within friendly and enemy ranks is rarely perfect, meaning plenty of authentic chaos.
         </p>
         <p>
          Since Take Command: Second Manassas, the two-man outfit that was MadMinute Games have parted ways. This, the first project from ex-MMGer Norb Timpko is based on a new engine that appears to have much in common with the old one. Apart from added multiplayer  admittedly a major advance  little seems to have changed. That suits me just fine, though it would have been nice to see the Shogun-style soldier sprites replaced by something a little chunkier.
         </p>
         <p>
          <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="385" width="480">
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
           </param>
           <param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/tV9G3eNhviM&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;">
           </param>
           <param name="allowfullscreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/tV9G3eNhviM&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
           </embed>
          </object>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           While waiting in the gateaux queue, a chance conjunction of cutlery and cocktail sausages reminded me of Digital Combat Simulator: A-10C.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/a10b.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23234" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/a10b.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The same part of me that cant look at a
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/2009/12/20/serious-sam-simulator/">
           SAM Simulator
          </a>
          screenshot without wishing to flick switches and twiddle knobs, cant wait to be let loose in the cockpit of Eagle Dynamics upcoming A-10C. Im not entirely sure this recreation of the modernised
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A-10_Thunderbolt_II">
           A-10 II
          </a>
          will be available in standalone form, but one things certain, like Black Shark, its going to be modelled to within a
          <span style="text-decoration: line-through;">
           inch
          </span>
          micron of its life. According to a recent forum post: Fly the DCS:A-10C and you will be able to fly the real thing. But the learning curve will be steep. In real life it takes 3 months to convert from A-10A to A-10C and operate correctly all the systems  avionics, weapons etc.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Gulp
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          EDs SU-25T  the star of the
          <a href="http://www.digitalcombatsimulator.com/index.php?scr=news&amp;news_sid=755&amp;lang=en&amp;end_pos=3">
           soon to be buffed-up Flamings Cliffs
          </a>
           remains my favourite ground-attack steed of all time. If the Warthog is as fastidiously fashioned and as characterful as I suspect it will be, then that could change.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           When conversation turned to the quality of signage on the M27, my thoughts turned to
           <a href="http://www.graviteam.com/games/kharkov-1943.html">
            Front Roads: Kharkov 1943
           </a>
           .
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/frontroads01.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23235" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/frontroads01.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Already released and demo-ed in Russia, this snow-draped company-level wargame with its strong Close Combat/Combat Mission echoes and intriguing strat layer, comes from a studio with a proven track-record for plausible, atmospheric armour sims. (If youre a combat simmer and dont already own
          <a href="http://www.steelfury.info/">
           Steel Fury
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://store.steampowered.com/app/1670/">
           the ridiculously cheap Iron Warriors
          </a>
          purchase them post-haste). Assuming the AI is fit for purpose and that chess-like op engine is as a promising as it appears, specialist Western publishers like Matrix and Battlefront should be beating a path to Graviteams door.
         </p>
         <p>
          <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="295" width="480">
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
           </param>
           <param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/3X1g1ADNEcM&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;">
           </param>
           <param name="allowfullscreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="295" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/3X1g1ADNEcM&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
           </embed>
          </object>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           And when the time came for parting pleasantries and cuddles, I hid in the cupboard under the stairs and mused on Storm of War: Battle of Britain
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/Storm01.jpg" rel="lightbox[23218]">
           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-23233" src="http://www.rockpapershotgun.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/Storm01.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I wrote my first SoW preview over three years ago and rereading it today I realise I knew about as much then about the sequel to IL-2 as I do now. Givens: the game will simulate those few months in 1940 when the fate of Britain was in the hands of exhausted young men in Hurricanes and Spitfires. It will feature flyable versions of all the obvious steeds plus a bevy of bizarre strangers like the
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiat_BR.20">
           Fiat BR.20
          </a>
          and the
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bristol_Fairchild_Bolingbroke">
           Bristol Bolingbroke
          </a>
          . Well train as RAF trainees trained, in Tiger Moths. The standard of cockpit craftsmanship will make even the most demanding simmer shed tiny blister-canopy-shaped tears ofjoy. Aircraft wear will be rendered and tracked. Clouds will be super-fluffy. Flak guns will be crewable. Bf 109s will be criminally underpowered according to several furious forum posters.
         </p>
         <p>
          The really crucial stuff: is Oleg doing a Broussard on us (the latest ETA is October)? Will SoWs dynamic campaign be as rich and riveting as
          <a href="http://www.a2asimulations.com/bob/">
           Battle of Britain IIs
          </a>
          ? Will its town and cityscapes be as
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5mcT_giikC4&amp;feature=related">
           jawdropping
          </a>
          as those in the recently stealth-launched
          <a href="http://www.airwargame.com/eng">
           Wings of Prey
          </a>
           that stuff remains veiled in Channel fog.
         </p>
         <p>
          <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="385" width="480">
           <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
           </param>
           <param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/Y9OWQ55n8ig&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;">
           </param>
           <param name="allowfullscreen" value="true">
           </param>
           <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/Y9OWQ55n8ig&amp;hl=en_GB&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
           </embed>
          </object>
         </p>
