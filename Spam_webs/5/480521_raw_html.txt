                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Quick Carrot Slaw and Finicky Fiancs
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2523777659773397461" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lwpvT5ePchg/U1W-SfYgJnI/AAAAAAAAGDE/Fn82YkjezDg/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-04-21+at+7.56.04+PM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="422" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lwpvT5ePchg/U1W-SfYgJnI/AAAAAAAAGDE/Fn82YkjezDg/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-04-21+at+7.56.04+PM.png" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Problem: Fiance dislikes all green food. Meaning side dishes are incredibly difficult to accomplish.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     To me, BBQ needs a good slaw on the side. Preferably a vinegar-based situation. However, Cory won't touch anything green, so my slaw supplies are typically low, since a cabbage will go bad before I can consume it all myself. When I made my
                     <a href="http://www.quarterlifecrisiscuisine.com/2014/04/bbq-apartment-chicken-when-bbq-craving.html">
                      BBQ Chicken
                     </a>
                     I really needed a palate cleanser and a fresh veggie combo to help tone down the heat and heaviness.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Carrots are one of the few veggies he'll eat, and while he didn't want to try this quick and tasty slaw, I knew the rest of the carrots wouldn't go to waste in the fridge. This slaw is quick and easy and makes a great tangy, light bite to pair with the spicy-sweet BBQ.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The carrots were provided by
                     <a href="http://www.grimmway.com/">
                      Grimmway Farms
                     </a>
                     , who is sponsoring our Brunch Week, coming soon! I love their carrots. I used the shredded variety here, but their carrot chips are PERFECT for dips and hummus!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      Quick Carrot Slaw
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       1 cup carrots
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       1/4 cup julienned cucumber
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       4 tbs white vinegar
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       1/2 tsp salt
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       1 tbs brown sugar
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       pinch of pepper
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       optional: sesame seeds
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     -Mix all ingredients together and let sit in fridge for at least 10 minutes. Serve cold.
