        <div class="info">
         <div class="regionintro base">
          <p>
           Discover Lancaster County
          </p>
          <p class="intro">
           Let the discovery begin. Let Lancaster County delight you with the things you always knew were here. And surprise you with a slew of things you didn't.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="regionintro antiques">
          <p>
           Antiques Capital
          </p>
          <p class="intro">
           Nestled in the countryside of northeastern Lancaster County, "Antiques Capital, USA" is recognized as the premier year-round antiques destination in the Northeast.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="regionintro smalltowns">
          <p>
           Small Towns &amp; Northern Countryside
          </p>
          <p class="intro">
           Stroll down a street in one of our small towns, and you'll think you've wandered right into a scene of a Norman Rockwell painting.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="regionintro amish">
          <p>
           Amish &amp; PA Dutch Countryside
          </p>
          <p class="intro">
           Pennsylvania Amish Country is a picturesque landscape that must be witnessed in person to be fully appreciated. Rolling hills with lush grasses and crops, farms with windmills dotting the horizon and horse and buggies sharing the road remind you that things are simpler here in Lancaster County.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="regionintro downtown">
          <p>
           Downtown Lancaster
          </p>
          <p class="intro">
           The city is situated in the center of Lancaster County, and its seven square miles are packed with art, museums, historical attractions, restaurants and cafes, theatres, shops and boutiques.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="regionintro riverhills">
          <p>
           River Hills
          </p>
          <p class="intro">
           Want even more of the great outdoors? The River Hills are home to a host of scenic biking tours and hiking trails through beautiful rolling hills.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="regionintro neighbors">
          <p>
           Next-door Neighbors
          </p>
          <p class="intro">
           While you are in town, take a short sightseeing trip from Lancaster County to explore our nearby neighbors. Tour awe-inspiring battlefields. Meander through historic mansions and museums.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>

      <h2 class="teal">
       Wine and Dine
      </h2>
      <p>
       With chicken pot pie and brazed buffalo medallions, theres no doubt weve got both down-home eats and upscale cuisine here in Lancaster. Enjoy upscale eating at John J. Jeffries, Fenz, Checkers Bistro, or Aussie and the Fox, or simpler but delicious fare at The Prince Street Caf, Annie Baileys, or the Pressroom.

       Historic Lancaster
      </h2>
      <p>
       Enjoy a Broadway-caliber show at the Fulton Theatre, which is 160+ years old - and the oldest continuously-operating theatre in the U.S. Browse the artwork at the Demuth Museum, or tour President James Buchanans Wheatland, the home of the man who preceded Lincoln in the White House, and you dont want to miss a stop at Central Market, the oldest operating farmers market.

      <h2 class="teal">

       Whether you are a fine art admirer, a handmade goods collector, an antiques lover, a theater-goer, a music lover, or an edible art seeker, we have got you covered. From art galleries to museums, local shops, theatres and the symphony, delicious farmers markets and more, the whole family is sure to love walking the streets of Downtown Lancaster.

      <h2 class="teal">
       Lancaster Central Market
      </h2>
      <p>
       Lancaster Central Market is the nations oldest continuously-operating farmers market, offering fresh meats and produce since 1730. Today, housed in a magnificent 1889 Victorian gem, Central Market offers locals and visitors the opportunity to buy everything from fresh-from-the-farm meats and produce to locally made artisan cheeses, delectable desserts, and even creations made from special family recipes from around the world. Open every Tuesday, Friday and Saturday.
