         <p>
          I love autumn but only with sunshine and crisp weather. Lately been looking for autumn songs, not just autumn in the title but also in the atmosphere or lyrics with that melancholic tune or melody, so characteristic for this nice season with truffles, game and dark red wine not to say dark chocolate as well.

           </embed>
          </object>
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           Autumn Leaves is a much-recorded popular song. Originally it was a 1945 French song Les feuilles mortes (literally The Dead Leaves) with music by Joseph Kosma and lyrics by poet Jacques Prvert. Yves Montand (with Irne Joachim) introduced Les feuilles mortes in 1946 in the film Les Portes de la Nuit. The film Autumn Leaves (1956) starring Joan Crawford featured the song, as sung by Nat King Cole over the title sequence.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Numerous covers were made of this song.
         </p>
         <p>
          A more recent excellent song
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kI50Gm262w0">
           t is autumn by Stacey Kent, here sung by Betty Boop
          </a>
          , here is
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QqRATDeaOak">
           a video with an interview with Stacey Kent
          </a>

         </p>

          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V0UcQDUR-fU">

          </a>
          , more funny as should be with the Kinks.
         </p>
         <p>
          From the disco and funk in the eighties
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hy-huQAMPQA">
           Earth Wind and Fire with a live performance of September
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          In classical music probably Vivialdis four seasons is the most well known, here is his autumn part:
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QOSg7LFgt6Y">
           Vivaldi, Four Seasons, Autumn
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          My favorite jazz artists besides
          <a href="http://www.shockmd.com/2009/12/20/bill-evans-the-most-influential-pianists-in-modern-jazz/">

          </a>
