     <p>
      <b>
       Zamanfou
      </b>
      , also known as
      <b>
       "ochaderfismos"
      </b>
      (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">
        Greek
       </a>
       ""
      </i>
      ) is a
      <a href="/wiki/Counterculture" title="Counterculture">
       counterculture
      </a>
      phenomenon in
      <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">
       Greece
      </a>
      which involves
      <a href="/wiki/Social_loafing" title="Social loafing">
       social loafing
      </a>
      as its principal characteristic.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">
       Etymology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zamanfou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The term is derived from the
      <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
       French
      </a>
      phrase
      <b>
       "je m'en fous"
      </b>
      (
      <i>
       foutre
      </i>
      ). The French expression
      <i>
       Je m'en fous
      </i>
      became known by the Greeks before
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      , when the
      <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
       French language
      </a>
      was the most common foreign language in
      <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">
       Greece
      </a>
      . It was later adopted into everyday slang vocabulary, with Greek spelling and pronunciation. It is roughly equivalent to the English 'I don't care'.
     </p>
     <p>
      The sister-term "ohaderfismos" derives from the Greek phrase "oh brother!" ( !).
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
       Background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zamanfou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Although Zamanfou might be considered a social phenomenon in Greece, it is no different from
      <a href="/wiki/Social_loafing" title="Social loafing">
       Social loafing
      </a>
      , as this appears in other cultures or epochs.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Zamanfou_in_popular_culture">
       Zamanfou in popular culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zamanfou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Zamanfou in popular culture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       "Zamanfou" is the title of a Greek song of Sakis Boulas.
      </li>
      <li>
       "Zamanfou" is the title of a Greek Hip Hop song of the rapper Eisvoleas.
      </li>
      <li>
       "Zamanfou" was the title of a Greek TV show by
       <a href="/wiki/Annita_Pania" title="Annita Pania">
        Annita Pania
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       "Zamanfou" is the title of a Greek Hip Hop song of the rapper
       <a href="/wiki/Taraxias" title="Taraxias">
        Taraxias
       </a>
       featuring
       <a href="/wiki/Sakis_Boulas" title="Sakis Boulas">
        Sakis Boulas
       </a>
       &amp; Vasia Patrikarea (new version of the Sakis Boulas' song).
      </li>
     </ul>
