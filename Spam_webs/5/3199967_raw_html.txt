            <h1 class="page-title">
             The Ski Season Survival Secrets These Seasonaires Swear By
            </h1>
            <div class="page-meta-wrapper">
             <span>
              By
              <span class="author">
               <a href="http://welove2ski.com/author/mpibrokers/" rel="author" title="Posts by Michael Pettifer">
                Michael Pettifer
               </a>
              </span>
              <span class="meta-sep">
               |
              </span>
              on October 16, 2014
              <span class="meta-sep">
               |
              </span>
              <a class="link-comments" href="http://welove2ski.com/ski-season-survival-secrets#comments">
               7 Comments
              </a>
             </span>
             <div class="categories">
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/brand-publisher" rel="category tag">
               Brand Publisher
              </a>
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/editors-picks" rel="category tag">
               Editor's Picks
              </a>
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/ski-holiday" rel="category tag">
               Ski Holiday
              </a>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="page-content content">
             <div class="page-featured-image">
             </div>
             <p>
              <img alt="Ski Seasonnaires in Morph Suits" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26730" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/ski-season-morph-suits.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               So youve bagged a job, booked your flights and youre off to work a season in a ski resort. Good work! Now you can prepare yourself for the wildest, most exhausting winter of your life. Michael Pettifer, MD of
               <a href="http://wl2s.co/mpibrokers" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://wl2s.co/mpibrokers', 'ski insurance specialists MPI Brokers');">
                ski insurance specialists MPI Brokers
               </a>
               , has been helping seasonaires recover from stolen wallets and blown knees for years, and knows a thing or two about the pickles youre likely to get into.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              If youre still gainfully employed by New Years Eve, youll look around and wonder what happened to all your comrades. A fair few will be back at home already, nursing broken bones or bruised egos. The wily veterans on their fifth season in a row will laugh: Who needs em? theyll say. They never had what it takes anyway.
             </p>
             <p>
              So what exactly
              <em>
               does
              </em>
              it take? What separates the apres-ski heroes from the faceplant zeroes? Well, here are a few golden rules, for starters
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              1. Be friends with EVERYBODY
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Ten Top Tips for Seasonnaires | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-26724" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/seasonaires-friends.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Yes, yes  of course, you need friends for all the cuddly stuff. But just as important is the fact that in a ski resort friendship is
              <strong>
               the quickest route to free stuff
              </strong>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              One experienced seasonaires advice is to make sure youre mates with people in as many different jobs as possible: ski shop employees (free ski servicing), bartenders (free drinks), coat room attendants (free storage), private chalet hosts (free chalet to hang out in), chalet staff (free leftovers), lift attendants (a blind eye turned when youve forgotten your lift pass). And make damn sure youve got something to offer in return beyond a hug and a thanks, man.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              2. Wave goodbye to a private life
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Goldfish in Glass Bowl" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26731" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/goldfish-glass-bowl.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Actually, its not so much like living in a goldfish bowl: more an aquarium packed with a wider cross-section of people  including some pretty odd fish and the occasional shark  than youve encountered before.
             </p>
             <p>
              Dont do anything you dont want the whole resort finding out about. When you meet a fellow seasonaire for the first time expect him or her to know your name, where youre from, and who youve been sleeping with.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              3. Pack about 500 pairs of underpants
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Twelve Top Tips For Seasonnaires | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-26706" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/IMG_3600.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Because youre never going to keep up with your laundry. The alternative? Have your Mum/friends/The Red Cross send fresh pairs over every week.
             </p>
             <p>
              That said, you should also avoid the temptation of bringing fashionable clothes. Most of the time you wont wear anything other than ski pants and thermals.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              4. Clean your bathroom
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Twelve Top Tips For Seasonnaires | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-26707" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/cleaning-the-bathroom.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Dont wait till the fungus starts to grow. Make a rota. Keep to it. The same goes for your chalet or apartment kitchen and its fridge. Exact a forfeit from the slackers
             </p>
             <p>
              Just because you spend every morning cleaning up after your guests it doesnt mean you cant take your work home with you. Epidemics of Skiers Tummy have ruined many a holiday and a proven cause is lack of hygiene among resort workers.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              5. Use hand cream, ALL THE TIME
             </h2>
             <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_26723" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Twelve Top Tips for Seasonaires | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-26723" height="393" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/22991155_m.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo: 
               <a href="http://www.123rf.com/profile_calvste" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.123rf.com/profile_calvste', 'calvste/123RF Stock Photo');" target="_blank">
                calvste/123RF Stock Photo
               </a>
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Thanks to Helen Peterson of
              <a href="http://www.leski.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.leski.com', 'Le Ski');" target="_blank">
               Le Ski
              </a>
              for this one. If any of your work involves cooking, cleaning, washing up or shovelling snow, your hands are going to fall apart without hand cream. Dont forget to use a high factor suncream all season and see our feature on
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/ski-clothing-winter-beauty-tips" target="_blank">
               winter beauty tips
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              6. Check the snow forecast before you go on a bender
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Twelve Top Tips For Seasonnaires | Welove2ski" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-26719" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/snow-forecast-December-25-21.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Its 5pm, and tomorrow is your day off. What do you next? Get the evening going with a couple of Jaegerbombs and a Flaming Sambuca? No. You check the
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/snow-forecast" target="_blank">
               snow forecast
              </a>
              . You dont want to stagger out of bed at 2pm to discover youve just missed the best powder day of the season.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              7. Get snow savvy
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Ski Season Survival Secrets | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-26753" height="670" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/haglofs-vojd.png" width="642"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              You dont want to become
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/how-to-ski/avalanche" target="_blank">
               an avalanche statistic
              </a>
              . Never head off-piste without full safety gear  including bleeper, probe and shovel. Listen to the professionals and catch
              <a href="http://www.henrysavalanchetalk.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.henrysavalanchetalk.com', 'Henrys Avalanche Talk');" target="_blank">
               Henrys Avalanche Talk
              </a>
              or a similar course. Dont just carry a bleeper, learn how to use it. If you can afford the outlay, invest in an ABS backpack like
              <a href="http://www.haglofs.com/en-uk/products/backpacksbags/ski/unisex/vojd_abs_30_en-us.aspx" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.haglofs.com/en-uk/products/backpacksbags/ski/unisex/vojd_abs_30_en-us.aspx', 'Haglfs');" target="_blank">
               Haglfs
              </a>
              Vojd ABS 18 or 30.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              8. Get some decent insurance
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Twelve Top Tips For Seasonnaires | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-26722" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/ski_fall1.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              And make sure it covers you for ski racing, the terrain park and activities such as heli-skiing, as well as those expensive doctors appointments in the resort. Its good to be covered for a trip home to Blighty in case of a family funeral, too.
             </p>
             <p>
              Holidaymaker policies dont cover you for a full season, you need a specialist one offered by
              <a href="http://wl2s.co/mpibrokers" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://wl2s.co/mpibrokers', 'a company that knows its onions');" target="_blank">
               a company that knows its onions
              </a>
              . Thievery thrives in ski resorts, so take care of your skis and lock them up if you are leaving them unattended. Dont leave your season pass and wallet in your jacket when you take it off in a bar or restaurant, and if youre taking your laptop dont forget to add it onto your policy.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              9. NEVER stop skiing
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Skiers Hiking in Darkness Never Stop Skiing" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-26729" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/never-stop-skiing.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Because if youre cleaning loos or cooking eight hours a day and driving to Geneva airport three times a week and you
              <em>
               dont
              </em>
              ski, then whats the point? Whatever the world throws at you in your working hours, skiing will make your winter beautiful.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              10. And remember, you can put anything in a baguette
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="Twelve Top Tips For Seasonnaires | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-26720" height="auto" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/IMG_3794.jpg" width="100%"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Here are a few ideas for
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/the-ultimate-ski-sandwich" target="_blank">
               what to put in a baguette
              </a>
              
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Have Your Say
             </h2>
             <p>
              Of course, theres no way this covers all the complexities of the ski season experience. But its a start, right?
             </p>
             <p>
              Help us turn it into the ultimate ski season survival guide. First,
              <strong>
               share it with your seasonaire friends
              </strong>
              . Next,
              <strong>
               add your suggestions
              </strong>
              in the comments box below  if theyre good, well include them in the list with full credit given to you. Finally,
              <strong>
               bookmark this page
              </strong>
              so you can come straight back to it throughout the season.
             </p>
             <p>
              May your season be full of powder days and polite clients. Good luck!
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               You can also read Michaels feature on
               <a href="http://welove2ski.com/avoid-skiing-injury-winter" title="Seven Ways to Avoid Injuring Yourself Skiing This Winter">
                Seven Ways to Avoid Injuring Yourself This Winter
               </a>
               . Photos on this page are by: Stock Photo, Izzi Hardy, Haglfs, Welove2ski.
              </em>
             </p>
