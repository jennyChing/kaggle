         NK Proteins wresting resources hard  Yet anticipatory bail for Nimish Patel rejected

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Pecuniary interests scoffed ethics; but befitting fruits are often reaped by thoroughgoing offenders. Nimish Patel of NK Proteins Ltd. was denied anticipatory bail by the city civil and session court under the MPID Act. His application for the bail was rejected, as his infractions have been attributed to various offences under sections 409, 465, 467, 468, 471, 474, 477-A and 120-B of the Indian Penal Code. Significantly, the offences were recorded by various investigating agencies such as EOW-SIT, CB-CID, Mumbai.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The emerged versions, in the bail rejection order, as per the statement recorded by the ED, were contrary to several accounts given by Nimish Patel including that he was not aware of the day to day affairs of NK Proteins Ltd. After detailed investigation, the investigating officer alleged that he was involved in the day to day affairs of N K Proteins, and also had the cognizance that the amounts received were siphoned off.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Collusion moorings stooped down to the steepest lows; NK Proteins Ltd. (NKPL)  which is one of the major defaulters in the NSEL crisis, and is obligated to pay back over 938 crore, was equally responsible in bulldozing the functionality of the spot exchange that, as echoed umpteen times, was created for the people to trade sell and transact with integrities.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Lately, specifically on 22
         <sup>
          nd
         </sup>
         October, 2014, Nilesh Patel of NK Proteins was arrested by the EOW of Mumbai police, and directed to police custody by a local Mumbai court. For over twenty years, Nilesh Patel, the man who built a business in oil seeds extraction, is tugging his political and police resourcefulness too hard.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.business-standard.com/article/companies/nilesh-patel-the-rs-900-cr-nsel-defaulter-113102300921_1.html" target="_blank" title="Business Standard ">
           It was in 1995-96 when we dealt with the company last to settle our receivables from the company. Since then, our bank does not make any transaction with them.
          </a>
          <strong>
           [Click Here to read complete News Article]
          </strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The government incessantly probed NK Proteins. An ardent entrepreneur and trader, Nilesh Patel has been known to be a businessman with strong political clout. Nilesh Patel hails from a district in Gujarat called Mehsana. He incepted NK Industries Ltd., along with his brother, Nimish Patel, who took the reins as chairman and managing director. Nilesh Patel is extremely conversant with construing the tendencies of commodities market. His well-known brand  Tirupati, leads the market in the cottonseed segment in Gujarat, with over 40% market stake.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Hes been an alleged notorious for evasion of payments towards farmers, traders and banks; he has been time and again accused of performing the act in the past. A public sector bank from Ahmedabad alleged that, in the mid-1990s, the company borrowed loans from a syndicate of banks and it dodged when it was their turn to repay. A banker from one of the public sector banks said,
         <em>
          It was in 1995-96 when we dealt with the company last to settle our receivables from the company. Since then, our bank does not make any transaction with them.
         </em>
         Hes also been known as a wanted defaulter and has been alleged for defaulting seed suppliers.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Earlier, in August 2014, movable and immovable properties of NK Proteins in Noida, Ahmedabad, Palanpur in Gujarat and Mumbai, worth over 18 crore, were seized by the Enforcement Directorate (ED), under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) provisions. Parallel investigations, along with the Mumbai police and other investigating agencies were being conducted on NKPL.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
          <strong>
           Await more about N K Proteins collusions in the next blog!
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
