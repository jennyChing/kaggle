        Logline:
       </strong>
       A man fights prejudice from both sides of the Civil War as he leads the first all-black volunteer company into battle against the Confederates.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Memorable Moments:
       </strong>
       The opening battle scenes are as grotesque and bloody as anything you will see in Saving Private Ryan. The rest of the film settles into a fascinating look at what life was like for black soldiers during the Civil War.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why Guys Dig It:
       </strong>
       There is a long debate that rages inside of us. Is it better to die a hero or live a coward? This film addresses it. Also, how can you go wrong with Morgan Freeman and Denzel Washington in the same movie?
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Glory-Matthew-Broderick/dp/0800177967/?tag=instash08-20">
        Buy the Movie
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        67. The Crow (1994)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/the-crow-1994.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="199" src="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/the-crow-1994.jpg" title="the-crow-1994" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Logline:
       </strong>
       A murdered musician returns from the grave to avenge the death of his lover.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Memorable Moments:
       </strong>
       While the film itself has many classic action scenes that include a dark, dirty, and deadly shootout between the hero and a roomful of hoods, the most memorable thing was also the most tragic. While shooting, star Brandon Lee, the son of Bruce Lee, was accidentally killed by a faulty handgun.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why Guys Dig It:
       </strong>
       Dark, moody, and horrific, The Crow combines the best elements of horror, action, and revenge drama.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Crow-Miramax-Dimension-Collectors/dp/B000059XUO/?tag=instash08-20">
        Buy the Movie
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        66. Reservoir Dogs (1992)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/reservoir-dogs-1992.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="199" src="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/reservoir-dogs-1992.jpg" title="reservoir-dogs-1992" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Logline:
       </strong>
       Seven strangers plan the perfect heist, but when things go haywire, they begin to suspect one of them is a cop.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Memorable Moments:
       </strong>
       Mr. Blonde (Michael Madsen) steals the show as a sick, twisted, and vile criminal with a hatred for cops and a penchant for sadism. He takes his time with a captive police officer, sawing off the young mans ear with a straight razor to the tune of Stealers Wheels Stuck in the Middle with You, a scene that results in one of the most glorious movie deaths of all time.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why Guys Dig It:
       </strong>
       Madsen, Harvey Keitel, Tim Roth, Chris Penn, Steve Buscemi, and the late great Lawrence Tierney in a violent bloodbath of twists, turns, and double crosses, written and directed by Tarantino. Nuff said!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Reservoir-Dogs-Harvey-Keitel/dp/B00008975Z/?tag=instash08-20">
        Buy the Movie
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        65. Saving Private Ryan (1998)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/saving-private-ryan-1998.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="199" src="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/saving-private-ryan-1998.jpg" title="saving-private-ryan-1998" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Logline:
       </strong>
       Eight U.S. soldiers are sent into harms way to bring home one man, whose brothers have all perished in World War II.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Memorable Moments:
       </strong>
       The opening segment is as violent, disgusting and haunting as anything youll see, made even more so because it really happened. Spielbergs use of violence renders the harsh reality of war that so many veterans long to forget. We owe it to them to understand what they went through.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why Guys Dig It:
       </strong>
       Most of us share a sense of duty to our country. Answering the moral question that this movie presents is infectious.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Saving-Private-Ryan-Special-Limited/dp/B00001ZWUS/?tag=instash08-20">
        Buy the Movie
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        64. Iron Man (2008)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/iron-man-2008.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="199" src="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/iron-man-2008.jpg" title="iron-man-2008" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Logline:
       </strong>
       A billionaire weapons manufacturer has a change of heart and develops a new super-suit to stop terrorism and injustice.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Memorable Moments:
       </strong>
       Robert Downey, Jr. chews the scenery and offers up many comical one-liners. The special effects are fantastic. Iron Mans suit looks even cooler brought to life than it ever did in the comics. And the relationship between Tony Stark (Downey) and Pepper Potts (Gwyneth Paltrow) feels warm and authentic.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why Guys Dig It:
       </strong>
       A guy owns all kinds of cool stuff, gets any girl he wants without commitment, fights the bad guys in a kickin suit, and gets to fly around as a rocket man. Good luck finding a guy who doesnt like this flick!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Iron-Man-Single-Disc-Robert-Downey/dp/B001C08RHA/?tag=instash08-20">
        Buy the Movie
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        63. Kill Bill (2003)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/kill-bill-2003.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="199" src="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/kill-bill-2003.jpg" title="kill-bill-2003" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Logline:
       </strong>
       A female assassin looks to even the score with her lover/boss, who shot her, took her baby, and left her for dead.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Memorable Moments:
       </strong>
       In Volume 1, it is the fight between The Bride (Uma Thurman) and Vernita Green (Viveca A. Fox). In Volume 2, it is the battle between The Bride and Elle Driver (Daryl Hannah). There is also the glorious anime sequence, and The Brides over the top handling of the Crazy 8s in Volume 1. And the extended wedding sequence in Volume 2.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why Guys Dig It:
       </strong>
       Chicks kicking butt. Graphic violence. Homage to the Kung Fu, Exploitation, and Spaghetti Western genres.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Kill-Bill-One-Uma-Thurman/dp/B00005JMEW/?tag=instash08-20">
        Buy the Movie
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        62. Leon: The Professional (1994)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/leon-the-professional-1994.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="199" src="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/leon-the-professional-1994.jpg" title="leon-the-professional-1994" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Logline:
       </strong>
       A hit man takes in a 12-year old girl orphaned by a corrupt gang of DEA agents.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Memorable Moments:
       </strong>
       The slaughter of Matildas family sticks with you, as does Natalie Portmans performance in the heat of the tragedy. Jean Reno plays dangerous killer, reluctant father, and uneducated child with the right amount of vulnerability that you cant help pulling for the guy, even if he does catch himself in some pretty compromising, but never line crossing, positions with a 12-year old.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why Guys Dig It:
       </strong>
       The hard-hitting action scenes. The depth of the characters. The pulse-pounding shootout at the end of the film. And the appeal this film has to our fatherly instincts.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Leon-Professional-Deluxe-Jean-Reno/dp/B0006GVJEE/?tag=instash08-20">
        Buy the Movie
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        61. Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom (1984)
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/indiana-jones-and-the-temple-of-doom-1984.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="199" src="http://www.instash.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/indiana-jones-and-the-temple-of-doom-1984.jpg" title="indiana-jones-and-the-temple-of-doom-1984" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Logline:
       </strong>
       Dr. Jones goes after mystical stones and a child slavery ring.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Memorable Moments:
       </strong>
       The freight car chase sequence and the adventures in eating monkey brains, bugs, baby snakes, and eyeball soup made this one stand out, as did the fire pit and a heart yanked, still beating, from a mans chest. While the bridge sequence was ripped off from the pirate film Nate and Hayes, its pretty exciting, too.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Why Guys Dig It:
       </strong>
       Are you kidding? Our childhood hero delving into impossibly ridiculous stunts with the beautiful Kate Capshaw and gadget expert Short-Round at his side! How could we not?
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Indiana-Jones-Temple-Doom-Special/dp/B0014Z4ON4/?tag=instash08-20">
        Buy the Movie
       </a>
      </p>
