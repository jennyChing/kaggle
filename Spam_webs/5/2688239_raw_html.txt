function updateCountdowns() {
	editbuttons = $('button.comment_edit:visible');
	if (editbuttons.length) {
		editbuttons.each(function() {
			timeremaining = $(this).data('editwindow-remaining');
			if (--timeremaining &lt; 0) {
				$(this).text('edit timer expired');
				$(this).fadeOut(); //no need for further time update; we only poll visibles
			} else {
				$(this).text('edit ('+timeremaining+' seconds remaining)');
				$(this).data('editwindow-remaining', timeremaining);
			}
		})
	} else {
		//no reason to keep this running when there's nothing to update
		clearInterval(countdownTimer);
	}
}

var countdownTimer = null;

$(document).bind('loginStatus', function(e,d){
	servertime = Number(d.unixtime);
	editing_window_seconds = Number(d.editing_window_seconds);
	if (d.logged_in) {
		userID = d.user_id;
		var handle = $("#user_login_control")
		.html('&lt;p&gt;You are currently logged in as: &lt;strong&gt;&lt;a href="http://reason.com/users/manage" class="handle"&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/strong&gt;. &lt;a href="http://reason.com/users/logout"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Sign Out&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;')
		.find('.handle')
		handle.text(d.handle)
		handle.attr('href', handle.attr('href')+'/'+userID);
		//look for all the editable comments by this user
		$('div#commentcontainer li[data-user-id='+userID+']').each(function( index ) {
			commenttimestamp = Number($(this).data('comment-timestamp'));
			elapsedtime = servertime - commenttimestamp;
			if (elapsedtime &lt; editing_window_seconds) {
				secondsremaining = editing_window_seconds - elapsedtime;
				$(this).find('button.comment_edit').show().data('editwindow-remaining', secondsremaining);
			}
		});
		countdownTimer = setInterval(updateCountdowns, 1E3);
	} else {
		$("#user_login_control").html('&lt;a href="http://reason.com/users/login"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Login&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt; or &lt;a href="http://reason.com/users/register"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Sign-up&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;');

When you find yourself repeatedly asking, Will they ever learn? the answer may be that the decision-makers have no incentive to do things differently. What looks like failure may be the intended outcome. Quagmires have their benefitsto the ruling eliteif American casualties are minimized.

         <p>
          The Obama administration is assisting Saudi Arabia in its bombing of Yemen, creatingin concert with the Saudi embargoa humanitarian catastrophe in the Middle Easts poorest country. Civilians are dying, and what infrastructure the country has is being destroyed.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/ahron/173731654/sizes/l" target="_blank">
           <span class="addcaption pic right" style="width:300px">
            <img alt="Credit: Ahron de Leeuw/Flickr" height="206" src="http://cloudfront-media.reason.com/mc/_external/2015_04/sanaa-yemen-credit-ahron-de-le.jpg?h=206&amp;w=300" title="Sana'a, Yemen" width="300"/>
            <span class="caption">
             Credit: Ahron de Leeuw/Flickr
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          Why? Secretary of State John Kerry
          <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/middleeast/yemen/11524933/John-Kerry-warns-Iran-that-US-will-not-stand-by-as-Tehran-backs-Yemen-rebels.html">
           says
          </a>
          the United States wont "stand by while the region is destabilized." Kerry is a veteran, and presumably a student, of Americas Indochina war. So he must know that bombing is a terrible way to prevent destabilization. Kerry isnt stupidbut that means hes a liar and a demagogue.
         </p>
         <p>
          Note that he says "the region,"not "Yemen."Why would a civil war in Yemen affect the region? Because according to the official narrative, faithfully carried by most of the news media, Yemen is under siege by agents of Iran, the Houthis.
         </p>
         <p>
          Iran today serves the same purpose the Soviet Union, or the International Communist Conspiracy, served from the end of World War II until 1989-91, when the Soviet empire collapsed. Iran is the all-purpose arch enemy on which virtually any evil can be blamed. So the war party and its Saudi and Israeli allies tell us every day that Iran is on the march, controlling capitals throughout the Middle East: Baghdad, Damascus, Beirut, and now Sanaa.
         </p>
         <p>
          But this is absurd. Iran is not on the march. George W. Bush knowingly delivered Baghdad to Iran-friendly Iraqi Shiites in 2003. The Assad regime in Syria is a long-time Iranian ally that Obama and his first secretary of state, Hillary Clinton, declared open season on, emboldening al-Qaeda and its more-virulent mutation, ISIS. Irans friend in Lebanon, the political party Hezbollah, formed itself in response to Israels 1982 invasion and long occupation. None of these demonstrate an aggressive Iran. A better explanation is that those alliances help Iran cope with the American
          <a href="https://twitter.com/mikopeled/status/581463361121968129">
           encirclement
          </a>
          . [Recall: the CIA overthrew Irans democratic government in 1953 and was complicit in Iraqs 1980s offensive war against Iran, in which Saddam Hussein used U.S.-facilitated chemical weapons. Since then, U.S. presidents and Israels government have attacked Iran in many ways: economic, cyber, proxy-terrorist, and covert.]
         </p>
         <div class="ym" id="ym_1044036704457308825">
         </div>

         <p>
          And what of Yemen, where the Houthis drove out the U.S.-backed autocratic president while also fighting declared enemies of the United States, Sunni al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula and the Yemeni affiliate of ISIS? Yes, the Houthis practice a kind of Shiite Islam, Zaidi, but it differs importantly from Iranian Shiism. In fact, the Houthis are merely the latest manifestation of a long-oppressed Yemeni religious minority seeking autonomy from the central government. After years of being frustrated, lied to, and double-crossed, it finally moved on that government. Say what you will about the group, but dont call it an agent of Iran.
         </p>
         <p>
          Saudi Arabia sees Iran as a menace, but the kingdom is hardly credible, and the Obama administration is likely to be placating the royal family now that a nuclear deal with Iran may be at hand. As independent researcher Jonathan Marshall
          <a href="https://consortiumnews.com/2015/04/13/how-washington-adds-to-yemens-nightmare/">
           notes
          </a>
          , "Decades before Iran became an enemy, however, Saudi Arabia began intervening in its southern neighbor [Yemen]. Besides grabbing land, the Saudis poured vast sums of money into Yemen to promote its extreme brand of Sunni Islam known as Wahhabism. In 2009, it invaded northern Yemen to attack the Houthis, unsuccessfully."
         </p>
         <p>
          Marshall adds, "Washington has also inserted itself in Yemens civil conflicts for decades."
         </p>
         <p>
          Of course Washington has been killing Yemenis with dronesnot all of them even "
          <em>
           suspected
          </em>
          terrorists"since 2001, when the corrupt and oppressive government in Sanaa became an ally in the "war on terror."
         </p>
         <p>
          "Yemens government repeatedly used U.S. military aid to support an all-out assault against the Houthis ('Operation Scorched Earth')," Marshall writes, "causing extensive civilian casualties."
         </p>
         <p>
          As we should know by now, U.S. intervention is no innocent mistake.
         </p>
