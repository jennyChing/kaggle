           Getting Started with OpenShift
          </h1>
          <p class="lead">
           Intrigued by the possibilities of developing web applications in the cloud? With this concise book, you get a quick hands-on introduction to OpenShift, the open source Platform as a Service (PaaS) offering from Red Hat.
          </p>

        <section id="ebook_summary">
         <h2>
          What you will learn with this eBook
         </h2>
         <p class="larger">
          Youll learn the steps necessary to build, deploy, and host a complete real-world application on OpenShift, without having to read long, detailed explanations of the technologies involved. Though the book uses Python, application examples in other languages are available on GitHub. If you can build web applications, use a command line, and program in Java, Python, Ruby, Node.js, PHP, or Perl, youre ready to get started.
         </p>
         <div class="row-fluid">
          <div class="span6">
           <div class="font-icon-block">
            <span aria-hidden="true" class="font-icon" data-icon="">
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Dive in and create your first example application with OpenShift
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="span6">
           <div class="font-icon-block">
            <span aria-hidden="true" class="font-icon" data-icon="">
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Modify the example with your own code and hot-deploy the changes
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="row-fluid">
          <div class="span6">
           <div class="font-icon-block">
            <span aria-hidden="true" class="font-icon" data-icon="">
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Add components such as a database, task scheduler, and monitoring service
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="span6">
           <div class="font-icon-block">
            <span aria-hidden="true" class="font-icon" data-icon="">
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Use external libraries and dependencies in your application
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="row-fluid">
          <div class="span6">
           <div class="font-icon-block">
            <span aria-hidden="true" class="font-icon" data-icon="">
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Delve into networking, persistent storage, and backup options
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="span6">
           <div class="font-icon-block">
            <span aria-hidden="true" class="font-icon" data-icon="">
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Explore ways to adapt your team processes to use OpenShift
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>

          <div class="span6">
           <div class="font-icon-block">

        <section id="ebook_authors">
         <h2>
          About the Authors
         </h2>
         <div class="row-fluid">
          <div class="span6">
           <img alt="Author - Steve Citron-Pousty" class="pull-left img-polaroid" src="https://www.openshift.com/sites/default/files/images/ebook_steve_citron-pousty.jpg"/>
           <h5>
            Steve Pousty
           </h5>
           <p>
            Steve Pousty is a Developer Advocate at Red Hat. Having earned a Ph.D. in Ecology, hes been mapping since the late 1980s and building applications for over 15 years. Steve has spoken widely on everything from developer evangelism to auto-scaling applications in the cloud.
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="span6">
           <img alt="Author - Katie Miller" class="pull-left img-polaroid" src="https://www.openshift.com/sites/default/files/images/ebook_katie_miller.jpg"/>
           <h5>
            Katie Miller
           </h5>
           <p>
            Katie Miller, an OpenShift Developer Advocate at Red Hat, is a polyglot programmer with a penchant for Haskell. A former newspaper journalist, Katie co-founded the Lambda Ladies group for women in functional programming.
           </p>
