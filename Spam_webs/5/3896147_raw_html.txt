             <h1 class="post-title">
              <a href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/cooking/2009/09/bacon-onion-cheddar-biscuits/" title="Bacon Onion Cheddar Biscuits">
               Bacon Onion Cheddar Biscuits
              </a>
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-tasks">
              <span class="author">
               Posted by Ree
              </span>
              <a class="comment-number-wrap" href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/cooking/2009/09/bacon-onion-cheddar-biscuits/#comments">
               <div class="comment-number">
                363
               </div>
               <span>
                Comments
               </span>
              </a>
              <a class="print-icon" href="#recipe-form-27627" id="print-recipe-link-27627">
               Print Recipe
              </a>
             </div>
            </header>
            <div class="post-padding">
             <p>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894838010/" title="TPW_0095 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
                <img alt="TPW_0095" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2554/3894838010_f2bf2b71df_o.jpg" width="630"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              These biscuits are hearty, sticky, substantial, and packed with all the goodness of life. You can serve them with eggs in the morning, tomato soup for lunch, or meatloaf for dinner. And theyre so rich and satisfying, one is
              <em>
               definitely
              </em>
              more than enough.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thats why I went ahead and ate two. Sorry.
             </p>
             <p>
              I originally set out to make a lighter muffin, but once I started figuring out ingredients, I realized the add-ins were too heavy and weighty for a lighter batter. So I went for more of a biscuitand I was glad I did.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3893981635/" title="TPW_0005">
                <img alt="TPW_0005" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2581/3893981635_60d4b7e85d.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Peppered bacon. Its the right thing to do.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3893984589/" title="TPW_0007">
                <img alt="TPW_0007" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3138/3893984589_dddcd6f79c.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Fry it up, baby. Oh, and I like to cut bacon in half before frying. It fits in the skillet better and the pieces fry more evenly, Ive found.
             </p>
             <p>
              Please listen to Pioneer Woman. I fry a lot of bacon. As in, the American Association of Cardiology sends me hate mail.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3893987273/" title="TPW_0009">
                <img alt="TPW_0009" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3512/3893987273_efa966e2da.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Fry the bacon till done but still a little chewynot burned to a crisp.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894778402/" title="TPW_0015">
                <img alt="TPW_0015" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2482/3894778402_e898d6c95e.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Remove the bacon from the skillet and set aside. When your husband (or wife, or child, or friend, or Basset Hound, whichever applies) enters the kitchen and looks at you with very sad eyes and begins to whimper, just get mad and say, 
              <em>
               OKAAAAAAAY! Take the bacon! Ill fry some moreI have nothing better to do
              </em>
              !
             </p>
             <p>
              Then fry some more. But only if you want to be just like me.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894773542/" title="TPW_0008">
                <img alt="TPW_0008" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3485/3894773542_3e1093df87.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Okay! Into a sifter (I use a fine mesh strainer) throw 2 cups all-purpose flour
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3893989327/" title="TPW_0013">
                <img alt="TPW_0013" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3511/3893989327_71558c3cf1.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              3/4 teaspoon salt
             </p>
             <p>
              I know thats an annoying amount, but 1/2 teaspoon isnt enoughand a full teaspoon is potentially too much.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3893991861/" title="TPW_0016">
                <img alt="TPW_0016" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2460/3893991861_87ddf5615b.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Next comes 1 heaping teaspoon baking powder. This probably translates to 1 1/2 teaspoons, but I like the organic, rustic feel of heaping.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3893992885/" title="TPW_0017">
                <img alt="TPW_0017" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2476/3893992885_66f4983ebc.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Sift this mixture into a bowl.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894781044/" title="TPW_0018">
                <img alt="TPW_0018" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2637/3894781044_cb5eab4bb0.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Then grab 1/4 cup shortening
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               &lt;
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894782570/" title="TPW_0019">
                <img alt="TPW_0019" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3478/3894782570_c3e962c526.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              And dot it all over the flour mixture.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3893996659/" title="TPW_0021">
                <img alt="TPW_0021" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2516/3893996659_17cd394cd0.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Use a pastry cutter to combine the ingredients
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3893998511/" title="TPW_0022">
                <img alt="TPW_0022" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2459/3893998511_10629d15ee.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Cut, cut, cutuntil all of the shortening is incorporated into the flour.
             </p>
             <p>
              Or all the flour is incorporated into the shortening. Which is it?
             </p>
             <p>
              We may never know the answers to such questions.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894000177/" title="TPW_0024">
                <img alt="TPW_0024" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2566/3894000177_de9d0a8bd7.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Now, in a separate bowl combine 10 tablespoons milk, 4 tablespoons vegetable oil, and 1 egg.
             </p>
             <p>
              If someone smarter than me wants to tell us all what 10 tablespoons is, Id appreciate it.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894022949/" title="TPW_0037">
                <img alt="TPW_0037" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2473/3894022949_f74bf77ae9.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Whisk it together with a fork.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894001263/" title="TPW_0025">
                <img alt="TPW_0025" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3484/3894001263_1f3b5d4809.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Measure a heaping (yes, I said it) cup of grated sharp cheddar cheese. Now, usually Im not a big fan of prepackaged grated cheese sold in bags. But when its used in bread recipes, I find that this cheese works a little better because its coated in a cornstarch substance that helps it stay put a bit in bread.
             </p>
             <p>
              At least thats what Im bankin on.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894002403/" title="TPW_0026">
                <img alt="TPW_0026" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2438/3894002403_66c1a92c7f.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Now, violently grab an onion.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894791224/" title="TPW_0028">
                <img alt="TPW_0028" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3496/3894791224_a8cb16a1cb.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Peel it, slice it, and dice it.
             </p>
             <p>
              (For once in my pitiful little life, I did not take photos of the onion dicing process. May we all take a moment to give thanks.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894794370/" title="TPW_0029">
                <img alt="TPW_0029" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3446/3894794370_0497a0bc1a.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Pour off almost all the bacon grease
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894795972/" title="TPW_0030">
                <img alt="TPW_0030" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3485/3894795972_c63555ce3b.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Then throw a good 1/2 to 3/4 cup of diced onion into the skillet.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894798524/" title="TPW_0031">
                <img alt="TPW_0031" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2671/3894798524_51de581602.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Stir it around and cook it a bit
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894800324/" title="TPW_0032">
                <img alt="TPW_0032" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2505/3894800324_cb198ebe6f.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Until the onions are nice and brown and colorful.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894806586/" title="TPW_0034">
                <img alt="TPW_0034" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3507/3894806586_7c15281d6f.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Set them aside to cool for a couple of minutes.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894805150/" title="TPW_0033">
                <img alt="TPW_0033" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2494/3894805150_cdac548d59.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Grab the bacon (cooled by now) and chop it up into small pieces
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894811366/" title="TPW_0038">
                <img alt="TPW_0038" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3520/3894811366_a53c58aa2e.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Time to mix it all up! Pour the egg/oil mixture into the sifted flour mixture.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894812944/" title="TPW_0040">
                <img alt="TPW_0040" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2575/3894812944_6d7aff802f.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894814170/" title="TPW_0041">
                <img alt="TPW_0041" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2640/3894814170_ee53600b07.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Gently mix this together until just barely combined.
             </p>
             <p>
              Note: This is not something you want to beat to heck. I folded this around for maybe ten strokes, pressing it and scraping it gently as I went.
              <strong>
               Itll be extremely thick and sticky.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894028729/" title="TPW_0042">
                <img alt="TPW_0042" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3486/3894028729_36c7b95251.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Dump in the cheddar cheese and the browned onions
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894031909/" title="TPW_0045">
                <img alt="TPW_0045" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2655/3894031909_9871137644.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              And of course, the bacon.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894034787/" title="TPW_0046">
                <img alt="TPW_0046" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2479/3894034787_68259194e9.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Again, fold this all together very gently until its all combined and very, very naughty looking.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894036185/" title="TPW_0048">
                <img alt="TPW_0048" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2481/3894036185_bd8778bd19.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Spoon batter evenly into (very greased) muffin tins.
             </p>
             <p>
              (I use that glorious Bakers Joy spray, and I dont care who knows it.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894037667/" title="TPW_0049">
                <img alt="TPW_0049" class="frame-img" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3489/3894037667_d72d98a9d7.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Then bake them in a 375 degree oven for 20 to 22 minutes
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894826822/" title="TPW_0084">
                <img alt="TPW_0084" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2574/3894826822_f1a9fff52a.jpg"/>
               </a>
              </span>
              Or until golden brown.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894828008/" title="TPW_0086">
                <img alt="TPW_0086" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2426/3894828008_8629d72011.jpg"/>
               </a>

              Oh, baby.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span class="horiz">
               <a class="flickr-image alignnone" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/3894041977/" title="TPW_0088">
                <img alt="TPW_0088" class="frame-img" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2667/3894041977_af14181bfb.jpg"/>

              <h4 class="recipe-sub-head">
               Preparation Instructions
              </h4>
              <div itemprop="instructions">
               <p>
                Sift together flour, baking powder, and salt. Using a pastry cutter, cut in shortening until all combined.
               </p>
               <p>
                Combine milk, oil, and egg in a separate bowl. Whisk together.
               </p>
               <p>
                Combine flour mixture, milk mixture, bacon, onions, and cheddar cheese in a large bowl. Stir gently until all combined.
               </p>
               <p>
                Spoon batter into greased muffin tins.  Bake for 20 to 22 minutes on 375 degrees until golden  Remove from pan and serve warm.
               </p>
