        <h1 itemprop="headline">
         The 19 Most Badass Spy Planes
        </h1>
        <div class="meta-wrap">
         <div class="meta">
          <a class="tracking_article_author" data-tracking-name="Attila Nagy" href="/author/attila-nagy/" rel="author">
           <span itemprop="author">
            Attila Nagy

         <div class="social tracking_article_social">
          <a class="btn btn-mini share anchor-link" data-target="#post-591057 .collapse" data-toggle="collapse" data-tracking-name="Share" href="#share" rel="nofollow" title="Share">

          <a class="btn btn-mini discuss anchor-link" data-tracking-name="Discuss" href="#comments" rel="nofollow" title="Discuss">

           Discuss
          </a>
          <a class="btn btn-mini bookmark disabled" data-post-id="591057" data-target="/wp-admin/admin-ajax.php?id=login_to_bookmark" data-toggle="modal" data-tracking-name="Bookmark" title="Bookmark this post">

        <div id="content_post" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="media_wrap image xlarge center">
          <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856ex7j56hbzjpg/original.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://img.gawkerassets.com']);" rel="modal">
           <img alt="" class="size_xlarge" src="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856ex7j56hbzjpg/ku-xlarge.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          There is little in the world that can match the simultaneous awesomeness and mystery of a spy plane. These are the paragon of modern innovation, yet largely pass invisible above us. They decide battles and wars, yet only a few lucky soldiers get to see them. Blah blah blah words. Lets look at some incredible machinery.
          <span id="more-591057">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Convair B-36 RB-36D Peacemaker
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856ex7j8gn11jpg/original.jpg" rel="lytebox">
           <div class="media_wrap image xlarge center">
            <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856ex7j8gn11jpg/original.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://img.gawkerassets.com']);" rel="modal">
             <img alt="" class="size_xlarge" src="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856ex7j8gn11jpg/ku-xlarge.jpg"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
          </a>
          <br/>
          The B-36 strategic bomber was not only the largest primary nuclear weapons delivery aircraft of the USAF in the Fifties, but the reconnaissance version, the RB-36D (below) was smart as well: It could carry 23 cameras AND a small darkroom where a photo technician developed the film.
          <br/>
          <small>
           Photo: US Air Force
          </small>
         </p>
         <h3>
          North American B-45 RB-45C Tornado
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cxqmpsf2fjpg/original.jpg" rel="lytebox">
           <div class="media_wrap image xlarge center">
            <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cxqmpsf2fjpg/original.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://img.gawkerassets.com']);" rel="modal">
             <img alt="" class="size_xlarge" src="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cxqmpsf2fjpg/ku-xlarge.jpg"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The RB-45C was the spying version of the B-45, the first jet-powered US bomber. This four-jet-engined beast carried 12 cameras and penetrated several enemy territories (North Korea, Eastern Europe) in the mid-1950s.
         </p>
         <p>
          <small>
           Photo: US Air Force
          </small>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Lockheed U-2
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cxukyu90kjpg/original.jpg" rel="lytebox">
           <div class="media_wrap image xlarge center">
            <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cxukyu90kjpg/original.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://img.gawkerassets.com']);" rel="modal">
             <img alt="" class="size_xlarge" src="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cxukyu90kjpg/ku-xlarge.jpg"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
          </a>
          <br/>
          The U-2 is a legend: It has delivered high-altitude, all-weather surveillance day and night for more than 50 years as the primary strategic reconnaissance aircraft of the USAF.
         </p>
         <p>
          <small>
           Photo:
           <a href="http://www.gizmodo.com.au/2012/08/the-most-elite-pilots-on-earth-looking-badass">
            US Air Force
           </a>
          </small>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Lockheed SR-71 Blackbird
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cy0hzpv4hjpg/original.jpg" rel="lytebox">
           <div class="media_wrap image xlarge center">
            <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cy0hzpv4hjpg/original.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://img.gawkerassets.com']);" rel="modal">
             <img alt="" class="size_xlarge" src="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cy0hzpv4hjpg/ku-xlarge.jpg"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
          </a>
          <br/>
          Any words we could write here would miserably fail to describe the Blackbirds
          <a href="http://www.gizmodo.com.au/2012/10/incredible-shot-of-sr-71-blackbirds-together">
           awesome badassery
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <small>
           Photo:
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/lockheedmartin/1038817987" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.flickr.com']);">
            Lockheed Martin
           </a>
          </small>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Grumman OV-1D / RV-1D Mohawk
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cy8dzt1dzjpg/original.jpg" rel="lytebox">
           <div class="media_wrap image xlarge center">
            <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cy8dzt1dzjpg/original.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://img.gawkerassets.com']);" rel="modal">
             <img alt="" class="size_xlarge" src="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cy8dzt1dzjpg/ku-xlarge.jpg"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
          </a>
          <br/>
          This observation aircraft was designed in the late 1950s as a dedicated reconnaissance plane, equipped with a Motorola APS-94F SLAR radar with a range of over 100km.
          <br/>
          <small>
           Photo:
           <a href="http://grin.hq.nasa.gov/ABSTRACTS/GPN-2000-001463.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://grin.hq.nasa.gov']);">
            J. David Clinton/NASA Glenn Research Center
           </a>
          </small>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Nasicornis Loening OL
         </h3>
         <p>
          Also known as the Loening Amphibian, the OL was a two-seat amphibious observation biplane built by Loening for the US Army and the Navy.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/1856cycc1o4kdjpg/original.jpg" rel="lytebox">

         <h3>
          Avro Vulcan B2(SR2)
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/18572pyq4bowtjpg/original.jpg" rel="lytebox">
           <div class="media_wrap image xlarge center">
            <a href="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/18572pyq4bowtjpg/original.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://img.gawkerassets.com']);" rel="modal">
             <img alt="" class="size_xlarge" src="http://img.gawkerassets.com/img/18572pyq4bowtjpg/ku-xlarge.jpg"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
          </a>
          <br/>
          I hope you wont mind me bringing up a childhood memory: In the Seventies and Eighties any Hungarian kid (or grownup) who was interested in science and technology used to watch this weekly show called
          <em>
           Delta
          </em>
          .
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jtlNdIA5SZA" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.youtube.com']);">
           This was the title sequence
          </a>
          . As a result, we all have the Avro Vulcan silhouette burned into our retinas forever. (The Avro Vulcan was a four-engine jet-powered delta wing strategic bomber, operated by the Royal Air Force (RAF) from 1956 until 1984. In the mid Seventies nine Vulcans were adapted for maritime radar reconnaissance operations, redesignated as B.2 (MRR).)
         </p>
