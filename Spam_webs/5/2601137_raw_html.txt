       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Benefits of Joining and Be Active on Blogging Communities
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-08-01T14:01:32+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          August 1, 2014
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://klinkk.com/blog/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Erik
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://klinkk.com/blog/blogging-communities/#comments">
           21 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         In this post you are going to read about the benefits of joining and, most of all, be active on blogging communities.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-280">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have at least some experience in internet marketing, then you should know that building relationships with influential bloggers and creating new connections within your niche is one of the best ways to expand your audience and
         <a href="http://klinkk.com/blog/premium-services/" target="_blank" title="Premium Services">
          promote your content
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Some great places to meet new people and get targeted traffic to your site are the blogging communities available online.
        </p>
        <p>
         When you join these places, you dont want to simply drop your URL and then leave.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is useless.
        </p>
        <p>
         It does not bring any results.
        </p>
        <p>
         You need to actually engage with other users, commenting on the blog posts and stories submitted, sharing your knowledge and helping spreading the content via social media.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can also use
         <a href="http://www.klinkk.com/groups.php" target="_blank">
          groups
         </a>
         , which is a feature that, for example at Klinkk, is notexploited at the moment.
        </p>
        <p>
         In this way, being a really active member, you will unleash the true potential of blogging communities.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Klinkk Until Today
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://klinkk.com/blog/submit-new-story/" target="_blank" title="How to Submit a New Story to Klinkk">
          Klinkk
         </a>
         was created just 9 months ago and has reached already pretty good stats:
        </p>
        <p>
          more than 1700 real users;
        </p>
        <p>
          almost 1000 comments;
        </p>
        <p>
          around 5000 stories submitted;
        </p>
        <p>
          high social signals;
        </p>
        <p>
          great rankings (check
         <a href="http://www.alexa.com/siteinfo/klinkk.com" target="_blank">
          Klinkk Alexa rank here
         </a>
         ).
        </p>
        <p>
         The traffic data are pretty encouraging : more than half is coming from search engines and the site already get somehundreds of visitors DAILY.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is the graphic of Klinkk traffic trend from the date it was founded (October 27th, 2013) untilJuly 29th, 2014.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://klinkk.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Klinkk-traffic-stats-since-November-2013-until-July-30th-2014.png">
          <img alt="Klinkk traffic stats since November 2013 until July 30th 2014 - screenshot" class="alignnone wp-image-283 size-large" height="84" src="http://klinkk.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Klinkk-traffic-stats-since-November-2013-until-July-30th-2014-1024x149.png" width="584"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is due to the many bloggers who have featured Klinkk in their blog posts, helping to increase the buzz.
        </p>
        <p>
         So Thank You!
         <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://klinkk.com/blog/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Interview with Some Experienced Bloggers
        </h2>
        <p>
         Ive made a couple of questions to some experienced bloggers, via MyBlogU.
        </p>
        <p>
         The interview title : Your Opinion on Blogging Communities.
        </p>
        <p>
         Here below you can read the answers.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Q. Have you ever joined any Blogging Community? Whats your experience?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a name="mbu-q-90">
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A.
          <a href="http://timemoneyproblem.com">
           Philtrate
          </a>
          <i>
           (. .)
          </i>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.gravatar.com/avatar/d87531b92da81586b4b99abeacb5f13c?s=100&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" style="float: right; margin: 5px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I have been a moderator on MyBlogGuest.com and MyBlogU.com since the early days, I think I started with MBG 4 years ago. I watched MBG grow, and I watched as members deserted it in the aftermath of Googles illogical smack.
        </p>
        <p>
         My experience of the community is pleasant and I enjoy my work. The only downside has been when SEO people have tried to automate their membership, outsourcing to poorly paid employees who do not have the networking authorisation or skills that membership requires.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A.
          <a href="http://www.jacobcurtis.com">
           JacobkCurtis
          </a>
         </strong>
         <i>
          (Digital Marketing Strategist)
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.gravatar.com/avatar/b0ed970d9bda88ebddd9de3943deeb83?s=100&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" style="float: right; margin: 5px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyone who has done their fair share of blogging research is familiar with blogging communities and the benefits of joining them. The first blogging community I joined was Triberr, a platform where like-minded bloggers read and share eachothers content. My positive experience was only due to my participation (sharing my own valuable social media tutorials from JacobCurtis.com), as well as engaging and sharing other bloggers content through my connected Twitter handle @JacobkCurtis. Its important to understand that just by registering your account in a blogging community will not guarentee game changing results, it takes being active and supporting other members. When joining a blogging community, think community.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A.
          <a href="http://www.marcomland.com">
           Ali Lawrence
          </a>
         </strong>
         <i>
          (Content Specialist and Blogger)
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5564/14543018717_3eb19b6f22_o.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 5px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive joined a few blogging communities, including Better Blogger Network and Blogher. While I dont spend a lot of time actually on the communnity site, I do use my association to build connections with other bloggers, which leads to guest post swaps and learning opportunities to become a better blogger.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A.
          <a href="http://hirebloggers.com/">
           AnnaFox
          </a>
         </strong>
         <i>
          (Blogger)
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://myblogu.com/forum//img/avatars/57247.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 5px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I am member of MyBlogGuest. I used to be an active member at 20sb.net as well as a few smaller mommy blogging communities I love meeting new people in the community; I love building better relationships with them, help them and get helped. I think the ability to join a community and find friends is one of the best things that the Internet has introduced into our lives. I also cannot imagine how I would promote my blog without the help of those micro-communities!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Q. In your opinion, what are the benefits of joining and be active on the most popular blogging communities?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a name="mbu-q-91">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A.
          <a href="http://timemoneyproblem.com">
           Philtrate
          </a>
         </strong>
         <i>
          (. .)
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.gravatar.com/avatar/d87531b92da81586b4b99abeacb5f13c?s=100&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" style="float: right; margin: 5px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I have made friends and earned money from my active blogging community participation. I believe in paying it forward and helping people whenever I can  I then find that people are willing to help me when I need it. There are many bloggers I have spoken with who would welcome me into their homes if I was ever in the vicinity.
        </p>
        <p>
         The main measurable benefit is that obtained by guest posting on other community members blogs. Readers follow my bio links and have left comments on my own blog. This kind of benefit is everlasting and reduces a bloggers dependence on search engin e traffic that could disappear with tomorrows algo change.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A.
          <a href="http://www.jacobcurtis.com">
           JacobkCurtis
          </a>
         </strong>
         <i>
          (Digital Marketing Strategist)
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://www.gravatar.com/avatar/b0ed970d9bda88ebddd9de3943deeb83?s=100&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" style="float: right; margin: 5px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Being active is the most important practice of any blogging community. Remember, the main purpose and goal of these blogging communities is to get your content shared and seen by new people, while also providing a hub of content that you can share to your own networks. However, I encourage bloggers to use these communities not only for their intended purpose, but more importantly as a valuable introduction to other industry bloggers. Once introduced through the community, proactively engaging with the friends you make across other social platforms, comment on their blogs, propose a guest blog or collaborate on a project together. By doing so, youll not only reap the benefits on being active in a blogging community, but establish relationships that will open even more opportunities in the future.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A.
          <a href="http://www.marcomland.com">
           Ali Lawrence
          </a>
         </strong>
         <i>
          (Content Specialist and Blogger)
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5564/14543018717_3eb19b6f22_o.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 5px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Being a part of a blogging community opens the door to so many opportunities such as guest posts, collaborating with other bloggers to build your readership, and a chance to learn from seasoned bloggers. Blogging communities makes it easy for companies to locate a blog to partner for ambassador programs and product reviews.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A.
          <a href="http://hirebloggers.com/">
           AnnaFox
          </a>
         </strong>
         <i>
          (Blogger)
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="http://myblogu.com/forum//img/avatars/57247.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 5px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The benefits may vary from community to community but generally here are those that are important for me:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Building close relationships
          </strong>
          with people who come to my blog, comment, share on social media, etc  all those little things that motivate me to keep blogging!
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Getting inspired and sharing knowledge
          </strong>
          : Everything Ive ever learned about blogging was from the forums and communities I participated in!
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Discovering new opportunities
          </strong>
          : Bloggers always share new promo or make-money opportunities, invite each other to participate in contests, etc
         </li>
        </ul>
