          <span class="st_email_vcount" st_title="How to Antique and Age Wood Instantly for a Weathered Look" st_url="http://www.lilblueboo.com/2014/04/how-to-antique-and-age-wood-instantly-for-a-weathered-look.html">
          </span>
          <span class="st_facebook_vcount" st_title="How to Antique and Age Wood Instantly for a Weathered Look" st_url="http://www.lilblueboo.com/2014/04/how-to-antique-and-age-wood-instantly-for-a-weathered-look.html">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/antiquewoodtutorial.jpg">
           <img alt="How to Antique and age wood instantly with items you p (weathering wood with vinegar and steel wool)" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47880" height="964" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/antiquewoodtutorial.jpg" title="How to Antique and age wood instantly with items you (weathering wood with vinegar and steel wool)" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          This is one of my favorite tricks that I use with home decor.a simple solution of white vinegar and steel wool. You can use it to age brand new wood to varying degrees depending on how the solution ages.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           Supplies:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Wood
          <br/>
          White Distilled Vinegar
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001JYVDSE/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001JYVDSE&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=liblbo-20" target="_blank">
           <em>
            <strong>
             0000 Steel Wool*
            </strong>
           </em>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          For the sign:
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00DXYDN3G/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00DXYDN3G&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=liblbo-20" target="_blank">
           <em>
            <strong>
             8 Oil Board Stencils
            </strong>
           </em>
          </a>
          <br/>
          Black spray paint
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <em>
           *I prefer the
          </em>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001JYVDSE/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B001JYVDSE&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=liblbo-20" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            fine graded
           </strong>
          </a>
          <em>
           because it disintegrates faster.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood14.jpg">
           <img alt="Aging Wood: Vinegar &amp; Steel Wool Solution (steel and vinegar wood stain directions) #diy #tutorial #distressed #antique" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47872" height="649" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood14.jpg" title="Aging Wood: Vinegar &amp; Steel Wool Solution (steel and vinegar wood stain directions) #diy #tutorial #distressed #antique" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          To make the mixture you pour vinegar into a bowl or jar and add steel wool (without the soap kind!):
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/steelwoodl.jpg">
           <img alt="How to faux paint barn wood or antique wood with Vinegar &amp; Steel Wool Solution  #diy #tutorial #distressed #antique" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47875" height="342" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/steelwoodl.jpg" title="How to faux paint barn wood or antique wood with Vinegar &amp; Steel Wool Solution  #diy #tutorial #distressed #antique" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The trick is figuring out how long to age it. A solution left to age just 15 minutes will produce different effect than a solution left aging for hours, months or years.
         </p>
         <p>
          Here is a solution left aging for 15 minutes where the vinegar has just started to disintegrate the steel wool. One or two coats of the mixture leaves just a light gray barn-wood like finish:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/antiquewoodfinish1.jpg">
           <img alt="Aging and Antiquing Wood (Faux Barn Wood) - 15 minute solution #diy #vinegar #barnwood " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47879" height="765" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/antiquewoodfinish1.jpg" title="Aging and Antiquing Wood (Faux Barn Wood) - 15 minute solution #diy #vinegar #barnwood " width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Leaving the solution to age a few more hours will produce a darker gray finish:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/antiquewoodfinish3.jpg">
           <img alt="Leaving the solution to age a few more hours will produce a darker gray finish (steel and vinegar wood stain directions)" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47878" height="763" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/antiquewoodfinish3.jpg" title="Leaving the solution to age a few more hours will produce a darker gray finish (steel and vinegar wood stain directions)" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I keep varying solutions in jars because if you leave it long enough the steel wool disintegrates completely and the mixture is allowed to produce rust, adding a reddish brown tone.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/antiquewoodfinish2.jpg">
           <img alt="Aging Wood: Vinegar &amp; Steel Wool Solution  (if you leave it long enough the steel wool disintegrates completely and the mixture is allowed to produce rust, adding a reddish brown tone)" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47877" height="763" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/antiquewoodfinish2.jpg" title="Aging Wood: Vinegar &amp; Steel Wool Solution  (if you leave it long enough the steel wool disintegrates completely and the mixture is allowed to produce rust, adding a reddish brown tone) " width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres a ten minute sign I made using a pre-cut piece of wood from the hardware store and
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00DXYDN3G/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00DXYDN3G&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=liblbo-20" target="_blank">
           <em>
            <strong>
             8 Oil Board Stencils:
            </strong>
           </em>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood01.jpg">
           <img alt='Making a sign with pre-cut piece of wood from the hardware store and 8" Oil Board Stencils:' class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47865" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood01.jpg" title='Making a sign with pre-cut piece of wood from the hardware store and 8" Oil Board Stencils:' width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          I spaced the letters out and taped them into place together:
          <span id="more-47846">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood02.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47870" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood02.jpg" title="AntiqueWood02" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          A few sprays of black matte spray paint did the trick:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood04.jpg">
           <img alt="How to make a 10 minute antique wood sign using supplies you probably already have around the house (as long as you have stencils!)" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47861" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood04.jpg" title="How to make a 10 minute antique wood sign using supplies you probably already have around the house (as long as you have stencils!)  " width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          I painted a few coats of my vinegar/steel wool mixture on top:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood06.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47863" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood06.jpg" title="AntiqueWood06" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          I let the solution pool in a few places for different degrees of aging:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood07.jpg">
           <img alt="Antique Wood Sign: Let the solution pool in a few places for different degrees of aging." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47864" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood07.jpg" title="Antique Wood Sign: Let the solution pool in a few places for different degrees of aging. " width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          A 10-minute DIY antique / aged Antiques sign:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood11.jpg">
           <img alt='A 10-minute DIY antique / aged "Antiques" sign using vinegar and steel wool. ' class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47868" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood11.jpg" title='A 10-minute DIY antique / aged "Antiques" sign using vinegar and steel wool.  ' width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I made a quick border for this sign using painters tape:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood08.jpg">
           <img alt="Using painters tape to create a painted border" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47869" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood08.jpg" title="Using painters tape to create a painted border" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I just used a razor blade to cut away any overlapped areas:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood09.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47866" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood09.jpg" title="AntiqueWood09" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          And masked off the letters:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood10.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47871" height="341" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood10.jpg" title="AntiqueWood10" width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          You can see that the Coffee sign is much darker. I used solution from the bottom of the jar where the rust sludge had settled. It produces a darker-reddish finish vs. the sign on the right with the lighter tone:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood1.jpg">
           <img alt="Aging Wood with Vinegar and Steel Wool: I used solution from the bottom of the jar where the rust sludge had settled.  It produces a darker-reddish finish vs. the sign on the right with the lighter tone.  " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47853" height="768" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood1.jpg" title="Aging Wood with Vinegar and Steel Wool: I used solution from the bottom of the jar where the rust sludge had settled.  It produces a darker-reddish finish vs. the sign on the right with the lighter tone.   " width="512"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood7.jpg">
           <img alt="DIY antique and distressed sign tutorial via lilblueboo.com " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-47852" height="768" src="http://www.lilblueboo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AntiqueWood7.jpg" title="DIY antique and distressed sign tutorial via lilblueboo.com " width="512"/>
