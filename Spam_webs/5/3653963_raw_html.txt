        <p>
         <img alt="Holiday Beer" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/holiday-beer.jpg" style="width:100%;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The holiday season is a time for celebrating the year with good food and good beer. December is a particularly great month for beer enthusiasts because it inspires brewers to take advantage of the great late-season flavors. If youre looking to get the right beer for your holiday party or simply want a good
         <a href="http://learn.kegerator.com/christmas-winter-spiced-beer/">
          winter ale
         </a>
         for those long nights by the fire, here are 10 of my favorite beers that are guaranteed to give you a bit of holiday spirit.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Tregs  Mad Elf Ale
        </h3>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Troegs The Mad Holiday Elf Ale" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/the-mad-elf.png" title="Troegs The Mad Holiday Elf Ale"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Pennsylvania is known for its festive holiday spirit, and local brewers take full advantage. With a ruby red color and cheerful label, Mad Elf Ale has plenty of character and a jolly flavor. Heavy on the chocolate malts with delicious notes of cherries, honey and spices, Mad Elf Ale is perfect to sip on during those cold winter nights.
        </p>
        <p>
         Serve around 53F in your favorite
         <a href="http://learn.kegerator.com/beer-glasses/">
          beer glass
         </a>
         . However, it carries a decent amount of alcohol, so stick to one or two when youre around the in-laws.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 11.0%
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: 15
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="http://www.troegs.com/our_brews/mad_elf_ale.aspx" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <h3>
         2. Sierra Nevada Celebration Ale
        </h3>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Sierra Nevada Celebration Ale" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/sierra-nevada-celebration-ale.jpg" title="Sierra Nevada Celebration Ale"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         My personal favorite,
         <a href="http://learn.kegerator.com/sierra-nevada-celebration-ale-2014/">
          Celebration Ale
         </a>
         is a winter seasonal that has been around since the early 1980s. And its been providing winter-haters with some much-needed comfort ever since.
        </p>
        <p>
         Because its a
         <a href="http://learn.kegerator.com/american-ipa/">
          fresh hop American IPA
         </a>
         , this is one of the most bitter and intense winter beers youll find. But dont let that scare you away. While it may be high in IBUs, its aroma and flavor consists of a piney citrus that is sure to please.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 6.8%
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: 65
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="http://www.sierranevada.com/beer/seasonal/celebration-ale" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Victory Winter Cheers" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/victory-winter-cheers.png" title="Victory Winter Cheers"/>
        </div>
        <h3>
         3. Victory Winter Cheers
        </h3>
        <p>
         Another unconventional winter beer, Victory brews this wheat ale to help lighten your spirits when the weather turns cold. The best way to describe this beer, is a hefeweizen with a spicy winter twist. Youll notice a strong fruity flavor with subtle hints of citrus, banana, and clove.
        </p>
        <p>
         Compared to other holiday ales, Winter Cheers is considerably light, refreshing and easy to drink. Distribution runs throughout the holiday season, so theres plenty of time to take advantage.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 6.7%
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: NA
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="http://www.victorybeer.com/beers/winter-cheers/" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <h3>
         4. Southern Tier 2xMas
        </h3>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Southern Tier 2xMas" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/southern-tier-2xmas.jpg" title="Southern Tier 2xMas"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         For those who fancy strong spice and anything fig, this is the holiday ale for you. A double-spiced ale thats perfect for any Christmas party, 2xMas was originally inspired by a Scandinavian Glogg party and embodies all the reasons why the Nordic country is the best place for the holidays.
        </p>
        <p>
         Best served around 43F, 2xMas medium-bodied and rich in flavor. In addition to figs, its brewed with cardamom, orange peel, clove, ginger root, and cinnamon, giving it the traditional winter spice youve come to expect this time of year.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 8.0%
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: 32
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="http://www.stbcbeer.com/seasonals/seasonal-standard/2xmas-beer-page/" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <h3>
         5. Highland Cold Mountain Winter Ale
        </h3>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Highland Cold Mountain Winter Ale" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/cold-mountain-winter-ale.jpg" title="Highland Cold Mountain Winter Ale"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         This beer is a fantastic choice for casual or extravagant Christmas get-togethers. The name alone even sounds cold enough for the holidays. Better even still, it has a flavor to match.
        </p>
        <p>
         With a malty body that is lightly hopped, Cold Mountain winter ale features an eclectic mix of spices like vanilla, hazelnut, caramel and other seasonal favorites. The perfect balance to keep you warm on a chilly night.
        </p>
        <p>
         Unfortunately, this Cold Mountain Ale is brewed in limited batches and only distributed in select states. If you do have a chance to buy it, get it quick because it will sell out before you know it.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 5.2%
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: 28
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="http://www.highlandbrewing.com/craft-brews/style/cold-mountain-winter-ale" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Samuel Adams Winter Lager" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/sam-adams-winter-lager.png" title="Samuel Adams Winter Lager"/>
        </div>
        <h3>
         6. Samuel Adams Winter Lager
        </h3>
        <p>
         For loyal fans of American craft beers, Christmas just isnt the same without this classic lager.
        </p>
        <p>
         Winter Lager has a rich, bold flavor that gives off just the right amount of your favorite holiday spices. Its available from November to December and is a good one to have around for just about any winter occasion.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 5.6%
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: 22
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="https://www.samueladams.com/craft-beers/winter-lager" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="High Point Ramstein Winter Wheat" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/ramstein-winter-wheat.jpg" title="High Point Ramstein Winter Wheat"/>
        </div>
        <h3>
         7. High Point Ramstein Winter Wheat
        </h3>
        <p>
         Christmas brings out High Point Brewings strongest beer. Not only is this a great full-body choice for cold nights and end-of-year reflection, but its smooth finish and complex character makes it one of the best Weiss beers out there.
        </p>
        <p>
         It starts with a rich, creamy head followed by malt, apple, and spicy flavors. It tends to leave a smooth caramel finish, and you may just be surprised to learn that this winter beer contains as much alcohol as it does.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 9.5%
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: 20
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="http://www.ramsteinbeer.com/pages/beers/beers_seasonals.html" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Samuel Smith Winter Welcome" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/samuel-smiths-winter-welcome.png" title="Samuel Smith Winter Welcome"/>
        </div>
        <h3>
         8. Samuel Smith Winter Welcome
        </h3>
        <p>
         Winter Welcome hails from Yorkshire, England, and its a good choice for those who favor true English taste. The light amber color smells slightly floral, yet has a rich caramel flavor. This is a good one to pair with those exotic dishes that you only put out during the holiday season roast goose, lamb, fruit cake, or smoked turkey.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 6.0%
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: 32
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="#" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <h3>
         9. Anchor Merry Christmas and Happy New Year
        </h3>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Anchor Merry Christmas and Happy New Year" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/anchor-merry-christmas.png" title="Anchor Merry Christmas and Happy New Year"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Even if you dont end up liking the taste of this beer, the name alone is reason enough to keep it on hand for the holidays.
        </p>
        <p>
         Anchors Merry Christmas And Happy New Year even has a new label and brand new recipe for the upcoming season. It tends to be sweet and spicy but generally light. Anchor doesnt divulge much in terms of ingredients, but most tasters end up satisfied with each new batch.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: variable
         </li>
         <li>
          IBU: variable
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="http://www.anchorbrewing.com/beer/christmas_ale" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <hr class="faq"/>
        <h3>
         10. Avery Old Jubilation Ale
        </h3>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Avery Old Jubilation Ale" src="http://learn.kegerator.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/avery-old-jubilation-ale.png" title="Avery Old Jubilation Ale"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         For a look, taste, and finish that wish you a happy holiday, you cant go wrong with this English ale. A classic example of the
         <a href="http://learn.kegerator.com/know-beer-styles-old-ale/">
          Old Ale style
         </a>
         , this seasonal from Avery Brewing should be a holiday tradition in every household.
        </p>
        <p>
         Spice-haters can rejoice, for this one offers only malty flavors with a hint of hazelnut. Old Jubilation pairs well with the majority of holiday meals, including like roast duck, rack of lamb and all styles of chicken.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          ABV: 8.3%
         </li>
         <li>
          Body: 45
         </li>
         <li>
          Availability: Winter Seasonal
         </li>
         <li>
          More Info:
          <a href="http://averybrewing.com/our-ales/old-jubilation-ale/" target="_blank">
           Official Page
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         The holiday season comes once a year, so make your next one count. Grab of few of these festive brews to spice up all of your winter events.
        </p>
