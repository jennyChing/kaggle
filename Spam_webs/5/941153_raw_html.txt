               <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
                <a href="http://ttwcreative.blogspot.com/2015/04/a-new-free-cowl-pattern.html">
                 A New, Free Cowl Pattern
                </a>
               </h1>
               <div class="postmeta">
                <span class="author">
                 Posted by faiz abdulmalik
                </span>
                <span class="clock">
                 Posted on 22.40
                </span>
               </div>
               <div class="post-header">
                <div class="post-header-line-1">
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6861499425703083775">
                <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                 A New, Free Cowl Pattern
                </h3>
                <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                 <span style="font-size: x-small;">
                  <span style="font-weight: normal;">
                   Found on archive.org
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </h3>
                <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298625567812344754" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYh_4W9B27I/AAAAAAAAAb8/pKCmMSnqA6k/s400/34154593.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 313px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                <span class="Apple-style-span">
                 <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: italic;">
                  <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: xx-small;">
                   Photo from Red Eye/Chicago Tribune
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </span>
                <br/>
                <div>
                 <span class="Apple-style-span">
                  <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: 17px;">
                   <span class="Apple-style-span">
                    Update:
                   </span>
                   <i>
                    <span class="Apple-style-span">
                    </span>
                   </i>
                   <i>
                    <span class="Apple-style-span">
                     If you're new here, welcome! I have a great pattern giveaway at
                    </span>
                    <span class="Apple-style-span">
                     this post
                    </span>
                    <span class="Apple-style-span">
                     ! Oh, and good luck with your cowl. I'd love a comment or photo of yours!
                    </span>
                   </i>
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 The
 average temperature last month in Chicago was a whopping 15 degrees. 
So, in homage to all who trudge in the wind, snow and ice this winter I 
am creating a
                 <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: italic;">
                  Chicago Winter
                 </span>
                 series of warm knitted accessories.  The first in this series is......
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: medium;">
                  <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
                   My Kind of Cowl
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBu7jPe-I/AAAAAAAAAbE/BhSZ2hxg2gg/s1600-h/IMG_2075.JPG">
                  <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298416498628328418" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBu7jPe-I/AAAAAAAAAbE/BhSZ2hxg2gg/s400/IMG_2075.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                 </a>
                 <div style="text-align: center;">
                  Materials:
                 </div>
                 <div style="text-align: center;">
                  1 skein Cascade Magnum (9341)
                 </div>
                 <div style="text-align: center;">
                  Thin waste yarn in contrasting color, preferably cotton
                 </div>
                 <div style="text-align: center;">
                  US 17 Needle
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfCv22ldPI/AAAAAAAAAb0/2E61i_fP4eQ/s1600-h/IMG_2078.JPG">
                 <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298417614058779890" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfCv22ldPI/AAAAAAAAAb0/2E61i_fP4eQ/s400/IMG_2078.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                </a>
                <div style="text-align: left;">
                 <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: 18px;">
                  <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
                   <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: medium;">
                    1
                   </span>
                  </span>
                  :
                 </span>
                 Provisionally cast on 26 stitches.  See this tutorial from Knit and Tonic.
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: left;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: left;">
                 <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: 18px; font-weight: bold;">
                  2
                 </span>
                 :  Knit in garter stitch until you have 44 ridges
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: left;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: left;">
                 <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
                  <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-size: medium;">
                   3:
                  </span>
                 </span>
                 Graft the two edges together.  This is tricky.  Magnum is soft and 
wonderful but it sticks to itself like nobody's business when you try to
 graft.  I got the best tip from Karen at Knit 1:
  first graft the piece with contrasting thin cotton waste yarn.  That 
way, when you do your "official" graft all you have to do is sew it 
together following the path of the contrasting-colored stitches.
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: left;">
                 The result will be a giant tube that looks like this:
                </div>
                <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfCvzSc0xI/AAAAAAAAAbs/hj4WJVfJ368/s1600-h/IMG_2084.JPG">
                 <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298417613101912850" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfCvzSc0xI/AAAAAAAAAbs/hj4WJVfJ368/s400/IMG_2084.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                </a>
                Wear your My Kind of Town Cowl under your jacket like this:
                <br/>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfCvoV09ZI/AAAAAAAAAbk/DDxiz4fI53Y/s1600-h/IMG_2077.JPG">
                  <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298417610163287442" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfCvoV09ZI/AAAAAAAAAbk/DDxiz4fI53Y/s400/IMG_2077.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                 </a>
                 <br/>
                 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfCvfKbCPI/AAAAAAAAAbc/3Tnb2yJEXr4/s1600-h/IMG_2076.JPG">
                  <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298417607699532018" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfCvfKbCPI/AAAAAAAAAbc/3Tnb2yJEXr4/s400/IMG_2076.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                 </a>
                 You can squish it up to your face while walking and then let it down in a pretty cowl when you get on the hot train or bus.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 Wear it like this
                 <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: italic;">
                  <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: bold;">
                   over
                  </span>
                 </span>
                 your coat for unexpected warmth.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBvEnPNmI/AAAAAAAAAbM/2cMSnQJEglI/s1600-h/IMG_2080.JPG">
                  <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298416501061006946" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBvEnPNmI/AAAAAAAAAbM/2cMSnQJEglI/s400/IMG_2080.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                 </a>
                 Wear it at home when watching TV.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBui67mqI/AAAAAAAAAa8/6Rs0lA1z2RQ/s1600-h/IMG_2073.JPG">
                  <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298416492016802466" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBui67mqI/AAAAAAAAAa8/6Rs0lA1z2RQ/s400/IMG_2073.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                 </a>
                 Or in bed while reading a book.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBuod4pTI/AAAAAAAAAa0/QJp4K0Oku6U/s1600-h/IMG_2072.JPG">
                  <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298416493505586482" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBuod4pTI/AAAAAAAAAa0/QJp4K0Oku6U/s400/IMG_2072.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                 </a>
                 Even wear it as an accessory over a gray cashmere sweater.  Accompany outfit with jeans or skirt and you will be warm and cozy.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBue1ZosI/AAAAAAAAAas/QNuoA-jZCmI/s1600-h/IMG_2070.JPG">
                  <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5298416490919862978" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524000457im_/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_jrpoGghxO9I/SYfBue1ZosI/AAAAAAAAAas/QNuoA-jZCmI/s400/IMG_2070.JPG" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 300px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 400px;"/>
                 </a>
                 There will be more patterns coming in the Chicago Winter series.  Stay tuned!
                </div>
