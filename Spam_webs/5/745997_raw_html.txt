      <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
       We bring to you our exclusive preview of the Champions League Match ofArsenal vs Bayern Munich.
      </h5>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       After snapping their winless streak in two games and most of all avenging their humiliation a 5-1 defeat at the hands of
       <a href="../sport/team/liverpool-fc">
        Liverpool
       </a>
       at Anfield two weeks ago,
       <a href="../sport/team/arsenal">
        Arsenal
       </a>
       will now take on defending
       <a href="../sport/event/uefa-champions-league/football">
        Champions League
       </a>
       holders
       <a href="../sport/team/bayern-munich">
        Bayern Munich
       </a>
       in the knockout stages for a place in Europes elite club competition. Its no doubt that this will be a lip smacking, jaw dropping and a gut wrenching affair of a repeat of last years clash over two legs. It will be a Arsene Wenger against Pep Guardiola battle in the sidelines which no doubt will be enthralling to watch as the two brilliant managers will raise raise the stakes in a fight to the finish.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Bayern Munich won the Champions League last season and they are running away with the
       <a href="../sport/event/bundesliga/football">
        Bundesliga
       </a>
       title this season. They are undefeated in domestic league play and they have scored the most goals while also conceding the fewest, a feat enviable in the whole of Europes elite. Can the Gunners score on a Bayern defense that has conceded only nine goals in 21 league matches?.Will the Bavarians decimate Arsenal's artillery or will Wenger's Gunners gun Guardiola's goons?
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Tactics and what to expect from the match:
       </strong>
       <a href="../sport/team/arsenal">
        Arsenal
       </a>
       was down 2-0 after 21 minutes in last season's home loss to Bayern and the Gunners must get off to a good start at the glittering Emirates on Wednesday night. The Gunners may have to rely on their defence more in this huge encounter to keep the dominating Bayern side quiet and look to combat them by their very own Oxlade Chamberlain. The twenty year old had a sublime match, scoring the opening goal and then setting up Arsenal's second for
       <a href="../sport/player/lukas-podolski/">
        Lukas Podolski
       </a>
       blistering his way past
       <a href="../sport/player/daniel-agger/">
        Daniel Agger
       </a>
       in
       <a href="../sport/team/liverpool-fc">
        Liverpool
       </a>
       s central defence as if the Dane wasnt there.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Come Wednesday too, Wenger should look to field Chamberlain on the flanks using him as a runner looking to throw out
       <a href="../sport/player/rafinha/">
        Rafinha
       </a>
       and
       <a href="../sport/player/david-alaba/">
        David Alaba
       </a>
       out of positions. Remember both of Bayerns full backs leave back spaces which at the customary end just tilt the score line by the thinnest of margins. Guardiols however will be looking to play his most intelligent player
       <a href="../sport/player/phillip-lahm/">
        Phillip Lahm
       </a>
       in midfield and look to use
       <a href="../sport/player/javi-martinez/">
        Javi Martinez
       </a>
       to disrupt Arsenals fluidity in the middle of the park. It will be interesting to see how
       <a href="../sport/team/arsenal">
        Arsenal
       </a>
       's 4-2-3-1 copes up against Bayern 's flexible and always interchanging 4-3-3, make no mistake however it will be gut wrenching to witness.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Sportzwikis suggestions:
       </strong>
       If I were Wenger I would play a 4-2-2-2 sacrificing a striker to overload Bayerns engine in the middle of the park. Wenger can look to play a false nine, beating Pep at his own game by lining Cazorla and Chamberlain against Rafinha and Alaba, making use of the pace of his younger players.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Predictions:
       </strong>
       Take my words, it will be a brilliant one to watch as a neutral as two top teams go against one another. Predicting correct score lines are tough but given Guardiolas tactical nous, I expect Bayern to win by 3-1.
      </p>
