       <div class="article-body-content" data-target-blank="" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         <iframe frameborder="0" height="423" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/CpKDteQ3FIQ" width="752">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         California native John Glaude feels greatand he should. In the last two years, hes lost 160 pounds through a combination of diet and exercise. Now a healthy 200 pounds, Glaude feels more energetic and self-confident, so long as he keeps his shirt on. Hes chronicled his weight-loss journey in a series of YouTube videos on his cleverly named channel,
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCzo4OXE8JxogJHWJ2SypiNg">
          Obese to Beast
         </a>
         . Glaudes videos discuss everything from
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCAEsRITb74&amp;list=UUzo4OXE8JxogJHWJ2SypiNg">
          low-calorie Starbucks options
         </a>
         to hitting (and overcoming) a
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_bIxXXYV19g&amp;list=UUzo4OXE8JxogJHWJ2SypiNg">
          weight-loss plateau
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <div class="ym" data-yieldmo="ym_966591849689717803" data-yieldmo-tablet="ym_976628814724144286" id="ym_966591849689717803">
        </div>
        <p>
         But there was one topic he was too nervous to discuss until this week: loose skin. In a society that scorns even the slightest bit of cellulite, exposing your transformed bodyextra skin and allis a hugely courageous move. Even more impressive than Glaudes courage is the message hes spreading: Hes proud of the way he looks.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im happy with where Ive come and where I am now. Im not walking around the beach with my shirt off, but this is who I am, and Im proud of it.
        </p>
