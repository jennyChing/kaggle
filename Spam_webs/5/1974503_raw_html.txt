        <h3>
         <a href="http://www.funwithjubby.com/ceremony/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Our Ceremony">
          Our Ceremony
         </a>
        </h3>
        <div class="date fl">
         March 17, 2010
        </div>
        <div class="hl">
        </div>
        <div class="entry">
         <p>
          Now that Ive exhausted probably every design element we poured our blood, sweat and tears into, Id like to celebrate my absolute favorite part of the entire day: Our ceremony.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.hotelcongress.com/club/">
           <img alt="clubcongress" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-525" height="299" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/clubcongress.jpg" title="clubcongress" width="448"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          We had ZERO decorations for the ceremony. No flowers, aisle runners, shepherdshooks at the end of rows, etc. But we also didnt need them, we got married on stage at one of our favorite rock clubs, the
          <a href="http://www.hotelcongress.com/club">
           Club Congress
          </a>
          ! It was one of our motivations to get married at the
          <a href="http://www.hotelcongress.com">
           Hotel Congress
          </a>
          , and it also meant our waiting area wasnt something as ordinary as a church lobby or side room, it was a
          <a href="http://www.hotelcongress.com/club/the-tap-room/">
           bar
          </a>
          !
         </p>
         <p>
          Although we did but effort into piecing together our readings and elements of the ceremony, the time we spent was completely dwarfed by amount of time spent on the reception. But it had so many lovely moments that left people laughing, crying, laughing again, cheering and then crying again.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="TMoney" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-527" height="299" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/TMoney1.jpg" title="TMoney" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          It all started with our friend Trent working the crowd with a stand-up routine.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="aislewalk" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-528" height="276" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/aislewalk.jpg" title="aislewalk" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Which transitioned into innocent walking down the aisle-ness for our officiant (Bobbys Kung Fu teacher), my sister and brother-in-law and Bobbys parents. And then, the crowd erupted in laughter when they spotted
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="ringbear" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-529" height="299" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/ringbear.jpg" title="ringbear" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          our ring bear. Thats my mom and my nephew Trinidad, the biggest sport ever. I doubt you could find another 11-year-old who volunteers to wear a hot and stuffy bear costume for a half hour and STILL poses for pictures like a celebrity after the ceremony. Fun fact: my mom found out probably one minute before walking down the aisle about our surprise. She wasnt exactly thrilled, but luckily the processional music started!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="brideandgroom" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-530" height="257" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/brideandgroom1.jpg" title="brideandgroom" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Next up was Bobby in his dashing custom-made suit from
          <a href="http://www.duchessclothier.com/">
           Duchess
          </a>
          and the moment Ill never forget, a standing room of all my loved ones and relatives as I walked with my amazing father. Did I mention the song we all walked to was Start of Something New from High School Musical? Oh yes, it was wonnndderrfullll.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our families are both avid Catholics, so we knew it would be strange for them that we werent getting married in the church. We passed around our rings so they could pray on them, make a silent wish for our future or just inject some good karma onto them. It was a way they could express their faith in the middle of a rock club.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="dads" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-531" height="215" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/dads.jpg" title="dads" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Bobby also read a Veterans day prayer (the holiday was the following week) which made even the manliest of men in the audience choke up. Our fathers were both in Vietnam and deal with their war experiences in completely different ways. This tie between them also united the two families in a profound way.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="reading" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-532" height="299" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/reading.jpg" title="reading" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Our only reading was a passage from The Velveteen Rabbit. It spoke to us because of the very last part Generally, by the time you are Real, most of your hair has been loved off, and your eyes drop out and you get loose in the joints and very shabby. But these things dont matter at all, because once you are Real you cant be ugly, except to people who dont understand. After the year wed had of scrimping to save enough for the wedding, layoffs at our workplaces, mountains of DIY projects, etc., we were in a constant state of feeling very shabby. At this point, we hadnt slept for a few days, after shuttling a giant van full of wedding stuff to Tucson, setting up for the reception, writing my vows at midnight the night before, we only hoped we didnt LOOK as shabby as we felt!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="crying" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-533" height="218" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/crying-copy.jpg" title="crying" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          After the reading and our
          <a href="http://www.funwithjubby.com/candy-candy-candy-i-cant-let-you-go/">
           candy ceremony
          </a>
          , it was time for a coin toss to determine who would say their vows first. Bobby won and he made me cry and cry and cry. It was the sweetest speech I have ever heard and at that moment it was just me, him and a bear out of the corner of my eye.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="kiss and rings" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-534" height="152" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/kiss-and-rings.jpg" title="kiss and rings" width="448"/>
          <br/>
          <img alt="bobbyandjulia" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-535" height="672" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/bobbyandjulia.jpg" title="bobbyandjulia" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          All that was left was some ring exchanging action, a kiss and the proclamation that we were now Mr. and Mrs. Julia &amp; Bobby!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="lightsabers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-536" height="146" src="http://www.funwithjubby.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/lightsabers.jpg" title="lightsabers" width="448"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Our last surprise was the Star Wars Throne Room song (a favorite of offbeat brides everywhere), but we also planted light sabers in the audience. Everyone cracked up and we even had an official archway to walk under.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you had told me the part of the day I would most cherish would be the part we didnt spend much money or time with a hot glue gun, I would have laughed at your naivete. We could have walked right out the door onto the streets of Tucson, and the day would have been perfect beyond my wildest dreams.
         </p>
        </div>
