      <div id="title-fullwidth">
       <h1>
        Google bought a company that makes solar drones, and were officially freaked out
       </h1>
      </div>
      <div id="primary">
       <div class="article clearfix">
        <div class="post-138043 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-google category-industry-news tag-project-loon tag-titan-aerospace" id="post-138043">
         <div class="meta-data clearfix">
          <div class="socialshare">
           <!-- Go to your Addthis.com Dashboard to update any options -->
           <div class="addthis_sharing_toolbox">
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="timestamp">
           <span class="author-avatar" style="width:45px;">
            <a href="https://plus.google.com/115994555433128474950?rel=author" rel="author">
             <img alt="Quentyn Kennemer" class="author-image" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-xGdPugFVeWw/AAAAAAAAAAI/AAAAAAAABBk/rNbHEh9yaBs/s250-c-k/photo.jpg" style="width: 32px!important; height: 32px!important;" width="32"/>
            </a>
           </span>
           <!-- #author-avatar -->
           <span style="width: 200px;">
            Apr 14th 2014 by
            <a href="https://plus.google.com/115994555433128474950?rel=author" rel="author">
             Quentyn Kennemer
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="entry">
          <p>
           <a href="http://phandroid.s3.amazonaws.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/titan-drone.jpg">
            <img alt="titan drone" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-138045 colorbox-138043" height="226" src="http://phandroid.s3.amazonaws.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/titan-drone.jpg" width="571"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <div class="ad_box1" id="topofarticle-ad-block">
          </div>
          <script async="" src="http://pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <!-- Phandroid_Custom_TopOfArticle -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-3288408743021834" data-ad-region="test" data-ad-slot="3110198050" style="display:inline-block;width:640px;height:100px">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
          <p>
           It isnt unheard of for some of Googles acquisitions to come with a bit of unease, but their latest one probably takes the biggest piece of cake of them all. The Mountain View company has confirmed the purchase of New Mexico-based Titan Aerospace, a company that specializes in creating high-altitude drone technology for use in whatever.
          </p>
          <p>
           The company is said to begin interfacing with the team working on
           <a href="http://phandroid.com/2013/06/15/google-xs-latest-moonshot-is-balloon-distributed-internet/" title="project loon">
            Project Loon
           </a>
           , which is a vision that Google hopes can bring balloon-carried high-speed WiFi transmitters and deliver widespread connectivity for the masses. The
           <a href="http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702304117904579501701702936522?mg=reno64-wsj&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fonline.wsj.com%2Farticle%2FSB10001424052702304117904579501701702936522.html">
            Wall Street Journal
           </a>
           also reports that Google could work with Makani, an internal team tasked with researching and developing airborne wind-powered turbines for more efficient energy.
          </p>
          <p>
           All of this sounds quite fine  in fact, its all kind of cool  but theres one other harrowing thing Google could be looking to do with their new-found knowledge of drone technology. Its said that the drones could likely be put to use for purposes of gathering and collecting data, likely to help improve Googles mapping business.
          </p>
          <p>
           Google already employs the likes of self-driving cars and camera-equipped airplanes to take snapshots of the world for use in Google Maps, but Titans drones could bring a flexible, modular system that does all of that and more. That more could including anything from atmospheric sensors for recording climate data to delivering aforementioned airborne WiFi to countries that arent well-equipped for hard-line access.
          </p>
          <p>
           The drones themselves arent your Amazon Drone Delivery or consumer-focused Parrot AR Drone-sized pieces of equipment, either  the smallest drone Titan makes is slightly larger than a Boeing 767, which is a passenger cruiser that can seat anywhere between 181 and 375 people. Let that sink into your brain for just a second.
          </p>
          <p>
           Of course, Googles intentions probably arent as scary as our brains will make them out to be, so well just have to wait and see what the culmination of all these different pieces (Google has
           <a href="http://phandroid.com/2013/12/04/andy-rubin-robots/">
            made a strong push to acquire robotics
           </a>
           and
           <a href="http://phandroid.com/2014/01/27/google-deepmind-acquisition/">
            AI companies
           </a>
           recently) ends up being.
          </p>
