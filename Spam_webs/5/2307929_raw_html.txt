          Kleenex
         </strong>
         is a brand name that screams
         <em>
          tissues
         </em>
         and Im willing to bet that the majority of people reading this post have used a Kleenex tissue at least once in their life. The first thing I think of when I hear of the Kleenex brand is soft tissues that help ward off red, irritated noses during flu season. However, these tissues have many more uses that you probably didnt even know existedor never thought to incorporate into your life.
        </p>
        <p>
         Without further ado, here are 11 alternative uses for Kleenex tissues (and their boxes) that have nothing to do with wiping your nose or covering up a sneeze:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Gentle Sweaty Forehead Wiper:
         </strong>
         While my sweetie takes along a washcloth to wipe sweat from his brow, I carry a bunch of Kleenex in my backpack
         <strong>
          when I go bike-riding
         </strong>
         because the softness of the tissues causes less irritation to my sun-kissed, sweaty, salty forehead.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Kleenex-Tissue-in-Bike-Riding-Backpack.jpg">
          <img alt="Kleenex Tissue in Bike-Riding Backpack" class="aligncenter wp-image-11731" height="297" src="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Kleenex-Tissue-in-Bike-Riding-Backpack.jpg" width="337"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Remove Mascara:
         </strong>
         Kleenex tissues provide a gentle way to remove mascara from your eyelashes without feeling like youre ripping out precious hairs. Of course, dampen your tissue and add a bit of soap or makeup remover beforehand.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissues-to-Remove-Mascara.jpg">
          <img alt="Using Kleenex Tissues to Remove Mascara" class="aligncenter wp-image-11732" height="389" src="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissues-to-Remove-Mascara.jpg" width="345"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. A Quick Countertop Clean-Up:
         </strong>
         Sometimes, a Kleenex delivers a helpful swipe of cleanliness to a kitchen or bathroom countertop, where using a paper towel or sponge seems like such a waste. I often use a Kleenex to wipe up spilled hair products, makeup, coffee dripped on the floor, toothpaste, and other small liquid-y mishaps. A box of Kleenex always sits on top of my toilet tank because I constantly need a tissue to wipe something uplike my hair that gradually collects in the corners of the bathroom floor.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissue-to-Wipe-Countertop-Messes.jpg">
          <img alt="Using Kleenex Tissue to Wipe Countertop Messes" class="wp-image-11736 aligncenter" height="315" src="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissue-to-Wipe-Countertop-Messes.jpg" width="343"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Clean Paintbrushes:
         </strong>
         A Kleenex tissue helps clean, absorb paint, and dry off bristles of a brush in-between changing colors when using acrylic paints and watercolors.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissue-to-Clean-Paint-Brushes.jpg">
          <img alt="Using Kleenex Tissue to Clean Paint Brushes" class="aligncenter wp-image-11733" height="220" src="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissue-to-Clean-Paint-Brushes.jpg" width="344"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. Quick Dust Job:
         </strong>
         For the times when you need to quickly dust off a mantle, TV set, bookshelf, and other small tasks, Kleenex tissues are up for the job. Dust bunnies cling for their lives to the material.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissue-to-Desk-BookShelves.jpg">
          <img alt="Using Kleenex Tissue to Desk BookShelves" class="aligncenter wp-image-11734" height="336" src="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissue-to-Desk-BookShelves.jpg" width="340"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          6. Nipple Protection:
         </strong>
         Ladies, to create a protective barrier for irritated nipples when wearing a bra, slip a folded Kleenex tissue between the sensitive area and material.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          7. Shoe Buffer:
         </strong>
         When I say buffer, Im not talking about shining your shoes, Im talking about
         <strong>
          avoiding a blister when a pair of shoes
         </strong>
         (usually those new, cute heels) are rubbing ferociously against the back of your heel. When Band-Aids are nowhere to be found, stick a Kleenex tissue inside the shoe (against your Achilles heel to be exact) to provide cushioning relief.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          8. Bug Removal:
         </strong>
         Although I do not personally use a Kleenex to send an offending bug in the house to a soft sleepmy sweetie doesnt hesitate to grab one for this purpose.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          9. Decorative Storage Box:
         </strong>
         Once youve used up all of your Kleenex tissues, remove the cellophane opening and transform the decorative box into a place to store and conceal a collection of small items. Think craft supplies, such as tape, markers, and crayons.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <img alt="Using Kleenex Tissues to Wipe Down Mirrors" class="alignright size-full wp-image-11745" height="426" src="http://artpark78.com/blog1/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Using-Kleenex-Tissues-to-Wipe-Down-Mirrors.jpg" width="319"/>
          10. Decorative Suggestion or Swear Box:
         </strong>
         With all of the fun designs and colors theyve been coming up with, an empty Kleenex box also makes an attractive swear box or suggestion box that the entire family can utilize.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          11.
         </strong>
         <strong>
          Cleaning Mirrors:
         </strong>
         Cure a dingy-looking bathroom mirror with a Kleenex tissue and a spritz of Windex, dab of rubbing alcohol, or even water.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Disclosure:
          </strong>
          I received a complimentary box of Kleenex Tissues to sample for being a Crowdtapper, and decided to write a blog post sharing different ways to use them. All of my opinions and experiences are 100% my own.
         </em>
        </p>
