       <h1>
        <img alt="angry businessman" height="217" src="/img/hate-solar.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 10px;" width="325"/>
        Why Your Electricity Company Hates Solar
       </h1>
       <p>
        4:00 PM. This is the traditional
        <i>
         golden hour
        </i>
        for electricity companies.
       </p>
       <p>
        The sun is still high in the sky. The waves of heat are swirling above the pavement. And many Aussie workers and schoolkids are just returning home from a long day, only to be greeted by a sweltering house that might as well be a convection oven.
       </p>
       <p class="p4">
        Like any right-minded human being who enjoys basic comforts, the grid-using public naturally cranks their air conditioners to HIGH, lest they should be roasted in their own skins like a cheap BBQ chicken.
       </p>
       <p>
        Compound that by 8.5 million air conditioners blasting at full for hours on end and youve got an incredible energy demand. Youve also got a bunch of Big Energy executives on the top floor of a high-rise somewhere with big smiles on their faces.
       </p>
       <p>
        This window of daytime energy, where the grid is pumping at near capacity, is sold at a premium
        <b>
        </b>
        by the electricity generators, giving the electric companies immense returns while leaving a big dent in the publics pockets.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         But the game has changed.
        </b>
        The window of time has lost its golden glow for big energy.
       </p>
       <p>
        More and more Australians are using the
        <span class="s1">
         power of the sun
        </span>
        to fight against the effects ofwellthe power of the sun.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         1.2 million
        </b>
        <b>
         Aussie households and rising
        </b>
        have been outfitted with high-efficiency solar panels that are putting a big damper on those electricity company profits and a whole bunch of frowns on the Big Energy executives faces.
       </p>
       <p>
        It certainly isn't rocket science. In fact, its a bit of a no-brainer. The sun is raw energy (in fact it is a Nuclear Fusion reactor if you wanna get technical). Australia is one of the top ten sunniest countries in the world. Harnessing the power of the sun through solar panels equates to easy, clean, and renewable energy right when we need it the most. And solar production is at its best in that same golden window when the market is most profitable.
       </p>
       <p>
        And now the cornered energy giants, whose production relies heavily on earth-scarring and air-polluting methods like coal burning, are spending millions to attack the renewable energy industry. They're heavily lobbying the government to reduce the Renewable Energy Target, a mandate created in the last decade to help make Australia greener, in order to give them another leg up in the market. But their archaic practices simply cannot compete with a plentiful and cheap resource like the sun. And they know it, too.
       </p>
       <p>
        So
        <em>
         Origin Energy
        </em>
        ,
        <em>
         AGL
        </em>
        and
        <em>
         Energy Australia
        </em>
        are pressuring your politicians to
        <a href="https://www.greenpeace.org.au/action/?cid=71&amp;src=FB3" target="_blank" title="sign the petition if you care">
         pull the plug on solar policies
        </a>
        . To protect their profits they want a future with higher household bills, a future relying on their dirty fossil fuels.
       </p>
       <p>
        The Big Energy beast is on the ropes. And, much like an outmatched boxer, they're making one last frenzied attack to try to even the match.
       </p>
       <p>
        But solar energy is proving to be too skilled of an opponent.
       </p>
       <p>
        Why? Because at current prices, solar power can be generated on your roof at
        <b>
         half the cost
        </b>
        of grid power. And to rub salt into the energy execs' wounds, affordable battery storage systems are now starting to come to market that can use that cheap solar power when the sun goes down!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Your electricity company's profits are under siege from solar
        </strong>
        , and it seems like jacking up electricity prices is their only defence.
       </p>
       <p>
        What's the best way to show them the door? Install a solar system
        <a href="https://www.solarquotes.com.au/abbott.html">
         whilst there is still a solar rebate
        </a>
        and start to wean your home off expensive grid electricity.
       </p>
