  <meta content="&lt;p&gt;Formerly the 60 Thompson hotel, New Yorks Sixty Soho  opened this month  is set to be the flagship property for the nascent American luxury boutique-hotel brand Sixty. That the British interior designer Tara Bernerd was enlisted to develop its aesthetic, rather than a homegrown talent, shouldnt come as a surprise. Hotels already in her portfolio include Belgraves in Londons Chelsea and her intention with this property was to create an interior that would reference the citys global character.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;I wanted to create a home from home Ms Bernerd told me when describing the style of Sixty Soho, that brings a little bit of Europe  Parisian chic, a taste of Milan, a bit of Britain  into the melting pot of downtown Manhattan. Its a real homage to all those people from different cultures around the world that visit and live in New York.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Catering to the brands target guests  those with a low-key and understated lifestyle  the design has what Bernerd calls an academic, edgy and industrial glamour. Pierre Chareaus Maison dVerre  the 1930s-build &quot;House of Glass&quot; in Paris - set a benchmark style for the project.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Today, the soul of the hotel lives in the characterful lobby lounge and bar, which blend into one another spatially and stylistically. The former features large picture windows and masses of books, mostly by Taschen; the latter, the Gordon Bar, combines the upscale vibe of a private members club with a relaxed, lived-in feel. It features vintage floor tiling, cow-hide rugs and eclectic furniture, as well as an airy greenhouse-like private function room with a living wall of vertical gardening at each end.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;For Bernerd, the bar &quot;reflects a European home, while still embracing that New York downtown energy&quot;. Adding to that homely touch, it is decorated with eccentric artworks from the private collection of Jason Pomeranc, the creator of the Sixty brand, as well as pieces chosen by the English art dealer Jay Jopling, who Bernerd describes as her &quot;personal art guru&quot;. Further works by Harland Miller, the British artist known for his giant paintings of Penguin book covers, hang throughout the common areas and in each of the guestrooms.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;Perhaps most impressive, though, is Sixty Loft, the hotels best suite. The two-storey residence includes a living room with an L-shaped velvet sofa, a four-poster bed, a fireplace as well as a private balcony in addition to a rooftop deck with sun loungers, offering unobstructed views of the city, including of the Freedom Tower and the Empire State Building. So impressed was Russell Crowe upon seeing this space, Sixty Sohos manager would later tell me, that he expressed interest in purchasing the loft for himself. Fortuitously, though, it remains available for bookings.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;Read the Telegraph's complete &lt;a href=&quot;http://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/hotel/150003/Sixty-Soho-hotel-New-York-USA-review.html&quot;&gt;Sixty Soho hotel review&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;Sixty Soho: doubles from $416.60 (275) per night; Sixty Loft from $5,000 (3,300) per night&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;" name="otsn:text"/>

          <h1 class="">
           Inside New York's Sixty Soho
          </h1>
          <h2 class="">
           The newly opened Sixty Soho is set to bring a little bit of Europe into the heart of downtown Manhattan
          </h2>

         <div class="widget storyContent article widgetId-36 widgetHomeSectionId-21 ">
          <div class="body">
           <p>
            Formerly the 60 Thompson hotel, New Yorks Sixty Soho  opened this month  is set to be the flagship property for the nascent American luxury boutique-hotel brand Sixty. That the British interior designer Tara Bernerd was enlisted to develop its aesthetic, rather than a homegrown talent, shouldnt come as a surprise. Hotels already in her portfolio include Belgraves in Londons Chelsea and her intention with this property was to create an interior that would reference the citys global character.
           </p>
           <p>
            I wanted to create a home from home Ms Bernerd told me when describing the style of Sixty Soho, that brings a little bit of Europe  Parisian chic, a taste of Milan, a bit of Britain  into the melting pot of downtown Manhattan. Its a real homage to all those people from different cultures around the world that visit and live in New York.
           </p>
           <p>
            Catering to the brands target guests  those with a low-key and understated lifestyle  the design has what Bernerd calls an academic, edgy and industrial glamour. Pierre Chareaus Maison dVerre  the 1930s-build "House of Glass" in Paris - set a benchmark style for the project.
           </p>
           <p>
            Today, the soul of the hotel lives in the characterful lobby lounge and bar, which blend into one another spatially and stylistically. The former features large picture windows and masses of books, mostly by Taschen; the latter, the Gordon Bar, combines the upscale vibe of a private members club with a relaxed, lived-in feel. It features vintage floor tiling, cow-hide rugs and eclectic furniture, as well as an airy greenhouse-like private function room with a living wall of vertical gardening at each end.
           </p>
           <p>
            For Bernerd, the bar "reflects a European home, while still embracing that New York downtown energy". Adding to that homely touch, it is decorated with eccentric artworks from the private collection of Jason Pomeranc, the creator of the Sixty brand, as well as pieces chosen by the English art dealer Jay Jopling, who Bernerd describes as her "personal art guru". Further works by Harland Miller, the British artist known for his giant paintings of Penguin book covers, hang throughout the common areas and in each of the guestrooms.
           </p>
           <p>
            Perhaps most impressive, though, is Sixty Loft, the hotels best suite. The two-storey residence includes a living room with an L-shaped velvet sofa, a four-poster bed, a fireplace as well as a private balcony in addition to a rooftop deck with sun loungers, offering unobstructed views of the city, including of the Freedom Tower and the Empire State Building. So impressed was Russell Crowe upon seeing this space, Sixty Sohos manager would later tell me, that he expressed interest in purchasing the loft for himself. Fortuitously, though, it remains available for bookings.
           </p>

            </em>
           </p>

             Sixty Soho: doubles from $416.60 (275) per night; Sixty Loft from $5,000 (3,300) per night
