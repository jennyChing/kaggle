        <h1>
         <a href="/49-running-and-human-evolution.html">
          Running and human evolution
         </a>
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="itemBody">
        <ul class="content-links">
        </ul>
        <p>
         There is a very good theory I personally support that we as human beings are designed to run, that we actually stood up onto two feet in our evolution as a species in order to let our lungs manage oxygen at will, unlike, lets say, the cheetah, the fastest animal on the planet, that has to breath in and out every time her back legs move. That particular trick helped us survive and hunt by running other animals down to exhaustion. Scared animals unable to run any more used to fall desperate for breath when we could go on for a much longer period of time on our feet, running. Its a good theory and I for one, excited, set out to prove it to myself.
        </p>
        <p>
         Our bodies are designed to work and that is a fact. A body that hasnt been worked is a weak body, unhappy and a sorry one. So, if we are designed for hardship why do our minds tell us to sit back and relax, sleep and eat as much as we can? Our self-preservation has moved on since the times when we actually had to fight for food but the primal function is still playing its role: stockpile. In the past we never knew when the next meal would be and it was natural to eat as much as we could when we could whereas now food is always available. Developing tastes for rich fatty foods doesnt help either. Our brain is not trying to trick us here when we are reaching for an extra slice of cake, no, on the contrary, its trying to ensure our survival by making us eat more. We simply havent developed as a species yet to assume that food is there and we will have access to it at all times. Incredible creatures we are, the only ones on the planet trying to promote the eating less habits in advanced societies. We havent restructured our genome yet and as a result we got an unhealthy and weak population on our hands.
        </p>
        <p>
         We are not being lazy because we are mentally ill, we are lazy because there is no gun to our heads we prefer a couch potato lifestyle to a gym-rat one. We had to run and fight for food because it was a question of our immediate survival when now it isnt. It will take us time to adjust and its not going to happen any time soon so as intelligent beings we have to make that choice on our own because we dont have it in our nature just yet.
        </p>
        <p>
         Pushing yourself hurts, here is another fact, but in a long run (literary also) it remakes you into something far more beautiful than you can imagine. I am not talking about physical beauty here, although for many this is the main goal, I am talking about a beautiful healthy person, capable and strong and worth admiring.
        </p>
        <p>
         Running, daily running and long distance running, liberates us in so many ways I dont even know where to start. And although a road to that is laid with pain its a price worth paying, after all, since we are actually designed for work as long as we dont give up, it passes. It doesnt get any easier since your horizons always expand but you find great joy in it after a while and its intoxicating. You dont get enough of it.
        </p>
        <p>
         When I first discovered that human beings are able to run for hundreds of kilometers without a rest (see Tarahumara) I was extremely curious to say the least. I thought, why cant I do the same since, well, I am the same species after all. Back then it was mostly a dare, half a joke, but once you get an idea, and its a good one it doesnt let go of you. Once you know it can be done, you know you can also do it too.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont look at me and think, sure, she just could do it because that wont be true. I myself have never been particularly good at sports and going to the gym was always something I had to do so I wont pig out. Running was my worst nightmare and a torture most of the time. But one day I decided to keep going and see where it took me and since then I began my own personal evolution as a human being.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once you start approaching something as being normal, doable and no biggy in general, it comes natural to you. The only brakes you actually have are in your mind, if you remove them, since they are in fact imaginary, you can move mountains.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </p>
