        <div class="body parsys">
         <div class="articlerelatedphotogallery section">

         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           From Damien Coxs
           <a href="http://thestar.blogs.com/thespin/">
            Spin on Sports
           </a>
           blog:
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           -
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Ten thoughts as the
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/sports/leafs.html">
            Maple Leafs
           </a>
           open a western Canadian road swing with
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/sports/leafs/2013/10/30/maple_leafs_kessel_and_kadri_continue_to_click_in_win_over_oilers.html">
            a victory in Edmonton
           </a>
           :
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            1. Put your feet up, son. Take a load off:
           </b>
           There arent many players in the NHL who are assigned the same tough minutes as Leaf captain Dion Phaneuf every night. So getting 19:11 in playing time Tuesday night in Edmonton was almost like a night off, with only his partner, Carl Gunnarson, getting less work on this night. Paul Ranger led the Leaf blueline in minutes played with 22:15 in the easy 4-0 win.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Many of the Leafs top players got less than their usual ice time. The nature of the game dictated that, and was a nice present going into a back-to-back situation in Calgary on Wednesday.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           As far as Phaneufs contractual situation goes  hes a UFA in July  there have been no substantive talks between GM Dave Nonis and Phaneufs agent, Don Meehan of Newport Sports. No numbers exchanged, no term discussed. Nothing. And contract talks arent at a preliminary stage. They havent started at all.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           That said, there is an understanding between Nonis and Meehan that an attempt will be made, likely before Christmas, to see if theres a deal there to be done. With Phaneuf already at a $6.5 million cap hit, the salary may not be a big issue. You cant ask him to take a pay cut, and $7 million or thereabouts seems like a fair raise.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           But term, and no-trade/no-movement stipulations, will be big issues. Other than Phil Kessel
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/sports/leafs/2013/10/01/maple_leafs_phil_kessels_contract_will_come_with_lots_of_questions_cox.html">
            and his eight-year
           </a>
           , the Leafs have done well in recent seasons to avoid the longer term deals seen around the NHL. Phaneuf is 28. Five years seems to be as long as the Leafs would want to go. But well see.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            2. If only Bernie hadnt bolted for the WHA:
           </b>
           Together, James Reimer and Jonathan Bernier have posted a save percentage of .933 this season, a big reason why the Leafs are sixth in the league in G.A.A. (2.31) despite having given up an average of 35.5 shots per game (29th out of 30 teams).
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Whos No. 1? Still to be determined, and its turning into a great battle, just as head coach Randy Carlyle hoped would be the case, with each goalie pushing the other.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Were only 13 games in, but the Reimer-Bernier combination certainly makes one think of some of the better Leaf goalie tandems of the post-expansion era.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Curtis Joseph and Glenn Healy did well. Felix Potvin and Grant Fuhr were together for a time. My favourite? That one season, 1971-72, when Jacques Plante and Bernie Parent combined to give the Leafs airtight netminding. Parent actually played games behind Plante the year before, although Bruce Gamble got into more than Parent. But then the 26-year-old Parent asserted himself as No. 1 in the 71-72 season over Plante, who turned 43 during the season, and both put up very good numbers.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The previous spring, of course, had included that infamous playoff incident in which Rangers forward Vic Hadfield threw Parents mask into the crowd at Madison Square Garden. The crowd wouldnt give it back, Parent wouldnt play without one and Plante was forced into the game and the Rangers went on to win the series.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           As it turned out, Parent did get the mask back  41 years later. It was bought at a memorabilia show and the new owner asked Parent to authenticate it, which he did.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The first thing I wanted to do when I saw the mask was call Hadfield and say, Thanks,  Parent told the Philadelphia Daily News.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Parent bolted for the WHA after the 71-72 season, and when he came back to the NHL, it was as a Philadelphia Flyer.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            3. Maybe they should have given him the $64 million earlier:
           </b>
           You never know what a contract is going to do to an athlete. For Phil Kessel, one of the intriguing effects is that, while always an accomplished passer, he seems to be more selfless with the puck than ever since signing an
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/sports/leafs/2013/10/01/maple_leafs_sign_phil_kessel_to_contract_extension.html">
            eight-year extension
           </a>
           on the eve of the season.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The latest came Tuesday when, with two goals already to his credit, Kessel passed up a good chance at a third in the final period to set up Morgan Rielly, who was then initially credited with his first NHL goal. On further review, however, the goal was given to Nazem Kadri, with the puck apparently grazing his hip before beating Richard Bachman in the Edmonton net.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Kessel will never be a fire-and-brimstone type leader. But that kind of play is another form of invaluable leadership.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            4. Restless natives and all that:
           </b>
           The mood is dark in Edmonton already where, with three wins in 14 games, the Oilers are already eight points out of a playoff berth.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Theres is great heat on GM Craig MacTavish already to do something, particularly something to help the teams weak goaltending and so-so blueline. Nobody wants to touch the blue-chip kids up front, but to make an impact move, MacTavish may have little choice.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Under Dallas Eakins, Edmonton is surrendering 3.79 goals per game, more than a goal more than last season when Ralph Krueger was the head coach. After so many top picks were spent on forwards, the Oilers finally invested in defence last June by drafting Darnell Nurse of the Sault Ste. Marie Greyhounds in the first round, but Nurse will take some time. Justin Schultz, the prize college free agent signing of a year ago, is minus-10 and doesnt have the same swagger as last season.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           MacTavish at the very least has to find some veteran help for his beleaguered back line. The Oilers face Detroit on the weekend at home, then hit the road for games against the Panthers, Lightning, Flyers and Blackhawks.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            5. Theyve already tried the healthy scratch route. Time for Oklahoma City?:
           </b>
           Nail Yakupovs season is turning into a bit of disaster, although Eakins gave him plenty of ice time against the Leafs, clearly trying to inspire some confidence in the first overall pick of the 2012 NHL draft.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Yakupov has one goal and two assists this season and is minus-10 after a minus-4 performance against Toronto. This is a new dynamic for the Oilers, who took Taylor Hall and Ryan Nugent-Hopkins first in their draft years and were almost immediately rewarded with a level of play and production that made them believe both could be standout NHLers.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           But Yakupov? He did have 17 goals last season, but this is a marked regression. Eakins has already scratched him, a decision that prompted Yakupov to say I really dont like skating all the time, and forechecking, and hitting somebody every shift. I dont think its my game.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           A stint in the minors might help. Then again, Yakupov might rebel, or threaten a jump to the KHL. This is a tough one for a team thats already got enough problems.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            6. Somethings gone sideways in the nations capital, and it has nothing to do with Mike Duffy:
           </b>
           The Sens have lost four out of five, but its the way theyre losing games that a bit shocking.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Theyre the only team in the NHL that is giving up more shots per game than the Leafs. Last year, with injuries with to Jason Spezza and Erik Karlsson, they were the NHLs hardest working team, and among the most miserly when it came to allowing goals. Everyone raved about the impact of Paul MacLeans coaching.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           This season, theyve gone from the second best defensive team in the league to the 24th. Could Daniel Alfredssons leadership be missed this much?
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            7. Surely this will work out better than Ryan Smyth:
           </b>
           The Leafs were one of the teams that was offered Tomas Vanek,
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/sports/leafs/2013/10/29/maple_leafs_was_toronto_offered_a_chance_to_land_thomas_vanek.html">
            reports the Stars Mark Zwolinski
           </a>
           , but the conversation was short. Presumably the Sabres would have asked for one of Torontos top forwards, but with none approaching UFA status other than the newly acquired David Bolland, there wasnt a match.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           The last time the Islanders, meanwhile, made this kind of move was six years ago when they acquired Smyth from the Oilers in a massive deal. Smyth played 23 games then headed as a UFA to Colorado, and Vanek might end up doing something.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           At least the Sabres are paying some of Vaneks salary and the first rounder given up by the Isles is protected if its in the top 10. But losing to the Rangers on Tuesday in Vaneks first game was an unsatisfying start.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Would the Sabres have traded Vanek to the Leafs, a key rival? Maybe. But the two teams havent made an impact swap in 20 years since Grant Fuhr went to western New York and Dave Andreychuk arrived in Toronto to become Doug Gilmours wingman.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            8. Seems theres more than one way to rebuild:
           </b>
           Its interesting that the Sabres, with four first-round picks from the last two years already in the lineup, and with nine picks in place for the first two rounds of the next two drafts, seem to be setting themselves up for an Edmonton-style rebuild.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Go to the bottom. Draft early and often. Pray that it works.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           And it might eventually, both in Edmonton and Buffalo.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           But there are only a few examples of this really working, with Chicago and Pittsburgh being the best ones. But when you look at the top 15 teams in the NHL standings as of Wednesday morning, most did nothing of the kind.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Thats because the likes of Sidney Crosby, Evgeny Malkin, Patrick Kane and Jonathan Toews arent available every year.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Then again,
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/sports/hockey/2013/10/14/ohl_phenom_connor_mcdavid_may_have_already_outgrown_otters_feschuk.html">
            Connor McDavid
           </a>
           looks like that sort of talent. But how do you plan to be dead last two Junes from now?
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            9. Apparently he was telling the truth: Since
           </b>
           speaking his mind on Sven Baertschi, we havent heard much from Brian Burke. He said when he was hired in Calgary he planned to stay in the background, and lo and behold, he made sure he was
           <a href="http://www.thestar.com/sports/leafs/2013/10/28/maple_leafs_brian_burke_to_miss_date_with_flames.html">
            out of town this week
           </a>
           when the Leafs swing by Cowtown for a visit.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Soon well be calling him Bashful Brian. On second thought, thatll never happen.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           <b>
            10. Returning to the scene of the divorce:
           </b>
           Mason Raymond has played awfully well so far for the Leafs on his $1 million free agent deal, and as he returns to Vancouver on Saturday, were left again to wonder what it was the Canucks no longer saw in him.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Perhaps that will become more evident over the course of the season, but right now, hes been a very good fit for the Leafs, and Carlyle seems to trust him in all situations.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           Clearly, hes a highly motivated young man. Maybe its being blown off by Vancouver, maybe its playing on a small contract with hopes of bigger money to follow.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
          <p itemprop="articleBody">
           As it stands, the Leafs seem unlikely to do anything about Raymonds contract until after this season. The insecurity does seem to be bringing out his best hockey.
          </p>
         </div>
