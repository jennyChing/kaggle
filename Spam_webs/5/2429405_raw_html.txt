             <h1 class="post-title">
              <a href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/homeandgarden/2010/11/the-mystery-make-up-product/" title="The Mystery Make-Up Product">
               The Mystery Make-Up Product
              </a>
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-tasks">
              <span class="author">
               Posted by Ree
              </span>
              <a class="comment-number-wrap" href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/homeandgarden/2010/11/the-mystery-make-up-product/#comments" title="comments">
               <div class="comment-number">
                134
               </div>
               <span>
                Comments
               </span>
              </a>
             </div>
            </header>
            <div class="post-padding">
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5158060938/" title="TPW_9537 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_9537" class="width_427 frame-img" height="640" src="http://farm2.static.flickr.com/1128/5158060938_84381a7064_z.jpg" width="427"/>
              </a>
              I love this make-up product. Its called blah blah blah French word French pronunciation blah blah eyeliner.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5157451541/" title="TPW_9562 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_9562" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm2.static.flickr.com/1368/5157451541_279057e816_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              Heres what it looks like when its new out of the box. (Note that it comes with a little lid; I pulled it off for the photos.)
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/pioneerwoman/5157450961/" title="TPW_9564 by Ree Drummond / The Pioneer Woman, on Flickr">
               <img alt="TPW_9564" class="width_630 frame-img" height="420" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4056/5157450961_b8872b1efa_z.jpg" width="630"/>
              </a>
              But then you dial up the bottom, and (you can see hints of it) the liquid eyeliner starts seeping into the brush.
             </p>
             <p>
              Eventually, it saturates the brush, but very lightly so.
             </p>
             <p>
              What you wind up with is an always-ready artists brush with ready-to-go black liquid eyeliner.
             </p>
             <p>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/homeandgarden/wp-content/uploads/sites/5/2010/11/eyeliner.jpg">
               <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3901 frame-img width_400" height="400" src="http://thepioneerwoman.com/homeandgarden/wp-content/uploads/sites/5/2010/11/eyeliner.jpg" title="" width="400"/>
              </a>
              This is obviously an extreme example, but it gives you an idea of what the eyeliners used for. It makes that perfect line along the upper eyelashes, which is always kind of vampyor retroor glamor (if used sparingly) just a good way to boost definition and add a little depth.
             </p>
             <p>
              Years and years ago, Prescriptives made a similar product, and I was devastated when they discontinued it in the same way I mourned the loss of my Victorias Secret bra and Estee Lauder lipstick I used to love so much. So when I found this Chanel counterpart a couple of years ago, I fell on my knees and started crying.
             </p>
             <h6>
              Heres what I love about this eyeliner:
             </h6>
             <p>
              * Its never, ever clumpy or thick or gloopy. Its always a perfect, relatively thin consistency
              <br/>
              * It goes on very matte. Theres no sheen to it at all.
              <br/>
              * Its always ready to use; just turn the dial on the bottom of the stick and a teeny tiny amount of liquid is pushed into the brush.
              <br/>
              * The shape makes it easy to apply, even if you dont have a lot of upper lid eyeliner experience
             </p>
             <h6>
              Heres what I do not love about this eyeliner:
             </h6>
             <p>
              *
              <em>
               It is not cheap
              </em>
              . You are definitely paying for the slick mechanism, the great eyeliner formula, and probably the Chanel brand.
             </p>
             <p>
              However, I will say that because I do not use it dailyI usually only whip it out when Im getting dressed to go somewhereit lasts a very, very long time, particularly if you take care of it and keep the lid on between uses. Also, I really havent been able to find another product like it. The closest Ive come is a cake liquid eyeliner from Cliniquethe kind where you wet the brush and swish it around on the solid black substance. Note: the typical ready-to-go liquid eyeliners sold in storesthe kind where you unscrew the lid and pull out the wandjust dont (in my dorky opinion) measure up to this product. I imagine its similar to the difference between really good chefs knives and so-so ones.
             </p>
             <p>
              Heres the link! If youre interested, you might stop by a store that sells Chanel sometime and try it out before you decide to order.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.chanel.com/en_US/fragrance-beauty/Makeup-Eyeliner-%C3%89CRITURE-DE-CHANEL-89243" target="_blank">
               Ecriture de blah blah blag Chanel Awesome Black Eyeliner
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              But Im fairly confident in highly recommending it.
             </p>
             <p>
              Es very, very goot.
             </p>
