       <h1 class="postTitle">
        Clues that Your Sewer Line Needs Repair
       </h1>
       <div class="blogMeta">
        November 28th, 2014 | By
        <a href="http://www.houstonfoundationcontractors.com/author/158845/" rel="author" title="Posts by 158845">
         158845
        </a>
        | Posted in
        <a href="http://www.houstonfoundationcontractors.com/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Uncategorized">
         Uncategorized
        </a>
        |
       </div>
       <div class="theContent">
        <p>
         Sometimes, when a sewer line breaks, the clues are obviousa flooded yard, or even water backing up into your sinks and toilets. There are other clues less obvious that you need to be aware of to prevent a larger catastrophe.
        </p>
        <p>
         A common problem that many homeowners report when a sewage issue arises is a foul smell. The basement seems to be the most affected room and the smell usually indicates a broken pipe. A broken pipe or a clogged pipe can also create a strange bubbling sound when you run water through a faucet or flush a toilet. The toilet should always refill with water after being flushed, or the water refills at a different level, the pipe could be blocked or clogged.
        </p>
        <p>
         The drain to the toilet is the largest within a home and often sewer problems are revealed first in the toilet. If you run a washing machine and it causes water to overflow in the toilet, there is usually a clog in the pipes. This is because the water is unable to drain properly and ends up going through the pipes connected to the toilet. Sometimes the sewer system only needs a little treatment, and sometimes the entire system needs to be replaced. The sooner the problem is caught, the better your chances of an easy fix are. We can conduct an evaluation of your sewer system if you feel you may have a problem.
        </p>
