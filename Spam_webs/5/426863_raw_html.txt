         <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
          What is Cyber Warfare?
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          We often hear about companies getting hacked and information stolen by unknown entities. We might wonder what the big deal is about cyber attacks. The virtual reality is that computer malware designed to infiltrate systems of intelligence are part of the struggle for international dominance. Cyber warfare is a component of total war, which is where opponents use every aspect of life to engage each other.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The goal for nation states involved in cyber warfare is to seek out information that will give them an advantage in using and building resources in order to become stronger than other nation states. It is not uncommon to hear news reports of major companies being infiltrated by hackers and e-mails and other personal information getting stolen. What we hear about less often on the news is how major defense companies are targeted. In the video link below an Australian defense company that works directly with their military and other Western countries has been a victim of information theft.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          See this link:
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zAS-agcQqEk">
           https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zAS-agcQqEk
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          China has been a high profile cyber warfare agent, and along with the United States is very active in building government agencies devoted to the work of hacking computers. In recent years this effort has translated into China gaining economic strength through copying many Western innovations and technologies and selling them at a fraction of the cost. Thus cyber warfare affects every other aspect of traditional power, as it has a direct effect on economic and labor issues, which are some of the most important drivers to a nations success. Next time you see a news item on a cyber attack, be aware that the hackers are after much more than just our personal information.
         </p>
