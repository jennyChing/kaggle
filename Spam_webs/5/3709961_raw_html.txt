   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Optoelectronics
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
     Jump to:
     <a href="#mw-head">
      navigation
     </a>
     ,
     <a href="#p-search">
      search
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="hatnote">
      Not to be confused with
      <a href="/wiki/Electro-optics" title="Electro-optics">
       electro-optics
      </a>
      .
     </div>
     <p>
      <b>
       Optoelectronics
      </b>
      is the study and application of
      <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">
       electronic
      </a>
      devices that source, detect and control
      <a href="/wiki/Light" title="Light">
       light
      </a>
      , usually considered a sub-field of
      <a href="/wiki/Photonics" title="Photonics">
       photonics
      </a>
      . In this context,
      <i>
       light
      </i>
      often includes invisible forms of radiation such as
      <a href="/wiki/Gamma_ray" title="Gamma ray">
       gamma rays
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">
       X-rays
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet" title="Ultraviolet">
       ultraviolet
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">
       infrared
      </a>
      , in addition to visible light. Optoelectronic devices are electrical-to-optical or optical-to-electrical
      <a href="/wiki/Transducer" title="Transducer">
       transducers
      </a>
      , or instruments that use such devices in their operation.
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Electro-optics" title="Electro-optics">
        Electro-optics
       </a>
      </i>
      is often erroneously used as a synonym, but is a wider branch of
      <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">
       physics
      </a>
      that concerns all interactions between light and
      <a href="/wiki/Electric_field" title="Electric field">
       electric fields
      </a>
      , whether or not they form part of an electronic device.
     </p>
     <p>
      Optoelectronics is based on the
      <a href="/wiki/Quantum_mechanics" title="Quantum mechanics">
       quantum mechanical
      </a>
      effects of
      <a href="/wiki/Light" title="Light">
       light
      </a>
      on electronic materials, especially
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semiconductors" title="Semiconductors">
       semiconductors
      </a>
      , sometimes in the presence of
      <a href="/wiki/Electric_field" title="Electric field">
       electric fields
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photoelectric" title="Photoelectric">
        Photoelectric
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photovoltaic" title="Photovoltaic">
        photovoltaic
       </a>
       effect, used in:
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Photodiode" title="Photodiode">
          photodiodes
         </a>
         (including
         <a href="/wiki/Solar_cell" title="Solar cell">
          solar cells
         </a>
         )
        </li>
        <li>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phototransistor" title="Phototransistor">
          phototransistors
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Photomultiplier" title="Photomultiplier">
          photomultipliers
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optoisolator" title="Optoisolator">
          optoisolators
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integrated_optical_circuit" title="Integrated optical circuit">
          integrated optical circuit
         </a>
         (IOC) elements
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Photoconductivity" title="Photoconductivity">
        Photoconductivity
       </a>
       , used in:
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Photoresistor" title="Photoresistor">
          photoresistors
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Photoconductive_camera_tube&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Photoconductive camera tube (page does not exist)">
          photoconductive camera tubes
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charge-coupled_imaging_device" title="Charge-coupled imaging device">
          charge-coupled imaging devices
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Stimulated_emission" title="Stimulated emission">
        Stimulated emission
       </a>
       , used in:
       <ul>
        <li>
         injection
         <a href="/wiki/Laser_diode" title="Laser diode">
          laser diodes
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Quantum_cascade_laser" title="Quantum cascade laser">
          quantum cascade lasers
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       Lossev effect, or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radiative_recombination" title="Radiative recombination">
        radiative recombination
       </a>
       , used in:
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Light-emitting_diode" title="Light-emitting diode">
          light-emitting diodes
         </a>
         or LED
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/OLED" title="OLED">
          OLEDs
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photoemission" title="Photoemission">
        Photoemissivity
       </a>
       , used in
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Photoemissive_camera_tube&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Photoemissive camera tube (page does not exist)">
          photoemissive camera tube
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Important applications
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      of optoelectronics include:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optocoupler" title="Optocoupler">
        Optocoupler
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Optical_fiber" title="Optical fiber">
        Optical fiber
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Telecommunication" title="Telecommunication">
        communications
       </a>
      </li>
     </ul>
