        <header class="entry-header">
         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Shipping Containers Helping Athletes In Sporting Events
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          posted on
          <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-05-04T03:57:37+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           May 4, 2014
          </time>
          <span class="entry-categories">
           in
           <a href="http://www.royalwolf.com.au/blog/category/construction/" rel="category tag">
            Construction
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Shipping containers have uses in all kinds of industries, including some that wouldnt necessarily occur to people. Sports teams and athletes need them for all kinds of things. Here are a few examples:
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Equipment Storage
          </span>
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_119" style="width: 650px">
          <img alt="cricket-pitch-roller (1)" class="wp-image-119 size-full" height="424" src="http://www.royalwolf.com.au/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/cricket-pitch-roller-1.jpg" width="640"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           <em>
            <strong>
             Shipping containers can be used to store sports equipment, including bulky items like cricket pitch rollers.
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Shipping containers make excellent storage facilities. Cricket clubs need to shelve their bats and stumps each winter, along with bulky items like lawnmowers and rollers. A
          <a href="http://www.royalwolf.com.au/storage-containers/20-shipping-containers/" target="_blank">
           20-foot container
          </a>
          with an added 30 cm of height can house it all!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Not every sport requires as much equipment as cricket. However, at places like schools, where several sports arehosted and practiced at once, all the balls and nets can really add up!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Shipping containers are a prime option because of their durability and weather resistance. Anything thats inside stays safe and secureespecially with the security measures available, which include
          <a href="http://www.royalwolf.com.au/accessories/padlocks/" target="_blank">
           padlocks
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.royalwolf.com.au/accessories/lock-boxes/" target="_blank">
           lock boxes
          </a>
          .Shipping containers can also be customised with
          <a href="http://www.royalwolf.com.au/accessories/shelving/" target="_blank">
           shelving
          </a>
          for easy organisation, and come in a variety of sizes.
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
          Sports Facilities
         </h2>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Many forward-thinking building designers and architects are interested in finding ways to recycle shipping containers into modern buildings. WhenLos Angeles studio
          <a href="http://m-rad.com/" target="_blank">
           M-Rad
          </a>
          proposed their design for
          <a href="http://m-rad.com/index.php?/m-rad-large-projects/vegas-extreme/" target="_blank">
           Vegas Extreme
          </a>
          , they envisioned a pinnacle of sustainability, innovation, and fun! The42 acre extreme sports park will be located a half block from the Las Vegas strip and include:
         </p>
         <ul style="text-align: justify;">
          <li>
           Five surf rider waves
          </li>
          <li>
           Three zip lines
          </li>
          <li>
           A six-acre shallow lake with a wakeboard tow and beach cabanas
          </li>
          <li>
           Four electric go kart tracks
          </li>
          <li>
           BMX tracks
          </li>
          <li>
           A skatepark
          </li>
          <li>
           A white water rafting course
          </li>
          <li>
           Extreme stunt performances by professionals
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Perhaps the most exciting aspect of Vegas Extreme is its boutique hotel, which will be comprised of corkscrewing towers made of repurposed shipping containers. Each room will provide a 180 degree view of the park, and the upper levels will have private infinity pools with even better views, as well as access to the rooftop bar.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          If all goes according to plan, the park will receive 85% of its power from an extensive solar field and small scale, low-magnitude wind turbines. Its ambitious, but as of January 2014 was almost fully funded and ready to proceed with development.Australian developer
          <a href="http://www.jrn.com/ktnv/news/Plans-for-an-extreme-sports-park-near-the-Las-Vegas-Strip-216867591.html" target="_blank">
           Joshua Kearney
          </a>
          says that his idea could change the modern theme park model around the world.
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
          Changing Rooms And Showers
         </h2>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Shipping containers can also be fitted with
          <a href="http://www.royalwolf.com.au/accessories/plumbing/" target="_blank">
           plumbing
          </a>
          to create sinks, toilets, and showers. When sports teams are unable to use fields that are in close proximity to changing rooms and showers, a shipping container can be customized to fill those needs. This allows teams to host away teams as well as have a place to change and shower themselves.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Facilities built to house showers are typically constructed from more than one 40-foot shipping container. A soccer team in
          <a href="http://containerliving.net/football-club-facilities-house-built-from-shipping-containers/" target="_blank">
           Essex, U.K.
          </a>
          has two containers altered to fill their needs. One side from each container was removed,creating a 40-by-16-foot space, and internal walls and doors were installed to divide the space into various rooms. A water tank was installed on the roof to feed the facilitys showers, and the shower room was tiled and waterproofed.
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
          Obstacle Races
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_120" style="width: 410px">
          <img alt="spartan-race-400x600 (1)" class="wp-image-120 size-full" height="600" src="http://www.royalwolf.com.au/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/spartan-race-400x600-1.jpg" width="400"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The Spartan Race makes use of shipping containers for many of its obstacles.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The
          <a href="http://spartanrace.com.au/" target="_blank">
           Spartan Race
          </a>
          is one of the worlds best obstacle races. In fact, it was voted as
          <em>
           the
          </em>
          best in 2012 by
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spartan_race" target="_blank">
           Outside Magazine
          </a>
          . The obstacle lineup can vary, and the organisers do not provide a list of events or a course map to participants until the day of the race, but some examples of commonly-used obstacles include:
         </p>
         <ul style="text-align: justify;">
          <li>
           <b>
            Fire
           </b>
           <strong>
            Jump
           </strong>
           :
           <b>
           </b>
           whereparticipants leap over flames
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Barbed Wire Crawl
           </b>
           : a crawl through mud under barbed wire
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Over-Under-Through
           </b>
           : runners must first climb over a wall, then under a wall, then through a tire or square hole placed in a wall
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Spear Throw
           </b>
           : athletes must throw a wooden spear into a target from a distance of 10-20 meters
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Wall Climb
           </b>
           : racers must climb over a wooden wall ranging from 1-3 meters in height
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Object Carry
           </b>
           : participants must carry a heavy object, such as a tire, a rock-filled bucket, or a sandbag, a certain distance
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Herculean Hoist
           </b>
           : using a pulley system, athletes must hoist a cement block or heavy bucket off the ground
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Traversal Wall
           </b>
           : participants must climb along a length of horizontal wall using small blocks as hand- and footholds.
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Slippery Wall
           </b>
           : a wall built at an incline is covered in soap or grease, and runners must sprint up it
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Gladiator Arena
           </b>
           : athletes must pass through a gauntlet of gladiators who try to knock them down
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Hobie Hop
           </b>
           : racers place a band around their ankles and hop through a row of several dozen tires
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Log Jump
           </b>
           : participants have to hop on logs arranged in a zig-zag pattern without touching the ground
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The Spartan Race has also made use of shipping containers in two of its most popularand difficultobstacle.They even made theMost Brutal Obstacles in Racinglist fromObstacle Racers, a community for obstacle race enthusiasts.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The first one of these is the Rope Climb, a classic military-style obstacle in which participants must climb a knotted rope. Climbers sometimes have to start in a waist-height pond of muddy water, which makes the rope slippery and hard to grip. The ropes are tied to a log that is suspended between two towers made of shipping containers, each stacked two containers high. Once they reach the top, they must ring a bell to signify that theyve completed the obstacle.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The Tyrolean Traverse is the number one most difficult obstacle in the racing world, according to Obstacle Racers. It consists of a taut rope suspended between two shipping containers, the goal being to drag yourself all the way across.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          If you fall, theres a crash pad beneath you, but youre not completely safeas with any obstacle you fail to complete, you have to perform 30 burpees before you can move on. Spartan Race Australia has given a sneak peek of this years traverse, which will be 2.5 meters off the ground and 12 meters long. The containers used to build it were provided by
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/SpartanRaceAustralia/posts/499815353472992" target="_blank">
           Royal Wolf
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           <em>
            Were you surprised at the variety of ways in which shipping containers help athletes?
           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
