        Here on the blog we have covered all the main components that make up our beautiful wooden kitchens, but it is often the smaller details that help a kitchen stand out.

       </p>

        Available in three styles  Shaker doors with frosted glass and Traditional doors with either frosted or tinted glass  a glazed door adds extra character to any kitchen, whilst also helping to reflect additional light around the space.
       </p>
       <p>
        Glazed doors are suited to almost any design  be it modern or traditional  and all feature quality Class A glass which meets the BS6206 standard, guaranteeing durability up to the highest rating awarded by the British and European Safety Standard tests.
       </p>
       <p>
        In a traditional
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/">
         solid oak kitchens
        </a>
        , consider either lacquered or dark painted doors to complement tinted glass, whilst frosted glass is more suitable for matching with doors painted in a lighter colour. For modern kitchens in the Shaker style, frosted glass is a stunning combination for any type of door  whether lacquered or painted.
       </p>
       <p>
        Like our standard oak doors, each glazed frontal is constructed from 20mm thick oak panels, and can be mounted to our wall cabinets using Blum BLUMOTION soft-close hinges.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you would like to see our variety of glazed doors for yourself, why not visit one of our
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/showrooms/oak-kitchens-showroom.html">
         kitchen showrooms
        </a>
        ? All three showrooms have a number of glazed cabinets on display, and our friendly team would be happy to talk you through the various options.

       </a>
      </h2>
      <!-- / TITLE -->
      <!-- TAGS, CATEGORY AND AUTHOR -->
      <div class="meta">
       Posted by admin on
       <strong>
        May 13, 2015
       </strong>
       at 1:49 pm. Filed under:
       <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/cabinets_blog/category/general/" rel="category tag">

       <span style="float: right;">

       <p>
        We are very proud to announce that our superb range of worktops  all of which are ideal for
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/">
         solid wood kitchens
        </a>
         has greatly expanded this week, with 2 new wood species added to the collection in the form of
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/ash-worktops.html">
         ash worktops
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/black-oak-worktops.html">
         black oak worktops
        </a>
        . These two worktops couldnt look more different, but both have unique qualities that make them an excellent choice for modern and traditional kitchen settings.
       </p>
       <div style="max-width: 641px; margin: auto;">
        <div style="float: left; margin: 10px;">
         <img alt="Black Oak worktops" src="/media/gbu0/black-oak-worktops-blog-130515.jpg" style="width: 100%;"/>

       </div>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
       <p>
        Our new ash worktops are made of brightly-coloured European ash, which is not only hard wearing but also possesses great aesthetic appeal: like a lighter version of oak, but with a finer grain pattern.  The grain is largely straight, but does feature some interesting curly formations that add extra character to the worktop surface.
       </p>
       <p>
        The other new option, our black oak worktops, are  as the name suggests  a darker form of our oak worktops.  Made from the same high-quality European oak as is used for our standard oak worktops, black oak is subjected to a special treatment process which darkens the wood throughout the full depth of the worktop, creating a darker and more luxurious surface that is a great alternative to
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/Wenge_Worktops.html">
         wenge
        </a>
        or
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/Black_American_Walnut_Worktops.html">
         black American walnut
        </a>
        worktops.
       </p>
       <p>
        As well as these two new species, we have also added a range of Deluxe worktops, including popular species such as oak, iroko, zebrano and both types of walnut.  These Deluxe worktops feature considerably wider 90mm staves that serve to better show off the elegant colour and grain of our natural timbers.
       </p>
       <p>
        Finally, we have also added a few new full stave species to our worktop collection:
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/full-stave-wenge-worktops.html">
         full stave wenge
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/full-stave-zebrano-worktops.html">
         full stave zebrano
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/full-stave-ash-worktops.html">
         full stave ash
        </a>
        .  These beautiful new worktops feature staves up to 90mm wide that run the full length of the worktop  as lavish as it gets, and sure to be a stunning centrepiece in any kitchen!
       </p>
       <p>
        To see any of our worktops up close, we invite you to order one of our
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/worktop_samples.html">
         worktop samples
        </a>
        for just 5 including delivery.  Each sample is cut from existing worktop stock and oiled on one side to give you a good representation of the finished product.

        Spaces that are filled with the luxurious materials that make up our kitchens  such as our solid oak kitchen cabinets and range of solid hardwood worktops  deserve equally rich colours to help them stand out from the crowd.
       </p>
       <p>
        To round off April, we are looking at a palette from Farrow &amp; Ball that features three opulent shades, all of which are ideal for a variety of design combinations in
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/">
         oak kitchens
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Incarnadine is arguably Farrow &amp; Balls most imposing shade of red, whilst Studio Green looks almost black in some lighting, and a sumptuous dark green in well-lit interiors.  Lastly, Vert de Terre, which was our Colour of the Month in
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/cabinets_blog/solid-oak-kitchens-vert-de-terre-mays-colour-month/">
         May 2014
        </a>
        , is best described as a complex green that is lighter than Farrow &amp; Balls Mizzle, and less stony than Pigeon, putting it firmly at the top of our Accents collection.
       </p>
       <p>
        Consider painting cabinet doors in the dark red tones of Incarnadine to complement lighter hardwood worktops such as our
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/Oak_Worktops.html">
         oak worktops
        </a>
        , whilst frontals painted in Vert de Terre suit a full-toned choices such as our
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/Walnut_Worktops.html">
         walnut worktops
        </a>
        or
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/Iroko_Worktops.html">
         iroko worktops
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Studio Green is an ideal accent colour, but can also be used to paint cabinet frontals if combined with lighter colours, as it can absorb light in darker kitchens.  Consider mixing it with
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/Maple_Worktops.html">
         maple worktops
        </a>
        or even our
        <a href="http://www.solidwoodkitchencabinets.co.uk/Wood_Worktops/Bamboo_Worktops.html">
         bamboo worktops
        </a>
        in an ultra-modern kitchen setting.
       </p>
