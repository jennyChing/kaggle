           <h1 itemprop="name">
            Mexican Rice Skillet
           </h1>
           <small>
            <i class="fa fa-user">
            </i>
            Erin S. | Dinners, Dishes, and Desserts
            <i class="fa fa-calendar">

          </header>
          <div class="hidden-print" id="post_social">
           <div class="pinit">
            <a data-pin-color="red" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" data-pin-height="28" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/">

          <p class="intro">
           This Mexican rice skillet is a one pot meal you'll make again and again. Especially because you can make it in under 30 minutes!
          </p>
          <div class="body" itemprop="articleBody">
           <div class="thumbnail" style="max-width:630px;margin:0 auto 10px;text-align:center;">
            <span>
             <a href="/gallery/mexican-rice-skillet-photo/">
              <div class="image_wrapper loading" style="padding-bottom:66.66666666666666%;">
               <img alt="Mexican Rice Skillet Photo" data-pin-description="Mexican Rice Skillet is an easy dinner after a busy day. So easy." data-pin-media="http://images.foodfanatic.com/iu/s--xx_o8fIM--/t_full/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_75/v1415923398/mexican-rice-skillet-photo.jpg" data-pin-url="http://www.foodfanatic.com/2014/11/mexican-rice-skillet/" data-src="http://images.foodfanatic.com/iu/s--QO6xjC-Z--/t_xlarge_l/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_75/v1415923398/mexican-rice-skillet-photo.jpg" itemprop="image" src="http://assets.foodfanatic.com/assets/loader-bc361980c1727a785b0adf18f46a0597.gif"/>
              </div>
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            I am kind of in love with skillet dinners lately, and I'm guessing I can't be the only one. They are so quick and easy to make, plus you can change them up in so many different ways.
            <a href="http://www.foodfanatic.com/2014/08/cheeseburger-macaroni/">
             Cheeseburger Macaroni Skillet
            </a>
            was the start of it. Mexican food is one of our favorites, so I switched it up and made an
            <a href="http://dinnersdishesanddesserts.com/enchilada-pasta-skillet/" target="_blank">
             Enchilada Pasta Skillet
            </a>
            that was a huge success. So this Mexican rice skillet was the next progression.
           </p>
           <div class="thumbnail" style="max-width:400px;margin:0 auto 10px;text-align:center;">
            <span>
             <a href="/gallery/mexican-rice-skillet-picture/">
              <div class="image_wrapper loading" style="padding-bottom:150.0%;">
               <img alt="Mexican Rice Skillet Picture" data-pin-description="Mexican Rice Skillet can be made all in one pot. Less dishes to wash!" data-pin-media="http://images.foodfanatic.com/iu/s--zaG-VzcT--/t_full/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_75/v1415923644/mexican-rice-skillet-picture.jpg" data-pin-url="http://www.foodfanatic.com/2014/11/mexican-rice-skillet/" data-src="http://images.foodfanatic.com/iu/s--A8TDzRj2--/t_xlarge_p/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_75/v1415923644/mexican-rice-skillet-picture.jpg" itemprop="image" src="http://assets.foodfanatic.com/assets/loader-bc361980c1727a785b0adf18f46a0597.gif"/>
              </div>
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Skillet dinners are great because you only get one pan dirty, and dinner is ready in about 25 minutes. On busy weeknights when getting dinner on the table is a struggle, you dont want to have a ton of clean up to do after dinner. So, one pan dinners are ideal. Between my sons basketball practice two nights a week, homework, and other activities some nights dinner is the last thing I want to think about. So it is really nice to have a few recipes in my arsenal, that I know everyone will eat, and come together quickly.
           </p>
           <div class="thumbnail" style="max-width:630px;margin:0 auto 10px;text-align:center;">
            <span>
             <a href="/gallery/mexican-rice-skillet-image/">
              <div class="image_wrapper loading" style="padding-bottom:64.28571428571429%;">
               <img alt="Mexican Rice Skillet Image" data-pin-description="Mexican Rice Skillet hardly takes any time at all. Dinner on the table in 30 minutes." data-pin-media="http://images.foodfanatic.com/iu/s--3RTmJ9r6--/t_full/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_75/v1415923781/mexican-rice-skillet-image.jpg" data-pin-url="http://www.foodfanatic.com/2014/11/mexican-rice-skillet/" data-src="http://images.foodfanatic.com/iu/s--PjdyJFip--/t_xlarge_l/f_auto,fl_lossy,q_75/v1415923781/mexican-rice-skillet-image.jpg" itemprop="image" src="http://assets.foodfanatic.com/assets/loader-bc361980c1727a785b0adf18f46a0597.gif"/>
              </div>
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Mexican rice skillet is so easy to customize to your families taste. I used corn kernels in mine, but you can use black beans if your family likes them. You can switch up the protein if you want too. Ground beef, turkey, chicken, or even a meat substitute would all work equally well. Plus, you can top it with any of your favorite taco toppings; tomatoes, avocado, black olives, sour cream, cilantro - you name it, it's good here. The leftovers are great for lunch the next day as well.
           </p>
           <div class="thumbnail" style="max-width:400px;margin:0 auto 10px;text-align:center;">
            <span>
             <a href="/gallery/mexican-rice-skillet-pic/">

              </div>
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
           <p>
            Serve Mexican rice skillet with a few tortilla chips and some fruit for a complete meal that will disappear quickly. If you've got a few extra minutes, this
            <a href="http://www.foodfanatic.com/recipes/winter-fruit-salad/">
             Winter fruit salad
            </a>
            would be fantastic too.
           </p>

              <h2 class="title">
               <a href="/recipes/mexican-rice-skillet-recipe/" itemprop="url">
                <span itemprop="name">
                 Mexican Rice Skillet Recipe
                </span>
               </a>
              </h2>
              <div class="bar">
               <div class="print">
                <a href="/recipes/mexican-rice-skillet-recipe/embed/">
                 <i class="fa fa-share">
                 </i>
                 Embed
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="details">
                <i class="fa fa-cogs">
                </i>
                <time datetime="PT5M" itemprop="prepTime">
                 5 m
                </time>
                <i class="fa fa-fire">
                </i>
                <time datetime="PT20M" itemprop="cookTime">
                 20 m
                </time>
                <i class="fa fa-user">
                </i>
                <span itemprop="recipeYield">
                 6 Servings
                </span>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="bottom">
               <div class="row">
                <div class="col-sm-8">
                 <h3>
                  Ingredients
                 </h3>
                 <ul class="ingredients">
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   1 pound lean ground beef
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   1 cup onion, finely chopped
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   1 packet taco seasoning
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   1 cup frozen corn
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   1 cup salsa
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   1 cup uncooked long grain white rice
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   2 cups chicken stock
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   1 10 ounce can ro-tel
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
                  </li>
                 </ul>
                 <b>
                  Toppings:
                 </b>
                 <ul class="ingredients">
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   black olives
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   diced tomatoes
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   avocado
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   sour cream
                  </li>
                  <li itemprop="ingredients">
                   fresh cilantro
                  </li>
                 </ul>
                 <h3>
                  Directions
                 </h3>
                 <div class="directions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                  <ol>
                   <li>
                    In a large skillet over medium high heat brown grown beef, breaking apart. Add onions, and cook until soft. Stir in taco seasoning until the meat is evenly coated.
                   </li>
                   <li>
                    Add corn kernels, salsa, rice, tomatoes, and chicken stock. Stir until well mixed. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce heat to a simmer. Cook for about 15 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through, and most of the liquid is absorbed.
                   </li>
                   <li>
                    Sprinkle cheese over the top of the dish. Cover and heat until cheese is melted.
                   </li>
                   <li>
                    Top with desired toppings to serve.
                   </li>
                  </ol>
                 </div>

                 Hi Liz,  I'm so sorry you're having trouble.  I double checked with Erin, and the long grain white rice called for in this recipe does not need to be "quick cooking"/parboiled rice - it simply needs to be any long grain white rice. You can use Jasmin rice if you want, but the majority of brands simply label it as "Long Grain White Rice".  Even without the added ingredients, any of these types of rice only need about 22 minutes to cook. The only way it wouldn't cook is if there wasn't enough liquid in the pan. If you added the 2 cups of chicken stock, plus the can of Ro-Tel tomatoes (undrained), as called for, there should be more than enough liquid to cook the recipe in the time allotted.
