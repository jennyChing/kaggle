          <p>
           <a href="http://issuesurgery.com/warning-signs-of-a-heart-attack/heart-attack-on-the-street/" rel="attachment wp-att-1595">
            <img alt="Heart Attack on the street" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1595" height="367" src="http://issuesurgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Heart-Attack-on-the-street.jpg" width="647"/>

          <p>
           Learning and understanding the Warning Signs of a Heart Attack can save someones life someday. So if you are or anybody near you have history of heart attack, it would be a good thing if you learn more about these signs, so that when it occur youll be steps ahead and able to provide the needed help much faster.
          </p>
          <p>
           Even though certain signs may different between one to another person, but generally Warning Signs of a Heart Attack involve chess discomfort, discomfort feeling in around the upper body area, heavy breathing, cold sweat, nausea, and light-head that may lead to unconsciousness. Some people usually dont recognize this as signs of heart attack and that is where everything usually got out of control.
          </p>
          <p>
           Also, learning about these Warning Signs of a Heart Attack would also save you tons of trouble, given the fact that it will allow you to get early notice of something wrong in you. Even if youre not sure about it, you may consider checking yourself up to the nearest clinic for sure. During the sudden heart attack, the best thing you can do is to call 911 for immediate help. Be sure to tell them your situation and the exact location to ease dispatchers to find you.
          </p>
