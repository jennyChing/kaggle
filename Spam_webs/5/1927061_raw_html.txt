       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff
       </h1>
       <h2 class="entry-meta">
        by
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/author/administrator/" rel="author" title="Posts by Dria">
         Dria
        </a>
        
        <time class="published" datetime="2014-07-04">
         July 4, 2014
        </time>
        
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/#comments">
         16 Comments
        </a>
       </h2>
      </header>
      <div class="entry-content">
       <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
       <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
        <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
        </script>
        <!-- Top Banner -->
        <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-9955246945132819" data-ad-slot="6588456572" style="display:inline-block;width:728px;height:90px">
        </ins>
        <script>
         (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
        </script>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/diohomeimprovementsrestorationhardwareknockoff/" rel="attachment wp-att-4006">
         <img alt="Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4006" height="492" src="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DIOHOMEIMPROVEMENTSRestorationHardwareKnockoff.jpg" width="853"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        I love Restoration Hardwares Deconstructed furniture, I have wanted to do a knockoff for a while. One of my sisters found a cane chair at a thrift store a while back for $5.00 she knew I was looking for one so she picked it up for me. I lived with it as it was for a while but the fabric was aged looking, I made the mistake of painting the fabric to make it look cleaner. That was a fail! It made the fabric really hard and uncomfortable and it made taking the fabric off the chair very hard when I decided to reupholster it. My advice if you ever plan to reupholster furniture dont paint the fabric!
       </p>
       <p>
        I started out by sanding the chair. I did this by hand, if I had used a power sander it would have gone a lot faster. After sanding my neighbor and I pulled the fabric off the chair, carefully remembering how each piece went back together. I also removed the cane from the chair.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        Before Sanding
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/diorestorationhardwarebeforesanding/" rel="attachment wp-att-4007">
         <img alt="Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff - Before" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4007" height="459" src="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DIORestorationHardwarebeforesanding.jpg" width="768"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        After Sanding
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/diorestorationhardwaredchairsanded/" rel="attachment wp-att-4008">
         <img alt="Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff - Before" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4008" height="459" src="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DIORestorationHardwaredChairsanded.jpg" width="625"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The color was a little too red for me after sanding so I mixed some paint together and watered it down to make a paint stain. I used white paint and some brown paint to get the desired brownish gray color I was going for.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        After staining
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/diorestorationhardwaredchairsanded2/" rel="attachment wp-att-4009">
         <img alt="Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff - After staining" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4009" height="409" src="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DIORestorationHardwaredChairsanded2.jpg" width="635"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        I also applied a paste wax after the stain dried to protect the finish. Once the frame was done I moved onto the upholstered pieces.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        The back buttons were pretty easy to get out. After I had the back taken apart I laid my new linen fabric over the foam backing to make small cuts for the buttons.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/restorationhardwareknockoffbeforebacking/" rel="attachment wp-att-4010">
         <img alt="Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff - Before" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4010" height="560" src="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/RestorationHardwareKnockoffBeforeBacking.jpg" width="560"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/diorestorationhardwaredback5/" rel="attachment wp-att-4011">
         <img alt="Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff - Before" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4011" height="459" src="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DIORestorationHardwaredback5.jpg" width="768"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        I used a staple gun to reattach the fabric to the back, pinching the fabric where needed to make pleats.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        I covered the buttons over the existing buttons using fabric adhesive spray.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/diorestorationhardwaredbuttons/" rel="attachment wp-att-4012">
         <img alt="Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff - Before" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4012" height="459" src="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DIORestorationHardwaredbuttons.jpg" width="614"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        Using the original outer back as a template I cut the new fabric, attached the padding and stapled this to the frame.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <a href="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/2014/07/04/restoration-hardware-deconstructed-chair-knockoff/diorestorationhardwaredchairstripped/" rel="attachment wp-att-4013">
         <img alt="Restoration Hardware Deconstructed Chair Knockoff - Before" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4013" height="529" src="http://diohomeimprovements.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DIORestorationHardwaredchairstripped.jpg" width="652"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        Again using the original as a guide I cut new fabric and sewed the seat cushion.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
