       <header class="single-entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Finding My Way in Kindergarten Genius Hour
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span class="entry-date">
          06/Nov/2014
         </span>
         <span class="entry-author">
          by Denise Krebs
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="single-entry-content">
        <p>
         After teaching grades 7 and 8 for seven years, it was a challenge for me to go down to Kindergarten. The first few months, the way was treacherous. Now looking back, after eight months or so, I can say overall it has been a delight, and I know it was a gift I didnt even know I needed.
        </p>
        <p>
         I find I can practice what I learned in my Masters program; that is, teaching young children literacy. Most importantly, though, the children are 
         <a href="http://youtu.be/ToT4eN2LlIA" title="Debbie Clement's ">
          wonderful, marvelous, beautiful, magical, filled with curiosity and dreams
         </a>
         . (Lyrics by
         <a href="https://twitter.com/KweezleQueen" title="Debbie on Twitter">
          Debbie Clement
         </a>
         ) They are loving and open. They are learning sponges with big, growing brains.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          But how can I do genius hour with them?
         </em>
         I wondered. I loved the engaged ownership in junior highers when they were given a chance to learn what they wanted in what we call
         <a href="http://mrsdkrebs.edublogs.org/2013/01/19/a-year-of-genius-hour-what-have-i-learned/" title="A Year of Genius Hour">
          genius hour
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         As
         <a href="http://faigemeller.wordpress.com/" title="Faige's Blog">
          Faige Meller
         </a>
         has suggested, genius hour in kindergarten may look like a maker space. In this tweet, she says making is what kindergarteners do and, in fact, makers are who they are. (Be sure to read Krissys
         <a href="http://venspired.com/making-makes-learning/" title="Making Makes Learning">
          original post
         </a>
         too.)
        </p>
        <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" width="400">
         <p>
          RT "
          <a href="https://twitter.com/venspired">
           @venspired
          </a>
          : Making Makes Learning
          <a href="http://t.co/SQD0jQRMJp">
           http://t.co/SQD0jQRMJp
          </a>
          <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/makerspace?src=hash">
           #makerspace
          </a>
          <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/edchat?src=hash">
           #edchat
          </a>
          <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/gtchat?src=hash">
           #gtchat
          </a>
          <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/maker?src=hash">
           #maker
          </a>
          
          <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/geniushour?src=hash">
           #geniushour
          </a>
          That's who/what kinders are/do
         </p>
         <p>
           Faige Meller (@dubioseducator)
          <a href="https://twitter.com/dubioseducator/status/450092242472955904">
           March 30, 2014
          </a>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js">
         </script>
        </p>
        <p>
         I believed in making, but I didnt know much about Kindergarten. I had learned to trust
         <a href="https://twitter.com/dubioseducator" title="Faige on Twitter">
          Faige
         </a>
         , though, so when I saw her tweet last March, I began to run with her ideas in Kindergarten. I began collecting supplies and asking families to do so, as well. We have quite a collection, and we go through a lot of materials.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 145px">
         <img alt="" height="240" src="https://farm4.staticflickr.com/3944/15539671039_ccff5e671d_m.jpg" width="135"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          A genius hour sculpture
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         When I learn something new about brain research,
         <a href="http://mrsdkrebs.edublogs.org/2014/08/11/reading-about-learning-brains-and-metacognition/" title="Reading about learning, brains, and metacognition">
          I share it with my Kindergarteners
         </a>
         too. They are not too young, and even though Im speaking a foreign language to them (they are native Arabic speakers), they understand enough. They know they are capable and creative, and as they create, they get smarter. And they know that
         <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/education/2014/sep/04/what-happens-to-the-brain-language-learning" title="What happens in the brain when you learn a language?">
          as they learn two languages, they get bigger brains
         </a>
         too!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 330px">
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/mrsdkrebs/15701855356/">
          <img alt="" height="180" src="https://farm4.staticflickr.com/3939/15701855356_710942527c_n.jpg" width="320"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          A matchbox car garage
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         So, we are definitely still
         <em>
          making
         </em>
         our way (pun intended), but weve had some huge successes. After
         <a href="http://krebs.edublogs.org/2014/10/27/do-you-like-our-repurposed-juice-bottles/" title="Do you like our repurposed juice bottles?">
          we made a small couch
         </a>
         for our reading center as a group project, one boy took on the task of making a very small chair with the ten juice bottles we had recently accumulated. He needed lots of help, but thats where I came in handy, helping to wrestle the juice bottles and operate the hot glue gun and packaging tape. He was the maker. I was the
         <em>
          sous
         </em>
         maker taking my orders from him.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 190px">
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/mrsdkrebs/15106190283/">
          <img alt="" height="320" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8135/15106190283_754a2a5961_n.jpg" width="180"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The little chair finished and ready for a cover.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Genius hour in Kindergarten. Its happening. We are calling it that, we are making and learning, but I am always open to suggestions you might have for helping us do it better!
        </p>
        <h3>
         Please leave a link in the comments to your primary genius hour projects and process. Or share on Twitter with the hashtag #PrimaryGH.
        </h3>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 510px">

         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Our growing collection of maker furniture
         </p>
