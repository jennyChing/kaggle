     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Oasis of the Zombies
       </b>
      </i>
      (originally called
      <i>
       <b>
        L'Abme des Morts-Vivants
       </b>
      </i>
      /
      <i>
       The Abyss of the Living Dead
      </i>
      ) is a 1982 film directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Franco" title="Jess Franco">
       Jess Franco
      </a>
      for French producer Marius Lesoeur.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oasis_of_the_Zombies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The plot involves
      <a href="/wiki/Treasure_hunting" title="Treasure hunting">
       treasure hunters
      </a>
      who track down a lost fortune in
      <a href="/wiki/Nazi_gold" title="Nazi gold">
       Nazi gold
      </a>
      in the desert, only to discover that the treasure is still guarded by the
      <a href="/wiki/Afrika_Korps" title="Afrika Korps">
       Afrika Korps
      </a>
      soldiers transporting it, who have become
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zombie_(fiction)" title="Zombie (fiction)">
       zombies
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Release">
       Release
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oasis_of_the_Zombies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The film has also been released over the years as
      <i>
       Bloodsucking Nazi Zombies
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       El deierto de los zombies
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       The Grave of the Living Dead
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       The Treasure of the Living Dead
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dendle_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-dendle-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Franco filmed a "Spanish version" simultaneously with the French version (under the title
      <i>
       <b>
        La tumba de los Muertos Vivientes
       </b>
      </i>
      ), probably to placate Lina Romay (since the French producer had chosen other actors to be in the "real version"). That version has never been released outside of Spain, if at all.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oasis_of_the_Zombies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Writing in
      <i>
       The Zombie Movie Encyclopedia
      </i>
      , academic
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Dendle" title="Peter Dendle">
       Peter Dendle
      </a>
      stated the film has had a negative reception, but it "presents a simple, unhurried, and unpretending appreciation of zombies and their habitat, a fresh and provocative desert landscape."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dendle_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-dendle-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Ian Jane of
      <a href="/wiki/DVD_Talk" title="DVD Talk">
       DVD Talk
      </a>
      rated it 3/5 stars and wrote, "It's terrible, but somehow trance inducing in its own bizarre way."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Gordon Sullivan of
      <a href="/wiki/DVD_Verdict" title="DVD Verdict">
       DVD Verdict
      </a>
      wrote, "It's really a mess of tepid Eurosleaze masquerading as a zombie flick, and it doesn't even do that well."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
