     <h1>
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/2011/05/french-toast-casserole/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link">
       <img alt="French Toast Casserole" class="ttf" height="22" src="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/plugins/ttftitles/cache/53085a7458686eaeda986f46fbcb9c3c.gif" width="251"/>
      </a>
     </h1>
     <h3>
      May 13, 2011 |
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/category/breads/" title="Breads">
       Breads
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/category/breakfast/" title="Breakfast">
       Breakfast
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/category/popular/" title="Popular">
       Popular
      </a>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_3230.jpg">
       <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6585" height="332" src="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_3230.jpg" title="french toast casserole" width="500"/>
      </a>
      Im offering up this incredibly delicious brunch dish on Friday so if you have any sort of breakfast or brunch plans this weekend, you can still include it. I made this last weekend for Mothers Day brunch at our house, and it was a huge hit. And it just happens to be perfect for a crowd because you assemble it the day before and then bake it the next day.
      <span id="more-6581">
      </span>
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_3226.jpg">
       <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6582" height="332" src="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_3226.jpg" title="buttered casserole" width="500"/>
      </a>
      Start by buttering a 913 casserole dish really liberally.
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_3227.jpg">
       <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6583" height="332" src="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_3227.jpg" title="french toast casserole bread slices" width="500"/>
      </a>
      Then dip the bread slices in the egg mixture and layer them in the casserole dish snugly in a double layer. Note that I used 6 bolillo bread rolls to make mine, a classic Mexican bread made with just a touch of sugar. Brioche or challah bread would work also.
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_32281.jpg">
       <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6591" height="332" src="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_32281.jpg" title="french toast casserole" width="500"/>
      </a>
      After you place all the bread in the casserole dish, pour the remaining egg mixture over the top. Top with the sugar-nut mixture and bake until set and bubbly.
      <a href="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_3229.jpg">
       <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6587" height="332" src="http://cookingwithmichele.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_3229.jpg" title="French toast casserole" width="500"/>
      </a>
      Serve with real maple syrup for a breakfast youre not soon to forget!
      <br/>

      <div class="ERSIngredients">
       <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
        Ingredients
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 tablespoons butter, softened
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         20 slices bread, 1" thick
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         8 large eggs
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 cups half and half
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 cup whole milk
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 tablespoons sugar
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 teaspoon vanilla extract
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          teaspoon cinnamon
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          teaspoon nutmeg
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          pound butter
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 cup brown sugar, packed
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 cup chopped pecans
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          teaspoon cinnamon
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          teaspoon ground nutmeg
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div class="ERSClear">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="ERSInstructions">
       <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
        Instructions
       </div>
       <ol>
        <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         Butter a 9x13 casserole dish with the 2 tablespoons of butter. Beat together eggs, half and half, milk, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon and nutmeg. Dip each slice into the egg mixture then layer bread slices in pan in 2-3 layers. Pour remaining egg mixture over bread slices and between the gaps. Combine remaining ingredients - butter through nutmeg - in a food processor and pulse until nuts are chopped and ingredients are well combined. Sprinkle over the top of the casserole. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
        </li>
        <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Bake uncovered until puffed up and golden and eggs are cooked, about 40 minutes. Serve with maple syrup.
        </li>
       </ol>
