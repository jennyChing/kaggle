              <p>
               US Sen. John Cornyn (R-Texas) proposed a bill last Friday that would allow people with gun permits to carry a concealed firearm in all 50 states.
              </p>
              <p>
               According to
               <a href="http://www.guns.com/2014/01/13/texas-senator-proposes-bill-nationwide-concealed-carry-reciprocity-video/" rel="nofollow">
                Guns.com
               </a>
               ,
               <em>
               </em>
               the
               <em>
                Constitutional Concealed Carry Reciprocity Act of 2014
               </em>
               would basically make concealed carry permits like drivers licenses.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               This bill strengthens two of our nations most fundamental rights, ensuring law-abiding gun owners can lawfully carry their weapons into like-minded states, while respecting the rights of states to adopt laws that are best-suited for the people of that state," said Sen. Cornyn in a
               <a href="http://www.cornyn.senate.gov/public/index.cfm?p=NewsReleases&amp;ContentRecord_id=09eec362-d3af-43f2-bb6c-0df1ce95a4ae" rel="nofollow">
                press release
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               "This is an important affirmation of the Second Amendment and one that has been a top priority of law-abiding gun owners in Texas for some time. It is time to get this done, added Sen. Cornyn.
              </p>
              <p>
               Gun permit holders would still have to obey every state's different law on concealed carry and guns.
              </p>
              <div class="in-content-ad" id="in-content-ad">
              </div>
              <div class="in-content-ad" id="in-content-ad-ssl">
              </div>
              <p>
               Just like traffic laws, its my responsibility to know as a driver what those laws are going to be in another state, San Angelo Police Department Assistant Chief Jeff Fant, who supports the measure, told
               <a href="http://www.conchovalleyhomepage.com/story/concealed-carry-reciprocity-act-proposed/d/story/jM772A1rzkm3vkMCJulbDw" rel="nofollow">
                Concho Valley
               </a>
               .
               <br/>
               <br/>
               It would make it a little more consistent across the nation and to persons from Texas crossing state lines, and since we do cross state lines quite a bit, especially in the southwest areas and such, it would make it a little easier for citizens that do travel back and forth to carry firearms with them, added Chief Fant.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Sources:
