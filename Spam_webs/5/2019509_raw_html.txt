              <h1 class="blog-title">
               <a href="http://epfilms.tv/nikon-d600-review/">
                Nikon D600 Review
               </a>
              </h1>
              <div class="clear">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="blog-content">
              <p>
               With a full-frame, 24 megapixels, and price to match, the D600 is a DSLR aimed at a wide range of photography enthusiasts. In fact, it is significantly more affordable than the D800, its big brother, and is basically on par with the EOS 6D, Canons 21-megapixel full-frame DSLR.
              </p>
              <h2>
               Nikon D600 Review &amp; Intro
              </h2>
              <div style="max-width:820px;">
               <iframe height="500" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/z1-BD9S08UQ?wmode=transparent" width="820">
               </iframe>
              </div>
              <p>
               For this
               <strong>
                Nikon D600 review
               </strong>
               , we have identified the following key specifications of this particular model.
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Reasonably priced full-frame
               </li>
               <li>
                Dimensions: 5.5  4.4  3.2 inches (141mm x 113mm x 82mm).
               </li>
               <li>
                Weight: 1.6 lbs. (760 g)  no battery, camera body only
               </li>
               <li>
                Full-frame CMOS sensor at 24.3 megapixels (10.5 mp if in DX-format crop mode)
               </li>
               <li>
                Full-HD video at 1080p30
               </li>
               <li>
                Continuous shooting at a maximum of 5.5 frames per second
               </li>
               <li>
                ISO level at 100-6400 (which can be expanded to ISO 50-25,600 equivalent)
               </li>
               <li>
                3.2in LCD screen, 921k dots
               </li>
               <li>
                39-point AF system with 9 cross-type AF points
               </li>
               <li>
                Video recording via HDMI, Uncompressed
               </li>
               <li>
                Audio monitoring (using a headphone jack) when in movie mode
               </li>
               <li>
                Viewfinder with single-axis electronic level; duel-axis when in live view (pitch and roll)
               </li>
              </ul>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               The D600 offers similar operational ergonomics and build quality as that of the renowned DX-format D7000. The D600 is nothing short of a significant camera, even with Canons EOS 6D giving it a tight competition.
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Nikond600reviews" class="size-medium wp-image-1446 alignleft" src="http://epfilms.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/nikon-d600-review-300x245.jpg"/>
               The D600 can match or exceed other full-frame DSLRs in terms of pixel count, at a certain price reach that makes it easier for most enthusiasts to acquire. In fact, it is interesting to note that the D600 is Nikons way of significantly undercutting Sonys prices for its full-frame products, such as the Cyber-shot RX1 and SLT-A99.
              </p>
              <p>
               Nikon D600 reviews reveal that the unit had a list price of approximately 2099 USD during its launch, making it one of the most reasonably priced cameras for its full-frame specs. This gave the D600 to supplant older DX-format brothers in terms of choice upgrades for the users of D7000, D3200, and D5100 models.
              </p>
              <p>
               However, the D600 isnt a no-frills unit despite its relatively low price tag. With features of full resolution shooting at 5.5 fps and viewfinder coverage at 100%, as well as video capture at full HD with the option for recording via HDMI any uncompressed footage and a 39-point AF system thats customizable, the D600 could have been a camera that costs a whole lot more  what with its impressive features. Fortunately for us, the D600 is quite affordable.
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="D600 Review and Test" class="size-medium wp-image-1467 alignleft" src="http://epfilms.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/nikond600-300x205.jpg"/>
               Ergonomics-wise, Nikons D600 will feel familiar to some extent when compared to its brothers. For example, its UI is quite similar to what the DX-format D7000 has, going so far as to have the same 39-point AF array. When it comes to functionality, the D800 (the older brother) has a lot in common with that of the D600, especially in terms of video-related specs.
              </p>
              <p>
               Another one of the great things that come with the D600 is that its seemingly the first Nikon DSLR to get the auto white balance (AWB) and exposure correctly. This means that youll rarely need to tweak the AWB or add exposure compensation regardless of the condition of your shoot.
              </p>
              <p>
               Simply put, this particular DSLR model should be worth investing in, if this Nikon D600 review is any indication. We hope the information provided will help you make an informed buying decision when you start your search for the camera of your dreams  although we bet youd love the D600 should you choose to go for it.
              </p>
