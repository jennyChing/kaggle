        <div class="article-content clearfix">
         <p>
          BetterOffDEAD has been out for just shy of a year now, and Brooklyns Flatbush ZOMBiES are feeling the pressure from their legion of hungry fans to deliver the next offering.
         </p>
         <p>
          In a clever move to stimulate their social media presence and hold listeners over until the full length is ready, the trio created a double-whammy subscription service where fans can get their FZ fix on a weekly basis, all while still prepping for the festival season and the new album. Somehow, in between the international flights, live shows, and tracking for Its All A Matter Of Perspective, Meechy Darko made just enough time to talk with me about the aforementioned and more.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           How are you doing?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Im all right. Been better.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Yeah? You a little jetlagged?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Jetlagged. Small Japanese beds shred my back. 13 hour flights shred my back. Trying to heal, you know?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           How were the shows while you were over there?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          We did a show with Mishka over therethat shit was beautiful, man. They said over there at the venue that was the best turnout theyd seen in a long, long time. Had a nice little line. It was good, though. One of the best places on earth.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Lets go into the FZ vaults and talk a little about the early days.Who do you feel had your back in the beginning, or put you on, so to speak?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Shit, nobody. When we first got into it, we kind of just gave it to everybody at the same time. Just put it out there. And in the beginning I wasnt really listening to what anybody was saying, to be honest. It was very therapeutic. It was like, Im making this cause I need to make this.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What about Pep [FZs manager]? When did he join the team?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Pep! We met Pep the first time we went to SXSW. He helped bring us out there. We really didnt know each other that well. He heard the music, he got a chance to see us perform live and that was mad early in the game. We had, like, two videos out. And we just gravitated toward each other since then. He seen passion. He seen its not just about trying to be famous, or just fuckin around. We love music. We love to do this.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           So have you guys gone down to SXSW every year since?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          We didnt go this year. Stayed my black ass home. Nobody from Beast Coast went! Joey [Bada$$] didnt go, UA didnt go, and we didnt go.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Thats something else I wanted to talk to you about. Can you break down for us what Beast Coast means? Is it a sound? A brand?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Beast Coast is a brotherhood. Beast Coast is a movement, ideology. Beast Coast is formed of Flatbush ZOMBiES, The Underachievers and Pro Era. Were from the same neighborhood; were cut from the same cloth; drink the same water; dodge the same bullets. You understand what Im saying. And it just so happened to grow at the same time, and found the same middle ground, which was music. Steez, R.I.P., actually created the terminology, and we just ran with it since then. R.I.P. Steez. So yeah, its a brotherhood. My brothers family.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           That was very sad.
          </strong>
          <strong>
           In addition to your peers in Beast Coast, FZ has grown alongside a number of other artistsDanny Brown, Action Bronson, Juicy Jwho seem tove come up quickly in the last year. Do you guys feel like youre on that same trajectory? Do you ever feel overlooked at times?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
          </strong>
          I live by this quote, and I die by this quote: You never really know how good the movie is when youre in it. I cant really tell you, because Im inside. I cant really explain. Sometimes you may think that people dont know, but they may know and just not understand it yet. I do what I do. And as far as my peers being successfulits not a race! The only person Im racing is myself. Thats really what it boils down to. Im happy for everybody, man. Theyre friends of mine. Im happy to see pictures of [Action] Bronson throwing people off stage. Im happy to see them killin shows, cause honestly this is a dream for a lot of people. A lot of us need this, man. Its bringing people together and thats all Im really about at the end of the day. Im not about being #1. Ill be #1 to somebody.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Flatbush ZOMBiES are from Brooklyn, where street art was basically born. Did you guys grow up in that scene at all?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          I can shout out this one dude from my hood. He tagshis name is Dink. Dinkus. I used to see his shit when I was young for
          <em>
           years
          </em>
          , like, Who is this dude? And then years later, I actually got to meet him.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What was your tag?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Back then, I was King B [
          <em>
           laughs
          </em>
          ].
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Back to the music from FZ- what are you guys working on right now? When are we going to see another release?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Well, right now we just announced this little thing that were doing for our fans since a lot of them complain about us not releasing shit. Were in the microwave era where people want a new song every second! Were very old school were doing it on our own. So we decided to come up with something where on Mondays we release visuals for our fans, on Tuesdays were going to release merchandise for our fans, on Wednesdays we do what we want, on Thursdays we release posters to our fans of exclusive photos that no one else has. Erick [Arc Elliott] is actually a very, very talented photographer. If people dont know this, they will know. Hes been taking pictures of our journey since the beginning and we have beautiful, beautiful photos. And then on Fridays were gonna release new music. Thats the Day of The Dead. And were gonna do that in succession for a few weeks, just to give people something.
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Are there any collaborations or features on any of those tracks?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          I cant let ya know! Expect the expected
          <em>
           and
          </em>
          the unexpected. But all that is leading up to our project thats gonna be coming out later called Its All A Matter Of Perspective.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Do you guys think this material has the potential to shape hip-hops future?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Answering questions like that can come off so vain, but I dont care. Remember how you used the term overlooked? A lot of times when things are overlooked, years later people look back and then they realize. I hate to use other peoples music as an example, but you know [Kanye Wests] 808s and Heartbreak came out and everybody was like, What the fuck is goin on? And hip-hop music hasnt really been the same since that. And thats just an example, but people had that same reaction when they heard us.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its not easily digested, but thats fine. And I feel like, it may not be our music
          <em>
           per se
          </em>
          , but we can plant a seed that could lead to the change like you just said. What we really want to bring to the table is the passion. A lot of people are not really making hip-hop music with passion, and thats the problem. I dont want this genre looked at as a joke anymore. I dont want to not be able to be put on festivals because our live shows are not looked at seriously because people are rapping on mp3s. I want to bring this to the next level. People really need to understand its not a joke.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Are there any artists coming up right now that you
           <em>
            do
           </em>
           think are doing it right? With passion and a serious attitude?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Id say Erick Arc Elliott! I think Erick has been overlooked for awhile because Erick has produced for himself from before Flatbush ZOMBiES. He has projects under his belt, you know what I mean? Not many people can say they produced full projects for themselves at 17 years old, and if you go back and listen to the music its good quality music. Its personal music. I think my friend is very, very overlookedmore overlooked than the group. Thats just me.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           How has growing up in Brooklyn around hip-hop and street culture affected your style?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          I grew up on that era of gritty-ness. In the beginning as a youth thats really what I gravitated toward. Thats what I used to love: the Wu-Tang, the gold teeth, the Kims, the Jesus piece. That was the style. And then as I got older, I started to grow and gain more culture. Not to say theres anything wrong with that stuff, but it grew into
          <em>
           my
          </em>
          style and not the people who I was listening tos style.
         </p>
         <p>
          As a teenager, we would wear the
          <em>
           weirdest
          </em>
          shit. We would be like, Everybodys wearin this? Were gonna find a way to change this into that and that into this. It was always that creative thing with clothes. Clothes are an expression of self, and people need to understand that. Clothes are not about fitting in, man. Like, Im gonna wear tie-dye because I feel vibrant and colorful, not because its a trend. When I wear all black, you know why Im wearing all black. Thats what people need to understand.
         </p>
         <p>
          Style is very funny to me, because some people could be wearin the same thing Im wearin and look like shit, and I could look marvelous. You know why? Because its for me. Im very strange. Im a psychedelic man, so material things are very, very complicated for me to discuss, especially as a rapper because materials are very important. People judge what they see first. And I definitely feel like I address it pretty well. Its very hard, I try not to even really talk about that stuff. If you listen to our music we dont really mention it much. Like I said before, were poor. But at the same time, I love to get fly!
          <br/>
          <strong style="line-height: 1.5em;">
           <br/>
           Anything thats a must have before you leave the house? Or maybe before you play a show?
           <br/>
          </strong>
          I feel weird when I perform without my gold fronts and a 40 oz.
         </p>
