        <h1>
         The Ultimate First Time Homebuyers Guide
        </h1>
        <h2>
        </h2>
        <p>
         This resource guide, compiled by a team at The Home Depot, is designed to assist first-time homebuyers through the process of evaluating a homes condition and understanding some of the most common repairs and renovations a house may require.
        </p>
        <div class="meta clearfix">
         <span class="author-link">
          <a href="http://blog.coldwellbanker.com/author/lindsay_listanski/" rel="author" title="Posts by Lindsay Listanski">
           Lindsay Listanski
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="post-date">
          Oct 21, 2013
         </span>
        </div>
        <p>
         Presented by
         <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/b/webapp/catalog/servlet/HomePageView?storeId=10051&amp;catalogId=10053&amp;langId=-1">
          The Home Depot
         </a>
         Taking the step from renter to homeowner is one of the most empowering and memorable moments in a persons life. Before investing savings and committing to a mortgage agreement, however, its important to thoroughly inspect the home you want to buy and to understand the extent and cost of renovations it may need. This resource guide, compiled by a team at
         <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/">
          The Home Depot
         </a>
         , is designed to assist first-time homebuyers through the process of evaluating a homes condition and understanding some of the most common repairs and renovations a house may require. The guide spans topics that include foundations,
         <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/h_d1/N-5yc1vZar6r/h_d2/Navigation?catalogId=10053&amp;langId=-1&amp;storeId=10051">
          roofing
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/h_d1/N-5yc1vZbqew/h_d2/Navigation?catalogId=10053&amp;langId=-1&amp;storeId=10051">
          plumbing
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/webapp/catalog/servlet/ContentView?pn=SV_HS_Heating_Cooling_Systems_Installation&amp;storeId=10051&amp;langId=-1&amp;catalogId=10053">
          heating and cooling
         </a>
         and the
         <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/h_d1/N-5yc1vZarcd/h_d2/Navigation?catalogId=10053&amp;langId=-1&amp;storeId=10051">
          electrical system
         </a>
         .
         <span id="more-15502">
         </span>
         <b>
          What to Look For During Your Home Inspection
         </b>
         <i>
          From the foundation to the roof to the electrical system, understand the basics during your walkthrough.
          <a href="http://www.coldwellbanker.com/real_estate/learn/what_to_look_for_during_your_home_inspection" target="_blank">
           Click Here
          </a>
         </i>
         <b>
          1. Is the home structurally secure?
         </b>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="267" src="http://blog.coldwellbanker.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/CB-1Homebuyers-Guide-House-with-Magnifying-glass.jpg" width="400"/>
         <b>
          DIY Home Inspector Checklist: 25 Places to Watch
         </b>
         <i>
          Before signing a contract, look out for the most common (and costly) problems
         </i>
         .
         <a href="http://www.popularmechanics.com/home/improvement/interior/4271761" target="_blank">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          Six Signs that the Home Could Be a Lemon
         </b>
         <i>
          Look out for common signs of hidden major problems.
         </i>
         <a href="http://realestate.msn.com/article.aspx?cp-documentid=15478195" target="_blank" title="Signs your home could be a lemon">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          Signs of Foundation Problems to Look Out For
         </b>
         <i>
          A home without a solid foundation can be a serious money pit.
         </i>
         <a href="http://www.houselogic.com/home-advice/foundations/understanding-foundation-problems/#" target="_blank">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          How to Assess the Severity of a Home Foundation Problem
         </b>
         <i>
          Notice cracks or fissures? Evaluate the extent of the damage before proceeding.
         </i>
         <a href="http://homeguides.sfgate.com/assess-severity-home-foundation-problem-31888.html" target="_blank" title="How to Assess the Severity of a Home Foundation Problem ">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          Identifying Termites and Termite Damage
         </b>
         <i>
          These pests can be tough to eradicate, and they can literally eat you out of house and home.
         </i>

          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          Look Out for Mold and Moisture
         </b>
         <i>
          Established mold can affect your familys health and require extensive renovations.
         </i>
         <a href="http://www.epa.gov/iedmold1/moldguide.html" target="_blank">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          How to Inspect Vinyl Siding
         </b>
         <i>
          Vinyl is affordable and attractive, but can hide damage underneath.
         </i>
         <a href="http://www.nachi.org/vinyl-siding-inspection.htm" target="_blank">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          2. Do You Have a Solid Roof Over Your Head?
         </b>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="263" src="http://blog.coldwellbanker.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/CB-2-Homebuyers-Guide-Roof.jpg" width="400"/>
         <b>
          Evaluate the Condition of the Roof
         </b>
         <i>
          From curling shingles to buckling, roof issues may be part of your negotiation process.
         </i>
         <a href="http://www.carlsonbrothers.com/warningsigns.html" target="_blank">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          Seven Signs That You Need a New Roof
         </b>
         <i>
          Look out for these common issues atop your new home.
         </i>
         <a href="http://www.angieslist.com/articles/7-signs-you-need-new-roof.htm" target="_blank">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          How to Find (and Fix) Leaks in a Roof
         </b>
         <i>
          Dont be caught off-guard by the first rainstorm in your new home.
         </i>
         <a href="http://www.familyhandyman.com/roof/repair/roof-repair-how-to-find-and-fix-roof-leaks" target="_blank">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          How to Replace a Roof  VIDEO
         </b>
         <i>
          Understand what it takes to repair and replace your new homes roof.
         </i>
         <a href="http://www.finehomebuilding.com/how-to/video/replacing-roof-series-introduction.aspx" target="_blank">
          Click Here
         </a>
         <b>
          3. Big Ticket Plumbing Issues to Inspect
         </b>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="265" src="http://blog.coldwellbanker.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/CB-3-Homebuyers-Guide-Rusty-Pipe.jpg" width="400"/>
         <b>
          Home Plumbing Inspection Checklist
         </b>
         <i>
          Get the big picture of how water flows through the home
