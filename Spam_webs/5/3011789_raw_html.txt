         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Our world is so full of wonders that new and amazing places are discovered every day. Different geographical locations, climatic conditions and even seasons offer the widest variety of natural wonders and other places you can hardly believe actually exist. Now I will introduce you some spots that Id like to visit when time and budget allow.

         <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
          Sisteron, France
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Sisteron is a commune in the Alpes-de-Haute-Provence department in the Provence-Alpes-Cte dAzur region in southeastern France. Sisteron is situated on the banks of the River Durance just after the confluence of the rivers Buch and Sasse. It is sometimes called the Gateway to Provence because it is in a narrow gap between two long mountain ridges. It is 135 km (84 mi) from Marseille.
          </span>
         </p>

         <h4>
          Staircase at Pailn del Diablo waterfall, Ecuador
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           The Paln del Diablo is a fairly large waterfall (located on the Pastaza River) just 30 minutes away from the town of Baos in Ecuador. It is considered to be one of the most popular attractions in the area. The name of the waterfall in Spanish (Paln del Diablo) means in English Cauldron of the Devil.
          </span>
         </p>

         <h4 style="text-align: left;">
          Swallows Nest, Ukraine
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           The Swallows Nest is a decorative castle located between Yalta and Alupka on the Crimean peninsula in southern Ukraine. It was built between 1911 and 1912 in Gaspra, on top of the 40-metre (130 ft) high Aurora Cliff, to a Neo-Gothic design by the Russian architect Leonid Sherwood.The castle overlooks the Cape of Ai-Todor on the Black Sea coast and is located near the remains of the Roman castrum of Charax. The Swallows Nest is one of the most popular visitor attractions in the Crimea, becoming the symbol of the Crimeas southern coastline.
          </span>
         </p>

         <h4>
          Haik Stairs, Hawaii
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           The Haik Stairs, also known as the Stairway to Heaven or Haik Ladder, is a steep hiking trail on the island of Oahu. The trail began as a wooden ladder spiked to the cliff on the south side of the Haik Valley. It was installed in 1942 to enable antenna cables to be strung from one side of the cliffs above Haik Valley to the other. A building to provide a continuous communication link between Wahiaw and Haik Valley Naval Radio Station was constructed at the peak of Puukeahiakahoe, elevation about 2,800 feet (850 m).
          </span>
         </p>

         <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
          Roman theater, Jordan
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The theatre was built during the reign of Antonius Pius (138-161 CE). The large and steeply raked structure could seat about 6,000 people: built into the hillside, it was oriented north to keep the sun off the spectators. It was divided into three horizontal sections (diazomata). Side entrances (paradoi) existed at ground level, one leading to the orchestra and the other to the stage. Rooms behind these entrances now house the Jordanian Museum of Popular Traditions on the one side, and the Amman Folklore Museum on the other side. The highest section of seats in a theatre was (and still is) called The Gods. Although far from the stage, even there the sightlines are excellent, and the actors could be clearly heard, owing to the steepness of the cavea.
         </p>

         <h4 style="text-align: left;">
          Dangar Falls, Australia
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Dangar waterfalls are located just 1.2 kilometers north of the little town of Dorrigo, on the Bielsdown River. The falls are not that big but they are Visually attractive, and popular among photographers. Right after rainfalls they are raging with large amount of water and get quite amazing. It is possible to climb down the banks and walk along the river below the water falls.
          </span>
         </p>

          <a href="http://www.earthtraveling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Dangar-Falls-Australia.jpg">

         <h4 style="text-align: left;">
          Emerald Lake, Canada
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Emerald Lake is located in Yoho National Park, British Columbia, Canada. It is the largest of Yohos 61 lakes and ponds, as well as one of the parks premier tourist attractions. Emerald Lake Lodge, a high-end lodge perched on the edge of the lake, provides local accommodation. A 5.2 km (3.2 mi) hiking trail circuits the lake, the first half of which is accessible to wheelchairs and strollers. During the summer months, canoe rentals are available; in the winter, the lake is a popular cross country skiing destination.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.earthtraveling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/emerald-lake-canada.jpg">
           <img alt="emerald-lake-canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-746" height="751" src="http://www.earthtraveling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/emerald-lake-canada.jpg" width="1000"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.google.com/imgres?newwindow=1&amp;client=firefox-a&amp;hs=5nb&amp;sa=X&amp;rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&amp;tbm=isch&amp;tbnid=1R_V2p0bGWiFtM:&amp;imgrefurl=http://www.panoramio.com/photo/22122273&amp;docid=DHEGVSp-atZLjM&amp;imgurl=http://static.panoramio.com/photos/large/22122273.jpg&amp;w=1024&amp;h=768&amp;ei=ddd_UvLoJKff4QSn8ICoBQ&amp;zoom=1&amp;ved=1t:3588,r:83,s:0,i:339&amp;iact=rc&amp;page=3&amp;tbnh=189&amp;tbnw=247&amp;start=63&amp;ndsp=39&amp;tx=177&amp;ty=140&amp;biw=1920&amp;bih=905" target="_blank">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <h4 style="text-align: left;">
          Rovinj, Croatia
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Rovinj is a city in Croatia situated on the north Adriatic Sea with a population of 14,294 (as of 2011). Located on the western coast of the Istrian peninsula, it is a popular tourist resort and an active fishing port. Istriot, a Romance language once widely spoken in this part of Istria, is still spoken by some of the residents.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.earthtraveling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/rovinjcroatia.jpg">
           <img alt="rovinj,croatia" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-748" height="750" src="http://www.earthtraveling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/rovinjcroatia.jpg" width="1000"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.google.com/imgres?newwindow=1&amp;client=firefox-a&amp;hs=vFH&amp;sa=X&amp;rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&amp;tbs=simg:CAQSEgnQf8eF6Ggb7yH5mmfbxcgJcQ&amp;tbm=isch&amp;tbnid=GNyS9gwuEkmVTM:&amp;imgrefurl=http://www.happycharter.com/fr/show-boat/more-pictures/bid/70140/&amp;docid=YHzGTBPObxrb5M&amp;imgurl=http://www.happycharter.com/images/boats/70/1/4/big_0701400edf70939a.jpg&amp;w=1024&amp;h=768&amp;ei=MNl_UvbWBaXv4QTzwICYCw&amp;zoom=1&amp;ved=1t:3588,r:10,s:0,i:89&amp;iact=rc&amp;page=1&amp;tbnh=180&amp;tbnw=257&amp;tx=141&amp;ty=131&amp;biw=1920&amp;bih=905" target="_blank">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <h4>
          Sliding Rock, North Carolina
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Sliding Rock has been a popular cooling off spot in the North Carolina mountains for many years. Located in the Pisgah National Forest near Brevard, the natural 50-foot waterslide delights many thousands each summer. They make a splash in a pool and swim a few strokes to the shore, get in line, and do it all over again. Lifeguards supervise the fun during the peak summer months.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.earthtraveling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Sliding-Rock-North-Carolina.jpg">
           <img alt="Sliding-Rock-North-Carolina" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-750" height="750" src="http://www.earthtraveling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Sliding-Rock-North-Carolina.jpg" width="1000"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.google.com/imgres?newwindow=1&amp;client=firefox-a&amp;hs=vkw&amp;sa=X&amp;rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&amp;tbm=isch&amp;tbnid=IWGYaGtzPC_vVM:&amp;imgrefurl=http://www.flickr.com/photos/rongunzburger/3754851655/&amp;docid=ph-G8Ru7-QnDXM&amp;imgurl=http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3485/3754851655_2982078751_o.jpg&amp;w=1000&amp;h=750&amp;ei=tNt_Ut2GHYittAaOmoFI&amp;zoom=1&amp;ved=1t:3588,r:68,s:0,i:294&amp;iact=rc&amp;page=3&amp;tbnh=182&amp;tbnw=229&amp;start=68&amp;ndsp=38&amp;tx=172&amp;ty=88&amp;biw=1920&amp;bih=905" target="_blank">
           Image Source
          </a>
         </p>
         <h4 style="text-align: left;">
          Natural Bridge, Virginia
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Natural Bridge in Rockbridge County, Virginia, is a geological formation in which Cedar Creek (a small tributary of the James River) has carved out a gorge in the mountainous limestone terrain, forming a natural arch 215 ft (66 m) high with a span of 90 ft (27 m). It consists of horizontal limestone strata, and is the remains of the roof of a cave or tunnel through which the creek once flowed. Natural Bridge has been designated a Virginia Historic Landmark and a National Historic Landmark.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.earthtraveling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/natural-bridge-virginia.jpg">
