     <p>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       tsai design studio: moyo waterfront restaurant + urban farm
      </strong>
      <br/>
      all images courtesy of tsai design studio
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       combining architecture with agriculture,
      </strong>
      the new development moyo by south african firm
      <a href="http://www.tsaidesignstudio.com/" target="_blank">
       tsai design studio
      </a>
      features a two storey restaurant and open air market. located at the V&amp;A waterfront on cape towns historic working harbor, the site is a major tourist destination with over 23 million visitors per year. the project sought to tell a story through food and the full cycle it goes through, from preparation to plate. the theatrical display of food in all its stages informs the the overall layout; starting with produce growth, the trading of it as a commodity, the culinary experience of cooking and preparation, consumption and finally as organic waste that can be used as fertilizer, completing the cycle.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      the market stalls consist of a cluster of prefabricated, flexible modular units that can be adapted to each tenants requirements. arranged around the main arcade, their formation creates small intimate streets, like that of a traditional african souk. the area is sheltered by a series of solar panels, that power the stalls during the day and act as a shading space from the heat of the sun.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="522" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/moyo-V+A-restaurant-tsai-design-studio-designboom01.jpg" title="moyo restaurant at V+A cape town by tsai design studio" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      the food market is a re-interpretation of the african souk
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       the urban farm features a customized aquaponic system
      </strong>
      that provides fresh greens and tilapia fish for the restaurant. located inside, an aquaponic green wall doubles as a visual feature, complete with plant enhancing LEDs. the ground floor is designed as an extension of the market, and is divided into three zones  food preparation (butchery and fish monger), cooking (a grill counter and a bakery) and dining area. a performance stage on the first floor is a focal point for the surrounding dining space.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="522" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/dbsub/33871/2013-10-03/img_9_1380816097_50f65f072e37b839a954bd8b9366bcfd.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      architecture meets agriculture  the aquaponic system that supplies the restaurant fresh vegetables and fish
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="522" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/dbsub/33871/2013-10-03/img_11_1380816097_8f491fe52d2283d92c462760a9fcd88d.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      view of the seating area under the arcade with arrays of solar panels that double as shading device
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="522" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/dbsub/33871/2013-10-03/img_10_1380816097_2fd979422f441fa8a10566cbb3e0f415.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      the market stalls are made of prefabricated panels that can be taken apart, or recycled completely
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="522" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/dbsub/33871/2013-10-03/img_12_1380816097_fcd031cb9edff4001079825ee2380981.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      fresh produce straight from the urban farm into the kitchen
     </p>

     </p>
     <p>
