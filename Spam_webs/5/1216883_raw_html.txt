       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         V. Day Special: Recreating Love in Your Woodland Garden
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">
         <a href="http://danapacificlandscape.com/blog/v-day-special-recreating-love-in-your-woodland-garden/#respond" title="Comment on V. Day Special: Recreating Love in Your Woodland Garden">
          <span class="leave-reply">
           Leave a reply
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <!-- .comments-link -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <style>
         #snippet-box{display:none}
        </style>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone" height="532" src="http://c4.staticflickr.com/8/7346/16261786769_fb3d8385f2_c.jpg" title="Valentine Celebration !deas" width="800"/>
         <br/>
         Valentines Day is a special fiesta for couples all around the world. A garden is the perfect way to convey your love to each other while creating something beautiful that youll be able to enjoy long after the holiday is over. This year make Valentines Day a holiday that youll both remember for years to come by putting together your own woodland garden.
        </p>
        <p>
         Begin the process of making a woodland garden by choosing a spot. Whenever possible, it is best to choose an area where plants are healthy and thriving. If necessary, test the soil for acidity/alkalinity to ensure that the plants that you choose will thrive.
        </p>
        <p>
         Soil test kits are available at garden centers. Consider the general aesthetic of the area as well. For example, a spot with scattered rocks or a space right near your
         <a href="http://www.danapacificlandscape.com/backyard-cleanup" style="color:green" target="_blank">
          backyard
         </a>
         seating area might work better than a bare patch along the side of the house.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once you have selected a space and determined the acidity/alkalinity of your soil, you can start choosing plants for your woodland garden. The following information provides several popular options for ground cover plants, perennials, and small shrubs.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ground plants
         </strong>
         <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/528821181220865184/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft" height="300" src="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/ae/57/a0/ae57a00dbcf6d7e0bac0adc11503fa02.jpg" title="garden design" width="260"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         There are three primary categories for flowering plants: early spring bloom, mid-spring bloom, and late spring/early summer bloom. Typically woodland gardens bloom in the spring. However, you may decide to create a garden that will boast color for months on end. Marsh marigold and Canada lily are just two popular, colorful flowering plants for
         <a href="http://www.danapacificlandscape.com/" style="color:green" target="_blank">
          woodland gardens
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Marsh marigold
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Contrary to its name, the marsh marigold is actually a member of the buttercup family. As such, its not surprising that the yellow flowers closely resemble buttercups. The flowers measure one to two inches in diameter with five to nine waxy, deep yellow petal-like sepals, which bloom from April through June.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Canada lily
         </strong>
         <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/528821181220865195/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" class="alignright" height="300" src="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/29/28/c3/2928c3602a7cbcceb1971ad8ed821c7c.jpg" title="garden landscaping design" width="260"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Canada lilies prefer partial or dappled sunlight, moist to mesic conditions, and slightly sandy or rich loamy soil. The number of flowers on each plant varies from one plant to the next. The floral nectar attracts large butterflies as well as several varieties of Swallowtail butterflies.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Perennials
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Annuals are plants that have their entire life cycle within a single growing season while biennials require two years for a full life cycle and perennials come back year after year. Many perennial plants keep their leaves all year round, offering aesthetically pleasing borders and ground cover. As perennials come back on their own, they often require little
         <a href="http://www.danapacificlandscape.com/full-service-landscape-maintenance/" style="color:green" target="_blank">
          maintenance
         </a>
         beyond basic care to keep them blooming.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Red trillium
         </strong>
         <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/528821181220865216/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft" height="275" src="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/c6/4b/31/c64b314402cea7f69a4b29a30c5aca2d.jpg" title="garden landscape design" width="260"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Red trillium, which also goes by the names Wake Robin and Purple Trillium, produces gorgeous, red flowers with three petals and three leaves. It thrives best in rich soil and moist shade. As it tolerates both acid and alkaline soils, it is fairly easy to grow. Other popular woodland trilliums include white, rose, painted, and yellow.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Dwarf crested iris
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Dwarf crested iris is one of the most famous North American natives. It produces small plants with large, flashy flowers. As the plants are small, its ideal to position them close to pathways or in other areas where larger plants wont overshadow them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Shrubs
         </strong>
         <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/528821181220865186/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" class="alignright" height="280" src="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/51/e0/e2/51e0e23cefe06b45cf41fcd72e068622.jpg" title="garden landscaping design" width="260"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Similar to ground plants, shrubs fall into three categories: early spring, mid-spring, and late spring/early summer bloom. It is up to you when you would like to have your shrubs blooming. Sweet pepperbush and sweetbox are both smaller shrubs that are ideal for woodland gardens.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Sweet pepperbush
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Sweet pepperbush is an upright, suckering shrub with scented white or pink flowers that hit their peak in late summer. The blooms resemble bottle brushes and are attractive to bees and butterflies.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Sweetbox
         </strong>
         <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/528821181220865199/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft" height="280" src="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/49/da/8f/49da8fcc96b588fbbeeb7bbc70f216a0.jpg" title="landscape garden design" width="260"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Sweetbox is an evergreen plant that loves the shade and has a wonderful fragrance when it blooms. The flowers tend to get hidden among the leaves, but youll be able to smell them from several feet away.
        </p>
        <p>
         As you fill in the rest of the garden, let nature be your guide. Instead of trying to create a brand new environment, work with the existing elements. For example, if there are already wildflowers in one area, add more plants instead of uprooting them and starting all of the plantings in a new area.
        </p>
        <p>
         You may want to think about adding water features, pathways, and seating arrangements. Ideally the pathways should be wide enough for two people to walk side by side. Top all of your paths with clean gravel. Do you have muddy soil? Build it up to keep the
         <a href="http://www.danapacificlandscape.com/about-us/" style="color:green" target="_blank">
          pathways
         </a>
         from flooding.
        </p>
