        <h1 class="entry-title">
         The Five Top Professionals who Risk Their Lives On Ladders Every Day
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.ladders4sale.co.uk/blog/ladders/the-five-top-professionals-who-risk-their-lives-on-ladders-every-day/" rel="bookmark" title="9:32 am">
          <span class="entry-date">
           August 11, 2013
          </span>
         </a>
         <span class="meta-sep">
          by
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn n" href="https://plus.google.com/114065609359805521254/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Nicholas Phillips">
           Nicholas Phillips
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          We are pleased to announce the results of our survey to find the top professional ladder-users in the UK. The poll, while not unpredictable, reinforced the British publics respect for all those who work at heights.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           1
          </strong>
          <strong>
           Firemen
          </strong>
          Of course, its firemen. The word brave is over-used or used incorrectly but these guys are the real deal  they regularly risk their lives on
          <a href="http://www.ladders4sale.co.uk/" title="ladders">
           ladders
          </a>
          . Firemen do not retreat from a burning building or any other dangerous situation. After all, who else is going to do the job, douse the building or save a child? Firemen won our poll by a huge amount, and rightly so, but they are not the only professional ladder users that our survey praised.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           2
          </strong>
          <strong>
           Window cleaners
          </strong>
          They work quietly, almost silently, and dont charge much. If you have to get up early and go to work, you can see the window cleaner doing his round before the shops open, before everyone else has woken up. When George Formby sang his famous song about window cleaning in 1936, the world was a different place. But now it is different. It is unlikely though that you will see a cheery, buck-toothed Lancastrian grinning through your bedroom window, chamois leather in one hand and ukulele in the other, strumming away as you awake, because he is dead.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           3 Painters
          </strong>
          Every homeowner knows that they need to keep their property in good repair. A little DIY on the ground floor is usually no problem, but barge boards and soffits are more of a problem. Most of us dont have the required ladders in our homes, so it is best to call a recommended local decorating company to restore the parts of the house that need a specialist ladder to reach. There really is no point trying to reach these areas if you have no experience. You can try to save money, but you may be risking your life or limbs, if you dont know what you are doing.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           4 Roofers
          </strong>
          Even more dangerous for the amateur DIY person than above, is the roof. Never, never, never go on to the roof of your house unless there is a lot of scaffolding. Professional roofers go on to roofs every day of their working lives. Just like watching tightrope walkers or Olympic gymnasts, it is not something to be attempted without the necessary training and experience. As above, call in the experts with their professional, dedicated ladders.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           5 Tree surgeons
          </strong>
          Cats are good at climbing trees. In fact, they do it very well. Squirrels are even better. But humans arent, and everyone knows someone who has either lost teeth or broken a bone falling from a tree. This is why, if you have a tree in your garden that needs attention, it is essential to employ a qualified tree surgeon, who has the right ladders for the job, to sort it out for you.
         </p>
