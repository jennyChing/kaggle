       <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
        <h2>
         Spencer Byles decided to work in the forest in solitudefor a year. In that time he created magnificent sculptures and overall tripped out fun, that will get even better once nature begins reclaiming the art.
        </h2>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
         <br/>
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">

        <br/>
        <img alt="SAM_5366" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-15362" height="563" src="http://www.powerfulprimates.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/SAM_5366-1030x773.jpg" width="750"/>
        <h2>
         From
         <a href="http://frenchforestsculptures.blogspot.fr">
          his website
         </a>
         :
         <br/>
         This project represents my experiences throughout a twelve month period exploring the back regions of three unmanaged local forests making onsite sculptures, in the region of Alpes-Maritimes, France.
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.powerfulprimates.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/forest-land-art-nature-spencer-byles-110.jpg">
          <img alt="forest-land-art-nature-spencer-byles-110" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15360" height="654" src="http://www.powerfulprimates.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/forest-land-art-nature-spencer-byles-110.jpg" width="880"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         All the sculptures are temporary, using only natural and found materials sourced at each location.
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.powerfulprimates.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/forest-land-art-nature-spencer-byles-210.jpg">
          <img alt="forest-land-art-nature-spencer-byles-210" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-15359" height="498" src="http://www.powerfulprimates.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/forest-land-art-nature-spencer-byles-210.jpg" width="750"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I love this. He continues:
        </p>
        <h2>
         The temporary nature of my sculptures is an important aspect of my experiences and understanding. I feel my sculptures are only really completed when nature begins to take hold again and gradually weave its way back into the materials. At this point it slowly becomes part of nature again and less a part of me.
        </h2>
