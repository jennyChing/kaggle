     <p>
      <b>
       Gillian Sankoff
      </b>
      is a Canadian-American
      <a href="/wiki/Sociolinguistics" title="Sociolinguistics">
       sociolinguist
      </a>
      , and professor emerita of linguistics at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">
       University of Pennsylvania
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She is known for her work on Montral French, on
      <a href="/wiki/Pidgin" title="Pidgin">
       pidgin
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Creole_language" title="Creole language">
       creole languages
      </a>
      , and on how speakers' use of language changes over the course of their lifespans.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1986 she received a Guggenheim fellowship.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      She was married to sociologist
      <a href="/wiki/Erving_Goffman" title="Erving Goffman">
       Erving Goffman
      </a>
      from 1981 to his death in 1982, and subsequently married sociolinguist
      <a href="/wiki/William_Labov" title="William Labov">
       William Labov
      </a>
      in 1993. She is the mother of award-winning sociologist,
      <a href="/wiki/Alice_Goffman" title="Alice Goffman">
       Alice Goffman
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
