       <p>
        A number of Nepal's iconic UNESCO World Heritage sites and landmarks, including the Dharahara Tower built for a queen, have been destroyed following the earthquake that hit the country Saturday morning, killing over 3,300 people.
       </p>
       <p>
        The 60-meter-high tower, built in 1832 with a viewing balcony
  that had been open to visitors for a decade, was destroyed by the
  magnitude 7.9 earthquake. A jagged stump 10 meters high is all
  thats left of the lighthouse-like structure, according to
  Reuters. As bodies were pulled from the ruins, a policeman
  reportedly said that up to 200 people had been trapped inside.
       </p>

       <p>
        Nepal's Kathmandu Valley, in the foothills of the Himalayas at
  the crossroads of ancient civilizations, includes seven groups of
  monuments featuring a range of religious and artistic
  achievements that have made it revered, according to UNESCO.
  Age-old temples are among them.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         "The unique tiered temples are mostly made of fired brick
  with mud mortar and timber structures. The roofs are covered with
  small overlapping terracotta tiles, with gilded brass
  ornamentation. The windows, doorways and roof struts have rich
  decorative carvings. The stupas have simple but powerful forms
  with massive, whitewashed hemispheres supporting gilded cubes
  with the all-seeing eternal Buddha eyes,"
        </em>
        a UNESCO
  description informs.
       </p>
       <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">

       </blockquote>
       <p>
        After the earthquake, rescuers scrabbled through destroyed
  buildings, among them ancient wooden Hindu temples.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <em>
         "I can see three bodies of monks trapped in the debris of a
  collapsed building near a monastery,"
        </em>
        Indian tourist Devyani
  Pant told Reuters. "
        <em>
         We are trying to pull the bodies out and
  look for anyone who is trapped."
        </em>
       </p>

       <p>
        Kathmandus Durbar Square, one of three Durbar royal palace
  squares in the valley, all of which are UNESCO World Heritage
  Sites, has also been leveled.
       </p>

  UNESCO World Heritage Site too. The landmark at the center of the
