           <div id="article-body-content" property="dc:description">
            <div class="article-body ">
             <div class="article-related-content-wrapper">
             </div>
             <p>
              Lunenburgs
              <strong class="character" displayname="bold" name="bold">
               Composites Atlantic Ltd.
              </strong>
              will have a hand in the manufacture of two of the worlds more technically sophisticated models of long-range business jets.
             </p>
             <p>
              Claude Baril, president and CEO, said Monday the company has won a multi-year contract with
              <strong class="character" displayname="bold" name="bold">
               Bombardier Aerospace
              </strong>
              in Belfast for the fabrication of composite parts on the horizontal stabilizer for the Global 7000 and Global 8000 business aircraft.
             </p>
             <p>
              They are among the top business aircraft in the world and we certainly hope Bombardier sells lots of them because that will be more work for us, Baril said in an interview.
             </p>
             <p>
              Bombardier Belfast is responsible for the design, manufacture and supply of the complete horizontal stabilizers for the Global family of aircraft.
             </p>
             <p>
              An estimate on the value of the contract was unavailable. Baril said the value over the life of the contract will be determined by how many jets are manufactured.
             </p>
             <p>
              Composites Atlantic has been a supplier to Bombardier Belfast since the mid-1990s and manufactures composite parts that are installed on many of Bombardiers aircraft.
             </p>
             <p>
              It will likely take until about 2020 to get to the maximum production rate of 75 aircraft per year and we will increase our production accordingly, Baril said.
             </p>
             <p>
              The pace and volume of production in Lunenburg will correspond with sales of the Global 7000 and Global 8000, which start at about $65 million each.
             </p>
             <p>
              The contract will help Composites Atlantic extend its long-term relationship with Bombardier, may eventually lead to some additional jobs and will at least see jobs maintained at Lunenburg, Baril said.
             </p>
             <p>
              We have some other projects on the go but cannot report anything just yet, he said.
             </p>
             <p>
              Joeann Stewart, director of customer liaison at Composites Atlantic, said working with Bombardier is recognition of the capabilities and competencies of the Nova Scotia operation.
             </p>
             <p>
              Its also an opportunity to learn from the best in the business, she said of Composites work with Bombardier.
             </p>
             <p>
              Benjamin Langford, liaison engineering manager at Composites Atlantic, said the Global aircraft product line is rated one of the top in the business aircraft market.
             </p>
             <p>
              We will be working in close collaboration with Bombardiers engineering and development team to ensure that the first parts are delivered on time to support Bombardiers rigorous schedule, he said in the news release.
             </p>
             <p>
              The company is headquartered in Lunenburg and employs over 370 people at facilities in Nova Scotia (Lunenburg, Mill Cove and Dartmouth), as well as Mirabel, Que., and the State of Washington.
             </p>
             <p>
              The province has a 50 per cent equity stake in Composites Atlantic and last August lent the aerospace firm $5 million to support its efforts to win new contracts.
             </p>
             <p>
              Composites Atlantic is a subsidiary of
              <strong class="character" displayname="bold" name="bold">
               Sogerma Group
              </strong>
              of France.
             </p>
