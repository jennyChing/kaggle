       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Hitting the Literary Wall? Tips on how to Smash through the Dreaded Writers Block
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">
         <a href="http://applecopywriting.com/hitting-the-literary-wall-tips-on-how-to-smash-through-the-dreaded-writers-block/#comments" title="Comment on Hitting the Literary Wall? Tips on how to Smash through the Dreaded Writers Block">
          <span class="dsq-postid" rel="2964 http://applecopywriting.com/copywriter/copywriter/?p=2964">
           4 Replies
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <!-- .comments-link -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Hitting the Literary Wall? Tips on how to Smash through the Dreaded Writers Block" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2965" height="198" src="http://applecopywriting.com/copywriter/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/creative-intelligence-mindmap.jpg" title="Hitting the Literary Wall? Tips on how to Smash through the Dreaded Writers Block" width="252"/>
         Writing professionally or for pleasure often involves the same pitfalls. When
         <strong>
          writers block
         </strong>
         appears it can seem impossible to put a mere sentence on the empty page in front of you, let alone an interesting
         <a href="http://applecopywriting.com/copywriter/copywriting/blog-writing-and-management/">
          <strong>
           blog post
          </strong>
         </a>
         or dynamic
         <strong>
          <a href="http://applecopywriting.com/copywriter/copywriting/article-writing/">
           seo article
          </a>
         </strong>
         . This happens to the best of writers so you should not get disheartened.
         <strong>
          Writers block
         </strong>
         is to writers what the wall is to runners  something which can be smashed through and defeated.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Exercises to Help your Creative Flow
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         There are some excellent methods of reigniting your thirst for writing which can be applied to web content, blogs and articles. They may sound simple and feel like a waste of time, but they are excellent ways of triggering your brain to focus on the job at hand. They dont take a large amount of time, and no one will ever read them so there is no pressure involved. This is all about you and the words, and that is all.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Freewrites
          </strong>
           The idea of a freewrite is to almost cancel out the thought process. You have your focus in mind, perhaps a topic of discussion, a new product or maybe just a simple word. Use the focus and start writing down anything that comes to mind without stopping. Forget about punctuation, it doesnt even have to make any sense. It doesnt matter what you put on the page, you have complete freedom. Give yourself five minutes and see what springs out of your liberated mind. Who knows, you could come across a gem of an idea or a killer statement that you can use in your piece.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Mind mapping
          </strong>
           Grab a piece of paper and write your main focus word on the page. Use connecting lines to other words which come into your mind. So for example if you started with the word sales you might think about other words such as: income, customers, products, advertising, money, costs, desire. The mind map you create can be beneficial in giving your piece direction or coming up with new concepts.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Create your Reader
          </strong>
           Spend some time to visualise your target audience. Create a character give them a name, an age and occupation and try to imagine why they might be searching for the information you are going to write. For example imagine you have been given the target phrase work from home, think why would your character be interested. They may be wanting to work from home, they may have already started and looking at tips on how to avoidinterruptions. By concentrating on your audience you might just create a question which can lead you into your next article.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         Step Away from the Page
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         There are times when the best thing to do is simply to get up off your seat and treat yourself to a complete break. Tired eyes and stressed out minds do not result in written excellence. Deadlines and
         <strong>
          writers block
         </strong>
         are not a great combination, but even just a five minute break can be productive.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          Go for a walk. Fresh air and a change of atmosphere is a good way of finding inspiration. Enjoy that oxygen and use it to fuel the creativity that is lying dormant in your brain.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          Have a conversation. Your friends or family can have some really good points so use them to bounce off ideas. Just venting can help relieve some of the tension so go ahead and ask someone to listen to you for a few minutes over a cup of tea.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          Exercise. If you have the time, why not work your body rather than your brain for half an hour. Writers are often stuck behind their desks and this is bad for health and for energy levels and therefore destructive tocreativeness.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Writers Block Isnt Worth Worrying About
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         You are allowed to freeze up creatively; everybody has their own form of
         <strong>
          writers block
         </strong>
         in every profession. The best thing is not to get hung up on the problem and to work through it. Use a combination of writing exercises and taking a small break and youll soon be pouring out words onto what were once your empty pages of despair.
        </p>
