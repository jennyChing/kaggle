      <div class="entry-heading">
       <h2>
        <a href="http://ridejimmyride.com/oh-training-crash/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Oh No! Training Crash!">
         Oh No! Training Crash!
        </a>
       </h2>
      </div>
      <div class="entry-excerpt">
       <p>
        Around noon on Saturday, July 12th, I went out to the Eldorado Wilderness for a mountain bike training ride. I had warmed up for about 10 minutes on the trail and I was feeling confident. I knew that the cliff was there.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="IMG_20140712_120724_381" class="aligncenter wp-image-753" height="1022" src="http://ridejimmyride.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_20140712_120724_381.jpg" width="575"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        I had always skirted around it before, but today I went for it. Straight over. Oops! There was not enough landing room for my speed and my bike hurtled over the edge.
       </p>
       <p>
        My shoulder hit with great force on the rocks in the foreground. My Specialized Epic 29er skittered on down to the bottom and came to rest under a pinon tree. I was stunned. I crawled to my feet and staggered back to the top to survey the damages.
       </p>
       <p>
        My left shoulder and my left wrist were throbbing. My left bicep was bleeding and turning purple. My left leg was skinned and gouged. Here are a couple of selfies that I snapped out on the trail.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="IMG_20140712_112042_623" class="aligncenter wp-image-757" height="1022" src="http://ridejimmyride.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_20140712_112042_623.jpg" width="575"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="IMG_20140712_112057_944" class="aligncenter wp-image-758" height="1022" src="http://ridejimmyride.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/IMG_20140712_112057_944.jpg" width="575"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        I retrieved my bike from the ravine and noted that, miraculously, there was only cosmetic damage. I cruised back to my car at the parking lot to gather my thoughts. I leaned my bike against the car and opened the hatch to pack it in.
       </p>
       <p>
        Wait a minute! Im a mountain biker. If you can ride, you do ride. See one of my earlier posts where I talk about always doing the work. Sure, I was hurting, but nothing appeared to be broken. As my old high school coach used to say, Son, Ive had worse than that on my eyeball. Dont come back here to the sideline unless you are really hurt.
       </p>
       <p>
        Todays workout called for 90 minutes of training to include three all-out interval efforts. So I did it. All of it. And yeah, it hurt, but its the price that you somteimes have to pay if you ride mountain bikes at crazy high speeds. After I finished the ride, I limped home and cleaned up. Here is the aftermath.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="photo" class="aligncenter wp-image-759" height="768" src="http://ridejimmyride.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/photo.jpg" width="576"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        The damages: sore left shoulder, sprained left wrist, contusions and abrasions on left bicep, raw and bloody left leg, multiple cuts and gouges on both hands and severely bruised ego. Ive had worse than that on my eyeball.
       </p>
       <p>
        So, they have picked me to finish last this week at Nationals, and now I have suffered a severe crash at the worst possible time, just a few days before the race. I have five days to heal before the gun goes off.
       </p>
       <p>
        As I said before, Ill be on the start line at Bear Creek. And Ill be ready.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Ride Jimmy Ride
       </p>
