        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <p>
          <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-fZkUJOpl8fk/UGshLJRlddI/AAAAAAAACZ0/ucAV5m0bzkE/s1600/moqueqa.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img alt="" border="0" height="400" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-fZkUJOpl8fk/UGshLJRlddI/AAAAAAAACZ0/ucAV5m0bzkE/s400/moqueqa.jpg" width="391"/>
          </a>
          I know this isnt really a popular stance right now, but I am SO ready for Summer to be over! Some of you are saying What do you mean? Its October and Summer has BEEN over!
         </p>
         <p>
          Unfortunately, here in South Carolina, where I currently live, its still hitting 90 degrees for a daytime temp. In fact, its 86 now and the only reason its not higher is because its an overcast day!
         </p>
         <p>
          The hot weather starts here in April (or even earlier) and continues until October. When I lived in New England, that sounded pretty awesome since the Summer is so short up there. The reality is that Id kill for a crisp fall day in the 60s, with temps dropping into the 40s at night! It will happen, but it could be a few weeks for us still.
         </p>
         <p>
          Meanwhile, Im already making soups and stews, hot weather or not. One thing I love about soup recipes (or stews, chilis, chowders or pretty much anything eaten out of a bowl) is that they are relatively easy to make low carb and gluten free, without sacrificing flavor or texture.
         </p>
         <p>
          This velvety shrimp Moqueqa meets both criteria, and is the perfect recipe to transition from Summer into Fall with. It comforts you like you need when the weather cools down and you feel like nesting, but it also stimulates you with a fiery heat and tropical flavor that makes you wish you were eating it on some Bahian beach with a cold beer by your side and your toes jammed into the sand.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="396" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-sFLl8kzhXeU/UGshG1g7RlI/AAAAAAAACZs/8iCUd3lZlPM/s400/moqueca3.jpg" style="border: 0px none;" width="400"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Skeptical that you can get all of that from a bowl of soup? Taste it for yourself and youll find out what Im talking about!
         </p>

             <div class="b-b h-1 strong" id="zlrecipe-title" itemprop="name">
              Brazilian Shrimp Stew (Moqueca de Camaroes)
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="zlmeta zlclear">
             <div class="fl-l width-50">
             </div>
             <div class="fl-l width-50">
              <p id="zlrecipe-yield">
               Yield:
               <span itemprop="recipeYield">
                6 servings
               </span>
              </p>
              <div id="zlrecipe-nutrition" itemprop="nutrition" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/NutritionInformation">
               <p id="zlrecipe-serving-size">
                Serving Size:
                <span itemprop="servingSize">
                 1 cup
                </span>
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="zlclear">
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="img-desc-wrap">
             <p class="t-a-c hide-card">
              <img alt="Brazilian Shrimp Stew (Moqueca de Camaroes)" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://www.ibreatheimhungry.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/moqueqafg-e1357252828123.jpg" title="Brazilian Shrimp Stew (Moqueca de Camaroes)"/>
             </p>
             <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
              <p class="summary italic">
               A delicious, easy to make bowl of soup that is Low Carb, Paleo, and Whole 30 compliant!
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
             Ingredients
            </p>
            <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 1/2 lbs raw shrimp, peeled &amp; deveined
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 cup olive oil
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 cup onion, diced
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 clove garlic, minced
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 cup roasted red pepper, diced
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
              (1) 14 oz can diced tomatoes w/ chili's
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 cup coconut milk
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 Tbl Sriracha hot sauce (sambal oelek if you're whole 30)
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 Tbl fresh lime juice
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-10" itemprop="ingredients">
              salt and pepper to taste
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
             Instructions
            </p>
            <ul class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Heat olive oil in a medium saucepan.  Saute onions for several minutes until translucent, then add the garlic and peppers and cook for several minutes more.  Add the tomatoes, shrimp and cilantro to the pan and simmer gently until the shrimp turns opaque.  Pour in the coconut milk and Sriracha sauce (or Sambal Oelek), and cook just until heated through - do not boil.  Add lime juice and season with salt and pepper to taste.  Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.  Cold beer optional.
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-notes">
             Notes
            </p>
            <div id="zlrecipe-notes-list">
             <p class="notes">
              Note:  If you can't eat shrimp, you can substitute any mild white fish, or even chicken, for the protein in this recipe and it will taste just as amazing!
             </p>
             <p class="notes">
              Approx nutrition info per serving:  294 calories, 19g fat, 5g net carbs, 24g protein
             </p>
