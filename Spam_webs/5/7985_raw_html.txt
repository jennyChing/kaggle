        Coastal Christmas Tree
       </h1>
       <a href="http://sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html" rel="bookmark">

          <p align="center">
           I think Im in love! Im in love with my Coastal Christmas Tree! I wish you all could see it up close because pictures never seem to do Christmas trees justice. The family room holds the traditional family tree, all adorn with the kids popsicle stick creations and those special ornaments passed down through the years. The foyer is where mamas new tree resides, my
           <strong>
            Coastal Christmas Tree
           </strong>
           !
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Coastal Christmas Tree" border="0" height="755" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-9yxx5-tVLEI/UJ0s8xOmj5I/AAAAAAAALpg/aw_GGnIzKyE/Coastal-Christmas-Tree-649x102423%25255B7%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Coastal Christmas Tree" width="519"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
          </p>
          <p>
           <a name="more">
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           The tree is decked out in finger starfish, shells, burlap loops, capiz, coral, and my
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/glass-float-ornaments.html">
            <strong>
             Glass Float Ornaments
            </strong>
           </a>
           (click the link to be taken to the posts). A few mercury glass and frosty white glass ornaments add some extra sparkle.
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Coastal Christmas Tree" border="0" height="447" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-crA6q1p5x-8/TuGawAiO41I/AAAAAAAALpo/1eFk8CLDkvA/Shell%252520ornaments%252520%2525281024x683%252529%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Shell ornaments " width="660"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           Remember the
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/11/im-dreaming-of-white-christmas-living.html">
            <strong>
             Capiz Shell Christmas Trees
            </strong>
           </a>
           I made for my mom? Well I had several left over, so I added a ribbon loop to the large, 3 inch capiz shells. The capiz shell garland, used for the smaller Capiz Tree, was cut into sections and hooked with a wire. I call them Capizsicles. The are so luminescent in the tree lights! And are just stunning at night!
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Capiz ornaments " border="0" height="773" src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/-LVWz4kuaNTU/TuGaxAoLSEI/AAAAAAAALps/5VAV2_J6sXQ/Capiz%252520ornaments%252520%252528576x800%252529%25255B4%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Capiz ornaments" width="490"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Capiz Shell Ornament " border="0" height="837" src="http://lh4.ggpht.com/-OHr2XVMFp8g/TuGayI_6C0I/AAAAAAAALpw/q9fwnRSQ_sg/Capiz%252520Shell%252520Ornament%252520%252528533x800%252529%25255B4%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Capiz Shell Ornament " width="486"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           When I posted the
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/11/coastal-holiday-wreath.html">
            <strong>
             Coastal Holiday Wreath
            </strong>
           </a>
           tutorial, the wreath had 2 white twig/coral picks added. They of course were artificial, but reminded me of real coral. Where could I find real coral? Well, weve had such gorgeous weather here the last few weeks and did some beachcombing Thanksgiving weekend at moms home. My kids and sisters (
           <a href="http://www.homestoriesatoz.com/">
            Home Stories A 2 Z
           </a>
           &amp;
           <a href="http://littlepageturners.blogspot.com/">
            Little Page Turners
           </a>
           ) helped collect driftwood, shells, and horseshoe crab shells, which I wanted to make ornaments out of but my sisters thought theyd look a little creepy.
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           The real jackpot was the coral! The beach was covered with bright purple Whip Coral! I knew exactly what Id be using it for, the tree! So I did what all
           <em>
            normal
           </em>
           people do I picked up as much of the stinky, rotting, now-I-smell-like-dead-fish, purple branches as I could manage!
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="beachcombing and whip coral" border="0" height="235" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-rv_nPRbnY4Y/TuGazBeAffI/AAAAAAAALp0/6lJWPutkYxY/beachcombing%25255B4%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="beachcombing" width="667"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           The coral is beautiful and so bright but purple just isnt in my Christmas color scheme. So after they dried in the sun for a few days and their smell faded (a bit), I coated several pieces of the coral in many coats of white spray paint. The coral is so porous they sucked up layer upon layer of paint. The paint masked the stink though!! Then they received a coat of spray adhesive and were sprinkled with Epsom salt. They are now white and sparkly, like newly dusted snow! Isnt it pretty?
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Coastal Christmas Decor " border="0" height="481" src="http://lh5.ggpht.com/-DEFbKVY3Afc/TuGa00d0wuI/AAAAAAAALp4/38SLEM09ULw/Coastal%252520Christmas%252520Decor%252520%2525281024x738%252529%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Coastal Christmas Decor" width="660"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           Branches of the tree were inserted into sea shells and starfish sit perched on the pine boughs. No ribbon or hooks needed! And loops of burlap garland were tucked in the tree for added softness and texture and to coordinate with the burlap ruffled tree skirt.
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Coastal Christmas Ornaments " border="0" height="572" src="http://lh6.ggpht.com/-9b8fdLyrL1U/TuGa2D2k3uI/AAAAAAAALp8/gZn1erCxmeg/Coastal%252520Christmas%252520Ornaments%252520%2525281024x878%252529%25255B4%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Coastal Christmas Ornaments" width="657"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Glass Float Ornament and starfish " border="0" height="772" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-O3yncOz-8VE/TuGa3DghKEI/AAAAAAAALqA/CMHZtbm6oO4/Glass%252520Float%252520Ornament%252520and%252520starfish%252520%252528538x800%252529%25255B8%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Glass Float Ornament " width="494"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           My real glass float, which is over one foot wide rests in a galvanized bucket filled with fish netting, shell garland, sea shells from our collection, and a present.
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Glass Float in Galvanized Tin pail " border="0" height="456" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-QwF_b48TV4Y/TuGa4BphvxI/AAAAAAAALqE/bkGKBFfehU4/Glass%252520Float%252520Tin%252520pail%252520%252528800x597%252529%25255B3%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Glass Float Tin pail " width="598"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="http://www.sandandsisal.com/2011/12/coastal-christmas-tree.html">
            <img alt="Coastal Chrismas Tree" border="0" height="941" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/-ed_TVVmsRpo/TuGa5tD_d8I/AAAAAAAALqI/5lp1DDUX11s/Coastal%252520Chrismas%252520Tree%252520%252528639x1024%252529%25255B4%25255D.jpg?imgmax=800" title="Coastal Chrismas Tree" width="599"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           I find my self standing in the foyer, just staring at the tree. Crazy I know, but I love all the natural elements, varying textures, colors, the twinkly lights and the homemade ornaments. Christmas is such magical time of year! I hope you enjoyed my
           <strong>
            Coastal Christmas Tree
           </strong>
           !
          </p>
          <p align="center">

             No-Sew Burlap Ruffled Tree Skirt
            </a>
           </strong>
           *
          </p>
