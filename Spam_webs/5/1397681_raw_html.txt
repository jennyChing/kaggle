         <h3>
          Made in North Carolina, With Love.
         </h3>
         <h5>
         </h5>
         <p>
          There are thousands of homemade foods sold across North Carolina every day. But theres one ingredient they all have in common.
         </p>

          <p>
           It wasnt until I moved here that I learned Krispy Kreme Doughnuts were made in North Carolina  born in Winston-Salem to be exact.
          </p>
          <p>
           Looking around and seeing that Krispy Kreme outnumbered Dunkin Donuts 2 to 1, I just thought that folks here loved them some Krispy Kreme. They do;
           <em>
            we
           </em>
           do. And they should, Krispy Kreme doughnuts are an amazing treat, a surefire hangover cure, the perfect post-church snack, and simply delicious.
          </p>
          <p>
           But really, North Carolina loves Krispy Kreme for more than their taste. Generations of Tar Heels have grown up with them. Theyre a source of pride, the food equivalent of the hometown boy done good. The same goes for Pepsi and Cheerwine and Texas Pete and Mt. Olive Pickles.
          </p>
          <p>
           Statewide, there are hundreds, maybe even thousands, of North Carolina foodstuffs sold in specialty stores, mom-and-pop gas stations, country grocery stores, on roadsides, and at farmers markets. I love to see someone set up selling watermelons out of the back of their truck, or fresh boiled peanuts, or five kinds of homemade jam. The same holds for farmers markets.
          </p>
          <p>
           At my favorite ones, not only do you have the bounty of the season, you also have the bakers bounty of bread, pies and cakes; the beekeepers jars of honey, the grandmothers jams and jellies, and the more adventurous offerings that are often unique to the region.
           <br/>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_64144" style="width: 210px">
           <a data-rel="wppa" href="http://www.ourstate.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/IMG_20711.jpg" style=" cursor:url(http://www.ourstate.com/wp-content/plugins/wp-photo-album-plus/images/magnifier-small.png),pointer;">
            <img alt="Muddy Creek Farms mushrooms don't sit for long at the Morganton farmers market." class="size-medium wp-image-64144" height="300" src="http://www.ourstate.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/IMG_20711-200x300.jpg" width="200"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Muddy Creek Farms mushrooms dont sit for long at the Morganton farmers market.
           </p>
          </div>
          <br/>
          At farmers markets around Morganton, youll often find
          <a href="http://www.ourstate.com/muddy-creek-farms/">
           Muddy Creek Farms shiitake mushrooms
          </a>
          . We dont have that in Wilmington. For mushrooms, were stuck with the grocery store. But even the stores  from Food Lion and Harris Teeter to Lowes Foods and The Fresh Market  are North Carolina companies.
          <p>
           What Wilmington does have is
           <a href="http://angelasppf.com/" target="_blank">
            Angelas Pepper-Pickled Foods
           </a>
           . She started by pickling some okra, pairing it in the jar with Fresno, jalapeno, and habanero peppers (for increasing heat intensity). From there, shes moved on to collards, peanuts (which are fantastic), the three peppers, and more. Ive watched her presence at Wilmingtons riverfront farmers market grow from a card table with a few jars to a full-fledged setup and a line of eager samplers and buyers.
          </p>
          <p>
           As someone who writes about food, Ive accumulated a collection of candies, pickles, barbecue sauces, jams, jellies, cookies, crackers, rubs and seasonings from across the state. From
           <a href="http://www.oldsalem.org/winkler-bakery.html" target="_blank">
            C. Winkler Bakerys Moravian Cookies
           </a>
           out of Old Salem to
           <a href="http://bambooladies.com/" target="_blank">
            Bamboo Ladies Bamboo Pickles
           </a>
           and S
           <a href="http://slingshotcoffeecompany.com/" target="_blank">
            lingshot Coffee
           </a>
           from Raleigh to a bevy of barbecue sauces from Greenville, Asheville and Charlotte, I have a pantry chocked full of North Carolina food. In my refrigerator and at my bar, youll find North Carolina well represented as well 
           <a href="www.motherearthbrewing.com/page/dark-cloud1.html" target="_blank">
            Mother Earth Brewings Dark Cloud
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.duckrabbitbrewery.com/" target="_blank">
            Duck-Rabbits Milk Stout
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.childressvineyards.com/wines/" target="_blank">
            Childress Vineyards Three White
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.southernartisanspirits.com/" target="_blank">
            Cardinal Gin
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.covingtonvodka.com/" target="_blank">
            Covington Sweet Potato Vodka
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           As great as these born-in-North-Carolina foods are, to me there is often one extra ingredient that our homemade efforts have over them: love. My friends ask for my barbecue sauce because its made with a little love  for the food, the making of the sauce, the friendship.
          </p>
          <p>
           A friend contacted me on Facebook at the end of summer. He told me his wife would be in town, and asked if we wanted to have her bring up some homemade fruit butter? Of course. When she arrived with a brown paper bag filled with jelly jars and gave me a jar each of blueberry butter, cherry butter and peach chutney, I couldnt have been happier. When I got home and tasted each, they were great. Why? The flavors, certainly  and also the skill in making these  but the love; thats what made the flavor for me, and what drove her to make them in the first place.
          </p>
          <p>
           
          </p>
