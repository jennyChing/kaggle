        <p>
         The only way for a company to become profitable is for its customers to purchase their products or services. Historically, companies have spent the majority of their marketing budgets on customer acquisition. The thought was, if companies reached more people, they could sell more. Today, as companies use data to determine customer motivation and behavior, they are realizing that customer retention is where they should be focusing their marketing efforts.
        </p>
        <p>
         If your company is still not sold on the idea of customer retention, these resources will be of immense value. Some of the resources analyze the value of customer acquisition versus customer retention to help you make more informed marketing decisions. If youre already moving toward a more customer-centric focus, these resources will help you focus your strategies and put their practical ideas and solutions to good use. Thats because we have gathered a variety of resources from customer loyalty, engagement, experience, and retention experts to give you the best information to set you on a path to more successful customer retention efforts.
        </p>

        <p>
         <strong>
          1.
          <a href="http://www.dbmarketing.com/articles/Art112.htm" target="_blank">
           Customer Retention: Integrating Lifetime Value into Marketing Strategies
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.ngdata.com/wp-content/uploads/Customer-Retention-Integrating-Lifetime-Value.png">
          <img alt="Customer Retention Integrating Lifetime Value" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2301" height="128" src="http://www.ngdata.com/wp-content/uploads/Customer-Retention-Integrating-Lifetime-Value.png" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Written by Arthur Middleton Hughes, considered by many in the industry as an icon of database marketing, this article outlines his belief that too many companies organize for acquisition rather than retention. Hughes also speaks to the importance of customer loyalty in this article, as well as to the need for using lifetime value analysis to test and improve retention strategies.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Three key topics we like from Customer Retention: Integrating Lifetime Value into Marketing Strategies:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The meaning of retention strategy
         </li>
         <li>
          How to set up a successful retention strategy
         </li>
         <li>
          How to measure your retention strategy using lifetime value
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cost:
         </strong>
         FREE
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2.
          <a href="http://www.etstrategicmarketing.com/smNov-Dec2/art11.html" target="_blank">
           Customer Retention: The Key toBusiness Performance
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.ngdata.com/wp-content/uploads/Customer-Retention-The-Key-to-Business-Performance.png">
          <img alt="Customer Retention The Key to Business Performance" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2305" height="233" src="http://www.ngdata.com/wp-content/uploads/Customer-Retention-The-Key-to-Business-Performance.png" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This customer retention article was written by K. Ramakrishnan, of the Strategic Marketing Research Team. The article offers explanations and support for Ramakrishnans assertion that the key to superior business performance is customer retention and arrested downward migration.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Three key points we like from Customer Retention: The Key to Business Performance:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Both attitudinal and behavioral variables need to be understood when studying customer retention
         </li>
         <li>
          Customer retention has a greater impact on profits than market share, economies of scale, or other variables thought to provide competitive advantage
         </li>
         <li>
          Retention strategies targeting deliberative loyalists are the most rewarding
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cost:
         </strong>
         FREE
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3.
          <a href="http://www.carolroth.com/blog/105-best-ways-to-deal-with-difficult-customers/" target="_blank">
           105 Best Ways to Deal with Difficult Customers
           <br/>
          </a>
         </strong>
         <a href="https://twitter.com/caroljsroth" target="_blank">
          @caroljsroth
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.ngdata.com/wp-content/uploads/105-Best-Ways-to-Deal-With-Difficult-Customers.png">
          <img alt="105 Best Ways to Deal With Difficult Customers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2304" height="280" src="http://www.ngdata.com/wp-content/uploads/105-Best-Ways-to-Deal-With-Difficult-Customers.png" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Carol Roth, CNBC contirbutor and a preeminent name in business news, relied on her contributor network of entrepreneurs and experts to get the best tips and advice for dealing with difficult customers. Dealing with difficult customers is a part of the job, and these strategies should help you not just handle them, but retain them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Three points we like from 105 Best Ways to Deal with Difficult Customers:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Listen first and then reason with them because most people just want to be heard and respected
         </li>
         <li>
          Give them what they want
         </li>
         <li>
          Apologize, fix it, and follow up
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Cost:
         </strong>
