      <h1 class="entry_title">
       What is a Troll?
      </h1>
      <div class="singlepostmeta" style="font-size:14px">
       <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-68 photo" height="68" src="http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/1fe4fac83d156a9401e0967e145c56d3?s=68&amp;d=monsterid&amp;r=g" srcset="http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/1fe4fac83d156a9401e0967e145c56d3?s=136&amp;d=monsterid&amp;r=g 2x" width="68"/>
       <strong>
        About Holte Ender
       </strong>
       <br/>
       Holte Ender will always try to see your point of view, but sometimes it is hard to stick his head that far up his @$$.
       <br/>
       <a href="http://madmikesamerica.com/author/holte-ender/">
        View all posts by Holte Ender 
       </a>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       A troll is a person who deliberately and intentionally attempts to disrupt the usability of website, a conversation or an activity which they should not be taking part. Trolling is a violation of Internet social spaces and is often done to inflame or invite conflict. It necessarily involves a value judgment made by one user about the value of another persons contribution. (Because of this it is considered not to be any more useful than the judgment I dont agree with you by many users, who prefer to focus on behaviors instead of on presumed intent). Not to be confused with large warty monsters thought to dwell under bridges, in caves etc.
      </p>
      <p>
       Trolling is not necessarily the same as vandalism (although vandalism may be used to troll).
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troll_%28Internet%29">
        The basic mindset of a troll
       </a>
       is that they are far more interested in how others react to their behavior or comments.
      </p>
      <p>
       We have all known a troll, they throw an explosive off-topic comment into a conversation, then leave. Below is a video of some out in the open trolling, which could also be called anti-social behavior.
      </p>
