        <p>
         Youll make some pretty big purchases throughout your life; like a college education, your first car or that fancy wedding reception you always wanted. But none will be so emotional, semi-permanent and financially steep as
         <a href="http://blog.allstate.com/4-big-money-mistakes-of-first-time-homeowners/" target="_hplink">
          buying your first house
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         To highlight the roller coaster of emotions youll experience when searching for your first forever (or at least, starter) home, weve partnered with
         <a href="http://www.Allstate.com" target="_hplink">
          Allstate Insurance
         </a>
         to bring you the 15 emotional stages of becoming a homeowner.
        </p>
        <p>
         <br/>
         <big>
          <strong>
           1. Youve Decided To Buy A House
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="204" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/mxDZecDOOsWCA" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         Youre sick of pouring all of your money into a rental, or
         <em>
          worse
         </em>
         , living with your parents. Youre ready to buy your very own house  you think.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           2. Excited, Nervous, Anxious ... What
           <em>
            Dont
           </em>
           You Feel?
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="240" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/XnxcIWjxqOj4c" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         After some initial research online, youre more overwhelmed than ever. Do you want a two bedroom or a three? Is a lack of an island counter in the kitchen a deal breaker?
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           3. Excitement Quickly Turns To Feeling  Nervous
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="269" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/XeMp0onMZQZgY" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         Your euphoric feelings of fledgling ownership quickly turn to nausea after receiving the pre-approval letter from the bank, and you realize how much debt youre about to take on.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           4. But Youre Mostly Excited
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="180" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/eCyNqJZteObi8" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         Still, youre going to own a house -- a place where no one but you can tell people to take off their shoes, and raiding the fridge is not just a privilege, but a sovereign right.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           5. You Get Emotionally Invested Too Soon
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="269" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/ZEw4IFdlCRcwU" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         How can you explain the instant connection you feel when stepping into the master bedroom of a house youve just seen for the first time today? It just feels ... right.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           6. The Concept Of Your 'Dream Home' Changes Drastically
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="240" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/12CtkDlw4QVnWw" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         The more houses you visit, the more you realize the "Dream House" you originally had in mind is, well, unattainable. Who needs a master bedroom, anyway?
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           7. Youve Found The One!
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="270" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/LSNqpYqGRqwrS" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         After shuffling your tired feet through countless open houses and unfamiliar living rooms, you finally find "The One." You take the plunge, put in your bid and hope for the best.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           8. And Then, You Get Outbid
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/Ci3NqgC2GfbXy" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         Or, the inspector finds some
         <em>
          issues
         </em>
         . Either way, your dreams are crushed, and youre back at square one.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           9. Just When You Thought Youd Never Love Again, You Do
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="260" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/oPVqKGwziOURq" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         After the initial heartbreak fades, you find a house even better than the one you initially wanted! You unabashedly put an offer down.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           10. Then, You Wait
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="270" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/11WwYyCcckTBuw" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         All this waiting is making you just a little bit crazy. Your mind is still on high alert for available listings,
         <em>
          just in case
         </em>
         something falls through.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           11. But They Quickly Go Away When, OMG, Youre Approved!!!
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <img src="https://38.media.tumblr.com/6330a19d656affe5b479222b583b3e99/tumblr_mlzj14zA8M1rcy99do1_500.gif "/>
         <br/>
         You might as well have won the lottery. This time, the inspection -- and everything else -- works in your favor.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           12. Your Relationship With Your Lawyer/Banker Gets Serious
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="206" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/zIPKUgO2Ln6XC" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         As you sort through the mounds of paperwork, youll discover yourself spending an unprecedented amount of time talking with your mortgage broker and a local insurance agent, shopping for the right policy to cover your future home.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           13. Closing Costs Set In, And Your Bank Account Goes 'Ow'
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <img src="http://mrwgifs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Kristen-Wiig-Help-Me-Im-Poor-In-Bridesmaids-Gif.gif  "/>
         <br/>
         Wow, those numbers suddenly look bigger! You can say, "See ya!" to your life savings.
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           14. But You Quickly Forget About That, Because Youre A Homeowner Now!
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" mozallowfullscreen="" src="//giphy.com/embed/YTbZzCkRQCEJa" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="480">
         </iframe>
         <br/>
         That's right, it's time to
         <em>
          celebrate
         </em>
         ! You feel as giddy as a little kid -- except much, much more responsible (this isnt Monopoly property anymore).
        </p>
        <p>
         <big>
          <strong>
           15. And Finally: Sweet, Sweet Satisfaction
          </strong>
         </big>
         <br/>
         <img src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-VY3JmHfydRw/Ub-vRDKgD3I/AAAAAAAAD_E/B17UBSv2qLY/s1600/Carrie%2Band%2BMr.%2BBig.gif"/>
         <br/>
         You can breathe an all-encompassing sigh of relief, because this entire process is behind you. You have a set of shiny new keys, a new place to call home," a reliable insurance policy and peace of mind that youre doing right by your precious new digs.
        </p>

           understand your insurance
          </a>
          needs so you have peace of mind that you are protected.
