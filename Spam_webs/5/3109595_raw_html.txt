             <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
              <a href="http://www.kateleong.com/2013/12/rainbow-of-hope.html">
               Rainbow of Hope...
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content">
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif; font-size: large;">
                Good news alert!!
               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif; font-size: large;">
                Our little girl is growing!!!!!!!!!!! Yesterday morning I took her in for a weight check. On Friday her weight was 5lbs, 13oz. And yesterday - four days later - she weighed in at SIX pounds and TWO ounces!! She passed her birth weight!! It was a huge relief to me.
               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif; font-size: large;">
                If someone asked me what my best advice would be to a pregnant or brand new Mom, it would be this: Don't assume or expect breastfeeding to be easy. It's not! There will be challenges and it will require a lot of dedication. Reach out to a lactation consultant (I'm lucky to have a great one in my corner!) if you run into issues or even have questions. And for SURE lose the guilt. There's already enough guilt in this parenting gig - let's all give ourselves a break. Do the best you can and lose the guilt. Hope and I seem to be getting into a rhythm with breastfeeding now. It's getting easier...thank God. The toughest part for me is rousing the little princess and then keeping her awake for a feed...especially in the middle of the night. She reminds me of Gavin in that way - we had to wake him for every feed, night or day. We'd take his clothes off - tickle him - tap his head - move his arms around...we tried everything. So there are many times in the middle of the night that I smile as I look at sleepy Hope and remember Gavin.
               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif; font-size: large;">
                <br/>
               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif; font-size: large;">
                We had an exciting day today! Granny came over and the two of us (plus Hope) picked Brian up from school with a big surprise. We were going to see Santa again!! Brian was so excited! If you missed our first Santa experience - you definitely need to read about it in
                <a href="http://www.kateleong.com/2013/11/dear-santa.html#.UrJJmGRDuXM" target="_blank">
                 THIS
                </a>
                post. I just HAD to bring Hope to meet him.         <h2 class="title">
         Gavin's Playground Project
        </h2>
        <div class="widget-content">
         Gavin's Playground Project was born from my desire to see an "All Abilities" playground built at Nemours/A.I. duPont Hospital for Children in Wilmington, Delaware.  This is the hospital where Gavin spent a lot of time during his life... and where he ultimately died, surrounded by friendly and familiar faces.  To mark the one year anniversary of his death, I held a fundraiser on April 5, 2014. You can read all about it in
         <a href="http://www.kateleong.com/2014/04/thank-you-for-supporting-gavins.html#.U2el_q1dXEc">
          THIS
         </a>
         post.
         <div>
          <br/>
         </div>
         <div>
          We raised a LOT of money, but not enough for a playground! If you would like to contribute to help make this playground a reality - so no kids will ever have to sit on the sidelines watching their peers play - you can contribute directly to the hospital through the Chasing Rainbows fundraising page
          <a href="http://givenow.nemours.org/faf/search/searchTeamPart.asp?ievent=1113352&amp;lis=0&amp;kntae1113352=872AD9493E134215AF4017D4102B9708&amp;supId=0&amp;team=6012157">
           HERE
          </a>
          .  Your donation will go directly to the hospital for the playground and is tax deductible.  You can also create your own fundraising team page and start collecting donations in honor of anyone you want!!  There is also a mailing address on their page if you prefer to mail a check.  Designate your contribution in memory of Gavin Leong - or write in that this is for the playground so your dollars will go to the right place.  Thank you for your support of Gavin's Playground Project!
