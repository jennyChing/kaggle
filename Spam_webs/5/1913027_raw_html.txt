            <p>
             Grilled eggplant can be one of the great treats of summer: lusciously tender eggplant, with bits of crispy charred edges here and there. Sadly, it can also be overcooked and flavorless or undercooked with a spongy texture. This easy recipe uses the power of salt water (a.k.a. brining) to guarantee great
             <a data-inlink="rvfoyIZLc6UTZJjklEhyWQ==" href="http://bbq.about.com/od/fruitandvegetables/a/aa091402a.htm">
              grilled eggplant
             </a>
             crispy brown on the outside, creamy sweet on the inside, and full of flavorevery time you pop a few slices of this brilliant vegetable on a hot grill.
            </p>
            <p>
             Feel free to embellish with spices and other flavors, including serving with a drizzle of
             <a data-inlink="4HpdveA1VraHJayZ8fjTjA==" href="http://culinaryarts.about.com/od/seasoningflavoring/qt/Balsamic-Vinegar.htm">
              balsamic vinegar
             </a>
             , a dollop of
             <a href="/od/summer/r/Pesto.htm">
              pesto
             </a>
             , a spoonful of
             <a href="/od/dipsspreads/r/romesco.htm">
              romesco
             </a>
             , or a scattering of crumbled feta cheese and make this simple technique a recipe of your own.
            </p>

          <div class="preparation expert-content-text" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           <h5 class="heading-style-a p-col">
            Preparation
           </h5>
           <ol>
            <li>
             In a large bowl dissolve 2 tablespoons of
             <a data-component="link" data-inlink="sCNzSF5Nzln4g9PPsJSUHA==" data-ordinal="1" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://homecooking.about.com/od/foodequivalents/a/saltmeasures.htm">
              salt
             </a>
             in 1 cup warm water. Add 2 quarts cold water. Set aside.
            </li>
            <li>
             Trim the eggplant. Peel it, if you like, and cut it into -inch thick diagonal, crosswise, or lengthwise slices. Put the slices in the salt water, weigh them down with an upside-down plate, and let sit 30 minutes.
            </li>
            <li>
             Meanwhile, heat a charcoal or gas grill to medium-high heat (you can hold your hand about an inch above the grill for 3 to 4 seconds).
            </li>
           </ol>
           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense ads-half" id="adsense1">
           </div>
           <div class="radlinks ads-half" data-character-limit="null" data-display-inline="true" data-display-label="false" data-num-links="5" id="radlinks3">
           </div>
           <ol class="cb-split">
            <li>
             Drain the eggplant and pat it dry with paper towels or a clean kitchen towel. Lay the slices on a large baking sheet or tray. Brush one side with olive oil and sprinkle with salt. Lay the slices oiled-side-down on the grill.
             <a data-component="link" data-inlink="26m99VvX9PfGtzc2siJEsQ==" data-ordinal="2" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" href="http://bbq.about.com/od/grillinghelp/f/f060604c.htm">
              Close the lid
             </a>
             if using a gas grill and cook until grill marks appear, about 5 minutes.
            </li>
            <li>
             Brush the top sides of the slices with oil and sprinkle them with salt. Turn the slices over, close the lid on a gas grill and cook until grill marks appear on the other side and the eggplant is very tender, about 5 minutes. Serve hot or at room temperature.
            </li>
           </ol>
