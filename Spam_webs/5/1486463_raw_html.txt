             <p>
              Its apretty common thing to hear from young nomads something like I wont go to college, thats what society expects me to do. Instead I will travel the world till the rest of my life. No matter how cool it might sound, there is actually nothing particularly outstanding in such choices.
             </p>
             <p>
              In fact our experience gives evidence that in order to work less and to travel more you actually need higher education. But what is even better, you can combine these two processes!
             </p>

             <div class="sc-title">
              <h2 class="title">
               <span>
               </span>
              </h2>
              <span class="sub-title">
               2 reasons to go to university
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
              Basically, there are just two reasons to get a university degree and none of them has anything to do with your intellectual abilities.
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               The better education (skills) you have, the more employers are ready to pay you. So the simplest way to guarantee yourself a life-time travel is to make sure that people will always be glad to hire you for a couple of months and give you enough money for travels for a couple of years.
              </li>
              <li>
               You can travel for free and get money for the mere fact that you are studying. Its called a scholarship and its the best thing humanity has invented. In the modern world its not that hard to get a scholarship and go to another country (or several countries) to live and travel there for money of local tax-payers.
              </li>
             </ol>

             <div class="sc-title">
              <h2 class="title">
               <span>
               </span>
              </h2>
              <span class="sub-title">
               How does it work?
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
              While its pretty easy to understand how the firsttrek works (you just invest in yourself and then get aprofit), the second one requires some specifications.
             </p>
             <p>
              We have studied (and got scholarships for that) in Ukraine, USA, Norway, Germany, Sweden, Poland and Belgium. Well, this list of countries is not that big, but there is serious doubt that we would be able to explore them so well if not for scholarships.
             </p>
             <p>
              In order to apply for a scholarship you need the following documents:
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Motivation letter (also known as personal statement). Its pretty easy to write it, but make sure somebody proofreads it.
              </li>
              <li>
               Recommendation letters (just ask professors who like you to write them).
              </li>
              <li>
               If you have bad grades, make sure to explain it in your motivation letter.
              </li>
              <li>
               Valid passport.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
              Some programs might require additional documents, but here is your core.
             </p>
             <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_15340" style="width: 690px;">
              <a href="http://crazzzytravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/dsc06268.jpg">

              </a>

               Wroclaw university hall for thesis presentations. Not bad, ha?
              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <div class="sc-title">
              <h2 class="title">
               <span>
               </span>
              </h2>
              <span class="sub-title">
               What programs to choose?
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
              Depending on your travel expectations, there are several types of programs you can apply for.
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               Short-term seminars, conferences and study trips. The big advantage of such programs is funding for excursions, transport and accommodation. Very often they also provide you with food, which makes them just perfect to fill a couple of weeks in your travel itinerary.
              </li>
              <li>
               Summer (winter) schools. They last for approximately 2-3 months and give you much more time to explore the country. Excursions are usually included into the study plan, so make sure you dont miss them.
              </li>
              <li>
               Degree programs. If you feel like moving to another country for a couple of years, this option is for you. The best part of it is that you get monthly stipend, which means that you are basically earning money for being so kind to travel and study in another country.
              </li>
             </ol>

             <div class="sc-title">
              <h2 class="title">
               <span>
               </span>
              </h2>
              <span class="sub-title">
               Its hard to get such funding though
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
              No, its not. Nastia got her first $11000 to participate in Harvard summer school when she was 18 years old (the earliest age you can travel abroad and earn money without the permission of parents). Was she skillful travel/ scholar then? Obviously, not.
             </p>
             <p>
              Getting scholarships depends exclusively on your luck (its like a lottery) and persistence.
             </p>
             <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_15341" style="width: 690px;">
              <a href="http://crazzzytravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/img_1949.jpg">

              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <p>
              You can be sure that you are doing well, if you get accepted to one program out of ten you apply for.
             </p>
             <div class="sc-title">
              <h2 class="title">
               <span>
               </span>
              </h2>
              <span class="sub-title">
               Where to look for announcements?
              </span>
             </div>
             <p>
              The best web-site ever for looking for funded study opportunities is
              <a class="ext-link" data-wpel-target="_blank" href="http://www.mladiinfo.eu/" rel="external nofollow" title="">
               mladiinfo.eu
              </a>
              . You can always find great stuff there + they have internship section in case you hate studying.
             </p>
             <p>
              You can also find some useful information at
              <a class="ext-link" data-wpel-target="_blank" href="http://www.studyabroad.com/" rel="external nofollow" title="">
               studyabroad.com
              </a>
              or just google for similar web-sites.
             </p>
             <p>
              Alternatively if you know already where you want to go, just search for available funding at the official page of university / NGO.
             </p>
             <p>
              Stay motivated and apply as much as you can.
             </p>
