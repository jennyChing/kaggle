     <p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
      <span class="pagetitle">
       TRANSCEND DRUGS
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent:.25in;text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       I recently reviewed the new health book TRANSCEND by Ray  Kurzweil and Terry Grossman. While I  recommended the book in an
       <a href="Review-Transcend.html">
        Amazon.com review
       </a>
       (low-calorie diet, exercise, and  relaxation are good things), a major  concern was their one-sided discussion of various nutritional supplements.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       How  does one know that a drug, vitamin, or nutritional supplement really works?
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       I showed how the arduous process of evaluating medical  evidence works in my essay
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       -
       <a href="EVIDENCE.html" target="_blank">
        Does Drug X  Really Work?
       </a>
       If you want to know what an A, B, or C
       <a href="http://naturalstandard.com/grading.asp" target="_blank">
        Evidence Grade
       </a>
       means, read  that essay (or just click
       <a href="http://naturalstandard.com/grading.asp" target="_blank">
        Evidence Grade
       </a>
       ).
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       For  those readers who just want the bottom line I will show the evidence grades
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       that the
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.naturalstandard.com/" target="_blank">
         Natural Standard
        </a>
       </strong>
       has assigned to some  of the nutritional supplements mentioned in TRANSCEND.
       <a href="EVIDENCE.html" target="_blank">
        My prior essay discusses the detailed process  by which Natural Standard assigns their grades
       </a>
       .  Here I'm just providing a quick overview of their recommendations for  some common indications - largely to provide balance to Ray's and Terry's enthusiastic  endorsements.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       Unfortunately,  Natural Standards reviews are not available to the public. However,
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       if you want to see a careful scientific review of your  favorite vitamin or supplement you can look
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       it up on the website of the National Center  for Complementary and Alternative Medicine (NCCAM).
       <a href="http://nccam.nih.gov/health/atoz.htm" target="_blank">
        This is NCCAMs A-Z Search Page
       </a>
       .
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Omega-3  Fatty Acids (Fish Oil, EPA, DHA)
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Fish  Oil gets an A Evidence Grade for preventing cardiovascular disease
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       in those that have it and for reducing elevated blood  pressure.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <strong>
       <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
        Fish intake
       </span>
      </strong>
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        gets  a B grade for preventing cardiovascular disease
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       in those that  don't have it already. (Note: that's
       <em>
        fish
       </em>
       not fish oil.)
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <strong>
       <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
        Fish oil gets a C grade for preventing atherosclerosis  and strokes
       </span>
      </strong>
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       and for preventing  restenosis after angioplasty.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       It gets Cs and Ds for a host of other diseases (arrhythmia, cancer, angina, diabetes).
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       I personally take EPA/DHA.. The American Heart  Association and the FDA both recommend eating fish, especially for those with  cardiovascular disease. (Note: Overfishing and other human-induced ecodisasters  are a concern of this website. Mercury  accumulation is also a health concern when eating predator fish. )
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       (Flash news item as of
       <a href="http://jama.ama-assn.org/cgi/content/short/303/3/250?home" target="_blank">
        January 2010: JAMA reports association of Omega-3 Fatty Acid consumption and preservation of telomere length
       </a>
       . You want your chromosomal telomeres to be long. They protect the integrity of DNA replication.)
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Vitamin  E (tocopherols, tocotrienols)
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Vitamin  E gets a D Evidence Grade (!!!) for Cancer Prevention,
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <strong>
       <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
        Heart Disease Prevention, and Stroke Prevention.
       </span>
      </strong>
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       Recall  that a D Grade means fairly good scientific evidence
       <strong>
        against this use
       </strong>
       .
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Vitamin  E gets a C grade (unclear or conflicting evidence) for dozens of indications  including atherosclerosis, use as an antioxidant, prostate cancer prevention.
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       If you take large amounts of vitamin E you need to be  aware of recent studies that have shown increased deaths.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       TRANSCEND,  to their credit, does discuss the studies that caused vitamin E to fall from  grace. Ray and Terry feel that the  studies were biased and should have administered all the various congeners of  vitamin E. Following these studies,  Kaiser Hospitals pulled all high dose vitamin E capsules off their  shelves. I also threw mine away.
      </span>
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Vitamin  D
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       Vitamin  D gets a B grade for Osteoporosis prevention.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       I take 400 units per day and also get outside  everyday.
      </span>
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Multivitamins
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       Multivitamins  are not listed among either the Natural Standard's
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       or NCCAM's reviews. I can see why.  Which do you include and how much of each?
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       To me the most impressive aspect of decades of  clinical research on vitamins is
       <strong>
        the failure to show any significant benefit
       </strong>
       .
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       I  take Centrum Liquid because it has only 100% of RDAs - probably worthless but  cheap.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       (I'm really wary of large doses of vitamin C - who  wants kidney stones?.) Ray and Terry  need to
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       cite the evidence that shows that large doses of  vitamins work.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Coenzyme  Q10
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       Coenzyme  Q10 gets a B grade for hypertension (yoga gets an A !).
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       For dozens of other indications, CoQ10 gets only a C: chronic fatigue syndrome,
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       cancer prevention, heart attack prevention, angina,  heart failure, Parkinson's.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       This is one supplement I've studied a lot, since I  take statin drugs to lower my cholesterol.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       I am quite convinced that statins lower your plasma  level of CoQ10
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       and that taking CoQ10 raises it. The real question is  whether intramitochondrial
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       CoQ10 is lowered enough in statin patients to cause symptoms.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       I'm not certain but this drug is part of my daily  regimen. If I didnt take statins, I  wouldnt take it.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Grape  Seed Extract
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       Grape  Seed Extract gets A grades for edema and for chronic venous insufficiency and B  grades for vascular fragility and prevention of diabetic retinopathy.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       However, for cancer prevention and as an antioxidant  it merits only a C. (I tried to take  this capsule a few years ago and had to stop - it felt like a bomb going off in  my esophagus.)
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Alpha-Lipoic  Acid
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       Alpha-Lipoic  Acid rates a B for angina and peripheral vascular disease
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       but only a C for dementia, depression, exercise performance,  fatigue, and memory.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       I had high hopes for this drug since I'm generally a  fan of UC Berkeleys Bruce Ames whose research backs it. Ames has studied mitochondrial function for  years and founded a company, Juvenon, that sells Alpha-Lipoic Acid in  combination with Acetyl-L-carnitine, another drug on the TRANSCEND list. Since I'm always looking for a safe energy or  cognitive enhancer, I tried Juvenon. It seemed to work for a few days then I  was back to baseline.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       This seems to be another drug that works in the lab  but doesn't stand up to prolonged clinical scrutiny.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       <strong>
        Resveratrol
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-autospace:none;">
      <span style="font-size:10.0pt; ">
       Resveratrol  merits only a C grade for prevention of cardiovascular disease and breast  cancer, and as an anti-aging tonic. I do not take this drug, although I'm very  interested in research done at Sirtris by  David Sinclair of Harvard that showed life extension in a variety of lab  animals when given huge doses. Sirtris  was acquired by Glaxo for 700 million dollars.
      </span>
     </p>
