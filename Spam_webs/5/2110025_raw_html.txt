       <a id="stepanchor-step2">

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 2: Cut thimble
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SXZW456GO502VMF" data-entry-url="/id/faux-flower-planter-necklace/step2/cut-thimble/" id="photoset-SXZW456GO502VMF">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SXZW456GO502VMF" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FGQ/L9P8/GOCNON2X/FGQL9P8GOCNON2X.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FGQL9P8GOCNON2X" data-notes="" href="/file/FGQL9P8GOCNON2X" id="photoset-link-FT1RWCBGO6VAIPR-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="">
           <img alt="Picture of cut thimble" class="photoset-photo id_FGQL9P8GOCNON2X " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FGQ/L9P8/GOCNON2X/FGQL9P8GOCNON2X.MEDIUM.jpg" style="height:300px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        Using a hacksaw, two slits were cut into either side of the thimble. Each slit is approximately half the length of the thimble. Depending on necklace type used, and thimble design, your cuts may be different.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        The chain will need to be separated and the ends placed into these slots, then glued in place. An alternative to cutting slits is to use a small drill bit and bore through the thimble. The necklace can then be laced through the openings.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Ensure that the necklace connection points are not too low on the thimble, else the planter will tip upside down when filled. Above halfway should suffice.

         Great question:,
         <strong>
          kristylynn84
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Yes, the planter will sit correctly when worn, but care needs to be taken when positioning the chain location in regards to the thimble.
         <a href="http://www.instructables.com/id/faux-flower-planter-necklace/step3/separate-chain-and-insert-into-thimble/" rel="nofollow">
          Step 3
         </a>
         states "
         <em>
          Take care to notice the position of your chain while the glue is setting, hold the slack of the chain above the rim of the thimble to ensure that the thimble will hang correctly when the necklace is worn
         </em>
         ."
         <br/>
         <br/>
         This step is directed towards planter placement when worn, proper planning and cuts into the thimble will keep your planter upright at all times. If in doubt, make sure that there is a weight in the bottom of the thimble, like a small nut or washer to add extra weight, or cut a smaller vertical slit for the planter to rest in. This will allow your planter to naturally hang with the bottom always down.
