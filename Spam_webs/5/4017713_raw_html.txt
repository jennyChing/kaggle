       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         St. Patricks Day Rainbow Ribbon Shakers
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-04T08:30:41+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 4, 2015
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://aparentingproduction.com/author/catherinem" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Catherine
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://aparentingproduction.com/2015/03/st-patricks-day-rainbow-ribbon-shakers.html#comments">
           5 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         I love a good St Patricks Day rainbow craft. It gets me in the mood for spring. I like spring. Its so much less snowy than this winter business weve been having. Rainbows and pots of gold are kind of an important part of St Patricks Day festivities right? Well, that and four leaf clovers, leprechauns, and parades.
        </p>
        <p>
         Each year my family makes our way to our citys downtown to watch or take part in the parade. When my oldest daughter was younger we would stand along the side of the parade route wave at floats of children, Irish dance groups, and local mayoral candidates grabbing candy or flyers from them as they all walked by. Now Madeline is usually marching in the St Patricks Day parade with her schools cheer team and that leaves my husband and I to hang out with my youngest along the route. While she is entertained with the usual floats, dancers, candy, and flyers, she also has a deep desire to do what her sister is doing. Shes not old enough for an official parade appearance but that doesnt mean we cant make her parade experience a little more festive. This year that means well be making some parade noise from the sidelines with some rainbow ribbon shakers.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-Craft-6.jpg">
          <img alt="How to make a St Patrick's Day Rainbow Ribbon Shaker Craft" class="aligncenter wp-image-11032" height="600" src="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-Craft-6.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The rainbow ribbon shaker is a magical mix of rainbow ribbon wand and rhythm shaker. Its not only a festive little St Patricks Day rainbow craft but its also a great addition to playtime, dance time, or parade time. After making these weve had some pretty fun rhythmic shaking and ribbon twirling dance parties in our living room.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-craft.jpg">
          <img alt="How to make a St Patrick's Day Rainbow Ribbon Shaker Craft" class="aligncenter wp-image-11034" height="400" src="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-craft.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Supplies Needed to Make a St. Patricks Day Rainbow Ribbon Shaker
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Toilet paper tubes
         </li>
         <li>
          Black and gold paint
         </li>
         <li>
          Paint brushes
         </li>
         <li>
          Ribbons in the each color of the rainbow cut to about 18 inches
         </li>
         <li>
          Stapler and Staples
         </li>
         <li>
          Beans, beads, rice, or other shaker filling
         </li>
         <li>
          Optional: Gold glitter glue, sparkly washi tape or ribbon, glitter stickers
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How to Make a St. Patricks Day Rainbow Ribbon Shaker
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shakers-Craft.jpg">
           <img alt="How to make a Rainbow Ribbon Shaker Craft" class="aligncenter wp-image-11036" height="400" src="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shakers-Craft.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Paint the about 2/3 of the toilet paper tube black and the remaining 1/3 gold. The tube will be the pot of gold your rainbow ribbons will lead to.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shakers-craft-3.jpg">
          <img alt="How to make a St Patrick's Day Rainbow Ribbon Shaker Craft" class="aligncenter wp-image-11035" height="400" src="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shakers-craft-3.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         When the paint dries pinch the edges of the black end of the tube together. Next, staple along the edge, closing off one end of the tube and creating a pocket. Fill the tube about 1/2 full with your shaker filling. We used beans in one and rice in others and have enjoyed the difference in sounds.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-Craft-4.jpg">
          <img alt="How to make a St Patrick's Day Rainbow Ribbon Shaker Craft" class="aligncenter wp-image-11030" height="400" src="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-Craft-4.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Gather your ribbon streamers together. You can wrap tape around one end to hold them together if youd like. Flatten the top together so its perpendicular to the bottom fold, ensuring the filling has room to move around inside the tube. Insert about a 1/2 inch of the ribbon into the open end of the shaker and staple the top edge together. You may need to add an extra staple or two to help secure the ribbons in place.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-Craft-7.jpg">
          <img alt="How to make a St Patrick's Day Rainbow Craft" class="aligncenter wp-image-11033" height="600" src="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-Craft-7.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want, you can add glitter, sparkly washi tape orribbon to your pot of gold to add some extra bling to your ribbon shaker.
        </p>
        <p>
         When youre finished let the shakers loose and have a blast shaking your pot of gold and twirling your rainbow ribbons around. You never know, maybe youll even lure a leprechaun your way.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-Craft-5.jpg">
          <img alt="How to make a St Patrick's Day Rainbow Ribbon Shaker Craft" class="aligncenter wp-image-11031" height="600" src="http://aparentingproduction.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Rainbow-Ribbon-Shaker-Craft-5.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
