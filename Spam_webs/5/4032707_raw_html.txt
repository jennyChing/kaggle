         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Dont rock the boat. Dont stir the pot. Leave well enough alone. Go with the flow. These were sayings that I had lived by for a long time.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          When I was little, I was very shy, quiet, and highly empathetic. I held onto the emotions I feltfiercely. I learned from a young age that speaking up meant possibly starting conflict (something that I was very afraid of), so I swallowed my feelings every time something happened that I didnt agree with. Or, if I did speak up and someone disagreed with what I had to say, Id immediately back down and apologize.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          When I began seeing energy healers Id hear over and over that my throat chakra was blocked and that I needed to express myself.I didnt understand. I had no issues with expressing love and affection. I didnt know what the problem was. What wasnt I seeing?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          With time, I learned that it was the negative emotions that I was expressing, not the positive ones. Every time I felt pain, sadness, discouragement, or anger, Id withhold my emotions and theyd energetically attach to my throat chakra. Every feeling that I did not acknowledge, express, and let go became stuck. It got so bad that for a while I stopped feeling any emotions at all. I felt numb. I couldnt feel very happy or very sad. I just had this vague sense of discontentment no matter how positive my life was. I was so afraid of conflict that I didnt express any negativity at all and I started having throat related problems (swollen glands, hypothyroidism) and big time emotional baggage. I also didnt have any energy, I always wanted to sleep and eat because they were the only activities that made me feel better.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          I had dug myself a big hole and I knew that Iwas the only one that could get myself out. So slowly, I had to deal with all of the emotions that I had stored for so many years. I had to chip away at themoutain of emotions,writing down everything that I felt so that I could isolate the problems in my life and then, eventually, discover the solutions.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Becoming aware of my issues was not all that difficult  painful at times, sure, but certainly easier than the next step of the process  changing my behaviour.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          I had to speak my truth. I knew that I needed to start communicating the buried feelingsI had discovered. I needed to speak to those who hurt me, stand up for myself, and change a lot in my life. I knew that this would involve the oh-so dreaded conflict. I decided that my fear of conflict did not outweigh my desire for change. I decided to speak up and stir the pot for once.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Im happy to report that Im still here! The conflict didnt kill me like my ego said it might. I made it out tothe other side in one piece having learnt a lot in the process. Ive written down 7 things Ive learned from unblocking and opening my throat chakra. I wish I had knownthemwhen I started this journey, so thats why I decided to share them with you today and hopefully, save you from struggling in the same way I did.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="color: #00ccff">
           1. Embrace those who challenge you
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Oh boy. There are a few people that come to mind as I write this. The people that challenge you most are typically the ones that you dont like. You dont like them because they make choices that you dont agree with. These people challenge your view of things. The trouble here is that often react byaggressively defending our own ways of doing things or by trying to change them so that they adopt our viewpoints.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          If you embrace them and accept them as the teachers that they are, youll stop fighting them and begin learning from them. These people are giving you the opportunity to change your way of doing things and see another way of living. They each have something to teach you. Look for the lesson and appreciate that they are in your life.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          For example, a person can teach you to stand up for yourself because they take advantage of you. They challenge your way of being by their treatment of you. Other times, a person can teach you about love by never expressing it to you. You learn that expressing love can make the receiver feel loved and supported and the opposite if love is not expressed. This person has challenged your way of being.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="color: #00ccff">
           2. Think before you express yourself
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Feelinga certain way doesnt give you permission to be insensitive to the feelings of others. Its okay to be angry or sad, but not to intentionally hurtanother with your words. Be sensitive. Communicate your message with as much love as you can.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="color: #00ccff">
           3. Let it Go
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Howthe other person will receive your message is out of your control. As long as you say your message with love, you must allow the other person to feel what they need to feel. Be you. Be loving. Be open and honest and let it go. Dont try to control the outcome.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="color: #00ccff">
           4. Conflict is healthy!
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Conflict is natural and is necessary in order for change to happen. When someone introduces a new idea to you, that fresh idea will conflict with your previous view of the world. You choose at that point to adopt the new idea or to reject it. Either way, there was an initial conflict when you heard about the new idea. If you never experienced conflict in your life, you would never grow. You needto be exposed to new ideas and behaviours to challenge your perceptions, otherwise you would be stuck experiencing the same thing over and over.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Ifyouve spoken up and created conflict, know that youve challenged that persons way of being. Allow them to accept or reject your idea without judgment and try your best to maintain your open, loving honesty.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Plus, people look silly when theyre screaming and waving their hands in the air and youre speaking calmly and acting cool as a cucumber!
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="color: #00ccff">
           5. Your emotions can get stuck
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          Your emotions colour your experience. Think of them as a lens that you view the world through everyday. Every time you have a different emotion, your lens changes colour. Choose the lens and then let it go. You dont need to hold onto a feeling.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          We get into trouble when we hold on tightly to past pain. People will do this out of fear. Fear of expression, conflict, powerlessness, helplessness, and so on.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="color: #00ccff">
           6. Do not force others to express themselves
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          It takes a confident person to be able to express themselves honestly, openly, and lovingly. You are not in control of how others deal with their personal expression. The most you can do is to create a safe, non-judgmental space for them to be honest with you. Be gentle and loving with them. Eventually, they will know it is safe to say what they have to say to you.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="color: #00ccff">
           7. You are in charge
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          If you get the sense youre carrying a lot of emotional baggage, remember that you are the only one who can release it. You can seek the help of a friend, family member, or qualified professional, but ultimately, if you arent willing to open up and let go, youll remain right where you are. So be strong. Be you and open your heart to change.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify">
          The truth about truth is that if you dont express it, youll continue to enable that which doesnt feel good to you.So if you want real change, speak up and open your beautiful throat chakra. Your words are powerful. Use them to create positive change in your life!
         </p>
