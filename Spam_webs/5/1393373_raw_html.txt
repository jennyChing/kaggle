         <p>
          A
          <a href="http://www.motorcycle.com/manufacturer/harley-davidson.html">
           <strong>
            Harley-Davidson
           </strong>
          </a>
          three-wheeler was caught by our spy photographer at the same time as the 2015
          <strong>
           Road Glide
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          It appears to be a new riff on the
          <strong>
           Lehman Trikes
          </strong>
          -derived architecture of the existing
          <strong>
           Tri-Glide
          </strong>
          , but with totally new bodywork and, unusually, no front fairing. That, along with the relatively slim front fender, gives it something of a
          <strong>
           Fat Boy
          </strong>
          stance, although the rear fenders are more in line with Harleys
          <strong>
           Breakout
          </strong>
          model.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.motorcycle.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/070214-2015-harley-davidson-freewheeler-trike-spy-HarleyTrike.b10.KGP_.ed-3355215501-O.jpg">
           <img alt="070214-2015-harley-davidson-freewheeler-trike-spy-HarleyTrike.b10.KGP.ed-3355215501-O" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11047" height="422" src="http://www.motorcycle.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/070214-2015-harley-davidson-freewheeler-trike-spy-HarleyTrike.b10.KGP_.ed-3355215501-O-633x422.jpg" width="633"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The bulbous trunk between them suggests decent luggage space even though the trike does without the top box of the Tri-Glide. The fork and front wheel are identical to the existing
          <strong>
           Street Glide
          </strong>
          and the new Road Glides, while the headlights chrome mounting panel appears to be unique to the trike.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.motorcycle.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/070214-2015-harley-davidson-freewheeler-trike-spy-HarleyTrike.b05.KGP_.ed-3355215466-O.jpg">
           <img alt="070214-2015-harley-davidson-freewheeler-trike-spy-HarleyTrike.b05.KGP.ed-3355215466-O" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-11041" height="422" src="http://www.motorcycle.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/070214-2015-harley-davidson-freewheeler-trike-spy-HarleyTrike.b05.KGP_.ed-3355215466-O-633x422.jpg" width="633"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          When it comes to a name, last year Harley trademarked the term Freewheeler specifically for a new three-wheeled model, so theres got to be a strong possibility thats the moniker it will carry when its officially unveiled later this year.
         </p>
         <p>
          Like the new Road Glide, the trike gets the
          <strong>
           High Output Twin Cam 103
          </strong>
          air-cooled motor instead of the
          <strong>
           Twin-Cooled
          </strong>
          version with water-cooled cylinder heads thats used in the existing Tri-Glide.
         </p>
