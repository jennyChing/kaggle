        <h2 class="entry-title">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/how-to-use-ezdrummer-with-mixcraft/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to How To Use EzDrummer With Mixcraft">
          How To Use EzDrummer With Mixcraft
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.acoustica.com/mixcraft/" target="_blank">
          <br/>
          Mixcraft 5
         </a>
         is
         <em>
          a powerful yet easy-to-use multi-track recording studio that enables you to record audio, arrange loops, remix tracks, compose with virtual instruments, score and edit video, and add effects to create stunningly professional compositions.
         </em>
         <br/>
         I find it to be an easy to get the hang of application to record/mix music with. I was able to record decent quality guitar in only a few minutes.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.toontrack.com/products.asp?item=7" target="_blank">
          EzDrummer
         </a>
         is a 
         <em>
          a multi-microphone drum sampler designed for musicians and producers in need of a compact, affordable, easy to handle plug-in without compromising sound quality or control.
         </em>
         <br/>
         This is a handy VSTi which can give you realistic sounding drums for your mix without too much cost or effort.
        </p>
        <p>
         In this how-to I will show you how to get EzDrummer working with Mixcraft and how to import the EzDrummer samples into your mix.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture10.png">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-137" height="269" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture10-1024x501.png" title="Capture10" width="550"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <span id="more-126">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="padding-left: 30px;">
         1.
        </h3>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         Firstly start by downloading both programs mentioned above. Then start Mixcraft.you will be shown the following screen.
         <br/>
         Select the build virtual instrument tracks option.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture2.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-128" height="436" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture2-1024x625.png" title="Capture2" width="717"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="padding-left: 30px;">
         2.
        </h3>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <strong>
         </strong>
         Now we want to change the instrument to our EzDrummmer VSTi, so we click this Icon:
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture4.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-130" height="351" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture4.png" title="Capture4" width="717"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         3.
        </h3>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         Under VSTi instruments we should see our ezdrummer preset if it installed correctly.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture5.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-131" height="344" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture5-1024x455.png" title="Capture5" width="777"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         4.
        </h3>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         Selecting it will show this drop down box of details. Select the edit option to open ezdrummer.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture6.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-132" height="464" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture6-1024x612.png" title="Capture6" width="779"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         5.
        </h3>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         This will open the following display. Give the VSTi some time to load all the drum samples and then click on the open grooves button.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture7.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-133" height="435" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture7-1024x575.png" title="Capture7" width="778"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         6.
        </h3>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         Doing so will open the library of drum loops that you can use in your mix. There are many expansions to EzDrummer which can provide many different sounds and loops that you can work with.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture8.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-135" height="456" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture8-1024x602.png" title="Capture8" width="777"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         7.
        </h3>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         When you have found a loop that you like simply drag it from the now playing box into the mixcraft timeline.
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture9.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-136" height="396" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture9-1024x524.png" title="Capture9" width="777"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         Now you can insert fills and record your music over the drum loop!
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
         <a href="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture10.png">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-137" height="380" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Capture10-1024x501.png" title="Capture10" width="777"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope this tutorial helps. I made if fairly brief so if you have any questions feel free to leave me a comment
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://danielflannery.ie/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
          Dan.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
