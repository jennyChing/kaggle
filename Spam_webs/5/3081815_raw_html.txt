                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LHoyk4RzxU8/T9KeshZwDsI/AAAAAAAAKXw/2bvULD1d-Lk/s72-c/IMG_0417.JPG" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Irish Cream Chocolate Chip Cookies
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4058418349955058530" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LHoyk4RzxU8/T9KeshZwDsI/AAAAAAAAKXw/2bvULD1d-Lk/s1600/IMG_0417.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="397" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LHoyk4RzxU8/T9KeshZwDsI/AAAAAAAAKXw/2bvULD1d-Lk/s400/IMG_0417.JPG" width="400"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <em>
                           For the chocolate chip lovers, here is a different version with quite a wonderful addition. Irish cream liqueur. Your in for a treat! Hope you like them as much as we did! You don't have to be Irish to love them but it's agreat time of the year to celebrateeither way and make them for St. Patrick's Day !
                          </em>
                         </span>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1/2 teaspoon baking powder
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1 teaspoon baking soda
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1/2 teaspoon salt
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1/2 cup butter, softened
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1/2 cup granulated sugar
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1 egg
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1 teaspoon vanilla extract
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1/2 cup Irish Cream
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      2 cups chocolate chips
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      Preheat oven to 325 degrees.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      In a medium bowl, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt; set aside.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      In a large mixing bowl, cream butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and egg together until light and fluffy. Add vanilla extract and Irish Cream; mix until combined.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      Gradually add dry ingredients to butter mixture and blend well. Fold in chocolate chips.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      Shape dough into 1-inch balls. Place on ungreased cookie sheets 1 1/2 inches apart. Flatten with the bottom of a glass coated with non-stick cooking spray.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until golden brown. Cool on cookie sheet for 1 minute
                     </span>
                     .
