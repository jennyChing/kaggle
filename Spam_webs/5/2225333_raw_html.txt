       <div class="fin_story_body">
        <p>
         EUGENE, Ore. - The devil, aliens or government agents?
        </p>
        <p>
         How about convenience store clerks?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://search.kval.com/default.aspx?ct=r&amp;q=%22conspiracy%20theories%22">
          Conspiracy theories
         </a>
         have surrounded crop circles for years - and the answer, according to a University of Oregon scientist, could be as simple as a microwave oven.
        </p>
        <p>
         The intricate designs started cropping up in Oregon fields in the 1960s. The art, darkness and mystery behind crop circles have boggled minds and thrilled movie goers for years.
        </p>
        <p>
         Richard Taylor, an Oregon physicist who directs the Material Science Institute, said the answer may be found in your kitchen.
        </p>
        <p>
         Taylor advanced a theory that artists using a device found in microwaves create crop circles in an article published in this month's "Physics World."
        </p>
        <p>
         His theory is that the heat from the microwave actually bends the stalk on the wheat or grass. When the stalk cools, it keeps the shape and contributes to the crop circle design.
        </p>
        <p>
         In other words, Taylor thinks crop circles are the work of artists and not aliens.
        </p>
        <p>
         "There is no doubt in my mind," he said. "I'm just presenting this hypothesis that the artists are using microwaves, but I am absolutely convinced it's artists."
        </p>
        <p>
         Taylor said a group of people will meet in the fields near Stonehenge later this month to test his theory that crop circles are deliberate creations.
        </p>
        <p>
         "The sort of idea that it's just a couple of people fooling around one night on the way back from the bar one night is just completely ridiculous," he added. "These people, the artists would have had to have mapped this out on a computer screen first, well in advance."
        </p>
        <p>
         Taylor suggests the artists carry GPS navigators to create hundreds of circles and intricate designs.
        </p>
        <p>
         But if this is the work of artists, wouldn't someone have come forward after all these years?
        </p>
        <p>
         "Well, this is one of the funny things about this art movement," Taylor said. "It was actually started trying to convince people that it was actually produced by aliens. They want to produce something that is so intricate that it's beyond human capabilities."
        </p>
        <p>
         And if artists aren't using microwaves? Taylor said they should.
        </p>
        <p>
         "It would make life a lot easier for them," he said.
        </p>
