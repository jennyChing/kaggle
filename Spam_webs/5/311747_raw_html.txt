       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Weight Loss Happens the Moment You Dont Give Up
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
           Tony Schober, CPT
          </span>
         </span>
         <div class="fbmeta">
          <div class="all-buttons">
           <!-- Facebook Like Button -->
           <div class="fb-share-button" data-type="button_count" href="http://www.coachcalorie.com/weight-loss-dont-give-up/">
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p class="entry-meta">
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://coachcalorie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/dont-give-up.jpg">
          <img alt="don't give up" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2401" height="314" src="http://coachcalorie.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/dont-give-up.jpg" title="don't give up" width="600"/>
         </a>
         How many times have you busted your butt in the gym and in the kitchen, only to step on the scale and not see your weight change one single bit? Maybe it even went up some! What gives?
        </p>
        <h2>
         Weight Loss is Not a Straight Line
        </h2>
        <p>
         First off, get rid of that scale  its worthless. Use
         <a href="http://www.coachcalorie.com/measure-weight-loss-progress/" target="_blank" title="10 Ways to Effectively Measure Weight Loss Progress">
          these 10 ways to effectively measure weight loss progress instead
         </a>
         . However, if youre dead set on using the scale, you need to understand that weight loss does not occur in a straight line. You could be doing everything right. You could be weight training several times a week. You could be doing cardio on your off days. You could have your diet perfect.
        </p>
        <p>
         Even with all the stars aligned, your weight might not change for weeks at a time. But doesnt 3500 calories equal a pound? If I eat 500 calories less each day, should I lose a pound a week? In theory, that is true. However, in practice, thats just not the case. Your metabolism is a unique individual. It has a mind of its own. It will drop the weight when its ready, but not a minute before then. So, when is it ready to let the fat go?
        </p>
        <div class="adsense1">
         <!-- CC Responsive Ad1 TI -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-3986402200140790" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="7869003019" style="display:block">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <h2>
         Weight Loss Happens the Moment You Dont Give Up
        </h2>
        <p>
         Thats how it works. You push and push and obsess over the scale because its not moving. You begin to question why you even try, if all your hard work is for nothing. Then, that moment comes  the moment of truth. Do you throw in the towel, or do you continue pushing forward? The majority of people throw in the towel, and thats unfortunate, because if they were to have just given it another day, that scale would have been their friend again.
        </p>
        <p>
         The moral of the story? Dont give up. If you know youre doing everything right, keep on doing it. Eventually, the weight is going to come off. You cant obsess over the scale. Your weight loss over the course of months is going to be one big zig-zag from point A to point B. However, point B is always going to be lower than A if you are doing everything right.
        </p>
        <p>
         Many people think that they should step on the scale every day, and it should be less than the day before. They believe that points A and B should be connected with a ruler  not going to happen.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Be patient. Be consistent. Push through the moment of quitting. Success will follow.
         </strong>
        </p>
