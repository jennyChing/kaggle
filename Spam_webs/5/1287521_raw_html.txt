          Top 15 Best Selling Toys in History

         <p>
          Anyone growing up with He-Man and Rainbow Brite in their toy collection may be a little biased when it comes to picking out the best selling toys to ever hit the market. Admittedly, its not an exact science as not all companies are open about their numbers, and in the case of some older toys, where the companies that made them are no longer around, data is hard (if not impossible) to access.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Still, certain toys stand the test of time and post some pretty amazing numbers in the process. Others blow up overnight and sell crazy numbers in just one or two decades. Youll find a little of each on our list of the
          <strong>
           Top 15 Best-Selling Toys in History
          </strong>
          . Let us know in the comments section whether you think these are deserving.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1. Barbie
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.csmonitor.com/The-Culture/Family/Modern-Parenthood/2013/0717/Barbie-sales-are-down-but-here-s-betting-she-s-not-out" target="_blank">
           The Christian Science Monitor
          </a>
          reported that sales had declined for
          <a href="http://www.barbie.com" target="_blank">
           Barbie
          </a>
          for the fourth consecutive quarter. That was in the middle of 2013. However, sales remain at $3 billion per year, and considering that the pint-sized princess has been a mainstay in little-girl culture for more than 50 years, wed be willing to bet shell be around for a long time to come. Mattel estimated that as of 2006, theyd
          <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/business/5370398.stm" target="_blank">
           sold more than one billion dolls
          </a>
          worldwide. Regardless of whats going on with Barbie in the present, one cannot deny the overwhelming impact the toy and its accessories have had on popular culture.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/1_barbie.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.sanfranciscosentinel.com/?p=20124" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           San Francisco Sentinel
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2. The Yo-Yo
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          It really is impossible to tell how many yo-yos have been produced since their invention around 2,500 years ago, but anything with that kind of staying power has to be considered one of the best selling toys of all time. On the commercial US market, theyve been around since Pedro Flores opened the Yo-yo Manufacturing Company in Santa Barbara, California, circa 1928. Just one year later, Flores had expanded his operations from one plant to three, employing 600 workers and producing 300,000 yo-yos per day, according to the
          <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20080120172822/http://www.nationalyoyo.org/museum/pedroflores.htm" target="_blank">
           National Yo-Yo Museum
          </a>
          . Donald F. Duncan knew a good thing when he saw it and purchased the business from Flores just one year later, and the
          <a href="https://www.yo-yo.com" target="_blank">
           Duncan Yo-Yo
          </a>
          was born. Today, people from all over the world participate in yo-yo competitions each year.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/2_the-yoyo.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yo-yo" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Wikipedia Commons
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3. Easy-Bake Oven
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          In February 2013, the Easy-Bake Oven celebrated its 50th anniversary. At that point,
          <a href="http://investor.hasbro.com/releasedetail.cfm?ReleaseID=737645" target="_blank">
           Hasbro
          </a>
          reported more than 30 million ovens had been sold throughout the products existence along with another 150 million+ mix refills. The core product continues to sell at or near seven figures each year, so there are absolutely no signs of slowing down.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/3_easy-bake-oven.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://investor.hasbro.com/releasedetail.cfm?ReleaseID=737645" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Hasbro
          </a>
          <span id="more-5949">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4. Radio Flyer
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          When Italian immigrant Antonio Pasin arrived in the US at the age of 16, he had no way of knowing that he would one day create a toy that still sells steadily close to a century later.
          <a href="http://www.radioflyer.com/media/wysiwyg/www-inc-com.pdf" target="_blank">
           Radio Flyer
          </a>
          , based in Chicago, came in to existence in 1917 and as of 2012 had sold more than 100 million wagons and carved a spot in popular culture. In 1992,
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0105211/?ref_=nv_sr_2" target="_blank">
           a feature film
          </a>
          narrated and starring Tom Hanks and directed by Richard Donner was even made, partly in its honor. While critics and audiences at the time were gun-shy about recommending the film due to its theme of child abuse, the film has since found a devoted following, thus bringing the little red wagon to a modern generation of fans.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/4_radio-flyer.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://blogs.truckinweb.com/6486821/editorials/forbidden-fantasy-bagged-wagon/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Blogs.Truckinweb.com
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5. Silly Putty
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          In 1943, Scottish engineer James Wright, while working at General Electrics laboratory in New Haven, Connecticut, combined boric acid and silicone oil in a test tube. The resulting compound became polymerized, according to the team at
          <a href="http://www2.crayola.com/mediacenter/index.cfm?display=press_release&amp;news_id=164" target="_blank">
           Crayola
          </a>
          . Wright removed a resulting goo from the test tube. He then shipped it around to a number of engineers, hoping there might be some use for his creation. From there a marketing consultant named Peter Hodgson saw the potential in it, and by 1955, kids in the 6 to 12 demographic were in love with the stuff. The last reported numbers from 2009 indicated that more than 300 million eggs of Silly Putty have been sold since its inception.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/5_silly-putty.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.kidsdiscover.com/quick-reads/weird-science-the-accidental-invention-of-silly-putty/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Kids Discover
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           6. Transformers
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          The shapeshifting robots would have to go on any list of best selling toys despite the fact that theyve only been around since the 1980s. Excluding the cartoon movie of that same decade and the hugely popular international toy line, the property has earned
          <a href="http://boxofficemojo.com/search/?q=transformers" target="_blank">
           more than $2 billion in profits
          </a>
          from just the three live-action films directed by Michael Bay. Obviously, a fourth film is planned. Meanwhile, the toys are as popular as ever thanks to the resurgence brought about by spinoff cartoons and the first live-action film of 2007.
          <a href="http://www.hasbro.com/transformers/en_us/" target="_blank">
           Hasbro
          </a>
          is the proud owner.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/6_Transformers.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20140314010014/http://www.fanpop.com:80/clubs/transformers/images/35024957/title/sandstorm-photo" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Fanpop
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           7. G.I. Joe
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.hasbro.com/gijoe/en_US/" target="_blank">
           The Greatest American Hero
          </a>
          was introduced in 1964 by Hasbro as an 11 1/2-inch male figure that wore uniforms representing the US military and featured 21 moving parts. By the 1980s, the product had undergone a bit of a facelift, turning in to a series of 3 3/4-inch figures modeled after the popular cartoon and comic book characters. As of 2004, the company had sold
          <a href="http://www.toyhalloffame.org/toys/gi-joe" target="_blank">
           more than 400 million figures
          </a>
           a pace of around 10 million per year. Considering Joe is as popular today as he was in the 80s, and much more popular than he was at his inception, wed say tacking on another 100 million to that number is a conservative estimate.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/7_gi-joe.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.16bit.com/toyfair2011-gijoe.asp" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           16bit.com
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           8. Hot Wheels
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.hotwheelsmedia.com/i/brand-facts/fast-facts" target="_blank">
           Hot Wheels
          </a>
          , the classic die cast toys from Mattel, were introduced in 1968. The company has gone on to produce more than 4 billion cars featuring 11,000 variations and over 800 models. Mattel estimates that 41 million have grown up playing with their miniature cars, and notes that the average collector has around 1,550 cars in their collection. The average child between the ages of five and 15 owns about 41 cars. None of these numbers take into consideration ancillary items like racetrack play sets.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/8_hotwheels.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hot_Wheels" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Wikipedia Commons
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           9. Etch-A-Sketch
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          In 1955, a 30-year-old electrician named Andre Cassagnes was installing a factory light switch plate and made some pencil markings on its translucent decal. After peeling the decal from the switch plate, he saw the image had transferred to the other side. This was the origin point for his idea that would become the childrens drawing toy. According to
          <a href="http://www.ohioart.com/about/etch-a-sketch" target="_blank">
           Ohio Art
          </a>
          , the makers of Etch-A-Sketch, more than 150 million of the devices have been sold since 1960, the year it first went to market.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/9_Etch-A-Sketch.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Taj_Mahal_drawing_on_an_Etch-A-Sketch.jpg" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Wikipedia Commons
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           10. LEGOs
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.lego.com/en-us/default.aspx" target="_blank">
           LEGO
          </a>
          , derived from the Danish words for play well, were invented by carpenter Ole Christiansen in 1949. The LEGO company patented the idea just nine years later. The original LEGOs were just red-and-white bricks that interlocked in a way that allowed children to build things without limits. According to
          <a href="http://content.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,2049243_2048651_2049040,00.html" target="_blank">
           Time
          </a>
          magazine, just six blocks can be combined in 102,981,500 ways. Today, the company has produced more than 320 billion blocks. Theyve also inspired some pretty genius creations from hobbyists.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/10_LEGOs.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://cubeme.com/tag/lego/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           CubeMe
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           11. Mr. Potato Head
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          The Spud was a groundbreaking toy in many ways. For one, it was the first toy with a dedicated TV commercial. Secondly, it earned more than $4 million in just a few months (in the 1950s). And most amazing of all, it actually got kids to touch their vegetables. Created with that specific purpose by George Learner, no one foresaw what a huge success it would become (and continues to be). As of 2012, Mr. Potato Head has sold more than 100 million toys in 30 markets, according to
          <a href="http://investor.hasbro.com/releasedetail.cfm?ReleaseID=648475" target="_blank">
           Hasbro
          </a>
          . He just turned 61 in 2013.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/11_mr-potato-head.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.humortimes.com/16962/exclusive-interview-with-mr-potato-head/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Humor Times
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           12. Hula Hoop
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          The Hula Hoop is considered the first major fad toy in the sense that when sales were good, they were unheard-of-good, but once the public tired of the product, they plummeted. Now theyre back up to a respectable level, according to the manufacturer
          <a href="http://www.wham-o.com/product.html" target="_blank">
           Wham-O
          </a>
          , but nowhere near the crazy first year of production. During that period (1958), the company sold more than 100 million Hula Hoops for $1.98 each. Based on those 365 days alone, weve got to think the toy has moved to the billion mark by now, but Wham-O isnt saying. They no longer release the individual sales numbers.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/12_hula-hoop.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://natalieplaton.com/five-fun-hula-hoop-games/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Natalie Platon
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           13. Star Wars Action Figures
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Never mind the huge resurgence in popularity of
          <a href="http://starwars.com" target="_blank">
           Star Wars
          </a>
          action figures. If you take just the Kenner line from 1978 to 1985, youre dealing with a product that easily deserves consideration as one of the best selling toys of all time. How do we figure? In a seven-year period, Kenner sold more than 300 million action figures. Hasbro has held the license since the brand left Kenner, and the figures continue to be a big money-maker for the toy company. If theyre not at one billion units yet, just wait until Star Wars: Episode VII releases in 2015.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/13_star-wars.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://starwarsplayset.blogspot.com/2012/07/bespin-cloud-city.html" target="_blank">
           StarWarsPlayset.blogspot.com
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           14. Rubiks Cube
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Created in 1974 but not licensed for sale until 1980, the
          <a href="http://www.rubiks.com" target="_blank">
           Rubiks Cube
          </a>
          hasnt had as long of a shelf life as many of the toys on this list. But in the abbreviated lifespan, its still managed to sell more than 350 million cubes. Hungarian inventor Ern Rubiks invention continues to give most people fits, too, so consider this a mainstay for the foreseeable future.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="300" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/14_rubiks-cube.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://maditsmadfunny.wikia.com/wiki/Rubik's_Cube" target="_blank">
           Mad Wiki Cartoon Network
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           15. Super Soaker
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Former NASA engineer Lonnie Johnson invented the Super Soaker in 1993. The gun was a reinvention of the classic water gun and was the summer-fun equivalent of bringing a bazooka to a gunfight. The Soaker shot distances of up to 50 feet and completely changed the face of backyard warfare. In just 20 years, it has gone on to sell 250 million units with the number continuing to rise. You can get yours from the
          <a href="http://www.hasbro.com/nerf/en_AU/" target="_blank">
           NERF website
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div align="center">
          <img alt="" class="center" height="237" src="https://nowblitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/15_super-soaker.jpg" width="400"/>
         </div>
         <p>
