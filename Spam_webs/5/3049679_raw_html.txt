            <p class="first-text" data-textannotation-id="dbee93508b5931f2b64389e7c81e1788">
             The new tab page built into Chrome (and
             <a href="http://www.extremetech.com/computing/115935-firefox-12-will-feature-long-awaited-new-tab-page-and-home-tab" target="_blank">
              soon, Firefox
             </a>
             ) is a nice touch, but it isn't incredibly customizable, nor is it particularly beautiful. If you're looking for something a little beyond what your browser's default start page has to offer, here are our favorite extensions and services that add a bit of extra functionality and pizazz to your new tabs.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="99ac7ee2da1b9e48ebb66c3e80930f2a">
             We're going to go over both browser extensions and online services, since you can find start pages in both flavors. While browser extensions offer better integratione.g., they have the ability to pop up on a new tab without another extensionweb services are cross-platform, and often offer syncing so you can get your start page on multiple machines. Here are our picks for the best in each category.
            </p>
            <h3 data-textannotation-id="c3230a8291404b93ef410379233e7b46">
             Chrome
            </h3>
            <p data-textannotation-id="09a68390742673016e3b0fa25b7c40c4">
             Chrome has a number of different new tab pages, most of which are based off
             <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5523176/speed-dial-for-chrome-makes-your-new-tab-page-look-awesome" x-inset="1">
              the original Speed Dial extension
             </a>
             for Chrome, with slightly different features. Our favorite is Speed Dial 2, which takes what was so great about the original Speed Dial and added tons of new configuration options.
            </p>

            <h4 data-textannotation-id="df7b3c2bd628f27cccc4f8da11cfb7df">
             The Best: Speed Dial 2
            </h4>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="e25919b27c2343ff07e37ad6d82cebd4">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Lifehacker Faceoff: The Best Start Pages for Your Browser" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--HIJJOYz1--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/18ix6llb9pt9zjpg.jpg" data-chomp-id="18ix6llb9pt9zjpg" data-format="jpg" height="310" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--HIJJOYz1--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/18ix6llb9pt9zjpg.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="0ec52cbb9099c3e1f807bc874fc097c4">
             <b>
              <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/jpfpebmajhhopeonhlcgidhclcccjcik" target="_blank">
               Speed Dial 2
              </a>
             </b>
             , on the surface, looks like a very basic start page. It's just a blank page on which you can add thumbnails to your favorite sites, complete with built-in logos for some of the most popular pages. If a page doesn't have a logo, it'll show a preview of the page, but you can always add your own logos if you want (though you'll have to upload them somewhere first). Mousing over to the right side of the page shows your Chrome bookmarks and recently closed tabs.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="1c8cc8046e9b1611d6b076a6603618a6">
             Where Speed Dial 2 really shines is its options. It has more than you'll probably even know what to do with, letting you customize every pixel of your speed dial page. From the number of columns to the dial spacing to the background, rounded corners of the thumbnail, and tons of other options, there is no shortage of ways to make your page look exactly how you want it to.
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="182810c8a005e8def51fb45a468e6e3e">
             The Rest
            </h4>
            <p data-textannotation-id="a52f1d1a09c06dcdd744f62eae2e1cc0">
             If you want a few more features and are willing to sacrifice looksor at least work very hard to get them looking as goodhere are our other favorites for Chrome.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="b40e1a1b205ed6e590ec4fcf0d1f5b9a">
             <b>
              <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/dgpdioedihjhncjafcpgbbjdpbbkikmi" target="_blank">
               Speed Dial
              </a>
             </b>
             (the original) doesn't offer a ton over Speed Dial 2, though it looks just as pretty. If all you want is something simple, it's a good option, plus it lets you see your bookmarks and recently closed tabs on the main speed dial page, rather than in the (sometimes annoying) sidebar that Speed Dial 2 has. This is easily our second favorite of the bunch.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="98a06798e067def978843bad0f3e09d8">
             <b>
              <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/gejpfnhaoddfpmnpcbiopknepgonapmf" target="_blank">
               EverTab
              </a>
             </b>
             is a new extension that offers a lot of customization, a slightly different look, and one big feature missing from the others: the ability to access Chrome's internal pages. That means you can access your extensions, apps, history, flags, and other Chrome settings pages right from your new tab page, which is incredibly awesome. It may not be quite as pretty as Speed Dial and Speed Dial 2 out of the box, but that feature alone makes it worth trying out.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="9168a14033723d6b2b22ee0abece7210">
             <b>
              <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/iniabgbbmccaomaocmhcfioahgipigbh" target="_blank">
               Start!
              </a>
             </b>
             is a lesser known extension that performs many of the same functions as the other new tab pages. You can add a ton of thumbnails to your main page, access your Chrome apps, and specify a custom background image (though it'll automatically pick one from Flickr if you don't). What separates Start from the others is the ability to add your favorite RSS feeds to the bottom of the page, which is pretty nice. Again, it doesn't necessarily look as pretty out of the box, but it can look pretty good if you're willing to put a bit of work into it.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="6ee9166cf63f7eab1e58508ced348da5">
             Lastly, if you're a fan of Windows' Metro UI,
             <b>
              <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/mgmiemnjjchgkmgbeljfocdjjnpjnmcg" target="_blank">
               Awesome New Tab Page
              </a>
             </b>
             adds it to your start page, letting you configure shortcuts and add other widgets, like notes, a clock, the weather, and more. It's in developer preview right now, but if you want a bit more than just shortcuts out of your new tab page, it's a really awesome (if slightly complicated) alternative.
            </p>
            <h3 data-textannotation-id="751ebc81b5f44cf36cd1685062259ea3">
             Firefox
            </h3>
            <p data-textannotation-id="1f1003310bda0af3ab1b7bf78db5b5ed">
             Firefox users don't have quite as amazing a selection as Chrome users, but its still chock full of new tab extensions for you to try out. Our favorite is FVD Speed Dial, which provides both great looks and a ton of features.
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="efd783c3779f20bec3144b0df76d6d27">
             The Best: FVD Speed Dial
            </h4>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="4698eed6063b1179912f99abd37d890b">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Lifehacker Faceoff: The Best Start Pages for Your Browser" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--cVEX_ta9--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/18ix6llb33usdjpg.jpg" data-chomp-id="18ix6llb33usdjpg" data-format="jpg" height="330" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--cVEX_ta9--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/18ix6llb33usdjpg.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="609129b96711e9277aded33916f4ef14">
             <b>
              <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/fvd-speed-dial" target="_blank">
               FVD Speed Dial
              </a>
             </b>
             is what you make of it. You can add new shortcuts and let them load previews of their sites, or give them custom logos to make the start page really pop. It also has built-in logos for popular sites, and while the list of sites isn't huge, you can choose from a
             <em>
              ton
             </em>
             of different thumbnails for each site, meaning you can make some pretty snazzy looking pages.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="3fa86af613ac9b7195748ea5f18d69d8">
             It also has a ton of options for how the page works, letting you add or remove a search bar, a "most visited" page, recently closed tabs, not to mention choose a background and customize the color of everything you see. It isn't quite as simple to use as its Chrome brethren, but with a bit of fiddling, you have the potential to make a pretty awesome looking page that behaves exactly like you want it to.
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="182810c8a005e8def51fb45a468e6e3e">
             The Rest
            </h4>
            <p data-textannotation-id="6c0a011cd22af519067805dbcc1083b0">
             <b>
              <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/new-tab-king" target="_blank">
               New Tab King
              </a>
             </b>
             is one of Firefox's most popular start page extensions, offering a few extra views, not to mention a collapsible sidebar that contains your recently closed tabs, other bookmarked sites, and even a to-do list. Its only downside is that it isn't very prettyat least not out of the boxand it's kind of hard to use. But, it does have a lot of options, so as long as you're willing to dig, you can do quite a bit with it.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="b46ecb9a0af6006a9604cbbbce5f1d70">
             <b>
              <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/super-start" target="_blank">
               Super Start
              </a>
             </b>
             has also become very popular on Firefox, and offers a happy medium between some of the features New Tab King adds without sacrificing looks. Essentially, it's your traditional thumbnail-based start page, but with a nice to-do list sidebar on the right (which you can use for other notes, or anything else you want). It also offers handy little buttons for recent tabs and bookmarks.
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="d90d5693274e6c756784ffa4015aaa63">
             Lastly,
             <b>
              <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/fast-dial-5721" target="_blank">
               Fast Dial
              </a>
             </b>
             is about as dead simple as it gets. You have a completely blank page with nine big thumbnails, which you can assign to your favorite sites, give big logos to, and give the whole page a theme to bring it together. You can find a lot of user-created themes and logos for Fast Dial over at
             <a href="http://userlogos.org/" target="_blank">
              userlogos.org
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <h3 data-textannotation-id="5345a59ace88bf7ab736d932da6dfafb">
             Cross-Browser
            </h3>
            <p data-textannotation-id="89e39f93b4f892f89622fdfb5ec0623d">
             These start pages aren't extensions, but instead web sites you visit or pages you store on your local machine. They should work with any browser (whether it be Firefox, Chrome, or something else).
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="83391004acdb92bdeec4cd03c9657b98">
             The Best: Myfav.es
            </h4>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="f6a2fe86692d2b245adb91a727a6c04d">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Lifehacker Faceoff: The Best Start Pages for Your Browser" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--_dyUeyVR--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/18ix6lp9dbtfajpg.jpg" data-chomp-id="18ix6lp9dbtfajpg" data-format="jpg" height="393" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--_dyUeyVR--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/18ix6lp9dbtfajpg.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="968956a6bd8520204a78cbfc09c3bdb4">
             <a href="http://myfav.es" target="_blank">
              Myfav.es
             </a>
             , while it doesn't have some of the browser-integrated features that the above extensions have, is a gorgeous looking page with a bunch of options that make your start page your own. It's so well put together that it's actually hard to make an ugly pagewhich is why its so great for those that don't want to spend hours on something just to make it look good. It has a ton of built-in sites that you can add to your page in one click, not to mention a huge list of custom icons for any pages that aren't already in its database. It also comes with a number of backgrounds, as well as the ability to create an account and sync your start page over to all your other machineswhich is a godsend if you have more than one computer. It even has a caching feature that stores your info offline, so there isn't a delay when you open it in a new tab.
            </p>
            <h4 data-textannotation-id="182810c8a005e8def51fb45a468e6e3e">
             The Rest
            </h4>
            <p data-textannotation-id="54d9301cb725f718307a0e09c3af97d0">
             <b>
              <a href="http://www.symbaloo.com/" target="_blank">
               Symbaloo
              </a>
             </b>
             is very similar to Myfav.es, though it's a bit more geared toward making pages with lots of different tiles. You can customize the tiles however you want, though the kicker is that each time someone creates a tile, they can share it with the communitywhich means that you probably won't have to make that many yourself (unless you have a very specific look you're going for).
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="1e0b11b19fb81c3610454d0d4f8bb954">

             </b>
             is a
             <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5811416/eight-is-a-stylish-windows-8+inspired-start-page-for-your-browser" x-inset="1">
              previously featured
             </a>
             start page based around Windows' Metro UI. It's very simple, but quite beautiful. You'll have to make it yourself by downloading images and tweaking the HTML, but if you aren't afraid to mess with some very basic code, you can create a pretty great start page in just a few minutes, that you store on your home machine.
            </p>
