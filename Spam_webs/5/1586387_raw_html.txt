        <h1 class="single-article__headinggroup__title__text">
         <span>
          <span>
           The 7 Most Beautiful Temples in Asia
          </span>
         </span>
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="single-article__headinggroup__meta col a-pad-0 a-margin-left-5 m-margin-left-4 t-margin-left-2 a-margin-bottom-3 m-margin-bottom-1">

       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Unless youre born and raised in Asia, you probably havent even heard of some of these places. But just like the much more famous European cathedrals and churches, Asias temples are amazing works of art just waiting to be discovered. Heres a look at the seven most beautiful holy places in Asia.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        <strong>
         7. Kinkaku-ji,  Japan
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1995" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/kinkakuji.jpg">
         <img alt="kinkaku-ji temple" class="size-full wp-image-1995" height="300" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/kinkakuji.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Kinkaku-ji temple
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Also known as the Golden Pavilion, Kinkaku-ji was built in 1397 as a retirement villa for one of Japans famous shoguns. It was his son who turned it into a Zen temple. This three-story building is where many important relics of Buddha are being kept.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1996" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/kinkakuji2.jpg">
         <img alt="Kinkaku-ji temple" class="size-full wp-image-1996" height="300" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/kinkakuji2.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Kinkaku-ji temple
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        It was burned down twice during the Onin War and once in 1950 accidentally, by a monk who then tried to kill himself. Kinkaku-ji was completely rebuilt in 1955 and the decaying gold leaf covering the top two stories was replaced by a thicker new one. The setting of the Golden Pavilion is also, as you can see, breathtaking.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Photo credits:
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/alaw168/352622847/" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/joi/1283721961/" target="_blank">
         2
        </a>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        <strong>
         6. Prambanan,  Indonesia
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1997" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/prambanan.jpg">
         <img alt="Prambanan temple" class="size-full wp-image-1997" height="266" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/prambanan.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Prambanan temple
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Built sometime around 850 CE, the Prambanan temple complex in Indonesia is one of the worlds largest remaining Hindu legacies. The foundation rises 47 meters above the ground and it symbolizes the triumph of the Hindu religion in Indonesia. It has been added on UNESCOs World Heritage Site list in 1991.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1998" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/prambanan2.jpg">
         <img alt="Prambanan temple" class="size-full wp-image-1998" height="300" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/prambanan2.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Prambanan temple
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        There are over 200 temples in the Prambanan temple complex, but the most impressive ones are the three set in the primary yard and dedicated to the gods Shiva, Vishnu and Brahma. The highest and largest one is dedicated to the goddess Shiva and it is beautifully decorated with statues and paintings depicting Hindu legends.
       </p>
       <p>
        Photo credits:
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/zsoolt/1333435323/" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/daveyll/536849559/" target="_blank">
         2
        </a>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        <strong>
         5. Borobudur,  Indonesia
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1999" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/borobudur.jpg">
         <img alt="Borobudur temple" class="size-full wp-image-1999" height="306" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/borobudur.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Borobudur temple
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        One of the oldest holy places in Indonesia, Borobudur is a Buddhist monument built sometime in the ninth century, where those of strong faith still come to pray. The three levels of Borobudur, that the pilgrims have to ascend, symbolize the three levels of Buddhist cosmology, Kamadhatu, Rupadhatu and Arupadhatu.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2000" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/borobudur2.jpg">
         <img alt="Borobudur temple" class="size-full wp-image-2000" height="300" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/borobudur2.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Borobudur temple
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Borobudur was deserted for centuries until 1814 when the English ruler of Java  Island at that time learned about it from the locals. Restoration started immediately and now this Buddhist place of pilgrimage is on UNESCOs World Heritage Site list. Unlike other temples, Borobudur was built atop a bedrock hill, 265m above sea-level.
       </p>
       <p>
        Photo credits:
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/elbisreverri/64133721/" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/tianyake/2544312523/" target="_blank">
         2
        </a>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        <strong>
         4. Shwedagon Pagoda,  Myanmar
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2001" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/shwedagon.jpg">
         <img alt="Shwedagon pagoda" class="size-full wp-image-2001" height="266" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/shwedagon.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Shwedagon pagoda
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Measuring 98 meters in height, this giant stupa is the most sacred Buddhist pagoda in all of Myanmar, hosting relics of the last four Buddhas enshrined inside it. There are no records of when the Shwedagon Pagoda was built, legends say it was 2500 years ago but archeological reports say the stupa dates back to somewhere between the 6
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        and 10
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        centuries.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2002" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/shwedagon2.jpg">
         <img alt="Shwedagon pagoda" class="size-full wp-image-2002" height="300" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/shwedagon2.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Shwedagon pagoda
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Shwedagon Pagoda is also known as the Golden Pagoda and unlike other structures that are painted golden, this Buddhist place of worship is actually covered with real gold. In the 15
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        century the queen ordered that it be leafed in gold and to this day, visitors sometimes buy small gold leafs and decorate the pagoda.
       </p>
       <p>
        Photo credits:
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/gepiblu/412940752/" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/0x/2332091353/" target="_blank">
         2
        </a>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        <strong>
         3. Tigers Nest,  Bhutan
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2003" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tigers_nest.jpg">
         <img alt="Tigers Nest Monastery" class="size-full wp-image-2003" height="300" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tigers_nest.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Tigers Nest Monastery
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        The most sacred place in Bhutan, Tigers Nest Monastery is also one of the most beautiful and impressive holy structures in the world. It sits atop a cliff, at a height of 3,120 meters, watching over the Paro valley below. Legend says Guru Rinpoche (an important figure in Buddhism) flew to this place on the back of a tiger and meditated in a cave for three months. The cave is said to be part of Tigers Nest Monastery.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2004" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tigers_nest2.jpg">
         <img alt="Tigers Nest Monastery" class="size-full wp-image-2004" height="263" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/tigers_nest2.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Tigers Nest Monastery
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        The Tigers Nest Monastery we see today was built in 1962, but it was seriously damaged in a fire that broke out in 1998. Reconstruction was done using old photographs and writings but there was hardly any documentation on the inside of the monastery and the objects it housed. People can journey to this incredible place by foot or on mules.
       </p>
       <p>
        Photo credits:
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/22132989@N04/2445379909/" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/babasteve/2758223825/" target="_blank">
         2
        </a>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        <strong>
         2. The Golden Temple,  India
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2005" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/golden-temple.jpg">
         <img alt="The Golden Temple" class="size-full wp-image-2005" height="300" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/golden-temple.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         The Golden Temple
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Sri Harmadir Sahib, as it is known in India, is one of the most sacred places for Sikhs everywhere. It started out as a quiet lake in the middle of a forest, a place where Buddha himself is said to have stopped for meditation. Guru Nanak, founder of the Sikh religion also stopped there and after his death, his followers continued to come here for meditation. Over the centuries this Sri Harmadir Sahib became more and more beautiful under the rule of each guru of the Sikhs.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2006" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/golden-temple2.jpg">
         <img alt="The Golden temple" class="size-full wp-image-2006" height="268" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/golden-temple2.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         The Golden Temple
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Today, the Golden  Temple is regarded as a symbol of original Sikh architectural style where the Moslem and Hindu ways of construction come together in harmony. The Golden Temple is built out of marble and gold and decorated with countless valuable gems. It is as peaceful as it is beautiful.
       </p>
       <p>
        Photo credits:
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/vinish/13118141/" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/jungle_boy/222159937/" target="_blank">
         2
        </a>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        <strong>
         1. Wat Rong Khun, Thailand
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2007" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/wat_rong_khun.jpg">
         <img alt="Wat Rong Khun" class="size-full wp-image-2007" height="266" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/wat_rong_khun.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Wat Rong Khun
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        The White Temple built in the Chiang Rai province is unlike any of the other temples of Thailand. It is a modern masterpiece built by Chalermchai Kositpipat, a popular artist who dedicated 10 years of his life to this grand project, in the name of Buddha. It is a modern project built almost entirely out of white materials and decorated with small pieces of mirrored glass that make it glitter, especially during the night.
       </p>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2008" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/wat_rong_khun2.jpg">
         <img alt="Wat Rong Khun" class="size-full wp-image-2008" height="300" src="http://www.hotelclub.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/wat_rong_khun2.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Wat Rong Khun
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="MsoNormal">
        Asked about his project, Chalermchai Kositpipat said he intended to build a replica of the magnificent and resplendent heaven. He managed to blend traditional Buddhist elements with modern, contemporary themes and thats what makes Wat Rong Khun unique. If youre ever in Thailand be sure to stop by the White Temple, it is more than worth the time.
       </p>
       <p>
        Photo credits:
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/fiftyfeet/1728034532/" target="_blank">
         1
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/95215349@N00/851975522/" target="_blank">
