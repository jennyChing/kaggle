      <p>
       <em>
        <strong>
         Modern Family
        </strong>
       </em>
       is universally loved; from the genuinely funny dialogue to the eclectic cast of characters, theres something in it for everyone. But what really gets us are those moments when we can empathize with what the characters are going through. The masterful writing and powerful themes are what make
       <em>
        <strong>
         Modern Family
        </strong>
       </em>
       one of the best shows ever aired and the following moments made us love it even more.
      </p>
      <h2>
       1. The Pilot (S01E01)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       I just dont want my kids to make the same bad mistakes I made. If Haley never wakes up on a beach in Florida half naked, Ive done my job.
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/110.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The pilot episode is the TV equivalent of curling up in bed at night with a cup of hot cocoa and a copy of
       <em>
        Charlie and the Chocolate Factory
       </em>
       ; it has a palpable feel-good vibe to it. Claires helicopter parenting (Wow, youre not wearing that outfit), Phil declaring Im a cool dad, thats my thang, believable banter between the kids, and Cameron hoisting their newly adopted child as 
       <em>
        The Circle of Life
       </em>
        plays in the background all come together in a ludicrous yet organic way that leaves you instantly in love with all the characters.
      </p>
      <h2>
       2. The Bicycle Thief (S01E02)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       Ninety percent of being a dad is just showing up
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/212.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Reminiscent of Vin Diesel in
       <em>
        The Pacifier
       </em>
       , Jay sheds off his tough exterior and shows us a softer side of his personality. He covers up for Mannys freewheeling, no-show father so that Manny doesnt get hurt; even going as far as ordering a limo to take them to Disneyland and crediting the idea to Mannys dad. Its probably safe to say that Manny wont be facing any daddy issues with Jay around.
      </p>
      <h2>
       3. Undeck the Halls (S01E10)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       Phil: Goodbye Dunphy Christmas. Haley, I guess youre not getting that car.
       <br/>
       Haley: I was getting a car?
      </blockquote>
      <blockquote>
       Phil: No, I was lying. Because thats what Dunphys do now, were liars.
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/34.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Alex taking the hit for a supposed cigarette burn on the couch so the Dunphy Christmas wouldnt be canceled is one of the shows most touching moments. No one really cared in the end what the cause of the cigarette burn was, it was still a great example of familial sacrifice that left us loving Alex even more than we already did.
      </p>
      <h2>
       4. Game Changer (S01E19)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       Phil: (to the iPad) I love you.
       <br/>
       Claire: (to Phil) I love you, too, honey.
       <br/>
       Phil: (to Claire) Oh, OK.
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/42.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       We hate seeing Phil sad; Claire forgot to get his iPad because she overslept, he spent his birthday alone at a ballpark, and then mistakenly walked into a random kids birthday party. It was by far the most melancholic moment on the show, and we cant help but quote Phils-osophy on this one: If you love something let it free. Unless its a tiger. Oh wait, that wasnt the one we were looking for.
      </p>
      <h2>
       5. Hawaii (S01E23)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       Jay: I just ordered an extra-long straw to avoid accidentally doing a sit-up
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/53.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        <strong>
         Modern Family
        </strong>
       </em>
       trips are always the perfect anticlimax; exhaustively planned trips spin out of control with a bevy of nonsensical plot twists. Haley gets a hangover, Jay cant be found for his own birthday and Mitch and Cam lose Lily. But the highlight of the trip was Claire and Phil renewing their wedding vows; in what was Claires defining moment on the show, she gave Phil the wedding they both always deserved, make-shift podium, Hawaiian pastor, and tropical theme in tow.
      </p>
      <h2>
       6. Two Monkeys and a Panda (S02E17)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       Cameron: [about the book he wrote for Lily] See, I made her a panda because shes Asian.
       <br/>
       Mitchell: And were monkeys because
       <br/>
       Cameron: I can draw monkeys.
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/62.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Mitchell and Camerons book for Lily to celebrate her adoption is just a defining moment in LGBT TV history. The sheer love that they have for her is enough to make even the biggest anti-gay bigot appreciate this family dynamic. And though they bicker over small things here and there, they let go of trivialities and come together to create a story that pulls at every heartstring you have.
      </p>
      <h2>
       7. The Musical Man (S02E19)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       Gloria: Where I come from, brothers respect each other.
       <br/>
       Jay: Thats why Colombia is such a peaceful country.
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/72.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Jays relationship with his brother is skin-deep at best, but theres an underlying current of intuitive understanding that they both have for each other. Jay finding out that his brother has cancer and learning to still treat him the way he always did gives you a great sense of how fraternal bonds run deeper than superficial banter.
      </p>
      <h2>
       8. The Future Dunphys (S04E19)
      </h2>
      <p>
       Cam [to Lily]: You know what? You just caused a scene in a restaurant and you cant get any more like your daddies than that.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/81.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Phil and Claire learning to appreciate their children and not alienate them is a great example of how the show has perfected the balance of expounding great family lessons without being too preachy. We were also touched by the scene where Cam and Mitch finally accept that Lily just wants to fit in with everyone and not be seen as different.
      </p>
      <h2>
       9. Schooled (S04E02)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       Phils-osophy: When life gives you lemonade, make lemons. Life will be all, like, WHAAAT?!
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/91.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Phils book of wisdom which he gives to Haley as they drop her off to college is the sweetest thing ever. Its the perfect token to remember home by. Not to mention, it has some of the most hilarious advice ever given to anyone (You can tell a lot about a person from his biography).
      </p>
      <h2>
       10. Suddenly, Last Summer (S05E1)
      </h2>
      <blockquote>
       Jay: They keep their tools in a pillowcase but they have an occasions closet
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="http://images.bidnessetc.com/content/uploads/images/source/102.jpg"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Because of the lifting of Prop 8, Cam and Mitch get the chance to legitimize their relationship in the court of law. Were pretty sure there wasnt a single dry eye in the audience when both Cam and Mitch kneel down to fix a flat tire, make eye contact, share a smile and just say yes at the same time. Also, Gloria getting a new proposal story, Phil and Claire sharing a romantic moment in front of the fireplace and Jay tearing up at Mannys goodbye letter are just the perfect examples for this show being one of our all-time favorites.
      </p>
