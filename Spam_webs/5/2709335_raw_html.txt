       <h2 style="text-transform: none; font-size: 16px; letter-spacing: 1px; color: #ff6600;">
        <strong>
         Hazaribagh National Park in India
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         Hazaribagh National Park
        </strong>
        consist of different wildlife species of flora and fauna made the Madhya Pradesh Government to declare Hazaribagh forest as a HazaribaghNational Park. Came into being in the year 1970 Hazaribagh National Park holds the highest number of wildlife species according to the census. However the populace of the tiger is less, hence the Tiger is considered as an endangered species in the park. Extensively covered by vertical hills with intense sultry forests, rich biodiversity and grassy meadows are exclusive features of the park that will mesmerize the visitors. Climate here is tropical, it is extremely hot during summers and cold during winters, so visitors are suggested to carry all the necessary things before the visit.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <strong>
         <span style="color: #339966;">
          Flora and Fauna
         </span>
        </strong>
        <b>
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Flora:
         </strong>
        </span>
        Hazaribagh National Park is one of the most significant wildlife sanctuaries in Jharkhand. Several varieties of flora are seen here. This area is filled with dense tropical forests, grassy meadows and steep hills.The park is a haven for intense tropical forests. Particularly in the monsoon period the tropical forest looks so diverse with verdant pasture covering extensively spread territories of land. The forest portrays as the green grass to be the carpet which has been spread to welcome the rain.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Fauna:
         </strong>
        </span>
        It is a very pleasant sight to the viewers to contemplate the variety of wild animals present in the forest. The different species of wild animals found in the park are Wild Boar, Sambar, The Kakar, Sloth Bear, Chital, Nilgai, Panther and the Cheetal are amongst the most profusely and easily found animals.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Park attractions :
        <span style="color: #000000;">
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </span>
        Canari hills are an added attraction located 5 km away from the park is an additional attraction. Natural hot Spring of Suraj Kund and Rajrappa Falls also form major attractions amongst the tourists. The presences of numerous towers inside the park are places for tourist to behold the wild animals sauntering in the forest.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Best time to visit :
         </strong>
        </span>
        October to April is the best time to visit as the park remains closed for during monsoons.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          Accommodation :
         </strong>
        </span>
        Tourists can avail their accommodation in Hazaribagh town near to the park. Hotels, guest houses and circuit houses are present here.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #339966;">
         <strong>
          How to reach HazaribaghNational Park:
         </strong>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        By Air:Ranchi is the nearest airport which is 91 km away from the park. Private vehicles can be hired from here.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        By Rail:Koderma is the nearest railway station; one can also approach the park from the Hazaribagh road railway station 67 km on the Howrah- Delhi Grand Chord line.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        By Road:
        <b>
        </b>
        Hazaribagh town is well connected to Ranchi and has a motor able road to the
        <strong>
         Hazaribagh National Park
        </strong>
        directly.
       </p>
