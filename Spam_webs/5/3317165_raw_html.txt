        <p>
         Go to a place where nature reigns; where elk, deer and antelope outnumber humans, sweeping prairies give way to mammoth mountain ranges and Colonel Custer had his notorious last stand. When you visit Billings, you'll not only arrive at Montana's Trailhead to Big Sky Country, you'll have a chance to experience a vibrant urban community on the edge of Montana's wild frontier. Here, you can:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Explore the Great Outdoors |
         </strong>
         One of Montana's best-known attractions is its untamed natural surroundings - from the enchanting thermal pools in Yellowstone National Park to the goat-specked Rocky Mountain range lacing the western part of the state. Gaze at 2,000-year-old rock paintings at Pictograph Cave State Park, capture a panoramic photo or hike the arresting sandstone rimrocks framing Billings. It's up to you how you want to explore Montana's great outdoors.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Discover the Riveting Past |
         </strong>
         Through interactive museums and monuments, you can learn about Montana's role in some of America's important moments in time. Discover interesting facts about the Lewis and Clark Expedition at Pompeys Pillar National Monument or venture outside of the city to walk the grounds where Col. Custer made his final stand at Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get a Good Night's Rest |
         </strong>
         Billings offers accommodations that fit the needs of every traveler. Whether you're looking for an upscale hotel in downtown Billings, a quiet bed and breakfast for a romantic weekend or a well-equipped campground big enough for the entire family, you're in luck. Learn more about Billing's versatile accommodations and
         <link1>
          travel deals.
         </link1>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Taste Montana's Local Fare|
