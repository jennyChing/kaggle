          <div id="jadens-flan">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/jadensflan-e1418114739487.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Jadens Flan
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://everydaypaleo.com/jadens-flan/" target="_blank">
             Everyday Paleo
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Flan is typically made with non-paleo ingredients like white sugar and sweetened-condensed milk, but here, Everyday Paleo presents a recipe using wholesome coconut milk and eggs. The coconut adds such a lovely, welcome flavorwe think its better than traditional flan!
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="spicy-tuna-cake">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/spicy-tuna-cakes-e1418114837303.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Spicy Tuna Cakes
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://nomnompaleo.com/post/91332244628/spicy-tuna-cakesstyle" target="_blank">
             Nom Nom Paleo
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            These easy, protein-rich tuna cakes are made from easy-to-find canned albacore tuna and paleo favorite, sweet potatoes! They bake up nice and crispy in nonstick muffin tins, making them easy to make in large batches. We love to make a big batch on a Sunday night and then eat these all week, ensuring we always have wholesome, high-quality, protein-rich snacks on hand.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="coconut-chocolate">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Coconut-Cake-e1418114984104.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Coconut Chocolate Cake
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://civilizedcavemancooking.com/grain-free-goodies/coconut-chocolate-cake/" target="_blank">
             Civilized Caveman
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            When we
            <a href="http://paleomagonline.com/paleo-diet-101/">
             committed to a Paleo diet
            </a>
            , we figured cake was pretty much out for us. But this rich, chocolatey almond flour cake from Civilized Caveman showed us that we really can have our cake and eat it too. We love this served with a scoop of paleo coconut milk ice cream on the side.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="blueberry-muffins">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/blueberry-muffins-e1418114486106.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Blueberry Muffins
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://paleocomfortfoods.com/in-the-kitchen/paleo-comfort-foods-blueberry-muffins-and-the-decatur-book-festival" target="_blank">
             Paleo Comfort Foods
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Once again, its almond flour for the win! These moist, tender muffins have all the benefits of healthy, low-sugar blueberries and fiber-rich almond flour, and their muffin form makes them easy to eat on the go. Oh, and, served with some lightly salted grass-fed butter, they are just about the most perfect afternoon snack on earth.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="pumpkin-pancakes">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/pumpkinpancakes1.png"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Pumpkin Pancakes
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://balancedbites.com/2012/10/easy-recipe-pumpkin-pancakes-from-practical-paleo.html" target="_blank">
             Diane Sanfilippos Balanced Bites
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            These moist pumpkin pancakes taste like fall on a plate, but since pumpkin puree is available canned or boxed year-round, they can be yours any time. We love them with a small amount of fresh grass-fed butter and real maple syrup, as an occasional treat, but theyre delicious with a light drizzle of coconut oil and a dash of salt as well. Oh, and they freeze like a dream, so make a big batch in advance, wrap tightly in foil or plastic wrap, and have pumpkin pancakes on hand for anytime the craving hits (and believe us, it will hit).
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="potato-salad">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/classic-potato-salad.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Classic Paleo Potato Salad
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://theclothesmakethegirl.com/2014/07/22/classic-paleo-potato-salad" target="_blank">
             The Clothes Makes The Girl
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            We highly doubt your non-paleo friends will be able to tell the difference between this potato salad and conventional potato salad. But even if they do, theyll be too distracted by the delicious bacon in this one to care. Weve also tried this one with sweet potatoes, and we highly recommend it.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="lasagne">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/grain-free-lasagna.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Grain-Free Lasagna
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://againstallgrain.com/2012/06/29/grain-free-lasagna-with-noodles/" target="_blank">
             Against All Grain
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Once you have all your ingredients assembled, this lasagne comes together quickly  and is a pretty amazing foil for the traditional kind. This recipe calls for making coconut flour crepes in advance, and using them as the tortillas. This takes a little time, but remember, its all in pursuit of rich, lasagne goodness. Its important to note that this one contains cheese, so youll want to skip it if you cant eat dairy.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="paleo-bread">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/grain-free-paleo.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Rustic Grain-Free Paleo Bread
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://theysmell.com/rustic-grain-free-bread-paleo/" target="_blank">
             Whats That Smell?
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            We get it. Sometimes, you just want a nice slice of bread. A sandwich, perhaps? Or just a piece of toast with butter. Thanks to this life-changing recipe from Whats That Smell, you can have it both waysand with no weird flours or gums. Just your run of the mill Paleo staples, coconut and almond flours.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="coconut-soup">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/thai-coconut-soup-e1418752015921.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Thai Coconut Soup
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://paleoleap.com/thai-coconut-soup/" target="_blank">
             Paleo Leap
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Coconut milk brings glorious richness to this soup, while somehow also keeping it light. Its brimming with flavor and high quality protein, thanks to the chicken, and is just the thing for a cool summer (or winter, or fall, or spring) evening.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="turkey-noodle-soup">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/turkey-noodle-soup.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Grain Free Turkey Noodle Soup
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://www.grassfedgirl.com/grain-free-turkey-noodle-soup-paleo-gaps-scd-low-carb/" target="_blank">
             Grass Fed Girl
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Regular noodles get the door-in-the-face treatment here, in favor of nutrient-dense spaghetti squash, bringing this turkey soup to new levels. This is a perfect soup to make right after Thanksgiving, though weve also made it using leftover cooked chicken, with great results.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="bacon-curry-burgers">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Bacon-Wrapped-Curry-Burgers1-e1418116805813.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Bacon-Wrapped Curry Burgers with Sweet Potato Buns
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://www.thepaleomom.com/2014/09/bacon-wrapped-curry-burgers-sweet-potato-buns.html" target="_blank">
             The Paleo Mom
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            These crazy-flavorful beef burgers (though the recipes says any ground meat will workwe think pork or chicken would be good too) are made easy to eat thanks to the genius sweet potato buns in the recipe. We found these to be so juicy that they hardly needed any condiments, but if you cant not doctor your burger up, a touch of spicy mustard would be delicious here.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="chicken-alfredo">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Paleo-Chicken-Alfredo1-e1418117272354.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Paleo Chicken Alfredo
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="https://www.paleoplan.com/2009/12-02/paleo-chicken-alfredo/" target="_blank">
             Paleo Plan
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            This outrageous chicken alfredo recipe absolutely nails the key to good kelp noodles: cooking them for a long long time. Like, way longer than you think you need to. The idea is to cook out their crunch to make them more closely mimic regular noodles. If you cant find kelp noodles, shirataki noodles will work well here. And the creamy cashew-based alfredo sauce? Unbelievably good.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="mocha-pot-roast">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/slow-cooker-mocha.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Slow Cooker Mocha-Rubbed Pot Roast
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://stupideasypaleo.com/2013/06/29/crock-pot-mocha-rubbed-pot-roast/" target="_blank">
             Stupid Easy Paleo
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Chocolate and coffee might seem like an odd accompaniment for beef, but just trust us here. The deep, dark flavors in both bring out the rich, umami essence of the beef, yielding a pot roast that will feed you all week. Try the meat wrapped in lettuce cups, as paleo tacos, layered atop a crunchy green salad, or on its own. You wont be able to stop eating (or thinking about ) it.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="cookies">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Maple_Bacon_Chocolate_Chip_Cookies-e1418117365252.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Maple Bacon Chocolate Chip Cookies
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://paleoonabudget.com/2014/01/13/maple-bacon-chocolate-chip-cookies-paleo-cookie-dairy-free/" target="_blank">
             Paleo on a Budget
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            It seems fitting that a Paleo chocolate chip cookie recipe would call for bacon, no? This one, made with almond flour, is full of salty-sweet contradiction, and makes an ideal post-workout treat.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="lettuce-wraps">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/ThaiChickenLettuceWraps-e1418117783499.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Thai Chicken Lettuce Wraps
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://savingdinner.com/recipes/thai-chicken-lettuce-wraps/" target="_blank">
             Saving Dinner
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Chicken lettuce wraps, a Thai and Chinese classic, are naturally Paleo, provided you temper the amount of sugar in them. This recipe from Saving Dinner, does just that, focusing instead on the bright flavors of coconut aminos and bright, fresh cilantro to carry the flavor of the dish. These make a perfect lunch or dinner, or a fantastic addition to an appetizer buffet.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="cauliflower-crust-pizza">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/cauliflower-crust-pizza.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Cauliflower Crust Pizza
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://www.carrieonliving.com/2013/04/23/cancer-free-cauliflower-crust-pizza/" target="_blank">
             Carrie on Living
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Though this crust calls for just a pinch of cornmeal and chickpea flour, weve discovered that you can get away with replacing them with an equal amount of almond or coconut flour. Be sure not to skip the parchment paper step  its crucial in developing a crisp, non-sticky base for your pizza!
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="energy-bars">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/paleo-energy-bars-e1418117981530.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Paleo Energy Bar
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://fitviews.blogspot.com/2012/08/paleo-energy-bar-recipe.html" target="_blank">
             FitViews
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Perhaps one of the biggest struggles we hear from readers is that its a challenge to find grab-n-go paleo snacks or on-the-go breakfasts. These energy bars seek to solve that problem, and they do a pretty darn good job. A food processor makes the job much easier, but a blender or even a mortar and pestle will work. Make an extra-big batch of these and keep them in the refrigerator or freezer so youre prepared with a healthy snack whenever hunger strikes.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="egg-rolls">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Egg-Rolls-1.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Paleo Egg Rolls
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://www.deliciousobsessions.com/2014/06/paleo-egg-rolls/" target="_blank">
             Delicious Obsessions
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            We must admit, since going Paleo, we miss Chinese egg rolls horribly! Thanks to this great recipe from Delicious Obsessions, we can have our egg rolls (and eat them, too!). The wrapper is made from paleo yucca root that is boiled and smashed and pressed in a really neat process. The filling in the recipe is a classic pork, but we cant wait to experiment with an all-vegetable filling, or a shrimp-and-pork combination.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="dill-garlic-salmon">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/creamy-dill-garlic-salmon.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Creamy Dill Garlic Salmon
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://thiswestcoastmommy.com/creamy-dill-garlic-salmon-recipe/" target="_blank">
             This West Coast Mommy
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            This Paleo take on a classic recipe calls for homemade olive oil mayonnaise, in place of the usual canola stuff. We like to make a big batch of olive oil mayonnaise to keep on hand anyway, so this was as good and excuse as any to make it. The salmon is ultra-tender, beneath its flavorful sauce, and we served it with a green salad and lemony cauliflower rice.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="mousse">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/strawberrymousse-1-e1418118129161.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Strawberry Mousse
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://www.agirlworthsaving.net/2014/02/strawberry-mousse.html" target="_blank">
             A Girl Worth Saving
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            We bet youll never guess the secret ingredient that makes this strawberry mousse ultra-creamy and luxurious. Give up? Its avocado. It seems crazy, but blended with sweet banana and dehydrated strawberries, it yields a super rich mousse without tasting like guacamole. Serve this one topped with some whipped coconut milk and a sprig of fresh mint for a classy dessert that even your non-paleo dinner guests will clamor for.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="pecan-bars">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Caramel-Apple-Bars.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Caramel Apple Pecan Bars
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://thehealthygflife.com/caramel-apple-pecan-bars-paleogfdf/" target="_blank">
             The Healthy GF Life
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Looking for the perfect fall dessert? These gluten-free bars are one of our all time favorite treats. Crafted with maple syrup and chestnut flour for the perfect texture and consistency, these perfectly moist caramel apple flavored bars will leave you licking your fingers and wishing that you had made more.
           </p>
          </div>
          <hr/>
          <div id="salmon">
           <p>
            <img alt="" src="http://paleomagonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/HoneyWasabiSalmon-640x336-e1418155949528.jpg"/>
           </p>
           <h3 style="display: inline;">
            Honey and Wasabi Salmon
           </h3>
           <p>
            via
            <a href="http://mylivingnutrition.com" target="_blank">
             My Living Nutrition
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            This sweet and spicy Asian style salmon is a quick and easy dish that takes only minutes to make. It can be served with your favorite woked vegetables, or atop a large green salad.
           </p>
          </div>
