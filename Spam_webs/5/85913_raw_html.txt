         My BFFs in Paris, Part Deux
        </h1>

          </a>

         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1755" style="width: 650px">
          <a href="http://thezorafiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/image32.jpg">
           <img alt="La Cuisine Paris" class="size-full wp-image-1755" height="517" src="http://thezorafiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/image32.jpg" width="640"/>

           La Cuisine Paris
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Photo courtesy
          <em>
           Lost in Cheeseland
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Back in
          <em>
           My Paris BFFs, Part Une
          </em>
          ,I spoke of my deep, abiding love of my #1BFF in Paris, Parisian Pharmacies.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats all very well and good for the body, butId like to tell you about anotherParis bestie,
          <em>
           La Cuisine Paris
          </em>
          , the well and good for the soul  food!
         </p>
         <p>
          During a particularly rough period in my life, not long after losing my father to cancer, I visited Paris knowingthat I could rely on thecity torenew my spirit. I bought my ticket, booked my hotel, prayed to the money gods that everything would be okay,
          <strong>
           <em>
            et voila
           </em>
          </strong>
          , I was set!
         </p>
         <p>
          Though I was travelling to my favorite place on earth, I knew that Ineeded to do
          <em>
           something different.
          </em>
          Shopping is never enough of a cultural adventure, and I am not afoodie in the true sense of the word (though,jadoreFrench pastries!), and there would be no hiking in the countryside to ponder or re-affirm lifes meaning and purpose  I needed
          <em>
           something different
          </em>
           So, what to do, what to do?
         </p>
         <p>
          While scouringsome really genericthings-to-do-in-Paris websites, I hit upon cooking/baking schools. Hmmm
         </p>
         <p>
          A more detailedsearch led me to
          <em>
           La Cuisine Paris
          </em>
          . The schoolslocation in Le Marais was manageable from my hotel and any certain wandering that I would do on any givenday. So, I signed up for their
          <em>
           Le Croissant and Traditional Breakfast Pastries
          </em>
          class. I figured, worst case scenario, I would have
          <em>
           someones
          </em>
          tasty pastries to enjoywhen the class was over. (I had very little faith in my own ability to create anything edible  can you tell?)
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           La Cuisine
          </em>
          turned out to be the absolute and brightest highlight of my trip!
         </p>
         <p>
          The staff were friendly, professional and Chef Justin madethe entire groupfeel comfortable and at homewhether we were experienced bakers or completely clueless. As a solo traveller and one of the completely clueless of the group, my thick New Yorkerskin can get a weebit prickly, but, at La Cuisine, I was able toshed that skin, team up with Chef Justin, relax andenjoy the experience.
         </p>
         <p>
          Check out the
          <em>
           Le Croissant and Traditional Breakfast Pastries
          </em>
          class description here: http://ow.ly/L30Um. Also, check out the classes and tours while youre at site they all aregreat! I was so emboldened after my first baking class, that, once home, I made my very own Pain au Chocolate:
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1764" style="width: 234px">
          <a href="http://thezorafiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Pain-au-chocolat.jpg">

          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Not bad, huh?
          </p>
         </div>

          <br style="clear: both"/>
         </div>
         <p>
          Each time I returnto Paris, I try to book at least one of
          <em>
           La Cuisine
          </em>
          s cooking or baking classes, or one of their culinary tours.
          <em>
           TheBellies on Foot
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           Fromage et Vin
          </em>
          <em>
           Discovery
          </em>
          culinary tours are wonderful little adventures in the French culinary art of
          <em>
           <strong>
            YUM
           </strong>
          </em>
          ! (Oh yes, and we learned stuff, too!)
         </p>
         <p>
          These days, I consider
          <em>
           La Cuisine Paris
          </em>
          a home-away-from-home in my one-day-soon-to-be-dream-home, Paris.
         </p>
         <p>
          During my Christmas visit, I had the pleasure of meeting the wonderful Jane Bertch of the school. If I had not developed a love-jones for this place by then, it was firmly in place after meeting Jane! Such a generous and genuine host!
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <em>
            Armed with the savoire faire, drive, the perfect partner in crime (Olivier, her husband), and a handful of talented chefs, she set out to create
           </em>
           <strong>
            <em>
             <span style="color: #000000;">
              <a href="http://www.lacuisineparis.com/" style="color: #000000;">
               La Cuisine Paris
              </a>
              .
             </span>
             
            </em>
            from the Blog
            <em>
             , Lost In Cheesland.
            </em>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          As it turned out, I discovered that
          <em>
           something different
          </em>
          that I needed in my very first class with
          <em>
           La Cuisine
          </em>
           a big, warm, welcominghug! I continue to receive those, literally and figuratively, with every visit to
          <em>
           La Cuisine.
          </em>
          I just wish I had done this on my first day in Paris instead of my last! Thank you, Jane, and the entire
          <em>
           La Cuisine Paris
          </em>
          team!
         </p>
         <p>
          If you have plans to visit Paris, make
          <em>
           La Cuisine
          </em>
          your first stop! The experience is amazing  dare I say transformative?!
         </p>
         <p>
          In dreams
         </p>
