             <h1>
              Want to Hire a Veteran? Use These Resources
             </h1>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
       </header>
       <div class="row">
        <div class="large-14 push-5 columns">
         <div class="padded-box popped-box plain-white pvs authored-content looser">
          <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Want to Hire a Veteran? Use These Resources" data-url="http://www.realbusiness.com/2014/11/human-resources/want-to-hire-a-veteran-use-these-resources/">
          </div>
          <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
          </div>
          <p class="sub-header prominent h5">
           <time class="updated" datetime="2014-11-11" pubdate="">
            November 11, 2014
           </time>
           By Sachin Shenolikar
          </p>
          <p>
           As we honor the sacrifices and commitments of our armed forces on Veterans Day, it is also important to be mindful of those searching for meaningful employment upon completion of active duty. Many former military personnel have trouble finding jobs after an extended time out of the traditional workforce. In 2013, there was
           <a href="http://www.reuters.com/article/2014/03/21/us-usa-economy-veterans-idUSBREA2J27120140321">
            a 9.0 percent unemployment rate
           </a>
           among people who had joined the military after 9/11  1.6 percent higher than civilians.
          </p>
          <p>
           The key point to remember is that hiring a veteran isnt just the right thing to do  its also
           <a href="http://www.realbusiness.com/2013/12/people-matters/hiring-a-vet-is-more-than-just-patriotic-its-good-business/">
            good for business
           </a>
           . And that goes for
           <a href="http://www.realbusiness.com/2014/05/people-matters/active-duty-how-military-spouses-can-help-your-company/">
            military spouses
           </a>
           too. Those with military experience have two essential and opposite qualities, says Gabriel Bristol, president/CEO of
           <a href="http://www.intelicaredirect.com">
            Intelicare Direct
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           They excel when working in a group because theyre used to being part of a unit, but they are also motivated self-starters who are great at working individually and without a lot of direction, he says.
          </p>
          <p>
           Bristol adds that veterans can generally pivot quickly when needed, pick up training quickly, and are
           <a href="http://www.comptia.org/about-us/newsroom/blog/comptia-blog/2014/11/06/why-hire-veterans-they-bring-a-lot-to-the-table-say-comptia-members">
            fiercely loyal
           </a>
           employees.
          </p>
          <p>
           Here are three resources that can help employers find veterans to hire.
          </p>
          <p>
           1. American Job Centers. AJC offers a hands-on approach for both employers and veterans. Each center has an employment outreach specialist who works with companies in their area to match available jobs with military personnel. The AJC website also has a list of vetted resources for both veterans and employers.
          </p>
          <p>
           2. Department of Labor: Veterans Employment and Training Service. This page has a comprehensive FAQ for employers, including how to find veterans looking for work, a review of the USERRA (Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act), and affirmative action rules. There is also a toolkit for employers with guides for strategy, recruiting, hiring, and onboarding.
          </p>
          <p>
           3. The U.S. Chamber of Commerces Hiring Our Heroes job fairs have long been a go-to resource for employers. The website has also added an interactive
           <a href="http://www.allvetstates.com/">
            State Resources map
           </a>
           where government officials provide customized information for veterans. Plus, there are tools such as the
           <a href="http://www.employerroadmap.org/">
            Employer Roadmap
           </a>
           for a personalized experience that includes tracking progress and receiving curated content, and
           <a href="http://www.uschamberfoundation.org/employer-webinars">
            interactive online workshops
           </a>
           on employer best practices.
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/tbMiVy0CgJk?feature=oembed" width="500">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
           Companies are also partnering and founding programs such as
           <a href="http://www.operationhomefront.net/">
            Operation Homefront
           </a>
           to spread the word about openings, and the 100,000 Jobs Mission to promote best practices and accountability.
          </p>
          <p>
           The 100,000 Jobs Mission represents a cross-section of major employers, including founding members such as AT&amp;T, Cisco, JPMorgan Chase, Verizon, NCR, and others. The initial group of 11 founding members has grown to more than 170 companies, including Xerox.
          </p>
          <p>
           Jobs Mission members have hired more than 190,000 veterans to date, almost doubling its initial goal six years early, according to a report released Monday.
          </p>
          <p>
           Successes such as these highlight that companies understand that having veterans on their team is a win-win situation. Now its a matter of finding, hiring, and retaining those employees.
          </p>
          <p>
           As an example of one way to address that challenge, Xerox has established
           <a href="http://www.xerox.com/jobs/military-leadership/enus.html">
            Heroes@Home
           </a>
           , a career portal geared to active military, veterans, and families by highlighting opportunities relevant to them. Xerox hired more than 1,700 veterans last year in various positions in locations throughout the U.S. and the world Through this program, qualified veterans and military spouses can search for employment opportunities, including work-from-home positions, says Ann Vezina, vice president of Human Resources for Xerox.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its not enough to say youre a veteran-friendly employer, says Bristol. You have to put your money where your mouth is.
          </p>
