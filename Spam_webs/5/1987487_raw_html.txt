          Wednesday Goodies: DIY Kid Safety Temporary Tattoo
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">

         <p>
          <a href="http://thepapermama.com/wp-content/uploads/616.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
            <img alt="DIY Kid Safety Temporary Tattoo // thepapermama.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7151" height="1579" src="http://thepapermama.com/wp-content/uploads/616.jpg" width="650"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thepapermama.com/2013/08/diy-kid-safety-temporary-tattoo/&amp;media=http://thepapermama.com/wp-content/uploads/616.jpg&amp;description=Wednesday Goodies: DIY Kid Safety Temporary Tattoo')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Tried it:
           </strong>
          </em>
          DIY Kid Safety Temporary Tattoo. Ive seen this a couple times on Pinterest, and recently found it again
          <a href="http://weightdownweightloss.wordpress.com/2013/06/26/diy-temporary-tattoo-for-kid-safety/" target="_blank">
           on this blog
          </a>
          . Its a nifty way to tattoo your kiddos phone number on their arm. Its perfect for going to busy spots like zoos or amusement parks, but I also love the idea of using this method if your kiddo has an allergy. You just grab a pen, write a small note &amp; number on their arm, and seal the writing with a
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001G7QRF8/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001G7QRF8&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thpama-20" target="_blank">
           liquid bandage
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Sealing it with the liquid bandage makes the writing sweat proof and waterproof. Its also VERY easy to take off when youre done. Just apply another layer of liquid bandage and wipe off then wash. Done. Bam. Love this idea.
         </p>
