     <p>
      <b>
       Poison the Well
      </b>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/Experimental_rock" title="Experimental rock">
       experimental
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_punk" title="Hardcore punk">
       hardcore
      </a>
      band from
      <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">
       Miami
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">
       Florida
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      who were last signed to
      <a href="/wiki/Ferret_Music" title="Ferret Music">
       Ferret Music
      </a>
      . In 2010, they announced a hiatus to explore other interests.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Guitarist
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryan_Primack" title="Ryan Primack">
       Ryan Primack
      </a>
      and drummer
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_A._Hornbrook" title="Chris A. Hornbrook">
       Chris Hornbrook
      </a>
      were the only remaining founding members, although vocalist Jeffrey Moreira featured on all five of their full-length albums.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Formation.2C_debut_EP_and_The_Opposite_of_December_.281997.E2.80.932000.29">
       Formation, debut EP and
       <i>
        The Opposite of December
       </i>
       (19972000)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Poison_the_Well_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation, debut EP and The Opposite of December (19972000)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The band were originally named "An Acre Lost"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and was started by singer Aryeh Lehrer and guitarist Ryan Primack. During their first few years, the band had two vocalists, Lehrer and Duane Hosein (who went on to form A Jealousy Issue). With this lineup, the band recorded the material for their first proper release, a split with South Florida band Promise No Tomorrow. After this album, the band changed their name to Poison The Well, after the popular rhetorical device. The band then released their first album under their new name,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Distance_Only_Makes_the_Heart_Grow_Fonder" title="Distance Only Makes the Heart Grow Fonder">
        Distance Only Makes the Heart Grow Fonder
       </a>
      </i>
      in 1998. The album gained them the attention of
      <a href="/wiki/Trustkill_Records" title="Trustkill Records">
       Trustkill Records
      </a>
      who signed them to a multi-album deal. After Lehrer and Hosein exited the band, Jeffrey Moreira came in to fulfill vocal duties and remained with the band until their break up. With this solidified lineup, the band released
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Opposite_of_December" title="The Opposite of December">
        The Opposite of December... A Season of Separation
       </a>
      </i>
      in 1999. Music magazine,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Guitar_World" title="Guitar World">
        Guitar World
       </a>
      </i>
      , cited
      <i>
       The Opposite of December
      </i>
      to be one of the greatest
      <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_punk" title="Hardcore punk">
       hardcore
      </a>
      albums of all-time in a 2006 issue.

      </i>
      was a transitional record for Poison The Well. Moving away from the traditional
      <a href="/wiki/Metalcore" title="Metalcore">
       metalcore
      </a>
      sound that they had helped establish and created with
      <i>
       The Opposite of December
      </i>
      , the band moved into more straight forward songwriting with more melody and more of an overall rock sound.
      <i>
       Tear from the Red
      </i>
      was recorded in six weeks at Studio 13 in Pompano Beach, Florida for $6,000.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      The band had wanted to make a record that had more melody but still could be extremely heavy at the same time. After the record was completed, the band started a rigorous tour cycle that took them all over the US and Canada. They toured with many bands including:
      <a href="/wiki/Hatebreed" title="Hatebreed">
       Hatebreed
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Kittie" title="Kittie">
       Kittie
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lambgoat.com_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-lambgoat.com-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Killswitch_Engage" title="Killswitch Engage">
       Killswitch Engage
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lambgoat.com_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-lambgoat.com-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Unearth" title="Unearth">
       Unearth
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Eighteen_Visions" title="Eighteen Visions">
       Eighteen Visions
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="You_Come_Before_You_.282003.E2.80.932005.29">
       <i>
        You Come Before You
       </i>
       (20032005)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Poison_the_Well_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: You Come Before You (20032005)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The success of
      <i>
       Tear from the Red
      </i>
      and their continuous touring schedule helped Poison The Well gain the attention of some major labels, which had started to take interest in bands coming from the hardcore scene. The band signed with
      <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">
       Atlantic Records
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in 2002 and started writing for their third studio album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/You_Come_Before_You" title="You Come Before You">
        You Come Before You
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Poison the Well wanted to continue the evolution of their sound and felt that they needed a team of people who understood where they were going and what they wanted to do. They recorded with Swedish producers Pelle Henricsson and Eskil Lvstrm.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Both had worked on many influential mid to late nineties Swedish hardcore records, including
      <a href="/wiki/Refused" title="Refused">
       Refused
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Songs_to_Fan_the_Flames_of_Discontent" title="Songs to Fan the Flames of Discontent">
        Songs to Fan the Flames of Discontent
       </a>
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Shape_of_Punk_to_Come" title="The Shape of Punk to Come">
        The Shape of Punk to Come
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The band first recorded in Van Nuys, California at Sound City Stuidos and completed the rest at Tonteknik Recording AB in Ume, Sweden. After the record was complete, the band started a year and a half tour cycle that took them to Japan, Australia, New Zealand, and Europe.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Towards the end, many of the band members were worn out and unsure if they wanted to continue with Poison the Well.
     </p>

       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">

         </a>

        Chris Hornbrook performs at Australia's Soundwave festival 2009

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Versions_.282005.E2.80.932008.29">
       <i>
        Versions
       </i>
       (20052008)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Poison_the_Well_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Versions (20052008)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Having experienced some level of success with their previous record,
      <i>
       You Come Before You
      </i>
      , a long and hard touring cycle left certain members of the band disenchanted. In the summer of 2004, guitarist Derek Miller quit the band. Miller had been in Poison the Well for all three prior releases and was a major part of the writing process. Poison The Well had been in the middle of pre-production for an entire record's worth of material when Miller announced his departure.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This material would eventually get scrapped due to the overall unhappiness with the direction he had taken the band. The remaining three members took some time to figure out what they wanted to do. They eventually regrouped, added guitarist Jason Boyer to replace Miller, and started working on new musical ideas that would be the early stages of their fourth studio album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Versions_(Poison_the_Well_album)" title="Versions (Poison the Well album)">
        Versions
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      After months of working and writing material, the band set off in early 2005 to work and record with Pelle Henricsson and Eskil Lvstrm at Tonteknik Studios in Ume, Sweden.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This would be the first session out of three that the band would record for
      <i>
       Versions
      </i>
      and what would eventually become
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/I/III_/_II/III_/_III/III" title="I/III / II/III / III/III">
        I/III / II/III / III/III
       </a>
      </i>
      in 2009. They had worked with Henricsson and Lvstrm on
      <i>
       You Come Before You
      </i>
      and had been also working with them on follow up material when Derek Miller had quit. The band felt that Henricsson and Lvstrm were perfect for producing and capturing this "new direction and sound" they were creating and moving towards. After completing the first two recording sessions and returning home, Poison the Well announced that they were parting ways with Atlantic Records after only one album because of "creative differences". Atlantic Records had given full creative control with the writing and recording of
      <i>
       You Come Before You
      </i>
      , not interfering and allowing the band to do what they pleased. But, after not seeing eye to eye on the direction Poison The Well had decided to move, Atlantic agreed to let the band go.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Even though the band had no label, they returned to writing material for the third and final session. The band had been in contact with
      <a href="/wiki/Ferret_Music" title="Ferret Music">
       Ferret Music
      </a>
      president Carl Severson about signing with the label. Serverson had been friends and a fan of the band for many years and expressed interest in signing them.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      After signing at the end of 2006, the band left once again for Sweden to finish up the record.
      <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.lambgoat.com/news/view.aspx?id=7942" rel="nofollow">
       [4]
      </a>
      <i>
       Versions
      </i>
      was released on Ferret Music on April 2, 2007 in Europe and April 3, 2007 worldwide. On February 22, 2007 they began touring in support of the album.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Tropic_Rot_and_hiatus_.282009.E2.80.932010.29">

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <p>
      The writing process for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Tropic_Rot" title="The Tropic Rot">
        The Tropic Rot
       </a>
      </i>
      started a few weeks after Poison the Well finished up the touring cycle for
      <i>
       Versions
      </i>
      . The band wanted to make a record that was more focused musically and bigger sonically. Poison the Well wrote the entire record in four and a half months in a condemned and haunted bar in West Palm Beach, Florida.
      <i>
       The Tropic Rot
      </i>
      had originally been slated to be made with producer J. Robbins, but had to be cancelled due to a serious family emergency.
      <a href="/wiki/Steve_Evetts" title="Steve Evetts">
       Steve Evetts
      </a>
      was picked among other potential producers the band thought could capture the record the way they wanted it and could start as soon as possible. The record was recorded at Castle Oaks Production and The Candy Shop studios and was released on July 7, 2009
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      through Ferret Music.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The album charted at No.180 in its first week of release on the
      <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">
       <i>
        Billboard
       </i>
       200
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      While on the first stop of their September 2009 tour with
      <a href="/wiki/Billy_Talent" title="Billy Talent">
       Billy Talent
      </a>
      , Poison The Well was robbed at a tour stop in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit,_Michigan" title="Detroit, Michigan">
       Detroit
      </a>
      . As the band was asleep in their hotel, thieves drove off with their van and trailer containing all of their musical gear, band merchandise, and a majority of their personal possessions and clothes. After taking a few days off to get a new van and obtain some temporary gear, the band quickly produced and began selling T-shirts to raise money and buy new equipment, with the shirts containing a detailed description of all musical gear stolen including guitar and amplifier serial numbers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On July 14, 2010, Poison the Well announced that they are going on hiatus to "explore other interests."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Since then, drummer
      <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hornbrook" title="Chris Hornbrook">
       Chris Hornbrook
      </a>
      has been doing session work as well as drumming for LA based dance/electronic band
      <a href="/wiki/Big_Black_Delta" title="Big Black Delta">
       Big Black Delta
      </a>
      and New Jersey punk/ hardcore band,
      <a href="/wiki/Senses_Fail" title="Senses Fail">
       Senses Fail
      </a>
      . As well,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryan_Primack" title="Ryan Primack">
       Ryan Primack
      </a>
      has still been involved with music, working with his former bandmate Derek Miller's band,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sleigh_Bells" title="Sleigh Bells">
       Sleigh Bells
      </a>
      as their production manager and second live guitar player.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Rise_Records" title="Rise Records">
       Rise Records
      </a>
      released a reissue of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Opposite_of_December" title="The Opposite of December">
        The Opposite of December
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tear_from_the_Red" title="Tear from the Red">
        Tear from the Red
       </a>
      </i>
      in late-2012.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
