       <article class="article">
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          There's no shortage of information out there on the health benefits of drinking green smoothies. They give you a huge amount of nutritional bang for your buck, boost your energy, strengthen your immune system, detox your body and beautify your hair, nails and skin too. Drinking green smoothies can inch you closer to consuming your recommended daily dose of fruits and veggies and also inch you closer to fitting into your skinny jeans!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          The point is, life is better when green smoothies are involved. But it's hard to make regular healthy smoothie drinking an enjoyable habit. Though they're one of the simplest nutritious meals you can make, just one little mistake can throw off the taste or texture, leaving you with a blender full of unpleasant mush.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Here are some tips to make sure youre dumping your green smoothie down your throat and not down your drain.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
          1. Freeze your bananas.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
         </b>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Frozen bananas are what make smoothies creamy and dreamy. They act as a perfect thickener, making it so you cant even tell theyre accompanied by stringy kale or spinach. Frozen bananas also add a substantial, yet mellow, sweetness to smoothies, making it so you cant even TASTE that the kale or spinach are in the same drink.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <em style="background-color: initial;">
          Trick of the trade:
         </em>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Peel and cut (in half or fourths) ripe (aka brown, speckled) bananas. Then, throw them in a baggie and freeze them so theyre ready to grab and blend into your green smoothies.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
          2. Punch up with no-cal flavors.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
         </b>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Adding herbs and spices to your smoothies is a fun and calorie-free way to really improve their flavor. Herbs like mint and basil are wonderful paired with berries and tropical fruits like mangos, watermelon and pineapple. Spices like cinnamon, pumpkin pie spice and cardamom are lovely with peaches and bananas.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <em style="background-color: initial;">
          Trick of the trade:
         </em>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Freeze your herbs when theyre on the verge of going bad. Youll waste less and you can use those frozen herbs to greatly enhance your smoothies.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
          3. Leave your ice in the freezer.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
         </b>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Believe it or not, adding ice to your smoothie typically turns it into a slushy. The texture will be way off, unless what youre going for is a mock 7-11 Slurpee. Instead, use frozen fruits in your smoothies! Theyre a two-for-one deal: they add wonderful flavor and nutrients AND they act as ice, making your smoothies thicker and chilled just the way you want it.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <em style="background-color: initial;">
          Trick of the trade:
         </em>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Tired of blending the same fruits from your grocery store's freezers? Try freezing ripe fruits that you cut up at home! You can buy a ripe papaya or cut up a perfect watermelon and freeze them in chunks to add to your smoothies.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
          4. Think outside the (base) box.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
         </b>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Theres no hard rule saying you have to make your green smoothies with a type of milk. Be it almond, soy, rice, hemp or dairy. How about adding the low calorie, naturally sweetness of coconut water? Or the zero calorie, yet super practical, no-excuse liquid called water? Or even a lovely flavored chilled tea?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          You can even go halfsies on these liquids
         </span>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          
         </span>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          half coconut water and half water. Half water and half milk. The options are really endless.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <em style="background-color: initial;">
          Trick of the trade:
         </em>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Brew a large batch of green tea, pour it into ball jars, and keep them in your fridge. Use this chilled green tea as a base for your morning green smoothie to give you an added boost of energy to kick off your day.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
          5. Make it part of your daily routine.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
         </b>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Drinking green smoothies is more of a habit you create than a craving you satisfy, at least at first. To get yourself in the habit of drinking green smoothies, youve got to schedule it into your day, as you would any other habit you were trying to build. When training for a 5K, you dont just wing your practice runs, you schedule them out, prioritize them and plan around them.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Same goes for green smoothies. Dont have time to blend one before work? Make it a priority to drink it when you get home during that in-between period when youre hungry but its not yet dinner time. To really reap the benefits of green smoothies, drinking them has got to become a daily habit.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <em style="background-color: initial;">
          Trick of the trade:
         </em>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Plan out your smoothies for the week. Plan out when youll drink them, what recipe youll make, and what you need to buy or freeze to carry this out. Then, stick to your plan  no matter what!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
          6. De-stem your greens.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b style="background-color: initial;">
         </b>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          The biggest mistake newbie green smoothie drinkers make is not realizing the need to de-stem their greens! With spinach this isnt a problem, but with kale, oh my. Whenever people complain about the horrible consistency of a kale smoothie, nine times out of 10 its because theyre blending up the stems. Even in a super powerful blender youll be better off without those harsh, thick, stringy stems. Just remove them! You (and your teeth) will be happy you did.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <em style="background-color: initial;">
          Trick of the trade:
         </em>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Save your stems! Instead of throwing them away, try boiling them for about 20 minutes and then adding them to a pesto recipe. Or, try sauting them and adding them to a medley of sauted veggies.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <strong style="background-color: initial;">
          7.
         </strong>
         <strong style="background-color: initial;">
          Act like a brat.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong style="background-color: initial;">
         </strong>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          Just because there are endless smoothie recipes on the web and in books doesnt mean you have to make them all! Making a smoothie you absolutely hate is probably more likely to discourage you from drinking them than to encourage you to try a different recipe. So be a brat when it comes to choosing your smoothie ingredients. If you cant stand the flavor of grapefruit, dont try a grapefruit-based smoothie! If cucumbers creep you out, dont blend in cucumbers! Its better to find a few perfect green smoothie combinations you absolutely adore than it is to try to force yourself to chug flavors you detest.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          You hereby have the power to veto any ingredient that will make your smoothie undrinkable, even if its the latest superfood that everyone swears by. Its better to drink your kale, banana and cinnamon smoothie that you enjoy, than to drink and gag over one sip of something funky you hate.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
         </span>
         <em style="background-color: initial;">
          Trick of the trade:
         </em>
         <span style="background-color: initial;">
          With great power comes great responsibility. You are responsible for getting some greens in your smoothies, so dont start claiming that greens make you gag. This is about finding the fruits and flavors that you adore that makes your greens smoothies something you cant live without.
         </span>
        </p>

$('.twitter_sharequote_button').click(function(event){
    event.preventDefault();
    var by = '';
    
        by = ' by @partyinmyplants';
        
    
    var maxTweetLen = 97 - by.length; // 140(max tweet length) - 42 - by.length - 1 (a space for an ending ellipsis).
    var link = 'https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?url=' + encodeURIComponent(MBGArticle.shortUrl);
    var text = $(this).parent().attr("data-text");
    var via = 'mindbodygreen';
    
    if(text.length &gt; maxTweetLen){
    	try{
    		var trimmedText = '';
    		var words = text.split(' ');
    		var counter = 0;
    		for(var i in words){
    			var w = words[i];
    			if(counter + w.length &gt;= maxTweetLen){
    				if(trimmedText.substr(trimmedText.length-1) === ' '){
    					trimmedText = trimmedText.substr(0, trimmedText.length-1);
    				}
    				trimmedText += ''; // finish the trimmed quote with an ellipsis.
    				break;
    			}else{
    				trimmedText += (w + ' ');
    				counter += (w.length + 1);
    			}
    		}
    		text = trimmedText;
    	}catch(err){
    		console.log(err);
    	}
    }
    
    link += '&amp;via=' + via + '&amp;text=' + encodeURIComponent(text) + by;
    window.open(link);
    try{
        ga('send', 'event', 'Article', 'Twitter Share Clicked', '14026');
    }catch(err){}
});

$('#article_email_modal_send_button').click(function(event){

    $('#article_email_modal_response_target').addClass('hide').removeClass('error').addClass('success').html('');
    if($('#article_email_modal_from').val().trim()===''){
        $('#article_email_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('success').addClass('alert').html('Whoops! You need to provide your email address to send this article!');
        return false;
    }
    var to = [];
    var email = {};
    var emails = $('#article_email_modal_to').val().split(',');
    $.each(emails, function(i, v) {
        if(v.trim()!==''){
            to.push(v.trim());
        }
    });
    if(to.length===0){
        $('#article_email_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('success').addClass('alert').html('Whoops! You need to provide some email addresses to send this article to!');
        return false;
    }
    
    email.fromEmailAddress = $('#article_email_modal_from').val().trim();
    email.emailAddresses = to;
    email.type = 'article';
    email.postId = '14026';
    
    var inputString = JSON.stringify(email);
    $.ajax({
        type : 'POST',
        url : MBGGlobals.getBaseUrl() + 'emailafriend.action',
        data: {
            input : inputString
        }
    }).done(function(obj) {
        if(obj.success === true) {
            var successMessage = 'Your email has been sent!';
            $('#article_email_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('error').addClass('success').html(successMessage);

                ga('send', 'event', 'Article', 'Article Emailed', '14026');

            $('#article_email_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('success').addClass('alert').html('Whoops! Something went wrong and we could not send your email. If this error persists, please write us at support@mindbodygreen.com. ' + obj.message);

        }
    }).fail(function(jqXHR, textStatus) {
        $('#article_email_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('success').addClass('alert').html('Whoops! Something went wrong and we could not save your article. If this error persists, please write us at support@mindbodygreen.com.');

    });

$('.article_save_button').click(function(event){
    event.preventDefault();
    
    var getChannelsShowModal = function(response,error){
        if(error!==null){
            console.log(error);
            return;
        }
        if(response!==null){
            if(response===false){
                console.log('Login returned false');
                return;
            }else if(response===true){
                MBGUI.toggleUtilityMenuToMyMBG();                   
            }
        }
        $.ajax({
            url: MBGGlobals.getBaseUrl() + 'getchannels.action',
            traditional : true,
            cache : false
        }).done(function(data){
            if(data.success===true){
                if(data.loggedIn === true){
                    var html = "";
                    $(data.channels).each(function(index,item){
                        html += "&lt;input type='checkbox' name='channels' value='" + item.id + "' label='" + item.name + "'/&gt;&nbsp;&nbsp;" + item.name + "&lt;br&gt;";
                    });
                    if(html != ""){
                        $('#article_save_modal_channel_target').html(html);
                    } else {
                        $('#article_save_modal_channel_target').html('You have no saved channels');
                    }
                    $('#article_save_modal').foundation('reveal','open');
                }else{
                    // server thinks the user is not logged in but client does, so show log in screenset
                    MBGSocial.displayLoginModal(getChannelsShowModal);
                }
            }else{
                console.log('An error occcured getting channels: ' + data.message);
            }           
        }).fail(function(xhr,status,error){
            console.log('An error occurred getting channels ' + status + ', ' + error);
        });
                
    };
    
    if(MBGSocial.isUserLoggedIn()){
        try{
            ga('send', 'event', 'Article', 'Logged In Save Button', '14026');
        }catch(err){}
        getChannelsShowModal(null,null);
    }else{
        try{
            ga('send', 'event', 'Article', 'Logged Out Save Button', '14026');
        }catch(err){}
        // use register screenset

$('#article_save_modal_save_button').click(function(event){
    event.preventDefault();
    var sub = {};
        sub.id = '14026';
        sub.objectType = 'post';
        var newChannel = $('#article_save_modal_new_channel').val().trim();
        var idCount = $('#article_save_modal input[name=channels]:checked').length;
        if (newChannel === '' &amp;&amp; idCount == 0) {
            $('#article_save_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('success').addClass('alert').html('Whoops! You need to select a channel or create a new one to save this article!');
            return false;
        }
        if (newChannel != '') {
            sub.newChannel = newChannel;
        }
        if (idCount &gt; 0) {
            var channels = [];
            $('#article_save_modal input:checked').each(function() {
                channels.push($(this).val());
            });
            sub.channelIds = channels;
        }
        
        var inputString = JSON.stringify(sub);
        $.ajax({
            type : 'POST',
            cache : false,
            url : MBGGlobals.getBaseUrl() + 'savechannelsubscription.action',
            data: {
                subscription : inputString
            }
        }).done(function(obj) {
            if(obj.success === true) {
                var successMessage = 'Your article has been saved!';
                if (newChannel !== '') successMessage = 'Your article and new channel have been saved!';
                $('#article_save_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('error').addClass('success').html(successMessage);
                setTimeout(function() {
                    console.log('closing modal');
                    $('#article_save_modal').foundation('reveal','close');
                    $('#article_save_modal_response_target').addClass('hide').html('');
                }, 3500);
                try{
                    ga('send', 'event', 'Article', 'Article Saved', '14026');
                }catch(err){}
            } else {
                $('#article_save_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('success').addClass('alert').html('Whoops! Something went wrong and we could not save your article. If this error persists, please write us at support@mindbodygreen.com.');
                console.log(obj.message);
            }
        }).fail(function(jqXHR, textStatus) {
            $('#article_save_modal_response_target').removeClass('hide').removeClass('success').addClass('alert').html('Whoops! Something went wrong and we could not save your article. If this error persists, please write us at support@mindbodygreen.com.');
            console.log(textStatus);
        });
        
        
});
