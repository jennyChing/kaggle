       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids Room
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-30T05:00:11+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 30, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://justagirlandherblog.com/author/abbylawson/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            justagirlabby
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://justagirlandherblog.com/5-ways-to-maximize-space-in-a-shared-kids-room/#comments">
           10 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10908" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/5-Ways-to-Maximize-Space-in-a-Shared-Kids-Room-Just-a-Girl-and-Her-Blog-453x680.png" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Hey there, friends! I am so excited to be participating in a super fun event this week: BOY MOM MADNESS! 15 boy mamas from around blogland will be teaming up to bring you boy-themed posts all week long yay!!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10911" height="525" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/boy-mom-madness-graphic-FINAL-680x525.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As you can see, were kicking things off with Boy Bedrooms! I recently revamped
         <a href="http://justagirlandherblog.com/shared-boys-bedroom/" target="_blank" title="A Shared Boys Bedroom: Some Updates {and FREE printables!}">
          the Cs shared room
         </a>
         a little bit, so today I thought Id talk about the steps I took to make the most of their not-so-huge space.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Make a plan.
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10912" height="349" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/The-Cs-Room-Floor-Plan-2.jpg" width="579"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         When we first started dreaming and scheming about the boys shared space, I knew it would have to be multi-functional. They had to have room to sleep, of course, along with clothes storage. We wanted to keep some toys in their room, have a spot for creating, and we also needed room to store their hand-me-downs. It was kind of a tall order for an 11 x 10 space that would house two little people.
        </p>
        <p>
         So instead of just going out and buying whatever furniture and storage pieces caught our eye, we made a plan for how we would squeeze in all of the functional elements and still have a fun space for our boys.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10920" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Beds1-453x680.jpg" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Use vertical space.
        </h3>
        <p>
         A big part of our plan involved making the most of the vertical space in the room. Bunk beds were an obvious choice, but we also used a vertical unit to store toys
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10913" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Shelf-Side1-453x680.jpg" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Donnie had actually made this ladder shelf for his dorm room when we were in college I bet he never thought it would end up as a storage unit for his kids toys!
        </p>
        <p>
         The Cs craft areaalso makes the most of vertical space
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10914" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Craft-Corner-453x680.jpg" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Fortunately, Connor is tall and can reach up pretty high and is also willing to help little Caleb grab what he needs.
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Get creative with the closet.
        </h3>
        <p>
         The closet was another area where we knew we had to use every possible inch. We had two dressers to fit into the room, so one of them went in the closet, where it fit perfectly underneath the boys hanging clothes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10915" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Closet1-453x680.jpg" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Hand-me-downs are stored in the back of the closet, and we decided to use pull-out drawers to give us easier access to things whenever we needed them (as opposed to stacking bins, which we would have to unstack and bring out of the closet to get something).
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10916" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Closet-Storage-453x680.jpg" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Corral and label.
        </h3>
        <p>
         I love, love, love all types of bins and storage containers, especially when it comes to toys. Its so much easier to keep sets together when they each have their own designated spot rather than one big toy box where everything gets jumbled up and lost.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10917" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Shelf-Detail1-453x680.jpg" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Equal to my love for bins is my love for labels! Just about anything will get a label if it sits still long enough at our house.
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10918" height="453" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Close-Bins-680x453.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         For the 1-2-3 bins, I designed labels on the computer, printed and laminated them, then punched a hole and hung them from the bins with ribbon. The plastic bins are labeled with adhesive vinyl lettering that I cut with my Silhouette machine. With individual bins + labels, the Cs know exactly where everything goes at clean-up time, and if theyre in the mood to play with a certain toy, its easy to pluck the correct bin off the shelf and have at it!
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Purge often.
        </h3>
        <p>
         Finally, clutter will make a shared space feel crowded
         <em>
          really
         </em>
         fast, so I am constantly purging the boys items to get rid of broken toys, clothes they have grown out of, worn out craft supplies, etc. By keeping only the things that are 1. in good shape and 2. used on a regular basis, we are able to keep the room clutter-free and manageable for the Cs when its time to clean up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10919" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Craft-Stuff-453x680.jpg" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         It is totally possible to get a lot of function out of a small, shared kids room! The Cs love sharing a space when weve asked them if theyd prefer to have their own, they always say, No way! and tell us they want to stay together. Following the steps I shared above really helps us to maketheir room a fun, comfortable space for both of them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10922" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Boots-453x680.jpg" width="453"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Want to check out more awesome boy rooms? Be sure to visit these other boy mamas for more inspiration!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="5 Ways to Maximize Space in a Shared Kids' Room | Just a Girl and Her Blog" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10921" height="680" src="http://justagirlandherblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Boy-Mom-Madness-Room-Collage-618x680.jpg" width="618"/>
        </p>
