        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Chicken Noodle Soup
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.mollyshomecooking.com/soups-stews-chili/chicken-noodle-soup/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://mollyshomecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cover-chicken-noodle-soup.jpg">
          <img alt="chicken noodle soup" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-605" height="393" src="http://mollyshomecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cover-chicken-noodle-soup.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         My husband hasnt been feeling well the last few days. That calls for one thing: Chicken noodle soup.
        </p>
        <p>
         Few things in life are more comforting than chicken noodle soup. It gives the full body warmth not many other foods offer. On sick days, like today, or cold days, annoying days, long days  chicken noodle soup always gets the job done.
        </p>
        <p>
         Long ago, I started making chicken noodle at home since relying on restaurants had proven to be a fruitless endeavor. Its so easy too, so why not?
        </p>
        <p>
         One problem Ive found with ordering chicken noodle soup at restaurants is that its usually bland. A major issue is lack of salt. Salt is the most necessary ingredient in soups. It brings out all of the vegetable and chicken flavor that you so carefully added when making your stock from scratch. No salt creates a sad, flavorless and disappointing soup. I always assume that a deli or restaurant with a tasteless soup is NOT using homemade stock in their soup. It makes a
         <em>
          huge
         </em>
         difference.
        </p>
        <p>
         So taste and season accordingly as you go.With great stock, some veggies, egg noodles, tender chicken and proper seasoning, your soup will be one to remember.
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope this hits the spot for you like it did for us!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-531">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Chicken Noodle Soup
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Adapted from
         <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/tyler-florence/chicken-noodle-soup-recipe0.html" target="_blank">
          Tyler Florence
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2 cups shredded chicken
         <br/>
         3 celery ribs, cut 1/2 inch thick
         <br/>
         3 carrots, diced 1/2 inch thick
         <br/>
         1 medium onion, chopped
         <br/>
         3 garlic cloves, minced
         <br/>
         2 quarts chicken stock (recipe below)
         <br/>
         8 oz egg noodles
         <br/>
         2 tablespoons olive oil
         <br/>
         5 fresh thyme sprigs
         <br/>
         1 bay leaf
         <br/>
         1/4 cup flat leaf parsley, roughly chopped
         <br/>
         salt + pepper
        </p>
        <p>
         Add oil to soup pot over medium heat. Add onions, celery, carrots, garlic, thyme, bay leaf and cook for 5-6 minutes. The vegetables should be soft, but not brown.
        </p>
        <p>
         Add chicken stock to the vegetables and bring it to a boil.
        </p>
        <p>
         When the vegetable and stock mixture begin to boil, add the egg noodles and continue simmering for 5 another minutes. Add the chicken and heat through. Season with salt and pepper, add some parsley and serve.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Chicken Stock
         </strong>
         <br/>
         3-4 lb chicken (breasts, thighs, wings)
         <br/>
         3 carrots, cut into chunks
         <br/>
         4 celery stalks, cut into chunks
         <br/>
         2 large onions, quartered
         <br/>
         1 head of garlic, halved
         <br/>
         2 bay leaves
         <br/>
         1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
         <br/>
         2 tablespoons olive oil
         <br/>
         fresh thyme
         <br/>
         fresh parsley
        </p>
        <p>
         Add oil to soup pot over medium heat. Add carrots, celery, onions and garlic. Sautfor several minutes before adding chicken, bay leaves, peppercorns, thyme and parsley.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.mollyshomecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/chicken-stock.jpg">
          <img alt="chicken stock" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-573" height="331" src="http://www.mollyshomecooking.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/chicken-stock.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Cover with water and bring to a boil. Lower heat to a simmer and cook for additional 1-1 1/2 hours, until the chicken is cooked.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remove chicken and shred for later.
        </p>
        <p>
         Drain the stock through a sieve into another container and discard the vegetables.
        </p>
        <p>
         Use stock immediately or store in the refrigerator (1 week) or freeze.
        </p>
