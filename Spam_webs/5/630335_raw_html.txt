       <h1 class="">
        Beauty Experiment: Olive Oil As An In-Shower Moisturizer
       </h1>
       <div class="byline">
        BY
        <a href="/contributors/petra-guglielmetti">
         Petra Guglielmetti
        </a>
        - 
                                February 17, 2009
       </div>
       <div class="heart-count" data-glm-id="1000000000111396" data-glm-stat="likes" data-glm-value="1">
        1
       </div>
       <div class="social-container top">

        </div>
       </div>

      <div class="span8 glamour-content">
       <div class="article">
        <div class="article-text">
         <p>
          Back when I revealed my new dry-skin solution--putting
          <a href="http://www.glamour.com/beauty/blogs/girls-in-the-beauty-department/2009/01/a-little-somethinsomethin-to-t.html">
           deep conditioner on my shower pouf
          </a>
          --readers like sruggi1, mandabear_heart and GLSD mentioned their creative in-shower uses of baby oil, vegetable oil and olive oil. Genius! I had to incorporate some oil into my own scrubdown routine.
         </p>
         <p>
          <br/>
          Conveniently, I had a slim mini bottle of oilve oil that came in some gift basket or something, so I just put it on my shower shelf, where it looks cute chillin' out amidst my more traditional beauty products.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;">
           <img alt="0217-olive_oil_shower_bd.jpg" class="mt-image-none" height="300" src="http://mtblog.glamour.com/beauty/blogs/girls-in-the-beauty-department/2009/02/11/0217-olive_oil_shower_bd.jpg" style="" width="400"/>
          </span>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p class="caption">
          You can find lots of cute mini bottles like this at the Container Store!
         </p>
         <p>
          One day after scrubbing and shaving, I rubbed about a teaspoon of oil on my legs, which tend to be dry and irritated in winter. I rinsed to prevent myself from smelling like salad all day, and the water beaded up afterward, indicating that my legs still had a very thin coating of the oil. What a difference this made! I followed with my regular moisturizer, and I loved how soft my skin felt all day. The only drawback: The oil did leave my shower floor pretty slick (be careful!).
         </p>
         <p>
          Next time, I'm going to bring in a little cupful of brown sugar to mix with the oil, per mandabear_heart's suggestion for a DIY body scrub. I think kosher salt would be good, too, for those seeking a deeper exfoliation.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Have any of you dreamed up a delicious DIY skin treatment lately?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          More recent Beauty Experiments:
          <a href="http://www.glamour.com/beauty/blogs/girls-in-the-beauty-department/2009/02/i-wore-monistat-on-my-face-las.html">
           Monistat as foundation primer
          </a>
          , and
          <a href="http://www.glamour.com/beauty/blogs/girls-in-the-beauty-department/2008/11/baby-wipes-as-face-wipes-genui.html">
           Pampers wipes as makeup-remover cloths
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          More ways to soothe your skin 'til spring comes:

           deep-conditioner as body wash
          </a>
          , Beth's
          <a href="http://www.glamour.com/beauty/blogs/girls-in-the-beauty-department/2009/01/the-moisturizing-product-thats.html">

          </a>
