         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Instruments for entire orchestra made from trash
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="publication-logo-wrapper article-subtitle">
         Local Source:
         <span class="publication-logo" style="color:#e6901e;">
          <a href="http://cultura.elpais.com/cultura/2014/11/25/actualidad/1416908969_402193.html" style="color:#e6901e;" target="_blank">
           El Pais
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="post-date">
          November 29, 2014
          <span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!--&lt;br class="clear"/&gt;-->
        <div class="content-wrapper">
         <div class="wrapper-1">
          <div class="post-thumbnail">
           <img alt="Instruments for entire orchestra made from trash" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="300" src="http://www.pangeatoday.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/spain12.jpg" width="560"/>
          </div>
          <p>
           The Paraguayan Orchestra of Recycled Instruments of Cateura has traveled to some 30 countries, most recently performing as the opening act for the legendary band Metallica on their tour in South America.
          </p>
          <p>
           Now, Madrid will be the second city in the world to have an orchestra of recycled instruments. Ecoembes is the environmental organization that is givingSpains capital its own orchestra of recycled instruments.
          </p>
          <p>
           With Ecoembes help and that of Favio Chvez, the original director of the Orchestra of Cateura, the first concerts should take place in January.
          </p>
          <p>
           Discipline, respect, commitment, responsibility, and creativity, Chavez said about what students gain from this project. The music beyond music: Recycling what seems to be no longer useful and giving it one more chance at life.
          </p>
          <p>
           Currently two centers are working on the project of creating instruments from trash. Children in one of the centers have already begun making their violins from the citys garbage.
          </p>
