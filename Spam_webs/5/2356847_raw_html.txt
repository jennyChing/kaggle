        <h2>
         <a id="roles" name="roles">
         </a>
         Roles of Chief Executive Officer
        </h2>
        <p>
         NOTE: References to a Boards of Directors in the following are in regard to 
  chief executive officers of corporations, whether for-profit or nonprofit.
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Leader
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Advises the Board
         </li>
         <li>
          Advocates / promotes organization and stakeholder change related to organization 
    mission
         </li>
         <li>
          Supports motivation of employees in organization products/programs and 
    operations
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         Visionary / Information Bearer
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Ensures staff and Board have sufficient and up-to-date information
         </li>
         <li>
          Looks to the future for change opportunities
         </li>
         <li>
          Interfaces between Board and employees
         </li>
         <li>
          Interfaces between organization and community
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         <b>
          Decision Maker
         </b>
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Formulates policies and planning recommendations to the Board
         </li>
         <li>
          Decides or guides courses of action in operations by staff
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         <b>
          Manager
         </b>
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Oversees operations of organization
         </li>
         <li>
          Implements plans
         </li>
         <li>
          Manages human resources of organization
         </li>
         <li>
          Manages financial and physical resources
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         <b>
          Board Developer
         </b>
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Assists in the selection and evaluation of board members
         </li>
         <li>
          Makes recommendations, supports Board during orientation and self-evaluation
         </li>
         <li>
          Supports Board's evaluation of Chief Executive
         </li>
        </ul>

        <h2>
         <a id="resp" name="resp">
         </a>
         Responsibilities of Chief Executive Officer
        </h2>
        <p>
         There is no standardized list of the
major functions and responsibilities carried out by position of
chief executive officer. The following list is one perspective
and includes the major functions typically addressed by job descriptions
of chief executive officers.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1.
         <b>
          Board Administration and Support
         </b>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Supports operations and administration of Board by advising and informing Board 
  members, interfacing between Board and staff, and supporting Board's evaluation 
  of chief executive
        </p>
        <h3>
         2.
         <b>
          Program, Product and Service Delivery
         </b>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Oversees design, marketing, promotion, delivery and quality of programs, products 
  and services
        </p>
        <h3>
         3.
         <b>
          Financial, Tax, Risk and Facilities Management
         </b>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Recommends yearly budget for Board approval and prudently manages organization's 
  resources within those budget guidelines according to current laws and regulations
        </p>
        <h3>
         4.
         <b>
          Human Resource Management
         </b>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Effectively manages the human resources of the organization according to authorized 
  personnel policies and procedures that fully conform to current laws and regulations
        </p>
        <h3>
         5.
         <b>
          Community and Public Relations
         </b>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Assures the organization and its mission, programs, products and services are 
  consistently presented in strong, positive image to relevant stakeholders
        </p>
        <h3>
         6.
         <b>
          Fundraising (nonprofit-specific)
         </b>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Oversees fundraising planning and implementation, including identifying resource 
  requirements, researching funding sources, establishing strategies to approach 
  funders, submitting proposals and administrating fundraising records and documentation
        </p>

        <h2>
         Recommended Books
        </h2>
        <p>
         There is an explosion of books about leadership.  
Some are about broad and general philosophies, paradigms, visions and values.  Others are about more specific models and theories. 
Still, others are about even more specific tips and tools.  Bibliographies of books on leadership span numerous pages.  The books mentioned on 
these pages are a reasonable beginning.  They are focused on books with both foundational principles and practical tips and tools.
        </p>
        <p>
         Note that, although many perspectives on leadership are about leading other individuals and groups, there are other domains of leadership, including leading 
oneself and organizations.  The books referenced from this page are in regard to all domains of leadership.
        </p>
        <hr align="left"/>
        <br/>
        <h3>
         Leading For-Profits and Nonprofits
        </h3>
        <p>
         There is much more in common between leading a for-profit and nonprofit than many people might realize.  Small nonprofits are a lot more 
like small for-profits, than large nonprofits.  Similarly, large nonprofits are a lot more like large for-profits, than small nonprofits.
Nonprofits often include leading volunteers.  A section, later on below, provides more books about leading specifically in nonprofits.
        </p>
