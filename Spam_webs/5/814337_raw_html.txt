        <h4 class="title">
         What did happen
        </h4>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p1">
         Authorities have determined that William J. Rengigas, 59, died Monday, March 16, of smoke inhalation in a fire at his home in TheDalles after deputies attempted to serve an eviction notice on him.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p2">
         Evidence suggests Rengigas shot one of his two beloved dogs, found in the home at 10th and Mt. Hood, and officials believe he likely was thwarted in shooting the second dog due to his gun jamming, according to a press release from the Wasco County district attorney and sheriffs office.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p3">
         The second dog was found deceased at Rengigas feet by law enforcement, the release stated.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p4">
         The state medical examiner concluded Rengigas committed suicide by smoke inhalation because he could have exited his home before smoke overcame him but chose not to, the release stated.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p5">
         The fact he likely killed his dogs also supports a suicide determination, the press release stated.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p6">
         Oregon State Police arson investigators determined the fire started in the garage and was likely arson, set by Rengigas, the sole occupant of the home, the release stated.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p7">
         Police were unable to find any next of kin for Rengigas and are asking the publics help in locating any of his family members.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p8">
         Agencies involved in the hours-long ordeal Monday were the Wasco County Sheriffs Office, The Dalles Police Department, The Dalles Area Command of the Oregon State Police, Mid-Columbia Fire &amp; Rescue, Oregon State Police Explosives and the Oregon State Police SWAT unit out of Salem.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p9">
         The Oregon State Police arson unit responded to investigate the fire.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p10">
         Additional assistance was provided by The Dalles Public works, NW Natural Gas, North Wasco PUD, Dallesport Fire Department, Dufur Ambulance Service, and Hood River Fire.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p11">
         The support by our fellow law enforcement and public safety partners is very appreciated, especially when incidents like this occur, said Lane Magill, Wasco County Sheriffs Office chief deputy, who supervised the scene.
        </p>
        <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p12">
         Wasco County District Attorney Nisley added, It is amazing to see so many different agencies work so well together.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p13">
        As soon as the bank told Dee Ashley last Friday that she had to give an eviction notice Monday to a man shed seen declining for months, she had a gut feeling it would end badly.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p14">
        She made an appointment for 8:30 a.m. on Monday, March 16 at William Rengigas house at the corner of 10th and Mt. Hood.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p15">
        As is protocol for a sheriffs lock-out, shed arranged to meet a sheriffs deputy at the house who would tell the man he had 15 minutes to get out of his foreclosed home, and a locksmith to change the locks.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p16">
        It ate at me all weekend. I just knew how hed been feeling depressed, knowing he had no place to go, she said of Rengigas.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p17">
        Shed even told her boss at Windermere Real Estate about her fears. I said I hope I dont get shot.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p18">
        At the house, she told the sheriffs deputy, I dont have a good feeling. Hes either going to shoot us or kill himself.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p19">
        The deputy asked if she wanted him to kick in the door. She asked him to knock instead. He knocked on the locked screen door and got no answer. The locksmith removed the screen door and then the deputy knocked on the main door.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p20">
        All of a sudden he said, Shots fired! Shots fired! And we all moved back, recounted Ashley, who was standing in the nearby alley and didnt hear the gunshots.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p21">
        Deputies heard about five shots fired within the house. Soon smoke started seeping from the homes eaves. Two area schools were quickly put on lock down and nearby homes evacuated. Later, power was shut off to the neighborhood to help firefighting efforts.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p22">
        Officers repeatedly tried to get a response from Rengigas on loudspeaker. They pulled back until an Oregon State Police SWAT team from Salem arrived.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p23">
        A robot was deployed but couldnt be used because of smoke.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p24">
        When the SWAT team breached the front door, the back of the house burst into open flame.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p25">
        Before then, it was smoking and smoking and smoking and once they allowed oxygen in, it went up in flames, Ashley said.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p26">
        After the fire was out, law enforcement went inside and found Rengigas body and two deceased dogs. He was 59. (See related story.)
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p27">
        Ashley wasnt certain before Monday morning that Rengigas had a gun, but I knew that he felt strongly about Second Amendment rights and she knew it was a possibility hed have a weapon.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p28">
        Ashley not only directly watched the event unfold, but also saw how social media became a garble of misinformation about the incident and condemnation of the deceased.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p29">
        Shed seen reports on Facebook that the landlord had been shot, and that officers had been shot. She saw reports that the police started the fire to smoke out the occupant; and that it was a drug bust gone wrong. None of it was true.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p30">
        You know what people? Before you have the facts, maybe not stick it on Facebook. Lordy, she said.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p31">
        People also criticized him for killing his dogs and being generally rude.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p32">
        Through tears, Ashley said, I just want people to know he wasnt a mean, violent man. He didnt kill the dogs out of violence and hate. He took them with him.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p33">
        She added, He had nowhere to go, no hope. He wasnt crazy. People have to have a little compassion. I see so much crap on Facebook.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p34">
        While people criticized him for killing his dogs, Ashley said, He probably felt nobody could care for them like he could. That was all they had and they were going together.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p35">
        She said, You can only imagine what his family is reading and seeing and I want them to know there are people who care about him and dont think hes crazy.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p36">
        Even before she began handling eviction proceedings last October, Ashley had noticed Rengigas decline.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p37">
        He wasnt taking care of himself or his yard like he used to. His once neat white beard was down to the middle of his chest and his yard was overgrown and trashy.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p38">
        She said, Watch out for your neighbors. Ask them if they need help. He wasnt bad. He wasnt a horrible person. He had a soft, kind side to him.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p39">
        Janna Hage, then director at Home at Last, agrees. Rengigas bought two Home At Last dogs in the mid-2000s.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p40">
        He was always a very nice, gentle man and we all liked him.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p41">
        He regularly donated supplies to Home At Last and sometimes walked dogs there.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p42">
        Three years ago, he came to Home At Last worried that he might lose his house and was afraid hed have to leave his dogs, Hage said.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p43">
        All he cared about was what was going to happen to his dogs, she said. I knew that that was really weighing on him.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p44">
        He walked his dogs almost daily on the Riverfront Trail.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p45">
        When I first met him he didnt have that crazy beard, Hage said. Hes not terribly outgoing. Hes a quiet man. I dont know if hes shy. Hes very respectful, just a gentle man.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p46">
        Neighbor Patti Smith had known Rengigas for eight years. She felt bad that she couldnt recall his last name. We called him Mr. Bill.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p47">
        She said he was a tall, slender man with a glass eye. She said he told her he was in the military and lost his eye in combat.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p48">
        His dogs would get out and hed call the police to report them missing and shed find them and bring them back to him.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p49">
        Every time hed give me a Starbucks card. Oh thank you, thank you, thank you! Smith recounted.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p50">
        His dogs were Smoky, Kona and Rusty. She thought he only had Rusty left when he died. His dogs, he absolutely loved them. He was a very sweet man. Very sweet. All this crazy stuff theyre saying about him, thats not the man I knew. He was always friendly, always kind.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p51">
        She said, Hes being extremely mistreated on Facebook. Its sickening. Let the man rest in peace. Maybe he had a bad moment but that doesnt account for his whole being.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p52">
        He had a medical marijuana card and she wonders if thats where the notion of a drug bust gone awry came about.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p53">
        I feel really bad, Ashley said. I wish he wouldve called me.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p54">
        She said she couldve gotten him relocation money from the bank.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p55">
        Youd be amazed what the bank will pay to have someone leave the house in broom-swept condition, she said.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p56">
        Ashley happened to know Rengigas because shed sold him the home when he moved up from California in 2006.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p57">
        She was involved with this eviction because she works with Fannie Mae, a government mortgage entity, on all of its evictions in the area.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p58">
        Because of her familiarity with the home, she even drew a floor plan of the house and gave it to police.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p59">
        Rengigas had been working at Cardinal Glass in Odell when he died, Ashley said.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p60">
        Ashley quickly notified the eviction attorney and the asset manager about what happened.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p61">
        They were just horrified.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p62">
        She said they asked if the media was on site and they were concerned about the tone of coverage.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p63">
        Were not the big bad people booting you out of your house. This has been going on for months and months. He felt he had nowhere to go, she said.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p64">
        Only a handful of foreclosures come to the level of needing a sheriffs lock-out, Ashley said.
       </p>
       <p class="permalinkable" id="h135261-p65">
        I just hope that the story that comes out is having some compassion for people and not jumping to conclusions, she said.
       </p>
