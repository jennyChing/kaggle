     <p>
      <strong>
       Where can you publish your video clips online
      </strong>
      ? This mini-guide of online video sharing sites should give you a good reference to find your preferred online video publishing outlet.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="video_publish_online_from_mobile_phone_id142426_size440.jpg" height="473" src="http://d3sdoylwcs36el.cloudfront.net/video_publish_online_from_mobile_phone_id142426_size440.jpg" width="440"/>
      <br/>
      <span class="photocredit">
       Photo credit: Stephen Coburn
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Online video sharing sites
      </strong>
      allow anyone connected to the web to easily upload digital video recordings so that these clips can be viewed by other people.
      <a href="http://www.masternewmedia.org/video_internet_television.htm" target="_blank">
       Online video sharing
      </a>
      sites have become a high in-demand online type of service because they allow small independent publishers the ability to publish video files of almost any size without problems.
     </p>
     <p>
      Normally, bloggers and other small independent publishers have only a limited amount of space on their web service provider server to upload additional files to be published on their web site. While this space maybe quite extensive for publishing hundreds of photos or audio files in mp3 format, video files can easily inflate to be several tens of megabytes each, if not hundreds, depending on video quality and length.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Consider for example that a video clip of someone singing a song
      </strong>
      , of about 5 minutes, recorded with a standard consumer camcorder and saved from
      <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/windowsxp/using/moviemaker/default.mspx" target="_blank">
       Windows Movie Maker
      </a>
      /
      <a href="http://www.apple.com/ilife/imovie/" target="_blank">
       iMovie
      </a>
      for web publication (say at 320 x 240 size and with a 512 Kbps data rate) can easily take up 20-30 megabytes.
     </p>
     <p>
      As you can see, the resolution of the video (that is the video window size at which the video is encoded - generally measured in pixels) and the "
      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bit_rate#Video_.28MPEG2.29" target="_blank">
       data rate
      </a>
      " (that is the amount of information that the video can send out to real-time viewers when played back - this is measured in the same unit as your net connection,
      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kbps" target="_blank">
       Kbps
      </a>
      (Kilobits per second), and in fact the video clips that you would like to publish on the web should be encoded at a "data rate" matching your typical reader connection speed) are two key factors that influence in a very significant way both the way your video "looks", as well as the "size" of its file.
     </p>
     <p>
      While having a video that is viewable in a bigger window and "looks" sharper (more defined) are qualities that need no other explanation, the issue of "file size" may not be obvious to all.
     </p>
     <p>
      The problem, when having a large file to be hosted on your web site just like any video clip would be, is two-fold:
     </p>
     <p>
      a)
      <strong>
       If you are uploading that video file to your own server
      </strong>
      or to an online video sharing service that does not support video streaming, your viewers will have to first download the whole video before they can actually watch it.
     </p>
     <p>
      b)
      <strong>
       If your readers like your video and come in hundreds or thousands to view it
      </strong>
      , you are going to be using a lot of "
      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bandwidth" target="_blank">
       bandwidth
      </a>
      ", that is, a lot of "data" will have to be served from your server to each one of your viewers. Problem is, that when you have a standard web site or blog, for which you pay a little hosting fee a month to an Internet Provider, you are paying for a very limited amount of that bandwidth, which is indeed abundant if you need to serve only web text pages with some images, but which suddenly becomes scarce the moment you start to publish video clips that have any kind of significant audience (a few hundreds or more a day).
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       This is why online video sharing sites have become so popular so rapidly
      </strong>
      . They fill a very real and tangible need. As small independent video publishers of all kinds, can buy good quality video cameras at increasingly lower prices and as it becomes easier and easier to download the recorded video onto a computer and then a web site, you can only expect a growing demand for more and more video sharing and publishing services.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       In this mini-guide round-up
      </strong>
      our Robin Good team looks at all the online video sharing sites that provide these capabilities while comparing their key traits and characteristics.
     </p>
     <p>
      Your input in the form of comments, suggestions and pointers is always very welcome (see bottom of mini-guide).

     <ul>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Google Video ***DEAD
       </strong>
       <p>
        <img alt="Google Video search" height="71" src="http://d3sdoylwcs36el.cloudfront.net/Google_video.gif" width="156"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://video.google.com" target="_blank">
         http://video.google.com
        </a>
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Google Video
        </strong>
        is an online video marketplace where you can search for, watch and buy videos including TV shows, movies, music videos, documentaries, personal productions and more. Google Video limits searches to video using Google's famous search engine.
       </p>
       <p>
        Now that Google have acquired YouTube, it is expected that features of both Google Video and YouTube may change over the coming months, but the services will retain their separate identities.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Interface
        </strong>
        : Google Video home page displays categories, popular videos and random picks. Or you can simply enter keywords and go. The results page displays a still image from the video along with program and episode name; and the date, time and length of the broadcast. The triangle icon is the play button that lets you watch a 10 second video snippet.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Viewing Options
        </strong>
        : You can view videos in the original size, double size, fit to window, or full screen.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Sharing
        </strong>
        You can download any of the videos to your desktop or email them to friends and family. Google also  provides the HTML code to embed the video onto a web page.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Community Features
        </strong>
        : Not Available
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Submitting Videos
        </strong>
        :
        <strong>
         Submit
        </strong>
        your media content to Google Video (it requires having a free Google account). Uploading videos to Google requires using their Uploader software.
       </p>
       <p>
        Google Video provides complete instructions on how to download and use Google Video Uploader.
       </p>
       <p>
        Google Video accepts videos in the following formats:
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.avi" target="_blank">
         AVI
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Systems_Format" target="_blank">
         ASF
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quicktime" target="_blank">
         QuickTime
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_Media" target="_blank">
         Windows Media
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mpeg" target="_blank">
         MPEG
        </a>
        formats.
       </p>
       <p>
        Google Video allows you to include a variety of metadata to help users find your video. You should include a descriptive title, create a clear description, and choose an appropriate category.
       </p>
       <p>
        Take a look at the Google Video guide for optimizing video information.
       </p>
       <p>
        Users are also able to find your video more easily if you create a transcript for each video file you've uploaded. You can upload a transcript via your Video Status page.
       </p>
       <p>
        Google Video provides a complete tutorial on uploading videos.
       </p>
       <p>
        Technical Requirements are also available.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Storage
        </strong>
        : Unlimited
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Additional Features
        </strong>
        :  Google Video includes copy-protected and fee-based videos. The search engine is free to use, of course. However, some videos require purchase. Once you've bought the video, you can download it multiple times so you can watch it on your other computers.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Videos that are copy-protected
        </strong>
        require using Google Video Player AND watching them while connected to the Internet. Unfortunately, Mac users can't watch purchased, copy-protected videos. They can only watch videos that aren't copy-protected.
       </p>
       <p>
        <br/>
        <br/>
       </p>
      </li>
      <li>
       <strong>
        YouTube
       </strong>
       <p>
        <img alt="you_tube_logo.gif" height="57" src="http://d3sdoylwcs36el.cloudfront.net/you_tube_logo.gif" width="139"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.youtube.com/" target="_blank">
         http://www.youtube.com/
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         YouTube is one of the most popular
        </strong>
        online video search engines. YouTube is a community just as much as it is a video search engine. Once logged in, you can upload videos, save your favorite videos, comment on videos, create subscriptions to particular users and tags, and create and join groups of common interest.
       </p>
       <p>
        Recently
        <a href="http://www.techcrunch.com/2006/10/09/google-has-acquired-youtube/" target="_blank">
         acquired by Google
        </a>
        , we can expect to see some changes in the service over the coming months, including perhaps the integration of Google AdSense, although Google has
        <a href="http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?file=/c/a/2006/10/09/BUGOVLLNEN4.DTL&amp;type=tech" target="_blank">
         announced that YouTube will remain separate
        </a>
        to Google Video, and will retain its name.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Interface
        </strong>
        : You can search their database of videos by category or keyword search. When doing a search for videos, 3 thumbnails appear along with the title, description, rating, and link to the video.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Viewing Options
        </strong>
        : Videos can be viewed their original window or full screen.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Sharing
        </strong>
        : Once you have found a video that you like, you can email it to friends or place it on your web site using the provided html code or through their
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Application_programming_interface" target="_blank">
         API
        </a>
        .

       <p>
        <strong>
         Community Features
        </strong>
        : Once users have signed up to the service it is possible to create a profile, and to
        <a href="http://www.jumpcut.com/groups/" target="_blank">
         sign in to, join or create a group
        </a>
        . The primary feature that makes Jumpcut more of a community based way of video sharing is the remixing of community members videos.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Submitting Videos
        </strong>
        : Submit your videos, images and music to JumpCut.
       </p>
       <p>
        You can upload videos in the following formats:
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FLV" target="_blank">
         .flv
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.mpg" target="_blank">
         .mpg
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.wmv" target="_blank">
         .wmv
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.avi" target="_blank">
         .avi
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.mov" target="_blank">
         .mov
        </a>
        ,and
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.mp4" target="_blank">
         .mp4
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        When uploading your video you need only choose if it will be public or private, and tag it.
       </p>
       <p>
        Videos must be less than 100 MB in size.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Storage
        </strong>
        : Unlimited
       </p>
      </li>
      <p>
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </p>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Veoh
       </strong>
       <p>
        <img alt="logo2.gif" height="61" src="http://d3sdoylwcs36el.cloudfront.net/logo2.gif" width="175"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.veoh.com/" target="_blank">
         <br/>
         http://www.veoh.com/
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Veoh offers a clearing house for independent and mainstream video content
        </strong>
        , allowing users to upload and share video through the Veoh network. User generated content sits alongside the output of major studios and production companies, making for a great way to reach a large audience.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Interface
        </strong>
        : Veoh's Interface offers the usual array of options, including most recent, most popular, recommended and most discussed videos, in addition to being able to search by tag or category. In addition, Veoh also offers a range of Internet TV channels including
        <a href="http://www.veoh.com/channel/indiefilms" target="_blank">
         Independent Films
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.veoh.com/channel/interviews" target="_blank">
         Conversations and interviews
        </a>
        that make for a refreshing way to access their content.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Viewing Options
        </strong>
        : Videos are at an impressive 500 x 400 pixels in dimension.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Sharing
        </strong>
        : Videos can be downloaded, embedded or emailed to a friend directly from the main video menu, in addition to being able to scrub through video, share it with other Veoh users and add it to a list of favorites. The ability to download content straight from the player is a nice feature.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Community Features
        </strong>
        : Each user, once
        <a href="http://www.veoh.com/login.html?redir=1&amp;noAction=1" target="_blank">
         signed up
        </a>
        can upload video, invite and share videos with friends, create a vlog, add favorites and make use of the Veoh client, which allows for easier uploading and downloading of high quality video content.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Submitting Videos
        </strong>
        : Videos can be submitted using your browser, or for larger files (those ober 50MB) the Veoh downloadable client is recommended, for easy uploading from your desktop.
       </p>
       <p>
        Veoh allows you to encode your video as you want it be seen, and does not transcode the end result, meaning that you can create high quality video files without fearing for them being reduced in quality, as is the case with some other services. Formats accepted are:
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.mpg" target="_blank">
         .mpg
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.wmv" target="_blank">
         .wmv
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.avi" target="_blank">
         .avi
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.mov" target="_blank">
         .mov
        </a>
        ,and
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.mp4" target="_blank">
         .mp4
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        When uploading your video you need to supply a name, description, genre, tags and advisory rating.
       </p>
       <p>
        Videos uploaded using the client can be of any size. Web uploads are limited to 100MB in file size.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Storage
        </strong>
        : Unlimited
       </p>
      </li>
      <p>
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </p>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Viddler
       </strong>
       <p>
        <img alt="viddlerlogo.jpg" height="59" src="http://d3sdoylwcs36el.cloudfront.net/viddlerlogo.jpg" width="131"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.viddler.com/" target="_blank">
         http://viddler.com
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Viddler allows for large video uploads in a number of formats
        </strong>
        , direct web-cam recording from your browser, timed tags in the video, for the classification of discrete moments in a video, and even timed comments that appear at key moments in video playback, allowing for both personal annotation and a strong community feedback element.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Interface
        </strong>
        : Viddler's collection can be explored through the week's most popular videos, recently uploaded video, popular tags, and by the newest or most active users in a given week, placing a strong emphasis on participation within the service.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Viewing Options
        </strong>
        : Videos are larger and clearer than average, can be scrubbed through in the usual way, in addition to being able to view at full screen, view all tags for a given video, or all comments. It is also possible to navigate the video using dotted tags and comments that appear in the time line, and to embed your own tags or comments using the built in menu.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Sharing
        </strong>
        : Video can be emailed to a friend, flagged as inappropriate, and specifically tagged moments (or the entire video) can be embedded into your blog or website.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Community Features
        </strong>
        : Beneath each video are options to find related videos, all videos by the same user, and the profiles of users that liked this video, so that it is possible to connect with people via videos that you share an interest in.
       </p>
       <p>
        The social dimension is further enchanced by being able to comment on specific moments in the video, and reply to others comments at these specific points in time, rather than comment streams based on the entire video. This allows people to be very specific as to the parts of the video that they choose to interact around.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Submitting Videos
        </strong>
        : Submit your videos to Viddler. You must sign up before you can upload videos and contribute to the community. It is possible to batch upload videos, which is a nice feature missing from most video sharing sites, and also to record video directly from your webcam.
       </p>
       <p>
        You can upload videos in the following formats:
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.avi" target="_blank">
         .AVI
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.mov" target="_blank">
         .MOV
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.wmv" target="_blank">
         WMV
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.mpg" target="_blank">
         .MPG
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Storage
        </strong>
        : Unlimited
       </p>
      </li>
