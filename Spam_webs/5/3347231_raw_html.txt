        <h1 class="entry-title">
         4 Real Foods You May Be Overeating
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="posted-on">
          Posted on
          <a href="http://stupideasypaleo.com/2013/12/16/4-real-foods-you-may-be-overeating/" rel="bookmark">

        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://cdn.stupideasypaleo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/800-x-419-nuts.png">
          <img alt="4 Real Foods You May Be Overeating | stupideasypaleo.com" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter wp-image-9304" data-lazy-src="http://cdn.stupideasypaleo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/800-x-419-nuts.png" data-lazy-type="image" height="419" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" title="4 Real Foods You May Be Overeating | stupideasypaleo.com" width="800"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="4 Real Foods You May Be Overeating | stupideasypaleo.com" class="aligncenter wp-image-9304" height="419" src="http://cdn.stupideasypaleo.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/800-x-419-nuts.png" title="4 Real Foods You May Be Overeating | stupideasypaleo.com" width="800"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
         When it comes to eating real foods, its still possible to overdo it.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre new to Paleo or a more general real food way of eating, it can be a monumental task to switch over from a diet of processed, nutrient-poor junk food to one thats healthier. Building a foundation on meat and eggs, veggies and fruit and healthy fats is one thatll take you far in your quest, but often, even the best of intentions can get us in trouble.
        </p>
        <p>
         You see, some foods, even though they fit a Paleo template, are easily over-consumed. Granted, its easy to eat too much of anything, and slipping up from time to time wont really make or break you in the long term. What I notice, though, are some real, Paleo foods that become problematic for folks even though theyre supposed to be okay to eat.
        </p>
        <p>
         With what frequency and in what quantity will consuming these foods be an issue? Its impossible to say for everybody, but if you notice you arent feeling or looking your best, it may be time to examine your eating patterns. Awareness, together with knowledge, is a powerful thing.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Real Food #1 You May Be Overeating: Nuts
        </h2>
        <p>
         Crunchy, fatty and satisfyingnuts are a common Paleo-friendly food thats over-consumed. Paleos definitely not a low fat approach to nutrition, but nuts are a dense source of polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFA), monounsaturated fatty acids (MUFA) and calories. Together with the fact that theyre portable, mindlessly munchable (or mindlessly spoonable out of a jar) and readily available, you can see why they could be an issue.
        </p>
        <p>
         What else about nuts makes them something to not crack out on? They contain phytic acid (one of the same reasons beans / legumes are avoided in Paleo) which prevents some of the minerals in the nuts from being available to us upon digestion. Another issue? Certain types of nuts particularly those with high PUFA content such as walnuts are prone to going rancid because these fatty acids are structurally more unstable.
        </p>
        <p>
         Im not telling you to never eat nuts ever again (I usually eat them once a day), but look for other healthy fat sources,like
         <a href="http://villagegreennetwork.com/marketplace/fats-oils-coconut-products/?AFFID=138556" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://villagegreennetwork.com/marketplace/fats-oils-coconut-products/?AFFID=138556', 'coconut products');" target="_blank">
          coconut products
         </a>
         , animal fats and olives, to incorporate into your diet. You can also soak nuts to reduce their phytic acid content (
         <a href="http://wholelifestylenutrition.com/recipes/appetizers-snacks/is-soaking-nuts-necessary-how-to-properly-soak-your-organic-raw-nuts/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://wholelifestylenutrition.com/recipes/appetizers-snacks/is-soaking-nuts-necessary-how-to-properly-soak-your-organic-raw-nuts/', 'click here for a tutorial from Whole Lifestyle Nutrition');" target="_blank">
          click here for a tutorial from Whole Lifestyle Nutrition
         </a>
         ). One of my favorite ways to use nuts is as a condiment for sprinkling on top of a dish for some crunch and texture.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Real Food #2 You May Be Overeating: Dried Fruit
        </h2>
        <p>
         Fruit is one of those real foods that gets a bad rap. Yes, its generally got more sugar than veggies and if youre particularly sugar-sensitive you may want to limit it to 1-2 servings a day, but its hardly the same as putting 3 spoonfuls of sugar in your coffee in the morning. Where fruit becomes more of an issue is when its dried.
        </p>
        <p>
         Removing the water from fruit makes the sugar highly concentrated, and since the fruits volume is reduced, its easy to keep popping it in your mouth. If youre sugar addicted (
         <a href="http://stupideasypaleo.com/how-i-conquered-sugar/" target="_blank" title="How I Conquered Sugar">
          like I was
         </a>
         ), you may do best to limit or avoid dried fruit. If and when I do eat it, its usually as a natural sweetener (say, in my
         <a href="http://stupideasypaleo.com/2013/11/10/paleo-thanksgiving-leftovers-sandwich/" target="_blank" title="Paleo Thanksgiving Leftovers Sandwich">
          cranberry sauce
         </a>
         ). I used to sit down to a bag of Trader Joes dried mango slices (some of you know exactly what Im talking about) and kill the whole thing in 5 minutes. Now, Im happy with half a sliced apple on top of my salad.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Real Food #3 You May Be Overeating: Kombucha
        </h2>
        <p>
         Jackie (
         <a href="http://thepaleomama.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://thepaleomama.com', 'The Paleo Mama');" target="_blank">
          The Paleo Mama
         </a>
         ), Vanessa (
         <a href="http://healthylivinghowto.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://healthylivinghowto.com', 'Healthy Living How To');" target="_blank">
          Healthy Living How To
         </a>
         ) and I were recently discussing the rise of cavities among heavy kombucha drinkers, and we all came to the same conclusion: kombucha is supposed to be a tonic, not a soda replacement.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kombucha is awesome (read more about
         <a href="http://www.cheeseslave.com/kombucha-health-benefits/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.cheeseslave.com/kombucha-health-benefits/', 'kombucha health benefits');" target="_blank">
          kombucha health benefits
         </a>
         ), and I love making it (heck, I even have a
         <a href="http://stupideasypaleo.com/2013/05/19/homemade-kombucha/" target="_blank">
          tutorial
         </a>
         and an
         <a href="http://stupideasypaleo.com/teach-me-how-to-make-kombucha-description/" target="_blank">
          e-class
         </a>
         for it) but its one of those real foods that can be overdone. Why? It does have sugar even though thats drastically reduced by the fermentation process and it is acidic. While consuming acidic foods isnt fundamentally bad, drinking a lot of kombucha can be just as hard on the enamel of your teeth as soda (which is acidic due to its carbonation). Yes, its still a great source of probiotics, but if youre drinking several cups a day, it might be time to cut back.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Real Food #4 You May Be Overeating: Natural Sweeteners
        </h2>
        <p>
         Artificial sweeteners suck (theyre hyper sweet and offer zero nutrition) but what about natural sweeteners that pop up in real food / Paleo recipes like honey, coconut sugar and maple syrup? Theyre still going to affect blood glucose and cause an insulin response, just like refined sugars. While you may argue that natural sweeteners have nutritional advantages over their refined counterparts and are therefore better choices, one things clear: continuing to eat high amounts of sugary-sweet substances in your diet isnt ideal.
        </p>
        <p>
         Again, if youre battling a sugar addiction, this ones particularly relevant, but even for people who arent, pouring natural sweeteners in your food or drink daily can turn into a problem. Your goals and context will dictate how much you can tolerate  from a physical and psychological perspective but just know that just because a sweetener is labeled natural doesnt mean it should be eaten with abandon. For more on sugar,
         <a href="http://www.whole9life.com/2012/08/the-sugar-manifesto/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.whole9life.com/2012/08/the-sugar-manifesto/', 'check out this article');" target="_blank">
          check out this article
         </a>
         .
        </p>
