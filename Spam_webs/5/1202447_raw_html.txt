             <p>
              ANAHEIM  Its only been a few days but Rene Bourque has adapted quickly to the Southern California lifestyle.
             </p>
             <p>
              After the morning skate in preparation for facing the Calgary Flames Tuesday night, Bourque emerged from the Anaheim Ducks dressing room in the standard SoCal wear  shorts and a t-shirt.
             </p>
             <p>
              I had to go buy them, he said. When I was in Hamilton, all I had was winter clothes.
             </p>
             <p>
              Guess you could say the sun is shining more right now for the former Flames winger who was dealt from the Montreal Canadiens to the Ducks last week, days after being sent to the AHLs Hamilton Bulldogs, along with his US$3.33 million salary cap hit which runs through the 2015-16 season.
             </p>
             <p>
              After all, the Ducks were willing to acquire him in exchange for defenceman Bryan Allen, giving Bourque his wish of a trade after it was more than obvious his time with the Canadiens was through.
             </p>
             <p>
              It was nice to get out of there. It was a tough place to play, Bourque said of his time in Montreal. Im not saying it was just the media, but it never really went right since I got there. Hopefully a change of scenery will help me out.
             </p>
             <p>
              My thought process was I had go down (to the minors, where he netted two goals and four points in four games) and prove to teams and whoever was watching that I can still play. It was nice to get a lot of ice time and a feel for the puck again and just kind of let the puck follow you around for a bit. I played pretty well. Hopefully that helped me get here. I just wanted to go down there and, basically, looked at it as a tryout every night and that I was playing well enough to get another chance.
             </p>
             <p>
              When his game is on, Bourque certainly has the ability to change things for the better. He showed that during much of his time with the Flames and certainly during the Canadiens playoff run in the spring, in which he collected eight goals and 11 points in 17 games.
             </p>
             <p>
              However, the flip side with Bourque is the maddening way he doesnt produce. He netted just nine goals and 18 points in 76 regular-season games last season and this campaign before the Habs sent him to the AHL. He has too many attributes, size, skill, skating ability, his shot, to be that ineffective.
             </p>
             <p>
              I knew I still had a lot left. I proved that last year in playoffs, Bourque said. It was just a matter of getting with the right fit with the right team. I lost my place in Montreal and never really got a chance this year. So, I think this move, obviously I have to prove myself, but I think I can help this team win.
             </p>
             <p>
              Bourque, who scored 27 goals in consecutive seasons for the Flames, should have been the perfect fit for Montreal, a team with plenty of great skaters but needing size on the wings.
             </p>
             <p>
              He believes the Ducks style, and location, work more to his game.
             </p>
             <p>
              I thought I played better against Western Conference teams, he said. For my game, its holding the puck down low, more physical, bigger teams. I get more engaged that way and get to the net. I just fit in better.
             </p>
             <p>
              We have a lot of right handed centreman, too, so that helps. Youre getting the puck more when youre coming out of your own zone. Hopefully that gives me a few more touches and gets me into the game more often, too.
             </p>
             <p>
              OFF THE GLASS
             </p>
             <p>
              Now that St. Louis Blues defenceman  and former Flames blueliner  Jay Bouwmeester has seen his ironman streak end at 737 games, the current active leader for consecutive games played is Ducks forward Andrew Cogliano, who skated in No. 563 Tuesday night versus the Flames.
             </p>
             <p>
              Whats more impressive is Cogliano hasnt missed a single NHL game in his career. He made his debut to kick off the 2007-08 season while with the Edmonton Oilers and played every game since.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thats something Ive taken pride in, Cogliano said. I played in Edmonton during some pretty bad years and to not be scratched, not to be someone the coach doesnt want in the lineup, is something that Ive held pride in, especially in those years, my early years.
             </p>
             <p>
              Its something Ive looked back on and been most proud of simply because its something where my coach has wanted me in the lineup because he thinks I can contribute.
             </p>
             <p>
              By comparison, the longest active streak among current Flames is Mark Giordano at 79 games.
             </p>
             <p>
              Now, to hope talking about his streak doesnt jinx it for Cogliano.
             </p>
             <p>
              I dont think about it too much, to be honest, Cogliano said. Its great but I just want to play. I think any guy in the league wants to play and Ive been pretty fortunate, lucky in a way, and hopefully it continues.
             </p>
             <p>
              The longest run in NHL history belongs to Doug Jarvis (964).
             </p>
             <p>
              IN THE CREASE
             </p>

              For buffs of Flames history, Tuesday marked the 33 rd anniversary of the trade that saw Lanny McDonald dealt to Calgary for Bob MacMillan and Don Lever ...               <p>
               ANAHEIM  Its only been a few days but Rene Bourque has adapted quickly to the Southern California lifestyle.
              </p>
              <p>
               After the morning skate in preparation for facing the Calgary Flames Tuesday night, Bourque emerged from the Anaheim Ducks dressing room in the standard SoCal wear  shorts and a t-shirt.
              </p>
              <p>
               I had to go buy them, he said. When I was in Hamilton, all I had was winter clothes.
              </p>
              <p>
               Guess you could say the sun is shining more right now for the former Flames winger who was dealt from the Montreal Canadiens to the Ducks last week, days after being sent to the AHLs Hamilton Bulldogs, along with his US$3.33 million salary cap hit which runs through the 2015-16 season.
              </p>
              <p>
               After all, the Ducks were willing to acquire him in exchange for defenceman Bryan Allen, giving Bourque his wish of a trade after it was more than obvious his time with the Canadiens was through.
