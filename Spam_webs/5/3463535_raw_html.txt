       <a href="/2013/03/homemade-nutella">
        <img alt="Homemade Raw Nutella" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2874" height="960" src="/wp-content/uploads/nutella01_text1.jpg" width="640"/>

      <p>
       I am a HUGE fan of Nutella, and I have been since I was a child!
       <br/>
       My grandmother always had a jar of thatdelicious nutty-cocoa-butterin thecupboard, and Iremembereating it with crispy crackers and drinking a glass of ice-cold milk along with it.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now I am a grown up, still madly in love with Nutella, but I never give my self the pleasure of buying it  because if I did, I would eat the whole jar within seconds.
       <br/>
       That is why I am thrilled to introduce you to this amazing homemade Nutella recipe!
       <!--more-->
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <img alt="Homemade Raw Nutella" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2881" height="427" src="/wp-content/uploads/nutella031.jpg" width="640"/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       NB:
       <em>
        You have to have a food processor to make this Nutella!
       </em>
      </p>
      <div class="recipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/Recipe">
       <img alt="Recipe: Homemade Nutella" itemprop="photo" src="http://modernwifestyle.com/wp-content/uploads/nutella02.jpg"/>
       <h2 itemprop="name">
        Recipe: Homemade Nutella
       </h2>
       <span class="summary" itemprop="summary">
        <em>
         Recipe adapted from
         <a href="http://ohsheglows.com/" target="_blank">
          Oh She Glows
         </a>
        </em>
       </span>
       <h4>
        Ingredients
       </h4>
       <ul>
        <li itemprop="ingredient" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/RecipeIngredient">
         <span itemprop="amount">
          300 g (2 cups)
         </span>
         <span itemprop="name">
          Hazelnuts
         </span>
        </li>
        <li itemprop="ingredient" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/RecipeIngredient">
         <span itemprop="amount">
          1.5 tbsp
         </span>
         <span itemprop="name">
          coconut oil
         </span>
        </li>
        <li itemprop="ingredient" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/RecipeIngredient">
         <span itemprop="amount">
          2 tsp
         </span>
         <span itemprop="name">
          Vanilla Extract
         </span>
        </li>
        <li itemprop="ingredient" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/RecipeIngredient">
         <span itemprop="amount">
          1.5 dl
         </span>
         <span itemprop="name">
          Coconut Palm Sugar (you can get it in health stores or online)
         </span>
        </li>
        <li itemprop="ingredient" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/RecipeIngredient">
         <span itemprop="amount">
          2 tbsp
         </span>
         <span itemprop="name">
          Cocoa
         </span>
        </li>
        <li itemprop="ingredient" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/RecipeIngredient">
         <span itemprop="amount">
          1 tsp
         </span>
         <span itemprop="name">
          Unsweetened Espresso Powder (I use this one, bought it at my local supermarket)
         </span>
        </li>
        <li itemprop="ingredient" itemscope="" itemtype="http://data-vocabulary.org/RecipeIngredient">
         <span itemprop="amount">
          1/4 tsp
         </span>
         <span itemprop="name">
          Salt
         </span>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h4>
        Directions
       </h4>
       <div itemprop="instructions">
        <ol>
         <li>
          Preheat the oven to 150C (300F)
         </li>
         <li>
          Roast the hazelnuts for about 15 minutes or until they have darkened and some shells have fallen off
         </li>
         <li>
          Place nuts into the centre of an old damp tea towel and wrap it up  and rub the nuts to loosen the skins
         </li>
         <li>
          Pick out the naked nuts and place into processor. Repeat towel rubbing for the rest. Its ok if all of the skins dont come off. Add the remaining nuts to the food processor
         </li>
         <li>
          Process the nutz until creamy. This takes about 12 minutes, stop every other minute to scrape down the sides
         </li>
         <li>
          Add the coconut oil and keep processing
         </li>
         <li>
          Slowly add the remaining ingredients while processing
         </li>
         <li>
          Process until silky smooth and shiny. All in all my processor was on for approximately 18-20 minutes
         </li>
         <li>
          Store it in an airtight container in the fridge
         </li>
        </ol>
       </div>
      </div>
