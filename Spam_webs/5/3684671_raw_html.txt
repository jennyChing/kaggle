       <h1>
        Differences between HD-SDI Security Camera and Analog Security Camera
       </h1>
       <div class="metainfo">
        Posted by
        <span class="author-name">
         <a href="https://www.securitycamerainstalls.com/author/sadmin/" rel="author" title="Posts by SecurityCameraInstalls">
          SecurityCameraInstalls
         </a>
        </span>
        on

    Tuesday, November 4th, 2014
    in
        <a href="https://www.securitycamerainstalls.com/category/security-cameras/" rel="category tag">
         Security Cameras
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="post_text">
        <p>
         A security camera is one of the best investments you can make to secure your property. A camera works in two ways, it works as a deterrent and can also help to track down those who are responsible for any crimes. When choosing the types of security camera to install, you may be confused by all the options. The two most popular types are HD-SDI and Analog security cameras. This guide will look at the differences between each of these to help decide which type is most suitable.
        </p>
        <h3>
         What is a HD-SDI Camera?
        </h3>
        <p>
         A HD-SDI camera is also known as a High Definition
         <a href="https://www.securitycamerainstalls.com/about-us/" title="security cameras">
          Security Camera
         </a>
         . If you have a high definition television then you should be aware that the picture displayed is much crisper and clearer. In exactly the same way a HD-SDI camera can capture a much higher resolution picture when it is recording. A high definition video is over double the size of the standard definition video recorded from an analog camera.
        </p>
        <h3>
         What is an Analog Camera?
        </h3>
        <p>
         An analog camera is a conventional type of security camera. These types of cameras were very common before high definition became all the rage. These were adequate at providing an image but worked mainly as a deterrent because people were aware they were being filmed by security cameras. Trying to make a positive identification from footage from an analog camera is extremely difficult.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Differences between HD-SDI and Analog Security Cameras
        </h3>
        <p>
          Video Size
         <br/>
          Video Quality
         <br/>
          Cost
         <br/>
          More Likely to lead to a conviction
        </p>
        <h4>
         Video Size
        </h4>
        <p>
         The size of a video recorded with a high definition security camera will be much larger than a traditional s. This is because there is much more detail which needs to be recorded and stored to the tape or hard drive. This will mean if you are upgrading your cameras to HD-SDI cameras, then you may also need to look at upgrading your recording hardware so that you can fit a whole nights worth of recordings on.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Video Quality
        </h4>
        <p>
         The main reason to choose to install a
         <a href="https://www.securitycamerainstalls.com/hd-sdi-security-cameras/" title="HD-SDI Bullets">
          HD-SDI camera
         </a>
         is because the quality is much better. These cameras can record in 1080p high definition, the same as a high definition television set. The increased quality will make it much easier to identify people and objects in the film which will make the footage much more useful.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Cost
        </h4>
        <p>
         Normally a HD-SDI camera will be more expensive to buy than a conventional standard definition analog camera. However, the fitting costs will be typically the same. A High definition camera will provide much more useful information and allow you to actually see and identify people on the television.
        </p>
        <h4>
         More likely to lead to a conviction
        </h4>
        <p>
         The footage recorded from a low resolution analog camera is of such poor quality that it can be difficult to identify who is in a shot. This means that it only has limited benefits and sometimes the footage might be of such a poor quality that it cant be used in court cases. A HD camera has a much larger and clearer image which should mean that its more likely the viewer is able to correctly identify the assailant.
        </p>
        <p>
         The only types of cameras you should really be installing are HD-SDI cameras. These are fully future proof and provide a clear image which makes identification easy.
        </p>
