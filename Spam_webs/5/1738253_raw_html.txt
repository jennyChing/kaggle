     </h2>

          <div class="post_inner_wrapper">
           <p dir="ltr">
            <span style="font-size: 14px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             Apartment hunting can be a daunting task. Youve seen so many disappointments that youre beginning to feel defeated. Then, it happens: you find the perfect apartment in an ideal location near all of your favorites shops and cafes. The catch? It has such an unusual layout that much of the living area appears to be wasted space.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p dir="ltr">
            <span style="font-size: 14px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             Those of us living in San Francisco can attest to the fact that the awkwardly laid out apartment is a fact of life. Whether its a bathroom separated into two individual (sink/shower and toilet) rooms, a 2nd-floor door that leads to nowhere, or a hallway thats got more square footage than both bedrooms combined, weve seen it all. Dont let these flaws run you off  embrace them! Those funny little niches have tons of character, and with a little creativity these areas may end up being your favorite space in the apartment.
            </span>
           </p>
           <h4 dir="ltr">
            Space To Curl Up
           </h4>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1892" style="width: 591px">
            <a href="http://hotpads.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/nook-remodelista.png">
             <img alt="Photo Credit: remodelista.com " class="size-full wp-image-1892" height="786" src="http://d6tfo4imeoxg8.cloudfront.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/nook-remodelista.png" width="581"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo Credit: remodelista.com
            </p>
           </div>
           <p dir="ltr">
            The above space became a reading nook with a plush lounge cushion, pillows and a reading lamp. The window just happens to be placed perfectly in this space of retreat. Here you can work on your laptop, read a book or just enjoy the view.
           </p>
           <h4 dir="ltr">
            Space for a Desk
           </h4>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1893" style="width: 622px">
            <a href="http://hotpads.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Nook-sashalanka.png">
             <img alt="Photo Credit: sashalanka.com " class="size-full wp-image-1893" height="770" src="http://d6tfo4imeoxg8.cloudfront.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Nook-sashalanka.png" width="612"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo Credit: sashalanka.com
            </p>
           </div>
           <p dir="ltr">
            These funky alcove areas are the perfect space for a desk, vanity or wet bar. You would be amazed what you can fit in a 3 foot wide space. If you choose to use this area as a desk  attach a reading lamp to the wall and add shelves for books.
           </p>
           <h4 dir="ltr">
            Space for the Kids
           </h4>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1889" style="width: 563px">
            <a href="http://hotpads.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/nook-designvisionsofaustin.png">
             <img alt="Photo Credit: designvisionsofaustin.com" class="size-full wp-image-1889" height="749" src="http://d6tfo4imeoxg8.cloudfront.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/nook-designvisionsofaustin.png" width="553"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo Credit: designvisionsofaustin.com
            </p>
           </div>
           <p dir="ltr">
            The apartment only has 2 bedrooms, where to put the kids? Bunk beds tucked away in an area no bigger than a closet is a cozy and secluded rest area. This leaves the floor open for play time. Drape the front of the beds from floor to ceiling for an even cozier feel.
           </p>
           <h4 dir="ltr">
            Space for a Guest
           </h4>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1891" style="width: 589px">
            <a href="http://hotpads.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/nook-kristemichelini.png">
             <img alt="Photo Credit: kristemichelini.com" class="size-full wp-image-1891" height="771" src="http://d6tfo4imeoxg8.cloudfront.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/nook-kristemichelini.png" width="579"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo Credit: kristemichelini.com
            </p>
           </div>
           <p dir="ltr">
            If you came upon this apartment, would you think to yourself  What would I ever do with this space? This alove area just needs a mattress, pillows and some bedding. Voila  space for an overnight guest!
           </p>
           <h4 dir="ltr">
            Space for Books
           </h4>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1890" style="width: 560px">
            <a href="http://hotpads.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/nook-housetohome-uk.jpg">
             <img alt="Photo Credit: housetohome.co.uk" class="size-full wp-image-1890" height="550" src="http://d6tfo4imeoxg8.cloudfront.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/nook-housetohome-uk.jpg" width="550"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo Credit: housetohome.co.uk
            </p>
           </div>
           <p dir="ltr">
            <span style="font-size: 14px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             These shallow alcoves are great storage for books or a place to display family photos. Use floating ledge shelves and install them in a staggered pattern to allow clearance of taller items.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p dir="ltr">
            Visit
            <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/search/pins/?q=nooks%20alcoves">
             Pinterest
            </a>
            for more ideas on decorating nooks, alcoves and niches.
           </p>
