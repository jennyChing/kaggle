        <h2>
         Why More Women Are Forgoing the Hospital When Baby Comes
        </h2>
        <p>
         Less than 1 percent of babies are born at home, but that number has seen a sharp increase in recent years. Lets explore the home birthing trend, the risks and benefits and those involved in helping bring life into the world outside a hospital setting.
        </p>
        <h2>
         State of Birth
        </h2>
        <p>
         Hospitals are the preferred place of birth for most parents-to-be, but it wasnt always so.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Home births by year
         </em>
         <br/>
         1900	95%*
         <br/>
         1940	44%
         <br/>
         1969	1%
         <br/>
         2009	0.72%
         <br/>
         * Nearly all births took place outside hospitals, so this is an estimate
        </p>
        <p>
         While home births have dropped overall, theyve risen in recent years, driven by a natural-birthing trend among white women. Rates have risen by nearly 30% in a short time.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Home births by year
         </em>
         <br/>

        <h2>
         Not Going It Alone
        </h2>
        <p>
         Most women have help during the birthing process, even if they give birth outside a traditional medical setting.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Certified nurse midwife
         </em>
         <br/>
         A person who has completed traditional nursing school and then gone on to complete midwifery training.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Certified professional midwife
         </em>
         <br/>
         A person with training in midwifery who is not a nurse.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Doula
         </em>
         <br/>
         Someone trained to assist emotionally during childbirth but not medically trained.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Home births by type of attendant
         </em>
         <br/>
         Physician	4.8%
         <br/>
         Certified nurse midwife	19.5%
         <br/>
         Other midwife	42.9
         <br/>
         Other attendant	32.9%
        </p>
