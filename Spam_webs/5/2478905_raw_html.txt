             <h1 itemprop="headline">
              Peyton Manning implies that Colorado pot laws are good for pizza business
             </h1>
             <div class="article-info">
              <div>
               <time datetime="2014-09-19T14:57-04:00" itemprop="datePublished" pubdate="">
                Published September 19, 2014
               </time>
               <div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">

             <div itemprop="articleBody">
              <div class="m">
               <img alt="manning_peyton.jpg" itemprop="image" src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/U.S./876/493/manning_peyton.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1"/>
               <div class="caption">
                <p itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">
                 Peyton Manning implies that Colorados law that legalize marijuana has been good for pizza business.
                 <span class="source" itemprop="name">
                  (AP)
                 </span>
                </p>
               </div>
              </div>
              <p>
               Peyton Manning thinks he knows why the pizza business in Colorado is booming.
              </p>
              <p>
               The Denver Broncos quarterback, who owns more than a dozen Papa Johns Pizza franchises in Colorado, told
               <a href="http://mmqb.si.com/2014/09/18/peyton-manning-denver-broncos-unplugged/" target="blank">
                Sports Illustrateds Peter King
               </a>
               that "recent law changes" have been good for the pie business.
              </p>
              <p>
               Pizza business is pretty good out here, believe it or not, due to some recent law changes, said Manning. So when you come to a different place, youve kind of got to learn everything that comes with it.
              </p>
              <p>
               While he didn't refer directly to Colorado Amendment 64 that legalizes recreational marijuana, Manning did purchase 21 Papa John's locations in Colorado shortly before voters passed the lawin 2012.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <a href="http://www.tmz.com/2013/10/16/peyton-manning-papa-johns-franchises/" target="blank">
                 TMZ reported last year
                </a>
               </strong>
               that some of his restaurants saw sales jump from $3,000-$4,000 a day to $5,000-$6,000 a day. One chain saw a 25 percent increase in sales from the previous year.
              </p>
              <p>
               Colorado and Washington are the only two states in the U.S. to legalized recreational marijuana.
              </p>
