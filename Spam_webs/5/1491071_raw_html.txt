              <div class="shift-to-hero">
               <p>
                <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/XEVlyP4_11M?enablejsapi=1&amp;" width="640">
                </iframe>
               </p>

              <p>
               In an interview with
               <em>
                Last Week Tonight
               </em>
               host John Oliver, whistleblower Edward Snowden shared some interesting revelations about the U.S. government's capabilities to intercept people's personal data  including dick pics.
              </p>
              <p>
               Oliver, who flew to Moscow to conduct the interview with Snowden, shows a segment with people worried about the government accessing their nude photos. Snowden denies there's a government program aimed precisely for that purpose, but that doesn't mean they can't see your private pics.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/12/29/snowden-nsa-problems-encryption/">
                 Snowden leaks reveal encryption programs that NSA couldn't break
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               "Well, the good news is there's no program named, 'the dick pic program'. The bad news is, they are still collecting everybody's information, including your dick pics,"  Snowden says.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                The Edward Snowden / John Oliver "dick pic" interview segment was epic (and should probably be shown in tech marketing classes)
               </p>
               <p>
                 Bret Taylor (@btaylor)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/btaylor/status/584972773803425792" target="_blank">
                 April 6, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                Edward Snowden explaining NSA capabilities to John Oliver in terms of dick pics is everything.
               </p>
               <p>
                 Chris Burlingame (@chrisburlingame)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/chrisburlingame/status/584962576359895040" target="_blank">
                 April 6, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               What follows is a hilarious and very detailed explanation of how the various U.S. surveillance programs work,  in terms of photos of someone's penis that is.
              </p>
              <p>
               In an oddly related and a particularly hilarious part of the interview, Oliver educates Snowden on truck nuts. If you, like Snowden, don't know what they are, see the photo below.
              </p>
              <figure class="image">
               <p>
                <img alt="truck nuts" class="" data-caption='John Oliver educates Edward Snowden on the "truck nuts" phenomenon. ' data-credit-link="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XEVlyP4_11M" data-credit-name="Last Week Tonight" data-credit-provider="Youtube" data-fragment="m!6592" data-image="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzA0LzA2LzU2L3RydWNrbnV0cy4yNmVlMi5qcGcKcAl0aHVtYgkxMjAweDk2MDA-/b950ccf5/2fb/truck-nuts.jpg" data-micro="1" data-width="960" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE1LzA0LzA2LzU2L3RydWNrbnV0cy4yNmVlMi5qcGcKcAl0aHVtYgkxMjAweDk2MDA-/b950ccf5/2fb/truck-nuts.jpg" title="truck nuts"/>
               </p>
               <figcaption class="image-caption">
                <p>
                 John Oliver educates Edward Snowden on the "truck nuts" phenomenon.

              </figure>
              <p>
               Not all of the interview is conducted in such a lighthearted tone. Oliver asks Snowden several hard questions, grilling him about not reading all of the NSA documents he leaked before handing them over to journalists.
              </p>
              <p>
               Responding to a question about a poorly redacted piece of the leaked information posted in
               <em>
                The New York Times
               </em>
               , Snowden recognizes it as a says this is something that happens in reporting.
              </p>
              <p>
               "In journalism, we have to accept that some mistakes will be made. This is a fundamental concept of liberty," Snowden says.
              </p>
              <p>
               "You're giving documents with information you know could be harmful, which could get out there," Oliver replies.
              </p>
              <p>
               "You will never be completely free from risk if you're free," Snowden says.
              </p>
              <p>
               Snowden also touches on his defection to Russia, where he has lived since June 2013.
              </p>
              <p>
               "I do miss my country. I do miss my home. I do miss my family," he says.
              </p>
