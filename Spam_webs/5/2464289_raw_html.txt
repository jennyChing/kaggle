           <firstimage>
            <img alt="firefox sync bookmarks" class="align-right" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/00-Firefox-Sync-Logo.jpg?9e15ff"/>
            I admit it  Im not a loyal
            <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tags/firefox/">
             Firefox
            </a>
            user. I stopped using it long ago since it constantly hogged and slowed down my system. But thats about to change. After a year since the last major version was released, Mozilla has launched Firefox 4 with the promise of a lighter and speedier browsing experience.
           </firstimage>
          </p>
          <p>
           In order to win back the hearts of its users, the latest version of the Fox offers many improvements and new features. One of the new features is Firefox Sync whichwill help users to synchronize their bookmarks across computers. While the feature might not be useful for users with one computer, those who work with multiple computers will really appreciate this ability.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Setting Up The Sync Environment
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            To utilize the sync feature, make sure that all of your computers have Firefox 4 installed. If your Firefox is still the older version, you need to
            <a href="http://www.mozilla.com/en-US/firefox/new">
             download the latest version
            </a>
            first.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="firefox sync bookmarks" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="150" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/00b-Firefox-4-Download.jpg?9e15ff" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            After Firefox 4 is installed, open it up and go to the 
            <em>
             Set Up Sync
            </em>
             menu. In the Mac version, this menu is located under 
            <em>
             Tools
            </em>
            ; while in the Windows version, its under the 
            <em>
             Firefox
            </em>
             menu.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="bookmark syncing firefox" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="176" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/01a-Set-Up-Sync.jpg?9e15ff" width="298"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            You can choose one of the computers to start setting up your Firefox Sync account and add the other computers to the account later.  Click the 
            <em>
             Create a New Account
            </em>
             button in the 
            <em>
             Firefox Sync Setup
            </em>
             window.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="bookmark syncing firefox" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="250" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/01b-Firefox-Sync-Setup.jpg?9e15ff" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Continue by filling in your email address and chosen password in the Account Details.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="bookmark syncing firefox" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="280" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/01c-Firefox-Sync-Setup-Details.jpg?9e15ff" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            You will get the Sync Key. You will use the key to set up and add another computer to the sync account. Due to its importance, its advisable to savethe key and keep it safe.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="01d Firefox Sync Setup  key" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="340" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/01d-Firefox-Sync-Setup-key.jpg?9e15ff" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <div>
           <div class="ad-unit-post" id="adsense2">
            <small>
             <a href="https://support.google.com/adsense/troubleshooter/1631343?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.makeuseof.com%2Ftag%2Fbookmarks-synced-computers-firefox-4%2F&amp;client=ca-pub-9894449314507324" style="color:LightGray">
              Ads by Google
             </a>
            </small>
            <script type="text/javascript">

          <ul>
           <li>
            The key will be saved as an HTML file. If you have a Dropbox account and have the application installed on the other computers that you use, its a good idea to save the Firefox Sync Keyfile in the Dropbox folder so you can access it easily.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="01e Save Sync Key" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="203" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/01e-Save-Sync-Key.jpg?9e15ff" width="424"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Now the set up process is complete. Click the 
            <em>
             Done
            </em>
             button to wrap up.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="01g Firefox Sync Setup  Done" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="463" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/01g-Firefox-Sync-Setup-Done.jpg?9e15ff" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Adding Other Firefoxes To The System
          </h2>
          <p>
           The next step is to add other Firefoxes in other computers to your account.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Go to the 
            <em>
             Set Up Sync
            </em>
             menu on the other Firefox and start the setup process again.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="firefox sync" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="263" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/02a_Sync_in_Windows.jpg?9e15ff" width="450"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            But now click the 
            <em>
             Connect
            </em>
             button instead of 
            <em>
             Create A New Account
            </em>
            .
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="firefox sync" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="250" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/02b_Connect.jpg?9e15ff" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            You will see 
            <em>
             Add a Device
            </em>
             window and you will have three sets of codes. Leave this window open for a while and go back to the first computer where you set up the Firefox Sync account.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="firefox sync" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="461" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/02b2_Add_A_Device.jpg?9e15ff" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            In the first computer, open 
            <em>
             Preferences
            </em>
             and choose the 
            <em>
             Sync
            </em>
             tab. Click the 
            <em>
             Add a Device
            </em>
             link and write down the three codes that you got in the previous step.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="firefox sync bookmarks" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="300" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/03a-Sync-Pref-Add-Device1.jpg?9e15ff" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Its possible that you may not have the first computer with you when you add another computer to your sync account. In this case, click the 
            <em>
             I dont have the device with me
            </em>
             link under the three sets of codes.  This other option requires you to sign in with your Sync account. Type in your email address and password. Open the saved Firefox Sync Key HTMLfile and type in the Sync Key.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <img alt="firefox sync bookmarks" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="461" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/03b_Sign_In.jpg?9e15ff" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            And thats it. Youve just added another computer to your sync account. Repeat the process for other computers that you use and you are ready to sync your bookmarks across computers.
           </li>
          </ul>

           <a href="https://market.android.com/details?id=org.mozilla.firefox">
            installing Firefox

           <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/firefox-home/id380366933?mt=8">
            Firefox Home
           </a>
