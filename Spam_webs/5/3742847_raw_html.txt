       <h1>
        The Warm-Up You Should Be Doing (But Probably Aren't)
       </h1>
       <p class="lead">
        Prep your body for its best workout yet with these multi-planar moves from master trainer Josh Stolz.
       </p>
       <p class="byline">
        Monday, June 9, 2014 |
        <a class="by" href="/articles?f=author!Lindsey%2BEmery" title="Lindsey Emery">

       </p>

       <p>
        If youve been sitting at a desk all day (or lying in bed all night), the last thing you want to do is jump right into a challenging cardio or strength routine. By easing your way into every workout and striving for more efficient movement in all of your joints before you begin, youll be able to perform at a higher level and reduce your risk of injury, no matter what youre doing, says New York City-based master trainer and Tier 4 coach
        <a href="http://q.equinox.com/articles/2013/09/plank-workout" target="_blank">
         Josh Stolz
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        <br/>
        This series of dynamic exercises preps your body to move in various planes of motion, helps correct any imbalances or tightness you might have, and safely increases both your mobility and stability pre-workout. First youll focus on muscle activation, then mobility, then stability/proprioception and last, but not least, neural activation. Start off slowly, practicing good form, and gradually pick up the pace as you go along, says Stolz.
       </p>
       <p>
        Perform this warm-up as a circuit, moving from one exercise to the next without rest, right before your regular strength and/or cardio workouts.
