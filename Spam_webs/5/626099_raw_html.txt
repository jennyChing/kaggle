         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Book releases that have caught my eye  March 2015
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-02-18T18:43:32+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           February 18, 2015
          </time>
          by
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/author/admin" itemprop="url" rel="author">
            <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
             Jo
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="entry-comments-link">
           <a href="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/2015/02/book-releases-caught-eye-march-2015.html#comments">
            4 Comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="7829344" data-link="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/2015/02/book-releases-caught-eye-march-2015.html" data-summary="" data-title="Book releases that have caught my eye - March 2015">
         </div>
         <p>
          Here are just a fewof the titles being released in March2015, or very late February, that have caught my eye.
         </p>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="wp-image-17785 size-grid-featured alignright" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/the-buried-giant-199x300.jpg" width="199"/>
           An extraordinary new novel from the author of
           <em>
            Never Let Me Go
           </em>
           and the Booker Prize winning
           <em>
            The Remains of the Day
           </em>
           .
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Youve long set your heart against it, Axl, I know. But its time now to think on it anew. Theres a journey we must go on, and no more delay
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <em>
            The Buried Giant
           </em>
           begins as a couple set off across a troubled land of mist and rain in the hope of finding a son they have not seen in years.Sometimes savage, often intensely moving, Kazuo Ishiguros first novel in a decade is about lost memories, love, revenge and war.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Buried-Giant-Kazuo-Ishiguro/9780571315031">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/the-buried-giant-kazuo-ishiguro/prod9780571315048.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Buried-Giant-novel-Kazuo-Ishiguro/dp/030727103X/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1424244266&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=buried+giant+ishiguro">
            Amazon
           </a>
           |
           <a href="https://store.kobobooks.com/en-US/ebook/the-buried-giant-2">
            Kobobooks
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/the-buried-giant-kazuo-ishiguro/1119702748?ean=9780307271037">
            B&amp;N
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <p>
          <span id="more-17782">
          </span>
         </p>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-17788 size-grid-featured" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/the-world-before-us-199x300.jpg" width="199"/>
           In the tradition of A. S. Byatts
           <em>
            Possession
           </em>
           , a hauntingly poignant novel about madness, loss, and the ties that bind our past to our present
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Deep in the woods of northern England, somewhere between a dilapidated estate and an abandoned Victorian asylum, fifteen-year-old Jane Standen lived through a nightmare. She was babysitting a sweet young girl named Lily, and in one fleeting moment, lost her. The little girl was never found, leaving her family and Jane devastated.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Twenty years later, Jane is an archivist at a small London museum that is about to close for lack of funding. As a final research projectan endeavor inspired in part by her painful pastJane surveys the archives for information related to another missing person: a woman who disappeared over one hundred years ago in the same woods where Lily was lost. As Jane pieces moments in history together, a portrait of a fascinating group of people starts to unfurl. Inexplicably tied to the mysterious disappearance of long ago, Jane finds tender details of their lives at the country estate and in the asylum that are linked to her own heartbroken world, and their story from all those years ago may now help Jane find a way to move on.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           In riveting, beautiful prose,
           <em>
            The World Before Us
           </em>
           explores the powerful notion that history is a closely connected part of uskept alive by the resonance of our daily choicesreminding us of the possibility that we are less alone than we might think.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/World-Before-Us-Aislinn-Hunter/9780553418521">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/the-world-before-us-aislinn-hunter/prod9780553418521.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="https://store.kobobooks.com/en-US/ebook/the-world-before-us-2">
            Kobobooks
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/World-Before-Us-Novel/dp/0553418521/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1424244393&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=the+world+before+us+hunter">
            Amazon
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/the-world-before-us-aislinn-hunter/1116816949?ean=9780553418521">
            B&amp;N
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-17783 size-grid-featured" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/the-fishermen-192x300.jpg" width="192"/>
           In this dazzling debut novel, four young brothers in a small Nigerian town encounter a madman, whose prophecy of violence threatens the core of their family Told from the point of view of nine-year-old Benjamin, the youngest of four brothers, The Fishermen is the Cain and Abel-esque story of an unforgettable childhood in 1990s Nigeria. When their father has to travel to a distant city for work, the brothers take advantage of his extended absence to skip school and go fishing. At the forbidden nearby river they encounter a madman, who predicts that one of the brothers will kill another. What happens next is an almost mythic event whose impact  both tragic and redemptive  will transcend the lives and imaginations of both its characters and its readers.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Chigozie Obioma emerges as one of the best new voices of modern African literature, echoing its older generations masterful storytelling with a contemporary fearlessness and purpose.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Fishermen-Chigozie-Obioma/9780957548855">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/the-fishermen-chigozie-obioma/prod9780957548855.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="https://store.kobobooks.com/en-US/ebook/the-fishermen-3">
            Kobobooks
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Fishermen-Novel-Chigozie-Obioma/dp/0316338370/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1424244527&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=fishermen+obioma">
            Amazon
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/the-fishermen-chigozie-obioma/1120058399?ean=9780316338370">
            B&amp;N
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <a href="http://www.tkqlhce.com/8k101ft1zt0GONINJMIGIIMPIKJN" target="_blank">
           <img alt="TheReadingRoom" border="0" src="http://www.lduhtrp.net/mf98qmqeki3BA5A6953559C576A"/>
          </a>
         </center>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-17793 size-grid-featured" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/the-boy-who-could-see-death-194x300.jpg" width="194"/>
           From Salley Vickers, bestselling author of
           <em>
            Miss Garnets Angel
           </em>
           , comes
           <em>
            The Boy Who Could See Death
           </em>
           , an enchanting and unsettling collection of short stories.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Eli is an ordinary boy with an extraordinary gift. It will shape the course of his whole life but, he learns the hard way, he must keep it hidden from those who know him best. Seeing death is a mixed blessing. Eli is not the only one defying the worlds expectations of him. Cousin Francesca, a charming spinster and a favourite with the children, is harbouring kleptomaniac tendencies. Sarah Palliser, living alone next to a ramshackle graveyard, is more scared of the small box under her stairs than the ghosts outside her window. Meanwhile dreamy artist Nan is nursing a growing obsession with wolves in Britain and the recently widowed Frances finds herself inventing an exotic imaginary boyfriend to pass the time. Push through an unassuming front door on an unremarkable street or peer into the glowing fluorescent windows of an urban office block and within youll find strange and unforgettable scenes, normal people caught in situations they do not quite comprehend
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Salley Vickers is a master of the uncanny and the unexpected. In this collection of eleven remarkable stories, she explores bereavement and betrayal, closely guarded secrets and common gossip, long-overdue endings and decidedly strange beginnings. Each story is perfectly formed: a snapshot of a total stranger, a fleeting glimpse of lives spiced with a little something extra.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Boy-Who-Could-See-Death-Salley-Vickers/9780241187692">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/the-boy-who-could-see-death-salley-vickers/prod9780241187692.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="https://store.kobobooks.com/en-US/ebook/the-boy-who-could-see-death">
            Kobobooks
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="alignright wp-image-17792 size-grid-featured" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/the-poser-199x300.jpg" width="199"/>
           A hilarious and dazzling debut novel about a master impressionist at risk of losing his true self.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           All his life, Giovanni Bernini has possessed an uncanny gift: he can imitate anyone he meets. Honed by his mother at a young age, the talent catapults him from small-town obscurity to stardom.As Giovanni describes it, No ones disguise is perfect. There is in every person, no matter how graceful, a seam, a thread curling out of them. . . . When pulled by the right hands, it will unravel the person entire. As his fame grows, Giovanni encounters a beautiful and enigmatic stage singer, Lucy Starlight the only person whose thread he cannot find and becomes increasingly trapped inside his many poses. Ultimately, he must assume the one identity he has never been able to master: his own.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           In the vein of Jonathan Lethems and Kevin Wilsons playful surrealism, Jacob Rubins
           <em>
            The Poser
           </em>
           is the debut of a major literary voice, a masterfully written, deeply original comic novel, and the moving story of a man who must risk everything for the chance to save his life and know true love.
          </h5>
          <h4 style="text-align: left;">
           Available from:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.bookdepository.com/Poser-Jacob-Rubin/9780670016761">
            Book Depository
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.booktopia.com.au/the-poser-jacob-rubin/prod9780670016761.html">
            Booktopia(Aus)
           </a>
           |
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Poser-Novel-Jacob-Rubin/dp/0670016764/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1424244797&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=poser+jacob+rubin">
            Amazon
           </a>
           |
           <a href="https://store.kobobooks.com/en-US/ebook/the-poser-1">
            Kobobooks
           </a>
          </h4>
         </div>
         <div style="padding: 25px 20px 10px 20px; text-align: center; background-color: #f2f2f2; margin: 0px 0px 25px 0px;">
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-17791 size-grid-featured" height="300" src="http://bookloverbookreviews.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/act-of-god-186x300.jpg" width="186"/>
           Its the summer of 2015, Brooklyn. The city is sweltering from another record-breaking heat wave, this one accompanied by biblical rains. Edith, a recently retired legal librarian, and her identical twin sister, Kat, a feckless romantic whos mistaken her own eccentricity for originality, discover something ominous in their hall closet: it seems to be phosphorescent, its a mushroom . . . and its sprouting from their wall.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Upstairs, their landlady, Vida Cebu, a Shakespearian actress far more famous for her TV commercials for Ziberax (the first female sexual enhancement pill) than for her stage work, discovers that a petite Russian girl, a runaway au pair, has been secretly living in her guest room closet. When the police arrest the intruder, they find a second mushroom, also glowing, under the intruders bedding. Soon the HAZMAT squad arrives, and the four women are forced to evacuate the contaminated row house with only the clothes on their backs.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           As the mold infestation spreads from row house to high-rise, and frightened, bewildered New Yorkers wait out this plague (is it an act of God?) on their city and property, the four women become caught up in a centrifugal nightmare.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Part horror story, part screwball comedy, Jill Ciments brilliant suspense novel looks at what happens when our lives-so seemingly set and ordered yet so precariously balanced-break down in the wake of calamity. It is, as well, a novel about love (familial and profound) and how it can appear from the most unlikely circumstances.
          </h5>

           Meet the Alter sisters: Lady, Vee and Delph. These three mordantly witty, complex women share their familys apartment on Manhattans Upper West Side. They love each other fiercely, but being an Alter isnt easy. Bad luck is in their genes, passed down through the generations. Yet no matter what life throws at these siblings, they always have a wisecrack  and each other.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           In the waning days of 1999, the sisters decide its time to close the circle of the Alter curse. But first, as the world counts down to the dawn of a new millennium, Lady, Vee and Delph must write the final chapter of a saga generations in the making  one that is inexorably intertwined with that of the twentieth century itself. Unspooling threads of history, personal memory and family lore, they weave a mesmerising account of their lives that stretches back decades to their great-grandfather, a brilliant scientist whose professional triumph became the sinister legacy that defines them.
          </h5>
          <h5 style="text-align: justify;">
           Magnificent and heartbreaking, A REUNION OF GHOSTS is an epic novel about three unforgettable women, bound to each other and their remarkable family through the blessings and the burdens bestowed by blood.
