     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Trinity Blood
       </b>
      </i>
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">
        Japanese
       </a>
       :
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">
        
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">
        Hepburn
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Toriniti Buraddo
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      is a series of Japanese
      <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">
       light novels
      </a>
      written by
      <a href="/wiki/Sunao_Yoshida" title="Sunao Yoshida">
       Sunao Yoshida
      </a>
      with illustrations by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thores_Shibamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thores Shibamoto (page does not exist)">
       Thores Shibamoto
      </a>
      and originally serialized in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Sneaker" title="The Sneaker">
        The Sneaker
       </a>
      </i>
      . Set 900 years after an apocalyptic war between
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humans" title="Humans">
       humans
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vampires" title="Vampires">
       vampires
      </a>
      , the series focuses on the ongoing cold war between the
      <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">
       Vatican
      </a>
      , the human government, and the "New Human Empire", the government of the vampiric
      <a href="/wiki/Methuselah" title="Methuselah">
       Methuselah
      </a>
      . Fighting on the Vatican's side is
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abel_Nightroad" title="Abel Nightroad">
       Abel Nightroad
      </a>
      , a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crusnik" title="Crusnik">
       Crusnik
      </a>
      - a vampire that feeds on vampires. The novels blend science fiction, fantasy, and political intrigue, with some in the Vatican and the Empire striving for peace, and the Rosen Kreuz Order doing anything in its power to stop it.
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Trinity Blood
      </i>
      has been adapted into both
      <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">
       manga
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">
       anime
      </a>
      series, with continuity differences between the three versions. After Yoshida's death on July 15, 2004,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kentaro_Yasui&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kentaro Yasui (page does not exist)">
       Kentaro Yasui
      </a>
      , his friend and the author of
      <a href="/wiki/Sword_of_the_Dark_Ones" title="Sword of the Dark Ones">
       Ragnarok
      </a>
      novels, stepped in to complete the remaining novels. Yasui also aided in completing the manga, with illustrations by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kiyo_Kuj%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kiyo Kuj (page does not exist)">
       Kiyo Kuj
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SilverBulletComics_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-SilverBulletComics-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The anime series, spanning 24 episodes, was produced by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gonzo_(studio)" title="Gonzo (studio)">
       Gonzo
      </a>
      and originally aired on
      <a href="/wiki/WOWOW" title="WOWOW">
       WOWOW
      </a>
      network.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trinity_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      When
      <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
       Earth
      </a>
      's population drastically increased, humanity, led by the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">
       United Nations
      </a>
      , attempted to colonize
      <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">
       Mars
      </a>
      . During the
      <a href="/wiki/Colonization" title="Colonization">
       colonization
      </a>
      , they discovered two alien technologies: the
      <a href="/wiki/Bacillus" title="Bacillus">
       Bacillus
      </a>
      virus and the Crusnik nanomachines. The colonists injected the Bacillus in their bodies which transformed them into "Methuselahs". They installed the Crusnik in the bodies of four test tube babies: Seth, Cain, Abel and Lilith whose enhanced bodies were the only ones able to survive the procedure.
     </p>
     <p>
      Continuing wars on Earth eventually led to "
      <a href="/wiki/Armageddon" title="Armageddon">
       Armageddon
      </a>
      ", the apocalyptic event that happened 900 years before the start of the story, and the colonists returned to Earth to help with rebuilding. However, when the colonists returned to Earth, a war broke out between the Methuselahs and the humans who had remained on Earth. Abel, Cain, and Seth took the Methuselah's sidewhile Lilith supported the Vatican, which guarded humanity. During the war, Cain went insane and killed Lilith. In grief, Abel took her body to the Vatican where she was buried. Abel remained at her side, weeping for her, for 900 years.
     </p>
     <p>
      At the start of the story, the vampiric Methuselahs, still a major political and military force, continue to wage war on the "Terrans", as they call the human inhabitants of Earth. The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">
       Roman Catholic Church
      </a>
      is a major military power determined to protect humans from the Methuselahs, with its seat of power based in the Vatican. The Methuselahs have their capital in
      <a href="/wiki/Byzantium" title="Byzantium">
       Byzantium
      </a>
      , which is surrounded by a field of particles to filter out
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UV_radiation" title="UV radiation">
       UV radiation
      </a>
      , protecting the Methuselah population.
     </p>
     <p>
      Both groups use "lost technologies", such as
      <a href="/wiki/Airship" title="Airship">
       airships
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Missile" title="Missile">
       missiles
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">
       computers
      </a>
      , to engage in a
      <a href="/wiki/Cold_war_(general_term)" title="Cold war (general term)">
       cold war
      </a>
      with one another. A third great power,
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Trinity_Blood_characters#The_Kingdom_of_Albion" title="List of Trinity Blood characters">
       Albion
      </a>
      also plays a role in the war with its superior arsenal of lost technology and weapons and higher level of manufacturing ability versus the Vatican. The independent
      <a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">
       monarchy
      </a>
      of Albion is a primarily human country, however the secret of their expertise in lost technology is found in the
      <a href="/wiki/Ghetto" title="Ghetto">
       Ghetto
      </a>
      , an underground city of enslaved Methuselah. It is these Methuselah who operate and manufacture the lost technology, but with the death of the Albion Queen, some of the enslaved vampires begin a rebellion for the freedom of all of the Ghetto residents.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_among_adaptations">
       Differences among adaptations
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trinity_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Differences among adaptations">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      While the novels, manga, and anime series all cover the same basic story and feature many of the same characters, they do have some minor and major differences. Each has its own unique variation on the major story arcs, and in general the novels give the most detail concerning the political elements and background information on the stories. There are a few minor name changes and many details found in the novels are not given in the anime or manga.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dietrichinmanga.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="104" data-file-width="200" height="104" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/be/Dietrichinmanga.jpg" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dietrichinmanga.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Dietrich's character design, as seen in the manga, is younger and more boyish than in the anime
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The artwork also differs from all three versions, as the character designs were each created by different artists. There are similarities, however, as Shibamoto is responsible for the novel illustrations, while Kiyo Kuj based his work in the manga on Shibamoto's original designs.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SilverBulletComics_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-SilverBulletComics-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the anime series,
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Trinity_Blood_characters#Dietrich_von_Lohengrin" title="List of Trinity Blood characters">
       Dietrich von Lohengrin
      </a>
      's cold, evil nature is reflected in his appearance, while in the manga he has a softer,
      <a href="/wiki/Bish%C5%8Dnen" title="Bishnen">
       bishnen
      </a>
      design that is incongruous with his actual nature. Similarly, Endre is described as looking like a boy of around 1012 years old,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RAM1_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-RAM1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      while in the anime he is given an adult appearance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ep11_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ep11-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In additions to variants in appearances, there are differences in personalities relationships between the versions, and there are some characters that are unique to each adaptation. Sister Noelle, whose death devastates Abel in the anime series and in the novels, does not exist in the manga series. Alternatively, the anime does not mention Tres' two "brothers".
     </p>
     <p>
      Depending on the adaptation, some events occur at varying points in the story, while other events are unique to a single adaptation. The anime series' introductory-style episodes are based on the novel segments with the same names, but they are told in a different story. For example, in the novels, "From the Empire", the chapter in which Abel meets Asthe is the third chapter, following Flight Night and Witch Hunt,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RAM1_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-RAM1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, in the anime,
      <i>
       From the Empire
      </i>
      is the eleventh episode, coming after the Star of Sorrow arc and the Silent Noise incident. Conversely, the manga starts right with the Star of Sorrow arc and Abel meeting Esther and Dietrich. The Neumann brothers and their related story elements are found only in the novels. In the novels, it was Abel who interrupted the Cardinal's gathering, rather than Caterina or Leon, and the only people available to investigate the Silent Noise incident in Rome are Abel, Tres, and Leon.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RAM2_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-RAM2-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Humans">
       Humans
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trinity_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Humans">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The humans once attempted to colonize
      <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">
       Mars
      </a>
      after a massive population explosion. While on Mars, they discovered the Bacillus and Crusnik
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanomachines" title="Nanomachines">
       nanomachines
      </a>
      . When the Bacillus virus was embedded in humans, they were infected with a virus that changed them into vampires who had to feed on blood to survive. While this was happening on Mars, on Earth, continuing wars resulted in
      <a href="/wiki/Armageddon" title="Armageddon">
       Armageddon
      </a>
      , the apocalyptic war that nearly destroyed humanity. The vampireswho now call themselves Methuselahsand the humans ended up in a war against each other when the Methuselah returned to Earth. Nine hundred years later the surviving humans are still trying to recover from the war's devastating effects while trying to find ways to co-exist with the Methuselah despite the distrust and hatred still evident on both sides. The Vatican serves as the primary leader for the people in the ongoing struggle for peace with the Methuselah, and in dealing with rogue factions from both sides that would like the war to continue. The Methuselah refer to humans as "Terrans", or the "Short-Lived Race".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manga2_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Manga2-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Methuselah">
       Methuselah
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trinity_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Methuselah">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Methuselah, or "Long-Lived Race", are vampire-like creatures that feed on human blood and have supernatural strength.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manga2_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Manga2-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Their name is likely derived from the biblical figure Methuselah, who was reported to have lived 969 years. Methuselah have a prolonged lifespan, superior strength, and enhanced speed. They also have healing and regeneration abilities.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manga1_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Manga1-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition to their athleticism and stamina, Methuselah also possess varying individual powers. Most frequently, this power manifests itself in the form of a blade that comes out of the arms, claws, or even as sharp, prehensile hair. Some Methuselah have displayed an ability to control some elemental powers such as fire and ice. When being born, a Methuselah behaves as any normal human. He or she is able to wake during daytime and lacks the supernatural life and shortcomings of the race.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Crusniks">
       Crusniks
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trinity_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Crusniks">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cain_transformed.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="269" data-file-width="480" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/07/Cain_transformed.JPG/250px-Cain_transformed.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/07/Cain_transformed.JPG/375px-Cain_transformed.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/0/07/Cain_transformed.JPG 2x" width="250"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cain_transformed.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Cain, Crusnik 01, in his Crusnik form
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Crusniks are vampires who feed on the blood of other vampires.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manga1_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Manga1-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      They possess immense power and destructive potential and are nearly invincible.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manga2_5-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Manga2-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Whereas the Methuselahs are either born or infected, the Crusniks are unique beings created as a result of an experiment during the Mars Colonization Project. The three Crusniks,
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Trinity_Blood_characters#Augusta_Vradica_.28Seth_Nightroad.29" title="List of Trinity Blood characters">
       Seth
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abel_Nightroad" title="Abel Nightroad">
       Abel
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Trinity_Blood_characters#Lilith_Sahl" title="List of Trinity Blood characters">
       Lilith
      </a>
      , were test tube babies created for the Mars Colonization Project by the UNASF with genetically enhanced bodies. They were infused with the Crusnik nanomachines that were found on Mars as their enhanced bodies were the only ones capable of surviving the procedure. The exact circumstances behind the infusions of Seth, Abel, and Lilith are unknown, while Cain's infusion was done by Seth to save his life after a fatal accident.
     </p>
     <p>
      Crusniks possess unique attributes that separate them from Methuselahs. For instance, both Abel and Cain sport angelic wings and can fly quite easily. Crusniks can transmute their blood into weapons and often possess psychokinetic powers. Abel can generate powerful bio-electric fields while Cain can use psychokinetic waves to pulverize objects and enemies and to generate barriers. Seth can reduce people and objects to dust using sound waves.
     </p>
     <p>
      In order to activate his Crusnik powers, Abel usually issues a verbal or mental command to the nanomachines in his body. The exact wording and method of activation varies among the novels, the manga, and anime, and between the original Japanese and the English translations. For example, in the anime he says "Nanomachines: Crusnik 02 - Power Output __% Activate" while in the manga he silently activates Crusnik mode and receives a confirmation from the nanomachines of "Crusnik 02 Loading. Limitation at __%... Acknowledged".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manga1_6-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Manga1-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the anime, neither Cain nor Seth demonstrated a need to do this in order to activate their powers, and there are several times when Abel activates his powers without saying his usual command, so it appears to just be a literary device; the only exception to this is in the third novel where Sister Paula Souwauski, who is in combat with him, prevents the change by not letting him finish the statement, as it would flip the playing field as Abel in his human form is ranked as having a B- fighting ability while his 40% Crusnik is described as an S+.
     </p>

      <span class="mw-headline" id="Novels">
       Novels
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h3>
     <p>
      Written by
      <a href="/wiki/Sunao_Yoshida" title="Sunao Yoshida">
       Sunao Yoshida
      </a>
      and illustrated by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thores_Shibamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thores Shibamoto (page does not exist)">
       Thores Shibamoto
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       Trinity Blood
      </i>
      consists of two six-volume sub-series. The first series is subtitled
      <i>
       Rage Against the Moons
      </i>
      (RAM), while the second series, which continues where the first leaves off, is subtitled
      <i>
       Reborn on the Mars
      </i>
      (ROM). The novels were first serialized in
      <a href="/wiki/The_Sneaker" title="The Sneaker">
       The Sneaker
      </a>
      and published in their completed form by
      <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">
       Kadokawa Shoten
      </a>
      in 2001.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TP_License_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-TP_License-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Following the novels, a book containing backstory, world information and unreleased stories entitled Canon Summa Theologica, was released May 1, 2005.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TB_Canon_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-TB_Canon-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In North America, the novels have been licensed for an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_(language)" title="English (language)">
       English language
      </a>
      release by
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyopop" title="Tokyopop">
       Tokyopop
      </a>
      . The series are on a four-month release schedule that alternates between
      <i>
       Rage Against the Moons
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Reborn on the Mars
      </i>

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       <i>
        Trinity Blood Canon Summa Theologica

      <i>

      </i>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">
       Manga
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trinity_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Manga">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The chapters of the
      <i>
       Trinity Blood
      </i>
      manga series are illustrated by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kiyo_Kuj%C5%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kiyo Kuj (page does not exist)">
       Kiyo Kuj
      </a>
      and are based on the original
      <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">
       light novel
      </a>
      series written by
      <a href="/wiki/Sunao_Yoshida" title="Sunao Yoshida">
       Sunao Yoshida
      </a>
      and illustrated by Thores Shibamoto. The ongoing series premiered in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo" title="Shjo">
        shjo
       </a>
      </i>
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_manga_magazines" title="List of manga magazines">
       manga anthology
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Monthly_Asuka" title="Monthly Asuka">
        Monthly Asuka
       </a>
      </i>
      in 2004.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SilverBulletComics_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-SilverBulletComics-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TP_License_7-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-TP_License-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Individual chapters are called "Acts", and the chapter titles are based on film titles,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MangaVol6Afterword_27-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-MangaVol6Afterword-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      except for chapter 22, the title of which comes from "the lyrics of the theme song of a certain pioneering kendo anime".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MangaVol6Afterword_27-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-MangaVol6Afterword-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The individual chapters are collected and published in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankbon">
        tankbon
       </a>
      </i>
      volumes by
      <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">
       Kadokawa Shoten
      </a>
      , with the first released on March 17, 2004;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v1_28-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-v1-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      as of March 24, 2015, 18 volumes were released.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v18_29-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-v18-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The series is licensed for English-language release in North America by
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyopop" title="Tokyopop">
       Tokyopop
      </a>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">
       Anime
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trinity_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Anime">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote">
      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Trinity_Blood_episodes" title="List of Trinity Blood episodes">
       List of Trinity Blood episodes
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       Trinity Blood
      </i>
      anime television series premiered on the satellite network
      <a href="/wiki/WOWOW" title="WOWOW">
       WOWOW
      </a>
      on April 28, 2005 and ran through October 6, 2005, spanning a total of 24 episodes. Directed by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tomohiro_Hirata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tomohiro Hirata (page does not exist)">
       Tomohiro Hirata
      </a>
      , it featured character designs by
      <a href="/wiki/Atsuko_Nakajima" title="Atsuko Nakajima">
       Atsuko Nakajima
      </a>
      and music by
      <a href="/wiki/Takahito_Eguchi" title="Takahito Eguchi">
       Takahito Eguchi
      </a>
      . The satellite channel
      <a href="/wiki/Animax" title="Animax">
       Animax
      </a>
      also aired the series in English across its networks in
      <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">
       Southeast Asia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">
       South Asia
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
       South Africa
      </a>
      , and in Spanish and Portuguese across its networks throughout
      <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">
       Latin America
      </a>
      , respectively.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49">
       <a href="#cite_note-49">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        48
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The series also aired in
      <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">
       Malaysia
      </a>
      on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ntv7" title="Ntv7">
       ntv7
      </a>
      , in Australia on the Australian Cartoon Network's Adult Swim, and in Spain on
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Buzz_Channel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Buzz Channel (page does not exist)">
       Buzz Channel
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Funimation, which owns the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_1" title="Region 1">
       Region 1
      </a>
      license for the series, gave
      <i>
       Trinity Blood
      </i>
      a 90-minute US theatrical release to promote the series. The film was made by combining the first four episodes of the series.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FilmDetails_50-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FilmDetails-50">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        49
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Named
      <i>
       Trinity Blood: Genesis,
      </i>
      the movie was released May 5, 2006 in conjunction with
      <a href="/wiki/Anime_Central" title="Anime Central">
       Anime Central
      </a>
      in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_IL" title="Chicago, IL">
       Chicago, IL
      </a>
      , followed by a limited run in select theaters and at other anime conventions.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trinity_Blood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The first volume of the
      <i>
       Trinity Blood
      </i>
      manga reached the 30th spot on the Top 100 Graphic Novel sales for November 2006.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55">
       <a href="#cite_note-55">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        54
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Subsequent volumes have also all appeared in the top 100 spots in March, July, and November 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56">
       <a href="#cite_note-56">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        55
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57">
       <a href="#cite_note-57">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        56
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58">
       <a href="#cite_note-58">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        57
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       Trinity Blood
      </i>
      anime series enjoyed strong television ratings when it originally aired in Japan.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59">
       <a href="#cite_note-59">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        58
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its North American debut was also considered successful.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60">
       <a href="#cite_note-60">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        59
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The series is one of four FUNimation properties credited with helping the company achieve a 47.8% sales gain in 2006.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61">
       <a href="#cite_note-61">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        60
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">
       Anime News Network
      </a>
      's Carl Kimlinger finds the series "shiny, attractive, andon the wholeentertaining".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62">
       <a href="#cite_note-62">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        61
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      But he adds that "given the premise (and skill of the writers) it really should have shot for pulpy thrills instead of stately political intrigue."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63">
       <a href="#cite_note-63">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        62
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Theron Martin says "for the series as a whole to be worth watching, though, the storytelling and character development need to improve".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64">
       <a href="#cite_note-64">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        63
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Melissa Harper adds it is a "slick, well animated show" but "disjointed story makes it difficult to know or care what is going on".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65">
       <a href="#cite_note-65">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        64
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Hyper_(magazine)" title="Hyper (magazine)">
        Hyper
       </a>
       '
      </i>
      s Daniel Wilks comments that the anime is "for most part rather formulaic but the series does really pick up when it comes to the action scenes which are fast, frenetic, highly creative and beautifully animated".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66">
       <a href="#cite_note-66">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        65
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
