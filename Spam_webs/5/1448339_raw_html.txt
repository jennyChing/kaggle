        <h1 class="entry-title">
         A North Carolina Backyard Wedding: Jennifer + Brad
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2011-06-30T06:00:13+00:00">
          Thursday, June, 30, 2011
         </span>
         at
         <span class="date published time" title="2011-06-30T06:00:13+00:00">
          6:00 am
         </span>
         //
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://greenweddingshoes.com/a-north-carolina-backyard-wedding-jennifer-brad/#comments">
           <span>
            Comments:
           </span>
           17
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Todays wedding is one of the most emotional weddings I think Ive seen (and I look at hundreds of weddings each week!). Captured by
          <a href="http://www.carolineghetes.com" target="_blank">
           Caroline Ghetes
          </a>
          ,Jennifer + Brad were inspired by their home on Lake Norman in North Carolina (and I can see why  its gorgeous!!). They alwayswanted a backyard wedding and when they were lucky enough to buy their dream home (or dream lot) exactly 7 months before their wedding date, they knew it was the place. And then they spent the next 7 months tearing it down and building it into their real dream home  definitely not ideal circumstances under which to be planning a wedding, but they made it work! From Jennifer, 
          <em>
           Since our wedding was at our home, everything about it took on an intimate and personal meaning. Brad and I are both creative and hands-on people, and it was important to us to put our stamp on several details. Our choices and the elements of the day kind of built on each other along the way, and in the end it all naturally came together and felt likeus.
          </em>
          
         </p>
         <p>
          Their wedding day was bittersweet.Brads mother had been battling a brain tumor for several months and she passed away on the morning of their wedding (Im actually tearing up as I write this because I cant even imagine all the emotions they went through that day). They never imagined it would happen that day, but it did. Jennifer told me it was a blessing that her mother-in-law could finally be at peace, and be in a place where she could actually be with them on that day. They are both very close to theirfamiliesso it was a very emotional day for everyone as I can only imagine. Jennifer + Brad, thanks for sharing your beautiful wedding day with us + we wish you so much happiness as husband + wife.
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding wood sign" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17487" height="486" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-01.jpg" title="backyard wedding wood sign" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="blue glass jars wedding decor" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17486" height="486" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-02.jpg" title="blue glass jars wedding decor" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          From Jennifer,
          <em>
           Right after we were engaged, Brads Mom found boxes of vintage mason jars that had been Brads grandparents. They really helped set the tone for the unfussy elegant vibe we wanted our wedding to have. I also loved the muted blue of several of them, which started our color scheme, if you could call it that. We used the jars on all of the tables for flower arrangements, and some as lanterns throughout the backyard.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="blue glass jars wedding decor" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17485" height="486" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-03.jpg" title="blue glass jars wedding decor" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="wedding first look photos" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17484" height="658" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-04.jpg" title="wedding first look photos" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Most memorable moment of your wedding day?
          </strong>
          <em>
           The most memorable moment of the day for us was our first look before the ceremony. Such a serene calm finally came over our house then, after there had been so much frenzy. And in that calm we could really feel all the happiness, and even the sadness of our day. Its hard to describe how we could be so happy and so sad at the same time, but it was all very true, and kept us very much in the present.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="wedding first look photos" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17483" height="650" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-05.jpg" title="wedding first look photos" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding " class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17482" height="485" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-06.jpg" title="backyard wedding " width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           We painted the wedding signs on wood salvaged from a historic Charleston home renovation.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding by a lake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17481" height="485" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-07.jpg" title="backyard wedding by a lake" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What song did you walk down the aisle to?
          </strong>
          <em>
           My Father walked me down the aisle to an acoustic version of Trains Soul Sister. Brad and I made our recessional to Im Yours, and our first dance was to You are the Best Thing. We so wish we had a recording of each of those performances by our band!!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding by a lake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17480" height="651" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-08.jpg" title="backyard wedding by a lake" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard-wedding-21" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17488" height="498" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-21.jpg" title="backyard-wedding-21" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding by a lake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17479" height="428" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-09.jpg" title="backyard wedding by a lake" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Brad built our wedding altar, which will forever have a special place in our backyard.Brad and I exchanged wedding rings that had been our grandparents. Brad has his Grandfathers, which Brads Mom had kept for him for many years, hoping he would wear it. I have my Grandmothers, which still bears the inscription from their wedding day over sixty years ago, and was given to me after my Grandfather passed away a few short months ago.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding by a lake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17478" height="485" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-10.jpg" title="backyard wedding by a lake" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding by a lake" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17477" height="840" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-11.jpg" title="backyard wedding by a lake" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="bride and groom on a boat" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17476" height="484" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-12.jpg" title="bride and groom on a boat" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="bride and groom on a boat" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17475" height="430" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-13.jpg" title="bride and groom on a boat" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard-wedding-reception tables" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17468" height="486" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-14.jpg" title="backyard-wedding-reception tables" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="rustic wedding flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17474" height="429" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-15.jpg" title="rustic wedding flowers" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="rustic wedding flowers" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17473" height="484" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-16.jpg" title="rustic wedding flowers" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Brads Mom and I found a beautiful floral fabric with muted blues and greens and purples that my mom sewed into runners for all of our tables.Brad and I like to moonlight as a homebrewer and letterpress printer (respectively) in our spare time. Brad and I designed our save the dates and invitations, and I hand-mixed the ink and printed them on my antique letterpress. Brad brewed kegs of his favorite homebrew beers for the reception.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="bride and groom" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17472" height="485" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-17.jpg" title="bride and groom" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17471" height="652" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-18.jpg" title="backyard wedding" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="wedding dancing" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17489" height="438" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-22.jpg" title="backyard-wedding-22" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="backyard wedding dancing under the stars" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17470" height="787" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/backyard-wedding-19.jpg" title="backyard wedding dancing under the stars" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p class="only-images">
          <img alt="bride and groom on a pier at night" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-17496" height="815" src="http://greenweddingshoes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/couple-on-pier.jpg" title="bride and groom on a pier at night" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Any advice for those planning now?
          </strong>
          <em>
           Dont be afraid to change your mind! Seriously. We were lining up vendors and making plans to have our wedding in Charleston (where we were engaged, kind of a second home for us) when the real estate gods intervened and helped us quickly sell our old house and find the home of our dreams. We knew right away we wanted to have our wedding at our new home, and pulled the brakes on the Charleston plans. We definitely created a lot of work and some aggravation for ourselves in doing that, but it was completely worth it.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Its okay to be ruthless with your guest list. We wanted our wedding to be about a celebration with our closest family and friends, and we stayed true to that.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           And, give yourself a break on DIY mania. We tried to prioritize the projects that were most meaningful to us and would have the biggest impact on our day and our guests.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           We wouldnt have been able to pull it off without the support of family and friends
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Our closest friends, who also gave us places to stay when the renovations on our house made it unlivable, were loyal and stuck it out with us, helping us in the weeks before to get the house and the property ready.
          </em>
          <em>
           My parents and brother were on hand in the days leading up to the wedding making all the last-minute details happenfrom ribbon wands for the ceremony, to luminaries and landscaping and other projects around the house, they were the glue that kept the wedding preparations together in the last few days!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           We were fortunate to have some very talented vendors involved in our ceremony and reception! Food, music, photos and the ceremony were very important to us, and not necessarily in that orderwe made those decisions very carefully. Choosing the inimitable Caroline Ghetes to be our photographer was the very first decision we made, before we even knew where the wedding would be. Our band, the Chris Crosby Group, has the best sound weve ever come across, and they really made the day for us. The reception was catered by Something Classic, and they worked with us to put together a fabulous menu, complete with key lime pies as our dessert. Our officiant, Reverend Rebecca Nagy, was sensitive and eloquent and funny, and helped us craft a ceremony that was meaningful and non-traditional.
          </em>
          <em>
           And one decision that seemed to make itself was working with Lily Greenthumbs for our flowers  I got a referral from a close friend, met Karen, and that was that. She immediately got the unfussy elegant idea, and the flowers were spectacular.
          </em>
          <em>
           Blush Wedding and Events provided day-of-coordination for us, and Irene Reinhard was a savior. She made everything come together and made it look effortless  even though I know she never stopped working!
          </em>
         </p>
