         <h1>
          Three Things Small Businesses Need to Learn About Design
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          by
          <a href="http://ericnovak.com/author/eric-novak/" rel="author" title="Posts by Eric Novak">
           Eric Novak
          </a>
          | Sep 22, 2010 |
          <a href="http://ericnovak.com/category/design/" rel="category tag">
           Design
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://ericnovak.com/category/uncategorized/" rel="category tag">
           Uncategorized
          </a>
          |
          <span class="comments-number">
           <a href="http://ericnovak.com/three-things-small-businesses-need-to-learn-about-design/#respond">
            0 comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           Every designer will tell you that it isnt always fun working with clients. Even Ive had to deal with at least one customer who insisted that I tell them exactly how I made their graphic, So I wont have to use you next time I need a project done!  Its kinda like asking a
           <a href="http://www.youtube.com/user/zeorge497#p/a/u/2/R2a8TRSgzZY">
            chef for his recipe
           </a>
           , so you can prepare his meal at home. If you think thats bad, there are are at least three more things small businesses need to learn about design if they ever want to class up and break into the real business world.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           1).
           <strong>
            Learn to Copy Big Businesses
           </strong>
            They spend a crap-load of money on research; Money that you will never have unless you follow in their footsteps. Take McDonalds for instance. They spent millions on traffic researching, looking for exactly the right place to put buildings. Instead of doing their own identical research, Burger King planted a restaurant across the street from wherever the golden arches sprung up. Successful businesses copy successful big business  including their design styles:
           <a href="http://www.frederiksamuel.com/blog/2007/10/nike-soccer.html">
            Nike
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.frederiksamuel.com/blog/2007/10/nike-soccer.html">
            Hidden Valley Ranch
           </a>
           and
           <a href="http://www.frederiksamuel.com/blog/2010/04/fedex-4.html">
            Fedex
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           2).
           <strong>
            Learn That in Design, Less is More
           </strong>
            Highly successful designs use white space. You might be paying for an expensive ad, but youll find that empty space catches the eye and your customers money. The Homeschool industry in particular needs to remember that while a lot of text on an ad says a lot about their product, it is also an eye sore and a pain to read. Ads that stick to minimum text and convey thoughts through pictures, are what small businesses should strive for:
           <a href="http://www.frederiksamuel.com/blog/2010/02/sandisk-micro.html">
            SanDisk
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.frederiksamuel.com/blog/2010/02/sandisk-micro.html">
            On The Job
           </a>
           , and
           <a href="http://www.frederiksamuel.com/blog/2010/02/ansell-gloves.html">
            Ansell Gloves
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           3).
           <strong>
            Learn to Hire Legit Designers and Listen to Them
           </strong>
            The problem that small businesses most commonly have, is that they dont know how to hire designers. Computers may have made everyone a designer, but they make everyone a
           <em>
            good designer
           </em>
           . Good designers wont ever use Comic Sans or Papyrus. They will avoid drop shadows and glow effects like the plague. Theyll design with subtleties . If you get a good designer you should pay them well and listen to their suggestions. Always look at your designers portfolio.
          </p>
          <p>
           Though there are many more subjects that small businesses need to study with, the first one is design. Appearance is everything and without a good appearance, every small business will stay that way  small.
          </p>
