      <div class="content-title-noshade-size5">
       Open Source IT Risk Management
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        One of the main goals of the Security Officers
            Management and Analysis Project (SOMAP.org) is to
            develop and maintain Open Source Information Security
            Risk Management
        <b class="txt-blue10">
         documents
        </b>
        ,
        <b class="txt-blue10">
         tools
        </b>
        and
        <b class="txt-blue10">
         utilities
        </b>
        .
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        It is our strong belief that risk management
            processes and best practices need to be developed and
            published in an
        <b class="txt-blue10">
         open and free
        </b>
        kind of way. Information Security is
        <b class="txt-blue10">
         not a competitive issue
        </b>
        and only
            freely available and cooperatively developed risk
            management utilities and tools can potentially lead to
            a better security management and to further development
            of the whole IT risk management field.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        <img alt="image" class="img-right-noborder-topmargin" src="media/sub_projects.png"/>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        Our activities are concentrating on four
            sub-projects:
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        The OGRCM3 project develops and documents a
            methodology on how to measure and manage risk.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        The ORIMOR contains a database model which is used
            as the basis for our own risk management framework and
            tool.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        The ORICO Framework and Tool are the (reference)
            implementation of our own maturity management
            methodology.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
      </div>
      <div class="content-title-noshade-size4">
       OGRCM3
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        The
        <a href="methodology/default.html">
         The Open
            Governance, Risk and Compliance Maturity Management
            Methodology
        </a>
        contains an overview of the risk and
            compliance management process and an description on
        <b class="txt-blue10">
         why and how
        </b>
        to manage
            risk.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
      </div>
      <div class="content-title-noshade-size4">
       ORIMOR
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        <a href="repository/default.html">
         The Open Risk
            Model Repository
        </a>
        are actually three things in
            one:
       </p>
      </div>
      <ul class="indent">
       <li>
        A central repository containing best practice
            details.
       </li>
       <li>
        A model how to store risk management data.
       </li>
       <li>
        An architecture to use a meta layer to store common
            type information.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
      </div>
      <div class="content-title-noshade-size4">
       ORICO Framework &amp; Tool
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
      </div>
      <div class="content-txtbox-noshade">
       <p>
        The
        <a href="orico/default.html">
         The Open Risk &amp;
            Compliance Framework and Tool
        </a>
        are two projects in
            one.
       </p>
      </div>
      <ul class="indent">
       <li>
        The Framework builds the foundation for a risk
            management tool. It implements all the building blocks
            like data abstraction and RAD tools which can be used
            when developing a risk management tool.
       </li>
       <li>
        The Tool is the reference implementation of the
            OGRCM3. It makes heavy use of the ORICO Framework and
            is developed as a desktop as well as a web
            application.
       </li>
      </ul>
     </div>
