      <h1>
       Complex Number Paradox
      </h1>
      <!-- End Title -->
      <!-- Begin Total Content -->
      <!-- Begin Content -->
      <p>
       An improper use of the symbol
       <img alt="sqrt(-1)" border="0" class="inlineformula" height="20" src="/images/equations/ComplexNumberParadox/Inline1.gif" width="33"/>
       for the
       <a href="/ImaginaryUnit.html">
        imaginary unit
       </a>
       leads to the apparent proof of a
 false statement.
      </p>

      <p>
       The reason for the fallacy is that
       <img alt="sqrt(-1)" border="0" class="inlineformula" height="20" src="/images/equations/ComplexNumberParadox/Inline17.gif" width="33"/>
       is not
 an ordinary (real) square root, hence the rule for computing the quotient of radicals
 does not apply to it.
      </p>
