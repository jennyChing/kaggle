         <h1 class="entry-title">
          <a href="/blog/2015/1/18/the-worlds-most-boring-evangelical-lesson">
           The world's most boring evangelical lesson
          </a>
         </h1>
        </header>
        <!--SPECIAL CONTENT-->
        <div class="body entry-content">
         <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1421630694032" id="item-54bc5929e4b0ff956991e7fc">
          <div class="row sqs-row">
           <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-1e1f24e43ecadad38074">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p>
               Recently I stumbled and listened to a TED talk entitled, "Thomas Hellum: The world's most boring television ... and why it's hilariously addictive"Thomas Hellum is a Norwegian television producer who creates TV show documentaries about boring stuff, similar to realtime TV shows about train rides and cruse boatvoyages.Insteadof these programs being television flops, they became television blockbusters. The phrase to describe this typeprogrammingcalled Slow TV. Theseshow topics are picked by
               <span>
                taking,Something long and make it a topic,
               </span>
               <span>
                like with the railway and the Hurtigruten,
               </span>
               <span>
                or we could take a topic and make it long.
               </span>
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block video-block sqs-block-video" data-block-json='{"layout":"caption-hidden","overlay":false,"description":{"source":"","html":""},"hSize":null,"floatDir":null,"html":"&lt;iframe src=\"https://embed-ssl.ted.com/talks/thomas_hellum_the_world_s_most_boring_television_and_why_it_s_hilariously_addictive.html\" width=\"640\" height=\"360\" frameborder=\"0\" scrolling=\"no\" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;","resolvedBy":"manual"}' data-block-type="32" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1421629689453_15401">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <div class="sqs-video-wrapper" data-html='&lt;iframe src="https://embed-ssl.ted.com/talks/thomas_hellum_the_world_s_most_boring_television_and_why_it_s_hilariously_addictive.html" width="640" height="360" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" webkitAllowFullScreen mozallowfullscreen allowFullScreen&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' data-provider-name="">
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1421629689453_40224">
             <div class="sqs-block-content">
              <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1421629689453_40147">
               In creating these shows, he stated that his aimsare:
               <span>
                You have to let the viewers make the stories themselves,
               </span>
               <span>
                and I'll give you an example of that.
               </span>
               <span>
                This is from last summer,
               </span>
               <span>
                and as a TV producer,
               </span>
               <span>
                it's a nice picture, but now you can cut to the next one.
               </span>
               <span>
                But this is Slow TV,
               </span>
               <span>
                so you have to keep this picture until it really starts hurting your stomach,
               </span>
               <span>
                and then you keep it a little bit longer,
               </span>
               <span>
                and when you keep it that long,
               </span>
               <span>
                I'm sure some of you now have noticed the cow.
               </span>
               "
              </p>
              <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1421629689453_40148">
               As I watched the talk, it occurred to methat the secret to keep people's attention is seeing that the viewer creates their own story, through the story that is given tothem. Storytellers don't just tell the story, but give people the material to create their own stories. Because of this, I believe we fail often with evangelism. We want to tell the Good News outside of a persons experience, and wedo notsee that the Good Newsis material for the people to tell their own story. Theological reflectionat its core helps people tellGod's story in the context of their life.
              </p>
