          <h1 class="entry-title">
           Why Bloodshed in Ukraine Can Help the United States
          </h1>
          <div class="entry-meta">
           <span class="author-name">
            <a href="http://sofrep.com/author/kerry-patton/" rel="author" title="Posts by Kerry Patton">
             Kerry Patton
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="comments-count">
            <a href="#respond">
             <span class="livefyre-commentcount" data-lf-article-id="33771" data-lf-site-id="297044">
              44 Comments
             </span>
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="posted-in">
            <a href="http://sofrep.com/op-ed/" rel="category tag">
             Expert Analysis
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </header>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           No one wants to speak about people dying or living a life saturated by military conflict through the lens of such atrocity being a good thing. Well, contrary to popular belief, violence and chaos can actually be a good thing and, in the case of the Ukrainian crisis, its potentially a great thing for the United States.
          </p>
          <p>
           Prior to the 2003 US invasion of Iraq, I was sent to Ukraine. Due to the nature of the mission, I will not address in any detail what it was I or my team was involved with. I will say, however, through a very broad and open-ended statement that our mission did have to do with Ukrainian involvement in assisting Saddam Husseins regime during the no-fly zone era.
          </p>
