            <article class="post-3650 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-decorate-your-home category-home-decor-projects category-diy-outdoor-projects category-seasonal-home-decor category-summer-decor tag-lighting tag-outside" id="post-3650">
             <header class="entry-header">
              <h1 class="entry-title">
               DIY Outdoor Chandelier {progress in the outdoor living room}
              </h1>
              <div class="entry-meta">
              </div>
              <!-- .entry-meta -->
             </header>
             <!-- .entry-header -->
             <div class="entry-content">
              <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
              <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="18139936" data-link="http://www.tinysidekick.com/diy-outdoor-chandelier/" data-summary="" data-title="DIY Outdoor Chandelier {progress in the outdoor living room}">
              </div>
              <p>
               The hubs is no longer thinking Im crazy for holding on to an ugly chandelier that we ripped out of our dining room over a year ago. Oh no now (even though I havent actually heard it,) he is singing my praises cause I took that old chandelier and turned it into this fun outdoor chandelier and it doesnt even require electricity! I guess the proper term would be Outdoor Candle Chandelier!
              </p>
              <p>
               How much did it cost me? Zero. Nada. Nothin. I reinventedit with supplies I
               <del>
                hoarded
               </del>
               already had on hand and Ill show you how you can do the same!
              </p>
              <p>
               What? You dont have one of these beauties on hand? Well, I see them at garage sales quite a bitor you might have some luck onCraigslist. The point is, this should be a very low cost project.
              </p>
              <p>
               (If you dont want to go through all of that trouble, I found one very similar
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0002D15BI/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0002D15BI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=tinysi-20&amp;linkId=5FJUL6UPRFKPL474" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                on Amazon
               </a>
               for only 35 bucks.)
               <br/>
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/how-to-make-an-outdoor-chandelier.jpg">
                <img alt="how to make an outdoor chandelier" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3654" height="1050" src="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/how-to-make-an-outdoor-chandelier.jpg" width="700"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               I doubt it, but you may remember this chandelier from
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/dining-room-makeover_3512/" target="_blank">
                the before post
               </a>
               of my dining room revamp. Im well aware that Ive been slacking on my dining room, but it sorta took a back seat to other projects because I didnt have a clear vision. You will be seeing it again late this summer but who even needs an indoor dining room this time of year? Anyway, we took it down over a year ago and it made a really great home for the spiders living in our garage, but it was time to dust it off and give it new life.
              </p>
              <p>
               As you know, Ive been working on creating a fun and relaxing outdoor living area and decided that a bit of diy outdoor lighting over the dining table would be one of those finishing touches to really make the space feel like a room. So I dug it out of the garage and got to work.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/Upcycled-Dining-room-chandelier.jpg">
                <img alt="Upcycled Dining room chandelier" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3659" height="350" src="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/Upcycled-Dining-room-chandelier.jpg" width="700"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               I knew I didnt want to deal with electricity at all, so the first thing I did was take the wires out. I did this by unscrewing the bulbs and disassembling the chandelier. I cut the wires and took off the metal parts that held the light bulb. That let the little dish parts come off (for painting later.)
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/DIY-Outdoor-Chandelier.jpg">
                <img alt="DIY Outdoor Chandelier" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3652" height="350" src="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/DIY-Outdoor-Chandelier.jpg" width="700"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               Once it was all apart I gave it a coat of paint using the leftover Rust-Oleum Key Lime spray paint that I had from the
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/diy-outdoor-curtains/" target="_blank" title="DIY Drop Cloth Outdoor Curtains &amp; a Summer Celebration Hop">
                outdoor curtains I made
               </a>
               . I let it dry between coats and turned it to get it sprayed from every angle.
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: left;">
               I needed the nut portion of the metal part that held up the bulb to reassemble later, so instead of trying to buy nuts to fit, the (smart) hubs just bent the long part (shown above) until it broke off and we were left with the nut.
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/upcycled-chandelier-for-outside.jpg">
                <img alt="upcycled chandelier for outside" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3658" height="350" src="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/upcycled-chandelier-for-outside.jpg" width="700"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: left;">
               Once it was dry, I put it back together andwe hung it from our patio cover using the original chain.
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: left;">
               Then, I used super glue to glue on some small glass votives that I had in my cupboard. You can get these at the dollar store if you dont already have some.
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/reuse-an-old-chandelier-outside.jpg">
                <img alt="reuse an old chandelier outside" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3657" height="350" src="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/reuse-an-old-chandelier-outside.jpg" width="700"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: left;">
               Once it was all dry, it was ready for candles! I used real ones and the light it gave off at night was so pretty. I do live in a high fire area though so if it is at all windy, Ill switch these out for some
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000VUKQ6C/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000VUKQ6C&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=tinysi-20&amp;linkId=MBW3L34UFNAXBOQB" target="_blank">
                fake LED tea light candles.
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/DIY-outdoor-candle-chandelier.jpg">
                <img alt="DIY outdoor candle chandelier" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3651" height="467" src="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/DIY-outdoor-candle-chandelier.jpg" width="700"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: left;">
               It is so weird that I didnt like this chandelier before, because now that it is green I love it!
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/electric-to-candle-chandelier.jpg">
                <img alt="electric to candle chandelier" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3653" height="580" src="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/electric-to-candle-chandelier.jpg" width="700"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               So lets do a little recap of this space so far.
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/outdoor-living-room-update/" target="_blank" title="Some VERY Exciting News and A Outdoor Living Room Update (I made a furniture choice!)">
                Comfortable outdoor furniture
               </a>
               , check!
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/diy-outdoor-curtains/" target="_blank" title="DIY Drop Cloth Outdoor Curtains &amp; a Summer Celebration Hop">
                Budget friendly outdoor curtains
               </a>
               and the
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/make-outdoor-curtain-rod-little-money/" title="How to Make an Outdoor Curtain Rod for Very Little Money">
                curtain rod
               </a>
               , check! Outdoor chandelier, check!
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               So that means I have one more project to show you next week and then it will be time to tour the entire space! And summer is only beginning go me!
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/outdoor-candelabra-from-a-chandelier.jpg">
                <img alt="outdoor candelabra from a chandelier" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3660" height="1400" src="http://tinysidekick.com/wp-content/uploads/outdoor-candelabra-from-a-chandelier.jpg" width="700"/>
               </a>
               Dont forget to
               <strong>
                PIN before you go
               </strong>
               by hovering over any image!
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               See the completed
               <a href="http://tinysidekick.com/turn-patio-outdoor-living-room-oasis-reveal/" target="_blank">
                outdoor living room reveal here!
               </a>
              </p>
