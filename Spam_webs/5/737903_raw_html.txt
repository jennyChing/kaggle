            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="f47a2790279bc61143fbc0f11441b2aa">

            <p data-textannotation-id="d81be6eea1fbe597cc7e505845f5d942">
             Breaking into many types of electronic safes (you know, ones with keypads) is surprisingly easy. You just need to
             <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5853610/crack-almost-any-electronic-safe-with-just-a-bounce" x-inset="1">
              bounce them
             </a>
             . It's a pretty simple technique, but looks like it takes a bit of practice. Basically, you drop part of the safe against the table (or whatever surface it's resting upon) while turning the locking knob. If your timing is right, you'll have turned the knob when the safe's lock bounces open for a brief moment. This works because many cheaper safes have locks that lift. Better safes don't have this vulnerability and this trick won't work on them, but if you've got a cheap one and lost your passcode you can just do this bouncing trick to open it right up.

             8. Release a Sliding Chain Lock with a Rubber Band
