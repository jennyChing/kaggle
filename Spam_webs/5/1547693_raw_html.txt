       <h2>
        <strong>
         Iron Dog Palin Exclusive  Todd Palin  HEAR IT
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Iron Dog Palin exclusive  Todd Palin tells his story in this interview by
        <a href="http://leaddogalaska.net/todd-palin-talks-iron-dog/" target="_blank">
         LeadDog Alaska
        </a>
        and US for Palin. LeadDog Alaska hosted the interview in which Palin answered questions from both site publishers.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         How Did You Get Started in Iron Dog Racing?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_77638" style="width: 650px">
        <a href="http://cdn2.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/sno-jet-file-photo-640.jpg">
         <img alt="Iron Dog Palin Exclusive - Todd Palin" class="size-full wp-image-77638" height="360" src="http://cdn2.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/sno-jet-file-photo-640.jpg" width="640"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Iron Dog Palin Exclusive  Todd Palin
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Palin grew up around snowmachines all his life. It was common for kids to drive them to school and have small races. When Palin settled in Wasilla, he first heard of the Iron Dog race from a buddy, Frank Woods. The pair signed up for their first race in 1993 finishing in fifth place. Because they had to get a snowmachine welded up in McGrath, they were busted down to Trail Class, but that did not matter. Palins Iron Dog career was launched and his course was charted.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         What Keeps You Wanting to Race?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        The Iron Dog gets under your skin. Its very hard to shake away, Palin said. He loves traveling through the remote Alaskan villages and catching up with friends he sees once a year. He said the Iron Dog race is like an expensive gym membership, and what a guy does to get out of town for a week.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         Whats Been Your Worst Experience on the Trail?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_77639" style="width: 650px">
        <a href="http://cdn1.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/field-repair-640.jpg">
         <img alt="Iron Dog Palin Exclusive with Todd Palin" class="size-full wp-image-77639" height="360" src="http://cdn1.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/field-repair-640.jpg" width="640"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Iron Dog Palin Exclusive with Todd Palin
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Palins two worst experiences were losing race by one second in 2006 and breaking his arm in 2008. Stuff that he considers normal: accidents, getting stuck in rivers, falling into bodies of water, blown engines, broken chains, blown shocks. Hes never had to walk back on the trail. All in all, its been great, he said.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         What is your most funny memory?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn3.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/iron-dog-joint-exclusive-feature.jpg">
         <img alt="Iron Dog Palin Exclusive - Todd Palin - HEAR IT" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-77630" height="360" src="http://cdn3.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/iron-dog-joint-exclusive-feature.jpg" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Palin said he did not find anything that warms your heart, about the race, but it has had its funny moments. He said coming across the finish line first is a big smile. Four times first is pretty awesome. So, I mean thats the whole goal of participating in this race: to win.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         What Drove Your Decision to Move from Ski-Doo to Polaris?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_77632" style="width: 650px">
        <a href="http://cdn4.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/early-polaris-640.jpg">
         <img alt="Iron Dog Palin Exclusive - Todd Palin" class="size-full wp-image-77632" height="480" src="http://cdn4.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/early-polaris-640.jpg" width="640"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Iron Dog Palin Exclusive  Todd Palin  one of Todd Palins first Polaris snowmachines.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Palins move to Polaris brings him full circle. His first snowmachine was a Montgomery Ward Sno Jet that wouldnt start given to him by his dad. If you can start it, you can drive it. Palin got his buddies and made the Sno Jet start. A year or two later, Palins dad bought a 250 cc Polaris which he put a lot of time on. Meanwhile, his mother in Dillingham was a Polaris dealer and Palin himself was a Polaris dealer out of Big Lake. From his first Iron Dog race in 1993 till 1998, Palin used Polaris snowmachines. Palin sold his dealership in 1996.
       </p>
       <p>
        Palin and his race partners switched to Arctic Cat in 1999. He remained Arctic Cat man for 13 years winning three out of his four championships on those sleds. Arctic Cat was the dominant manufacturer of the era and provided the most support.
       </p>
       <p>
        In 2013,
        <a href="http://us4palin.com/palin-davis-clock-3700-miles-on-ski-doo-snowmachines-pre-iron-dog/" target="_blank">
         Palin switched to Ski-Doo
        </a>
        first with Scott Davis, then in 2014 with Tyler Huntington. Ski-Doos ETEC engine and the snowmachines chassis proved to be an advantage at the time. Arctic Cat had not developed a real Iron Dog sled.
       </p>
       <p>
        In 2015, the full-circle return was brought on by Tyler Huntington. Hes a Polaris guy and had a really good deal with the Outpost Dealership in Fairbanks, Palin said. Huntington wanted to get back on Polaris, so Palin made the switch.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         How Would You Describe This Years Conditions?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_77631" style="width: 650px">
        <a href="http://cdn2.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/todd-palin-snowmachine-no-snow-640.jpg">
         <img alt="No Snow" class="size-full wp-image-77631" height="266" src="http://cdn2.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/todd-palin-snowmachine-no-snow-640.jpg" width="640"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Iron Dog Palin Exclusive  Todd Palin  This years iron Dog was raced over 300 miles of trails with not a flake of snow.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        This year, over 300 miles of trail did not have one flake of snow on the ground. They were conditions more suited to an ATV, than a snowmachine. The
        <a href="http://www.polaris.com/en-us/snowmobiles/switchback" target="_blank">
         Polaris Switchback AXYS 600 snowmachines
        </a>
        held up.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         What are Your Own Iron Dog Plans for the Future?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Huntington Retires from Iron Dog
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://us4palin.com/wet-iron-dog-todd-palin-mama-grizzly-radio/" target="_blank">
         As previously reported
        </a>
        , Tyler Huntington severely injured his hips and pelvis in a 2012 training accident. Huntington injured one of his arms in what was supposed to be 1,000-mile training run this year. Huntington had an accident and by the time Palin saw him, he T-boned Huntingtons sled, knocking both practice sleds out of commission. Huntingtons hips are held together with screws and chains, Palin said. Its amazing he even raced at all.
       </p>
       <p>
        The combination of Huntingtons injuries, the short training run, and the snowless conditions added up to a bad run where even finishing at all was a win. Palin and Huntington came in 10th place 
        <a href="http://us4palin.com/iron-dog-2015-finish-sarah-palin-on-trail-future-more/" target="_blank">
         they were recorded as being in ninth when our story went to press
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Palin Still Making His 2016 Decision
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Palin is busy getting sleds back to the dealers, thanking sponsors and doing other post-race tasks. In next years race, Palin will be 52. He said he is one of the older guys in the Pro Class. With Huntington retired, Palin would need a new partner and that may also mean another snowmachine change. In past years, we have announced Palins forthcoming races in November.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         Anything Else You Wish to Share?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_77636" style="width: 650px">
        <a href="http://cdn4.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/2015-03-01-15.41.41.jpg">
         <img alt="Iron Dog Palin Exclusive -  Todd Palin - HEAR IT" class="size-full wp-image-77636" height="427" src="http://cdn4.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/2015-03-01-15.41.41.jpg" width="640"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Iron Dog Palin Exclusive  Todd Palin
         <br/>
         Iron Dog races would not be possible without family support.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Palin said Iron Dog races would not be possible without help from the manufacturers and sponsors, and blessing from the family. He said the race puts a lot of tension on the family and without their support, I would have been done a long time ago. Indeed, his wife, Sarah structured her February speaking circuit around the race and more than likely canceled a once-in-a-lifetime speech at Cambridge University to be at the finish line.
       </p>
       <p>
        He said Iron Dog racing is like a marathon. You have to put the time in or youll have a bad run.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_77652" style="width: 289px">
        <a href="http://cdn4.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/irondog-champ.jpg">
         <img alt="Iron Dog Palin Exclusive - Todd Palin - HEAR IT" class="size-full wp-image-77652" height="404" src="http://cdn4.us4palin.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/irondog-champ.jpg" width="279"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Iron Dog Palin Exclusive
         <br/>
         Todd Palin 4-Time Champion
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
