           <img alt="It takes mud for a seed to grow." class="size-medium wp-image-531" height="222" src="http://whatworkscoaching.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/seeds-sprouting-in-soil-300x222.jpg" width="300"/>

          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           It takes mud and darkness for a seed to grow.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          This morning, I was doing my daily spiritual readings and I
          <a href="http://www.dailyword.com/dailyword/growth-wednesday-december-03-2014" target="_blank" title="Read what I found in the Unity Daily Word">
           stumbled upon something
          </a>
          I needed to be reminded of. Seeds need dirt and water (mud) to grow. Mud is messy. Its not pretty, it gets all over you and, if you let it dry, it can be hard to chip off. But its absolutely necessary for growth. When the seed is in the dark, its still getting nurtured by heat, water, soil, minerals, and nutrients. Its not a pretty place to be, but soon the seed uses those tools to sprout. Soon, the plant is reaching up toward the sunlight. Its fruitful, with flowers or food. It is fulfilling its lifes purpose.
         </p>
         <p>
          Do you ever feel like the seed under the mud? Its difficult to see the benefits and pre-growth you are experiencing when you are shrouded in darkness. But its there, nourishing you, fortifying you, and helping you sprout to your next challenge.
         </p>
         <p>
          Im reminded of when I moved to Carson City. Most people plan a move based on job opportunities, schools, family, or other supportive factors. I moved here under none of those conditions. I was
          <a href="http://whatworkscoaching.com/how-the-universe-took-control-of-my-life/" target="_blank" title="How the universe took control of my life.">
           thrown here by the universe
          </a>
          , thrust underground compared to my fast-paced lifestyle in Dallas or Los Angeles, to recover, learn, germinate, and sprout. Do I know whats next? No. Am I grateful for the shift? Absolutely!
         </p>
         <p>
          I encourage my clients to plan, but I also encourage them to leave space for opportunity to enter. Our finite minds cannot imagine what is possible when we allow room for the infinite to come in and use our right action to create magic in our lives. Its amazing!
         </p>
         <p>
          If you are stuck in the dark, I encourage you to try a few exercises in
          <a href="http://whatworkscoaching.com/free-gifts/sample-page/" target="_blank" title="Free Workbook">
           my free workbook
          </a>
          .Or, just read something positive to shift you. Understand that the muddy mindset or situation is only a temporary state and you can learn from it to grow toward the light.
         </p>
