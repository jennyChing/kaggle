     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Bacopa monnieri
       </b>
      </i>
      (
      <b>
       waterhyssop
      </b>
      ,
      <b>
       brahmi
      </b>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRIN_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-GRIN-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <b>
       thyme-leafed gratiola
      </b>
      ,
      <b>
       water hyssop
      </b>
      ,
      <b>
       herb of grace
      </b>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRIN_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GRIN-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <b>
       Indian pennywort
      </b>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRIN_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-GRIN-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ) is a
      <a href="/wiki/Perennial_plant" title="Perennial plant">
       perennial
      </a>
      , creeping
      <a href="/wiki/Herb" title="Herb">
       herb
      </a>
      native to the wetlands of southern India, Australia, Europe, Africa, Asia, and North and South America.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRIN_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-GRIN-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bacopa is an important medicinal herb used in
      <a href="/wiki/Ayurveda" title="Ayurveda">
       Ayurveda
      </a>
      , where it is also known as "Brahmi," after
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brahm%C4%81" title="Brahm">
       Brahm
      </a>
      , the creator God of the Hindu pantheon. Bacopa has traditionally been employed as a neurological tonic and cognitive enhancer, and it is currently being studied for its possible neuroprotective properties.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pase_2013_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pase_2013-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aguiar_and_Borowski_2013_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Aguiar_and_Borowski_2013-5">

        </span>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
       Description
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopa_monnieri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bacopa_monnieri_W_IMG_1612.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="607" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Bacopa_monnieri_W_IMG_1612.jpg/190px-Bacopa_monnieri_W_IMG_1612.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Bacopa_monnieri_W_IMG_1612.jpg/285px-Bacopa_monnieri_W_IMG_1612.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Bacopa_monnieri_W_IMG_1612.jpg/380px-Bacopa_monnieri_W_IMG_1612.jpg 2x" width="190"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bacopa_monnieri_W_IMG_1612.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <i>
         Bacopa monnieri
        </i>
        in
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyderabad,_India" title="Hyderabad, India">
         Hyderabad, India
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">
       leaves
      </a>
      of this plant are succulent, oblong and 46 millimeters thick. Leaves are oblanceolate and are arranged oppositely on the stem. The
      <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">
       flowers
      </a>
      are small and white, with four or five petals. Its ability to grow in water makes it a popular
      <a href="/wiki/Aquarium" title="Aquarium">
       aquarium
      </a>
      plant. It can even grow in slightly
      <a href="/wiki/Salinity" title="Salinity">
       brackish
      </a>
      conditions. Propagation is often achieved through cuttings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Purdue_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Purdue-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">
       Ecology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopa_monnieri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      It commonly grows in
      <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">
       marshy
      </a>
      areas throughout
      <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
       India
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">
       Nepal
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">
       Sri Lanka
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
       China
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">
       Pakistan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">
       Taiwan
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">
       Vietnam
      </a>
      . It is also found in
      <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">
       Florida
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">
       Hawaii
      </a>
      and other southern states of the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      where it can be grown in damp conditions by a pond or bog garden.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This plant can be grown hydroponically.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Traditional_uses">
       Traditional uses
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopa_monnieri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Traditional uses">

     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Ayurveda" title="Ayurveda">
       Ayurvedic treatment
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Epilepsy" title="Epilepsy">
       epilepsy
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">
       asthma
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rajani_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-rajani-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is also used in Ayurveda for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulcers" title="Ulcers">
       ulcers
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tumors" title="Tumors">
       tumors
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ascites" title="Ascites">
       ascites
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enlarged_spleen" title="Enlarged spleen">
       enlarged spleen
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Indigestion" title="Indigestion">
       indigestion
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Inflammation" title="Inflammation">
       inflammations
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Leprosy" title="Leprosy">
       leprosy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Anemia" title="Anemia">
       anemia
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biliousness" title="Biliousness">
       biliousness
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Purdue_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Purdue-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Nomenclature">
       Nomenclature
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopa_monnieri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Nomenclature">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <i>
       Brahmi
      </i>
      is also the name given to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Centella_asiatica" title="Centella asiatica">
        Centella asiatica
       </a>
      </i>
      , particularly in
      <a href="/wiki/North_India" title="North India">
       North India
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">
       Kerala
      </a>
      where it is also identified in
      <a href="/wiki/Malayalam" title="Malayalam">
       Malayalam
      </a>
      as
      <i>
       muttil
      </i>
      () or
      <i>
       kodakan
      </i>
      . This identification of
      <i>
       brhm
      </i>
      as
      <i>
       C.asiatica
      </i>
      has been in use for long in northern India, as Hmdri's Commentary on Agahdaya (yuvdarasyana) treats
      <i>
       makapa
      </i>
      (
      <i>
       C.asiatica
      </i>
      ) as a synonym of
      <i>
       brahmi
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Warrier_et_al_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Warrier_et_al-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      although that may be a case of mistaken identification that was introduced during the 16th century.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bacopa monnieri was initially described around the 6th century A.D. in texts such as the Charaka Samhita, Atharva-Veda, and Susrut Samhita as a medhya rasayanaclass herb taken to sharpen intellect and attenuate mental deficits. The herb was allegedly used by ancient Vedic scholars to memorize lengthy sacred hymns and scriptures.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_constituents">
       Chemical constituents
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopa_monnieri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chemical constituents">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The best characterized compounds in
      <i>
       Bacopa monnieri
      </i>
      are
      <a href="/wiki/Dammarane" title="Dammarane">
       dammarane
      </a>
      -type
      <a href="/wiki/Triterpenoid" title="Triterpenoid">
       triterpenoid
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saponins" title="Saponins">
       saponins
      </a>
      known as
      <a href="/wiki/Bacoside" title="Bacoside">
       bacosides
      </a>
      , with
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jujubogenin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jujubogenin (page does not exist)">
       jujubogenin
      </a>
      or
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pseudo-jujubogenin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pseudo-jujubogenin (page does not exist)">
       pseudo-jujubogenin
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Moiety_(chemistry)" title="Moiety (chemistry)">
       moieties
      </a>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Aglycone" title="Aglycone">
       aglycone
      </a>
      units.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bacosides comprise a family of 12 known
      <a href="/wiki/Structural_analog" title="Structural analog">
       analogs
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Other saponins called
      <a href="/wiki/Bacopaside" title="Bacopaside">
       bacopasides
      </a>
      IXII have been identified more recently.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Alkaloid" title="Alkaloid">
       alkaloids
      </a>
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brahmine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brahmine (page does not exist)">
       brahmine
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nicotine" title="Nicotine">
       nicotine
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Herpestine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Herpestine (page does not exist)">
       herpestine
      </a>
      have been catalogued, along with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D-mannitol" title="D-mannitol">
       D-mannitol
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Apigenin" title="Apigenin">
       apigenin
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hersaponin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hersaponin (page does not exist)">
       hersaponin
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Monnieraside&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Monnieraside (page does not exist)">
       monnierasides
      </a>
      IIII,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cucurbitacins" title="Cucurbitacins">
       cucurbitacin
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Plantainoside&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Plantainoside (page does not exist)">
       plantainoside
      </a>
      B.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The constituent most studied has been
      <a href="/wiki/Bacoside_A" title="Bacoside A">
       bacoside A
      </a>
      , which was found to be a blend of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bacoside_A3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bacoside A3 (page does not exist)">
       bacoside A3
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopacide_II&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bacopacide II (page does not exist)">
       bacopacide II
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopasaponin_C&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bacopasaponin C (page does not exist)">
       bacopasaponin C
      </a>
      , and a jujubogenin isomer of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bacosaponin_C&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bacosaponin C (page does not exist)">
       bacosaponin C
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      These assays have been conducted using whole plant extract, and bacoside concentrations may vary depending upon the part from which they are extracted. In one
      <i>
       Bacopa monnieri
      </i>
      sample, Rastogi et al. found this bacoside profilebacopaside I (5.37%), bacoside A3 (5.59%), bacopaside II (6.9%), bacopasaponin C isomer (7.08%), and bacopasaponin C (4.18%).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Research">
       Research
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopa_monnieri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Research">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Bacopa monnieri
      </i>
      displays
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/In_vitro" title="In vitro">
        in vitro
       </a>
      </i>
      <a href="/wiki/Antioxidant" title="Antioxidant">
       antioxidant
      </a>
      and cell-protective effects.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In animals, it also inhibits
      <a href="/wiki/Acetylcholinesterase" title="Acetylcholinesterase">
       acetylcholinesterase
      </a>
      , activates
      <a href="/wiki/Choline_acetyltransferase" title="Choline acetyltransferase">
       choline acetyltransferase
      </a>
      , and increases cerebral blood flow.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Several studies have suggested that
      <i>
       Bacopa monnieri
      </i>
      extracts may have protective effects in animal models of
      <a href="/wiki/Neurodegeneration" title="Neurodegeneration">
       neurodegeneration
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dhanasekaran_22-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-dhanasekaran-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Small clinical trials in humans have found limited evidence supporting improved free memory recall, with no evidence supporting other cognition-enhancing effects.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pase_2013_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pase_2013-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Toxicology">
       Toxicology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bacopa_monnieri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Toxicology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Aqueous extracts of
      <i>
       Bacopa monnieri
      </i>
      may have reversible adverse effects on
      <a href="/wiki/Spermatogenesis" title="Spermatogenesis">
       spermatogenesis
      </a>
      , sperm count, and fertility in male mice.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Singh2009_23-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Singh2009-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The most commonly reported adverse side effects of
      <i>
       Bacopa monnieri
      </i>
      in humans are nausea, increased intestinal motility, and gastrointestinal upset.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary.2C_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable secondary sources. (January 2014)">
          non-primary source needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
