                     <br/>
                     <h4 style="text-align: center;">
                      Travel Info (What to do upon arrival):
                     </h4>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Once landed in
                     <b>
                      Gran Canaria
                     </b>
                     you can choose to reach your destination in many ways:
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       <b>
                        Bus
                       </b>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <b>
                        Transfer from the airport
                       </b>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <b>
                        Taxis
                       </b>
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       <b>
                        <a href="http://clkuk.tradedoubler.com/click?p=74392&amp;a=2471838&amp;g=17436198&amp;url=http://banner.nonstoppartner.de/common/banner.php?i=click&amp;client=autoeurope&amp;camp=tradedoubler&amp;l=it&amp;nw=a0pf&amp;tdurl=http://www.auto-europe.co.uk/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                         Car Rental
                        </a>
                       </b>
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Bus:
                     </b>
                     The bus stop is located in the departure terminal (salidas comunitarias). As soon as you leave  the baggage claim you have to go up one floor (with the escalator or elevator) and follow the signs Buses or, as they are called in the Canary Islands, "Guaguas".
                     <br/>
                     You can buy the ticket directly on the bus, or if you have time, at the ticket machine that is in front of the bus stop.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     To get to the tourist areas, in the south of the island, you have to take the bus 66 or 91 or 90.
                     <br/>
                     To get to
                     <a href="http://www.latanadelserpente.it/p/contatti.html" target="_blank">
                      my apartment in Puerto Rico
                     </a>
                     you have to
                     <b>
                      take the bus 91
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     The bus line 91, leaves from the capital of the island (Las Palmas), makes a stop at the airport and up to Playa del Cura, stopping also in the tourist centers of Puerto Rico and Amadores.
                     <br/>
                     The agency's Global bus provides a regular service throughout the day from 6:15 am to 20:15 pm.
                     <a href="http://www.globalsu.net/en/lineas.php" target="_blank">
                      Click here
                     </a>
                     to view and download all the lines and the buses schedules updated.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span id="docs-internal-guid-aefb3c3a-f0c0-da89-d3dc-6f2874ed7985">
                      <span style="background-color: white; font-family: Verdana; font-size: 16px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">

                     <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.mydatafocus.GuaguaBus&amp;hl=it" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

                     <h4 style="text-align: center;">
                     </h4>
                     <h4 style="text-align: center;">
                      Airport Transfers
                     </h4>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     If you prefer organizing everything from home before leaving, you can lean to a company where you can hire "car and driver". Upon your arrival the driver will be waiting in front of the arrivals door. You will recognize him because he will hold up a sign with your surname on it.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I used this service during my first trip to Gran Canaria to reach the airport. Unfortunately, I left early, at 5 am, and the bus there was still active so I booked a private transfer with driver. When I left the resort where I was staying, the driver was there waiting for me. The advantage of this option is that you already know how much you spend and there are no unpleasant surprises or price increases on the agreed price.
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.shuttledirect.com/en/php/airport_shuttle_direct.php?o_airport=LPA" target="_blank">
                      Click here
                     </a>
                     to be directed to the page of the shuttle direct transfers or here to
                     <a href="http://clkuk.tradedoubler.com/click?p=74392&amp;a=2471838&amp;g=17436198&amp;url=http://banner.nonstoppartner.de/common/banner.php?i=click&amp;client=autoeurope&amp;camp=tradedoubler&amp;l=it&amp;nw=a0pf&amp;tdurl=http://www.auto-europe.co.uk/chauffsplash.cfm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      AutoEurope Transfer e chauffeure service
                     </a>

                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <h4 style="text-align: center;">
                      TAXI
                     </h4>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     If you arrive in a time not covered by guaguas lines (buses), you have the option of taking a taxi or rent a car. The taxi ranks are located in all the arrivals terminal (EU and others).
                     <br/>
                     Out of the exit door, you will see a row of taxis. Head to the first in line, show the address or tell to the taxi driver the name of the resort where you will stay and... enjoy the landscape while the driver takes you to your destination.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     If you want to do a rough calculation of transport costs
                     <a href="http://www.taxigrancanaria.com/en/price-transfers-gran-canaria.php" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      click here
                     </a>
                     and you'll get an idea using the online calculator.

                     <h4 style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       Public Transportation
                      </b>
                     </h4>
                     <div>
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     In case you want to take a trip to Las Palmas (the capital), but you do not trust to drive in the busy city, you can opt for a tour of the famous city
                     <a href="http://www.city-sightseeing.com/" target="_blank">
                      sightseeing bus
                     </a>
                     , hop on-hop off.
                     <br/>
                     The alternative is to use the
                     <a href="http://www.guaguas.com/" target="_blank">
                      Municipal Buses
                     </a>
                     and buy travel tickets prepaid.
                     <br/>
                     Gran Canaria has a
                     <b>
                      bus service "Global"
                     </b>
                     all around the resorts, from the resorts to the major towns, around and between the towns. You can rely on the public bus system to get you around most of the island. The service that goes into the center of the island and the smaller villages however is very infrequent and I wouldn't recommend relying on a service that isn't from one town or resort to another.
                     <br/>
                     Global also have a
                     <a href="http://www.globalsu.net/" target="_blank">
                      website
                     </a>
                     in Spanish, English and German which shows you which service to catch for which resort, the bus timetable, the fare and more.

                     <b>
                      Shopping Center El Tablero

Anyway in the tourist resorts there are various local hotspots to connect to wi-fi service. It's not free but it is a paid service. The prices vary according to the local manager, however, to get an idea of prices, I write the rates of 2 Wifi hotspot that I receive from my balcony:

                     <br/>
                     <h4 style="text-align: center;">
                      Weather
                     </h4>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     You may not know that the climate of the Canary Islands is considered one of
                     <a href="http://www.thebestclimateintheworld.com/" target="_blank">
                      the best in the world
                     </a>
                     according to studies carried out by universities and climate experts.
                     <br/>
                     The air is clear and dry for the duration of the year, the little pollution, the absence of changes in temperature has a positive effect on health, especially on
                     <b>
                      joint pain and respiratory disorders.
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <br/>
                     </b>
                     If you want to live in the perfect climate and try on your skin what it means to be always in spring, see the benefits it can give you, the easiest solution is to go to Puerto Rico and discover the beauty of the island climate. I strongly recommend the south-west area of Gran Canaria, at least in winter, which remains the most warm and protected from the wind.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The temperature of the seawater is also agreeably warm, this together with the 2,700 hours of sunshine means that you can truly get the most out of your days down on the beach. The magnificent weather of Gran Canaria makes it into the
                     <a href="http://www.grancanaria.com/patronato_turismo/Sports.360.0.html" target="_blank">
                      ideal place for all kinds of open-air sports
                     </a>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     People often make the mistake of thinking that if the winter weather is so mild that the summers must be suffocating hot. This is not correct, thanks to the Trade Winds, the summer in the Canary Islands is mild thanks to the sea breezes and not at all stuffy or intolerable with temperatures around 26-29 degrees just perfect for endless days down on the beach.
