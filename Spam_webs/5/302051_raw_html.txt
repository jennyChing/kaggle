                     Future Richest Man in the world
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">

                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7060149300627527695" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <span>
                      <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
                       <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                        All of us know that the current richest man in the world is Bill Gate but do you know who is the future richest man in the world? Well, Zhang Jian, a China netizen has been proclaiming that he is the future richest man in the world. Zhang Jian runs his company called Yun Shu Mao which is a multi-level-marketing (MLM) company and the companys ads on the internet tell people that big monies can be earned and people can drive fancy cars without having to work. Zhang Jians tagline goes something of creating millionaires around the world. Investors or members can get an income of between RM$2,700 to RM$6,800 monthly without much efforts by merely investing RM$300 in the MLM scheme. Stock returns through e-shares and luxury items like BMW cars are also promised to members via lucky draws.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <span>
                      <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
                       <span style="font-size: x-large;">
                        <br/>
                        The face of this future millionaire from China has also been appearing in billboards in Malaysisa promoting his get-rich-quick scheme. Malaysias newspapers have also reported that Zhang Jians company has been banned in China and Zhang Jian has been hauled up by police in China five times.
                        <br/>
                        <br/>
                        Today (29 Oct 2014), according to Thailand newspapers, Zhang Jian was arrested by local police over a pyramid scheme that reportedly duped people in China and Malaysia of 6 S$235 million) and S$117 million respectively.
                        <br/>
                        <br/>
                        A caveat to all readers: if something seems too good to be true, often it is. What a Future Richest Man for Zhang Jian!
