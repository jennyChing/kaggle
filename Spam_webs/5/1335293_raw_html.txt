              Which prompts at least one intriguing lineup question this week. With defenceman Roman Polak set to return from a knee injury, coach Randy Carlyle will have to take one of his best of late, Korbinian Holzer, out of the lineup. A physical defenceman for a physical defenceman seems a simple enough swap, but in his 10-game run, Holzer has been adding some points of late.
             </p>
             <p>
              As Toronto is getting fine two-way play from its fourth line, dont expect Carlyle to use seven defencemen, although he has the option to sit the sometimes flummoxed Jake Gardiner to see what Holzer and Polak look like as a shut-down pairing.
             </p>
             <p>
              Roman was a big part of our penalty killing and added stiffness to our back end, as Holzer has done, Carlyle said on Wednesday. Those are going to be tough decisions with people currently in your lineup that have had success.
             </p>
             <p>
              Carlyle called Polak a game-time decision for Thursday in Carolina, while a stat was produced that the Leafs have a record of 19-10-3 with Holzer in the lineup this year and in 2012-13.
             </p>
             <p>
              Holzer, who felt he didnt make the most of that first 22-game NHL stint a couple of years ago, has shown some tremendous poise. Long playoff runs with the AHL Marlies the past three years taught the big German a lot about performing under pressure. But he didnt want to get into lineup speculation now that Polak is coming back.
             </p>
             <p>
              I just focus on hockey and the rest will take care of itself, Holzer said. Im just trying to play my game and help the team win. So far its been a good, solid 10-game stint for me.
             </p>
