         <p>
          <script src="http://KDFW.images.worldnow.com/interface/js/WNVideo.js?rnd=74540;hostDomain=www.myfoxdfw.com;playerWidth=620;playerHeight=355;isShowIcon=true;clipId=8422398;flvUri=;partnerclipid=;adTag=News;advertisingZone=;enableAds=true;landingPage=;islandingPageoverride=false;playerType=STANDARD_EMBEDDEDscript;controlsType=overlay" type="text/javascript">

          <a href="http://www.myfoxdfw.com" title=" Dallas News | myFOXdfw.com">
           Dallas News | myFOXdfw.com
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          An armed elderly man in Dallas shot and killed a suspected burglar after finding him trying to break into his Dallas home this week. However, he was also forced to defend his actions after the would-be robbers family showed up at his home.
         </p>
         <p>
          The homeowner, a repeat burglary victim, found 33-year-old Deyfon Pipkins attempting to climb in the house through the window and fired at least one shot at the criminal, hitting his target.
         </p>
         <p>
          Dallas police Sgt. Calvin Johnson
          <a href="http://www.myfoxdfw.com/story/21237261/homeowner-kills-intruder-in-oak-cliff-home">
           told KDFW-TV
          </a>
          that the homeowner is covered by the so-called Castle Doctrine, which provides legal protection to gun owners in Texas and other states who are forced to use deadly force against a home intruder.
         </p>
         <p>
          It means they dont actually have to retreat once someone comes in their home, Johnson said. You have the option of using deadly force if you believe your life is in danger.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_531153" style="width: 620px">
          <a href="http://www.theblaze.com/stories/2013/02/20/another-reason-why-its-a-bad-idea-to-break-into-a-texas-home-armed-elderly-man-kills-home-intruder/21237261_bg1/" rel="attachment">
           <img alt="Armed Elderly Man Kills Home Intruder in Dallas" class="size-large wp-image-531153" height="348" itemid="http://www.theblaze.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/21237261_BG1-620x348.jpg" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" src="http://www.theblaze.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/21237261_BG1-620x348.jpg" title="home intruder" width="620"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           KDFW-TV
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Once police identified the suspect as Pipkins, officers notified his family. Some of those family members
          <a href="http://www.myfoxdfw.com/story/21237261/homeowner-kills-intruder-in-oak-cliff-home">
           showed up at the house
          </a>
          and expressed their anger at the homeowner for not issuing a warning.
         </p>
         <p>
          He could have used a warning, Lakesha Thompson, Pipkins sister-in-law, said. He could have let him know that he did have a gun on his property and he would use it in self-defense.
         </p>
         <p>
          The suspect reportedly had a extensive criminal record and had served time in prison. He was previously convicted of theft, possession of a controlled substance and criminal trespassing.
         </p>
         <p>
          Police have not charged the homeowner with any crime, but Dallas police will now refer the case to the Grand Jury to determine whether he followed the law when he killed the home intruder.
         </p>
