              Action Points
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="ap-content">
             <ul>
              <li>
               Note that this study was published as an abstract and presented at a conference. These data and conclusions should be considered to be preliminary until published in a peer-reviewed journal.
              </li>
              <li>
               Note that this retrospective study demonstrated higher rates of influenza in patients with inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) compared with matched controls.
              </li>
              <li>
               Be aware that guidelines already suggest vaccinating all patients with IBD.
              </li>

          <p>
           SAN DIEGO  --  Patients with inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) have significantly higher rates of influenza than the greater population, a researcher said here.
          </p>
          <p>
           In a retrospective review, IBD patients had rates of influenza that were 1.6 times higher than controls (incidence rate ratio 1.58, 95% CI 1.49-1.68), reported
           <a href="https://www.doximity.com/pub/andrew-tinsley-md" target="_blank">
            Andrew Tinsley, MD
           </a>
           , of the Penn State Hershey IBD Center at the
           <a href="http://gi.org/education-and-meetings/acg-annual-meeting-and-postgraduate-course/" target="_blank">
            annual meeting of the American College of Gastroenterology.
           </a>
          </p>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-left desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           Outbreaks and epidemics of influenza A and B typically occur between the late fall and early spring, and during the past 100 years there have been several pandemics, the most recent being in 2009 caused by a novel H1N1 virus, he said.
          </p>
          <p>
           Chronic illness, older age, and weakened immune systems are thought to be risk factors for influenza and related complications such as pneumonia and hospitalizations.
          </p>
          <p>
           "Our GI professional guidelines include recommendations that we vaccinate our patients against influenza annually, but the incidence of this respiratory illness in patients with IBD remains unclear," Tinsley said.
          </p>
          <p>
           To estimate this, he and his colleagues looked at claims between January 2008 and December 2011 in the
           <a href="http://marketscan.truvenhealth.com/marketscanportal/" target="_blank">
            MarketScan health claims database
           </a>
           , which includes 38 million individuals and reflects inpatient, outpatient, and pharmacy data.
          </p>
          <p>
           For each of the 140,480 patients with ulcerative colitis or Crohn's disease identified, they chose a control matched for age and sex.
          </p>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-left tablet-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           Mean age was 43, and there was a slight female predominance.
          </p>
          <p>
           Most of the patients were on medications such as corticosteroids, immunosuppressives, biologics, and calcineurin inhibitors.
          </p>
          <p>
           Overall, the incidence of influenza among patients with IBD was approximately 700 per 100,000 patient-years.
          </p>
          <p>
           In contrast, the incidence among controls was 450 per 100,000 patient-years.
          </p>
          <p>
           The incidence was higher in the pediatric and young adult groups compared with older age groups, and in the Crohn's disease subgroup compared with the ulcerative colitis group.
          </p>
          <div class="tablet-ad-wrap-container">
           <div class="ad-slot ad-right tablet-ad">
           </div>
           <div class="ad-slot ad-right tablet-ad">
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           The reason for the increased risk among younger patients likely related to the unusual 2009 outbreak, which preferentially affected the young.
          </p>
          <p>
           After calculating the incidence rate ratio, Tinsley used a proportional hazards model to generate hazard ratios that would account for confounding factors such as comorbid illness, the use of immunosuppressive medications, influenza vaccine status, and healthcare utilization during the previous 6 months.
          </p>
          <p>
           The risk remained significantly higher, with a hazard ratio of 1.28 (95% CI 1.19-1.38) after adjustment.
          </p>
          <p>
           The researchers also found that complications such as pneumonia and hospitalization within 30 days of the influenza diagnosis were higher among IBD patients than controls.
          </p>
          <p>
           This analysis did have limitations, including reliance on a commercial administrative database, which can be associated with misclassification of exposure and outcome variables, and the exclusion of Medicare and Medicaid patients.
          </p>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right tablet-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           "Nonetheless, it did seem that in this large group of patients, influenza risk was increased in patients with IBD," he said.
          </p>
          <p>
           More studies will be needed to further assess the influence of medications and vaccination status on risk, Tinsley noted.
          </p>
          <p>
           "It's already flu season, so make sure you get your flu vaccine and give it to your patients," he added.
          </p>
