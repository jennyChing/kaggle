      <div class="map cfix">
       <div class="media-map" id="mapContainer">
        <a class="gaTrackable" data-gaaction="Article top promo 940x50 click" data-gacategory="Ultimate Footy Promos" data-galabel="Article 940x50 v2" href="/ultimatefooty" sl-processed="1" title="Ultimate Footy, the ultimate fantasy football afl experience">

        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       At last year's Hawthorn best and fairest count, champion midfielder Sam Mitchell described Max Bailey's ability to overcome injury and his generosity of spirit as the greatest story of the club's 2013 premiership team.
      </p>
      <p>
       But a new book has revealed just how close Bailey was to enduring yet more heartbreak and having his premiership dream erased, having been told after round 14 last year his twice-reconstructed right knee may not survive the season.
      </p>
      <p>
       Scans had shown a major deterioration in the knee. So serious was the situation that fitness boss Andrew Russell told coach Alastair Clarkson there was no guarantee Bailey would play out the season.
      </p>
      <p>
       ''In fact, there's a reasonable chance at this point he won't get through the season,'' Russell said.
      </p>
      <div class="ad adAlignRight" id="adspot-300x250-pos3" style="display:none;">
       <small>
        Advertisement
       </small>
       <script>
        titan.requestAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       <em>
        Playing to Win
       </em>
       , written by senior Fairfax Media journalist Michael Gordon, touches on Bailey's story in a season where the Hawks were determined to avenge their 2012 grand final defeat.
      </p>
      <p>
       Bailey initially struggled to deal with the results of the scan but would manage 19 matches, a career-high in terms of a single season, and would play in all three finals.
      </p>
      <p>
       ''There was a period where he withdrew into himself and then something clicked and he was almost a different person.'' Russell said in the book. ''It happened overnight. You could see that he had dealt with the fact that he wasn't going to play again  and decided: 'I'm going to enjoy this, and I'm going to find a way to finish the season' - and he did.''
      </p>
      <p>
       Gordon was given unprecedented access into the Hawks' inner sanctum, detailing the changes they made on and off the field in a bid to claim a second premiership under Clarkson. This included how the Hawks embraced the ethos of the US Navy SEALs before last season.
      </p>
      <p>
       Gordon wrote: ''No player in the competition was more qualified for the Navy SEALs' descriptor, 'Forged by adversity', yet Bailey's capacity to endure hardship and focus on doing whatever was required to reach his goals was matched by his ability to roll with the punches, accept the hand he had been dealt and find positives out of disappointments and even tragedy.''
      </p>
      <p>
       Bailey would retire in the days after the premiership, his career restricted by injury to just 43 matches after being a first-round draft pick in 2005.
      </p>
