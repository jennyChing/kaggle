        <p>
         <strong>
          Debt freedom
         </strong>
          its a pretty cool thing! But if youre in a spot where it look like a really far away dream, dont give up! We have some very realistic ideas for you today on becoming
         <strong>
          debt free
         </strong>
         !
        </p>
        <h3>
         <a href="http://www.therealisticmama.com/ways-to-save-money/" title="Creative Ways to Save Money &amp; Make Money">
          Save money
         </a>
         on the small stuff
        </h3>
        <p>
         Small stuff adds up way too quickly!A whole bunch of small spending can be way worse than large spending because most of the time you dont realize how much you are spending.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.therealisticmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tons-of-small-ways-to-save-money-in-this-post.jpg">
          <img alt="Tons of small ways to save money in this post" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3297" height="985" src="http://www.therealisticmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Tons-of-small-ways-to-save-money-in-this-post.jpg" width="650"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Make coffee at home
         </li>
         <li>
          Make your own homemade creamer
         </li>
         <li>
          Eat leftovers for lunch
         </li>
         <li>
          Rent a movieinstead of going to the movie theater
          <em>
           (or even better see what movies your friends own and borrow from them!)
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          Instead of paying a babysitter, trade babysitting days with another mom
         </li>
         <li>
          Stretch your haircuts a little further apart
         </li>
         <li>
          Watch a video tutorial on how to cut your husbands hair
         </li>
         <li>
          Meal plan instead of throwing together last minute meals
          <em>
           (youll be shocked at how much time and money this will save! Stop by our
           <a href="http://www.therealisticmama.com/recipes/">
            recipes page
           </a>
           if you need inspiration.)
          </em>
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: left;">
          Have a date night at home.
          <em>
           {See this list of
           <a href="http://www.therealisticmama.com/175-fun-date-ideas/" target="_blank" title="175+ Fun Date Ideas">
            175 Inexpensive Date Ideas
           </a>
           including some at home dates.}
           <img alt="175 Cheap Date Ideas!" class="aligncenter wp-image-2480" height="470" src="http://www.therealisticmama.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/175-Cheap-Date-Ideas-.jpg" width="400"/>
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          Skip the huge, stressful vacation and opt for a bunch of
          <a href="http://www.therealisticmama.com/mini-vacations/" target="_blank" title="How to take mini vacations with your family">
           mini vacations
          </a>
          instead.
         </li>
         <li>
          See the complete list of ways to
          <a href="http://www.therealisticmama.com/ways-to-save-money/">
           spend less money AND ways to make money {from home} here
          </a>
          .
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3 style="text-align: left;">
         After you save transferthat money right away!
        </h3>
        <p>
         Congrats! You stopped yourself from spending $4.45 on a mocha  awesome! Now move that money right away to a savings account.I love using my mobile banking for this but you can also make yourself a note and transfer the money when you get home or on your next visit to the bank.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive also used this trick if a bill, like our electric, is a little lower one month, Ill transfer the difference right away into our savings.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Consolidate your debt into one payment
        </h3>
        <p>
         Combine your credit card balances into one payment.You can then use the money in the savings account

          (from above)
