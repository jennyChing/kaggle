        <div class="storytext leftrail-indent">
         <p>
          There is a ton of buzz surrounding Actifio today, and for good reason:
          <a href="http://betaboston.com/news/2014/03/24/actifio-funding-ipo-valuation-billion-dollar-boston-tech/" target="_blank">
           the company just raised a $100 million round of funding and has been valued at $1 billion
          </a>
          . That sets the stage for an IPO likely planned for next year.
         </p>
         <p>
          So what exactly does Actifio do? This is how the company was recently described on Twitter by chief marketing officer Mike Troiano:
         </p>
         <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-partner="tweetdeck">
          <p>
           Use whatever data you need, whenever it was created, wherever you need to. Thats
           <a href="https://twitter.com/actifio" target="_blank">
            @actifio
           </a>
           , in a tweet.
          </p>
          <p>
            Michael Troiano (@miketrap)
           <a href="https://twitter.com/miketrap/statuses/446726948749066241" target="_blank">
            March 20, 2014
           </a>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <!--more-->
         </p>
         <p>
          One most interesting things about
          <a href="http://www.actifio.com/" target="_blank">
           Actifio
          </a>
          is some of the fascinating people involved in building the potential unicorn.
         </p>
         <div class="ad-inside-content">
          <div class="adsense-wrapper bigbox">
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          Prior to starting Actifio, chief executive Ash Ashutosh founded ApplQ, a company that
          <a href="http://idm.net.au/node/2853" target="_blank">
           specialized in
          </a>
          open storage area network management and storage resource management technology. ApplQ was acquired by Hewlett-Packard in 2005, and Ashutosh went on to work for HPs
          <a href="http://www8.hp.com/us/en/products/data-storage/" target="_blank">
           StorageWorks
          </a>
          devision.
         </p>
         <p>
          After HP, Ashutosh joined Greylock Partners, the well-known Palo Alto venture capital firm. He left Greylock and founded Actifio in 2009.
         </p>
         <p>
          Another intriguing figure at Actifio is Mike Troiano, who recently told me how he connected with the company in the first place. The anecdote is a great example of how best to leverage your network and friends to land your dream job.
         </p>
         <p>
          Troiano is a major player in the Boston tech community; he is the first entrepreneur (read: non-VC) to join the New England Venture Capital Associations board of directors.
         </p>
         <p>
          The road that led him to Actifio took him through NEVCA and made Troiano appreciate what separates Boston from other tech hubs: namely, its people.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           NEVCA Connects with the Community
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          I went to Harvard Business School with a guy named James Nahirny, Troiano explained in a recent sit down. He is one of my best friends and he was at Bain Capital (now hes starting a private equity fund, Leerink Swann).
         </p>
         <p>
          [Nahirny] told me he was doing this thing called the
          <a href="http://www.newenglandvc.org/" target="_blank">
           New England Venture Capital Association
          </a>
          . He was the chairman and he said they really needed some help and were trying figure out a new direction.
         </p>
         <p>
          Troiano found out that James Goldstein (of
          <a href="http://www.nbvp.com/" target="_blank">
           North Bridge Venture Partners
          </a>
          ) was playing a key role at NEVCA and knew of him, so he wanted to get involved in some way.
         </p>
         <p>
          What struck me, at the time, was how utterly disconnected [NEVCA] was from the startup community. It was fun for what it was, but there was new leadership and they wanted to move it in the direction of being closer to the startup community.
         </p>
         <p>
          The next year, Jamie took over and had more specific idea on how to integrate the Venture Capital community with the startup community; the guys with money and the guys with ideas. He wanted to try to bring them together more. It really had a huge impact on NEVCA.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Helping NEVCA leads to Actifio
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Troiano work for HollandMark at the time and ended up helping with NEVCA, which eventually led to an opportunity at Actifio:
         </p>
         <p>
          While I was at Holland Mark, we did a whole lot for [NEVCA]brand identity, a new website, etc., all around this new mission, Troiano said.
         </p>
         <p>
          They were very grateful for the work and during the course of that Goldstein asked, What can I do for you?
         </p>
         <p>
          Troiano asked Goldstein if he could connect him with some of his startup-y portfolio companies, explaining he wanted to work in on something cooler.
         </p>
         <p>
          [Goldstein] said, you know Im invested in this company called Actifio. They are having a little bit of trouble figuring out how to tell their story, but it is an amazing team, in a huge space, and he said I should meet them.
         </p>
         <p>
          I met Ash Ashutosh and it took a couple of meetings to figure out what [Actifio] was, but after I did and I met Jim Sullivan, who runs sales, and Donna Williams, who runs services and support, and I was blown away.
         </p>
         <p>
          Troiano did some work with Actifio for HollandMark and was offered a job at Ashutoshs company. However, he said no at first.
         </p>
         <p>
          At one point, Troiano said, Ash approached me and said, the work youre doing is really great, but we need someone to come in here and lead the change  and you are the guy.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Actifio: Building a Legacy
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Before making anything official, Troiano needed to talk to Actifios board, which included Jit Saxena from Neteeza, Jamie Goldstein, Peter Levine from AndreessenHorowitz, and Bob Hower.
         </p>
         <p>
          I met with these guys, he explained, and they were like This is a once in a lifetime opportunity.'
         </p>
         <p>
          I met with Jit and he said, If you want to do something for the Boston startup community, build the next big technology brand here in Boston.
         </p>
         <p>
          I came onboard, Troiano explained, and it has been a rocket ride since.
         </p>
         <p>
          When you talk to Mike Troiano, youll probably end up talking about a myriad of different topics, from Springsteen, to great food, to an artists perspective on life. But one thing that stands out is how he finds a way to return to his life-changing decision to join Actifio.
         </p>
         <p>
          I always wanted to find something that I could have my name connected with and be proud of; the thing that would be my legacy, Troiano said before pausing for a brief, undisclosed thought.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is the thing, Actifio. This is going to be big, he concluded.
         </p>
