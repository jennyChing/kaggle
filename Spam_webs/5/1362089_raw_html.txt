        <h2 class="entry-title">
         Frankfurt, The German City That Doesn't Drink Beer
        </h2>
        <div id="where">
         <b>
          Where:
         </b>
         <a href="/city/Frankfurt">
          Frankfurt
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/country/de">
          Germany
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="date">
         <abbr class="updated published">
          August 23, 2013 at 2:32 PM
         </abbr>
         |
         <span class="vcard">
          by
          <a class="url fn n author" href="/user/wakeandwander" rel="author" title="View all posts by wakeandwander">
           wakeandwander
          </a>
         </span>
         |
         <a href="/story/2013/8/21/15470/2650#comment-block">
          Comments (0)
         </a>
        </div>
        <p class="entry-summary">
        </p>
        <p>
         <img class="top" src="http://www.jaunted.com/files/113893/IMG_5040.JPG"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The city of Frankfurt boasts the largest skyline in Europe (shown above in all its modesty), but that's not why it stands out amongst the crowd. No,
         <strong>
          this Germany city has an even bigger secret: It doesn't drink beer.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         We know what you're thinking. A German city that doesn't drink beer? Blasphemy! But no, seriously, it's true. In Frankfurt, it's
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apfelwein">
          apfelwein
         </a>
         (apple wine) that fills the glasses of the locals.
        </p>
        <p>
         Apfelwein is  made in the same way as wine -- by pressing apples and adding yeast -- but it tastes more like a cider and it drinks like a beer with an alcohol content of 4 to 7 percent. It is served to patrons in what's called a bembel (that blue and white pitcher in the photo below) and drank from a special glass called a
         <a href="https://www.google.com/search?q=geripptes&amp;rlz=1C1CHFX_enUS521US521&amp;tbm=isch&amp;tbo=u&amp;source=univ&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=azkWUtbLMYqqyQHG64HADg&amp;ved=0CCwQsAQ&amp;biw=1241&amp;bih=567">
          geripptes
         </a>
         , which has ridges cut into it to prevent it from slipping out of your hand (back in the day, the Germans ate pork and sausage with their hands while they drank, which made them greasy).
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="story-body">
        <p>
         <img class="top" src="http://www.jaunted.com/files/113893/applewine2.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The traditional varietal isn't sweet like Magner's or plain apple juice/cider. It's actually a little tart, very light and very easy to drink. Like wine and grapes, the different types of apples produce different styles of apfelwein, and so it's possible to find versions that have a higher sugar content.
        </p>
        <p>
         An apfelwein festival is held every year in the beginning of August, which is obviously a great time to pass through town. Producers come from the surrounding villages and rural areas to put their product on display, and there are of course the traditional food vendors slinging bratwurst and the like. If you aren't able to time your trip with the festival, head on over to the neighborhood of Sachsenhausen anytime of year for an apfelwein hall crawl. There are a lot apfelwein pubs amongst the small streets and half-timbered houses.
        </p>
        <p>
         Another option is to take a 20-minute ride outside the city limits and visit an actual apfelwien orchard --
         <strong>
          a beer garden that serves apfelwein
         </strong>
         , if you will. We visited
         <a href="http://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&amp;sl=de&amp;u=http://www.obsthof-am-steinberg.de/&amp;prev=/search%3Fq%3DObsthof%2Bam%2BSteinberg%26rlz%3D1C1CHFX_enUS521US521">
          <strong>
           Obsthof am Steinberg
          </strong>
         </a>
         and enjoyed the picnic-table seating under the trees, the apples just above our heads.
        </p>
        <p>
         Who would have thought... cider in Germany!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img class="top" src="http://www.jaunted.com/files/113893/applewine.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

        </p>
