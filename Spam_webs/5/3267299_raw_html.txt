      <h2>
       Eco-Friendly Spring Activities for Kids
      </h2>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       By
       <a href="http://scjohnson.com/en/family/family-economics/meet-the-bloggers.aspx" title="Diane Hoffmaster">

      <p>
       <img alt="take a hike" src="/Libraries/cc_blog_images_2012-13-b/Diane-Hoffmaster_Eco-Friendly-Spring-Activities-to-Do-with-Your-Kids_April2013.sflb.ashx" style="float: right; margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 15px;"/>
       During the cold, winter months it is often hard to find fun activities that keep kids busy. After being cooped up inside for much of the winter, I am looking forward to spending some time outdoors. If you are looking for a few creative ways to keep your older kids busy this spring, try one of my fun and eco-friendly suggestions below!
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        1. Plant a garden
       </strong>
       : Get your kids involved in gardening this spring. Take them to your local nursery and have them help you choose the plants and supplies you need. Next, spend a few hours in the yard getting dirty! If youre a first time gardener check out this post from fellow Blogger Panel Member Scarlet on 
       <a href="https://www.scjohnson.com/en/green-choices/Everyday-Alternatives/Articles/Article-Details.aspx?date=13-03-22&amp;title=10-Easy-Plants-To-Grow-For-First-Time-Gardeners" target="_blank" title="10 Easy Plants To Grow For First Time Gardeners">
        10 Easy Plants To Grow For First Time Gardeners
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        2. Take a hike
       </strong>
       : Take your kids into the woods to help them appreciate the nature around them and get some exercise! Go to
       <a href="http://www.trails.com/" target="_blank" title="http://www.trails.com/">
        http://www.trails.com/
       </a>
       to find a trail near you. Make sure to pack the right supplies, wear sturdy hiking shoes and for safety reasons, always let someone know where you will be going.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        3. Make a nature scavenger hunt
       </strong>
       : Make your kids a list of easy-to-find objects in your neighborhood and head out together to find them. For starters, add pine cones, acorns or leaves of specific colors to your list!
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        4. Learn about geocaching
       </strong>
       : Geocaching is a high-tech scavenger hunt using a map and a handheld GPS unit. It can be a very educational and fun way to spend the afternoon. For more information on Geocaching visit
       <a href="http://www.geocaching.com/" target="_blank" title="http://www.geocaching.com/">
        http://www.geocaching.com/
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        5. Have a picnic
       </strong>
       : Pack a cooler full of sandwiches, drinks, snacks and plenty of ice and take your lunch on the road to the nearest park, field, or even your backyard. Bring some books and enjoy a quiet story after you finish eating. To ensure that your picnic is as green as the grass you are sitting on, visit my post
       <a href="http://www.scjohnson.com/en/green-choices/Everyday-Alternatives/Articles/Article-Details.aspx?date=12-08-06&amp;title=Eco-Friendly-Picnics" target="_blank" title="here">
        here
       </a>
       !
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        6. Visit a pick-your-own produce farm
       </strong>
       : There are many farms that allow you to pick-your-own produce. This is a great way to spend the afternoon with your child and teach them about reducing their
       <a href="https://www.scjohnson.com/en/green-choices/Conserving-energy/Articles/Article-Details.aspx?date=13-04-08&amp;title=Tips-to-Eat-More-Local-This-Year" target="_blank" title="carbon footprint">
        carbon footprint
       </a>
       . When you get home, have your children help you wash and prepare the produce for your next meal. If you dont plan on using all your produce right away, store it in a
       <a href="http://www.ziploc.com/Products/Pages/FreezerBagsSmartZipSeal.aspx?SizeName=Pint" target="_blank" title="Ziploc Brand Freezer Bag with the Smart Zip Seal">
        Ziploc Brand Freezer Bag with the Smart Zip Seal
       </a>
       and place in the freezer for future use.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Spring is the perfect time to get your children outside and teach them about nature and the environment. What is your familys favorite spring activity?
       </strong>
      </p>
