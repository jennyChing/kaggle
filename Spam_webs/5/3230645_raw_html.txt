         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           As winter approaches, the first blankets of snow are falling on Earth.Travel the globe and visit these incredible cold-weather destinations.
          </span>
          <span id="more-526">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             1.
            </span>
            The ice chunks in Jkulsrln Lagoon, Iceland.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-1" class="aligncenter wp-image-528" height="684" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-1.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           This massive lagoon, located 230 miles east of Reykjavk, is one of Icelands best-known natural wonders. Water from the ocean flows in and out of the glacial lake with the tides, along with chunks of the glacier itself.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline">
             <span style="color: #000000;">
              2
             </span>
             .
            </span>
            The icy crust above Lake Huron, Michigan.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-2.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-2" class="aligncenter wp-image-531" height="1441" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-2.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           A fishing hole reveals the lake water below the frozen surface.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             3.
            </span>
            The giant redwoods of Sequoia National Park, California.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-3.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-3" class="aligncenter wp-image-532" height="683" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-3.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           The ochre tones in the gigantic tree trunks gleam in the sun against their wintry backdrop.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             4.
            </span>
            The frozen forest atop Levi fell in Finnish Lapland.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-4.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-4" class="aligncenter wp-image-535" height="410" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-4.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Blowing snow freezes to the trees in this fell (another name for a barren mountainside), creating these statuesque formations.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             5.
            </span>
            The cracked surface of Abraham Lake, Alberta, Canada.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-5.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-5" class="aligncenter wp-image-536" height="683" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-5.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           The clear ice looks like a massive sheet of glass.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             6.
            </span>
            The rainbow hot springs in Yellowstone National Park, Wyoming.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-6.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-6" class="aligncenter wp-image-537" height="683" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-6.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Bacteria living in the hot springs causes the rainbow striations in the water, which freezes in the winter to create colored snow.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
            7.
           </span>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            A frozen lake in Gates of the Arctic National Park, Alaska.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-7.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-7" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-538" height="661" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-7.jpg" width="990"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Gates of the Arctic is the true final frontier. The nature preserve is 8.4 million acres of unimproved land. No trails, no roads, no guides. For expert outdoorsmen only.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <strong>
           <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
            8.
           </span>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            A splintering glacier in Antarctica.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-8.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-8" class="aligncenter wp-image-539" height="860" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-8.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           The fissures zigzag in a ribbon pattern that defies the eye.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             9.
            </span>
            The Blue Ice Cave of Skaftafell, Iceland.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-9.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-9" class="aligncenter wp-image-540" height="635" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-9.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Ice caves are incredible natural formations that occur on the edge of glaciers. Once inside the cave, one feels as though they have been transported to a mysterious alien world. This particular cave is located in the Svnafellsjkull glacier in Skaftafell, Iceland.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
            10.
           </span>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            The sunset over Crater Lake, Oregon.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-10.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-10" class="aligncenter wp-image-541" height="683" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-10.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           White mountains and a glassy lake surface amplify incredible sunsets to create this spectacular effect.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             11.
            </span>
            The outer lighthouse on St. Joseph Pier, Michigan.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-11.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-11" class="aligncenter wp-image-543" height="576" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-11.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           During the bitterly cold winter months, lake-spray freezes on the structure to create incredible ice formations.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             12.
            </span>
            The frozen falls of Godafoss (Waterfall of the Gods), Iceland.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-12.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-12" class="aligncenter wp-image-544" height="683" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-12.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Godafoss plays an important role in Icelandic religious history. One look at the icicles dangling above the churning arctic pools, and you can see why.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            <span class="buzz_superlist_number_inline" style="color: #000000;">
             13.
            </span>
            And lastly: the Northern Lights in Alaska.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-13.jpg">
           <img alt="Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-13" class="aligncenter wp-image-545" height="683" src="http://naturelandscapes.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Top-13-Winter-Landscapes-You-Wont-Believe-Exist-On-Earth-13.jpg" width="1024"/>
          </a>
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           If winter had a crown and that crown had jewels, the Northern Lights would be those jewels. These undulating bands of color can be seen from many places across the globe, but Alaska provides some of the most spectacular views thanks to its high latitude.
          </span>
         </p>
