       <div class="post_body">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Design-Sponge-DIY-Clay-Platter-8.jpg">
          <img alt="Design Sponge DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220101 size-full" height="1050" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Design-Sponge-DIY-Clay-Platter-8.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         As Im getting older, Im beginning to discover how much I enjoy entertaining, whether its bringing friends and family together to celebrate special occasions or just making an everyday meal into more of a memorable moment. When I have plenty of preparation time its wonderful to put together decorations and create a beautifully styled table. But when time is limited to style the room, styling the food can turnan everyday meal into something really special.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Design-Sponge-DIY-Clay-Platter-7.jpg">
          <img alt="Design Sponge DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220100 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Design-Sponge-DIY-Clay-Platter-7.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Having beautiful and interesting plates and crockery make all the difference when youre styling your food. Showing a meal at its best will highlight the time and effort put in and immediately create a sense of occasion. Platter boards are a great way to present your meal on the table in a clean and inviting way, and your guests will gladly help themselves. Theyre also really simple to make from air-dry clay. Perfectfor your next dinner party. 
         <em>
          Fran
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-220030">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platters-Materials.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platters | Materials" class="alignnone wp-image-220047 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platters-Materials.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Materials
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Air-dry clay
         </li>
         <li>
          Clay roller
         </li>
         <li>
          Craft knife
         </li>
         <li>
          Sponge brush
         </li>
         <li>
          Dcopatch No. 2 ultra gloss sealing varnish /Decopatch VA90 Aquapro Professional Varnish
         </li>
         <li>
          Baking paper
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Directions
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Place a sheet of baking paper onto a solid surface. You need the baking sheet to stop the clay from sticking to any surface you use it on. It will make it easier to flip over whilst you roll out.
        </p>
        <p>
         Using a rolling pin, slowly and evenly roll the clay out into a large rectangle until its around 1/4 thick.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-1.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220031 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-1.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-2.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220032 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-2.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. With the craft knife, gently mark out the shape of the platter. Lightly skim the surface so if you make a mistake you can blend the clay back to a smooth surface.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-3.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220033 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-3.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-4.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220034 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-4.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Once youre happy with the shape and size of the platter, use the knife to slice all the way through the clay. Use the indented lines as a guide.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-5.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220035 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-5.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Smooth over any cracks with water, wetting the clay and running your finger over any imperfections.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-8.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220038 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-8.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-6.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220036 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-6.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         To give the platter crisp edges, run the flat edge of the craft knife along the wet clay, removing any excess as you go.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-9.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220039 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-9.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-10.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220040 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-10.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Use a straw or cylindrical object to create a hole for string on the handle of the platter.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-7.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220037 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-7.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now leave the platter board to dry out for 48 hours, turning it over after 24 hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-12.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220042 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-12.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Once the clay is dry and an even bright, white color it is ready to prepare for varnishing. Take a medium grade sandpaper and sand the edges and any imperfections over the surface of the platter.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-13.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220043 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-13.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         7. To varnish, Im usingDcopatch No. 2 ultra gloss sealing varnish/Decopatch VA90 Aquapro Professional Varnish. Its a water-based gloss varnish that can safely come into contact with food. Use the sponge brush to paint very thin layers onto the platter. Ive used a sponge brush because it will soak up any excess varnish that would otherwise leave a yellow tint. Keep the layers thin and light and leave them to fully dry between 4-5 coats.
        </p>
        <p>
         After the varnish is dry the platter board will wipe clean, but resist submerging it in water unless you want to see your lovely platter board disintegrate!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-14.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Clay Platter" class="alignnone wp-image-220044 size-full" height="467" src="http://www.designsponge.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/DIY-Clay-Platter-14.jpg" width="700"/>
