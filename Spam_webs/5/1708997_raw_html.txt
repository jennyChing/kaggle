      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       The Best First-Person Shooters Of 2014
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->
      <div class="mninst">
      </div>
      <div class="comment_links">
       <a class="scroll_to post_your_comment" href="#comment_reply">

       <div class="body_inner">
        <figure class="featured_image">
         <img alt="" itemprop="image" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2014/12/halo-mcc.jpg"/>
         <figcaption>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Like most years, 2014 was a pretty good year for first-person shooters. Some great games were released, and others have been teased for next year or released in Alpha.
        </p>
        <p>
         For the purposes of this list well look just at titles that launched this year as full gamesnot Early Release or Alpha or Pay for an unfinished game first just because! titles. Im also leaving out DLC and expansions, though I am including re-releases.
        </p>
        <p>
         Nor did I play every game out there, so if I missed something that you think is terrific and ought to be on the list, please let me know.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Runner-Up:
         </strong>
         Halo:
         <em>
          The Master Chief Collection
         </em>
         (Xbox One)
        </p>
        <p>
         Easily one of the best deals of the year, Xbox One owners were treated to fully remastered versions of
         <em>
          Halo: Combat Evolved, Halo 2, Halo 3,
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          Halo 4.
         </em>
         The collection comes with all the DLC and multiplayer modes as well. It all looks and plays great, but the release was marred by matchmaking issues.
        </p>
        <p>
         While its an amazing value no matter what, the matchmaking issues combined with the fact that this is a re-release rather than a brand-new
         <em>
          Halo
         </em>
         (
         <em>
          Halo 5
         </em>
         is coming next year) means Master Chief remains confined to the runners-up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Runner-Up:
         </strong>
         <em>
          Titanfall
         </em>
         (Xbox One, PC)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="titanfall" class=" size-full wp-image-28561" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2014/12/titanfall.jpg"/>
         <!--donotpaginate-->
         <em>
          Titanfall
         </em>
         is a lot of fun. It takes the
         <em>
          Call of Duty
         </em>
         formula, adds in parkour movement, bigger maps, and great big mechs, thenpits players against one another and a host of bots in various multiplayer matches. Movement in the game feels pretty great, though maybe not quite as open and freeing as a game like
         <em>
          Tribes: Ascend.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Unfortunately, theres no single-player campaign, and the multiplayer isnt quite strong enough on its own to make
         <em>
          Titanfall
         </em>
         rise above good to great. Its a solid first step toward something better, however, and especially fun for players new to twitch-shooters.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Runner-Up:
         </strong>
         <em>
          Destiny
         </em>
         (Xbox 360, Xbox One, PS3, PS4)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="destiny new sparrow" class=" size-full wp-image-28562" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2014/12/destiny-new-sparrow.jpg"/>
         <em>
          Destiny
         </em>
         also feels like a first step. While the game is gorgeous and the gameplay is solid, the games initial campaign felt gutted compared to what wed seen advertised.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a lot to love, especially for people who enjoy grinding and MMO fans, but a lack of matchmaking options and the limited story hold back an otherwise great game.
        </p>
        <p>
         For others, like myself, too much grinding and too thin a storynot to mentionlimited social options-makes
         <em>
          Destiny
         </em>
         looklike a work in progress rather than a finished game.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres potential here, but its not been fully realized yet.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Runner-Up:
         </strong>
         <em>
          Shadow Warrior (PS4, Xbox One,PC)
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Shadow Warrior" class=" size-full wp-image-28563" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2014/12/Shadow-Warrior.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The black humor, fast-paced gunfighting, and general irreverence of
         <em>
          Shadow Warrior
         </em>
         make it a ton of fun to play. Besides, you have an awesome katana and tons of demonic super-powers at your disposal. And limbs just go flying when you start swinging that blade. Its hilarious.
        </p>
        <p>
         The remake of the classic first-person shooter originally came to PC, but was released on new-gen systems in 2014. It plays great even with a gamepad, though youll never quite match a mouse-and-keyboard for accuracy. Then again, this isnt an accuracy-based shooter, its a spray-and-pray and hack-and-slash game thats far faster and weirder than any of its modern brethren.
        </p>
        <p>
         Still, like
         <em>
          The Master Chief Collection
         </em>
         , this is a re-release rather than a 2014 original, even if it is a great addition to the new-gen catalog.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          AWPMedal:
          <em>
          </em>
         </strong>
         <em>
          Call of Duty: Advanced Warfare
         </em>
         (Xbox 360, Xbox One, PS3, PS4, PC)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Call of Duty" class=" size-full wp-image-28564" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2014/12/Call-of-Duty.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Finally the
         <em>
          Call of Duty
         </em>
         formula has been changed, altered more drastically than any year since
         <em>
          Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Advanced Warfare
         </em>
         has a fun, if overly predictable, single-player campaign, a decent co-op mode, and terrific competitive multiplayer.
        </p>
        <p>
         Like
         <em>
          Titanfall
         </em>
         , the game focuses on new ways to move around a map, introducing double-jumps and dodge boosts and other movement options more closely associated with arena shootersthough this is not, by any stretch, an arena shooter. It retains the twitch-shooter DNA that have made the game so popular, but adds in more verticality and far more dynamic matches.
        </p>
        <p>
         The graphics are also outstanding, with some facial animation that defies anything on the market. All told, a solid third place.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Gravity GunMedal:
         </strong>
         <em>
          Wolfenstein: The New Order
         </em>
         <span>
          (Xbox 360, Xbox One, PS3, PS4, PC)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="wolfenstein" class=" size-full wp-image-28565" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2014/12/wolfenstein.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The first
         <em>
          Wolfenstein
         </em>
         game in years,
         <em>
          The New Order
         </em>
         is set decades after WWII in an alternative timeline in which the Nazis have won the war thanks to their industrial-mech technology.
        </p>
        <p>
         The game is single-player only, so that may turn off multiplayer gamers who would do better withreally any of the previously mentioned titles save for
         <em>
          Shadow Warrior
         </em>
         . But as a single-player only shooter its a lot of fun, mixing great level design with solid gameplay.
        </p>
        <p>
         The story is over-the-top and gritty and the voice-acting is fantastic. Graphically the game looks fantastic on new-gen machines and oozes style. In fact,
         <em>
          The New Order
         </em>
         is such a great solo shooter it was really a toss-up between it and.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          BFG9000Medal:
         </strong>
         <em>
          Far Cry 4
         </em>
         <span>
          (Xbox 360, Xbox One, PS3, PS4, PC)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Far Cry 4 (five)" class=" size-full wp-image-28566" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/erikkain/files/2014/12/Far-Cry-4-five.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         While its not a far cry from its predecessor,
         <em>
          Far Cry 4
         </em>
         is still the most fun Ive had with a first-person shooter all year. Its chalk full of things to do
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/insertcoin/2014/12/02/far-cry-4-and-the-problem-of-too-much-to-do/">
          perhaps too many things to do
         </a>
         but it has a terrific campaign, incredible online co-op, and some of the most striking visuals of any release this year.
        </p>
        <p>
         Capturing outposts, freeing hostages, storming fortresses, and interacting with the marvelous villain Pagan Minnot to mention hang-gliding, jetskiing, offroading, and wingsuitingare just a handful of the things you can do, and theyre all terrific. Its also just a really solid shooter with one of the most exotic and lovely settings of any game Ive played.
        </p>
        <p>
         The formula may be getting a bitformulaic at this point, but if it aint broke, why fix it? Its not like Ubisoft is skimping on content, and the game is different enoughadding in things like game-changing quest choicesand elephant-ridingto be its very own beast.
        </p>
        <p>
         ~
        </p>
        <p>
         There are more first-person shooters coming up soon, from the new
         <em>
          Unreal Tournament
         </em>
         to the indie arena shooter
         <em>
          Toxikk
         </em>
         , tothe cops-and-robbers
         <em>
          Battlefield Hardline
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         What were your favorite shooters of the year? What are you looking forward to in 2015?
        </p>
        <p>
         Shout out in the comments and on social media.
        </p>
