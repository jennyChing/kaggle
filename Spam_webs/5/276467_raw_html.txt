       <h1>
        Biographical Information
        <br/>
        Robert T. Carroll, Ph.D.
       </h1>
       <p align="left">
        <img alt="Bob Carroll - October 2014" class="floatRight" height="226" src="refuge/graphics/rtc2014.jpg" width="177"/>
        I was a full-time 
    teacher in the philosophy department at Sacramento City College from 1977 
    until my retirement in 2007.  
    I 
    received my Ph.D. from the University of California at San Diego in 1974. 
    My 
    doctoral dissertation was done under the direction of
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Popkin">
         Richard H. Popkin
        </a>
        and was entitled
        <i>
         <a href="http://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-94-010-1598-1/page/1">
          The Common-Sense Philosophy of Religion of Bishop Edward Stillingfleet (1635-1699)
         </a>
         .
        </i>
        It was published by 
    Martinus Nijhoff, The Hague, in 1975.
    I began publishing  on the WWW in 1994. Since then I have posted several thousand articles on my website
        <a href="http://www.skepdic.com/">
         skepdic.com
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        I began investigating
    
    controversial beliefs about 50 years ago and posted my first articles on the Internet more than 20 years ago. My main research interests have been in philosophy of religion, the history of 17th century philosophy, epistemology, skepticism, critical thinking, and the psychology of belief. 
    I have applied these interests to a wide range of topics. I am interested not only in
        <em>
         what
        </em>
        people believe, but
        <em>
         why
        </em>
        we believe what we do. I am especially interested in the cognitive biases and logical fallacies that unconsciously drive much human thinking on subjects we care most passionately about.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        My textbook
        <i>
         <a href="http://skepdic.com/ctinfo.html">
          Becoming a Critical Thinker
         </a>
        </i>
        was published by Pearson in 2000. A second edition was published in 2005. 
     The following chapters are available online for free:

        I was invited by James Randi to speak at the first Amazing 
    Meeting in 2003 and to conduct a workshop on critical thinking at the 5th 
    meeting in 2007. In 2003 I gave 
    an invited talk at the CSICOP conference on
        <a href="http://skepdic.com/essays/fraudsprankshoaxes.html">
         Frauds and Hoaxes

    of the scientific proof for the paranormal. In 2010, I was elected a fellow 
    of the
