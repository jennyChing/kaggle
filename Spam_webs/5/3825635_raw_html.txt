      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        <em>
         Gillian Anderson talks with Chris and Matt about what life was like when she first got
        </em>
        The X-Files
        <em>
         , her show
        </em>
        <a href="http://www.netflix.com/WiMovie/70272726">
         The Fall
        </a>
        <em>
         on Netflix, and her book
        </em>
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Vision-Fire-EARTHEND-SAGA-ebook/dp/B00IWTWL3U/ref=sr_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1421111682&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=a+vision+of+fire">
         A Vision of Fire
        </a>
        <em>
         . Chris and Matt also try to convince her to pitch an
        </em>
        X-Files
        <em>
         reboot for 2015!
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        I would be f***king overjoyed.
       </p>
       <p>
        This is what our fearless leader Chris Hardwick said when
        <a href="http://www.nerdist.com/tag/gillian-anderson">
         Gillian Anderson
        </a>
        askedwhat the reaction would be if she and partner-in-crime
        <a href="http://www.nerdist.com/tag/david-duchovny">
         David Duchovny
        </a>
        returned to make more episodes of
        <a href="http://www.nerdist.com/x-files">
         <em>
          The X-Files
         </em>
        </a>
        now, more than 20 years after the show premiered.
       </p>
       <p>
        Dont worry, friends. While its just a rumor at the moment, if Anderson,
        <a href="http://www.nerdist.com/tag/chris-hardwick">
         Hardwick
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.nerdist.com/tag/matt-mira">
         Mira
        </a>
        have anything to say about it,
        <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/xfiles2015?f=realtime&amp;src=hash" target="_blank">
         <em>
          #XFiles2015
         </em>
        </a>
        will take the internet by complete storm, and we will have our
        <em>
         X-Files
        </em>
        renaissance before you can marathon all the good, old mythology episodes.
       </p>
       <p>
        Anderson satdown with Chris and Matt Mira for a new episode of the
        <a href="http://www.nerdist.com/podcast_channel/nerdist-podcast-channel/" target="_blank">
         Nerdist Podcast
        </a>
        this week, and while we wont give away all of the choicest bits  because we know you want to hearScully swear repeatedly and laugh over how great David Duchovny looks in blue jeans  we
        <strong>
         will
        </strong>
        say that knowing Anderson is as big of a fan of the show as we all are is simply stellar.
       </p>
       <p>
        A little over halfway through their talk, the conversation turnedto
        <em>
         X-Files
        </em>
        and the experience that Anderson had working on thegroundbreaking show. Mulder/Scully was one of the first intelligent pairingsto grace our television screens in the 90s. Aside from the fashion flashbacks you will have in rewatching the series on Netflix, revisiting the episodes in streaming form will just make you want new installments all the more. And between Chris, Matt, and their guest, what started as a hypothetical back and forth about interest in restarting the series quickly becamea real call to action to send out to the internet.
       </p>
       <p>
        Should Fox do anything in their power to make it happen?, Anderson asked at one point. The response is overwhelming. And that hashtag we put up there at the top of this piece? Its real. Tweet it from here to eternity, and make your voice heard!
       </p>
       <p>
        Yes, the trio came up with the perfect hashtagto begin aninternet campaign to bring the dynamic duo back to the small screen. This is my call-out to everything in the nerdosphere, Hardwick said. Simply tweet all your
        <a href="http://ctt.ec/6e60O">
         #XFiles2015
        </a>
        feelings to Fox for your chance to be heard.We want to believe.
       </p>
