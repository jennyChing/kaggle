           <h1>
            The 2014 LARP Census &amp; Community
           </h1>
           <span class="author">
            by
            <span itemprop="author">
             Jideobi Odunze
            </span>
           </span>
           <div class="the-content">
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.geekedoutnation.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Larp-2014-Census.jpg">
              <img alt="Larp 2014 Census" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-35160" height="243" src="http://www.geekedoutnation.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Larp-2014-Census-600x243.jpg" width="600"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Aaron Vanek and Ryan Paddy are long time larpers who have taken the time to tackle this project that is a worldwide larp census that self-identifies larpers on a grand scale All you need to do to help is visit the LARP Census site to fill out the survey
             <a class="yt-uix-redirect-link" dir="ltr" href="http://larpcensus.org" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://larpcensus.org">
              http://larpcensus.org
             </a>
             , and do your part to support the LARP community. Something that can be done again if successful in a few years, to track changes in larp across the planet. That kind of private information can be VERY useful in expansion and doing things having knowledge of what will work and what will not. The accuracy of information is key to anything having that chance of coming to fruition.
            </p>
            <p class="jetstrap-highlighted">
             The information they acquire will be publicly shared so scholars can analyze the data and make conclusions that will, hopefully, increase both the quantity and quality of larps around the globe.
            </p>
            <p class="">
             All you have to do is answer the census if you have ever gone to a larp, or considered larping. You only have to answer once and if you have the time you can take the 15 minute questionnaire which helps give a more detailed explanation of the type of larp that you do. Everything in the long run is to help improve the larp community when we know just how many out there do larp, where they do, how they do and know just whether or not that growth we say there is to larping is really there.
            </p>
            <p class="">
             In case you are new to larping;
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p class="jetstrap-highlighted">
              <em>
               Larp or LARP is an acronym that stands for Live Action Role Playing. Some people think of larp as a sport, some as a hobby, some as a medium of expression, and some as an art form.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p class="jetstrap-highlighted">
              <em>
               Larp means you portray a characterwho may be a mindless zombie or who may be very close to the real youwith other people doing the same. The overall experience is designed and guided by someone or a group who may or may not also portray characters during the event. There is rarely a detailed script to follow like staged theater. This improv acting is performed for personal satisfaction and the fulfillment of your fellow role-players; larp is not for a passive audience that has no agency in the narrative.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p class="jetstrap-highlighted">
              <em>
               Larp goes by many names, and instances of it have appeared throughout global history. It appears to be in a global resurgence, with larp communities in many different countries.
              </em>
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <h4>
             <strong>
              Word of mouth matters, so make sure that even if you do fill this out others know to do the same!
             </strong>
            </h4>

	//equal height columns
	function equalHeightColumns(group) {
		tallest = 0;
		width = jQuery(window).width();							
		group.each(function() {
			jQuery(this).removeAttr('style');			
			thisHeight = jQuery(this).height();
			if(thisHeight &gt; tallest) {
				tallest = thisHeight;
			}
		});
		if(width &gt; 767) {	
			group.height(tallest);	
		}
	}
