       <h1 class="entry-title">
        3 Reasons to Ditch Table Salt &amp; Use Natural Sea Salt Instead
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2015-04-24T07:00:16+00:00">
         April 24, 2015
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://musingssahm.com/author/erika/" rel="author">
           Erika
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://musingssahm.com/2015/04/reasons-natural-sea-salt/#comments">
          17 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px;">
        </div>
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <img alt="3 Reasons to Ditch Table Salt &amp; Use Natural Sea Salt Instead" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32134" height="585" src="http://musingssahm.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Pink-Sea-Salt.jpg" width="821"/>
         Did you know that unrefined natural salt, such as Himalayan orCeltic salt,is better for you than regular table salt?
        </p>
        <p>
         I may get some flack for saying that because many folks dont think its any different, but Ibelieve it.
        </p>
        <p>
         We ditched table salt a year ago and only use naturalsea salt now. Its one of many simple steps my family has taken toward living a healthier, less-processed lifestyle. And this one is soeasy too.
        </p>
        <p>
         When I say natural sea salt, Im talking about Himalayan Pink Salt or Celtic Salt. {Not the processed version of sea salt on the shelf of the grocery store. Ill explain how you can tell the differencelater.}
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="3 Reasons to Ditch Table Salt &amp; Use Natural Sea Salt Instead" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32132" height="849" src="http://musingssahm.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Reasons-to-Use-Natural-Sea-Salt.jpg" width="566"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Heres a 3 reasonswhy you should make the switch from table salt to natural sea salt too:
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Regular table salt goes through a rigorous process of refining and is stripped of nutrients.Natural sea salt undergoes very little, if any, processing.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Theresnatural minerals  trace amounts of calcium, potassium, magnesium, and iron  in sea salt. Its a tiny amount (far less than youd get from eating a healthy balanced diet) but its still there nonetheless.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive heard it said that because the amount is so small, it doesnt make a difference. But I dont follow that logic because even a very tiny amount isbetter than none at all!
        </p>
        <p>
         2.
         <strong>
          Regular table salt has non-caking additives
         </strong>
         to prevent it from clumping together.
        </p>
        <p>
         While Im not aware of any studies showing this additive isnt good for you, I prefer to avoid extra additives. Natural, with no additives, is always better in my book. If you can avoid additives, why not? Especially when its such an easy change.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Beyond any nutritional claims,
         <strong>
          natural sea salt tastes better than table salt.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I did an experiment one day with several different kinds of salt  regular table, kosher, store refined sea salt, Himalayan, and Celtic. The Himalayan and Celtic sea saltshad the best flavor by far.
        </p>
        <p>
         Give it a try  youll see what I mean!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="3 Reasons to Ditch Table Salt &amp; Use Natural Sea Salt Instead" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32136" height="283" src="http://musingssahm.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/sea-salt-varieties.jpg" width="424"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         How do I know whats really natural sea salt?
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Check the label.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you seesodium chloride, it pretty much means refined, so youll want to keep looking.Manybrands use the term natural on their labels these days, but itreally means nothing since theres no regulations behind it. Anyone can slap the natural label on any food. Something to think about, isnt it?
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Check the salt color.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Another hint: pure white finely grained salt is almost always refined. Natural sea salt will be different colors  off-white or even pink. And its often larger grained too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Tip: If youre just getting started, try a fine grain version of natural sea salt.
         </strong>
         Theres larger grain salts and those are great to use too, but if you want something simple that will work in your salt shaker, go with fine grain.
        </p>
        <p>
         I first tried a regular Celtic Salt and while it tasted amazing, it always clogged our salt shaker, so it wasnt easy to get my whole family to use it. My next attempt was with a fine grain Pink Himalayansalt that worked perfectly and allowed us to completely ditch the table salt.
        </p>

         If you cant find natural sea salt in the store, you can find a ton of great varieties on Amazon (affiliate link) for decent prices:
        </p>
