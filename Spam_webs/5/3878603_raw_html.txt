         <p>
          We will soon be seeing green, with St. Patricks Dayjust a few weeks away. Although I am not of Irish descent, I have always loved the festivities that comes with this holiday.In addition to making a corned beef dinner andwearing something green, its also fun to make a St. Patricks Day inspired sweet treat like these Green Tea Shamrock Cookies.
         </p>
         <p>
          These cookies are so tasty! The green tea flavor blends perfectly with the basic sugar cookie recipe. I especially like that these are not overly sweet cookies and have a nice bit of crunch to them.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Matcha-Green-Tea-Shamrock-Cookies-Recipe.jpg">
           <img alt="Matcha Green Tea Shamrock Cookies" class="aligncenter wp-image-28120 size-full" height="1053" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Matcha-Green-Tea-Shamrock-Cookies-Recipe.jpg" width="700"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Whenever possible, I opt for using green foodsinstead of food coloring.
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/st-patricks-day-food-for-kids-pea-pesto/" target="_blank">
           Pea Pesto
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/st-patricks-day-food-for-kids-green-macaroni-and-cheese/" target="_blank">
           Green Macaroni and Cheese
          </a>
          are a couple of my favorite kid-friendly green meals thatget their color from greenvegetables.Green Tea (matcha) which is often used in Japanese sweets,is perfect for giving St. Patricks Day cookies a green color.
         </p>
         <p>
          Loaded with antioxidants, Match Green Tea is really good for you too!
         </p>
         <h1>
          <span style="color: #008000;">
           Green Tea Shamrock Cookies
          </span>
         </h1>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ingredients:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          2/3 cup butter, softened
          <br/>
          3/4 cup granulated sugar
          <br/>
          1 egg
          <br/>
          1 teaspoon vanilla extract
          <br/>
          1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
          <br/>
          2 tablespoons matcha green tea powder
          <br/>
          1 teasoop baking powder
          <br/>
          1/2 teaspoon salt
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Directions:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          In a large bowl, mix the butter with sugar until light and fluffy; beat in egg and vanilla. Whisk together flour, matcha green tea powder, baking powder and salt. Gradually stir into the butter mixture until dough comes together.
         </p>
         <p>
          Divide the dough in half, form into discs and wrap each in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for 1 hour. Let stand at room temperature until soft enough to roll out, about 15 minutes.
         </p>
         <p>
          Preheat oven to 350 F. Line baking sheet with parchment paper, set aside.
         </p>
         <p>
          Place the dough on a lightly floured surface and roll to 1/4 inch thick. Use shamrock shaped cookie cutter to make cookie shapes, re-rolling any scraps. Repeat process with the remaining dough.
         </p>
         <p>
          Place cookies onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 10 to 12 minutes or until edges are light brown. Let cookies cool for a minute on baking sheets then transfer to racks and let them cool completely.
         </p>
         <p>
          Recipe yields 1 1/2  2 dozen cookies, depending on the size of your cookie cutter.
         </p>

            type  = this.fieldType(field);

          this.checkboxAndRadioValidation(field);

        }

        return 'checkboxes';

      } else {
