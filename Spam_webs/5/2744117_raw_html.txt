      <h2>
       <a href="http://www.russianseason.net/index.php/2009/11/vyprazany-syr-slovak-fried-cheese/" title="Vypran syr (Slovak Fried Cheese)">
        Vypran syr (Slovak Fried Cheese)
       </a>
      </h2>
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        <img alt="Vyprazany syr" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-308" height="308" src="http://www.russianseason.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/syr6.jpg" title="Vyprazany syr" width="410"/>
        <br/>
        Ive been going to post this recipe for ages but never had any decent photos! Today I finally wasnt in a hurry (you know, Vypran syr is a very quick dish to make, so its good to make when youre short on time) so I made some shots for our blog!
        <br/>
        Vypran syr is probably my favourite recipe from Slovakia. I had eaten it a few times in my hometown before, but it was Slovakia where I first tried it as a main course  accompanied by pommes frites and vegetables. I have to say there is quite a limited choice of main courses for vegetarians in Slovak restaurants, so I always opted for fried cheese  and never regretted that, as it was equally delicious in all towns I visited. This summer I learned how to make it at home, and even though I havent yet mastered the technique of frying cheese so that it never leaks out of the crust (tips and suggestions are welcome!), I have made some useful notices about the process in general. The first one is to use a harder sort of cheese with few holes  Gouda cheese is pretty perfect in this respect. Another thing I consider crucial is the breadcrumbs. Do not use any store-bought breadcrumbs; make your own for an extra-crunchy, extra-flaky crust. The smell of fried home-made breadcrumbs mixed with eggs is so cosy and warming!
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-307">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Ingredients
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        6 slices of Gouda cheese, 0.7cm to 1 cm thin
        <br/>
        2 eggs
        <br/>
        3 tbsp milk
        <br/>
        1 cup breadcrumbs
        <br/>
         cup wheat flour
        <br/>
         tsp salt
        <br/>
        Vegetable oil
       </p>
       <p>
        Serves 3
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        In a medium-sized bowl, beat the eggs with milk and salt.
        <br/>
        Coat each slice of cheese in flour.  Take the slices one by one and soak them in the egg mix, then coat with breadcrumbs, then return to the egg mix and coat with breadcrumbs again. That is, each slice of cheese should have a single layer of flour and two layers of eggs and breadcrumbs. Dont forget to coat the edges well, otherwise cheese will leak as soon as you put it into the pan (Okay mine leaks anyway from time to time, but things could be worse!).
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Ingredients for Vyprazany syr" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-309" height="308" src="http://www.russianseason.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/syr1.jpg" title="Ingredients for Vyprazany syr" width="410"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Making Vyprazany syr" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-310" height="308" src="http://www.russianseason.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/syr2.jpg" title="Making Vyprazany syr" width="410"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        In a large frying pan, heat about 3 tablespoons vegetable oil and put the slices of cheese into it. Fry them on a medium high heat on both sides, until golden. Add a tablespoon oil or more as you turn the cheese over.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Frying Vyprazany syr" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-311" height="308" src="http://www.russianseason.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/syr3.jpg" title="Frying Vyprazany syr" width="410"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Originally Vypran syr is served with pommes frites and fresh vegetables. We prefer a lighter option with Frillice, black olives and tomato salad and fresh ciabatta. I also enjoy a weird combination of Vypran syr with some slices of mozzarella in the salad  I call this Cross-national Double Cheese! :)
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Vyprazany syr" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-312" height="308" src="http://www.russianseason.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/syr4.jpg" title="Vyprazany syr" width="410"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Vyprazany syr" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-314" height="308" src="http://www.russianseason.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/syr51.jpg" title="Vyprazany syr" width="410"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Vyprazany syr" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-315" height="308" src="http://www.russianseason.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/syr7.jpg" title="Vyprazany syr" width="410"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        To prepare home-made breadcrumbs, grind slices of dry wheat bread on a hand grinder. In a non-stick pan, toast the breadcrumbs for a couple of minutes until dry and slightly golden.
       </p>
