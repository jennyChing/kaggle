             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               Turns out, you don't have to have a long resume to host one of the most popular fake news shows on cable.
              </p>
              <p>
               Just five months after joining Comedy Central's
               <em>
                Daily Show
               </em>
               as a new cast member, 31-year-old Trevor Noah has been named the new host of the popular weeknight "news" show. The news comes just weeks after long-time host Jon Stewart announced he was
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/02/10/jon-stewart-to-step-down-from-the-daily-show-later-this-year/">
                stepping down
               </a>
               from the show after 16 years. Comedy Central said Noah will take over the show "later this year."
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/03/30/trevor-noah-stand-up/">
                 10 Trevor Noah standup clips that prove he deserves the 'Daily Show' gig
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               Noah is largely unknown to American audiences. Before joining
               <em>
                The Daily Show
               </em>
               last October, the youthful comedian had built a following in his native South Africa, where he hosted music, film, television award shows and even his own talk show,
               <em>
                Tonight with Trevor Noah
               </em>
               . That hosting experience could serve him well as the ring leader of Comedy Central's somewhat anarchic news show. He also had a comedy special on Showtime in 2013. Below, you can see a supercut of Trevor Noah's best bits.
              </p>
              <div class="content-brightcove" data-video="4142421833001" id="4142421833001">
              </div>
              <p>
               Some are hailing the tapping of Noah, who was born to a black South African mother and a white European father, as another late night win for diversity. The cable network recently installed African American comedian Larry Wilmore as the new host of
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2015/01/20/the-nightly-show-with-larry-wilmore-yep-its-definitely-about-race/">
                his own show
               </a>
               in the time slot vacated by Stephen Colbert late last year. That show airs back-to-back with
               <em>
                The Daily Show
               </em>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               Others, though, were hoping Comedy Central would name a woman as the new host. Comedy Central President Michele Ganeless, however, told
               <em>
                The New York Times
               </em>
               We talked to women. We talked to men. We found in Trevor the best person for the job."
              </p>
              <blockquote class="instagram-media" data-instgrm-captioned="" data-instgrm-version="4" style=" background:#FFF; border:0; border-radius:3px; box-shadow:0 0 1px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.5),0 1px 10px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.15); margin: 1px; max-width:658px; padding:0; width:99.375%; width:-webkit-calc(100% - 2px); width:calc(100% - 2px);">

              <p>
               While some hoped the next
               <em>
                The Daily Show
               </em>
               would be a woman, Comedy Central is being recongnized on Twitter as offering one of the more diverse late-night lineups.
               <br/>
               In a statement to
               <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2015/03/31/arts/television/trevor-noah-to-succeed-jon-stewart-on-the-daily-show.html?src=twr&amp;_r=0" target="_blank">
                <em>
                 The New York Times
                </em>
               </a>
               , outgoing host Stewart said: "I'm thrilled for the show and for Trevor. He's a tremendous talent that we've loved working with."
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                It's official: we're thrilled to confirm
                <a href="https://twitter.com/Trevornoah" target="_blank">
                 @Trevornoah

                will be the next

                 #DailyShow

                host.

                </a>
                <a href="http://t.co/ts2UrnAqrV" target="_blank">
                 pic.twitter.com/ts2UrnAqrV
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                 Comedy Central (@ComedyCentral)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/ComedyCentral/status/582527758388973569" target="_blank">
                 March 30, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               As for Noah, he may still be in a state of shock. He told the
               <em>
                Times
               </em>
               , "You don't believe it for the first few hours. You need a stiff drink and then unfortunately youre in a place where you can't really get alcohol."
              </p>
              <p>
               Noah later tweeted his gratitude.
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
               <p>
                No-one can replace Jon Stewart. But together with the amazing team at The Daily Show, we will continue to make this the best damn news show!
               </p>
               <p>
                 Trevor Noah (@Trevornoah)
                <a href="https://twitter.com/Trevornoah/status/582525079960981504" target="_blank">
                 March 30, 2015
                </a>
               </p>
              </blockquote>
              <p>
               While he's been featured in
               <em>
                GQ South Africa
               </em>
               ,
               <em>
                Rolling Stone
               </em>
               , and CNN, Noah isn't oblivious to his meteoric rise. In a release announcing
               <em>
                The Daily Show
               </em>
               gig, the comedian noted, In my brief time with the show theyve made me feel so welcome. Im excited to get started and work with such a fantastic group of people.
              </p>
              <p>
               Within minutes of the story breaking, "Trevor Noah" was the number one trending topic on Twitter (in the U.S.). Among the first reactions was this tweet from comedian Chris Rock:
              </p>
              <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">

              </blockquote>
              <p>
               <em>
                The Daily Show

               tweeted just the kind of congratulations you would expect:
              </p>
