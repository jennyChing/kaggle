       <h1>
        February Favourites
       </h1>
       <span class="title-divider">
       </span>
       <span class="post-date">
        Posted on 3 Mar
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="post-img">
      </div>
      <div class="post-entry">
       <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
       <p>
        <img alt="February Favourites" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3239" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/srslylou.co.uk/wp/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/february-favourties-2015.png?resize=680%2C472"/>
        <br/>
        Im well aware that February is the shortest month, but boy did it whiz past. If Id have blinked I would have missed it. There was still plenty of time to amass some monthly favourites though. As I had a trip to London and made various online orders (tut, tut) I had a lot to try.
       </p>
       <p>
        First on my list to purchase last month was the new
        <strong>
         <a href="http://fave.co/1BbwDzy" target="_blank">
          NarsAll Day Luminous Weightless Foundation
         </a>
        </strong>
        . I was taken in by a few reviews Id seen and decided it was a must for my oily skin. I have to say that Ive been very impressed with it,it gives a finish thats more matte than Sheer Glow, but its still radiant looking. Full thoughts and rambles soon!
       </p>
       <p>
        As Im already chatting about Nars I may as well mention how amazing the
        <a href="http://fave.co/1K9GkDx" target="_blank">
         <strong>
          Smudge Proof Eyeshadow Base
         </strong>
        </a>
        is. I have quite oily eyelids, and they always get slicker throughout the day. Id tried so many primers, but this beats them all. Yes it is quite  for a smallish tube of primer, but daaammmn does it do its job. Ive had shadow last more than 8,9,10 hours, and the only time its moved is when Ive taken it off. Seriously impressive.
       </p>
       <p>
        Sticking with eyelids, Ive been using the
        <a href="http://fave.co/1Bbu2FC" target="_blank">
         <strong>
          Smashbox Double Exposure Palette
         </strong>
        </a>
        most days since I picked it up. I wanted to step out of my neutral eyeshadow comfort zone, and while I wont be sporting neon shadows any time soon, this palette is a safe bet for now. Its got a great mix of purple, blue, and more neutral shades. Ive mostly been reaching for the dark purple Temper or the red tinged Copper mixed with a lighter shade. Formula wise theyre spot on,and pigmentation is on point too.
       </p>
       <p>
        My mascara needs have swayed somewhat over the past few weeks. With my favourite Rimmel mascara drying out I switched back to the
        <a href="http://fave.co/1pMPDt8" target="_blank">
         <strong>
          BourjoisVolume 1 Seconde Mascara
         </strong>
        </a>
        . I wiggle this stuff from left to right first, before giving it a sweep upwards. It helps to create decent volume and length, and its pretty black too  though I want to try the newer ultra black version.
       </p>
       <p>
        Having used up theday moisturiser I was using, I needed to get a replacement. After reading some reviews and many a google search, I went with the
        <a href="http://fave.co/1DygsIl" target="_blank">
         <strong>
          PaiGeranium &amp; Thistle Rebalancing Day Cream
         </strong>
        </a>
        . It has a lovely textureand leaves the skin with a slightly matte finish. Its not too heavy, which for some reason it quite hard to come across in a day cream, and sits under makeup.
       </p>
       <p>
        My final special mention has to go the the
