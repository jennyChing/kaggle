          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            The App Store is absolutely chocked up with all sorts of mobile game apps. Some of them are good, most of them are bad. Some of them are profitable. Most of them are at a total loss. Some of them are popular. Most of them will never be discovered.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            So what is it that distinguishes
            <strong>
             a good game from a bad game
            </strong>
            , or a popular game from
            <em>
             the-run-of-the-mill?
            </em>
            And how can we harness the same tactics to make our own awesome game apps?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Game Development" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4479" height="4605" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Game-Development2.jpg" width="2550"/>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: large;">
             Unfortunately theres no ONE solution to building games that rock. But there are certain elements within popular games that really stand out to create an engaging experience.
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            In this post well go through what I call
            <strong>
             The 4 Rules of Developing Addictive Mobile Games
            </strong>
            and how
            <em>
             you
            </em>
            can apply these rules to create a mobile game
            <strong>
             that people love.
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            <strong>
             First off, just to clarify something
            </strong>
            : Every game has to be
            <em>
             profitable
            </em>
             otherwise youll end up creating a very costly and time-consuming hobby. In this post were not going into detail about
            <a href="http://www.buzinga.com.au/smartphone-apps/making-money-from-mobile-apps/" title="Making Money From Mobile Apps">
             how to make money from m
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.buzinga.com.au/smartphone-apps/making-money-from-mobile-apps/" target="_blank">
             obile games but you can read more about that here.
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            First off, you need to recognise what the most important aspect of a mobile game really is:
            <strong>
             The user experience.
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Without a
            <strong>
             faaaanshmastic
            </strong>
            user experience, you have no chance of driving profit at all. So this is where your focus needs to be and thats exactly what this post is all about. So lets talk about the
            <strong>
             4 Rules of Developing Addictive Mobile Games.
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            There are 4 areas of any game that create an engaging experience. By nailing each of these areas your users will love your game, share your game and will be willing to pay for your game.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="font-size: large;">
             The 4 Rules of Developing Addictive Mobile Gamesare:
            </span>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 id="every-game-app-should-tell-a-story">
           1) Every game app should tell a story
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="story board your mobile game" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3549" height="200" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/story-board-your-mobile-game.png" width="590"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            A mobile game without a story isboring. Pointless. Without a story, your users wont feel
            <em>
             connected
            </em>
            to your game.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Im not talking about some sort of deep spiritual connection between
            <em>
             man-and-game
            </em>
             Im talking about the
            <strong>
             emotional connection
            </strong>
            we experience when we really engage in the story of a game.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            And without sounding like the biggest-loser-in-the-whole-world, Ive been TRULY engaged in games before. Im talking about a deep sense of fulfillment and purpose. I know thats weird but some games are really good like that and 16 was a sensitive age for me
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Without a
            <strong>
             compelling story
            </strong>
            , your players are practically just completing a series of mindless tasks. Which is exactly what our girlfriends and wives think when they see us sitting there jamming our latest game
            <strong>
             because theyre NOT ENGAGED in the story
            </strong>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            You can easily turn this around by creating a story to preframe the user to feel and think the way you want them to feel and think when theyre playing your game.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            <a href="http://www.ironhidegames.com/" target="_blank">
             Ironhide Game Studio
            </a>
            did a great job of this in their latest mobile game app 
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/kingdom-rush-frontiers-hd/id598581619?mt=8" target="_blank">
             Kingdom Rush Frontiers
            </a>
            . They lay the story out before each chapter so that the player knows
            <strong>
             what theyre fighting for
            </strong>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3550" style="width: 487px">
           <img alt="Kingdom Rush Frontiers" class=" wp-image-3550 " height="269" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Kingdom-Rush-Frontiers.png" width="477"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Kingdom Rush Frontiers
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: large;">
             It sounds kind of stupid, but it works. I was engaged. I may even have cried a little
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Creating a storyboard is easy. You can tell the story through
            <strong>
             still images and text
            </strong>
            like in Kingdom Rush Frontiers. This helps the player know
            <em>
             how you want them to feel
            </em>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Another great way is to use
            <strong>
             in-game story telling
            </strong>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            By controlling the gameplay you can guide the user to taking the actions you want them to take. Thereby allowing them to
            <strong>
             learn about the features of the game
            </strong>
            before activating the real gameplay. As opposed to throwing them in the deep end and
            <em>
             hoping
            </em>
            theyll learn how to swim!
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/au/app/plants-vs.-zombies-2/id597986893?mt=8" target="_blank">
             Plants Vs Zombies 2
            </a>
            is a great example of story-telling within the game-play.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Early on in the game they introduce a character called Crazy Dave, whos basically a moron but acts as
            <strong>
             comic relief and guidance
            </strong>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3555" style="width: 528px">
           <img alt="Plants Vs Zombies - Crazy Dave" class=" wp-image-3555" height="374" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Plants-Vs-Zombies-Crazy-Dave.png" width="518"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Crazy Dave with Penny the Campervan  Plants Vs Zombies 2
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Whenever a new feature is introduced Crazy Dave will appear on the bottom left of the screen and gives the user a
            <strong>
             quick walk-through
            </strong>
            to show them exactly what to do.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Crazy Dave and Penny (the Time-Travelling Campervan) also narrate the story in-between levels. Pretty clever
            <em>
             .
            </em>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           2) Make it dead-easy to use
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="make your game app easy to use" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3557" height="200" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/make-your-game-app-easy-to-use.png" width="590"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            If you do nothing else apart from applying this next tip you still have a great chance at developing a fantastic, engaging and
            <strong>
             profitable mobile game
            </strong>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Making a game app look good goes without saying, right? But if thats the case, then why are there
            <em>
             so many ugly apps around
            </em>
            ! Beats me.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            A nice, fluent, functional,
            <strong>
             bug free
            </strong>
            app will go a lot further than an app that looks as if though it was thrown together by a 6 year old during arts and crafts hour
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            But the visual aspect shouldnt just look good, it also needs to be
            <strong>
             easy to use.
            </strong>
            That means important buttons/elements are within easy reach of your thumbs, they stand out and their function is obvious (intuitive design).
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/au/app/clash-of-clans/id529479190?mt=8" target="_blank">
             Clash of Clans
            </a>
            is a great example of this. They make most important buttons
            <span style="font-size: x-large;">
             large
            </span>
            and
            <strong>
             <span style="color: #ff0000;">
              c
             </span>
             <span style="color: #ff6600;">
              o
             </span>
             <span style="color: #ffff00;">
              l
             </span>
             <span style="color: #00ff00;">
              o
             </span>
             <span style="color: #00ccff;">
              r
             </span>
             <span style="color: #800080;">
              f
             </span>
             <span style="color: #0000ff;">
              u
             </span>
             <span style="color: #ff0000;">
              l
             </span>
            </strong>
            so that they dont get lost in the rubble. Once again, guiding users to
            <strong>
             make the decisions they want them to make
            </strong>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3559" style="width: 586px">
           <img alt="Clash of Clans" class=" wp-image-3559" height="384" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Clash-of-Clans.png" width="576"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Frequently used buttons are big and close to your thumbs  Clash of Clans
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Try to avoid making the mistake of thinking that your users will figure it out or that they will understand how to play your game. Treat them
            <strong>
             as if they have no idea at all
            </strong>
            and youll see a big increase in user engagement.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 id="gamify-your-game">
           3) Gamify Your Game
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="gamify your game" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3562" height="200" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/gamify-your-game.png" width="590"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Using badges, titles and other achievements you can
            <strong>
             gamify your game
            </strong>
            even further.
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: large;">
             Gamification is to turn something into a game.
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Understandably your game is ALREADY A GAME. But the idea is to get your players
            <strong>
             even more involved
            </strong>
            by adding another level of gamification into the mix.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            This can easily be done by creating a system for rewarding your players and making them feel
            <em>
             special
            </em>
            . (is that too mush?)
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/au/app/hay-day/id506627515?mt=8" target="_blank">
             Hay Day
            </a>
            does a wonderful job at this by
            <strong>
             rewarding the player
            </strong>
            with achievements and bonuses.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>
            <img alt="Hay Day gamification" class="aligncenter wp-image-3564" height="515" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Hay-Day-gamification.jpg" width="614"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Hay Day is a social game where you as the player is tasked to build a farm.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Youre rewarded with extra bonuses and achievements for completing certain tasks pretty much doing what you would be doing anyway.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            This gamification model adds another
            <em>
             level
            </em>
            to the depth of the game which keeps things interesting. Its pretty simple really, but heck, it works right?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           4) Test and Tweak your gameplay
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="tweak your mobile game" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3566" height="200" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/tweak-your-mobile-game.png" width="590"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Heres the thing. Developing a mobile app is easy. But developing applications that are
            <strong>
             intuitive yet challenging, engaging and addictive
            </strong>
            takes time, effort and a tonne of creativity.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Most people understand that a mobile game app can take anywhere between 3-6 (or more) months to develop. What they
            <em>
             fail to understand
            </em>
            is that once thats done there is a period of about 1-3 months needed for
            <strong>
             just tweaking the gameplay.
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Why?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Because its hard to know exactly how everything is gonna work/look/feel until you get to play the-real-deal.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            <strong>
             Example:
            </strong>
            How high does the character jump? How many times can you get shot until you die? How much corn will the cow eat until it produces milk?
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            These things are tweaked and tested right at the end to create an
            <strong>
             engaging
            </strong>
            experience that
            <strong>
             people love
            </strong>
            .
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Test it with several different people to ensure that the gameplay is intuitive. If its not then you need to tweak it to ensure that it is. Period.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Weve made the mistake before of launching a game prematurely and the results wereweakat first.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            It pays to take your time with the last bit of tweaking until youre satisfied with the gameplay.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="font-size: large;">
             But the testing doesnt end there.
            </span>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Once the app is launched to the App Store you need to analyse the user experience using live analytical data
            <em>
             (so many big words)
            </em>
            to tweak the experience even further.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Theres nothing better for understanding what your players want than APP ANALYTICS. Heres a post we did a while back on the
            <a href="http://www.buzinga.com.au/buzz/10-most-popular-mobile-app-analytics-tools/" target="_blank" title="10 Most Popular Mobile App Analytics Tools">
             10 Most Popular App Analytics Tools
            </a>
            available in the app market.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large; line-height: 19px;">
            As one of my clients likes to say: Tweak Like A Freak
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           The Wrap
          </h3>
          <p>
           <img alt="the wrap" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3567" height="200" src="http://loganzone.buzingaapps.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/the-wrap.png" width="590"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            The
            <strong>
             4 Rules of Developing Addictive Mobile Games
            </strong>
            may seem pretty straight forward. But youd be surprised to hear how many app developers DONT apply them in their development process.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            So with that said, you need to ensure that either you work with an app developer who understands these rules OR guide your development team to apply them in the development of YOUR app.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            This is one of
            <strong>
             many tips
            </strong>
            we give our own clients during the design and development of their apps. If you want to more tips like this, grab a copy of our
            <strong>
             3 app development guides full of actionable tips and
            </strong>
            strategies below.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size: large;">
            Thanks for reading and if you have any tips of your own related questions please post them below.
           </span>
          </p>
