         <p>
          Imagine being an archaeologist on a dig  Id love to be one  scraping away at the earth day after day. You might find a little clay pot or the odd earring every now and then. Apart from that, days are probably pretty uneventful but all the time youd be secretly hoping for the big find; the one thats going to hit the headlines.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 590px">
          <a data-rel="lightbox[gallery-86Z9]" href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/blogger/-SKddklJojqY/S_AIX-CdWHI/AAAAAAAAAsQ/i8gz3ikWi44/s1600/2010_0514Bodrum0019.JPG">
           <img alt="Tlos Near Fethiye" border="0" height="435" src="//www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/blogger/-SKddklJojqY/S_AIX-CdWHI/AAAAAAAAAsQ/i8gz3ikWi44/s640/2010_0514Bodrum0019.JPG" title="Tlos" width="580"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Tlos has an amazing, dramatic setting overlooking the Xanthos Valley
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          If you read this blog a lot, youll know that
          <a href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/2010/05/day-trip-from-fethiye-tlos.html" title="Day Trip From Fethiye - Tlos">
           Tlos
          </a>
          is one of our favourite archaeological sites with its mixture of Roman ruins and Lycian rock tombs  so we were really interested when we found out some time ago that a team of archaeologists would begin another dig there. Weve made it up there a couple of times recently and each time, a little more of the Roman past has been uncovered  great to see more and more being gradually revealed.
         </p>
         <p>
          And then on Thursday, we were reading this article in
          <a href="http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/n.php?n=excavations-at-tlos-reveal-roman-works-2011-08-18" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/n.php?n=excavations-at-tlos-reveal-roman-works-2011-08-18', 'Hrriyet');" target="_blank" title="Hrriyet Article About Tlos">
           Hrriyet
          </a>
          . I wonder which archaeologist it was that was scraping the earth and then hit something hard. Scraped a bit moreand a bit moremarble reveals itselfbingo! Statues of the
          <b>
           <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadrian" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadrian', 'Roman Emperor Hadrian');" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Hadrian">
            Roman Emperor Hadrian
           </a>
          </b>
          , emperor
          <b>
           <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoninus_Pius" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoninus_Pius', 'Antoninus Pius');" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Antoninus Pius">
            Antoninus Pius
           </a>
          </b>
          and his daughter,
          <b>
           Faistinaminor
          </b>
          , the
          <b>
           goddess Isis
          </b>
          and it says Mareus Aurellus but we think this may be a misprint. Were wondering if it should say
          <b>
           <a class="zem_slink" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcus_Aurelius" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcus_Aurelius', 'Marcus Aurelius');" rel="wikipedia" target="_blank" title="Marcus Aurelius">
            Marcus Aurelius
           </a>
          </b>
          as all these men were contemporaries and their statues always look similar.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 590px">
          <a data-rel="lightbox[gallery-86Z9]" href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/blogger/-FAUVq9uyabU/TlCb3ys0J4I/AAAAAAAADcU/tJ6bvw8AeC8/s1600/Fethiye%2BMuseum%2BTurkey.JPG">
           <img alt="Tlos Roman Statues At Fethiye Museum" border="0" height="435" src="//www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/blogger/-FAUVq9uyabU/TlCb3ys0J4I/AAAAAAAADcU/tJ6bvw8AeC8/s640/Fethiye%2BMuseum%2BTurkey.JPG" title="Fethiye Museum" width="580"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Fethiye Museum  You can see the statues lined up by the entrance gate
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          We assumed the statues would be whisked away to Istanbul, Ankara or at best,
          <a href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/2011/03/antalya-museum-konyaalti-beach.html" title="Antalya Museum">
           Antalya Archaeological Museum
          </a>
           but, thanks to Twitter, we saw one of the Turkish language newspapers had reported the statues had been taken to
          <a href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/2011/08/visiting-fethiye-museum.html">
           Fethiye Museum
          </a>
          . Often overlooked by visitors to Fethiye, the museum finally has in its possession a bit of a draw in the recently discovered statues.
         </p>
         <p>
          We went up there yesterday to see if we could see them, expecting them to be packed away somewhere, not for public viewing just yet. But, maybe we got there at the perfect time and the museum hasnt decided what to do with the statues yet because there they were, greeting us at the gate.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 450px">
          <a data-rel="lightbox[gallery-86Z9]" href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/blogger/-_auCYluPCIg/TlCcvBMt8ZI/AAAAAAAADcY/bHALRKk8i4Q/s1600/2011-08-20.jpg">
           <img alt="Roman Statues From Tlos At Fethiye Museum" border="0" height="275" src="//www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/blogger/-_auCYluPCIg/TlCcvBMt8ZI/AAAAAAAADcY/bHALRKk8i4Q/s400/2011-08-20.jpg" title="Roman Statues From Tlos At Fethiye Museum" width="440"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Goddess Isis and the daughter of Antoninus
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          How the archaeologists managed to identify the statue on the right is beyond us being that she has no face. Maybe there are clues in the clothing shes wearing. Whatever the case, these are the two female statues  and one of them is a goddess.
         </p>
         <p>
          As for the males. Ive looked online at various sites, including the
          <a href="http://www.britishmuseum.org/explore/highlights/highlight_image.aspx?image=greek_statue.jpg&amp;retpage=25544" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.britishmuseum.org/explore/highlights/highlight_image.aspx?image=greek_statue.jpg&amp;retpage=25544', 'British Museum');" target="_blank" title="British Museum Website">
           British Museum
          </a>
          website to try and identify which is which  and Ive got to say, they all look very similar. Well have to wait till the staff at Fethiye Museum mount them and label them.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_14596" style="width: 490px">
          <a data-rel="lightbox[gallery-86Z9]" href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/tlos-statue-fethiye-museum-11.jpg">
           <img alt="Roman Statue From Tlos At Fethiye Museum" class="size-full wp-image-14596" height="640" src="//www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/tlos-statue-fethiye-museum-11.jpg" width="480"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           A Young Marcus Aurelius?
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          If it was a misprint in the newspaper and the third statue is Marcus Aurelius, were guessing this is him as he was younger than Hadrian and Antoninus.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_14597" style="width: 490px">
          <a data-rel="lightbox[gallery-86Z9]" href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/Tlos-statue-Fethiye-Museum1.jpg">
           <img alt="Emperor Hadrian Statue From Tlos Outside Fethiye Museum" class="size-full wp-image-14597" height="640" src="//www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/Tlos-statue-Fethiye-Museum1.jpg" width="480"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Emperor Hadrian?
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Is this the statue of Emperor Hadrian? Curly hair and beards were in fashion at the time and so all three of these important people have curly hair and beards  not good for an amateur identifier of Roman statues!
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_14598" style="width: 490px">
          <a data-rel="lightbox[gallery-86Z9]" href="http://www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/tlos-statue-fethiye-museum-21.jpg">
           <img alt="Antoninus Pius Statue From Tlos Outside Fethiye Museum" class="size-full wp-image-14598" height="640" src="//www.turkeysforlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/tlos-statue-fethiye-museum-21.jpg" width="480"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Antoninus Pius?
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          So that must mean that this is Antoninus Pius  his face is slimmer than that of Emperor Hadrian and thats the only reasoning I can provide.
         </p>
         <p>
          We were lucky to be able to get photos of these fantastic finds from Tlos. If Fethiye Museum chooses to move them indoors for display, cameras are not allowed to be used inside. So, if youre in the area and youre interested in archaeological discoveries  and taking photographs of them  get yourself to Fethiye Museum.
         </p>
