      <h1 class="omc-post-heading-standard">
       Offline Glass: Keep your mates phoneless at the pub
      </h1>
      <div id="nr_fo_top_of_post">
      </div>
      <ul id="shareit" name="stickysocial" style="margin-left:-115px;">
       <li>
        <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http://www.beertruth.com/offline-glass-keep-your-mates-phoneless-at-the-pub/&amp;layout=box_count&amp;show_faces=false&amp;width=60&amp;action=like&amp;colorscheme=light&amp;height=65" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:54px; height:65px;">

       </li>

        <a class="twitter-share-button" data-count="vertical" data-via="[twitter]" href="http://twitter.com/share">

      <p>
       A pint glass has been designed by a Brazilian advertising agency with a very interesting objective in mind: keeping your friends face out of their phone when youre down the pub.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Offline Glass, created by Fischer &amp; Friends and unveiled at the Salve Jorge bar in Sao Paolo, does just that through its ingenious design, which ensures the drinker is unable to rest their pint on the table without using their phone as a coaster.
      </p>
      <p>
       While smartphones are an amazing step forward in terms of technology, they sure do have the potential to make people anti-social at the pub. Their GPS functionality may get you to the boozer when youre half-cut and have no idea where youre going, but upon arrival they are nothing but a scourge to conversation. No one wants to go for a pint with someone who has their face nose deep in Facebook or Candy Crush, and Fischer &amp; Friends has taken a very proactive approach to the problem.
      </p>
      <p>
       Claiming to rescue people from the online world, the Offline Glass is made like a traditional Brazilian chopp glass, but has a key gap in base that goes half way across and has a depth equivalent to a typical smartphone. If the space is not filled when the glass is put down on a table, it will topple over with all the precious beer going with it.
      </p>
      <p>
       Flavius Cinira, one of the owners of the Sao Paulo bar, explained how the establishment believes in a happy environment centred on good food and cold beer.
      </p>
      <p>
       Wanderley Romano, the other owner, insists that the Offline Glass embodies this. He said: It is an invitation for people to come here after the daily battle, turn off the online world, chat with friends and live in the reality around them, at least for a few hours.
      </p>
      <p>
       Those worried about their phone getting broken when using one of the novel glasses need not worry either, as it has been designed in such a way to prevent condensation running down off the glass onto the phone. Although it is still dangerously close to the suds in the event that one of your clumsy pals knocks the table and spills the pint.
      </p>
      <p>
       Despite the obvious risk and the rather paternal attitude to a common pub issue, the Offline Glass certainly gets the thumbs up. Just need one for a wine glass now.
      </p>
