     <h1 class="title" id="page-title">
      <a class="active" href="/article/howtos/control_your_mac_pc">
       Control Your Mac From A PC
      </a>
     </h1>
     <div class="region clearfix" id="main-content">
      <div class="node article published promoted not-sticky full-view author-cyoung odd clear-block" id="node-article-3777">
       <div class="node_article">
        <div class="info_bar clearfix">
         <h1 class="title_smaller noav">
          Control Your Mac From A PC
         </h1>
         <div class="submitted noav">
          <span class="posted_date">
           Posted 01/20/2009 at 10:47am
          </span>
          <span class="posted_by">
           | by Joe Rybicki
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="social_links_top">
          <div class="comments_wordbubble_wrap">
           <a href="/article/howtos/control_your_mac_pc#comments" title="Control Your Mac From A PC">
            <div class="comments_wordbubble">
             6
            </div>
           </a>
           <div class="comments_wordbubble_label">
            <a href="/article/howtos/control_your_mac_pc" title="Control Your Mac From A PC">
             Comments
            </a>
           </div>
          </div>
          <span class="st_facebook_hcount" displaytext="Share" st_title="Control Your Mac From A PC" st_url="http://www.maclife.com/article/howtos/control_your_mac_pc">
          </span>
          <span class="st_twitter_hcount" displaytext="Tweet" st_title="Control Your Mac From A PC" st_url="http://www.maclife.com/article/howtos/control_your_mac_pc">
          </span>
          <span class="st_sharethis_button" displaytext="Share" st_title="Control Your Mac From A PC" st_url="http://www.maclife.com/article/howtos/control_your_mac_pc">
          </span>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="blue_line">
        </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="Illustration of I'm a PC commericial" height="269" src="/files/u36/mac-PC_01_380.jpg" width="380"/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Even your PC can be a Mac.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Though Apples share of the computing market is continually growing, its still largely a Windows world. For many of us, that means we sit in front of Windows machines at work, at school, while visiting family, or when traveling. But that doesnt mean we have to be out of touch with our Macs altogether. In fact, with a couple pieces of completely free software and a few easy steps, you can take full control of your Mac from just about any Windows machine, whether its on the other side of the room or the other side of the planet.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What you need: A Mac running Mac OS 9 or later, for the Mac: Vine Server (free,
          <a href="http://www.testplant.com/downloads" target="_blank">
           www.redstonesoftware.com)
          </a>
          , a PC running Windows 95 or later, for the PC: VNC Viewer (free,
          <a href="http://www.realvnc.com/" target="_blank">
           www.realvnc.com
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Make Your Mac Receptive
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <img alt="Vine Server Pref menu screen shot" height="301" src="/files/u36/mac-PC_02_380.jpg" width="380"/>
         <strong>
          <br/>
          If your Mac and PC are on the same network, this is all you have to do on the Mac side.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Download Vine to your Mac and drag the Vine Server into your Applications folder. When launched, it configures itself to allow control of the main display over port 5900. If youre looking to control this machine from a PC on the same home network, youll need to do little else on this end except set a password: Go to Vine Server &gt; Preferences and enter one in the Connection tab. While youre there, take a look at the Device tab and make sure youre fine with the default settings there; if the Mac has a screen of its own youll probably want to check the Allow Display Dimming and/or Allow Screen Saver To Start boxes.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Get In The Numbers Game
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <img alt="external IP address screen shot" height="331" src="/files/u36/mac-PC_03_380.jpg" width="380"/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Write down your external (blurred in the screenshot) and internal IP addresses.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now head back to Vine Servers main menu. In the middle of the screen youll see two IP addresses; one is the number your Mac uses to identify itself on your local network, and the other is the number it uses to identify itself to the Internet. Write both numbers down. (A note about the external IP address: This is something you dont want made public, so guard it carefully, unless you want to open your machine up to Internet attacks.)
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Bare Your Soul
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <img alt="screen shot of Portforward.com" height="81" src="/files/u36/mac-PC_04_380.jpg" width="380"/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://portforward.com/" target="_blank">
           Portforward.com
          </a>
          can direct you to instructions that will enable your router to work with RealVNC.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre looking to access your Mac from somewhere other than a PC on the same network, youre going to need to take one more step to allow VNC Viewer to reach your Mac. This involves diving into the settings of your network routertrouble is, every router is different, so we cant tell you exactly how to do that. Luckily, the Internet can! Head over to portforward.com/english/routers/port_forwarding/routerindex.htm and select your router from the list. On the following screen, select RealVNC as the program and follow the directions.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4. Make Your PC More Outgoing
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          <img alt="VNC ip address screen shot" height="154" src="/files/u36/mac-PC_05_380.jpg" width="380"/>
          <br/>
          Your IP address (internal or external) goes in the Server field.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, on your PC, download the standalone VNC Viewer. Extract it if necessary and save it someplace memorable. Start it up, and youll be presented with a screen asking you to input the server info. If youre on the same network as your Mac, enter the internal IP address. If youre trying to access your Mac from anywhere other than your home network, enter the external IP address. A note about the external IP address: Chances are, unless youve negotiated a static IP address from your Internet provider, this number will change on a regular basis. So youll want to double-check the external IP frequently. A service like Dynamic DNS (free, www.dyndns.com/services/dns/dyndns) will map your dynamic IP address to a hostname you can use instead.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5. Fine-Tune Your Setup
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <img alt="screen shot VNC Viewer options" height="386" src="/files/u36/mac-PC_06_380.jpg" width="380"/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          If youre connecting to your Mac over the Net, dial down the colors.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Before clicking OK, lets make sure VNC Viewer is set up correctly for your connection. Click Options and choose the Color &amp; Encoding tab. Make sure the Auto Select box above the Preferred Encoding field is checked; this will ensure the client and host talk to each other as efficiently as possible. The one thing you may want to change here is Color Level. If both your PC and Mac are connected to the same network with wired connections, set Color Level to Full. If both machines are on the same network but one or both has a slow wireless connection, you probably wont want to go above Medium or youll experience some frustrating lag. And if youre connecting over the Internet, try either Low or Very Low, depending on your connection speed.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          6. Behold the Cross-Platform
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <img alt="screen shot of garage band app on a pc" height="304" src="/files/u36/mac-PC_07_380.jpg" width="380"/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Running GarageBand inside a window on a PCthats Mac-tastic!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Once thats set, go ahead and click OK. Youll be prompted for a password; enter the one you set in Step 1. Hey, look, a Mac window! On your PC! You can right-click the program name in your PCs taskbar to switch to full screen (hit F8 to switch back to window view) or to adjust options like Color Level, in case your connections not quite what you thought itd be. And there you have it: Now you dont have to be away from your Mac ever again.
        </p>

          <p class="post-info">

          <div class="content">
           <p>
            I use LogMeIn (www.logmein.com) which allows you to use their basic product for non-business purposes FOR FREE. I do this with several of my machines and have a couple of friends that I support (for free) configured with this product. I've been using LogMeIn for more than 6 months now, with no hassles from their sales dept. to purchase, no technical issues to speak of, and a lot of successful use.LogMeIn works without making holes in your firewall and does authentication through their servers. it works within the hosts web browser and has a small component that installs on the target. You establish an account with them (like I said - free) and install the client on the PC or Mac you want to control remotely. It is very easy to do and takes little time. I use it on the Mac (host) from within both Safari and Firefox. On the Windows PC's it works with IE, Firefox... look at their web site for details.The free version does not offer file transfer between target and host, otherwise it is in parity with their paid basic version. I also use the paid version to support 1 server and 10 workstations and am very happy, for those interested.I have no relationship with LogMeIn other than being a satisfied user &amp; customer.
