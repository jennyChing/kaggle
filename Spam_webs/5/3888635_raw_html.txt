      <h1>
       How to build a simple picnic table.
      </h1>
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="clear">
    </div>
    <div class="grid_5 alpha">
     <div class="grid_text">
      Tired of  imported junk that falls apart in a few months? Or just cheap? Here are a few ideas on how to put together a garden table or picnic table.
      <br/>
      The point of this is to do it cheap, with scarp lumber or used lumber. So the only plan is to be able to use the wood you find. A  detailed plan would mean you'd have to go buy something, and if you price materials you'll be well over $100 and have a wobbly table that may fall apart. So for at least your first try, use junk to make junk.
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="grid_8">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox caption-from-next" href="/images/grid24_24/6670/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-simple-end.jpg" title="Simple picnic table legs">
      <img alt="Simple picnic table legs - grid24_12" class="grid_image" height="409" src="/images/grid24_12/6670/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-simple-end.jpg" title="Simple picnic table legs - grid24_12" width="310"/>
     </a>
     <div class="grid_image_caption">
      There are several important points about an outdoor table. You'll need to brace the legs both ways. The 1X3 or 1X4 is keeping  the table from rocking one way and the 90 degree is keeping it stable the other.
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="grid_11 omega">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox caption-from-next" href="/images/grid24_24/6905/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-simple-brace.jpg" title="picnic table legs and bracing frame">
      <img alt="picnic table legs and bracing frame - grid24_12" class="grid_image" height="426" src="/images/grid24_12/6905/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-simple-brace.jpg" title="picnic table legs and bracing frame - grid24_12" width="430"/>
     </a>
     <div class="grid_image_caption">
      The original table had two nails from the top to the brace. After 50 years they got loose and I had to put in a  metal clip and some short screws to secure it back to the table top.
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="clear">
    </div>
   </div>
   <div class="container_24">
    <div class="grid_8 alpha">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox caption-from-next" href="/images/grid24_24/8446/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-upside-down.jpg" title="upside down picnic table">
      <img alt="upside down picnic table - grid24_12" class="grid_image" height="248" src="/images/grid24_12/8446/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-upside-down.jpg" title="upside down picnic table - grid24_12" width="310"/>
     </a>
     <div class="grid_image_caption">
      The original cross member was nailed, a couple of screws stabilized it. These are redwood legs, notice they are slightly eaten.
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="grid_8">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox caption-from-next" href="/images/grid24_24/4857/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-end1.jpg" title="How to build a  picnic table. Bracing is important and the metal clips help.">
      <img alt="How to build a  picnic table. Bracing is important and the metal clips help. - grid24_12" class="grid_image" height="232" src="/images/grid24_12/4857/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-end1.jpg" title="How to build a  picnic table. Bracing is important and the metal clips help. - grid24_12" width="310"/>
     </a>
     <div class="grid_image_caption">
      This table is out of 2x4's and 2x6's, you'll need help just to right it. Notice the metal clip.
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="grid_8 omega">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox caption-from-next" href="/images/grid24_24/875/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-end-braces.jpg" title="picnic table bracing">
      <img alt="picnic table bracing - grid24_12" class="grid_image" height="232" src="/images/grid24_12/875/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-end-braces.jpg" title="picnic table bracing - grid24_12" width="310"/>
     </a>
     <div class="grid_image_caption">
      This is the repair the engine or cut up the cow table. As long as the bracing holds it will support a great deal of weight.
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="clear">
    </div>
    <div class="grid_24 alpha omega">
     <div class="grid_text">
      The legs need to be all the same length, hard to fudge on that. So line them up and cut them all at the same time, about 30 inches if vertical, 32 if spread out a little.
      <hr/>
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="clear">
    </div>
   </div>
   <div class="container_24">
    <div class="grid_4 alpha">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox" href="/images/grid24_24/7541/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-top.jpg" title="the top of a finished picnic table">
      <img alt="the top of a finished picnic table - grid24_6" class="grid_image" height="344" src="/images/grid24_6/7541/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/picnic-table-top.jpg" title="the top of a finished picnic table - grid24_6" width="150"/>
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="grid_9">
     <div class="grid_text">
      <script type="text/javascript">

      <br/>
      If you use fir 2x6's for the table top you'll get splinters!
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="grid_11 omega">

     <div class="grid_image_caption">
      An old commercially made redwood picnic table.
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="clear">
    </div>
   </div>
   <div class="container_24">
    <div class="grid_8 alpha">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox caption-from-next" href="/images/grid24_24/12162/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/gate-table.jpg" title="This outdoor table was built out of a gate, that had been built out of a fence. Took about 1 hour to build and a few screws and metal clips.">
      <img alt="This outdoor table was built out of a gate, that had been built out of a fence. Took about 1 hour to build and a few screws and metal clips. - grid24_12" class="grid_image" height="179" src="/images/grid24_12/12162/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/gate-table.jpg" title="This outdoor table was built out of a gate, that had been built out of a fence. Took about 1 hour to build and a few screws and metal clips. - grid24_12" width="310"/>
     </a>
     <div class="grid_image_caption">
      If you understand the basics of how to make a table you can use almost any material to make one quickly.  This was a garden gate that still had good wood but the cross brace had a bunch of nails in it but still sagged.
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="grid_8">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox" href="/images/grid24_24/12163/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/gate-table-side.jpg" title="The sides were two pieces of cedar fence that had bad ends. I cut the bad parts off.">
      <img alt="The sides were two pieces of cedar fence that had bad ends. I cut the bad parts off. - grid24_12" class="grid_image" height="283" src="/images/grid24_12/12163/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/gate-table-side.jpg" title="The sides were two pieces of cedar fence that had bad ends. I cut the bad parts off. - grid24_12" width="310"/>
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="grid_8 omega">
     <a class="grid_image_lightbox" href="/images/grid24_24/12164/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/gate-table-under.jpg" title="Notice the metal holding the 1-2 lath as the cross braces. The metal makes it easy to adjust the table to make it square.">
      <img alt="Notice the metal holding the 1-2 lath as the cross braces. The metal makes it easy to adjust the table to make it square. - grid24_12" class="grid_image" height="265" src="/images/grid24_12/12164/garden/howto/pictures/furniture/gate-table-under.jpg" title="Notice the metal holding the 1-2 lath as the cross braces. The metal makes it easy to adjust the table to make it square. - grid24_12" width="310"/>
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="clear">
    </div>
    <div class="grid_24 alpha omega">
     <div class="grid_text">
      But laying on it side it makes a great table top, perfect for one or two people or a laptop. The only new things for this table were the screws and a few of the metal clips.

     </div>
