     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Fried Apple Rings
     </h1>
     <img height="521" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Fried-Apple-Rings-Main-790x521.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         January 19, 2013
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="659" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/fried-apple-rings/fried-apple-rings-main/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/fried-apple-rings-main" rel="attachment wp-att-1085">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         What is not to love about onion rings? Fried, salty and the perfect vehicle for a variety of condiments. Every time I am enjoying onion rings, I think to myself, what other wonders would be great in this format? Apples are always my first thought. So here you go, fried apple rings for your enjoyment. Perfect for your next football gathering or just because!
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="338" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/y6GlBHHuIgU" width="600">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1
            <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/ingredient/egg/">
             Egg
            </a>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cup Buttermilk
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cup all-purpose flour
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon Baking Powder
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon Salt
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            4 large Granny Smith apples
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            Vegetable Oil
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            Sea Salt
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/fried-apple-rings/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            4 servings
           </span>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT30M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            30 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT15M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            15 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <meta content="PT15M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Cook time
           <div style="float: right">
            15 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Recipe Types:
           </h2>
           Appetizer, Dessert
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Main Ingredient:
           </h2>
           Apple
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Diet:
           </h2>
           Vegetarian
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. Combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder and salt. Set aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="699" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/fried-apple-rings/making-batter/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/making-batter" rel="attachment wp-att-1089">
          <img alt="Making Batter" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1089" height="419" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Making-Batter-600x419.jpg" title="Making Batter" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Whisk 1 egg and then whisk in 1 cup of buttermilk.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Slice 4 large Granny Smith apples into 1/4 inch thick slices using varying sized biscuit cutters to make rings out of the slices, discarding smallest circle containing core.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/fried-apple-rings/sliced-apples/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/sliced-apples" rel="attachment wp-att-1088">
          <img alt="Sliced Apples" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1088" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Sliced-Apples-600x399.jpg" title="Sliced Apples" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/fried-apple-rings/apple-rings/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/apple-rings" rel="attachment wp-att-1087">
          <img alt="Apple Rings" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1087" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Apple-Rings-600x399.jpg" title="Apple Rings" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Heat oil to 375 degrees F. (Caution: Do not let the oil get too hot or your rings will burn quickly. If rings are cooking too quickly, lower the heat of the oil a bit.) Combine the liquid and dry ingredients while the oil heats up.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Dip apple rings into batter, removing any excess. A little is okay, it gives them a nice swirly look. Cook in oil in small batches, turning frequently to monitor browning.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/fried-apple-rings/drying-fried-apple-rings/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/drying-fried-apple-rings" rel="attachment wp-att-1086">
          <img alt="Drying Fried Apple Rings" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1086" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Drying-Fried-Apple-Rings-600x399.jpg" title="Drying Fried Apple Rings" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         6. When the rings are golden brown, remove to a wire rack and sprinkle with sea salt.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="1500" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/fried-apple-rings/fried-apple-rings-full/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/fried-apple-rings-full" rel="attachment wp-att-1084">
          <img alt="Fried Apple Rings Full" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1084" height="900" itemprop="image" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Fried-Apple-Rings-Full-600x900.jpg" title="Fried Apple Rings Full" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
