          <h2 class="hide">
           Tilt-Shift, Time-Lapse Video from Camera Phone Transforms the Real World into a Mini Toyland
          </h2>
          <p>
           This colorful image may look like a miniature set of model cars, foam buildings and painted grass, but it's nothing of the sort. It's a still photo from a
           <a href="http://kakepipe.com/?p=5" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            time-lapse video
           </a>
           that
           <a href="http://kakepipe.com/?page_id=8" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Stu Kennedy
           </a>
           shot in his hometown of Lincoln, England. But it's not your ordinary time-lapse. Kennedy used his trusty new
           <a href="http://www.samsung.com/global/microsite/galaxys2/html/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Samsung Galaxy S2
           </a>
           and its 8-megapixel camera to capture the video in high-definition (1080p). And that's not all. He also used a post-editing technique called
           <a href="http://www.wonderhowto.com/search/tilt-shift/">
            tilt-shift
           </a>
           , which transforms the normal footage into a miniature toy illusion. Check out the video.
          </p>
          <span class="cb">
          </span>
          <figure class="whtGallery" id="19443965id57310413GdocPart2" role="group">

          <p>
           Normally, photographers wishing to get the above look would resort to digital SLRs with special, expensive lenses. Canon sells
           <a href="http://www.learn.usa.canon.com/resources/articles/2011/intro_tilt_shift_lenses_article.shtml" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            two types
           </a>
           of tilt-shift lenses for its DSLRs, but the prices range from
           <a href="http://www.google.com/products/catalog?q=canon+tilt+shift+lens&amp;cid=7550825049155367244" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            $2,000
           </a>
           to
           <a href="http://www.google.com/products/catalog?q=canon+tilt+shift+lens&amp;cid=1085089140392005304" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            $2,400
           </a>
           , making it difficult for anyone but professionals to enjoy the fine art of
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tilt-shift_photography" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            tilt-shift photography
           </a>
           (unless you want to make your own
           <a href="http://www.wonderhowto.com/how-to-tilt-shift-photography-create-miniature-effect-284738/">
            DIY tilt-shift lens
