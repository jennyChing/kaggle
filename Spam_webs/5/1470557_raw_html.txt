         <p>
          I have been craving coconut cake for weeks. Normally I make one for Easter, but I simply ran out of time this year. Ever since Ive been trying to find the perfect occasion to make one. Yesterday I decided that I dont need a special occasion to bake a cake, and whipped up this Two Layer Coconut Cake just because.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/two-layer-coconut-cake.jpg">
           <img alt="Two Layer Coconut Cake Recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-23745" height="427" src="http://www.aboutamom.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/two-layer-coconut-cake.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          My son happened to stop by just as I was finishing the cake, so you might notice that there are a couple of pieces missing from this picture. He seems to have good luck like that. I think he must have some sort of sixth sense or radar that goes off whenever I am baking something.
         </p>
         <p>
          I hope you enjoy this every day two layer coconut cake as much as we have.
         </p>
         <p>
          You will need one white cake recipe (below), buttercream frosting (recipe below) and 4 cups of shredded coconut for this cake.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Batter:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          5 eggs
          <br/>
          1 cup unsalted butter
          <br/>
          1 cup whole milk
          <br/>
          3 cups sifted cake flour
          <br/>
          1 tablespoon baking powder
          <br/>
          1/2 teaspoon salt
          <br/>
          2 cups sugar
          <br/>
          1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Prepare Batter:
          </strong>
          Let eggs, butter, and milk stand at room temperature for 30 minutes. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. In a medium bowl combine cake flour, baking powder, and salt; set aside. Butter two 81-1/2-inch round cake pans and line bottoms with waxed paper. Butter waxed paper and lightly flour; set aside.
         </p>
         <p>
          In large mixing bowl beat butter with an electric mixer on medium to high speed for 30 seconds. Add 2 cups sugar and 1 Tbsp. vanilla; beat 3 to 4 minutes on medium speed until well combined. Add eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Alternately add flour mixture and milk to butter mixture, beating on low after each addition until just combined. Divide batter between the two pans.
         </p>
         <p>
          Bake 20 to 25 minutes or until tops spring back when lightly touched. Cool layers in pans on wire racks for 10 minutes. Remove the cake layers from pans; peel off waxed paper and discard. Cool on wire racks.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Buttercream Frosting:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          3 cups confectioners sugar
          <br/>
          1 cup butter
          <br/>
          1 teaspoon vanilla extract
          <br/>
          1 to 2 tablespoons whipping cream
         </p>
         <p>
          In a standing mixer fitted with whisk attachment, mix together sugar and butter. Mix on low speed until well blended and then increase speed to medium and beat for another 3 minutes.
         </p>
         <p>
          Add vanilla and cream and continue to beat on medium speed for 1 minute more, adding more cream if needed for spreading consistency.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Assemble Cake:
          </strong>
          On a cake platter, frost the top of first layer and sprinkle with coconut. Place the second layer of cake on top of the first, and frost top and sides of cake. Sprinkle coconut generously over top and sides of cake, pressing in gently with your fingers as necessary.
         </p>

  extend(Mimi.Signups.EmbedValidation.prototype, {

            this.textValidation(input);
          } else {
            this.dropdownValidation(field, input);
          }
        }
      }
      this.htmlEmbedDropdownValidation(field);
    },

      if(input.id == 'signup_email') return;
 
      var val = input.value;
      if(val == '') {
        this.textFieldError(input);
        this.isValid = false;
      } else {
        this.removeTextFieldError(input)
      }
    },
 
    dropdownValidation: function(field, input) {

        this.isValid = false;
