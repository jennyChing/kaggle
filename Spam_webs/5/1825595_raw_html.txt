         <div class="av-special-heading av-special-heading-h3" style="padding-bottom:10px;">
          <h3 class="av-special-heading-tag" itemprop="headline">
           Round Table: Reflections On Baselworld 2015
          </h3>
          <div class="special-heading-border">
           <div class="special-heading-inner-border">
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <section class="av_textblock_section" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="https://schema.org/BlogPosting">
          <div class="avia_textblock" itemprop="text">
           <p>
            Its already a tradition! Please join our Quill &amp; Pad round table discussion on the fair that was: Baselworld 2015. Our participants are:
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             IS Ian Skellern
            </strong>
            , co-founder of Quill &amp; Pad
            <br/>
            <strong>
             ED Elizabeth Doerr
            </strong>
            , co-founder of Quill &amp; Pad
            <br/>
            <strong>
             BP Boris Pjanic
            </strong>
            , resident vintage virtuoso for Quill &amp; Pad, founder of
            <a href="http://www.exitwatch24.com/blog/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.exitwatch24.com/blog/', 'Watches &amp; Art');" target="_blank">
             Watches &amp; Art
            </a>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             NO Nancy Olson
            </strong>
            , resident pen expert for Quill &amp; Pad and editor of
            <a href="http://nancyolsonink.blogspot.ch/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://nancyolsonink.blogspot.ch/', 'NancyOlsonInk');" target="_blank">
             NancyOlsonInk
            </a>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             LN Louis Nardin
            </strong>
            , editor-in-chief of
            <a href="http://www.thewatches.tv/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.thewatches.tv/', 'The Watches TV');" target="_blank">
             The Watches TV
            </a>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             BC Ben Clymer
            </strong>
            , executive editor of
            <a href="http://www.hodinkee.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.hodinkee.com/', 'Hodinkee');" target="_blank">
             Hodinkee
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             IS:
            </strong>
            In comparison with the cold and gloom of the SIHH  the gloom in that instance being generated by the shock of the announcement of a stronger Swiss franc rather than the actual weather  Baselworld was all sunshine and smiles. It was amazing the difference a couple of months makes.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            Yes, indeed, Ian! The Swiss franc announcement didnt seem to hold perpetual sway, and I had the distinct feeling that people were generally satisfied with the current commercial environment.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             IS:
            </strong>
            While Baselworld was by no means economically sunny for all brands, the mood in general was much more positive than it has been for the last few months.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            Which is quite interesting, actually, when you consider that not only the Swiss franc, but also the topic of the Apple Watch, and smartwatches in general, has been wreaking a bit of havoc.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             BC:
            </strong>
            Baselworld 2015 was my seventh, and perhaps its my undoubted levels of horological overexposure or simply my old age, but I found it to be one of the least rewarding. The industry seemed to be simultaneously in a total panic over looming smartwatch technology and as shiny and confident as ever in its supreme Swiss luxuriousness; I think both are probably accurate, for the record.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             LN:
            </strong>
            Apple and the other digital tycoons have been frightening the traditional watch industry for years now with the wrist computers they are working on. In my opinion, the worst thing would be to think it wont have a radical impact on the relation people will have with their wearable objects (watches in our case).
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             BP:
            </strong>
            While having feared that the smartwatch would intimidate the luxury watch world, I personally could not feel any such intimidation at all at Baselworld. The overall mood seemed very upbeat, positive. I had a short conversation with Swatch Group representatives about the smartwatch, and people there seemed to have no fear of it, either.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             NO:
            </strong>
            Smartwatches, I think, are just beginning to explore their potential. I believe well see much more of an explosion of hybrid smartwatches at next years Baselworld.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             LN:
            </strong>
            Actually, the most interesting thing here is not the fact that a new type of watch is emerging, but rather that the vision of Apple, in particular, seems to be to transform its devices into luxury items. It is true that an iPhone is a lot of things (including expensive), but they can take a step further as digitech analysts say. Indeed, if you have a look at luxury industry figures, they are appealing enough to motivate new players. The concept of luxotronics should become more and more visible in the months to come and maybe it will create tension with what is a key point in luxury: the objects must last and they must be reparable.
           </p>
           <p>
            This is why, today, a connected watch is not a smartwatch. Indeed, playing with words is a nice game, and this one started a few days before Baselworld began with
            <a href="http://www.h-moser.com/en" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.h-moser.com/en', 'H. Moser &amp; Cie');" target="_blank">
             H. Moser &amp; Cie
            </a>
            announcing a smartwatch as a teaser. The
            <a href="http://www.h-moser.com/en/collection/endeavour-perpetual-calendar-1341-0103-argente" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.h-moser.com/en/collection/endeavour-perpetual-calendar-1341-0103-argente', 'Endeavour Perpetual Calendar');" target="_blank">
             Endeavour Perpetual Calendar
            </a>
            that was released a few days after the teaser was surprising and amusing. And it also reinforced the notion of elegance, taste, and education that must go with luxury.
           </p>
           <p>
            In that same vein, Baselworld this year was really full of smart watches. (Thank you for driving that point home, Edouard.)
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11745" style="width: 1040px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Laurent_Ferrier_cushion_3760.jpg">
             <img alt="Laurent Ferrier Galet Square" class="size-large wp-image-11745 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Laurent_Ferrier_cushion_3760.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="686" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Laurent_Ferrier_cushion_3760-1030x686.jpg" width="1030"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Laurent Ferrier Galet Square
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Controversy. What controversy?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             IS:
            </strong>
            Two Baselworld controversies (and I use the term loosely) passed me by completely during the course of the fair. The first was that surrounding the
            <a href="http://www.laurentferrier.ch/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.laurentferrier.ch/', 'Laurent Ferrier Galet Square');" target="_blank">
             Laurent Ferrier Galet Square
            </a>
             apparently the cushion-shaped case was too Panerai-like for some people. All I can say to that is, what about all the brands with nearly identical round cases?
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             BC:
            </strong>
            Laurent Ferrier really impressed me with this new watch, which unusually for the brand also comes in stainless steel.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             IS:
            </strong>
            The second storm-on-a-pinhead was in response to the new
            <a href="http://www.patek.com/en/mens-watches/complications/5524G-001" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.patek.com/en/mens-watches/complications/5524G-001', 'Patek Philippe Calatrava Travel Time');" target="_blank">
             Patek Philippe Calatrava Travel Time
            </a>
            . Many thought that the watch was an outrage because the brand didnt have a history  or a large enough history  in making pilots watches, so it should never do one.
           </p>
           <p>
            During the fair, we basically had creationists (brands should only offer the same style of watches they had created in the beginning) versus evolutionists (brands can allow collections to evolve over time). I quite liked the Calatrava Travel Time. As far as sacrilege goes, its hardly a frontrunner.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11787" style="width: 1040px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Patek-Philippe-Travel-Time_6238.jpg">
             <img alt="Patek Philippe Calatrava Travel Time" class="wp-image-11787 size-large pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Patek-Philippe-Travel-Time_6238.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="1030" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Patek-Philippe-Travel-Time_6238-1030x1030.jpg" width="1030"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Patek Philippe Calatrava Travel Time
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             NO:
            </strong>
            Patek Philippes new pilot watch did seem to incite a lot of discussion, Ian. In my opinion, its a winner and I commend the brand for stepping outside of its traditional comfort zone, much like it did when the then-controversial Twenty-4 launched in 1999.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            I, too, like the Calatrava Travel Time and see no reason why it should have sparked so much insane discussion. Perhaps the only error was in the brand pre-releasing its press release so that bloggers and online journalists were judging solely on renderings and packshot photos, which were admittedly less aesthetic than the actual watch. I was very relieved to see the timepiece in the metal and discover it is as aesthetic and interesting as a Patek Philippe should be.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11878" style="width: 1040px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Patek-Philippe_Rattrapante_5370_6291.jpg">
             <img alt="Patek Philippe Rattrapante Reference 5370P" class="wp-image-11878 size-large pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Patek-Philippe_Rattrapante_5370_6291.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="1030" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Patek-Philippe_Rattrapante_5370_6291-1030x1030.jpg" width="1030"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Patek Philippe Rattrapante Reference 5370P
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             BP:
            </strong>

This watch has a wonderful aura thanks to its stunning enamel dial in black, a deep, shiny black. The watch combines a taste of vintage with modern technology; the case is also extremely nice as is the movement.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             BC:
            </strong>

           </p>

            <strong>
             Impressive or not?
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             BC:
            </strong>

There is simply no reason for all brands to offer $50,000 watches.
           </p>
           <p>
            There is indeed a natural pecking order to Baselworld and watches in general, and those at the bottom are just as important as those at the top in their own way. Frankly, a simple, in-house, well-priced, time-only watch like
            <a href="http://www.rolex.com/watches.html#oyster-collection" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.rolex.com/watches.html#oyster-collection', 'Rolex');" target="_blank">
             Rolex
            </a>
            s $5,700 39 mm
            <a href="http://www.rolex.com/watches.html#oyster-collection" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.rolex.com/watches.html#oyster-collection', 'Oyster Perpetual ');" target="_blank">
             Oyster Perpetual
            </a>
            is far more exciting to any real watch collector or journalist than, say, a tourbillon from Raymond Weil.
           </p>

            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            While I agree with you, Ben, what I saw when I visited
            <a href="http://www.tagheuer.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.tagheuer.com/', 'TAG Heuer ');" target="_blank">
             TAG Heuer
            </a>
            (which also happened to be my very last appointment) impressed me more than I thought it would. We had all been speculating on what exactly will happen with TAG Heuer now that Jean-Claude Biver has established order among the LVMH brands (see my speculations by reading
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/2014/12/11/jean-claude-biver-rearranges-lvmh-watch-brands-tag-heuer-ceo-stephane-linder-resigns-coincidence/" target="_blank">
             Jean-Claude Biver Restructures LVMH Watch Brands; TAG Heuer CEO Stphane Linder Resigns. Coincidence?
            </a>

           </p>
           <p>

            <a href="http://us.tagheuer.com/en/watch-news/tag-heuer-carrera-calibre-heuer-01" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://us.tagheuer.com/en/watch-news/tag-heuer-carrera-calibre-heuer-01', 'Carrera Heuer 01');" target="_blank">

            and 02 models: the 01 is an in-house Swiss chronograph retailing for less than 5,000 Swiss francs. Going one further, the yet-to-be-released 02 is this same chronograph with the addition of a tourbillon at 6 oclock. It is set to retail for less than 15,000 Swiss francs. (Read this whole story at
            <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/elizabethdoerr/2015/03/30/tag-heuers-2015-baselworld-cheapest-swiss-tourbillon-smartwatch-announcement-price-reductions/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.forbes.com/sites/elizabethdoerr/2015/03/30/tag-heuers-2015-baselworld-cheapest-swiss-tourbillon-smartwatch-announcement-price-reductions/', 'TAG Heuers 2015 Baselworld: Cara Delevingne Special Edition, Smarwatch Announcement');" target="_blank">

           <p>

           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             BC:
            </strong>
            I began to think about why some of the lower end brands appear to be dabbling in the up-market and I came to the conclusion that they, of all Swiss watchmakers, are to be the most impacted by what Apple and the rest of Silicon Valley does with their watches. The lower level mechanical watches have much to fear because it is their customers that are less emotionally (and financially) vested into their mechanical watches, and they will be the first to say Okay, Ill give it up and try the Apple Watch.
           </p>
           <p>
            That much is clear, though I think this realization made me have an even stronger distaste for a $2,500 watch company making a $25,000 watch because it actually hasnt even done anything to warrant this upgrade. Had such companies spent years developing their own movements or building out great materials, that is one thing. But so many of these brands simply bought a movement from a neighbor and added a zero to the price. If there is one way to fall victim to the Apple watch and those like it, its by being lazy. Unfortunately, that is what I saw a lot of this year at Baselworld.
           </p>

            </a>
            and came away with deep respect in just how well the pieces are crafted and how tactile the objects are. The Bulgari Serpenti really blew me away.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11575" style="width: 1040px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Chopard-Happy-Fish_4382.jpg">
             <img alt="Lume: In the light this looks like a normal Chopard Happy Diamonds, but in the dark a larger fish glowingly appears" class="wp-image-11575 size-large pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Chopard-Happy-Fish_4382.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="686" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Chopard-Happy-Fish_4382-1030x686.jpg" width="1030"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             In the light the Happy Fish looks like a normal Chopard Happy Diamonds model, but in the dark a larger fish glowingly appears
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            The jeweled watches arent usually my personal favorites either, Ian. But there were jewelry watches that I really enjoyed this year like the Chopard Happy Fish, which boasted luminous mother-of-pearl and diamonds for a superb glow-in-the-dark effect (see
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/2015/03/31/lighting-up-with-lume-at-baselworld-2015-warning-sunglasses-advised/">
             Lighting Up With Lume At Baselworld 2015. Warning: Sunglasses Advised!
            </a>
            ). How creative!
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             NO:
            </strong>
            Im happy to proclaim that ladies mechanical watches are no longer a trend  theyre now an integral part of the language of fine watches for women. This gives me the freedom to delve into the intricacies of the mechanism, rather than just stopping short at the fact that there is a mechanism!
           </p>
           <p>
            I was quite impressed with
            <a href="https://www.faberge.com/Splash.aspx" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.faberge.com/Splash.aspx', 'Faberg');" target="_blank">
             Faberg
            </a>
            s new
            <a href="http://www.faberge.com/maincategory/87-timepieces.aspx" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.faberge.com/maincategory/87-timepieces.aspx', 'Lady Complique');" target="_blank">
             Lady Complique
            </a>
            collection with its Agenhor-developed movement. This collection represents the best of both worlds of fine watchmaking and great design  exactly what one would expect from a brand like Faberg.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11436" style="width: 1040px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Faberge_Lady-Compliquee_Peacock.jpg">
             <img alt="Faberg Lady Complique Peacock" class="size-large wp-image-11436 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Faberge_Lady-Compliquee_Peacock.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="1030" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Faberge_Lady-Compliquee_Peacock-1030x1030.jpg" width="1030"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Faberg Lady Complique Peacock
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            I completely agree with you, Nancy. That is one incredible timepiece, and it by far overshadowed the only marginally less interesting tourbillon-outfitted, Giulio Papi-created Faberg offering for men.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             NO:
            </strong>
            Ladies watch dials featured everything from pearl marquetry to butterfly wings (Harry Winston) to embroidery (Dior)  lots of thread this year!  and though we keep hearing that yellow gold is making its way back, white gold and rose gold cases still obviously rule.
           </p>
           <p>
            There doesnt seem to be such a great chasm between high jewelry watches and mechanical movements anymore. It used to be that if a watch was designed for after 5 oclock, it was invariably quartz. Not so anymore. I really liked
            <a href="http://www.breguet.com/en" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.breguet.com/en', 'Breguet');" target="_blank">
             Breguet
            </a>
            s latest
            <a href="http://www.breguet.com/en/timepieces/reine-de-naples" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.breguet.com/en/timepieces/reine-de-naples', 'Reine de Naples');" target="_blank">
             Reine de Naples
            </a>
            Haute Joaillerie and Jaquet Drozs new ladies timepieces.
            <a href="http://www.dior.com/home/en_int" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.dior.com/home/en_int', 'Dior');" target="_blank">
             Dior
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.chanel.com/en_US/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.chanel.com/en_US/', 'Chanel ');" target="_blank">
             Chanel
            </a>
            cant lose with new renditions of the
            <a href="http://www.dior.com/diortv/en_us/videos/timepieces/dior-viii-grand-bal-r%C3%A9sille" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.dior.com/diortv/en_us/videos/timepieces/dior-viii-grand-bal-r%C3%A9sille', 'Dior VIII Grand Bal');" target="_blank">
             Dior VIII Grand Bal
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.chanel.com/en_US/Watches/j12_white-classic#j12-collector-H4341" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.chanel.com/en_US/Watches/j12_white-classic#j12-collector-H4341', 'J12');" target="_blank">
             J12
            </a>
            respectively.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11487" style="width: 1040px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Jaquet-Droz_Lady-8-Flower_open-closed_2.jpg">
             <img alt="Jaquet Droz Lady 8 Flower with lotus flower closed (left) and open" class="size-large wp-image-11487 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Jaquet-Droz_Lady-8-Flower_open-closed_2.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="891" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Jaquet-Droz_Lady-8-Flower_open-closed_2-1030x891.jpg" width="1030"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Jaquet Droz Lady 8 Flower with lotus flower closed (left) and open
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            Faberg and
            <a href="http://www.jaquet-droz.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.jaquet-droz.com/', 'Jaquet Droz');" target="_blank">
             Jaquet Droz
            </a>
            were the big, big winners in terms of complicated ladies watches as far as I am concerned. The
            <a href="http://www.jaquet-droz.com/en/collections/novelties/lady-8-flower" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.jaquet-droz.com/en/collections/novelties/lady-8-flower', 'Lady 8 Flower ');" target="_blank">
             Lady 8 Flower
            </a>
            is even one of my very top picks for the fair. I could not stop pressing the button to make the lotus flower bloom. It just mesmerized me! (See
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/2015/03/26/jaquet-droz-in-bloom-the-astonishing-lady-8-flower-automat/">
             Jaquet Droz In Bloom: The Astonishing Lady 8 Flower Automat
            </a>
            .)
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Trends and Bubbles
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             NO:
            </strong>
            In all, the story begun at SIHH continued at Baselworld, with vintage styling, thinner cases, new in-house movements, and skeleton designs being popular. Many brands, from Aerowatch to Hublot introduced skeleton models: the former in the form of a ladies watch and the latter in combination with the phases of the moon.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             LN:
            </strong>
            I found that the vintage trend seems to be getting short of breath  as well as the unofficial competition to have the most complex, spectacular, innovative, unexpected new product too. And this is not a bad thing. Instead of revisiting the past or looking for technical oohs and aahs in order to get attention, brands seemed to forget about the rest and focus on who they really are and what they really are able to do. Sometimes it is just modifying the ergonomics of a case to make it more comfortable, sometimes it is question of redesigning a dial to make it better balanced, or to decorate it in very special way that fits the brand DNA better.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             IS:
            </strong>
            Im not sure if this is a trend (I hope so), but quite a few brands appeared to be quite serious in pushing prices down rather than continuing the habitual year-on-year rises. This may only be a response to the stronger Swiss franc (which is mainly a problem for the euro-zone countries), but long may it spread and continue.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11452" style="width: 1040px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Corum-Bubble_black_6529.jpg">
             <img alt="Corum Bubble All-Black" class="size-large wp-image-11452 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Corum-Bubble_black_6529.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="686" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Corum-Bubble_black_6529-1030x686.jpg" width="1030"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Corum Bubble All-Black
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            I thought that
            <a href="http://www.corum.ch/en" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.corum.ch/en', 'Corum');" target="_blank">
             Corum
            </a>
            absolutely nailed its new
            <a href="http://www.corum.ch/en/corums-novelties" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.corum.ch/en/corums-novelties', 'Bubble');" target="_blank">
             Bubble
            </a>
            . While it was definitely the same design, the case had been reformatted for a nicer fit. A very attractive proposition at around 3,500 Swiss francs for the limited edition models. (See
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/2015/03/25/the-corum-bubble-is-back/">
             The Corum Bubble Is Back!
            </a>
            )
           </p>
           <p>
            And
            <a href="http://ressence.eu/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://ressence.eu/', 'Ressence');" target="_blank">
             Ressence
            </a>
            is just getting better and better, too. Two years ago I would not have thought that possible, the product was already so good. But now the little tweaks and color enhancements are definitely noteworthy.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             IS:
            </strong>
            I liked the new Corum Bubble so much I ordered one!
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             LN:
            </strong>
            Industrially speaking, brands seem to be introducing more new time-only calibers instead of looking for the next baroque complication. These efforts have increased the level of consistency in the products by making them really unique, different, interesting and, in the end, increasing the value you get for the money you pay. In other words, it seemed to me that the motto to prepare this fair was: really be yourself or you will become invisible and disappear.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11050" style="width: 696px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Hermes_Slim_8817.jpg">
             <img alt="Slim dHerms 32 with diamond-set bezel" class="size-large wp-image-11050 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Hermes_Slim_8817.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="1030" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Hermes_Slim_8817-686x1030.jpg" width="686"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Slim dHerms 32 mm in with diamond-set bezel
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             ED:
            </strong>
            And Herms did that just beautifully. I mean really beautifully. The new
            <a href="http://www.hermes.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.hermes.com/', 'Slim dHerms ');" target="_blank">
             Slim dHerms
            </a>
            is an a-plus winner in my book as a new brand pillar collection. Herms has really captured the essence of day-to-day wear without losing its signature playfulness. (See
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/2015/03/18/introducing-slim-dhermes-the-elegant-new-backbone-of-the-hermes-collection/" target="_blank">
             Introducing Slim dHerms: The Elegant New Backbone Of The Herms Collection
            </a>
            .)
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_11836" style="width: 1040px">
            <a href="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/QP-Converse.jpg">
             <img alt="If the shoe fits. High heels versus Quill &amp; Pad Converse." class="size-large wp-image-11836 pib-hover-img" data-pin-media="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/QP-Converse.jpg" data-pin-url="http://quillandpad.com/2015/04/09/round-table-reflections-on-baselworld-2015/" height="1030" src="http://quillandpad.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/QP-Converse-1030x1030.jpg" width="1030"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             I told Ian to wear sensible shoes!
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             IS:
            </strong>
            Finally, I just wanted to mention this: if anyone thinks covering Baselworld is endless champagne and roses, I borrowed a fitness band for the week and discovered that we were walking 8-10 kilometers (5-6 miles) a day, each and every day, just getting from meeting to meeting. They should market Baselworld to the press as a health event (though it isnt always healthy for the wallet).
           </p>
          </div>
         </section>

String.prototype.trim = function() {
  return this.replace(/^\s+|\s+$/g,"");

function createCookie(name,value,days) {
  if (days) {
    var date = new Date();
    date.setTime(date.getTime()+(days*24*60*60*1000));
    var expires = "; expires="+date.toGMTString();
  } else {
    var expires = "";
  }
  document.cookie = name+"="+value+expires+"; path=/";
}

function readCookie(name) {
  var nameEQ = name + "=";
  var ca = document.cookie.split(';');
  for(var i=0;i &lt; ca.length;i++) {
    var c = ca[i];
    while (c.charAt(0)==' ') {
      c = c.substring(1,c.length);
    }
    if (c.indexOf(nameEQ) == 0) {
      return c.substring(nameEQ.length,c.length);
    }
  }
  return null;
}

function eraseCookie(name) {
  createCookie(name,"",-1);
}

      jQuery(this).parent().parent().find('span.collapse').removeClass('collapse').addClass('expand');

    return false;
  });
  jQuery('#' + id + ' .collapse').live('click', function() {
    collapseCat(this, expandSym, collapseSym);
    return false;
  });
}
