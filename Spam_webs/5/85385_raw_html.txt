         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Redesigning the Egg Carton
          <span class="post-date">
           by
           <a href="http://thisiscolossal.com/about" rel="author external" title="Visit Christopher Jobsons website">
            Christopher Jobson
           </a>
           on June 19, 2011
          </span>
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12566" height="400" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/egg-1.jpg" title="egg-1" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12567" height="400" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/egg-2.jpg" title="egg-2" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12568" height="400" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/egg-3.jpg" title="egg-3" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12569" height="400" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/egg-4.jpg" title="egg-4" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12570" height="400" src="http://thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/egg-5.jpg" title="egg-5" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Im such a sucker for any attempt to redesign common objects, things that have an almost ubiquitous design that nobody has considered reengineering or improving them in ages (
          <a href="http://www.oxo.com/">
           OXO
          </a>
          created an entire company around this idea). Anyway, this slick design for a collapsable egg carton is by industrial design student
          <a href="http://evivalicsek.tumblr.com/">
           va Valicsek
          </a>
          from the Institute of Applied Arts, Sopron. The cardboard flaps are attached with rubberbands to hold the eggs firmly in place and presumably allow for the packaging to fold in on itself. I like that the eggs remain protected, yet accessible, and though I cant see millions of eggs being transported like this, its a good way to get hardboiled eggs to a picnic in some flashy modern packaging. (via
          <a href="http://lovelypackage.com/student-work-eva-valicsek/">
           lovely package
          </a>
          )
         </p>
