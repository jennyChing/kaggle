         <p>
          <strong>
           Mike Colman
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          has been a voice inside my head for many years. As a sports nut and a follower of any sporting persuasion I have read an awful lot over the years. I may not always have agreed with Mikes articles, magazine and opinion pieces over the years but I have always admired his passion, fairness and integrity.
         </p>
         <p>
          Currently based in the old dart as a European correspondent this interview over twitter was conducted in a piece meal manner due to our time differences. That said I dont think I have ever asked questions in this format and received such thoughtful answers condensed into 140 characters.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           And all this from a man who clings on precariously to the North Sydney Bears 
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          I hope you enjoy reading this twinterview as much as I enjoyed conducting it.
         </p>
         <div>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span style="line-height: 1.4em; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
            SO WHATS A TWINTERVIEW
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre new to the concept, a Twinterview is an Interview that I conduct over Twitter. I cold call (Tweet) people who Im interesting in talking to and hopefully ask questions that are not like the standard interview questions they get asked over and over again. 7 Q &amp; A  140 characters. No waffle allowed!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.fullhalfglass.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/mike-colman.jpg">
           <img alt="mike colman" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6434" height="168" src="http://www.fullhalfglass.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/mike-colman.jpg" width="299"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: 18px;">
           <strong>
            MIKE COLMAN
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           FHG
          </strong>
          : Has your writing style changed over the years. From the typewriter / copy boy days 2 laptop &amp; The Net. Shorter sentences, Less descriptive?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Mike Colman
          </strong>
          : Its changed in that Ive spent 40 years learning how to do it across lots of platforms. Sometimes short sentences are the most descriptive
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           FHG
          </strong>
          : Pls choose one option from each pair and tell me why: Love or Lust, PC or Mac, Winter or Summer, Sweet or Savoury, Bald or Hair, Sin or Safe
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Mike Colman
          </strong>
          : Love  lasts longer. PC  bosses wont give me a Mac. Winter  footy, Savoury  steak &amp; taters guy, Bald  no choice, Safe  see under bald.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           FHG
          </strong>
          : What or who defines Oz moral compass / political correctness / public interest. What is your fall point when deciding if or when to publish?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Mike Colman
          </strong>
          : MC: conscience based on life experience. PC: those who believe they know best. PI: media. Put my family in place of those Im writing about.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           FHG
          </strong>
          : GREAT ANSWERS! Now, Cheer or Jeer  NSW S.O.O Team, Bean Sprouts, Lava Lamps, Bingo, Deadlines, Horror Movies, Winter Olympics, Referees?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Mike Colman
          </strong>
          :Jeer. Huge cheer in laksa. Cheer but only if wearing kaftan. Cheer,wouldnt finish anything otherwise. Disinterested. Cheer. Referees what?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           FHG
          </strong>
          : I believe that evone in life has a sliding doors moment; an opportunity to take. Ever had a what if I did moment &amp; regretted not acting
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Mike Colman
          </strong>
          : In Lawrence of Arabia he goes through sandstorm to save kid. Later kid is killed anyway. Arabs say It was written. I believe that, so no.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           FHG
          </strong>
          : Bit more about you. Your fav  non mainstream sport, place to eat fish, superhero, bred of dog, beach retreat, self indulgence, comfort food
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Mike Colman
          </strong>
          : Woodchopping, overlooking beach, Captain Klutz (by Don Martin), English Springer Spaniel, Noosa, Sunday arvo nap, laksa
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           FHG
          </strong>
          : Thanks for being involved in the Twinterview Mike. I thoroughly enjoyed your terrific and thoughtful answers. One final q then were done.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           FHG
          </strong>
          : If I could give you Harry Potters invisibility cloak name a dinner party of 4 people youd like to eavesdrop on. Why these 4 people?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Mike Colman
          </strong>
          : Carrie Fisher, Robert Downey, Rita Rudner, Tom Wolf. Talent, pain, intelligence, wit, insight, ego. The Right Stuff for a great mag feature.
         </p>
