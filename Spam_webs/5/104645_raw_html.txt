         <div style="float:left; width:80px;padding-right:10px; margin:4px 4px 4px 4px;height:30px;">
          <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="none" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thetravelersway.com/getting-around-chicago-a-pedway-for-all-seasons/&amp;media=http://thetravelersway.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/chicago-pedway-symbol.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thetravelersway.com/getting-around-chicago-a-pedway-for-all-seasons/&amp;media=http://thetravelersway.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/chicago-pedway-symbol.jpg', '']);">
          </a>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div style="clear:both">
        </div>
        <div style="padding-bottom:4px;">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thetravelersway.com/getting-around-chicago-a-pedway-for-all-seasons/chicago-pedway-symbol/" rel="attachment wp-att-6158">
          <img alt="Chicago Pedway Symbol" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6158" height="339" src="http://thetravelersway.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/chicago-pedway-symbol.jpg" width="505"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Some visit Chicago for the infamous Ledge on the Skydeck of the new Sears Tower. Some visit to take in the views from the Hancock Observatory. Others fancy some shopping on the Miracle Mile and taking in a show while theyre in the windy city. But whatever your reason for visiting, you probably dont have the Chicago pedway on your list of things to do.
        </p>
        <p>
         So what is the Chicago pedway? And why should you care anyway?
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Going underground in Chicago
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         It started in 1951 with one underground passage built to connect the Blue Line and Red Line underground stations at Washington and Jackson. Little by little, and by a variety of different private enterprises, more sections were added over time. Today this has resulted in a series of passageways covering roughly five miles and connecting more than 50 buildings in the center of Chicago. Its an alternative way of getting around the city away from the traffic and crowds at street level. But theres a catch.
        </p>
        <p>
         And the catch comes in the way its been built. Because each section was constructed at different times by different companies, theres no apparent order or system to the passageways. Exits and entrances pop up in the strangest of places. And you may find yourself retracing your steps and looking for another route when you find yourself turning the corner to a dead end.
        </p>
        <p>
         Which means that if youre new here, finding your way around the Chicago pedway can be an adventure in itself. Which leads on to the next question, is it worth the trouble?
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          A visitors take on the pedway
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Whether or not youll choose to include the pedway on your list of things to do will depend partly on your personal travel interests, and partly on the season you choose to visit Chicago. Heres what its all about:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thetravelersway.com/getting-around-chicago-a-pedway-for-all-seasons/metra-millenium-station-pedway/" rel="attachment wp-att-6159">
          <img alt="Metra Millenium Station Pedway" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6159" height="336" src="http://thetravelersway.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/metra-millenium-station-pedway.jpg" width="510"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Its great for:
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Finding a different take on Chicago. Like many cities with an underground level, the passageways have a character and vibe of their own. And because of the non-uniform nature of this network, the twists and turns feel a little like a patchwork quilt of possible destination. If you have an active sense of adventure and like to do some exploring of your own, the pedway is a good place to start.
         </li>
         <li>
          You can walk all the way to Macys using the pedway without ever having to deal with a sidewalk or traffic. If you dont like the crowds, take the underground route.
         </li>
         <li>
          Lets face it, Chicago is a city of weather. Summer is hot and humid. Winter is cold and windy with some big dollops of snow to add the finishing touch. In-between, spring and autumn are a nice break but they dont last that long. If youre here for either of the extremes, the pedway is a welcome relief from the outdoor highs and lows.
         </li>
         <li>
          Saving on parking costs. If you choose to drive around the city, not only do you deal with the stop start of city driving, but you also have to pay every time you want to park up and spend some time on foot. The pedway connects you to all the major attractions and lets you get on and off whenever you please.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Its not so great for:
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Signage and knowing exactly where you are. And its where your sense of adventure needs to be alive and kicking if youre going to enjoy getting your head around the pedway. If youre the kind of traveler who needs the support of a good map and directions that clearly mark whats coming next, youre probably better off above ground.
         </li>
         <li>
          Those who dont like walking. The pedway is all about your legs and not just in the forward and backwards sense. Theres plenty of up and down with stairways and escalators so its important that you dont easily run out of steam.
         </li>
         <li>
          Nervous dispositions. Because various parts of the pedway have been built by different companies, those same parts are also maintained to different standards. For example, Block 37 has recently been renovated and has some excellent shopping and eating options for visitors. But then in other parts of the pedway it can feel like youre in some dimly lit corridor thats part of an haunted house attraction. Definitely not recommended if youre a solo traveler.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thetravelersway.com/getting-around-chicago-a-pedway-for-all-seasons/pedway-at-randolph-street/" rel="attachment wp-att-6160">
          <img alt="Pedway At Randolph Street" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6160" height="342" src="http://thetravelersway.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/pedway-at-randolph-street.jpg" width="510"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Three tips for using the Chicago pedway
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         If youve looked at the pros and cons of the Chicago underground, and decided that the pedway is something youd like to experience, here are three tips to help you on your way:
        </p>
        <p>
         1. At the time of writing, there is no official map of the pedway available, however, Mark Spiegl has put together an excellent resource over on this
         <a href="http://www.spiegl.org/pedway/Pedwaymapandlegend.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','download','http://www.spiegl.org/pedway/Pedwaymapandlegend.pdf']);">
          Chicago pedway page
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         2. If you fancy doing something a little different in the pedway, why not try a pub crawl. Just note that you need be aware of what time the different sections of the pedway close to make sure you can fit everything in. For a guide that takes you from start to finish, see this route published by the Chicago Tribune.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. And finally, if you want to try the pedway but dont feel so confident going it alone, you can always choose to take a tour and let someone else do the navigating for you. For more information, see this
         <a href="http://chicagopedwaytour.com/Home.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://chicagopedwaytour.com/Home.html', 'Chicago pedway tour page']);" title="Chicago Pedway Tour">
          Chicago pedway tour page
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Whatever your reasons for visiting Chicago, the Ledge, the Hancock Observatory, or Macys, if you do try the pedway, wed love to hear your comments about your experience.
        </p>
