     <h1>
      The most epic Petra video ever
     </h1>
     <div class="post-inner clearfix">
      <div class="post-meta">
       <div class="post-meta-author">
        by
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/author/joshywashington/">
         Joshywashington
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="post-meta-date">
        March 25, 2012
       </div>
       <div class="post-avatar">
        <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-100 photo" height="100" src="http://0.gravatar.com/avatar/058eb213eac9a08be09243dda9528fa7?s=100&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" srcset="http://0.gravatar.com/avatar/058eb213eac9a08be09243dda9528fa7?s=200&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g 2x" width="100"/>
       </div>
       <div class="post-share-wrapper post-share--vertical" data-post-id="178254" data-sticky="" data-sticky-max-width="991" data-sticky-offset-top="69" data-sticky-parent-contain="" data-sticky-parent-selector=".page-section">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="345" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/k33DVYI0BNI?rel=0" width="620">
        </iframe>
       </p>
       <div class="subtitle">
        Can you handle how epic this video is?
       </div>
       <div class="post-social-video row">
        <div class="col-sm-6">
         <a class="vimeo-btn btn" href="http://vimeo.com/matadornetwork">
          Follow Matador on
          <span class="network-title">
           Vimeo
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="col-sm-6">
         <a class="youtube-btn btn" href="http://www.youtube.com/subscription_center?add_user=MATADORnetwork">
          Follow Matador on
          <span class="network-title">
           YouTube
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        THE START OF THIS VIDEO IS JARRING. Im looking out the dingy back window of a sedan driving away from a checkpoint. The narrator, likely the man holding the camera, tells me that these are the first images after crossing the Israel /
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/destinations/middle-east/jordan/">
         Jordan
        </a>
        border. Somehow this feels like a confession or a threat or the last banal minutes before something seriously crazy happens.
       </p>
       <p>
        But no, it is just an enthusiastic and camera-savvy Chilean (whos name I could not glean from the YouTube account) and his lady friend,
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/community/fireandtea/stepping-into-petra/">
         driving to Petra
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        However, after a scant 16 seconds this seemingly innocuous travel video takes a turn for the epic. The beautiful and enigmatic Helena is introduced, the music builds, and Petras history is laid out in the opening titles.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2012/03/Picture-21.png">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2012/03/Picture-21-300x200.png" title="Helena and Petra"/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-178257 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2012/03/Picture-21-300x200.png" height="200" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The hair stands up on the back of my neck.
       </p>
       <p>
        The genius of this video is its dance of innocence and bravado. The courageous overture is always building, always piling on the violins and crescendos to accompany the good-natured, apparently happy, slightly cheesy couple who seem intrepidly oblivious and altogether agreeable.
       </p>
       <p>
        What I mean is, they seem just like you and me.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/tv/why-the-best-travel-videos-are-shot-at-home/">
         The shots are epic
        </a>
         a camera sweeps past Helenas jaw as she bites into an apple, some kind of horse / donkey cart clatters through a sandstone tunnel that leads to the lost but found ancient city.
       </p>
       <noscript>
