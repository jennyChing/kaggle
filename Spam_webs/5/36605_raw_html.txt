    Temporal paradox
   </h1>

      This article is about logical problems involved in the concept of time travel.  For the controversy over the origin of birds, see

     <div class="hatnote">
      "Time paradox" redirects here. For the 2008 fantasy novel, see
      <a href="/wiki/Artemis_Fowl:_The_Time_Paradox" title="Artemis Fowl: The Time Paradox">
       Artemis Fowl: The Time Paradox

     </div>
     <p>
      A
      <b>
       temporal
      </b>
      ,
      <b>
       time
      </b>
      , or
      <b>
       time-travel paradox
      </b>
      is a logical contradiction or
      <a href="/wiki/Paradox" title="Paradox">
       paradox
      </a>
      that appears to be inherent in at least one of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theoretical" title="Theoretical">
       theoretical
      </a>
      scenarios suggested by the supposition that "
      <a href="/wiki/Time_travel" title="Time travel">
       time travel
      </a>
      " into the past is possible. These include at hypothetical or fictional forms of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Backward_causation" title="Backward causation">
       backward causation
      </a>
      mediated by "time travel" from one time to a time in its past:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Causality_loop" title="Causality loop">
        causality loop
       </a>
       , also called a bootstrap paradox, ontological paradox, or predestination paradox, where an event influences the past and causes itself to occur, or where an object originates through time travel, only to be sent later to the time when it originated, thus having no discernible independent origin.
      </li>
      <li>
       the
       <a href="/wiki/Grandfather_paradox" title="Grandfather paradox">
        grandfather paradox
       </a>
       , where an event, that presupposes a specific earlier event,
       <i>
        prevents
       </i>
       it, by its (backward) influence on the earlier event
      </li>
     </ul>
