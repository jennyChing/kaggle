      ABOUT THE TALK
      <span class="mobArrow">
      </span>
     </div>
     <div class="extraInfoBody">
      <p>
       Accidental Yiddishist Miriam Udel discovered her passion for the language in the unlikely locale of Havana, Cuba. Yiddish literature, she argues, is vulnerable; it has been covered over with time and neglect. With stirring examples of universally human yet uniquely Jewish tales from master storytellers, Dr. Udel demonstrates how Yiddish literature can and should have a place on every modern Jewish bookshelf.
       <br/>
      </p>

     <div class="speakerMob">
      <p>
       Miriam Udel is an assistant professor of Jewish Studies and German Studies at Emory University, where her research and teaching focus on the modern Jewish literary complex and especially Yiddish. She is currently completing her first book, about the anti-heroic hero of 20th-century Jewish fiction. When lecturing and teaching in the Jewish community, Dr. Udel enjoys bringing classical Jewish sources, such as rabbinic midrash and medieval biblical interpretation, into conversation with more recent Jewish literature.
