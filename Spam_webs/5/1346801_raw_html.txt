          <p>
           Vitamin C is commonly used as a supplement to support immune system health. However, while supplementation is sometimes necessary, its almost always better to get our nutrients from whole food sources.
          </p>
          <p>
           This recipe is about making your own whole food Vitamin C Pills from herbs. These little pills are not only high in Vitamin C but also high in a plethora of important antioxidants and nutrients that work in synergy with the vitamin C.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Red rose hips from close" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3562" height="480" src="http://wbg4n2tpldv1q21e93syia8p.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMAGE-2.jpg" width="720"/>
          </p>
          <h4>
           Rose hips (
           <em>
            Rosa spp
           </em>
           .)
          </h4>
          <p>
           Rose hips are famously high in vitamin C. This was discovered in Britain during WWII when citrus foods were hard to come by and scurvy (extreme lack of vitamin C) was an increasing problem. Scientists began testing local food sources and found that rosehips actually have more vitamin C than oranges!
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Acerola" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3563" height="477" src="http://wbg4n2tpldv1q21e93syia8p.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMAGE-3.jpg" width="720"/>
          </p>
          <h4>
           Acerola (
           <em>
            Malpighia glabra
           </em>
           )
          </h4>
          <p>
           Acerola is native to the tropics of South America and is now also being grown as far north as Texas. Acerola is famous for its high vitamin C content although it also contains other important nutrients such as B vitamins and bioflavonoids. According to the book Plants in Human Health and Nutrition Policy there are 1677.6 mg of vitamin C in 100 g of fruit.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Whole Food Vitamin C Pills" class="alignnone size-remedy-featured wp-image-3561" height="473" src="http://wbg4n2tpldv1q21e93syia8p.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMAGE-4-710x473.jpg" width="710"/>
          </p>
          <h4>
           Amla (
           <em>
            Phyllanthus emblica
           </em>
           )
          </h4>
          <p>
           Amla is a cherished herb from India where it is famously used in the Triphala formula. Like the other herbs in this recipe, amla is celebrated for its incredibly high vitamin C content.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           How much vitamin C is in each vitamin C pill?
          </h3>
          <p>
           Honestly I dont know. Vitamin C content varies with the plant, the location where it grew, the way it was dried, the date it was dried and on and on. Thats why its a good idea to get a variety of vitamin C sources in your diet. Rest assured though, these pills do have a good amount of vitamin C as well as other important nutrients.
          </p>
          <h4>
           What youll need
          </h4>
          <ul>
           <li>
            1 tablespoon rose hip powder
           </li>
           <li>
            1 tablespoon amla powder
           </li>
           <li>
            1 tablespoon acerola powder
           </li>
           <li>
            Honey
           </li>
           <li>
            Orange peel powder (optional)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="avia-button-wrap avia-button-center ">
           <div class="avia-button-wrap avia-button-center ">
            <a class="avia-button avia-icon_select-no avia-color-custom avia-size-large avia-position-center " href="http://iwantherbs.com" style="background-color:#f89721; border-color:#f89721; color:#ffffff; " target="_blank">
             <span class="avia_iconbox_title">
              Need organic herbs or supplies?
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: Bariol Bold;">
               Get them here!
              </span>
             </span>
            </a>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           Mix together the powdered herbs, breaking up any clumps.
          </p>
          <p>
           Pour slightly warmed honey into the powdered mix. I never measure how much honey I use. Just pour a tiny bit, stir, add a bit more, stir. You want the final consistently to hold together but not be too moist or sticky.
          </p>
          <p>
           Once the powders are mixed with the honey, form the mixture into pea sized balls. These can then be rolled in orange powder or another one of the powders in the mixture.
          </p>
          <p>
           Makes 45 pea sized pills.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Whole Food Vitamin C Pills" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3560" height="480" src="http://wbg4n2tpldv1q21e93syia8p.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMAGE-5.jpg" width="720"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Store in an airtight container. These should last a very long time.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Whole Food Vitamin C Pills" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3559" height="480" src="http://wbg4n2tpldv1q21e93syia8p.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMAGE-6.jpg" width="720"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Whole Food Vitamin C Pills" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3558" height="480" src="http://wbg4n2tpldv1q21e93syia8p.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMAGE-7.jpg" width="720"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           To use take 1-3 balls per day. Its safe to take more than three pills a day; the adverse effect from getting too much vitamin C is loose stools. If you get loose stools then stop using them for the day and use less in the future.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Vitamin C shouldnt be taken in excess during pregnancy.
           </em>
          </p>
