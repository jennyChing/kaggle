          <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
           Traveling Green For Your Environmentally Friendly Vacation
          </h1>
         </header>
         <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
          <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
          <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">

          <p>
           The pressure to behave in an environmentally responsible (
           <em>
            <strong>
             traveling green
            </strong>
           </em>
           ) way has never been greater. Ordinary people everywhere are now familiar with the damage an excessive carbon footprint can cause, and there is a growing impetus to be as environmentally-friendly as you possibly can be.
           <a href="http://gogreentravelgreen.com" target="_blank" title="Traveling Green">
            Traveling green
           </a>
           , whether for business or pleasure, is one of the most direct ways you can reduce your personal impact on the environment, and there are a number of new destinations that are springing up as
           <a href="http://gogreentravelgreen.com/12-paper-free-guidebooks-25-days-of-green-travel-day-5/" target="_blank" title="Green Alternatives">
            green alternatives
           </a>
           for vacationers. But what are the most effective ways of traveling green for a vacation that is a bit kinder to the planet?
          </p>
          <h2>
           <b>
            Traveling Green For Your Environmentally Friendly Vacation
           </b>
          </h2>
          <p>
           One of the worst things you can do when you are traveling in a green way is to fly. Air travel is one of the most carbon intensive ways to go  perhaps understandably, given the amount of fuel required to propel such a vast, heavy vehicle through mid air at 30,000 feet. If you have to fly, you can wave goodbye to your eco-travel brownie points. As compared to other forms of transport, flight has a much more aggressive emissions footprint per person. This makes it irrelevant where you go, in terms of the damage you are causing to the planet. Longer flights are worse, of course, but in general, flying is the least green way to travel.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://aswetravel.smugmug.com/Travel/GoGreenTravelGreen-2014/35355225_J5HVDr#!i=3022437844&amp;k=jx23SGD&amp;lb=1&amp;s=A" rel="nofollow" title="traveling, green, your, environmentally, friendly, vacation">
            <img alt="traveling, green, your, environmentally, friendly, vacation" src="http://aswetravel.smugmug.com/Travel/GoGreenTravelGreen-2014/i-jx23SGD/0/L/traveling-green-for-your-environmentally-friendly-vacation1-L.png" title="traveling, green, your, environmentally, friendly, vacation"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           This then makes it worth considering vacations closer to home. Of course, the US is a vast country, spanning almost 3,000 miles from coast to coast, and
           <span class="removed_link" title="http://www.valpak.com/coupons/stores/orbitz-promotion-codes">
            promotional codes for Orbitz flights
           </span>
           may come in handy. Depending on where you live, trying to vacation locally is one of the best ways to cut down on your overall contribution to CO2 emissions. Fortunately, there are many destinations across the mainland that are in themselves green, and an excellent choice for anyone who chooses to support the planet and the environment.
          </p>
          <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
          <div style="float:right;margin:10px 0 10px 10px;">
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
           </script>
           <!-- GoGreenTravel -->
           <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-4730152652671852" data-ad-slot="7269021688" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
           </ins>
           <script>
            (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
           </script>
          </div>
          <p>
           The iconic skyline of
           <a href="http://www.sfgov.org/index.asp" rel="nofollow">
            San Francisco
           </a>
           hides the reality of this city, as one of the most forward thinking in the entire country. San Francisco as a city is one of the pioneers of recycling, with some 80% of household and commercial waste now able to be reused, thanks to an effective policy of encouraging personal responsibility. For those visiting the city, it is a breath of fresh air, quite literally, as compared to many other destinations around the country.
           <b>
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://aswetravel.smugmug.com/Travel/GoGreenTravelGreen-2014/35355225_J5HVDr#!i=3022437862&amp;k=pGbhqRZ&amp;lb=1&amp;s=A" rel="nofollow" title="traveling, green, your, environmentally, friendly, vacation">
            <img alt="traveling, green, your, environmentally, friendly, vacation" src="http://aswetravel.smugmug.com/Travel/GoGreenTravelGreen-2014/i-pGbhqRZ/0/M/traveling-green-for-your-environmentally-friendly-vacation2-M.png" title="traveling, green, your, environmentally, friendly, vacation"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           In a similar mold is
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Portland, Oregon
           </a>
           , widely regarded as the capital of cool. With over 200 miles of bike lanes crossing the city, Portland is leading the way on environmental issues, and is an active force for positive change across the wider US. It also happens to be a great city to visit for a vacation.
           <b>
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           Green travel is a growing issue, and one that more people are emphasizing each year when choosing vacation destinations. Flying is one of the most damaging ways to go, with the associated carbon footprint much more significant than other forms of mass transport. Instead, by looking to vacation closer to home, and to destinations with a particular green focus, it can be possible to offset the environmental damage by
           <em>
            <strong>
             traveling green
            </strong>
           </em>
            without sacrificing a great vacation.
          </p>
