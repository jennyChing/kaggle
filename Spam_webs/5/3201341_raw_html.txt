        <p>
         Since the inception of Counter Strike:Global Offensive and Dota 2, just recently CS:GO has been among the list of the most played and viewed games throughout the world.
         <br/>
         <span>
         </span>
         <br/>
         Just yesterday for the very first time, Counter Strike: Global Offensive left behind Dota 2 in the number of concurrent players at a time. This comes after a very long time as Dota 2 has been topping charts ever since it hit Steam. Dota 2 has been the most popular game beating everyone by a far mile on steam and being the 2nd most played game around the world. What comes as surprise is that the stat came up on a time when the qualifier for the biggest Dota 2 event throughout the year is underway.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Steam" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1647" height="360" src="http://d3psvddqt9zx7g.cloudfront.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Steam.jpg" width="640"/>
         <br/>
         CS: GO was also able to beat Dota 2 for the number of viewers on twitch as can be seen below
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="CSGO" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1646" height="288" src="http://d3psvddqt9zx7g.cloudfront.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/CSGO.jpg" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Throughout the last six months CS:GO has gained a great amount of hype with the recent Katowice 2015. Furthermore, Steam has also recently provided a patch update with Operation Bloodhound, a sort of push the game needed for long to outrun the legendary level and viewership it had in the era of 1.6.
        </p>
