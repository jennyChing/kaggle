          Smokey Rosemary-Mustard Chicken Drumsticks + Gluten-Free Picnic Menu
         </a>
        </h1>
       </td>
      </tr>
     </table>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <a href="http://www.carascravings.com/2012/07/smokey-rosemary-mustard-chicken-drumsticks-gluten-free-picnic-menu.html/drumsticks-2" rel="attachment wp-att-5720">
       <img alt="grilled chicken drumsticks, rosemary mustard chicken, rosemary mustard marinade, july 4th recipes, picnic recipes, gluten free july 4th, gluten free barbecue, gluten free picnic, dairy free picnic recipes, kosher chicken recipes,  healthy chicken recipes,  easy chicken recipes" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5720" height="560" src="http://www.carascravings.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/drumsticks-2-560x560.jpg" title="drumsticks 2" width="560"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Its only July 2nd, but I can already hear myself on September 1st:
     </p>
     <p>
      Wahhh. Boohoohoo. Summer is
      <em>
       over
      </em>
      and how many times did we go to the beach? How about that hike we promised to go on with the dog? And how come we never made it to that outdoor jazz concert with picnic basket in tow?
     </p>
     <p>
      Last summer, my answers would have been: 1) Zero. Unless you count once in Israel. Kind of pathetic for the fact that I live less than 90 minutes from the ocean. 2) We didnt have a dog last summer, but still, I dont know why we didnt take more hikes. And 3) because were always so busy and dont own a picnic basket?
     </p>
     <p>
      This summer, I plan to change all that. Just ask me on September 1st, and I guarantee Ill have happier answers. Why? Because life is meant to be lived. And because I refuse to let my legs stay pasty all summer.
     </p>
     <p>
      Im already one step closer to #3, thanks to my friend Amie, author, gluten-free chef, and hollistic health counselor behind the blog
      <a href="http://thehealthyapple.com/" target="_blank">
       The Healthy Apple
      </a>
      . Amie and I chatted it up for a long hour on the phone one afternoon, lamenting what a shame it was that we couldnt get together for a cooking extravaganza (although, since shes only a few hours away by car, maybe that will change someday!) In the meantime, we thought it would be fun to pool our brains together and create a fresh, tasty gluten-free picnic menu that you can use this week (hello, 4th of July!) and all summer long.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://www.carascravings.com/2012/07/smokey-rosemary-mustard-chicken-drumsticks-gluten-free-picnic-menu.html/drumstick-collage" rel="attachment wp-att-5722">
       <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5722" height="560" src="http://www.carascravings.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/Drumstick-Collage-560x560.jpg" title="Drumstick Collage" width="560"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Im generally easy to please, but when I started thinking about what makes perfect picnic food, I came up with a slew of criteria:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       It can be made ahead of time and refrigerated before serving, and doesnt require any last minute assembly or garnishes
      </li>
      <li>
       Hot food need not apply; it has to be tasty chilled or room temp
      </li>
      <li>
       Hand-held food is great. Food that requires a fork is fine. But a knife and fork? Count it out! You know that awkward moment where youre trying to balance a solo cup AND slice into a piece of steak? Yeah, not happening at my picnic.
      </li>
      <li>
       It should be healthy, flavorful, and you should have a big variety of colors and flavors. Oh wait, thats my criteria
       <em>
        all
       </em>
       the time
       <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.carascravings.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      When Amie declared her
      <a href="http://thehealthyapple.com/2012/07/02/4th-of-july-picnic-menu/" target="_blank">
       Asparagus Goat Cheese Pasta Salad
      </a>
      , it took me about 2 seconds to decide what I was making. Grilled chicken drumsticks in a smokey mustard-rosemary marinade seemed like the perfect accompaniment. And the thing about chicken drumsticks is that you can grill them up a head of time, and enjoy them cold the next day. With a marinade like this one, spiked with lemon and garlic, you can guarantee theyll stay nice and juicy too. No dry chicken at my picnic, promise!
     </p>
     <p>
      To round out the picnic menu, I selected some goodies that Amie and I have each made in the past, that all fit the bill described above. Simple, make-ahead, healthy goodies to stuff the cooler with and tote along  we got you covered!
     </p>

      <span class="item">
       <h2 class="fn">
        Smokey Rosemary-Mustard Chicken Drumsticks
       </h2>
      </span>
      <p class="time" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left; clear:left">
       <strong>
        Yield:
       </strong>
       <span class="yield">
        1 dozen
       </span>
      </p>
      <p class="time" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left">
       <strong>
        Prep Time:
       </strong>
       <span class="preptime">
        10 minutes + 6 hours to marinate
       </span>
      </p>
      <p class="time" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left">
       <strong>
        Cook Time:
       </strong>
       <span class="cooktime">
        45 minutes
       </span>
      </p>
      <div class="summary" style="clear:left">
       <p>
        Juicy mustard-and-rosemary infused chicken legs with a hint of smoke - perfect right off the grill or for bringing on your next picnic.
       </p>
      </div>
      <h3 style="clear:left">
       Ingredients:
      </h3>
      <div class="ingredient">
       <p>
        12 meaty chicken drumsticks
        <br/>
        3/4 cup plain unsweetened almond milk
        <br/>
        1/2 cup grainy Dijon mustard
        <br/>
        3 tablespoons lemon juice
        <br/>
        4 cloves of garlic, peeled
        <br/>
        2 tablespoons minced rosemary
        <br/>
        2 tablespoons honey
        <br/>
        2 teaspoons liquid smoke
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon smoked paprika
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon salt
        <br/>
        freshly ground black pepper
       </p>
      </div>
      <h3 style="clear:left">
       Directions:
      </h3>
      <div class="instructions">
       <p>
        Arrange chicken drumsticks in a 9x13" baking dish. Combine remaining ingredients in a blender or food processor and process until smooth. Pour over chicken. Cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 6 hours or overnight, turning occasionally.
       </p>
       <p>
        Remove chicken from refrigerator. Spray grill grates with olive oil cooking spray. Preheat grill so that chicken can be cooked over indirect heat.*
       </p>
       <p>
        Transfer chicken to the grill and cook for about 45 minutes, turning once or twice, until chicken is no longer pink in the thickest part of the meat. (If the chicken does not seem to be cooking through, you may need to turn the middle burners onto low heat.)
       </p>
       <p>
        *I turn the outside burners onto medium-high, and leave the ones in the middle of the grill off.
       </p>
      </div>
     </blockquote>
