         <h1 class="title">
          How To Permanently Delete Your Google Account
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/howto/author/bogdan/" rel="author" title="Posts by Bogdan Bele">
            Bogdan Bele
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         |
         <abbr class="date time published" title="2012-03-18T15:00:02-0700">
          March 18, 2012
         </abbr>
         <span class="small">
          in
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/howto/">
           How-To
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <section class="entry">
         <p>
          There are times in your online life when, due to various reasons, drastic measures have to be taken. Permanently deleting your Google account is one of them. Ill show you how to do it but remember that the deletion will be permanent. But dont worry, youll download your important documents first.
         </p>
         <p>
          First log in to your Google account. Click the drop down next to your picture icon and select Account Settings.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Account Settings" border="0" height="428" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Account-Settings.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Account Settings" width="551"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Back Up Your Google Data
         </h3>
         <p>
          Before starting the deletion process, download and save your data before you do anything you go any further. That can be done by following our guide on
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/howto/google-download-all-personal-data-from-services/">
           Google Takeout
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Google Takeout" border="0" height="563" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Google-Takeout.png" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Google Takeout" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Or by selecting Data Liberation on the left side of the screen that comes up. Its good to keep a backup of all of your important documents and other data you have stored on Google servers just in case you need something youve stored there over the years.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Data Liberation" border="0" height="473" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Data-Liberation.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Data Liberation" width="593"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Permanently Delete Your Google Account
         </h3>
         <p>
          Time to get down to business and delete your Google account. Youll notice that getting rid of a Google Account is easier than actually getting one.
         </p>
         <p>
          Go back into Account Settings, then click Account Overview on the menu on the left.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Account Overview" border="0" height="341" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Account-Overview.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Account Overview" width="473"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Under Services, click Close Entire Account and Delete All Services and Info Associated With It. If you click the link above it, only your Google+ profile and associated features are deleted.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="delete entirely" border="0" height="366" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/delete-entirely.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="delete entirely" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Google will ask you to confirm your understanding of the services youre losing access to. It will ask for your password, a final confirmation and youll still need to pay any pending charges you might happen to have.
         </p>
         <p>
          Check all of the boxes if you dont want anything left. OK, last chance to reconsider. No? Still want to delete it? Then, click Delete Google Account.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Delete Google Account" border="0" height="385" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/Delete-Google-Account.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Delete Google Account" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its gone. Youll see the following Account Deleted confirmation screen.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="account deleted" border="0" height="227" src="//www.groovypost.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/account-deleted.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="account deleted" width="506"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats it! Your Google account is permanently gone. You can still use Google search though, if thats your engine of choice.
         </p>
         <p>
          There are several reasons you might want to blow away your Google Account. Perhaps you have a few dupes you created in the past. Or perhaps youre not happy with the
          <a href="http://www.groovypost.com/remove-google-web-history-privacy/" title="Google Privacy policy">
           new Google Privacy policy
          </a>
          that went into place March 1 of this year? Whatever the reason, please be careful because once its gone. Its gone.
         </p>
