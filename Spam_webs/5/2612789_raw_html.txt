      <p>
       WASHINGTON (AP) - Some MARC commuter rail service has been restored after officials say a person was hit by a train north of Washington's Union Station.
      </p>
      <p class="hidden">
       <a href="#continue">
        Continue reading
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="story-embed">
      </div>
      <p id="continue">
       D.C. police and fire officials say a train hit a person Thursday morning in a rail yard near New York Avenue in northeast Washington.
      </p>
      <p>
       Maryland Transit Administration spokesman Paul Shepard says the person was hit by a MARC commuter train. The MTA says some Penn line trains have been delayed and others canceled. Amtrak says the incident delayed several trains
      </p>
      <p>
       D.C. Fire department spokesman Tim Wilson says paramedics responded, but he did not have information about the person's condition.
      </p>
