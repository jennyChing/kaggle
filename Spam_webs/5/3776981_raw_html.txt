            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <p style="margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px">
              From his
              <a href="https://www.ted.com/speakers/frans_de_waal" target="_blank">
               TED profile
              </a>
              :
             </p>
             <p style="margin-left:0px; margin-right:0px">
              Dr. Frans B. M. de Waal is a biologist and primatologist known for his work on the behavior and social intelligence of primates. His first book,
              <em>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Chimpanzee-Politics-Power-among-Apes/dp/0801863368?tag=tedspeakers-20" target="_blank">
                Chimpanzee Politics
               </a>
              </em>
              (1982), compared the schmoozing and scheming of chimpanzees involved in power struggles with that of human politicians. Ever since, de Waal has drawn parallels between primate and human behavior, from peacemaking and morality to culture. His scientific work has been published in hundreds of technical articles in journals such as Science, Nature, Scientific American, and outlets specialized in animal behavior. His popular books  translated into fifteen languages  have made him one of the worlds most visible primatologists. His latest books are
              <em>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Our-Inner-Ape-Frans-Waal/dp/1573223123?tag=tedspeakers-20" target="_blank">
                Our Inner Ape
               </a>
              </em>
              (2005, Riverhead) and
              <em>
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Age-Empathy-Natures-Lessons-Society/dp/0307407764?tag=tedspeakers-20" target="_blank">
                The Age of Empathy
               </a>
              </em>
              (2009, Harmony).
             </p>
             <p>
              De Waal is C. H. Candler Professor in the Psychology Department of Emory University and Director of the
              <a href="http://www.emory.edu/LIVING_LINKS/" target="_blank">
               Living Links Center
              </a>
              at the
              <a href="http://www.yerkes.emory.edu/" target="_blank">
               Yerkes National Primate Center
              </a>
              , in Atlanta. He has been elected to the National Academy of Sciences (US), the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, and the Royal Dutch Academy of Sciences. In 2007, he was selected by
              <em>
               Time
              </em>
              as one of
              <a href="http://www.time.com/time/specials/2007/article/0,28804,1595326_1595329_1616472,00.html" target="_blank">
               The Worlds 100 Most Influential People Today
              </a>
              , and in 2011 by
              <em>
               Discover
              </em>
              as among 47 (all time) Great Minds of Science.
             </p>
