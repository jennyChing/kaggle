         Yesterday was Bens birthday. In addition to the gift I bought him, I told him he could choose whatever he wanted for dinnerthe dessert and all. He came up with
         <a href="http://penniesonaplatter.com/2009/02/09/pasta-fagioli/">

          Cheesy Garlic Biscuits

         <p>
          While making the biscuits, I realized I really needed to update the recipe and re-post them. And, for ease of searching, I also decided that they needed to be renamed to the real title used by the popular restaurant chain known for these.
         </p>
         <p>
          We love these biscuits and especially how easy they are to whip together. Its not often that Ill use Bisquick, but its the key here to baking these up in minutes. And let me tell you, Im all for a quick option thats THIS tasty!

             <div id="zlrecipe-summary" itemprop="description">
              <p class="summary italic">
               A copycat version of the cheesy garlic biscuits you find served at Red Lobster.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 cups Bisquick mix
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 1/2 cups shredded cheddar
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
              2/3 cup milk
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 tbsp butter
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 tsp dried parsley
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
              1/2 tsp garlic salt
             </li>
            </ul>
            <span class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
             <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Preheat oven to 400? and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or use a baking stone.
             </p>
             <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Combine Bisquick, garlic powder and cheese in medium bowl.  Stir in milk until just combined.  Drop lumps of dough onto cookie sheet and bake for 10 minutes.
             </p>
             <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Meanwhile, melt the butter. Stir in parsley and garlic salt.  When the timer goes off after 10 minutes, brush the butter mixture onto the biscuits. Bake for an additional 5 minutes, or until golden brown.
             </p>
             <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Optional: Before serving, brush biscuits with additional garlic butter mixture.
             </p>

          <div class="comment-content">
           <p>
            To protect your health, minimize prepared foods. This recipe is greatly improved with the following substitutions (explanation included)
           </p>
           <p>
            Substitute the 2 cups Bisquick mix with:
           </p>
           <p>
            2 cups unbleached white flour plus 4 teaspoons baking powder and 2 teaspoons baking soda (Bisquick mix includes additional preservatives and chemicals not needed by your body)
           </p>
           <p>
            Substitute 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder with:
            <br/>
            1 small fresh clove of garlic, finely chopped (dried garlic retains about 10% of the nutritional value of fresh garlic)
           </p>
           <p>
            1 1/2 cups shredded cheddar
            <br/>
            2/3 cup milk
            <br/>
            2 tbsp butter
           </p>
           <p>
            Substitute 1 tsp dried parsley with:
            <br/>
            1 teaspoon fresh chopped parsley (dried parsley retains 10% of the flavor)
           </p>
           <p>
            Substitute 1 tsp garlic salt with:
            <br/>
            Regular table salt and 1/2 teaspoon finely chopped garlic (add to melted butter)
           </p>
           <p>
            I made these last night and decided that even though they are delicious, they dont flake enough. Next time I will add some frozen chopped butter to the batter and see if it helps.
           </p>
