       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         London Hotel Tips for American Tourists  How to Acclimatize to Life in Englands Capital
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="Hotel in Earls Court, London" class="alignright wp-image-285" height="285" src="http://blog.amsterdam-hotel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Triple-Room-Apartment-Earls-Court.jpg" title="Hotel in Earls Court, London" width="400"/>
         On any given day, scores of our American friends check in to the
         <em>
          <strong>
           Hotel Amsterdam
          </strong>
         </em>
         for a London vacation theyll never forget. With Buckingham Palace, Piccadilly Circus, Harrods and countless other world famous attractions all on our doorstep, Englands capital city is as exciting as it can be daunting for those making their first visit.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Indeed, whilst lots of aspects of London life will seem familiar to those travelling from the USA, there are a few things we do differently over here that its worth familiarizing yourself with before you land.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         So, whether youre coming to our West London hotel for a spot of sightseeing, to take care of International business, or to watch our sporting giants at nearby Stamford Bridge, heres our top London tips for American tourists.
         <span id="more-1127">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h1 style="text-align: justify;">
         Remember your adapters
        </h1>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Probably one of the most obvious things youll notice when you first land at The Amsterdam, our plug sockets have a different configuration than those youll be used to over in America. If you dont have an adapter to hand, youll be out of luck when it comes to charging your phone, your iPad or even plugging in your hair dryer.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         If you forget to bring one, dont worry, there are loads of London shops near to the Hotel Amsterdam that stock plug adapters and other travel essentials.
        </p>
        <h1 style="text-align: justify;">
         Avoid rush hour when travelling around London
        </h1>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The easiest way for tourists to get around London is to take what the locals call The Tube, or, to give its proper name, the London Underground network. When you stay at
         <a href="http://www.amsterdam-hotel.com/">
          the Amsterdam Hotel
         </a>
         , Earls Court Station is your first port of call when it comes to accessing all that London has to offer, though we recommend avoiding using them in rush hour, when hundreds of London residents cram on to the tubes to make their commute to work.
        </p>
        <h1 style="text-align: justify;">
         Prepare for the lingo
        </h1>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Having welcomed guests from all four corners of the globe, were well versed in the terms and phrases our international guests are familiar with, but if youre speaking to a fellow guest or another Londoner, there might be a few phrases that dont immediately make sense. If youre given directions to your hotel room and told to take the lift, we do of course mean the elevator. A mobile is a cell phone, and yes, as you might have heard, lots of Londoners do call each other mate.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Are you a US visitor to the Amsterdam Hotel?
         </strong>
         What tips would you give to your fellow Americans? Wed love to hear from you. Get in touch, and if we may even include them in a future blog.
        </p>
