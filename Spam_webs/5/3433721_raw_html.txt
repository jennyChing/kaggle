  alliance between gay activists and striking miners in his new film Pride

        <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           Andrew Scott has a way of making an entrance. As Moriarty in the BBCs Sherlock, it was a long-awaited and much-hailed one around a deserted swimming pool at midnight. Sidling in, his greeting to the sleuths was a taunting, reedy, high-pitched Hiii!, which put us on instant madman alert  and brought him to the attention of every casting director in the country.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           These are busy times for Scott. Hes starred in a Royal Court play this year (
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/theatre-reviews/10755070/Birdland-Royal-Court-Theatre-review.html" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             Birdland
            </strong>
           </a>
           ) and featured in three independent films in quick succession (Locke, The Stag, Jimmys Hall). Shooting has also wrapped on his first two studio pictures, Victor Frankenstein, opposite James McAvoy and Daniel Radcliffe, and Alice er, Alice II? Alice Through the Looking Glass? As I think Lewis Carroll preferred to call it.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           All this sudden demand  a lot of it prompted by his Bafta-winning performance as Moriarty  could make a man grand. Scott is anything but. In person he is all smiles. The 37-year-old Irish actor is nimble, chatty and the epitome of laid-back, dressed in a bright citrus T-shirt and off-white trousers.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           The truth is, Scott has been acting his socks off for the best part of 20 years, never seeking the limelight, only the parts that will let him flex and expand his talent best. I first became aware of Scott in a 2006 Royal Court play called Dying City, in which he played identical twin brothers  a Harvard-educated squaddie sent to Iraq, and his gay brother. He was dazzling in both parts, and found far more definition and contrast in them than just straight versus gay.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           As fans of his Moriarty will attest, he can be a blazingly vivid performer, a real live wire. But hes just as great at quiet, subtle, soulful roles, at acting whats not spoken. In the sweet Irish lad-com The Stag, he was incredibly charming and sad as the best man unable to admit his broken-hearted crush on the bride. Scott told us everything with rueful close-ups  it must have been a directors dream to be able to ditch exposition almost entirely and just rely on his face.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="related-links-float-design related_links_inline" id="tmg-related-links">
          <div class="headerOne styleSix">
           <h2>
            <span>
             Related Articles
            </span>
           </h2>
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="blog">
            <p>

         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           He has a similar task in his new movie, Pride. Matthew Warchuss big-hearted comedy-drama tells of the alliance between gay activists and struggling Welsh miners under Thatchers government. When Dominic West, with earrings and blond highlights, clears the floor in a packed miners social hall and gyrates his hips to Shirley &amp; Companys Shame, Shame, Shame, the film establishes its keynote of rebellious exuberance.
          </p>
          <p>
           So its typical of Scott to play a different note, more softly, and in a minor key. Hes a Welshman called Gethin, boyfriend of Wests Jonathan, and owner of the Gays the Word bookshop in Bloomsbury. As a boy, Gethin was forced to leave home, back in the days when being the only gay in the village wasnt a feasible life path. Hes completely estranged from his uncomprehending religious mother, and cant really even bear to think about her.
          </p>
          <p>
           When I met Matthew [Warchus], my concern was, how do you make somebody who doesnt speak a huge amount register on screen in an ensemble of very busy, effervescent people? And that, by the way, is what the real people are like! That spirit is so alive among them. So we spoke about the moments that would stick out.
          </p>
          <p>
           Scott didnt have Gethins trouble  hes talked about how coming out was actually kind of a delightful experience, and is happily committed in a long-term, London-based relationship. Hes played all sorts of gay characters over the years, and at least as many straight ones, and seems entirely without preference in this department.
          </p>
          <p>
           In relation to this film, people keep talking about the notion of 'playing gay. Which I always think is preposterous, that supposition. When youve got 10 leading characters who are gay, youre not playing 'the gay one. This is a character where its much more about his nationality, his national identity, anyway.
          </p>
          <p>
           Growing up in Dublin in the Eighties must have given him his own particular view of Thatchers Britain. I remember the miners strike, of course, and I remember her presence. I remember that feeling of hatred. But I really do hate the idea of us and them, the idea that you have to be naturally suspicious of people that are different to you. He describes the film, very astutely, as essentially a romantic comedy between two opposing groups, rather than two people.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its saying that were much more similar to each other than we think. Im reluctant to say that now is a terrible time, because I have to believe that now is a good time, and I do! But theres still something missing. Having seen Pride, someone said to me that they were satiated by something that they didnt even know they were hungry for.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img caption="" copyright="Nicola Dove" declared-caption="" height="287" name="pride-dominic-west-andrew-scott" photographer="" refid="3036569" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/03036/pride-dominic-west_3036569c.jpg" version="c" width="460"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <sub>
            Ben Schnetzer, Joseph Gilgun, Andrew Scott and Dominic West in Pride
           </sub>
          </p>
          <p>
           Given the, shall we say, inglorious Tory record on gay rights in that era, does he find it at all ironic that its under Camerons government that gay marriage has finally been voted through? I think thats pretty cool, actually! Its preposterous to me  sometimes I think people talk about different types of sexuality as if they were invented in 1973. You know, its been going on since the dawn of humanity, and will continue until the end of humanity, whenever that may be.
          </p>
          <p>
           He throws down a napkin theatrically and does a cross-legged leap back in his seat. I dont believe people are intrinsically homophobic. I think theyre ignorant, and they need to be exposed to things. Theres something very relieving about the idea of attributes of compassion and heroism and a belief in everybody. So its not a film for just gay people or miners. Just because youre not a woman, doesnt mean you dont believe in womens rights!
          </p>
          <p>
           I dont want to use the term game-changer about Scotts Moriarty, but luckily he uses it for me, invisible quotation marks and all. Theres no doubt it has boosted his profile  he says he has to get a bit fighty to avoid being asked to repeat himself, still finding his own independent avenues. And hes glad fame didnt come too soon: Nor do I want it now, he adds.
          </p>
          <p>
           You can understand why. Thanks to Sherlock, he has an almost scarily devoted fan base who watch his every move. Theres YouTube footage of him being mobbed at the stage door after a performance of Birdland, uncomfortably pressed into a brief telephone chat with someones sister in Spain. It can get a bit crazy. You have to escape via an alternative exit. But then youll walk down the street unrecognised to Prt  Manger.
          </p>
          <p>
           He must see what its like for his friend Benedict Cumberbatch and recoil even further. He copes with it really well. The Sherlock stars, from the sound of it, are all adjusting to the new audience they bring along. Birdland was sold out, and Martin [Freeman]s play [Richard III at Trafalgar Studios] was sold out, and
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/11003309/Benedict-Cumberbatch-a-Hamlet-worth-waiting-for.html" target="_blank">
             I believe Benedicts play [Hamlet at the Barbican] is doing OK
            </a>
           </strong>
           ?! I sent him a text the other day, saying Id pay up to three grand for a ticket, book me in.
          </p>
          <p>
           Scott hasnt forgotten the tabloid rumours of overexcited Freeman fans disrupting performances of Richard III. But its brilliant that young people get excited, he says. I feel very strongly that the theatre isnt just for white, middle-class people. Someones phone might go off, but for the most part theyre extremely respectful, they know the rules. I dont think its fair to sensationalise and say theyre throwing cake at each other. Its fear-mongering and its wrong. A little bit like Pride, that idea of us and them, I really abhor that. I hate it.
          </p>
          <p>
           If theres a surprising dearth of any one thing on Scotts rsum, its Shakespeare. Ive always thought he could be one hell of a Dane, with that aching, soul-sick charisma of his. Will it be his turn soon? Well, erm. Im really looking forward to seeing Benedict. But there are, erm whats the thing to say? Its in the ether.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Pride is out now
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>

                        '&lt;div id="nextArticle"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleInnerWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;p id="readNextLabel"&gt;MOST VIEWED IN NEWS&lt;/p&gt;';
                        
				if(settings.articleImage &amp;&amp; settings.articleImage!="null" &amp;&amp; settings.displayThumbnail){
					template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageThumbnail" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;img src="'+ settings.articleImage +'"/&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';
				}
                        
				template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageTitle" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;' + settings.articleTitle + '&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="closeBtn"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="#"&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;iframe style="border:none;position:absolute;top:0;left:0; height:100%;width:100%;z-index:-1;" id="iefix" src="about:blank"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' + //fix for IE bug
                        '&lt;/div&gt;';
                return template;
            };


            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }

                        if($(window).scrollTop() + $(window).height() == $(document).height()) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }
                    });
                }
            };

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();

    };
  </script>
