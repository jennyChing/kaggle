           LIGHTSPEED (Now it can be told!)
          </a>
         </dt>
         <dd class="entry-text">
          <dl class="vcard author">
           <dt>
            <img alt="Boosh Rabbit" height="100" src="http://l-userpic.livejournal.com/63060996/934598" width="100"/>
           </dt>
           <dd class="username">
            <span class="ljuser i-ljuser i-ljuser-type-P " lj:user="pfarley">

          <div class="entry-content">
           It looks like I can now speak publicly about the
           <em>
            secret film project
           </em>
           I worked on for the past 2.5 years: Its titled
           <strong>
            LIGHTSPEED
           </strong>
           , its written by Terry Rossio and Bill Marsilii, and its been purchased by Disney :
           <a href="http://bit.ly/aa8kyA" rel="nofollow">
            http://bit.ly/aa8kyA
           </a>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           If I had to give an elevator pitch, I'd describe it as "
           <em>
            The Fast &amp; The Furious
           </em>
           meets
           <em>
            The Last Starfighter
           </em>
           ," but since I'm not an official representative of the film, don't quote me on that. All I can tell you is,
           <strong>
            it's not a remake
           </strong>
           . It's a brand spanking new space opera set in a completely original SF universe.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           My involvement was the creation of concept art for the screenplay, although there's no telling if my designs will be used in the actual film or not. If they are, cool! I'll get some money and maybe see some toys based on my artwork appearing on store shelves. If not, well... Igot to draw spaceships and aliens for two years. That was pretty awesome. I can't complain about that.
          </div>

           <b>

           </b>

I don't know when I'm going to get everything fixed and uploaded, let alone when I will complete your favorite story. But there's the new home, and to underscore how shitty, broken down, incomplete and primitive it all is, I've skinned the site in a prehistoric 2-bit CRT green.
