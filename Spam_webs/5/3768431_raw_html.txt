        <div class="source-title">
         Word Origin and History for
         <span class="u-headword">
          mercurial
         </span>

        <div class="source-box oneClick-area">
         <div class="source-data">
          <div class="def-list">
           <span class="dbox-italic">
            adj.
           </span>
           <section class="def-pbk">
            <div class="def-set">
             <p>
              late 14c., "pertaining to the planet Mercury" (see
              <a class="dbox-xref" href="/browse/Mercury">
               Mercury
              </a>
              ). Meaning "sprightly, volatile, quick" (1590s) is from supposed qualities of those born under the planet Mercury (they also are the qualities of the god Mercury), probably also partly by association with the qualities of quicksilver. A variant in this sense was
              <span class="dbox-italic">
               mercurious
              </span>
              (1590s). Related:
              <span class="dbox-italic">
               Mercurially
              </span>
              ;
              <span class="dbox-italic">
               mercuriality
              </span>
              .
             </p>
            </div>
