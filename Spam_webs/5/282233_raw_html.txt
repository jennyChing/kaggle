        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Grow Cucumbers up instead of along the ground
        </h1>
        <h2 class="entry-meta">
        </h2>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         Give your cucumbers a Lift
        </h2>
        <p>
         Anyone who has grown cucumbers knows that they take up a LOT of room in the garden. One way to overcome this space problem is to use trellises or other climbing structures so that your cucumbers will grow vertically instead of crawling along on the ground and using up precious garden space.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thegardeningcook.com/grow-cucumbers-up/cucumbersupinsteadofout/" rel="attachment wp-att-15357">
          <img alt="For an easy harvest, grow your cucumbers up instead of out!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15357" height="901" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cucumbersupinsteadofout..jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
        <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
         <center>
          <!--TBN BTF 300x250 pos 4 STARTS-->
          <div id="tbn-2023b47e-aed3-457b-85af-40aa870397e4">
           <script type="text/javascript">
            window.$tbn = window.$tbn || {"d":function(){}}; $tbn.d("2023b47e-aed3-457b-85af-40aa870397e4");
           </script>
          </div>
          <!--TBN BTF 300x250 pos 4 ENDS-->
         </center>
        </div>
        <p>
         An added benefit of growing cucumbers on trellises is that they will be easier to harvest, less prone to diseases and the fruit will be cleaner.
        </p>
        <p>
         I admit I am not the handiest person with a hammer and bolts, so I just used some old wire fence posts and jute to tie up my Cucumber trellis in a square shape. It is not beautiful but it does the trick nicely.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thegardeningcook.com/grow-cucumbers-up/cukeup3/" rel="attachment wp-att-4899">
          <img alt="Cucumber trellis" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4899" data-id="4899" height="412" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cukeup3.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         The vines are grabbing both the jute and the fence posts and growing up them. It makes it so much easier to see where the cucumbers are and keeps it all contained very nicely.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thegardeningcook.com/grow-cucumbers-up/cukeup2/" rel="attachment wp-att-4898">
          <img alt="jute tied to fence posts" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4898" data-id="4898" height="938" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cukeup2.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         I just wrapped jute three times around the posts at foot intervals along the side and kept walking around them until it was wrapped like a cage.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://thegardeningcook.com/grow-cucumbers-up/cukeup1/" rel="attachment wp-att-4897">
          <img alt="cucumbers growing up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4897" data-id="4897" height="901" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cukeup1.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         The cucumbers love it. They are already growing up to the top of it.
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/TheGardeningCook" target="_blank">
          Gardening Cook on Facebook
         </a>
         has some creative people who contribute to the page. This cucumber trellis was built by fan
         <strong>
          <em>
           Allison Normans
          </em>
         </strong>
         husband. Want to share your husband with me for a weekend Allison?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://thegardeningcook.com/grow-cucumbers-up/gardens-100/" rel="attachment wp-att-5070">
          <img alt="a frame trellis" class=" wp-image-5070 aligncenter" data-id="5070" height="487" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/gardens54.jpg" width="365"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Another of my Facebook fans 
         <strong>
          <em>
           Debbie Smith Runnels
          </em>
         </strong>
         suggested this option for a trellis:
         <em>
          I had some cut off limbs from my confederate roses, which I cut every year when finished blooming. Itied them like a tepee and ran my cumber vines up them and it works wonderfully, and it was really cheap too!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want a professional looking cucumber trellis, this A frame model might suit you.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmenttherapy.com/super-simple-diy-cucumber-trel-153202" rel="attachment wp-att-4428" target="_blank">
          <img alt="A frame Cucumber trellis" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-4428" data-id="4428" height="333" src="http://thegardeningcook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/gardens26-500x333.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
         To build this trellis you will need:
        </p>
        <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
        <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
