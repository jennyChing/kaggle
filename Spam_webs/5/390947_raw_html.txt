        <h2 class="headline">
         Slimming World On A Forced Budget
        </h2>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <span class="post_date" title="2014-10-30">
          October 30, 2014
         </span>
         <span class="post_author_intro">
          by
         </span>
         <span class="post_author author">
          admin
         </span>
         <a class="num_comments_link" href="http://myweightlossdream.co.uk/slimming-world-on-a-forced-budget/#commentform" rel="nofollow">
          <span class="num_comments">
           0
          </span>
          comments
         </a>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="post_content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="food-budget" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-6231 pib-hover-img" data-lazy-src="http://cdn.myweightlossdream.co.uk/images/food-budget-300x196.jpg" data-pin-url="http://myweightlossdream.co.uk/slimming-world-on-a-forced-budget/" height="196" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="food-budget" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-6231 pib-hover-img" data-pin-url="http://myweightlossdream.co.uk/slimming-world-on-a-forced-budget/" height="196" src="http://cdn.myweightlossdream.co.uk/images/food-budget-300x196.jpg" width="300"/>
        </noscript>
        Often with diets like Slimming World, Weight Watchers &amp; the Paleo diet it is often thought that to lose weight and follow a healthy diet that you need the bank balance to match.
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I have found this is more so in the US because of how expensive fruit and vegetables are. For example I was recently reading an article via Facebook about the cost of fruit and vegetables at the other side of the Atlantic and I nearly fainted at the cost of eating healthy.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Someone was celebrating on Facebook about how much they were paying for some apples it was $7.99 for a Kilo which transferred into Euros worked out at 6,39 and we had bought some ourselves on the same day for 1,29 and we just couldnt believe the difference. Especially, when you consider apples to be such a cheap fruit and not expensive like some fruits and vegetables can be.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         If you are on a budget though it must be very hard to diet, because take Slimming World and how much of the free food is fruit and vegetables and if you are paying large sums for this and this is without even factoring in dairy products, meat or fish then you are going to struggle to adapt it for you.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I have never felt poor on the Slimming World diet myself though.
         <a href="http://myweightlossdream.co.uk/slimming-world-abroad-part-1-slimming-world-free-foods/">
          <strong>
           I blogged not so long ago about adapting the diet for Portugal for example I am not willing to pay the silly prices for the yoghurts over here or the cottage cheese
          </strong>
         </a>
         . But I have found plenty of other things for me and with fruit and vegetables being cheaper here than in England I dont feel like I miss out.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         What if I did have an enforced budget?
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <em>
          This is exactly what happened about 10 days ago..
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Dominic ended up spending the night in A&amp;E and was away from me and Kyle from first thing in the morning until the evening the next day. As such I didnt know he had some spare cash in the cupboard at the time, he had a flat battery on his phone so we couldnt communicate and I realised I had 5 for me and Kyle until he came home. He has the bank cards and I dont even know the pin numbers for them. Plus its the not knowing how long he was going to be and when you are pregnant and eating for two you eat a lot more!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I also hate the oven we have and have never used it (just the rings on top) and with being as blind as a bat Dominic always does the food in the steamer.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Therefore I am left (the day before our weekly shop) with hardly any money, not knowing when he is going to be returning and little kitchen appliances at my disposal.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         And it got me thinking about when you have a budget enforced especially when you dont know when you will next have money available. And I thought I would share with what we had:
        </p>
        <center>

         <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
          Day 1
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          I skipped lunch because I worried about Dominic who has a really high blood pressure and higher than anyone else I have ever seen. And settle for just a little bit of chocolate that is in the cupboard. We have so much dark chocolate each in the place so as I know he wont be home anytime soon I eat his!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Then for dinner in the evening I use the cupboard cans and have some beans on wholemeal toast with Kyle. He normally gets a sardine sandwich in the evening and is happy to get something cooked. He hates eggs and it was on the menu for lunch at school so he had gone with hardly any lunch.
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
          Day 2
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          For breakfast there are two bananas left (it is shopping day) and Kyle and I have one each with our cereal. Dominic normally does the school run because with me still working long hours and being in the last trimester of pregnancy he likes me to have a sleep in during the morning.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Kyle doesnt seem too taken back that he has to walk to school though and that has to be progress!!!!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          For dinner Kyle and I share a chicken curry with some chips. My mum gave me a jar of curry sauce and as Dominic hates curry we take the chance to have some. It is one of those stir in tika sauces that is processed and I am not a fan of them but I am surprised by how amazing it tastes. We are out of onions so we put in it a chicken breast, a can of hotdogs (to bulk it up a bit), throw in a bit of vegetables and then have it with some chips made in the Airfryer.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          All in the entire thing would still remain on plan with Slimming World with the use of a few Syns. I am impressed with you go through the cupboard what you can achieve. I am also thinking from a week in hospital prospective and that I have enough in the freezer and in cupboards to get us by and just by getting some cheap fruit and vegetables from the shop and reminding Kyle that he has lost his luxuries I know we are not going to starve to death.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <em>
           Kyle takes the news that we wont be able to top up on chocolate, cereal bars, milkshakes and yoghurts in his stride and is a delight to be around.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Just as we are finishing our curry Dominic walks in the door with his high blood pressure medication and takes us through the tales of what it is like spending the night in A&amp;E and how he feels much better.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          I am grateful to have him home and remind him in future to make sure his phone is charged and to leave me some spare cash!!!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          It just makes you realise that you can stick to plan when youre stressed or when you think your food budget has been cut. And I actually enjoyed the process of going through the cupboard and what to make out of a few ingredients and it felt like I was a contestant on Ready Steady Cook  though I am a slow cook so it was certainly not made in 20 minutes!!!!
         </p>
