                  <img alt="AP" class="wp-image-3726" height="384" src="http://www.todayspigskin.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Browns-WRs.jpg" width="512"/>
                 </a>

                <p style="text-align: center;">
                </p>
                <p style="text-align: center;">
                 <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
                  <strong>
                   Poppen Off! Browns Weak WRs
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 With superstar wide receiver Josh Gordon suspended for the season Cleveland enters 2014 with the absolute worst receiving corps in football.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The Browns projected starting receivers combined for just one touchdown reception last season. Not a single one of these receivers would be anything more than a slot receiver in any other offense in the league. Cleveland failed to address the position in the draft even with the knowledge that this suspension was looming, passing on names like Sammy Watkins, Mike Evans and Kelvin Benjamin.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The organizational failure displayed by the Browns is absolutely pathetic. How can the team expect either Hoyer or Manziel to succeed with this current crop of receivers?
                </p>
                <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_3725" style="width: 310px">
                 <a href="http://www.todayspigskin.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Miles-CLE.jpg">
                  <img alt="AP Image" class="size-medium wp-image-3725" height="211" src="http://www.todayspigskin.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Miles-CLE-300x211.jpg" width="300"/>
                 </a>
                 <p class="wp-caption-text">
                  AP Image
                 </p>
                </div>
                <p>
                 Miles Austin is essentially a walking hamstring injury having missed time just about every season with some form of leg ailment. Nate Burleson is better known for his pizza incident than he is for being an NFL wide receiver. Andrew Hawkins is decent, but at 58 does not exactly scream No. 1 receiver. Hawkins fizzled out in Cincinnati due to injury concerns and his small stature makes it likely he gets hurt again for the Browns.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Passing up on literally every receiver in a very deep rookie class will ultimately be the downfall of the Browns this season. Losing Josh Gordon for the year absolutely cripples any chance of this team making a run at the postseason. Brian Hoyer has been set up to fail by the Browns front office. Hoyer comes into the year with nothing more than tight end Jordan Cameron as a legitimate receiving target.
                </p>
                <p>
                 It is a shame that the Browns dropped the ball at the receiver position. The rest of this roster is actually quite talented, and in a very winnable AFC North could have made some noise in 2014.
                </p>
