        <div class="editors-post-body">
         <p>
          <em>
           Every week,
           <a href="/users/26598-carolinewright">
            Caroline Wright
           </a>
           creates simple, civilized recipes for Food52 that feed four -- for under $20, in under 20 minutes.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Today: Bring those unloved chicken breasts back to life with a quick simmer in milk.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="/recipes/22067-milk-poached-chicken-with-tarragon-and-peas">
           <em>
            <img alt="" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8106/8642731353_7af2dc6944_z.jpg" width="620"/>
           </em>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I dont love boneless, skinless chicken breasts. They rank among the ingredients that, as a recipe developer by profession, I see often enough in my work life that I would rarely  if ever  bring them home in a grocery sack of my own choosing.
         </p>
         <p>
          Poaching chicken breasts in milk, however, is a very simple way to enhance the cuts texture and flavor through tender, gentle cooking that ends with tucking it into a bed of noodles. Watch the milk carefully to ensure it never visibly simmers  the key to this recipes success is taking usually quick-cooking ingredients and slowing down the tempo to find their softer side.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="/recipes/22067-milk-poached-chicken-with-tarragon-and-peas">
           Milk-Poached Chicken with Tarragon and Peas
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Serves 4
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2 large boneless, skinless chicken breasts (1 pound)
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           2 garlic cloves, smashed with flat side of knife
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           4 strips lemon zest
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           3 cup whole milk
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           4 sprigs fresh tarragon, divided
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           4 cups wide egg noodles
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           1/2 cup sour cream
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           2 cups frozen peas, thawed
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="/recipes/22067-milk-poached-chicken-with-tarragon-and-peas">
           See the full recipe (and save and print it) here.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
