          <p>
           <script>
            ComplexEmbed.Application.initialize({cId: "N0MHRjbjrALXwXw0OIS1SZA5vgej9QUx",adSetCode: "6d27de9204b549c6a7acd80852e47a20",site: "stupiddope",kw: ""});
           </script>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The city of Atlanta was hit hard by a billowing wave of rollerbladers and extreme sport enthusiasts for the fifth annual A-Town Stomp. The Stomp was borne from the steaming pavement of Hotlanta, and the rising temperatures alerted everyone that some stupidDOPE blading was in the citys forecast. The showcasing of extraordinary talent was the idea of brothers Carson and Brian Starnes, and they believe the raw talent to be observed in the streets from whence it came. With skater funded dollars, the Stomp had over $2000 in cash and prizes for the top spots.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           In its growth as a grassroots competition, the A-Town Stomp has taken place in all corners of the city except the West side of Atlanta. Needless to say, this shindig went off without a hitch Well one hitch for a boat. But we will get to that.
           <br/>
           The competition all went down on June 14th 2014, and the locations predetermined to accomodate the horde of skaters and fans alike.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           After meeting up at Fourth Ward Skatepark for registration, the contest relocated to the first spot, where a train of skaters made for rather quick thinking situations. The crowd of onlookers were able to spot the top skating manuevers from a short distance. The zooming of urethane wheels on the concrete became a buzzing, akin to a bees nest. But with a first place prize that included over $1000 in money and swag, every skater was out for a taste of the honey.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The second spot raised the bar from its predecessor. A handrail sloping down a hill provided a much more advanced obstacle to the skaters who were still hungry for the top spot. The idea of trying to perform more than a couple grinds on the rail to stair combo was enough to weed out a majority of the competitors, but a select few were still enticed to move forward (sometimes fakie too).
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           We here at stupidDOPE can say that there were many astounding feats accomplished at the second spot. With every great trick, there was a spill though, as a few bladers felt the body jarring results of missing the footing on a rail. But lets get to this afformentioned boat
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The third and final location for the A-Town Stomp is always the most creative of the spots chosen for the competition. Whether its an urban DIY skatepark, an old parking lot, or even a car outfitted with various rainbow rails and ramps, the Starnes brothers certainly know how to incite a riot of wonderment, leaving the audience and participants alike wondering What the hell just happened? This year, a boat was the centerpiece of the third spot. How do we mean, you ask? This boat was stripped of its innards, as the remaining competitors were able to grind the sides of the vehicle. A runway contructed in the middle of the boat allowed the best bladers to launch out to an obstacle placed a couple feet away. Thanks to great construction and teamwork, the third spot was smooth sailing, so to speak. Dope music, sandwiches, iniebriants and smiling faces made the third spot bearable for the spectators as the sun tore into the cell walls of everyones skin. But the heat was only an annoyance to the dedicated at the foundation spot.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           As the contest came to a close, it was clear that we were in the presence of some truly dope athletes. The after party came later that night where the brothers Starnes announced the winners and best tricks amongst an intoxicated sea of people.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The top three to place are as follows:
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           1. Kevin LaPierre  Quebec
           <br/>
           2. Jarrod Banning  Texas
           <br/>
           3. Philip Moore  Alabama
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Get to know these individuals, because they are the reason that rollerblading is so incredible.
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            A special shoutout to Carson and Brian Starnes for hosting the A-Town Stomp, we here at stupidDOPE are proud to say that Atlanta is on the forefront of a new age in action sports.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Thanks to my girlfriend for keeping me on track and helping me lug gear and get shots with fellow skaters. Lastly, thanks to everyone who participated, donated or even spread the word about the Stomp, as it surely would not have been the positive environment that we were able to be a part of, even if just for a day.
          </p>
