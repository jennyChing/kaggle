           <span class="first-paragraph">
            <span class="paragraph-0">
             <p>
              MOULTRIE  Moultrie police seized on Wednesday a large quantity of suspected crystal meth and several weapons and arrested three men at a Moultrie motel.
             </p>
            </span>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-1">
            <p>
             Based on their investigation, officers searched a room at Cocomo Inn, 1713 First Ave. S.E.
            </p>
           </span>
           <div class="p402_hide no-images no-quotes yes-pagination">
           </div>
           <span class="paragraph-2">
            <p>
             We got information about individuals selling crystal meth out of a room, Moultrie Police Department Inv. Eric Fries said.
            </p>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-3">
            <p>
             After getting a search warrant based on that information, officers with the police department and Colquitt County Sheriffs Office entered the room at about 11 p.m. Wednesday.
            </p>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-4">
            <p>
             We located about 85 grams of crystal meth, Fries said. We were able to tie three individuals to the room and we were able to apprehend them a short time later. They actually returned to the room while we were still there.
            </p>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-5">
            <p>
             Officers also found three pistols and seized a significant amount of cash from one of the men, Fries said.
            </p>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-6">
            <p>
             The substance seized was not the normal methamphetamine that is made locally in plastic drink cans using decongestant pills.
            </p>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-7">
            <p>
             It is the form of the drug that requires an industrial-type setting for its manufacture, Fries said. It likely is being imported to larger cities like Miami and Atlanta from which it is being distributed to surrounding cities.
            </p>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-8">
            <p>
             From what Im being told its not being manufactured in the U.S., he said.
            </p>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-9">
            <p>
             Charged with trafficking methamphetamine, possession of a firearm during the commission of a felony and obstructing or hindering a law enforcement officer were: Stacy B. Blackwell Jr., 21 of Moultrie; Karron Blackwell, 21, of Ellenton; and Rodney Rashard Morris, 22, of Moultrie.
            </p>
           </span>
           <span class="paragraph-10">
            <p>
             The money seized was found on Morris, Fries said. He did not give the amount of the cash that officers found.
            </p>
           </span>
