     <div class="e-content entry-content copy" itemprop="description articleBody">
      <p>
       Extending from the Nile River to ancient Rome, the Mediterranean Sea is home to some of historys most fascinating cities and sights. Starting this summer, it will also be the new home of
       <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/findacruise/ships/class/ship/home.do?shipCode=AL">
        <em>
         Allure of the Seas
        </em>
       </a>
       <em>
        .
       </em>
       She will be sailing round-trip from Barcelona and Civitavecchia (near Rome) all summer long.
      </p>
      <p>
       Even with all its star turns in movies and history books, how well do you really know the Mediterranean? In preparation for
       <em>
        Allure
       </em>
       s arrival, weve compiled a list of 10 facts that may surprise you. So test your knowledge and tuck these away. You never know when theyll come in handy (hint: Trivia Night).
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        The Romans thought the sea was, literally,
        <strong>
         the middle of the earth
        </strong>
        . Because of this, they first called the Mediterranean
        <em>
         mediterraneus
        </em>
        Latin for middle of the earth.
       </li>
       <li>
        Believe it or not,
        <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/findacruise/ports/group/home.do?portCode=ROM">
         Rome
        </a>
        is the
        <strong>
         birthplace of the shopping mall
        </strong>
        . The first-ever mall was built by Roman Emperor Trajan between 107 and 110 AD. Trajans Market consisted of several levels and more than 150 outlets that sold everything from clothes to food and spices.
        <a href="http://d38s399h0y8g1i.cloudfront.net/connect/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Rome-Italy-Royal-Caribbean.jpg?dcb443">
         <img alt="OLYMPUS DIGITAL CAMERA" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10111 u-photo" itemprop="image" role="img" src="http://d38s399h0y8g1i.cloudfront.net/connect/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Rome-Italy-Royal-Caribbean-1650x1227.jpg?dcb443"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        The Strait of Gibraltarwhere the Mediterranean Sea joins with the Atlantic Ocean, and Spain nearly touches the top of Africais only
        <strong>
         eight miles wide at its narrowest point
        </strong>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        At the height of the Roman Empire, the entire border of the sea belonged singularly to the Empire for several centuries.
        <strong>
         Today, the sea touches three continents and 21 modern states
        </strong>
        , including 11 countries in Europe, five in Asia, and five in Africa.
       </li>
       <li>
        In 1830, Antica Pizzeria PortAlba, widely believed to be
        <strong>
         the worlds first pizzeria
        </strong>
        , opened its doors in Naples. Today, the restaurants ovens are lined with lava rocks from nearbyMount Vesuvius. Drop in for wood-fired slices or take
        <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/shoreExcursions/product/detail/view.do?sourcePage=shorexByPort&amp;ProductCode=NP87&amp;DestinationCode=">
         a pizza making class
        </a>
        where you can learn how to make your own authentic Neapolitan pie.
       </li>
       <li>
        Mlaga, Spain, a port-of-call on
        <em>
         Allure of the Seas
        </em>
        <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/findacruise/cruiseDetails/itinerary.do?state=&amp;packageCode=AL12T030&amp;hasFireandPolice=&amp;hasMilitary=&amp;hasSenior=&amp;date=&amp;cruiseType=CO">
         transatlantic Mediterranean itinerary
        </a>
        , was built by the Phoenicians in the 7
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        and 8
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        centuries BC, making it
        <strong>
         one of the oldest cities in the world
        </strong>
        . You can still see some of the original city walls in the cellar of the Museo Picasso Mlaga. (That museum, by the way, was built to honor one of the citys most famous natives:
        <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/shoreExcursions/product/detail/view.do?sourcePage=shorexByPort&amp;ProductCode=BR20&amp;DestinationCode=">
         Pablo Picasso
        </a>
        .)
        <a href="http://d38s399h0y8g1i.cloudfront.net/connect/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Malaga_Spain_Royal_Caribbean.jpg?dcb443">
         <img alt="People walking on bridge El Tajo Gorge in Ronda city" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10112 u-photo" height="1178" src="http://d38s399h0y8g1i.cloudfront.net/connect/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Malaga_Spain_Royal_Caribbean-1650x1178.jpg?dcb443" width="1650"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/findacruise/ports/group/home.do?portCode=BCN">
         Barcelona
        </a>
        was recently ranked the Best Beach City in the world by
        <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/top-10/beach-cities-photos/">
         <em>
          National Geographic
         </em>
        </a>
        , butthe citys
        <strong>
         beaches werent even used for leisure until 1992
        </strong>
        when the coastline was revamped for the Summer Olympics (including a five-mile strip of sand imported from Egypt).
        <a href="http://d38s399h0y8g1i.cloudfront.net/connect/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Barcelona-Beach-Royal-Caribbean.jpg?dcb443">
         <img alt="Barceloneta Beach in Barcelona at sunrise" class="u-photo aligncenter wp-image-10109 size-large" height="1342" src="http://d38s399h0y8g1i.cloudfront.net/connect/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Barcelona-Beach-Royal-Caribbean-1650x1342.jpg?dcb443" width="1650"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        In ancient
        <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/findacruise/ports/group/home.do?portCode=ROM">
         Rome
        </a>
        , some residences not only had central heating and indoor plumbing, but also
        <strong>
         heated
        </strong>
        <b>
         floors
        </b>
        . A few more inventions we can thank the Romans for: concrete, newspapers and sewer systems.
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/shoreExcursions/product/detail/view.do?sourcePage=shorexByPort&amp;ProductCode=LL64&amp;DestinationCode=">
         Pisas famous landmark
        </a>
        <strong>
         isnt the only leaning tower in the area
        </strong>
        : the Church of San Nicola and the bell tower at the Church of San Michele degli Scalzi are also taking a similar posture, because of unstable soil in the area. Might be time to hire a new architect.
        <a href="http://d38s399h0y8g1i.cloudfront.net/connect/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Leaning-Pisa-Tower-Royal-Caribbean.jpg?dcb443">
         <img alt="Leaning-Pisa-Tower-Royal-Caribbean" class="u-photo aligncenter wp-image-10119 size-large" height="1650" src="http://d38s399h0y8g1i.cloudfront.net/connect/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Leaning-Pisa-Tower-Royal-Caribbean-1389x1650.jpg?dcb443" width="1389"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        Thats a rap:
        <a href="http://www.royalcaribbean.com/findacruise/ports/group/home.do?portCode=MRS">
         Marseilles
        </a>
        , Frances largest Mediterranean city and cruise port, is
        <strong>
         well-known for itship hop music scene
        </strong>
