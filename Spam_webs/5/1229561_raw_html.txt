        <h1 class="entry-title">
         4 Things to Check Before Your Summer Road Trip
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2014-06-07T12:17:07+00:00">
          Saturday, June 7, 2014
         </span>
         / By
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://thenerdswife.com/author/thenerdswife" rel="author">
            Arena Blake
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <script>
          &lt;!--
dynamicgoogletags.update();
//--&gt;
         </script>
         <p>
          <img alt="4 Things to Check Before Your Summer Road Trip" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8271" height="585" src="http://thenerdswife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/4-Things-to-Check-Before-Your-Summer-Road-Trip-500x585.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Summer is here, and its time to hit the road! And in honor of
          <a href="http://bit.ly/TnYQiU" rel="nofollow">
           Tire Safety Week
          </a>
          , the folks at Right Turn want to be sure that you know these 4 Things To Check Before Your Summer Road Trip.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-8267">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          We are *constantly* in motion during the summer  whether its a visit to SeaWorld or a trip to the beach, our family spends a lot of time on the road. Between packing all the paraphernalia needed for a feisty two-year-old and making sure we dont forget  or lose  anything, checking my tires is not usually something I worry about.
         </p>
         <p>
          But I should!
         </p>
         <p>
          Not only can making sure your tires are in good condition help improve gas mileage, its critical in ensuring your familys safety while on the road.
         </p>
         <h2>
          1. Pressure
         </h2>
         <p>
          <img alt="Pressure" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8272" height="110" src="http://thenerdswife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Pressure-500x110.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Make sure you check the pressure on all four tires, including the spare! And make sure that you check them at least three hours after driving to get an accurate reading  tires heat up when you drive, increasing the pressure. This should be done monthly, as changes in weather can affect pressure, and tires naturally lose pressure over time.
         </p>
         <h2>
          2. Alignment
         </h2>
         <p>
          <img alt="Alignment" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8273" height="110" src="http://thenerdswife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Alignment-500x110.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Did you know that when your wheels are misaligned, they can cause your tires to wear rapidly and unevenly? Check your owners manual for the recommended interval to get your wheels aligned.
         </p>
         <h2>
          3. Rotation
         </h2>
         <p>
          <img alt="Rotation" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8274" height="110" src="http://thenerdswife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Rotation-500x110.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Tires wear differently depending on their location on your vehicle  even if its properly aligned. So its important to have them regularly rotated to allow them to wear consistently. Again, you can check your owners manual for the recommended interval. Its usually 5,000 to 6,000 miles. I like to have mine done every-other oil change. Having a schedule helps me remember to get it done!
         </p>
         <h2>
          4. Tread
         </h2>
         <p>
          <img alt="Tread" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8275" height="110" src="http://thenerdswife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Tread-500x110.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The amount of tread remaining on a tire is probably the easiest  and one of the most important  things to check. If your tires are too worn, it can create a deadly situation for yourself and anyone else in your vehicle. Its important to replace worn tires before an accident can occur.
          <a href="http://bit.ly/TnYQiU" rel="nofollow">
           A visual inspection
          </a>
          is a great way to ensure whether your tires have enough tread remaining.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its amazing how much an effect tread depth can have on a vehicles stopping distance:
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/aXNUU21KAL0?rel=0" width="500">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Luckily, if you do need to have your tires replaced, Right Turn is the perfect place to, well, turn. You can shop online for a set of tires, pay a great price, and find a local dealer-certified installer to put them on your vehicle. Its that easy!
         </p>
         <p>
          To learn more about Tire Safety Week,
          <a href="http://bit.ly/TnYQiU" rel="nofollow">
           click here
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          Do you have any road trips planned this summer?
