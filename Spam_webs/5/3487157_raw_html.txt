      <h2>
       Creativity Prompt #32  Alter A Chipboard Childrens Book
      </h2>
      <div class="postinfo">
      </div>
      January 2nd, 2009 by Avital
      <br/>
      <div class="entry">
       <div addthis:title="Creativity Prompt #32  Alter A Chipboard Childrens Book" addthis:url="http://www.creativityprompt.com/creativity-prompt-32-alter-a-chipboard-childrens-book/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style">
        <a class="addthis_button_google_plusone">
        </a>
        <a class="addthis_button_twitter">
        </a>
        <a class="addthis_button_stumbleupon">
        </a>
        <a class="addthis_button_facebook_like">

       <p>
        <img alt="Altered Chipboard Book" height="291" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3259/3116465383_d856306c6a.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed orange; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;" width="450"/>
        <br/>
        On December 4th my husband and I landed in California after an 11 hours flight and were welcomed by our wonderful friends. Theyd hosted us for over a week, until we found an apartment, and drove us around for all our initial errands. I am very thankful for their help and hospitality. On one of our first shopping sprees, Andrea, my good friend, has introduced me to the one dollar bin at Target, where we bought a childrens chipboard book.
       </p>
       <p>
        The idea of altering a childrens chipboard book has sat in my mind for quite a while and as soon as I saw these one dollar books at Target, I knew I have a project at hand. I made Andrea buy one as well, so she would join me for the craft-session and I enjoyed her talented company a lot.
       </p>
       <p>
        <font style="color: orange;">
         <strong>
          So for this weeks creativity prompt I will show you how I turned this:
         </strong>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Original Book" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3231/3116456509_0c01f5b734_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <font style="color: orange;">
         <strong>
          Into that:
         </strong>
        </font>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Altered Book" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3198/3117291634_ed0b5d64d5_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.creativityprompt.com/my-toolbox/" rel="nofollow" target="blank">
          Supply -
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Chipboard book.
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.shareasale.com/r.cfm?u=279580&amp;b=33086&amp;m=7429&amp;afftrack=&amp;urllink=store.scrapbook.com%2Fbg-urp-1327.html" target="blank">
          Patterned paper
         </a>
         .
        </li>
        <li>
         Glue stick.
        </li>
        <li>
         Craft Knife &amp; Cutting mat.
        </li>
        <li>
         Ruler.
        </li>
        <li>
         Bone folder or a paper trimmer with a scoring blade.
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h3>
        <font style="color: orange;">
         Step By Step Instructions -
        </font>
       </h3>
       <ol>
        <li>
         First, measure the size of each page, the spine and the page fold. Write down the numbers on a scrap of paper.
         <br/>
         <img alt="measuring the spine" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3198/3116457057_48f86bbc30_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
         <br/>
         <font style="color: gray;">
          The Spine.
         </font>
         <br/>
         <img alt="measuring" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3246/3117285030_74366e5592_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
         <br/>
         <font style="color: gray;">
          The Page Fold.
         </font>
        </li>
        <li>
         Cut the patterned paper to the pages size and adhere them on each of the books pages with a generous amount of glue stick. Make sure that the glue is completely dry before moving to the next page, otherwise, the pages will stick together and tear.
         <br/>
         <img alt="gluing" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3096/3116458317_dcbb00d934_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
         <br/>
         <img alt="Laying paper on glue" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3116/3117286480_d482dce347_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
        </li>
        <li>
         After adhering each patterned paper, flip the book and cut the excess paper with a sharp craft knife.
         <strong>
          Make sure not to glue two papers to both sides of the same page before cropping the excess out!
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <img alt="cutting the excess paper" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3275/3116460483_19d5493901_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
        </li>
        <li>
         When applying the patterned paper to the cover, stop at the first score mark on each page. (One is probably flash with the spine and the other with the pages fold.)
         <br/>
         <img alt="The cover" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3185/3116460007_0373cba548_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
        </li>
        <li>
         To conceal the spine start by trimming a patterned paper to the same height of the book and 2 wider than the spine.
        </li>
        <li>
         Next, score the strip at 1 on each side (lengthwise)  that should create the spines score lines.
        </li>
        <li>
         Then score the strip again on one side at the same size of the pages fold that you have measured on step 1. Make the score line, measuring from the spines score line to the edge.
         <br/>
         <img alt="Scoring" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3105/3117288576_149824d015_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
         <p>
          You should end up with a strip of patterned paper which looks like that:
          <br/>
          <img alt="The spine cover" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3224/3116461661_db930cbea8_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
         </p>
        </li>
        <li>
         Glue the patterned paper strip to the book, using a glue stick.
         <br/>
         <img alt="Gluing The Spine Cover" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3295/3117290378_d7189f8dfb_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
        </li>
        <li>
         Cut the excess with a craft knife.
         <br/>
         <img alt="Cutting the excess paper off the spine" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3010/3116463513_390127c2c7_m.jpg" style="border: 0.5px dashed gray; margin: 1em; padding: 2px;"/>
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Voil! You are done and free to embellish the altered book as you wish.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div style="border: 1px dashed orange; padding: 1em;">
        <h3>
         Enjoy!!!
        </h3>
        <p>

          <p>
           Hi Meg. There are two things I can think of that can make that happen: (1) the creases/folds of the papers are covered and thus preventing the pages from being folded (2) the book is too bulky for the width of the spine.
          </p>
          <p>
           To address the (1)st issue, you can use a craft knife to cut, carefully and shallowly, the edges of each crease to re-score it. You may also try to use a scoring tool to do that.
          </p>
          <p>
           To address the (2)nd issue, try to add closure to the book. Maybe just a ribbon or rubber band that ties around it.
          </p>
          <p>
           I hope I was helpful to you.
