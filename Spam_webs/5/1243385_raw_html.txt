      <h1>
       BACKPACKERS FIRST AID KIT
      </h1>
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        There are a number of excellent backpacking/hiking First Aid Kits available from commercial providers. I prefer to put together my own kit. A basic backpacking First Aid Kit should contain, at a minimum:
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-1880">
        </span>
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         A few antibiotic ointment packs
        </li>
        <li>
         Extra-strength non-aspirin tablets for adults &amp; for some for kids
        </li>
        <li>
         6-12 Antiseptic cleansing pads
        </li>
        <li>
         6-12 Alcohol pads
        </li>
        <li>
         A burn treatment gel pad
        </li>
        <li>
         A cold compress
        </li>
        <li>
         Gauze roll bandage
        </li>
        <li>
         Large butterfly wound bandage
        </li>
        <li>
         A few of various size adhesive plastic bandages
        </li>
        <li>
         A sterile eye pad
        </li>
        <li>
         Several gauze pads or varied sizes
        </li>
        <li>
         Roll of adhesive tape
        </li>
        <li>
         First Aid Guide
        </li>
        <li>
         Vinyl gloves
        </li>
        <li>
         Some medium safety pins
        </li>
        <li>
         Scissors
        </li>
        <li>
         Tweezers
        </li>
        <li>
         Several cotton tip sticks/applicators
        </li>
        <li>
         CPR Rescue Breathing Face Mask
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        Splints may be fashioned from natural and man-made materials (branches, towels, tent poles, tarps, etc.). At least one of your group should be CPR and First Aid certified, in my opinion.
       </p>
       <p>
        Additionally, be certain to carry any special medications required by the group (for asthma, diabetes, etc.). A snake bite kit is NOT recommended, as more damage is often done cutting wound &amp; applying tourniquets, than by bites themselves. Simply keep victim calm and motionless, lower bite below heart, send or call for help, and remain with bite victim.
       </p>
