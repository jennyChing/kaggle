          <a href="http://www.streetsandyos.com/archives/2005/11/hand_socks_a_kn.php">
           Hand Socks, a Knitalong and more
          </a>
         </h3>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <div class="entry-body">
           <p>
            Long long ago, my third knit project ever was a pair of fingerless mitts for my friend, Karen.  She calls them "hand socks". After a fine season or two of use, through an unfortunate turn of events, one hand sock was lost. Luckily, I had more yarn! (note: one skein of Cascade 220 will make 3 fingerless mitts in the smallest size, with yarn leftover)
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="HandSock_flat.jpg" height="300" src="http://www.streetsandyos.com/archives/HandSock_flat.jpg" width="400"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            The pattern is from
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1584792914/104-6518345-6647954?v=glance&amp;n=283155&amp;n=507846&amp;s=books&amp;v=glance">
             Weekend Knitting
            </a>
            . I added a little crochet edging to clean up the garter stitch edge. This is slip stitch crochet done with a hook a couple sizes larger.
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="HandSock_edge.jpg" height="222" src="http://www.streetsandyos.com/archives/HandSock_edge.jpg" width="400"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            I tried it on to make sure it fits. Karen's hands are smaller than mine, so it will actually have more room. I promise I didn't stretch it out!
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="HandSock_model.jpg" height="300" src="http://www.streetsandyos.com/archives/HandSock_model.jpg" width="400"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Get this.  My sister,
            <a href="http://www.dopaminefiend.com/">
             SQ
            </a>
            ,  decided she wanted to learn how to knit a braid cable. Did she consult a pattern book or the internet?  nope.  She sat down with yarn and needles and *figured it out*. The resulting
            <a href="http://www.dopaminefiend.com/archives/2005/11/cable_wristband.php">
             chart
            </a>
            is probably unlike any other cable chart you've seen.  Its color-coded!
           </p>
           <p>
            Since SQ is on cable kick, we've decided to knit Demi together. Would anyone else like to knit along? Guaranteed cabley fun! The pattern is from
            <a href="http://www.theknittinggarden.com/ro-magvintagestyle.htm">
             Rowan Vintage Style
            </a>
            (aka "
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1570763127/104-6518345-6647954?v=glance&amp;n=283155&amp;s=books&amp;v=glance">
             Vintage Knits
            </a>
            ") and calls for an Aran weight yarn (16sts/4" in st st).  I'm knitting with Peace Fleece, and SQ is using Harrisville tweed. Leave a comment or drop me an email if you're interested. We plan to start next week, but its casual, you can start anytime in the next couple weeks.
           </p>
           <div align="center">
            <img alt="vintstyle_demi.jpg" height="242" src="http://www.streetsandyos.com/archives/vintstyle_demi.jpg" width="150"/>
           </div>
           <p>
            The NY marathon is this Sunday!!! In case you're out watching the race, I'll be number 15823, going at a 10min/mile pace...or faster if all goes well. 26.2 miles, here I come!
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="Marathon_card.jpg" height="143" src="http://www.streetsandyos.com/archives/Marathon_card.jpg" width="300"/>
           </p>
