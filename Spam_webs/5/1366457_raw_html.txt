          <h1 class="article-title">
           25 Canned Beers for Every Type of Outdoor Fun
          </h1>
          <div class="article-author">
           <em>
            By:
           </em>
           <a href="/1746646/scott-yorko">

             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/canned-beer-every-activity_s.jpg?itok=Z9AOjL6f"/>

            <div class="pg-slide-content">
             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              The stigma is gone. For years, canned beers were derided for their metallic taste. Worse, the options were limitedfew breweries canned beer that youd want to sample, let alone drink 12 ounces of. Then, Oskar Blues started canning its flagship
              <a href="http://brew.oskarblues.com/ob-beers/year-round/dales-pale-ale/" target="_blank">
               Dales Pale Ale
              </a>
              in 2002. And everything changed. Today, more than 500 craft breweries in more than 40 states have fomented the canned revolution.
             </p>
             <p>
              And thats a great thing. Cans save on space and are better for the environment because theyre lighter to ship. But theyre indispensable for the outdoors in other ways: their shatterproof packability and fresh taste, preserved from the depredations of sunlight.
             </p>
             <p>
              The surge in canned-beer offeringsmade possible by mobile canning services and consumer demandhas been excellent for beer aficionados. But the options can be daunting. Our solution? A taste test. After drinking hundreds of canned beers, weve put together the ultimate activity-based sampler list. No matter what your weekend plans are, theres a beer for you.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>

             <a class="pinit-btn" data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=/1678471/destihls-brewery-abbeys-singles&amp;media=http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/migrated-images/destihls-brewery-abbeys-single_s.jpg&amp;description=Abbey's Single">
             </a>
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/pizza-port-swamis-ipa_s.jpg?itok=88YzdBRa"/>

             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              Swami's India Pale Ale
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Pizza Port Brewing Company
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Best for
              </b>
              : Hiking and Camping
             </p>
             <p>
              Crack open this quintessential
              <a href="http://www.pizzaport.com/grog/cans/" target="_blank">
               West Coast IPA
              </a>
              and youll be hit with a strong citrus and pine aroma that blends well with the flavor. Things start sweet, then transition to hoppy bitterness. With a nice light body and a dry finish with lingering hops, its crisp and refreshing while still being bold enough to complement spicy food.
             </p>
             <div class="ad-block-holder">
             </div>
            </div>

            <div class="img-wrap">

             <h2 class="pg-slide-title">
              Heal the Bay IPA
             </h2>
             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Golden Road Brewing
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Best for
              </b>
              : Hiking and Camping
             </p>
             <p>
              As the first and still one of the only breweries canning in Los Angeles, Golden Road gets it right. Of their three canned IPAs,
              <a href="http://www.goldenroad.la/heal-the-bay/" target="_blank">
               Heal the Bay
              </a>
              is the most straightforward, with a bright and citrus-forward taste. Be prepared for the malt to be followed by a brisk blast of fruit. And enjoy the dry finish.
             </p>

             </div>

             </a>
             <img class="slide-img lazy" src="http://www.outsideonline.com/sites/default/files/styles/img_600x600/public/migrated-images/joseph-james-brewing-hop-box-imperial-ipa_s.jpg?itok=wG_Zsur8"/>
             <p class="slide-caption">

             <p class="pg-slide-dek">
              Joseph James Brewing Company
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Best for
              </b>
              : Hiking and Camping
             </p>
             <p>
              This
              <a href="http://www.jjbrewing.com/hop-box-imperial-ipa/" target="_blank">
               rugged IPA
              </a>
              with a long and strong (9.3% alcohol) finish will keep you sipping. Youll be forgiven for drinking it like you would stiff cocktail. The brew has a strong hop aroma, and the caramel malts are exceptionally dark and sweet. Expect little carbonation, a dry finish, and some spiciness.
             </p>

             </div>

             </a>

             <p class="slide-caption">
              <em>
               Photo:
              </em>

             <p class="pg-slide-dek">

             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               Best for
              </b>
              : Hiking and Camping
             </p>

              Just as certain scents can transport you back to a special place, a swig of this
              <a href="http://www.fiddleheadbrewing.com/beer/" target="_blank">
               medium-bodied beer
              </a>
              will have you pining for a campsite hammock, unless youre already in one. The brew is dry hopped for over a month with nearly three pounds of Citra and Simcoe hops per barrel, so expect plenty of bitterness. But it remains surprisingly drinkable, with strong summer fruit throughout.

              </b>

              <a href="http://www.payettebrewing.com/beer/outlaw-ipa" target="_blank">
               Outlaw
              </a>
              hits everything youd expect in a quality IPA. Its crisp, refreshing, and full of a citrus, pine, and hop aroma and taste. Hop bitterness and notes of caramel from the malts combine for an unexpected fruit profile in Payette Brewing Companys first foray into the world of cans.
             </p>

             </div>

            <div class="img-wrap">

              <a href="http://oskarblues.com/" target="_blank">

               Saint Archer
              </a>

Using experimental hops to meld a strong beer with dark color and a slightly crisp mouthfeel, theyve smoothly balanced the caramel malts with light bitterness and a few sweet notes.

The pairing of Narragansett Brewings lager with Dels (a Rhode Island favorite) lemon concentrate is the ultimate refresher, with a balance of malty sweetness and lemonade tartness. When nice and cold, it tastes just like sweet lemon-water ice and gives you an easy buzz, without the sugar crash.
