              <div class="" id="watch-description-text">
               <p id="eow-description">
                Fluffy, decadent and DIVINE! These sweet potato pancakes, stuffed with ricotta cheese and dripping with maple syrup, are just too good to be true. Seriously, they might be illegal. Don't nark on me!
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                1 large sweet potato
                <br/>
                1 1/3 cup flour
                <br/>
                1 1/2 cup milk
                <br/>
                2 eggs
                <br/>
                3 tablespoons brown sugar
                <br/>
                2 teaspoons baking powder
                <br/>
                1/2 teaspoon salt
                <br/>
                1/2 cup ricotta cheese
                <br/>
                Warmed maple syrup
                <br/>
                Pecans, chopped
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Peel the sweet potato.
                <br/>
                Wrap it in tinfoil and bake it for about an hour at 400 degrees in a pre-heated oven.
                <br/>
                Add the flour, salt and baking powder to a sieve and sift it into a large mixing bowl.
                <br/>
                Add the brown sugar.
                <br/>
                Crack and separate the two eggs.
                <br/>
                Whisk the egg yolks with the milk.
                <br/>
                Remove the sweet potato from the oven when done and rice it. You can also mash with a potato masher or a fork.
                <br/>
                Add the riced sweet potato to the dry mixture, mixing it well.
                <br/>
                Work in the whisked egg and milk mixture, until completely combined.
                <br/>
                Beat the egg whites until they are fluffy and stiff.
                <br/>
                Fold the egg whites into the sweet potato mixture, do not overly mix it.
                <br/>
                In a buttered skillet, over a medium heat, ladle in the sweet potato pancake mixture.
                <br/>
                When bubbles form on top and the edges look crispy, flip the cakes.
                <br/>
                Why these are cooking, whisk the ricotta cheese in a small bowl, giving it a creamier texture and making it easier to spread.
                <br/>
                When the pancakes are done, layer with ricotta and top with maple syrup and diced pecans.
               </p>
