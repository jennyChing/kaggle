       <p>
        <strong>
         Easyshed have put together the 15 best DIY Tips ever to help you get deal with difficult tasks easier.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        1. To avoid getting paint on the floor- glue a paper plate to the bottom of the paint can- the plate can then catch drips
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.beastsheds.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/bees-wax-polish.jpg">
         <img alt="bees-wax-polish" class="alignnone wp-image-2842" height="315" src="http://www.beastsheds.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/bees-wax-polish-300x300.jpg" width="315"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.tv3.co.nz/The-15-Best-DIY-Tips/tabid/4173/articleID/77792/Default.aspx" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
         Source
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        2. To cover any scratches on wood furniture or floorboards- make a thick paste of instant coffee and a little water. Rub it into the nicks and scratches. Allow to dry and then apply a layer of furniture polish, shoe polish is an excellent alternative. Works a treat!
       </p>
       <p>
        3. Fix any squeaky doors by sprinkling talcum powder on the hinges- this is great for squeaky floorboards too!
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.beastsheds.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/paint-types.jpg">
         <img alt="paint-types" class="alignnone wp-image-2851" height="294" src="http://www.beastsheds.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/paint-types-300x277.jpg" width="317"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.tv3.co.nz/The-15-Best-DIY-Tips/tabid/4173/articleID/77792/Default.aspx" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
         Source
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        4. To keep paint looking fresh, you need four tablespoons of methylated spirits, then seal and store. Do not stir until using the paint again.
       </p>
       <p>
        5. Use dental floss to quiet a dripping tap. Tie on end of the floss around the tap and the other end of the dental floss down the drain. The drips travel along the thread instead of into the sink.
       </p>
       <p>
        6. Make threading a needle easier by squirting the thread with hairspray to make it stiff.
       </p>
       <p>
        7. To remove all paint stains  soften with linseed oil and wipe off. For tough stains, use a razor blade and scrape off.
       </p>
       <p>
        8. To stop rugs from slipping, use double sided tape
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.beastsheds.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Cutting-Fabric-Stripes1.jpg">
         <img alt="Cutting-Fabric-Stripes1" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2844" height="228" src="http://www.beastsheds.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Cutting-Fabric-Stripes1-300x228.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.tv3.co.nz/The-15-Best-DIY-Tips/tabid/4173/articleID/77792/Default.aspx" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
         Source
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        9. Dip scissors in boiling water to make it easier to cut through delicate fabrics
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.beastsheds.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/670px-MixVanilla-Step-2.jpg">
         <img alt="670px-MixVanilla-Step-2" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2840" height="200" src="http://www.beastsheds.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/670px-MixVanilla-Step-2-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.tv3.co.nz/The-15-Best-DIY-Tips/tabid/4173/articleID/77792/Default.aspx" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
         Source
        </a>

       <p>
