     <p>
      <b>
       Emmett Lathrop
      </b>
      "
      <b>
       Doc
      </b>
      "
      <b>
       Brown
      </b>
      ,
      <b>
       Ph.D.
      </b>
      , is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">
       fictional character
      </a>
      in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Future_(franchise)" title="Back to the Future (franchise)">
        Back to the Future trilogy
       </a>
      </i>
      . He is the inventor of the first
      <a href="/wiki/DeLorean_time_machine" title="DeLorean time machine">
       time machine
      </a>
      , which he builds out of a
      <a href="/wiki/DeLorean_DMC-12" title="DeLorean DMC-12">
       DeLorean
      </a>
      sports car. The character is portrayed by
      <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Lloyd" title="Christopher Lloyd">
       Christopher Lloyd
      </a>
      in all three films, as well as in the live action sequences of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Future:_the_Animated_Series" title="Back to the Future: the Animated Series">
       animated series
      </a>
      . He is voiced by
      <a href="/wiki/Dan_Castellaneta" title="Dan Castellaneta">
       Dan Castellaneta
      </a>
      in the animated series. The character's appearance and mannerisms are loosely inspired by
      <a href="/wiki/Leopold_Stokowski" title="Leopold Stokowski">
       Leopold Stokowski
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">
       Albert Einstein
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-q.26a_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-q.26a-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2008, Dr. Emmett Brown was selected by
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empire_Magazine" title="Empire Magazine">
        Empire
       </a>
      </i>
      magazine as one of
      <i>
       The 100 Greatest Movie Characters of All Time
      </i>
      , ranking at #76.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Character_background">
       Character background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Emmett_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Character background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Emmett refers to himself as "a student of all sciences" and is depicted as a passionate inventor. His homes in 1985, 1955, and 1885 are shown to contain various labor-saving gadgets, and he tests an intended mind-reading device on
      <a href="/wiki/Marty_McFly" title="Marty McFly">
       Marty McFly
      </a>
      , during the scene in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Future" title="Back to the Future">
        Back to the Future
       </a>
      </i>
      when the latter visits him in 1955.
     </p>
     <p>
      He appears to be heavily influenced by scientists of previous eras, naming successive pet dogs
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copernicus_(Back_to_the_Future)" title="Copernicus (Back to the Future)">
       Copernicus
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Einstein_(Back_to_the_Future)" title="Einstein (Back to the Future)">
       Einstein
      </a>
      , and having portraits of
      <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Newton" title="Isaac Newton">
       Isaac Newton
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">
       Benjamin Franklin
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Edison" title="Thomas Edison">
       Thomas Edison
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">
       Albert Einstein
      </a>
      in his laboratory. He also states in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Future_Part_III" title="Back to the Future Part III">
        Back to the Future Part III
       </a>
      </i>
      that his favorite author is
      <a href="/wiki/Jules_Verne" title="Jules Verne">
       Jules Verne
      </a>
      , and reveals to Marty that his family name was originally von Braun before
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">
       World War I
      </a>
      , most likely an allusion to the real-life
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rocket_scientist" title="Rocket scientist">
       rocket scientist
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Wernher_von_Braun" title="Wernher von Braun">
       Wernher von Braun
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Emmett's family moved to Hill Valley, the fictional setting of the series, in 1908. Although initially wealthy because of his family's savings, Emmett states in
      <i>
       Back to the Future
      </i>
      that he spent his "entire family fortune" on his time travel project. Newspaper clippings at the beginning of the film show the Brown mansion was destroyed by fire in 1962, and the property sold to developers; Doc subsequently resided in the mansion's garage. Once broke, Doc established a privately owned business to offer 24-hour scientific services, building ingenious devices for his customers.
     </p>
     <p>
      Emmett's work appears to be highly regarded; a scene from
      <i>
       Back to the Future Part II
      </i>
      shows a newspaper article describing his winning an award for his work. However, he is shown as
      <a href="/wiki/Absent-minded_professor" title="Absent-minded professor">
       absent-minded
      </a>
      at times, and various statements by other characters inhabiting Hill Valley indicate that he is generally regarded as strange, eccentric, or insane. He often speaks with wide-eyed expressions and broad gestures"
      <a href="/wiki/Great_Scott" title="Great Scott">
       Great Scott
      </a>
      !" being one of the character's well-known catchphrasesand tends to be
      <a href="/wiki/Verbosity" title="Verbosity">
       overly verbose
      </a>
      in his delivery, referring in one case to a school dance as a "rhythmic ceremonial ritual".
     </p>
     <p>
      No film in the trilogy shows Emmett having any friends besides Marty and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jennifer_Parker" title="Jennifer Parker">
       Jennifer
      </a>
      , Marty's girlfriend. The films do not depict how Doc and Marty originally met, but production notes and comments by franchise creators
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bob_Zemeckis" title="Bob Zemeckis">
       Bob Zemeckis
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Bob_Gale" title="Bob Gale">
       Bob Gale
      </a>
      have stated that Doc and Marty met several years prior to the events of
      <i>
       Back to the Future
      </i>
      , when Marty sneaked into Doc's lab after being warned by his parents to stay away from him. Happy to be revered as 'cool', Doc hired Marty as his part-time lab assistant.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the original timeline, in contrast with Marty's unassertive father, George McFly, Doc is an encouraging and supportive mentor figure for Marty. Doc's positive influence in turn allows Marty to mentor George in 1955, which appears to encourage his later success as a novelist, and help him become a better husband and father. One line in particular, "If you put your mind to it, you can accomplish anything," originates from Doc. Marty repeats it to George in 1955, who repeats it back to Marty in the "improved" 1985 shown at the end of
      <i>
       Back to the Future.
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Back to the Future
      </i>
      alludes to Doc being involved with illegal and criminal enterprisesalbeit as a means to obtain items for his inventions he could not purchase legallybut he is nave and flippant about the consequences of his actions. In an early scene, he excitedly tells Marty how he cheated
      <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">
       Libyan
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">
       terrorists
      </a>
      out of stolen
      <a href="/wiki/Plutonium" title="Plutonium">
       plutonium
      </a>
      : "they wanted me to build them a bomb, so I took their plutonium and, in turn, gave them a shoddy bomb casing full of used
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinball_machine" title="Pinball machine">
       pinball machine
      </a>
      parts!"
     </p>
     <p>
      Doc Brown begins the trilogy somewhat innocent, and very enthusiastic about the possible uses of his time machine, initially actively trying to alter the past or future of the principal characters to improve their lives. However, events throughout the story, particularly in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Future_Part_II" title="Back to the Future Part II">
        Back to the Future Part II
       </a>
      </i>
      , lead him to conclude that time travel is too hazardous for humankind, and that the time machine should be destroyed. His conviction is strengthened in
      <i>
       Back to the Future Part III
      </i>
      , when he realizes that he has unwittingly altered history by preventing the death of Clara Clayton in 1885; he concludes that the time machine has "caused nothing but disaster."
     </p>
     <p>
      However, after having been left behind in 1885 when Marty departs in the DeLorean for 1985, Doc starts a family with Clara. He eventually creates another time machine and builds it into a steam locomotive, which he uses to return to 1985 with the intent of collecting his dog, Einstein. It is clear that he traveled to an unspecified point further in the future, as his locomotive time machine is shown at the end of
      <i>
       Back to the Future Part III
      </i>
      with a "hover conversion" akin to that of the DeLorean at the end of the first movie. The trilogy ends with Doc and his family departing 1985 to an unspecified destination that is not in the future, as seen from 1985.
     </p>

     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Future" title="Back to the Future">
       Back to the Future
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Early in
      <i>
       Back to the Future
      </i>
      Doc describes his inspiration for the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flux_capacitor" title="Flux capacitor">
       flux capacitor
      </a>
      (the essential component of time travel in the series) as a "vision" which comes to him on November 5, 1955, after he slipped off his toilet while standing on it to hang a clock and bumping his head on the sink. In relaying this story, Doc states that it took his "entire family fortune" to realize this vision, the time machine he installed into a DeLorean DMC-12 sports car. He is shot by a group of
      <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">
       Libyan
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terrorists" title="Terrorists">
       terrorists
      </a>
      early in
      <i>
       Back to the Future
      </i>
      , apparently in retaliation for stealing plutonium from them to fuel the time machine.
     </p>
     <p>
      Marty, during his accidental excursion to 1955, makes contact with Doc's younger self to try and repair the damaged DeLorean to allow him to return home, the two eventually deciding to use a 'scheduled' lightning strike to the courthouse clock as a substitute power source. While in the past, Marty repeatedly tries to inform Doc about the mortal risk he faces in 1985, but Doc refuses to accept any information about the future, saying Marty's good intentions could "backfire drastically." A later scene shows Marty secretly writing and leaving Doc a letter describing the latter's shooting on October 26, 1985. Doc discovers and tears up the letter just before successfully sending Marty back to 1985. Doc evidently later decides to use the information, as he is found by Marty, when the latter returns to 1985, to have worn a
      <a href="/wiki/Bulletproof_vest" title="Bulletproof vest">
       bulletproof vest
      </a>
      on the night of the time machine test, which allows him to survive being shot. He subsequently produces Marty's warning letter, taped back together, informing Marty that, despite his protests about the warnings of future information, he eventually figured "What the Hell?".
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Back to the Future
      </i>
      ends with Doc, having traveled alone 30 years into the future, picking up Marty and Jennifer in a now-flying DeLorean to take them "back to the future" because "something's got to be done about [their] kids."
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Back_to_the_Future_Part_II">
       <i>
        Back to the Future Part II
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Emmett_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Back to the Future Part II">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Future_Part_II" title="Back to the Future Part II">
       Back to the Future Part II
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The first act of
      <i>
       Back to the Future Part II
      </i>
      is set in the Hill Valley of 2015. It comprises Doc and Marty's efforts to prevent Marty's son,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_McFly_Jr." title="Martin McFly Jr.">
       Marty Jr
      </a>
      , from going to jail for participating in a robbery, an event Doc states he learned of after going "further ahead into time," during his solo, unseen, excursions into the future. During these excursions, Doc underwent a "rejuvenation treatment", resulting in him looking like his younger self, discarding the make-up he initially used to appear the same age as when he left.
     </p>
     <p>
      Although Doc and Marty successfully prevent the crime from taking place, Jennifer, who has accompanied the pair to 2015, happens through coincidence to encounter her 2015 self. Although Jennifer simply passes out in shock, Doc also theorized that the event could "create a time
      <a href="/wiki/Paradox" title="Paradox">
       paradox
      </a>
      ...and destroy the entire universe." Doc now appears convinced that time travel is profoundly hazardous, and he announces his intention to destroy the time machine after returning to 1985.
     </p>
     <p>
      However, an earlier scene, in which Doc discovers Marty has purchased a sports almanac to win bets in the past, is overheard by the now-elderly
      <a href="/wiki/Biff_Tannen" title="Biff Tannen">
       Biff Tannen
      </a>
      . After Doc throws away the almanac, Biff recovers it, and is able to steal the DeLorean while it is unattended. He subsequently goes back in time to 1955, gives the Almanac to his 1955 self, and returns the DeLorean to 2015.
     </p>
     <p>
      When Doc, Marty, and Jennifer return to 1985, they find a drastically altered Hill Valley, which has been completely subjugated by Biff. A fictional newspaper article shows
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">
       Richard Nixon
      </a>
      is still in office, and the
      <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">
       Vietnam War
      </a>
      is still being fought. In a later scene, Doc, in his now-abandoned garage, produces a 1983 newspaper article describing his committal to an insane asylum.
     </p>
     <p>
      After ascertaining from the contemporary Biff when "Old" Biff gave him the almanac, Doc and Marty travel back to 1955 to retrieve and destroy it. Although the pair are eventually successful, the DeLorean is struck by lightning while being piloted by Doc, and disappears. The scene ends with
      <a href="/wiki/Western_Union" title="Western Union">
       Western Union
      </a>
      delivering a letter from Doc to the now-stranded Marty. Doc's letter assures Marty he is alive and living in the year 1885.
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       Back to the Future Part II
      </i>
      ends with Marty returning to the 1955 Doc- who has just sent Marty back to 1985, as depicted in
      <i>
       Back to the Future
      </i>
      - for help. The latter promptly faints in shock.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Back_to_the_Future_Part_III">
       <i>
        Back to the Future Part III
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Emmett_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Back to the Future Part III">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Future_Part_III" title="Back to the Future Part III">
       Back to the Future Part III
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Marty convinces the 1955 Doc that he has returned by showing him the letter he received from the 1885 Doc. Doc describes where the disabled DeLorean is hidden, and instructs Marty to return directly to 1985, to avoid "unnecessary time travel". However, the accidental discovery of the 1885 Doc's tombstone reveals that Doc was shot days after he wrote the letter, which convinces Marty and the 1955 Doc that Marty must attempt to rescue him.
     </p>
     <p>
      Marty travels to September 2, 1885 and locates Doc, but the DeLorean is damaged on arrival, rendering it unable to move under its own power. While planning their return to 1985, Doc and Marty observe a woman on a wagon being pulled by out-of-control horses. Doc plucks the woman from the wagon before it falls into a ravine, and she introduces herself as Clara Clayton. Doc later realizes that the ravine is called 'Clayton Ravine' in 1985, and surmises that Clara was "supposed" to die there. He gravely states that he may have "seriously altered history" by rescuing her, and regrets ever inventing the time machine.
     </p>
     <p>
      Nonetheless, Doc and Clara share a strong attraction, and to Marty's despair, they begin a relationship. Their happy scene together at the Hill Valley Festival is interrupted by
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Back_to_the_Future_characters#Buford_.22Mad_Dog.22_Tannen" title="List of Back to the Future characters">
       Buford Tannen
      </a>
      , who Marty narrowly prevents from shooting Doc in a dispute over money. Doc later takes Clara home, and the pair share their mutual love for Jules Verne, and their first kiss.
     </p>
     <p>
      Despite Doc's wish to remain in 1885 with Clara, Marty convinces him that they must return to 1985. In the scene in which Doc explains to Clara why he is leaving, she reacts angrily, believing Doc has lied to her, and rejects him. The following morning Marty finds Doc in the local saloon, where he has spent the night nursing a glass of whiskey and lamenting his loss, and emphatically insists they return to 1985 as planned. Doc agrees, downs his whiskey in an act of finality, and immediately collapses. Marty and the bartender use a concoction called "
      <a href="/wiki/Prairie_oyster_(cocktail)" title="Prairie oyster (cocktail)">
       wake-up juice
      </a>
      " to revive Doc, but by then Buford has arrived for a showdown with Marty, and the scene ends with Doc captured.
     </p>
     <p>
      Meanwhile, the train the pair plan to use is departing with Clara on board, who is abandoning Hill Valley in grief. In a scene aboard the train she overhears two passengers discussing a man's heartbreak and, after establishing they are talking about Emmett, stops the train and returns to Hill Valley. In Doc's workshop, Clara discovers the model train he had built to illustrate the plan to return to 1985, and realizes Doc was telling the truth.
     </p>
     <p>
      Meanwhile, Marty resolves his showdown with Buford without bloodshed, and departs with Doc. After the two intercept and hijack the locomotive, Clara catches up to the train on horseback, having deduced their plan. Doc quickly decides to take her to 1985 with them, since the train cannot now be halted before reaching the unfinished bridge at the end of the track. Clara slips and falls while trying to traverse the locomotive to reach Doc. Doc rescues her, but they are too late to get back to the DeLorean, which reaches 88mph and departs with Marty. After emerging in 1985, the DeLorean is almost immediately destroyed in a head-on collision with a diesel locomotive, which Marty narrowly escapes.
     </p>
     <p>
      In a later scene, Marty surveys the remains with Jennifer, sadly observing that Doc can never return. The pair are surprised by Doc's reappearance in a time machine built into a steam locomotive, having returned to pick up his dog, Einstein. He is now married to Clara, and they happily introduce their two sons, Jules and Verne. In an optimistic parting speech, Doc tells Marty and Jennifer that their future "hasn't been written yet," and that they should "make it a good one."
      <i>
       Back to the Future Part III
      </i>
      ends with the train taking off, and the Brown family departing for times unknown.
     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Doctor" title="Fourth Doctor">
       Fourth
      </a>
      incarnations of the titular
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_(Doctor_Who)" title="Doctor (Doctor Who)">
       Doctor
      </a>
      (portrayed by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nice_Peter" title="Nice Peter">
       Nice Peter
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/George_Watsky" title="George Watsky">
       George Watsky
      </a>
      ) from the science fiction TV series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">
        Doctor Who
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Christopher Lloyd reprises the role for a cameo in
      <a href="/wiki/Seth_MacFarlane" title="Seth MacFarlane">
       Seth MacFarlane
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Million_Ways_to_Die_in_the_West" title="A Million Ways to Die in the West">
        A Million Ways to Die in the West
       </a>
      </i>
      when Albert Stark (MacFarlane) finds Doc Brown trying to hide the DeLorean in a building where Albert sees a strange light.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This film takes place in 1882 and is inconsistent with the Back To The Future timeline, which places Doc in the west during 1885 and later.
     </p>
     <p>
      In December 2014, an Italian theatre group, the "Officina degli Attori", uses "Doc. Emmett Brown" and his DeLorean as a Spirit of Christmas Past in a musical adaptation of "A Christmas Carol" inspired by Charles Dickens' Masterpiece for charity purposes. During the theatral piece, he travels alone from 1985 to 1845, and together with Ebenezer Scrooge from 1845 to 1775 and later to 1790.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>

          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
