       <h1 class="title entry-title">
        Everybody Hurts: How NOT To Deal With Rejection.
       </h1>
       <div class="content clear">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         <i>
          You have to build a brand!
         </i>
         they keep telling me.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Blogging isnt about what you do  its about the personality you convey.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Youre not selling your stories. Youre selling YOU.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          When I started Domestiphobia nearly 5 years ago, I didnt realize I was entering a life of prostitution.
         </strong>
         <span id="more-15868">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         And I imagine thats how it happens with some people. You try something new, make yourself a little vulnerable, get drawn into the allure of a glamorous lifestyle, and before you know it, youre dancing on a pool table and doing body shots off some guys hairy stomach, hoping desperately for someone to notice you.
        </p>
        <p>
         And look. Ive finally realized 
        </p>
        <p>
         I cant sell me.
        </p>
        <p>
         I mean, look what were working with, here:
        </p>
        <figure class="alignnone full-width-mobile thin size-full wp-image-13349" id="attachment_13349">
         <img alt="AwkwardTween-web" height="423" src="http://www.domestiphobia.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AwkwardTween-web.jpg" width="700"/>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Sure, this was circa 1995 or whatever, but I really havent changed much since then.
        </p>
        <figure class="alignnone full-width-mobile thin size-full wp-image-13355" id="attachment_13355">
         <img alt="AwkwardTween3-web" height="541" src="http://www.domestiphobia.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/AwkwardTween3-web.jpg" width="282"/>
        </figure>
        <p>
         I ditched the mom jeans and somehow managed to land myself a really hot husband (the trick is getting the braces removed), but I still have horrible posture, and Im pretty sure that denim shirt is shoved somewhere in the back of my closet, just waiting for theShawshank Redemption look to make a comeback.
        </p>
        <p>
         So its time to accept the fact that
         <a href="http://www.domestiphobia.net/2014/04/24/get-knocked/" target="_blank" title="I Get Knocked Down">
          Ive never been the cool girl
         </a>
         , and Im not destined to
         <em>
          be
         </em>
         the cool girl.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          I is what I is.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The other day, my friend said something interesting to me. She was worried about whether her brilliant daughter would get acceptedinto an educational program at school, and she said, I just really hope she makes it. She doesnt deal well with rejection.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now.
        </p>
        <p>
         I love my friend. I love her daughter. I dont want to see her disappointed any more than her mother wants to see her disappointed. But it was hard for me not to laugh and say, How do you think you learn to deal with it? By experiencing it. By feeling it. By letting it hit you again and again so you can realize that while it doesnt exactly feel like a day at the spa on a Carnival cruise, its also not as bad as being told that the ships going down and there arent enough life boats.
        </p>
        <p>
         Disappointment sucks, but you can
         <em>
          always
         </em>
         recover.
        </p>
        <p>
         As a writer and general seeker of opportunities, I feel like Ive experienced the gamut of professional rejection. (Talking about
         <em>
          personal
         </em>
         rejection would require opening a whole vault of suppressed memories from the Great Denim Era pictured above, and Im not quite ready to crack the seal on that one.) Sometimes Ive dealt well, and sometimes Ive well I couldve dealt better. But each and every time, I learned a little bit more about myself. About what I can take. About how it helps  and hurts  to react.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h1>
         How NOT to deal with rejection:
        </h1>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Dont treat disappointment with depressants.
          </strong>
          Guess what? Alcohol is a depressant. One time I made the mistake of reading a rejection email on my phone
          <em>
           while
          </em>
          I was drinking socially with friends, and it was like my brain was telling me that the wine was Novocaine for my soul  I just needed more of it to get things good and numb. This proves that inebriated brains cant be trusted, and I also have a horrible tendency toself-psychoanalyze with alternative music from the 90s.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Dont act like the insecure ex.
          </strong>
          Listen. Breakups are tough. But 99.9% of the time, a person isnt going to do it unless he
          <em>
           really
          </em>
          wants to do it. So no amount of but whhhyyyyys and are you suuuures are going to change his mind. If anything, it only strengthens the breaker uppers resolve that the breaker upee has issues. Just like personal relationship breakups, the only shot you have at getting back together after a professional breakup isby giving them the space to realize what theyre missing. Of course, that might not actually happen, but the idea is to actually give
          <em>
           yourself
          </em>
          the time to realize that you dont really need that relationship to survive.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Dont let it affect your overall self-esteem.
          </strong>
          Every time I deal with rejection, I visualize another coat of wax on my already rain-resistant finish. Those hard little pellets of pain? Just beads of water, baby. They slide right off. (Not really, of course, but this is what I envision when Im balled up on the floor doing the ugly cry.) Really, denial is key. Or, if youre too smart for denial, try being
          <a href="http://www.domestiphobia.net/2013/08/07/unsmotable/" target="_blank" title="Unsmotable">
           unsmotable
          </a>
          . Whatever helps you understand that the disappointment is merely a moment with which you have to deal  not a permanent state of mind.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <figure class="alignnone full-width-mobile thin size-full wp-image-12907" id="attachment_12907">
         <img alt="Chance" height="448" src="http://www.domestiphobia.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chance.jpg" width="720"/>
        </figure>
        <p>
         That girl in the denim was
         <em>
          really
         </em>
         good at bouncing back from rejection, and Id like to say shes gotten better with age.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its because I finally realized 
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The only real way to move past thehurt ofdisappointment from rejection is to accept who I am, accept when that doesnt work for someone, and then? Move on.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Professionally, I still need to pitch. To make myself vulnerable. To continue to improve, show what I can do, and find out where I fit. But theres a difference between showcasing and selling. One involves an apology-free spotlight  you get what you get  and the other reeks of desperation and used cars.
        </p>
        <p>
         It took a couple of decades, but the girl in the denim is finally figuring it out.
        </p>
        <figure class="alignnone full-width-mobile thin size-full wp-image-1328" id="attachment_1328">
         <img alt="mismatched shoes" height="1633" src="http://www.domestiphobia.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/1289620-small.jpg" width="2449"/>
        </figure>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Its only when you stop trying to sell that it ceases to matter if anyones buying.
         </strong>
        </p>

       <div class="content">
        <p>
         I was a giant nerd growing up.  Never had the latest clothes, always had my nose in a book.  I was usually ahead in classes (because of moving around not from being brilliant or anything, although I was an A student) so most devastatingly awkward was my senior year since all my friends graduated a year ahead of me.  I have never been a glamor or remotely cool, but sometime in my 30s I realized I didnt give a f*ck! I love the opening about blogging being prostitution, because it so true, or at least it is an addiction.  You want to feel that high? Youve got to do more.  Need another hit? Youve got to do more.  Starting last year I was just over it and I am still in that mode.  I am doing things the way I want to do it and if it works out so be it, if not, well then there will be a small set of friends who have supported me and probably aint going anywhere!
