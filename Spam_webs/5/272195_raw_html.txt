        <p>
         Lets cut to the chase: fictional detectives love their booze. And after a long night of dodging hoods and busting crooks, who wouldnt want a stiff drink?
        </p>
        <p>
         Thankfully, you dont have to hit the streets to drink with the best of them. So don that tan trench coat and crack open the liquor cabinet. Here are a few of our favorite detective cocktail recipes to put you in the sleuthing mood.
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h4>
         1. Philip Marlowes Gimlet
        </h4>
        <p>
         Raymond Chandlers quick-talking gumshoe Philip Marlowe drinks down this cocktail in the opening scenes of
         <i>
          <a href="http://amzn.to/1yNi2aK" rel="nofollow" target="new">
           The Long Goodbye
          </a>
         </i>
         , alongside a shifty barfly by the name of Terry Lennox. While Lennox clearly spells trouble, Marlowe stays for another round, as the Gimlet goes down like gangbusters.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Instructions
        </h4>
        <p>
          2 oz gin
         <br/>
          1 oz lime juice
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Pour gin and lime juice in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir well and strain into a chilled old-fashioned glass. Garnish with lime.
         </em>
        </p>
        <h4>
         2. Nick and Nora Charles Bronx Cocktail
        </h4>
        <p>
         Dashiell Hammetts lush crime solving sweethearts from New York City in
         <i>
          <a href="http://amzn.to/1uhmxUE" rel="nofollow" target="new">
           The Thin Man
          </a>
         </i>
         share a love for snappy dialogue and copious amounts of alcohol. This speakeasy cocktail is the perfect compliment to a mysterious night out on the town.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Instructions
        </h4>
        <p>
          1 1/2 oz gin
         <br/>
          1/2 oz sweet vermouth
         <br/>
          1/2 oz dry vermouth
         <br/>
          1 oz orange juice
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Combine ingredients in a shaker. Shake well and strain into a cocktail glass. Garnish with an orange peel.
         </em>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <a href="http://the-lineup-new.openroadintegrat.netdna-cdn.com/app/uploads/2014/12/bogartcroppedweb.jpg">
          <img alt="humphrey bogart maltese falcon" class="image" src="http://the-lineup-new.openroadintegrat.netdna-cdn.com/app/uploads/2014/12/bogartcroppedweb.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
        </h4>
        <h4>
         3. Sam Spades A Reasonable Amount of Trouble
        </h4>
        <p>
         Hammetts second creation, the moody San Francisco sleuth Sam Spade from
         <i>
          <a href="http://amzn.to/1uhmCaU" rel="nofollow" target="new">
           The Maltese Falcon
          </a>
         </i>
         , is the exact opposite of Nick and Nora; he is gruff, rough, and an absolute loner. Nevertheless, this icy PI shares one thing in common with the fun-loving couple  a definite love of drink. Nashvilles
         <a href="http://thepattersonnashville.com/" target="_blank">
          Patterson House
         </a>
         created a cocktail in honor of beloved gumshoe.
         <em>
          [via
         </em>
         <a href="http://pursuitmag.com/pursuit-vice-sam-spade/" target="_blank">
          Pursuit Magazine
         </a>
         <em>
          ]
         </em>
        </p>
        <h4>
         Instructions
        </h4>
        <p>
          2 oz spiced rum
         <br/>
          1/4 oz Lyles golden syrup
         <br/>
          7 drops lime bitters
         <br/>
          1 dash Peychauds Bitters
         <br/>
          Mezcal-rinsed glass
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Combine Lyles, rum, and lime bitters in a mixing glass with ice. Stir and strain over ice-filled glass that has been rinsed with Creme de Mezcal. Add a dash of Peychauds and zest with orange peel.
         </em>
        </p>
        <h4>
         4. James Bonds Vesper Martini
        </h4>
        <p>
         James Bond is no stranger to martinis and this truly unique mixed drink is the best of the bunch. Bond creates the cocktail on the spot in
         <i>
          <a href="http://amzn.to/12p63mZ" rel="nofollow" target="new">
           Casino Royale
          </a>
         </i>
         , Ian Flemings first installment in the beloved spy series. The drink is named after 007s love interest in the novel, the alluring Vesper Lynd.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Instructions
        </h4>
        <p>
          1/2 oz Lillet blanc
         <br/>
          3 oz gin
         <br/>
          1 oz vodka
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Combine ingredients in an ice-filled shaker. Shake and strain into a cocktail glass. Garnish with a twist of lemon. Makes 2 martinis.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://the-lineup-new.openroadintegrat.netdna-cdn.com/app/uploads/2014/12/Rust_Cohlecroppedweb.jpg">
          <img alt="rust cohle true detective" class="image" src="http://the-lineup-new.openroadintegrat.netdna-cdn.com/app/uploads/2014/12/Rust_Cohlecroppedweb.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h4>
         5. Rust Cohles Rust-y Nail
        </h4>
        <p>
         Fans of
         <i>
          <a href="http://amzn.to/1uhmIzj" rel="nofollow" target="new">
           True Detective
          </a>
         </i>
         know that Rust Cohle is no stranger to the bottle. And while theres no question that the solitary detective would prefer a six pack of Lone Star over some extravagant cocktail, this sweet and smoky Bourbon street treat from
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.bkmag.com/2014/03/07/true-detective-specific-cocktails-what-to-drink-on-sunday/" target="_blank">
           Brooklyn Magazine
          </a>
         </em>
         would surely catch his eye.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Instructions
        </h4>
        <p>
          1 1/2 oz bourbon
         <br/>
          3/4 oz Drambuie
         <br/>
          Lemon twist for garnish
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Pour bourbon and Drambuie into an old-fashioned glass filled with ice. Stir well. Garnish with the lemon twist.
         </em>
        </p>
        <h4>
         6. Kurt Wallanders Irish coffee
        </h4>
        <p>
         Henning Mankells brooding Swedish detective can often be found contemplating his case with a glass of straight whiskey and an opera record on repeat. Nevertheless, we feel a warm and caffeinated cocktail would do wonders for the obsessive detective  especially on those long and lonely Scandinavian nights in
         <i>
          <a href="http://amzn.to/1BxbgrU" rel="nofollow" target="new">
           Faceless Killers
          </a>
         </i>
         .
        </p>
        <h4>
         Instructions
        </h4>
        <p>
          1 cup freshly brewed hot coffee
         <br/>
          1 tbsp brown sugar
         <br/>
          1 1/2 oz whiskey
         <br/>
          Heavy cream, slightly whipped
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Pour hot coffee into a warmed mug. Add brown sugar and stir until completely dissolved. Blend in whiskey. Top with whipped heavy cream by pouring over the back of a spoon.
         </em>
        </p>
        <h4>
         7. Inspector Jules Maigrets The Corpse Reviver #2
        </h4>
        <p>
         Georges Simenons persistent Inspector Magritte from
         <i>
          <a href="http://amzn.to/1z3Nn7O" rel="nofollow" target="new">
           Dirty Snow
          </a>
         </i>
         always seems to find time for a quick bite and fine drink at a French cafe while cracking each case. This smooth cognac cocktail is the perfect treat for a determined detective with a highly refined palate.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Instructions
        </h4>
        <p>
          1 oz gin
         <br/>
          1 oz Cointreau orange liqueur
         <br/>
          1 oz Lillet blanc
         <br/>
          1 dash of absinthe
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Combine ingredients in an ice-filled shaker. Shake well and strain into a chilled cocktail glass.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://the-lineup-new.openroadintegrat.netdna-cdn.com/app/uploads/2014/10/william-gillette-sitting.jpg">
          <img alt="sherlock holmes" class="image" src="http://the-lineup-new.openroadintegrat.netdna-cdn.com/app/uploads/2014/10/william-gillette-sitting.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h4>
         8. Sherlock Holmess Chilled Black Tea Toddy
        </h4>
        <p>
         Many are astonished to discover Sherlock Holmes complicated history with substance abuse  cocaine and morphine use appears throughout Sir Arthur Conan Doyles
         <i>
          <a href="http://amzn.to/1uhnlJb" rel="nofollow" target="new">
           The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes
          </a>
         </i>
         . Thankfully, the brilliant British detective had a taste for far less illicit vices as well. This smoky tea-infused cocktail is the perfect to sip while contemplating the mysteries of the foggy city streets.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Instructions
        </h4>
        <p>
          1 1/2 oz single-malt Scotch
         <br/>
          2 oz chilled brewed Lapsang souchong tea
         <br/>
          1 oz honey syrup
         <br/>
          3/4 oz fresh lemon juice
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Pour ingredients into a mixing glass. Stir well and strain into an ice-filled glass.
         </em>
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
