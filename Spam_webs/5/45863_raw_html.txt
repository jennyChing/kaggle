      <h1 class="entry-title article-title">
       Creating The Perfect Sleeping Environment
      </h1>
      <section class="post-info">
       <img class="avatar-image" src="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/20710_009-GUY-MEDOWS-SCREEN-GRAB-e1415794487644.jpg"/>
       <span class="author">
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/author/dr-guy-meadows/" rel="author" title="Posts by Dr Guy Meadows">
         Dr Guy Meadows
        </a>
       </span>
       <span class="date updated">
        December 29, 2014
       </span>
       <span class="views">
        Views
        <strong>
         5330
        </strong>
       </span>
      </section>
      <img class="featured-image" src="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/sleeping-environment.jpg"/>
      <div class="content-wrap clear">
       <p>
        Your body has billions of tiny sensors that detect things like comfort, light, noise, and smell, and feed this information back to your brain constantly. Its good to understand this before you start trying to create your perfect bedroom environment.
       </p>
       <p>
        Thats because the more sensory information your brain receives, the more awake it becomes. So when you are trying to get to sleep, you want your bedroom to give off as little sensory stimulation as possible.
       </p>
       <p>
        The aim of this blog is help you create the ideal sleeping environment. One that helps you fall asleep easily, and promotes a great nights sleep.
       </p>
       <h3>
        <strong>
         1. Choosing the perfect bed
        </strong>
       </h3>
       <p>
        You will spend one third of your life asleep in bed, and naturally youll want that to be the best sleep possible. That means making sure your bed provides the right level of comfort.
       </p>
       <p>
        Everybodys idea of the perfect bed is different. For some, only a
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/mattresses#/comfort_rating/1">
         super-soft mattress
        </a>
        will do. For others it has to be
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/mattresses#/comfort_rating/5">
         extra-firm
        </a>
        , or the comfort only an
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/mattresses#/brand/sealy">
         orthopaedic mattress
        </a>
        can offer.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you share a bed, youll both want enough space to move around without waking each other up. That may mean joining two single mattresses together. Or how about a mattress that regulates your
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/catalogsearch/result/?q=igel">
         temperature
        </a>
        while you sleep, preventing you overheating or getting too cold?
       </p>
       <p>
        If youre unsure where to start, we recommend heading to your nearest Bensons for Beds
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/store-finder/">
         store
        </a>
        . There youll find our unique
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/comfort-station">
         Comfort Stations
        </a>
        , where you can try out a range of beds and
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/mattresses">
         mattresses,
        </a>
        to help find the right one for you. Otherwise you can always give us a call  our experienced and friendly team is always here to help. Or visit our website where youll find a video which explains the whole process, step by step.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Comfort Station with Bensons for Beds" class="aligncenter wp-image-742 size-medium" height="127" src="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/comfort-station-image-250x127.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0 auto;" width="250"/>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         2. Pitch black is best
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Ever wondered how your brain knows when to fall asleep? Its actually quite simple. Each day our planet cycles between light and dark. Our eyes are sensitive to this change and send signals to your brain telling it whether to sleep or be awake.
       </p>
       <p>
        Light stimulates the release of Cortisol, a hormone responsible for waking you up. When it gets dark your internal body clock triggers the release of the sleep-promoting hormone Melatonin, which prepares you for sleep.
       </p>
       <p>
        This is why you sleep better in a dark room, as opposed to one thats brightly lit.
       </p>
       <p>
        Unfortunately, in our modern world, light pollution is on the rise, even in the bedroom. Alarm clocks, phones, TVs, radios, baby monitors all emit small amounts of light, known as blue light, and even small amounts of light can disturb your sleep. If you live in a town or city, you may have the added problem of streetlights flooding your bedroom with orange light  when it really should be dark.
       </p>
       <p>
        So ridding your bedroom of any unnecessary devices and covering up the rest is a good start. You could consider installing heavy curtains or blackout blinds too, especially if the streetlights or the early morning sun disturbs your sleep.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/pitch-black-sleep.jpg">
         <img alt="A room black room helps your sleep best" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-744" src="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/pitch-black-sleep-250x126.jpg"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <strong>
         3. A silent night
        </strong>
       </h3>
       <p>
        It goes without saying that a quiet bedroom makes for better sleep. But noise pollution is also on the rise. And recent sleep research suggests that your sleeping brain is constantly listening out for familiar noises. This explains why you might be woken up by the sound of your name being called. Or why parents will often wake up to their childs cry.
       </p>
       <p>
        So if you have trouble sleeping, reducing noise levels is a useful first step. If this isnt possible, you could consider earplugs. There are many different types available, ranging from your standard foam, to custom-made wax earplugs. The key is to try a range until you find the perfect pair for you.
       </p>
       <p>
        Sometimes, of course, you may feel that theres no escape from noise pollution, for example if your partner snores badly. You may find yourself worrying about the noise before its even started, and this worry affects your sleep even more. One way to deal with this is to think about the noise objectively. Rather than listening to what your mind is saying about the noise, focus on what you can actually hear in reality  youll often find this will lessen its impact.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/silentnight-best-for-sleep.jpg">
         <img alt="A silent night is best for sleep" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-745" src="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/silentnight-best-for-sleep-250x118.jpg"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <strong>
         4. Keeping cool
        </strong>
       </h3>
       <p>
        Your body temperature plays an important role in how you sleep. A slight drop in temperature at the start of the night will help get you get to sleep, while a room thats warming up will help you wake up in the morning.
       </p>
       <p>
        During the night, youll sleep better if its cool, around 17
        <strong>
         
        </strong>
        C ideally. So consider keeping a window open and the central heating turned off. Also, try using sheets and blankets instead of
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/bedding#/group_type/duvet">
         duvets
        </a>
        . They allow you to regulate your body temperature quickly and easily.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you still find yourself too hot or too cold, you could try a
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/catalogsearch/result/?q=igel">
         mattress
        </a>
        that actually helps regulate your temperature.
       </p>
       <p>
        Intelligent Temperature Regulating
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/catalogsearch/result/?q=igel">
         iGel mattresses
        </a>
        absorb heat and store it if you are too hot, then release it back when you are too cold.
        <a href="http://http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/catalogsearch/result/?q=igel">
         iGel
        </a>
        works constantly, adjusting with your body temperature, to provide a more balanced, natural sleeping temperature through the night.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/optimal-sleep-temperature.jpg">
         <img alt="Having your bedroom cool, helps you sleep better" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-743" src="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/optimal-sleep-temperature-250x135.jpg"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <strong>
         5. Fresh air is best
        </strong>
       </h3>
       <p>
        You may not realise this, but the level of humidity or water content in the bedroom can also have a large impact on sleep quality. For example, if the air is overly dry, like when youre travelling by plane, you may find your sleep disturbed by a dry mouth. Or if the air is very wet, in tropical climates for example, you can feel too sticky to sleep.
       </p>
       <p>
        Ideally, your bedroom humidity wants to be around 50%. The simplest (and cheapest!) way to achieve this in the UK is to keep your bedroom window open. That way you keep fresh air circulating. If this is not an option for you, a humidifier or dehumidifier will help.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/bedroom-humidity.jpg">
         <img alt="Fresh Air is Best " class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-746" src="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/bedroom-humidity-250x127.jpg"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <strong>
         6. Clean and tidy bedroom
        </strong>
       </h3>
       <p>
        The state of your bedroom is a bit like the state of your mind. Whilst not scientifically proven, many people associate a clean and tidy bedroom with a better nights sleep. This makes sense because we sleep better when we are calm and relaxed. So if a tidy bedroom chills you out, that can only be a good thing! Simple habits such as making your bed each day, regularly changing your sheets, folding away clothes and keeping your room clean will all help. Investing in furniture with storage facilities will help keep your bedroom tidier too. At Bensons for Beds, we have
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/catalogsearch/result/?q=ottoman">
         ottoman beds
        </a>
        which lift up to reveal storage space; divan beds available with
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/divan-beds#/storage/2_drawers">
         two drawers
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/divan-beds#/storage/22_drawers">
         two plus two drawers
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/divan-beds#/storage/4_drawers">
         four drawers
        </a>
        ; and bed frames with
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/bed-frames#/storage/end_drawer">
         footend drawers
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/clean-and-tidy.jpg">
         <img alt="A clean and tidy bedroom " class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-747" src="http://www.bensonsforbeds.co.uk/sleep-school/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/clean-and-tidy-250x132.jpg"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Youre all set to create your perfect sleeping environment
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Sleep plays an important role in your life. It can help boost your day-to-day performance, health and wellbeing. Investing some time creating the perfect bedroom environment is one of the simplest ways you can improve the quality of your sleep. You may even be surprised by the results. Good luck!
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
