     <p>
      <b>
       Stratosphere Las Vegas
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">
       hotel
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">
       casino
      </a>
      located on
      <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Boulevard" title="Las Vegas Boulevard">
       Las Vegas Boulevard
      </a>
      just north of the
      <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Strip" title="Las Vegas Strip">
       Las Vegas Strip
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">
       Las Vegas
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">
       Nevada
      </a>
      , United States.
     </p>
     <p>
      The property's signature attraction is the 1,149ft (350.2m) Stratosphere Tower, the tallest
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_towers_in_the_world" title="List of tallest towers in the world">
       freestanding observation tower
      </a>
      in the United States,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the second tallest in the
      <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">
       Western Hemisphere
      </a>
      , surpassed only by the
      <a href="/wiki/CN_Tower" title="CN Tower">
       CN Tower
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">
       Toronto
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">
       Ontario
      </a>
      . It is the tallest building west of the Mississippi River
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and also the
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_Las_Vegas" title="List of tallest buildings in Las Vegas">
       tallest structure in Las Vegas and in the State of Nevada
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STRAT_emp_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-STRAT_emp-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The hotel is a separate building with 24 stories, 2,427 rooms and an 80,000sqft (7,400m
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ) casino. The Stratosphere is owned and operated by
      <a href="/wiki/American_Casino_%26_Entertainment_Properties" title="American Casino &amp; Entertainment Properties">
       American Casino &amp; Entertainment Properties
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      While the traditional definition of the Strip excludes the Stratosphere, it is often included in travel guides as a Strip attraction. Using this alternate definition, the Stratosphere is the northernmost of the major Strip resorts, and is the only Strip hotel actually located within the City of Las Vegas.
     </p>

     <p>
      In the late 1980s, the Stratosphere was conceived by
      <a href="/wiki/Bob_Stupak" title="Bob Stupak">
       Bob Stupak
      </a>
      as an addition to his
      <a href="/wiki/Vegas_World" title="Vegas World">
       Vegas World
      </a>
      casino. He initially wanted to create a tower resembling the Eiffel Tower in Paris, but the narrow footprint of the available property would not permit such a broad base. Searching for options, he contacted an engineering firm in Texas whose expertise was in designing radio transmission towers. They produced a series of possible configurations based on computer 'stretch-outs' of their standard skeletal tower designs. When Stupak showed these to CEO Lou Papais of Ad Art, Inc., Stockton, CA, he and his Executive Art Director, Chuck Barnard, agreed the designs were unsatisfactory and Barnard produced the concept for the 1149ft. tower. Ad Art design associate, Jack Dubois, then created a giant color rendering of the tower which Stupak used to secure financial backing for construction. Architect Ned Baldwin, creator of the CN Tower in Toronto, Canada, was brought in and assembled a team of local engineers and architects to flesh out the design and move the project to completion. At the conception of the project, one of the planned rides was a giant ape that would carry riders up and down on one of the tower's columns.
     </p>
     <p>
      The original design envisioned the Stratosphere exceeding the height of the
      <a href="/wiki/CN_Tower" title="CN Tower">
       CN Tower
      </a>
      (1,815ft (553m)) to become the
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_freestanding_structures_in_the_world" title="List of tallest freestanding structures in the world">
       tallest freestanding structure in the world
      </a>
      at that time. However, due to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FAA" title="FAA">
       FAA
      </a>
      's concerns about possible interference with nearby
      <a href="/wiki/McCarran_International_Airport" title="McCarran International Airport">
       McCarran International Airport
      </a>
      , and any possible flights that come through Las Vegas, the Tower's proposed height shrank multiple times, bringing it to its current height of 1,149ft (350m).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On August 29, 1993, the Tower caught fire while still under construction. No one was injured, but the fire forced repairs and rebuilding that led to numerous delays in the construction of the Tower.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1995,
      <a href="/wiki/Grand_Casinos" title="Grand Casinos">
       Grand Casinos
      </a>
      was brought on as an equity partner for the still privately-funded project under construction. While construction was still progressing, the
      <b>
       Stratosphere Corporation
      </b>
      was formed as a public company with shares being offered to the public.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Stratosphere opened on April 30, 1996. Shortly after opening, the Stratosphere Corporation was forced to file
      <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">
       bankruptcy
      </a>
      . This caused construction on the second tower to stop, with only a few stories partially built, and it allowed
      <a href="/wiki/Carl_Icahn" title="Carl Icahn">
       Carl Icahn
      </a>
      to gain control through one of his companies by buying a majority of the outstanding bonds.
     </p>
     <p>
      A major addition was completed in June 2001 for $1 billion that included finishing the 1000-room second hotel tower.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the early 2000s, the company attempted to get approval for a roller coaster that would run from several hundred feet up the tower and, in the last proposal, across
      <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Boulevard" title="Las Vegas Boulevard">
       Las Vegas Boulevard
      </a>
      . Part of that last proposal included an entry monument on the ride over Las Vegas Boulevard welcoming people to the City of Las Vegas. The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_City_Council" title="Las Vegas City Council">
       Las Vegas City Council
      </a>
      did not approve the project due to objections from the neighbors over possible noise from the enclosed cars on the proposed ride.
     </p>
     <p>
      In January 2010, American Casino &amp; Entertainment Properties announced a new thrill ride for the top of the tower: SkyJump, a controlled-descent,
      <a href="/wiki/Bungee_jumping" title="Bungee jumping">
       bungee jumping
      </a>
      like ride that will allow riders to plummet 855 feet (261m) attached to a high-speed descent wire. It opened on April 20, 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaavegas1_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-aaavegas1-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Radio stations
      <a href="/wiki/KOAS" title="KOAS">
       KOAS 105.7 (FM)
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/KVGS" title="KVGS">
       KVGS 107.9 (FM)
      </a>
      have on-channel FM boosters broadcasting from an antenna at the top of the tower's structure. Licensed as KOAS-FM1 and KVGS-FM1, they are the only radio stations with transmitters at the tower. However, the signals being transmitted from this structure are relatively low-power and only cover the immediate Las Vegas area on a "fill in" or "booster" basis. Both of these stations have their main transmitter sites located elsewhere, and those transmitter sites are what give these stations more widespread regional coverage.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>

As part of a $20 million renovation plan, improvements were made to many hotel rooms, the casino and the main entrance area. Improvements and upgrades have also been made in the Top of The World Restaurant and Level 107 lounge.

     <p>
      The top of the tower has two
      <a href="/wiki/Observation_deck" title="Observation deck">
       observation decks
      </a>
      , a restaurant known as "Top of the World" (
      <a href="/wiki/Revolving_restaurant" title="Revolving restaurant">
       revolving restaurant
      </a>
      ), and four thrill rides:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Big_Shot_(ride)" title="Big Shot (ride)">
        Big Shot
       </a>
       at 1,081ft (329m) is the highest
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amusement_ride" title="Amusement ride">
        thrill ride
       </a>
       in the world.
      </li>
      <li>
       Insanity, opened in 2005, at 900ft (270m) is the second highest thrill ride in the world; it dangles riders over the edge of the tower and then spins in a circular pattern at approximately forty miles per hour.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In two separate incidents in 2005, riders were left dangling several hundred feet above the Las Vegas Strip for nearly an hour and a half when Insanity shut down. The ride did not malfunction, but was programmed to cease operation if a fault or problem is detected by the ride's control system.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         16
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup>
        [source for second incident?]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/SkyJump_Las_Vegas" title="SkyJump Las Vegas">
        SkyJump Las Vegas
       </a>
       , a controlled-descent, Bungee-jumping-like ride that will allow riders to plummet 855ft (261m) attached to a high-speed descent wire.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaavegas1_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-aaavegas1-8">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         8
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       SkyJump opened on April 20, 2010.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/X-Scream" title="X-Scream">
        X-Scream
       </a>
       at 866ft (264m) is the third highest thrill ride in the world.
      </li>
     </ul>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Gaming">
       Gaming
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stratosphere_Las_Vegas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Gaming">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Some of the casino games include
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slot_machines" title="Slot machines">
       slot machines
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Video_poker" title="Video poker">
       video poker
      </a>
      . The Stratosphere has inherited some unusual variations on casino games from its predecessor. The 80,000-square-foot (7,400m
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ) casino includes 50 table games, 1,500 slot and video poker machines, a poker room, and a race and sports book.
     </p>
