       <div class="body">
        <p class="p-justify">
         When you borrow a loan from the bank to buy a car, you are required to have your vehicle fully insured. When you do this, your lender will have no complaints.
        </p>
        <p class="p-justify">
         Unfortunately, many car owners dont act sincerely on the insurance front. Either they dont take any insurance at all, or they fail to pay their premiums on time or they let their insurance lapse. The negligence on part of the borrower costs the lender dearly. To protect themselves from incurring losses, lenders resorted to the use of
         <strong>
          collateral protection insurance
         </strong>
         (CPI). With CPI, a lender who has offered a loan to a borrower for purchasing a car will succeed in cutting off losses if the car bought by the borrower gets badly damaged in an accident. Of course, CPI is not just limited to cars. This insurance can even be taken out by home loan lenders.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="programstracking082cf05c28fa279c40fc8017" src="http://cdn.sett.com/images/user/20150120/programstracking082cf05c28fa279c40fc80171db86230.png"/>
        </p>
        <p class="p-justify">
         When the borrower of a home loan or car loan does not get their collateral insured, their lender can place the amount of the coverage directly on the collateral. It is the borrower who will have to make the premium payments that will be added to the loan amount. When the borrower submits adequate insurance proof, the amount that had been placed on them would be refunded and thus the loan amount would revert to what it was at the beginning.
        </p>
        <p class="p-justify">
         There came a time when borrowers started filing a lot of CPI-related lawsuits and the claim they made was that they didnt have much knowledge about forced placed coverage. The rise in lawsuits could also be attributed to the fact that more and more lenders started taking recourse to CPI for protecting their interests.
        </p>
        <p class="p-justify">
         As time passes by, the scenario regarding collateral protection insurance started changing for the better; so much so that today we have many professional service providers who are readily available online who offer
         <a href="http://www.arcanainsurance.com/insurance-tracking-cpi.htm" rel="nofollow">
          <strong>
           collateral protection insurance and tracking
          </strong>
         </a>
         to their clients. These experts can let a lender know whether their borrower has taken proper insurance or not. This kind of service really helps in doing away with a lot of confusion. The interest in the case of CPI has been increasing steadily since 2000 and one reason behind this is that the time duration of the loan has also increased. Lenders now are more apprehensive that their borrowers may fail to repay their loan or allow their insurance to lapse.
        </p>
        <p class="p-justify">
         It is important to know that collateral protection insurance does not just benefit lenders, borrowers too can stand to gain from it. In the case of some CPI policies, if a piece of collateral like a car sustains damage, the repairing cost could be covered by the insurance policy and the borrower may not be required to part with their possession. And sometimes, when the damage done to the collateral is beyond repair, the CPI in fact could help in paying off the entire loan amount.
        </p>
        <p class="p-justify">
         <em>
          So, you see that just like other important insurance policies, collateral protective insurance is now becoming significant.
         </em>
        </p>
