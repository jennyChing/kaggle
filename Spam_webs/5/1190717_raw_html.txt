         <a class="embed-infog" data-link="http://blog.mint.com/consumer-iq/the-10-most-beautiful-cars-ever-and-what-they-cost-092012/?cid=soc_infogrph-embed_The+10+Most+Beautiful+Cars+Ever+%26%238212%3B+and+What+They+Cost " href="http://blog.mint.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/corvette-.jpg" title="http://blog.mint.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/corvette-.jpg">
          <span>
           Embed Infographic
          </span>
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </figcaption>
     </figure>
     <p>
      The most beautiful car of all time is a 1954 Mercedes-Benz 300SL.
     </p>
     <p>
      Or so says Total Car Score, which admits its new list of theTop 10 Best Looking Cars of All Timeis highly subjective. But its team says its picks, listed by age rather than beauty, all boast signature contours that continue to influence the design of todays cars.
     </p>
     <p>
      Total Car Score didnt provide sticker prices, so we did instead.
     </p>
     <h2>
      1954 Mercedes-Benz 300SL
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame
      </strong>
      : A two-seat sports car known for its gull-wing doors.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price
      </strong>
      : Around $1 million, but prices vary. A pre-production model was recently spotted for sale for$850,000, and a 1955 with an aluminum alloy body went for$4.62 million.
     </p>
     <h2>
      1961 Ferrari 250 GT California
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      Youd probably recognize this car as the one trashed in the Matthew Broderick filmFerris Buellers Day Off although that was a fiberglass fake, because even in 1986,the makers couldnt even afford to rent one, much less buy it and smash it.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      $10,976,000
     </p>
     <h2>
      1963 Chevrolet Corvette
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      Also called a Sting Ray, this years model was the first time a Corvette coupe was ever seen  the first generation was onlyconvertibles and it was the only year to feature a weirdlysplit rear window. (Car and Driverspeculated that feature might return, butAutomobilesays that isnt happening, at least not in the 2014 model.)
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      $40,000 to $60,000
     </p>
     <h2>
      1964 Aston Martin DB5
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      Another car with an iconic place in film:Sean Connery drove one as James Bondin Goldfinger, and its been in other Bond films since. (Itll reportedly be in the upcoming Bond flick, Skyfall.)
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      The Bond car sold in 2010 for$4.6 million, although a non-Bond car sold for$833,000just a few months earlier.
     </p>
     <h2>
      1965 Jaguar E-Type
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      Enzo Ferrari  guess which brand he started? called itthe most beautiful car ever made.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      $87,000
     </p>
     <h2>
      1966 Ford GT40
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      This is theonly American carto be overall winner in 24 Hours of Le Mans, the oldest car-endurance rally in the world  which it won four times in a row from 1966 to 1969.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      $1 to 2 million
     </p>
     <h2>
      1967 Ferrari 275 GTB/4
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      Only 330 were ever made, but itsconsideredby many Ferrari fans the best looking and performing variant of the late-1960s V-12 berlinetta.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      $1.65 million
     </p>
     <h2>
      1970 Dodge Challenger R/T
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      This was the real star of the 1971 police chase film Vanishing Point 
      <em>
      </em>
      which coincidentally has a scene where this car races a Jaguar E-Type.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      $104,000
     </p>
     <h2>
      1971 Lamborghini Miura SV
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      The last model of the Miura and thefastest Lamborghinitill that point, it does 0-to-60 in 5.5 seconds, with a top speed of 170 mph.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      $900,000
     </p>
     <h2>
      1973 Porsche Carrera RS
     </h2>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Claim to fame:
      </strong>
      Porsche 911s have had a long production run  continuous since 1963  but this is the most popular classic model among collectors, and it was only produced for two years, in 1973 and 1974.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Recent price:
      </strong>
      $225,000
     </p>
     <h2>
      Buying a classic
     </h2>
     <p>
      Dreaming of these beauts or something similar? Theyre not a purchase to make lightly, even if you have the cash.
     </p>
     <p>
      Here are a few things to consider:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Maintenance
       </strong>
       . Finding parts and qualified mechanics can be a nightmare, and incredibly expensive.
      </li>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Value
       </strong>
       . Unlike most cars, classics have a chance to go
       <em>
        up
       </em>
       in value, but dont count on it. Buy for love, not money.
      </li>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Popularity.
       </strong>
       Look for slightly different models or those outside the most famous years. A Mercedes 280SL is around $15,000, a fraction of the 300SL or even many new cars.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      What do you think is the most beautiful car ever?
     </p>

       </a>
        was provided by MoneyTalksNews.com.
      </em>
     </p>
