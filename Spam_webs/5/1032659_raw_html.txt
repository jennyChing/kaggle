      <section class="entry col-left">
       <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Travelers Beware: 6 Hand Gestures That Could Get You in Hot Water Overseas" data-url="http://blog.esurance.com/travelers-beware-6-hand-gestures-that-could-get-you-in-hot-water-overseas/">
       </div>
       <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
       </div>
       <p>
        Traveling abroad gives you the chance to do a number of daring things. And whether its by using the right utensils for an exotic entre or the
        <a href="http://blog.esurance.com/5-tips-for-driving-on-the-left-side-of-the-road/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://blog.esurance.com/5-tips-for-driving-on-the-left-side-of-the-road/', 'left lane for a drive around town');">
         left lane for a drive around town
        </a>
        , its fun (and occasionally crucial) to tackle new experiences the way the locals do.
       </p>
       <p>
        This brings us to perhaps the most exhilarating way to try blending in with the locals in a foreign country: communicating! Of course, in places where you know only a few basic phrases, hand gestures are key to getting your message across. But, surprisingly, this is where things can get quickly off track  and instead of blending in, you accidentally stir up trouble.
       </p>
       <p>
        From the unintentionally rude to the patently absurd, hand gestures abroad can say something entirely different than they do at home.
       </p>
       <p>
        To help you avoid embarrassment (or worse) on your next journey, here are 6 hand gestures to avoid abroad. After all, as the saying goes, When in Rome  just keep your arms to your sides, ok?
       </p>
       <h2>
        <b>
         1.
        </b>
        <b>
         Thumbs-up
        </b>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         What youre probably trying to say:
        </b>
        This is how
        <a href="http://jimmysintension.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/fonzie.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://jimmysintension.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/fonzie.jpg', 'the Fonz');" target="_blank">
         the Fonz
        </a>
        would describe your incredible local cuisine!
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         What you could accidentally be saying:
        </b>
        Im not particularly fond of you.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Where this mix-up can occur:
        </b>
        In parts of the Middle East, the Mediterranean, Asia, and the Philippines, the thumbs-up is a much more aggressive signal than it is in America, similar to our middle finger.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         How to atone for your mistake:
        </b>
        With a gift-wrapped DVD box set of
        <i>
         Happy Days.
        </i>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <b>
        </b>
        <b>
         2.
        </b>
        <b>
         High five
        </b>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         What youre probably trying to say:
        </b>
        Great job, Hello, Hold on, or (20 years ago) Talk to the hand.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         What you might accidentally be saying:
        </b>
        Talk to the hand (present day).
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Where this mix-up can occur:
        </b>
        Greece.
        <b>
        </b>
        While the open palm has gone out of style as a sassy gesture here in the U.S., its still going strong in this Mediterranean hub. And, although not quite as inflammatory as in Greece, the open palm could also spell trouble in parts of the Middle East and Africa.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         How to atone for your mistake:
        </b>
        Fist bump.
        <b>
        </b>
       </p>
       <h2>
        <b>
        </b>
        <b>
         3.
        </b>
        <b>
         Peace sign
        </b>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         What youre probably trying to say:
        </b>
        Peace (obviously).
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         What you could accidentally be saying:
        </b>
        <i>
         Er, 
        </i>
        Not peace.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Where this mix-up can occur:
        </b>
        This can be a very insulting hand gesture in places like the U.K., Ireland, New Zealand, and Australia, but only when the palm faces inward, an error famously committed by
        <a href="http://libcom.org/files/images/blog/churchill.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://libcom.org/files/images/blog/churchill.jpg', 'Winston Churchill');" target="_blank">
         Winston Churchill
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         How to atone for your mistake:
        </b>
        Become the most revered prime minister andinspirational leader the offended countrys ever seen (still, that might be too little too late).
       </p>
       <h2>
        <b>
        </b>
        <b>
         4.
        </b>
        <b>
         Beckoning finger
        </b>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         What youre probably trying to say:
        </b>
        Come here a minute.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         What you could accidentally be saying:
        </b>
        Death to you. (Yikes!)
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Where this mix-up can occur:
        </b>
        In Japan, Malaysia, Singapore, and some parts of Africa, your casual invitation isnt what you thought it was.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         How to atone for your mistake:
        </b>
        Buy them a round once they get over to you.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <b>
        </b>
        <b>
         5.
        </b>
        <b>
         Fingers pressed to your nose
        </b>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         What youre probably trying to say:
        </b>
        Theres a funky smell in here.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         What you could accidentally be saying:
        </b>
        I dont trust you.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Where this mix-up can occur:
        </b>
        In southern Italy, something as innocuous as a foul odor can turn into a much deeper issue if youre not careful.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         How to atone for your mistake:
        </b>
        Share a juicy page of your diary to show the offended parties theyre back in your inner circle.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <b>
         6.
        </b>
        <b>
         Pointing
        </b>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         What youre probably trying to say:
        </b>
        Do I go that way?
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         What you could accidentally be saying:
        </b>
        Well  the same thing, just with a whole lot more tude.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Where this mix-up can occur:
        </b>
        Many places  probably best to give up this hand gesture anywhere abroad. Using an open hand to motion this way or that is typically a softer, more respectful approach than pointing.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         How to atone for your mistake:
        </b>
        Um, buy them a round. Who said its only a good remedy for one mistake?
       </p>
