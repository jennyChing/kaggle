     <p>
      <b>
       Yvonne Ridley
      </b>
      (born 23 April 1958) is a British journalist and former
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RESPECT_The_Unity_Coalition" title="RESPECT The Unity Coalition">
       Respect Party
      </a>
      activist. She was captured by the
      <a href="/wiki/Taliban" title="Taliban">
       Taliban
      </a>
      in 2001, and converted to
      <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">
       Islam
      </a>
      after release, later becoming an outspoken opponent of
      <a href="/wiki/Zionism" title="Zionism">
       Zionism
      </a>
      and vocal critic of
      <a href="/wiki/Western_media" title="Western media">
       Western media
      </a>
      portrayals of the
      <a href="/wiki/War_on_Terror" title="War on Terror">
       War on Terror
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yvonne_Ridley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Ridley attended the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_College_of_Printing" title="London College of Printing">
       London College of Printing
      </a>
      . As a journalist, she has written for
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Sunday_Times" title="The Sunday Times">
        The Sunday Times
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Independent_on_Sunday" title="The Independent on Sunday">
        The Independent on Sunday
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Observer" title="The Observer">
        The Observer
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">
        The Mirror
       </a>
      </i>
      and the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World" title="News of the World">
        News of the World
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcpro_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbcpro-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She was deputy editor of
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wales_on_Sunday" title="Wales on Sunday">
        Wales on Sunday
       </a>
      </i>
      and was chief reporter when the
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunday_Express" title="Sunday Express">
        Sunday Express
       </a>
      </i>
      sent her to Afghanistan after
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/11_September_attacks" title="11 September attacks">
       9/11
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      She has also delivered lectures on issues relating to Iraq, Israel, Afghanistan, Chechnya, Kashmir and Uzbekistan, Women in Islam, the War on Terror and journalism at universities across the US, Australia, South Africa and the Middle East.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guarad_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-guarad-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She has written two books called
      <i>
       In The Hands of the Taliban
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hands_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-hands-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <i>
       Ticket to Paradise
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ticket_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ticket-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Ridley is a patron of the UK-based pressure groups
      <a href="/wiki/Cageprisoners" title="Cageprisoners">
       Cageprisoners
      </a>
      , the European President of the International Muslim Women's Union and the Vice President of the European Muslim League based in Milan and Geneva.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She is a member of the
      <a href="/wiki/Stop_the_War_Coalition" title="Stop the War Coalition">
       Stop the War Coalition
      </a>
      , at whose rallies she has spoken,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Respect_Party" title="Respect Party">
       Respect Party
      </a>
      , for whom she has stood in parliamentary elections.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Capture_by_the_Taliban">
       Capture by the Taliban
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yvonne_Ridley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Capture by the Taliban">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Yvonne Ridley came to prominence on 28 September 2001, when she was captured by the Taliban in
      <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">
       Afghanistan
      </a>
      whilst working for the
      <i>
       Sunday Express
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nzhdon_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nzhdon-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the days before the start of the
      <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001present)">
       US-led invasion of Afghanistan
      </a>
      , refused an entrance visa, she decided to follow the example of BBC reporter
      <a href="/wiki/John_Simpson_(journalist)" title="John Simpson (journalist)">
       John Simpson
      </a>
      , who had crossed the border anonymously in a
      <a href="/wiki/Burqa" title="Burqa">
       burqa
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nzhvei_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nzhvei-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      She entered on 26 September and spent 2 days undercover in Afghanistan. It was on her return, traveling with her guides, that she was uncovered when the donkey she was on bolted and her camera was seen by a Taliban soldier.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nzhdon_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nzhdon-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She was accused of being a spy, which carried a death sentence, and at the very least faced jail for illegally entering Afghanistan.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The British high commissioner to
      <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">
       Pakistan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Synnott" title="Hilary Synnott">
       Hilary Synnott
      </a>
      , met the Taliban ambassador in
      <a href="/wiki/Islamabad" title="Islamabad">
       Islamabad
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mullah_Abdul_Salam_Zaeef" title="Mullah Abdul Salam Zaeef">
       Mullah Abdul Salam Zaeef
      </a>
      , and opened negotiations for her release.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On 8 October she was released and escorted to the border, where she was handed over to the Pakistani authorities.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It had been feared that this would be jeopardised by the bombing of Afghan targets as part of the
      <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001present)">
       War in Afghanistan
      </a>
      that had commenced the previous day.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      She revealed that she had kept a concealed diary inside a box for a toothpaste tube and in the inside of a soap wrapper. She had been on hunger strike throughout her captivity. She described her experience as terrifying but she was not physically hurt.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nzhdon_9-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-nzhdon-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After her release, her guides Jan Ali and Nagibullah Muhmand, as well as his five-year-old daughter Basmena, were held in prison in Kabul. At least three of Muhmand's relatives were also arrested for aiding Ridley after the Taliban developed the film in her camera.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Conversion_to_Islam">
       Conversion to Islam
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yvonne_Ridley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conversion to Islam">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Taliban_treatment_of_women" title="Taliban treatment of women">

      </a>

      </a>
      , Ridley urged all Muslims in Britain to "boycott the police and refuse to co-operate with them in any way, shape or form until the boys are released," including "asking the community copper for directions to passing the time of day with a beat officer." Her comments were labelled as "sheer, undiluted madness" by Shadow Home Secretary
      <a href="/wiki/David_Davis_(British_politician)" title="David Davis (British politician)">
       David Davis
      </a>
      , who added that "To not co-operate would be of no benefit to the Muslim community; no benefit to the police; and no benefit to the security of our country."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49">
       <a href="#cite_note-49">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        49
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Respect MP
      <a href="/wiki/George_Galloway" title="George Galloway">
       George Galloway
      </a>
      quickly distanced himself from her comments, saying Ridley was wrong and, "Our policy is not that we should withdraw co-operation from the police." Ridley later claimed that she only wanted Muslim leaders to stop co-operating, not the whole community; this is not supported by her comment about not asking directions from the police.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50">
       <a href="#cite_note-50">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        50
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The two men were subsequently released without charge and an official apology was later issued to the family by the Metropolitan Police.
     </p>
     <p>
      After the Chechen leader Shamil Basayev (responsible for the terrorist attack on the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beslan_school_massacre" title="Beslan school massacre">
       Beslan school
      </a>
      ) was killed, Ridley wrote an article referring to Basayev by the Muslim honorific
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaheed" title="Shaheed">
        shaheed
       </a>
      </i>
      , meaning "martyr". She went on to refer to Basayev as leader of "an admirable struggle to bring independence to Chechnya".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guarad_4-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-guarad-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Initially, Ridley strongly opposed the
      <a href="/wiki/2011_military_intervention_in_Libya" title="2011 military intervention in Libya">
       Western intervention
      </a>
      in the 2011
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Libyan_Civil_War" title="2011 Libyan Civil War">
       Libyan Civil War
      </a>
      , and spoke in a rally opposing it held in Central London where she likened asking for Western support to making a pact with the Devil. However she later travelled to rebel-held territory, where she became a wholehearted supporter of the Libyan rebels' cause and accepted that they had no choice but to ask for the Western powers' help.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51">
       <a href="#cite_note-51">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        51
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2012 she expressed her strong support for the end of secret evidence used in the
      <a href="/wiki/Special_Immigration_Appeals_Commission" title="Special Immigration Appeals Commission">
       SIAC
      </a>
      case against the Jordanian Islamist cleric,
      <a href="/wiki/Abu_Qatada" title="Abu Qatada">
       Abu Qatada
      </a>
      , and her opposition to Home Secretary
      <a href="/wiki/Theresa_May" title="Theresa May">
       Theresa May
      </a>
      's plans to deport him on the basis of the secret evidence from the UK.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52">
       <a href="#cite_note-52">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        52
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Denial_of_entry">
       Denial of entry
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yvonne_Ridley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Denial of entry">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Because of her views, she was denied entry to India in 2010 to address the Muslim Women's Conference in
      <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">
       Kerala
      </a>
      . Later, in January 2013, she was scheduled to attend the Spring of Islam Conference organised by the
      <a href="/wiki/Jamaat-e-Islami_Hind" title="Jamaat-e-Islami Hind">
       Jamaat-e-Islami Hind
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad" title="Hyderabad">
       Hyderabad
      </a>
      . She was given all necessary clearances by the external affairs ministry, but was denied a visa at the last minute because of the tense situation in Hyderabad following the arrest of controversial local legislator
      <a href="/wiki/Akbaruddin_Owaisi" title="Akbaruddin Owaisi">
       Akbaruddin Owaisi
      </a>
      a few days before the event was scheduled to take place. However Ridley, through video conference, addressed three sessions of girls, women and journalists during the conference.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53">
       <a href="#cite_note-53">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        53
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
