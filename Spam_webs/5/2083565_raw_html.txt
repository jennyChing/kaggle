At the very basic identity, I am human. It has taken me my entire life to simply just grasp the fact that I am human. In the sense of the word of being human; to accept my flaws, my mistakes, my hurt, my wants, and desires. It is the everyday practice of accepting everything and expecting nothing.
             <br/>
             My yoga practice has brought me to this expansion of the present moment and accepting myself. When I come onto my mat, I am removing thoughts of the outside world by practicing non-judgment, love, attaching to nothingness, and dancing with my breath. It is the self-study on and off the mat that brings me to live in the presence of Being. My hope for this world is to bring yoga within each of us. When we expand our understanding that the Self is our reflection in others, we open the world to peace. I am You. And You are Me. This life is every life.
