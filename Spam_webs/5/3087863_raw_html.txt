       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Make CANNED CHICKEN  Lasts on the Shelf for 3+ Years!
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="How to Can Chicken! Making your own canned chicken is not as scary as it seams. It tastes great and can last 3+ years! Prepared-Housewives.com #canning #cannedchicken " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11681" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/how-to-canned-chicken.jpg" title="How to Can Chicken" width="467"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Making my own canned chicken was one of those projects I kept putting off until I ordered 80 lbs of chicken and either had to can it or waste it.I was leaving town for a week so I stayed up for 24 hoursstraight and got it all canned!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Luckily nothing blew-up, but canning chicken was only half of the issue, I now had to eat it! Im not going to lie, I was scared I was going to kill not only myself, but myfamily and land a spot on
         </span>
         <em>
          Dumbest Ways to Die
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          I am proud to say though, over two years later we are allstill alive and itssome ofthe best meat Ive ever had! I love the convenience of pulling out a jar of cooked chicken and putting it into any meal. This is definitely something I plan to do with my chicken from now on!Its even better having the peace of mind knowing I dont have to show my face or purple pjs at the store for years to come.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Canned chickens nutritivevalue is best forup to 3 years, but can last much longer.So go ahead, give it a try  I dare you ;)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="How to Can Chicken! Making your own canned chicken is not as scary as it seams. It tastes great and can last 3+ years! Prepared-Housewives.com #canning #cannedchicken " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11675" height="467" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/can-chicken-pressure-canner.jpg" title="Pressure Can Chicken" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         <span style="color: #28a3a3;">
          STEPS TO CANNING CHICKEN:
         </span>
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          (NOTE: Be sure to read your Instruction Manual before operating your Pressure Cooker/Canner. ImNot responsible for death only for AWESOME chicken :)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          <strong>
           Short Version:
          </strong>
          Put raw chicken in jars, put 1/4-1/2 tsp of salt on top, &amp; then pressure cook for 75 (pint) or 90 (quart) minutes!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          You can use this same procedure to bottle most meats (some just need to be cooked slightly and maybe need broth added)  beef, venison, elk, sausage, brisket, etc.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h1>
         1- Get Supplies Ready:
        </h1>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           <strong>
            PRESSURECANNER
           </strong>
           Ilove the
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00004S88Z/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00004S88Z&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=preparhousew-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00004S88Z/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00004S88Z&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=preparhousew-20', 'All American 921 Pressure Cooker/Canner']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             All American 921 Pressure Cooker/Canner
            </a>
           </strong>
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           <strong>
            CANNING SUPPLIES
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001NNJ42I/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001NNJ42I&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=preparhousew-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001NNJ42I/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001NNJ42I&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=preparhousew-20', 'Utensil Set']);" target="_blank">
             Utensil Set
            </a>
           </strong>
           (Tongs, Funnel, Lid Holder, etc.)
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           <strong>
            SALT
           </strong>
            Pint (abt. 1/4 tsp) &amp; Quart (abt. 1/2 tsp)
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           <strong>
            VINEGAR
           </strong>
            To wipe Rim of Jar
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           <strong>
            CANNING JARS
           </strong>
           
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001DIZ1NO/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001DIZ1NO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=preparhousew-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001DIZ1NO/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001DIZ1NO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=preparhousew-20', 'Pint']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             Pint
            </strong>
           </a>
           (holds 1 lb) or
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00AA1H3VI/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00AA1H3VI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=preparhousew-20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00AA1H3VI/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00AA1H3VI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=preparhousew-20', 'Quart']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             Quart
            </strong>
           </a>
           (holds 2lbs)  I Prefer Wide Mouth (Easier to fit meat in, and Im able to use the same lids for all my jars)
          </span>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             Inspect jars for chips in the rim and hairline cracks and discard damaged ones.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             Inspect metal rings and discard any with dents or rust.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             Wash jars, metal screw bands and lids in hot soapy water. Rinse.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             Place jars upside down on a clean, dry cloth or leave them in the dishwasher until needed.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             DO NOT reuse flat metal lids.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Materials Needed for Canning Chicken" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11686" height="500" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/materials-for-canning-chicken.jpg" title="Materials Needed for Canning Chicken" width="700"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         2- Cut &amp; Put Raw Chicken into Jars
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Slice the chicken however you like so it will fit in the jar  leave about 3/4 to an 1 inch of headspace. You can cube it, slice it, or dice it, I really dont care.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          There is no need to pre-cook chicken, because it will cook in the pressure canner. Chicken also produces enough juice so that no water needs to be added. It makes its own chicken broth that you can choose to use if you like.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Cutting Chicken for Canning" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11679" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/cutting-chicken-for-canning.jpg" title="Cutting Chicken for Canning" width="466"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         3- Add Salt
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Put about 1/4  1/2 tsp of salt for Pint size jars and put 1/2  1 tsp of salt for Quart size jars.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Add salt before canning chicken." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11687" height="466" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/add-salt-before-canning.jpg" title="Add salt before canning chicken." width="700"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         4- Wipe Rim with a Rag or Paper Towel &amp; Screw on Lids
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Much of your canning success depends on this one step.
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Lately, I have wiped the edges of myjars with a little vinegar and all of them have sealed perfectly.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Screw on lids finger tight, do not over tighten or else it will not be able to exhaust properly, but dont do too loose or it may notseal (no pressure or anything ;)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Wipe Jar Rim with Vinegar" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11678" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/clean-jar-lids-with-vinegar.jpg" title="Wipe Jar Rim with Vinegar" width="467"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         5- Get Pressure Canner Ready:
        </h1>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           Check metal-to-metal seal for lubrication.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           Check to make sure vent pipe is clear.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           Place 1 1/2 of water in bottom of canner.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           Place a rack in bottom of pressure canner with the rim facing down.
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Getting Pressure Canner Ready" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11677" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/canning-chicken-in-pressure-cooker.jpg" title="Getting Pressure Canner Ready" width="466"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         6- Put Jars in Pressure Canner
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Make sure toput jarson top of the rack. Never place jars directly on the bottom of the canner.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Also make sure to staggerthe top layer if there are two layers, using a rack between layers. I can fit about 7 pint jarson the bottom and 7-9 on the top.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Put jars on rack in pressure canner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11685" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/putting-jars-in-pressure-cooker.jpg" title="Put jars on rack in pressure canner" width="466"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         7- Put Cover on Canner
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Place cover on cooker bottom so that arrow on cover aligns with the arrow on the bottom. Gradually and evenly
          <strong>
           tighten two opposite wing nuts at the same time
          </strong>
          , maintaining the same size gap around the entirecanner where the cover meets the bottom.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          NEVER tighten just one wing nut at a time  thats a big NO NO! You dont want to end up with a lop-sided lid!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="put lid on pressure canner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11684" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/put-cover-on-pressure-cooker.jpg" title="put lid on pressure canner" width="467"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         8 Exhaust for 7-10 minutes
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          After placing lid on canner, turn stove onto high and allow steam to escape from the vent pipe.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Once you seesteam escaping start timingfor 7 minutes to allow for the air to escape from the jars.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="exhaust pressure canner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11680" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/exhaust-pressure-canner.jpg" title="exhaust pressure canner" width="466"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         9- Set Pressure Regulator Weight Over Vent Pipe for 10 P.S.I.
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Depending on your elevation, set the weight over the vent pipe, aligning the weight so that the hole corresponds to the desired pressure (for sea level orTexas
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          its 10 p.s.i.).
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="set pressure regulator weight" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11683" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/pressure-regulator-weight.jpg" title="set pressure regulator weight" width="466"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         10- Build Pressure &amp; Wait for Regulator Weight to Jiggle &amp; Sputter:
        </h1>
        <h3>
         Start Timing
        </h3>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          When the regulator weight begins to jiggle and sputter immediatelystart timing:
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           <strong>
            Pint-Size Jars
           </strong>
            75 Minutes
          </span>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span style="font-size: 16px;">
           <strong>
            Quart-Size Jars
           </strong>
            90 Minutes
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         Adjust theHeat
        </h3>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Adjust the heat until it
          <strong>
           jiggles only one to four times a minute
          </strong>
          (On my stove I set it between 3.5-5). Try to keep the pressure as steady as possible by regulating the amount of heat applied to the cooker. At no time should the pressure be allowed to rise above 15 P.S.I.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          <em>
           Do not expect the gauge to always show the exact pressure setting, when the weight jiggles and sputters its releasing pressure to maintain the setting on the pressure regulator weight. Even in the situation where the gauge is not registering correctly for any reason, the cooker should still be functioning at the pressure regulator weight setting.
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <h1>
         11- Turn off Heat
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          When cooking is completed turn the heat off and allow itto cool until the steam pressure gauge reads zero.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Remove the selective pressure regulator weight slowly and do not release steam pressure too rapidly as liquid will be drawn from jars. You want to make sure tonot move the pressurecanner until the pressure is completely reduced.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="pressure can chicken" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11682" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/pressure-can-chicken.jpg" title="pressure can chicken" width="466"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         12- Remove Cover
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          CAUTION: Never loosen wing nuts until the steam pressure gauge
          <strong>
           registers zero
          </strong>
          , and you have allowed any remaining pressure to escape by carefully removing the selective pressure regulator weight. (The longer you wait without removing the pressure regulator weight after it reaches zero P.S.I., the more vacuum will form, pulling the cover tighter and tighter to the bottom.)
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Remove cover, raising farthest edge first to protect face and arms from steam.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h1>
         13- Yay, you now have CANNED CHICKEN!!!  Remove Screw Bands
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Pull chicken out and set on towel to cool. As jars seal you will hear a popping sound and you can check by seeing if the lid has popped down in the center. If a jar does not seal, refrigerate contents and use or reprocess within 24 hours of the original processing.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Wipe containers off after they are cool. Label with the date and contents. You can also choose to add a lot number if you canned more than one lot in a day.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          <strong>
           <em>
            Screw bands are not needed on stored jars. They can be removed easily after jars are cooled.
           </em>
          </strong>
          If left on stored jars, they become difficult to remove, often rust, and may not work properly again.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="canned chicken from pressure canner" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11676" height="700" src="http://prepared-housewives.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/canned-chicken-from-pressure-cooker.jpg" title="canned chicken from pressure canner" width="466"/>
        </p>
        <h1>
         How long does Canned Chicken last?
        </h1>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          For best eating quality and nutritive value, use within 3 years. Its still good for much longer, some have eaten their chicken at 10+ years, but optimal nutrition value is within 3 years.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 16px;">
          Never eat anything you think might be spoiled. Discard it. As an added safety precaution you can also boil meats for 20 minutes in an open pan before eating. This will destroy the botulism toxin, should any be present.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h1>
         Cooking with Canned Chicken
        </h1>
        <p>
         You can substitute most of your recipes that call for chicken with the chicken you canned.
        </p>
        <p>
         No need to cook it again, its ready to throw into any meal you want! It falls apart very easy so its great for chicken salad, tacos, casseroles, and much more.Another great part about canned chicken is the liquid inside is now chicken broth you can use too!
        </p>
        <p>
         Ihave a few
         <a href="http://prepared-housewives.com/food-storage-recipe-index/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           canned chickenrecipes
          </strong>
         </a>
         you can use,butwill be definitely beadding more so stay tuned ;)
        </p>
