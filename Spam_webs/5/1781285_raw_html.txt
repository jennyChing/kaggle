             <p>
              French television station
              <a href="http://www.canalplus.fr/c-infos-documentaires/pid3357-c-special-investigation.html?vid=1122650&amp;sc_cmpid=SharePlayerEmbed" target="_blank">
               Canal+
              </a>
              recently sat down with Dr. Patrick Moore for an upcoming documentary. Dr. Moore, who claims to be an ecologicalexpert and is currently the frontman forEcosense Environmental, stated to the interviewer that Monsantos weed killer Roundup was not responsible for skyrocketing cancer rates in Argentina.
             </p>
             <p>
              This claim comes on the heels of last weeks
              <a href="http://theantimedia.org/roundup-probably-causes-cancer-in-humans/" target="_blank">
               World Health Organization report
              </a>
              citing the weed killer as a probable cause of cancer.
             </p>
             <p>
              Soon after the interview began, it took a turn for the surreal.
             </p>
             <p>
              Dr. Moore insisted that Roundup is safe to drink, at which point the interviewer did the only logical thing one could do in that situation. He offered the doctor a glass of the weed killer to allow him an opportunityto back up his statement. The following is the text from that exchange.
             </p>
             <p>
              Dr. Patrick Moore:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               You can drink a whole quart of (Roundup) and it wont hurt you.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Canal+:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               You want to drink some? We have some here.
              </em>
             </p>
             <div class="ad-inserter ad-inserter-3" style="float:left;margin:8px 8px 8px 0px;">
              <div>
               <center>
                <font size="1">
                 Advertisements
                </font>
               </center>
               <script id="mNCC" language="javascript">
                medianet_width='336';  medianet_height= '280';  medianet_crid='613310285';
               </script>
               <script id="mNSC" language="javascript" src="http://contextual.media.net/nmedianet.js?cid=8CUE061R9">
               </script>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              Moore:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               Id be happy to, actually. UhhNot.. Not really. But I know it wouldnt hurt me.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Canal+:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               If you say so, I have some glyphosate, have some.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Moore:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               No. Im not stupid.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Canal+:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               So, its dangerous, right?
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Moore:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               No, People try to commit suicide with it and fail; fail regularly.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Canal+:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               Tell the truth, its dangerous.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Moore:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               Its not dangerous to humans.
              </em>
             </p>
             <div class="ad-inserter ad-inserter-2" style="float:left;margin:8px 8px 8px 0px;">
              <div>
               <center>
                <font size="1">
                 Advertisements

             <p>
              Canal+: 
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               So, are you ready to drink one glass?
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Moore: 
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               No, Im not an idiot. Interview me about golden rice, thats what Im talking about.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Canal+:
              <em style="font-style: italic;">
               We did.
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Moore then abruptly ends the interview by calling the host a complete jerk and storms off.
             </p>
             <p>
              Greenpeace, an organization to which the doctor turned lobbyistbelonged in the 1970s, issued this
              <a href="http://www.greenpeace.org/usa/en/media-center/news-releases/greenpeace-statement-on-patric/" target="_blank">
               statement
              </a>
              in part in 2008 regardingDr. Patrick Moore.
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               <em style="font-style: italic;">
                Patrick Moore often misrepresents himself in the media as an environmental expert or even an environmentalist, while offering anti-environmental opinions on a wide range of issues and taking a distinctly anti-environmental stance. He also exploits long-gone ties with Greenpeace to sell himself as a speaker and pro-corporate spokesperson, usually taking positions that Greenpeace opposes.
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               <em style="font-style: italic;">
                While it is true that Patrick Moore was a member of Greenpeace in the 1970s, in 1986 he abruptly turned his back on the very issues he once passionately defended. He claims he saw the light but what Moore really saw was an opportunity for financial gain. Since then he has gone from defender of the planet to a paid representative of corporate polluters.
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               <em style="font-style: italic;">
                Patrick Moore promotes such anti-environmental positions as clearcut logging, nuclear power, farmed salmon, PVC (vinyl) production, genetically engineered crops, and mining. Clients for his consulting services are a veritable Whos Who of companies that Greenpeace has exposed for environmental misdeeds, including Monsanto, Weyerhaeuser, and BHP Minerals.
               </em>
              </p>
             </blockquote>
