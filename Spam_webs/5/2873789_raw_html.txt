        <h1>
         I Climbed Two Mountains
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="post-title-subline">
        Words by Pete Heck / Photography by Pete and Dalene Heck
       </div>
       <p>
        We had secured three weeks in Fort William, Scotland after excitedly accepting an idyllic
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/housesitting" title="Review  Seaview Fife Cottages and Pitcairlie Holiday Apartments and Lodge">
         house-sit
        </a>
        in this true nature lovers haven  we were minutes from endless paths through the Highlands with frequent waterfalls and gorgeous views. Fort William is also home to the highest peak in the United Kingdom  Ben Nevis. We could see it just around the corner from the house, and I could feel it taunting me while I sat inside and worked. Instead, I was focused on a different base camp  one that started with my laptop, camera, and a mountainous list of tasks to complete.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_14280" style="width: 984px">
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Dog-Sitting.jpg">
         <img alt="Lonely" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-19932" height="649" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Dog-Sitting.jpg" width="974"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Also, who could resist this face?
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        We had to catch up from our recent flurry of travel, prepare for the upcoming
        <a href="http://navigatemediagroup.com/bloghouse" target="_blank" title="BlogHouse">
         BlogHouse Ireland
        </a>
        which we were managing, draft a couple big pitches, and of course maintain our own blog and social media channels. It was exhausting and frustrating to stare at the beauty just out of our reach, but we kept climbing and plowing through. Fortunately the weather had been absolute shit and the rain helped to dissolve the guilt of our entrapment. Our daily dog walks gave us some reprieve and and a small taste of our surroundings.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Dog-Sitting-3.jpg">
         <img alt="Scotland-Dog-Sitting-3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-19931" height="649" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Dog-Sitting-3.jpg" width="974"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        When the three weeks ended we had reached our goal and were ahead of the game of work (for the moment). Presentations were set, blog posts were scheduled and pitches were ready to go.
       </p>
       <p>
        The homeowners, Katie and Mark, miraculously brought blue skies with them upon their return, and also graciously let us stay on as our flight was not for another couple of days. Even though Mark had just returned from a grueling 10 day bicycle journey from the top of the UK to the bottom he asked if I wanted to climb The Ben. With work under control, I did not hesitate to answer yes.
       </p>
       <p>
        The clouds lifted in the morning and the path was clearer than it had been in all of our three weeks prior.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Ben-Nevis-Path.jpg">
         <img alt="Path up Ben Nevis" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-19925" height="649" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Ben-Nevis-Path.jpg" width="974"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        It was six hours of pure bliss and climbing Ben felt like a reward for the metaphoric mountain I had conquered over the previous three weeks.
       </p>
       <p>
        The actual climb was not very difficult nor technical so it gave Mark and I numerous moments to turn around and admire the view.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Climbing-Ben-Nevis.jpg">
         <img alt="Near Summit Ben Nevis" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-19929" height="649" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Climbing-Ben-Nevis.jpg" width="974"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        And when we finally reached the summit, we were above all clouds and could see for miles. We lingered for over an hour and in the first thirty minutes we had it all to ourselves.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Ben-Nevis-Summit-2.jpg">
         <img alt="Ben Nevis Summit" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-19926" height="649" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Ben-Nevis-Summit-2.jpg" width="974"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Ben-Nevis-North-Face.jpg">
         <img alt="Ben Nevis North Face" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-19924" height="649" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Ben-Nevis-North-Face.jpg" width="974"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        We had overheard a local saying that this was his sixteenth time at Ben and had never seen it like this before. It being both Marks and my first time up, we had caught a bit of luck. It was worth the wait.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Ben-Nevis-Summit.jpg">
         <img alt="Photo Atop Ben Nevis" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-19927" height="1461" src="http://www.hecktictravels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Scotland-Ben-Nevis-Summit.jpg" width="974"/>
        </a>
       </p>
