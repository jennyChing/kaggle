          <p>
           <strong>
            INDIA WIN BY 127 RUNS
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           So, India handsomely level the ODI series 1-1 after that 127-run thumping. Jadeja (man of the match with 61 runs and two wickets). Top marks proved the difference with bat and ball. Together with MS Dhoni, India's 108 runs in the last 10 overs were always going to prove a step too far. After a decent start, England never looked like recovering after Kevin Pietersen's dismissal and they were firmly dead and buried when they twice lost two wickets in an over towards the end. A grimacing ending, but a joyful one for India fans, who might yet see their side win a series at home. All to play for. See you on Saturday for the third ODI at Ranchi.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           <img align="left" alt="Wicket" height="40" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01825/sport_cricket_1825506a.gif" width="45"/>
           <strong>
            WICKET! Dernbach run out 2 ENG 158 ALL OUT
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Yes, Samit. Save some grace before the third ODI why don't you. Patel phones Kochi double four six four in the first four deliveries. Darts down the track over midwicket, then clears cover, a massive six over long on boundary before cuts hard over the covers. But two balls later Patel attempts to steal a single off the last ball and Dernbach is well short. Patel finishes unbeaten on 30.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 35: ENG 140/9 (Dernbach 1* Patel 9*)
           </strong>
           England take the batting powerplay. Surely now we'll see Samit Patel free his arms. But Jadeja, surely now the man-of-the-match after his later order blitz, keeps it tight though and there's only four off the over.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 34: ENG 135/9 (Dernbach 1* Patel 9*)
           </strong>
           Umpire Davis' howler will give a turgid end to this match. Dernbach gets off the mark with a single to square. Four wickets in four overs.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="left" alt="Wicket" height="40" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01825/sport_cricket_1825506a.gif" width="45"/>
           <strong>
            WICKET! Finn c Dhoni b Ashwin 0 ENG 135/9
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Okay, it may be a wicket-fest of late but for some unfathomable reason Umpire Davis' finger goes up for a caught behind. Daylight robbery. This may be a day-nighter but Finn missed that by a country mile. Aswhin on a hat-trick.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="left" alt="Wicket" height="40" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01825/sport_cricket_1825506a.gif" width="45"/>
           <strong>
            WICKET! Tredwell lbw Ashwin 1 ENG 135/8
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Keep it straight is the mantra and this will be a swift conclusion. Ashwin holds Tredwell back in his crease and inevitable finger goes up.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 33: ENG 135/7 (Tredwell 1* Patel 9*)
           </strong>
           Two singles off Jadeja's over. All to quick.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 32: ENG 133/7 (Tredwell 0* Patel 8*)
           </strong>
           Patel ekes out a welcome single as Tredwell sees out the rest of the over. The question now is when Patel will say enough is enough.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 31: ENG 132/7 (Tredwell 0* Patel 7*)
           </strong>
           A blistering double-wicket maiden for India. Jadeja's figures? 5-1-6-2. And England need 153 more runs to win (India's 108 off their last 60 balls is going to prove the difference)
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="left" alt="E-mail" height="40" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01817/email_1817839a.gif" width="45"/>
           <strong>
            Dan Lucas
           </strong>
           missives: "I know you think he has something, but Kieswetter's been playing for three years now and hasn't made any progress from the questionable keeper who hits the odd six (but nowhere near enough to justify the lack of other any other runs) he started out as. It seems to me that England are using this series to try new things out; see Root bowling nine overs in the first match and only two here and Samit doing almost the opposite, as well as the flexible batting order. By playing someone who apparently has no aptitude for progressing beyond that grey area between mediocrity and sheer uselessness ahead of someone with the potential to be brilliant like Buttler seems pretty pointless.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="left" alt="Wicket" height="40" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01825/sport_cricket_1825506a.gif" width="45"/>
           <strong>
            WICKET! Woakes lbw Jadeja 0 132/7
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Third ball and the end looks forthcoming as Jadeja skids one on to Woakes' pads. It looks to have been caught, brilliantly low down at first slip. But it's pad first and this game is as good as done.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="left" alt="Wicket" height="40" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01825/sport_cricket_1825506a.gif" width="45"/>
           <strong>
            WICKET! Root b Jadeja 36 ENG 132/6
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Jadeja in and Root is deceived first up by the flight and spin. It's tossed up again and Root is beaten, again. It's through the gate and he had little clue about that. Still, not a bad 50-ball knock.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 29: ENG 128/5 (Root 34* Patel 5*)
           </strong>
           Jadeja, who is having a grand game, is back on. With the pitch dying, and the ball keeping a bit low as well as turning, this is a tough ask for England and they can only get two off the over.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 28: ENG 126/5 (Root 33* Patel 4*)
           </strong>
           Not much of an over from Ashwin but England only fiddle five from it. Oi oi. Tudes is in off the long run.
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe frameborder="0" height="133" id="tweetframe29116202012705177616892692061358254375" marginheight="0" marginwidth="0" scrolling="no" src="http://s.telegraph.co.uk/graphics/html/TwitterEmbed/Version1/web10271.html" width="460">
            &lt;noframe&gt;Twitter: Alex Tudor - Work that goes on behind the scenes &amp;amp; a lot of money with young players going out 2 India every winter nothing seems 2 change or am I wrong?&lt;/noframe&gt;
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 27: ENG 121/5 (Root 29* Patel 3*)
           </strong>
           Unmitigated drivel from Yuvraj - as David Gower says "not one good ball in it" - and indeed they are all short, wide or both. Root pulls one ball for four and there are nine off the over.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Peter Rowntree
           </strong>
           writes: "Think Kieswetter is making an excellent case for the inclusion of Jos Buttler in the next ODI team."
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 26: ENG 112/5 (Root 22* Patel 1*)
           </strong>
           Samit's off the mark right away but is also beaten by Ashwin.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="left" alt="Wicket" height="40" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01825/sport_cricket_1825506a.gif" width="45"/>
           <strong>
            WICKET! Kieswetter c Raina b Ashwin 18
           </strong>
           Oh what a gift. Ashwin has not bowled well so far. His first ball of the over is short and Kieswetter pulls it away hard for four. Two balls later, a similarly whiffy delivery, but for some reason CK just tries to dink it into the legside rather than giving it the pasting it deserved, and he chips tamely to Raina at midwicket. Bathetic end to a pretty ghastly innings and that's
           <strong>
            110/5
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 25: ENG 106/4 (Root 21* Kieswetter 14*)
           </strong>
           Good shot from Root, confident firm sweep of Yuvraj, right out of the middle and that's four. So 20 off the last three overs and the corpse is twitching a bit. At halfway England need 180 from 25.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            OVER 24: ENG 99/4 (Root 16* Kieswetter 12*)

            var settings = {
                articleImage: "",

                articleId: "",
                currentArticleId: "",
                articleList: [],
                articleListToSkip: [],
                readNextCookieName: "tmg_read_next",

                closeButton: "#closeBtn a",
                articleContainer: "#readNextArticleContainer",
                widgetContainer: "#readNextWidget",
                displayThumbnail: false,
                panelOpen: false,
                autoOpen: true
            };

            var renderTemplate = function() {
                var template = '&lt;div id="readNextWidget" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextOpen"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div id="readNextOpenInner"&gt;&lt;a href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Next Story&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="icn"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +

                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleInnerWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;p id="readNextLabel"&gt;MOST VIEWED IN NEWS&lt;/p&gt;';
                        
				if(settings.articleImage &amp;&amp; settings.articleImage!="null" &amp;&amp; settings.displayThumbnail){
					template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageThumbnail" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;img src="'+ settings.articleImage +'"/&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';
				}
                        
				template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageTitle" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;' + settings.articleTitle + '&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +

                        '&lt;div id="closeBtn"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="#"&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;iframe style="border:none;position:absolute;top:0;left:0; height:100%;width:100%;z-index:-1;" id="iefix" src="about:blank"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' + //fix for IE bug
                        '&lt;/div&gt;';
                return template;
            };


            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }

                        if($(window).scrollTop() + $(window).height() == $(document).height()) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }
                    });
                }
            };

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();

    };
