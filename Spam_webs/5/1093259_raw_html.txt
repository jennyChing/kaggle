           <h3 class="entry-header" style="font-size: 20px;line-height: 28px;font-weight: bold;color:#3366FF;">

              FRAMESHOP:
             </span>
             FRAMESHOP: A PICTURE OF THE GOP ON CIVIL RIGHTS
            </a>
           </h3>
           <!-- .entry-header -->
           <h3 style="font-size: 16px;line-height: 28px;font-weight: bold;color:#666;">
            The one photo the GOP does not want anyone to see was snapped at yesterday's NAACP GOP Presidential Candidate Forum. The NAACP invited all 9 Republican candidates to the forum, but only one showed up: Tom Tancredo. All the Democratic...
           </h3>
           <p>
            <a href="http://twitter.com/home?status=Frameshop: A Picture of the GOP on Civil Rights%20http://jeffrey-feldman.typepad.com/frameshop/2007/07/frameshop-a-p-1.html" target="">
             &gt;&gt;Twitter this post!
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <!-- -->
          <!-- user photo -->
          <div style="height:90px;margin:16px 0 0 0;padding: 5px 8px 0 0; border-top: 1px #0000FF solid; border-bottom: 1px #0000FF solid;font-size:12px;line-height:1.6em;position:relative;">
           <div style="position:relative;float:left;">
            <div class="module-content" style="margin:5px 2px 0px 0px;">
             <img alt="My Photo" height="85px;" src="http://jeffrey-feldman.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451cad869e20105371b40c4970b-150wi"/>
            </div>
            <!-- .module-content -->
           </div>
           <div style="position:absolute;bottom:2px;left:0px;margin:0px 0px 0px 80px;">
            <a href="http://jeffrey-feldman.typepad.com/about.html">
             Jeffrey Feldman
            </a>
            , Editor-in-Chief
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.frameshopisopen.com">
             Frameshop
            </a>
            , 
07/13/2007
            <a href="http://twitter.com/JeffreyFeldman">
             <img align="top" alt="" src="http://jeffrey-feldman.typepad.com/frameshop_design_test/twitter-bird.gif" title="TwitterID: JeffreyFeldman" width="24px"/>
            </a>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="entry-content" style="margin: 0 0 0 -60px;">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <div style="margin: 5px;">
             <a href="http://tinyurl.com/3dlno6">
              <img alt="photo credit: Ricardo Thomas / The Detroit News" src="http://farm2.static.flickr.com/1237/798430673_32b2d29274.jpg?v=0" title="photo credit: Ricardo Thomas / The Detroit News" width="430"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <p>
             The one photo the GOP does not want anyone to see was snapped at yesterday's NAACP GOP Presidential Candidate Forum. The NAACP invited all 9 Republican candidates to the forum, but only one showed up: Tom Tancredo. All the Democratic Presidential hopefuls showed up for their forum.
            </p>
            <div style="margin: 10px; float: left;">
             <script type="text/javascript">
              digg_url = 'http://jeffrey-feldman.typepad.com/frameshop/2007/07/frameshop-a-p-1.html';
             </script>
             <script src="http://digg.com/tools/diggthis.js" type="text/javascript">
             </script>
            </div>
            <p>
             The excuses given by the Republican campaigns mostly had to do with scheduling conflicts--just too busy to make it.
            </p>
            <p>
             The resulting photo of Tancredo--standing on a stage of empty podiums--sums up the Republican party's commitment to civil rights in America: the only Republican interested is the guy running to deny immigrant workers their rights.
            </p>
            <p>
             One has to wonder why this photo was not the lead on every morning show and on the front pages of every morning newspaper in America.
            </p>
            <div>
             <a href="http://blogs.usatoday.com/onpolitics/2007/07/tancredo-gets-s.html">
              <img alt="Tancredo" src="http://tinyurl.com/yv8k7n" title="USA Today" width="430"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <p>
             The reason, most likely, is a coordinated effort by Republicans to pressure news agencies to downplay the obvious implications of having 8 out of 9 of their Presidential candidates as "no shows" for a debate at the NAACP.
            </p>
            <div style="margin: 10px; float: left;">
             <center>
              <a href="http://www.blogreaderproject.com/survey/271c4719fa777f7fd0fca674778987c2">
               Please take my
               <br/>
               Blog Reader
               <br/>
               Project survey.
              </a>
             </center>
            </div>
            <p>
             What is keeping the obvious story about Republicans and racism out of today's headlines? David Beckham's arrival in Hollywood.
            </p>
            <p>
             This is an ideal moment to stand up and demand the kind of news coverage we want in America. Contact your local TV stations and newspapers. Ask them to run this photo and to write an article about Republicans, civil rights and racism.
            </p>
           </div>
