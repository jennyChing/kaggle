     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blake_Fleming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Born in Alton, IL (birthplace of
      <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">
       Miles Davis
      </a>
      and home to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Pershing_Wadlow" title="Robert Pershing Wadlow">
       Robert Pershing Wadlow
      </a>
      , tallest man in recorded history) in 1972, Fleming started drumming at age 8, spending several years in fife and drumming corps, bagpipe bands, jazz bands, marching bands and orchestras.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      While in the Alton Colonial Fife and Drum Corps, Blake studied under Jerry Whitaker, the East Coast Rudimental Snare Drum Champion from West Point.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SUCO_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-SUCO-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Band_History">
       Band History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blake_Fleming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Band History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      At the age of 15, Fleming co-founded his first band,
      <a href="/wiki/Dazzling_Killmen" title="Dazzling Killmen">
       Dazzling Killmen
      </a>
      . The Killmen toured the US extensively, recording four 7 singles in addition to two full-length studio albums and one live album (Skin Graft/Touch and Go Records). They worked closely with Jeff Tweedy (Wilco, Uncle Tupelo) and Steve Albini (Nirvana, The Pixies, PJ Harvey) and shared the stage with
      <a href="/wiki/Fugazi" title="Fugazi">
       Fugazi
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/The_Jesus_Lizard" title="The Jesus Lizard">
       The Jesus Lizard
      </a>
      , Neurosis, Helmet,
      <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Tupelo" title="Uncle Tupelo">
       Uncle Tupelo
      </a>
      , Sleep, Shellac, Agent Orange,
      <a href="/wiki/Bad_Livers" title="Bad Livers">
       The Bad Livers
      </a>
      and Jim O'Rourke.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SUCO_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-SUCO-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Dazzling_Killmen" title="Dazzling Killmen">
       Dazzling Killmen
      </a>
      s 1994 sophomore studio album, Face of Collapse, was named the "number one heavy record of the decade by Alternative Press Magazine, the criteria for which included the dexterity of the rhythm section.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Killmen_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Killmen-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Fleming traversed the US with the Japanese progressive punk band
      <a href="/wiki/Zeni_Geva" title="Zeni Geva">
       Zeni Geva
      </a>
      on their 1996 Freedom Bondage tour. Later that year, he moved from St. Louis to New York City and formed the influential instrumental quartet
      <a href="/wiki/Laddio_Bolocko" title="Laddio Bolocko">
       Laddio Bolocko
      </a>
      . Laddio Bolocko independently recorded and produced three studio albums, and toured the US and Europe numerous times between 1998 and 2000, earning Fleming the reputation of being a master drummer both in the US and Europe.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SUCO_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-SUCO-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After Laddio disbanded the previous fall, Fleming moved to Long Beach, CA in March 2001 to co-found
      <a href="/wiki/The_Mars_Volta" title="The Mars Volta">
       The Mars Volta
      </a>
      , and recorded their very first demo recordings (The Haunt of Roulette Dares and Cicatriz ESP) for Grand Royal (the Beastie Boys record label).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TMVdemos_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-TMVdemos-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He also drummed on guitarist
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omar_Rodriguez-Lopez" title="Omar Rodriguez-Lopez">
       Omar Rodriguez-Lopez
      </a>
      's first solo album
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Manual_Dexterity:_Soundtrack_Volume_1" title="A Manual Dexterity: Soundtrack Volume 1">
       A Manual Dexterity: Soundtrack Volume 1
      </a>
      . Fleming then returned to New York City and, in 2002, formed Electric Turn To Me,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ETTM_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ETTM-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      who toured the US and Europe extensively before splitting up in 2005.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SUCO_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-SUCO-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Fleming briefly rejoined The Mars Volta after drummer Jon Theodore's departure in June 2006, playing a sold-out arena tour opening for the Red Hot Chili Peppers throughout the US and Canada. He also appeared with the band on the Henry Rollins Show on the Independent Film Channel.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rollins_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Rollins-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Upon Fleming's return to The Mars Volta in 2006, vocalist
      <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Bixler-Zavala" title="Cedric Bixler-Zavala">
       Cedric Bixler-Zavala
      </a>
      stated:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
      <tr>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
        I feel as if [former drummer]
        <a href="/wiki/Jon_Theodore" title="Jon Theodore">
         Jon Theodore
        </a>
        's drumming was based on the simplicity of, like,
        <a href="/wiki/John_Bonham" title="John Bonham">
         John Bonham
        </a>
        . As some kids were saying, its like Jon was more groove-oriented. Blake can be, too, but Blake likes to flip beats on you, and he likes to throw you off and have fun with it. He leans more toward [the style of]
        <a href="/wiki/Zach_Hill" title="Zach Hill">
         Zach Hill
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Hella_(band)" title="Hella (band)">
         Hella
        </a>
        's drummer. I find myself tapping my toes while Im singing to stay on top of the beat  figuring out how to [perform] and ignore the drums sometimes, and flow over it.
        <p>
         Blake is the father of most of The Mars Volta songs. Hes the one that came up with a lot of beats for us. Even on
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Frances_the_Mute" title="Frances the Mute">
           Frances the Mute
          </a>
         </i>
         , "
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%27Via_L%27Viaquez" title="L'Via L'Viaquez">
          L'Via L'Viaquez
         </a>
         " and certain parts [of the songs on the album], Blake Fleming beats [were taught to]
         <a href="/wiki/Jon_Theodore" title="Jon Theodore">
          Jon Theodore
         </a>
         . Sometimes [wed have] to not let Jon know that they were Blakes beats, because hed have a bit of a problem with that, since Blake was our first drummer and tracks like "Cicatriz ESP" and "Roulette Dares (The Haunt Of)" were Blake Fleming beats.
        </p>
       </td>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
      </tr>
     </table>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Studio_Work_.26_Collaborations">
       Studio Work &amp; Collaborations
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blake_Fleming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Studio Work &amp; Collaborations">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      After the Chili Peppers tour, Fleming returned to New York City, devoting his time to teaching and session work, with artists including Evan Dando (Lemonheads) and Gibby Haynes (Butthole Surfers),
      <a href="/wiki/The_Ropes" title="The Ropes">
       The Ropes
      </a>
      (Fleming drummed on their 2008 L.P. What They Do For Fun, which was #15 in the ABC News Top 50 Albums of 2008),
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Taylor_(musician)" title="Kim Taylor (musician)">
       Kim Taylor
      </a>
      (NPR Artist of the Month), Israel Nash Gripka, Chris Riffle,
      <a href="/wiki/The_Rollo_Treadway" title="The Rollo Treadway">
       The Rollo Treadway
      </a>
      , Father Divine, Martin Walker, Jimi Zhivago, Underground River, and Vajra.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lemonheads_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Lemonheads-8">
        <span>

     </p>
     <p>
      In 2009, Fleming worked with singer/songwriter Israel Nash Gripka, drumming on New York Town and The Gold Mine is Flooding.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IsraelNYT_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-IsraelNYT-15">

        </span>

         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Together, they toured the Netherlands and England (where the record New York Town was in the top 10 Americana charts for both countries), playing prestigious Americana festivals (including the Take Root and Roepaen festivals in the Netherlands), and recording TV and radio shows in Amsterdam and London. Their BBC Radio 2 taping was with the legendary
      <a href="/wiki/Bob_Harris_(radio)" title="Bob Harris (radio)">
       Bob Harris
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BobHarris_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BobHarris-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>

        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/The_Old_Grey_Whistle_Test" title="The Old Grey Whistle Test">
       The Old Grey Whistle Test
      </a>
      (a famous British music TV show from the 70's) and who has hosted everyone from John Lennon to Bob Marley. Also with Israel, Fleming appeared on Uncut Magazine's CD compilation (April 2010 issue) and on the Best of the Songwriter's Hall of Fame New Writers CD (2010).
     </p>
     <p>
      Fleming played on New York City singer/songwriter Chris Riffles 2010 debut album, Introducing..., which placed Chris on the 2011 Grammy nominee ballot for Best Contemporary Folk Album.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RiffleGrammy_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-RiffleGrammy-18">
        <span>

        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>

     </p>
     <p>
      In April 2011, Fleming gave a solo drum concert alongside live painters and a VJ in a multi-media performance at St. Peters Church in New York City.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StPeters_19-0">

      <a href="/wiki/Janet_Biggs" title="Janet Biggs">
       Janet Biggs
      </a>
      , composing soundtracks and performing live at art festivals and other installations. Their collaborations have been featured at the Art Basel Festival in Miami (where he performed solo drumset poolside with Olympic synchronized swimmers); The Gibbes Museum of Art (Charleston, SC); Solomon Projects (Atlanta, GA); Smack Mellon (Brooklyn, NY); The Johnson Museum (at Cornell University, Ithaca, NY); The Mint Museum of Art Permanent Collection (Charlotte, NC); International Video Art Fair (Barcelona, Spain); The European Media Art Festival (Osnabrueck, Germany); North Sea Film Festival for Underwater Movies (the Hague, Netherlands); and the Claire Oliver Gallery (New York, NY).
