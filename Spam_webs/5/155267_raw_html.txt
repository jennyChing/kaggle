      <p>
       <img alt="Bath Fizz" src="/Libraries/cc_blog_images_2012-13/Scarlet-Paolicchi_DIYHow-to-make-your-own-Bath-Fizz3_December2012.sflb.ashx" style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 15px; float: right; width: 400px; height: 300px;"/>
       Giving handmade gifts during the holiday season can reduce waste and save money. One of my favorite gifts to make and give is bath fizz. When placed in the tub, it releases soothing scents and oils, allowing you to lie back, relax and enjoy. After you learn how easy these are to make, you will want to create extras for yourself!
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        Ingredients
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       1 cup baking soda
       <br/>
       1/2 cup corn starch
       <br/>
       1/2 cup citric acid
       <br/>
       1 teaspoon coconut oil or light vegetable oil
       <br/>
       1 teaspoon of lavender essential oil, or your favorite fragrance
       <br/>
       Water in spray bottle
       <br/>
       Silicone mold in the shape of small balls or other desired shapes
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        <img alt="Mixing ingredients" src="/Libraries/cc_blog_images_2012-13/Scarlet-Paolicchi_DIYHow-to-make-your-own-Bath-Fizz2_December2012.sflb.ashx" style="float: left; margin-top: 5px; margin-right: 15px; margin-bottom: 10px;"/>
       </em>
       A note on the ingredients: Baking soda is known for its cleansing properties and will help to create the fizz. Citric acid is naturally found in citrus and can be purchased in a variety of places, including online and in the supplement sections of health and grocery stores. Coconut oil is a light oil known as a great skin moisturizer.
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        Instructions
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       1. Mix baking soda, corn starch and citric acid together with a whisk in a medium sized bowl until evenly distributed.
      </p>
      <p>
       2. Pour coconut oil and essential oil into a small jar and shake. Pour one teaspoon of oil mixture at a time into your dry mixture, mixing well as you go.
      </p>
      <p>
       3. Lightly spray water onto the ingredients and mix all ingredients together with hands. Add one spritz of water at a time, seven total, or until the ingredients are the consistency of wet sand starting to stick. If the mixture begins to fizz, there is too much water.
       <img alt="getting the right consistency" src="/Libraries/cc_blog_images_2012-13/Scarlet-Paolicchi_DIYHow-to-make-your-own-Bath-Fizz_December2012.sflb.ashx" style="float: right; margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 15px;"/>
       Keep mixing the contents with your hands to stop the fizzing. When the ingredients can hold a shape, it is ready to be molded. If you find that the shape is crumbling easily, add another spritz of water, mix and try again.
      </p>
      <p>
       4. Fill your silicone molds to the top and slightly press down. Make sure you dont over stuff the molds as it will be harder to remove the bath fizz. Leave molds to dry over night for a minimum of four hours.
      </p>
      <p>
       5. When dry, gently remove the shapes from their molds and place them
       <a href="http://www.ziploc.com/Products/Pages/ContainersSmartSnapSeal.aspx?SizeName=Medium Rectangle" target="_blank" title="Ziploc Brand Containers with the Smart Snap Seal">
        <img alt="ready for gifts" src="/Libraries/cc_blog_images_2012-13/Scarlet-Paolicchi_DIYHow-to-make-your-own-Bath-Fizz4_December2012.sflb.ashx" style="float: right; margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 15px;"/>
       </a>
       into
       <a href="http://www.ziploc.com/Products/Pages/ContainersSmartSnapSeal.aspx?SizeName=Medium Rectangle" title="Ziploc Brand Containers with the Smart Snap Seal.">
        Ziploc Brand Containers with the Smart Snap Seal.
       </a>
       Add a ribbon or bow and your gifts are ready!
      </p>
      <p>
       Note: If your shapes come apart, place back into a bowl and re-wet with a spritz of water. Restart the mixing and molding process, or save them for yourself. They will still fizz and make your bath smell wonderful.
      </p>
      <p>
       What are your favorite homemade holiday gifts?
