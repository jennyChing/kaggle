       <div class="post-content">
        <h1 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
         <a href="http://basseast.com/one-two-combo-for-catching-fall-bass/" title="One -Two Combo for Catching Fall Bass">
          One -Two Combo for Catching Fall Bass
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          One of the first moves that boxers learn is the combination punch, or the old one-two. The sequence of a left jab followed by a right cross is a basic fundamental for any prize fighter. Similarly, bass fisherman can quickly find and catch bass on lakes and reservoirs in the fall by using a simple horizontal and vertical presentation combo. Simply put, cover a lot of water with fast moving, horizontally swimming baits like topwaters, spinnerbaits and crankbaits.
          <span id="more-11085">
          </span>
          Once you get a few bites in an area, slow down and thoroughly fish the area with vertically falling lures like jigs and worms and youll often strike the mother lode.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the fall, bass are on the chase following shad and they can be located from the main lake to the very backs of creeks. Often, this location changes daily. Some days theyll be on deeper cover, while other days theyll be right up on the bank. Points, creek channels, and ledges can all be big producers in the fall, although frequently the bass will just be meandering over expansive flats. Fishing methodically with a worm would take hours to locate the fish, so I work fast from the mouth of creeks to the very back until I find them, then I slow down and try to catch every one of them in the area.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://basseast.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/comedero-8.5-lb-iii.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-11088" height="231" src="http://basseast.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/comedero-8.5-lb-iii-300x231.jpg" title="comedero 8.5 lb  iii" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Start by covering water fast with moving baits, like spinnerbaits, topwaters, and crankbaits. In the fall, use shad color schemes, primarily whites and chromes, and work these baits fast with erratic stop and go retrieves to trigger strikes from active fish. Since bass tend to key on the smaller young-of-the year shad, I typically downsize my baits to replicate the forage. Spinnerbaits are a natural anytime bass key on shad, so  and 3/8 oz sizes with white or chartreuse and white skirts and tandem silver willow leaf blades work well for me. Depending on the fishs mood, 3/8 oz white chatterbaits with a 3.5 Live Magic Shad trailer often produces better than a spinnerbait, especially for bigger fish. Topwaters are normally thought of as low light baits, but they frequently work all day long in the fall because bass are chasing shad to the surface. I prefer small poppers on calm days, while the walking action of Sammy type baits work better when there is more chop. My favorite bait is the Lucky Craft Gunfish 95. This bait doubles as a popper with a stop and go retrieve, or you can walk the dog with it. Try a variety of retrieves with the Gunfish and let the fish tell you how they want it. Long casts spook less fish and also allow you to cover more water, but that can sometimes be hard with lighter topwater baits. I rig these on the Dobyns 704CB Glass rod with 15 lb mono line and I can whip even the smallest poppers a mile, plus the slower action of glass rods let them take the bait deeply for better hookups. Finally, my favorite search baits are shallow to medium running crankbaits, especially if there is wood, rock, or weed cover. With a variety of cranks and line sizes, I can cover water from 1 to the deep weed edge in 12. Little square billed cranks, like Lucky Crafts LC 1.5 or the BDS 3 are my favorites and work great in about 4 or less. Ill also try medium runners like the Skeet Mini MR and the LC 1.5 DD to probe the deep weed edge. Of course, dont forget to try the Lucky Craft RTO 50 and other lipless crankbaits if the fish are chasing shad in the fall. Remember with all of the cranks that erratic action triggers strikes, so snap these baits free of grass, run them into rocks and wood, and momentarily pause during the retrieve or youll pass up a lot of fish.
         </p>
         <p>
          Finding fish is the hard part. Once a school is located with moving baits and the action slows, switch to soft plastics and youll likely catch more fish from the same area, possibly a lot more. My favorite soft plastic rigs for the fall are wacky rigs and weightless soft plastic jerkbaits. For the wacky rig, I use Hyper Finesse Worms in shades of watermelon on sunny days and June bug or green pumpkin colored ones on cloudy days. The segmented tail on this bait really quivers with every twitch of the rod when hooked wacky style and the slow fall triggers many strikes. Some days they hit on the fall, others it will be more when sitting still on the bottom, so experiment until you find what works best that day. Another technique that excels is soft plastic jerkbaits like Magic Shads, Hyper Sticks and Live Magic Shads, rigged weightless Texas style. Just like the wacky rig, some days the bass will chase these as they are steadily twitched over the grass, while other times youll do best by twitching your bait a couple times and then letting it fall to the bottom. Long casts are important with these baits as well, so I use a 73 Dobyns 733C to launch them way out there &amp; still have enough backbone to set the hook. Finally, for lunker bass during the fall, pitch a weedless jig like the MPack jig from Lake Fork Trophy Lures to the heaviest cover you can find in the areas youve already caught fish. I like black/blue colored jigs on cloudy days and watermelon jigs on sunny days, trimmed with a matching Lake Fork Craw trailer. You wont catch a ton of fish on the jig, but theyll likely be the biggest fish of the day.
         </p>
         <p>
          To land a haymaker on the bass this fall, try the horizontal-vertical combo and you might become the bass heavyweight champ. Heres hoping you catch the lunker of your dreams. If I can be of assistance, please contact me at 214-683-9572 or e-mail me through my website,
          <a href="http://www.LakeForkGuideTrips.com">
           www.LakeForkGuideTrips.com
          </a>
          . By Tom Redington
         </p>
         <p>
          Tom Redington is a full time bass guide on Lake Fork &amp; a FLW Tour pro. He is sponsored by Lake Fork Trophy Lures, Dobyns Rods, Ranger Boats, Mercury, Diamond Sports Marine, Lucky Craft, Costa Sunglasses, Lowrance, Navionics, &amp; Power Pole.
         </p>
