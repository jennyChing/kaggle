        <h3>
         The 10 Highest Grossing Box Office Stars Who Have Never Made a Sequel
        </h3>
       </a>
       <p class="posted">
        By
        <a href="http://www.pajiba.com/staff/dustin-rowles-1.php">
         Dustin Rowles
        </a>
        |
        <font color="#a33735">
         <a href="http://www.pajiba.com/box_office_round-ups/">
          Box Office Round-Ups
         </a>
        </font>
        | August  4, 2013 |
        <a href="http://www.pajiba.com/box_office_round-ups/the-10-highest-grossing-box-office-stars-who-have-never-made-a-sequel.php#comments">
         Comments (
         <script src="http://disqus.com/forums/jiba/get_num_replies_for_entry.js?url=http://www.pajiba.com/box_office_round-ups/the-10-highest-grossing-box-office-stars-who-have-never-made-a-sequel.php" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
         <noscript>
          View
         </noscript>
         )
        </a>
       </p>
       <hr color="#dddddd" size="1" width="600"/>
       <img alt="Denzel-Washingto.jpg" class="mt-image-none" src="http://cdnimages.pajiba.com/assets_c/2013/08/Denzel-Washingto-thumb-550x356-75959.jpg"/>
       <!-- Go to www.addthis.com/dashboard to customize your tools -->
       <div class="addthis_responsive_sharing">
       </div>
       <p>
        Denzel Washington and Mark Wahlberg, both of whom make the list of the highest grossing Hollywood stars never to have made a sequel, debuted at the top of the box office this weekend with
        <i>
         2 Guns
        </i>
        , which delivered a decent $27 million for the $61 million film (that is apparently slightly below expectations, but it's also in line with Denzel's recent history of opening weekends). I
        <i>
         love
        </i>
        Denzel, as I made clear
        <a href="http://www.pajiba.com/film_reviews/2-guns-is-the-denzeliest-goddamn-movie-of-the-year.php">
         here
        </a>
        , and part of the reason I like him so much is that he doesn't sell out to franchises. He makes Denzel movies (as opposed to Wahlberg, who I also like, but who has
        <i>
         tried
        </i>
        , to no avail, to make franchises in
        <i>
         Max Payne
        </i>
        , the
        <i>
         Planet of the Apes
        </i>
        reboot).
       </p>
       <p>
        Wahlberg, in fact, won't be on this list much longer, now that
        <i>
         Ted 2
        </i>
        has been greenlit, and now that he's agreed to be in
        <i>
         Transformers 4
        </i>
        , double disqualifying him. But for now, he still hasn't made a sequel, believe it or not.  It's difficult, in fact, to find big box office stars without sequels on their resume; number ten on this list is actually number 138th on the overall box-office list, as ensemble films like the
        <i>
         Oceans
        </i>
        movies,
        <i>
         Lord of the Rings
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         Harry Potter
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         Night at the Museum
        </i>
        , or one of the many superhero movies have disqualified the first 101 people on the highest box-office grossing actors. I also didn't include voice roles or cameos in the calculation, while I
        <i>
         did
        </i>
        disqualify two who would've otherwise made the list -- Woody Harrelson and Stanley Tucci -- because they not only have sequels coming out very soon (
        <i>
         Catching Fire
        </i>
        ) but because the original installment in those franchises are the only reason they are on the list. I didn't really want to because it's not the kind of cash-in sequel that made him a box-office star, but I also disqualified Jack Nicholson, too, for the
        <i>
         Two Jakes
        </i>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Here are the 10 highest grossing box office stars who do not include a sequel in their filmography (although, in most cases, reboots, remakes, and adaptations figure heavily in their box-office tallies).
       </p>
       <p>
        (
        <i>
         Update: As a couple people who kind of miss the point of the list -- which is to say, it's about actors who have amassed box-office tallies because of sequels -- have pointed out, Leo was actually in
         <i>
          Critters 3
         </i>
         . However, that never had a theatrical release, and thus does not count in his box-office tally, and for the purposes/point of this list, it is not included. But yes! You are right. He was in Critters 3. You win the Internet, you incredibly smart person, you.
        </i>
        )
       </p>
       <p>
        1.
        <strong>
         Leonardo DiCaprio
        </strong>
        -- $2.2 billion
       </p>
       <p>
        2.
        <strong>
         Denzel Washington
        </strong>
        -- $2.08 billion
       </p>
       <p>
        3.
        <strong>
         Mark Wahlberg
        </strong>
        -- $1.86 (soon to be disqualified by
        <i>
         Ted 2
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         Transformers 4
        </i>
        )
       </p>
       <p>
        3.
        <strong>
         Alec Baldwin
        </strong>
        -- $1.78 billion
       </p>
       <p>
        5.
        <strong>
         Meryl Streep
        </strong>
        -- $1.76 billion
       </p>
       <p>
        6.
        <strong>
         Kevin Costner
        </strong>
        -- $1.73 billion
       </p>
       <p>
        7.
        <strong>
         Ben Affleck
        </strong>
        -- $1.65 billion
       </p>
       <p>
        8.
        <strong>
         Russell Crowe
        </strong>
        -- $1.55 billion
       </p>
       <p>
        9.
        <b>
         Jamie Foxx
        </b>
        -- $1.54 billion (will soon be disqualified by
        <i>
         The Amazing Spider-Man 2
        </i>
        )
       </p>
       <p>
        10.
        <b>
         Richard Gere
        </b>
        -- $1.37 billion
       </p>
