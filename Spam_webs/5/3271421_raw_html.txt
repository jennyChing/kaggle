         <h2 class="title">
          These 25 Creepy Insects Could Be Crawling Around In Your Home Right NOW
         </h2>
         <header class="details clearfix">
          <time class="detail left index-post-date" datetime="2014-12-14">
           Dec 14, 2014
          </time>
          <span class="detail left index-post-author">

         <div class="single-content">
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="code-block code-block-2" style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
           <div align="center">

          <p>
           Everyone has at least one fear in life, be it public speaking, ghosts, or even dying alone. However, the fear of insects is one of the most common fears amongst humans. Insectsare everywhere in the world and outnumber us by a large margin. Youll unfortunately have to encounter these creepy-crawlies at one point or another, and there are certain few that you would definitely not want in your house. Theinsects below are deadly, plentiful, and an annoyance. They are the kind of insectsthat you would never want to encounter, but could still be fairlyclose to home.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           1.) Giant Huntsman Spider
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="1.) Giant Huntsman Spider" src="http://i.imgur.com/yIhtTIs.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <div class="code-block code-block-3" style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
           <div align="center">
            <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
            </script>
            <!-- buzzvirals-content2-responsive -->
            <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-4390703503842584" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="6092486551" style="display:block">
            </ins>
            <script>
             (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
            </script>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           With eight eyes, eight legs, and all sorts of scary, the giant huntsman spider can reach a leg span of about 10-12 inches.
          </p>
          <h2>
           2.) Africanized Bees
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="2.) Africanized Bees" src="http://i.imgur.com/3GzZfZr.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you find one of these guys in your bed, you probably have a much bigger problem, since there is likely an entire hive in your house. These bees are insanely aggressive and will attempt to kill anything that they want to.
          </p>
          <h2>
           3.) Tsetse Fly
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="3.) Tsetse Fly" src="http://i.imgur.com/H0hQKaA.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This fly is a known carrier of the sleeping sickness, which is fatal to humans. Luckily, if its caught early enough, it can be treated with modern medicine.
          </p>
          <h2>
           4.) Japanese Mountain Leeches
          </h2>
          <div class="code-block code-block-4" style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
           <div align="center">
            <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
            </script>
            <!-- buzzvirals-content3-midarticle -->
            <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-4390703503842584" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="9634062154" style="display:block">
            </ins>
            <script>
             (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
            </script>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           <img alt="4.) Japanese Mountain Leeches" src="http://i.imgur.com/0GX5GWA.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           What isnt there to fear about this leech? It can live comfortably on dry land, is pretty large, and wants to suck your blood.
          </p>
          <h2>
           5.) Milkweed Grasshopper
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="5.) Milkweed Grasshopper" src="http://i.imgur.com/LHqIEar.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you find one of these around your house, do your best to keep it calm. When this bug feels threatened, it shoots out toxic liquids from its legs.
          </p>
          <h2>
           6.) Thorny Devil Stick Insect
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="6.) Thorny Devil Stick Insect" src="http://i.imgur.com/m9iO0g5.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This insect is usually harmless to humans, but it doesnt negate its creepy appearance. It can grow up to 6 inches.
          </p>
          <h2>
           7.) Giant African Millipede
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="7.) Giant African Millipede" src="http://i.imgur.com/zG99Jq7.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The giant African millipede is one of the largest millipedes in existence. It grows up to 15.2 inches and sports 256 legs.
          </p>
          <h2>
           8.) Giant Centipede
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="8.) Giant Centipede" src="http://i.imgur.com/Z5lZiS3.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This creepy crawly can grow up to 12 inches, which would certainly put a scare in you if you saw it in bed.
          </p>
          <h2>
           9.) American Cockroach
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="9.) American Cockroach" src="http://i.imgur.com/kA1Glyf.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The worst part about these bugs is that if you see one, there are thousands of other ones to follow. No one wants that.
          </p>
          <h2>
           10.) Japanese Giant Crab
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="10.) Japanese Giant Crab" src="http://i.imgur.com/ArgxN2u.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           With the largest leg span of any arthropod, the Japanese giant crab is a disturbingly big. It reaches up to 12 feet and weighs up to 42 pounds. (Okay, so maybe youll never find this one in your own house.)
          </p>
          <h2>
           11.) Asian Giant Hornet
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="11.) Asian Giant Hornet" src="http://i.imgur.com/OYIL6c9.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This is the worlds largest hornet. As you can imagine, this wasp has a particularly painful sting described as a
          </p>
          <h2>
           12.) Hercules Beetle
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="12.) Hercules Beetle" src="http://i.imgur.com/8GNBHh9.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This beetle can lift up to 850 times its body weight. Yes, I think Hercules is a good name for it.
          </p>
          <h2>
           13.) Giant African Land Snail
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="13.) Giant African Land Snail" src="http://i.imgur.com/Hvu37WM.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The creature is considered to be the most damaging snail in the world because it consumes at least 500 different types of plants. Its a slimy, gelatinous, slider that can reach up to 8 inches in length and 4 inches in diameter.
          </p>
          <h2>
           14.) Human Botfly
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="14.) Human Botfly" src="http://i.imgur.com/DA792zM.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Human Botfly is known for laying eggs within peoples skin. Thats not what youd call a good house guest.
          </p>
          <h2>
           15.) Bullet Ant
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="15.) Bullet Ant" src="http://i.imgur.com/GCEdt4y.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Bullet Ant has a bite so fierce that survivors of it say it feels like they were shot. You dont want this thing anywhere near you.
          </p>
          <h2>
           16.) Giant Water Bug
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="16.) Giant Water Bug" src="http://i.imgur.com/7sIVL4N.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           These guys are known to have quite a painful bite. Legend has it that they eat water snakes and baby turtles.
          </p>
          <h2>
           17.) Male Dobsonfly
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="17.) Male Dobsonfly" src="http://i.imgur.com/vMvw2HM.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Just look at it. No thank you.
          </p>
          <h2>
           18.) Mosquito
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="18.) Mosquito" src="http://i.imgur.com/jwiskBd.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           These guys are known carriers of many diseases, and are quite common. Most people have encounters with them through their lives. Plus, their bites itch a lot.
          </p>
          <h2>
           19.) Camel Spider
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="19.) Camel Spider" src="http://i.imgur.com/lMP1aJu.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Camel Spider, which isnt even technically a spider, could give anyone nightmares. It has 10 legs, can grow to about 6 inches, and has jaws that weigh around 1/3 their body mass.
          </p>
          <h2>
           20.) Brazilian Wandering Spider
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="20.) Brazilian Wandering Spider" src="http://i.imgur.com/gghyvne.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Better hope this thing doesnt wander into your house. This is the worlds most venomous spider.
          </p>
          <h2>
           21.) Giant Weta
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="21.) Giant Weta" src="http://i.imgur.com/OD4He2C.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This enormous grasshopper is endemic to New Zealand and can grow anywhere between 4 inches to 8 inches, depending on the species.
          </p>
          <h2>
           22.) Giant Isopod
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="22.) Giant Isopod" src="http://i.imgur.com/2FGFFPt.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I suppose people dont like insects that have giant in their name. While most Isopods are generally pretty small, a Giant Isopod can grow to nightmarish sizes.
          </p>
          <h2>
           23.) House Centipede
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="23.) House Centipede" src="http://i.imgur.com/TcrWkdn.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This 15 legged nightmare is harmless to humans, but would certainly send chills up a persons spine if you felt it crawling up your leg.
          </p>
          <h2>
           24.) Black Widow Spider
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="24.) Black Widow Spider" src="http://i.imgur.com/9ifoIVp.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I think everyone knows about the dangerous Black Widow. Theyre small but extremely venomous, and could potentially kill you.
          </p>
          <h2>
           25.) Bed Bugs
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="25.) Bed Bugs" src="http://i.imgur.com/pMNsX1m.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           You certainly dont want Bed Bugs in your bed. They feed on your blood and reproduce faster than you imagine (200-250 eggs in 6-10 days).
          </p>
          <p>
           I think nows the best time to check under your bed, under your covers, and in your closet for any of these creepy insects.These intense creepy crawlers probably arent hiding at the foot of your bed, under your sheets, and just waiting to bite you.
           <em>
            Probably
           </em>
           .
          </p>
