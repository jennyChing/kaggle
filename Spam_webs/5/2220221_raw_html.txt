      <h2 class="post-title heading-font">
       No Painting, No Special Effects! The Fly Geyser in Nevada
      </h2>
     </div>
     <div class="post-page-content">
      <div class="post-page-thumbnail">
       <img alt="fly-geyser-nevada" class="post-thumbnail wp-post-image wp-post-image" height="250" src="http://cdn.hispotion.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/fly-geyser-nevada.jpg" width="320"/>
      </div>
      <div class="wptouch-ad ad-location-pre_content">
       <!-- HisPoMo responsive up -->
       <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-4576599867562592" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="6126752377" style="display:block">

      <p>
       [note_box]The Fly Geyser, also known as Fly Ranch Geyser, is a small man-made geothermal geyser, located in Washoe County, Nevada, and besides spitting water up to 5 feet into the air, it strikes you with beautiful vibrant colors, making the whole landscape look like a painting. The geyser actually developed by mistake from a leaking geothermal well, which was first drilled back in 1916. Sometime in the 1960s the geothermally heated water found a crack and erupted. It has been spewing gushes of hot water ever since.
      </p>
      <p>
       The colored rock itself has been growing and forming from the dissolved minerals, which continue accumulating and thus shape the travertine mound around the geyser. The beautiful colors form due to the thermophilic algae. As the geyser is located on a private ranch and has its owner, its hidden from the viewers behind a high fence and a locked gate with spikes on top of it.[/note_box]
       <br/>
