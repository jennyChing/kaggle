       <h3 class="entry-title">
        <a href="https://blogs.oracle.com/cx/entry/it_s_time_to_get" title="permalink">
         Its Time to Get Omni-Geo: Applying Omnichannel CX Rules to International Selling
        </a>
       </h3>
       <h4 class="entry-meta">
        By Christine Bardwell | Commerce Product Strategy -Oracle on
        <a href="#">
         Jul 09, 2015
        </a>
       </h4>
       <div class="entry-body">
        <font face="verdana,arial,helvetica,sans-serif" size="2">
         eCommerce
        </font>
        <span style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
         growth in the more mature European markets has plateaued and many brands are looking to new country markets to achieve continued sales growth. But dealing with customer expectations becomes trickier as brands expand internationally.
        </span>
        <font face="verdana,arial,helvetica,sans-serif" size="2">
         The headaches around offering shoppers a seamless, omnichannel experience on home territory simply multiply with each new country market.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Shoppers wont understand if a product isnt available to them when its available to customers in another country. The same is true of promotions, and delivery options. Shoppers may expect to place an order using a money-off coupon in one country and collect from a store in another country, while adding points to their loyalty card. They wont understand why thats not possible 
         <i>
          its all through the same brand after all.
         </i>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Some brands try to tackle this issue by not allowing customers to see other country websites at all by geo-blocking, i.e. blocking access to websites based on location, or diverting the page back to a local browser - a practice which only further frustrates consumers.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <u>
          So why dont all the rules that apply to omnichannel  such as brand consistency, service offerings and stock consistency  also apply across geographical borders too
         </u>
         ?
        </font>
        <p>
        </p>

        <p class=" ">
         <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          The Oracle Service Cloud
team has been thinking a lot about whats most relevant to the people we are
serving. What are the day-to-day
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NFbPutrU0YA">
           challenges
          </a>
          folks on the
front line helping customers solve their service issues are facing? What are the
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NFbPutrU0YA">
           trends
          </a>
          that continue to
impact how organizations deliver
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NFbPutrU0YA">
           customer service
          </a>
          ? Below are a few
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NFbPutrU0YA">
           shifts
          </a>
          in the customer
service landscape that are disrupting business as usual. These
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NFbPutrU0YA">
           trends
          </a>
          cant be ignored byany global, modern customerservice
organization:
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
           <b>
            The
     usage of mobile devices
           </b>
           <b>
            continues to disrupt and
     drive changes in consumerattitudes and behaviors.
           </b>
           Organizations
     should respond (if they havent already) and be ready for all things
     mobile.
          </font>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
           <b>
            Knowledge
     needs to be the foundation of all service channels and engagements.
           </b>
           Modern
     customer service teams simply cannotbe successful without a single
     knowledge base that underpins both self-service and assisted service
     channels.As customer preferences tilt toward
     web-basedself-service (both Gartner and
           <a href="https://www.forrester.com/Brief+Focus+On+Three+New+Service+Experiences+To+Thrive+In+The+Age+Of+The+Digital+Customer/fulltext/-/E-RES121061">
            Forrester
           </a>
           now state the web has surpassed voice as the most common customer support
     channel),easy access to knowledge andfindabilityis
     becoming a key responsibility of customer service leaders.
          </font>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
           <b>
            Customer
     Service will increasingly include machines talking to machines
           </b>
           (as opposed
     to humans talking to humans). Soon there will be 20 billion devices
     connected to the internet. A few years back the concept of leveraging theInternet
     of Things (IoT) for Customer Service wasstill in its infancy. Today
     those initial barriers are gone and Service with IOT is ready for
     mainstream.
          </font>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
           <b>
            Customer
     Service will continue to extend from engagement centers to other functions
     in the enterprise.
           </b>
           Organizations are increasingly accepting that service
     is a key component during the entire customer journeyand part of
     this realization move is connecting the service function with functions
     (and systems, platforms, tools) in other parts of the organization. This
     way the journey from that first brand touch point all the way to the technician
     standing in your living room is supported by one consistent service
     platform.
          </font>
         </li>
        </ul>

challenges into opportunities to deliver better service!

        <a href="https://blogs.oracle.com/cx/entry/in_sales_or_with_fitbit" title="permalink">
         In Sales or with Fitbit, You Cant Manage What You Cant Measure by Greg Swender
        </a>
       </h3>
       <h4 class="entry-meta">
        By Emily Creech-Oracle on
        <a href="#">
         Jul 06, 2015
        </a>
       </h4>
       <div class="entry-body">
        <p>
         My wife bought me a Fitbit bracelet for my birthday. My experience has been really positive so far. The iPhone app is easy to setup, and tracking meals, calories and exercise is simple. The Fitbit has also shaped my behavior. I have transitioned to baked chips for lunch, plain bagels with no cream cheese (most of the time), and hold the mayo on my sandwiches. It has also pushed me to take the stairs more often and walk more frequently to hit my daily distance goal. These are all good things. On the other hand, tracking sleep habits has proven slightly challengingonly because the tapping motion to put the bit into sleep mode is awkward and honestly the routine really irritates my wife. Picture this at bedtime: tap wrist repeatedly, but device fails to sleep REPEAT (this time with harder/louder taps) nope REPEAT you get the idea.
        </p>
        <p>
         Nighttime tapping aside, this simple device and app show the amazing power of two things: metrics and user adoption.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <b>
           Metrics
          </b>
          : The daily and weekly report in the app that make my eating habits and exercise very visible, along with any improvements or decline over time vs. my goals.
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           User Adoption
          </b>
          : Did I wear the device today? Did I enter my meals today? If either answer is no, I havent adopted the tool and theres no hope it will help me.
         </li>
        </ul>

        </p>

       <h3 class="entry-title">
        <a href="https://blogs.oracle.com/cx/entry/the_top_tech_trends_impacting" title="permalink">
         The Top Tech Trends Impacting the Future of Field Service Management by Stephen Fioretti
        </a>
       </h3>
       <h4 class="entry-meta">
        By Christine Friscic-Oracle on
        <a href="#">
         Jun 29, 2015
        </a>
       </h4>
       <div class="entry-body">
        <!--EndFragment-->
        <p class="normal">
         <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 115%;">
           Since the Oracle
Service Cloud added field service management to its portfolio of customer
experience
          </span>
          solutions through the acquisition of TOA Technologies, the leader
in cloud-based field service management solutions, Ive had the opportunity to
see firsthand how modern field service solutions have changed the way people
think about this space. Over the past several years, field service management
has transformed from being a cumbersome but necessary part of the business into
a key way for businesses to drive value, increasing operational efficiencies
and leveraging important face-to-face interactions with customers to improve
their experience with an organization.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="normal">
         <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          And whats really exciting about this market is that it has the
potential to continue transforming as new advancements and trends in the
technology world make their way into the field service space. Advancements such
as cloud services and trends like the Internet of Things are transforming the
way organizations manage their field teams. Field service operations are
becoming increasingly connected  field technicians have access to their peers,
managers and critical information in real-time via mobile devices. Furthermore,
machines are talking directly to field service teams as well as backend systems
via
          <a href="https://blogs.oracle.com/cx/entry/rise_of_machine_to_machine">
           machine-to-machine
communication
          </a>
          (M2M), speeding the identification of issues and ultimately
problem resolution.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="normal">
         <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          In a recent article that I authored for Field Technologies
magazine, I discuss a few of the trends that will power transformation in the
field service market. The article, titled 
          <a href="http://www.fieldtechnologiesonline.com/doc/creating-the-super-connected-field-service-ecosystem-of-the-future-0001">
           <i>
            Creating the Super-Connected Field Service Ecosystem
of the Future
           </i>
          </a>
          , highlights some of the trends in technology that I believe
will have the biggest impact of the future of field service. These include:
          <br/>
         </font>
        </p>
        <div style="text-indent: 0px;">
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
            <b style="text-indent: -0.25in;">
             Cloud services:
            </b>
            <span style="text-indent: -0.25in;">
             While already having a significant impact on the field service space, cloud
services will continue to drive important changes, removing silos and allowing
systems to work together fluidly.
            </span>
           </font>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
            <b style="text-indent: -0.25in;">
             Internet of
Things:
            </b>
            <span style="text-indent: -0.25in;">
             What we call the Internet of Smart Things will become the norm.
This will really accelerate field service because the IoST will allow field
organizations to not only respond to issues faster, but it will allow them to
respond smarter
            </span>
            <i style="text-indent: -0.25in;">
             by identifying potential
causes before a technician is dispatched
            </i>
            <span style="text-indent: -0.25in;">
             .
            </span>
           </font>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
            <b style="text-indent: -0.25in;">
             Augmented
reality:
            </b>
            <span style="text-indent: -0.25in;">
             I am very excited to see where this trend takes the field service
market. This is one trend that will significantly improve day-to-day field
service work, especially when leveraged with wearables. Applied in field
service, augmented reality could enable field service employees to overlay
schematics across his field of vision via augmented reality-enabled glasses, so
he can see exactly what needs to be done on the piece of equipment he is
working on.
            </span>
           </font>
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="normal">
         <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          The Oracle Service Cloud team is excited to welcome a modern
field service solution, Oracle Field Service Cloud, into our product family,
and help our customers extend the customer service they provide all the way to
the field. Now, Oracle Service Cloud customers can deliver a consistent,
exceptional customer experience across all points of the service cycle
from web self service, to the contact center, to engagement via social
channels and live chat and all the way to what is often a companys only
face-to-face interaction with their customers, a field service appointment.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="normal">
         <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          And, as the field service space continues to evolve with the
latest technology trends, organizations will be able to deliver an increasingly
seamless and smarter customer experience,.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="normal">
         <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          Learn more about the latest technology trends that are
impacting the future of the field service industry in my recent
          <a href="http://www.fieldtechnologiesonline.com/doc/creating-the-super-connected-field-service-ecosystem-of-the-future-0001">
           article
for Field Technologies magazine
          </a>
          , and
discover how
          <a href="https://www.oracle.com/applications/customer-experience/service/field-service-management/index.html">
           Oracle
Field Service Cloud
          </a>
          can help your business power operational efficiencies
and improve customer satisfaction.
         </font>
        </p>

       <h4 class="entry-meta">

       <div class="entry-body">
        <p>
         When making a purchasing decision customers need to feel confident that 
what theyre buying is going to meet their specific needs. A proven way 
to build that confidence is by providing a personal sales experience. 
Unfortunately, tailoring a personalized solution for each deal takes 
time. The sales rep has to understand the requirements and find the 
product set that best matches those needs, determine the highest price 
the customer will accept, and present that solution to the customer. 
Each of these steps is an opportunity for sub-optimal choices by the 
sales rep and may require multiple revisions. Thats why the products 
off the shelf are often cheaper than the tailored solutions; the seller 
passes the cost of customization on to the customer. But if companies 
can deliver the experience of a tailored solution with the efficiency of
 a standard solution, they stand to differentiate themselves from their 
competitors.

         Visual Commerce  Content + Commerce Evolved
        </a>
       </h3>
       <h4 class="entry-meta">
        By Jeri Kelley -  Commerce Product Strategy-Oracle on
        <a href="#">

        </a>
       </h4>
       <div class="entry-body">
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="font-size: 12px; line-height: 16px;">
         <font face="verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
          Unlike a lot of folks
          <a href="http://www.nielsen.com/us/en/insights/news/2014/millennials-prefer-cities-to-suburbs-subways-to-driveways.html">
           around my age
          </a>
          who are migrating toward city living and fighting a move to the suburbs, my husband and I recently took the opposite approach.With a toddler, a massive dog, and a baby on the way, we made the decision to pick up our life in Boston and move to a more laid-back (read: less expensive) place to call home - Montana.There have been
          <i>
           many
          </i>
          positives to the move, but one major downside is the lack of local retail options Id become accustomed to in Boston.Ive always been a big fan of shopping online for items such as clothes and make-up but I was use to being able to go into a store to touch and feel bigger purchases  like furniture, sporting equipment, etc.
         </font>
        </p>

        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="font-size: 12px; line-height: 16px;">
         <span style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">

         </span>
         <u style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          big
         </u>
         <span style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          purchases  everything from cabinets to light fixtures to furniture.How was I going to do this with the limited options of physical retailers in my area?Luckily, a lot of brands are taking the next step with their online sites to improve the experience, but I was surprised by how many brands still dont incorporate anything more than standard product shots to help shoppers make decisions.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="font-size: 12px; line-height: 16px;">
         <span style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          In my quest for the perfect new purchases for my house I started thinking a lot about the types of experiences brands are offering to bring their products to their customers so they can
         </span>
         <b style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          visualize
         </b>
         <span style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          how the product looks, feels, and functions while shopping online.I want to see what that new couch will look like in my living room, know how the motion activated on/off feature of the facet worksyou get the picture.The term
         </span>
         <b style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          visual commerce
         </b>
         <span style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          is used often to describe this type of content or experience but what does that actually mean?
         </span>
        </p>

        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           SaaS. Cloud. On-demand.
On-premise. Managed Services. Hosted. Multitenant. Single tenant. Public cloud.
Private cloud. Hybrid cloud.
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           We are surrounded by these
terms, but if you
           <i>
            really
           </i>
           know the differences, you are part of a
seriously small minority. So when
           <a href="http://www.marketwatch.com/story/online-retailers-expect-to-increase-revenue-by-17-percent-in-2015-2015-04-22">
            <span style="color: #420178;">
             half
            </span>
           </a>
           of retailers say they
           <i>
            want
           </i>
           to
go cloud in the next year in commerce, whatdo
theymean?And from a product perspective, whats the right fit?
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           Software has been
           <a href="http://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424053111903480904576512250915629460">
            <span style="color: #420178;">
             eating
            </span>
           </a>
           the world for years now, and Software
as a Service (SaaS), hosted in The Cloud makes it all easier, faster, cheaper.
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           In an effort to be ready for
the SaaS boom, SaaS and cloud in the commerce space have become
interchangeable. But good messaging and subscription-like finance terms does
not define a SaaS product.
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           And software doesnt just
live a singular Cloud, there are many clouds, and not all clouds are created
equal. Public clouds, private clouds, hybrid clouds all vary in security,
scale, performance, capabilities, and hardware depending on provider.
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           From a product perspective,
heres what "cloud" can mean today:
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 0.5in; text-indent: -0.5in; line-height: 150%;">
         <!--[if !supportLists]-->
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
           </span>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 0.5in; text-indent: -0.5in; line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
           </span>
           
           <span style="line-height: normal; font-family: 'Times New Roman';">
           </span>
          </span>
          <b>
           <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
            SaaS
           </span>
          </b>
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           (multitenant) delivers upon the promise of commerce in The Cloud: zero
infrastructure or upgrade management, housed in a public cloud/data center on a
server with other storefronts (multitenancy) to take advantage of automatic
push upgrades, elastic scaling, and shared cost. True SaaS is appealing as it
requires no CapEx budget, implementations are typically less expensive, and
customers are typically billed via monthly subscription.Todays SaaS
commerce solutions offer slick tooling and beautiful templates, but not the
brand control, features, or proven scale for growth needed by marquee brands (or
those aspiring to be). For these vendors, in order to push a single release of software without breaking sites, the ability
to customize each storefront is limited  leaving many sites looking the same. Questions about infrastructure and
scalability raise concerns with performance and security.
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 0.5in; text-indent: -0.5in; line-height: 150%;">
         <!--[if !supportLists]-->
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
           </span>
           
           <span style="line-height: normal; font-family: 'Times New Roman';">
           </span>
          </span>
          <b>
           <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
            On-demand / hosted
solutions
           </span>
          </b>
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           (single tenant / private cloud) can
simply be an on-premise technology (or an on-premise platform re-architected
for the cloud) deployed on a private server on or off site. It can also be a
hosted in a private or hybrid cloud at a commerce vendor or hosting provider,
and may feature a full or limited feature set of on-premise software that can
be customized. On-demand / hosted deployments typically require moderate CapEx
budget, and billing models vary.While these solutions offer benefits of
multi and single tenancy, they are not SaaS products. An IT staff may not bemaintaining the deployment on-site 
but someone somewhere is still maintaining it, and someone is paying for that maintenance. Longer custom deployments
and upgrade management still leave much to be desired in terms of true agility with on-demand / hosted cloud solutions.
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then, there's:
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 0.5in; text-indent: -0.5in; line-height: 150%;">
         <!--[if !supportLists]-->
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;">
           </span>
           
           <span style="line-height: normal; font-family: 'Times New Roman';">
           </span>
          </span>
          <!--[endif]-->
          <b>
           <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
            On-premise
           </span>
          </b>
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           (single tenant, on-site, private) ensures total customization  and
everything that comes with ownership from a cost perspective. Many merchants
welcome this approach, as it is the only option that can meet their
requirements. They may look to the cloud  or move elements of their deployment
to the cloud  but they see no other realistic option for their core site.
Requires more substantial CapEx budget, billing models vary.
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           Today, the reality is that
there is no perfect SaaS or cloud solution. Many merchants happily compromise
because they have to  whether its scale, growth, control, customization, or
cost. Some are left with a dilemma: do I lower my management and infrastructure
costs, or do I make sacrifices with the customer experience?
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           Complex commerce
organizations craving total control and customization wont get what they need
from a SaaS or on-demand solution. Growing organizations with limited resources
kick the can down the road until their SaaS solution becomes restrictive to the
brand experience, and cost prohibitive. On-demand users can say they are
cloud, but are still left wanting more in terms of agility and cost savings.
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           What if there wasan
opportunity in the market to marry the agility and cost-savings of a true
multitenant SaaS solution, with the brand control and experience customization youd
get with an on-premise platform?
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <font size="2">
          <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
           In a landscape peppered with
cookie-cutter sites and environments that cant scale, its time for a shake
up. Online businesses have been waiting!
          </span>
          <span style="line-height: 150%;">
           <o:p>
           </o:p>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%;">
          <font size="2">
          </font>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%;">
         <span style="line-height: 150%; font-family: Verdana;">
          <font size="2">
           Stay tuned
for an interview with Oracle Commerce Development to hear how we are working to
solve these challenges
          </font>
         </span>
         <o:p>

       <h3 class="entry-title">
        <a href="https://blogs.oracle.com/cx/entry/linkedin_wins_gartner_1to1_media" title="permalink">
         LinkedIn Wins Gartner &amp; 1to1 Media Customer Service Excellence Award by Stephen Fioretti
        </a>
       </h3>
       <h4 class="entry-meta">
        By Tuula Fai on
        <a href="#">
         Jun 22, 2015
        </a>
       </h4>
       <div class="entry-body">
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/">
          LinkedIn
         </a>
         was named a
         <a href="http://www.1to1media.com/view.aspx?docid=35378">
          Gold winner
         </a>
         in the Customer Service Excellence category for the 2015
         <a href="http://www.1to1media.com/cxexcellence/">
          Gartner &amp; 1to1 Media Customer Experience Excellence Awards
         </a>
         . For over a decade, Gartner and 1to1 Media have used this joint awards program to honor select, customer-centric organizations that have achieved outstanding business performance by implementing enterprise-wide, service-focused strategies. LinkedIn will be presented with this innovation in
         <a href="http://www.1to1media.com/view.aspx?docid=35378">
          service excellence award
         </a>
         at the
         <a href="http://www.gartner.com/events/na/crm">
          Gartner 360 Summit
         </a>
         September 9-11 in San Diego.
         <img align="right" src="http://www.1to1media.com/images/g1images/2015/ads/LinkedIn_website_300w.jpg"/>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         What helps LinkedIn bring people, processes, and technologies together to provide a superior customer experience?
         <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/ayasutake">
          Andy Yasutake
         </a>
         , Director of Tech Solutions and Operations, outlines several principles LinkedIn follows to increase business efficiency and member satisfaction.
         <br/>
         <b>
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Focus on Member First in all you do.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         LinkedIns mission is to connect its users and make them more productive and successful. Thus, the company implemented a 
         <a href="https://youtu.be/UHzt_YuN3_A?list=PL5Nry73Mn3b9Nlw8OGwDoE9kB_QFevgfy">
          Members First
         </a>
          initiative, and started making changes based on the Voice of the Customer. For example, when LinkedIn learned that its members placed high value on rapid response time, it encouraged agents to respond more quickly to inquiries. With the help of
         <a href="https://www.oracle.com/applications/customer-experience/service/index.html">
          Oracle Service Cloud
         </a>
         , LinkedIn achieved an 85% decrease in average initial response time, a 68% decrease in resolution time, and a 23% increase in customer satisfaction!
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Your customers dont care about organizational boundaries.
          <br/>
          Break them down.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         LinkedIn recognized that its members interacted with the brand as a whole, not just exclusively with sales, marketing, or service. As a result, the company broke down information silos between these departments and encouraged employees to step outside their given expertise to resolve issues quickly and/or prevent them from escalating. Leveraging all its assets, rapidly responding to member feedback, and rethinking roles allows the company to maximize the value of every touch and bring added value to the member journey.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          Help your customers help themselves.
         </b>
         <br/>
         With over 350 million worldwide users, and more than two new members joining per second, LinkedIn expected to employ around 5,000-10,000 service representatives. However, with
         <a href="https://www.oracle.com/applications/customer-experience/service/index.html">
          Oracle Service Cloud
         </a>
         , LinkedIn was able to achieve a 97% self-service rate, thereby reducing the companys potential need for support staff. The result? Instead of employing 5,000-10,000 reps, LinkedIn has been able to maintain agents at 750-800, easing costs and boosting efficiency, all while growing 40% year-over-year.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         On the digital front, LinkedIn noticed that 50% of its web traffic came through mobile devices, and that 21% of users accessing its knowledge center were not there to solve a specific issue, but instead to learn about the product and develop their career. Therefore, the company placed emphasis on creating knowledge base articles and a mobile
         <a href="https://youtu.be/UHzt_YuN3_A?list=PL5Nry73Mn3b9Nlw8OGwDoE9kB_QFevgfy">
          Help Center
         </a>
         , enabling users to enjoy this content easily from any device.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Optimizing its support processes and empowering members to self-serve has allowed LinkedIn to efficiently provide top-tier service.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Congratulations to LinkedIn for your
         <a href="http://www.1to1media.com/view.aspx?docid=35378">
          outstanding achievement
         </a>
         . Oracle is honored to have partnered with you to deliver your award-winning customer experience!
        </p>

release in May 2015. With a focus on integration, as well as improving both
user and customer experience, new features included in the latest Oracle Field
Service Cloud include:

This mobile-friendly redesign of the Oracle Field Service Core Manage solution
makes it easier for service managers and dispatchers to manage field teams from
smartphones and tablets, helping customers keep pace with consumer trends. A
resized display and improved scrolling and swipe functionality provide a
consistent user experience across all devices.

Accelerator

center to the field  leveraging the Oracle Field Service Clouds predictive
routing and scheduling functionality directly within the Oracle Service Cloud
Agent Desktop. Learn more about this Accelerator in this recent

post

Service Cloud.

      <div class="entry" id="hacking_growth_ecommerce_innovators_re">
       <h3 class="entry-title">
        <a href="https://blogs.oracle.com/cx/entry/hacking_growth_ecommerce_innovators_re" title="permalink">
         Hacking Growth: eCommerce Innovators Re-Imagining Familiar Consumer Markets
        </a>
       </h3>
       <h4 class="entry-meta">
        By Katrina Gosek, Director | Commerce Product Strategy -Oracle on
        <a href="#">
         Jun 15, 2015
        </a>
       </h4>
       <div class="entry-body">
        <p class="p1">
         <span style="font-family: verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: small;">
          Idistinctly remember the evening I learned about Uber from a work colleague in2012. It was a moment that forever changed mylevel of work-travel-stress forever it seared into my memory.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <font face="verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          We finisheddinner at a restaurant just outside of Philadelphia  a location just exteriorenough to the city that it would be a pain tocall and then wait for a taxi.Probably a wait of only 20 mins  butjustlong enough to become irritating when you desperately justneed some shuteyebefore the next long day.
          <span class="s1">
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <font face="verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          So if youcan travel back in time to the olden days of 2012  still a moment in timebefore we dideverything(well,everythingmundane at least) on your mobiles  Uber seemed trulymagical. My colleague pulled up herphone, showed me the little ant-likecars nearby, ordered one  and we were atthe hotel in less than 10 mins  exiting the car without worrying about havingenoughcash, giving directions to the lost driver, or tipping. We pulled up tothe lobby. Jumped out. Done. Done!
          <span class="s1">
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <font face="verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          Problemsolved. New loyal customer gained.
          <span class="s1">
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <font face="verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          In the 3short years since that evening, terms like just Uber it, its an Uber-likebusiness  have entered the daily lexicon. And notjust for companies like Uber Airbnb, Amazon Prime, Instacart, Warby Parker are all changing the waycompanies sell online forever.
          <span class="s1">
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <font face="verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          Moreimportantly  for us consumers -- the most innovative ecommerce startups arerevolutionizing the mundane parts of our day-to-day lives. Saving usprecioustime! Making things easier forus  so we can use those collective saved minutes to become moreinteresting, readmore books, travel the world, have more mind-space. At least that is what Imusing my extra time for  ;)
          <span class="s1">
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <font face="verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          Just takea look at the list of top ecommerce startups for 2014-2015. They are allapplying innovation to industries / services whereshopping experiences haventchanged in decades.
          <span class="s1">
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="p2">
         <font face="verdana, arial, helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
          They areall about creating an x experience that looks nothing like the x experienceof the olden days  a disruptive re-imaginingconsumer experiences inestablished industries.
         </font>
        </p>
