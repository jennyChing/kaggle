      <p>
       <span>
        ROCKFORD, Mich.  The streak continues. A Michigan couple welcomed their 13th son into the world on Wednesday.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span>
        Jay Schwandt told Good Morning America he hoped the couple's 13th child would be a girl.On Wednesday morning,
       </span>
       <span>
        Kateri Schwandt gave birth to an 8 pound 15 ounce boy who measured 22 inches long.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span>
        Jay posted on Facebook, "It's a BOY! Now we need to choose a name! BLESSED beyond belief!"
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Baby #13 was due Saturday, but arrived four days late. The Schwandt's struck to their tradition of not finding out in advance whether they were having a boy or girl.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Schwandt boys range from 22 to the most recent addition. When the 13 boys were asked if they wanted another boy in the family, only three raised their hands.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Schwandt's told Good Morning America they use charts to manage chores and keep chaos at a minimum.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
        "There's a lot of activity, a lot of commotion, a lot of chaos, but there's also a lot of love," Kateri Schwandt told Good Morning America.
       </span>
      </p>
