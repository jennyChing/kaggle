        <p>
         It was bound to happen once they started putting advertising on the sides of trains: Coke ads.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/danielbowen/6388121261/" title="Coke advert on a Siemens train by Daniel Bowen, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Coke advert on a Siemens train" height="508" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7163/6388121261_cc892bf41f_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Given trains are seen not just by passengers but also by motorists and pedestrians at level crossings, it could be quite lucrative for Metro. One can only hope the money goes into better services.
        </p>
        <p>
         My guess is we wont see these on Comeng trains, due to the fluted sides. Too hard to stick ads onto.
        </p>
        <p>
         But they are putting ads
         <em>
          inside
         </em>
         the Comeng trains:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/danielbowen/6393953273/" title="Advert inside a Comeng train by Daniel Bowen, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Advert inside a Comeng train" height="371" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7019/6393953273_e410b7ea96_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         And another recent addition is this trial video screen at Melbourne Central Station:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/danielbowen/6393954303/" title="Video screen, Melbourne Central Station by Daniel Bowen, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Video screen, Melbourne Central Station" height="412" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7175/6393954303_699f1eae72_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         As with (apparently) the ones at stations in Sydney and elsewhere, it goes blank when a train approaches, so as not to be a distraction.
        </p>
        <p>
         Maybe all this advertising is paying for the shiny new flat-screens popping on the concourse and platforms at the underground stations.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/danielbowen/6393962497/" title="New screens at Melbourne Central by Daniel Bowen, on Flickr">
          <img alt="New screens at Melbourne Central" height="356" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7165/6393962497_27d5b6873f_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
