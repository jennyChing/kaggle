       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Fortune Cookie Charm Polymer Clay Tutorial
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://thetinybeeblog.com/2014/01/19/fortune-cookie-polymer-clay-tutorial/" rel="bookmark" title="11:42 pm">
         <span class="entry-date">
          January 19, 2014
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://thetinybeeblog.com/author/thetinybee/" title="View all posts by thetinybee">

        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Fortune Cookie Charm Polymer Clay Tutorial" class="aligncenter wp-image-1419" height="327" src="http://thetinybeeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Fortune-Cookie-Charm-Polymer-Clay-Tutorial--1010x1024.jpg" style="border: 0.25px solid black;" width="322"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         These polymer clay fortune cookie charms are a quick, easy, and super cute project. I attached my charms to key chains (for sale in my
         <a href="https://www.etsy.com/listing/154624786/fortune-cookie-key-chain-polymer-clay?ref=shop_home_active_14" target="_blank">
          Etsy store
         </a>
         ) but you could make them smaller for necklaces or larger for window hangs. I carry one on my key chain and fall more in love with it every time I open a door.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          <span id="inserted6882" style="font-size: 16px;">
           Make your own by following my fortune cookie polymer clay tutorial.
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          Things you need:
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Polymer Clay- I used Fimo sahara, metallic gold, cherry red, and white
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Round cookie cutter
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Head pin
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Rolling pin (optional)
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Jewelry flat and round pliers
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span id="inserted609" style="font-size: 25px;">
          1.
         </span>
         <img alt="cut the circle" class="aligncenter wp-image-1417" height="213" src="http://thetinybeeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/cut-the-circle--1024x341.jpg" style="border: 0.25px solid black;" width="640"/>
         Flatten a piece of tan polymer clay slightly larger than your circle cookie cutter. I blended
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000GBO8UG/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000GBO8UG&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ournewtho-20">
          Fimo sahara
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=ournewtho-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000GBO8UG" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         <br/>
         with a
         <em>
          smidge
         </em>
         of
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000Y3ETHS/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B000Y3ETHS&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ournewtho-20">
          Fimo metallic gold
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=ournewtho-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B000Y3ETHS" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         <br/>
         to get the color and a
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004E3S4AW/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004E3S4AW&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=ournewtho-20">
          Sculpey rolling-pin
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=ournewtho-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B004E3S4AW" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         <br/>
         to flatten my clay.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span id="inserted1772" style="font-size: 25px;">
          2.
         </span>
         <img alt="folding the fortune cookie polymer clay charms" class="aligncenter wp-image-1418" height="213" src="http://thetinybeeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/folding-the-fortune-cookie-polymer-clay-charms-1024x341.jpg" style="border: 0.25px solid black;" width="640"/>
         Fold your clay circle up like a taco pressing the top of the taco together gently. Then, using a round tool (clay tool, chopstick, pen) indent the bottom center of the taco. Use your fingers to squeeze the sides towards each other to create the cookie shape.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span id="inserted3722" style="font-size: 25px;">
          3.
         </span>
         <img alt="fortune paper" class="aligncenter wp-image-1420" height="213" src="http://thetinybeeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/fortune-paper-1024x341.jpg" style="border: 0.25px solid black;" width="640"/>
         Now its time to make your fortune. Take a small piece of white clay and shape it into a nice chunky rectangle. Thin fortunes are fragile and will break off so make sure its got some meat on its bones. For your heart, roll out a tiny ball of red clay. Shape one side like a triangle and use a pin to indent the other side to make it look like a heart. Press the heart onto the white clay. Open one side of your cookie slightly to slip the fortune in and then press the cookie closed to secure.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span id="inserted8713" style="font-size: 25px;">
          4.
         </span>
         <img alt="head pin" class="aligncenter wp-image-1421" height="213" src="http://thetinybeeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/head-pin-1024x341.jpg" style="border: 0.25px solid black;" width="640"/>
         And finally, add your head pin (not an eye pin) so that the cookie can be used as a charm. Insert the pin into the crease of the cookie and feed through to the top towards the back. Then bake @ 230 degrees for 15 minutes. Once your cookie has cooled use jewelers tools to close your head pin into a loop. Here is a good video showing how to close a head pin.
        </p>
