           <p>
            More importantly right now, do you really only have days or weeks until Christmas and you havent finished your shopping? Please tell me that you already got your wife something. No? Wow. No worries, Ive compiled a collection of Christmas gift ideas for you to make the task easier and less time consuming.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you havent been able to come up with the right gift for her this year, there should be something here that will fit the bill.
           </p>
           <h1>
            Keep Her Warm and Cozy
           </h1>
           <p>
            Not every part of the country gets down to the bitter temperatures of Minnesota or North Dakota, but most of us get a significant enough drop to warrant a separate winter wardrobe. I know that my wife hates to be cold and cant get enough warm and comfortable stuff once fall starts roaring away.
           </p>
           <p>
            First the easy stuff. Ive not met many women who didnt appreciate
            <b>
             robes
            </b>
            and
            <b>
             long underwear.
            </b>
            These are two of my wifes favorite Christmas gifts. By long underwear, I mean the soft and snuggly kind like CuddleDuds, not the mesh stuff that you get for hunting from Kmart. Please. A big, thick, fuzzy robe is the perfect thing to keep warm and covered around the house without having to wear sweatshirts and sweatpants. Not that theres anything wrong with that.
           </p>
           <p>
            Another essential for women in the winter is the throw blanket. Wrapping up on the couch with a good book and a soft, warm blanket is the perfect way to spend a cold and dreary winter day. Orvis claims to make the
            <a href="http://www.orvis.com/store/product.aspx?pf_id=1E46&amp;adv=39026&amp;cm_mmc=Gifts.com*Prod_feeds*Prod_feeds*39026&amp;CA_6C15C=480853367">
             softest blanket ever
            </a>
            , and they must be at least close because the site was sold out of every size when I went. Keep checking.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_4165" style="width: 255px">
            <a href="http://us.topshop.com/webapp/wcs/stores/servlet/ProductDisplay?searchTerm=hooded+duffle+coat&amp;storeId=13052&amp;productId=11178252&amp;urlRequestType=Base&amp;categoryId=&amp;langId=-1&amp;productIdentifier=product&amp;catalogId=33060">
             <img alt="duffle coat" class="size-medium wp-image-4165" height="300" src="http://cdn.fueld.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/duffle-coat-245x300.png" width="245"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Wool hooded Duffle Coat from Topshop
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            When she does have to venture from the warmth of your house, you want to make sure that those CuddleDuds are properly covered with nice warm outerwear. You dont want her catching a cold. If the air gets fairly nippy in your neck of the woods, nothing beats the look, feel, and warmth of a well-made
            <b>
             wool coat
            </b>
            . I still have my peacoat from my Navy days, and it is the warmest thing Ive ever worn. Shell love something stylish like this
            <a href="http://us.topshop.com/webapp/wcs/stores/servlet/ProductDisplay?beginIndex=0&amp;viewAllFlag=&amp;catalogId=33060&amp;storeId=13052&amp;productId=11178252&amp;langId=-1&amp;categoryId=&amp;parent_category_rn=&amp;searchTerm=TS07H01ENAV&amp;resultCount=1&amp;geoip=home">
             wool hooded duffle coat
            </a>
            from TopShop.
           </p>
           <p>
            You can pretty much say categorically that women like shoes without worrying about repercussions due to unwarranted stereotyping, which is why I just did. That said, lets talk footwear for just a moment. There is literally no end to the types and styles of shoes you could shop for when a woman is concerned, so Im going to stay with the warm and dry theme here. Hunter makes top quality rain boots and wet-weather footwear, but they also make them stylish and not just functional. Their
            <a href="http://usa.hunter-boot.com/product/original-short-gloss-rain-boots">
             Short Gloss rain boots
            </a>
            will keep her toes dry and warm and give her a fun and sassy look (especially in the red or purple).
           </p>
           <p>
            If you something with similar protection but a little more of a city look over country, Cole Haan makes a stylish
            <a href="http://www.colehaan.com/henson-bootie---waterproof-black/D40963.html?dwvar_D40963_color=Black#cgid=womens_new_shoes&amp;start=39">
             waterproof Henson Bootie
            </a>
            that shell absolutely love. I know, Im a guy, but I can talk this way and discuss these things intelligently after thirteen years of marriage, I assure you.
           </p>
           <p>
            Shirts and sweaters are like shoes when youre shopping for your wife. There are more options and choices than you could ever consider. If you want to make an impact with your gift, get her an
            <b>
             Alpaca sweater
            </b>
            instead of some regular old sweater. Theyre lighter, warmer, and last longer.
           </p>
           <p>
            You need to make sure her hands stay warm as well. These days gloves can be a pain when we are constantly trying to reply to text messages or snap a quick shot for Instagram. There are plenty of cheap and cheesy smartphone gloves out there, but some are very nice, like the
            <a href="http://www.markandgraham.com/products/411785/?catalogId=19&amp;srccode=cii_5217346&amp;cpncode=32-295122582-2&amp;bnrid=1402113&amp;cm_ven=Gifts.com&amp;cm_cat=Shopping&amp;cm_pla=Feed&amp;cm_ite=Gifts.com">
             cashmere
            </a>
            or
            <a href="http://www.mujjo.com/leather-touchscreen-gloves">
             leather
            </a>
            versions.
           </p>
           <p>
            Finally, you can solve the mystery of the changing thermostat (and save money over time as well) and make sure that your home stays at just the right temperature by picking up the
            <a href="https://nest.com/thermostat/life-with-nest-thermostat/">
             Nest thermostat
            </a>
            . This does the same fancy stuff as others like letting you control it remotely via an app, but the big difference is that the Nest thermostat learns your patterns and starts to automatically adjust itself to your preferences. Eventually, you shouldnt have to ever touch the thermostat again. Now thats exciting stuff.
           </p>
           <h1>
            The Gift of Stuff
           </h1>
           <p>
            If youre gonna get stuff as gifts, at least get good stuff. What I mean is that if you want to give a gift that you can physically wrap and put under the tree, make it impactful somehow. Well discuss non-material gifts later.
           </p>
           <p>
            If shes like my wife at all, she hates gadgets until she gets one and learns how to use it, at which point it becomes an indispensable part of daily life. If shes a reader and she doesnt have a
            <b>
             Kindle
            </b>
            yet, make it this year. Theyve come way down in price and you can load them up with books ahead of time. You can even get a ton of free books through Amazon that are high quality from sources like
            <a href="http://kindlenationdaily.com/kindle-nation-daily-free-and-bargain-book-listings/">
             Kindle Nation Daily
            </a>
            , where you can search by a variety of means (its become a bit of a pain, with lots of scrolling involved, to get the free section. have patience). I also get emails from
            <b>
             BookBub
            </b>
            and
            <b>
             Pixel of Ink
            </b>
            every day with a good selection of free and discounted books for the day.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_4166" style="width: 310px">
            <img alt="Kindle Fire HDX" class="size-medium wp-image-4166" height="166" src="http://cdn.fueld.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Kindle-HDX-300x166.jpg" width="300"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Kindle Fire HDX
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            A note on the Kindle: you will likely be tempted to explain in a variety of ways why she should just get a tablet and use the Kindle app. After all, it does so much more! Dont fall into this if she wants a Kindle. Just get her a Kindle. Or even better, get her a
            <b>
             Kindle Fire
            </b>
            and ease her into the tablet world. Remember, youre getting a gift for her, not you.
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             However,you may want to preload the 50 Shades of Gray trilogy on it, which holds the possibility of rewards for you.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            Another tech possibility is a laptop. I hear a few of you saying Im not wasting $600 on a laptop that shell only use to email and check Facebook. Ahh, but it is you I am addressing. See the laptop I recommend in this situation is not really a laptop, and its not that expensive. Googles
            <b>
             Chromebook
            </b>
            is made for just this type of user, that does everything in the cloud and has no need for big hard drives, photoshop, and powerful spreadsheets. And theyre
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Samsung-XE303C12-A01US-Chromebook-Wi-Fi-11-6-Inch/dp/B009LL9VDG">
             under $300
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Theres nothing wrong with giving a
            <b>
             gift basket
            </b>
            as a gift, with conditions. The Brachs chocolate gift basket that was in the aisle at Walgreens is not a good choice. A basket from
            <a href="http://www.harryanddavid.com/h/home">
             Harry &amp; David
            </a>
            is a different story. The key is, if youre buying a gift basket that is prepackaged, it needs to be impressive. You can, however, save some money and score more points by
            <b>
             handpicking
            </b>
            a gift basket full of items that are special to her in particular. This doesnt have to be bath soaks and scented candles, or food and candies either. You could make her a gift basket of supplies for her favorite hobby, or of a variety of things that all hold to a central theme. Use your imagination on this if you do it.
           </p>
           <p>
            Speaking of
            <b>
             hobbies
            </b>
            , they always supply good ideas for gifts. You dont have to limit it to a gift basket of goodies. If shes a biker of the non-Harley persuasion, you could buy her a new bike or upgrades for her current bike. A golfer always likes a nice club. Scrapbooking, knitting, or skydiving, whatever floats her boat, give her the perfect
            <b>
             complement to her favorite pastime
            </b>
            this Christmas.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_4169" style="width: 310px">
            <img alt="Get her yoga classes...you might want to join too." class="size-medium wp-image-4169 " height="224" src="http://cdn.fueld.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/yoga-pose-300x224.jpg" width="300"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Get her yoga classesyou might want to join too.
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Or, maybe theres something new that shes talked about trying before but never got around to it.
            <b>
             Make it happen
            </b>
            . If shes wanted to take yoga classes, sign her up. If shes longed to try painting, set her up with an easel, paints, brushes, canvases, and maybe even an area for a studio! Classes other than yoga are always an option too. Give her the materials and the time to chase after a new interest, and if she enjoys it then encourage it. Trying new things always brings excitement and purpose to life.
           </p>
           <p>
            Give the gift of
            <b>
             pampering
            </b>
            that lasts. A
            <b>
             spa day
            </b>
            is a nice relaxing way for your wife to be pampered, but the next day its only a memory. Pamper her every day going forward, at least until the warranty expires, with a
            <a href="http://www.brookstone.com/oversized-towel-warmer?bkeid=compare%7Cchanneladvisor%7Cgifts%7Cdatafeed&amp;CA_6C15C=622755037">
             towel warmer
            </a>
            . Most women love to feel warm and snuggly with blankets and pajamas, and they want their towels to feel that way too. A towel warmer is something she can use every day of the year, but one that will definitely stay in use for a few months after Christmas.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_4167" style="width: 223px">
            <a href="http://unique-gifts.novica.com/sculpture/romantic-gifts/wood-sculpture-upside-down-love/185556/">
             <img alt="wood sculpture" class="size-medium wp-image-4167" height="300" src="http://cdn.fueld.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/wood-sculpture-213x300.jpg" width="213"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Suar Wood Heart Sculpture
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Sentimental items are always a good move. An easy gift is to get a home decoration that is meaningful in some way, like a
            <a href="http://unique-gifts.novica.com/sculpture/romantic-gifts/wood-sculpture-upside-down-love/185556/">
             romantic sculpture
            </a>
            or maybe a way to display your wedding pictures.
           </p>
           <p>
            If she needs some stress relief, try getting her the
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Complete-Relief-Acupressure-Health-magazine/dp/B0049Q0P9M%3FSubscriptionId%3DAKIAJFDWI7DARHXWAJGA%26tag%3Dgiftscom07-20%26linkCode%3Dxm2%26camp%3D2025%26creative%3D165953%26creativeASIN%3DB0049Q0P9M">
             Nayoya Wellness acupressure mat
            </a>
            and neck pillow. The set can relieve stress, back and neck pain, headaches, and insomnia.
           </p>
           <p>
            Finally in the stuff category, I bring you an awesome mashup from that smorgasbord of unique gifts, SkyMall. For the lady who loves a glass of wine but cant seem to keep from spilling it on herself, we give you the
            <a href="http://www.skymall.com/sippy-wine-glass/CL5212.html">
             Sippy Wine Glass
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <h1>
            Memories, the Gift You Remember
           </h1>
           <p>
            Im a big fan of unique, fun, unusual, and meaningful gifts. That doesnt mean I dont want a Nexus 7, PS4, Galaxy Gear, and Oculus Rift VR headset for Christmas, but I go a little deeper. Not much, but a little. Okay, Im fairly shallow when it comes down to it, but Im trying to avoid a bout with holiday depression so Im avoiding self-reflection. Are you okay with that?
           </p>
           <p>
            Ive always liked the idea of
            <b>
             charitable donations given in someones name
            </b>
            as a gift, but thats not the only idea that can lead to an information card under the tree instead of a goodie. Making plans for big things can be a great gift in itself, especially if those plans will be highly memorable.
           </p>
           <p>
            You can get away with a quick and easy plan for a memorable time. Wine tastings, bike trips, and visiting galleries can all be fun and
            <b>
             memorable short trips
            </b>
            . They can become a little more adventurous if you travel further, to different states or even countries, but then they require a little more thought and planning (especially if youre leaving the country!).
           </p>
           <p>
            This idea can be taken to another level as well. You can base the trip around her interests, hobbies, or things she wants to experience and go all out. You can go as far as you want if the budget fits. If she likes to bike: 2 new bikes (his and hers), helmets and other gear, bike rack for the car, tickets to France to follow the Tour de France route, whatever.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_4170" style="width: 237px">
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Rosetta-Stone-Italian-Level-1/dp/1617160377/ref=sr_1_3?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1387102537&amp;sr=8-3&amp;keywords=rosetta+stone+italian">
             <img alt="rosettastone" class="size-medium wp-image-4170 " height="300" src="http://cdn.fueld.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/rosettastone-227x300.jpg" width="227"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Rosetta Stone Italian
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            If she likes the outdoors, get camping gear and plan a trip to a National Park for a weekend. If shes always wanted to go to Italy, get her a
            <b>
             Rosetta Stone
            </b>
            course to learn the language, new luggage, and 2 tickets to Rome.
           </p>
           <p>
            Keep in mind that these ideas are more long-term plans that are given as a gift now. This means that you can pay for the plans over whatever time frame you have between now and then. An extravagant trip might be more affordable if youre taking it in six to nine months.
           </p>
           <p>
            I had a thought this year about a similar type of plan that wouldnt be as expensive as an exotic trip, but would last longer and involve more activities. Most of all, it would be meaningful.
            <b>
             Plan an entire year of memories
            </b>
             places to go, things to do, new experiences to share 
            <b>
             and record them
            </b>
            all in images, words, and video as you go. You dont have to actually plan every event up front, but make a commitment to plan something however often you decide  weekly, bi-weekly, monthly  and stick to it. Mix up the ideas and do both classy and crazy stuff. Just make sure that you have fun and record the time for the project. At the end of the year you can package it all together and have a years worth of good memories all in one spot, like season one of Everybody Loves Raymond on DVD. Except better.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you want to take the really easy road but still get good results, buy an
            <a href="http://www.lovebookonline.com/activity_book_bg.php">
             activity book for couples
            </a>
            that you can do together and set a date night once a week until you finish it. Its the same idea on a smaller scale, and it will be appreciated.
           </p>
           <h1>
            Low-Budget, High Quality
           </h1>
           <p>
            The older Ive gotten, the more Ive realized that theres nothing wrong with giving little inexpensive gifts, as long as they provide value to the person getting them. Things that might be considered stocking stuffers in better financial years will likely be a welcome gift in the leaner years.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_4168" style="width: 278px">
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/3000mAh-Portable-Lipstick-Sized-External-Battery/dp/B00EET7UHE/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1387101951&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=portable+charger+pink">
             <img alt="portable charger" class="size-medium wp-image-4168" height="300" src="http://cdn.fueld.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/portable-charger-268x300.png" width="268"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Portable Lipstick-Sized External Battery Backup Charger
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            The first inexpensive gift you should consider is a
            <b>
             portable cell phone charger
            </b>
            for her purse. Im not making any accusations or stereotyping in any way, Im just saying that there is rumor on the streets that sometimes, in certain places, some women have been known to not always have a good charge on their phones, allegedly.
           </p>
           <p>
            There are few women that dont love
            <b>
             scented candles
            </b>
            , and they arent hard to find. You can add to them by picking up
            <b>
             unique candle holders
            </b>
            online, or just go check your local TJ Maxx home section.
           </p>
           <p>
            Believe it or not, those pictures on your iPhone can be printed, I kid you not. Somehow, even in this digital world, the right
            <b>
             framed photograph
            </b>
            can be a very sentimental gift that doesnt cost much.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you really need to go cheap, pick up a DVD copy of her favorite old movie. Make it an old movie so you can get the disc for a couple of bucks. Then plan a date night to eat take-out Chinese and watch the movie. Hey, its something.
           </p>
           <p>
            Finally, if you want to give her something that, very probably, no one she knows will get as a present, I leave you with this option. For a mere $49.99, you can buy her
            <a href="http://www.buyireland.com/home.php">
             one square foot of land in Ireland
            </a>
            , complete with certificate of ownership and a picture of the spot. What are you going to do with it? What do you mean? Its a plot of land in Ireland, for Petes sake.
           </p>
