        <a href="http://www.mbvmusic.com/" target="_blank">

        </a>
        (really like this blog!)
       </p>
       <p>
        where else do you reliably get good free tracks from? which mp3 blogs do you frequent? hoping to get some good new bookmarks and sources of recommendations, especially as i have time to check things out over the break.

            Radio Friendly Unit Shifter 09 by Nick Catchdubs &amp; Mr. Ducker
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="content">
           <div class="comment_content">
            <p>
             <a href="http://mishkanyc.com/bloglin/2009/04/07/presenting-radio-friendly-unit-shifter-09-by-nick-catchbubs-mr-ducker/" target="_blank">
              http://mishkanyc.com/bloglin/2009/04/07/presenting-radio-friendly-unit-shifter-09-by-nick-catchbubs-mr-ducker/
             </a>
             <br/>
             Better than the first, imho.
            </p>
            <p>
             FACT tends to have a decent series of mp3 mixes.
             <br/>
             <a href="http://www.factmagazine.co.uk" target="_blank">
              http://www.factmagazine.co.uk
             </a>
             <br/>
             They've just re-uploaded a best-of pick of the stuff from the past year.
            </p>
            <p>
             Dummy mag also makes some good stuff available.
             <br/>
             <a href="http://www.dummymag.com/" target="_blank">
              http://www.dummymag.com/
             </a>
             <br/>
             Dummy was set up by a founder of Jockey Slut and, alongside DiS and FACT, forms a top 3 of music sites I bother listening to to find out what's going on.
            </p>
            <p>
             I'm subscribed to fabricfirst, and aside from the FABRICLIVE CDs, they do make some good mixes available, too (the FABRIC technoi/house CDs all get punted on via Amazon Marketplace).
            </p>
            <p>
             See also: the DFA radiomix series
             <br/>
             <a href="http://www.dfarecords.com/radiomixes/" target="_blank">
              http://www.dfarecords.com/radiomixes/
             </a>
             <br/>
             I haven't really mined this resource, but it's a goodie.
            </p>
            <p>
             Final place I'll mention for downloads is Radio Bermuda.
             <br/>
             <a href="http://www.radiobermuda.net/" target="_blank">
              http://www.radiobermuda.net/
             </a>
             <br/>
             A bit of an oddity, this one.  I've never seen it mentioned other than once by a friend a couple of years ago.  Some of the mixtapes are top class but some are a bit meh, so check the playlists page to see which ones you think are worth DLing.
            </p>
            <p>
             _______________
             <br/>
             Re: Streams/DLs:
            </p>
            <p>
             Streams are ok for individual tracks.
            </p>
            <p>
             Downloadable mp3 file for anything longer than a 5 min track so that I can transfer it to my phone and listen to it later, at my leisure.
            </p>
            <p>
             It'd be nice to have a few downloadable bits and bobs from DiS to go alongside the above stuff.
            </p>
            <p>
             Whilst I'm on a similar subject - that It's DiSmasss Spotify mix was boss.  I listened to the whole thing yesterday and it was a cracking soundtrack to day.
            </p>
