     <br/>
     Famed British street artist
     <a href="http://www.banksy.co.uk/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.banksy.co.uk/', 'Banksy');">
      Banksy
     </a>
     has never done any work on Toronto streets beforeat least not, it looks like, until now.
     <br/>
     Three new pieces in Banksys distinctive style have popped up on walls in Toronto over the past two days: ones near Trinity Bellwoods Park, anothers near Union Station, and a final ones around the Rogers Centre. (Weve been asked to keep the exact locations private, lest bad intentions lead to them being chipped away and sold.)
     <br/>
     Simon Cole, director of
     <a href="http://showandtellgallery.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://showandtellgallery.com/', 'Show &amp; Tell Gallery');">
      Show &amp; Tell Gallery
     </a>
     and something of a graffiti expert, is himself convinced that the three are the real thingI have it on really good word, he says, that they are (and he
     <a href="http://news.showandtellgallery.com/2010/05/banksy-in-toronto.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://news.showandtellgallery.com/2010/05/banksy-in-toronto.html', 'featured them on the Gallerys blog today');">
      featured them on the Gallerys blog today
     </a>
     ). We hear, too, that Banksy is currently in town to coincide with the launch of his film,
     <a href="http://www.banksyfilm.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.banksyfilm.com/', 'Exit Through the Gift Shop');">
      <em>
       Exit Through the Gift Shop
      </em>
     </a>
     . Of course, we cant phone the man himself up to confirm that the pieces are legit, but weve got a pretty big hunch that they are.
     <div style="width:100%; border-bottom: 1px dotted #e6e6e6; margin-top:10px; margin-bottom:10px;">
     </div>
     <p>
      <span class="asset-footer">

      <br/>
      <span class="asset-footer">
       MAY 10, 9:00 A.M.
      </span>
      We got our hands on four more photos of four more new pieces in Toronto by either Banksy or someone doing an awfully good Banksy impressionone piece in Chinatown, and three more close to the waterfront (but not to each other). The latest additions start
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2010/05/banksy_comes_to_toronto.php?gallery0Pic=4#gallery">
       in the gallery above, here
      </a>
      . Spot any others? Email
      <a href="mailto:tips@torontoist.com">
       tips@torontoist.com
      </a>
      .
      <br/>
      <span class="asset-footer">
       <a name="gone">
       </a>
       MAY 10, 5:52 P.M.
      </span>
      Just as fast, the pieces are starting to disappear.
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2010/05/banksy_comes_to_toronto.php?gallery0Pic=2#gallery">
       This one
      </a>
      , which was located at Adelaide and Brant, was gone as of this morning:
      <br/>

       <div class="image-none" style=" width:640px; ">

        <br/>
        <i>
         Photo by Nick Kozak/Torontoist.
        </i>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
      </span>
      <br/>
      and weve heard from a few people now (including
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2010/05/banksy_comes_to_toronto.php#comment-2569587">
       commenters
      </a>
      ) that
      <a href="http://torontoist.com/2010/05/banksy_comes_to_toronto.php?gallery0Pic=7#gallery">
       this one
      </a>
      , on the back of a sign in the Port Lands, has vanished, too.
      <br/>
      <span class="asset-footer">
       <a name="cbc">

       now reporting
      </a>
      that Banksys publicist has confirmed that at least some of the pieces are real work, and with all other evidence pointing in the direction of our earliest hunch, its time we ditched the question mark in this articles original title (Banksy Comes to Toronto?) for an exclamation point. Were as certain as we can be that these works are the real thing.
