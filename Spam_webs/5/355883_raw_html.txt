      <h1>
       <a class="nounderlineahref" href="http://www.onlyorganic.org/get-facts/means-organic/" rel="bookmark" title="Link to: What It Means to Be Organic">
        What It Means to Be Organic
       </a>
      </h1>
      <h2>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Its fairly simple. The
       <a href="http://www.ams.usda.gov/AMSv1.0/ams.fetchTemplateData.do?template=TemplateN&amp;navID=OrganicStandardsLinkNOPNationalList&amp;rightNav1=OrganicStandardsLinkNOPNationalList&amp;topNav=&amp;leftNav=&amp;page=NOPOrganicStandards&amp;resultType=&amp;acct=nopgeninfo" target="_blank">
        organic seal
       </a>
       indicates that food has been grown in waysthat has passed natures own test for being A-OK. Organic farmers must work in sync with the environment. They also look out for the health of humans and animals by avoiding the use of harmful substances, like toxic pesticides.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://my8hf4aiyep2tvxyk466murm.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/organic_natural_small2.jpg" target="_blank">
        <br/>
        <img alt="organic_natural_small" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-533" src="http://my8hf4aiyep2tvxyk466murm.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/organic_natural_small2.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Unlike conventional farming, organic crops cannot be:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        grown with help from
        <a href="http://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/text-idx?c=ecfr&amp;SID=9874504b6f1025eb0e6b67cadf9d3b40&amp;rgn=div6&amp;view=text&amp;node=7:3.1.1.9.32.7&amp;idno=7#7:3.1.1.9.32.7.354.2">
         toxic pesticides
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        grown with
        <a href="http://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/text-idx?c=ecfr&amp;SID=9874504b6f1025eb0e6b67cadf9d3b40&amp;rgn=div6&amp;view=text&amp;node=7:3.1.1.9.32.7&amp;idno=7#7:3.1.1.9.32.7.354.2">
         most synthetic fertilizers
        </a>
        , or
       </li>
       <li>
        grown with genetically engineered seeds
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The organic seal also guarantees that farmers:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        meet
        <a href="http://www.organicvalley.coop/why-organic/humane-treatment/" target="_blank">
         animal health and welfare standards
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        refuse to use antibiotics and growth hormones, and
       </li>
       <li>
        ensure animals have access to the outdoors
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Growing organic helps improve water quality, keeps carbon locked up in our soil, and reduces our exposure to dangerous pesticides. Although pesticides are designed to kill pests, many pesticides can also pose risks to people.
       <a href="http://www.epa.gov/pesticides/food/risks.htm" target="_blank">
        Toxic pesticides have been associatedwith health problems
       </a>
       including
       <a href="http://www.epa.gov/pesticides/health/cancerfs.htm" target="_blank">
        cancer
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.epa.gov/scipoly/oscpendo/index.htm" target="_blank">
        endocrine system disruption
       </a>
       , which can lead to developmental or reproductive problems. Whats more, the overuse of
       <a href="http://www.cdc.gov/narms/animals.html" target="_blank">
        antibiotics in conventional livestock
       </a>
       production has caused the development of antibiotic-resistant strains of some dangerous microbes. Organic farmershelp keep antibiotics working by using alternative methods to treat sick animals.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
