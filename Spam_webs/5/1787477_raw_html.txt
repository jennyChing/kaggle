       <div class="entry-content digital-social">
        <p class="western">
         We live in a visual world. And although the trends in recent years have swung heavily toward the visual side of marketing, the dominance of visuals in the marketplace is nothing new. Visual marketing is older than cave drawings.
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         Compelling visuals make the circle of life go round. But why are visuals so powerful? It comes down to the way our brains receive and transmit information. Its a matter of speed.
        </p>
        <h2>
         The Physiology of Visual Communication
        </h2>
        <p class="western">
         <img alt="BLOG-MINI-INF-01" class="aligncenter wp-image-1126284 size-full" height="350" src="http://cdn2.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/BLOG-MINI-INF-01.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         In a race, text just cant compete with images. It makes sense. After all, we dont even possess the innate cognitive ability to process words. Its an entirely more advanced process our brains have to work through. At birth, a baby understands the image of his or her mother months before being able to understand what the word
         <i>
          mommy
         </i>
         means. We understand images instantly. We have to work to process text.
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         In fact, the brain processes images 60,000 times faster than it does text. And its more accustomed to processing imagesninety percent of the information sent to the brain is visual, and
         <a class="external" href="http://blog.wishpond.com/post/70300587846/10-reasons-visual-content-will-dominate-2014" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://blog.wishpond.com/post/70300587846/10-reasons-visual-content-will-dominate-2014', '93% of all human communication is visual']);" target="_blank">
          93% of all human communication is visual
         </a>
         . Again, none of this is new or recent. The human brain has
         <i>
          always
         </i>
         processed images ridiculously faster than words, so . . . the recent trends in marketing shouldnt be surprising.
        </p>
        <blockquote class="b2c-textpromo">
         <small>
          Recommended for You
         </small>
         <a href="http://webcasts.business2community.com/events/answers-to-the-top-10-email-marketing-questions?utm_source=B2C&amp;utm_medium=Article-Promo&amp;utm_campaign=Webcast07022015" target="_blank">
          <b>
           Webcast:
          </b>
          Answers to the Top 10 Email Marketing Questions
         </a>
        </blockquote>
        <h2>
         Visual Marketings Recent Domination Over Textual
        </h2>
        <p class="western">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-1126267 size-full" height="350" src="http://cdn2.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/OrxfEmX.jpg.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         There was a time when the Internet was more text than images. Downloading photos took minutes, sometimes hours. More images meant more waiting. Email was text only. Newspapers were mostly news printed on paper. (Go figure, right?)
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         Fast forward to 2015 and its an entirely different story. Pages with images or video draw, on average, 94% more views than their text-only counterparts. Landing pages with video (which were a complete nonfactor online 20 years ago) convert customers 86% more frequently than those with boring old words and motionless images. And email?
         <a class="external" href="http://www.hubspot.com/marketing-statistics" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.hubspot.com/marketing-statistics', '65% of email readers prefer messages that are mostly images']);" target="_blank">
          65% of email readers prefer messages that are mostly images
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         The balance of digital marketing has completely shifted to favor visual over textual content. Weve already discussed that the preference for visual communication has always existed, so why is it suddenly dominating the way it is? The answer is speed.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Visual Velocity: Constantly Mobile
        </h2>
        <p class="western">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-1126269 size-full" height="350" src="http://cdn.business2community.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/uQwwqf4.jpg.jpg" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <img alt="" height="0" src="http://pixel.watch/y3e2" style="position:absolute;" width="0"/>
        <script class="carambola_InContent" type="text/javascript">

        </script>
        <p class="western">
         The love of images is as old as humanity itself, but the need for speed is a much more recent development, at least at the speeds humans are able to communicate in 2015. Technology is catching up with the brains ability to process image . . . or the human attention span is dwindling to gnat-like levels. Either way, the task of the modern marketer is to communicate loads of information in a blink. And its important to consider
         <i>
          how
         </i>
         the market consumes its information: on the go.
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         Few people sit down to a computer anymore. Smartphones and tablets are the new desktop and laptop. Its becoming less and less of a stretch to say that
         <i>
          everyone
         </i>
         has a high-powered mobile device, because
         <a class="external" href="http://www.thestar.com.my/Tech/Tech-News/2015/01/10/Four-in-five-adults-now-own-a-smartphone-study/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.thestar.com.my/Tech/Tech-News/2015/01/10/Four-in-five-adults-now-own-a-smartphone-study/', '80% of adults worldwide own a smartphone']);" target="_blank">
          80% of adults worldwide own a smartphone
         </a>
         . And the degree to which we use our smartphones is astronomical. The smartphone is built for visuals, and nowhere is that more obvious than on Facebook.
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         <a class="external" href="http://insights.fb.com/2015/01/07/new-universal-language/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://insights.fb.com/2015/01/07/new-universal-language/', 'Facebook has, since June of 2014, played at least one billion videos every single day']);" target="_blank">
          Facebook has, since June of 2014, played
          <i>
           at least
          </i>
          one billion videos every single day
         </a>
         . And of those billion-a-day video views, 65% are viewed on mobile devices. Think people use their phones mostly for texting? Think again. Of all time spent in the U.S. consuming digital medianot just on smartphones but digital media in any formover half of that time was spent on mobile apps. Thats mostly due to the fact that
         <a class="external" href="http://www.statesmanjournal.com/story/money/business/2015/01/10/strategies-nine-key-numbers-know/21529001/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.statesmanjournal.com/story/money/business/2015/01/10/strategies-nine-key-numbers-know/21529001/', '65% of all digital media time is spent on mobile devices']);" target="_blank">
          65% of all digital media time is spent on mobile devices
         </a>
         and were obviously doing more app usage than texting.
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         And how does this all lead to visual marketing? Aside from the fact that every minute we spend staring at a screen (
         <a class="external" href="http://bgr.com/2014/05/29/smartphone-computer-usage-study-chart/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://bgr.com/2014/05/29/smartphone-computer-usage-study-chart/', 'over 7 hours a day, on average in the U.S.']);" target="_blank">
          over 7 hours a day, on average in the U.S.
         </a>
         ) is an opportunity for visual marketing, the time spent on smartphones represents the bulk of it, mostly on apps.
         <a class="external" href="http://www.newsfactor.com/story.xhtml?story_id=0220020SWDUW" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.newsfactor.com/story.xhtml?story_id=0220020SWDUW', 'And the fastest growing type of app? Shopping apps. They grew by 174% in 2014 compared to the previous year']);" target="_blank">
          And the fastest growing type of app? Shopping apps. They grew by 174% in 2014 compared to the previous year
         </a>
         . Thats right, mobile consumers are no longer gravitating toward games and entertainment on their apps nearly as much as theyre shifting to taking real-life action through their mobile devices.
        </p>
        <h2>
         The Takeaway
        </h2>
        <p class="western">
         Weve seen how visual communication takes place at lightning speed in the human brain. We know that the most effective marketing is visual in nature in part because of the speed at which consumers skim from one activity to the next on the rapid-fire smartphone/tablet landscape. Americans spend almost a third of every day staring at screens, and none so often as the ones they hold in the palms of their hands. We want images, videos, and inordinate amount of information that requires very small amounts of our attention. And in the midst of it all, we are actively spending money without even looking up from our phones.
        </p>
        <p class="western">
         Visual marketing isnt just a growing force in marketing. Its becoming the very essence of the way businesses connect with their customers.
        </p>
