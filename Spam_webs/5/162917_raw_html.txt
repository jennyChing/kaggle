                 <span style="text-decoration:none; font-style:normal; font-weight:400; color:rgb(98, 98, 98); ">
                  <span style="text-decoration:none; font-style:normal; font-weight:400; color:rgb(98, 98, 98); ">
                   <u>
                    <strong>
                     Shaobing/ Huoshao (Clay Oven Roll)
                    </strong>
                   </u>
                   <br/>
                   <span>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                   <span>
                   </span>
                   <em>
                    This is healthy to consume because it is made of soybeans as an alternative for cow's milk. Since soybeans is composed of carbohydrates, proteins, and potent assortment of phytonutrients, the Soybean milk has been consumed by most locals due to its potency in reducing blood cholesterol and supporting cardiovascular wellness.
                   </em>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   <u>
                    <strong>
                     Soybean milk
                     <br/>
                    </strong>
                   </u>
                   <em>
                    <span style="">
                     <span style="">
                      This is a traditional dessert min Chinese Beijing thats most popular with over thousands years of history. Using Chinas own local recipes, the Ba Bao Fan is made across the countrys regions. Nevertheless, all recipes depend on the sticky rice ingredients and 8 various dried fruits. Jiang Nan is the most famous Ba Bao Fan recipe in Chinas southern area.
                     </span>
                    </span>
                   </em>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   <u>
                    <strong>
                     Ba Bao Fan (8 Treasures Rice)
                     <br/>
                    </strong>
                   </u>
                   <em>
                    <span style="">
                     <span style="">
                      This food is believed by Chinese to bring good luck to eaters, maybe because of the shoe-shaped look of the dumpling coloured silver or gold ingots. In most often times, you will find Jiaozi or Dumpling on the tables in any certain Spring Festival. By and large, individuals set up dumplings on the final day of last year before midnight; before they will eat them after the bell is jingled in New Year's eve.
                     </span>
                    </span>
                   </em>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   <u>
                    <strong>
                     Jiaozi (Boiled Dumpling)
                     <br/>
                    </strong>
                   </u>
                   <em>
                    <span style="">
                     <span style="">
                      This food is sometimes called Chinese steamed bun. This type of steamed bun is originated from China. Mantou or bun is classically served up in Chinese cuisine. It is composed of wheat flour (milled), leavening agents, and water similar in dieting qualities of the West white bread.
                     </span>
                    </span>
                   </em>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   <u>
                    <strong>
                     Mantou (Steamed Bun)
                    </strong>
                   </u>
                   <br/>
                  </span>
                  <span style="">
                   <em>
                    This is a deep fried, golden brown dough strip in Chinese cuisine. It is delicious that is regularly consumed for breakfast. Youtiao is also called as Guozi in the northern section of China. This is in fact popular food in Myanmar for breakfast, where it is known as kya kway.
                   </em>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                  </span>

Many new visitors from foreign countries found Chinese foods as mentioned very tasteful and healthy.
