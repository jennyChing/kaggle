     <h1 class="posttitle">
      Does your garden hose contain lead?
     </h1>
     <div class="divider">
      <p class="byline">
       by
       <strong>
        <a href="/bloggers/jenifer-scharpen/">
         Jenifer Scharpen
        </a>
        <a href="/bloggers/jenifer-scharpen/rss.xml">
         (Subscribe to Jenifer Scharpen's posts)
        </a>
       </strong>
       <br/>
       May 29th 2007 3:06PM
      </p>
      <p class="comments-link">
       <a href="http://www.parentdish.com/2007/05/29/does-your-garden-hose-contain-lead/#comments" title="View reader comments on this entry">
        <span class="miniCount">
         11
        </span>
       </a>

     <div class="fbParentdish" style="margin:7px 0px 0px 0px">
      <a name="aol-facebook-like">
       <fb:like action="like" href="http://www.parentdish.com/2007/05/29/does-your-garden-hose-contain-lead/" layout="button_count" show_faces="false" width="300">
       </fb:like>
      </a>
     </div>
     <p class="filed-under">
      <strong>
       Filed under:
      </strong>
      <a href="/category/health-and-safety-babies/">
       Health &amp; Safety: Babies
      </a>
     </p>
     <!--div class="textSize"&gt;Text Size: &lt;a title="Smaller" class="small" href="javascript:resize(-1)"&gt;&nbsp;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;a title="LARGER" class="large" href="javascript:resize(1)"&gt;&nbsp;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div-->
     <div class="postbody">
      Last week I went to the local nursery with my daughter, Willow, to get a few things for the yard. We got a new sprinkler, a new garden hose, and a garden hose winder-upper with a handle because I'm done being the only person who ever loops it back up onto the wall-mounted holder.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Willow really wanted to get her bathing suit on and test out the new sprinkler in the front yard, so we got the new garden hose and sprinkler out and hooked everything up. The hose was packaged in a thin cardboard wrap-around label, and I noticed that part of the printing inside that label said, "WARNING!"
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <em>
       Warning?
      </em>
      I thought, what's it going to say,
      <em>
       Don't wrap around neck?
      </em>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <a href="http://www.consumerreports.org/cro/home-garden/lawn-garden/drinking-from-a-garden-hose-505/index.htm">
       Turns out, the warning was actually warranted. The garden hose we bought (and it wasn't the cheapest on the shelf, either) contains lead. The warning on the label said not to drink from it, and to wash your hands after handling it.
      </a>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Wow.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Since
      <a href="http://www.ansci.cornell.edu/plants/toxicagents/lead/lead.html">
       lead poisoning
      </a>
      is not something to mess around with, I was
      <em>
       really surprised
      </em>
      that I hadn't known that garden hoses could be a lead hazard. I don't think my kids drink from the hose, but they love to play in the water. If we had a vegetable garden, we'd water ours with the hose. The bullfrogs in the backyard, and the lizard that lives in the house (that is a whole 'nother post in itself) all have hose water in their enclosures.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      There are
      <a href="http://www.metaefficient.com/archives/gardening/leadfree-garden-hoses.html">
       lead-free hoses
      </a>
      on the market, of course. I think whether or not you are directly exposed to the water coming from your garden hose, the best choice for the planet and all the beings who inhabit it is to make sure your garden hose doesn't contain lead.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Have I been living under a rock? Does this news surprise any of you, too?
