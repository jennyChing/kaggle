        Its called the Itzbeen because it is a timer that tracks how long
        <em>
         it has been
        </em>
        since you last fed, changed, or put your child down for a nap. You know, in case your screaming live human infant isnt a clue that you are neglecting him, or if it isnt extremely obvious that the breathing lump of flesh next to you is your napping child.
       </p>
       <p>
        Granted, in a slightly sleep-deprived state, you might accidentally refer to your child by your pets name (Ive definitely done this over the past two weeks), but things like feedings dont slip though the cracks  your child makes sure of that. And, if youre so sleep deprived that you dont remember feeding your child, you shouldnt be alone with your baby. Hire a babysitter for a few hours instead of thinking this device will be the solution.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heck, if its really difficult to remember when you recently fed your child, you could just
        <strong>
         write it down
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        The following statement from the product description made me laugh aloud:
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Wonderful tool to help first-time parents learn the basic needs of a baby.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        What?! Are they serious? Do they believe that there are first-time parents out there who are savvy enough to figure out how to use this device but somehow dont know how to meet their childs most
        <em>
         basic
        </em>
        needs?? Hysterical.
       </p>
       <p>
        The
        <a class="external" href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000MEB3GE/unclutterer-20/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000MEB3GE/unclutterer-20/', 'Itzbeen');" rel="nofollow">
         Itzbeen
        </a>
        is clutteriffic on so many levels.
       </p>
