       <header class="article-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title single-title" itemprop="headline">
         The Health Benefits of Chocolate
        </h1>
        <p class="byline vcard">
         <time class="updated" datetime="2015-04-30" pubdate="">
          April 30, 2015
         </time>
         |
         <span class="author">
          <a href="https://mysweetchocolat.com/blog/author/webdrafter/" rel="author" title="Posts by My Sweet Chocolat">
           My Sweet Chocolat
          </a>
         </span>
         |
         <a href="https://mysweetchocolat.com/blog/category/chocolatier/" rel="category tag">
          Chocolatier
         </a>
        </p>
       </header>
       <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         The next time youre tempted to eat a piece of chocolate, go for it! Did you know that it has plenty of health benefits, including antioxidant properties and possibly even helping with heart and cholesterol problems? As a
         <a href="https://mysweetchocolat.com/chocolatier-twin-cities.html">
          chocolatier in the Twin Cities
         </a>
         , we cannot tout chocolate enough. We think its delicious, and
         <a href="http://www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/270272.php">
          scientists are now researching its health benefits
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         For example, research is now starting to indicate that chocolate may lower cholesterol. There was a study published in the Journal of Nutrition that showed a link between chocolate and lowered cholesterol levels. While there were definite conclusions, the research indicated that there may be a link.
        </p>
        <p>
         Further studies are needed, but research also indicates that chocolate could reduce the risk of a heart attack. Scientists published a study in the British Medical Journal that found a corelation between chocolate consumption and the lowered risk of heart disease. Their research indicated that eating chocolate could decrease the risk of cardiometabolic disorders by about one-third.
        </p>
        <p>
         Along the same lines,
         <a href="http://www.medicalnewstoday.com/releases/179078.php">
          Canadian scientists studied the effects of chocolate on stroke victims
         </a>
         . They found that those who ate chocolate were 46% less likely to die as a result of their stroke, and they also found that people who had yet to suffer from a stroke who also ate chocolate were 22% less likely to suffer from a stroke.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are plenty of other proven and theorized health benefits of chocolate. For example, some research has indicated that teenagers who eat a lot of chocolate are more likely to be slimmer than their non-chocolate-eating counterparts. And dark chocolate is also linked to an increase in walking distance for those suffering from peripheral artery disease (PAD).
        </p>
        <p>
         While a chocolatier like us does not believe that chocolate is a cure-all or should be a substitute for medication and medical treatment, we do love the fact that chocolate is now being studied for its health benefits.
        </p>
        <p>
         And who doesnt love biting into a piece of chocolate? It is an incredibly popular candy, and its a sweet treat throughout the entire world. For those who are watching what they eat, eating chocolate still is not recommended. But a piece or two here and there certainly couldnt hurt. Everyone deserves a treat every once in awhile!
        </p>
        <p>
         So if you would like more information on a chocolatier in the Twin Cities, call Sweet Chocolat at 651-488-6340,
         <a href="https://mysweetchocolat.com/Molded-Chocolate.html">
          Browse Our Site
         </a>
         or
         <a href="https://mysweetchocolat.com/help.php?section=contactus&amp;mode=update">
          Contact Us
         </a>
         .
        </p>
