      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       Box Office: 'Ouija' Tops 'Nightcrawler' On Halloween Friday With $3.5M, 'Saw' Tanks
      </h1>
      <!-- Make individual group author appear -->
      <div class="mninst">
      </div>
      <div class="comment_links">
       <a class="scroll_to post_your_comment" href="#comment_reply">
        Comment Now
       </a>
       <div class="comments_follow followable_block">
        <div class="followable_block initializing" data-object-json="{&quot;title&quot;:&quot;Box Office: 'Ouija' Tops 'Nightcrawler' On Halloween Friday With $3.5M, 'Saw' Tanks&quot;,&quot;avatar_src&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;follow_count&quot;:&quot;&quot;}" data-target-id="blogAndPostId/blog/post/2595-13171" data-target-name="" data-target-type="article" data-tracking-link-name="articleLink_ArticleCommentsFollow" data-type-advoice="false">
         <div class="follow_unireg">

     <div class="article_body article" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         As a general rule, Hollywood does not like it when Halloween falls on a Friday. The reasons should be obvious, but let me just ask: How many of you ditched your familys trick-or-treating plans or your friends Halloween party so you could go to a movie last night? For reference, the last time Halloween fell on a Friday, in 2008, the previous weeks number one film
         <span class="apple-converted-space">
         </span>
         <em>
          High School Musical 3
         </em>
         dropped 90% from Friday to Friday, which is the biggest such drop I can recall.Even
         <em>
          Saw V
         </em>
         , which had opened the same weekend, dropped 67% for the weekend, which is the biggest drop in the seven film series for a second weekend frame. So yeah, Halloween falling on a Friday is scary for movie theaters and terrifying for movie studios except for horror films, which is why there wasnt much action on the horizon. Also of note, since the would-be holiday season starts over the first weekend in November, the last weekend in October is often the proverbial calm before the storm. In a somewhat ironic development, one of the best films of the year debuted in wide release this weekend and will likely top the weekend box office by default.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="K72A3451d.tif" class=" size-large wp-image-13173" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/scottmendelson/files/2014/11/o-NIGHTCRAWLER-TRAILER-facebook-1940x970.jpg"/>
         <span>
          I have long argued that rank doesnt matter.But for the sake of the increased publicity that Open Roads
          <span class="apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          <em>
           Nightcrawler
          </em>
          will receive for (Im guessing) topping the box office with its $3.2 million Friday (behind
          <em>
           Ouija
          </em>
          for the night)and likely $11-$12m weekend (just ahead of what Im guessing is a $10.5m weekend for
          <em>
           Ouija
          </em>
          ), the smaller studio deserves a token acknowledgement in terms of savvy release date musical chairs. In a normal weekend, with a handful of new releases and something somewhere earning around $20 million or more,
          <em>
           Nightcrawler
          </em>
          would have been that great film that struggled to get much attention. But as basically the only major new wide release of the weekend, theJake Gyllenhaal thriller capitalized on superb reviews and a vaguely scary marketing campaign to become the de-facto Halloween movie of choice for the weekend. The picture cost just $8.5m to produce which is a number it should clear by the end of today. Gyllenhaal is generally good for around $10-12m on an opening in a lower-profile film like this (think
          <em>
           End of Watch
          </em>
          or
          <em>
           Source Code
          </em>
          ), so this is right in his wheelhouse.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          Again, had it pulled in the same $11-12m opening weekend next weekend, it would have been eclipsed by
          <span class="apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          <i>
           Big Hero 6
          </i>
          and
          <em>
           Interstellar
          </em>
          . But this weekend it is the so-called top film, and Open Road Films deserves credit for cannily scheduling a somewhat unconventional thriller in a weekend where it wouldnt face much competition and it had the slightest tinge of being seasonally-appropriate. It didnt win Halloween night, but it will probably win Halloween weekend, which will be a slight boost for a film that needs all the help it can get in what is about to be a very crowded marketplace.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          The other semi-wide new release was the Nicole Kidman/Colin Firth thriller
          <span class="apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          <i>
           Before I Go to Sleep
          </i>
          . The film opened with next-to-no buzz and few pre-release reviews, so the fact that itopened yesterday with $0.58 million and will earn $1.5m, give or take, this weekend is not a surprise. Its another blockbuster debut from the new distributor that gave us
          <em>
           And So It Goes
          </em>
          ($4.6m) and
          <em>
           Legends of Oz: Dorothys Return
          </em>
          ($3.7m). As of now, the distributor doesnt seem to have anything on its future slate, so the short life of the distributor may be one of those weird box office stories of 2014. Although to be fair, even Summit Entertainment had to deal with a learning curve with the likes of
          <em>
           P2
          </em>
          ($2m debut for a really great thriller by the way) and
          <em>
           Sex Drive
          </em>
          ($3.6m for an okay sex comedy with a fun James Marsden turn) before it had
          <em>
           Twilight
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           Red
          </em>
          .
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          The only other major new release was the 10th anniversary theatrical reissue of Lionsgates
          <em>
           Saw
          </em>
          . The house that Jigsaw built celebrated the anniversary by dropping the film into 2,000 screens for this week only, but audiences didnt seem to care. The film made just $320,000 last night and should do about $600k for the weekend.If
          <span class="apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          the idea was to gauge interest in a reboot or continuation four years after the fact, I guess its game over. As a fan, Im glad.
          <em>
           Saw
          </em>
          was a defining franchise, but I dont need to see anotherpropertyextended well-beyond its natural shelf life and/or revived purely because its a known entity.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          In holdover news, Universals (
          <a href="/companies/comcast/">
           Comcast
          </a>
          <span class="quotecard_hook" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-naturalid="fred/company/1046" data-quotes-closing="63.18" data-quotes-now="63.7" data-ticker="CMCSA" data-type="organization">
           <span class="wrapper">
            <a href="/companies/comcast/">
             Comcast
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
          Corp.)
          <em>
           Ouija
          </em>
          was the top film on Friday, with $3.5 million on Halloween night. The horror film of the season, by default, dropped 58% from last Friday, which means that the film was helped a bit by the holiday. The $5m cheapie now sits with $27.56m and should end the frame with around $35m in ten days. Lionsgates
          <em>
           John Wick
          </em>
          earned $1.825m on its second Friday, dropping a horrible 66% from its opening Friday. Id like to think the holiday is partially to blame and well see if it recovers today and tomorrow. The Keanu Reeves vehicle was actually the top film for much of the week but it may-well have been a one-weekend wonder destined for cult status.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <span>
           St. Vincent
          </span>
         </em>
         <span class="apple-converted-space">
          <span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span>
          earned $1.68 million yesterday, dropping just 24% from last Friday and bringing its total to $13.2m. The Bill Murray/
          <a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/melissa-mccarthy/">
           Melissa McCarthy
          </a>
          comedy should earn around $6m for the frame and bring its total to $18m. I saw the film yesterday, and while it could have been written in a quirkie indie comedy Mad Libs book, it works. Warner Bros.(
          <a href="/companies/time-warner/">
           Time Warner
          </a>
          <span class="quotecard_hook" data-exchange="NYSE" data-naturalid="fred/company/4333" data-quotes-closing="88.63" data-quotes-now="89.89" data-ticker="TWX" data-type="organization">
           <span class="wrapper">
            <a href="/companies/time-warner/">
             Time Warner
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
          Inc.)
          <em>
           The Judge
          </em>
          earned $0.8m yesterday, down 35% from last Friday and bringing the
          <a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/robert-downey-jr/">
           Robert Downey Jr
          </a>
          . vehicles current total to $37m.
          <em>
           Annabelle
          </em>
          earned another $0.69m last night (-35% from last Friday) to bring its cume to $77m.
          <em>
           Dracula Untold
          </em>
          crossed $50m domestic today.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <span>
           Gone Girl
          </span>
         </em>
         surpassed the $127milliongross of
         <em>
          The Curious Case of Benjamin Button
         </em>
         on Thursday to become director David Finchers biggest grosser in America. The film is holding strong and earned $1.8m last night to bring its cume to $129.6m.
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/brad-pitt/">
          Brad Pitts
         </a>
         <span class="apple-converted-space">
         </span>
         <i>
          Fury
         </i>
         earned $1.95 million on its third Friday. The Sony release should earn around $9m for the frame and bring its domestic cume to over/under $60m.
         <i>
          The Equalizer
         </i>
         earned $400k yesterday, which should lead to $1.8m weekend and a $96.5m cume for the Denzel Washington vehicle.Finally
         <em>
          Birdman
         </em>
         expanded to 231 theaters and Fox Searchlights would-be Oscar champearned $0.5m and brought its domestic cume to $3m.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          Thats it for today. Join
          <span class="apple-converted-space">
          </span>
          metomorrow for weekend estimates and more holdover updates.
         </span>
        </p>
