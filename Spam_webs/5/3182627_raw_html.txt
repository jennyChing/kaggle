         <img alt="Prevent Childhood Obesity: Keep Kids from Overeating During the Holidays" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="846" src="http://i1109.photobucket.com/albums/h430/OurFamilyWorld/Kids%20Health/PreventChildhoodObesityHolidays_zps0544527d.jpg" style="border: 0px none;" title="Prevent Childhood Obesity: Keep Kids from Overeating During the Holidays" width="580"/>
         <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2013/12/11/prevent-childhood-obesity-holidays/&amp;media=http://i1109.photobucket.com/albums/h430/OurFamilyWorld/Kids%20Health/PreventChildhoodObesityHolidays_zps0544527d.jpg&amp;description=Prevent Childhood Obesity: Keep Kids from Overeating During the Holidays | http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2013/12/11/prevent-childhood-obesity-holidays/')">
         </span>
        </span>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Okay, bad poetry aside, this is by far the roughest time of the year to prevent childhood obesity. Some would say Halloween is the hardest, but honestly, thats just one day. The holidays last more than a month. Thats over 30 days from Thanksgiving to New Years in which you have to find a way to moderate all those goodies without being the Grinch Who Stole Christmas Candy. Fortunately, were here with tips to help prevent childhood obesity during the holidays by keeping your kids from overindulging.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span style="color: #ff0000;">
        Keep kids from binging on sweets during the holidays to prevent childhood obesity
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       First, lets take a moment to look at WHY its important to keep kids from overeating during the holidays to prevent childhood obesity. It takes about 3 weeks to form a bad habit. While this estimate is based more on experience than actual clinical trials, according to
       <span style="color: #0000ff;">
        <a href="http://science.howstuffworks.com/life/form-a-habit1.htm" target="_blank">
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          How Stuff Works
         </span>
        </a>
       </span>
       , it is pretty much the accepted time frame to form a new habit. So if youre letting your child eat out of control for just those 30 days, youre setting him up for a struggle after the New Year. Then there is the sheer amount of calories a kid can consume during holiday binging. A cookie here, a cake there, the calories really add up!
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span style="color: #ff0000;">
        Tips to keep kids from overeating during the holidays
        <br/>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Dont put a ban on all sweets.
        </strong>
        I know I stressed the importance of not letting kids binge but you also cant go to the other extreme. I guarantee you that your child will find a way to get her hands on some goodies. When she does, shes going to sneak as many of them as possible before you catch on.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Do set ground rules in advance!
        </strong>
        Heading to a holiday party with dozens of desserts? Tell your child ahead of time that he is limited to X amount of sweets. Dont spring it on him when hes in the middle of piling goodies on his plate. Instead, give him the rules before the party so he can scope out the goodies and make his selection ahead of time.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Reduce holiday stress.
        </strong>
        Kids tend to overeat when theyre stressed out.
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2012/05/22/the-link-between-stress-and-childhood-obesity/" target="_blank">
          <span style="color: #0000ff;">
           Stress and childhood obesity
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
        are strongly linked, so dont overwhelm your kids! Keep holiday activities to a fun amount rather than booking something for every spare moment. Dont drag them shopping with you if you can avoid it. Strive for fun, not perfection when decorating. Anything you can do to reduce stress helps!
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Fill up with the good stuff before you go near the sweets.
        </strong>
        If you know your child will be faced with temptation, dont leave enough room in her belly to overdo it. Have a big healthy lunch before going to a cookie-baking party. Serve a filling soup recipe, like our
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2013/11/01/easy-soup-recipe-roasted-pumpkin-soup/" target="_blank">
          <span style="color: #0000ff;">
           Roasted Pumpkin Soup
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
        , before you head out the door for a holiday party. Then, when you get there, your child wont be so tempted to fill up on all the goodies.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Make the baking the main event.
        </strong>
        Get your kids involved in baking the goodies! Grab our easy
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2013/12/06/easy-christmas-cookie-recipes-for-kids/" target="_blank">
          <span style="color: #0000ff;">
           Christmas cookie recipes for kids
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
        and make it a family activity. Have fun with it! Then help your child package the cookies up in pretty decorative boxes and take them to friends and neighbors. Keep just a few at home to savor the memory of your bonding time.
       </li>
       <li>
        <strong>
         Stay active!
        </strong>
        The best way to prevent childhood obesity during the holidays and any time of the year is to keep your child active. If it snows in your area, head outside for a day of sledding!
        <a href="http://www.prettyopinionated.com/2013/01/how-to-build-a-snow-fort/" target="_blank">
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          Build a snow fort
         </span>
        </a>
        ! Go ice-skating. Just get them moving!
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Letting kids enjoy goodies in moderation is key if you want to prevent childhood obesity. Put the focus on all the other wonderful things about holidays. The time together, the fun
       <span style="color: #0000ff;">
        <a href="http://www.mykidsguide.com/christmas-craft-for-kids-santa-candle-holder/" target="_blank">
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          Christmas crafts for kids
         </span>
        </a>
       </span>
       , the lights, music and wonderful feelings that they bring are far more important than the food on a plate.
      </p>
