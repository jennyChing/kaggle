          <h3 class="subtitle">
           And On That Bombshell
          </h3>
          <h1 class="title">
           What Would You Do If You Had Clarksons Money?
          </h1>
          <div class="articleLead">
           <p>
            <strong>
             In an episode of Top Gear Jeremy Clarkson once famously described the Weissman MF3 as a Marmite Car  meaning, just like the spread, youll either love it or hate it. Considering the mixed reactions to his recent sacking its also fair to say that Clarkson himself is the ultimate Marmite Man.
            </strong>
           </p>
          </div>
          <img alt="What Would You Do If You Had Clarksons Money?" class="big" src="//media.lottoland.co.uk/cms/55141c1ce4b049042e47beb5/Jezza.jpg" title="What Would You Do If You Had Clarksons Money?"/>
          <div class="articleContent">
           <p>
            For every celeb that condemns him youll find others who praise him. The general public, too, are divided. The saga has even divided Number 10 with the PM giving him a scolding whilehis daughter, 11 year old Nancy Cameron, has stated that she will go on hunger strike until hes back on Top Gear. All of this following last week's stunt where crazed fans tried to blockade the BBC with a tank to get him reinstated
           </p>
           <p>
            As for the catalyst in all this, this recent fracas, as the media keep calling it, its a word Jeremy probably doesnt like
            <em>
              because its French.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Indeed its hard to find a nationality or group that Clarkson hasnt had a go at over the years; the French, the Germans, the Mexicans, the Argentinians, strikers, lorry drivers, speed cameras
           </p>
           <p>
            Clarkson has always been controversial and is well known for his jingoistic and anti-environmentalist stance. It's fair to say he doesnt have a politically correct bone in his body, and we all knew this, but more recently his choice of words have been indefensible. So his sacking should come as no surprise.
           </p>
           <p>
            Still, we must give credit where credit was due. With Clarkson in the driving seat Top Gear became one of the most entertaining shows the BBC has ever made. He took what was once a serious and often dour motoring show and transformed it into a vividly-coloured cinematic spectacle. Road tests of family saloons became track races with exotic supercars, laddish humour and dramatic automotive pyrotechnics.
           </p>
           <p>
            Who can forget the all those stars in reasonably priced cars, caravan conkers, theindestructible Hilux, the Toybota, the DIY limos, the time the Top Gear gang got chased out of Alabama, or that time Clarksons face wobbled as he got an epiglottis full of bees?
           </p>
           <p>
            Seriously, we could do this all day!
           </p>
           <h2>
            <strong>
             Spare Wheels
            </strong>
           </h2>
           <p>
            Top Gear apparently has an 18 year waiting list for audience members. Since Clarksons sacking the show itself may not have a future.
           </p>
           <p>
            That hasnt stopped people from speculating who his replacement might be. Not surprisingly the top online bookmakers are already taking bets.
           </p>
           <p>
            According to the punters Chris Evans is the most likely candidate. Other names that were mentioned range from Steve Coogan to, strange but true, Stephen Fry.Stranger stillPiers Morgan's name has also been mentioned - if the BBC ever did want to get revenge on Jezza then he'd be the man to pick.
           </p>
           <p>
            Others, meanwhile, have suggested that his replacement will have a truemotor racing pedigreesuch as Eddie Irvine, Eddie Jordan, Damon Hill or Nigel Mansell.
           </p>
           <p>
            This week Boris Johnson openly admittedhe'd also like the job,though hes hardly the only one to voice such sentiments. Id like to throw my hat in the ring too, I mean who wouldnt love to get paid a fortune to travel from one exotic location to the next driving the fastest and most incredible cars 
            <em>
             IN THE WORLD!
            </em>
           </p>
           <h2>
            <strong>
             Money Cant Buy You Happiness
            </strong>
           </h2>
           <p>
            Which brings us onto the next point about Jeremy Clarkson
            <em>
              hes also minted.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            The man has essentially become a multimillionaire by behaving like an overgrown schoolboy, nice work if you can get it, and, despite being fired, hes still worth
            <em>
             an absolute fortune!
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Plus, like they say in showbiz, theres no such thing as bad publicity so even if we never see him on telly again (unlikely) no doubt Clarkson will continue to earn a large salary through writing and public appearances.
           </p>
           <p>
            Clarkson therefore leaves the BBC with whats commonly referred to as screw you money,something which, lets face it, many of us wish we had. The kind of money that lets you burn bridges with gusto, allowing us be as gobby as Clarkson to the people we once had to kowtow to. Thenleaving them to eat our dust as we speed off to our mansion in a V12 supercar to roll around in a fat pile cash. Yeah, that would be sweet.
           </p>
           <p>
            So if you fancy getting your hands on some screw you moneyyou need to get
            <em>
             <a href="https://www.lottoland.co.uk/euromillions">
              ONE OF THESE!
             </a>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Play this Fridays
            <a href="https://www.lottoland.co.uk/euromillions">
             EuroMillions
            </a>
            for a high-octane jackpot worth 47 million.
           </p>
