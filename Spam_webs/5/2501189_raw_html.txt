     <h1>
      London life in sound
     </h1>
     <p>
      Welcome to the London Sound Survey, a growing collection of Creative Commons-licensed sound recordings of people, places and events in the capital. Historical references too are gathered to find out how London's sounds have changed.
     </p>
    </header>

        <div id="unseen">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/london_unseen/pitcairn/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           LONDON UNSEEN
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          A new series of sound graphics featuring field recordings from anonymised London districts, with the only clues being the distances between recording points given as walking times and the sounds themselves. Do you know London well enough to guess where these places might be?
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/london_unseen/pitcairn/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Gothere 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="andre">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/andre/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           ANDRE'S LONDON
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Hear the city's busy thoroughfares and quieter corners through the ears (and Jecklin disc stereo array) of musician and recordist Andre Louis. His thoughts on why he records are rendered in braille to form the basis for a new London sound graphic.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/andre/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Gothere 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="hackney">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/hackney_year/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           THE HACKNEY YEAR
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          A hundred recordings of birdsong and city ambience captured by wide-awake wildlife recordist Richard Beard in a back garden in Hackney, north-east London. Part of a series made each morning around 6.30am between March 2012 and March 2013.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/hackney_year/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Gothere 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="projects">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/projects/12_tones_intro/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           12 TONES OF LONDON
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          London's geography and demographics explored by using statistics to sort 2011 Census data into clusters. 12 archetypal council wards are then selected to record sound profiles which, touch wood, can be generalised across much of the city. The first part of the new
          <i>
           Projects
          </i>
          section.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/projects/12_tones_intro/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Gothere 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="radio">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/radio_actuality_recordings/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           RADIO ACTUALITY
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          The sounds of London events and street life from the 1920s to the 1950s in old BBC radio broadcasts, digitised for the first time from their original 78 rpm transcription discs. Now with a new, layered sound map. Reproduced by kind permission of BBC Worldwide.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/radio_actuality_recordings/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Gothere 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="water">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/waterways/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           WATERWAYS SOUND MAP
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Recordings collected along London's canals, lesser rivers and streams and made into a pastiche of the London Underground map. Man-made noise, the calls of wildlife and the restless voice of water passing through culverts, weirs and channels.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/waterways/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Gothere 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="wild">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/hackney_wildlife/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           RICHARD BEARD'S HACKNEY WILDLIFE
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          High-quality urban wildlife recordings made by Stoke Newington- based recordist Richard Beard. This addition to the original
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/wildlife/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           London wildlife
          </a>
          section features birdsong and the calls of some other animals from Abney Park, Walthamstow Marshes and elsewhere.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/hackney_wildlife/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Go there 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="maps">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/soundmaps/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           SOUND MAP RECORDINGS
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Stereo recordings of ambient sounds all across London, including a grid series of recordings made at regular points on the map. From woodland and suburban streets to steam museums and night-time West End crowds.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/soundmaps/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Go there 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="estuary">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/estuary/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           THAMES ESTUARY RECORDINGS
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Recordings made along the Kent and Essex shores of the Thames estuary, as well as further inland, capturing the sounds of industry, wildlife, marshland, and towns from Dartford to Sheerness.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/estuary/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Go there 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="actions">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/soundacts/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           SOUND ACTION RECORDINGS
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Stereo recordings of sounds designed and made to have an impact on other people, and also of events where there's a main focus of attention. Includes traders' cries in London markets, voices of officialdom, hustlers, buskers, pub singalongs, carnivals and parades.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/soundacts/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Go there 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div id="history">
         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/historical/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           HISTORICAL SOUNDS AND MAPS
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          London history explored through its past sounds in works by Pepys, Dickens, Charlotte Bronte, Orwell and others. Accounts include how loud the London Bridge cataracts were and the sales-patter of quack doctors. Also, search for time-obliterated places with
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/historical_london_maps/">
           historical London maps
          </a>
          in high resolution.
          <a href="http://www.soundsurvey.org.uk/index.php/survey/historical/" onfocus="this.blur()">
           Go there 
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>

       <h3>
        Sounds of London's past
       </h3>
       <div id="quotebox">
        <p>
         A quiet mechanic came in, and sat near a decent woman with children, a bundle, a basket, and a cabbage. Thirty yards on the car rumbled, and suddenly its hinder end was taken in a mass of peoplehowling, struggling, and blasphemingwho stormed and wrangled in at the door and up the stairs. There were lads and men whooping and flushed, there were girls and women screaming choruses; and in a moment the seats were packed, knees were taken, and there was not an inch of standing room. The conductor cried, All full! and tugged at his bell-strap, whereunto many were hanging by the hand; but he was swept from his feet, and made to push hard for his own place. And there was no more foothold on the back platform nor the front, nor any vacant step upon the stairway; and the roof was thronged; and the rest of the crowd was fain to waylay the next car.
        </p>
       </div>
