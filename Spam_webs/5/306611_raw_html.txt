          <h2>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Can you really watch TV on your computer?
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           As everyone knows High Definition (HD) TV is so much better than regular TV. I didnt have an HD capable TV myself and I didnt want to run out and spend hundreds or thousands of dollars on another TV, so I thought I would investigate other alternatives. I thought there was probably a way to watch TV on my computer, but I wasnt sure about if it would be in HD or not. I found the answer.
          </p>
          <p>
           I dont know that much about TVs, I just know that watching HDTV vs. a normal analog signal is about as drastic of a difference as color was to black-and-white. And then I of course heard about how all
           <a href="http://www.fcc.gov/cgb/consumerfacts/digitaltv.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.fcc.gov/cgb/consumerfacts/digitaltv.html', 'television will be going digital');">
            television will be going digital
           </a>
           in less than a year and those of us with old-school TVs will have to get an adapter and possibly a new antenna just to watch the local news.
           <a href="http://e13o13.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/watch-hdtv-from-your-pc.jpg">
            <img align="left" alt="TV tuner to watch HD from your computer" border="0" class="alignright" height="125" src="http://christianpf.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/watch-hdtv-from-your-pc-thumb.jpg" style="border-width: 0px" width="125"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           So anyway, I was pleased when I got
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FHauppauge-WinTV-HVR-950-Tuner-Personal-Recorder%2Fdp%2FB000J1CCGA%3Fie%3DUTF8%26s%3Delectronics%26qid%3D1206804158%26sr%3D8-1&amp;tag=hd01-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FHauppauge-WinTV-HVR-950-Tuner-Personal-Recorder%2Fdp%2FB000J1CCGA%3Fie%3DUTF8%26s%3Delectronics%26qid%3D1206804158%26sr%3D8-1&amp;tag=hd01-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325', 'this cool little TV tuner');">
            this cool little TV tuner
           </a>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=hd01-20&amp;l=ur2&amp;o=1" style="border-style: none ! important; margin: 0px" width="1"/>
           for my birthday a couple of weeks ago. It plugs into your computer via a USB port and is about the size of a jump drive.
          </p>
          <p>
           I am still really excited about my little toy. I keep finding out more and more features:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            It has a TV scheduling program that will allow you to schedule a time for it to record a TV show for you directly to your computers hard drive. Basically just like a TIVO. Now I will never miss another episode of The Office again.
           </li>
           <li>
            When ATSC digital TV comes next February the TV tuner will also receive the new digital TV channels, so I wont have to buy an adapter or anything else.
           </li>
           <li>
            It also comes with an RCA adapter and an S-Video adapter which will allow you to hook up a VCR or DVD player and play those videos on your PC as well AND record them. So, now I can take all of my old video tapes and record them and burn them to DVD.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Steps to watch and record HD TV on your computer
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div style="display:block;float:right;">
           <script src="http://lapi.ebay.com/ws/eBayISAPI.dll?EKServer&amp;ai=dms%7Drniyrhon%7F%7Biisin%7Btnh%7F%7Dvw%7Dh&amp;bdrcolor=FFCC00&amp;catid=3667+31530&amp;cid=0&amp;eksize=7&amp;encode=UTF-8&amp;endcolor=FF0000&amp;endtime=y&amp;fbgcolor=FFFFFF&amp;fntcolor=000000&amp;fs=0&amp;hdrcolor=FFFFCC&amp;hdrimage=1&amp;hdrsrch=n&amp;img=y&amp;lnkcolor=0000FF&amp;logo=3&amp;maxprice=99&amp;minprice=1&amp;num=25&amp;numbid=y&amp;paypal=n&amp;popup=y&amp;prvd=9&amp;query=HDTV+tuner+pc&amp;r0=4&amp;sacategoryin=3667+31530&amp;shipcost=n&amp;siteid=0&amp;sort=MetaEndSort&amp;sortby=endtime&amp;sortdir=asc&amp;srchdesc=n&amp;tbgcolor=FFFFFF&amp;tlecolor=FFCE63&amp;tlefs=0&amp;tlfcolor=000000&amp;toolid=10004&amp;track=CPF-HD&amp;width=570">

&lt;ol&gt;
&lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;You will first need to get a HDTV tuner card&lt;/strong&gt;, I got &lt;a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FHauppauge-WinTV-HVR-950-Tuner-Personal-Recorder%2Fdp%2FB000J1CCGA%3Fie%3DUTF8%26s%3Delectronics%26qid%3D1206804158%26sr%3D8-1&amp;tag=hd01-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FHauppauge-WinTV-HVR-950-Tuner-Personal-Recorder%2Fdp%2FB000J1CCGA%3Fie%3DUTF8%26s%3Delectronics%26qid%3D1206804158%26sr%3D8-1&amp;tag=hd01-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325', 'this gadget');"&gt;this gadget&lt;/a&gt;&lt;img style="border-style: none ! important; margin: 0px" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=hd01-20&amp;l=ur2&amp;o=1" border="0" alt="" width="1" height="1" /&gt; and it costs less than $65, but there are bunch that you can use to watch TV (check closely, because many are TV tuners, but not all are HDTV tuners). I chose the one I did, because it was very simple to use &#8211; just plug in and go. The &lt;a href="http://e13o13.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/how-to-watch-hd-tv-on-your-computer.jpg"&gt;&lt;img style="border-width: 0px; margin: 5px 0px 0px 5px" src="http://christianpf.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/how-to-watch-hd-tv-on-your-computer-thumb.jpg" border="0" alt="how to watch HD TV on your computer" width="172" height="172" align="right" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;downside, of course, to having an external one as opposed to an internal one is that it is taking up space in your USB port. I prefer this over having to do PC surgery to install the internal one.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Installation was very simple for me. It comes with a CD that contains the necessary software and drivers. You just &lt;strong&gt;drop the CD in, run the setup program&lt;/strong&gt; and it installs all of the programs and files for you.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Now that the software is installed, you can plug in the tuner stick. The one I received came with a small antenna that attaches to the stick to allow for better reception. Or, you if you have cable you can connect the cable line to it as well.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Next, you can open the software program that the TV tuner came with. You will then need to have the tuner scan for TV channels. It will probably do this for you automatically, if not you may need to dig around the menus to find the option. I was pleasantly surprised that my TV tuner found about twice as many channels as I could watch on my regular TV.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;li&gt;Now, you are ready to start watching TV on your computer!! It&#8217;s just like the Jetsons!!&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ol&gt;

              It can be relatively easy if you have the right connections. Otherwise it can be a major pain.
             </p>
             <p>
              If you have a newer TV it may have a DVI or VGA port and you have plug in your TV like a monitor. Some laptops will also have S-Video outputs, but then you wont be getting HD quality picture.
             </p>
             <p>
              If those options are no good for you, youd need to insert something in the middle to either convert your connections (I have a firewire capture box that can take firewire from my Mac and send SVideo/Stereo audio to the TV) or there are also some wireless solutions on the market that will send video wirelessly to your TV using your home network (assuming you have one).
             </p>
             <p>
              One box sits at your TV and your computer can broadcast to it. DLink has one I think. Slingbox may do this also, though Im not as familiar with that.
