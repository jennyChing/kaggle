      <p style="text-align: left;">
       Gotta catch em all!Event marketing is like fishing with numerous types of tempting bait, poles all hanging off the side of your boat while you strategically dip them into the water at different times in highly calculated combinations.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <img alt="infographic event marketing timeline GIF" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-680" height="300" src="http://guidebook.com/mobile-guides/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Event-Marketing-Timeline.gif" width="500"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       Thats why we created this event marketing timelinean infographic that illustrates the types of complex and unexpected ways youll capture registrants using many marketing channels.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       Youll meet all the classic event attendees:
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       <strong>
        Go Get Em Gary
       </strong>
       , a total fanboy who sings your praises to all his Twitter friends and responds to early bird discounts. He came to your event last time. Hell come next time. He knows your home address.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       <strong>
        Takin it Serious Sam
       </strong>
       , who demands access to a really compelling agenda before hes ready to sign up. Hes all business. He brings a solar device-charging backpack filled with 5-Hour Energy. Hes there to LEARN.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       <strong>
        Unbearably Busy Bev
       </strong>
       probably saw your direct mail campaign, emails, digital ads and special offers but her assistant is in Boca Raton, her expense reports are due, and she just ate a drive-thru car taco while speeding to an offsite WHILE APPLYING LIPSTICK. Shes glad for a quick call to remind her to register.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       Finally, your full event marketing timeline delivers with
       <strong>
        Make-You-Work-For-It Walt
       </strong>
       , who wont register until you tell him there are only 17 tickets left, FOMO gets the best of him, and he signs up just under the wire.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       It takes a village to market an event. And a lot of organization. Lets catch em all!
      </p>
