        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         Tips to Keep Your Noggin Young
        </h2>
        <p>
         From where we placed our keys to how we spent our 30th birthday, memory helps us get through our day with ease and can bring a smile when reminiscing. For some
         <strong>
          16 million
         </strong>
         people in the U.S. living with
         <strong>
          cognitive impairments,
         </strong>
         memory is something they struggle with on a daily basis. With the number of Americans with dementia 65 and older expected to rise from
         <strong>
          5.1 million
         </strong>
         in 2014 to
         <strong>
          13.2 million
         </strong>
         by 2050, memory loss is a growing concern from many.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Exercise, word puzzles and painting
         </strong>
         are activities we can incorporate into our daily lives to help keep our minds quick. A healthy,
         <strong>
          Mediterranean style diet
         </strong>
         high in
         <strong>
          fruits and vegetables
         </strong>
         has also been shown to reduce cognitive decline. Reducing some bad habits like
         <strong>
          smoking and drinking
         </strong>
         more than occasionally can help keep the mind sharp. By making changes in our life today we can better ensure our memory will stay sharp.
        </p>
