             <p>

             <p>
              You know how some announcers have a thing? Maybe its a catchphrase, or quirk in their personality. Maybe its saying
              <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zL6yLXpd7Gs" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
               back-back-back
              </a>
              during the Home Run Derby, or getting
              <a href="http://www.sportsgrid.com/ncaa-basketball/gus-johnson-calls-the-frantic-ending-of-george-masons-win-over-villanova/" target="_blank">
               really, really excited about March Madness
              </a>
              . Maybe its
              <a href="http://www.sportsgrid.com/mlb/mccarved-up-brown-eyed-handsome-man-edition/" target="_blank">
               pointing out obvious things
              </a>
              , or
              <a class="ext-link" href="http://boxingstop.blogspot.com/2009/06/larry-merchant-quotes.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
               going off ontangents,
              </a>
              or, simply,
              <a href="http://www.sportsgrid.com/ncaa-basketball/morehead-state-louisville-onions-bill-raftery/" target="_blank">
               ONIONS!
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              In the case of ESPN
              <em>
               Monday Night Football
              </em>
              color commentator
              <a href="http://www.sportsgrid.com/power-grid/tv-and-radio-announcers/jon-gruden/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Jon Gruden
              </a>
              , its using the phrase This Guy. A lot.
             </p>
             <p>
              Gruden, the tremendously enthusiastic former coach who has made a name for himself by announcing football games as only a tremendously enthusiastic former coach could, was in midseason This Guy form last night during the Jets  Texans preseason game.
             </p>
             <p>
              Here were his stats, as tabulated by SportsGrid
              <a class="ext-link" href="http://twitter.com/#!/bubbaprog" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
               Video Assignment Editor Timothy Burke:
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              * Gruden had a total of 11 This Guys, up from 5 last week. Our tally was
              <a class="ext-link" href="http://www.pineriders.com/2011/08/this-guy-chronicles-jets-texans.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
               a bit different than this one, which had Gruden clocked in at 14 total.
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              * Broken down by quarter, Gruden had none in the 1st, 7 in the 2nd, 4 in the 3rd, and none in the 4th.
             </p>
             <p>
              * Most of Grudens This Guys occurred during a five-minute span in the 2nd quarter, leading us to believe that ESPNs producers saw his dangerously low output from the 1st quarter and relayed a dramatic WE NEED MORE THIS GUYS plea into the
              <em>
               MNF
              </em>
              booth.
             </p>
             <p>
              * We did not count borderline occurrences or slightly altered versions of This Guy, such as These Guys or That Guy, because were trying to do serious work here, and if we start counting such occurrences well be headed down a slippery slope, as Mr. Burke points out.
             </p>
             <p>
              * All of the This Guys were lead-ins to anoverwhelminglypositive point about the player in question.
             </p>
