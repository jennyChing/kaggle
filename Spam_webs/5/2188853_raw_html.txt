          <p>
           Describes as an irrational, intense and persistent fear of certain situations, activities, things, animals or people by Wikipedia, approximately 1 in every 23 people suffer from phobias (according to Phobia Statistics). 20 percent of these phobias disappear on their own in adults, whereas, the remaining have to be treated. Today we list the top 10 oddest phobias.
          </p>
          <h1>
           No 10. Pteronophobia
          </h1>
          <p>
           <a href="http://realitypod.com/2010/08/top-10-oddest-phobias/pteronophobia/" rel="attachment wp-att-4785">
            <img alt="Pteronophobia 550x412 Top 10 Oddest Phobias" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4785" height="412" src="http://realitypod.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/Pteronophobia-550x412.jpg" title="Pteronophobia" width="550"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Any guesses what this is? It is the fear of being tickled by feathers. Yes, and it can develop due to many reasons; like when a baby is tickled by feathers he might feel trapped and this can lead to Pteronophobia during the later years.
          </p>
          <h1>
           No 9. Taphophobia
          </h1>
          <p>
           <a href="http://realitypod.com/2010/08/top-10-oddest-phobias/burial_4/" rel="attachment wp-att-4786">
            <img alt="burial 4 550x444 Top 10 Oddest Phobias" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4786" height="444" src="http://realitypod.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/burial_4-550x444.gif" title="burial_4" width="550"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           While this wasnt totally irrational before the advancement to modern medicine, it is now. It is the fear of being buried alive. There are many cases in history of people being buried alive because of lack of adequate medical techniques for finding out whether a person was actually dead or, say suffering from paralysis. If you are afraid of being buried alive, then ask your family members to bury you in a safety coffin so you can let them know if you delay your trip to the heavens.
          </p>

              <p>
               As one with a phobia I am surprised at how little I empathize with other sufferers. I am deathly afraid of black water. I believe it stems from seeing on a Black &amp; White TV, a boy my age drown in a weir. Just writing that word sends a shiver of fear through me.
               <br/>
               Many years later in my forties I got my advanced open water divers certification and had to face black water on a night dive. I think it was one of the most physically difficult things I have ever done. Once underwater I felt fine, just a normal dive, but getting out I kept looking at the boat, away from the water. have done it a few times since and none were as hard, but it still isnt easy. But after one time I said No. It was important for me to show myself, that I could do it, so that I could decline, knowing that I was refusing, not on the basis of fear, but because I didnt feel inclined to.
              </p>
