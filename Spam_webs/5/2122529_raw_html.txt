         My friend FreeWilly sent me a note asking if I would be interested in him sending me some assorted .303 British ammo to test. Of course, I said, Sure! smilie
        </p>
        <div class="content-ad">
         <!-- TheBoxOTruth.com_FP/BOT_ROS_300x250_content -->

        <p>
         He was particularly interested in the bullet construction of these rounds. He had sectioned some of the bullets to allow us to see how they were constructed.
        </p>
        <p>
         As we noted in an earlier post, the .303 British round is an old round with a lot of history. The British attempted to improve its lethality and accuracy in several ways. One was to cut off the nose of the bullet, as shown in the Dum-Dum Bullets O Truth post.
        </p>
        <p>
         This practice was outlawed by the Hague Convention, but the Brits didnt give up. They noticed that if you had a light-weight nose on the bullet, it would tumble (actually yaw) more easily. This would cause more destruction and make them more deadly. So, they often made bullets with a light-weight nose.
        </p>
        <p>
         They also believed (correctly) that a bullet with a light-weight nose would be more accurate. (Modern BTHP rifle bullets like the Sierra 2200M, .30 caliber, 168 gr. bullet are also based upon this idea.)
        </p>
        <p>
         The Brits were convinced that their bullets actually settled down at long distances and were actually more accurate at long distances. This idea was originally laughed at by American competitive shooters; until they watched the British shooters shoot tighter groups at long distances than at close distances.
        </p>
        <p>
         We took the Waterbox O Truth to the range, along with 34 gallons of water, and did some shooting.
        </p>
        <p>
         I was shooting my Savage No.4, MK1.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-1.jpg">
          <img alt="37-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1519" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-1.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         First a round of Greek XTP, which has a standard, full lead core.
        </p>
        <p>
         It is good ammo and totally reliable.
        </p>
        <p>
         I first shot at a paper target to establish the sight off-set.
        </p>
        <p>
         You would be surprised at the difference in the sight off-set shown in the samples we shot.
        </p>
        <p>
         It varied from 3 to 6 inches above point of aim at 45 yards.
        </p>
        <p>
         I had to adjust for each ammo type.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-2.jpg">
          <img alt="37-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1520" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-2.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         We were then ready for a shot at the Waterbox.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-3.jpg">
          <img alt="37-3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1521" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-3.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         As expected, the bullet did not upset very much, but did flatten a little bit.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-4.jpg">
          <img alt="37-4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1522" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-4.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Next, a round of South African Ball R/M, that has an aluminum core in the nose.
        </p>
        <p>
         Since it is British stuff, be sure to pronounce that Al-U-min-e-um.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-5.jpg">
          <img alt="37-5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1523" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-5.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It hit the water jugs and deviated so much from the flight path, that it hit the side of the box and was lost.
        </p>

        <p>
         We had to take a second shot.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was flattened out and bent in the center.
        </p>

        <p>
         Next a round of British RG50, with a wooden nose cone.
        </p>
        <p>
         It is rumored that the Brits actually sterilized this wooden plug before making the rounds.
        </p>
        <p>
         I guess they didnt mind blowing a big hole through your guts, but didnt want you to get a nasty infection, old boy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Also notice the cardboard plug, which was placed above the cordite.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-7.jpg">
          <img alt="37-7" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1525" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-7.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This round busted the jugs and the heavy part, the lead filled jacket was recovered in the last jug it penetrated, while the empty bullet nose was on the floor of the box.
        </p>
        <p>
         We didnt find the wooden nose plug.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-8.jpg">
          <img alt="37-8" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1526" height="391" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-8.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         You will also notice that the old Waterbox O Truth is taking a beating.
        </p>
        <p>
         I tried to push it back together to finish the shoot.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-9.jpg">
          <img alt="37-9" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1527" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-9.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         Lastly, a round of British GB 1944, which also had the wooden core.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-10.jpg">
          <img alt="37-10" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1528" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-10.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         The water jugs are starting to pile up.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-11.jpg">
          <img alt="37-11" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1529" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-11.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         This bullet was badly flattened and bent into a J hook,
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-12.jpg">
          <img alt="37-12" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1530" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-12.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1531" style="width: 550px">
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-13.jpg">
          <img alt="Another angle." class="size-full wp-image-1531" height="403" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-13.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Another angle.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Looks like I am going to have to re-build the old Waterbox, yet again.
        </p>
        <p>
         It is a pitiful wreck.
        </p>
        <p>
         It sure takes a beating when we shoot rifles into it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Most people are just not aware of how powerful a battle rifle cartridge really is.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-14.jpg">
          <img alt="37-14" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1532" height="361" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/37-14.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h5>
         Lessons learned:
        </h5>
        <ol>
         <li>
          As expected, the Greek solid lead filled bullet stayed together pretty well.
         </li>
         <li>
          The bullets with the light weight front cores bent or broke apart, as designed. The action to the Waterbox was violent.
         </li>
         <li>
          All of the bullets deviated from the straight flight path upon hitting the water jugs. We lost a couple of them out the top of the box and had to re-shoot because they left the Waterbox.
         </li>
         <li>
          It is easy to see why this ammunition had a reputation as excellent anti-personnel ammo. Anyone that says that getting shot with Ball ammo is like getting stabbed with an ice-pick has never shot much of this stuff.
         </li>
         <li>
          Shooting stuff is fun.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         Thanks to FreeWilly for the ammo, and thanks to Tman for the photo help.
        </p>
