            <h4 id="pressboard-ad-sponsorship-tag" style="margin-bottom: 35px;">
            </h4>
            <div class="pf-content">
             <p>
              Vancouver Island resident Sean Smiths rant against BC Ferries cruise ship-like operations has gone viral on Facebook.
              <!--more-->
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               <strong>
                SEE ALSO:
                <a href="http://www.vancitybuzz.com/2014/11/bc-ferries-releases-open-letter-response-vancouver-island-mans-viral-rant/" target="_blank">
                 BC Ferries releases open letter in response to Vancouver Island mans viral rant
                </a>
               </strong>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              Smith, who is a Canadian war veteran, published his rant just two days ago. At the time of publishing, the post has received nearly 4,000 likes and 3,300 shares.
             </p>
             <p>
              The post targets the ferry corporations vessel amenities and marketing strategies, even though it is an essential service between the Island and the mainland.
             </p>
             <p>
              This comes after the
              <a href="http://www.vancitybuzz.com/2014/11/b-c-government-axes-proposal-eliminate-horseshoe-bay-nanaimo-bc-ferries-route/">
               quick demise of last weeks revealed plan
              </a>
              that both the ferry corporation and provincial government were considering to axe the major route that runs between West Vancouvers Horseshoe Bay and Nanaimos Departure Bay.
             </p>
             <p>
              With fares rising beyond inflation and cuts in service, the ferry corporation has been a subject of much-heated controversy in recent years. A
              <a href="http://www.vancitybuzz.com/2014/09/bc-ferries-fare-hikes-cost-economy-2-3-billion-10-years/" target="_blank">
               recent report
              </a>
              revealed that fare hikes beyond inflation have crippled ridership growth and cost the provincial economy $2.3-billion in GDP over the last decade.
             </p>
             <div id="fb-root">
             </div>
             <p>
              <script type="text/javascript">
               // &lt;![CDATA[
(function(d, s, id) { var js, fjs = d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0]; if (d.getElementById(id)) return; js = d.createElement(s); js.id = id; js.src = "//connect.facebook.net/en_US/all.js#xfbml=1"; fjs.parentNode.insertBefore(js, fjs); }(document, 'script', 'facebook-jssdk'));
// ]]&gt;
              </script>
             </p>
             <div class="fb-post" data-href="https://www.facebook.com/SeanSmithCR/posts/1645613445665586" data-width="750">
              <div class="fb-xfbml-parse-ignore">
               <a href="https://www.facebook.com/SeanSmithCR/posts/1645613445665586">
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Here is the full text of Sean Smiths rant on Facebook:
             </p>
             <blockquote>
              <p>
               Dear BC Ferries.
              </p>
              <p>
               I know that you are having a hard time trying to figure out ways to save money. Please, let me help you.
              </p>
              <p>
               1. You are not a cruise ship line. You are a bus.
              </p>
              <p>
               2. You are not a travel agency. You are the travel method.
              </p>
              <p>
               3. You do not need to advertise. You are the ONLY alternative.
              </p>
              <p>
               So, with these three things in mind, please consider the following. You need a news stand, not a gift shop. You need a cafeteria, not a restaurant. You dont need slot machines, you need good WiFi and some big screen TVs. You dont need a marketing department, you need a full on Social Media and Customers Service department. You dont need a fancy travel office or vacation planning department, you need plan to keep the actual travel agents informed of what is happening with your ferry service.
              </p>
              <p>
               I shouldnt see the BC Ferries logo on the boards at Rogers Arena during a Canucks game, or on a TV commercial. Believe it or not, people really are smart enough to figure out that if there isnt a bridge or a tunnel to the Islands, then they will have to take a ferry or a plane..and you cant get your car into your carry on luggage.
              </p>
              <p>
               See. I just saved you millions upon millions of dollars.
              </p>
              <p>
               Youre welcome.
              </p>
             </blockquote>
             <p>
              <strong>
               UPDATE:
              </strong>
              On Thursday, BC Ferries released an open letter to address Smiths criticisms. You can read the full text of the open letter
              <a href="http://www.vancitybuzz.com/2014/11/bc-ferries-releases-open-letter-response-vancouver-island-mans-viral-rant/" target="_blank">
               here
              </a>
              .
             </p>
