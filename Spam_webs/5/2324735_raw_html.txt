       <p>

       <p>
        Heres a delicious recipe for a spring or summer brunch:
        <strong>
         Overnight Blueberry French Toast
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Overnight Blueberry French Toast Recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32787" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Overnight-Blueberry-French-Toast-Recipe.jpg" width="600"/>
        <br/>
        I have had this recipe sitting in my email for years 4 years to be exact! I dont remember how I came to ask for it. I must have been on Twitter (the big thing back then) tweeting to my friends to send me recommendations for a good brunch casserole for Easter or Mothers Day. One of my friends really came through for me because this overnight french toast casserole is a big time winner. The recipe comesfrom my friend Janet (an internet friend for many years, but Ive never actually met her in person!!) Janet is a majorly talented birthday and wedding cake baker and designer 
        <strong>
         <a href="http://luvandbuttah.com/" target="_blank">
          Luv and Buttah
         </a>
        </strong>
         in Phoenix, AZ. She sent me this recipe in an email four years ago and Im finally sharing it with you today!
        <span id="more-32783">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Overnight Blueberry French Toast 2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32785" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Overnight-Blueberry-French-Toast-2.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        This blueberry overnight breakfast casserole recipe uses 3 key ingredients that make it awesome: challah bread (my favorite), mascarpone cheese (you can sub cream cheese if youd like) and blueberries.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Overnight Blueberry French Toast 1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32784" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Overnight-Blueberry-French-Toast-1.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        An egg/milk mixture is poured on top of all of that bread. Its covered and refrigerated overnight to let the bread soak up all of that eggy mixture.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Overnight Blueberry French Toast 3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32786" height="400" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Overnight-Blueberry-French-Toast-3.jpg" width="600"/>

         <span itemprop="name">
          <h2 class="fn">
           Overnight Blueberry French Toast
          </h2>
         </span>
         <img align="right" class="photo nopin" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://www.recipegirl.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Overnight-Blueberry-French-Toast-Recipe-200x150.jpg"/>
         <h3 id="ingredients">
          Ingredients:
         </h3>
         <div class="ingredients">
          <h4>
           French Toast:
          </h4>
          <ul>
           <li class="p1">
            <span class="s1">
             12 slices thick staleChallah bread
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            Two8-ounce packagescream cheese (or mascarpone)
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            1 cup fresh blueberries (maybe a little more)
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            12 large eggs
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            2 cups milk
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            1 teaspoon vanilla extract
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            1/3 cup maple syrup
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h4 class="p1">
           Sauce:
          </h4>
          <ul>
           <li class="p1">
            <span class="s1">
             1 cup granulated white sugar
            </span>
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            2 tablespoonscornstarch
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            1 cup water
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            1 cup fresh blueberries
           </li>
           <li class="p1">
            1 tablespoonbutter
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <h3 id="directions">
          Directions:
         </h3>
         <div class="instructions">
          <span itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           <ol>
            <li class="p1">
             <span class="s1">
              Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Spray a9x13-inch baking dish with nonstick spray and set aside.
             </span>
            </li>
            <li class="p1">
             Cut the bread into 1-inch pieces. Place half into prepared baking dish. Cut cream cheese (or mascarpone) into cubes (or scoop with a small cookie scoop) and place on top of thebread. Top with the blueberries and then remaining bread cubes.
            </li>
            <li class="p1">
             Whisk theeggs in a large bowl. Whisk inthe milk, vanilla and syrupuntil well combined. Pour over the bread cubes. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight(I like to place a book on top of the plastic wrap to sink the bread into the liquid).The next morning remove the pan from the fridge 1/2hour before baking. Remove the plastic wrap, then cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foiland bake another 25 to 30 minutes, or until the center is set and color is golden.
            </li>
            <li class="p1">
             <strong>
              While the french toast is baking, prepare the sauce:
             </strong>
             In a medium saucepan whisk togetherthe sugar and cornstarch. Add the water and bring to a boil over medium heat. Stir constantly and boil for about 3 minutes. Stir in the blueberries and reduce heat to low. Simmerfor about 10 minutes or until the blueberries begin to pop. Stir in the butter until melted. Pour warm sauce over individual servings.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="notes">
          *I like to use whole milk- it just makes the dish a little bit more decadent all around.

*Mix in chopped fresh peaches for a peach and blueberry overnight french toast!
