         <p>
          <span id="bodycontent_0_ArticleDescription">
           <p>
            <em>
             Its not easy being a girl.
            </em>
            While boys get to just brush their teeth and pull up their jeans (insert Levis commercial), we ladies have got a 1,000-step
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/how-to-get-ready-in-20-minutes/" target="_blank">
             morning routine
            </a>
            to put together  and thats not even on a dress up kinda day. Being a beauty-loving gal is ALL sorts of fun, but having a few extra tricks up our sleeves never hurt! With tips straight from those who know best, weve made a list of our top 10 most remarkable and useful tricks to keep in mind day-to-day! Feast your eyes, ladies 
           </p>
           <p>
            <img alt="best beauty tips" height="1008" src="~/media/B8A7828BDA214428A605C6EBEB9097B5.ashx?la=en" width="600"/>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              1. Dry Shampoo Fix
              <br/>
             </span>
             <br/>
            </strong>
            Finding the right
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/reasons-to-use-dry-shampoo/">
             dry shampoo
            </a>
            is like finding the right guy. Serving multiple purposes, a spray of this stuff will not only absorb oil buildup around your roots, but itll give your hair plenty of body and volume! 
            <a href="http://instagram.com/femininewiles">
             <strong>
              Anna Wiles
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , hairstylist
             <br/>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              2. Brows on Point
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Trace the underside of your
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/eyebrow-products-guide/" target="_blank">
             brows with a pencil
            </a>
            and use the line as your guide. Pencil hair-like strokes upwards from the guide and finish by gently brushing with a
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/what-is-a-spoolie/" target="_blank">
             spoolie
            </a>
            to make it look natural. Clean it up with a little concealer and you'll never have a bad brow day again. This move changes lives.
            <strong>
             
            </strong>
            <a class="black-link-text" href="http://www.elysereneau.com" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Elyse Reneau
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , makeup artist
             <br/>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              3. Best Blowdry
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            When youre doing a blowout on your hair with a
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/hot-air-brush-trick/" target="_blank">
             round brush
            </a>
            , you should rough
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/how-to-blow-dry-hair/" target="_blank">
             dry your hair
            </a>
            to about 75-80 percent first before using the brush. This will take out most of the moisture and make it quicker and easier to style. 
            <strong>
            </strong>
            <a class="black-link-text" href="@theroselion" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Rosie Shapiro
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             ,
            </strong>
            <strong>
             hair stylist
             <br/>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              4. Perfectly Sculpted Cheekbones
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            The quickest way to
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/how-to-fake-supermodel-cheekbones/" target="_blank">
             create cheekbones
            </a>
            is to do your traditional foundation and then take a cream one two to three shades darker than your true face color and contour. Make
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/contouring-rules/" target="_blank">
             stripes on your face
            </a>
            around your temples, right under your jawline and then make a "fish face" by sucking in your cheeks, and where you are hollow apply that same foundation there. Then BLEND like a champ until it comes together as one.
            <strong>
             
            </strong>
            <strong>
            </strong>
            <a class="black-link-text" href="http://elleleary.com" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Elle Leary
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , makeup artist
             <br/>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              5. Soft, Smooth Pout
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Because
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/how-to-rock-dark-lipstick/" target="_blank">
             dark lipstick
            </a>
            is the shade of the season, and its not so forgiving when it comes to chapped lips, are we right?!
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/body-scrub-for-every-skin-type/" target="_blank">
             Scrub
            </a>
            your lips with sugar and coconut oil prior to applying bold lip color so that your lips are nice and hydrated and lipstick wont crack!
            <strong>
             
            </strong>
            <a class="black-link-text" href="http://instagram.com/allie_makeup" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Allison OConnor
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , makeup artist &amp; esthetician
             <br/>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              6. Never Skip SPF
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            This may seem obvious, but some of you are still breaking the
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/sun-protection/" target="_blank">
             sun protection
            </a>
            rule. No matter the season, no matter the
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/winter-skin-problems/" target="_blank">
             weather conditions
            </a>
            , you must wear an SPF of 30, daily.
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/nighttime-skincare-routine/" target="_blank">
             No skipping
            </a>
            in the winter either because sunlight reflects off of the snow and can cause severe sun damage.
            <strong>
             
            </strong>
            <a class="black-link-text" href="http://www.juvaskin.com" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Dr. Bruce Katz
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , dermatologist
             <br/>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              7. Shampoo &amp; Shine
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Concentrate on your
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/cold-weather-hair-treatments/" target="_blank">
             scalp and roots
            </a>
            when washing your hair because the shampoo will work its way through your ends when
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/how-to-shampoo-the-right-way/" target="_blank">
             rinsing out
            </a>
            . This way, you save time and shampoo! And for
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/ruin-hair-shine/" target="_blank">
             added shine
            </a>
            , do a cold rinse in the shower or a great secret is rinsing hair with apple cider vinegar for MAJOR shine. 
            <a class="black-link-text" href="http://instagram.com/ashflarehair" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Ashley Flaherty
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , hair stylist
             <br/>
            </strong>
            <strong>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              8. Barely There Makeup
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            If youre having one of those days where you want to only have a
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/simple-stunning-makeup/" target="_blank">
             little bit of makeup
            </a>
            on and not the full-facial coverage that your foundation provides, create your own tinted moisturizer! Mix a small amount of your everyday foundation with your
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/moisturizers-guide/" target="_blank">
             favorite moisturizer
            </a>
            and viola  youve got yourself light coverage makeup.
            <strong>
             
            </strong>
            <a class="black-link-text" href="http://jessydeortiz.com" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Jessy De Ortiz
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , makeup artist
            </strong>
            <strong>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              9. White Liner Love
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            Use white eyeliner as a
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/places-to-highlight-your-face/" target="_blank">
             highlighter
            </a>
            right below your brow, shade in the white pencil and blend it out using a short blending brush.You can also use white eyeliner in your
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/lining-your-waterline/" target="_blank">
             waterline
            </a>
            to open up your eyes more and use it on the inner corner of your eye (the tear duct) to brighten up your eyes!
            <strong>
             
            </strong>
            <a class="black-link-text" href="http://ladyartlooks.com/2013/04/04/beauty-tricks-white-eyeliner/" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Alana Dawn
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , makeup artist
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             <span style="font-size: 18px;">
              10. Day to Night Smoky Eye
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            A
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/daytime-smoky-eye/" target="_blank">
             daytime smoky eye
            </a>
            can be achieved by using a smoky eye shadow smudged around the eye, very close to the lash line. To keep it from looking harsh, only fill in your waterline. To take it from
            <a href="http://www.makeup.com/day-to-night-smoky-eye/" target="_blank">
             day to evening
            </a>
            , add more of the shadow higher on the lid and underneath the bottom lash line. Then, use a pencil around the eye, on both your upper and lower lash line for more definition. 
            <a class="black-link-text" href="http://instagram.com/dellysl" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Dell Ashley
             </strong>
            </a>
            <strong>
             , YSL National Makeup Artist
            </strong>
           </p>

            What is your most remarkable beauty tip?
