                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       Stuffed, rolled, coned shaped..yup..all up my alley in the food department! No shocker here since I am all about nibbles! For some reason or another, I tend to find my meals more satisfying when all packaged up in some type of seaweed, leafy veggie, or cabbage. It kind of reminds me of little presents that we are creating for ourselves (a little peculiarmaybe)  With that said, I thought why not use some Swiss Chard as my rolling star of the week! After all, when
                       <strong>
                        boiled
                       </strong>
                       , they are soft, pliable and certainly roll-able! However, when
                       <strong>
                        boiled
                       </strong>
                       , the flavor can be rather bland, so it is necessary to use tasty flavors to to kick it up a bit. Other than the basic onions, garlic and spices, I additionally added some sweeter components.raisin, brown sugar and spicy cinnamon. If you wish to leave these ingredients out be my guest. Do I recommend it? Nahhhhh. Try this recipe as isits my little gift to you!!!
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                        <strong>
                         Ingredients
                        </strong>
                       </span>
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1 lb. lean ground meat
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1 medium yellow onion chopped
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       3 garlic cloves minced
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       2 cup low-sugar tomato sauce
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       2 tablespoon light brown sugar
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1/4 cup plump raisins
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1/4 cup chopped pecans
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1/2 teaspoon salt
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1/2 cup already cooked brown rice, wild rice or rice pilaf
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1/2 teaspoon paprika
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1/2 teaspoon onion powder
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       Ground pepper to taste
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       2 tablespoon olive oil
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1 bunch swiss chard washed and dried
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                        <strong>
                         Directions
                        </strong>
                       </span>
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       1.) Preheat oven to 350 degrees and fill a large Stock Pot or Dutch Oven with water and boil. In a small bowl mix together raisins, pecans, brown sugar &amp; cinnamon.
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_1122" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5181" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_1122-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       2.) Holding the chard by the stems plunge it into the boiling water just until it slightly tenderizes for about 20 seconds. Remove immediately plunge into a bowl of ice cold water to shock them.
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_1111" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5179" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_1111-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       3.) Pat dry with a paper towel then cut then cut the stem off making a tiny V shape in the chard.
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       4.) Discard water in the Dutch Oven. Once completely dry, heat olive oil on the Dutch Ovenon medium-high and cook onions for 5 minutes. Add in garlic about halfway through.
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_1115" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5180" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_1115-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       5.) Crumble in meat, salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder &amp; paprika; cook until meat is cooked through. Add in rice and raisin mixture and stir.
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       6.) Place Chard leaves flatly on a cutting board and place about 2 tablespoons of the mixture at the base of the leave. (Try not to over-stuff)
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_1125" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5183" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_1125-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       7.) Now fold the base of the leave over the filling and roll. Tuck the sides of the leaves under.
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="stuffed collard_crop" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5189" height="239" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/stuffed-collard_crop-300x239.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       8.) Repeat with the remaining meat mixture making about 8 rolls.
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       9.) Spoon half of your sauce into a medium sized casserole dish and add the rolls seam side down.
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_1134" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5184" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_1134-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       10.) Spoon remaining sauce on top and bake for 30 minutes covered (Feel free to uncover last 5 minutes to get the top a little toasty.)
                      </p>
                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                       Nics Tips  As you can see my rolls are not perfect! The sauce will cover them so not to worry!!
                      </p>
