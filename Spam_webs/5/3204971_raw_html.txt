       <h1>
        Half of Israelis back Reform, Conservative Judaism
       </h1>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Poll reveals that 49 percent of 500 respondents believe these streams of Judaism should be recognized
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        A recent poll has revealed that nearly half of Israelis believe the country's Chief Rabbinate should officially recognize the Reform and Conservative streams of Judaism.
       </p>
       <p>
        Out of the 500 adults who took part in the survey, 49 percent "strongly felt" these streams should be recognized, because this will in turn strengthen the connection with American Jews," the
        <em>
         Walla
        </em>
        website reported on Thursday.
       </p>
       <p>
        When questioned on the subject of whether the views of American Jews on the peace process should be taken into account, 30 percent said to some extent, 22 percent said to a great extent and 18 percent said not at all.
       </p>
       <p>
        Another question asked respondents whether they felt Diaspora Jews should have Knesset representation.
       </p>
       <p>
        Thirty-one percent of respondents said they should not be represented in Israel's parliament, while 29 percent they should be represented to some extent.
       </p>
       <p>
        The poll was published Thursday and was commissioned by the Ruderman Family Foundation, ahead of a trip to New York and Boston that the Foundation is organizing for five Knesset members to learn more about American Jewry.
       </p>
       <p>
        The poll, published Thursday, was commissioned by the Ruderman Family Foundation and conducted by Teleseker polling. It focused on how Israelis perceive the relationship between their countrymen and state institutions and US Jews.
       </p>
