   <h1>
    <b>
     Non-destructive editing.
    </b>
    Never start over.
   </h1>
   <div id="columns">
    <div id="column1">
     <p>
      <b>
       Fear not.
      </b>
      In Naked light, it's impossible to
            ruin an image. All your original images and negatives
            are stored safely in your Library, so you'll never have
            to worry about writing over a file or over tweaking a
            layer.
     </p>
     <p>
      Because all nodes and filters can always be re-edited
            later, you never have to worry about making
            mistakeseverything can be quickly fixed or
            tweaked without having to start over from a backup.
     </p>
    </div>
    <div id="column2">
     <p>
      <b>
       Non-linear editing
      </b>
      Naked light's non-destructive
            nature opens up whole new concepts of photo editing.
            Rather than applying filters one at a time and hoping
            you got the settings on the previous filter just right,
            you can setup all your filters and go back and forth
            tweaking each one, while seeing the cumulative result
            of all of them.
     </p>
     <p>
      You can even paint, dodge, and burn under
            filtersand see the filtered resultlive.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       590 quintillion colors.
      </b>
      Naked light's entire
            compositing engine uses an unprecedented 32 bits per
            color channel.
     </p>
     <p>
      In an 8-bit (and sometimes even a 16-bit) environment,
            rounding errors accumulate after every filter you
            apply, creating posterization, banding, and other
            visual artifacts. Naked light uses over 590 quintillion
            colors to achieve flawless, silky results, time after
            time.
     </p>
    </div>
