         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.bakerella.com/hope-these-put-a-smile-on-your-face/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Hope these put a smile on your face">
           Hope these put a smile on your face
          </a>
         </h2>
         <div class="content">
          <p>
           Just look how cute they are
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bakerella/2751339851/" title="Smiley Face Pops by Bakerella, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Smiley Face Pops" height="333" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3019/2751339851_41e4b15727.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           And yet so vicious!
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bakerella/2752173030/" title="Ouch that hurts! by Bakerella, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Ouch that hurts!" height="333" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3075/2752173030_4feb098139.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           And without regret!
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bakerella/2751339641/" title="Tasty by Bakerella, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Tasty" height="333" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2133/2751339641_c001b16546.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           (Ouch! Painful, but delicious.)
          </p>
          <p>
           Now, if youre a little less mainstream, heres an alternative one that rocks:
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bakerella/2752173932/" title="Nirvana Pop by Bakerella, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Nirvana Pop" height="333" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3082/2752173932_ef78e1c8fd.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Anyway, hope youre smiling and 
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bakerella/2751340107/" title="Smile! by Bakerella, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Smile!" height="333" src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3255/2751340107_ff223847ff.jpg" width="500"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Want to make your own smiley face cake pops?
          </p>
          <p>
           1 box cake mix (cook as directed on box for 13 X 9 cake)
           <br/>
           1 can frosting (16 oz.)
           <br/>
           Wax paper
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2F1LB-Merckens-Yellow-Coatings%2Fdp%2FB0008D6R9W%3Fie%3DUTF8%26s%3Dgourmet-food%26qid%3D1218420562%26sr%3D1-15&amp;tag=bakerella-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325">
            Yellow candy melts
           </a>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=bakerella-20&amp;l=ur2&amp;o=1" style="border: medium none  ! important; margin: 0px ! important;" width="1"/>
           (1 lb. pkg.)
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2Fs%2Fqid%3D1218420456%3Fie%3DUTF8%26rs%3D%26keywords%3DLollipop%2520sticks%26rh%3Di%253Aaps%252Ck%253ALollipop%2520sticks%252Ci%253Agarden&amp;tag=bakerella-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325">
            Lollipop sticks
           </a>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=bakerella-20&amp;l=ur2&amp;o=1" style="border: medium none  ! important; margin: 0px ! important;" width="1"/>
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/redirect.html?ie=UTF8&amp;location=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2FAmericolor-34Gourmet-Writer-Decorating-Colors%2Fdp%2FB0012DMI1S%3Fie%3DUTF8%26s%3Dhome-garden%26qid%3D1218420627%26sr%3D8-14&amp;tag=bakerella-20&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325">
            Edible ink pens
           </a>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=bakerella-20&amp;l=ur2&amp;o=1" style="border: medium none  ! important; margin: 0px ! important;" width="1"/>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            After cake is cooked and cooled completely, crumble into large bowl.
           </li>
           <li>
            Mix thoroughly with 1 can frosting. (I use the back of a large spoon, but it may be easier to use fingers to mix together. But, be warned, it will get messy. Also, you may not need the entire can of frosting, so start out by using almost the entire can and add more if you need to.)
           </li>
           <li>
            Roll mixture into quarter size balls and place on wax paper covered cookie sheet. (Should make 45-50)
           </li>
           <li>
            Melt chocolate in the microwave per directions on package. (30 sec intervals, stirring in between.)
           </li>
           <li>
            Dip the tip of your lollipop stick in a little of the melted candy coating and insert into the cake balls. (Insert a little less than halfway.)
           </li>
           <li>
            Place them in the freezer for a little while to firm up.
           </li>
           <li>
            Once firm, carefully insert the cake ball into the candy coating by holding the lollipop stick and rotating until covered. Once covered remove and softly tap and rotate until the excess chocolate falls off. Dont tap too hard or the cake ball will fall off, too.
           </li>
           <li>
            Place in a styrofoam block to dry.
           </li>
           <li>
            Once dry, draw faces with an edible ink pen and allow ink to dry!
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           Note: I think cake pops are a little more difficult to make than the cupcake pops because of trying to get the candy coating smooth. So, If you havent made them before, check out this post for
           <a href="http://www.bakerella.com/red-velvet-cake-balls/">
            red velvet cake balls
           </a>
           and this one for
           <a href="http://www.bakerella.com/make-your-cupcakes-pop/">
            cupcake pops
           </a>
           .
          </p>

           <p>
            Cream Cheese Buttercream frosting
           </p>
           <p>
            1 stick butter (softened)
            <br/>
            8 oz. cream cheese (softened)
            <br/>
            4 cups confectioner's sugar
            <br/>
            2 teaspoons vanilla
           </p>
           <p>
            Beat softened butter and cream cheese until well blended. Add sugar and vanilla until creamy. Easy!

            jenn  Are you doing the pops or the balls? What brand of candy melts are you using?
           </p>
           <p>
            For the candy melts, I melt in the microwave in 30 sec intervals, stirring in between until melted. Then I dip the balls, one at a time in to the candy coating with a spoon. Cover the top of the ball if your bowl isnt deep enough. Then lift out and tap off excess chocolate by tappin spoon against the side of the bowl. Then, let ball slide off spoon onto wax paper. If its still, too thick, you can add a drop of veg oil to the chocolate or use something called paramount crystals.
           </p>
           <p>
            Hope that helps.
