        <p>
         <a href="http://bizthoughts.mikelee.org/wp-content/uploads/twitter.gif">
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-740" height="51" src="http://bizthoughts.mikelee.org/wp-content/uploads/twitter.gif" title="Twitter" width="170"/>
         </a>
         <strong>
          Have you checked your Twitter reputation lately?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A reputation is an ephemeral thing based on the collective opinion of others. But nevertheless, enterprising individuals have built a variety of tools to compute your Twitter reputation. If anything, its a fun way to evaluate your tweets.
        </p>
        <dl>
         <dt>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://twinfluence.com/">
            TwInfluence
           </a>
          </strong>
         </dt>
         <dd>
          This tool measures the combined influence of twitterers and their followers, with a few social network statistics thrown in as bonus. That means the influence of your followers followers (your second-order followers) are also factored. TwInfluence is perhaps the most academic and thorough reputation measurement tool currently available. As of this post, my rank (and reach) is
          <a href="http://twinfluence.com/index.php?u=mikeleeorg">
           43,527 (81%)
          </a>
          , my
          <a href="http://twinfluence.com/about.php#velocity">
           velocity
          </a>
          is 4,499 second-order followers/day, my
          <a href="http://twinfluence.com/about.php#socialcapital">
           social capital
          </a>
          is 6,585.3 +2.2 Very High, and my
          <a href="http://twinfluence.com/about.php#centralization">
           centralization
          </a>
          is 13.20% / 0.0 Average  Resilient.
         </dd>
         <dt>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://twitter.grader.com/">
            Twitter Grader
           </a>
          </strong>
         </dt>
         <dd>
          This tool aims to measure the power, reach and authority of a twitter account by using a proprietary algorithm that considers factors such as number of followers, power of followers, updates, update recency, follower/following ratio, and engagement. As of this post, my grade is
          <a href="http://twitter.grader.com/mikeleeorg">
           93 out of 100
          </a>
          .
         </dd>
         <dt>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.twitalyzer.com/">
            Twitalyzer
           </a>
          </strong>
         </dt>
         <dd>
          This tool analyzes the last seven days of Twitter usage and provides a measurement of impact, engagement, generosity, velocity, clout, and influence. As of this post,
          <a href="http://www.twitalyzer.com/profile.asp?u=mikeleeorg">
           my rankings are
          </a>
           impact: 0.3%, engagement: 25.0%, generosity: 100%, velocity: 1.3%, clout: 0.3%, and influence: 0.2%.
         </dd>
         <dt>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://klout.com/">
            Klout
           </a>
          </strong>
         </dt>
         <dd>
          This tool uses semantic analysis to determine what a person talks about and then measures how influential they are on that topic. As of this post, my score is
          <a href="http://klout.com/profile/summary/mikeleeorg/">
           15 out of 100
          </a>
          . According to Klout, I am a casual user and dont take this Twitter stuff too seriously. Heh, true.
         </dd>
         <dt>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://twitterholic.com/">
            Twitterholic
           </a>
          </strong>
         </dt>
         <dd>
          This tool scans Twitters public timeline for new twits to tweet. A few times a day, we calculate individual statistics for each twittering twit in our database, whatever that means. As of this post, my rank is
          <a href="http://twitterholic.com/mikeleeorg/">
           158,477
          </a>
          .
         </dd>
         <dt>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://twanalyst.com/">
            Twanalyst
           </a>
          </strong>
         </dt>
         <dd>
          This tool is a tongue-in-cheek tool that analyzes a profiles last 100 tweets for
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automated_Readability_Index">
           readability
          </a>
          and types of tweets. As of this post, my Twitter personality is
          <a href="http://twanalyst.com/mikeleeorg/">
           popular, inquisitive, &amp; cautious
          </a>
          , with a chatty &amp; coherent style. I am a parrot. (Interesting; I used to be a
          <a href="http://twitter.com/mikeleeorg/status/1599161606">
           writer
          </a>
          .)
         </dd>
        </dl>
        <p>
         Whats your current Twitter reputation?
        </p>
