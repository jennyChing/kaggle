      <p>
       <img alt="airplane window" height="513" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/photos/i-jD2zXXD/0/690x690/i-jD2zXXD-M.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Airlines created frequent flyer programs to encourage repeat business from regular travelers. Frequent flyer programs are also set up with the hopes that casual travelers will stick with the same airline but confusing enough that few people will actually redeem miles for free flights. Unfortunately for most people this calculated confusion gives the impression that free flights using miles are only for business travelers or wizards descended from Maiar.
      </p>
      <p>
       But if you regularly take two flights per year, chances are youll be (or already are) eligible for a free flight or upgrade within 24 months. Whether youre already signed up for a program or waiting to do so, there are a few things you can do (that the airlines dont advertise) to make your account much more effective.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         1. The Best Airlines To Sign Up For In Order Are: United, American And Delta
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Even if you dont fly with these specific airlines, sign up with their programs. Theyre a part of
       <a href="http://www.staralliance.com/en/" target="_blank">
        Star Alliance
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.oneworld.com/" target="_blank">
        oneworld
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="http://www.delta.com/planning_reservations/plan_flight/flight_partners/skyteam_alliance/index.jsp" target="_blank">
        SkyTeam
       </a>
       programs respectively, but the only airlines whose miles can be earned with partners. For example, I regularly fly Turkish Airlines from
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2013/10/17/photo-shows-landmarks-look-flying-istanbul/">
        Istanbul
       </a>
       , but use my United Mileage Plus number when booking. United honors miles earned by flying on partner airlines but it doesnt work in reverse. Learn
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2011/12/20/how-do-you-accumulate-all-of-your-frequent-flyer-miles-in-one-place/">
        how to accumulate your miles in one place
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="united mileage plus premier" height="219" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/photos/i-kmHQRWw/0/690x690/i-kmHQRWw-L.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        These alliances cover over 45 major airlines but there arent any true budget carriers among them. Budget airlines with frequent flyer programs are generally US-based, such as
        <a href="http://www.airtran.com/Home.aspx" target="_blank">
         AirTran
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.southwest.com/" target="_blank">
         Southwest
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="http://www.flyfrontier.com/" target="_blank">
         Frontier Airlines
        </a>
        .
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         2. Redeem Flights For Peak Travel Times 330 Days In Advance
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Seats reserved for award
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/category/travel/books/">
        travel
       </a>
       are
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2013/10/03/ask-frequent-flyer-hacker-get-free-tickets-fly-just-anywhere/comment-page-1/#comment-478911">
        set aside 330 days in advance of the flight date
       </a>
       frequent flyer hacker
       <a href="http://www.extrapackofpeanuts.com/" target="_blank">
        Travis Sherry
       </a>
       told us in
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2013/10/03/ask-frequent-flyer-hacker-get-free-tickets-fly-just-anywhere">
        a recent live chat
       </a>
       . That means for busier seasons and around specific events like the World Cup, those seats go quickly as the airlines would rather have paying butts sitting in them. Even if youre
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2010/07/15/4-things-every-traveler-without-a-plan-should-be-prepared-for/">
        a traveler without a plan
       </a>
       , booking 330 days out can guarantee you a free seat  plus may come at a discounted mileage rate.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="singapore business class" height="517" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/Asia/Japan/Tokyo/i-GMMZVdw/0/690x690/P1140441_2_3c-L.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         3. Calling Is The Best Way To Book
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       The online booking systems provided by the major airlines are useful for getting a sense of what award seats may be available when, but aside from a glimpse into the 1998 Internet, thats about all you should use it for. Calling the mileage programs directly almost always uncovers routes not shown online. Plus,
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2013/10/03/ask-frequent-flyer-hacker-get-free-tickets-fly-just-anywhere/comment-page-1/#comment-479245">
        if youve got elite status
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2009/08/25/use-sun-tzus-the-art-of-war-to-win-battles-at-the-ticket-counter/">
        some Art of War charm
       </a>
       , it is often possible to talk your way out of fuel surcharges or other fees.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="trinity matrix phone" height="276" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/photos/i-3wDWTbT/0/O/i-3wDWTbT.gif" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Airline Contact Numbers
        </strong>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         United Mileage Plus: 1-800-421-4655
        </li>
        <li>
         American Airlines AAdvantage: 1-800-882-8880
        </li>
        <li>
         Delta SkyMiles: 1-800-323-2323
        </li>
       </ul>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         4. Sometimes Its Better To Pay For A Flight
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Theres an intricate mesh of connections between airline contracts that can leave you paying hundreds of dollars for a fuel surcharge  making a free flight, well, not close to free. Surcharges are calculated a number of ways (
       <a href="http://www.extrapackofpeanuts.com/finding-the-fuel-surcharge/" target="_blank">
        Extra Pack Of Peanuts has a good price cruncher
       </a>
       ) but in short, if the fuel charges are near the price of an economy flight, you might want to keep just buy the tickets and earn miles. Alternatively, shifting the dates of your trip by a few days may also work.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="socotra yemem gas petrol station" height="517" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/Asia/Yemen/Socotra/i-KfxMXvD/0/690x690/P1270248_49_50c-L.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         5. Layovers Under 24 Hours Dont Count As Stops
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2010/06/04/how-to-use-multi-city-flights-to-see-more-places-for-less/">
        Multi-city flights are a crafty way to see more places for less
       </a>
       and if youre up for a free stopover in one of your connecting cities, time it so the layover is just shy of 24 hours. Otherwise youll be charged miles for two legs of the route separately. Again, calling the airline and asking for the longest layover possible (I cant imagine many people do) is how you can work in a stopover, double budget travel bonus! (If that stopover happens to be in
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2010/02/25/how-to-make-the-most-out-of-your-stopover-in-iceland/">
        Iceland, heres how to make the most of it
       </a>
       .)
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Hallgrimskirkja Church Tower reykjavik iceland" height="518" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/Europe/Iceland/Reykjavik/i-cs7CF8x/0/690x690/CIMG2348-L.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         6. You Can Earn Miles Without Flying
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Those of you who are
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/countries/united-states-travel-information/">
        United States
       </a>
       or Canadian residents have the advantage of
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2010/07/06/7-ways-to-bump-your-frequent-flyer-earning-up-a-notch/">
        bumping up your earning with credit card offers
       </a>
       but its not a requirement. You can
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2011/12/08/how-to-earn-frequent-flyer-miles-without-getting-more-credit-cards/">
        earn frequent flyer miles without getting credit cards
       </a>
       and buying things you already would at the right online stores is just one of a few ways to do so.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="kuala lumpur petronas mall" height="518" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/Asia/Malaysia/Kuala-Lumpur/i-CFsN72W/0/690x690/P1140666_7_8c-XL.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        Additionally,
        <a href="http://www.extrapackofpeanuts.com/2-ways-to-get-the-targeted-65000-mile-offer-for-the-chase-united-explorer/" target="_blank">
         calling the credit card company directly
        </a>
        to ask for their best signup bonuses can often net you 40% or more miles. The biggest bonus offers are usually only mailed out to targeted (read: very frequent flyers) customers but researching those offers and politely asking for them
        <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2013/10/03/ask-frequent-flyer-hacker-get-free-tickets-fly-just-anywhere/comment-page-1/#comment-479236">
         is usually a good bet
        </a>
        .
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         7. Miles Can Be Used To Book Flights For Others
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Really struggling to find a good birthday gift? Well, your frequent flyer miles arent only for you to use, they can be redeemed for anyone you choose. A free flight to
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2013/12/17/mayan-afterlife-looks-like-photo-cenote-zaci-valladoild-mexico/">
        Mexicos Yucatan
       </a>
       is one of the few gifts that doubles for both best friends and annoying family members. Bon voyage!
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="snl high five" height="385" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/photos/i-8Q3ww87/0/O/i-8Q3ww87.gif" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         8. You Dont Have To Spend Miles On Flights
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       All of the major airline alliance programs (reread #1) let you convert miles into gifts cards or points through their online stores but not cash. Speaking of, its rarely a good idea to buy miles  one dollar buys you 25 miles on average  unless youre looking for an inexpensive bump up to a given award level (e.g. purchasing 2,000 miles to get a free upgrade at 60,000).
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="romanian lei" height="402" src="//photos.foxnomad.com/photos/i-D4rV3L3/0/690x690/i-D4rV3L3-L.jpg" width="690"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         Remember, Miles Arent Any Good If Theyre Expired
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Most earned miles with frequent flyer programs expire after 12 or 18 months but you dont have to spend them to prevent them from disappearing. You just need to use the account either by flying, spending a few bucks on through their online store, or using your program-affiliated credit card once per expiration term. Although the airlines are resisting free services like
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2010/02/16/use-award-wallet-to-keep-track-of-all-your-frequent-flyer-miles/">
        AwardWallet which make tracking your miles simple
       </a>
       , features such as automatic expiration reminders are handy to have when available.
      </p>
      <p>
       Lastly, its good to know that you can claim miles 12-18 months after youve flown, a process made much easier if you
       <a href="http://foxnomad.com/2012/05/22/hold-boarding-pass-stubs-least-4-weeks-flying-ensure-frequent-flyer-mileage-credit/">
        keep your ticket stubs for 4 weeks after any flight
       </a>
       . Aside from being the required proof you actually did fly, those stubs can help you claim miles in instances where a given flight wasnt credited to your account.
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        Frequent flyer programs arent inherently complicated to use, its the cloudy information surrounding them which makes it seem so. What are some of your best tips on using frequent flyer miles and some of the biggest mistakes youve made? Share with us in the comments so we can all start to see the friendly skies.
       </em>
      </p>

                 <div itemprop="commentText">
                  <p>
                   Its better to look at it in terms of airline alliance. So, although youre in Europe, hardly or ever flying United, American, or Delta, heres why its advantageous to sign up for each, respectively:
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Say you only fly Lufthansa, Brussels Airlines, and SAS. If you use your Lufthansa Miles and More number, the miles you earn will only be good on Lufthansa Miles and More members (13)  not SAS  although they are both Star Alliance members.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miles_%26_More" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miles_%26_More
                   </a>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   You fly Brussels Airlines or other partners occasionally and again, the miles earned are only good on those specific airlines (
                   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Alliance#Full_members_and_their_member_affiliates" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Alliance#Full_members_and_their_member_affiliates
                   </a>
                   )
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   This will scatter your airline miles, making them more difficult to redeem and tying you to one specific airline carrier or 13 instead of over 45 (Star Alliance and partner airlines).
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   *However* if you were to sign up for United Mileage Plus and use that number when you fly Lufthansa, Brussels Airlines, SAS, etc. the miles all go into your Mileage Plus account. The way the major airline alliances are set up  members like Lufthansa, Brussels Airlines, Turkish Airlines, etc. *must* honor the United miles. This lets you pool all of your Star Alliance miles as if all 28 + partner airlines were the same carrier.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Same process goes for the other two major alliances I mentioned above (i.e. oneworld and SkyTeam). I hope this helps clear that up a little bit.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   As for credit card offers, outside of the United States and Canada, youll have to look locally:
                  </p>

                   </a>
