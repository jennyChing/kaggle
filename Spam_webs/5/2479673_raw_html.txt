        <span itemprop="dateline">
         San Francisco, CA -- (
         <a href="http://www.releasewire.com/" id="sbwlink">

         </a>

        </span>
        There is something quaint and familial about walking down the main street of a small town. The lack of chain restaurants and shopping malls, the slower paced lifestyle, and the neighborly greeting that you can't find in a big city all hold great appeal. However, when the economy dips or job opportunities dry up, this can be detrimental to Small Town U.S.A.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        One town in Maryland is taking back the reigns to revive its own downtown. Real estate broker and small business owner Deborah SeBour recently created the Arbutus Commercial Revitalization Group to think of ways to improve the business corridor of Arbutus.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        "We're spending our money here to grow the business and we'd really like to see other people do the same," said SeBour, who said she recently spent $100,000 for an addition to the renovated colonial where her real estate business operates. As a local business owner, she is inspiring others to join in her pursuit to invest in revitalizing her community. "There are properties in Arbutus for sale and we're seeing turnover, where properties are now becoming available that have been owned for a long time. They need new blood, new business and young people with a little bit of money."
        <br/>
        <br/>
        According to Forbes.com, over 50% of the working population (120 million individuals) works in a small business and there are almost 28 million small businesses in the U.S. Many of these are located in small towns and are benefiting from working with

        <br/>
        These lawyers represent a virtual marketplace of thousands of attorneys located all over the United States, along with
        <a class="extlink" href="https://www.upcounsel.com/maryland-attorneys" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="lawyers in Maryland">
         lawyers in Maryland
        </a>
        , including popular cities like Baltimore. These lawyers are able to offer much more competitive costs for legal services since they are primarily based online and carry minimal overhead when compared to big law firms or expensive
        <a class="extlink" href="https://www.upcounsel.com/baltimore-attorneys" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Baltimore Lawyers">
         Baltimore Lawyers
        </a>
        . While small towns may not have a variety of lawyers available to those who need specific law expertise, online lawyers offer a larger pool of talent at a lower cost.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Many small businesses are not equipped to handle employee disputes, real estate law, filing for patents, copyrights or trademarks, or various tax laws. Business lawyers can provide the information that the entrepreneur needs to create a successful company, regardless of where he or she may live.
