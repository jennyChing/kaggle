        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Chocolate Chip Gooey Butter Bars
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-05-03T17:51:07+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 3, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.restlesschipotle.com/author/littlebit/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Marye Audet
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.restlesschipotle.com/2015/05/chocolate-chip-gooey-butter-bars/#comments">
           21 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Gooey Butter Bars
          </strong>
          are a shortbread cookie dough topped with a gooey cheesecake-like topping. This from scratch recipe includes chocolate chips and the unique nutty flavor of browned butter in the shortbread crust.
         </em>
         <span id="more-31180">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.restlesschipotle.com/2015/05/chocolate-chip-gooey-butter-bars/gooey-butter-bars-title__1430690233_108-195-76-101/" rel="attachment wp-att-31189">
          <img alt="Chocolate chip gooey butter bars" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31189" height="900" src="http://www.restlesschipotle.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Gooey-Butter-Bars-title__1430690233_108.195.76.101.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         My family pretty much takes the fact that our lives revolve around food for granted. They really have no clue that they eat really well. Well, until they move out of the house, that is. At that point there is usually a new appreciation for Moms cooking. If I sound frustrated, well I am.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, my husband told me that he needed to bring something to work for a buffet meal they were having. You know, build camaraderie, esprit de corps, and all of that. He offered to pick something up at the grocery store bakery and I thought I was going to have to slap him.
        </p>
        <p>
         
         <em>
          This is what I do for a living
         </em>
         I spat out between gritted teeth.
        </p>
        <p>
         So I wanted to do something that would knock everyones socks off  just to show the family that
         <em>
          I am frickin amazing in the kitchen.
         </em>
         I decided on these bars. I havent made them in years. I used to make them for our small group fellowships back in my other life but I had kind of forgotten about gooey butter bars until I was trying to think of something that would bring the world to its knees in appreciation or at least be something that everyone would like.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.restlesschipotle.com/2015/05/chocolate-chip-gooey-butter-bars/gooey-butter-bars-feat_optimized/" rel="attachment wp-att-31190">
          <img alt="Chocolate chip gooey butter bars are made from scratch without cake mix...restlesschipotle.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-31190" height="450" src="http://www.restlesschipotle.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/gooey-butter-bars-feat_optimized.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont know anyone who doesnt like these. They are gooey, they are buttery, they are sweet, they are indescribable perfection on a plate. These are not made from cake mix. I use almond flour to make the bottom layer a bit more like shortbread and browning the butter gives them that toasty, nutty, caramelly flavor. So good!
        </p>
        <p>
         I also add a handful of chocolate chips on top of the cookie layer before I spoon the cream cheese mixture on top.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Tip for Browning Butter
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you havent browned butter (on purpose) before you can do this  its easy. You want to let the butter melt in a heavy pan over medium low heat and once its melted leave it there, watching carefully as the clear liquid turns from a yellow color to a golden brown  about the color of caramel or maybe just slightly darker. If you get to the color of coffee youve gone too far. Watch it carefully, and push the light colored foam on the top aside so you can see the actual color of the butter.
         <br/>
        </p>

          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Melt the butter in a small pan and allow it to turn golden brown.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove from heat and allow to cool.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Blend the flour, almond flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in the bowl of a mixer.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the browned butter, eggs, vanilla, and milk.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Beat until the dough is firm and sticks together.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Press the dough into the bottom of a greased 13x9-inch pan.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Set aside.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Gooey Layer
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a clean mixer bowl blend the cream cheese, eggs, vanilla, butter, and confectioner's sugar until well blended and smooth.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Sprinkle the chocolate chips on the bottom layer then carefully pour the cream cheese mixture over the top.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake at 350F for 40 minutes. DO not overbake.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cool completely before cutting in bars.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Store, tightly covered, in the refrigerator.
           </li>
          </ol>
