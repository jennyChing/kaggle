         <h1 class="entry-title">
          10 Royalty Free Music Sites Every Online Video Creator Should Know
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div id="social-context">
         <!-- AddThis Button BEGIN -->
         <div class="addthis_toolbox">
          <ul>
           <li>
            <div class="ad_count">
            </div>

        <p class="p-meta">
         By
         <a class="authorname" href="http://www.adweek.com/socialtimes/author/megan">
          Megan O'Neill
         </a>
         <span>
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-02-10T10:30:59+00:00">
           on Feb. 10, 2011 - 10:30 AM
          </time>
         </span>
         <span>
          <a class="comment_icon" href="http://www.adweek.com/socialtimes/royalty-free-music/38475#disqus_thread">
           Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="entry-content content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.adweek.com/socialtimes/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2011/02/Music-Rainbow.jpg">
           <img alt="Music Rainbow" class="alignright size-full wp-image-38477" height="200" src="http://www.adweek.com/socialtimes/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2011/02/Music-Rainbow.jpg" width="200"/>
          </a>
          Finding free, legal music for your online videos can be difficult. As an online video creator you want the perfect soundtrack for your video masterpieces, but you also want to make sure that you dont break any copyright laws. After all, theres nothing more frustrating then working hard on a video only to have YouTube take it down because of a music
          <a href="http://www.adweek.com/socialtimes/2010/12/youtube-make-money-off-your-videos/" target="_blank">
           copyright violation
          </a>
          . Luckily there are some great sources online where you can find the perfect music for your video project. Weve put together a list of ten great sites for finding royalty free music for your online videos.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Updated:
           <a href="http://www.adweek.com/socialtimes/6-places-video-creators-can-find-royalty-free-music/3" rel="bookmark">
            6 More Places Video Creators Can Find Royalty-Free Music
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h5>
          Incompetech
         </h5>
         <p>
          <a href="http://incompetech.com/m/c/royalty-free/" target="_blank">
           Incompetech
          </a>
          is one of my go-to places for great royalty free music. The site was created by composer Kevin MacLeod and has tons of music, categorized by genre and feel. In the sites
          <a href="http://incompetech.com/m/c/royalty-free/faq.html" target="_blank">
           FAQ
          </a>
          youll see that the music on the site is free to use for your video soundtrack. Kevin just asks that you place a credit within the video. My favorite music on this particular site are the tracks in the
          <a href="http://incompetech.com/m/c/royalty-free/index.html?genre=Silent%20Film%20Score" target="_blank">
           Silent Film Score
          </a>
          section, but you can find all sorts of great stuff from horror soundtracks to polka, rock, pop and everything in between.
         </p>
         <h5>
          DanoSongs
         </h5>
         <p>
          Dan-O is a composer that offers his original songs for free download at
          <a href="http://www.danosongs.com/" target="_blank">
           DanoSongs.com
          </a>
          . You can use his songs for free in your project provided you link to his site or credit him in your video.
         </p>
         <h5>
          Moby Gratis
         </h5>
         <p>
          If you want to use music by a well-known artist without paying licensing fees then check out
          <a href="http://www.mobygratis.com/film-music.html" target="_blank">
           Moby Gratis
          </a>
          . At MobyGratis.com, Moby provides doe of his music for free download and use. The music is free to use, provided your film is non-commercial or non-profit.
         </p>
         <h5>
          Free Soundtrack Music
         </h5>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.freesoundtrackmusic.com/" target="_blank">
           FreeSoundtrackMusic.com
          </a>
          provides exactly what it advertises  royalty-free tracks for use in films, YouTube videos, games or other multimedia productions. Some of the music on the site does cost money. However, a lot of it is labeled as FREE and can be easily downloaded and added to your video production.
         </p>
         <h5>
          ccMixter
         </h5>
         <p>
          <a href="http://ccmixter.org/" target="_blank">
           ccMixter
          </a>
          is a community music site where you can find tons of music that falls under the
          <a href="http://creativecommons.org/" target="_blank">
           Creative Commons
          </a>
          license. The site lets you listen to music, sample music, create mashups and more. Just keep in mind that some creative commons licenses may require that you give credit to the source within your video.
         </p>
         <h5>
          Partners In Rhyme
         </h5>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.partnersinrhyme.com/" target="_blank">
           Partners In Rhyme
          </a>
          has got all sorts of great stuff, from free music loops to sound effects, midi files and more. Some of the royalty free music on Partners In Rhyme costs money, but theyve got a selection of
          <a href="http://www.partnersinrhyme.com/pir/free_music_loops.shtml" target="_blank">
           free music loops
          </a>
          and full-length tracks for free download as well.
         </p>
         <h5>
          PacDV
         </h5>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.pacdv.com/sounds/free-music.html" target="_blank">
           PacDV
          </a>
          offers free music and sound effects. The tracks are free to use in your video, film, audio and multimedia projects. All they ask is that if you use their tracks you link back to their site or list them in your credits.
         </p>
         <h5>
          Public Domain 4U
         </h5>
         <p>
          When content falls into the public domain it means that there are no longer any intellectual property rights attached to them, either because the rights have expired or been forfeited. Public domain music, video and other content can be used in any way, including in your online videos.
          <a href="http://www.publicdomain4u.com/" target="_blank">
           Public Domain 4U
          </a>
          is a great site for finding public domain music downloads. A lot of the music is old (music from the early 1900s is now in the public domain if copyright was never renewed), but there is also more modern music that is free with artist permission.
         </p>
         <h5>
          Musopen
         </h5>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.musopen.org/" target="_blank">
           Musopen
          </a>
          is another free site that, like Public Domain 4U, provides music that has the copyright expired. On the sites
          <a href="http://www.musopen.org/about.php" target="_blank">
           about page
          </a>
          they say, We provide recordings, sheet music, and textbooks to the public for free, without copyright restrictions. At Musopen you can browse music by composer, performer, instrument, period or form. Musopen is great, especially if you are looking for classical music for your video.
         </p>
         <h5>
          Beatpick
         </h5>
         <p>
          Finally,
          <a href="http://www.beatpick.com/player/index" target="_blank">
           Beatpick
          </a>
          offers a great selection of licensed music and if you are using it in a non-commercial or non-profit production its free. Once youve chosen a song that you want to use, click on License Song and choose Non Commercial projects.
         </p>
         <p>
          Have you used any of these services to find music for your online videos? If not, what services do you recommend?
         </p>
