         <p>
          Parents may be fed up of hearing youngsters belt out songs from
          <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/frozen">
           Disney classic Frozen
          </a>
          - but one Tyneside couple will always have a special place for the film in their hearts.
         </p>
         <p>
          Mark Deeks and Angela Lawery got engaged after lip-synching their way through a track from the film.
         </p>
         <p>
          And they even managed to film their special moment to treasure forever.
         </p>
         <p>
          Mark, 37, a musical director from Newcastle, said: I have to take responsibility for it, Angela is a big music theatre fan and Frozen is one of her favourite films - shes a big kid at heart.
         </p>
         <p>
          I wanted to incorporate some of this in proposing.
         </p>
         <figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
          <div class="image-credit-wrapper">
           <span class="image-credit">
            Chronicle
           </span>
           <img alt="Mark Deeks and Angela Lawery" class="captify" height="409" src="http://i4.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article4901624.ece/ALTERNATES/s615/Mark-Deeks-and-Angela-Lawery.jpg" title="Wedding bells: Happy couple to be Mark Deeks and Angela Lawery" width="615"/>
          </div>
          <figcaption>
           Wedding bells: Happy couple to be Mark Deeks and Angela Lawery
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>
          The Camperdown couple have watched the Disney film together countless times, and love to sing along to the soundtrack,
          <a href="http://www.chroniclelive.co.uk/news/north-east-news/watch-tyneside-couples-frozen-marriage-8365885">
           reports the Gazette.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          They had a long-running joke that if Mark ever proposed they would make a film based on them lip-synching to one of the tracks, Love Is An Open Door.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the song, Prince Hans
          <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/marriage">
           proposes
          </a>
          to Princess Anna.
         </p>
         <p>
          For months if we went out in the car we would lip-synch to the song as a joke but Angela didnt realise it was really me rehearsing, said Mark.
         </p>
         <p>
          When wed get the end of the song where the prince proposes, Id joke that I wouldnt need to practice that part.
         </p>
         <p>
          Before we did it I hadnt mentioned marriage for a while to try and throw her off the trail - to the point where a couple of days before, Angela had a go at me about it.
         </p>
         <p>
          On the big day, Mark asked his friend, photographer Serena Taylor, to meet the couple in Rising Sun Park, in Benton, as he and Angela went for a walk.
         </p>
         <figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
          <div class="image-credit-wrapper">
           <span class="image-credit">
            Disney
           </span>
           <img alt="Queen Elsa" class="captify" height="409" src="http://i2.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article4843793.ece/ALTERNATES/s615/Queen-Elsa.jpg" title="Queen Elsa: Frozen has sold more than 3.8m DVDs since its release" width="615"/>
          </div>
          <figcaption>
           Queen Elsa: Frozen has sold more than 3.8m DVDs since its release
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>
          The chance encounter then saw Serena mention Frozen - which prompted Mark and Angela to show off their lip-synching skills while Serena snapped away.
         </p>
         <p>
          As Mark got down on one knee to pop the question, Serena captured the moment on film.
         </p>
         <p>
          Angela, a 26-year-old teacher at Langley First School, in Monkseaton, North Tyneside, was visibly stunned - but fortunately she said yes.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now, the couple are planning their wedding, due to take place at Easter 2016. And even though they arent planning a Frozen theme, there might be the odd reference to the film.
         </p>
         <p>
          Mark said: I wont be buying icicles or balloons.
         </p>
         <p>
          Weve had a bit of stick but Im sure one of the songs will work its way in.
         </p>
         <p>
          Angela added: Mark doesnt want it to be Frozen-themed but Im sure there will be something.
         </p>
         <p>
          In the four weeks
          <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/christmas-gifts">
           before Christmas,
          </a>
          Frozen shifted more than 790,000 copies, and has sold more than 3.8m DVDs since its release.
         </p>
