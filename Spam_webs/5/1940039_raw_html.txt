          <a href="http://www.iogostevia.ca">
           <img alt="The new stevia sweetener" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-301220" src="http://ca.glam.com/files/2013/08/CA_iogoStevia_Collage6.jpg"/>

         <p>
          A quick stroll down the confectionary aisle of any grocery store will reveal an endless array of sugar alternatives lining the shelves. But how do the health-conscious with a sweet tooth decide which are actually good for you vs. too good to be true? The latest sweetener to crop up on ingredient lists is stevia extract. We wanted to get the lowdown on how the natural alternative measures up to other sweeteners.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1. What is stevia?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Stevia extract is a sweetener found in the stevia plant native to Paraguay. The sweetener compounds are manufactured by infusing the dried leaves to extract steviol glycosides, which make the stevia extract sweet.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2.  Is stevia really safe?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          While the Guarani Natives of South America have been using stevia leaves for centuries, stevia extract has been popping up on ingredient lists in Canada since November of 2012 when Health Canada gave it the green light. Its also been FDA approved in the US since 2008. Research has shown that consuming stevia in a single large dose or lower doses over a longer period of time has no toxic effect.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3. How is it added to foods?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Because stevia extract on its own can leave a slightly bitter aftertaste, it is often added to food alongside a small amount of sugar to keep calories low without noticeably compromising taste. Stevia extract is both calorie-free and carb-free, so its diet-friendly.
         </p>
