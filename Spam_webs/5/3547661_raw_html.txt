     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Sovereign Military Hospitaller Order of Saint John of Jerusalem of Rhodes and of Malta
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
       Italian
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="it">
       <i>
        Sovrano Militare Ordine Ospedaliero di San Giovanni di Gerusalemme di Rodi e di Malta
       </i>
      </span>
      ), also known as the
      <b>
       Sovereign Military Order of Malta
      </b>
      (
      <b>
       SMOM
      </b>
      ),
      <b>
       Order of Malta
      </b>
      or
      <b>
       Knights of Malta
      </b>
      , is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">
       Roman Catholic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Laity" title="Laity">
       lay
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Religious_order" title="Religious order">
       religious order
      </a>
      of, traditionally, a military,
      <a href="/wiki/Chivalry" title="Chivalry">
       chivalrous
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">
       noble
      </a>
      nature.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is the world's oldest surviving
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_chivalry" title="Order of chivalry">
       order of chivalry
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Sovereign Military Order of Malta is headquartered in
      <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">
       Rome
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
       Italy
      </a>
      , and is widely considered a
      <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">
       sovereign
      </a>
      subject of
      <a href="/wiki/International_law" title="International law">
       international law
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      SMOM is the modern continuation of the original medieval order of Saint John of Jerusalem,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      known as the
      <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">
       <i>
        "Fraternitas Hospitalaria"
       </i>
      </a>
      and later as the
      <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">
       Knights Hospitaller
      </a>
      , a group founded in
      <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">
       Jerusalem
      </a>
      around the year 1050 as an
      <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Amalfi" title="Duchy of Amalfi">
       Amalfitan
      </a>
      hospital to provide care for poor and sick
      <a href="/wiki/Pilgrimage" title="Pilgrimage">
       pilgrims
      </a>
      to the
      <a href="/wiki/Holy_Land" title="Holy Land">
       Holy Land
      </a>
      . After the conquest of Jerusalem in 1099 during the
      <a href="/wiki/First_Crusade" title="First Crusade">
       First Crusade
      </a>
      , it became a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_order_(society)" title="Military order (society)">
       military order
      </a>
      under its own charter. Following the loss of Christian held territories of the Holy Land to
      <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">
       Muslims
      </a>
      , the order operated from
      <a href="/wiki/Rhodes" title="Rhodes">
       Rhodes
      </a>
      (13101523), and later from
      <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">
       Malta
      </a>
      (15301798), over which it was sovereign.
     </p>
     <p>
      Although this state came to an end with the ejection of the order from Malta by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">
       Napoleon Bonaparte
      </a>
      , the order as such survived. It retains its claims of
      <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">
       sovereignty
      </a>
      under international law and has been granted
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_observers" title="United Nations General Assembly observers">
       permanent observer status
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">
       United Nations
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The order is notable for issuing its own international
      <a href="/wiki/Passport" title="Passport">
       passports
      </a>
      for travel, postal stamps, along with its formal insignia, often portrayed as a white or gold
      <a href="/wiki/Maltese_cross" title="Maltese cross">
       Maltese cross
      </a>
      . The order nominally invokes the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blessed_Virgin_Mary" title="Blessed Virgin Mary">
       Blessed Virgin Mary
      </a>
      under the venerated Marian title of
      <i>
       "Our Lady of Mount Philermos"
      </i>
      as its
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patroness" title="Patroness">
       patroness
      </a>
      and spiritual
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intercessor" title="Intercessor">
       intercessor
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Today the order has about 13,000 members, including Knights and Dames as well as auxiliary members; 80,000 permanent volunteers; and 20,000 medical personnel including doctors, nurses, auxiliaries and paramedics in more than 120 countries.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-M13000_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-M13000-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The goal is to assist the elderly, handicapped, refugeed, children, homeless, those with terminal illness and leprosy in all parts of the world, without distinction of race or religion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-M13000_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-M13000-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In several countriesincluding France, Germany and Irelandthe local associations of the order are important providers of first aid training, first aid services and emergency medical services. Through its worldwide relief corps
      <a href="/wiki/Malteser_International" title="Malteser International">
       Malteser International
      </a>
      the order is also engaged to aid victims of natural disasters, epidemics and armed conflicts.
     </p>
     <p>
      In February 2013 the order celebrated its 900th anniversary recognising the
      <a href="/wiki/Papal_bull" title="Papal bull">
       Papal bull
      </a>
      of sovereignty
      <i>
       "Pie Postulatio Voluntatis"
      </i>
      formally issued by
      <a href="/wiki/Pope_Paschal_II" title="Pope Paschal II">
       Pope Paschal II
      </a>
      on 15 February 1113, with a general audience given by
      <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">
       Pope Benedict XVI
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Mass" title="Holy Mass">
       Holy Mass
      </a>
      celebrated by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vatican_Secretary_of_State" title="Vatican Secretary of State">
       Vatican Secretary of State
      </a>
      Cardinal
      <a href="/wiki/Tarcisio_Bertone" title="Tarcisio Bertone">
       Tarcisio Bertone
      </a>
      at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Peter%27s_Basilica" title="Saint Peter's Basilica">
       Saint Peter's Basilica
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Name_and_insignia">
       Name and insignia
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name and insignia">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bust_portrait_of_a_knight_of_Malta_cropped.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="454" data-file-width="354" height="282" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Bust_portrait_of_a_knight_of_Malta_cropped.jpg/220px-Bust_portrait_of_a_knight_of_Malta_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Bust_portrait_of_a_knight_of_Malta_cropped.jpg/330px-Bust_portrait_of_a_knight_of_Malta_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1b/Bust_portrait_of_a_knight_of_Malta_cropped.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bust_portrait_of_a_knight_of_Malta_cropped.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Bust portrait of a Knight of Malta
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The order has a large number of local priories and associations around the world, but there also exist a number of organizations with similar-sounding names that are unrelated, including numerous fraudulent (self-styled) orders seeking to capitalize on the name.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the
      <a href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical_heraldry" title="Ecclesiastical heraldry">
       ecclesiastical heraldry
      </a>
      of the Roman Catholic Church, the Order of Malta is one of only two orders (along with the
      <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Holy_Sepulchre" title="Order of the Holy Sepulchre">
       Order of the Holy Sepulchre
      </a>
      ) whose insignia may be displayed in a clerical
      <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">
       coat of arms
      </a>
      . (Laypersons have no such restriction.) The shield is surrounded with a silver
      <a href="/wiki/Rosary" title="Rosary">
       rosary
      </a>
      for professed knights, or for others the ribbon of their rank. Members may also display the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maltese_Cross" title="Maltese Cross">
       Maltese Cross
      </a>
      behind their shield instead of the ribbon.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">
       Knights Hospitaller
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The birth of the order dates back to around 1048. Merchants from the ancient
      <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Amalfi" title="Duchy of Amalfi">
       Marine Republic of Amalfi
      </a>
      obtained from the Caliph of Egypt the authorisation to build a church, convent, and hospital in Jerusalem, to care for pilgrims of any religious faith or race. The Order of St. John of Jerusalem  the monastic community that ran the hospital for the pilgrims in the Holy Land  became independent under the guidance of its founder, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blessed_Gerard" title="Blessed Gerard">
       Blessed Gerard
      </a>
      . With the Bull of 15 February 1113,
      <a href="/wiki/Pope_Paschal_II" title="Pope Paschal II">
       Pope Paschal II
      </a>
      approved the foundation of the Hospital and placed it under the aegis of the Holy See, granting it the right to freely elect its superiors without interference from other secular or religious authorities. By virtue of the Papal Bull, the hospital became an order exempt from the control of the local church. All the Knights were religious, bound by the three monastic vows of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Poverty,_chastity_and_obedience&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Poverty, chastity and obedience (page does not exist)">
       poverty, chastity and obedience
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The constitution of the
      <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Jerusalem" title="Kingdom of Jerusalem">
       Kingdom of Jerusalem
      </a>
      regarding the crusades obliged the order to take on the military defence of the sick, the pilgrims, and the territories that the crusaders had captured from the Muslims. The order thus added the task of defending the faith to that of its hospitaller mission.
     </p>
     <p>
      As time went on, the order adopted the white eight-pointed Cross that is still its symbol today. The eight points represent the eight "beatitudes" that Jesus referred to in his
      <a href="/wiki/Sermon_on_the_Mount" title="Sermon on the Mount">
       Sermon on the Mount
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Rhodes">
       Rhodes
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rhodes">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      When the last Christian stronghold in the Holy Land fell after the
      <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Acre" title="Siege of Acre">
       Siege of Acre
      </a>
      in 1291, the order settled first in Cyprus and then, in 1310, led by Grand Master Fra
      <a href="/wiki/Foulques_de_Villaret" title="Foulques de Villaret">
       Foulques de Villaret
      </a>
      , on the island of
      <a href="/wiki/Rhodes" title="Rhodes">
       Rhodes
      </a>
      . From there, defense of the Christian world required the organization of a naval force; so the Order built a powerful fleet and sailed the eastern Mediterranean, fighting many famous battles for the sake of Christendom, including Crusades in Syria and Egypt.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the early 14th century, the institutions of the order and the knights who came to Rhodes from every corner of Europe were grouped according to the languages they spoke. The initial seven such groups, or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tongue_(Knights_Hospitaller)" title="Tongue (Knights Hospitaller)">
       <i>
        Langues
       </i>
       (Tongues)
      </a>
       Provence, Auvergne, France, Italy, Aragon (Navarre), England (with Scotland and Ireland), and Germany  became eight in 1492, when Castille and Portugal were separated from the
      <i>
       Langue
      </i>
      of Aragon. Each
      <i>
       Langue
      </i>
      included
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Priories" title="Priories">
       Priories
      </a>
      or Grand Priories,
      <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick" title="Bailiwick">
       Bailiwicks
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Commandry_(feudalism)" title="Commandry (feudalism)">
       Commanderies
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The order was governed by its Grand Master (the Prince of Rhodes) and Council. From its beginning, independence from other nations granted by pontifical charter and the universally recognised right to maintain and deploy armed forces constituted grounds for the international sovereignty of the Order, which minted its own coins and maintained diplomatic relations with other States. The senior positions of the order were given to representatives of different
      <i>
       Langues
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Malta">
       Malta
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Malta">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/History_of_Malta_under_the_Order_of_Saint_John" title="History of Malta under the Order of Saint John">
       History of Malta under the Order of Saint John
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      After six months of siege and fierce combat against the fleet and army of Sultan
      <a href="/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent" title="Suleiman the Magnificent">
       Suleiman the Magnificent
      </a>
      , the Knights were forced to surrender in 1523 and left Rhodes with military honours. The order remained without a territory of its own until 1530, when Grand Master Fra Philippe de Villiers de lIsle Adam took possession of the island of
      <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">
       Malta
      </a>
      , granted to the order by Emperor Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor and his mother Queen Joanna of Castile as monarchs of Sicily, with the approval of Pope
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clement_VII" title="Clement VII">
       Clement VII
      </a>
      , for which the order had to honour the conditions of the
      <a href="/wiki/Tribute_of_the_Maltese_Falcon" title="Tribute of the Maltese Falcon">
       Tribute of the Maltese Falcon
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reformation" title="Reformation">
       Reformation
      </a>
      which split Western Europe into
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protestant" title="Protestant">
       Protestant
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">
       Roman Catholic
      </a>
      states affected the Knights as well. In several countries, including England and Scotland, the order was disestablished. In others, including the Netherlands and Germany, entire bailiwicks or commanderies (administrative divisions of the order) experienced religious conversions. The
      <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_John_(Bailiwick_of_Brandenburg)" title="Order of Saint John (Bailiwick of Brandenburg)">
       "
       <i>
        Johanniter
       </i>
       orders"
      </a>
      are the continuations of these converted divisions in
      <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
       Germany
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">
       Netherlands
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">
       Sweden
      </a>
      , and other countries, including the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
       South Africa
      </a>
      . It was established that the order should remain neutral in any war between Christian nations.
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dt>
       Great Siege
      </dt>
     </dl>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Great_Siege_of_Malta" title="Great Siege of Malta">
       Great Siege of Malta
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      In 1565 the Knights, led by Grand Master Fra Jean de Vallette (after whom the capital of Malta,
      <a href="/wiki/Valletta" title="Valletta">
       Valletta
      </a>
      , was named), defended the island for more than three months during the Great Siege by the Turks. The fleet of the order contributed to the ultimate destruction of the Ottoman naval power in the
      <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lepanto" title="Battle of Lepanto">
       Battle of Lepanto
      </a>
      in 1571, led by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_Juan_of_Austria" title="Don Juan of Austria">
       Don Juan of Austria
      </a>
      , half brother of King
      <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">
       Philip II of Spain
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean">
       Caribbean
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Caribbean">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Hospitaller_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Hospitaller colonization of the Americas">
       Hospitaller colonization of the Americas
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hospitaller_colonization.png">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="603" data-file-width="1123" height="118" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Hospitaller_colonization.png/220px-Hospitaller_colonization.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Hospitaller_colonization.png/330px-Hospitaller_colonization.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Hospitaller_colonization.png/440px-Hospitaller_colonization.png 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hospitaller_colonization.png" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The order's colonies in the Caribbean
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      From 1651 to 1665, the Order of Saint John ruled four islands in the Caribbean. On 21 May 1651, it acquired the islands of
      <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint Barthlemy">
       Saint Barthlemy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts" title="Saint Kitts">
       Saint Christopher
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Saint_Croix,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Croix, U.S. Virgin Islands">
       Saint Croix
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">
       Saint Martin
      </a>
      . These were purchased from the French
      <a href="/wiki/Compagnie_des_%C3%8Eles_de_l%27Am%C3%A9rique" title="Compagnie des les de l'Amrique">
       Compagnie des les de l'Amrique
      </a>
      which had just been dissolved. In 1665 the four islands were sold to the
      <a href="/wiki/French_West_India_Company" title="French West India Company">
       French West India Company
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Exile">
       Exile
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Exile">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Two hundred years later, in 1798, the order surrendered the Maltese islands to the French First Republic. The knights were expelled from Malta.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Amiens" title="Treaty of Amiens">
       Treaty of Amiens
      </a>
      (1802) obliged the United Kingdom to evacuate Malta which was to be restored to a recreated Order of St. John, whose sovereignty was to be guaranteed by all of the major European powers, to be determined at the final peace. However, this was not to be because objections to the treaty quickly grew in the UK.
     </p>
     <p>
      Bonaparte's rejection of a British offer involving a ten-year lease of Malta prompted the reactivation of the British blockade of the French coast; Britain declared war on France on 18 May.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The 1802 treaty was never implemented. The UK gave its official reasons for resuming hostilities as France's imperialist policies in the West Indies, Italy and Switzerland.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Rome">
       Rome
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Rome">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      After having temporarily resided in Messina, Catania, and Ferrara, in 1834 the precursor of the Sovereign Military Order of Malta settled definitively in Rome, where it owns, with
      <a href="/wiki/Extraterritoriality" title="Extraterritoriality">
       extraterritorial
      </a>
      status, the
      <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Malta" title="Palazzo Malta">
       Magistral Palace
      </a>
      in Via Condotti 68 and the
      <a href="/wiki/Villa_del_Priorato_di_Malta" title="Villa del Priorato di Malta">
       Magistral Villa
      </a>
      on the
      <a href="/wiki/Aventine_Hill" title="Aventine Hill">
       Aventine Hill
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The original hospitaller mission became the main activity of the order, growing ever stronger during the last century, most especially because of the contribution of the activities carried out by the Grand Priories and National Associations in so many countries around the world. Large-scale hospitaller and charitable activities were carried out during World Wars I and II under Grand Master Fra
      <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Chigi_Albani_della_Rovere" title="Ludovico Chigi Albani della Rovere">
       Ludovico Chigi Albani della Rovere
      </a>
      (19311951). Under the Grand Masters Fra
      <a href="/wiki/Angelo_de_Mojana_di_Cologna" title="Angelo de Mojana di Cologna">
       Angelo de Mojana di Cologna
      </a>
      (19621988) and Fra
      <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Bertie" title="Andrew Bertie">
       Andrew Bertie
      </a>
      (19882008), the projects expanded.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Malta">
       Return to Malta
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Return to Malta">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:St-Angelo-Malta.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="703" data-file-width="1176" height="132" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/St-Angelo-Malta.jpg/220px-St-Angelo-Malta.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/St-Angelo-Malta.jpg/330px-St-Angelo-Malta.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/St-Angelo-Malta.jpg/440px-St-Angelo-Malta.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:St-Angelo-Malta.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Flags of Malta and the SMOM on
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Saint_Angelo" title="Fort Saint Angelo">
         Fort Saint Angelo
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Two bilateral treaties have been concluded with the Government of the Maltese State. The first treaty is dated 21 June 1991 and is now no longer in force.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The second treaty was signed on 5 December 1998, but ratified on 1 November 2001.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      This agreement grants the Order the use with limited extraterritoriality of the upper portion of
      <a href="/wiki/Fort_St_Angelo" title="Fort St Angelo">
       Fort St Angelo
      </a>
      in the city of
      <a href="/wiki/Birgu" title="Birgu">
       Birgu
      </a>
      . Its stated purpose is "
      <i>
       to give the Order the opportunity to be better enabled to carry out its humanitarian activities as Knights Hospitallers from Saint Angelo, as well as to better define the legal status of Saint Angelo subject to the sovereignity of Malta over it"
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The agreement has a duration of 99 years, but the document allows the Maltese Government to terminate it at any time after 50 years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Under the terms of the agreement, the flag of Malta is to be flown together with the flag of the Order in a prominent position over Saint Angelo. No asylum may be granted by the Order and generally the Maltese courts have full jurisdiction and Maltese law shall apply.
     </p>
     <p>
      A number of immunities and privileges are mentioned in the second bilateral treaty. No such immunities were contemplated by the first treaty.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="International_status">
       International status
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International status">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote">
      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_the_Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta" title="Foreign relations of the Sovereign Military Order of Malta">
       Foreign relations of the Sovereign Military Order of Malta
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Permanent_Observers_of_the_Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta_to_the_United_Nations" title="List of Permanent Observers of the Sovereign Military Order of Malta to the United Nations">
       List of Permanent Observers of the Sovereign Military Order of Malta to the United Nations
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_of_the_Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta" title="List of diplomatic missions of the Sovereign Military Order of Malta">
       List of diplomatic missions of the Sovereign Military Order of Malta
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_to_the_Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta" title="List of diplomatic missions to the Sovereign Military Order of Malta">
       List of diplomatic missions to the Sovereign Military Order of Malta
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      With its unique history and unusual present circumstances, the exact status of the Order in international law has been the subject of debate. It describes itself as a "sovereign subject of international law." Its two headquarters in
      <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">
       Rome
      </a>
       the
      <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Malta" title="Palazzo Malta">
       Palazzo Malta
      </a>
      in Via dei Condotti 68, where the Grand Master resides and Government Bodies meet, and the
      <a href="/wiki/Villa_del_Priorato_di_Malta" title="Villa del Priorato di Malta">
       Villa del Priorato di Malta
      </a>
      on the
      <a href="/wiki/Aventine_Hill" title="Aventine Hill">
       Aventine
      </a>
      , which hosts the Grand Priory of Rome 
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Saint_Angelo" title="Fort Saint Angelo">
       Fort Saint Angelo
      </a>
      on the island of Malta, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embassy" title="Embassy">
       Embassy
      </a>
      of the Order to
      <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">
       Holy See
      </a>
      and the Embassy of the Order to
      <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
       Italy
      </a>
      have all been granted
      <a href="/wiki/Extraterritoriality" title="Extraterritoriality">
       extraterritoriality
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri_stemma_Cavalieri_di_Malta.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2524" data-file-width="3236" height="172" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri_stemma_Cavalieri_di_Malta.JPG/220px-San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri_stemma_Cavalieri_di_Malta.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri_stemma_Cavalieri_di_Malta.JPG/330px-San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri_stemma_Cavalieri_di_Malta.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri_stemma_Cavalieri_di_Malta.JPG/440px-San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri_stemma_Cavalieri_di_Malta.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri_stemma_Cavalieri_di_Malta.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Coat of arms of the Knights of Malta,
        <br/>
        from the faade of the church of
        <a href="/wiki/San_Giovannino_dei_Cavalieri" title="San Giovannino dei Cavalieri">
         San Giovannino dei Cavalieri
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">
         Florence
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Unlike the
      <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">
       Holy See
      </a>
      , however, which is sovereign over
      <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">
       Vatican City
      </a>
      and thus has clear territorial separation of its sovereign area and that of Italy, SMOM has had no territory since the loss of the island of Malta in 1798, other than only those current properties with extraterritoriality listed above. Italy recognizes, in addition to extraterritoriality, the exercise by SMOM of all the prerogatives of sovereignty in its headquarters. Therefore, Italian sovereignty and SMOM sovereignty uniquely coexist without overlapping.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">
       United Nations
      </a>
      does not classify it as a "non-member state" or "
      <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">
       intergovernmental organization
      </a>
      " but as one of the "other
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_observers" title="United Nations General Assembly observers">
       entities
      </a>
      having received a standing invitation to participate as observers."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      For instance, while the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Telecommunication_Union" title="International Telecommunication Union">
       International Telecommunication Union
      </a>
      has granted radio identification prefixes to such quasi-sovereign jurisdictions as the United Nations and the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palestinian_Authority" title="Palestinian Authority">
       Palestinian Authority
      </a>
      , SMOM has never received one. For awards purposes, amateur radio operators consider SMOM to be a separate "entity", but stations transmitting from there use an entirely unofficial
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Callsign" title="Callsign">
       callsign
      </a>
      , starting with the prefix "1A".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Likewise, for internet and telecommunications identification, the SMOM has neither sought nor been granted a
      <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">
       top-level domain
      </a>
      or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Country_calling_code" title="Country calling code">
       international dialling code
      </a>
      , whereas the Vatican City uses its own domain (
      <a href="/wiki/.va" title=".va">
       .va
      </a>
      ),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and has been allocated the country code
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%2B379" title="+379">
       +379
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      There are differing opinions as to whether a claim to sovereign status has been recognized.
      <a href="/wiki/Ian_Brownlie" title="Ian Brownlie">
       Ian Brownlie
      </a>
      , Helmut Steinberger, and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wilhelm_Wengler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wilhelm Wengler (page does not exist)">
       Wilhelm Wengler
      </a>
      are among experts who say that the claim has not been recognized. Even taking into account the Order's
      <a href="/wiki/Ambassador" title="Ambassador">
       ambassadorial
      </a>
      diplomatic status among many nations, a claim to sovereign status is sometimes rejected.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Order maintains
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_of_Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta" title="List of diplomatic missions of Sovereign Military Order of Malta">
       diplomatic missions
      </a>
      around the world and many of the states reciprocate by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_in_Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta" title="List of diplomatic missions in Sovereign Military Order of Malta">
       accrediting ambassadors to the Order
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <span id="Vehicle_registration_plate">
      </span>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Smom-reg-plate.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="214" data-file-width="823" height="57" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Smom-reg-plate.jpg/220px-Smom-reg-plate.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Smom-reg-plate.jpg/330px-Smom-reg-plate.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Smom-reg-plate.jpg/440px-Smom-reg-plate.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Smom-reg-plate.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_registration_plate" title="Vehicle registration plate">
         Vehicle registration plate
        </a>
        of the Order, as seen in
        <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">
         Rome
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Wenglera German professor of international lawaddresses this point in his book
      <i>
       Vlkerrecht
      </i>
      (1964), and rejects the notion that recognition of the Order by some states can make it a subject of international law. Conversely, professor Rebecca Wallace writing more recently in her book
      <i>
       International Law
      </i>
      (1986)explains that a sovereign entity does not have to be a country, and that SMOM is an example of this.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This position appears to be supported by the number of nations extending diplomatic relations to the Order, which more than doubled from 49 to 100 in the 20-year period to 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1953, the Holy See decreed that the Order of Malta's quality as a sovereign institution is functional, to ensure the achievement of its purposes in the world, and that as a subject of international law, it enjoys certain powers, but not the entire set of powers of sovereignty "in the full sense of the word."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On 24 June 1961, Pope John XXIII approved the Constitutional Charter, which contains the most solemn reaffirmations of the sovereignty of the Order. Article 1 affirms that "the Order is a legal entity formally approved by the Holy See. It has the quality of a subject of international law." Article 3 states that "the intimate connection existing between the two qualities of a religious order and a sovereign order do not oppose the autonomy of the order in the exercise of its sovereignty and prerogatives inherent to it as a subject of international law in relation to States."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      SMOM has formal diplomatic relations with 105 states
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relations_32-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-relations-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and has official relations with another six countries and the
      <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
       European Union
      </a>
      . Additionally it has relations with the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Committee_of_the_Red_Cross" title="International Committee of the Red Cross">
       International Committee of the Red Cross
      </a>
      and a number of international organizations, including
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_observers#Other_entities" title="United Nations General Assembly observers">
       observer status
      </a>
      at the UN and some of the
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_specialized_agencies_of_the_United_Nations" title="List of specialized agencies of the United Nations">
       specialized agencies
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its international nature is useful in enabling it to pursue its humanitarian activities without being seen as an operative of any particular nation. Its sovereignty is also expressed in the issuance of
      <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta_passport" title="Sovereign Military Order of Malta passport">
       passports
      </a>
      , licence plates,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      stamps,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and coins.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
       <a href="#cite_note-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      The proceedings of the Order are governed by its Constitutional
      <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">

      </a>

     <p>
      The supreme head of the Order is the
      <a href="/wiki/Grand_Master_(order)" title="Grand Master (order)">
       Grand Master
      </a>
      , who is elected for life by the Council Complete of State, holds the precedence of a
      <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">
       cardinal
      </a>
      of the Church since 1630 and received the rank of
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reichsf%C3%BCrst" title="Reichsfrst">

       Fra' Matthew Festing
      </a>
      was elected by the Council as 79th Grand Master on 11 March 2008, succeeding
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fra%27_Andrew_Bertie" title="Fra' Andrew Bertie">
       Fra' Andrew Bertie
      </a>
      , who was Grand Master until his death on 7 February 2008. Electors in the Council include the members of the Sovereign Council, other office-holders and representatives of the members of the Order. The Grand Master is aided by the Sovereign Council (the government of the Order), which is elected by the Chapter General, the legislative body of the Order. The Chapter General meets every five years; at each meeting, all seats of the Sovereign Council are up for election. The Sovereign Council includes six members and four High Officers: the
      <a href="/wiki/Gherardo_Hercolani_Fava_Simonetti" title="Gherardo Hercolani Fava Simonetti">
       Grand Commander
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Mazery" title="Jean-Pierre Mazery">
       Grand Chancellor
      </a>
      , the Grand Hospitaller
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
       <a href="#cite_note-40">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        40
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the Receiver of the Common Treasure.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
       <a href="#cite_note-41">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        41
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Grand Commander is the chief religious officer of the Order and serves as "Interim Lieutenant" during a vacancy in the office of Grand Master. The Grand Chancellor, whose office includes those of the Ministry of the Interior and Ministry of Foreign Affairs, is the head of the executive branch; he is responsible for the Diplomatic Missions of the Order and relations with the national Associations. The Grand Hospitaller's responsibilities include the offices of Minister for Humanitarian Action and Minister for International Cooperation; he coordinates the Order's humanitarian and charitable activities. Finally, the Receiver of the Common Treasure is the Minister of Finance and Budget; he directs the administration of the finances and property of the Order.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">
       Membership
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Membership">

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Knight_of_Malta_XXI_century.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1383" data-file-width="588" height="282" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Knight_of_Malta_XXI_century.JPG/120px-Knight_of_Malta_XXI_century.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Knight_of_Malta_XXI_century.JPG/180px-Knight_of_Malta_XXI_century.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Knight_of_Malta_XXI_century.JPG/240px-Knight_of_Malta_XXI_century.JPG 2x" width="120"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Knight_of_Malta_XXI_century.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        A Knight of Grace and Devotion in contemporary
        <a href="/wiki/Religious_habit" title="Religious habit">
         habit
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Membership in the order is divided into three classes and subdivided into several categories, i.e.:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
       <a href="#cite_note-43">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        43
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <b>
        First Class
       </b>
       , containing only one category:
       <i>
        Knights of Justice
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        Professed Knights
       </i>
       , and the
       <i>
        Professed Conventual Chaplains
       </i>
       , who take
       <a href="/wiki/Religious_vows" title="Religious vows">
        religious vows
       </a>
       of poverty, chastity, and obedience and form what amounts to a religious order (until the 1990s membership in this class was restricted to members of families with noble lineages).
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Second Class
       </b>
       :
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Knight_of_Obedience" title="Knight of Obedience">
         Knight and Dames of Obedience
        </a>
       </i>
       , similarly restricted until recently, these knights and dames make a promise, rather than a vow, of obedience. This class is subdivided into three categories, namely that of
       <i>
        Knight and Dames of Honour and Devotion in Obedience
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Knight and Dames of Grace and Devotion in Obedience
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        Knight and Dames of Magistral Grace in Obedience
       </i>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Third Class
       </b>
       , which is subdivided into six categories:
       <i>
        Knights and Dames of Honour and Devotion
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Conventual Chaplains ad honorem
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Knights and Dames of Grace and Devotion
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Magistral Chaplains
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Knights and Dames of Magistral Grace
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        Donats (male and female) of Devotion.
       </i>
       All categories of this class are made up of members who take no vows and who had to show a decreasingly extensive history of nobility (knights of magistral grace need not prove any noble lineage and are the commonest class of knights in the United States).
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Within each class and category of knights are ranks ranging from bailiff grand cross (the highest) through knight grand cross, and knight  thus one could be a "knight of grace and devotion," or a "bailiff grand cross of justice." The final rank of
      <i>
       donat
      </i>
      is offered to some who join the order in the class of "justice" but who are not knights. Bishops and priests are generally honorary members, or knights, of the Order of Malta. However, there are some priests who are full members of the Order, and this is usually because they were conferred knighthood prior to ordination. The priests of the Order of Malta are ranked as Honorary Canons, as in the Order of the Holy Sepulchre; and they are entitled to wear the black mozetta with purple piping and purple fascia.
     </p>
     <p>
      Prior to the 1990s, all officers of the Order had to be of noble birth (
      <i>
       i.e.
      </i>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Armiger" title="Armiger">
       armigerous
      </a>
      for at least a hundred years), as they were all knights of justice or of obedience. However, Knights of Magistral Grace (
      <i>
       i.e.
      </i>
      , those without noble proofs) now may make the Promise of Obedience and, at the discretion of the Grand Master and Sovereign Council, may enter the
      <a href="/wiki/Novitiate" title="Novitiate">
       novitiate
      </a>
      to become professed Knights of Justice.
     </p>
     <p>
      Worldwide, there are over 13,000 knights and dames, a small minority of whom are professed religious. Membership of the Order is by invitation only and solicitations are not entertained.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Order's finances are audited by a Board of Auditors, which includes a President and four Councillors, all elected by the Chapter General. The Order's judicial powers are exercised by a group of Magistral Courts, whose judges are appointed by the Grand Master and Sovereign Council.
     </p>

     <p>
      In 1947, after the post-World War II peace treaty forbade Italy to own or operate bomber aircraft and only operate a limited number of transport aircraft, the Italian Air Force opted to transfer some of its SM.82 aircraft to the Sovereign Military Order of Malta, pending the definition of their exact status (the SM.82 were properly long range transport aircraft that could be adapted for bombing missions). These aircraft were operated by Italian Air Force personnel temporarily flying for the Order, carried the Order's roundels on the fuselage and Italian ones on the wings, and were used mainly for standard Italian Air Force training and transport missions but also for some humanitarian tasks proper of the Order of Malta (like the transport of sick pilgrims to the Lourdes sanctuary). In the early '50s, when the strictures of the peace treaty had been much relaxed by the Allied authorities, the aircraft returned under full control of the Italian Air Force. One of the aircraft transferred to the Order of Malta, still with the Order's fuselage roundels, is preserved in the
      <a href="/wiki/Italian_Air_Force_Museum" title="Italian Air Force Museum">
       Italian Air Force Museum
      </a>
      .
