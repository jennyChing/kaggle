       <p>
        Liverpools Slovakian defender Martin Skrtel has lost his appeal against the three-match ban handed to him by the FA. Skrtel was banned following his stamp on Manchester United goalkeeper David de Gea in the dying embers of the Premier League match on Sunday which Liverpool went on to lose 2-1.
       </p>
       <p>
        Referee Martin Atkinson did not see the alleged stamp by Skrtel and so subsequently the defender was not sent off during the game, however having reviewed footage of the incident the FA charged Skrtel, which he subsequently appealed but has now lost.
       </p>
       <p>
        An independent FA disciplinary panel has upheld the charge handed to Skrtel who will now miss Liverpools Premier League fixtures against Arsenal and Newcastle and will also miss the FA Cup quarter-final replay clash with Blackburn Rovers.
       </p>
