   $(document).ready(function() {		
	
	//Execute the slideShow
	slideShow();

});

function slideShow() {

	//Set the opacity of all images to 0
	$('#gallery div').css({opacity: 0.0});
	
	//Get the first image and display it (set it to full opacity)
	$('#gallery div:first').css({opacity: 1.0});
	
	//Set the caption background to semi-transparent
	$('#gallery .caption').css({opacity: 0.7});

	//Resize the width of the caption according to the image width
	$('#gallery .caption').css({width: $('#gallery div').find('img').css('width')});
	
	//Get the caption of the first image from REL attribute and display it
	$('#gallery .content').html($('#gallery div:first').find('img').attr('rel'))
	.animate({opacity: 1}, 400);
	
	//Call the gallery function to run the slideshow, 6000 = change to next image after 6 seconds
	setInterval('gallery()',8000);
	
}

function gallery() {
	
	//if no IMGs have the show class, grab the first image
	var current = ($('#gallery div.show')?  $('#gallery div.show') : $('#gallery div:first'));

	//Get next image, if it reached the end of the slideshow, rotate it back to the first image
	var next = ((current.next().length) ? ((current.next().hasClass('caption'))? $('#gallery div:first') :current.next()) : $('#gallery div:first'));	
	
	//Get next image caption
	var caption = next.find('img').attr('rel');	
	
	//Set the fade in effect for the next image, show class has higher z-index
	next.css({opacity: 0.0})
	.addClass('show')
	.animate({opacity: 1.0}, 1000);

	//Hide the current image
	current.animate({opacity: 0.0}, 1000)
	.removeClass('show');
	
	//Set the opacity to 0 and height to 1px
	$('#gallery .caption').animate({opacity: 0.0}, { queue:false, duration:0 }).animate({height: '1px'}, { queue:false, duration:300 });	
	
	//Animate the caption, opacity to 0.7 and heigth to 100px, a slide up effect
	$('#gallery .caption').animate({opacity: 0.7},100 ).animate({height: '150px'},300 );

	
	//Display the content
	$('#gallery .content').html(caption);
	
	
}
  </script>

     <h1>
      Research on Environmental Conservation
     </h1>
     <br/>
     <img alt="enviroment conservation" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="121" src="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/vin-material-source-path/uploads/2014/01/enviroment-conservation.jpg" width="140"/>
     <p>
      Environmental volunteers interviewed 140 people and observe same number of households. Volunteers are doing research on environmental issues in
      <a href="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/vin_communities/jitpurphedi-kathmandu/" target="_blank">
       Jitpurphedi
      </a>
      . The aim of research is to explore knowledge, practices and challenges on waste management in Jitpurphedi. It is planned to interview 360 people and observe same number of households about 40% of total households in Jitpur. It is expected to find out the existing knowledge and practices on waste management among people from jitpur and challenges in waste management at community level.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      In Nepal access to basic health services including hygiene and sanitation facilities is very limited and awareness of effective interventions for hygiene practices do not exist in the communities. In 1990, the national sanitation coverage was only 6% of the population; however, it reached 43% in 2010.
      <i>
       (Country paper Nepal, SACOSAN IV, 2011)
      </i>
      In Nepal, 16 million people (57 %) daily defecate out in the open due to the lack of latrines in their home. Only 41 % of public and community schools have latrine facilities
      <i>
       . (Nepal MDG Progress report 2010, GoN and UNDP)
      </i>
      In Jitpur, 200 households do not have toilet (out of 917) and there is no waste management system existed. Also no policy document is available on waste management and sanitation.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Government of Nepal aims to achieve universal access to coverage for water and sanitation services and interventions by 2017, which is basically guided by a 20-year vision (19972017) and the National Water Plan 2005. VIN and VDC had planned to declare Jitpur as an Open defecation free community with existing mechanism of waste management. The report from this research will guide to develop policy regarding hygiene and sanitation particularly for Jitpur.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      Research helps to explore the situation and provide guidance to develop policy and interventions.VIN Health volunteers had conducted several researches which were proved important for the project plan development of organization. It is a great opportunity to get involved in the VIN research program such as
      <a href="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/environment-conservation-program/climate-change-research/" target="_blank">
       Climate change research
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/environment-conservation-program/environment-initiative/" target="_blank">
       Environment Initiative
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/environment-conservation-program/water-research/" target="_blank">
       water research
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/environment-conservation-program/agrofarm/" target="_blank">
       Agro farm
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/environment-conservation-program/agroforestry/" target="_blank">
       Agro forestry
      </a>
      under
      <a href="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/environment-conservation-program/" target="_blank">
       Environment and conservation program
      </a>
      . Are you interested to join VIN as a
      <a href="http://www.volunteersinitiativenepal.org/volunteer-and-internship-programs/" target="_blank">
       volunteer or intern
      </a>
      ?
     </p>
