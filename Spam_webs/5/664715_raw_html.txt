      <div class="EchoTopic">
       <span id="intelliTxt">
        <span class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          The always diverse Visions Festival 2015 is drawing ever nearer and now the final wave of artists have been added to the line-up which now features 25 exciting acts of a variety of musical genres - no taste will go unsampled this year.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="Visions logo" height="220" src="http://www.contactmusic.com/images/feature-images/visions-2015-logo-636-220.jpg" style="border: 1px solid black;" title="Visions logo" width="636"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Joining the previously announced Camera Obscura, Merchandise, The Antlers, Son Lux and The Fat White Family - among others - are seven electrifying additions from the well-established to the brand new, such as The Great Escape Festival favourites Ho99o9 (pronounced 'horror'), Brighton's own
          <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/theo-verney">
           Theo Verney
          </a>
          whose 'Brain Disease' EP comes out June 8th, and all-female quartet The Big Moon from London.
         </p>
         <p>
          Arguably the most highly anticipated new announcement comes in the form of the crudely named Canadian electronica 4-piece Holy F**k, whose next album we're still desperately awaiting after 2010's stellar release 'Latin'. In fact, Visions will mark their first appearance in the UK since then. They'll be among the small selection of Transatlantic musicians at the festival, joining Shamir from Las Vegas and Florida trio Merchandise.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           More:
           <a href="http://www.contactmusic.com/holy-fuck/music/holy-fck-latinx26x05x10" title="Read our review of 'Latin'">
            Read our review of 'Latin'
           </a>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Closer to home, though, we'll see Brighton producer Gazelle Twin (real name Elizabeth Bernholz), who will doubtlessly be supporting her 2014 album 'Unflesh' which she released in September. More in the way of new faces, London rockers Claw Marks will be putting on a lively show alongside Loyle Carner, whose latest EP 'A Little Late' is out now.
         </p>
         <p>
          This third edition of Visions Festival is set to hit London Fields on August 8th 2015, with a number of amazing spaces hosting this year's musical haul. Among them are The Laundry, Space Gallery, London Fields Brewhouse and St John at Hackney Church.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tickets for the event are priced at a not to be sniffed at 30, but get yours soon as only a limited number are still available.
         </p>
