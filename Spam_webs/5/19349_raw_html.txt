        <p>
         When it comes to ensuring the performance and overall quality of machinery, it is best to conduct routine checks and maintenance before any problem gets worse. For instance, rotating machinery may undergo excessive vibration, which results in intense levels of noise and inefficiency in performance. Over time, this issue can impact the life and quality of shaft bearings negatively, until damages in the machine occur.
        </p>
        <p>
         With this in mind, it is recommended to detect the actual reason for such vibration and resolve this problem to facilitate the smooth operation of the machinery. It is only a matter of getting deeper into the issue through appropriate testing and checks that will resolve unbalance or vibration, which can extend the lifespan of your machines.
        </p>
        <h2>
         How Balancing Works
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_63908" style="width: 322px">
         <a href="http://www.lselectric.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Onsite-Balancing.jpg">
          <img alt="Onsite Balancing Services" class="size-full wp-image-63908" height="183" src="http://www.lselectric.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Onsite-Balancing.jpg" width="312"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          On-Site Dynamic Balancing Services from L&amp;S Electric
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         In principle, minimizing any imbalances in machinery can reduce the amount of noise or vibration that goes on while the equipment is being used. However, this method should be done in a way that will not have an effect on the life or performance of the bearing. Primarily,
         <em>
          balancing is the process that eliminates any forces causing imbalance or vibration in machinery
         </em>
         . The unbalance usually occurs due to mass eccentricity that leads to the mass centerline being displaced from its true axis.
        </p>
        <p>
         With balancing, the units weight is removed or added, depending on what is necessary for the mass centerline to reach the true axis. In particular, dynamic balance or multi-plane methods are done to resolve unbalance. In this situation, the technician will use the balancing machine, and any imbalances at both planes will be detected by using bearing pedestals attached to the sensors, which are both found in the balancing machine.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then, computer control or electronics can facilitate the placement of the correction planes, which is ideally at the most suitable position along the entire length of the unit that is relative to the bearings in motion. As for long sized units, two or more planes are used, and this is considered as a special concern once the unit operates at its super-critical or lateral critical speed.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Misalignment Leads to Trouble Ahead
        </h2>
        <p>
         According to experts, at least 70% of all problems related to machinery concerns are caused by unbalance and misaligned equipment. So it is important for technicians to make sure that the equipment is aligned and balanced properly to prevent any serious issues, which can damage the machinery. Nevertheless, continuous use of the machinery can still result to unbalance, and a critical initial sign of this problem is too much vibration and noise.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are numerous causes or concerns that lead to unbalance including changes in the conditions affecting machinery operation, bruising, adhesions to material and wear and tear of the material. Thus, equipment such as augers, hay grinders, fans of different sizes, combination rotors, and flail stalk chopper rolls can maximize the benefit from undergoing balancing.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Why Dynamic Balancing
        </h2>
        <p>
         There are several reasons why
         <a href="http://bit.ly/1MWjq1g" target="_blank" title="L&amp;S Electric's Balancing Service">
          performing dynamic balancing
         </a>
         on machinery is essential:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Elimination of forces that cause noise, mechanical damages, and vibration
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Dynamic balancing is effective in checking the actual cause of vibration and excessive noise in machinery. As a result, you can target the root of the problem and conduct the right solution to address it completely.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Increase the lifespan of the machinery
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         Replacing machines once these incur severe damages can be very costly. To prevent unnecessary expenses, dynamic balancing should be done as the need arises. This method helps improve the lifespan of the bearing, as well as the other parts of the machinery.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. Improve the efficiency of the machine
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
         With dynamic balancing, consumption of motor power is minimized while efficiency of the equipment is enhanced. This is a significant benefit that balancing offers as you can also cut expenses to boost your profits over time.
        </p>
        <p>
         In addition to these benefits, there are other reasons why
         <strong>
          dynamic balancing
         </strong>
         is a must in every industry that uses rotating machinery. These include reduction of risk conditions, prevention of structural stress, enhancement of machine finishes and convenience in balancing rotating equipment on-site.
        </p>
        <p>
         To prevent a large percentage of machinery problems with your equipment, it only makes perfect sense to conduct routine dynamic balancing. By doing so, your machinery maintains proper alignment while improving efficiency and product quality.
        </p>
