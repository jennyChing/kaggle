         Penguin 2.0  My Status Update #2  Disavow and Reconsideration
        </div>
        <div itemprop="articleBody">
         <h2 itemprop="alternativeHeadline">
          <img alt="ouch-150x150" class="lazy lazy-hidden alignright size-full wp-image-102565" data-lazy-src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/ouch-150x150.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="150" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="150"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="ouch-150x150" class="alignright size-full wp-image-102565" height="150" src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/ouch-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
          </noscript>
          The Road To Recovery
          <br/>
          From Penguin 2.0
          <br/>
          (Update 2)
         </h2>
         <p>
          In
          <a href="http://just-ask-kim.com/penguin-20-hits-jak-update-1/" target="_blank">
           the first update
          </a>
          , nearly 2 weeks ago, I shone some light on the stats behind the trouble that this blog has had with the most recent Penguin 2.0 update.
         </p>
         <p>
          I did a lot of pouring through data from my side and repeatedly came up empty handed in terms of what might have been setting Googles alarm off accidentally.
         </p>
         <p>
          Have a look at devastating free report from Fruition.coms Google Penalty Checker.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="fruition-google-penalty-checker-1" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter wp-image-102782" data-lazy-src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/fruition-google-penalty-checker-1.png" data-lazy-type="image" height="495" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="545"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="fruition-google-penalty-checker-1" class="aligncenter wp-image-102782" height="495" src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/fruition-google-penalty-checker-1.png" width="545"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Thanks to
          <a href="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/weekly-marketing-skinny-june-15-2013" target="_blank">
           Ana from TGC for introducing
          </a>
          me to Fruition. Go here to check out Fruitions
          <a href="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/google-penalty-checker-tool" target="_blank">
           Google Penalty Checker
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Google Webmaster Tools Site Links section seem as though it should contain the answers, but is littered with not only multiple thousands of links but many of which dont actually seem to point to this site. I felt like I was banging my head against a wall looking through their listing.
         </p>
         <p>
          Finally, I purchased a Link Audit report from Clint Butler. This is a really affordable SEO service he offers. Start with a free site-check at
          <a href="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/website-check" target="_blank">
           Olympia SEO
          </a>
          and then follow up from there.
         </p>
         <p>
          My first assumption was to sort the output Excel file by Rule (meaning which rule they were breaking) but I finally settled on sorting the report by Risk. I did this since not all breakers of the same rule had the same risk.
         </p>
         <p>
          From this link report, I found 6 Deadly Risk and 48 High Risk flagged links. I added these to a notepad file (copy/paste) which I named disavow.txt.
         </p>
         <p>
          While looking at these 54 links, I noticed that many .WS sites were being flagged. It said it was likely due to issues with their theme but my gut tells me its more likely universal with that domain postfix and something Ill need to watch for since I have a couple friends using that domain postfix.
         </p>
         <p>
          I took the 54 links and filed a 
          <a href="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/disavow-links" target="_blank">
           Disavow Link
          </a>
           request for those 54 links. I did this at a link level rather than a domain level since there were very few duplicates.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="disavow-links-1" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-medium wp-image-102730" data-lazy-src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/disavow-links-1-471x309.png" data-lazy-type="image" height="309" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="471"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="disavow-links-1" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-102730" height="309" src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/disavow-links-1-471x309.png" width="471"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          Once that was done, I headed over to file a 
          <a href="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/answer.py?hl=en&amp;answer=35843" target="_blank">
           Reconsideration Request
          </a>
           with the Google Webmaster Tools form.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="reconsideration-request-1" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-full wp-image-102731" data-lazy-src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/reconsideration-request-1.png" data-lazy-type="image" height="751" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="453"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="reconsideration-request-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-102731" height="751" src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/reconsideration-request-1.png" width="453"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          About 12 hours later I received an email from the webform stating that my request had been received and that I could expect to hear something in one to two weeks.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="reconsideration-request-2" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-medium wp-image-102732" data-lazy-src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/reconsideration-request-2-497x309.png" data-lazy-type="image" height="309" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="497"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="reconsideration-request-2" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-102732" height="309" src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/reconsideration-request-2-497x309.png" width="497"/>
         </noscript>
         <p>
          I dont think there is any way I could have  as a single person  walked through more than 30,000 links to my site in the site report and picked out the 6 deadly risk links with full accuracy. Im incredibly glad I got the report from Clint, even though it is only a partial report, but also incredibly frustrated at what this means for the average person trying to keep their site ranking without having to micromanage SEO risks.
         </p>
         <p>
          Fortunately or unfortunately, Google replied with
          <img alt="google-reconsideration-request-no-penalty-1" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-full wp-image-102783" data-lazy-src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/google-reconsideration-request-no-penalty-1.png" data-lazy-type="image" height="649" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="536"/>
         </p>
         <noscript>
          <img alt="google-reconsideration-request-no-penalty-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-102783" height="649" src="http://just-ask-kim.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/google-reconsideration-request-no-penalty-1.png" width="536"/>
         </noscript>
         Well.. spiffy but not useful.
         <p>
          Great, so theres no manual penalty (knew that) but that of course doesnt resolve the current algorithm change issues.
         </p>
         <p>
          Oh well, back to the drawing board
         </p>
         <p>
          Are we having fun with Google algorithm changes yet?
         </p>
         <p>
          ~ Kim ~
          <br/>
          Simple Tech Tips For Marketing
         </p>
         <p>
          PS: I still suggest grabbing the link report. You can never go wrong getting damaging links out of your link profile.
         </p>

            <time class="comment-time" datetime="2014-02-18T15:44:38+00:00" itemprop="commentTime">

             </a>
            </time>
           </p>
          </header>
          <div class="comment-content" itemprop="commentText">
           <p>
            Its important to understand that a reconsideration request and disavowing links are different tools. Google have explained it pretty clearly.
           </p>
           <p>
            I honestly doubt that you got a penalty. More likely you got hit by an algo change, which quite simply means that the ranking score of your site went down quickly. If you have been prevalently using a single strategy for link-building (example: blog commenting) it may well be that Google simply devaluated the practice and so you lost your ranking. This is why its always a good idea to diversify links as much as possible.
           </p>
