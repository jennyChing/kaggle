      </b>

      <b>
       Master Wel
      </b>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nathaniel_1-0">

      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       American
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">
       composer
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Playwright" title="Playwright">
       playwright
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Poet" title="Poet">
       poet
      </a>
      ,

       pianist
      </a>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Weldon_Irvine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Irvine, an
      <a href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">
       African American
      </a>
      , was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Hampton,_Virginia" title="Hampton, Virginia">
       Hampton, Virginia
      </a>
      on October 27, 1943. He moved to
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      in 1965.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was involved with various musical genres including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jazz-Funk" title="Jazz-Funk">
       Jazz-Funk
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">
       jazz
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">
       hip hop
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">
       funk
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">
       rhythm and blues
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">
       gospel
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nathaniel_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nathaniel-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He served as the bandleader for jazz singer
      <a href="/wiki/Nina_Simone" title="Nina Simone">
       Nina Simone
      </a>
      and was a
      <a href="/wiki/Mentor" title="Mentor">
       mentor
      </a>
      to many New York hip-hop artists, including
      <a href="/wiki/Q-Tip_(musician)" title="Q-Tip (musician)">
       Q-Tip
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">
       Mos Def
      </a>
      . He wrote over 500 songs,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artistdirect_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-artistdirect-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      including the lyrics for "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/To_Be_Young,_Gifted,_and_Black" title="To Be Young, Gifted, and Black">
       To Be Young, Gifted, and Black
      </a>
      ", performed live for the first time by
      <a href="/wiki/Nina_Simone" title="Nina Simone">
       Nina Simone
      </a>
      on the album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Black_Gold_(Nina_Simone_album)" title="Black Gold (Nina Simone album)">
        Black Gold
       </a>
      </i>
      (1970). It has been dubbed the "official"
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_Rights_anthem" title="Civil Rights anthem">
       Civil Rights anthem
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1999, Irvine contributed on
      <a href="/wiki/Mos_Def" title="Mos Def">
       Mos Def
      </a>
      's debut solo album
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_On_Both_Sides" title="Black On Both Sides">
        Black On Both Sides
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Irvine's last major project was
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Price_of_Freedom" title="The Price of Freedom">
        The Price of Freedom
       </a>
      </i>
      (1999), a compilation of original songs by hip-hop, jazz, funk, and R&amp;B artists to respond to the shooting of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amadou_Diallo" title="Amadou Diallo">
       Amadou Diallo
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2000, Irvine provided the vocal introductions to tracks on the first CD of the album
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.discogs.com/Big-Bud-Late-Night-Blues/release/9591" rel="nofollow">
       Late Night Blues
      </a>
      by UK Drum &amp; Bass producer Big Bud. He co-wrote and performed vocals and played the Fender Rhodes on the first track entitled Return of Spiritman. He also played the Fender Rhodes on closing track Persian Blues.
     </p>
     <p>
      Irvine committed
      <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">
       suicide
      </a>
      by shooting himself outside of EAB Plaza and in front of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nassau_Coliseum" title="Nassau Coliseum">
       Nassau Coliseum
      </a>
      located in
      <a href="/wiki/Uniondale,_New_York" title="Uniondale, New York">
       Uniondale, New York
      </a>
      on April 9, 2002.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-allmusic-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2003,
      <a href="/wiki/Madlib" title="Madlib">
       Madlib
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mr._Dibbs" title="Mr. Dibbs">
       Mr. Dibbs
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Breakestra" title="Breakestra">
       Breakestra
      </a>
      produced a tribute to Weldon Irvine, "Suite for Weldon". The following year, Madlib released the full length album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Tribute_to_Brother_Weldon" title="A Tribute to Brother Weldon">
        A Tribute to Brother Weldon
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hiphopsite_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-hiphopsite-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
