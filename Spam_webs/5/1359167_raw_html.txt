        <p>
         <img alt="" class=" wp-image-1456 size-full aligncenter" height="384" src="https://www.stdcheck.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/ohcrabS.png" width="576"/>
         So you have an STD. Maybe youre a good person who had a momentary lapse of judgment after a few too many Jagerbombs. Maybe you need a refresher course on how to properly put on a condom. Maybe perhaps youre just a badass with a devil-may-care attitude about having bareback sex with strangers. Whatever the case, it sucks to have an STD. Whether yours involves bumps, burning, or drips, youve put everyone youre sleeping with at risk of having the disease, too.Hopefully, you are aware that you have to inform anyone youre sleeping with about your little problem, even if you choose to forego owning up to it and resort to using an
         <a href="https://www.stdcheck.com/anonymous-notification.php" target="_blank">
          Anonymous Notification Tool
         </a>
         . In this case, no news is NOT good news.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-576">
         </span>
         <br/>
         When it comes time to break the news, make sure youre a decent human being who doesnt deliver the message in any of the following ways:
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. By Email
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="iPhone-Mail-badge-2" class="size-medium wp-image-578 aligncenter" height="285" src="https://www.stdcheck.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/iPhone-Mail-badge-2-450x285.jpg" width="450"/>
         There are many situations where email is both appropriate and necessary- communicating with your boss at work, sending your parents recent pictures of your kids, updating all your friends on how much fun youre having on vacation in Thailand, etc. Telling your partner that they need to take an
         <a href="https://www.stdcheck.com" target="_blank">
          STD test
         </a>
         is not one of those situations. When you find yourself in that predicament, the best thing to do is tell them in person. Give them the chance to slap you in the face. Even a phone call would be better than an email. Screaming obscenities at you over the phone is way more cathartic than typing an angry email response full of exclamation points.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. By telling their friend and having their friend tell them
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="telephone-game-girls" class="size-full wp-image-579 aligncenter" height="288" src="https://www.stdcheck.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/telephone-game-girls.jpg" width="440"/>
         This method is reserved for the lowest of the low. Now, not only have you
         <a href="https://www.stdcheck.com/blog/faqs-about-stds/" target="_blank">
          possibly given her an STD
         </a>
         , but all of her friends are going to know. Youve added an entirely new layer of embarrassment to the crap cake that you baked with your genitals. You shouldnt be spreading gossip -or anything else- about such a delicate topic.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
        </center>
        <h3>
         3. By leaving a note right after sex
         <img alt="herpes note" class="size-full wp-image-580 aligncenter" height="300" src="https://www.stdcheck.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/herpes-note.jpg" width="400"/>
        </h3>
        <p>
         If youre leaving a note for someone youve slept with, it should be cute or sexy. Writing Lets get freaky on a napkin and leaving it on the bar for the cute bartender is one thing; scribbling a warning that their genitals will probably start itching soon after sleeping with them is a completely different move. Its called a dick move and if you do it, you are a dick.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <center>
        </center>
        <h3>
         4. With a gift
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="cake and chlamydia" class="size-full wp-image-581 aligncenter" height="450" src="https://www.stdcheck.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/10710906-19520857-thumbnail.jpg" width="450"/>
         Maybe youre a sarcastic guy who thinks its funny to give someone really bad news with a gift. Maybe youre an idiot who legitimately thinks that leaving the note on top of an Xbox will somehow make the guy you just gave chlamydia feel a little less angry. Either way, nothing says Destroy Me quite like what would be an otherwise awesome gift thats actually informing you that you probably have an STD.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. With an eCard
         <img alt="STD ecard" class="size-full wp-image-582 aligncenter" height="294" src="https://www.stdcheck.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/1334876425106_8155813.png" width="420"/>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Just, no. With the possibility of using an
         <a href="http://www.stdcheck.com/anonymous-notification.php">
          Anonymous Notification Tool
         </a>
         to alert someone that they need to get tested without including your name, why would you ever try to combine humor with something as serious as sexual health? Your Facebook timeline should be a safe haven full of videos of cats playing piano and Sarcastic Willy Wonka memes, not a traumatic reminder of the skank from the John Mayer concert who gave you
         <a href="https://www.stdcheck.com/blog/why-is-gonorrhea-called-the-clap/" target="_blank">
          the Clap
         </a>
         . Thats just rude.
        </p>
