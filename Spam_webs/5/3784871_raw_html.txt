       <p>
        <a href="http://i1.wp.com/howtoo.altervista.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/0e55028ee982a20761b89bb0f5672b892.jpg">
         <img alt="0e55028ee982a20761b89bb0f5672b89" class="size-medium wp-image-69" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/howtoo.altervista.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/0e55028ee982a20761b89bb0f5672b892.jpg?resize=220%2C300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Using copper pennies (be sure they are older than 1983),
        <a href="http://thesecretyumiverse.wonderhowto.com/how-to/10-extraordinary-uses-for-ordinary-lemon-0126602/">
         lemon juice
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://thesecretyumiverse.wonderhowto.com/how-to/9-unconventional-uses-for-paper-towels-0140136/">
         paper towels
        </a>
        and sandpaper, you can construct your own DIY battery that generates enough juice to light up an LED.
       </p>
       <p>
        The process is simple: simply sand down one side of a penny with sandpaper (be careful not to scratch your fingers) until its zinc core is exposed. Repeat for five other pennies.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-68">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        Afterwards, soak penny-sized paper towel disks in lemon juice (add a dash of
        <a href="http://thesecretyumiverse.wonderhowto.com/how-to/13-unexpected-uses-for-salt-0137940/">
         salt
        </a>
        ) for ten minutes. Then, create a multi-layered battery alternating between sandpapered pennies and lemon juice-soaked paper towels, with the zinc side of the pennies all facing the same way.
       </p>
       <p>
        For other variations, you can use cardboard in lieu of paper towels or salt water in lieu of lemon juice.
       </p>
