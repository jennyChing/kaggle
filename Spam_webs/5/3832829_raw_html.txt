       <h1>
        <a href="http://www.gleaningsfromscripture.com/death-faith-perspective/" rel="bookmark" title="What Is Death From A Faith Perspective?">
         What Is Death From A Faith Perspective?
        </a>
       </h1>
      </header>
      <section class="entry">
       <p>
        To receive insight on this vital question, I propose we keep in mind the twenty third psalm: Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil. Notice, in this familiar phrase, death is described as a shadow. Not darkness, a total absence of light but only a temporary blockage of the light. As the physical senses dim and die, a shadow may be cast upon the soul. But that shadow is dispelled by the glory of God in the face of Jesus. In the famous love chapter, First Corinthians chapter thirteen, we are promised that we shall see Him face to face.
       </p>
       <p>
        Turn up your powers of observation and focus on that word valley. Death, from a faith perspective is not a blind alley, which comes to a screeching halt at the grave; its rather a valley, with light at both ends. The letter V that changes it from an alley to a valley is the victory of faith. This is the victory which overcomes the world, even our faith. May the stone of doubt be rolled away, so that we might see the tomb in which Jesus lay not as a prison, but rather a passage to life eternal. Only the eye of faith can see that to be absent from the body is to be present with the Lord.
       </p>
       <p>
        With our Easter faith we can also highlight that preposition through. Our Good Shepherd will not lead his sheep into the valley and abandon them. He will lead them all the way through. On the other side is the house of the Lord forever. Nothing can compare with that. In the wildest stretch of our imagination we couldnt conceive of such a place.
       </p>
       <p>
        Jesus promised to prepare a place for us. Not just a state of mind, or a poetical ideal, but a place. He also prepares us for that place by giving us the opportunity to grow our faith. As we feast on the green pastures of sacred scripture our faith is growing. We are developing the spiritual resources to look beyond the things that are seen, which are temporary, to the things which are unseen, which are eternal.
       </p>
       <p>
        What is the ground of our confidence? Let me suggest the phrase: For his names sake. The reputation of the shepherd is at stake. If he were to loose a straying lamb in the gorge of gloom, his good name would be tarnished. Its not about us, its about his unblemished record. For the sake of his good name and reputation, he will lead us through.
       </p>
       <p>
        Come to God in prayer and humbly ask in all sincerity for the gift of faith and see what happens. You might be surprised.
       </p>
