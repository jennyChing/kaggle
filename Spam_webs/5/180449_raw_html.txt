       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Art on the Road: Denver
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-12-19T19:42:36+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 19, 2012
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Ken Kaminesky
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/2012/12/19/art-on-the-road-denver/#comments">
           4 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         This has been a whirlwind year of travel for me and I must admit to being quite fortunate with the weather for the most part when I am on the road. I do however have the reputation of bringing snow with me to several places that are not so well known for the fluffy white stuff. In the last couple of years it has snowed in Florence, Atlanta, and Florida upon my arrival. Washington DC and New York City both were hit with massive snowstorms a day after I arrived and now we can add Denver stop on my Art on the Road tour to that list.
        </p>
        <img alt="Sue Bell artist" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9002" height="910" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Susan-Bell.jpg" title="Susan-Bell" width="800"/>
        <p>
         Sure, snow is hardly a foreign substance in this part of the US but in early October I was hoping for something slightly warmer. After all it was just 2 months since I was in Washington DC where the temperature was in the mid 90s. Thats a huge jump in very little time and just goes to show how diverse the climate is in America.
        </p>
        <div>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9005" height="294" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/canyonponies.jpg" title="canyonponies" width="600"/>
        </div>
        <div>
         <p>
          The same can be said for the types of art that I have encountered thus far in my travels on the Art on the Road tour with SpringHill Suites and Im just at the official mid-way point. In my Denver stop the theme was Frontier Art and there is no better example of this type of art than the paintings of Susan Bell.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9006" height="368" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/WarPaint30x40oil.jpg" title="WarPaint30x40oil" width="500"/>
         <p>
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9007" height="587" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/lazyday.jpg" title="lazyday" width="600"/>
         <p>
          It was nice to be in the company of a very easy going artist whos view on art is similar to mine. In my very humble opinion, art should be affecting you in some visceral fashion. If it makes you happy, sad, angry, then it is doing its job. Art is subjective and the more kinds I see, the more I love not just the art but the art form and the artists that create their oeuvres.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9009" height="291" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/indiansummer.jpg" title="indiansummer" width="600"/>
         <p>
          Some of the magic of Bells art is obvious just by looking at it, yet I was lucky enough to be in her cozy daylight studio at the back of her Denver gallery as she began on a new canvas. As someone who can barely draw stick figures I am in awe of artists who can create something beautiful with a simple brush and paint. It seems easy enough to do when watching a talented painter but Im sure that is all part of what makes art so magical to me. We all have gifts that make us who we are and Bells gift with brushes and paint really comes to light when standing next to her as she gets lost in the painting she is working on at the moment.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9011" height="444" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/crsingSaveryCk.jpg" title="crsingSaveryCk" width="600"/>
         <p>
          Not only does Bell celebrate her own art in her gallery but as an art lover she also shares the works of several other artists in her intimate and informal gallery on Pearl Street. With the cold weather and first snow of the season, the fireplace was turned on and classical music was coming from the studio section of the gallery (although dont be surprised to hear Led Zeppelin). All these homey elements add to the experience of viewing the frontier style art and if you ever find yourself in Denver, treat yourself to some great art and charming company at
          <a href="http://www.susanbellfineart.com/contact.htm">
           Susan Bells Gallery
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9012" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3619.jpg" title="IMG_3619" width="600"/>
         <p>
          In the midst of my exploration of Denver in search of frontier type art I discovered a very special place. How appropriate to find a museum dedicated to outdoor art on my stop here in Colorado. The Museum of Outdoor Arts was one of those special discoveries that made my time in Denver so much better. The vast quantity of pieces of art made it impossible to photograph the collection in its entirety but I got as many iPhone photos as possible to share with you.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9495" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3658.jpg" title="IMG_3658" width="600"/>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9496" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3693.jpg" title="IMG_3693" width="600"/>
         <p>
          The main collection of work is located in and around the Englewood Civic Center, with a secondary location situated around Greenwood Village. There are many different types of artwork but most of the art is sculpture or statues. Youll even come across some living art that is constantly evolving with the seasons.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9497" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3697.jpg" title="IMG_3697" width="600"/>
         <p>
          Youll find modern abstract sculptures, kinetic works of art, bronze statues, and a serious amount of sculptures devoted to Alice in Wonderland.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9498" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3696.jpg" title="IMG_3696" width="600"/>
         <p>
          While this was probably not designed to be an actual part of the collection for MOA, they have one piece that I just fell in love with. There is a water fountain just in front of the Englewood Civic Center where they have coloured the water pink in honor of breast cancer awareness month. I found that to be a very touching display which pays tribute to all the women who we have known that have been affected by this deadly disease.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9500" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3661.jpg" title="IMG_3661" width="600"/>
         <p>
          The cold weather and heavy rain kept me from seeing as much as I could have but I did get the opportunity to photograph the elusive and rare Colorado giant blue bear. I had heard about this mythical creature and that it has a penchant for modern glass and steel buildings. As luck would have it I found one at the Colorado Convention Center.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9501" height="673" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Blue-bear.jpg" title="Blue-bear" width="928"/>
         <p>
          If there is one thing that Canadians know about (other than hockey and maple syrup) its bears  or more precisely, bear attacks! I wasnt going to take any chances with this big blue beast, so I made my way on to Civic Center Park to photograph me some Cowboys.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9502" height="673" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Bronco-Buster.jpg" title="Bronco Buster" width="928"/>
         <p>
          I was able to get a nice shot of the frontier styled statues of a cowboy and Native American just before the skies opened up with quite a heavy rainfall. In between the intermittent showers I met a former US president, a guitar playing cowpoke, and saw what may be the worlds smallest horse or the worlds biggest chairYou decide.
         </p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9503" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3683.jpg" title="IMG_3683" width="600"/>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9504" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3676_Snapseed.jpg" title="IMG_3676_Snapseed" width="600"/>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9505" height="600" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/IMG_3684.jpg" title="IMG_3684" width="600"/>
         <p>
          Any Story about art in Denver is not complete without a photo of the spectacular Denver Art Museum and I saved that final treat to the very end of my day.
         </p>
         <img alt="The Denver Art Museum building" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9015" height="673" src="http://blog.kenkaminesky.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Denver-Art-Museum.jpg" title="Denver-Art-Museum" width="928"/>
         <p>
          Any Story about art in Denver is not complete without a photo of the spectacular Denver Art Museum and I saved that final treat to the very end of my day.
         </p>
         <p>
          Next stopMinneapolis!
