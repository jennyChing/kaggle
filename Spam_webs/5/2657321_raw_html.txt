       <section>
        <p>
         Almost every other
         <i>
          X-Files
         </i>
         episode begins with some quiet hamlet getting plagued by a paranormal thingamabobber, at which point Scully, Mulder, and Scully's
         <a href="http://fucknoshoulderpads.tumblr.com/" target="c">
          absolutely smashing 1990s fashion ensembles
         </a>
         are sent to investigate. And if
         <i>
          The X-Files
         </i>
         were still on the air today, the following recent real-world happenings would inevitably be adapted for episodes. (And to add to the horror, the theme song would be
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pDlLBo6Bpms" target="c">
          remixed as dubstep
         </a>
         .)
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #4. A Hundred Elk Randomly Die, Bear Christmas Ensues
        </h2>
        <p>
         A single square mile of land just north of Las Vegas recently saw 100 elk simply
         <a href="http://www.krqe.com/news/local/elk-herd-found-dead-in-northeastern-nm?hpt=us_bn10" target="a">
          stop being alive
         </a>
         over a span of 24 hours. When we say "stop being alive," we mean this:
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/8/9/207489.jpg?v=1" height="818" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://www.krqe.com/news/local/elk-herd-found-dead-in-northeastern-nm" target="c">
           KRQE
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Casinos ... they don't fuck around.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yup, they just fucking fell over and died like
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LHZ8CaaqtSc" target="a">
          that scene from the movie
          <i>
           Surrogates
          </i>
         </a>
         , if
         <i>
          Surrogates
         </i>
         starred elk and people actually saw it. Wildlife officials have already
         <a href="http://www.livescience.com/39522-mysterious-elk-deaths-plague-new-mexico.html" target="a">
          ruled out
         </a>
         gunshots, anthrax, and pesticide poisoning. Lightning is an outside possibility, but the radius o' death is too large -- Thor would've basically had to get drunk and fry the entire forest.
        </p>
        <p>
         At this point, the likeliest culprits are either A) an insect-borne illness known as
         <a href="http://www.michigan.gov/dnr/0,1607,7-153-10370_12150-26647--,00.html" target="a">
          epizootic hemorrhagic disease
         </a>
         or B) bear genies. Seriously, the neighborhood apex predators woke up that day to a goddamn free buffet as far as the eye can see.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/8/7/207487.jpg?v=1" height="196" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://www.krqe.com/news/local/elk-herd-found-dead-in-northeastern-nm" target="c">
           KRQE
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          And so Bear Cinderella's Beary Godmother spontaneously murdered all the animals of the forest, and they all lived happily ever after. (Including the elk, who were much happier as bear shit.)
         </font>
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #3. Switzerland's Artsy, Extremely Quiet Bigfoot
        </h2>
        <p>
         According to perturbed locals, an enigmatic figure known as "Le Loyon" has been
         <a href="http://www.news.com.au/travel/news/mystery-figure-haunting-the-swiss-woods-for-over-a-decade8217/story-e6frfq80-1226717453647" target="a">
          haunting the same rural path
         </a>
         in western Switzerland every day for the past 10 years. This mysterious mummer wears a military cape, boots, and an antique gas mask -- kind of like if Banksy invented Sasquatch.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/8/8/207488_v1.jpg" height="213" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://www.lematin.ch/faits-divers/loyon-rire/story/25650014" target="c">
           lematin.ch
          </a>
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         This eerie woodland dweller has apparently been around for a while, but it was only recently that an amateur photographer submitted the above picture to the Swiss news site
         <a href="http://www.lematin.ch/faits-divers/loyon-rire/story/25650014" target="a">
          Le Matin
         </a>
         . The photographer allegedly got within a dozen meters of Le Loyon, who responded by briefly staring back, turning around, and trudging away silently.
        </p>
        <p>
         This run-in echoed other folks' encounters with this eccentric, whose strolls have dissuaded some residents from walking in the woods. Police haven't intervened with this dead-eyed wraith, who so far has minded his own business like a total maniac. Curiously, nobody has considered the possibility that Le Loyon is in fact a masked vigilante who's chosen to patrol the most boring thoroughfare in all of Switzerland.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/8/8/207488_v1.jpg" height="213" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://www.lematin.ch/faits-divers/loyon-rire/story/25650014" target="c">
           lematin.ch
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          "He's the antisocial weirdo we deserve, but not the one we need right now."
         </font>
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #2. Moscow's Exorcist Pigeons
        </h2>
        <p>
         We all know that pigeons are an urban nuisance or, to be blunt, winged shit machines. They generally inspire profanity instead of screams, unless they happen to be
         <a href="http://www.rferl.org/content/russia-moscow-zombie-pigeons-deaths/25075473.html" target="a">
          Moscow's totally disturbing "zombie" pigeons
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/9/3/207493.gif?v=1" height="215" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://america.aljazeera.com/watch/shows/live-news/2013/8/zombie-pigeons-fallfromrussiansky.html" target="c">
           Al Jazeera
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          More character development than two episodes of
          <i>
           The Walking Dead
          </i>
          .
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes, the aerial rats of the Russian capital have been afflicted with a mystery disease that causes them to become comatose, walk in circles, drop through windows, and pull a full-blown Linda Blair. Some residents have dubbed this a total bird apocalypse. (Such hyperbole must be taken seriously, as Russia is the land of shrugging at
         <a href="http://www.cracked.com/article_20164_5-hilariously-bad-ideas-that-actually-solved-huge-problems.html" target="a">
          dashboard camera demolition derbies
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.cracked.com/quick-fixes/5-meteor-videos-that-prove-russians-dont-give-f2340k/" target="a">
          meteor strikes
         </a>
         .)
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/9/4/207494.gif?v=1" height="215" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://america.aljazeera.com/watch/shows/live-news/2013/8/zombie-pigeons-fallfromrussiansky.html" target="c">
           Al Jazeera
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          "YOUR MOTHER EATS CRUMBS IN HELL!"
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ornithologists have confirmed that these creatures are plagued with not one, but a bunch of debilitating infections, as if natural selection was running behind on its monthly quotas. And some of these infections -- such as
         <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1526237/?page=1" target="a">
          Newcastle disease
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://ideas.health.vic.gov.au/bluebook/psittacosis.asp" target="a">
          ornithosis
         </a>
         -- can also be transmitted to people, so hooray for that.
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #1. English Town Keeps Seeing the Same Scary Clown Everywhere
        </h2>
        <p>
         Occasionally, an actual, breathing human carries out an act so sinister and in defiance of rational thought that any sane onlooker has no choice but to simply submit to the enormity, like a drowning victim whose lungs are filling with water.
        </p>
        <p>
         What the hell are we talking about? In Northampton, England,
         <a href="http://www.theatlanticwire.com/global/2013/09/theres-clown-terrorizing-small-british-town/69425/" target="a">
          there's a guy going around dressed like this
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/9/0/207490.jpg?v=1" height="213" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="https://twitter.com/jogo78/statuses/378621337797165056" target="c">
           twitter.com/jogo78
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          It's too late to close your browser. He already knows where you live.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         As reported by
         <a href="http://www.northampton-news-hp.co.uk/News/Northampton-News/Spooky-clown-mystery-grips-Northampton-14092013.htm" target="a">
          the town's local paper
         </a>
         , "He doesn't juggle. He doesn't twist balloons into animal shapes. He just stares."
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/9/1/207491.jpg?v=1" height="225" width="350"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 350px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/spotnorthamptonsclown" target="c">
           facebook.com/spotnorthamptonsclown
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Pro Tip: Filling balloons with the souls of your victims gets them to float longer than helium.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         And here he is again, just standing around, being a fucking nightmare:
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/quickfix/4/9/2/207492.jpg?v=1" height="302" width="225"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 225px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/spotnorthamptonsclown" target="c">
           facebook.com/spotnorthamptonsclown
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Those children were real 10 minutes before this photo was taken.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is his gift to the world -- showing up on random corners of a single town. And he's so elusive that he has only been photographed from a distance. Of course, that might have something to do with the fact that he's downright horrifying.
        </p>
        <p>
         And if you're wondering, no, they have no idea who the fuck he is -- just that he's now a part of life and there's nothing we can do about it. Sleep tight.
        </p>
