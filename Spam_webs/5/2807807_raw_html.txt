     <p>
      <b>
       Captain Canuck
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Canadian_comics" title="Canadian comics">
       Canadian comic book
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">
       superhero
      </a>
      . Created by cartoonist
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ron_Leishman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ron Leishman (page does not exist)">
       Ron Leishman
      </a>
      and artist/writer
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_Comely" title="Richard Comely">
       Richard Comely
      </a>
      , the original Captain Canuck first appeared in
      <i>
       Captain Canuck
      </i>
      #1 (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cover-date" title="Cover-date">
       cover-dated
      </a>
      July 1975). The series was the first successful Canadian comic book since the collapse of the nation's comic-book industry following
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edwardson_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-edwardson-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Three
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">
       characters
      </a>
      have worn the maple leaf costume of Captain Canuck. The first Captain Canuck patrolled Canada in the then-future year of 1993, where "Canada had become the most powerful country in the world." He was the costumed agent of the "Canadian International Security Organization" (CISO).
     </p>
     <p>
      Like most
      <a href="/wiki/Alternative_comics" title="Alternative comics">
       independent comics
      </a>
      , Captain Canuck's adventures have been published sporadically.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Captain Canuck is sometimes confused with
      <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">
       Marvel Comics
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/Guardian_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Guardian (Marvel Comics)">
       Guardian
      </a>
      , leader of
      <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Flight" title="Alpha Flight">
       Alpha Flight
      </a>
      , created by
      <a href="/wiki/Chris_Claremont" title="Chris Claremont">
       Chris Claremont
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/John_Byrne_(comics)" title="John Byrne (comics)">
       John Byrne
      </a>
      in 1978. Both characters have very similar costumes, as both are based on the colours of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_flag" title="Canadian flag">
       Canadian flag
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_history">
       Publication history
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Publication history">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Tom_Evans">
       Tom Evans
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tom Evans">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Canuck's first appearance was in 1975, published by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comely_Comix" title="Comely Comix">
       Comely Comix
      </a>
      of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winnipeg,_Manitoba" title="Winnipeg, Manitoba">
       Winnipeg
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">
       Manitoba
      </a>
      . The story followed
      <b>
       Tom Evans
      </b>
      , a Canadian secret agent who gained superhuman strength from contact with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life_in_popular_culture" title="Extraterrestrial life in popular culture">
       extraterrestrials
      </a>
      . This first version of the Canadian
      <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">
       superhero
      </a>
      ran for three issues before going on hiatus in 1976. In 1979, it came back, with Comely being backed by CKR Productions, and publishing 11 more issues, plus a summer special, concluding in early 1981; it was drawn mostly by
      <a href="/wiki/George_Freeman_(comics)" title="George Freeman (comics)">
       George Freeman
      </a>
      , taking over from Comely, who did the writing and lettering. He was pushed out of CKR after issue #12 was completed and issue #13 and #14 were outlined. The completed issue #15 (written and pencilled by George Freeman and inked, lettered and coloured by Claude St. Aubin) was finally published in 2004 as a limited edition by Comely.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Darren_Oak">
       Darren Oak
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Darren Oak">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Since the original, two newer incarnations of the Canadian icon have appeared: Comely launched a second version in 1993, under the imprint
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Semple_Comics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Semple Comics (page does not exist)">
       Semple Comics
      </a>
      . Set in the present,
      <i>
       Captain Canuck: Reborn
      </i>
      featured a new Captain Canuck,
      <b>
       Darren Oak
      </b>
      , who fought a global conspiracy. This title lasted only four issues (#0-3) and was written and drawn by Richard Comely,
      <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Kirk" title="Leonard Kirk">
       Leonard Kirk
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Carruthers" title="Sandy Carruthers">
       Sandy Carruthers
      </a>
      with inks by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eric_Theriault" title="Eric Theriault">
       Eric Theriault
      </a>
      . That incarnation continued as a newspaper comic strip for a short while.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="David_Semple">
       David Semple
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: David Semple">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A third incarnation (sometimes called the "West Coast Captain Canuck"), edited by Comely but written and drawn by brothers Riel and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Drue_Langlois&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Drue Langlois (page does not exist)">
       Drue Langlois
      </a>
      , appeared in 2004 under the banner of Comely Comics, entitled
      <i>
       Captain Canuck: Unholy War
      </i>
      . Yet another man,
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">
       RCMP
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Constable" title="Constable">
       Constable
      </a>
      <b>
       David Semple
      </b>
      , adopts the guise of Captain Canuck, to take on a biker gang called the Unholy Avengers. "Unholy War" was slated as a three-issue
      <a href="/wiki/Limited_series" title="Limited series">
       mini-series
      </a>
      , the third and final installment being published in January 2005. However, the series came out with a fourth issue (under the '
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Hail_Comics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hot Hail Comics (page does not exist)">
       Hot Hail Comics
      </a>
      ' banner) in August 2007, which fully concluded the character.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Captain_Canuck:_Legacy">
       <i>
        Captain Canuck: Legacy
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Captain Canuck: Legacy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A miniseries, written and illustrated by Comely
      <i>
       Captain Canuck: Legacy
      </i>
      , began in the fall of 2006. It contained two continued stories: One detailed the efforts of the second Captain Canuck to prevent illegal weapons from reaching Canada, whilst the second chronicled the continued adventures of the third (West Coast) Captain Canuck.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      While the series has remained in limbo for some time, the official Captain Canuck website stated that the remainder of the series would be completed in 2009. Captain Canuck Legacy 1.5 was published in August 2011 as a limited edition of 5,000 copies only distributed in Ontario.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Captain_Canuck_Collections">
       Captain Canuck Collections
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Captain Canuck Collections">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      IDW Publishing published two volumes of collected editions of the 1975-1980
      <i>
       Captain Canuck
      </i>
      series.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its first release in June 2009 contained issues #4-10. Volume two, released December 2009, contained the 1980 Summer Special. In November 2011, IDW released
      <i>
       Captain Canuck The Complete Edition
      </i>
      as a 375-page trade paperback with issues #1-15, the Summer Special, the newspaper strips, sketches and
      <i>
       Captain Canuck: Legacy
      </i>
      #1.5.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_film">
       Proposed film
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Proposed film">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Canadian company Mind's Eye Entertainment announced at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic-Con_International" title="Comic-Con International">
       Comic-Con International
      </a>
      2011 that it planned to develop a Captain Canuck feature film. No scriptwriter or director were attached.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Mind's Eye announced at Comic-Con 2012 that it had selected
      <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">
       Vancouver
      </a>
      -based screenwriter Arne Olsen to script the feature. Olsen was chosen from a solicitation of pre selected, accredited Canadian writers, who submitted treatments. The final two choices submitted copies of sample scripts.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Web_series">
       Web series
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Web series">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 2013 Captain Canuck was reimagined for a five-episode animated
      <a href="/wiki/Web_series" title="Web series">
       web series
      </a>
      by Captain Canuck Inc and Smiley Guy Studios.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kris_Holden-Reid" title="Kris Holden-Reid">
       Kris Holden-Reid
      </a>
      voices the Captain and
      <a href="/wiki/Paul_Amos" title="Paul Amos">
       Paul Amos
      </a>
      his antagonist, Mr. Gold. Several other actors voiced roles in the series including
      <a href="/wiki/Tatiana_Maslany" title="Tatiana Maslany">
       Tatiana Maslany
      </a>
      as Redcoat and
      <a href="/wiki/Laura_Vandervoort" title="Laura Vandervoort">
       Laura Vandervoort
      </a>
      as Blue Fox.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="New_Ongoing_Comic_Book_Series">
       New Ongoing Comic Book Series
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_Canuck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: New Ongoing Comic Book Series">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]

      </span>

     <p>
      The success of the Web series allowed for a one shot comic book, The 2014
      <i>
       Captain Canuck Summer Special
      </i>
      , which was released on Canada Day, July 1, 2014 at comic shops across Canada.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This is the first time since 1981 that the Summer Special was released. In 2015 a
      <i>
       Captain Canuck #0
      </i>
      issue was released on Free Comic Book Day,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      which is the first Saturday of May each year. This issue preceded the new ongoing
      <i>
       Captain Canuck
      </i>
      series published by
      <a class="external text" href="http://chapterhouse.ca/" rel="nofollow">
       ChapterHouse Comics
      </a>
      , the first issue of which was available in comic shops worldwide on May 27, 2015.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>

        </span>

      </sup>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">
       Influences

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
