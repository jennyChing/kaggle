           <h4>
            A vaporizer company out of Denver is looking to a hire a full-time cannabis specialist, whose primary task will be to test new products.
           </h4>
           <p>
            Earlier this month, Open Vape listed a job opening through its Facebook page for a Cannabis Quality Control Specialist who will sample, evaluate and document details about our cannabis products. According to the
            <a href="https://www.facebook.com/notes/openvape/do-you-want-to-consume-cannabis-for-a-living/353393101467826">
             listing
            </a>
            , salary will be determined based on experience.
           </p>
           <p>
            While the job seems simple enough, Open Vape Vice President Todd Mitchem told
            <a href="http://www.dailydot.com/business/open-vape-contest-smoking-pot-job/">
             The Daily Dot
            </a>
            that the company is looking for a true expert on the subject.
           </p>
           <p>
            Mitchem says that means someone who can, for example, tell the
            <a href="http://www.leafscience.com/2013/12/09/california-scientist-thinks-strain-names-fake/">
             difference between an indica and a sativa
            </a>
            based on smell alone, and someone who is familiar with all forms of cannabis, including flowers, concentrates, edibles and topicals.
           </p>
           <p>
            The job also entails accurate, objective reporting of the user experience.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_6484" style="width: 600px">
            <img alt="(Photo: CannabisEncyclopedia.com)" class="size-full wp-image-6484" height="361" src="http://www.leafscience.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/hiring-colorado-company-2-02-01.jpg" width="590"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             (Photo: CannabisEncyclopedia.com)
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Open Vape makes vaporizer pens and cannabis oil cartridges that are sold in various dispensaries across Colorado. But with the kick-off of legal recreational marijuana sales, the company, like many others, is poised for expansion.
           </p>
           <p>
            For instance, Medicine Man, a Denver-based dispensary that carries Open Vape products, is already seeing a boost in business from the
            <a href="http://www.leafscience.com/2014/01/10/legal-marijuana-expensive-in-colorado/">
             recreational market
            </a>
            . By this spring, the dispensary will undergo a $2.6 million expansion that will double its growing capacity and revamp its storefront,
            <a href="http://www.denverpost.com/marijuana/ci_24943347/family-owned-pot-shop-denver-seeks-become-national">
             The Denver Post
            </a>
            reports.
           </p>
           <p>
            In addition to a standard resume, Open Vape is inviting applicants to submit a short video describing what qualifies them for the job. So far, the company has received over 1,000 submissions, which Mitchem says range widely from absurd to serious.
           </p>
           <p>
            The deadline for those interested in becoming a legitimate Cannabis Quality Control Specialist is March 1.
           </p>
