          <p>
           Many people are already planning where you will enjoy the holidays. And the iOS devices, Apple, are great companions for long trips. Therefore, separated some simple tips to help you keep your gadget live longer.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Invest in a battery case:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           A battery case for your iPhone extends the duration of his battery and also protects you from any falls and hits. Most cases of this type comes with plugs made to fit with the 30-pin connector (used to the iPhone 4S) or Lightning (iPhone 5 forward), which they use to deliver the cargo to your smartphone. The only downside is that you can not use any accessories like docking stations without removing the iPhone case.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.onclickinfo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/iPhone-6-Plus.jpg">
            <img alt="iPhone 6 Plus" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1283" height="390" src="http://www.onclickinfo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/iPhone-6-Plus.jpg" width="520"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Press one charger
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           No need to carry the iPhone charger and the other that came with the iPad. Save space to only take what is sent along with the tablet. Visually, the iPad charger is larger, with pins that can be pulled and removed from the body of the accessory, like the MacBook charger. The iPhone charger is smaller, and their pins can not be removed.
          </p>
          <p>
           But what if you have an iPhone 5 or later (with Lightning connector) and, say, a third-generation iPad (with the older standard 30-pin dock)? You can also load only the iPad wall charger and carry along the Lightning to USB cable and also the dock 30-pin to USB, or take a Lightning adapter for 30-pin.
          </p>
          <p>
           The iPhone and iPad chargers provide different levels of energy. The increased demands of the iPad demand the strongest Adapter  USB adapter 10 or 12-watt depending on the iPad compared to the iPhone USB adapter and iPad mini, which has a 5 watt output. You can use the iPhone adapter for your iPad, but the tablet will take much longer to achieve a full charge, especially if you are an iPad third or fourth generation.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.onclickinfo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/ipad-mini.jpg">
            <img alt="ipad mini" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1284" height="326" src="http://www.onclickinfo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/ipad-mini.jpg" width="435"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Adjust the settings of the iPhone and iPad
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Save your battery charge can be as simple as adjusting some settings on your iPhone and iPad.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ff0000;">
             Disable the wireless options:
            </span>
           </strong>
           Disable Wi-Fi when not using it. Go to Settings -&gt; Wi-Fi, and then move the switch to Off. The same goes for Bluetooth  disable if not using. In this case, go to Settings -&gt; General -&gt; Bluetooth, and move the switch to Off.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ff0000;">
             Disable notifications:
            </span>
           </strong>
           Disable or limit their notifications by data from other applications worth it, but you have to do this manually with each app. Go to Settings -&gt; Notifications, select each app you want to adjust, and then change the Notification Center switch to Off.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ff0000;">
             Enable Airplane Mode:
            </span>
           </strong>
           Activate Airplane Mode disables all wireless features (wireless) iPhone  mobile data, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, GPS and other location services. To do this, go to Settings and enable Airplane Mode (first choice of the native app).
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ff0000;">
             Disable location services:
            </span>
           </strong>
           Either disable location services, and you will save a lot of energy, not to mention the money if you are traveling outside of your home country. Go to Settings -&gt; Location Services and change the key to Off.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="color: #ff0000;">
             Use automatic brightness:
            </span>
           </strong>
           The control for Auto Brightness (Auto-Brightness), which automatically adjusts the brightness of the screen based on ambient light conditions, is located in Settings -&gt; Brightness. Decrease the brightness manually screen can also extend battery life.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #ff0000;">
            <strong>
             Bonus tip:
            </strong>
           </span>
           Do not leave your player gets too hot or cold. Apple recommends keeping your smartphone from 0 to 35 degrees Celsius. So if you travel anywhere with extreme temperatures, consider protecting your iPhone or iPad ambient temperature. (For more tips on conserving battery iPhone, see the Apple battery guide itself.)
