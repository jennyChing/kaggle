     <h2 class="page_title">
      Cheese and Leek Bread Pudding with Cranks
     </h2>
     <img alt="Cheese and Leek Pudding" class="nimbus_750_900 wp-post-image" height="480" src="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Cheese-and-Leek-Pudding1.jpg" width="640"/>
     <div class="row single_meta">
      <div class="col-sm-8">
       <p>
        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/category/dinner/" rel="category tag">
         Dinner
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/category/reviews/eating-out-events-and-visits/" rel="category tag">
         Eating Out, Events and Visits
        </a>
        |                                         9th April 2015 | By
        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/author/choclette/" rel="author" title="Posts by Choclette">
         Choclette
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-sm-4">
       <p class="text-right">
        <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/2015/04/cheese-and-leek-bread-pudding-with-cranks/#comments">
         36 comments
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="16577022" data-link="http://tinandthyme.uk/2015/04/cheese-and-leek-bread-pudding-with-cranks/" data-summary="" data-title="Cheese and Leek Bread Pudding with Cranks">
     </div>
     <p>
      Cranks and I go back a long way. The Dartington Cider Press branch, now Cranks Kitchen, opened many years ago, when I was at school. It was a very exciting prospect for an ever hungry bunch of teenagers and it was only a couple of hundred yards down the hill. We couldnt afford much, but I do remember wolfing down a fair number ofcheese baps and date slices. I have been a frequent visitor ever since. When I was invited to attend a new product launch event there a few week ago, I was not going to say no.
      <span id="more-5290">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      Cranks was a hippy vegetarian wholefood cafe that first opened its doors in Carnaby Street in 1961. It has been weathering the storms of foodie fashion ever since by staying true tothe concept ofits founder, David Canter  delicious food that is good for both body and soul.
      <a href="http://www.cranks.co.uk/gb/cranks-kitchen" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       Cranks Kitchen
      </a>
      is now the sole restaurant and it still cooks slow food using locally sourced ingredients wherever possible. Much of the same foodthat was available to me back in the 1970s is still on the menu now. Latterly, Richard Corrigan has teamed up with Cranks bakery to createsome new slow rise breads. As well as
      <em>
       Carry on Carrot
      </em>
      and
      <em>
       Breaditerranean,
      </em>
      there is one which particularly resonates with me:
      <em>
       Hippity Hoppity
      </em>
      is a cheese and potato bread inspired by the famous Cranks homity pie. Ive been eating and enjoying homity pie for more years than I care to remember.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Richard-Corrigan.jpg">
       <img alt="Richard Corrigan" class="img-responsive wp-image-5306 size-medium alignright" height="300" src="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Richard-Corrigan-224x300.jpg" width="224"/>
      </a>
      I was lucky enough to sit with Richard Corrigan and beentertained by his Irish wit and free flowing conversationthroughout our meal. It was his suggestion to share a platter of food from the menu rather than each ordering our own individual meal. This was a great idea that immediately appealed to me, as I too like to try a little bit of everything. The staff coped with good will and grace to our unusual request and a large plate full of savoury bites and colourful salads soon appeared. Quite what we had has rather faded from memory now, but it was all very tasty. I know we had some homity pie, Im not likely to forget that and I also know we had some of this
      <a href="http://www.cranks.co.uk/gb/recipes/savoury-cheese-and-leek-bread-pudding" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       savoury cheese and leek bread pudding
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Cranks-Cake.jpg">
       <img alt="Cranks Cake" class=" size-medium wp-image-5307 img-responsive alignleft" height="233" src="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Cranks-Cake-300x233.jpg" width="300"/>
      </a>
      The restaurant was packed with happy diners, mostly regular customers invited for the launch, which seemsa particularly gracious way of saying thank you. The staff were cheerful and helpful, the food was excellent and we were all given a rather large goodie bag on arrival. The festive atmosphere was supported by a generous number of balloons  and there was cake. Cake always puts a smile on peoples faces and it certainly did on ours. Not a crumb remainedby the time we left the table. The goodie bag contained a loaf of Cranks wholemeal, which incidentally is one of the nicest wholemeal loaves Ive ever had; also included were a
      <em>
       Hippity Hoppity
      </em>
      loaf, a couple of bags of dried beans and excitingly, anew edition of
      <em>
       Cranks Recipe Book
      </em>
      by David Canter,first published in 1982.
     </p>
     <p>
      I was so excited by the new Cranks recipe for savoury cheese and leek bread pudding, that I made some for supper the very next day. We were both rather hungryand I didnt have time for pictures, so what you can see at the top of this post are the leftovers. These didnt stay leftover for long; the pudding was almost as delicious cold as it was hot. We ate ours with purple sprouting broccoli  highly recommended.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/RecipeClippings-logo.jpg">
       <img alt="Recipe Clippings Logo" class=" img-responsive alignright wp-image-5313 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/RecipeClippings-logo-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
      </a>
      As we were given a paper recipe card to take away, I am entering this into
      <strong>
       Recipe Clippings
      </strong>
      over at
      <a href="http://farmersgirl.blogspot.co.uk/2015/03/the-slow-cooked-challenge-march-round.html" target="_blank">
       FarmersgirlKitchen
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Bookmarked Recipes" class=" size-full wp-image-5101 img-responsive alignright" height="200" src="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/bookmarked-recipes-new.jpg" width="172"/>
      Likewise I am entering this into Jacs
      <strong>
       Bookmarked Recipes
      </strong>
      over at
      <a href="http://www.tinnedtomatoes.com/2015/04/11-vegggie-vegan-recipes-to-bookmark.html" target="_blank">
       Tinned Tomatoes
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>

     <p>

      <a href="http://tinandthyme.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Credit-Crunch-Munch.gif">

        <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
         Ingredients
        </div>
        <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
          2 medium leeks - sliced
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
          400g (14oz) Cranks Hippity Homity Loaf- sliced and cut into triangles (I used Cranks wholemeal loaf).
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
          150g (5oz) cheddar cheese - grated
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
          40g (1.5oz) salted butter
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
          600ml (1 pint) milk
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
          3 medium eggs
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
          freshly ground black pepper
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="7">
           tsp sea salt (I omitted this as I figured the cheese and butter were quite salty enough).
         </li>
        </ol>

       <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="1">
        <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
         Instructions
        </div>
        <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
          Grease a 1.5l baking dish with a little butter. Melt the remaining butter in a frying pan and gently fry the leeks for about five minutes, until softened.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
          Put a layer of bread in the base of the baking dish. Scatter half the leeks on top, then sprinkle with half the grated cheese. Arrange the remaining bread triangles over the surface, followed by the rest of the leeks.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
          Beat the milk and eggs together and season to taste. Pour evenly over the bread, then sprinkle with the remaining cheese. Leave to stand for 20 to 30 minutes.
         </li>
         <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">
          Preheat the oven to 190C / 375F / gas mark 5. Bake the pudding for 30 to 35 minutes, until puffed-up and golden brown.
         </li>
