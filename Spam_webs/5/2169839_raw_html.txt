       </p>

       </p>

       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/354897/files/20140207015919-stretch.png?1391767159" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <b>

       </b>
       iOS support will be through Bluetooth v4 (BLE/Smart)
       <br/>
       Compatible devices: iPhone 4s, 5, 5s, 5c; iPad 3, 4, Mini, Air
       <br/>
       <br/>
       All current features that are listed below will be supported on iOS.
       <br/>
       Future upgrades which involve higher data rates may not be supported.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="line-height: 1.45em;">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/323055/files/20140122050446-whatispe.png?1390395886" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <i>
         <span style="line-height: 1.45em;">
          Pockethernet is the first smartphone connected Ethernet cable and network tester that is affordable and fits into your pocket.
         </span>
        </i>
       </p>
       <br/>
       If you are familiar with setting up computer networks you will know that Ethernet cables are still crucial in providing homes, offices and data centers with internet. Pockethernet will make it dead easy to install, troubleshoot and maintain Ethernet networks. We have created a fully featured Ethernet cable and network tester at 1/10 the price of it's competition.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Are you
       <br/>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Installing and troubleshooting Ethernet cables and devices?
        </li>
        <li>
         Setting up routers, switches, computers, IP phones and cameras?
        </li>
        <li>
         Working as a network administrator or installation technician?
        </li>
        <li>
         Building Ethernet networks at home and at friends?
        </li>
       </ul>
       <br/>
       Then you'll be interested in what Pockethernet can do!
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/323059/files/20140122050527-importantfeatures.png?1390395927" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        <i>
         <b>
          Measure the length of cables to see where they are going.
         </b>
         <br/>
         Or how much is left on the reel.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/334404/files/20140128062705-wb2-cable-lenght.png?1390919225" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <br/>
       <p align="center">
        <i>
         <b>
          Find faults like split pairs and short circuits.
         </b>
         <br/>
         Pockethernet tells you exactly where they are located.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/334406/files/20140128062733-wb-short-circuit.png?1390919253" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <br/>
       <p align="center">
        <i>
         <b>
          Locate cables in the wall or in batches with the toning feature.
         </b>
         <br/>
         If the cable is connected, use the port LED blinking function.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/334408/files/20140128062808-wb-toner.png?1390919288" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <br/>
       <p align="center">
        <i>
         <b>
          Check Ethernet and Internet connectivity.
         </b>
         <br/>
         From duplex mismatches to routing errors, find out what's wrong.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/334410/files/20140128062859-wb-2.png?1390919339" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <br/>
       <p align="center">
        <i>
         <b>
          When you're done, send a pdf report to your client or for your own record.
         </b>
         <br/>
         Have an up to date database and prove your work.
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/334416/files/20140128063041-wb-pdf.png?1390919441" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <br/>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/323061/files/20140122050556-smartphone_conn.png?1390395956" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       The Pockethernet hardware works together with the device you are probably having in your pocket right now: your smartphone.
       <br/>
       Bluetooth enables Pockethernet to communicate with your smartphone. The app is tailored to both the needs of professionals and semi-professionals. 
Youll be able to run tests in simple and advanced modes and easily read out the results from your phone.
       <br/>
       <p>
        <b>
         Simple                 Advanced
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/323081/files/20140122052123-ui_simple3.png?1390396883" style="width: 161.995px; height: 339px;max-width:580px;"/>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/323082/files/20140122052308-cableip.png?1390396988" style="line-height: 1.45em; width: 175.698px; height: 341px;max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <br/>
       <p>
        The app is currently only available for Android.
        <br/>
        iOS will be supported if we reach the stretch goal.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img src="https://images.indiegogo.com/file_attachments/323063/files/20140122050620-techdetails.png?1390395980" style="max-width:580px;"/>
       </p>
       <i>
        Measurement functions:
       </i>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <b>
          Wiremap including shield
         </b>
         <br/>
         Test if you punched the pins in the correct order on the RJ45 jacks. Pockethernet also tests the shield for continuity.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          TDR-based, single ended length measurement and fault locating
         </b>
         <br/>
         Advanced
 cable diagnosis with just one end connected to Pockethernet. See how 
long the cable is and if there are any short circuits, split pairs or 
bad terminations. Pockethernet also detects if the cable is connected to a switched off computer or switch.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Voltage detection
         </b>
         <br/>
         Find out if there's a phone line or passive PoE on one of the pairs, to detect services and to protect your network equipment.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          PoE supply detectionMeasure the presence and voltage of Power over
         </b>
         Ethernet, to ensure proper power supply of your PoE devices.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Legacy PoE device detection
         </b>
         <br/>
         Find out if there is a non-standard PoE device (e.g. old IP phones) at the other end of the cable that need an upgrade.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Cable toner with customizable tones
         </b>
         <br/>
         Locate where cables are running in the wall and identify single cables in batches or at patch panels. You can choose from a wide variety of tones that go beyond the usual ringing.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Bit error rate test (10/100/1000)
         </b>
         <br/>
         Real-world cable quality measurement at gigabit speeds. Stop finger-pointing with measurement data.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Port blinker
         </b>
         <br/>
         Let Pockethernet toggle the Link LED on switches and routers, so you know which cable you are looking for.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Link speed and duplex identification (10/100/1000)
         </b>
         <br/>
         Find out the Ethernet capabilities of that unused port you're wondering about and rule out duplex conflicts.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Link establishment test (10/100/1000)
         </b>
         <br/>
         Check if a link can be established at different speeds. Make sure the wiring is up to date for gigabit.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          DHCP, DNS, Ping, Traceroute tests (10/100)
         </b>
         <br/>
         Test the network connection to see if you can get an IP address on a port and reach internal servers or the Internet.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Netscan, traffic detection (10/100)
         </b>
         <br/>
         See what other devices are reachable on that port, what kind and how much traffic is directed there.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          PDF report generation
         </b>
         <br/>
         Have a detailed log about your work: Just add location and port ID and you'll always have an up-to-date database and a proof of your work.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Send report via email
         </b>
         <br/>
         With the press of a button, email the report for your own records, to an issue tracking system or to your clients. No more lost reports, tedious downloads from the device or manual editing.
        </li>
       </ul>

           <br/>

           <br/>
           <br/>
