        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Apple Crisp with Snickerdoodle Cookie Crust
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2014-09-16T13:33:50+00:00">
          September 16, 2014
         </span>
         By
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://busy-mommy.com/author/emily" rel="author">
            Emily
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          You can probably tell that fall is one of my favorite times of year by my recent postings {hello
          <a href="http://busy-mommy.com/2014/09/pumpkin-streusel-dessert-pizza.html">
           Pumpkin Streusel Dessert Pizza
          </a>
          !} Pumpkin, apples and cinnamon are the ultimate fall flavors and Im all about fall desserts. Theres nothing better than cooking soup in the slow cooker and having a pumpkin or apple dessert on a chilly, fall night.
         </p>
         <p>
          This
          <strong>
           Apple Crisp
          </strong>
          combines the delicious cinnamon-and-sugary snickerdoodle cookie with the perfect in-season fruit right now: apples.
         </p>
         <p>
          Unfortunately we didnt get any apples this year on
          <a href="http://busy-mommy.com/2014/06/backyard-garden.html" target="_blank">
           our apple trees
          </a>
          since they were just planted, but Im hoping for next year.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://busy-mommy.com/2014/09/apple-crisp-snickerdoodle-cookie-crust.html/apple-crisp-snickerdoodle-cookie-crust-2" rel="attachment wp-att-15564">
           <img alt="Apple Crisp with Snickerdoodle Cookie Crust" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15564" height="840" src="//busy-mommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Apple-Crisp-Snickerdoodle-Cookie-Crust.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          This recipe is easy. You can use your favorite snickerdoodle cookie recipe, or mine below.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://busy-mommy.com/2014/09/apple-crisp-snickerdoodle-cookie-crust.html/img_0646" rel="attachment wp-att-15558">
           <img alt="Snickerdoodle Cookie Dough" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15558" height="840" src="//busy-mommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_0646.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I must say, this is the best snickerdoodle recipe though. You might want to try it.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://busy-mommy.com/2014/09/apple-crisp-snickerdoodle-cookie-crust.html/img_0653" rel="attachment wp-att-15557">
           <img alt="Apples" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15557" height="840" src="//busy-mommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_0653.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Feel free to use whatever apples you prefer. I used granny smith and gala.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://busy-mommy.com/2014/09/apple-crisp-snickerdoodle-cookie-crust.html/img_0684" rel="attachment wp-att-15556">
           <img alt="Apples + Snickerdoodles" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15556" height="840" src="//busy-mommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_0684.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          All you do is plop that snickerdoodle dough wherever youd like. You can do small drops or layer the whole pan with the dough. Sprinkle with a bit of cinnamon and sugar and bake at 350 for 40 minutes. Whatever you do, serve this WARM!
         </p>
         <h1>
          Apple Crisp with Snickerdoodle Cookie Crust
         </h1>
         <p>
          6 apples peeled, sliced and cored.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Snickerdoodle dough:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          3 3/4 cup flour
          <br/>
          1/2 tsp. baking soda
          <br/>
          1/2 tsp cream of tarter
         </p>
         <p>
          1/2 tsp. salt
          <br/>
          1 cup butter
          <br/>
          1 3/4 cup sugar
          <br/>
          1 tsp vanilla
          <br/>
          2 eggs
          <br/>
          1/4 cup milk
         </p>
         <p>
          1/4 cup sugar + 1 tsp cinnamon, divided
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Preheat oven to 350 and grease a 913 baking pan.
          </li>
          <li>
           Stir togetherflour, baking soda, cream of tarter and salt.
          </li>
          <li>
           In a mixer, beat butter. Add sugar and beat until fluffy. Add eggs, milk and vanilla.
          </li>
          <li>
           Add dry ingredients and mix.
          </li>
          <li>
           Peel, slice and core the apples and place in the baking pan. Sprinkle 1/2 of the cinnamon and sugar mixture over the apples.
          </li>
          <li>
           Place drops of snickerdoodle dough over the apples. Sprinkle the remaining cinnamon and sugar over the cookie dough.
          </li>
          <li>
           Bake at 350 for 40-45 minutes. Serve warm!
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          <a href="http://busy-mommy.com/2014/09/apple-crisp-snickerdoodle-cookie-crust.html/img_0708" rel="attachment wp-att-15555">
           <img alt="Apple Crisp with a Snickerdoodle Cookie Crust" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15555" height="840" src="//busy-mommy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_0708.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
