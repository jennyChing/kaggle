   <h3 class="post-title">
    <a href="/craft_blog/2014/12/grey-scarf.aspx" title="permalink">
     Grey Scarf
    </a>
   </h3>
   <div>
    The (oh-so-slight) downside of getting your husband to go into fabric stores and yarn stores with you for a decade-and-a-half is that he will eventually learn that there are things there that he might like ... things that end up being
    <a href="http://futuregirl-dot-com.tumblr.com/post/98815817728/made-two-more-handkerchiefs-for-andrew-gotta">
     projects
    </a>
    for you
    <a href="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/2013/9/tutorial-handkerchiefs.aspx">
     to make
    </a>
    .
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/12/grey-scarf01.jpg"/>
    </div>
    In this case, a soft squishy ball of lovely mottled grey wool-silk blend became a request for a soft, light scarf."Of course I can do that," I answer.And the giant ball of fingering weight yarn comes home with us.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/12/grey-scarf02.jpg"/>
    </div>
    Fast forward 7 months after countless swatches, needle size tests, calculations, and charts. It took me forever to settle on a simple linen stitch to show off the subtle bright patches in the yarn.I love the way the slipped stitches break up the colors and give the scarf a pixelated look, like the static of an old black-and-white TV
    <br/>
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/12/grey-scarf03.jpg"/>
    </div>
    After the first 5 inches (seen here) I did a quick wet block.This yarn feels so incredible in linen stitch with a slightly stretched block (7 inches across became 7.5 inches). It will be a very light scarf, which is exactly what was requested.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    I have put all my other knitting on hold until I finish this, which has lit a fire under me, for sure.In just 8 days, I've made it almost half way through the scarf!I set some aggressive goals for finishing it so I can be sure I'm not still knitting it over the holidays, because, although I'm happy to knit this for Andrew, plain linen stitch isn't very interesting to knit for 50-inches.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    I calculated that this scarf will take 56,000 stitches and 31 hours to knit. I've never done calculations like that before, and Im pretty sure I never will again.It's pretty daunting, to say the least.
    <br style="clear:both;"/>

    <a href="/craft_blog/2014/12/tree-ornament-2014.aspx" title="permalink">
     2014 Ornament - Tree-ception
    </a>
   </h3>
   <div>
    I have a very good friend
    <a href="http://www.gingerbreadsnowflakes.com/">
     Pam
    </a>
    <a>
    </a>
    who loves the winter holidays, so every year I make her a Christmas ornament.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    When November 1st rolled around, I started brainstorming.Every time I had an idea I realized I'd already used the motif ...
    <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/projects/futuregirl/crochet-christmas-owl-ornaments">
     an owl
    </a>
    ,
    <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/projects/futuregirl/pine-cone-collection">
     a pincone
    </a>
    ,
    <a href="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/2014/1/cabled-pillow.aspx">
     a heart
    </a>
    , and
    <a href="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/2010/12/xmas-to-eleven.aspx">
     a star
    </a>
    ... and I was starting to worry and then the cutest image popped into my ravelry pattern feed.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    The
    <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/projects/beakaptur/christmas-tree-ornament">
     Christmas Tree ornament
    </a>
    (ravelry account needed for link) by beakaptur is so adorable!She hasn't published her pattern yet, so I did a ravelry search of Christmas Tree patterns (knit and crochet) and found
    <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/little-christmas-tree">
     Little Christmas Tree
    </a>
    , and I loved just as much.Bonus, it is a free pattern!
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/12/tree01.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/12/tree02.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/12/tree03.jpg"/>
    </div>
    My only modifications were adding random purl stitches to make the tree textured, and I crocheted the trunk because it's so much easier to crochet tiny things than to knit them.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    I resisted an urge to sew every bead, bobble, button, and sequin I own to the tree; I kept it simple.In my imagination, I see the pearly beads and gold sequins as reflectors of whatever colors and lights are on the tree where it will hang.That way it will always coordinate and be a mini-me of the bigger tree.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    A tree to hang on your tree= tree-ception!

     River of Doubt - The Source
    </a>
   </h3>
   <div>
    I've started a new sweater named
    <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/projects/futuregirl/river-of-doubt">
     River of Doubt
    </a>
    .It's come together from a lot of different sources:
    <ul>
     <li>
      I wanted to make a sweater with contiguous sleeves.
     </li>
     <li>
      I wanted to make a sweater with triangle-y knit-purl design.
     </li>
     <li>
      I have had 12 skeins of this yarn in my stash for ... a long time.
     </li>
     <li>
      I had just purchased the book "River of Doubt" and it matched the color of the yarn and the gist of the design.I recommend the book, by the way.
     </li>
     <li>
      My birthday was coming up and I was in my normal "what does the future hold" mindset, just like Roosevelt before his journey in "River of Doubt."
     </li>
    </ul>
    I bought the pattern for
    <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/crazy-stripes-tee">
     Crazy Stripes Tee
    </a>
    because I like the neckline (and would eventually like to make that sweater).I followed the directions for the top-down, seamless, contiguous set in sleeves.So fantastic!
    <br/>
    <br/>
    I had to chart every row of the front, back, and sleeves so I could figure out where the diamond and chevron stripes should be so that they'd all line up once I got to the armpits and started knitting the body in the round.That took quite a bit of work.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    And, about the time I got to the armpits, I realized I was making a sweater 3 sizes too big.My normal ... haha.So I frogged the whole thing, redrew the charts, and now things are moving much faster!
    <br/>
    <br/>
    Here are photos of my progress over the last couple of months:

   <h3 class="post-title">
    <a href="/craft_blog/2014/7/tutorial-lucky-wristlet-strap.aspx" title="permalink">
     Tutorial: Make a Ribbon Strap for the Lucky Wristlet
    </a>
   </h3>
   <div>
    This is the photo tutorial for making a ribbon strap for the Lucky Wristlet.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_020.jpg"/>
    </div>
    The tutorial is broken down, step-by-step.Let me know if something isn't clear.I will be happy to help you out.
    <br/>
    <br/>
    <strong>
     Detailed instructions
    </strong>
    (text is above its corresponding photo)
    <br/>
    <br/>
    1.You'll need a crocheted Lucky Wristlet using my
    <a href="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/downloads.aspx">
     free PDF crochet pattern
    </a>
    and one yard of ribbon.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_001.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    2.Find the side of your wristlet that doesn't have a spike crochet "belt loop" for your ribbon.This side has a spike.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_003.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <!-- clip -->
    <br/>
    3.This side does not have a spike.This is the strap side.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_002.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    4.Fold your ribbon end so you can easily thread it through the first spike stitch.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_004.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    5.Keep threading your ribbon under the spike stitches ...
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_005.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    6.... until you've gone all the way around.Adjust the ribbon so both loose ends are the same length.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_006.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    7.Take one ribbon end and wrap it around the other ribbon end.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_007.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    8.Make a simple knot.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_008.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    9.Pull the knot tightly against the wristlet.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_009.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    10.Here is what the strap looks like with the first knot completed.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_010.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    11.The other strap is nowlonger than the knotted strap.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_011.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    12.Take the longer strap and wrap it around the shorter strap.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_012.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    13.Make a simple knot near the end.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_013.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    14.Pull the knot tightly.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_014.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    15.We are going to finish the ribbon ends now.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_015.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    16.Trim both ends on a diagonal.I trim them separately, not at the same time.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_016.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    17.Here they are trimmed.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_017.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    18.I use Fray Check to secure the threads at the end of the ribbon so they don't fray and look a mess.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_018.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    19.The strap is done.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_020.jpg"/>
    </div>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    20.Now you can use your wristlet.
    <br/>
    <div align="center">
     <img src="http://www.futuregirl.com/craft_blog/images/2014/06/wristlet-ribbon-tutorial_021.jpg"/>
    </div>
