         <figure class="post-image pull-right">
          <a href="/blog/2014/12/content-closers-how-leverage-content-sales-enablement-gp.html">
           <img alt="Content is for Closers: How to Leverage Content for Sales Enablement " src="/content/dam/blogs/legacy/2014/12/6a00e54ee3905b883301b7c70f07c6970.jpg" title="Content is for Closers: How to Leverage Content for Sales Enablement "/>

         <p>
          Put. That coffee. Down. Coffees for closers only.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you work in Sales or Marketing (and were born before 1992), you probably recognize that line from Alec Baldwins character in
          <a href="http://youtu.be/r6Lf8GtMe4M?t=41s" target="_blank">
           <em>
            Glengarry Glen Ross
           </em>
          </a>
          . But lets shake it up a bit.
         </p>
         <p>
          Forget coffee.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Content
          </em>
          is for closers.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its time to consider content an integral part of the sales process. We all know that content can be a reliable source for lead generation, keeping us flush with a steady stream of qualified prospects. But, in many cases, thats where the ball drops.
         </p>
         <p>
          From a marketing perspective, leads come in and we do our best to qualify them, score them, and nurture them. Eventually, we pass them off to sales and all of a sudden the steady flow of useful content dries up for these sales qualified leads.
         </p>
         <p>
          Maybe its not being sent, or maybe it doesnt exist.
         </p>
         <p>
          The sales process becomes long and cumbersome. The prospect begins to waver in their belief about the value you provide. They doubt whether they truly need your product.
         </p>
         <p>
          This, of course, is a scenario that can be avoided.
         </p>
         <p>
          Leveraging content throughout the sales process is a powerful way to educate and persuade potential customers, clarify the value proposition, manage objections and expedite the sales cycle.
         </p>
         <p>
          To be clear, Im not talking about bottom-of-the-funnel content versus top-of-the-funnel content. Im talking about
          <a href="http://hub.uberflip.com/h/i/28583845-creating-content-for-sales-enablement/109406" target="_blank">
           content your sales team can use
          </a>
          to help close a deal. Depending on the specific challenges their prospect is facing, this content might sit anywhere along your funnel.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youre a content marketer, you know that its important to create content at every stage of the buyer journey. On paper, this seems like a nice, clean way to guide people towards a sale. People will consume content at one stage, then move along to the next.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="asset-img-link" href="/content/dam/blogs/legacy/2014/12/6a00e54ee3905b883301b8d0997e1e970.jpg" style="display: inline;">
           <img alt="Arrows-pink-1" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e54ee3905b883301b8d0997e1e970c image-full img-responsive" src="/content/dam/blogs/legacy/2014/12/6a00e54ee3905b883301b8d0997e1e970.jpg/_jcr_content/renditions/800wi.jpg" title="Arrows-pink-1"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Photo courtesy of
           <a href="http://www.uberflip.com/" target="_blank">
            Uberflip
           </a>
           .
          </em>
          <em>
           <a href="http://contentmarketinginstitute.com/2014/03/content-marketing-plan-influencers-partners/">
            <br/>
           </a>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          But the reality is not that linear  people wont just magically find their way along the path you created. While its true that great content marketing can help bring a prospect close to becoming a sale, there are often more questions or concerns that arise once they speak to a sales rep. The more complex and expensive your product, the more likely this is to occur. This is why marketing and sales have to work together to expose relevant content
          <em>
           during
          </em>
          the sales cycle.
         </p>
         <p>
          But, before we get into how you can do this, allow me to bust a few myths.
         </p>
         <h4>
          Myth #1: Content is the marketers domain.
         </h4>
         <p>
          Yes and no. Social, blog articles, websites, videos, the list goes on and onthey all start with the formulation of content. Many take this to mean that content belongs only to the marketing team. To craft great content you need to stretch past the perspective of a marketer. Yes, you need to talk to customers. But beyond that, your content must be informed by sales.
         </p>
         <p>
          If your sales team can provide input about the type of content thats created, theyll be more likely to use it. On the flip side, understanding the conversations that sales reps are having daily can help marketers create amazing content.
         </p>
         <h4>
          Myth #2: Salespeople dont care. They just want
          <em>
           something
          </em>
          to send in their follow up email.
         </h4>
         <p>
          Wrong. Good salespeople 
          <em>
           effective
          </em>
          salespeople  do care. They understand the impact that a well-crafted, strategically placed piece of content can have on the sales cycle. And if they dont, fire them.
         </p>
         <h4>
          Myth #3: Salespeople are lazy.
         </h4>
         <p>
          While in some cases this might be true, lazy isnt necessarily the problem. But busy might be. If youre on the marketing side, how many times has a sales rep asked,
          <em>
           Do we have a blog post about X?
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          So, you go to the blog, search for the post, forward the link, and as your temper rises you secretly imagine the sales rep spontaneously combusting. After all, couldnt he have done all that himself?!
         </p>
         <p>
          Part of making sure your sales reps use content throughout the sales cycle is to make it as easy as possible to access the right content at the right time.
         </p>
         <h3>
          A Framework for Using Content to Enable Sales
         </h3>
         <p>
          In a perfect world, marketing and sales are like bees and flowers, participating in a give-and-take relationship that enables both of them to flourish. But the world isnt perfect and history has shown that marketing and sales need defined processes in order for a company to thrive. It doesnt have to be a complicated process, but it does have to be explicitly understood.
         </p>
         <p>
          When it comes to leveraging content for sales enablement, there are a few things you should consider as you develop your process.
         </p>
         <h4>
          Aligning Content Creation With Sales Insights
         </h4>
         <p>
          Mining insight from your sales team can help you surface gaps in your content, identify questions at each stage of the sales cycle and manage objections via content.
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are a couple of questions I like to ask my sales team to get the conversation going:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           What is the biggest non-product-related question you hear?
          </li>
          <li>
           What are the most common objections?
          </li>
          <li>
           What is the biggest pain point were solving for people?
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Creating content around the answers will give you the opportunity to address them before the prospect even speaks to a sales rep. And if they dont see the content beforehand, then your sales reps are now armed with relevant content that they can use to help close the deal.
         </p>
         <p>
          Once youve had the first conversation with your sales team, keep them in the loop. Continue to refine the process and the content as your company and customer evolves.
         </p>
         <h4>
          A Tailored Content Experience for Prospects
         </h4>
         <p>
          Whether youre sending a blog post, a whitepaper or a video, the content experience is key. Remember that perfect world we talked about? In it, sales reps can send prospects to a highly targeted stream of content curated just for them.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres a great example of how a custom content stream that we created for one segment of our
          <a href="http://hub.uberflip.com/h/c/124438-everything-pardot" target="_blank">
           customers who use Pardot
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="asset-img-link" href="/content/dam/blogs/legacy/2014/12/6a01b8d0612dd7970c01bb07b41d3e970.jpg" style="display: inline;">
           <img alt="Untled" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a01b8d0612dd7970c01bb07b41d3e970d img-responsive" src="/content/dam/blogs/legacy/2014/12/6a01b8d0612dd7970c01bb07b41d3e970.jpg/_jcr_content/renditions/800wi.jpg" title="Untled"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This was created by our marketing team and provides information about our Pardot integration and marketing automation in general. Its a great place for our sales reps to send prospects that fit into this segment.
         </p>
         <p>
          Lately, weve been getting even more targeted thanks to one of our account executives, Jon, who was able to take it one step further with our content marketing software.
         </p>
         <p>
          Below is an example of a custom stream created specifically for one of his prospects. The stream is hidden, meaning that only those with the link can access it, but you can see that everything about the experience is targeted at that company including the title, description, even the call-to-action that prompts them to email Jon directly.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a class="asset-img-link" href="/content/dam/blogs/legacy/2014/12/6a01b8d0612dd7970c01bb07b41d38970.jpg" style="display: inline;">
           <img alt="J" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a01b8d0612dd7970c01bb07b41d38970d img-responsive" src="/content/dam/blogs/legacy/2014/12/6a01b8d0612dd7970c01bb07b41d38970.jpg/_jcr_content/renditions/800wi.jpg" title="J"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          At Uberflip we have an advantage. Our product revolves around content marketing and as a result everybody (including our sales team) buys into the power of great content. If your sales team isnt ready to take the initiative themselves, then as marketers we need to create these experiences for them to leverage.
         </p>
         <h4>
          Making it Easy for Sales Reps
         </h4>
         <p>
          Once youve identified the right content and experience, how do you make it easy for the sales rep to actually use the content?
         </p>
         <p>
          You employ tools that make it easy to introduce content into their process.
         </p>
         <p>
          There are a few ways you can do this:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Create a central resource library or content hub where they can go to access the right content.
          </li>
          <li>
           If your team is using something like Salesforce to manage email followups, include content links within the email template.
          </li>
          <li>
           Use a content marketing platform that facilitates the search and curation of your content so your sales reps can easily do it themselves.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <h3>
          Closing With Content
         </h3>
         <p>
          A sales team armed with content  content inspired by their own insights  is a sales team that can more effectively close deals. To make this happen youll need the right tools combined with the right processes for your company. So before you craft another piece of content, take a step back, walk over to the nearest sales rep, and have a conversation.
         </p>
