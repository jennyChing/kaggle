        <h1 class="entry-title">
         <a href="http://www.therideadvice.com/the-evolution-of-motorcycle-body-position/" rel="bookmark">
          The Evolution of Motorcycle Body Position
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="sep">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.therideadvice.com/the-evolution-of-motorcycle-body-position/" rel="bookmark" title="1:11 am">
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-06-14T01:11:32+00:00">
           June 14, 2014
          </time>
         </a>
         <span class="by-author">
          <span class="sep">
           by
          </span>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.therideadvice.com/author/efleming/" rel="author" title="View all posts by EBF">
            EBF
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="comments-link" style="display:none;">
         <a href="http://www.therideadvice.com/the-evolution-of-motorcycle-body-position/#comments">
          <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="543 http://therideadvice.com/?p=543">
           14
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" id="entry-content">
        <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="The Evolution of Motorcycle Body Position" data-url="http://www.therideadvice.com/the-evolution-of-motorcycle-body-position/">
        </div>
        <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
        </div>
        <p>
         Proper motorcycle body position, when used appropriately makes you a faster, and safer rider. By shifting your weight to the side of the bike, a rider reduces the bikes lean angle for a given speed, ensuring a greater contact patch between the road and tire.
        </p>
        <p>
         But it hasnt always been like and in fact, the way some riders went into corners looks not only archaic, but somewhat dangerous. As this article will show, motorcycle body position has evolved along with motorcycle technology.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          And in the Beginning
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Tracing the origin of something can sometimes be like looking into a stadium full of people and trying to locate a familiar face among thousands. Usually, certain styles, approaches and techniques happen all of a sudden among many people.
        </p>
        <p>
         With what well call modern motorcycle body position, the first rider to really show how it was done was Kenny Roberts, the Godfather of American road racing. Not only was Roberts the first American to win a Motorcycle GP Championship, he ushered in an era of American dominance, where riders from the United States won 12 of the next 15 championships.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_544" style="width: 460px">
         <a href="http://therideadvice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Giocomo-Agostini-02.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-544">
          <img alt="The Great Giocomo Agostini had no need to lean." class="wp-image-544 size-medium" height="305" src="//therideadvice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Giocomo-Agostini-02-450x305.jpg" width="450"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The Great Giocomo Agostini had no need to lean.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         When Roberts joined the championship, all other racers effectively sat upright on their bikes into corners, legs clenched to the side of the tanks with bits of bike scraping the ground as they took turns. Due to Roberts background in dirt bike riding, he changed this completely. He forced the motorcycles rear wheel to break traction to steer around a corner, breaking early and accelerating out of the turn as soon as possible, whereas other riders would brake late and accelerate late.
        </p>
        <p>
         His style worked and proved to be a legacy. From 1983 to 1999, every 500cc world champion had a dirt bike background. Roberts riding style required him to use duct tape on his knees so as not to wear a hole in his leathers  knee sliders hadnt been invented yet!
        </p>
        <p>
         After Roberts, more American greats followed  Freddie Spencer, Eddie Lawson and Wayne Rainey.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_547" style="width: 460px">
         <a href="http://therideadvice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Kenny-Roberts-Snr-04.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-547">
          <img alt="Yes, that's duct tape on his knee" class="wp-image-547 size-medium" height="319" src="//therideadvice.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Kenny-Roberts-Snr-04-450x319.jpg" width="450"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Yes, thats duct tape on his knee
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Spencer, Lawson, Rainey and all other riders carried on with this new body position. There were slight refinements, but nothing really changed for the next few years. And then Mick Doohan came along.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

           <div class="dsq-comment-message" id="dsq-comment-message-9">
            <p>
             A while ago I read a biographical article on and interview with Kenny Roberts (fair disclosure: hes my motorcycling hero!). It was said his style was inspired by the styles of Jarno Saarinen and Paul Smart. Jarno, in fact, did hang off, as you will see if you look at his images on a Google search. Kenny was one of the people who pioneered rear-wheel steering, and the narrower front tyres.
             <br/>
             As for the modern body position, I think thats a fair dinkum furphy (all round bulltish). Again, Kenny influenced the style, but it has evolved with the equipment and the riders.
            </p>
            <p>
             I would hazard that Marquezs style is largely influenced by his experience on the Moto2 bike, where the rider is far more contributory to a fast lap than in MotoGP. Because of the lower power level, it is easier for a rider to look for better ground clearance and to push his CG further into the turn (by hanging off more).
            </p>
            <p>
             Youll see similarities in the styles of all the ex-Moto2 riders, and the good MotoGP riders (such as Stoner and Lorenzo since a couple of years ago and Rossi this year) have also adopted the style to keep in front. This is the elbow-dragging style.
             <br/>
             That is why, in my opinion, there is no such thing as a modern motorcycle body position. The photo you provide certainly doesnt show Marquez deep into a turn. The attached photo is a bit better, but this is during free practice at Phillip Island so he doesnt have everything on the deck.
            </p>
