       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://blog.kaplan.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/career_change_kaplan_crop2.jpg">
          <img alt="career change advice" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1938" height="367" src="http://blog.kaplan.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/career_change_kaplan_crop2.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Changing career can be one of the biggest decisions in your life, and while it may be daunting, retraining could lead to greater earning potential and improved job satisfaction.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-1899">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre thinking of a career change, first ask yourself these eight questions:
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Is it my career or my job that I am dissatisfied with?
        </h3>
        <p>
         If youre unhappy in your professional life then a change could be the answer, but that change doesnt need to be radical. Switching to a different job of a similar ilk is much easier if you have the relevant experience.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whats the answer?
         </strong>
         Make a list of reasons why youre dissatisfied with your current working circumstances, and determine whether they are job or career-specific.
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. What qualifications/skills will I require?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Often the largest obstacle of a career change is the time and investment required to get the level of qualification necessary and determining if you have the right skills to do the job..
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whats the answer?
         </strong>
         Talk to a recruitment consultant and someone in the industry. They will be able to inform you of exactly what is needed to land a new career.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Where will I be in five years?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Make realistic five year plans for both your current career and the career you are considering switching to. Try to estimate where you will be: think earning potential, opportunities for career progression and anticipated happiness in the roles.
        </p>
        <p>
         Which is a more satisfactory career? If both deliver you to a similar point in 5 years time, the switched-to career represents a quicker progression , given it includes time to re-train.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whats the answer?
         </strong>
         Ask your employer and more experienced co-workers what you could expect from a career in the industry or even in the company.
        </p>
        <p>
         Training course providers can help you too. At
         <a href="http://financial.kaplan.co.uk/?utm_source=blog&amp;utm_medium=bancarticle&amp;utm_campaign=careerchange" target="_blank" title="Kaplan Financial">
          Kaplan Financial
         </a>
         we can help you compare different accountancy course options and get an idea of how long it will take to become qualified.
        </p>
        <p>
         A well-informed training provider can also give you an indication of the earning potential of careers opened up by the qualifications they offer.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="career change advice" class="alignnone wp-image-1906 size-full" height="427" src="http://blog.kaplan.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/confused_fotolia_edit1.jpg" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
        </h3>
        <h3>
         4. How will it affect my earning potential?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Although we may not like to admit it, one of the biggest factors in deciding whether to switch careers is earning potential. This is especially true if youre in a low-paying career or if your wage doesnt support your lifestyle.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whats the answer?
         </strong>
         Determine the cost of the lifestyle that you and your family aspire to then estimate which career will help you achieve this. Both short-term and long-term earning potential needs to be considered.
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Can I realistically dedicate sufficient time and effort?
        </h3>
        <p>
         If you are working 9-5 and plan to train for another career, you may run out of hours in the day.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whats the answer? Review training course details and determine the number of hours in a week that you will need to train. Factor in breaks, work and social events to make sure you can cope. Research whether course providers can offer you flexible training arrangements such as daytime, evening and weekend classes or Classroom, Live Online and Distance learning options.
        </p>
        <h3>
         6. Are there jobs in the Industry?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Research job sites to determine whether recruiters are looking for people in your desired career in your area before commencing with the course.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whats the answer?
         </strong>
         Talk to a recruitment professional to get an idea of how many jobs there are in your chosen industry. Its also a good idea to keep an eye on job sites.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="career change advice" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1913" height="406" src="http://blog.kaplan.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/career_change_advice_3.jpg" width="638"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         7. Will I get the support I require?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Retraining for a new career can be daunting. It is important that you have support from both the people in your life and from the organisation providing the training.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whats the answer? Research the level of support you can expect from training providers and I dont understand what seek out reviews means?. Trustworthy educators will work closely with the institutions, and you can often get insight into their style and quality of teaching by looking at their free materials.
        </p>
        <p>
         Kaplan Financial provide tools such as
         <a href="http://www.kaplan-exam-tips.com/?utm_source=blog&amp;utm_medium=bancarticle&amp;utm_campaign=careerchange" target="_blank" title="Kaplan Exam Tips">
          Kaplan Exam Tips
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://kfknowledgebank.kaplan.co.uk/?utm_source=blog&amp;utm_medium=bancarticle&amp;utm_campaign=careerchange" target="_blank" title="The Kaplan Knowledge Bank">
          The Kaplan Knowledge Bank
         </a>
         to help you with areas such as learning technique, core skills and revision.
        </p>
        <h3>
         8. Am I prepared to start from the bottom again?
        </h3>
        <p>
         This question is pertinent for professionals who have already worked their way up the ladder. Reputation takes time to build, and the prospect of having to start again can be off putting.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Whats the answer?
         </strong>
         This one can only come from you. Ask yourself whether you would be willing to do tasks of a junior level, if it means you have the long-term gain of a new career. Speak to Recruitment Consultants or even training providers about what would be involved.
        </p>
        <p>
         At Kaplan Financial we work closely with businesses to upskill their employees and can share our experience to help how understand which courses could help to propel you up the ladder.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Your ultimate goal
        </h3>
        <p>
         Its human nature to believe the grass will be greener in another role, thats why its so important to talk to experts and professionals in your chosen industry and specialism. The more professionals and questions you ask, the more realistic your expectation and the more successful your endeavours will be.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          If youre thinking of a change in career, why not check out our
          <a href="http://financial.kaplan.co.uk/?utm_source=blog&amp;utm_medium=bancarticle&amp;utm_campaign=careerchange" target="_blank" title="Kaplan Financial">
           full range of courses and qualifications
          </a>
          .
         </em>
        </p>
