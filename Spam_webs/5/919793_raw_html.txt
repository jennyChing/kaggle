       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Wine Wednesday: Riding the Napa Valley Wine Train
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/author/jdombrowski/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Jennifer Dombrowski
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/napa-valley-wine-train/#comments">
           27 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <h6>
         <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Napa Valley Wine Train" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5004" height="450" src="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-2.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         All aboard! I boarded the 1915-1917 Pullman Le Petite Gourmet dining car and took my seat at the picture window on the Napa Valley Wine Train. The Napa Valley Wine Train runs runs for 36 miles fromMcKinstry Street Station in Napa all the way to St. Helena through the heart of the world famous wine region.
        </h6>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5006" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-4.jpg">
          <img alt="Napa Valley Wine Train" class="size-full wp-image-5006" height="450" src="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-4.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Some of the scenery as you pass through the heart of Napa Valley
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         As the Napa Valley Wine Train chugged out of McKinstry Street Station, we traveled north through Napa Valley toward Rutherford and I enjoyed a three-course gourmet lunch along the way. Meals are prepared under the direction of Executive Chef Kelly MacDonald right on board in a specially crafted kitchen car. You can even watch the meal preparation through a glass wall! Lunch includes choice of soup or salad, a selection of seasonal entrees including a daily chef special, and dessert and coffee.
        </p>
        <p>
         Guests can ride the Napa Valley Wine Train for the full 36 miles round trip or can book at winery tour. I disembarked in Rutherford for a tour and wine tasting at Grgich Hills Estate. Grgich Hills founder and winemaker Miljenko Mike Grgich became famous after he crafted the 1973 Chateau Montelena Chardonnay that won the historic 1976 Paris Tasting. Not only was I excited to visit Grgich Hills Estate because I had just watched the movie
         <em>
          Bottle Shocked,
         </em>
         which is about Chateau Montelena and the 1976 tasting, but also because Mike Grgich hails from Croatia, where he also has a winery still today.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-9.jpg">
          <img alt="Grgich Hills Estate" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5011" height="600" src="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-9.jpg" width="450"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5010" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-8.jpg">
          <img alt="Grgich Hills Estate" class="size-full wp-image-5010" height="450" src="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-8.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Grgich Hills Estate
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Grgich Hills has 336 total acres across five vineyards throughout Napa Valley. Because the climate varies from vineyard to vineyard, different grapes are produced based on which is the best environment for them to grow. 17.3 acres of Cabernet and one acre of Petit Verdot surround the estate in Rutherford, the green vines practically sparkling in the sun.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-7.jpg">
          <img alt="Grgich Hills Estate" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5009" height="600" src="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-7.jpg" width="329"/>
         </a>
         The Grgich Hills Estate is most famous for its Chardonnay, which has been served at State Dinners by some of the worlds leaders such as Presidents Reagan and Clinton, Queen Elizabeth II, and French President Franois Mitterrand. While I wasnt so lucky as to taste the 1973 Chateau Montelena Chardonnay, I did taste a 2010 Chardonnay. It was crisp with flavors of tropical fruit and white peaches, perfect and refreshing on such a hot day as my visit.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5008" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-6.jpg">
          <img alt="Grgich Hills Estate" class="size-full wp-image-5008" height="450" src="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-6.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Wine tasting at Grgich Hills Estate
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Grgich Hills doesnt just produce the white wine they are so famous for. We tasted a 2009 Cabernet Sauvignon produced from a blend of grapes at the Rutherford and Calistoga vineyards. Full of rich scents like black cherry, black licorice, and a hint of roasted coffee, our guide recommended that this would be perfect with barbecue.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5007" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-5.jpg">
          <img alt="Grgich Hills Estate" class="size-full wp-image-5007" height="450" src="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-5.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Inside the Grgich Hills Estate
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The Chardonnay and Cabernet Sauvignon were my favorites and had I not been traveling carry-on luggage only, I would have been toting at least a bottle each with me all the way back home to Italy. I did get sent home, as did all the other guests, with my very own wine glass with the Grgich Hills name and logo etched into it.
         <em>
          The Chardonnay retails for $42 per bottle and the Cabernet Sauvignon retails for $60 per bottle.
         </em>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_5005" style="width: 610px">
         <a href="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-3.jpg">
          <img alt="Napa Valley Wine Train" class="size-full wp-image-5005" height="450" src="http://luxeadventuretraveler.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Jdombs-Travels-Napa-Valley-Wine-Train-3.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Lounge car
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         We heard the train horn in the distance and soon it was time to board the Napa Valley Wine Train again as it pulled up along the Grgich Hills Estate. Chocolate tarts were served in the lounge car with coffee as we chugged along back toward McKinstry Street Station in downtown Napa. Perhaps it was the wine, but after dessert I made my way back to the caboose and was more than happy to return a wave to passersby.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Know Before You Go
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="http://winetrain.com/winery-tours/grgich-hills">
           Grgich Hills Winery Tour
          </a>
          is available every lunch and costs $144 per person. Wine on board the train is not included.
         </li>
         <li>
          A welcome glass of wine prior to departure at the McKinstry Street Station is included and wine can be brought on board for a small corkage fee of $15 for 750ml bottle.
         </li>
         <li>
          On board the train, visit Napas most unique tasting bar which is usually stocked with 30-40 boutique Napa Valley wines. At the tasting bar, guests can sample four tastes of wine for $10.
         </li>
