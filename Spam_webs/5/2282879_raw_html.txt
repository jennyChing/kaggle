      <h2 class="entry-title">
       Missing package?  Package missing its contents?
      </h2>
      <div class="entry-meta">
       <span class="entry-date">
        February 8, 2008 - 12:52 am
       </span>
       <span class="metasep">
        |
       </span>
       <span class="entry-author">
        <a class="url" href="http://www.gotogreatpanes.com/blog/author/admin/" title="View all posts by GoTo">
         GoTo
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        If you have a package that
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         is missing en-route,
        </li>
        <li>
         has Delivery Confirmation that saysits delivered but the customer doesnt have it,
        </li>
        <li>
         you suspect was stolen,
        </li>
        <li>
         has been rifled through or
        </li>
        <li>
         has arrived without its contents
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        it may be time to contact the post office. Shipping times may vary for different times of the year as well as different regions throughout the US, but if its been three weeks and you didnt send it Media Mail, its probably time to lookforit.
       </p>
       <p>
        First, we ask the receiver to look around their porch, back door, garage, etc., and to check with the neighbors to see if the postal employee left it with them.  It might seem obvious, but often packages are left where you wouldnt expect to find them, and sometimes well-intentioned neighbors hold onto a package accidentally for a month.  (
        <i>
         Feel free to share the odd places youve found packages in the comments below!
        </i>
        )
       </p>
       <p>
        If you purchased Delivery Confirmation (
        <b>
         DC
        </b>
        ), insurance or paid for one of the higher end services that includes a tracking number, you should start by calling one of three places:
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         the post office you sent it from,
        </li>
        <li>
         the post office you sent it to or
        </li>
        <li>
         the USPS toll free number 1-800-ASK-USPS
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        They will call each other about the missing package to see if its in either of the post offices.  They can track the package using the DC number, even if youve already checked on-line, they are privy to more details that might help.
       </p>
       <p>
        We had a package that was missing, and when I called the destination post office and the toll free hotline number they both saw that the package had arrived at the destination post office though I couldnt see that online using the DC number.  The destination post office said that they didnt know what happened to it after that.  The toll free number folks said all they do is call the destination post office, and, well, I already knew what they had to say
       </p>
       <p>
        When I asked the hotline person what happens next, they said nothing, thats the end of the line.  They told me that unless I bought insurance there is nothing else that can be done, but I found out thats not true.
       </p>
       <p>
        I asked the hotline person if the info was gathered someplace, so if lots of packages go missing they get an idea of what location/route was having issues.  They said no, thats it.
       </p>
       <p>
        I just couldnt believe that this information was unimportant, so after a good deal of research that I found the
        <a href="http://postalinspectors.uspis.gov/" target="_blank">
         US Postal Inspectors website
        </a>
        .  I dont know why it was so hard to come across this information, nor why the USPS Help Hotline wouldnt have directed me there when there was nothing else they could do, but it was and they didnt.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Happily our package turned up
        </b>
        I think had I not been persistant with keeping the post office looking for it I would have had to re-send the shipment and take a business loss.
       </p>
       <p>
        <u>
         Even if you didnt purchase insurance or delivery confirmation
        </u>
        , you can report your missing package/missing contents to the U.S. Postal Inspection Service by filling out this form:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="https://postalinspectors.uspis.gov/forms/MLNtRcvd.aspx" target="_blank" title="Postal Form">
         https://postalinspectors.uspis.gov/forms/MLNtRcvd.aspx
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        If you prefer to mail it to them, you can print out the mail theft reportits a PDF file:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://postalinspectors.uspis.gov/radDocs/Mail_Theft_Complaints.pdf" target="_blank">
         http://postalinspectors.uspis.gov/radDocs/Mail_Theft_Complaints.pdf
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        You may not get your money or goods back, but you can help the U.S. Postal Inspection Service, and thereby everyone who uses the US Postal Service, by reporting any of the issues at the top of this post.  If they arent made aware of problems, they wont have a chance to fix them.
       </p>
       <p>
        You can contact them by phone also by calling 1-877-876-2455 from 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. in all time zones:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="https://www.usps.com/postalinspectors/tollfreeno.htm" target="_blank" title="Postal Inspector Contact">
         https://www.usps.com/postalinspectors/tollfreeno.htm
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        or check out their entire website for more info:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://postalinspectors.uspis.gov/" target="_blank">
         http://postalinspectors.uspis.gov/
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
