       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         Hello, MCS readers! Its Mary from
         <a href="https://justmommatters.wordpress.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://justmommatters.wordpress.com/', 'justmommatters']);" target="_blank" title="justmommatters">
          justmommatters
         </a>
         back this month to share a quick way to update any piece of furniture: add wrapping paper.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its a habit of mine to paper the bottom of any thrifted dresser or desk drawer once Ive refinished the piece. Although I clean each piece thoroughly inside and out, I feel like adding that thin layer of paper insures me against any old germs that may still be lingering. Plus, it covers up any markings or imperfections its previous owner left behind. Thats why Im surprised I hadnt tried adding paper to the sides of drawers or to the top of a table before now.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/easy-paper-update.jpg">
          <img alt="easy paper update" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4443" height="300" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/easy-paper-update.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
         <span id="more-4427">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Paper on furniture is really a simple way to add interest to a piece or an unexpected pop of color to a room. Its a creative way to breathe new life and new personality into everyday, ho-hum furniture. Give it a try!
        </p>
        <p>
         What youll need:
         <br/>
         * Wrapping paper (the thicker, the better)
         <br/>
         * Scissors
         <br/>
         * Paintbrush
         <br/>
         * Mod Podge
         <br/>
         * Disposable cup
         <br/>
         * Fine grit sandpaper
         <br/>
         * Rag
        </p>
        <p>
         Roll out the wrapping paper on your workspace and place your drawers on top to determine the amount of paper youll need. Use scissors to cut the paper, leaving an inch or so extra to hang over the sides of the drawer. No worries if the edges of your paper end up jagged, theyll be smoothed out later.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4435" style="width: 410px">
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01dea13ca5f5c3c700b1e888f0a9233293465ae0b1_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="Thick, thick paper is easiest to work with. Thin paper bubbles and tears and will drive you crazy." class="size-full wp-image-4435" height="533" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01dea13ca5f5c3c700b1e888f0a9233293465ae0b1_opt.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Choose thick, thick paper. Thin paper bubbles and tears and will drive you crazy.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Pour your Mod Podge or favorite adhesive sealer into a disposable cup large enough to fit your paintbrush.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/013770af997f2625326cfac619e5c744b78248c76e_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="013770af997f2625326cfac619e5c744b78248c76e_opt" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4441" height="533" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/013770af997f2625326cfac619e5c744b78248c76e_opt.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Dip your brush into the adhesive and apply an even coat to the entire surface youll be covering with paper. Repeat the process on the paper, but dont worry if you dont cover it completely. (I found it difficult to get glue to the edges without getting glue all over my fingers and then on the design-side of the paper, so I left those spots glue-free.)
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01d7007caa05ba4bc0f728c2b755fa99053083f475_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="01d7007caa05ba4bc0f728c2b755fa99053083f475_opt" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4434" height="533" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01d7007caa05ba4bc0f728c2b755fa99053083f475_opt.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Starting at one end, carefully place the paper on one end of the drawer side and press firmly. Smooth the paper all the way down the length of the side, paying attention to the alignment of the paper as you go. Smooth out any bubbles that appear as you notice them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Through trial and error, I found that super thick paper bubbles the least and is the most forgiving to work with all around. If your paper is thick enough, pull it up and reattach it, if needed. And after trying everything under the sun, I had the best luck using the pads of my fingers to smooth out the bubbles.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4436" style="width: 410px">
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/017c7facaa8b7f041b2600bd0eb1bfc0b5cf60f4a4_00001_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="Allow to dry for an hour or so." class="size-full wp-image-4436" height="300" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/017c7facaa8b7f041b2600bd0eb1bfc0b5cf60f4a4_00001_opt.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Allow to dry for an hour or so.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Fold the edges of the paper over the sides of the drawer. Using fine grit sandpaper (I used 220-grit) , lightly rub the edges of the drawer where the paper is folded.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/0116db63f36c5ce72899b0eb3bcd500755c62163b2_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="0116db63f36c5ce72899b0eb3bcd500755c62163b2_opt" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4437" height="300" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/0116db63f36c5ce72899b0eb3bcd500755c62163b2_opt.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/0169eb6ae445e48f9a5e2b924517cdfd7cf3bc85ac_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="0169eb6ae445e48f9a5e2b924517cdfd7cf3bc85ac_opt" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4439" height="533" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/0169eb6ae445e48f9a5e2b924517cdfd7cf3bc85ac_opt.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Pull the paper away from the drawer gently along the sanded side.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01623eb7988ee77c726f4d66c23bff054a8fb495d9_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="01623eb7988ee77c726f4d66c23bff054a8fb495d9_opt" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4440" height="300" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01623eb7988ee77c726f4d66c23bff054a8fb495d9_opt.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Use sandpaper to remove any areas where the paper is jagged or torn-looking. Wipe away all sanding dust with a damp rag.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/0164e5f65fbed9ff7b5f9a7dcb318face0aa068dcd_opt.jpg">
          <img alt="0164e5f65fbed9ff7b5f9a7dcb318face0aa068dcd_opt" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4438" height="533" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/0164e5f65fbed9ff7b5f9a7dcb318face0aa068dcd_opt.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Apply one or two coats of Mod Podge directly on top of the papered drawer to seal it, allowing each coat to dry 30 minutes before applying the next. Let dry a day or two before inserting into your piece of furniture.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01cfa079e845522384df027d7de7d395649aa6b81b_00001_opt1.jpg">
          <img alt="01cfa079e845522384df027d7de7d395649aa6b81b_00001_opt(1)" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4433" height="300" src="http://mycraftyspot.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/01cfa079e845522384df027d7de7d395649aa6b81b_00001_opt1.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Check out
         <a href="https://justmommatters.wordpress.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://justmommatters.wordpress.com/', 'justmommatters']);" target="_blank" title="justmommatters">
          justmommatters
         </a>
         for the full reveal of this antique side table. I added the same paper to the inset in the top of the table to replace the original worn-out leather. It turned out pretty cool!
        </p>
