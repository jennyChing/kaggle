       Christopher Nolan In Talks To Oversee Justice League, More Batman
      </h1>

     <div class="article_body article" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         <a href="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/markhughes/files/2013/02/Banner-6.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-5897" height="138" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/markhughes/files/2013/03/Banner-6-300x13853.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         The impossible may be happening, fans  we could be returning to Gotham City at the same bat-time, same bat-channel.
         <a href="http://latino-review.com/2013/03/03/exclusive-dcu-takeover-huge-developments-justice-league/" target="_blank">
          Latino Review is reporting
         </a>
         that director
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0634240/" target="_blank">
          Christopher Nolan
         </a>
         is in talks to come back for more. More Batman, that is. But in what manner? Not what youd expect  read on for details!
         <span id="more-5896">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Keep in mind, the reports are that negotiations and talks are still ongoing and nothing is set in stone yet. So, this news is all tentative at the moment, but
         <a href="http://latino-review.com/2013/03/03/exclusive-dcu-takeover-huge-developments-justice-league/" target="_blank">
          the reports from
         </a>
         <a>
          Latino Review
         </a>
         jibe with what Ive heard from some of my own sources. Batman-On-Film (
         <a href="http://www.batman-on-film.com/" target="_blank">
          BOF
         </a>
         )
         <a href="http://www.batman-on-film.com/batman-movies_news_Nolan-Snyder-Bale-onboard-JL-WF_3-4-13.html" target="_blank">
          has some additional insights in Bill Rameys report
         </a>
         as well.*
        </p>
        <p>
         The director would reportedly bring Batman back to the screen in the same overall world that existed in the previous three films and
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0000288/" target="_blank">
          Christian Bale
         </a>
         is reportedly expected to return as Bruce Wayne!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/markhughes/files/2013/02/ManOfSteel.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-5898" height="300" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/markhughes/files/2013/03/ManOfSteel-202x30053.jpg" width="202"/>
         </a>
         But if thats not enough of a shocking revelation for you, try this on for size: the new film would actually be a team-up film, putting Nolans Batman alongside
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0147147/" target="_blank">
          Henry Cavill
         </a>
         reprising his role as the new cinematic Superman (introduced in this summers upcoming
         <a href="http://manofsteel.warnerbros.com/index.html" target="_blank">
          <em>
           Man of Steel
          </em>
         </a>
         from Nolan,
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0811583/" target="_blank">
          Zack Snyder
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0333060/" target="_blank">
          David Goyer
         </a>
         ) and possibly/probably additional superheroes as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats right, dear readers, the Nolan Batman may be teaming up with the rebooted Superman, in a Justice League movie (or, possibly, a Superman-Batman team-up movie, but Justice League seems more likely at the moment).
        </p>
        <p>
         At this time, Nolan is reportedly in talks to be involved as producer, with Snyder as a producer and possible director, and it might involve a story by Goyer.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is among the biggest film news of the year, along with the announcement of
         <a href="http://disney.com/" target="_blank">
          Disney
         </a>
         buying
         <a href="http://lucasfilm.com/" target="_blank">
          LucasFilms
         </a>
         to develop new
         <a href="http://starwars.com/" target="_blank">
          <em>
           Star Wars
          </em>
         </a>
         movies. And it would involve one of the most powerful filmmaking teams, coming aboard what could potentially become the biggest superhero franchises in cinema.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/markhughes/files/2013/03/Justice-League.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-6131" height="190" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/markhughes/files/2013/03/Justice-League-300x190.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         The monetary value of this deal for everyone involved, and for the studio, would be hard to
         <a href="http://blogs.forbes.com/sites/fathom/">
          fathom
         </a>
         at this point. Consider what Marvels
         <a href="http://marvel.com/avengers_movie/" target="_blank">
          <em>
           The Avengers
          </em>
         </a>
         was capable of with a slate of lesser-known characters whose films never approached the worldwide $1 billion range of Nolans Batman or the name recognition (and likely upcoming $800 million or more global box office) of Superman. A Justice League film with this filmmaking team, staring these performers as these characters, and filmed and released in 3D, could have global box office potential of perhaps $2 billion or more.
        </p>
        <p>
         What other characters might be involved is not clear at this time. However, its important to note that Goyer has a Flash screenplay thats reportedly pretty excellent just sitting on a shelf at Warner. If Goyer is involved in the Justice League project, its reasonable to speculate that his Flash script could be dusted off and put back into the mix.
        </p>
        <p>
         Goyer is one of the most knowledgeable screenwriters when it comes to comic book adaptations, and hes clearly enthusiastic about finding the best ways to bring the characters to the big screen. His involvement, then, would be a real plus for any Justice League project, and could mean were more likely to get some top notch stories for adapting other
         <a href="http://www.dccomics.com/" target="_blank">
          DC Comics
         </a>
         characters.
        </p>
        <p>
         Many fans will find it hard to believe this could be happening. There will be some who cheer the reports and parade in the streets, there will be those who are furious and would want no part of it, and there will be those who are just stunned and await confirmation and explanation.
        </p>
        <p>
         Id advise everyone to stay calm and wait for more information, and to remember that this is a creative team who have earned a lot of trust so far, including when they took big risks and made bold choices of which many fans were initially skeptical (the Joker wearing makeup, for example).
         <!--donotpaginate-->
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/markhughes/files/2013/02/batimax.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-5899" height="300" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/markhughes/files/2013/03/batimax-202x30053.jpg" width="202"/>
         </a>
         Its true that Nolan previously focused on the idea of illusionary realism, and kept other superheroes out of the Batman cinematic universe. And when I first heard about all of this, I was stunned and couldnt imagine how it could possibly work. But if anyone can find a good story to tell and make sense of it, a good way to bring Bruce Wayne back after the ending of
         <em>
          The Dark Knight Rises
         </em>
         , and a good way to merge the illusionary realism of Batman with a world in which superpowered characters exist, then its this creative team who can do it.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, after my initial shock over these reports and my skepticism about Bruce Wayne returning, I thought about it some more and came to realize a few narrative reasons exist for Bruce coming out of retirement without undoing the themes and point of
         <em>
          The Dark Knight Rises
         </em>
          ending, all of which Ill explain more in my follow-up article later this week.
        </p>
        <p>
         Meanwhile, if the story is true and the reported negotiations bear fruit, then obviously I was utterly wrong about the future of the Batman franchise  when Nolan insisted he was done with Batman movies and DC superheroes, I was convinced he wouldnt return and that his Batman world had ended with
         <em>
          The Dark Knight Rises
         </em>
         . So if and when there is an official announcement, Ill apologize to those fans whom I insisted should stop believing the Nolanverse would or could be revisited on film.
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, we may have more Nolan Batman on the way, dear readers, alongside the new Superman. Ill have a lot more to say about this in the coming days and weeks  and possibly some new, additional information that hasnt been revealed or confirmed yet.
        </p>
        <p>
         For now, though, I know plenty of YOU out there have your own strong reactions to these reports, so lets hear it  sound off in the comments below!
        </p>
        <p>
         Hear me discussing the reports on
         <a href="http://www.modernmythmedia.com/2013/03/mmm-podcast-episode-110-batman-dream-team-returns/" target="_blank">
          this special edition Modern Myth Media podcast
         </a>
         with Sean Gerber and Josh Costella!
        </p>

         <div class="comment_content">
          <p>
           They did actually address how he survived the nuke. Remember that he claimed the Bat vehicle didnt have an autopilot, so he couldnt eject from it since he had to keep flying it to get the bomb away from Gotham? Well, afterward when everyone thinks Bruce is dead, they show Lucius Fox finding out that the autopilot had been repaired after all, by Bruce himself. The point being, he actually lied to everyone about not having an autopilot, so theyd think he died.
          </p>
          <p>
           The top speed of the Bat could hypothetically be similar to the top speed of an Osprey, which would mean the Bat could reach a top speed of nearly 320 mph. That would be nearly 5 and a half miles per minute. So, if he turned the autopilot on and ejected from the Bat 30 seconds before the bomb went off, he couldve been more than 2.5 miles from the blast. And, remember that the Tumblers ejection was a secondary vehicle, so maybe the Bats ejection seat is a capsule that would let him sink down into the water to be even safer from the blast. If the capsule had some basic means of propulsion, then he couldve hit the water and put even more distance between himself and the bomb blast.
          </p>
          <p>
           Since we know he repaired the autopilot and that it was obviously the key to his escape, its sensible to assume a secondary vehicle like the Tumbler. And since the Bat is inspired by the Osprey its reasonable to use that speed for our calculations. So, Bruce could easily have been 3 miles from the blast, under water in a vehicle, and thus his survival isnt that surprising at all. :)
          </p>
          <p>
           And remember, the bomb was a neutron bomb  designed to cause maximum environmental damage via radiation etc to kill off human populations but with a blast designed specifically to reduce (relatively) the damage to industry and infrastructure (the idea being a neutron bomb will cause maximum human casualties and allow the victor to then move into the existing now-empty cities and repair them easier). So that all likewise works in favor of Bruces survival, too.
          </p>
