        Kitchen Intuition: Travel food

       <p>
        Ive been traveling this past week, so instead of spending time in my kitchen, Ive been thinking about the strategies for eating well when staying in hotels while also finding inspiration to take home with me. This has not been a road trip (Ill likely be writing about that later this summer when I do some driving up and down the East Coast), but rather a visit to a couple of unfamiliar countries (
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungary">
         Hungary
        </a>
        and the
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czech_Republic">
         Czech Republic
        </a>
        ), so my mind has been especially focused on foreign cookery. Here are some guidelines Ive been following.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Eat the local cuisine.
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Traveling is, after all, a cultural experience, and one of the best ways to have a truly local adventure is by tasting the food that feeds the natives. Not only does eating local enhance your travel experience, but it also gives you something to take homeyou might find a new ingredient that youd like to find in stores after your return or a whole new cuisine you want to explore in your kitchen after you get back. For example, I picked up some hand-mixed Hungarian paprika at a market in Budapest after enjoying the local dishes. Of course, care must be taken when visiting a place where youre not sure if the food or water is safe for consumption. When I was in China a few years ago, I wanted to try the local street food, but to be safe, I stuck with fried foods, as the hot oil ensured that the food was very well-cooked.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Take a break from dietary restrictions ...
         <br/>
        </strong>
        ... if possible. I realize this is not an option for everyone, but if you can relax your usual rules about food (within reasonyou dont want to make yourself sick), youll have more options when eating local. I dont normally eat a lot of meat, but while in central Europe, Ive tried all sorts of foods that are not typically found in Chattanooga restaurants or marketsthings such as hare, venison, goose, boar and
        <a href="http://modernfarmer.com/2014/03/meet-mangalitsa-hairy-pig-thats-kobe-beef-pork/">
         Mangalitsa pork
        </a>
        . And as mentioned above, when I was in China, I lifted my usual prohibition on fried foods.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Dont eat near the hotel.
        </strong>
        <br/>
        And don't eat in the hotel, unless the restaurant is singled out as one to try. The restaurants surrounding hotels tend to be franchises and chains, or at least eateries that cater to a homogenized expectation for food. Theres always a McDonalds or KFC to be found at international travel destinations, but often, getting a little way off the beaten path will lead you to a more unique experience with local color and flavor. Additionally, distancing yourself from the hotels and tourist traps gets you away from not only inauthentic fare, but also from the price gouging that often follows tourists around.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Take advantage of appetizers on the menu.
        </strong>
        <br/>
        This is a great option for anyone who is reluctant to experiment with unfamiliar food. If you want to try something new but dont want to risk your entire meal on exotic fare, order an appetizer. That way, you can try a smaller portion of something new, and if it doesnt suit your palate, you can still fill up on the main course.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Use the hotel breakfast buffet.
        </strong>
        <br/>
        At many international hotels, there are a lot of options that arent typical breakfast fare for Americans (this is another good opportunity to sample new things!) but can serve to get you well-fueled for the day. Eat a good mix of protein and complex carbs for energy, and when available, take advantage of the fruits and vegetables on offer so that youll be fortified with nutrients. That leaves you a solid foundation so you can play with new flavors the rest of the day!
       </p>
