        Ive written about struggling with infertility for OurFamilyWorld for a few years now. In fact, when Olfa first brought me on as a writer, it was to be my specialty. I struggled with infertility for many years. Now Im sharing my personal story in the hopes that it helps someone else get through the struggle.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 590px">
        <img alt="Struggling with Infertility: My Personal Journey Through Grief" height="692" src="http://i1109.photobucket.com/albums/h430/OurFamilyWorld/Should%20I%20worry/StrugglingwithInfertility_zps01b17522.jpg" title="Struggling with Infertility: My Personal Journey Through Grief" width="580"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo source: Unsplash.com
        </p>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Let me tell you something, struggling with infertility is life-altering. When youre going through it, it becomes this all-consuming thing that takes over every aspect of your life. Even simple things like going to the grocery store become a matter of maybe I should get extra orange juice in case this is the month! Orange juice is full of folic acid and I want my baby to be smart! Every time you get a craving for a certain food, you think maybe the test was wrong! Why else would I be craving orange juice?
       </p>
       <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #ff0000;">
         My story of struggling with infertility
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        I started trying to conceive back in early 1999. With a family of fertile Myrtles I thought it would be a cake walk. Id try one month, deliver nine months later. Bam! Easy-peasy. I even quit drinking (I was a major social drinker and was tired of getting sick all the time from it anyway) and started taking prenatal vitamins. I was almost 24 at the time and pretty healthy.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Six months went by with no positive
        <a href="http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2013/07/29/five-of-the-best-pregnancy-tests/" title="Five of the Best Pregnancy Tests">
         pregnancy test
        </a>
        result. I saw a doctor and she did tests. Some of them hurt! She couldnt find a single reason for why I was struggling with infertility. When my ex left the Navy, I lost the coverage to get more tests. My basic cruddy health insurance didnt cover that sort of thing, so I started reading every single book and website on infertility that I could find. I learned a lot. I tried a lot. I failed a lot.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
         <img alt="Struggling with Infertility: My Personal Journey Through Grief" class="aligncenter" height="361" src="http://i1109.photobucket.com/albums/h430/OurFamilyWorld/Should%20I%20worry/Pregnancy%20and%20Fertility/StrugglingwithInfertilityQuote_zps3b1fac7e.jpg" title="Struggling with Infertility: My Personal Journey Through Grief" width="580"/>
         <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2014/03/03/struggling-with-infertility-personal-story/&amp;media=http://i1109.photobucket.com/albums/h430/OurFamilyWorld/Should%20I%20worry/Pregnancy%20and%20Fertility/StrugglingwithInfertilityQuote_zps3b1fac7e.jpg&amp;description=Struggling with Infertility: My Personal Journey Through Grief | http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2014/03/03/struggling-with-infertility-personal-story/')">
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        During those six years, I become depressed to the point of contemplating suicide. I dealt with clinical depression as a teenager and it was nothing compared to the despair of an empty womb month after month. Imaging going through the
        <a href="http://psychcentral.com/lib/the-5-stages-of-loss-and-grief/000617">
         five stages of grief
        </a>
        <i>
         every single month.
        </i>
        See, you start every month with hope, assuming that it just HAS to be the time for you. Then when you start to feel those twinges telling you that your period is on the way, you go into denial. You start thinking maybe those cramps are implantation pains. The blood is just spotting.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Next comes the anger. Why cant you have a baby? Why is the universe, god or whatever you believe in doing this to you? Why is the girl down the street
        <a href="http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2011/09/19/16-and-pregnant-what-are-my-options/" title="16 and Pregnant: What Are My Options?">
         16 and pregnant
        </a>
        while youre nearing your 30s and still with empty arms? Why? Why? Why?
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Then comes the bargaining. I begged the gods to let me have a baby. Just one. Id be happy with just one. I didnt need a big family. Just give me one little baby, one child to love, one being to raise. One. I swore Id be happy. Id never ask for anything again. Just one baby. Please?
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Bargaining soon fades into depression, and let me tell you, that depression is deep. I was ready to just end it all at time. To those who dont understand what struggling with infertility is like, that may sound downright crazy. I would sit there looking at my friends babys pictures and cry. Just cry and cry and cry.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Finally, you reach acceptance. You realize that the month has come and gone once again. You get your hopes up for the next month. You take different vitamins and herbs. You try different positions. You try, try, try. Then you go through the whole cycle again. Every month. Until you want to rip your own heart out just to stop feeling pain.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        After six years of trying, I got my hands on some Clomid. I tried it for one month and conceived my son. Why no one gave it to me before, I dont know. They thought I didnt need it because I was ovulating just fine. For whatever reason, they were wrong. At 7 months, I developed
        <a href="http://www.ourfamilyworld.com/2011/10/26/preeclampsia-the-risk-factors-and-warning-signs/" title="Preeclampsia: The Risk Factors and Warning Signs">
         preeclampsia
        </a>
        and delivered a preemie with a broken arm (he was full breech, the cord was wrapped around his arm, they snapped it getting him out).
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        In the 8 years since my son was born, I never tried to have another baby. I also never tried to stop it from happening. I never got pregnant again. I kept my word to the universe. I never asked for another.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        My story ended with a child. Too many women dont get that happy ending. They keep struggling with infertility until their bodies stop giving them chances to get pregnant. For them, I hope. While theyre grieving, I am continuing to hope. Every woman struggling with infertility needs someone to keep hope alive.
       </p>
