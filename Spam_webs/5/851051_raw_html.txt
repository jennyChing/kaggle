       <h1 class="cb-entry-title cb-single-title" itemprop="headline">
        Runners Guide to Staying Hydrated: Knowing What to Drink
       </h1>
       <p class="cb-byline vcard">
        By
        <span class="author" itemprop="author">
         <a href="http://runstreet.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="By Marnie">
          <span class="fn">
           Marnie

      <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
       <div class="cb-featured-image">
        <img alt="Fruit shake in a glass" class="attachment-cb-thumb-600-crop wp-post-image" height="350" src="http://runstreet.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/318304_242805849089624_198572036846339_641990_7165330_n.jpg" width="318"/>

       <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="5396459" data-link="http://runstreet.com/nutrition/runners-guide-hydration-drinking-water/" data-summary="" data-title="Runners Guide to Staying Hydrated: Knowing What to Drink">
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_910" style="width: 282px">
        <a href="http://runstreet.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/318304_242805849089624_198572036846339_641990_7165330_n.jpg">
         <img alt="Fruit shake in a glass" class="size-medium wp-image-910" height="300" src="http://runstreet.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/318304_242805849089624_198572036846339_641990_7165330_n-272x300.jpg" title="fruit-shake-drink-glass" width="272"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Smoothies are a great way to replenish the nutrients you lose during tough workouts.
        </p>
       </div>
       Knowing what to drink to stay hydrated and when to drink it can be confusing, with every runner and article offering different advice. The truth is that what you should drink and when you should drink it depends on the runner. What you need to stay hydrated is influenced by the distance youre running, your individual taste and preferences and your running intensity. Heres a rundown of the common drinks for runners and when to grab a gulp of them:
       <p>
        <b>
         Water
        </b>
        <br/>
        Water is calorie-free and easy to digest, making it a great staple drink for runners. If youre doing a run of 30 minutes or less, water will be enough to keep you hydrated. Water is also good to drink throughout the day to keep your body fueled for workouts at any time. If you dont like regular water, try flavored water or vitamin water.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Sports Drinks
        </b>
        <br/>
        Sports drinks offer carbohydrates, which deliver energy to your body quickly during workouts and replace carbs you burn running. For short runs under 30 minutes, your body will have enough carbs stored for energy. For longer runs of more than 30 minutes, gulp down some sports drinks such as Gatorade or Powerade during and after the workout. Sports drinks also help replenish electrolytes that you lose through sweating.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-909">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Endurance Sports Drinks
        </b>
        <br/>
        Endurance sports drinks usually offer the same amount of carbs as regular sports drinks but add extra electrolytes in the form of potassium and sodium. The added electrolytes can prevent you from cramping during long endurance runs and also replenish your electrolytes if youre a heavy sweater. Reach for an endurance sports drink on runs that are more than an hour, such as half marathons or marathons or long training runs.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Energy Drinks
        </b>
        <br/>
        Energy drinks come in regular formulas  such as Red Bull or Monster  and in sports formulas, such as Gatorade Energy Drink. Most energy drinks contain caffeine and are high in sugar, which gives you a quick energy boost. The problem with high sugar levels is that all the sugar can slow your bodys absorption of fluids. You also may experience a crash soon after your sugar high, as your body burns sugar quickly. Stimulants such as guarana, ginseng, taurine, and L-carnitine are also common in energy drinks, and they can give you a quick shot of energy but also increase your heart rate and blood pressure and make you feel jittery. Because of the side effects, regular energy drinks are not good for running. Sports energy drinks usually have less sugar and offer carbs that can be beneficial during your workout. If youre going to try an energy drink, use a sports energy drink before or after a workout, and make sure you are well-hydrated and do not have a history of heart problems.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Recovery Drinks
        </b>
        <br/>
        Recovery drinks are designed to help replenish the nutrients your body uses up while working out. Recovery drinks usually contain a mix of carbs and protein to help replace your glycogen stores and promote muscle recovery. Recovery drinks are good for refueling after workouts, especially long or intense runs. If youre trying to lose weight or just doing short runs of 30 minutes or less, you wont need recovery drinks. If youre doing ultra marathons, on the other hand, you may want to try recovery drinks during your workouts to help your body stay fueled.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Smoothies
        </b>
        <br/>
        Smoothies are a great way to refuel after long runs or intense workouts. You can create your own healthy concoction by using ingredients with carbs and protein, such as peanut butter, bananas and yogurt or mixing milk, strawberries, bananas and protein powder. You can also buy ready-made smoothies or protein for smoothies at health food stores or your local grocery store. Try gulping down a smoothie within a half hour of long runs that last an hour or more.
       </p>
       <p>
        *Note: Take your taste and digestion into consideration when trying new drinks. If you have a sensitive stomach, stay away from energy drinks and flavors of sports drinks that upset your stomach. If, on the other hand, you have difficulty drinking water because it tastes too plain, try flavored water or sports drinks to help you stay hydrated.
       </p>
