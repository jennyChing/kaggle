             <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:justify;display:block;">
              <span style="text-decoration:none; font-style:normal; font-weight:400; color:rgb(208, 227, 230); ">
               <span style="text-decoration:none; font-style:normal; font-weight:400; color:rgb(208, 227, 230); ">
                <span style="text-decoration:none; font-style:normal; font-weight:400; color:rgb(208, 227, 230); ">
                 <a href="http://www.lichtfeld.ch" target="_blank">
                  Licht Feld
                 </a>
                 (Basel) showed pieces by
                 <font color="#8273da">
                  <strong>
                   Peter Dauphin Gennant Muth
                  </strong>
                 </font>
                 ,
                 <font color="#e0915c">
                  <strong>
                   Hubert Kretzschmar
                  </strong>
                 </font>
                 and
                 <font color="#a9e976">
                  <strong>
                   Marck
                  </strong>
                 </font>
                 at Context Miami 2014.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Marck is one of the first video artists to make video art that functions in the same way as a painting or hanging sculpture. There is something contained in the
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 You can see more by Marck
                 <a href="http://www.lichtfeld.ch/artists/marck/" target="_blank">
                  HERE
                 </a>
                 .
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Muth's work is technical and detailed--he often spends extensive time in a city learning the ins and outs of the municipality's character. He sometimes uses tiny stencils in his work as well. They are remarkable expressive works that convey that essence of architecture with, for lack of a better term, humanity.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Kretzschmar's piece below is something fans of 80s electronic music will, uniformly, want hanging in their living rooms. The artist is, by training, a graphic artist and illustrator. He was an early proponent of street art as fine art and also designed numerous iconic LP covers for the likes of Iggy Pop, Rolling Stones, Led Zeppelin and, of course, Kraftwerk (a variation of that cover is shown here).
