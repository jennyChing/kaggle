         The #1 Sleep Mistake Youre Probably Making Every Night
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="byline small">
        <meta content="2014-01-29" itemprop="datePublished"/>
        <meta content="2014-01-29" itemprop="dateModified"/>

       <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
       <p align="center">
        <em>
         Plus  win a pillow from
        </em>
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/sleepinnovations">
         <em>
          Sleep Innovations
         </em>
        </a>
        <em>
         .
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8203.jpg">
         <img alt="IMG_8203" border="0" height="405" src="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8203_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="IMG_8203" width="594"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        It snowed yesterday! Henry was SO excited to see snow, which isnt super common in Charlotte. It snowed last year, but he was far too little to appreciate it. This year, I had to
        <em>
         force
        </em>
        him to come back inside because I knew his hands mustve been hurting so much, despite the mittens. He spent fifteen minutes reaching desperately for the the door knob, insisting, Henry outside.
        <em>
         Snowing
        </em>
        ! Henry outside.
        <em>
         Snowing
        </em>
        !
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8174.jpg">
         <img alt="IMG_8174" border="0" height="604" src="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8174_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="IMG_8174" width="594"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        I finally figured out what hed be happy playing inside if I scooped a bit of snow into a Tupperware for him to inspect! And thanks to all that snow activity, Henry slept well last night. But this morning, he was back at it
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8177.jpg">
         <img alt="IMG_8177" border="0" height="446" src="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8177_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="IMG_8177" width="594"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        I slept well last night, too, but but it wasnt from romping in the snow. Ive declared 2014
        <strong>
         The Year That I Get Better Sleep
        </strong>
        and have been implementing and testing out all these sleep tricks and tips.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        My sleep is not horrible, its just not satisfying or long enough. Said every adult ever, right? Hah. But you know what I mean  I go to bed exhausted, I wake up groggy, and I usually get hit hard by the 3 PM sleepies. This is still a huge improvement over my sleep about four or five years ago, when I would always wake up with muscle aches and pains. We got a memory foam mattress topper (which I lovingly refer to as The Cloud because its so soft), and now, I dont have sore shoulders or stiff hips when I wake up.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        So, upgrading our mattress to memory foam was a big one, but like I said, there are a few other tricks that Im implementing to help with sleep.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        In bed by 10:30 MAX. I aim for 10:00, but thats challenging.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        Ive been consistently using an eye mask to block out any extra light.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        I go to bed and wake up at the same times, regardless of whether its the week or weekend (I hate this rule, but it definitely helps).
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        I use a humidifier.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        I rub sleep-inducing essential oils onto my wrists and spray the scent onto my pillow.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Ive gotten *really* into essential oils after writing a freelance article on the chemical and biological powers of aromatherapy. I interviewed an expert in molecular aromatherapy named Dr. Christian Moretti; he believes that smells possess an incredible power. The chemical properties of certain scents send signals to your brain, influencing behavior and mood.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        <img alt="photo (4)" src="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/photo4_thumb.jpg" style="display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto"/>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        According to Dr. Moretti, the right aroma can help you to think more clearly, eat slower, or even fall into a deep, satisfying slumber. He recommended scents like mandarin oil for bedtime, as the deeply rich scent assists in calming nerves and promotion relaxation. Jasmine is also a popular sleep smell. Personally,
        <strong>
         <font color="#9c85c0">
          lavender
         </font>
        </strong>
        is my favorite. (I wrote a little more about using essential oils in
        <a href="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/2014/01/bath-time-for-adults.html">
         this Bath Time post
        </a>
        .)
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        So  the comfy memory foam mattress and pillows, eye mask, and essentials oils have certainly HELPED things. If anything, they made me feel more relaxed when I get in bed, which chases away the days anxieties so I end up falling asleep faster. But Ive still got that groggy feeling more often than not.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Tom Rath is the New York Times bestselling author of the book 
        <em>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Eat-Move-Sleep-Choices-Changes/dp/1939714001">
          Eat, Move, Sleep: How Small Choices Lead to Big Changes
         </a>
        </em>
        . I shot Tom an e-mail interview and was so pleasantly surprised when he took the time to write back. I asked Tom, Why do I always feel groggy? Im getting enough sleep, right? And maybe if Im not getting enough-enough, shouldnt I just be able to power through during the day?
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        <a href="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/71x5DvMAZYL._SL1500_.jpg">
         <img alt="71x5DvMAZYL._SL1500_" border="0" height="692" src="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/71x5DvMAZYL._SL1500__thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; margin-left: auto; border-left-width: 0px; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px" title="71x5DvMAZYL._SL1500_" width="454"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        The short answer: No.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        One of the most common mistakes that adults make is believing that
        <strong>
         truly tough and successful people just dont need as much sleep
        </strong>
        . You hear all the time about Mrs. Famous CEO only sleep four hours a night, and how Mr. Powerful Politician goes to bed at midnight and wakes up at 5:00 AM. Getting by on less sleep in order to spend more time doing more stuff isnt tough  its actually ineffective. The problem is that one less hour of sleep is not equal to an extra hour of achievement. In many cases, the opposite occurs, wrote Tom
        <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2013/12/19/how-to-improve-and-increase-your-sleep/">
         in this WSJ article
        </a>
        about sleep.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        <a href="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Untitled1.jpg">
         <img alt="Untitled" border="0" height="805" src="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Untitled_thumb1.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Untitled" width="590"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Quality sleep means that you can be a better worker, parent, and athlete. Without decent sleep, youre fighting an uphill battle in quicksand. Your thoughts are slower, your logic is faulty, and simple tasks like math may confound you. You cant afford to show up to work on four or five hours of sleep, Tom told me. The research that I have read indicates that this may be as bad as showing up for work drunk. Tom pointed to
        <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/health/2012/05/31/study-sleepy-drivers-equally-as-dangerous-as-drunken-drivers/">
         this study
        </a>
        , which found that a loss of four hours of sleep is roughly equal to drinking six beers!
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        Fun Facts:
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        The average American gets 6 hours and 51 minutes of sleep.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        Research shows that top performers sleep an average of 8 hours and 36 minutes each night.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        Lack of sleep costs the American economy $63 billion a year in lost productivity alone.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        People suffering from sleep deprivation are more likely to make poor dietary choices and skip workouts.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        Studies show that regular runners fall asleep faster and sleep more deeply than people who dont exercise.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        (
        <a href="http://www.runnersworld.com/race-training/owners-manual-sleep-your-way-pr?page=single">
         Source
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2013/12/19/how-to-improve-and-increase-your-sleep/">
         Source
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://runningresearchnews.com/News_And_Events.php?cid=1&amp;iid=157">
         Source
        </a>
        )
       </p>
       <p align="center">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Tom says that most of us should aim to get between seven to nine hours of sleep a night. While nine hours may be challenging, Tom points out that even an extra 15 minutes could make a big difference in the way you feel. If youre currently training for a big event, like a marathon, sleep is even more important, as you need increased time asleep to help recover and repair your body.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Okay  so whats the #1 sleep mistake that youre probably making every single night?
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-52343">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        <a href="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8207.jpg">
         <img alt="IMG_8207" border="0" height="432" src="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8207_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="IMG_8207" width="594"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        "The hour before bed, it is very important that you eliminate sources of bright light, said Tom. More than 90% of Americans admit to using electronic devices like iPhones in the hour before bed. The problem is that the
        <strong>
         light emitted from these devices interferes with your production of melatonin
        </strong>
        , suppressing your production of this important sleep-triggering hormone by as much as 20% (
        <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2013/12/19/how-to-improve-and-increase-your-sleep/">
         Source
        </a>
        ). If youre going to use a device like a Kindle, Tom suggests setting the backlight as low as possible. Reading from an actual book with a small lamp is better than sitting in bed with the full overhead light on.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Tom points out that its not just the light emitted from your iPhone thats the issue. If youre checking work emails at 11 PM, your mind is going to be in work mode, not sleep mode. Things can probably wait until the next day instead of interrupting your final hour of thought, says Tom.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        I am working REALLY hard to implement this tip. I love to browse my phone before bed, but if I think about, sometimes the light gives me a headache, and I stay up much later than I wouldve otherwise. Putting down the phone is a small change that can make a big difference.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        <a href="http://www.healthytippingpoint.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_8198.jpg">
