            <figcaption>
             <div class="title" itemprop="headline">
              Productivity hits the road
             </div>
             <div class="body" itemprop="caption">
              <p>
               The PC simply isn't the end-all and be-all of the computing world anymore. Instead, it has become just one of many screens vying for your attention, as more and more of our tasks shift to tablets and smartphones.
              </p>
              <p>
               But getting things done on disparate devices doesn't mean you have to abandon a cohesive workflow. These 13 Android apps sync, link, connect, or just plain play
               <em>
                nice
               </em>
               with your computer in ways that make it easier to stay productive no matter where you areand with minimal hassle. Check 'em out.
              </p>
             </div>
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
          </div>

          <div class="excerpt" id="slide2Excerpt" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/MediaObject">
           <figure class="slide" itemprop="image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
            <div class="img-wrapper">
             <img alt="" class="lazyslide" data-original="http://images.techhive.com/images/article/2013/11/astro-slide-100068803-gallery.png"/>

             </noscript>
            </div>
            <figcaption>
             <div class="title" itemprop="headline">
              Astro File Manager
             </div>
             <div class="body" itemprop="caption">
              <p>
               Staying productive on your mobile device often involves mucking around in the Android file system, placing and plucking files from here, there, and
everywhere. That file system is hidden from users by default. Astro lets you dive in. 'Nuff said.
              </p>
              <p>
               Actually, not quite. Astro also has killer search capabilities that can find your files no matter where they're hidden on your phone, and "Cloud Hopping"
technology that lets you seamlessly move files between multiple cloud storage services
               <em>
                without
               </em>
               downloading them to your phone first.
              </p>
              <p>
               Astro File Manager:
               <em>
                <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.metago.astro" target="_blank">
                 Free
                </a>
               </em>
              </p>
             </div>
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
          </div>

            <div class="img-wrapper">

            <figcaption>
             <div class="title" itemprop="headline">
              AirDroid 2
             </div>
             <div class="body" itemprop="caption">
              <p>
               The awesome AirDroid 2 app lets you wirelessly move files between your 'Droid and your PC, even if they're nowhere near each another. But the handy-dandy features don't end there: Once you have AirDroid up and running, you can sling text messages, pinpoint your phone's location, and even manage app installations from within the sanctity of your Web browser. Or you can push URLs from your PC to your Android, or copy clipboard text from your phone or tablet to your PC.
              </p>
              <p>
               Seriously: AirDroid is one mighty useful app, and more featuresincluding remote phone usage and camera controlsare unlocked if you opt for a $45 per year
               <a href="http://www.airdroid.com/pay/premium/" target="_blank">
                premium subscription
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               AirDroid:
               <em>
                <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.sand.airdroid" target="_blank">
                 Free
                </a>
               </em>
              </p>
             </div>
            </figcaption>
           </figure>

            <figcaption>
             <div class="title" itemprop="headline">
              Folder Sync
             </div>
             <div class="body" itemprop="caption">
              <p>
               If AirDroid sounds intriguing only because of its wireless file management, check out Folder Sync, yet another incredible app.
              </p>
              <p>
               Folder Sync relies on customizable sync rules designed to place your phone or tablet's folders in the cloud service (or personal server) of your
choiceSkyDrive, Dropbox, SugarSync, Box, and Ubuntu One are just a few of the many options supported. Want to sync the folders only when changes are made on your phone, or when they're updated in the cloud, too? Which folders? And when: on a schedule or any time changes are made? This app does it all, and it makes shifting files from your phone to your PC and back again utterly seamless.
              </p>
              <p>
               Folder Sync:
               <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=dk.tacit.android.foldersync.full" target="_blank">
                <em>
                 $2.95
                </em>
               </a>
              </p>
             </div>
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
          </div>

          <div class="excerpt" id="slide5Excerpt" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/MediaObject">
           <figure class="slide" itemprop="image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
            <div class="img-wrapper">

            <figcaption>
             <div class="title" itemprop="headline">
              EasyTether
             </div>
             <div class="body" itemprop="caption">
              <p>
               Sometimes, your phone's most useful productivity feature is its mobile Internet connection, which can be a lifesaver when you need to break out your laptop on the road. Some wireless subscription plans include wireless hotspot capabilities baked in, but if yours doesn't, give EasyTether a whirl.
              </p>
              <p>
               EasyTether's a rare breed: It lets you tether your laptop to your mobile device's broadband via USB
               <em>
                without
               </em>
               the need to root your phone. You'll need to install both the app on your phone and an EasyTether program on your PC, so you'll need to set it up
               <em>
                before
               </em>
               you're in dire need of the mobile Web. The free version is great by itself, but it doesn't allow you to browse sites with HTTPS encryption. It's worth paying the one-time, $10 fee for the full version to remove that obstacle.
              </p>
              <p>
               EasyTether:
               <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.mstream.easytether_beta" target="_blank">
                <em>
                 Free
                </em>
               </a>
               or
               <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.mstream.easytether_polyclef" target="_blank">
                <em>
                 $10
                </em>
               </a>
              </p>
             </div>
            </figcaption>
           </figure>

             <div class="title" itemprop="headline">
              Lookout Antivirus and Security
             </div>
             <div class="body" itemprop="caption">
              <p>
               Before you go plugging your phone into your PC, you'll want to make sure your device isn't host to a swarm of malwaresome of which is actually
               <a href="http://www.pcworld.com/article/2027488/the-10-weirdest-wildest-most-shocking-security-exploits-ever.html" target="_blank">
                designed to use Android as a sort of Trojan Horse
               </a>
               , carrying very bad things into your computer.
              </p>
              <p>
               Don't sweat the overhyped Android
               <a href="http://www.techhive.com/article/2038836/mobile-security-reality-check-what-you-really-need-for-protecting-your-phone.html" target="_blank">
                malware threat
               </a>
               too much, though. Just slap the free, lightweight
               <a href="http://www.techhive.com/article/2047886/lookout-antivirus-and-security-review-basic-protection-for-your-android-phone.html" target="_blank">
                Lookout Antivirus and Security app
               </a>
               on your phone and/or tablet and never look back.
              </p>
              <p>
               Lookout Antivirus and Security:
               <em>
                <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.lookout&amp;hl=en" target="_blank">
                 Free
                </a>
               </em>
              </p>
             </div>
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
          </div>

          <div class="excerpt" id="slide7Excerpt" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/MediaObject">
           <figure class="slide" itemprop="image" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
            <div class="img-wrapper">
             <img alt="" class="lazyslide" data-original="http://images.techhive.com/images/article/2013/11/officesuite-pro-slide-100068808-gallery.png"/>
             <noscript>
              <img alt="" itemprop="image" src="http://images.techhive.com/images/article/2013/11/officesuite-pro-slide-100068808-gallery.png"/>
             </noscript>
            </div>
            <figcaption>
             <div class="title" itemprop="headline">
              OfficeSuite Pro
             </div>
             <div class="body" itemprop="caption">
              <p>
               But what if you actually need to bang out some work on your Android? Productivity suites are a dime a dozen in the Play store, but many lack the crucial
ability to save your work to the cloud, orin the case of
               <a href="http://www.techhive.com/article/2049047/google-makes-quickoffice-free-throws-in-an-extra-10gb-of-storage.html" target="_blank">
                Google's free Quickoffice
               </a>
               and Microsoft's premium
               <a href="http://www.pcworld.com/article/2045576/microsoft-releases-office-mobile-for-android-phones-tablets-need-not-apply-.html" target="_blank">
                Office Mobile for Android phones
               </a>
               only support limited cloud options.
              </p>
              <p>
               OfficeSuite Pro, on the other hand, plays nice with Google Drive, Box, Dropbox, SkyDrive,
               <em>
                and
               </em>
               SugarSync. Cloud-saved files are files that are easily accessed on your PC! The app also plays nice with Microsoft Office's myriad document, spreadsheet, and presentation formats, as well as PDF files.
              </p>
              <p>
               Sure, OfficeSuite costs a bit more than free alternatives like QuickOffice and
               <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=cn.wps.moffice_eng" target="_blank">
                Kingsoft Office
               </a>
               , but the price is worth it: Besides the widespread cloud and file format support, OfficeSuite is hands-down the slickest, fullest-featured mobile office suite around.
              </p>
              <p>
