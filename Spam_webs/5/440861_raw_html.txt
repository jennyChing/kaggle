Casual Connects' Indie Prize competition has been one of the premiere events for indie developers since 2013, with multiple, continent-specific competitions running every year for independently-produced mobile and PC titles  both those that are finished, and others still in development. Past winners have included games like Blast-a-Way, Devil's Attorney, Dark Deception, and Party Hard - the Best in Show Critic's Choice winner at the most recent award show in Amsterdam, earlier this year. Highlighting both free-to-play and paid games, the Indie Prize showcase is an opportunity not only for some friendly indie competition, but a platform for small companies to get their games out on a big stage, with sponsors including Google Cloud, Unity, Amazon, Intel, Gamblit, and PocketFullofApps  and judges from Facebook, Unity, PlayStation Lifestyle, and Ubisoft, among many others. Currently, Casual Connects is accepting applications for its upcoming Indie Prize Asia showcase in Singapore this May (as well as this summer's USA showcase in August)  so for indies looking to show their game to industry professionals, receive constructive criticism and advice from proven developers, and possibly win a prize or two, head over to Casual Connect's application page and apply before April 15th to be considered in the competition." name="twitter:description"/>

     <h1 class="title" id="page-title">
      Indie Prize Asia gears up for 2015 Competition
     </h1>
     <div about="/content/indie-prize-asia-gears-2015-competition" class="ds-2col-stacked-fluid node node-news view-mode-full clearfix" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">
      <div class="group-header">
       <div class="ds_author">
        Randy Dankievitch
       </div>
       <div class="ds_date">
        04/07/2015
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="group-footer">
       <div class="ds_paragraph">
        <div class="ds_cover">
         <img alt="Indie Prize Asia gears up for 2015 Competition" src="http://www.theappzine.com/sites/default/files/styles/cover/public/xnews_indieprize.jpg,qitok=k6Wd3NtT.pagespeed.ic.XrQRQayUcm.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         The last decade has seen a rebirth of sorts for the indie game, those small-scale titles pieced together as a labor of love for extremely small production teams. They've found homes on new platforms like the App Store and Steam, have been courted by giants like Sony and Microsoft to help kick off the next generation of console gaming  and most importantly, thanks to tools like Kickstarter and indie showcase events, have been able to reach out to the gaming world to help bring their projects to life (and more importantly, to the attention of gamers around the world).
        </p>
        <p>
         Casual Connects' Indie Prize competition has been one of the premiere events for indie developers since 2013, with multiple, continent-specific competitions running every year for independently-produced mobile and PC titles  both those that are finished, and others still in development. Past winners have included games like
         <em>
          Blast-a-Way, Devil's Attorney, Dark Deception,
         </em>
         and
         <em>
          Party Hard -
         </em>
         the Best in Show Critic's Choice winner at the most recent award show in Amsterdam, earlier this year. Highlighting both free-to-play and paid games, the Indie Prize showcase is an opportunity not only for some friendly indie competition, but a platform for small companies to get their games out on a big stage, with sponsors including Google Cloud, Unity, Amazon, Intel, Gamblit, and PocketFullofApps  and judges from Facebook, Unity, PlayStation Lifestyle, and Ubisoft, among many others.
        </p>
        <p>
         Currently, Casual Connects is accepting applications for its upcoming Indie Prize Asia showcase in Singapore this May (as well as this summer's USA showcase in August)  so for indies looking to show their game to industry professionals, receive constructive criticism and advice from proven developers, and possibly win a prize or two, head over to
         <a href="http://asia.indieprize.org/#apply">
          Casual Connect's application page
         </a>
         and apply before April 15
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         to be considered in the competition.
        </p>
