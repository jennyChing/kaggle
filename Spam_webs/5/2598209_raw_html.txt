         Black Chador

        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Black Chador is a full-body-length semicircle of fabric that is open down the front. Beginning in 1980, a year after the revolution, the new government began to enforce women covering their hair. Nowadays only religious or traditional women wear black Chador in outdoor, the rest mostly wear headscarves so far back on their heads it barely covers it, and often combined with makeup
