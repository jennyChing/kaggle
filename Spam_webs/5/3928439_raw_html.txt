             <span style="max-width:270px;margin-top:5px;float:left;clear: both;">
              <div>
               <a class="make_big" href="javascript:void(0);" onclick='loadPopupImage("http://i.space.com/images/i/000/046/028/original/spacemobile-vintage-retro-advertisement.jpg?1425331857",this,0);' rel="#custom0">

               </a>
              </div>
             </span>
            </td>
           </tr>
          </table>
         </span>
         <p>
          It's a blast from the space-age past: A poster from 1965, featuring a space explorer traversing a distant world, was unearthed from the National Archives and posted online on Feb. 26.
         </p>
         <p>
          The awesomely retro print is dated Feb. 26, 1965, and it captures the aesthetic of
          <a href="http://www.space.com/27729-excerpt-from-art-of-space-by-ron-miller.html">
           space-based art
          </a>
          at the time  it looks as if it could be the cover of a 1960's science fiction novel.
         </p>
         <p>
          The poster is advertising NASA's traveling space-education program "the Spacemobile."
          <a href="http://www.collectspace.com/ubb/Forum3/HTML/000905.html">
           These mobile outreach units
          </a>
          would travel to schools and events to tell students and members of the public about space sciences and the work being done by NASA.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="about_adsense" id="nointelliTXT">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           tmntag.adTag('ad_sense1');
          </script>
         </div>
         <p>
          The Spacemobile program was active in the U.S. in the 1960s  the decade when America was embroiled in a race with Russia to send people into space and to the moon. In 1965, Edward H. White II became the first American to perform a spacewalk. And in 1969, Apollo 11 became the first lunar landing mission to put humans (Neil A. Armstrong, Edwin E. Aldrin and Michael Collins) on the surface of the moon.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://todaysdocument.tumblr.com/post/112133146387/the-spacemobile-2-26-1965-from-the-series">
           This particular poster
          </a>
          is advertising the arrival of the Spacemobile in New Zealand, jointly sponsored by the New Zealand Department of Education and NASA. The National Archives put the poster in a series titled, "Propaganda Posters Distributed in Asia, Latin America and the Middle East, ca. 1950  ca. 1965."
         </p>
         <p>
          Earlier this year, we wrote about a
          <a href="http://www.space.com/28211-nasa-alien-planet-travel-posters.html">
           new series of NASA posters
          </a>
          with a retro feel, advertising tourism to planets outside our solar system. But the Spacemobile poster is the real deal: a genuine artifact of NASA advertising in the 1960s.
         </p>
