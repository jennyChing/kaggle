          <div id="ArticleContent" style="text-align:justify;">
           <p>
            <strong>
             Roscosmos will soon consider a project to prevent a large asteroid from colliding with Earth after 2030, the head of Russias space agency said on Wednesday.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            A scientist recently told me an interesting thing about the path [of an asteroid] constantly nearing Earth He has calculated that it will surely collide with Earth in the 2030 s, Anatoly Perminov said during an interview with the Voice of Russia radio.
            <!--more-->
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_15485" style="width: 620px">
            <a href="http://spacefellowship.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/300762main_image_1258_1024-768.jpg">
             <img alt="Asteroid. Credit: NASA" class="size-full wp-image-15485" height="457" src="http://spacefellowship.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/300762main_image_1258_1024-768.jpg" title="Asteroid. Credit: NASA" width="610"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Asteroid. Credit: NASA
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            He referred to Apophis, an asteroid that he said was almost three times as large as the Tunguska meteorite.
           </p>
           <p>
            On June 30, 1908, an explosion equivalent to between 5 and 30 megatons of TNT occurred near the Podkamennaya Tunguska River in a remote region of Russias Siberia. The Tunguska blast flattened 80 million trees, destroying an area of around 2,150 sq km (830 sq miles).
           </p>
           <p>
            Perminov said Russia was not planning to destroy the asteroid.
           </p>
           <p>
            No nuclear explosions [will be carried out], everything [will be done] on the basis of the laws of physics, he said.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Russian space official also said after having considered the project, Russia could invite experts from Europe, the United States and China to join it.
           </p>
           <p>
            Peoples lives are at stake. We should pay several hundred million dollars and design a system that would prevent a collision, rather than sit and wait for it to happen and kill hundreds of thousands of people, Perminov said.
           </p>
           <p>
            Though Apophis is currently considered the largest threat to our planet, NASA scientists published in October an update of its orbit indicating a significantly reduced likelihood of a hazardous encounter with Earth in 2036.
           </p>
           <p>
             RIA Novosti
           </p>
