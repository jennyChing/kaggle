       <div class="title colordefault" style="clear: both;">
        <h4>
         Why You Should Vacation with your Family
        </h4>
       </div>
       <div class="mbf clearfix block block-system">
        <div about="/best/traveling/why-you-should-vacation-your-family.html" class="node node-article node-promoted clearfix" id="node-6271" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">
         <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="Why You Should Vacation with your Family" property="dc:title">
         </span>
         <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="0" datatype="xsd:integer" property="sioc:num_replies">
         </span>
         <div class="submitted">
          <span content="2014-09-08T23:24:31+08:00" datatype="xsd:dateTime" property="dc:date dc:created" rel="sioc:has_creator">
           Submitted by
           <a about="/users/brianna-krueger" class="username" datatype="" href="/users/brianna-krueger" property="foaf:name" title="View user profile." typeof="sioc:UserAccount" xml:lang="">
            Brianna Krueger
           </a>
           on Mon, 09/08/2014 - 23:24
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="user-picture">
          <a href="/users/brianna-krueger" title="View user profile.">
           <img alt="Brianna Krueger's picture" src="https://www.wanderingeducators.com/system/files/styles/userpicture/private/files/pictures/picture-1671-1435014972.jpg?itok=6zfZ15rR" title="Brianna Krueger's picture" typeof="foaf:Image"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
          <div class="field-items">
           <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
            <p>
             My cousin Karl said it well, Theres a reason Kruegers have been coming to Port Austin since the 1940s, and its not just because Port Austin, Michigan is great. Which it is. Its pretty damn great. Lake Huron, sandy beaches, storms that blow over quickly, sunsets that melt into the lake like butter, bonfires that blaze, stargazing that expands far as the eyes can see, all at our fingertips and literally so because we are on the tip of the thumb.
             <strong>
              But theres more
             </strong>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              There are the people you vacation with.
             </strong>
             Family - or ass rash when they annoy* you, but still best, family.
            </p>
            <p>
             <sub>
              *Kitty Foremen (
              <em>
               That 70s Show
              </em>
              ) said it wisely: You know I love my family, but sometimes I just want to get in the car and run them over. It happens to the best of us, but we move forth, forget, and carry on.
             </sub>
            </p>
            <p>
             While
             <strong>
              Port Austin
             </strong>
             is damn great, what gives it a damn is family. And you should vacation with them.* All 24 of them loveable knuckleheads. Aunts, Uncles, and cousins  oh my  Are you in for a grand time! Because it will be the best of times, and sometimes the worst of times, but its still family time.
            </p>
            <p>
             <sub>
              *By them, I mean your family. Not mine. Although you are welcome to join.
             </sub>
            </p>
            <p>
             I recognize that my family is fortunate enough to all
             <a href="http://www.wanderingeducators.com/best/traveling/how-survive-vacationing-your-family.html" target="_blank">
              get along
             </a>
             and like each other since, according to TV, no one ever seems to like their in-laws. Personally, as someone who has always grown up with my extended family, I dont understand not liking them  maybe in few, stand-alone moments because lets face it everyone gets on each others nerves once in a while. Overall, we like each other, and for that I am thankful.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Here are 10 reasons why you should vacation with your extended family.
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Because what else would you talk about at holiday gatherings without a little reminiscing?
             </strong>
             How boring work is or how cold the weather is? Maybe briefly, but laughing at the
             <a href="http://www.wanderingeducators.com/best/stories/lessons-i-learned-vacation-my-dad.html" target="_blank">
              good
             </a>
             (and often
             <a href="http://www.wanderingeducators.com/best/stories/weird-moments-make-best-memories.html" target="_blank">
              weird
             </a>
             ) memories weve had is a bit more enjoyable. Such as that one time someone turned off the water at the cottage we were staying at, thinking it was a light switch and no one had a clue the two were attached. We had to dig a hole nicknamed the Billyto go about our bathroom needs. Twenty people, one hole, no fun, but always a classic Krueger memory.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Because theyre family.
             </strong>
             Theyre some of the only people that will love you unconditionally, without reason, and still take you back when you
             <a href="http://www.wanderingeducators.com/best/stories/getting-kicked-out-germany.html" target="_blank">
              lose your cool.
             </a>
             You cant ask for better company than that; someone who always has your back and will cheer you on, and always someone to hang out with.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             While were on that note
             <strong>
              Because its
              <u>
               uninterrupted
              </u>
              family time.
             </strong>
             Life can get in the way. Jobs, school, sports. But when we get together, all those real life worries disappear and its just family time, filled with rambunctious talking, radiating love, and infectious laughter. Its time to just be together and enjoy the company.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              <img alt="Why You Should Vacation with your Family - reason 3" src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-9x5NtZxhsVM/VAz9bgJBBwI/AAAAAAAAYsE/eYsSTBdYJhA/s640/15063276015_6c35ffae8a_o.jpg" style="height:338px; width:450px"/>
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              With family, its always a good time.
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Because it brings you closer.
             </strong>
             Much as I hate the clich out of sight, out of mind its true for a reason. Whether its a friend or family member, distance isnt easy and takes patience in understanding and communications. Sometimes you need to close the distance to make the heart (clich-ly) grow fonder. While special moments can be created via skype, its the
             <a href="http://www.wanderingeducators.com/best/stories/how-vacations-can-get-weirder.html" target="_blank">
              moments youre together
             </a>
             you tend to cherish.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Because who else are you supposed to vacation with?
             </strong>
             Your friends might sound like a good answer because theyre people you chose to be a part of your life and basically make family, but that doesnt always make it easy to vacation with them. No matter how close you are, the elements of travel do test relationships; when youve vacationed with family for so long you know everyones elements and how to get along best without potentially ruining a relationship.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Because its fun.
             </strong>
             Families dont judge each other (much) so when it comes to fun
             <a href="http://www.wanderingeducators.com/best/stories/miscommunication-joy-family-vacation.html" target="_blank">
              were all in this together
             </a>
             (and very likely
             <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NDHYZtwjFTs" target="_blank">
              singing along
             </a>
             ). You cant find very many people so willing to celebrate Krueger Khristmas in August Kocktail Kelebration Extravaganza, and so many willing to decorate and dress up for the occasion. Nor can you find so many people willing to take the polar plunge into Lake Huron.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Why You Should Vacation with your Family: Krueger Khristmas in August Kocktail Kelebration Extravaganza. Please note the jorts were a white elephant gag gift." src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-vyfKC15yVAk/VAz9UIuna5I/AAAAAAAAYq8/7Rvrsp1vrmI/s640/15040280926_cf1991440f_o.jpg" style="height:338px; width:450px"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              Krueger Khristmas in August Kocktail Kelebration Extravaganza. Please note the jorts were a white elephant gag gift.
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Because its not always about the journey.
             </strong>
             Its sometimes about the destination. The specific place we gather in Port Austin is rich in beauty, adventure, and relaxation. Beauty is created by the unique shoreline of giant boulders, stretches of sand, and willowy trees. Adventure begins with jumping off a giant boulder surrounded by water and continues with digging for clay in the sand. Relaxation simply comes with being there and napping on the rocks.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Because it creates (and maintains) tradition.
             </strong>
             In a land before time, a time before me, my parents, and their parents, my great-grandparents vacationed every year to a place called paradise. (Other may know paradise as the private beach community Broken Rocks Association just outisde
             <a href="http://www.wanderingeducators.com/best/traveling/foodie-finds-definitive-guide-where-eat-port-austin-michigan.html#!" target="_blank">
              Port Austin village limits
             </a>
             .) And since, its been a tradition up-held through the generations, all of us experiencing where nature did her best.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Because the more the merrier.
             </strong>
             Another clich proven true. More people = more to do = more fun = more memories. Of which the more memories to reminisce at holiday gatherings, which then makes you want to vacation with your family all the more. Its a circle of life and love and family.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Finally
             <strong>
              Because you love them
             </strong>
             . Simply, the only reason you need. Spending time with the ones you love is always a good idea.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Why You Should Vacation with your Family" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-1ptsSGIsQto/VAz9VYlQXUI/AAAAAAAAYrE/1cAaxB6nwMM/s640/14876627770_3a20ed9457_o.jpg" style="height:338px; width:450px"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              This is family.
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              What reasons do love family get-togethers? Share with us in the comments!
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
