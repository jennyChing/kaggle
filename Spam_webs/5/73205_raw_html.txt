        <p>
         My oldest child was born while my husband was deployed in the first gulf war. Our squadrons support group organized dinners for me every night until my husband returned 4 weeks later. But that is not all. Whoever brought me dinner packed a lunch for me to enjoy the next day. Seriously.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have been paying that amazing gift forward for the last 20 years.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of my favorite dishes to take when a family needs a meal is Enchilada Stuffed Pasta Shells. One of the benefits of this recipe is that it makes enough for two 13 x 9 baking dishes, which means I dont neglect my family when I am feeding another family. Another benefit is that, in a pinch, a new mom can pick the shells up and eat them like tacos while holding a baby in her other arm.
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://344apz3bh6di1m0sag1etg3fxyz.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont like waste, but this is one time where I liketo usedisposable aluminum pans. Then the familyI deliver a meal to does not have to worry about washing the pan or returning it.
        </p>

          <h1 class="fn">
           Enchilada Stuffed Pasta Shells
          </h1>
          <p class="yield">
           Yield  8 servings
          </p>
          <p class="prepTime">
           Preparation Time  15 minutes
          </p>
          <p class="cookTime">
           Cooking Time  45 minutes
          </p>
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient">
            1 lb. hamburger
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            1/2 cup diced onion
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            3 teaspoons
            <a href="http://www.5dollardinners.com/homemade-taco-seasoning/" target="_blank">
             homemade taco seasoning
            </a>
            or 1 package taco seasoning
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            1 3/4 cups
            <a href="http://www.5dollardinners.com/how-to-cook-dried-beans/" target="_blank">
             cooked black beans
            </a>
            or 1 can black beans
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            1 can diced tomatoes, do not drain
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            12 oz. package jumbo shells
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            2 cups shredded cheese
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            1 1/2 cups tomato sauce
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            1/2 cup medium salsa
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient">
            1 1/2 cups shredded cheese
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           Directions
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Put a large pot of water on to boil. Once it begins boiling add shells and cook according to the package directions.
           </li>
           <li>
            While the water is heating, cook hamburger and onion over a medium flame until hamburger is browned.
           </li>
           <li>
            Add taco seasoning, beans, and diced tomatoes. Be sure to add the juice from the diced tomatoes too. Simmer over low heat for for 5 minutes.
           </li>
           <li>
            Remove from heat aside and let cool while the shells cook. Once the meat mixture is close to room temperature, add 2 cups of cheese and mix well.
           </li>
           <li>
            In a small bowl, combine tomato sauce and salsa. Spoon 1/2 cup of tomato sauce mixture into each 13 x 9 baking dish.
           </li>
           <li>
            Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
           </li>
           <li>
            After the shells have been drained, spoon meat mixture into each shell and place it in a baking dish.
           </li>
           <li>
            After all the shells have been filled. Spoon 1/2 cup of the tomato sauce mixture over the shells in each baking dish. Sprinkle 3/4 cup cheese over the stuffed shells in each baking dish.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <a href="http://344apz3bh6di1m0sag1etg3fxyz.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/Stuffed-pasta-with-Cheese-640x456.jpg">
            <img alt="Stuffed pasta with Cheese (640x456)" class="alignnone wp-image-16953" src="http://344apz3bh6di1m0sag1etg3fxyz.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/Stuffed-pasta-with-Cheese-640x456.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Cover one baking dish with foil and bake at 350 degrees for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 5 minutes.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p>
         If you are taking this to another family, you can cover the dish with foil and put the baking directions on a sticky note on top of the foil.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is also a great cook one, freeze one meal. If you are not taking a meal to another family, cover one baking dish with plastic wrap and freeze. Defrost in the refrigerator on the day you want to cook the stuffed shells for dinner.
        </p>
        <p>
         I serve Enchilada Stuffed Pasta Shellswith a garden salad and
         <a href="http://www.premeditatedleftovers.com/2011/04/mexican-rice.html" target="_blank">
          Mexican Rice
         </a>
         .
        </p>
