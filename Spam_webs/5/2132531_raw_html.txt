       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Roasted Beef Tenderloin with Mushroom Ragout
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-categories">
        <a href="http://blog.williams-sonoma.com/cat/cook/" rel="category tag">

      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        <a href="http://blog.williams-sonoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/WS_MEAT_BeefTenderloin_Mushroom_01.jpg">
         <img alt="Roasted Beef Tenderloin with Mushroom Ragout " class="alignnone size-large wp-image-27644" height="885" src="http://blog.williams-sonoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/WS_MEAT_BeefTenderloin_Mushroom_01-652x885.jpg" width="652"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Tenderloin, a tender, special-occasion cut, is quite mildnot as beefy as rib roastso this recipe includes an earthy contrast of sherry-flavored woodsy mushrooms to serve alongside the meat. Serve hot or cold with a dollop of horseradish crme frache.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Roasted Beef Tenderloin with Mushroom Ragout
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        For the horseradish crme frache:
       </p>
       <p>
        3 Tbs. freshly grated horseradish
       </p>
       <p>
        1 cup (8 oz./250 g.)crme frache
       </p>
       <p>
        1 Tbs. plus 2 tsp. finely snipped fresh chives
       </p>
       <p>
        1/2 tsp. freshly ground white pepper
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        1 beef tenderloin, 3 1/2 to 4 lb. (1.75 kg.-2 kg.), silverskin removed or snipped in several places
       </p>
       <p>
        2 Tbs. whole-grain mustard
       </p>
       <p>
        1 1/2 Tbs. olive oil, plus more for cooking
       </p>
       <p>
        1 1/2 tsp. dried thyme
       </p>
       <p>
        Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        For the mushroom ragout:
       </p>
       <p>
        2 Tbs. unsalted butter
       </p>
       <p>
        2 large shallots, finely chopped
       </p>
       <p>
        1 lb. (500 g.) mixed mushrooms, such as oyster and cremini, brushed clean and thickly sliced
       </p>
       <p>
        1/4 cup (2 fl. oz./60 ml.) Madeira or medium-dry sherry
       </p>
       <p>
        1/4 cup (2 fl. oz./60 ml.)heavy cream
       </p>
       <p>
        2 tsp. finely snipped fresh chives
       </p>
       <p>
        1/4 tsp. salt
       </p>
       <p>
        Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        To make the horseradish crme frache, in a bowl, whisk together the horseradish, crme frache, chives and white pepper. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Remove the tenderloin from the refrigerator and let stand at room temperature for 2 hours.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Preheat an oven to 425F (220C).
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        In a small bowl, whisk together the mustard, the 1 1/2 Tbs. olive oil and the thyme. Set aside.Using kitchen string, tie the tenderloin firmly at 1 1/2-inch (4-cm.) intervals to keep it plump and round during cooking. Pat thoroughly dry and season all sides generously with salt.
        <br/>
        Heat a large roasting pan over medium-high heat until it is very hot, about 3 minutes. Add enough oil to the pan to coat the bottom. When the oil is shimmering, add the tenderloin and sear without moving for 2 1/2 minutes. Turn the tenderloin and continue to sear until it has an even, dark brown crust on all sides. Transfer the tenderloin to a cutting board, brush with the mustard mixture and season generously with black pepper.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Set a rack in the roasting pan and place the tenderloin on the rack. Transfer the pan to the oven and roast until an instant-read thermometer inserted into the center of the meat registers 125 to 130F (52 to54C), 15 to 20 minutes. The temperature will range from rare to medium-rare in different parts of the tenderloin. Transfer the tenderloin to a platter, cover loosely with aluminum foil, and let rest for at least 15 minutes or up to 30 minutes.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Meanwhile, prepare the mushroom ragout: In a large fry pan over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the shallots and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until they release their liquid, about 10 minutes. Add the Madeira and simmer until almost completely evaporated. Stir in the cream, chives, salt and black pepper. Continue to cook, stirring, until the ragout is thick, creamy, and bubbling, 1 to 2 minutes more.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Snip the strings and cut the tenderloin into slices about 1/2 inch (12 mm.) thick. Arrange on plates with the ragout and serve immediately. Pass the horseradish crme frache alongside. Serves 6 to 8.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Recipe from
        <em>
         The Cook &amp; the Butcher
        </em>
        , by Brigit Binns.
       </p>
