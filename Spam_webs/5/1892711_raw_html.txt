        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Easy DIY Infant Bear Costume
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         If you havent planned the babys Halloween costume yet, here is a quick DIY for you! This is as easy and inexpensive as it gets- I should know- I made three of them! I definitely cant afford expensive costumes for all the kids this year, so I had to get creative. This DIY bear costume is perfect for infants and toddlers, and even those with beginner sewing skills can complete it. The costume base is a store bought fleece jacket, and it just builds from there. I purchased my jackets from Kohls for $6.99, and I had the rest of the supplies at home. So $6.99 for a costume? Thats about as cheap as it gets! Pair the jacket with matching brown pants for a full costume.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bear-costume.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Bear Costume Tutorial " class="aligncenter wp-image-1778" height="558" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bear-costume.jpg" width="701"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Supplies:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul style="text-align: center;">
         <li>
          Dark brown fleece zip-up jacket with hood
         </li>
         <li>
          Dark brown scrap fleece
         </li>
         <li>
          Tan scrap fleece
         </li>
         <li>
          Thread to match and sewing machine
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Lay out the fleece jacket flat.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume from It Happens in a Blink" class="aligncenter" height="614" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostuemjacket.jpg" width="458"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Cut a circle from the tan fabric. I used a small bowl. The circle should fit nicely in the middle of the jacket.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="373" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumemakecircle.jpg" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Pin the circle to the middle of the jacket.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="426" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcsotumepinnedcircle.jpg" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Sew using a zig-zag stitch. Do not sew over the zipper. This needs to be usable to make sure the jacket can fit over the babys head.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="373" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcircle.jpg" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="373" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumecircle2.jpg" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Cut loose threads.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Now begin with ears. Cut small ear (2) and large ear (4) patterns.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="384" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumetwoears.jpg" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Pin small ear in the middle of large ear. Stitch using a zig zag stitch.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="597" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumecut.jpg" width="480"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Pin this ear to a plain ear, right sides together. Stitch using a 1/4 seam allowance. Clip curves and trim bulk.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Turn ear right side out.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="614" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumeearspinned.jpg" width="431"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Do the same with the other ear.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Now cut slits in the top of the jacket to insert the ears. I cut a 3 slit, 3 from the middle and 3 from the edge.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="375" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumeslit.jpg" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Insert ear into slit, turning under raw edges.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="373" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumeear1.jpg" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Stitch close to the fold.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="434" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumeear.jpg" width="640"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Do the same for other ear.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Bear Costume Tutorial" class="aligncenter" height="404" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bearcostumeears.jpg" width="720"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Finished! The zipper can be undone from the inside to make it slide easily over the babys head.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bear-costume.jpg">
          <img alt="DIY Bear Costume Tutorial " class="aligncenter wp-image-1778" height="564" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bear-costume.jpg" width="708"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         We used this costume for our BRAVE family Halloween. Since we have triplet boys, they were all bears. Our daughter was
         <a href="http://ithappensinablink.com/2013/11/disney-brave-merida-costume-with-simplicity-pattern-1557.html" target="_blank" title="Disney Brave Merida Costume with Simplicity Pattern 1557">
          Merida
         </a>
         ! See her costume {
         <a href="http://ithappensinablink.com/2013/11/disney-brave-merida-costume-with-simplicity-pattern-1557.html" target="_blank" title="Disney Brave Merida Costume with Simplicity Pattern 1557">
          here
         </a>
         }.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://ithappensinablink.com/2013/11/disney-brave-merida-costume-with-simplicity-pattern-1557.html" target="_blank" title="Disney Brave Merida Costume with Simplicity Pattern 1557">
          <img alt="Merida Costume Simplicity pattern" class="aligncenter wp-image-2033" height="802" src="http://ithappensinablink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/merida-1words-title-557x1024.jpg" width="436"/>
         </a>
        </p>
