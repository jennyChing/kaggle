             <h1 class="page-title">
              Legal obligations of marketing
             </h1>
            </div>

            <div aria-labelledby="ctl00_ctl00_PlaceHolderPageMainContent_PlaceHolderMain_PlaceHolderMain_ctl02_label" class="ms-rtestate-field" id="ctl00_ctl00_PlaceHolderPageMainContent_PlaceHolderMain_PlaceHolderMain_ctl02__ControlWrapper_RichHtmlField" style="display:inline">
             <p>
              When setting your prices and advertising your products or services, you need to be mindful of the relevant regulations, to ensure you aren't misleading your
              <a href="/business-topics/selling-products-and-services/customers/Pages/default.aspx">
               customers
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              Consider the following list of regulations when conducting your marketing activities:
             </p>
             <h2>
              Advertising
             </h2>
             <p>
              When you advertise or market your business you need comply with a number of regulations:
             </p>
             <h3>
              Misleading &amp; deceptive conduct
             </h3>
             <p>
              When promoting your products or services, you need to ensure that any branding, statement, quote or any other representation is not false or misleading. Component pricing is when the price of a product or service is advertised or displayed in separate parts. For example, when the single price of a car is displayed and additional on road costs are listed separately. If you advertise using component pricing you must also provide the full price inclusive of additional costs in a more prominent way.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Bait advertising
             </h3>
             <p>
              Bait advertising is where a product is advertised at a certain price without a reasonable supply. Bait advertising is illegal if a business sells the product knowing that they cannot meet expected demand.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Competitions
             </h2>
             <p>
              Competitions, lotteries or promotions over a certain amount are regulated by your state or territory through a permit. You can download a permit form from the
              <a href="http://ablis.business.gov.au/">
               Australian Business Licence Information Service
              </a>
              (ABLIS) website.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Signage
             </h2>
             <p>
              Signage including 'A' frames, sandwich boards, or permanent signs on buildings, footpaths or roads are regulated by your state or territory government. Before you erect a sign, you need to apply for a permit and in some circumstances you may also require public liability insurance.
             </p>
             <p>
              You can download a permit form from your state or territorygovernment through the
              <a href="http://ablis.business.gov.au/">
               Australian Business Licence Information Service
              </a>
              (ABLIS) website.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Direct marketing
             </h2>
             <p>
              Most direct marketing activities are regulated by federal or state laws. Before you conduct direct marketing, check that you comply with the privacy legislation and spam regulations.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Do Not Call Register
             </h2>
             <p>
              If you wish to conduct telemarketing or fax marketing, you need to comply with the
              <em>
               Do Not Call Register Act 2006
              </em>
              . The
              <a href="https://www.donotcall.gov.au/">
               Do Not Call Register
              </a>
              is a list of protected phone and fax numbers. If you contact a number on the register, you may be in breach of the Act and could face penalties and enforceable undertakings.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Spam
             </h2>
             <p>
              Before you send out marketing material via email, you need to ensure you comply with the
              <em>
               Spam Act 2003
              </em>
              . Under the Act it's illegal to send unsolicited commercial electronic messages without consent. Find out more about
              <a href="http://business-topics/selling-products-and-services/customers/Pages/e-marketing.aspx">
               e-marketing
              </a>
              in our Online business topic.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Privacy
             </h2>
             <p>
              Care must be taken when you collect, use, secure and disclose a customer's personal information to ensure you comply with the Privacy Act 1988. Find out more in our Online business topic.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Brochures
             </h2>
             <p>
              Handing out brochures, flyers or promotional materials on public property typically requires a permit from your state or territory government. You can download a permit form from the
              <a href="http://ablis.business.gov.au/">
               Australian Business Licence Information Service
              </a>
              website. Under state environment protection legislation, it may be illegal to place advertising material on a vehicle.
             </p>
             <p>
              Find your local
              <a href="/business-topics/business-planning/environmental-management/Pages/environmental-management-in-your-state-or-territory.aspx">
               Environment Protection Authority
              </a>
              on our website.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Bill posting
             </h2>
             <p>
              Bill posting is where a brochure or other promotional material is attached to a piece of public property. Some states have designated bill poster locations and others require a permit. Find out if you need a permit on the
              <a href="http://ablis.business.gov.au/">
               Australian Business Licence Information Service
              </a>
              (ABLIS) website.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Spruiking
             </h2>
             <p>
              Spruiking is where an operator entices walk-in traffic by marketing products or services to passers-by (e.g. when using a microphone). Spruiking is regulated at the state level and may require a permit. Find out if you need a permit on the
              <a href="http://ablis.business.gov.au/">
               Australian Business Licence Information Service
              </a>
              (ABLIS) website.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Pricing regulations
             </h2>
             <p>
              When you price your products or services or advertise a price, you need to comply with pricing regulations and display the price clearly and accurately.
             </p>
             <p>
              Read our pages on
              <a href="/business-topics/selling-products-and-services/fair-trading/Pages/pricing-regulations.aspx" title="Pricing regulations">
               Pricing regulations
              </a>
              and
              <a href="/business-topics/selling-products-and-services/fair-trading/Pages/displaying-prices.aspx" title="Displaying prices">
               Displaying pricing
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <h2>
              Intellectual property
             </h2>
             <p>
              When you use another person's or business'
              <a href="/business-topics/business-planning/intellectual-property/Pages/default.aspx">
               intellectual property
              </a>
              for the purposes of branding or selling, you need to comply with Intellectual Property (IP) regulations including trade mark laws.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Trade mark laws
             </h3>
             <p>
              When preparing your branding, business name or website name, you need to ensure aren't in breach Australian or international trade mark laws.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Music
             </h3>
             <p>
              If you want to use music in your advertising or even play music in your business, you need to obtain a licence with the Australasian Performing Right Association Limited (APRA) and Australasian Mechanical Copyright Owners Society Limited (AMCOS). To find out more visit the
              <a href="http://www.apra-amcos.com.au/">
               APRA-AMCOS
              </a>
              website.
             </p>
             <h2>
              International regulations
             </h2>
             <p>
              When you export or expand overseas, it's important to comply with both Australian and international regulations including:
             </p>
             <h3>
              International pricing regulations
             </h3>
             <p>
              If you're considering exporting your products or services, ensure you're aware of any international regulations that may impact your pricing decisions. See the Austrade website for further details on how to determine your
              <a href="http://www.austrade.gov.au/Export/About-Exporting/Export-pricing">
               export prices
              </a>
              as well as the
              <a href="http://www.austrade.gov.au/Export/About-Exporting/Legal-issues">
               legal issues
              </a>
              that may affect your decision.
             </p>
             <p>
              When preparing your branding, business name or website name, you need to ensure it doesn't breech Australian or
              <a href="http://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/get-the-right-ip/trade-marks/international-trade-marks/">
               international trademark laws
              </a>
              .
             </p>
