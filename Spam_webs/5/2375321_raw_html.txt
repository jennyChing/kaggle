      <h2>
       Forex Quotes - The Tricks to Decipher Their Power
      </h2>
      <h4 class="article-details">
       <span class="submitted">
        Submitted by
        <a href="/trading-forum/user/4272-adil" rel="nofollow" target="_BLANK" title="View user profile.">
         adil
        </a>
        on Thu, 05/21/2015 - 16:54
       </span>
      </h4>
      <span class="tag-details">
       Tagged as: Forex Trading School    , Forex Trading
      </span>
      <hr/>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Contrary to what various brokers
and investors portray about FOREX quotes, every trader must be
cautious when working with them. One wrong inference and the
consequences can be devastating.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       FOREX quotes are completely
different from stock quotes. Many traders who have experience with
stock trading think they can deal with FOREX quotes with the same
principles, but they are proven wrong time and again.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Learning the FOREX
quotes:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The trick to understanding FOREX
quotes is to remain concentrated on keeping balance, dont waste
time in gathering too much information, but dont ignore the
importance of FOREX quotes.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The currency included in the first
turn is known as base currency and the base currency value is
always one (as an indicator of its stability). The FOREX quotes
usually consist of two values: ask and bid price.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <u>
        ASK price
       </u>
       : the price at
which trader can buy the base currency and sell the counter
currency
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <u>
        BID price
       </u>
       : the price at
which trader can sell the base currency
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Tips for understanding
FOREX quotes:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li style="text-align: justify;">
        As a standard, USD is the base
currency for all FOREX quotes. Examples include USD/CAD, USD/JPY
AND USD/EUR.
       </li>
       <li style="text-align: justify;">
        If both the currencies in a FOREX
quote are traded for each other in the FOREX market, the quote is
known as the cross. For example, the USD/CHF denotes the FOREX
quote applicable on the buying of CHF using USD.
       </li>
       <li style="text-align: justify;">
        FOREX quotes are always denoted by
a ratio with USD value of 1. As an example: a FOREX quote of
USD/JPY: 123.4 means 1 USD is equal to 123.4 JPY.
       </li>
       <li style="text-align: justify;">
        The higher the value of FOREX
quote, the stronger the USD has become, the value of counter
currency has diminished consequently.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <u>
        Important information
       </u>
       : For
the following three currencies, the USD doesnt act as the base
value
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li style="text-align: justify;">
        British Pound (GBP)
       </li>
       <li style="text-align: justify;">
        Euro (EUR)
       </li>
       <li style="text-align: justify;">
        Australian Dollar (AUD)
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       If the FOREX quote is USD/EUR:
1.832, it denotes that 1 EUR can be purchased by spending 1.832
USD. This is very confusing for new traders, and since most of them
trade of famous currency pairs of USD/EUR, USD/GBP etc. where FOREX
quotes have different meaning, they tend to experience losses.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       It is always important to remember
when USD is used as base currency, and when not. For most of the
times, the USD value remains consistent, but of course there are
ups and downs. The currencies that rely on USD experience the same
effect, but currencies that are symbol of powerful and independent
economies may experience a reciprocal effect.
      </p>
