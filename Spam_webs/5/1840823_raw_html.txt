       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Friday Project: Braided T-Shirt Rag Rug
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2012-03-16T10:43:00+00:00">
         March 16, 2012
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://www.dosmallthingswithlove.com/author/Nancy" rel="author">
           Nancy Bandzuch
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://www.dosmallthingswithlove.com/2012/03/friday-project-braided-t-shirt-rag-rug.html#comments">
          48 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-QyQJBrlzuEo/T2NWldRWirI/AAAAAAAACb0/-3i08jcyJNc/s1600/T-shirt+rug.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="braided t-shirt rag rug" border="0" height="433" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-QyQJBrlzuEo/T2NWldRWirI/AAAAAAAACb0/-3i08jcyJNc/s640/T-shirt+rug.jpg" style="border: 0px;" title="braided t-shirt rag rug" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         One of my favorite things to do while crating is to upcycle. Therefore, this craft,
         <strong>
          Braided T-Shirt Rag Rug
         </strong>
         , is a personal favorite. It is super easyand who doesnt have extra t-shirt laying around?  And plus, making it fit into my Lenten goals.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Among other things, I decided to give up buying craft supplies this lent. I also gave up thrift shopping (it has been hard!). Although I have not been perfect in my resolutions, I have been pleased to see the true creativity inside of me when forced to use stuff just lying around the house for my projects. That is why I wasnt unhappy when one of my husbands former roommates brought over a couple of tubs filled with my husbands old clothes, most of which were t-shirts. After my husband decided he didnt want any of it, I moved the tubs directly down to my craft room and got started on this project.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         As a child I remember my grandmothers house filled with beautiful braided rugs. I like this style of rug because of how the colors weave in and out of each other. Since I was making my rug out of t-shirts, mine wasnt going to be as vintage feeling as hers, but that is fine since mine is destined for a childs playroom.
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Making a braided rag rug is very simple, but pretty time consuming. Also, at the end of it you will likely have some nice looking calluses on your fingers.
        </div>
        <p>
         Before we get into the roundup I just wanted to tell you about a book my kids and I have been working our way through around hereand using lots of otherwise-would-be-trash items along the way:
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1594745951/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1594745951&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=dosmathiwitlo-20&amp;linkId=5B65EVDE2JWZ7ECH">
          Craft-a-Day: 365 Simple Handmade Projects
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=dosmathiwitlo-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1594745951" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         . Check it out if you have lots of little crafters at home, like I do. We are having so much fun with this!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.dosmallthingswithlove.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/880x0_p18mn31qc52dg1mj0o8jigmhr76.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="880x0_p18mn31qc52dg1mj0o8jigmhr76" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6523" data-id="6523" height="500" src="http://www.dosmallthingswithlove.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/880x0_p18mn31qc52dg1mj0o8jigmhr76-750x500.jpg" width="750"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.dosmallthingswithlove.com/2012/03/friday-project-braided-t-shirt-rag-rug.html&amp;media=http://www.dosmallthingswithlove.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/880x0_p18mn31qc52dg1mj0o8jigmhr76-750x500.jpg&amp;description=Friday Project: Braided T-Shirt Rag Rug')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         And, onto rug making!
        </div>
        <h2 style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Heres how to create a Braided T-Shirt Rag Rug
        </h2>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Supplies:
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         LOTS of colorful old t-shirts
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Sharp scissors (or a rotary cutter is better)
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Needle and Thread (the type you put in a sewing machine)
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         Step 1: Cut the t-shirts into 2 inch strips (or loops which you cut). I did not use the top part of the t-shirt because I had plenty of t-shirts. The strips arent very long, but thats okay because strips too long will make braiding difficult. Also, dont go narrower than 2 inches. You will need the bulk to keep the rug from bubbling when you stitch it (I learned that the hard way!)
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BC6gSs9INTw/T2NZoUM_PmI/AAAAAAAACb8/O4Xmq7_Xx90/s1600/P1120009.JPG" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="braided t-shirt rag rug" border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BC6gSs9INTw/T2NZoUM_PmI/AAAAAAAACb8/O4Xmq7_Xx90/s640/P1120009.JPG" style="border: 0px;" title="braided t-shirt rag rug" width="480"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Step 2: Start braidingand keep braiding! Since this is a rag rug I simply tied on a new strip when one got to the end, making sure to vary where the knots were so there wasnt a huge bump in the completed rug.
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-GOBvDvOQz0Y/T2NZuAwPinI/AAAAAAAACcE/U-JF36oUeRg/s1600/P1120011.JPG" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="braided t-shirt rag rug" border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-GOBvDvOQz0Y/T2NZuAwPinI/AAAAAAAACcE/U-JF36oUeRg/s640/P1120011.JPG" style="border: 0px;" title="braided t-shirt rag rug" width="496"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
         Step 3: When you have quite a length of braided t-shirt, start stitching it together. Working from the back and do a simple whip stitch between the two pieces of braid. I chose to do an oval shape by folding over the first section of braided t-shirt and stitching it together, and then wrapping around and stitching after that.
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-S56SnoBZ4jY/T2NZ11CxmPI/AAAAAAAACcM/CiN-fKnikh8/s1600/P1140025.JPG" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
