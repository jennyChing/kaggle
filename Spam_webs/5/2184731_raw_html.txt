     <p>
      August 13 is designated
      <b>
       International Lefthanders Day
      </b>
      by Lefthanders International. It was first observed on the 13th of August, in the year 1976. As the name suggests, it is meant to promote awareness of the inconveniences faced by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-handed" title="Left-handed">
       left-handers
      </a>
      in a predominantly
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right-handed" title="Right-handed">
       right-handed
      </a>
      world. It celebrates their uniqueness and differences, who are from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-handedness#Demographics" title="Left-handedness">
       seven to ten percent
      </a>
      of the world's
      <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">
       population
      </a>
      . Thousands of left-handed people in today's society have to adapt to use right handed tools and objects.
     </p>
     <p>
      Theres also a more serious side to the day, which spreads awareness about the special needs of left-handed kids. Also, left-handers are far more likely to develop
      <a href="/wiki/Schizophrenia" title="Schizophrenia">
       schizophrenia
      </a>
      than right-handers, for reasons not well understood.

       ]

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Lefthanders_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      While it had been observed on Aug. 13 of each year by many lefthanders since 1976, Left-handers Day was declared by the Left-Hander's Day Club on August 13, 1996.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was made to annually celebrate certain people's sinistrality and raise awareness of the advantages and disadvantages of being left-handed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Global_awareness">
       Global awareness
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Lefthanders_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Global awareness">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Left-handers Day is now celebrated world-wide. In fact, in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       United Kingdom
      </a>
      alone, there have been over 20 events that mark this celebration. These events include celebrating a left-hander's
      <a href="/wiki/Creativity" title="Creativity">
       creativity
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Adaptability" title="Adaptability">
       adaptability
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athleticism" title="Athleticism">
       athleticism
      </a>
      .
