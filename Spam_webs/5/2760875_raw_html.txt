     <p>
      <b>
       Salem bin Mohammed bin Awad bin Laden
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        .  
      </span>
      ) (1946May 29, 1988) was a
      <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">
       Saudi Arabian
      </a>
      investor and businessman.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Life">
       Life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salem_bin_Laden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Considered the eldest son of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohammed_bin_Laden" title="Mohammed bin Laden">
       Mohammed bin Laden
      </a>
      , the founder of
      <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Binladin_Group" title="Saudi Binladin Group">
       Saudi Binladin Group
      </a>
      and a half-brother and cousin of
      <a href="/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden" title="Osama bin Laden">
       Osama bin Laden
      </a>
      . Bin Laden was educated at
      <a href="/wiki/Millfield" title="Millfield">
       Millfield
      </a>
      and acted as the patriarch of the
      <a href="/wiki/Bin_Laden_family" title="Bin Laden family">
       bin Laden family
      </a>
      after the 1967 death of his father. Salem managed the family's extensive investment portfolio and was in charge of family income distribution. He also oversaw the individual education plans for each of his (half-) brothers and (half-) sisters. Just like his father, he highly valued close relationship of the bin Ladens with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saudi_royal_family" title="Saudi royal family">
       Saudi royal family
      </a>
      . He provided means and support to the family during the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Mosque_Seizure" title="Grand Mosque Seizure">
       Mecca uprising of 1979
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He owned a house in
      <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">
       Orlando, Florida
      </a>
      and often used it for vacation stays.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pete_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pete-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Death">
       Death
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salem_bin_Laden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Salem bin Laden died on May 29, 1988 when he accidentally drifted into high voltage electrical power lines adjacent to the Kitty Hawk Field of Dreams Ultra-Lite Flying Field at the edge of
      <a href="/wiki/Schertz,_Texas" title="Schertz, Texas">
       Schertz
      </a>
      , a northeastern
      <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">
       San Antonio
      </a>
      suburb. The Sprint
      <a href="/wiki/Ultralight_aircraft_(United_States)" title="Ultralight aircraft (United States)">
       ultralight aircraft
      </a>
      he was flying fell 115 feet to the ground after the wire strike. Salem, who was not wearing a safety helmet, died of head injuries from the resulting fall. The
      <a href="/wiki/National_Transportation_Safety_Board" title="National Transportation Safety Board">
       National Transportation Safety Board
      </a>
      did not conduct an accident investigation since the aircraft was an ultralight aircraft, which was not covered under their mandate due to exemption while operating under
      <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Regulations" title="Federal Aviation Regulations">
       FAR
      </a>
      Part 103 Provisions required by Federal law. The Schertz Police, who attended to the incident, stated in the report that Salem died in a freak accident.
     </p>
     <p>
      This was the second plane crash-related death in the
      <a href="/wiki/Bin_Laden_family" title="Bin Laden family">
       Bin Laden family
      </a>
      , as Salem's father
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohammed_bin_Laden" title="Mohammed bin Laden">
       Mohammed bin Laden
      </a>
      was also killed in a plane crash in 1967.
     </p>
