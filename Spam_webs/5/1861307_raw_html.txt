       <h1 class="entry-title">
        What To Expect From WWDC 2014
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://blog.qresolve.com/blog/2014/05/30/what-to-expect-from-wwdc-2014/" rel="bookmark" title="8:16 am">

          May 30, 2014
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">

        </span>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         The Apples developers conference WWDC 2014 is just a few hours away during which the tech giant is expected to make many important hardware and software related revelations. The event which is scheduled to take place during June 02-06, 2014 at San Franciscos Moscone West venue in San Francisco, California has become the pivot of discussion among major technology players worldwide.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-7482 alignleft" height="244" src="http://www.qresolve.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/WWDC-14.jpg" width="500"/>
         Let us quickly catch up the expectations from the event or what Apple is expected to offer during the event.
        </p>
        <p>
         Looking at the past WWDC events, it is likely that we will get new software and hardware from Apple. Last year during WWDC 2013, Apple had unveiled OS X 10.9 Mavericks, iOS 7, a new Mac Pro, a new MacBook Air, iTunes Radio and iWork for iCloud.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Things we can expect during WWDC 2014:
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          OS X 10.10
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         There is strong probability that the next version of Mac OS X, which is also known as OS X 10.10 will be previewed at the WWDC 2014. It is rumored that Apple design chief Jony Ive has been personally involved in the design and development of OS X 10.10, so it is likely that it could be inspired from iOS 7.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          iOS 8
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         It is believed that the iOS8 might also make its presence during the event. Since Apple has already given iOS a significant redesign with iOS7, therefore anything drastic is not expected but some other tweaks and improvements are on the cards for this year event.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Retina MacBook Air
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Apart from software improvements, update, and releases the company is also expected to make news for hardware. On the basis of past trend, it is highly probable that Apple may update the existing MacBook Air as Retina MacBook Air.
        </p>
        <p>
         However, Apple has recently released an update to the MacBook Air, but the update didnt accommodate Retina to the lightweight notebook PC. It is widely rumored that Apple is planning to bring a high-resolution display to the MacBook Air, so it is quite possible that a Retina model is in the pipeline to get displayed earlier next month during the event.
        </p>
