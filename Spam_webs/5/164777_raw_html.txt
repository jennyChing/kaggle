      <p>
       Well, fret not, lovers of gritty martial arts movies; below we have compiled a list of seven more action films to at least partially tide you over until The Raid 3 punches its way through the doors of your local cinema. What were our criteria? First: an emphasis on violence over plot. Second: all the kick-punching has to look organic - minimal wirework and CGI. Third: a degree of obscurity - multi-million-dollar Western blockbusters need not apply.

       1. Merantau (Indonesia)
      </div>
      <p>

      <p>
       <strong>
        What Is It?
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Raid director
       <a class="autolink" data-legacy-id="20014547" href="http://www.ign.com/stars/gareth-evans" title="Gareth Evans">
        Gareth Evans
       </a>
       's first (proper) film. Yuda (Raid star Iko Uwais) comes to Jakarta from the Indonesian sticks with a dream of teaching Silat - the style of fighting made famous by The Raid. Instead, he winds up on the wrong side of two Western brothers who spend their time talking in jarringly different accents and trafficking young women into the sex trade. So begins a comparatively low-budget martial arts romp through seedy nightclubs, construction sites and the like - but the fights are brutal and a younger Uwais a pleasure to watch, if pleasure is the right word for watching someone get impaled on a pipe or beaten half to death in an elevator.
      </p>

      <p>
       In near-future Paris, portly, well-heeled government officials have cordoned off the city's poorest and most violent area - District 13 - with a huge wall and armed police checkpoints on the roads. Like the worst ever Care in the Community programme, District 13 is now run by gangsters who are free to murder each other, enrich themselves with drug money and do lots and lots of parkour training. This unhappy solution chugs along right up until the gangsters steal a nuclear warhead and stick up Paris proper, forcing the government to send in their baddest cop and an imprisoned street tough to steal it back.
