          <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
           <a href="http://www.ayearofslowcooking.com/2008/05/crockpot-fried-rice-recipe.html">
            CrockPot Fried Rice Recipe
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="post-header-line-1">
          </div>
          <div class="post-body entry-content">
           <div>
            <div>
             <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_GxgLNKoVUA4/SBsJVVNJf-I/AAAAAAAABDs/pm2MNCGdzK4/s1600-h/IMG_1828.JPG">
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
              </span>
             </a>
             <span style="font-family: arial;">
             </span>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-tED-UHdyvlA/VUTsEnkMOAI/AAAAAAAAGDw/CxoEHBE0Boc/s1600/finished%2Bcrockpot%2Bfried%2Brice.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                <img alt="use your crockpot slow cooker to make fried rice" border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-tED-UHdyvlA/VUTsEnkMOAI/AAAAAAAAGDw/CxoEHBE0Boc/s1600/finished%2Bcrockpot%2Bfried%2Brice.jpg" title="this is such a great idea! use up all the leftover rice, meat, veggies, throw in an egg and some seasoning and you're done!" width="320"/>
               </a>
              </div>
              <span style="font-family: arial; font-size: x-small;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               <span style="font-size: x-small;">
                photo updated 5/15 via
                <a href="http://depositphotos.com/?ref=4668291">
                 deposit photos@gnohz
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               Day 123.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               <br/>
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               It's day 1 2 3! Which is more amusing to me than it should be with no coffee in sight.
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               So lets just say that you've been using your
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_0">
                crockpot
               </span>
               every single day and your refrigerator has a million little plastic containers in it filled with meat and veggies and rice and
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_1">
                quinoa
               </span>
               .
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               <br/>
              </span>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               And your kids are a bit tired of eating "mommy's leftovers!" for breakfast and lunch so you need a way to disguise them. Because you can't throw away food due the starving children thing and the global food shortage thing and you're Scottish.
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               You can use your leftovers to make fried rice.
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               for
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_2">
                reals
               </span>
               .
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               in the
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_3">
                crockpot
               </span>
               .
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               I know! It totally blew my mind, too.
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: arial;">
                The Ingredients.
               </span>
              </b>
             </div>
             <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5195757441291747314" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_GxgLNKoVUA4/SBsJ3VNJf_I/AAAAAAAABD0/I5v1oWBe788/s320/IMG_1820.JPG" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center;" title="slow cooker fried rice ingredients"/>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               2 cups leftover rice/
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_4">
                quinoa (brown rice, etc. totally fine to use)
               </span>
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               3 tablespoons butter
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               2 tablespoons soy sauce (LaChoy is gluten free)
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               2 teaspoons
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_5">
                worcestershire
               </span>
               sauce (Lea Perrins in US is gluten free)
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               1/2 teaspoon black pepper
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               1/4 teaspoon kosher salt (or 1/8 tsp iodized)
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               1/2 diced yellow onion
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               1 cup of whatever frozen or fresh vegetables you have on hand. (I had a bit of asparagus, some carrots and peas)
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               2 cups leftover meat, diced (I had leftover chicken and 3/4 of a cheeseburger patty from a restaurant--I told you I was Scottish!)
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               1 egg (no shell. DUH!)
              </span>
             </div>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               sesame seeds for garnish (optional)
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: arial;">
                The Directions.
               </span>
              </b>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               Plop everything on in there together.
              </span>
             </div>
             <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5195759193638404098" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_GxgLNKoVUA4/SBsLdVNJgAI/AAAAAAAABD8/27fS2PTecME/s320/IMG_1821.JPG" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center;" title="what it looks like when it's not cooked yet"/>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               Mix it around, and cook on high for 2-3 hours, or on low for 3-4. You are only heating everything up and cooking the egg.
              </span>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <b>
               <span style="font-family: arial;">
                The Verdict.
               </span>
              </b>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               This tasted great! The kids were happy, and Adam happily ate 2 bowls.
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               Now I have one
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_6">
                tupperware
               </span>
               in the fridge instead of seventy-eight gazillion, which makes me happy.
              </span>
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div>
              <span style="font-family: arial;">
               Happy Friday!
              </span>
