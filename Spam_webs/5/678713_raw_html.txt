           <h1>
            Dont Ignore Depression Like Germanwings Pilot Andreas Lubitz; Treat It With These 4 Herbs
           </h1>
          </div>
          <div class="single-author">
           <div class="postdet">
            <p class="pull-left ">
             <span class="time">
             </span>
             Fri, Mar 27th 2015
            </p>
            <p class="pull-left">
             <span class="written">
             </span>
             <a class="undlinetext" href="http://www.zliving.com/author/jonathan-dsouza/">
              Dr. Jonathan D'Souza
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p class="minread">
           4 mins read
          </p>
          <img alt="Andreas lubitz" class="attachment-article-page-760x428 wp-post-image" data-src="http://www.zliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Andreas-lubitz-760x428.jpg" height="428" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAAAAACH5BAEKAAEALAAAAAABAAEAAAICTAEAOw==" width="760"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="Andreas lubitz" class="attachment-article-page-760x428 wp-post-image" height="428" src="http://www.zliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Andreas-lubitz-760x428.jpg" width="760"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="article-content">
           <div>
            <!--&lt;div class="fl100 pull-left"&gt;
                                                            &lt;/div&gt;-->
            <div class="fl100 pull-left contart">
             <p>
              The German co-pilot Andreas Lubitz, responsible for deliberately crashing Germanwings flight 9525 into the Alps killing 149 passengers, was reportedly suffering from burnout syndrome, or
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/conditions/depression-24404/">
               depression
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              An estimated 19 million American adults are living with depression,
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/diseases-conditions/video/depression-conventional-view-bedside-manor-drhoward-goldberg-49770/">
               symptoms
              </a>
              of which include fatigue, insomnia, loss of interest in activities once enjoyed, headaches, feelings of worthlessness, overeating or loss of appetite, and suicidal thoughts.
             </p>
             <p>
              Depression is a psychological problem and you need a number of strategies to deal with it.
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/diseases-conditions/video/depression-antidepressants-48915/">
               Antidepressant medication
              </a>
              , psychotherapy, residential treatment programs and alternative treatments (
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/complementary-alternative-medicine/video/beat-depression-yoga-pose-41268/">
               yoga
              </a>
              ,
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/complementary-alternative-medicine/guide/accupuncture-natural-drug-free-effective-stay-healthy-41733/">
               acupuncture
              </a>
              ,
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/yoga/mindfulness-meditation-vipassana-6943/">
               meditation
              </a>
              and
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/diseases-conditions/video/herbal-remedies-antidepressants-bedside-manor-drkeith-deorio-drhoward-goldberg-49260/">
               herbs
              </a>
              ) are proven to be beneficial in fighting depression and will help you come out of it. (Also read:
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/diseases-conditions/depression-men-cues-germanwings-pilot-andreas-lubitz-53864/">
               How Depression is Different In Men
              </a>
              )
             </p>
             <p>
              Heres a list of effective herbs that can help you maintain your mental balance.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               1.
               <a href="http://www.zliving.com/herbs/st-johns-wort-41114/">
                St Johns Wort
               </a>
              </strong>
              <br/>
              Its the most commonly used herb for depression and treating the conditions accompanying it, such as anxiety, loss of appetite, tiredness and trouble sleeping. Its beneficial in providing
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/diseases-conditions/video/natural-ways-relieve-migraine-headache-46650/">
               relief from migraine
              </a>
              ,
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/natural-remedies/mustard-oil-treatment-muscle-pain-31500/">
               muscle pain
              </a>
              ,
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/conditions/chronic-fatigue-syndrome-36163/">
               chronic fatigue syndrome
              </a>
              and exhaustion. (1,2)
              <br/>
              <strong>
               How To Take It:
              </strong>
              Two to four grams of powdered St. Johns wort shouldbe taken three times a day with water. You can also take it twice a day as a tea made with 1-2tsp of the dried herb. Buy it
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Daps&amp;field-keywords=St.%20John%27s%20Wort" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               here
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               2. German Chamomile
              </strong>
              <br/>
              Useful in treating
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/diseases-conditions/channing-tatum-voices-adhd-43199/">
               attention deficit-hyperactivity disorder
              </a>
              (ADHD), restlessness,
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/diseases-conditions/gallery/guide-4-fibro-friendly-exercises-47232/">
               fibromyalgia
              </a>
              , and
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/complementary-alternative-medicine/gallery/yogic-lullaby-peaceful-sleep-41457/">
               trouble sleeping
              </a>
              , this herb provides relief from anxiety and depression.(3)
              <br/>
              <strong>
               How To Take It:
              </strong>
              Steep a teaspoon of German chamomile flowers for five to 10 minutes and take it twice a day to obtain relief from depression. You can also buy German chamomile supplements
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Natures-Way-Chamomile-Flowers-Capsules/dp/B0006U3QJQ" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               online
              </a>
              and take them twice daily to see noticeable results.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               3. Ashwagandha
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/herbs/ashwagandha-4455/">
               Ashwagandha
              </a>
              has a calming anxiolytic (antianxiety) effect that was comparable to the drug Lorazepam in all three standard anxiety tests. Clinical studies support the use of ashwagandha as a mood stabilizer in clinical conditions of anxiety and depression.(4,5)
              <br/>
              <strong>
               How To Take It:
              </strong>
              Drinking a cup of hot milk containing a teaspoon of powdered ashwagandha before bedtime is beneficial in relieving depression. You canbuy ashwagandha powder
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_noss_1?url=search-alias%3Dhpc&amp;field-keywords=ashwagandha%20powder&amp;sprefix=ashwagandha+pow%2Chpc" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               here
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               4. Ginkgo Biloba
              </strong>
              <br/>
              One of the longest-surviving trees in the world, the extracts of
              <a href="http://www.zliving.com/health/natural-remedies/ginkgo-biloba-powerful-herb-14100/">
               this herb
              </a>
              are used totreat a range of conditions such as depression, anxiety, dizziness, tinnitus, memory and concentration problems, confusion and headache.
              <br/>
              <strong>
               How To Take It:
              </strong>
              Ginkgo supplements can be taken twice daily for up to 12 weeks to find relief from depression symptoms. You can buy the supplements
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_ss_i_1_17?url=search-alias%3Dhpc&amp;field-keywords=ginkgo+supplement&amp;sprefix=ginkgo+supplement%2Chpc%2C1155&amp;rh=n%3A3760901%2Ck%3Aginkgo+supplement" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               here
              </a>
              .
             </p>
