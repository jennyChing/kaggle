                     Best DIY Skin Care for Oily Skin

                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-skEPkDocfrI/UcDaQyzPbTI/AAAAAAAABCw/iQSuaEqg6f8/s1600/IMG_2264.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="524" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-skEPkDocfrI/UcDaQyzPbTI/AAAAAAAABCw/iQSuaEqg6f8/s640/IMG_2264.JPG" width="640"/>

                     If you have skin that is anything like mine, then you suffer from oil overload. For my entire life I have wondered what it would be like to be one of those girls who frequently spoke the words "oh, I need some lotion" or "I just put on my facemoisturizer". Instead, I frequently say things like, "oh, it looks like I just ran a marathon" or "my eyeliner is running like I just got out of the pool".
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     My oily face issues were solved for the most part by some dermatologist prescribed medications, but I recently found out that I am pregnant and the medication isn't really meant to be taken while pregnant. I freaked out of course and started researching home remedies. Lucky for me I stumbled upon this MIRACLE combination. It works EVEN BETTER than the prescriptions I was taking, and is way cheaper, easier, and safe for my baby! The baking soda soaks up the oil and exfoliates, and the aloe heals blemishes as well asmoisturizeswithout adding oil.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <h4>
                      Step 1: Scrub With Baking Soda
                     </h4>
                     <div>
                      Wet face, then gently buff on dry baking soda. It will stick to the moisture already on your skin. Leave on for 2-5 minutes, then rub/rinse off. (I do this when I shower)
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      Do this daily to exfoliate and remove dead skin, dirt, and of course, oil!
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <h4>
                      Step 2: Repair Skin with Aloe
                     </h4>
                     <div>
                      Put a thin layer of aloe vera all over your face. Add more to trouble areas. (I do this after I shower and then let it soak into my skin as I sleep.)
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      It is a myth that people with oily skin don't need to moisturize. Aloe Vera is the perfect moisturizer for oily skin because it doesn't create that greasy feeling. It also reduces skin inflammations and helps the skin heal more rapidly.
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      Good Luck!!
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>

                       Bonus Tip:
                      </b>
                      I also recently switched my foundation to a dry powder matte foundation and it does wonders for my skin!
