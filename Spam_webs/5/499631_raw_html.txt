          These pictures may actually make you appreciate your office desk.

          <p>
           Technology sure has come a long way since the 80s. Back then, a cutting edge desk had a computer (probably an Apple IIe), a big calendar, a clock, writing utensils, notepads, maybe an in/out boxthe works. 20 years later, a look at our work space shows that technology has come a long way in reducing the clutter on our desk.
          </p>
          <p>
           The team at Harvard Innovations labs put together a video encapsulating the history of technology, watching the evolution of the desk from 1984-2014, which pretty much now can exist with just a computer and a smartphone.
          </p>
          <h3 style="text-align: center;">
           1980  The cluttered desk, covered with books, a computer, Rolodex and much more.
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wackulus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/evolution-of-the-desk-designboom-02.jpg">
            <img alt="Evolution of the desk" class="aligncenter wp-image-1137" height="400" src="http://wackulus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/evolution-of-the-desk-designboom-02.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 style="text-align: center;">
          </h3>
          <h3 style="text-align: center;">
           2005  Much better, but I still see a Rolodex.
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wackulus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/evolution-of-the-desk-designboom-03.jpg">
            <img alt="Evolution of the desk" class="aligncenter wp-image-1138" height="400" src="http://wackulus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/evolution-of-the-desk-designboom-03.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
