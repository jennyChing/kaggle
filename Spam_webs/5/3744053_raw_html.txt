                  <p>
                   Wouldn't it be fun to play
                   <em>
                    Family Feud
                   </em>
                   with the topic of Thanksgiving side dishes? If you haven't seen this American TV game show, two sets of extended family members square off against each other to try to predict what the most common responses are for a particular topic. I'm guessing the number-one response for Thanksgiving side dishes would be mashed potatoes or stuffing, but after that, who knows?
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Thanksgiving dinner may be the only holiday meal where we take the main course  for granted, and the real attention is on side dishes. Most people like roast turkey well enough, but it's menu items like mom's famous caramelized sweet potatoes or your favorite version of stuffing that really define this holiday.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Coming up with a top 10 list of Thanksgiving side dishes was a challenge, so let me start by clarifying that I'm defining "side dishes" as things that share the plate with juicy slices of turkey. I'm not including salads or homemade rolls, which I think have their own category, and in my mind, side dishes are most often vegetables, so that's what I'm focusing on. Of course everyone probably has their own picks for a top 10 list of Thanksgiving side dishes, but here are some dishes I'd suggest you take a look at and consider adding to your holiday table. And if I've missed something that you consider a must-have side dish for Thanksgiving, please let me know about it in the comments!
                  </p>
                  <p>
                  </p>

                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    Thanksgiving Side Dish #1: Mashed Potatoes
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   At Thanksgiving mashed potatoes are what's under the delicious homemade gravy, and even if you don't need a recipe to make basic mashed potatoes,
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.feelslikehomeblog.com%2F2009%2F11%2Feasy-thanksgiving-recipes-crock-pot-mashed-potatoes%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Crockpot Mashed Potatoes
                   </a>
                   from Feels Like Home may be an idea you'd find useful. If you'd like something a bit more special, may I suggest
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.lifesambrosia.com%2F2009%2F10%2Fhorseradish-and-sour-cream-mashed-potatoes-recipe.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Horseradish and Sour Cream Mashed Potatoes
                   </a>
                   from Life's Ambrosia, or
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fpinchmysalt.com%2F2008%2F11%2F13%2Fholiday-recipes-with-a-twist-extra-creamy-mashed-potatoes%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Mashed Potato Casserole
                   </a>
                   from Pinch My Salt?
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    Thanksgiving Side Dish #2: Stuffing
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   There are a lot of variations of stuffing, but when I asked people on Twitter about their favorite Thanksgiving side dishes, we give two thumbs up to
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Felise.com%2Frecipes%2Farchives%2F000036moms_turkey_stuffing.php" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Mom's Turkey Stuffing
                   </a>
                   from Simply Recipes. I also like the sound of
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fkitchenparade.com%2F2009%2F11%2Fsausage-stuffing.php" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Nana's Sausage Stuffing
                   </a>
                   from Kitchen Parade,
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.whatwereeating.com%2Frecipes%2Fholiday-recipes-cornbread-sausage-stuffing%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Cornbread and Sausage Stuffing
                   </a>
                   from What We're Eating, and
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.dawnsrecipes.com%2Fwild-rice-stuffing-with-hazelnuts-and-dried-cranberries-26.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Wild Rice Stuffing with Hazelnuts and Dried Cranberries
                   </a>
                   from Dawn's Recipes. A Year of Slow Cooking also has
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fcrockpot365.blogspot.com%2F2008%2F11%2Fperfect-homemade-stuffing-crockpot.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Perfect Homemade Stuffing in a Crockpot
                   </a>
                   .
                  </p>

                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    Thanksgiving Side Dish #3: Green Beans
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Of course, there are those traditional canned green bean casseroles with mushroom soup that some people love, but fresh green beans are what I'd go for as a Thanksgiving option. Lisa is Cooking has a "made from scratch"
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Flisaiscooking.blogspot.com%2F2009%2F11%2Fgreen-bean-casserole.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Green Bean Casserole
                   </a>
                   , Your Home-Based Mom has
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.yourhomebasedmom.com%2Fdinner-party-tropical-green-beans%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Tropical Green Beans
                   </a>
                   , and Andrea Meyers has
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.andreasrecipes.com%2F2007%2F10%2F09%2Fbrown-butter-green-beans-with-almonds%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Brown Butter Greens Beans with Almonds
                   </a>
                   .
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    Thanksgiving Side Dish #4: Sweet Potatoes
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   I'm not a fan of those traditional "candied" sweet potatoes, but I do like the sound of
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.101cookbooks.com%2Farchives%2Fnikkis-sweet-potatoes-recipe.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Nikki's Sweet Potatoes
                   </a>
                   from 101 Cookbooks, which use coconut milk to add flavor. I'm also drooling over
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Ffoodblogga.blogspot.com%2F2009%2F11%2Fcooking-with-beer-rockin-recipe-for.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Roasted Sweet Potatoes with Maple Smoked Bacon and Beer
                   </a>
                   from Food Blogga and
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fveganyumyum.com%2F2007%2F11%2Fcandied-lime-sweet-potatoes%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Candied Lime Sweet Potatoes
                   </a>
                   from Vegan Yum Yum.
                  </p>

                    Lemony Quinoa with Butternut Squash
                   </a>
                   from Fat Free Vegan Kitchen

                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    Thanksgiving Side Dish #5: Butternut Squash
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   I'm a huge fan of butternut squash, and I'd love to see it on the Thanksgiving table whether it was a roasted squash dish like
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.theinadvertentgardener.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F01%2F29%2Fmaple-pecan-roasted-butternut-squash-with-goat-cheese%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Maple-Pecan Roasted Butternut Squash with Goat Cheese
                   </a>
                   from The Inadvertent Gardener, a casserole like
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fdlynz.com%2F%3Fp%3D3391" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Butternut Squash Gratin
                   </a>
                   from Dlyn or something non-traditional like the
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.fatfreevegan.com%2F2009%2F10%2Flemony-quinoa-with-butternut-squash.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Lemony Quinoa with Butternut Squash
                   </a>
                   from Fat Free Vegan Kitchen.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    Thanksgiving Side Dish #6: Brussels Sprouts
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   You might consider Brussels sprouts to be one of those "love 'em or hate 'em" types of vegetables, but in recent years roasted or pan-fried Brussels sprouts dishes have been winning over a lot of Brussels sprouts naysayers. I've shared 10 outstanding recipes for
                   <a href="http://www.blogher.com/weekend-menu-planning-thanksgiving-worthy-brussels-sprouts-take-trial-run">
                    Thanksgiving-Worthy Brussels Sprouts
                   </a>
                   , all of which can help you make the case for Brussels sprouts.
                  </p>

                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    Thanksgiving Side Dish #7: Onions
                   </strong>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Are you surprised to see onions on this list? When I was a kid we always had Onions with Cheese Sauce for Thanksgiving. I certainly think a dish like the
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.msadventuresinitaly.com%2Fblog%2F2007%2F05%2F29%2Fparmigiano-reggiano-stuffed-onions-wrapped-in-prosciutto-di-parma%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Parmigiano-Reggiano-Stuffed Onions Wrapped in Prosciutto di Parma
                   </a>
                   from Ms. Adventures in Italy would make a perfect Thanksgiving side dish. For other onion options, how about
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.diannesdishes.com%2F2008%2F10%2Froasted-balsamic-onions.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Roasted Balsamic Onions
                   </a>
                   from Dianne's Dishes,
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fretro-food.com%2F2009%2F06%2F10%2Fstuffed-onions%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Baked Stuffed Onions
                   </a>
                   from Retro Food, or
                   <a class="external-link elf-add-back-link" href="http://www.blogher.com/frame.php?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.crumblycookie.net%2F2009%2F09%2F28%2Fherb-roasted-onions%2F" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                    Herb Roasted Onions
                   </a>
                   from The Way the Cookie Crumbles?
