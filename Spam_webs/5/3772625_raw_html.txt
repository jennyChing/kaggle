     <p>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       the paint evolution by culdesac for valentine
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      valencia-based designers
      <a href="http://www.culdesac.es/" target="_blank" title="">
       culdesac
      </a>
      have created the paint evolution for the paint company
      <a href="http://www.valentine.es/portalbv/" target="_blank" title="">
       valentine
      </a>
      . their collaborative project
      <br/>
      celebrates the ever changing nature of artistic tools and implements within the valentine brand.
      <br/>
      brushes and rollers in this creative collection feature an interactive, form-as-function approach to the construction of each piece.
      <br/>
      one may anticipate the mark and movement of the implement in an artists hand simply by examining the form of each object.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <em>
       genetic mutations give birth to imaginative instruments: the brush compass, pendulum or whip give us a clue
       <br/>
       to the behavior of paint through sketches and through history
      </em>
      -culdesac
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="612" src="/weblog/images/images_2/leigha/paint%20evolution/1.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       <br/>
       the whip cephalus is an essential species in the action painting technique
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="528" src="/weblog/images/images_2/leigha/paint%20evolution/2.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       a brush which allows for painting by hand
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="625" src="/weblog/images/images_2/leigha/paint%20evolution/3.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       a brush in which the painter moves the brush with a clenched fist
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="589" src="/weblog/images/images_2/leigha/paint%20evolution/collection3.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       a passive painterly approach may be reached by applying color to the top of this paintbrush balloon and allowing the piece to float
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="591" src="/weblog/images/images_2/leigha/paint%20evolution/10.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       <br/>
       formacterus was developed due to several geometric mutations, today evolved to photoshop
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="576" src="/weblog/images/images_2/leigha/paint%20evolution/5.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       the circle dipus brush is the parent of the perfect circle
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="638" src="/weblog/images/images_2/leigha/paint%20evolution/collection9.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       <br/>
       the base to this brush allows for careful contemplation between each stroke
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="" height="599" src="/weblog/images/images_2/leigha/paint%20evolution/collection5.jpg" title="" width="818"/>
      <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
       <br/>
       with the rolling, angled sponge brush, the painter is able to seemlessly paint corners
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
