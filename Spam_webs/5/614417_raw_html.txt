          <a class="cboxModal" href="https://racingnews.co/files/2014/02/Renault-Kwid-Concept-car-Interior-CARS-.jpg" rel="lightbox[7084]" title="CARS: Renault Kwid Concept Car">
           <img alt="Renault Kwid Concept Car Interior ( CARS )" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-7085" height="200" src="https://racingnews-walterswebdesign.netdna-ssl.com/files/2014/02/Renault-Kwid-Concept-car-Interior-CARS--300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          <h2>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            Renault Kwid Concept Car
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
            The Renault Kwid is the first Renault concept car to be unveiled outside Europe, at the Delhi Auto Show 2014. It showcases the companys commitment to new markets like India, Russia or Brazil. Robust and hyper connected, thanks to its flying companion, Kwid aims to meet the needs of young customers in these markets.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Renault Kwid
           </strong>
           was designed with inputs from
           <strong>
            Renault Design India
           </strong>
           and is covered by the Explore petal of Renaults lifecycle-based design strategy.
          </p>
          <div class="ad-box-responsive">
           <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-8911975381026287" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="1313449637" style="display:block;">
           </ins>
           <script>
            (adsbygoogle=window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
           </script>
          </div>
          <p>
           Itsprotective guards, chunky wings,very short overhangs and oversized wheels give this compact concept a robust appearance. The Renault
           <strong>
            Kwid
           </strong>
           comes with a two-wheel drive transmission, but projects the facade of an off-road car.
          </p>
          <p>
           The interior of the
           <strong>
            Renault Kwid
           </strong>
           is designed to serve as a snug cocoon and is inspired by a birds nest. The vehicles seats, suspended over a solid base in white, give an impression of lightness, while their 2-tone elastomer material provides a sense of brightness.
           <strong>
            RenaultKwid Concept
           </strong>
           also features rear air conditioning with controls on the driver seats back as well as ventilation through concentric perforations. The dashboard is an integrated tablet for commanding the navigation of the
           <strong>
            Flying Companion
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           The
           <strong>
            Flying Companion
           </strong>
           is the first of its kind in the automotive world. Taking off from the rotating rear portion of the Renault
           <strong>
            Kwid
           </strong>
           roof, it can scout traffic, take landscape pictures and detect obstacles on the road ahead.
           <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Renault Kwid
           </strong>
           is also Z.E ready and can receive batteries for electric use. It ispowered by the latest-generation downsized 1.2-litre petrol turbocharged engine mated to dual-clutch transmission EDC. Other technology-rich features include electric operated front bench seat on a center rail, electric opening doors, LED lighting, lack of gearshift (command with touches next to the steering). The vertically-mounted TFT touchscreen display acts as the dashboard in Renault
           <strong>
            Kwid Concept
           </strong>
           and also provides access to connected services.
          </p>
          <div>
           <h4>
            International, from conception to revelation
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Renault Kwid
            </strong>
            is an internationalconcept car, conceived by a team of international designers, coming from France, India, Russia, Japanand Brazil.
           </p>
           <p>
            The first Renault concept car to be unveiled outside Europe, Renault
            <strong>
             Kwid
            </strong>
            also emphasises Indias significant place in Renaults global strategy as well as the companys ability to offer appealing products in the compact car segment.
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             This is the first time we have chosen to reveal a concept car outside Europe and this is an eloquent sign of our commitment to India. Young customers in India are often trend setters, looking forward to pushing the envelope when it comes to technology and enjoyable drives. Renault
             <strong>
              Kwid
             </strong>
             with its Flying Companion, meets this forward-looking spirit with both its dynamic styling and hyper connectivity.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            
            <b>
             Gilles Normand, Chairman of Asia-Pacific Region, Renault
            </b>
           </p>

             Renault
            </a>
           </p>
