          There are so many different knives. There are different brands, styles, cuts and sizes. If youre new to cooking or you just want to clean out your kitchen cabinet of all the things you dont really use, this guide will help you know where to begin.
         </p>
         <p>
          This Paleo Guide To Knives will
          <strong>
           explain the only knives you really need in your kitchen, and break down the reasons why all the other knives really just take up space
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-22767">
          </span>
          I came across this really great infographic that explains all the different knives (see below), how each knife is used and how to maintain each one. I was going to just
          <a href="http://pinterest.com/paleoporn">
           pin it

         <h3>
          The Only Knife You Need
         </h3>
         <p>
          Truthfully,
          <strong>
           I feel you only need one knife.
          </strong>
          Let me explain first by talking a little about each of the knives you dont need.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <em>
           Not Needed
          </em>
          : Bread Knife
         </h3>
         <p>
          I think this one is the most obvious with regard to the Paleo lifestyle, but since youre not eating
          <a href="http://paleoporn.net/breads/">
           bread
          </a>
          , youll no longer need a bread knife. Its as easy as that.
         </p>
         <p>
          I will admit that I do own a bread knife because I owned one B.P. (Before Paleo) when I used to make my own bread at home, but now it is only used to cut whole watermelon.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <em>
           Not Needed
          </em>
          : Boning Knife
         </h3>
         <p>
          Unless you filet your own
          <a href="http://paleoporn.net/baked-cod/">
           fish
          </a>
          often, youre not going to need to own a boning knife.
         </p>
         <p>
          A boning knife is a sharp knife with a narrow blade. Its used just as the name claims, for removing bones from poultry, meat and fish.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres an easy way to bypass needing this type of knife. Ask your butcher to filet your fish and debone any cuts for you before you leave the store, processor or farm.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <em>
           Not Needed
          </em>
          : Cleaver
         </h3>
         <p>
          Most of the uses for a cleaver fall into cutting large pieces of meat and bone, such as spatchcocking a chicken.
         </p>
         <p>
          I have never owned a cleaver. I have made do with my kitchen sheers and other knives for most additional uses for a cleaver aside from those already listed.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <em>
           Not Needed
          </em>
          : Paring Knife
         </h3>
         <p>
          A paring knife is used for mincing, peeling, coring and removing partial pieces of potatoes and other vegetables.
         </p>
         <p>
          The thing is, if you have a
          <a href="http://www.pamperedchef.com/shop/Cook%27s+Tools/Serrated+Peeler/1072">
           peeler
          </a>
          then youll probably use that for the task, like I do.
         </p>
         <p>
          With mincing, you can do that with your typical everyday knife.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <em>
           Not Needed
          </em>
          : Utility Knife
         </h3>
         <p>
          Utility knives are used for peeling, slicing and trimming.
         </p>
         <p>
          They can have a straight or serrated edge, though like the paring knife, I find that I can use my everyday knife to do all those things and therefore I dont own a utility knife either.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <em>
           Not Needed
          </em>
          : Santoku Knife
         </h3>
         <p>
          The Santoku knife is a multi-purpose knife. They are typically lighter, thinner and sharper than most Western-style knives.
         </p>
         <p>
          Since this is a multi-purpose knife, I tend to use my Chefs knife regularly and therefore dont need another everyday knife. Youre only going to hold one knife in your hand at a time so multiples of the same knife arent really necessary.
         </p>
         <h3>
          <em>
           Not Needed
          </em>
          : Every Specialty Cutting Item. Ever
         </h3>
         <p>
          Let me explain. Have you seen the pineapple corer and the apple corer and the avocado peeler and the egg slicer andandandnone of these items are truly necessary. Whether youre trying to pare down or just starting from scratch please do not start out thinking you need all these specialty items.

         <h2>
          What Knife Do You Need?
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>

          </strong>
          No, I didnt make that name up, thats really the name of the type of knife.
         </p>
         <p>
          As Ive mentioned in several sections above, the Chefs knife is a multi-purpose tool that can be used for chopping, slicing, mincing, dicing, and depending on the size, peeling as well. Sizes range from 6 to 14 inches.

         <h3>
          Basic Care For Your Knife
         </h3>
         <p>
          When it comes to caring for your knife theres only a few basic things you need to know.
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <strong>
            A sharp knife is the best knife.
           </strong>
          </li>
          <p>
           Brands really dont matter as much as keeping it sharp. Find an individual or business nearby you can rely on to help keep your knives sharp and youll lose less blood.
          </p>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Hand wash only.
           </strong>
          </li>
          <p>
           Putting your knives in the dishwasher will diminish the blade and if you refer to number 1 above, you want to keep it sharp, so dont do that. Hand wash the knife with a little soap and hot water.
          </p>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Store your knife carefully.
           </strong>
          </li>
          <p>
           If your knife came with a cover, youll want to protect your knife from getting nicked by other utensils in the drawer. An easy fix for this is to not overcrowd your drawer and to store your knife covered.
           <em>
            Since I only use one knife, my 6 Chefs knife lives on my cutting board on my counter top at all times.
           </em>
          </p>
         </ol>
