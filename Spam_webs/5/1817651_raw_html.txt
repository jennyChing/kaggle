       <div class="page-title">
        <h1>
         Its a marathon, not a sprint
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="articleFunctions">
        <div class="hours">
         <span>
          1 year ago
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="divider">
         |
        </div>
        <div class="post-title-author">
         <span>
          <span class="hidden-phone">
           Author:
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.debtfreedirect.co.uk/making-money-go-further/author/dfd_wp_admin_blog">
           MMGF Team
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="comments">
         <img alt="Comments" src="http://www.debtfreedirect.co.uk/making-money-go-further/dfd_blog_content/themes/dfd_blog/App_Graphics/comment-icon-grad.png" title="Comments"/>
         <span>
          (0)
         </span>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="row-fluid">
       <div class="span12">
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Signing up to a marathon is a big commitment, one many of us make at the start of a new year. It is also a big commitment many fail to fulfil. According to marathon directors, up to 75% of people who sign up through commercial training programmes or charity ones, drop out.
         </p>
         <p>
          Why is this? Well a common conclusion is goal management.
         </p>
         <p>
          Towards the end of last year, we undertook a survey looking at attitudes towards New Years Resolutions. One of the key things that came out of it was people saying they set unrealistic goals and didnt plan properly; making a decision that is very much spur of the moment.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.debtfreedirect.co.uk/debt-management/" title="Say No to New Year's Resolution">
           As weve already seen earlier this month
          </a>
          , health and fitness is a popular January resolution. It is perhaps this motive that encourages some to blindly sign themselves up to completing a marathon  despite having never run one before, or even taken running seriously.
         </p>
         <p>
          So, whats the solution? Well weve been having a good look around and experts suggest planning, and setting realistic goals is key and weve been sharing even more tips on
          <a href="https://twitter.com/MMGFblog" target="_blank" title="@MMGFblog">
           @MMGFblog
          </a>
          and
          <a href="https://twitter.com/search?q=%23ImProving2014" target="_blank" title="#ImProving2014">
           #ImProving2014
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           A few pointers
          </b>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Some people estimate that it can take a novice runner 18 months to properly prepare for a marathon. But getting to that stage requires a plan and a fair amount of willpower to stick to it.
         </p>
         <p>
          The biggest hurdle is conditioning the body to be ready for long distance running. A lot of sources will encourage tackling a shorter run  perhaps a 10k, then a half marathon, before taking on the big goal.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.debtfreedirect.co.uk/making-money-go-further/dfd_blog_content/uploads/2014/01/running.jpg">
           <img alt="running" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-1301" height="339" src="http://www.debtfreedirect.co.uk/making-money-go-further/dfd_blog_content/uploads/2014/01/running-605x339.jpg" width="605"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p id="yui_3_11_0_4_1388665639920_674">
          Photo source: Living Fitness UK
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          A training plan, i.e. running a set amount of miles a week will help the conditioning of the body. But remember to pace yourself. Dont get too excited as youll run the risk of an injury.
         </p>
         <p>
          But remember to reward yourself. At each achieved goal, give yourself a reward, something to make the short term goals more enticing.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <b>
           Attitudes to debt
          </b>
         </h2>
         <p>
          We have noticed a similarity between preparing for a marathon and
          <a href="http://www.debtfreedirect.co.uk/debt-management/" target="_blank">
           debt management
          </a>
          . Both are big goals and the best way to achieve them is by taking them a step at a time (excuse the horrible pun). Taking on too much too soon can be daunting and lead to failure. Setting a plan, sticking to it and making small progress will help you on your way.
         </p>
         <p>
          The initial stage is finding the best route for you  both in terms of debt solution and race. For debt advice, check our
          <a href="http://www.debtfreedirect.co.uk/debt-management/" target="_blank">
           debt management
          </a>
          page.
         </p>
