         <p>
          Following up on one of the most popular articles ever published on Urban Ghosts  examining
          <a href="http://www.urbanghostsmedia.com/2013/11/13-abandoned-stations-disused-platforms-london-underground/" target="_blank">
           13 abandoned London Underground stations and platforms
          </a>
           weve returned to the Tube to explore more of its forgotten subterranean spaces, and some above-ground ones too.Throughout Londons abandoned underground, entire ghost stations coexist alongsidemiles of forgotten, long-sealed tunnels and disused platforms eerily hidden within stations that otherwise remain active. Discerning commuters may notice large steel gatesto nowhere, beyond which lingers a murky urban underworld unexplored for decades. But for the most part, those using the 151-year-old service remain blissfully unaware of its darker nooks and crannies. This article examines 10 more abandoned Tube stations, platforms, buildings and tunnels.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Kings Cross St. Pancras (Original Metropolitan Line Station)
         </h3>
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="kings-cross-st-pancras-abandoned-metropolitan-line-station" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-23139" height="408" src="http://www.urbanghostsmedia.com/home/twamoran/urbanghostsmedia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/kings-cross-st-pancras-abandoned-metropolitan-line-station.jpg" width="640"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.urbanghostsmedia.com/2014/09/abandoned-london-underground-10-derelict-tube-stations-deserted-platforms/&amp;media=http://www.urbanghostsmedia.com/home/twamoran/urbanghostsmedia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/kings-cross-st-pancras-abandoned-metropolitan-line-station.jpg&amp;description=Abandoned London Underground: 10 (More) Derelict Tube Stations and Deserted Platforms')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="kings-cross-st-pancras-abandoned-metropolitan-line-station-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-23140" height="418" src="http://www.urbanghostsmedia.com/home/twamoran/urbanghostsmedia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/kings-cross-st-pancras-abandoned-metropolitan-line-station-2.jpg" width="640"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.urbanghostsmedia.com/2014/09/abandoned-london-underground-10-derelict-tube-stations-deserted-platforms/&amp;media=http://www.urbanghostsmedia.com/home/twamoran/urbanghostsmedia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/kings-cross-st-pancras-abandoned-metropolitan-line-station-2.jpg&amp;description=Abandoned London Underground: 10 (More) Derelict Tube Stations and Deserted Platforms')">
           </span>
          </span>
          <em>
           (Images:
           <a href="https://www.flickr.com/search?user_id=24772733%40N05&amp;sort=relevance&amp;text=st%20pancras" target="_blank">
            bowroaduk
           </a>
           , see
           <a href="http://www.blurb.co.uk/b/1896269-london-underground-review-of-2010?redirect=true" target="_blank">
            book
           </a>
           )
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its no secret that busy stations on Londons incredible underground railway network contain hidden corners, long sealed passageways, abandoned sidings and disused platforms. The bustling Kings Cross St. Pancras Tube station is no exception. These photographs reveal the silent remains of the first Kings Cross platforms to open in 1863, a
          <a href="http://www.abandonedstations.org.uk/Kings_Cross_station.html" target="_blank">
           sub-surface station
          </a>
          serving the Metropolitan line and City Widened lines, which were later taken over by
          <a href="http://www.abandonedstations.org.uk/Kings_X_Thameslink.html" target="_blank">
           Thameslink
          </a>
          trains. The original Metropolitan line station was relocated in 1941 to enable a more convenient interchange between the Kings Cross mainline station and deep level Tube lines. The 19th century platforms and running tunnels were thus abandoned, separated by a wall from the Thameslink station which was itself closed in 2007 after being relocated beneath the mainline St Pancras terminal.
         </p>
