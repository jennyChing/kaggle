         <h1>
          10 Electronic Music Documentaries You Probably Haven't Seen Before
         </h1>
         <div class="date-time-container" data-publish-date="1389061740">
          <span class="date">
           January 7, 2014
          </span>
          <span class="time">
           2:29 AM
          </span>
          <span class="byline">
           <a href="//thump.vice.com/en_uk/contributor/vivian-host">
            <span class="by">
             By
            </span>
            Vivian Host
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <section class="share-widget facebook-twitter" data-media="https://thump-images.vice.com/images/articles/meta/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422359014964.jpg?crop=0.5464566929133858xw:1xh;center,top&amp;output-quality=75" data-title="10 Electronic Music Documentaries You Probably Haven't Seen Before" data-topic="words" data-tweet="10 Electronic Music Documentaries You Probably Haven't Seen Before" data-url="https://thump.vice.com/en_uk/article/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see" id="">
          <ul>
           <li class="facebook" data-share-type="facebook">
            <span class="site-icon facebook-logo">
            </span>
            <!--
-->
            <span class="share-text">
             Share
            </span>
           </li>
           <!--
-->
           <li class="twitter" data-share-type="twitter">
            <span class="site-icon twitter-logo">

           </li>

         </section>
         <div class="rich-text">
          <p>
           <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360511115.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           While Netflix continues to suck a fat one, YouTube is actually becoming the best resource for watching cool movies. Below are a selection of tight electronic music documentaries - many from that
           <em>
            other
           </em>
           time that electronic music blew up, and was supposed to save the world (ahem, the 1990s).
          </p>
          <p>
           From loved-up soccer hooligans (and dads), to 24-hour-party-people, to a hungover Luciano and a pre-teen Hawtin, here's a selection of documentaries on various beautiful, wonderful niches of this big, wide raving world we live in.
          </p>
          <p>
           Note: Many foreign language docs have English subtitles. Hit the "Close Caption" button on the bottom right of the video, and away you go!
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             GABBER
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360517257.jpg"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            No matter how hard EDM gets, it will never be as hard as the original Dutch gabber scene. Never. In 1995, Lola de Musica chronicled the Rotterdam hardcore scene - the daddy to today's hardstyle craze - in this awesome documentary, featuring mental gabbers in equally mentaltrack suitsdoing hakken, and gurning Dutch raverswith saucer eyes. There's also the legend Paul Elstak and DJ Ruffneck of Ruffneck Alliance, who looks straight haunted, b -he's like gabber game
           </span>
           <em>
            Interview With A Vampire.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iO1oLIOS-kY#t=309" target="_blank">
            <span>
             WATCH IT HERE
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <b>
             DON'T FORGET TO GO HOME (FEIERN)
            </b>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360524666.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Once upon a time, Berlin was not filled with Canadian grahpci designers and American DJs, but with Germans. These Germans found it perfectly normal to stay up for 72 hours on weekends, getting completely off their tree at clubs like Berghain (often referred to as Ostgut, after the label with which it's associated), Watergate, Club Der Visonaere, Bar 25... and then go back to work on Monday like nothing happened. Filmed in 2006, this documentary explores the effect of the techno lifestyle on one's whole life - identity, relationships, emotions, career - and shows the highs and lows of the commitment to clubbing. Mixing up Berlin locals, it also manages to showcase a softer side of Luciano, Villalobos, Nick Hppner, Ewan Pearson, and more.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ogspER2e7h0" target="_blank">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             MUSIC IS MY DRUG
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360531439.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            It's true that an image is worth a thousand words. This documentary about psychedelic trance starts off with a naked man doing a headstand next to a Brahma cow, which basically just screams "TRIPPER" one thousand times over. It's got everything you would want from film about psychedelics; from a tour of the Roland synthesiser factory, to Japanese psy-trance heads with the most amazing clothes, consciousness-raising Israeli trance "situations" and rabbis, to lots of naked fools in Goa. It's allset to the
           </span>
           <em>
            Mortal Kombat-esque
           </em>
           <span>
            sounds of psy-trance. A seriously good movie.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS6vRNnt21w" target="_blank">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             <span>
              WHAT IF YOU DID E WITH YOUR DAD
             </span>
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360537799.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This one will fuck you up; a rural Northern California family where the dad, deep in the throes of a mid-life crisis, gets way into going to raves and popping pills-with his kids. His house turns into the raver party house, his ex-wife is freaking out, and he's even convinced his 13-year-old son to join in. I wouldn't call this
           <em>
            fun
           </em>
           to watch. It's more of a horror movie, frankly.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=madKm20lEfE" target="_blank">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             E IS FOR ECSTASY
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360544457.jpg"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Once upon a time, ecstasy was actually made of pure MDMA, as you can tell from this film which - despite feeling like a PSA/Just Say No ad campaign from the way it's shot - extols the psychological and cultural virtues of the pill. That's nice and everything, but the real fun of watching this is seeing the clothes, hairstyles, attitudes and dances of the early '90s. It seems so earnest in these sordidtimes.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=a349t8i3kN" target="_blank">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             JUNGLE NATION
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360551378.jpg"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            Many docs about drum &amp; bass are just producers pontificating about how musical and future-forward the genre is, but not this one. This documentary celebrates 20 years of jungle raving at UK party Jungle Nation, and it's shot by DJ, producer, label owner and scene stalwart Teebone. It features plenty of over-40 jungle legends getting mash-up, proving that you never have to stop. There are appearances by Jumpin' Jack Frost, MC Navigator, GQ, and lots more, and if you make it through the entire hour and a half, you know you're hardcore.
           </span>
           <span>
            Reeeeeeewind.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WXHDmng6-Tg" target="_blank">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             SUMMER OF RAVE
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360557950.jpg"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Don't let the nafftitle stop you from watching this well-researched BBC documentary about acid house in the UK, featuring plenty of people old enough to be your parents. It's full of lots of interesting facts: such as how raves converted the London football hooligans from fighting each other to loving each other, and how Gatecrasher started as an organisation throwing teen dances.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-XrlMpwEuM" target="_blank">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             SUB BERLIN: THE STORY OF TRESOR
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360564999.jpg"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Tresor is a glorious, dank, dark box of hard, pounding techno - the very kindyour parents warned you against. This documentary tells the history of the Berlin institution, which sprang up after the Berlin Wall came down and still runs today. Local scene legends give up the story, and you just might learn a thing or too about recent German political history in the process.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U9E59b3rli0" target="_blank">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             ELECTRONIC MUSIC PIONEERS: RICHIE HAWTIN
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360571666.jpg"/>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Don't even try to tell me you're a Richie Hawtin fan unless you've watched all 70 minutes of this. Including interviews with Derrick May, Magda, Mr. &amp; Mrs. Hawtin, and Richie himself, there'sembarrassing childhood photos and even a breakdown of the infamous Sven Vath andHawtin make-out incident, all somehow paid for with T-Mobile's money. Essential.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bc6474KUBV8">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             <span>
              23 MINUTE WARNING: SPIRAL TRIBE
             </span>
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span>
             <img class="vmp-image" src="//staging-thump-images.vice.com/images/2015/1/27/10-electronic-music-documentaries-you-must-see-1422360579564.jpg"/>
            </span>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           In 1990, around the same time a nascent Burning Man was getting off its feet, the Spiral Tribe Soundsystem were in the UK revolutionsing the rave. A sort of techno version of Crass, these dance punks transporting huge rigs all over the UK to throw massive free parties, showing that the act of raving could be both political and transformative, and all the while throwing a big middle finger up to Parliament. This documentary finds the clan talking about what makes them tick. Inspirational.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RuOFQThcrqw">
            WATCH IT HERE
           </a>
          </p>
