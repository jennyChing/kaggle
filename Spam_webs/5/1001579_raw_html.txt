     <span id="print_title">
      <h2>
       Beijing Walking Tour: Hou Hai
      </h2>
     </span>
     <p class="title">
      <span id="print_date">
       2014-05-28
      </span>
      <span style="padding-right:10px;">
       <a href="javascript:printArticle();">
        <img alt="print" src="http://www.visitbeijing.com.cn/upload/cn_visitbj/new/print_btn.gif"/>
       </a>
      </span>
     </p>
     <div class="cont" id="cont">
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        Hou Hai is a manmade lakethe largest of three consecutive lakes lined with willow trees and surrounded by a rambling maze of hutongs, shops, and cafes. Start at the former residence of Madame Soong, on the northwest bank, just east of Deshengmennei Dajie. Dusk is a beautiful time to visit, since the courtyard stays lit throughout the evening.
       </font>
      </p>
      <table align="center" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
       <tbody>
        <tr>
         <td align="center">
          <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
           <tr>
            <td>
             <img alt="Beijing Walking Tour: Hou Hai" src="http://images.visitbeijing.com.cn/20140528/Img215003111.jpg"/>
            </td>
           </tr>
          </table>
          <a href="http://images.visitbeijing.com.cn/20140528/Img215003111.jpg">
          </a>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </tbody>
      </table>
      <br/>
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        Walk south along the northern edge of the lake to Silver Ingot Bridge (Yinding Qiao) before crossing the bridge, turning left and then a quick right onto
        <span class="link">
         <a href="http://english.visitbeijing.com.cn/shopping/markets/n214694406.shtml" target="_blank">
          Yandai Xiejie
         </a>
        </span>
        , or Slanted Pipe Lane, an alleyway formerly known for a shop that sold long Chinese pipes. The pipes that are left are souvenirs, but the streetwith its wooden pushcarts, snack vendors, and courtyard homes with lacquered red doorwaysis still a charming way to reach the Drum Tower on Gulou Dajie.
       </font>
      </p>
      <table align="center" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
       <tbody>
        <tr>
         <td align="center">
          <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
           <tr>
            <td>
             <img alt="Beijing Walking Tour: Hou Hai" src="http://images.visitbeijing.com.cn/20140528/Img215003112.jpg"/>
            </td>
           </tr>
          </table>
          <a href="http://images.visitbeijing.com.cn/20140528/Img215003112.jpg">
          </a>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </tbody>
      </table>
      <br/>
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        If you're hungry along the way, stop at any of the street vendors on Yandai Xiejie and try Beijing treats, from pork dumplings to CD-sized scallion or meat-stuffed pancakes, to a multilayered, crispy-chewy crepe, to the steamed giant flatbread that could feed four. If you're thirsty, walk north along Jiugoulou Dajie and turn right onto Doufuchi Hutong. Number 43 is Caf Sambal, a breezy, trendy bar and restaurant good for an iced tea or cocktail.
       </font>
      </p>
      <table align="center" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
       <tbody>
        <tr>
         <td align="center">
          <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">

      <br/>
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        A nominal admission fee (about $2.50) gives you access to the Drum Tower, and the steep, ancient steps lead to the fabulous view of the lakes at the top. Find your way back to the Silver Ingot Bridget, a white marble bridge that divides Hou Hai from Qian Hai ("front lake").
       </font>
      </p>
      <table align="center" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
       <tbody>

          <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">

        </tr>

      </table>
      <br/>
      <p>
       <font face="arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
        Cross the bridge and turn right onto Hou Hai Nanyan, Hou Hai's willow-shaded southern frontage. Turn left onto Liuyin Jie; your final destination is
        <span class="link">
         <a href="http://english.visitbeijing.com.cn/sightseeing/n214673078.shtml" target="_blank">
          Prince Gong's Mansion
         </a>
        </span>
        , the former home for Emperor Qianlong and Heshen, his corrupt eunuch. The house and grounds are extensive, although not entirely accessible. The pricier admission fee (about $8) includes a performance of opera and acrobatics in the Mansion's Grand Opera House.
       </font>
      </p>
