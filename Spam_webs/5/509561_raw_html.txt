       <h2>
        Clem Kadiddlehopper  Red Skeltons country bumpkin character
       </h2>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Clem Kadiddlehopper
        </strong>
        was one of
        <a href="http://red-skelton.info/biographies/red-skelton-biography/" title="Red Skelton biography">
         <strong>
          Red Skeltons
         </strong>
        </a>
        most popular characters, portrayed on radio and television for several decades. Clem Kadiddlehopper was a country bumpkin, slow witted and big hearted
        <span id="more-24">
        </span>
        . Although never intentionally hurtful, he would often cause clownish harm, typically to his long-suffering father. (When the stork brought you, Clem, I shouldve shot it on sight! )
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://red-skelton.info/wp-content/uploads/red-skelton-clem-kadiddlehopper-radio.jpeg">
         <img alt="Clem Kadiddlehopper, Red Skelton's country bumpkin character" class="size-full wp-image-387 alignleft" height="256" src="http://red-skelton.info/wp-content/uploads/red-skelton-clem-kadiddlehopper-radio.jpeg" style="margin: 5px;" width="197"/>
        </a>
        Clem Kadiddlehopper was one of Red Skeltons most versatile characters, often placed in a rural setting
        <strong>
         to contrast Clems down-home values with higher society.
        </strong>
        Examples include when Clems worthless paintings become valuable impressionistic art (
        <a href="http://red-skelton.info/dvd-collection/amc-red-skelton-show-14-episodes-red-skeltons-tv-series/" title="AMC  The Red Skelton Show  14 episodes of Red Skeltons TV series">
         Clem the Artist
        </a>
        , a hilarious episode), when Clem sells his familys fountain of youth water and becomes a Hollywood make up artist (guest starring Audrey Meadows of the Honeymooners), or when Clem turns the tables on a pair of city slickers trying to con Clem and his family out of their oil-rich land.
       </p>
       <p>
        Depending on the needs of the story, Clem was either afraid of girls or actively chasing them, living on the family farm or working in town (such as in 
        <a href="http://red-skelton.info/dvd-collection/amc-red-skelton-show-14-episodes-red-skeltons-tv-series/" title="AMC  The Red Skelton Show  14 episodes of Red Skeltons TV series">
         Fish Market
        </a>
         )
       </p>
       <h2>
        Trivia about Red Skeltons Clem Kadiddlehopper character:
       </h2>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Rep
         <img alt="Red Skelton's painting of Clem" class="alignright size-full wp-image-388" height="235" src="http://red-skelton.info/wp-content/uploads/clem-kadiddlehopper-painting-red-skelton.jpeg" style="margin: 5px;" width="215"/>
         resentatives for Red Skelton contacted the producers of 
         <strong>
          The Rocky and Bullwinkle Show
         </strong>
          claiming that Bullwinkles voice was an unauthorized use of a character voice created and owned by Red Skelton for his character Clem Kadiddlehopper. Producer Jay Ward responded by having a segment on the show
         <strong>
          where Bullwinkle would address the issue in a voice that was a deliberate imitation of Clem Kadiddlehopper
         </strong>
         .
        </li>
       </ul>
