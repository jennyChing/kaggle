         <strong>
          RABI UL AWWAL
         </strong>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        Rabi ul Awwal is the 3rd month in islamic calender. Islamic calender is based on the movement of moon. This month is believed to be the luckiest and very blessed month as Rabi ul Awwal means beggining of spring as its position in Arabian pre-Islamic Calender . It holds a special value as Sunni Muslims believe that the birth of their beloved last Holy Prophet Muhammad (S) was on 12th of Rabi ul Awwal, while some Shia Muslims believe it was on 17th of Rabi ul Awwal. Allah knows the best. Muslims celeberate this month with spirit and love.
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <strong>
          CELEBRATIONS MADE FOR RABI UL AWWAL
         </strong>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <b>
        </b>
        Birthday of Holy Prophet Muhammad (s) is celebrated on 17th and 12th of rabi ul awwal as it is not much confirmed. It is celebrated differently according to the country, in some countries celebrations are held throughout the month while in some only for the week and some celebrate the birthDAY only.
        <br/>
        Some Muslims consider Rabi ul Awwal as the 3rd eif while other two are Eid ul Adha and Eid ul Fitar. But Many of the Muslims dont celebrate Birthday of their Beloved Last Holy Prophet as it is not evidenced about celebrating this day in Quran nor is it mentioned in Hadith.
       </p>

         </strong>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        As Islamic Calender is Lunar Calender which is based on the Moon, it gets 11 to 12 days shorter each year from the solar calender. it is 1437AH according to Islamic Calender.
       </p>
