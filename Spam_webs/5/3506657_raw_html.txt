       <section class="photo">
        <p>
         <img alt="TOWIE, Jessica Wright, psoriasis" src="http://cdn.images.express.co.uk/img/dynamic/11/590x/24f31jess-484381.jpg"/>

       <section class="text-description">
        <p class="storycopy">
         I was driving on the M25, going home from university when a tyre burst and I flew across three lanes of traffic before hitting a barrier and coming back again. I landed in the middle lane, facing all the lorries and traffic.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         It was terrifying. I thought I was going to have a million cars on top of me, she says.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Yet miraculously the wall of oncoming traffic swerved around Jess as she sat, shaken and stunned, in her wrecked car. The paramedic couldnt believe that I wasnt seriously hurt, she recalls. I walked away with only whiplash. Or so she thought at the time.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         A few weeks later Jess, now 28, began to suffer panic attacks. Suddenly I couldnt breathe and my limbs felt really heavy. At one point I had three in one day.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Around the same time angry red patches began to appear on her scalp and Jess knew straight away that it was psoriasis, an inflammatory skin condition which afflicts around 1.8 million people in the UK.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Psoriasis occurs when the production of skin cells goes into overdrive and creates crusty patches of red flaky skin which are covered with silvery scales. It is caused by a glitch in the immune system and tends to run in families. Like Jess, one in three people with psoriasis has a close relation with the condition.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         It can be triggered by an injury to the skin, certain medicines, bad habits such as smoking and drinking to excess or, as happened with Jess, stress.
        </p>
       </section>
       <section class="pull-quote on-right">
        <blockquote class="nointellitxt ctx_blocked" id="nointellitxt">
         <p class="quote">
          On the red carpet it's all I can focus on
         </p>
         <p class="author">
          Jessica Wright
         </p>
        </blockquote>
       </section>
       <section class="text-description">
        <p class="storycopy">
         She says: For as long as I can remember my dad has suffered from severe psoriasis and I had always dreaded it happening to me. I was devastated when I got it because I had seen him struggle with it for years.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         However Jess is quick to point out that her psoriasis is mild compared to the problems her father and many others experience. At the moment my arms are fine and I just have a little on my head and ears.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         For some, psoriasis can be intensely damaging because it undermines self-esteem and can trigger anxiety and depression.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         As many as one in five people with the condition will also develop psoriatic arthritis which leads to pain, swelling and stiffness in the joints.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Jess says that being in the spotlight and having a career that is so focused on appearance magnifies the problem. There have been times when Ive been photographed on a red carpet showing my arms and the psoriasis is all I can focus on. Im sure nobody else has been able to spot it but its all I can see.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         When it flares up she avoids wearing black because she worries that the flakes of skin will be too obvious and the condition can play havoc with her fake tan, an essential for any self-respecting Essex girl.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         When its flaking you end up with white patches which I have to cover with make-up if Im doing a shoot, she explains.
        </p>

        </p>

         Its also one she is well qualified to pursue. Jess has a degree in business management and marketing from Westminster University in London and her dissertation looked at celebrity endorsements.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         It was around the time Kylie and Jennifer Lopez were bringing out their own fragrances, way before TOWIE, she adds.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         The influence of celebrity is the reason she has decided to contribute to a special e-book called Everyday Psoriasis which provides personal accounts of living with the condition along with tips and advice on how to manage it.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         The guide, published by Exorex, a health care company which makes a range of psoriasis treatments, also includes a chapter from comedian and writer Toby Hadoke who has severe psoriasis and admits he was always dubious of the stress thing. However he says he didnt get on top of his condition until undergoing dermo-psychology treatment at Londons Royal Free Hospital.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         The unit deals with psoriasis sufferers by treating their minds rather than their bodies, he says. The success rate is extraordinary.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Jess says: Its important for people to realise how common it is. I think it helps to see that there are lots of other people, including those in the public eye, who know what they are going through. Yet she adds: Im aware that mine is a relatively mild case compared to thousands of others so I also spend a lot of time feeling guilty for feeling sorry for myself which can be confusing.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Since developing psoriasis seven years ago Jess has tried all manner of potions and lotions and even turned to Chinese herbs to try to clear the condition.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         She avoids scented beauty products which might irritate her skin but says for her the key to coping is a healthy lifestyle and as little stress as possible, although she admits that being in the public eye can make this difficult. She says: I have learned to let things go because if you dont its only yourself you are hurting.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         Jess says she is too impatient for yoga and is not a fan of the gym. I do a lot of weight-training and a little cardiovascular work, just enough to make sure I get up a sweat. I used to dance five times a week when I was growing up and I am still really flexible.
        </p>
        <p class="storycopy">
         If the producers of Strictly Come Dancing came knocking Jess said she would jump at the chance to be on the show. I would absolutely love to do it.
        </p>
