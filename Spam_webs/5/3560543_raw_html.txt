              <h1>
               Government help for first-time home buyers
              </h1>
              <div class="abstract clearfix">
               <div id="ctl00_PlaceHolderMain_ctl01_label" style="display:none">
                Page Content
               </div>
               <div aria-labelledby="ctl00_PlaceHolderMain_ctl01_label" class="ms-rtestate-field" id="ctl00_PlaceHolderMain_ctl01__ControlWrapper_RichHtmlField" style="display:inline">
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div id="ctl00_PlaceHolderMain_ctl02_label" style="display:none">
              Main Content Body
             </div>
             <div aria-labelledby="ctl00_PlaceHolderMain_ctl02_label" class="ms-rtestate-field" id="ctl00_PlaceHolderMain_ctl02__ControlWrapper_RichHtmlField" style="display:inline">
              <h2 class="ms-rteElement-H2B">
               Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)
              </h2>
              <p>
               This federal government program allows you toborrow up to $25,000 from your RRSP tax free to fund your purchase. If both you and your spouse qualify, you can eachborrow up to $25,000 from your RRSPs, for a total of $50,000. The money must have been in your RRSP for at least 90 days. Here's how it works:
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Qualifying for the HBP
                </strong>
                <strong>
                </strong>
                 You must not have lived in a home owned by you or your spouse in the last 5 years. You must have entered into a written agreement to buy or build a home that you intend to occupy as your principal residence.
               </li>
               <li>
                <strong>
                 Repaying your RRSP
                </strong>
                You have up to 15 years to repay the amount you borrowed from your RRSP.You must start to make repayments in the second yearafter the year in which you made your withdrawal(s).In general, you have to repay 1/15 of the total amount you withdrew each year.After15 years, any amount you have not paidback to your RRSP will be taxed. You will owe this tax when you file your incometax return in the following year.
               </li>
              </ul>
              <p>
               Learn more about
               <a href="http://www.cra-arc.gc.ca/tx/ndvdls/tpcs/rrsp-reer/hbp-rap/menu-eng.html" target="_blank">
                how the HBP works
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <div class="ms-rteElement-Callout1">
               Find out about municipal government help for first-time buyers. Your city may offer additional refunds.
              </div>
              <h2 class="ms-rteElement-H2B">
               Land Transfer Tax (LTT) Refund
              </h2>
              <p>
               You may receive a refund from the Ontario government of up to $2,000 of the land transfer tax you paid on your first home. Other provinces have similar programs. Here's how it works:
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                You must be at least 18 years old.
               </li>
               <li>
                You cannot have previously owned a home, or an interest in a home, anywhere in the world.
               </li>
               <li>
                Your spouse cannot have owned a home, or an interest in a home anywhere while they were your spouse.
               </li>
               <li>
                You may receive the refund at the time you register your home purchase. If not, you must apply forit no later than 18 months after the registration date.
               </li>
              </ul>
              <p>
               Learn more about
               <a href="http://www.rev.gov.on.ca/en/refund/newhome/index.html" target="_blank">
                how the LTT Refund works
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <h2 class="ms-rteElement-H2B">
               First-time Home Buyers' Tax Credit (HBTC)
              </h2>
              <p>
               You or your spouse can claim this tax credit on your annual income tax return. The credit is based on an amount of $5,000. To calculate, multiply the lowest personal income tax rate for the year (15% in 2011 and 2012) by $5,000. For these years, the maximum credit is $750. Here's how it works:
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                You or your spouse must have acquired a qualifying home acquired after January 27, 2009.
               </li>
               <li>
                You cannot have lived in another home owned by you or your spouse in the year you buy your first home  or in any of the 4 preceding years.
               </li>
              </ul>
              <p>
               Learn more about
               <a href="http://www.cra-arc.gc.ca/gncy/bdgt/2009/fqhbtc-eng.html" target="_blank">
                how the HBTC works
               </a>
               .
              </p>
