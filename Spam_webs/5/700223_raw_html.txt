         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Chicken Alfredo Pizza with Broccoli
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-03-11T05:29:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           March 11, 2011
          </time>
          by
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

         <p>
          <a href="http://lh6.ggpht.com/_Bq7mCCDENkg/TXmyTpVWjvI/AAAAAAAAC1c/dxp9CNFMCqs/s1600-h/Chicken-Alfredo-Pizza-with-Broccoli-.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/lh6.ggpht.com/_Bq7mCCDENkg/TXmyTpVWjvI/AAAAAAAAC1c/dxp9CNFMCqs/s1600-h/Chicken-Alfredo-Pizza-with-Broccoli-.jpg');">
           <img alt="Chicken Alfredo Pizza with Broccoli (640x477)" border="0" height="211" src="http://lh3.ggpht.com/_Bq7mCCDENkg/TXmyUCr5U-I/AAAAAAAAC1g/bmH61Lse-4Q/Chicken-Alfredo-Pizza-with-Broccoli-%5B1%5D.jpg?imgmax=800" style="background-image: none; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="Chicken Alfredo Pizza with Broccoli (640x477)" width="340"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          This is my favorite way to use up
          <a href="http://www.premeditatedleftovers.com/2010/10/pasta-with-vegetables-and-alfredo-sauce.html" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.premeditatedleftovers.com/2010/10/pasta-with-vegetables-and-alfredo-sauce.html');" target="_blank">
           leftover Alfredo Sauce
          </a>
          , but I have no problem whipping up a batch of Alfredo sauce just to make this pizza.
         </p>
         <p>
          I used my
          <a href="http://premeditatedleftovers.com/slightly-healthier-gluten-free-garlic-and-herb-pizza-crust" target="_blank">
           Slightly Healthier Gluten Free Garlic and Herb Pizza Dough
          </a>
          recipe to make the crust for this pizza. Use your favorite pizza dough recipe, but add 1 teaspoon garlic powder, 1 teaspoon rosemary, and 1 teaspoon basil when mixing the dry ingredients to create an especially flavorful pizza.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ingredients:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          enough pizza dough for 1 large pizza
          <br/>
          Alfredo Sauce (recipe below)
          <br/>
          1/2 cup cooked chicken, diced
          <br/>
          1 cup broccoli, finely chopped (asparagus would also be good)
          <br/>
          3 green onions, thinly sliced
          <br/>
          1 1/2 cups mozzarella cheese
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Directions:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Prepare pizza dough according to the recipe directions, but dont forget to add garlic powder, rosemary, and basil when mixing the dough.
         </p>
         <p>
          While the pizza crust is baking, I make the Alfredo sauce and prepare the other ingredients.
         </p>
         <p>
          After removing pizza crust from oven top with Alfredo sauce and chicken:
          <br/>
          <a href="http://lh4.ggpht.com/_Bq7mCCDENkg/TXmyVuKku7I/AAAAAAAAC1k/pZP1Ka4w674/s1600-h/Alfredo%20Pizza%20%28640x477%29%5B5%5D.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/lh4.ggpht.com/_Bq7mCCDENkg/TXmyVuKku7I/AAAAAAAAC1k/pZP1Ka4w674/s1600-h/Alfredo%20Pizza%20%28640x477%29%5B5%5D.jpg');">

         <p>
          <strong>
           Alfredo Sauce
          </strong>
          (this is the same Alfredo Sauce Recipe that I used in
          <a href="http://www.premeditatedleftovers.com/2011/03/steak-and-asparagus-frittata-and-pasta.html" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.premeditatedleftovers.com/2011/03/steak-and-asparagus-frittata-and-pasta.html');" target="_blank">
           Pasta Alfredo with Asparagus
          </a>
          , but I included it here to make it more convenient should you choose to try this recipe).
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ingredients:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          2 tablespoons butter
          <br/>
          1 tablespoon cornstarch (or 2 T. flour)
          <br/>
          1/2 cup milk
          <br/>
          1/2 cup vegetable broth
          <br/>
          1 cup Mozzarella cheese (or 3/4 cups mozzarella and 1/4 cup parmesan)
          <br/>
          1 teaspoon garlic powder
          <br/>
          1 tablespoon fresh parsley (or 2 teaspoons dried)
          <br/>
          dash of pepper
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Directions:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Melt butter in a medium sauce pot. Once the butter is melted add cornstarch. Stir until smooth. Cook over a medium-high flame until the mixture is smooth and bubbly, stirring constantly.
         </p>
         <p>
          Add milk slowly, stirring until well blended. Add broth, cheese, garlic powder, parsley and pepper. Stir over a medium flame, until cheese is melted and the sauce is smooth.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Thoughts on Making Pizza
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          For a long time, I was afraid of making my own pizza dough. I didnt think I could make it look pretty  and I cant. I have still never made a perfectly round pizza, the closest I have come is a fat oval (my daughter said it would be more politically correct to say a wide oval). But you know what? Ugly homemade pizza tastes way better than a perfectly formed packaged or delivery pizza. It is also healthier and a lot less expensive. As with most homemade food, you get twice the flavor, for half the price.
         </p>
