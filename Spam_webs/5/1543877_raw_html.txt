          <p>
           In android, geeky users will handle issues with ease since they came across many stuffs like rooting, Custom ROMs and many more on the other hand for normal users it will be even hard to root their phones. So today for average users Im gonna share 4 useful troubleshooting tips for android devices
          </p>

          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/android.jpg">
            <img alt="4 useful troubleshooting tips for android device" class="aligncenter wp-image-828 size-large" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/android.jpg?resize=750%2C468"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h3>
          </h3>
          <h3>
           4 useful troubleshooting tips for android device
          </h3>
          <p>
           So starting with safe boot mode in android
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            1. Reboot your Android device into safe mode
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Any strange annoying things beginning to occur in your device or performance drags due to user apps? Well even sometimes your device may misbehave due to system apps, you can confirm it by rebooting your android device into safe mode. Rebooting android device into safe mode is like rebooting your windows PC into safe mode. In android safe mode you can have only access to the default apps, no users apps can be executed in the safe mode, you can also notice the performance of your device will be smooth. If you found that the device doesnt lag in safe mode while it lags in normal mode you can confirm that the issues are caused by the user apps, so you have to analyze and remove the user app which makes trouble, well if you found that device still lags even in safe mode then the problem is in your system UI.
          </p>
          <p>
           How to reboot your android device into safe mode?
          </p>
          <p>
           1. Long press power button to enter to the Shutdown menu
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/power-off.png">
            <img alt="4 useful troubleshooting tips for android device" class="aligncenter wp-image-829 size-medium" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/power-off.png?resize=168%2C300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           2. Tap and hold power off option until another window pops up
          </p>
          <p>
           3. In the pop up menu it will ask u whether you want to Reboot to safe mode or not? Simply select OK to reboot to safe mode, cancel if you dont want to
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/safe-mode.png">
            <img alt="4 useful troubleshooting tips for android device" class="aligncenter wp-image-831 size-medium" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/safe-mode.png?resize=168%2C300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>

          <h4>
           <strong>
            2. Recovery mode
           </strong>
          </h4>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://i0.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/recovery-mode.jpg">
            <img alt="4 useful troubleshooting tips for android device" class="aligncenter wp-image-833 size-full" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/recovery-mode.jpg?resize=640%2C427"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Every android device has recovery mode which is separated from the main android OS which has bunch of useful troubleshooting menus, learning how to use recovery mode in your device will help you a lot. In recovery mode you can factory reset your device, apply new updates to your device and wipe your cache partition. Entering recovery mode in android device will be different, So Google your android device model know how to enter into recovery mode or search in XDA developers forum
          </p>
          <h4>
           <strong>
            3. Forgotten the password of your device?
           </strong>
          </h4>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/screen-lock.png">
            <img alt="4 useful troubleshooting tips for android device" class="aligncenter wp-image-832 size-medium" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.techinbuzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/screen-lock.png?resize=168%2C300"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Android provides great security by locking your device in 3 ways with password, pin and pattern locks. Users can select the any of these locks based on their preference. Some users may forget their device password or mess up by entering wrong passwords with multiple attempts. After several unsuccessful attempts the device will allow you to redeem by entering your Google account information. Even if you fail to enter your Google account password then we have another way. Yes the recovery mode which I mentioned above. Enter into the recovery mode and do a factory reset, thats now you can access your device. But beware if your perform a factory reset all your contacts, third party apps will be gone
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            4. Bricked your device? You can install original firmware
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Even devices will show middle finger to geeky users when they mess up things while experimenting their devices. Phone bricked by custom ROM? Want to revert back to original ROM? You can install OS (i.e. original firmware) in your device. Installing OS android device is not that much complex
          </p>
          <p>
           YouTube will help you better to install original firmware
          </p>
          <p>
           All you have to do is
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Search and find a perfect tutorial in
            <a href="https://www.youtube.com/" target="_blank">
             YouTube
            </a>
            on How to install firmware in your device?
           </li>
           <li>
            Download firmware in your PC
           </li>
           <li>
            You have to install Odin (Samsung devices) or ADB fast boot (for Nexus devices) in your PC
           </li>
           <li>
            Enter into recovery mode and connect your device to PC with USB cable
           </li>
           <li>
            By executing commands in Odin or ADB fast boot, you can install firmware in your android device all by yourself
           </li>
          </ol>
