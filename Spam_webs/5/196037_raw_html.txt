       <div id="articleTranscript">
        <p>
         German journalist and author Jrgen Todenhfer is perhaps best known for supposedly being the first Westerner to have embedded with Islamic State militants.
        </p>
        <p>
         He spent ten days in
         <span class="caps">
          ISIS
         </span>
         controlled territories in Iraq and Syria, and has now released an interview with a German Islamic state fighter Christian Emde.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the video, the suspected militant reveals the group has big ambitions beyond its borders and wants to kill all Shiite Muslims.
        </p>
        <p>
         Euronews spoke to Todenhfer about his 13-minute film which has sparked a row within Germany about the militant groups ambitions.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Todenhfer: terrorist violence is the fallout of our own wars.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Kirsten Ripper, euronews: Whats your view about the raids in several European countries and the Belgian authorities foiling an alleged attack?
        </p>
        <p>
         Jrgen Todenhfer, writer and journalist: I think that our security organisations are doing everything they need to, but Im afraid of hysteria.
        </p>
        <p>
         KR, euronews: For years you have been arguing for greater understanding of Islam in your books. Why do you think the religion is treated unfairly?
        </p>
        <p>
         JT: During the last 200 years an Arabic country has never attacked the West. We must explain why we fought wars in Afghanistan, in Iraq and in Libya. And if we are looking for a reason why this horrible organisation
         <span class="caps">
          ISIL
         </span>
         exists, we must have a look at its history.
         <span class="caps">
          ISIL
         </span>
         was founded some weeks after the US-led invasion of Iraq. The organisation is the  baby of George W. Bush. And the violence, that we face now is the fallout or boomerang effect of our own wars.
        </p>
        <p>
         KR, euronews: What surprised you most during your visit to
         <span class="caps">
          ISIL
         </span>
         controlled territories
        </p>
        <p>
         JT: This organisation is much stronger than our politicians, Western politicians believe.  We have no strategy against
         <span class="caps">
          ISIL
         </span>
         , because to bombard cities where only 5,000
         <span class="caps">
          ISIL
         </span>
         fighters are, would lead to the killing of thousands of civilians and this leads to new terrorism and it will not defeat
         <span class="caps">
          ISIL
         </span>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         KR, euronews: You were heavily criticised for your interview with a German jihadi.  Could you have been tougher?
        </p>
        <p>
         It doesnt matter what I think about IS. I have written this several times. My judgement of
         <span class="caps">
          ISIL
         </span>
         is devastating. It was important to find out what
         <span class="caps">
          ISIL
         </span>
