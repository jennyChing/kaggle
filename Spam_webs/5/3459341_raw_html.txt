          <blockquote>
           <p>
            Clutter is not just physical stuff. Its old ideas, toxic relationships and bad habits. Clutter is anything that does not support your better self.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.anxietycure.co/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/clutter.png">
            <img alt="clutter" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1711" height="600" src="http://www.anxietycure.co/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/clutter.png" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Daily life seems so much easier when rooms are clean and tidy. With the rubbish sorted and discarded, and everything in its place, the house is a calmer, more efficient and pleasant place to be. Mind decluttering has a similar effect. Sorting out an untidy room involves choosing what to keep and what to discard. Sorting out redundant ideas and patterns of thinking is a similar process.
          </p>
          <p>
           First of all, address your physical wellbeing to give you the energy to declutter your mind. Make sure youre getting enough quality sleep, and take some exercise on a regular basis. Just a walk in the fresh air can make all the difference. If you feel particularly energetic, do some gardening or go for a run. These are mindless activities that demand physical, rather than mental, input. If you are well rested and refreshed, you will have more will and energy to tackle a mind declutter. Its like opening a window in a room to let the fresh air inside.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do some simple breathing exercises. Take a few minutes to engage in conscious breathing. Concentrate on your in-breath, feeling your chest expand, then release the breath slowly. Focus as much of your attention as you can on this action, letting other thoughts drift away. You cant stop the thoughts, but you can maintain a distance, choosing not to engage with thoughts, but just watch as they pass. This process of mindfulness will take a little practice, but is worth the effort for the relaxation and calmness it can induce.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.anxietycure.co/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cluttered-mind-quote.png">
            <img alt="cluttered-mind-quote" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1712" height="800" src="http://www.anxietycure.co/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/cluttered-mind-quote.png" width="800"/>
           </a>
          </p>

          <p>
           Take a piece of paper and write down a list of what is really important in your life, and another list of what is not. Be honest with yourself. Its like clearing out a cupboard. Take each item out and examine it. Do you really need to keep it, or is it just adding to the clutter?
          </p>
          <p>
           When your lists are complete, take the list of unimportant things outside and burn it. Watch as it disintegrates. Watch the smoke drift away, let your thoughts of unimportant things drift away with it. Later, when your mind wanders to any of the unimportant things on the list, remember the moment you chose to let go. Remember the smoke and let your unimportant thoughts go.
          </p>
          <p>
           Never underestimate the power of nature to put things in perspective. If you have a garden, spend more time in it. Watch carefully from day to day as plants grow. If you dont have a garden, find a park or other green space and spend regular time there, watching nature at work. If you focus on this, your mind will be drawn away from the jumble of everyday thought. In effect, this exercise gives your mind a well-deserved rest.
          </p>
          <p>
           When you have more time, try to spend time by the sea or a river, or near woods and mountains. Perceiving yourself in relation to the magnitude of the natural world can put your problems into perspective very quickly.
          </p>
          <p>
           Let go of negative emotions, especially anger and guilt. This can be difficult, especially if youve held on to them for a long time. The past is gone, you cant change it. All you change is how it affects your future. Imagine holding a small object, a book, for example, at arms length. At first, it requires little effort, but after a few minutes, the book seems heavier. The longer you hold on to the book, the heavier it gets. Imagine that book is full of all your negative thoughts. Imagine how much relief you will feel when you put it down.
          </p>
          <p>
           Multi-tasking is addictive, and you may feel as if youre not working to capacity if you dont do it. Try to focus on just one task at a time, restricting your thoughts to that task as much as possible.
          </p>
          <p>
           Keep a journal recording your decluttering journey. On slow days, when you dont feel as if youre getting anywhere, looking back on your journal can be a good way of seeing how far youve come and what youve already discarded. The process isnt as quick as clearing out a cupboard, or a room, but day by day, by repeating the exercises, your mind will get rid of some junk and be more able to focus with clarity on what really matters in your life.
          </p>
