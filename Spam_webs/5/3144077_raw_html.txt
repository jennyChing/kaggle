       <h1 itemprop="headline name">
        Oliver James: Love Bombing helps a child through divorce
       </h1>
       <h2 itemprop="alternativeHeadline description">
        Child psychologist and author Oliver James from The School of Life says that 
  Love Bombing is an ideal way to make a divorce happier for a child.
       </h2>
       <style media="print">
        .newGigyaShare { display: none !important; }
       </style>

              <span class="caption">
               Lessons in love: a divorced parent needs to give their child an intense experience of feeling completely loved
              </span>

        <div class="byline">
         <div>
          <p class="bylineBody">
           <span itemprop="author">
            By Oliver James, child psychologist and author
           </span>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p class="publishedDate">
          5:48PM BST 21 Jun 2013
         </p>
         <meta content="2013-06-21" itemprop="datePublished"/>
         <div class="cl">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           <strong>
            <sup>
             Find out more at
             <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sponsored/lifestyle/legal-services/">
              The Co-operative Legal Services homepage
             </a>
            </sup>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           Conventional counselling for your child will encourage them to express their distress about the divorce. Common behavioural techniques will be offered by psychologists.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           And if your child is hyperactive or depressed prescription medication may be offered. None of these is nearly as good as the method I developed, called Love Bombing.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           Love Bombing gives your child an intense, condensed experience of feeling completely loved and completely in control. It is suitable for all children aged three up to puberty and is an ideal way to make a divorce happier for the child.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           The period during which you enter the Love Bombing zone can be 48 hours, 24 hours, a single day or shorter bursts. Whichever period you use, you subsequently rekindle that experience daily for half an hour. It can be done without financial cost.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class=" related_links_inline" id="tmg-related-links">
          <div class="headerOne styleTwo">
           <h2>
            <span>
             Related Articles
            </span>
           </h2>
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="bullet">
            <p>
             <a href="/sponsored/lifestyle/legal-services/10113090/good-divorce-advice.html">
              How to have a good divorce
             </a>
            </p>
            <span class="relContDate">
             12 Jun 2013
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           Thousands of parents have found it helps for a wide variety of problems. Since insecurity is a major consequence of divorce, I would recommend doing it separately with all your children if they are still pre-adolescent.
           <img align="right" caption="" declared-caption="" height="84" name="Co_op_pullquote1" photographer="" refid="2594464" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/02594/Co_op_pullquote1_2594464a.jpg" width="247"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           First, you explain to your child that sometime soon the two of you are going to spend time together and are going to have a lot of fun.
          </p>
          <p>
           You give the message that this is going to be a big event  Its coming soon, how exciting!  and your child then draws up a list of fun activities. It doesnt matter if that includes lots of watching
           <i>
            SpongeBob SquarePants
           </i>
           : the key is that your child has chosen it.
          </p>
          <p>
           Throughout the experience, you are trying as much as possible to give them the feeling of Whatever I want, I get  a very unusual one of being in control and of being gratified, as well as bombed with love.
          </p>
          <p>
           It might sound bonkers to do that if your child is badly behaved or you are sick of being messed about by his or her wilfulness, like rewarding bad behaviour.
          </p>
          <p>
           But, oddly enough, dramatic shifts result in the childs personality and behaviour. When it comes to dealing with disobedient, shy, clingy or aggressive or impatient children, love and control, it seems, really are the answer.
          </p>
          <p>
           It is especially helpful in creating a happy divorce for the child, particularly if there were problems in early life. From a very young age, childrens cortisol levels (the fight-or-flight hormone secreted when threatened) are affected by marital discord.
          </p>
          <p>
           Studies show that small children (aged two to three) who had sensitive mothering earlier on are less likely to get upset when parents row  early action has a protective effect.
          </p>
          <p>
           Alas, if mothering early on was disrupted  through no fault of the mother, perhaps because she was depressed  then children have greater vulnerability when parents row. Their cortisol levels are much more reactive.
          </p>
          <p>
           When older, the quality of early care explains why there are big differences in how different siblings react to marital rancour. Some respond with passive vigilance, as if their whole system has shut down (sometimes a precursor to depression); others externalise, with all systems going full throttle, becoming aggressive.
          </p>
          <p>
           Those who had sensitive early care are able to cope with the parental rancour best: upset but becoming neither aggressive nor depressed.
          </p>
          <p>
           Whichever kind of child you have, Love Bombing is likely to correct cortisol imbalances resulting from early care deficits. The alternatives are years of child psychotherapy (which can be very helpful but also expensive and time-consuming), pills or rats-in-a-maze behaviourism.
          </p>
