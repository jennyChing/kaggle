         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Black Bottom Cupcakes
         </h1>
         <ul class="post-details clearfix">
          <li>
           6 years ago by
           <a href="http://www.blisstree.com/author/heather/" rel="author" title="Posts by Heather">
            Heather
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.blisstree.com/2009/07/23/food/black-bottom-cupcakes/#comments" title="Comment on Black Bottom Cupcakes">
            <span class="dsq-postid" rel="44084 http://www.blisstree.com/chocolatebytes/?p=2533">
             9 Comments 
            </span>
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="#top" onclick="show_modal_window('share-tip-window');">
            Share a Tip
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
          </li>
         </ul>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          While doing some link surfing recently I came across quite a few recipes for Black Bottom Cupcakes. Being a sucker for anything involving chocolate and cream cheese, I had to give these a try!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="blackbottomcupcakes1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2534" height="357" src="http://www.blisstree.com/files/2009/07/blackbottomcupcakes1.jpg" width="489"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The addition of the mini chocolate chips in the cream cheese mix adds a perfect texture to the surprise filling. The cream cheese was actually not as prominent as I would have liked it to be, and actually the next time I make these Im going to play around with it a bit and add some sour cream to the mix and see how that works out.
         </p>
         <p>
          The cupcakes stayed moist in the fridge with plastic wrap and were devoured within two days! This is definitely a keeper.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="blackbottomcupcakes2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2535" height="376" src="http://www.blisstree.com/files/2009/07/blackbottomcupcakes2.jpg" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          This particular version of the recipe comes from
          <a href="http://worthhersalt.com/archives/166" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://worthhersalt.com']);">
           Worth Her Salt
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Black Bottom Cupcakes
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          1 8 oz package cream cheese, softened
          <br/>
          1 egg
          <br/>
          1/3 c sugar
          <br/>
          1/8 t salt
          <br/>
          1 c miniature semisweet chocolate chips
         </p>
         <p>
          1 1/2 c all-purpose flour
          <br/>
          1 c sugar
          <br/>
          1/4 c cocoa powder
          <br/>
          1 t baking soda
          <br/>
          1/2 t salt
          <br/>
          1 c water
          <br/>
          1/3 c vegetable oil
          <br/>
          1 T cider vinegar
          <br/>
          1 t vanilla extract
         </p>
         <p>
          ~ Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Line muffin tins with paper cups or lightly spray with non-stick cooking spray.
         </p>
         <p>
          ~ In a medium bowl, beat the cream cheese, egg, 1/3 cup sugar and 1/8 teaspoon salt until light and fluffy. Stir in the chocolate chips and set aside.
         </p>
         <p>
          ~ In a large bowl, mix together the flour, 1 cup sugar, cocoa, baking soda and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Make a well in the center and add the water, oil, vinegar and vanilla. Stir together until well blended. Fill muffin tins 1/3 full with the batter and top with a dollop of the cream cheese mixture.
         </p>
         <p>
          ~ Bake in preheated oven for 25 to 30 minutes.
         </p>
