     <div class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody">
      <div class="cT-imagePortrait">
       <img alt="CSIRO Staff Association fears up to 100 Queensland jobs will go as a result of nation-wide cuts." itemprop="image" src="/content/dam/images/z/s/j/z/t/image.related.articleLeadNarrow.300x0.zsju6.png/1403579767437.jpg" title=""/>
       <p>
        CSIRO Staff Association fears up to 100 Queensland jobs will go as a result of nation-wide cuts.
       </p>
      </div>
      <!-- class:cT-imageLandscape/cT-imagePortrait -->
      <p>
       Up to 100 CSIRO jobs could go in Queensland from the about 700 lost Australia-wide, the CSIRO's Staff Association said.
      </p>
      <p>
       The job losses will affect CSIRO facilities at Dutton Park's Boggo Road laboratory, St Lucia, Pullenvale and the education units at Dutton Park and in Townsville, according to staff association spokesman Paul Girdler who listed the areas where the CSIRO cuts are likely to impact in Queensland.
      </p>
      <p>
       Theses included mathematicians, computer mapping specialists, education specialists and communications staff, he said.
      </p>
      <p>
       "Some of the areas haven't finally worked out exactly what their numbers are going to look like," Mr Girdler said.
      </p>
      <div class="ad adAlignRight" id="adspot-300x250-pos3" style="display:none;">
       <small>
        Advertisement
       </small>
       <script>
        titan.requestAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
       </script>
      </div>
      <p>
       "But we believe it could be up to 100." he said.
      </p>
      <p>
       The association had identified these areas in Queensland likely to be affected by nation-wide cuts:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Mathematicians from the Computational Informatics at Dutton Park's Boggo Road CSIRO centre;
       </li>
       <li>
        Marine and Atmospheric Division at Dutton Park's Boggo Road centre;
       </li>
       <li>
        Mining Science and Engineering at Pullenvale;
       </li>
       <li>
        CSIRO's Plant Industry at the University of Queensland;
       </li>
       <li>
        Economic and Social Sciences area at Dutton Park's Boggo Road centre;
       </li>
       <li>
        The education unit at Boggo Road;
       </li>
       <li>
        Two information specialists and librarians from Pullenvale and Boggo Road; and
       </li>
       <li>
        Some communications jobs that will impact on the three main Brisbane sites.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Mr Girdler said the cutbacks were quite widespread.
      </p>
      <p>
       "The impact on science is obviously going to be quite dramatic," he said.
      </p>
      <p>
       A protest against
       <a href="http://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/queensland/csiro-cuts-to-hit-alternative-fuel-research-20140520-zrix0.html">
        Queensland's CSIRO job cuts
       </a>
       was staged at the University of Queensland on Tuesday.
      </p>
      <p>
       Across Australia, the CSIRO Staff Association is warning of a scientific jobs crisis where a further 700 CSIRO job will go Australia-wide.
      </p>
      <p>
       Staff Association Acting Secretary Dr Michael Borgas forecast more scientific jobs will go.
      </p>
      <p>
       Its no exaggeration to say that CSIRO faces a jobs crisis, we could be looking at the largest reduction in staffing at CSIRO in the organsisations history, Dr Borgas said.
      </p>
      <p>
       The CSIRO faces a cut of 700 staff in the coming year of 2014-15, he said.
      </p>
      <p>
       This number of 700 is made up of 420 jobs lost to direct government cuts and 280 jobs gone as a result of the organisational restructure and cuts to important support functions.
      </p>
      <p>
       The 700 is on top of 477 jobs already cut over the past twelve months, which includes the results of the federal governments public sector recruitment freeze.
      </p>
      <p>
       In Queensland CSIRO staff are protesting at the CSIROs St Lucia work site on the corner of Carmody Road and Chancellor Place, St Lucia from 12.30pm.
      </p>
      <p>
       Those Queensland jobs cuts have already begun  in CSIRO's Education Units in Brisbane and Townsville.
      </p>
      <p>
       In late May CSIROs chief executive Dr Megan Clark warned that jobs would be lost in geothermal energy research, carbon capture and storage, liquid fuels, neurosciences, urban water research, schools education and clinical medicine in areas outside nutrition.
      </p>
