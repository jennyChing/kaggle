&lt;div itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Article"&gt;
  &lt;h1 itemprop="headline" class="BlogPTitleDetail"&gt;
    Convector vs. radiant heaters
  &lt;/h1&gt;
  &lt;div class="BlogPImage"&gt;
    &lt;span st_image="https://alintaenergy.com.au/savewithgas/getattachment/e339e6de-b43b-4241-8f55-a35d0e134f8b/Convector-vs-radiant-heaters?maxsidesize=700" &gt;&lt;img itemprop="image" src="/savewithgas/getattachment/e339e6de-b43b-4241-8f55-a35d0e134f8b/Convector-vs-radiant-heaters?maxsidesize=700" alt="Convector vs. radiant heaters " /&gt;&lt;/span&gt;
  &lt;/div&gt;
  &lt;div class="BlogPBodyDetail textContent"&gt;
    &lt;div class="summary"&gt;
      &lt;div class="BlogPostSummaryText" itemprop="articleBody"&gt;&lt;p&gt;
	Space heaters are a fantastic way to heat a single room or provide a boost of heat when the temperature drops. The two most prevalent methods for raising the ambient temperature are convection and radiant heat and your choice will be guided largely by what you want to heat.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;/div&gt;
    &lt;/div&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;
	Convector heaters warm the air in the room, whilst radiant heaters focus their heat on the surfaces of the nearest objects, generally people. In other words, do you want to heat the entire room, including multiple people sitting apart from one another, or do you want a personal heater which will radiate the heat it generates towards those sitting near to it? We guide you through the options to help you make an informed decision.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;
	Convector heaters&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;
	How do they work?&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;
	Convector heaters rely on the process of convection, whereby air convection currents circulate within the heater, across a heating element (often aided by a fan). The air absorbs the heat, increases in volume and rises, circulating through the room. When the thermostat senses the space has reached the desired temperature, it shuts itself off and will turn itself back on when the temperature drops low enough.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;
	Where should they be used?&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;
	Because convection heaters circulate warm air around an enclosed space, they are the best option for heating an entire room for an extended period of time. They take longer to heat up but are more efficient.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;
	What are the options?&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;
	Convection heaters are a popular choice and as such they are available in a number of forms, either powered by electricity or gas. Electric convection heaters are available in the form of wall-mounted panel heaters, wall-mounted or freestanding fan heaters, oil column heaters, and ducted central heating systems. Gas-fired convection heaters include flued and unflued heaters, wall-mounted furnaces, hot water radiators powered by a gas boiler, and ducted central heating systems.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;
	Radiant heaters&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;
	How do they work?&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;
	Radiant heaters use infrared rays of heat to radiate warmth to nearby objects rather than raising the temperature of the air, similar to a fire.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;
	Where should they be used?&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;
	Radiant heaters are best in smaller rooms with fewer people, all of whom will remain in one place and are close to the heater - a TV room is the perfect example. They are a great option as a personal heater when you need an extra boost of heat, such as in a home office. They are also ideal for those with dust allergies as, because the air isn&rsquo;t circulated, drafts are avoided. Radiant heaters are also often used outdoors.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;
	What are the options?&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p&gt;
	Electric models include ceiling or wall-mounted panels and tubular heaters, and gas varieties are available in the form of infrared outdoor heaters, radiant tube heaters, and gas fires (although many use both radiant and convection heating methods).&lt;/p&gt;
