            <h1 id="page-title">
             Stella Artois ads make you think
            </h1>

                <div class="article node node-print node-promoted node-odd node-full clearfix" id="article-122234">
                 <div class="section field field-name-field-print field-type-image field-label-hidden">

                  <h2 class="node-title">
                   <a class="" href="/ooh/stella-artois-ads-make-you-think">
                    Stella Artois ads make you think
                   </a>
                  </h2>
                 </div>
                 <div class="footer submitted clearfix">
                  <div class="user-picture">
                   <a href="/adgrunts/caffeinegoddess" title="View user profile.">
                    <img alt="caffeinegoddess's picture" data-src="https://b0wie.s3.amazonaws.com/styles/large/s3/avatarshots/picture-3-1392833021.jpg?itok=ljMklhJB" src="http://adland.tv/files/tb_wall_lazyload_images/183x276.png" title="caffeinegoddess's picture"/>
                   </a>
                  </div>
                  By
                  <a class="username" href="/adgrunts/caffeinegoddess" title="View user profile.">
                   caffeinegoddess
                  </a>
                  on
                  <span class="time pubdate" title="2005-08-06T20:37:07+02:00">
                   06 Aug 2005, 20:37
                  </span>
                 </div>
                 <div class="node-content">
                  <div class="post-container">
                   <div class="post-button post-expanded" style="">
                    <div class="pw_widget pw_copypaste_true pw_counter_true pw_counter_buttons">
                     <a class="pw_googleplus_button">
                     </a>
                     <a class="pw_facebook_button">
                     </a>
                     <a class="pw_twitter_button">
                     </a>
                     <a class="pw_email_button">
                     </a>
                     <a class="pw_linkedin_button">
                     </a>
                     <span class="centup">
                     </span>
                    </div>
                    <script charset="utf-8" src="http://i.po.st/share/script/post-widget.js#publisherKey=178psqf7n1m3204b50uf" type="text/javascript">
                    </script>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                  <div class="section field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                   <div class="field-items">
                    <div class="field-item odd">
                     <p>
                      This summer, Stella Artois been running a campaign in magazines, newspapers and outdoor, supporting their "Live Film" initiative. The ads are like a "Where's Waldo" of film trivia.
                      <a class="elf-external elf-icon" href="/elf/go?url=http%3A//www.brandrepublic.com/sectors/advertising/creative/advertisement/19256/stella-artois-film-live-lowe-london/" rel="nofollow">
                       All portray typically English scenes
                      </a>
                      that have been invaded by characters, props and scenarios from classic films. Each of the three ads contains approximately 20 films.
                     </p>
                     <!--break-->
                     <p>
                      <a class="elf-external elf-icon" href="/elf/go?url=http%3A//observer.guardian.co.uk/uk_news/story/0%2C6903%2C1530469%2C00.html" rel="nofollow">
                       The campaign
                      </a>
                      aims to entice drinkers with prizes including DVDs and a holiday in San Francisco.
                      <br/>
                     </p>
                     <blockquote>
                      Ed Morris, executive creative director of Lowe, the agency which created the adverts, said: "A promotion is quite a hard sell, so we wanted to aim for something big. We started out with one film reference - Superman in a phone box. Then the idea took shape and went to a grander scale. The next stage was: "What if we put 20 film references in one landscape?"
                      <p>
                       "There are some we knew ourselves and some we researched, so that people would immediately recognise some and be drawn in by others which are more subtle. So in the park you're likely to spot The Birds but the falling plane engine is more tricky."
                      </p>
                     </blockquote>
                     <p>
                      <a class="elf-external elf-icon" href="/elf/go?url=http%3A//www.stevenageherald.co.uk/content/herts24/news/story.aspx%3Fbrand%3DHADOnline%26category%3DNews%26tBrand%3Dherts24%26tCategory%3Dnewshertsad%26itemid%3DWEED30%2520Jun%25202005%252014%253A30%253A04%253A893" rel="nofollow">
                       St. Albans Market Place
                      </a>
                      was chosen "because it, along with the rest of the city, has featured in so many films and television productions," a spokesperson for Stella Artois said.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Not only has there been chatter around the web trying to solve these ads, but
                      <a class="elf-external elf-icon" href="/elf/go?url=http%3A//www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/story.jsp%3Fstory%3D655283" rel="nofollow">
                       The Belfast Telegraph
                      </a>
                      also reports "advertising executives at Stella Artois have been fielding calls from moviegoers and motorists trying to connect the iconic cars with legendary movies in their recent billboard campaign".
                     </p>
                     <blockquote>
                      <p>
                       Spokesperson for Bass Ireland, who have Stella Artois in their stable of drinks, Brian Houston, described the lager's first outdoor campaign as a "thinking man's ad" which has "stirred up so much interest it's unbelievable".
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       "We think it's a very appropriate campaign as it's building on Stella's associations with film and cinema and we've been taking dozens and dozens of calls with people wanting to know what it's about."
                      </p>
                     </blockquote>
                     <p>
                      This isn't the first time Stella has been connected to films either. They started partnering up with celluloid back in 1995. For more on the history check out
                      <a class="elf-external elf-icon" href="/elf/go?url=http%3A//www.brandchannel.com/features_effect.asp%3Fpf_id%3D265" rel="nofollow">
                       Brand Channel
                      </a>
                      's write up.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      For the curious, solutions to the ads are here:
                      <a class="elf-external elf-icon" href="/elf/go?url=http%3A//observer.guardian.co.uk/uk_news/story/0%2C6903%2C1530471%2C00.html" rel="nofollow">
                       Park
                      </a>
                      ,
                      <a class="elf-external elf-icon" href="/elf/go?url=http%3A//observer.guardian.co.uk/uk_news/story/0%2C6903%2C1530472%2C00.html" rel="nofollow">
                       Town
                      </a>
                      , and
                      <a class="elf-external elf-icon" href="/elf/go?url=http%3A//observer.guardian.co.uk/uk_news/story/0%2C6903%2C1530470%2C00.html" rel="nofollow">
                       Beach
                      </a>
                      .
                     </p>
                    </div>
