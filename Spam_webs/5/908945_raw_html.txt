     <p>
      <b>
       Mohammed Atef Al-Masri
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        
      </span>
      ,
      <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Arabic transliteration">
       <i>
        Atef Al-Masri
       </i>
      </span>
      ) (born
      <i>
       Sobhi Abu Setta
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guard_1-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-guard-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      also known as
      <i>
       Abu Hafs al-Masri
      </i>
      ) was the military chief of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Qaida" title="Al-Qaida">
       al-Qaida
      </a>
      , although his role in the organization was not well known by intelligence agencies for years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guard_1-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-guard-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was killed in a US drone strike in November 2001.
     </p>
     <p>
      Atef served two years in the
      <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_Air_Force" title="Egyptian Air Force">
       Egyptian Air Force
      </a>
      and became an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agricultural_engineer" title="Agricultural engineer">
       agricultural engineer
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guard_1-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-guard-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was also a police officer and a member of the group
      <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_Islamic_Jihad" title="Egyptian Islamic Jihad">
       Egyptian Islamic Jihad
      </a>
      before he moved to Afghanistan to repel
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_invasion_of_Afghanistan" title="Soviet invasion of Afghanistan">
       the Soviet invasion
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reports_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-reports-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      while operating from
      <a href="/wiki/Peshawar" title="Peshawar">
       Peshawar
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noodles_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-noodles-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He has been credited as having convinced
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abdullah_Azzam" title="Abdullah Azzam">
       Abdullah Azzam
      </a>
      to abandon his life and devote himself to preaching
      <a href="/wiki/Jihad" title="Jihad">
       jihad
      </a>
      at this time.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noodles_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-noodles-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Atef was sent to an
      <a href="/wiki/Afghan_training_camp" title="Afghan training camp">
       Afghan training camp
      </a>
      where he met
      <a href="/wiki/Ayman_al-Zawahiri" title="Ayman al-Zawahiri">
       Ayman al-Zawahiri
      </a>
      , who later introduced him to
      <a href="/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden" title="Osama bin Laden">
       Osama bin Laden
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guard_1-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-guard-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He attended two meetings from August 1120 in 1988, along with Bin Laden, al-Zawahiri,
      <a href="/wiki/Mamdouh_Mahmud_Salim" title="Mamdouh Mahmud Salim">
       Mamdouh Mahmud Salim
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jamal_al-Fadl" title="Jamal al-Fadl">
       Jamal al-Fadl
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wael_Hamza_Julaidan" title="Wael Hamza Julaidan">
       Wael Hamza Julaidan
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Loay_Bayazid" title="Mohammed Loay Bayazid">
       Mohammed Loay Bayazid
      </a>
      and eight others, to discuss the founding of "al-Qaeda".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bin Laden later sent a letter to Mohammed Loay Bayazid informing him that Atef and
      <a href="/wiki/Abu_Ubaidah_al-Banshiri" title="Abu Ubaidah al-Banshiri">
       Abu Ubaidah al-Banshiri
      </a>
      were to each be given 6,500
      <a href="/wiki/Saudi_riyal" title="Saudi riyal">
       Saudi riyals
      </a>
      monthly, the same as they had been given for their work in
      <a href="/wiki/Maktab_al-Khidamat" title="Maktab al-Khidamat">
       Maktab al-Khidamat
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shay_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-shay-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_Sudan">
       In the Sudan
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohammed_Atef&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In the Sudan">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Atef followed al-Qaeda to the Sudan in 1992 until the group was forced to leave,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      following the execution of the teenaged son of
      <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_Salama_Mabruk" title="Ahmad Salama Mabruk">
       Ahmad Salama Mabruk
      </a>
      , and Atef moved to Afghanistan.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hrw_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-hrw-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1994, he refused to allow American
      <a href="/wiki/Double_agent" title="Double agent">
       double agent
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ali_Mohammed" title="Ali Mohammed">
       Ali Mohammed
      </a>
      to know which name and passport he would be traveling under, expressing concerns that Mohammed could be working with the American authorities.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He traveled to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mombassa,_Kenya" title="Mombassa, Kenya">
       Mombassa, Kenya
      </a>
      where he met with
      <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_Odeh" title="Mohammed Odeh">
       Mohammed Odeh
      </a>
      and gave him money to purchase himself a 7-tonne
      <a href="/wiki/Fishing_trawler" title="Fishing trawler">
       trawler
      </a>
      and start a fishing business.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berg_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-berg-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      While in the Sudan, he allegedly conducted a study which resulted in him presenting al-Qaeda details on why
      <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_hijacking" title="Aircraft hijacking">
       aircraft hijackings
      </a>
      were a poor idea as they were engineered to allow the negotiation of hostages in exchange for prisoners, rather than inflicting damage.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Another alleged study he carried out determined that the
      <a href="/wiki/Afghan_Arabs" title="Afghan Arabs">
       Afghan Arabs
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Taliban" title="Taliban">
       Taliban
      </a>
      could together topple the dictatorship of
      <a href="/wiki/Pervez_Musharraf" title="Pervez Musharraf">
       Pervez Musharraf
      </a>
      in Pakistan, and the government of Iran.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>

      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Atef was killed, along with his guard Abu Ali al-Yafi'i and six others,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wnc_37-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-wnc-37">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        37
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
       <a href="#cite_note-38">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        38
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in a
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       U.S.
      </a>
      air-strike on his home near Kabul during the
      <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001present)">
       U.S. invasion of Afghanistan
      </a>
      at some time during November 1416, 2001.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guard_1-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-guard-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      American intelligence intercepted communications from those digging through the rubble of Atef's home, leading them to believe they had been successful in killing him.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reports_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-reports-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      According to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combatting_Terrorism_Center" title="Combatting Terrorism Center">
       Combatting Terrorism Center
      </a>
      he was killed in a strike on an "al Qa'ida safehouse".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CtcAbHafs_39-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-CtcAbHafs-39">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        39
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Although initial reports said that American
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombers" title="Bombers">
       bomber aircraft
      </a>
      had destroyed the house, it was later revealed that
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MQ-1_Predator" title="MQ-1 Predator">
       MQ-1 Predator
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAV" title="UAV">
       UAVs
      </a>
      had attacked the structure.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
       <a href="#cite_note-40">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        40
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
       <a href="#cite_note-41">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        41
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Donald_Rumsfeld" title="Donald Rumsfeld">
       Donald Rumsfeld
      </a>
      was initially cautious and indicated only that reports of Atef's death "seem authoritative".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-compound_42-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-compound-42">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        42
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His death was confirmed when the ambassador of the
      <a href="/wiki/Taliban" title="Taliban">
       Taliban
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abd_Al-Salam_Dhaif&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abd Al-Salam Dhaif (page does not exist)">
       Abd Al-Salam Dhaif
      </a>
      , said three days later, "Abu Hafs al-Masri died from injuries he suffered after US warplanes bombed his house near Kabul."
     </p>
     <p>
      When American forces sifted through the rubble of his house, they found a number of videocassettes, including five that carried
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martyrdom" title="Martyrdom">
       martyrdom
      </a>
      messages from
      <a href="/wiki/Abderraouf_Jdey" title="Abderraouf Jdey">
       Abderraouf Jdey
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ramzi_bin_al-Shibh" title="Ramzi bin al-Shibh">
       Ramzi bin al-Shibh
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Sa%27id_Ali_Hasan" title="Muhammad Sa'id Ali Hasan">
       Muhammad Sa'id Ali Hasan
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abdul_Rahim_Jenko" title="Abdul Rahim Jenko">
       Abd Al-Rahim
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khalid_Ibn_Muhammad_Al-Juhani" title="Khalid Ibn Muhammad Al-Juhani">
       Khalid Ibn Muhammad Al-Juhani
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
       <a href="#cite_note-43">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        43
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Another videocassette included
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hashim_Abas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hashim Abas (page does not exist)">
       Hashim Abas
      </a>
      casing American institutions in Singapore for
      <a href="/wiki/Singapore_embassies_attack_plot" title="Singapore embassies attack plot">
       possible attack
      </a>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/Jemaah_Islamiyah" title="Jemaah Islamiyah">
       Jemaah Islamiyah
      </a>
      in 1999,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
       <a href="#cite_note-44">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        44
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but was not turned over to Singapore authorities until December 14.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">
       <a href="#cite_note-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It showed a bus station where American military personnel departed for their base, a temple adjoining American military barracks, a park where off-duty soldiers gathered and the Eagle's Club restaurant owned by the American government for its local workers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">
       <a href="#cite_note-46">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        46
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" title="Wall Street Journal">
        Wall Street Journal
       </a>
      </i>
      reporter
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Cullison&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alan Cullison (page does not exist)">
       Alan Cullison
      </a>
      purchased two computers that had been looted from the home on the
      <a href="/wiki/Black_market" title="Black market">
       black market
      </a>
      , and noted that while Atef's computer had relatively few files, the other computer appears to have belonged to
      <a href="/wiki/Ayman_al-Zawahiri" title="Ayman al-Zawahiri">
       Ayman al-Zawahiri
      </a>
      and held nearly a thousand files, including some of importance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47">
       <a href="#cite_note-47">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        47
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On November 8, Bin Laden delivered a joint eulogy for Atef and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jummah_Khan_Namangani" title="Jummah Khan Namangani">
       Jummah Khan Namangani
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48">
       <a href="#cite_note-48">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        48
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Following his death, it was rumored that
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saif_al-Adl" title="Saif al-Adl">
       Saif al-Adl
      </a>
      would take over his position as Military Chief of al-Qaeda.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guard_1-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-guard-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49">
       <a href="#cite_note-49">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        49
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Under interrogation, a number of suspected militants including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ibn_Shaykh_al-Libi" title="Ibn Shaykh al-Libi">
       Ibn Shaykh al-Libi
      </a>
      later invented fictitious ties from Atef to other non-involved entities to distract American attention from their true colleagues. al-Libi told interrogators that Atef had sent an emissary named Abu Abdullah to
      <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">
       Iraq
      </a>
      to obtain
      <a href="/wiki/Chemical_warfare" title="Chemical warfare">
       chemical
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Biological_warfare" title="Biological warfare">
       biological warfare
      </a>
      training for two al-Qaeda members in December 2000. This led the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIA" title="CIA">
       CIA
      </a>
      to release a paper tying al-Qaeda to Iraq in January 2003, and justifying the invasion two months later, which arguably relieved some of the pressure on militants in Afghanistan.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50">
       <a href="#cite_note-50">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        50
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Atef appeared in a video released in September 2006 that showed the planning of the September 11 attacks.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cnn20060908_51-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Cnn20060908-51">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        51
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Atef has been named as a conspirator in the conspiracy charges against several of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guantanamo_captive" title="Guantanamo captive">
       Guantanamo captives
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChargeMohammedHazaAlDarbiPdf_52-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ChargeMohammedHazaAlDarbiPdf-52">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        52
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In March 2002, Bosnian security forces raided a
      <a href="/wiki/Benevolence_International_Foundation" title="Benevolence International Foundation">
       Benevolence International Foundation
      </a>
      office in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarejevo" title="Sarejevo">
       Sarejevo
      </a>
      and seized a computer which contained a number of documents suggesting a degree of complicity with al-Qaeda, including a letter to Atef from
      <a href="/wiki/Enaam_Arnaout" title="Enaam Arnaout">
       Enaam Arnaout
      </a>
      stating that "the organization loaned us a
      <a href="/wiki/Howitzer" title="Howitzer">
       Howitzer
      </a>
      cannon, and it must be returned so that it can be transferred to Kabul".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shay_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-shay-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
