          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_cowboyaliens/" rel="attachment wp-att-93175">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93175" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_cowboyaliens.jpg" title="ttt_cowboyaliens" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           In many ways, the western is a dead genre, but I like to think of it as the genre that just wont die. Like trying to put down the toughest, meanest gunslinger in the old west, the western film keeps popping back up in the least expected times and places but, not always in the traditional style were used to seeing. Call it survival of the fittest, but the western is far from dead, as is apparent with Jon Favreaus new COWBOYS &amp; ALIENS, which opens this Friday, July 29th, combining the western with the science-fiction, creating a crossover with twice the fun. In honor of the opening of this rare hybrid, were looking at our favorite Western Crossover films this week in Top Ten Tuesday.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_goodbadweird/" rel="attachment wp-att-93162">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93162" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_goodbadweird.jpg" title="ttt_goodbadweird" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Honorable Mention: THE GOOD, THE BAD &amp; THE WEIRD
          </h3>
          <p>
           This modern ode to the western from Korean filmmaker Jee-woon Kim is less of a hybrid than an over-the-top, good time cinematic high that doesnt quit. Just about every element of this film is derived from the handbook for making western films, but injected with enough steroids to make the average bat boy a contender for breaking Hank Aarons homerun record. Horse chases, gun fights, speeding locomotives and thieving outlaws, everything you want in a western is here for the taking, with a generous bonus of Asian action-adventure flair.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_paintyourwagon/" rel="attachment wp-att-93161">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93161" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_paintyourwagon.jpg" title="ttt_paintyourwagon" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           10. PAINT YOUR WAGON
          </h2>
          <p>
           Go ahead and laugh, mock it if you will, but the truth is PAINT YOUR WAGON isnt as bad as everyone makes it out to be. While some may run away screaming at the idea of hearing Clint Eastwood sing, there are far worse voices in the music industry. This western musical is more western than musical and co-stars Lee Marvin (DIRTY DOZEN) and Jean Seburg (BREATHLESS). While there were other musicals such as OKLAHOMA and SEVEN BRIDES FOR SEVEN BROTHERS that employ the old west, theyve all been light on the elements that make great westerns. This is where PAINT YOUR WAGON takes its place above the rest.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_backtothefuture3/" rel="attachment wp-att-93163">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93163" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_backtothefuture3.jpg" title="ttt_backtothefuture3" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           09. BACK TO THE FUTURE, PART III
          </h2>
          <p>
           Mix sci-fi and western  with a dash of comedy thrown in  and you come up with the perfect cowboy movie in BACK TO THE FUTURE, PART III. This time director Bob Zemeckis has Marty McFly and Doc Brown travelling from 1985 to 1885 for one last round-up. McFly faces Biff Tannens ancestor, Buford Mad Dog Tannen, in a final showdown while Doc finds true love with a pioneer woman who loves Jules Verne. While you may feel at times like youre watching John Fords STAGECOACH, Part 3 is deftly filled with nods to both genres with names like Clint Eastwood, the town saloon and a runaway steam locomotive that transforms into a hover-train.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_roadwarrior/" rel="attachment wp-att-93165">
            <img alt="" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_roadwarrior.jpg" title="ttt_roadwarrior" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           08. MAD MAX 2: THE ROAD WARRIOR
          </h2>
          <p>
           A shotgun wielding hero, the frontier settlers and a band of baddies  who  gallop into town encapsulates everything a classic western should  be except that George Millers MAD MAX 2: THE ROAD WARRIOR is set in a   bleak wasteland where the villains are bikers trying to take over the   oil fields from the settlers and Mel Gibsons Max, a man who wandered   into the wasteland, rides in to save the day on his Pursuit Special   vehicle. Cant you hear the twangy sounds of Clint Eastwoods HIGH   PLAINS DRIFTER resonating throughout?
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_rango/" rel="attachment wp-att-93169">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93169" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_rango.jpg" title="ttt_rango" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           07. RANGO
          </h2>
          <p>
           How many animated westerns are there to choose from? Exactly. Not many. However, RANGO doesnt make this list on technicalities. Surprisingly, this was one of the best animated films Ive seen in recent years and definitely captures the essence of some of the best and most unique western films. Filled with stylistic influences and homages to the greats, RANGO takes a lost pet chameleon and throws him into a strange scenario reminiscent of The Man With No Name.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_eltopo/" rel="attachment wp-att-93164">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93164" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_eltopo.jpg" title="ttt_eltopo" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           06. EL TOPO
          </h2>
          <p>
           Mexican writer, director and star Alejandro Jodorowsky truly puts the weird in the western with EL TOPO, creating a journey of one legend and his six-year old son. El Topo is on a quest to become the greatest fighter, as he confronts the warrior masters, each one with a unique specialty. This odd-ball twist on the western has the taste of so many old school martial arts films, creating one of the most bizarre and surreal cult spaghetti westerns of all time.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_redsun/" rel="attachment wp-att-93172">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93172" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_redsun.jpg" title="ttt_redsun" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           05. RED SUN
          </h2>
          <p>
           With extraordinary connections like SEVEN SAMURAI inspiring THE MAGNIFICENT SEVEN, it was only a matter of time before someone melded the samurai film with the western. RED SUN is just that very brilliant melding. Director Terence Young places the western icon Charles Bronson and the eastern icon Toshiro Mifune on the big screen together, a meeting of two cultures toward a common goal against a common enemy. Gunslinger ans samurai paired in a way that is as bold as it is unexpectedly natural.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_westworld/" rel="attachment wp-att-93166">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93166" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_westworld.jpg" title="ttt_westworld" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           04. WESTWORLD
          </h2>
          <p>
           Why dont you make arrangements to take our hovercraft to Medieval World, Roman World and Westworld. Contact us today, or see your travel agent. Boy, have we got a vacation for you. However, not quite the one the guests of the DELOS Resort had in mind. Michael Crichtons WESTWORLD pits vacationer Richard Benjamin against Yul Brenners Gunslinger in the ultimate gun fight. A premise thats not entirely far-fetched, this twist on the classic westerner embodies a futuristic, adventure crossover. This time, the conflict is between Cowboys and Robots.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_sukiyaki/" rel="attachment wp-att-93167">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93167" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_sukiyaki.jpg" title="ttt_sukiyaki" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           03. SUKIYAKI WESTERN DJANGO
          </h2>
          <p>
           Japanese filmmaker Takashi Miike takes blending the east and the west one wild step further with SUKIYAKI WESTERN DJANGO. Directly inspired by Sergio Leones spaghetti westerns, a stranger crosses paths with two rival clans, both seeking fortune in a small town. The mysterious gunslinger offers his services to both sides, planning to take advantage of the situation the entire time. Being Takashi Miike, he adds his own trademark flair in both the characters and the visual style.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_serenity/" rel="attachment wp-att-93170">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93170" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_serenity.jpg" title="ttt_serenity" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           02. SERENITY
          </h2>
          <p>
           There is no shortage of fandom for this contemporary classic, spawned from Joss Whedon and his TV series
           <em>
            Firefly
           </em>
           , but its reputation is well-earned. Those who havent seen SERENITY would not understand, but this affectionately coined Space Western teleports the trademark elements of a good western and character traits into a science-fiction adventure story in space. The heroic cowboy, the bounty hunter, the frontier settlers, the dangerous uncharted territories, the (deadly) damsel in distress its all there and tons of fun.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wearemoviegeeks.com/2011/07/top-ten-tuesday-crossover-westerns/ttt_blazingsaddles/" rel="attachment wp-att-93171">
            <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-93171" height="300" src="http://fo4mw16y1z42edr6j2m4n6vt.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/ttt_blazingsaddles.jpg" title="ttt_blazingsaddles" width="560"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           01. BLAZING SADDLES
          </h2>
          <p>
           Mel Brooks comical parody of the old west may seem like an unlikely choice, but its so pitch-perfect and hilarious that it had to be the top pick. BLAZING SADDLES has every necessary element of a classic western, but Brooks turns it all into a farce, pushing boundaries and poking fun at the inherent flaws of the genre. Cleavon Little plays the black sheriff Bart, while Gene Wilder plays Jim, his cowardly deputy. The top notch comedic cast runs away with their exaggerated stereotypes while delivering dialogue that is often politically incorrect, but intelligently appropriate.
          </p>

               <p>
                Paint Your Wagon and Blazing Saddles are two of my favorite movies. Yes, PYW was too long, and gasp, had singing, and a lack of bloody shootouts, but still has a terrific underlying philosophy of Man, Nature and Progress. Marvins singing was absolutely perfect for his character, and Clints light singing wasnt bad at all  no one bitched about him singing in Honkytonk Man. This may be the only major movie made about one of the most important events in US history  the California Gold Rush. The scenery was spectacular, the costumes historically accurate, and the muddy reality of life in a mining camp and boomtown was not glossed over as in most westerns.
