      <header class="post-title">
       <h1 class="heading">
        How to be a pro at Pinterest
       </h1>
       <p class="post-byline">
        <time datetime="2015-Feb-FriT07:02:54UTC" pubdate="pubdate">
         February 13, 2015
        </time>
       </p>
      </header>
      <figure class="feature">
       <img alt="How to be a pro at Pinterest" class="attachment-watson_featured wp-post-image" height="400" src="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/How-to-be-a-pro-at-Pinterest-feature-image-new-608x400.jpg" width="608"/>
       <figcaption>
       </figcaption>
      </figure>
      <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14766448" data-link="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/2015/02/13/how-to-be-a-pro-at-pinterest/" data-summary="Pinterest is the second biggest referrer to my site after Bloglovin, and in my opinion, its highly undervalued as a marketing tool for bloggers! Here are my 6 top tips for how to be a pro at Pinterest." data-title="How to be a pro at Pinterest">
      </div>
      <h3>
       I get more views from Pinterest than any other social media platform.
      </h3>
      <p>
       Itsthe second biggest referrer to my site after Bloglovin, and in my opinion, its highly undervalued as a marketing tool for bloggers!
      </p>
      <p>
       Ive been on Pinterestsince 2011 and have been embarrassingly addicted ever since. In the 6 months since I created my account for
       <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/frugaltravel/" target="_blank">
        A Globe Well Travelled
       </a>
       , Ive had almost 3000 referrals from the site.
      </p>
      <p>
       The best thing is, you dont need to have loads of followers on Pinterest for you to receive traffic from it. If youre smart about how you pin, then other userswill do most of the work for you!
      </p>
      <p>
       Here are my 6 top tips for being a pro at Pinterest.Click on the images for larger versions.
      </p>
      <hr/>
      <h4>
       <a href="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot01.jpg">
        <img alt="Create exciting visuals with large text" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4943" height="420" src="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot01.jpg" width="850"/>
       </a>
      </h4>
      <h4>
       <strong>
        1. Create exciting visuals for your posts
       </strong>
      </h4>
      <p>
       I make a square version of the feature images I use on all my posts, often enlarging the text size as well. This means when its posted on Pinterest, its easily readable at a glance. You only have a fraction of a second to grab someones attention with a pin, so make sure it counts!
      </p>
      <p>
       Heres where Ive revealed the secrets on
       <a href="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/2015/03/13/create-awesome-feature-images-blog-posts/" title="How to create awesome feature images for your blog posts">
        how I make my blog post feature images
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h4>
       <a href="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot02.jpg">
        <img alt="Tall photos" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4944" height="420" src="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot02.jpg" width="850"/>
       </a>
      </h4>
      <h4>
       <strong>
        2. UPLOAD TALL OR SQUARE PHOTOS
       </strong>
      </h4>
      <p>
       Landscape photos dont do so well on Pinterest, mainly because of the format of the site. Since imagesare displayed in columns, tall photos are more likely to stand out.
      </p>
      <p>
       I also upload square photos and I find they do fairlywell. All of my Instagram images end up on Pinterest and are re-pinned regularly.
      </p>
      <p>
       Make sure youre uploading good quality images. Nothing below 500px! I hate openinga thumbnail image only to find that its actually thumbnail size. Ergh, the worst.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <a href="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot03.jpg">
        <img alt="Intriguing descriptions" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4945" height="420" src="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot03.jpg" width="850"/>
       </a>
      </h4>
      <h4>
       <strong>
        3. USE INTRIGUING DESCRIPTIONS AND INCLUDE YOUR BLOG NAME
       </strong>
      </h4>
      <p>
       You want to entice people to click on the post, and users willwant to know what will happenafter they do. A simple description with the post title and your blog name is usually enough.
      </p>
      <p>
       If the image features a location photo, include the place in the description. Theres nothing worse than findinga lovely photo and not knowing where it was taken!
      </p>
      <h4>
       <a href="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot04.jpg">
        <img alt="Link to your website" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4946" height="420" src="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot04.jpg" width="850"/>
       </a>
      </h4>
      <h4>
       <strong>
        4. LINK TO YOUR WEBSITE
       </strong>
      </h4>
      <p>
       Immediatelyafter you upload an image, click edit (the pencil icon in the top right corner when you hover over the image), then paste the link to your website or post in the source box. This way people will be directed to your site when they click on your pin.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <a href="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot05.jpg">
        <img alt="Make your home page look sexy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4947" height="420" src="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot05.jpg" width="850"/>
       </a>
      </h4>
      <h4>
       <strong>
        5. Make your home page look sexy
       </strong>
      </h4>
      <p>
       This is the landing page that people will see first when they click on your name, so youve got to do everythingyou can to draw them in from here. The top row of boards should be the ones you want people to view most. You can rearrange your boards by dragging them around the page.
      </p>
      <p>
       Ive made some cover images for my top row of boards as I think this makes them more enticing, but you really just need to make sure the cover photois a sexy one(which you can change by clicking edit underneaththe board).
      </p>
      <h4>
       <a href="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot06.jpg">
        <img alt="Pin to group boards" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4948" height="420" src="http://aglobewelltravelled.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2015/02/Screenshot06.jpg" width="850"/>
       </a>
      </h4>
      <h4>
       <strong>
        6. Pin to group boards
       </strong>
      </h4>
      <p>
       This is by far the best way to get your pins seen by the most users. Find some group boards (defined by the grey people icon in the top right corner) and request to be added as a pinner. Then when you pin something into the group board, everyone who is following that board will see your pin. Instant exposure!
      </p>
      <hr/>
      <p>
       If youre looking to gain more followers on Pinterest, Drew from The Hungry Partier has some really good tipsin
