       <h1 class="headline">
        UFC, The Time is Now
       </h1>
       <span class="post-byline">
        By
        <a href="http://www.MMAAlive.com/author/dale-harwood" rel="author" title="Posts by Dale Harwood">
         Dale Harwood
        </a>
        on December 22, 2014
       </span>
      </div>
      <!--title-main-->
      <div id="home-main">
       <div class="post-1006 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-ufc-news tag-76 tag-the-time-is-now tag-ufc" id="post-area">
        <div id="social-box">
         <ul class="post-social">

           <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="horizontal" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.MMAAlive.com%2Fufc-the-time-is-now.html&amp;media=http://www.MMAAlive.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/UFC-Images.jpg&amp;description=UFC, The Time is Now">

        <!--social-box-->
        <div id="content-area">
         <div class="post-image">
          <img alt="UFC Images" class="attachment-post-thumb wp-post-image" height="338" src="http://www.MMAAlive.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/UFC-Images.jpg" width="600"/>
         </div>
         <!--post-image-->
         <p>
          <strong>
           The Time Is Now
          </strong>
          is the fitting UFC slogan for the list of epic upcoming fights that will kick off 2015.  The list of fights set for January and February 2015 are headlined by some of the UFC biggest names.
         </p>
         <p>
          Setting off the New Year will be the highly anticipated grudge match between the sports pound for pound king, light heavyweight champion Jon Jones (20, 1, and 0) and undefeated challenger Daniel Cormier (15-0-0) at UFC 182.  The event held on January 3rd, 2015 in Las Vegas, NV. will be the culmination of the feud that came to a boiling point at UFC 178-media day this past August, when Jones and Cormier fought in the lobby of the MGM Grand.  Since the altercation and subsequent war of words this fight has become one of the biggest and most eagerly anticipated fights in the history of the promotion.
         </p>
         <p>
          The second event of the UFC The Time is Now roster will be UFC Fight Night McGregor vs. Siver, on Jan. 18 2015 in Boston, MA.  This 5 round Featherweight bout will feature one of the most exciting and talked about fighters in the sport The Notorious Conor McGregor (16-2-0) as he attempts to take one more step toward a shot at the title.  Opposite McGregor will be the 8th ranked Dennis Siver.  Siver is a striker and former German kickboxing champion.
         </p>
         <p>
          Up next is a Light Heavyweight bout set for January 24th in Stockholm, Sweden between #1 ranked Alexander Gustafson (16-2-0) and #3 ranked Anthony Rumble Johnson (18-4-0).  Gustafsons most notable performance was a close loss in September of 2013 against lightweight champ Jon Jones.  The 6-5 Gustafson impressed many during that match and some believed that he deserved to win the fight.   His opponent, Anthony Johnson has had one of the most incredible and noteworthy comebacks in the sport.  After returning to the UFC, Johnson has tallied impressive victories against the legendary Antonio Rogeiro Nogueria and high level wrestler Phil Davis.  Johnsons power and athleticism lead many to believe that he has the makings of a potential UFC champion.
         </p>
         <p>
          Next, two legends take center stage at UFC 183 as former pound for pound king and Greatest of All Time Anderson Silva makes his return to the cage against MMA superstar Nick Diaz.  The return will mark the first time in the cage for the 39 year-old Silva since he suffered a horrifying leg injury in a UFC 168 loss against Chris Weidman.  Diaz, the 31 year old Gracie Jiu Jitsu black belt, will be competing in his first bout since his 2013 loss against George St. Pierre for the UFC welterweight belt.
         </p>
         <p>
          Rounding out UFC, The time is Now is UFC 184, to be held on Feb 28, 2015, in Los Angeles, CA.  This event features middleweight champion Chris Weidman (12-0-0) vs. #3 ranked challenger Vitor Belfort (24-10-0).  This bout is a clash of styles pitting the decorated All-American wrestler Weidman against the explosive striker Belfort.  Co-headlining this fight card is womens bantamweight champion Ronda Rousey (10-0-0) vs #1 ranked contender Cat Zingano for the womens bantamweight title.  Rousey, the most dominate woman in the sport, will likely face her toughest test in the undefeated Zingano and this will mark the first time in the history of the UFC that the two best women fighters on the planet at 135lbs will face each other for the title.
         </p>
        </div>
