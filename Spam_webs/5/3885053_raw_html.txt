        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-1.jpg">
          <img alt="8-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-515" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-1.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The cloth includes 2 layers of blue jean material and then several layers of terry cloth towels.
        </p>
        <p>
         I realize that there would be an almost infinite number of variables, this is just a common example of thick clothing.
        </p>
        <p>
         First, a .22 LR from a pistol.
        </p>
        <p>
         I shot a CCI Minimag HP.
        </p>
        <p>
         It went through the clothing and through the water jug and embedded itself into the first pine board.
        </p>
        <p>
         I dug around the bullet to help you see it.
        </p>
        <p>
         It did not expand, but was slightly sideways in the board.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-2.jpg">
          <img alt="8-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-517" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-2.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then the mighty .32 ACP from my Keltec P-32.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tman caught me in full recoil.
        </p>
        <p>
         Just kidding. There was very little recoil.
        </p>
        <p>
         We shot a Federal Hydrashock.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-3.jpg">
          <img alt="8-3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-518" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-3.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The round went through the clothing and water jug and was in the first pine board.
        </p>
        <p>
         I dug it out to see it better.
        </p>
        <p>
         As you can see, it did not open up at all.
        </p>
        <p>
         This was puzzling, as we have shot water jugs before with this round and they expanded perfectly.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_519" style="width: 550px">
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-4.jpg">
          <img alt="This is it." class="size-full wp-image-519" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-4.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          This is it.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         How about a 9mm JHP.
        </p>
        <p>
         I used a 115 grain JHP out of a Beretta 92FS.
        </p>
        <p>
         It went through the clothing, water, 3 pine boards, and somewhere beyond.
        </p>
        <p>
         We were unable to recover the bullet.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-5.jpg">
          <img alt="8-5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-527" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-5.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         We then realized that we needed something to stop any bullet that went through the BoT.
        </p>
        <p>
         We then added a solid cement block in case any other rounds went through all the pine boards.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was solid cement.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-6.jpg">
          <img alt="8-6" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-528" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-6.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then the .45 ACP JHP.
        </p>
        <p>
         I used a Black Talon I had.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-7.jpg">
          <img alt="8-7" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-529" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-7.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It went through the clothing and water jug, and two pine boards.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was found laying on the bottom of the box, un-expanded, but crushed slightly sideways.
        </p>
        <p>
         You will also notice a piece of green cloth that it drug through the water jug with itself.
        </p>
        <div class="taboola">
         Continued below...
         <div id="taboola-below-article-thumbnails-2nd">
         </div>
         <script type="text/javascript">
          window._taboola = window._taboola || [];
								  _taboola.push({
								    mode: "thumbnails-c",
								    container: "taboola-below-article-thumbnails-2nd",
								    placement: "Below Article Thumbnails 2nd",
								    target_type: "mix"
								  });
         </script>
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-8.jpg">
          <img alt="8-8" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-531" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-8.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then the .357 S&amp;W Magnum, 158 grain JHP.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_532" style="width: 550px">
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-9.jpg">
          <img alt="A nice pic of the mighty .357 Mag at work." class="size-full wp-image-532" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-9.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          A nice pic of the mighty .357 Mag at work.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         The bullet went through the entire set-up and was found on the bottom of the box after the last board.
        </p>
        <p>
         Damage to the bullet was mostly sideways.
        </p>
        <p>
         It did not expand very well.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_534" style="width: 550px">
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-10.jpg">
          <img alt="This is it." class="size-full wp-image-534" height="449" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-10.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          This is it.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Rumors had it that the .30 Carbine had problems penetrating the thick clothing of the North Koreans during the Korean War.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets see what happens.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-11.jpg">
          <img alt="8-11" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-535" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-11.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The bullet, a military Ball, went through the entire box set-up, blew up the water jug, and flattened itself out against the solid concrete block, knocking out a big chunk of cement.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-12.jpg">
          <img alt="8-12" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-536" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-12.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Then the favorite of
         <a href="http://www.AR15.com">
          AR15.com
         </a>
         , the AR15 (what else?).
        </p>
        <p>
         Using XM-193.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-13.jpg">
          <img alt="8-13" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-537" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-13.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         And what a fine job it did.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is the first pine board.
        </p>
        <p>
         Notice the hole by the bullet tip.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was traveling sideways.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-14.jpg">
          <img alt="8-14" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-538" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-14.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         We felt that we needed some control rounds to compare with the first results.
        </p>
        <p>
         We shot the .357 Mag again, without the clothing in front of the jug.
        </p>
        <p>
         Notice the prefectly mushroomed bullet on the left that was recovered, as compared to the first one on the right.
        </p>
        <p>
         Big difference in performance.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-15.jpg">
          <img alt="8-15" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-539" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-15.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         We tried the .45ACP again, without the clothing.
        </p>
        <p>
         I included this photo as it is pretty neat.
        </p>
        <p>
         The pistol is in recoil, the slide is coming back, the bullet is in route to the target, but has not hit the jug yet.
        </p>
        <p>
         That bullet is in the air somewhere between the pistol and the jug.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-16.jpg">
          <img alt="8-16" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-540" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-16.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Notice the Federal Hydrashock on the right, perfectly mushroomed, compared to the poorly expanded bullet on the left.
        </p>
        <p>
         Big difference.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_559" style="width: 550px">
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-17.jpg">
          <img alt="This is the result." class="size-full wp-image-559" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-17.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          This is the result.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Lastly, a round of Corbon 9mm into the clean jug.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-18.jpg">
          <img alt="8-18" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-560" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-18.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is all we could find of the bullet after the water jug.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-19.jpg">
          <img alt="8-19" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-561" height="404" src="http://cdn.theboxotruth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/8-19.jpg" width="540"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h5>
         Lessons learned:
        </h5>
        <ol>
         <li>
          As Forrest Gump said, You never know what your going to get. We were surprised by the difference the clothing made in the pistol rounds performance. It sure seemed to make a big difference in whether the bullets expanded or not.
          <br/>
          Conclusion: Heavy layers of clothing can prevent Jacket Hollow Points (JHP) from expanding.
         </li>
         <li>
          The clothing didnt seem to be much of a factor for rifle rounds.
         </li>
         <li>
          If the North Koreans seemed uneffected by the .30 Carbine rounds, they were probably not being hit. Because clothing wont stop a 30 Carbine.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         Thanks to Tman for taking the pictures and for donating his best pair of blue jeans for the tests.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
