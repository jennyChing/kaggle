     <h3 class="first">
      What is xmonad?
     </h3>
     <p>
      xmonad is a dynamically tiling X11 window manager that is written
            and configured in Haskell. In a normal WM, you spend half your time
            aligning and searching for windows.
      <em>
       xmonad makes work
            easier
      </em>
      , by automating this.
     </p>
     <h3>

     <h3>
      Why should I use xmonad?
     </h3>
     <dl>
      <dt>
       xmonad is
       <em>
        tiling
       </em>
       .
      </dt>
      <dd>
       xmonad automates the common task of
              arranging windows, so you can concentrate on getting stuff
              done.
      </dd>
      <dt>
       xmonad is
       <em>
        minimal
       </em>
       .
      </dt>
      <dd>
       out of the box, no window decorations, no status bar, no icon dock.
              just clean lines and efficiency.
      </dd>
      <dt>
       xmonad is
       <em>
        stable
       </em>
       .
      </dt>
      <dd>
       <a href="http://haskell.org/">
        haskell
       </a>
       + smart programming
              practices guarantee a
       <em>
        crash-free
       </em>
       experience.
      </dd>
      <dt>
       xmonad is
       <em>
        extensible
       </em>
       .
      </dt>
      <dd>
       it sports a vibrant
       <a href="documentation.html">
        extension
              library
       </a>
       , including support for window decorations, status
              bars, and icon docks.
      </dd>
      <dt>
       xmonad is
       <em>
        featureful
       </em>
       .
      </dt>
      <dd>
       core features like
       <em>
        per-screen workspaces
       </em>
       , true
              xinerama support and managehooks can't be found in any other
              wm.
      </dd>
      <dt>
       xmonad is
       <em>
        easy
       </em>
       .
      </dt>
      <dd>
       we work hard to make common configuration tasks one-liners.
      </dd>
      <dt>
       xmonad is
       <em>
        friendly
       </em>
       .
      </dt>
      <dd>
       an active, friendly
       <a href="http://www.haskell.org/mailman/listinfo/xmonad">
        mailing
              list
       </a>
       and
       <a href="community.html">
        irc channel
       </a>
       are
       <em>
        waiting to help
       </em>
       you get up and running.
      </dd>
     </dl>
