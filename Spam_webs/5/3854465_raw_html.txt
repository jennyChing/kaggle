        <p>
         Have you heard of Six Week Bran Muffins? I have made them for a hundred years! They are moist, delicious and so convenient to have in your fridge.This batter can be stored in your refrigerator, unbaked for 6 weeks. You only need to scoop out the amount you want.
        </p>
        <p>
         Your biggest challenge will be finding bowls and a container large enough to hold this rather large volume of ingredients. But, trust me, its worth the search!
        </p>
        <p>
         Over sleep? Throw a batch in the oven while having your shower.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mother-in-Law drops in and the cupboards are bare? Throw a batch in the oven while making coffee. She will be so impressed.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cleaning out the book bag at 11PM and find out you are supplying tomorrows school snack? No problemfill up those muffin tins and throw them in the oven.
        </p>
        <p>
         Feeling a bit peckish? Throw a couple in the oven while the kettle is boiling for tea.
        </p>
        <p>
         You get the idea. Having this container tucked away in the fridge will save your bacon on many occasions.
        </p>
        <p>
         Makes approximately 100 muffinsthat is a lot of muffins!!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20842" height="425" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/059-1-edited.png" width="616"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         As I mention in the notes below, the brans may be mixed up and the raisins are optional. I am not certain of the volume of this container, its not marked. But I would say it is a minimum of four quarts.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20843" height="466" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/062-edited.png" width="616"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         For a creamy mixture, be sure your butter has been softened. I used a dark brown sugar (it has more molasses in it), but any brown sugar would work.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20844" height="468" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/063-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Cream together until light and fluffy. (Side note  Im not sure whats happening to the enamel on my beaterits starting to peel off . Must be the constant uselol)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20845" height="409" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/068-edited.png" width="616"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         While the sugars and butters are creaming together, add the water to your cereals.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20846" height="456" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/070-edited.png" width="616"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Give it a little stir and let it sit.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20847" height="448" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/072-edited.png" width="616"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Whisk your dry ingredients together.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20848" height="468" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/075-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Add your eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20849" height="468" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/078-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Add a litre (4 cups ) of buttermilk.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20850" height="544" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/084-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Then add you flour in 3 or 4 batches, mixing gently after each addition.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20851" height="452" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/087-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Dont over mix the batter by machine or the muffins will be tough. Just mix enough to incorporate the flour and then switch to a spatula and gently mix by hand.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20852" height="466" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/088-edited.png" width="616"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Dont forget to get all the flour from the very bottom of the bowl, flour likes to hide down there.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20853" height="468" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/090-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Add the mixed batter, a quarter to a third at a time, to the now mushy cereals.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20854" height="468" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/093-edited.png" width="618"/>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20855" height="466" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/095-edited.png" width="616"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Keep adding until all the batter has been incorporated with the cereals and the entire works has been thoroughly combined.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20856" height="437" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/096-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Once everything has been mixed together, the batter will look something like this.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20857" height="458" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/098-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Scoop into sprayed muffin tins. I use this handy 1/2 cup scoop, its the perfect size! (I also have a 1/4 cup scoop that is perfect for cookies.)
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20858" height="472" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/099-edited.png" width="616"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20859" height="459" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/103-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Store the unused batter in a tightly covered container for up to six weeks. If you feel like a muffin, just scoop out two or three, bake and presto, homemade muffins in minutes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20860" height="468" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/106-edited.png" width="618"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         To keep track of how long the batter has been in the fridge, I always mark the date I made it on the calendar.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Six Week Bran Muffins" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20861" height="702" src="http://oldermommystillyummy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/113-edited-with-font.png" width="646"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         The finished product!
        </p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 cups Bran flake cereal*
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups All bran cereal*
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups boiling water
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup butter, softened
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cups white sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cups brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 cups buttermilk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup molasses
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            5 cups flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Tbsp baking soda
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tbsp baking powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups raisins**
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a LARGE food safe container put cereals and boiling water. Stir and let sit.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Whisk together dry ingredients (flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt and raisins) Set aside.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In mixing bowl, cream butter and sugars together until light and fluffy.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Mix in buttermilk and then molasses.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add flour mixture and stir gently to combine
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add to cereals mixture in large container. Stir to combine.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Fill greased muffin tins,  full and bake in a 375 degree oven for 20-25 minutes or until a cake tester comes out clean.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cool in tins for 5-10 minutes, remove from pans to wire rack.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           *Brans may be switched up to use:
           <br/>
           - 2 cups bran flakes and 4 cups bran cereal
           <br/>
           - 3 cups of each cereal
           <br/>
           - use natural bran to replace one of the cereals
           <br/>
           I have tried all of these variations and they work just fine
           <br/>
           <br/>
           **You don't have to stick to the addition of raisins if that's not to your taste. Substitute any dried, chopped, raisin sized fruit or I have used chocolate chips...delicious and especially loved by the kids..
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            NOTE -
           </strong>
           You must store this muffin batter in your refrigerator!
          </div>

             I am so sorry Maria, that is a very important bit of information, isnt it?  Thank you for pointing this omission out, I have updated the recipe on the blog.
             <br/>
             You should:
             <br/>
             Fill greased muffin tins, 3/4 full.
             <br/>
             Bake in a 375 degree oven for 20-25 minutes or until a cake tester comes out clean.
             <br/>
             Cool in tins for 5-10 minutes, remove from pans to wire rack.

  var form = document.getElementById("mad_mimi_signup_form"),

      email = /.+@.+\..+/;
      
  form.onsubmit = function(event) {
    var isValid = validate();
    if(!isValid) {
      for(var i = 0; i &lt; form.elements.length; ++i) {
        var input = form.elements[i];
        if(input.className.indexOf("required") &gt;= 0) {
          input.onchange = validate;
        }
      }
      return false;
    }
    if(form.getAttribute("target") != "_blank") {
      form.className = "mimi_submitting";
      submit.value = submit.getAttribute("data-submitting-text");    
  		submit.disabled = true;
  		submit.className = "disabled";
    }

    setTimeout(function() {
      for(var i = 0; i &lt; form.getElementsByTagName("input").length; ++i) {
        var input = form.getElementsByTagName("input")[i];
        if(input.getAttribute("type") == "text") {
          input.value = "";
        }
        if(input.id == "signup_email") {
          input.placeholder = "you@example.com";
        } else {
          input.placeholder = "";
        }
      }
    }, 3000);
  };
  
  function validate() {
    var isValid = true;
    
    for(var i = 0; i &lt; form.elements.length; ++i) {
      var input = form.elements[i],
          allDivs = input.parentNode.getElementsByTagName("div");

      if(input.className.indexOf("required") &gt;= 0) {
        if(input.id == "signup_email") {
          if(!email.test(input.value)) {
            emailErrorMessage(input, allDivs);
            isValid = false;
          } else {
            removeErrorMessage(input, allDivs);
          }
        } else {
          if((input.type == "checkbox" &amp;&amp; !input.checked) || input.value == "" || input.value == "-1") {
            fieldErrorMessage(input, allDivs);
            isValid = false;
          } else {
            removeErrorMessage(input, allDivs);
          }
        }
      }
    }
    
    form.className = isValid ? "" : "mimi_invalid";
    submit.value = isValid ? submit.getAttribute("data-default-text") : submit.getAttribute("data-invalid-text");
		submit.disabled = !isValid;
		submit.className = isValid ? "submit" : "disabled";
    
    return isValid;
  }

  function emailErrorMessage(input, allDivs) {
    if(input.getAttribute("data-webform-type") == "iframe") {
      input.className = "required invalid";
      input.placeholder = input.getAttribute("data-required-message") || "This field is required";
    } else {
      allDivs[0].innerHTML = input.getAttribute("data-invalid-message") || "This field is invalid";
    }
  }

  function fieldErrorMessage(input, allDivs) {
    if(input.getAttribute("data-webform-type") == "iframe") {
      input.className = "required invalid";
      input.placeholder = input.getAttribute("data-required-message") || "This field is required";
    } else {
      for(var i = 0; i &lt; allDivs.length; ++i) {
        var element = allDivs[i];
        if(element.className.indexOf("mimi_field_feedback") &gt;= 0) {
          return element.innerHTML = input.getAttribute("data-required-message") || "This field is required";
        }
      }
    }
  }

  function removeErrorMessage(input, allDivs) {
    if(input.getAttribute("data-webform-type") == "iframe") {
      input.className = "required";
    }

    for(var i = 0; i &lt; allDivs.length; ++i) {
      var element = allDivs[i];
      if(element.className.indexOf("mimi_field_feedback") &gt;= 0) {
        return element.innerHTML = "";
      }
    }
  }
})();
