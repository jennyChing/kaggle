      <h1>
       God Bless You to be Banned In Government Buildings
      </h1>
      <div class="pmeta">
       Written by
       <a href="http://blog.sarcasmsociety.com/" rel="author external" title="Visit The Sarcasmists website">
        The Sarcasmist
       </a>
       on April 16, 2010 -
       <a href="http://blog.sarcasmsociety.com/society/god-bless-you-to-be-banned-in-government-buildings.html#comments" title="Comment on God Bless You to be Banned In Government Buildings">
        Comments
       </a>
       (47)
      </div>
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        As part of a strict interpretation of the United States Constitution, a new bill has been introduced which will ban the term God bless you from being uttered in government buildings. This ban is meant to enforce the separation of church and state as required by the U.S. Constitution.
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         If there is going to be true separation of church and state, then there must be absolutely zero overlap when it comes to religion and government. There are plenty of other other terms that can be used instead of God bless you. said a representative of the senator sponsoring the bill.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        As can be expected, the proposed legislation is drawing criticism and is not going unchallenged.
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         It is absolutely ridiculous to ban a phrase that is simply a practice in etiquette. said an opposition representative.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
