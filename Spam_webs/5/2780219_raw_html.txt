       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Whimsical Valentine Wreath
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2010-01-12T07:12:00+00:00">
         January 12, 2010
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="pw-widget pw-size-large pw-horizontal" pw:image="" pw:title="Whimsical Valentine Wreath" pw:url="http://tatertotsandjello.com/2010/01/whimsical-valentine-wreath.html">

         </a>

        <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="Whimsical Valentine Wreath" data-frizzlyposturl="http://tatertotsandjello.com/2010/01/whimsical-valentine-wreath.html" type="hidden" value=""/>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wjzVbMVQI/AAAAAAAAHj4/0Cne19vzEkk/s1600-h/heading+pic.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wjzVbMVQI/AAAAAAAAHj4/0Cne19vzEkk/s640/heading+pic.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          I was reading Martha Stewart last month (I think) and they had these giant Christmas balls made out of cupcake liners.
          <strong style="color: red;">
           And I thought to myself  maybe I could use cupcake liners to make a wreath for Valentines Day
          </strong>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wkyB6CyDI/AAAAAAAAHkA/IL8hQq1SZ04/s1600-h/one+side.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/1.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wkyB6CyDI/AAAAAAAAHkA/IL8hQq1SZ04/s576/one+side.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         I love the texture of the liners. After a little trial and error, this is what I came up with.
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wY0Gw3NYI/AAAAAAAAHiY/xWFTY4C443c/s1600-h/closeup+of+liners.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wY0Gw3NYI/AAAAAAAAHiY/xWFTY4C443c/s576/closeup+of+liners.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         I found some cupcake liners at Hobby Lobby. I really wanted polka dot ones but couldnt find any at any of the stores around here.
        </div>
        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wZIqptMQI/AAAAAAAAHio/XVFhQRBp3l4/s1600-h/valentines+wreath+015.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wZIqptMQI/AAAAAAAAHio/XVFhQRBp3l4/s576/valentines+wreath+015.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         Then I found this little wreath 8-inch at the Dollar Store.
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
        </div>
        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wccSaizxI/AAAAAAAAHjg/rDV92cwlY6I/s1600-h/wreath.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wccSaizxI/AAAAAAAAHjg/rDV92cwlY6I/s576/wreath.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         The first thing I did was wrap the entire wreath with ribbon that I had left over from Christmas. I wrapped it tightly around the wreath form.
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wbw7nQHOI/AAAAAAAAHjY/xo3B3EHrxiA/s1600-h/two+wreaths.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wbw7nQHOI/AAAAAAAAHjY/xo3B3EHrxiA/s320/two+wreaths.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         Then I put a dot of hot glue on the front of the wreath and attached a cupcake liner.
         <strong>
          I bunched the liner up at the bottom by pressing all of the side together like a flower.
         </strong>
         Then I pressed that down on to the wreath form. and held it for a few seconds until the hot glue cooled off. I attached about 9 more to that section, pressing them tightly together.
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wZ-njjjCI/AAAAAAAAHjI/VQLnPB9x6Po/s1600-h/valentines+wreath+012.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wZ-njjjCI/AAAAAAAAHjI/VQLnPB9x6Po/s320/valentines+wreath+012.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         I worked in small sections, leaving space in between each section. I found that if I tried to do too big of a section at once, that the glue would activate the cooled glue at the base of the other liners and make the whole thing unstable.
         <strong>
          By working in small sections and leaving space between, it allowed the glue to completely cool and the sections to stabilize.
         </strong>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wbRA1RCvI/AAAAAAAAHjQ/2CBVRk-JZr4/s1600-h/making+the+wreath.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wbRA1RCvI/AAAAAAAAHjQ/2CBVRk-JZr4/s320/making+the+wreath.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         Once I worked my way all the way around the wreath, I then went back and filled in the bare sections. For my 8 inche wreath, I used about 100 wrappers.
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         I was going to attach liners to the inside and outside of the wreath, but I found that they stuck out more than I thought they would and I thought the two inside sections would meet and then it wouldnt look like a wreath, but more like a ball. I actually like it this way because the ribbon shows and it is a nice contrast.
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         Once the wreath was filled in, I took a white cupcake liner and bunched it the same way, then I took the top of the liner and fanned it out like a flower. I put a dab of hot glue at the base of the liner and stuck it in one side of the wreath. Then I took a polka dotted scrapbook brad and attached some hot glue to the bottom and put it in the center of the flower.
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wnxvIhI1I/AAAAAAAAHkI/CnviTcRKLGM/s1600-h/one+more+view.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wnxvIhI1I/AAAAAAAAHkI/CnviTcRKLGM/s576/one+more+view.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wYxMLJ-1I/AAAAAAAAHiQ/I4eon_Mt3wQ/s1600-h/closeup+flower.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <br/>
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
         The last thing I did was turn the wreath over and attach a length of ribbon to the back of each side of the wreath. Then I hung it up!
        </div>
        <div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wosZJgbbI/AAAAAAAAHkQ/HZwOC_sBm-w/s1600-h/bottom+picture.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img alt="" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/4.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wosZJgbbI/AAAAAAAAHkQ/HZwOC_sBm-w/s576/bottom+picture.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_NgH-mlTcwHE/S0wY286AxII/AAAAAAAAHig/LpVDZ5LgopA/s1600-h/cupcake+wrapper+wreath.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <br/>
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div style="color: #e06666; font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           It puts a smile on my face!
          </span>
         </strong>
        </div>
