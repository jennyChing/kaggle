     <p>
      Y'know, every time I read the core book for an RPG, they always have an example of play. Usually goes like this.
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "Here is an example of play that doesn't get into the rules (we'll leave that for a later chapter) but rather demonstrates roleplaying. The DM is Chris, John is playing Urthden, a human fighter, Giles is a dwarven barbarian, Gwen is an elven ranger, and Billy is playing a human wizard! They've just come to the entrance of Malik Hur, a terrible tomb of a lich that was buried eons ago!
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <b>
        Chris:
       </b>
       The crypt awaits before, a large stone slab of a door taunting you with its immobility.
       <br/>
       <b>
        Billy:
       </b>
       Blast! How shalt we get through this door? King Darod and his Kingdom are depending upon us to retrieve the cure!
       <br/>
       <b>
        Gwen:
       </b>
       Don't worry, I have an idea! Remember earlier when we were interrogating that skeleton? He mentioned that the water moves when the stone strikes it . . .
       <br/>
       <b>
        John:
       </b>
       And that stones move when water touches them! Brilliant! Alright, I'll go and grab some water from that creek that Chris mentioned earlier"
       <br/>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      And on and on in perfect harmony. Who has ever REALLY played a game like that?
      <a href="/wiki/Glossary#ITT" title="Glossary">
       ITT
      </a>
      we write more realistic game session transcripts.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Example_1">
       Example 1
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/index.php?title=Examples_of_Play&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Example 1">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      You're at the crypt. There's a door.
      <br/>
      <b>
       John:
      </b>
      I open the door.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      Can't  there's no handles or anything.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Billy:
      </b>
      I fucking break it down, then, I thought it was implied if the door doesn't open the first time we BREAK IT DOWN.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Giles:
      </b>
      Hey, hey can I lockpick the door?
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      We
      <br/>
      <b>
       John:
      </b>
      No, you're a goddamn barbarian *snort*  that, that's like me rolling to cast Magic Missile.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      Hey! HEY! Pay attention. Anyway, the door's made of stone.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Billy:
      </b>
      I can break stone.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      What?
      <br/>
      <b>
       Billy:
      </b>
      I can fucking do it, I have a magic +1 hammer, it doesn't break.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      So you're going to spend all day hitting a chunk of stone with your hammer.
      <br/>
      <b>
       John:
      </b>
      Yeah, and by my measurements, if my calculations are correct, and assuming this door is, like, two feet thick, it'll take thirty-two hours to bust down, we can do this.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Giles:
      </b>
      Hey, hey, hey, I have an axe, I can, I can help!
      <br/>
      <b>
       Billy:
      </b>
      No, no you friggin' can't, it's a plain axe and you made a shitty barbarian so, like, you'd make it worse.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      Look, maybe GWEN might remember that interrogation Wait, where's Gwen?
      <br/>
      <b>
       Billy:
      </b>
      She showed up one game and hasn't come back. You've been playing her character, dingus.
      <br/>

      <br/>

      </b>

      <br/>
      <b>

      <br/>

      </b>

      <br/>
      <b>
       Daniel:
      </b>
      Total moooooo
. . . you hit him, he dies.
      <br/>
      <b>

      </b>

      <br/>
      <b>

      <br/>
      <b>

      <br/>
      <b>
       Daniel:
      </b>
      I shoot him too.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      No, you can't do that. It's against your alignment.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Daniel:
      </b>
      Chaotic Neutral.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>

      <br/>
      <b>

      </b>

      <br/>
      <b>
       Tom:
      </b>
      So, I heard Daniel was a fag.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Daniel:
      </b>
      What? Well, you're fat.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Tom:
      </b>

      <br/>
      <b>
       Daniel:
      </b>
      It's on, fatass.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>

      <br/>
      <b>
       Daniel:
      </b>
      Well fine, I just shoot the kid, then.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      There are other people who heard the shot, too. They'll see you.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Daniel:
      </b>
      Well, I point at Tom and say he did it.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Tom:
      </b>
      Oh, you bitch. That's a motivation. I shoot Daniel in the head.
      <br/>
      <b>
       DM:
      </b>
      You don't actually say that, do you, Daniel?
      <br/>
      <b>
       Tom:
      </b>
      I got a 19. That's a hit. Damage . . . 19.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Daniel:
      </b>
      What the fuck? I'm on low HP.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Tom:
      </b>
      Ooops. Oh well, I loot your body.
      <br/>
      <b>
       Zack:
      </b>
      Wait, what happened? I was just doing stuff on my mobile.
