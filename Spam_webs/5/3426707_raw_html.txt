         <h2>
          5 Reasons to Support Small Business Saturday
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           by Jenny Ford, Monkey Toes Shoes
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://launchher.com/2011/11/5-reasons-to-support-small-business-saturday/383334_300648623286401_167641889920409_1199470_1588684814_n/" rel="attachment wp-att-4750">
           <img alt="small business saturday minneapolis" class="alignleft size-large wp-image-4750" height="154" src="http://launchher.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/383334_300648623286401_167641889920409_1199470_1588684814_n-e1321419381152-500x376.jpg" title="small business saturday minneapolis" width="205"/>
          </a>
          I love that LaunchHersupports
          <strong>
           women-owned business
          </strong>
          ! And Im thrilled and honored to be a part of it, I just adore Tracy &amp; Kara to pieces. They asked me to try to get the point across about why to support local, and a lot of times women-owned, businesses. I havent always been perfect about this  I totally admit. But Im making a change, and I get that we cant purchase EVERYTHING at a
          <strong>
           local boutique
          </strong>
          , but when we can, we should. And heres why
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Your dollars keep the
           <strong>
            community
           </strong>
           full of thriving businesses
           <br/>
           and shopping centers looking pretty  ghost towns are ugly!
          </li>
          <li>
           Youre not only helping the boutique owners, but youre
           <br/>
           keeping small businesses (the actual products), like mine, thriving too. The
           <br/>
           smaller boutiques dont ask us to give them discounts, they pay a normal and
           <br/>
           <strong>
            fair wage
           </strong>
           for it, which keeps everyone happy.
          </li>
          <li>
           You are
           <strong>
            reinforcing the value
           </strong>
           of taking the leap of faith
           <br/>
           and becoming a business owner. Entrepreneurship is hard, folks, go give them a
           <br/>
           financial pat on the back.
          </li>
          <li>
           Even though sometimes you may spend a little teenie bit
           <br/>
           more than at those box stores, your generating
           <strong>
            good karma.
           </strong>
          </li>
          <li>
           Youre going to find incredibly
           <strong>
            unique gifts
           </strong>
           at these
           <br/>
           little shops. Youll never have to worry about showing up to Aunt Bettys house
           <br/>
           with the same gift as your brother  and you know you want to top your brothers
           <br/>
           gift, right?!
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          I hope youll join myself and my family in
          <strong>
           supporting small business and shopping local
          </strong>
          , when and where you can. If there is something you cannot find locally;
          <em>
           ask your favorite store to carry it
          </em>
           they love hearing from their customers.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <strong>
           Happy local shopping and Happy Holidays!
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>

          </strong>
          on
          <strong>
           Small Business Saturday (November 26, 2011)
          </strong>
          in Cherry Creek North in Denver, CO.
