           <p>
            A CSA, or Community Supported Agriculture, is a way to support farmers in your area by buying into a share of
            <a href="http://ecowatch.com/living/food-living/">
             food
            </a>
            for the growing season. Similar modelsexisted in Europe, Japan and elsewhere before its arrival inthe U.S. in the 1980s.
           </p>
           <figure aria-labelledby="figcaption_attachment_355882" class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_355882" style="max-width: 610px">
            <span class="image-container wp-image-355882 size-full" style="max-width:600px;max-height:324px;">
             <img alt="csa600" class="wp-image-355882 size-full" height="324" src="//ecowatch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/csa600.jpg" width="600"/>
            </span>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text" id="figcaption_attachment_355882">
             Fresh Fork Market CSA in Cleveland, Ohio aggregates products from about 100 farms within a 75-mile radius. Photo credit: Fresh Fork Market
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            Inspired by Rudolf Steiner, the philosopher and social reformer who is credited with developingWaldorf education and biodynamic agriculture, a German and a Swiss biodynamic farmer
            <a href="http://www.newfarm.org/features/0104/csa-history/part1.shtml" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.newfarm.org/features/0104/csa-history/part1.shtml', 'brought the concept to the U.S.');" target="_blank">
             brought the concept to the U.S.
            </a>
            and simultaneously but independently established Indian Line Farm in Massachusetts and Temple-Wilton Community Farm in New Hampshire in 1986.
           </p>
           <p>
            Flash forward to 2015 and there are now several thousand farms participating in CSA programs across the U.S. The popularity of these programs stems from a desire for consumers to connect with and support local growers who are
            <a href="http://ecowatch.com/2015/01/26/farmers-rewarded-carbon-farming/">
             farming in an ecologically sound way
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <div class="unit" id="div-gpt-ad-1433512244421-0" style="height:90px; width:728px;margin-bottom:14px;">
            <script type="text/javascript">
             googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display("div-gpt-ad-1433512244421-0"); });
            </script>
           </div>
           <p>
            Its a win-win for consumers and growers because consumers get delicious, fresh and mostly, if not all,organic produce throughout the growing season, and growers have the financial support from their customers atthe beginning of the season.
            <a href="http://www.localharvest.org/csa/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.localharvest.org/csa/', 'Other benefits');" target="_blank">
             Other benefits
            </a>
            for farmers include being able to marketthe food early in the year before the height of the growing season and being able to know the people they are feeding with their food.
           </p>
           <p>
            Farming has always been risky business. Pests, disease, natural disasters and now
            <a href="http://ecowatch.com/climate-change-news/">
             climate change
            </a>
            can devastate a farm. The benefit of a CSA is that members pay upfront and agree to beflexible with what comes in their share. For example, late-season frost may destroy stone fruits like peaches one season, andcustomers have to deal with that. Most CSA programs can compensate for a shortage of one crop with other produce.
           </p>
           <p>
            CSA programs vary. Some are as small as a dozen customers while others have 3,500 customers. There areCSAs made up of one farm and others aggregate products from 100or more farms in the area.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://freshforkmarket.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://freshforkmarket.com/', 'Fresh Fork Market CSA');" target="_blank">
             Fresh Fork Market CSA
            </a>
            in Cleveland, Ohio aggregates products from about 100 farms within a 75-mile radius. It operates year-round, providing its 3,500 customers with a weekly share in the summer and a bi-weekly share in the winter. Fresh Fork offers meat, dairy, vegetables, fruits, grains and even value-added products such as pies, salsa and Bloody Mary mix that are made using the areas products. Some of the producers include Newswanger Meats,
            <a href="http://www.eshlemanfruitfarm.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.eshlemanfruitfarm.com/', 'Eschelman Fruit Farm');" target="_blank">
             Eschelman Fruit Farm
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.snowvillecreamery.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.snowvillecreamery.com/', 'Snowville Creamery');" target="_blank">
             Snowville Creamery
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.firstlightcsa.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.firstlightcsa.com/', 'First Light Farm');" target="_blank">
