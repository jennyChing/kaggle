        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Make Your Own Bug Spray!
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2014-04-29T01:00:12+00:00">
          April 29, 2014
         </span>
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://aproverbs31wife.com/author/proverbs-wife/" rel="author">
            Kendra
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://aproverbs31wife.com/make-your-own-bug-spray/#comments">
           6 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          When it comes to bugs, they are not welcome in my house, pretty sure they are not welcome in yours either. However conventional bug sprays are not welcome either. Today our sponsor Kate, from
          <a href="http://katesbodyworkandfitnessblog.com" target="_blank">
           Kates Bodywork and Fitness
          </a>
          is here to tell us how to make your own bug spray.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          This is a very timely post, since Ive noticed a lot more bugs in the past week already.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="Make your own bug spray this spring!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6871" height="402" src="http://aproverbs31wife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/spring.png" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Ah, spring! Soon it will be summertimecookouts, camping, baseball games, or just sitting outside enjoying the weather. A beautiful picture
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Until the bugs show up. Ugh.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          It may start slowly, as you swat them away, slightly annoyed. Then you feel itchy, start to scratch.then BAM, a full on assault of nasty pests!! Nothing ruins the ambience of a summer evening faster than bugs.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <a href="http://aproverbs31wife.com/make-your-own-bug-spray/images-16/" rel="attachment wp-att-6869">
           <img alt="No more bugs! Make your own bug spray this year!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6869" height="208" src="http://aproverbs31wife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/images-16.jpg" width="240"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          And its not just outside. Those pesky guys can invade your house too. SPIDERS!!!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          So you pour on the Bug Spray, hoping to OFF the little buggers. Or you Bug Bomb your house to rid yourself of icky creatures. But do you know what youre putting on your skin!? Do you know how harmful just breathing in a little of that bomb can be?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          DEET. Poison.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          The National Institutes of Health have a
          <a href="http://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/ency/article/002763.htm" target="_blank">
           webpage
          </a>
          devoted to Bug Spray Poisoning. Most bug repellents contain DEET (N,N-diethyl-m-toluamide) as their active ingredient. DEET is one of the few insect repellants that work. It is recommended to prevent mosquito-born diseases such as malaria, dengue fever, and West Nile virus.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Ok.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          But is
          <strong>
           VERY HARMFUL
          </strong>
          !
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Insect Repellents containing DEET can cause skin irritations, such as hives or mild redness. However, (p)ersons who use very high concentrations of DEET on their skin over a long period of time (such as military personnel or game wardens) may have more severe skin reactions that include blistering, burning, and permanent scars of the skin. Other symptoms associated with long-term use of high amounts of DEET (over 50% concentration) include insomnia and mood changes.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          That is scary.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          What is more scary is the effect it can have on kids. Long term exposure to children can cause seizures. And it should not under any circumstances be used on infants. Why would we ever want to expose our kids to anything that harmful?
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          There is an alternative. Essential oils can provide a safe  and importantly  effective method of keeping those pests at bay.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://katesbodyworkandfitnessblog.files.wordpress.com/2014/04/terrashield_card_hteo.jpg">
           <img alt="terrashield_card_HTEO" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-182" height="237" src="http://katesbodyworkandfitnessblog.files.wordpress.com/2014/04/terrashield_card_hteo.jpg?w=300" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Terrashield is doTerras proprietary blend of 15 essential oils that have been specifically proven to more effectively repel biting insects than synthetic repellents. Just a small amount of Terrashield will last up to 6 hours. 6 hours of safe and harmless protection.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          A few of the oils included in Terrashield are Lemon Eucalyptus, known for its ability to repel cockroaches, silverfish and other insects; Citronella, widely known for its repellent properties; and Lemongrass, also known as a strong insect repellent.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          You can diffuse Terrashield in your home and outside your home and you may also apply a small amount on your skin. Another great idea is to place the oil on ribbons or strings and place near air vents, windows or other openings where bugs might invite themselves in.
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: left;">
          <a href="http://aproverbs31wife.com/make-your-own-bug-spray/hand-holding-a-spray-can-isolated-over-white-background/" rel="attachment wp-att-6870">
           <img alt="Make your own bug spray with these natural solutions!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6870" height="424" src="http://aproverbs31wife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/make-bug-spray.jpg" width="283"/>
          </a>
          More options to make your own bug spray:
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Terrashield isnt the only essential oil option you have. Peppermint, Lavender, Basil,
          <span style="color: #404040;">
           Patchouli, and Clove are just a few of the single note oils that can deter bugs. For a more comprehensive list of the oils and the bugs that hate them, check out
           <a href="http://doterrablog.com/customized-pest-control/" target="_blank">
            doTerras Blog
           </a>
           for a complete list of ways to make your own bug spray.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #008000;">
           <strong>
            For more healthy home solutions, check out our
            <a href="http://katesbodyworkandfitnessblog.com/2014/04/22/171/" target="_blank" title="Healthy Home Solutions  Recipes for a Green Clean">
             <span style="color: #008000;">
              recent post
             </span>
            </a>
            .
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Enjoy your summer without pesky, uninvited guests!
