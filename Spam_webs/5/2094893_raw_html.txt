  <article class="post-2611 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-anniversary category-birthday category-fall category-just-because tag-gift-wrapping tag-gifts">
   <div class="hero">
    <img alt="" class="hero-img" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/fall-gift-wrap_hero.jpg"/>
    <header class="header-single container has-img">
     <h1 class="entry-title">
      DIY: 4 Fabulous Fall Gift Wraps
     </h1>
     <div class="meta right">
      <time class="published" datetime="2014-09-04T08:28:30+00:00">
       September 4, 2014
      </time>
      <p class="byline author vcard">
       By 
			Shari's Berries
      </p>
     </div>
    </header>
   </div>
   <!-- /.hero -->
   <div class="wrap">
    <div class="container" role="document">
     <div class="content row">
      <main class="main " role="main">
       <p class="lead">
        The leaves are turning. The air is crisp. And as our social calendars begin to fill up with autumn gatherings and holiday celebrations, our minds begin to wander to all the gifts we'll be giving. Whether it's to thank a gracious host or simply say "Happy Fall", this is the time of year when we love to show off our personal style. This gift wrap DIY makes it easy.
       </p>
       <div class="entry-content col-md-offset-1 col-md-10 col-lg-offset-2 col-lg-8">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <a>
          <img alt="DIY: Fall Gift Wrap" class="wp-image-2615 aligncenter" height="1087" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/fall-gift-wrap-04.jpg" width="801"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="su-row">
         <div class="su-column su-column-size-1-2">
          <div class="su-column-inner su-clearfix">
           <h4>
            Materials:
           </h4>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Brown wrapping paper or
             <a href="http://www.target.com/p/scotch-postal-wrapping-paper-30ft-x-30in/-/A-13331719" target="_blank">
              postal paper
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             Colorful fall leaves
            </li>
            <li>
             Twigs
            </li>
            <li>
             Yarn (in a complementary color)
            </li>
            <li>
             Hole punch
            </li>
            <li>
             Scissors
            </li>
            <li>
             Tape
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="su-column su-column-size-1-2">
          <div class="su-column-inner su-clearfix">
           <h4>
            Steps:
           </h4>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Wrap gift in brown paper
            </li>
            <li>
             Cut enough yarn to wrap around gift several times
            </li>
            <li>
             Break twig into small pieces and tie together with yarn
            </li>
            <li>
             Punch a hole in one or more of the leaves and tie to twigs
            </li>
            <li>
             Set twigs and leaves on top of gift, wrap excess yarn around gift
            </li>
            <li>
             Tie off yarn on back side of gift, secure in place with tape if needed
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a>
          <img alt="DIY: Fall Gift Wrap" class="size-full wp-image-2613 aligncenter" height="1087" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/fall-gift-wrap-02.jpg" width="801"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="su-row">
         <div class="su-column su-column-size-1-2">
          <div class="su-column-inner su-clearfix">
           <h4>
            Materials:
           </h4>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <a href="http://www.papersource.com/color/roll-wrap/3650.030/404.html" target="_blank">
              White wrapping paper
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             Burlap
            </li>
            <li>
             Quill feather (bonus points if its a
             <a href="http://www.nostalgicimpressions.com/Pheasant_Quill_Nib_Pens_p/200jx.htm" target="_blank">
              quill that can be used for calligraphy
             </a>
             ). Note: if you find the feather outside be sure the
             <a href="http://www.howtocleanstuff.net/how-to-clean-feathers/" target="_blank">
              clean it thoroughly.
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             String
            </li>
            <li>
             Scissors
            </li>
            <li>
             Tape
            </li>
            <li>
             Hot glue (optional)
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="su-column su-column-size-1-2">
          <div class="su-column-inner su-clearfix">
           <h4>
            Steps:
           </h4>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Wrap gift in white paper
            </li>
            <li>
             Cut a piece of burlap to wrap around center of gift (as if it were ribbon), and secure in place on back of gift with tape (or hot glue)
            </li>
            <li>
             Attach quill feather to top of gift using string and secure it with small piece of tape
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a>
          <img alt="DIY: Fall Gift Wrap" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2619" height="1087" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/fall-gift-wrap-011.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="su-row">
         <div class="su-column su-column-size-1-2">
          <div class="su-column-inner su-clearfix">
           <h4>
            Materials:
           </h4>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <a href="http://www.papersource.com/item/Lokta-Chocolate-Feathers-On-Cream-Fine-Paper/101945.html" target="_blank">
              Feather wrapping paper
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             <a href="http://www.papersource.com/item/Orange-Raffia/713058571339.html" target="_blank">
              Orange raffia
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             White flag-shaped gift tags (you can make these by cutting plain rectangular tags)
            </li>
            <li>
             Gold metallic marker
            </li>
            <li>
             Tape
            </li>
            <li>
             Scissors
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="su-column su-column-size-1-2">
          <div class="su-column-inner su-clearfix">
           <h4>
            Steps:
           </h4>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Draw a tribal pattern on gift tag with gold marker
            </li>
            <li>
             Wrap gift in feather paper
            </li>
            <li>
             Wrap orange raffia around gift several times
            </li>
            <li>
             Place tag under orange raffiato hold in place
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a>
          <img alt="DIY: Fall Gift Wrap" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2614" height="1087" src="http://www.berries.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/fall-gift-wrap-03.jpg" width="801"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="su-row">
         <div class="su-column su-column-size-1-2">
          <div class="su-column-inner su-clearfix">
           <h4>
            Materials:
           </h4>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Brown wrapping paper or
             <a href="http://www.target.com/p/scotch-postal-wrapping-paper-30ft-x-30in/-/A-13331719" target="_blank">
              postal paper
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             Letter stickers (simple is best, in a fall color)
            </li>
            <li>
             Tape
            </li>
            <li>
             Scissors
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="su-column su-column-size-1-2">
          <div class="su-column-inner su-clearfix">
           <h4>
            Steps:
           </h4>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Wrap gift in brown paper
            </li>
            <li>
             Write your message on top of gift with stickers (for a Thanksgiving hostess try give thanks, for a fall anniversary fall in love or for a birthday a simple celebrate will do)
            </li>
           </ul>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h4>
         Thats a Wrap
        </h4>
        <p>
         Which wrap was your favorite? Do you have a fall gift wrapping idea of your own? Wed love to hear about it in the blog comments.
        </p>
       </div>
      </main>
