           <a href="http://www.payonperformance.com.au/searchable-twitter-google-friends-again/" rel="bookmark" title="12:20 am">
            <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-03-19T00:20:25+00:00">
             March 19, 2015
            </time>
           </a>
          </div>
          <!-- .entry-meta -->
         </header>
         <!-- .entry-header -->
         <div class="entry-image">
         </div>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p class="lead">
           Twitter hashtags are searchable &amp;ranking on Google once again. As Bloomberg Business
           <a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2015-02-05/twitter-said-to-reach-deal-for-tweets-in-google-search-results">
            reported
           </a>
           in early Feb, the two tech giants reached an agreement to begin indexing tweets as they did from 2009 to 2011 before their previous agreement ended.
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            What do we know so far?
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p class="lead">
           Hashtag results pages are now searchable and appear to be fully indexed. The hashtag pages are also ranking better than weve seen in four years.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            SEO tweets:
            <a href="http://bit.ly/1EsktPd">
             http://bit.ly/1EsktPd
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            Digital marketing tweets:
            <a href="http://bit.ly/1x249ag">
             http://bit.ly/1x249ag
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            #RaceTogether (trending today):
            <a href="http://bit.ly/193e83G">
             http://bit.ly/193e83G
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            SXSW tweets:
            <a href="http://bit.ly/1H3lbIz">
             http://bit.ly/1H3lbIz
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p class="lead">
           Some of the cache dates on these hashtag pages are not super-recent (ie. RaceTogether is March 6
           <sup>
            th
           </sup>
           .) Most seem to be within the last 3 days, however.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           Being back in Google could mean massive traffic on popular hashtags such as #yesallwomen  a platform for stories about misogyny and violence against women. The hashtag page is currently outranking Wikipedia, Time &amp; Mashable for the search term yesallwomen.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           While hashtags are (nearly) fully indexed, not all individual tweets are yet.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           The SEO strength of individual hashtag pages depends on the topic, inbound links, of course, and the competition. While weve seen hashtag pages outranking the relevant Wiki page, some low competition hashtag pages are not ranking at all. The search term seoperth has only 93,000 results on Google but the hashtag page is nowhere to be found, despite being filled with content.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           Hashtags themselves seem to also be searchable, although not perfectly. #seoperth brings up the Twitter stream #1, as does #seomelbourne and the results for seomelbourne and #seomelbourne are clearly different.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           There does not, however, seem to be a clear real time section of Google results currently. Most 2011 real-time users would remember the separate news-like section for real-time Tweets. This is the biggest indication that were not fully live yet.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           <img alt="static1.squarespace" class=" size-full wp-image-631 aligncenter" height="200" src="http://www.payonperformance.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/static1.squarespace.jpg" width="300"/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            What dont we know so far?
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p class="lead">
           We dont know how deep the indexing reaches yet. Twitter has nearly 300 million active users and 500 million tweets per day and much of this is duplicate content. We dont (and may not for a long while) know exactly how much of the stream Google indexes or cares to index.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           We also do not know if this is the official roll out of the firehose or just what Google can find on their own. Is Twitter supplying direct data? We believe this is an early reindexing &amp; not the full data supply. All tweets are not yet indexed and some of the older cache dates lead us to believe its not being processed real-time yet. Hubspot agrees  they found
           <a href="http://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/google-twitter-indexing-tweets-data">
            only 7.4% of Tweets were indexed
           </a>
           as of 3 weeks ago.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           We also dont know how links in Tweets will affect SEO results but we would assume someone is already starting the test now.
           <em>
            (We will be doing the same thing shortly.)
           </em>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            How This Helps You as Marketers
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p class="lead">
           Many keywords you care about may have weak hashtag archives. There will definitely be some blue ocean to swim in for a little while. We have already identified 3-4 hashtags that are popular Google searches with very weak Tweet archives. Those become strong targets to dominate and work on a variation of barnacle marketing.
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           Also, you can easily find related hashtags &amp; topics with a search like
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           site:twitter.com inurl:hashtag KEYWORD
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           Using marketing as the keyword, we find this:
           <a href="http://bit.ly/1AH8xrl">
            http://bit.ly/1AH8xrl
           </a>
           We would assume #emailmarketing, #contentmarketing and #digitalmarketing would be in there but you may not have considered #cmworld, #marketers or (page 2) #hashtagmarketing
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           Not surprisingly, top users also benefit the most from tweet indexing. Nearly all of the tweets we found that had been indexed today were from users with over 5,000 followers. Many of them were also already found &amp; indexed on aggregators such as http://newsjs.com/
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
          </p>
          <p class="lead">
           What is your top suggestion for using the Google + Hashtags in your marketing campaigns?
          </p>
         </div>
