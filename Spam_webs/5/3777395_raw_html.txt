       <h2 class="posttitle">
        The Top 22 New Years Eve Resolutions (Sure to be Broken!)
       </h2>
       <span class="by">
        Posted on Wednesday, December 25, 2013 //
        <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http://www.goodnewsweekly.ca/2010/12/top-22-new-years-eve-resolutions-sure.html&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=false&amp;width=100&amp;action=like&amp;font=arial&amp;colorscheme=light" style="border:none; float:right; margin-left:20px; overflow:hidden; width:290px; height:25px;">

        <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_Bnd2XJeUJaQ/TQfhdqVWu7I/AAAAAAAABuE/l6uOO_sGrVA/s1600/nye+resolution.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
         <img border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_Bnd2XJeUJaQ/TQfhdqVWu7I/AAAAAAAABuE/l6uOO_sGrVA/s320/nye+resolution.jpg" width="320"/>

       </div>
       <b>
        Ah, the good ol New Year's Resolution.
       </b>
       A time to make changes in your life, to make changes in the lives of others, a time to bring peace and harmony to the world around you. Well... that is how it goes in theory anyway.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <b>
        Let's be honest.
       </b>
       How many of us have made NYE resolutions? And how many have actually followed through on them? Seriously, how money of you have made a resolution, and then bailed on it long before the NYE hangover has even had a chance to go away?
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Ya us too!
       <br/>
       <br/>
       But hey,
       <b>
        New Years Resolutions are meant to be broken, right?
       </b>
       Agreed! So in honor, we bring you the top 22 NYE Resolutions. Enjoy!
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <b>
        The Classic Resolutions
       </b>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <ol>
        <li>
         I will Pay off my credit card (because this couldnt have been done before making it a New Years resolution)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will Drink less alcohol (The New Year s Day hangover usually elicits this one)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will Get in better shape (Making January the busiest month at the gym, every year)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will Volunteer to help others (see above comment)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will Make an appointment for a checkup (12 months to do something that takes less than half an hour. Way to go out on a limb)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will Conquer a Fear (My favourite one on the list. If you can do this, thats something to hang your hat on for the entire year)
        </li>
       </ol>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <b>
        The Offbeat Resolutions
       </b>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <ol>
        <li>
         I will Change the CD in my CD Player (If you still have a CD player, this should be at the top of the list)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will Do less laundry, and wear more deodorant (Time AND environmentally efficient)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will not waste time relieving the past, but rather spend it worrying about the future (This could keep you busy for the entire year)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will not bore my boss by with the same excuse for taking leaves. I will think of some more excuses (He/she will appreciate this more than you will ever know)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will look up the definition of resolution (This probably should have been at the very top of the list)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will stop watching Jersey Shore (Impossible. Cant be done)
        </li>
       </ol>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <b>
        Resolutions for the Computer Addict
       </b>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <ol>
        <li>
         I will not email my spouse (Because face to face communication still exists)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will think of another password other than password (May we suggest goodnews)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will  stop sending e-mail, tweets, Instant Messages while out for dinner (This wont make it past the appetizer)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will not reply with LOL every time I read something funny online (But how will everyone know for sure that you are actually laughing out loud?)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will go one full day without checking my Facebook (But what if two people you dont know break up? How could you live without this vital information??)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will finally accept that the fact that ICQ is no longer popular (Moving on can be so hard to do)
        </li>
       </ol>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <b>
        Resolutions for Men
       </b>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <ol>
        <li>
         I will read the instructions before I build something (*insert a womans eye roll here)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will not order pizza every time its my turn to cook dinner (Somewhere in Vancouver a delivery boys New Year has just been ruined)
        </li>
       </ol>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <b>
        Resolutions for Women
       </b>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <ol>
        <li>
         I will not drag my boyfriend with me to a chick flick (He will appreciate this)
        </li>
        <li>
         I will stop reminding my spouse how much smarter I am than he is (I give this one till Valentines Day. Tops.)
        </li>
       </ol>
       <br/>
       <br/>

       <b>
        <i>
         From Wikipdia:
        </i>
       </b>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Recent research shows that while 52% of participants in a resolution study were confident of success with their goals, only 12% actually achieved their goals. Men achieved their goal 22% more often when they engaged in goal setting, (a system where small measurable goals are being set, such as, a pound a week, instead of saying "lose weight"), while women succeeded 10% more when they made their goals public and got support from their friends.
