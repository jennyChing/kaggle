    <h1>
     Speaking up about Construction Site Safety
    </h1>
    <!-- Article Start -->
    <p class="articleIntro">
     Spending too much time thinking about safety? Its time to make commitments and start talking about it.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     Posted via
     <a href="http://www.industrytoday.co.uk">
      Industry Today
     </a>
     . Are you into it?  Follow us on Twitter
     <a href="http://twitter.com/IndustryToday">
      @IndustryToday
     </a>
    </p>
    <p>
     Protec International Ltd is proud to have attended Asdas Annual Supply Chain, Health, Safety &amp; Environmental conference in Leeds.
    </p>
    <p>
     Talks were led by key motivational speaker, Jason Anker from Proud to be Safe (
     <a href="http://www.p2bs.org/" style="line-height: 1.6;" target="_blank" title="Proud 2BSafe">
      Proud 2BSafe
     </a>
     ), who is leading a social campaign to encourage construction personnel to commit to safety on sites. The Proud 2BSafe program aims to be a low cost, highly effective behavioural programme which maintains momentum on health and safety standards in the workplace.
    </p>
    <p>
     Ankers first hand experience makes the talks particularly hard hitting. Anker fell from a ladder whilst at work which resulted in him being paralysed from the waist down. The incident was completely avoidable, had correct safety methods been taken. Anker was only 24 at the time.
    </p>
    <p>
     Edit Fillingham, Protecs Key Account Manager heading up Protec's Sustainability initiatives, attended the conference, said It wasnt just a typical off the peg talk. Ankers talk challenged both individuals and corporate commitments to health and safety on construction sites. There was an emphasis in the talk about the lasting impact Ankers disability has had on his family and friends which made the message particularly salient.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="" src="http://industrytoday.co.uk/app/webroot/files/image/Construction Site Safety_1425550164.jpg" style="float:left; height:208px; margin:15px; width:313px"/>
     Ankers case is not isolated, nor is it the worst. The HSE states that there are over 100 fatal injuries at work in the UK every year with more than a third of these happening on construction sites. The main cause of fatal injuries are falls from height, contact with machinery and being struck by machinery. The Proud 2B Safe campaign aims to prevent injuries and fatalities by getting individuals and corporate bodies to commit to and, most importantly, speak up about safety.
    </p>
    <p>
     Make your commitment to safety by
     <a href="http://youtu.be/bzadIyKspc4" target="_blank">
      watching this video
     </a>
     , visiting the
     <a href="http://www.p2bs.org/" target="_blank" title="Proud 2BSafe">
      Proud 2B Safe website
     </a>
     and
     <a href="http://www.facebook.com/Proud2bSafe" target="_blank">
      liking the page on Facebook
     </a>
     , it could prevent you or someone around you being seriously injured. If you manage construction site safety, contact
     <a href="http://www.protection.co.uk/" target="_blank" title="Protec International Ltd">
      Protec International Ltd
     </a>
     for anti-slip, high visibility temporary protection materials to improve the safety of your employees.
    </p>
