        <div class="pf-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="image of Cowardly Lion from Wizard of Oz" class="right frame" height="334" src="http://netdna.copyblogger.com/images/cowardly-lion.jpg" title="If I only had the nerve " width="280"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Ever feel like youre a little bit of a  coward?
         </p>
         <p>
          No, youre not spending your days hiding, running, and weeping, but you havent exactly lived up to your expectations, either.
         </p>
         <p>
          Your blog isnt getting nearly as much traffic as you thought it would. You still havent finished that product you intend to sell. In fact, months or years have gone by, and you havent made much progress at all. Youre beginning to wonder if you ever will.
         </p>
         <p>
          You could rattle off a dozen reasons as to why, and most of them even sound pretty reasonable, but down deep, you know theres only one thing really holding you back:
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-13617">
          </span>
          Youre afraid.
         </p>
         <p>
          Youre afraid of telling your family and friends to leave you alone so you can work.
         </p>
         <p>
          Youre afraid of creating a product no one will want to buy.
         </p>
         <p>
          Youre afraid of quitting your job and watching the bills piling up and having everyone look at you like a failure.
         </p>
         <p>
          And secretly, youre ashamed.
         </p>
         <p>
          You know entrepreneurs are supposed to be big and bold, laughing in the face of danger and persisting even when the whole world has turned against them. You know you have to take risks. You know youll never be in control of your life until you can get some time away from your friends and family and job and start working on whats important to you.
         </p>
         <p>
          Yet, you cant seem to make yourself do it. Youve been trying for so long that youre even beginning to wonder if youre not cut out to be an entrepreneur.
         </p>
         <p>
          But its not true. To prove it to you, Id like to take you back all the way to 1939 and reintroduce you to an unlikely hero you might have forgotten.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The Cowardly Lion
         </h3>
         <p>
          In the classic film The Wizard of Oz, the Cowardly Lion is  well  a coward.
         </p>
         <p>
          Hes so afraid of everything that he cant sleep. He cries when Dorothy slaps his nose. He runs for his life when the Wizard gets angry.
         </p>
         <p>
          And hes ashamed of it. So ashamed, in fact, that he cant believe Dorothy and her companions would even allow him to travel with them.
         </p>
         <p>
          Most struggling entrepreneurs have exactly the same problems. They cant sleep, theyre terrible at conflict management, and they get really skittish around
          <a href="http://authorityrules.com/">
           authority figures
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          When they fail, they move to a different city, switch careers, and sometimes even abandon their family, all because they cant stand to have anyone look at them.
         </p>
         <p>
          But it doesnt have to be that way. The Cowardly Lion was able to overcome his fears, and so can you. You dont even need
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Scoble">
           The Great and Powerful Oz
          </a>
          to give it to you.
         </p>
         <h3>
          How to conquer your fears
         </h3>
         <p>
          In the film, the Wizard hands the Cowardly Lion a medal and tells him he has just as much courage as any hero, giving the Lions story a sweet and satisfying ending.
         </p>
         <p>
          The lesson is that courage isnt about acting in the absence of fear; its about acting
          <em>
           despite
          </em>
          it.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats profound, but it also leaves a big question unanswered: how do you convince yourself to act when youre scared to death?
         </p>
         <p>
          Well, lets take a look at the story.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           1. Get disgusted with yourself.
          </strong>
          When The Cowardly Lion comes across Dorothy and her friends in the woods, hes so overcome with shame that he cant stop weeping. He desperately wants to be courageous, and that desperation propels him to go see the Wizard of Oz.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its counterintuitive, but for most of us, thats the way motivation works. If you want to do big things, stop waiting for a time when everything is perfect, and you feel comfortable. Itll never happen. Youre far better off thinking about how crappy your situation is, getting genuinely frustrated with yourself, and then using that emotion to push yourself to make changes. Misery is a useful tool. Use it.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           2. Find a mentor.
          </strong>
          The Wizard Of Oz could have given The Cowardly Lion a medal for courage the first time he came to see him, but he didnt.
         </p>
         <p>
          Instead, he gave him a quest,
          <a href="http://www.copyblogger.com/get-rich-quick/">
           a chance to grow and to prove himself
          </a>
          and to ultimately discover that he always had what he was looking for. Thats what good mentors do. They dont actually give us anything; they merely guide us through discovering it for ourselves. And thats indispensable.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           3. Test your assumptions.
          </strong>
          By going on a quest to retrieve the Wicked Witchs broomstick, The Cowardly Lion challenged his assumption that he was a coward, and he ended up proving himself wrong.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats what we all have to do. One of the greatest lessons from cognitive psychology is you cant trust your own thoughts. When were depressed, we all become negatively biased, believing the way we see things is the way things are, and thats not usually true. If you challenge your assumptions, youll usually find out things arent nearly so bleak as you think they are, and you can do a lot more than you think you can.
         </p>
         <p>
          That little voice in your head is not, as it turns out, all-knowing. Pay no attention to the man behind the curtain.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           4. Fight for someone else.
          </strong>
          The Cowardly Lion would rather have run than try to protect himself, but when Dorothy was kidnapped by the Wicked Witch of the West, he stormed a castle of winged monkeys to save her.
         </p>
         <p>
          Most of us are this way. Well
          <a href="http://www.copyblogger.com/fight-for-your-ideas/">
           fight 10 times harder
          </a>
          to avoid letting our friends down than we will to avoid letting ourselves down. So why not harness it?
         </p>
         <p>
          If you look into the past of almost any successful entrepreneur, youll find they had partners pushing them, depending on them, and cheering for them. Its a perpetual source of motivation, and you can draw on it to do things you wouldve thought yourself incapable of doing.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           5. Remind yourself of successes.
          </strong>
          When The Cowardly Lion returned triumphant, the Wizard gave him a medal for courage to remind him that hes a hero.
         </p>
         <p>
          Most people arent aware of this, but in the novels following the original
          <em>
           The Wonderful Wizard Of Oz
          </em>
          book, The Cowardly Lion continued doing brave deeds, and he continued to be afraid, but he reminded himself of his successes and continued anyway.
         </p>
         <p>
          One of the best ways to build your own self-confidence is to keep a journal of your own victories over fear and to regularly reread them to give yourself courage. We all tend to get fixated on our failures, but having a record of your successes is a good way to keep yourself positive.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The sad truth about entrepreneurship
         </h3>
         <p>
          The fear never goes away.
         </p>
         <p>
          You just get better at dealing with it.
         </p>
         <p>
          I was just as afraid when I started my fifth company as I was when I started my first one. In fact, I was probably more afraid, because I understood how truly difficult it is to build a business from scratch.
         </p>
         <p>
          But it didnt stop me, and really, I think thats what defines people we call heroes. Theyre just as afraid as anyone else, but they keep going anyway.
         </p>
         <p>
          Theyre
          <em>
           willing
          </em>
          to lie in bed, sweating and worrying about how theyll pay the bills.
         </p>
         <p>
          Theyre
          <em>
           willing
          </em>
          to do the unpopular thing and for everyone to think theyre an ass.
         </p>
         <p>
          Theyre
          <em>
           willing
          </em>
          to invest years of their life into a product, only to see it flop and have to start over again.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its not because theyre special. They just know the price, and theyre willing to pay it.
         </p>
         <p>
          And really, thats the secret. So many people I see trying to build businesses online invest all of their mental energy into SEO or the fine points of pay-per-click, or even
          <a href="http://www.copyblogger.com/magnificent-copy/">
           writing killer content
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <h3>
          But all of those tactics will fail if you dont have the right mindset
         </h3>
         <p>
          Mindset is what lets you turn tactics into an actual thriving business. Its what lets you pay the price, to act when youre scared out of your pants.
         </p>
         <p>
          And you have to proactively create it. If you hang around successful people, youll notice they go to a lot of seminars, listen to a lot of motivational material, and surround themselves with other successful business people trying to do the same things.
         </p>
         <p>
          Its not a coincidence. They understand the value of mindset, and they set aside time every day to work on that mindset, to put themselves into the proper frame of mind to stand up and fight.
         </p>
         <p>
          Because at heart, were all cowardly lions. Everyday, we have to wake up and remind ourselves how to roar.
         </p>

Its your frame of mind with regards to business, finance, life and leadership.
          </p>

           I was born an entrepreneur.
