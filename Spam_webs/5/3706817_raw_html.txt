          <h1 class="post">
           Hands Off Celebrations
          </h1>
          <div class="clearfix bottomGreyDots spaceBottom10">
           <ul class="floatlL horizontal postDetail clearMargin">
            <li class="spaceBottom5 suffix10">
             <span class="iconCommunity iconReply">

          <div class="commentBlock spaceBottom10 ">
           <div class="clearfix flags_group">
            <div class="avatar clearfix suffix10 spaceBottom10 avatar_flags">
             <a class="avatarMed suffix10 floatL" href="/home/Veronica">
              <img alt="Veronica" class="gen-avatar" height="75" src="http://ugc-01.cafemomstatic.com/gen/constrain/75/75/75/2013/07/29/12/ek/43/avaalqmlsscv.jpg" width="75"/>

           <div class="boardPostBody" oncontextmenu="return disableImageClick(event);">
            <p class="p1">
             This season is a perfect excuse for overachieving, type-a, perfectionist types like myself to show out. Pinterest, lifestyle magazines, and Martha Stewart everything are our holiday bibles. Thanks to the endless stream of inspiration at our fingertips we can happily whip ourselves into a yuletide induced frenzy chasing elusive (to us average folk) picture perfect holiday dreams. Handmade Christmas cards in the mail by Thanksgiving, homemade kraft paper wrapped edible gifts for coworkers, and a meticulously decorated home ready to welcome a steady stream of visitors are totally gonna happen this year.
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             <img alt="" height="375" src="http://ugc-01.cafemomstatic.com/gen/constrain/500/500/80/2013/11/30/22/d3/np/povpwkv7wocv.jpg" width="250"/>
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             Not quite.
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             Unfortunately my absurd fantasies have been on hold for as long as I've been a mother due to our lifestyle. Student housing apartments aren't the best spaces for entertaining. While we enjoyed our holidays they aren't what I had in mind when I became a mother. Thankfully we've finally settled down and purchased a home. Soon enough i'll be able to indulge my yuletide dreams.
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             We spent Thanksgiving in upstate New York with my husband's family. My husband and I decided to make the 16+ hour drive instead of flying because there's no easy way to get to my husband's hometown from Indianapolis. Unfortunately upon our departure our three year old Cameron came down with an awful cold. The ride was eventful but we arrived safely.
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             <img alt="" height="167" src="http://ugc-01.cafemomstatic.com/gen/constrain/500/500/80/2013/11/30/22/6l/rp/pomzka638ccv.jpg" width="250"/>
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             I spent most of my time tending to Cameron and once Thanksgiving eve arrived I had no desire to stay up late cooking. The next morning I made an easy to prepare Toll House Cookie pie and some simple candied yams. That's it. My husband and I and our sniffling semi-feverish children made our way to Grandma and Grandpas for a low key evening together.
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             My in laws prepared a beautiful dinner while the kids played underfoot. I enjoyed some solo time and took a handful of pictures of the lovely set up and the kids having fun. Everything was relaxed. No pressure and no worries. It was perfect. I'm sure things were slightly less relaxing for our hosts but I enjoyed myself and the simplicity of the evening. There were no burlap chair banners, my family wasn't dressed in color coordinated outfits, and we didn't have a hot chocolate bar with peppermint candy cane stirrers.
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             No one cared. We were together and that was enough.
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             <img alt="" height="375" src="http://ugc-01.cafemomstatic.com/gen/constrain/500/500/80/2013/11/30/22/az/of/potfyjafk8cv.jpg" width="250"/>
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             <strong>
              Do you prefer flawless planning and execution or do you take a more laid back and relaxed approach to the holidays?
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             <strong>
              <br/>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p class="p1">
             <strong>
              #mykindofholiday
             </strong>
            </p>
