              <h2 class="title">
               Court allows criminally insane man to drive Australian taxi
              </h2>
              <div class="node-time">
               Updated 19 December 2011, 13:06 AEDT
              </div>
              <div class="node-teaser">
               <p>
                A court in the south eastern state of Victoria has ruled that a criminally insane man convicted of killing his wife should be allowed drive a taxi.
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="clearfix">
              </div>
              <div class="content">
               <p>
                The man killed his wife, but escaped a murder conviction on the grounds of insanity.
               </p>
               <p>
                The Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal says he should be allowed to drive taxis.
               </p>
               <p>
                However, the state's public transport minister, Lynne Kosky, says the law will be changed to stop people with similar backgrounds getting taxi licences.
               </p>
               <p>
                "The assurance that I can give to the public is that we will ensure that out taxi industry is safe, we will take all measures and we're doing that to make sure that our taxi industry is safe, we've currently got audits in place, and we will take every step possible to appeal this decision".
               </p>
