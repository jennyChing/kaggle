       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         IRON MAN MARK VI ARC REACTOR GLOW IN THE DARK COOKIES
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="posted-on">
          <a href="http://ambyfelix.com/iron-man-mark-6-arc-reactor-glow-in-the-dark-cookies/" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date published updated" datetime="2015-05-08T16:17:22+00:00">
            May 8, 2015
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://ambyfelix.com/author/ambycakes/">
            AmbyCakes
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="comments-link">
          <a href="http://ambyfelix.com/iron-man-mark-6-arc-reactor-glow-in-the-dark-cookies/#comments">
           26 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
       </header>
       <div class="clearfix entry-content">
        <p>
         Hey everyone!
        </p>
        <p>
         I have been day dreaming about making a cookie like this for a really long time. Ive been trying to get create a UV responsive cookie that will mimic one of Iron Mans arc reactors. It took me quite a while to realize a butter cream method is best. I did a few test runs and over complicated things without results I was happy with. I couldnt use fondant because the quinine was not showing up under my black light. I even made things too complicated by creating a UV responsive hard candy layer and tried (and failed) to luster some modeling chocolate silver and added it on top.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_6781.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_6781" class="aligncenter wp-image-1366 " height="521" src="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_6781-1024x768.jpg" title="" width="695"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         See? :/ WTF? lol! Hey, I tried. It was a great idea in theory. It didnt exactly turn out how I wanted it too and was kinda ridiculous, unrealistic and too time consuming. I had everyone in mind who would want to recreate these so a buttercream layered cookie was the best answer.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_6683-e1430355338141.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_6683" class="aligncenter wp-image-1364 " height="533" src="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/IMG_6683-e1431130466751-1024x768.jpg" title="" width="710"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I used this cool
         <a href="https://www.etsy.com/listing/208710213/iron-mans-arc-reactor-cookie-cutter" rel="nofollow">
          Arc Reactor cookie cutter I found on Etsy
         </a>
         as a guide. If you have the skills, you can skip this step. I recommend dusting it lightly with either confectioners sugar or a little bit of corn starch. This will prevent a suction when the cookie cutter is on top of the buttercream. You can skip this step, but it makes assembling go by much easier so you dont have to wash the cookie cutter after each use as often.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ARC-REACTOR-COOKIE-COLLAGE-1.jpg">
          <img alt="ARC REACTOR COOKIE COLLAGE (1)" class="aligncenter wp-image-1415 " height="231" src="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ARC-REACTOR-COOKIE-COLLAGE-1-1024x256.jpg" title="" width="924"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I simply frosted my cookie with a small, offset spatula and let them chill in the fridge for about 10 minutes. Then I gently made an indentation with my cookie cutter to give me a bit of a guideline for my silver luster. Starting from the inside and working my way out, I painted the cookies and then finished by painting the perimeter. ^_^
        </p>
        <p>
         I know under the UV light, every flaw shows up, but its something I had a difficult time avoiding. I even painted one cookie under the light and it was just a dumb idea. Good in theory, but dumb lol! Silver is already a difficult shade to work with in pastry because just like as a clothing item, it shows every single little flaw easily. No excuses though, right?!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ARC-REACTOR-MARK-6-IRON-MAN-SUGAR-COOKIE-THAT-GLOWS-IN-THE-DARK.jpg">
          <img alt="ARC REACTOR MARK 6 IRON MAN SUGAR COOKIE THAT GLOWS IN THE DARK" class="aligncenter wp-image-1416 " height="577" src="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/ARC-REACTOR-MARK-6-IRON-MAN-SUGAR-COOKIE-THAT-GLOWS-IN-THE-DARK-1024x768.jpg" title="" width="770"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you use an aerosol edible paint for this project, I recommend that you spray it directly into your container and let it chill out for a few minute before panting. This gives it time to not only evaporate and thicken a little but it will kill that spray paint aroma. If you can get your hands on silver luster in powdered form, I recommend you making your own paint that way with vodka (dont worry, the alcohol evaporates nobody will be intoxicated its used all the time in pastry decorating). I ran out of powdered luster and couldnt find any on Amazon Prime. Didnt want to wait 2 weeks since I was in the middle of this project already.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/IMG_7331.jpg">
          <img alt="IMG_7331" class="aligncenter wp-image-1418 " height="536" src="http://ambyfelix.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/IMG_7331-1024x768.jpg" title="" width="715"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, I really hope you enjoy this recipe! ^_^
        </p>

           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            For Glow in the Dark Buttercream
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             2 Sticks Unsalted Butter (at room temperature)
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             3.5 Cups Confectioner's Sugar
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             1 TSP Vanilla Extract
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             5 TBSP Tonic Water
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             1 Drop Blue Food Coloring (I used pastel food colors)
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-section" yumprintsection="2">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Other Ingredients
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             Silver Luster
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="3">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            For Sugar Cookies
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
             1. Combine butter, Shortening, cream cheese, egg, sugar and vanilla extract until creamy.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
             2. Add remainder ingredients.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
             3. Form dough into a disc and wrap with plastic wrap. Chill in fridge for at least a half an hour before rolling out.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">
             4. Using a 3.5" diameter cookie cutter (mason jar lid is perfect) cut out your cookies.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="4">
             5. Chill cookie sheet in freezer for 5 minutes. This prevents cookies even more from spreading and turning too dark.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="5">
             6. Bake in oven on a silicon lined baking sheet for about 16 minutes at 375.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="4">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            For Glow in the Dark Buttercream
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
             1. Using a hand mixer, whip room tempered butter until fluffy and white.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
             2. Add in confectioner's sugar, about 1/2 cup at a time, and continue to whip until very thick.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
             3. Add in vanilla and 1 TBSP at a time of tonic water and continue to mix.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="3">
             4. Store buttercream in an airtight container in the fridge until ready to use.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-note-section" yumprintsection="5">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Notes
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-notes">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="0">
             Use a new, fine tip paint brush!
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="1">
             Cream cheese and flash freezing cookie dough helps prevent cookies from spreading!
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="2">
             Store buttercream in the fridge for up to one month.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="3">
             Buttercream will be very stiff when you take out of the fridge, so be sure to temper it down for about 15 minutes before spreading onto your cookies.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
