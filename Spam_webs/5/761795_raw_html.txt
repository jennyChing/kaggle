     <p>
      <b>
       <a href="/wiki/Pseudoscience" title="Pseudoscience">
        Pseudoscience
       </a>
      </b>
      is a broad group of theories or assertions about the natural world that claim or appear to be
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific" title="Scientific">
       scientific
      </a>
      , but that are not accepted as scientific by the
      <a href="/wiki/Scientific_community" title="Scientific community">
       scientific community
      </a>
      . Pseudoscience does
      <i>
       not
      </i>
      include most obsolete scientific or medical theories (see
      <a href="/wiki/Category:Obsolete_scientific_theories" title="Category:Obsolete scientific theories">
       Category:Obsolete scientific theories
      </a>
      ), nor does it include every idea that currently lacks sufficient scientific evidence (e.g.
      <a href="/wiki/String_theory" title="String theory">
       String theory
      </a>
      )
     </p>
     <p>
      This category comprises well-known topics that are generally considered pseudoscientific by the scientific community (such as
      <a href="/wiki/Astrology" title="Astrology">
       astrology
      </a>
      ) and topics that have very few followers and are obviously pseudoscientific (such as the modern belief in a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flat_Earth_Society" title="Flat Earth Society">
       flat Earth
      </a>
      ). The
      <a href="/wiki/Pejorative" title="Pejorative">
       pejorative
      </a>
      term itself is contested by various groups for various reasons. Generally speaking, if an article belongs in this category, the article's lead will contain a well-sourced statement that the subject is considered pseudoscience.
     </p>
