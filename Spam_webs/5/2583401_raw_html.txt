     <p>
      <b>
       GLAD
      </b>
      is one of the pioneers of Christian pop/rock and
      <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">
       a cappella
      </a>
      music, having formed as a progressive rock group in 1972 and discovered a large audience for their a cappella music in 1988.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Powell_Fe_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Powell_Fe-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Today, with over 1.5 million albums sold, they continue to perform concerts and release occasional recordings. As Contemporary Christian Music (
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/CCM_Magazine" title="CCM Magazine">
        CCM Magazine
       </a>
      </i>
      ) described it, "GLAD's elegant vocals helped set them apart from other pioneers of
      <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_Christian_music" title="Contemporary Christian music">
       Contemporary Christian music
      </a>
      . That vocal sound has since evolved into a complex, self-sustaining life form of its own..."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Glad_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      GLAD formed on the campus of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Chester_State_University_of_Pennsylvania" title="West Chester State University of Pennsylvania">
       West Chester State University of Pennsylvania
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Powell_Fe_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Powell_Fe-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      when singer Ed Nalle auditioned for a new Christian band. Nalle, along with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bob_Kauflin" title="Bob Kauflin">
       Bob Kauflin
      </a>
      would write and produce much of GLAD's early material in addition to his role as lead vocalist. The other members (from
      <a href="/wiki/Temple_University" title="Temple University">
       Temple University
      </a>
      in Philadelphia) were Kauflin (keyboards, vocals), T. Coble (bass, vocals), Don Nalle (bass and lead vocals ) John Bolles (guitar, vocals), and Brad Currie (drums). The group's name was chosen from a poll taken at a shopping mall.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      (interestingly enough, GLAD was not the winner of the shopping mall poll). Playing over 200 dates annually at college campuses, churches and concert halls throughout the United States, GLAD released their first two albums on
      <a href="/wiki/Myrrh_Records" title="Myrrh Records">
       Myrrh Records
      </a>
      :
      <i>
       GLAD
      </i>
      (1978) and
      <i>
       Beyond a Star
      </i>
      (1980). Brothers Wayne Scott Farley (guitar, vocals) and Mark Farley (drums) briefly joined the group to record the latter album,
      <i>
       Beyond a Star.
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1981, the groups signed with
      <a href="/wiki/Benson_Records" title="Benson Records">
       Benson Records
      </a>
      (on their Greentree label) and began releasing albums with different inflections:
      <i>
       Captured in Time
      </i>
      (1982) was markedly jazzy,
      <i>
       Champion of Love
      </i>
      (1985) was big and brassy, and
      <i>
       Who Do You Love?
      </i>
      (1987) offered driving synth rock.
     </p>
     <p>
      "The Reason" (on
      <i>
       Beyond a Star
      </i>
      ) was their first a cappella song, which they subsequently re-recorded for their watershed 1988 album,
      <i>
       The Acapella Project
      </i>
      . That album was released with some trepidation because there was no track record in the genre for the Christian market, but it proved tremendously popular (selling over 400,000 copies
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Powell_Fe_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Powell_Fe-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ), and received a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dove_Award" title="Dove Award">
       Dove Award
      </a>
      nomination and a
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">
        Billboard
       </a>
      </i>
      Critic's Choice Award.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Since that time, GLAD has alternated a cappella recordings with more subdued Adult Contemporary albums, the notable exception being 1995's lively
      <i>
       Color Outside the Lines
      </i>
      , co-produced by industry veterans Mark Baldwin and Joe Hogue for
      <a href="/wiki/Light_Records" title="Light Records">
       Light Records
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      GLAD made a venture into the mainstream later that year with
      <i>
       A Cappella
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gershwin" title="Gershwin">
        Gershwin
       </a>
      </i>
      (featuring arrangements by the legendary
      <a href="/wiki/Gene_Puerling" title="Gene Puerling">
       Gene Puerling
      </a>
      ), which helped introduce their music to a wider audience.
      <a href="/wiki/The_Charleston_Gazette" title="The Charleston Gazette">
       The Charleston Gazette
      </a>
      wrote, "GLAD has long been Christian pop music's most sophisticated ensemble... The singing, built around Ed Nalle's uncanny high tenor, has the elegance and sophistication the source material demands."
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      To date the group has produced twenty-three albums and performed thousands of concerts; additionally, since 1985 they have helped to secure sponsors for thousands of needy children in
      <a href="/wiki/Third_World" title="Third World">
       Third World
      </a>
      nations through
      <a href="/wiki/Compassion_International" title="Compassion International">
       Compassion International
      </a>
      and trips to countries such as
      <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">
       Haiti
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
       Mexico
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">
       Ecuador
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">
       Kenya
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
       Brazil
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">
       Indonesia
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      GLAD's current lineup includes Nalle and longtime members Chris Davis (a musician with a background in classical and electric guitar, plus session work in the Northwest) and John Gates (an experienced studio percussionist who backed
      <a href="/wiki/Gladys_Knight_%26_the_Pips" title="Gladys Knight &amp; the Pips">
       Gladys Knight &amp; the Pips
      </a>
      , Frankie and the Knockouts, and others), plus alternating keyboardists Paul Langford and Don Pardoe. Kauflin and Don Hart are the group's primary a cappella arrangers. Recently, the group has released recordings inspired by the writings of
      <a href="/wiki/John_Piper_(theologian)" title="John Piper (theologian)">
       John Piper
      </a>
      and the worship music of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sovereign_Grace_Ministries" title="Sovereign Grace Ministries">
       Sovereign Grace Ministries
      </a>
      (formerly PDI).
     </p>
