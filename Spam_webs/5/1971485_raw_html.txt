            <div class="content narrow-rule ">
             <p class="content-excerpt">
              Monocle Magazine has released its annual Quality of Life rankings for 2010.
             </p>
             <div class="post-index-meta clearfix">
              <a class="author" href="/author/jamie-song" title="PSFK Writer Jamie Song">
               Jamie Song
              </a>
              <ul class="data">
               <li class="date">
                16 june 2010
               </li>
               <li class="category">
                <a class=" light-green-color" href="/category/arts-culture">
                 Arts &amp; Culture
                </a>
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <p>
              <span id="more-66756">
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                4. Tokyo
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Despite the bustling crowds and high-rise buildings, Japans capital remains clean and eco-conscious. By 2013 the city is expected to create 200 hectares of green space alongside with 200,000 new roadside trees.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               image by
               <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tiltonlane/2470063712/">
                Tilton  Lane
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="helsinki" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66776" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/helsinki.jpg" width="525"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                5. Helsinki
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              This small city provides a welfare system that ensures a high quality of life, free education for everyone, and even free wifi. In addition, the city is connected to major cities  just 8 hour flights to both New York and Asia.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               image by
               <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/claudio_ar/2535281684/">
                Claudia.Ar
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="stockholm" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66781" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/stockholm.jpg" width="525"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                6. Stockholm
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Stockholm boasts Europes cleanest air and provides areas where you can swim in clear waters and cross-country ski across forests. Recently, the redevelopment of its docks and new residential projects has helped the city to rank at number six.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               image by
               <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/claudio_ar/2621893969/">
                Claudia.Ar
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="paris" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66780" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/paris.jpg" width="525"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                7. Paris
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Recent developments of reconnecting the city with its disenfranchised suburbs and taking back its place in the culinary world has leveraged Paris beyond its postcard-perfect streets and charming cafes.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               image by
               <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/skene/531192678/">
                skene
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="vienna" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66783" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/vienna.jpg" width="525"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                8. Vienna
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Vienna is home to top-ranking health care, a reliable public transportation network, acres of green space. On top of that, the Austrian capital continues its redevelopment efforts for its shorelines and sponsors numerous public cultural events.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               image by
               <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kliefi/2085536945/">
                Kliefi
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="melbourne" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66778" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/melbourne.jpg" width="525"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                9. Melbourne
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              Beyond its fun-loving cultural image, Melbourne means business. The states average economic growth is 3.3% and job growth is at 2.1%.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               image by
               <a href="http://http//www.flickr.com/photos/mtb_rider/2314279528/">
                alistair_35
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="madrid" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66777" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/madrid.jpg" width="525"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                10. Madrid
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              The capital of Spain has taken on a new endeavor titled the Madrid Rio Project, which reclaims 8km of river parkland and urban beaches. Other ground breaking projects include the creation of 16,000 new homes and green space.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               image by
               <a href="http://http//www.flickr.com/photos/ramonduran/3398988543/">
                R.  Duran
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <strong>
                The rest of the 2010 Most Liveable Cities Ranking are as follows:
               </strong>
              </em>
             </p>

              <br/>
              21. Singapore
              <br/>
              22. Portland
              <br/>

              <br/>
              24. Hamburg
              <br/>
              25. Lisbon
             </p>
