      <h2>
       HOW TO CLEANSTAINLESS STEEL APPLIANCES
      </h2>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       Ive had
       <a href="https://www.whirlpool.com/kitchen-collections_kitchen-stainless-collection.content.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Whirlpool stainless steel appliances
       </a>
       in my kitchen for 5 months now. If you own
       <strong>
        stainless steel appliances
       </strong>
       , you may be in the same boat I was in losing a battle..
       <em>
        a battle against fingerprints and smudges.
       </em>
       The grimy streaks were winning!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       I whined to my friends about the smudges all over my pretty new appliances. I asked everyone I knew, including
       <a href="http://bit.ly/howtocleanss" target="_blank">
        Mr. Google
       </a>
       ,
       <strong>
        how to clean stainless steel appliances
       </strong>
       . I got a slew of answers, from window cleaner to alcohol and baby oil, to simply pure water and numerous specially formulated cleaners. I tried, a clean dish cloth, paper towels and cloth diapers.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       Nothing was working.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       I vented my frustrations one day when I was on the phone with Jim, product manager at
       <strong>
        Whirlpool
       </strong>
       , when he told me something that changed my life!
       <span id="more-22962">
       </span>
       He said he uses
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.affresh.com/our_products/affresh_stainless_steel_cleaner" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         affresh Stainless steel cleaner
        </a>
        and a microfiber cloth
       </strong>
       . Jim told me he sprays the cloth with
       <strong>
        affresh Stainless steel cleaner
       </strong>
       then wipes down his appliances and has had great luck with that method.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <img alt="cleaning stainless steel with affresh TidyMom" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22965" height="735" src="http://tidymom.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/cleaning-stainless-steel-with-affresh-TidyMom.jpg" title="Learn how to clean stainless steel appliances and be free of smudges and fingerprints  at TidyMom.net" width="450"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       To be honest, I doubted it was going to work, but figured what did I have to lose? I already had a bottle of
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004VJSUJY/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        <strong>
         affresh Stainless steel cleaner
        </strong>
       </a>
       and a clean
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00AZUC0QW/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         microfiber cloth
        </a>
       </strong>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       As soon as we hung up the phone I headed to the kitchen to give it a go. To my amazement it WORKED!!!
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        If appliances have visible grime and food, take a clean microfiber cloth and a little warm soapy water to wipe down surface (always wipe with the grain of the stainless steel). Then take the same cloth, rinse it well and wipe down surface again, removing soapy residue.
       </li>
       <li>
        Use a dry microfiber cloth and to spray the
        <strong>
         affresh Stainless steel cleaner on the cloth, NOT on the appliance
        </strong>
        (I usually just give it 2 squirts  dont saturate the cloth)
       </li>
       <li>
        Wipe entire surface in the direction of the grain.
       </li>
       <li>
        Flip microfiber cloth over and buff surface using a little elbow grease until shiny!
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       I still have to clean them daily, but I have to tell you, I recently hired a cleaning lady to help me out a few times a month and when she walked into my kitchen she said
       <em>
        <strong>
         your appliances look AMAZING! how do you keep them so clean?!
        </strong>
       </em>
       .TRUE STORY!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <img alt="affresh stainless steel cleaner atTidyMom.net" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-22964" height="679" src="http://tidymom.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/affresh-stainless-steel-cleaner-TidyMom.jpg" title="Use affresh stainless steel cleaner and learn how to be free of smudes and fingerprints  at TidyMom.net" width="450"/>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align: left;">
       5 DONTS WHEN CLEANING STAINLESS STEEL
      </h3>
      <ol>
       <li>
        Do NOT use anything abrasive, such as steel wool or scrubber sponges. These may scratch the finish.
       </li>
       <li>
        Never clean in circular motion or against the grain. Always clean stainless steel with the grain.
       </li>
       <li>
        Never use chlorine bleach on stainless steel. It will ruin the finish.
       </li>
       <li>
        Do not allow residue to sit on surface. Grime, dirt and cleaning products should be removed as soon as possible.
       </li>
       <li>
        Do not expect stainless steel to look beautiful on its own. Its going to take a little elbow grease and good daily maintenance to keep it looking shiny.
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       Not only am I still using the affresh Stainless steel cleaner on all of my stainless steel appliances daily, but I REALLY think
       <strong>
        the key is to use a microfiber cloth
       </strong>
       (I keep two that are only used on my appliances)
       <strong>
        and to spray the affresh Stainless steel cleaner on the cloth, NOT on the appliance.
       </strong>
       <strong>
        <em>
         Im now winning the battle with the fingerprints and smudges
        </em>
       </strong>
       and the BEST part.
       <strong>
        my appliances are clean AND streak free
       </strong>
       !! (so many other methods would remove smudges, but then left streaks).
      </p>

       I talked to the people at
       <a href="http://www.affresh.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

       and they said theyd be happy to hook up some TidyMom readers with their Stainless steel cleaner!

         <p>
          I agree that this post feels very commercialized with all of the the Amazon affiliate links and MANY MANY URLs to the product.
         </p>
         <p>
          The sad fact is that these cleaning product companies are confusing what is really going on with stainless steel. You dont need to CLEAN anything, its almost impossible to clean stainless steel entirely because there are millions of tiny, micro-scopic pores, cracks, and streaks that make up the surface of stainless steel (thats how it has a grain direction). The streaks and fingerprints you see are whats left after cleaning the top of the surface and the oils that got there from fingers, etc. are inside the hard-to-reach grooves like hiding in a mountainous valley. All you have to do is fill in the valleys. Olive oil will do this nicely. Think of it like sanding and priming wood. Fill in ALL the gaps with oil and theres no place for the fingerprint oils to go. Just wipe occasionally with the grain and its like painting the steel over anew. The only thing is that the olive oil (or any other oil that you use) will attract dust more than raw steel will, but a simple wipe will make it all go away.
         </p>
         <p>
          Not only does olive oil work on all of my S/S Frigidaire appliances, but its also safe for kids and pets because its just olive oil.no chemicals.
         </p>
