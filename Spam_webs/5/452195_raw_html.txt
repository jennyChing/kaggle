     <p>
      <b>
       Elattuvalapil Sreedharan
      </b>
      is a retired
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Engineering_Service" title="Indian Engineering Service">
       Indian Engineering Service
      </a>
      (IES) officer popularly known as the "Metro Man". He is regarded as legend of India. He is credited for changing the face of public transport in India by his leadership for building the
      <a href="/wiki/Konkan_Railway" title="Konkan Railway">
       Konkan Railway
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Metro" title="Delhi Metro">
       Delhi Metro
      </a>
      when he served as the managing director of Delhi Metro between 19952012.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metromankochi_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-metromankochi-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was awarded the Padma Shri by the Government of India in 2001,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chevalier_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Chevalier-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the Padma Vibhushan in 2008,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Padmavibhushan_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Padmavibhushan-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%A9gion_d%27honneur" title="Lgion d'honneur">
       Chevalier de la Lgion d'honneur
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chevalier_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Chevalier-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in 2005 and named one of Asia's Heroes by TIME magazine in 2003.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=E._Sreedharan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      E. Sreedharan was born on 12 June 1932 in
      <a href="/wiki/Pattambi" title="Pattambi">
       Pattambi
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Palakkad_district" title="Palakkad district">
       Palakkad district
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">
       Kerala
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thehindubiography_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-thehindubiography-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-delhimetro_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-delhimetro-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His family hails from
      <a href="/wiki/Karukaputhur" title="Karukaputhur">
       Karukaputhur
      </a>
      , near
      <a href="/wiki/Koottanad" title="Koottanad">
       Koottanad
      </a>
      , part of
      <a href="/wiki/Thrithala" title="Thrithala">
       Thrithala
      </a>
      legislative assembly, Palakkad district, Kerala. He completed his education at the
      <a href="/wiki/Basel_Evangelical_Mission_Higher_Secondary_School" title="Basel Evangelical Mission Higher Secondary School">
       Basel Evangelical Mission Higher Secondary School
      </a>
      and then went to the
      <a href="/wiki/Government_Victoria_College,_Palakkad" title="Government Victoria College, Palakkad">
       Victoria College
      </a>
      in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palghat" title="Palghat">
       Palghat
      </a>
      . He later on completed his Civil Engineering from the
      <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru_Technological_University,_Kakinada" title="Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, Kakinada">
       Government Engineering College
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kakinada" title="Kakinada">
       Kakinada
      </a>
      , Andhra Pradesh known as
      <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru_Technological_University,_Kakinada" title="Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, Kakinada">
       JNTUK
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_a_lecturer">
       Career as a lecturer
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=E._Sreedharan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career as a lecturer">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      For a short tenure, he worked as a lecturer in Civil engineering at the Government Polytechnic,
      <a href="/wiki/Kozhikode" title="Kozhikode">
       Kozhikode
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thehindubiography_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-thehindubiography-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-delhimetro_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-delhimetro-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and a year at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombay" title="Bombay">
       Bombay
      </a>
      Port Trust as an apprentice.
     </p>
     <p>
      Later he joined the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Engineering_Service" title="Indian Engineering Service">
       Indian Engineering Service
      </a>
      (IES), after clearing ESE-1953 conducted by the UPSC. His first assignment was in the Southern Railway as a Probationary Assistant Engineer in December 1954.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Government_career">
       Government career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=E._Sreedharan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pamban_Bridge_2009.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="709" data-file-width="1086" height="131" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Pamban_Bridge_2009.jpg/200px-Pamban_Bridge_2009.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Pamban_Bridge_2009.jpg/300px-Pamban_Bridge_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Pamban_Bridge_2009.jpg/400px-Pamban_Bridge_2009.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pamban_Bridge_2009.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Pamban Bridge
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In December 1964, a cyclone washed away parts of
      <a href="/wiki/Pamban_Bridge" title="Pamban Bridge">
       Pamban Bridge
      </a>
      that connected
      <a href="/wiki/Rameswaram" title="Rameswaram">
       Rameswaram
      </a>
      to mainland
      <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">
       Tamil Nadu
      </a>
      . The Railways set a target of six months for the bridge to be repaired while Sreedharan's boss, under whose jurisdiction the bridge came, reduced it to three months. Sreedharan was put in-charge of the execution and he restored the bridge in just 46 days.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thehindubiography_7-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-thehindubiography-7">
        <span>
         [

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Konkan_Railway">
       Konkan Railway
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=E._Sreedharan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Konkan Railway">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Konkan_Railway" title="Konkan Railway">
       Konkan Railway
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      He was promoted as general manager, Western Railway in July 1987, and in July 1989 elevated to the post of Member Engineering, Railway Board and ex-officio Secretary to the Government of India.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-delhimetro_8-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-delhimetro-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On his retirement in June 1990, the Government made it clear it still needed his services and he was appointed the CMD of
      <a href="/wiki/Konkan_Railway" title="Konkan Railway">
       Konkan Railway
      </a>
      on contract in 1990 by the then railway minister,
      <a href="/wiki/George_Fernandes" title="George Fernandes">
       George Fernandes
      </a>
      . Under his stewardship, the company executed its mandate in seven years. The project was unique in many respects. It was the first major project in India to be undertaken on a BOT (Build-Operate-Transfer) basis; the organisation structure was different from that of a typical Indian Railway set-up; the project had 93 tunnels along a length of 82km and involved tunnelling through soft soil. The total project covered 760km and had over 150 bridges. That a public sector project could be completed without significant cost and time overruns was considered an achievement by many.
     </p>

       Chevalier de la Lgion d'honneur

        </span>
        12
        <span>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=E._Sreedharan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A Book on Sreedharan's life is 'Karmayogi: E. Sreedharante Jeevitha Katha' (Translated: The story of E. Sreedharan's life) is an authentic biography by M. S. Asokan. A short biography on E. Sreedharan was penned by P.V. Alby, named 'Jeevithavijayathinte Paadapusthakam' (translated: A Texbook on success in life). Both these books were top sellers in
      <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">
       Kerala
      </a>
      during the time Sreedharan took up his post on the Kochi Metro project as
      <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Metro_Rail_Corporation" title="Delhi Metro Rail Corporation">
       Delhi Metro Rail Corporation
      </a>
      's principal advisor.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Family">
       Family
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=E._Sreedharan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Family">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Sreedharan's wife is Radha Sreedharan. The couple has four children: eldest son Ramesh is one of the vice-presidents at Tata Consultancy Services; daughter Shanthi Menon runs a school in Bangalore, another son, Achyuth Menon, is a doctor in the UK. Their youngest son is M. Krishnadas, who works at ABB.
