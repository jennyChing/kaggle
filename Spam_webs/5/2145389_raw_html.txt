         </strong>
         , do you picture
         <a href="http://myitchytravelfeet.com/2011/05/10/baby-boomer-adventure-lucca-italy/" target="_blank" title="Renaissance era towns">
          <strong>
           Renaissance era Italian towns
          </strong>
         </a>
         clinging to the side of a hill? So did we. UntilAlan and I experienced
         <strong>
          Sovana
         </strong>
         during our
         <a href="http://myitchytravelfeet.com/2010/10/05/italy-seabourn-adventure/" target="_blank" title="boomer road trip to Northern Italy">
          <strong>
           boomer road trip to Northern Italy
          </strong>
         </a>
         . The
         <strong>
          Etruscan village in southern Tuscany
         </strong>
         was the last stop on our land tour before boarding
         <a href="http://myitchytravelfeet.com/2010/12/08/seabourn-sojourn-review-accommodations/" target="_blank" title="Seabourn Sojourn">
          <strong>
           Seabourn Sojourn
          </strong>
         </a>
         in Civitavecchia, the port for Rome, for a ride through the Mediterranean and across the Atlantic Ocean to Ft. Lauderdale.
        </p>
        <p>
         After driving up and down the curvy, hilly roads of Tuscany, Claudio Fontana of
         <a href="http://www.alcitalytours.com/" target="_blank" title="A La Carte Italy Tours">
          <strong>
           A La Carte Italy Tours
          </strong>
         </a>
         , headed the van up one last hill to the
         <strong>
          village of Sovana
         </strong>
         . Gray, stone buildings crowded the narrow, cobblestone main street. In this
         <strong>
          off-the-beaten-path Tuscany destination
         </strong>
         , 100+residents live with
         <a href="http://www.borghitalia.it/html/borgo_en.php?codice_borgo=487" target="_blank" title="Etruscan history">
          <strong>
           Etruscan and Medieval history
          </strong>
         </a>
         in what feels like an open air museum.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 490px">
         <a href="http://photography.myitchytravelfeet.com/Travel/Europe/Italy-Sovana/i-fNzLN24/0/L/P1110187-L.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="sovana-tuscany-maze" class=" " height="360" src="http://photography.myitchytravelfeet.com/Travel/Europe/Italy-Sovana/i-fNzLN24/0/L/P1110187-L.jpg" title="maze-sovana-hotel-resort-italy" width="480"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Maze at Sovana Hotel &amp; Resort
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         When Claudio parked in front of the entrance to
         <a href="http://www.hotelsovana.com/" target="_blank" title="Sovana Hotel and Resort">
          <strong>
           Sovana Hotel and Resort
          </strong>
         </a>
         , I wasnt so sure that we had made the right lodging choice. But the buildings edifice didnt yield any clues as to what we would find inside. Walking through a nondescript doorway, we discovered a lobby that could have been confused for a spacious wine cellar. I imagined that the thick, stone walls provided a cool respite during hot Italian summers. A wrought iron staircase led to spacious accommodations with cozy public spaces tucked here and there. From the window in our room, we could see the hotels gardens and boxwood maze. There was definitely more to
         <strong>
          Sovana Hotel and Resort
         </strong>
         than its curb appeal indicated.
        </p>
        <p>
         Later we met our traveling companions, Jack and Sue, on the patio where Claudio had arranged a platter of appetizers with Italian wine to celebrate the end of our
         <strong>
          boomer travels
         </strong>
         . Since it was October, the gardening season was winding down yet roses still bloomed along a pleasant path with views of an olive grove planted on a distant hill.
        </p>
        <p>
         For dinner, we walked up the quiet, main street of Sovana to
         <a href="http://www.ristorantedeimerli.net/" target="_blank" title="Ristorante dei Merli">
          <strong>
           Ristorante dei Merli
          </strong>
         </a>
         to discover an empty dining room. Either Sovana is definitely off-the-beaten-path or our October arrival was well beyond tourist season. Our small group of travelers had the place to ourselves as Claudio translated one last Italian dinner menu for us.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 467px">
         <a href="http://photography.myitchytravelfeet.com/Travel/Europe/Italy-Sovana/i-RX2gF84/0/L/P1110160-L.jpg" target="_blank">
          <img alt="tuscany-religion-history-duomo" class=" " height="420" src="http://photography.myitchytravelfeet.com/Travel/Europe/Italy-Sovana/i-RX2gF84/0/L/P1110160-L.jpg" title="duomo-sovana-italy-middle-ages" width="457"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Sovana Duomo
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Since this was a one-night stop, there wasnt much time to explore the Etruscan and Medieval history that surrounded us. Sovana houses two churches of note  the Romanesque church of Santa Maria Maggiore (12  13th century) and a cathedral from the same time period. Tiny shops line the street while Etruscan tombs and architectural remains are strewn about the hill and in the surrounding area. Its a destination for soaking in the ambiance and doing nothing in particular. Our group agreed that Sovana deserved more than one night on the itinerary. It would have made a great place for boomers to stop, rest and regroup before touring more of Tuscany.
        </p>
        <p>
         What will we do on our next visit? Explore the Etruscan tombs  dating from the 7th century BC just west of town, take a dip in nearby thermal springs and book a hiking or biking excursion with Sovana Hotel and Resort.
        </p>
        <p>
         Have you been to Sovana? Post a comment to share your travel experience. Alan and I hope to return during one of our boomer adventures for a longer visit.
        </p>
