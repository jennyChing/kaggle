       <h1 class="title">
        The Wonders of Green Smoothies
       </h1>
       <div class="post-meta">
        <span class="small">
         By
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://www.passionatehomemaking.com/author/ledmonds" rel="author" title="Posts by Lindsay">
           Lindsay
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="small">
         on
        </span>
        <abbr class="date time published" title="2009-02-17T01:00:43+0000">
         February 17, 2009
        </abbr>
        <span class="small">
         in
        </span>
        <span class="categories">
         <a href="http://www.passionatehomemaking.com/category/recipes-kitchen-tips" title="View all posts in recipes &amp; kitchen tips">
          recipes &amp; kitchen tips
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         <img alt="img_5296" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-4383" height="300" src="http://www.passionatehomemaking.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/img_5296-200x300.jpg" title="img_5296" width="200"/>
         <em>
          Did you know that through one green smoothie you can consume 5-8 servings of fruits and vegetables? Did you also know that eating green smoothies is one of the easiest way to digest the full nutrients of fruits and vegetables?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Green smoothies are a wonderful alternative, as opposed to juices, of absorbing the full potential by retaining the entire whole form of the fruit/vegetable, thus receiving all the fiber. They can assist in
         <strong>
          boosting energy, building your immune system, detoxing impurities, and loosing weight
         </strong>
         . Greens are
         <strong>
          rich in magnesium
         </strong>
         and alkaline minerals like
         <strong>
          calcium
         </strong>
         and are superb in their
         <strong>
          Omega-3 unsaturated fat content
         </strong>
         . Believe it or not, greens are one of the
         <strong>
          richest sources of protein
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Another one of my new year goals was to begin eating more greens in our diet. What better way than to include these in our morning smoothies? We serve kefir smoothies three times a week for breakfast, so my desire was to discreetly begin adding some nutritious greens to the mix. I have found it to be incredibly simple, delicious, and so much easier to eat than a large salad (especially for the kids). Not only can you really not taste the greens at all, but you are getting a huge boost in nutrition. Greens are especially frugal and thus another good encouragement to begin consuming them. We have stayed amazingly healthy over this past month since beginning the green smoothie pursuit, even when we have been exposed to some nasty illnesses around us. Could it be?
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-4227">
         </span>
         The best combination is a balance of 40% greens to 60% fruits. Keep the greens varied as well. Spinach is our favorite, in addition to kale. Other options include: celery, chard, romaine, alfalfa, parsley, beet greens, and collard greens.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have found you can make a green smoothie out of pretty much any fruit and greens combination!
        </p>
        <p>
         Here is how I start. Combine:
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          I have found it easiest with a traditional blender to combine the greens first in order to get them real smooth. This would not be necessary with a Vita-Mix, but with a standard blender combining all the ingredients at once usually leaves you with green clumps.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <img alt="img_5298" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-4384" height="300" src="http://www.passionatehomemaking.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/img_5298-200x300.jpg" title="img_5298" width="200"/>
          1 1/2 cups of liquids
         </strong>
         <em>
          (I usually use a combination of water, coconut milk, and kefir)
         </em>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          3 oz or more fresh greens
         </strong>
         , which is approximately half a bag or 3 large handfuls (spinach or kale are best!) -
         <em>
          start with smaller quantities and work you way up!
         </em>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          2-3 Tbsp orange juice concentrate
         </strong>
         , optional
         <em>
          (this is a more frugal alternative than using pre-mixed juices  it stretches the juice further)
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         After these ingredients are thoroughly blended, you can add an assortment of fruit.
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are our favorite additions:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1/2-1 cup Frozen strawberries or raspberries
          <br/>
          1 Banana
          <br/>
          1/2 cup Frozen Blueberries
          <br/>
          1/4 cup Frozen Pineapple
          <br/>
          2-3 Frozen Peach slices
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I have also used canned pears on occasion as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         Blend and add a little stevia or honey to sweeten and any additional boosters.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Additional boosters:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ground flax seeds
         <br/>
         Ground pumpkin seeds
         <br/>
         Coconut oil
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy!
        </p>

            I just wanted to let everyone know that the bags of prewashed greens in the produce section should not be used. I am in the process of starting my own sprouts so that I can include them (some sprouts are even more nutritious than greens and good organic greens are not always available here).

                The vitamin A in kale comes from beta carotene, which is only converted to vit A by the body when needed. The rest is discarded.
