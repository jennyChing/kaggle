      <div class="post-content">
       <div class="ip-wrapper" id="ap-in-post-1">
        <div class="adslot" data-dfp="ap2_in_post_1" data-mapping="allWideVariant" data-sizes="300,336,468,336,468,728" id="ap2_in_post_1">
        </div>
        <script type="text/javascript">
         ap_adsense("ap-in-post-1", "6264997281", "1");
        </script>
        <script>
         (deployads = window.deployads || []).push({});
        </script>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a class=" no-style" href="http://www.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/projectglasstiny.jpg">
        </a>
        They may not be available for sale yet, but Google would like you to know that
        <a href="http://www.androidpolice.com/2012/02/21/google-is-planning-glasses-with-an-augmented-reality-heads-up-display-may-sell-them-by-years-end/">
         the future is coming
        </a>
        . Dubbed "Project Glass", Google has released some photos of what the now-confirmed Google Glasses might look like when released, alongside a video demonstrating how the UI might work. It's all just show and no tell right now, but it's enough to whet our appetite.
       </p>
       <p>
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="376" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/9c6W4CCU9M4?fs=1&amp;feature=oembed" width="668">
        </iframe>
       </p>
       <p>
        It's not quite enough, though, to allay our reservations. For starters, the glasses, while not particularly gaudy and actually somewhat stylish, seem to only have one tiny lens hovering over a small portion of the right eye. It's unclear how this is supposed to work. The video demonstrates an obviously-simulated UI that encompasses the entire field of vision, but the prototype glasses seem to be incapable of displaying such a UI.
       </p>
       <p align="center">
        <a class=" no-style" href="http://www.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/glass1.jpg">
         <img alt="glass1" height="244" src="http://www.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/glass1_thumb.jpg" style="display: inline" title="glass1" width="215"/>
        </a>
        <a class=" no-style" href="http://www.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/glass2.jpg">
         <img alt="glass2" height="244" src="http://www.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/glass2_thumb.jpg" style="display: inline" title="glass2" width="215"/>
        </a>
        <a class=" no-style" href="http://www.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/glass3.jpg">
         <img alt="glass3" height="244" src="http://www.androidpolice.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/glass3_thumb.jpg" style="display: inline" title="glass3" width="215"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        It's worth noting that one of the engineers on Project Glass has a history with eye-based displays. Babak Parvis, an associate professor at the University of Washington, previously worked on a project that brought a single pixel display to contact lenses. Google's going to need more than a single pixel for its displays, but who knows? Maybe this team is exactly what is needed to bring an entirely glasses-free heads-up display to the world. Though, we're likely a long ways away from that technology.
       </p>
       <div class="inj-ad-wrapper post-ad" id="ap-in-post-2">
        <div class="adslot" data-dfp="ap2_in_post_2" data-mapping="injectedVariant" data-sizes="300,300,468,336,468,468" id="ap2_in_post_2">
        </div>
        <script>
         (deployads = window.deployads || []).push({});
        </script>
       </div>
       <p>
        Still, these aren't necessarily what Google will release. The Google+ post announcing Project Glass even specifically states that Google is looking for input from you, the lovely loyal users. While Google isn't exactly a stranger to user feedback, this is a pretty big opportunity to weigh in on what could be, if implemented properly, a whole new market of devices.
       </p>
       <p>
        So what do you think? Too weird looking? Prefer contact lenses? Wouldn't be caught dead with any heads-up display on your face? Sound off in the comments. Then be sure to let Google know what your thoughts are as well.
       </p>
