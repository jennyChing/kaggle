     <p>
      Evidence
of one conspiracy does not prove another, yet -- in
      <a href="http://www.opednews.com/articles/Manning-Snowden-and-the-M-by-Cheri-Roberts-Change_Congress_Corruption_Government-130831-375.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       <u>
        today's hyper-vigilant
atmosphere
       </u>
      </a>
      , it is easy to jump to assumptions, especially when it's
related to a hot button issue.
     </p>
     <p>
      Conspiracies
happen all of the time and they happen all over the world. They are not unique
to America or even the internet. The internet however, does allow   the   conspiracy seeds to grow at astronomical rates  . In
fact, to some, the mere writing of this piece is a conspiracy and I will be
attacked for writing it.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ironically,
I often get attacked more relentlessly because  I myself am a conspiracy theorist   with   deep
opinions on things like 9/11  ,
      <a href="http://www.opednews.com/articles/Medicine-Money-a-Prescrip-by-Cheri-Roberts-130131-403.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       <u>
        the FDA
       </u>
      </a>
      and  more  so the expectation
of many is that I too will buy into another conspiracy.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       <i>
        But, They're Real"
       </i>
      </b>
     </p>
     <p>
      Although
conspiracies are real -- and those trying to expose them are often lambasted,
[it has been my experience] the average conspiracy theorist spends little time
investigating anything
      <i>
       outside
      </i>
      of the theory they believe and when they do, it is with
confirmation biased eyes.
     </p>
     <p>
      Of course this only narrows the view of the believer and little
else can eek its way through.
     </p>
     <p>
      To be
fair, a turn of the mind after September 11th, 2001 and the previously
unconstitutional  legislative
constriction on American's rights  has left many
feeling [rightfully] suspicious of just about anything.
     </p>

     <p>
      <b>
       <i>
        Michael Hastings
       </i>
      </b>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the
case of the unexpected and  unusual
death of award-winning journalist Michael Mahon Hastings  (January
28, 1980 -- June 18, 2013),there
      <i>
       are
      </i>
      enough red flags to
warrant at least a serious and open-minded look; after all, it's
      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Runaway_General#Rolling_Stone_article_and_resignation" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       <u>
        not like he was just some
schmuck reporter no one knew
       </u>
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Michael Hastings  didn't make waves,
he made tsunamis and for
      <a href="http://www.salon.com/news/opinion/glenn_greenwald/2011/02/27/hastings/index.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       <u>
        those who have been
caught in the deluge
       </u>
      </a>
      , instant enemies; enemies that included
powerful people.
     </p>
     <p>
      There
      <i>
       should
      </i>
      be
a
      <i>
       real
      </i>
      , and
thorough independent investigation and until there is, there are going to be
questions and conspiracies.
     </p>
     <p>
      And,
just like those who
      <i>
       do not
      </i>
      believe in "crazy conspiracies" will not take the time to
look at any evidence of one, it is unfortunately equally true that
      <a href="http://cherispeak.wordpress.com/2013/07/26/ssg-biggs-has-hastings-friend-turned-family-foe/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
       <u>
        those perpetuating the
conspiracies about Hastings' death
       </u>
      </a>
      also give little credence to
things that point away from their theory and corroborate a non-conspiracy.
     </p>
