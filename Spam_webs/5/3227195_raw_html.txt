        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          The look on Frank Lampards face when he scored for Manchester City against Chelsea early this season took his father back more than 30 years, to his sons first football match.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Solemn, says Frank Snr. That was his expression that day which seems a lifetime ago now and it was the same when he scored that equaliser against his old club.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Young Frank was five when his dad took him to a park near their home on the Essex-East London borders and asked the organiser of a schoolboy five-a-side if his lad could join in.
         </font>
        </p>

So when the ball came to him in front of the posts he just banged it in, before remembering which way he was supposed to be kicking.

This time the restrained response stemmed from his maturity of 36 years, rather than childlike innocence.

          <img alt="Lampard's last goal for Chelsea came against Stoke in April last year - but he wasn't offered a new contract" class="blkBorder img-share" height="411" id="i-e25cfee593acd655" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/01/29/1CDCC90F00000578-2931468-image-a-51_1422547126441.jpg" width="634"/>
         </div>
         <p class="imageCaption">
          Lampard's last goal for Chelsea came against Stoke in April last year - but he wasn't offered a new contract
         </p>
        </div>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Then football became Franks life. He was not the most naturally gifted. He had to work hard on his game. Very hard. Nothing came on a plate. He had to prove himself at West Ham. He had to prove himself at Chelsea when he first went there and they played him out of position wide on the right until he kept scoring goals in tough away games.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Years ago I told him that the first law of the game is to play. I said it again. Because that is always what we love the most and what we will miss the most. That was my opinion but hes very much his own man. He made the decision.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          That decision was to rankle in New York when Pelligrini decided to keep Lampard in Manchester until the end of this season. But there will be no further extension of that arrangement.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Lampard Snr says: Since I go to most of his games I expect Ill be making half a dozen or so trips over there later this year. Im really looking forward to watching Frank play his first matches in New York and America this summer.
         </font>
        </p>
        <div class="artSplitter mol-img-group">
         <div class="mol-img">
          <img alt="Frank Snr, pictured with his boy and future daughter-in-law Christine Bleakley, goes to young Frank's games" class="blkBorder img-share" height="347" id="i-acacd88fe5d3b31e" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/01/29/0B427556000005DC-2931468-Frank_Snr_pictured_with_his_boy_and_future_daughter_in_law_Chris-m-62_1422548742029.jpg" width="634"/>

         <p class="imageCaption">
          The proud dad was on hand when Frank was awarded his 100th England cap at Wembley in November 2013

        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Make that in mid-June at the latest. Perhaps as early as May if City fall out of contention for the Premier League and are knocked out of the Champions League before the final.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          The timing will depend on professional criteria, not how much he is enjoying himself in Manchester.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Lampard settled in quickly at the Etihad, in part with the help of City legend Mike Summerbee who is now an ambassador for the club.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Frank Snr explains: Mike and I go back a long way. When I was a full back at West Ham and him a winger with City we had a good rivalry. We used to kick lumps out of each other. We were hard and honest with each other because unlike most wingers, he kicked back. Since then weve always been good to each other down the years.
         </font>
        </p>
        <div class="artSplitter mol-img-group">
         <div class="mol-img">
          <img alt="Lampard made his West Ham debut in January 1996 when his uncle, Harry Redknapp, was the manager" class="blkBorder img-share" height="421" id="i-ce818567db54c9b5" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2015/01/29/0B66ACD900000578-2931468-image-m-56_1422547875500.jpg" width="634"/>
         </div>
         <p class="imageCaption">
          Lampard made his West Ham debut in January 1996 when his uncle, Harry Redknapp, was the manager
         </p>
        </div>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          As soon as Frank got there he told me that Mike was very welcoming and kind to him so I knew he was in safe hands. I knew the manager would take to him, too, once he got to know him. Because what you get with Frank is a model professional.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Citys fans have been fantastic with him too, just like Chelseas. Great.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Now comes the litmus test of the Stamford Bridge love affair with their all-time leading scorer and top midfield player.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          The Lampards have no qualms about returning, even though City will be striving to prevent Chelsea pulling vitally further clear in the title race.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Frank Snr says: They know the depth of our feeling for the club. We both still live within a stones throw of the Bridge. I am sure they remember everything Frank has done for them. Like playing in that Champions League semi-final against Liverpool only a couple of days after his mother died.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Avram Grant was the manager and when we lost Pat he offered to rest him. But my lad did not want to let the fans down. He insisted on playing. Scored the penalty which got them to the final. And we will always carry with us that image of him pointing to his mum in heaven.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          He scored in the final, too. And although Chelsea were to lose to that other team from Manchester on penalties that was yet another of those goals which have hallmarked his success.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          His father says: He also had to work hard to win over the England fans. He got a lot of jibes from the Wembley crowd at one point but I said to him that his goals, on top of his hard work, would turn it round. Scoring is the hardest thing and when you put your goals on the table you earn your respect.
         </font>
        </p>

        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          There were more than 200 for Chelsea. Now one against them could be enough to complicate Jose Mourinhos campaign to regain the championship from City.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Will they face him from the start or from the bench?
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          The Lampards dont know yet, although much of his playing time for City has been as a scoring substitute.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Either way, Frank Snr knows how his son will react.
         </font>
        </p>

         <p class="imageCaption">
         </p>

        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Hell approach it as a proper professional. If he does score he wont celebrate. He has too much respect for Chelsea, especially the fans. Me too. I think I may have invented that run round the corner flag when I scored one of my rare goals with a diving header against Everton at Elland Road in an FA Cup semi final. But on Saturday Ill just sit quietly watching.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          I have a good relationship with the fans. When I was caught up in the traffic going to the Etihad last weekend they were joking through the car windows and having a giggle with me. And Ill be among friendly faces in my usual seat, like I was on Tuesday when I went to see them beat Liverpool. Dont forget, me and Frank have kept our season tickets at the Bridge.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Whatever happens its an incredible story which not even Charles Dickens could have written.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          As it happens, Frank Jnr is penning the final chapter with his exploits on the field of play.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Will it have a fairytale ending, like the popular childrens books he now enjoys writing?
         </font>
        </p>
        <p class="mol-para-with-font">
         <font style="font-size:1.2em">
          Not even the Lampards know that.
         </font>
        </p>
