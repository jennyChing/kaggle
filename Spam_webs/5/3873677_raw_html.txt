    <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-leftRailSpanner" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Egg Baked Inside a Muffin
     </h1>
     <img height="526" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Egg-Baked-Inside-a-Muffin-2-790x526.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         March 25, 2014
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/egg-baked-inside-a-muffin-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/egg-baked-inside-a-muffin-2" rel="attachment wp-att-5404">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I admit it, I really love sticking an egg in something. Oh, get your mind out of the gutter! But if youve seen my
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/avocado-fried-egg/" title="Avocado Fried Egg">
          Avocado Fried Egg
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/kale-baked-eggs/" title="Kale Baked Eggs">
          Kale Baked Eggs
         </a>
         or my
         <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/artichoke-baked-egg/" title="Artichoke Baked Egg">
          Artichoke Baked Eggs
         </a>
         , you know exactly what I am talking about. So an egg baked inside a muffin seems like the logical next step, yes? Oh, and it is savory, cheesy and sweet yolk running everywhere. Yum!
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            8
            <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/ingredient/egg/">
             Eggs
            </a>
            , room temperature
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            Water and Ice
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Red Onion, small dice
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Cups Flour
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Teaspoons Salt, divided
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon Ground Black Pepper
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tablespoon Baking Powder
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon Baking Soda
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cups Buttermilk
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Butter
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 sprig Thyme, leaves minced
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            3 Ounces Cheddar
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            3 Ounces Gruyere
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            3 Ounces Parmesan
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Teaspoon Olive Oil
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6 stuffed muffins
           </span>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT47M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            47 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT25M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            25 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <meta content="PT22M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Cook time
           <div style="float: right">
            22 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <h2>
           Oven Temperature:
           <span>
            400 F
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Recipe Type:
           </h2>
           Muffins
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. Bring 12 cups of water to a full boil and add
         <strong>
          1 teaspoon of the salt
         </strong>
         . Gently, yet quickly, lower
         <strong>
          6 eggs
         </strong>
         into the water. Boil the eggs for 4 minutes and 30 seconds.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Create an an ice bath while the eggs are boiling, using a large mixing bowl. Once the eggs have cooked for the allotted time, immediately transfer them the ice bath.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="606" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/ice-bath/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/ice-bath" rel="attachment wp-att-5392">
          <img alt="Ice Bath" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5392" height="363" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Ice-Bath-600x363.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. This next step is like one of those scenes from a Kung Fu movie where a budding warriors patience and skill is tested by catching flies in chopsticks and what not. Seriously, find your moment of Zen because if you dont youre gonna go all Hulk here. Gently tap the top and bottom of each egg on the kitchen counter. Then, while applying NO pressure other than what is needed to roll the egg with your palm, give it a few rolls. Now peel the eggs under a thin stream of warm water, carefully removing the membrane but being careful enough to not crack the egg white. Gently dry them with a paper towel and set aside. This is what they look like inside.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="441" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/soft-boiled-egg/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/soft-boiled-egg" rel="attachment wp-att-5395">
          <img alt="Soft Boiled Egg" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5395" height="264" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Soft-Boiled-Egg-600x264.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Warm the
         <strong>
          teaspoon of olive oil
         </strong>
         in a small frying pan and add in the
         <strong>
          diced red onion
         </strong>
         . Cook over a medium-high heat until the onions have caramelized and begun to just char a bit. Toss them with a
         <strong>
          pinch of salt
         </strong>
         and set aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="610" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/caramelized-onions-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/caramelized-onions-2" rel="attachment wp-att-5387">
          <img alt="Caramelized Onions" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5387" height="366" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Caramelized-Onions1-600x366.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         5. In a large mixing bowl combine the
         <strong>
          2 1/2 cups flour
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          teaspoon salt
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          1/4 teaspoon ground pepper
         </strong>
         ,
         <strong>
          tablespoon baking powder
         </strong>
         , and
         <strong>
          1/2 teaspoon baking soda
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="620" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/muffin-mix/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/muffin-mix" rel="attachment wp-att-5393">
          <img alt="Muffin Mix" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5393" height="372" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Muffin-Mix-600x372.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         6. In a small saucepan, warm the
         <strong>
          1/2 cup butter
         </strong>
         until just melted. Add in the
         <strong>
          1 1/4 cup of buttermilk
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="626" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/butter-buttermilk/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/butter-buttermilk" rel="attachment wp-att-5385">
          <img alt="Butter Buttermilk" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5385" height="375" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Butter-Buttermilk-600x375.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         7. In a small mixing bowl, lightly whisk
         <strong>
          2 eggs
         </strong>
         together and then mix in the butter-buttermilk mixture.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="563" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/buttermilk-muffin/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/buttermilk-muffin" rel="attachment wp-att-5386">
          <img alt="Buttermilk Muffin" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5386" height="338" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Buttermilk-Muffin-600x338.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Pour the liquid mixture with the dry mix, stirring until the ingredients are mostly combined. Dont over mix! Fold in the caramelized onions,
         <strong>
          minced thyme leaves
         </strong>
         , and
         <strong>
          shredded cheese
         </strong>
         , setting some of the cheese aside for topping.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/onion-cheese-muffin/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/onion-cheese-muffin" rel="attachment wp-att-5394">
          <img alt="Onion Cheese Muffin" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5394" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Onion-Cheese-Muffin-600x399.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         9. Pre-heat the oven to 400F and place the baking rack in the middle of the oven. Grease the inside of muffin tin, just six of them.
        </p>
        <p>
         10. Place a heaping spoonful of the muffin batter into each greased space. Place an egg in the batter, pointy side up, wiggling it gently to set it. Then pipe the batter around each egg in a spiral pattern, completely covering the egg. Add more to the top so that if the batter slides at all, the egg is not exposed while cooking.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="655" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/egg-baked-inside-muffin/egg-muffins/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/egg-muffins" rel="attachment wp-att-5391">
          <img alt="Egg Muffins" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-5391" height="393" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Egg-Muffins-600x393.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         11. Cover the muffins with the remaining cheese and bake them in a preheated oven for 17 minutes at 400 degrees F, rotating halfway through the baking time. The crust should be firm and golden brown when done. Remove them from the oven and let cool for 10 minutes before serving.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy!
        </p>

         </a>
        </p>
