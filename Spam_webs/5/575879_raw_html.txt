        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Anyone else lay in bed at night unable to fall asleep because you cant stopplanning home improvement projects?? Or am I just crazy?Im pretty sure itsa very normal side effect of home ownership and maybethe factthat I pinobsessively to
         <a href="Anyone%20else lay in bed every night planning all the home improvement projects they want to do? Or am I the only crazy one?! I'm pretty sure this is a natural side effect of home ownership... and probably the fact that I scroll through Pinterest every night before falling asleep. I've developed quite a healthy obsession with pinning home decor inspiration to this board." target="_blank">
          this board
         </a>
         every night.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         After looking atdozens and dozens of beautiful rooms with artfully placed items and perfectly coordinated colors,I start to feel super confident that I, too, can turn every room in our house into something worthy ofanyrespected home decor magazine!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         and then I walk into any one of the handful of bare, blank rooms in the ol homestead to plan a design and dun dun dunnothing. Notta. I have literally no idea what to do or where to start. All of the inspiration and creativity goes right out the door. I become a voidless pit of non-knowledge. Designing a room from scratch feels likean insurmountable task. Dont you agree?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         I
         <em>
          finnallllly
         </em>
         finished decorating oneroom in our house (almost) completely last week and stumbled on some tips and tricks along the way. Do you remember this embarrassment I posted in
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/home-tour-part-two/" target="_blank" title="Home Tour: Take Two!">
          part two of my home tour
         </a>
         ?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_0429.jpg">
          <img alt="DSC_0429" class="aligncenter wp-image-613 size-large" height="440" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/DSC_0429-660x440.jpg" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Well, heres how it looks today!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Room-Update-After.jpg">
          <img alt="Guest Room Update After" class="aligncenter wp-image-614 size-large" height="440" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Room-Update-After-660x440.jpg" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         I still want to paint the walls a light grey and add some wainscoting on the lower half, but I at least feel like its presentable to guests and when I walk in I dont gruntat the thought ofall the projects that still remain (like I do in every other room). It takes me a loooong time to commit to a design theme
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         So on tomy first tip for designing a room from scratch!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span id="more-589">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Find a colorful piece that will inspire the rest of your design.
         </strong>
         For me, it was theselovely little ikat pillows from Target. It was love at first sight!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Close-Up.jpg">
          <img alt="Guest Bedroom Close Up" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-603" height="696" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Close-Up.jpg" width="464"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Theres just something about a good ikat print that makes my world seem right. Ahhhhh. :) Maybeyoull choose alovely patterned rug, or some swanky drapes or your fav piece of art. Once you have it, your world opens up! Now you have a basis to inspire the rest of your rooms design!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         But dont go hog wildlike I did on my first try
         <em>
          Bright red planter pot?
         </em>
         Yes!
         <em>
          Metallic blue curtains?
         </em>
         Dont mind if I do!
         <em>
          Bright yellow accent pillow?
         </em>
         Nailed it!But not really. It was a haphazard mess of circus colors in the end. Not pretty.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         So that brings us to tip #2:
         <strong>
          Pick a couple of neutral colors and one more playful accessory color.
         </strong>
         Thats if you like things fairly simple. If youre a little more brave with color then by all means, grab a second accessory color! I threw in just a bit of turquoise (a second accessory color) in this corner.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Love.jpg">
          <img alt="Guest Bedroom Love" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-597" height="597" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Love.jpg" width="398"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         But mostly I stuckwith a gray and white (neutral colors) and mustard yellow (main accessory color) combo, which to my surprise have somehow become my favorite colors to design with right now. So weird. Never thought mustard yellow and I would have such a tight bond in my adult years. In my younger years, I wouldnt have been caught dead near mustard yellow. Always casting such a ghastly glow on my skin. And even the name is unappealing!
         <em>
          Mustard yellow.
         </em>
         Bleh.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         But here I am! Just loving the crap out of it for some inexplicable reason.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Annywayyyys Take that color youre so adoringly smitten with and balance it out with some neutrals: white, beige, gray or black. Thatll help your design look grounded and cohesive.
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Update-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Guest Bedroom Update 2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-604" height="695" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Update-2.jpg" width="464"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Now to tip #3:
         <strong>
          Dress up the room with statement pieces.
         </strong>
         <em>
         </em>
         I fell hard forthis grey tufted headboard. It added the feminine sophistication I love in a bedroom and loopedin one of my neutral colors.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Update.jpg">
          <img alt="Guest Bedroom Update" class="wp-image-590 aligncenter" height="696" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Update-400x600.jpg" width="464"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         The room was still lacking a little flair and I wanted to add something that would have my guests eyes travel up, so I incorporated this sweet chandy I had stowed away in the closet.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Chandy-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Chandy 2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-610" height="696" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Chandy-2.jpg" width="464"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         I meant to use it at thesweetheart table at our wedding, but alas, it didnt make the cut. Luckily its finally finding a use! Plus, dont let these pics fool you, this room is in the basement and only gets a tiny stream of light so the blingincrystals will help add some more!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Chandy-Up-Close.jpg">
          <img alt="Chandy Up Close" class="aligncenter wp-image-595 size-large" height="440" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Chandy-Up-Close-660x440.jpg" width="660"/>
         </a>
         <br class=""/>
         One morehelpful tip that I truly only stumbled on while designing this room:
         <strong>
          Usethe same colors in multiple rooms.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         It not only ties your whole house together but will create happy accidents! This room only found its final personality afterI switched out the curtains. Yep, it only took onepair of curtains for the design to finally make sense. I was actually looking for drapes to tie our entryway into the yellow Im using in the living room. Found those babiesfor a steal at Home Goods! They looked great in the hall, but thenI got to thinking whatwould they look like with the mustard yellow pillow in the bedroom (
         <em>
          This is a dangerous thought that happens often and usually leads to many items getting switched around all the rooms in our house. Poor hubs never knows what to expect when he walks through the door.
         </em>
         )
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         And voila! The curtains looked even more spectacular in the guest bedroom! Andfor the first time in
         <del>
          two years
         </del>
         awhile, I wasable to stand in the room without grunting. Sweet relief.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-3.jpg">
          <img alt="Guest Bedroom 3" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-605" height="600" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-3-400x600.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         So, back to ourdesigning from scratchtip. The real magic started happening after the curtains went up. All of a sudden the design took shape and I could imagine all the other statement pieces and furniture the room needed and since Im also using the same colors in a lot of other rooms, I was able to pull it all together right then and there.  FOR FREE! Anightstand from another guest room, a chandy from the closet, some art from the guest bathroom and some accessories from the living room. All for free and all by accident.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         And thats whats so great about decorating multiple rooms in similar colors, it costs nothing to update a room! You can literally shop other rooms in your own home. Thats endless possibilities for rearranging when you get bored! Which is kind of an annoying vice I have to the dismay of the hubs
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Common home color scheme = shopping your own rooms for free = less money spent = happy husband.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Sothat final designing from scratch tip might just save a marriage, people.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Now letsend today with a juicy before and after pic!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Before-and-After-FINAL.jpg">
          <img alt="Guest Bedroom Before and After FINAL" class="aligncenter wp-image-623 size-large" height="943" src="http://www.pintucksandpeonies.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Guest-Bedroom-Before-and-After-FINAL-660x943.jpg" width="660"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         So, have you been stuck in a rut with design? Which tip will help you out the most? Id love to see some of your pics!
        </p>
