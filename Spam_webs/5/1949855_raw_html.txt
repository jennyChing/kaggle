        <p style="text-align: center;">
         This modestly scaled vacation retreat designed by
         <strong>
          <a class="external" href="http://www.claessonkoivistorune.se/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Claesson Koivisto Rune
          </a>
         </strong>
         is located in the Stockholm archipelago, for a young family. Only 8 meters uphill is a neighboring house, leading to the decision for a completely closed facade in that direction for privacy. However, all the other facades facing the waterline just 4 meters away are open and invite wonderful views across the Baltic Sea. The facade of the building is composed entirely of locally sourced spruce wood.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         The 484 square foot (45 square meters) house follows the topography and folds slightly in plan where a little ravine runs perpendicular to the house. This fold made the hybrid roof possible. The living room/kitchen wing, which has the highest ceiling height, has a normal single-pitch roof whose peak is continued in plan over the longer bedroom wing. This helps to form a pitched roof with its peak running-somewhat unusually-diagonally across.
        </p>
