       <h1>
        <a href="http://trailandtarmac.com/18/5-best-post-ride-beers/">
         5 Best Post-Ride Beers
        </a>
       </h1>
       <div class="meta">
        <div class="date">
         <span class="day">
          27
         </span>
         Jan
         <br/>
         <strong>
          2014
         </strong>
        </div>
        <div class="metaRight">
         Posted in:
         <a href="http://trailandtarmac.com/category/bike-riding-lifestyle/" rel="category tag">
          Riding Life
         </a>
         <br/>
         By
         <a href="http://trailandtarmac.com/author/pdo/" rel="author" title="Posts by Paul">
          Paul
         </a>
         <img src="http://trailandtarmac.com/wp-content/themes/wpanniversary/images/ico_comments.png"/>
         <a href="http://trailandtarmac.com/18/5-best-post-ride-beers/#comments">
          1 Tweet
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        Finished a ride and looking for a brew? Try one of the top-5 listed below. You wont be disappointed.
       </p>
       <h2>
        5) Guinness
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/stormino/3884911660/">
         <img alt="IMG_0351" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-110" height="225" src="http://trailandtarmac.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/guinness-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Guinness is a meal in a pint glass, which makes it a great post-ride brew. Not too boozy. Not too heavy. Feeling famished
        <em>
         and
        </em>
        thirsty? Grab one of these. Or more.
       </p>
       <h2>
        4 ) PBR
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/nadya/2886278727/">
         <img alt="pbr-bike" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-112" height="233" src="http://trailandtarmac.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/pbr-bike1-300x233.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The single-speed riders of Portland, OR popularized this cheap beer. More flavorful per penny than most similarly-priced brews. Got a party brewing or a peloton coming over? Grab a 30-pack.
       </p>
       <h2>
        3)Kronenbourg 1664
       </h2>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/6410324585/">
         <img alt="french-beer" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-109" height="240" src="http://trailandtarmac.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/french-beer-300x240.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Guess what the riders of the Tour de France drink after a stage? If theyre drinking beer, theyre probably drinkingKronenbourg 1664, the most popular brew in France. Drink yours without HGH and EPO.
       </p>
       <h2>
        2) Utah 3.2
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_111" style="width: 310px">
        <a href="http://drivinginertia.com/2294/i-love-utah-state-liquor-stores/">
         <img alt="Utah Beer Warning. Danger!" class="size-medium wp-image-111" height="179" src="http://trailandtarmac.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/utah-beer-300x179.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Utah Beer Warning. Danger!
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Utah beer gets bad-mouthed for good reason  it is watery swill  but that makes it a near-perfect post-ride beer. Low in alcohol so it hydrates more than its boozier peers. Only available in Utah and bottled under various labels, they all taste basically the same. And after having the sun beat on you while riding the Slickrock Trail, they all taste perfect.
       </p>
       <h2>
        1) Fat Tire Amber Ale
       </h2>
       <p>
        New Belgium, the most widely-available bike-themed brewery makes great beer. Its wind-powered factory in Fort Collins, CO is worth the pilgrimage. Cracking one of these after a ride is near-bliss.
       </p>
       <p>
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Tzd-RLBmYGA" width="560">
        </iframe>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Got another beer pick? Leave a note in the comments.
        </em>
       </p>
