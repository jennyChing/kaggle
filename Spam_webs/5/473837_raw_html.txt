       <section>
        <p>
         War! You know what it
         <i>
          is
         </i>
         good for? Stories of unfathomable badassery, that's what. Over the years, we at Cracked have gathered a formidable collection of these stories, and we've put the very best of them here so that a whole new generation of readers can feel inadequate about their life choices. Read on and you'll learn all the military history too pants-burstingly awesome to make it into your history books.
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #94. Commandos in Fake Boobs and Blackface
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/3/1/277531_v1.jpg" height="203" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         What if you had to disguise some commandos who were going to be walking
         <em>
          right past
         </em>
         enemy guards? You'd have to come up with something amazing -- lives are at stake here.
        </p>
        <p>
         Or, you could come up with something so stupid that the enemy finds it too awkward to make eye contact.
         <a href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/55655861/Blood-and-Rage-a-Cultural-History-of-Terrorism" target="C">
          Such was
         </a>
         the thinking of Israeli special forces commandos who
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Spring_of_Youth" target="C">
          infiltrated Beirut
         </a>
         in 1973 to kill three leaders of the PLO. To not arouse suspicion, they took several hulking special forces guys and dressed them up as women, complete with wigs, high heels and fake boobs.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/2/7/277527.jpg?v=1" height="286" width="232"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          "Uh, guys, the mission's been over for like a month ..."
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/jsource/History/opspring.html" target="C">
          Pairing up
         </a>
         with men dressed as men, they walked along in each other's arms like they were on dates. They walked right past police, bodyguards, etc. without raising any questions, such as why that woman had a five o'clock shadow and an Adam's apple, or what that lump was that looked like an Uzi hidden under their clothes.
        </p>
        <p>
         When they got to the Palestinian leaders' bedrooms, they kicked in the doors, whipped out their guns and killed everyone.
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh, and lest you think this was a bad career move, one of the "women" was Ehud Barak, who later became Prime Minister of Israel
         <a href="http://www.knesset.gov.il/mk/eng/mk_eng.asp?mk_individual_id_t=28" target="C">
          and is currently
         </a>
         Defense Minister.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/2/9/277529_v1.jpg" height="276" width="208"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 208px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Ehud_Barak_at_Pentagon,_11-2009.jpg" target="c">
           Robert D. Ward
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          We repeat: People died because they mistook this man for a woman.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Israelis hardly invented this technique, by the way. In 1943,
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Jaywick" target="C">
          11 Australian commandos
         </a>
         , all white,
         <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/history/ww2peopleswar/stories/90/a5536190.shtml" target="C">
          disguised themselves
         </a>
         as Malay fishermen by dyeing their skin brown and boarding a fishing boat. They sailed through 2,000 miles of Japanese-controlled ocean from Australia to Singapore.
         <a href="http://www.anzacday.org.au/history/ww2/anecdotes/1943.html" target="C">
          At one point they
         </a>
         even traveled right alongside a Japanese warship without them noticing anything strange (which was good, because none of the commandos could speak Malay). They then took canoes right into Singapore Harbor, where they blew up seven Japanese ships before escaping.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/3/5/277535_v1.jpg" height="208" width="321"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 321px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Krait_%28AWM_067338%29.jpg" target="c">
           Australian War Memorial
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          So racism is OK as long as you have plenty of bombs and canoes.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         But to top them all,
         <a href="http://archive.org/stream/nursespyinuniona00edmo#page/106/mode/2up" target="C">
          Sarah Edmonds
         </a>
         , a 21-year-old white woman working as a spy for the Union Army, infiltrated Confederate territory in Virginia disguised as a black man. Somehow, this totally worked, and she snagged the plans to a fort and the identities of some Confederate spies before "
         <a href="http://www.spartacus.schoolnet.co.uk/USACWthompsonF.htm" target="C">
          escaping
         </a>
         " back to Union lines.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/3/3/277533.jpg?v=1" height="346" width="234"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 234px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://archive.org/stream/nursespyinuniona00edmo#page/112/mode/2up" target="c">
           archive.org
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Yet neither of her identities were allowed to vote.
         </font>
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #93. Matt Hughes Curves the Bullet
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/3/9/277539_v1.jpg" height="203" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Welsh Royal Marine sniper Matt Hughes was participating in the 2003 invasion of Iraq, looking for a perfect occasion to shoot some dudes from really far away. He found it in two Iraqi troops who were holding up the offensive. Hughes was ordered to take them out. And not out to dinner, unless they both ordered a
         <em>
          lead steak
         </em>
         . A tiny one, shaped like a bullet.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/3/7/277537.jpg?v=1" height="220" width="320"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          "Sir, I understand you didn't like it, but you've already eaten most of it. I'll have to get a manager."
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          The Shot:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The problem was that the wind was blowing tremendously. See, this is something that doesn't come up in the movies -- when you're trying to shoot from far away with any kind of wind, you have
         <em>
          almost no goddamned idea where the bullet will end up
         </em>
         . Sniping isn't just holding the cross hairs steady on the tiny soldier in the scope; it's trying to predict gusts of wind that could push the bullet into some innocent tree trunk 50 feet away. And yes, that's how much of a difference wind can make. You can not only miss the guy, but miss the whole house he's standing in.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/4/1/277541_v1.jpg" height="320" width="263"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          So

         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/9/3/277593_v1.jpg" height="226" width="333"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          "Funny ... the way you're playing that sounds like a high-pitched scream."
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         With other soldiers frantically gesturing at him to find some cover and just really having a war all over the place, Millin walked slowly and bolt upright, playing "
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NMASHA8NTRI" target="c">
          Blue Bonnets Over the Border
         </a>
         ." Millin later talked to some of the Germans who had been captured to ask why they never shot him, and discovered it was because
         <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/military-obituaries/special-forces-obituaries/7952729/Piper-Bill-Millin.html" target="c">
          they thought he had gone mad
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/9/5/277595_v1.jpg" height="237" width="314"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          War is hell.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         And if anyone's harboring any ill thoughts toward Lord Lovat for basically risking his own man's life for what were ostensibly the most fuck-stupid reasons imaginable, it's probably important to note that Millin
         <a href="http://www.clement-jones.com/ps01/ps01_266.htm" target="c">
          played the pipes at the Lord's funeral
         </a>
         after his death in 1995. So clearly he was OK with the way things went. For some reason.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/5/9/9/277599_v1.jpg" height="211" width="312"/>
         <font size="1" style="color: #909090; display:block; margin-top: -10px; height: 5px; width: 312px; text-align: right;">
          <a href="http://www.dawlishnewspapers.co.uk/news.cfm?id=29113&amp;headline=Death%20of%20%27Piper" target="c">
           dawlishnewspapers.co.uk
          </a>
         </font>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          "It was the greatest damn gig I ever did."
         </font>
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #86. Pavlov's Platoon Holds Off the Nazis. All of Them.
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/0/3/277603_v1.jpg" height="203" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          The Half-Assed Hollywood Effort:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here's a story implausible enough it could only have come from the fantasy genre, specifically the Battle of Helm's Deep from
         <em>
          Lord of the Rings
         </em>
         . A bunch of under-equipped warriors find themselves holed up in a fortress, outnumbered 30 to one. Knowing that death is all but inevitable, they decide to fend off the vastly superior army for a miraculous stretch of time as a pure exercise in ball-flexing manliness, before being rescued by a wizard.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/0/1/277601_v1.jpg" height="241" width="262"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Also, the fat elf dies.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          Topped by Real Life When:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Imagine if Helm's Deep had only been defended by two dozen guys and the enemy crossed the sheer overwhelming math of a zombie horde with the Empire's propensity for terrifying marshal efficiency.
        </p>
        <p>
         That's what one Sergeant Yakov Pavlov's platoon found themselves facing down in September of 1942. The Nazis were pushing into Russia as part of the biggest military operation in the history of the human race, and everything was about to come to a head in the city of Stalingrad with a battle over a single bombed-out apartment building.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/0/5/277605_v1.jpg" height="203" width="312"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          They called it the "Battle of Stalingrad" because "The Battle of That Building Where Sergei's Mom Used to Live" didn't sound quite as impressive.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Pavlov and his platoon was tasked with the thankless job of retaking the building after the Nazis had seized it. To get a snapshot of what their mindset was like heading in, it's helpful to know that the assignment was considered an extremely dangerous one by the Soviet Army, and that the Soviet Army's slogan at the time was "die for Russia."
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/0/9/277609_v1.jpg" height="228" width="312"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          Somehow, the slogan failed to raise morale.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         Doing the quick math, Pavlov realized his only chance was to throw his whole platoon into the meat grinder, and hope that the speed with which they passed through left at least a few alive. He lost all but four men in the assault, but eventually his plan worked and they took the building. Had they known they were dealing with a man who considered four people surviving a success, the Nazis probably would have realized that they were in for some serious shit. Having barely enough survivors to outfit a respectable zombie movie, Pavlov could only station one soldier to each floor. However, the drop-dead gorgeous line of sight it offered was enough for them to unleash a mountain of unholy hell against all Fascist comers.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/0/7/277607_v1.jpg" height="262" width="184"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          The last face many Nazis ever saw.
         </font>
        </p>
        <p>
         The building was subjected to relentless fire--as were the civilians huddled in its basement--but Pavlov's unit held out long enough to be reinforced by a still-tiny 25 men. Not a wizard, but it was all they needed. His men were given machine guns, rifles, mortars, barbed-wire, anti-tank mines, some body armor and a PTRS-41 anti-tank rifle which Pavlov personally used to snipe
         <em>
          a dozen tanks
         </em>
         from the rooftop. They basically used what little equipment they had to convert the apartment into a goddamn anti-Nazi death machine that could annihilate whatever came at it from a kilometer in every direction.
        </p>
        <p>
         As long as everyone conserved their ammo and manned their posts, the only real danger posed to the building came from flamethrowers. Fortunately, with legendary snipers like 19-year-old
         <a href="http://books.google.com/books?id=0tBPlBbl_kYC&amp;pg=PA155&amp;dq=Anatoly+Chekhov&amp;cd=1" target="c">
          Anatoly Chekhov
         </a>
         on the top floor, this usually resulted in a Viking funeral for the Nazis.
        </p>
        <p>
         Wave after wave of the German army hammered the building. And died.
        </p>
        <p>
         Later, Pavlov's men could boast that they killed
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pavlov%27s_House" target="c">
          more Germans
         </a>
         defending their one building than the French killed in the entire fall of Paris. And unfortunately for French egos, they were still alive to boast--by February 2 the next year, the Battle of Stalingrad was over. Pavlov was named a Hero of the Soviet Union, and the building he defended was made into a monument.
        </p>
        <h2 class="subheading">
         #85. The Ministry of Ungentlemanly Warfare
        </h2>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/1/1/277611_v1.jpg" height="203" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          The Half-Assed Hollywood Effort:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Hopefully you didn't see the movie
         <em>
          The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen
         </em>
         but did read the comics, which feature a band of legendary fictional characters such as Captain Nemo, the Invisible Man and Dr. Jeckyll/Mr. Hyde, all coming together from separate fictional universes to save the world.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="Title">
          The Badass True Story:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         What if we told you that there was a secret military unit during World War II which featured this guy:
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/1/3/277613_v1.jpg" height="194" width="262"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         And this guy:
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/1/5/277615_v1.jpg" height="209" width="312"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         ... who operated out of Sherlock Holmes' headquarters and saved the world from nuclear annihilation at the hands of the Nazis?
        </p>
        <p>
         Meet the
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_Operations_Executive" target="c">
          Special Operations Executive
         </a>
         , a super-secret branch of the U.K. military personally tasked by Winston Churchill to "set Europe ablaze." On the crew were James Bond-creator Ian Fleming (who would base Bond on his own experiences), as well as members who would be Fleming's inspirations for M, Q, Miss Moneypenny and the sultry Vesper Lynd. They were joined by the future Dracula/Saruman/Dooku
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Lee#Involvement_in_World_War_II" target="a">
          Christopher Lee
         </a>
         . They were stationed at Baker Street. Yep, the place where the fictional Sherlock Holmes solved his mysteries.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/1/7/277617_v1.jpg" height="260" width="213"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         These "Baker Street Irregulars" were Churchill's go-to guys and girls for "ungentlemanly" warfare. If there was a bridge that needed
         <a href="http://books.google.com/books?id=g5c9AAAAIAAJ&amp;printsec=frontcover&amp;dq=Special+operations+executive&amp;cd=1" target="c">
          busting
         </a>
         or an Axis officer who needed seducing, you'd better believe the SOE had all the cloaks and daggers necessary to make sure Colonel Arschloch spent his last moments of World War II getting murdered in his bed anywhere from the English Channel to Southeast Asia.
        </p>
        <p>
         The ministry's greatest achievement, and perhaps the single
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norwegian_heavy_water_sabotage" target="c">
          finest
         </a>
         act of sabotage in all of World War II, was
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norwegian_heavy_water_sabotage" target="c">
          Operation Gunnerside
         </a>
         : a crossover between the Ministry of Ungentlemanly Warfare and their cousins in the Norwegian Resistance. Their mission: train a crack commando unit of former-Vikings to join SOE on a secret mission to destroy a heavy water plant in Norway before the Nazis could build an
         <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/8289847.stm" target="c">
          atomic bomb
         </a>
         with it. It's thanks to these unknown bastards of World War II that Hitler didn't have any nuclear-tipped V-2 rockets to turn the last months of the war into something akin to Judgment Day.
        </p>
        <p align="center">
         <img data-img="http://i.crackedcdn.com/phpimages/article/6/1/9/277619_v1.jpg" height="312" width="218"/>
         <br/>
         <font size="1">
          <i>
           If only.
          </i>
         </font>
        </p>
