          <p>
           For most of
           <a href="http://www.markevans.ca/clients">
            my clients
           </a>
           , the creation of clear and well-articulated messaging delivers a super-solid foundation for their marketing and sales activities.
          </p>
          <p>
           Messaging is the story that underpins the operations of startups and fast-growing businesses. It gives them clarity, coherence, and cohesiveness when dealing with customers, employees, partners, investors and the media. In other words, everything works off the same page.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img align="left" alt="Screen Shot 2015-05-20 at 3.08.10 PM" border="10" class="img vspace=" height="193" hspace="10" src="http://www.markevans.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Screen-Shot-2015-05-20-at-3.08.10-PM.png" width="288"/>
           My approach to messaging is agile, flexible and fluid. It is not an exercise that needs a lot of time to do. When a business is aggressively moving forward, messaging may need to change quickly to reflect new opportunities and challenges. Translation: messaging isnt something written in stone.
          </p>
          <p>
           This is why Im an advocate of
           <strong>
            Minimal Viable Messaging
           </strong>
           <strong>
            (MVM).
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           MVM is a fast-moving and structured process that gives startups and fast-growing companies what need right now.
          </p>
          <p>
           It is designed to easily embrace new ideas, products, features, opportunities and competitive threats. It gives companies a dynamic tool to remain competitive, vibrant and aligned with their customers and marketplace.
          </p>
          <p>
           One of MVMs key considerations is each iteration performs when it is required. MVM doesnt need to be perfect, but it needs to work well and reflect the needs and interests of target audiences.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            So how is MVM created? From a high level, this is how it works:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           1. Discovery: To kick things off, the key stakeholders come together to put everything on the table  the companys history, mission and vision, the customers being targeted, the product, marketplace, and competitors. Its a data dump that involves a lot of information and insight, good and bad ideas, and exploration. Its everything and anything that could play a role in the development of messaging.
          </p>
          <p>
           2. Information Analysis: All the information gathered during the discovery stage is synthesized and filtered. Youre looking to identify interesting ideas, concepts, and keywords  anything with the potential to become an integral part of messaging. It can be a challenging process, but it puts the spotlight on the most interesting and thought provoking content.
          </p>
          <p>
           3. Brainstorming: Once all the information has been scrutinized and reviewed, there will be a handful of ideas used as the building blocks for messaging. Getting together key players makes it easier for messaging to be quickly developed. At this stage, it helps to have third party perspective to have afresh set of eyes on the process. This reduces the risk of falling back on old ideas.
          </p>
          <p>
           4. Testing: Once messaging is developed, it needs to be tested. Were not talking about extensive testing, but testing that involves a variety of people with different needs and interests. You are looking for feedback that sticks out  words, phrases and concept that arent clear or deliver the wrong impression.
          </p>
          <p>
           5. Go live: Integrate the messaging into your value propositions, boilerplate, social media profiles and Website content.
          </p>
          <p>
           While MVM involves several stages, the process moves quickly and decisively. It gives companies what they need when they need it.
          </p>
          <p>
           And heres the thing: Messaging will need to evolve, probably sooner than later. Change is a fact of life, so messaging needs to stay current and relevant.
          </p>
          <p>
           This is the beauty of MVM; it is an agile and user-friendly tool that makes it easier to develop new (and sometimes improved) messaging.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            For fast-moving companies, MVM is a powerful tool to constantly connect with target audiences and outflank the competition.
           </strong>
          </p>

           If you want to learn more about how your company can embrace MVM,
