     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Tasmanian pademelon
      </b>
      (
      <i>
       Thylogale billardierii
      </i>
      ), also known as the
      <b>
       rufous-bellied pademelon
      </b>
      or
      <b>
       red-bellied pademelon
      </b>
      , is the sole
      <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">
       endemic
      </a>
      species of
      <a href="/wiki/Pademelon" title="Pademelon">
       pademelon
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Marsupial" title="Marsupial">
       marsupials
      </a>
      found in
      <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">
       Tasmania
      </a>
      , and formerly throughout south-eastern Australia. This pademelon has developed heavier and bushier fur than its northern relatives, who inhabit northern
      <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
       Australia
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">
       Papua New Guinea
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Males reach around 12 kg (26.5 lbs) in weight, 11.2 metres in length including the tail, and are considerably larger than the females, which average 3.9 kg (8.6 lbs).
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">
       Habitat
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_pademelon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Habitat">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tasmanian-pademelon-and-joey.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1845" data-file-width="1741" height="233" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Tasmanian-pademelon-and-joey.jpg/220px-Tasmanian-pademelon-and-joey.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Tasmanian-pademelon-and-joey.jpg/330px-Tasmanian-pademelon-and-joey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Tasmanian-pademelon-and-joey.jpg/440px-Tasmanian-pademelon-and-joey.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tasmanian-pademelon-and-joey.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Female and her
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joey_(marsupial)" title="Joey (marsupial)">
         joey
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Pademelons are solitary and nocturnal, spending the daylight hours in thick vegetation.
      <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">
       Rainforest
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sclerophyll" title="Sclerophyll">
       sclerophyll
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">
       forest
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scrubland" title="Scrubland">
       scrubland
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Menkhorst_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Menkhorst-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      are preferred, although wet gullies in dry open
      <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">
       eucalyptus
      </a>
      forest are also used. Such places, next to open areas where feeding can occur, are especially favoured. After dusk, the animals move onto open areas to feed, but rarely stray more than 100 metres from the forest edge.
     </p>
     <p>
      The species is abundant and widespread throughout Tasmania.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">
       Diet
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_pademelon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Diet">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Tasmanian pademelon is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nocturnal" title="Nocturnal">
       nocturnal
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Herbivore" title="Herbivore">
       herbivore
      </a>
      feeding on a wide variety of plants, from herbs, green shoots and grass, to some nectar-bearing flowers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Menkhorst_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Menkhorst-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Once a part of the diet of the
      <a href="/wiki/Thylacine" title="Thylacine">
       Thylacine
      </a>
      , the Tasmanian pademelon is still preyed upon by other predators of the island, including the
      <a href="/wiki/Tasmanian_devil" title="Tasmanian devil">
       Tasmanian devil
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Quoll" title="Quoll">
       quolls
      </a>
      . Even so, they are abundant to the point of being
      <a href="/wiki/Culling" title="Culling">
       culled
      </a>
      occasionally (along with other
      <a href="/wiki/Wallaby" title="Wallaby">
       wallabies
      </a>
      ) to reduce competition for grass with the farmed animals. Hunting of the Tasmanian pademelon is allowed, its pelt having some economic value and its meat being palatable.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">
       Breeding
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_pademelon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Breeding">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      There is no specific breeding season, though 70% of pademelon births seem to occur around the beginning of winter.
      <a href="/wiki/Gestation" title="Gestation">
       Gestation
      </a>
      for the female is 30 days. The young are in the pouch for about 6 months thereafter, and are weaned at around 8 months. Joeys are sexually mature at 1415 months. Pademelons live between 5 to 6 years in the wild.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
