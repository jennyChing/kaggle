          <div class="article-body__content">
           <p>
            Millennials are not budging from their parents' basements, even though the job market is on the mend.One really bigreason? Student loans.
           </p>
           <p>
            Last year, the rate of 25- to 34-year-olds living at home
            <a data-web-url="http://www.census.gov/hhes/families/data/adults.html" href="http://www.census.gov/hhes/families/data/adults.html" target="_blank">
             rose
            </a>
            to 17.7 percent amongmen and 11.7 percent for women, Census data showed last week. That is a record high for both genders.
           </p>
           <p>
            Rising co-residence rates are correlated more closely with student debt than with factors like economic conditions and the housing market, according to a
            <a href="http://www.newyorkfed.org/research/staff_reports/sr700.html" target="_blank">
             staff report
            </a>
            in Novemberfromthe Federal Reserve Bank of New York. The regional bank
            <a data-web-url="http://libertystreeteconomics.newyorkfed.org/2015/02/household-formation-within-the-boomerang-generation.html#.VNJZiWjF92Q" href="http://libertystreeteconomics.newyorkfed.org/2015/02/household-formation-within-the-boomerang-generation.html#.VNJZiWjF92Q" target="_blank" title="Read NY Fed's Blog Post">
             wrote
            </a>
            about the trend today in its blog called "Liberty Street Economics."
           </p>
           <div data-view-uid="1|0_5_1_5">
            <div class="ad-v2 on-mobile" data-position="box1" id="1|0_5_1_5">
             <script type="text/javascript">

           <p>
            "State-cohort groups who were more heavily reliant on student debt while in school are significantly and substantially more likely to move home to parents when living independently, and are significantly and substantially less likely to move away from parents when living at home," the report said.
           </p>
           <figure class="inline-image inline-media center ">
            <div class="inline-media__unlinked-image">
             <img alt="" data-attachment-key="208521454" itemprop="image" src="http://assets.bwbx.io/images/iz.VNZP.Lpgg/v1/-1x-1.jpg"/>
            </div>
            <figcaption class="inline-media__info">
             <div class="inline-media__caption">
              The increasing rate of parental co-residence among 25 year olds from 2002-03 to 2012-13
             </div>
             <div class="inline-media__credit">
              Source: Federal Reserve Bank of New York
             </div>
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            To make things even worse,a super-hothousing market is making it too expensive for even gainfully employed millennials to get their own place.
           </p>
           <p>
            "Strengthening youth labor markets support moves away from home, but rising local house prices send independent youth back to parents," the report said.
           </p>
           <figure class="inline-image inline-media center ">
            <div class="inline-media__unlinked-image">
             <img alt="" data-attachment-key="208524822" itemprop="image" src="http://assets.bwbx.io/images/imFA0PvOXy08/v1/-1x-1.jpg"/>
            </div>
            <figcaption class="inline-media__info">
             <div class="inline-media__credit">
              Source: Federal Reserve Bank of New York
             </div>
            </figcaption>
           </figure>

            The economy may be improving, but sorry, Mom and Dad: the "
