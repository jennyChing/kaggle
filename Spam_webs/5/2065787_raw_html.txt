          Thirsty Thursdays: How to make or order a Classic Martini
         </a>
        </h1>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         A Martini is one of those drinks that looks wonderfully sophisticated. So wonderfully sophisticated that I decided to order one at a cocktail bar once upon a time, so everyone could see how wonderfully sophisticated I was. Not sophisticated enough, however, to know what was actually in a (dry Gin) Martinineedless to say, I ordered a lemonade and stealthily poured the Martini in.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thankfully, our friends at
         <a href="http://ghergich.com/" target="_blank">
          Ghergich &amp; Co.
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.fix.com/blog/more-martinis-please/" target="_blank">
          FIX
         </a>
         have produced this useful infographic so that you dont have to go through the same trauma as I did, and you can even make your own Martini at home to check you like them before ordering them at Dirty Martini.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-428">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class=" aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.fix.com/assets/content/15600/more-martinis-please.jpg?w=500"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Where will you be drinking Martinis this evening? Let me know in the comments or on social with #ThirstyThursdays
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
