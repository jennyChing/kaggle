    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      A
      <b>
       cat lady
      </b>
      is a single woman who dotes upon her pet cat or cats. The term is usually considered
      <a href="/wiki/Pejorative" title="Pejorative">
       pejorative
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Forbes-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      though it is sometimes embraced.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timesunion.com_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-timesunion.com-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_and_association">
       Usage and association
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cat_lady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Usage and association">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Women who have cats have long been associated with the concept of
      <a href="/wiki/Spinster" title="Spinster">
       spinsterhood
      </a>
      . In more recent decades, the concept of a cat lady has been associated with "romance-challenged (often career-oriented) women".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Forbes-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A cat lady may also be an
      <a href="/wiki/Animal_hoarding" title="Animal hoarding">
       animal hoarder
      </a>
      who keeps large numbers of cats without having the ability to properly house or care for them.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      They may be ignorant about their situation.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cat_Ladies_documentary">
       <i>
        Cat Ladies
       </i>
       documentary
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cat_lady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cat Ladies documentary">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The 2009 documentary
      <i>
       Cat Ladies
      </i>
      tells the stories of four women whose lives have become dedicated to their cats. The film was directed by Christie Callan-Jones and produced by Chocolate Box Entertainment, originally for
      <a href="/wiki/TVOntario" title="TVOntario">
       TVOntario
      </a>
      . It was an official selection at the 2009 Hot Docs Festival, Silverdocs Festival, and San Francisco's DocFest.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Naftali Berrill, Ph.D., Director of the New York Center for Neuropsychology and Forensic Behavioral Science told AOL Health, "These may be people who have a very hard time expressing themselves to other people. They may find the human need for affection is met most easily through a relationship with a pet." This devotion can sometimes signal mental or emotional issues such as depression.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huso_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-huso-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Crazy_Cat_Lady_Syndrome">
       Crazy Cat Lady Syndrome
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cat_lady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Crazy Cat Lady Syndrome">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crazy_Cat_Lady_Syndrome" title="Crazy Cat Lady Syndrome">
       Crazy Cat Lady Syndrome
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Recent research indicates a link between the parasite
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Toxoplasma_gondii" title="Toxoplasma gondii">
        Toxoplasma gondii
       </a>
      </i>
      , which sexually reproduces exclusively in cats, and numerous psychiatric conditions, including OCD.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Atlantic_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Atlantic-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Compulsive_hoarding" title="Compulsive hoarding">
       compulsive hoarding
      </a>
      of cats, a symptom of obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD), has long been associated with "crazy cat ladies".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Mass media has drawn on this stereotype to coin the term
      <i>
       Crazy Cat Lady Syndrome
      </i>
      to refer to the association between
      <i>
       T. gondii
      </i>
      and psychiatric conditions.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Atlantic_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Atlantic-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Famous_cat_ladies_and_their_cats">
       Famous cat ladies and their cats
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cat_lady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Famous cat ladies and their cats">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Florence_Nightingale" title="Florence Nightingale">
        Florence Nightingale
       </a>
       had many cats named after famous public figures such as
       <a href="/wiki/William_Ewart_Gladstone" title="William Ewart Gladstone">
        Gladstone
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Otto_von_Bismarck" title="Otto von Bismarck">
        Bismarck
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         9
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edith_Ewing_Bouvier" title="Edith Ewing Bouvier">
        Edith "Big Edie" Ewing Bouvier Beale
       </a>
       and her daughter
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edith_Beale" title="Edith Beale">
        Edith "Little Edie" Bouvier Beale
       </a>
       had many cats living with them in their decrepit home
       <a href="/wiki/Grey_Gardens_(estate)" title="Grey Gardens (estate)">
        Grey Gardens
       </a>
       . Reportedly, some 30 cats lived in the house by the time
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Little_Edie" title="Little Edie">
        Little Edie
       </a>
       sold it in 1979.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         10
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Bertha Rand was Winnipeg's notorious Cat Lady, who for years battled her neighbours and city hall to save her dozens of cats. Even years after her death, she still holds a place in Canadian popular culture. Maureen Hunter's play
       <i>
        The Queen of Queen Street
       </i>
       is based on Rand's life.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         11
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Susan Ashworth in The Cat Lady computer game, who battled with depression and owned many cats.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">
       In popular culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cat_lady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In popular culture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Cat ladies in popular culture include:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Catwoman" title="Catwoman">
        Selina Kyle (Catwoman)
       </a>
       from
       <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">
        DC Comics
       </a>
       is a known cat lady.
      </li>
      <li>
       A key scene in the movie
       <a href="/wiki/A_Clockwork_Orange_(film)" title="A Clockwork Orange (film)">
        A Clockwork Orange
       </a>
       has the violent sociopath Alex DeLarge murdering a
       <a href="/wiki/Paranoia" title="Paranoia">
        paranoid
       </a>
       cat lady, for which he is convicted and sentenced to a prison term during which he undergoes behavioral training to become a vastly-different person.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Erin_Cardillo" title="Erin Cardillo">
        Erin Cardillo
       </a>
       's character of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emma_Tutweiller" title="Emma Tutweiller">
        Emma Tutweiller
       </a>
       in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Suite_Life_on_Deck" title="The Suite Life on Deck">
         The Suite Life on Deck
        </a>
       </i>
       has 30 cats in her cabin.
      </li>
      <li>
       In "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cats_in_the_Cradle_(CSI_episode)" title="Cats in the Cradle (CSI episode)">
        Cats in the Cradle
       </a>
       ", an episode of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">
         CSI: Crime Scene Investigation
        </a>
       </i>
       , a cat lady is revealed to have been murdered by a young girl, after the girl and her sister wanted a cat she possessed, which the lady was unwilling to part with as she considered it one of her children.
      </li>
      <li>
       In
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">
         The Simpsons
        </a>
       </i>
       , the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crazy_Cat_Lady" title="Crazy Cat Lady">
        Crazy Cat Lady
       </a>
       is a recurring character whose real name is Eleanor Abernathy.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Angela_Martin" title="Angela Martin">
        Angela Martin
       </a>
       is a character on
       <a href="/wiki/The_Office_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Office (U.S. TV series)">
        <i>
         The Office
        </i>
        (US)
       </a>
       who is a cat lady.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Dana_Carvey" title="Dana Carvey">
        Dana Carvey
       </a>
       played "Grandma Puggy", a widowed grandmother who had cats everywhere and their hair got on the guests. She was also mentioned in a
       <i>
        Wayne's World
       </i>
       sketch by Garth (also played by Carvey) as trick-or-treating at "some weird old lady who had about a gazillion cats and their hair got on my candy apple".
      </li>
      <li>
       In
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Lego_Movie" title="The Lego Movie">
         The Lego Movie
        </a>
       </i>
       , Mrs. Scratchen-Post is a cat lady minifigure who is one of Emmet Brickowski's neighbors.
      </li>
      <li>
       In the books
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Beloved_Dearly&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Beloved Dearly (page does not exist)">
         Beloved Dearly
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=I_Know_Who_Likes_You&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="I Know Who Likes You (page does not exist)">
         I Know Who Likes You
        </a>
       </i>
       by
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Doug_Cooney&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Doug Cooney (page does not exist)">
        Doug Cooney
       </a>
       , Catherine is a cat lady.
      </li>
      <li>
       In
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Codename:_Kids_Next_Door" title="Codename: Kids Next Door">
         Codename: Kids Next Door
        </a>
       </i>
       , the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Crazy_Old_Cat_Lady&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Crazy Old Cat Lady (page does not exist)">
        Crazy Old Cat Lady
       </a>
       is a villain who lives with thousands of black and white cats and possesses the power to control them.
      </li>
     </ul>
