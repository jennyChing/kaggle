        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Lemon Garlic Roasted Brussels Sprouts
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-02-26T06:00:18+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 26, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.acedarspoon.com/author/juliacedar/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Julia
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.acedarspoon.com/lemon-garlic-roasted-brussels-sprouts/#comments">
           14 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="4749521" data-link="http://www.acedarspoon.com/lemon-garlic-roasted-brussels-sprouts/" data-summary="" data-title="Lemon Garlic Roasted Brussels Sprouts">
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Lemon Garlic Roasted Brussels Sproutsare crispy and packed full of flavor from the garlic and lemon juice. This delicious side dish is even better with a little freshlygrated parmesan cheese.
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.acedarspoon.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_8104.jpg">
            <img alt="Garlic Lemon Roasted Brussels Sprouts" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-14251" height="3264" src="http://www.acedarspoon.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_8104.jpg" width="2448"/>
           </a>
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Lemon Garlic Roasted Brussels Sprouts
         </strong>
         are a game changer. Probably not for the every day person that likes brussels sprouts, but for Will they are.
        </p>
        <p>
         We had a break through two weeks ago at dinner. If you have a picky eater at home you know the feeling when your picky child decides out of the blue to try something new. Will was a great eater as a baby. If you put it on his plate he would eat it. As Willbecame an opinionated toddler he decided he didntwant to eat anythingmixed together.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.acedarspoon.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_8138.jpg">
          <img alt="Lemon Garlic Roasted Brussels Sprouts" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-14253" height="3264" src="http://www.acedarspoon.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_8138.jpg" width="2448"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         That means if you put macaroni and cheese in front of Will he wont eat it. Weird, right? A three year old that wont eat mac &amp; cheese?
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.acedarspoon.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         Pizza? Not a chance. A casserole? Nope! Anyways, one thing that makes me happy is that he eats almost all fruits and vegetables. Bu there are two vegetables Will wouldnt eat, one being spinach {which he secretlygets insmoothies} and brussels sprouts.
        </p>
        <p>
         Twoweeks ago those little Lemon Garlic Brussels Sprouts with a sprinkle of parmesan cheese were starring at Will from his plate just waiting to be eaten. And they won! Willsaid in an excited voice I am going to try my brussels sprouts.
        </p>
        <p>
         Mike and I quickly looked at each other in shock and quietly continued eating. To our amazement Will proceeded to eat his brussels sprouts and asked for seconds. In the house of a picky three year old I call that an accomplishment.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.acedarspoon.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_8137.jpg">
          <img alt="Garlic Lemon Roasted Brussels Sprouts" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-14252" height="2448" src="http://www.acedarspoon.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_8137.jpg" width="3264"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Moral of the story is that if you are persistent and patient with your picky eaters you might get these small break throughs here and there. And then maybe as time goes on you will have bigger break throughs and then one day your child might just eat everything you put on their plate.  Am I dreaming? Probably. But hey, my kid just ate brussels sprouts!
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" id="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="er-top-box">
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.acedarspoon.com/easyrecipe-print/14228-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
           Lemon Garlic Roasted Brussels Sprouts
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="er-recipe-details">
          <div class="er-half-width er-float-left">
           <p>
            Prep time:
            <span>
             <time datetime="PT5M" itemprop="prepTime">
              5 mins
             </time>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Cook time:
            <span>
             <time datetime="PT40M" itemprop="cookTime">
              40 mins
             </time>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            Total time:
            <span>
             <time datetime="PT45M" itemprop="totalTime">
              45 mins
             </time>
            </span>
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="er-half-width er-float-left" itemprop="nutrition" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/NutritionInformation">
           <p>
            Serves:
            <span>
             4-6
            </span>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="er-photo-details">
          <div class="er-photo-container">
           <img itemprop="image" src="http://www.acedarspoon.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/IMG_8104.jpg"/>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
           Lemon Garlic Roasted Brussels Sprouts are crispy and packed full of flavor from the garlic and lemon juice. This delicious side dish is serve warm with freshly grated parmesan cheese.
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 lb brussels sprouts, ends trimmed and yellow leaves removed, chopped in half
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 Tablespoons olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 garlic cloves, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Dash of pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup freshly grated parmesan cheese
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 400 degrees F.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cut off the brown ends of the brussels sprouts and pull off any yellow outer leaves. Mix the brussels sprouts in a large bowl with the olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, salt and pepper.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour them on a baking sheet and roast for 35 to 40 minutes, until crisp on the outside, stirring occasionally while roasting.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove from oven, sprinkle with a bit more salt and the parmesan and serve warm.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           If you like your parmesan melted on the brussels sprouts roast them for 3-5 more minutes or until the cheese melted.
          </div>
