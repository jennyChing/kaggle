         THE PETROVSKY POPSICLE
        </h1>

      <div class="entry-media">

      <div class="wrapper">
       <div class="single-box clearfix entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         <a href="http://ladyandpups.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/cherry-black-tea-popsicle-front.jpg">
          <img alt="cherry-black-tea-popsicle-front" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21380" height="614" src="http://ladyandpups.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/cherry-black-tea-popsicle-front.jpg" width="1000"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          DONT WE NEED OUR RELATIONSHIPS
          <br/>
          WITH A LITTLE BIT OF MILK
         </h2>
        </blockquote>
        <div class="ezcol ezcol-one-half">
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          I DONT know if this will completely sack my credentials (
          <a href="http://ladyandpups.com/2014/03/09/cheesy-chick-flick-popcorn-eng/" title="CHEESY CHICK-FLICK POPCORN">
           <em>
            AGAIN
           </em>
          </a>
          ) as an angry, opinionated love-cynic who just fell asleep when you mentioned the words my ex-boyfriends facebook. Im not even sure if this is a well-adviced public statement for anyone who isnt fabulously gay. But this is probably as important to know about me as acknowledging the fact that I eat canned sardines in tomato sauce over oatmeals, that I also have a decade-long, still on-going, life-threatening addiction for
          <em>
           Sex And The City
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The key point in what I just said, is
          <em>
           on-going
          </em>
          . Like,
          <em>
           seriously
          </em>
          . Youd think that no one can be addicted to something that no longer exists. Itd be like trying to smoke a cigarette butt, found underneath the bookshelf, thats accidentally dropped in diesel. You just cant smoke that shit anymore. Oh, but I
          <em>
           can
          </em>
          . Since its last season ending in 2004, I had been waiting and watching every season that re-runs in turnson HBO on-demand every months,
          <em>
           r
          </em>
          <em>
           epeatedly
          </em>
          , until 2008whenI left New York. Then I had to purchase the complete-seasons DVD to
          <em>
           continue
          </em>
          watching every single episodes that Ive
          <em>
           continuously
          </em>
          watched for however long
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Dont even get me started on the movies. I mean thesecond onegave me a bad tripbut where the fuck is my third?
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="ezcol ezcol-one-half ezcol-last">
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Think what you will about me its probably all true And as if what I just said wasnt lame and pathetic enough, allow me to kick it up a notch. Ive made myself
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          the Petrovsky popsicle.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Its an intensely black-tea-flavoured popsicle thats sweetened with cherry preserve which also give it a slight tang. I was told, by a character from a TV-show that ended 10 years ago, that its how the Russians sweeten their teas, and I never doubted it for a second. The idea stayed inside my head for all this time, until a week ago when I purchased my first set of popsicle molds, the Russians invaded. If youre into tea-flavoured everything, youll have the hots for these this summer. I did Petrovsky justice by powdering the tea leaves to emboldenthe flavour and adding an entire jar of sour cherry preserve with large chunks of cherries. These popsicles are cold, black and caffein-charged with hidden tang and sourness inside their hidden pockets like the character himself.But of course, mellowed out with whole and sweetened condensed milk.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Because dont we allneed our relationships with a little bit of milk. Even if its an icy one.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="ezcol-divider">
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="ezcol ezcol-one-quarter">
         <a href="http://ladyandpups.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/cherry-black-tea-popsicle16.jpg">
          <img alt="cherry-black-tea-popsicle16" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21348" height="400" src="http://ladyandpups.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/cherry-black-tea-popsicle16.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </div>

         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="ezcol-divider">
        </div>
        <hr/>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Makes: 8 ~ 9 popsicles
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The black tea leaves is powdered in spice-grinder to give the popsicle a very intense tea-flavour. Its a good trick for maximizing tea-flavour in any recipes (such as
         <a href="http://ladyandpups.com/2013/06/13/breakfast-milk-tea-honey-pound-cake-eng/" title="breakfast milk tea &amp; honey pound cake">
          this cake
         </a>
         ). If you dont have a spice grinder, you can sort of
         <a href="http://www.thekitchn.com/the-mason-jar-blender-trick-do-you-know-about-this-195182" target="_blank">
          fake one with this trick
         </a>
         . Or, you might have to use more tea leaves to brew a stronger base for this recipe. I used 6 tbsp of sweetened condensed milk for this batch of popsicle and found the sweetness on the mild side, making this a more refreshing popsicle than a creamy one. You can increase the sweetness and creaminess by adding more if you like.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         You can definitely use other types of fruit jams/preserves that you like, or trying to use up for this recipe.
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="ezcol ezcol-one-half">
         <a href="http://ladyandpups.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/cherry-black-tea-popsicle10.jpg">
          <img alt="cherry-black-tea-popsicle10" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21343" height="1050" src="http://ladyandpups.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/cherry-black-tea-popsicle10.jpg" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="ezcol ezcol-one-half ezcol-last">
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Ingredients:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul style="text-align: justify;">
          <li>
           4 tbsp (23 grams) of Assam black tea leaves
          </li>
          <li>
           2 1/2 cups (610 grams) of whole milk + another 1/2 cup (120 grams)
          </li>
          <li>
           3 tbsp of cornstarch
          </li>
          <li>
           1 cup (240 grams) of black cherry, or sour cherry preserves
          </li>
          <li>
           6 tbsp ~ 1/2 cup of sweetened condensed milk
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Powder the Assam black tea leaves in a spice grinder until finely ground. Mix the tea-powder into 2 1/2 cups of whole milk in a pot, and set over medium heat. Once the milk has come to a very gentle simmer (careful not to scorch the milk), turn off the heat immediately and let steep for 1 min, then strain the milk-tea through a
          <strong>
           fine sieve
          </strong>
          into another pot (to eliminate any large tea leaves). Whisk the cornstarchtogether with 1/2 cup of milk, then whisk itinto the milk-tea until its slightly thickened. Then whisk inblack cherry (or sour cherry) preserves and 6 tbsp of sweetened condensed milk until even. Add more sweetened condensed milk to adjust the sweetness if needed.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Divide the mixture into popsicle molds and freeze until hard. Enjoy.
         </p>
