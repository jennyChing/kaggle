       <strong>
        National Chocolate Covered Anything Day
       </strong>
       , . The Simpsons made its debut as a standalone show on Dec. 17, 1989, and along the way the yellow family has taken over pop culture. Which should make it easy to have a Simpsons night, if youre looking for something to do. Play Simpsons Monopoly, Clue or Uno, watch DVDs of the TV show or movie, serve Duff beer, or whatever else you can think of.
      </p>
      <p>
       2. The big movies opening this week are Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows, rated PG-13, starring Robert Downey Jr. and Jude Law; Alvin and the Chipmunks: Chip-Wrecked, rated G; and Carnage, R, starring Jodie Foster, Kate Winslet, Christoph Waltz and John C. Reilly.
      </p>
      <p>
       3. Friday is National Chocolate-Covered Anything Day, one of the best holidays of the year. Mmm, chocolate.  You can make hot chocolate or chocolate chip cookies, eat chocolate candy or ice cream, or celebrate any way youd like.
      </p>
      <p>
       4. Still dont have all your holiday shopping done? You might want to hit the stores this weekend. Check the newspaper for sales and coupons.
      </p>
