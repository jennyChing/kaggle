           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             9 Essential Facts About Atrial Fibrillation
            </h2>
            <p>
             You might have heard about atrial fibrillation, or afib, without knowing exactly what kind of heart condition it is. Atrial fibrillation is an irregular heartbeat rhythm that starts in the upper part of your
             <strong>
              <a href="/heart-disease/index.aspx">
               heart
              </a>
             </strong>
             . Normally your heart rhythm should be controlled by a structure called the sinoatrial (SA) node, located in the upper right part of your heart, known as the right atrium. The SA node generates an electrical impulse that spreads in a very organized way through the left and right atria, causing them to contract and push blood into the lower parts of the heart (the ventricles). If you have atrial fibrillation, instead of starting at the SA node, the electrical impulse starts up in many different areas at once and becomes very
             <em>
              dis
             </em>
             organized. This causes the atria to quiver, or fibrillate, in an uncontrolled way, and your heartbeat becomes rapid and weak.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Atrial Fibrillation Statistics
            </h2>
            <p>
             Atrial fibrillation is the most common of the serious types of irregular heartbeats, or arrhythmias. It affects about 2.7 million Americans, according to the U.S. Centers for Disease Control. The
             <strong>
              <a href="/heart-health/atrial-fibrillation-basics.aspx">
               risk of atrial fibrillation
              </a>
             </strong>
             increases with age, usually showing up after age 60, but about half the people with this condition are younger than 75. Men are more likely to have atrial fibrillation than women, and Caucasians are more likely to have it than African- or Hispanic-Americans.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Types of Atrial Fibrillation
            </h2>
            <p>
             There are two types of atrial fibrillation. If you have atrial fibrillation that comes and goes on its own, it is called paroxysmal
             <strong>
              <a href="/heart-health/atrial-fibrillation-basics.aspx">
               atrial fibrillation
              </a>
             </strong>
             . Paroxysmal atrial fibrillation can last for minutes or for days. If you have atrial fibrillation for more than seven days, it is called persistent or chronic atrial fibrillation. This type of atrial fibrillation usually goes away only with treatment.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Atrial Fibrillation Symptoms
            </h2>
            <p>
             You may have atrial fibrillation without obvious or even any symptoms. You may just feel tired or out of breath and not realize you have an irregular heartbeat. The most common
             <strong>
              <a href="/atrial-fibrillation/symptoms/">
               atrial fibrillation symptoms
              </a>
             </strong>
             include a pounding or fluttering feeling in your chest (sometimes referred to as
             <strong>
              <a href="/heart-health/questions-to-ask-your-doctor-about-palpitations.aspx">
               palpitations
              </a>
             </strong>
             ), a lack of energy, feeling lightheaded, pain in your chest, and difficulty catching your breath.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Atrial Fibrillation Versus Other Irregular Heartbeats
            </h2>
            <p>
             Having occasional premature extra heart beats is more common than atrial fibrillation. They also start in the upper part of the heart, but they usually cause no symptoms, go away quickly, and are not serious.Atrial tachycardia is a rapid heart rate that comes and goes suddenly. It usually is seen in younger people and is rarely serious.Irregular heartbeats can also start in the lower part of the heart, called the ventricles. Ventricular fibrillation is a dangerous irregular heartbeat that needs emergency treatment.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             The Dangers of Atrial Fibrillation
            </h2>
            <p>
             Most people can live well with atrial fibrillation symptoms, as long as they are treated.
             <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/quizzes/afib-do-you-know-how-to-reduce-your-risk-of-complications.aspx">
              Atrial fibrillation could be dangerous
             </a>
             if it comes on while driving. It can also cause fainting, which could result in a fall. The biggest danger is from blood clots. A
             <strong>
              blood clot
             </strong>
             can form in your heart because blood pools there when your heart is fibrillating. If a blood clot travels to your brain, it can cause a stroke.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Complications of Atrial Fibrillation
            </h2>
            <p>
             Stroke is the most dangerous
             <a href="/atrial-fibrillation/complications/">
              <strong>
               complication of atrial fibrillation
              </strong>
             </a>
             . For someone with atrial fibrillation, the risk for stroke is about six times greater than the risk for a person without atrial fibrillation. Atrial fibrillation also weakens your heart over time. As your heart weakens, fluid can accumulate in the lower parts of your body and in your lungs, a condition called
             <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/heart-health/bad-news-if-your-bmi-is-high-fat-causes-heart-failure-2345.aspx">
              congestive heart failure.
             </a>
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Atrial Fibrillation Treatment
            </h2>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <a href="/atrial-fibrillation/treatment/">
               Atrial fibrillation treatments
              </a>
             </strong>
             include
             <strong>
              cardioversion
             </strong>
             , which is getting your heart back into a normal rhythm. This can be done with medications or with an electric shock. Medications may simply slow down your heart rate without actually changing your heart beat back to normal, or they may help keep your heart from fibrillating. It is also important to take medications that prevent blood clots. A procedure called catheter ablation may be used to give you long-term relief if you do not respond well to medications.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
         </li>

           <span class="slide-copy">
            <h2 itemprop="name">
             Atrial Fibrillation Safeguards
            </h2>
            <p>
             In many cases, there is no obvious
             <a href="/atrial-fibrillation/causes/">
              <strong>
               cause of atrial fibrillation
              </strong>
             </a>
             . Although there is no way to prevent atrial fibrillation, you can decrease your
             <strong>
              <a href="/heart-health/7-reasons-you-may-be-at-risk-for-atrial-fibrillation.aspx">
               afib risk
              </a>
             </strong>
             by avoiding some of the common factors that may contribute to arrhythmia. Avoid too much coffee and alcohol, keep your blood pressure and your cholesterol levels under control, dont smoking, maintain a healthy weight, and see your doctor regularly.
            </p>
           </span>
          </span>
