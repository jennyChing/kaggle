                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Lemon Mint Ros Cocktail
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2396279107097773098">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-koTUIwvs3zU/VDe6JFENZsI/AAAAAAAAc90/GKbi-SdcahA/s1600/IMG_2140-21.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-koTUIwvs3zU/VDe6JFENZsI/AAAAAAAAc90/GKbi-SdcahA/s1600/IMG_2140-21.jpg" width="454"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <b>
                      <i>
                       <br/>
                      </i>
                     </b>
                     <b>
                      <i>
                       A dry ros wine fortified with vodka and some muddled mint, lemon and sugar makes a beautiful cocktail, extending the drinkability of ros into the autumn months for ros summer-only purists.
                      </i>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Pink wine snob reversal
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     Until I started dating my future husband, I knew very little about wine. I dont remember either of my parents drinking wine, except for a phase when my mom would buy Cold Duck  a sort of sweet pink sparkling wine made by mixing red wine with bubbly - or Mateus, a Portuguese labeled ros that, even as a child, I understood was less than stellar in the wine category, the fancy bottle notwithstanding. As I reached an age where I could order wine in a bar, I always went for a crisp dry white, with the theory that a cheap white was usually more palatable than a cheap red, and wine-by-the-glass back then was invariably of the cheap variety. When we moved to Paris, I was shocked to find that the good-wine obsessed French drank pink wine during the hot summer months! And that it could be dry and not sweet. I became a fan of the pink.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     As the summer days are waning and the autumn is coming on, unused bottles of summer ros can be transformed into cocktails, extending their welcome refreshment into the next season.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Ingredients
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     Several fresh mint leaves
                     <br/>
                     1 lemon wedge
                     <br/>
                     1 oz or 30ml vodka
                     <br/>
                     1/2 cup or 120ml ros
                     <br/>
                     1/2  1 teaspoon sugar (or more, depending on your taste for sweet drinks)
                     <br/>
                     Ice
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Optional garnish: sprig of mint
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Method
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     Place mint, lemon and sugar in a glass and muddle well.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UXb-0JRPAYs/VDe6Jd-Z77I/AAAAAAAAc94/P9_FhZdR8fo/s1600/IMG_2142-23.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="426" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UXb-0JRPAYs/VDe6Jd-Z77I/AAAAAAAAc94/P9_FhZdR8fo/s1600/IMG_2142-23.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Add several cubes of ice to your glass and then pour in the vodka.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-fPG0J0CaBkc/VDe6G_jfwqI/AAAAAAAAc9Y/oKQhapYzos4/s1600/IMG_2127-8.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-fPG0J0CaBkc/VDe6G_jfwqI/AAAAAAAAc9Y/oKQhapYzos4/s1600/IMG_2127-8.jpg" width="426"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Add the ros and stir. Garnish with sprig of mint, if desired.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_veAtWh-2TI/VDe6Hl_VWVI/AAAAAAAAc9g/O2_i31PRPs0/s1600/IMG_2131-12.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-_veAtWh-2TI/VDe6Hl_VWVI/AAAAAAAAc9g/O2_i31PRPs0/s1600/IMG_2131-12.jpg" width="466"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Enjoy!
                     <br/>
