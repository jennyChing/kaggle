      <h1>
       A very unique bathroom
      </h1>
      <p class="blog-author">
       Posted  by
       <strong>
        Will Ryles
       </strong>
       on
       <strong>
        03/04/2013
       </strong>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       A very unique bathroom
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       If you're looking for a unique bathroom, or just want a little bit of inspiration then this little gem is for you.
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       Designed by Peace Design, it's a totally bespoke project that includes a rather gorgeous raw steel counter top and bowl.
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       While the stand out feature of the room has to be the numerous licence plates that adorn the walls, the designers still managed to complement such an outlandish (yet brilliant) statement with a vanity unit, mirror and even bathroom lighting that brought the design back down to earth and finished off the room in style.
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       Combining the raw steel counter with a very country looking worn oak vanity unit and an old school light bulb reinforces the classic feel of this unique bathroom design; preventing it from becoming an automobile focused and rather garish stunt.
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       Pair that fantastic vanity unit and bulb with a unique bathroom mirror that looks more like it's suited to a country bar than a bathroom and you begin to get the feeling this very unique bathroom could be straight out of route 66.
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       If you're wondering, the plates on the walls are held on by simple nails, while the steel counter top was fabricated in a local workshop; if only we had such talented craftsmen in our local area!
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       Of course, this stunningly unique bathroom won't work in your average house, but that doesn't mean there isn't something we can learn from it.
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       If anything it's a simple example of how being individual with our bathroom designs is a must; even just being creative with a bathroom mirror can lift a design.
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       By banishing the established beige norm and producing a unique bathroom design that's at the very least tailored to your own needs and not just off the shelf, you might just achieve something special!
      </div>
      <div style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">
       While licence plates might not be right for your bespoke bathroom, if you want to try something a little bit different, why not consider some wooden bathroom wall panels?
      </div>
      <div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <img alt="A Unique Bathroom" id="blog_image" src="http://www.dovcorbathrooms.co.uk/uploads/assets/large/Auniquebathroom.jpg" style="vertical-align: middle;" title="A Unique Bathroom" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       If you're looking for a unique bathroom, or just want a little bit of inspiration then this little gem is for you.
      </p>
      <p>
       Designed by
       <a href="http://www.peacedesign.org/flash/home.html" title="Peace Design">
        Peace Design
       </a>
       , it's a totally bespoke project that includes a rather gorgeous raw steel counter top and bowl.
      </p>
      <p>
       While the stand out feature of the room has to be the numerous licence plates that adorn the walls, the designers still managed to complement such an outlandish (yet brilliant) statement with a vanity unit, mirror and even bathroom lighting that brought the design back down to earth and finished off the room in style.
      </p>
      <p>
       Combining the raw steel counter with a very country looking worn oak vanity unit and an old school light bulb reinforces the classic feel of this unique bathroom design; preventing it from becoming an automobile focused and rather garish stunt.
      </p>
      <p>
       Pair that fantastic vanity unit and bulb with a unique bathroom mirror that looks more like it's suited to a country bar than a bathroom and you begin to get the feeling this very unique bathroom could be straight out of route 66.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you're wondering, the plates on the walls are held on by simple nails, while the steel counter top was fabricated in a local workshop; if only we had such talented craftsmen in our local area!
      </p>
      <p>
       Of course, this stunningly unique bathroom won't work in your average house, but that doesn't mean there isn't something we can learn from it.
      </p>
      <p>
       If anything it's a simple example of how being individual with our bathroom designs is a must; even
       <a href="http://www.dovcorbathrooms.co.uk/blog/view/get-creative-with-your-bathroom-mirror" title="Be creative with your bathroom mirror.">
        just being creative with a bathroom mirror
       </a>
       can lift a design.
      </p>
      <p>
       By banishing the established beige norm and producing a unique bathroom design that's at the very least tailored to your own needs and not just off the shelf, you might just achieve something special!
      </p>
      <p>
       While licence plates might not be right for your bespoke bathroom, if you want to try something a little bit different, why not consider some wooden
       <a href="http://www.dovcorbathrooms.co.uk/blog/view/are-bathroom-wall-panels-the-future" title="Bathroom Wall Panels">
        bathroom wall panels
       </a>
       ?
      </p>
