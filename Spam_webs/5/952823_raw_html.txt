      <div class="text">
       <p>
        <img alt="6 Essential Pantry Ingredients and 3 Surprising Ways to Use Them" src="http://foodnetwork.sndimg.com/content/dam/images/food/fullset/2015/3/20/0/fnd_foodlets-6-pantry-ingredients.jpg.rend.snigalleryslide.jpeg"/>
        As the mom of four kids under age 6, Ive found the secret to stocking a useful pantry: Every item needs to be versatile. From breakfast to lunch and snacks through dinner, my favorite pantry items can be used over and over again in totally different ways.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Old-Fashioned (Whole) Oats:
        </strong>
        I buy the generic store brand. While we make our share of oatmeal in the morning  three minutes in the microwave, then drenched in whole milk and sprinkled with chia seeds and dried fruit  theres so much more!
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Breakfast:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2015/03/17/banana-oat-cups-with-peanut-butter-and-jam/" target="_blank">
         Banana Oat Cup Muffins with Peanut Butter and Jam
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Snack:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2014/01/09/2-ingredient-banana-cookies-and-neither-one-is-sugar/" target="_blank">
         2-Ingredient Cookies
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Dinner:
        <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/ellie-krieger/new-classic-meatloaf-recipe.html" target="_blank">
         New Classic Meatloaf
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-86300">
        </span>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Raisins:
        </strong>
        Sure, theyre great as a snack straight up, but raisins are so good in so many other kid-friendly ways, like these:
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Snack:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2015/02/28/no-bake-chocolate-fruit-nut-bars/%20" target="_blank">
         No-Bake Chocolate Fruit and Nut Bars
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Lunch:
        <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/geoffrey-zakarian/gingered-coleslaw-with-golden-raisins-recipe.html" target="_blank">
         Gingered Coleslaw withGolden Raisins
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Dinner:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2012/07/30/sausage-spinach-with-raisins-pine-nuts-rice/" target="_blank">
         Sausage and Spinach with Raisins Over Rice
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Honey:
        </strong>
        Consider honey for everything from a drizzle on top of plain yogurt to start the day to dinners secret ingredient. It is stocked in my pantry always.
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Breakfast or Snack:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2013/08/16/no-sugar-blueberry-muffins-sweetened-with-honey/" target="_blank">
         Blueberry Muffins Sweetened with Only Honey
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Dinner:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2012/04/19/chicken-vegetable-stir-fry-with-noodles/" target="_blank">
         Chicken and Vegetable Stir-Fry with Noodles
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Dinner:
        <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/food-network-kitchens/apple-honey-drumsticks-recipe.html" target="_blank">
         Apple Honey Drumsticks
        </a>
        from Food Network Magazine
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Potatoes:
        </strong>
        Whether theyre russets, Yukon golds or babies, potatoes can become almost anything, anytime of day.
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Breakfast: Giada De Laurentiis
        <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/giada-de-laurentiis/salami-bacon-and-spinach-hash-recipe.html" target="_blank">
         Salami, Bacon and Spinach Hash
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Brunch or Dinner: Ina Gartens
        <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/ina-garten/potato-latkes-recipe.html" target="_blank">
         Potato Latkes
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Lunch or Dinner:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2014/10/02/slow-cooker-potato-soup-fully-loaded/%20" target="_blank">
         Slow-Cooker Potato Soup with Toppings
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Quinoa:
        </strong>
        The beauty of this seedis how fast it cooks, not to mention howthat built-in pop of protein ensuresa hearty dinner, even if you forgot to thaw the chicken. Its an excellent base for baby food and works in tons of other family favorites:
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Breakfast or snack:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2015/01/13/diy-kind-bars/" target="_blank">
         DIY FruitBars with Quinoa
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Dinner: Guy Fieris
        <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/guy-fieri/turkey-and-quinoa-stuffed-peppers-recipe.html" target="_blank">
         Turkey-and-Quinoa-Stuffed Peppers
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Dinner:
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2013/01/31/roasted-sweet-potatoes-and-chicken-with-quinoa-in-mustard-sauce/" target="_blank">
         Roasted Sweet Potatoes and Chicken with Quinoa in Mustard Sauce
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Marinara Sauce:
        </strong>
        Yes, marinara sauce is typically limited to dinner, but, boy, are the options wide open from there. A few of our kids favorites:
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        <a href="http://foodlets.com/2014/02/04/baked-pasta-with-spaghetti-squash/" target="_blank">
         Baked Pasta with Spaghetti Squash
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        The Pioneer Womans
        <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/ree-drummond/easy-calzones-recipe.html" target="_blank">
         Easy Calzones
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 30px">
        Giadas
        <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/giada-de-laurentiis/eggs-in-purgatory-recipe.html%20" target="_blank">
         Eggs in Purgatory
        </a>
       </p>
