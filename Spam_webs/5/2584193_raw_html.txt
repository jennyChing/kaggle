                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Lovely appliqu cardigan tutorial
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-9039317727115325122" itemprop="articleBody">
                     I thought it was time for another tutorial! Today I want to show you a cute hand-sewing technique you can do to embellish a cardigan with a pretty look for summer. I was inspired by this lovely thing from Anthropologie:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ0HIycoHI/AAAAAAAADRk/kq-d-oq12E4/s1600/010091_030_b.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ0HIycoHI/AAAAAAAADRk/kq-d-oq12E4/s400/010091_030_b.jpg" width="427"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ0LW110rI/AAAAAAAADRs/BNmT4V32cEE/s1600/010091_030_e+cattleya+cardi.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ0LW110rI/AAAAAAAADRs/BNmT4V32cEE/s400/010091_030_e+cattleya+cardi.jpg" width="427"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I don't love the sweater itself all that much, but the flowers on the side are just a piece of cotton hand-sewn invisibly down--I like that idea! Easy and pretty. So, I tried it myself--here's the tutorial!
                     <br/>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       Take a plain sweater, preferably cotton, that needs a little something extra to make it interesting! This is an old one from Target.
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ0lhowS7I/AAAAAAAADSE/HpDSj5PBygw/s1600/S7300960.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="640" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ0lhowS7I/AAAAAAAADSE/HpDSj5PBygw/s400/S7300960.JPG" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       Use a fabric that goes well in color and style. The pinks in the print are close to the pink in the sweater, and it's a fresh summery print for the lightweight cotton sweater. I have this darling vintage cotton print that I got from somewhere ages ago, probably Goodwill Outlet but I don't remember. The apple and cherry print cracks me up--pretty sure they're not both in season at the same time, but... okay...
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ01JGDPLI/AAAAAAAADSU/x8O_czGvzLs/s1600/S7300962.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ01JGDPLI/AAAAAAAADSU/x8O_czGvzLs/s400/S7300962.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       Carefully, with sharp, sharp small scissors, cut around the design you're going to use leaving 1/4"-1/2" extra fabric that we'll turn under. If the design is simple, with few bends and curves, I'd use closer to 1/2" than I did. Add little clips toward the design on all of the curved edges, so you don't get wrinkling when you turn under.
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ1TciJAWI/AAAAAAAADSs/epnN4s8EWTU/s1600/S7300966.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="480" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ1TciJAWI/AAAAAAAADSs/epnN4s8EWTU/s400/S7300966.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       Carefully press under the fabric 1/4"-1/2", whichever you used when cutting. You may have to add some clips on the curves and the indents to make it lay flat.
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ1dykcHrI/AAAAAAAADS8/iveZ_peH0F8/s1600/S7300969.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ1dykcHrI/AAAAAAAADS8/iveZ_peH0F8/s400/S7300969.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <ul>
                      <li>
                       Position and pin the design on your sweater where you think you'll want it. I didn't get too creative; I just stuck it where the Anthro sweater had a design, but you could use this technique on the hip of the sweater, or on the shoulder... wherever!
                      </li>
                      <li>
                       Carefully whipstitch the design down, making sure to keep all sides of the design flat so it doesn't pucker when it's done. I caught about three threads of the appliquwith each stitch, so my sewing just barely shows on the top.
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ36et6rYI/AAAAAAAADT0/VWOIiZm-fAU/s1600/S7301106.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ36et6rYI/AAAAAAAADT0/VWOIiZm-fAU/s400/S7301106.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ4bCz3PcI/AAAAAAAADUU/888YmGT6Taw/s1600/S7301108.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_gslEOK_wkyU/TCQ4bCz3PcI/AAAAAAAADUU/888YmGT6Taw/s400/S7301108.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Ta-da!
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Varml1uXOeA/Un_Ggc1vaBI/AAAAAAAABpU/dpW-feCqG1A/s1600/S7301297.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="850" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Varml1uXOeA/Un_Ggc1vaBI/AAAAAAAABpU/dpW-feCqG1A/s1600/S7301297.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ECthuVzSgu0/Un_GgoJhggI/AAAAAAAABpc/ns0e7f6OC2A/s1600/S7301305.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="850" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ECthuVzSgu0/Un_GgoJhggI/AAAAAAAABpc/ns0e7f6OC2A/s1600/S7301305.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     Great to pair with a summery skirt and top for going into air-conditioned buildings!
