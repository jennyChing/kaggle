        <h1 class="article-title">
         Here's What a Miyazaki Theme Park Might Look Like
        </h1>
        <div class="article-meta">
         <span class="author">
          By
          <a href="/author/beckettmufson">
           Beckett Mufson
          </a>
         </span>
          Feb 4 2015
        </div>
        <div class="header-socials share-widget" data-media="" data-permalink="http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/heres-what-a-miyazaki-theme-park-might-look-like" data-title="The Creators Project" data-topic="" data-tweet="The Creators Project" data-twitter-username="CreatorsProject" data-url="http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/heres-what-a-miyazaki-theme-park-might-look-like" id="">

           Tweet

       <div class="article-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="https://thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/6671b159da9e3cae5699ed80fadb6b06.jpg" style="height: 446px; width: 640px;"/>
         <em>
          Images
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/takumitoxin" target="_blank">
           via
          </a>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         It's easy to think of Hayao Miyazaki as the Walt Disney of Japan, but had he followed the American animator's career to a T, he might have created a "Ghibli Land" to complement the Disney World theme parks in the West. An animator who goes by the name
         <a href="https://twitter.com/takumitoxin" target="_blank">
          TAKUMI
         </a>
         has envisioned this alternate reality, weaving together some very well thought-out plans for a theme park based entirely on Miyazaki's films.
        </p>
        <p>
         Howl's moving castle stands in the center of the park, replacing Sleeping Beauty's decadent estate and serving as a strong indicator of just how different the Ghibli Land experience would be from "The Happiest Place on Earth." From there visitors can relax in Totoro's treehouse, glimpse
         <em>
          Princess Mononoke
         </em>
         's Great Forest Spirit from the ramparts of Irontown, and hop on the Cat Bus monorail before spending the night in the bathhouse from
         <em>
          Spirited Away
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         While the
         <a href="http://www.ghibli-museum.jp/en/" target="_blank">
          Ghibli Museum
         </a>
         already exists and looks an awful lot like a theme park itself, given the fantastical nature of Miyazaki's movies, it's hard not to imagine a world in which Tokyo Disneyland has been replaced by its anime counterpart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="https://thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/e6081a5720c45f8075589b5d4b0e2582.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 373px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="https://thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/8661055ac5786e95413c10311fd1066a.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 379px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="https://thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/ad98b0fa6134d1e2df7a098254837fcd.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 461px;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="https://thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/d6e62a094d7a9c840eef1bef1d868c6f.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 469px;"/>
         <em>
          A sketch of TAKUMI's 'Tokyo Ghibli Land.'
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" src="https://thecreatorsproject-images.vice.com/content-images/contentimage/no-slug/15bf2bd8ca7dcc5d072779c281072500.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 570px;"/>
         TAKUMI's 'Tokyo Ghibli Land' follows a similar design to Tokyo Disneyland, pictured above. Image
         <a href="http://www.tokyodisneyresort.jp/en/tds/atrc/images/map.jpg" target="_blank">
          via
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Visit
         <a href="https://twitter.com/takumitoxin" target="_blank">
          TAKUMI's Twitter page
         </a>
         for future updates to Ghibli Land's repertoire.
        </p>
