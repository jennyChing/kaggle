          <h3 class="entry-header">
           my other mother loves me
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             Yesterday, Anne convinced me to take a break from work so we could go see a matinee of
             <em>
              <a href="http://movies.yahoo.com/movie/1809418061/info">
               Coraline
              </a>
             </em>
             , which was playing in 3D at a local theater. I love the
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Coraline-Neil-Gaiman/dp/0061139378%3FSubscriptionId%3D0PZ7TM66EXQCXFVTMTR2%26tag%3Dwilwheatodotn-20%26linkCode%3Dxm2%26camp%3D2025%26creative%3D165953%26creativeASIN%3D0061139378">
              book
             </a>
             , and I love
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Nightmare-Before-Christmas-Collectors-Digital/dp/B001AIRUOU%3FSubscriptionId%3D0PZ7TM66EXQCXFVTMTR2%26tag%3Dwilwheatodotn-20%26linkCode%3Dxm2%26camp%3D2025%26creative%3D165953%26creativeASIN%3DB001AIRUOU">
              <em>
               Nightmare Before Christmas
              </em>
             </a>
             , so it seemed like a no-brainer.
            </p>
            <p>
             But 3D? I wasn't so sure. I'm not a huge fan of 3D. It always feels gimmicky and intrusive, and I'm always wondering when Doctor Tongue is going to thrust a cat toward the audience.
            </p>
            <p>
             However, my love of the source material, an excuse to play hooky with my wife, and how excited I've been to see the movie since I first heard they were making it was enough to get me into the theater without any real argument.
            </p>
            <p>
             I am so glad that I went, because I loved the movie. I heard that Henry Selick wanted to use the 3D technology to give the movie depth, rather than shove things into the audience's faces, and I thought he did exactly that. I told my friends that they don't
             <em>
              have
             </em>
             to see it in 3D, but they kind of have to see it in 3D.
            </p>
            <p>
             Speaking as a fan of the book, I was mostly happy with the adaptation. They added an entirely new character, which I wasn't thrilled about, but he didn't feel like Scrappy Doo to me, and if I wasn't already a fan of the book, he wouldn't have felt out of place to me, at all. Everything I wanted to see was there, and they managed to create the world that I'd created in my head when I read it with eerie perfection.
            </p>
            <p>
             Speaking as a fan of movies and stop-motion animation, I was delighted. All the actors are fantastic, and the set design and animation was breathtaking. You don't
             <em>
              need
             </em>
             to see it in 3D, but I thought they used the 3D experience perfectly, and if you have a choice, I'd take the 3D option (which is something I never thought I'd say.)
            </p>
            <p>
             Coraline gets 4.5 out of 5 Beldams, on the Wheaton Scale of Randomly Rating Movies In A Way Which Is Amusing To Wil.
            </p>
            <p>
             Seriously, people, go see it. I think you'll love it.
            </p>

            I am so glad that I went, because I loved the movie. I heard that Henry Selick wanted to use the 3D technology to give the movie depth, rather than shove things into the audience's faces, and I thought he did exactly that. I told my friends that they don't
            <em>
             have
            </em>
            to see it in 3D, but they kind of have to see it in 3D.
           </p>
           <p>
            Speaking as a fan of the book, I was mostly happy with the adaptation. They added an entirely new character, which I wasn't thrilled about, but he didn't feel like Scrappy Doo to me, and if I wasn't already a fan of the book, he wouldn't have felt out of place to me, at all. Everything I wanted to see was there, and they managed to create the world that I'd created in my head when I read it with eerie perfection.
           </p>
           <p>
            Speaking as a fan of movies and stop-motion animation, I was delighted. All the actors are fantastic, and the set design and animation was breathtaking. You don't
            <em>
             need
            </em>
            to see it in 3D, but I thought they used the 3D experience perfectly, and if you have a choice, I'd take the 3D option (which is something I never thought I'd say.)
           </p>
           <p>
            Coraline gets 4.5 out of 5 Beldams, on the Wheaton Scale of Randomly Rating Movies In A Way Which Is Amusing To Wil.
           </p>
           <p>
            Seriously, people, go see it. I think you'll love it.
           </p>
