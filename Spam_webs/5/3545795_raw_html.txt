             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               Two Adele ballads have leaked online, giving fans their first new music from the 26-year-old singer since 2012, when Adele released her
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2013/02/24/adele-oscars/">
                Oscar
               </a>
               -winning "
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2012/10/04/adele-skyfall-james-bond-theme-video/">
                Skyfall
               </a>
               " theme song for the James Bond movie of the same name. That song also
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2012/10/02/adele-james-bond-theme-song-leaks-skyfall/">
                leaked
               </a>
               before its release.
              </p>
              <p>
               The Labor Day weekend leaks of "You'll Never See Me Again" and "Never Gonna Leave You" come on the heels of
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/07/16/adele-album-25-tour-2015/">
                rumors
               </a>
               that she might make her awaited comeback in 2015 with her third studio album and a tour to promote it.
              </p>
              <div class="see-also">
               <p>
                See also:
                <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2014/08/29/maroon-5-gwen-stefani-my-heart-is-open-listen-audio/">
                 Maroon 5's Ballad With Gwen Stefani Is a Close Second to 'Stay With Me'
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               Adele hasn't released an album or gone on tour since 2011. Her sophomore outing,
               <em>
                21
               </em>
               , won
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2012/02/15/adele-grammy-tweets/">
                six Grammys
               </a>
               , became the first album to go
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2012/02/22/adele-21-itunes-double-platinum/">
                double platinum
               </a>
               on iTunes in the United States, and became the best-selling album of 2011 and 2012.
              </p>
              <p>
               The two leaked songs, below, were
               <a href="http://www.digitalspy.com/music/news/a593683/adele-two-previously-unheard-tracks-leak-online.html#~oOIwYFoVn4eKPG" target="_blank">
                reportedly
               </a>
               recorded in 2010, though it's unknown whether Adele's camp has plans to include them on any upcoming albums.
              </p>

                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    The softcore rockers are one of the most popular acts in the world, so it doesn't take much publicity for them to be heard. Dropping their last album,
                    <em>
                     Mylo Xyloto
                    </em>
                    in 2011, the band surprisingly released a single on Feb. 25, titled "Midnight." The track came without warning, and also ushered in an announcement of a new album.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Video: YouTube,
                   <a href="http://gdata.youtube.com/feeds/api/users/ColdplayVEVO" target="_blank">
                    ColdplayVEVO
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Image - Flickr" data-id="554c52b5b589e402e70019c8" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3L2I1L3llZXp1cy4zNWYxMS5qcGcKcAl0aHVtYgkxODB4MTQwIwplCWpwZw/241e44dd/168/yeezus.jpg">
                 <figure>
                  <img alt="Yeezus" src="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3L2I1L3llZXp1cy4zNWYxMS5qcGcKcAl0aHVtYgk4NTB4ODUwPgplCWpwZw/3966ee01/168/yeezus.jpg"/>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   5. Kanye West
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    When it comes to rap, hardly any artist is more publicized than Kanye West. No matter what he's doing, he's constantly in the headlines, bragging about his talent like rap's Muhammad Ali.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    That's why the radio silence around his sixth album
                    <em>
                     Yeezus
                    </em>
                    was so bizarrely out of character. The hype machine whirled so deafeningly, West only needed to make minimal moves, choosing to release the album without a cover, or a single, leaving radio stations scrambling to pick the best track.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Image: Flickr,
                   <a href="http://p3.no/" target="_blank">
                    NRK P3
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Video - Youtube" data-id="554c52b5b589e402e70019c9" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3LzIwL29HNmxUUU5XMDRJLjFhMDlkLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTE4MHgxNDAjCmUJanBn/68d8f1fe/60b/oG6lTQNW04I.jpg">
                 <figure>
                  <div class="aspect-16x9">
                   <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="dnr" frameborder="0" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/oG6lTQNW04I">
                   </iframe>
                  </div>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   6. Vampire Weekend
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    There are two things Vampire Weekend don't care about:
                    <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P_i1xk07o4g&amp;feature=kp">
                     Oxford commas
                    </a>
                    and album promotion. To announce their third album,
                    <em>
                     Modern Vampires of the City
                    </em>
                    , the band
                    <a href="https://twitter.com/vampireweekend/status/298415647552049153">
                     tweeted
                    </a>
                    "NYT Classifieds," signaling fans to grab a copy of
                    <em>
                     The New York Times
                    </em>
                    and peruse the Classifieds section.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Sure enough, a tiny
                    <a href="http://pitchfork.com/news/49230-vampire-weekend-announce-album-release-date/">
                     announcement
                    </a>
                    revealed the name and release date of the upcoming album.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Video: YouTube,
                   <a href="http://gdata.youtube.com/feeds/api/users/VampireWeekendVEVO" target="_blank">
                    VampireWeekendVEVO
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Image - Flickr" data-id="554c52b5b589e402e70019ca" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3LzNiL3dlZWtuZDEuZTUyMjguanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/122bfc01/417/weeknd1.jpg">
                 <figure>
                  <img alt="Weeknd1" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3LzNiL3dlZWtuZDEuZTUyMjguanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDg1MD4KZQlqcGc/5d133cc0/417/weeknd1.jpg"/>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   7. The Weeknd
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    When you're an up-and-coming artist, you work like a madman to get your name out there -- but not if you're the Weeknd. The R&amp;B singer (real name Abel Tesfaye), quietly dropped a few songs on YouTube, which quickly caught the ear of the music world and got a boost when rapper Drake
                    <a href="https://twitter.com/Drake/status/44570226611847168">
                     tweeted
                    </a>
                    about the song "Wicked Games."
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    The publicity grew, but Tesfaye stayed silent (most fans didn't even know what he looked like), dropping three full mixtapes and not doing a single interview. He finally succumbed to a 2013 cover story with
                    <em>
                     <a href="http://www.complex.com/music/2013/07/weeknd-interview-cover-story">
                      Complex
                     </a>
                    </em>
                    , in order to promote his proper first album,
                    <em>
                     Kiss Land
                    </em>
                    .
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Image: Flickr,
                   <a href="http://www.flickr.com/people/karaokequeen3/" target="_blank">
                    jyw104
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Video - Youtube" data-id="554c52b5b589e402e70019cb" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3LzUzL0JaQVJDd1NJRS5hN2M2Yy5qcGcKcAl0aHVtYgkxODB4MTQwIwplCWpwZw/b2ac9e9c/285/B--ZARCwSIE.jpg">
                 <figure>
                  <div class="aspect-16x9">
                   <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="dnr" frameborder="0" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/B--ZARCwSIE">
                   </iframe>
                  </div>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   8. Jay Z
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    In the midst of the
                    <em>
                     Yeezus
                    </em>
                    hype, Mr. Carter made a special announcement: On July 4, he would be dropping an album, called
                    <em>
                     Magna Carta Holy Grail
                    </em>
                    (which was viewed as a slightly
                    <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/09/06/jay-z-magna-carta-holy-grail-tour-kanye_n_3880873.html">
                     attention-grabbing
                    </a>
                    move). He also announced a new business deal with Samsung, so anyone with a phone from the company could download the album for free. In another odd promo move, Jay performed the song "Picasso Baby" for
                    <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xMG2oNqBy-Y">
                     six hours
                    </a>
                    with performance artist Marina Abramovi at New York City's Pace Gallery.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Video: YouTube,
                   <a href="http://gdata.youtube.com/feeds/api/users/samsungmobileusa" target="_blank">
                    Samsung Mobile USA
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Video - Youtube" data-id="554c52b5b589e402e70019cc" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3L2MzL0s0SEtJaUR6TWhJLmFlM2Y2LmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTE4MHgxNDAjCmUJanBn/d871926f/a50/K4HKIiDzMhI.jpg">
                 <figure>
                  <div class="aspect-16x9">
                   <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="dnr" frameborder="0" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/K4HKIiDzMhI">
                   </iframe>
                  </div>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   9. Garth Brooks (as "Chris Gaines")
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    By 1999, Garth Brooks' country music popularity was waning, so he tried something different -- an
                    <em>
                     entirely new
                    </em>
                    persona. He created an alter ego named Chris Gaines, a hard rocker with a fictional past (which included a car crash that left him in need of vast amounts of plastic surgery). Brooks was supposed to star in a movie called
                    <em>
                     The Lamb
                    </em>
                    as Gaines, but when it was scrapped, he carried on, releasing an album later that year called
                    <em>
                     Greatest Hits
                    </em>
                    , also known as
                    <em>
                     Garth Brooks...In the Life of Chris Gaines
                    </em>
                    .
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    For more promotion, he hosted
                    <em>
                     Saturday Night Live
                    </em>
                    but performed as Gaines. Though a few eyebrows were raised towards Brooks' sanity, the album spawned a hit song called "Lost In You," which climbed to number 5 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Video: YouTube,
                   <a href="http://gdata.youtube.com/feeds/api/users/wg4L6pz4CUa5I1h2kUhJ8Q" target="_blank">
                    Chris Gaines
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Video - Youtube" data-id="554c52b5b589e402e70019cd" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3LzdhLzhuVEZqVm05c1RRLmQ1MTA1LmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTE4MHgxNDAjCmUJanBn/899806ab/fba/8nTFjVm9sTQ.jpg">
                 <figure>
                  <div class="aspect-16x9">
                   <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="dnr" frameborder="0" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/8nTFjVm9sTQ">
                   </iframe>
                  </div>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   10. Radiohead
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    The forefathers of alt-rock were not interested in regular promotion by the time they finished their seventh studio album, 2007s
                    <em>
                     In Rainbows
                    </em>
                    . Thus, they decided to leak it themselves, making it available for download on their website. They also didnt put a price on it, allowing fans to pay whatever they wanted for the album.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Video: YouTube,
                   <a href="http://gdata.youtube.com/feeds/api/users/radiohead" target="_blank">
                    radiohead
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Video - Youtube" data-id="554c52b5b589e402e70019ce" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3LzliL1pHVG5rcnczVGMwLmRjNjMxLmpwZwpwCXRodW1iCTE4MHgxNDAjCmUJanBn/f2a7f955/ea5/ZGTnkrw3Tc0.jpg">
                 <figure>
                  <div class="aspect-16x9">
                   <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="dnr" frameborder="0" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/ZGTnkrw3Tc0">
                   </iframe>
                  </div>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   11. Four Tet
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    Electronic artist Four Tet (real name Kieran Hebden) isn't mainstream, but he has a massive underground following. When it came time to release his album
                    <em>
                     Beautiful Rewind
                    </em>
                    in 2013, he announced it just two weeks beforehand, with a
                    <a href="https://twitter.com/FourTet/status/359293890718732288">
                     cheeky tweet
                    </a>
                    : "no pre order, no youtube trailers, no itunes stream, no spotify, no amazon deal, no charts, no bit coin deal, no last minute rick rubin." (The Rubin joke was a reference to last minute producing on Kanye West's
                    <em>
                     Yeezus
                    </em>
                    ).
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Video: YouTube,
                   <a href="http://gdata.youtube.com/feeds/api/users/arrepiosonoro" target="_blank">
                    Arrepio Sonoro
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Image - Flickr" data-id="554c52b5b589e402e70019cf" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3LzAzL3ByaW5jZTEuNzIxNGIuanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/84d81fa2/81b/prince1.jpg">
                 <figure>
                  <img alt="Prince1" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDE0LzAyLzI3LzAzL3ByaW5jZTEuNzIxNGIuanBnCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDg1MD4KZQlqcGc/8c93c9f1/81b/prince1.jpg"/>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   12. Prince
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    When it's time to release your 32nd studio album, how do you keep things fresh? Such was Prince's conundrum in 2007, for
                    <em>
                     Planet Earth
                    </em>
                    . His solution? Releasing a free physical copy of the album in an edition of
                    <em>
                     <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/home/moslive/article-466634/New-Prince-album-FREE-inside-weekends-Mail-Sunday.html">
                      The Mail on Sunday
                     </a>
                    </em>
                    , a British newspaper.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                   Image: Flickr,
                   <a href="http://www.flickr.com/people/petertea/" target="_blank">
                    Peter Tea
                   </a>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
