       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">
         <a href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/2014/07/17/pirate-island-tic-tac-toe-quiet-book-page/#respond">
          <span class="leave-reply">
           Leave a reply
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="aligncenter" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/patterns/ani-41.gif"/>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: center;">
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          Tic Tac Yo-Ho-ho!
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Jax has recently taken an interest in learning how to play tic-tac-toe (Noughts and crosses, Xs and Os, etc) so I thought it would be a fun and quick page to sew up. This project would also work great as a stand-alone 9 square felt tic-tac-toe set. Well be going to the beach in September, so I thought up a pirate island theme!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         This page can be made as a stand-alone game by sewing it to a 9 square background.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #99cc00;">
           Pair this page with:
           <a href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/2012/01/09/treasure-quiet-book-page/" title="Treasure Quiet Book Page">
            <span style="color: #99cc00;">
             The Treasure Quiet Book Page
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ttt01.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="wp-image-6139 aligncenter" height="491" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ttt01-1000x715.jpg" width="687"/>
         </a>
        </h2>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #99cc00;">
          What I used:
         </span>
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/patterns/PirateTicTacToe-Quietbook-Pattern.pdf" target="_blank">
           The pattern
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          Wool blend felt from
          <a href="http://www.americanfeltandcraft.com/" target="_blank">
           American Felt and Craft
          </a>
          in
          <a href="http://www.feltandcraft.com/pastry/" target="_blank">
           pastry
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.feltandcraft.com/doe/" target="_blank">
           doe
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.feltandcraft.com/gold-nugget/" target="_blank">
           gold nugget
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.feltandcraft.com/big-apple/" target="_blank">
           big apple
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.feltandcraft.com/ice/" target="_blank">
           ice
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.feltandcraft.com/black/" target="_blank">
           black
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.feltandcraft.com/white/" target="_blank">
           white
          </a>
          . I also used some
          <strong>
           dark brown
          </strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00CLQWNME/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00CLQWNME&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=stephaniesthough&amp;linkId=NSZ7FBPQLJS5UQUK">
           100% wool felt
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=stephaniesthough&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00CLQWNME" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          for the chest because it is very thick and sturdy. Mine was a few dollars at the craft store. You can use thinner felt, but I recommend doubling each side of the pocket.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002PNNO7G/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B002PNNO7G&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=stephaniesthough&amp;linkId=C72LYWIE44T7ATOW">
           Felt glue
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=stephaniesthough&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B002PNNO7G" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         </li>
         <li>
          Stuffing or batting
         </li>
         <li>
          Sequins: small blue crystal iridescent and large/XL gold
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.feltandcraft.com/faux-pearl-buttons/" target="_blank">
           Faux pearl buttons
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          One small sew-on snap
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ttt03.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="aligncenter wp-image-6133" height="481" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ttt03-1000x708.jpg" width="679"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          <strong>
           Sewing the Island
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Following the template, sewing the tic-tac-toe grid onto the island piece.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/sand2.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="aligncenter wp-image-6144" height="410" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/sand2-1000x605.jpg" width="678"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Pin down the island with a bit of stuffing or batting underneath for dimension. Sewing it down, then embellish it with
         <a href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/2013/04/02/sewing-basics-french-knots/" target="_blank" title="Sewing Basics  French Knots">
          French knots
         </a>
         and tiny stitches to show the sand texture.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/sand.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="wp-image-6134 aligncenter" height="518" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/sand-1000x750.jpg" width="691"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Decorate the waters edge with blue crystal sequins. Decorate the sand with some pirates treasure: gold sequins and pearl buttons.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ttt04.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="wp-image-6135 aligncenter" height="487" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ttt04-1000x708.jpg" width="688"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          <strong>
           Treasure Chest
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Stitch two gold bands onto each side of the treasure chest. I used thick 100% wool felt, so I didnt line the chest. If you are using regular felt, place a blank chest piece behind each side of the chest and sew them together along the long top side.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/chest.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="wp-image-6141 aligncenter" height="442" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/chest-1000x673.jpg" width="657"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Place the two finished sides together and sew the right sides and bottom together, then place it on the page as shown and sew the left side together through the page. This will make a pocket that is hinged to the page. You can flip it out of the way when it is time to play. Add a snap to the back of the chest on the loose side, then sew the matching half to the page. This holds the pocket still while you flip through your quiet book.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ttt02.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="wp-image-6136 aligncenter" height="498" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ttt02-1000x708.jpg" width="703"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          <strong>
           Cross Bones
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Using a light layer of felt glue, tack down two of the bones into a cross shape onto black felt. Once it is dry, cut it out and sew it all around the outside. Repeat so you have 5 Xs.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/pieces.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="wp-image-6138 aligncenter" height="306" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/pieces-1000x444.jpg" width="689"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #008000;">
           <span style="color: #008080;">
            Ruby Rings
           </span>
           <br/>
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         With on strand of pink thread, sew the gem lines onto each felt ruby. Start with a long horizontal line, then make two Vs above it and one large V below it. Use the template as a guide.
        </p>
        <p>
         Using a light layer of felt glue, tack down a gold ring onto brown felt. Glue a ruby on top as shown. once it is dry, cut it out and sew it all around the edges. Repeat so you have 5 Os.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #008080;">
           Arrrrh! Have Fun!
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a data-gal="prettyPhoto[6128]" href="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/play.jpg">
          <img alt="Pirate Island Tic-Tac-Toe Quiet Book Page" class="wp-image-6140" height="450" src="http://www.imagineourlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/play-1000x655.jpg" width="687"/>
         </a>
        </p>
