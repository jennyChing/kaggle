     <p>
      <b>
       Roland Lee Gift
      </b>
      (born 28 May 1961)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is a British singer and actor. He was the lead singer of the band
      <a href="/wiki/Fine_Young_Cannibals" title="Fine Young Cannibals">
       Fine Young Cannibals
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Roland_Gift&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Gift was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Sparkhill" title="Sparkhill">
       Sparkhill
      </a>
      in Birmingham
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      to a
      <a href="/wiki/White_people" title="White people">
       white
      </a>
      mother and a
      <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">
       black
      </a>
      father,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MusicianGuide_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-MusicianGuide-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and lived in the city until the age of 11.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He then moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">
       Hull
      </a>
      , where his mother, Pauline, ran several second-hand clothes shops. He was educated at
      <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_Hall_School" title="Kelvin Hall School">
       Kelvin Hall School
      </a>
      in Hull.
     </p>
     <p>
      Gift has two sisters, Helga and Jay, and one brother, Paul.
     </p>
     <p>
      One of his children, son Louis performs in the experimental
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acrobatic" title="Acrobatic">
       acrobatic
      </a>
      circus company
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Barely_Methodical_Troupe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Barely Methodical Troupe (page does not exist)">
       Barely Methodical Troupe
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Akrylykz_and_Fine_Young_Cannibals">
       Akrylykz and Fine Young Cannibals
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Roland_Gift&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Akrylykz and Fine Young Cannibals">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      His first
      <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction" title="Sound recording and reproduction">
       recording
      </a>
      on which he played the saxophone
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MusicianGuide_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-MusicianGuide-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      was with Hull
      <a href="/wiki/Ska" title="Ska">
       ska
      </a>
      band
      <a href="/wiki/Akrylykz" title="Akrylykz">
       Akrylykz
      </a>
      , the second release on nearby York's
      <a href="/wiki/Red_Rhino_Records" title="Red Rhino Records">
       Red Rhino Records
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indie.2C_Tony_K_obit_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Indie.2C_Tony_K_obit-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Although this record was unsuccessful, it did bring him to the attention of
      <a href="/wiki/Andy_Cox" title="Andy Cox">
       Andy Cox
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/David_Steele_(musician)" title="David Steele (musician)">
       David Steele
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/The_Beat_(British_band)" title="The Beat (British band)">
       The Beat
      </a>
      . The Akrylykz toured with The Beat,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      which led to them in around 1985 asking him to be the lead singer of their new band
      <a href="/wiki/Fine_Young_Cannibals" title="Fine Young Cannibals">
       Fine Young Cannibals
      </a>
      after their old band, The Beat, had broken up. He also was a guest artist on the Ska City Rockers' "Time Is Tight" single.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1990, he was named by
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">
        People
       </a>
      </i>
      magazine as one of the "50 Most Beautiful People" in the world.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">
       Solo career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Roland_Gift&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Gift contributed a cover of "
      <a href="/wiki/That%27s_How_Strong_My_Love_Is" title="That's How Strong My Love Is">
       That's How Strong My Love Is
      </a>
      " for the soundtrack of the 1996 film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Girls_(film)" title="Beautiful Girls (film)">
        Beautiful Girls
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2002, Gift released a self-titled solo album, featuring the single "It's Only Money". In 2007, Gift was in a recording studio working on a follow-up album.
     </p>
     <p>
      Gift has performed as a solo artist again, appearing at the Rewind Festival in Henley.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On 13 February 2012, Gift appeared on
      <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Solent" title="BBC Radio Solent">
       BBC Radio Solent
      </a>
      promoting the gigs and announced a potential new album in 2012. This will be a soundtrack to a film Gift is involved with. "The Prisoner" was played on the show and Roland said this would probably be included in the soundtrack.
     </p>
     <p>
      He also appeared in Jools Holland's 20th annual 'Hootenanny' show, which aired overnight on BBC2 on 31 December 2012 / 1 January 2013,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the last to be recorded at BBC Television Centre. Gift sang the Fine Young Cannibals hits 'Good Thing' and 'Suspicious Minds'.
     </p>
     <p>
      Gift was a guest vocalist on Jools Holland's 2013 tour.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In August 2014 Gift gave four live UK performances at Hare &amp; Hounds Birmingham, Freedom Festival Hull, Sage Gateshead and Caf de Paris, London.
     </p>
     <p>
      As of February 2015, Roland Gift is recording a new album, 'Return to Vegas'.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">
       Acting
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Roland_Gift&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acting">

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rolandgift.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1399" data-file-width="2470" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Rolandgift.JPG/280px-Rolandgift.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Rolandgift.JPG/420px-Rolandgift.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Rolandgift.JPG/560px-Rolandgift.JPG 2x" width="280"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">

        2011
       </div>

     </div>
     <p>
      In 1987, Gift had his first screen role in the film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sammy_and_Rosie_Get_Laid" title="Sammy and Rosie Get Laid">
        Sammy and Rosie Get Laid
       </a>
      </i>
      . In 1990 he did his first stage work, playing Romeo in the
      <a href="/wiki/Hull_Truck_Theatre" title="Hull Truck Theatre">
       Hull Truck Theatre
      </a>
      's production of Shakespeare's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">
        Romeo and Juliet
       </a>
      </i>
      , a production which had a brief run in the United States at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Staller_Center_for_the_Arts" title="Staller Center for the Arts">
       Staller Center for the Arts
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He also appeared as a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lounge_singer" title="Lounge singer">
       lounge singer
      </a>
      (singing songs that were included in the Fine Young Cannibals album
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Raw_and_the_Cooked_(album)" title="The Raw and the Cooked (album)">
        The Raw and the Cooked
       </a>
      </i>
      ) in the film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tin_Men" title="Tin Men">
        Tin Men
       </a>
      </i>
      , directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Barry_Levinson" title="Barry Levinson">
       Barry Levinson
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1989, he appeared in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Scandal_(1989_film)" title="Scandal (1989 film)">
        Scandal
       </a>
      </i>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Edgecombe" title="Johnny Edgecombe">
       Johnny Edgecombe
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Christine_Keeler" title="Christine Keeler">
       Christine Keeler
      </a>
      's boyfriend.
     </p>
     <p>
      In December 1992, he began the first of five appearances as the evil
      <a href="/wiki/Immortal_(Highlander)" title="Immortal (Highlander)">
       Immortal
      </a>
      Xavier St. Cloud in the television series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Highlander:_The_Series" title="Highlander: The Series">
        Highlander: The Series
       </a>
      </i>
      , and appeared in an episode of the Yorkshire Television series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Heartbeat_(UK_TV_series)" title="Heartbeat (UK TV series)">
        Heartbeat
       </a>
      </i>
      . He also had a small role as the jazz saxophonist Eddie Mullen in the mini-series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Painted_Lady" title="The Painted Lady">
        The Painted Lady
       </a>
      </i>
      (1997), starring
      <a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">
       Helen Mirren
      </a>
      , and appeared in the movie
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Island_of_the_Mapmaker%27s_Wife" title="The Island of the Mapmaker's Wife">
        The Island of the Mapmaker's Wife
       </a>
      </i>
      (2001).
     </p>
