        <header class="entry-header">
         <h1 class="entry-title">
          HP Proves Me Right on Immersive Technology
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <a class="author" href="/author/sam-volkering/" rel="author">
           Sam Volkering
          </a>
          /
          <a class="entry-date" href="./">
           October 31, 2014
          </a>
          /
          <a href="/category/featured/" rel="category tag">
           Featured
          </a>
          /
          <a href="/category/immersive-technologies/" rel="category tag">
           Immersive Technologies
          </a>
         </div>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <div class="entry-thumbnail" style="float:right; padding:15px;box-shadow:none;">
          <a href="./">
           <img alt="Hewlett-Packard corporate headquarters in Silicon Valley" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="126" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/HP_computers250-220x126.jpg" width="220"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          Before I get into todays Tech Insider, I want to ask you something.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive been writing about cyber security for a while now. But I realized that I never asked the most obvious question:
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <u>
            Have you ever been a victim of cyber crime?
           </u>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Im curious to know who among you has felt the wrath of cyber crooks first hand? If youve been a victim of cyber crime, I want to hear your story.
         </p>
         <p>
          The more we share about everyday cyber crime, the more we can learn and help protect each other from it. Think of it as crowd sourcing experiences.
         </p>
         <p>
          If youd like to share your story, please write to me at
          <a href="mailto:letters@techinsider.com.au?subject=My%20Cyber%20Security%20Story">
           letters@techinsider.com.au
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          I wont publish any stories without consent, and you can remain anonymous if you wish.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now on to todays story
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          <center>
           The game changing tech thats proven me right
          </center>
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Everyone knows Hewlett Packard (
          <strong>
           HP
          </strong>
          ). The company has been around for about 75 years. Founders Bill Hewlett and Dave Packard started HP in 1939.
         </p>
         <p>
          Hewlett and Packard were both engineering graduates. And like all good billion dollar tech companies, they started in a garage. With an initial investment of US$538, the global tech giant is worth US$65.99 billion today. Not a bad ROI
         </p>
         <p>
          The company has always been an information technology company, developing software, hardware and services to consumers and businesses.
         </p>
         <p>
          During the 1990s, the company really began to benefit from the wave of personal computing. In 1991, you could pick up shares in HP for around US$4. By the end of the decade, the stock was trading above US$70. The PC was an absolute winner, and the company made many investors a lot of money.
         </p>
         <p>
          HP became the poster child in the US for technology and innovation.
         </p>
         <p>
          And then the tech bubble burst, and even sound, money making companies like HP fell with the rest of the market. By October 2002, the stock was kicking back around US$12.
         </p>
         <p>
          The stock bounced around during the following decade. It was still floundering around US$12 just a couple of years ago. HP was still the leader in printers and services, but the company was struggling to truly innovate. It seemed as though theyd lost their mojo.
         </p>
         <p>
          And worryingly for HP, the PC game had changed dramatically. PCs were all the rage in the 90s and early 2000s thanks in large part to the internet.
         </p>
         <p>
          The number of PCs worldwide surged past one billion in 2008  but then came to a grinding halt.
         </p>
         <p>
          The PC game seemingly changed overnight. No longer was it the huge money spinner it used to be. That was because of the mobile revolution. Mobile phones and tablets have dominated PCs over the last five years.
         </p>
         <p>
          It wasnt the end of the PC, but the market lacked innovation. It was waiting for a game changer
         </p>
         <p>
          Well, it looks like HP has just changed the game.
         </p>
         <p>
          HP launched a new 3D printer this week. But also  and this is the game changer  they introduced a new
          <strong>
           immersive computing
          </strong>
          platform they call Sprout.
         </p>
         <p>
          HPs executive vice president of Printing and Personal Systems (PPS) is Dion Weisler. Weisler is Aussie. Hes was educated at Monash University and cut his teeth in the IT world at Telstra. Talk about an Aussie lad come good!
         </p>
         <p>
          Weisler is the lead for these two game changing technologies. During the official HP release, he stated:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           
           <em>
            We are on the cusp of a transformative era in computing and printing. Our ability to deliver Blended Reality technologies will reduce the barriers between the digital and physical worlds, enabling us to express ourselves at the speed of thought  without filters, without limitations. This ecosystem opens up new market categories that can define the future, empowering people to create, interact and inspire like never before.
           </em>
           
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          This is the Sprout.
         </p>
         <p align="center">
          <img alt="TEC20141031a HP Proves Me Right on Immersive Technology" src="//portphillippublishing.com.au/images/TEC20141031a.jpg" title="HP Proves Me Right on Immersive Technology"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Its a new kind of immersive computer, designed specifically to enhance our ability to create with our hands.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive been talking about the 
          <a href="/category/immersive-technologies/" title="more on immersive technology ">
           <strong>
            immersive technology
           </strong>
          </a>
           revolution ever since I started
          <em>
           Tech Insider
          </em>
          . Immersive tech is the convergence of the physical and digital worlds into one. Im not normally one to gloat, but HP just proved me right.
         </p>
         <p>
          HPs SVP of Consumer PC &amp; Solutions, Ron Coughlin said,
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           
           <em>
            Until now, the physical and digital worlds have largely been separated and digital creation has remained in 2D. With Sprout by HP, we introduce the first immersive computing platform, seamlessly merging these two worlds together, enabling people to intuitively bring their creations, work, and projects to life in 3D.
           </em>
           
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          I urge you to go to HPs website and check out the Sprout. I think its a game changer and a sign that the HPs got its mojo back.
         </p>
         <p>
          And dont forget about the 3D printer  the Multi Jet Fusion. Marketed at companies rather than consumers, the Multi Jet Fusion, with 30,000 print nozzles, can print in high-resolution 10-times faster than any other 3D printer on the market.
         </p>
         <p>
          HP has taken their expertise in desktop printers, using their inkjet technology, and applied it to 3D printing. Its a masterstroke.
         </p>
         <p>
          These two huge technology releases appeared overnight. HP has been keeping things low key intentionally. Even to HP, the Sprout is a chameleon. Theyre not exactly sure exactly who their customer will be.
         </p>
         <p>
          I believe itll be everyone. It has the potential to completely change how we create, and how we interact with computers. I believe this is the arrival of the big shift in computing that Ive been calling for years.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you cant tell, Im excited, and you should be too. This is a turning point for HP as well as the humble PC.
         </p>

           little wacky at times
          </a>
          . They certainly have a unique approach to technology and design. Sometimes it results in the weirdest junk youve ever seen in your life. But sometimes theyll unearth something truly revolutionary.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="//www.wired.com/2014/10/fiat-selling-ferrari-whats-worst-happen/" rel="nofollow">
           Ferrari is going public
