    <h2>
     Schwarzenbach
    </h2>
    <h3>
     [1] : Porzellanfabrik Oscar Schaller &amp; Co. (1882 until 1917)
    </h3>
    <p>
     The company was founded in the year 1882 by the businessman
     <em>
      Oscar Schaller
     </em>
     and after exactly ten years he changed his mark into the symbol representing a stylized 'S &amp; C' for
     <em>
      Schaller &amp; Co.
     </em>
     .
    </p>
    <h3>
     [2] : Porzellanfabrik Oscar Schaller &amp; Co. Nachfolger (1917 until 1950)
    </h3>
    <p>
     In 1917 the company was taken over by the
     <a href="../../../germany/bavaria/roeslau-01/index.php">
      Gebrder Winterling O.H.G.
     </a>
     in Rslau (Bavaria) and renamed to
     <em>
      Oscar Schaller &amp; Co. Nachfolger
     </em>
     . The upward trend was so promising that they bought and converted the textile factory
     <a href="../../../germany/bavaria/kirchenlamitz-01/index.php">
      Kirchenlamitz
     </a>
     (Upper Franconia, Bavaria) in 1920 and took over the company
     <a href="../../../germany/bavaria/windischeschenbach-01/index.php">
      Porzellanfabrik Eduard Haberlnder
     </a>
     in Windischeschenbach (Upper Palatinate, Bavaria) in 1929. All these subsidiaries were kept under the name of
     <em>
      Oscar Schaller &amp; Co.
      <a href="../../../resources/vocabulary/nachfolger.php">
       Nachfolger
      </a>
     </em>
     . In 1938 the group also took over the
     <a href="../../../germany/thuringia/triptis-01/index.php">
      Triptiser Porzellanfabrik A.G.
     </a>
     (Triptis, Thuringia) that remained a standalone factory for the next years until its expropriation and partial nationalization in 1947 which was completed after the founding of East Germany in 1949.
    </p>
    <p>
     An interesting fact is that the company also manufactured some souvenir pieces for the town of Hallerstein, which at that time had a constant flow of tourists visiting their church in Zell-Hallerstein which was based on a chapel built in 1420. Today, pieces marked with 'Hallerstein' are much sought after pieces for collectors of
     <em>
      Schaller
     </em>
     or
     <em>
      Winterling
     </em>
     items.
    </p>
    <h3>
     [3] : Porzellanfabrik Gebrder Winterling A.G. (1950 until 2000)
    </h3>
    <p>
     In 1950 the company was changed to corporation status and gave out shares; the family members being the main shareholders. All daughter companies were re-named to
     <em>
      Gebrder Winterling A.G.
     </em>
     and the marks used up until then were changed.
    </p>
    <h3>
     [4] : Triptis Porzellan G.m.b.H. &amp; Co. K.G. (2000 until 2004)
    </h3>
    <p>
     After the bankruptcy of the
     <em>
      Gebrder Winterling A.G.
     </em>
     in November 2000, the facility and mark were taken over by the
     <a href="../../../germany/thuringia/triptis-01/index.php">
      Triptis Porzellan G.m.b.H. &amp; Co. K.G.
     </a>
     located in Triptis (Thuringia). But the Triptis management had bitten off more than they could chew. Confronted with large scaled financial problems, Triptis themselves went insolvent on October 7th 2004 and had to file for bankruptcy a month later. In June 2005 the Triptis company was refounded as
     <em>
      Neue Porzellanfabrik Triptis G.m.b.H.
     </em>
     and continued business, please view the Triptis entry for further info.
    </p>
