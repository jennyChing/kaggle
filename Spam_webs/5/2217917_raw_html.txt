      <div class="mt-story-text-share article-txt-top no-flow entry-txt">
       <div class="videoplayer brightcove embed-responsively" data-autoplay="true" data-params="@videoPlayer=4025822161001" data-player="article" data-schema="1">
       </div>
       <p>
        <br/>
        PHOENIX - Believe it or not, it was NFL Networks Marshall Faulk - he of the sour grapes whinery when it comes to anything regarding the New England Patriots - who said it best when asked if the latest scandal to embroil the Patriots is damaging to the legacy of head coach Bill Belchick.
       </p>
       <p>
        Who cares? Faulk asked. I dont think
        <em>
         he
        </em>
        cares.
       </p>
       <p>
        Hey, heres a tip,
        <em>
         nobody
        </em>
        should care.
       </p>
       <p>
        Well, at least as long as you're a card-carrying member of the New England fan base. Root for any of the other 31 NFL teams, and knock yourself out. Hate on the Pats all you want. Theres plenty of disdain out there for Patriots fans to soak themselves in if they relent to the disgust. But I had to laugh, guffaw even, when someone mentioned to me on Thursday that it must be so damned hard to be a Patriots fan these days, with Deflategate, Spygate, and whatever other controversy you want to pin on the Patriots swirling as they prepare to face the Seattle Seahawks in Sundays Super Bowl.
       </p>
       <p>
        Please.
       </p>
       <p>
        Is that an ethical stance to take, deeming guilt in an ongoing investigation that so far has been run with the efficiency of a flat tire on sand? Dont want to feel dirty rooting for your countrys brand of NFL laundry? Im sure theres a high school gymnasium somewhere where the team preaches good sportsmanship in the complete absence of constant winning.
       </p>
       <p>
        I mean, the suggestion is laughable.  Is it hard to be a Patriots fan?
       </p>
       <p>
        Hell no. Have you followed them the last 10-15 years? asked one Patriots fan roaming Radio Row at Super Bowl Central here in downtown Phoenix. Id rather win and have scandal than lose and be ignored. I mean, cmon man.
       </p>
       <div>
        <img alt="c3ce6e878fb64e72a472c49b6e512664-c3ce6e878fb64e72a472c49b6e512664-0.jpg" class="article-art article-lead-art" src="http://www.boston.com/sports/columnists/wilbur/c3ce6e878fb64e72a472c49b6e512664-c3ce6e878fb64e72a472c49b6e512664-0.jpg"/>
        <figcaption class="caption story-caption reverse">
         <!--Put a caption for the image here.-->
         <i class="credit">
          <!--Here, credit the image.-->
         </i>
        </figcaption>
       </div>
       <p>
        <br/>
        Seriously. Just who are Patriots fans to whine when their team is a perennial Super Bowl contender with one of the best quarterbacks ever to play the game at the helm? Jets fans get to whine. Browns fans get to feign embarrassment Jaguars fans get towell, we need to find them first, I suppose.
       </p>
       <p>
        Look at who I root for, one fan in an Oakland Raiders jersey said. You think I give a damn? No. No. Sign me right up today. Wouldnt care.
       </p>
       <p>
        Even Seattle fans are downright diplomatic with their response.
       </p>
       <p>
        Youve got to be proud of what theyve done, one Seahawks fan said. Put the deflategate aside. Theyre a good team.
       </p>
       <p>
        Good team. I dont want to get too novel here, but maybe thats why they win?
       </p>
       <p>
        I know, its crazy.
       </p>
       <p>
        And yet, here we are, debating the legacies and long-term viewpoints of a duo in Belichick and Tom Brady, who are generally seen as cheaters across the country. I mean, the rest of the country also eats at Hardees. Why should their take affect Pats fans in any way?
       </p>
       <p>
        Who cares? Who really [expletive] cares?
       </p>
       <p>
        Faulks former teammate, Kurt Warner,
        <a href="http://www.boston.com/blogs/sports/columnists/kaufman/2015/01/more_deflategate_sour_grapes_kurt_warner_questions.html">
         was at it again this week in Phoenix
        </a>
        , telling a radio station that he still has some lingering doubt over the authenticity of the Patriots victory over St. Louis in 2002.
       </p>
       <p>
        I dont want to believe that there was anything outside of [Belichicks] team beat our team, the Hall of Fame candidate told Sports Radio 950 KJR at Tuesdays Media Day. Thats what I want to believe. Yeah, theres a sliver of a doubt because I think, as a human, you cant help it. To know that if you were a part of that process at that time, was there any advantage they gained in any game, not just our Super Bowl game, but maybe a game before that to get to the Super Bowl? I mean, all those things enter your mind.
       </p>
       <p>
        Its not because Im bitter. Its not because I say they cheated, because I have no idea, but it adds a sliver of doubt that I think is unfair to everybody. Its unfair to them and their legacy. Its unfair to me and my legacy because I dont want to have to wonder, well did they beat me fair and square or was there something extra? And thats the unfortunate part that I dont think youll ever get over because you know something was done outside the rules. I dont know how it helped them. I dont know if it gave them an advantage on one play  that turned into an interception or a touchdown  or it gave them no advantage. I dont know.
       </p>
       <p>
        Its been 13 years of this same nonsense from the Rams, so you know that even a win over Seattle on Sunday probably wont remove the controversy from the legacy dockets of Belichick and Brady.
       </p>
       <p>
        But does it at least shut people up about the Well, they havent won one since they got caught
       </p>
       <p>
        You mean does it erase Spygate? Faulk asked. I dont know why a win would erase other facts. Youre speaking like we speculated about Spygate. It wasnt a speculation, it was facts. The fines, the draft picks thats a fact.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres another one. The Patriots beat the Rams.
       </p>
       <p>
        Fact.
       </p>
       <p>
        Listen, every game that Ive lost, I feel like I was cheated, Faulk said. So
       </p>
       <p>
        He let the suggestion dangle, but his emphasis was clear
       </p>
       <p>
        Thirteen years ago, the Patriots were darlings of the NFL, an up-and-coming feel-good story that took down the leagues most-dominant team. This time, they are the hated Empire, the successful team America is rooting agains in the name of some brand of ill-conceived justice.
       </p>
       <p>
        Embrace it.
       </p>
       <p>
        I dont know what Americas view is, Faulk said. My view is the Patriots, they have to play the Seahawks, and weve got a great game on Sunday and were going to find out about that. Once the league finishes with their investigation, well get facts about that and then we can comment. But until then, were wasting time speculating when weve got a heck of a game on Sunday to focus on.
       </p>
       <p>
        Yup. Sure is hard to watch your team play in the Super Bowl. Again.
       </p>
