       <h2 class="posttitle">
        Pumpkin Chip Cupcakes with Brown Sugar Buttercream
       </h2>
       <div class="postmeta">
        <em>
         Posted By
        </em>
        Lindsay
        <em>
         on
        </em>
        October 11, 2009
       </div>
       <!-- end .postmeta -->
      </header>
      <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
      <p>
       <img alt="Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Mummy Cupcakes with Brown Sugar Buttercream" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22511" height="683" src="http://www.loveandoliveoil.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/pumpkin-mummy-cupcakes2.jpg" width="550"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Boo! Oh, sorry. I forgot that its still 3 weeks until Halloween. You see, Ive got pumpkin on the brain. Ever since I realized there was a pumpkin shortage (yes, I said shortage.
       <a href="http://www.latimes.com/business/la-fi-pumpkin10-2009oct10,0,3694102.story">
        Its true
       </a>
       .) Ive been craving all things pumpkin. I finally was able to get my hands on some canned pumpkin last weekend, and have been putting it to good use. Im talking cupcakes and cookies and muffins and breads we even made a savory salad with a fresh pie pumpkin (I had such high hopes for it, but it was a disappointment, to say the least, its not a recipe youll be seeing here. darn.) One cant turn orange from eating too much pumpkin, right?
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Mummy Cupcakes with Brown Sugar Buttercream" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-22512" height="683" src="http://www.loveandoliveoil.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/pumpkin-mummy-cupcakes.jpg" width="550"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       These cupcakes have to be one of my favorites. If youve been reading this blog since last year you might recognize the recipe. Call it a bit of cupcake mix-n-match, I took my
       <a href="http://www.loveandoliveoil.com/2008/11/pumpkin-chip-cupcakes-with-cinnamon-rum-buttercream.html">
        pumpkin chip cupcake recipe
       </a>
       , and topped it with a delicious
       <a href="http://www.loveandoliveoil.com/2009/02/oatmeal-chocolate-chip-cupcakes-with-brown-sugar-buttercream.html">
        brown sugar buttercream
       </a>
       . Pumpkin + brown sugar, well, it was meant to be.
      </p>
      <p>
       The mummy-inspired decor is surprisingly simple, basically strips of frosting piped on with a flat tip. The sugar eyes are something Ive had in my stash for a while, just waiting for the perfect use for them. You can find them in white, as well as multi color and jumbo size at one of my favorite cupcake-supply shops,
       <a href="http://www.bakeitpretty.com/item_339/Candy-Sugar-Eyes.htm">
        Bake it Pretty
       </a>
       . These are my entry into
       <a href="http://iheartcuppycakes.com/2009/09/24/cupcake-herooctober/">
        Octobers Cupcake Hero
       </a>
       , with a (duh) pumpkin theme (what else?). Be sure to stay tuned and vote for me when the poll opens. :)
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Pumpkin Chip Cupcakes with Brown Sugar Buttercream" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1330" height="336" src="http://www.loveandoliveoil.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/101109_1.jpg" title="Pumpkin Chip Cupcakes with Brown Sugar Buttercream" width="450"/>
       <br/>
       <img alt="Pumpkin Chip Mummy Cupcakes with Brown Sugar Buttercream" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1328" height="308" src="http://www.loveandoliveoil.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/101109_2.jpg" title="Pumpkin Chip Mummy Cupcakes with Brown Sugar Buttercream" width="450"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the meantime, if you see canned pumpkin, stock up. Make sure you have plenty to feed your pumpkin cravings until the supply is back to normal.
      </p>
      <div class="recipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
       <div class="recipebuttons">
        <img align="right" class="photo nopin" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://www.loveandoliveoil.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/101109_2-130x130.jpg"/>
        <a class="print" href="http://www.loveandoliveoil.com/2009/10/pumpkin-chip-cupcakes-with-brown-sugar-buttercream.html/print/" title="Print Recipe">
         Print Recipe
        </a>
        <div class="paperclip">
        </div>
       </div>
       <h2 itemprop="name">
        Pumpkin Chip Cupcakes with Brown Sugar Buttercream
       </h2>
       <div class="time">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Yield:
         </strong>
         <span itemprop="recipeYield">
          1 dozen cupcakes
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Total Time:
         </strong>
         <meta content="PT1H" itemprop="totalTime"/>
         1 hour
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3 id="ingredients">
        Ingredients:
       </h3>
       <div class="ingredients">
        <h4>
         Cupcakes:
        </h4>
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 cup canned pumpkin
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/3 cup oil
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 cup granulated sugar
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/4 cup milk or soymilk
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 teaspoon vanilla extract
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/2 teaspoon baking powder
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/2 teaspoon baking soda
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          3/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/8 teaspoon each ground cloves, nutmeg, and ginger
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/4 teaspoon salt
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/2 cup mini chocolate chips
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h4>
         Frosting:
        </h4>
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 large egg whites
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/2 cup packed light-brown sugar
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1/4 teaspoon salt
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          3/4 cup (1 1/2 sticks) unsalted butter, room temperature, cut into tablespoons
         </li>
        </ul>
       </div>
       <h3 id="directions">
        Directions:
       </h3>
       <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
        <ol>
         <li>
          Preheat oven to 350F. Line muffin pan with cupcake liners.
         </li>
         <li>
          In a medium bowl, stir together pumpkin, oil, sugar, soymilk, and vanilla. Sift in the flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, and spices. Gently whisk by hand until just combined, don't use an electric mixer or overmix the batter as it may become too gummy. Once combined, fold in the chocolate chips.
         </li>
         <li>
          Fill liners with 1/4 cup batter (cups should be approximately 2/3 full). Bake for 22 to 24 minutes, or until toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Transfer to a wire rack and let cool completely before frosting.
         </li>
         <li>
          For frosting, in a heatproof bowl set over (not in) a pan of simmering water, whisk together egg whites, sugar, and salt. Cook, whisking constantly, until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture is warm to the touch. Transfer to the clean bowl of an electric mixer. Beat on medium speed until fluffy and cooled, about 15 minutes. Raise speed to high; beat until stiff peaks form. Reduce speed to medium-low; add butter, 2 to 3 tablespoons at a time, until fully incorporated. Spread or pipe onto cooled cupcakes.
         </li>
        </ol>
       </div>
      </div>
