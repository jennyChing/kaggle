        <h1>
         5 Steps to Building Quality LinkedIn Connections
        </h1>
        <p class="postmetadata">
         By
         <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/author/jon-rognerud/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jon Rognerud">
          Jon Rognerud
         </a>

         <span class="datetime">

         </span>

       <p>
        <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/category/how-to/" target="_blank">
         <img alt="social media how to" class="alignright" height="166" src="http://cdn.socialmediaexaminer.com/images/how-to-pose.png" style="margin-left: 5px; margin-right: 5px;" title="social media how to" width="190"/>
        </a>
        Are you trying to
        <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/lead-generation-with-linkedin/" target="_blank">
         find quality leads
        </a>
        for your business on LinkedIn?
       </p>
       <p>
        Are you rising above the noise with quality engagement?
       </p>
       <p>
        Using your
        <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/linkedin-professional-portfolio/" target="_blank">
         LinkedIn profile
        </a>
        to connect with market influencers and potential clients is smartwhen done right.
       </p>
       <p>
        In this article, youll
        <b>
         discover a five-step process for building the solid relationships you need to generate a steady flow of leads from LinkedIn
        </b>
        .
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 490px">
        <img alt="linkedin istock image" height="560" src="http://cdn.socialmediaexaminer.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/jr-linkedin-crop.png" width="480"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         LinkedIn is a great marketing tool if you create relationships based on trust. Image source: iStockPhoto.com.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <span id="more-56012">
        </span>
       </p>
       <h3>
        #1: Get Your House in Order
       </h3>
       <p>
        Before you start looking for leads on LinkedIn, its important to
        <a href="http://help.linkedin.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/530" target="_blank">
         understand LinkedIn basics
        </a>
        and the expectations of its users.
       </p>
       <p>
        Take a few minutes to
        <strong>
         make sure you have a
         <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/preparing-for-the-new-linkedin-design-how-to-optimize-your-page-and-profile/" target="_blank">
          professional profile
         </a>
         and picture
        </strong>
        , and
        <strong>
         join a few groups
        </strong>
        to
        <strong>
         get a feel for how they work
        </strong>
        . Go a step further and
        <strong>
         figure out the
         <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/how-to-boost-engagement-on-your-linkedin-company-page/" target="_blank">
          best engagement times to post on LinkedIn
         </a>
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 490px">
        <img alt="linkedin profile" height="287" src="http://cdn.socialmediaexaminer.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/jr-linkedin-profile.png" width="480"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Even little details, like a completed profile, affect how potential leads respond to you.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        When youre confident you have the basics down and are ready to start meeting others and creating leads,
        <strong>
         make a spreadsheet to track your progress and connections
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        I like to keep track of current personal connections, new connections, groups my target influencers and customers are in, popular group topics, how many members are in a group and which members are most active.
       </p>
       <h3>
        #2: Find Your Market Influencers on LinkedIn
       </h3>
       <p>
        Before you post and push content to your intended audience, be crystal-clear about their interests. Only then can you
        <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/5-steps-to-successful-linkedin-advertising/" target="_blank">
         create ads
        </a>
        or messages that speak directly to them.
       </p>
       <p>
        As you look for market leaders,
        <strong>
         review your personal connections and existing clients, then find them on LinkedIn
        </strong>
        . You most likely already have a priority list of companies and people whom you want to connect with. Next, go to LinkedIn and
        <strong>
         search for other companies and people who are active in your market
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 490px">
        <img alt="linkedin company search" height="596" src="http://cdn.socialmediaexaminer.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/jr-linkedin-company-search.png" width="480"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Search for all of the companies youd like to follow.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        When you connect with people,
        <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/successful-linkedin-networking/" target="_blank">
         dont send network invitations with Hey, were in the same network, I thought that
        </a>
        or I liked your website, I have a question types of messages to connect with them. Theyll get deleted, ignored or flagged.
       </p>
       <h3>
        #3: Join Relevant LinkedIn Groups
       </h3>
       <p>
        Its not enough to find the leading voices in your market, you have to know where they hang out and what topics they find interesting.
        <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/linkedin-group-tips/" target="_blank">
         LinkedIn groups
        </a>
        are a good place to get a feel for this.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 489px">
        <img alt="linkedin company search" height="234" src="http://cdn.socialmediaexaminer.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/jr-linkedin-groups.jpg" width="479"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Look at which LinkedIn groups market leaders are participating in, then join the ones that fit your needs.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Look at your list of influencers, then
        <strong>
         visit their LinkedIn profiles and scroll down the page to see their Groups section
        </strong>
        . Here you can see all of the groups theyre part of.
       </p>
       <p>
        Visit these groups and
        <strong>
         join the ones that fit your needs
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <h3>
        #4: Create an Editorial Calendar
       </h3>
       <p>
        When you
        <strong>
         visit a group
        </strong>
        , you can
        <strong>
         sort the conversations by Popular and Recent
        </strong>
        to see which questions and content are most relevant to members (and who offers the most responses). These conversations are your target areas of opportunity. Let those topics guide your own contributions.
       </p>
       <p>
        Make a note in your spreadsheet about popular topics and the names of the most active group members.
        <strong>
         Use that information to develop an editorial calendar
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        As you create your calendar, remember that generic content wont get you the attention you deserve and want. Instead, ask intriguing questions and share thoughtful, useful information to build a high-converting
        <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/how-to-create-written-content-that-generates-leads/" target="_blank">
         customer/client-building funnel
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        In one of my market-relevant groups, the topic of tools for writers came up, so I shared an article Id written that has related tips and detailed information about writing resources and tools. The content was relevant and it linked to my blog; I added value to the group
        <i>
         and
        </i>
        drove traffic to my website.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 486px">
        <img alt="linkedin plugin post" height="155" src="http://cdn.socialmediaexaminer.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/jr-linkedin-plugin-post.jpg" width="476"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Create engaging posts that speak to your audience.
        </p>
       </div>
       <h3>
        #5: Post the Right Kind of Content
       </h3>
       <p>
        Your
        <a href="http://www.socialmediaexaminer.com/26-tips-to-create-a-strong-social-media-content-strategy/" target="_blank">
         content
        </a>
        should
        <strong>
         address the issues being discussed in the group
        </strong>
        and
        <strong>
         offer help or more information
        </strong>
        without being a sales message in disguise.
       </p>
       <p>
        When you publish new online content,
        <strong>
         use your websites
         <a href="https://developer.linkedin.com/share-plugin" target="_blank">
          social sharing widget or plugin
         </a>
         to push the post to your targeted LinkedIn groups
        </strong>
        . When you click the LinkedIn Share option, check the Post to Groups checkbox and enter an intriguing question as the title.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 490px">
        <img alt="linkedin post" height="508" src="http://cdn.socialmediaexaminer.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/jr-linkedin-actual-posting.jpg" width="480"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Target your message directly from your blog via the LinkedIn Share plugin.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        If you post and share often,
        <strong>
         keep both shared content and your question relevant to each groups discussions
        </strong>
        to avoid looking like spam. When visitors hop over to your blog post or website,
        <strong>
         rely on the conversion tactics you have in place to capture your new leads
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Make sure you have
        <a href="http://help.linkedin.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/1480" target="_blank">
         notifications turned on
        </a>
        so youll know when you get a LinkedIn comment. You dont want to miss a chance to respond.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Conclusion
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Hard sells (e.g., self-serving emails and posts) just dont work on LinkedIn. Comments, likes and direct responses to your content arent permission to pitch. Instead,
        <strong>
         continue the conversation and carefully consider the content you share and how you present it
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Research what each group wants
        </strong>
        , then offer yourself as a resource.
        <strong>
         Ask more questions, make recommendations and offer solutions
        </strong>
        or if you cant help, refer people to someone who can. You can
        <b>
         r
        </b>
        ise above the noise with quality engagement that makes you stand out.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         What do you think? How do you generate leads from LinkedIn? Are you happy with the quality of your interactions? Is there a tactic that has worked well for you?
        </strong>
        Leave a comment with your advice or questions below.
       </p>
