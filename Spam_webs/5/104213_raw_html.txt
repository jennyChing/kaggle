       <h2>
        #1. Cultivate your "circle of safety"
       </h2>
       <div class="CalloutBox" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; box-sizing: border-box">
        <h3>
         <span style="font-size: 23px;">
          <span style="font-weight: normal;">
           The whole purpose of maintaining the Circle of Safety is so that we can invest all our time and energy to guard against the dangers outside. Its the same reason we lock our doors at night." -@simonsinek
          </span>
          <span style="color: rgb(41, 41, 41);text-align: justify;">
           <span style="font-weight: normal;">
            //
           </span>
           <a href="https://www.getflashnotes.com/join/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <b>
             grab the full book summary here
            </b>
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <br/>
        </h3>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         Sineks Circle of Safety tells us that we should focus on helping each other rather than one-upping each another to make
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/time-management/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          management
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         happy. It also tells us that when we know + trust that the people inside the Circle of Safety will look out for us and protect us from the dangers of the outside, were more likely to freely exchange information and ideas that will move the organization forward vs keep things to ourselves because were afraid someone might steal our idea.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         So, whats amongst one of the
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/hooked-how-to-build-habit-forming-products-summary.html">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          biggest habits you can develop
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         in order to cultivate your Circle of Safety?
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         In a word...
         <b>
          EMPATHY.
         </b>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          Empathy is a crucial commodity
         </b>
         these days, and we need leaders like Johnny Bravo in our corporations and companies; in our businesses and organizations To truly lead. Because when people know that their leaders are providing protection from above, they can work hard on the ground, without being concerned about the dangers of the outside. (Empathy = the ability to recognize or share the feelings of another.)
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          - Treat your employees with respect
         </b>
         and dignity and youll see the
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/weight-lifting-benefits.html">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          benefits (as results) in every dimension of your life and business
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         .
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          - A core tenet of workplace management
         </b>
         , human relations, leadership, and basically everything else in life, is that when we feel hopeless and devastated (like many of us do with our work situation) about some area of life, and we feel like we have no control, then the result is rarely positive (at least until we overcome it).
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          AUTONOMY MATTERS.
         </b>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          Lack of control at work = stress
         </b>
         +
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/emotional-intelligence-training.html">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          <b>
           emotional strain
          </b>
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         . One in three employees considered leaving their jobs in 2010-2011 (according reports from consultancy firm Mercer, LLC). This tells us two things:
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <ol>
          <li>
           That there are A LOT of people that want to quit their jobs, and/or
          </li>
          <li>
           People feel like theyve got no other option but to stay put (due to money, family, and insecurities about being unable to find other work)
          </li>
         </ol>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          ENVIRONMENT IS EVERYTHING.
         </b>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          - When the environment at work is
         </b>
         one of encouragement, and one that meets the basic human needs to live, to learn, to feel valued and significant, we do more than just survive 
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/flow.html">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          we thrive.
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         We soar higher than expected. Its the leaderships responsibility to set up the right conditions/environment for this to happen. We do not have the power to change people. We must enhance the environment. Change whether good or bad  is the result of environment. And if we want to see change  we need to focus less on ourselves and more on others/the team/organization/etc
        </span>
       </p>

       <h2 style="text-align: left">
        #2. Get to know your "happy chemicals"
       </h2>
       <div class="CalloutBox" style="box-sizing: border-box">
        <h3>
         <span style="font-size: 23px;font-weight: normal;">
          Some believe we should always put others firstthat if we dont look out for the group, the group wont look out for us. Others believe we should always put ourselves first and that if we dont take care of ourselves first, then we would be of no use to anyone else. The fact is, both are true."- @simonsinek //
         </span>
         <a href="https://www.getflashnotes.com/join/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" rel="nofollow" style="text-align: justify;font-size: 23px;background-color: transparent;" target="_blank">
          <b>
           grab the full book summary here
          </b>
         </a>
        </h3>
       </div>
       <div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight">
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/leaders-eat-last-simon-sinek/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         <img src="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/images/SmileyFaceDeanBokhari.jpg" width="245"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         Based on the quote above, Simon sets the stage for an all out analysis on why we do what we do. And with his background in biology + anthropology, he starts explaining what makes us tick through the
         <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/uncovering-happiness-mindfulness-depression.html">
          <span style="font-size: 17px;">
           four chemicals in our bodies
          </span>
         </a>
         . 80% of the book is based around these four chemicals and how they
         <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/zero-to-one-by-peter-thiel.html">
          <span style="font-size: 17px;">
           drive those of us in leadership roles
          </span>
         </a>
         .
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          Get to know the four happy chemicals in your brain + body &gt;&gt;
         </b>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <ul>
          <li>
           Endorphin: the pain-masking chemical
          </li>
          <li>
           Dopamine: the goal achieving chemical
          </li>
          <li>
           Serotonin: the leadership chemical
          </li>
          <li>
           Oxytocin: the chemical of love
          </li>
         </ul>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          We have four chemicals in our bodies that work towards driving our behavior.
         </b>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         Simon calls the first two Selfish and the second two Selfless.
        </span>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          <b>
           "Selfish" chemicals:
          </b>
          Endorphins and Dopamine. These work to help us
          <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/time-management/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" target="_blank">
           <span style="font-size: 17px;">
            get things done.
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </li>
        <li>
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          <b>
           Selfless chemicals:
          </b>
          Serotonin and Oxytocin. These work to help strengthen our social bonds, fostering connection and collaboration.
         </span>
        </li>
       </ul>

       <p>
        <span style="background-color: transparent;">
         the old command + control model of management  which has not only been proven NOT to work, but has also been shown to spike our egos and get dopamine surging through our bodies.
        </span>
        <span style="background-color: transparent;">
         This often
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/uncovering-happiness-mindfulness-depression.html" style="background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">
         making US feel good
        </a>
        <span style="background-color: transparent;">
         at the expense of others. And thats bad. Really bad. Its so bad that it usually bites us in the butt Whats worse,
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/how-to-wake-up-early-and-workout.html" style="background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">
         Endorphins + Dopamine are highly addictive
        </a>
        <span style="background-color: transparent;">
         .
        </span>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <span style="font-weight: normal;">
         Endorphin [the runner's high]
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <b>
         Ever hear of a 
         <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/how-to-wake-up-early-and-workout.html">
          <b>
           runners high
          </b>
         </a>
         ? Thats just endorphin
        </b>
        surging through your body. Its there to mask the pain were putting our bodies through when we
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/weight-lifting-benefits.html">
         beat up our muscles in the gym
        </a>
        , or run for long periods of time. Endorphin was useful back in the paleolithic era when hunters would go all day long in the grueling heat or freezing cold, hunting for food Endorphin would kick in at just the right time to mask the pain and make us feel good enough to get our bodies to keep moving, and eventually, to capture the prey Its likely that hunter-gathers became addicted to hunting, much like some of us become
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/weight-lifting-benefits.html">
         addicted to weight-lifting
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h3 style="text-align: right">
        <span style="font-size: 25px;">
         <a href="https://www.getflashnotes.com/join/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" rel="nofollow" style="text-align: justify;font-size: 23px;background-color: rgb(252, 255, 156);" target="_blank">
          <span style="font-size: 25px;">
           Diggin' this? Grab the full book summary for $1 &gt;&gt;
          </span>
         </a>
        </span>
       </h3>
       <h3>
        Dopamine [the goal-achieving chemical]
       </h3>
       <div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight">
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" target="_blank" title="image credit-http://pindropmedia.com/">
         <img alt="image credit-http://pindropmedia.com/" src="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/images/target-goal.png" title="image credit-http://pindropmedia.com/" width="286"/>
         <span class="Caption CaptionCenter" style="max-width:286px">
          GET MORE BUSINESS BOOK SUMMARIES LIKE THIS ONE HERE &gt;&gt;
         </span>
        </a>
        <div class="pinit">
         <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="horizontal" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.meaningfulhq.com%2Fleaders-eat-last.html&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.meaningfulhq.com%2Fimages%2Ftarget-goal.png&amp;description=">
          <img border="0" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <b>
         Dopamine is the dangerous chemica
        </b>
        l  but only when abused. Cocaine, nicotine and alcohol all send dopamine surging through your system.. The reason we get addicted to this stuff is because it provides near-instant gratification/pleasure  which, again, is just dopamine making you feel good.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        You know what else sends dopamine surging through your system?
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         A sense of completion
        </b>
        [that extra burst of feel-good vibes that arise when we're approaching the finish line].
       </p>
       <p>
        Achieving
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/how-to-make-new-years-resolutions-stick.html">
         Goals
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        Completing
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/time-management/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" target="_blank">
         Tasks
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        Getting things done...
        <br/>
        <br/>
        The gratification of crossing off that jar of almond butter on your grocery list.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        That awesome feeling you get when you click that little box and see the check-sign show up on your
        <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/evernote/id281796108?mt=8&amp;uo=4&amp;at=11l9yA" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
         favorite productivity app
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Dopamine is that compelling force that makes you feel like you absolutely NEED
        <i>
        </i>
        to reply to that little buzz, bing, or flash coming from your phone, notifying you of that text you just got from Lucy ... and regardless of how many miles over the speed limit you might be driving in -- by golly you'llnudge your thumb towards that little green icon marked 'messages' and reply to that text you just got from Lucy, regardless of much of a drunk it makes you look like on the road, because you just feel uneasy if you don't. This reaction is the result of your good friend dopamine surging through your body in anticipation of achieving some task; which, in this case happens to be reading and replying to that text...
        <i>
         even
        </i>
        if you have to put yourself at risk of getting into an accident to do it.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Dopamine isn't always rational.
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Dopamine was put into place by Mother Nature to incentivize us to move forward with what weve committed ourselves to doing by giving us little bursts or hits of the dopamine chemical whenever we take a small step towards achieving a desired outcome of some sort.
       </p>
       <p>
        The problem though, is that even the good things about dopamine can get addictive.
       </p>
       <p>
        For example, consider your to-do list...
       </p>
       <p>
        Do you add obviously easy tasks to the list just to make it seem like you're getting stuff done? I know I do.
       </p>
       <p>
        I remember going grocery shopping recently with a list of items to get, and as I was strolling down the aisles picking up my goodies, I saw almond butter on the shelf and remembered that I'd actually forgotten to add it to my grocery list... no problem. I grabbed it and put it in my basket -- and then I wrote 'almond butter' down on my grocery list and crossed it out.
       </p>
       <p>
        Why'd I do that? Because I've become addicted to the short burst of dopamine that results from checking tasks off of my to-do list - regardless of whether it actually makes any logical sense for me to do so...
        <i>
         that's
        </i>
        dopamine.
       </p>
       <a href="http://www.audibletrack.com/click.track?CID=102175&amp;AFID=352426&amp;ADID=671645&amp;SID=">
        <img border="0" src="http://www.audibletrack.com/impression.track?CID=102175&amp;AFID=352426&amp;ADID=671645&amp;SID="/>
       </a>
       <h2>
        Serotonin &amp; Oxytocin
       </h2>
       <p>
        Serotonin + Oxytocin are the chemicals of contribution,
        <b>
        </b>
        trust + belonging. When we include these two chemicals in our 4-way cocktail, the result is a healthy (and necessary) synergy that results in more cooperation and more collaboration but those are just poo-poo office words to some people. What about the tangibles, they say. Well, if youre looking for measurable results and tangibles that show up in the numbers  youve got to STEP AWAY from the spreadsheets and the computer screen; youve got to get out of the board room, youve got to do more than blast off a memo here and a memo there  youve got to show em that you care and show em that youre there. Youve got to be there not as a cheerleader for the good times, but as a leader that gives a damn - regardless of the peaks and valleys of the economy.
       </p>
       <h2>
        How Do We Put These Warm &amp; Fuzzy Chemicals To Use?
       </h2>
       <p>
        Sinek tells a great story about a company named
        <a href="http://www.barrywehmiller.com/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
         Barry-Wehmiller
        </a>
        ,and their losing battle against a series of economic blows that sent the company tumbling into a financial crisis they just couldn't shake.
       </p>
       <p>
        Although they were advised to begin instituting massive layoffs to keep their business above ground and help them weather the economic storm - they kept pushing back and refusing to sacrifice their employees in hopes that they could come up with something -
        <i>
         anything
        </i>
        - that could help them survive this financial hardship and continue to provide the members of Barry-Wehmiler with the job-security they deserved.
       </p>
       <p>
        Unfortunately enough, the day eventually came when they were left with no other choice but to start laying people off..
       </p>
       <p>
        Until someone came up with a better idea....
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <i>
          {Side note: do you notice how lay-offs were an absolute last resort with this particular company, as opposed to a quick-fix resolution that CEOs of today jump at without a flinch or hint of hesitation?}
         </i>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        The CEO of Barry-Wehmiller, Bob Champan,
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/decision-making-matrix.html">
         decided
        </a>
        that he wouldn't be going down the same road most of his peers - both past and present - usually tend to go down when face with similar situations, which is a massive onslaught of lay-offs in the name of "balancing the
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
         books
        </a>
        "...This particular CEO however, cared about his people and he decided to demonstrate that by coming up with an idea that would avoid lay-offs -- and even bring the organization closer together in the process.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        <b>
         Want to know what he did?He instituted a furlough.
        </b>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        This means that every employee (CEO included) was required to take four unpaid weeks off from work per year in order to balance the inevitable financial blows that threaten the survival of any thriving business or company. Sounds pretty simple, doesn't it? Everyone takes four weeks of unpaid vacation time and the company becomes stable again. Boom.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        After reading about this furlough idea, I couldn't help but think"Wow. Duh. Why haven't more of us thought of that idea?"
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        And then I realized why it's so important for leaders at every level to read this book --
        <i>
         <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/leaders-eat-last-simon-sinek/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
          Leaders Eat Last
         </a>
        </i>
        is more than just a catchy title filled with inspiring stories -- it's a testament to the few true leaders in the business world today, and a blueprint filled example after example of how these principles of conscious, caring
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/leadership-qualities-list.html">
         leadership can be cultivated
        </a>
        and applied in the modern businesses of today.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: left;">
        Because of leaders like Bob Chapman, who refused to let his people go when his company hit hard times, the people in the company came together as a unified team to see to it that the organization would make it through the tough times and prosper once again.
       </p>
       <p>
        And it all began with one person: the leader.
       </p>
       <h2>
        #3. Become a long-term leader
       </h2>
       <div class="CalloutBox" style="box-sizing: border-box">
        <h3>
         <span style="font-size: 23px;font-weight: normal;">
          "Good leadership is like exercise. We do not see any improvement to our bodies with day-to-day comparisons. In fact, if we only compare the way our bodies look on a given day to how they looked the previous day, we would think our efforts had been wasted. Its only when we compare pictures of ourselves over a period of weeks or months that we can see a stark difference. The impact of leadership is best judged over time." - @simonsinek //
         </span>
         <a href="https://www.getflashnotes.com/join/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" rel="nofollow" style="text-align: justify;font-size: 23px;background-color: transparent;" target="_blank">
          <b>
           grab the full book summary here
          </b>
         </a>
        </h3>
       </div>
       <div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight">
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" target="_blank" title="long-term-leadership">
         <img alt="long-term-leadership" src="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/images/long-term-leader-looking-telescope.jpg" title="long-term-leadership" width="199"/>
         <span class="Caption CaptionCenter" style="max-width:199px">
          get more summaries at: getflashnotes.com
         </span>
        </a>
        <div class="pinit">
         <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="horizontal" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.meaningfulhq.com%2Fleaders-eat-last.html&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.meaningfulhq.com%2Fimages%2Flong-term-leader-looking-telescope.jpg&amp;description=">
          <img border="0" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        I love that little nugget of wisdom Leading for the long-term is a win-win-win for everyone because short-term fixes, though they may seem like the best
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/decision-making-matrix.html">
         decisions
        </a>
        at the time, are nothing more than shortsighted means of immediate gratification. And when so many CEOs only hold their positions for no more than a few years, they base their decisions on their OWN best interest for their OWN short tenure as leader of the organization.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        In other words, when Mr. Joe the CEO leaves his position after only three years  hes more concerned about short-term gain at the cost of the companys
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/weight-lifting-benefits.html">
         long-term health
        </a>
        and prosperity. Its selfish.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Why the "Welch-Way" is the Wrong Way
       </h2>
       <p>
        Sinek solidifies his argument that leading for long-term is better than leading for the short-term by comparing two such leaders of two large organizations  both of which youve probably heard of:
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         The short-term leader: Jack Welch, former CEO of General Electric
        </li>
        <li>
         The long-term leader: Jeff Sinegal, former CEO of Costco
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        Sinek compares GE vs Costco by measuring profits via the rise and fall of their stocks, and then connecting those ebbs and flows to the decisions made by the leadership.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres what he found:
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         GE / Jack Welch / Short-term leadership:
        </b>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Profits at GE were like a roller coaster ride due to irrational decision making without regard for long-term implication
        </li>
        <li>
         Every year Jack would fire the bottom 10% of his managers at GE to balance the books
        </li>
        <li>
         GE did indeed make profit, but it was more like rolling the dice and gambling with lives of employees for selfish gain  this is not strategy, this is a man on high on dopamine
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <b>
         Costco / Jeff Sinegal / Long-term leadership:
        </b>
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         While Welch was striking fear into the hearts of his managers, Jeff Sinegal was concerned about giving his employees a raise  at a time when the US was in an economic crisis Sinegal would say that they should be helping the employees in bad times, not letting them go.
        </li>
        <li>
         Costcos stock was (and still is) stable and predictable  nothing exciting about it, just great, clean, predictable progress, performance and profit when youre the 2nd largest retailer in the country, predictability is better than spontaneity.
        </li>
       </ul>

       <h2>
        Final note
       </h2>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          Its also worth noting
         </b>
         that he used almost every major financial institution as an example of corruption (Merryl Lynch, Wells Fargo, Citi Group, etc)  which I see as no surprise, as most of them are aberrant by design, but we wont get in to that here. Sinek also dives into example after example of how major corporations, like Goldman Sachs lost touch with what originally served as the foundation of their success. This happens when the leadership sets up an environment that is the very opposite of the Circle of Safety, pitting employees against one another, which in turn sets up a scarcity-based me-first
         <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/the-one-thing.html">
          <span style="font-size: 17px;">
           mentality that places priority
          </span>
         </a>
         on profits before people. Bad.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         <b>
          But it wasnt all negative.
         </b>
         In fact, almost every example of bad leadership was contrasted with an example of leadership one can aspire to strive towards becoming. Organizations like
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/next-jump.html">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          NextJump
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         , 3M, and even the Marines were applauded as
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/leadership-qualities-list.html">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          extraordinary examples
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         of cooperation, collaboration, and creativity and they werent just canned stories either. Many of them were stories of leaders (with and without formal authority) who exemplified the character traits, values and ethics that most of our so-called leaders of today could learn a thing or two from.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         Though there werent any new principles introduced in this book  the traditional leadership theories of yesterday were updated with examples and stories that illustrate the reality of workplace conditions today. And most of the principles were delivered with near-flawless impact. The Circle of Safety is very similar to
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/tag/stephen-covey/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" target="_blank">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          Dr. Stephen Coveys ideas
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         , who, as Sinek mentions in the acknowledgements of his first book
         <i>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1591846447/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=1591846447&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=wwwdeansdaily-20&amp;linkId=6FJMJIOG6EUNDYX4" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           <span style="font-size: 17px;">
            Start With Why
           </span>
          </a>
          ,
         </i>
         influenced him quite a bit BUT, if you look at who originally influenced much of
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/tag/stephen-covey/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          Dr. Coveys work
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         , youll find that a great deal of it originates from books written by
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/the-effective-executive/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" target="_blank">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          Peter Drucker
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         . And if you look into who influenced
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.getflashnotes.com/the-effective-executive/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" style="background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);" target="_blank">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          Peter Drucker
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;background-color: transparent;">
         (which I havent), you may find that many of his ideas came about as a result of someone elses influence.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         The bottom line is, that very few ideas are actually new, but
        </span>
        <a href="https://www.getflashnotes.com/join/" onclick="return FIX.track(this);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          the way in which theyre actually presented
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         is often worth reframing for the times. That said, Sinek has done a
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/stand-and-deliver-by-dale-carnegie.html">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          stand up
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
         job at crafting stories based on real-life studies and situations that drive his points into the 
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.meaningfulhq.com/uncovering-happiness-mindfulness-depression.html">
         <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          heads and hearts
         </span>
        </a>
        <span style="font-size: 17px;">
          of the reader they certainly did for me.
        </span>
