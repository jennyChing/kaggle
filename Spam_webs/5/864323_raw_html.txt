&lt;h2&gt;Save the Fuzz(y) is on GoFundMe&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;As I mentioned yesterday in a very hastily written post, our cat, Fuzzy, is ill. We have started a &lt;a href="http://www.gofundme.com/savethefuzzy"&gt;GoFundMe campaign&lt;/a&gt; in the hopes of partially defraying the cost of his care. John and I made a commitment to Fuzzy when we adopted him. He is part of our family and we will care for him as long and as well as we can, just as we promised. He regards us as his parents and his protectors, we are honored to have his love and will prove worthy of his trust.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;John and I have accepted responsibility for Fuzzy&#8217;s medical bills regardless of whether or not anyone is willing or able to help. We&#8217;re also (not so) secretly hoping that folks will share his story with their friends so that we may reach more kindhearted animal lovers with the means and inclination to assist.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;A Little Bit About Fuzzy&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Fuzzy is the cat we refer to as our &#8220;baby kitty&#8221;. He requires a lot of attention, affection, and reassurance. We adopted him when he was about 7 or 8 years old from the Virginia Beach SPCA. He had been surrendered there by his previous caregivers &#8212; likely due in part because of a handful of mild medical issues that they did not want to pursue. For example, Fuzzy has always had a sensitive digestive system that we were able to care for through diet. If he doesn&#8217;t have that, however, then he sometimes experiences what we refer to as &#8220;poo problems&#8221;. He also has seasonal allergies and is prone to upper respiratory infections. Since he was surrendered, too, he has struggled with anxiety &#8212; especially separation anxiety. (Yes, cat&#8217;s get that too.) None of these issues are life threatening, they just mean that he requires a bit more care than what some people are willing to give to a cat. That&#8217;s their loss, though, because Fuzzy returns everything we give him a hundredfold with unconditional love and devotion. He gives us joy.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Fuzzy&#8217;s best friend is our other cat, Buddy. Buddy is another remarkable cat, but in very different ways. They liked one another from the day we brought Fuzzy home, and in the years since they have grown closer and closer. Buddy is more reserved with his affections than Fuzzy, but together they have learned to balance one another out. More importantly, they have come to a point in their relationship where they show one another a great deal of affection, attention, and support. Neither one likes to be far from the other for very long. This has been difficult for them both.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;John and I love Fuzzy and Buddy with all our hearts. I don&#8217;t usually like to say that we love them like they are our children because some people find that to be minimizing of the relationship between human parents and children. The simple fact is, though, that it really isn&#8217;t meant to be. When a bond is very strong between an animal and a person, it is actually an incredibly special thing. It&#8217;s not the same thing, but it&#8217;s the closest thing we can come up with to explain the depth of connection to someone who hasn&#8217;t experienced it. It&#8217;s not about me trying to understand or emulate your relationship with your children &#8212; I can&#8217;t speak to that &#8212; it is about me relating an experience you may not have had in a way that you might be more able to grasp. The closest corollary between the love John and I have for Buddy and Fuzzy is like that of a parent to children &#8212; deep, unconditional, and eternal.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;&lt;a href="http://itrytaph.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Photo-on-11-29-12-at-10.28-AM.jpg"&gt;&lt;img class="aligncenter wp-image-914 size-large" src="http://itrytaph.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Photo-on-11-29-12-at-10.28-AM-1024x683.jpg" alt="Save the Fuzzy" width="900" height="600" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Medical Background&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Fuzzy is currently in the BluePearl animal hospital in Eden Prairie, MN. Over the weekend he was with their associated emergency clinic (same location). This clinic is about a 2 hour drive from where we live. The reason he is there is because none of the Veterinarians in our medium sized city have either the diagnostic tools necessary to determine the cause of Fuzzy&#8217;s illness or the ability to provide the 24/7 medical monitoring that is in his best interests. I deeply appreciate that our local vets care so much about Fuzzy (and all their patients) to know and immediately tell us when the situation progressed beyond their capabilities as well as refer us to another clinic.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Here&#8217;s the situation:&lt;br /&gt;
Fuzzy has always been a good eater. In fact, he&#8217;s struggled a lot more with NOT eating so much. That is, until recently. Starting in about Februarywe noticed that he wasn&#8217;t eating as much. He was still eating his food fine, but he wasn&#8217;t also trying to steal Buddy&#8217;s food &#8212; which was a change. He had an upper respiratory infection prior to that and we mostly chalked up his lesser appetite to that. When it continued into March, however, we made the decision to err on the side of caution and took him back to the vet to have his teeth checked. Sure enough, he had two teeth that needed to be extracted. His dental surgery was the beginning of April.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Following his dental surgery Fuzzy seemed to be back to his old self for the rest of the month. Oh, he still wasn&#8217;t really going after Buddy&#8217;s food but we figured that was largely because we had changed their feeding set-up a bit to make things more challenging and because he had lost some weight before the surgery and now had fewer calorie requirements. He was still eating his own food well,so we just decided to keep a watchful eye on him.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In the last couple of weeks, though, he stopped cleaning his plate. This time last week he started skipping some meals. He also just wasn&#8217;t seeming as &#8220;Fuzzy-ish&#8221; to me. On Thursday he didn&#8217;t leave us his usual stinky gift and really only ate one of his meals (he usually gets a can split into 3 meals). I was concerned, but knew he could just be a little off and it could resolve itself. By Friday morning, though, things just felt WRONG somehow. He ate part of his breakfast, but he just seemed unenthusiastic about everything. He wasn&#8217;t really Fuzzy-ing in his usual way&#8230; so I got him an appointment with our local vet. From here, I will tell the story via a timeline:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Timeline&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Friday:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
Vet appointment. Blood was taken for a standard check as well as a screening for pancreatitis. Fuzzy wasgiven an injection of an anti-nausea medication to help settle any stomach issues he may have had and stimulate his appetite a bit. When we got home he finished his breakfast with gusto. Later that evening, though, he was back to picking at his food.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Saturday:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
Fuzzy didn&#8217;t get up from the chair he was sitting on when I came out of our bedroom. This is rare as he usually greets us every morning. I dished out his food and brought it to him on his chair. He ate a bit of it, but not much. He seemed lethargic &#8212; like he wasn&#8217;t feeling at all well. He tried to get up and walk around a bit, I think to ease my fears, but he wasn&#8217;t really up to taking more than a few steps. I called our vet again to see if I could get him seen again during their Saturday morning hours. One of his vets called me back and told me that his blood work all came back within normal levels, but that she agreed that it sounded like something was wrong. Unfortunately none of our local clinics have ultrasound equipment, which was what would really be necessary in order to even begin to figure out what might be up. She referred us to BluePearl/AEVS (Affiliated Emergency Veterinary Service).&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;At AEVS the doctor was able to do a rapidultrasound and tell us that Fuzzy had a small left kidney, air in his stomach, and some inflammation in his side. The Internal Medicine Vet was due to be in on Sunday morning and he recommended that Fuzzy stay overnight for observation and to get to see the other doctor for a more comprehensive ultrasound the next morning. We agreed that was probably the best option for Fuzzy and went ahead and made the difficult decision to leave him there overnight.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Sunday:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
Fuzzy had the more in depth ultrasound performed by the specialist. She found a thickened gall bladder wall &amp; enlarged bile duct with no gall bladder obstruction &#8212; she thought that it was probably what is known as &lt;a href="http://www.vet.cornell.edu/FHC/health_resources/Cholangiohepatitis.cfm"&gt;cholangiohepatitis&lt;/a&gt;and likely caused by some type of infection of the gall bladder/liver. She also found some mild swelling of his abdominallymph nodes and intestines measuring at the high end of normal. This could, again, potentially be attributed to an infection. The small kidney was also still an issue and indicated the early stages of kidney disease.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;All of these things are treatable or manageable with medication and home care, so we returned to Eden Prairie, got our little guy, and brought him home. He seemed to be in good spirits on the trip home. Unfortunately, he soon fell into a funk and didn&#8217;t do much more than walk back and forth between two rooms and stare off into the middle distance at nothing in particular. After researching all of his medications I found out that, in rare cases, some cats respond unfavorably to the appetite stimulant they had given him and tried to give him some space. At the same time, however, we were trying to entice him to eat something. Anything. We offered him no less than 6 different types of food including canned foods, a pouch of food, shredded chicken that we had baked fresh for him, and even chicken baby food. We even got a bit desperate and tried to give him some kibble (he always liked it, but it&#8217;s never set well with him and, given his kidney issue, it&#8217;s not something that we ever intend to let him have again). He didn&#8217;t want any of it. Not at all. He also didn&#8217;t drink anything. John and I were up with him most of the night, check up, seeing if he had eaten anything and making sure that he didn&#8217;t suddenly take a turn for the worse.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Monday:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
When Monday morning rolled around we called BluePearl and let them know that we were returning because Fuzzy hadn&#8217;t eaten or drunk anything. That meant that he hadn&#8217;t had any fluids since he left them the day before and hadn&#8217;t eaten anything since that little bit on Saturday morning. It&#8217;s not commonly known that if cats go more than 24 hours without eating they are putting a good deal of strain on their liver. If they go 48 hours they can do permanent damage. Considering his liver was already compromised we couldn&#8217;t risk him going any longer without eating. Also, considering his kidney issue we didn&#8217;t want him to go long without fluids. So we all decided that it was in his best interests to have a &lt;a href="http://catinfo.org/?link=feedingtubes"&gt;feeding tube&lt;/a&gt; placed and be given fluids intravenously. He would have to spend the night again. Luckily we would be able to see an internal medicine doctor the next day as well.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;By this point John and I were completely exhausted so we decided to spend the night in one of the nearby hotels. Luckily John got us a good deal on a room with a kitchenette so we were able to get some pasta, make and eat dinner, and get to sleep.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Tuesday:&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
Fuzzy did well overnight. He didn&#8217;t mind his feeding tube much and he was similarly okay with his iv catheter. We were able to visit him and see the specialist. We talked about the fact that we still didn&#8217;t really know the cause of his refusal to eat. She recommended that we do another ultrasound to see if things had gotten any worse and determine if the abdominallymph nodes were getting any worse &#8212; something that would indicate potential cancer, which would then require further inspection. She also recommended that we do a endoscopy &#8212; putting a tube with a camera through his digestive tract. This test was to see if he might have &lt;a href="http://www.vet.cornell.edu/FHC/health_resources/brochure_ibd.cfm"&gt;inflammatory bowel disease&lt;/a&gt;, or anything else out of the ordinary (IBD is a manageable condition with diet and, as necessary, medication).&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Fuzzy would need to spend another night, so John and I returned home to check on Buddy and try to get some rest. We&#8217;ve heard back from the doctor about the results of the ultrasound. Luckily the issues with the lymph nodes seems to be resolving itself so there was no need to further investigate the lymph nodes (i.e. no reason to suspect cancer there, yay!). The endoscopy is scheduled for first thing tomorrow morning. We hope it will give us some more definitive answers and allow us to decide what our next steps will be.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&nbsp;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;So far we&#8217;ve spent a little under $6,000 on tests and care for Fuzzy. That is, obviously, a financial burden. All indications, however, are that his health could be managed with a combination of specialized diet and some medications. With these things he could be a very happy and healthy kitty for many years to come.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;That is the full background. If you&#8217;ve read all of this postthen you are quite dedicated and I thank you. If you&#8217;ve skimmed it, that&#8217;s still pretty impressive.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;If you are interested in helping via a donation, &lt;a href="http://www.gofundme.com/savethefuzzy"&gt;please visit the GoFundMe page&lt;/a&gt; and give whatever you feel comfortable with. Again, Fuzzy will be cared for regardless, but the more help we get from kind folks like you, the less crushing debt we&#8217;ll have. We&#8217;re also planning to sell some things (anyone looking to buy a sweet monitor?), and I&#8217;m also still in the job huntand pursuing freelance writing opportunities.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;We appreciate every donation, and every dollar counts. If that just isn&#8217;t something you can do for financial or other reasons, though, we understand and hope that you will consider sharing the campaign with your networks anyway.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Thank you so much!&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Further updates on Fuzzy&#8217;s progress are available via the &lt;a href="http://www.gofundme.com/savethefuzzy"&gt;GoFundMe Campaign Page&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
