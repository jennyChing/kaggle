            <h2>
             <b>
              Shwedagon Pagoda, Yangon
             </b>
            </h2>
            <div class="wpgeo_map wp_geo_map" id="wpgeo_map_4997_2" style="width:100%;height:300px;">
            </div>
            <p>
             Approaching the Shwedagon Pagoda, up one of four long wide and covered staircases, is like trying to reach Nirvana itself. It takes time (ok, only about five minutes), patience (there are a lot of stairs that seem to be neverending) and good karma (by trying to politely decline all the offers to sell you a souvenir).
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-52_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11402" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-52_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-51_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11403" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-51_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             But, like entry to the holy eternity, it is worth it. Because here in the middle of Yangon, Myanmars biggest city, is the breathtaking centre of the countrys Buddhist belief.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-62_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11399" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-62_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Shwedagon Pagoda is at least a thousand years old  although legend says it has actually been around for much longer. Over the centuries it has gradually been extended and repaired and its now 99 metres tall. The (actual) gold plates on the exterior catch the suns rays and the whole complex glows around you. At the very tip of the stupa is a 76 carat diamond.
            </p>
            <p>
             The stupa is the focus and each of the compass points is an altar where people stop to pray. But circling the base are dozens more small statues of Buddha and shrines where people gather to light incense and wash the figurine.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-42_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11406" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-42_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-53_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11401" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-53_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Shwedagon dominates the skyline around much of Yangon and all you can see is the tall golden spire. But up the top here now, I appreciate that theres much more. Its like a large religious complex with maybe forty or fifty other small buildings around the outskirts.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-57_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11400" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-57_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: left;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-68_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11398" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-68_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: right;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <div style="clear: both;">
            </div>
            <p>
             Some of the pavilions have more statues of Buddha in various poses, others have monks sitting in quiet contemplation, or sometimes families are just relaxing in the shade and having something to eat. The sounds of a choir lure me to one shrine where I stop for a while and enjoy their melodies.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-46_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11404" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-46_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: left;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-45_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11405" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-45_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: right;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <div style="clear: both;">
            </div>
            <p>
             Foreigners pay five dollars for entry but the pagoda is, naturally, free for locals. For them, it seems to be about more than just simple religion. It is also a bit of a sanctuary from the chaos of the Yangon streets below.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-72_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11396" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-72_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <h2>
             <b>
              Special tip!
             </b>
            </h2>
            <p>
             If youre planning to visit yourself, Ive got a little inside tip for you. Most people will go and see the pagoda and then go on their merry way. But at the base of the southern staircase is another pagoda that nobody seems to know about it. Its called the Maha Wizaya Pagoda and its quite unique because the stupa is hollow. But its not just hollow  it has the most incredible design inside to make it look like a forest and a ceiling which shows the constellations of the sky.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-34_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11409" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-34_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-36_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11408" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-36_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: left;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-37_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="shwedagon pagoda, yangon, myanamar, largest buddhist paya in myanmar, burma, things to do in yangon" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-11407" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Myanmar-2013-37_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: right;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <div style="clear: both;">
            </div>
            <p>
             I was the only foreigner there when I wandered up to have a look. When I tried to inconspicuously take a photo inside, a man saw me and stood up from his mat. I thought he was going to come and berate me but he actually when to turn on the lights in the ceiling so I could get a better shot.
            </p>
