       8 Tips to Speed Up Windows 7
      </a>
     </h2>
     <div class="titleinfo">
      Filed under
      <a href="http://www.quertime.com/category/info-news/" rel="category tag">

      <p>
       Over time computers seem to get slower, even with new processors like
       <a href="http://www.quertime.com/tag/windows/">
        Windows
       </a>
       7 built in to them for speed and efficiency. This is due to the junk that accumulates on your hard drive and unnecessary programs that run in the background. Factory settings dont do well as peopleuse more of their computers memory and hard drive.
      </p>
      <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->

      <p>
       There are eight changes you can make in your computer settings to
       <strong>
        speed up your Windows 7 processor
       </strong>
       .
       <span id="more-21484">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Troubleshoot Performance
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/troubleshoot_performance.jpg">
        <img alt="troubleshoot_performance" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21490" height="230" src="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/troubleshoot_performance.jpg" width="468"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       This first step you should take when trying to make Windows 7 faster is troubleshoot the computers performance. PC Mag says there could be something specific that is slowing down your computer. To do this, you first need to open Control Panel and type troubleshooting in the search box. Go to System and Security and you will find the option to Check for performance issues. Selecting this option could find the source of your slow-down problems.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Install Anti-Virus Software
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Malware or viruses could be the problem to your slow computer. Installing an anti-virus software would prevent future malware from settling in your hard drive. You can set up the program to automatically scan for viruses every day or you can manually scan and remove malware. There are many quality free options, such as Panda Cloud Antivirus, as well as paid software like Spyware Doctor with Antivirus.
      </p>
      <p>
       If youve already contracted a virus on your computer and nothing has helped to take it off, perhaps searching for
       <a href="http://shop.lenovo.com/us/en/landingpage/promotions/ideapad/weekly-sale/" target="_blank">
        top laptop deals
       </a>
       is in your future. Just remember to get anti-virus software next time so you dont run into this problem again.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Perform Disk Cleanup
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/perform_disk_cleanup.jpg">
        <img alt="perform_disk_cleanup" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21492" height="291" src="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/perform_disk_cleanup.jpg" width="468"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Temp files, downloaded program files, and your recycle bin are examples of files taking up your computers memory that arent needed. By performing a disk cleanup, these files areremoved easily. You can find Disk Cleanup by going to your Start menu then selecting All Programs, accessories, and System Tools. Check all files for the most space, but it would be a good idea to read through the options if you want to keep some.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Run Disk Defragment
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/run_disk_defragment.jpg">
        <img alt="run_disk_defragment" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21493" height="370" src="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/run_disk_defragment.jpg" width="468"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Some programs and other data have portions that arent needed yet saved on your computer. This information is inone chunk, and running a disk defragmentwill get rid of the unnecessary parts. Windows 7 has the disk defragment run at a scheduled time, usually in the middle of the night. However, if the laptop is not on at that time, the defragmentdoesnt run. You can either manually run the defragment or you can set it up for a time that you know your computer will be on everyday.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Remove Unused Programs
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/remove_unused_programs.jpg">
        <img alt="remove_unused_programs" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21494" height="329" src="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/remove_unused_programs.jpg" width="468"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Sometimes, you install a program that you dont end up using very much. That program still uses up space on your computer and will cause it to operate slower. Open the Control Panel and select Uninstall a Program to see a list of all programs on your computer. You can choose which ones you do not use. Select Turn Windows Features On or Off to see a list of programs that came with your computer.
      </p>
      <p>
       From there, you can remove unneeded ones. Before removing anything youre not sure of, Google search to see what the program does. Some are essential for your computer to work and removing the program would cause complications.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Disable Unnecessary Programs from Startup
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/disable_unnecessary_programs_from_startup.jpg">
        <img alt="disable_unnecessary_programs_from_startup" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21495" height="312" src="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/disable_unnecessary_programs_from_startup.jpg" width="468"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Your computer automatically allows certain programs to run at startup. Many arent needed at startup and are disabled so they dont slow down your computer while its booting. To do this, type msconfig in the search bar in your Start menu. Click the Startup tab and youll see the list of programs that run. Many are important to get your computer going, but many like QuikTime or GoogleUpdate are unnecessary.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you dont recognize a few, type their name into processlibrary.com to figure out what they are. Some could be malware that you need to remove completely from your computer.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Turn Off Sound Effects
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/turn_off_sound_effects.jpg">
        <img alt="turn_off_sound_effects" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21496" height="321" src="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/turn_off_sound_effects.jpg" width="468"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Something as small as disabling sound effects can speed up your computer, according to Hongkiat.com. In your start menu, search mmsys.cpl and the sound window will pull up. Under the Sounds tab, set the scheme to No sounds. You can also disable the Play Windows Startup sound option.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Stop Search Indexing
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/stop_search_indexing.jpg">
        <img alt="stop_search_indexing" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21497" height="332" src="http://www.quertime.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/stop_search_indexing.jpg" width="468"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       TechRepublic says that Windows 7 search can slow down the systems performance. To turn it off, search index in the Start search and click Indexing Options. Select Modify to remove locations and file types being indexed.
      </p>
      <p>
       Following these tips should definitely speed up your Windows 7 computer.
       <br/>
       (Image Source: Flickr.com)
      </p>
