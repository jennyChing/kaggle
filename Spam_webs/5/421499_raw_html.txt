     <p>
      A
      <b>
       pistol sword
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Sword" title="Sword">
       sword
      </a>
      with a
      <a href="/wiki/Pistol" title="Pistol">
       pistol
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Revolver" title="Revolver">
       revolver
      </a>
      attached, usually alongside the blade. It differs from a
      <a href="/wiki/Rifle" title="Rifle">
       rifle
      </a>
      with a
      <a href="/wiki/Bayonet" title="Bayonet">
       bayonet
      </a>
      in that the weapon is designed primarily for use as a sword, and the firearm component is typically considered a secondary weapon designed to be an addition to the blade, rather than the sword being a secondary addition to the pistol. In addition, the two components of these weapons typically cannot be separated, unlike most bayonet-fixed rifles.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pistol_sword&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Historically, some
      <a href="/wiki/Flintlock" title="Flintlock">
       flintlock
      </a>
      pistols of the 17th and 18th centuries were constructed as gun-swords, with the barrel of the pistol attached to the side of the blade of a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shortsword" title="Shortsword">
       shortsword
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Dagger" title="Dagger">
       dagger
      </a>
      . A shell guard protected the firing mechanism when it was used as a sword. These were used by French and German hunters to kill wounded
      <a href="/wiki/Wild_boar" title="Wild boar">
       wild boar
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Examples of these weapons can be found in the armoury of
      <a href="/wiki/Wawel_Castle" title="Wawel Castle">
       Wawel Castle
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Krak%C3%B3w" title="Krakw">
       Krakw
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">
       Poland
      </a>
      ). Similar weapons were made in India,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      including the
      <a href="/wiki/Katar_(dagger)" title="Katar (dagger)">
       Katar
      </a>
      (), a thrusting
      <a href="/wiki/Dagger" title="Dagger">
       dagger
      </a>
      with two built-in pistols that could be fired by squeezing the bars of the handgrip together.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Military_use">
       Military use
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pistol_sword&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Military use">

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Elgin_cutlass_pistol.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="652" data-file-width="1818" height="79" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Elgin_cutlass_pistol.jpg/220px-Elgin_cutlass_pistol.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Elgin_cutlass_pistol.jpg/330px-Elgin_cutlass_pistol.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Elgin_cutlass_pistol.jpg/440px-Elgin_cutlass_pistol.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Elgin_cutlass_pistol.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Elgin Cutlass Pistol at The
        <a href="/wiki/Mariners%27_Museum" title="Mariners' Museum">
         Mariners' Museum
        </a>
        in
        <a href="/wiki/Newport_News,_Virginia" title="Newport News, Virginia">
         Newport News, Virginia
        </a>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
         <a href="#cite_note-3">
          <span>
           [
          </span>
          3
          <span>
           ]
          </span>

      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In 1838, the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">
       United States Navy
      </a>
      developed the .54 caliber, single-shot smoothbore Elgin pistol, which was equipped with an 11.5-inch
      <a href="/wiki/Bowie_knife" title="Bowie knife">
       Bowie knife
      </a>
      blade
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and was intended for use by boarding parties; it was the first
      <a href="/wiki/Percussion_cap" title="Percussion cap">
       percussion cap
      </a>
      gun in naval service,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but only 150 were made. The Navy specifically intended them for the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Exploring_Expedition" title="United States Exploring Expedition">
       Wilkes-South Seas expedition
      </a>
      . Reportedly, in 1840 a naval landing party used the pistol to good effect when Fijian warriors attacked the sailors on the island of
      <a href="/wiki/Malolo" title="Malolo">
       Malolo
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A few Elgin pistols were still in use during the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Civil_War" title="US Civil War">
       US Civil War
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but proved unpopular. The Navy quickly replaced them with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M1860_Cutlass" title="M1860 Cutlass">
       M1860 Cutlass
      </a>
      , which remained in service until the 1940s. Some found their way into civilian hands and some ended up in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_West" title="Old West">
       Old West
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2008)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinfire" title="Pinfire">
       Pinfire
      </a>
      cartridge gun-swords were produced in Belgium during the mid-19th century, although in limited quantity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pinfireswords_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pinfireswords-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      These custom-made weapons were sometimes used by European officers and featured a loading gate behind the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basket_hilt" title="Basket hilt">
       basket hilt
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pinfireswords_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-pinfireswords-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1866 T Rauh of Solingen filed a United States
      <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">
       patent
      </a>
      on the design of a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/9mm_caliber" title="9mm caliber">
       9mm caliber
      </a>
      pistol sword with a 32in blade.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pinfireswords_8-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-pinfireswords-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      During
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">
       World War I
      </a>
      , the British manufactured a limited number of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Webley_revolver" title="Webley revolver">
       Webley revolvers
      </a>
      with folding blades, similar in design to the Pritchard
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pistol_bayonet" title="Pistol bayonet">
       pistol bayonet
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      These were used by officers in the trenches for close quarters fighting as the confined space made it difficult to use a sword. However, few were produced due to the expense and scarcity of raw materials.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A rare variant of the
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      Japanese
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nambu_automatic_pistol" title="Nambu automatic pistol">
       Nambu automatic pistol
      </a>
      was a pistol sword. It is possible that this non-regulation weapon was privately purchased by an officer as only one example is known to exist.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Civilian_use">
       Civilian use
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pistol_sword&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Civilian use">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Another notable example of a pistol sword was the Swedish 1865
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cutlass_Pistol" title="Cutlass Pistol">
       Cutlass Pistol
      </a>
      ; 500 were ordered by the government and issued to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prison_guard" title="Prison guard">
       prison guards
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was a breech-loading 2 shot weapon with a 14in by 2in blade weighing 2.5lb. A few ended up on the other side of the Atlantic and one became part of
      <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bill" title="Buffalo Bill">
       Buffalo Bill
      </a>
      's gun collection.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arnow_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-arnow-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the late
      <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">
       Victorian era
      </a>
      , some French
      <a href="/wiki/Swordstick" title="Swordstick">
       swordsticks
      </a>
      had built-in pinfire
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pepperbox_revolver" title="Pepperbox revolver">
       pepperbox revolvers
      </a>
      to increase their lethality; these were carried by civilians for self-defence. However this idea was far from new; combination swordsticks and wheel lock pistols have been in use since the 16th century.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arnow_14-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-arnow-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Disadvantages">
       Disadvantages
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pistol_sword&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Disadvantages">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Pistol swords were not widely used and became uncommon relatively quickly, due to their expense and because instead of getting two weapons in one, one got a heavy pistol and a heavy, off-balance sword, as shown by the poor performance of the Elgin pistol.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Modern versions occasionally appear on the market, however, as novelties or collectors' items, including the Sierra Madre knife pistol.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Similar_weapons">
       Similar weapons
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pistol_sword&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Similar weapons">

     <ul>
      <li>
       Edged weapons with built-in pistols were common in Eastern Europe. The flintlock axe pistol was a trademark
       <a href="/wiki/Polish_cavalry" title="Polish cavalry">
        Polish cavalry
       </a>
       weapon from the 16th until the 18th century. Similar guns were made in Hungary and a multi-barreled version was invented in Germany.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ricketts_17-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ricketts-17">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         17
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       Axe pistols were also issued to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_navy" title="Swedish navy">
        Swedish navy
       </a>
       in the early 18th century.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       Some
       <a href="/wiki/Linstock" title="Linstock">
        linstocks
       </a>
       of the Renaissance and late medieval period had a matchlock pistol concealed in the blade.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ricketts_17-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ricketts-17">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         17
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>

        Henry VIII
       </a>
       's bodyguards were equipped with iron
       <a href="/wiki/Round_shield" title="Round shield">
        round shields
       </a>
       fitted with a pistol. The English also combined pistols with
       <a href="/wiki/Mace_(club)" title="Mace (club)">
        maces
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         18
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>

       <a href="/wiki/Morning_star_(weapon)" title="Morning star (weapon)">
        morning star
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         19
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       The king would carry it while walking through the city at night to check up on the constables.

        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         20
         <span>

       <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_London" title="Tower of London">
        Tower of London
       </a>

        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         21
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       In the late 19th century Paris street gangs carried
       <a href="/wiki/Apache_revolver" title="Apache revolver">
        Apache revolvers
       </a>
       which were a combination of knife, revolver and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knuckleduster" title="Knuckleduster">
        knuckleduster
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         22
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       Knife pistols with folding blades were popular in England during the mid Victorian era. These were made by Unwin and Rodgers, used
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_powder" title="Black powder">
        black powder
       </a>
       and were available in various small calibers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frost_23-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Frost-23">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         23
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       Like the modern
       <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Army_knife" title="Swiss Army knife">
        Swiss Army knife
       </a>
       they contained a variety of tools, from blades to corkscrews, and were often used by sailors. A modern version that fires
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/.22_caliber" title=".22 caliber">
        .22 caliber
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Rimfire_ammunition" title="Rimfire ammunition">
        rimfire
       </a>
       cartridges is still in production.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       Belgian gunsmiths made revolvers with
       <a href="/wiki/Bowie_knife" title="Bowie knife">
        Bowie knife
       </a>
       blades, some of which were used by French officers during the
       <a href="/wiki/Crimean_War" title="Crimean War">
        Crimean War
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Franco-Prussian_War" title="Franco-Prussian War">
        Franco-Prussian War
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frost_23-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Frost-23">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         23
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       In modern times
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KA-BAR" title="KA-BAR">
        KA-BAR
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=LASERLYTE&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="LASERLYTE (page does not exist)">
        LASERLYTE
       </a>
       have teamed up to produce a Pistol Bayonet for today's tactical market.
      </li>
      <li>
       French Tactical Security Instructor, Firearms and Martial Arts expert
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeff_Thenier" title="Jeff Thenier">
        Jeff Thenier
       </a>
       designed a pistol shaped tactical folding knife called the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=P001&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="P001 (page does not exist)">

       </a>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/STI_KNIVES" title="STI KNIVES">

      </li>
