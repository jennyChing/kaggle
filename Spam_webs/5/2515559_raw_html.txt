         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Comfort and Mens Wedding Rings
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          <span class="date">
           <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-06-13T10:58:36+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
            June 13, 2014
           </time>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          Many men consider comfort first when choosing a wedding ring. This is because, for a great number of men, their wedding ring will be their only ring, and perhaps the only jewelry they wear. Since the ring will be worn frequently, if not all the time, it needs to feel right. Some men like the weight of a heavier ring; others want to forget they are wearing a ring at all.
         </p>
         <p>
          Mens wedding rings, like womens wedding rings, are available in many different metals and designs. Gold, either yellow or white, is the most traditional choice, with platinum becoming more prized of late for its intrinsic value. Platinum is heavier than gold, however, and considerably more expensive.
         </p>
         <p>
          For men with lesser jewelry budgets, both titanium and tungsten work without compromising on comfort, durability or attractiveness. Titanium is the hardest natural metal in the world, so a titanium wedding ring is strong  much more bend, dent, and scratch resistant than gold, platinum (or silver). Most jewelry grade titanium can be cut off in the event of a medical emergency, but it cant be resoldered, so resizing is not an option.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tungsten is also very durable and scratch-proof. It is also heavier and has a permanent polish. Unfortunately, tungsten cannot be cut and resoldered, so tungsten rings have to be properly measured and ordered, and the mans ring size must stay relatively constant in order for a tungsten ring to continue to fit properly.
         </p>
         <p>
          Historically, many wedding rings were made of silver, but silver is a much softer metal than even gold or platinum and so it is much more easily bent or dented. Also, silver tends to tarnish, so it must be polished and maintained in order to look its best. Most jewelers do not recommend silver jewelry for everyday wear.
         </p>
         <p>
          Comfort-fit styles are available for all types of rings. A comfort-fit ring is one in which slightly more metal has been added to the inside of the ring to make it arced or domed. This arcing makes it easier for men to slide the ring off and on over larger knuckles, and it feels more comfortable than a standard-fit ring on the finger. Choosing the comfort-fit option in gold and platinum will add the cost of a ring, but will pay off over time as you enjoy wearing it.
         </p>
         <p>
          No matter the material of the ring or the fit style, there are many options for the look of the ring, from plain and traditional to carved and inlaid. Gems can be added for a more striking look. Take your time choosing your wedding ring, gentlemen. Its an important piece of jewelry and will be a significant part of your future wardrobe. Of course, if you find that your wedding ring is not what you wanted or not what feels right, Premier Jewelers can help you resize or redesign it so it fits you and your style the way that it should.
         </p>
