     <p>
      <b>
       Black Talon
      </b>
      is a brand of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollow-point_ammunition" title="Hollow-point ammunition">
       hollow-point
      </a>
      pistol and rifle
      <a href="/wiki/Ammunition" title="Ammunition">
       ammunition
      </a>
      introduced in 1991 by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winchester_Ammunition" title="Winchester Ammunition">
       Winchester
      </a>
      , primarily intended for law enforcement and personal defense use. Black Talon rounds were known for the unique construction of the bullet and its sharp petal shape after expansion following impact with tissue or other wet media.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Black Talon ammunition was produced in the following calibers:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/9mm_Luger" title="9mm Luger">
       9mm Luger
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/10mm_Auto" title="10mm Auto">
       10mm Auto
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/.40_S%26W" title=".40 S&amp;W">
       .40 S&amp;W
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/.45_ACP" title=".45 ACP">
       .45 ACP
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/.357_Magnum" title=".357 Magnum">
       .357 Magnum
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/.44_Magnum" title=".44 Magnum">
       .44 Magnum
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/.300_Winchester_Magnum" title=".300 Winchester Magnum">
       .300 Winchester Magnum
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/.308_Winchester" title=".308 Winchester">
       .308 Winchester
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/.338_Winchester_Magnum" title=".338 Winchester Magnum">
       .338 Winchester Magnum
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/.30-06_Springfield" title=".30-06 Springfield">
       .30-06 Springfield
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
       Overview
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Talon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Black Talon handgun
      <a href="/wiki/Bullet" title="Bullet">
       bullet
      </a>
      is a jacketed
      <a href="/wiki/Hollow-point_bullet" title="Hollow-point bullet">
       hollow-point bullet
      </a>
      with perforations designed to expose sharp edges upon
      <a href="/wiki/Expanding_bullet" title="Expanding bullet">
       expansion
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taxdebate_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-taxdebate-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The bullet included a
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lubalox&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lubalox (page does not exist)">
       Lubalox
      </a>
      coating, a proprietary oxide process.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Though widely misreported to be
      <a href="/wiki/Teflon-coated_bullet" title="Teflon-coated bullet">
       Teflon
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Molybdenum_disulfide" title="Molybdenum disulfide">
       molybdenum disulfide
      </a>
      , or wax, the ammo has an unusual black appearance compared to other copper-jacketed or lead bullets. The black appearance was due to the oxidized copper jacket. This Lubalox coating was to protect the barrel
      <a href="/wiki/Rifling" title="Rifling">
       rifling
      </a>
      , and did not give the bullet armor-piercing capabilities. This coating is still widely used on many of Winchester's rifle bullets today.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The bullet was designed in 1991 under the supervision of Alan Corzine, who at that time was VP of research and development for Winchester.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The round quickly developed a reputation as a very effective expanding bullet. The alleged armor-piercing (AP) notion is urban legend, and was a result of media hyperbole (perhaps confusing "Talon" with the "Teflon" that coated KTW AP bullets)rather than test or field data from actual shootings considering the short time it was on the market. Col Leonard J. Supenski of the Baltimore County police department said "It has the
      <a href="/wiki/Stopping_power" title="Stopping power">
       stopping power
      </a>
      that police officers need and it is less likely to ricochet or go through the bad guy,"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taxdebate_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-taxdebate-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      something that could be said of any reliably expanding hollow-point. Despite its unique design, the Black Talon was found to be comparable in performance to conventional hollow-points.
     </p>
     <p>
      The first shooting which brought critical media attention to Black Talon was by Gian Luigi Ferri, who used Black Talon and other ammunition to shoot 15 people at a San Francisco law office in 1993; nine of whom died (the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/101_California_Street_shootings" title="101 California Street shootings">
       101 California Street shootings
      </a>
      ). Dr. Boyd Stevens, the San Francisco Medical Examiner who performed the autopsies, later reported at the 1994 International Wound Ballistics Association's conference in Sacramento that the wound trauma caused by Black Talons was unremarkable and that all victims died due to penetration of a vital structure.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The rifle version did not share the handgun bullet's barbed petal design and was basically a solid copper hollow-point with a hollowed out rear shank containing a lead core in a steel liner to prevent jacket rupture thus maintaining necessary penetration in tough game. This ammunition line was renamed Fail Safe.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Response">
       Response
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Talon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Response">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The ammunition was decried by those opposed to civilian ownership of handguns.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Some medical personnel were concerned that the sharp barb like tips could potentially cause tears in the surgical gloves and hands of the medical workers, exposing them to greater risk of infection,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taxdebate_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-taxdebate-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      however there are no documented reports of this actually happening.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Most jacketed bullet designs that expand expose sharp edges or corners.
     </p>
     <p>
      The ammunition was used by the gunman of the 1993
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colin_Ferguson_(convict)" title="Colin Ferguson (convict)">
       Long Island Railroad mass murder
      </a>
      for his
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruger_P_series" title="Ruger P series">
       Ruger P-89 9mm pistol
      </a>
      . In 1996 a lawsuit was subsequently filed by one of the victim's family members (
      <i>
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=McCarthy_v._Sturm,_Ruger_and_Co.,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="McCarthy v. Sturm, Ruger and Co., Inc. (page does not exist)">
        McCarthy v. Sturm, Ruger and Co., Inc.
       </a>
      </i>
      , 916 F.Supp. 366 (S.D.N.Y., 1996)) claiming that Olin Corp. should be liable for the shooting spree based on the design, manufacture, marketing, and sale of Black Talon ammunition. The claims against Olin were dismissed because it was held that the bullets "functioned exactly as designed".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1993 Winchester removed the ammunition from public sale,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but at no time was Black Talon ammunition uniformly prohibited by US law. Winchester's Ranger SXT line of ammunition is of the same basic design.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Current_status">
       Current status
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Talon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Current status">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Winchester_Black_Talon_9mm_(detail).jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Winchester_Black_Talon_9mm_%28detail%29.jpg/220px-Winchester_Black_Talon_9mm_%28detail%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Winchester_Black_Talon_9mm_%28detail%29.jpg/330px-Winchester_Black_Talon_9mm_%28detail%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Winchester_Black_Talon_9mm_%28detail%29.jpg/440px-Winchester_Black_Talon_9mm_%28detail%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Winchester_Black_Talon_9mm_(detail).jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Detail of 9mm Black Talon bullet
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Winchester discontinued the Black Talon line completely in 2000. The Ranger SXT ammunition sold later by Winchester is very similar to the Black Talon though without the black Lubalox coating on the bullet. Among shooters, a running joke is that SXT stands for Same eXact Thing", though the official branding is Supreme eXpansion Technology. However, there are differences in the anatomy of the bullets which become apparent when carefully examined side by side. The hollow point cavity dimensions and angles of the
      <a href="/wiki/Meplat" title="Meplat">
       meplat
      </a>
      were altered to enhance reliability of expansion, though the basic "reverse taper" design pioneered by the Black Talon was retained. This "reverse taper" refers to the bullet's jacket being thicker at the tip than toward the base, enhancing rigidity which allows the sharp petals to remain largely perpendicular to the wound path, unlike traditional designs where the expanding jacket petals would peel back almost completely behind the expanded lead mushroom. This difference is obvious after firing into
      <a href="/wiki/Ballistic_gelatin" title="Ballistic gelatin">
       ballistic gelatin
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2007 Winchester updated their Ranger SXT line and renamed it
      <i>
       Ranger T-Series
      </i>
      . Besides further dimensional changes to the hollow point for reliable expansion, the distinctive perpendicular petals were made longer yet more rounded at the tips to retain stiffness.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="PDX1">
       PDX1
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Talon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: PDX1">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 2009 Winchester-Olin released a new hollow point bullet in its Supreme Elite line of handgun ammunition called the Bonded PDX1.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is similar to the Ranger SXT series and the older Black Talon design in its structure, and is available in several calibers and loadings. The most obvious difference from the SXT is that the bonded design, meant to maintain structural integrity through difficult intermediate barriers like auto-glass, largely prevents the sharp petals from peeling away from the lead core and fully protruding into the wound track. The
      <a href="/wiki/.40_S%26W" title=".40 S&amp;W">
       .40 S&amp;W
      </a>
      PDX1 cartridge is the primary service ammunition of the
      <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Investigation" title="Federal Bureau of Investigation">
       FBI
      </a>
      and is available for purchase by civilian shooters.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Use_in_Reeva_Steenkamp_shooting">
       Use in Reeva Steenkamp shooting
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Talon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Use in Reeva Steenkamp shooting">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In March 2014,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">
        The Guardian
       </a>
      </i>
      newspaper reported that South African
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paralympic" title="Paralympic">
       paralympic
      </a>
      athlete
      <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Pistorius" title="Oscar Pistorius">
       Oscar Pistorius
      </a>
      had shot to death his girlfriend
      <a href="/wiki/Reeva_Steenkamp" title="Reeva Steenkamp">
       Reeva Steenkamp
      </a>
      on Valentine's Day the previous year with four Black Talons fired from a 9mm pistol. Describing the round, South African police ballistics expert Captain Christian Mangena explained:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       "It hits the target, it opens up, it creates six talons, and these talons are sharp. It cuts through the organs of a human being."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AP_16-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-AP-16">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         16
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
