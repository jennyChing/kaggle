     <p>
      everything in nature is made up of components. they start from basic units such as atoms and molecules, and grow to bigger pieces like tissues and organs in order to construct a live body. the artificial environment is quite the same. it all begins with simple terms like points and lines, and expand to create surfaces and ultimately form a space.
      <strong>
       following this methodology and flow of repetition,
       <a href="http://hg-architecture.com/" target="_blank">
        HG-architecture
       </a>
       has designed part to whole, an installation placed within
       <a href="http://www.mmca.go.kr/" target="_blank">
        koreas national museum of modern and contemporary art
       </a>
       .
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="hg architecture part to whole national museum modern contemporary art korea" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-210158" height="461" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/hg-architecture-part-to-whole-national-museum-modern-contemporary-art-korea-designboom-02.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      elevation
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       the structure is composed of 9,076 wooden modules thatdisplay the qualities of tectonics through weaving and stacking.
      </strong>
      here,a set of circles moving along the curve constructs a continuous opening. it digs the specific volume out of the cube, which simultaneously maximizes the available space and minimizes the amount of material.
      <strong>
       the result is an effect referred to as stippling, a pattern created by varying degrees of solid and void.
      </strong>
      in this case, an even ratio of 50:50 articulates two-dimensional patterns of vectors and vertices, which produces three-dimensional visuals depending on positions within the gallery.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="hg architecture part to whole national museum modern contemporary art korea" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-210159" height="548" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/hg-architecture-part-to-whole-national-museum-modern-contemporary-art-korea-designboom-03.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      resting space
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="hg architecture part to whole national museum modern contemporary art korea" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-210160" height="548" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/hg-architecture-part-to-whole-national-museum-modern-contemporary-art-korea-designboom-04.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      space with people
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="hg architecture part to whole national museum modern contemporary art korea" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-210161" height="545" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/hg-architecture-part-to-whole-national-museum-modern-contemporary-art-korea-designboom-05.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      path view
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="hg architecture part to whole national museum modern contemporary art korea" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-210162" height="577" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/hg-architecture-part-to-whole-national-museum-modern-contemporary-art-korea-designboom-07.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      pattern transitions
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="hg architecture part to whole national museum modern contemporary art korea" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-210163" height="545" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/hg-architecture-part-to-whole-national-museum-modern-contemporary-art-korea-designboom-08.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      looking directly through the void like a wave
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="hg architecture part to whole national museum modern contemporary art korea" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-210164" height="572" src="http://www.designboom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/hg-architecture-part-to-whole-national-museum-modern-contemporary-art-korea-designboom-09.jpg" width="818"/>
      <br/>
      the patterns use multiple layers of solid and void
