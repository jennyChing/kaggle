         <h1>
          Lazy Updates?
         </h1>
         <div class="post-meta">
          Posted on
          <time datetime="2013-01-10T10:54">
           January 10, 2013
          </time>
          by
          <a href="http://dolphinupgrades.com/author/siren/" rel="author" title="Posts by Lorelei Gibb">
           Lorelei Gibb
          </a>
          in
          <a href="http://dolphinupgrades.com/category/social-networking/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Social Media">
           Social Media
          </a>
         </div>
         <!--.post-meta-->
        </header>
        <div class="post-content">
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <em>
           Laziness may appear attractive, but work gives satisfaction.
          </em>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.brainyquote.com/quotes/authors/a/anne_frank.html">
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          ~Anne Frank
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2157" style="width: 160px">
          <a href="http://www.dolphinupgrades.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Picture-039.jpg">
           <img alt="Pig Lazy" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-2157" height="150" src="http://www.dolphinupgrades.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Picture-039-150x150.jpg" title="Lazy Pig" width="150"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Lazy Updates?
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Were well into January and those wonderful resolutions we promised ourselves wed stick too are really starting to hurt. Could there be, perhaps, a way of making things a little easier? A way of cutting corners without it affecting the final outcome?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Probably not.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          One of the most precious commodities for any small business is that of time. If you have embraced social media (as you know you should) youve probably discovered that whilst it is 
          <em>
           free
          </em>
           in terms of financial commitment, it is most certainly
          <strong>
           not free
          </strong>
          in terms of time.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you are to be a success online you cannot cut corners.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           What do I mean by this?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          If I have shown enough interest in your company to connect with you on Linkedin, to have followed you on Twitter, to have liked you over on Facebook
          <em>
           and
          </em>
          to be keeping up with your Pins on Pinterest, do you know how infuriating it is to see the same messages on all platforms?
          <strong>
           How infuriating?
          </strong>
          Enough, in some cases, to cut the ties.
         </p>
         <p>
          Your Twitter audience is a different bunch to your Facebook fans, which are a different bunch to your Pin followers  well, with the exception of me who foolishly felt there was benefit to be gained from following all of your accounts. If you want each medium to be successful you need to take the time to post different information, geared at the different audiences, at different times.
         </p>
         <p>
          Yea Gods you cry!
          <strong>
           How am I supposed to find the time to do that?
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Well, either you do find a way yourself, or you employ a social media manager, or you use the analytics readily available to ascertain which social network works best for you, your company, and your target audience. It is far better to be successful on one social media platform than to fail miserably on several.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Rant over by
           <a href="http://uk.linkedin.com/in/loreleigibb" target="_blank">
            <span style="color: #333399;">
             <strong>
              Lorelei Gibb
             </strong>
            </span>
           </a>
          </em>
         </p>
