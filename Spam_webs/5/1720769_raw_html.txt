       <h1 class="entry-title">
        How-Tuesday: Faux Bois Napkin Rings
       </h1>
       <img alt="Etsy.com handmade and vintage goods" class="main" src="https://blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/10/faux-bois-napkin-rings-finished-horizontal1.jpg"/>
       <div class="share-byline">
        <p class="photo-by">
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="byline">
        <img alt="houseofearnest" class="avatar photo avatar-default" height="75" src="//img1.etsystatic.com/000/0/13213402/iusa_75x75.20129685.jpg" width="75"/>
        <p class="store-by">
         Story by
         <a class="profile" href="https://blog.etsy.com/en/authors/houseofearnest/">
          House of Earnest
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="published">
         Published on
         <span class="posted">
          Nov 05, 2013
         </span>
         in
         <a class="category" href="https://blog.etsy.com/en/category/make/">
          Make
         </a>
        </p>
        <!-- get photo credits -->
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="https://blog.etsy.com/en/authors/houseofearnest/">
          <img alt="houseofearnest" class="author-photo" src="https://blog.etsy.com/en/files/2012/10/AP_ERIN.jpeg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I love a minimal and modern spin on nature. These elegant faux bois napkin rings are made using just oven-bake clay and a wood grain rubber stamp.Mixed with a vintage floral napkin, they really show off their clean, simple design. Dinner guests are always shocked that theyre handmade, and Im happy because I saved a ton of money. For under $10, creating a classic, natural entertaining accessory is easy.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre a talented artist, etching your own wood grain pattern might be an option. I, however, am going to stick with the stamp!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_205288" style="width: 580px">
         <img alt="diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-materials" class="size-full wp-image-205288 " height="400" src="//blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/10/diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-materials.jpg" width="570"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          You Will Need:
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Wood grain rubber stamp
         <br/>
         White oven-bake clay
         <br/>
         X-Acto knife
         <br/>
         Rolling pin
         <br/>
         Oven
         <br/>
         Cutting mat (optional)
         <br/>
         Timer (optional)
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_205298" style="width: 580px">
         <img alt="house-001" class="size-full wp-image-205298 " height="400" src="//blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/10/house-001.jpg" width="570"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step One:
         </strong>
         Cut 1/4 of the clay block to work with for one napkin ring.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_205290" style="width: 580px">
         <img alt="diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-002" class="size-full wp-image-205290 " height="400" src="//blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/10/diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-002.jpg" width="570"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step Two:
         </strong>
         Using a non-stick surface, roll out 1/4 of the clay into a long strip.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_205291" style="width: 580px">
         <img alt="diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-003" class="size-full wp-image-205291 " height="400" src="//blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/10/diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-003.jpg" width="570"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step Three:
         </strong>
         Use a knife to cut out a rectangle that is 5 inches long by 1 1/2 inches wide.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_205292" style="width: 580px">
         <img alt="diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-004" class="size-full wp-image-205292 " height="400" src="//blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/10/diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-004.jpg" width="570"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step Four:
         </strong>
         Very firmly stamp the clay surface using your rubber stamp.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_205293" style="width: 580px">
         <img alt="diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-005" class="size-full wp-image-205293 " height="400" src="//blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/10/diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-005.jpg" width="570"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step Five:
         </strong>
         Bend the rectangle into a loop and press the ends together firmly to create a ring with the wood grain on the outside. Bake the napkin rings for 30 minutes at 275? Fahrenheit.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_205294" style="width: 580px">
         <img alt="diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-final" class="size-full wp-image-205294 " height="400" src="//blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/10/diy-how-tuesday-etsy-house-of-earnest-faux-bois-napkin-rings-final.jpg" width="570"/>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_205538" style="width: 580px">
         <img alt="faux_bois_napkin_rings_vertical" class="size-full wp-image-205538" height="798" src="//blog.etsy.com/en/files/2013/11/faux_bois_napkin_rings_vertical.jpg" width="570"/>
        </div>
