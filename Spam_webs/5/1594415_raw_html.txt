               <div class="node-content-wrapper">
                <h1 class="title">
                 Op-Ed: Thomas Jeffersons Assault Rifle  The Girardoni Air Rifle
                </h1>
                <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="Op-Ed: Thomas Jeffersons Assault Rifle  The Girardoni Air Rifle" property="dc:title">
                </span>
                <div class="byline">
                 <div class="field field-name-field-by-line field-type-text field-label-hidden">
                  <div class="field-items">
                   <div class="field-item even">
                    by Steve Straub
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div class="submitted">
                 <span class="date">
                  <span content="2014-07-04T15:00:00-04:00" datatype="xsd:dateTime" property="dc:date dc:created">
                   3:00pm Friday, July 04, 2014
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="content">
                 <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                  <div class="field-items">
                   <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                    <p>
                     The Girardoni air rifle was a 22-shot, magazine-fed, nearly silent .46 caliber repeating rifle adopted in 1780 by the Austrian Army. Thomas Jefferson purchased two of these rifles, which he sent west with Lewis and Clark.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Does it have a high-capacity magazine? Got it!
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Is it capable of firing 22 aimed shots in a minute? Got it!
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Is it a military weapon? Got it!
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     Was it manufactured during the American Revolutionary War? Got it!
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     While the standard arm of the day was a single-shot flintlock, the Girardoni offered a massive firepower advantage to the men who carried it.
                    </p>
                    <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/2dZLeEUE940?rel=0" width="560">
                    </iframe>
                    <p>
                     Next time you hear a gun control supporter say the founding fathers would have excluded assault weapons if the knew of them, show them this!
                    </p>
                    <p>
                     <em>
                      <a href="http://www.thefederalistpapers.org/founders/jefferson/thomas-jeffersons-assault-rifle-the-girardoni-air-rifle" target="_blank">
                       Click here
                      </a>
                      to read the entire op-ed at TheFederalistPapers.org.
                     </em>
                    </p>
