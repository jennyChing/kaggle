             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               ZeptoLab, the makers of mobile smash
               <em>
                <a href="http://mashable.com/category/cut-the-rope/">
                 Cut The Rope
                </a>
               </em>
               , announced Tuesday morning it is releasing its first new franchise in two years:
               <em>
                Pudding Monsters
               </em>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                <a href="http://puddingmonsters.com" target="_blank">
                 Pudding Monsters
                </a>
               </em>
               introduces new, cute characters to mobile players. The Pudding Monsters live in a fridge, and are on a mission to save their pudding friend who was taken by a human.
              </p>
              <p>
               The puzzle-based gameplay is split into levels similar to
               <em>
                Cut the Rope
               </em>
               . Players must stick the Pudding Monsters together to make them large enough to get through a level, as well as use the special powers of some monsters to get past some objectives. For instance, a Slime Monster leaves a trail of green goo that will be necessary to solve some puzzles. The monsters can stick together to reach any size  from a kitchen table to a tall building.
              </p>
              <p>
               A spokesperson for ZeptoLab said
               <em>
                Pudding Monsters
               </em>
               was dreamed up by an employee during one of the company's internal competitions for new game ideas.
              </p>
              <p>
               ZeptoLab has tremendous success with
               <em>
                Cut the Rope
               </em>
               and its successor,
               <em>
                Cut the Rope: Experiments
               </em>
               .
               <em>
                Cut the Rope
               </em>
               has sold more than 250 million copies since 2010 to players who want to reunite the cute monster "Om Nom" with his beloved candy. Om Nom has been licensed for merchandise by Hasbro and Burger King, and he's appearing in a
               <a data-crackerjax="#post-slider" href="http://mashable.com/2011/07/20/cut-the-rope-comic-series/">
                comic book
               </a>
               and a weekly animated series called
               <em>
                Om Nom Stories
               </em>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                Pudding Monsters
               </em>
               will be available on Dec. 20 for iPhone, iPad and Android.
              </p>
