         <h1 class="entry-title">
          See Pluto in 2010
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <!-- Grab the author and the date of post -->
          <span class="byline">
           By:
           <span class="by-author">
            <span class="author vcard">
             <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.skyandtelescope.com/author/tony-flanders-2/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Tony Flanders">
              Tony Flanders
             </a>
            </span>
           </span>

           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2010-03-24T06:14:00+00:00">
            March 24, 2010
           </time>
          </span>
          <div class="sharethis">
           <span class="st_facebook" data-st_title="See Pluto in 2010" data-st_url="http://www.skyandtelescope.com/sky-and-telescope-magazine/beyond-the-printed-page/see-pluto-in-2010/">

           <span class="st_twitter" data-st_title="See Pluto in 2010" data-st_url="http://www.skyandtelescope.com/sky-and-telescope-magazine/beyond-the-printed-page/see-pluto-in-2010/">
           </span>
           <span class="st_linkedin" data-st_title="See Pluto in 2010" data-st_url="http://www.skyandtelescope.com/sky-and-telescope-magazine/beyond-the-printed-page/see-pluto-in-2010/">

           </span>
           <span class="st_pinterest" data-st_title="See Pluto in 2010" data-st_url="http://www.skyandtelescope.com/sky-and-telescope-magazine/beyond-the-printed-page/see-pluto-in-2010/">
           </span>
          </div>
          <!-- sharethis -->
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Have you ever seen Pluto through a telescope? Do you want to try? All you need is a good 8-inch scope, access to dark skies, lots of persistence, and excellent star charts.
         </p>
         <p>
          We can't help you with the telescope, dark-sky site, or necessary character qualities. But this article contains all the
          <a href="/wp-content/uploads/Pluto_2010_Fnder.pdf" target="new_window">
           charts you need to locate Pluto
          </a>
          in May or June 2010. The July issue of
          <i>
           Sky &amp; Telescope
          </i>
          includes charts for July through October.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="FW_EM_MediaContainer wp-caption alignright" style=" width: 341px;">
          <img alt="" class="FW_EM_Image" src="/wp-content/uploads/Pluto_2010_Key_341.jpg" style=" width: 341px;"/>
          <div class="FW_EM_Caption wp-caption-text">
           Click on the chart above for a full-page PDF chart showing how to locate Pluto in May and June 2010.
          </div>
          <div class="FW_EM_Credits wp-caption-text">
           Tony Flanders
          </div>
         </div>
         This is one of the best years in Pluto's 249-year orbit around the Sun to view the dwarf planet. In May and June, it crosses Messier 24, the Small Sagittarius Star Cloud, one of the pieces of sky most densely packed with stars. A high-power telescopic field of view will include hundreds of stars comparable to Pluto in brightness. Then, in July, Pluto crosses Barnard 92, one of the most prominent of all dark clouds, where typical backyard telescopes show no stars whatsoever.
         <p>
          Whether you call it a planet, a dwarf planet, a minor planet, an asteroid, a comet  or all of the above  Pluto is a special object. It's the most distant chunk of solid material that can be seen through the eyepiece of normal backyard telescopes.
         </p>
         <p>
          Pluto is almost impossible to see when the Moon is up, so each month there's a two or three-week window of visibility, depending precisely where you live. At mid-northern latitudes, these windows are roughly:
         </p>
         <p>
          May 6-24 from 2 a.m. to dawn
          <br/>
          June 3-22 from midnight to 3 a.m.
          <br/>
          July 2-19 from nightfall to 1 a.m.
          <br/>
          Aug. 1-15 from nightfall to 11:30 p.m.
          <br/>
          Aug. 29 - Sept. 11 from nightfall to 9:30 p.m.
          <br/>
          Sept. 27 - Oct. 9 at nightfall
         </p>
         <p class="no-break">
         </p>
