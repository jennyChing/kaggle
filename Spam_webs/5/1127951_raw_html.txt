        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Who Is Winning the Ukraine Social Media Wars?
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-05-13T14:55:41+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          2014-05-13
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://everything-pr.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Richard D. Pace
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://everything-pr.com/winning-the-ukraine-social-media-wars/248956/#comments">
           6 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_48962" style="width: 760px">
         <a href="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Putintwitter.jpg">
          <img alt="Putin winning on Twitter" class="size-full wp-image-48962" height="585" src="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Putintwitter.jpg" width="750"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Ill give you three guesses.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Monitoring and commenting on an unraveling US policy toward Russia over Ukraine today, I decided to show those interested some compelling news even the US State Department is not aware of. Western mainstream media hammering away, thousands of US government employees have been tasked with rolling out Obama administration propaganda, the mention battle for Ukraine on Twitter is all but lost for John Kerrys minions.
        </p>
        <p>
         Is the truth coming through? What does Twitter influence say?
        </p>
        <p>
         Using a slick metrics and monitoring tool called
         <a href="http://www.brandwatch.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="brandwatch">
          Brandwatch
         </a>
         , Ill show you who is winning and who is losing the PR war over Ukraine. As you can see below, Ive provided a screenshot of my Brandwatch dashboard for #Ukraine. The last seven days have been busy, with something on the order of 77 thousand mentions for just the #hashtag #Ukraine on Twitter.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_48957" style="width: 760px">
         <a href="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Brandwatch.jpg">
          <img alt="The Brandwatch dashboard for #Ukraine - watch " class="size-full wp-image-48957" height="469" src="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Brandwatch.jpg" width="750"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The Brandwatch dashboard for #Ukraine  watch this
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         The Influence Gurus Come Out
        </h2>
        <p>
         Leverage, this is what moves the social world. Whether or not you are an influencer, being able to convince is the single most important communicative skill there is. Youll notice I said skill, instead of gift etc. To acquire this skill, you first have to understand the weight of influence on networks like Twitter and Facebook. For finding out whos influential in the #Ukraine media war, in social, Brandwatch helps us establish the winners and losers very quickly. RTs stringer Graham W. Phillips (
         <a href="https://twitter.com/grahamwp_uk" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          @grahamwp_uk
         </a>
         ), for example, is by far the most influential tweep of the last week. The tag cloud (mention cloud) I generated below shows this from among all top tweeters this week.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_48958" style="width: 760px">
         <a href="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/twitter.jpg">
          <img alt="GrahamWPhillips eclipsed all other tweeps for RT " class="size-full wp-image-48958" height="455" src="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/twitter.jpg" width="750"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          GrahamWPhillips eclipsed all other tweeps for RT
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         It turns out, Graham is not alone telling the other side (not NATO propaganda) of the Ukraine story. In the mix of most influentials this week on Twitter, RTs Middle East Bureau Chief, Paula Slier (
         <a class="customtip" href="https://twitter.com/paulaslier_rt" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          PaulaSlier_RT
         </a>
         ),
         <a class="customtip" href="http://twitter.com/rt_com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          RT
         </a>
         proper (
         <a class="mentionUrl customtip" href="https://twitter.com/rt_com" rel="nofollow" style="color: #208ec4;" target="_blank">
          @rt_com
         </a>
         ), and on the Ukrainian unity side of the coin, Christopher Miller, compete for reach and mentions combatively. In the end though, its
         <span style="color: #4b4949;">
          ironic that even though Mashables (Kyiv Post and Miller) social weight is thrown into the chasm, Millers and Kievs view have become obscure since days now.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Additionally, even though Russia Today is the dominant presence today, two weeks ago parity in between pro and anti-Russian sentiment existed. Whats made the difference more than RT coverage, has been independent freelancers like
         <a class="customtip" href="http://twitter.com/rozakazancctv" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Roza Kazan
         </a>
         (@rozakazancctv) whosetweets of images have received hundreds of tweets and re-tweets. Shes second behind Graham in influencing the view on Twitter this week, whereas self proclaimed #frustrated GermanSteiner(
         <a class="mentionUrl customtip" href="http://twitter.com/steiner1776" rel="nofollow" style="color: #208ec4;" target="_blank">
          @steiner1776
         </a>
         ) has made a difference too. Just as has Kiev advocacy accountEuromaidan PR (
         <a class="mentionUrl customtip" href="http://twitter.com/euromaidanpr" rel="nofollow" style="font-weight: bold; color: #208ec4;" target="_blank">
          @euromaidanpr
         </a>
         ), falling in on the Ukraine unity side behind Kyiv Post/Mashable editor/writer Christopher Miller, whose Mashable tweets allow Kievs regime to stay in the social game.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_48959" style="width: 760px">
         <a href="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/paula.jpg">
          <img alt="Brandwatch" class="size-full wp-image-48959" height="469" src="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/paula.jpg" width="750"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Adding voice to the conversation by helping influencers by influencers
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Now, in order to affect this situation, tools like Brandwatch can be really effective, if a bit subtle. As you can see (hopefully) from the screen below, Ive pulled up the most impactful recent tweet from a user named Paul Payer  this RT of
         <span style="color: #4b4949;">
         </span>
         <a class="tweet-url username twitter-username" data-screen-name="PaulaSlier_RT" href="https://twitter.com/PaulaSlier_RT" rel="nofollow" style="color: #208ec4;" target="_blank">
          @PaulaSlier
         </a>
         (as above) adds weight to the financial side of the twitter battle for Ukraine influence. In case you were unaware, this is why you see outlets like the Wall Street Journal, Bloomberg, and in particular the
         <a class="customtip" href="http://twitter.com/TheEconomist" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          The Economist
         </a>
         (
         <a class="mentionUrl customtip" href="http://twitter.com/TheEconomist" rel="nofollow" style="color: #208ec4;" target="_blank">
          @TheEconomist
         </a>
         ) so prevalently not onlyinGoogle and Bing News, but in social as well. Still another reason may be, the fact hedge players and politicians play the margins on the stock market manipulating policy like this. But more on that in another post.
        </p>
        <p>
         Before this past week, as I mentioned, news outlets like BBC, Sky News, even the Wall Street Journal, and especially
         <a class="customtip" href="http://twitter.com/euromaidanpr" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Euromaidan PR
         </a>
         (
         <a class="mentionUrl customtip" href="http://twitter.com/euromaidanpr" rel="nofollow" style="color: #208ec4;" target="_blank">
          @euromaidanpr
         </a>
         ) achieved rough parity (equality) of mentions and force with a low volume of tweets compared to pro-Russia supporters. Since the Odessa carnage tho0ugh, this has changed dramatically. AfterMay 2, when pro-Russian activists were trapped inside Odessas
         <span style="color: #362f2d;">
          Trade Unions building, over 14,000 mentions reflect first competing sentiment on the causes etc., and then remained almost completely quiet where pro-Kiev sentiment was concerned.
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_48960" style="width: 630px">
         <a href="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/tag-cloud.jpg">
          <img alt="Odessa" class="size-full wp-image-48960" height="415" src="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/tag-cloud.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          #Odessa  definitely a frown time sentiment for the great majority :(
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #362f2d;">
          Its interesting to note a shift in favor of the Russian side of this affair since this event. After the initial news cycle, where the most prominent tweeters were BBC, Bloomberg, and traditional news followers, what you might call the core influencers  passionate tweeps or those on the ground in Ukraine, demolished less vehement sentiment. Above, the mention cloud reflects a lot about what was tweeted, especially poignant is the frowny face :(  all kidding aside.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Finally, monitoring topical social events like Ukraine, then interceding on one side or the other, becomes a simpler and more clearly defined task if one can compare and set one influencer against the other. One thing I find really interesting here is, even though NATO and some heavy hitting Twitter accounts (like the State Department) do tweet these issues, these are almost always outweighed by the smaller voices (If I may). Add to this the individual effort by newsroom people like
         <a class="customtip" href="http://twitter.com/lizziephelan" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Lizzie Phelan
         </a>
         (
         <a class="mentionUrl customtip" href="http://twitter.com/lizziephelan" rel="nofollow" style="color: #208ec4;" target="_blank">
          @lizziephelan
         </a>
         ), and the Russian side of the Ukraine story does get out. In my estimation this is a bit about agency understanding of social media, and in RTs case cooperative reporting. This is something other news agencies suck at, to be honest. However, nible the Russia Today reports are though, its clear too that the aggregate story, the weight of social sharing evidence (if we can call it that) eventually outweighs PR and propaganda fluff.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets face it, dozens of human beings roasted in a building together, tanks running over civilians, all those wolfsangle symbols, they outweigh John Kerry harping about Vladimir Putin invasions. A lie just does not resonate when it comes to war and peace, not with the normal people. Sure, the American public is tired and apathetic, but Obamas side has overdone it again in hyping Russophobia. At least this is how it plays out in my world of
         <a href="http://everything-pr.com/social-media/" title="Social Media News Blog">
          social media
         </a>
         . My suggestion for Obamas PR team? Try and hire one of the RT reporters if you can, your millions spent on the Cold War story arent showing an ROI in Ukraine. Not yet, anyway.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_48961" style="width: 760px">
         <a href="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/influence.jpg">
          <img alt="And that my friends is how you beat a big PR team :) " class="size-full wp-image-48961" height="401" src="http://everything-pr.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/influence.jpg" width="750"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          And that my friends is how you beat a big PR team :)
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Oh, and to show how Brandwatch helps me influence a bit, the mashup above shows Graham Phillips mention count before and after I tweeted his story with 9 twitter accounts our businesses own. Just in case influence is something you need in the future.
        </p>

          <div class="comment-content" itemprop="commentText">
           <p>
            A good example why Twitter recording tools can be terribly misleading. In the last week, many people retweeted @GrahamWP_uk from the Kremlin propaganda outlet RT.com, as well as @lizziephelan from Ruptly, RT.coms stringer company, because they disagreed with them and were arguing with them  not because they were influenced by them. Influence is ultimately a meaningless term that only describes numerical superiority of traffic, not content or conversion.
           </p>
           <p>
            During this last week, there were the clashes in Mariupol, where a Ruptly stringer was shot after Russian-backed separatists shot at Ukrainian forces and they retaliated  thus the news of RT.coms reporter being shot vastly increased interest and traffic. Furthermore, Graham Philips made the false claim that more than 100 people were killed in Mariupol, based on his mistaken translation of a source and the desire to portray the Ukrainian forces in the worst light. So numerous discussions were had with him about this, because the Ukrainian government said there were at most 20, Western news outlets were also estimating or reporting 20. Anna Neistat of Human Rights Watch ultimately actually went to the morgue and interviewed medical workers and discovered there appeared to be no more than 7. There were never any 100s.
           </p>
           <p>
            Influence doesnt equal truth, nor does it even mean that readers believe you. It can mean just the opposite. In the old days, a company that sold the most newspapers might be a tabloid that exaggerated stories with lurid headlines, or it might be a company that had achieved the public trust and reached the most. Mechanical tools cannot tell that difference.
           </p>
           <p>
            Tune in next week and see if RT.com keeps that advantage gained by having their own person run straight into a gunfight and then having their reporters exaggerated outrageously the victims.
           </p>
