            <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
             Walt Disney Created His Most Famous Character In A Fit Of Rage
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta entry-meta-above">
             Posted on
             <time class="entry-date published updated" datetime="2014-10-28T16:02:13+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
              October 28, 2014
             </time>
             by
             <span class="entry-author vcard" itemprop="author" itemptype="http://schema.org/Person" itemscope="">
              <a class="author-link fn nickname url" href="http://rabidofficemonkey.com/author/stacy/" itemprop="url" title="View all posts by Stacy Jackson">
               <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
                Stacy Jackson
               </span>
              </a>
             </span>
             
             <a class="entry-comments" href="http://rabidofficemonkey.com/walt-disney-created-his-most-famous-character-in-a-fit-of-rage/#comments" title="Walt Disney Created His Most Famous Character In A Fit Of Rage  Comments">
              0 Comments
             </a>
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->
           </header>
           <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
            <div id="triberr_endorsement">
            </div>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/walt-disney-created-mickey-mouse-in-anger-2014-10#ixzz3HT87Xwn9" target="_blank">
              <img alt="" class="alignleft" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/rabidofficemonkey.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/2198054a-ad10-477a-a790-392ea9382724.jpg" style="width: 100%;"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              One of the most epic creations in the history of popular culture grew out of anger, fear, and desperation.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             Source:
             <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/walt-disney-created-mickey-mouse-in-anger-2014-10#ixzz3HT87Xwn9" target="_blank">
              www.businessinsider.com
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             As an entrepreneur, how would you feel if your team were hired out from under you and the guy who did it offered you a contract at lower pay and a demand to the full rights of your work/intellectual property?
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Sounds pretty devastating, huh? Especially if every other venture (before this one great one) had failed! This one seemed like your ticket to Awesomeville.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             What would you do? Go home, cry into a pint of ice cream, then start working on your resume? Or, would you channel all of your fear and rage into something even better than what you could have ever imagined before?
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Walt Disney faced these challenges, and Mickey Mouse was born out of his fear and anger at a jerk who stole his team and demanded the full rights to Disneys first successful creation, Oswald the Lucky Rabbit.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             Remember Walt Disney next time you face a major entrepreneurial obstacle. Will you turn tail and run, or will you channel your energy into something great?
            </p>
