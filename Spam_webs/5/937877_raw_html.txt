       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Immediate gratification: Employees say long-term benefits can wait
       </h1>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
       <div class="beforepostmeta">
        November 27, 2012
        <span>
         |
        </span>
        Categories:
        <a href="http://mthink.mercer.com/category/infographics/" rel="category tag">
         Infographics
        </a>
        <span>
         |
        </span>
        Tags:
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         New Mercer research shows that workers around the world, when given the choice, would pick an additional week of paid time off over a range of other benefit options that have the potential to deliver greater long-term value. Mercers
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.mercer.com/benefit-choice-research">
           Making Smart Benefit Choices Survey
          </a>
         </em>
         was conducted among more than 10,400 workers in 10 major markets in JulyAugust 2012 to determine which benefits employees value most. Underscoring this trend of immediacy over longer-term benefits, employees selected a salary increase over all benefit offerings listed in the survey (except in Canada, where paid time off edged out a salary increase).
        </p>
        <p>
         The survey is very revealing, in that employees in many countries place greater value on certain reward elements that actually offer them less value, over time, from a financial or wellbeing perspective, says Amy Laverock, a Partner with Mercer Marsh Benefits. While uncertain economic conditions may explain the preference for a pay increase or more paid time off, these preferences underscore the need for employers to communicate to their employees the importance of striking the right balance between benefits that provide short- and longer-term value.
        </p>
        <p>
         In addition, she notes, Differentiated benefits continue to make a meaningful difference in any organizations employee value proposition, helping to attract and retain the right talent.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           Take a look at benefit preferences around the world.
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
