            <div class="ad ad--epic" data-ad-text="show">
             <div class="ad-ad_ns_btf_01" id="ad_ns_btf_01">

            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           If you've picked up fruit at Costco, Kroger or Walmart stores recently, keep reading.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Wawona Packing Co. is voluntarily recalling peaches, nectarines, plums and pluots that were packed at its Cutler, California, warehouses between June 1 and July 12. Wawona believes the products may be contaminated with
           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/08/23/health/listeria-fast-facts/">
            Listeria monocytogenes
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           <a href="http://www.costco.com/wcsstore/CostcoUSBCCatalogAssetStore/Attachment/recalls/140721-Wawona-Packing-Co-FDA-PRESS-RELEASE.pdf" target="_blank">
            Costco
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://www.kroger.com/recall_alerts" target="_blank">
            Kroger
           </a>
           and
           <a href="http://corporate.walmart.com/recalls" target="_blank">
            Walmart
           </a>
           , which also operates Sam's Club stores, have all posted notices about the fruit recall on their websites. So have
           <a href="http://www.wholefoodsmarket.com/product-recalls" target="_blank">
            Whole Foods Market
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.traderjoes.com/about/customer-updates-responses.asp?i=108" target="_blank">
            Trader Joe's
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://www.ralphs.com/recall_alerts" target="_blank">
            Ralphs
           </a>
           and
           <a href="https://www.food4less.com/recall_alerts" target="_blank">
            Food 4 Less
           </a>
           . The recall is nationwide, according to the
           <a href="http://www.fda.gov/Safety/Recalls/ucm405943.htm" target="_blank">
            U.S. Food and Drug Administration
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Consumers should look for a sticker on their fruit that says "
           <a href="http://www.fda.gov/Safety/Recalls/ucm405945.htm" target="_blank">
            SWEET 2 EAT
           </a>
           ," according to the recall warnings.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Internal testing at Wawona revealed the potential listeria contamination, the company says. Listeria was found on on two nectarines and one peach. It is not yet clear how they became contaminated.
           <strong>
           </strong>
           The facility was shut down and sanitized, and subsequent tests have been negative for the bacteria.
          </p>

           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/04/23/health/foodborne-illness/index.html">
            How to protect yourself from food poisoning
           </a>
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Smittcamp is urging customers not to eat any of these stone fruits -- meaning fruits with large pits -- and to either destroy them or return them to a nearby store for a refund.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Whole Foods is also recalling several of their "
           <a href="http://assets.wholefoodsmarket.com/www/misc/20140723-StoneFruitRecall.pdf" target="_blank">
            made-in-store items
           </a>
           such as cakes, tarts, salsas, and prepared salads" that may contain the contaminated fruit.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           In addition, Wegmans has recalled several of its baked goods that contain fruit from Wawona Packing Co. This includes cakes, pies, tarts and other pastries. For a full list, see
           <a href="http://www.fda.gov/Safety/Recalls/ucm405956.htm" target="_blank">
            FDA.gov
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Listeria monocytogenes is a bacterium that can cause listeriosis. The most common symptoms of listeriosis are gastrointestinal issues (such as diarrhea), fever and muscle aches. Pregnant women, infants, older adults and people with weakened immune systems are particularly at risk for a more serious infection, according to the
           <a href="http://www.cdc.gov/listeria/definition.html" target="_blank">
            Centers for Disease Control and Prevention
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           If you have already eaten one of the recalled products and begin to feel ill, see your doctor and tell him or her about the recall.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           "Your doctor will make a diagnosis and should report any positive test findings for Listeria monocytogenes to public health authorities, who will then report it to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention," Wawona
           <a href="http://www.wawonapacking.com/FAQ.pdf" target="_blank">
            states in an FAQ
           </a>
           about the recall.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           The CDC estimates about 1,600 illnesses and 260 deaths are caused by listeriosis each year in the United States. Overall, outbreaks have been on the decline since 2001, but the largest in U.S. history occurred in 2011. Cantaloupes from a farm in Colorado sickened 147 people and killed nearly three dozen. The farmers responsible
           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2014/01/28/justice/cantaloupe-listeria-deaths-sentencing/">
            recently were sentenced
           </a>
           to five years' probation, including six months of in-home detention.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2014/02/21/health/cheese-listeria-outbreak/">
            1 dead, 7 sickened by listeria linked to cheese
           </a>
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/10/23/health/meat-recall/">
            22,000 pounds of meat recalled
           </a>
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2014/07/04/health/salmonella-chicken-recall/index.html" target="_blank">
            California firm recalls chicken over salmonella fears
           </a>
          </p>
