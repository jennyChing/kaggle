           <h3>
            By LOCAL 12 (YouTube)
           </h3>
           <div style="text-align: justify;">
            <span class="cb-dropcap-small">
             T
            </span>
            he love between a boy and his dog is almost universal. In fact, here is one duo thats shedding light on how pets can help children with autism.9-year-old Jonny Hickey and his 2-year-old pit bull Xena both had a rough starts in life. When Jonny was born, his parents were overjoyed but soon their joy became concern.To get home and eight months later to have them tell you well, ya know something just isnt quite right, said Jonnys father Grant Hickey.Eventually, Jonny was diagnosed with autism. He barely interacted with any one and spoke very few words.Meanwhile, not too far from where the Hickeys lived, a pit bull puppy was found near death due to starvation and months of abuse.Veterinarians said there was a 1% chance she would live.It just took my breath away, said Jonnys mother Linda.When it looked like Xena would survive, Linda told her husband she wanted to adopt the dogI said i dont know if i really want a pit bull in our house, said Grant. What kind of issues is the dog gonna have?There were no issues, but something did happen the moment Jonny and Xena met.I had never heard Jonny talk so much in this house as he did that day, said Linda. Johnny started doing things he wouldnt do before.I could just cry thinking about it because it was the dog had such an impact on our son that tens of thousands of dollars of therapists didnt have, said Grant.
