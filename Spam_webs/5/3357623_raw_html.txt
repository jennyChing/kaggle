        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Tomato Parmesan Slow Cooker Soup
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-12-18T10:19:41+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 18, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://lifecurrents.dw2.net/author/admin/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Debi
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">

           84 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         On a particularly busy week, the ease of makingTomato Parmesan Slow Cooker Soup in the {affiliate}
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003UCG8II/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B003UCG8II&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=lifecurr-20&amp;linkId=GBK4D5GYPHBDGISK">
          crock pot or slow cooker
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=lifecurr-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B003UCG8II" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         is just so awesome! And when the soup comes out as tasty as this one, well, you have a winner  easy and yummy! everything you could want! Especially when its so cold out and you just need something warm in your tummy.
        </p>
        <p>
         And, in full disclosure, I am fairly awful at making roux, so my awesome husband always makes the roux for me. Thanks, Dan!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lifecurrents.dw2.net/tomato-parmesan-slow-cooker-soup/tomato-parmesan-soup_horizontal/" rel="attachment wp-att-13210">
          <img alt="Tomato Parmesan Slow Cooker Soup  |  Life Currents  vegetarian crock pot http://lifecurrents.dw2.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-13210 size-full" height="433" src="http://lifecurrents.dw2.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/tomato-parmesan-soup_horizontal.jpg" title="Tomato Parmesan Slow Cooker Soup  |  Life Currents  " width="650"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         When I make something in the crock pot, I like to make bread in the {affiliate}
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001C2KY7Y/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B001C2KY7Y&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=lifecurr-20&amp;linkId=JNW65VLPJWGQX5GQ">
          bread machine
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=lifecurr-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B001C2KY7Y" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         at the same time. Then, when its time for dinner, everything is all fresh and hot! These
         <a href="http://lifecurrents.dw2.net/buttermilk-dinner-rolls/" target="_blank">
          Buttermilk Dinner Rolls
         </a>
         can be made as a whole loaf of bread, and would itd be awesome to make the leftover bread into French Toast the next day.
        </p>
        <p>
         Use the milk of your choice: half and half will make a thicker richer soup thats higher in calories; skim milk will make a thinner soup thats lower in calories. I like the compromise of whole milk in this soup. You could even use a non-dairy milk like canned coconut milk (that would give a great thick soup), or almond milk.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lifecurrents.dw2.net/tomato-parmesan-slow-cooker-soup/tomato-parmesan-slow-cooker-soup-main/" rel="attachment wp-att-13208">

           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Ingredients
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-ingredients">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="0">
             1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes, undrained
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="1">
             4 carrots, sliced
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="2">
             1 large yellow onion, chopped
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="3">
             1 teaspoon dried oregano
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="4">
             1 tablespoon dried basil
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="5">
             4 cups vegetable broth
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="6">
             1 bay leaf
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="7">
             1 teaspoon salt
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="8">
              teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="9">
              cup olive oil
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="10">
              cup butter
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="11">
              cup flour
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="12">
              cup freshly shredded Parmesan cheese
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-ingredient-item" itemprop="ingredients" yumprintitem="13">
             2 cups whole milk*
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-method-section" yumprintsection="1">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Instructions
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-methods" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="0">
             Add first nine ingredients, tomatoes through black pepper, to the bowl of a 4-quart slow cooker. Cover and cook on low for 5-7 hours, or until vegetables are softened.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="1">
             About 30 minutes before serving the soup, prepare the roux. Heat oil and butter over low heat in a sauce pan and add flour. Stir constantly with a whisk for 5-7 minutes, until roux is a smooth light brown paste. Slowly stir 1 cup of hot soup into the roux, until fully mixed in. Add another 3 cups soup to the mixture, and continue to stir until smooth. Add thickened soup back into the slow cooker, and mix in the Parmesan and milk.
            </li>
            <li class="blog-yumprint-method-item" yumprintitem="2">
             Cover and cook on low for another 30 minutes. Taste for seasonings, and enjoy.
            </li>
           </ol>
          </div>
          <div class="blog-yumprint-note-section" yumprintsection="2">
           <div class="blog-yumprint-subheader">
            Notes
           </div>
           <ol class="blog-yumprint-notes">
            <li class="blog-yumprint-note-item" yumprintitem="0">
             *Use the milk of your choice: half and half will make a thicker richer soup that's higher in calories, skim milk will make a thinner soup thats lower in calories. I like the compromise of whole milk in this soup. You could even use a non-dairy milk like canned coconut milk (that would give a great thick soup), or almond milk.
            </li>
           </ol>
