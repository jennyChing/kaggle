     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Planet of the Apes
       </b>
      </i>
      is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">
       science fiction
      </a>
      television series that aired on
      <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">
       CBS
      </a>
      in 1974. The series stars
      <a href="/wiki/Roddy_McDowall" title="Roddy McDowall">
       Roddy McDowall
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ron_Harper_(actor)" title="Ron Harper (actor)">
       Ron Harper
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/James_Naughton" title="James Naughton">
       James Naughton
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Lenard" title="Mark Lenard">
       Mark Lenard
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Booth_Colman" title="Booth Colman">
       Booth Colman
      </a>
      . It is based on the 1968
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Planet_of_the_Apes_(1968_film)" title="Planet of the Apes (1968 film)">
        Planet of the Apes
       </a>
      </i>
      film and its
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planet_of_the_Apes_(franchise)" title="Planet of the Apes (franchise)">
       sequels
      </a>
      , which were inspired by the
      <a href="/wiki/Planet_of_the_Apes_(novel)" title="Planet of the Apes (novel)">
       novel of the same name
      </a>
      by
      <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Boulle" title="Pierre Boulle">
       Pierre Boulle
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
       Overview
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planet_of_the_Apes_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The series begins with the crash of an Earth spaceship that encountered a time warp while approaching Alpha Centauri on August 19, 1980. The date on the ship's chronometer is given as March 21, 3085 but we are told that it could have simply stopped at that point while the ship was still travelling. It could therefore be many thousands of years into the future. The spaceship is manned by three astronauts, one of whom has died in the crash. The other two astronauts, Colonel Alan Virdon and Major Peter J. Burke, are unconscious but are rescued by a human who carries them to an old bomb shelter. After the human opens a book containing historical text and pictures of Earth circa 2500, the two astronauts are convinced that they are indeed on a future Earth.
     </p>
     <p>
      The crash is also witnessed by a young chimpanzee who tells
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veska" title="Veska">
       his father
      </a>
      , a village official who alerts the authorities. Ape counselor Zaius (an analog of the
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Planet_of_the_Apes_characters#Dr._Zaius" title="List of Planet of the Apes characters">
       character
      </a>
      from the
      <a href="/wiki/Planet_of_the_Apes_(1968_film)" title="Planet of the Apes (1968 film)">
       original movie
      </a>
      ), notes that another such incident occurred ten years earlier. He orders the head gorilla, General Urko, to find the humans and bring them back alive. Zaius wants to find out as much as he can about the humans before they are eventually killed. Zaius doesn't trust General Urko to follow his orders and bring back any surviving humans, so he sends along his newly hired chimpanzee assistant, Galen.
     </p>
     <p>
      Both Virdon and Burke go back to their ship to check the ship's chronometer. They are more than 1000 years in the future from when they left Earth. Virdon insists on retrieving the ship's flight log in the hopes that they will be able to analyze it and be able to return to their own time period, but while they are at the ship, they are captured, and the old man is subsequently killed by a group of apes.
     </p>
     <p>
      Galen finds the human book that the old man had been carrying. He reads parts of the book and begins to doubt the history that he has been told: apes have always been dominant, and humans have always been inferior and subservient. When Galen finds out that Urko has arranged for the two astronauts to escape and be killed in the attempt, he stops the shooter and helps the humans escape.
     </p>
     <p>
      Galen discusses the book that he found with Zaius, who then accuses him of heresy. Galen is sentenced to death for his crime. Then Virdon and Burke find out about his sentence and rescue Galen. They are all then declared enemies of the state and become fugitives. The three fugitives wander around the territory that used to be the western United States having various encounters with apes, humans, and old human civilization ruins.
     </p>

     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Roddy_McDowall" title="Roddy McDowall">
        Roddy McDowall
       </a>
       as Galen, a young chimpanzee that is sent by Zaius with Urko to ensure the safety of two humans that have survived a crash landing on Earth. McDowall previously played Cornelius and Caesar throughout the various film versions.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Ron_Harper_(actor)" title="Ron Harper (actor)">
        Ron Harper
       </a>
       as Colonel Alan Virdon, the captain of the crash-landed ship that left Earth in 1980.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/James_Naughton" title="James Naughton">
        James Naughton
       </a>
       as Major Peter J. Burke, an astronaut major from ANSA that also survived the crash.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Mark_Lenard" title="Mark Lenard">
        Mark Lenard
       </a>
       as General Urko (11 episodes), a violent gorilla and the series' main antagonist.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Booth_Colman" title="Booth Colman">
        Booth Colman
       </a>
       as Councillor Zaius (6 episodes), the highest-ranking member of the High Council of Central City in the year 3085.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/John_Hoyt" title="John Hoyt">
        John Hoyt
       </a>
       as Barlow (2 episodes), a chimpanzee prefect of Kaymak that had introduced gladiatorial fights between the humans.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Jacqueline_Scott" title="Jacqueline Scott">
        Jacqueline Scott
       </a>
       as Kira (1 episode), a chimpanzee surgeon in the Central City hospital. Kira and Galen had previously been engaged to be married. Also portrayed Zantes in a different episode.
      </li>
     </ul>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planet_of_the_Apes_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Discussions for a
      <i>
       Planet of the Apes
      </i>
      television series were made by producer
      <a href="/wiki/Arthur_P._Jacobs" title="Arthur P. Jacobs">
       Arthur P. Jacobs
      </a>
      as early as 1971. Because of the success of the films, the idea of a television series was put on hold until after the completion of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_for_the_Planet_of_the_Apes" title="Battle for the Planet of the Apes">
        Battle for the Planet of the Apes
       </a>
      </i>
      in the first half of 1973.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, shortly after the premiere of
      <i>
       Battle
      </i>
      , Jacobs died, and his production company APJAC Productions sold all
      <i>
       Planet of the Apes
      </i>
      rights and privileges to
      <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">
       20th Century Fox
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Subsequently, television rights for the first three
      <i>
       Planet of the Apes
      </i>
      films were sold to
      <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">
       CBS
      </a>
      and aired successfully in September 1973. Based largely on high viewership of "movie-of-the-week" TV broadcasts of the first few films, CBS began to focus away from other contenders for a new science-fiction series, including
      <a href="/wiki/Gene_Roddenberry" title="Gene Roddenberry">
       Gene Roddenberry
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/Genesis_II_(film)" title="Genesis II (film)">
       <i>
        Genesis II
       </i>
      </a>
      (1973) and look solely at the prospects for an
      <i>
       Apes
      </i>
      TV series.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Fox and CBS went on to continue Jacob's plans of a series the following year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      CBS ordered 14 episodes of
      <i>
       Planet of the Apes
      </i>
      to be produced. The series was filmed for the most part on location at what is now
      <a href="/wiki/Malibu_Creek_State_Park" title="Malibu Creek State Park">
       Malibu Creek State Park
      </a>
      , with a budget of about $250,000 for each episode.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Originally slated to air during CBS's Tuesday night family hour,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the first regular episode of the series aired on Friday, September 13, 1974 from 8:00-9:00 PM. The remainder of the series aired in this same time slot until December 27, 1974, when its thirteenth and final broadcast was shown as a result of a premature cancellation of the series due to low ratings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Music">
       Music
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planet_of_the_Apes_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The series' main theme was composed by
      <a href="/wiki/Lalo_Schifrin" title="Lalo Schifrin">
       Lalo Schifrin
      </a>
      ; Schifrin also scored three episodes of the series - "Escape From Tomorrow," "The Gladiators" and "The Good Seeds" (the latter, though not the premiere episode, was the first to be scored).
      <a href="/wiki/Earle_Hagen" title="Earle Hagen">
       Earle Hagen
      </a>
      composed the scores for "The Legacy" and "Tomorrow's Tide," while Richard LaSalle wrote an original score for "The Trap." In addition, three episodes received partial scores - Hagen composed one for "The Surgeon," LaSalle handled "The Deception," and music supervisor
      <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Newman" title="Lionel Newman">
       Lionel Newman
      </a>
      composed his only music for the series with "The Interrogation" (those last three, like the other episodes, were otherwise tracked with the previously written music).
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast_history">
       Broadcast history
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planet_of_the_Apes_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Broadcast history">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The series ran in the U.S. from September 13 to December 20, 1974. The series was canceled after half a season because of low ratings due to direct competition by
      <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">
       NBC
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sanford_and_Son" title="Sanford and Son">
        Sanford and Son
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Chico_and_the_Man" title="Chico and the Man">
        Chico and the Man
       </a>
      </i>
      . Only thirteen of its fourteen episodes were broadcast; all 14 episodes were later included in the
      <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">
       DVD
      </a>
      box set.
     </p>
     <p>
      It was screened in Britain by 13 of the 14
      <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">
       ITV
      </a>
      companies from 13 October 1974 each Sunday, until 18 January 1975.
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Television" title="Scottish Television">
       STV
      </a>
      never screened the series in 1974/75, opting to broadcast
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Sale_of_the_Century_(UK_game_show)" title="Sale of the Century (UK game show)">
        Sale of the Century
       </a>
      </i>
      instead. The series was repeated in many regions from September 1975 until 1978, but was still not seen on STV. The series then received its first UK-wide transmissions on
      <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">
       Channel 4
      </a>
      in 1994, and later on the
      <a href="/wiki/Syfy_(UK_and_Ireland)" title="Syfy (UK and Ireland)">
       Sci Fi Channel
      </a>
      . The TV movie compilations have also been screened on
      <a href="/wiki/Sky_Movies" title="Sky Movies">
       Sky Movies
      </a>
      and the Horror Channel.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Telefilms">
       Telefilms
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planet_of_the_Apes_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Telefilms">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1980, several episodes of the series were edited into five made-for-television movies.
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <i>
        Back to the Planet of the Apes
       </i>
       ("Escape from Tomorrow" &amp; "The Trap")
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         9
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Forgotten City of the Planet of the Apes
       </i>
       ("Gladiators" &amp; "Legacy")
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Treachery and Greed on the Planet of the Apes
       </i>
       ("Horse Race" &amp; "The Tyrant")
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Life, Liberty and Pursuit on the Planet of the Apes
       </i>
       ("The Surgeon" &amp; "The Interrogation")
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Farewell to the Planet of the Apes
       </i>
       ("Tomorrow's Tide" &amp; "Up Above The World So High")
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      When the
      <i>
       Planet of the Apes
      </i>
      telefilms entered syndication,
      <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">
       ABC
      </a>
      's
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Owned_and_operated" title="Owned and operated">
       owned and operated
      </a>
      stations, who picked them up for their afternoon movie programs (under titles such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_4:30_Movie" title="The 4:30 Movie">
        The 4:30 Movie
       </a>
      </i>
      ), called upon Roddy McDowall to re-create his role of Galen in a series of new openings and closings specifically for these stations, billed as "The New Planet of the Apes." The introductions originally created by 20th Century Fox to open each film were replaced by a now-aged Galen (McDowall) looking back on the events of the telefilms. The openings and closings revealed Virdon and Burke's final fates: "They found their computer in another city and disappeared into space as suddenly as theyd arrived." According to "TV Zone Special" (1995 issue) McDowall filmed these "wraparounds" in 1980. The ABC openings and closings of these telefilms were neither aired on other stations nor included on any home media release.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Spinoffs">
       Spinoffs
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planet_of_the_Apes_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Spinoffs">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Most of the books and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planet_of_the_Apes_(comic_book)" title="Planet of the Apes (comic book)">
       comics
      </a>
      based on
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planet_of_the_Apes_(franchise)" title="Planet of the Apes (franchise)">
        Planet of the Apes
       </a>
      </i>
      are based on the films, not the TV series. However, there are some titles that do involve the TV show characters:
     </p>
     <p>
      NovelizationsFour novelizations of episodes, written by
      <a href="/wiki/George_Alec_Effinger" title="George Alec Effinger">
       George Alec Effinger
      </a>
      , were published by Award Books. Their titles are:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Planet of the Apes #1: Man the Fugitive
      </li>
      <li>
       Planet of the Apes #2: Escape From Tomorrow
      </li>
      <li>
       Planet of the Apes #3: Journey Into Terror
      </li>
      <li>
       Planet of the Apes #4: Lord of the Apes
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      British AnnualsBrown-Watson Books published three hardcover annuals featuring original stories about Virdon, Burke and Galen. These stories are a combination of comic strips and short fiction.
     </p>
     <p>
      Audio AdventuresPower Records produced four audio-only adventures based on the TV show. Their titles were:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Mountain of the Delphi
      </li>
      <li>
       Battle of Two Worlds
      </li>
      <li>
       Dawn of the Tree People
      </li>
      <li>
       Volcano
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Argentine ComicsSeven Spanish-language comics were published in Argentina, written by Jorge Claudio Morhain and Richard Barreiro, and illustrated by Sergio Mulko and T. Toledo. Released only in Argentina, they have never been officially published in English. However, PDFs of the comics, translated to English by fans, are available at Kassidy Rae's site. (See link below.)
     </p>
     <p>
      Filmstrip StoryChad Valley, a U.K. toy company, produced 32 short film-based comic strips containing an original TV-series-era story, packaged as the Chad Valley Picture Show Planet of the Apes Sliderama Projector (very similar to the many Give-a-Show projector sets of the 1970s). These strips are extremely rare and difficult to come by.
     </p>
