         <b>
          <dd>
           Noodling is the practice and sport of using your arm as bait to catch your prey. 
The word noodle is slang for a foolish person. Noodling is currently legal in only
eleven US states.
          </dd>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.shangralafamilyfun.com/ShangyFunList.html">
           <img alt="Shangrala's Extreme Noodling" height="425" src="http://www.ShangralaFamilyFun.com/2010/no1.jpg" width="640"/>
           <br/>
          </a>
          <dd>
           To begin with, a catfish noodler goes underwater to depths ranging from only a few feet 
to up to twenty feet, placing his hand inside a discovered catfish hole. If all goes 
as planned, the catfish will swim forward and latch onto the fisherman's hand, 
usually as a defensive maneuver in order to try to escape the hole. 
If the fish is particularly large, the noodler can hook the head around its gills.
          </dd>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.shangralafamilyfun.com/ShangyFunList.html">
           <img alt="Shangrala's Extreme Noodling" height="960" src="http://www.ShangralaFamilyFun.com/2010/no2.jpg" width="640"/>
           <br/>
          </a>
          <dd>
           Most noodlers have spotters who help them bring the catfish in, either to shore or 
to their boat. When a catfish bites onto a noodler, it holds on for quite a while.
          </dd>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.shangralafamilyfun.com/ShangyFunList.html">
           <img alt="Shangrala's Extreme Noodling" height="776" src="http://www.ShangralaFamilyFun.com/2010/no3.jpg" width="640"/>
           <br/>
          </a>
          <dd>
           With some of the biggest fish caught weighing in at up to 50-60 pounds, 
very few noodlers are strong enough to attempt noodling by themselves.
          </dd>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <a href="http://www.shangralafamilyfun.com/ShangyFunList.html">
           <img alt="Shangrala's Extreme Noodling" height="545" src="http://www.ShangralaFamilyFun.com/2010/no4.jpg" width="640"/>
           <br/>
          </a>
          <dd>
           Although carrying the fish after they have been subdued is not difficult, 
trying to secure a fish and remove it from one's hand at the same time 
can be a challenge.
          </dd>
