       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         FRAMED BATHROOM MIRROR
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-10-08T11:00:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 8, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://madincrafts.com/author/madincrafts/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            madincrafts
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://madincrafts.com/framed-bathroom-mirror/#comments">
           4 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="5447254" data-link="http://madincrafts.com/framed-bathroom-mirror/" data-summary="" data-title="FRAMED BATHROOM MIRROR">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/HowtoFrameaBathroomMirror.png">
          <img alt="How to Frame a Bathroom Mirror" border="0" height="1049" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/HowtoFrameaBathroomMirror_thumb.png" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="How to Frame a Bathroom Mirror" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ever since we first put an offer on The Mad House, I have wanted to frame out the mirror in
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/diy-bathroom-makeover-reveal/" target="_blank">
          the upstairs bathroom
         </a>
         . I have seen other bloggers who have framed out builder grade mirrors, and it makes the bathroom look so polished and finished. We needed to do a small bit of engineering with our frame, but it turned out to be a fairly easy upgrade to the room.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/unframedbathroommirror.jpg">
          <img alt="unframed bathroom mirror" border="0" height="1053" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/unframedbathroommirror_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="unframed bathroom mirror" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         My dad did all the hard work measuring, cutting and mitering the molding for the mirror frame. We bought 1/2 x 2 pine molding to using as furring strips since the front of the mirror stood about half an inch out from the wall. My dad cut the furring strips to just fit around the edges of the mirror. They are narrower than the molding for the actual frame, so the frame molding overlaps the mirror a bit and hangs over the edges of the furring strips a bit. I stained just the outside edges of the furring strips with the same gel stain that I used on the sink cabinetbecausethose edges would be visible behind the mirror frame.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/applyliquidnails.jpg">
          <img alt="apply liquid nails" border="0" height="1053" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/applyliquidnails_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="apply liquid nails" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         After the gel stain dried, I applied a generous zig zag of liquid nails heavy duty adhesive to back of the bottom strip.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/reinforcewithtape.jpg">
          <img alt="reinforce with tape" border="0" height="465" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/reinforcewithtape_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="reinforce with tape" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I positioned the strip flush to the mirror and wedged a few shims in the gap between the strip and the top of our countertop. I pressed the furring strip against the wall firmly, and then used some lengths of painters tape to be sure the strip stayed in place. TIP: If you are doing this project alone have the tape torn and waiting for you to use, so you can get everything up on the wall quickly.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/furringstripsaroundmirror.jpg">
          <img alt="furring strips around mirror" border="0" height="466" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/furringstripsaroundmirror_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="furring strips around mirror" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I repeated the process with the other three pieces of furring, making sure that the stained edge was always on the outside and that the strip was flush to the mirror.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/tapestripsinplace.jpg">
          <img alt="tape strips in place" border="0" height="1053" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/tapestripsinplace_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="tape strips in place" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         After the liquid nails were dry, I removed the painters tape so I could adhere the molding to the furring strips. This time I added the liquid nails to the front of the furring strips NOT the backs of the molding. You dont want adhesive on the part of the molding that overlaps the mirror, as it would be visible in the mirrors reflection.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Matchupcorners.jpg">
          <img alt="Match up corners" border="0" height="466" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Matchupcorners_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="Match up corners" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I started with by centering the bottom piece of molding and made sure it was level. That piece can serve as the reference for the rest of the frame. The side pieces went up the same way as the bottom piece, but I was careful to make sure the lines of the molding matched up at the corners.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Addmoldingtomirror.jpg">
          <img alt="Add molding to mirror" border="0" height="1053" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Addmoldingtomirror_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="Add molding to mirror" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I checked to make sure the side pieces were plum and pressed the molding onto the furring strips. More pieces of painters helped to keep the molding from slipping around as I added in the other two sides of the frame.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Framemirrorwithmolding.jpg">
          <img alt="Frame mirror with molding" border="0" height="1053" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Framemirrorwithmolding_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="Frame mirror with molding" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         After the liquid nails was dry, I removed the painters tape and the project was done! I love how finished the bathroom looks with the frame around the mirror.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Mirrorbeforeandafter.jpg">
          <img alt="Mirror before and after" border="0" height="405" src="http://madincrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Mirrorbeforeandafter_thumb.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto; border-width: 0px;" title="Mirror before and after" width="700"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         It doesnt seem like a little bit of molding should make a big difference, but it definitely does. Switchingthe two tone light fixture helps out with a
         <a href="http://www.menards.com/main/p-2360596-c-7502.htm" target="_blank">
          more updated schoolhouse fixture
         </a>
         helps too, of course. If you have a big plain mirror in your bathroom, I really suggest trying this project out yourself. As DIY projects go, it is not too difficult and the results are fantastic!
        </p>
