         <p>
          This weeks Friday Favorite is David Boozer from the beautiful Pacific Northwest. Ive been following David for over a year now, and what I really like about him is he taught me how to craft a blog post that would be interesting and visually appealing.
         </p>
         <p>
          When youre building your blog, you dont want posts that have what look like run-on sentences, paragraphs that seem to go on forever, and no graphics.
         </p>
         <p>
          You want shorter paragraphs, you want large, bolded headings and some good visuals.  And I dont mean icons and cartoon graphics.  So David helped me by teaching me about imagery, not just images.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/CwLAs3zrEsw?rel=0" width="640">
          </iframe>
         </center>
         <p>
          David takes you step-by-step in crafting a visually appealing and interesting blog post and helps you set up your blog in a nice, clean way, with all the proper WordPress plugins that help making blogging a lot easier.
         </p>
         <p>
          Davids online mentoring program will help you build, manage and brand an online business from start to finish.  So if youre feeling stuck or you dont know in which direction to go, you should really check out his free videos and his
          <a href="http://davidboozer.com/" target="_blank">
           online mentoring program
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Here is a great video from David to give you an example of why hes such a great online mentor:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/WrnM6xC4Vms?rel=0" width="640">
          </iframe>
         </center>
         <p>
          And like every other person I follow, David doesnt believe in the hype either.  He gives away a lot of information for free because if you give away as much as you can afford, it will come back to you.
         </p>
         <p>
          What you give away for free is just one of the best ways for people to get to know, like and trust you.  Once youve built that trust, those who can will purchase your services and theyll purchase products and services that you recommend because theyll trust your judgment.
         </p>
         <p>
          And allow me to repeat how to know who you should follow, and why I recommend you follow the same people that I do.
         </p>
         <p>
          You should only be following people who:
          <br/>
          youre comfortable with
          <br/>
          you resonate with
          <br/>
          speak to you, not at you
          <br/>
          you can follow along easily with what theyre talking about
          <br/>
          what theyre saying meets with your goals
          <br/>
          teach you in a way that you can learn easily
          <br/>
          know how to take the time and have the patience to build your trust
         </p>
         <p>
          And again, if youre struggling with building and branding your online business and you want to know the steps to building trust with your visitors, subscribe to Davids YouTube channel and his blog and youll be in good hands!
         </p>
