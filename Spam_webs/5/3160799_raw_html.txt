        <p>
         H.T. was my date tonight at a sneak peak of
         <a href="http://www.wholefoodsmarket.com/stores/columbia-md" target="_blank" title="Whole Foods Columbia">
          Whole Foods Columbias
         </a>
         new menu for The Counter, anin-store Americana style diner. H.T. looked so cute in his suit jacket and Chuck Taylors  his signaturelook. Its kind of hipster for an 8-year-old, but thats how he rolls. It was fun to see him interact with a room full of adults hes never met before. He was surprisingly (and charmingly) shy.
        </p>
        <p>
         I love these events for local journalistsand bloggers because its so much fun to talk shop with other people who are obsessed with food, Pinterest and their bounce rates. Its like I can let my inside voice outside  I can actually sayall that random stuff Ive been thinking about but keeping to myself.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Counter menu was a new take on American diner food with healthier ingredients. There were some caramelized Brussels sprouts that were so good I know I ll try to replicate them at home, and a short rib blend burger with cartelized unions and mushrooms that was huge hit all the way around the room. Short rib burgers? Yes! But notuntil we finish our Whole30. You can try the new Counter menu yourself starting tomorrow, Friday, 16 January.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heresa healthy, filling recipe for Winter Vegetable Soup that is chock-full of butternut squash, carrots and spinach. Make a big pot on Sundayand eat it all week for lunch.

            1 butternut squash, peeled and chopped into half-inch dice
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            8 cups chicken or vegetable broth
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 14-ounce cans whole tomatoes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 sprigs thyme
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tablespoons fresh oregano, chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon sea salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups fresh spinach leaves, packed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Juice from half a lemon
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a large stockpot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the leeks, garlic and onion and cook until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the carrots and butternut squash and cook an additional 5 more minutes, stirring to keep the vegetables from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour in the stock and bring the soup to a boil. Add the tomatoes, thyme, rosemary, oregano, pepper and salt. Turn the heat down to low and simmer until vegetables are tender, about 30 minutes. Stir in the fresh spinach and lemon juice. Serve hot.
           </li>
          </ol>

        <h3>
         About the Photographs
        </h3>
        <p>
         This is another straightforward shot taken just before dinner, with J. and the boys waiting in the wings. I always find the color of tomatoes really tricky to photograph  theyre so easily made into an otherworldly, much too intense orange. I tried to avoid that by adding fresh green spinach just before I took the photographs, after Id already set up the angle and the lighting.
        </p>
        <p>
         I took these photos with my
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Nikon-10-2MP-Digital-18-135mm-Zoom-Nikkor/dp/B000HGIWN4" target="_blank" title="Nikon D80">
          Nikon D80
         </a>
         , using a
         <a href="http://www.nikonusa.com/en/Nikon-Products/Product/Camera-Lenses/AF-Nikkor-28mm-f%252F2.8D.html?CID=SEM-Q114-Google_Nikon_-_DSA" target="_blank" title="Nikkor 28 mm lens">
          28mmf2.8 NIKKOR wide anglelens
         </a>
         . I used a Lowell Ego tabletop light like
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Lowel-Digital-Imaging-Tabletop-Fluorescent/dp/B0009K50RO" target="_blank" title="Lowell Ego light">
          this one
         </a>
         , at around 5p.m., in partial darkness, with all otherindoor lights turned off but with some sunlight streaming through the windows.The light was positioned directly to the right of the food. I used a white foam boardto the left of the food as a reflector. My ISO was set at 500, and the shutter speed was 1/200. The aperture was 2.8in the photo at the top of the page and 3.5in the shotbelow it.
        </p>
