            <p>
             As the amount of data we produce every day is growing quickly and many of us now have a tons offile, documents, photos, music, and videos.More files you have, your storage will getting bigger and bigger, also its getting harder to manage tons of file, so we need to backup and protect those files. It is important to backing up our data locally, but its good to backup your data on a cloud storage, so you dont need to worry if you accidentally delete your data from your local storage. The
             <strong>
              Cloud Storage
             </strong>
             promised us us infinite cheap storage where we can save our data secure and encrypted. Cloud backup must be a part for your backup plan.
            </p>
            <p>
             So this time I will give you list of a
             <strong>
              Worth to try6 Cloud Storage for Linux,
             </strong>
             There are plenty of personal
             <strong>
              cloud storage
             </strong>
             service that available on the net. Here are a few online backup tools that aim to make cloud storage backups easy to use.
            </p>
            <h2>
             1. Google Drive
            </h2>
            <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_409" style="width: 160px">
             <a href="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-1.png">
              <img alt="Worth to try 6 Cloud Storage for Linux - 1" class="wp-image-409 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-1-150x150.png" width="150"/>
             </a>
             <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
              Google Drive
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
            <p>
             This one must befamiliar to us,
             <strong>
              Google Drive
             </strong>
             is a service from Google that focused on a Cloud Storage. If you have Google Account, just open
             <a href="%20http://drive.google.com" target="_blank" title="Google Drive Link">
              http://drive.google.com
             </a>
             to use
             <strong>
              Google Drive
             </strong>
             service. If you have not register to Google yet, you should register and try this
             <strong>
              Google Drive
             </strong>
             , its worth. Because when you register to Google you will get 15GB storage for free. Your storage works with Gmail, Google+, you can keep your files, save your email attachments and backup your files directly to your
             <strong>
              Google Drive
             </strong>
             , there is also a storage plan if you need more space. Officially Google still working for the Client application, so you can open your drive just via your web browser. But dont worry there is an Unofficial Google Drive Client like
             <strong>
              Insync,
             </strong>
             <strong>
              drive,
             </strong>
             and
             <strong>
              Grive
             </strong>
             .
            </p>
            <h2>
             2. DropBox
            </h2>
            <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_410" style="width: 160px">
             <a href="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-2.png">
              <img alt="Worth to try 6 Cloud Storage for Linux - 2" class="wp-image-410 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-2-150x150.png" width="150"/>
             </a>
             <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
              Dropbox
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
            <p>
             This one must be familiar too,
             <a href="https://www.dropbox.com" target="_blank" title="Dropbox Official Website">
              <strong>
               Dropbox
              </strong>
             </a>
             is a free file hosting service by Dropbox, Inc. Dropbox offers cloud storage, file synchronization, personal cloud, andof course client software.
             <strong>
              Dropbox
             </strong>
             lets you bring your documents, photos, and videos, anywhere, with Dropbox you can share them easily.
             <strong>
              Dropbox
             </strong>
             will create a special folder on their computer, which uses to synchronizes data on that special folder to
             <strong>
              Dropbox
             </strong>
             cloud Storage, so the same file and the same contents will be appears on the other computer that install
             <strong>
              Dropbox
             </strong>
             Client.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Dropbox
             </strong>
             can be opened via Web Browser or you can install the client on your computer, smartphones, or tablets.The client is well-designed to make it easy-to-use, just drag and drop and
             <strong>
              Dropbox
             </strong>
             will sync the file automatically. If you register to Dropbox you will get
             <strong>
              Dropbox Basic
             </strong>
             package that give you free 2GB space, but if you need more free space there arevariety of ways to get more space, you can earn free space by referring your friends or you can upgrade your account to
             <strong>
              Dropbox Pro
             </strong>
             ? thats your choice, there is also Dropbox for Business for only 15$/month per users.
            </p>
            <h2>
             3.Copy
            </h2>
            <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_411" style="width: 160px">
             <a href="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-3.png">
              <img alt="Worth to try 6 Cloud Storage for Linux - 3" class="wp-image-411 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-3-150x150.png" width="150"/>
             </a>
             <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
              Copy
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
            <p>
             <a href="https://www.copy.com/" target="_blank" title="Copy Official Website">
              <strong>
               Copy
              </strong>
             </a>
             similar to Dropbox, it has folder just like Dropbox, so you will have a
             <strong>
              Copy
             </strong>
             folder. The Client has support for every Linux distribution and the support is really wonderful. Why ? because
             <strong>
              Copy
             </strong>
             do not use any binaries to install.
             <strong>
              Copy
             </strong>
             has provide just a .tar .gz file, so all you need to is just extract the file and run the executeable, it is really simple, you cant really beat it. Its a great choice and worth to try for everyone that need a cloud storage for work, because
             <b>
              Copy
             </b>
             comes with 15GB disk space for free.
            </p>
            <h2>
             4. pCloud
            </h2>
            <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_412" style="width: 160px">
             <a href="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-4.png">
              <img alt="Worth to try 6 Cloud Storage for Linux - 4" class="wp-image-412 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-4-150x150.png" width="150"/>
             </a>
             <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
              pCloud
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
            <p>
             <a href="https://www.pcloud.com" target="_blank" title="pCloud Official Website">
              <strong>
               pCloud
              </strong>
             </a>
             is a decent cloud storage service,
             <strong>
              pCloud
             </strong>
             has support for Linux, you can see on their websites there is currently listed the links to Ubuntu binaries, but there is no other distribution officially supported.
             <strong>
              pCloud
             </strong>
             client application is well designed and it works well with the system overall. If you sign up to pCloud you will get free
             <strong>
              20GB
             </strong>
             storage.
            </p>
            <h2>
             5. SpiderOak
            </h2>
            <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_413" style="width: 160px">
             <a href="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-5.png">
              <img alt="Worth to try 6 Cloud Storage for Linux - 5" class="wp-image-413 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-5-150x150.png" width="150"/>
             </a>
             <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
              SpiderOak
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <a href="http://spideroak.com" target="_blank" title="SpiderOak Official Website">
               SpideOak
              </a>
             </strong>
             is another great cloud storage service that support multi-platform especially for Linux foryour backup management.
             <strong>
              SpiderOak
             </strong>
             <strong>
             </strong>
             offer a folder syncing and sharing features that focused on security.
             <strong>
              SpiderOak
             </strong>
             values your security and privacy, its a serous option for who thosepreferconfidentiality.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              SpiderOak
             </strong>
             clients are available for Linux, Windows, Mac, Windows, Android and IOS.
             <strong>
              SpiderOak
             </strong>
             has a Wonderful Linux client that makes it easier to use and make it simple for you to backup your files. If you register yourselfto
             <strong>
              SpiderOak
             </strong>
             Services you will get 2GB free storage, also if you need 100 GB of extra space you can pay up for $10/month.
            </p>
            <h2>
             6.ownCloud
            </h2>
            <figure class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_414" style="width: 160px">
             <a href="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-6.png">
              <img alt="Worth to try 6 Cloud Storage for Linux - 6" class="wp-image-414 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://opnux.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Worth-to-try-6-Cloud-Storage-for-Linux-6-150x150.png" width="150"/>
             </a>
             <figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">
              ownCloud
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
            <p>
             <a href="http://owncloud.org/" target="_blank" title="ownCloud Official Website">
              <strong>
               ownCloud
              </strong>
             </a>
             is very similar to the commonly known
             <strong>
              Dropbox
             </strong>
             .
             <strong>
              ownCloud
             </strong>
             is commonly termed as file hosting, the main difference of
             <strong>
              ownCloud
             </strong>
             to another cloud storage is free and opensource, and because of that its allowing anyone to install and operate it without any charge at all on their own server, of course with no limits on storage space or number of connected client.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              ownCloud
             </strong>
             make itvery easy to setup your own cloud storage service, and also its Dropbox-like.
             <strong>
              ownCloud
             </strong>
             has its own task editors, task list, calender, and more and that means you can get more that a just file sync service. Beyond from that, you also get a built-in music player inside your
             <strong>
              ownCloud
             </strong>
             , there is also a place to store your contacts, bookmarking service, and a photo gallery.
            </p>
            <h2>
             Conslusion
            </h2>
            <p>
             Today, its easier to securely backup your files because the Cloud Storage service. The most service above offer a free level of service, so you will not losing any money. Whatever cloud storage service you are using right now, the main thing about cloud storage is backup you files regularly, every cloud storage service has its own pros and cons, so pick your choice.
            </p>
