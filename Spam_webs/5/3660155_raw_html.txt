        <h1 class="page-title entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Googles Penguin Penalty: Is Your Site Affected?
        </h1>
       </header>
       <section class="post-meta">
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.eyeflow.com/author/cory/" title="View all posts by Cory Bosket">
          Cory Bosket
         </a>
        </span>
        on
        <a class="post-date updated" href="http://www.eyeflow.com/googles-penguin-penalty-site-affected/" itemprop="datePublished" rel="bookmark" title="1:40 pm">
         12/31/2014
        </a>
        <span class="comments-link">
         <span class="mdash">
          
         </span>

       <section class="post-entry">
        <div class="excerpt-thumb">
         <img alt="google penalty diagnosis" class="attachment-full wp-post-image" height="295" itemprop="thumbnailURL" src="http://www.eyeflow.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/744x295xgoogle-penalty-diagnosis.jpg.pagespeed.ic.wVBTMRX8aF.jpg" width="744"/>
         <!-- .video-btn -->
        </div>
        <!--.excerpt-thumb -->
        <span itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          If your site has experienced a sudden decrease in organic traffic or you begin to see your targeted keyword rankings fall from Googles front page results, your site may be experiencing the effects of a Google Penalty.
         </p>
         <p>
          While there are a variety of Google search engine penalties that may be causing the problem, the Penguin Algorithm is one of the most common causes. Since April 2012, Googles Penguin algorithm has been working to help clean up some of the most common over-optimization, low-quality link building and spamming techniques from across the web.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you notice a sudden drop off in your sites organic traffic, dont panic. Keeping an eye out for the warning signs of a Penguin Penalty can help you quickly diagnose and fix the problem. With
          <a href="http://searchengineland.com/google-says-penguin-shift-continuous-updates-210580" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://searchengineland.com/google-says-penguin-shift-continuous-updates-210580', 'rumors');">
           rumors
          </a>
          circulating that Google will now be updating the Penguin algorithm on a continuous, rolling basis, its now more important than ever to consistently monitor your site for signs of a Penguin Penatly.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Conducting a GooglePenguin Penalty Diagnosis
         </h2>
         <h3>
          1. Check for Google Manual Actions
         </h3>
         <p>
          <img alt="manual penguin penalty from google webmaster tools" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7568" height="292" src="http://www.eyeflow.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/manual-penguin-penalty.png.pagespeed.ce.1YbJLuyPJQ.png" width="699"/>
          <br/>
          If your site is showing signs of a Penguin Penalty, the first course of action you should take is to login to your Google Webmaster Tools account. Within the Webmaster Tools platform, check if your site has any manual action notifications as a result of potentially spammy inbound links to your site. If you do have one of these manual action notifications, it is crucial that you take immediate action to remedy the problem and file a reconsideration request.
         </p>
         <p>
          A manual action notification means that a Google employee manually identified links to your site that may be associated with spam content. Even if your site isnt receiving a manual action notification it may still be affected by the Penguin algorithm so you may want to consider running a quality link assessment on your site.
         </p>
         <h3>
          2. Penguin Penalty and Your Sites Analytics
         </h3>
         <p>
          Another way to diagnose a potential Penguin Penalty is to check your sites analytical data. Using an organic research data analysis tool, like
          <a href="http://www.semrush.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.semrush.com/', 'SEMrush');">
           SEMrush
          </a>
          , will help you track your keyword position changes as well as chart your organic traffic over time.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_7570" style="width: 753px">
          <a href="http://algoroo.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://algoroo.com/', '');">
           <img alt="Tools like algoroo.com and mozcast.com monitor daily changes." class="wp-image-7570 size-full" height="190" rel="magnific" src="http://www.eyeflow.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/743x190xalgoroo-google-algorithim-penalties.jpg.pagespeed.ic.tUUlvd8jVj.jpg" width="743"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Tools like algoroo.com and mozcast.com monitor daily changes.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          If you notice that your sites organic traffic levels suddenly drop after a certain date, check to see if the drop date corresponds with a
          <a href="http://moz.com/google-algorithm-change" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://moz.com/google-algorithm-change', 'recorded algorithm update');">
           recorded algorithm update
          </a>
          . If thedrop in traffic matches up with a Penguin update, chances are that your site is reaping from the effects of a Penguin Penalty.
         </p>
         <h3>
          3. Assess Your Link Building History
         </h3>
         <p>
          Since 2012, SEOs have seen a major shift in the way that a site can earn backlinks. In the past, it was common practice to spread as many inbound links as possible across the web and several SEOs and webmasters used this tactic to gain a quick boost in their sites search engine rankings.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, all this changed when Google unleashed their Penguin algorithm. Nowadays, Google aims to severely punish sites who are breaking
          <a href="http://www.eyeflow.com/demystifying-link-schemes-the-3-golden-rules-of-link-building/">
           The Golden Rules of Link Building.
          </a>
          If your site has an SEO strategy in place, it is important that you know what link building tactics will both help and harm your website.
         </p>
         <h3>
          4. Checking Your Sites Backlink Profile
         </h3>
         <p>
          The most effective way to assess your
          <a href="https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/55281?hl=en" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/55281?hl=en', 'websites backlink profile');">
           websites backlink profile
          </a>
          is by using Google Webmaster Tools. Googles Links to Your Site page will list all the inbound links that search engine bots discover throughout the web. This feature provides information about the most common link sources and what pages on your site are being linked to.
         </p>
         <p>
          Conducting a comprehensive link audit is the first step to diagnosing or even avoiding a future penalty. Consider using a variety of link analysis tools from across the web to gather more information about your sites backlinks from across the web.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Open Site Explorer backlink anchor text analysis by moz.com." class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7573" height="274" src="http://www.eyeflow.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/906x274xopen-site-explorer-backlink-anchor-text.jpg.pagespeed.ic.JwM2IZSkzS.jpg" width="906"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          As you make your way through the list of links to your site, visit a few along the way  specifically those that appear to be linking to your site more often than others. Keep an eye out for some of the most common sure ways to get a penguin penalty including:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Keyword heavy anchor text
          </li>
          <li>
           Links that come from Spam sites
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/2700611?hl=en" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/2700611?hl=en', 'Unnatural links');">
            Unnatural links
           </a>
           to your site
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          As a rule of thumb, making sure that the inbound links to your site are naturally earned and are in-line with Googles Webmaster Guidelines will help you keep your site penalty free.
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Road to Penguin Penalty Recovery
         </h2>
         <p>
          If you have determined that your sites performance is suffering as the result of a Penguin Penalty, take immediate action is crucial to help keep your websites SEO performance up to par. Bringing your case to the SEO experts at Eyeflow Internet Marketing can help your site
          <a href="http://www.eyeflow.com/services/google-penalty-recovery/">
           recover faster than ever
          </a>
          . Our dedicated staff lives and breathes SEO, committing themselves to repairing your site with their comprehensive manual link analysis and disavowing all unnatural, malicious links to your site in the process. However, our work doesnt end there. After your site has removed all bad links, we help provide your site with the roadmap to Penguin Penalty recovery, constantly monitoring your site for signs of success and help prevent future penalties. Dont allow your site to fall victim to a Penguin Penalty, contact Eyeflow today and let us help your site regain and build momentum towards its search engine success.
         </p>
