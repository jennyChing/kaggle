        Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow
       </a>
      </h1>
      <div id="creditsHolder" style="margin:10px 0 0 0;">

       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_149507" style="width: 636px">
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="size-large wp-image-149507" data-lazy-src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/480984229-626x417.jpg" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="626"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="size-large wp-image-149507" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/480984229-626x417.jpg" width="626"/>
        </noscript>
        Seth K. Hughes
       </div>
       <p>
        Theres no better way to grow closer to your family or friendsor your carthan to go on an extended road trip.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-149498">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        To guarantee memories to last a lifetime, select the most interesting roads possible and skip the big population centers rife with chain restaurants, cookie-cutter hotels, and crowded freeways. In other words, explore the far less traveledand far more interestingprecincts of our country. Of course, when youve put a little distance between you and crowded civilization, you dont want any surprises. Here are eight tips to follow in the months and weeks leading up to departure that that will increase your odds of coming home happy.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Choose Your Destination
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        First decide how much time you can invest in your trip. Then determine how many hours you want to drive each day (keeping in mind that your companions might have different endurance limits). Then comes the fun part: choosing your destinations. Keep in mind that if your trip will last more than a few days, youll likely want to build in a day off or two at particularly interesting stops along the route.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-149506" data-lazy-src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/90441733-626x417.jpg" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="626"/>
       </p>
       <noscript>
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-149506" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/90441733-626x417.jpg" width="626"/>
       </noscript>
       <div style="margin-top: -3px; margin-bottom: 20px; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #666666; font-size: 7.5pt; line-height: 8.5pt; text-align: left;">
        Car Culture/Getty Images
       </div>
       <p>
        <b>
         Focus Your Trip
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Now that you know where you want to go and what you want to do, make sure those choices agree with the vehicle you plan to take. A sports car with super-sticky tires is perfect for mountain passes but is completely wrong for off-road canyon exploration. Yes, you can cover a wide variety of topography in an SUV or crossover, but such a vehicle is a jack of all trades, master of none. Decide what vehicle you want to use and plan a trip that suits its talents. Or just get a rally
        <a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/porsche/911">
         Porsche 911
        </a>
        like the one pictured above, although you might need a support vehicle if youre bringing the kids.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-149505" data-lazy-src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/112301286-626x417.jpg" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="626"/>
       </p>
       <noscript>
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-149505" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/112301286-626x417.jpg" width="626"/>
       </noscript>
       <p>
        <b>
         Check Your Tires
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        At this point, you have an estimate of how many miles youll be traveling. Make sure you have enough tire life to complete the trip. As roads get more interesting, tire stores usually become scarce, especially those that stock the sort of high-performance rubber fitted to sports cars or the huge rollers on todays 20-inch (or larger) SUV wheels. Head off potential tire problems before you get started by making sure theyre in top condition, and make sure that your vehicle is shod appropriately for the expected terrain and weather.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Check Your Vehicle
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Rural America is not the place to replace your battery, brake pads, or shock absorbers, particularly if youve upgraded your car with non-factory items. Check your consumable components and replace any that are marginal. And dont forget to check your spare tire, or whatever flat-tire sealing/inflation kit your vehicle uses. Flat tires are rare, but they happenand being stranded is never fun.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-149503" data-lazy-src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/112301285-626x417.jpg" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="626"/>
       </p>
       <noscript>
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-149503" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/112301285-626x417.jpg" width="626"/>
       </noscript>
       <p>
        <b>
         Pack an Emergency Kit for You
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Accidents can happen, and you never know when you might get stranded by the whims of Mother Nature or your vehicle. Basic first-aid supplies, some warm clothes, a good umbrella, a few quarts of water, and non-perishable snacks will keep you going if you hit some really bad luck. A wise man once suggested to us dog food as emergency rations because it lasts forever, and you wont eat it unless you really have to.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Pack an Emergency Kit for Your Car
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        When properly maintained, modern cars dont blow radiator hoses or throw accessory drive belts. But when you drive something even as borderline exotic as, say, a BMW M3 or Chevrolet Corvette, you might want to bring along any special fluids it needs. If your trip includes lots of dirt trails, extra air and cabin filters cant hurt.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-149502" data-lazy-src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/102071767-626x417.jpg" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="626"/>
       </p>
       <noscript>
        <img alt="Planning an Epic Road Trip: 8 Tips You Need to Follow" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-149502" height="417" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/102071767-626x417.jpg" width="626"/>
       </noscript>
       <p>
        <b>
         Pack Shrewdly
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Although your car or truck has the power to haul a few steamer trunks, you dont want to be packed to the gills. A road trip is so much nicer (and safer) when you can see out of every window and theres nothing hard ricocheting around your trunk when youre challenging the Beartooth Highway.
       </p>
       <hr noshade="noshade"/>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/road-trip-pre-departure-checklist-last-minute-items-that-can-make-or-break-your-trip">
          Road Trip Pre-Departure Checklist: Last-Minute Items that Can Make or Break Your Trip
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/features/1977-mercedes-benz-450sel-69-road-trip-feature">
          Golden Brown: Four Days in a Mercedes-Benz 450SEL 6.9
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/columns/john-phillips-the-perils-of-cross-country-trips-column">
          John Phillips: The Perils of Cross-Country Trips
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <hr noshade="noshade"/>
       <p>
        <b>
         Make a Checklist of Critical Items
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Are you allergic to bee stings? Is anyone on the trip prone to carsickness? Do you have the latest updates for your navigation systems database, or a list of the speed traps on your planned route? National Park passes? Your personal stash of hot sauce? All of us have between one and two dozen items specific to our lives that are critical to a successful trip. Start making a list a couple weeks before you leave to make sure you dont leave anything behind.
       </p>
