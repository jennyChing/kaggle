        <div class="article-content clearfix">
         <p>
          No. Not viral marketing for the NYC-set
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/202326/syfy-announces-sharknado-2-asks-twitter-for-a-tag-line/" target="_blank">
           Sharknado sequel
          </a>
          . This is something that stinks much worse. Early Wednesday morning, around midnight somewhere near to the 34th St Station in midtown Manhattan, an Instagrammer snapped a photo of a dead shark found inside a subway car. Gotta love NYC,
          <a href="http://instagram.com/p/csw8TIBsYv/" target="_blank">
           reads
          </a>
          the caption. Casual dead shark on the subway.
         </p>
         <p>
          The photo, along with several others, were submitted to the metro blog Gothamist with the
          <a href="http://gothamist.com/2013/08/07/photos_dead_shark_on_subway.php#photo-1" target="_blank">
           below description
          </a>
          of the photographers experience riding aboard that subway car with the carcass:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           I board a car thats not terribly full and as soon as I enter, a stench hits my nose. Its not the typical urine/trash smell, its  fishy? I look down to the end of the car to see a dead shark on the floor. I think I stood there for a good minute just staring, thinking Is this for real?! Oh come ON, NYC! One of my fellow passengers remarked, Ive been riding the subway for 15 years, and this is the weirdest thing Ive seen. And Ive seen EVERYTHING. The train filled as we made our way to Astoria, every new passenger was getting excited about it. Once we got to Queensboro Plaza, an MTA employee made all of us move to another car.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Others found the discovery amusing.
         </p>
         <blockquote class="twitter-tweet">
          <p>
           Savages! MT

          <p>
            Mike Hayes (@michaelhayes)
           <a href="https://twitter.com/michaelhayes/statuses/365146675444056064">

         </blockquote>

         <p>
          An MTA supervisor confirmed the authenticity of the shark, placed it in a garbage bag and threw it in the trash  because thats what we do with dead sharks in New York City. Check out more photos from the
          <a href="http://gothamist.com/2013/08/07/photos_dead_shark_on_subway.php#photo-1" target="_blank">
           subway shark incident here
