              <a href="http://backlinko.com/viral-marketing" target="_blank">

             </p>

              This post is going to show you how I reached the top 5 (and still climbing) for a competitive SEO keyword.
             </p>
             <p>
              Before I jump into the intense stuff, let me explain why viral marketing works for SEO.

             <h2>
              <span id="Why-Does-Viral-Marketing-Work-for-SEO">
               Why Does Viral Marketing Work (for SEO)?
              </span>
             </h2>
             <p>
              The reason why it works is actually pretty simple:
             </p>
             <p>
              highly valuable piece of content + real social shares + authoritative links over time = increased rankings
             </p>
             <p>
              If youre missing one of these elements, you wont see the ranking improvements.
             </p>
             <p>
              Keep in mind, the type of viral Im talking about is essentially building authority and trust for a resource overtime. When Google sees that links are consistently hitting a resource and there are social signals to back it, the algorithm is going to strongly believe that the resource is of high value.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <span id="The-Details">
               The Details
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              Like some of my other experiments such as
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/how-to-build-tier-one-backlinks/#What-About-Social-Signals8230Do-They-Work" target="_blank">
               the social signals test
              </a>
              , I decided to test this one on my own blog.
             </p>
             <p>
              For this test to be successful, I had to use an article that is popular on my blog and know that it would continue to gain traction over time.
             </p>
             <p>
              The post that fulfilled this criteria was
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/anchor-text/" target="_blank">
               my anchor text article
              </a>
              . Obviously, I was wanted to rank this post for the keyword anchor text, which gets around 1,900 searches per month.
             </p>
             <p>
              On the surface, this isnt a ton of volume, but A) its in the competitive SEO industry and B) I knew that the article would rank for long-tail keywords as well, which would boost the overall search volume.
             </p>
             <p>
              Keep in mind, I wasnt doing this experiment as way to explode traffic to my site. I was doing it because I wanted to see the ranking effects. If it worked, I could then use the same exact strategy on my personal and clients websites.
             </p>
             <p>
              So, HOW did I do it?
             </p>
             <h3>
              <span id="How-I-Simulated-Viral-Marketing">
               How I Simulated Viral Marketing?
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              The simulated process was broken into six elements:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Content Analysis
              </li>
              <li>
               Link Analysis
              </li>
              <li>
               Content Development
              </li>
              <li>
               Social Signal Attraction
              </li>
              <li>
               Link Acquisition
              </li>
              <li>
               Link Strengthening
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              The most important part of this process is in the analysis. After reading Backlinkos case study, I immediately began my analysis of the site that achieved viral rankings. I started by looking at this content.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <span id="Element-1-Content-Analysis">
               Element #1: Content Analysis
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              I quickly analyzed the content of
              <a href="http://bootstrapbay.com/blog/free-stock-photos/" target="_blank">
               the viral article
              </a>
              and it wasnt text heavy at all. So I knew my longer text-based post would perform better in the SERPs for both my main keyword and long tails.
             </p>
             <p>
              Some other important things I saw were that he was linking out to several relevant authority sites, which establishes the article as a resource to Google.
             </p>
             <p>
              Another important aspect I analyzed was keyword density. For free stock photos the density was around 1.52%, which is actually higher than I expected, but thats mainly because the article wasnt text heavy. Either way, keeping the density below 2% if usually a good rule of thumb and thats exactly what I did for my article as well (its around 1.91%).
             </p>
             <p>
              Also, he had one internal link in the article going back to his homepage  personally, Im not a fan of linking back to the homepage because there is way more potential to build the overall authority of your site if you use
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/seo-case-study-2/" target="_blank">
               a silo structure
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              Either way, since my article is 6,000 + words, I knew there was going to be a lot of opportunity to link to some of my other resources.
             </p>
             <p>
              Lastly, he used tags, which I wont be using because they have ZERO impact on your rankings and produce unnecessary duplicate content on your site.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <span id="Element-2-Link-Analysis">
               Element #2: Link Analysis
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              After analyzing the content, I knew I could produce something of equal value, and all I needed to do was analyze the links he acquired. I immediately tossed his site into the big three link analysis tools: Ahrefs, Majestic, and Open Site Explorer.
             </p>
             <p>
              There are three important factors I was looking for when analyzing his links:
             </p>
             <ol>
              <li>
               link velocity
              </li>
              <li>
               link authority
              </li>
              <li>
               anchor text diversification
              </li>
             </ol>
             <h5>
              Link Velocity
             </h5>
             <p>
              I personally prefer Ahrefs for analyzing the velocity of links. In seconds, I was able to see that his velocity was natural and easily something I could replicate in that same amount of time.
             </p>
             <p>
              Heres what I saw:
              <br/>
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Link-Velocity.png">
               <img alt="Link Velocity" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3063" height="352" src="//www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Link-Velocity.png" width="837"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              So, in around 8 months, he acquired 90 + referring domains. I knew I would only need half or less than that amount because I was going to be in direct control of the links.
             </p>
             <h5>
              Link Authority
             </h5>
             <p>
              Next, I had to analyze how authoritative his links actually were. For the most part, the majority of his links were coming from social bookmarking websites.
             </p>
             <p>
              He did have a contextual link from Backlinko, which will obviously help to maintain his rankings because Brians site is very strong.
             </p>
             <p>
              But besides that, there were probably 20-30 contextual links hitting his resource. The other referring domains were the result of social shares and bookmarks.
             </p>
             <p>
              Both things that I could easily create artificially if necessary.
             </p>
             <p>
              The only thing I really cared about in his link profile were the contextual links, which are obviously the most difficult to acquire.
             </p>
             <h5>
              Anchor Text Diversification
             </h5>
             <p>
              I was now pretty confident that I could replicate his link profile after my analysis, and the last thing I had to do was analyze his anchor text diversification.
             </p>
             <p>
              And just like I expected, it was extremely unoptimized.
             </p>
             <p>
              The majority of his anchor text consisted of variations of his title, naked links, and only a very small amount of exact or partial match anchors. One important thing to keep in mind is that his target keyword is in his title, so whenever it is used as an anchor it is boosting the articles authority for that keyword.
             </p>
             <p>
              As I explained in case study #3,
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/seo-case-study-3/" target="_blank">
               you dont need to use many exact match anchors to rank
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Majestic-Anchor-Text.png">
               <img alt="Majestic Anchor Text" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3065" height="594" src="//www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Majestic-Anchor-Text.png" width="487"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Ahrefs-Anchor-Text.png">
               <img alt="Ahrefs Anchor Text" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3066" height="738" src="//www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Ahrefs-Anchor-Text.png" width="1168"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              So, the strategy I was going to implement was simple: I was going to use many variations of my title and naked links as anchor text.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <span id="Element-3-Content-Development">
               Element #3: Content Development
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              Ive kind of already hit on the content development portion of this project, but let me explain a little more. So I took my original article about anchor text that was around 1,500 words and turned it into a massive resource of nearly 7,000 words. I knew this would be more than enough to have success in the experiment. More importantly, for the content to attract social shares naturally, it needed to be unlike anything else.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <span id="Element-4-Social-Signal-Attraction">
               Element #4: Social Signal Attraction
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              So, this article naturally acquired a decent amount of social shares and continued to do so, but the way I really got my social shares to climb was by first promoting it on Twitter and Stumbleupon, and then using a social locker to block off some of the valuable content. Im no longer using the social locker because the article has a sufficient amount of shares.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <span id="Element-5-Link-Acquisition">
               Element #5: Link Acquisition
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              Okay now the part youve been waiting for
             </p>
             <p>
              How did I simulate a similar viral effect without needing to reach out for links?
             </p>
             <p>
              This is going to be a crazy revelation for you, so please fasten your seat belt.
             </p>
             <p>
              I used my money and gave it to someone to get my links.
             </p>
             <p>
              CRAZY right?!
             </p>
             <p>
              The real magic is what TYPES of links I went after. I wanted the majority of my links to live on sites with a DA above 30 and I achieved that pretty well.
             </p>
             <p>
              Here are the metrics for the sites Ive acquired links from:
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Domain-Authority.png">
               <img alt="Domain Authority" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3073" height="301" src="//www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Domain-Authority.png" width="133"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Here are the anchor text variations for those that are more detail orientated:
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/anchor-text.png">
               <img alt="anchor text" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3070" height="430" src="//www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/anchor-text.png" width="303"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              100% of the links I acquired were contextual and within decent quality articles relevant to the topic.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <span id="Element-6-Link-Strengthening">
               Element #6: Link Strengthening
              </span>
             </h4>
             <p>
              After I felt the tier one links hitting the article were pretty solid, I started to build a small amount of
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/how-to-build-tier-two-backlinks/" target="_blank">
               tier two links
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              My weapon of choice?
             </p>
             <p>
              PBN links.
             </p>
             <p>
              I actually only hit one of the articles with a PBN network and it boosted the pages PA to 62. If necessary, Ill build more T2 links to other articles, but right now its not looking like I need to.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <span id="Obvious-Conclusions">
               Obvious Conclusions
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              You know I hate theory, but I can make conclusions based on this test and others Im currently doing.
             </p>
             <p>
              First, the weight of good content continues to increase in importance. Yes, links still matter for giving the resource valid votes, but it seems that when the content is long, unique, and has social activity, Google doesnt need as many links to prove that its a strong resource.
             </p>
             <p>
              Secondly, contextual links from authority sites are the only way to go. Dont waste your time with sites with a DA below 15 when going out for links.
             </p>
             <p>
              Lastly, Ive said this once and Ill say this again, social signals act as a verifier of your links.
             </p>
             <p>
              My rankings wouldnt be as high without them.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <span id="Some-Other-Factors-Effecting-My-Rankings">
               Some Other Factors Effecting My Rankings
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              Aside from all the goodies I explained above, there could also be some other factors effecting my rankings including internal links throughout my site that are funneling authority to the page, and also the fact that my domain strength has risen.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <span id="What-Happened-What-Now-and-What-Should-YOU-Do">
               What Happened, What Now, and What Should YOU Do?
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              Well, what happened is exactly what you can expect: Im currently ranking between #4  #5 for anchor text and will probably reach the top three soon. Prior to this little experiment, I was ranking around #40 or so. It only took a few months for me to reach the top 5 with this viral marketing technique.
             </p>
             <p>
              It actually proved to be a lot easier than I expected because I didnt even need that many links or even that many social shares.
             </p>
             <p>
              So, what now?
             </p>
             <p>
              As a rule of thumb, once one of my sites or clients sites reaches the first page, I decrease the intensity tenfold. And once they reach the top 5, I generally wont build many more tier one backlinks.
             </p>
             <p>
              Why?
             </p>
             <p>
              Because I know that the resource will now do its own marketing and get links and social shares naturally. IF for some reason this doesnt happen, I will build additional tier two links to boost the rankings further.
             </p>
             <p>
              Obviously, you can could build more tier one links and get it ranking higher, but I just prefer keeping more links down on the second tier.
             </p>
             <p>
              Alright, so this information is probably pretty cool to learn, but what you should YOU do now?
             </p>
             <p>
              Heres exactly what you need to do:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Create super resources (quality over quantity)
              </li>
              <li>
               Build contextual authority links to the resource at low-medium velocity
              </li>
              <li>
               Provoke social shares by using a social locker and just having good content
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              Not to sound all white hat guru on you, but valuable content is really the key to this viral marketing technique to work for SEO.
             </p>
             <p>
              Please dont think this only applies to resources.
             </p>
             <p>
              These same exact principles can be used to rank any website.
             </p>
             <h3>
              <span id="BONUS-Tip">
               BONUS Tip
              </span>
             </h3>
             <p>
              Since the article in this case study is so long, I wanted to help people navigate through it more easily and so I decided to install
              <a href="https://wordpress.org/plugins/table-of-contents-plus/" target="_blank">
               this awesome free plugin
              </a>
              , Table of Content Plus.
             </p>
             <p>
              It allows you to create table of contents just like Wikipedia.
             </p>
             <p>
              Now theres more to this strategy than meets the eye. First, Google LOVES it because its helpful for users reading the article. Secondly, people will actually link to specific sections of the article, which obviously increases the authority, and is once again helpful for the person clicking through on the shared link.
             </p>
             <p>
              Lastly, the best part, if Google really like your resource (and your site), it will give you preferential treatment in the SERPS.
             </p>
             <p>
              Check this out:
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="https://www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/CTR-Increase.png">
               <img alt="CTR Increase" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3075" height="443" src="//www.gotchseo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/CTR-Increase.png" width="807"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              So whenever someone searches variations of the anchor text seed such as variations of anchor text or really anything close to my headings in the TOC, it will show that little section link in the SERP description.
             </p>
             <p>
              Anything like this that gives you a CTR advantage in the SERPS is well worth it.
