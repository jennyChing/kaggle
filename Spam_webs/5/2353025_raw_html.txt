                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Mexico: Zapatistas Say Don't Vote, Organize
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6765748320385943411">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-xpl2OwOk5YI/VUqWWOmoktI/AAAAAAAAFQU/eIrPKt1eKV0/s1600/escuelita3_crop1430948142484.jpg_1718483346.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="181" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-xpl2OwOk5YI/VUqWWOmoktI/AAAAAAAAFQU/eIrPKt1eKV0/s1600/escuelita3_crop1430948142484.jpg_1718483346.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <b>
                      The social movement rejected claims that it supports spoiling ballots, but also denies that it supports the electoral process.
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The Zapatista Army of National Liberation (EZLN) reject calling for an abstention of Mexicos upcoming midterm summer elections, emphasizing instead the need for people in Mexico to organize.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     As the Zapatistas that we are, we do not call for not voting, nor for
                     <br/>
                     voting. As the Zapatistas that we are, we do what we do, all that can be
                     <br/>
                     done, that is to tell the people that they should organize to resist,
                     <br/>
                     to struggle, so as to have what they need, said the group in
                     <br/>
                     Wednesdays communique, read by Subcomandante Moises at the Critical
                     <br/>
                     Thinking Against the Capitalist Hydra Seminar in San Cristobal de las
                     <br/>
                     Casas, Chiapas.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The indigenous social movement that rose up in arms in 1994 after the
                     <br/>
                     North American Free Trade Agreement was put into effect, denounced
                     <br/>
                     assertions made that they backed a growing campaign to boycott the
                     <br/>
                     state, federal and municipal elections on June 7.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     The Zapatista spokesman reiterated the call to organize for results
                     <br/>
                     outside of the electoral process. We the Zapatistas never tire in
                     <br/>
                     saying that you should organize yourselves, organize ourselves, each one
                     <br/>
                     in their place, we struggle to organize, we work to organize, we think
                     <br/>
                     to begin to organize  to unite our organizations for a world where the
                     <br/>
                     peoples govern and the government obeys, affirmed Moises.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     In the wake of the September attack and forced disappearance of the 43
                     <br/>
                     Ayotzinapa students by police in Iguala, Guerrero, as well as recent
                     <br/>
                     spikes in organized crime violence and documented human rights abuses by
                     <br/>
                     the authorities, various citizens movements have organized campaigns to
                     <br/>
                     nullify the elections.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     In the states of Guerrero and Oaxaca, members of the National
                     <br/>
                     Coordinator of Education Workers; Section 22 of the National Teachers
                     <br/>
                     Union; and the Regional Coordinator of Communal Authorities, or the
                     <br/>
                     Community Police; are organizing to ensure that elections are not
                     <br/>
                     carried out in various regions of the two southwestern states. A poll
                     <br/>
                     last month revealed that less than half of the population of Mexico has
                     <br/>
                     faith in the electoral process.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Source :

                      Telesur
                     </a>
