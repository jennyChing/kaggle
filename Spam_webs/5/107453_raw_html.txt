        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Plantpot bread  great cooking activity for kids (The Kids Only Cookbook recipe)
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-12-27T00:11:45+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          27 December, 2013
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/author/emily-leary/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Emily Leary
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="7568938" data-link="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/2013/12/plant-pot-bread-great-cooking-activity-for-kids-kids-only-cookbook-recipe/" data-summary="We gave the 'Flowerpot bread' a go, which was really easy and quick as it contains no yeast. The kids really enjoyed themselves, with JD (6) reading the instructions and sharing the weighing and mixing with Miss J (2). They were delighted to have taken the lead and very proud of their flowerpot loaf." data-title="Plantpot bread - great cooking activity for kids (The Kids Only Cookbook recipe)">
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Plant pot bread - great cooking activity for kids " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-16072" height="379" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Flower-pot-bread.png" title="Plant pot bread - great cooking activity for kids " width="569"/>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Did we really make that?! -JD, 6
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         To keep us busy over the Christmas holidays, we were sent
         <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/The-Kids-Only-Cookbook-Quinn/dp/1849493316" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.co.uk/The-Kids-Only-Cookbook-Quinn/dp/1849493316', 'The Kids Only Cookbook');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          The Kids Only Cookbook
         </a>
         by Sue Quinn. Its an illustrated recipe book, packed with 50 simple cooking ideas with a slightly irreverent edge, such as kicknroll tin can ice cream, which involves packing a large tin can with ice and rock salt, insert a sealed ziplock bag containing cream, sugar and vanilla and kick it to the park or the beach for 25 minutes to produce homemade ice cream!
        </p>
        <p>
         All the recipes are written in child-friendly language with plenty of photos andeasy-to-follow captions, making it an engaging and fun read.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="The Kids Only Cookbook" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-16071" height="854" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/kids-only-cookbook.jpg" title="The Kids Only Cookbook" width="569"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         We gave the Flowerpot bread a go, which was really easy and quick as it contains no yeast. The kids really enjoyed themselves, with JD (6) reading the instructions and sharing the weighing and mixing with Miss J (2). They were delighted to have taken the lead and very proud of their flowerpot loaf.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres what to do if youd like to try it.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Ingredients
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 clean, unused flowerpot approx 10cm wide
         </li>
         <li>
          40g butter, plus a bit more for greasing
         </li>
         <li>
          225g self-raising flour, plus about 1 teaspoon more for flouring the pots
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 teaspoon salt
         </li>
         <li>
          125ml milk, plus extra for brushing
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Cooking with kids" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-16016" height="379" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/baking.png" title="Cooking with kids" width="569"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Instructions
        </h2>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Preheat the oven to 200C (180C fan assisted). Grease the insides of the flowerpot with butter and use a bit of kitchen roll to spread it round.Hold the pot sideways and sprinkle in about  teaspoon of flour. Turn the pot, shaking it as you go, until the insides are covered in flour.
         </li>
         <li>
          Tip all the flour and salt into a large bowl, then cut up and add the butter and rub it into the flour with your fingers. You dont want any lumps of butter left.
         </li>
         <li>
          Make a hole in the flour so that you can see the bottom of the bowl. This is called a well. Stir in the milk and the flour bit by bit. Gradually add in just enough milk to make a sticky dough.
         </li>
         <li>
          When the dough begins to look a bit scraggily, start using your hands. Add a tiny bit more milk to the mixture if the dough is too dry. Shape into a large ball. Shape into a ball and put into the flowerpot and brush the top with milk.
         </li>
         <li>
          Wearing oven gloves, put the pot into the oven and set the timer for about 30 minutes. The exact time will depend on the size of your flowerpot so keep an eye on their progress. When its ready the kitchen should smell like bread and the top of the loaf should be hard and golden.
         </li>
         <li>
          Leave it to sit in its pot for at least 10 minutes. When the pot is cool enough to handle, very carefully run a flat, blunt knife around the sides between the bread and the pot. Tip upside down. Ta dah!
         </li>
         <li>
          This is best with butter and some honey or jam. It doesnt stay fresh for long but it tastes brilliant toasted.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Plant pot bread - great cooking activity for kids " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-16072" height="379" src="http://www.amummytoo.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Flower-pot-bread.png" title="Plant pot bread - great cooking activity for kids " width="569"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Let me know if you try it.
        </p>
