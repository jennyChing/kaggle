                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Magic Dragon Painted Rocks &amp; Storage Tower
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4124470455500356325" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-6rGPUcatOtE/UXbwifQ-FhI/AAAAAAAAA2M/oX6nOstky-g/s1600/Magic+Dragon+Painted+Rocks+-+header+resized.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="590" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-6rGPUcatOtE/UXbwifQ-FhI/AAAAAAAAA2M/oX6nOstky-g/s640/Magic+Dragon+Painted+Rocks+-+header+resized.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <i style="background-color: #fafafa; color: #333333; font-family: 'IM Fell English'; font-size: 14px; line-height: 19px;">
                       (This post contains affiliate links for your convenience.)
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">
                       I was inspired to make this project by my lovely and creative blogging friend Stephanie at
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://www.two-daloo.com/" style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">
                       Twodaloo
                      </a>
                      <span style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">
                       . It all startedwhen I saw
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://instagram.com/p/XhnhXUGaxa/" style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">
                       this wonderful picture
                      </a>
                      <span style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">
                       on her Instagram feed. After I told her how much I loved her idea and wanted to paint a set of magic stones, we decided to collaborate on a larger project. She set off to make
                       <a href="http://www.two-daloo.com/2013/04/25/magic-fairy-painted-rocks-and-storage-tower/">
                        magic fairy stones
                       </a>
                       (sneak peak at the bottom of this post)and I set off to make my magic dragon rocks!
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <b>
                       Here are the materials for this project:
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <b>
                       For the Magic Rocks:
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Rocks - I found mine at the beach, but you can also find them at your local craft store or dollar store.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Acrylic Paint - I really like
                      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B007GHIFM0/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B007GHIFM0&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thri360livi-20">
                       Martha Stewart High Gloss Paints
                      </a>
                      because they are made for all surfaces.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Paint Brushes - My favorite are small watercolor brushes for details like the dots you see on these rocks.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Craft Gems - I used Martha Stewart gem stickers I found at Michael's. I used Super Glue to make sure they stayed in place.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <b>
                       For the Storage Container:
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Empty oatmeal container.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Decorative paper - I used left over scraps I had of
                      <a href="http://www.paper-source.com/cgi-bin/paper/item/Lokta-Multicolor-Squares-Fine-Paper/3102.001/103341.html">
                       this paper from Paper Source
                      </a>
                      .
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Glue or tape.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Scissors.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <b>
                       Other:
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Dragons - We bought this
                      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00132NWIQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00132NWIQ&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thri360livi-20">
                       set of dragons
                      </a>
                      by Safari LTD.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      *Blocks - Our blocks are by Haba. Here is a
                      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00067Y4AO/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00067Y4AO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thri360livi-20">
                       similar set
                      </a>
                      .
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1WQYDNWeO_c/UXfyXjbHe2I/AAAAAAAAA3U/1I5NrQfC34I/s1600/One+Dragon+on+a+rock+resized.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="426" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1WQYDNWeO_c/UXfyXjbHe2I/AAAAAAAAA3U/1I5NrQfC34I/s640/One+Dragon+on+a+rock+resized.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      I love that something as natural as a rock can be turned into a magical piece of art for a child or a child-at-heart. Painting stones isn't hard, you just have to find what works for you. Even a simple, one color shape can look beautiful (this is for my friends who say they can't draw).
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-RM_Uq4Wi0k8/UXb7iBK3lrI/AAAAAAAAA28/aqu_l0GK8iw/s1600/Dragon+Storage+Tower.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="534" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-RM_Uq4Wi0k8/UXb7iBK3lrI/AAAAAAAAA28/aqu_l0GK8iw/s640/Dragon+Storage+Tower.JPG" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      These stones were designed specifically to invite dragons to come out and play.
                      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00132NWIQ/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00132NWIQ&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=thri360livi-20">
                       Our dragons
                      </a>
                      live in a special tower, and they don't like to leave their home unless there is a magical rock playground.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-vKTDDmuZ7fc/UXbwzR7Tt1I/AAAAAAAAA2c/TeNP2A8Hyx8/s1600/magic+rocks+resized.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="440" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-vKTDDmuZ7fc/UXbwzR7Tt1I/AAAAAAAAA2c/TeNP2A8Hyx8/s640/magic+rocks+resized.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      The tiny, sparkling diamonds are what initially attract the attention of the dragons. They're drawn to anything shiny and colorful.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-LForAfWPUnw/UXbwtePY9ZI/AAAAAAAAA2U/9UODxxN0PDQ/s1600/Dragons+on+rocks+resized.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="488" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-LForAfWPUnw/UXbwtePY9ZI/AAAAAAAAA2U/9UODxxN0PDQ/s640/Dragons+on+rocks+resized.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      The dragons also enjoy when you add play structures to their magical park. You could even make your own play structures out of cardboard and toilet paper rolls. When the dragons are tired and ready for a nap, store them safely in their tower along with the rocks until they are ready to play again next time.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-G4rxlkte2o4/UXfxpEPUQSI/AAAAAAAAA3M/ECkrsWsLxd0/s1600/Tower+and+rocks+no+header+resized.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="590" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-G4rxlkte2o4/UXfxpEPUQSI/AAAAAAAAA3M/ECkrsWsLxd0/s640/Tower+and+rocks+no+header+resized.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      These rocks are a perfect way to help your child get excited about pretend play. You can also have your child help you paint the rocks. An easy way would be to paint the rock all one color, let it dry, and then have your child use a paintbrush or their fingers to add additional colors. Remember this: there is no wrong way to paint a rock.
                     </div>

                      <br/>
