      <p>
       The reaction is hydrogen peroxide breaking down into one of its two components, oxygen. A mixture of water (the other product of hydrogen peroxide decomposing) and the liquid soap are responsible for the foam, and potassium iodide is used as a catalyst to make the decomposition occur faster. What you get is a large amount of oxygen trying to break out which causes the soap bubbles to quickly expand. Food coloring can also be used as a catalyst for the reaction. For those who dont understand the technical jargon, just watch the video as it is awesome.
