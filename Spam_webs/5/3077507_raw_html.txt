          <p>
           <strong>
            Pop. 2,527,010
            <br/>
            GPS: 4038'N 7357'W
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The best park, the truest urban wilderness that Frederick Law Olmsted designed is not Central Park (he was horrified when they added boulevards to the south end), but Prospect Parkin Brooklyn. Call us biased, since more than half the
           <em>
            Adventure
           </em>
           staff lives here, but Brooklyn is NYCs best urban wilderness, just as Olmstead planned it.
          </p>
          <p>
           PLAY
          </p>
          <p>
           Brooklyn is full of surprises, sometimes in the most obvious places. In the park, theres real, intact woodland, with honest-to-goodness mountain biking and XC-skiing trailsall in the middle of a vast urban landscape. Go to Rockaway. Its got arguably the best surf break on Long Island, and its possible to get there by subway (no argument: the most hardcore surfers paddle out at Rockaway, usually during hurricane season). Sometimes the surprises are unexpected, like the largest climbing gym in all five boroughs that just sprang up in an old parking garage near family-friendly Park Slope (
           <a href="http://www.brooklynboulders.com" target="_blank" title="http://www.brooklynboulders.com">
            www.brooklynboulders.com
           </a>
           ). Brooklyn's also endlessly bikeable, especially on the greenway.
          </p>
          <p>
           EAT
          </p>
          <p>
           Um, the options are as manifold and great as Manhattanjust less pretentious and a bit easier on the pocketbook. Di Fara is
           <em>
            the
           </em>
           classic slow pizza (
           <a href="http://www.difara.com/" target="blank">
            www.difara.com
           </a>
           ), Peter Lugers is
           <em>
            the
           </em>
           steakhouse (
           <a href="http://www.peterluger.com/" target="blank">
            www.peterluger.com
           </a>
           ), and, hey, stroll through the waterfront DUMBO (its Directly Under the Manhattan Bridge Overpass) to the Vinegar Hill House, in Vinegar Hill, for that quaint BK neighborhood feel (
           <a href="http://www.vinegarhillhouse.com/" target="blank">
            www.vinegarhillhouse.com
           </a>
           ).
          </p>
          <p>
           SLEEP
          </p>
          <p>
           Bed and Breakfast on the Parkyes, its a B&amp;B, but it's in Brooklyn. Its hip(ish). And across the street from Prospect Park (doubles from $250;
           <a href="http://www.bbnyc.com/" target="blank">
            www.bbnyc.com
           </a>
           ).
          </p>

                $element.addClass('visible');

        });

    /**
     * Add a script inside the interstitial div, that initialize
     * the slot to show the GPT ad; this function also save the
     * slot's instance in window, to refresh the ad every time
     * this function is called.
     * ad-gallery-container is the div's id
     */
    function interstitialAd(adunit, adsize, divId) {
        var gtag = document.createElement('script');
        gtag.type = 'text/javascript';
        gtag.innerHTML = 'googletag.cmd.push(function() {' +
            'if(window.adInterstitial == undefined){' +
                'var slot1 = googletag.defineSlot("2994/' 
                    + adunit + '", [' + adsize[0] + ', ' 
                    + adsize[1] + '],"'+divId+'");' +
                'window.adInterstitial = slot1;' +
            '}' +
            'else{' +
                'slot1 = window.adInterstitial;' +
            '}' +
            'slot1.addService(googletag.pubads());googletag.'+
                'enableServices();googletag.display("'+divId+'");});';
        //add the script tag inside the div
        $('#'+divId).append(gtag);
        //refresh only the instance of the interstitial ad
        googletag.pubads().refresh([window.adInterstitial]);
