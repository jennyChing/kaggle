     <h1 class="posttitle">
      <a href="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/2014/07/21/barbecue-roasted-potatoes/" rel="bookmark">
       Barbecue Roasted Potatoes
      </a>
     </h1>
     <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="18245592" data-link="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/2014/07/21/barbecue-roasted-potatoes/" data-summary="" data-title="Barbecue Roasted Potatoes">
     </div>
     <p>
      <em>
       These roasted red potatoes are tossed in a homemade barbecue seasoning giving them an amazing flavor! Theyre tender and fluffy on the inside, and crispy and flavorful on the outside.
      </em>
      <br/>
      <img alt="Oven Roasted Barbecue Potatoes, aka my new favorite roasted potatoes EVER!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11404" data-lazy-src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/how-to-roast-potatoes-in-the-oven.jpg" height="939" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="625"/>
     </p>
     <noscript>
      <img alt="Oven Roasted Barbecue Potatoes, aka my new favorite roasted potatoes EVER!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11404" height="939" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/how-to-roast-potatoes-in-the-oven.jpg" width="625"/>
     </noscript>
     <p>
      So, I have this friend who was asking if anyone knew if you could overdose on potatoes?
     </p>
     <p>
      Apparently, she ate an entire pound of these roasted red potatoes all by herself. I mean, who would ever do such a thing? Certainly not me! But my friend did! Yep.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Oven Roasted Barbecue Potatoes, aka my new favorite roasted potatoes EVER!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11402" data-lazy-src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/barbecue-roasted-red-potatoes-recipe1.jpg" height="416" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="625"/>
     </p>
     <noscript>
      <img alt="Oven Roasted Barbecue Potatoes, aka my new favorite roasted potatoes EVER!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11402" height="416" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/barbecue-roasted-red-potatoes-recipe1.jpg" width="625"/>
     </noscript>
     <p>
      The thing is, these potatoes are perfectly popable. You might eat a respectable amount for dinner, but then when its time to clean up after dinner, the bowl of potatoes is just sitting there. Beckoning to you. And so you pop one in your mouth and carry on. And then walk by and pop another in. And before you know, youve popped a pound of potatoes in your mouth in the time it took you to wash the dishes.
      <br/>
      <span id="more-11401">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      I mean, thats what I heard anyway. Personally, I would never do that.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Oven Roasted Barbecue Potatoes, aka my new favorite roasted potatoes EVER!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11406" data-lazy-src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/barbecue-roasted-potatoes.jpg" height="416" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="625"/>
     </p>
     <noscript>
      <img alt="Oven Roasted Barbecue Potatoes, aka my new favorite roasted potatoes EVER!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11406" height="416" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/barbecue-roasted-potatoes.jpg" width="625"/>
     </noscript>
     <p>
      So, anyway, these potatoes are pretty dangerous. The seasoning is just like what youd find on barbecue potato chips. Seriously. But then the potatoes are roasted to creamy, tender perfection and everything is right in the world.
     </p>
     <p>
      Now, I was totally leery of this recipe. I mean, I like mashed potatoes and roasted potatoes and baked potatoes, but I never put big flavors on them. Who ever heard of putting barbecue seasoning on roasted potatoes? Well, one bite in and I was hooked! Big, bold barbecue flavors totally belong on roasted potatoes. Im petitioning the FDA about this right now. Theyre going to add these to the food pyramid, Im sure of it.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Oven Roasted Barbecue Potatoes, aka my new favorite roasted potatoes EVER!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11405" data-lazy-src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/how-to-make-oven-roasted-potatoes.jpg" height="416" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="625"/>
     </p>
     <noscript>
      <img alt="Oven Roasted Barbecue Potatoes, aka my new favorite roasted potatoes EVER!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11405" height="416" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/how-to-make-oven-roasted-potatoes.jpg" width="625"/>
     </noscript>
     <p>
      <span style="color: #444444;">
       <div class="recipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
        <meta content="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/2014/07/21/barbecue-roasted-potatoes/" itemprop="url"/>
        <img align="right" alt="" class="photo nopin" data-lazy-src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/barbecue-roasted-red-potatoes-recipe1-195x195.jpg" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" title=""/>
        <noscript>
         <img align="right" alt="" class="photo nopin" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/barbecue-roasted-red-potatoes-recipe1-195x195.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <p class="printbutton">
         <a href="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/2014/07/21/barbecue-roasted-potatoes/print/" title="Print Recipe">
          <img alt="Print" data-lazy-src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/themes/bunsinmyoven/images/print.png" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="Print" src="http://www.bunsinmyoven.com/wp-content/themes/bunsinmyoven/images/print.png"/>
          </noscript>
          Print Recipe
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2 itemprop="name">
         Barbecue Roasted Red Potatoes
        </h2>
        <div class="time">
         <p>
          <strong>
           Yield:
          </strong>
          <span itemprop="recipeYield">
           serves 4
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Prep Time:
          </strong>
          <meta content="PT10M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          10 minutes
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Cook Time:
          </strong>
          <meta content="PT40M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          40 minutes
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Total Time:
          </strong>
          <meta content="PT50M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          50 minutes
         </p>
        </div>
        <h3 id="ingredients">
         Ingredients:
        </h3>
        <div class="ingredients">
         <p>
          1 1/2 pounds baby red potatoes
          <br/>
          1 tablespoon olive oil
          <br/>
          1 tablespoon chili powder
          <br/>
          2 teaspoons packed brown sugar
          <br/>
          1 1/2 teaspoons seasoned salt
          <br/>
          1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
         </p>
        </div>
        <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
         Directions:
        </h3>
        <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         <p>
          Preheat oven to 425 degrees. Line a baking sheet with foil and spray with non-stick cooking spray.
          <br/>
          Wash the potatoes and then pat dry. Cut each potato in half and place in a large bowl. Toss the potatoes with the olive oil to coat.
          <br/>
          In a small bowl, combine the chili powder, sugar, seasoned salt, and garlic powder. Sprinkle over the potatoes and stir to coat.
          <br/>
          Place the potatoes on the baking sheet in a single layer.
          <br/>
          Bake for 40 minutes or until browned and the potatoes are easily pierced with a fork.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="source">
         <p>
          recipe from
          <a href="http://www.mccormick.com/Recipes/Salads-Sides/Roasted-Barbecue-Potatoes" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           McCormick
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
