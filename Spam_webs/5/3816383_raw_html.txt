    <h1 class="title">
     <a href="http://girltalkhq.com/isis-terrorists-running-fear-kurdish-female-fighters/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to ISIS Terrorists Running In Fear From Kurdish Female Fighters">
      ISIS Terrorists Running In Fear From Kurdish Female Fighters
     </a>
    </h1>
    <div id="content">
     <div class="col_main">
      <div class="post-meta">
       September 2, 2014 at 12:30 pm
      </div>
      <!-- /.post-meta -->
      <div class="clearfix post-9605 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-politics-and-world-matters tag-isis tag-kurdish-female-fighters tag-middle-east tag-terrorists-military tag-war" id="post-9605">
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         <a href="http://girltalkhq.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/76279476_023015262-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Kurdish-female-fighters" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9609" height="351" src="http://girltalkhq.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/76279476_023015262-1.jpg" width="624"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         There is one particular group of warriors that are making the ISIS terrorists run and cower in fear  Kurdish Female Fighters. Why? The simple explanation is that the militant extremists believe if they are killed by an armed woman, they wont go to heaven. They have no problem killing a defenseless woman (or man or child, lets be clear), but a woman facing them down is something that is striking fear into their very hearts, and that is a good thing!
        </p>
        <p>
         The Kurdish fighters, or Peshmerga (the Kurdistan Regions security forces), are quite revered in Kurdistan. In fact it is one of a few Muslim countries where women are respected and revered in society, and especially in the military according to the documentary video below. It is also one of the few Muslim regions where women are allowed to serve in the military and in combat roles. The female unit of the Peshmerga formed in 1996 to oppose Saddam Husseins regime, and remains a strong sector to this day.
        </p>
        <p>
         The units commander, Col. Nahida Ahmed Rashid, says more women are enlisting today to defend Iraqs Kurdish region from Islamist extremists.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theyve taken up arms and gone to battle to protect Kurdistan, but also to say that theres no difference between men and women, Col. Rashid said to
         <a href="http://www.pbs.org/newshour/rundown/female-kurdish-fighters-take-arms-islamic-state-extremists/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.pbs.org/newshour/rundown/female-kurdish-fighters-take-arms-islamic-state-extremists/', 'PBS Newshour');" target="_blank">
          PBS Newshour
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://girltalkhq.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/76279237_023015319-1-e1408914121953.jpg">
          <img alt="Kurdish-female-fighters" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9607" height="309" src="http://girltalkhq.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/76279237_023015319-1-e1408914121953.jpg" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Women are also involved in Kurdish resistance to the Islamic States advances in Syria.
         <a href="http://www.syriadeeply.org/articles/2014/08/5923/isis-advances-kurdish-female-fighters-stand/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.syriadeeply.org/articles/2014/08/5923/isis-advances-kurdish-female-fighters-stand/', 'Some 30 percent');">
          Some 30 percent
         </a>
         of the armed wing of the Kurdish Democratic Union Party (PYD) there, which also fights against Jabhat al-Nusra, al-Qaedas Syrian branch, are female.
        </p>
        <p>
         I believe in a greater cause, which is protecting our families and our cities from the extremists brutality and dark ideas, Col. Rashid continued. They dont accept having women in leadership positions. They want us to cover ourselves and become housewives to attend to their needs only. They think we have no right to talk and control our lives.
        </p>
        <p>
         These women are on the front lines of the Kurdish strategy because of the fact that ISIS are scared of the religious implications of being killed by a woman. They have been instrumental in protecting cities and the Kurdish people, and are seen as a valuable asset to the Kurdish forces. Like Col. Rashid said, it is not just about being a woman (other than using it to their advantage because ISIS are so scared) but it is about these citizens of Kurdistan who are taking up the fight to protect their nation.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://girltalkhq.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/76042192_iraq_syria_isis_caliphate_624_03-07-14v2-e1408914143597.gif">
          <img alt="ISIS-occupation" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9606" height="420" src="http://girltalkhq.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/76042192_iraq_syria_isis_caliphate_624_03-07-14v2-e1408914143597.gif" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Col. Rashid told the
         <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-28308632" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-28308632', 'BBC');" target="_blank">
          BBC
         </a>
         that the womens husbands and children are very supportive of what they are fighting for, as they see all the horrific things ISIS are doing via the Youtube videos being posted online.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have a daughter  shes 10 years old  and when she sees the videos of Isis attacks on Facebook and on the internet, she tells me: Please mum, when you go to fight on the front line, please just take me with you.'
        </p>
        <p>
         A lot of women are volunteering to fight with us at the moment, she adds. They join because they want to defend other women in areas of conflict.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://girltalkhq.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/76279471_kurd_3-e1408914170940.jpg">
          <img alt="Kurdish-female-fighters" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9608" height="309" src="http://girltalkhq.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/76279471_kurd_3-e1408914170940.jpg" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its an honor to be part of a modern Muslim country that allows women to defend the homeland, said an unidentified mother who has been a soldier since the battalions creation in 1996. We enjoy the same treatment as male fighters do as required by law.
        </p>
        <p>
         There is a lot of news about these brave female Kurdish fighters on the front lines of a rather ugly situation. While people are waiting on the US and other governments to act and defend the civilians in Iraq and Kurdistan, these women are showing the world that there is no job they arent willing to do, and no length they arent willing to go to ensure the safety and future of their nation.
        </p>
        <p>
         The 3 videos below are from 2012 when Vice News went inside the Peshmerga to document the female fighters. This documentary at the bottom of the post from 2013 goes into the history of the women in the Kurdish Security Forces. These are a must watch!
        </p>
