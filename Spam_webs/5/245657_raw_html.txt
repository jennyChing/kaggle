          J.K. Rowling's Q&amp;A Resolved a Lot of Unanswered Questions Fans Had About the Series

         <p>
          This
          <a href="http://www.accio-quote.org/articles/2007/0730-bloomsbury-chat.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           Bloomsbury Q&amp;A
          </a>
          with J.K. Rowling is an oldie - from all the way back in 2007, after
          <i>
           Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows
          </i>
          was released - but man, it's a goodie! While many of the questions she answered have been revealed in one way or another over the years, this Q&amp;A is probably one of my favorites. I decided to pick some of my favorite answers (some of which aren't quite as well-known) and put them all in one convenient place.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you've ever wondered what happened to Winky the House Elf or what song Dumbledore would want playing at his funeral, you've come to the right place! And, of course, there is tons more information that has been released after the Harry Potter books concluded (generous woman, that J.K. Rowling), but these are just from this one, quite lengthy, Q&amp;A.
         </p>
         <h3>
          How did the wizarding world change after Voldemort was defeated?
         </h3>
         <figure data-id="280591">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://images-cdn.moviepilot.com/image/upload/c_fill,h_275,w_640/t_mp_quality/screen-shot-2015-02-27-at-2-12-19-pm-j-k-rowling-s-q-a-resolved-a-lot-of-unanswered-questions-fans-had-about-the-series-png-280591.jpg"/>
          <figcaption>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>
          <b>
           Rowling:
          </b>
          The Ministry of Magic was de-corrupted, and with Kingsley at the helm the discrimination that was always latent there was eradicated.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          As adults, what do the main characters do for a living?
         </h3>
         <figure data-id="280590">
          <video autoplay="autoplay" loop="loop" muted="muted" poster="http://images-cdn.moviepilot.com/image/upload/c_fill,h_207,w_500/t_mp_quality_gif/trio-3-harry-ron-and-hermione-26030628-500-207_large-j-k-rowling-s-q-a-resolved-a-lot-of-unanswered-questions-fans-had-about-the-series-gif-280590.jpg">

           </source>
           <source src="http://images-cdn.moviepilot.com/image/upload/c_fill,h_207,w_500/t_mp_quality_gif/trio-3-harry-ron-and-hermione-26030628-500-207_large-j-k-rowling-s-q-a-resolved-a-lot-of-unanswered-questions-fans-had-about-the-series-gif-280590.webm" type="video/webm">

           <div class="mx-gif" data-pause_src="http://images-cdn.moviepilot.com/image/upload/c_fill,h_207,w_500/t_mp_quality_gif/trio-3-harry-ron-and-hermione-26030628-500-207_large-j-k-rowling-s-q-a-resolved-a-lot-of-unanswered-questions-fans-had-about-the-series-gif-280590.jpg" data-play_src="http://images-cdn.moviepilot.com/image/upload/c_fill,h_207,w_500/t_mp_quality_gif/trio-3-harry-ron-and-hermione-26030628-500-207_large-j-k-rowling-s-q-a-resolved-a-lot-of-unanswered-questions-fans-had-about-the-series-gif-280590.gif">

          </figcaption>

         <p>
          <b>
           Rowling:
          </b>
          Hermione began her post-Hogwarts career at the Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures where she was instrumental in greatly improving life for house-elves and their ilk.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          She then moved (despite her jibe to Scrimgeour) to the Dept. of Magical Law Enforcement. where she was a progressive voice who ensured the eradication of oppressive, pro-pureblood laws.
         </p>
         <p>
          Kingsley became permanent Minister for Magic, [with] the new improved Percy ended up as a high-ranking official under [him], and naturally he wanted Harry to head up his new Auror department. Harry did so (just because Voldemort was gone, it didn't mean that there would not be other Dark witches and wizards in the coming years).
         </p>
         <p>
          Ron joined George at Weasleys' Wizarding Wheezes, which became an enormous money-spinner.
         </p>
         <p>
          After a few years as a celebrated player for the Holyhead Harpies, Ginny retired to have her family and to become the Senior Quidditch correspondent at the Daily Prophet!
         </p>
         <p>
          *Author's note: Just to clarify, Ron did work in the Auror department briefly with Harry before deciding to join George as a partner at WWW.
         </p>
         <h3>
          How has George handled Fred's death?
         </h3>
         <figure data-id="280581">
          <video autoplay="autoplay" loop="loop" muted="muted" poster="http://images-cdn.moviepilot.com/image/upload/c_fill,h_207,w_500/t_mp_quality_gif/tumblr_nhz8ayzk181sp4tfto6_500-j-k-rowling-s-q-a-resolved-a-lot-of-unanswered-questions-fans-had-about-the-series-gif-280581.jpg">

         <h3>
          What happened to Winky? Does she still drink a lot of Butterbeer?
         </h3>
         <figure data-id="280585">

           Rowling:
          </b>
          She's still at Hogwarts, and she was one of the oncoming house-elves who attacked the Death Eaters in the final battle. And She's dried out a bit now.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Are Harry and Voldemort related?
         </h3>
         <figure data-id="280586">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://images-cdn.moviepilot.com/image/upload/c_fill,h_287,w_640/t_mp_quality/screen-shot-2015-02-27-at-2-09-07-pm-j-k-rowling-s-q-a-resolved-a-lot-of-unanswered-questions-fans-had-about-the-series-png-280586.jpg"/>
          <figcaption>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>
          <b>
           Rowling:
          </b>
          Yes, Harry and Voldemort are distantly related through the Peverells. Of course, nearly all wizarding families are related if you trace them back through the centuries. As was made clear in 'Deathly hallows', Peverell blood would run through many wizarding families.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          What did Dudley see when he was attacked by the Dementors?
         </h3>
         <figure data-id="280587">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://images-cdn.moviepilot.com/image/upload/c_fill,h_323,w_640/t_mp_quality/screen-shot-2015-02-27-at-2-09-52-pm-j-k-rowling-s-q-a-resolved-a-lot-of-unanswered-questions-fans-had-about-the-series-png-280587.jpg"/>
          <figcaption>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>
          <b>
           Rowling:
          </b>
          I think that when Dudley was attacked by the Dementors he saw himself, for the first time, as he really was. This was an extremely painful, but ultimately salutory lesson, and began the transformation in him.
         </p>

          What did Petunia want to tell Harry when the Dursleys were leaving Privet Drive?
         </h3>
         <figure data-id="280589">

          </figcaption>

           Rowling:
          </b>
          I think that for one moment she trembled on the verge of wishing Harry luck; that she almost acknowledged that her loathing of his world, and of him, was born out of jealousy.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          But she couldn't do it; years of pretending that 'normal' was best had hardened her too much.

         </h3>
         <figure data-id="280604">

          <br/>
         </p>

          It is a very cosy and welcoming place, as dissimilar as possible from Snape's dungeon. Lots of yellow hangings, and fat armchairs, and little underground tunnels leading to the dormitories, all of which have perfectly round doors, like barrel tops.

          </figcaption>
