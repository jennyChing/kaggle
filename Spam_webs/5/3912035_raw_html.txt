       <h3 id="post-5564">
        <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/the-first-time-a-boy-roughed-me-up-and-why-i-took-the-blame/" rel="bookmark" title="The First Time a Boy Roughed Me Up and Why I Took the Blame">
         The First Time a Boy Roughed Me Up and Why I Took the Blame
        </a>
       </h3>
       By
       <span class="meta-author">
        <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/author/darleneouimet/" rel="author" title="Posts by Darlene Ouimet">
         Darlene Ouimet
        </a>
       </span>
         Comments
       <span class="meta-comments">
        <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/the-first-time-a-boy-roughed-me-up-and-why-i-took-the-blame/#comments" title="Comment on The First Time a Boy Roughed Me Up and Why I Took the Blame">
         (136)
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
     </div>
     <!--post text with the read more link-->
     <iframe allowtransparency="true" class="fbLikeContainer" frameborder="0" id="fbLikeIframe" name="fbLikeIframe" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Femergingfrombroken.com%2Fthe-first-time-a-boy-roughed-me-up-and-why-i-took-the-blame%2F&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=true&amp;width=300&amp;height=25&amp;action=like&amp;font=arial&amp;colorscheme=light" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:300px; height:25px; display:inline;">
     </iframe>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/christina-11.jpg">
        <img alt="christina 11" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-5566" height="300" src="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/christina-11-225x300.jpg" width="225"/>
       </a>
       The first time a boy got a little rough with me I was 14. I had been swimming at our community pool and Mike the 17 year old life guard let me wear his diving watch in the water, which I thought was so cool. My boyfriend Rob showed up and called me over to the gate and I forgot to take the watch off when I went to meet him. I could tell he was angry.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       I grabbed my towel and as we were walking away from the pool I had a sinking feeling in my gut. Looking back it was a familiar feeling, one
      </span>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       that I had had often in my lifetime; it was the feeling of impending doom.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       My internal dialogue went like this; I have a bad feeling. Something bad is going to happen. Something bad my tummy hurts, I dont feel good; I dont like this feeling.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       I remember my boyfriend started questioning me.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       Rob: Who is that guy?
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       Me: Hes just the life Guard
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       Rob: Why are you wearing that watch?
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       Me: Because he let me wear it to dive in the deep end. Its so cool!
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       Rob: Give me that watch.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       Me: NO, I have to give it back to Mike.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       By now the feeling of impending doom is almost making me throw up. I am scared; I feel like I have done something really bad but I am not sure what. I want to hide and there is nowhere to hide. I want to disappear. I want Rob to stop breathing in that angry way. I want him to calm down and listen to me.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       I can see that Rob is getting more agitated. He grabs my wrist with one hand (OW, stop it, you are hurting me!) and he rips the watch off my wrist with the other hand. Then he goes back to the fence surrounding the pool and he throws the watch over the fence and into the pool. I am just standing there dripping wet, feeling scared and stupid and starting to give myself shit for being so dumb. Earlier in the year Rob had beat up a boy at school because he said something flirty to me and it took three teachers to break the fight up and now I am really scared he is going to beat up the life guard. But he doesnt go into the pool area.
      </span>

       Read More

       For the past few years I have not thought about myself as a daughter on Mothers day, I have thought of myself as a mother instead. When I used to think of myself as a daughter the purpose of mothers day was to celebrate someone who constantly reminded me that I was not the daughter that she wanted. I celebrated someone who treated me like I was lacking in some ways. I celebrated someone who seemed to be exasperated with me and communicated to me that I was somewhat of a pain in her butt. I celebrated my mother even though she blamed me for more things than I care to list in this post and I celebrated a mother who caused me untold grief. (Well actually, it was untold until 6 or 7 years ago. Now I have told)
      </span>
     </p>

     </p>

      <span style="font-family: georgia,palatino; font-size: 14pt;">
       Mothers day in the past was about celebrating someone who hurt me. It doesnt make much sense to me today when I put it that way but back then I never thought about it that way. I was in the deep fog of conditional love, brainwashed to believe that mother is god and that parent entitlement rules over all else, no matter what

         When Mothers Blame Others for their own Disgraceful Behavior

       </h3>
       By
       <span class="meta-author">
        <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/author/darleneouimet/" rel="author" title="Posts by Darlene Ouimet">

     <iframe allowtransparency="true" class="fbLikeContainer" frameborder="0" id="fbLikeIframe" name="fbLikeIframe" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Femergingfrombroken.com%2Fwhen-mothers-blame-others-for-their-own-disgraceful-behavior%2F&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=true&amp;width=300&amp;height=25&amp;action=like&amp;font=arial&amp;colorscheme=light" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:300px; height:25px; display:inline;">
     </iframe>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Blog-pic-1.jpg">
        <img alt="Blog pic 1" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-5501" height="300" src="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Blog-pic-1-224x300.jpg" width="224"/>
       </a>
       My Mother Blamed Me for her Disgraceful Behavior
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       I was lying in bed the other morning and this phrase when mothers blame others kept running through my mind as though some unknown source was whispering at me to write about it. I agreed that it would make a catchy title but I questioned why it was running through my head in the first place.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       And then I laughed!
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       This idea is so prevalent that its a wonder it isnt in my head all the time. Survivors of dysfunctional mother daughter relationships cant escape the constant reminder that some mothers will blame anyone and everyone as long as they dont have to look at their own actions. It is still frustrating to me that no matter what proof I had, no matter how many times I tried to explain the situation, no matter how much I defended myself, my mother blamed me OR she blamed something or someone else for HER decisions and behavior.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       And although this problem is more widely discussed when it is the adult daughter who is targeted and blamed, this happens very often with adults sons as well. This isnt exclusive to mothers who blame daughters, but very often fathers blame daughters and or sons as well. Sometimes ALL the children in the family are blamed and defined as the problem and sometimes only one or two of them are singled out and blamed and defined as the problem in the family.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       Many adult children of Narcissistic mothers know this all too well and although
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/understanding-narcissism-and-the-root-of-abusive-behaviour/" target="_blank" title="Understanding Narcissism">
        <strong>
         my mother is not a true narcissist
        </strong>
       </a>
       (because she has total control over her actions), she fits the narcissistic mother pattern of not taking responsibility for her own actions that have ultimately led to the failure of our relationship. Most importantly for the purpose of this article is the fact that the results have been the same with my mother and I as they are with others who do have more typically narcissistic mothers.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       When children (of any age) are blamed and labeled as the problem, a burden or less important than the parents, the damage to the self-esteem and overall emotional wellbeing of the child is substantial!
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       And the treatment and tactics used by the parents are so typical that it is almost as though there is secret manual that these mothers (parents) subscribe to. A
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/why-people-discount-the-adult-child-and-defend-the-abuser/" target="_blank" title="Why People Discount the child and defend the abuser">
        <strong>
         manual endorsing that parents have the right to do this stuff
        </strong>
       </a>
       and act this way with their children without any consequences to themselves!
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       The children of these mothers, MEN and WOMAN who have been blamed as children for the ways in which we have been treated, are blamed as adults as well;

      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">

     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="color: #000000; font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">

       Letstalk about this.

        my problem.
       </a>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       The first thing that came to my mind was the child, (and not just the child but the child who was ME) who is abused, molested, discounted, shamed, hit, blamed, neglected,  is being told that he or she should not have ever expected to be loved, cherished, nurtured, respected, protected and taken care of.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">

      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       Then I thought about how this is the same self -blame that I talk about all the time; if only I had never expected to be loved, then I wouldnt have been disappointed.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       This directive suggests that asking for simple respect and regard isexpecting too much.

       And what about the part that directs us to accept things for what they are. Its interesting to me that this writer didnt realize that accepting things as they really are is exactly what this blog is about; the difficulty is that actually doing it is not nearly as easy or simple as it sounds and we are NOT trying to accept that our expectations are too high in the first place because they arent.      <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       Christmas and the holiday season in general is often a really difficult time for anyone who is involved with or has ever been involved with dysfunctional family situations. It has been at least 6 years now since I have spoken to my mother at Christmas time. Each year is easier. This year when I began to think about writing something about this time of year for my blog, I realized that I dont think about my family very much anymore during the holidays. For a minute I wondered if I should feel guilty about that but the truth is that by their own choice they are not part of my life and when I think about it, there is nothing to miss. I havent spent Christmas with my family of origin since I was age 15 with my mother and age 18 with my father.

     </p>

        </a>
       </h3>
       By
       <span class="meta-author">

     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/The-Path-for-EFB.jpg">
        <img alt="The Path for EFB" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-5346" height="300" src="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/The-Path-for-EFB-224x300.jpg" width="224"/>
       </a>
       Child abuse and neglect result in low self-esteem, depression and a whole lot of other issues. Part of the grooming process that occurs in ALL abuse including emotional abuse and psychological abuse is that the blame is transferred to the child and in order to cope and survive children accept that blame and focus inward in order to try harder for the love and acceptance they long for. In the dysfunctional family, the abuse doesnt end in childhood and often the child who is now an adult will seek professional help in order to overcome the damage that the child abuse caused. Just as our parents and all adult abusers, controllers and manipulators had positional power, doctors, therapists and helping professionals have it too.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       When we have been convinced through actions, inactions and words that there is something wrong with us and we finally go to a helping professional such as a therapist, counselor or psychologist ~ if that mental health professional
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/why-people-discount-the-adult-child-and-defend-the-abuser/" target="_blank" title="Why people discount the adult child and defend the abuser">
        <strong>
         defends our parents, or focuses on US as the problem
        </strong>
       </a>
       it serves us as confirmation that we are in fact the problem and it is very much a re-traumatization.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       Mental health professionals have tons of positional power ~ they are endorsed and accredited as being able to help and therefore we often see them as an even bigger authority then the way that we saw our parents when we were kids, so if they AGREE with our parents or if they focus
       <strong>
        with us
       </strong>
       on what WE could do or could have done differently, it very often causes a bigger problem than the one we went to talk about.

         <img alt="Christina 8" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-5325" height="179" src="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Christina-8-300x179.jpg" width="300"/>

        Please help me welcome back one of our most popular guest writers ~ Pam Witzemann! In this post Pam shares about how seeing the truth in a bigger picture way, helped her to recognize what her inner critic voices were telling her. Truth was the balance in accountability that Pam needed to silence the lies those inner voices told her about herself that she had believed for so long as part of her coping method. This post is extremely content rich and I encourage you to read it throughmore than once! ~ Darlene

     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       How The Truth Silences Inner Critics Voices and Healing Begins by Pam Witzemann
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       An abusive childhood left me with little self-worth and a damaged ability to trust and form healthy relationships. I have lived most of my life with both a strong inner and outer critic. The inner critic tells me that Im defective and responsible for every bad thing that happens to me. The outer critic tells me that most human beings shouldnt be trusted because they are all potentially, dangerous. Both my
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/hearing-negative-self-talk/comment-page-1/" target="_blank" title="hearing negitive self talk on EFB">
        <strong>
         inner critic and outer critic lie to me
        </strong>
       </a>
       and they present themselves as my greatest obstacle in healing from the abuse I suffered during my childhood. Truth is the balance in accountability I need to heal from childhood abuse. Only the truth has the power to silence my inner and outer critics, who are never satisfied until they fully disable me, driving me into deep depression and isolation from others.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       Human beings are social creatures. I am a human being and I too am meant to enjoy relationships. However, my early childhood taught me that I wasnt quite human and the second half of my childhood taught me that all human beings, not just my alcoholic parents, were dangerous. I decided that if I had the choice, Id rather not be a human being and I spent several decades of my life seeking safety through various forms of isolation and very limited close relationships. As a small child, my isolation was involuntary and imposed on me by poor health and by the way my parents chose to treat my unhealthy condition. From birth, until age seven, I spent most of my time in bed and usually, I was medicated with alcohol. All during my elementary school years I was often, sick and kept in bed. I had a deep longing for something that I didnt understand, an empty, excruciating, emotional ache; but I grew used to being alone and that state of aloneness became my safe haven from the alcoholic drama that characterized my home life.

         Stepping out of Victim Mentality by Understanding What It Is
        </a>
       </h3>
       By
       <span class="meta-author">
        <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/author/darleneouimet/" rel="author" title="Posts by Darlene Ouimet">
         Darlene Ouimet
        </a>
       </span>
         Comments
       <span class="meta-comments">
        <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/stepping-out-of-victim-mentality-by-understanding-what-it-is/#comments" title="Comment on Stepping out of Victim Mentality by Understanding What It Is">
         (157)
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
     </div>
     <!--post text with the read more link-->
     <iframe allowtransparency="true" class="fbLikeContainer" frameborder="0" id="fbLikeIframe" name="fbLikeIframe" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Femergingfrombroken.com%2Fstepping-out-of-victim-mentality-by-understanding-what-it-is%2F&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=true&amp;width=300&amp;height=25&amp;action=like&amp;font=arial&amp;colorscheme=light" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:300px; height:25px; display:inline;">
     </iframe>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/EFB-Carla-Sunset.jpg">
        <img alt="EFB Carla Sunset" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-5315" height="300" src="http://emergingfrombroken.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/EFB-Carla-Sunset-224x300.jpg" width="224"/>
       </a>
       Victim mentality is the wish, hope and belief that by accepting nasty behavior and even covering up or excusing nasty behavior, that love will be the end result. I cant think of one time that compliance led to love. Not even once.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       As a Victim I believed that my love could heal others.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       I believed that if I could prove to them that they were lovable, that they would love me back. And I put a lot of effort into proving that they were worthy of love. I cooked, I cleaned and I complied. I was quiet and polite or I was funny and bright;
       <a href="http://emergingfrombroken.com/how-victim-mentality-works-in-relation-to-family-secrets/" target="_blank" title="victim mentality and family secrets">
        <strong>
         I kept the secret, I didnt bring shame on them
        </strong>
       </a>
       , I turned a blind eye. I accepted what they dished out as it was the normal that they taught me. I thought that was love. I thought that my love was unconditional.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       I tried to earn love.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       I tried to prove my worth so that I would BE loved.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       I didnt really understand love.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       As a victim I believed that if I was compliant, and if I did what they seemed to require from me that I would be appreciated. But the rules always changed. Instead of realizing that their rules always changed, I thought I was stupid.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <span style="font-family: times new roman,times; font-size: 14pt;">
       I believed that if I jumped through their hoops and proved that I was trying to be who they wanted me to be, that they would SEE me as worthy. Nobody ever saw me
      </span>

       Read More
      </a>
     </p>
