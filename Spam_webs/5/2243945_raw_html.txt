      <h1 itemprop="headline">
       How To Book A Villa
      </h1>

      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         I stay in hotels almost exclusively when I travel, occasionally invading the guest room of a kind friend. But lately, my mind has wandered to France, Italy and visions of my friends traveling together, opening a bottle of wine, and cooking a huge dinner from items we picked up at a local farmers market. Wed watch the sun set over the Tuscan hills or flower-dotted fields of Provence. This is the ultimate villa sales ad.
        </p>
        <p>
         I started researching villa rentals  which includes all types of homes, not just fancy Italian castles like the world villa implies. And I became so frustrated that I just shut my computer down. The sheer volume of useless information is almost comical.
        </p>
        <p>
         I decided to consult with a few experts in the field, true experts that know every last detail of the homes in their portfolio from the WiFi speed to what the views are. Renting a villa can be a pleasant experience if you know where to go and how to do it.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_562" style="width: 570px">
         <img alt="" class=" wp-image-562 " height="375" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/anniefitzsimmons/files/2012/08/akprovence.jpg" width="560"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          A&amp;K Villa - Provence
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         I connected with Huw and Rossella Beaugi of
         <a href="http://www.thinksicily.com/" target="_blank">
          Think Sicily
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.thinkpuglia.com/" target="_blank">
          Think Puglia
         </a>
         , as well as Adam Coats, Head of Sales &amp; Operations for
         <a href="http://www.akvillas.com/" target="_blank">
          Abercrombie &amp; Kent Villas
         </a>
         to create this list.
        </p>
        <p>
         1.
         <strong>
          Start With the Basic Questions:
         </strong>
         Decide who youre traveling with and ask these questions: What do we want from our villa vacation  lots of cultural visits, time by the pool? Are we celebrating something? Where do we want to go? Narrow it down to 2-3 regions. When do we want to go? How many bedrooms do we need? Do we need service and a cook every day, some days, or never? What is our budget?
        </p>
        <p>
         2.
         <strong>
          Connect With Villa Rental Specialist:
         </strong>
         Go straight to the specialist. A company like Think Sicily &amp; Think Puglia has concentrated knowledge of two specific areas, which essentially guarantees an expert experience. Other companies like A&amp;K Villas have a broader portfolio but is backed by the name Abercrombie &amp; Kent, one of the most highly regarded travel companies in the world. Consider recommendations from friends and family. Look for client reviews and press coverage from various sources, and a good company will provide this to you.
        </p>
        <div>
         <p>
          3.
          <strong>
           Provide As Much Detail As Possible:
          </strong>
          List out your specific requirements  Think food needs, children, wheelchair accessibility, likes and dislikes. Coats says these details are key even if some of it seems trivial. Not all villas will have super fast WiFi or be complete with AC. Dont assume your villa stay will have these facilities, but it they are absolutely essential, let the agent know so they can advise accordingly, and steer you in the right direction, he says.
         </p>
         <p>
          4.
          <strong>
           Think Location, Location, Location:
          </strong>
          Coats says never assume that the villa is a 2-minute walk to the nearest restaurant or shop. He stresses that villas are private, family homes and are often deliberately built away from towns and roads in order to ensure peace and privacy. Ask what the drive is to the nearest conveniences. For some, being far away from sightseeing and town is a plus, for others, its important to be close to the action.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_564" style="width: 570px">
          <img alt="" class=" wp-image-564 " height="372" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/anniefitzsimmons/files/2012/08/thinksicily.jpg" width="560"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Think Sicily Villa
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          5.
          <strong>
           Dont Be Afraid To Ask Lots Of Questions:
          </strong>
          The Beaugis say there is no dumb question when it comes to renting your perfect villa. Ask everything you can possibly think of. Ask about the level of maid service included, about chefs, transfers and access to local experiences. Ask if the company has a Hosted (concierge-style) level of service to ensure that everything is taken care of, if thats something you want. Coats says paying a bit extra for a more inclusive level of service is often important for large, multi-generational groups and those planning on a busy schedule. This way, it doesnt fall on the shoulders of one member of the group.
         </p>
         <p>
          6.
          <strong>
           Always Ask The Agent This Question:
          </strong>
          Have they personally visited the property and how recently has someone inspected it? Coats stresses that villas are private homes in private locations, with lots of idiosyncrasies, so it is important that you are in touch with someone who can confirm its suitability for your group. Something might look great online but turn out to be next to a motorway. The Think Sicily and Think Puglia teams regularly visit and stay at their villas and know the areas well.
         </p>
