        <h1>
         St. John's Walking Tour - Hiking Newfoundland
        </h1>
        <div class="region region-content">
         <div class="block block-system" id="block-system-main">
          <article class="node node--blog node--promoted node--full node--blog--full clearfix" id="node-408" role="article">
           <section class="node-submitted">
            <span class="submitted">
             By
             <span class="username">
              Barry
             </span>
            </span>
            <span class="date">
             25 / Sep / 2012
            </span>
           </section>
           <div class="node-content">
            <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
             <div class="field-items">
              <div class="field-item even">
               <p>
                Beginning an adventure to explore a bit of Newfoundland for the first time.
               </p>
               <p>
                <img alt="" class="image-large" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/styles/large/public/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_54.jpg?itok=UQt1wAG0" style="height:404px; width:640px"/>
               </p>
               <p>
                Thetwo-hour drive south from
                <strong>
                 Dieppe
                </strong>
                in
                <strong>
                 New Brunswick
                </strong>
                to the
                <strong>
                 Halifax Stanfield
                </strong>
                <strong>
                 International Airport
                </strong>
                in
                <strong>
                 Nova Scotia
                </strong>
                begins before sunrise. The entire trip is under overcast skies as
                <strong>
                 Hurricane Leslie
                </strong>
                moves north in the
                <strong>
                 Atlantic Ocean
                </strong>
                pushing heavy weatherahead of the major storm.
               </p>
               <p>
                Following a quick breakfast at
                <strong>
                 Tim Hortons
                </strong>
                near the airport we check in for our direct hour and a half flight to
                <strong>
                 St. John's
                </strong>
                ,
                <strong>
                 Newfoundland
                </strong>
                aboard
                <strong>
                 Porter Airlines
                </strong>
                on a
                <strong>
                 Bombardier Q400
                </strong>
                turboprop airplane.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_02.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14298" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_02.jpg" style="height:473px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                The flight leaves ahead of time. Condiments, including beer and wine are complementary. Service is excellent. The flight is predominantlyabove cloud, first over
                <strong>
                 Nova Scotia
                </strong>
                , then over water for most of the trip. It is uplifting to see the
                <strong>
                 Newfoundland
                </strong>
                coastline for the first time. We are told we fly over the small French owned islands
                <strong>
                 St. Pierre and Miquelon
                </strong>
                .Shortly afterwe land early at the
                <strong>
                 St. John's Airport,
                </strong>
                <strong>
                 Newfoundland Visitor Services
                </strong>
                provides us with a precise route to get from the airport to our hotel. We collect our rental car.
                <strong>
                 Mlanie
                </strong>
                drives. I navigate.
               </p>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_05.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14299" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_05.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                The first view of the Avalon Peninsula, Newfoundland from the plane.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_12.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14300" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_12.jpg" style="height:384px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                These are not the French Islands of St. Pierre et Miquelon but we flew by them on the flight path from Halifax, Nova Scotia to St. John's, Newfoundland
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                Somewhat miraculously, we survive the complex road system and arrive at our accommodation on the other side of the city. We have the afternoon to sightsee, and we are staying close todowntown at
                <strong>
                 Extended Stay Deluxe
                </strong>
                so, armed with a map, we begin ourhaphazardwalking tour. Later, at the
                <strong>
                 St. John's Tourism and Culture Centre on Water Street
                </strong>
                , we learn there are four formal walking tours to more thoroughly discover the long and fascinating history of this city. Our first discovery is the
                <strong>
                 George Street United Church
                </strong>
                , built in 1873 and one of a few churches which survived the
                <strong>
                 Great Fire of 1892
                </strong>
                .
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_13.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14301" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_13.jpg" style="height:803px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                Further east, at the end of
                <strong>
                 George Street
                </strong>
                is an interestingstatue representing several venues of
                <strong>
                 Newfoundland
                </strong>
                culture and entertainment.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_15.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14302" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_15.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_16.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14303" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_16.jpg" style="height:853px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                The old part of the city is built on a hill. Many of the street levels are connected by elaborate stone stairways. It is interesting to note the
                <strong>
                 Island of Newfoundland
                </strong>
                hosts the greatest historical depth of discovery in
                <strong>
                 Canada
                </strong>
                andis also the last
                <strong>
                 Province
                </strong>
                to join
                <strong>
                 Confederation in 1949
                </strong>
                . Tradition runs deep here.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_18.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14304" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_18.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                <strong>
                 Mlanie
                </strong>
                <strong>
                 and I
                </strong>
                walk down to the waterfront of
                <strong>
                 St. John's Harbour
                </strong>
                where the iconic
                <strong>
                 Signal Hill
                </strong>
                stands sentinel over the
                <strong>
                 Narrows
                </strong>
                whichseparate
                <strong>
                 St. John's Harbour
                </strong>
                from the
                <strong>
                 Atlantic Ocean.
                </strong>
                A light rain begins. It is common and we are equipped to handle it.
               </p>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_19.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14305" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_19.jpg" style="height:398px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                Cabot Tower stands in the mist on top of Signal Hill above St. John's Harbour in St. John's, Newfoundland
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_20.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14306" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_20.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_26.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14307" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_26.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                The waterfront at
                <strong>
                 St. John's Harbour
                </strong>
                isa combination of
                <em>
                 busy shipyard
                </em>
                , hostingevery conceivablevariety of vessel,
                <em>
                 and museum
                </em>
                . There are brass plaques and storyboards attached to nearly everything. History here is long and deep. We have no more than cursory opportunity to examine content. It is exciting and frustrating. Several days could easily be spent replacing the few hours we have.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_27.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14308" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_27.jpg" style="height:496px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_28.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14309" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_28.jpg" style="height:432px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                We take a ride on the
                <strong>
                 Duffet Docks
                </strong>
                . The water is reasonably calm but, it quickly becomes apparent, motion is unpredictable in every direction and angle. It would be easy to get pitched into the harbour.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_29.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14310" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_29.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_30.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14311" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_30.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                Following is a single example of scores of brass plaques at the waterfront. Note the dates.
                <img alt="" class="image-large" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/styles/large/public/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_34.jpg?itok=9yDyjlQQ" style="height:640px; width:569px"/>
                Someof the stairways between streets contain elaborate war memorials.
               </p>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_36.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14313" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_36.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                Looking up from the bottom.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_37.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14314" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_37.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                Looking down from the top.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                To say the least, residential streets are colorful. Many of the homes are very old and have been restored many times. Foundations rest on solid rock. Real estate is expensive and property, for the most part, is meticulously maintained.
               </p>
               <p>
                Streets are on steep hills, and are often one way or blind alleys. Street names can change 3 or 4 times in half a kilometre. Everything is at angles. There are no 'blocks' downtown. Old is mixed with new. Signage is unpredictable and often missing or too late.
               </p>
               <p>
                Parking is a crap shoot. Residential streets are by permit only. I have never seen anything like it before. Street planning is a dog's breakfast. Be forewarned. Plan ahead.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_38.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14315" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_38.jpg" style="height:284px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_39.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14316" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_39.jpg" style="height:334px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                As we begin the return walk to the hotel to rest and prepare for dinner, we walk past scores of historic structures. Each has a story board like theexamples below. The depth and preservation of historical information in
                <strong>
                 St. John's
                </strong>
                is truly impressive. This blog post barely scratches the surface of the volume of content we visit in just a few hours.
               </p>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <img alt="" class="image-large" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/styles/large/public/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_39a.jpg?itok=W4fkzQzL" style="height:614px; width:640px"/>
                Click on the image to expand for reading. A second click will enlarge it to full screen - Click on the browser back button to return to the post.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_40.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14318" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_40.jpg" style="height:853px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                <img alt="" class="image-large" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/styles/large/public/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_41a.jpg?itok=I_bAIAX-" style="height:640px; width:640px"/>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_42.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14320" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_42.jpg" style="height:486px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                We spend a few minutes at the
                <strong>
                 Basilica of St. John the Baptist
                </strong>
                and see anoverview portion of the huge cathedral and complex. It deserves more time and we shall return another day. A separate post will be prepared for the impressive and historically significant structure.
                <img alt="" class="image-large" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/styles/large/public/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_43a.jpg?itok=O316C7aP" style="height:640px; width:564px"/>
                <img alt="" class="image-large" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/styles/large/public/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_43b.jpg?itok=RlhNeC7N" style="height:578px; width:640px"/>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_44.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14323" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_44.jpg" style="height:455px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_49.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14324" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_49.jpg" style="height:397px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_50.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14325" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_50.jpg" style="height:364px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                The Convent is adjacent to the massive stone Basilica.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_51.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14326" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_51.jpg" style="height:406px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                St. Patrick's Hall, constructed in 1877, is across the street from the Basilica in St. John's Newfoundland.
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                At the next corner, west of the
                <strong>
                 Basilica,
                </strong>
                resides the
                <strong>
                 National Historic Site
                </strong>
                dedicated to
                <strong>
                 Sir John Harvey
                </strong>
                and
                <strong>
                 Fort Townshend
                </strong>
                . We are getting close to our accommodation.
                <img alt="" class="image-large" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/styles/large/public/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_51a.jpg?itok=XLmtP3Ef" style="height:317px; width:640px"/>
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_52.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14328" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_52.jpg" style="height:534px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                Just behind
                <strong>
                 Fort Townsend
                </strong>
                is a modern structure called
                <strong>
                 'The Rooms'
                </strong>
                . It is an impressive structure with internal open spacecontaining large quantities of black granite and glass. The structure provides rooms for archives, museum exhibits and an art gallery.
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_53.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-14478" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_53.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_54.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14479" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_54.jpg" style="height:404px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                'The Rooms' - Archives - Museum - Art Gallery
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <div style="margin: 0px auto; width: 650px; display: block;">
                <a href="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_55.jpg">
                 <img alt="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada" class="size-full wp-image-14494" src="/sites/hikingwithbarry.com/files/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/StJohnsWalk_55.jpg" style="height:480px; width:640px" title="St. John's Walking Tour - St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada"/>
                </a>
                A view through an upstairs window in 'The Rooms' across the front of the Basilica and St. John's Harbour towards Signal Hill above St. John's, Newfoundland
                <p>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p>
                It is the first day of the first time
                <strong>
                 Mlanie
                </strong>
                <strong>
                 and I
                </strong>
                have set foot on
                <strong>
                 Newfoundland
                </strong>
                and it is a placefull of fascinating wonder and surprise. The depth of history is staggering and the degree of preservation is impressive.
               </p>
               <p>
                Back at the hotel, we prepare for dinner anddrive the cardowntown to the waterfront for supper at
                <strong>
                 Smitty's Restaurant
                </strong>
                in the
                <strong>
                 Courtyard Marriott
                </strong>
                on
                <strong>
                 Duckworth Street.
                </strong>
                We enjoy dinner in the lounge while watching ships sail through the
                <strong>
                 Narrows
                </strong>
                into
                <strong>
                 St. John's Harbour
                </strong>
                . One of the very large ships is bright orange and we are told it makes nearly daily voyages shuttling supplies to and from
                <strong>
                 Hibernia
                </strong>
                . The impressive vessel parksat the docks directlyin front of us.
               </p>
               <p>
                On the return walk to the car,
                <strong>
                 Mlanie
                </strong>
                encourages a stop for treats at a popular
                <strong>
                 Water Street
                </strong>
                chocolatier. It is a relaxing end to a long day of travelling and touring the downtown of
                <strong>
                 St. John's
                </strong>
                ,
                <strong>
                 Newfoundland
                </strong>
                on foot. This trip is hosted by my long-time friend and hiking partner and the initiative is truly appreciated.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
