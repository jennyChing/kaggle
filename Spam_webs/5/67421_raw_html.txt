        <p dir="ltr">
         This Valentines Day, I have turned vegan when it comes to men, disregarding any square-jawed, broad-nosed, scruff-faced bloke that comes across my path. But just because I have made this decision does not mean I am through with the dating game. If only.
         <strong>
          In actuality I am dating sixteen companies at once.
         </strong>
         What can the world of dating teach you about the job search? Read on to find out
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Desperation is Unattractive
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I wake up in the morning and check my phone. Hmmmm, no new messages. Im annoyed. I start making coffee. Then BUZZ. My phone vibrates and I jump at it, but its only an email from Nordstrom. Ive applied to like 16 jobs over the past two weeks and all I get is an email from Nordstrom asking me to spend money I dont have??? Where is that chocolate bar I bought last night?!
         <strong>
          My desperation is thick and just as unattractive to potential employers as it is to potential new boos.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Allison Linn warns in her article, 
         <a href="http://on.today.com/13jLrwf" target="_blank">
          Job Search Gimmicks Get Attention, Maybe Not Jobs
         </a>
         , that
         <strong>
          desperation will lead to the wrong kind of attention
         </strong>
         . She quotes Jane Cranston, a career coach with
         <a href="http://www.executivecoachny.com/" target="_blank">
          Executive Coach NY
         </a>
         , Its like getting on a dating site and putting up a naked picture of yourself. You are going to get a lot of attention, you may even get a job, but will it be your soulmate job? Will it be the job you want to spend Sunday morning with? Or it will be the creepy job you severely regret having that one night stand with?
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. You May Have to Turn to Unconventional Resources
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I have yet to try an online dating site when it comes to finding a boyfriend, but maybe I will use one to find a job. Infamous online dating website
         <strong>
          eHarmony has started thinking about using their matchmaking powers to bring employers and employees together
         </strong>
         . They have been matching people based on their compatibility for 13 years. Why shouldnt they begin
         <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Akbz0n5QFM0" rel="lightbox-video-0" target="_blank">
          matching job seekers with employers?
         </a>
         Is this the way to find the job of my dreams?
        </p>
        <p>
         Or maybe I should try speed dating. That way I can get the most face time with potential employers. In this infographic, 
         <a href="http://theundercoverrecruiter.com/speed-dating-interviewing/" target="_blank">
          Speed Dating &amp; Interviewing How to Find The One
         </a>
          shared by The Undercover Recruiter, video interviews are compared with speed dating.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Traditional interviewing, just like traditional dating, can take up too much time and money.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         According to this infographic, video interviews can help companies get impressions of prospective employees without having to put so much time into the process. As the infographic states, not every interview will lead to a hire. Phone calls tend to last 30 minutes. If youre not a good fit for a company, youre not a good fit. Why waste those 30 minutes trying to find out? Speed dating and video interviews can save you time. If you realize that a company is not a good fit, you can move on to the next.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. He (The Employer) Might Just Not Be That Into You
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The search for the one is no easier when referring to a job than when it is referring to a life partner. As I do every Valentines Day, Ive spent some time flipping through my copy of
         <em>
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/He's_Just_Not_That_into_You" target="_blank">
           Hes Just Not That Into You
          </a>
         </em>
         and watched the movie about four times (thats not weird, right??). Those six words have become my mantra. Luckily for me,
         <a href="http://www.snagajob.com/" target="_blank">
          Snagajob
         </a>
         has already applied it to the job hunt. I grab a pint of Ben &amp; Jerrys Phish Food Fro-Yo and sit down at my laptop for some
         <a href="http://www.snagajob.com/resources/how-to-handle-job-search-rejection" target="_blank">
          Hes Just Not That Into You Interview Tips
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Just because I am not searching for a new boyfriend this Valentines Day does not mean I have escaped from the dating scene.
        </p>
        <p>
         The job search is just another form of dating and the same rules and regulations apply.
        </p>
        <p>
         Keeping this in mind, I am going to take control of my life and pursue a new job sans desperation and with the knowledge that I may have to try new things. Ill read the signs to avoid waiting around for a job that is just not that into me. That way Ill avoid a typical Valentines Day night eating chocolates and ice cream, watching
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1126618/?ref_=sr_1" target="_blank">
           Morning Glory
          </a>
         </em>
         (a romantic comedy focused on the relationship between a girl and her job), and wondering if that job I like is ever going to call.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          P.S.
         </strong>
         Do you treat the job search like the quest for a romantic partner? Have any tips for being irresistible to employers? Let us know in the comments!
        </p>

        expires = "; expires=" + date.toGMTString();
