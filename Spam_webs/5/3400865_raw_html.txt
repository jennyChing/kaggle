         <strong>
          6 things happy parents say no to
         </strong>
         <a class="inline ">
          <span>
           SingaporeScene
          </span>
         </a>
         <span>
          Saturday, 23 May, 2015
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span>
          Daniel Wong
         </span>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="uim" id="cardview_article">
       <div class="bd">
        <div class="description uic">
         <br/>
         As a parent, it can sometimes feel like your to-do list is never-ending.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         You have so many responsibilities to fulfill, chores to complete, and errands to run.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Stressful, isnt it?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Through
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.daniel-wong.com/">
          <span>
           my work
          </span>
         </a>
         , I interact with lots of parents. Sadly, it seems like many parents are overwhelmed by the hustle and bustle of life, and have
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.daniel-wong.com/2012/05/26/10-questions-that-successful-parents-ask-themselves/">
          <span>
           lost the joy of parenthood
          </span>
         </a>
         .
         <br/>
         <br/>
         In this article, Ill share with you six things to say no to as a parent. When you say no to these things, Im confident that youll become a happier  and better  parent.
         <br/>
        </div>
        <div class="uim plain-ad bdr">
         <div class="bd">
          <div class="uip first">
           <a href="http://1289298.r.msn.com/?ld=b2CzQC2ekNf0nGX213GpiLdDVUCUxKZlmZ0V4QwdS-5yoYV47nbh4rvWBfyOFEQ_HueS63ztBhT89ypLZtbtVcMtFYwu8BZJRtC9kWMttgDRc9G96kaFetigAcSZF2MYz6o3NDBg&amp;u=www.theinsolestore.com%2finsoles-store-brands%2fspenco-insoles-and-orthotics-from-the-insole-store.html%3flength%3d29%26utm_source%3dadCenter%26utm_medium%3dcpc%26utm_campaign%3d(roi)%2binsoles%2b-%2bspenco%26utm_content%3dfull%2blength%2binsoles%26utm_term%3dspenco+full+length+medical%26utm_creative%3dc%26utm_device%3dc">

        </div>
        <div class="description uic">
         <br/>
         Here they are:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         1. Say no to perfection
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Do you strive for perfection?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Some parents hold on to these ideals:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - They should never lose their temper.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - They should always be cheerful.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Their home should be neat and clean all the time.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - They should be involved in charity work and in serving the community.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - They should have an active social life.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Their children should be well-behaved.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Their children should perform well in school.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Their children should excel in their co-curricular activities.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Dont get me wrong. We should set goals in the different areas of our lives. And yes, we should develop ourselves physically, mentally and emotionally.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         But we must also accept that were not perfect.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Lets be kind to ourselves. Lets not hold ourselves to unattainable standards. Lets say no to perfection, and instead say yes to progress.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         2. Say no to over-scheduling your childrens lives
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Every parent wants their children to maximise their potential.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Wouldnt it be a waste if your child had musical or artistic talent, but never took music or art lessons?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         But this mindset can become too extreme.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Ive worked with students who participate in three co-curricular activities, as well as music and dance lessons. This is on top of their other academic enrichment classes.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Talk about being scarily busy!
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Childhood and adolescence are times of intense growth and development. But for
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.daniel-wong.com/2014/12/01/raising-resilient-children/">
          <span>
           optimal growth and development
          </span>
         </a>
         , children need time to think, dream, reflect and rest.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Its almost impossible to do this when theyre rushing from lesson to lesson and activity to activity.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Whats more, parents of these children often spend most of their weekends sending their children to the various enrichment classes. As such, these families dont spend much quality time together.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Ive worked with some families that dont even have any regular family time at all.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         So I encourage you not to register your children for more than one music/art-related activity and one sports-related activity. This way, everyone in the home will be happier and less stressed.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         3. Say no to comparing your children with others
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Im a parent myself, so I know this is easier said than done.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         But at some level, we realise that there will always be children who are smarter, more talented, more athletic, and more hardworking than our children.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Of course, you should encourage your children to adopt the right attitude and to cultivate a strong work ethic. But you, too, must do your best not to compare them with other children  especially not in front of them.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Making comparisons will cause your children to feel hurt. They may start to believe that your love for them is conditional.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Your children need to know that
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.daniel-wong.com/2014/10/14/7-phrases-children-need-to-hear-from-parents/">
          <span>
           you love them the same
          </span>
         </a>
         , no matter how well-behaved they are, no matter what grades they get, and no matter how many awards or medals they win.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         To help you say no to making comparisons, ask yourself if youre hanging out with bad company. Do your friends (who are also parents) frequently make comparisons? Are they overly competitive? Do they make you feel afraid of losing out?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         If your friends arent a good influence, minimise your contact with them. Find new friends who will spur you on to become a better, more joyful parent.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         4. Say no to comparing yourself with other parents
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Im sure you know some parents who seem to have it all.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         They have a thriving marriage and career. They have great kids. Theyre energetic and cheerful. And they own a nicer house and car than you.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         With both envy and curiosity, you wonder: How do they do it?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Comparing yourself with other parents is unhealthy, just as comparing your children with others is unhealthy. By comparing yourself with people who seem to have the perfect life, youll begin to feel dissatisfied with your own life.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Envy is the enemy of gratitude. And gratitude is a key factor that leads to long-term happiness and success.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         When you make comparisons, you cant focus on all that you have to be thankful for: your family, your friends, your health, your job, and the many comforts you enjoy on a daily basis.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         We must all decide to run our own race. In this race, the aim isnt to finish first. Its to finish well.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Finishing well is about serving others, making a contribution, establishing meaningful relationships, and building a home thats filled with warmth and love.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Finishing well doesnt require a huge amount of wealth, nor does it require you to have a perfect life.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Whats the first step to finishing well? Say no to comparing yourself with other parents.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         5. Say no to being overprotective of your children
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Ive interacted with countless students who dont understand that choices lead to consequences.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Why dont they grasp this reality of life?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Because throughout their lives, their parents have sheltered them from negative consequences:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - When they forget to bring their homework to school, their parents drop it off later that day.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - When they get in trouble with their teacher, their parents prevent them from getting punished.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - When they dont get into the co-curricular activity or club they want, their parents intervene.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - When they wake up late for school, their parents write a note to excuse them.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         These are just a few examples of how well-intentioned parents prevent their children from learning responsibility. Overprotective parents raise children who are
         <a class="inline " href="http://www.daniel-wong.com/2015/05/11/15-things-i-want-my-son-to-know/">
          <span>
           ill-prepared for the real world
          </span>
         </a>
         , where youre forced to bear the consequences of your actions.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         So as parents, lets allow our children to make mistakes. Lets allow them to face challenges, to fail, to overcome setbacks. In so doing, theyll become more independent and resilient.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         (Of course, if theyre in physical danger, we should step in.)
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Its heartbreaking to see our children fall. But if we dont let them fall when theyre kids or teenagers, they might never learn to get up on their own. And as Victor Kiam once noted, Even if you fall on your face, youre still moving forward.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         6. Say no to putting your needs last
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Without a doubt, parenthood involves sacrifice. A lot of it.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         In fact, when I became a first-time parent, my mum told me that parenthood can be summed up in one word: sacrifice.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         But its unwise to continually put our needs last, because well eventually burn out.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         As the saying goes, we cant give away what we dont already have. So if we want to share joy, happiness, kindness and love with those around us, we must first have those things inside of us.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         How do we ensure this?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         By taking care of our needs:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Making time to catch up with close friends
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Having a weekly or fortnightly date with our spouse
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Spending some alone time every day to think and reflect
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Making sleep and exercise a priority
         <br/>
         <br/>
         - Asking for help when necessary
         <br/>
         <br/>
         When we do these things, well be recharged and re-energised. Well be better parents too.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         The bottom line
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Saying no is hard, especially when it comes to the six things mentioned in this article.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         But as you intentionally put these tips into practice, youll have the time and energy for the truly meaningful things in life.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         So start saying no today, and watch as you become a happier, more effective parent.
