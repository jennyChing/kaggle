           Do you trust my judgment?
           <br/>
           I dont know about some of the compatibility hacks you put in there, but sure.
           <br/>
           Sit down. I need you to look as me as I tell you this.
          </p>
          <p>
           Mark slowly rounded the table in the spartan SCIF. Something designed to keep secret the most sensitive operations and information of the United States government, he had never asked why one was in the basement of Microsoft Research. Or why he was being allowed in, which made null any attestations Microsoft could make about it being clean.
          </p>
          <p>
           Mark, Satya wasnt an accident. Ive simulated the inputs to every version of the driving algorithms that have ever gone GDR. Even LDR. Ive simulated against every type of CPU errata, Ive simulated triple simultaneous cosmic bit flips across every byte of memory. Stuff even NASA thinks is too impossible to validate against. I couldnt convince our software to drive that car off that cliff with the data it was being fed.
          </p>
          <p>
           Russinovich sat back and narrowed his gaze, as if physically burdened. His mind was blazing.
          </p>
          <p>
           Mark. He was murdered. Our CEO was murdered. Daves face was expressionless. Marks was not, trying to merge the conflicting information he now possessed.
           <br/>
           But we have the logs. We have every tick showing what happened. Your testing must be flawed.
           <br/>
           <span>
            Ive been building instruction sets since you were a kid. The logs are fake.
            <br/>
           </span>
           <span>
            There are no discrepancies. The digital signatures are valid. It would take billions of years. You know this.
            <br/>
           </span>
           <span>
            Not if you had figured out how to more efficiently factor large numbers. Not if you had Azure. Not if you were siphoning away 50% of the CPUs for three consecutive nights. Not if you were a machine.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Cutler, what are you saying.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Cortana. Im saying Cortana, Mark.
          </p>
         </div>
