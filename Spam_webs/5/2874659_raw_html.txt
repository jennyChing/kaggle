         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.bakerella.com/dont-lick-bite%e2%80%a6/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Dont lick, bite">
           Dont lick, bite
          </a>
         </h2>
         <div class="content">
          <p>
            into these sweet cake pops. I wanted to make cake balls this weekend, and I wanted them to be pink inside, so I tried making a white cake with raspberries. It made the cake pinkish, but its consistency just wouldnt work, so I decided to stick with the
           <a href="http://bakerella.blogspot.com/2007/12/red-velvet-cake-balls.html">
            red velvet cake ball recipe
           </a>
           and went to the store and bought a chocolate cake mix to substitute. I also used some pink candy melts instead of the chocolate bark. And just to make them even a little cuter, stuck them on lollipop sticks Cakesicles anyone?
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bakerella/2224040183/" title="Chocolate Cake Balls by Bakerella, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Chocolate Cake Balls" height="299" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2321/2224040183_96191f8065_o.jpg" width="450"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you want to make these, too, just follow the instructions for the
           <a href="http://bakerella.blogspot.com/2007/12/red-velvet-cake-balls.html">
            red velvet cake balls
           </a>
           and use lollipop sticks to insert into the balls and then stick in the fridge or freezer for a little while before you dip in the coating. I also used a styrofoam block to stick the pops in to dry.
          </p>

            Sure, first roll the balls compactly by hand, insert the lollipop stick and then, make sure you freeze them for a little  while or either put them in the fridge for a few hours. I think I had to hold and rotate it upside down for a few seconds until it started to set so when I turned it right side up, it wouldnt drip.

           <p>
            Hey, Bakerella! I wanted to give you &amp; your readers a couple of tips.
           </p>
           <p>
            First, my stepmother used to be very big into candymaking and still does it from time to time for holidays. When painting her candy molds, shed have several soup-size mugs full of the colored candy chips warmed up at the same time. She did this by using an
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Presto-06852-16-Inch-Electric-Skillet/dp/B0007XRTDG" rel="nofollow">
             electric skillet
            </a>
            about a third full of water. I think the setting would be on low or medium. This keeps the chocolate warmed up and ready to use, plus you can have several colors at your fingertips.
           </p>
           <p>
            Just two important things to remember:
            <br/>
            <b>
             Do NOT get any water in the chocolate.
            </b>
            <br/>
            <b>
             Rinse the the bowl/mug out while the chocolate is warm when youre done (its a pain otherwise). This means that you start with a small amount of choc to begin with  you can always add more!
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            Craft stores like Hobby Lobby &amp; Michaels are good for finding the colored chocolate. I know my stepmom gets her supplies at a local store that specializes in cake decorating supplies.

           <p>
            Love this recipe!  A girlfriend forwarded to me as a good idea for a bridal shower.  I made them from scratch using the following recipes for red velvet cake and frosting:
           </p>
           <p>
            Red Velvet Cake
           </p>
           <p>
            2.5 C cake flour
            <br/>
            3 T cocoa powder (not dutch process)
            <br/>
            1 t baking soda
            <br/>
            1 t salt
            <br/>
            1 C buttermilk
            <br/>
            2 T red food coloring
            <br/>
            1 t vanilla extract
            <br/>
            1 t distilled white vinegar
            <br/>
            8 T (one stick) unsalted butter, at room temperature
            <br/>
            1.5 C granulated sugar
            <br/>
            2 large eggs, at room temperature
           </p>
           <p>
            Preheat oven to 350 with rack in center.  Butter two 9-inch layer pans.  Line bottoms of pans with parchment, then dust with flour and tap out excess (I just did a 913 pan since the cake would be crumbled after baking anyway).
           </p>
           <p>
            Sift flour, cocoa, baking soda and salt together.  Mix buttermilk, food coloring, vanilla and vinegar in glass bowl.  Beat butter and sugar in a large bowl with hand held mixer on high speed until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes.  Add eggs, one at a time.  On low speed, add the flour in 3 additions, alternating with 2 additions of buttermilk mixture, beginning and ending with the flour mixture.  Beat until smooth, scraping sides of bowl often with a rubber spatula.  Spread evenly in pan(s).
           </p>
           <p>
            Bake until a wooden toothpick inserted into center of cake comes out clean, about 25-30 minutes.  Transfer to wire racks and cool for 10 minutes.  Invert onto racks and remove parchment.  Cool completely.
           </p>
           <p>
            Grandmas Cream Cheese Frosting
           </p>
           <p>
            8oz cream cheese
            <br/>
            16oz powdered sugar
            <br/>
             C butter
           </p>
           <p>
            Mix ingredients well until combined.

            deeann  I melt the chocolate in a cereal-size bowl (not too shallow). I kind of swirl a hole in the chocolate I drop the ball in the bowl, use a small spoon to cover top with chocolate. Then I scoop up the ball with the spoon and tap off the extra on the side of my bowl. (I tap it a few times, and let the excess fall off the sides of the spoon while its still over the bowl.)
