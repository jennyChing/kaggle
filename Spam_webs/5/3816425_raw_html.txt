       <h2>
        Residential and Commercial Security Lighting in Maryland
       </h2>
       <div class="entry">
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.tristarelectric.net/blog/maryland-electrician/residential-and-commercial-security-lighting-in-maryland/attachment/ocupation-burglar/" rel="attachment wp-att-444">
          <img alt="Security Lighting Maryland" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-444" height="300" src="http://www.tristarelectric.net/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Security-Lighting-Maryland-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         There are 157,913 property crimes in the state of Maryland annually, according to Neighborhoodscout.com. These crimes include burglary, larceny, theft, motor vehicle theft, arson, shoplifting, and vandalism. The chances of being a victim of a property crime are 1 in 38. Additionally, Maryland experiences 28,089 violent crimes annually: murder, rape, robbery, and assault. The chances of being a victim of a violent crime are 1 in 211.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Are you safe?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Good lighting deters crime and produces a more secure atmosphere, according to a U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ) report.
         <span id="more-443">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         Residential and Commercial Security Lighting in Maryland
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Keep your home or business safe from predators and criminals with security lighting that is not only functional, a proven deterrent to crime, but also stylish, capable of instantly boosting curb appeal. Our skilled electricians will inspect your property and its surroundings to determine the best lighting system for your unique needs.
        </p>
        <p align="center" style="text-align: center;">
         <b>
          <a href="http://www.tristarelectric.net/contact.php">
           Click Here
          </a>
         </b>
         to find out more!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Our goal is to place lights in strategic positions: entrance ways and exits, along with common outside areas, such as driveways and
         <a href="http://www.tristarelectric.net/blog/parking-lot-security-lighting/parking-lot-crime-statistics-and-security-lighting/">
          parking areas
         </a>
         . This will help maximize visibility and deter criminal activity. All the wiring for these lights will be securely hidden from view.
        </p>
        <p align="center" style="text-align: center;">
         <b>
          <i>
           Keep your family, your house, your customers, or your business safe and secure from unwanted visitors and criminals.
          </i>
         </b>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         Additional Security Measures
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         According to Alarm System Report, over 2 million home burglaries are reported in the United States each year and, on average, a burglary of a home occurs every 13 seconds. That breaks down to about four burglaries a minute, 240 an hour, and nearly 6,000 a day. Is your home or business well-protected?
        </p>
        <ol style="text-align: left;">
         <li>
          <b>
           Security Alarms:
          </b>
          Data showed that a steady decrease in burglaries in Newark between 2001 and 2005 coincided with an increase in the number of registered home burglar alarms, said study author Dr. Seungmug (a.k.a. Zech) Lee.
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Windows and Doors:
          </b>
          About 30% of all burglaries are through an open or unlocked window or door. For these reasons, it is important to ensure your windows are securely locked.
         </li>
         <li>
          <b>
           Fencing:
          </b>
          A fence is a great, cost-effective deterrent to criminal activity.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         According to the DOJ, the single most important CPTED (Crime Prevention Through Environmental Design) security feature is lighting.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         If you are interested in learning more about
