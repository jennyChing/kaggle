      <h2 class="storytitle">
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/first-look-the-nolitan-hotel/" rel="bookmark">
        Style Arrives on Kenmare Street: A First Look At The Nolitan Hotel
       </a>
      </h2>
      <p>
       By Terry Trucco
      </p>
      <div class="meta">
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/category/first-look/" rel="category tag">
        First Look
       </a>
       | tags:
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/tag/edmond-li/" rel="tag">
        Edmond Li
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/tag/grzywinskipons/" rel="tag">
        Grzywinski+Pons
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/tag/hotels/" rel="tag">
        hotels
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/tag/new-hotel/" rel="tag">
        new hotel
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/tag/nolita/" rel="tag">
        Nolita
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/tag/nolitan/" rel="tag">
        Nolitan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/tag/red-flower/" rel="tag">
        Red Flower
       </a>
       |  August 5, 2011
      </div>
      <div class="storycontent">
       <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_2206" style="width: 283px">
        <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/the-five-best-new-york-hotel-libraries/nolitan-library/" rel="attachment wp-att-2206">
         <img alt="Nolitan library" class="size-full wp-image-2206" height="364" src="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/wp-content/uploads/nolitan-library.jpg" width="273"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Nolitan library
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        One day a parking lot, the next day a hotel. Okay, the
        <strong>
         Nolitan
        </strong>
        wasnt built in a day.
       </p>
       <p>
        But after years when new hotels mostly grew out of old buildings, New Yorks latest properties are rising from the ground up. Consider the
        <strong>
         <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/the-takeaway-great-escapes-new-design-for-new-york-hotels/">
          Mondrian SoHo
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/trending-is-the-check-in-desk-dead/" target="_blank">
          Yotel Times Square
         </a>
         , Dream Downtown
        </strong>
        and
        <strong>
         <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/the-five-best-hotels-for-bike-riders/" target="_blank">
          the James
         </a>
         ,
        </strong>
        to name a few.
       </p>
       <p>
        The 55-room Nolitan, which opened this week, joins this group with one big difference. This is the first hotel in Nolita, the crunchy, east-of-SoHo neighborhood that nudges Chinatown and Little Italy (hence the North of Little Italy moniker).
       </p>
       <p>
        A spiritual subset of the Lower East Side, Nolita feels like a genuine neighborhood with its mix of hip restaurants and shops next to old-time bakeries, glam-free warehouses, auto repair shops and miraculously, almost no chain stores. Greenery is scarce, but the skyline is low.
       </p>
       <p>
        How does this translate into a hotel? I headed downtown to find out.
        <span id="more-1954">
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_1957" style="width: 308px">
        <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/first-look-the-nolitan-hotel/nolitan-room/" rel="attachment wp-att-1957">
         <img alt="Nolitan guest room." class="size-full wp-image-1957" height="397" src="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/wp-content/uploads/Nolitan-room.jpg" width="298"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Nolitan guest room.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        An early Web site photo displayed a traditional brick edifice, so we were startled to see a wide glass-and-metal structure cutting a hip but low 21
        <sup>
         st
        </sup>
        -century profile that blends with the surroundings. Owner Edmond Li, a local real estate developer, wanted an open, light-filled lobby  nothing dark or intimidating, we were told  and he got it. Build a hotel from scratch, and floor-to-ceiling windows are yours.
       </p>
       <p>
        Those windows amplify the space as does the open, loft-style layout. (Grzywinski+Pons, the hotel design team, also did the contempo-chic Hotel on Rivington.) For a small hotel, the airy Nolitan feels larger than it is. The lobby offers two modestly generous seating areas punctuated by kicky mid-century mod-style pendant lights that channel Calder mobiles. Tufted cognac-colored leather sofas dress up the cozier seating area, a laid-back library well stocked with Phaidon coffee table books and bordered by a clever frosted glass panel, ridged like a Ruffles potato chip, and nifty wood-framed mirrors that swivel. Behind stands ellabess, the hotel restaurant helmed by chef Troy Unruh and furnished in downtown-spare dark-wood tables and chairs. (Ellabess hadnt yet opened when we visited, but
        <strong>
         <a href="http://ny.eater.com/archives/2011/08/gnbn_ellabess_left_bank.php" target="_blank">
          early reviews
         </a>
        </strong>
        range from raves to
        <em>
         meh
        </em>
        .)
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_1959" style="width: 374px">
        <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/first-look-the-nolitan-hotel/nolitan-chair/" rel="attachment wp-att-1959">
         <img alt="Guest room chair." class="size-full wp-image-1959" height="273" src="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/wp-content/uploads/Nolitan-chair.jpg" width="364"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Guest room chair.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        The laid-back-glam-meets-industrial-loft vibe migrates to the rooms upstairs. Consider the chic oak floors, concrete ceilings and ceiling fans. Corner rooms are the showpieces. A brief hallway with a closeted toilet and a generous clothes closet leads to a sleeping area that overlooks an urban cityscape  and the Williamsburg Bridge. Storage drawers hide beneath the queen wood platform bed, dressed in 300-count cotton sheets from One Park Avenue and a zingy red throw. Frosted glass windows, paired with large clear glass panes, offer privacy and light (floor-to-ceiling curtains swish closed for privacy).
       </p>
       <p>
        The good-looking open bathroom features a Corian washstand, stall shower and a gorgeous egg-shaped soaking tub with a window view. The room has so much panache you almost forget its rather small.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_1960" style="width: 283px">
        <a href="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/first-look-the-nolitan-hotel/nolitan-tub/" rel="attachment wp-att-1960">
         <img alt="Corner room tub." class="size-full wp-image-1960" height="364" src="http://blog.overnightnewyork.com/wp-content/uploads/nolitan-tub.jpg" width="273"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Corner room tub.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Single-window rooms, also compact, look much the same, with peek-a-boo stall showers overlooking the room (a curtain pulls over the glass) but no bathtubs. Better still, more than half the rooms sport glass-fronted balconies  narrower than Juliet would have liked but plenty big if you want a breath of air or a glass of wine alfresco.
       </p>
       <p>
        What we liked best were the details. Magnetized Do Not Disturb signs, a bright red yoga mat in the closet, bespoke bath products by Nolita-based Red Flower, a packet of discount cards from local shops like Selima Optical, Sigerson Morrison and Vince, free WiFi  fun stuff, in other words. You can borrow a bike or a skateboard. You can check out at 2 pm. The hotel even has its own newspaper, the
        <em>
         Nolitan
        </em>
        . The
        <em>
         de rigueur
        </em>
        roof deck/bar/lounge is coming soon.
       </p>
       <p>
        Lots of thought went into the place, in other words. In the curious, competitive world of boutique hotels, the Nolitan more than holds its own. Rooms start at $259 through the end of August.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         The Nolitan, 30 Kenmare Street at Elizabeth Street, 212 925-2555.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
