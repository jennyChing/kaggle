            Feet are considered spiritually and symbolically dirty, and so a person should never point their feet at another person, or at a temple. (This is mostly a concern when sitting, especially if cross-legged in a chairyour crossed foot should not dangle in someones direction.)
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/caitlinator/">
             Caitlin Regan
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts5-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts5-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           9
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            On the flip side, the head is the holiest part of the body. Thus, you should never touch someones headeven a child's.
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/frosch50/">
             Georgie Pauwels
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="pullquote-center">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/site/matador-logo-small.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class=" lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/site/matador-logo-small.png" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
         <em>
          See more:
          <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/trips/7-adventures-thailand-shouldnt-miss/" target="_blank" title="7 adventures in Thailand you shouldn't miss">
           7 adventures in Thailand you shouldn't miss
          </a>
         </em>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts9-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts9-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           10
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            Thailand has a college just for monkeys. It's called Thani Monkey College, and the students learn all sorts of street-preforming tricks, as well as how to collect coconuts. Speaking of monkeys, Thailand also has a special banqueta monkey banquet, where thousands of monkeys are served platters of fruit and vegetables.
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/andrewrennie/">
             Andy Rennie
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts17-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts17-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           11
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            Thailand is a constitutional monarchy, and most Thais adore their king and queen. Many families display the king's face in their homes, and its pretty much illegal to speak ill of him. The Thai royal families have been so revered that, in the past, no one was allowed to even touch them; in fact, in the 1800s, a queen drowned when her boat capsized and no onlookers came her to rescue due to this strict rule.
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/maxxp/">
             Maxence
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts15-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts15-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           12
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            Many Thais have a firm belief in ghosts. For example, after purchasing a house, its very common to build a small spirit house for whoever occupied the site in the past, and to give offerings to the spirits. If you were to stay in a Thai home, you might be asked to make a small offering and ask permission from the spirits for your stay. Of course, every family and their degree of belief is different, but walking around after dark just got a little spookier.
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/rcbodden/">
             Ray Bodden
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts13-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts13-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           13
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            Thailand is one of the most Buddhist nations on Earth, with 95% of the population identifying as such. Its common for Thai men to spend a little time in their youths trying out life as a monk, though most do not enter monkhood.
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/104284854@N07/">
             KX Studio
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts16-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts16-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           14
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            Everyone loves Thai food, right? Next time, try one of these more authentic dishes:
            <em>
             goong ten
            </em>
            (live shrimp salad),
            <em>
             larb mote daeng
            </em>
            (red ants and their eggs),
            <em>
             baak bpet
            </em>
            (duck mouths), or
            <em>
             mok huak
            </em>
            (grown tadpoles in fermented fish sauce).
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/jaaronfarr/">
             J Aaron Farr
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts8-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts8-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           15
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            In Bangkok, its normal to see the thermometer soar above 40C (104F), and wintertime temps hang out around 26C (79F).
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/travelbusy/">
             Travelbusy.com
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="pullquote-center">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/site/matador-logo-small.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class=" lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/site/matador-logo-small.png" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png"/>
         <em>
          See more:
          <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/trips/8-ways-get-utterly-beaten-track-thailand/" target="_blank" title="8 ways to get utterly off the beaten track in Thailand">
           8 ways to get utterly off the beaten track in Thailand
          </a>
         </em>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts11-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts11-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           16
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            The Vivaldi Restaurant in Bangkok served the worlds most expensive cocktail, the Valentines Cocktail. Coming in at 540,000 baht (thats more than $15,000 US), it was garnished with a five-karat ruby instead of an olive, and served with a six-course dinner, a night in the adjoining luxury hotel, and a bottle of Dom. Happy Valentines Day, millionaires.
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/dinnerseries/">
             Didriks
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts14-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts14-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           17
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            Thailand is home to one of the worlds largest freshwater fish, the Mekong giant catfish, which can weigh up to 700lbs, as well as the worlds smallest bat, the bumblebee bat (or Kittis hog-nosed bat), which grows to a little over an inch and weighs two grams.
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/sanmartin/">
             Gilles San Martin
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts18-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/thailand-facts18-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           18
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            Songkran is one of the worlds largest water festivals and takes place in one of the hottest months of the Thai yearApril. It's also a time for cleansing (both personally and in the home, similar to spring cleaning) and signals the Thai New Year. By the way, in Thailand, the current year is 2557 (543 years ahead of the Gregorian Calendar).
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cm_john/">
             John Shedrick
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-gallery-item">
         <noscript>
          <img alt="" src="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/yi-peng-940x705.png" title=""/>
         </noscript>
         <img class="post-gallery-item-img lazy" data-original="http://cdn2.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2014/08/yi-peng-940x705.png" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
         <div class="post-gallery-item-meta">
          <div class="post-gallery-item-count">
           19
          </div>
          <div class="post-gallery-item-caption">
           <p>
            Another famous festival is Yi Peng, which takes place in Novemberan excellent month to visit Thailand, weather-wiseand is a celebration that sees thousands of paper lanterns lit and released to the sky. The festival also includes boat races, beauty contests, fireworks, and parades galore.
            <br/>
            Photo:
            <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cm_john/">
             John Shedrick
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>

          <p>
           Mostly good stuff here. My only gripe is with the names Thailand and Siam Thailand probably means simply Land of the Thais (Or Country of the Thais more literally), not Land of the Free. The Thai speaking people have always identified themselves as Thai, and while its true that thai can mean free (depending on the tone), the term really refers to an ethnic group, not an adjective. I would also question Siam coming from Sanskrit. The histories that Ive read say that Siam derives from Xian, which is what early Chinese traders called the Ayutthaya kingdom. The Europeans heard this as Siam, so the word doesnt really mean anything to Thais at all, apart from the history that it calls to mind of course. Id argue that Siam has nothing to do with Sanskrit at all. Also, its not okay to step on Thai bank notes because every note has a portrait of the King. Its not the money that should be respected per se; its the person pictured on the money.
