        <img alt="" height="402" src="/assets/Article/2676/images/vow-28-Horses-A-3D-paper-craft-poster-project-dry-the-river-yatzer-3.jpg" width="714"/>

       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="font-size: x-small;">
         Video screen shot

       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="color: #f60e8b;">
         <strong>
          V.O.W N28
         </strong>
        </span>
        (
        <em>
         11 - 17 July 2011
        </em>
        )
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="font-size: x-large;">
         P
        </span>
        osters are an integral part of visual and music culture. Many of our beloved epic designers started their careers developing graphics for music bands and it was through that experience, that they fell in love with visual communication and the connection it has with viewers. This week, we bring you a special and quite different kind of music poster. The UK based band
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.drytheriver.net/" target="_blank">
          Dry the River
         </a>
        </strong>
        collaborated with
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.xavierbarrade.com/" target="_blank">
          Xavier Barrade
         </a>
        </strong>
        from
        <a href="http://www.foamagency.com/" target="_blank">
         <strong>
          FOAM
         </strong>
        </a>
        to unfold a dimensional experience for their latest song
        <strong>
         <span style="color: #f60e8b;">
          Horses
         </span>
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" height="436" width="714">
         <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
         </param>
         <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
         </param>
         <param name="src" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/qkNYS_lip_k?version=3&amp;hl=en_US&amp;autoplay=1&amp;rel=0&amp;hd=1">
         </param>
         <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="436" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/qkNYS_lip_k?version=3&amp;hl=en_US&amp;autoplay=1&amp;rel=0&amp;hd=1" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="714">
         </embed>
        </object>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        The poster contains a three dimensional horse, created in
        <strong>
         <a href="http://sketchup.google.com/" target="_blank">
          Google Sketchup
         </a>
        </strong>
        and then printed and folded to create an impressive structure when displayed throughout the city. Each component of the horse is assembled by hand and then installed across the city. Each horse structure took around
        <strong>
         35 hours to complete
        </strong>
        and the rewards are nothing but remarkable. Watch the video and see the various reactions the posters have on the city dwellers. Will we see more of these 3D paper posters out in our cities? Let's hope so...
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <img alt="" height="398" src="/assets/Article/2676/images/vow-28-Horses-A-3D-paper-craft-poster-project-dry-the-river-yatzer-1.jpg" width="714"/>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <span style="font-size: x-small;">
         Video screen shot
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        Also, check out
        <strong>
         Xavier
        </strong>
        's latest project which invites everyone to participate in drawing illustrations on the infinite canvas at
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.epicexquisitecorpse.com/" target="_blank">
          epicexquisitecorpse.com
         </a>
        </strong>
        . We can see that participation and interaction is part of
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.xavierbarrade.com/" target="_blank">
          Xavier
         </a>
        </strong>
        's vision in his art.
       </p>
