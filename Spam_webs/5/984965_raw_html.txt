      <h2>
       <a href="http://www.portesgaragedoorrepair.ca/repair-tips/tips-to-prevent-garage-break-ins-garage-doors/">
        Garage Door Repair Professionals Give 7 Tips to Prevent Garage Break-ins
       </a>
      </h2>
      <div class="summary-meta">
       <b>
        November 11, 2014
       </b>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="content">
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       A garage door is a perfect component to add value to your home, but unfortunately, this component also leaves the home vulnerable to break ins. In fact, anything neglected in terms of safety has the potential to cause damage. For now, lets have a look at these 7 tips to protect your garage door from mischievous people and criminals.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <b>
        Keep the remote hidden:
       </b>
       Some people will leave their remote in their vehicle even when parking on the street. It is important to hide the remote somewhere in the car or bring it home so that it stays away from the eyes of potential thieves. By following this simple advice, you will prevent a criminal from grabbing the remote out from your car and gaining access to your garage.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Emergency release
       </strong>
       : Most garage doors come with an emergency release. This feature allows anyone to easily open the door and gain access. Using some zip ties to secure the door is a low-tech solution, but it will prevent any future problems.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Close the door
       </strong>
       : People often leave their door wide open when going in and out of the garage. It only takes one time for a criminal to slip in and steal a valuable item. Remember closing and locking the door when away for more than a few minutes. There are many criminals who will stand by and wait for a garage owner to make this seemingly small mistake.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Cover windows
       </strong>
       : While windows on an overhead door are aesthetically pleasing, they showcase all of the items to potential burglars. It is better to keep the windows covered with a piece of newspaper or cardboard. This will not give people an opportunity to peep in the garage and make plans.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Maintenance
       </strong>
       : Neglecting the maintenance of the door is probably one of the biggest mistakes that homeowners make. They do this because garage is a room where most people do not spend much of their time. Unfortunately, neglecting the regular maintenance and necessary repairs will leave your door vulnerable to break ins. It is important to call your local garage door company and schedule a maintenance. Without a doubt, this is the best preventive method to protect your door from opportunistic thieves who are on the lookout for damaged or worn doors.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Code
       </strong>
       : The new overhead doors come with an outdoor key pad that allows the user to enter the area by using a code. Make sure to choose a unique password to prevent smart criminals from gaining an access to the garage and the house.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        Camera
       </strong>
       : A CCTV camera is a good deterrent to mischievous individuals. Place a camera directly above your door to keep most criminals away from the house.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       By following these tips, you can protect your home and garage from potential thieves. Better yet  call
       <b>
        garage door repair professionals
       </b>
       for an inspection, repair and maintenance of your overhead door.
      </p>
