        How Much Hemp Seeds Should I Eat?
       </h1>
       <div class="BlockContent">

         <p>
          <a href="http://www.hippiebutter.com/hemp-seed-food">

          <a href="http://www.hippiebutter.com/hemp-seed-food">
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.hippiebutter.com/hemp-seed-food">
           <img alt="How Much Hemp Seeds Should I Eat?" src="http://cdn2.bigcommerce.com/server3000/2f91a/product_images/uploaded_images/how-much-hemp-seeds-should-i-eat.png?t=1432774928"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Hemp seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
          have become quite popular. From vegan and vegetarian to primal and Paleo diets, many people are looking for a good source of fat and protein that is as natural as possible. But, eventually you will ask yourself, how much should I eat? While
          <strong>
           <u>
            hemp seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
           health benefits are quite notable, we need to put our consumption of them within a context of a variety of healthy fats.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Healthy Diet
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Those of us living a vegan lifestyle need to get protein and fat wherever we can find it.
          <strong>
           <u>
            Hemp seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
          offer us an excellent option in this regard. The small, nutty-tasting seeds have a texture somewhere between sunflower and sesame seeds. Just one tablespoon of
          <strong>
           <u>
            hemp seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
          , tossed on a salad, provides about four grams of fat and 3-5 grams of protein. The fats contain a healthy quantity of Omega-6 and Omega-3 fatty acids.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.hippiebutter.com/hemp-seed-food">
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Skin Care
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Hemp Seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
          are served in a number of fashions, from the whole seeds to a crushed meal and even oil. While the oil itself is also ready to eat, it is a good choice for
          <a href="http://www.hippiebutter.com/categories/Skin-Care/">
           hemp skin care
          </a>
          as well.
          <a href="http://www.hippiebutter.com/hemp-seed-oil">
           Hemp seed oil
          </a>
          has an ideal balance of essential fatty acids that is similar to the lipids naturally present in our skin. As a result,
          <a href="http://www.hippiebutter.com/hemp-seed-oil">
           hemp seed oil
          </a>
          is useful to apply directly to the skin to prevent dehydration.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Eating Hemp Seeds
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Now that you know how good
          <strong>
           <u>
            hemp seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
          are for your body, you just need to figure out how much to eat. Like most seeds, a tablespoon or two a day of seeds or seed meal is all that is necessary. While
          <strong>
           <u>
            hemp seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
          have a low ratio of Omega-6 to Omega-3 fatty acids, at 3:1, they are still comparatively high in polyunsaturated fat. As such, we suggest that
          <strong>
           <u>
            hemp seed
           </u>
          </strong>
          consumption should be balanced with saturated and monounsaturated fats, found in coconut, nuts and even animal products.
         </p>
         <p>
          Just a tablespoon of
          <strong>
           <u>
            hemp seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
          each day is enough to give a flavorful crunch and pack a nutritional wallop for our diets. When eating
          <strong>
           <u>
            hemp seeds
           </u>
          </strong>
          in moderation, we cannot lose.
         </p>
