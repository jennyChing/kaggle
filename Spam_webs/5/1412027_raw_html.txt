         <h1>
          4 Ways to Get the Most Out of Quick Trips Abroad
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          by
          <a href="http://acruisingcouple.com/author/pwpadmin/" rel="author" title="Posts by A Cruising Couple">
           A Cruising Couple
          </a>
          | Jan 13, 2015 |
          <a href="http://acruisingcouple.com/travel-inspiration/" rel="category tag">
           Travel Inspiration
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://acruisingcouple.com/destinations/world/" rel="category tag">
           World
          </a>
          |
          <span class="comments-number">
           <a href="http://acruisingcouple.com/2015/01/4-ways-get-quick-trips-abroad/#respond">
            16 comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
          <div id="triberr_endorsement">
          </div>
          <p>
           Hope everyone is having a great start to their 2015! Were back in North Carolina after our NYC escapade, enjoying the slightly less cold weather and spending as much time as we can with family and friends. We still have so many stories to share about our recent travels  as well as reveal where it is were headed next in just two weeks! But in the meantime, we hope you enjoy this guest post from Damien Dante. Hes got four great tips hes sharing today on how to get the most out of quick trips abroad!
          </p>
          <h2>
           Do you want something more?
          </h2>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h4>
           Something more than just an all-inclusive hotel with free drinks and an all-you-can-eat buffet?
          </h4>
          <p>
           <br/>
           Most of us do. We want to feel the connection with the place we travel to. Feel the differences of it.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/acruisingcouple/14676494702" title="Fishing in the Lofotens_8.jpg by Daniel Moore, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Fishing in the Lofotens_8.jpg" height="464" src="//farm3.staticflickr.com/2909/14676494702_6409af9fa6_z.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Feel like weve experienced something important.
          </p>
          <p>
           But theres never enough time for that, right?
          </p>
          <p>
           Believe me, its not your fault if you feel like youre not getting the most out of your trips.
          </p>
          <p>
           I was lost for a long time. I thought that becoming one with a place was about the amount of hours I was gonna walk around or about how many national galleries I was gonna visit.
          </p>
          <p>
           Its not, I learned with time.
           <br/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Here are my 4 quick ways to feel like youve maxed out the experience.
          </h3>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            1. Whats your definition of success?
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/acruisingcouple/14004714077" title="Pulpit Rock by Daniel Moore, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Pulpit Rock" height="427" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7346/14004714077_4683831233_z.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           When it comes to most areas of our lives, we have a definition of what it means to succeed. You know what you want to achieve in your work life, right?
          </p>
          <p>
           You have a measurement when youre happy with your physical looks. You have a scale for success in your finances, your relationships.
          </p>
          <p>
           Did you create one for your travels?
          </p>
          <p>
           Do you know how hard it is to feel satisfied if you dont have a measurement of what satisfied means?
          </p>
          <p>
           Create your definition of success for your next trip. Meet that expectation. Its as simple as that.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            2. Its all about the money
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           One of my favourite ideas about success in work and life is, Youre good if you do something for the money that you would do even if they didnt pay you.
          </p>
          <p>
           Life is about processes.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do you remember the moment when you read a really fascinating book and you forget about anything else in life?
          </p>
          <p>
           Or when youre in the cinema and theres nothing else but the movie?
          </p>
          <p>
           Theres no YOU, no EGO, only the process of being so deep into something the whole world stops.
          </p>
          <p>
           Find these processes, discover what pulls you in the most, and start doing as much of it as possible every day.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            3. The way to experience life the local way
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/acruisingcouple/14454700545" title="DSC_3085.jpg by Daniel Moore, on Flickr">
            <img alt="DSC_3085.jpg" height="400" src="//farm4.staticflickr.com/3837/14454700545_74f27e4fb3_z.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Most people say that you should try to interact with the local people. See how differently they live and experience their way of existence.
          </p>
          <p>
           That might mean sharing a coffee, couch surfing, or simply stopping by and having a conversation with a stranger.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ive tried all of these. Theyre pretty nice. But Ive found something thats even more fun.
          </p>
          <p>
           Instead of trying to experience life by asking questions and interacting, SPY instead.
          </p>
          <p>
           Yes, you read it correctly. I want you to spy on people.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do this Sit down in the nearest coffee shop. Find a target, somebody that looks interesting, and then try to find out as much as possible about that person without actually interacting with him/her.
          </p>
          <p>
           That usually means following and spying. Its fun and great way to feel the tempo and style of people around.
           <br/>
           I spy on people at least once in every country.
          </p>
          <p>
           Try it out for yourself. Youll discover a whole different side of the city youre in.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            4. How to ruin your travels
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           You know one thing that constantly destroys even the best trip idea?
          </p>
          <p>
           Being an adult.
          </p>
          <p>
           The opposite of that is being a child.
          </p>
          <p>
           Adults JUDGE. Children dont. Children are curious.
          </p>
          <p>
           As soon as you take a perspective of an adult, you lose the ability to be wowed and amazed because everything has to fit into a category. Everything has to have a label.
          </p>
          <p>
           Thats simply how we operate.
          </p>
          <p>
           My suggestion is this: Dont judge, be CURIOUS instead. Thats when you experience magic around.
          </p>
          <p>
           Ask quality questions.
          </p>
          <p>
           What else could this mean? is a great one to test on anything that you look at. Why? is another one worth asking a couple times a day.
          </p>
          <p>
           And then answer your questions. Theyre like mysteries waiting for you to discover answers to them.
          </p>
          <p>
           The world around you holds many of these mysteries. But if you label them, you cut yourself away from the chance of adventure.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Time is not your problem
           </b>
          </p>
          <p>
           We all want to experience similar emotions in life. We all want to feel, in the end, weve succeeded and had life beyond our wildest dreams.
          </p>
          <p>
           For many of use that success has a different label. Theres no one definition that fits all.
          </p>
          <p>
           One thing Ive been learning lately is that a journey is not about time. Time is not the most precious resource we have in our lives.
          </p>
          <p>
           Theres one missing ingredient to seizing the moment. Its called ATTENTION.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/acruisingcouple/13124908753" title="Garrafon Reef Park at Isla Mujeres, Mexico by Daniel Moore, on Flickr">
            <img alt="Garrafon Reef Park at Isla Mujeres, Mexico" height="402" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7404/13124908753_998d368e95_z.jpg" width="600"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Time without attention is just something that flows by.
          </p>
          <p>
           But if we put our full attention into the moment, the power of now, and we explore the world around us with the curiosity and smile of a child, we create happiness.
          </p>
          <p>
           Inside, but mostly around us.
          </p>
          <p>
           The world around us wants to be discovered and treated as something fresh and new. Without judgments.
          </p>
          <p>
           And if we can make the world around us a happier place, it gives back that happiness.
          </p>
          <p>
           And then we go to bed and fall asleep with a smile on our face.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do you agree?
          </p>
          <p>
           I hope you want that to happen as much as I do.
          </p>
          <p>

          </p>
