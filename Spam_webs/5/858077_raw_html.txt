              <!--cached-Sat, 11 Jul 2015 12:58:07 +0000-->
              We have had home stay students stay with us a couple of times (we dont have kids though).After the second one we decided it wasnt for us, however some people absolutely love it.I would recommend hosting students who are travelling as part of a group  we had an older student travelling alone and he was always at home, didnt ever bring friends around or show any interest in going out and doing things with others. When we had a group student it was a lot easier as he would spend time with other host families and their students, and we could have his friends over for meals etc as well.Some groups have organised activities too which gives you a bit of breathing space in the weekends.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Obvious things to consider are language and cultural differences  be prepared to be very specific with instructions eg. using the bathroom.On the other hand, its really interesting to be able to learn about another culture from the horses mouth, so to speak.Also obvious but there are big differences between male and female students, from what I saw within our group, the females very much became a part of their host families and were very interactive with their host brothers and sisters, whereas the male students were a lot more independent, so it depends which would work better in your family.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              From a financial point of view the money is quite good, but there is a lot of work involved.
              <br/>

              My parents used to have homestay students from Japan.Initially they just had short term ones (6 weeks or so) but later on some of the short term ones asked if they could come and stay longer while they did whole degrees in Australia (wouldn't have taken on a long term one without knowing them first).
