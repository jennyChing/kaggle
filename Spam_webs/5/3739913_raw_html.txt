          <p>
           If a victim of domestic violence was sitting in your living room, would you even know it? With almost a third of women experiencing intimate partner violence during their lifetime, theres a good chance someone already has.
          </p>
          <p>
           Many of us expect a blackened eye or a desperate midnight call to tell us that someone we care about is being harmed, but thats rarely the case. More often its that that hard-to-describe feeling in the pit of your stomach the last few times your friends boyfriend has tagged along. Its the feeling you swat away with the flick of your wrist and a reminder to yourself that youre always overreacting.
          </p>
          <p>
           But if something doesnt feel right, its often not. Here are five more signs that you ought to listen to your gut when it comes to your friends relationship.
           <b>
           </b>
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
            <b>
             Domestic Violence Sign 1:
            </b>
           </span>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
             <b>
              She lies to him about where shes going
             </b>
            </span>
           </span>
          </h1>
          <p>
           Even if shes just stopping by to watch Homeland, she tells her guy that shes picking up a few things at the grocery store. Its a major red flag when you find your friend having to hide the truth of her whereabouts and activities from her partner. Abusive partners are often jealous  of everyone  and victims start living in fear of dealing with it. She might say things like, Its just easier to tell him Im there, or, I just dont want him to worry. If so, you might want to worry.
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
            Domestic Violence Sign 2:
           </span>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
             <b>
              He just seems different
             </b>
            </span>
           </span>
          </h1>
          <p>
           If your friends mood seems to have taken an unexpected nosedive, it could be relationship trouble. Abuse victims can appear depressed or highly anxious, or sometimes just numb. They are often living with a great deal of stress, compounded by fear and isolation. When you ask them about how they are feeling, they might even blame themselves: Oh, not great. I keep messing things up with our credit and I just cant get it together. Julie is pissed all the time and yelling at me and calling me an idiot. I would be too.
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
            Domestic Violence Sign 3:
           </span>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
             <b>
              Shes getting texts constantly
             </b>
            </span>
           </span>
          </h1>
          <p>
           If her phone looks like its about to blast off and its always her husband, it could be a sign of trouble. Abusive partners are often in constant contact. They cant stand not knowing whats going on with the victim and feeling a sense of control over their daily lives. That might look like persistent text messaging, or frequent phone calls. He might even show up at the bar while you are out for another friends bachelorette party.
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
            Domestic Violence Sign 4:
           </span>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline; font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
            <b>
             Hes wearing long sleeves on the hottest day in July
            </b>
           </span>
          </h1>
          <p>
           Physical signs of abuse are the generally the least common because it can take a while for things to escalate and victims might be good at hiding evidence. However, they certainly shouldnt be ignored when they are observed, or when someone youre concerned about starts doing unusual things to hide parts of their body. Its worth probing a bit. Maybe his t-shirts are all in the washer. Or maybe not.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
            Domestic Violence Sign 5:
           </span>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 18px;">
             <b>
              Shes told you that shes worried what hell do if she breaks up with him
             </b>
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           A classic sign of an abusive relationship is when one partner feels afraid of anothers reaction. When the fear is so great that it keeps someone in a relationship thats already headed south, theres a problem. Some abusive mates will threaten their partners with physical violence, but even more often theyll threaten to harm themselves or warn of other negative consequences (e.g. Youll never graduate without me by your side.) If someone you care about is in a relationship out of fear of the future, they might need help.
          </p>
          <p>
           No one sign is enough to know for sure whats going on behind closed doors, but it could clue you in to trouble. With so many intimate partner violence situations turning deadly each year, its worth sharing your concerns with your loved one. Check in on how he or she is doing and offer your support. Keep in mind that your efforts might not lead to immediate disclosure or change, but at least the person knows youre there if and when they are ready to talk.
          </p>
