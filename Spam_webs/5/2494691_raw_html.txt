           <br/>
           <br/>
           There is a lot that I can say about this interview, I can't quite make out his stance, his political beliefs or if he really has 'fallen off', on the one hand he seems to have jumped on the industry bandwagon, on the other he seems just as socially/political aware as always. In this interview he also questions the change campaign, distances himself from those who are endorsing Obama, as well as distancing himself from the thin boundary between race, politics and hip hop. Yet seems to echo some of their rhetoric.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Then there is the album title, which I am still unsure about (as in my opinion on out!)I don't think he has fully explained what he meant to articulate - or reaction he wished to create by calling his album
           <em>
            Nigger
           </em>
           (or untitled for now). I understand how it is a phrase which is nearly always used in Hip hop but why has he chosen to call the album this?
           <br/>
           <br/>
           His confusion (which is shown in this interview) has left me confused! I am very interested to see how everyone else perceives this interview.
