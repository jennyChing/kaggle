          <p class="p1">
           <span class="s1">
            <strong>
             Xanax is a commonly prescribed medication used to treat anxiety, panic attacks, and insomnia.
            </strong>
            Although its effective at reducing anxiety and promoting sleep, its not without side effects, some of which are serious. What are Xanax risks and is it safe to take this medication?
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p class="p1">
            <strong>
             How xanax works in the brain
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="p1">
           <span class="s1">
            Xanax is a type of drug known as a benzodiazepine which acts directly on the central nervous system in a way which isnt entirely understood. It depresses the nervous system in such a way that anxiety symptoms are reduced and sleep is easier. Its also used to treat panic disorder and certain phobias such as agoraphobia, a fear of going into public places. This medication is a scheduled medication meaning its use is closely regulated because of the risk for abuse.
           </span>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p class="p1">
            <span class="s1">
             <strong>
              What are Xanax Risks?
             </strong>
            </span>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="p1">
           <span class="s1">
            Probably the most serious Xanax risk is the potential for becoming dependent on the medication. Using Xanax for even short periods of time increases the risk of dependence which can lead to withdrawal symptoms once its discontinued. The higher the dose given, the greater the risk of dependence and the subsequent risk of experiencing withdrawal symptoms. When the medication is discontinued after prolonged use, serious symptoms such as seizures and worsening of anxiety symptoms can occur. Xanax can also make driving and operating machinery dangerous because it reduces mental alertness. The list of possible side effects associated with use of Xanax is extensive. At high doses, it can suppress breathing and even result in death.
           </span>
          </p>

              Xanax Risks: The Elderly Are Particularly Susceptible
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="p1">
           <span class="s1">
            One group thats more prone to the side effects of Xanax is the elderly. Xanax can cause dizziness, lightheadedness, and disorientation that poses a particular danger for the elderly. There are numerous cases of elderly people falling while getting up to go to the bathroom at night while on Xanax due to the dizziness and lightheadedness this drug can cause. Such falls increase the risk of hip fracture which can be fatal in the elderly.
           </span>
          </p>

             <span class="s1">
              It Interacts With Other Medications
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="p1">
           <span class="s1">
            Xanax is broken down by the same liver enzymes that break down many other medications. Taking these medications along with Xanax can alter Xanax blood levels leading to greater nervous system depression. Xanax should never be used with alcohol since the combination will depress the nervous system even more. It should also not be used during pregnancy.
           </span>
          </p>

           <p class="p1">

             <span class="s1">
              Xanax Risks: The Bottom Line?
             </span>
            </strong>
           </p>

          <p class="p1">
           <span class="s1">
            Xanax is a medication with serious side effects and should only be used when other measures have failed. It should be completely avoided in the elderly due to their high risk for falls. Talk to your doctor about other alternatives before taking Xanax or any other benzodiazepine.
