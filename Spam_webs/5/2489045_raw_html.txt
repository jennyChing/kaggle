        Chipotle Soup with Potatoes and Blackened Corn
        <br/>
        
       </a>
      </h2>
      <div class="entry-date">
       <abbr class="published" title="2015-07-12T22:04:33-0400">
        Sunday, July 12, 2015
       </abbr>
      </div>
      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.culinarytribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/198.jpg" rel="lightbox[36377]">
         <img alt="198" class="size-full wp-image-36378 aligncenter" height="717" src="http://www.culinarytribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/198.jpg" width="506"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Chipotle Soup with Potatoes and Blackened Corn
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        I knew chipotle was peppers but I didnt know they are  smoke-dried jalapeo peppers.
       </p>
       <p>
        I was so excited when I bought my first chipotle peppers in adobo sauce can.
       </p>
       <p>
        Its spicy but also very flavorful!
       </p>
       <p>
        The smoky flavor is so unique.
       </p>
       <p>
        I wanted to add this wondeful flavor to my vegetable soup and the soup turned out to be absolutely delicious!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Im on
         <a href="https://instagram.com/culinarytribune/" target="_blank">
          Instagram
         </a>
         . Check out my page.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div id="recipe">
        <strong>
         Chipotle Soup with Potatoes and Blackened Corn
        </strong>
        <p>
         canola oil
         <br/>
         2 cloves garlic, chopped
         <br/>
         1 onion, chopped
         <br/>
         1 orange pepper, chopped
         <br/>
         2 cups bite sized pieces potatoes
         <br/>
         32 oz vegetable stock
         <br/>
         3 oz tomato paste
         <br/>
         2 bay leaves
         <br/>
         1 teaspoon dried oregano
         <br/>
         1 tablespoons cumin
         <br/>
         1 tablespoon sugar
         <br/>
         salt and pepper
         <br/>
         chipotle paste
         <br/>
          1 pepper with 1 teaspoon adobo sauce from canned chipotle pepper in adobo sauce
         <br/>
          2 small corn tortilla, torn into small pieces
         <br/>
         2 ears corn, blackened
         <br/>
         chopped cilantro
         <br/>
         chopped green onion
         <br/>
         sour cream  optional
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Saute garlic, onion, pepper, and potatoes in oil for a few minutes.
         <br/>
         2. Add vegetable stock. Boil and continue to cook over low heat. Add tomato paste, bay leaves, oregano, cumin, sugar, and salt and pepper.
         <br/>
         3. Make chipotle paste. Combine the chipotle, adobo sauce, and tortilla. Puree them with a hand blender until it becomes creamy.
         <br/>
         4. Whisk the chipotle paste into the soup. Add blackened corn.
         <br/>
         5. Serve with cilantro, green onion, and sour cream (optional).
        </p>
       </div>
