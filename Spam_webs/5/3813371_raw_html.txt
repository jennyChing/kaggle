       <h2 class="post-title">
        <a href="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/2014/10/02/arata-endo-the-architect-who-carried-on-frank-lloyd-wrights-legacy-in-japan/" rel="bookmark" title="Arata Endo: the architect who carried on Frank Lloyd Wrights Legacy in Japan">
         Arata Endo: the architect who carried on Frank Lloyd Wrights Legacy in Japan
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="post-meta">
        <span class="post-date">
         <a href="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/2014/10/02/arata-endo-the-architect-who-carried-on-frank-lloyd-wrights-legacy-in-japan/" title="Arata Endo: the architect who carried on Frank Lloyd Wrights Legacy in Japan">
          October 2, 2014
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="date-sep">
         /
        </span>
        <span class="post-author">
         <a href="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by Johnny">
          Johnny
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="date-sep">
         /
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/2014/10/02/arata-endo-the-architect-who-carried-on-frank-lloyd-wrights-legacy-in-japan/#comments">

       </div>

      <div class="post-content">
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29708">
        <img alt="Imperial Hotel Wright frank lloyd wright" class="wp-image-29708 size-full" height="584" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Imperial_Hotel_Wright_House.jpg" width="900"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         the entrance to Wrights Imperial Hotel (1922) | images courtesy wikimedia commons
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Famed architect Frank Lloyd Wright discovered Japan through the same method as many in his time: ukiyo-e prints. I remember when I first met Japanese prints, Ill never forget it, Wright once said in a filmed interview. Japanese art had a great influence on my feeling and thinking.
       </p>
       <p>
        Wright first arrived in Japan in 1917 and that same year he met
        <a href="http://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%81%A0%E8%97%A4%E6%96%B0" target="_blank">
         Arata Endo
        </a>
         27 years old at the time and fresh off the team working on plans to construct Meiji Shrine. Like Wright, Endo had also risen to notoriety after publishing a scathing criticism of architect Kingo Tatsuno.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-29707">
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29709">
        <img alt="Imperial Hotel Wright frank lloyd wright" class="wp-image-29709 size-full" height="592" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/ImperialHotelFacade.jpg" width="900"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         the building was damaged during WWII but was recreated and can be seen near Nagoya in the Meiji-Mura Museum
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        The two
        <a href="http://www.wrightinjapan.org/eng_wij/e_appentices/endoarata_e.html" target="_blank">
         architects bonded
        </a>
         both had an infectious wit, no tolerance for mediocrity, and a belief in the sanctity of architecture  and Wright hired Endo as chief draftsman on the Imperial Hotel project. As fate would have it, Okura &amp; Co., whom Wright attributes as his first encounter with Japan 20 years earlier at the Worlds Fair in Chicago, financed and built the hotel.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29712">
        <img alt="jiyu gakuen frank lloyd wright" class="wp-image-29712 size-full" height="405" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Jiyu_gakuen_myonichikan.jpg" width="720"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Jiyu Gakuen Girls School, Tokyo, Japan. Designed by Frank Lloyd Wright in 1921 | images courtesy
         <a href="http://tokyowing.wordpress.com/2010/12/09/jiyu-gakuen-school-myonichikantokyo-1921-by-frank-lloyd-wright/" target="_blank">
          tokyowing
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29710">
        <img alt="jiyu gakuen frank lloyd wright" class="wp-image-29710 size-full" height="405" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Jiyu_gakuen_myonichikan-2.jpg" width="720"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Endo introduced Wright to his acquaintances Yoshikazu and Motoko Hani, who founded Jiyu Gakuen.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <img alt="jiyu gakuen frank lloyd wright" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-29711" height="405" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Jiyu_gakuen_myonichikan-3.jpg" width="720"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        However, ballooning costs for the Imperial Hotel project 
        <a href="http://online.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052748704026204575267433344670608" target="_blank">
         it climbed
        </a>
        to an extraordinary $3 million  put pressure on Wright. Its unclear whether he was fired or resigned (
        <a href="http://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%81%A0%E8%97%A4%E6%96%B0" target="_blank">
         wikipedia states
        </a>
        he was fired) but Wright left Japan and his primary protg Endo oversaw the completion. Endo also continued to oversee projects that Wright had begun, like Jiyu Gakuen, the Tokyo school for girls, and the only surviving Frank Lloyd Wright residence in Japan: the Yodoko Guest House.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29717">
        <img alt="yodokoo guest house frank lloyd wright" class="wp-image-29717 size-full" height="585" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/yodokoo-guest-house-1.jpg" width="780"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         The Yodok Guest House in Hyogo prefecture designed in 1918 and completed in 1924 | images courtesy flickr user
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/rangaku1976/" target="_blank">
          rangaku1976
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29718">
        <img alt="yodokoo guest house frank lloyd wright" class="wp-image-29718 size-full" height="581" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/yodokoo-guest-house-2.jpg" width="775"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         It was built as the summer villa for the well-to-do brewer of Sakura-Masamune sake, Tazaemon Yamamura.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29719">
        <img alt="yodokoo guest house frank lloyd wright" class="wp-image-29719 size-full" height="570" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/yodokoo-guest-house-3.jpg" width="747"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         In 1947, the house became the property of Yodogawa Steel Works, and was used as an official residence for the company president. It was opened to the public in 1989.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        As an architect and disciple of Wright, Endo can be easily criticized for never fully emerging from the shadow of Wrights hegemony. His most important architectural contribution is, arguably, the Koshien Hotel, which was heavily influenced by Wrights Imperial Hotel.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29716">
        <img alt="koshien hotel frank lloyd wright" class="wp-image-29716 size-full" height="375" src="http://www.spoon-tamago.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Koshien-Hotel.jpg" width="610"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         The Koshien Hotel in Hyogo opened in 1930. From 1944, it was used as an Imperial Navy hospital. It is now used as a hall forming part of Mukogawa Womens University, and is known as the Kshien Kaikan.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Currently there is an accelerating revival movement to rediscover the work of Endo. This fall, for the first time ever, the
        <a href="http://hhtrust.jp/" target="_blank">
         Heritage Houses Trust
        </a>
        is opening one of Endos private residences
        <a href="http://www.pen-online.jp/news/design/kajivilla_arata_endo/" target="_blank">
         for public display
        </a>
        . The Kaji Villa in Hayama was completed in 1928 and is significant in that Endo designed not only the home but the lighting and furniture as well. For a limited time only (October and November of 2014) the owner is allowing a public viewing.
       </p>
