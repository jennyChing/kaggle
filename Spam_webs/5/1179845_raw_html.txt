width: 100%;
padding-bottom: 10px;
margin-bottom: 20px;
}
             </style>
             <div class="post-boilerplate boilerplate-before" id="boilerplate_1734886">
              If youre not reaching, engaging, and monetizing customers on mobile, youre likely losing them to someone else. Register now for the
              <a href="http://events.venturebeat.com/event/mobilebeat2015/?utm_source=vb&amp;utm_medium=boilerplate&amp;utm_content=top-category&amp;utm_campaign=mobilebeat2015">
               8th annual MobileBeat
              </a>
              , July 13-14, where the best and brightest will be exploring the latest strategies and tactics in the mobile space.
              <hr/>
             </div>
             <p>
              Ad tech has grown into a jungle of acronyms. Today,
              <a href="http://www.adform.com" target="_blank">
               Adform
              </a>
              is adding one more, in the hope of actually making life simpler for publishers.
             </p>
             <p>
              Itsnew acronym is PPAS, which stands for Programmatic Publisher Ad Server. And to understand why this Copenhagen-based global company believes its new offering simplifies things and makes them better for digital publishers, lets first get the lay of the land.
             </p>
             <p>
              Digital advertising is, as one might expect, largely controlled by computerized processes where machines rapidly negotiate with machines.
             </p>
             <p>
              One group using these machines is, of course, the advertisers. They have
              <a href="http://digiday.com/platforms/wtf-demand-side-platform/" target="_blank">
               demand-side platforms (DSPs)
              </a>
              for bidding on available space in digital properties, like websites, mobile websites, or apps.
             </p>
             <p>
              Then there are the providers of the ad spaces  like website owners or app creators. Theyre often called publishers for short. They have
              <a href="http://digiday.com/platforms/wtf-supply-side-platform/" target="_blank">
               supply-side platforms (SSPs)
              </a>
              , since they have the supply of ad space that needs to be filled with ads.
             </p>
             <p>
              Both the publishers and the advertisers also have
              <a href="http://www.adopsinsider.com/ad-ops-basics/why-do-publishers-and-marketers-have-separate-ad-servers/" target="_blank">
               ad servers
              </a>
              . The advertisers manage ads across various sites and apps, and the publishers deal with various advertisers ads on their site or app.
             </p>
             <p>
              Between publishers and advertisers is the
              <a href="http://digiday.com/platforms/what-is-an-ad-exchange/" target="_blank">
               ad exchange,
              </a>
              a digital marketplace of ad impressions. There are also
              <a href="http://digiday.com/platforms/what-is-a-dmp-data-management-platform/" target="_blank">
               data management platforms (DMPs)
              </a>
              , which take in data like cookies, segment users into useful categories like those who have visited car company websites, and help target the delivery of the ads, like car ads.
             </p>
             <p>
              Higher-end publishers dont rely entirely on computerized  that is, programmatic  platforms to automatically sell their ads. Because they offer more desirable ad space, they sometimes also have sales people do direct deals for, say, big ads for an upcoming HBO series on the home page.
             </p>
             <p>
              Got that? Heres a nifty graphic from AdForm:
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1707105" style="max-width:800px;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Adform.png">
               <img alt="A simplified view of digital ad tech, with PPAS taking the role of the DMP, SSP, and private marketplace." class="wp-image-1707105 size-large" data-src="http://1u88jj3r4db2x4txp44yqfj1.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Adform-800x497.png" height="497" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" width="800"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="A simplified view of digital ad tech, with PPAS taking the role of the DMP, SSP, and private marketplace." class="wp-image-1707105 size-large" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Adform.png?resize=800%2C497"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Above: A simplified view of digital ad tech, with Adforms Publisher Suite with PPAS taking the role of the ad server, SSP, and DMP.
              </p>
              <div class="vb_image_source">
               <em>
                Image Credit:
                <a href="http://www.adform.com" target="_blank">
                 Adform
                </a>
               </em>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              Adforms new PPAS, then, is an ad server that can handle programmatic (computerized) inventory sales and can execute direct sales that salespeople have contracted. Integrated with a supply-side platform and a data management platform already available in Adforms existing Publisher Edge suite, the company claims its new PPAS ad server completes the picture, offering everything needed for ad management and serving on the publishers side.
             </p>
             <p>
              The integrated server is also designed to handle rich media and all kinds of ad formats, including
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/2015/04/16/sharethrough-launches-programmatic-native-ads-monitored-by-a-quality-score/">
               native ads
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1707704" style="max-width:800px;">
              <a href="http://venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Forecasting-updated2.png">
               <img alt="A screen from Adform's new PPAS" class="wp-image-1707704 size-large" data-src="http://1u88jj3r4db2x4txp44yqfj1.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Forecasting-updated2-800x430.png" height="430" src="//vbstatic.co/assets/img/logo-placeholder.png" width="800"/>
               <noscript>
                <img alt="A screen from Adform's new PPAS" class="wp-image-1707704 size-large" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/venturebeat.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Forecasting-updated2.png?resize=800%2C430"/>
               </noscript>
              </a>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Above: A screen from Adforms new PPAS
              </p>
              <div class="vb_image_source">
               <em>
                Image Credit:
                <a href="http://www.adform.com" target="_blank">
                 Adform
                </a>
               </em>
              </div>
             </div>
             <p>
              Adform chief marketing officer Martin Stockfleth Larsen described this all-in-one system as an inventory management platform, and pointed to its benefits.
             </p>
             <p>
              It reduces the complexity of managing and coordinating several systems, he told me, replacing as many as five different publisher-side systems with one log-on. It also eliminates discrepancies that can exist between different systems, like accounting for ad impressions.
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <p>
              VentureBeat is studying email marketing tools.
              <br/>
              <a href="http://insight.venturebeat.com/email-marketing">
               Chime in, and well share the data with you
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <hr/>
             <p>
              It is also designed to get the highest rates for ad space. In part, it does this by eliminating the waterfall model, where ad space not sold by a publisher in a direct sale might otherwise move to a programmatic platform and, if not sold there, might be offered on lower quality exchanges. The ad space, looking for an acceptable price, becomes less valuable at each level.
             </p>
             <p>
              Multiple publisher-side systems, he added, are also more expensive because each system requires a separate fee. And theres more control of the user data in the Publisher Edge suite, Larsen said, because the data management platform is now integrated and under the publishers control.
             </p>
             <p>
              Adforms
              <a href="http://blog.adform.com/products/message-from-our-ceo-adform-publisher-edge-is-here/" target="_blank">
               recently released Publisher Edge suite
              </a>
              , with the new PPAS, represents an expansion by the companyinto the publishers side. Previously, ithas been focused primarily on the advertisers side  except that it had offered a private marketplace for publishers through its DSP.
             </p>
             <p>
              Larsen says that the only other comparable all-in-one-system for publishers is
              <a href="http://www.google.com/doubleclick/publishers/welcome/" target="_blank">
               DoubleClick for Publishers
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              But, he pointed out, Adform is media agnostic and can work with any ad exchange because it does not own one, while
              <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rOVQSpXpCjI" target="_blank">
               Googles DoubleClick does
              </a>
              . Additionally, he said, DoubleClicks solution does not have an integrated data management platform.
             </p>
