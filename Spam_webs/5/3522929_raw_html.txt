      <h1 class="article-headline">
       He Sold Us Out: Activists Fuming Over Bob Fiorettis Come to Rahm Moment Endorsing Mayor Emanuel
      </h1>
      <p class="article-deck">
       The former progressive mayoral critic has come out in support of Rahm Emanuel, which some former allies see as a gross betrayal.
      </p>
      <span class="author">
       BY
       <a href="/community/profile/136010">
        Miles Kampf-Lassin
       </a>
      </span>

      <div class="content-body">
       <div id="article-inset">
        <p class="pullquote">
         Now that the runoff is a reality, Fioretti appears to have had his own Come to Rahm moment, reneging on his previous condemnations of a mayor he once described as having failed us by every measure.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Chicago has seen its share of odd political bedfellows this election season. The trend has been a typical (if cynical) one for this city: past opponents of the mayor falling in line, helping to ward off any potential challenge to the machine.
       </p>
       <p>
        In early February, Congressman Bobby Rushpreviously a vocal critic of Mayor Rahm Emanuel, who in July
        <a href="http://www.nbcchicago.com/blogs/ward-room/Bobby-Rush-Rahm-Emanuel-Karen-Lewis-mayor-Chicago-267507121.html">
         slammed
        </a>
        the mayors mass school closings while urging Chicago Teachers Union President Karen Lewis to mount a challengereversed course and
        <a href="http://abc7chicago.com/politics/rahm-emanuel-gets-unlikely-endorsement-for-re-election/510647/">
         endorsed
        </a>
        Emanuel.
       </p>
       <p>
        Illinois Sen. Mark Kirk, a prominent Republican, publicly voiced support for Emanuel in early March, with a dog-whistle warning that if the mayor isnt re-elected, Chicago could
        <a href="http://chicago.suntimes.com/chicago-politics/7/71/407760/sen-kirk-re-elect-rahm-chicago-end-like-detroit">
         end up like Detroit
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        And over 70 union locals have come out
        <a href="http://www.chicagotogether.org/blog/campaign-updates/press-release-70-local-unions-endorse-mayor-rahm-emanuels-re-election/">
         in support
        </a>
        of the mayor, despite his
        <a href="http://inthesetimes.com/article/17548/rahm_emanuel_unions">
         well-established
        </a>
        anti-union record. UNITE HERE has even
        <a href="http://inthesetimes.com/working/entry/17783/unite_heres_new_pro_rahm_emanuel_ads_ooze_rahm_love_for_mayor_1">
         funded an ad campaign
        </a>
        in which their members credit Rahm Emanuel and his Rahm Love for victories on the job, rather than their own dedicated work.
       </p>
       <p>
        But perhaps the most striking case of political treachery in this election came on Saturday night, when former progressive alderman and mayoral candidate Bob Fioretti made a perfect about-face and
        <a href="http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/chi-fioretti-endorses-emanuel-20150329-story.html#page=1">
         endorsed Rahm Emanuel
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Fioretti had been one of Emanuels fiercest critics, both in the City Council and the mayoral race, before he was eliminated in the February 24 election. He failed to capture enough votes to challenge the mayor in a runoff, and lost his aldermanic seat due to redistricting. On the eve of that vote, Fioretti
        <a href="http://wgntv.com/2015/03/29/alderman-bob-fioretti-endorsing-rahm-emanuel-for-mayor/">
         stated
        </a>
        that he would support anybody but Rahm in a runoff election.
       </p>
       <p>
        Now that the runoff is a reality, however, Fioretti appears to have had his own Come to Rahm moment, reneging on his previous condemnations of a mayor he
        <a href="http://chicago.cbslocal.com/2014/09/13/its-official-ald-fioretti-to-challenge-mayor-rahm-emanuel/">
         once described
        </a>
        as having failed us by every measure.
       </p>
       <p>
        On issue after issue in the campaign, Fioretti pulled no punches when going after Emanuel, from school and mental health clinic closings togiveaways of taxpayer money to large corporations, red light cameras, and the horrid plague of shootings facing the city.
       </p>
       <p>
        But now, just over a week away from the runoff election, Fioretti apparently has concluded that all of these criticisms should be sidelined because Emanuel has a fiscal plan for the citya plan Fioretti
        <a href="http://www.progressillinois.com/posts/content/2014/10/15/emanuel-delivers-2015-budget-address-fioretti-slams-mayor-election-year-flu">
         not so long ago said
        </a>
        doesnt solve the problem and shortchanges Chicagos long-term future.
       </p>
       <p>
        At a benefit concert for Rahm Emanuels progressive challenger Jesus Chuy Garcia on Sunday night, Grammy-award winning rap artistand former 20th Ward aldermanic candidateChe Rhymefest Smith claimed that Fioretti had no integrity after endorsing the mayor.
       </p>
       <p>
        Smith also presented a red-light ticket on stage, blasting Emanuel for unfairly raising revenue on the backs of low-income city residents, before ripping it up into shreds in front of the crowd to loud applause. Garcia has vowed to take down the citys red-light cameras on his first day in office as mayor.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" src="http://inthesetimes.com/images/uploads/IMG_4603.JPG" style="height:450px; width:600px"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Rhymefest's torn-up red light ticket at Sunday night's Chuy Garcia concert. (Micah Uetricht)
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        The charge that Fioretti has now lost any claim to integrity has been echoed across the city. On Monday, the
        <em>
         Chicago Reader
        </em>
        published a
        <a href="http://www.chicagoreader.com/Bleader/archives/2015/03/30/corrections-and-clarifications-from-bob-fioretti">
         damning satirical account
        </a>
        of Fiorettis rationale behind the endorsement, highlighting his past criticisms of the mayor. David Schaffer, a Garcia adviser,
        <a href="http://www.chicagomag.com/Chicago-Magazine/Felsenthal-Files/March-2015/Chuy-Debate-Prep/">
         tells
         <em>
          Chicago
         </em>
         magazine
        </a>
        that hes not sure the endorsement means much because I dont think Bob has much credibility left.
       </p>
       <p>
        Former mayoral candidate Amara Enyiawho herself endorsed Fioretti after dropping out of the race in Decembertells
        <em>
         In These Times
        </em>
        that she wasnt expecting the endorsement of Emanuel.
       </p>
       <p>
        Because of his record, it was surprising to me, Enyia says. It was definitely a shift in direction. But Im sure he accepts the consequences and did his own set of analyses.
       </p>
       <p>
        It seems that one of those consequences could be paying down Fiorettis massive campaign debt, with the help of Emanuel and his large donor base.
       </p>
       <p>
        When asked at a joint-press conference about the likelihood that the endorsement would result in financial help for Fiorettis beleaguered campaign fund, both he and Emanuel indicated that it was
        <a href="http://www.chicagonow.com/public-affairs-with-jeff-berkowitz/2015/03/ald-anybody-but-rahm-fioretti-endorses-rahm-emanuel-and-denies-a-quid-pro-quo-for-his-endorsement/">
         in the cards
        </a>
        . Asked if he would approach his donors to help retire Fiorettis campaign debt, Emanuel responded, Of course. I dont have a problem with that.
       </p>
       <p>
        On Sunday, members of Chicagos Mental Health Movement crashed a lunch between Emanuel and Fioretti, in an action attempting to
        <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4qpa61bJhvQ">
         Buy Back Bob
        </a>
        by raising money to pay off his campaign debt in order to compete with Emanuels vast warchestand buy back his friendship with the mental health community.
       </p>
       <p>
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/4qpa61bJhvQ" width="560">
        </iframe>
       </p>
       <p>
        Fioretti had previously spoken out strongly against Emanuels closure of half of the citys mental health clinics, and advocates are furious over what they see as Fiorettis spurning of them by coming out in support of Emanuel.
       </p>
       <p>
        He sold us out, says NDana Carter, a Mental Health Movement activist. He has been courting us for two years, guaranteeing us he would work to get the clinics open, even before he ran for mayor. And now he turns on us.
       </p>
       <p>
        Of the Buy Back Bob campaign, Carter explains, we are trying to pay him to have him come back from the dark side. Rahm Emanuel wants to close the clinics, which means Fioretti is now dirty. We can never trust anything he says. He has turned on the people.
       </p>
       <p>
        We were crushed when we found out. Right at this moment we have a person here that is homeless based on what Rahm Emanuel did. We are tired of this, she says.
       </p>
       <p>
        Neither Emanuel nor Fioretti ruled out any political appointments or favors as a result of the endorsement. Now that Fioretti is no longer an alderman, he could be looking for any kind of help he can get in resurrecting his political life.
       </p>
       <p>
        But after years of denouncing Rahm Emanuels policies, and being one of the few dissenting, progressive voices in the City Councilto turn on a dime and throw his support behind a widely-despised mayor just ahead of a historic election is likely not a good look.
       </p>
       <p>
        Unless, of course, Fioretti is just now showing his true political colors.
       </p>
