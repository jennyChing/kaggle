        <div class="page-title post-title">
         <h1>
          Treating dry skin on your feet
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="post-view">
         <p class="post-date when">
          This entry was posted on March 11, 2015
          <span class="by-author">
           by Simply Feet
          </span>
          .
         </p>
         <div class="post-entry entry std">
          <p>
           The skin on the feet can be much thicker than the skin anywhere else on the body.
           <!--more-->
           Pressure and weight-bearing lead to a thickening of the skin, and it can often become dry, uncomfortable and unsightly. To ensure that your feet are ready for the spring and summer, you need to start caring for them now. Fortunately, it is straightforward and easy to get your feet into top condition. Follow our tips to treating dry skin on your feet.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.simplyfeet.co.uk/index.php/news/treating-dry-skin-on-your-feet/">
            <img alt="Treating dry skin on your feet" class="aligncenter wp-image-828" height="240" src="http://www.simplyfeet.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Treating-dry-skin-on-feet-200x300.jpg" title="Treating dry skin on your feet" width="160"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            What causes dry feet?
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           Unlike the skin on most other parts of the body, the skin on the soles of the feet does not contain sebaceous glands, so the feet are not naturally moisturised. Hot baths can contribute to the dryness, as can cold weather. As you get older, dry foot skin becomes more common, and you may also experience dry cracked feet, especially if you spend long periods in the sun or in a warm environment. It is also important that the atmosphere in your home or where you work is not too dry, as this can also contribute to a lack of moisture in your feet.
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            How to treat dry feet
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           Dry feet can be treated at home by using an effective skin-care regime. It can take a couple of days, but you will soon have soft, silky skin on your feet.
          </p>
          <p>
           Initially, you need to remove some of the dead thick skin on the feet. This is easily done by using a
           <a href="http://www.simplyfeet.co.uk/index.php/footcare/skincare/foot-files/heavy-duty-foot-file-white.html" target="_blank">
            heavy-duty foot file.
           </a>
           There are different grades of grit on each side, so start off with the coarser side and then smooth with the finer-grit side.
          </p>
          <p>
           When you have removed the dead skin, moisturise the feet with a good moisturiser.
           <a href="http://www.simplyfeet.co.uk/index.php/footcare/skincare/dry-or-chapped-skin/simply-feet-10-urea-cream.html" target="_blank">
            Simply Feet 10% Urea Cream
           </a>
           is ideal for this, as it contains natural ingredients that help to rehydrate dry skin. If you want to treat your feet for a special occasion, you can use Gel Pamper Socks overnight to soften and moisturise them.
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            Preventing dry feet
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
           </strong>
           Continue to keep your feet soft and moisturised with these tips:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Avoid using highly perfumed soaps as these may irritate and dry the feet.
           </li>
           <li>
            Choose shoes made of natural materials that will allow your feet to breathe.
           </li>
           <li>
            Moisturise your feet daily and after bathing or showering.
           </li>
           <li>
            Keep the temperature in your home moderate to avoid a drying atmosphere.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
