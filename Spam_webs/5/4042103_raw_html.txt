        <header>
         <h1 class="title entry-title">
          La Traviata at Virginia Opera
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="small">
          By
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://dcmetrotheaterarts.com/author/tiffany-draut/" rel="author" title="Posts by Tiffany Draut">
            Tiffany Draut
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="small">
          on
         </span>
         <abbr class="date time published updated" title="2015-03-22T04:40:14-0400">
          March 22, 2015
         </abbr>
        </div>
        <!--Test Comment !! -->
        <section class="entry">
         <div id="fb-root">
         </div>
         <div class="pf-content">
          <div class="printfriendly pf-aligncenter">
           <a class="noslimstat" href="http://www.printfriendly.com/print?url=http%3A%2F%2Fdcmetrotheaterarts.com%2F2015%2F03%2F22%2Fla-traviata-at-virginia-opera%2F" onclick="window.print(); return false;" rel="nofollow">
            <img alt="Print Friendly" src="http://cdn.printfriendly.com/button-print-blu20.png" style="border:none;-webkit-box-shadow:none; box-shadow:none;"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <p>
           In a fitting capstone to Virginia Operas 40thanniversary season, the Virginia Opera, in conjunction with Des Moines Metro Opera, presents a thrilling production of Verdis masterpiece
           <em>
            La Traviata
           </em>
           . Playing through today  March 22, 2015 at 2 PM, at the George Mason University Center for the Arts, this production is not to be missed, primarily for the thrilling performances of Cecilia Violetta Lopez as Violetta, and Rolando Sanz asAlfredo Germont.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_173584" style="width: 470px">
           <a href="http://g1j1g1up91ni2kf7q4x2f132.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1890979_10153711106283135_741811563480500657_n2.jpg">
            <img alt="Rolando Sanz and Cecilia Violetta Lopez, Photo by Lucid Frame Productions. " class="size-large wp-image-173584" height="452" src="http://g1j1g1up91ni2kf7q4x2f132.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1890979_10153711106283135_741811563480500657_n2-460x452.jpg" width="460"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Rolando Sanz and Cecilia Violetta Lopez, Photo by Lucid Frame Productions.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           <em>
            La Traviata
           </em>
           tells the story of the courtesan Violetta and her tragic, tumultuous relationship with the nobleman Alfredo, as she is dying from consumption. Set in Paris in the 1850s, the opera is brought alive with elaborate sets that fill the stage, depicting balls and even a country cottage, courtesy of Set Designer Robert Little. Complementing the sets was Lighting Designer Bradley Kings expressive lighting: the scenes when Alfredo and Violetta are happier are lit brighter and warmer, while such scenes as the opening ball with Violetta and her socialite friends, are lit much more sterilely, underscoring Violettas disconnect from those people and their hollow way of life.
          </p>
          <p>
           The ornate, beautiful costumes, as well as the intricate wigs (Wig and Makeup Designer James P. McGough), also really helped set the scenes and draw the audience in. The contrast between the ostentation of the wigs and costumes in the first acts made the simplicity of the third act even starker, helping to convince the audience that Violetta truly was wasting away and dying.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_173585" style="width: 173px">
           <a href="http://g1j1g1up91ni2kf7q4x2f132.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/10998821_10153705638653135_3836929242757820173_n.jpg">
            <img alt="Cecilia Violetta Lpez as Violetta. Photo credit Lucid Frame Productions." class="size-medium wp-image-173585" height="300" src="http://g1j1g1up91ni2kf7q4x2f132.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/10998821_10153705638653135_3836929242757820173_n-163x300.jpg" width="163"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Cecilia Violetta Lpez as Violetta. Photo credit Lucid Frame Productions.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           I adored Cecilia Violetta Lope performance as Violetta. Her strong, intense voice and her soaring high notes perfectly expressed the range of Violettas emotions, from her anguish in the separation from her lover to the joy of being reunited. The vocal pyrotechnics she displayed in her arias at the end of Act I,
           <em>
            Ah forse lui
           </em>
           and
           <em>
            Sempre libera
           </em>
           , were breathtaking, as she leaped from low to high notes and trills and all sorts in between. Yet at no time was the sincerity and integrity of the character sacrificed to vocal prowess: Lopezs powerful acting and her sheer charisma and stage presence made it so that the audience felt truly connected to Violetta and her journey.
          </p>
          <p>
           The rest of the cast also sang beautifully, exhibiting great enthusiasm and strong, clear singing. Rolando Sanz as Violettas lover Alfredo Germont was superb and his thrilling tenor voice filled the auditorium during the rendition of
           <em>
            Libiamo ne lieti calici.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Other fine vocals were displayed by Malcolm MacKenzie (Georgio Germont, Alfredos father), Courtney Miller (Flora Bervoix), and Andre Chiang, as Baron Douphol.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_173562" style="width: 470px">
           <a href="http://g1j1g1up91ni2kf7q4x2f132.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/11079657_10153722974653135_5531835796869260039_n.jpg">
            <img alt="Cecilia Violetta Lpez as Violetta and Malcolm MacKenzie as Giorgio Germont. Photo courtesy Virginia Opera." class="size-large wp-image-173562" height="305" src="http://g1j1g1up91ni2kf7q4x2f132.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/11079657_10153722974653135_5531835796869260039_n-460x305.jpg" width="460"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Cecilia Violetta Lpez (Violetta) and Malcolm MacKenzie (Giorgio Germont). Photo courtesy of Virginia Opera.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           The Richmond Symphony under the direction of Andrew Bisantz played the score so beautifully.
           <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
            Their rendering of Verdis music expressive, complementing the singers in perfect harmony. It is always a delight to hear these grand operas performed by a skilled, full orchestra. Such complementation was at fine form in the famous drinking song from Act 1,
           </span>
           <em style="line-height: 1.5;">
            Libiamo ne lieti calici
           </em>
           <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
            . Many in the audience were dancing in their seats, along with the actors on stage!
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           While
           <em>
            La Traviata
           </em>
           is certainly a familiar opera for most, the excellence of this production, especially the outstanding Cecilia Violetta Lopez as Violetta, and Rolando Sanz as Georgio Germont, makes it a perfect choice to close out the 40thanniversary season of Virginia Opera.
          </p>
          <p>
           Running Time: Three hours, including one-15 minute intermission.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://g1j1g1up91ni2kf7q4x2f132.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/10994051_10153657615498135_5811421454842097953_n1.jpg">
            <img alt="10994051_10153657615498135_5811421454842097953_n" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-173583" height="170" src="http://g1j1g1up91ni2kf7q4x2f132.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/10994051_10153657615498135_5811421454842097953_n1-460x170.jpg" width="460"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #0000ff;">
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.vaopera.org/season-tickets/2014-15-season/la-traviata.html" style="color: #0000ff;">
