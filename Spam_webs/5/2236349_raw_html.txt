     <h2 style="">
      Win the Weight Loss Mind Game
     </h2>
     <h3 style="">
      Setting Goals and Rewarding Yourself
     </h3>
     <h2 style="">
      <span style=" ">
       Set Your Weight Loss Goals
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       If youve ever traveled, youll know that a road map is important. That concept of a roadmap is crucial for your weight loss plan as well. When you set goals, you create a picture of where you are, where you want to go and how youre going to get there. The key is to set realistically attainable goals in your weight loss journey.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       An attainable goal is important for your success. If the goal you set is not realistic then youll wind up failing and wondering why you couldnt lose 50 pounds in a month. Keep in mind the following about goal setting:
      </span>
      <strong style=" ">
      </strong>
     </p>
     <ul style="">
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Goals should have three components in order to guarantee your success. They should be attainable,
        <div class="articleImage articleImageRight" style=" width: 300px; max-width: 47.46835443038%;">
         <a href="/Media/Measurable_Goals" style="">
          <img alt="Measurable Goals" src="http://media.infobarrel.com/media/image/77225_max.jpg" style="float: right; width: 265px;" width="265"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        measurable and specific.
       </span>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul style="">
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        People run the risk of failing at weight loss when their goals lack one of the above characteristics. An example of a weight loss goal that is attainable, measurable and specific is to set the goal of losing 1-2 lbs a week. This is a realistic and healthy amount of weight to lose in that time period. It is specific, measurable and attainable.
       </span>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <div class="adSkyscraper">
      <!-- IBArticle-Skyscraper -->
      <div id="div-gpt-ad-2" style="width:160px; height:600px;">
       <script type="text/javascript">
        googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-2');
       </script>
      </div>
     </div>
     <ul skyscraper="1" style="">
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Keep your weight loss goals focused on the short term. While you might like to lose 50 pounds in all, set goals to lose the first five or ten lbs is a lot more realistic especially if you plan to reward yourself for meeting your goals. Even more realistic than basing your weight loss goals on specific numbers is to set behavior based goals.
       </span>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <ul style="">
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        An example of a behavior based goal is to set the goal to drink 8 glasses of water each day in a week. It is an attainable goal, requires specific action and is measurable. Weight loss, itself, depends on all kinds of factors, some of them out of your reach, but you totally control your own behavior.
       </span>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       When you use goal setting properly, they can be powerful motivators in your weight loss plan. For example, when you set a goal for eating a certain number of fruits and veggies each day, record in a journal how much you do eat and whether youre meeting that goal. You will be achieving the goal by doing it and writing it down while at the same time encouraging yourself to continue. And continuing to do so will inevitably lead to weight loss and increased health.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       You can use goal setting to stay on track. Monitor your goals so that they do not become too broad or ambitious (and end up being unrealistic). Realistic goals will help you stay focused.
      </span>
     </p>
     <h2 style="">
      <span style=" ">
       Reward Yourself When Meeting Weight Loss Goals
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       Sometimes dieting can feel like a chore especially if youre waiting for that big day when you reach your goal weight and its taking a while to become a reality. You can motivate yourself to continue with the long haul if you reward yourself along the way. Here are some weight loss land marks that can be rewarded:
      </span>
     </p>
     <p style="">
     </p>
     <ul style="">
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Every five lbs lost
       </span>
       <div class="articleImage articleImageRight" style=" width: 303px; max-width: 47.943037974684%;">
        <a href="/Media/Win_the_Weight_Loss_Game_76775" style="">
         <img alt="Win the Weight Loss Game(76775)" src="http://media.infobarrel.com/media/image/76775_max.jpg" style="float: right;"/>
        </a>
       </div>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Bigger weight loss goals like 20 or 50 lbs gone
       </span>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Drinking that daily water over two weeks or a month
       </span>
       <strong style=" ">
       </strong>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Exercise goals like working out a certain number of times in a week or a month
       </span>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Lowering your medication dosage due to improved health from weight loss
       </span>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Improved blood test results (like your overall cholesterol dropping under 200, for example)
       </span>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p style="">
     </p>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       When choosing rewards to celebrate a mile stone, do not use food as a reward since you want to undo any associations of food and celebration in your weight loss plan and your rewards system.
       <strong style="">
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       Reward yourself in ways that are good for your mind and body to keep you motivated. Some examples of these kinds of rewards:
      </span>
     </p>
     <ul style="">
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        A manicure or pedicure
       </span>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        A full body or foot massage
       </span>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        A new outfit in your new, smaller size
       </span>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        A movie or a new good book
       </span>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        A new piece of jewelry or handbag (theyll always fit you!)
       </span>
      </li>
      <li style="">
       <span style=" ">
        Take a mini-vacation or staycation
       </span>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       Embarking on a weight loss plan is usually a long term project for most folks. Setting short term goals can keep one motivated during the long journey. It feels great to accomplish a goal, no matter if its small or big, and these victories can go a long way in terms of keeping you motivated to continue with your plan.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p style="">
      <span style=" ">
       When you meet your weight loss goal, think of how you can reward yourself for doing so. Celebrate those small accomplishments especially since they will keep you positive about working towards your end goal number of overall weight loss.
       <strong style="">
       </strong>
      </span>
     </p>
