                <h4>
                 Spinal Adjustment: A Simple Fix for the Complex Spine
                </h4>
                <i>
                 Chiropractors work largely by making adjustments to the spinal column. Learn why this can offer relief with Groupon's look at this treatment technique.
                </i>
                To anyone whos ever had to stare down a wall of heating pads, braces, pain relievers, balms, and ointments at the drugstore, it should come as no surprise that
                <a href="http://nccam.nih.gov/health/pain/spinemanipulation.htm">
                 roughly 80% of the population experiences back pain at some point
                </a>
                . And its easy to understand why: the vertebral column comprises dozens of bones (more than 15% of all bones in an adult body), all of which work in concert to protect the thick bundle of nerves running the length of the spine. During spinal manipulation, chiropractors perform a series of swift motions that realign all those intricate moving partsusually with their hands but sometimes with a small device to help wrangle bones and soft tissue.  

The spines organizational complexity calls into question the idea that someone can simply crack your back and fix it, but a wide range of studies have shown that over time there can be spinal benefits to having a trained professional make minor physical adjustments. Some studies have found that spinal manipulation can alleviate lower-back pain as effectively as pain-relief medications and other conventional treatments. The therapy also doesnt carry some of the side effects of daily medication, and a chiropractor, osteopath, or physical therapist can customize treatments to target specific regions of tension and soreness.
