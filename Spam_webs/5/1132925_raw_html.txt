          <h1 class="title single-title">
           Environmentally Friendly, but are Electric Vehicles Cost-Effective?
          </h1>
          <div class="post-info">
           <span class="theauthor">
            by
            <a href="http://nvate.com/author/beatty/" rel="nofollow" title="Posts by beatty">

          <p>
           <em>
            By Bobby Miller
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Worldwide, motor vehicles currently emit well over 900 million metric tons of [carbon dioxide] each year, according to the World Resources Institute. These emissions account for more than 15 percent of global fossil fuel CO2 releases. This obviously has devastating consequences on the environment. It is no wonder, then, that many environmentally conscious people are eager to transition to electric-powered vehicles. Unlike the standard internal combustion engine, an electric cars battery doesnt pollute the air as its used.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_9749" style="width: 410px">
           <a href="http://nvate.com/?attachment_id=9749" rel="nofollow">
            <img alt="Nvate Electric Cars Ford Focus Nissan Leaf Chevy Volt Toyota Prius electric car battery foreign oil" class="size-full wp-image-9749" height="283" src="http://nvate.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/ID-10050694.jpg" title="Nvate Electric Cars Ford Focus Nissan Leaf Chevy Volt Toyota Prius electric car battery foreign oil" width="400"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            Credit: freedigitalphotos.net
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Some critics note that, to charge an electric vehicles battery, youre probably plugging into a coal-powered electric grid. Still, according to SolarChargedDriving.com, studies have shown that CO2 emissions are lower when plugging into a power companys energyeven if its 100 percent coal-poweredas opposed to using an internal combustion engine. And power companies in the United States do not rely entirely on coal.
          </p>
          <p>
           Even in states such as West Virginia, Indiana and Wyoming, which rely heavily on coal, about 17 percent of the electricity comes from cleaner sources. In states such as Idaho, Oregon and Washington, which use more renewable energy sources, an electric vehicle is helping the environment even more. These states receive less than 6 percent of their electricity through burning coal, as SolarChargedDriving.com reported. So, even if youre not plugging an electric vehicle into a solar panel or other entirely clean energy source, its still beneficial to the environment.
          </p>
          <p>
           Plus, by reducing our dependence on gasoline, electric vehicles also reduce the United States dependence on foreign oil. Nearly all of the electricity generated in America uses domestic fuel sources, Ted Craver, the chairman and CEO of public utility company Edison International told GreenBiz.com.
          </p>
          <p>
           While electric vehicles clearly benefit the environment and the United States oil situation, are they affordable to the average consumer?
          </p>
          <h3>
           Can You Overcome the Initial Costs?
          </h3>
          <p>
           Its no secret that, in order to buy an electric vehicle, you have to throw down a lot of money upfront. The Chevy Volt, for instance, will cost you at least $39,995, according to the Washington Post. And the well-advertised Nissan Leaf is priced around $36,050. Even with the federal tax credit of $7,500, the cars still cost about $30,000 each. State tax credits may alleviate this pain to some extent, however. A hybrid like the Toyota Prius is significantly more affordable at $24,000, according to the official Toyota website, but even this is more costly than the average car.
          </p>
          <p>
           To put things in perspective, lets compare the gasoline-powered Ford Focus to the electric-powered one. The traditional model has a base price of $16,200, as listed on Yahoo Autos. According to PlugInCars.com, the electric version has a starting cost of $39,995, which falls to $32,495 when the $7,500 federal tax credit is taken into account. So, the electric model costs roughly twice as much as the standard version.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_9737" style="width: 440px">
           <a href="http://nvate.com/?attachment_id=9737" rel="nofollow">
            <img alt="Nvate Electric Cars Ford Focus Nissan Leaf Chevy Volt Toyota Prius electric car battery foreign oil" class=" wp-image-9737 " height="323" src="http://nvate.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/fordfocuselectric.jpg" title="Nvate Electric Cars Ford Focus Nissan Leaf Chevy Volt Toyota Prius electric car battery foreign oil" width="430"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            The electric model of the Ford Focus.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           However, as fans of electric vehicles are quick to point out, you dont have to empty your wallet at the gas pump anymore. How long, though, does it take to break even in terms of financial cost? We must remember that plugging in an electric vehicle isnt free because somebody has to pay for that electricity. According to Scientific American, a 2007 study by the non-profit Electric Power Research Institute found that plugging in an electric vehicle would cost the equivalent of roughly 75 cents per gallon of gasolinea price not seen at the pump for 30 years. While thats far lower than the average gas price, it still adds to the burden of breaking even.
          </p>
          <p>
           According to the Washington Post, the Energy Information Administration has found that, in order to recoup the initial costs of buying an electric vehicle, gas prices would have to be above $6 per gallon, nearly double the prices today. And even then, you would have to drive for a while to hit the break-even point.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Battery Costs
          </h3>
          <p>
           We must also take into account the fact that batteries in electric vehicles dont last forevereventually, they will have to be replaced. Thankfully, according to CarsDirect.com, an electric car battery can last over 100,000 miles, after which it will lose some of its recharging capacity. And most batteries come with a 10 year or mileage limit warranty in order to protect buyers.
          </p>
          <p>
           After the warranty has expired, however, theres little incentive to buy a new battery. According to the Boston Consulting Group, a 24 kilowatt-hour battery pack is estimated to cost $15,600. This is practically the price of a new gasoline-powered vehicle, and it is roughly half the cost of a new electric vehicle.
          </p>
          <p>
           Since so much of the cost behind an electric vehicle is in its battery, this should mean that, when battery prices decline significantly, so will the prices of electric vehicles. Experts are currently debating whether or not we can expect the costs to fall.
          </p>
          <p>
           Some optimistic experts believe battery costs may be cut in half during the next few years. As the production of these vehicles increases, so will the efficiency of making them. U.S. Energy Secretary Steven Chu hopes that an electric car battery will soon cost as much as a standard car battery. Hopefully, by the end of this decade, were going to be at parity, Chu told GreenBiz.com.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Washington Post reported that analysts from McKinsey &amp; Company believe that the price for lithium-ion batteries could fall by as much as two-thirds by 2020. So while the projections vary, many believe that we may have affordable electric cars in the not-too-distant future.
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_9738" style="width: 394px">
           <a href="http://nvate.com/?attachment_id=9738" rel="nofollow">
            <img alt="Nvate Electric Cars Ford Focus Nissan Leaf Chevy Volt Toyota Prius electric car battery foreign oil" class=" wp-image-9738 " height="231" src="http://nvate.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/lg_chem.jpg" title="Nvate Electric Cars Ford Focus Nissan Leaf Chevy Volt Toyota Prius electric car battery foreign oil" width="384"/>
           </a>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            An LG Chem plant devoted to the production of lithium-ion batteries for electric cars.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p>
           Other experts arent so confident. According to the Wall Street Journal, more than 30 percent of the cost of the batteries comes from metals such as nickel, manganese and cobalt [since] lithium makes up only a small portion of the metals in the batteries. There is no evidence to suggest that the prices of these materials will fall with increasing battery production. In fact, the costs may increase alongside demand, as a study by the Academies of Science has speculated.
          </p>
          <p>
           It is clear that the cost of electric cars will remain a significant issue in years to come. However, it is important to remember that, even if you believe electric cars are out of your price range, there are still many other ways to curb CO2 emissions.
          </p>
          <p>
           Stephen Rees, owner of a transportation blog, http://www.StephenRees.WordPress.com, said, On the whole I think the problem in both Canada and the [United States] is the over dependence on private cars and the general inadequacies of public transportation and human powered modes (walking and cycling) rather than how [cars] are powered. Public transportation and walking are options to consider too, as well as fuel-efficient, gasoline-powered vehicles.
          </p>
          <p>
           For some people, though, the environmental benefits of driving an electric vehicle make the high price worth it. Hopefully, in years to come, these prices will come down so that we see more electric vehicles on the road.
          </p>
