      <h2 class="post-title">
       Hello Dolly Bars Recipe
      </h2>
      <div class="postmeta">
       <em>
        posted by
       </em>
       Ali
       <em>
        on
       </em>
       April 11, 2015 //
       <a href="http://momspark.net/hello-dolly-bars-recipe/#respond">

      <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
      <p>
       <img alt="Hello Dolly Bars Recipe" class="aligncenter wp-image-48392 size-full" height="900" src="http://momspark.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/dolly-bars-1-copy.jpg" title="Hello Dolly Bars Recipe" width="600"/>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Hello Dolly Bars, often referred to as Magic Bars, are a classic bar dessert that many home bakers have made in some form or another. The appeal to these tasty bars is that they contain a bit of everything good. Chocolate, butterscotch, coconut and nuts over a graham cracker crumb crust.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Be prepared to give some awayto your friends and family as these are addictive and hard to stop eating. Enjoy!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <span style="color: #444444;">
        <div class="recipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <meta content="http://momspark.net/hello-dolly-bars-recipe/" itemprop="url"/>
         <img align="right" class="photo nopin" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://momspark.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/dolly-bars-1-copy.jpg"/>
         <div class="printbutton">
          <a href="http://momspark.net/hello-dolly-bars-recipe/print/" title="Print Recipe">
           Print Recipe
          </a>
         </div>
         <h2 itemprop="name">
          Hello Dolly Bars Recipe
         </h2>
         <div class="time">
          <p>
           <strong>
            Yield:
           </strong>
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            24 bars
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Prep Time:
           </strong>
           <meta content="PT10M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
           10 minutes
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Cook Time:
           </strong>
           <meta content="PT25-30M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
           25-30 minutes
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Total Time:
           </strong>
           <meta content="PT35-40M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
           35-40 minutes
          </p>
         </div>
         <h3 id="ingredients">
          Ingredients:
         </h3>
         <div class="ingredients">
          <p>
           1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
           <br/>
           1/2 cup butter, melted
           <br/>
           1 14 ounce can sweetened condensed milk
           <br/>
           1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
           <br/>
           1 cup butterscotch chips
           <br/>
           1 1/3 cups sweetened flaked coconut
           <br/>
           1 cup finely chopped nuts (walnuts or pecans traditionally)
          </p>
         </div>
         <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
          Directions:
         </h3>
         <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          <p>
           1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Line a 13 x 9 inch baking sheet with parchment paper.
          </p>
          <p>
           2. In a small bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs and butter until all the crumbs are moist. Press the mixture into the baking sheet being sure the bottom is covered and the layer is even. Pour the condensed milk evenly over the graham cracker mixture.
          </p>
          <p>
           3. Sprinkle the chocolate chips on top of the condensed milk and then sprinkle the butterscotch chips evenly over the chocolate. Top with the coconut and nuts.
          </p>
          <p>
           4. Bake in a 350 degree oven for 25  30 minutes or until golden brown. Cool completely before slicing. Store in a airtight container.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </span>
      </p>
