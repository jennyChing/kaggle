            <h3>
             Always have people smarter than yourself around you.
            </h3>
            <p>
             <strong>
              If you are the smartest person in the room then you are in the wrong room!
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             In an online environment, I try to network in smaller more exclusive settings where people have to greatly contribute or pay to be involved. It weeds out the tire kickers.
            </p>
            <p>
             What that means is: joining exclusive groups, mastermind Skype chats, paid online forums, etc.
            </p>
            <p>
             I also generally stay away from free, public online forums or discussion groups. The exception would be if they require some sort of exchange or commitment.
            </p>
            <p>
             The reason why is simple, but one that most people dont understand.
            </p>
            <!--Ad Injection:random-->
            <div style="float:right;margin-left:5px;">
             <a href="http://www.startupguild.org" target="_blank">
              <img src="//www.lionsbrain.com/images/SUG3.png" width="300"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <p>
             Its very difficult to weed through huge massive communities to find the diamond in the rough. In a public, free setting it can be very difficult to tell who the smarterpeopleare. And if the free community is huge and you can figure out who the real stars are, then they are probably getting bombarded and dont have time to help you much.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              If you are the smartest person in the room then you are in the wrong room!
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             This is an age old saying that is extremely true yet commonly overlooked. Its easy for our egostothink that we are the smartest person in the world. Im not saying that you arent smart, but if you think you are the smartest person in the world in every single topic then you are sadly mistaken.
            </p>
            <p>
             The human lifespan isnt enough time for us to be become the smartest and best in every single topic and field. Its just not possible. You cant be the worlds best martial artist, scientist, race car driver, football player, basketball player, singer, dancer, entrepreneur, skater, novelist, mathematician, spelling bee champion, pulitzer prize winner,and oscar winning film-maker all at the same time. Its just not going to happen. Theres not enough time to be the best and be a true master of all of those things in a single lifetime.
            </p>
            <h3>
             How to surround yourself with smarter people
            </h3>
            <p>
             What you can do is surround yourself with people that are smarter than you the fields that you want to become better.
            </p>
            <p>
             Lets say you are an entrepreneur. What a smart entrepreneur will do is realize his or her weakness and find people to network with and maybe even hire that are better than them in the given field.
            </p>
            <p>
             For example, lets say Im starting an online restaurant review business. Im not an expert in restaurant entrepreneurship, nor an expert in the restaurant review business. However, what I would do is find people that are smarter than me in those fields. And, if I had the budget I would hire them, if I didnt have the budget I would find ways to network with them and exchange services. Maybe I could teach them something and they could teach me something. Or maybe I could hire them as a consultant, buy their course or pay for a phone call.
            </p>
            <p>
             This is what has led me to finding exclusive organizations, groups, paid online forums etc to join. The reason I stick with the premium, exclusive natured groups is that I find that there are a lot of tire kickers in the public sector and its hard to do any beneficial networking with people smarter than me.
            </p>
            <p>
             You also get a lot of negativity in public forums. People like to complain about their failures instead of doing something about it.
            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              The paid, exclusive forums have people that are in the Ready Mindset
             </b>
            </p>
            <p>
             The big benefit to me in joining exclusive, paid groups and communities is that people are putting up money and therefore are ready to go. They dont want to waste their investment and are willing to take the necessary action steps in their business or career, in most cases.
            </p>
            <p>
             I find that when people pay for something, a switch goes off in their mind and they become that much more likely to actually do something productive than those who arent paying.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the coming month I will be introducing an exclusive online community for Entrepreneurs and Startups. No tire kickers, just dedicated people that want to learn and a set of mentors that are out of this world! If you sign up to my email list orfollow me on Twitter
             <a href="http://www.twitter.com/briandevans">
              @briandevans
             </a>
             , Ill be sure to send you the link when its live.
            </p>
