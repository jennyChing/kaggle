     <p>
      <b>
       Collectivist anarchism
      </b>
      (also known as
      <b>
       anarcho-collectivism
      </b>
      ) is a revolutionary
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Patsouras.2C_Louis_2005._p._54_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Patsouras.2C_Louis_2005._p._54-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarchist" title="Anarchist">
       anarchist
      </a>
      doctrine that advocates the abolition of both the
      <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">
       state
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Private_property" title="Private property">
       private ownership
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Means_of_production" title="Means of production">
       means of production
      </a>
      . It instead envisions the means of production being owned collectively and controlled and managed by the producers themselves.
     </p>
     <p>
      For the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collectivization" title="Collectivization">
       collectivization
      </a>
      of the means of production, it was originally envisaged that workers will revolt and forcibly collectivize the means of production.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Patsouras.2C_Louis_2005._p._54_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Patsouras.2C_Louis_2005._p._54-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Once collectivization takes place, money would be abolished to be replaced with
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_voucher" title="Labour voucher">
       labour notes
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worker" title="Worker">
       workers
      </a>
      ' salaries would be determined, in democratic organizations of voluntary membership, based on job difficulty and the amount of time they contributed to production. These salaries would be used to purchase goods in a communal market.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bakunin_Mikail_1980._p._369_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bakunin_Mikail_1980._p._369-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This contrasts with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarcho-communism" title="Anarcho-communism">
       anarcho-communism
      </a>
      where wages would be abolished, and where individuals would take freely from a storehouse of goods "to each according to his need." Thus, Bakunin's "Collectivist Anarchism," notwithstanding the title, is seen as a blend of
      <a href="/wiki/Individualism" title="Individualism">
       individualism
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Collectivism" title="Collectivism">
       collectivism
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Collectivist anarchism is most commonly associated with
      <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Bakunin" title="Mikhail Bakunin">
       Mikhail Bakunin
      </a>
      , the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-authoritarian" title="Anti-authoritarian">
       anti-authoritarian
      </a>
      sections of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_International" title="First International">
       First International
      </a>
      , and the early
      <a href="/wiki/Anarchism_in_Spain" title="Anarchism in Spain">
       Spanish anarchist movement
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_First_International">
       The First International
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bakuninfull.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="359" data-file-width="250" height="316" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Bakuninfull.jpg/220px-Bakuninfull.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/28/Bakuninfull.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/28/Bakuninfull.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bakuninfull.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Collectivist anarchist
        <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Bakunin" title="Mikhail Bakunin">
         Mikhail Bakunin
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Fanelli" title="Giuseppe Fanelli">
       Giuseppe Fanelli
      </a>
      met Bakunin at
      <a href="/wiki/Ischia" title="Ischia">
       Ischia
      </a>
      in 1866.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBookchin199814_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBookchin199814-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In October 1868 Bakunin sponsored Fanelli to travel to
      <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">
       Barcelona
      </a>
      to share his libertarian visions and recruit revolutionists to the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Workingmen%27s_Association" title="International Workingmen's Association">
       International Workingmen's Association
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBookchin199812.E2.80.9315_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBookchin199812.E2.80.9315-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Fanellis trip and the meeting he organised during his travels provided the catalyst for the
      <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Republican_government_in_exile" title="Spanish Republican government in exile">
       Spanish exiles
      </a>
      , the largest workers' and peasants' movement in modern Spain and the largest Anarchist movement in modern Europe.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBookchin199812_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBookchin199812-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Fanelli's tour took him first to Barcelona, where he met and stayed with Elie Recluse.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBookchin199812_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBookchin199812-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Recluse and Fanelli were at odds over Recluse's friendships with Spanish republicans, and Fanelli soon left Barcelona for Madrid.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBookchin199812_6-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBookchin199812-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Fanelli stayed in Madrid until the end of January 1869, conducting meetings to introduce Spanish workers, including
      <a href="/wiki/Anselmo_Lorenzo" title="Anselmo Lorenzo">
       Anselmo Lorenzo
      </a>
      , to the First National.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBookchin199813_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBookchin199813-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In February 1869 Fanelli left Madrid, journeying home via Barcelona.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBookchin199814_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBookchin199814-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      While in Barcelona again, he met with painter Jos Lus Pellicer and his nephew,
      <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Farga_i_Pellicer" title="Rafael Farga i Pellicer">
       Rafael Farga Pellicer
      </a>
      along with others who were to play an important role establishing the International in Barcelona,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBookchin199814_4-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBookchin199814-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      as well as the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Alliance_of_Socialist_Democracy" title="International Alliance of Socialist Democracy">
       Alliance
      </a>
      section.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1870 Bakunin led a failed uprising in
      <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">
       Lyon
      </a>
      on the principles later exemplified by the
      <a href="/wiki/Paris_Commune" title="Paris Commune">
       Paris Commune
      </a>
      , calling for a general uprising in response to the collapse of the French government during the
      <a href="/wiki/Franco-Prussian_War" title="Franco-Prussian War">
       Franco-Prussian War
      </a>
      , seeking to transform an imperialist conflict into social revolution. In his
      <i>
       Letters to A Frenchman on the Present Crisis
      </i>
      , he argued for a revolutionary alliance between the working class and the peasantry, advocated a system of militias with elected officers as part of a system of self-governing communes and workplaces, and argued the time was ripe for revolutionary action:
     </p>
     <blockquote class="templatequote">
      <p>
       we must spread our principles, not with words but with deeds, for this is the most popular, the most potent, and the most irresistible form of propaganda.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marxists.org_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-marxists.org-8">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         8
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      These ideas and corresponded strikingly closely with the program of the
      <a href="/wiki/Paris_Commune" title="Paris Commune">
       Paris Commune
      </a>
      of 1871, much of which was developed by followers of
      <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Joseph_Proudhon" title="Pierre-Joseph Proudhon">
       Pierre-Joseph Proudhon
      </a>
      ; Marxists were almost entirely absent from the Commune. Bakunin was a strong supporter of the Commune, which was brutally suppressed by the French government. He saw the Commune as above all a "rebellion against the State," and commended the Communards for rejecting not only the State but also revolutionary dictatorship.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In a series of powerful pamphlets, he defended the Commune and the First International against the Italian nationalist
      <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Mazzini" title="Giuseppe Mazzini">
       Giuseppe Mazzini
      </a>
      , thereby winning over many Italian republicans to the International and the cause of revolutionary socialism. The collectivist anarchists at first used the term "collectivism" to distinguish themselves from the
      <a href="/wiki/Mutualism_(economic_theory)" title="Mutualism (economic theory)">
       mutualism
      </a>
      of the followers of
      <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Joseph_Proudhon" title="Pierre-Joseph Proudhon">
       Proudhon
      </a>
      and the state socialists associated with
      <a href="/wiki/Karl_Marx" title="Karl Marx">
       Karl Marx
      </a>
      . Bakunin wrote, "we shall always protest against anything that may in any way resemble communism or state socialism," which Bakunin regarded as fundamentally authoritarian ("Federalism, Socialism, and Anti-Theologism," 1867).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bakunin's disagreements with Marx, which led to the attempt by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marx_party" title="Marx party">
       Marx party
      </a>
      to expel him at the Hague Congress (see below), illustrated the growing divergence between the "
      <a href="/wiki/Anti-authoritarianism" title="Anti-authoritarianism">
       anti-authoritarian
      </a>
      " sections of the International, which advocated the direct revolutionary action and organization of the workers and peasants in order to abolish the state and capitalism, and the sections allied with Marx, which advocated the conquest of political power by the working class. Bakunin was "Marxs flamboyant chief opponent", and "presciently warned against the emergence of a communist authoritarianism that would take power over working people."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Anti-authoritarianism" title="Anti-authoritarianism">
       anti-authoritarian
      </a>
      majority, which included most sections of the International, created their own First International at the
      <a href="/wiki/Saint-Imier" title="Saint-Imier">
       St. Imier
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Anarchist_St._Imier_International" title="Anarchist St. Imier International">
       Congress
      </a>
      , adopted a revolutionary anarchist program, and repudiated the Hague resolutions, rescinding Bakunin's alleged expulsion
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Although Bakunin accepted elements of Marxs
      <a href="/wiki/Class_analysis" title="Class analysis">
       class analysis
      </a>
      and theories regarding capitalism, acknowledging "Marxs genius", he thought Marx's analysis was one-sided, and that Marx's methods would compromise the social revolution. More importantly, Bakunin criticized "
      <a href="/wiki/Authoritarianism" title="Authoritarianism">
       authoritarian socialism
      </a>
      " (which he associated with Marxism) and the concept of
      <a href="/wiki/Dictatorship_of_the_proletariat" title="Dictatorship of the proletariat">
       dictatorship of the proletariat
      </a>
      which he adamantly refused.
      <span id="Maxim">
      </span>
     </p>
     <blockquote class="templatequote">
      <p>
       If you took the most ardent revolutionary, vested him in absolute power, within a year he would be worse than the Tsar himself.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         13
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      The anti-authoritarian sections of the First International proclaimed at the St. Imier Congress (1872) that "the aspirations of the proletariat can have no purpose other than the establishment of an absolutely free economic organization and federation, founded upon the labour and equality of all and absolutely independent of all political government," in which each worker will have the "right to the enjoyment of the gross product of his labours and thereby the means of developing his full intellectual, material and moral powers in a collective setting." This revolutionary transformation could "only be the outcome of the spontaneous action of the proletariat itself, its trades bodies and the autonomous communes."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackrosebooks.net_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-blackrosebooks.net-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A similar position was adopted by the Workers' Federation of the Spanish Region in 1882, as articulated by an anarchist veteran of the First International, Jose Llunas Pujols, in his essay, "Collectivism."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackrosebooks.net_14-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-blackrosebooks.net-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      By the early 1880s, most of the European anarchist movement had adopted an anarchist communist position, advocating the abolition of wage labour and distribution according to need. Ironically, the "collectivist" label then became more commonly associated with Marxist state socialists who advocated the retention of some sort of wage system during the transition to full
      <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">
       communism
      </a>
      . The anarchist communist,
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Kropotkin" title="Peter Kropotkin">
       Peter Kropotkin
      </a>
      , attacked this position in his essay, "The Collectivist Wages System", which was reprinted in his book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Conquest_of_Bread" title="The Conquest of Bread">
        The Conquest of Bread
       </a>
      </i>
      in 1892.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Theory">
       Theory
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bakunin_speaking.png">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1721" data-file-width="1263" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Bakunin_speaking.png/220px-Bakunin_speaking.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Bakunin_speaking.png/330px-Bakunin_speaking.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Bakunin_speaking.png/440px-Bakunin_speaking.png 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bakunin_speaking.png" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Bakunin speaking to members of the IWA at the Basel Congress in 1869
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Bakunin's socialism was known as "collectivist anarchism", where "socially: it seeks the confirmation of political equality by economic equality. This is not the removal of natural individual differences, but equality in the social rights of every individual from birth; in particular, equal means of subsistence, support, education, and opportunity for every child, boy or girl, until maturity, and equal resources and facilities in adulthood to create his own well-being by his own labor."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Revolutionary_Catechism_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Revolutionary_Catechism-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Collectivist anarchism advocates the abolition of both the
      <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">
       state
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Private_property" title="Private property">
       private ownership
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Means_of_production" title="Means of production">
       means of production
      </a>
      . It instead envisions the means of production being owned collectively and controlled and managed by the producers themselves. For the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collectivization" title="Collectivization">
       collectivization
      </a>
      of the means of production, it was originally envisaged that workers will revolt and forcibly collectivize the means of production.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Patsouras.2C_Louis_2005._p._54_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Patsouras.2C_Louis_2005._p._54-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Once collectivization takes place, money would be abolished to be replaced with
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_voucher" title="Labour voucher">
       labour notes
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worker" title="Worker">
       workers
      </a>
      ' salaries would be determined in democratic organizations based on job difficulty and the amount of time they contributed to production. These salaries would be used to purchase goods in a communal market.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bakunin_Mikail_1980._p._369_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bakunin_Mikail_1980._p._369-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Critique_of_Marxism">
       Critique of Marxism
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The dispute between Mikhail Bakunin and Karl Marx highlighted the differences between
      <a href="/wiki/Anarchism" title="Anarchism">
       anarchism
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Marxism" title="Marxism">
       Marxism
      </a>
      . Bakunin arguedagainst certain ideas of a number of Marxiststhat not all revolutions need be violent. He also strongly rejected Marx's concept of the "
      <a href="/wiki/Dictatorship_of_the_proletariat" title="Dictatorship of the proletariat">
       dictatorship of the proletariat
      </a>
      ", a concept that vanguardist socialism including MarxistLeninism would use to justify one-party rule from above by a party 'representing' the proletariat.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GW158_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-GW158-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bakunin insisted that revolutions must be led by the people directly while any "enlightened elite" must only exert influence by remaining "
      <a href="/wiki/Invisible_dictatorship" title="Invisible dictatorship">
       invisible
      </a>
      ...not imposed on anyone...[and] deprived of all official rights and significance".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Struggle.ws_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Struggle.ws-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He held that the state should be immediately abolished because all forms of government eventually lead to oppression.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GW158_16-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-GW158-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Libertarian Marxists argue Marx used the phrase to mean the worker control at the point of production, not a party, would still be a state until society is reorganized according to socialist principles.
     </p>
     <blockquote class="templatequote">
      <p>
       They [the Marxists] maintain that only a dictatorshiptheir dictatorship, of coursecan create the will of the people, while our answer to this is: No dictatorship can have any other aim but that of self-perpetuation, and it can beget only slavery in the people tolerating it; freedom can be created only by freedom, that is, by a universal rebellion on the part of the people and free organization of the toiling masses from the bottom up.
      </p>
      <div class="templatequotecite">
       Mikhail Bakunin,
       <i>
        Statism and Anarchism
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         18
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </div>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      While both social anarchists and Marxists share the same final goal, the creation of a free,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egalitarian" title="Egalitarian">
       egalitarian
      </a>
      society
      <a href="/wiki/Classless_society" title="Classless society">
       without social classes
      </a>
      and government, they strongly disagree on how to achieve this goal. Anarchists believe that the classless, stateless society should be established by the
      <a href="/wiki/Direct_action" title="Direct action">
       direct action
      </a>
      of the masses, culminating in
      <a href="/wiki/Social_revolution" title="Social revolution">
       social revolution
      </a>
      , and refuse any intermediate stage such as the
      <a href="/wiki/Dictatorship_of_the_proletariat" title="Dictatorship of the proletariat">
       dictatorship of the proletariat
      </a>
      , on the basis that such a dictatorship will become a self-perpetuating fundament. For Bakunin, the fundamental contradiction is that for the Marxists, "anarchism or freedom is the aim, while the state and dictatorship is the means, and so, in order to free the masses, they have first to be enslaved."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      However, Bakunin also wrote of meeting Marx in 1844 that:
     </p>
     <blockquote class="templatequote">
      <p>
       As far as learning was concerned, Marx was, and still is, incomparably more advanced than I. I knew nothing at that time of political economy, I had not yet rid myself of my metaphysical observations... He called me a sentimental idealist and he was right; I called him a vain man, perfidious and crafty, and I also was right.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         20
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Bakunin found Marx's economic analysis very useful and began the job of translating
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Das_Kapital" title="Das Kapital">
        Das Kapital
       </a>
      </i>
      into Russian. In turn Marx wrote of the rebels in the Dresden insurrection of 1848 that "In the Russian refugee Michael Bakunin they found a capable and cool headed leader."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Marx wrote to Engels of meeting Bakunin in 1864 after his escape to Siberia saying "On the whole he is one of the few people whom I find not to have retrogressed after 16 years, but to have developed further."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bakunin has sometimes been called the first theorist of the "
      <a href="/wiki/New_class" title="New class">
       new class
      </a>
      ", meaning that a 'class' of intellectuals and bureaucrats running the state in the name of the people or the proletariat  but in reality in their own interests alone. Bakunin argued that the "State has always been the patrimony of some privileged class: a priestly class, an aristocratic class, a bourgeois class. And finally, when all the other classes have exhausted themselves, the State then becomes the patrimony of the bureaucratic class and then fallsor, if you will, risesto the position of a machine."
     </p>
     <p>
      Also Bakunin had a different view as compared to Marx's on the revolutionary potential of the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Lumpenproletariat" title="Lumpenproletariat">
        lumpenproletariat
       </a>
      </i>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Proletariat" title="Proletariat">
       proletariat
      </a>
      . As such "Both agreed that the proletariat would play a key role, but for Marx the proletariat was the exclusive, leading revolutionary agent while Bakunin entertained the possibility that the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peasants" title="Peasants">
       peasants
      </a>
      and even the lumpenproletariat (the unemployed, common criminals, etc.) could rise to the occasion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bakunin "considers workers' integration in capital as destructive of more primary revolutionary forces. For Bakunin, the revolutionary archetype is found in a peasant milieu (which is presented as having longstanding insurrectionary traditions, as well as a communist archetype in its current social formthe peasant commune) and amongst educated unemployed youth, assorted marginals from all classes, brigands, robbers, the impoverished masses, and those on the margins of society who have escaped, been excluded from, or not yet subsumed in the discipline of emerging industrial work...in short, all those whom Marx sought to include in the category of the lumpenproletariat."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Statism_and_Anarchy.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="835" data-file-width="524" height="351" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Statism_and_Anarchy.jpg/220px-Statism_and_Anarchy.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Statism_and_Anarchy.jpg/330px-Statism_and_Anarchy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Statism_and_Anarchy.jpg/440px-Statism_and_Anarchy.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Statism_and_Anarchy.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <i>
         Statism and Anarchy
        </i>
        by Bakunin, Russian first print 1873
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Comparison_with_communist_anarchism">
       Comparison with communist anarchism
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The difference between collectivist anarchism and
      <a href="/wiki/Anarchist_communism" title="Anarchist communism">
       anarchist communism
      </a>
      is that under the former, a wage system is retained based on the amount of labor performed. Anarchist communism, like collectivist anarchism, also advocates for the socialization of production, but the distribution of goods as well. Instead of 'to each according to his labor', in anarcho-communism the community would supply the subsistence requirements to each member free of charge according to the maxim 'to each according to his needs'.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The difference between collectivist anarchism and anarchist communism is that collectivist anarchism stresses collective ownership of productive, subsistence and distributary property, while communist anarchism negates the concept of ownership in favor of usage or possession with productive means being a possession not owned by any individual or particular group.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Communist Anarchists believe that subsistence, productive and distributive property should be common or social possessions while personal property should be private possessions.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Collectivist anarchists agree with this, however, disagree on the subject of remuneration; some collectivist anarchists, such as Mikhail Bakunin, believe in the remuneration of labor, while communist anarchists, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Kropotkin" title="Peter Kropotkin">
       Peter Kropotkin
      </a>
      , believe that such remuneration would lead to the recreation of currency and that this would need a State.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Thus, it could be said that collectivist anarchists believe in freedom through collective ownership of production and a communal market of sorts to distribute goods and services and compensate workers in the form of remuneration. Thus, collectivist anarchism could be seen as a combination of communism and mutualism.
     </p>
     <p>
      Collectivist anarchists are not necessarily opposed to the use of currency, but some while opposing the retaining of money propose the adoption of
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_voucher" title="Labour voucher">
       labour vouchers
      </a>
      or "personal credit" (such as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Participatory_Economics" title="Participatory Economics">
       Participatory Economists
      </a>
      ). Most collectivist anarchists see their philosophy as a carryover to communist anarchism, but some see the system and the use of a
      <a href="/wiki/Labour_voucher" title="Labour voucher">
       labour voucher
      </a>
      system as permanent rather than a transition. Collectivist anarchist James Guillaume argued that such a society would
      <i>
       "guarantee the mutual use of the tools of production which are the property of each of these groups and which will by a reciprocal contract become the collective property of the whole ... federation. In this way, the federation of groups will be able to ... regulate the rate of production to meet the fluctuating needs of society."
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      They argue for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Workplace_autonomy" title="Workplace autonomy">
       workplace autonomy
      </a>
      and self-management "the workers in the various factories have not the slightest intention of handing over their hard-won control of the tools of production to a superior power calling itself the 'corporation.'"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Anarchist FAQ compares and contrasts collectivist anarchism with communist anarchism this way:
     </p>
     <table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
      <tr>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
        The major difference between collectivists and communists is over the question of "money" after a revolution. Anarcho-communists consider the abolition of money to be essential, while anarcho-collectivists consider the end of private ownership of the means of production to be the key. As Kropotkin noted,
        <i>
         "[collectivist anarchism] express[es] a state of things in which all necessaries for production are owned in common by the labour groups and the free communes, while the ways of retribution [i.e. distribution] of labour, communist or otherwise, would be settled by each group for itself."
        </i>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
         <a href="#cite_note-32">
          <span>
           [
          </span>
          32
          <span>
           ]
          </span>
         </a>
        </sup>
        Thus, while communism and collectivism both organise production in common via producers' associations, they differ in how the goods produced will be distributed. Communism is based on free consumption of all while collectivism is more likely to be based on the distribution of goods according to the labour contributed. However, most anarcho-collectivists think that, over time, as productivity increases and the sense of community becomes stronger, money will disappear.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
         <a href="#cite_note-33">
          <span>
           [
          </span>
          33
          <span>
           ]
          </span>
         </a>
        </sup>
       </td>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
      </tr>
     </table>
     <p>
      The collectivist anarchists at first used the term "collectivism" to distinguish themselves from the
      <a href="/wiki/Mutualism_(economic_theory)" title="Mutualism (economic theory)">
       mutualism
      </a>
      of the followers of
      <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Joseph_Proudhon" title="Pierre-Joseph Proudhon">
       Proudhon
      </a>
      and the state socialists associated with
      <a href="/wiki/Karl_Marx" title="Karl Marx">
       Karl Marx
      </a>
      . Bakunin wrote, "we shall always protest against anything that may in any way resemble communism or state socialism," which Bakunin regarded as fundamentally authoritarian ("Federalism, Socialism, and Anti-Theologism," 1867).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Performance">
       Performance
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Application of many collectivist anarchists projects have been successful,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-libcom.org_35-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-libcom.org-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      sources during the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Revolution" title="Spanish Revolution">
       Spanish Revolution
      </a>
      noted that in the Catalan region,
     </p>
     <table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
      <tr>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
        <i>
         "In distribution the collectives' co-operatives eliminated middlemen, small merchants, wholesalers, and profiteers, thus greatly reducing consumer prices. The collectives eliminated most of the parasitic elements from rural life, and would have wiped them out altogether if they were not protected by corrupt officials and by the political parties. Non-collectivised areas benefited indirectly from the lower prices as well as from free services often rendered by the collectives (laundries, cinemas, schools, barber and beauty parlours, etc.)"
        </i>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
         <a href="#cite_note-36">
          <span>
           [
          </span>
          36
          <span>
           ]
          </span>
         </a>
        </sup>
       </td>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
      </tr>
     </table>
     <p>
      Tom Wetzel describes another collectivization...
     </p>
     <table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
      <tr>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
        <i>
         "Another industry that was totally re-organized was hair-cutting. Before July 19th, there had been 1,100 hairdressing parlors in Barcelona, most of them extremely marginal. The 5,000 assistant hairdressers were among the lowest-paid workers in Barcelona. The Generalitat had decreed a 40-hour week and 15 percent wage increase after July 19th  one of the Esquerras attempts to woo worker support. This spelled ruin for many hairdressing shops. A general assembly was held and it was agreed to shut down all the unprofitable shops. The 1,100 shops were replaced by a network of 235 neighborhood haircutting centers, with better equipment and lighting than the old shops. Due to the efficiencies gained, it was possible to raise wages by 40 percent. The entire network was run through assemblies of the CNT barbers union. The former owners became members of the union(38)."
        </i>
