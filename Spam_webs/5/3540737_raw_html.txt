          <p>
           <img alt="self destructing email" class="align-right" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/burnnote-icon.png?f35dd4"/>
           <em>
            This message will self-destruct
           </em>
           . If theres one thing every kid watching spy shows dreams of, its exploding messages that disappear forever. The explosion is cool, but so is the idea that you can send a message to your friend without anyone else ever being able to read it afterwards.
          </p>
          <p>
           Burn Note gives you exactly this functionality, albeit without the exploding tape or piece of paper. Write a message and send a link to your friend. They can read the message, but not easily copy it. A certain amount of time after they open the message, it is deleted forever. You can even add a password, if you want additional
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/service/security/">
            security
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Its not quite as impactful as a message that literally explodes, but it gets the job done if you want to send a self destructing email without it lasting forever in someones inbox.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Sending A Secret Self Destructing Email
          </h2>
          <p>
           Head over to
           <a href="https://burnnote.com/">
            BurnNote
           </a>
           and you can start writing your message immediately. Theres no formatting or pictures: just good old-fashioned text.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="self destructing email" class="aligncenter" height="184" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/burnnote-message.png?f35dd4" width="578"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           As you can see, Ive composed a deeply serious, top-secret message. I simply cannot let this fall into the wrong hands. So I head to the options.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="self destructing message" class="aligncenter" height="198" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/burnnote-options.png?f35dd4" width="494"/>
          </p>
          <div>
           <div class="ad-unit-post" id="adsense2">
            <small>
             <a href="https://support.google.com/adsense/troubleshooter/1631343?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.makeuseof.com%2Ftag%2Fsend-selfdestructing-messages-minions-burn-note%2F&amp;client=ca-pub-9894449314507324" style="color:LightGray">
              Ads by Google
             </a>
            </small>

          <p>
           I can set how the message will be displayed, and for how long. The message can be displayed as plain text, which can be copied, or in short phrases that cannot (easily). I can even add a password, if Im feeling particularly paranoid.
          </p>
          <p>
           Once Im done, I get a link to my message.The message will be deleted in 72 hours, regardless of whether its opened or not.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="self destructing message" class="aligncenter" height="210" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/burnnote-link.png?f35dd4" width="579"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Im now ready to send this to one of my loyal minions. I can send the link via email, instant message or carrierpigeon.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Reading A Message
          </h2>
          <p>
           When my minion gets the link, they can open the message. The countdown to destruction does not start until they open it.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="self destructing message" class="aligncenter" height="189" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/burnnote-receive.png?f35dd4" width="580"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           So far, so good. When the message is opened, it looks something like this:
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="self destructing email" class="aligncenter" height="117" src="http://cdn.makeuseof.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/burnnote-reading.png?f35dd4" width="470"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Since I chose 
           <em>
            short phrases
           </em>
           , it is shown a few words at a time and cannot be copied easily, outside of several precisely timed screenshots.
          </p>
          <p>
           My minion is particularly vigilant, so they will click BURN IT the moment they are done reading. This way no nefarious members of the public will get a chance to read it, and they can commence the operation  meeting me for lunch downtown.
          </p>
          <p>
           Okay, so my personal mission isnt as secret as I let on. Its still a cool site though, isnt it?
          </p>
          <h2>
           Conclusion
          </h2>
          <p>
           If you need to securely send a message over the Internet never use email or IM. They simply werent designed to be secure forms of communication, and could easily end up in the wrong hands. Thats why Im glad Burn Note exists. It lets me send messages over email without leaving a long-term record of the contents.
          </p>
          <p>
           If youre concered about the security of Burn Note itself, check out the
           <a href="https://burnnote.com/faq">
            Burn Note FAQ page
           </a>
           . It nicely outlines when and how your messsages are deleted and is well worth a read if you want to rely on the service for serious, non-lunch related missions.
          </p>
          <p>
           Are you looking for alternative ways to send secure notes? Linux users can try
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/send-signed-encrypted-email-evolution-linux/">
            sending encrypted emails with Evolution
           </a>
           . But if youre not a Linux fan, you could check out these
           <a href="http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/3-secure-encrypted-email-providers-online/">
            secure and encrypted email providers online
           </a>
           . None of these solutions automatically delete the contents of your message, but theyre still worth trying out.
          </p>
          <p>
           Do you know of other services for sending secure messages? If so, share them in the contents below.Also, please dont tell my wife Im referring to her as my minion now.
          </p>
