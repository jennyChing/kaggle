      sweeps away the shadows byspotlightingNewmans formidable racing career  a career so magnificent, a documentary could be built around it.
     </p>
     <p>
      Carolla and his co-director, Nate Adams along with executive producersNorm Pattiz and Matt DAndria slingshotted this film past the checkered flagsfor a first-place finish.
      <em>
       Winning
      </em>
      goes beyond just a word in the films title. This movie is a win for everyone involved inits production.
     </p>
     <p>
      For a car guy like me who isnt particularly a racing fan,
      <em>
       Winning
      </em>
      still spoke to me on a human-interest level, and thats important if this documentary is to succeed with non-car people. When I read books about the history of certain car models, I usually start skipping pages once I reach the racing history portions of the respective chapters. If those chapter sections were written more like this documentary, not only would I read car history books in their entirety, but also I think Id actually become a racing fan. The idea that I could even becomea racing fan is as foreign to me as a desert tortoise in Antarctica.
     </p>
     <p>
      Because of this movie, I am now most definitely a fan of Paul Newmans racing career.
     </p>
