     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Ferrari P4/5
      </b>
      (officially known as the
      <b>
       Ferrari P4/5 by Pininfarina
      </b>
      ) is a
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/one-off" title="wiktionary:one-off">
       one-off
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Sports_car" title="Sports car">
       sports car
      </a>
      made by
      <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
       Italian
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Sports_car" title="Sports car">
       sports car
      </a>
      manufacturer
      <a href="/wiki/Ferrari" title="Ferrari">
       Ferrari
      </a>
      but redesigned by
      <a href="/wiki/Pininfarina" title="Pininfarina">
       Pininfarina
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
       film director
      </a>
      and stock exchange
      <a href="/wiki/Business_magnate" title="Business magnate">
       magnate
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/James_Glickenhaus" title="James Glickenhaus">
       James Glickenhaus
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The car was an
      <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Ferrari_(automobile)" title="Enzo Ferrari (automobile)">
       Enzo Ferrari
      </a>
      but the owner James Glickenhaus preferred the styling of Ferrari's 1960s
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_car" title="Race car">
       race cars
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_P" title="Ferrari P">
       P Series
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercars_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-supercars-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The project cost Glickenhaus
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Dollar" title="US Dollar">
       US$
      </a>
      4million and was officially presented to the public in August 2006 at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pebble_Beach_Concours_d%27El%C3%A9gance" title="Pebble Beach Concours d'Elgance">
       Pebble Beach Concours d'Elgance
      </a>
      . Several websites were allowed to publish images of the clay model in July 2006.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autoblog_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-autoblog-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pininfarina_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pininfarina-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Development">
       Development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ferrari_P4/5_by_Pininfarina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In March 2005 Glickenhaus, stock exchange magnate and car collector, was approached by Pininfarina who asked if he was interested in commissioning a one-off car.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autoblog_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-autoblog-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Pininfarina" title="Andrea Pininfarina">
       Andrea Pininfarina
      </a>
      , grandson of the companys founder later said "The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferrari_612_Kappa" title="Ferrari 612 Kappa">
       Ferrari 612 Kappa
      </a>
      and this P4/5 are the first. But we want to grow this business." indicating that Pininfarina is interested in producing other unique cars.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cardriver_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-cardriver-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Glickenhaus replied that he would like a modern
      <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_P" title="Ferrari P">
       Ferrari P
      </a>
      , and in June of that year he signed a contract with Pininfarina to produce the car including the price, approximately
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_dollar" title="US dollar">
       US
      </a>
      $4 million though in an interview he said "I feel they gave me more than I expected".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autoblog_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-autoblog-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Glickenhaus purchased the last unsold
      <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Ferrari_(automobile)" title="Enzo Ferrari (automobile)">
       Enzo Ferrari
      </a>
      and upon receipt of the car he took it to Pininfarina to be redesigned similar to his 1967
      <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_P#330_P3.2F4_and_P4" title="Ferrari P">
       Ferrari 330 P3/4
      </a>
      chassis 0846 which he also delivered to Pininfarina.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercars_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-supercars-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cardriver_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-cardriver-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultimate_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ultimate-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Pininfarina's styling team leader,
      <a href="/wiki/Ken_Okuyama" title="Ken Okuyama">
       Ken Okuyama
      </a>
      said that "Pininfarina wanted to stay away from retro design and move towards a more forward thinking supercar" as they were excited by the opportunity to build the car, not just design it.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercars_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-supercars-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pininfarina_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-pininfarina-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Its design began in September 2005 with sketches by
      <a href="/wiki/Jason_Castriota" title="Jason Castriota">
       Jason Castriota
      </a>
      moving through computer aided sculpture and stringent wind tunnel testing.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pininfarina_4-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-pininfarina-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cardriver_5-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-cardriver-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      More than 200 components were designed especially for the car. Most components, including the engine, drivetrain, are modified from the original Enzo Ferrari.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pininfarina_4-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-pininfarina-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vehicle_Identification_Number" title="Vehicle Identification Number">
       Vehicle Identification Number
      </a>
      (VIN) is unchanged from the Enzo from which it was derived.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cardriver_5-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-cardriver-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The P4/5 was publicly revealed on August 18, 2006 at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pebble_Beach_Concours_d%27El%C3%A9gance" title="Pebble Beach Concours d'Elgance">
       Pebble Beach Concours d'Elgance
      </a>
      and shown again at the
      <a href="/wiki/Paris_Motor_Show" title="Paris Motor Show">
       Paris Motor Show
      </a>
      in late September.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qv500_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-qv500-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Upon seeing P 4/5
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luca_di_Montezemolo" title="Luca di Montezemolo">
       Luca di Montezemolo
      </a>
      felt that the car deserved to be officially badged as a Ferrari and along with Andrea Pininfarina and James Glickenhaus agreed that its official name would be "Ferrari P 4/5 by Pininfarina". Ted West wrote an article in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Car_and_Driver" title="Car and Driver">
        Car and Driver
       </a>
      </i>
      about how this came to be "The Beast of Turin".
     </p>
     <p>
      On September 2009, Glickenhaus announced his intention to race a new version of the P4/5 in the 2010
      <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_N%C3%BCrburgring" title="24 Hours Nrburgring">
       24 Hours Nrburgring
      </a>
      . The car, called the P4/5 Competizione, would not be a conversion of his road car but instead an entirely new car with a Ferrari chassis, VIN and drivetrain. On May 2010 however, it was revealed that the Competizione would in fact be raced in 2011, based on a
      <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F430" title="Ferrari F430">
       430 Scuderia
      </a>
      . It would be built to FIA GT2 standards and raced by Scuderia Cameron Glickenhaus in an Experimental Class under the direction of Paolo Garella, former Head of Special Projects at Pininfarina. Ferrari completely distanced themselves from the P4/5 Competizione project in 2011, refusing to sell the team parts for vital engine rebuilds between races.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The car finished 39th in the 2011 24 Hours Nrburgring (after bursting into flames) and in 2012 won its class and finished 12th overall.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-posey_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-posey-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">
       Specifications
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ferrari_P4/5_by_Pininfarina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specifications">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Ferrari P4/5 can accelerate from 0-100kilometresperhour (0-62mph) in 3.0seconds (0.14seconds quicker than the Enzo).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qv500_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-qv500-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It has a top speed of 233mph (375km/h). The car has a frontal area of 1.906 square metres (20.52sqft) and the sharp nose and smooth curves mean it has a
      <a href="/wiki/Drag_coefficient" title="Drag coefficient">
       drag coefficient
      </a>
      of only 0.34.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Interior">
       Interior
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ferrari_P4/5_by_Pininfarina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Interior">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_P4-5.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="923" data-file-width="1880" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ferrari_P4-5.jpg/300px-Ferrari_P4-5.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ferrari_P4-5.jpg/450px-Ferrari_P4-5.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ferrari_P4-5.jpg/600px-Ferrari_P4-5.jpg 2x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_P4-5.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The P4/5
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The interior of the P4/5 was designed by Glickenhaus himself with an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IPod_nano" title="IPod nano">
       iPod nano
      </a>
      stereo and a tablet PC which features not only
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Global_positioning_system" title="Global positioning system">
       GPS
      </a>
      but a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Three-dimensional_space" title="Three-dimensional space">
       3D
      </a>
      model of the car as well as a complete parts list and manual for easy servicing. The P4/5 also sports improved air conditioning over the Enzo and a high-strength alloy roll bar redesigned because the original was too thick and obstructed Glickenhaus' view.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultimate_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ultimate-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The seats are custom built, Glickenhaus' and his son's bodies were scanned so Pininfarina could mould the seats for their comfort, accessibility and view of the road (as with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_car" title="Race car">
       race cars
      </a>
      ). With a frame of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber_reinforced_plastic" title="Carbon fiber reinforced plastic">
       carbon fibre composite
      </a>
      , the seats are covered with a black mesh and red leather as selected by Glickenhaus' daughter.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autoblog_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-autoblog-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Pininfarina rearranged the wiring of the car so as to make the car easier to service and 595lb (270kg) lighter than the Enzo.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qv500_8-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-qv500-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Exterior">
       Exterior
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ferrari_P4/5_by_Pininfarina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Exterior">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The exterior of the car is made entirely of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber_reinforced_plastic" title="Carbon fiber reinforced plastic">
       carbon fibre reinforced plastic
      </a>
      and is similar in shape to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferrari_330_P4" title="Ferrari 330 P4">
       Ferrari 330 P4
      </a>
      as Glickenhaus requested, however it has been called a "rolling history of Ferrari-racing-DNA" sharing elements from several historic Ferrari vehicles, not just the 330 P4. The rear window is similar to that of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferrari_512S" title="Ferrari 512S">
       Ferrari 512S
      </a>
      , the side vents are similar to the
      <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_P" title="Ferrari P">
       Ferrari 330 P3
      </a>
      and the nose is similar to that of the
      <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_333_SP" title="Ferrari 333 SP">
       Ferrari 333 SP
      </a>
      which improves cooling and the car's frontal crash safety.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cardriver_5-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-cardriver-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Butterfly_doors" title="Butterfly doors">
       butterfly doors
      </a>
      (similar to those of the
      <a href="/wiki/McLaren_F1" title="McLaren F1">
       McLaren F1
      </a>
      ) are designed such that even at 160mph (260km/h) there is no wind noise.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The improved aerodynamics give the car greater downforce, yet less drag, than the Enzo, which makes the car more stable than the Enzo at high speeds.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Powertrain">
       Powertrain
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ferrari_P4/5_by_Pininfarina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Powertrain">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_P45_plate.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3245" data-file-width="2625" height="247" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Ferrari_P45_plate.jpg/200px-Ferrari_P45_plate.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Ferrari_P45_plate.jpg/300px-Ferrari_P45_plate.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Ferrari_P45_plate.jpg/400px-Ferrari_P45_plate.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_P45_plate.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The P4/5's New York license plate
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The P4/5 has the same engine as the Enzo Ferrari it was built on, a 65
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F140_engine" title="Ferrari F140 engine">
        Ferrari F140 B
       </a>
      </i>
      <a href="/wiki/V12_engine" title="V12 engine">
       V12
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultimate_7-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-ultimate-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The 12cylinders have a total capacity of 5,998
      <a href="/wiki/Cubic_centimetre" title="Cubic centimetre">
       cc
      </a>
      (366
      <a href="/wiki/Cubic_inch#Engine_displacement" title="Cubic inch">
       cuin
      </a>
      ), each with 4
      <a href="/wiki/Poppet_valve" title="Poppet valve">
       valves
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultimate_7-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-ultimate-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Redline_rpm" title="Redline rpm">
       redline rpm
      </a>
      at 8200 and the torque of 485lbft (658Nm) at 5500rpm are both the same as the Enzo, but it produces marginally more power with 660 brake horsepower (492kW) at 7800
      <a href="/wiki/Revolutions_per_minute" title="Revolutions per minute">
       rpm
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p45_6-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-p45-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultimate_7-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-ultimate-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The P4/5 uses the 6speed
      <a href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_transmission" title="Semi-automatic transmission">
       semi-automatic transmission
      </a>
      of the Enzo with black shifting paddles behind the wheel. It has two directional indicator buttons, one mounted on each side of the steering wheel.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Chassis">
       Chassis
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ferrari_P4/5_by_Pininfarina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Chassis">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Much of the suspension was unchanged from the original Enzo, with the same push-rod suspension at the front and rear, and the same
      <a href="/wiki/Brembo" title="Brembo">
       Brembo
      </a>
      carbon-ceramic
      <a href="/wiki/Anti-lock_braking_system" title="Anti-lock braking system">
       anti-lock
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disc_brakes" title="Disc brakes">
       disc brakes
      </a>
      with diameter of 340 millimetres (13.4in) at the front and rear.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercars_1-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-supercars-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alloy_wheels" title="Alloy wheels">
       aluminium alloy wheels
      </a>
      are 510 millimetres (20in) in diameter, the front tyres have
      <a href="/wiki/Tire_code" title="Tire code">
       codes
      </a>
      of ZR255/35 and the rear, ZR335/30.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pininfarina_4-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-pininfarina-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
