      <h2 id="post-8084">
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/dominique-ansel-tokyo-omotesando-japan-inspired-menu/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Dominique Ansel Tokyo opens in Omotesando with new Japan-inspired menu">
        Dominique Ansel Tokyo opens in Omotesando with new Japan-inspired menu
       </a>
      </h2>
      <small>
       Written by: William  on June 25, 2015 at 8:42 am | In
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/category/lifestyle/" rel="category tag">
        LIFESTYLE
       </a>
       |
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/dominique-ansel-tokyo-omotesando-japan-inspired-menu/#respond">
        No Comments
       </a>
      </small>
      <small>
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </small>
      <p>
       Dominique Ansel, the acclaimed Parisian-style New York bakery, has opened its first overseas outlet  in Tokyo.
      </p>
      <p>
       The new bakery opened at the plush Omotesando Hills complex on June 20th, joining the growing ranks of high-end global food and drink creators, especially third-wave coffee roasters like
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/three-hour-blue-bottle-coffee-tokyo-opens/">
        Blue Bottle
       </a>
       and Fuglen, or craft beer makers like
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/brewdog-roppongi/">
        BrewDog
       </a>
       , who have opted to expand overseas with a first outpost in Tokyo.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8086" height="465" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-1.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The menu features all the
       <a href="http://dominiqueanseljapan.com/en" target="_blank">
        Dominique Ansel Tokyo
       </a>
       favorites like
       <a href="http://dominiqueanseljapan.com/en/cronut-101" target="_blank">
        Cronuts
       </a>
       (the Dominique Ansel-trademarked croissant and donut hybrid), Frozen SMore, and Cookie Shot, plus many Japan-exclusive items developed and inspired by local cuisine elements.
      </p>
      <p>
       Dominique Ansel Tokyo is a standalone three-story affair, with two dining spaces and an upstairs kitchen. The ground floor offers take-out and eat-in, while the second floor is a restaurant with waiters and a separate menu.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8087" height="465" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-2.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8088" height="413" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-3.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8090" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-5.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The first floor interior is inspired by Paris and New York subway stations, with a seasonally changing photo diorama. The subway theme continues with custom art by Vahram Muratyan that shows a mashup of the two cities train systems. Observant visitors will spot that the stations are actually various chefs names.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8089" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-4.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Clearly, the Japanese obsession with lining up outside stores for hours, or even overnight in advance of major releases, also extends to Cronuts. Two men
       <a href="http://www.eater.com/2015/6/19/8814735/dominique-ansel-tokyo-massive-line" target="_blank">
        started camping
       </a>
       in front of the Tokyo bakery 12 hours before the doors opened on Saturday morning.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="http://dominiqueanseljapan.com/en/food" target="_blank">
        Japan-only food items
       </a>
       include a special Cronut, made with
       <a href="http://www.eater.com/2015/6/16/8788683/dominique-ansel-first-cronut-tokyo-bakery-japan" target="_blank">
        Hokkaido milk honey and yuzu cream
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8091" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-6.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8092" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-7.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       There is also Paris Tokyo (A twist on the traditional Paris Brest with matcha ganache and a soft passion fruit curd), Monaka Cookie (A fun texture twist with a crispy monaka shell and a moist matcha financier cookie), Mr Roboto (Our take on the melon pan filled with a caramel black truffle custard), and Mont Blanc Wagashi (Chestnut cream, meringue, and orange confit done in the style of a wagashi pairs perfectly with tea and is only offered in our private Japanese tea room).
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8093" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-8.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8094" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-9.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       There are even yuzu- and vanilla-filled cream puffs stacked and shaped to look like Maneki Neko cat figures.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="dominique ansel tokyo bakery japan menu cronuts omotesando hills" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8095" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/dominique-ansel-tokyo-bakery-japan-menu-10.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.japantrendshop.com/cat-tights-p-1743.html">
        <img alt="cat_tights" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1926" height="86" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/cat_tights.jpg" width="540"/>
       </a>
      </p>

      <h2 id="post-7697">
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/saitama-pose-prefecture-campaign-meme-pr-sodasaitama/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Saitama creates Saitama pose to promote much-maligned prefecture">
        Saitama creates Saitama pose to promote much-maligned prefecture
       </a>
      </h2>
      <small>
       Written by: William  on May 7, 2015 at 10:27 am | In
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/category/lifestyle/" rel="category tag">
        LIFESTYLE
       </a>
       |
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/saitama-pose-prefecture-campaign-meme-pr-sodasaitama/#comments">
        1 Comment
       </a>
      </small>
      <small>
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </small>
      <p>
       Pity Saitama.
      </p>
      <p>
       A prefecture so unoriginal that it shares its name with its capital city.
      </p>
      <p>
       A prefecture confined in the popular consciousness to being an extended suburb of Tokyo.
      </p>
      <p>
       A prefecture usually only visited by Tokyoites when they go to see a concert at Saitama Super Arena.
      </p>
      <p>
       Even women suffer for the ignominy of being residents of Saitama: a few years ago they were revealed (not literally) to have the
       <a href="http://www.tokyokinky.com/japan-sexy-underwear-lingerie-bust-breast-size/" target="_blank">
        smallest busts in Japan
       </a>
       (*NSFW).
      </p>
      <p>
       While we thought Ibaraki already had the dubious honor of being
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/ibaraki-least-popular-prefecture/">
        Japans least appealing prefecture
       </a>
       , apparently Saitama is a closer runner for the title too.
      </p>
      <p>
       Hence some creative residents have manufactured the Saitama pose.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="so!da saitama soda pose meme prefecture tourism pr campaign website mayors girls celebrities" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7705" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/soda-saitama-pose-1.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       To go with the meme-waiting-to-happen (or not, as the case may be), they have also launched a website,
       <a href="http://soudasaitama.com/" target="_blank">
        So!daSaitama.com
       </a>
       (Thats right, Saitama).
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="so!da saitama soda pose meme prefecture tourism pr campaign website mayors girls celebrities" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7704" height="327" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/soda-saitama-pose.png" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       In a nutshell, you put your fingers together in an okay sign and cross your arms.
      </p>
      <p>
       Media reports claim it is buzzing online, though we suspect this is a pure fabrication at this point. There is a golden rule in the digital age: call it a meme and it will become a meme. Things are announced as trends by the Japanese media often long before they genuinely become a thing.
      </p>
      <p>
       After prefectures like Kanagawa and Saga
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/akb48-koisuru-fortune-cookie-marketing-staff-videos/">
        successfully used online platforms
       </a>
       to promote themselves and Shibuya
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/pharrell-williams-happy-harajuku-music-video/">
        hijacked Pharrell Williams
       </a>
       , every region in Japan is competing to be next to go viral.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="so!da saitama soda pose meme prefecture tourism pr campaign website mayors girls celebrities" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7706" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/soda-saitama-pose-2.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       So!daSaitama.com features pictures of currently
       <a href="http://soudasaitama.com/special/3923.html" target="_blank">
        21 mayors
       </a>
       (over half the cities in Saitama) and
       <a href="http://soudasaitama.com/saitama-bijin" target="_blank">
        young local girls
       </a>
       (a guaranteed way to get clicks) all adopting the signature pose, which is apparently inspired by the Saitama official bird, the Eurasian collared dove. Your hands become the birds wings while the linked fingers form a ring (a reference to the tama of Saitama, which means ball).
      </p>
      <p>
       It all started last September with this music video. The lyrics play on the prefectures reputation for being
       <em>
        dasai
       </em>
       (uncool).
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="350" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/RU-OQ2AcHX4" width="620">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p>
       The video features 837 people from 46 local businesses and organizations.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was marketed in the same vein as the Kanagawa Prefecture
       <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RBBbuspVyyU" target="_blank">
        Koi suru Fortune Cookie AKB48 video
       </a>
       that was a hit with netizens (4 million views), though ultimately Saitamas is stuck at a so-so 75,000. Close, but no cigar, as they say.
      </p>
      <p>
       The whole PR campaign is the brainchild of
       <a href="http://tenka-jaya.com/" target="_blank">
        Tenka Jaya
       </a>
       , an online design agency based in  youve guessed it  Saitama.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="so!da saitama soda pose meme prefecture tourism pr campaign website mayors girls celebrities" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7707" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/soda-saitama-pose-4.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       While we cant help raising our eyebrows at the earnestness of the campaign, it is certainly a welcome change to the usual
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/tag/mascots/">
        <em>
         yuru-kyara
        </em>
       </a>
       strategies.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.japantrendshop.com/yozora-oekaki-art-penlight-for-iphone-ipad-p-1680.html">
        <img alt="penlight" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1925" height="86" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/penlight.jpg" width="540"/>
       </a>
      </p>

      <h2 id="post-7664">
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/suntory-brewery-tour/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Get Your Tour and Pour On at Suntory Brewery">
        Get Your Tour and Pour On at Suntory Brewery
       </a>
      </h2>
      <small>
       Written by: Tokyo Cheapo on May 1, 2015 at 8:53 am | In
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/category/lifestyle/" rel="category tag">
        LIFESTYLE
       </a>
       |
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/suntory-brewery-tour/#respond">
        No Comments
       </a>
      </small>
      <small>
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </small>
      <h4>
       This article by
       <a href="http://tokyocheapo.com/author/angelaschnabel/" target="_blank">
        Angela Schnabel
       </a>
       first appeared on
       <a href="http://tokyocheapo.com/food-and-drink/drinking/suntory-brewery-tour-tokyo/" target="_blank">
        Tokyo Cheapo
       </a>
       .
      </h4>
      <p>
       <em>
        For just an hour of your time you can enjoy a free factory tour and beer tasting at Suntorys Musashino Brewery in Fuchu, Tokyo.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       Get off at Bubaigawara Station, catch the factorys free shuttle, and youre on your way to getting an up-close and personal look into beer giant Suntorys Premium Malt brewing process. After about a 5-minute shuttle ride you arrive at the lobby and are promptly greeted by the friendly staff.
      </p>
      <p>
       Your eyes are immediately drawn to the center wall that features the hallmark blue and gold Suntory logo, but before you go anywhere be sure to check in at the reception desk on the right side of the lobby.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="suntory beer musashino brewery fuchu tokyo visit tour" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7667" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/suntory-brewery-musashino-fuchu-tokyo-tour-1.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       If you have some time to kill before your tour starts be sure to check out the gift shop and the beer-themed photo props next to the logo wall so you can make your very own Suntory factory souvenir photo. If your tour begins right away dont worry, you can visit these areas again at the end of the tour.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="suntory beer musashino brewery fuchu tokyo visit tour" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7668" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/suntory-brewery-musashino-fuchu-tokyo-tour-2.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       When its time to begin, youll take the elevator upstairs where youll be shown a short video about the quality of the spring water they use in their brewing process.
      </p>
      <p>
       The first stop after the introduction is the hops display where youll learn about the type of hops that Suntory uses. Youll even get to taste and smell a sample!
      </p>
      <p>
       Next youll be led through a long hallway to the vat area where you can look inside a port-hole window that allows you to see what the vat looks like.
      </p>
      <p>
       Then youll move on to the filling and packaging area. Youll watch a short video about their process and you can look through the large windows that allow you to see the factory floor.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="suntory beer musashino brewery fuchu tokyo visit tour" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7669" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/suntory-brewery-musashino-fuchu-tokyo-tour-3.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       And now for best part of the tour: the beer tasting. You get to wet your whistle on some freshly brewed Suntory beer straight from the factory. After all, you worked hard learning all about the process on the tour, so you deserve a few cold ones!
      </p>
      <p>
       Each guest can savor 3 frosty beers from Suntorys selection of the day. Youll also find Suntory-branded salty snacks on the table to pair with your brew.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="suntory beer musashino brewery fuchu tokyo visit tour" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7670" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/suntory-brewery-musashino-fuchu-tokyo-tour-4.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       After everyone gets their beer your tour guide will show you how to properly pour it so you get a nice foam, just like in the Suntory commercials.
      </p>
      <p>
       True to Japans affinity for convenience and customer service, Suntory even offers a mail-order option where they will deliver fresh Suntory beer to your doorstep if you live in Tokyo.
      </p>
      <p>
       After the tasting you head back down to the lobby where you can check out the gift shop and souvenir photo wall if you didnt have time before the tour. At the gift shop youll find a variety of their beers, handmade glassware, t-shirts, key chains, and other gift ideas.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you buy nothing else I highly recommend the
       <a href="http://www.japantrendshop.com/fujiyama-mount-fuji-beer-glass-p-2176.html">
        Fujiyama beer glass
       </a>
       , designed to resemble the iconic Mt. Fuji when a beer is poured into it. The Fujiyama glass won a Tokyo Midtown Award for its design and level of craftsmanship. Its a little small (and a little pricey too at about 5,000), but its hand blown glass from a master glass blower and comes in an elegant bamboo gift box, making it a fantastic souvenir for yourself, or a unique gift for someone back home.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="suntory beer musashino brewery fuchu tokyo visit tour" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7671" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/suntory-brewery-musashino-fuchu-tokyo-tour-5.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Important points to consider:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Cameras are welcome, just know that there are some areas of the factory where photography and recording are not allowed. Youll see signs advising where you can and cant take pictures.
       </li>
       <li>
        The tour, handouts, and display placards are all in Japanese, so be sure to bring a native Japanese speaker if you want to learn about all the nitty-gritty details involved in beer brewing. Even without a native speaker to help you can still get the general idea, after all, beer is a universal language.
       </li>
       <li>
        Children and non-beer drinkers are welcome, as there are soft drinks and water available in the tasting room in lieu of beer.
       </li>
       <li>
        If youll have more than 10 people in your group youll need to make a reservation, which can be done over the phone, or on their website. Be sure to use Google Translate or the Google Chrome browser to help with making an online reservation.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Access:
       </strong>
       Its about a 1.6-km walk from Bubaigawara station, or you can take the free shuttle on the south side of the station near the horse-mounted samurai statue.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Tour times:
       </strong>
       Monday-Friday 10:00 am-4:00 pm. Saturday, Sunday, and Japanese public holidays 10:00 am-11:30 am.
      </p>
      <p>
       And if you really love free beer (and sake!), you can also check out the free tour at
       <a href="http://tokyocheapo.com/entertainment/ishikawa-brewery/" target="_blank">
        Ishikawa Brewery
       </a>
        the Disneyland for beer lovers.
      </p>

        Halls Delivery Bear Service sends polar bear to heal stressed Japanese women
       </a>
      </h2>
      <small>
       Written by: William  on April 24, 2015 at 9:15 am | In
       <a href="http://www.japantrends.com/category/lifestyle/" rel="category tag">

       </a>
       |

       </a>
      </small>
      <small>
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </small>
      <p>
       Last year there was a lot of buzz about a strange thing that happened in Shibuya. A Russian-looking woman was apparently leading a live polar bear through the crowds of Center Gai and then across Scramble Crossing. This obviously drew a lot of attention from people in the area, who werent sure what was taking place.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/LALSH/515205745291991" target="_blank">
        LALASH campaign
       </a>
       succeeded in getting lots of
       <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/news/world-news/mystery-blonde-takes-polar-bear-4413034" target="_blank">
        global press
       </a>
       and online chatter about whether it was a real bear, who was the Russian chick, and what LALASH meant.
      </p>
      <p>
       Of course, it wasnt real and, also of course, it was a marketing stunt  for
       <a href="http://www.halls.jp/index.html" target="_blank">
        Halls
       </a>
       (rearrange the letters LALASH and youll get it).
      </p>
      <p>
       Now comes the follow-up:
       <a href="https://hallsdeliverybearservice.com/" target="_blank">
        Halls Delivery Bear Service
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="halls delivery bear service polar white animal campaign" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7611" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/halls-delivery-bear-service-1.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Since Halls sweets deliver a cooling menthol sensation and since their icon is a polar bear, it makes sense that they play on the two. And Japanese consumers are suckers for anything cute, as we know.
      </p>
      <p>
       This service offers you the chance to meet a bear, whose cuddly charms will relieve you of your stress. One lucky winner will win the unique experience with the huggable bear.
      </p>
      <p>
       Applications are being accepted from April 20th to May 18th, 2:59 p.m.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="halls delivery bear service polar white animal campaign" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7612" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/halls-delivery-bear-service-2.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="halls delivery bear service polar white animal campaign" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7613" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/halls-delivery-bear-service-3.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Halls will dispatch their animal therapy to anywhere in Japan for free. As we can see from the marketing, this is being aimed squarely at kids and women in need of some
       <em>
        iyashi
       </em>
       (healing).
      </p>
      <p>
       The delivery service has been launched to celebrate the release of two new Halls products into Japans packed FMCG market.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="halls delivery bear service polar white animal campaign" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7614" height="" src="http://www.japantrends.com/japan-trends/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/halls-delivery-bear-service-4.jpg" width="620"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Halls demonstrated what the lucky winner can expect with voice actress Yoshino Nanjo.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="350" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/kY94nk_-zE4" width="620">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p>
       The question everyone wants to know the answer to is: Will it be a real polar bear or this fake one?
      </p>
      <p>
       There is a disclaimer at the bottom of the campaign site saying the polar bear feels handmade, which is an ambiguous way of saying it is a guy in a furry suit.
      </p>
      <p>
       Still, such gripes aside, compared to most Japanese marketing that predominantly resorts to lazy advertising with a pop star or celebrity, this is a cool idea.
      </p>

       </a>
