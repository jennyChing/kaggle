            <li>
             Glue stick
            </li>
           </ul>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Photograph of the child (head shot is best)
            </li>
           </ul>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Cup or circle template
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            How to Make This Hand-y Flower Craft
           </p>
           <ol>
            <li>
             Talk about flowers and how much they mean to mother on Mothers Day. Ask your child to pick out two colors of construction paper they love most. Its also okay if the child only wants one color.
            </li>
            <li>
             Trace around the childs hand on this colorful paper with a pencil. Depending on your childs age and size of hands will determine how many handprints to make. For the craft photographed above, we made six handprints for a child around seven years old.
            </li>
            <li>
             If your child has good cutting skills, let him cut out the handprints. If not, an adult can do this part.
            </li>
            <li>
             Arrange the handprints in a circular fashion to make the flower. Glue these together in the center, one overlapping the other.
            </li>
            <li>
             Use a cup or circle template to cut out the middle pod of the flower. Glue this in the center of the handprint flower.
            </li>
            <li>
             This is where you will place the childs photo. If it is small you can leave it square or cut it into a circle if its a larger picture. Glue this to the flower pod.
            </li>
            <li>
             Take green construction paper and cut a long rectangular stem. Then, cut two green leaves. Attach these to both sides of the stem.
            </li>
            <li>
             When dry, insert the stem into the handprints or to the very back of the hand-y flower. Glue this in place.
            </li>
            <li>
             Let all the part dry thoroughly.
            </li>
            <li>
             There are many ways to personalize this flower, but most of all, make sure to write the childs name and date somewhere on the flower craft. This is the perfect craft to tuck away in a memory box and bring out years to come to remember your precious child.
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            Think about poems or a special letter written to Mom. Adults will need to do the writing for very small children, where older kids can try to write the letter or at least sign their names.
            <br/>
            Here is a poem I put together and you are welcome to use it if you wish.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             The parts of this flower are parts of me,
            </em>
            <br/>
            <em>
             Made with my hands as you can see.
            </em>
            <br/>
            <em>
             As years go by the flower will show you,
            </em>
            <br/>
            <em>
             How I have grown and how much I love you!
            </em>
           </p>
