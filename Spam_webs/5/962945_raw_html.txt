     <h2>
      Chocolate Peanut Butter Cookie Bars
     </h2>
     <div class="postmeta">
      <em>
       by
      </em>
      Two Peas
      <em>
       on
      </em>
      February 2, 2011
     </div>
     <p>
      <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Cookie Bars" src="http://twopeasandtheirpod.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/cookiebar-2.jpg"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Today we have a special guest post from Jessica, who blogs at
      <a href="http://www.howsweeteats.com/" target="_blank">
       How Sweet It Is
      </a>
      . If you dont follow Jessicas blog, you better go take a look. She is always cooking and baking something delicious. She also writes over at the
      <a href="http://thepioneerwoman.com/tasty-kitchen-blog/" target="_blank">
       Tasty Kitchen blog
      </a>
      with me. Today Jessica is sharing one of her favorite recipes, Chocolate Peanut Butter Cookie Bars. I know these will soon be one of my favorites too:)
     </p>
     <p>
      <span id="more-8899">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      I could not be more excited to share one of my favorite recipes with you today. I am thrilled to be a guest on Maria and Joshs delightful blog  they have been tantalizing my taste buds for well over a year, and I often find myself drooling over Marias incredible cookie recipes, keeping a (very long!) mental list of which one will get baked next. My all time favorite are her
      <a href="http://twopeasandtheirpod.com/soft-gingersnap-cookie-recipe/" target="_blank">
       soft gingersnaps
      </a>
      and I urge you to try them as soon as you get in the kitchen!
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://twopeasandtheirpod.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/cookiebar1-1.jpg">
       <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8911" height="399" src="http://twopeasandtheirpod.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/cookiebar1-1.jpg" title="cookiebar1-1" width="600"/>
        <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.twopeasandtheirpod.com/chocolate-peanut-butter-cookie-bars/&amp;media=http://twopeasandtheirpod.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/cookiebar1-1.jpg&amp;description=Chocolate Peanut Butter Cookie Bars')">
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      When it came time to choose a recipe, it wasnt difficult to pick a favorite. If there is one combination that belongs together, it is easily chocolate and peanut butter. A favorite of mine since childhood, I have debated on combining every chocolate and peanut butter ingredient that has ever crossed my path. And Im not ashamed to admit that I often top a peanut butter spoon with a sprinkle of chocolate chips. A snack doesnt get any better than that.
     </p>
     <p>
      Since Maria is the cookie queen, I wanted to share one of my favorite cookie recipes with you in bar form. Cookie brownies, if you will. When you bake the cookie dough in a baking dish, the recipe yields a thick, chewy and delicious bar. It is nearly impossible to stop at one! I should know I attempted to store these in the freezer, but instead learned a very important lesson: no matter how frozen baked goods are, they still find their way into my mouth. Can you blame me?
     </p>
     <p>
      I hope you enjoy these chocolate peanut butter bars as much as I do!
     </p>
     <p>

      <span class="item">
       <h2 class="fn">
        Chocolate Peanut Butter Cookie Bars
       </h2>
      </span>
      <div class="time">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Yield:
        </strong>
        <span class="yield">
         12-16 bars
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Cook Time:
        </strong>
        <span class="cooktime">
         25-30 minutes
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <h3>
       Ingredients:
      </h3>
      <div class="ingredient">
       <p>
        1 cup butter, at room temperature
        <br/>
        1 1/2 cups sugar
        <br/>
        2 eggs
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon vanilla extract
        <br/>
        2 cups flour
        <br/>
        1/2 cup cocoa powder
        <br/>
        1 teaspoon baking soda
        <br/>
        1/2 teaspoon course sea salt
        <br/>
        1/2 cup peanut butter
        <br/>
        1 cup peanut butter chips
       </p>
      </div>
      <h3>
       Directions:
      </h3>
      <div class="instructions">
       <p>
        Preheat oven to 325.
       </p>
       <p>
        Cream butter, peanut butter, sugar, eggs and vanilla until fluffy, about 3 minutes. Add flour, cocoa powder, baking soda and salt and mix until just combined.
       </p>
       <p>
        Fold in peanut butter chips. Spread dough in a 9 x 13 baking dish sprayed with non-stick spray. Other sizes will work as well - they will just determine the thickness and cooking length of the bars.
       </p>
       <p>
        Bake at 325 for 25-30 minutes. If bars are not done (due to using a different sized dish), bake for 5-6 minute increments. Let cool completely before slicing.
       </p>
      </div>
