            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="d49cafe5d0feea4bb6e8a72399b43c17">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Rough Crafts Makes A Custom Harley I'd Actually Ride" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--O3zq53KY--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/bk2t0buqwyqlztpcnbsg.jpg" data-chomp-id="bk2t0buqwyqlztpcnbsg" data-format="jpg" height="424" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--O3zq53KY--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/bk2t0buqwyqlztpcnbsg.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p class="first-text" data-textannotation-id="9c088df1f86fc21f5d46eb8600852f7f">
             The idea of a Harley that rides as good as it looks ranks right up there with a supermodel that can run a Fortune 500 company  they exist, but it takes more work than it's worth. The Rough Crafts "Urban Calvary" is proof that the effort can pay off.
            </p>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="06b65cee2fc6d595b6baf07e11289148">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Rough Crafts Makes A Custom Harley I'd Actually Ride" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--rIcuv8qc--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/e9wskx4qtgvsgadjnfgl.jpg" data-chomp-id="e9wskx4qtgvsgadjnfgl" data-format="jpg" height="424" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--rIcuv8qc--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/e9wskx4qtgvsgadjnfgl.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="0844dd1bb60db5f49b7b25c6ae90a882">
             Taiwan-based Rough Crafts, led by Winston Yeh, has shot to the top of the Harley customizing game in the past few years, and their builds have garnered the notice of the Milwaukee mothership. In fact, H-D Taiwan reached out to the shop for a custom build  something that never happens  and the result is the Dyna Street Bob-based Urban Calvary.
            </p>
            <div class="ad-unit js_ad-unit hide-contents ad-mobile js_ad-mobile">
             <div class="ad-mobile-inner">
              <p class="ad-label proxima">
               <small class=" proxima">
                Advertisement
               </small>
              </p>
              <div class="ad-container js_ad-container" data-zone-type="MOBILE" id="ad-container-18614043">
              </div>
              <p class="ad-label-bottom">
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="cc6820f33f1d741f9d4eee43f3b1ba35">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Rough Crafts Makes A Custom Harley I'd Actually Ride" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--6su2HDPn--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/ince1pndyyfymwa9ilue.jpg" data-chomp-id="ince1pndyyfymwa9ilue" data-format="jpg" height="424" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--6su2HDPn--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/ince1pndyyfymwa9ilue.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="1dbaec4bdbd50539918eea5c18e2682f">
             Starting with a reworked chassis and the 1,689cc V-twin, Yeh slapped on a set of Super G carbs from S&amp;S and then fitted the party piece: a stunning titanium replica of the shop's Bomber 2-into-1 exhaust from Dog House.
            </p>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="897e59bbc8cd10abbab0f0a259f0dc89">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Rough Crafts Makes A Custom Harley I'd Actually Ride" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--ZlWK2Oqg--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/lzghsfhwnl3pmb1dxzjt.jpg" data-chomp-id="lzghsfhwnl3pmb1dxzjt" data-format="jpg" height="424" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--ZlWK2Oqg--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/lzghsfhwnl3pmb1dxzjt.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="0f7b12bb60dfa7f2b6589525cd94d4b7">
             A BDL open belt drive helps reduce weight, but the real work is in the suspension and brakes.
            </p>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="4fcc3125288fa9c30a0b4a4f48e1dc99">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Rough Crafts Makes A Custom Harley I'd Actually Ride" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--ERCFKkA0--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/pvtyjdcqaggc5hg6dosf.jpg" data-chomp-id="pvtyjdcqaggc5hg6dosf" data-format="jpg" height="424" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--ERCFKkA0--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/pvtyjdcqaggc5hg6dosf.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="fad03c56c5e32e95470da05874686297">
             <span>
              An aluminum swing arm is mounted out back, while upside-down Kawasaki sport bike forks have been swapped in, with custom Ohlins internals front and back.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="005e404f9fc6a4bcf2d9ca3de879eca2">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Rough Crafts Makes A Custom Harley I'd Actually Ride" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--ycbCxzkb--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/ibag8ipnzy11zinkjiym.jpg" data-chomp-id="ibag8ipnzy11zinkjiym" data-format="jpg" height="424" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--ycbCxzkb--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/ibag8ipnzy11zinkjiym.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="5f4df6fc074fc3035a5dfd347f9976af">
             The six-piston calipers are from ISR  a Swedish brand with a cult following  while the discs are made of a dark-colored metal composite from Lyndall Racing in the U.S. The Arlen Ness wheels are blacked-out to match the rest of the build, with a narrow dark grey tank and tail smoothing things out.
            </p>
            <p class="has-media media-640 has-image" data-textannotation-id="ad0860d9aabfe05b8b442f1e98d47056">
             <span class="lightBoxWrapper">
              <span class="img-border">
               <img alt="Rough Crafts Makes A Custom Harley I'd Actually Ride" data-asset-url="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--jHVFwiiB--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/zy4hup2sxkedpfzrw2dz.jpg" data-chomp-id="zy4hup2sxkedpfzrw2dz" data-format="jpg" height="424" src="http://i.kinja-img.com/gawker-media/image/upload/s--jHVFwiiB--/c_fit,fl_progressive,q_80,w_636/zy4hup2sxkedpfzrw2dz.jpg" width="636"/>
              </span>
              <span class="magnifier lightBox hide">
               <span class="icon icon-white icon-search-plus">
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p data-textannotation-id="6be59ee7736c1af3fbd25e8bfa0fc45d">
             It all adds up to one of the first Harley's I'd gladly romp around (save one of the AMA Pro Vance &amp; Hines bikes), and
             <a href="http://www.bikeexif.com/harley-davidson-dyna" target="_blank">
              BikeEXIF has a great interview with Yeh
             </a>
             about the build.
            </p>
