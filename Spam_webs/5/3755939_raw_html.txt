        <h2 class="post-title">
         Nursing Homes vs Home Care
        </h2>
        <div class="post-content">
         <div class="lr_horizontal_share" data-counter-url="https://www.hometeamcare.com/blog/guides/nursing-homes-vs-home-care/" data-share-url="https://www.hometeamcare.com/blog/guides/nursing-homes-vs-home-care/">
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://careguardian.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/home-care-vs-nursing-home-Copy.jpg">
           <img alt="home care vs nursing home - Copy" class="aligncenter wp-image-149 size-full" height="400" src="http://careguardian.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/home-care-vs-nursing-home-Copy.jpg" width="800"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          With social security not providing as much as hoped and knowing that two-thirds of seniors will become physically, or mentally incapacitated at some point in their lifetime, its important to review your
          <a href="http://careguardian.org/resources/senior-care-options/" title="Senior Care Options">
           Senior Care Options
          </a>
          . Nursing Homes are facilities that offer a high degree of medical support and care, and generally serve those seniors who require the most help in performing activities of daily living (ADLs). However, they are also very expensiveat nearly $100,000 per year. With
          <a href="http://careguardian.org/resources/why-home-care/" title="Why Home Care?">
           Home Care
          </a>
          , a family canrely on a home care company to understand the needs of the senior, develop a plan of care, and pro-actively monitor their health and day-to-day needs.
         </p>
         <p>

          </a>
         </p>
