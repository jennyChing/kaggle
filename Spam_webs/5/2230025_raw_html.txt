       Sources suggest Apple will debut new desktops later this month.
      </div>
      <span class="article-byline icon-pointy">
       By Scott Lowe
      </span>
      <aside class="ign-ad ign-ad-300x250 no-hero">
       <div class="sugarad" id="sugarad-300x250">
        <script type="text/javascript">
         googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('sugarad-300x250');});

      <p>
       As a company, Apple has a strong strategy for rolling out new products. With enough precedent to create a semi-regular schedule for product unveilings but virtually no pre-announcement allusions or teasing, Apple knows how to get fans to foam at the mouth for a glimpse of their latest batch of products. The most eagerly hoped for product from Apple this year will undoubtedly be a new iPhone in June or July, but in the meantime Apple may be rolling out some new desktops.
      </p>
      <p>
       <!-- poilib start -->
      </p>
      <div class="imageWithCaption">
       <img src="http://gearmedia.ign.com/gear/image/article/958/958480/macmini_01_1236037774.jpg"/>
      </div>
      <p>
       <!-- poilib end -->
       <br/>
       According to My Apple Guide, a site dedicated to all things iBranded, sources close to Apple say that there will be an event dedicated to new desktops on March 24. The site also went so far to say that the company will be unveiling a new Mac mini, Mac Pros packing Xeon processors, and iMacs with new NVIDIA chips.  In most cases we would write the rumor off as a shot in the proverbial dark, but earlier this week another Apple-centric site posted similar reports, citing the March 24 reveal.
      </p>
      <p>
       As far as rumors go, the viability of a late-March product unveil is pretty strong. The last time the iMacs underwent an update was April of last year, and it wouldn't be surprising to find Apple honoring an early spring tradition. While the iMacs could certainly do with a refresh, the Mac mini is by far the most deserving; the Mac mini was introduced in 2005 and the last update it received was in 2006. In the time that has elapsed since it was rolled out, Apple has continued production on the Mac mini with relatively successful sales.
      </p>
      <p>
       Of course nothing is official until it is official, and we'll be awaiting a formal announcement from Apple, but we wouldn't be surprised to see a desktop product refresh later this month.
      </p>
