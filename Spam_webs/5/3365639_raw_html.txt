     <p>
      <b>
       Aslan Usoyan
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">
       Georgian
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="ka" xml:lang="ka">
        
      </span>
      ;
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
       Russian
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">
        
      </span>
      ) (27 February 1937  16 January 2013), also known as
      <b>
       Grandpa Hassan
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
       Russian
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">
        
      </span>
      <i>
       Ded Hasan
      </i>
      ) was a
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_mafia" title="Russian mafia">
       Russian mafia
      </a>
      boss, an ethnic Kurdish
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yezidi" title="Yezidi">
       Yezidi
      </a>
      mobster and
      <a href="/wiki/Thief_in_law" title="Thief in law">
       thief in law
      </a>
      , who began his career operating in
      <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">
       Georgia
      </a>
      , continued in
      <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">
       Moscow
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ural_(region)" title="Ural (region)">
       Ural
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">
       Siberia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">
       Uzbekistan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar" title="Krasnodar">
       Krasnodar
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">
       Sochi
      </a>
      , and other parts of the
      <a href="/wiki/Post-Soviet_states" title="Post-Soviet states">
       former Soviet Union
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      According to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">
        The Economist
       </a>
      </i>
      , he was "reputed to be Russias mafia boss."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Criminal_acts">
       Criminal acts
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aslan_Usoyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Criminal acts">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Starting in 2007, soyan was embroiled in a gang war with Georgian mobster
      <a href="/wiki/Tariel_Oniani" title="Tariel Oniani">
       Tariel Oniani
      </a>
      , who was seeking to reestablish himself in Moscow. Several of soyan's top lieutenants were killed including the Armenian national Alek Minalyan, a man allegedly in charge of extorting construction firms working on the
      <a href="/wiki/2014_Winter_Olympics" title="2014 Winter Olympics">
       2014 Winter Olympics
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In July 2008 police raided Oniani's yacht as a meeting took place amongst the criminal leaders in an attempt to settle the conflict. soyan was not however amongst those detained. He later gave an interview to a newspaper, denying the stories of escalating violence and stated that "We are a peaceful people and don't bother anybody, we are for peace in order to prevent lawlessness".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Vyacheslav_Ivankov" title="Vyacheslav Ivankov">
       Vyacheslav Ivankov
      </a>
      was brought in to mediate the conflict, in which he sided with soyan's faction. He was however shot by a sniper while leaving a Moscow restaurant in July 2009, and died of his wounds in October that year. Although he did not attend, soyan sent an elaborate wreath to Ivankov's funeral saying "To our brother from Grandpa Hassan".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In April 2010, soyan was arrested by Ukrainian security forces after entering the country illegally using false documents. His business in Ukraine was allegedly connected to a rift with an Armenian organized crime group.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On 16 September 2010, soyan was shot by a 9mm calibre bullet fired by an unidentified assailant in central Moscow, but survived the attack along with his bodyguard who was also wounded. It was at first announced to the press that soyan had died to ensure his safety.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the early 2010s the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Obama_administration" title="Obama administration">
       Obama administration
      </a>
      placed sanctions on members of the alleged criminal organisation the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brothers%E2%80%99_Circle" title="Brothers Circle">
       Brothers Circle
      </a>
      . It has been speculated that the so-called Circle is a stand-in for soyan's network.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Alleged_ties_to_PKK">
       Alleged ties to PKK
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aslan_Usoyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Alleged ties to PKK">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      soyan, an ethnic Kurd, was rumoured to be one of the suppliers of illicit weapons to the Kurdish separatist movement
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PKK" title="PKK">
       PKK
      </a>
      that is fighting
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish%E2%80%93Turkish_conflict" title="KurdishTurkish conflict">
       an armed struggle against the Turkish state
      </a>
      for an autonomous
      <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan" title="Kurdistan">
       Kurdistan
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Human_rights_of_Kurdish_people_in_Turkey" title="Human rights of Kurdish people in Turkey">
       cultural and political
      </a>
      rights for the
      <a href="/wiki/Kurds_in_Turkey" title="Kurds in Turkey">
       Kurds in Turkey
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tahiri_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-tahiri-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Death">
       Death
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aslan_Usoyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      On 16 January 2013, soyan was shot in the head by a sniper perched on the sixth floor of an adjacent apartment building after leaving a restaurant which served as his 'office', and despite efforts of his bodyguards and ambulance workers he died en route to the hospital.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The family decided to fly soyan's body to be buried in his native Tbilisi, but the
      <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi_International_Airport" title="Tbilisi International Airport">
       Tbilisi International Airport
      </a>
      refused to accept the plane.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His death is thought to be likely to spur chaos in the criminal world. The day Usoyan was shot dead, an Armenian crime fiction writer, Sergey Galoyan said, in a conversation with a local news website,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      that the mafia king's murder might be linked to a certain unrest in the criminal world, particularly the construction of the Sochi Olympic facilities which are said to have attracted considerable investments.
     </p>
     <p>
      The main suspects behind Usoyan's murder are
      <a href="/wiki/Tariel_Oniani" title="Tariel Oniani">
       Tariel Oniani
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Rovshan_Janiev" title="Rovshan Janiev">
       Rovshan Janiev
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
