     <p>
      The
      <b>
       <i>
        Dallas Observer
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Limited_partnership" title="Limited partnership">
        LP
       </a>
      </b>
      is a free
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alternative_weekly" title="Alternative weekly">
       alternative weekly
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">
       newspaper
      </a>
      distributed in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dallas-Fort_Worth" title="Dallas-Fort Worth">
       Dallas-Fort Worth
      </a>
      area and headquartered in
      <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">
       Dallas
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At its inception in October, 1980, by partners Ken Kirk and Bob Walton, it was conceived as a weekly local arts and cinema review publication, with the credo "Advocate for Excellence in Arts and Entertainment" on the cover. For a time during the early years, the paper switched to a biweekly publishing schedule. In 1985, the paper reverted to a weekly schedule and began concentrating on local news as well as the arts. While it is now known for its investigative stories of the local government, it also covers local
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sports" title="Sports">
       sports
      </a>
      stories,
      <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">
       restaurants
      </a>
      , events, and
      <a href="/wiki/Concert" title="Concert">
       concerts
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The former Mayor of Dallas,
      <a href="/wiki/Laura_Miller" title="Laura Miller">
       Laura Miller
      </a>
      , previously worked as a reporter for the
      <i>
       Dallas Observer
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1991 the
      <i>
       Observer
      </i>
      was bought by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Times_Media" title="New Times Media">
       New Times Media
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2005, New Times acquired
      <a href="/wiki/Village_Voice_Media" title="Village Voice Media">
       Village Voice Media
      </a>
      , and changed its name to
      <a href="/wiki/Village_Voice_Media" title="Village Voice Media">
       Village Voice Media
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In September 2012, Village Voice Media executives Scott Tobias, Christine Brennan and Jeff Mars bought Village Voice Media's papers and associated web properties from its founders and formed
      <a href="/wiki/Voice_Media_Group" title="Voice Media Group">
       Voice Media Group
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       Dallas Observer
      </i>
      is a member of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Association_of_Alternative_Newsweeklies" title="Association of Alternative Newsweeklies">
       Association of Alternative Newsweeklies
      </a>
      .
     </p>
