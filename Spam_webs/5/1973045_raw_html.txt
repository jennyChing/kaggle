      <h2>
       Five Gorgeous Script Fonts
      </h2>
      <div style="margin-top:1px;height:35px">
       <span style="float:left;">
        <g:plusone href="http://www.devlounge.net/design/five-gorgeous-script-fonts" size="medium">

      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        My mother did professional calligraphy for a time, and I dabbled quite a bit in it myself- which is why Im always drawn to script fonts. More stylish than practical, theyre best used in small doses- headlines or posters, for example. Here are five of my current favorite script fonts, displaying more of my favorite design-related quotes. Theyre all free, of course:
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="font-killigraphy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3719" height="300" src="http://www.devlounge.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/font-killigraphy.gif" title="font-killigraphy" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.urbanfonts.com/fonts/Killigraphy.htm">
          Killigraphy
         </a>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        This one by Rob Dobi has been around for a while, but still packs quite a punch for me. Its beautiful, spiky letters are elegant but bold.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="font-olho" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3721" height="300" src="http://www.devlounge.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/font-olho.gif" title="font-olho" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.urbanfonts.com/fonts/Olho_De_Boi.htm">
          Olho de Boi
         </a>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        A more recent find for me, Olho de Boi won me over right away. Its a little bit distressed, with imperfections that make it seem more handwritten than most computer fonts. I particularly like its capitals, which have a bit of swash to them. A Billy Argel Font.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="font-promocyja" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3722" height="300" src="http://www.devlounge.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/font-promocyja.gif" title="font-promocyja" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.fontsquirrel.com/fonts/Promocyja">
          Promocyja
         </a>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Promocyja by Gluks clean lines make this one perhaps a little more accessible, and more readable, than most script-type fonts. This is a pretty font (and I love pretty fonts!)- and is one Ive been searching for a reason to use lately.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="font-miama" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3720" height="300" src="http://www.devlounge.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/font-miama.gif" title="font-miama" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.fontsquirrel.com/fonts/Miama">
          Miama
         </a>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Heres one Id
        <em>
         love
        </em>
        my handwriting to look like. Linus Romers Miama is long, luxurious and clear enough to use in smaller sizes- although, as with all script fonts, I would suggest
        <em>
         not
        </em>
        using it in long paragraphs. Elegant and beautiful, without being too fancy.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="font-quilline" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3723" height="300" src="http://www.devlounge.net/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/font-quilline.gif" title="font-quilline" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.urbanfonts.com/fonts/Quilline_Script_Thin.htm">
          Quilline Script
         </a>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Ive had Quilline Script in my font library for some time now- its one Ive used quite often, because: a) Its pretty!, b) Its shorter than other script fonts, so I dont need to increase the leading too much, and c) Its modern and readable, and looks great paired with sans-serif fonts.
       </p>
       <p>
        What are
        <em>
         your
        </em>
        favorite script fonts?
       </p>
