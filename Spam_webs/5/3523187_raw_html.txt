         <figure class="featured-img prem-template-c image-container">
          <img alt="Prime Rib with Horseradish Sauce" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="524" src="http://cdn.mademan.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/primerib.jpg" width="970"/>
         </figure>
         <p>
          While this recipe is technically about a whiskey horseradish sauce, its really more about what that sauce is for: a huge hunk of meat known as a prime rib roast. Its the primal cut of beef  essentially three massive bone-in ribeyes in a pre-butchered state. And its one of the most impressive cuts to serve a crowd, especially around the holidays.
         </p>
         <p>
          The booze comes in the form of a spicy horseradish cream sauce flavored with lots of green onions, black pepper, and, of course, whiskey. A more perfect and decadent beef condiment has yet to be invented. We recommend making the cream sauce while the rib roast is resting, as its best served right away.
         </p>
         <p>
          We made our prime roast on a rotisserie but it can be done on the grill or in the oven. Any way you cook it, your guests will be happy (unless you overcook it; that will ruin Christmas for everyone).
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Prime Rib
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Prime Rib Roast, 5-6 pounds
          <br/>
          Salt and pepper, a lot
          <br/>
          Olive oil
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Massage the outside of the meat with olive oil then aggressively season it with salt and pepper. Let the roast sit at room temperature for one hour before cooking.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Prime Rib with Horseradish Sauce" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-165231" height="524" src="http://cdn.mademan.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/primerib-5.jpg" width="970"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Set your oven or grill to 375F. Cook the meat for around 2 hours, until the internal temperature of the meat is 135F.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Prime Rib with Horseradish Sauce" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-165233" height="524" src="http://cdn.mademan.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/primerib-4-2.jpg" width="970"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Remove the roast to a platter, then cover with aluminum foil and allow it to rest for 30 minutes before slicing and serving.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Prime Rib with Horseradish Sauce" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-165235" height="524" src="http://cdn.mademan.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/primerib-2.jpg" width="970"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Whiskey Horseradish Sauce
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          1 stick butter
          <br/>
          5 green onions, sliced
          <br/>
           cup whiskey
          <br/>
          1 cup heavy whipping cream
          <br/>
          1-3 tablespoons freshly-grated horseradish, depending on your desired level of spice
          <br/>
          2 tablespoons sage Chiffone
          <br/>
          Freshly ground black pepper
          <br/>
          Kosher salt
         </p>
         <p>
          Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium-low heat. Add the onions and aggressively add salt and pepper. Add the whiskey and allow the alcohol to cook out, about one minute. Then add the whipping cream and horseradish and bring to a low simmer and cook for about 5 minutes, allowing it to thicken. Add the sage and more salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat and serve over the prime rib ASAP.
         </p>
