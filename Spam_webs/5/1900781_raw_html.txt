             <a href="http://www.surlatable.com/gs/bissinger-chocolate-liqueur-cups.shtml" rel="nofollow">
              chocolate liqueur cup
             </a>
             filled with milk = fun + yum!
            </p>
            <h3>
             <span>
              Oreo Cupcake Shout-Out
             </span>
            </h3>
            <p>
             Before I detail how to make the Oreo cupcake and also present some key tips on how to eat it without getting milk everywhere, I have to give a major shout-out to Kendall from
             <a href="http://resopuhleese.blogspot.com/">
              Res-o-puh-leese
             </a>
             . I get reader questions all the time, and Im always happy to help where I can. However, I LOVE answering questions that are really challenging  questions that I have never thought of before.
            </p>
            <p>
             Kendall emailed me saying, Im co-hosting a St. Patricks Day party next month, and I had the idea to make shot glass cupcakescupcakes hollowed out around a container holding some Baileys. I was immediately enamored with the concept of a cupcake with a built-in cup. I decided to try it out with more of a kid theme  but wouldnt the Baileys idea be fantastic?! Be sure to visit
             <a href="http://resopuhleese.blogspot.com/">
              Res-o-puh-leese
             </a>
             , Kendall clearly has some great ideas!
            </p>
            <h2>
             Now.. onto to the Oreo cupcake details
            </h2>
            <p>
             <span>
              <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/__IP6S4qrDTw/SaVr2Fr78YI/AAAAAAAAB-s/MVSMVaNbyq8/s1600-h/liqueur+cups+2.jpg">
               <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/liqueur+cups+2.jpg"/>
              </a>
              Aside from Oreos,
              <a href="http://www.surlatable.com/gs/bissinger-chocolate-liqueur-cups.shtml" rel="nofollow">
               Bissingers liqueur cups
              </a>
              were the key ingredient in these Oreo cupcakes. The cups are the perfect size to fit inside the cupcakes and they are able to hold the milk (which soon turns into chocolate milk) without it leaking into the cupcake.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/__IP6S4qrDTw/SaVrs1GGZAI/AAAAAAAAB-k/LyytEJEwBRI/s1600-h/liqueur+cups+1.jpg">
              <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/liqueur+cups+1.jpg"/>
             </a>
             The liqueur cups come in a 12 pack and you can buy them at
             <a href="http://www.surlatable.com/gs/bissinger-chocolate-liqueur-cups.shtml" rel="nofollow">
              Sur La Table
             </a>
             . (UPDATE: It looks like they are sold out. You could buy this
             <a href="http://astore.amazon.com/httpwwwstldio-20/detail/B001MNTYR4">
              similar product on Amazon
             </a>
             .) Since Im in St. Louis, we have
             <a href="http://www.bissingers.com/">
              Bissingers shops
             </a>
             all over and I was able to pick them up locally. You could, of course, make your own chocolate cups. I dont imagine it would be all that hard  just time intensive.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/__IP6S4qrDTw/SaVrV3XsYLI/AAAAAAAAB-c/MNOTwyW4o2o/s1600-h/oreo+cupcake+hole.jpg">
              <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/oreo+cupcake+hole.jpg"/>
             </a>
             The Oreo cupcakes rose nice and high, which left tons of room to put the chocolate cup in the cupcake. If you are going to use this technique with a different kind of cupcake, make sure that the cupcake is tall and domed, not flat. Before putting the liqueur cup in the cupcake, make sure that the cupcake is COMPLETELY cooled. You do not want to melt the chocolate.
            </p>
            <p>
             Use a small paring knife to cut a wedge out of the cupcake and then stick the cup in the void. I used some cake from the wedges to make the mouths on my cupcakes. The wedges are also perfect to simply snack on!
            </p>
            <p>
             I found that the easiest way to fill the cup with milk was to use a small funnel. Dont fill the cups until right before you are ready to serve the cupcakes.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/__IP6S4qrDTw/SaVq6enN_WI/AAAAAAAAB-U/J6BTFXlTw4w/s1600-h/Oreo+Dunking+Sequence.jpg">
              <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/Oreo+Dunking+Sequence.jpg"/>
             </a>
             Just like there are many ways to eat an Oreo, there are many ways to eat an Oreo cupcake. However, there is one key guideline: Do not bite into the cup until you have drunk all the milk or you will end up with mess! I had twelve cupcakes with cups and only one taster ended up with a milky mess -Alan of
             <a href="http://www.patric-chocolate.com/store/">
              Patric Chocolate
             </a>
             . Alan claimed that it had nothing to do with him biting into the cup and everything to do with his cup having a crack. Im still inclined to blame user error.
            </p>
            <p>
             You can use up the milk by dunking Oreos in the cup as shown above. This works best if you have mini Oreos. I used my
             <a href="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/2009/02/oreo-cookies-made-from-scratch-just.html">
              homemade Oreos
             </a>
             . You could also just break up full-sized ones up into pieces.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/__IP6S4qrDTw/SaVqL9P4VaI/AAAAAAAAB-M/XnxFX-09kHU/s1600-h/oreo+cake+dunking.jpg">
              <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/oreo+cake+dunking.jpg"/>
             </a>
             Another idea is to break of the bottom of the cupcake and dunk pieces of the cake in the milk. To get those last few sips, just hold the cupcake up to your face and drink! Dont worry if you get frosting on your face  just look in the mirror, smile, and then lick it off!
            </p>
            <h2>
             <span>
              Oreo Cupcake Recipe
             </span>
            </h2>
            <p>
             I got the Oreo cupcake recipe from
             <a href="http://lcsa99.wordpress.com/2008/11/19/you-cant-have-just-one/">
              Heaven is Chocolate, Cheese, and Carbs
             </a>
             . It is a really good, basic chocolate cupcake with Oreo bits mixed in. You could use any chocolate or yellow cake cupcake recipe you like in place of this one and simply mix in Oreo bits. I converted the recipe (shown below) from its original metric to US customary units, and added my notes.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="zlrecipe-container-border" id="zlrecipe-container-268" style="border: 1px dotted;">
             <div class="serif zlrecipe" id="zlrecipe-container" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
              <div id="zlrecipe-innerdiv">
               <div class="item b-b">
                <div class="zlrecipe-print-link fl-r">
                 <a class="butn-link" href="javascript:void(0);" onclick="zlrPrint('zlrecipe-container-268', 'http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/plugins/ziplist-recipe-plugin/'); return false" title="Print this recipe">
                  Print
                 </a>
                </div>
                <div class="b-b h-1 strong" id="zlrecipe-title" itemprop="name">
                 Oreo Cupcakes  With a Built-In Cup for Milk
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="zlmeta zlclear">
                <div class="fl-l width-50">
                </div>
                <div class="fl-l width-50">
                 <p id="zlrecipe-yield">
                  Yield:
                  <span itemprop="recipeYield">
                   18 cupcakes*
                  </span>
                 </p>
                </div>
                <div class="zlclear">
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="img-desc-wrap">
                <p class="t-a-c hide-card hide-print">
                 <img alt="Oreo Cupcakes  With a Built-In Cup for Milk" class="photo" itemprop="image" src="http://www.cupcakeproject.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/oreo+cupcake.jpg" style="width: 250px;" title="Oreo Cupcakes  With a Built-In Cup for Milk"/>
                </p>
               </div>
               <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
                Ingredients
               </p>
               <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
                 3/4 C butter, room temperature
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
                 2 1/4 C brown sugar
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
                 8 oz dark chocolate, melted
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
                 3 large eggs, separated
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
                 2 t vanilla extract
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
                 2 C all-purpose flour
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
                 1 t baking powder
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-7" itemprop="ingredients">
                 1 t baking soda
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-8" itemprop="ingredients">
                 1 3/4 C milk
                </li>
                <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-9" itemprop="ingredients">
                 8 Oreo cookies, smashed to bits
                </li>
               </ul>
               <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
                Instructions
               </p>
               <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Beat butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Mix in egg yolks.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Mix in vanilla and chocolate.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 In a separate bowl, mix the flour, baking soda, and baking powder.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Alternate adding the flour mixture and milk to the butter/sugar mixture.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-5" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Stir in Oreos.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-6" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 In a separate bowl, whip egg whites until peaks form.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-7" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Fold the batter into the egg whites.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-8" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Fill cupcake liners almost to the top - remember that you want these to be tall.
                </li>
                <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-9" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
                 Bake for 20-25 minutes at 350 F or until a toothpick comes out clean.
                </li>
               </ol>
               <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-notes">
                Notes
               </p>
               <div id="zlrecipe-notes-list">
                <p class="notes">
                 *Only 12 got the special cup treatment. The rest just got slathered with Oreo cream cheese frosting.
                </p>
