           Interview With Aaron Sorkin
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="postauthor">
          Author:
          <a href="http://scriptwrecked.com/author/scriptwrecked/" rel="author" title="Posts by Trevor Mayes">

        <div class="entry">
         <p>
          Joshua Stecker, West Coast Editor of Script Magazine, interviews screenwriter Aaron Sorkin (
          <em>
           The Social Network, A Few Good Men, Charlie Wilsons War, The American President, The West Wing
          </em>
          ).
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are some of the topics Sorkin talks about in this 20 minute interview:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <img alt="Aaron Sorkin Interview" class="alignright size-full wp-image-3727" height="150" src="http://scriptwrecked.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/AaronSorkinInterview.jpg" title="Aaron Sorkin Interview" width="200"/>
           The connection between his great teachers and his success
          </li>
          <li>
           How he learned to write screenplays
          </li>
          <li>
           The important lesson William Goldman taught him
          </li>
          <li>
           What activity he does for inspiration
          </li>
          <li>
           What medium he prefers to write in
          </li>
          <li>
           His screenwriting weakness
          </li>
          <li>
           The basic rules of drama
          </li>
          <li>
           Advice for aspiring screenwriters
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          My favorite quote from the interview (especially in light of the recent controversy over whether screenwriting can be taught):
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          I can trace so much of what I do every day, when Im writing, to what I was taught back then by my teachers at Syracuse.
         </p>
