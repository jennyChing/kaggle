        <span class="st_reddit_hcount" st_title="31 Strange But True Laws Still On The Books" st_url="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/blog/31-strange-bizarre-true-laws/">
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span class="st_stumbleupon_hcount" st_title="31 Strange But True Laws Still On The Books" st_url="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/blog/31-strange-bizarre-true-laws/">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        All across the Internet, you will be able to find lists and lists of strange laws that either once existed or still exist depending on whos doing the reporting. But what we found when looking at these examples, is that many either never existed or cannot be proven to have existed without searching through old, dusty print archives and records offices around the world.
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Such is the Internet. People hear something without substantiation, it lodges in their brains, and gets passed along as fact.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        Thankfully, there are authorities on the matter with legitimate legal training  people who can say that theyve been there and seen the records for themselves and can confirm which bizarre laws existed and which legal oddities did not.
       </p>
       <p>
        Two we turned to for
        <strong>
         31 Strange But True Laws That Are Still On The Books!
        </strong>
        include communications law professor and LegalZoom contributor
        <a href="https://www.legalzoom.com/articles/top-craziest-laws-still-on-the-books" target="_blank">
         Stephanie Morrow
        </a>
        and NYC attorney Nathan Belofsky, whose
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Book-Strange-Curious-Legal-Oddities/dp/0399535950/ref=la_B004APTTRG_1_1?s=books&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1426037741&amp;sr=1-1" target="_blank">
         The Book of Strange and Curious Legal Oddities: Pizza Police, Illicit Fishbowls, and Other Anomalies of the Law That Make Us All Unsuspecting Criminals
        </a>
        , is pretty much the Bible on the subject.
       </p>
       <p>
        Included below, as the title says, are strange but true laws that are still on the books. Some hail from municipalities, some are state, some federal. But all are funny! Hold on to your hats.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         1. No Margarine In Wisconsin!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Well, thats at least how many websites will report it when talking about strange laws.
        <em>
         The reality is that you can get margarine within the state of Wisconsin
        </em>
        ,
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         but it comes with a series of strings attached
        </span>
        . For instance, there are stipulations regarding the size of the container (no more than 1 pound) and the size of font on the packaging (must plainly say margarine and be at least as large as any other type or lettering on the label in a color of print which clearly contrasts with its background). Youll find it on the books as
        <a href="https://docs.legis.wisconsin.gov/statutes/statutes/97/18" target="_blank">
         Wisconsin State Statute 97.18
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws1.jpg">
         <img alt="I can't believe it's not legal - strange laws graphic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32315" height="333" src="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws1.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image:
        <a href="http://www.oddee.com/_media/imgs/articles2/a96828_a509_margarine-illegal.jpg" rel="nofollow">
         Oddee
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         2. Childrens Lemonade Stands Are Illegal.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Childrens lemonade stands are part of a longstanding rite of childhood. Parents use them to teach their kids lessons on the value of working for a dollar through this method. But for a growing number of municipalities, they are against the law. Take
        <a href="http://www.kcrg.com/news/local/Coralville-Police-Shutdown-Several-Childrens-Lemonade-Stands-126592563.html" target="_blank">
         this story
        </a>
        from Coralville, Iowa, in which
        <em>
         4-year-old Abigail Krutsinger had her stand shut down by police 30 minutes after she opened it
        </em>
        . Forbes has
        <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/erikkain/2011/08/03/the-inexplicable-war-on-lemonade-stands/" target="_blank">
         several other similar cases here
        </a>
        if you think you can keep your blood pressure under control.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         3. Blue Laws
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Alabamas fake mustache law, wherein
        <em>
         its said to be illegal to make a person in church laugh by wearing a fake mustache on Sunday
        </em>
        , is a prime example of Blue Laws. (Never mind the fact that there is no mention of mustaches or church in the
        <a href="http://codes.lp.findlaw.com/alcode/13A/12/1/13A-12-1" target="_blank">
         actual statute
        </a>
        .) The spirit of the law is there, and it says that out of religious respect  mostly to Christians  certain activities, like selling alcohol, should be prohibited on Sundays.
       </p>
       <p>
        Stephanie Morrow shared one from Salem,
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         West Virginia
        </span>
        , where
        <em>
         its against the law to eat candy less than an hour and a half before church service
        </em>
        . Another, in Winona Lake, Wisconsin, prohibits the eating of ice cream at a counter on Sunday. And in a handful of states, Morrow claims, Marbles, Dominoes, and yo-yos are banned on Sunday.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         4. Breaking The Speed Limit  By Going Too Slow.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         You cant get a drivers license without knowing that its unacceptable to go faster than the posted speed limit
        </em>
        . While police may allow five to 10 miles over, anything beyond that will probably get you a warning or a ticket. That said, most motorists  even those whove been driving for a very long time  seem unaware that
        <em>
         you can also break the speed limit by going too slow
        </em>
        . In comments to
        <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2007/LIVING/wayoflife/12/26/unusual.laws/" target="_blank">
         CNN
        </a>
        , Pete Kim of the California Highway Patrol admitted that it wasnt a common thing, but it was possible to get a ticket if youre blocking traffic or creating a road hazard on the freeway. (So a bit more pedal, slow-pokes!)
        <span id="more-32325">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         5. Bingo Sessions Must Not Last Longer Than Five Hours
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        According to the North Carolina Department of Public Safetys
        <a href="https://www.ncdps.gov/div/ALE/bingolaws.pdf" target="_blank">
         Rule 14-309.5
        </a>
        , the number of bingo sessions conducted or sponsored by an exempt organization shall be limited to two sessions per week and such
        <em>
         sessions must not exceed a period of five hours each
        </em>
        per session.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws5.jpg">
         <img alt="Bingo Sessions Strange Laws Graphic" class="size-full wp-image-32316 aligncenter" height="333" src="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws5.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image:
        <a href="https://howlingforjustice.files.wordpress.com/2010/12/bingo-pointssouth-net.jpg" rel="nofollow">
         Howling For Justice
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         6. Canadas No Penny Law
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        While our neighbors to the north do not forbid the use of pennies, Belofsky notes that they do have limitations for accepted use. That means if Indiana citizen James Sanders had tried to pay his $702 fine with more than 70,000 pennies  as noted in this piece from
        <a href="http://www.jconline.com/article/20130915/NEWS/309150036/Man-uses-common-cents-to-pay-fine?gcheck=1&amp;nclick_check=1" target="_blank">
         JC Online
        </a>
         he would have been flatly refused.
        <em>
         Canada will allow a person to pay with no more than 25 pennies
        </em>
        . If more is owed, youll have to switch to something bigger.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         7. Imprisonment For Fake Weather Reports
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Federal law dictates that anyone who issues or publishes a fake weather report and claims its from the Weather Bureau is subject to imprisonment for not more than ninety days. Simply being bad at your meteorology job doesnt count as fake, though.
        <a href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2074" target="_blank">
         (18 U.S.C. Section 2074)
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         8. Fortune-telling Bad, Unless Its Healthcare-Related
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        One obviously antiquated New Orleans law  especially if youve ever been there to see just how many people are making a living at it  forbids the business of fortune-telling, astrology, or palmistry to settle lovers quarrels  locate buried or hidden treasures  remove evil influences  reveal secrets  or to bring together the bitterest enemies converting them into staunchest friends. However, nothing herein contained shall apply to any branch of medical science. (Probably not covered by your ACA policy,
        <a href="https://www.municode.com/library/la/new_orleans/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=PTIICO_CH54CRCO_ARTVIOFAFPUGE_DIV1GE_S54-312FO" target="_blank">
         New Orleans Municipal Code Section 54-312
        </a>
        ).
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws8.jpg">
         <img alt="Fortune-Telling Strange Laws Graphic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32317" height="333" src="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws8.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image:
        <a href="http://rodtanchanco.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/512px-Palmistry_Chiromancy_Palm_Reading.jpg" rel="nofollow">
         Rod Tanchanco, M.D.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         9.  31. Roundup Of Forgotten U.S. Laws
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Two and a half centuries is a long time to be making laws. Its only natural that in our nations history, some of the crazier ones would slip through the cracks and remain on the books in the 21st Century. In most cases,
        <strong>
         its entirely possible that lawmakers arent even aware that these are the laws they should still be enforcing
        </strong>
        . Because several of these dont need a lot of fleshing out, weve done a quick roundup with hat tips to
        <em>
         Morrow
        </em>
        and
        <em>
         Belofsky
        </em>
        . Not all of these will be outdated, but the vast majority are. For the few scattered in that take their cues from the 21st Century  youd have to ask the lawmakers.
       </p>
       <p>
        9. In Pennsylvania, you actually have to dismantle your car and hide it if youre sharing the road with a team of skittish horses. (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p>
        10. In Missouri, you cannot, under any circumstances,
        <em>
         drive with an uncaged bear in your car
        </em>
        . (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws10.jpg">
         <img alt="Bears Can't Drive Cars Strange Laws Graphic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32318" height="333" src="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws10.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image:
        <a href="http://wikiality.wikia.com/Bear_Is_Driving" rel="nofollow">
         Wikiality
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        11. Floridas
        <em>
         elephant riders get no special treatment
        </em>
        . If planning on parking their elephants at a paid meter, they must pop in some change. (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p>
        12. North Dakota forbids serving beer and pretzels at the same time. (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p>
        13. Oklahoma warns that
        <em>
         you can expect to pay a fine if you make ugly faces at someones dog
        </em>
        . (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p>
        14. Illinois forbids you from giving your pup a lighted cigar no matter how much he wants one. (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws14.jpg">
         <img alt="Dogs can't have Lighted Cigars in Mouth Strange Law Graphic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32319" height="333" src="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws14.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image:
        <a href="http://www.worldofstock.com/slides/PPT1487.jpg" rel="nofollow">
         World of Stock
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        15. In Cleveland, Ohio,
        <em>
         women are strictly forbidden from wearing patent leather shoes
        </em>
        , particularly if they are wearing a skirt because it could unfairly tempt a man. Its known as the citys peep show ordinance. (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p>
        16. Florida is also tough on the unmarried woman, who plans on doing her parachuting on Sundays. Not a problem if youre in the company of your husband, though. (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p>
        17. Ladies, plan on chopping off all your hair for the summertime? Michigan says no unless you first get your husbands permission. (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p>
        18. Memphis, Tennessee, doesnt have a problem with
        <em>
         women driving
        </em>
        , but they
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         must be proceeded by a man waving a red flag
        </span>
        . (Morrow)
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws18.jpg">
         <img alt="Women Can't Drive Unless Preceeded by a Man Strange Law Graphic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32320" height="333" src="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws18.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image:
        <a href="http://www.nextstepbeauty.co.uk" rel="nofollow">
         YouTube
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        19. In New Hampshire, ice dealers who dont weigh the ice on request are subject to a $50 fine (Ice Section 434:7). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        20. Also in New Hampshire,
        <em>
         you cannot sell a lightning rod without a license
        </em>
        . (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws20.jpg">
         <img alt="Can't sell a Lightning Rod without a License Strange Law Graphic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32321" height="333" src="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws20.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image:
        <a href="http://thesubtimes.com/2011/09/28/bradbury-play-makes-nw-premier-at-lakewood-playhouse/" rel="nofollow">
         The Suburban Times
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        21. Grand Forks, North Dakota, forbids the throwing of a snowball on public or private property (City Code Section 9-0123). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        22. Also in Grand Forks,
        <em>
         you cannot throw candy from a float during a parade
        </em>
        (City Code Section 9-0211). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        23.
        <em>
         Alabama
        </em>
        frowns on taking a long time in the voting booth.
        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
         If you take longer than four minutes, you can be asked if you need assistance
        </span>
        . If not, and there is a high volume of voters waiting, you are given one more minute to vote and then asked to leave (Code of Alabama, Section 17-9-13(b) (2006)). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        24. Deadwood, South Dakota, doesnt allow one to engage in psychic powers, spirits, seership, palmistry, necromancy, science cards, charms, potions, magnetism, or Oriental mysteries (5.52.010). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        25. Rhode Islands Mayhem laws will slap you with one to 20 years in prison if you decide to cut or bite off the limb or member of another human being. It does have to be intentional, though (Section 11-29-1). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        26.
        <em>
         Joliet, Illinois, has a real problem with you saying the name of their town wrong
        </em>
        , and theyve even gone so far as to draft a special ordinance that defines the proper pronunciation. The only official, correct and proper pronunciation of the name of this city shall be Jo-li-et: the accent on the first syllable, with the o in the first syllable pronounced in its long sound, as in the words so, no, and foe, and any other pronunciations to be discouraged as interfering with the desired uniformity in respect to the proper pronunciation of the name of this city (Code of Ordinances of City of Joliet, Ch. 2, Art. 1, Section 2-8). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <a href="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws26.jpg">
         <img alt="Correct Pronounciation of Joliet Strange Law Graphic" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-32322" height="333" src="http://www.robertreeveslaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/strangelaws26.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Image:
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joliet,_Illinois" rel="nofollow">
         Wikipedia Commons
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        27. In Massachusetts,
        <em>
         using a boom box without earphones in public can get you tossed in the clink
        </em>
        for as much as 30 days (Crimes Against the Person, Section 265-42). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        28. In Manteno, Illinois, No person shall drop, throw or place any used facial tissue or paper handkerchief upon any public way (Manteno Village Code, Title 5, Ch. 1, Section 5-1-11). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        29. In West Virginia, it is a crime against the government to display or possess a red or black flag (West Virginia Code, Section 61-1.6). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        30. Millcreek Canyon, Utah has made it unlawful to possess an unleashed dog on even-numbered days (8.04.170). (Belofsky)
       </p>
       <p>
        31. Little Rock, Arkansas, Municipal Code has made it
        <em>
         illegal to honk your horn after 9:00 p.m. at any place where sandwiches or cold drinks are served
        </em>
        (Section 18-53, Art. II, Subsection 18-54). (Belofsky)
       </p>

        </a>
        hope youve enjoyed this odyssey through some of the more peculiar U.S. laws that are still on the books. Are there any in your state that weve left off? Share them in the comments section!
