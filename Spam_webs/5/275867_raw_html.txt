     <p>
      <b>
       David Mark Weber
      </b>
      (born October 24, 1952
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      ) is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">
       science fiction
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">
       fantasy
      </a>
      author. He has written several
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sci-fi" title="Sci-fi">
       sci-fi
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantasy_(genre)" title="Fantasy (genre)">
       fantasy
      </a>
      books series, the best known of which is
      <a href="/wiki/Honorverse" title="Honorverse">
       the Honor Harrington series
      </a>
      of sci-fi books. His first novel, which he worked on with Steve White, sold in 1989 to Baen books, which has since remained his major publisher.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_career">
       Writing career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Weber&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writing career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Born in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleveland,_Ohio" title="Cleveland, Ohio">
       Cleveland, Ohio
      </a>
      , Weber began writing while in
      <a href="/wiki/Elementary_school" title="Elementary school">
       fifth grade
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WildViolet_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-WildViolet-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Some of Weber's first jobs within the writing/advertising world began after high school when he worked as copywriter, typesetter, proofreader, and paste-up artist. He later earned an undergraduate degree and
      <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">
       M.A.
      </a>
      in History.
     </p>
     <p>
      His first published novels grew out of his work as a war game designer for the Task Force game
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starfire_(strategy_game)" title="Starfire (strategy game)">
        Starfire
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WebScription_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-WebScription-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Weber wrote short stories set in the
      <i>
       Starfire
      </i>
      universe for
      <a href="/wiki/Task_Force_Games" title="Task Force Games">
       Task Force Games
      </a>
      '
      <i>
       Nexus
      </i>
      magazine, and he wrote the
      <i>
       Starfire
      </i>
      novel
      <i>
       Insurrection
      </i>
      (1990) with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_White_(science_fiction)" title="Steve White (science fiction)">
       Stephen White
      </a>
      after
      <i>
       Nexus
      </i>
      was cancelled; this book was the first in a tetralogy that continued through their last collaboration,
      <i>
       The Shiva Option
      </i>
      (2002), which made
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Bestseller_List" title="The New York Times Bestseller List">
       <i>
        The New York Times
       </i>
       Best Seller List
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-designers_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-designers-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      His novels range from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epic_fantasy" title="Epic fantasy">
       epic fantasy
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       Oath of Swords
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       The War God's Own
      </i>
      ) to
      <a href="/wiki/Space_opera" title="Space opera">
       space opera
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       Path of the Fury
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       The Armageddon Inheritance
      </i>
      ) to
      <a href="/wiki/Alternate_history" title="Alternate history">
       alternate history
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       1632
      </i>
      series with
      <a href="/wiki/Eric_Flint" title="Eric Flint">
       Eric Flint
      </a>
      ) and
      <a href="/wiki/Military_science_fiction" title="Military science fiction">
       military science fiction
      </a>
      with in-depth characterization.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A lifetime military history buff, David Weber has carried his interest of history into his fiction. He is said to be interested in most periods of history, with a strong emphasis on the military and diplomatic aspects of it.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CrowsNest_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-CrowsNest-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      He prefers to write about strong characters, developing a character's background story in advance in considerable detail, because he wants that degree of comfort level with the character.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WildViolet_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-WildViolet-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He has said he writes primarily in the evenings and at night.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CrowsNest_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-CrowsNest-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Weber has said he makes an effort to accept as many invitations to science fiction conferences and conventions as he can, because he finds the opportunities for direct feedback from readers which conventions offer is extremely useful. He makes a habit of
      <a href="/wiki/Tuckerization" title="Tuckerization">
       Tuckerizing
      </a>
      people from fandom, particularly in the Honor Harrington books (see, e.g.,
      <a href="/wiki/Jordin_Kare#Filk_music_and_science_fiction" title="Jordin Kare">
       Jordin Kare
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CrowsNest_5-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-CrowsNest-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2008, he donated his archive to the department of Rare Books and Special Collections at
      <a href="/wiki/Northern_Illinois_University" title="Northern Illinois University">
       Northern Illinois University
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
