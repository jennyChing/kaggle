         <div class="entry-inner">
          <h3 class="entry-header">
           The Magic of Vinegar
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             <a href="http://silvertreelandscapes.typepad.com/.shared/image.html?/photos/uncategorized/2007/04/12/img_8944.jpg" onclick="window.open(this.href, '_blank', 'width=800,height=533,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0'); return false">

             </a>
             <a href="http://silvertreelandscapes.typepad.com/.shared/image.html?/photos/uncategorized/2007/04/12/img_9572_2.jpg" onclick="window.open(this.href, '_blank', 'width=786,height=800,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0'); return false">
              <img alt="Img_9572_2" border="0" src="http://silvertreelandscapes.typepad.com/design/images/2007/04/12/img_9572_2.jpg" style="margin: 0px 5px 5px 0px; float: left; width: 148px; height: 149px;" title="Img_9572_2"/>
             </a>
             <a href="http://silvertreelandscapes.typepad.com/.shared/image.html?/photos/uncategorized/2007/04/12/img_9584.jpg" onclick="window.open(this.href, '_blank', 'width=800,height=533,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0'); return false">
              <img alt="Img_9584" border="0" height="149" src="http://silvertreelandscapes.typepad.com/design/images/2007/04/12/img_9584.jpg" style="margin: 0px 5px 5px 0px; float: left;" title="Img_9584" width="225"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             So it can be bit smelly, but vinegar has so many wonderful uses besides just drizzling it over fresh mozzarella or taking the burn away from your first sunburn of the season. Here are some uses for vinegar outside in your garden and just around your house.
            </p>
            <p>
             <u>
              FOR THE GARDEN
             </u>
             :
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Grow beautiful azaleas by occasionally watering plants with a mixture of two tablespoons vinegar to one-quart water. Azaleas love acidic soil.
             </li>
             <li>
              Kill grass on walks and driveways. Pour full strength on unwanted grass.
             </li>
             <li>
              Kill weeds. Spray full strength on new growth until plants have starved.
             </li>
             <li>
              Increase soil acidity. In hard water areas, add a cup of vinegar to a gallon of tap water for watering acid loving plants like rhododendrons, gardenias, or azaleas. The vinegar will release iron in the soil for the plants to use.
             </li>
             <li>
              Freshen cut flowers. Add 2 tablespoons vinegar and 1 teaspoon sugar for each quart of water.
             </li>
             <li>
              Prolong the life of flowers in a vase. Add two tablespoons of vinegar plus three tablespoons of sugar per quart of warm water. Stems should be in three to four inches of water.
             </li>
             <li>
              Neutralize garden lime. Rinse your hands liberally with vinegar after working with garden lime to avoid rough and flaking skin. Clean pots before repotting, rinse with vinegar to remove excess lime.
             </li>
             <li>
              To get ride of calcium buildup on brick or on limestone, use a spray bottle with half vinegar and half water, then just let it set. The solution will do all the work.
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
           <a id="more">
           </a>
           <div class="entry-more">
            <p>
             <u>
              BUGS AND ANIMALS
             </u>
             :
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Fish bowl cleaner: eliminate that ugly deposit in the gold fish
tank by rubbing it with a cloth dipped in vinegar and rinsing well.
             </li>
             <li>
              Eliminate animal urine stains from carpet. Blot up urine with a
soft cloth, flush several times with lukewarm water, then apply a
mixture of equal parts vinegar and cool water. Blot up, rinse, and let
dry.
             </li>
             <li>
              Deter ants. Spray vinegar around door and window frames, under appliances, and along other known ant trails.
             </li>
             <li>
              Remove skunk odor from a dog. Rub fur with full strength vinegar; rinse.
             </li>
             <li>
              Keep cats away. Sprinkle vinegar on an area to discourage cats from walking,sleeping, or scratching on it.
             </li>
             <li>
              Keep dogs from scratching ears. Clean the inside of the ears with
a soft cloth dipped in diluted vinegar. Keep away fleas and mange. Add
a little vinegar to your pet's drinking water.
             </li>
             <li>
              Keep chickens from pecking each other. Add cider vinegar to their drinking water.
             </li>
             <li>
              Clean milking equipment. Rinse with vinegar to leave system clean, odorless, and bacteria free without harmful chemical residue.
             </li>
             <li>
              You can pour vinegar around a sandbox to keep cats from using it
as their litter box. About every two months I reapply just to be sure.
It has worked so far.
             </li>
             <li>
              If you rub or spray yourself with vinegar, it will keep ticks and
chiggers off. They may get on you, but will not stay... And no, once it
dries, you do not smell like vinegar.
             </li>
             <li>
              Being that there are Banana Slugs in the Santa Cruz area, if you
do have a slug problem, you can drop a few drops (an eye dropper works
well) of white vinegar on them and they will dissolve. But be careful
not to get the vinegar on plants, it will kill them.
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             <u>
              OTHER USES
             </u>
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Soak a paintbrush in hot vinegar, and then wash out with warm, sudsy water to soften it up.
             </li>
             <li>
              Varnished wood often takes on a cloudy appearance. If the
cloudiness hasn't gone through to the wood, the cloudiness can be
removed by rubbing the wood with a soft lint-less cloth wrung out from
a solution of 1 tablespoon of distilled vinegar in a quart of lukewarm
water. Complete the job by wiping the surface with a soft dry cloth.
             </li>
             <li>
              Dirt and grime can be easily removed from woodwork with a
solution of 1 cup of ammonia, 1/2 cup of distilled vinegar, and 1/4 cup
of baking soda in a cup of warm water. This solution will not dull the
finish or leave streaks.
             </li>
             <li>
              Wood paneling may be cleaned with a mixture of 1 ounce of olive
oil and 2 ounces of distilled vinegar in 1 quart of warm water. Moisten
a soft cloth with the solution and wipe the paneling. The yellowing is
then removed by wiping with a soft, dry cloth.
             </li>
            </ul>
