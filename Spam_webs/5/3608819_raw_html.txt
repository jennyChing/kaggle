     <h2>
      Kusudama Tutorial part 2
     </h2>
     <div class="contenttext">
      <p style="text-align: left;">
       Today I am showing you part 2 of how to make a kusudama ball. You can find the first part
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/2008/11/kusudama-tutorial-part-1/">
        here.
       </a>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/kusudama-single-ft.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1463 aligncenter" height="353" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/kusudama-single-ft.jpg" title="kusudama-single-ft" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span id="more-1444">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       In part 1, I showed you have to make the individual flowers; you should now have 12 flowers made from 60 individual petals.
      </p>
      <p>
       For part 2 you will need:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        12 flowers (made from 60 petals)
       </li>
       <li>
        Glue
       </li>
       <li>
        String or a ribbon
       </li>
       <li>
        Bead(s)
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/6-flowers.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1464 aligncenter" height="321" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/6-flowers.jpg" title="6-flowers" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       As youmight beable to see from the finished kusudama at the top of the page, I made 6 flowers from blue paper and 6 flowers from a recycled map. To make it easier to see how I am putting the ball together I will use1 blue flower in the middle and 5map flowers around it.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-1.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1465 aligncenter" height="225" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-1.jpg" title="step-1" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Start to glue the flowers together one petal at the time this will give the nicest result. In the photo (right) you can see that youapply the glue a little to the right of the middle line. Hold the 2 pieces together in place with your fingers until the glue is dry. Thenglue thesecond petal in the same way.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-2.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1466 aligncenter" height="225" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-2.jpg" title="step-2" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       When you add the 3rd flower, there are3 petals to connect.Make sure you work from the middle; glue, hold in place, wait until the glue is dry. Now work your way all around the first flower.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/two-halves-kusudama.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1467 aligncenter" height="231" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/two-halves-kusudama.jpg" title="two-halves-kusudama" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       After you have attached all 6 flowers you end up with 2 sets ofhalf a kusudama. Now it is time to attach the cord.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-3.jpg">
       </a>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step3.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1483 aligncenter" height="225" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step3.jpg" title="step3" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       I used 3 beads on the bottom. (Left pic) I made a knot over the first bead to lock it in place and then put the other beads on the string, locking it in again with an overhand knot.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-4.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1469 aligncenter" height="225" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-4.jpg" title="step-4" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Now take one of your 1/2 kusudamas and put some glue on the top. You can see the yellow glue I used on the right-hand picture.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-5.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1470 aligncenter" height="225" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-5.jpg" title="step-5" width="450"/>
       </a>
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/step-5.jpg">
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Glue your stringdown, making sure it is nice and straight. Now glue the other half of the string down, making sure this time that you put the glue exactly on the middle of the petal. After this is nice and dry, start gluing the other half of the kusudama on top of the first half. I normally work around from the top.
      </p>
      <p>
       Thats it! You have finished your kusudama ball, now find a nice spot in your home for it.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/kusudama-flowers-ft.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-1471 aligncenter" height="322" src="http://foldingtrees.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/kusudama-flowers-ft.jpg" title="kusudama-flowers-ft" width="450"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the last photo you can see another kusudama I finished earlier. As you can see, I used the variation on the pattern I showed you in

      <p>
       I have found that using paper clips greatly reduces the time you need to spend making these flowers.  I made the individual petals with tape also, which helped as well.  When you would fold over and glue together the petals, a small piece of tape holds very nicely and is still hidden from view.  Also, when putting the petals together, five paper clips can be used to hold it in place.  Removing one at a time allows you to glue all of them in quick succession, if you replace it right after gluing.  Then you can leave it to dry and move to the next.  The same works when combining the flowers into the ball.  One final note- at the center of the ball, if you place two paper clips in a cross, you get an invisible hanger instead of the beads, if you prefer it to not be seen, and floss or thread make it virtually unnoticeable.
      </p>
