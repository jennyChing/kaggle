       <a href="http://www.askmen.com/money/career_250/269_survive-a-job-you-hate.html" omni_link="job" title="job">
        job
       </a>
       for life? No? To be fair, it was a long time ago. Dad put in a few decades of diligent service at the plant and was rewarded with a gold watch and a modest pension. Throughout his tenure, the thought of a job shift, let alone a
       <a href="http://www.askmen.com/money/career/38_career.html" omni_link="career change" title="career change">
        career change
       </a>
       , was never part of the equation. Stability was the order of the day and your family was taken care of.
       <p>
        But again, that was a long time ago. In our current economy and job market, a career hop is often a necessary step in order to generate an increase in income. "Necessary step" is the key term to consider, because for many who execute a career change, there is a dark side to contend with.
       </p>
       <p>
        Our advice is not to throw caution to the wind but to maintain a checklist of 12 universal blunders people make when changing careers. Memorize them in order to avoid making mistakes when you make your big move.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h3>
        1- There's no viable reason to change
       </h3>
       This one is common among people with career ADHD and a chronic obsession with change. The best advice is to look before you leap  a mantra to serve you until death, in fact. A career change is not akin to a switch in breakfast cereal. Think of it as a monumental life step to gain the proper perspective. People who feel the need to change at the drop of a hat suffer from an identity and confidence crisis. They fear
       <a href="http://www.askmen.com/dating/curtsmith_100/131_dating_advice.html" omni_link="commitment" title="commitment">
        commitment
       </a>
       and, in the end, will probably never secure a position of importance.
       <br/>
       <h3>
        2- Lack of knowledge and experience
       </h3>
       Without the proper dose of knowledge and experience in a given field, a potential career change in that domain may spell your doom. If you are fortunate enough to have an human resource manager take a leap of faith on you, despite the lack of either, prepare to dazzle the company brass. If not, your experiment may flop. The obvious measure necessary is to educate yourself on the industry before you embark on a journey in the dark.
