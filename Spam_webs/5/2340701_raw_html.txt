        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <em>
          If youre looking for a new winter soup, try this delicious
          <strong>
           shrimp and potato chowder
          </strong>
          recipe! Also, learn about my new-found food allergy.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Shrimp and Potato Chowder | Perfect way to warm up on a chilly day! www.honeyandbirch.com #soup #seafood" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8650" data-jpibfi-description="Shrimp and Potato Chowder | Perfect way to warm up on a chilly day! www.honeyandbirch.com #soup #seafood" height="1036" src="http://www.honeyandbirch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/shrimp-and-potato-chowder.jpg" width="680"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just before Christmas, I got to take my very first trip to the emergency room! Sounds exciting, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         Its an interesting story, if not an exciting one.
        </p>
        <p>
         I mentioned a while back that I was making an effort to attend more blog related events. In December, I attended 3! Thats a big increase from the usual 1 every other month and it was kind of fun. But of course, it wouldnt be my style if it didnt end with a bang. The third event I attended was for a food taste-test panel  the main event being a hands on cooking demo with cricket flour. The whole thing made me really squeamish, but my friend convinced me to go. Also, I figured its time for me to step out of my comfort zone.
         <span id="more-8616">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Everything started off great  we started with a new coffee product and a protein bar made of crickets while the moderators finished setting up the kitchen. Both products were delicious  though I found the protein bars to be a little spicy. (This should have been a clue to me) We then got to work, cooking with the cricket flour. It was really interesting  when you think about it, crickets and other insects are consumed all over the world to add protein to diets. I think transitioning Americans into consuming insects through flour is ingenious.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.honeyandbirch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/shrimp-and-potato-chowder-4.jpg">
          <img alt="Shrimp and Potato Chowder | Perfect way to warm up on a chilly day! www.honeyandbirch.com #soup #seafood" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8652" data-jpibfi-description="Shrimp and Potato Chowder | Perfect way to warm up on a chilly day! www.honeyandbirch.com #soup #seafood" height="702" src="http://www.honeyandbirch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/shrimp-and-potato-chowder-4.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Anyway, about an hour into the event, I started to get a stomach ache. I had seen my doctor the week before for some GI pains so I assumed that it was related to that. No worries, right?
        </p>
        <p>
         By the time the event ended 2 1/2 hours later, the pain in my stomach had grown intense  sharp and stabbing. I somehow managed to drive my friend home and raced home myself. I will spare you the details, just know that I made it home just in time. After that, I laid down on the couch and called Mr. B. at work. I was convinced I had food poisoning. He agreed and I tried to take a nap . The pain in my stomach continued, like a knife going into my abdomen.
        </p>
        <p>
         An hour later, I went into the bathroom, thinking I was going to be sick again. I took one look at myself in the mirror and panicked. Hives, literally, were all over my face, neck, and chest and I could see some on my scalp through my very thick hair. I called my mom this time, who instructed me to take 2 benedryl immediately. She raced over to my house. By the time she and my stepdad arrived, I had started to turn bright (Starburst) red.
        </p>
        <p>
         They raced me to emergency room. Even with the Benedryl in my system, my symptoms continued to get worse. By the time we arrived at the ER intake desk, my hands and feet were beginning to swell. Even though the waiting room was full, they took one look at me and rushed me in right away. Thanks to whatever was stuck in my IV, I was able to go home 6 hours later and finish my recovery there over the next day or so.
        </p>
        <p>
         As Im sure you guessed, I had an allergic reaction to something I ate at the taste testing. Seeing as I had no known food allergies and was eating cricket products for the first time, we assumed that was what it was. A trip to an allergist a week later showed a brand new food allergy to crab, and there is a known correlation to seafood allergies and crickets.
        </p>
        <p>
         Nooooooo!
        </p>
        <p>
         I love seafood and this makes me so sad.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.honeyandbirch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/shrimp-and-potato-chowder-3.jpg">
          <img alt="Shrimp and Potato Chowder | Perfect way to warm up on a chilly day! www.honeyandbirch.com #soup #seafood" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8653" data-jpibfi-description="Shrimp and Potato Chowder | Perfect way to warm up on a chilly day! www.honeyandbirch.com #soup #seafood" height="1096" src="http://www.honeyandbirch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/shrimp-and-potato-chowder-3.jpg" width="680"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Shrimp and Potato Chowder
        </h2>
        <p>
         Hey Jen, this soup has shrimp in it  not crab. What does that have to do with your new recipe?
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, let me tell you.
        </p>
        <p>
         The second time I made this was after the aforementioned cricket incident and for some reason I just couldnt get the taste right. I figured I must have written the amount of crushed red pepper wrong because it was so spicy  I didnt even want to add any salt or pepper this time because it had so much kick to it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then Mr. B. came in and took a little sip, Ugh. Put some Salt or Pepper in that right away! Its so bland.
        </p>
        <p>
         Looks like my crab allergy is becoming a shrimp allergy too. Luckily for all of us, Mr. B and I have very similar palletes (or used to..) and he was able to help me tweak the spices in the soup. And I trust his guarantee that I got the flavors correct. Just for good measure, we had our neighbor Z come over and do a taste test. Two thumbs up for this shrimp and potato chowder from him too.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whew! I wont have to risk my life to taste this recipe again.
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" data-jpibfi-description="" src="http://www.honeyandbirch.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.honeyandbirch.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/shrimp-and-potato-chowder-5.jpg">

         </a>

        </p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup bacon, sliced into small chunks
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup diced celery
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup diced onion
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 clove garlic, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 cups chicken broth
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 cups diced potato chunks
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 pound cooked medium shrimp, shells and tail removed, cut into 3 pieces each
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 teaspoon chopped fresh parsley
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon crushed red pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup milk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup heavy cream
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            salt and pepper to taste
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a large soup pot, cook the bacon over medium heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Then add the celery, onion and garlic and cook until the onions are translucent and the bacon is crispy.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the chicken broth and potatoes. Cook for 10 minutes over medium high heat. Stir.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Reduce heat to medium and add shrimp, fresh parsley and red pepper. Then add milk and heavy cream. Cook for 10 minutes, or until the chowder is back to eating temperature. Season with salt and pepper.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Service with oyster crackers or saltines.
           </li>
          </ol>
