      <h2>
       Eco-friendly Halloween Craft: Painted Bottles
      </h2>

      </p>

      <p>
       I absolutely love decorating for the holidays but prefer to find ways to use recycled items rather than buy new dcor. One of my favorite Halloween crafts is to turn old bottles into pumpkins and ghosts. Just take bottles from your recycle bin and turn them into creative and spooky dcor! This fun and easy fall craft is a great use of your recyclable items and has minimal out-of-pocket costs.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Halloween decorated bottles" src="/Libraries/cc_blog_images_2014b/Ecofriendly-fall-craft.sflb.ashx"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Depending on the art supplies you have, use traditional spray or a regular paint to coat the cans and bottles. Make sure to cover your workspace with plenty of newspaper to protect your table or floor.
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        Materials
       </em>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Bottles, with labels removed using soapy water and a paint scraper
       </li>
       <li>
        Fall-colored paint, such as orange and brown for pumpkins and white and black for ghosts
       </li>
       <li>
        Assorted-sized paint brushes, larger for painting the bottle and smaller for painting eyes and trim
       </li>
       <li>
        Newspaper
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <em>
        Directions
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       Make sure the bottles are washed and dried thoroughly. Cover the work area with newspaper and place the bottles on top in an upright position.
      </p>
      <p>
       Paint bottle with orange or white paint, depending on desired design. Let dry and paint a second and/or third coat, if needed.
      </p>
      <p>
       Using a fine-tipped paint brush, paint a spooky, goofy or sweet face onto the outside of the bottle and let dry. You can also do this with a fine-tipped permanent marker. If you don't think you can draw the face yourself, you can also print out a face on computer paper, cut it out and glue on the pieces.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now you just need to find a creative place to display your eco-friendly fall craft!
      </p>
