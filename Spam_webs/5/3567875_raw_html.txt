          <h3 class="entry-header">
           Oink Oink
           <br/>
           Pulled Pork Barbecue
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             <span style="float: left; font-size: 44px; line-height: 35px; padding-top: 3px; padding-right: 3px; font-family: Times, serif, Georgia; color: #800000;">
              N
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             ot too long ago, I bought my very first slow cooker.  Better late than never, right?
            </p>
            <p>
             When I bought it, the saleslady raved about a particular pulled pork recipe and she insisted I wouldn't be sorry if I tried it. Boy was she ever right.
            </p>
            <p>
             I've made the barbecue recipe several times over the last few months and I love it and I think you will too.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752c16970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Barbecue-bun" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a511752c16970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752c16970c-800wi" title="Barbecue-bun"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
            </p>
           </div>
           <a id="more">
           </a>
           <div class="entry-more">
            Now this is what barbecue is supposed to look like.... and taste like too.
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             This recipe creates a super tender meat that practically melts in your mouth with just the right combination of barbecue tang and slightly rustic, smokey-flavored sweetness.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752c45970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Barbecue2" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a511752c45970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752c45970c-800wi" title="Barbecue2"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             I used a
             <a href="http://www.williams-sonoma.com/" target="_self" title="Williams-Sonoma">
              Williams-Sonoma
             </a>
             recipe (only slightly tweaked it) and I created a PDF for you with all the ingredients and directionswhich you can download here:
             <span class="asset asset-generic at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a511f4c279970c img-responsive">
              <a href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/files/pulled-pork-barbecue-recipe-3.pdf">
               Pulled Pork Barbecue recipe
              </a>
              .
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             This recipe calls for 4-pounds of boneless pork
             <em>
              shoulder
             </em>
             .  For some reason pork shoulder was hard to find in my neck of the woods.
            </p>
            <p>
             No problem, Pork butt or rump (seen here) is a worthy substitute... despite it's unsavory-sounding name.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752c4e970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Barbecue-pork" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a511752c4e970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752c4e970c-800wi" title="Barbecue-pork"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             Cut the pork in multiple pieces and saute in canola oil until well-browned on all sides.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             Remove the meat and place in the slow cooker.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             Drain all but about a tablespoon of the oil from the pan.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a73d80698b970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Pulled-pork-stove" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a73d80698b970d image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a73d80698b970d-800wi" title="Pulled-pork-stove"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Saute onions in the remaining oil.
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #c00000;">
              <strong>
               <em>
                NOTE: Refer back to the PDF download linked earlier for the complete instructions.
               </em>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a73d806996970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Pulled-pork-onions" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a73d806996970d image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a73d806996970d-800wi" title="Pulled-pork-onions"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             Add all other ingredients to the slow cooker including vinegar, tomato ketchup, brown sugar, light molasses, red pepper flakes, Worcestershire sauce, dry mustard, kosher salt and ground pepper.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             Give everything a good stir to make sure everything is coated.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752c90970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Pulled-pork-pot" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a511752c90970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752c90970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Pulled-pork-pot"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             I love that you can just toss all these ingredients into the slow cooker and just walk away and get on with the rest of the day.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             My barbecue cooked for about 8-9 hours on medium heat (but make sure you double check the cooking recommendations your particular appliance manual).
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a3fcc55b0c970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Pulled-pork-slow-cooker" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a3fcc55b0c970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a3fcc55b0c970b-800wi" title="Pulled-pork-slow-cooker"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             At the end of the cooking cycle, the meat chunks will be tender but still mostly intact. Remove the meat from the slow cooker and pull it apart using two forks.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             This step is so easy because the meat is soooo tender at this point, only needs a light touch to shred it.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752ad3970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Barbecue-pull" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a511752ad3970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511752ad3970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Barbecue-pull"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Most slow cookers will shift into a warming mode once the main cooking cyle is complete.
            </p>
            <p>
             Return the pulled pork to the sauce and give everything another good stir to incorporate.
            </p>
            <p>
             Keep setting on warm while serving.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a3fcc55958970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Pulled-pork" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a3fcc55958970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a3fcc55958970b-800wi" title="Pulled-pork"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Try using fresh burger buns from your grocer's bakery for sandwiches.
            </p>
            <p>
             Pop the buns in the oven and lightly toast the bread just before you're ready to serve to family, friends and guests.
            </p>
            <p>
             Hey, how'd you like to take a bit out of this?
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a73d8069e3970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Barbecue-bun2" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a73d8069e3970d image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a73d8069e3970d-800wi" title="Barbecue-bun2"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Pulled pork barbecue is fantastic when topped off with a delicious side of cole slaw.
            </p>
            <p>
             I love the combo of ingredients in this cranberry and broccoli recipe. So many yummy ingredients in this including carrots and poppy seed dressing.
            </p>
            <p>
             I've tried (unsuccessfully, so far) to duplicate this Fresh Market recipe but until I figure out the right mix of ingredients, I'll just have to continue buying it from the deli department.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a73d806a39970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Barbecue-slaw" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a73d806a39970d image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a73d806a39970d-800wi" title="Barbecue-slaw"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             Consider using this pulled pork as a pizza topping.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             Just skip the pepperoni on your next homemade pizza and substitute this homemade pulled pork for a barbecue pizza your guests will remember.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511f4caa3970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="Barbecue pork graphic" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e008c99598883401a511f4caa3970c image-full img-responsive" src="http://www.celebrate-creativity.com/.a/6a00e008c99598883401a511f4caa3970c-800wi" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Barbecue pork graphic"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             <span style="text-align: justify;">
              Tempted to make your own pulled pork barbecue now?
             </span>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             <span style="text-align: justify;">
              Hmmm... I thought so.
             </span>
             Oink, oink.
             <span style="color: #c00000; font-size: 14pt;">
              
             </span>
            </p>
