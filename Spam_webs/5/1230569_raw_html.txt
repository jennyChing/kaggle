       <h1 class="entry-title">
        5 Reasons Professionals Who Read More Succeed More
       </h1>
      </header>
      <p class="entry-meta">
       <span class="entry-meta-author vcard author">
        Posted By:
        <a class="fn" href="http://bluescrubsmd.com/author/mdavey/">
         Milford Davey
        </a>
       </span>
       <span class="entry-meta-date updated">
        April 13, 2015
       </span>
      </p>
      <div class="entry-content clearfix">
       <br/>
       <div style="text-align:center; padding:5px; background-color:#e8f1fb; border-style: solid; font-family: Arial; border-width: 1px;">
        <p>

       <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="16925175" data-link="http://bluescrubsmd.com/5-reasons-professionals-who-read-more-succeed-more/" data-summary="" data-title="5 Reasons Professionals Who Read More Succeed More">
       </div>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_194" style="width: 524px">
        <div class="wwm_socialshare_imagewrapper">

       <p>
        When I was growing up as a kid, we tended to have more books in the house than we had toys. At the time, it sucked, because I wanted the same new toys the neighbour kid had and I was instead handed books andnovels by my parents instead.
       </p>
       <p>
        Now that Im an adult and into the professional world, I couldnt be more thankful to my parents for giving me more books than toys. I realized thatthe way I looked around me at the world and interpreted it was influenced a lot by the fact that Id been reading since I was a kid.
       </p>
       <p>
        Im all the more aware of that now that Im making my forays into the professional world. Its nosecret that
        <strong>
         people who read more tend to succeed more
        </strong>
        in their professional careers, andheres
        <strong>
         5 of the main benefits of reading
        </strong>
        and why you should read more too (and what to do if you dont have time or dont know what to read):
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         1. ITS THE BREAK YOU NEED!
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Whenever I need to take a break from the chaos around me, I turn to a book. When you sit back on your favorite couch and relax with a good book, you will get lost in the wonders inside. Itll take you places  countries youve never been to, places youve never seen, things youve never heard of. There have been many times when I easily spend more than two hours reading a good paperback. Time flies when you do so and temporarily makes you forget about your worries.
       </p>
       <p>
        One of the many
        <strong>
         benefits of reading
        </strong>
        more is that its an easy way to disconnect yourself from the rest of the world when you feel you need to, and no matter what profession youre in youre going to have a lot of moments like that. Try it out  the next time you think the pressure is too much, find a nice, quiet place with a book youve always been wanting to read, and see what happens!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Related:
         <a href="http://bluescrubsmd.com/6-things-to-do-before-bed-for-a-better-tomorrow/">
          6 Things To Do Before Bed For A Better Tomorrow
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         2. It will make you smarter.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Youre probably pretty smart already. But Im not talking about book-smart kind of smart.
       </p>
       <p>
        Most people can easily tell the difference between a person who doesnt read and one whos relatively well read. Most of us can, and the it just shows, in the way well-read people talk, act, and carry themselves, and by God, its a very attractive quality to have. Very classy indeed.
       </p>
       <p>
        Reading makes you smarter by improving your vocabulary. Its obvious, and simple  the more you read, the more words you come across that you didnt know before. You start using these words in your daily life, and voila, youre smarter. Armed with all these new words, youll automatically start to communicate much more clearly, whenever you speak or write. Theres evidence that people who articulate themselves better are more successful.
       </p>
       <p>
        Another no-brainer  when you read, you acquire new knowledge. The more you read and the more topics you read about, the more youre going to learn about those topics. John Grishams going to teach you a bit about the law. Reading non-fiction (biographies, stories, etc) by doctors will teach you about medicine. Its a win for you because anything new you learn will almost always come in handy, sooner or later.
       </p>
       <p>
        And you dont have to take it from me 
        <strong>
         <a href="http://gse3.berkeley.edu/faculty/aecunningham/Readingcanmakeyousmarter!.pdf">
          theres
         </a>
         <a href="http://lifedev.net/2009/06/reading-makes-you-better/">
          a
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.popsci.com/technology/article/2012-09/science-confirms-obvious-reading-literature-good-your-brain">
          ton
         </a>
         of
         <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8447247">
          evidence
         </a>
        </strong>
        to supportmy point. Who said theres no
        <strong>
         benefits of reading
        </strong>
        ?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         3. It will improve your writing skills.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Having good writing skills is pretty much a basic need in a professional world, no matter what you do. If you cant write an essay that anyone can read without cringing, for example, you really cant make it far.
       </p>
       <p>
        And guess what, reading improves your writing skills too. When you read, you dont only just learn new words  you pick up the different ways writers use language to get their point across. You learn the technicalities of writing, like grammar, punctuation and sentence structure. Best part is, you dont even necessarily realize youre doing it, but it still happens, and it automatically builds itself into whatever you write. You dont have to actively take a book apart to learn this stuff, and the more you do it, the more you absorb, and the better your writing gets.
       </p>
       <p>
        To illustrate my point, heres a secret. I dont remember 85% of what I learnt in school about the parts of the English language  pronouns, past participles, and a hundred other things. And it didnt really matter, because Ive been an avid reader since childhood and it helped me develop the writing skills that I have now.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres a
        <a href="http://writetodone.com/2008/01/31/how-to-use-reading-to-become-a-better-writer/">
         deeper look
        </a>
        at how reading will make you a better writer.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         4. It improves your analytical/logical thinking skills.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Most professions require you to have the ability to think quickly and logically, almost all the time. For example, doctors need a kick-ass ability to think critically when theyre working in an ER and have minutes to decide what to do and how to do it.
       </p>
       <p>
        I thinkone of the
        <strong>
         best benefits of reading
        </strong>
        is that itsharpens your mind. A lot. When youre deep in a thriller, in the mind of a detective trying to outsmart a serial killer, the neurons in your brain are firing like crazy. When you read, youre constantly thinking about whats going on in the book  why are the characters doing what theyre doing or what could happen next or how things could have been different. All these thought processes are good for you  they improve your analytical thinking skills, and make your mind sharper.
       </p>
       <div>
        <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">

       <p>
        <strong>
         5. Youll be a more well-rounded [insert profession here].
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        People who read are equipped with ideas and thoughts they talk can about with other people. This makes you a well-rounded professional, and this skill can be incredibly handy, especially when youre out networking with people. For example, if the guy youre trying to land for your company mentions he reads Frederick Forsyth, and you happen to enjoy Forsyth as well, youve already done half the work.
       </p>
       <p>
        Another example: a well-read medical student will likely become a well-read doctor who will invariably have good bedside skills too. And hey, if Im ever a patient and it turns out my doctor loves to read the same kind of stuff I do, Id begin to feel better already.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         You may also like:
        </strong>
        <a href="http://bluescrubsmd.com/11-things-you-can-do-to-improve-your-mind-and-body-today/">
         11 Things You Can Do To Improve Your Mind and Body Today
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Read:
        <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/what-successful-people-read-before-bed-2014-2">
         What 13 Successful People Read Before Bed
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         WAIT I DONT KNOW WHAT TO READ
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        At this point, you might be like, All youve told me is great, but I dont know what to read!
       </p>
       <p>
        It depends on you. What kind of stuff do you like, or would rather want to read about? You dont have to limit yourself to anything. I like to read books in a lot of different genres, from fiction thrillers to war novels to non-fiction biographies.The most important thing for me is that whatever I read must keep me interested and impart value at the same time. Its never too hard to find something good.
       </p>
       <p>
        Youll probably find great books to read in your library. Or, you could check out lists on the internet, such as on
        <a href="http://goodreads.com">
         GoodReads
        </a>
        or
        <a href="http://amazon.com">
         Amazon
        </a>
        . Or, just ask your friends.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Read
        </strong>
        :
        <a href="http://goweloveit.info/general/what-successful-people-read-the-30-essential-books-you-must-read/#cgviXFDFkiGvP5TT.99">
         What successful people read? The 30 Essential Books You Must Read!
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Remember, the point isnt to find the perfect book, if there even is such a thing. The point is to find something good, and stick with it till the end.
       </p>
       <p>
        Which brings me to 
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         BUT I DONT HAVE TIME
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Theres never enough time for anything that you want to do. But you figure out the things you truly value, and you
        <strong>
         make time
        </strong>
        for them, right? Outside of your studying/sleeping hours, time can be spent on watching TV or it could be spent with a book. You decide whats more important.
       </p>
       <p>
        People have different ways of making time to read. One way you might do this is by telling yourself that you will read 10 pages every day, for example. Or that youll read for 30 minutes every day. Or you read just before bed
       </p>
       <p>
        That might work for some people, but might be too rigid for others  some days you just wont be able to make time (happens to all of us). In that case, try this little technique I learnt about a few weeks ago and am using myself:
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Read ONE (1) book a month
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Pick out a book you want to read, and make a goal of finishing it within the month. This way, you have some flexibility  you can read some days and not read others  while still finishing one book a month. Thats 12 books a year. Not bad at all. And in case youre thinking 1 book a month is too easy, it can be
        <i>
         very
        </i>
        difficult. Try it!
       </p>
       <p>
        One word of caution here: Choose a book of reasonable length. You cant expect to read
        <em>
         War and Peace
        </em>
        in a month and do justice to it without a serious investment of time.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         So,
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Did I convince you about the
        <strong>
         benefits of reading
        </strong>
        ? Are you an avid reader? Do you want to start reading more but are facing problems? Have any tips on how professionals can find more time to read? Share your thoughts in the comments below!
       </p>
