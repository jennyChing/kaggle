     <p>
      <b>
       Eric Moussambani Malonga
      </b>
      (born May 31, 1978) is a swimmer from
      <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">
       Equatorial Guinea
      </a>
      . Nicknamed "Eric the Eel" by the media after the name first appeared in an article by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Craig_Lord&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Craig Lord (page does not exist)">
       Craig Lord
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">
        The Times
       </a>
      </i>
      newspaper in
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      , Moussambani won brief international fame at the
      <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">
       2000 Summer Olympics
      </a>
      when he swam his heat of the 100m
      <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_swimming" title="Freestyle swimming">
       freestyle
      </a>
      in 1:52.72.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His time was more than twice that of his faster competitors, and outside even the 200m world record. However, he set a new personal best and Equatoguinean national record.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As of 2012 Moussambani is the coach of the national swimming squad of Equatorial Guinea.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
       Career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eric_Moussambani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Moussambani gained entry to the Olympics without meeting the minimum qualification requirements via a
      <a href="/wiki/Wild_card_(sports)" title="Wild card (sports)">
       wildcard
      </a>
      draw designed to encourage
      <a href="/wiki/Developing_country" title="Developing country">
       developing countries
      </a>
      without expensive training facilities to participate. While
      <a href="/wiki/Pieter_van_den_Hoogenband" title="Pieter van den Hoogenband">
       Pieter van den Hoogenband
      </a>
      won in a time of 48.30 (setting a world record of 47.84 in the semi-finals), Moussambani splashed his way to the finish to the cheers of the crowd in more than twice that time (1:52.72). "The last 15 metres were very difficult", Moussambani said. However, because the other two swimmers in his heat made false starts, and were thus disqualified, he won the heat unopposed. Before coming to the Olympics, Moussambani had never seen a 50m (160ft) long
      <a href="/wiki/Olympic-size_swimming_pool" title="Olympic-size swimming pool">
       Olympic-size swimming pool
      </a>
      . He took up swimming only eight months before the Olympics and had practiced in a lake
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Malabo" title="Malabo">
       Malabo
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      His performance generated spectator and media interest in the only other Equatoguinean swimmer at the Sydney Olympics,
      <a href="/wiki/Paula_Barila_Bolopa" title="Paula Barila Bolopa">
       Paula Barila Bolopa
      </a>
      , who competed in the women's 50 meters freestyle event. Barila struggled to finish the race with a time of 1:03.97, setting a record for the slowest time in Olympic history for that event, and also achieved minor celebrity status.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Moussambani was denied entry into the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_Olympic_Games" title="2004 Olympic Games">
       2004 Olympic Games
      </a>
      due to a
      <a href="/wiki/Visa_(document)" title="Visa (document)">
       visa
      </a>
      bungle,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      despite the vast improvement in his swimming over the previous four years, with his personal best down to under 57 seconds.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He did not take part in the
      <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">
       2008 Summer Olympics
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In March 2012 he was appointed coach of the national swimming squad of Equatorial Guinea.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">
       Democratic Republic of the Congo

      <a href="/wiki/Elis_Lapenmal" title="Elis Lapenmal">

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamza_Abdu" title="Hamza Abdu">
