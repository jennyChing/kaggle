   function extractParamFromUri(uri, paramName) {
  if (!uri) {
    return;
  }
  var uri = uri.split('#')[0];  // Remove anchor.
  var parts = uri.split('?');  // Check for query params.
  if (parts.length == 1) {
    return;
  }
  var query = decodeURI(parts[1]);

  // Find url param.
  paramName += '=';
  var params = query.split('&amp;');
  for (var i = 0, param; param = params[i]; ++i) {
    if (param.indexOf(paramName) === 0) {
      return unescape(param.split('=')[1]);
    }
  }
}

           <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-26075" height="90" src="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Leaderboard_socialhacks.jpg" width="730"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <p>
          Editors Note: This article is a part of our Hero Conf Guest Post Giveaway. Based on a combination of pageviews, shares and editorial review from the PPC Hero writing staff, a winner will be chosen from the 4 finalists we post throughout the week. Todays post comes to us from JD Prater(
          <a href="https://twitter.com/jdprater" target="_blank">
           @jdprater
          </a>
          ), Paid Search Specialist at
          <a href="http://www.goinflow.com/" target="_blank">
           Inflow
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD1.png">
           <img alt="JD1" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-26158" height="689" src="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD1.png" width="992"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Are you a
          <em>
           House of Cards
          </em>
          fan? Good. Me too. In honor of
          <em>
           House of Cards
          </em>
          season three, lets hone our inner Frank Underwood and examine how to stay ahead of the curve when managing our accounts. Like Frank, PPCers understand that knowledge is power and power is a lot like real estate. . . The closer you are to the source, the higher your property value.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          With that in mind, lets get close to the source of power and raise our property value. To this, I turn to Google Analytics, which is so full data that it can be difficult to know where to begin.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          To help you get the most from your account, here are 4 Google Analytics hacks that will improve your accounts ROI.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Theres no better way to overpower a trickle of doubt than with a flood of naked truth.
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Have you ever wondered how our ads perform in certain positions? Are they better in 1, 2, 3 or 4? Have you said to yourself, Maybe I should raise that CPC so I can move from a 3.1 avg position to 2.2? Have you stopped to see if the data warrants that action? Do you even know where to find that information?
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          To find the answer to this question, head over to Analytics?Acquisition?AdWords? Campaigns. From here, select the Campaign ? Ad Group where the keyword resides. Now, select the secondary dimension Ad Slot Position.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <a href="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD2.png">
           <img alt="JD2" class=" wp-image-26159 aligncenter" height="211" src="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD2.png" width="357"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p class="normal">
          The Ad Slot Position shows all the ad positions for every keyword. Funnel your list down to the keyword you want to analyze by typing it into the advanced search bar.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p class="normal">
          Heres an example of the same keywords performance across the top 4 positions sorted by Revenue.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p class="normal">
          <a href="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD3.png">
           <img alt="JD3" class=" size-full wp-image-26160 aligncenter" height="205" src="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD3.png" width="992"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p class="normal">
          This is fantastic information to help with your keyword bid strategy. Plus, it highlights that it may not be worth bidding for the top position.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p class="normal">
          Another way to look at your ad performance is by location in the SERPs. Run the secondary dimension Ad Slot to see how well your keyword performs at the Top or RHS (right hand side) of the hosting page.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p class="normal">
          <a href="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD4.png">
           <img alt="JD4" class=" size-full wp-image-26161 aligncenter" height="105" src="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD4.png" width="992"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          You could also make Ad Content the primary dimension and run the secondary dimensions Ad Slot Position and/or Ad Slot to see how well you perform in certain ad positions. This is a good way to analyze your ad headlines to find the naked truth that leads to an increased ROI.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          I have no patience for useless things.  Keyword Match Type
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          How well are my exact match keywords performing compared with my modified broad or phrase match keywords?
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Like Frank Underwood, PPCers dont have time to waste on useless keywords. If your keywords arent bringing in value, its time to take action.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Often, we target search queries with the same keyword but a different match type. To see how well that particular match type is performing, make your primary dimension the keywords you want to dissect. Next, learn how keywords are performing by examining their match type with a secondary dimension called Keyword Match Type.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          It should look like this:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD5.png">
           <img alt="JD5" class=" size-full wp-image-26162 aligncenter" height="241" src="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD5.png" width="767"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Above is a great example of a phrase match keyword that was added as an exact match keyword due to performance. Looking at the data, we can see that the exact match keyword is well on its way to an excellent ROI.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Ive worked too hard to come within arms reach of the prize only to have my hand cut off just before I seize it. Destination URLS
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          As account managers, we know that too much work goes into keyword research, structuring accounts, writing ads and bid management  just to send people to a poor landing page. There are several ways to analyze your landing pages in Google Analytics. One way is to make Ad Content the primary dimension and Destination URL the secondary dimension. This allows you to see how well your ads are doing based on the landing page.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD6.png">
           <img alt="JD6" class=" size-full wp-image-26163 aligncenter" height="164" src="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD6.png" width="517"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          This report will help you diagnose which landing pages are performing with which ads. To take this a step further, review how your well your landing pages and keywords are working together by making Keyword your primary dimension and using Destination URL as the secondary.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          However, always keep in mind which landing pages are working for which keywords. Once you know that answer, go to AdWords and set the destination url at the keyword level. This should increase conversions and keep your hand from being cut off.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          I can smell which way the wind is blowing
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Do you know how your ad spend is being used across Googles Search Network?
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Have you thought to check your performance on Googles Search Partners? Its a default under the Campaign settings that often goes unnoticed. However, left unchecked, Googles Search Partners could be wasting a lot of spend.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          To find this information in Analytics, start by honing in on the campaign. Then, choose the secondary dimension Ad Distribution Network and select Ecommerce in Explorer.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Your results should look similar to this:
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD7.png">
           <img alt="JD7" class=" size-full wp-image-26164 aligncenter" height="346" src="http://ppchero.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/JD7.png" width="992"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          From here, you can easily compare Google Search and Search Partners performance. For this campaign, the Search Partners results are a little better, so it might be beneficial to leave it on and continue to monitor its progress.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Take these hacks, use them, benefit from them and always remember to Pay attention to the fine-print . . . Its far more important than the selling price.
         </p>
         <p>
