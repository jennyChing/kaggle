       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Braised Brussels Sprouts in Bacon and Shallots
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="byline">
          <em>
           by
          </em>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://whiteonricecouple.com/author/todd-diane/">
            Todd &amp; Diane
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10860" height="732" src="//whiteonricecouple.com/recipe/images/brussels-sprouts-ps-6-550x830.jpg" title="brussels sprouts recipe " width="485"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Update: Click here for more fabulous
         </span>
         <span style="color: #993300;">
          <strong>
           <a href="http://whiteonricecouple.com/tag/brussels-sprouts/" style="color: #505050;" target="_blank">
            <span style="color: #993300;">
             Brussels Sprouts Recipes
            </span>
           </a>
          </strong>
         </span>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          .
         </span>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <br/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I think by now weve established that we like brussels sprouts. Between the
         <span style="color: #993300;">
          <a href="http://whiteonricecouple.com/recipes/roasted-brussels-sprouts-balsamic-vinegar/" target="_blank" title="Roasted Brussels Sprouts Recipe">
           <span style="color: #993300;">
            balsamic roasted brussels sprouts recipe
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         and the
         <span style="color: #993300;">
          <a href="http://whiteonricecouple.com/recipes/brussels-sprouts-recipe-with-parmesan-cheese/" target="_blank" title="Baked Brussels Sprouts Recipe">
           <span style="color: #993300;">
            baked brussels sprouts with parmesan
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         we have converted more than a few doubters to joys of this little cabbage-esque vegetable. It is one of our favorite rough-ages and over the years weve come up with recipes that allow these little bundles of goodness to shine and be enjoyed by many. Recently weve been preparing it in a way which makes everything better. Braised with bacon.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-9388">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-10862" height="731" src="//whiteonricecouple.com/recipe/images/brussels-sprouts-21-550x829.jpg" title="brussels sprouts recipe bacon " width="485"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         This is a super simple recipe for braising brussels sprouts. Saute up a bit of bacon then add some chopped shallots for sweetness. After they have cooked and sweetened up a but,add in the green balls of joy, a bit of broth
         <em>
          (we usually use chicken broth but feel free to use whatever kind you like or have on hand)
         </em>
         and some soy sauce for seasoning and umami. Cover and braise until tender. Easy and delicious.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         The savory and slightly salty bite of the bacon combined with the tender sweet brussels sprouts is divine. Particularly with the added sweetness of the shallots and the tying-in element of the soy sauce. The ingredients and technique are so simple it is hard not to love it. Especially because of the bacon, since we all know that bacon makes everything better. Now if our garden brussels sprouts would just start producing some for us to eat so we wouldnt have to go out and buy them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10857" height="451" src="//whiteonricecouple.com/recipe/images/brussels-sprouts-ps.jpg" title="brussels-sprouts-bacon recipe " width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          above: render bacon fat then add shallots
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10858" height="325" src="//whiteonricecouple.com/recipe/images/brussels-sprouts-ps-3.jpg" title="brussels-sprouts-recipe " width="650"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          Add soy sauce and broth, then braise brussels sprouts in liquid
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>

         <span class="item">
          <h2 class="fn">
           Braised Brussels Sprouts with Bacon Recipe
          </h2>
         </span>
         <div class="time">
          <p class="time" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left">
           <strong>
            Yield:
           </strong>
           <span class="yield">
            4 as a side dish
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="time" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left">
           <strong>
            Cook Time:
           </strong>
           <span class="cooktime">
            30 minutes
           </span>
          </p>
         </div>
         <h3 style="clear:left">
          Ingredients:
         </h3>
         <div class="ingredient">
          <ul>
           <li>
            1 lb brussels sprouts, washed, cut in half
            <em>
             (or quarters as long as they are about same size)
            </em>
           </li>
           <li>
            3 slices bacon (about 100g),
            <em>
             cut into small pieces
            </em>
           </li>
           <li>
            2 tablespoons minced Shallots
            <em>
             (about 2 medium shallots)
            </em>
           </li>
           <li>
            1 tablespoon Soy Sauce
           </li>
           <li>
            1/4 cup broth
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <h3 style="clear:left">
          Directions:
         </h3>
         <div class="instructions">
          <ol>
           <li>
            Heat pan on medium heat then add bacon. Fry up till bacon has rendered a good amount of the fat, about 5-10 minutes. Add shallots. Stir shallots well into the bacon, fry shallots till they become fragrant and soft, but not burnt.
           </li>
           <li>
            Add brussels sprouts to the pan then add soy sauce and broth. Stir evenly.
           </li>
           <li>
            Cover, and turn heat to low and allow brussels spouts to slowly braise in the liquid for about 15-20 minutes, or until the brussels sprouts are tender.
           </li>
          </ol>
         </div>
