       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
         Skittles Vodka Tutorial
        </h1>
        <meta content="http://mixthatdrink.com/skittles-vodka-tutorial/" itemprop="url"/>
        <meta content="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-finished.jpg" itemprop="image"/>
        <meta content="Infusing vodka with Skittles is a very popular trend right now. There are a couple of different ways to do it. My way involves..." itemprop="description"/>
        <p class="entry-meta">
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Infusing vodka with Skittles is a very popular trend right now. There are a couple of different ways to do it. My way involves separating all the Skittles into their separate flavors and making five different bottles of Skittles vodka.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13821" height="900" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-pin-600x900.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         Skittles Vodka
        </h2>
        <p>
         Over the years since I originally published this, Ive found better ways to do some of it. Most importantly, I found a
         <strong>
          better way to strain it, and Ive tested it with rum.
         </strong>
         In fact, the green one in this batch is rum. More on that at the end.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Time to complete: 24 hours at most.
         </strong>
         Thats about 30 minutes of prep; 15 minutes altogether for the occasional shaking; a good 14 hours to just let it do its thing; and a good hour or two for straining. Need something quicker and easier?
         <a href="http://mixthatdrink.com/jolly-ranchers-vodka/">
          Jolly Ranchers Vodka
         </a>
         doesnt need any straining or shaking, and infuses in about half the time.
        </p>
        <p>
         For this project, youll need:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          One 1.75 liter bottle of vodka (I used Stoli  you dont need the most expensive vodka, but do avoid the cheap ones)
         </li>
         <li>
          Five 8.5 ounce flasks or bottles (you can buy them from
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0001UZOE6/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0001UZOE6&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=mixthatdrink-20&amp;linkId=LKFRW7V6IUAH6SDZ" rel="nofollow">
           Amazon
          </a>
          . For a Halloween party, serve these neon-colored concoctions in
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0057OSBMI/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B0057OSBMI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=mixthatdrink-20&amp;linkId=YX7FODQEFYZIRK7N" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           test tube shooter glasses
          </a>
          .)
         </li>
         <li>
          One
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000OIT8T2/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=mixthatdrink-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=217145&amp;creative=399369&amp;creativeASIN=B000OIT8T2" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           1 pound bag of Skittles
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          Five empty plastic water bottles
         </li>
         <li>
          A
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000079XWD/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=mixthatdrink-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=217145&amp;creative=399369&amp;creativeASIN=B000079XWD" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           funnel
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          Bowls for separating the Skittles into flavors
         </li>
         <li>
          A measuring cup
         </li>
         <li>
          Cheesecloth and coffee filters
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Youll also probably want to cover your workspace with newspaper or freezer paper  this infusion can get messy. If youre making a different sized batch, heres the formula you need to know: I used 70 Skittles to 7 ounces of vodka, which yields about 7-8 ounces of infused vodka. If you want to make 1.5 times that, or 3 times that, just multiply your amounts.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 1: Separate Skittles into Flavors
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Start by simply separating the Skittles into flavors. You want 70 of each.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13822" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-separated-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Related:
         <a href="http://mixthatdrink.com/starburst-vodka-tutorial/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Starburst Vodka Tutorial">
          Starburst Vodka Tutorial
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 2: Put Skittles in bottles
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Now put your Skittles into 5 bottles. I used some old water bottles I had on hand, but you can use any container with a secure lid  plastic or glass. Mason jars work great because whatever bottles you use, theyre going to end up with a lot of gunk to be washed out, and Mason jars are easy to wash out.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13819" height="400" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-in-bottles-600x400.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 3: Add the vodka
        </h2>
        <p>
         Now add the 7 ounces (or whatever measurement youre using) of vodka to each bottle. When this is done, all five bottles will have a pile of Skittles soaking in the vodka.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13820" height="400" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-start-600x400.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: left;">
         Step 4: Shake em, shake em, shake em
        </h2>
        <p>
         Now your infusion bottles all have Skittles in the bottom. Give each bottle a good shake  the more, the better. The candy coating will start to rub off and color the vodka, but the white insides of the Skittles still have a lot of dissolving to do. After shaking them, set them aside for a few hours. I think shaking them every few hours, up to maybe 4 times altogether, helps them dissolve faster  especially if youre making a bigger batch. My Skittles all dissolved overnight, but some people have had it take longer, so be sure to do this a full day in advance of when you want to serve them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13824" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-shaken-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         See the white stuff at the bottom? Thats what I was talking about. Once theyre all dissolved, youll notice a lot of white muck floating at the top. Well take care of this in Step 6.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 5: Strain
        </h2>
        <p>
         Since I first did this one, Ive found that the quickest and most thorough way to strain these is through:
         <strong>
          2 layers of cheese cloth inside a coffee filter.
         </strong>
         Ive tried a lot of combinations, and this is the one that worked for me. It strained out
         <em>
          everything
         </em>
         in one pass, and didnt take too long.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want suggestions on how to arrange that stuff, heres what I did.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13825" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-strainer-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Looks like something from a mad scientist lab, huh? I clipped the coffee filters and cheesecloth (you cant see the filters for the cheesecloth) onto a funnel with clips, and then stuck that contraption over a measuring cup. The thing about using a funnel is that it directs the flow of the stuff toward the bottom, which makes it go a little quicker.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then I poured one of the infusions into my strainer setup. I had to pour about a quarter of my infusion in, then wait for some of it to soak through, then pour another quarter in, until I was done. If the strainer clogs with white gunk, you can use a spoon to scrape the white gunk out of the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13828" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-gunk-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         By the way, if youre wondering whether its going to slowly, this is about the stream you should expect if its filtering everything out like it should:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13829" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-straining-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         In the end, youll have about 8 ounces of Skittle infused vodka. Then I dumped the funnel and coffee filters into one of the bowls Id used earlier for counting out Skittles, to catch drips as I carried it over to the sink. I washed everything out for re-using.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then I took the liquid from the measuring cup and poured it into a flask. Repeat this process for each flavor.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Step 6: Chill and Serve
        </h2>
        <p>
         Now youve got 5 flasks or bottles of Skittles infused vodka:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13830" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-finished-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Stick them in the freezer for several hours  overnight is fabulous and theyre ready to serve:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13831" height="400" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-chilled-600x400.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="A tutorial for making Skittles Vodka." class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-13832" height="600" src="http://mixthatdrink.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/skittles-vodka-chilled-2-600x600.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Related:
         <a href="http://mixthatdrink.com/salted-caramel-vodka-recipe/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Salted Caramel Vodka Recipe">
          Salted Caramel Vodka Recipe
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, these are pretty strong on their own  nearly pure vodka. They are also very sweet. You can drink them straight, served up in a chilled martini glass, or you can use them in a cocktail:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Mix them with ginger ale, 7-up or Sprite to taste
         </li>
         <li>
          Mix them with cola to taste (diet cola cuts the sweetness a little)
         </li>
         <li>
          Serve them on the rocks to water it down a little
         </li>
         <li>
          Some people mix them with fruit juice. I didnt try this, because to me the Skittles were plenty of sweetness for my taste buds.
         </li>
         <li>
          Everyones taste varies  there are so many directions you can take this drink in, you really should try anything that sounds good to you!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Cheers!
        </p>
        <h2>
         Frequently asked questions:
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           How long will this keep?
          </strong>
          Im not a scientist, but I can tell you Ive kept these for about a month with no problem. Strangely, the purple color can fade to pink, but that was the only issue. Everything tasted just like it always does and no one got ill. To be on the safe side, I recommend making these no more than 2 weeks ahead.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Can you do it with rum?
          </strong>
          Absolutely! The green one in this batch is rum  it takes the same amount of time, strains the same way, etc. But the rum will add its own flavor. If youve ever had rum balls or butter rum candy, thats the flavor it adds, and its delicious. I actually prefer the rum, but it depends on your preference. Both ways are terrific.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Can you strain it with [insert alternative here]?
          </strong>
          You can strain it with many methods, but the one I gave you above is what Ive found to be both the quickest without letting through gunk and needing a second pass.
         </li>
        </ul>
