          <h2 class="topTitle">
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/2011/03/21/building-a-screen-rack/">
            Building a screen / rack
           </a>
          </h2>
          <div class="CommentsBubble">
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/2011/03/21/building-a-screen-rack/#comments" title="Comment on Building a screen / rack">
            41
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="cleared">
          </div>
          <p class="topMeta">
           by
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/author/jutta/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jutta">
            Jutta
           </a>
           on Mar.21, 2011, under
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/category/decorating/" rel="category tag">
            decorating
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/category/diy/" rel="category tag">
            DIY
           </a>
          </p>
          <div class="topContent">
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5546565924/" title="Finished screen by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Finished screen" height="753" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5011/5546565924_d3581034d6_b.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Now we have a screen! This was such a
            <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/2011/02/14/screen-options/">
             fun and shared project
            </a>
            , I truly couldnt have made it without you guys. Thank you for your great ideas! We ended up building the rack ourselves with instructions from
            <a href="http://www.designspongeonline.com/2009/06/diy-project-kates-rolling-coat-rack.html" target="_blank">
             Design*Sponge
            </a>
            . We opted for the DIY version because we needed a very specific size. The space also clearly called for a white rack.
           </p>
           <p>
            You can find great
            <a href="http://www.designspongeonline.com/2009/06/diy-project-kates-rolling-coat-rack.html" target="_blank">
             instructions
            </a>
            on
            <a href="http://www.designspongeonline.com/" target="_blank">
             Design*Sponge
            </a>
            . We had to do some adjustments though, mainly due to the larger size of our rack. I thought Id share with you the process.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5545967465/" title="Building a rack / screen by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Nuts and bolts" height="723" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5258/5545967465_205fd92330_b.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Heres what you need if you want to make your own screen / rack:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Steel plumbers pipe, 3-4 pieces. You only really need 3 pipes, but we got the fourth one to serve as a weight in the bottom. The pipes come in two finishes, galvanized and black. We got black because they were cheaper and we were going to paint it anyway. In hardware stores you can get them in standard sizes (in Finland 1 m, 1,5 m and 2 m). The measurements werent quite right so we ordered custom cut pipes from a local plumbing shop. We ended up getting two 145 cm (57) and one 130 cm (51) piece, all 3/4 thick.
            </li>
            <li>
             Wood board. We had ours cut to measure at a hardware store, its 150 cm long, 30 cm wide and 3 cm thick (59 x 12 x 1,2).
            </li>
            <li>
             2 elbows to connect the pipes and 2 flanges to attach them to the board
            </li>
            <li>
             4 castors
            </li>
            <li>
             16 screws and washers to attach the castors
            </li>
            <li>
             4 bolts and nuts to attach the flanges to the board
            </li>
            <li>
             Primer and paint. I used primer that suits both metal and wood, plus semi-glossy window and door paint.
            </li>
            <li>
             Paint brush
            </li>
            <li>
             Fabric
            </li>
            <li>
             Needle and yarn (possibly a sewing machine and buttons)
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5546549268/" title="Sanding the rust by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Sanding the rust" height="642" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5013/5546549268_b180a9ebec_b.jpg" style="border: none; padding-left: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Get so excited about the project that you forget to think. Screw the pipes together tightly with the elbows to form a massive U. Only then realize that black iron pipes might be rusty and need sanding (especially if they are orange all over) and that handling them is a lot easier if they are not stuck together.
           </p>
           <p>
            Iron dust gets stuck everywhere, so unless you have an area where it doesnt matter, drag your U-shaped construction outside. After sanding, drag it back in (if its below freezing point outside) and wash it with soap and water. Galvanized pipes dont need sanding, just a wash.
           </p>
           <p>
            Or  first sand and wash, then screw tightly.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5545967647/" title="Marking the spots by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Marking the spots" height="333" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5294/5545967647_e68fb570d7.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Sand the board if theres any rough spots. I also wanted to round the corners a bit. Wipe any dust off with a moist cloth.
           </p>
           <p>
            Screw the flanges on the pipes and measure where you want them on the board.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5545967331/" title="Building a rack / screen by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Building a rack / screen" height="333" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5020/5545967331_a8cb0b935e.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Flip the board and measure where you want the castors to be. We tried 1 cm (1/2) from both edges, but the wood chipped. In the end Panu screwed them about 2 cm (1) distance from the edge.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5546549098/" title="Building a rack / screen by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Building a rack / screen" height="333" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5132/5546549098_1a337f0ac5.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Because our rack is very top-heavy, we tried to add more weight to the bottom, so it wouldnt flip easily. The pipes are heavy and the board is quite light. If there were small children around, I would make the board wider, so the castors would be wider apart, or add even more weight to the bottom. Maybe a thicker and/or heavier board for example.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5546549366/" title="Screwing it on by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Screwing it on" height="333" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5099/5546549366_4ec73a890c.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Bolt the flanges onto the board and secure with nuts on the other side.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5545967697/" title="Pains by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Paints" height="377" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5060/5545967697_85f1a4e2c8.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Now you have a rack. You can leave it as it is, or paint it. We used a primer that suits both metal and wood. After priming, I painted two coats with window and door paint. Its still a bit transparent, but I like it that way.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5546549502/" title="Hanging the fabric by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Hanging the fabric" height="377" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5055/5546549502_06c5cf54a9.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Now its time to get your fabric out. We were going to buy
            <a href="http://www.marimekko.com/collections/interior-decoration/fabrics/cotton-fabrics/keisarinna-630" target="_blank">
             pink Keisarinna
            </a>
            fabric by Marimekko, but realized we had an old curtain of Marimekkos
            <a href="http://www.marimekko.com/collections/interior-decoration/fabrics/cotton-fabrics/ananas-fabric-664" target="_blank">
             green Ananas
            </a>
            at the attic. It happened to be just the right size. When I get tired of Ananas, Keisarinna is next in line.
           </p>
           <p>
            Iron your fabric and trim the sides if necessary. One option is is to sew buttons on the fabric, that way you can change it easily. However, I opted for hand-stitching. Because I might want to use mine as a curtain again, I didnt want button holes on it. (Bad mistake.)
           </p>
           <p>
            Get desperate, because your hand-stitching looks worse than a first-graders. Promise your spouse/boyfriend/girlfriend/family member you will peel potatoes if he/she will do the rest of the stiching. Hope that no one ever looks at your screen up close.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5546565998/" title="Finished screen by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Finished screen" height="333" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5300/5546565998_04554c8f17.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            TA-DAH! And you are finished! Admire your work (from a distance). Make a mental note next time to use buttons.
           </p>
           <p>
            If the fabric gets creased from sewing, spray water lightly on it. Wait til next morning until it has dried and straightened.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5546567858/" title="It could also be a rack by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="It could also be a rack" height="753" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5255/5546567858_43696b160d_b.jpg" style="border: none; padding: 0;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            If you (no longer) need a screen you can easily turn it into a normal rack. Ive always dreamt of one so this was the perfect excuse to build it.
           </p>

            </a>
           </p>

          <h2 class="topTitle">
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/2011/02/14/screen-options/">
            Screen options
           </a>
          </h2>
          <div class="CommentsBubble">
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/2011/02/14/screen-options/#comments" title="Comment on Screen options">
            20
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="cleared">
          </div>
          <p class="topMeta">
           by
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/author/jutta/" rel="author" title="Posts by Jutta">
            Jutta
           </a>
           on Feb.14, 2011, under
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/category/decorating/" rel="category tag">
            decorating
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/category/diy/" rel="category tag">
            DIY
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/category/inspiration/" rel="category tag">
            inspiration
           </a>
          </p>
          <div class="topContent">
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5444761152/" title="Screen option 1 by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Screen option 1" height="333" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5218/5444761152_d9fb987a64.jpg" style="border:none;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Oh my, I am blown away by your great ideas on the
            <a href="http://www.kootutmurut.com/2011/02/10/new-kinda-light-table/">
             light table post
            </a>
            ! Thank you!! My brain has been working full speed ever since. At first I was thinking about a screen of lower height with a sheer light fabric, like
            <a href="http://www.zuhauseingermany.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">
             Juliette
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://georgiannalane.blogspot.com/ " target="_blank">
             Georgianna
            </a>
            suggested. From Kirsi I got the idea that the fabric could be patterned,like wallpaper. But turns out that a screen with a fabric streched inside it is not so easy to build. Especially since I want to be able to change the fabric when I get tired of it (which is probably at least twice a year).
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5444761292/" title="Clothes rack by Seletti by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Clothes rack by Seletti" height="532" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5299/5444761292_04648fdcf3_o.jpg" style="border:none;" width="490"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Not to worry.
            <a href="http://www.pellonpientareella.com/" target="_blank">
             Pellon pientareella
            </a>
            suggested I get a coat rack from Ikea to hang the fabric. The
            <a href="http://www.ikea.com/fi/fi/catalog/products/60179434" target="_blank">
             Ikea racks
            </a>
            are inexpensive, but Im afraid they wouldnt last our dogs. We need something more sturdy. Last night I did some intensive googling but it seems that beautiful coat racks are difficult to find! You always see gorgeous interior shots with coat racks and assume you can buy them anywhere. Not true. However, I did manage to find one that is exactly what I had in mind. Its a clothes rack made from hydraulic pipes by
            <a href="http://www.seletti.com/storage/06contrasto.html" target="_blank">
             Seletti
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5444761248/" title="DIY coat rack by Design*Sponge by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="DIY coat rack by Design*Sponge" height="685" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5258/5444761248_3b5a946531_b.jpg" style="border:none;" width="490"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            I also found a DIY project on
            <a href="http://www.designspongeonline.com/2009/06/diy-project-kates-rolling-coat-rack.html" target="_blank">
             Design*Sponge
            </a>
            how to make one yourself. Now I cant make up my mind what the fabric should be like. I did some Photoshop magic to test a few. There is the option of white, sheer fabric like in the top photo. Its best for the light flowing in the room. But what about a strong statement like the pink
            <a href="http://www.finnishdesignshop.com/decoration-fabrics-marimekko-fabrics-keisarinna-fabric-pink-p-2671.html" target="_blank">
             Keisarinna fabric
            </a>
            from
            <a href="http://www.marimekko.com/" target="_blank">
             Marimekko?
            </a>
            I
            <em>
             have
            </em>
            been thinking I should simplify the living room, theres so many colors. But how can I resist a bold, colorful fabric like that?
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5444159659/" title="Screen option 3 by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Screen option 3" height="333" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4100/5444159659_dac7ec22bc.jpg" style="border:none;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            Or One could always go with a more neutral palette
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/kootutmurut/5444159567/" title="Screen option 2 by jutta / kootut murut, on Flickr">
             <img alt="Screen option 2" height="333" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5298/5444159567_9b72c31be7.jpg" style="border:none;" width="500"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            What do you think?
           </p>
