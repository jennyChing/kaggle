        <div class="wwsgd" style="display:none;">

        <p>
         <img alt="Chef Orlando" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-15809" height="188" src="http://wanderlustandlipstick.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/Chef-Orlando.jpg" title="Chef Orlando" width="250"/>
         You can be in the running for a $100 gift card from American Express, simply by telling me about your favorite restaurants in the New Orleans area!
        </p>
        <p>
         Over the past 2 weeks, youve been helping me plan a long weekend get away, courtesy of American Express. First, the consensus was
         <a href="../2011/tell-me-where-to-go-win-a-100-amex-gift-card/">
          to send me to New Orleans
         </a>
         . Then you guided me with
         <a href="http://wanderlustandlipstick.com/2011/youre-sending-me-to-new-orleans-now-i-need-a-hotel/">
          some places to stay
         </a>
         . And NOW, you can help me eat!
        </p>
        <p>
         So, with the 150,000 points that have been added to my AmEx card, Im flying to Cajun country (50,000 points for the flight). Because I couldnt resist the chance to stay in the heart of Cajun country where I could easily venture to zydeco and Cajun performances, I decided to stay at at the Old Castillo Bed and Breakfast in Lafayette (55,000 points).
        </p>
        <p>
         And now, because a girls gotta eat, I need a few suggestions for meals! And guess what, you might be rewarded for helping me!
        </p>
        <p>
         Just leave leave a comment with your ideas about places to eat in the New Orleans area and youll be
         <strong>
          entered to win a $100 gift card from AmEx
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Weve got about 45,000 points to work with on the food front and here are my thoughts around meals:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Though Im mostly a vegetarian and sometimes a vegan, I will allow some seafood into my diet when I travel.
         </li>
         <li>
          Meals dont have to be Michelin-rated. I love local specialties, roadside stands and anything that will help me meet the locals.
         </li>
         <li>
          Ill likely have a rental car and I dont mind driving well outside the city of New Orleans or Lafaytte for something authentic and interesting!
         </li>
         <li>
          Please check the
          <a href="https://axptravel.americanexpress.com/consumertravel/travel.do?a=reward-travel&amp;inav=travel_rewardtravel" target="_blank">
           Pay with Points
          </a>
          site to see if your suggested restaurant is actually available.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         I can purchase a $100 OpenTable gift card for 12,500 AmEx points. A couple of these cards will give me the chance to enjoy some incredible meals. Here are a few restaurants that struck me:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/Jacmelinn" target="_blank">
           Jacmel Inn
          </a>
           Louisiana Contemporary Cuisine in Hammond, LA
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://danteskitchen.com/" target="_blank">
           Dantes Kitchen
          </a>
           Wide range of Louisiana cooking in New Orleans
          <strong>
           <img alt="Dantes Kitchen" class="alignright size-full wp-image-15810" height="206" src="http://wanderlustandlipstick.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/Dantes.jpg" title="Dantes Kitchen" width="310"/>
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.bernadettesrestaurant.com/" target="_blank">
           Burnadettes Restaurant
          </a>
          - Fine French and Cajun cuisines in Burnside
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Have a different idea on how I can use my AmEx points? Check out these restaurants on the
         <a href="http://www.opentable.com/best-new-orleans-louisiana-restaurants?li=928&amp;topic=Creole+%2f+Cajun+%2f+Southern" target="_blank">
          OpenTable site
         </a>
         and then let me know what you think!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         You can also check out the
         <a href="http://bit.ly/jwcUpA" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          American Express Membership Rewards
         </a>
         site. Heads up that this is a bit more limiting in terms of options meaning there are fewer restaurants listed and many are chains.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         *****
        </p>
        <p>
         Once youve come up with a good recommendation for
         <strong>
          restaurants
         </strong>
         <em>
          or
         </em>
         you like one of the options Ive mentioned above, leave a comment and tell me where to eat. Its that easy. Youre then
         <strong>
          in the running for the $100 gift card from American Express
         </strong>
         !
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Rules
         </strong>
          In order to qualify for the the $100 gift card from American Express, an email must be entered for each comment so that we may contact you. Only one email/person per week, please. You may, however, enter each week for the 3 weeks for a total of 3 tries at winning. Multiple entries in the same week will be disqualified.
        </p>
        <p>
         Each weeks contest will be closed at the end of the 7th day of that weeks post. Dates are July 11, July 18 and July 25 at 23:59 PST. (This means you cannot go back and enter your suggestion for Week 1 if you discover the contest during Week 3.)
        </p>
        <p>
         A random winner, based on all 3 weeks comments, will be chosen on July 27th.
        </p>
        <p>
         Winner must reply within 3 days of being notified, otherwise, a new winner will be chosen.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          OK, tell me where to eat

         <div class="comment-content">
          <p>
           For authentic Spanish food in a beautiful old building on the Mississippi, be sure to go to Galvez Restaurant, in the French Market District, the Beet Salad was delish! i know you dont eat meat,but the filet on the Surf and Turf was indeed one of the best steaks I have ever had. I enjoyed the Bouillabaise, and overabundant stew of seafood!
          </p>
          <p>
           The Crescent City Brewhouse is the perfect place to get fresh shucked local oysters and a pint of some local brew, sit at the bar and you can watch the shucker open your oysters while you enjoy a nice cold one.   I have eaten a lot of oysters in my life and the first time I had a Louisianna oyster, I was Pleasantly surprised, they are quite a thick and meaty variety, rich and fulfilling! As a rule of thumb though, it is not recommended to eat southern oysters in months that do not contain an R, these being the hottest months of the year when the water is warmest and bacteria counts are highest.
          </p>
          <p>
           You must go to the Cafe du Monde and get beignets and coffee or milk; it is quite a simple, unique cafe with an extremely limited menuthe line can be long at different times of the day, usually it moves along pretty good though.   Its always a good time for fried dough with sugar!!!
          </p>
          <p>
           For a traditional, gourmet, dining experience in an old restaurant in the French Quarter you must go to Muriels; the food and service are superior, the Tuna Carpacio just melts in your mouth and the Puppy Drum was a nice local fish  entree,it was crusted with pecans.   After living in so. FL for so long, I really enjoy the old buildings that New Orleans is made of.
          </p>
