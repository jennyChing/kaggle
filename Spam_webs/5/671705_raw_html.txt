          Make an Edible Candy Bowl
         </a>
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="post-date1">
        <span style="font-size: 12px">
         Dec
        </span>
        <br/>
        <span style="font-size: 21px">
         20
        </span>

      <div class="metadata">

      <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14238430" data-link="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/" data-summary="" data-title="Make an Edible Candy Bowl">
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/candy-bowl-with-text/" rel="attachment wp-att-3290">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3290" height="399" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/candy-bowl-with-text-600x399.jpg" title="candy bowl with text" width="600"/>
       </a>
       Todays post is a lesson in improvisation. Why? Well, this project was INTENDED to be a candy tray, a fancy version of something I saw done flawlessly
       <a href="http://tuttobellablog.com/2011/12/19/diy-serving-tray-2/">
        at Tutto Bella
       </a>
       . After reading through that tutorial, I thought it would be a breeze! Famous last words, right?
      </p>
      <p>
       Now, if I was a crafting perfectionist, or blogging perfectionist, or if Id had two more cups of coffee today, maybe Idve tried a second time to make my vision workbut Im not, Im not, and I didnt. So, instead, you get first dibs at seeing my next submission to
       <a href="http://craftfail.com/">
        Craft Fail
       </a>
       , and hopefully a little inspiration to think quickly on your crafting feet before you completely scrap the next project that doesnt turn out as it should. Also, please do try this project with your kiddos! Its a great activity for this holiday break, costs only $2 or $3, and yours might actually turn out properly!
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/candy-bowl-supplies/" rel="attachment wp-att-3283">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3283" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/candy-bowl-supplies-300x199.jpg" title="candy bowl supplies" width="300"/>
       </a>
       Supplies:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        candy canes- $1 for twelve
       </li>
       <li>
        old fashioned hard candies- $1
       </li>
       <li>
        starlight mints- $1 for a bag, I used just one for this particular project
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/candy-bowl-canes/" rel="attachment wp-att-3284">
        <img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3284" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/candy-bowl-canes-300x199.jpg" title="candy bowl canes" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/candy-bowl-smash/" rel="attachment wp-att-3285">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3285" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/candy-bowl-smash-300x199.jpg" title="candy bowl smash" width="300"/>
       </a>
       Unwrap the candy canes and starlight mint and arrange on a baking sheet lined with wax paper or parchment paper. Itll be okay if there are some spaces in between candy canes, as theyll spread when they melt. Put the hard candies into a bag and pound them with a rolling pin or other hard object to break them into little pieces. The littler the pieces the better here (I realized this too late).
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/candy-bowl-prebake/" rel="attachment wp-att-3286">
        <img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3286" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/candy-bowl-prebake-300x199.jpg" title="candy bowl prebake" width="300"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/candy-bowl-fail/" rel="attachment wp-att-3287">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3287" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/candy-bowl-fail-300x199.jpg" title="candy bowl fail" width="300"/>
       </a>
       Bake at 350 for 8-10 minutes, but watch it closely the whole time!! Please tell me you can see what I was going for in this first picture? I envisioned a stained glass window sort of effecta beautiful little tray to serve treats or leave out with Santas cookies on Christmas Eve. Exceptwell, you can see what happened. The outsides melted way faster than the insides, and bubbled up, ruining the look I was hoping for. I pulled it out of the oven, and set it aside for a minute planning to just throw it away.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/candy-bowl-improv/" rel="attachment wp-att-3288">
        <img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3288" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/candy-bowl-improv-300x199.jpg" title="candy bowl improv" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/12/make-an-edible-candy-bowl/candy-bowl-complete/" rel="attachment wp-att-3289">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3289" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/candy-bowl-complete-300x199.jpg" title="candy bowl complete" width="300"/>
       </a>
       Then I realized it was still quite hot and pretty pliable, so I quickly grabbed a bowl, turned it upside down and placed the whole thing on top. I tried to form it a bit as I went along, very gently so it wouldnt crack and break. I let it cool completely, turned it over and removed the wax paper. Is it the cutest thing youve ever seen? I sure hope not!!! But its not a total flop! Its a cute way to display candies on the counter, or to hold trail mix at your holiday party this weekend. Its fragile, it wont last a super long time. But honestly Im kinda proud of it! It looks interesting, and will probably be a great conversation starter when friends come to call!
      </p>

I took a sucker stick-(like a tootsie pop,or anything round to put a hole in it so I can put a ribbon in it and hang on the tree.It turned out nice. I just experimented the first time.
