   <h4>
    Is there a Cure for Eczema?
   </h4>
   <h4>
    <span style="color: #ff9900;">
     <em>
      What is Eczema?
     </em>
    </span>
   </h4>
   <p>
    The word
    <strong>
     eczema
    </strong>
    could be used to refer to a family of skin conditions that has to do with the distortion of the skin surface. It is characterised with inflamed patches of the skin, marked and itchy. Also referred to as atopic dermatitis, it is very common in young children and babies, however, adults are also victims of this condition. It appears on the face, the elbow, behind the knee, the back and several other parts of the body. This skin condition is caused by an overactive immune system and research conducted by the American Academy of Dermatology has revealed that about 20% of children suffer from this skin condition.
   </p>
   <p>
   </p>

   <h4>
   </h4>
   <h4>
    <span style="color: #ff9900;">
     <em>
      Types of Eczema
     </em>
    </span>
   </h4>
   <p>
    There are different skin conditions that could lead to eczema. A comprehensive understanding of them is important in order to develop an effective treatment plan. Here are some different types of eczema:
   </p>
   <ul>
    <li>
     <strong>
      Atopic Dermatitis
     </strong>
     : This is very generic and is mostly referred to as eczema. It begins early in life for those who were exposed to such things as inhalant allergies. Sufferers generally experience itching in the elbow, neck, knee and ankle, but can also experience on other parts of the body.
    </li>
    <li>
     <strong>
      Irritant dermatitis
     </strong>
     : When the skin is repeatedly exposed to toxic substances, this can occur.
    </li>
    <li>
     <strong>
      Allergic contact dermatitis
     </strong>
     : When you are repeatedly exposed to a poisonous substance, the immune system of the body becomes more active at that site, leading to the development of eczema.
    </li>
    <li>
     <strong>
      Stasis dermatitis
     </strong>
     : For those who have poor blood circulation in the veins in the legs, they tend to suffer from this type of eczema.
    </li>
    <li>
     <strong>
      Fungal Infections
     </strong>
     : This develops a pattern identical to other forms of eczema. When scraped under a microscope, the fungus can be identified.
    </li>
    <li>
     <strong>
      Lichen simplex chronicus
     </strong>
     : This is the formation of thick plaques of skin around the shins and neck.
    </li>
    <li>
     <strong>
      Nummular Eczema:
     </strong>
     This is observed as plaques of scaling skin in the form of coin-shapes, affecting the lower legs of the sufferer.
    </li>
    <li>
     <strong>
      Seborrheic eczema
     </strong>
     : This appears in the form of a rash on the scalp, ears, face and in some cases, mid chest region. This is common in infants, producing an oozy rash behind the ears. In some cases, it could affect the entire body.
    </li>
   </ul>

     <em>
      Eczema Facts

    </li>
    <li>
     Distinguishing between the different types of eczema is important so that you will know which treatment will be most effective.
    </li>
    <li>

   <h4>
   </h4>
   <h4>
    <span style="color: #ff9900;">
     <em>
      Causes of Eczema
     </em>
    </span>
   </h4>
   <p>
    There are a lot of factors that could lead to eczema, although the cause of some types are not well known, others are clear cut. For example, irritant dermatitis, a type of eczema, is caused after a brief exposure to a strong irritant such as acid from a battery or any other string substance of such nature. Other types like allergic contact dermatitis is formed when a substance to which a person is allergic is exposed to touches the skin. Some of these substances include nickel and poison ivy. Interestingly, many items used by humans contain nickel, some of which are jewellery, coins, buttons, eye glass frames and many more.
   </p>
   <p>
    Although the exact cause of eczema is still being researched, it is believed that atopic dermatitis is a result of a combination of factors which includes the genes of the sufferer, an overactive immune system, leading to what is referred to as barrier effect. Barrier effect means gap in the skin. The gap allows the skin to lose water. This provides an avenue for germs and other contaminants to enter the body.
   </p>
   <p>
    Another type of eczema that is formed through the interaction of several factors is Seborrheic Eczema. This includes the genes, live yeast in the skin, climates, stress and the general health condition of the sufferer. It has been observed through research that this form of eczema is prevalent in those who are suffering from HIV, and this is an indication that the immune system of the sufferer plays a role in this.
   </p>
