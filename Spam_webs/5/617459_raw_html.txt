      <p>
       <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        New guidelines for preventing heart attacks and strokes are using aformula for estimating someone's risk levels that includes many factors besides cholesterol. For the first time in over 10 years, America is using new criteria to determine who should be taking cholesterol-satin drugs, and this will include twice as many Americans as before. The
        <a href="http://www.heart.org/HEARTORG/">
         American Heart Association
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.cardiosource.org/acc">
         American College of Cardiology
        </a>
        recently issued theseguidelines that call for considering many factors besides just cholesterol, which is the main focus now. They are taking aim at strokes, not just heart attacks and setting a lower threshold for using medicines to reduce risk.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        The new criteria take into consideration among other things:
       </span>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         People with heart disease
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         Those with LDL ("bad" cholesterol) of 190 or higher
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         People aged 40 to 75 with Type 2 Diabetes
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         People aged 40 to 75 with an estimated 10 year risk of heart attackof 7.5% or higher
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        According to the new guidelines, if you answered yes to any of the above questions, you should be on a statin. These are drugs that treat high cholesterol and heart disease, and you can compare them by effectiveness, safety and price
        <a href="http://www.consumerreports.org/health/resources/pdf/best-buy-drugs/StatinsUpdate-FINAL.pdf">
         HERE
        </a>
        . How do you determineif your 10 yr risk of heart attackis 7.5% or higher? In
        <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/11/12/health/cholesterol-guidelines/index.html?hpt=hp_t1#cnn-disqus-area">
         this CNN article
        </a>
        Dr. Donald Lloyd-Jones explains how age, sex, race, among other factors affect your risk level.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        To estimate your 10 yr risk of having a heart attack, use this
        <a href="http://cvdrisk.nhlbi.nih.gov/calculator.asp">
         free risk assessment tool
        </a>
        from the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute.
       </span>
      </p>
