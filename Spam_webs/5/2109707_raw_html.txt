      <a href="http://www.messynessychic.com/2013/05/02/paris-sous-vos-pieds/parisreservoir/" rel="attachment wp-att-36405">
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-36405" height="729" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Parisreservoir.jpg" title="Parisreservoir" width="1405"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <span style="color: #000000;">
       <strong>
        <em>
         T
        </em>
       </strong>
       his breathtaking pool of glistening turquoise water under creamy ecclesiastical stone vaulting is the underground water reservoir tank of Mountsouris, a hidden treasure in the south of the Paris
      </span>
      . Sitting undisturbed beneath the feet of unknowing Parisians, the original entrance is unfortunately closed to the public. The photograph above was taken by urban explorer photographer
      <strong>
       <a href="http://www.degraaf.fr" target="_blank">
        De Graaf
       </a>
      </strong>
      .
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <a href="http://www.messynessychic.com/2013/05/02/paris-sous-vos-pieds/montsouris2/" rel="attachment wp-att-36418">
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-36418" height="420" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/montsouris2.jpg" title="montsouris2" width="600"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <em>
       <a href="http://www.paris.fr/accueil/accueil-paris-fr/a-la-decouverte-du-reservoir-de-montsouris/rub_1_actu_119358_port_24329" target="_blank">
        Franois Grunberg
       </a>
      </em>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      A few trout can occasionally be found enjoying to the waters purity in the tank whos surface area reaches up to three hectares and holds 202 000 m of water. When it was builtin 1874 by Eugne Belgrand, a Haussman engineer, it was, at this time, the biggest tank in the world.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <a href="http://www.messynessychic.com/2013/05/02/paris-sous-vos-pieds/monstouris/" rel="attachment wp-att-36419">
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-36419" height="319" src="http://static.messynessychic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/monstouris.jpg" title="monstouris" width="480"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      <em>
       Entrance to the underwater tank, prohibited to the public
      </em>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: justify;">
      Today it still supplies 1/5 of the Parisian population with water in the south and the centre of Paris. The Montsouris tank is said to have a better taste than all the other sources in the capital.
     </p>
