           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             An important part of this is to not to see yourself through the eyes of others. Doing that can build a self-image and expectations that become very hard to live up to. It can make you end up filled up with negative feelings and that wont help anyone. And, as I said above, you dont really know what people think about you anyway. Your worries about what people may think about you are probably mostly in your head.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             What they may actually think about you shouldnt take too seriously anyway since its just their opinion. And such things are
             <a href="http://www.positivityblog.com/index.php/2007/08/20/how-to-handle-criticism-and-get-something-good-out-of-it/">
              often more about the guy/gal with the opinion
             </a>
             rather than the person/thing they have an opinion about.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <strong>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            2. Forget about perfectionism.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
         <blockquote>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Perfectionism is pretty pointless. Its just you putting unnecessary pressure on yourself and thereby reducing your ability to do excellent work. Take a look at the next point to find a better replacement for perfectionism.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <strong>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            3. Focus on the process, not on the outcome.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
         <blockquote>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Detaching from the possible outcomes and just keeping your focus on the work you are doing makes it easier to both live and live up to your potential by reducing many of your inner, mental obstacles such as performance anxiety and other worries. You can read more about this in
             <a href="http://www.positivityblog.com/index.php/2007/10/10/the-relaxifying-secret-to-success/">
              The Relaxifying Secret to Success
             </a>
             .
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <strong>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            4. Be OK with mistakes and failure.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
         <blockquote>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             This one ties in to the two previous tips. If you allow yourself to redefine mistakes youll be less prone to get yourself stuck in perfectionism.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             And the world wont collapse if you make mistakes or fail.
            </span>
           </span>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            The morning of the day when you learned to ride a bike you fell off it time and time again. But you just brushed yourself off, perhaps cried for a minute or two and then you got up on the bike again. And towards the afternoon, or the next day, you probably started to become pretty good at riding your bike.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            The same applies here. You have work on your skills to sharpen them. See failure or rejection not as something incredible negative that might end your life if it strikes. Redefine it in your mind to lessen the negative emotional impact and the fear. See failure simply as feedback on what you need to improve on. Listen to the advice that failure gives you and you will improve.
            <span class="body">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <strong>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            5. Just do it!
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
         <blockquote>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             If you often get stuck in procrastination and get little done the pressure can start to build up. Just doing it is a great way to get out of an overthinking or procrastinating state and just get going with what you want to do.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             But thats not always so easy. Then everybody would be doing it.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             So, whats the problem? I think two important factors are that you may get too wrapped up and too closely identified with your thoughts and emotions. But you arent them, you are the observer of them. So you dont have to obey what your thoughts or what your inner emotional resistance may say. You can get going anyway. For a more thorough explanation of this have a look at
             <a href="http://www.positivityblog.com/index.php/2007/10/02/just-do-it/">
              Just Do It!
             </a>
             .
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <strong>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <strong>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            6. Cut down on busywork and unimportant stuff.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
         <blockquote>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Feeling like you just have too much to do and too little time for the most important things? Then you probably need to do some decluttering in your life. Eliminate some of the less important stuff.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             To prioritize and find whats really important to spend most of your time on, both at work/in school and life in general you can use
             <a href="http://www.positivityblog.com/index.php/2006/11/12/prioritize-with-the-pareto-principle/">
              the 80/20 rule
             </a>
             .
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             To not get stuck in that unimportant busywork ask yourself: 
             <a href="http://www.positivityblog.com/index.php/2007/05/08/is-this-useful/">
              Is this, what Im doing right now, useful?
             </a>
              a few times throughout your day. To remember to actually ask yourself this, consider writing it down on a piece of paper and put that paper where you cant avoid seeing it. Or use that question as your screensaver.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             I also think its important to realize that you dont really have to do everything you are doing. You always have a choice. Sometimes there will be big consequences if you choose to do or not do something. But you always have a choice. And if you choose to spend more of your time on the most important stuff and less  or none  on the unimportant things your life will most likely improve in any area you apply this thinking.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <strong>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            7. Dont take things too seriously.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
         <blockquote>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Im not saying that you shouldnt do a good job. Or that you should avoid responsibility. But having the attitude that the sky will fall down if something goes wrong is most often inaccurate outside of your own head. And its not a good headspace to be in to perform or feel well. I guess this one ties into many of the other tips but I think its good to emphasise how helpful a more relaxed attitude can be both for you and the people around you.
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Realise you have a choice about what attitude you should adopt, just like with what you choose to do/not do. It might take some time to replace an old and ingrained habit for another. But over time you can do it. If youd like further tips on living a more relaxed life
             <a href="http://www.positivityblog.com/index.php/2007/03/15/14-sure-fire-ways-to-live-a-more-relaxing-life/">
              here are 14 of them
             </a>
             . And here are
             <a href="http://www.positivityblog.com/index.php/2007/06/02/13-additional-sure-fire-ways-to-live-a-more-relaxing-life/">
              13 more
             </a>
             .
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <strong>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            8. Take 30 belly breaths.
           </span>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
         <span class="body">
          <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div align="left">
         <blockquote>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
           <span class="body">
            <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             This is more of a temporary solution compared to the ones above. But its also an immediate one. If you are feeling pressured and stressed out right now, you can use a few dozen belly breaths or so to relieve some of that for a while. Heres what you do:
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 54pt; line-height: 13pt">
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Sit in a relaxing position with your legs apart.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 54pt; line-height: 13pt">
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Put your hands on your stomach. Using your stomach breathe in slowly through your nose. If you are doing it right your stomach will expand and youll feel it with your hands.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 54pt; line-height: 13pt">
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Breathe out slowly through your nose and do it with some force so you feel your stomach pull slightly inwards towards your spine.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 54pt; line-height: 13pt">
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             Breathe in and out 30 times. Take
           </span>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            slow
           </span>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            and
           </span>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            deep
           </span>
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
            breaths.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 54pt; line-height: 13pt">
           <span lang="EN-GB" style="color: #353535">
             After you have taken 30 breaths and focused on counting them you should not only feel more relaxed and centered. Your body will also be able to continue breathing in this manner without you focusing on it. And thats it. Continue with your normal day.
            <span class="body">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
        </div>
        <p align="left" class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 13pt">
