            <h2>
             Auschwitz-Birkenau Concentration Camp, Poland
            </h2>
            <div class="wpgeo_map wp_geo_map" id="wpgeo_map_2617_2" style="width:100%;height:300px;">
            </div>
            <p>
             If you consider just the numbers, its hard to comprehend. When you think about the individuals, it starts to feel more real. Galka Wladyslaw, inmate number 10557. Wrebski Walenty, inmate number 10410. Libucha Jan, inmate 10743.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-323_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12255" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-323_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Their names, their photos and their numbers line the hall of one of the internment buildings of the German concentration and extermination camp, Auschwitz. One has a flower placed on it. Many of these people have faded into history, the names forgotten. Thankfully the crimes have not been.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-306_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12259" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-306_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-338_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12252" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-338_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             But for every name on this wall there are thousands more who never even had their photo taken. The men and women with records here were at least given the chance to survive the concentration camp at Auschwitz, however awful that experience must have been. The others never had a hope.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-356_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12249" height="1024" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-356_web-lrg.jpg" width="683"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Hundreds of thousands of prisoners were taken by the German guards straight from the train to the gas chambers. It wasnt until the last moment that they realised what was happening  their fate had been determined long before that, though.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-318_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12257" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-318_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-322_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12256" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-322_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <h2>
             Visiting Auschwitz
            </h2>
            <p>
             L. Berman from Hamburg. The name is handwritten on a suitcase that is part of a pile on display in one room of the Auschwitz museum. The other collections of shoes, glasses, plates, and trinkets are also all belongings which once had owners. They are the reminders of those who walked straight from a train carriage to their death.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-309_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12258" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-309_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             When they entered the room they thought they were having a shower and being deloused. When the pellets of poison were dropped in through holes in the roof, and the reality struck, the guards of the camp were already thinking about rummaging through those suitcases for valuables. They didnt realise the most valuable thing was written on the outside of the case a name.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-358_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12248" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-358_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: left;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-327_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12254" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-327_web-lrg.jpg" style="float: right;" width="49.5%"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <div style="clear: both;">
            </div>
            <p>
             In another room of the museum, the memories of the individuals are blurred into an amorphous tangle. You can see visitors inhale and reel as they enter the room and see, behind metres and metres of glass, an enormous pile of hair. You can make out different colours and different lengths, but theyre all mixed together into a heap as large as a van. The guards at Auschwitz shaved the heads of their prisoners and were planning to sell the hair. These people were just a commodity to them.
            </p>
            <p>
             1.3 million people. Thats the figure now accepted as the death toll of Auschwitz, although therell never be a way to know for sure. Theres a strange symmetry in that number  its exactly the same as the number of visitors who now, more than six decades later, pass through the gates to see the site for themselves.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-354_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12250" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-354_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-335_web-lrg.jpg">
              <img alt="auschwitz, visiting auschwitz, museum, poland, birkenau, german concentration camp" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12253" height="683" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/Poland-2012-335_web-lrg.jpg" width="1024"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Imagine if every one of those visitors was killed and the last sights they saw were the train tracks, the gates, the smiling guards, the chamber and the screaming prisoners climbing over each other to get away from the poison. Just imagine.
            </p>
            <p>
             How could humans do that to other humans? How could they get away with it?
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/UNESCO_Logo.jpg">
              <img alt="UNESCO world heritage site" class="alignnone wp-image-2418" height="75" src="http://www.timetravelturtle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/UNESCO_Logo-150x150.jpg" style="float: left;" title="UNESCO_Logo" width="75"/>
             </a>

            </p>
