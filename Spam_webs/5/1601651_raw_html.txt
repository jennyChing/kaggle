        <hr/>
        <p>
         The International Consumer Electronics Show likes to go big: big numbers (140,000+ attendees), big announcements and, well, big flops. This year's CES is no different.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A Rift with reality
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Everyones curious about Oculus Rift, the virtual reality headset Facebook spent a fortune to acquire in March. Theres still no retail product still not in sight, but a lucky few got to try it at CES. And apparently,
         <a href="http://www.cnet.com/products/oculus-rift/">
          Oculus Rift keeps getting better
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          So that happened 
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://www.cnet.com/videos/xyzprinting-3d-food-printer-can-make-cookies-and-more/">
          food printer
         </a>
         is getting ready for mass market. Hungry? Just print some cookies. Nauseated? I can't help you there.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Straight from sci-fi
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mercedes demoed a new concept car that that looksand is
         <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2015/1/5/7495355/mercedes-benz-f-015-luxury-in-motion-self-driving-ces-2015">
          pleasingly futuristic
         </a>
         . Its self-driving, of course, and Mercedes make a major redesign of the interior space that tips its hat to the fact that driving ourselves will look and feel different: The front seats actually turn around to face the rear ones. Make sure to check out the
         <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2015/1/5/7495355/mercedes-benz-f-015-luxury-in-motion-self-driving-ces-2015">
          photos
         </a>
         . And in an invention that's both sci-fi-worthy and practical, BMW previewed an automobile that retrieves itself from a parking garage.
        </p>
        <p>
         For those of us who wont be running out to buy a Mercedesor upgrading our clunkeranytime soon, Parrot offers a great product to
         <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2015/1/4/7491577/parrot-rnb6-smartcar-screen-apple-carplay-android-auto-ces-2015">
          endow your old car with modern functionalities
         </a>
         like gas mileage tracking. The dashboard connects through any car that can accommodate a CD player (if youre still driving that classic with the 8-track, youre out of luck) to connect to your Android or iPhone, allowing you to use voice commands to control your apps. It also hooks into a camera you can mount to provide features like lane assist and braking alerts.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          For true IT geeks
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         For a sneak preview at some of the high-end machines Microsoft unveiled behind the scenes, check out
         <a href="http://www.geekwire.com/2015/ces-heres-microsoft-showing-behind-scenes/" target="_blank">
          Geekwires alluring photos
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A different point of view: 20 reasons why CES is just the worst
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just
         <a href="http://pando.com/2015/01/06/20-reasons-why-ces-is-just-the-worst/">
          read the article
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On the Verge of sanity
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Amusingly, a Verge writer thinks a high-end wireless router looks just a
         <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2015/1/4/7491241/ces-2015-insane-wireless-router-d-link">
          little too advanced
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy reading about tech trends? Check out

          Are robots coming for your job?
         </a>
        </p>
