       <h1 class="entry-title">
        How To Make Crustless Pizza
       </h1>
       <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190511_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]" title="How To Make Crustless Pizza">
        <img alt="110629-190511_Lg" class="post-image" height="375" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190511_Lg-500x375.jpg" width="500"/>
       </a>
       <div class="post-info">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Okay, listen up:
        </p>
        <h2 style="padding-left: 30px;">
         <strong>
          THIS. IS. AMAZING.
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Im not kidding. I came up with the recipe because
         <a href="http://willpowerisforfatpeople.com/" target="_blank">
          Im eating low carb during the week now
         </a>
         , but this is so good I have to try hard to
         <em>
          not
         </em>
         eat it on the weekend.
        </p>
        <p>
         Good wood-oven pizza gets a lot of flavor from the slightly toasted cheese. This recipe is almost
         <em>
          nothing but
         </em>
         toasted cheese. It is seriously, amazingly good.
         <br/>
         <span id="more-5738">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Ingredients
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-185519_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-185519_Lg" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-7056" height="180" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-185519_Lg-240x180.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
         1 cup fresh shredded parmigiano cheese
         <br/>
         3 slices provolone cheese
         <br/>
          cup peperoni, diced
         <br/>
         3-4 tablespoons pizza sauce
         <br/>
         <em>
           optional 
         </em>
         <br/>
         your favorite pizza toppings, diced
         <br/>
         [
         <strong>
          NOTE:
         </strong>
         See this link for
         <a href="http://cheese.about.com/od/cheesebasics/f/parm_parmigiano.htm" target="_blank">
          the difference between parmesan and parmigiano aka parmigiano-reggiano
         </a>
         .]
        </p>
        <h3 style="clear: both;">
         Directions
        </h3>
        <p>
         You might remember the
         <a href="../fried-parmigiano-bowls/index.html" target="_blank">
          fried parmigiano salad bowls
         </a>
         I did a while back. Tons of flavor, a bit of structure, but a little fragile. Then there were the
         <a href="../asiago-cheese-crisps/index.html" target="_blank">
          Asiago cheese crisps
         </a>
         . More flavor than plane Asiago, holds together well, but not much structure.
        </p>
        <p>
         Hey, I know  how about I do a hard cheese base with soft cheese on top? And thats what I did.
        </p>
        <p>
         Warm up a non-stick or well-seasoned cast iron pan over medium heat. Dump in the parmigiano and make sure it covers the bottom of the pan evenly. Add the provolone on top.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-185645_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-185645_Lg" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-7057" height="180" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-185645_Lg-240x180.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-185710_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-185710_Lg" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-7058" height="180" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-185710_Lg-240x180.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Spread out the pepperoni or other toppings.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-185832_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-185832_Lg" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-7059" height="180" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-185832_Lg-240x180.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont use big slices, dice everything nice and small. The pieces you cut this into will be much smaller than regular pizza, and youll want to have a good distribution of toppings.
        </p>
        <p>
         When the cheese is bubbling and starting to turn brown on the edges, slip a spatula under and make sure it isnt sticking. Youll want to check out
         <a href="http://willpowerisforfatpeople.com/crustless-pizza/" target="_blank">
          the video over on my other site
         </a>
         to see how.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once youre sure its not sticking  you should be able to shake the pan and the cheese will slide around  add just enough sauce to cover almost up to the edge.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190058_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-190058_Lg" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-7060" height="180" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190058_Lg-240x180.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Slide the pizza out onto a cutting board and let it rest for a minute or two.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190236_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-190236_Lg" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-7061" height="180" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190236_Lg-240x180.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This gives the cheese time to set up a little bit, and the sauce time to warm up. (Starting with room-temperature sauce instead of refrigerated is a good idea.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Slice the pizza before transferring to a plate to serve.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190422_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-190422_Lg" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-7062" height="180" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190422_Lg-240x180.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190711_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-190711_Lg" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-7064" height="180" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190711_Lg-240x180.jpg" width="240"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Hey, theres a piece missing. Yeah  it never made it onto the plate.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190511_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">
          <img alt="110629-190511_Lg" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7063" height="375" src="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190511_Lg-500x375.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         This is one crust that nobody is going to feed to the dog after eating the good part.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cooklikeyourgrandmother.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/110629-190802_Lg.jpg" rel="lightbox[5738]">

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 cup fresh shredded parmigiano cheese
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             3 slices provolone cheese
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
              cup peperoni, diced
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             3-4 tablespoons pizza sauce
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             -- optional --
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             your favorite pizza toppings, diced
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <span class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add the parmigiano cheese and make sure it covers the bottom of the pan evenly. Top with the provolone, and the pepperoni and whatever toppings you like.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             When the cheese is bubbling and starting to brown on the edges, slip a spatula under to make sure it's not sticking. When the cheese slides around easily without any sticking, top with sauce almost to the edge.
            </p>
            <p class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Slide out onto a cutting board and let it rest a minute or two. Cut into six or eight pieces and enjoy.
            </p>

            <p>
             Its always good to have at least a thin layer of a really dry, hard cheese first to give it some structure. I like parmigiano reggiano for this.
            </p>
            <p>
             One of the problems with store pre-shredded cheese is that they dust it in cellulose powder  basically really finely ground sawdust  to keep it from sticking together. By the time you overcome the cellulose, the cheese is starting to separate. Thats why I always shred my own cheese when Im doing this.
            </p>
            <p>
             Lower moisture cheese will provide more structure, high moisture provides flexibility. Its a bit of a balancing act to get it exactly right.
            </p>
