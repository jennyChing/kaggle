        <article class="post-566 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-bargain-hunter category-frequent-flyer tag-airports tag-annoyances tag-baggage tag-family-travel tag-security tag-travel-preparation" id="post-566">
         <div class="post-content">
          <!-- test &lt;img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-571" src="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-4-Red-Suitcase-in-Baggage-Claim.jpg" alt="w23-4-Red Suitcase in Baggage Claim" width="1000" height="667" /&gt; -->
          <p>
           Every holiday season, more than 100 million people jet around the country for homemade pie, holiday games, and heated arguments about Uncle Joes new girlfriend. How to get a leg up on those weary travelers? Ahead, we look at the years most need-to-know stats and situations so that you can get home for the holidays without losing your cool.
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            Buy, Baby, Buy
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="position:relative; display:block;">
            <span style="position: absolute;top: 8px;right: 10px;">
             <a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.flights.com%2Fblog%2F2014-holiday-travel-update-crowds-baggage-and-security-oh-my%2F&amp;media=http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-1-Buying-Plane-Tickets-Online.jpg&amp;description=2014+Holiday+Travel+Update%3A+Crowds%2C+Baggage%2C+and+Security%2C+Oh+My">
             </a>
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-1-Buying-Plane-Tickets-Online.jpg">
             <img alt="w23-1-Buying Plane Tickets Online" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-568" height="665" src="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-1-Buying-Plane-Tickets-Online.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           There are more opinions about the best time to buy holiday airfare than there are cheese-stained shirts in Philly, but one things for certain: If your travel dates are already solid, grab those tickets stat. Though several airlines are adding extra flights to accommodate the holiday rush, even Santa would have to fork over some crazy cash to score a last-minute seat.
          </p>
          <p>
           That said, when you fly is just as important as when you buy.
           <strong>
            Keep the below things in mind while you browse your flight options:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Thanksgiving-week departures are usually cheapest on Monday or Tuesday, but avoid the following Sunday like the plague. Its often the busiest day of the season. Youll be better off spending the extra night in your parents den and skipping town Monday morning.
           </li>
           <li>
            Because Christmas falls on a Thursday this year, weekend-to-weekend flights are destined to be doozies. The Sunday rule applies here too; unless you have a billion dollars burning a hole in your designer handbag, stick to the mid-week flights.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            Hangar in There
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="position:relative; display:block;">
            <span style="position: absolute;top: 8px;right: 10px;">
             <a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.flights.com%2Fblog%2F2014-holiday-travel-update-crowds-baggage-and-security-oh-my%2F&amp;media=http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-2-Airport-Hangar.jpg&amp;description=2014+Holiday+Travel+Update%3A+Crowds%2C+Baggage%2C+and+Security%2C+Oh+My">
             </a>
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-2-Airport-Hangar.jpg">
             <img alt="w23-2-Airport Hangar" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-569" height="667" src="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-2-Airport-Hangar.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Its the most wonderful time of the year! says anyone who hasnt slogged through an airport on December 23. No matter how many gingerbread lattes you guzzle, holiday cheer flies right out the window once those tinned carols and frazzled families start impeding on your personal space. Deck the halls with boughs of ugh.
          </p>
          <p>
           There is, however, one simple thing you can do to make the airport situation slightly less painful this year: Give yourself way more time than you think youll need. Were talking at least 90 minutes for a domestic flight and double that for international. Sure, you could wind up with time to kill, but maybe youll actually taste that gingerbread latte for once.
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            The Security Skinny
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="position:relative; display:block;">
            <span style="position: absolute;top: 8px;right: 10px;">
             <a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.flights.com%2Fblog%2F2014-holiday-travel-update-crowds-baggage-and-security-oh-my%2F&amp;media=http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-3-Airport-Security-Dog.jpg&amp;description=2014+Holiday+Travel+Update%3A+Crowds%2C+Baggage%2C+and+Security%2C+Oh+My">
             </a>
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-3-Airport-Security-Dog.jpg">
             <img alt="w23-3-Airport Security Dog" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-570" height="667" src="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-3-Airport-Security-Dog.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Wed gladly hand over the last piece of pumpkin pie for a security line that didnt snake around the terminal. Luckily for pretty much everyone, there might be a light at the end of that tunnel. This summer, the TSA began testing expedited pre-checks at airports across the country, officially known as
           <a href="http://www.tsa.gov/tsa-precheck">
            TSA Pre
           </a>
           , and officials have stated that the practice could eventually become the new normal. If thats the case, youll do a quick screening before dashing, dancing, prancing, and vixening through security without even having to take off your shoes. Talk about a Christmas miracle.
          </p>
          <p>
           Still, do the agents a favor: Dont show up with wrapped gifts. More often than not, theyll have to tear into the packages for security purposes, and thats just a pain for everyone involved. May we suggest gift cards?
          </p>
          <h2>
           <strong>
            Youve Got Baggage
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="position:relative; display:block;">
            <span style="position: absolute;top: 8px;right: 10px;">
             <a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.flights.com%2Fblog%2F2014-holiday-travel-update-crowds-baggage-and-security-oh-my%2F&amp;media=http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-4-Red-Suitcase-in-Baggage-Claim.jpg&amp;description=2014+Holiday+Travel+Update%3A+Crowds%2C+Baggage%2C+and+Security%2C+Oh+My">
             </a>
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-4-Red-Suitcase-in-Baggage-Claim.jpg">
             <img alt="w23-4-Red Suitcase in Baggage Claim" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-571" height="667" src="http://www.flights.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/w23-4-Red-Suitcase-in-Baggage-Claim.jpg" width="1000"/>
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Youve also got fees. The good news is that the old standards are mostly unchanged: Carry-on baggage is generally free, the first checked bag hovers between $15 and $25, and the second ranges from $20 to $75. Any luggage after that will give your budget a good scare  some airlines charge up to $150  but its nothing you havent muddled through before.
          </p>
          <p>
           The bad news is that were also seeing a few new rules crop up among the classics.
           <strong>
            Watch out for these baggage updates:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Though it once offered free baggage, Porter Airlines now charges $25 for a first checked bag and $35 for a second.
           </li>
           <li>
            Frontier Airlines has adopted similar fees according to weight, and its also charging $50 for any bag that has to be checked at the gate.
           </li>
           <li>
            Spirit Airlines is adding an additional $2 surcharge for all checked baggage between December 18 and January 5.
           </li>
           <li>
            The TSA has increased its fees for nonstop flights by 124%: $5.60 each way as opposed to the previous $2.50.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           Check with your airline for specifics, but dont let the extra fees get you down. One sip of Uncle Joes eggnog will clear up any case of the baggage bah-humbugs. Trust us. Theres one in every family.
          </p>

        {
            //Check the file URL parameter value

                url: mp3url
            });

            if (flg == 'play') {
