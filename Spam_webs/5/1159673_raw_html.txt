       <h1>
        100 Things to Buy Before Going to University
       </h1>
       <span class="meta">
        19 September 2014
       </span>
       <p>
        <em>
         Written by Joe Griffiths
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/joe-uni.png" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Its that time of the year again On the one hand, from a parents perspective, there will be tears. On the other hand, from a first year students perspective, there will be fist pumps. Im a fan of the student view so LET THERE BE FIST PUMPS.
       </p>
       <p>
        Yes, youve made it to university, embarking on what promises to be a potentially life changing experience. I finished my 4 year stint at the University of Gloucestershire last July; entering my beloved halls of residence a boy, leaving my own student house a man. My time there consisted of friendships, relationships, laughter, studying and sick-days. However, I couldnt have made it through in one piece without some essential purchases.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" height="320" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-life.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        If I could pass on one tip and one tip only to my fellow students; enjoy your student loan. While budgeting is seriously important (check out my survival guide for that stuff), particularly once you realise that youve literally eaten your way through first terms loan on a diet of pizza and Chinese takeaway, it wont hurt to treat yourself to a few luxuries before you get there while also ticking off the bare essentials.
       </p>
       <p>
        To help inspire you, Im listing 100 awesome things to buy before your folks ship you off to uni and you begin celebrating your independence. Better still, you can probably twist your parents arm to shell out for a load of the following items anyway. Click ahead for the top 25
        <a href="#bedroom">
         Bedroom
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="#kitchen">
         Kitchen
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="#social">
         Social
        </a>
        or
        <a href="#lifestyle">
         Lifestyle
        </a>
        items on the list.
       </p>
       <h2 id="bedroom">
        Bedroom
       </h2>
       <p>
        The first thing thats going to greet you when you arrive at university, is an incredibly plain bedroom. If youre lucky, youll have enough room to swing a cat. Think of it like a blank canvas and youre ready to chop an ear off. Fill it with as much of your personality as you can, turning it into a proper home-from-home. These are the first 25 items that you cant go without:
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h3>
        1.) Bedding
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          So youve arrived in your new room. First thing that came to my mind was wow, this party really died. The only comfort in there is a pretty basic looking bed. First things first, make it comfortable and ready for you to crawl into later. Definitely one for the bank of mum and dad to help out with.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="392" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room1.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        2.) Laptop/Mac
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Now that the beds made, you need to update your Facebook status about how cool your bed is. Whip out your brand spanking new computer and hook up to the internet ASAP. The world must hear about your new bed! A laptop isnt a bad shout for doing assignments on throughout the year either With laptops available for all budgets, think of that sweet, sweet student loan.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="361" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room2.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        3.) TV
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          While not necessarily an essential, I never regretted buying myself a small flat screen TV for my uni room. It took up hardly any space and did the trick when it came to playing my Xbox or watching some Jeremy Kyle during free mornings. You can even pick one up for less than 100!
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        4.) Posters
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Now to sort out those blank walls. Putting up posters is a no brainer when it comes to brightening up a bedroom. With a million designs out there, youre pretty spoil for choice. Let everyone know youre a Breaking Bad fan or just kiss your beloved Harry Styles goodnight before you go to sleep.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="400" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room4.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="450"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        5.) White tack
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          How are you going to put up these posters? With white tack of course! Blue is so last year Plus, it wont leave stains on your walls. Bonus.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        6.) Photos
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          As much as living at university will kick ass, youre bound to miss friends and family from back home. There wont be a single room without pictures plastered on the walls or on desks. Keep that bottom lip from blubbering and print out some photos to take with you.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="356" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room6.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        7.) Cork board
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Another simple yet ridiculously effective thing to buy for your room. Pin letters, pictures and more on this bad boy.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        8.) Tacks
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Youll need these to pin the latter on the board! Stock up.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        9.) Post-it notes
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Youre going to have a lot on your plate at once, slap a few post-it notes around the room to stay on top of things
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        10.) Rubbish bin
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          No one likes dirty tissues and crumpled up post-its lying around. Chuck them in the bin you filthy animal!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="393" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room7.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="406"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        11.) Laundry basket
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Its not just dirty tissues likely to find a home on your bedroom floor. Smelly socks and last nights outfit need a new home so put them in your laundry basket.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        12.) Clothes horse
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Hopefully all of these suggestions are music to your parents ears. Tell them you need a clothes horse and theyll probably have a heart attack. Hang your freshly cleaned pants and socks out to dry.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        13.) Hangers
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Once your clobber is suitably dry, whack them straight onto some hangers, an essential to avoid the pain in the arse job of what parents call ironing. What is this magic?! Too much hard work, I think.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="342" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room13.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="470"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        14.) Door stop
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          To ensure you come across as a friendly soulto your new neighbours, pack a door stop and leave your door propped open. This is a great way to build friendships and let people suss out that youre a trusted shoulder to cry on for the next year.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        15.) Toiletries
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Nobody likes a smelly person. No spray, no lay. Too much?
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Fresh smelling goat" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room99.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Uni toiletries"/>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        16.) Alarm clock
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          This will become a life saver once you start your routine of rolling into bed at 4am on a school night.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        17.) Paper
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Great for making lists or brainstorming for an assignment. Also doubles up as handy projectiles to shoot in the bin.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="397" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room10.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="400"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        18.) Pens
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Dont be caught short when desperately searching for a pen before your lecture.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        19.) Mini fridge
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Another luxury item but well worth the extra cash. You cant beat the satisfaction that comes from grabbing an ice cold can of coke without ever having to leave your chair. Also great for storing midnight munchies.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        20.) Phone charger
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Buy yourself a spare charger. The amount of times Id left mine elsewhere, resulting in me having a cold sweat as I watch the numbers dwindle on my phones battery life.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="325" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room11.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        21.) Cushions
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          That beds going to look pretty uninviting without some random cushions. These are essential when you have people in your room, lined up on the bed with their back on the hard wall. Good hosts make good friends.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        22.) Loo roll
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Self-explanatory. The less said, the better.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="354" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room14.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        23.) Extension cord
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          You can never have enough plug sockets. Also great for charging your laptop whilst sat on the loo.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        24.) Desk lamp
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Working conditions will make the world of difference when tackling assignments through the night. Youll begin reacting to bright lights like a vampire would to seeing the sun. Dim the lights with a desk lamp and stay focused.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        25.) Febreeze
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          I fondly remember one boys room in my building, absolutely stinking. It reminded me of post-Sunday dinners at my Nans. Cabbage; dont be that person!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="398" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-room15.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="498"/>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h2 id="kitchen">
        Kitchen
       </h2>
       <p>
        The communal kitchen will soon be adjudicated as your student digs main social hangout. I spent plenty of time here with my friends. Its the perfect size for pre-drink gatherings and morning-after debriefs. Whether youre cooking, reminiscing or partying; the kitchen is the perfect place to enjoy a cuppa and hang out with friends. Read on for a further 25 kitchen themed essentials:
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h3>
        1.) Tea
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Many tales will be told over a cup of tea.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-kitchen.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        2.) Coffee
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          But even more hours sleep will be missed over a cup of coffee.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" height="400" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-kitchen1.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="400"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        3.) Bottle opener
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Its all well and good being able to open a bottle with your teeth, until you end up looking like one of Jeremy Kyles finest.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-kitchen2.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        4.) Clingfilm
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Left over pizza never tasted so good.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        5.) Student cookbook
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Youll soon realise that a diet of baked beans, chips and toast will only make you sad. These student cookbooks are life savers.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-kitchen3.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        6.) Cutlery
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Get something distinctive so you know that theyre yours.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        7.) Mugs
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          These mugs will become your best friends. Always there when you need them.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        8.) Pans
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Not just for DIY drum kits. Pans also help cook things.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        9.) Plates and bowls
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Again, not just for Greek themed nights, these are perfect for eating food from.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        10.) Wooden spoon
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          If theres one utensil worth having, its a wooden spoon. Stir those beans like a boss.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        11.) Baking Tray
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          The mother of all trays. Cook your food on this. Simples.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        12.) Tin foil
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Sod washing up the baking tray though, always put on a sheet of tin foil before cooking. Just throw it away once youre done.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        13.) Washing up products
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          However, if you want to avoid the wrath of your fellow housemates, dont let the odd bowl and plate turn into a crockery mountain. Ive never seen so many washing up related arguments before going to uni.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-kitchen4.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        14.) Tea towels
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          A true multi-purpose tool, ideal for drying up, mopping up and whipping your mates with the wet end.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-kitchen5.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        15.) Mini white board
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Leave insults and detailed drawings of willies for your house mates. Hilarity ensues.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        16.) George Foreman grill
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Oh baby; bacon sandwiches never tasted so good. Perfect for the morning after a big night out.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-kitchen6.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        17.) Casserole Dish
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Pasta bakes, cottage pies; you name it, this dish will help cook it.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        18.) Wok
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Probably my most used kitchen item. You can cook just about anything in one of these. Fajitas, curry, stir fry; no problemo.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-kitchen7.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        19.) Toasted sandwich maker
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Another ingenious invention. The result of a toasted sandwich is godly.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        20.) Tin opener
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Im sure theres an unwritten uni rule somewhere that states thou shalt not eat fresh goods. If its not out of a tin, youre doing it wrong.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        21.) Vegetable peeler
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>

        Social

       <p>

If I could only pass on one piece of advice to you though; just be yourself. No one likes a try-hard who is overly loud and obnoxious! Heres the next 25 things to buy to help you slide right into your new social surroundings:
       </p>

       </p>

        1.) Alcohol
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>

         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-social1.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>

       <dl>
        <dd>

         </p>

          <img alt="" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-social7.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/>
         </p>

         </p>

        <dd>

       <dl>
        <dd>

         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        7.) Football/rugby ball
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Fancy a kick about? Four simple words that help to build friendships.

        </dd>

        8.) Games console
       </h3>

        <dd>
         <p>

You cant beat rolling up to a lecture in a pair of jogging bottoms, flip-flops and a hoody. Check out the final part of this list for everything you need to keep organised, warm and on track to nail your experience at uni.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <h3>
        1.) Dressing gown
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Inspired by the Big Lebowskis main character the dude. Combining ultimate comfort with convenience; you can roll out of bed and straight into this fashion masterpiece.
         </p>
         <p>

       </dl>
       <h3>
        2.) Jogging bottoms
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>

         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        3.) Mobile phone or tablet
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          If this list was ordered in terms of importance, this would go in at number 1. Youre going to meet lots of new people, organise lots of events and burn through countless hours on Facebook and Twitter during Monday morning seminars.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Cant live without a phone" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-lifestyle1.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Uni lifestyle phones"/>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        4.) Headphones
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Find your zone and stay focused when writing assignments with the help of a good pair of headphones. No distractions, just you, your tunes and a 2000 word piece on how social media has changed advertising.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        5.) iPod or MP3 player
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          If youre planning on staying in shape whilst at uni, your iPod will become your best bud.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Cat listening to an iPod" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-lifestyle2.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Uni iPod"/>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        6.) Painkillers
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          I got though my fair share of paracetamol at uni. Its a must have when spending so much time in front of computers and drinking.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        7.) Camera
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Youll walk away from university with enough pictures to fill the pages of War and Peace. Invest in a camera and capture the moment where your new best friend throws up, dressed as a Smurf.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Student in a bath of booze" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-lifestyle3.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="Uni cameras"/>
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        8.) Planner/diary
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Uni isnt all dossing and hanging out with friends. Stay organised and keep up with all the busy goings on around you.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        9.) Med kit
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>

         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>

        10.) USB stick
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>

        </dd>
       </dl>

        11.) Bicycle
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          The quickest, cheapest and easiest way to get around your new home town. However, dont forget a bike lock!

        12.) Casual clothes
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          The idea of looking your best for lectures will wear off within 2 weeks. Pack for the roll out of bed look.
         </p>
        </dd>
       </dl>
       <h3>
        13.) Going out clothes
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Smart shirts for the boys, pretty dresses for the girls. Let the hanky, see the panky.

        </dd>
       </dl>

        14.) Smart clothes
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Getting a job whilst at uni is a worthy consideration. Pack interview clothes for when the opportunity comes around.

          <img alt="Smartly dressed dog" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-lifestyle5.jpg" title="Uni smart clothes"/>

       </dl>

        15.) Warm clothes

       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>

        16.) Winter coat

        <dd>

        <dd>

        22.) Backpack or bags
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Dont get something too flash and unpractical. Find a sturdy bag to carry your files to and from campus. I cant guarantee that you wont be called briefcase w***er though.

          <img alt="Briefcase w***er" src="https://www.vouchercodespro.co.uk/images/uni-lifestyle8.jpg" title="uni rucksacks"/>

        23.) Folders
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>

        </dd>

        24.) Time of the month kits
       </h3>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <p>
          Im talking hot water bottles, pain killers, tampons, pads and chocolate. Stock up to avoid biting off your housemates heads.

        25.) Musical instruments

        <dd>
