       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Toasted Bread and Parmesan Kale Salad
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2015-02-11T08:16:41+00:00">
         February 11, 2015
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="pintie">
        <a data-pin-color="white" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/">
         <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_white_20.png"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          <img alt="The Best Toasted Bread, Parmesan, and Kale Salad tossed in a tangy shallot, lemon, and olive oil dressing! YUM!" class="aligncenter wp-image-22207" height="900" src="http://cdn.pinchofyum.com/wp-content/uploads/kale-salad-6.jpg" width="600"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I might have just crossed the line:Bread, Parmesan, and Saladalltyped together in the samerecipe title.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, uhmmm, yes  this IS the best day of 2015 so far, thanks for asking.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its almost hard for me todescribe how much I love this salad AND I KNOW I say that almost every time, but this time (just, come on, stick with me) its really the truth. Its like everything wonderful that I love all in a bowl waiting toadd extra happy to mymeal with its tangy, crunchy, delicious green presence, and yet the only words the come to mind(mind-blowing! life-changing! the best kale salad ever!) cant even really touch it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Were talking about bits of buttery toasted bread, a handful of finely shredded Parmesan cheese, and shredded baby pieces of kale that are small enough to be super delicious all tossed together in a bowl with a homemade shallot, olive oil, and lemondressing.
        </p>
        <p>
         The pure definition of simple and MOST YUMMY SALAD. These foods just bring out my best creative writing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="The BEST Simple Kale Salad - finely shredded kale with Parmesan, golden toasted bread crumbs, and a simple lemon and olive oil dressing. Tangy, crunchy, SO GOOD." class="aligncenter wp-image-22208" height="900" src="http://cdn.pinchofyum.com/wp-content/uploads/kale-salad-4.jpg" width="600"/>
         <span id="more-22205">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Pinkie promise, I would eat this kale salad every day for the rest of the month and never get tired of it. It goes with everything (even
         <a href="http://pinchofyum.com/skillet-chicken-bacon-white-wine-sauce" target="_blank" title="Skillet Chicken with Bacon and White Wine Sauce">
          skillet chicken with bacon and white wine sauce
         </a>
         , maybe?) and it always always ALWAYS tastes good to me.
        </p>
        <p>
         But so lets talk about what it is, in case you were feeling confused about the title or the fact that this is indeed a kale salad in spite of it being mostly a bread and cheese bowl.Im a born rule-breaker.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ill start with the part where we soak the bread cubes in a little bit () of butter and then toast them in the oven to make some kind of crazy simple and delicious homemade crouton-esque crunchies.
        </p>
        <p>
         Always a good place to start, friends. Alwaysssss.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          <img alt="The Best Simple Kale Salad" class="aligncenter wp-image-22210" height="900" src="http://cdn.pinchofyum.com/wp-content/uploads/kale-salad-2.jpg" width="600"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          <img alt="The Best Simple Kale Salad" class="aligncenter wp-image-22209" height="900" src="http://cdn.pinchofyum.com/wp-content/uploads/kale-salad-3.jpg" width="600"/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         And then, that dressing.HELLO THAT DRESSING.
        </p>
        <p>
         Why is it so good?
        </p>
        <p>
         Something about the olive oil and lemon juice and delicately minced shallots? I dont even pretend to know these things, but its seriously the simplest thing youve ever shaken up together in a jar and it will leave your mouth a tiny bit puckery and so very happy for a lifetime.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thank you, toasted bread, Parmesan, and kale with a homemade shallot dressing, formaking my Minnesota-in-February life a totally crunchy, colorful, bearablething right now.
        </p>
        <p>
         KALE YEAH!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          <img alt="The Best Simple Kale Salad featuring a lemon and olive oil dressing, homemade breadcrumbs, and Parmesan cheese. YUM!" class="aligncenter wp-image-22211" height="900" src="http://cdn.pinchofyum.com/wp-content/uploads/kale-salad-9.jpg" width="600"/>
         </span>
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <link href="http://cdn.pinchofyum.com/wp-content/uploads/kale-salad-6.jpg" itemprop="image"/>
         <div class="ERSRatings" itemprop="aggregateRating" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/AggregateRating">
          <div class="ERSRatingOuter">
           <div class="ERSRatingInner" style="width: 100%">

         </div>
         <div class="ERSSavePrint">
          <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
           <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://pinchofyum.com/easyrecipe-print/22205-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Print
           </a>
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Toasted Bread and Parmesan Kale Salad
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSDetails">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Pinch of Yum
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTimes">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSHead">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6-8 (we served it as a side with 7 people and still had a little left over)
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Dressing:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup white wine vinegar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            juice of one lemon
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            juice of one orange
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 shallot, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon salt
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           For the Salad
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            6-8 slices French bread or baguette
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 tablespoons butter, melted
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup grated Parmesan cheese
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            5-6 cups fresh kale, very finely chopped
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Dressing: Place all the dressing ingredients in a jar and shake to mix. Taste and adjust as desired.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Breadcrumbs: Preheat the oven to 300 degrees. Place the bread on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping the pieces over halfway through baking. When the bread is very dry and crusty, remove from oven and allow to cool. Break the bread into pieces and place in a food processor. Pulse until crumbs form. Toss the breadcrumbs with the melted butter and a good pinch of salt and pepper. Spread on the baking sheet and bake for another 5-10 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Salad: Toss the kale, breadcrumbs, Parmesan, and dressing together. Serve immediately.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           If you're storing leftover salad that's already been tossed with the dressing, you'll probably find that the bread soaks up the dressing so you might need an extra squeeze of lemon juice or olive oil to re-moisten the salad.
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
