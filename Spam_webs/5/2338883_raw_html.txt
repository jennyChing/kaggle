               Make wrist weights from socks.

              <ul>
               <li>
                Use a scale to adjust the weight. Fill the sock as full as you want based on weight and then cut the excess fabric. If you want to make heavier weights but the inside material won't fit, use a larger sock.
               </li>
               <li>

               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 12 Version 2.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/d/da/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-12-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-12-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              2
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Make a barbell with water bottles.
              </b>
              Take 2 packs of 6 bottles each and duct tape them symmetrically to an iron bar you can grip easily. This barbell will be good for any exercise you do with a barbell, like lifts and presses.
              <ul>
               <li>
                If these 2 packs are too much, don't use half filled bottles. Half-filled bottles will slosh around and shake the bar. Instead, tape individual filled bottles to the bar.
               </li>
               <li>
                If 2 packs aren't enough, use four or six packs of bottles taped to the bar. Alternately, tape individual bottles to each end of the bar. First line them horizontally along the bar side by side, then stack them on top of each other. Make sure to leave plenty of room for your hands to grip the bar in wide and narrow grips.
               </li>
               <li>
                The taping must be functional. Make horizontal, vertical, and diagonal rounds to wrap the packs to the bar.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-13-Version-2.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-13-Version-2.jpg">
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 13 Version 2.jpg" height="503" id="55a3cdec093c8" src="http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/c/cc/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-13-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-13-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
               <script>
                var img = document.getElementById('55a3cdec093c8');
               </script>
              </a>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 13 Version 2.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/c/cc/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-13-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-13-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              3
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Find old tires lying around the yard.
              </b>
              Tires are used in many workout and bodybuilding routines. You can add additional weights to regular tires when doing workouts, or you can go to a junkyard and find tractor tires. Flipping them and tying a rope to them to pull behind you are only two ways you can use a tire as a weight.
              <sup class="reference" id="_ref-6">
               <a href="#_note-6">
                [6]
               </a>
              </sup>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-14-Version-2.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-14-Version-2.jpg">
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 14 Version 2.jpg" height="503" id="55a3cdec0957b" src="http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/4/47/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-14-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-14-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
               <script>
                var img = document.getElementById('55a3cdec0957b');
               </script>
              </a>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 14 Version 2.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/4/47/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-14-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-14-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              4
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Build a slosh tube.
              </b>
              Slosh tubes are long plastic tubes filled with about 40 pounds of water. But the workout benefits come from the uneven, sloshing water, which makes you use muscles as you try to keep the water balanced as it goes from one end of the tube to the other. You can make your own slosh tube with a PVC pipe. The pipe should be about 4 inches in diameter and 9-10 feet long. Place a cap on one end, then fill the pipe halfway with water. Cap the other end.
              <sup class="reference" id="_ref-7">
               <a href="#_note-7">
                [7]
               </a>
              </sup>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-15-Version-2.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-15-Version-2.jpg">
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 15 Version 2.jpg" height="503" id="55a3cdec0972d" src="http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/d/d9/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-15-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-15-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
               <script>
                var img = document.getElementById('55a3cdec0972d');
               </script>
              </a>
              <noscript>
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 15 Version 2.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/d/d9/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-15-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-15-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              5
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Use a duffle bag to make a sandbag.
              </b>
              Sandbags are similar to slosh tubes in that they are unstable, shifting weights that require you to engage more muscles. To make an easy sandbag, fill 5 or 6 gallon freezer bags with sand. You want the sandbag to be around 50 or 60 pounds. Double bag the bags so they don't break, and then tape the end. Place the bags into the duffle bag. Zip up the duffle bag, and you are ready to work out!
              <sup class="reference" id="_ref-8">
               <a href="#_note-8">
                [8]
               </a>
              </sup>
              <ul>
               <li>
                An alternate way to make a sandbag is to use an old army/navy rucksack or canvas laundry bag. Use contractor garbage bags to fill with pea gravel. You can fill them with 10, 20, or 25 pounds. Fill 5 or 6 bags with gravel, and secure them with duct tape. Add them to the bag until your desired weight.
                <sup class="reference" id="_ref-9">
                 <a href="#_note-9">
                  [9]
                 </a>
                </sup>
               </li>
               <li>
                Add and remove bags of sand or gravel for different weights. Use a scale to determine how heavy the bag is before you start working out, and add or remove weight accordingly. If you don't want to change the weight, you can add the sand or gravel directly into the bag. You cannot easily remove the weight or add weight if you do this.
               </li>
               <li>
                Make sure to leave some room in the interior bags so the sand or gravel can move around.
               </li>
               <li>
                If you are adding a large amount of weight, use a sturdier duffle bag.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>

&lt;div class="altblock"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;span class="mw-headline" id="Making_Homemade_Kettlebells"&gt;Method 3 of 3: Making Homemade Kettlebells&lt;/span&gt;
&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;div id="steps_3" class="section_text"&gt;
&lt;ol class="steps_list_2"&gt;
&lt;li&gt;
&lt;div class="mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter " style="max-width:670px"&gt;
&lt;a href="#/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-16-Version-2.jpg" class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-16-Version-2.jpg?ajax=true"&gt;&lt;img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 16 Version 2.jpg" src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/5/51/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-16-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-16-Version-2.jpg" width="670" height="503" id="55a3cdec098e9"&gt;&lt;script&gt;var img = document.getElementById('55a3cdec098e9');

               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 16 Version 2.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/5/51/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-16-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-16-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>

              1

             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Use a milk or juice jug.
              </b>
              Fill a clean, plastic gallon jug or 2 liter (0.5USgal) bottle with water or sand. Make sure that the jug has a handle; this is needed to complete kettlebell exercises.
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-17-Version-2.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-17-Version-2.jpg">
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 17 Version 2.jpg" height="503" id="55a3cdec09a9b" src="http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/9/91/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-17-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-17-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>

                var img = document.getElementById('55a3cdec09a9b');

              <noscript>
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 17 Version 2.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/9/91/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-17-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-17-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              2
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Use dumbbells and rope.
              </b>
              Another technique for making homemade kettlebells is to wrap a rope around each end of the handle of a dumbbell. The thicker the rope, the more this will work your grip. Grab the rope in the middle so the dumbbell hangs below your hands evenly. Now you can do swings and presses and have the displaced weight effect of kettlebells. When you need to adjust the weight, simply use a different size dumbbell.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Be careful when swinging a dumbbell. It will swing and fly more than a traditional kettlebell. Be careful not to hit yourself with the dumbbell.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-18-Version-2.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-18-Version-2.jpg">
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 18 Version 2.jpg" height="503" id="55a3cdec09c4c" src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/c/cf/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-18-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-18-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
               <script>
                var img = document.getElementById('55a3cdec09c4c');
               </script>

              <noscript>
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 18 Version 2.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/c/cf/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-18-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-18-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>

             <div class="step_num">
              3
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Make a kettlebell out of a potato sack.
              </b>
              Purchase a potato, rice, or sugar shipping sack, which can be found at most grocery stores. Fill the sack with sand until you have reached the desired weight. At the top of the sack, tie a loop for your hand. Use rope or duct tape to secure the loop so it doesn't detach. You can reinforce the sides and bottom of the bag with duct tape.
              <sup class="reference" id="_ref-10">
               <a href="#_note-10">
                [10]
               </a>
              </sup>
              <ul>
               <li>
                You can use this method to make multiple kettlebells of different weights. Use a scale to measure out how many pounds you are putting into the sacks before tying the top of the sack.
               </li>
              </ul>
             </div>
             <div class="clearall">
             </div>
            </li>
            <li class="final_li">
             <div class="mwimg largeimage floatcenter " style="max-width:670px">
              <a class="image lightbox" data-href="/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-19-Version-2.jpg?ajax=true" href="#/Image:Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-19-Version-2.jpg">
               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 19 Version 2.jpg" height="503" id="55a3cdec09dfb" src="http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/f/f8/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-19-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-19-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>

               <img alt="Make a Homemade Weight Set Step 19 Version 2.jpg" height="503" src="http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/f/f8/Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-19-Version-2.jpg/670px-Make-a-Homemade-Weight-Set-Step-19-Version-2.jpg" width="670"/>
              </noscript>
             </div>
             <div class="step_num">
              4
             </div>
             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Use PVC pipe and old basketballs to make a kettlebell.
              </b>
              Buy a 1"x 24" PVC pipe, secure one end with duct tape, and fill with sand. Secure the other end. Place the PVC pipe in the oven at 450 degrees for 10 minutes. You want the plastic to become pliable, not melt. You are going to shape the plastic into the kettlebell handle. Watch the pipe carefully.
              <ul>
               <li>
                Remove the pipe from the oven, and bend the pipe into a handle, bringing the two ends together. Tape the ends together. Dip the pipe in cold water to help set it.
               </li>
               <li>
                Cut a slit into a basketball with the two handle holes at each end. Stick the handle inside the ball to make sure you have cut the holes wide or narrow enough for the handle to be at the height you desire.
               </li>
               <li>
                Mix quick mix concrete in a separate container, then scoop the concrete into the ball. Add the handle towards the end. Let the concrete set for two or three days before using.
                <sup class="reference" id="_ref-11">
                 <a href="#_note-11">
                  [11]
                 </a>
                </sup>
               </li>
              </ul>

          <h2>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Warnings">
            Warnings
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="section_text" id="warnings">
           <ul>
            <li>
             Test the homemade weights carefully before using them in intense workouts. You want to make sure the tape is secure or that nothing will fall out or off and hurt you.
            </li>
            <li>
             If using a homemade barbell as described or otherwise, make sure to employ spotters as appropriate to ensure your safety. This is especially important in the bench press, where muscular failure could result in a crushed larynx or worse.
            </li>
            <li>
             Be careful with your homemade kettlebell; if your wrist hurts after (or during) your workout, stop using it and buy a kettlebell.
            </li>
            <li>
             Always consult with a doctor or qualified health professional before beginning an exercise program.
