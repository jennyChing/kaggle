      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-macro-cci-drop-initial">
       </span>
       Nobody is going to blame you if your holiday party spread includes a bowl of mixed nuts, some grapes and a wedge or two of cheese. After all, most people enjoy them and are willing to nibble mindlessly on them. But it is easier than you think to elevate your feast by bolstering those tired party snack cliches with a few more creative offerings.
      </p>
      <p>
       You wont need to work very hard at this. Most holiday party snacks can be assembled in less than 10 minutes, and many can be assembled ahead of time. And the only truly important rule is to make sure everything is neat and easy to eat (one-handing it really is best for party food).
      </p>
      <p>
       To help get your party started, weve assembled some of our favorite snack ideas. Weve even given you a way to jazz up your basic bowl of mixed nuts.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Spiced nuts:
        </span>
        Beat 1 egg white with 1 teaspoon of water until frothy. Add 3 cups of your favorite unsalted nuts and toss to coat. In another bowl, whisk together
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         4
        </sub>
        cup sugar,
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        teaspoon five-spice powder, 1 teaspoon cinnamon, a pinch of cayenne and
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        teaspoon kosher salt. Toss the sugar mixture with the nuts and spread on a rimmed baking sheet lined with kitchen parchment. Bake at 350 F until fragrant and dry.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Marinated mozzarella with anchovy and peanuts:
        </span>
        Mash 3 anchovies with a fork in a medium bowl. Add
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        cup good olive oil, 1 pound cubed, drained fresh mozzarella, a pinch of red pepper flakes, 2 cloves grated garlic,
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         4
        </sub>
        cup torn fresh basil leaves, and
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        cup lightly chopped unsalted peanuts. Serve with bread for scooping.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Sweet and spicy cracker bark:
        </span>
        Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil and coat with cooking spray. Arrange saltine crackers in a single layer covering the entire baking sheet. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine 1 cup butter, 1 cup brown sugar,
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        teaspoon cayenne (more or less to taste),
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        teaspoon pumpkin pie spice blend,
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        teaspoon kosher salt and
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        teaspoon ground black pepper. Bring to a simmer and stir to combine. Pour the mixture over the crackers, spreading until the crackers are evenly covered. Bake at 350 F until golden brown and bubbling, about 5 minutes. Sprinkle the top
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-147-1-0-26">
        </span>
        with
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        cup crumbled crisp-cooked bacon and
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         4
        </sub>
        cup toasted sunflower seeds. Allow to cool completely, then break into bite-sized pieces.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Marinated olives:
        </span>
        Whisk together 2 tablespoons olive oil, 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar, the zest of 1 orange, 2 minced cloves garlic and 2 tablespoons finely minced fresh rosemary. Toss with 2 cups mixed pitted olives. Refrigerate and allow to marinate for at least 4 hours. Bring to room temperature before serving.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Honeyed goat cheese:
        </span>
        Set an 8-ounce log of soft goat cheese on a serving plate. Drizzle the cheese liberally with honey, then sprinkle with toasted pecans, chopped fresh tarragon, grated lemon zest and ground black pepper. Serve with crackers or sliced baguette.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Apricot prosciutto beans:
        </span>
        Spread a half slice of prosciutto very thinly with apricot preserves. Wrap the prosciutto around several haricot verts (thin French green beans) and a thin strip of roasted red pepper. Make a dozen or so bundles in this fashion, then stack on a serving tray. Sprinkle with ground black pepper.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Bacon-date cream cheese dip:
        </span>
        Beat an 8-ounce block of cream cheese with 2 tablespoons of milk until light and fluffy. Stir in
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        cup crumbled well-cooked bacon,
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        cup chopped dates and 2 thinly sliced scallions. Season with salt and ground black pepper. Top with a drizzle of balsamic glaze.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Avocado shrimp cocktail:
        </span>
        In a food processor, combine the flesh of 3 avocados with
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        cup cocktail sauce, 2 tablespoons horseradish, the juice of 1 lime, a splash of hot sauce and a bit of salt and pepper. Process until smooth, then transfer to a serving bowl. Top with sliced scallions and serve with cooked, chilled shrimp.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Candied kielbasa bites:
        </span>
        Slice 1 pound of kielbasa into thin ovals. Arrange in a single layer on a foil-lined rimmed baking sheet. In a small bowl, mix together
        <sup>
         3
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         4
        </sub>
        cup brown sugar with
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        /
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
        teaspoon cayenne pepper. Sprinkle evenly over the kielbasa. Bake at 350 F until the slices are golden brown and the sugar is bubbly, about 30 minutes.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-leadin">
        <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-108-FRT7-187">
         
        </span>
        <span class="-newsgate-character-cci-bold">
         <span class="-newsgate--ccix-command-133">
         </span>
         Smashed lemon cannellini beans:
        </span>
        Drain and rinse a 15-ounce can of cannellini beans. Pat dry with paper towels, then spread on a dinner plate. Roughly smash the beans with a fork or potato masher. Drizzle all over with olive oil and lemon juice. Sprinkle with grated lemon zest, chopped fresh oregano, coarse sea salt, black pepper and smoked paprika. Serve with toasted pita chips.
       </span>
      </p>
