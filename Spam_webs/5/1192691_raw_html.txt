          <p>
           North Korea has itself an allegedly home-grown "hand phone," one that's making the first chairman proud.
          </p>
          <figure class="img_full">
           <noscript>
            <img alt="(FILES) This undated file picture released by North Korea's official Korean Central News Agency (KCNA) on August 11, 2013 shows North Korean leader Ki..." class="img-responsive img_inline" itemprop="image" src="http://media4.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2013/august/130813/6c8606739-130813-nk-smartphone-kim1.nbcnews-fp-360-360.jpg" title=""/>
           </noscript>
           <a class="js-lightbox lightbox_link" href="http://media4.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2013/august/130813/6c8606739-130813-nk-smartphone-kim1.nbcnews-ux-2880-1000.jpg">
            <button class="btn btn_overlay">
             <i class="fa fa-expand">
             </i>
            </button>
            <img alt="(FILES) This undated file picture released by North Korea's official Korean Central News Agency (KCNA) on August 11, 2013 shows North Korean leader Ki..." class="js-lazy img-responsive img_inline" data-original="http://media4.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2013/august/130813/6c8606739-130813-nk-smartphone-kim1.nbcnews-ux-600-480.jpg" src="//sslnodeassets.nbcnews.com/images/transparent-placeholder.gif" title=""/>
           </a>
           <div class="js-caption_wrapper">
            <figcaption class="img-caption img-caption_default no-margin-bottom">
             <span class="img-caption_txt">
              North Korean leader Kim Jong Un (C) inspects the production process of the new touch-screen mobile phone "Arirang" at the May 11 factory, in this photo provided by the KCNA.
             </span>
            </figcaption>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.kcna.co.jp/item/2013/201308/news10/20130810-20ee.html" target="_blank">
            According to North Korea's own Korean Central News Agency
           </a>
           , the phone, called "Arirang,
           <font color="#333333">
            "
           </font>
           runs a version of Android. Photographs of the phone reveal a back-facing camera, one that "has high pixels," according to the press release.
          </p>
          <p>
           KCNA reports that Kim Jong Un toured the May 11 Factory and "highly appreciated the creative ingenuity and patriotic enthusiasm" behind the new devices.
          </p>
          <div class="ad-container ad-container_default ad-container_mobile ad-hide">
           <div class="ad-label ad-hide">
            <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/id/31066137/media-kit/" target="_blank">
             advertisement
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="ad-content ad-xs box1" data-placement="box1">
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           Photographs of the event show the North Korean leader sitting at a table stacked high with new, boxed phones.
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p>
           While news reports claim the phone has been manufactured at this facility,
           <a href="http://www.digitaltrends.com/mobile/the-arirang-a-smartphone-made-or-boxed-in-north-korea/" target="_blank">
            numerous
           </a>
           <a href="http://www.northkoreatech.org/2013/08/12/kim-jong-un-visits-cell-phone-factory/" target="_blank">
            blogs
           </a>
           guess that it's more likely that the phones, like Apple's iPhones and countless Android devices, are made in China and merely boxed locally.
          </p>
          <figure class="img_full">
           <noscript>
            <img alt="North Korean leader Kim Jong-Un visits the May 11 Factory in this undated photo released by North Korea's Korean Central News Agency (KCNA) in Pyongya..." class="img-responsive img_inline" itemprop="image" src="http://media1.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2013/august/130813/6c8606740-130813-nk-smartphone-kim2.nbcnews-fp-360-360.jpg" title=""/>
           </noscript>
           <a class="js-lightbox lightbox_link" href="http://media1.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2013/august/130813/6c8606740-130813-nk-smartphone-kim2.nbcnews-ux-2880-1000.jpg">
            <button class="btn btn_overlay">
             <i class="fa fa-expand">
             </i>
            </button>
            <img alt="North Korean leader Kim Jong-Un visits the May 11 Factory in this undated photo released by North Korea's Korean Central News Agency (KCNA) in Pyongya..." class="js-lazy img-responsive img_inline" data-original="http://media1.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2013/august/130813/6c8606740-130813-nk-smartphone-kim2.nbcnews-ux-600-480.jpg" src="//sslnodeassets.nbcnews.com/images/transparent-placeholder.gif" title=""/>
           </a>
           <div class="js-caption_wrapper">
            <figcaption class="img-caption img-caption_default no-margin-bottom">
             <span class="img-caption_txt">
              Kim Jong Un sits at a table with what appear to be boxed devices at the May 11 Factory in this photo released by the KCNA.
             </span>
            </figcaption>
           </div>
          </figure>
          <p>
           Even if the people of the republic did line up round the block to buy themselves a new device, it's a puzzle what they'll be able to do with them, as Internet access in Korea is
           <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/technology/north-koreas-internet-what-internet-most-online-access-doesnt-exist-1C9143426" target="_blank">
            restricted to a handful of government and military leaders
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Recently, the country
           <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/world/2013/jan/21/north-korea-foreigners-mobile-phones" target="_blank">
            began allowing foreign visitors to bring in their smartphones
           </a>
           , but
           <a href="http://www.wired.co.uk/news/archive/2013-03/25/north-korea-3g-revoked" target="_blank">
            after one, golden, 3G-enabled month
           </a>
           , those were barred from accessing the Internet as well.
          </p>
          <div class="ad-container ad-container_default ad-container_mobile ad-hide">
           <div class="ad-label ad-hide">
            <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/id/31066137/media-kit/" target="_blank">
             advertisement
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="ad-content ad-xs box2" data-placement="box2">
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           Some speculate that the phone would be equipped with spyware to enable the nation to better track its citizens.
          </p>
          <p>
           Specs of the new North Korean phone weren't provided, but descriptions were. Here's how Apple and North Korea describe their prized devices, respectively, in their marketing materials:
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            On origins:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            "Designed in California" - iPhone
            <br/>
           </li>
           <li>
            "Mass produced" in North Korea - Arirang
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <strong>
            On usefulness:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <div class="ad-container ad-container_default ad-container_mobile ad-hide">
           <div class="ad-label ad-hide">
            <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/id/31066137/media-kit/" target="_blank">
             advertisement
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="ad-content ad-xs box3" data-placement="box3">
           </div>
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li>
            "There's iPhone. And then there's everything else." - iPhone
            <br/>
           </li>
           <li>
            "Very convenient for their users" - Arirang
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <strong>
            On the operating system:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            "The world's most advanced mobile operating system  Safety and security by design." - iPhone
            <br/>
           </li>
           <li>
            "An application program in Korean style which provides the best convenience to the users while strictly guaranteeing security" - Arirang
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <strong>
            On the touch screen:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            "The revolutionary MultiTouch interface in iOS was designed for your finger" - iPhone
            <br/>
           </li>
           <li>
            "Convenient for its user when that part of the phone is sensitive" - Arirang
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ad-container ad-container_default ad-container_mobile ad-hide">
           <div class="ad-label ad-hide">
            <a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/id/31066137/media-kit/" target="_blank">
             advertisement
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="ad-content ad-xs box4" data-placement="box4">
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
            On the camera:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            8 megapixels - iPhone
            <br/>
           </li>
           <li>
            "high pixels" - Arirang
            <br/>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <strong>
            On popular demand:
           </strong>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            "Loving it is easy. That's why so many people do." - iPhone
           </li>
           <li>
            Kim Jong Un "was pleased as they are liked by people." - Arirang
           </li>
          </ul>
