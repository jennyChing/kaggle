      <h1 class="headline">
       5 Dollar Preps  Fishing Gear and First Aid
      </h1>
      <div class="post_content">
       <p>
        We decided to start another new weekly column here on Survival Cache called 5 Dollar Preps. Each week we are going to buy $5 dollars worth of survival gear to show people that preparing doesnt have to be prohibitively expensive. You can get a surprising amount of useful survival preps with just 5 bucks and here is what we got this week:
       </p>
       <h3>
        Lucas
       </h3>
       <p>
        I got a great little collection of fishing supplies. Fishing Line, Assorted hooks, and a small pack of weights.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://survivalcache.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/line1.png">
         <img alt="Fishing Line" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-808" height="150" src="http://survivalcache.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/line1-150x150.png" title="line" width="150"/>
        </a>
        I was able to get
        <a href="http://survivalcache.com/gear/fishingline/">
         500 yards of 15lb test line
        </a>
        for $2.50. I went for the 15lb pound test even though it sounds like overkill. Fishing line is one of those things that you can use for a hundred different problems. Its not Duct Tape or WD40, but its on that list. 15lb test is enough to be able to use it for other heavier things and fishing.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://survivalcache.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/hooks1.png">
         <img alt="Fishing Hooks" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-809" height="150" src="http://survivalcache.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/hooks1-150x150.png" title="hooks" width="150"/>
        </a>
        A pack of
        <a href="http://survivalcache.com/gear/hooks/">
         assorted hooks
        </a>
        . These were $2.96, and I decided to go a little over budget because of the dial box they came in so I can just toss it in my
        <a href="http://survivalcache.com/bug-out-bag/">
         Bug Out Bag
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Finally, I got a small pack of fishing weights for $1.50.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://survivalcache.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/weights1.png">
         <img alt="Fishing Weights" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-810" height="150" src="http://survivalcache.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/weights1-150x150.png" title="weights" width="150"/>
        </a>
        Like I said I went over budget to get the hooks with the container but for a total of $6.96 I was able to get a pretty effective basic fishing set up. I wish I had looked online before shopping because I found this
        <a href="http://survivalcache.com/gear/hooks/">
         Hook, Sinker, and Swivel pack
        </a>
        all in the same type of dial container for $3.99
       </p>
       <p>
        We also have a turn key
        <a href="https://forgesurvivalsupply.com/index.php?option=com_virtuemart&amp;page=shop.browse&amp;category_id=26&amp;Itemid=3" target="_blank" title="Survival Fishing Kit">
         Survival Fishing Kit
        </a>
        for $7.95 on
        <a href="https://forgesurvivalsupply.com/index.php?page=shop.product_details&amp;flypage=tpflypage.tpl&amp;product_id=173&amp;category_id=26&amp;option=com_virtuemart&amp;Itemid=3" target="_blank" title="Forge Survival Supply">
         Forge Survival Supply
        </a>
        that covers about everything you need.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Zack
       </h3>
       <p>
        <a href="http://survivalcache.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/gauze.png">
         <img alt="Gauze Adhesive Bandage" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-812" height="199" src="http://survivalcache.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/gauze-300x199.png" title="gauze" width="300"/>
        </a>
        Zack decided to go for some supplies for his first aid kit. First aid supplies are a bit more expensive than fishing line, but every single thing you buy for survival preparedness is a step in the right direction.
       </p>
       <p>
        The problem with giant pre-made first aid kits is that they have tons of stuff but usually only a little bit of everything. Having something for every possibility is important, but we like to plan our kits based on specific application and the likelihood that they will be needed. Todays idea was cuts and scrapes too large for band-aids that need to be dressed and protected. We did like the pre-made kits from
        <a href="https://forgesurvivalsupply.com/index.php?option=com_virtuemart&amp;page=shop.browse&amp;category_id=69&amp;Itemid=3" target="_blank" title="Adventure Medical Kits">
         Adventure Medical Kits
        </a>
        , they seemed to have a good mix of supplies (including an emergency first aid book) and different size bags for you Bug Out Bag. We also like the
        <a href="https://forgesurvivalsupply.com/index.php?page=shop.product_details&amp;flypage=tpflypage.tpl&amp;product_id=227&amp;category_id=69&amp;option=com_virtuemart&amp;Itemid=3" target="_blank" title="Quick Clot">
         quick clot
        </a>
        option for stopping severe bleeding.
       </p>
       <p>
        A roll of gauze for $1.72 is perfect anything larger than a small cut and once dressed you can wrap it with a self adhesive bandage for $3.84
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         I know some of our readers have creative ideas for $5 dollar preps. Leave us a comment and let us know what youre doing to prepare.
        </em>
       </p>
