          quick proportion tips
         </a>
        </h3>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a class="tumblr_blog" href="http://motorcyclles.tumblr.com/post/117461401452/quick-proportion-tips">
         motorcyclles
        </a>
        :
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         <a class="tumblr_blog" href="http://atalana.tumblr.com/post/117414060777">
          atalana
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <a class="tumblr_blog" href="http://they-chose-family.tumblr.com/post/117406454851/quick-proportion-tips">
           they-chose-family
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <a class="tumblr_blog" href="http://cyborgraptor.tumblr.com/post/116279980331/quick-proportion-tips">
            cyborgraptor
           </a>
           :
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            - eyeballs are an eyeball width apart
            <br/>
            - ears align with the top of your brows to the bottom of your nose, and are the center-point of a profile view
            <br/>
            - lip corners line up to the center of each eye
            <br/>
            - hands are roughly the size of your face
            <br/>
            - feet are the same size as your forearm
            <br/>
            - elbows are aligned with your belly-button
            <br/>
            - your hands reach down mid-length of your thighs
            <br/>
            - both upper and lower legs (individually) are roughly the same size as your torso
            <br/>
            <i>
             (this is all rough estimates for proportion! feel free to add more to help others)
            </i>
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           YOU ARE A FUCKING SAINT
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          - the length of your legs + feet is about the same as the length ofyour torso+ everything above it
         </p>
         <p>
          - collar bones extend directly from the shoulders
          <br/>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         -wrists align with crotch
        </p>
       </blockquote>
      </article>

     <div id="content">
      <div id="question">
       <table>
        <tr>
         <td class="askicon">
          <img src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/avatar_376a44ea7a8f_48.png"/>
         </td>
         <td class="ask">
          <p>
           <a class="asker" href="http://kid-kaneda.tumblr.com/">
            kid-kaneda
           </a>
           said:
          </p>
          Aah I have a question about coloring! I adore the way you color, and with your shading you always use really interesting colors that are very different from the base color but still work and look really cool and I'm wondering, how do you choose what colors to use to shade? Like I've learned to use magenta and purples for coloring like skin and stuff, but sometimes i'll be coloring and have nooo idea what color to shade with ;o; I especially have trouble shading things with a grey base color
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
      </div>
      <div id="answer">
       <p>
        <a class="tumblr_blog" href="http://kinomatika-deactivated20150207.tumblr.com/post/86868857546">
         kinomatika-deactivated20150207
        </a>
        :
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Learn how to use a colour wheel!
        </p>
        <p>
         The reason magenta and purple and puce colours work so well with shading white skin is that they are more or less complimentary, that is, on the exact opposite side of the spectrum from one another. Observe:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/9a95ce94e45d6585ebae3984d52d4bfb/tumblr_inline_n6615eRQUG1rn5que.gif"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you look at where the yellow and the purple colours are on the colour wheel, theyre nearly perfectly opposite one another. Red to green, blue orange, purple to yellow, etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/b6e62f6470e9b6ccd34dba966a0e30bc/tumblr_inline_n6615kOaYH1rn5que.gif"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This colour wheel however is an example of a triadic colour scheme. Purple, orange and green are used together to create depth, volume and visual interest.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/5823b0f45f64e90040f68356b733c9b8/tumblr_inline_n6615rtZ1s1rn5que.gif"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This layout is called split complimentary. If you were to take the farthest away dot and move it to the true yellow colour swatch (which in this instance, is our closest colour to white skin) the other two dots would move to the true blue and the magenta swatches, This is generally the colour family I tend to use when shading skin, at least for white people.
        </p>
        <p>
         As far as shading things with a grey base colour, you can still use the colour wheel to help you as most greys are not
         <em>
          true
         </em>
         greys. Ill show you what I mean:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/a8a15c934053a5f76f106b8ab3bd4b2f/tumblr_inline_n6615yUJSX1rn5que.png"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         All Ive done here is taken the above colour wheel and desaturated it almost completely.
        </p>
        <p>
         As you can see, the colours were looking at are very much grey, but theyre still working with certain colour families. To the right is a more neutral, true grey (desaturated from yellows). To the left and upper left is a more warm grey (desaturated from pinks and purples) and to the lower left and middle is a more cool grey family (desaturated from blues and greens)
        </p>
        <p>
         Its important to understand colour temperature. When I say cool, warm, and neutral Im referring to the temperature of the colour family youre looking at on the colour wheel. We associate cooler colours with blue and green, warmer colours with reds, pinks, and some purples. Yellows and purples in particular can actually go either way, in that some purples (violet, for example) can be cooler and some (magenta) can be warmer. Yellows are the same. The closer yellow gets to the green end of the hue spectrum, the cooler it becomes. The closer it gets to orange and red, the warmer it gets.
        </p>
        <p>
         That being said, you can very easily shade grey using these rules. Using more saturated colours on top of certain greys is really great for learning to shade neutral colours where you dont want to completely desaturate the image.
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope that helped!
        </p>
       </blockquote>
