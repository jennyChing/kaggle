       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         How to Create Compelling Content That Converts
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-07-19T06:17:02+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          July 19, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.steamfeed.com/author/atomicreach/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Atomic Reach
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.steamfeed.com/create-compelling-content-converts/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://3.s.steamfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/SteamFeed-Corporate-Syndication.pdf" target="_blank">
          <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-02-12 at 8.13.26 AM" class="size-full wp-image-19401 alignnone" height="51" src="http://www.steamfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/Screen-Shot-2014-02-12-at-8.13.26-AM.png" width="561"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="AR-HowToCreateContent-645x300" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-23789" height="300" src="http://www.steamfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/AR-HowToCreateContent-645x300.png" width="645"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I recently read an insightful
         <a href="http://marketeer.kapost.com/interactive-content-conversions/">
          blog post
         </a>
         by Jean of Kapost suggesting that a laissez-faire approach to creating content wont cut it anymore. New research from
         <a href="http://www.demandmetric.com/content/content-buyers-journey-benchmark-report">
          Demand Metric
         </a>
         shows that formats that used to work for marketers are not working anymore. Meaning: they dont pull audience interest like they once did.
        </p>
        <p>
         It seems thatthe revolution of content marketing is producing content clutter which in turn results in content fatigue. To combat this, marketers must innovate and delight their audience with new forms of content.
        </p>
        <p>
         So how do we push through the clutter and create content tostimulate engagement with our target audience?
         <span id="more-11205">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Below, Ive compiled some helpful tactics that prove effectiveness in keeping our audiences engaged, each of them backed with recent and impressive stats.
        </p>
        <h2>
         1) Nothing Matters More Than Your Headlines in Blog Posts
        </h2>
        <p>
         When youre writing your blog post, spend a big chunk of time on perfecting your headline because it is the most important element in an article. You will find that there are countless title tips on the web, but if you dont have time to sift through every recommendation (at last count, Google pulls up more than
         <a href="https://www.google.com/search?q=how+to+write+a+great+headline&amp;oq=how+to+write+a+great+headline&amp;aqs=chrome..69i57j0l5.3487j0j7&amp;sourceid=chrome&amp;es_sm=91&amp;ie=UTF-8">
          18 million search results
         </a>
         ) then may I suggest using our
         <a href="http://www.atomicreach.com/">
          plugin
         </a>
         ? It will tell you if your headline is optimized for engagement in a flash!
        </p>
        <p>
         Or you can use one of my favorite headline formulas by
         <a href="http://www.koozai.com/blog/search-marketing/content-marketing-seo/anatomy-perfect-blog-post/">
          Lenka Istvanova
         </a>
         .It works like a charm! Check out the ultimate headline formula presented below:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Screen-Shot-2014-05-27-at-1.34.44-PM" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-23791" height="344" src="http://www.steamfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/Screen-Shot-2014-05-27-at-1.34.44-PM-1024x579.png" width="610"/>
         <a href="http://blog.atomicreach.com/how-to-create-compelling-content-that-converts/screen-shot-2014-05-27-at-1-34-44-pm/" rel="attachment wp-att-11257">
          <br/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         2) Post the Most Engaging Content for Facebook
        </h2>
        <p>
         Its a challenge (especially for brands) to get engagement and interaction on Facebook. So whats the secret? A study by
         <a href="http://www.emarketer.com/Article/Photos-Cluttering-Your-Facebook-Feed-Herersquos-Why/1010777/1">
          Socialbakers
         </a>
         proves that photos posted on Facebook receive the most engagement and interaction.Want to know how much more? A whopping 75%! Keep in mind, as with anything you post online, make sure you share high quality photos in order to see a high response rate.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="FB-graph" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-23792" height="311" src="http://www.steamfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/FB-graph.png" width="392"/>
        </p>
        <h2>
         3) Tweet Click-Worthy Content
        </h2>
        <p>
         Research by Twitter shows that multimedia increases engagement.And by this, they mean that tweets including a photo or videoreceive
         <a href="http://www.poynter.org/latest-news/media-lab/social-media/206402/twitter-research-shows-5-ways-multimedia-increases-engagement/">
          3 to 4 times more engagement
         </a>
         (retweets, replies, etc.) versus those that dont. As you can see here, visual content is essential when youre sharing content on Twitter. The display of images in Twitters busy timelines will help you grab your audiences attention and get them clicking through the link.
        </p>
        <h2>
         4) Heres the Most Liked Pins on Pinterest
        </h2>
        <p>
         Here are some
         <a href="http://blog.pinterest.com/post/85824761394/pin-trends-of-the-week">
          Pinteresting stats
         </a>
         on images that perform well on the visual discovery tool. If your content fits into one of these categories, take advantage of what Pinterest found to be popular on each day of the week.
        </p>
        <p>
         Monday:the week starts with good intentions in
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/all/health_fitness/">
          fitness
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Tuesday:next up, gadgets are all the rage in
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/all/technology/">
          technology
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Wednesday: need a little something to get through the week -
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/all/quotes/">
          inspirational quotes
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thursday:threads lead Thursday with
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/explore/fashion/">
          fashion
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Friday:GIFs bring some comic relief to the end of the week with
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/all/humor/">
          humor
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Saturday:summer vacations are top of mind with
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/all/travel/">
          travel
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sunday:the week closes with
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/all/food_drink/">
          food
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/all/diy_crafts/">
          craft ideas
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-23793 size-full" height="406" src="http://www.steamfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/pin-e1405661120323.jpg" width="610"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         5) You Must Post Your B2B Content on SlideShare
        </h2>
        <p>
         Why? Because
         <a href="http://www.slideshare.net/about">
          the site averaged 60 million unique visitors a month
         </a>
         and 215 million page views. Thats why. SlideShare iscontent marketings 
         <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/marketshare/2013/02/28/slideshare-the-quiet-giant-of-content-marketing/">
          sleeping giant
         </a>
          and there is no denying that. If you have a B2B audience, I highly recommend that you post your content on SlideShare. It drives 500 percent
         <a href="http://www.b2bmarketing.net/blog/posts/2013/10/10/how-slideshare-revolutionizing-b2b-content-marketing">
          more traffic from business owners
         </a>
         than Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, and LinkedIn.This is a great platform for infographics, documents, one-pagers and presentations.
        </p>
        <p>
         Check out more mind-blowing stats about SlideShare
         <a href="http://www.cmo.com/articles/2014/3/10/mind_blowing_stats_slideshare.html">
          here.
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Your Turn
        </h3>
        <p>
         What tactics do you use when youre sharing content online? What gets your audience engaged? Tell us in the comments section!
        </p>
