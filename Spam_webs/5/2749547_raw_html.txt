        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2014-12-01T07:00:48+00:00">
          December 1, 2014
         </span>
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">

            Designer
           </a>

         </span>

           3 Comments

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 300; color: #807069;">
           Hi Everyone, Its
          </span>
          <a href="https://plus.google.com/108503561717438572284/posts" style="font-weight: 300; color: #e45792;" target="_blank">
           Becky
          </a>
          <a href="http://thermoweb.com/blog/dear-stella-snowman-quilt-tutorial/snowmanquilttutorial/" rel="attachment wp-att-15331" style="font-weight: 300; color: #e45792;">
           <span style="font-weight: 300; color: #807069;">
            from
           </span>
          </a>
          <a href="http://patchworkposse.com/" style="font-weight: 300; color: #e45792;" target="_blank">
           Patchwork Posse.
          </a>
          <a href="http://thermoweb.com/blog/dear-stella-snowman-quilt-tutorial/snowmanquilttutorial/" rel="attachment wp-att-15331" style="font-weight: 300; color: #e45792;">
           <span style="font-weight: 300; color: #807069;">
           </span>
          </a>
          <span style="font-weight: 300; color: #807069;">
           Home of fun sewing projects, quilt alongs, and a lot of chit chat!
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Keeping it simple and easy today with ornaments we are all busy, but making a little bit of something handmade is special. These ornaments are great for hanging on wreaths, banners or adding to a present as an extra little goodie to hang on the tree.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="quick and easy ornaments " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15470" height="673" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quickandeasyornaments.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/quickandeasyornaments.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </center>
         <p>
          To keep it simple I went with fabric that had a repeat or a panel. These designs were around 3 round for the larger one-the smaller snowflake is around 2 round.
         </p>
         <p>
          Lucky for me the larger ornaments had a circle printed in the design so that was so easy to follow and sew on top of.
         </p>
         <p>
          Watch for fabric that has the repeats the snowflake were repeated and overlapped each other just a little bit, but I found that you could fussy cut the pieces. There is a little bit of waste when you fussy cut and use panels, but they are really perfect for making little ornaments out of.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="ornaments5" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-15467" height="400" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornaments5-300x400.jpg" width="300"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornaments5-300x400.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="ornaments6" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-15468" height="400" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornaments6-300x400.jpg" width="300"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornaments6-300x400.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </center>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Materials Needed:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          fabric either a panel or one with a repeat
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.thermowebonline.com/p/heatnbond-fleece-fusible-high-loft-white-22-in-x-1-yard-pack/interfacing-stabilizers_fleece?pp=24" target="_blank">
           HeatnBond Fusible Fleece
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          backing fabric
         </p>
         <p>
          ribbon 5
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="ornaments7" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15469" height="450" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornaments7.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornaments7.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </center>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Quick and Cute Ornaments Tutorial~
         </h2>
         <p>
          1) Fuse your fabric to
          <a href="http://www.thermowebonline.com/p/heatnbond-fleece-fusible-high-loft-white-22-in-x-1-yard-pack/interfacing-stabilizers_fleece?pp=24">
           fusible fleece
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          2) Lay on top of the backing anduseyour fabric fuse glue or glue stick to secure in place
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="fabricornament" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15463" height="450" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/fabricornament.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/fabricornament.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </center>
         <p>
          3) Trim just the top of the ornament with the pinking shears the smaller ornament I went ahead and trimmed the whole circle. The layers can shift, so I do suggest waiting till the end, but either way will work.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="ornament2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15464" height="450" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornament2.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornament2.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </center>
         <p>
          4) Tuck the ribbon into the top in between the layers and pin in place
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="ornament3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15465" height="450" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornament3.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornament3.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </center>
         <p>
          5) Sew around the panel or design to combine the layers and to secure the ribbon hanger
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="ornament4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15466" height="450" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornament4.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/ornament4.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </center>
         <p>
          6) Trim the ornament with pinking shears
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <center>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="simpleornamenttutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-15471" height="450" src="http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/simpleornamenttutorial.jpg" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://thermoweb.com/blog/quick-and-cute-fusible-fleece-holiday-ornaments/&amp;media=http://thermoweb.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/simpleornamenttutorial.jpg&amp;description=Quick and Cute Fusible Fleece Holiday Ornaments')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </center>
         <p>
          Time for you to dig around in your fabric stash and find a few prints that would work for this project. Its great for whatever you have. The backing fabric I used plain green fabric, but explore making it double sided, or using plain fabric that you can write a quick note on it with a fabric marker or sharpie. Dont be stuck with the shape either if your panel comes in a square, that would work just fine also.
         </p>
         <p>
          Another thing you could explore would be the stitching and color of thread. Move past the straight stitch and try a few stitches that come with your machine that you dont use very often.
         </p>
         <p>
          With thread you can stick with matching it to the fabric or go for a bit of crazy and try out a variegated thread or a bright color that would compliment the fabric.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is simple enough you could include your younger sewers who are learning a little bit about fabric, design and sewing.
         </p>
