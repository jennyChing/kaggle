         <h1 class="title">
          Mega Skyscrapers That Eat Pollution Are Coming to China
         </h1>
         <div class="headline">
          The smog-choked nation is coming up with innovative solutions to its air-quality problem.
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="section">
         <div class="row row-remove-xs">
          <div class="main-image">
           <img alt="" class="main-media" src="http://www.takepart.com/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/Phoenix-Towers-website.jpg?itok=QYDkPehR"/>
           <div class="caption">
            <p>
             (Photo: Courtesy Chetwoods Architects)
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="section">
         <div class="row">

            <div class="published-at col-xxs-10 col-xxs-offset-2">
             Jun 27, 2014
            </div>
            <div class="image col-xxs-2">
             <img class="img-circle" src="http://www.takepart.com/sites/default/files/styles/70x70_thumbnail/public/profiles/photos/Lizdwyerbp.jpg?itok=N8XjPAvF"/>

         <div class="main-content">
          <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even">
             <p>
              It looks like China is taking on Saudi Arabia for the worlds tallest building crown. When construction on the Phoenix Tower, a pointy two-building complex in Wuhan (in central China), is finished, one of the mega skyscrapers will match the
              <a href="http://www.takepart.com/article/2014/04/28/how-long-elevator-ride-saudia-arabias-building-1-km-high-tower" target="_self">
               Saudis Kingdom Tower
              </a>
              in height. But the 3,280-foot-tall building will do more than just pierce the atmosphere. It will eat pollution floating in the air.
             </p>
             <div class="takepart-take-action-widget align-left" data-action-id="38369" data-article-id="45283" data-widget-type="actions_widget" data-widget-width="300">
             </div>
             <p>
              Designed by U.K.-based architecture firm
              <a href="http://chetwoods.com/portfolio/phoenix-towers-wuhan-china/" target="_blank">
               Chetwoods
              </a>
              , the towers will be covered with a smog-eating coating. The building materials and veneers are photocatalytic: That means they are able to use sunlight to turn particulate matter into inert salts, a kind of technology thats quickly becoming the hottest thing in architecture and design. The
              <a href="http://www.takepart.com/article/2014/06/18/italys-designing-entire-building-will-suck-pollution-sky" target="_self">
               Italy Pavilion
              </a>
              being designed for the 2015 Worlds Fair in Milan is being constructed with a similar material. But the need for air-purifying construction is especially needed in China.
             </p>
             <p>
              Air quality is so bad in the Asian nation that earlier this month the government announced plans to
              <a href="http://www.takepart.com/article/2014/05/30/pollution-china-yanking-millions-cars-off-roads" target="_self">
               yank thousands of cars off the roads
              </a>
              . New emissions standards are also being tightly enforced on factories, and the average person has been empowered to
              <a href="http://www.takepart.com/article/2014/06/10/app-whistleblowing-local-polluters-china" target="_self">
               blow the whistle on the worst
              </a>
              contaminators. Officials in Wuhan, which is on the
              <a href="http://www.greenpeace.org/international/en/multimedia/slideshows/Fishing-and-Pollution-in-Yangtze-River-China/" target="_blank">
               horrifically toxic Yangtze River
              </a>
              , are eager to change Chinas polluting reputation.
             </p>
             <p>
              The most amazing thing for me is that in the U.K. we strive as designers to get things built, and theres a lot of red tape, but the Chinese seem to have a different view of things, the architecture firms founder, Laurie Chetwood, told
              <a href="http://www.fastcoexist.com/3032168/these-pollution-cleaning-towers-in-china-will-be-the-tallest-skyscrapers-in-the-world" target="_blank">
               FastCoExist
              </a>
              . I think theyre incredibly optimistic.
             </p>
             <p>
              Construction is expected to be completed in 2017. While the buildings are an innovativeand tallapproach to improving the regions air, a skyscraper cant be the only thing used to curb smog. Lets hope the worlds most populous country keeps getting those cars off the road. In the meantime, Saudi Arabia, your move.
             </p>
            </div>
