        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Olive Oil Granola
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         in
         <span>
          <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/category/breakfast/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Breakfast">
           Breakfast
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/category/homemade/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Homemade">
           Homemade
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/olive-oil-granola">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-7585" height="400" src="http://acozykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/OliveOilGranola-OH.jpg" title="OliveOilGranola OH" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         For the past couple of weeks, Ive been partying hardcore with
         <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/rocky-road-two-ways/#more-7312">
          butter
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/fried-mozzarella-caprese-salad/">
          cheese
         </a>
         and more
         <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/egg-in-a-hole-grilled-cheese/">
          cheese
         </a>
         . Youve been here to witness all of them, and some of you lunatics even participated. Thank you for that.
        </p>
        <p>
         But sometimes my body needs a butter and cheese break. I didnt make any crazy plans or goals or get on any weird dietI just woke up on Monday morning and didnt want a ham and cheese croissant with my coffee. CRAZY!
        </p>
        <p>
         Instead, I walked into my kitchen, looked in the cupboards and took out all of the ingredients for exactly what my body was calling for: Olive Oil Granola.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/olive-oil-granola">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-7592" height="400" src="http://acozykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/GranolaMixture.jpg" title="GranolaMixture" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <span id="more-7584">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive actually had quite a bit of Olive Oil Granola in my day, since the coffee shop I frequent serves it. I get it on the regs. Olive oil is kind of my jam.
        </p>
        <p>
         At one point, I had an
         <a href="http://usa.loccitane.com/FO/Normal-Skin,cst_NormalSkin.htm">
          olive oil face wash, body lotion, and body scrub
         </a>
         . Getting ready in the morning made me want chicken and salmon. It was weird. Ive since switched to different stuff
        </p>
        <p>
         Nonetheless, olive oil is awesome in all sorts of unexpected foods, granola included.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youve never made homemade granola, it truly is the way to go. You can control the sugar, which is major for me, since I think most store-bought granola is WAY too sweet.
        </p>
        <p>
         Also, I like that I can control the fruit and nut situation. Control your fruit and nut situation, will you? Its important.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/olive-oil-granola">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-7587" height="400" src="http://acozykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/Pour.jpg" title="Pour" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         For this one, I chose some pumpkin seeds (its Fallalmost!), pistachios, dried figs and dried cranberries. I sweetened it with some agave and brown sugar. Oh and make sure you use a good quality olive oil. You want the flavor to come out!
        </p>
        <p>
         Hey, you see that picture with the bowl. Thats so so wrong. I kinda messed up. Ive made granola a ton of times, but for some reason my brain was like put the fruit in tooitll look pretty!
        </p>
        <p>
         You want to add the fruit AFTER the granola toasts in the oven or else the fruit will become ultra dried out.
        </p>
        <p>
         But guess what? Miraculously, the dried fruit didnt become any drier than it already was. So it was all good!!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/Pan.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-7586" height="400" src="http://acozykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/Pan.jpg" title="Pan" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <!--more-->
        </p>
        <p>
         Im digging the little healthy tip Ive been on!
        </p>
        <p>
         The past few days have been filled with workouts, foods like sunflower enchiladas, black bean burgers, vegan BLTS (coconut bacon!!!) and tons of this olive oil granola.
        </p>
        <p>
         I ate mine with some cold almond milkI bet some greek yogurt would be amaze.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont worry, though Ill be back on butter soon enough. I could never give it up completely. #ilovebutterdotcom
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://acozykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/Long4.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-7597 aligncenter" height="600" src="http://acozykitchen.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/Long4.jpg" title="Long4" width="400"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Olive Oil Granola
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Recipe adapted from the
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2009/07/15/dining/151arex.html">
          NY Times
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/acozykitchenprintablerecipes/olive-oil-granola">
          Print this recipe!
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3 cups rolled oats
         <br/>
         1 cup pumpkin seeds
         <br/>
         1 cup pistachios
         <br/>
         1/2 cup brown sugar
         <br/>
         1 teaspoon salt
         <br/>
         1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
         <br/>
         1/4 teaspoon cardamon
         <br/>
         3/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
         <br/>
         1/4 cup agave
         <br/>
         1 cup dried figs, chopped
         <br/>
         1 cup dried cranberries
        </p>
        <p>
         Preheat oven to 300 degrees. In a large bowl, combine oats, pumpkin seeds, pistachios, brown sugar, salt, cinnamon, cardamon, olive oil and agave. Spread mixture on a rimmed baking sheet in an even layer and bake for 35 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes or so, until golden brown and well toasted.
        </p>
        <p>
         Transfer granola to a large bowl and add figs and cranberries, tossing to combine.
        </p>
