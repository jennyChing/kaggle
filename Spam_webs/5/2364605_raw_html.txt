        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         It All Started at Lion Country 
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-11-18T14:22:43+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          November 18, 2014
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://annemckinnell.com/author/anne/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Anne McKinnell
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://annemckinnell.com/2014/11/18/started-lion-country/#comments">
           8 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         It all started at Lion Country in California when I was six years old. I remember driving through the park, amazed by both the openness of the landscape and the variety of large creatures that lived there.
        </p>
        <p>
         The lions came very close to the car and they were more formidable than I imagined. Then there were the giraffes, so graceful with their slow steps that seemed to take them so far so easily. I got to hold a baby cougar.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know there must have been many other animals too, but the one I remember the most, the one that made a lasting impression that would persist throughout my whole life, was the elephant.
        </p>
        <p>
         It wasnt during the drive, but afterwards. I dont remember how it all came about, but I will never forget riding the baby elephant. He was just young and so was I. I felt a kinship with him. I liked the feel of his thick skin, so wrinkled for just a youngster. And he had these wiry hairs like long whiskers on his back. Even today, riding the baby elephant is among the best experiences of my life.
        </p>
        <p>
         I loved the elephant, and ever since Ive wanted to see a wild one. A wild baby elephant. In Africa. Thats the dream.
        </p>
        <p>
         40 years have passed since then! Ive travelled to many places, but none that had wild elephants. Now that is about to change.
        </p>
        <p>
         I made the decision after a health scare last year. Waiting to find out whether I had cancer was excruciating. While I was going through it, thinking perhaps the end was near, I wondered why I never made the time to go to Africa.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive had 40 years, why didnt I go? I was mad at myself. How could I have allowed a lifelong dream to slip by?
        </p>
        <p>
         Even though I changed my life 3 years ago to become a full-time travelling photographer, I still havent made it to Africa. So I decided right then, in the midst of my anger at myself, that if I lived, I would go. Period. No worrying about the cost, no excuses, Im going.
        </p>
        <p>
         And so I am. Today.
        </p>
        <p>
         I hope Ill see a baby elephant.
        </p>
