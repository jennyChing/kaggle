            <img alt="bad credit business loans" class=" size-full wp-image-2510" height="392" src="http://horizonbusinessfunding.com/old-blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/bad-credit-business-loans46.jpg" width="640"/>

          <p>
           Theres an old saying that goes two heads are better than one. By this logic, even better than two heads are a group of them which can give commentary and critique, pointing a conversation in a direction that is as helpful and insightful as possible. Small business owners can put this principle to work for them with a little bit of planning and oversight, utilizing the power of a forum towards the goal of optimizing their business processes. Bear in mind, the drawback present when more voices are added to a brainstorming session is the heightened potential for distraction and a breakdown of meeting organization. In order to maximize the positive potential for collaboration on the part of small business owners and there staff, here is a framework for leading and moderating a forum for brainstorming.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Designate a moderator for the forum.
           </strong>
           Within a forum for discussion, its important for business owners to serve as moderators who will keep the conversation on topic, ensuring that all relevant items are brought up and given an appropriate amount of time to be addressed. Small business owners make ideal forum moderators because in that roll they can direct brainstorming sessions, as well as sit back and gather insight on their business from their managers, giving them a better idea of what is going on in a day in the life of their enterprises.
          </p>
          <p>
           <b>
            Create a framework for keeping the discussion moving.
           </b>
           If one person is the only one speaking within a forum, that is a problem. As moderator, small business owners should facilitate a moving dialogue. One of the ways they can do this is by allocating a time limit for speakers. Another way is by asking guiding questions to particularly silent members of a panel. Consider the different perspectives of those within the forum that you are guiding. You will know already that certain members of your forum will have different perspectives. In order to make the most of these, stimulate some debate by asking one party to follow up on the point of another, in a respectful and constructive way.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Designate someone to take minutes.
           </strong>
           A meeting without minutes is like taking a road trip and forgetting your camera. You may wind up visiting some great ideas, but without anyone recording them in the moment there is a real danger that they will get lost or be forgotten. Someone should be present at each of your meetings in order to help you work on retaining information as well as taking the next steps actually discussed in your meetings. Implementation of new ideas should not be removed from your planning process, instead it should be an organic part of your forum in order to avoid great ideas going stale and not followed up on.
          </p>
