     <h1 class="ipsType_pagetitle">
      Colts Countdown to Kickoff: No. 26 Kelvin Hayden
     </h1>
     <div class="desc lighter blend_links">
      Aug 12 2014 12:00 AM | Views: 807 | 
	Brandon Bender
 in
      <a href="http://profootballspot.com/_/nfl/afc-south/indianapolis-colts/">
       Indianapolis Colts
      </a>
     </div>
     <br/>
     <div>
      <div class="ipsPad ipsType_textblock">
       <img alt="" height="301" src="http://profootballspot.com/uploads/5628ff337d99e6708ff02cda1cad158b.png" style="float: left;" width="600"/>
       <br/>
       <div class="teaser">
        <em>
         <i>
          Pro Football Spot's "Countdown to Kickoff" series continues with No. 26 Kelvin Hayden, who became a hero for Colts fans by returning a 56-yard interception for a touchdown on the way to victory in Super Bowl XLI.
         </i>
        </em>
       </div>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         Peyton Manning and the Indianapolis Colts had momentarily silenced their critics and finally lived up to their promise of going to the Super Bowl.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         Now that they were there, they found themselves in a tight game in the fourth quarter of Super Bowl XLI with the outcome hanging in the balance.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         The Colts led 22-17 early in the fourth quarter when the Bears got the ball at their own 20. With much-maligned quarterback Rex Grossman leading the way, he had a chance to cement his place in Chicago sports immortality with a touchdown. By the end of the drive, he had done just that.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         Except that it was for the other team.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         With 11:44 remaining, Grossman underthrew a sideline pass intended for Muhsin Muhammad that landed into the hands of Colts cornerback Kelvin Hayden, who tightroped the sideline to return it 56 yards for a touchdown. Now up 29-17, the Colts had broken the game open.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         Time was growing short for Chicago, and on the next possession Grossman threw another pick, this time to Bob Sanders. The Colts were on their way to being world champions!
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         Hayden couldnt have picked a more opportune time for his first career interception, even if it was against the team he rooted for growing up. A Chicago native, Hayden was a four-year starter at Hubbard High School where he played quarterback, cornerback and tailback.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         After high school, he played at Joliet Junior College for two years and was used exclusively as a wide receiver. As a sophomore, he was named the NJCAA National Offensive Player of the Year and also the MVP of the 2002 national championship game.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         After Joliet, Hayden transferred to the University of Illinois in 2003 and led the team with 52 receptions for 592 yards. For 2004, he switched to cornerback on defense full-time, and started all 11 games with 71 tackles and four interceptions.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         Needing to address their secondary, the Colts selected Hayden in the second round of the 2005 NFL Draft after taking cornerback Marlin Jackson in the first round.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         Hayden remained with the Colts for four more seasons after winning Super Bowl XLI. He signed with the Atlanta Falcons in 2011, and made 16 total tackles with two interceptions.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         It was time to go home for Hayden in 2012. On April 5, he signed a one-year deal with the Bears and for the season led the league (along with four other players) with four fumble recoveries.
        </span>
       </span>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <span style="font-size: 14px">
        <span style="font-family: arial">
         The Bears re-signed him to another one-year deal in 2013, but he tore his hamstring in training camp and missed the entire season. He became a free agent again after the 2013 season, but was re-signed for a third time on February 28, 2014.
        </span>
       </span>
