     <p>
      <b>
       Puerto Octay
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Chile" title="List of towns in Chile">
       town
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">
       commune
      </a>
      located on the north shore of
      <a href="/wiki/Llanquihue_Lake" title="Llanquihue Lake">
       Llanquihue Lake
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Lagos_Region" title="Los Lagos Region">
       Los Lagos Region
      </a>
      in the south of
      <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
       Chile
      </a>
      . It was settled by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">
       German colonists
      </a>
      in 1852. Puerto Octay was an important port with regular traffic to
      <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Varas" title="Puerto Varas">
       Puerto Varas
      </a>
      before the coming of railway in 1912.
     </p>

     </h2>
     <p>
      Its origin dates back to the German colonization in 1852 driven by
      <a href="/wiki/Bernhard_Eunom_Philippi" title="Bernhard Eunom Philippi">
       Bernhard Philippi
      </a>
      . With the passage of time becomes one of the major ports of Lake Llanquihue. On 22 December of 1891 is established as a municipality, under the Presidency of the Republic
      <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Montt" title="Jorge Montt">
       Jorge Montt
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="German_heritage_history">
       German heritage history
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Octay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: German heritage history">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote">
      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/German_colonization_of_Valdivia,_Osorno_and_Llanquihue" title="German colonization of Valdivia, Osorno and Llanquihue">
       German colonization of Valdivia, Osorno and Llanquihue
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Puerto_Octay.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="532" data-file-width="800" height="106" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Puerto_Octay.jpg/160px-Puerto_Octay.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Puerto_Octay.jpg/240px-Puerto_Octay.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Puerto_Octay.jpg/320px-Puerto_Octay.jpg 2x" width="160"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Puerto_Octay.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        typical
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_architecture" title="German architecture">
         German architecture
        </a>
        in Puerto Octay.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Puerto Octay currently has a rich and varied architecture, mostly built of wood, which is the historical reflection of the colonization process, driven by the State of Chile in the mid-nineteenth century, with families coming from Germany.
     </p>
     <p>
      So constructions emerge as the House Niklitschek, Hotel Haase, Wulf House, current Colegio San Vicente de Paul and House Werner to name a few. What is extraordinary about this city is that despite the lapse of more than 100 years, it is still possible to assess this type of buildings.
     </p>
     <p>
      As a way to cherish and protect this architectural heritage, now taking place in Puerto Octay a process of declaration of Typical Zone, where they will identify a representative area of identity and history, which will be legally protected by the State of Chile.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">
       Demographics
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Octay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Demographics">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      According to the 2002 census of the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Statistics_Institute_(Chile)" title="National Statistics Institute (Chile)">
       National Statistics Institute
      </a>
      , Puerto Octay spans an area of 1,795.7km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      (693sqmi) and has 10,236 inhabitants (5,391 men and 4,845 women). Of these, 3,403 (33.2%) lived in urban areas and 6,833 (66.8%) in rural areas. The population fell by 7.4% (815 persons) between the 1992 and 2002 censuses.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_2-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-INE-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">
       Administration
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Octay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Administration">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As a
      <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">
       commune
      </a>
      , Puerto Octay is a third-level
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_division_of_Chile" title="Administrative division of Chile">
       administrative division of Chile
      </a>
      administered by a
      <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">
       municipal council
      </a>
      , headed by an
      <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">
       alcalde
      </a>
      who is directly elected every four years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Official-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Within the
      <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Chile" title="Electoral divisions of Chile">
       electoral divisions of Chile
      </a>
      , Puerto Octay is represented in the
      <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Chile" title="Chamber of Deputies of Chile">
       Chamber of Deputies
      </a>
      by Mr. Fidel Espinoza (
      <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_of_Chile" title="Socialist Party of Chile">
       PS
      </a>
      ) and Mr. Carlos Recondo (
      <a href="/wiki/Independent_Democratic_Union" title="Independent Democratic Union">
       UDI
      </a>
      ) as part of the 56th electoral district, (together with
      <a href="/wiki/Puyehue,_Chile" title="Puyehue, Chile">
       Puyehue
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Negro,_Chile" title="Ro Negro, Chile">
       Ro Negro
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Purranque" title="Purranque">
       Purranque
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Fresia,_Chile" title="Fresia, Chile">
       Fresia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Frutillar" title="Frutillar">
       Frutillar
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Llanquihue,_Chile" title="Llanquihue, Chile">
       Llanquihue
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Varas" title="Puerto Varas">
       Puerto Varas
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Muermos" title="Los Muermos">
       Los Muermos
      </a>
      ). The commune is represented in the
      <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Chile" title="Senate of Chile">
       Senate
      </a>
      by Camilo Escalona Medina (PS) and Carlos Kuschel Silva (
      <a href="/wiki/National_Renewal_(Chile)" title="National Renewal (Chile)">
       RN
      </a>
      ) as part of the 17th senatorial constituency (Los Lagos Region).
     </p>
