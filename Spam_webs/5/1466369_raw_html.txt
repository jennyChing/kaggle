    <div class="single-title">
     <h1>
      5 Simple Ways to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint
     </h1>
     <div class="clear">
     </div>
     <div class="single-info">
      <span class="single-date">
       <strong>
        Published On
       </strong>
       24/03/2015 |
      </span>
      <span class="single-author">
       By Anne Weaver |
      </span>
      <span class="single-category">
       <strong>
        <em>
         <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/category/urban-design/energy-urban-design/" rel="category tag">
          Energy
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/category/lists/" rel="category tag">
          Lists
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/category/urban-design/sustainable-living/" rel="category tag">
          Sustainable Living
         </a>
        </em>
       </strong>
      </span>
     </div>
    </div>
    <div class="clear">
    </div>
    <!-- Single Content -->
    <div class="single-content">
     <p>
      People often believe that even if they reduce their energy use and attempt to live a greener lifestyle, they wont make a difference in a sea of over-consuming citizens. I believe this logic is detrimental to progress and should be put to rest. If everyone thinks their actions wont make a difference, our society would bedoomed for any sort of change.
     </p>
     <p>
      When consumers start making minimal changes in their lives, well begin to see a shift in consumption patterns. Even if climate change is irreversible, simple modifications in reducing consumption and waste canhave significant impacts on the future of our planet. Here are a few simple tips on how to reduce your carbon footprint.
     </p>
     <h3>
      1. Curb Driving Habits
     </h3>
     <p>
      Paying attention to the way you drive is the first step in reducing the carbon footprint related to your car. Driving better  includingaccelerating more slowly, driving the speed limit, and maintaining your speed with little fluctuation  can make a huge difference in your
      <a href="http://cleantechnica.com/2013/07/17/drivers-could-save-20-per-cent-on-fuel-by-embracing-eco-driving/" target="_blank" title="CleanTechnica - Eco-Driving Habits Could Save Drivers 20% on Fuel">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         miles per gallon (MPG) consumption
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition, proper maintenance of your car can also help reduce your carbon footprint. Make sure your tires are inflated correctly, and always replace your oil and fuel filters on schedule to ensure efficient car travel.
     </p>
     <h3>
      2. AlternativeTransportation
     </h3>
     <p>
      If you live in an area where there are multiple options for commuting, try a new source of transportation when youre traveling for work or leisure.
      <a href="http://www.bicycling.com/news/advocacy/america-s-top-50-bike-friendly-cities/2-minneapolis" target="_blank" title="America's Top 50 Bike-Friendly Cities">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         Manycities are extremely bike-friendly
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      , so invest in a bike if you dont already have one (bikes dont emit any CO2). If your commute is short (a mile or less), try walking! Personally, I almost always choose this option because it saves me money, it allows me to get some extra exercise, and its great for the planet.
     </p>
     <p>
      Public transportation is always a great choice as well. Most of the time, taking a subway trainor bus will take less time than driving during busy rush hours. Carpooling is another surefire way to be more carbon efficient.
     </p>
     <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_7548" style="width: 581px">
      <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/526826997_a3efe67846_o.jpg">
       <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
        <img alt="Shopping at a farmer's market is a smart choice for indirectly decreasing your carbon footprint. Photo via Ian (flickr)." class=" wp-image-7548" height="428" src="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/526826997_a3efe67846_o.jpg" width="571"/>
        <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/5-simple-ways-reduce-your-carbon-footprint/&amp;media=http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/526826997_a3efe67846_o.jpg&amp;description=5 Simple Ways to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint')">
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
      <p class="wp-caption-text">
       <em>
        Shopping at a farmers market is a smart choice for decreasing your carbon footprint. Imagevia Flickr.
       </em>
      </p>
     </div>
     <h3>
      3. Alternative Food Consumption
     </h3>
     <p>
      While what you eat and where you get your food are indirect factors of your carbon footprint, they make a huge difference in your energy usage. Buying food from a local source, such as a community garden or farmers market, saves on carbon emissions; a five-pound package of food flown across the country to arrive at your plate creates an average of twelve pounds of CO2.
     </p>
     <p>
      Unfortunately for meat-lovers, the
      <a href="http://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-greenhouse-hamburger/" target="_blank" title="How Meat Contributes to Global Warming">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         consumption of red meat
        </strong>
       </span>
      </a>
      is another practice that creates a significant amount of CO2 pollution. By simply swapping out several meat-based meals each week for vegetarian ones (or even poultry dishes), youll save an astounding about of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions.
     </p>
     <h3>
      4. Be Mindful Around the House
     </h3>
     <p>
      There are so many little steps you can take to
      <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.powerhousegrowers.com/controlled-environment-reducing-energy-consumption-of-buildings/" target="_blank" title="Controlled Environment: Reducing Energy Consumption of Buildings">
         maximize the efficiency of your home
        </a>
       </strong>
      </span>
      while also minimizing its carbon footprint. Simple adjustments such as using a laptop rather than a desktop computer can reduce your energy usage (laptops are designed to be more energy efficient).
     </p>
     <p>
      Adjusting the thermostat in the winter and summer is another trick to saving money and energy; turning off the heat or air conditioning when youre not home can save over 15% on your energy bill. Being mindful with your gadgets is good for both the planet and the devices themselves; unplugging your phone charger or laptop is just one way to be energy smart. Lastly, adjusting the lighting of your home is a quick fix to reducing your electricity spending. Change your lightbulbs to compact fluorescent bulbs and youll greatly reduce energy consumption, thus reduce carbon dioxide pollution.
     </p>
     <h3>
      5. Travel Tips
     </h3>
     <p>
      Taking one large vacation infrequently is greener than taking frequent trips on airlines. This is because air travel is not very carbon-friendly. Choosing a nonstop flight rather than multiple connectingflights (if possible) is also a greener option. While youre traveling, always remember to turn off all your electronics at home, unplug appliances, and turn down both the thermostat and water heater to save energy.
     </p>
     <p>
      Be wary when youre traveling; using public transportation instead of a rental car is a wise choice, as well as booking flights with airlines that have strong environmental agendas.
     </p>

       Letter From The Editor
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="textwidget">
      <p>
       As the editor of PowerHouse Growers Group (PHG), I strive to ensure that our content is timely, well-researched, and on the cutting edge of urban agriculture and sustainability.
      </p>
      <p>
       I edit for PHG because I admire its vision, and I love being a part of moving that vision forward in a way that positively impacts individuals and organizations in all areas. In the future, I aim to further the cause of green building practices by developing sustainable homes and buildings that go above and beyond certifications like LEED - structures that not only serve our basic human needs, but provide healthy habitation while maintaining modern design.
      </p>
      <p>
       All of our news, features, and editorials are free to our readers. If you would like to see your work published on PowerHouse Growers or have a comment email me at
       <a href="mailto:daniel@powerhousegrowers.com">
        daniel@powerhousegrowers.com
       </a>
       .
      </p>
