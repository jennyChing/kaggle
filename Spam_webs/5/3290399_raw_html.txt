         </p>

          Moore came to the lot and, from a dumpster, retrieved the gold model
          <a href="http://www.space.com/16398-could-star-trek-enterprise-be-built.html">
           Enterprise
          </a>
          ships that used to be in the observation lounge of the starship. "And they've been sitting in storage ever since," he said in a phone interview with Space.com.
         </p>
         <p>
          The incident is symbolic of what Moore sees as a problem in Hollywood: There is no dedicated motion-picture museum in Tinseltown, despite immense fan interest and the fact that studios all over the city have props squirreled away on their lots. [
          <a href="http://www.space.com/20848-star-trek-tng-bridge-restoration-called-for-in-kickstarter-campaign-video.html">
           Video: 'Star Trek's' Bridge Restored
          </a>
          ]
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="about_adsense" id="nointelliTXT">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           tmntag.adTag('ad_sense1');
          </script>
         </div>
         <p>
          Some things are even used over and over again. For example, Moore said he heard a tale of original "Star Trek" uniforms from the 1960s being reused on the 1970s and 1980s sitcom "Mork &amp; Mindy." This all fed into his motivation to join the board of directors of the New Starship Foundation, which is planning to build the first stage of a Hollywood museum in 2015.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Displaying the bridge
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          The Hollywood Science Fiction Museum aims to showcase exhibits from sci-fi history, including several space franchises.
         </p>
         <p>
          Moore, an executive producer on "
          <a href="http://www.space.com/25667-star-trek-richard-hatch-fan-film.html">
           Battlestar: Galactica
          </a>
          ," said he's hoping to get a Cylon Raider and other big pieces from the show into the museum. Other planned exhibits include props from "Babylon 5," "Star Wars," "Doctor Who," "
          <a href="http://www.space.com/20482-2001-space-odyssey-infographic.html">
           2001: A Space Odyssey
          </a>
          " and "Alien," among other space franchises.
         </p>
         <p>
          The most iconic "Star Trek: TNG" piece would likely be a version of the bridge used in "Star Trek: The Experience," a Las Vegas theme park that closed in 2008. (The one used on the show was blown up as part of the plot for the movie "Star Trek: Generations," according to New Starship CEO Huston Huddleston.)
         </p>
         <p>
          The bridge sat outside for about five years and is in need of restoration. Huddleston successfully
          <a href="http://www.space.com/20852-star-trek-enterprise-bridge-restoration.html">
           raised $68,611 in a Kickstarter campaign last year
          </a>
          (more than triple its $20,000 goal) to fix it up, but he said the focus now is on finding a place to put it.
         </p>
         <p>
          "The Hollywood Sci Fi Museum is the extension of the bridge restoration, because we'd gone as far as we could go without more money or a place to put the bridge once it was completed," he told Space.com in an email. The team has launched another Kickstarter campaign to raise $82,300 for the museum's construction. So far, they have raised about $53,600, with three days to go.
         </p>
         <p>
          The full budget for the museum is not being released publicly, Moore said, and negotiations are ongoing to find a site for the museum. A prototype version will open in 2015, if all goes according to plan, and the larger museum is scheduled to be completed in 2018.
         </p>

          <strong>
           Science fiction, past and present
          </strong>

         <p>
          Moore still keeps in touch with his colleagues from "
          <a href="http://www.space.com/16413-star-trek-s-warp-drive-are-we-there-yet-video.html">
           Star Trek
          </a>
          " and "Battlestar Galactica." He met up with several "Star Trek" franchise stars at a convention in Germany a few months ago, and on May 30, he attended a "Star Trek" event at the TCL Chinese Theatre in Hollywood.
         </p>
         <p>
          Even the new Blu-rays for "Star Trek" are providing opportunities for informal meet-ups, as crew members reunite to film the special features.
         </p>
         <p>
          Meanwhile, Moore's keeping busy with "Outlander"  a new time-travel show about a World War II-era nurse suddenly taken back to the 1740s  and "Helix," a show about a potential disease outbreak in the Arctic that is entering its second season.
         </p>
         <p>
          Moore also plans to be at Comic-Con International in San Diego July 24-27 to promote the new shows, and probably the Hollywood museum idea as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          "That's plenty to keep me out of trouble," he joked.
