         22 pounds, you guys. Paul has lost 22 pounds in six weeks from running every day and watching what he eats. No crazy diet fads or pills. Just routine, hard work, and portion control.
        </p>
        <p>
         (Alternatively, I think Ive gained 2 pounds from eating white chocolate chips every day. But this isnt about me. *cough*)
        </p>
        <p>
         Me: When you achieve a goal, youre supposed to reward yourself, right?
         <br/>
         Paul: I need a new wrist band for my watch.
         <br/>
         Me: um, really? Thats sad. Think bigger.
         <br/>
         Paul: how about a Tag Heuer watch, instead of the wrist band?
         <br/>
         Me: uuuum. Think smaller!
         <br/>
         Paul: something gooey. With butter.
         <br/>
         Me: done!
         <span id="more-19343">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         There are a few food items he deeply loves. A sloppy burger, beer, anything dill, and pineapple. Not necessary together. Becauseew. Although it is Paul, so I wouldnt put it past him.
        </p>
        <p>
         I went with the pineapple. And I gave him gooey. With butter. And he loved every single bite. And then said, dont ever make that again. HA.
        </p>
        <p>
         This monkey bread isso good!

         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Pina Colada Monkey Bread
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://veryculinary.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Pina-Colada-Monkey-Bread-print.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">

         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          Ooey gooey pull-apart bread with coconut milk, pineapple, macadamia nuts, and rum!
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/4 cup (1/2 stick) unsalted butter
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/4 cup coconut milk, divided
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 tablespoon rum + a splash more
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tube (16 ounce) Pillsbury Homestyle Original Grands refrigerated biscuits
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3/4 cup granulated sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 can (8 ounce) canned crushed pineapple, drained and divided
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup chopped macadamia nuts, divided
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1/2 cup powdered sugar
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Directions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Coat a bundt pan with nonstick cooking spray.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a small saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter, 2 tablespoons coconut milk, and 1/2 tablespoon of rum until smooth. Remove from heat.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Separate biscuits and cut each one into quarters.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Gently dip half of the biscuit pieces into the butter mixture, then roll in the granulated sugar and place in prepared bundt pan. Evenly Sprinkle half of the pineapple and half of the nuts over the top. Repeat a second layer with the remaining biscuits pieces, pineapple, and nuts.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake until golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out mostly clean, about 35 minutes. Transfer to a cooling rack; cool 5 minutes in pan. Carefully invert monkey bread onto a plate.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a small bowl, whisk powdered sugar, remaining 2 tablespoons coconut milk, and a splash of rum until smooth. Drizzle glaze over the warm bread. Serve warm and enjoy!
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           alternatively, you skip the layers and gently mix all the biscuits, butter mixture, sugar, pineapple, and nuts together in a large bowl. Make sure all the pieces are coated, place in the bundt pan and bake. It doesnt render the same texture, but still delicious!
