     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The Cure for Insomnia
       </b>
      </i>
      , directed by John Henry Timmis IV, is a film which on its release in 1987 was according to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">
        Guinness World Records
       </a>
      </i>
      the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_longest_films_by_running_time" title="List of longest films by running time">
       longest running
      </a>
      . At 5,220 minutes long (87 hours, or 3 days and 15 hours) in length, the film has no plot, instead consisting of artist L. D. Groban reading his 4,080-page poem "A Cure for
      <a href="/wiki/Insomnia" title="Insomnia">
       Insomnia
      </a>
      " over the course of three and a half days, spliced with occasional clips from
      <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">
       heavy metal
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pornographic" title="Pornographic">
       pornographic
      </a>
      videos.
     </p>
     <p>
      It was first played in its entirety at
      <a href="/wiki/Art_Institute_of_Chicago" title="Art Institute of Chicago">
       The School of the Art Institute
      </a>
      in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" title="Chicago, Illinois">
       Chicago, Illinois
      </a>
      , from January 31 to February 3, 1987, in one continuous showing. It has not been released on DVD or other home video formats.
     </p>
     <p>
      In December 2011, Lee Groban died from heart failure.
     </p>
