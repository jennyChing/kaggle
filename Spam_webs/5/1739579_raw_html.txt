         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Return to the Sea: Saltworks by Motoi Yamamoto
          <span class="post-date">
           by
           <a href="http://thisiscolossal.com/about" rel="author external" title="Visit Christopher Jobsons website">
            Christopher Jobson
           </a>
           on August 12, 2012
          </span>
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-25994" height="948" src="http://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/salt-1-640x948.jpg" title="salt-1" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-25995" height="425" src="http://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/salt-2-640x425.jpg" title="salt-2" width="640"/>
         </p>

          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-25996" height="426" src="http://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/salt-3-640x426.jpg" title="salt-3" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-25997" height="480" src="http://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/salt-4-640x480.jpg" title="salt-4" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-25998" height="759" src="http://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/salt-5-640x759.jpg" title="salt-5" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-26001" height="480" src="http://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/salt-6-640x480.jpg" title="salt-6" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="424" mozallowfullscreen="" src="http://player.vimeo.com/video/42177795?title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0&amp;color=da9315" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="640">
          </iframe>

         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="480" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/dAnPCjjJ61Q" width="640">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Japanese artist
          <a href="http://www.motoi.biz/">
           Yamamoto Motoi
          </a>
          was born in Hiroshima, Japan in 1966 and worked in a dockyard until he was 22 when he decided to focus on art full-time. Six years later in 1994 his younger sister died from complications due to brain cancer and Yamamoto immediately began to memorialize her in his labyrinthine installations of poured salt. The patterns formed from the salt are actually quite literal in that Yamamoto first created a three-dimensional brain as an exploration of his sisters condition and subsequently wondered what would happen if the patterns and channels of the brain were then flattened. Although he creates basic guidelines and conditions for each piece, the works are almost entirely improvised with mistakes and imperfections often left intact during hundreds of hours of meticulous pouring. After each piece has been on view for several weeks the public is invited to communally destroy each work and help package the salt into bags and jars, after which it is thrown back into the ocean, a process you can watch in the video above by
          <a href="https://vimeo.com/42177795">
           John Reynolds &amp; Lee Donaldson
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Yamamoto recently finished a new installation at the
          <a href="http://halsey.cofc.edu/exhibitions/return-to-the-sea-saltworks-by-motoi-yamamoto/">
           Halsey Institute of Contemporary Art
          </a>
          in Charleston, South Carolina and will soon be in Los Angeles at the
          <a href="http://cfa.lmu.edu/labandartgallery/exhibitions/upcoming/">
           Laband Art Gallery
          </a>
          where hell begin work on a new piece. You can stop by the gallery August 29, 30, 31 and September 4, 5, 6, 2012 from 12-4pm to see the work in progress which will finally open in its completed state on September 8th. You can follow along via his
