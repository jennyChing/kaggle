                Rooting With Vroot
               </span>
              </h3>
              <p>
               <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
               </span>
              </p>
              <div id="pagelist">
               <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                vRoot is also known as Root Master. Its a Windows Application that  helps you to root any android phone in a single click. Here, we are  going to guide you how to use vRoot to root your Android Device.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h2>
                <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                 How to use vRoot to root any Android Device
                </span>
               </h2>
               <p>
                <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                 <b>
                  Step 1
                 </b>

                  To enable USB debugging
                 </b>
                 , Open
                 <b>
                  Settings
                 </b>
                 &gt;
                 <b>
                  Developers Option
                 </b>
                 &gt;
                 <b>
                  USB Debugging
                 </b>
                 &gt;
                 <b>
                  Tick
                 </b>
                 to Enable.
                </span>
                <br/>
                <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
                 (Developers Option is hidden for Android 4.2.2 or above, to unhide Go to
                 <b>
                  Settings
                 </b>
                 &gt;
                 <b>
                  About Phone
                 </b>
                 &gt;
                 <b>
                  Built Number
                 </b>
                 (Tap on it for 5-8 times).
                </span>
               </p>

                <span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
