             <h3 class="el__headline">
              Story highlights
             </h3>
             <ul class="el__storyhighlights__list">
              <li class="el__storyhighlights__item el__storyhighlights--normal">
               A shuttle from "2001: A Space Odyssey" was purchased by the motion picture academy
              </li>
              <li class="el__storyhighlights__item el__storyhighlights--normal">
               It will be put on display to the public for the first time
              </li>
              <li class="el__storyhighlights__item el__storyhighlights--normal">
               The 1968 classic is considered one of the most influential science-fiction movies ever
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="ad ad--epic" data-ad-text="show">

          <div class="el__leafmedia el__leafmedia--sourced-paragraph el__leafmedia--">
           <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
            <cite class="el-editorial-source">
             (CNN)
            </cite>
            After decades in private hands, a spaceship from the classic film "2001: A Space Odyssey" will go on display for the first time to the public.
           </p>
          </div>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences, the group that presents the Oscars,
           <a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/academy-purchases-space-shuttle-2001-785166" target="_blank">
            purchased
           </a>
           the spacecraft at auction Saturday for $344,000. The academy will display the ship in its
           <a href="http://www.oscars.org/museum" target="_blank">
            museum of movie history
           </a>
           , scheduled to open in 2017 in Los Angeles.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           "We're excited to add such an iconic piece of movie history to our collection," the academy told CNN in a statement. "We look forward to sharing the '2001' Aries 1B Trans-Lunar Space Shuttle with the public once the Academy Museum opens."
          </p>
          <div class="el__image--standard js__image--standard">
           <img alt="The Aries spacecraft, moments before touching inside the great Lunar crater Clavius in a scene from the film &#39;2001: A Space Odyssey&#39;, 1968.           <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           In case you're wondering, the craft is the spherical lunar lander that transports Dr. Heywood R. Floyd to the moon in "2001."
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Sorry, it's not the Discovery One, the spaceship where mission pilot Dave Bowman later famously implored the ship's computer to "open the pod bay doors, HAL."
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           A collector and former art teacher in the UK
           <a href="http://english.beneo.org/11045-Hollywood_Live_Auction_Extravaganza_XVII_Premiere_Props.html" target="_blank">
            had kept it for the past 40 years
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show">

          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           <a href="http://www.icollector.com/2001-A-Space-Odyssey-Hero-Screen-Used-Aries-1B-Trans-Lunar-Space-Shuttle_i21553709" target="_blank">
            According to the auction house
           </a>
           , the spaceship is one of the few sets left from the 1968 film.
          </p>
          <p class="zn-body__paragraph">
           Almost all the props, sets, models and costumes from "2001: A Space Odyssey" were destroyed by director Stanley Kubrick so they could not be used in other productions, the Premiere Props auction house said.
          </p>
