&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;Did you know Big Bird had gay fathers? Kermit Love and Christopher Lyall spent 50 years together populating the world with fanciful puppets and living their own chapter of gay history; a story so unique, we really need to make sure it is never lost.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I have been worried about Big Bird lately. It isnt easy to live with a death threat hanging over your head. I heard the rumor thatSecretaryClinton has hidden him out in a safe nest to keep him from the roving Mitt squads. Then I heard about the creator of Big Bird asking the candidates to stop using the giant yellow Muppet in their political slugfest, and I feared the worst. Was Big Birds creator a Romney supporter, and if so, does that mean the Romney/Ryan campaign has an assassin in Big Birds inner circle? After all, it was &lt;em&gt;Obama For America&lt;/em&gt; that put out the tongue-in-cheek ad using Big Bird. It wouldn&#8217;t be a long jump to the conclusion Big Bird&#8217;s creator was not pleased with that campaign video.&lt;/p&gt;

&lt;p&gt;You can breathe a sigh of relief. It turns out the Big Bird creator with the plea for Muppet neutrality is not a Romney supporter. He is a very private gay gentleman named Christopher Lyall, who doesnt even live in the United States. After spending half a century here, in a loving, committed relationship, Lyall went home to live out his remaining years in his native New Zealand after his partner passed away of congestive heart failure in 2008.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Christopher Lyall is not on Wikipedia. He can&#8217;t even be found on &lt;a href="http://muppet.wikia.com/wiki/Muppet_Wiki"&gt;Muppet Wiki&lt;/a&gt;.But someone really should try to persuade Lyall to tell his story for the &lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.imfromdriftwood.com/"&gt;I&#8217;m from Driftwood&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;project, because he lived a unique chapter of gay history that should be preserved and celebrated. For fifty years Lyall and the love of his life, a puppeteer named Kermit Love,&lt;a href="http://muppet.wikia.com/wiki/Was_Kermit_named_after_a_childhood_friend_of_Jim_Henson%3F"&gt;(No relation to the frog of the same name,)&lt;/a&gt;brought smiles and giggles and comfort to the children of the world through their amazing puppet creations.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The couple met in 1957. Love, who had been into puppetry since he was a young boy, was already an accomplished costume designer for Broadway shows, so they began to work together on designs for Jerome Roberts and Balanchine ballets and other New York live theater events. In the early 1960s the couple met Jim Henson of the Muppet fame, and the three collaborated on a creation we all recognize today as the La Choy dragon. That success prompted Henson to approach Love and Lyall to create a puppet for Sesame Street from a sketch he gave them of an antique toy; a bottom-heavy duck that when rocked repeatedly sipped from a bowl of water.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I like to picture Lyall and Love working together with their chicken wire and their bags of yellow feathers, birthing Big Bird. Just think, all those years on Sesame Street, all those Big Bird plush dolls tucked into cribs and strollers, and we never knew Big Bird was the product of two loving same-sex parents. How many generations of children have grown up cuddling the creation of a gay couple; a pair so dangerous to the fabric of society they gave us Snuffleupagus and the Snuggle fabric softener bear.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;I dont think Mitt Romney was aware of Big Birds origins when he made his ominous threat to shut down Sesame Street. I am convinced he sees the Muppets we all grew up loving, only as figures on a balance sheet. But now that we know about Kermit Love and Christopher Lyall, maybe we should work just a little bit harder for Obama/Biden, to make sure the little bit of gay magic they shone into the world is not lost to politics. Big Bird for Mitt Romney is by no stretch of the imagination, an equitable trade.&lt;/p&gt;
