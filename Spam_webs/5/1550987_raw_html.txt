          This is something extremely personal, that I for the most part, keep extremely private. However, I have come to realize that it does more harm than good trying to cover up the imperfections and serious health flaws that set me apart from a healthy 24 year old woman. In buying into societys standards, indulging in Instagram photos filtered beyond recognition and trips that are the most brag worthy in order to create photos to share, social media leaves us with impressions on millions of peoples lives. We think we know them, what they do, how they wake up, what they eathow lucky they are to be them and not us.
         </p>
         <p>
          This focus on the prim and proper, the glam and fab leaves no room for a discussion about the serious ailments that many of us go through every day. I am just as guilty of indulging in this dangerous culture as anybody else. This attractive culture that leaves no room for scars, no room for a few extra pounds and definitely no room for anything but sunshine and rainbows.
         </p>
         <p>
          I pick profile pictures in which I look the happiest and to my eye the most attractive, I Instagram photos that to me scream look what I am doing and how cool it is and how happy I must be when I may in reality be sitting by myself in my living room full of melancholy. So then why do some of us insist on comparing our whole lives to their whole Instagram queue?

         <p>
          I finally made it to college and never felt so small, scared and anxious in my life. I wanted to go home. I hated UCSB. I called my mother crying. When school started, I always wanted more. I was never satisfied. I was obsessed with an idea of what college should be like and I was convinced I didnt have it yet. I said yes to everything, I realized the more I drank the most people liked me, I went everywhere. I was desperate to fit in. To be cool somehow. To finally feel accepted.
         </p>
         <p>
          I ended up making a great group of girlfriends, but even then it didnt seem to do the trick. I had to do more. I had to drink more. This culminated in me hurting those closest to me. My behavior was erratic. But on the outside, I was a good time.
         </p>
         <p>
          It was around this time that my kid brother got a DUI in the car my mom had just bought me. My car was so wrecked in the accident that it was salvaged out. My little brothers life was ruined for the time being. But he was okay physically and I was grateful, it was all still too much for me and I broke down. I finally decided to go to therapy, or counseling at the UCSB Counseling and Psychological Health Services. This was the first time I was told I had a mild form of
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="col-md-10 col-md-offset-1 row-divider">
        <div class="divider-count">
         <span class="page-count">
          5/7
         </span>

         </div>

          Navigating mental health disorders      -
         </h4>
         <h5 class="divider-line">
          mental Health
         </h5>
         <h5 class="divider-line">
          Anxiety
         </h5>
         <h5>
          Bi-polar
         </h5>
         <div class="clearfix">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="clearfix">
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="row">
       <div class="col-md-10 col-md-offset-1 post-content-page-wrapper">
        <div class="post-content-page" id="">
         clinical depression by a therapist. After several appointments, missed appointments and what not I chose not to take anti-depressants or any other pills and never went back. A huge part of the reason, I realize now is that theres a stigma against pills. People are reluctant to accept that there are illnesses of the brain that need to be treated with not just therapy, but also medicine. But I digress.
         <p>
          It was not until about 9 months ago that I decided to return to therapy. It was extremely helpful, but I could not rid myself of obsessive thoughts that were severely impacting my relationship. My doctor referred me to a psychiatrist and I put off actually making an appointment for about 4 months. When I finally made an appointment I was diagnosed with an anxiety disorder and put on Zoloft. This did the trick for a bit. At our most recent appointment, after a particularly intense panic attack, and after I had explained to him what I had been going through in the past several months since I had seen him, I was told I may be showing signs of bi-polar disorder and was put on the mood stabilizer, Lamictal. This truly came as a shock to me. I knew there was something stronger than my will and deeper than my conscious driving certain actions I have partaken in over the past years that hurt others as well as my reputation in the process, but I never expected this. With the current stigma on bi-polar disorder, I was terrified.
         </p>
         <p>
          I went home and cleaned the kitchen,
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="col-md-10 col-md-offset-1 row-divider">
        <div class="divider-count">
         <span class="page-count">

          Navigating mental health disorders      -
         </h4>
         <h5 class="divider-line">
          mental Health

          Anxiety

         <h5>
          Bi-polar

      <div class="row">
       <div class="col-md-10 col-md-offset-1 post-content-page-wrapper">
        <div class="post-content-page" id="">
         organized, turned tables over, reorganized again, threw things out and never stopped. I picked up my pills the next day in a sort of state of disbelief. And then I cried. I took my first Lamictal two days ago and the second just last night. Im only on 25mg right now, but will be worked up. Im also still on my Zoloft since it did wonders for my social anxiety.
         <p>
          What I am hoping to accomplish through this post is to show how much lies behind every single person. I am hoping that eventually the stigma that comes with mental health is dispelled and we can open up as a community and allow people to safely discuss their issues and get the help and support that they need, want and deserve. I hope that you can identify and relate with some of my issues or experiences. Or even if you dont I hope you look into mental health or pass the message along that mental disorders are nothing to be ashamed about.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive gone my whole life being ashamed of who I am when there is no reason to be. Im not crazy, Im human. My brain just needs a little more help than others. :)
         </p>

   Handlebars.registerHelper('ifEqual', function(v1, v2, options) {
		if(v1 === v2) {
			return options.fn(this);
		}
		return options.inverse(this);
	});
	Handlebars.registerHelper('contains', function(v1, v2, options) {
		if ( v1 instanceof Array &amp;&amp; v1.indexOf(v2) &gt; -1 ) {
			return options.fn(this);
		}
	});
	Handlebars.registerHelper('isEditable', function(created_date, options) {
		var created = new Date(created_date);
		var now = new Date();
		if ( now - created &lt; 259200000 ) {
			return options.fn(this);
		}
	});
	Handlebars.registerHelper('formatDate', function(date) {
		return moment(date).fromNow();
	});

	//Note, we should refactor below once the messaging system is out.
	//Below is from https://gist.github.com/arbales/1654670
	var ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION, LINK_DETECTION_REGEX, MARKSAFE_FUNCTION, emojify, formatTextForHTML, linkify;

	//below regex is from: http://stackoverflow.com/questions/6927719/url-regex-does-not-work-in-javascript
	LINK_DETECTION_REGEX = /(([a-z]+:\/\/)?(([a-z0-9\-]+\.)+([a-z]{2}|aero|arpa|biz|com|coop|edu|gov|info|int|jobs|mil|museum|name|nato|net|org|pro|travel|local|internal))(:[0-9]{1,5})?(\/[a-z0-9_\-\.~]+)*(\/([a-z0-9_\-\.]*)(\?[a-z0-9+_\-\.%=&amp;]*)?)?(#[a-zA-Z0-9!$&amp;'()*+.=-_~:@/?]*)?)(\s+|$)/gi;

	ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION = Handlebars.Utils.escapeExpression;

	MARKSAFE_FUNCTION = function(str) {
	  return new Handlebars.SafeString(str);
	};
	linkify = function(safeContent) {
	  return safeContent.replace(LINK_DETECTION_REGEX, function(url) {
	  	brEnd = false;
	  	if(url.indexOf("\n")) {
	  		brEnd = true;
	  	}
	  	url = url.trim();
	    url = url.replace(/(?:\r\n|\r|\n)/g, '');
	    var address;
	    address = /[a-z]+:\/\//.test(url) ? url : "http://" + url;    
	    url = url.replace(/^https?:\/\//, '');
	    link = "&lt;a href='" + address + "' target='_blank' rel='nofollow'&gt;" + url + "&lt;/a&gt;";
	    if(brEnd) {
	    	return link + '&lt;br/&gt;';
	    } else {
	    	return link;
	    }
	  });
	};

	formatTextForHTML = function(content) {
	  var safeContent;
	  safeContent = ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION(content);
	  safeContent = linkify(safeContent);
	  safeContent = safeContent.replace(/(?:\r\n|\r|\n)/g, '&lt;br /&gt;');
	  //safeContent = emojify(safeContent);
	  return MARKSAFE_FUNCTION(safeContent);
	};

	Handlebars.registerHelper('formatTextForHTML', formatTextForHTML);
