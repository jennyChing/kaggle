        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Does St. Johns Wort Help With Depression?
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-02-24T12:56:58+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 24, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://wholisticfitliving.com/author/daisy" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Dr. Daisy Sutherland
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://wholisticfitliving.com/does-st-johns-wort-help-with-depression#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://wholisticfitliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Hypericum_perforatum-IMG_4353.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-27726">
          <img alt="Does St. John's Wort Help With Depression?" class="aligncenter wp-image-27726" height="453" src="http://wholisticfitliving.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Hypericum_perforatum-IMG_4353-1024x768.jpg" width="604"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         There are many herbs, oils and natural remedies on the market today and many more people are opting for natural alternatives for common ailments. One of such natural remedies is St. Johns Wort and many have asked if it will help with depression. Here is information that will help answer the question:
         <em>
          Does St. Johns Wort help with depression?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
          St. Johns Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is an herb thats sometimes used effectively for treatment of depression. It is believed to contain ingredients that may help the brain produce more serotonin, which produces feelings of well-being in the body.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         <span class="s1">
          The plant is named after St. John, the Baptist and produces yellow petals in groups of five that resemble a halo. Red sap runs from the plant and is said to be a symbol of the blood of St. John. Part of its scientific name, Hypericum, means 
          <em>
           greatest health
          </em>
           in Greek.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         <span id="more-27725">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         <span class="s1">
          For many centuries, St. Johns Wort has been used as a sedative and to treat wounds, burns and sleep disorders. Now, St. Johns Wort is believed to be valuable in treating anxiety, sleep disorders such as insomnia and depression.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         <span class="s1">
          St. Johns Wort can be purchased in capsules, extracts and teas. In the United States, its not sold as a prescription, but can be purchased over-the-counter. In Europe the herb is prescribed by physicians for depression and other illnesses.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         <span class="s1">
          Even though St. Johns Wort has been tested and scientifically researched its never safe for you to diagnose your own illness. Here are some things you need to consider before taking an herbal remedy  or any type of medication:
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li class="p3">
          <span class="s1">
           If youre severely depressed and have suicidal tendencies, consult a health professional before taking St. Johns Wort.
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li class="p3">
          <span class="s1">
           Dont take St. Johns Wort if youve been diagnosed as bipolar.
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li class="p3">
          <span class="s1">
           Dont stop prescription medications  consult a health professional first.
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <ul>
         <li class="p3">
          <span class="s1">
           If youre pregnant or lactating, dont take anything without first consulting a health professional.
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p class="p3">
         <span class="s1">
          Some side effects associated with taking St. Johns Wort are mild to severe allergic reactions, sensitivity to light, tiredness and nausea. You could also experience a lessened libido, headaches, dizziness and dry mouth.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="p3">
         <span class="s1">
          If youre thinking of taking St. Johns Wort, seek the advice of a health professional before you begin as it may react dangerously with some medications and ingredients in food.
         </span>
        </p>
