         CHORES FOR KIDS: PROMOTING DEVELOPMENTAL SKILLS
        </h1>

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <b>
          Chores for kids
         </b>
         theyre great for fostering independence, promoting a sense of responsibility, and making kids feel like capable members of a household.
        </p>
        <p>
         But what if we told you that chores for kids are also
         <i>
          amazing
         </i>
         for promoting all kinds of
         <a href="http://theinspiredtreehouse.com/the-abcs-of-child-development-developmental-skills/" title="THE ABCS OF CHILD DEVELOPMENT: DEVELOPMENTAL SKILLS">
          <b>
           developmental skills
          </b>
         </a>
         ? Yep, now you have more reason than ever to put those kiddos to work!
         <br/>
         <img alt="chores for kids to promote developmental skills" class="aligncenter wp-image-12729 size-full" height="653" src="//theinspiredtreehouse.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/chores-for-kids-to-promote-developmental-skills.jpg" width="450"/>
        </p>
        <div class="advads-general-posts" id="advads-1578146252" style="text-align: center; margin-top: 10px; margin-right: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 10px; ">

         </ins>

         </script>

        <p>
         Read on to learn how common everyday chores can help boost
         <a href="http://theinspiredtreehouse.com/strengthening-for-kids-why-todays-kids-arent-as-strong-as-they-used-to-be/" title="WHY TODAYS KIDS ARENT AS STRONG AS THEY USED TO BE">
          <b>
           strength
          </b>
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://theinspiredtreehouse.com/sensory-processing-proprioception/" title="SENSORY PROCESSING: PROPRIOCEPTION">
          <b>
           proprioception
          </b>
         </a>
         , bilateral coordination, midline crossing, fine motor skills, visual skills, and cognitive skills!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">

        <p>
         <b>
          Chores for Improving Strength and Proprioception in Kids
          <br/>
         </b>
         Proprioception is the way joints and muscles send messages to the brain to help coordinate movement.
         <a href="http://theinspiredtreehouse.com/sensory-processing-proprioception/" title="SENSORY PROCESSING: PROPRIOCEPTION">
          <b>
           Proprioception
          </b>
         </a>
         also allows us to regulate the force of our movements. As kids move against an opposing force, they strengthen their proprioceptive systems. This kind of movement is also great for all over
         <a href="http://theinspiredtreehouse.com/child-development-core-strengthening-for-kids/" title="CORE STRENGTHENING EXERCISES FOR KIDS">
          <b>
           strengthening
          </b>
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Help kids improve
         <b>
          proprioception and strength
         </b>
         with chores that involve
         <b>
          lifting, pulling, and movement against resistance
         </b>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         -Taking out the garbage
        </p>
        <p>
         -Washing the floor (squeezing out a mop or sponge, scrubbing the floor)
        </p>
        <p>
         -Loading the washer and dryer with clothes
        </p>
        <p>
         -Raking, shoveling, pulling weeds
        </p>
        <p>
         -Taking sheets, blankets, and pillowcases off beds to be washed
        </p>
        <p>
         -Making beds
        </p>
        <p>
         -Vacuuming
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Chores for Promoting Bilateral Coordination in Kids
          <br/>
         </b>
         Bilateral coordination refers to how we use both sides of our bodies together smoothly. From doing
         <a href="http://theinspiredtreehouse.com/child-development-quick-tip-jumping-jacks/" title="CHILD DEVELOPMENT QUICK TIP: JUMPING JACKS">
          <b>
           jumping jacks
          </b>
         </a>
         to catching a ball with two hands to buttoning a shirt, this is an important skill for all kids!
        </p>
        <p>
         Help kids work on
         <b>
          bilateral coordination
         </b>
         skills with chores that involve
         <b>
          using the right and left hands together
         </b>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         -Sweeping the floor
        </p>
        <p>
         -Folding laundry
        </p>
        <p>
         -Washing dishes
        </p>
        <p>
         -Cooking (especially stirring and pouring tasks)
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <strong>
           <br/>
           <img alt="chores for kids promoting development2" class="aligncenter wp-image-12733 size-full" height="856" src="//theinspiredtreehouse.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/chores-for-kids-promoting-development2.jpg" width="600"/>
          </strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Chores for Promoting Midline Crossing in Kids
          <br/>
         </b>
         Crossing the midline means spontaneously using a body part (arm/hand or leg/foot) on the opposite side of the body to complete a task. Being able to cross midline during movement activities is an important precursor for establishing hand dominance and other motor skills.
        </p>
        <p>
         Help kids work on
         <b>
          crossing midline
         </b>
         with chores that involve
         <b>
          reaching across the body
         </b>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         -Dusting/wiping down tables or counters
        </p>
        <p>
         -Washing windows
        </p>
        <p>
         -Sweeping and mopping
        </p>
        <p>
         -Washing the car
        </p>
        <p>
         -Raking or shoveling
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Chores for Promoting Fine Motor Skills and Hand Strength in Kids
          <br/>
         </b>
         Fine motor skills refer to small muscle movements in the fingers and hands, including finger isolation, in-hand manipulation and more! Fine motor skills and hand strength give kids the ability to
         <a href="http://theinspiredtreehouse.com/super-fingers/" title="CHILD DEVELOPMENT: KIDS AND GRASPING SKILLS">
          <b>
           grasp
          </b>
         </a>
         and manipulate objects 
         <i>
          so
         </i>
         important for school activities and
         <a href="http://theinspiredtreehouse.com/kids-clothing-fasteners/" title="CHILD DEVELOPMENT: KIDS AND CLOTHING FASTENERS">
          <b>
           self-care tasks
          </b>
         </a>
         !
        </p>
        <p>
         Help kids work on
         <b>
          fine motor skills and hand strength
         </b>
         with chores that require
         <b>
          pinching, pulling, squeezing, and grasping
         </b>
         :
        </p>
        <p>
         -Washing anything using a sponge or cloth that needs to be squeezed out (dishes, floor, windows, car)
        </p>
        <p>
         -Folding laundry
        </p>
        <p>
         -Watering plants with a
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00UF0JVQO/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00UF0JVQO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theinsptree0f-20&amp;linkId=CPXKZLDXZSCOM4SK" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <b>
           spray bottle
          </b>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         -Hanging laundry using
         <b>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002CGV57M/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B002CGV57M&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theinsptree0f-20&amp;linkId=GHTF3D52GHAY4GEJ" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
           wooden clothespins
          </a>
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Chores for Promoting Visual and Cognitive Skills in Kids
          <br/>
         </b>
         Kids rely on visual motor integration and visual perceptual skills to coordinate their movements and to make sense of what they see. Visual motor integration and visual perception are closely related to cognitive skills like memory, problem-solving, and sequencing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Help kids work on
         <b>
          visual and cognitive skills
         </b>
         by encouraging them to do chores that involve
         <b>
          sorting, organization, and multi-step tasks.
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         -Following a recipe
        </p>
        <p>
         -Making and packing snacks/lunches for school
        </p>
        <p>
         -Putting away groceries
        </p>
        <p>
         -Unloading the dishwasher
        </p>
        <p>
         -Setting the table
        </p>
        <p>
         -Putting away laundry, matching socks
        </p>
        <p>
         -Tidying up and sorting toys and other objects
        </p>
        <p>
         -Picking out clothes
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Do your kids help out around the house? Do they have any favorite chores? Wed love to add to our list!
         <br/>
         Share with us in the comments below!
        </p>
