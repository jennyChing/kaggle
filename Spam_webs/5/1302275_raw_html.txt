        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Be Prepared With A Survival Kit Checklist
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Emergencies usually popup without warning. Thats what makes them an emergency.
        </p>
        <p>
         It could be a natural disaster like a flood, earthquake, or forest fire. Or it could be man-made.
        </p>
        <p>
         Either way, the best thing to boost your chances of survival is to be prepared. The best way to be prepared is to have the essential life saving items on hand when you need them.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are pretty much two ways to go with this  build your own kit or buy a pre-made kit. I favor the do-it-yourself method because its more customizable and youll be more familiar with each component than if someone else chooses all your gear.
        </p>
        <h1>
         Survival Kit Checklist
        </h1>
        <h4>
         The 10 Essentials
        </h4>
        <p>
         As the name implies,
         <a href="http://www.adventurestrong.com/hiking/10-essentials-hiking-adventure-survival" target="_blank">
          the 10 Essentials are
          <em>
           absolute must-have items
          </em>
         </a>
         . This isnt opinion, this is fact.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Shelter
          </strong>
           Emergency shelter will save your life.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Water
          </strong>
           Have at least 3-days worth plus a way to purify water.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Food
          </strong>
           Have at least 3-days worth.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Clothing
          </strong>
           Remember, Cotton kills. Go with synthetic clothing, outdoor gear is ideal.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Fire Starter
          </strong>
           Storm-proof lighter or matches plus a fire starter kit.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Navigation
          </strong>
           Waterproof maps and compass (and know how to use them).
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           First Aid
          </strong>
           Have the supplies and the knowledge to use them properly.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Sun Protection
          </strong>
           Hat, sun glasses, sunscreen.
         </li>
         <p>
          <img alt="SOG Survival Knife" class="alignright size-full wp-image-3284" height="300" src="http://www.adventurestrong.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/SOG-Survival-Knife.jpg" width="400"/>
         </p>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Light
          </strong>
           Flashlight with extra batteries.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.adventurestrong.com/survival-2/best-survival-knife-guide" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            Knife
           </strong>
          </a>
           Ideally, a full-tang, fixed-blade knife.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          For sanitation concerns
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Hand Sanitizer
         </li>
         <li>
          Garbage Bags
         </li>
         <li>
          Zip Ties
         </li>
         <li>
          Moist Towelettes
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Germs can cause a lot of problems. Do you remember the game Oregon Trail? Dont be the one that dies of dysentery.
        </p>
        <p>
         From here you can build out with whatever you think youll need or want.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Be sure to include personal items
         </strong>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Medication
         </li>
         <li>
          Feminine Products
         </li>
         <li>
          Baby Stuff (diapers, etc)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Things like paracord, bleach, fire extinguisher, etc. are all useful to have. Also, keep in mind youll need to scale up the food and water to your anticipated group size. If you have kids, pack something non-digital/electronic to entertain them  books, cards, games, etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Here is a table with gear to consider when assembling your survival kit.
         </strong>
        </p>

         <strong>
          Cost
         </strong>
         : $ = Under $50, $$ = $51-100, $$$ = $100+
        </p>
