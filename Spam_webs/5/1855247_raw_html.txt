       <h1 itemprop="headline name">
        How Everest is changing the double glazing game
       </h1>
       <h2 itemprop="alternativeHeadline description">
        The windows and home improvement company has made some key changes to their 
  products and personnel since being acquired by Better Capital
       </h2>
       <style media="print">
        .newGigyaShare { display: none !important; }
       </style>

          <p class="bylineBody">
           By
           <span rel="author">
            Virginia Blackburn
           </span>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p class="publishedDate">
          11:13AM BST 16 Jun 2014
         </p>
         <meta content="2014-06-16" itemprop="datePublished"/>
         <div class="cl">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           When Roy Saunders was appointed chief executive of
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sponsored/property/everest-windows/">
            Everest
           </a>
           in August 2012 after it had been acquired by Better Capital, revolution was in the air. I felt my task was in part changing perceptions about the sector and the sales of double glazing, he says of the home improvement company. And that mission was to be accomplished in a number of different ways: an enhanced product range and customer experience, a huge shake-up in the recruitment and training of the sales force and much sleeker presentation techniques, ranging from a specially designed sales kit to a new range of brochures.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           The most important work was on the product range. When Saunders joined, there was no product room in Everests Hertfordshire HQ in which to display the companys goods: this was installed and a survey conducted into potential Everest customers buying habits. About 60 per cent of people who didnt buy from us didnt go on to purchase at all, which meant they wanted to, but we just didnt have a product that was right for them, says Saunders. The solution was to extend the product range to three lines for varying budgets: the Essential range, which was more affordable; the traditional Exclusive range; and, at the upper end of the scale, Everest Elite windows.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           <img align="right" caption="" declared-caption="" height="111" name="Pullquote" photographer="" refid="2961726" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/02961/Pullquote_2961726a.png" width="238"/>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           Saunders says the reason Better Capital acquired the company and why he decided to join was the same: the strength of the brand. The unquestioned market leader, Everest has around a 95pc recognition factor. Its practically synonymous in the publics eye with windows and double glazing  although its product line stretches to doors, conservatories, garage doors, roofline and much else. However, he does concede that the sector had an image problem, mainly to do with overzealous salespeople who were paid entirely on commission, and so there has been a revolution in this area, too.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           Traditionally, this sector has churned a huge number of people who were not properly managed or trained, he says. We made a big change in sales. On May 9, 2013, we replaced 144 self-employed area agents with 46 managers who are employed. We are the only company in the sector to do this. We give all our new sales consultants a thorough induction with a five-day residential course, emphasising that we expect certain standards of customer service and behaviour.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class=" related_links_inline" id="tmg-related-links">
          <div class="headerOne styleTwo">
           <h2>
            <span>
             Related Articles
            </span>
           </h2>
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="bullet">
            <p>
             <a href="/sponsored/property/everest-windows/10897946/everest-new-products-happy-customers.html">
              Everest reviews product range to focus on customers
             </a>
            </p>
            <span class="relContDate">
             16 Jun 2014
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           The company also appointed six human resource business partners to support the sales team and to monitor and respond to customer feedback. A high-calibre sales director, Tom Devine, was also hired. He had previously worked at BSkyB and Vodafone and, as part of Everest's ongoing commitment to sales consultant training, is currently planning the opening of the Everest Training Academy. Based in nearby St Albans, this is another industry first.
          </p>
          <p>
           Weve seen more change in a year and a half than we had in the previous 20 years, says Saunders. It is working: as the company prepares to celebrate its 50th birthday next year, it reports that one-third of business is now repeat.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>

             Everest reviews page 
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
