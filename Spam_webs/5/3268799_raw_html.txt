                <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
                 <span itemprop="headline">
                  Fire Phone Gets Large Price Reduction by Amazon
                 </span>
                </h1>
                <h3>
                 <span itemprop="author">
                  <span content="2015-05-16T09:47:22-04:00" datatype="xsd:dateTime" property="dc:date dc:created" rel="sioc:has_creator">
                   Submitted by
                   <span about="/users/lord-marin" class="username" datatype="" property="foaf:name" typeof="sioc:UserAccount" xml:lang="">
                    Lord Marin
                   </span>
                   on Sat, 05/16/2015 - 09:47
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </h3>
                <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-646">
                 <div id="body-post">
                  <div itemprop="articleBody">
                   <div class="entry-thumbnail margin-bottom-30">
                    <img alt="Fire Phone Gets Large Price Reduction by Amazon" class="attachment-post-thumbnail" height="auto" src="http://cdn.biztekmojo.com/sites/default/files/Amazon-Fire-Phone.jpg" typeof="foaf:Image" width="580"/>
                   </div>
                   <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
                   <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                    <div class="field-items">
                     <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                      <p>
                       Amazon is now slashing a big percentage off of its Fire Phone models in what seems to be a strategy to clear out the stocks of the said units.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Both models of the Amazon Fire Phone will be affected by the price reduction. The 32GB version drops from 399.99 down to 99.99, while the larger $64GB version is now at 179.99 from its original 99.99. That is quite a very deep price cut. However, Amazon is only offering the reduced prices up to May 22. Interested buyers ought to know that they will be locked to O2 as their network carrier, according to
                       <a href="http://www.techradar.com/news/phone-and-communications/mobile-phones/amazon-s-fire-phone-just-got-an-almighty-price-cut-1293945" target="_blank">
                        Tech Radar
                       </a>
                       .
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       For those who forgot what the Amazon Fire Phone packs, we'll have a quick run through of its specs and features.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Display
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       The Amazon Fire Phone has a 4.7-inch screen with an HD pixel resolution of 1208 x 720. Because of its size, it has a fairly clear 315 ppi. It also boasts a 1000:1 contrast ratio and 590cd/m2 brightness.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Camera
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       The 13MP front and rear cameras can record Full HD videos at 1080p resolution and 30fps, which makes it ideal for amateur film makers.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Processor, RAM, GPU
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Amazon Fire Phone is powered by a quad-core Snapdragon 800 processor clocked at 2.2 GHz. It is coupled with 2 GB of RAM for smoother operation of apps. For games, it is equipped with the Adreno 330 GPU.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Battery
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Amazon claims that the Fire Phone can last up to 11 hours of video playback, 65 hours for audio playback, 17 hours of talk time and 285 hours of standby time.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Firefly
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       The Amazon Fire Phone has a Firefly button which can identify DVDs, CDs and more using the cameras, according to
                       <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/news/technology-science/technology/amazon-fire-price-cut-deals-5702382" target="_blank">
                        Mirror
                       </a>
                       . You can point the camera at a book cover to find out how much it costs or to read reviews about it online.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       You can also look up details of movies and TV shows via the IMDb integration. Firefly can also identify songs and download the correct additional information such as the artist and album art.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Firefly is also useful for saving contact details that are on print. Instead of opening the contacts app and typing it manually, Firefly can automatically save them for you at a glance using the camera. It can also translate text to and from English, Spanish, French, Italian and German. It can be an indispensable tool for tourists.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Amazon Appstore
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       By default, you can buy and download apps from their own Amazon Appstore. It is a phone truly made for Amazon users and fans.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Mayday
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Stuck on something in Amazon? Fire Phone users are entitled to a free 24/7 365 days a year live video support with an expert from the company. All you need is a reliable Wi-Fi connection or a good signal on the O2 mobile network.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       The expected waiting time before an Amazon expert helps you on Mayday is around 15 seconds or less. Be careful as normal data charges are applied when the mobile network is utilized.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Dynamic Perspective
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       One unique feature the Amazon Fire Phone has is the Dynamic Perspective, which allows the user to tilt, swivel and peek at the phone for additional control in apps. There are also games that make use of Dynamic Perspective such as Lili, which requires the user to move the head in order for the in-game character to look around obstacles. It also has the auto-scroll feature which is very useful especially when on a long train ride.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       The Fire Phone has a four star rating on Amazon itself. Considering the specs and the features, it could be worth grabbing it now.
                      </p>
