          <h1>
           Johnny Marr heading to NZ for one show
          </h1>
          <p class="author">
           24 September - NEWS
          </p>
          <p>
           British guitarist and singer-songwriter
           <strong>
            Johnny Marr
           </strong>
           is heading to
           <strong>
            New Zealand
           </strong>
           for one show in
           <strong>
            Auckland
           </strong>
           at the
           <strong>
            Powerstation
           </strong>
           on
           <strong>
            Saturday February 7th
           </strong>
           .
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Having played sold out shows in
           <strong>
            Melbourne
           </strong>
           and
           <strong>
            Sydney
           </strong>
           to great acclaim earlier this year, these will be Marr's first as a solo artist when he and his band roll in to
           <strong>
            Perth, Adelaide, Brisbane
           </strong>
           and
           <strong>
            Auckland
           </strong>
           .
           <br/>
           <br/>
           As a special bonus each lucky ticket buyer also has the opportunity to channel the guitar icon himself and win a Johnny Marr signature
           <strong>
            Fender Jaguar
           </strong>
           guitar. The mighty instrument is valued at $3,500 and has a plethora of specialised features that reference Marr's decades of experience. Simply head to
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.frontiertouring.com/johnnymarr" target="_blank" title="www.frontiertouring.com/johnnymarr">
             www.frontiertouring.com/johnnymarr
            </a>
           </strong>
           for entry details.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           A founding member of cultural institution
           <strong>
            The Smiths
           </strong>
           and performer alongside the likes of
           <strong>
            Modest Mouse, Beck, Talking Heads, The The
           </strong>
           and
           <strong>
            Oasis
           </strong>
           , Marr was already rock royalty before releasing his Top 10 debut solo album
           <strong>
            The Messenger
           </strong>
           in February 2013.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           In a poll conducted by the
           <strong>
            BBC
           </strong>
           , Marr was voted fourth best guitarist of the last 30 years and
           <strong>
            Phil Alexander
           </strong>
           , Editor-in-Chief of
           <strong>
            Mojo
           </strong>
           magazine described him as "arguably Britain's last great guitar stylist".
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Nothing if not prolific, Marr had begun writing sophomore solo effort
           <strong>
            Playland,
           </strong>
           out
           <strong>
            October 3
           </strong>
           through
           <strong>
            Warner Music
           </strong>
           , almost immediately after The Messenger's release "...I kept on writing. I liked that the band had a momentum going on tour and a connection with the audience, and I thought that energy would be good to capture on the new record."
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Energetic, post-punk song writing complemented by Marr's characteristic guitar licks, engaging lyrical ideas and poised vocal phrasing. Playland is composed around the central theme that "songs that move at the speed of life" and captures much of the spirit that made his debut so memorable.
          </p>
          <p>
           Marr collected rave reviews when last in Australia with critics spotlighting his ability to combine the best of his new material with select highlights from his epic back catalogue, including The Smiths and many others.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           He's one the most celebrated guitarists in the world. Armed to the teeth with classics and his own lauded solo works, when Johnny Marr hits Australia and New Zealand in January 2015 the shows promise to be exhilarating displays of talent from a revered virtuoso.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             Johnny Marr
            </span>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Saturday February 7th: The Powerstation,
           <strong>
            Auckland
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.frontiertouring.com/johnnymarr" target="_blank" title="frontiertouring.com/johnnymarr">
             Frontier Members pre-sale
            </a>
           </strong>
           fromThursday 2 October at 3pm NZDT to Friday 3 October 3pm(or ends earlier if pre-sale allocation exhausted)
           <br/>
           <br/>
           General public on sale via
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.ticketmaster.co.nz" target="_blank" title="Ticketmaster">
             Ticketmaster
            </a>
           </strong>
           from 12pm Tuesday 7 October
          </p>
