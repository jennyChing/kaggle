         <h2>
          Video: Jumping into the amazing costume of Weidmllers u-remote robot at the SPS IPC Drives in Nuremberg
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          December 17, 2013 | 
			Posted in
          <a href="http://technojunkyard.net/topic/robots/" rel="category tag">
           Robots
          </a>

            on Video: Jumping into the amazing costume of Weidmllers u-remote robot at the SPS IPC Drives in Nuremberg
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://technojunkyard.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/metroccolis-weidmueller-u-remote-robot-sps-trade-fair-nuremberg.jpg">
           <img alt="metroccolis-weidmueller-u-remote-robot-sps-trade-fair-nuremberg" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4791" height="168" src="http://technojunkyard.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/metroccolis-weidmueller-u-remote-robot-sps-trade-fair-nuremberg-300x168.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          Weidmller is an international company with roots in Germany manufacturing a wide product range around electricity and automation. Their latest product is called u-remote  a neat control unit for complex electric systems.
          <span id="more-4789">
          </span>
          And to promote it they made a cool CGI video starring the u-remote robot that uses his power to improve a production process in a factory. That video was quite successful, so the next step was to have a real-life version at Weidmllers booth at the SPS IPC Drives trade fair for automation in Nuremberg. And since we are pretty experienced in doing the robot, they hired us to jump into their costume.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="metroccolis-weidmueller-u-remote-robot-act-win" height="333" src="http://metroccolis.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/metroccolis-weidmueller-u-remote-robot-act-win.jpg" width="592"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Responsible for the impressive costume were the guys from
          <a href="http://www.steegmueller-skulpturen.de" target="_blank">
           Steegmller Skulpturen
          </a>
          located in Ostfildern near Stuttgart. They worked day and night for 5 weeks to create a mesmerizing robot suit that wasnt extremely comfortable to wear, but stunning to look at. All trade fair visitors who made it to Weidmllers booth stopped and watched. And many were asking the staff, if that robot was real, which is always the best compliment we can wish for. The combination of costume and our moves made the illusion perfect. And word of mouth propaganda made us become the biggest highlight of the whole event.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="metroccolis-weidmueller-u-remote-robot-act-nuremberg-spectators" height="333" src="http://metroccolis.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/metroccolis-weidmueller-u-remote-robot-act-nuremberg-spectators.jpg" width="592"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Some competitors of Weidmller even sent their marketing guys to have a closer look. And they all left impressed and  as always  with tons of photos and videos. Our client was extremely happy with all the resonance and we are quite optimistic that this wasnt the only occasion where you could see the u-remote robot in action. Now, enjoy a bunch of pictures of our performance and scroll down to the bottom for a little video we put together on the train ride back to Berlin.
         </p>
