        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://myrecipemagic.com/magic?aHR0cDovL215cmVjaXBlbWFnaWMuY29tL3JlY2lwZS9yZWNpcGVkZXRhaWwvcGVhY2gtcGllLXdpdGgtc3RyZXVzZWwtdG9wcGluZw==">
          <img alt="" src="//www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/magic-button1.png"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Since peach season is here (and almost over) in Utah I thought it only fitting to make apeach pie. Now, if you read my post for
         <a href="%20http://www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/2014/08/blueberry-crumb-bars/">
          Blueberry Crumb Bars
         </a>
         , you know how I think its a tragedy to use fresh fruit in desserts. I just love fresh fruit for snacking and typically gobble it up before it gets used in a dessert recipe, so this recipe actually uses frozen peaches, but fresh are fine too, which works well since they are in season.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span id="more-7156">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Peach-Pie2.jpg">
          <img alt="Peach Pie2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7160" height="825" src="//www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Peach-Pie2.jpg" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         One of my favorite things to do with fruit pies, is to put streusel on top. Dont get me wrong, I LOVE pie crust, but a crunchy streusel is just as delicious to me! Its like putting granola on top of my fruit and yogurt. So good!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Peach-Pie3.jpg">
          <img alt="Peach Pie from DessertNowDinnerLater.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7162" height="825" src="//www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Peach-Pie3.jpg" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         This recipe is great for the summer or holidays because the cinnamon makes it homey and scrumptious! And I could totally eat pie any day of the week, because its one of my favorite desserts! I hope you get the chance to try this peach pie sometime soon!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Peach-Pie4.jpg">
          <img alt="Peach Pie from DessertNowDinnerLater.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7163" height="550" src="//www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Peach-Pie4.jpg" width="825"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Peach Pie
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="//www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Peach-Pie11.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/easyrecipe-print/7156-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            8 servings
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             recipe
            <a href="http://www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/2013/11/perfect-pie-crust-tutorial/" target="_blank">
             Perfect Pie Crust
            </a>
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Peaches:
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 to 5 cups of sliced peaches (or 24oz frozen slices)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            6 Tbsp sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp cinnamon
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 Tbsp cornstarch
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Streusel
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup butter, cold
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup oats
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup flour
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp cinnamon
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Make pie crust, place it into a pie dish, and crimp the edges. Find a pie crust tutorial
            <a href="http://www.dessertnowdinnerlater.com/2013/11/perfect-pie-crust-tutorial/" target="_blank">
             HERE
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a large bowl, toss the peach slices in the sugar, cinnamon, and cornstarch. Pour peaches and any liquid into the pie crust.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a medium sized bowl, combine the ingredients for the streusel and mix with a fork, pastry blender, or your hands, until it comes together and is crumbly, not dry. Sprinkle streusel over the peaches.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake at 350 degrees Fahrenheit for 60 minutes or until pie crust is golden brown and peach filling is bubbly in the center. Cool completely and then cut and serve.
           </li>
          </ol>
