     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Carrie &amp; Lowell
       </b>
      </i>
      is the seventh
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">
       studio album
      </a>
      by American musician
      <a href="/wiki/Sufjan_Stevens" title="Sufjan Stevens">
       Sufjan Stevens
      </a>
      , released through
      <a href="/wiki/Asthmatic_Kitty" title="Asthmatic Kitty">
       Asthmatic Kitty
      </a>
      on March 31, 2015.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-announce_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-announce-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Unlike Stevens's previous studio album, the
      <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">
       electronic
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Age_of_Adz" title="The Age of Adz">
        The Age of Adz
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       Carrie &amp; Lowell
      </i>
      is sparsely instrumental and marks a return to the performer's
      <a href="/wiki/Indie_folk" title="Indie folk">
       indie folk
      </a>
      roots.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pm_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-pm-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The album was given a one-week preview on
      <a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">
       NPR
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npr_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-npr-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The album was released to high critical acclaim, with many critics calling it one of Stevens' best.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Recording">
       Recording
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carrie_%26_Lowell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Recording">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The simple folk tunes on the album were recorded in 2014 at Stevens' home studio in
      <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">
       Brooklyn
      </a>
      and some songs in Oregon, at Portland's Flora Studios and on an iPhone in a Klamath Falls hotel room.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pm_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-pm-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The songs were inspired by the 2012 death of his mother, Carrie, and the family trips they took to Oregon in Stevens' childhood. The album title also references his stepfather.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pm_1-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-pm-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">
       Commercial performance
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carrie_%26_Lowell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Carrie &amp; Lowell
      </i>
      debuted at number ten on the
      <i>
       Billboard
      </i>
      200 with 53,000
      <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200#Incorporation_of_streaming_data_and_track_sales" title="Billboard 200">
       equivalent album units
      </a>
      ; it sold 51,000 copies in its first week,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      with the remainder of its unit count reflecting the album's
      <a href="/wiki/Streaming_media" title="Streaming media">
       streaming
      </a>
      activity and
      <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">
       track
      </a>
      sales.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The album also debuted at number 10 on the
      <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Albums_Chart" title="Canadian Albums Chart">
       Canadian Albums Chart
      </a>
      with sales of 4,400.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      As of July 2015,
      <i>
       Carrie &amp; Lowell
      </i>
      has sold 105,000 copies in the United States, with approximately 30% (32,000) of its total having been sold in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vinyl_record" title="Vinyl record">
       vinyl
      </a>
      configuration.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">
       Critical reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carrie_%26_Lowell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       Carrie &amp; Lowell
      </i>
      received rave reviews upon its release. At
      <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
       Metacritic
      </a>
      , which assigns a
      <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">
       normalized
      </a>
      rating out of 100 to reviews from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_critic" title="Music critic">
       music critics
      </a>
      , the album has received an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" title="Weighted mean">
       average
      </a>
      score of 90, indicating "universal acclaim", based on 40 reviews.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-metacritic-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Andrew Hannah of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_405" title="The 405">
       The 405
      </a>
      proclaimed that "Carrie &amp; Lowell is just the latest in a long line of unimpeachable achievements."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_405_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_405-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In his review for
      <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">
       Pitchfork Media
      </a>
      , writer Brandon Stosuy wrote, "Sufjan Stevens' new album,
      <i>
       Carrie &amp; Lowell
      </i>
      , is his best."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Stephen Carlick of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Exclaim!" title="Exclaim!">
        Exclaim!
       </a>
      </i>
      called the record "a quietly triumphant return for Stevens, announced not by fireworks but by a series of small, elegant moments that reach for the heart."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exclaim_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-exclaim-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
