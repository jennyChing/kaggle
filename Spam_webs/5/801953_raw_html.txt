        <h2>
         Co-founder and visual artist Allison Kunath calls this movement The Love Mob, a growing community that fosters love, self-expression and connection. The Mob embraces people from all walks of life through organized acts of love, from flash mobs to clothing drives to dinner parties serving up soulful music, dance and performance.
        </h2>
        <p>
         Its to bring people together, to celebrate each other and to express and acknowledge the fact that we need more connection in our world, Allison says.
        </p>
        <p>
         Allison believes this sense of belonging inspires people to follow their passions, express themselves and spread the love to others. With co-founders Preston Smiles and Mustafa Shakir, she dedicates her artistic gifts to
         <a href="http://thelovemob.org" target="_blank">
          The Love Mob project
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h3>
         Go west, young artist
        </h3>
        <p>
         The artistic journey wasnt always roses and puppies for Allison.
        </p>
        <p>
         From childhood, she knew she wanted to be an artist someday. But as she got older, doubts set in.
        </p>
        <p>
         As a child, I felt really nurtured and enabled to express myself growing up, but somewhere along the line, I came up with this crazy belief that it wasnt possible or realisticto pursue art as a career, she says.
        </p>
        <p>
         As a college athlete studying design, she felt out of place socially, and unfulfilled creatively.Then came a move that was the turning point in her life.
        </p>
        <p>
         It wasnt until I moved to California and started to get to know who I actually am, and find people who really resonate with my true essence and bring out the best sides of me and nurture my creativity again, that I gave myself permission to get back into my fine art and realize that that passion has been there since Day 1, she says.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allisonkunath.com" target="_blank">
          Today, shes recognized as a visual artist, fashion designer and a maker of all sorts of things.
         </a>
         Youll find her either working on a beautiful piece of geometrical illustration or creating eye-popping clothing to add to her wardrobe.
        </p>
        <p>
         At the heart of her work is a mission:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          My purpose is to inspire and empower people to find their passion and use their voice, she says. Im here to experience and create love.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h3>
         The Hollywood premiere
        </h3>
        <p>
         Finding kindred spirits in California changed her life, and Allison dreamed of bringing that kind of connection to others.
        </p>
        <p>
         (
         <a href="http://thelovemob.org" target="_blank">
          The Love Mob
         </a>
         ) was really born from this desire to connect and bring folks together, she says. Its sort of like waiting for that ideal client to come where I could bring my message into the world or get behind somebody whos actually saying something that I believe in. And Im like, Why dont you make the message? Why dont you be that client? Why dont you create that voice that people want to hear?
        </p>
        <p>
         So thats what she, Preston and Mustafa decided to do.
        </p>
        <p>
         Were basically birds of a feather where our favorite things in the worldareexpressionand connecting with people, she says.We were constantly bringing people together and uniting folks from different circles, and decided to expand on it.
        </p>
        <p>
         These three creatives decided to take their passion for connection to the next level by gathering a couple hundred people for a flash mob, or love mob, at Hollywood &amp; Highland center in L.A.
        </p>
        <p>
         We didnt know if wed have five people show up or 500, and the fact that people showed up with such energy and the response was so positive, we knew we really had something then, she says.
        </p>
        <p>
         That was the start of a series of events. The common themes are self-expression, creativity, being your truest self, connecting with people around you, honoring our planet  kind of like all of these basic things that we know to make our lives better., she says. Were really just creating a container for community where people can come and feel like theyre not alone.
        </p>
        <p>
         One of her favorite events was a giant clothing drive and block party they hosted on Skid Row in downtown L.A. called JustBECause.
        </p>
        <p>
         Allison handles the visuals and branding for many of The Love Mobs events.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          All the work I do for the Love Mob to me feels like my most direct contribution, she says. Its branding for this social movement that makes people feel good. It empowers them, it inspires them (and) makes them feel connected to their community and the world. And thats really special to me.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/videoseries?list=UUz-bqP4pSWZzPZEJaJorz0w" width="640">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Mob rule
        </h3>
        <p>
         Allison wants to see Love Mobs overrunning the world, whether theyre five or 500 people strong.
        </p>
        <p>
         Were not just the Love Mob in L.A., she says. Were a template; were an example of what can happen. On the groups website, you can find some handy how-tos on holding your own Love Mob event.
        </p>
        <p>
         She also hopes to take The Love Mob to campuses so both students and teachers can get involved.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre just as pumped up as we are in spreading love to the world, you can tap into The Love Mob community through Facebook and email for announcements on upcoming events.
        </p>
        <h3>
        </h3>
        <p>
         If you need some spiritual refreshment in your life, Allison recommends
         <a href="http://designgood.com/books-were-addicted-to/" target="_blank" title="DesignGood Books">
          <em>
           The Kybalion
          </em>
         </a>
         . Its Hermetic philosophy that precedes most of the philosophy that we know today, but it breaks it down into seven principles that are really basic. It may be a refresher and a reminder in different words. Get on over to our Good Books section and check it out.
        </p>
