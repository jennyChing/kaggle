               <p>
                So, I am still massively enjoying that the Autumn has finally arrived. I may have had an epically bad week (you know it isnt going to go well when you have fallen off your bike by 8:10 am on Monday morning) but that doesnt taint my love of the Autumn. I have worn my woolly hat and scarf with aplomb!
               </p>
               <p>
                After my
                <a href="http://creatingcalifornia.com/2014/10/recipe-pumpkin-pie-spice/" target="_blank" title="Recipe: Pumpkin Pie Spice">
                 last post
                </a>
                , I havent been able tostop thinking about all the wonderful Autumnal flavours and foods, especially those from the States  the Americans really know how to do Autumn (or should I say Fall?!), and I realised that there is one homemade milk (well actually there is more than oneI smell a project!) that I havent tried yethomemade Pecan Nut Milk!
               </p>
               <p>
                I got so excited about this, pecans are such a yummy Autumnal nut  who doesnt love a pecan pie with cinnamon?! (well, to be honest, my husband Adam doesnt, thats who. In fact he doesnt like ANY nutcrazy!), and I think pecan nut milk will go so so SO well in all the yummy Autumn drinks (gingerbread latte with pecan nut milk anyone?!) and cereals (pecan maple granola with pecan nut milkhello!).
               </p>
               <p>
                I decided to approach this in the same way as I did my
                <a href="http://creatingcalifornia.com/2014/08/recipe-homemade-almond-milk/" target="_blank" title="Recipe: Homemade Almond Milk">
                 Almond Milk
                </a>
                and
                <a href="http://creatingcalifornia.com/2014/08/recipe-homemade-cashew-nut-milk/" target="_blank" title="Recipe: Homemade Cashew Nut Milk">
                 Cashew Nut Milk
                </a>
                and it worked really well  Im so pleased! It is perhaps more similar (in method) to the cashew nut milk, because there isnt much pecan meal left at the end  pecans are quite soft nuts and so they blend pretty thoroughly into the water.
               </p>
               <p>
                This milk is just divine! It has the creamyness of the cashew nut milk but with that distinctive pecan flavour (I have been sitting here for about 10 minutes trying to work out how to describe the flavour of pecans.earthy? woody? burnt?  let me know in the comments if you can think of a way to describe them!!). In fact, I think this milk holds the flavour of the nutsbetter than anyof the other ones I have tried.
               </p>
               <p>
                So, if you want to join me in this delicious Autumn treat, then here is the recipe!
               </p>
               <h2>
                Homemade Pecan Nut Milk Recipe
               </h2>
               <p>
                <a href="http://creatingcalifornia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pecan-Nut-Milk.jpg">
                 <img alt="Pecan Nut Milk" class="alignnone wp-image-701 size-full" height="587" src="http://creatingcalifornia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Pecan-Nut-Milk.jpg" width="587"/>
                </a>
               </p>
               <h3>
                Ingredients:
               </h3>
               <ul>
                <li>
                 1 cup pecan nuts
                </li>
                <li>
                 Water to soak
                </li>
                <li>
                 2 cups cold water to blend
                </li>
               </ul>
               <h3>
                Method:
               </h3>
               <ol>
                <ol>
                 <li>
                  Soak the pecans for about an hour. They are quite soft so dont really need longer
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Drain and rinse pecans. Dispose of the water that you soaked them in.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Add the fresh water and then the pecans to your blender and blend for about a minute, until the nuts are very fine and the water is milky.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Hold your nut milk bag over a bowl or large jug and fill it with the blended pecanmixture.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Squeeze milk through the bag, this will be easier than when you
                  <a href="http://creatingcalifornia.com/2014/08/recipe-homemade-almond-milk/" target="_blank" title="Recipe: Homemade Almond Milk">
                   make almond milk
                  </a>
                  because there wont be much meal left to wring milk from.
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Your fresh pecanmilk is what is left in the bowl!
                 </li>
                </ol>
               </ol>
               <blockquote>
                <p>
                 <span style="color: #48b2a8;">
                  Top Tip:I havent tried it yet, but I think maple syrup would be an amazing way to sweeten this milkif you think it needs sweetening that is!
                 </span>
                </p>
               </blockquote>
