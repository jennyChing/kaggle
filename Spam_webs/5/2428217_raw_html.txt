    <div id="sinker">
     <article>
      <h1>
       Day 1: Fyrsta
      </h1>
      <div class="media video" id="youtube-SDezzDQVy6M-460-259">
      </div>
      <noscript>
       <a href="http://youtube.com/v/SDezzDQVy6M" itemprop="video">
        View this video on Youtube.com
       </a>
      </noscript>
      <p>
       The song is called "Fyrsta", which means "First". Because it is the first song of the series, but also because this piano theme was the first thing i managed to write while temporarily living in Los Angeles after weeks of severe creative block while trying to adjust to a new and strange place.
      </p>
     </article>
     <article>
      <h1>
       Day 2: Near Light
      </h1>
      <div class="media video" id="youtube-UHVh_L_kv1Q-460-259">
      </div>
      <noscript>
       <a href="http://youtube.com/v/UHVh_L_kv1Q" itemprop="video">
        View this video on Youtube.com
       </a>
      </noscript>
      <p>
       The second installment in my Living Room Songs series. This time i tried adding some electronics. My mother and sister came by for a visit so I made them play some synths as well.
      </p>
     </article>
     <article>
      <h1>
       Day 3: Film Credits
      </h1>
      <div class="media video" id="youtube-U8U073nbyn4-460-259">
      </div>
      <noscript>
       <a href="http://youtube.com/v/U8U073nbyn4" itemprop="video">
        View this video on Youtube.com
       </a>
      </noscript>
      <p>
       "Film Credits" is the third installment in lafur Arnalds' "Living Room Songs" and is a new variation of a theme from his score to the film
       <a href="http://www.anotherhappydaymovie.com">
        Another Happy Day
       </a>
       .
      </p>
     </article>
     <article>
      <h1>
       Day 4: Tomorrow's Song
      </h1>
      <div class="media video" id="youtube-W9t3mbv2Hd8-460-259">
      </div>
      <noscript>
       <a href="http://youtube.com/v/W9t3mbv2Hd8" itemprop="video">
        View this video on Youtube.com
       </a>
      </noscript>
      <p>
       Those who attended one of the Berlin dates of lafur's improvisational solo-tour may recognize this theme. It is built around an idea that was improvised live in Berlin, polished a little bit and now recorded.
      </p>
     </article>
     <article>
      <h1>
       Day 5: gst
      </h1>
      <div class="media video" id="youtube-LYvlmiwEP9M-460-259">
      </div>
      <noscript>
       <a href="http://youtube.com/v/LYvlmiwEP9M" itemprop="video">
        View this video on Youtube.com
       </a>
      </noscript>
      <p>
       This is an idea from last August, it had lots of electronics in it and a big arrangement. But I had been struggling with it for a while and was never really happy with it. While looking for ideas for Day 5 I decided to go back to the basic idea - the way it was before i piled all this stuff on it, and i found this lovely violin melody beneath it all. I added the viola for some movement and made a new structure, and here we are! Much happier now..
      </p>
     </article>
     <article>
      <h1>
       Day 6: Lag fyrir mmu
      </h1>
      <div class="media video" id="youtube-Fyxu3LLwSV4-460-259">
      </div>
      <noscript>
       <a href="http://youtube.com/v/Fyxu3LLwSV4" itemprop="video">
        View this video on Youtube.com
       </a>
      </noscript>
      <p>
       "Lag fyrir mmu" means "Song for grandma". It is the 6th installment of Living Room Songs and a tribute to an amazing person and a good friend who I miss dearly.
      </p>
     </article>
     <article class="l">
      <h1>
       Day 7: This Place is a Shelter
      </h1>
      <div class="media video" id="youtube-wMSDPLOSHyQ-460-259">
      </div>
      <noscript>
       <a href="http://youtube.com/v/wMSDPLOSHyQ" itemprop="video">
        View this video on Youtube.com
       </a>
      </noscript>
      <p>
       This is the last installment of lafur Arnalds' Living Room Songs series. I decided to throw a little 'wrap party' to celebrate the end of the series and we shot this there. "This place is a shelter" refers to my home, to which i have invited you all with this series. I am away much of the time and what keeps me going is knowing that I'll always have 'home' to come back to.
      </p>
     </article>
