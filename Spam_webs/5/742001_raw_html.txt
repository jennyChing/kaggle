          <p>
           Adobe is an innovative developer and its Creative Cloud applications are often the best tool for what professionals require, but the subscription-only model isn't for everyone.
          </p>
          <p>
           For those
           <span>
            who are holding out from joining Adobes Creative Cloud, t
           </span>
           he
           <em>
            Digital Arts
           </em>
           team have chosen a selection of art, design, photography, video and VFX tools for professional creatives.
          </p>
          <p>
           There are a million free and cheap creative tools out there, and a small number provide a welcome alternative for students and hobbyists. However being
           <em>
            Digital Arts
           </em>
           , we focus on tools that are suitable for professional designers, artists and editors. That
           <span>
            dramatically reduces
           </span>
           the number of such tools we'd consider.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span>
            Some pro-level tools are also available for free, and were certainly not going to turn our noses up at them. Some are open source and developed for the love, but mostly theyre free because their developers are getting paid somewhere else, such as Google's banner ad design tool.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you're making a living from digital content creation youd probably rather pay for an up to date tool that lets you create amazing works in an efficient manner, offers good support terms and won't let you down on a critical job. It's these tools that form the main focus for our feature.
          </p>
          <p>
           Some of these applications are by long-standing rivals to Adobe (such as Autodesk and Avid), some are by independent developers who created a tool for a specific purpose because offerings by Adobe were too broad (Sketch), and a few are by companies who you might have previously associated with parish newsletter preparation, but have actually created a top notch, professional-level app (we're looking at you Serif for Affinity Designer).
          </p>
          <p>
           There really are a lot of alternative choices out there for professionals, so check out these great products.
          </p>

        <h4 class="nsSteper">
         Step 19
        </h4>
        <h2>
         Final Cut Pro X
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Alternative to:
         </strong>
         Premiere Pro for video editing
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Price:
         </strong>
         199.99
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Platform
         </strong>
         : MacOS X 10.9.2
        </p>
        <p>
         This
         <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/final-cut-pro/id424389933?mt=12">
          Mac-only
         </a>
         NLE has managed to weather the storm of re-engineering and accusations of dumbing down to become a serious choice for professionals once more.
        </p>
        <p>
         Although the workflow was revolutionary for video editing software when
         <span>
          FCP X
         </span>
         first appeared, Apple got it in the neck from unimpressed broadcast video editors and has since responded to user demands from this quarter with almost every release.
         <span>
          As a result some pro editors now swear by the magnetic timeline approach, rather than at it.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The latest 64-bit version offers native MXF import, edit, and export, as well as support for
         <span>
          import and editing with Panasonic AVC-LongG media and the
         </span>
         option to export AVC-Intra MXF files, while recent updates include the ability to apply a standard (Rec. 709) look in real time to high dynamic range and wide colour gamut video from ARRI, Blackmagic Design, Canon, and Sony cameras.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.digitalartsonline.co.uk/reviews/creative-software/apple-final-cut-pro-x-1003/">
          Read a review of Final Cut Pro X
         </a>
        </p>
        <img alt="" id="nsSlide_18" src="http://cdn2.digitalartsonline.co.uk/cmsdata/slideshow/3592295/FCPXMacbookPro-Retina-FCPX-Skiing-PRINT.jpg" width="510"/>

        <h4 class="nsSteper">
         Step 20
        </h4>
        <h2>
         DaVinci Resolve
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Alternative to:
         </strong>
         Speedgrade for colour grading and Premiere Pro for video editing
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Price:
         </strong>
         Free/635 plus VAT
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Platform:
         </strong>
         Mac OS X 10.8.5, Windows 7
        </p>
        <p>
         Resolve is an advanced 64-bit video finishing tool, multitrack editor and colour correction software, offering a collaborative workflow.It offers 32 bit floating point image processingand uses a unique YRGB colour space for grading.
        </p>
        <p>
         Resolve uses node based processing where each node can have colour correction, power windows and effects applied.Editing tools are context sensitive and multiple people can work together on the same timeline at the same time.
        </p>
        <p>
         Resolve is highly scalable and supports up to eight GPUs working together and offers powerful delivery options for versioning, finishing and delivery at any resolution. It can share sequences and round trip projects with Final Cut Pro X, Media Composer and Premiere Pro CC, while VFX shots from Fusion and After Effects CC can be dropped directly into the timeline for final grading and finishing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/uk/products/davinciresolve">
          Read more about Resolve
         </a>

         <strong>
          VFX &amp; Motion Graphics
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Blackmagic Fusion
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Alternative to:
         </strong>
         After Effects for VFX
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Price:
         </strong>
         Free
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Platform:
         </strong>
         Windows 7/8
        </p>
        <p>
         Fusion 7 is a 64-bit visual effects and motion graphics suite that was recently released as a free product by new owner Blackmagic Design. Originally developed by Eyeon, the software has been a mainstay of
         <a href="https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/products/fusion/credits">
          VFX studios worldwide
         </a>
         for many years.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fusion 7 features an infinite 3D workspace and node-based workflow for quickly building unlimited effects, as well as advanced 3D compositing, paint, rotoscope, retiming, stabilisation and titling tool
        </p>
        <p>
         It also includes a 3D particle generator and multiple keyers, including Primatte. Fusion 7 lets customers import and render 3D geometry and scenes from other applications as well as create their own elements from scratch.
        </p>
        <p>
         Also available is Fusion 7 Studio, a complete bundle of VFX and motion graphics solutions with collaborative tools and unlimited network rendering for creative teams and larger facilities, available for 635.
        </p>
        <p>
         Though both the free version of Fusion 7 andFusion 7 Studio are Windows only, a Mac release is thought to be in the pipeline
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/uk/products/fusion">
          Read more about Fusion 7
         </a>
        </p>

        <h2>
         Autodesk Smoke 2015
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Alternative to:
         </strong>
         After Effects for VFX and motion graphics
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Price:
         </strong>
         1,280 plus VAT
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Platform:
         </strong>
         Mac OS X 10.8.5/9/10 (for Smoke Extension 1)
        </p>
        <p>
         This professionalediting and effects system has made a big impact in post production environments, with dedicated support for the Mac Pro and XML interoperability with Final Cut Pro X adding to its appeal for Mac-based facilities.
        </p>
        <p>
         Effects tools are integrated within a standard timeline editing environment, where you can integrate lighting effects, work with 3D geometry from Maya in scenes, mix multi-resolution video layers and make changes interactively.
        </p>
        <p>
         The node-based compositing environment is accessible directly from the timeline and a 3D Tracker lets you duplicate real-world camera movement for compositing elements into shots. Smoke 2015 Extension 1 further allows you to apply LUTs and colour transforms and more.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.autodesk.com/products/smoke/overview">
          Read more about Smoke 2015

        <img alt="" id="nsSlide_21" src="http://cdn2.digitalartsonline.co.uk/cmsdata/slideshow/3592295/smoke-2015-screenshot-connectfx-node-based-compositing.jpg" width="510"/>

        <h4 class="nsSteper">
         Step 23
        </h4>
        <h2>
         The Foundry Nuke 9
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Alternative to:
         </strong>
         After Effects for VFX
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Price:
         </strong>
         2,534 plus VAT
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Platform:
         </strong>
         Mac OS X 10.8/9, Windows 7/8 64-bit
        </p>
        <p>
         The Foundry's award-winning compositor is an industry standard in film post production. The price is steep compared to the Adobe tools, but Nuke is a powerhouse for visual effects.
        </p>
        <p>
         It has the power to combine live action 2D and 3D elements in an integrated 3D environment and offers a resolution-independent, multi-channel pipeline. It uses a node-based workflow to apply industry standard keyers (Ultimatte, Primatte, Keylight), together with rotoscope and vector paint tools, time manipulators and multiple colour correction approaches, as well as the ability to relight renders in the comp environment.
        </p>
        <p>
         GPU-acceleration makes it possible to speed up interactive processing for tasks such as motion blur, denoise, retiming and defocus, while Deep Compositing tools allow artists to work with 'deep images' containing multiple opacity and colour samples per pixel. It also offers stereoscopic tools and full support for HDRI formats.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="%20http://www.thefoundry.co.uk/products/nuke/">
          Read more about NUKE
         </a>
