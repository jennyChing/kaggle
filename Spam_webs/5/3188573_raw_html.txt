         <h1>
          Massachusetts Enacts Paid Sick Leave
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          by
          <a href="http://insuranceexchangeus.com/author/kelsey/" rel="author" title="Posts by Kelsey Schneider">
           Kelsey Schneider
          </a>
          | Jan 15, 2014 |
          <a href="http://insuranceexchangeus.com/category/blog/" rel="category tag">
           Blog
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://insuranceexchangeus.com/category/massachusetts/" rel="category tag">
           Massachusetts
          </a>
          |
         </p>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           Massachusetts voters approved a ballot measure to provide earned paid sick leave to employees on Nov. 4, 2014.
           <a href="http://www.sec.state.ma.us/ele/ele14/pip144.htm">
            <strong>
             Question 4
            </strong>
           </a>
           , also known as the
           <strong>
            Massachusetts Paid Sick Days Initiative,
           </strong>
           passed by a 60 to 40% margin.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            July 1, 2015
           </strong>
           is the date the Initiative will take effect.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <img alt="US Insurance Exchange" class="alignright wp-image-13767 size-full" height="391" src="http://insuranceexchangeus.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/US-Insurance-Exchange15.bmp" width="587"/>
            All employers in Massachusetts will be required to comply
           </strong>
           with this new earned sick time law in general. The mandate requiring
           <em>
            paid leave
           </em>
           only applies to employers with 11 or more employees however. Under this new law:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Employers with
            <strong>
             11 or more employees
            </strong>
            must allow all employees to earn and use up to 40 hours of
            <strong>
             paid
            </strong>
            sick leave per calendar year.
           </li>
           <li>
            Employers with
            <strong>
             fewer than 11 employees
            </strong>
            must allow all employees to earn and use up to 40 hours of
            <strong>
             unpaid
            </strong>
            sick leave per calendar year.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           More generous than the new earned sick time law, employers may adopt or continue earned sick time policies. Employers must also continue to comply with existing collective bargaining agreements or contracts that have more generous leave provisions.
          </p>
          <p>
           Starting July 1, 2015, under the law, employees earn a minimum of
           <strong>
            one hour of sick leave for every 30 hours worked
           </strong>
           , up to a maximum of
           <strong>
            40 hours per calendar year
           </strong>
           . Employers may restrict the use of accrued sick leave for the first 90 days of employment however.
          </p>
          <p>
           Into the next calendar year, Employees must be permitted to carry over up to 40 hours of earned but unused sick leave. The law does not require employers to allow employees to use more than 40 hours in a calendar year however.
          </p>
         </div>
