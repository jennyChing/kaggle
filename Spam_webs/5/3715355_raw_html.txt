         <h1>
          A tsunami can't stop Japan making the new Honda Civic - what's to stop us buying it?
          <br/>
         </h1>
         <p>
          <span class="article-timestamp article-timestamp-updated">
           <span class="article-timestamp-label">
            Updated:
           </span>
           17:01 EST, 10 March 2012
          </span>
         </p>

         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           For you, Six Nations rugby reaches its
 climax next Saturday  but Im writing this on day two, having just 
watched the utterly brilliant Wales-Ireland game.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Mud, blood, sin bins,
 choice words and the smashing together of blokes with faces like root 
vegetables: its what widescreen tellys for.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           With a few cold beers 
inside me, I didnt want it to end and flipping through the sports pages
 just now I noticed it was Super Bowl day. Why dont I make a night of 
it, I thought? Ill watch the rugby and American football, and see how 
the two compare.
          </font>
         </p>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <div class="thinCenter">
          <img alt="Given that Honda engines are most fun at high revs, it's both good and bad having that four-cylinder whine muffled. But it definitely feels more grown up" class="blkBorder" height="286" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2012/03/08/article-2112104-12156050000005DC-553_468x286.jpg" width="468"/>
          <p class="imageCaption">
           Given that Honda engines are most fun at high revs, it's both good and bad having that four-cylinder whine muffled. But it definitely feels more grown up
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Well, Im halfway through Super Bowl 
XLVI (as its called) and apart from the shape of the ball theyve 
nothing in common.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           In the American game, 52 players run out for each 
team  then half of them immediately sit down, because you field a 
different lot depending on whether youre on the offense or defense;
 one athletic, the other made of heavy goods vehicles.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           It took me a 
while to notice this because for two hours the only people doing 
anything were girls with big pom-poms. Then the footballers came out. 
Then they disappeared again. Thered been a time out, apparently.
          </font>
         </p>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <div class="thinCenter">
          <img alt="The steering is more direct and although they've stiffened the chassis, the ride is actually smoother thanks to fluid-filled bushings (the bits that reduce vibration)" class="blkBorder" height="286" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2012/03/08/article-2112104-12155FDB000005DC-914_468x286.jpg" width="468"/>
          <p class="imageCaption">
           The steering is more direct and although they've stiffened the chassis, the ride is actually smoother thanks to fluid-filled bushings (the bits that reduce vibration)
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Heres what Ive gathered: when the 
quarterback throws the ball forward, you need offensive players who can 
run very fast, dart between the dumper-truck defenders, catch the ball 
and carry it over the touchline. Simple  so why does it all unfold so 
slowly?
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           If they stop waving flags and showing adverts long enough for 30
 seconds of play, its followed by two minutes of standing around while 
20st defenders are attached to oxygen machines. I reckon I could do 
their job. If I ate a few more pies.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <div class="thinCenter">
          <img alt="The split-level dashboard is still here, but the materials feel good and now that I'm used to it, it's very easy to use" class="blkBorder" height="286" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2012/03/08/article-2112104-12155FF0000005DC-90_468x286.jpg" width="468"/>
          <p class="imageCaption">
           The split-level dashboard is still here, but the materials feel good and now that I'm used to it, it's very easy to use
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Yank football is good fun, but needs 
to go on a diet. You could argue the same goes for their motor industry.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
           Look at Detroit, the motor city only gradually getting back on its feet
 after a subsidy from President Obama dragged the car giants back from 
the brink of oblivion.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Now compare that to Japan, where Honda
 rode out not just a recession but a flipping tsunami (with nuclear 
crisis thrown in) so nimbly that within a few months they were back up 
to full production  just in time to deliver this new Civic.
          </font>
         </p>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <div class="thinCenter">
          <img alt="It's a much better car than the old one, but I'm worried about the 24,500 price" class="blkBorder" height="286" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2012/03/08/article-2112104-12155FFB000005DC-818_468x286.jpg" width="468"/>
          <p class="imageCaption">
           It's a much better car than the old one, but I'm worried about the 24,500 price. For a few thousand more you'll get a BMW 3 Series
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Actually, this was assembled in 
Swindon. But the components came from Japan and its the parts that are 
the key to Hondas success, with its reliable engines found in 
everything from lawnmowers to mopeds to speedboats and F1 cars.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           A new 
1.6-litre diesel out this year produces 120hp but is so low-emitting 
its road-tax exempt. This new Civic will be the first to get it. So 
what does the ninth generation Civic have to offer?
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Well, of course, one of the most 
reliable drivetrains anywhere  built around a 1.8-litre petrol unit in 
this case.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Its got engine start/stop and a gear-change indicator to 
help you drive more greenly, and theyve lengthened the gear ratios to 
make it more laid-back and fuel-efficient.
          </font>
         </p>
         <div class="clear">
         </div>
         <div class="thinCenter">
          <div class="thinArtSplitter">
           <div class="splitLeft">
            <img alt="Honda Civic 1.8 EX GT" class="blkBorder" height="250" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2012/03/08/article-2112104-12155FE7000005DC-655_224x250.jpg" width="224"/>
           </div>
           <div class="splitRight">
            <img alt="Honda Civic 1.8 EX GT" class="blkBorder" height="250" src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2012/03/08/article-2112104-12156027000005DC-298_224x250.jpg" width="224"/>
           </div>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
          </div>
          <p class="imageCaption">
           The Honda Civic has engine start/stop and a gear-change indicator to help you drive more greenly
           <br/>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           The steering is more direct 
and although theyve stiffened the chassis, the ride is actually 
smoother thanks to fluid-filled bushings (the bits that reduce 
vibration).
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
           I took an old Civic on a bad road near me and needed a 
chiropractor afterwards; this one had no such problem.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
           Thats down to the seats as well as 
the suspension. Theyve put air chambers and lumbar supports in the 
front on this model. The back seats are the interesting bit: Hondas 
Magic Seat system means you can lift, flip and fold them flat in one 
movement, giving you a 5ft 2in by 4ft 5in load floor.
          </font>
         </p>

         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           The design on the old Civic was bold, probably a bit too bold for most customers, with its split-level spaceship dashboard, triangular tailpipes and weird plastic shelf glued to the rear windscreen.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Theyve kept its aero-dynamic silhouette  in fact, theyve improved it with a lower, wider stance  but theyve toned down the madness.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Theres still a spoiler-type thing on the back, but it sits lower down so visibility is much better and theres room for a wiper.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           The split-level dashboard is still here, but the materials feel good and now that Im used to it, its very easy to use.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
           The new Civics also much quieter. Given that Honda engines are most fun at high revs, its both good and bad having that four-cylinder whine muffled. But it definitely feels more grown up.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Ive always liked Hondas light, chucky gearboxes and this five-speed feels good to use, matching the improved steering.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
           Power-wise, youre not going to win any drag races. The 2.2-litre diesel will have more torque.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Overall, its a much better car than the old one, but Im worried about the 24,500 price.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
           Yes, that includes heated leather seats, adaptive cruise control, sat-nav, parking camera and the new Collision Mitigation Braking  but whos going to buy a specced-up, range-topping Civic, when for a few thousand more theyd get a BMW 3 Series?
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           But thats the top end. They start at 8,000 less and the Civic is a huge draw, with 650,000 sold in the UK so far. Whos buying them? People who read reliability surveys, which the Civic tops over and over again.
           <br/>
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size:1.2em;">
           The base market is sensible older people. And though Im not quite ready to join them yet, Im glad the Civic continues to improve. By the time I am, itll be a masterpiece.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font style="font-size: 1.2em;">
           Talking of older people, its half time at the Super Bowl and Madonnas just come on. I think I prefer singing hookers. Back to the rugby next weekend, eh?

          </font>

           <span style="font-weight: bold;">

The F12 Berlinettas mid-front mounted 6.3-litre V12 engine develops a giddy 730hp, hitting 62mph in 3.1 seconds, with a top speed of 210mph. Theres a shortened wheelbase and a new Scaglietti spaceframe using advanced alloys for 20 per cent greater rigidity but 70kg less weight than the 599. No production car has lapped the Fiorano test track faster. Prices are to be announced.
