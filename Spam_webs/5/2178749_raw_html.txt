           'Just some of your things, like you asked.
          </p>
          <p>
           'But specifically what things? I ask, turning off Radio 4.
          </p>
          <p>
           'I was listening to that, he says, perplexed.
          </p>
          <p>
           'But I just want to know that I wont be wearing tops as skirts and gloves as socks this weekend.
          </p>
          <p>
           'Why would you be wearing that?
          </p>
          <p>
           'Because men cant pack for women. Everyone at work says.
          </p>
          <p>
           'Well, everyone at work is mad.
          </p>
          <p>
           We continue on our journey. We talk about very little and instead listen to the radio. I think to myself, 'Oh no, we have become one of those couples you see who seem frozen in silence and bored with one another. But perhaps I was wrong about those couples. Because I am not bored with him. I am just comfortable enough that I dont feel the need to fill every silence. And anyway, all I can think about is what he has packed for me.
          </p>
          <p>
           We stop at a service station to get some snacks. I tell him I need to stay in the car to make a work call. But he knows me too well. 'Bryony, you can see what I have packed for you in about an hour. Relax!
          </p>
          <p>
           When we arrive at his childhood home I am all smiles but I have visions of coming down to breakfast in a sarong and a bikini top or, worse, a corset, stockings and suspenders (though I am not sure I even own any).
          </p>
          <p>
           When we finally take our things up to the room, I rip open the bag to reveal a pair of jeans that no longer fit, a beach skirt, a running T-shirt, some plimsolls and 15 vests. There are some knickers, a pair of socks and a pair of tights and he has packed his washbag, in which he has stuffed my toothbrush and some eye cream.
          </p>
          <p>
           I turn and smile at him. Its not perfect, but its much better than I thought.
          </p>
