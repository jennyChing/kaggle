          Evgeniy Garkaviy

          <span style="color: #0c343d;">
           <span lang="en-US">

           </span>
          </span>
         </em>

          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="color: #222222;">
          <span lang="en-US">
           <b>

           </b>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="color: #222222;">
          <span lang="en-US">

If it hadnt been for the blog, it would be very unlikely I wouldve had the chance to have ever met these people.
          </span>

          </b>
         </span>

I love that facebook fan pages play such an integral role in the engagement of your readership, people are so tied into social media that through a fanpage, people can instantly ask and comment on your content, lifestyle, choices. And in turn,

         <span lang="en-US">
          <b>
           you can answer any questions

         </span>

         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
          that awaits when you take a flight and then hit the ground with your notebook and your camera and begin sharing it with your readers.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         <span lang="en-US">
          There is a feeling you get when youre walking through the airport terminal, about to board a plane for a night flight over the ocean. Youll land and
         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
          <b>
           everything will be new
          </b>
         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
          , and different, and youll hear different languages and see signs you cannot read and taste foods that arent familiar. You take it all in, you share it, you write about it, you put people who might never get a chance to experience another country and place them right there with you. I often like to think of my blog as a way to let someone become a little tiny person, who slips into your pocket, and each time you look over at an impressive monument, a startling vista, you can say
         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
          <b>
           hey, look at that!
          </b>
         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
           and
         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
          <b>
           wow thats cool, check this out!
          </b>
         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
          
         </span>
        </p>
        <p lang="en-US" style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
         There are so many beautiful places to see in the world, and being able to record them, photograph them, ask the locals about them, thats what I can share on my travel blog. And for me because we publish stories by hundreds of different authors, its about a much larger group of travelers. Some of our writers go to places Id never visit, but I get to experience them and be that little guy in their pocket when I publish their stories and view their photographs.
        </p>

         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">

           opened up so many opportunities on the Internet
          </b>
         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">

         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
          <b>
           to document our travels and adventures
          </b>
         </span>
         <span lang="en-US">
          so we can look back on it in years from now and remember all the fun (and work hard) we had. Being a travel blogger has provided us with a new perspective on life and we love every minute of it!
