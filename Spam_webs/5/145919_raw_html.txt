       <header class="post-header">
        <h2 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
         <a href="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/the-7-most-beautiful-villages-in-france/" rel="bookmark" title="My Favorite Villages in France">
          My Favorite Villages in France
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <div class="post-content">
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         After driving from Monaco to tretat, and from Carcassonne to Lyon, I think Im allowed to put on theproverbial France expert hat. Ive seen my fair share of villages, landmarks and open countryside to know which are worth making a detour for  or as we call it, taking the scenic route. And although some would argue that France is, as such, one giant postcard, there are some places that I feel took this picture perfect notion to another level.
        </p>
        <h2>
         So, where are the most beautifulvillages in France?
        </h2>
        <h2>
         Riquewihr
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12629" style="width: 885px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Beautiful Villages in France" class="size-large wp-image-12629" height="583" src="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/07-IMG_9636-930x620.jpg" width="875"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/the-7-most-beautiful-villages-in-france/&amp;media=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/07-IMG_9636-930x620.jpg&amp;description=My Favorite Villages in France')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Riquewihr
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Riquewihr was without a doubt the highlight of
         <a href="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com.com/alsace-roadtrip-itinerary/">
          my Alsace roadtrip
         </a>
         a few years ago. Its main street flanked by ancient timber houses colored in all hues of the rainbow, enticingRiesling cellars and delicious foodie delicacies; its very hard not to like Riquewihr. I felt like I had stepped in the Disneyland of 16th century Europe, knowing that the village has changed very little since then  nothing short of a miracle, considering most towns in this area of France were badly damaged during World War II.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Salers
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12627" style="width: 885px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Beautiful Villages in France" class="size-large wp-image-12627" height="570" srcset="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_8735.CR2_-930x606.jpg, http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_8735.CR2_-930x606@2x.jpg 2x" width="875"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/the-7-most-beautiful-villages-in-france/&amp;media=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_8735.CR2_-930x606.jpg&amp;description=My Favorite Villages in France')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Salers
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Welcome to
         <a href="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/my-beautiful-auvergne-a-photo-essay/">
          my beloved Auvergne
         </a>
         ! Although not the most famous nor the most visited, Auvergne is nonetheless one of the most strikingly beautiful thanks to its volcanoes (yes, there are volcanoes in France!) and its medieval villages like Salers, in the heart of Cantal department. Most buildings are made of Auvergnes signature black volcanic stone, endemic to the area. Salers being in France, it doesnt come as a surprise that the food isexceptionally good; its eponymous cheese is to die for, as is the local beef, one of the most sought-after breeds in the country.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Cassis
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12628" style="width: 885px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Beautiful Villages in France" class="size-large wp-image-12628" height="540" srcset="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_0528.CR2_-930x574.jpg, http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_0528.CR2_-930x574@2x.jpg 2x" width="875"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/the-7-most-beautiful-villages-in-france/&amp;media=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_0528.CR2_-930x574.jpg&amp;description=My Favorite Villages in France')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Cassis
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I pretty much havent stopped talking about Cassis ever since I visited in the summer of 2012. But with its warmly colored buildings, its salty sea breeze, its narrow alleys filled with bright bougainvilleas and its proximity to the jaw-droppingly beautiful Calanques, I think I am more than right to spread the word about this gorgeous town. Watching the sun set behind the high cliffs along the Mediterranean Sea while having dinner by the beach is one of my favorite memories of my time as an expat in France, and I owe it to Cassis.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Hell, I even proclaimed it to be the
         <a href="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/photos-of-cassis/">
          best village in France
         </a>
          that says something!
        </p>
        <h2>
         Saint-milion
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12626" style="width: 885px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Beautiful Villages in France" class="size-large wp-image-12626" height="582" srcset="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_5590.CR2_-930x619.jpg, http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_5590.CR2_-930x619@2x.jpg 2x" width="875"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/the-7-most-beautiful-villages-in-france/&amp;media=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_5590.CR2_-930x619.jpg&amp;description=My Favorite Villages in France')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Saint-milion
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         While my visit to
         <a href="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/a-very-rainy-afternoon-in-saint-emilion/">
          Saint-milion was not blessed by Mother Nature
         </a>
         , I still enjoyed myself immensely. Saint-milion is often regarded as being the finest example of a medieval village run bya wine-based economy  even today, 62% of the villages activities are connected to wine in one way or another. I simply loved wandering the narrow streets and admiring the architecture (most of the villages buildings are made out of theendemic yellowish stone once found in the regions stonepits) hopping from one wine cellar to another and enjoying a nice glass of wineal fresco.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Annecy
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12625" style="width: 885px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Beautiful Villages in France" class="size-large wp-image-12625" height="493" srcset="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_8503-930x524.jpg, http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_8503-930x524@2x.jpg 2x" width="875"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/the-7-most-beautiful-villages-in-france/&amp;media=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_8503-930x524.jpg&amp;description=My Favorite Villages in France')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Annecy
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         I think Ive made
         <a href="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/love-visiting-annecy/">
          my love for Annecy pretty obviousby now
         </a>
          Ive visited half a dozen time so far and I still cant get enough. From the legendary cheese fondue to the romantic canal in the center of the old town and the turquoise waters of the eponymous lake, Annecy just cannot bore me. Not to mention the fact that there are countless things to do in the region, should you ever want to get to know Rhne-Alpes a bit better. Annecy is simply stunning, and Im already looking forward to my next visit.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Saint-Martin-de-R
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12624" style="width: 885px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Beautiful Villages in France" class="size-large wp-image-12624" height="805" srcset="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_7843-930x856.jpg, http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_7843-930x856@2x.jpg 2x" width="875"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/the-7-most-beautiful-villages-in-france/&amp;media=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_7843-930x856.jpg&amp;description=My Favorite Villages in France')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Saint-Martin-de-R
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Ive left a huge piece of my heart in Ile de R,
         <a href="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/ile-de-re-daytrip-la-rochelle/">
          an idyllic island just off the coast of La Rochelle
         </a>
         . The town oozes with charm thanks to its laid back atmosphere (it is filled with French people on vacation, and as we all know, French people are always on vacation) and atypical, flavorful seaside cuisine. Mind you, I couldve picked any village on the tiny island; every single thing about Ile de R is absolutely dreamy and deserves to be visited. Between abbey ruins, flower-filled alleys, stunning ocean views, historic lighthouses and fortifications, Ile de R is just a giant living postcard waiting to be explored.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Honfleur
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_12623" style="width: 885px">
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
          <img alt="Beautiful Villages in France" class="size-large wp-image-12623" height="586" srcset="http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_9563.CR2_-930x623.jpg, http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_9563.CR2_-930x623@2x.jpg 2x" width="875"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/the-7-most-beautiful-villages-in-france/&amp;media=http://toeuropeandbeyond.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/1-IMG_9563.CR2_-930x623.jpg&amp;description=My Favorite Villages in France')">
          </span>
         </span>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Honfleur
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Honfleur is by no means a hidden gem; if the popularity of its picture-perfect streets and colorful port on Pinterest are any indication, about half the planet is planning to visit the town at some point. But that shouldnt deter visitors from coming to this famous Normandy town, whichhas inspired countless Impressionist painters including the most famous of all, Claude Monet. And being fromCanada, Honfleur was perhaps even more significant for me as it is the town where seafarer Samuel de Champlain started his 1608 voyage that would lead to the foundation of Qubec City. Talk about history being made!
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          <em>
           What are the most beautiful villages in France in your opinion? Have you visited any that arent listed here?
          </em>
