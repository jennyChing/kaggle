        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         My New Favorite Travel Purse: PacSafe 200
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-02-16T09:34:36+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 16, 2015
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.travelbelles.com/author/margo/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Margo Millure
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <h3>
         Review of the PacSafe Citysafe 200 travelpurse
        </h3>
        <p>
         I purchased a new travel pursefor a recent trip, the
         <strong>
          Pacsafe Citysafe 200
         </strong>
         (
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B006QN2VKW/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B006QN2VKW&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=lifintheshola-20&amp;linkId=ZVA2XEE5O6VTZ6A2">
          Pacsafe Luggage Citysafe 200 Gii Handbag, Black
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=lifintheshola-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B006QN2VKW" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         ). Choosing it for its size and safety features, how did this travel handbag fare?
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          When it comes to finding the perfect travel handbag, I always feel a little like Goldilocks.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="pacsafe travel purse review" class="aligncenter wp-image-92479" height="690" src="http://www.travelbelles.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/pacsafe1-1024x1024.jpg" width="690"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The travel pursethat holds everything is too big. The one that doesnt is too small, but besides being barely big enough to hold my wallet, it would sure look cute flitting about town. There are seemingly endless tradeoffs to consider, and in some cases analyzed to death. Judging from the 1000s of reviews of travel handbags on Amazon, Im not alone.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have to ask myself:
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Will I be carrying both cameras and extra lenses?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Will it hold my laptop?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Will my husband be embarrassed carrying it?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Is it going to fall apart?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Is it safe?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Will I be comfortable carrying this handbag into a nice restaurant? Is there a danger they might think Im carrying a bomb?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         And last, but not least,
         <em>
          Is it cute?
         </em>
         (Definitely in direct opposition to possibility of husband being okay with carrying it.)
        </p>
        <p>
         I was struck by Citysafes clean and classic lines combined with being constructed of durable materials. Offered in a variety of cross-body and backpack styles and colors, they dont draw attention to themselves. While I wouldnt call them cute, neither do they say,
         <em>
          Cute! Come get me!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         With sizes indicated by numbers (50  400), they run from mini to gargantuan, making choosing the right size for me and this trip somewhat challenging. After reading tons of customer reviews and much deliberation, I settled on the Citysafe 200 in black. When I un-boxed it, I was rather stunned by its large size. I wondered if I had ordered the Citysafe 400 model by mistake. Then I started loading it up.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_92470" style="width: 640px;">
         <img alt="fitting everything in pacsafe citysafe 200" class="wp-image-92470 size-full" height="424" src="http://www.travelbelles.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/413_2041.jpg" width="640"/>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Yes, thats a MacBook Air, an iPhone and an iPad.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         My need for a new travel pursewas two-fold: Having recently downsized cameras to an Olympus micro 4/3 with one zoom lens from a DSLR Nikon with multiple lenses, I would be leaving my well-worn and beloved Epiphanie camera bag behind. Second, since I usually check my suitcase, I needed an accessible pocket to carry my 13 MacBook Air with me through airport security. Although big and a bit unwieldy, my camera bag had held all these things and more.
        </p>
        <p>
         The inner pocket configuration of the Citysafe 200 seems to have been thought out with someone like me in mind. Various sized pockets and one zippered pouch neatly accommodate business cards, pens, and personal items, with two large compartments for separating my camera from the rest. The light green lining in the interior made it easy to see everything. On the outside, there is one good sized zippered pocket plus two small open compartments at either end for putting your cellphone and other small items for quick access.
        </p>
        <p>
         The bag is padded meaning that my camera was adequately protected from potential drops and bangs. (If youd like to convert any purse into a camera bag, check
         <a href="http://www.makeit-loveit.com/2012/03/turn-a-purse-into-a-padded-camera-bag.html">
          this
         </a>
         out.) I was surprised how easily my MacBook did fit in the inner compartment (but because of my camera, only without its power cord.) For this trip my husband ended up carrying my laptop in his briefcase, but the fact that I could even fit my MacBook in my handbag was a huge bonus.
        </p>
        <p>
         As the name implies, Pacsafe bags have many
         <a href="http://www.travelbelles.com/2012/12/6-travel-safety-trips-for-belles/" title="6 Travel Safety Tips for Women">
          safety
         </a>
         features. Their website states: At the heart of every innovative Pacsafe product is one clear purpose. To watch your back while you travel, so you can focus on having fun and doing more.
        </p>
        <p>
         Most touted is Pacsafes use of
         <strong>
          RFID technology,
         </strong>
         which protects items such as credit cards from identity theft. The Citysafe 200 features one RFID protected pocket that fit my wallet perfectly. I felt strongly about this feature, as my husband was a victim of this kind of theft several months back at an airport. Additional safety features include straps that are reinforced with lightweight stainless steel wire to thwart bag slashers from slicing through them. One of my favorite safety features that gave me a bit of peace of mind when out and about on busy city streets was relatively low tech: a simple zipper lock for the main compartment meant to deter pickpocketing attempts.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_92482" style="width: 480px;">
         <img alt='Here we are at the Palace of the Legion of Honor in San Francisco with me carrying the bag so you can see how it "fit" me.' class="size-full wp-image-92482" height="640" src="http://www.travelbelles.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/P1250049.jpg" width="480"/>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          Here we are at the Palace of the Legion of Honor in San Francisco with me carrying the travel purseso you can see how it fit me.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Last but not least, while I would often have the camera, my husband was perfectly comfortable carrying this travel handbag. Unless you look closely, it could pass for a camera bag; yes, a little on the big side, but not totally boxy. The only thing that I consider a downside is that although the strap is adjustable, when weighed down with all my things, it doesnt stay put, meaning that sometimes the bag would end up hanging down too low. But I probably can find a solution to this. Also, depending on where I was traveling, next time I would also pack a cute clutch for carrying in the evenings.
        </p>
        <p>
         As long as it holds up, my Citysafe 200 is going with me everywhere. Since it works so well as a work bag, since Ive been home, I havent moved out of it!
        </p>
