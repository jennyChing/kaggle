                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.otakureviewers.com/2015/06/difference-between.html">
                      Difference Between
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2882227405859669890" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <h2>
                      American Cartoons and Japanese Anime
                     </h2>
                     <br/>
                     <h3>
                      1. Invincible superheroes vs. Training day in and day out heroes
                     </h3>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In American cartoons:
                     </b>
                     Superheroes are practically invincible. Their powers are limitless or at least they cannot be exhausted. To be defeated, a superhero has to be caught, trapped, or totally annihilated in one shot or something.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In Japanese cartoons:
                     </b>
                     Heroes have to train hard day in and day out to get strong but once they do, they are clearly portrayed as having more strength than an average human, even with the constant reminders of the such. They can be defeated, but most likely by either a baddie who's trained just as much or is practically invincible, like the kind of hero mentioned above ^
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <h3>
                      2. Senseless violence vs. Senseless flashing
                     </h3>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In American cartoons:
                     </b>
                     Looney toons, the first contemporary cartoons had more violence than ever and the characters recovered easily. Thetrend, became less extreme as people realized that they could actually be hurt by the same things but there is still the sense in comedy toons that the character can recover from any weirdinjury.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In Japanese anime:
                     </b>
                     Japanese anime contains a lot of flashing, a trend that started in old anime but is starting to be only reserved for ecchi and hentai. But in the late 90's, early 2000's most anime had at least some scenes where a character (usually a female) might be a bit more naked than expected for a cartoon...hehe.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <h3>
                      3. Seeing yourself in a good light vs. Advertising your flaws
                     </h3>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In American cartoons:
                     </b>
                     The main character is always the best, no questions asked (usually). Usually when watching an American toon, you know that you should like the main character because they're always seen in a good light.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In Japanese cartoons:
                     </b>
                     Flaws are advertised to humanize the character and gain popularity with the fans. Most Japanese anime run polls for the fans to rank their favorite characters and the more human and relatable a character, the higher they are ranked. Often the less excellent the character, the higher they rank.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <h3>
                      4. One sided baddies vs. Multi-dimensional multi-tasking enemies
                     </h3>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In American cartoons:
                     </b>
                     The main bad guy is always 100 percent bad or evil. They devote most of their evil plan to getting the good guys for good (i.e. forever), and often fail because they're too perfectionistic. But they are quite persistent for being all bad, right?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In Japanese cartoons:
                     </b>
                     The main bad guy has lots of stuff going on, including an agenda that includes the demise of the main character, along with some other scheme for money or power. Often, the demise of the main character is a side project which is often a tragic flaw of a lot of Japanese bad guys. Side projects must be some kind of big thing in Japan...
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <h3>
                      5. Being the best vs. Doing the best things
                     </h3>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In American cartoons:
                     </b>
                     The most liked character is also the best character. If a viewer would be asked, who is their favorite character they most always say the main character. If there is a trio, it's always the main character of the trio. Unless the show is watched by more than one person, then one person might choose another character and try to justify why they are the best even if they are just a foil (or reflective of the greatness of the main character), often to their frustration with the creators.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In Japanese cartoons:
                     </b>
                     The most liked character might not be the main character. If there is a special moment in the story where a side character does something amazing that evoked emotions, then that character might be more well-liked and considered the best by the majority of anime fans. Possibly Alphonse Elric (FMA brotherhood), Gohan (DBZ), Kagome (Inuyasha) are some examples.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <h3>
                      6. Telling jokes vs. Being the jokes (the dealbreaker)
                     </h3>
                     <h4>
                      This is the dealbreaker, because both kinds of cartoons have perfected humor. If you truly like cartoons, you have laughed heartily at both kinds but for different reasons. This one is up to the reader.
                     </h4>
                     <b>
                      In American cartoons:
                     </b>
                     Characters have a repitoireof great jokes, and often a recurring joke that makes the viewer like the cartoon and the character. The characters have something great to say all the time and make the viewer feel like they have a lot to live for, at least in a group when someone is always stealing the attention. A punchline is key and usually punchlines that can be quoted during everyday life are numerous. This makes American cartoons surprisingly relatable, their quotability.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      In Japanese cartoons:
                     </b>
                     Characters do things in anime that are funny, but only in relation to the other characters. It's hard to laugh AT a character in an anime, without actually laughing about something that might have actually happened in your life. Anime makes normal life events that have already happened, seem more funny when the characters do them. It must be the mystique of anime, that romanticizes everyday life.

                      Japan Day 2015 in Central Park, NYC Review!
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3019328036414967739" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      Japan Day NYC 2015 IN Central Park!!
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      A picture-by-picture retelling of an awesome Otaku Event, by chansu :P
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <img height="400" src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/53909892e4b05f5bac99bffe/t/5526fd5ee4b05a7749c11d45/1428618595664/?format=750w" width="300"/>

                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-xyV2TBV_Aoo/VVJWTgn5fkI/AAAAAAAADGw/SFGdlH4SRJ0/s1600/20150502_173138.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="180" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-xyV2TBV_Aoo/VVJWTgn5fkI/AAAAAAAADGw/SFGdlH4SRJ0/s320/20150502_173138.jpg" width="320"/>

                      #sushimoney #otakuswagger #biginjapan
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-J1tIvofuvKM/VVJWL4JRxgI/AAAAAAAADGQ/mFz4SaT5lRA/s1600/20150510_144148.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="360" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-J1tIvofuvKM/VVJWL4JRxgI/AAAAAAAADGQ/mFz4SaT5lRA/s640/20150510_144148.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Oh beautiful Central Park. New York's finest slice of nature and good times in good weather. That is, especially when the biggest Japan culture event hosted by some awesome people comes to town! That's right, chansu, along with a couple random friends, visited Japan Day 2015, with hopes of seeing some cool cosplays, cool concerts, and cool tent activities!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-NbikvDgbMqQ/VVJWQocN-rI/AAAAAAAADGg/xfjcER6flXY/s1600/20150510_145100.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-NbikvDgbMqQ/VVJWQocN-rI/AAAAAAAADGg/xfjcER6flXY/s320/20150510_145100.jpg" width="180"/>
                      </a>
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-g0m5MiWhf6E/VVJWQwkNEhI/AAAAAAAADGk/w3vPyMYdH9w/s1600/20150510_145107.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-g0m5MiWhf6E/VVJWQwkNEhI/AAAAAAAADGk/w3vPyMYdH9w/s320/20150510_145107.jpg" width="180"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      The Japan Day map, in all it's splendor. No loss of Hello Kitty tens, face painting, traditional Japanese games, and the main studio area, with entertainment up the wazoo!
                     </div>
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-g0m5MiWhf6E/VVJWQwkNEhI/AAAAAAAADGk/w3vPyMYdH9w/s1600/20150510_145107.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                      <br/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--BTGutpIJ6g/VVJWXp_lzcI/AAAAAAAADG4/QLAzhWGalec/s1600/20150510_145136.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="225" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--BTGutpIJ6g/VVJWXp_lzcI/AAAAAAAADG4/QLAzhWGalec/s400/20150510_145136.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-EY_PCC9LEYQ/VVJXCiDtKOI/AAAAAAAADIg/TX6xTg565Q8/s1600/20150510_161258.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-EY_PCC9LEYQ/VVJXCiDtKOI/AAAAAAAADIg/TX6xTg565Q8/s320/20150510_161258.jpg" width="180"/>
                      </a>
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AfrOI0YMvkI/VVJWyCnVtbI/AAAAAAAADII/2GhgvdANY1M/s1600/20150510_161256.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AfrOI0YMvkI/VVJWyCnVtbI/AAAAAAAADII/2GhgvdANY1M/s320/20150510_161256.jpg" width="180"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      Among the cool tents were the Charity tent, where they sold newly made Japan Day T-shirts to support the cause of celebrating Japanese culture, and Yo-yo tent which isn't your traditional Yo-yo -- it's a Japanese water balloon attached to an elastic string. Unforunately I didn't have the patience to win one!
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-uUt4Me4BfgY/VVJWZiFPbxI/AAAAAAAADHM/Ec9Sc4xlTck/s1600/20150510_150059.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="360" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-uUt4Me4BfgY/VVJWZiFPbxI/AAAAAAAADHM/Ec9Sc4xlTck/s640/20150510_150059.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Among the performances were amazing Taiko drumming by Soh Daiko, Japanese folkdance, sword fighting, and karate demonstrations. and more. Above, you can see a Live Painting Performance by Takeshi Sato. I wonder what he's painting? :)

                    <meta content="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-zPy5R13Gg70/VUgxuKlv0cI/AAAAAAAAC0I/uN8TqvOR0MY/s640/night1.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://www.otakureviewers.com/2015/05/nightcore-hardcore-not-so-new-music.html">
                      Nightcore:  Hardcore Not-So-New Music Craze of your Nights
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7922929581629175130" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-zPy5R13Gg70/VUgxuKlv0cI/AAAAAAAAC0I/uN8TqvOR0MY/s1600/night1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="360" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-zPy5R13Gg70/VUgxuKlv0cI/AAAAAAAAC0I/uN8TqvOR0MY/s640/night1.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <h3>
                      Have you ever wondered what it would be like to hear all your favorite popular American songs and also your favorite J-pop songs in a new Otaku way? To some Otaku, the answer is holding their breath until they turn blue. To the Otaku who are well versed in Youtube, the answer is NIGHTCORE! That is correct, Nightcore is the new or not-so-new otaku music editing craze that transforms ordinary music into cool remixes fit for a discerning Otaku ear.
                     </h3>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-MGLAqanuU8U/VUgy2IoUAVI/AAAAAAAAC0Q/Q4YZYziW41s/s1600/night2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="223" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-MGLAqanuU8U/VUgy2IoUAVI/AAAAAAAAC0Q/Q4YZYziW41s/s400/night2.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     At first listen, these songs seem like
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      <span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace;">
                       "chipmunk"
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     edits but they are NOT
                     <span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                      "chipmunk"
                     </span>
                     edits... However, they are not mere switches of the pitch to create dull
                     <span style="font-family: Courier New, Courier, monospace; font-size: large;">
                      "chipmunk"
                     </span>
                     edits. Those are the bad and/or amateur songs that you may be unfortunate enough to come across when searching for your favorite songs.
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       Nightcore
                      </b>
                     </span>
                     music is totally edited and not just the pitch of the song but also the tempo, parts of the track timing and also equalizing elements such as treble and bass are tweaked. To the experienced DJ. the possibilities for a good
                     <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                      <b>
                       "Nightcore"
                      </b>
                     </span>
                     edit are endless or possibly not because of their expertise.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-sxaalHSj6-I/VUgzIzAt2JI/AAAAAAAAC0Y/TMRnp2kuKuo/s1600/night3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="211" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-sxaalHSj6-I/VUgzIzAt2JI/AAAAAAAAC0Y/TMRnp2kuKuo/s400/night3.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Every Nightcore edit on youtube most usually has an anime character, Japanese culture, or manga screenshot added. That means that it's an Otaku thing and not just some punks addicted to Helium who are trying to make everything like their own projection of the world. As much as I love Helium and world projections, Nightcore is about finding a spark of newness and otakulture in otherwise boring mainstream music and overplayed J-pop songs.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Here are a few "Nightcore" edits to show you what I mean (sorry in advance for too many Ariana Grande songs).
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/4SRUE5mVuRE" width="560">
                      </iframe>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/47ZMVQ266-U" width="560">
                      </iframe>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/pLMHl1XNRoU" width="560">
                      </iframe>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <br/>

    function generateMultipleOfThree(){

    }
