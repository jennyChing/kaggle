                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://www.lazybudgetchef.com/2014/06/24-ways-to-make-camping-and-cooking.html">
                      24 Ways to Make Camping and Cooking Easy and Awesome
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     When we were barely married, Husband and I tested our marriage by borrowing a tent to road trip from Ohio to North Dakota for a family reunion. Some of my family felt sorry for us because we chose to camp.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     What they didnt realize, that while camping allowed us to make the trip after attending five weddings that year (and throw our own,) we used my childhood RV camping experience and Husbands foodie skills to cook amazing meals and eat very, very well.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Heres how.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-aoc2zFj61vk/U44MhNlS5dI/AAAAAAAAMOg/8HjgCabGg5Y/s1600/24thingstomakecookingandcampingeasier.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a data-pin-config="above" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.lazybudgetchef.com%2F2014%2F06%2F24-ways-to-make-camping-and-cooking.html&amp;media=https%3A%2F%2Fimages-blogger-opensocial.googleusercontent.com%2Fgadgets%2Fproxy%3Furl%3Dhttp%3A%2F%2F4.bp.blogspot.com%2F-aoc2zFj61vk%2FU44MhNlS5dI%2FAAAAAAAAMOg%2F8HjgCabGg5Y%2Fs1600%2F24thingstomakecookingandcampingeasier.png%26container%3Dblogger%26gadget%3Da%26rewriteMime%3Dimage%2F*&amp;description=24%20ways%20to%20make%20cooking%20real%20food%20while%20camping%20easier%20so%20you%20can%20enjoy%20the%20fun%20without%20being%20stuck%20in%20the%20kitchen%20for%20the%20whole%20trip!%20Good%20ideas%20for%20campers%2FRVs%20and%20tent%20camping." style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_gray_20.png"/>

                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00ESI96SW/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00ESI96SW&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=conblu-20&amp;linkId=LMT2MVYGIIM6YYYE" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      <b>
                       Wonderbag
                      </b>
                     </a>
                     non electric slow cooker because I can use it to slow cook any recipe I make at home. It doesn't matter if we have electric hookup or not. Slow cookers give the camp cook more time to play!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?_encoding=UTF8&amp;ASIN=B00ESI96SW&amp;Format=_SL250_&amp;ID=AsinImage&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;WS=1&amp;tag=conblu-20" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

                      </a>
                     </div>

                      <i>
                       I cook in my Wonderbag even when I'm not camping. Love. It.
                      </i>

                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Solar ovens
                     </b>
                     are another power free slow cook option if you camp in where there is enough sun to heat the oven.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?_encoding=UTF8&amp;ASIN=B00BW6TZ2K&amp;Format=_SL250_&amp;ID=AsinImage&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;WS=1&amp;tag=conblu-20" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

                      </a>

                      <i>

                        All American Sun Oven
                       </a>
                       .
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     If you want to slow cook in or over a campfire, make sure you use a
                     <b>
                      Dutch oven
                     </b>
                     with a lip on the lid that keeps the coals out of the pot like the
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00008GKDW?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creativeASIN=B00008GKDW&amp;linkCode=xm2&amp;tag=conblu-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      my dream cast iron Dutch oven from Lodge (drool over it here.)
                     </a>
                     <br/>

                       Sure, there are digital and battery powered meat thermometers. I have one in my kitchen too. I bypass mine and go with the Classic when we are away from home because the batteries don't die when it is time for me to use it.
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     And speaking of food storage and temperature, pop a refrigerator thermometer like the
                     <a href="http://%22http//www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000BPE88E/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B000BPE88E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=conblu-20&amp;linkId=2MDJPJ4LFMFMSM5Q" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                      Taylor Food Service Classic Series Freezer-Refrigerator Thermometer
                     </a>
                     in your food cooler and/or RV refrigerator and freezer to make sure everything is at a safe to eat temperature, especially if your RVs power goes out (it can happen) or you misjudge when you need to add more ice to a cooler. I like the Taylor because it has the food safe temperatures marked and wont rust in a wet cooler because it is stainless steel.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?_encoding=UTF8&amp;ASIN=B000BPE88E&amp;Format=_SL250_&amp;ID=AsinImage&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;WS=1&amp;tag=conblu-20" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?_encoding=UTF8&amp;ASIN=B000BPE88E&amp;Format=_SL250_&amp;ID=AsinImage&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;WS=1&amp;tag=conblu-20"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <i>
                       I didn't buy a special thermometer for camping. I pop the one we have
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <i>
                       in the refrigerator in our cooler before a trip.
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     What are your camping must haves?
