          <p>
           If you have a slow metabolism, then dieting and exercise can seem almost pointless at times. You may spend hours at the gyms and starve yourself only to see little to no results. Slow metabolisms are not only frustrating, but they can also lead to other health risks, like eating disorders and heart problems.
          </p>
          <p>
           In order to speed up your metabolism, you should evaluate your current eating, exercise and sleep habits. If these are not in order, then you run the risk of slowing down our metabolic rate. Below is an outline of 5 tips to help improve these habits and send your metabolism sky high!
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Boost Your Metabolism Tip 1
           </span>
          </h1>
          <h2>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Eat More
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           _______________________________________________________________________________________________________
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/151921148.jpg">
            <img alt="Metabolism_eat" class="alignleft wp-image-26165" height="280" src="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/151921148-450x300.jpg" width="320"/>
           </a>
           This may seem backwards, but eating more- more of the right types and quantities of food- is single handily the most important way to boost your metabolism. First and foremost, if you are a long-term dieter (meaning you have been consuming a caloric deficit for longer than a year) you should
           <i>
            slowly
           </i>
           increase your caloric intake, by approximately 10%. Many people go on extreme diets, lose weight, gain it back and then jump onto the next crazy diet trend. If you continuously do this, you are wreaking havoc on your bodys metabolism. Give your system a break and gradually increase your calories for a few weeks. Eventually, you will reach a
           <i>
            healthy
           </i>
           caloric intake, meaning one that you can maintain without feeling weak or gaining weight.
          </p>
          <p>
           Along with increasing your caloric consumption, you should also eat more of the right types and quantities of food. The right types of food mean REAL food, like fresh fruits and vegetables, whole wheat and grains and lean meats. These foods contain fewer preservatives and more of the essential nutrients your metabolism needs to stay healthy and humming.
          </p>
          <p>
           In addition, you need to eat the right quantities of REAL foods to speed up your metabolism. If you are not already, you should be eating some sort of lean protein, such as lean fish, chicken or protein powder, at
           <i>
            every
           </i>
           meal. Protein has a greater thermic effect than the other macronutrients (carbs and fat), and thus it requires more energy to digest. Add more protein to your die, and you will stay full longer, build muscle and ramp up your metabolism!
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Boost Your Metabolism Tip 2
           </span>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            <br/>
           </span>
          </h1>
          <h2>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Drink More
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            _______________________________________________________________________________________________________
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/153489618.jpg">
            <img alt="Metabolism_drink" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-26166" height="300" src="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/153489618-228x300.jpg" width="228"/>
           </a>
           If you want to boost up your metabolism, then you should focus on drinking more of the fat-blasting liquids- water and green tea. Water is our crucial to our health and vital functions of the body. It helps detoxify the body and carries essential nutrients to our cells and organs. Aim to drink at least 5 liters of water to speed up your metabolism.
          </p>
          <p>
           Research has proven that regularly drinking green tea helps increase the bodys metabolism. Green tea contains polyphenols, which boost your metabolic rate, as well as antioxidants and caffeine to naturally boost your mood and energy levels. Drink at least 3-4 cups of green tea (in the morning) to maximize your fat burning potential.
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Boost Your Metabolism Tip 3
           </span>
          </h1>
          <h2>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Lift More
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            _______________________________________________________________________________________________________
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/122407067.jpg">
            <img alt="metabolism_lift" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-26167" height="300" src="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/122407067-202x300.jpg" width="202"/>
           </a>
           Lifting weights is not only a great form of exercise for burning fat and building muscle, but it also keeps your metabolism elevated for hours after your workout. Research has shown that your body will continue to burn calories for up to 24-48 hours
           <i>
            after
           </i>
           a strength training workout!
          </p>
          <p>
           In addition, weight/resistance training helps build muscle and thus increases your lean body mass. The more muscles you have, the more calories you will burn, even at a rested state. So start lifting, add more muscle to your frame and watch your metabolism rev up!
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Boost Your Metabolism Tip 4
           </span>
          </h1>
          <h2>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Sleep More
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            _______________________________________________________________________________________________________
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/143080390.jpg">
            <img alt="metabolism_sleep" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-26168" height="300" src="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/143080390-199x300.jpg" width="199"/>
           </a>
           Many people underestimate the power of sleep and its benefits to our health. Research has proven that lack of sleep causes higher blood sugar levels, higher stress levels and an increased appetite. Alternatively, those get adequate rest, approximately 8 hours, have higher energy and thus faster metabolisms. Turn off the computer and TV at least one hour before bed and catch your Zs to naturally increase your metabolic rate.
          </p>
          <p>
           Lifting weights is not only a great form of exercise for burning fat and building muscle, but it also keeps your metabolism elevated for hours after your workout. Research has shown that your body will continue to burn calories for up to 24-48 hours
           <i>
            after
           </i>
           a strength training workout!
          </p>
          <p>
           In addition, weight/resistance training helps build muscle and thus increases your lean body mass. The more muscles you have, the more calories you will burn, even at a rested state. So start lifting, add more muscle to your frame and watch your metabolism rev up!
          </p>
          <h1>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Boost Your Metabolism Tip 5
           </span>
          </h1>
          <h2>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            Sprint More
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span style="color: #2a3555;">
            _______________________________________________________________________________________________________
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/161721801.jpg">
            <img alt="metabolism_sprint" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-26169" height="300" src="http://www.modvive.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/161721801-270x300.jpg" width="270"/>
           </a>
           Like strength training, sprinting and other forms of HIIT (High Intensity Interval Training) burns calories during the workout and raises your metabolism for hours afterward. HIIT describes any workout that alternates between
           <span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: medium;">
            highly intense bursts of activity for 30 seconds to a minute with slow recovery for one to two minutes.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Below is example of a HIIT workout. Try doing this 2-3 times a week to significantly boost your metabolism!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           0-5 minutes: Warm up, light jog
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           5-10 minutes: Sprint 30 seconds, jog 30 seconds
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           10-15 minutes: Sprint 1 minute, jog 1 minute
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           15-20 minutes: Cool down, light jog
          </p>
