       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         How to Find Your Local Farms
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-08-08T16:20:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          August 8, 2013
         </time>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://thecrunchymoose.com/how-to-find-your-local-farms/#comments">
           3 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <span>
         <em>
          <b>
           <span style="font-size: x-small;">
            DISCLOSURE:
           </span>
          </b>
          <span style="font-size: small;">
           Please note these statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This is for informational purposes only and is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure or prevent disease. I am not a doctor. I am simply sharing my experiences.
           <em>
           </em>
          </span>
         </em>
        </span>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <p>
         Small, local farms are everywhere. But they rarely have an advertising budget. You dont see commercials on tv for them and you dont see ads for them in your local newspaper. They are farmers, not graphics designers, so they often dont have great websites. Luckily, there are lots of young farmers that are doing great things with social media. You have to find them. Heres how you can find your local farms.
        </p>
        <div>
         <a class="external" href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-_RfX106Awk8/UgPD2JcDsCI/AAAAAAAAERg/xfY-jSldrEg/s1600/DSC_1421.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-_RfX106Awk8/UgPD2JcDsCI/AAAAAAAAERg/xfY-jSldrEg/s1600/DSC_1421.jpg', '');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="228" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-_RfX106Awk8/UgPD2JcDsCI/AAAAAAAAERg/xfY-jSldrEg/s320/DSC_1421.jpg" width="320"/>
         </a>
         <span id="more-95">
         </span>
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <div>
         <a class="external" href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-K2paGy67Xfs/UgPD6Bj2BgI/AAAAAAAAERo/vlaKSQ2YoJU/s1600/DSC_1439.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-K2paGy67Xfs/UgPD6Bj2BgI/AAAAAAAAERo/vlaKSQ2YoJU/s1600/DSC_1439.jpg', '');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-K2paGy67Xfs/UgPD6Bj2BgI/AAAAAAAAERo/vlaKSQ2YoJU/s320/DSC_1439.jpg" width="228"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div>
         <div>
          <ul>
           <li>
            I found my favorite farm (I am a farm volunteer with them),Cardos Farm Project, on the website of my local health food store (The Cupboard). I was on The Cupboards website, and they had a list of suppliers where they get their food. I went to each farms website to find out more information. Luckily, Cardos was looking for volunteers then &amp; I was able to sign up.
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             So ask your local health food store for a list of suppliers and check their website to see if they post a list
            </span>
            . Many of these stores focus on transparency and are proud of their food, so they are more than happy to share the info with you!
           </li>
           <li>
            Also check the labels at your health food store. I went to The Cupboard one day with a pen and a piece of paper. I checked all the labels on the meat in the meat freezer and jotted down the info. Local, grass-fed, &amp; free range ranches with chickens, cows, and other meat animals proudly put their information on their products.
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             So look at the labels of meat and check out the ranches and farms online.
            </span>
            Thats how I found my first source of grass-fed meats,
            <a class="snppopup external" href="http://texasgrassfedbeef.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://texasgrassfedbeef.com/', 'Slankers');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Slankers
            </a>
            . Slankers ships their products using dry ice. I still buy their buffalo from The Cupboard, but I dont mass order from them and have it shipped anymore because I found a local farm I purchase from (
            <a class="snppopup external" href="http://www.blogger.com/Homestead%20Farmshttp://thecrunchymoose.blogspot.com/2013/08/homestead-farms.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.blogger.com/Homestead%20Farmshttp://thecrunchymoose.blogspot.com/2013/08/homestead-farms.html', 'Homestead Farms');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Homestead Farms
            </a>
            ). So Slankers is a great option if you cant find anything close to you or prefer to order online.
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
             Go to your farmers markets!
            </span>
            Talk to the farmers. Many farms have farm stands you can purchase directly from.
            <a class="snppopup external" href="http://thecrunchymoose.blogspot.com/2013/08/fresh-milk.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://thecrunchymoose.blogspot.com/2013/08/fresh-milk.html', 'Earthwise Gardens');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Earthwise Gardens
            </a>
            is a local farm that has a great farm store. Cardos is moving to a new location and they will also have a great store.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div>
           <a class="external" href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Q0dnv547E6Y/UgPELDVRbXI/AAAAAAAAERw/SHQveuFxOUU/s1600/DSC_0066.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Q0dnv547E6Y/UgPELDVRbXI/AAAAAAAAERw/SHQveuFxOUU/s1600/DSC_0066.jpg', '');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="228" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Q0dnv547E6Y/UgPELDVRbXI/AAAAAAAAERw/SHQveuFxOUU/s320/DSC_0066.jpg" width="320"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <div>
           <a class="external" href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ERcNKrkywQQ/UgPELV6YB2I/AAAAAAAAER4/kGrjUEtcRi8/s1600/DSC_0077.jpg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ERcNKrkywQQ/UgPELV6YB2I/AAAAAAAAER4/kGrjUEtcRi8/s1600/DSC_0077.jpg', '');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            <img alt="" border="0" class="aligncenter" height="228" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ERcNKrkywQQ/UgPELV6YB2I/AAAAAAAAER4/kGrjUEtcRi8/s320/DSC_0077.jpg" width="320"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <div>
           You will find that once you get involved with the farming community, you will find more and more sources for great local food! As a volunteer with Cardos, I interact with lots of other farms and get new information from them regularly.
          </div>
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Here are some great websites to help you get started (these are all external links):
         </div>
         <div>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a class="snppopup external" href="http://www.localharvest.org/organic-farms/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.localharvest.org/organic-farms/', 'Local Harvest');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Local Harvest
            </a>
            Type in your zip code and farms and dairies in your area pop up! Its a great list.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a class="snppopup external" href="http://www.eatwild.com/products/index.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.eatwild.com/products/index.html', 'Eat Wild');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Eat Wild
            </a>
            List of grass-fed meats and dairies. They break it up by state. Even if they arent in your immediate area, still check out their website. Maybe they ship (like Slankers does and I used to order from them). Or maybe they are close enough for you to drive to every few months and stock up. Thats what I do. Our farm (Homestead) that we buy meat from is about 45 minutes away. We go once every 3 or 4 months and buy a freezer full of meat. Its all frozen.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a class="snppopup external" href="http://www.realmilk.com/real-milk-finder/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.realmilk.com/real-milk-finder/', 'Real Milk');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Real Milk
            </a>
            Extensive list of dairies.
           </li>
          </ul>
