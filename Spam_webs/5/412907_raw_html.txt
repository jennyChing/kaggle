           <header class="entry-header">
            <h1 class="entry-title">
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2013/02/01/superman-re-skinned-as-other-pop-icons/" rel="bookmark">
              Superman Re-Skinned as Other Pop-Icons
             </a>
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta">
             on February 1, 2013 by Benjamin Starr
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->
           </header>
           <!-- .entry-header -->
           <div class="entry-content">
            <p>
             <img alt="Mike Mitchell Superman Re-Skinned 1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-74695" height="703" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Mike-Mitchell-Superman-Re-Skinned-1.gif" width="600"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Perhaps the most famous of all Superman imagery comes, not surprisingly, from his very first comic features.
             </strong>
             The cover of
             <a href="http://www.metropolisplus.com/superman/Superman06.jpg">
              Superman #6
             </a>
             featured a smiling hero, his radiant face looking out on a world he was happy and generous enough to save. Its classic Americana to the max. When artist Mike Mitchell recently took on the iconic image, he gave it everything but a new face  re-skinning Superman with the clothing of pop-culture icons far and wide, old and new.
             <span id="more-74694">
             </span>
            </p>
            <div class="see-also">
             <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2012/05/06/its-history-superheros-in-old-war-photos/" title="It's History: Superheroes in Old War Photos">
             </a>
             <p>
              See Also
              <a href="http://www.visualnews.com/2012/05/06/its-history-superheros-in-old-war-photos/" title="It's History: Superheroes in Old War Photos">
               It's History: Superheroes in Old War Photos
              </a>
             </p>
             <div class="clearfix">
             </div>
            </div>
            <p>
             Hardly a character has been left alone: we have Charlie Brown and Wonder Woman taking us back to other classic comics, and there is The Dude and Margo from The Royal Tenenbaums bringing us into modern cinema. Perhaps the hardest hitting of the series are the most odd, like a version of Spongebob Squarepants or Ralphie from The Christmas Story in full bunny attire. If youre interested in more pop-icons gone Superman, head to Mike Mitchells site,
             <a href="http://www.sirmikeofmitchell.com/">
              SirMikeOfMitchell.com
             </a>
             , where you can find
             <a href="http://sirmitchell.com/">
              prints and t-shirts
             </a>
             too.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Mike Mitchell Superman Re-Skinned 2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-74696" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Mike-Mitchell-Superman-Re-Skinned-2.png"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Mike Mitchell Superman Re-Skinned 3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-74697" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Mike-Mitchell-Superman-Re-Skinned-3.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Mike Mitchell Superman Re-Skinned 4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-74698" height="347" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Mike-Mitchell-Superman-Re-Skinned-4.jpg" width="600"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Mike Mitchell Superman Re-Skinned 5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-74699" height="443" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Mike-Mitchell-Superman-Re-Skinned-5.jpg" width="600"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="Mike Mitchell Superman Re-Skinned 6" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-74700" height="443" src="http://cdn.visualnews.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Mike-Mitchell-Superman-Re-Skinned-6.jpg" width="600"/>
            </p>
