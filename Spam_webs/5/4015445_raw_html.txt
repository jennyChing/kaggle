          <h2 class="graf--h2" id="title" name="title">
           Failure Is Temporary
          </h2>
          <h4 class="graf--h4" id="subtitle" name="subtitle">
           Why falling on your face is the first step to success
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p graf--empty">
           <br/>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="a910" name="a910">
           Most of us falsely believe in the permanence of failure.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="8042" name="8042">
           We assume that if we experienced one great mistake, one terrible tragedy, we would forever be sunk. Our career over, our reputation ruined, and our future kaput.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="94de" name="94de">
           But when has that
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            ever
           </em>
           happened? When was the last time a single decision destroyed you, totally messed up everything? It doesnt happen. And yet we are still deathly afraid that it
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            might
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="a8be" name="a8be">
           That needs to stop. And it needs to stop now.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body" name="8532">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <h4 class="graf--h4 graf--first" id="a1f8" name="a1f8">
           How failure happens
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p" id="cd5b" name="cd5b">
           Every
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://www.epicfail.com/" href="http://www.epicfail.com/" rel="nofollow">
            epic fail
           </a>
           is the result of bad habits practiced over time. One poor decision leads to another and so on until, before you know it, youre walking down a road you never intended to be on.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="7230" name="7230">
           This is how addictions happen, how relationships end, and how the world goes to hell. That is, slowly.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="f820" name="f820">
           We become what we are by
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://goinswriter.com/practice/" href="http://goinswriter.com/practice/" rel="nofollow">
            practicing
           </a>
           . By slowly exerting effort over time until it becomes natural. Habitual, even.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="24f3" name="24f3">
           Nothing happens, as it turns out, by accident. No consistent failure comes to us and no fear is realized that we didnt have some part in creating.
          </p>
          <blockquote class="graf--blockquote graf--startsWithDoubleQuote" id="31f4" name="31f4">
           You areyour life, and nothing else.
           <em class="markup--em markup--blockquote-em">
            
           </em>
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--blockquote-anchor" data-href="http://www.goodreads.com/work/quotes/8826974" href="http://www.goodreads.com/work/quotes/8826974" rel="nofollow">
            <em class="markup--em markup--blockquote-em">
             Jean-Paul Sartre
            </em>
           </a>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="graf--p" id="f0f4" name="f0f4">
           We see this every day: in friends unwilling to change, in our own stubbornness or compulsions, in the growing number of people medicating their own anxiety. We fail over time, never at once.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="0fef" name="0fef">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            But at any given point, you can turn around. You can begin again.
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body" name="f005">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <h4 class="graf--h4 graf--first" id="c980" name="c980">
           The power of resurrection
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p" id="f287" name="f287">
           Theres something powerful about a second chance. I should know; Ive had about a thousand of them.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="2e37" name="2e37">
           Theres something liberating about starting over, about seeing something you thought was dead come back to life (unless, of course, youre watching
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mUSvdqpQ6d0" href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mUSvdqpQ6d0" rel="nofollow">
            <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
             Pet Sematary
            </em>
           </a>
           then its just plain creepy).
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="aba0" name="aba0">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            After a resurrection, we always appreciate life more.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="edf8" name="edf8">
           When I learned one decision couldnt ruin my life, that I had more Get Out of Jail Free cards than I thought, I was finally free. To take more risks. To put myself out there. To fail.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="6c40" name="6c40">
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://goinswriter.com/fear-art/" href="http://goinswriter.com/fear-art/" rel="nofollow">
            Fear
           </a>
           becomes a distant apprehension instead of a familiar anxiety when we have the assurance that failure wont kill us. Yes, it comes and its inconvenient, but you
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            will
           </em>
           survive. And because of this knowledge, you can risk and trust and grow.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="5d40" name="5d40">
           For me, there is still the occasional fearthe fear of failure or fear of being rejectedbut its more a reverential awe than a haunting of the soul.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="798b" name="798b">
           When I attempt something, I respect the possibility that it might not work, that there is real risk in the endeavor. And this drives me to succeed.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="295b" name="295b">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            Fear tells me what Im doing is worthwhile, because it could fail.
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </section>
       <section class=" section--body" name="ed7e">
        <div class="section-divider layoutSingleColumn">
         <hr class="section-divider"/>
        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <h4 class="graf--h4 graf--first" id="de79" name="de79">
           A little taste of heaven
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p" id="1fbf" name="1fbf">
           Success is not guaranteed, but failure is. You
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            will
           </em>
           fall short of some goals, your dreams wont always come true, and you will eventually die.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="50ff" name="50ff">
           So thats our baseline. We can all agree on that, yes? If those are our definites, heres what we dont know: what will you do with today? How hard you will go? What will you sacrifice? And what, pray tell, will you do with failure?
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="5858" name="5858">
           Will it keep you from starting over or fuel your desire to go again?
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="15ac" name="15ac">
           This is what makes any game worth playing and every life worth livingit doesnt last forever. Theres a cost not only to win, but just to play. You could get hurt or disowned by your loved ones or just plain embarrassed.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="1039" name="1039">
           But when you win, and sometimes you do, the rewards taste that much sweeter because of all you endured to arrive at paradise.
          </p>
          <blockquote class="graf--blockquote graf--startsWithDoubleQuote" id="4c08" name="4c08">
           Everybody wants to go to heaven. Nobody wants to die.
           <em class="markup--em markup--blockquote-em">
            
           </em>
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--blockquote-anchor" data-href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Lb-EJEWRxlM" href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Lb-EJEWRxlM" rel="nofollow">
            <em class="markup--em markup--blockquote-em">
             Albert King
            </em>
           </a>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="graf--p" id="e315" name="e315">
           This is the beauty of falling on our faces and getting back up. Its the liberation we need to do great work. Without the risk, the rise to the top doesnt feel like much of an ascent.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="a03f" name="a03f">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            You cant really win without loss, and success feels small when theres nothing at stake.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="ab31" name="ab31">
           So what do we do? Learn to love the risk and accept the inevitable failure that comes with every great pursuit.
          </p>
          <h4 class="graf--h4" id="8dc3" name="8dc3">
           Not everything is temporary
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p" id="6806" name="6806">
           Failure fades. Thats the good news.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="5e38" name="5e38">
           You can always try a different approach, take another stab at the problem, move to a foreign country, date somebody new, or start over with your manuscript.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="4381" name="4381">
           This feeling of falling short wont last forever; it eventually goes away. What does last, though, is your perseverance and the impact you create by not giving up.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="2bf3" name="2bf3">
           Your successes and failures, even accolades and critiques, are all short-lived in the grand scheme of the cosmos. But some things
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            are
           </em>
           eternal.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="62e1" name="62e1">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            Anyone can lead a safe, comfortable life, free from failing. But where is the fun in that? Where is the glory, the legacy?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="2cd9" name="2cd9">
           Its in the very thing youre avoiding: the risky moments.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="41cc" name="41cc">
           When you play a bigger game, one that involves danger and potential failure, you place yourself amongst historys heroes. You walk the halls of legend. You become a myth.
          </p>
          <blockquote class="graf--blockquote graf--startsWithDoubleQuote" id="154f" name="154f">
           Pain heals. Chicks dig scars. Glory lasts forever.
           <em class="markup--em markup--blockquote-em">
            
           </em>
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--blockquote-anchor" data-href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0191397/quotes" href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0191397/quotes" rel="nofollow">
            <em class="markup--em markup--blockquote-em">
             Shane Falco, The Replacements
            </em>
           </a>
          </blockquote>
          <p class="graf--p" id="0ef8" name="0ef8">
           This is where we discover the thrill of doing something that mattersin the midst of failure.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="61fb" name="61fb">
           Sure, youll mess up and miss the boat; youll say something dumb or get told you have nothing of value to offer. You may even screw up so badly you have to change your name and move to Idaho.But even then, you must endure and remember:
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="11c4" name="11c4">
           <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">
            This is not the end; you are not yet finished.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="de9e" name="de9e">
           This hiccup is another leg in the journey, a reminder you are still alive. This game is not yet over. Which means youve got at least one more chance to make your dent in the universe.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="5aae" name="5aae">
           Just dont forget the most important part of leaving a legacy, of achieving success that lives on long after youre gone:
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p graf--last" id="edd4" name="edd4">
           You can not quit.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
