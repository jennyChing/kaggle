           <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
            Why I Think The New Google Play Website Is A Good Thing And Everyone Is Unhappy With Progress
           </h1>
           <div class="meta-info">
            <div class="td-post-author-name">
             By
             <a href="http://freedupthoughts.com/author/tobiassmann" itemprop="author">
              Tobias Mann

         <div class="td-ss-main-content">
          <div class="td-post-featured-image">
           <a data-caption="" href="http://freedupthoughts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/13%20942%20PM.png">
            <img alt="" class="entry-thumb td-modal-image" height="315" itemprop="image" src="//freedupthoughts.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/13%20942%20PM.png" title="" width="681"/>

          <div class="td-post-content td-pb-padding-side">
           <p>
            Yesterday Google released there updated Google Play website, further implementing consistency across their various platforms.
           </p>
           <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle" style="margin: 5px; padding-right: 10px; float:right;">
            <style>
             .in-post-ad-1{width:320px;height:100px;}@media(min-width: 500px) { .in-post-ad-1 { width: 200px; height: 200px; } }

            </script>

            <ins class="adsbygoogle in-post-ad-1" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7069344527606799" data-ad-slot="4753811860" style="display:inline-block">
            </ins>
            <script>
             (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
            </script>
           </div>
           <p>
            When I first saw the news I was rather surprised at the response. It seems that in updating the interface Google has put off implementing features. Probably to ensure those features were implemented smoothly and consistently, all the while matching up with the new card style layout we saw earlier on the Android variation of the market.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <span id="more-52">
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            So why is it everyone has to jump on Google when they reinvent their image. Well Google has long had a tendency to transition to new ideas rather than make jumps. Here is my logic: When Google released Hangouts at I/0 the capacity to make phone calls from Gmail was lost, however a few weeks later it was reintroduced.
           </p>
           <p>
            Google will mostly likely reintroduce any missing features from the previous edition of the Google Play Store when it is possible to do so.
           </p>
           <p>
            In my eyes this change is a good one. It is difficult for people to transition from one style to another and keeping something fairly consistent across platforms just makes for a better experience. Not to mention progress is good, and Google does this well. Keeping their products fresh means they care about them, and are willing to invest in those products futures.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             So tell me, how do you feel about the recent changes to Google Play?
            </b>
           </p>
