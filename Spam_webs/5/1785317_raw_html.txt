     <div class="entry" id="post-191">
      <h3 class="storytitle">
       <a href="http://www.businesscreditcards.com/bootstrapper/hidden-gems-the-100-edu-sites-every-entrepreneur-should-read/" rel="bookmark">
        Hidden Gems: The 100 .edu sites every Entrepreneur Should Read
       </a>
      </h3>
      <p class="date">
       Wednesday, October 24, 2007 at 11:25am by Site Administrator
      </p>
      <div class="post">
       <p>
        Whether youre an ivy-leaguer or a high school dropout, chances are you still have quite a bit to learn from others. Why not learn from the people who do learning best? Check out these .edu blogs and other informative sites for strategies, theory, and concrete resources for building and growing your business.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Marketing
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Marketing may be one of the many things you try to squeeze into your business, but for the people behind these sites, this is all they do. Take advantage of their ability to specialize and use their knowledge to better market your business.
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://cci.mccombs.utexas.edu/">
           Center for Customer Insight &amp; Marketing Solutions
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This center at the University of Texas focuses on customer-driven business practices and marketing.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.journals.uchicago.edu/JCR/">
           Journal of Consumer Research
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Learn more about consumers with this journal.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://blogs.adison.edu/visions">
           Marketing Visions &amp; Business Software Solutions Blog
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Get an academic perspective on Internet marketing here.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href=" http://advertising.utexas.edu/world/">
           Advertising World
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Get linked up with lots of marketing services and ideas in this huge directory from the University of Texas.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://marketing.blogs.ie.edu/">
           Marketing Weblog
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Learn about building brands, marketing over Second Life, and more on this blog from Instituto de Impresa.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.nova.edu/~marker/blog.html">
           Research Design and Statistics
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Learn about psychology, behavioral economics, and decision making in this blog by Craig Marker.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Innovation
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Inside every great entrepreneur is an inventor, constantly thinking of new and exciting ways to solve problems and create new things. These sites support the inventor inside, offering encouragement, resources, and a whole lot more.
       </p>
       <ol start="7">
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href=" http://web.mit.edu/invent/">
           Invention Dimension
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Invention Dimension, a part of the Lemelson-MIT Program, is full of inventor profiles and resources for innovative entrepreneurs.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://ieblog.clarku.edu/">
           The Art of the New
          </a>
         </strong>
         : The Art of the New encourages and shares innovative thinking.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/jim/">
           Innovation
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Jim Moore takes a look at innovation, intellectual property, economics, and more.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.innovations.harvard.edu/">
           Government Innovators Network
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Check out this network if you plan to do contract work with the government.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href=" http://sulcus.berkeley.edu/FLM/SH/MDL/Invention/Invention.Masterlist.html">
           Invention Master Resource List
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This resource list from Berkeley is full of links for innovative entrepreneurs.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href=" http://web.mit.edu/cipd/">
           Center for Innovation in Product Development
          </a>
         </strong>
         : This center at MIT promotes innovative products, offering resources from concept to market launch.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://patriotentrepreneur.gmu.edu/">
           The Patriot Entrepreneur
          </a>
         </strong>
         : On this blog, youll get news about innovation at George Mason University and beyond.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://scripts.mit.edu/~innovation/vlog/">
           MIT Innovation Club Blog
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Read this blog to learn about some of the smart innovations MIT students and alumni have created.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href=" http://www.nttc.edu/">
           National Technology Transfer Center
          </a>
         </strong>
         : NTTC offers assistance to businesses with innovative ideas.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href=" http://invention.smithsonian.org/home/">
           The Lemelson Center
          </a>
         </strong>
         : The Lemelson Center at the Smithsonian Institute promotes the study of invention and innovation.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Industries
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Educational resources can sometimes be frustrating because they tend to focus on theory, while youre looking for concrete ideas. Check out these blogs for some specialization in a few industries.
       </p>
       <ol start="17">
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www-rcf.usc.edu/~pgordon/blog/">
           Peter Gordons Blog
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Find out what this USC professor thinks about the economy and real estate development.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://blog.sessions.edu/">
           Notes on Design
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Learn about design from both academics and professionals on this blog.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://blog.vcu.edu/nascar/">
           The View from Here: The Business of Nascar
          </a>
         </strong>
         : Read this blog to understand how business works in a place you might not think of.
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://blog.lib.umn.edu/levin031/transportationist/">
           The Transportationist
          </a>
         </strong>
         : David Levin writes about transportation and the economy.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <strong>
         International Business
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        In todays global economy, entrepreneurs must be familiar with the ideas of international business. Learn about law, events, and more issues in international business with these sites.
       </p>
       <ol start="21">
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://ciberweb.msu.edu/cibers.asp">
           CIBER
          </a>
         </strong>
