     <p>
      <b>
       Ninetales
      </b>
      , known in Japan as
      <b>
       Kyukon
      </b>
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
        
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <i>
        Kykon
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon" title="List of Pokmon">
       Pokmon species
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">
       Nintendo
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Game_Freak" title="Game Freak">
       Game Freak
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon" title="Pokmon">
        Pokmon
       </a>
      </i>
      franchise. Ninetales evolves from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vulpix" title="Vulpix">
       Vulpix
      </a>
      when exposed to a Fire Stone. Created by
      <a href="/wiki/Ken_Sugimori" title="Ken Sugimori">
       Ken Sugimori
      </a>
      , it first appeared in the video games
      <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Red_and_Blue" title="Pokmon Red and Blue">
       <i>
        Pokmon Red
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        Blue
       </i>
      </a>
      and later appear in subsequent sequels, various merchandise, spinoff titles, and animated and printed adaptations of the franchise. It is known as the
      <a href="/wiki/Fox" title="Fox">
       Fox
      </a>
      Pokmon.
     </p>

     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Prince_Hanzoku_terrorised_by_a_nine-_tailed_fox.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2130" data-file-width="1398" height="267" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Prince_Hanzoku_terrorised_by_a_nine-_tailed_fox.jpg/175px-Prince_Hanzoku_terrorised_by_a_nine-_tailed_fox.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Prince_Hanzoku_terrorised_by_a_nine-_tailed_fox.jpg/263px-Prince_Hanzoku_terrorised_by_a_nine-_tailed_fox.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Prince_Hanzoku_terrorised_by_a_nine-_tailed_fox.jpg/350px-Prince_Hanzoku_terrorised_by_a_nine-_tailed_fox.jpg 2x" width="175"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Prince_Hanzoku_terrorised_by_a_nine-_tailed_fox.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>

     <p>
      Ninetales, also known as the Fox Pokmon, is a golden-white nine-tailed fox, based on the
      <a href="/wiki/Kitsune" title="Kitsune">
       kitsune
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japanese
      </a>
      fox spirit.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGNorigin_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGNorigin-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Kybi
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
        
       </span>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      , which held similar powers such as
      <a href="/wiki/Kitsune#Shapeshifting" title="Kitsune">
       shapeshifting
      </a>
      , were the main inspiration for the Pokmon. Ninetales' name was derived from the number of its tails, nine, and the fact that the idea for it came primarily from ancient Japanese tales,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGNorigin_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGNorigin-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      although the English name may also be a reference to a kind of
      <a href="/wiki/Whip" title="Whip">
       whip
      </a>
      known as a
      <a href="/wiki/Cat_o%27_nine_tails" title="Cat o' nine tails">
       cat o' nine tails
      </a>
      . This fox-like Pokmon is covered with a thick, luxurious golden-white fur, with a fluffy crest atop its head and a similar ruff around its neck. Ninetales have gleaming red eyes that are said to give them the power of mind control.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its nine different tails hold strange, cosmic powers
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      that let it live for 1000 years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Ninetales are highly intelligent Pokmon that understand human speech.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      They are very vengeful and have been known to curse those who mistreat them for 1000 years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Many legends surround this Pokmon, one of which states that Ninetales was born when nine saints were united and reincarnated as this Pokmon.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_video_games">
       In the video games
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ninetales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In the video games">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Ninetales first appeared in
      <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Red_and_Blue" title="Pokmon Red and Blue">
       <i>
        Pokmon Red
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        Blue
       </i>
      </a>
      , though it was only obtainable in
      <i>
       Blue
      </i>
      without trading. It has since appeared in every main
      <i>
       Pokmon
      </i>
      title since, including remakes of the original games including
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Yellow" title="Pokmon Yellow">
        Pokmon Yellow
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_FireRed_and_LeafGreen" title="Pokmon FireRed and LeafGreen">
       <i>
        Pokmon FireRed
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        LeafGreen
       </i>
      </a>
      . Outside of the main titles, it appears in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Pinball" title="Pokmon Pinball">
        Pokmon Pinball
       </a>
      </i>
      titles,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Trozei!" title="Pokmon Trozei!">
        Pokmon Trozei!
       </a>
      </i>
      , the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Mystery_Dungeon" title="Pokmon Mystery Dungeon">
        Pokmon Mystery Dungeon
       </a>
      </i>
      titles, the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Ranger" title="Pokmon Ranger">
        Pokmon Ranger
       </a>
      </i>
      titles,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Rumble" title="Pokmon Rumble">
        Pokmon Rumble
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9Park_Wii:_Pikachu%27s_Adventure" title="PokPark Wii: Pikachu's Adventure">
        PokPark Wii: Pikachu's Adventure
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Mystery_Dungeon:_Blue_Rescue_Team_and_Red_Rescue_Team" title="Pokmon Mystery Dungeon: Blue Rescue Team and Red Rescue Team">
       <i>
        Pokmon Mystery Dungeon: Blue Rescue Team
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        Red Rescue Team
       </i>
      </a>
      , Ninetales cast a curse on a human that would turn it into a Pokmon, bringing on many natural disasters. The player is blamed for this and travels to the home of Ninetales at the top of Mt. Freeze to learn the truth. Ninetales later appears when
      <a href="/wiki/Gengar" title="Gengar">
       Gengar
      </a>
      (the real cursed human) wishes to right his wrongs. Ninetales is an important part of the plot of
      <i>
       Pokmon Mystery Dungeon: Blue Rescue Team
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Red Rescue Team
      </i>
      , because of its involvement in the change of the player, as rumors say.
     </p>

     <p>
      Ninetales first appeared in the
      <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_(anime)" title="Pokmon (anime)">
       anime
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       Beauty and the Breeder
      </i>
      . A friend of Suzy named Zane had a Ninetales and used it in a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_breeding" title="Pokmon breeding">
       Pokmon breeding
      </a>
      contest. In
      <i>
       Just Waiting On A Friend
      </i>
      , a very old Ninetales befriended
      <a href="/wiki/Brock_(Pok%C3%A9mon)" title="Brock (Pokmon)">
       Brock
      </a>
      , who bore a resemblance to her long lost master. This Ninetales created all sorts of illusions, including one of a woman through which it spoke. Many other Ninetales have made minor appearances such as one being used by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blaine_(Pok%C3%A9mon)" title="Blaine (Pokmon)">
       Blaine
      </a>
      to battle
      <a href="/wiki/Ash_Ketchum" title="Ash Ketchum">
       Ash
      </a>
      in their Gym battle.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Adventures" title="Pokmon Adventures">
        Pokmon Adventures
       </a>
      </i>
      manga,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_(Pok%C3%A9mon_Adventures)" title="Red (Pokmon Adventures)">
       Red
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_(Pok%C3%A9mon_Adventures)" title="Blue (Pokmon Adventures)">
       Blue
      </a>
      both tried catching the same Ninetales. In the scuffle for its Pok Ball, Blue managed to grab it first.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_Magma" title="Team Magma">
       Magma
      </a>
      Admin Courtney uses a Ninetales as her main battler. In '
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Mystery_Dungeon:_Ginji%27s_Rescue_Team" title="Pokmon Mystery Dungeon: Ginji's Rescue Team">
        Pokmon Mystery Dungeon: Ginji's Rescue Team
       </a>
      </i>
      , the curse that Ninetales can conjure is one of the main points of the plot.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ninetales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Ninetales has had generally positive reception.
      <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">
       IGN
      </a>
      called Ninetales "one heck of a Pokemon that too many people tend to overlook" and a surprise "bombshell-dropper".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGN-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The reviewer continued, exaggerating that Ninetales may be "considered the new god of all that is holy."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGN-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      IGN also called it "one of the most visually impressive Pokemon."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      IGN readers ranked it the 31st Pokmon ever. IGN's Kristine Steimer called it "elegant and powerful" and called it "one of the more impressive evolutions" from
      <i>
       Pokmon Red
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Blue
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Game_Informer" title="Game Informer">
        Game Informer
       </a>
      </i>
      's O'Dell Harmon also ranked it at 31 on his list of the best Pokmon.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Capital_Times" title="The Capital Times">
        The Capital Times
       </a>
      </i>
      concurred that Ninetales was "particularly powerful".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, IGN also noted that the character served little purpose until later games beyond looking attractive.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_10-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGN-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Many reviewers have commented on the perceived aesthetic beauty of Ninetales. IGN claimed that, while Ninetales may not be as cute as other Pokmon in the series, it makes up for this with sheer beauty.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_10-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGN-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Another noted Ninetales primarily for its visual appeal.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGNorigin_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGNorigin-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Several sources have commented on Ninetales' name, with various reporters calling it "whimsical"
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and "fanciful".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Another IGN reviewer expressed a particular fondness for Ninetales, calling it "elegant".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN2_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-IGN2-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Kotaku" title="Kotaku">
       Kotaku
      </a>
      's Patricia Hernandez cited Ninetales as one of the better Pokmon
      <i>
       Red
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       Blue
      </i>
      due to its "elegance."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
