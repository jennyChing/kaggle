          Adonit's Jot Touch and Jot Script 2 make a strong case for the stylus
         </a>
        </h2>
       </div>
       <p class="info">
        <span class="byline-container">
         <img class="avatar" height="24px" src="
    http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-global/dims/GLOB/5/24/24/http://www.blogcdn.com/www.engadget.com/media/2013/01/4-1358186128.jpg

       <p>
        I've never been a big fan of the stylus. Sure, I don't really have a choice when I'm using
        <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2015/03/02/wacom-cintiq-13hd-touch/">
         Wacom's Cintiq
        </a>
        <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2015/03/02/wacom-cintiq-13hd-touch/">
         pen displays
        </a>
        , but other than that, I don't care much for styli. Adonit has a pair of them -- the
        <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/06/18/adonits-latest-jot-touch-stylus-works-with-adobes-cloud-softwa/">
         Jot Touch
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2013/10/28/jot-script-evernote-edition/">
         Jot Script 2
        </a>
        -- and they promise a more pen-like feel, so now seemed like as good a time as any to give the stylus another shot. Alas, though, after spending a few weeks getting to know these two gadgets, I can confidently say that I'm not ready to give up the ol' pen and paper just yet.
       </p>

To celebrate the occasion, we joined Neil deGrasse Tyson, his colleagues, and a theater full of science geeks at the American Natural History Museum to explore what the New Horizons mission really means.          </a>

       <p>
        WiFi-equipped gadgets don't really say anything to each other before they connect, which limits what they can do -- you can't use them as Bluetooth-like
        <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/11/12/samsung-proximity/">
         smart beacons
        </a>
        , or quickly find who's sharing photos.  All that's going to change soon, though, thanks to the newly unveiled
        <a href="http://www.wi-fi.org/news-events/newsroom/wi-fi-certified-wi-fi-aware-personalizes-the-mobile-experience-everywhere-users">
         WiFi Aware
        </a>
        spec.  The technology has devices swapping tiny messages about their services when they detect each other, making it easy to send alerts and connect only to those devices you care about.  You could quickly find nearby rivals for a multiplayer game, for example, or get a notification about a sale when you wander by a preferred store.  And no, battery life shouldn't take a hit.  Although WiFi Aware can run in the background, its use of a common "heartbeat" for the sync process could actually make it more efficient than conventional WiFi.
       </p>

        YouTube phenom CaptainSparklez, whose real name is Jordan Maron, has partnered with Activision co-founder Howard Marks to develop
        <a href="https://www.startengine.com/startup/xreal">
         <em>
          Fortress Fury
         </em>
        </a>
        , a competitive mobile game. Maron and Marks operate at seemingly opposite ends of the video game industry, but working together, they've found common ground and cultivated more than 1 million downloads of
        <em>
         Fortress Fury
        </em>
        in its first few weeks on iOS and Android. In a video interview, the unlikely duo tell us how they first met (Grandmother Sparklez makes an appearance), and how the industry is evolving with crowdfunding, YouTube and eSports.
       </p>
