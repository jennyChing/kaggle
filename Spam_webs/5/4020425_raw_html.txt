       <h2 class="post">
        <a href="http://www.realfarmacy.com/usda-approves-gmo-apples-heres-know-difference/">
         USDA Approves GMO Apples. Heres How to Know The Difference
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p class="meta">
        Feb 13 
        <a href="http://www.realfarmacy.com/the-word/" rel="category tag">
         Breaking Stories
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.realfarmacy.com/in-the-news/gmos/" rel="category tag">
         GMOs
        </a>
         3368 Views 
        <span>
         Comments Off
        </span>
       </p>
       <div style="clear: both;">
       </div>
       <div class="hrline">
       </div>
       <div style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
        <style>
         .responsivet { width: 320px; height: 100px; }
@media(min-width: 700px) { .responsivet { width: 728px; height: 90px; } }
        </style>
        <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
        </script>
        <!-- ResponsiveT -->
        <ins class="adsbygoogle responsivet" data-ad-client="ca-pub-5590547337599978" data-ad-slot="5296748449" style="display:inline-block">
        </ins>
        <script>
         (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
        </script>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.realfarmacy.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/GMO-Apples.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="GMO Apples" class="alignleft wp-image-35474" height="355" src="http://www.realfarmacy.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/GMO-Apples.jpg" width="376"/>
        </a>
        Today, the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) let down U.S. apple growers and the public by wasting resources on a useless and risky food. To make matters worse, these new genetically engineered apples are NOT required to be labeled and consumers will not know that the sliced apples they are feeding to their children have undergone genetic modification. Whats more is that this
        <a href="http://ecowatch.com/news/food-agriculture/gmo-genetically-modified-organism/" target="_blank">
         genetically engineered
        </a>
        specialty fruit has been created to prevent apples from browning over time, as if that is an important issue worth harming the environment and consumer health.
       </p>
       <div style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
        <script type="text/javascript">
         google_ad_client = "ca-pub-5590547337599978";
    google_ad_slot = "6443576446";
    google_ad_width = 336;
    google_ad_height = 280;
        </script>
        <!-- RealFarmMobile1 -->
        <script src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/show_ads.js" type="text/javascript">
        </script>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        They are called Arctic Apples and have the ability to resist browning when sliced, something apple growers and consumers have been able to deal with forever. This is a mere cosmetic trait which can mislead consumers into thinking theyre eating a fresh apple when in fact they are eating apples on the verge of rotting.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Ecowatch
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://ecowatch.com/2015/02/13/usda-approves-gmo-arctic-apples/" style="color: #0000ff;" target="_blank">
          reports
         </a>
        </span>
        :
       </p>
       <div style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;margin-top:8px;margin-bottom:8px;">
        <script type="text/javascript">
         google_ad_client = "ca-pub-5590547337599978";
    google_ad_slot = "8414173241";
    google_ad_width = 300;
    google_ad_height = 250;
        </script>
        <!-- In post 2 -->
        <script src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/show_ads.js" type="text/javascript">
        </script>
       </div>
       <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
        The USDAs environmental review received 73,000 comments that
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://ecowatch.com/2013/12/02/comment-deadline-looming-on-gmo-arctic-apple/" style="color: #0000ff;" target="_blank">
          overwhelmingly opposed the commercialization of Arctic Apples
         </a>
        </span>
        . This decision marks the first approval of an aesthetically-improved genetically engineered food and will expand the reach of GMO products into the produce aisle, which currently only offers a small number of GMO foods.
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
        The USDA has neglected to look at the full range of risks from these apples. In its environmental assessment, the USDA glossed over the possibility of unintentional effects associated with the technology used to engineer these apples, potential economic impacts on the U.S. and international apple market, effects of potential contamination for non-GMO and organic apple growers and the impact of the non-browning gene silencing which also can weaken plant defenses and plant health.
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The new method used to modify the apple is known as RNA interference. RNA is similar to DNA and deals specially with gene coding and regulation. Mounting evidence has shown that meddling with the genes can result in unintended effects within the plant and within those who eat the plant.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The genes altered in this apple are heavily involved in a plants natural defense system against pests and disease. This could lead to apple trees that are more susceptible and therefore require more chemicals to produce a crop. It begs the popular question, why is our food being made and modified by chemical pesticide and herbicide companies? That is a huge conflict of interest in and of itself. Besides, this wouldnt be the first time an unintended result from genetic engineering resulted in an exponential increase in the amount of poison sprayed on the food supply.
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left: 60px;">
        <strong>
         The amount of Roundup used on the biggest three GE crops
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          <a href="http://www.realfarmacy.com/bill-nye-takes-controversial-issue-way-pretty-much-everyone-can-understand/" style="color: #0000ff;" target="_blank">
           has increased 1000%
          </a>
         </span>
         from what it was before the introduction of genetically engineered seeds.
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_30077" style="width: 410px">
        <a href="http://www.realfarmacy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/gmo-glyphosate-chart.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto">
         <img alt="gmo glyphosate chart" class="wp-image-30077 size-full" height="473" src="http://www.realfarmacy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/gmo-glyphosate-chart.jpg" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Glyphosate aka Roundup use has increased 1000% since the introduction of genetically engineered crops.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        One huge con to genetically engineered food is that the crops have been known to infect organic crops on the other side of town through pollen drift. This can spread like wildfire and devastate the local organic and even conventional industry. This is a major reason the domestic apple industry opposes the new franken-apple which will be marketed as Arctic Granny and Arctic Golden.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Some food companies have stated that they would not sell Arctic Apples. The U.S. Apple Association, Washington Apple Commission and other grower groups have spoken up and pointed out the negative impact GMO apples could have on the apple industry and environment. Even GMO-peddlers
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://naturalsociety.com/3-companies-using-toxic-gmos-baby-formula/" style="color: #0000ff;" target="_blank">
          Gerber
         </a>
        </span>
        and
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://www.realfarmacy.com/shocking-ingredients-found-mcdonalds-french-fries/" style="color: #0000ff;" target="_blank">
          McDonalds
         </a>
        </span>
        stated that they would not use GMO apples in their food products.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The bottom line is that the GMO apple is a risk that is unnecessary. Cosmetic issues should be the last priority for an industry that claims they want to end world hunger, which of course is a lie. The world has enough food, people are starving because there isnt enough money to pay for it. They really just want to sell more pesticide.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        The biotech industry is in a prime position. They modify the food supply which
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://goo.gl/tGqye4" style="color: #0000ff;" target="_blank">
          creates crops dependent
         </a>
        </span>
        on more and more poisonous pesticides and herbicides, have
        <span style="color: #0000ff;">
         <a href="http://www.monsanto.com/whoweare/pages/monsanto-relationships-pfizer-solutia.aspx" style="color: #0000ff;" target="_blank">
          monetary ties
         </a>
        </span>
        to giant pharmaceutical corporations that bank off of sick people, and all the while they are the ones selling these very same pesticides and herbicides, which make people sick.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        Consumers and the industry have dealt with browning apples successfully forever. The USDA has let down hard-working farmers who go back generations in America, again. They have let down the everyday consumer who wants to know what they are eating. They have jeopardized the environment and the health of the very citizens these alphabet governmental agencies are supposedly meant to protect. And for what? A prettier apple? Yeah, one that tastes like shit because its seconds away from rotting. But you wont know that until you bite into it.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
