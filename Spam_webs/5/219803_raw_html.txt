       <h1 class="entry-title">
        So they mutilated your genitals, why are you still afraid? says US Government
       </h1>
       <h2 class="entry-meta">
        by
        <a href="http://neurologicalcorrelates.com/wordpress/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by swivelchair">
         swivelchair
        </a>
        
        <time class="published" datetime="2008-03-20">
         March 20, 2008
        </time>
        
        <a href="http://neurologicalcorrelates.com/wordpress/2008/03/20/it-claims-that-female-genital-mutilation-is-no-basis-for-allowing-asylum-claims-as-once-the-mutilation-has-already-occurred-there-is-no-reason-to-believe-that-you-would-be-further-persecuted/#respond" title="Comment on So they mutilated your genitals, why are you still afraid? says US Government">
         0 Comments
        </a>
       </h2>
      </header>
      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        If someone mutilates your genitals, would you be afraid of future persecution from them? You shouldnt be, according to the U.S. government, as detailed in  recent
        <a href="http://www.fas.org/sgp/crs/misc/RS22810.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.fas.org/sgp/crs/misc/RS22810.pdf', 'Congressional Research Service report']);" target="_blank" title="Yule Kim, ">
         Congressional Research Service report
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://neurologicalcorrelates.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2008/03/female-genital-mutilation.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://neurologicalcorrelates.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2008/03/female-genital-mutilation.jpg', '']);" title="Female Genital Mutilation via Wikipedia (creator: Kaylima)">
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <a href="http://neurologicalcorrelates.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2008/03/female-genital-mutilation.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://neurologicalcorrelates.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2008/03/female-genital-mutilation.jpg', '']);" title="Female Genital Mutilation via Wikipedia (creator: Kaylima)">
         <img alt="Female Genital Mutilation via Wikipedia (creator: Kaylima)" height="374" src="http://neurologicalcorrelates.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2008/03/female-genital-mutilation.jpg" width="345"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        <em>
         Diagram 1:This image shows the different types of FGC and how they differ to the normal female anatomy. (Via
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Female_genital_cutting" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Female_genital_cutting', 'Wikipedia']);" target="_blank" title="Female Genital cutting, Wikipedia entry, visited 03.19.08">
          Wikipedia
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Image:FGC_Types.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Image:FGC_Types.jpg', 'Wikimedia commons']);" target="_blank" title="Female Genital Cutting, Wikimedia Commons, visited 03.19.08">
          Wikimedia commons
         </a>
         , creator: Kaylima)
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-448">
        </span>
        According to the
        <a href="http://www.who.int/reproductive-health/fgm/index.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.who.int/reproductive-health/fgm/index.html', 'World Health Organization']);" target="_blank" title="WHO Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) landing page, visited 03.19.08">
         World Health Organization
        </a>
        :
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         <span style="font-weight: 400">
          Female          genital mutilation
          <span lang="en-gb">
          </span>
          comprises all procedures involving partial or          total removal of the female external genitalia or other injury to the          female genital organs for non-medical reasons.
         </span>
         <span style="font-weight: 400">
          Between 100 and 140 million girls and women in the world are          estimated to have undergone such procedures, and 3 million girls are          estimated to be at risk of undergoing female genital mutilation every          year. Female genital mutilation has been documented in 28 countries in          Africa and in several countries in Asia and the Middle East. Some forms          of the practice have also been reported from other countries, including          among certain ethnic groups in Central and South America. There is also          evidence of increasing numbers of girls and women living outside their          place of origin, including
          <span lang="en-gb">
           in
          </span>
          North America and western Europe, who have          undergone or may be at risk of undergoing female genital mutilation.
         </span>
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        In the judicial branch, federal courts treat past genital mutilation as a well-founded fear of future persecution.  But, in the executive branch, the Board of Immigration Appeals rejects this position, arguing that FGM is a one-time procedure, and that once inflicted, an applicant will not be persecuted with FGM again, and thus cannot act as a basis for an asylum application.
       </p>
       <p>
        Make no mistake, there is a continuing harm to the women and girls. Women with FGM are significantly more likely than those without FGM to have adverse obstetric outcomes. Risks seem to be greater with more extensive FGM.
        <br/>
        <span style="padding: 5px; float: left">
        </span>
        <span class="Z3988" title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Ajournal&amp;rft.aulast=&amp;rft.aufirst=&amp;rft.au=+ &amp;rft.title=The+Lancet&amp;rft.atitle=Female+genital+mutilation+and+obstetric+outcome%3A+WHO+collaborative+prospective+study+in+six+African+countries&amp;rft.date=2006&amp;rft.volume=367&amp;rft.issue=9525&amp;rft.spage=1835&amp;rft.epage=1841&amp;rft.genre=article&amp;rft.id=info:DOI/10.1016%2FS0140-6736%2806%2968805-3">
        </span>
        (2006). Female genital mutilation and obstetric outcome: WHO collaborative prospective study in six African countries.
        <span style="font-style: italic">
         The Lancet, 367
        </span>
        (9525), 1835-1841. DOI:
        <a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(06)68805-3" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(06)68805-3', '10.1016/S0140-6736(06)68805-3']);" rev="review">
         10.1016/S0140-6736(06)68805-3
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        In trying to understand this, about the only thing I can come up with is circumcision: male babies are routinely circumcised. Although at some point in the future, we may be horrified by this, as of now it seems to be pretty standard, and, of course, males have no fear of future persecution.  Female genital mutilation, of course, is a perversion of all of that, and although it can only happen once, it is enough to instill fear into anyone. It is considered by the WHO to be a human rights violation.
       </p>
