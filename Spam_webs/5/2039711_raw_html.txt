       <h1 class="entry-title">
        I ate this, I ate that, some more over there
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on

        <a href="http://worthallthewhile.com/2013/09/20/4722/i-ate-this-i-ate-that-some-more-over-there/" rel="bookmark" title="6:00 am">

        <p>
         Ah. The Great State Fair of Oklahoma, which arrives only once a year.
         <br/>
         Thank heavens.
         <br/>
         Its a gustatory nightmare, I tell you.
         <br/>
         Whilst amongst its gates, I manage to eat much. But we loosened up the internal cavity by starting with a corn dog at the On Cue before entering the blacktopped folds of the Fair.
         <br/>
         Why On Cue? 1: because Bubs was staaaaaaaaarvingand only a corn dog would do! I didnt mention the irony of eating
         <em>
          before
         </em>
         going to the fair, because, you know, he was already saving me five bucks.
         <br/>
         And b: On Cue has tickets behind the counter to save me from standing in long lines to get tickets. There are lines? I queried. In reply I received a noncommittal shrug from the clerk. I bought tickets.
         <br/>
         And off to the Fair we go!
         <br/>
         Aroma de Cow hit us acres away from the entrance gate.We were forced to park three city blocks from any entrance. Its like a law. And invariably it rains during the fair even though it may not have rained since
         <em>
          last
         </em>
         September, it will always always always rain during the fair  thus your car is rendered inserviceable (nope, not a word, but you get my meaning), thanks to mud and the trenching involved in removing your vehicle from the once proud prairie it was only hours before the deluge hit. Its tradition. Thats why you should always wear boots and a slicker to the fair: to get your car out of the lot. (I however always sport the French
         <em>
          Flip de Flop
         </em>
         for my adventuring.)
         <br/>
         Once within the ten-foot chain-link walls of the compound, we aim toward food.
         <br/>
         (Side note: no line at the ticket counter. None. Four vendor windows wide open. I should always take a noncommittal shrug as what it is: insouciance and a need to get me to move on.)
         <br/>
         Whats the Trifecta of fair food, the three that will make you most
         <em>
          ill
         </em>
         ?
         <em>
          Lead the way
         </em>
         !
         <br/>
         Catfish! Fried Twinkie! and Chocolate Covered Bacon! Gag!
         <br/>
         <em>
          Ill take it!
         </em>
         <br/>
         Just kidding.
         <br/>
         Fair Fare of the Year: Fried garlic mashed potatoes  on a stick!
         <br/>
         And Fried Mac-n-Cheese! (whaaaaat?) and the new Pot Roast sundae has expanded its possibilities to include not only beef, but pork or chicken. All smothered in mashed potatoes and gravy and topped with a cherry tomato. So cute.
         <br/>
         But toooo cutesy for me at that moment; I was ravenous and dying for something real.
         <br/>
         So I
         <em>
          inhaled
         </em>
         a bar-b-q pulled pork sandwich like it was from an oxygen tank on the moon.
         <br/>
         My stomach rumbled:
         <em>
          MORE
         </em>
         ! it cried. (Really. It did.)
         <br/>
         Well, then lookie over there at the spiraled spud! Yes, please! With a plate piled high with paper thin helices of potato parts, I gleefully turned toward the Bubs to share my bounty. But lo, whats this? A high prairie wind! Which blew three of my lovely curls up and off my whimsically thin paper plate and on to the scrabble hard top surface where twenty gajazillion feet have trod! Three bucks o spud, on the ground.
         <br/>
         Oh well. Its a Fair Casualty; deal with it and eat the survivors!
         <br/>
         And we did.
         <br/>
         Yet there was more than enough for the Bubs and I to eat our fill and wander to a trash can, green gilled and nauseated, to throw some away.
         <br/>
         <em>
          AND WHATS NEXT?
         </em>
         my belly roared.
         <br/>
         To which my brain replied: Yeah, okay, justgimme a minute 
         <br/>
         Belly: Chicken.
         <br/>
         Brain: (ruminatingha! get it??) Yeah, well, I saw a chocolate chip cookie dough stand out there. Youd better get ready, smartie.
         <br/>
         Belly: BRING IT!!!
         <br/>
         And I did.
         <br/>
         Plus two Dr Peppers at $3.50 a pop  $3.50!!!! which the sellers said was entirely the doing of the Powers that Be, so dont blame the messenger  one peach bellini and two cherry limeades that Bubs couldnt power down.
         <br/>
         (He doesnt have his Fair Gut yet, but give him a year, hell be in tip top shape.)
         <br/>
         Big sigh.
         <br/>
         I love the fair.
        </p>

         Ill take it!
        </p>
